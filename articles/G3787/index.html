Rope knots in our lives

Knitting knots is one of the first things that humanity has learned in the process of its evolution. They fastened the tools, made the first snares and traps for hunting, as well as castles.

The very first ropes were stripes from the bark of trees, vines, animal skins. They fastened the first clothes and the first bed. Gradually, people mastered weaving and were able to make ropes on their own, making them more durable and longer. Yes, and it was thanks to them that its main symbol, the flag, first ascended over the state.

A rope is a faithful companion of a person on travels, both sea and air. Using a rope without knitting knots is hard to imagine. These words have long been associated with each other. It was the sailors who became the first to knit marine knots, schemes of which can be found in textbooks on rigging.

Nautical knots: history and scheme of knitting

They are an integral part of human culture, it is difficult to imagine our world without them. New nodes continue to be invented today, and each of them receives authorship, as an invention, and a patent.

Their genius is simplicity. They can be stopper, quick-release, protracted and non-retentive. This art has been passed down from generation to generation. Thanks to the ropes, the first devices for swimming on water appeared - rafts. The knot also has a mystical meaning - this is not just a way of fastening, but a talisman that protects its owner from harm.

The sailors of Northern Europe had a belief about three magic knots - correctly stored on ship's gear, they were kept from collapse. There was even a ritual to unleash, which was supposed to cause the wind.

In the heyday of seafaring, there were about 500 knots. Their names derive from the gear to which they were applied - for example, pile or boat. Nowadays, nodes are also identified with the country where it was invented - the Spanish gazebo or the Flemish loop.





The working ends of the cable with which knitting is carried out are called running. They are worn around the indigenous, which remain motionless. These ends are opposite to each other.

And to this day, you can find nationalities that are not affected by the benefits of civilization, and they still make weapons and snares for hunting and fishing using knitting knots.

Rope knots: their types and methods of knitting

You will need a rope, not very thick and soft enough. Just like in knitting, training is needed here. The first time you remember a little, therefore, each new unit must be worked out several times. And of course, they need to be used in everyday life, otherwise they will quickly be erased from memory.

Yes, each of them has its own name. But do not focus too much on them, it is better to remember the sequence of combinations. There are not many established names, and more often they depend on the source.

The simplest knot is knitted like this: at one end of the rope a loop is made into which the running end is threaded. All. Its disadvantage is a decrease in the strength of the rope. An experienced fisherman or seafarer always cuts off random knots or replaces them.

Testing of knitting skills can begin with the G8. It does not reduce strength and is easily untied. The practical value is to limit the movement of the rope. Eight is an element of other more complex nodes.





If you need to connect two ropes, knit a straight (or reef) knot. To increase reliability and strength at the running ends, you need to make eights. When knitting, two ropes cross twice in succession. One of the free ends doubles to form a bow. If the number of running revolutions is made larger, a surgical unit will be obtained that fits on tensioned cables.

Excess crochets and turns can not only ruin everything, but also make one knot completely different. Therefore, when knitting, carefully monitor the directions of the turn.

Classification

Direct or reef included in 24 major marine nodes. Why are they called that? It is believed that they are most used in navigation and it was with them that it all started. In this classification, direct and reef are two nodes, although the principle of their mating is exactly the same. The figure eight and the surgical (or double straight) also apply here.

In those cases when it is necessary to tie the ends of two ropes, they knit a flat knot or bayonet. Its distinguishing feature is its symmetry. Knitting technology: one of the ends is folded with a loop, the second end is wrapped around in the same way, the ends are pulled together. They must be intertwined one through the other.

One of the most used in rigging is a pile unit. The lead end is looped into the loop with the same loop. They drag it around an object, otherwise it will not drag on.
