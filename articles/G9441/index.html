Holy Panteleimon Church . The first construction of the temple dates back to 1875. Then the first immigrants in this area - the Greeks - built a temple in honor of the Holy Great Martyr and Healer Panteleimon and placed there an icon brought earlier from Turkey.

In 1936, the temple was destroyed, like many throughout Russia.

The current building was erected in 1994 with donations from the local population and is active.

Battery captain Zubkov . This is an open-air branch of the Gelendzhik Museum. Here are genuine historical exhibits of the 394th battery of the 1st Guards Artillery Division of the Novorossiysk Naval Base of the Black Sea Fleet. The commander of the battery was from 1941 to 1943, Andrei Emanuilovich Zubkov.

Under his command, she heroically restrained the onslaught of the enemy for a year and did not allow the Nazi invaders into the strait, although all this time the enemies ruled over most of Novorossiysk. But in mid-September 1943, the battery fired a final salvo at the outgoing enemies.

Kastalskaya font . A great place to relax among juniper thickets with cozy gazebos. The second name is Trout Farm. In the center is a lake where you can catch fish, which will be cooked here in the kitchen.





Since the Kastalsky font is popular among locals, reserving places is in advance.

Old park . It is considered the main attraction. Here is the only place where architectural features of all cultures and peoples of the world are brought together and uniquely interwoven with natural elements. This park was opened in 2007, and is called the old one due to the fact that ancient cultures are represented there. A great opportunity to take a break from saturated sunny days and spend time in the shade and cool under the dense greenery.

There is also a dolphinarium and an aquarium, entertainment clubs, possibly yachting, diving. And if you want something more active, you can get to Gelendzhik or Novorossiysk.
