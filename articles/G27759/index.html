Small geographic overview





Sugar is known for three main types of surfaces: erga, rega and hamada. Ergi is a huge sandy area covered with dunes. Rega is a plain that is constantly and continuously blown by the winds, covered with sand from coarse gravel, gravel and pebbles. And the gamada is a high plateau that is covered with rock and shale. It is because of the presence of all types of desert surfaces that the Sahara belongs to the sandy-stony deserts.

Gamada is the primary formation in relation to ergs and regs.
