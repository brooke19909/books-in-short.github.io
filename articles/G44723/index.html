What to see in Yalta? Yalta: excursions. Yalta: "Swallow's Nest"

Yalta is a large administrative and resort region of the Southern Crimea. Every corner of this city is notable for its unique objects and seaside picturesque landscapes. Over the past decades, the tourism industry has significantly improved in the region, landscaping and landscaping work has been carried out.

The resort has a favorable climate: like an old amphitheater, it is located on the hills of the Crimean mountains, which create a protective barrier from strong winds. The air here is special, saturated with the aroma of cypresses, lavender fields and the breath of the Black Sea. At all times, the region was considered one of the best in the world.





Arriving on vacation, tourists do not always know what to see in Yalta and where to go. The surrounding area of ​​the resort is fraught with a huge number of outstanding attractions. Numerous temples, cathedrals, park areas deserve special attention and interest. There are no unique places in the region!

Various routes and tours can be offered by Yalta: “Swallow's Nest”, Nikitsky Garden, Ai-Petri Mountain, Massandra Palace and much more. More about this later. People come here to rest here all year round. The resort is considered one of the cleanest and most landscaped. It is buried in protected parks and lush plantings.

Start your journey should be from the main street - Embankment them. Lenin and move towards the General Post Office. The promenade is completely reconstructed: framed by beautiful palm trees, flower beds, equipped with attractions, entertainment venues and shopping centers, this is a real paradise for tourists. Those wishing to take a tour of the historical past of the city, get acquainted with the culture and traditions of the local population, should visit Old Yalta with its rugged streets and ancient buildings.

Despite the apparent crampedness and modern architecture, it is quite nice and cozy here. It is Old Yalta that reminds guests and residents of a rich history. Wellness complexes, squares, perfectly preserved centuries-old cypresses and mighty cedars are concentrated in this area. But let's move from nostalgia to the main thing - the sights of the Crimean peninsula.

The legendary castle "Swallow's Nest"





The city of Yalta is rightfully proud of this ancient building. The “Swallow's Nest” in the 19th century was the estate of a Russian general whose name is still unknown. The cottage was built on the very slope of the 40-meter Ai-Todor mountain and previously was a wooden cottage.

Many famous artists painted amazing paintings from him. After the death of the general, the estate became the property of the court doctor Tobin, who later donated it to his beloved wife. In 1903, the cottage was bought by an unknown merchant, and from that time the estate began to be called "Swallow's Nest."

The whole story of this magnificent castle is real, it is very eloquently told to visiting tourists who, as if spellbound, listen to the guide. Today, this miniature palace with a spacious panoramic platform and restaurant is a symbol of the coast. From its height, a picturesque view of the Yalta Bay.

The estate really bears a resemblance to a bird's nest. The unique man-made structure is owned by the municipality of the city. It is this architectural monument that is famous for Yalta. “Swallow's Nest” is a real work of art that evokes vivid emotions among visitors. Here once filmed the film "Ten Little Indians". It is adjacent to the stone building "Captain's Bridge", near which there is a rugged rock Sail with a sculpture of an eagle. Yachts, boats and motor ships moor in this bay.

Climb to the famous peak





This is not limited to the list of outstanding objects. Believe me, what to see in Yalta is not a question! This amazing region has many amazing places. One of the main attractions is Mount Ai-Petri. There are several ways to get to the rock: by car, by cable car, by bicycle and on foot. From its elevation, a magnificent panorama of the entire South Coast opens. Here, on a mountain plateau, connoisseurs of photographs gather.

Mount Ai-Petri organizes horse and bike tours of the rocky terrain. Climbers come here for thrills. In winter, there is a lot of snow on the slope, the temperature is quite low. There are slides for sledding and skiing. For guests there are restaurants, cafes and souvenir shops.

Livadia Palace

What else is Yalta famous for? Where to go, what to see at the resort? Arriving on the Crimean peninsula, be sure to visit the Livadia residence, in which Russian emperors lived. Today it is a popular museum with well-groomed alleys, a quiet park and a magnificent Italian architectural ensemble.

The territory contains several objects: the Big (White) Livadia Palace, the Holy Cross Exaltation Church, the Page Corps, the building of the Minister of the Courtyard and a small Florentine courtyard with open gazebos and benches. They will tell you about the life of the state of that period and the major events held in this palace and park estate.

Guests will see unique ancient expositions devoted to the work of the Yalta Conference, and will visit the bedchairs of members of the Tsar's family Nikolai ΙΙ. Presidential receptions and important business conferences of heads of state are regularly held in the White Palace.

Rock Demerdzhi - a valley of stone "ghosts"





Yalta offers excursions to Mount Demerdzhi, where there are unique blocks of stone and massive piles. The area resembles the planet Mars or the kingdom of stone hills of an unusual shape. Frightening and at the same time bewitching beauty attracts hundreds of prying eyes.

There is practically no vegetation here, from the top a fantastic view of the Valley of Ghosts opens - bizarre stone sculptures. Here a huge gigantic mushroom "grows" and a 25-meter pillar rises, on which the stone bird Phoenix sits . From the "damn fingers" sticking out of their earth, goose bumps run around. Tall pyramidal towers calmly rise ...

Famous parks of Yalta





So, tourists visited the most popular places ... What to see in Yalta yet? This is the main question that always interests tourists. Natural and architectural parks deserve special attention. These include the Massandra Palace Park, located 3 km from the resort center. It preserved paintings by famous artists of the twentieth century. The whole territory is surrounded by marvelous plants, colorful violet flower beds and streams.

Seaside park





The ancient and beautiful park of the peninsula. Before the war, this place was an unsightly wasteland without a single blade of grass, but now the whole territory is a real work of art with an area of ​​more than two kilometers. From there comes the fragrance of fabulously beautiful roses and flowering shrubs.

The area is ennobled by ornamental trees and rare plants that are more than two hundred years old. This place is popular with local citizens.

Couples in love are often met here, because the romantic atmosphere is in the park. Around there are many children playing on green alleys. The park has a pebble beach, monuments to the great writers Gorky and Chekhov are erected, a spa hall is opened, where dances are held in the evenings and songs are played. Often concerts are organized with the participation of foreign celebrities.

Sacred sights





Admirers of holy places often wonder what to see in Yalta. On the Crimean peninsula there are a lot of ancient temples and cathedrals of various faiths, attracting with its magnificent architecture. The most popular is the Alexander Nevsky Cathedral of the 19th century. It is decorated in the national Old Russian style, its facade is framed by pilasters and portals. Not far from the temple is an artificially created grotto.

The second, no less famous and luxurious is the Orthodox Cathedral of John Chrysostom. It stands on a mountain hill and is a kind of lighthouse for maritime transport. Its gilded dome is visible from the Quay itself.

In the heart of the city you can admire the Roman Catholic Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary. Religious meetings, services and concert programs of organ music are regularly organized there.

Another masterpiece of sacred architecture is the Armenian church of St. Hripsime. Its peculiarity is that many steps lead to the entrance, which must be overcome in order to enter inside.

For parents with children





Couples with children are always interested in whether Yalta is suitable for family leisure? Where to go, what to see with the kids? People of different age categories will like the “Fairy Tale” zoo at the foot of the mountain archipelagos - an amazing oasis of wildlife with unique inhabitants.

A fascinating and memorable trip will be a trip to the Dolphin show. Here you will see artistic mammals that you can touch, feed, photograph and swim with.

Children will get tremendous pleasure from the Blue Bay Water Park in the village of Simeiz. It offers a variety of water rides, slides, trampolines, pools and all kinds of entertainment. We advise you to visit the crocodile aquarium, a farm inhabited by reptiles, monitor lizards, huge turtles, and pythons.

Visit to fairy-tale characters

Take the kids to a real museum called Glade of Fairy Tales. The only entertaining and informative exposition in the open. Children will be delighted with the colorful cartoon characters gathered in a crowd in one territory. The museum has more than two hundred heroes: Mowgli, Snow White, characters from the fairy tale "Twelve Months" and others. Even adults will again feel like children in this fabulous town.

All these attractions are just a fraction. Come and explore the amazing surroundings!
