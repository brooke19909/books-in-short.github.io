Kommunarka - firing range in Moscow

In the Commune near Moscow there was a summer residence of Heinrich Yagoda, who for a long time served as chairman of the OGPU and the people's commissar of the NKVD. After that, this place became a special facility where people who were subjected to repression were shot and buried. Kommunarka - a firing range that was not previously available, but is now open to visitors. Now there is a cemetery with a small memorial complex and a man‚Äôs monastery with vigilant prayers for the souls of all those who died in the dungeons. Lists of facility workers are declassified gradually, and lists of victims have already been published. And not all were rehabilitated after the death of Stalin.

All the shooting places and burial places of political prisoners were strictly guarded, and only the state security itself knew about them. Even not all employees of such facilities were aware of what they were guarding, only the curators of firing special facilities knew something, but their duties were limited to ensuring the safety of territories and preventing strangers from entering the facility. Disclosure of secrets

Now the secret cover is gradually removed, archives are opened, the testimonies of local residents are recorded (although what can they know?). Historians study the hit lists that the FSB declassifies. The burial sites themselves are also examined: pits were counted, measured, traces of bullets in the trees were found. All this work began only in the nineties of the last century. The Moscow government has subsidized memorial projects at Kommunarka and at the Butovo training ground. However, the projects were not implemented, since the whole territory since 1999 has been under the jurisdiction of the Moscow Patriarchate.

Relatives

Stalin's death lists contain 44.5 thousand names, of which 38 thousand were shot. A total of 383 lists were preserved relative to the period from February 1937 to October 1938, where in addition to the Stalinist one there are signatures of Molotov, Voroshilov, Kaganovich, Zhdanov, Yezhov and Kosior.

Most of the people on the lists were not subjected to trial or investigation before being shot. These are the NKVD employees and relatives of these employees. Country house

The one-story wooden house of the People's Commissar Berries was built in 1928. The left half consisted of six or seven bright and spacious rooms, while the right half contained utility rooms and service rooms. The hosts did not come here to relax in a hammock or in beds. This cottage was primarily a residence for meetings and secret work, and in the second - for the celebration of all kinds of anniversaries and solemn dates.

Almost all those who remained forever in the land of Kommunarka first visited this cottage as welcome guests. It is not in vain said that the revolution devours all its children. When the guests arrived, the service staff was kept in the back room under the castle. The cooks and other staff of the cottage owners brought with them until they opened the Kommunarka firing range. The date of occurrence is considered from the time of the first mass execution - September 2, 1937. And previously, Berry himself was arrested - in March.
