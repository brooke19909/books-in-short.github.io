Philosophy





What sciences study society and man? One of them is philosophy, which considers the universal laws of the development of the world as a whole. There are other definitions. So, philosophy is called a special form of knowledge of the world that studies the most common properties and phenomena of surrounding reality. Modern scholars do not like to call philosophy a science, since it often contains completely contradictory provisions that researchers do not even try to reconcile or find out which one is correct. Just as in physics they try to reconcile the general theory of relativity with quantum field theory with one degree or another degree of success.

But in the framework of philosophy, atheistic materialism and agnostic idealism can simultaneously exist. That is, philosophy can be called the answer to the question "what sciences study society" only conditionally. This form of knowledge of the world poses such questions.

Sociology





What sciences study society, man, social relations and institutions? Correctly related to sociology discipline. They include not only the science considered in this subsection, but also, for example, social work. But sociology is the science of society, social institutions (historical forms of its self-regulation), which aims at explaining and predicting certain social phenomena.

Social Psychology

This science is similar to sociology, but is distinguished by another subject of study - people in the framework of a particular society. And the analysis of social phenomena is done at more detailed levels - personal and interpersonal. Thus, the study of social psychology includes the analysis of interactions between people, as well as concepts such as leadership, conformism, nonconformism, and a number of others.

Law





One of the aspects of studying most of the socionomic sciences (studying society) is the system of social norms. They are religious, moral, group. And there is a special category of them - legal norms, which are a means of expressing the will of the state. Actually, jurisprudence - this is the science that studies the legal norms, the features of their functioning in relation to a particular state or as a whole. The closest connection is this discipline with social psychology, social work and sociology.

Economy





Economics is a science that studies the economic activities of society, relations related to money and property, production, distribution, exchange and consumption. This discipline is a mechanism that regulates the material side of life of each member of society.

Political science

Political science is the science of a special form of people's activities related to power relations, as well as possible political systems, institutions and norms. This science also studies the relationship between the state and its individual citizens.
