The magnificent resort city, located on the Crimean peninsula, has long been famous for its mild climate and healing air. Mountains surround the coast of Yalta: in the northeast, Nikitskaya Yaila, and in the north and northwest, Ai-Petrinskaya Yaila. This natural barrier protects the city from cold winds.

Yalta has a subtropical climate. The average annual temperature is +13 ¬∞ C. In summer, the air warms up to +24 ¬∞ C, in winter the temperature ranges from +3 to -4 ¬∞ C. Therefore, Yalta is a year-round resort.





Off the coast of the city, the water temperature in the summer months is +25 ¬∞ C. The beach season begins in June and lasts until the end of September. Snow in Yalta is a rather rare occurrence. Even when it falls out, it melts right away. Meanwhile, there is a lot of snow on the tops, so in winter you can climb the mountain lifts and go skiing.
