Where is Malta, there is a cloudless vacation

Where Malta is located is not known unless a person who has not attended school. But this is a small archipelago, far from all are aware. In fact, the state in the Mediterranean Sea, which is located almost a hundred kilometers south of Sicily and 300 kilometers north of the coast of Libya, consists of the islands of Malta (the largest in the archipelago), Comino, Cominotto, Gozo and Filfa. The latter is still uninhabited, although it is located only five kilometers from the main island. All land areas are low. The maximum height - Ta'Dmerjrek Hill - is only 253 meters above sea level.





Knowing where Malta is located, it is easy to guess that the climate throughout the archipelago is Mediterranean. This means that even in January the temperature there is above zero, often lingering at around + 12 ° C. And in summer it’s really hot there: + 30 ° . Surprisingly, there are no rivers or lakes on the islands. At the same time, 400 thousand people live on tiny shreds of land, the total area of ​​which is 316 sq. Km (and even more in the summer with tourists). Thus, Malta is one of the most densely populated countries in Europe.

Now that we have specified where Malta is located, consider the size of the island itself. The largest in the entire archipelago, it is only 27 km long and 15 km wide. Gozo is three times smaller than Malta, but its ecology is more successful. Here, high cliffs and secluded bays are a great place for diving enthusiasts. Also, connoisseurs of calm measured relaxation and delicious food come here. The tiny islet of Comino attracts both tourists with children (as there are sandy beaches and sheltered from the waves of the harbor), and divers. By the purity of coastal waters, the Blue Lagoon and Mellieha Bay are most famous for all the islands of Malta .





Despite the fact that most of the beaches throughout the archipelago are pebble, and some of them are also inaccessible, in the last 10 years, it was marine recreation in Malta that has developed . Reviews of tourists talk about the great opportunities of diving. What kind of diving in the Mediterranean? - you ask. - Watch the mackerel or the bulls? In fact, the island nation is famous for its scuba diving schools. There you can take the PADI or CMAS exam and get a certificate. In addition, do not forget that people sailed in the Mediterranean Sea since the invention of shipping. So those who dare to plunge into the depths of the sea, has the opportunity to look at her "trophies".





On the main island of the archipelago, in addition to natural beauties, you can meet a lot of historical attractions. Sightseeing tours to Malta are very popular among Russian tourists. After all, these lands were developed during the Neolithic. The Phoenicians ruled here, for a long time the monks knights of the Order of St. John ruled. The Ioannites had the main commander there, and the Grand Master of the Order, Jean de la Valetta, founded and fortified the main city named after him from the Osman raids. And the monastic order itself is better known as the Maltese Order (now it is not engaged in wars, but exclusively in charity).

Excursions mainly include visits to Fort Valletta, St. John's Cathedral with the tombs of the Masters, a museum of weapons. The most curious tourists will need to call in Aura and Bugiba - according to the Acts, in the local bay, the St. Apostle Paul who crashed on the sea sailed to the sea . But neighboring Gozo boasts that the nymph Calypso lived here - the cave where the fabulous beauty lived was preserved here.
