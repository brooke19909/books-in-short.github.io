Market policy is the same as for all large banks with foreign capital

But you can notice the trend and market conditions on which all banks that have foreign capital offer cooperation. Since they are financed in foreign currency, they do not need high cash requirements from the USA or the European Union. This is reflected in low deposit rates in dollars or euros (about 1-3%).

Meanwhile, relatively low rates can be observed. Why? There are at least two reasons for this.





The first is the total cash flow. Since such banks are represented in many large regions by a wide and developed network consisting of branches, offices, ATMs, they can afford to accumulate a large cash flow from all over the country.

This allows us not to be focused on a certain category of clients or on the principles of a regional economy, depending on the infrastructure and industrial development of the area in which the bank is present.

It makes no sense to attract new customers by changing rates - image plays a role

Due to this, you can collect money from everywhere and from everyone. Thus, the amount of deposits is growing quite simply. After all, somewhere there will always be people who do not want to make money on their contribution, but simply want to save money in a safe place.

The second is the image of a bank that belongs to a foreign financial group with a high image. Of course, people trust these institutions more and are ready to give them money, considering them a reliable place to store their savings.

After all, who do you want to give your money to - a bank that exists for one week or a large financial group that has worked for a large number of years and is represented in a certain number of foreign countries?





After all, foreign capital is protected from the political situation or the problems of the economy of one country, since in case of need, financial resources can be easily transferred from outside.

All this contributes to the fact that large banks, which are part of an international financial group, have no need to conquer the market by increasing the proposed deposit rates.
