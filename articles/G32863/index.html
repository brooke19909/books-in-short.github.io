The cult skyscraper Empire State Buildings and its history

Skyscrapers - this is what distinguishes New York from other cities. The most attractive metropolis for tourists has a business card by which everyone recognizes it.

The tallest building of the city has become the main symbol of the "capital of the world", testifying to the unbending American spirit.

The construction of the first skyscrapers

1889 marked the first construction of a skyscraper in New York. For forty years, record high buildings appeared in the city, but on May 1, 1931 the grand opening of Empire State Buildings took place, which became a real phenomenon in the architectural world. A unique building, which had no equal, opened its doors for all visitors.



For many years, not a single building in the world could beat the achievement of American builders.

The legend that gave the city and skyscraper its name

An interesting story is known, according to which the English navigator Henry Hudson, during his journey, sailed along the river, which later received his name. Construction History

The first skyscraper in the world, with 102 floors 443 meters high, was erected in just one year. It was originally planned to make it a place for mooring airships, but later this beautiful idea was abandoned due to strong air currents.



The history of the skyscraper is closely linked to the economic recovery of the 20s of the last century, which gave rise to a real boom in construction. The sharp increase in the price of land has led to the construction of multi-storey buildings built at a high technological level.

Observation Decks

On the 86th and last, 102nd floors, there are observation platforms, and to get to them, tourists stand for several hours. The ticket price for their visit starts at twenty dollars.

In difficult economic times, suicides came here, and sad statistics tell of 40 deaths.

At the very top there is a spire visible at the entrance to New York, on which special television and radio equipment is installed, and about seven million residents of the metropolis receive a signal from it.

Advanced lighting system

The legendary Empire State Building (New York) is incredibly beautiful after dark. Illuminated by an entire system of 400 fixtures, it captivates with a magnificent view. By the way, the colors are known in advance, most often they are confined to any cultural events and city holidays.



Until 2012, spotlights could create a palette of only nine shades. But after the introduction of a new system of dynamic lighting, which reproduces over sixteen million colors, even pastel, a variety of "live" effects will surprise the most demanding travelers. The skyscraper sparkling with bright lights will present a catchy spectacle, so no tourist goes past the main attractions of the metropolis.

Where is Empire State Buildings located?

New York's business card is located at the intersection of Fifth Avenue and 34th Street in Midtown Manhattan (Midtown Manhattan).

Nearest underground station to the high-rise: 34th Street - Herald Square.
