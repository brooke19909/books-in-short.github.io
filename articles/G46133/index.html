About the Stavropol Territory





From 1768 to 1774, one of the Russo-Turkish wars lasted, the final stage of which was the signing of the Kuchuk-Kainardzhinsky world. This meant that the new border should begin from the mouth of the Terek River, flow through Mozdok, and further north-west, to the fortress of St. Dmitry. Today it is a well-known Rostov-on-Don. Thus, it became necessary to close the territory on the southern side of Russia. The task of creating powerful outposts to protect the state was entrusted to General Potemkin. He wrote a report to Catherine II on the need to build 10 fortresses along the entire distance from Azov to Mozdok. Interesting Facts





You can talk about the cities of the Stavropol Territory for a long time, both about the regional center and the resorts of the region. There are a lot of attractions and lots of beautiful places. For example, in Stavropol is located Victory Park - the first in the country and one of the largest at the moment in Russia. Its area is more than 200 hectares.

Karl Marx Avenue in the regional center is now the oldest city boulevard. It began to be planted with trees back in 1839, and 10 years later it was finished. On the same street you can find the first stone house (now it is assigned number 71). Then, in 1799, the building belonged to the merchant Volkov.
