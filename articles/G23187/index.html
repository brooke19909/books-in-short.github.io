Lithuania is a country in Northern Europe. Description, climate, features

Lithuania (Lithuania) is a country located on the continent of Eurasia, in the northern part of Europe. It is one of the three states of the Baltic states and borders with the Kaliningrad region of the Russian Federation, Latvia, Poland and Belarus. In the west it is washed by the Baltic Sea. The capital of the state is Vilnius.





Short description

Lithuania (Lithuania) is the largest country among the Baltic states. Its area is more than 65 thousand square meters. km By the size of the territory, it occupies the 123rd place in the world. Three main cities can be named: Vilnius (the capital), Kaunas (the temporary capital and the second largest city in the state) and Klaipeda (the largest port).

In administrative-territorial terms, the country is divided into 10 counties with local self-government. Railroads of the USSR format were laid here (gauge - 1,520 mm), there are 4 airports and a seaport. Public transport runs in cities, including trolleybuses.

According to the state system, Lithuania is a country that is officially called the Republic of Lithuania and is parliamentary in form of government. The head of the named state is the president, elected by popular vote for 5 years. The parliament - the Diet, consists of 141 officials. Lithuania is a member of the UN, the European Union and NATO, and it is also included in the Schengen zone. Since 2015, the national currency of the country has become the euro.



Historical information

The current territory of Lithuania was inhabited as far back as the 10th century BC. e. Since ancient times, people who lived on these lands were engaged in cattle breeding, fishing and farming. During the Neolithic period Indo-European tribes came to these places - the Balts.

As a state, Lithuania was born in the 13th century. The formation of statehood occurred during the existence of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania. Cities and population began to grow, significantly expanding the territory of the principality.

In the middle of the XVI century, Lithuania, having united with Poland, forms the largest state of Eastern Europe - the Commonwealth. After World War I, Lithuania was an independent kingdom for several years, and before the start of World War II, it became part of the USSR, in which it remained until its collapse.

Climate

The climate in Lithuania is temperate continental, in the coastal region - marine. Average January temperatures are from -1 to -3 ¬∞ , July - from +17 to + 19 ¬∞ . There are no sharp fluctuations in this area. The average annual rainfall is from 500 to 700 mm. Very often, at any time of the year, Lithuania has a drizzling rain. Such days are accompanied by fogs.

Climate formation is greatly influenced by air masses from the Atlantic and the Baltic Sea. A full warm summer lasts two months - July and August. This period is favorable for rest and recovery with the help of sea air.





Population

Lithuania (Lithuania) is a country where about 3 million people live, 550 thousand of them in the capital. By ethnic composition, 85% are Lithuanians, 6.5% are Poles, 6% are Russians, as well as Belarusians, Ukrainians, Jews, and others. According to religious affiliation, about 70% of the country's inhabitants are Catholics, only 5% are Orthodox, the rest consider themselves atheists.

The official language of the country is Lithuanian. It is native to the vast majority of residents, but many citizens understand and speak Russian well.

Culture

Lithuanians are very proud of their culture, traditions and identity. They managed to maintain a strong connection with the past, and this can be seen in the sights of the country. The history of the state dates back several centuries, during this time a large number of churches, temples, castles, monasteries and other architectural monuments were erected in Lithuania.





The most popular tourist sites are: St. Anne's Church, Gediminas' Tower, Kaunas Castle, Artillery Bastion. Among the natural attractions, one can distinguish: Aukstaitija National Park, Curonian Spit, Kaunas Botanical Garden.
