The most important biological property

Irritability is in biology a property of tissues that can perceive internal or external interference and respond to it by going into an excited state. Such tissues are called excitable and have a certain number of characteristic qualities. These include the following:

1. Irritability. This is when cells, tissues and organs are capable of responding with the intervention of certain stimuli - both external and internal.

2. Excitability. This is the quality of animal or plant cells, in which it becomes possible to change the resting state to the state of physiological activity of the body.

3. Conductivity. This is the ability to spread excitatory reactions. It depends on the structure of the tissue and its functional features.
