How to disassemble the Nokia N8: step-by-step instructions

Before proceeding, it is necessary to inspect the purchased case or part that needs to be replaced. The fact is that there is a high probability that you have a non-original phone, and the case you bought, on the contrary, meets the generally accepted standard. However, there are a lot of unfavorable options, so before you take apart the device, carefully consider the device that will undergo the process of ‚Äúrebirth‚Äù. If you are convinced that everything is in order, perhaps you can start.

Step # 1: end cap mounting screws





The Nokia N8 phone has protective covers at the top and bottom. The lower part of the panel is fixed with two side screws located near the edge of the base. The top cover is attached with a single bolt, which is hidden under the HDMI plug. Turn them off.

Attention: the screws have a different cross section and texture. Therefore, before dismantling them, be kind, draw a schematic arrangement of the bolts to be removed on a piece of paper - thereby you will protect yourself from the situation of "breaking your head, what and where from."





Step number 2: remove the covers and remove the battery

The lower end protection element (casing) is quite easy to remove. Flip the special retainer and remove the device‚Äôs battery . The Nokia N8 phone, the photo of which you see here, is not particularly pliable when it comes to separating the top cover from the main body. Because this part is an antenna unit. The cover fits snugly onto the phone's motherboard that peeks out from underneath. To disassemble without risking damage, you need tape. Fasten the edge of the tape (adhesive base down) on the front of the cover. Then, leaving 5-7 cm free, do the same with the back wall of the part to be removed. Then you can safely pull the makeshift tail of the tape. The part will easily separate.

Important : in the process of removing the antenna module, the HDMI plug must be removed from the system connector of the phone.

Step # 3: removing the touch panel and screen

Please note (the phone is facing down) at the edges of the panel at the top and bottom are a pair of fastening bolts. As you know, they need to be unscrewed. The next step will be the flip of the smartphone and the separation of the display part of the device, as well as the touchscreen from the case base.

  1. Gently pry the upper edge of the sensor module and gently lift it to a favorable moment - a good grip of the dismantled part with your free hand.
  2. At the bottom of the phone are the connecting pads of the cables (unless, of course, your Nokia N8 phone is the original). Just separate the connectors connected to the board.

Step number 4: the final part of the showdown





After you can successfully remove the ‚Äúfriendly tandem‚Äù - the touchscreen and the screen, you should unscrew the two screws located in the central part of the case (again - along the edges) and one screw in close proximity to the front camera. Only after the above steps can you begin to dismantle the internal components of the smartphone.

  1. Pry off the steel plate with a pick or a credit card prepared for this purpose.
  2. Carefully remove the polyphonic speaker and flash module.

The disassembly process can be considered complete.
