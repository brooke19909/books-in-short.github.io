Indicators for scalping without redrawing: features, advantages and disadvantages

In trading in the financial markets, there are various techniques by which traders earn money. Each trading system has its own characteristics and characteristics, and most of them use special tools.

Traders conclude various transactions by duration, someone opens orders with a long-term perspective, and some use short-term transactions. A fairly large number of speculators apply Scalping techniques in trading. In this article, the reader will learn about various types of trading tools, including indicators for Scalping without repainting.

Definition of Scalping Trading Technique

Scalping is a short-term transaction. Traders compare this term with Native American customs when the winners took scalps from the enemy. Transactions opened by Scalping trading systems have the following features:

  1. Timeframe M1-M15, less often M30.
  2. Profit - only a few points, an average of 3-10.
  3. A very short time period of several minutes, sometimes even seconds, up to 1 hour.
  4. A large number of transactions in one trading day.

If a trader is trading in the medium or long term, then he necessarily carries out an analysis of market movement and quite often uses fundamental analytics. In trading strategies, Scalping speculators are only interested in the technical form of analysis, since they expect to make a profit in the shortest possible time and close the deal. By and large, it does not matter to them where the market will move in the future, their methodology is based on the principle of "here and now."

Types of Indicators

According to their characteristics and functions, all technical indicators are divided into several categories.

Varieties of indicators:

  1. For trending movements.
  2. For trading in a calm market or during a flat.
  3. For areas of uncertainty (consolidation, redistribution).

And also they are divided depending on the duration of the positions:

  1. For short-term deals and scalping.
  2. For long term positions.
  3. For transactions with medium maturities.

A very important role here is played by the time period during which the trader trades. The fact is that one and the same indicator, but on different timeframes, can give completely opposite results.

It is also important to understand that if the indicator parameters are calculated for trend movement, then this tool should in no case be used in a calm market. Any technical tool has its own settings, features and characteristics, and if, say, a trader uses trend indicators in trading, then they will give false signals during a flat. As a result, instead of the expected profit, the speculator will receive losses.

At this time, market movement is weak, volatility is significantly reduced, and trading takes place in any range.

But at the same time, flat trading allows traders, in particular scalpers, to make good money. The fact is that just at this time the market movement is the most predictable, and it lends itself perfectly to an analytical forecast.
