Choosing an inexpensive phone with a good camera

Smartphones can be classified by the number of image sensors, matrix production technology, the number and size of pixels, the lens aperture, the presence of optical stabilization, etc.

When evaluating smartphone specifications, one should not forget about the following:

  • The number of megapixels of the image sensor is not as important as the diameter of the pupil of the lens. Optics with a wide relative aperture (the ratio of the focal length to the effective aperture; a smaller number means a larger lens width) transmits more light, improving the quality of photographs in low light.
  • Pixel sizes and working in low light are important, but high specifications rarely mean good camera performance in real conditions.
  • The fashionable optical image stabilizer, although it should improve the quality of photographs in low light, does not always do this, since the photo processing software must interact with it properly.
  • Not all dual cameras are the same. Some smartphones (for example, iPhone X) use them to get a bokeh effect. Others (such as the LG V30) allow you to switch between wide-angle and standard lenses.
  • Is portrait mode necessary? It can have various names. In ‚ÄúiPhone‚Äù it is ‚ÄúPortrait mode‚Äù, and in Note 8 - Live Photos, but other phones also allow you to achieve a bokeh effect when the subject is in focus and the background is artistically blurred. This function is mainly supported by smartphones with two image sensors, but inexpensive mobile phones with a good camera can achieve the same result by software. Some models provide the ability to change the blurriness of the background of an already finished photograph.
  • Specifications of selfie cameras are equally important. As the number of self-portrait enthusiasts grows steadily, one should not lose sight of inexpensive phones with a good front camera. To understand how the smartphone will shoot in low light, in addition to resolution, you need to check the aperture. Some front cameras (for example, in Pixel 2) are capable of producing the same effects as dual main ones.
  • Keep in mind the video. An inexpensive phone with an excellent camera can capture more than still images. You need to know with what resolution and frame rate capture is performed. For example, the LG V30 is capable of recording UHD video at a speed of 30 fps, and the iPhone 8 with twice the frame rate. It is also worth looking for a camera with filters and a time lapse function.
