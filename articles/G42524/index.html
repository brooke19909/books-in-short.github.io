The battleship "Potemkin" - a ship of revolution

The battleship "Potemkin" was launched in September 1900 from the stocks of Nikolaev. At that time, he was considered the most powerful in the Black Sea Fleet. The creation of this ship became a landmark for the transition from outdated technical solutions to more modern ones.



The project was developed and built by engineer E. Schott, a student of the famous shipbuilder N. E. Kuteinikov.

In the fall of 1905, an armed rebellion was planned in the Navy , which was to become decisive for general performance. However, the battleship "Potemkin", on which an uprising broke out months earlier, was ahead of the planned events. The response to the repression was the disarmament of officer sailors and gunfire. The ship's commander, as well as several senior officer ranks, were killed. The rest are taken into custody.

At the same time, Vakulenchuk, who was initially against the uprising on the battleship Potemkin-Tauride, broke out separately from the general movement, nevertheless took command of the ship.
