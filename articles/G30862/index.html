Egyptian Seas: Red and Mediterranean

Egypt is an independent Arab Republic and is located in northeast Africa and approximately 6% of the Sinai Peninsula. It is washed by two seas: the north of the country is the Mediterranean, which the Egyptians call the White, and the east is washed by the Red. The seas of Egypt and their coasts have a completely different climate, depending on seasonal winds. In the Red Sea, tourists can afford to swim all year, and in the Mediterranean season it is open from May to the end of October. Therefore, we can say that the seas of Egypt are equally beautiful in absolutely all resort towns.



Red sea

The Red Sea is unusual in that it does not have flowing rivers. Experts say that this can explain the crystal clear sea waters and their temperature regime, which is very comfortable for relaxation. Due to deep temperatures and increased salinity, the water in it is the warmest and most saline in the oceans, and it also has incredible underwater fauna and flora. For lovers of underwater entertainment and diving, this place seems like a paradise.

Tourists are attracted to Egypt, the Red Sea, relaxing on its beaches not only with the hot sun and pleasant sea water, but also with famous coral reefs with a rich palette of the underwater world, in which you can see fancy plants, many beautiful species of fish and various marine inhabitants. By the way, corals love only clean and salty water, so we can say that the places here are environmentally friendly. The seas of Egypt seemed to be created for snorkeling and scuba diving. The most prominent coastal cities are Hurghada and Sharm El Sheikh. It was in Hurghada that the first hotels of the highest international class began to appear. It has beautiful sandy beaches with a gradual, gentle entry into the water, which is perfect for vacationers with kids. And Sharm el-Sheikh is the country's most popular Europeanized resort with an incredible number of bays, bays and excellent hotel service. The beaches in the city are represented by a long promenade with a variety of cafes, restaurants, shops and nightclubs. The resort is not afraid of the wind due to the indented coastline of the bays and the protection of the mountains.



Mediterranean Sea

If we talk about the Mediterranean Sea, then it became famous for its calm disposition and beauty, though in some seasons the sea ​​water becomes seething and huge waves crashes ashore. For a long time Egypt (especially the Mediterranean Sea) has been attractive to tourists for its favorable climate, characterized by hot, long summers and short, humid winters. The entire coast is strewn with luxury hotels and hotel complexes, as well as small private villas. There are also multi-storey buildings, from which the most vivid view opens, especially during sunset and sunrise. The most popular Mediterranean resorts are Mersa Matruh and Alexandria. Their sandy beaches stretch for tens of kilometers, and in the cities you can see ancient sights. Impressions will be unforgettable from boating, swimming under water and swimming in warm sea water. The seas of Egypt are full of numerous artifacts and underwater historical sights that have sunk centuries ago.
