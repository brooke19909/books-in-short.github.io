About Schaub Lorenz

Technically, the company appeared in 1889. But then it was simply called Lorenz. By the name of its creator. Receivers, communication devices and other things were produced. In the prewar years, the company moved from one syndicate to another. After World War II, the lion's share of production capacity remained in the territories of the German Democratic Republic occupied by Soviet power. The company had to re-establish production. In 1988, the company was bought by the Finnish Nokia. From this moment, the journey through various trading groups begins again.

This is the main story of Schaub Lorenz. What kind of company, which, in fact, could never boast of independence? All is not quite so. In 2007, the company was freed from the yoke of other manufacturers and began its own production. However, the first model of built-in household appliances was released in 2015. The company's production facilities are located in Turkey. However, all parts are brought from Germany. Everything fell into place. However, the time has come to take a closer look at the Schaub Lorenz washing machine. We will review reviews about it a little later. Key Specifications

The Schaub Lorenz washing machine, reviews of which we will analyze a little later, can take on board 6 kilograms of dirty laundry. The maximum spin speed is 1000 rpm. Not so much. But the machine almost does not vibrate, which is important for built-in appliances. There is an option to select the spin speed (suitable for delicate fabrics). Also, the spin can be completely canceled. The machine is completely protected from leaks and has a unique foot structure that dampens vibration almost completely. There is also an option to select the washing temperature and the ability to wash the shirts. We continue to consider Schaub Lorenz washing machines. Wash Modes and Advanced Options

What washing modes can please this consumer machine? There are special modes for bedding, jeans, delicate fabrics, wool and cotton, downy outerwear, mixed fabrics, just outerwear and other fabrics. Noise level and power consumption

So, does the machine make a lot of noise during washing? Not really. In rinse mode, the noise level does not exceed 58 dB. And if the machine is built-in, then even less. In fact, it is the quietest of all modern washing machines of the same class. During the spin cycle, the noise level increases to 77 dB. But it can not be called critical. In general, the car runs quite quietly in any mode. And now about energy consumption. This washing machine belongs to the energy efficiency class A ++. This means that at a very high specific power, it consumes ridiculously little electricity. An ordinary microwave consumes many times more.
