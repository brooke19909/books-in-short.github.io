Dominican Republic - where is it located?

The Dominican Republic and the Republic of Haiti territorially divide the island into two parts. The Antilles are located at the crossroads of seafaring from South America to the North, in the west of the Atlantic Ocean. Not far from the island is Central America.

On the island of Hispaniola, two metropolitan cities of two states coexist - Haiti and Dominican Republic. The strait, called Mona, separates Hispaniola from Puerto Rico in the east, and in the west the Windward Strait lies between the Dominican Republic and Cuba.

To understand where the Dominican Republic is located in, one should turn to history. The island of Haiti received its name from Christopher Columbus, who discovered it in 1492 and named it Hispaniola. Since then, the era of the conquest of America originates, and at the beginning of the 16th century the island was colonized by the Spaniards.

In addition to the central territory - the islands of Haiti, the Dominican Republic also owns a group of small islands. The most significant of them:

  • Catalina - off the southern coast of the island of Haiti;
  • Saona - from its southeastern part;
  • Cayo Levantado - near the north-eastern part of the island;
  • Beata is on the southwestern side of Haiti.

Each of these islands has a completely unique nature, for which tourists who are looking for places with unique exotic are especially appreciated.

The Dominican Republic is considered a mountainous state, and it contains the Cordillera-Central ridge, some of whose peaks have a height above sea level of more than 3 kilometers. History of the Dominican Republic

In addition to Spanish colonization, Haiti has long attracted other European invader countries. So, France after the bloody wars conquered the western part of the island, leaving Spain eastern.

Only at the beginning of the 19th century did the colonial milestone in the history of the Dominican Republic end. This happened after a major uprising erupted on the island, the result of which was the expulsion of the European colonialists. From then until today there has been a long struggle of the republic for independence from the United States of America. Knowing the historical milestones of Haiti, you can understand where and in which country the Dominican Republic is located.

Economy of the Dominican Republic

Dominican Republic is not a very wealthy state. According to the data provided for 2009, the poverty level came close to 50%, and GDP amounted to $ 8,000 per year.

Tourism and services are the main sources of income in the state. They occupy about 68% of total GDP. In second place is industry. Among other things, the Dominican Republic produces ferronickel, gold and sugar. Completes the list of agricultural income items. It accounts for 11% of all revenues.

The country has many state programs aimed at maintaining and developing tourism, so this industry is extremely important for the Dominican Republic. Despite the high prices, Dominican vacations remain one of the most amazing and tempting.

What is the currency in the Dominican Republic?

The official circulation in the Dominican Republic has only the Dominican peso (DOP - international abbreviation). However, in many small cities and settlements where there are no banks or exchange offices, it is better to have funds on bank cards so that you can pay.

All, even small stores, accept Master Card, American Express, and Visa cards. Of course, in the large cities of the Dominican Republic, where banks and money exchange points are located, everything is simpler and there are no problems either with paying for goods or with exchanging money.

$ 1 is approximately equal to 16 pesos. In local institutions, it is customary to pay either in dollars (which is not entirely legal) or with credit cards.

Language of the Dominican Republic

They speak in the Dominican Republic in Spanish. It is also the only state language. There are a lot of tourists and emigrants in the country. Therefore, the second place is occupied by English. A small group of people from Haiti speak the Creole dialect.

Religion of the Dominican Republic

In the Republic of Dominican Republic 95% of Christians, of which 88% are Catholics. The rest of the population are Protestants. This is due to the long Spanish colonization of the country.

In border villages, voodoo traditions are not uncommon. The Dominican Republic does not officially recognize this religion, but markets are littered with goods with voodoo symbols, which are very popular with tourists.
