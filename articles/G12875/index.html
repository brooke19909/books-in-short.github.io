Embankment of Feodosia: description, buildings, monuments. The beaches of Feodosia

Feodosia is one of the largest resort cities located on the Crimean Black Sea coast. This place attracts tourists with its unique history, cozy streets, an abundance of beaches, fortresses and monuments. Also, it has become almost the center of the entire peninsula thanks to convenient road and rail interchanges. The culture of the city, its sights are of interest to many, but most of all among the townspeople and guests, the embankment of Feodosia is in demand.

What to see on the promenade

Choosing places for walking in the ancient city, local residents prefer the long-time-loved picturesque corners, which are also advised to visit and tourists. But before you plunge into the acquaintance with ancient history, going to study the coastal streets, it is important to remember that there is the Black Sea Embankment and Desantnikov Embankment (Primorskaya) in Feodosia, so you should carefully study their location so as not to get lost.





So, the center of Feodosia is considered to be the Seafront, and in the midst of the season it becomes the most lively part of the city. She is visited in order to admire the amazing sea views, look at the trading shops with souvenirs, sit in cafes and restaurants. Even in the driest weather, the embankment of Feodosia in Crimea is constantly drowning in greenery, as the authorities try to water plants daily, despite the lack of water. Many vacationers will be pleased to sit on benches located under the sprawling branches of trees and take a break from the hot sun. Visitors will certainly be amazed by the abundance of flower beds, entertainment venues and other places.

It is noteworthy that the embankment can be viewed directly from the train car, since it contains part of the railway track. The building of the railway station, built in 1892, at the time of the opening of the Dzhankoy - Feodosiya line, may turn out to be an interesting object. In addition, the embankment of Feodosia is a place with stunning views of the bay. A sophisticated tourist will certainly appreciate the multiple scenery for interesting photographs.

It is worth noting that it is on the promenade that fairs and holidays such as City Day, Maslenitsa, Christmas, Victory Day, etc. are held. There is also a pier near it, from where pleasure boats, yachts and sightseeing boats depart.

Historic sites on the territory of Primorskaya embankment

Admiring the Theodosian Gulf, one can not but notice the Genoese fortress, built in the 14th century with the aim of defending the city, which was previously called Kafa. Its remains are a historical and architectural reserve and allow you to find out what were the fortifications and what was located in the citadels.
