Construction and the first owners

Curious to know when the Swallow's Nest castle was erected? Crimean land has always attracted a large number of people. Rich and noble people sought to buy a plot here at a bargain price. There was nothing surprising in this. Indeed, the wonderful mountain air, the endless sea, beautiful nature had a beneficial effect on the human body and well-being. Here they built estates, built parks.

One of the rocks especially attracted attention. They came here to meet the dawn of the sun. Once, one general (his name, unfortunately is unknown) came up with the idea to build a summer house here. He called it the "Castle of Love." Perhaps in honor of the memory of his beloved or dreams of her. This remains a mystery. Every day the general watched the construction of his castle and admired it. Although it was only one-story and looked more like a summer house. After the death of the general, the building was sold to Albert Tobin.





Other owners

The next owner of the "Swallow's Nest" was a Moscow merchant - Anna Rakhmanova. She decided to demolish the wooden buildings and erect a new stone building. But, she soon sold the house to the German baron von Stengel. The new owner decided to completely remodel the house.

A small castle was built on the Aurora rock, which surprisingly resembled a knight's. But, only in miniature execution. The building was simply magnificent, however, the baron did not admire it for long. The first world war began and he was forced to leave for Germany. Before leaving, he sold the Swallow‚Äôs Nest to the wealthy merchant Pavel Shelaputin, who opened a restaurant here. But there was a war, and there were few people who wanted to have fun in such a difficult time. Therefore, the restaurant did not exist for long and was soon closed.

After several earthquakes, the building was declared damaged and closed for a while. After carrying out restoration work, they began to conduct fascinating excursions.





Title

Everyone who plans to visit the Swallow‚Äôs Nest in Crimea, the history of the name will also be interesting.

Many of the questions that tourists have here immediately find their answers. When you first see the castle, an association arises that it is just as attached to the rock, like a nest of a swallow on the wall of a house. Moreover, the dimensions of this building are very small. Which also emphasizes the resemblance to a bird house. Its width is only ten meters and a length of twenty.

But, and that's not all. Once upon a time, a section of a high cliff, from which the sea was perfectly visible, was chosen by swallows for its housing. Hence the name of this place.





Curious facts

Getting acquainted with the history of the "Swallow's Nest" in the Crimea, you can find out a lot of interesting information. Many readers will probably be interested to know the following details about this place:

  • The history of the construction of the "Swallow's Nest" in Crimea is of interest not only to Russians, but also to foreign tourists.
  • This place was depicted by many famous artists. Among them are Ivan Aivazovsky and Alexey Bogolyubov.
  • The construction is made in the Gothic style. The construction reflects its characteristic features. Such as: elongated windows; pointedness; ribbed, repeating lines and more. Such buildings were more characteristic for Europe than for Russia.
  • Castle "Swallow's Nest" has repeatedly been partially destroyed. Several times restoration work was carried out here.
  • There was once a restaurant in the castle, and then a reading room.
  • It often hosts exhibitions of paintings by famous artists.
  • "Swallow's Nest" had other names - "Generalif" and "Castle of Love."
  • Here scenes were shot from once popular films - Amphibian Man and Ten Little Indians.
  • Once there was a garden near the castle. But, as a result of an earthquake, he crashed into the sea. They did not begin to restore it.




Where is it located and how to get there?

How many readers know the exact address of the "Swallow's Nest"? And how can I get here? Now you will know about it. The exact address of the castle is the following - Crimea, the village of Gaspra, Alupkinskoye Shosse, 9 A. You can get here in the following ways:

  • Own car. One of the most convenient and fast ways.
  • Taxi. It can be taken from the Yalta bus station.
  • Buses It is best to go from Yalta. Route numbers: 102, 132. Travel time is thirty to forty minutes.
  • On the boat. One of the most enjoyable ways. You can see the beautiful castle not only from land but also from the sea.
