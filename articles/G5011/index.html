And how do you like such an abandoned metro station? Moscow "Vorobyovy Gory" ("Lenin") is well known. Station opened on January 12, 1959. It was sheltered by the lower tier of the Luzhnetsk metro bridge. In 1983, signs of destruction of the base object began to be observed: the poor quality of the concrete affected. He could not withstand the vibration of the bridge from the effects of intense auto flow.

Stopping traffic for two to three years (to fix the problem) meant leaving a huge part of the city without communication with the center (you won‚Äôt jump over the Moscow River). The solution was found in the construction of two roundabout mini-bridges (1984) and the station was quietly closed for repairs.

But it was not possible to fulfill what was planned, the destruction only worsened. At the end of the 90s, the question was no longer about the reconstruction of the Sparrow Hills, but about the repair of the entire Metro Bridge. The restoration was carried out in stages: first, the upper level was transformed, then it came to the lower, station level. Waves of crises complicated and tightened the work. The station opened only on December 14, 2002. The train tracks and the platform were separated, so now the "mountains" are not shaking.
