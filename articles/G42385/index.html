Kemer Attractions

Resting in the center of Kemer, simply obliges you to see not only the entire resort, but also the nearest attractions. Tourists are usually attracted by the ancient ruins and natural beauties of these places. In the north of the resort is the Yoruk Park, which is an authentic village. It recreated a series of expositions of everyday life of Turkish tribes. A visit to the park is accompanied not only by viewing costumes and household items, but also by refreshments with traditional dishes of local cuisine. Entire dwellings and workshops of nomads were recreated on the territory of the complex.



Near Kemer is the ancient city of Phaselis. If you believe the legends, it was founded a long time ago, back in the seventh century BC. At one time, the city was very large and had three harbors. Over the course of his long history, he changed rulers many times, among which was Alexander the Great. And later, Phaselis fell under the rule of Rome, after which the heyday of the city lasted for three hundred years. Currently, the ruins of buildings that date back to the Byzantine and Roman periods have been preserved.

Another ancient city worthy of the attention of tourists is Olympos. It was he who was first mentioned in the ancient Lycian annals. Olympos was founded in the second century BC near modern Kemer. Initially, it was inhabited by pirates, and later the Roman warriors took the land, joining them to their vast empire. In Olympos you can see the ruins of the temple of Hephaestus, the bridge and the amphitheater. Today the city is included in the National Park. It is poorly preserved, but still worth seeing the ancient monuments of architecture.

A very interesting sight is Yanartash - "burning mountain" in translation. On the mountain, flames erupt in places from the ground, and burning does not stop, which is why the area is shrouded in smoke. According to scientists, the reason for this interesting phenomenon is natural gas, which comes to the surface and when combined with oxygen, a flame arises. Of course, there is a more fantastic explanation for the phenomenon. An ancient legend says that Bellefront (the Greek hero) killed the monster Chimera, who burned everything with his fire, and threw it into the mountains. That is why fire to this day comes from the Chimera. Of course, this is just a beautiful legend, but an amazing phenomenon is worth a look.



A very interesting cave complex near Kemer called Beldibi. The picturesque place is surrounded by coniferous forests. Here the bugle river begins, there is a waterfall. In ancient times, caves served as a place of shelter from the elements for people and animals. In the last century, archaeologists carefully studied the underground galleries and discovered a lot of interesting things in them - fragments of household items, cave paintings, tools. In general, the caves are very interesting, but you can‚Äôt go to them yourself, because there is a very deep cliff near the entrance.
