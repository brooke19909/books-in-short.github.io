Innsbruck ski resort: description, photos, reviews. Holidays in Austria

The ski resort, which is located in Innsbruck - the capital of the Tyrol region - has existed for more than one century. This is the most popular and famous place for lovers of Austria. Here you can not only explore the Old Town and the picturesque Alps, but also spend a few winter days especially actively!

Innsbruck can be safely called the center of winter sports, as it is here that the sophistication of this amazing city and the characteristics of the best ski resort are harmoniously combined. It is surrounded by numerous areas where you can snowboard and ski. More information about the ski resort Innsbruck in Austria and reviews about it can be found further.

Local lifts and slopes

In Innsbruck, all conditions for off-piste skiing are created, there is a lot of space for extreme downhill skiing. There are quite a lot of tracks for cross-country skiing and back country, parks for snowboarders, as well as a large number of other amenities. Therefore, people jokingly say that in Innsbruck skiing is the main way to travel.

The city is surrounded by 9 ski areas, accessible by free bus. In addition, for the convenience of vacationers, a separate ski pass is provided here, which allows you to use the slopes of Innsbruck more freely.

The main attraction and pride of this city is called fully equipped and even somewhat unique tracks. There are 5 separate areas fully equipped for skiing that are optimally suited for skiing. They consist of a large number of tracks that are optimal for skiing people with different levels of training for skiing.

In the Hungerburg district there are trails for every ski lover. Here is the world famous Olympic track, suitable for riding on it even for athletes.

According to reviews, the Glungetser region is ideal for professional athletes, as well as amateurs with sufficient experience, since local routes are more than 4 km long, and the terrain is quite difficult for beginners.

Mutterer Alm is an ideal place in which the tracks are mainly aimed at amateurs and beginners, since the slopes are distinguished by a relatively calm relief, making the area very attractive and often visited for modern tourists in a rather short time.

Innsbruck Infrastructure

In the ski regions there are many hotels and hotels offering visitors accommodation at different prices. There are also many restaurants and establishments for good nutrition, where you can try food from different countries. Most restaurants are located near hiking trails or near hotels. Innsbruck also has enough cafes and bars.

All winter, various sporting events, concerts are held here, and a wide variety of entertainment is offered to modern skiers. The reviews most often praise Christmas markets, horse-drawn cart rides, ice climbing, bobsledding, ice skating, and undoubtedly skiing.

In winter, tourists are advised to visit also Alpenzoo - a local zoo. It is located on a mountain, and its peculiarity is that the enclosures are located downward by a beautiful cascade. In addition, there are many museums in Innsbruck:

  1. Muenze Hall, telling the story of medieval Austria. You can also climb to the very top to explore the area from above.
  2. Audioversum is a unique interactive museum that sends guests to the enchanting world of sounds and hearing, truly impressing with sound effects and artifacts. Local installations are created using unusual technologies. This museum will teach you to truly be surprised, hear and feel.
  3. The Bell Museum consists of more than 200 small bells and large bells that every guest can ring.
  4. Swarovski Kristallwelten is a truly noteworthy museum, even a huge complex consisting of the museum itself, huge gardens, an exclusive store, as well as many other premises. The exposition consists of unique works inlaid with the famous Swarovski crystals. In the gardens of tourists, small waterfalls, multi-colored flowering flower beds, as well as original lanterns that look amazing against the backdrop of mountains and descents await you. The store sells accessories and jewelry with Swarovski crystals.

Things to Do in Innsbruck

To diversify a fairly active ski holiday on the slopes, you can visit the indoor premises, where there is everything you need to play squash and curling, or if you wish, go to the indoor pool. In addition, you can fly on a ball or an airplane, mark ice rods and go ice skating.

After an active holiday, you can visit local discos and bars to enjoy delicious beer or aromatic tea. Also, if you wish, you can buy souvenirs to relatives and friends, or explore other attractions: for example, the Local History Museum, the Golden Roof bay window, the Hofgarten park, the Helbling House, the Ottoburg town hall, the Olympic Games Museum, the Hofburg Imperial Palace, the Triumphal Gate, the Congress Palace and others. .

Where to stay?

More than 200 comfortable hotels in Innsbruck annually host hundreds of thousands of skiers. The most budget room for 2 people costs about 60 euros per day. This price includes a bathroom with shower, free Wi-Fi and a kitchenette.

The average room rate is about 80 euros per day. Usually this amount includes breakfast, as well as a small kitchen, a terrace and some equipment for a comfortable stay. Often, these rooms can have a separate entrance, as well as a cozy area for lunch.

It is important to note that the Innsbruck hotels located near the slopes are distinguished by a high level of service and services, so even the most budget room will be worthy.

Conclusion

Innsbruck has truly unlimited sporting opportunities, which is why professional skiers, as well as not fully trained travelers, loved the described area. Here, each guest will be able to find for themselves the best option for the route and suitable accommodation. The advantage is that the Innsbruck Airport is located near the city. A week-long vacation in this place will not "eat" all the savings. And adding a fairly developed infrastructure, you can get a wonderful resort for ski holidays in the winter.
