How is a mortgage different from other types of loans?

The most important difference is the interest rate. Mortgage loans are much lower than car loans, consumer loans, etc. And this is explained very simply. As a rule, people take mortgages for quite large amounts, because real estate is not cheap. And interest on the loan is accrued on the entire amount of the debt. And if the mortgage rate was the same as the consumer loan, people would not have enough 1 life to pay the money on the loan. And then this duty would be inherited by their children. Because the amount of overpayments would exceed the amount of the mortgage by 3 or even 4 times.

For greater clarity, consider an example. Initial data: you want to buy an apartment in a mortgage for 5,000,000 rubles. You are not ready to make a down payment, but you are ready to pay 86.5 thousand rubles. If the mortgage rate equaled even the practically minimum rate on a consumer loan, then you would have to pay money for a mortgage for 20 years and you would have overpaid 315%, which is equivalent to 15.7 million rubles! That is, you would pay the cost of 4 of your apartments !





Pay attention when you pick up a mortgage program for the loan term. That is, with our initial data, the overpayment for 20 years is 315%, and if we increase the payment by only 2.7 thousand rubles, then the period will decrease by as much as 5 years! And the overpayment will decrease by 91%! Feel what a global difference? !





And with a period of 10 years and payment of 98 thousand rubles per month, your overpayment will be 135%, because 6.7 million rubles .





If you follow the trend, it becomes clear that for every 5 years of a mortgage, the amount of interest is equivalent to the value of your apartment!

But this is at a rate of 20% per annum. In our country, as a rule, it is lower for mortgage programs and ranges from 13%. Let's make a calculation for a similar time frame and compare the result.

If we consider the period of 20 years, the monthly payment is 59.5 thousand rubles, which is 27 thousand lower than at a rate of 20%. Feel what role the difference of 7% plays ?! The total amount of overpayments is 9.3 million rubles, i.e. 185% in 20 years. See how big the difference is ?





When reducing the period by 5 years, the payment increases by only 4.5 thousand, but the overpayment decreases by 55%! And it is 6.5 million rubles !





With a loan term of 10 years and a payment of 75.3 thousand rubles, the overpayment will be 81%, namely 4 million rubles. As you can see, the difference is very noticeable .

Borrower Requirements

They differ in each bank, but there are a number of criteria that are the same in all banks. Let's look at them:

1. Age. Many banks have a minimum age of 21 years to process an application. And this is understandable. As a rule, in this time period a person graduates from an educational institution and has a certain length of service.

2. Work experience is the second criterion by which banks determine how reliable a borrower is. The minimum work experience at the last place of work must be at least a year. In addition, employees pay attention to the fact that a person‚Äôs reliability indicator is the frequency with which he changes jobs. If a person does not stay anywhere for more than 1 month, this fact is unlikely to indicate its reliability.

3. Credit history. Having a positive credit history will only benefit you. Because you have shown yourself to be a responsible person and it‚Äôs easier for a bank to approve a mortgage loan.

So, to summarize, what do you need to take a mortgage for an apartment? Good credit history, work experience of at least a year and age of the borrower 21+.

Mortgage and Maternity Capital

Is it possible to use maternity capital to repay a mortgage and how to do it? What do you need to buy an apartment in a mortgage? Using capital to pay off mortgages is a common practice. But not many people know how to do this correctly. At some banks, you can use it as a down payment.

To do this, you need to warn bank employees in advance that you will make a down payment, not in cash, but with a certificate. And also you need to collect an extended package of documents.

Part of the certificates you will need to take from the Pension Fund, for example, a certificate of the status of your account. And to submit some documents to the Pension Fund, as everything is carefully checked, because there are many cases when people just wanted to cash out maternity capital.
