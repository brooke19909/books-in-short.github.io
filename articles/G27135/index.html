Whom to pay?

Who should pay such a fee? Practice (and legislation) shows that all taxpayers are subject to income tax. That is, individuals resident in the country. Non-residents should not be forgotten either. True, the second category of people usually pays this fee only when they have any income in the territory of the Russian Federation.

In addition, there is also a corporate income tax. He is also considered. It turns out that all firms, companies and organizations, as well as working individuals, pay income tax. Regardless of age. The main thing is that they should be officially hired. Exceptions exist in the case of individuals and organizations. Which ones? Who and when can be exempted from the annual report and payment? What is the procedure for calculating income tax for citizens and companies? We will answer all questions in this article.



Who does not pay?

Of course, in all the rules there are exceptions. And taxes also apply. So, in Russia, citizens who do not have any tax are exempt from annual income payments. Most often these are women on maternity leave, elderly people, and also minor children. Nevertheless, if the latter go to official work and begin to receive income, you will have to pay the corresponding tax. What to do with companies and firms? For organizations

It is very easy to get confused. Yes, there is a single formula for calculations. But in each case it changes in one direction or another. When it comes to organizations, the calculations are completely thought-provoking. Especially when you consider that the firm must clearly know what it has income and expenses. It also takes into account what exactly needs to be borne in mind when implementing this function. Tax calculations of organizations can be carried out using two different methods. More precisely, there are different forms of tax accounting. What kind?



The first is the accrual method. Here the date of receipt and expenditure of funds is not taken into account. That is, all expenses and incomes are simply indicated for the period when they took place. The second is the cash method. It is more convenient. And practiced in many organizations. Here, for the tax report, the specific time and date of the income and expenses received plays a role. They must be indicated.

Single formula

Well, before you look at an example of calculating income tax in a particular case, you should find out a unified calculation formula. It is suitable for both organizations and individuals. True, it will have to be changed depending on the situation. The thing is that the amount of tax depends on the so-called tax base and rate. Moreover, the dependence is direct. The work of these is the profit tax. The calculation formula does not change either for companies, or for legal entities, or for individuals. The difference is only in tax rates. And in the appropriate database.

What is it about? As we have already found out, tax calculations of income are made using the accounting of revenues and expenses. The tax base is the amount received after deduction of losses. That is, income-expenses are equated to it. Everything is easy and simple. If there are no expenses, then only profit plays a role.

Organization Betting

Of course, for tax calculation to be accurate, you need to know the appropriate rates. There are many nuances. Therefore, special attention is paid to individuals, and then to organizations. Without a tax rate, an example of settlements will simply lose its meaning. So what can you prepare for in the Russian Federation?



The main income tax rate in Russia for 2016, according to information posted on the website of the tax authorities of the Russian Federation, is 20%. That is, it is this percentage of the base that will be paid to the state treasury by organizations most often.

If the income is obtained from securities, then you will have to pay 30%. they pay half less when it is received by the owners of municipal and state enterprises, as well as from dividends of a foreign organization on shares of Russian companies. Depositary receipts allow you to pay 13%. Foreign organizations that are not connected with activities in Russia through a permanent government pay income tax in the amount of 10 percent, and 9% is the rarest rate. It is possible upon receipt of income on certain municipal securities (for details, see article 284 of the Tax Code of the Russian Federation, part 4, paragraph 2).

Personal Income Rates

An example of calculating corporate income tax can already be given. But if we are talking about individuals, do not rush. The thing is that the single income tax rate for citizens in the Russian Federation is set at 13%. This figure is taken into account most often. But there are exceptions. For example, you can take 9% of the profit for the tax rate. But such a rule applies only when dividends are received by citizens until 2015, and bonds with a mortgage - until 2007. Dividends from organizations received by individuals who are not residents of the Russian Federation are taxed at 15%. Other income requires payment of 30%, and winnings, and, for example, credit ruble loans - 35.



Example (enterprises)

Now that all the basic information is known, you can give a clear example of the calculation of income tax. And for individuals and for organizations. Let's start with the second option. After all, there are a lot of nuances. It is important to consider all input data. Otherwise, errors in the calculations are possible.

Suppose we have an enterprise in Russia. It receives a loan from the bank for this tax reporting period in the amount of one million rubles. In this case, the prepayment fluctuates at the level of 400,000. Revenues and VAT amounted to 1,770 and 270 thousand rubles, respectively, in the first quarter. At the same time, production costs amounted to 560,000, 350,000 were paid to employees, insurance amounted to about 91,000, depreciation - 60,000. The company also gives a loan to another company in the amount of 25 thousand rubles, and in the previous period tax loss amounted to 120,000.

So, how to calculate how much and where to pay? First you need to calculate the costs for the specified quarter. It summarizes salary, depreciation, issued credit, insurance, production costs. It turns out that the expense will be: 560 + 350 + 25 + 91 + 60 + 350 = 1,086 thousand rubles.

Next, you need to find out how much will be the tax base. For this, all expenses and VAT are subtracted from revenue. Losses in the previous reporting period are also counted. The following example will turn out: 1,770,000 - 270,000 - 1,086,000 - 120,000 = 294 thousand rubles. This amount will be taxed.

What's next? Only a few left. For example, in our case, the company does not have any features. So, it‚Äôs enough to multiply the tax base by the tax rate. And we will get the exact amount due for income tax for our organization. It turns out: 294,000 * 20% = 58,800 rubles. They must be paid in the first quarter as a tax.

Settlements for individuals

This is not worth ending. For individuals, there are also very interesting options. For example, when they are entitled to any deduction. Suppose that citizen N receives a salary of 58 thousand rubles and has one child. An example of calculating income tax will be as follows.

The standard deduction for a minor in this case will be 1,400 rubles. This amount should be taken from the salary. We get a tax base of 56,600. From this amount, you will have to calculate the income tax of 13%. It turns out: 56 600 * 13% = 7 280 rubles. That is how much you will need to pay as income tax.



IP with the simplified tax system

As already mentioned, the so-called simplified system is characteristic of individual entrepreneurs. They pay income tax, but in a slightly different form. The rate will be 6% for 2016. Perhaps this is the only contribution (with the exception of contributions to the FIU and insurance), which worries entrepreneurs.

The example of calculating income tax in this case is more than simple. It all depends on what kind of income a citizen receives. The amount necessary for payment will be taken from him. For example, an entrepreneur earned 360,000 rubles a year. He works on the simplified tax system. At the end of the tax reporting period (this is by April 30) you need to pay: 360,000 * 6% = 21,600 rubles. Everything is easy and simple. No expenses - only income.
