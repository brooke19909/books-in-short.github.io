What can be made of disks - the second life of old things

Modern technology is evolving at the speed of sound. A couple of dozen years ago, the world's population used vinyl records with might and main, enjoying recordings of their favorite songs, fairy tales and operas. Then they were replaced by tape cassettes . Then the first video tapes appeared. Now, people do not know what to do with CDs. Numerous "blanks" are now gathering dust in cabinets, folders and bedside tables without any work. What can be done from disks that have been damaged and become unreadable?

Crafts from CDs are a widespread hobby all over the world. After all, if a person has a little time, then even outdated things can be given a second wind.

The easiest idea for using old, useless discs is to use them as cup holders. In order to make them more interesting and original, compacts can be painted with acrylics.

Original curtains - this is what can be made of discs. To do this, it is enough to have a needle and wire. Using a heated needle, holes are created in the disk, which are then wired to holes in other disks.

You can give a second, more durable, life to the discs, using them as an element of interior decor. For example, such painted discs hang on my living room wall.

Here is another design option.

What can be made of disks is to cut them into many small pieces. The reasonable question is - why? And the goal is this:

1. Using the reflective side of the old compact, you can create a delicious disco ball that will decorate any room.

2. The photo frame adorned with slices of the mirror side of the discs will look just fine.

3. In the same way, you can decorate any cardboard box, giving it an original look.
