What is a throne? The meaning of the word and its role in the life of kings

About what the throne, you can talk for hours. After all, this word has so many definitions that even an experienced linguist can be confused. Perhaps someone will now think that this is a clear exaggeration. Then let's try together to sort out this difficult question.





What is the throne: definition

First of all, the throne is the chair of the monarch. Although you should not take the word "chair" so literally. Indeed, in reality it can be a bench, an armchair, a small sofa and so on. The key factor is that only the supreme ruler can sit on it. That is, he is a kind of symbol of royalty.

However, do not forget about other definitions of the word. In particular, one should understand what the throne is in the church world. So, in any church there is an altar in which there is a table. His priests are called the throne.

Such a device is necessary in order to conduct rites on it. In particular, everyone who took the sacrament in the church knows perfectly well what the throne is. After all, it is on it that bread and wine are blessed, which are then distributed to parishioners.





What is a throne through the eyes of monarchs?

Nevertheless, the word "throne" is much more often used in the description of royal power. After all, it was from here that it took its life. In addition, there are quite a few stable phrases associated with it. Moreover, they can be used both in direct and figurative meaning.

For example, the expression "he sat on the throne" means - someone has become a monarch. This word can also mean all royal power as a whole. Therefore, quite often in books or historical records you can find such a phrase as "inherited the royal throne."
