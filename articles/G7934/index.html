Secrets of a sound device, or How to connect speakers to a TV

Sometimes very funny situations happen. For example, a home theater purchased for savings does not want to reveal the secret of its connection. The whole family council has been in a thoughtful state for two hours in a row: how to connect speakers to the TV? Despite the fact that all the cords are connected where necessary strictly according to the instructions, still there are three unused “tulips”, and the same number of so-called “bells” have already been arranged in free TV sockets. Nevertheless, the sound coming from the speakers did not at all reflect the “surround quality” declared by the manufacturer.





How to connect speakers to a TV: a simple solution to a pressing issue

First of all, pay attention to the following moment: how many audio outputs are in your TV device? Typically, these jacks are labeled with the following designation: “Audio IN / OUT” or “Audio R / L”. Depending on the modification of the television receiver, the device may be equipped with a SCART-connector, optical or coaxial output. All this depends primarily on the novelty of your blue screen. The best option can be considered a regular analog audio output ("tulips"), which are equipped with almost all modern TVs. With their help, you can connect the speakers to the TV. Again, it should be understood that the output to the speakers is through the jacks of the device, labeled “Audio OUT”.

Sound quality and power

Of course, you cannot achieve a wide range of sound range by a two-channel type by connecting, that is, when using two speakers connected through “tulips”. To hear the surround accompaniment of the video, you need a receiver or multi-channel amplifier. It is such a device that allows you to give the moment of film adaptation the effect of saturation and realism. And all thanks to the divided sound channels and loud subwoofer operation. To do this, just connect the speakers 51 to the TV and place the speakers correctly, since the quality of perception depends on the positioning of the sound devices.





Connection Methods

  • Option one: connect a sound amplifier and a TV receiver through a standard RCA cable or SCART cable. Observe the polarity of the outputs and do not confuse “IN” with “OUT”.
  • The second option: use the “OPTICAL OUT” or “COAXIAL OUT” connector on the TV and the same designation with the ending “IN” on the home theater receiver or stereo system.
  • The third method allows you to connect computer speakers to the TV. To do this, connect the plug from the speakers to the headphone jack, usually located on the front of the TV, and you can enjoy stereo sound.
  • Sometimes the fourth option is quite advisable, especially when you consider that the old music center is practically not used. The miniJack-2RCA cord is the perfect medium for sound effects. Using the “AUX IN” connector of your CD-DVD processor, connect the television “Audio OUT” output, using the two “R / L” channels. Typically, music centers are equipped with a built-in equalizer, so the sound can be output.

Impeccable quality, or How to make a sound come to life

Let's focus on the option when you need a clear sound of notes and a pleasant timbre of speech. Only digital sound can be worthy to be called comprehensive and pervasive. So, the question "how to connect speakers to a TV" should, first of all, imply a quality solution. Agree, it is much more comfortable to watch a movie with surround sound. Therefore, only the use of modern technology allows you to get real pleasure from the perception by our hearing aid of the entire gamut of sound.





Finally

When making any of the fundamentally different types of connection, you should always remember that such a phenomenon as statistical stress can be the culprit in the failure of your electronics. In order to avoid such consequences, connect only with the equipment turned off. Now you know how to connect speakers to the TV, what you need to know. But still, the main options are taken into account, so you should not have difficulty with the "extraction" of sound from the TV and its transfer to other devices.
