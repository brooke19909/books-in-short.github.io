Hotel "St. Petersburg", Pirogovskaya embankment, 5/2: description, review and reviews

One of the most popular cities in Russia, without a doubt, is the magnificent St. Petersburg. Enjoy white nights, stroll along the Neva and watch the construction of bridges, visit Peterhof and the Hermitage - this is just a small list of what tourists can do in this wonderful city. And so that the impressions of visiting the Northern capital of Russia are not overshadowed by unsuccessful accommodation, you should choose some good hotel for your stay.

An excellent choice can be the hotel "St. Petersburg" (Pirogovskaya embankment, 5/2), which is located in the heart of the city.

Historical background of the hotel

The history of the hotel business in St. Petersburg dates back to the time of Peter the Great. The very first hotels were opened by Prince A.D. Menshikov. In 1917, more than 120 different hotels functioned in the city. However, after the revolution, the rapid development of places to temporarily stop guests was noticeably stopped, and only in the years after the war did a new construction stage begin.





The hotel, which today is called St. Petersburg, was first called Leningrad. Its construction in the period from 1967 to 1970 was a real breakthrough in the field of construction of hotels of that time. The authors of the individual project of this building were: V. E. Struzman, N. V. Kamensky and S. B. Speransky, and the construction was carried out by Yugoslav workers who used only advanced technologies for those years.

Features of the hotel St. Petersburg

During its existence, the St. Petersburg Hotel (Pirogovskaya Naberezhnaya, 5/2) has hosted a lot of celebrities. It became the first place in Russia where new types of breakfasts were introduced: continental and buffet-style. Also, there were many very important meetings, events and conferences.

For example, in 1986, it was in the walls of this hotel that the second part of the world championship match was held, in which Anatoly Karpov and Garry Kasparov competed. Major chess tournaments such as the Rudenko Memorial and the Chigorin Memorial were also held here. G8 finance ministers came to G8 for their second meeting in 2006.

For those who plan to visit the delightful Northern capital of Russia, it is very important to know where good hotels are located in the city. Address at which the St. Petersburg Hotel is located: Pirogovskaya embankment, 5/2.

What is next to the hotel

This is one of the magnificent places in the historical center of the city, from where such attractions as St. Isaac's Cathedral and the Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood are clearly visible. In the immediate vicinity of the hotel is the Sampsonian Bridge, a little further away is the Liteiny Bridge. It is very convenient to get to the numerous cultural and important places of the city from here, as the Ploshchad Lenina metro stop is just a 10-minute walk away. Finland Station is also nearby.

Description of the hotel Saint Petersburg

Hotel St. Petersburg (Pirogovskaya nab., 5/2), the description of which is of interest to many guests of the city, is a twelve-story building. The first two floors are non-residential, the rooms are located on the third and higher.

Floor A, or the first floor, welcomes guests with a well-groomed and spacious lounge and reception desk. There is also a lobby bar and a special exhibition area. On the B floor (on the second) is the Bering Restaurant with stunning panoramic views. It is here that guests can enjoy breakfast and lunch on a buffet basis, as well as taste dishes of various European cuisines, which are prepared by chef Dmitry Shcherbakov.
