We study geography: where is Novorossiysk located?

Novorossiysk is a large city and seaport located in the most convenient and non-freezing bay of the Black Sea. Where is Novorossiysk, in which country of the Black Sea basin can you visit so many memorable places of military glory, feel the power of the north-east wind and see a huge number of ships on the roads? A large settlement plunges into the sea, protecting itself by the slopes of the mountainous Caucasus.





Geography and climatic conditions of the city

Novorossiysk is located on the shore of the Tsemess Bay of the Russian Black Sea coast. Climate formation in this territory is greatly influenced by proximity to the sea and the protection of the Caucasus Mountains. In winter, the air masses characterize the type of climate as moderate, and in the summer season tropical air circulation rules the ball. The region where Novorossiysk is located is the epicenter of natural anomalous phenomena and is characterized by the action of a northeast wind from mountain ranges. The comfortable temperature of the summer months under the influence of the anticyclone can become abnormal for some period and reach +40 degrees. Such disasters occur in Novorossiysk every 8-10 years. The impact of the sea smooths out abnormal phenomena: winters are often warm and short, summers are hot and long. The climate of the village in its characteristics is close to the Mediterranean type.





Historical reference

The history of a large seaport and resort of the country goes back to the distant medieval period, when the ancient Greeks founded a trading pier here called Bata. The area where the city of Novorossiysk is located has always been attractive for its convenient geographical location. In ancient times, the Greeks settled here, the Alans nomads conquered the territory, the Turkish sultans erected fortifications. And only in 1838, Russian troops entered the Tsemess bay, where they founded a military settlement. For three years of the Crimean War, the port was severely destroyed, as the country's fleet was inferior to the technical equipment of the Anglo-French squadron. For 10 years, the large center of the Black Sea province - Novorossiysk has grown on the ruins of the fortification. The second destruction came with World War II. German forces had their views of the city and its strategic importance in the course of hostilities. The legendary port survived and rose from the ashes of the phoenix, was rebuilt and rebuilt, breathed modern peace.
