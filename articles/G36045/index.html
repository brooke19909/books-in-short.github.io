Tula region, the village of Konduki, Blue Lakes: description, photos and coordinates

It is difficult to believe that not far from large cities preserved pieces of pristine nature, striking in its beauty and unusual landscape. Residents of the central region of the Russian Federation can verify this statement personally. Just 3.5 hours drive from Moscow is the village of Conduki. The blue lakes in this area will captivate with their beauty and amaze.

Where did the Blue Lakes come from in the Tula Region?

In the vicinity of Tula in the 60s of the last century, brown coal was actively mined . Useful rock in this area lies quite close to the surface layers of the soil. For this reason, coal was mined in many places by the open pit - through quarrying. Already in the 1980s, the appropriateness of mining aroused a lot of questions. Coal itself was considered insufficient quality, while production costs increased. Gradually, mines and quarries began to close. In memory of the times of mining in the Tula region there are many mining villages and villages, the surroundings of which are decorated with high mountains (heaps) and deep lakes - flooded quarries.





One of the most interesting and picturesque places characterized by an atypical landscape for the middle strip is the village of Konduki. Blue Lakes is a network of flooded quarries, the water in which pleases with all shades of azure and turquoise.

Conduction: photos and description

What awaits a traveler who decides for the first time with his own eyes to look at the Blue Lakes of the Tula region? The local landscape at the same time resembles the surface of another, unknown planet, and Altai, and Karelia: sandy hills and real mountains, lakes of all sizes and shapes, as well as fields and green forests. It is worth noting that we are talking about a fairly vast territory.





Blue Lakes (Tula Oblast, Konduki) is not a 2-3 reservoir, but a whole network of flooded quarries. At the height of the warm season, many vacationers with children from all over the central region of the Russian Federation come to the unofficial recreation area. They swim in the ponds, and sunbathe on the sandy beaches. Many tourists prefer to spend all weekend in Konduk, setting up campgrounds. You can pick mushrooms and berries in the surrounding forests and fields. The village of Konduki, Blue Lakes is a great place to relax with the whole family.

Myths and Facts

Careers in the vicinity of the village of Conduki are not an officially recognized recreation area. This place has gained incredible popularity in recent years solely due to its unusual nature. At the same time, the administration of the Tula region is in no hurry to improve beaches and check their sanitary and hygienic condition. It is good to know if you are going to visit Conduki for the first time: Blue Lakes are not officially intended for swimming. Moreover, the unusual color of the water in these reservoirs is probably due to the special chemical composition of the liquid. Horror stories about harmful substances and impurities contained in the water of the Blue Lakes are quite common.





Do not rush to be upset; when inspected without special equipment, local water bodies do not cause negative emotions. Local water is odorless, it seems transparent or bluish-green. Do not forget that when bathing in any career should be careful. The bottom of such reservoirs is rarely even, more often it retains traces from the buckets of excavators that once dug out the soil here. Do not sail far from the coast if you feel in the water not confident enough!

How to get to the Blue Lakes?

The unofficial recreation area does not have an exact address; you should focus on the village of Konduki (Uzlovsky District). Blue lakes in the Tula region are located at some distance from large settlements. They can only be reached by private car. The next turn on the sign is to Konduky, after it you need to go straight. Having passed a small half-abandoned village, you will find yourself on a dirt road.
