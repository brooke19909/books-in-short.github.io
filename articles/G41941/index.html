How to check the last 5 actions performed on a number on MTS?

The need to verify completed transactions on a number may arise for a number of reasons. One of them is an unreasonable decrease in balance. If you are sure that you did not activate any additional services and newsletters, did not use the Internet, but the funds were debited from the account, then most likely you will be interested in the question of how to check the last 5 actions on MTS. It is about him that will be discussed in this article. You will learn how to get similar information about your number, what is the cost of such a service, and are there any restrictions.





How to check the last 5 actions on MTS

Inexplicable deductions from a mobile phone‚Äôs account can occur for several reasons: additional options or newsletters are connected to the number, the subscriber makes calls without thinking about their cost (for example, long-distance), an Internet connection is made (it is recommended to turn off mobile Internet, unless, of course , unlimited packages are not connected), etc. To understand what caused the change in balance in the negative direction, you need to check what actions were performed on the number. To do this, you can send the appropriate request. MTS will send the last 5 actions as a text message. In SMS, it will be clear how much was spent on each of the operations, whether it be a call, the Internet, sending a message, or debiting for a certain service. The request is sent using the USSD command * 152 * 1 #. If it does not work, then you can enter * 152 #, and then select "1".

The cost of the last 5 actions of MTS

This service is provided free of charge to MTS subscribers. With its help, you can easily understand what the money was spent on. Please note that the message that will be sent to you in response to the request will contain only the latest account transactions. If they do not seem strange to you and the write-offs on them will be justified, then it makes sense to order the details. This service is provided on a paid basis and allows you to receive data for the last six months (the subscriber can choose any period in this range) via the Internet, for three years - in the office of the mobile operator.
