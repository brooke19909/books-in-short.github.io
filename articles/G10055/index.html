Is Sberbank a commercial or state bank?

Sberbank OJSC is the largest financial institution not only in Russia, but also in the CIS. It has a large network of units. The main field of activity of the institution is the provision of a wide range of investment and banking services. The main shareholder and at the same time the founder of the financial institution is the Central Bank of the Russian Federation. He owns more than half of the authorized capital and the voting share. About 40% of the shares are owned by foreign companies. The position of president of the institution and chairman of the board belongs to German Gref.

A bit of history





The central Sberbank began its history in 1841. It was during that period that Emperor Nicholas 1 issued a decree on the formation of savings banks. The first ticket office was opened in 1842 in the territory of St. Petersburg. In 1987, it was she who served as the basis for the formation of a bank for savings and lending to the population, including legal entities. The financial institution included 15 republican banks. At that time, it was very problematic to say unequivocally that Sberbank is a commercial or state financial institution. Since the summer of 1990, by decision of the Supreme Council of Russia, the Republican Bank was officially recognized as the property of the Russian Federation. By the end of 1990, the financial institution was transformed into a battery. In 1991, it was already possible to unequivocally answer the question of whether Sberbank is a commercial or state-owned bank. After the institution became the property of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, it received the official status of a joint-stock commercial bank.

Bank activities today





The answer to the question as to whether Sberbank is a state or commercial bank is obvious. The commercial structure takes a leading position in terms of asset volume, as well as in the number of settlement accounts of corporate clients, of which there are at least a million. Despite the fact that the shares of Sberbank are mainly owned by the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, it continues to flourish as an independent institution, it accounts for at least 45% of private deposits of the domestic market. About 11 million people receive salaries through a financial institution; more than 12 million people receive pensions. The financial institution has established about 19 thousand ATMs and 70 thousand self-service terminals throughout the country and issued more than 30 million plastic cards.
