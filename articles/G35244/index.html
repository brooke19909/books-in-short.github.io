Spain Square in Barcelona: history, how to get, what to see, reviews of tourists with photos

Spain Square in Barcelona - one of the main squares in the capital of Catalonia and the second largest in the country, is a landmark of the city. The area serves as an important transport hub, under it is a metro station. Near the Plaza de Espana there are many attractions that will be interesting to see for guests of beautiful Barcelona.

Location

Piazza di Spagna in Barcelona is located at the foot of Montjuic. It is located at the intersection of the main streets of the city: Gran Via, Creu Cobert, Parallel Avenue, Queen Maria Cristina Avenue and Tarragona Street.

How to get there

One of the most convenient ways to get to Spain Square in Barcelona is by metro. To do this, you need to board a train following one of the lines L1, L3 or L8, and get off at Pl. Espanya. You can get there on one of the buses under the numbers 37, 65, 79, 91, 109, 165 and get off at Pl. Espanya.

Reviews

Guests who have visited Spain Square in Barcelona speak of the special atmosphere and beauty of this place. Many people really like the attentive attitude towards elderly people, people with disabilities and just tired tourists - the authorities organized escalators and comfortable places of relaxation so that you could listen to street musicians.

In the evening, there is a lot of police in the square, which guarantees the safety of everyone here, but experienced tourists are advised to be careful and carefully monitor their belongings.

City guests recommend visiting the National Palace, where you can see a lot of interesting things. It is better to go up to him on the escalator to save strength, and go down the stairs to give a good look at the view from there.

The fountain show starts at 20:00, but it is recommended to come in advance. By evening, crowds of people flock to this place and occupy all places convenient for viewing. By the beginning of the show it is impossible not only to sit somewhere nearby, but also to find a corner to admire the magnificent show while standing.

Sightseeing, according to tourists, it is better to start around nine in the morning, since at a later time the area is very hot, there is not even a small shade to hide from the scorching sun.

Traveler reviews on visiting Spain Square in Barcelona are positive. Guests of the city remain very impressed and recommend a visit to this magnificent place.

The negative factors are hot weather, a large concentration of tourists and the lack of ancient monuments. In addition, after an incendiary show of fountains, it is very difficult to get into the subway, since most of the spectators go to the subway. The most convenient option is to rent a hotel room near the square.
