The Decembrists Movement: A Brief History of Organizations

Why did the Decembrists come together in secret communities? First, the emergence of Decembrist organizations was influenced by the ideas of the enlightenment of revolutionary France. Views on the structure of the state are reflected in the charters of the communities. Secondly, having been in overseas campaigns after defeating Napoleon, the Decembrists recognized the European way of life. These trips convinced them that they could live much better. Thirdly, during the same World War II, the Decembrists became more familiar with the main population of our country - peasants. They learned better their way of life and life, which led the conspirators to the realization of the need for change. And fourthly, the movement of the Decembrists, which is briefly discussed in the article, was greatly influenced by the indecision of Alexander the First in carrying out the reforms.



The organizations of the Decembrists begin to be created two years after the great campaigns abroad. So, already in 1816 a secret society was created - the Union of Salvation. It includes guard officers who develop ideas for a new state structure. This society does not have its own charter and program, and therefore quickly disintegrates. Following it, the Union of Welfare is created. This organization is more successful: a clear composition of participants is determined, its own program appears. The community exists for two years, after which it breaks up. Now comes the time of the legendary Southern and Northern communities. The Southern Society of Decembrists led Pestel. Their program was called "Russian Truth" and consisted of the following conditions: the overthrow of the autocracy, of course, the abolition of serfdom, the creation of a legislative people's governing body.
