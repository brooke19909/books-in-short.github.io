The king ordered - the people did. And the Egyptian city, which is only 20 years old, has long and firmly held the laurels of one of the best resorts in the world, and does not intend to give them away.

The special charm of Sharm El Sheikh





The Egyptian leader was not mistaken, indicating the location of the future settlement. The city of Sharm el-Sheikh is protected from strong winds by the island of Tirana and many bays. Therefore, even in the season of strong winds and sea currents, the resort is quite comfortable. In the summer, from April to September, the air temperature ranges from 22 to 38 degrees Celsius. From October to March, in winter, the air warms up to +23. The maximum water temperature is 26 degrees in summer and winter.

Monastery of St. Catherine

For those guests who are not used to sitting in one place, Verginia Sharm Hotel offers an excursion to the monastery of St. Catherine at the foot of the Sinai Mountain. It is 190 km from the hotel.

It was erected in the VI century A.D. by decree of the Byzantine emperor Justinian, after the foundation he received the name of the Monastery of the Transfiguration or the Monastery of the Burning Cupina (this shrine is located on its territory).

The history of the construction of the monastery is as follows. Hermit monks looking for the relics of one of the first saints in the highlands, Catherine of Egypt, and discovering Mount Moses, repeatedly asked the emperor to erect a temple in this place. Justinian‚Äôs assistant, having received instructions on the construction, decided to build a thick-walled monastery not at the top of the mountain, but down into the gorge, where it was much safer to repel the raids of enemies. Historical chronicles indicate that the assistant was executed. However, for the entire time the monastery existed, it turned out to be the patronage of the greats of this world. In times of war and armed conflict, this fact acted as a reliable defense of the monastery.

In the XI century, the monastery was renamed the monastery of St. Catherine in honor of the Great Martyr, who lived from 287 to 305. AD According to legend, she was incredibly beautiful, intelligent, from her youth believed in Christ, converted many around to Christianity, for which she even became incapable of her own father. Emperor Maximin executed Catherine.

According to legend, her body was transferred by angels to the top of Sinai. The monks who discovered the remains were identified by the ring that Jesus Christ gave to Catherine. Since then, the relics of the saint rest in the church of the monastery and are available for worship every day.

In 2002, the monastery was included in the UNESCO list of World Heritage Sites.
