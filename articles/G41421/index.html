Famous and amazing sights of Hanover

Hanover occupies a special place among the famous German cities. Initially, it is the merit of the countless festivals and exhibitions that take place here. But you can not go past the famous museums that are the owners of the richest collections of works of art.



Hanover Attractions: Old Town Hall

Its construction took almost 100 years. The oldest part of it dates back to 1410, then the Pharmaceutical and Market wings were attached to it. The monument amazes with Gothic carvings, frescoes depicting portraits and coats of arms of the Grand Dukes, as well as showing medieval life, games and folk entertainment. Its facade depicts Til Uhlenshpiegel, a popular German prankster, showing the language to passers-by. This image is intended to protect the Town Hall from evil.

Hanover Attractions: New Town Hall

With its splendor, the Town Hall takes us to the time of the kingdom of Hanover. Although in reality it opened only in 1913.

At the moment, in her hall you can see 4 models of Hanover, which represent him during the Middle Ages, at the beginning of the XX century, in the ruined form of the war period and in modern condition. You can also get to the observation deck under its dome, which offers amazing views of the city.

Hanover Attractions: Opera House

The opera was created by G.F. Laves in 1845-52 as the Royal Court Theater. Near the building there are two wings that belong to the period of late classicism. The opera house burned out during the war, after which it was reconstructed. At the moment, it is one of the best European theaters, with stage equipment and excellent acoustics.





Hanover Attractions: St. Aegidius Church

Here the main events of the Reformation of the city took place. This is one of the greatest architectural monuments of medieval architecture that can be seen in the city of Hanover, Germany. The sights of the city were mostly destroyed during the war, the same thing happened with this church.

Hannover Attractions: Herrenhausen Garden

The famous garden appeared thanks to Sophia the Palatinate. The meeting at Versailles with Louis XIV made such an impression on her that Sophia ordered the French-style garden to be set up in her homeland.

400 thousand tourists come to admire this attraction. After the opening of the fantastic grotto called Nicky de Saint-Fall, there were a few more of them.
