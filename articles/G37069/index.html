Palm measurement technology

Knitting fingerless gloves with knitting needles according to the description drawn up by an experienced needlewoman is very simple. But most of the ready-made instructions use standard parameters. And it seems that the exact observance of each step of the master class does not allow mistakes. However, in this case, there is a risk that the accessory made by someone else‚Äôs standards will not fit in size - it will turn out to be large or, conversely, small. To prevent this, it is better to independently measure the palm for which the studied product will be connected. This is much simpler than it might seem. It is only necessary to prepare the main tool - a centimeter tape. And so that the parameters do not fly out of the head, they should be fixed on paper. We need to find out only five values:

  1. Girth of the wrist.
  2. The length of the main part of the accessory.
  3. The distance from the wrist to the base of the thumb.
  4. Girth of the thumb through the bone.
  5. Girth of the palm.

Step-by-step master class

Both experienced craftsmen and beginner fingerless gloves are knitted using the same technology. Let's consider it further:

  1. We begin to perform the studied accessory from the cuff, so we dial the following number of loops: palm girth in cm times 2.
  2. We distribute the loops on the hosiery knitting needles and knit an arbitrary number of rows with a single or double elastic band. Both options are a series of seamy and facial loops throughout the series. But in the first, the rapport is two loops, and in the second - four.
  3. Having connected the cuffs, we subtract the current number of loops from the necessary for girth of the palm.
  4. In the next row we add the missing, but not all at once, but evenly.
  5. Next, we begin to knit a wedge of the thumb. Using a thread of a different color, we separate four loops on the side. In each odd row, add two air ones - before and after the labeled ones. We knit all even rows without changes.
  6. We continue in this way until we form the desired size hole for the thumb.
  7. Then we tie the remaining number of rows to cover the distance from the wrist to the base of the thumb.
  8. Separate the loops for knitting the main part of the accessory, transfer the rest to the pins.
  9. We knit a fingerless glove of the required length. If desired, we knit several rows of elastic at the end.
  10. Close the hinges and return to the thumb hole. It can simply be tied or lifted into several rows. In the first case, we use a hook, collecting loops and creating a beautiful edge. In the second - we work with knitting needles, moving in a circle the desired number of rows.
  11. By analogy, we knit a pair of accessory. However, the thumb hole should be on the other side.

For readers who are interested in how to knit long fingerless gloves with knitting needles, it is important to clarify that the technology in this case will be identical. But you should start knitting cuffs with the number of loops equal to the girth of the arm in the place where the lower edge of the accessory will be located.
