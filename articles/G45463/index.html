Description of the mud lake in Golubitskaya

This therapeutic reservoir is located directly near the sea and has underwater communication with the sea coast. From above, the sea and the lake are separated from each other by a small sand-shell mound. During storms, sea spray, and sometimes waves, reach a small reservoir, throwing shells, small animals there. Subsequently, in stagnant water, microorganisms die and are deposited at the bottom of the mud lake. In Golubitskaya the length of the reservoir is approximately 500 meters and the width is 140. The depth is shallow - up to 1.5 m. On hot summer days there is an active evaporation of water, the lake is very shallow - up to 0.5 m.





During this period, maximum water salinity occurs (40 grams per 1 liter). In spring or autumn, frequent rains dilute the water, so that the level of minerals becomes minimal (10 grams per 1 liter).

Healing properties

The mud lake in Golubitskaya represents two main horizons. Upper black silt, which is a therapeutic mud, and another underlying. The second layer is dark gray, mixed with clay and sandy bottom.

The healing layer is 40 cm deep, and near the coast - 5 cm.

Black mud has a high hydrogen sulfide content. This explains the peculiar rotten smell of water. The solution also contains bromine, iodine, which only add effectiveness during treatment.





In the village of Golubitskaya, the mud lake is classified as silt strongly sulfide and medium saline. Compared to the similar Kuyalnitsky estuary in Odessa, the lake has more Fe (HS) 2 and organic matter. When the mud is in a solid state, it contains less calcium and magnesium carbonates, and almost no gypsum.

Contraindications

Before using the natural power of the mud lake in Golubitskaya, you need to carefully familiarize yourself with a number of contraindications so that there is no significant deterioration.
