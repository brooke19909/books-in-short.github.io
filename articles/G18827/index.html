Kazan River Station: History

From the very moment Kazan emerged, its development history has been closely connected with one of the largest rivers in Russia - the Volga. It was she who contributed more to the development of the economy and politics of the Tatars of the Volga regions. Thanks to this beautiful river, Kazan has become the center of intersection of trade routes of Asia and Europe. The Great Volga Trade Route, even under the Kazan Khanate, passed through this river.

The river port of Kazan has a curious origin.

From the Volga through the river. Bulak and Kazanka merchant ships caravans hit the bazaars of Kazan. And already after the Volga region and the Moscow state joined, the vessels of merchants from Western Europe began to come here, who went further along the Volga across Russia.

Peter I recognized the importance of this marina and established the Kazan Admiralty in 1718. Thanks to this, shipbuilding began to strengthen, and, thus, the Russian fleet began to gain strength. Shipyards Upper and Lower Uslon significantly increased the number of ships and vessels, both merchant and military.

In connection with the development of shipping, river ports began to be built. Among them is the river port of Kazan. Already at the end of the XIX century it was the second on the Volga in size after the port of Nizhny Novgorod. The movement of steamboats (passenger) quickly began to improve. And after the construction of the Volga-Baltic Canal was completed, Kazan began to be called the port of 5 seas.

Transformation of the river port: description

Today's Kazan river port has undergone significant changes. The modern marina, functioning today, appeared much later. A wonderful project was presented by the Moscow Institute for the Design of Transport Facilities (River).

New, very convenient and roomy port. The mooring wall extended for the whole kilometer. It should be noted that the Kazanka river was forced to change the old channel for the convenience of ships.



It has become very convenient to travel to St. Petersburg, Moscow and many other beautiful cities of Russia. In addition, the port of Kazan is located almost in the historical center of the city and is the largest on the Volga River.

An excellent view opens from the pier to the Kazan Kremlin, the famous Leaning Tower Syuyumbike and the world-famous Kul Sharif mosque in the Kremlin.

Kazan-Bolgar Cruise (river port)

Another of the most interesting monuments not only of Tatarstan, but also of Russia is the city of Bulgaria, which for 10-15 centuries determined the fate of the peoples of the Urals and Volga region, the Volga Bulgaria and the Golden Horde. The city played a large role in the history of all of Eastern Europe.



The skills of artisans-craftsmen of Bolgar in the manufacture of products were distributed throughout the Volga-Kama region.

In this historical place you can see beautiful white-stone buildings, preserved from the once crowded ancient majestic city of Bolgar. This place is sacred to the Tatar people, it is a place of pilgrimage and worship of Muslims who want to touch the shrines. Here you can find many legends associated with various historical facts.

Bulgar (the capital of the Volga Bulgaria) is the place where Islam was officially adopted in 922. In the X century, a coin was minted here for the first time, which became a symbol of the new state.
