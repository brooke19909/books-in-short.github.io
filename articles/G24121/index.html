What is sinking?

This needlework appeared in the 17th century in England. Masters used this pattern as a decoration for houses. Carnations were driven into the boards, the threads were wound in a certain sequence, and an unusual element was created. Subsequently, clove boards were replaced with cardboard, velvet paper with holes. And some craftswomen transferred it to a cloth.

From geometrical figures and lines magnificent pictures are made by isonia. Schemes of simple plots can be found in needlework books. Here is a little information on creating complex pictures.

Pictures by isonia: schemes of the main elements

Let's start with the circle. Draw a circle, divide it into even segments. By embroidering chords in a certain sequence, you get a filled or incomplete circle. Consider 3 ways to embroider a circle. On the template, you can draw a circle with a compass. In the finished picture, use prepared samples so that there are no unnecessary punctures. Mark on the circle 19 points, number them. Put the template on the foam, pierce the dots. The drawing and puncture are done from the wrong side. Next, insert the thread, make a large knot, drive the needle from the wrong side to the first point. According to this principle, all works in the technique of sinking go. Schemes for beginners:

Schemes of triangles and arcs

The pattern depends on the length of the triangles. The number of points must be the same. For example, the distance between the points can be through a centimeter on all sides or on one side the same number of points alternates after five millimeters.

Number the sides of the triangle. Start embroidering by pulling the needle from the end of one edge and entering a point from the corner of the other side. For example, from the first point of edge A on the front side, go to the second point of edge B. From the wrong side B, make a small stitch to the third point. On the front side, enter the fourth, etc.

From the triangles we get interesting pictures. Izon with schemes of complex circles consisting of triangles allows you to get beautiful abstract balls. Only in this case, number all three sides of the figure. Examine the pattern for embroidering the balls carefully. In one embodiment, the triangles are fully embroidered, while in another they form petals. Arcs are numbered twice, starting from the second point. Combine figures, get unusual plots.
