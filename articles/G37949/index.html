When is the best time to go to Iceland? Holidays in Iceland in winter and summer

Iceland is the world's largest volcanic island. An amazing place where ice and flame coexist is a popular tourist destination. If you do not know when it is better to go to Iceland, our article will help you understand this issue.

Reasons to visit Iceland

Not all travelers love warm countries and beach vacations. Some tourists are impressed by the cold beauty of the northern regions. Iceland is a very interesting area in this regard. This wonderful country surprises and fascinates with its natural beauties.

Iceland is an island nation located on the very edge of Europe. The country impresses guests with its northern exoticism. Here you can see contrasting phenomena such as millennial glaciers and hot geysers, roaring waterfalls and lavender fields, beautiful national parks and rocky fjords. An amazing country can impress even the most seasoned travelers.

According to agencies, currently there is a tendency to increase the interest of tourists in Iceland. But still, the flows of foreign travelers cannot be called powerful. Not as many tourists enter the country as we would like. Probably the considerable cost of the rest and flight affects. And the tourist season on the island does not last long - from June to September. Many inexperienced travelers wonder: "When is the best time to go to Iceland?" Much depends on what exactly you want to see in this amazing country.

It was at this time that the largest number of travelers came to the country, because in the summer warm weather is established on the island. Low season

When it is better to go to Iceland, you decide. The low season on the island, by our standards, begins very early. In mid-September, most hotels are closed because weather conditions are getting worse, and buses run less and less. But rest in Iceland in winter is much cheaper than in summer. Hotels that receive guests in the winter significantly reduce the price in the low season. The cost of living is getting cheaper by 30-40 percent. Yes, and renting a car in winter will be much cheaper. Having a car will allow you to freely move around the island. The only thing that can interfere with movement is snowdrifts, so the car must be selected wisely so that it can move along snowy tracks.

The best period for excursions

Tours to Iceland are preferred by those people who want to escape to the ends of the world in order to relax their soul and body. The island is located near the Arctic Circle and is the northernmost European country. The fantastic landscapes of Iceland are the craters of active and extinct volcanoes. Such landscapes seem ethereal to guests.

In order to fully admire all these beauties, it is better to go on a vacation to Iceland in the summer. At this time of the year, warmer weather sets in, and local landscapes delight guests with lush greenery.

Climate of Iceland

To accurately characterize Iceland, it should be said that it is incredibly contrasting. This becomes clear at a glance at the landscapes of the island, where hot springs and glaciers peacefully coexist. The climate of Iceland is quite severe, and the weather is unpredictable. Most of the island is covered with glaciers. If not for the warm Gulf Stream, then the country would be subarctic. But in Iceland, thanks to the Atlantic, mild winters and cool and short summers. The local climate is considered useful despite the fact that winds blow throughout the year. The average life expectancy of the local population is 79.5 years.

In spring, nature comes to life on the island, and ice floes off the coast. The air temperature reaches +7 ... + 10 degrees. Birds return to Iceland from the south, and the landscapes become more colorful. The meltwater of the glaciers intensively nourish the waterfalls, which are gaining full strength. In spring, the weather remains unstable and changes very quickly.

Summer season

During the summer period in Iceland comes the time of white nights. The warmest time on the island is July. At this time, the air temperature reaches +15 ... + 20 degrees. July and August in Iceland is the peak of the high season. According to experts, it is at this time that it is best to visit the Blue Lagoon. The spa will delight with its open-air hot springs.

It is worth noting that there are practically no trees in Iceland. But there is a lot of greenery in large greenhouses and greenhouses heated by underground sources. A tourist is definitely worth a visit to the Eden Park, which is loved by locals. Despite the scarcity of the plant world, with the advent of summer, the rarest birds that live here flock to the island.

Autumn in Iceland

September on the island can be considered a relatively "summer" month. But in October there is a sharp deterioration in the weather, which is explained by the arrival of cyclones. In November, a dark period begins. At this time, the sun is low, and the duration of daylight hours does not exceed five hours. For this reason, the cost of tours from September to April is significantly reduced. But at the same time, the nature on the island at that time is majestic and beautiful. A lot of ash is applied to the snowless endless fields by the wind. The volcanoes of Iceland erupt directly under the ice. Such a sight is something amazing!

Winter

There is no polar night on the island. But the sun is at a height of several degrees. This period lasts from November to early February. In Iceland in winter you can admire the northern lights. Active volcanoes, giant glaciers and fjords fascinate with their beauty.

Rains are a common occurrence in Iceland. Most of them fall in autumn and winter. There are no heavy downpours and thunderstorms on the island. Precipitation falls in the form of a light drizzle. Far more inconvenient are the winds that blow here all the time. On average, the temperature in winter does not drop below zero degrees. But this does not mean that such a temperature always prevails. At night, the temperature usually drops to -4 degrees. In the coldest periods, the island does not fall below -10 degrees. Snow falls less often in cities, but it melts quickly thanks to the Gulf Stream. In the inner part of the island, more severe colds are possible. The temperature here can drop to -20 degrees.

Fishing season

The island has developed fishing sport. There are so many fish that it is enough for all tourists. The sea in Iceland is an ideal place for fishing, no one returns without a catch. The reason for this is the warm Gulf Stream, which brings schools of fish. The river fishing season lasts from June to October. At the same time, salmon is best caught. You can go to sea all year long, but you should pay attention to the weather.

Whale watching season

Oddly enough, but Iceland is one of those countries that attracts nature lovers. On its coast you can watch such amazing creatures as whales. The best time to watch them is from mid-spring to September.

Places such as Hervey and Fahsaflows are most attractive in this respect.

Valley of Geysers

In the south of the island is the famous Valley of Geysers. This place is an open-air thermal spa. Near the sources are equipped institutions for the treatment of various ailments. Healing water has a rejuvenating and healing effect. Blue Lagoon is considered one of the symbols of the country. The average temperature of the water in the springs is approximately +37 degrees. In winter, locals come here to keep warm. It is believed that the best effect of thermal baths is observed after taking them in cold and cloudy weather. In the summer, bathing is quite difficult.

Not far from the Valley of Geysers is the active volcano Hekla. The most famous geyser of the valley is the Big Geyser, or Geysir.

It is worth noting that local residents actively use hot springs. The famous valley heats 85% of residential buildings, which makes it possible not to use oil products.
