It is rich in cities with a bright reputation of Russia! Nizhny Tagil is one of them. Many associate it with prisons and camps. But not only in places not so remote and Siberian ores this city is remarkable.

Geographic characteristics

The city of Nizhny Tagil is located almost on the border separating Europe from Asia. To the point of intersection of two continents from it only some 20 kilometers. And to the heart of the Urals - Yekaterinburg - 150 km. Refers to the Sverdlovsk region. Index Nizhny Tagil is easy, memorable. It starts at 622 and then the post office code is attached to it.



The city is located in a mountainous area - in the middle section of the eastern slope of the Ural Range. Its area is just over 297 square kilometers. In the urban area there are three mountains that smoothly pass into the plains of the West Siberian lowlands in the east.

Nizhny Tagil is not deprived of water resources. Fourteen rivers flow through it! The largest of them is called Tagil.

Near the city are the largest ore deposits - iron and magnetite. Pyrite, limestone and some other minerals are also mined here.

The climate in these places is severe - Siberian or, in scientific terms, sharply continental. Cold winters, lasting six months, and short cool summers (what can be called heat lasts from the strength of a week or two). It rains almost constantly. Unusual weather in Nizhny Tagil in the spring - within one day the thermometer can "jump" twenty degrees up or down. And the average annual temperature does not exceed 1.7 degrees.

History of Nizhny Tagil

Nizhny Tagil officially keeps its chronicle since 1722, although "life was born" on its territory much earlier. So, for example, it is known that at the end of the sixteenth century Ermak stood here with his army.

After more than a century, the tidbit of the Ural territory was given by Peter the First oligarchs by today's standards - the Demidov family, who built an ‚Äúiron-making‚Äù plant on land abundant in minerals. With this step, the sovereign pursued the goal of strengthening Russia in military-technical terms.



The first smelting of cast iron dates back to October 8, 1722. It is this day that historians consider the beginning of the existence of Nizhny Tagil.

Vyiska smelter was the largest in the Russian Empire. But the village around him for a long time remained small, quiet, unpretentious. In addition to the uprising of Pugachev and Yulaev, as well as the hot "red-white" battles during the civil war, this area is practically not remarkable in historical terms.

After the revolution, the village began to develop more actively. The construction of new plants and factories required the expansion of infrastructure, and in 1931 Nizhny Tagil was given the status of a city.

During the Second World War, like all of Russia, Nizhny Tagil made every effort to defeat the enemy. The Ural Industrial Center has become a haven for Soviet metallurgy. The legendary T-72 tank was produced here.

By the end of the eighties, the city had grown very much, having grown by as much as 70% in some 50 years.



Population

One of the most important characteristics of any city is its population. Nizhny Tagil is home to 356,744 people (data for 2015). In the last few decades, the demographic picture here was depressing - mortality exceeded the birth rate, people left. If we recall that back in 1988 the population of Nizhny Tagil had 425 thousand in number, it turns out that the city lost almost 70 thousand people in forty years.

Today, the situation has stabilized somewhat - at least, local authorities declare this. The birth rate is promoted by the practice of allocating maternal capital, and mortality is reduced by improving the environmental situation and improving the quality of medical services.

What does the population do here? Nizhny Tagil, as you know, is a city of large industrial enterprises. Therefore, the bulk of the Tagil people are workers. A lot of pensioners live in the city (harmful industry involves early retirement), as well as people with disabilities, children who have lost the bread-winner, and representatives of other socially unprotected categories of citizens. But there are few entrepreneurs in Nizhny Tagil. People are afraid to be left alone with economic instability and complicated legislation, so they prefer to stick to state or large private enterprises.

Economy

Nizhny Tagil keeps afloat at the expense of two enterprises - the Nizhny Tagil Metallurgical Combine and the Ural Carriage Works. They are city-forming and provide jobs for about 55 thousand people. Another flourishing plant is UralChemPlast, which many locals dream of getting on.

In addition to heavy industry, trade is very developed in the city. A large number of supermarkets, markets, shopping centers, etc. need a constant influx of workers. By the way, thanks to such serious competition in terms of price, Nizhny Tagil is quite an attractive city. Food and manufactured goods here can be bought a little cheaper than in neighboring smaller settlements.



The city is connected by rail with the main megalopolises of the country and has its own airport. Flights are made both within Russia and abroad.

Crime

There is an opinion that Nizhny Tagil is the criminal capital of the Russian Federation. And there is some truth in such conviction. After all, the city is surrounded by "zones", and, having served their sentence, their "inhabitants", as a rule, remain in Tagil. In addition, the Gulag prisoners, who also later became Tagil, were driven to the construction of the city. And genetic memory is an important thing.

But, in fairness, it should be noted that if a couple of decades ago the criminal situation here could be called a stalemate and even the execution of a dozen people did not cause local special emotions, then today there is still no such rampant crime.

Interesting facts about Tagil

Few people know that the city called Nizhny Tagil on the map of Russia could not have existed if not for ... bureaucracy. In 1861, the industrialists Demidov filed an application to rename it to Aleksandrovsk. But the Russian bureaucratic machine moved so slowly that the case got stuck, but it was never completed.

More interesting facts: malachite, mined at a local mine, adorns the walls of the Winter Palace (Malachite Hall), and Tagil steel went to the construction of many skyscrapers in Chicago and New York.

Well, and, of course, the information about the T-72 tank, which is the most massive combat vehicle in the history of the planet, deserves attention. He bears the title "Kalashnikov among the tanks", and Nizhny Tagil is sometimes called "Tank-hail."
