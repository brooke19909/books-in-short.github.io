Railway

This is the cheapest option for organizing a trip from Minsk to Molodechno. There are many long-distance trains and suburban trains running between the cities. The first ones are more expensive, since some of them are international, which are formed by Russian Railways and UZ (Ukrainian railways). They can follow from Kiev to Riga or from Baranovichi to Kaliningrad. It takes about an hour to go on such trains from the capital to Molodechno, but buying tickets is unprofitable, too expensive per kilometer. For example, in the train Moscow - Kaliningrad, the tariffs in Russian rubles are as follows:

  • Reserved seat - from 300 rubles.
  • Kupeyny - from 1500 rubles.
  • Sleeping - from 5400 rubles.

The exchange rate of the Belarusian ruble may change, at the moment it is approximately equal to 30 Russian.

Bus

Buses and minibuses from Minsk to Molodechno depart from the central bus station. They are in transit from 70 minutes to two hours. The first flight leaves at 6 in the morning, and the last at 8 in the evening. The destination for the bus can be not only Molodechno, but also the city of Pastavy or the resort village on Lake Naroch.

Car

A distance of 75 kilometers from Minsk to Molodechno can be reached by car in about an hour. The exact time of the trip will depend on traffic jams and weather. You need to move northwest of Minsk along the M-7 and R-28, through the city of Zaslavl. There will be no other large settlements along the way.

What to see in Molodechno?

The city is an ordinary Belarusian regional center without any special attractions. This is not such a famous tourist city as Polotsk or Nesvizh. There are no castles or their ruins, as in Lyubcha or Navahrudak.

When walking around Molodechno, you should pay attention to the following objects:

  • Orthodox churches and a modern Catholic church, which is interesting in that its towers are decorated with an ornament in the style of the Belarusian cornflower.
  • Central square. It houses the Kupalye fountain with beautiful nude sculptures. Opposite the square is a park with a memorial to those killed during the Second World War.
  • The memorial complex "Stalag".
  • Museum of Local Lore. It has a good historical exposition illustrating different periods of the city‚Äôs history.
  • The local Arbat is Pritytsky Street.
  • Photogenic sculptures, for example, a herald and a slender passenger near the station.
