Edge, not outskirts





The word "land" by the meaning of "piece, piece of land" has been known since the days of the Old Slavic language. And today this word exists in many Slavic languages, because Slavic tribes always had lands that were separated by natural borders - a river, a forest, a swamp. Therefore, this word also had a meaning - the extreme part of the territory, the beginning or end of the land of the tribe.

Where did the name Ukraine come from is very interesting. In the Old Slavic period, the word kraina (country) was born in the meaning - the territory that belongs to the tribe. Next to the word "land" in the Old Slavonic language there was the word "steal", which meant - a piece from a piece, a distant piece of land, the extreme boundary of a remote part of the tribe's territory.

Lithuania and Poland

Starting from the middle of the XIV century, many principalities of Kievan Rus, from which the Ukrainian people later formed, fell under the domain of Lithuania and Poland. Since then, the name Ukraine has also been applied to territories that fall under this power. Near Lithuania were Chernigov, Kiev, Pereyaslav and most of the Volyn principality, and everything was called Lithuanian Ukraine, and under Poland came Galitsky, part of Volyn and the lands were called Polish Ukraine.

With the advent of the Cossacks, the Dnieper lands began to be called Cossack Ukraine. The history of Ukraine recalls this in songs - "oh in the mountains, in the valleys, in the Cossack Ukraine ..."

Khmelnitsky





During the military operations of Ukrainians against the Poles under the leadership of Bogdan Khmelnytsky (1648-1654), not only Zaporizhzhya lands were called Ukraine, but also all the Dnieper lands. Most likely, starting from this moment, the whole country began to be called Ukraine. Then this name spread to the Eastern Slavic lands, as well as to Slobozhanshchina, which for a short time was called Sloboda Ukraine. And the western lands were called Russia for a long time, but soon the name Ukraine became native to the inhabitants of the entire ethnic state of Ukrainians.

Ukraine - where does the name come from? Over time, the initial geographical concept of Ukraine became a national idea, which united such lands as Polesye, Sivershchina, Slobozhanshchina, Donbass, the Black Sea region, Volhynia, Podolia, Bukovina, Carpathian and Transcarpathia.

Therefore, for Ukrainians it was very important that the word "Ukraine" meant the name of the country created by Bogdan Khmelnitsky. History





History ... We can hear this word every day, but we don‚Äôt think at all about its meaningfulness. As a result of the fact that Ukraine gained independence, Ukrainians began to be interested in the history of their people. Indeed, only knowledge of the past will make it possible to understand the present and build a bright future. An important role in understanding your story is played by saved memos. Museums are called upon to preserve the historical memory of the people for future generations and study it.

The history of Ukraine and the people that inhabit this territory are rooted in ancient times. The sites of the very first man were seen on the territory of independent Ukraine many hundreds of thousands of years ago, in the era of the early Paleolithic. Man conquered these territories and the natural wealth of the subsoil from nature. From the primitive forms of gathering, hunting and fishing, he moved on to farming and cattle breeding. Representatives of Trypillian culture left a very important mark in the difficult history of Ukraine in the 4th-3rd centuries BC. Tripoli were the most civilized representatives of the human race in the Neolithic era. They mainly engaged in agriculture, pottery, and construction. As a result of the expansion of the nomads and the cooling of the climate, this culture gradually disappeared. After that, the Sarmatians, the Kemmerians, and the Scythians lived in the Ukrainian territories. Greek peoples had a very big influence on the peoples who inhabited the vast expanses of Ukraine.

East Slavs

The roots of the Eastern Slavs are not well understood today. The pre-Slavic period is associated with the emergence on the right-bank forest-steppe Dnieper of the Zarubinets culture, which is a joint for all Slavs. For the first time, the Slavs are recalled in the works of Tacitus, Ptolemy under the name "Wends". Starting from the VII century, there are already memories of the Slavs. The early Slavs settled mainly along the banks of rivers and lakes. Their huts were wooden and clay. The public spirit of the early Slavs is characterized by a transition from the primitive to the military tribal. Then power is transferred by right of inheritance. The life and work of the Eastern Slavs has always been closely connected with nature and family. This laid the foundation for the culture of the Slavs.

Culture

Folk culture in the traditions of the Ukrainian people played a role. In ancient times, when the feudal peak of society adopted Catholicism and European culture, and the peak of Cossack foreman became Russified, the Ukrainian community developed without a national cultural elite. And only the broad masses remained to carry the culture, which at that time was popular. The main place in the culture was taken by folklore, namely folk traditions and color. All this was very clearly visible in folk songs, thoughts. Thanks to the people, it became possible to raise Ukrainian culture in the 16th-17th centuries and revive in the 19th century.

Many talented Ukrainians have contributed to the Polish, Russian, world culture as a whole. This became possible thanks to the original education system, thanks to which the population was mostly literate. And the role of Ukraine-Rus as a center of Christianity among the Eastern Slavs became huge. The higher education system was especially developed. Ukrainian culture was open to the world, it lacked xenophobia and humanism was present. A huge contribution to the world heritage was made by such philosophers, poets and famous personalities as Skovoroda, Prokopovich, Kulish, Shevchenko and many others.
