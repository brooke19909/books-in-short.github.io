Remembering the old days: how to make a paper bomb

Earlier, in those days when dads and mothers of modern youth wiped their pants at school, a paper bomb was considered a very popular entertainment. This simple craft in those days was created from drafts and notebook sheets, since they were in large numbers in children. A paper water bomb was made in a matter of minutes, and could bring joy for several hours. In the hot season, children threw themselves with such blanks as snowballs, accompanying this action with laughter and screams. When colliding with a target, the bomb exploded, in other words, burst. The victim was most often wet through, but in summer this circumstance brings relief rather than problems.

The elders taught a "trifle" on how to make a paper bomb. Thus, this technology entertained more and more new generations. But with the advent of computers, everything has changed. Today, children prefer to spend time behind the monitor, rather than on the street. They are not at all interested in learning how to make a paper bomb. They rarely walk even in the summer, perfect for swimming in the river and running around the garden.

Nevertheless, any child from two entertainments will surely choose the one that will bring more joy and pleasure. And it is quite possible that if parents show their child how to make a paper bomb and how to use it, then it will prefer a more active lesson. It is best to present this material before leaving for any body of water. In this case, the child will have a great opportunity to practice a new skill and better learn how to make a paper bomb.

But what if adults forgot about this technology? Do not despair! Our step-by-step lesson on how to make a paper bomb will help you freshen up the material and provide a good family vacation.

For work, we need a square sheet of paper. Add it diagonally. Then expand.





Now we repeat the same with the second diagonal.





Fold the square in half.





And concave the upper corners inward as follows.





Now on both sides we are doing the following operation. We bend the lower corners of the triangle so that they touch the upper one.





This step is also performed on both sides. We bend the lateral corners of the square to the center line.





Now we fill the tails (4 pieces) into the pockets as shown in the photo.





Here is what should happen.





In order to add volume to our model, you need to blow into a small hole located at the bottom of the product.





Our bomb is ready. In order for it to become watery, it is necessary to draw liquid into it. This can be done using a thin trickle from the tap or use a tube. To do this, insert one end of it into the same hole through which we inflated the figure, and substitute the second under the water.





That's all, now our water bomb is ready for use, and you are ready for an active holiday near the reservoir.
