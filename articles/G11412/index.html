Cassette air conditioner: features and specifications

Cassette air conditioner is an equipment focused on eliminating excess moisture, heat, dust, and harmful fumes from the air in a room. This type of device turned out to be very effective when working in large rooms, where the ceiling height is three meters or more. Such requirements are due to the fact that the cassette air conditioner is usually placed behind a mandatory suspended ceiling, taking over a certain fraction of the height of the room. The installation of such a finishing structure "steals" 0.25-0.5 meters in height.



This space is usually enough for hidden placement in it of the internal block of the cassette system. The invisibility of the installation of this element can be achieved due to the fact that in the lower part the cassette air conditioner is comparable in size to one or two ceiling tiles. From below, the entire structure is covered with decorative grilles that do not contradict the design of the ceiling of the room. Typically, a grille with distribution louvers has the form that does not attract attention.



Cassette air conditioning involves the placement of an external unit outside the building. Through the lower part is the supply of purified and cooled air. Air dampers are designed to regulate the flow of flows in certain directions. Due to this feature of the device, it is possible to carry out a quick and very uniform mixing of chilled air with warm air available in the room. The cassette air conditioner has such a main advantage as a uniform distribution with high speed in all directions.



All these advantages make it possible to guarantee that in a room where many people congregate, the required microclimate parameters are collected. That is why such blocks are used in offices, shops and shopping centers, because this equipment is recognized as the most effective. Cassette devices can have different power, which is in the range of 2-16 kilowatts.
