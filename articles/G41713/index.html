Knitting scheme for stoles with explanations and notation

Palatine is a type of cape covering the back and shoulders. The length of the stole varies from 1.5 to 2.5 m. Such a scarf can completely cover the shoulders and back or, if the length allows, several times wraps around the body. Hand knitting the stole with knitting needles, the schemes and description of which are carefully disassembled, is an exciting lesson for the knitter.

Palatine "Openwork"

The knitting pattern for the stole is simple and straightforward. The pattern is formed due to the alternation of simple loops - front, back, yarn, knitted loops together. The pattern is formed from a small rapport, which is convenient for calculating the width of the stole. To make it bigger, repeated repetition of rapports (10-15) is used, a narrower scarf will be obtained by applying up to 10 rapports in knitting.

Abbreviations:

  • lp - front loop;
  • I.P. - purl loop;
  • n - yarn;
  • 3 vm. - three loops are knitted together with a front loop with an inclination to the left side.

The last look fits like this: one loop is removed unbind, the next two are knitted together over the front walls. Then the formed loop is pulled through the removed one.





Loops in asterisks make a rapport of 6 loops.

Description in order:

  • 1 - edge, * 3 hp, n., 3 vm., N. *, Etc .;
  • 2 - all loops are wrong;
  • 3 - edge, * n., 3 vm., N., 3 hp, n., 3 vm., N. *, Etc .;
  • 4 - all wrong.

Palatine "Airy"

This is an interesting knitting pattern for stoles and quite light. The scarf knitted in this pattern is delicate and airy. For knitting, it is recommended to choose mohair, thin wool or angora. A fluffy thread is perfect for this pattern - the impression of frosty frost on the branches of trees. The work is carried out strictly according to the drawing. The scheme of knitting stoles with needles is quite laborious. If you follow the pattern correctly, you will get a beautiful delicate pattern.





Legend:

  • vertical dash - front loop;
  • horizontal dash - purl loop;
  • the letter "l" with a die to the left - 2 loops knitted together with a front loop with an inclination to the left side;
  • the letter "l" with a die to the right - 2 loops knitted together with a front loop with an inclination to the right side;
  • circle - yarn.

Palatine "Easy"

An unusual airy knitting pattern for palatine knitting offers to master the "leaves" pattern. This pattern is perfect for knitting light and delicate items - scarves, wraps, blouses for the warm season. The pattern is delicate, airy. A large number of crochets, which alternate with loops of the front and wrong sewing, form an interweaving of symbolic leaves on the branches of yarn. This pattern looks good when made from cotton yarn or kidmoher. These threads are elastic and well suited for knitting openwork stoles.
