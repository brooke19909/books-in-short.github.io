Passion for the culture of Ancient Egypt

At the beginning of the 19th century, a craze began in Russia in Ancient Egypt, and St. Petersburg was no exception. Increased attention to the bygone civilization led to the fact that a bridge decorated in the style of the country of pyramids was built across the Fontanka. In our article, we will consider interesting facts related to the construction of the building, about which many ancient legends are composed, and we will describe in detail where the Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg is located.



Unusual bridge

The project was unusual for the city handsome masters who created many attractions. In 1825, the construction of a picturesque bridge began, which differed in appearance from the present. All guests were greeted by gates painted with hieroglyphs, and the unusual Egyptian ornament surprised with its color. The Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg was installed on supports faced with granite, and the entire structure was supported by three powerful chains.

Of particular interest were the four sphinxes, on whose heads a high golden crest is worn, and the appearance of the mythical animal was more reminiscent of Greek sculptures. Egyptian creatures were portrayed with male facial features, rather than female, as in this embodiment.



One of many legends tells that the face of the sphinx copies the face of the extremely attractive wife of Emperor Alexander I.

Funeral Songs

On August 25, the Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg, decorated with hieroglyphs, was inaugurated. The people called this bridge ‚Äúsinging‚Äù because, when walking, we heard strange mournful sounds that made chains. And many noted that the design in a fashionable style sang to itself a funeral song.

Collapse and its versions

In the winter of 1905, the chain structure collapsed under the squadron of the cavalry regiment, and only by fortunate accident not a single person was injured. Only four gloomy sphinxes looked at what was happening.

Then the inhabitants remembered the gloomy sounds and concluded that this is real rock that pursues the structure. And experts considered the main culprits of the incident miscalculations in the design.



However, the true reasons that the strong chains are broken are unknown. Builders who repeatedly repaired the bridge noted the good condition of the structure as a whole. The military was accused of swinging the structure due to a well-coordinated step in the foot, and in many textbooks the collapse of the structure is associated with the so-called resonance effect.

Eyewitnesses to the tragedy claimed that the soldiers did not go over the bridge, but rode horses, so this version is considered very doubtful. Contemporaries say the main cause of the accident is the fragility in the cold of the metal.

Mystical legends

The Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg since its inception has been surrounded by various legends and rumors. The people did not skimp on fantasy, and the most fantastic explanations of the incident were put forward. They remembered a girl deceived by the military, who in her hearts wished to fall under the ground to her offenders.

They said that the granite blocks that faced the Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg were removed from the walls of the notorious moat in front of Mikhailovsky Castle. Ostensibly, stones were originally brought from ancient temples where mystical rituals were held, and pagan gods brought down their fury on people.



Another version of the catastrophe is associated with the curse of the Sphinxes, although they had nothing to do with Ancient Egypt, but the people still attributed supernatural power to them. The sounds of chains that held the Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg, whose photo captivates with its beauty, were attributed by people to rumors to mystical creatures, endowing them with a prophetic gift.

Wooden building

For some time, traffic across the bridge ceased, but after the construction of a wooden crossing, which served faithfully for about 50 years, Petersburgers were restored again. Due to inconvenience, the question arose of the construction of a new bridge. In 1955, more than 20 projects were submitted, of which only one met all the requirements of the appearance.

New design with the same look

True, the design was simplified, but the design remained the same - in the Egyptian style. And the former sphinxes are watching with an indifferent facial expression over passing people. On a modern bridge, in order to avoid new troubles, the roadway slab is well fixed on nine frames.

In 1989, another incident happened. A truck hit a sphinx statue, a figure fell into a river and was seriously damaged. During the restoration it turned out that the heads of the sculptures had gilding, which wore off over time.



In 2004, the sphinxes returned to their original appearance, perhaps even in the hope that they would no longer make sad sounds, and would never cause the Egyptian bridge in St. Petersburg to be a tragedy.
