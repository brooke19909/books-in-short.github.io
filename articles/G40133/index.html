Museum-reserve "Mikhailovsky". Memorial Museum-Reserve of A. S. Pushkin "Mikhailovsky"

The life and work of the greatest poet Alexander Sergeevich Pushkin excite poetry lovers all over the world. From early childhood, we are fascinated by the tales and poems that engender in us love for everything that surrounds us, and interest in the poet himself.

Land Pskov



Hundreds of people come from all over the world to Pskov. Mikhailovskoye and its environs have long been united in the world-famous State Historical and Literary Memorial and no less revered natural landscape museum-reserve named after A.S. Pushkin. The museum has more than seventy attractions, cultural and historical monuments . Visitors to the reserve will be able to get acquainted with the life, work and environment of the Russian poet A.S. Pushkin. They better recognize the colorful life of the Russian village at the beginning of the 19th century, they will see the noble estate of Pushkin‚Äôs times, and they will hear how the Mikhailovsky Museum-Reserve of Pushkin was created.

The foundation of the famous museum-reserve dates back to the reign of Nicholas II. Then the village of Mikhailovskoye was bought from the heirs of the great poet, and it began to belong to the state. In 1908, a fire broke out and the estate burned down, but it was rebuilt in 1911 and a museum was opened in memory of the poet along with a colony for elderly writers. At that time, the museum‚Äôs researcher was the writer Timofeeva-Pochinkovskaya Varvara Vasilievna, who once worked as secretary to F. M. Dostoevsky. She played the role of a guide to the Pushkin Museum. The village of Mikhailovskoye suffered a lot, history loudly speaks of all the events that left their mark on the fate of the estate.

History tour



The actual date of foundation of the world-famous museum reserve is March 17, 1922, when the Council of People's Commissars that existed at that time issued a decree that the estate with the grave of Pushkin is a protected territory. Then it received the name "Museum-Reserve of A. S. Pushkin" Mikhailovsky "".

The museum includes:

  • Museum-estate "Mikhailovsky" (family estate of the poet's mother).
  • Museum-estate "Trigorskoe" (the estate of close friends of the poet).
  • Museum-estate "Petrovsky" ("family nest" of the poet‚Äôs great-grandfather).
  • Museum "Mill" in the village of Bugrovo (a water mill is still operating properly on the territory).
  • Museum "Pushkin Village", located in Bugrovo.
  • Grave of A. S. Pushkin, necropolis of the Hannibals - Pushkins family in the ancient Svyatogorsky monastery.
  • Scientific and cultural center located in the village of Pushkinskiye Gory.

Over the years, a special mystical Pushkin world has been formed on the territory of the reserve, which attracts more and more new fans.

No one is forgotten



The Mikhailovsky Museum has undergone many troubles. This is the February Revolution of 1918, when not one thousand manors of the Pskov region were ruthlessly burned. Among them, Trigorskoye, Petrovskoye, and Mikhailovskoye burned down. Only the small house of the nanny and the huge stone barn that Pushkin's son built were saved from the fire. In the years 1931-1934 in this protected area was a pig-breeding complex.

In the year of the centenary of the death of A. S. Pushkin (1937), the Mikhailovskoye Museum-Reserve was restored, but not for long, as the Great Patriotic War unleashed. The Germans captured the Pskov region, and the territory of the reserve was occupied. Part of the buildings of the village was completely destroyed, and part was disfigured, Mikhailovsky changed beyond recognition.

Second wind

The history of restoration began in 1945, when Semyon Stepanovich Geychenko became the director of the museum-reserve.



He is an ascetic who has gone through the war, a great enthusiast and admirer of the great poet, in addition, he is a museum worker and a native of Peterhof. This man was to restore numerous buildings from the ruins, to eliminate the dugouts, to put in order parks and the surrounding area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe reserve. S. S. Geychenko fulfilled his task brilliantly and even made sure that the territory of the historical museum-reserve began to look exactly the same as during the poet himself. He restored Trigorskoye, Mikhailovskoye, Petrovskoye, there were museums and the "Water Mill" in the village of Bugrovo, and the "Pushkin Village". Under him, the Assumption Monastery of Svyatogorsky was revived, which was given with great difficulty. This object caused special battles, and each time I had to prove that it was needed and important for posterity.

A.S. Pushkin is important for visitors to the reserve, and A.S. Pushkin and S.S. Geychenko are important for museum employees. There are legends about both of them.

Huge and invaluable contribution to the development of the museum S. S. Geychenko. His plans are being implemented until our time, the territory is expanding and the Museum Reserve is being transformed. Mikhailovsky is expanding, and in 1995 the estates Voskresenskoye and Golubovo were added. The ancient settlement Velje joined with the village of the same name.
