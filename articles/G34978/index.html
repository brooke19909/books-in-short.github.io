Prague in October: weather, sightseeing, tourist tips

Not everyone can go on vacation in the summer. Many find the opportunity to relax only in the fall, for example, in October. Some tourists do not want to go to tropical resorts, since a large number of tourists actually prefer to study the characteristics of a country, its history and culture. Europe is an excellent solution in this situation. A great place for such a vacation would be the Czech Republic. Prague in October offers a large selection of excursions and activities. So, what is the weather like in the city in the fall? What to take on a trip? We will talk about this later.

Climatic features of the Czech Republic

This is a country with a temperate climate, influenced by mountains that prevent the passage of cold air currents. Thus, in the Czech Republic it is usually not too hot in summer, and winters are moderately cold and humid.

Weather in Prague in October

In Prague, as a rule, moderate temperature indicators in any season. It is not very cold or hot here. The weather in Prague in October allows you not to think about whether you will freeze or it will become hot. Just put on a light sweater and you will feel good without worrying about temperature. Even at night in Prague this month there is less than 5 degrees of heat. Rains are usually short and do not interfere with walks.





Tourist season in Prague

Holidays in Prague in October will bring you not only colorful memories, but also become an inexpensive alternative to more popular places this season, especially if you are traveling with your family members.

When the summer season ends, travel agencies tend to reduce the cost of tours to the Czech Republic (a similar situation with airline tickets).

They also go to hotels that have to rethink pricing in the fall. Not so long ago, a weekend tour became popular among compatriot tourists. In addition to housing, it should include free guided tours of Prague.

Most of the excursions begin in the region of Hradcany. This is a lot of old streets that amaze with their beauty and bright colors. The center of the district is unchanged: Gradchan Square is considered to be such. The history of its foundation begins back in the 7th century. The main attraction of the square is considered the building of the Sternberg Palace, which today houses the National Gallery.

You must be prepared that the Old Town area involves a large number of walks. This is a kind of open-air museum. The date of its foundation dates back to the X century. The Old Town is located where the Old Town Square and the Old Town Hall are located. The building of the latter has a peculiar decoration in the form of astronomical clocks in which sculptures of famous historical figures move.

Prague in October is a great opportunity to visit the sights of the city, a list of which is not complete without the Cathedral of the Virgin, as well as the Jewish Quarter, where the construction of the Staronova Synagogue is located. The style of this cult building dates back to early Gothic. In the same place you should definitely take a walk along the Charles Bridge, where it is customary to make wishes.





In addition, tourists recommend visiting concerts in the Rudolfinum hall, where in October you can hear artists performing at the autumn festival of classical music.

Not far from the area where Tsepetnaya Street is located, Prague Castle is located. Various cultural events are often held there.

Prague in October also cannot do without visiting the city pubs, where you can taste the famous quality Czech beer.

Also, one of the attractions is the funicular, which rises to Petrshin hill. A romantic mood when walking around the city at this time of the year is provided for you.

Those who love wine drinks will also find something to do in Prague in October, as this month they hold festivals and celebrations of wine and winemaking in the city.

The last numbers in October were marked by the organization of the International Jazz Festival. The city hosts many concerts at various venues. On October 28, the Czech Republic also celebrates the holiday of the Republic. On this day, folk processions, concerts are held, and the population celebrates the triumph in numerous bars and restaurants.
