Self-firmware iPhone: program

Despite the fact that the iPhone is famous for its reliability, even with these devices problems sometimes occur. And if mechanical damage is entirely the fault of the users themselves, then software problems can occur at all for no apparent reason. So, for example, iPhone firmware can fail at any time, turning an expensive phone into a useless piece of plastic.

If such a nuisance also happened to you, then you just have to contact the service center or solve the problem yourself. Fortunately, Apple employees have made the process of flashing their devices as simple as possible, so that anyone, even untrained users, can handle it. And this article will help you with this.

What is firmware?

Almost any modern electronic device has its own operating system or its simplified version. Speaking specifically about the iPhone, then this is iOS. Depending on the year of their release, different versions of the operating system are installed on various models of smartphones from Apple, which, in turn, are divided into smaller editions. They are called iPhone firmware. In other words, different editions of the same iOS version can be installed on two identical devices.

In addition, iPhone firmware (or flashing) is often referred to as the process of updating or restoring the operating system. There may be several reasons for this operation, so each of them should be considered separately.

Why reflash an iPhone?

Most iPhone owners do not need to carry out any manipulations with the operating systems of their devices. Nevertheless, the need for this may appear in the following situations:

  • Hangs or spontaneous device reboots. Most likely, this happens due to malfunctions in iOS.
  • Viruses. Malicious software can cause serious harm to your smartphone. Including lead to problems with the operating system.
  • The smartphone has stopped starting. If this was not preceded by any mechanical damage, then, most likely, the iPhone firmware failed.
  • Desire to upgrade iOS to a newer version. Usually this operation is performed in automatic mode, but sometimes you have to do it manually.

In addition, the need for a device flashing may arise if you want to access functions that are not available in the basic version of the operating system.

Symptoms of firmware problems

It may happen that problems with your smartphone are caused by reasons that are not related to the operating system. Therefore, before proceeding with any actions, it is important to make sure that the firmware is damaged or completely out of order. Here are the main signs of this:

  • Often error messages began to appear. However, they are not associated with any specific applications and arise randomly.
  • The smartphone began to slow down and buggy. Sometimes this is accompanied by crashes from applications, spontaneous launch of various programs and similar problems.
  • The iPhone does not turn on, the device does not respond at all to the press of the Power button.
  • The phone starts up, but hangs on the Apple logo.
  • The device turns on, but displays a picture with a USB cable and iTunes icon on the screen.
  • The smartphone turns on, starts, but immediately goes into reboot.

In addition, it is important to inspect the device for mechanical damage. Particular attention should be paid to the battery.

Training

So, you finally decided to reinstall the operating system on your smartphone. You need to thoroughly prepare for this operation. Before installing the firmware on an iPhone, do the following:

  • Create a copy of all the important information stored on your device. Save photos, videos, documents, contacts, and other valuable files. During the flashing process, all this will be deleted. Of course, this is relevant only if your phone is in working condition.
  • Make sure your smartphone‚Äôs battery is fully charged. If the phone turns off during firmware, you will at least have to start all over again.
  • Inspect the USB cable for mechanical damage. The same applies to USB ports on the computer and the iPhone itself.
  • Check the stability of your internet connection. Due to problems with it, certain problems may also occur.

It would be nice to take into account such a factor as a sudden power outage. But this is a circumstance that does not depend on most users, so they will have to be neglected (well, or use an uninterruptible power supply).

Flashing options

Oddly enough, but there are several options for flashing the iPhone. More specifically:

  • Recovery Mode. Needed to restore the operating system. It is used if the phone has stopped functioning normally.
  • DFU Mode. It differs from Recovery Mode in that it bypasses the protective mechanisms of the operating system. Most often needed if the normal recovery mode does not work.
  • RedSnow. It is named after the program of the same name for iPhone firmware. Allows you to make the so-called jailbreak. Simply put, to access the functions of the device, deliberately disabled by the developers (for example, the file system).

As you can see, each of the methods has its own characteristics and is intended for different purposes. Here it is up to you to decide which one is suitable specifically for your situation.

Recovery mode

Regardless of which firmware you intend to install on an iPhone, in most cases Recovery Mode is suitable for this. This operation is performed as follows:

  • Turn off your smartphone and wait until the display goes blank.
  • Connect the device to the computer by holding down the Home key. The PC recognizes the phone and reports that Recovery Mode is running.
  • Launch iTunes. A window pops up asking you to restore your phone‚Äôs operating system. Click OK. If the program didn‚Äôt react to the device in any way, you need to click on the smartphone‚Äôs icon and manually select the ‚ÄúRestore iPhone‚Äù item, having previously pressed the Shift key.

DFU Mode

If for some reason it was not possible to flash your smartphone in Recovery Mode, then you can try to do it in DFU Mode. To do this, you first need to enable the mode of the same name, and this is done like this:

  • Connect the phone to the computer and disconnect it. Wait until the screen goes completely blank.
  • Hold the combination of the Power and Home buttons. Keep them in this state for at least 10 seconds.
  • Now release the Power button, but hold Home. Do not let it go until the computer recognizes your smartphone in DFU mode.

After this happens, you will need to start installing the firmware. This is done in the same way as in the case of using Recovery Mode.

  • Your smartphone screen will go blank. Do not be alarmed by this, and in no case disconnect the device from the PC. You need to wait until the message Done!

After that, the program will create a modified version of the firmware. You need to install it on your smartphone using Recovery Mode.

Possible problems

Sometimes it happens that the firmware is not installed on the device. Here are the most common causes leading to this problem:

  • You downloaded the wrong firmware.
  • There is no stable internet connection.
  • Your antivirus blocks iTunes.
  • The USB cable is damaged. In addition, it is advisable to use the original cable.

Also, problems may arise due to the fact that you are not using the latest version of iTunes. It is recommended to always update it before proceeding with the firmware of the smartphone.
