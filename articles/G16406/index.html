Caorle (Italy) Attractions

But to devote the whole vacation to sweet doing nothing and beach bliss is still not worth it. After all, Caorle has a glorious centuries-old history. It was founded in the 1st century BC, and archaeologists find evidence of the existence of people from the Bronze Age on this site. Caorle has always been an important strategic point that connected the Apennine Peninsula with the rest of Europe. All the narrow streets of the resort breathe antiquity. But there are real pearls among beautiful buildings. What you should definitely visit in Caorle? The city is under the Christian patronage of the First Martyr Stephen. Therefore, the cathedral is dedicated to this saint. The temple dates from 1038 and is made in the Byzantine Romanesque style. The facade of this ancient building is decorated with figures of saints. No less beautiful and inside. The central apse stores traces of 17th century frescoes. The walls of the cathedral are decorated with paintings by famous painters of the Venetian school, among which the Last Supper Giorgio Lazzarini stands out. A true masterpiece is the golden altar of Pala d'Oro. It was donated to the cathedral by the Queen of Cyprus Catherine Cornaro, who, after the shipwreck, found shelter in Caorle. It will be interesting to pious tourists that a relic is stored in the cathedral‚Äôs cancer - the skull of the martyr Stephen.

Other Caorle Attractions

Near the main temple of the city, a bell tower rises up 48 meters. It is unique in that it has an atypical cone-shaped spire. The bell tower itself, according to the canon of Romanesque architecture, has a cylindrical shape. It is ten years younger than the cathedral, that is, dates from the middle of the XI century. The bell tower should be admired not only from the outside - be sure to go inside, because there is a museum of church utensils. On the promontory overlooking the sea, near the port of Caorle in Italy, stands the Church of Our Lady. Its foundation is very ancient, but since the location of the building made it a victim of storms, only the construction of the eighteenth century has survived to this day. But the strict silhouette of the Church of the Virgin serves as a wonderful backdrop for beach photos.
