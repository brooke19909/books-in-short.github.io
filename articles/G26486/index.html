City Norilsk: population, climate, attractions

Often you can hear about a city like Norilsk. And no wonder, since this is a large settlement that deserves special attention. It occupies a fairly large territory, there are many industrial and industrial facilities. It is worth getting to know this wonderful city. Norilsk, whose population is small, has many interesting sights. The article will also discuss climate, nature, and interesting facts related to this locality.





Norilsk: general information about the city

The city is located in the Krasnoyarsk Territory, is the administrative center of the district. Interestingly, it ranks second in terms of the number of people living on its territory (Krasnoyarsk is in the first place).

The city is located in a rather severe climatic zone. Norilsk received the status of the northernmost city, in which a population of more than 150 thousand people lives.

However, his records do not end there. The city of Norilsk is among the settlements with the worst environmental conditions. This happened due to the fact that the main activities of the city are mining and metal production. This area has won a leadership position since there are no industrial zones of this magnitude anywhere else in the world.

An interesting fact is that the entrance to the city is closed to foreign citizens. To visit Norilsk, foreign tourists must go through the procedure for obtaining a special permit. Such measures have been in force since 2001. Prior to this, since 1991, foreign citizens have been able to freely visit Norilsk.





What areas are included in the city?

Previously, Norilsk was not such a large settlement. After the accession of neighboring cities to it, it became much larger. Norilsk consists of several areas:

  • Central.
  • Talnakh.
  • Kayerkan.
  • Snezhnogorsk.
  • Oganer.

All of them are noticeably different from each other. Some areas, such as Central, were built earlier, Leningrad architects were invited to design them. Others relate to a later period of time. Basically, they are built up with houses created according to standard designs.

National composition of the population

It is necessary to analyze in detail who lives in this region. The city of Norilsk boasts a rich national composition. Since ancient times, several indigenous peoples lived here, among which were the Nenets, Nganasans, Dolgans, and Enets. Now they can be found here quite rarely. As for the resident population, now in Norilsk mainly Russians, Ukrainians, Azerbaijanis and other peoples predominate. The indigenous population is almost gone. The city is mostly inhabited by people who moved here already in the middle and end of the 20th century, as well as their descendants.

A noticeable influx of population into this city was due to the extensive development of industry and other local enterprises. A large number of jobs were created here, as well as the infrastructure that made life in this place more comfortable.

Features of living in the city

Separately, it is worth paying attention to the characteristic features of local life, of which there are quite a lot. These features were formed over a long period, taking into account the climatic, weather and geographical conditions in which Norilsk is located. The population of the city in a special way relates to the preparation of food and its reception. Among the inhabitants of the city a large number of anglers and hunters. Due to this, a frequent dish is kebab and squiggle. The second is worth a little more detail. Sugudai is an appetizer consisting of raw fish, in which vegetable oil, salt, pepper, onion, grated apple, vinegar and various spices are added. As for other popular products, residents often pick mushrooms, blueberries, lingonberries and cloudberries.

Prices in Norilsk are average, but for some seemingly ordinary products they are too high here. For example, bread can be bought for 60 rubles, a kilogram of chicken for 132 rubles, a kilogram of rice for 70 rubles, vegetable oil costs 89 rubles. Buckwheat prices in Norilsk start from 160 rubles, apples are estimated at 109 rubles, potatoes cost 53 rubles, and onions - 70 rubles.

It is believed that Norilsk belongs to the Taimyr Peninsula. This region can be reached by two modes of transport: water or air. There is no ground communication with the rest of Russia (the so-called mainland).

City climate

Natural and weather conditions in Norilsk are quite severe. The city is located in the Far North, in connection with which the local climate will be very severe for a person not accustomed to it. The weather in Norilsk is usually cold. The climate here is subarctic.

The city is considered one of the coldest in the world. So, many are interested in what is the weather in Norilsk most often? Winter lasts a long time and is characterized by severe cold. In January, the average temperature is around -28 ‚Å∞. The characteristic weather for winter is frequent frosts with strong winds. The cold period lasts about 280 days, it begins in mid-September and lasts until early May.

Summer is set only at the end of June and lasts until the end of August. Usually it is cloudy and cool. The average temperature in July is about + 10.7 C. If we talk about the average annual temperature, then it is -9.6 C.

Another interesting fact is that Norilsk is on the list of the five most windy settlements in the world.





Nature

Many are interested in what kind of natural zone is Norilsk? On the map, it is located almost on the Taimyr Peninsula, where the forest-tundra dominates. This type of landscape is characterized by some features. Mostly here you can see woodland, which is interspersed with shrub tundra.

Thus, it becomes clear in which natural zone Norilsk is located. On the map you can also see that a large number of rivers flow here.

Despite the harsh natural conditions, this area has a fairly diverse fauna. The fauna in the forest-tundra is mainly represented by lemmings, reindeer, shrews, arctic foxes. These places are popular among hunters.

Of the birds here you can often meet various species of partridges, polar owl and others. The region is home to many migratory birds.

Sights and culture of Norilsk

It is worth telling about the objects of historical heritage that exist in this wonderful city. There are several museums in Norilsk, for example, the Art Gallery, as well as a museum of the history of the development and development of this region. The gallery is widely known and is very popular. This is the second largest collection of art in the entire Krasnoyarsk Territory. She began her work in 1980.

Reviews about museums and theaters are mostly positive. The gallery is especially attractive to visitors, as some unique works are stored here. Architectural sights of Norilsk and their features

Interestingly, some areas of Norilsk are surprisingly reminiscent of St. Petersburg. Many will ask themselves: why do two cities have such a similarity? This is primarily due to the fact that many Leningrad architects were invited to Norilsk, who designed various buildings. Once here, you should definitely see the houses located on Leninsky Prospekt and Guards Square.

After some time, the architecture of the city changed slightly, since in the 1960s, typical buildings gained popularity here, as in many other settlements of Russia.

The construction and architecture of Norilsk has a lot of features. They are mainly associated with the fact that when building buildings on permafrost soils, specific factors must be taken into account. Here, architects managed to gain a unique experience in construction in harsh environmental conditions. In the future, this knowledge was actively used in other cities with a similar climate.





Industry and Economics of Norilsk

As you know, the city is a real center of metallurgy. The region produces a large number of non-ferrous metals such as copper, cobalt, nickel, as well as precious metals: gold, silver, platinum, palladium. In addition, sulfur, metallic selenium, sulfuric acid, etc. are produced here. Of particular interest is the fact that about 96% of nickel produced in Russia is produced in Norilsk. Thus, we can say that the city is a major producer and supplier of many materials, not only in the Krasnoyarsk Territory, but throughout the country. According to some reports, the income of the Krasnoyarsk Territory consists of almost 60% of the revenues that Norilsk provides. Russia includes many similar regions, but this city is considered the largest industrial center of this type.

Transport

Be sure to discuss the transport component of the city. As you know, air communication with other settlements is carried out, since there is an airport near the city. Norilsk has 1 air port, which is called Alykel. It is located 52 kilometers from the city.

Also, the city is connected by a transport network with Dudinka. Moving between them is carried out using buses. In summer, it is also possible to travel along river routes through Dudinka to Krasnoyarsk. In winter, there is a transport connection with Arkhangelsk also through the specified settlement. However, the easiest way to get to the city is the airport. Norilsk is considered a rather inaccessible city.

The transport network in the city itself is well developed - you can move around Norilsk with the help of buses and minibus.

Ecological situation

It is no secret that because of the large number of harmful industries in the city, the environment has deteriorated greatly. Various studies have been carried out, which showed that Norilsk is the village with the most polluted ecology in Russia. Here, many harmful substances are released into the air, such as chlorine, sulfuric acid, hydrogen fluoride, selenium dioxide and many others. Currently, statistical data on various types of pollution are continuously collected here, as well as their analysis. The nature around the city is badly damaged, many ecologists say that the region has a very high risk of environmental disaster.
