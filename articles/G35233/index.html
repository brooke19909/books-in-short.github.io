Secrets of Anenerbe: history, artifacts, archives

During construction in a city in southern Ukraine, workers stumbled upon strange graves. At first they thought it was a Scythian burial, but a Nazi medallion was discovered in a decayed coffin. Archeologists who arrived at the site were shocked: dozens of graves were covered with chlorine, part of human remains were sawn into the spine, some had trepanation of the skull or no heads at all, traces of exposure to chemical compounds remained on the bones. Glasses from quartz came across in coffins, on which, as scientists suggested, cell mutations were studied.

Researchers found that the burial sites were traces of the work of Anenerbe, the most secret organization of the Third Reich. At first it was a rather harmless society, which was created to study the heritage of ancestors, a departure from Christianity and a return to Germanic roots. The organization received so powerful funding and support from the state that it covered almost all aspects of the life of the Third Reich.

For the first time, the shocking scale of the organization recognized as criminal was announced at the Nuremberg trials. The Anenerbe archives were divided among the winning countries.

Runes in the Third Reich: special characters of the "higher race"

The Germans searched for evidence of their ideas in the most ancient Germanic and Scandinavian runes. Northern pagan peoples encrypted sacred knowledge for posterity with the help of secret writing. Ancient cryptography in the early twentieth century became fashionable and even became part of culture. Newspapers and magazines used runes as emblems; this sign was even placed on food packaging.

In the USA, swastika - the oldest sign of prosperity and success - on winding gum inserts meant a win, and in 1929 the Coca-Cola company issued a promotional watch pocket with a swastika image. German and Austrian soldiers of the First World War wore security amulets with runes on their chests. Among these soldiers was the future leader of the Third Reich, Adolf Hitler. Heinrich Himmler also showed an increased interest in the runes as a letter to the ancient "Nordic ancestors".

Until 1939, all SS members studied runes without fail as part of a general training program. The swastika began to be used on the eve of the war, and in 1920 Hitler chose this sign to decorate the party banner.

The Sun Wheel (Zonnerad) was the emblem of the Viking Division, and later the Nordland Division, which was staffed primarily by Scandinavian immigrants. Two runes "zig" made up the SS emblem. The Wolfsangel was the emblem of the Reich tank division.

Top-secret project "Bell": the study of gravity

Another secret of the Third Reich and Anenerbe is the Bell project. There is evidence that the Nazis conducted research with an object in the form of a bell made of heavy metal and filled with a violet liquid similar to mercury. The liquid was stored in a thermos, packed in a lead casing of three centimeters thick. The studies were carried out under a thick cap, the room area was 30 m 2 , and the surfaces were covered with plates with a rubber lining. After each experiment, the laboratory was treated with saline for 45 minutes. The procedure was performed by prisoners of the Gross-Rosen concentration camp.

Each experiment was carried out for a minute. The bell emitted a bluish glow. All electrical equipment within a radius of 150-200 meters failed, almost all liquids (including blood) were divided into fractions. The first group of researchers broke up after the death of five out of seven scientists. In the second series of experiments, the risks were slightly reduced due to equipment modifications. Shortly before the end of the war, all the documents were taken out by the SS team in an unknown direction, and all the scientists who took part in the program were shot by the Germans themselves between the twenty-eighth of April and the fourth of May 1945.

Perhaps the flow of a mysterious violet substance, similar to mercury, could excite a gravimagnetic field of considerable intensity. This is very likely from a scientific point of view. In addition, it is known that attempts to build an aircraft with a mercury-based installation were made back in the eighteenth century.
