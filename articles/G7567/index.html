Salt, alkaline (alkaline) or lithium?

Many people wonder, "Which batteries are better: alkaline or alkaline?" If you are one of them, then we hasten to disappoint you, because the question is posed incorrectly. These are two names for the same kind of power source. Although it is not surprising that most people do not know this. However, we suggest that you familiarize yourself with the basic theoretical information on this subject by examining the list below.

  1. Salt batteries. The cheapest and most primitive variety of chargers. Inside such elements there are electrodes made of zinc and manganese oxide. They are interconnected by a special bridge through which current is supplied. However, it is precisely because of this that the old models simply "flowed" among many users. To date, such a scenario is extremely rare.
  2. Alkaline or alkaline batteries. The most common type of charger, which is actively used in almost all devices that require a battery. The latter have a fairly good capacity, as well as a long shelf life. In addition, the process of their production is not particularly difficult, so they are relatively inexpensive.
  3. Lithium The newest variety of chargers, which include a lithium cathode and anode (made of different materials). Between these two elements is a diaphragm and a separator impregnated with organic electricity. Due to the complex design, the energy consumption of the battery is very large, however, this same feature leads to the high cost of the batteries.

Thus, if you decide to choose the best finger batteries, then you should first consider their variety. You can find out about it by consulting with a sales assistant or by carefully studying the packaging with the charging element. For example, on batteries containing lithium, the English-language inscription ‚ÄúLithium‚Äù will be present. Well, professionals can determine the type of charger by weight.

What batteries do not need to be bought?

Now you know which batteries are the best (alkaline, saline, lithium, and rechargeable). However, in the pursuit of economy, many buyers make the same common mistake - they purchase cheap power supplies from an unknown company, which claims that their products have unrivaled characteristics. However, is it worth trusting a company that could not achieve great heights in the market?

As a rule, such manufacturers attract the attention of the buyer with what they can best realize - beautiful packaging and relatively low price. However, do not forget that a tasteless candy will not change its properties from the fact that it is wrapped in a beautiful wrapper. By making such a purchase, the buyer runs the risk of getting a very weak device that will give out a couple hundred mAh maximum power.

Also, do not forget that the manufacturer may simply not talk about some of the characteristics of his product, if he does not consider it necessary. Most people can see on the battery of dubious quality something like: "It will last 20 years - we guarantee it." Thoughts immediately arise in my head: "Even if I have to recharge it often - it's better than overpaying." But who said that the battery has good insulation that will allow it to last at least 2 years?

What nuances should I pay attention to?

It's time to talk about some selection tips that will allow our readers to purchase the best batteries for a particular device. After all, it is not necessary for the remote control on the TV to buy an expensive lithium battery, overpaying 10 times. Therefore, when choosing a battery, consider the following nuances.

  1. Type of electrolyte (battery composition). Salt batteries are best suited for timers, calculators, remotes, and other devices that do not have much power. Alkaline will ideally cope with stronger loads: kitchen scales, radio-controlled toys, players and so on. Well, lithium will show maximum efficiency in film cameras, tonometers and microphones.
  2. Duration of operation. Do not forget that products can also be divided into disposable and battery ones. The latter can be recharged without any difficulty from a conventional network using a special device. As a rule, one battery equals the power of 4 ordinary batteries. So if you plan to use this or that device for a long time, then think about buying such a device.
  3. Shelf life. Each battery has a specific time until it fails. It is worth paying attention to this nuance especially if you purchase batteries at a relatively high cost. However, it makes no sense to try to find salt power sources with a long life, since they will discharge much faster than their time runs out.
