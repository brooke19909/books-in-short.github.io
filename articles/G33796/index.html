Ambiguous criticism of the works of Aleksandrov

Kirill Alexandrov is a historian whose name provokes mixed reviews. So, some consider him a fearless enthusiast, and the second - an unscrupulous charlatan. The reason for this is his scientific work on the activities of Vlasov and his team. What in them so excites consciousness of people?

If we talk about the positive aspects, then Kirill Alexandrov used in his works the most accurate and relevant materials. In order to get them, he for a long time raked up the rubble in the archives of Soviet and foreign special services. Thanks to this, his dissertations are filled with facts hitherto hidden from the eyes of ordinary citizens.

However, despite the uniqueness of his work, there are several points that cause outright indignation among some historians. The reason is too loyal attitude towards Vlasov. On the pages of the dissertation there are many references to the atrocities of Comrade Stalin, but at the same time, the author overlooks the cruelty of the Germans and the Vlasovites themselves.

Nevertheless, both dissertations of Kirill Alexandrov were successfully defended. And this suggests that the historian carefully worked out all the material, eliminating all false facts. Therefore, it does not matter how other people relate to his activities - he knows his business for sure.





Scientific activity

First of all, Kirill Alexandrov is known as a specialist working with the history of Russia of the XIX-XX centuries. He gained most of his knowledge by studying the old archives of Russia, Germany, France, and the United States. Moreover, he worked with classified data under the custody of the FSB. In particular, Alexandrov was able to read almost all the cases related to the activities of General Vlasov.

Thanks to this, the scientist wrote more than 250 publications on the history of Russia. Most of the materials were published on the anti-Stalinist movement, as well as on the secrets of World War II. In addition, Kirill Alexandrov repeatedly conducted journalistic investigations for many historical magazines and newspapers.
