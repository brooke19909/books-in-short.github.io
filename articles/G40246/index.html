Barcelona: tourist reviews, free entertainment, resorts, attractions, beautiful places

It has everything for a perfect vacation: warm sea, well-groomed beaches, a rich excursion program and a large number of restaurants and night clubs. The city is also the capital of the autonomous region of Catalonia and has an ancient history. It is located on the Mediterranean coast, therefore it is considered an important tourist center not only in Spain, but throughout Europe.

More information about the city, its infrastructure and attractions is described in this article. Reviews of tourists about Barcelona will also be discussed below.

Transport infrastructure

Barcelona is a big city, so tourists are advised to plan their routes in advance in reviews. They note that the transport infrastructure here is very well developed, and for visitors the government has prepared convenient incentives to save money.

Tourists can move around the city by city trains, buses, trams, taxis or metro. Judging by the reviews, the latter option is the most optimal and budget for travelers, since most of the attractions are in the center, so you can move between them on foot. The subway opens at 05:00 and closes at midnight. On Fridays, she works until 02:00. And on Saturday and does not close at night.

How much is the metro in Barcelona? One ticket will cost 2.15 euros (approximately 165 rubles). Tourists in the reviews note that it is much more profitable to purchase a ticket immediately for 10 trips, which will cost 10.30 euros (about 800 rubles). At the same time, it can be used to travel not only by metro, but also by buses, city trains and trams. Also, travel cards are popular with tourists, the validity of which is from 2 to 5 days. With their help, visitors can travel around the city an unlimited number of times by metro or bus. The cost of such a ticket starts from 14 euros (about 1050 rubles).

In addition, tourists can buy a ticket for a tourist bus. Its route includes the main attractions of Barcelona. In the reviews, travelers indicate that such a transport is ideal for those who are poorly oriented in unfamiliar places. In addition, tourists can always rent a bicycle or even a car.

Barcelona resorts and beaches

Barcelona is a city located on the shores of the Mediterranean Sea. Therefore, tourists combine sightseeing here with a beach holiday. Excursion program: museums and main attractions

First of all, Barcelona is famous for its amazing and colorful architecture, because it is not in vain that it is called an open-air museum. It was here that the famous architect Gaudi created, who created many masterpieces for the city. At the same time, most of the sights of Barcelona are located in the historical center. The list of interesting places in Barcelona that are worth visiting can be listed for a very long time. Each building in the Old Quarter, for example, has its own history. But still there are significant sights that cannot be missed during the trip. In reviews, tourists are advised to visit the famous unfinished Sagrada Familia. A huge number of museums are open in Barcelona. They are dedicated to the history of the city, the culture of Catalonia, science and even football. Some of them tell about the life of artists who lived and worked in Barcelona. So, the most popular museums are Pablo Picasso and Antonio Gaudi.

Sagrada Familia

Many tourists consider the unfinished Sagrada Familia Cathedral the most beautiful place in Barcelona. Its construction began at the end of the XIX century. According to the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe chief architect Antonio Gaudi, he was to become a unique original temple, unlike any other in the world. The cathedral was to consist of three facades. Gaudi wanted to decorate each of them with four towers up to 120 meters high. The architect considered the Sagrada Familia to be his main work. He worked on it for more than 40 years, and for some time even lived on its territory in a special cell.

However, the construction of the cathedral has not been completed so far. However, this does not prevent thousands of tourists from visiting it at any time of the year. Free of charge, they can admire its external design. But the entrance fee is charged. In the reviews, tourists are advised to pay an English-language audio guide at the entrance in order to better study the history and structure of this original cathedral. Sagrada Familia will be included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The government of Barcelona plans to complete the cathedral by 2026, to coincide with its opening on the centenary of the death of Gaudi.

Casa Batllo

Not sure what to see in Barcelona on your own? Go to the Eixample district, where you can see the most original creations of Antonio Gaudi. For example, the house of Casa Batllo. It is also sometimes called the "House of Bones." It is noteworthy that the original building, built at the end of the 19th century for the Catalan magnate, looked a little different. It received a unique appearance only in 1906, when Gaudi took up its restructuring. At first they wanted to demolish the building, but the architect decided to keep it, redoing only two main facades. A unique feature of this house is the complete absence of straight lines in its design.

In the reviews, tourists indicate that this is one of the most original buildings in Barcelona, ‚Äã‚Äãso you need to see it. Moreover, not only the external, but also the interior decoration is original. To visit, Casa Batllo is open daily. It works from 9:00 to 21:00, entrance for both children and adults is paid.

Pedestrian street La Ramba

Tourists who do not know what to see in Barcelona on their own can recommend a walk along La Rambla. It is considered not only the most popular street of the city, but also a tourist attraction throughout Spain. La Rambla stretched from the seaport to the very center of Barcelona - Plaza Catalunya. Its central part is considered pedestrian. Many cafes and restaurants, flower stalls and souvenir shops are open here. A lot of tourists always walk on the street. Visiting La Rambla, you will long remember the atmosphere of universal fun that reigns here. Tourists in reviews speak with admiration about the ancient buildings of the XVI-XVIII centuries, which are located here. Theaters and museums are open in them.

La Rambla itself is conditionally divided into 5 segments. Walking along it, you can also get to other streets of Barcelona. Montjuic Mount

Montjuic is a famous mountain located in the heart of the city. You can visit her parks for free, so in the summer in Barcelona it is especially popular with tourists. On its territory there are many attractions. The 1992 Olympic Games, as well as the 1929 World's Fair, were also held here.

What to see in the area? Tourists in their reviews indicate that the National Palace, the entrance to which is decorated with a futuristic fountain, is a must to visit. It is worth a look at the Museum of Art of Catalonia, which contains paintings and sculptures of famous creators of the city. Arriving in Barcelona with children, be sure to visit the Montjuic parks, which are famous for their unique flora and fauna. You can climb the mountain by bus, funicular or cable car.
