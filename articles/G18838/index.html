But if you are looking for peace and quiet, if you come to the sea with a child, then it is better to find a quieter resort. Mui-ne is just what you need. The former fishing village six kilometers northeast of Phan Thiet has been rapidly transformed in recent years. Today it is a solid resort area with luxurious new hotels that stretch along a wonderful sandy beach.

A bit about the resort

Climatic conditions in this part of Vietnam allow you to relax here almost all year round. Mui Ne was chosen not only by travelers seeking tranquility and measured relaxation, but also various surfers. But these two categories of tourists do not intersect. Athletes on boards, sailing or parachuting frolic on huge waves in the eastern part of the beach, at the cape. There is no wind in the center of Mui Ne Bay, and especially on its western outskirts. Storms in the sea are reliably suppressed by breakwaters.

By the way, the described hotel Aroma Beach Resort (Vietnam) is located just in the western part of the beach, on the front page. Hotel Territory

In the new resort, there are simply no old hotels. Aroma Beach Resort & Spa Muine 4 * was erected in 2011, and the last renovation took place in 2016. So the case shine with novelty. The territory of the hotel is not too large, and given the fact that it consists of 28 one- and two-story bungalows, 16 villas and four more buildings. But space is used wisely.

The whole territory is a continuous garden with flowers, palm trees, exotic trees. The restaurant is located on the beach, so you can enjoy Asian dishes right to the sound of the waves. The hotel has a pool, if you believe the reviews of tourists, very spacious and clean. No mosquitoes were observed on the territory. At the reception there is a currency exchange. According to tourists, the course in it is better than in the city, and they do not charge a commission there. Nearby, 50 meters from the entrance to the hotel, there is a rental scooter and a couple of inexpensive cafes and shops. It will be interesting for family tourists to find out that there is a playground on the territory of the hotel, and the pool has a shallow area for bathing babies.

Beach and sea

The Aroma Beach Resort Hotel (Vietnam) in Mui Ne has a small portion of the coast. Before it, from the farthest buildings from the sea, go about 50 meters. What about the hotel beach? The coastline is narrower than that of neighboring hotels. But the sand is cleaner. All garbage caused by the sea or left by vacationers is immediately removed by the servants. Even in a season not very favorable for swimming, the waves here are small, not higher than a meter. But since Mui Ne is known as a resort for surfers, here you can meet people on boards and kites.

Anyone can stay on the beach, but sun loungers and umbrellas are reserved only for hotel guests. Since the hotel is on the edge, there are very few traders here. Beach towels are provided free of charge by the pool. Low tides are observed, but they are not noticeable. The sea recedes just a couple of meters. Entering the water is not quite suitable for bathing babies due to the rough surf. But it is worth overcoming, and you can enjoy swimming in a calm sea.

Life hacks

Tourists are advised not to buy the free offer to go to Phan Thiet. This is a waste of time. You will be transported to pearl jewelry workshops, souvenir shops and other commercial points where the guide gives a percentage of your purchases. It's much cheaper to shop on your own. Getting to Phan Thiet from Aroma Beach Resort Hotel (Vietnam) is very easy. The hotel is located closest to the rest in Mui Ne from this city. A special access road even leads to it.

So, you need to leave the hotel and immediately turn right. After a couple of minutes, you will reach the main highway connecting Mui Ne to Phan Thiet. Cross the road and wait for the red bus number 1 and 9. These routes go along different streets of Phan Thiet, but you will get to the city for sure. The ticket is bought from the conductor on the bus. But public transport runs only until 17:30, so fans of evening walks will have to fork out for a taxi.
