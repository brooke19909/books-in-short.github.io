Mayrhofen ski resort, Austria: description, features and reviews

On the territory of Tyrol, the Austrian federal state in the western part of the country, is located the largest ski resort of the Zillertal Valley - Mayrhofen. Due to the fact that it is located at a comfortable height, just 633 meters above sea level, this area is preferred by families with children, as well as older people. For small tourists, all the necessary conditions are created here, up to kindergartens. Mayrhoven (Austria) is most popular among Russians, British and residents of Benelux countries.

Keep the path to the mountains

To find yourself in the Tyrolean Alps, you need to fly from Moscow to Salzburg or Munich. The flight will take 2.5 hours, then you should transfer to a comfortable train, and now it is in front of you in person the ski resort of Mayrhofen, Austria.





Another route runs through Innsbruck. To do this, you need to fly by plane from Russia to the capital of the Winter Olympics of 1964 and 1976 for 3 hours, and upon arrival by car drive 75 kilometers to the final destination. The cost of an air flight from Russia to Innsbruck will be 260–960 euros in economy class, prices vary depending on the airline you choose.

Want to spend the night in an igloo?

Of course, tourists who arrived in Mayrhofen (Austria) will first of all begin to look for a refuge in which they will live for the entire time they are at the resort. Problems with finding shelter will not arise, it remains only to choose exactly where to settle. So, at your service:

  1. Hotel accommodation. A room in a three-star hotel will cost 110–140 euros, and in a five-star hotel - 290 euros per day.
  2. Rental housing: an apartment can be rented for a week for 500-800 euros. For this money you will have at your disposal 3 rooms, a bathroom, equipped kitchen and a beautiful view of the mountains. If the plans for vacation are only a couple of days, then you can always find daily housing. Prices for such apartments usually start from 100 to 170 euros per day.
  3. Chalet rental. These wooden rural houses are equipped with everything necessary: ​​they have at least 3 rooms, a cozy kitchen, a living room with a fireplace, a veranda.
  4. Fans of all kinds of unusual things can stay in the alpine hotel White Lounge. Her building is an igloo in which all rooms and even furniture are made of ice and snow. Air temperature in a hotel room minus 1 degree. Usually no one spends more than one night here. To ensure that guests of the White Lounge do not turn into snowmen overnight, warm sleeping bags are provided . Such an extreme stay will cost you a lot - 350 euros per day. To spend the weekend on holidays, you need to book rooms in advance. Next to this hotel is the same snowy restaurant. Of course, the food is not icy, and it is presented with dishes of Austrian cuisine.




What does Mayrhofer offer tourists?

You can relax in this resort all year round, and the Tuxer glacier with an excellent ski area is available to skiers regardless of the season. Mayrhofen (Austria) is famous for its attractions:

  • lovers of steep descents will be happy to go to the Harakiri track with an angle of 78 degrees, it is located on Mount Penken;
  • the local cable car is the longest in Austria;
  • the local funicular simultaneously accommodates 160 people, and its area is 40 m²;
  • It offers tourists 42 lifts and 159 kilometers of excellent slopes.




Depending on the level of preparedness, everyone can choose a corner to their liking:

  • Ailsenalm with its simple sections is suitable for children;
  • Penken will appeal to beginner skiers;
  • Hintertux Glacier with its steep turns will attract experienced athletes;
  • Burton Park Mayrhofen will open its possession to snowboarders. Ski jumps, rails and a halfpipe are equipped for them here. In the park, both beginners and professionals will be able to test their capabilities.

Wie viel wird es kosten? (How much is it?)

The cost of a ski pass for a week will be about 200 euros per person. Equipment rental costs from 50 to 60 euros per week. The funicular card will cost more - 43 euros per day, and you can ride on children sledges for 3 euros.



Those who first came to the resort and do not know the skills of skiing will benefit from lessons at a ski school. Tuition fees, which on average lasts 2.5 hours per day, range from 102 to 111 euros for 3-4 days. If you prefer to practice individually with a personal instructor, you will need to pay from 140 to 160 euros per day for 4-hour classes. By the way, kids can go to ski school after they turn 4 years old.

Holidays in the summer months

Many Austrian settlements - Sölden, Kaltenbach, Serfaus, Zell am See, as well as Mayrhofen - ski resorts that await guests not only in winter but also in summer. At this time of year, tourists will find something to their liking:

  • Storming the peaks of Ahorn and Penken using climbing equipment.
  • Bicycle tours for all ages - after all, 800 kilometers of special roads have been laid in this area. This is a great opportunity to feel like a mountain king, as in the cycling Tour de France, Vuelta, Giro d´Italia.
  • Hiking in the forests and valleys of Zillertal, swimming in the beautiful lakes.
  • Riding on the river on balloons.
  • Rest in a nature park.

In addition to outdoor activities in the mountains, Mayrhofen (Austria) can offer a host of other activities, including entertainment: sports shopping, discos, a bowling alley, a visit to the water park, and in April there is an international festival of electronic and dance music.

Tyrolean cuisine

Hungry holidaymakers who have a great appetite in the fresh air will feed Mayrhofen restaurants and eateries with delicious and satisfying Austrian dishes . Most of the catering buildings were built many decades ago and are designed in the traditional Tyrolean style.





An extensive menu will help to make a delicious set lunch (2 dishes will cost about 20 euros): for the first - a rich rich soup with meatballs, for the second - roast pork and potatoes sprinkled with fresh herbs, unusual pasties stuffed with cheese and potatoes, dessert for 3 euros - sponge cake with whipped cream, decorated with lingonberries. As a drink, a cappuccino is offered for 3 euros, and mulled wine, which costs 3.9 euros, will help keep warm from the cold.

Mayrhofen (Austria): reviews

Of course, many of those who happened to relax in the Tyrolean Alps, want to return here again. This is facilitated by the excellent infrastructure, excellent weather, all conditions for an unforgettable vacation, ranging from magnificent ski slopes to a high level of service at affordable prices. According to tourists, this region attracts them with its special atmosphere, friendly people, and the fact that it is in Mayrhofen that the Austrian ski team conducts training, one of the world leaders in this sport.





The small town of Mayrhofen (Austria), in which less than 4 thousand people live, hospitably welcomes lovers of ski holidays, and will forever remain in their memory as a place where they spent unforgettable days.

Friendly people, amazing atmosphere of home comfort, bright sunshine, dazzling smiles and laughter laughs - all these are Mayrhofen (Austria), tours to which will delight you with a great time!
