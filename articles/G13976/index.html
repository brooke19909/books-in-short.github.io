Evidence of probability

Of course, no one would argue that cryogenic freezing of a person is possible without significant evidence.

In 1966, for example, it was possible to prove that the brain restores electrical activity after freezing to -20 ¬∞ C. In 1974, an experiment was conducted in which gray matter partially restored its activity after 7 years of storage under appropriate conditions.

In 1984, it was proved that large organs are not exposed to structural damage during freezing. And in 1986, scientists found out: large mammals can be brought back to life if they stay for three hours in a state of clinical death at a temperature of -3 ¬∞ C.

In 2002, an experiment was conducted during which it turned out that the brain retains memory, even if it is cooled to -10 ¬∞ C. In 2004, doctors successfully transplanted kidneys after they were frozen at -45 ¬∞ C and then warmed up.

The next experiment, conducted in 2006, proved that complex neural connections maintain their vital activity even during vitrification (transition of a liquid into a glassy state).

In 2015, the world learned that an animal subjected to freezing and rebirth did not lose its memory. In the same year, an experiment was conducted on the cryonization and restoration of the whole brain of a mammal. Researchers assured: everything went perfectly.

Connection system for perfusion

After all the previously described steps, special tubes are inserted into the arteries and veins of the body. With their help, blood is removed from the body. And the body is filled with a solution. To control the process, use a device such as a refractometer. With its help, it is possible to identify the percentage of concentration of the solution in the tank (which in this case is the body).

60% - this is the degree of saturation established by specialists. Once this indicator is reached, the procedure is completed. Blood is completely replaced by solutions. It is impossible to allow even its smallest part to remain in the body. Because in this case, the processes of change will accelerate.

Here, however, is the whole answer to the question of how cryogenic freezing occurs. Then the body is placed in storage. The operation itself lasts about 4 hours, 6 specialists work on the patient, including 2 surgeons and 4 assistants.
