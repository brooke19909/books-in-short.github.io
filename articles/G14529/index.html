Creation

The man decided to go in for creativity. Suppose he chose plastic material for his purpose. That is, one that, when pressed, takes a given shape easily and retains it for a long time, without returning to its original form.

Stocking up materials, in the modern variety of products on offer, anyone who has decided to mold a parrot from plasticine, like any researcher, will have to act by trial and possibly error. But in the end, you can choose the right one specifically for you.

Plasticine

This is the children's favorite craft material. You can mold plasticine from a parrot, a cake, a computer, and a real photo frame. Yes, everything your heart desires!

Previously, plasticine was made from clay, to which animal fats and wax were added, so that the mass did not dry longer.

Now plasticine is made from technological materials, such as high molecular weight polyethylene, rubbers and others, and painted in all possible colors.

And why exactly a plasticine parrot?

Hurrah! Sculpt!

Step 1. We fashion the blanks for future parts of the body of the parrot:

  1. The body is a cylinder of red plasticine, rounded on top.
  2. A flat "apron" that will be the tummy of the bird.
  3. Two green, flattened, but thicker than the tummy, pear-shaped blanks on the wings.
  4. Brown flagella: 2 thicker and more authentic for paws, and 8 thinner and shorter for fingers (4 each).
  5. Black pyramid (future beak).
  6. A flat but rather thick orange horseshoe crest.
  7. Two white cakes for the eyes and two smaller black ones for the pupils.

Some parts need to be marked with a stack. It will need to cut out the details.

Step 2. Cut the stack on the blanks:

  • on the yellow tummy we make a cut, like a T-shirt;
  • on the wings, according to the markup, we make 4 feather fingers;
  • make a cut in the center of the beak;
  • cut feathers on an orange crest.

Step 3. Connect:

  • fingers with paws;
  • pupils with eyes.

Step 4. Connect all the details in a single volumetric figure.

The parrot is ready!

Just do not need to hope that such a heavy figure will stand on such thin legs. The secret of sculptors and masters of volumetric plasticine animation is that such figures have a wire frame.
