Soraya Manouchehri: history, interesting facts, photos

Soraya Manouchehri is an Iranian girl who gained fame, having died due to the old death penalty "stoning", which was used by ancient Jews. In 2008, her story became the basis of the American drama Cyrus Nauraste, which was released under the title "Stoning Soraya M.".

Tragic story

The story of Sorayu Manouchehri became known thanks to a book by an Iranian of French descent Freudun Sahejan. It tells about the life of a girl from an ordinary Iranian village.

Sahejjan describes how a car broke down in an Iranian province. When he was forced to wait for her to be repaired, one woman told him how the day before her niece had been beaten to death. Based on her story, he wrote a documentary short story.

Soraya Manuchehri was married to a man named Ali. They had four children - two young daughters and two teenage sons. At some point, Ali decided to divorce his wife, and to take his wife younger. Of course, under Iranian laws, he had the right to have two wives, but did not want to spend money on their maintenance.

He invited Soraya to divorce, but she showed obstinacy. The woman did not want to leave herself with her daughters without a livelihood and a breadwinner, so she did not give a divorce to Ali. Even his betrayals and regular beatings did not help.

Soraya understood that the father would take the sons, and that she and her daughters would actually leave her on the street.

Chance of salvation

Enlightenment came in the story of Soraya Manuchehri when she was offered a job as a servant for a widower who had recently lost his wife. She dreamed of earning at least some money in order to gain independence from her husband and leave him. Therefore, I agreed to this work.

Soraya Manouchehri took up housekeeping, helped the widower in everything. This occasion was used by her husband, who wanted to get a divorce at any cost. He organized everything in such a way that the local council accused and condemned the woman of treason. As it turned out later, he did this using intimidation, blackmail, and also processed many local residents to form the appropriate public opinion.

The local council, guided by the laws of Sharia, decided to kill Soraya in a cruel, but simple and economical way - to stone for frivolous cohabitation with a neighbor.
