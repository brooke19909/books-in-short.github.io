The Origin and Origin of Life on Earth: Basic Hypotheses

If you bring to a table the hypotheses of the origin of life on Earth, invented at different times, A4 sheet is not enough for it, so many different options and theories have been developed by people since ancient times. The three main and largest groups of assumptions are communication with the divine essence, natural evolution, and cosmic population. Each option has followers and opponents, but the main scientific option is the theory of biochemistry. Consider different systems and ideas about how organic life was born on our planet.

It is itself!

One of the options for explaining the origin and development of life on Earth is spontaneous generation. For the first time such ideas were born a very, very long time ago. According to scientists, the start of everything was inanimate matter, and it was from it that organic matter appeared. Numerous experiments were organized, the task of which was either to confirm the correctness of the assumption, or to refute it. At one time, Pasteur was given a prize for experiments with boiling broth in a flask, since they allowed to prove that life comes only from living things. But this did not answer the question of where did the very organisms that started the process come from.

External forces

For a long time there have been in society ideas about the origin of life on Earth, explaining everything with divine intervention. Presumably, all living things on the planet appeared at once, but this happened because some higher being expressed its will and activated its unique power. This creature must have incredible strength, abilities incomprehensible to humans. Who created life, opinions differ. Some call the creator the Absolute, others - the supreme deity, a kind of great mind.

This explanation was first invented in ancient times. World religions are based on this assumption. At the moment, it has not been possible to refute the assumption, since there is no unambiguous scientific answer to explain all those processes, phenomena that are observed on our planet.

True, astrophysicists have proven that the universe has formed 16 billion years, and the cause of this was the primary explosion. Such scientific calculations contradict the theory of panspermia, which does not prevent a number of followers from upholding their case.

Biochemistry

In short, the hypothesis of the origin of life on Earth related to the specifics of biochemical processes is dominant in the scientific world today. It was first formulated by the famous biochemist Oparin. Based on his work, life forms appeared due to the evolution of chemical interactions. Such reactions are the basis of organic life. Probably, the cosmic body (our planet) first formed, then the atmosphere. The next step in evolution was organic synthesis, reactions, the results of which were indispensable for our life substances. Millions, billions of years have gone into the evolution of species and the formation of the diversity of life that can be observed at present.

The correctness of this theory is confirmed by a number of scientific experiments. Although it is currently considered the main one, there are a number of opponents who disagree with the explanation.

Oparin's Ideas

This scientific origin of life on Earth is also associated with the name of the prominent scientist Haldane. Back in the 24th, Oparin, then still not an academician, published an article in which he considered the features of the formation of organic life. In the 38th, the material was translated into English, and it immediately aroused public interest. Oparin thought that when using concentrated liquid with high molecular weight compounds, it is possible to obtain zones of especially high concentration, which form spontaneously. Such sites stand out from the general environment and can enter into chemical and energy metabolism with it. It was decided to call such formations coacervates.

Oparin suggested that the emergence of organic life occurred in stages. First organic compounds appeared, the next step was the formation of protein molecules, and the final step was considered protein bodies. In many respects, this theory is based on earlier studies of cosmic bodies. The work of astronomers show that planetary systems, stars are formed by gas and dust matter, in which there are metals, oxides, ammonia, methane, water, hydrogen. When the primary ocean appeared on our planet, along with it created the conditions in which organic life could appear. Hydrocarbons in liquids could enter into chemical interactions, including complex structured reactions and transformations. Gradually, the molecules became more complex, which led to the formation of carbohydrates.

Step by step - to the truth

Developing his theory about the origin of life on Earth, Oparin was able to prove that ultraviolet radiation is a sufficient condition for the formation of amino acids and a number of other biochemical compounds important for organic life. It was possible to achieve a reaction under artificial conditions. In order for protein bodies to form, coacervates had to appear. It is known that under specific conditions, the water shell can be clearly defined, fencing off the molecule from the environment in which it is located. Molecules having such a shell could interact, uniting, and this became the mechanism for the appearance of multimolecular structures called coacervates. As further studies have shown, simple mixing of polymers also allows one to obtain such formations. Polymer molecules independently assembled into complex structural formations that could be seen through a microscope.

According to theoretical biology, the origin of life on Earth has become possible, since coacervates are able to take matter from the environment. This type was called open systems. A catalyst can be included in a drop of coacervate (enzymes belong to this category), and this initiates a number of biochemical reactions. Among other variety, polymerization of monomers taken from the surrounding space is available. Drops are able to grow, add weight, break up. Coacervates, as shown by studies, are able to grow and multiply, they are available metabolic processes. Evolution was realized through natural selection.

Continuing research

In biology, the hypotheses of the origin of life on Earth were tested in 53rd, when Miller took up the experiments. A mixture of four molecules was created, which was placed in a confined space and began to process with electric current. As an analysis of the results showed, amino acids are formed with the participation of such a catalyst. The continuation of a series of experiments made it possible to obtain reactions, the results of which were nucleotides, sugars. This allowed scientists to authoritatively conclude that evolution is possible if coacervates exist, but independent reproduction of the system is not inherent.

Although the hypothesis of the origin of life on Earth received official justification, there were still some ambiguities. At first, scientists simply turned a blind eye to them. It was known that a successful molecular protein structure may appear in the coacervate, moreover, the process does not have a clear system and proceeds by chance. Thus, for example, effective catalysts could be formed, due to which a particular coacervate could actively grow and multiply. At the same time, it was not possible to explain how such catalysts can be copied so that the next generation of coacervates could also use them. There was no explanation for the exact reproduction of single protein structures that turned out to be particularly effective.

Spontaneous generation and event versions
