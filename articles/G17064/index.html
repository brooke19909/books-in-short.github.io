Historical reference

The first underground opened in London. The first line stretched only 6 kilometers. They launched it back in 1863. Initially, the metro operated on steam traction and only eventually switched to electric. By the end of the 19th century, the first elevated line was launched in America.





Later, a reaction to this type of public transport followed around the world. The subway opened in Budapest, Glasgow, Paris and Berlin. In Russia, the first underground park appeared in Moscow in 1936. Then they began to open the metro in St. Petersburg, Kiev, Baku, Kharkov, Tashkent, etc. But at that time no one thought that in a few decades there would be people who want to find the most beautiful metro.

Beauty underground

It so happened that in many cities around the world the subway has become not just a means of transportation, but also a cultural center. Art objects underground have also become the place where exhibition halls, contests and festivals are organized. Some subways are visited by tourists just to see the beauty and appreciate the artistry of the design of the halls.

The most beautiful metro stations can now be found in St. Petersburg, Minsk, Munich or Stockholm. They are all very different and are stunning with their architecture. The subways have a bizarre shape, an unusual design and interesting color schemes.

Scandinavian art

Unusual turned out Stockholm metro station. The Town Hall remains special. This station is considered the most popular among tourists. Building was not easy. I had to cut tunnels at a depth of 30 meters. Moreover, the base underground was rocky.





It was the rock that it was decided to make the basis of decorative decoration. The metro was commissioned by leading artists and sculptors. They had to process everything in the Scandinavian style, preserving the national flavor. Each station turned out to be individual and unique, one of a kind.

To the tone of the underground "chandeliers", the travel stations were also made ultramarine. The station has become a modern metro, despite the lack of pronounced architecture. The Westfriedhof turned out to be solemn and attractive not only for visitors, but also for residents of the city.
