When to go to Alanya

In Turkey, the climate is mainly Mediterranean, subtropical. This means mild and very rainy winters and hot, dry summers. Almost an annual rainfall pours on the Mediterranean coast of Turkey from November to March. Since April, rain has become a rare guest. But the coast of Alanya is unique in terms of climate. The fact is that from the north it is reliably protected from the cold winds by the Taurus Mountains. The calm sea in this region warms up quickly, and cools very slowly. Thanks to this, the longest swimming season is here: from late April to mid November. Although those healthy people for whom + 18 degrees seem like a comfortable temperature can plunge into the waters of the Mediterranean Sea in December and even in January. So the rest in Alanya can be called year-round. The peak season is traditionally in the summer months and September. But at this time, the cost of a trip to Turkey increases. If you want to save, it is better to choose the second half of spring for a trip. May is especially comfortable for relaxation. Alanya, unlike neighboring Kemer and Antalya, is the first to enter the summer. During the day, real heat reigns here, and the nights are still fresh, not stuffy. Cheap vouchers (this does not apply to the period of holidays in the first decade of the month), excellent shopping, a lot of various excursions - this is what Turkey in May. Alanya, in addition to this, offers a full summer beach holiday. In the second half of the month, the water temperature off the coast of the resort reaches + 23-24 ¬∞ C.

Alanya

This city arose in the second century BC. Its rich history has left its mark in the ancient fortress city on the cliff and many mosques. Antique Korakesion and medieval Alaya towered on a high peninsula that stands out to sea. This strategically important point was of considerable importance during the Ptolemaic era (Hellenistic period), the Roman, Byzantine and Ottoman empires. But Alanya experienced the greatest flowering in the Middle Ages. It was then that the city was surrounded by a double row of walls, since a citadel castle was erected at the very top of the rocky cape. Also at the time of the Seljuks belong the shipyards of Tersane in the grottoes and the Red Tower (Kyzyl-Kul). Alanya - reviews of tourists do not cease to repeat about it - has many small caves. One is called Phosphoric because its walls glow from the presence of this mineral in rock, the other is called Lovers, and the third, the most famous, is Damlatash. There is also the "Pirate Cave", but it can be accessed only from the sea, by boat. Other attractions worth noting are the 1230 mausoleum of Aksebe Turbesi and the Aladdin Keikubat I Mosque. Since the beginning of the 60s of the last century, the city gradually began to transform into a seaside resort. There were beautiful embankments, boardwalks, arose chic and budget hotels. Alanya was surrounded by satellite villages. These are Konakli, Inzekum, Avsallar, Mahmutlar and Okurcalar. The last of them is located Akin Paradise Hotel 4 *.



How to get to Alanya

This large resort town is located one hundred and forty kilometers from the provincial capital - Antalya. Most tourists arrive there. Travel time by bus from Antalya Airport to Alanya is about one and a half hours. But first, an independent tourist needs to get to the capital's bus station and buy a ticket (about five euros). The station where cars arrive is located three kilometers from the center of Alanya. Vouchers usually include the cost of transfer from the airport to the hotel, so organized tourists have nothing to worry about. But Russians traveling on their own leave a lot of advice on the forums on how best to get to a vacation spot. If you decide to soak up the sights of Istanbul before basking in the Anatolian Riviera, fly to Gazipasha Airport. From there to Alanya go only half an hour. You can arrive by bus from Adana (10 hours, 12 –Ñ) and Konya (six and a half hours, 10 –Ñ). From the port of Alanya you can go to Northern Cyprus, to Kyrenia. The cost of the ferry ticket is 32 –Ñ. It must be purchased no later than the day before the trip. City buses tirelessly run around the city itself and to all satellite villages. They are called "dolmushi". There is such a minibus in Okurcalar, where the Akin Paradise Hotel 4 * is located. The fare in it is 3-8 Turkish liras (depending on the distance).

Rooms at Akin Paradise 4 *

Of course, they are not going to Turkey to sit in a room, but comfort is also worth considering. Especially if you travel with a child. How do tourists rate the services and facilities of the hotel?

For many advanced tourists, the priority when choosing a hotel is the availability of Wi-Fi. In many hotels in Turkey, it is available for a fee. Experienced travelers, who have something to compare with, say that this hotel even exceeded their expectations.
