Comoros on a world map. Comoros Tours

Comoros on a world map can be found in the Strait of Mozambique (in its northern part). They are components of a small state. Its name is the Union of Comoros. This state is located on the territory of the Comorian archipelago. Moreover, it consists of three large islands. Their list includes Moheli, Grand Comor and Anjouan. The archipelago also has the island of Mayotte, which is ruled by France. It is worth saying that there are volcanoes on the islands that retain their activity to date.

Climate

Comoros are in the tropical zone. The climate is humid and quite hot. In this case, there are two pronounced seasons. So, from November to April, the wettest and warmest period is observed. The rest of the year is dry and cool.



The average monthly temperature in the Comoros is twenty-four to twenty-seven degrees Celsius. During the year, 1100 millimeters of precipitation falls. More rain in central areas. Up to 3000 mm of precipitation falls here.

Comoros is undesirable to visit during the wet season (November-April). At this time, there is a sweltering heat. At the same time, it is accompanied by almost one hundred percent humidity.

Comoros are best for relaxing during the period from May to October. In this cool season, the air temperature does not rise above twenty-five degrees. It is from May to October that an almost perfect climate reigns over most of the archipelago. In the cool season, the air is fresh due to ocean trade winds and is full of aromas of flowering cloves, vanilla, cinnamon and ylang-ylang. However, during this period, the weather sometimes deteriorates sharply due to the arrival of cyclones and monsoon winds from the ocean.

Nature

Comoros is known for its unique flora and fauna. Almost thirty-seven species of animals live here, many different plants grow that are not found anywhere else in the world. The oldest forms of life on our planet have been discovered in the waters of the coastal ocean.



On the tops of the slopes of the mountains of the Comoros, tropical forests grow, and below are thickets of shrubs and savannahs. From crops, carnation and coffee tree, coconut palm and sugar cane, as well as bananas, are cultivated here.

Among the representatives of the fauna of the Comoros, one of the rarest animals on earth stands out. It's about a livingston bat. Rainforests are the habitat of mongooses, lemurs and tenreks. In the waters of the coastal ocean, coelocants were caught. These are bristled fish that were found in ancient times (almost four hundred million years ago).

Ponds

Comoros do not have permanent rivers. However, there are freshwater lakes. They are located in the craters of inactive volcanoes.

A real natural monument is Lake Sal (Salt Lake). For many decades, it has attracted the attention of not only travelers, but also scientists who seek to unravel its secrets.

Comoros. Grand Comor Map

In the northern part of this island you will find Lake Sal. It occupies the vent of an extinct volcano. With reliability, no one can judge the depth of this reservoir. In this regard, it is also called the Abyss. The uniqueness of the lake lies in the fact that it contains salt water. Why? There is no single answer to this question. There is simply the assumption that the reservoir feeds the ocean. Another unique feature of the lake is the presence of a large number of algae in it. Because of this vegetation, it changes color twice during the day. In this case, the lake may look dark green or shimmering blue.

Comoros (photos of amazing landscapes are presented in the article) attract tourists with its exoticism. Only here you can see a large number of unusual birds and bright flowers. An attractive landscape with tropical plants and steaming volcanoes decorated with frozen lava will delight the eye of vacationers.



It should be borne in mind that if you decide to buy tours to Comoros, they will offer you considerable prices. The cost of a ten-day permit for one person is within fifty thousand Russian rubles. Another hundred thousand will have to pay for the ticket. Rest here is expensive. However, this is a wonderful place with luxurious beaches, covered with fine white sand. In addition, the Comoros attract diving and other active water sports.

Hotels

In Comoros, the same classification of hotels that exists around the world is accepted. That is why all tourists will find an acceptable place to stay here without any problems. When planning a trip to Comoros, tours can be selected both with accommodation in a budget hotel, and with accommodation in a five-star hotel with comfortable conditions.

sights

Having visited Comoros, you should certainly go on an excursion to the largest city of the archipelago - Moroni. Its second name is Port-au-Butr. This is the youngest city in the country on the island of Grand Comor. It is the capital of the state. Moroni has a large number of mosques. The oldest of them is Friday. This building is the most popular attraction of the capital of the Comoros. The Friday Mosque was erected as early as 1472. Coral limestone, which has retained its white color to this day, served as the material for the construction of the temple. The appearance of the Friday Mosque clearly shows the bright features of Arabic architecture. They were reflected in two-tier arched galleries, a high carved border located along the perimeter of the roof, a minaret, balustrades and a green dome with a crescent moon located on its top.

Another attraction of Moroni is a bright and colorful bazaar. A popular destination for excursions is the port of Medina. It is interesting to wander through the ancient Arab quarter, which will amaze tourists with its magnificent architecture.



Eleven kilometers south of the Medina is the city of Mitsuje. It is well known for woodworking workshops located on its territory. At the local market you can buy caskets and a variety of crafts. All of them are made from expensive wood species growing in the Comoros.
Mitsuja is also famous for ancient tombstones decorated with artful ornaments. Locals believe that under one of them lie the remains of a saint, protecting them from evil spirits.

The magnificent Sultan's Palace can be seen in Mutsamudu. Not far from the center of this city is the picturesque Dziyankundre waterfall.

Local population

Comorians are welcoming and hospitable people. Many vacationers notice their shyness and modesty. Restrained orders dominate even in the shopping arcades, where the seller advertises, but does not impose his goods at all.



A great honor for a guest is an invitation to visit a local house. Small presents are given to the owners, but by no means money.
