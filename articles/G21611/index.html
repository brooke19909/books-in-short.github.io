The value of the individual pension coefficient (IPC) - what is it? Formation of an insurance pension

Since 2015, the procedure for calculating pensions has changed. Now, the calculation of cash payments according to the seniority of a citizen is carried out taking into account the IPC, that is, the individual pension coefficient is calculated based on which a person will receive a certain amount of money every month. A similar system has long existed in the West and in Europe. However, if the procedure is quite understandable for the inhabitants of these countries, then this program is not yet completely obvious to the Russians.





The value of the individual pension coefficient (IPC): what is it?

Previously, in order to start receiving a pension, a citizen had only to indicate his seniority and confirm it with documents. But since 2015, future retirees can independently regulate their retirement income. For this, the pension is divided into several parts: funded and insurance. The latter can be fixed and settlement.

In this case, a citizen must meet several important parameters:

  • Have an insurance experience of at least 15 years.
  • Reach retirement age.
  • Provide data that confirm that the retirement rate was at least 30 points for the entire length of service.

In turn, according to the scoring system, the work experience and the level of wages are taken into account. Until 2014, this indicator also depended on the amount of contributions to the tax service.

Accordingly, today, if a citizen received a greater income in 2017 than in 2016, then the individual pension coefficient (pension score) will be higher.

It is also worth considering that from 2015 to 2025 a special transitional period is provided . This means that the conditions for calculating pensions will be tightened every year, the value of the calculated points will grow according to inflation and economic indicators in the country.

What else does IPC depend on

How is the annual IPC calculated?

Prior to 2015, the government had compiled an individual pension coefficient formula, which is as follows: PC = MF / S, where:

  • PC represents the total amount of pension points of a particular citizen.
  • MF - this is part of the insurance (taking into account the deduction of the fixed and funded part of the pension).
  • C represents the value of the IPC at the time of calculation.




According to this formula, it is quite easy to independently calculate the amount of accumulated points. Accordingly, in order to find out what pension compensation awaits a citizen, it is not necessary to contact the FIU. The only thing that needs to be clarified is the benefit rate, which can be 6%, 10% or 16%.

Calculation Example

To make it easier to deal with all the intricacies of the IPC, consider an example. Suppose a certain citizen decided to independently calculate how many points she could already accumulate for her labor activity and how much she still needed in order to receive monthly payments in the amount of 25,000 rubles.

The fixed part of the insurance pension is usually 4559 rubles. At the same time, a pension equal to 18,300 rubles is accrued to the woman.

Accordingly, the amount of payments for the future pensioner will amount to 13 741 rubles (we take away from the 18 300 fixed insurance part).

To calculate the IPC, divide 13 741 by 74.28 and get about 185 points. This is the desired value. If you make several reverse calculations, it is easy to find out that in order to receive 25,000 rubles a pensioner must accumulate another 90 points per month.





How much is IPC

By and large, the cost of this indicator is a specific number, which is determined by the state annually. For example, in 2015, the IPC was equal to 64 rubles, and in February 2016, due to the general increase in inflation, this indicator increased to 74 rubles. This year, the cost of this ratio is 78 rubles with a penny.

If a pensioner earns a larger IPC, then only the maximum possible indicator will be taken into account.
