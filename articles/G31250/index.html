Battle of kindergartens

In order to decide which garden to give the child to (municipal or private), it is necessary to highlight the positive and negative sides of each of them. The first and often decisive selection criterion is price. The cost of attending a private kindergarten is usually ten times higher than the cost of attending a public educational institution.

The second important aspect of choosing a private kindergarten is a small number of children in one group. As practice shows, in the current municipal kindergartens, the number of children in one group is close to thirty. In private, in a group of six to fifteen children.

Another important factor is the frequency and diet of children. As for the number of meals, it is five times a day. It is difficult to judge regarding quality. But in private gardens, food is usually more delicious, high-quality and wholesome, since high-quality products of the corresponding price category are used for its preparation.

Of course, in state kindergartens they also feed food that does not harm children, but it is not so tasty and varied. It should be noted that these characteristics can be classified as ideal. In practice, there are situations when in private kindergartens the level of education is lower and the quality of food offered to children is worse and the incidence rate is higher.
