Electric circuit of a microwave oven: device and principle of operation

The number of household appliances in our kitchens is growing steadily year by year. What 15 years ago was a curiosity is now taken for granted. Today it is impossible to imagine an apartment in which there would be no microwave oven. This is a really convenient device that allows you to not only quickly heat up or defrost, but also to cook food. Moreover, contrary to the fears of many people, the energy consumption during its use is small. This article will discuss microwave oven circuits and other related information.

What is the principle of a microwave oven?

Many do not understand why food is heated in the microwave, and an empty plate remains cold, regardless of the time it was inside. Schemes of microwave ovens and their principle of operation involves increasing only liquids, fats and sugars. This happens due to the fact that the high-frequency radiation generated by the magnetron (it is also called the gun) accelerates only dipole molecules that do not exist outside the humid environment. For this reason, ceramics, glass or plastic do not heat up.

Microwaves themselves cannot penetrate deeply into warmed food, by a maximum of 2-3 cm, but this is quite enough to increase the temperature throughout the entire volume of the product. That is why it seems that the dish is warming up from the inside.

How does a microwave oven work: scheme, operation procedure

If you use special terminology, the information may seem too complicated. Therefore, it makes sense to consider the circuit diagram of a microwave oven with a description of the work in simpler words, as they say "human language".

The basis of the microwave is a high-voltage transformer with two windings - low current (voltage on it rises to 1600 V, and the resistance is about 180 Ohms) and incandescent (not more than 3 V). One of the conclusions of the high-voltage winding is shorted to the device body, and the second goes to the rectifier capacitor, which performs the functions of separation and smoothing. It turns out that it does not allow a possible short circuit to disable the transformer and does not allow ripple.

The filament circuit consists of a winding, combined with a spiral located inside the magnetron. Thanks to their interaction, high-frequency radiation is generated. However, modern microwave ovens are quite moody due to the addition of completely unnecessary components to the electrical circuit of a microwave oven. They, being a weak point, often lead to breakdowns. At the same time, old models work flawlessly for many years.

The most common problem is the lack of contact at the terminal connections, while diagnosing such malfunctions is quite difficult. This happens because without a load all the connections are in working condition, but when the magnetron is started, the problem is detected. Additional microwave features

Such household appliances can be equipped with a grill and (or) convection. It is worth understanding how these functions are useful and how they are implemented. What is a grill is clear to everyone. But how it is arranged in a microwave oven, few people understand. To implement this function, an ordinary dry heater (steel, brass or copper tube with a spiral inside) or a quartz heater, which is a glass tube filled with sand, is installed in the chamber. It is he who isolates the spiral preventing it from contacting the shell. Both species have certain features. TENY most often you can move them horizontally (from above) to vertical (to the back wall), but the glass tube is easier to clean.

Some electrical circuits for microwave ovens include a fan that delivers hot air from the coil into the chamber. This mode is called convection.

What kind of dishes can I use for a microwave

Many believe that if a microwave oven heats food as if from the inside, then what kind of dishes are used for this is not important. But this is a very dangerous misconception. Metal containers or a spoon in a plate will quickly disable a household appliance. This is what happens.

The microwave oven circuit involves high-frequency radiation, which heats up liquids. If metal comes in its way, the wave is reflected, heading in the opposite direction. The first thing that suffers is the mica plate covering the magnetron gun. This is especially evident if it contains droplets of not washed fat. Then the radiation begins to hit the magnetron, as a result of which it fails.

Do not think that only ironware is dangerous. Plaques with a dusting, foil pattern and even a metallized rim can easily harm the microwave. Therefore, you should carefully consider which cups are in the chamber.

Microwave Usage Guidelines

It should be understood that some products are not intended for cooking or heating with a similar household appliance. For example, the microwave oven circuit does not allow its use for raw eggs, jacket potatoes, and hard-walled sausages. Such products in the microwave simply explode. Before cooking, eggs should be freed from the shell, and sausages and potatoes should be pierced with a fork.

If the food is not heated in the microwave, but is cooked, it is better to salt it immediately - so the process will go faster. Also in the device you can soften stale bread by putting it in a plastic bag, however, you should be careful. If you overexpose it a little, it will begin to char from the inside, although outwardly this will not be noticeable. When you turn on the function "convection" soften it will not succeed, rather the opposite. It is worth seeing how the fan and the heater are included in the circuit. What are the indicators of the power network?

Microwave ovens are quite sensitive to voltage drops. If it is lowered, outwardly it will be almost imperceptible, however, the load on all nodes will increase significantly. In this case, the heating rate will be much lower. As for voltage surges, they can easily damage the magnetron or the entire control circuit of the microwave oven.

If in the area where the user lives, such cases are not uncommon, you should take care of acquiring a stabilizing device that will protect not only the microwave, but also other household appliances. In extreme cases, you can do with the installation of a voltage relay. Although it does not equalize the indicators, it will be able to turn off the power in time, not allowing overloads on the magnetron.
