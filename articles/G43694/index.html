The salary of an economist. The average salary of an economist in Russia

The salary of an economist in Russia depends on many factors: basic education, length of service, availability of additional skills and knowledge, city, company welfare or institution budget. In order to analyze the level of wages, it is necessary to consider all these points.





Key Responsibilities of an Economist

The standard job duties of an employee-economist suggest that such a specialist:

  • helps to increase the economic efficiency and profitability of the enterprise or company through planning, analysis of financial and economic activities;
  • develops and coordinates budgeting and monitors budget execution;
  • accompanies any economic activity, which includes the calculation of the cost of services, products and the like;
  • compiles reporting.




Graduate with no work experience. Economist assistant

Specialist salary and education

Recent studies have shown that the salary of an economist can greatly depend on which institution the graduate graduated from. Priority is given to the leaders who hire young employees are those who studied at the economic and financial faculties of Moscow State University, MGIMO, the Financial Academy under the Government of the Russian Federation, as well as St. Petersburg State University and the Higher School of Economics. Their salaries at the initial stage can vary from 46,000 to 80,000 rubles.





Economist with experience

With an increase in work experience, the accumulation of additional skills (for example, budgeting), one can claim a higher salary. In Moscow, on average, such a specialist can count on 35,000 rubles, in St. Petersburg - 25,000, and in other major cities - 15,000 rubles.

The salary of an economist with three years of experience can increase two or two and a half times compared with one that does not require work experience. In addition, knowledge of international financial reporting standards (IFRS) and good knowledge of a foreign language will allow you to qualify for a fee of 50,000-65,000 rubles in Moscow.
