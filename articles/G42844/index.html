Halkidiki

What is Halkidiki? This is a Greek nom located in Central Macedonia. It occupies more than 50% of the Halkidiki peninsula. In the north and north-west it borders with the nome of Thessaloniki, and on other sides it is washed by the Aegean Sea. The region includes the islands: Athos, Kassandra and Sithonia. Its capital is Poliyiros.

Halkidiki is called the center of Greek beach tourism. If you visit the Mendi Hotel 4 *, you can see the Petralona Cave. It was in it that the remains of the most ancient man of Europe were found.

The surface of the peninsula is a plateau with domed peaks up to 2033 meters high. These peaks are Mount Athos. Relict pine forests and oak groves grow on the peninsula. On Mount Athos there are amazing fir and beech forests. At the very shore of the sea grows ordinary maquis. The entire coastal zone here is dotted with small bays and bays, endless sandy beaches.

Kassandra

Kassandra is administratively included in the nome of Halkidiki. This is a peninsula located in the northern part of Greece. In general, the Halkidiki Peninsula includes three peninsulas. Kassandra is the most western of them. He was named after the king of Macedonia.





The peninsula is separated from the mainland by the Potidea canal, dug in the narrowest section of the isthmus. The channel connects Thermaikos Bay with Kassandra Bay. The surface is hilly, reaches a height of 353 meters. Tourism on the peninsula is very developed: there are beaches awarded the Blue Flag, hotel complexes.
