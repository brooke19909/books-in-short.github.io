Epic hero Churila Plenkovich

Churila Plenkovich - an epic hero, an extraordinary handsome man, aware of the degree of influence on women of his outstanding appearance. He is not famous for heroic deeds, like the famous Russian heroes, he does not perform feats for the sake of saving the fatherland or any person. The story about him comes down to love adventures. There are only three Bylin about Churil, although they are told in different versions, and an erroneous opinion is created about their larger number.

Russian epics

Bylina is a folk epic song that tells about significant events or the heroic feat of the Russian hero. In epic stories, historians see the events that took place in Russia in the period from X to XIII centuries. The work was performed by a singer-storyteller, often accompanied by a guselny accompaniment.

In the center of many epics stands the figure of Prince of Kiev, Vladimir. Churila Plenkovich also lives near Kiev. Bylina tells about events taking place in the same regions.

In each epic there is a description of either a terrible battle or a feast of fun - two events that could be described in a colorful and fascinating way for a simple listener.

There are widely known Russian heroes, whose strength and loyalty to the Fatherland are described by epic storytellers. These are Ilya Muromets, Alyosha Popovich, Dobrynya Nikitich and others, heroes loved by the people. There are other, visiting heroes, the attitude to which, judging by the stories of epics, is far from unambiguous. For example, Churila Plenkovich or Dyuk Stepanovich.

The name of the hero

Researchers still have not come to a consensus about the origin of the name Churil. Some argue that this is a derivative of Dzhurilo or Tsurilo, and academician Veselovsky is inclined to believe that the old Russian name Cyril is distorted.

The patronymic of the hero is also not easy. Plenkovich is the son of the Film. But the fact is that at first the father was not in epics, only the middle name of the son sounded. Researchers believe that it arose from the characteristics of the hero himself. Chip - a dandy, spit - flaunt. And at first Churila's middle name was Schaplenkovich, that is, Shchegolevich. Then it was transformed into Plenkovich, and then in the eyes of the people the image of the father of the hero was formed.

The image of Churila

What was the hero of the visiting Churila Plenkovich? Positive or negative hero? He is handsome, and from his beauty all women immediately lose their heads. He red tape, does not miss a single girlish look, not a single reddened cheek.

He even differs in some humanity among the heroes of Prince Vladimir, at least in relation to women. All his thoughts are about them, all his dreams are connected with them. Critics note that in the epics there is not a single rude or vulgar expression in the descriptions of the hero himself and his visits to women. It has been suggested that the epic cycle about Churil was written for female performance.

Novgorod origin of the hero

Trying to trace the life of the hero, the researchers came to the conclusion that he lived seven miles from Kiev for only a short time. Most likely, Churila Plenkovich, whose biography interested historians, is the specific prince. But where from?

His wealth, which struck Prince Vladimir and his squad; warriors of the hero who dared not only with impunity to take prey from hunters and fishermen, but also to beat them; the large number of his troops, whom Vladimir was frightened at the very beginning of his acquaintance, all speak of Churil as a native of the northern lands of Russia. These are principalities separated from Kiev, which became independent, surpassing Kiev in strength and wealth, but lacking that power.

It was suggested that the epics about Churil Plenkovich are the result of Novgorod folk art.
