Skipper Channel - a street paved with history

In the western part of Vasilyevsky Island from the street. Bering, leaving Galernaya Gavan on the right side, and Skipersky Canal on the left, then turning right, ends at the marina, the old Skipersky Protok street in St. Petersburg winds.

Historical reference

The harbor itself was created by decree of Peter I. In 1721, a swimming pool for galley ships was dug through a small river called the Black Channel. Then a canal was created, which is now called Skipersky.

Once upon a time here in Galernaya Gavan was formed Skipper settlement, named after the craft of its inhabitants. From its name the name of the street was formed.

At first (from 1812) it was Skiperskaya Street, then (from 1891) - Skiperskaya Embankment, and from 1893 - the embankment of Skipersky Canal.

In the 18th century, the so-called nautical signs were installed at the entrance to Galernaya Gavan - kronshpits or watch towers. These are buildings, previously wooden, then rebuilt from stone, which were crowned with high flagpoles.

They still stand there along the Skipper Channel - West Kronshpitz on 14 Skippersky Canal Street and East Kronshpitz - at 12 AB along the same street. The building of the second, once sentinel, building now houses a museum of the history of rescue on the waters.

Modern street

The length of Skipersky Canal is 2.2 km. Now on this street there are only a couple of hostels and several residential buildings, mainly industrial facilities, research institutes, historical and museum buildings are located here.

Of course, most of the territory is industrial. In general, Vasilievsky Island in this area recently began to be built up with residential buildings.

Sights of the Skipper Channel
