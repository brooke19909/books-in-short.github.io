A brief history of the city

Crimea, Theodosius! These words fill a person with a premonition of meeting with beautiful antiquity, with the magic brush of Ivan Aivazovsky, with the fabulous dreams of the great romantic Alexander Green.

The ancient city with a convenient bay, which housed merchant ships, survived the destructive attacks of the Alans, Byzantines, Khazars, Ottomans. In the XV century, it was owned by Genoa. It was a busy port, in which there were trade missions of various countries. In the city with 20 thousand houses, which was called Kafa, there was a mint where its coins were minted. There was a theater, more than a hundred fountains, shops, bazaars, temples and palaces. At the same time, it was the center of the slave trade. Later, the Crimean Khanate arose on the peninsula, which raided the cities of Russia.

Only in 1783 Theodosius, along with the peninsula after the Crimean War, became part of the Russian Empire. It was a small provincial town in which life was revived only after the construction of the port and the railway. The age-old lull has broken. In the XIX century, the great marine painter Ivan Aivazovsky settled in Crimea in Feodosia.

Together with his family, he did a lot to transform the town. Here, on the Black Sea, he worked every day, and now in his house is a museum with the country's most complete collection of paintings by the great artist.

Theodosius, together with the whole country, suffered the upheavals of the 20th and 21st centuries: the Civil and Patriotic War, the restoration of resorts, the transfer of Crimea to Ukraine, a quarter century of desolation and, finally, return to the Russian Federation.

Cottage Stamboli

Tobacco tycoon I. Stamboli, whose ancestors came from Turkey, built a magnificent oriental-style building on the waterfront. It is decorated with domes, miniature minarets, arched galleries and lined with cypresses. In the yard there is a fountain.

The interior of the country house with high ceilings, stucco molding, marble of various breeds, and patterned parquet is also striking.

Feodosia in the winter

The holiday season ends, but this does not mean that tourists do not come to the city at all. This is the best time to thoroughly visit all museums. The city is beautiful in any weather. At least once you need to see Theodosius in January. You can walk around it for hours without sweating like in the summer, enjoying the peace and beauty of the old mansions. The sea wind is healing in itself. Almost every day there are agricultural fairs from the surrounding areas. Here you can buy delicious products at fairly low prices.
