About the developer

The Oykumena company has been operating in the real estate market since 1997. Since 2005, it has been actively developing the territories of the North-Western District of St. Petersburg, and Graffiti is its fifth project in the housing segment. The developer is considered reliable, delivering objects on time. In addition, the company has its own capacity for the production of building materials, which allows it not to depend on suppliers and build its facilities with minimal costs. A feature of the developer, as already mentioned, is the creation of original architectural forms and the use of advanced technologies in the construction industry. Housing provided on the market receives only positive feedback from buyers, and therefore we can conclude: LCD Graffiti (St. Petersburg) is a worthy place that can be considered as an excellent option for buying an apartment.

About the complex

The construction of the Graffiti residential complex started in 2013. It is planned to build 16 buildings in total. 15 - in twenty four floors and one - in ten. The first was commissioned in 2015. Customers are invited to purchase in it any of the 200 apartments that they like. These are studios, 1, 2 and 3-room apartments. All premises belong to the comfort class, in connection with which they have already been finished. In total, 482 residential premises were put up for sale, but half of them have already been purchased. The completion date of the entire complex is 2019. Construction is carried out using monolithic-brick technology.



Location of the residential complex "Graffiti" - St. Petersburg, Primorsky district. The houses will be located at the intersection of Parachute Street with Shuvalovsky Prospect. The nearest metro station is Komendatsky Prospekt. Getting to her on foot is quite difficult, but public transport can be reached in 15 minutes.

Near the buildings there are several parks and a conservation area, which also contributes to clean air and allows you to spend weekends and leisure hours not in the walls of stuffy high-rise buildings, but in nature.
