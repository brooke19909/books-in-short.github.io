How is a credit card different from a debit card?

Often, the name bank card refers to both a credit and a debit payment instrument. Many even confuse them. In fact, there is a significant difference between them.

The debit card is intended for more convenient use of money - receipt and withdrawal of salaries, purchases in the store, storage of funds in electronic form.

A credit card issued once allows you to use borrowed funds of a financial organization throughout the life of a payment instrument. In fact, this is a reusable loan. Grace period

The grace period is the period of time during which the banking institution allows the borrower to use the money without interest. As a rule, the cards of the company in question have a 50-day grace period. In some cases, this period may be changed.

It is most advantageous to return the borrowed funds to the bank during the grace period, since in this case the borrower does not overpay the extra money.

The terms of use for this period must be specified in the contract.
