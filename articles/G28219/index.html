Reasons for the absence of children in the family

Why didn‚Äôt Lenin have children? Some of his biographers believe that the leader of the world proletariat did not start offspring due to systematic congestion. They are sure that Vladimir Ilyich was afraid that children could become his weak spot. Through them pressure will be exerted.

Recently, historical documents have been found that partially explain why Lenin did not have children. His wife, Nadezhda Krupskaya, had serious problems with the reproductive organs. Historians admit that she acquired an incurable disease due to emigration or during prolonged exile.

At the beginning of the twentieth century, the gynecologist diagnosed her with genital infantilism, at that time there was no cure for this disease. This partially explains why Lenin did not have children.

Skeptics consider this version unreliable, and they explain their position by the fact that in a letter to mother-in-law Krupskaya notes that there are no problems with her health.

It is likely that at that time, and the letter was sent in 1899, the wife of the leader of the world proletariat really believed that she was absolutely healthy. There is also a position according to which Nadezhda did not want to upset her mother-in-law and talk about why Lenin did not have a child.

The closest associates of Vladimir Ilyich repeatedly mentioned in their memoirs how seriously Lenin and his faithful companion lived in the absence of children. For example, Zlata Lilina (wife of Grigory Zinoviev) wrote about how he played with her son, could carry a child on his shoulders for hours, and crawled along with him on the floor.
