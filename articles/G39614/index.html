Voivodeships of Poland: description, history, list and interesting facts

Almost all states are somehow divided into regions, districts, etc., depending on the structure of the country. In this regard, Poland is no exception. The administrative division of the state implies the division of the country into voivodships. These units are entrusted with special functions of self-government. The voivodeships of Poland have their capitals - the largest administrative centers in each of the regions of the country.

What is a voivodship?

Voivodships are called the largest administrative units of a given state. Executive functions belong to the marshal. Represent regions in the national Council of Ministers of Voivods.

What are the powers of the authorities of administrative units? First of all, these are issues that are not resolved at the city and oblast levels. The voivodship of Poland has the right to solve problems associated with the distribution of funds, trade within the region. Also in their competence are issues relating to education, both school and university, as well as investment.





Capitals of voivodeships

Each province has its own capital. It is the largest city in the region in which local authorities are located. The Voivodship Center in Poland, as a rule, is a cultural, economic and political center. The state, in accordance with the 1999 reform, is divided into 16 administrative units with its capitals.

It should be said about the Warmian-Masurian Voivodeship with its center in Olsztyn, Lodz with the capital Lodz, Masovian with Warsaw, Opole with the city of Opole, Sventoszk and the city of Kielce, Greater Poland (Poznan); Lublin (Lublin); Lower Silesian (Wroclaw); Podlaski (Bialystok); Silesian (Katowice); Pomeranian (Gdansk); West Pomeranian (Szczecin); Lubuskie (Gorzow Wielkopolski and Zielona Gora); Subcarpathian (Rzeszow); Kuyavian-Pomeranian (Bydgoszcz and Torun), as well as Lesser Poland (Krakow).

Historical reference

According to the original plan, it was supposed to divide the state into 12 parts, but later on they took as a basis the scheme, which was considered in 1975 and provided for division into 17 large regions. Thus, the 1999 reform reduced the voivodship of Poland from 49 to 16. Moreover, a sufficient number of large cities lost the title of capital. Masovian Voivodeship

The largest in the state is precisely the Masovian Voivodeship. Poland owes him his famous capital Warsaw. In this part of the country there are a large number of famous attractions and historical monuments. Travelers love to come to see the wonderful palaces, dense forests and villages that open up a lot of new things for travelers. Here you can have a wonderful rest.





The usual tourist route in the Masovian land includes a visit to Plock, Vyshogrud, Modlin, Mlawa, Ciechanow, as well as the castle in Czersk.

The region is home to F. Chopin and J. Kokhanowski, which also attracts those who travel to Poland.

In this province there is an opportunity to plunge into the waters of such rivers as the Bug, Narew, Vkra, as well as swim in the Zegrzy Gulf. The land of Mazovia will spoil travelers with a paste of fish, saltseson and honey beer.
