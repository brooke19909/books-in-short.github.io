Terms

The exposition in photography is a list of specific parameters, each of which adjusts to the situation in which the shooting takes place. Your gadget, acting through the lens, measures the amount of light that enters from the outside, and sends commands to the settings. Good light is a vital factor for a good photo.

Expoparameters

We analyze the value of the exposure parameters.

  • Aperture (f). The lens of your device has thin petals (plastic or iron) that open and close the hole in the lens. Aperture limits the flow of light that passes through the lens. This value is marked with the letter f, and the number next to it will show a smaller or larger value. There is such a thing as aperture number. It is denoted by f / x. In this fraction, x is the focal length of the lens.
  • Exposure This parameter is measured in seconds and fractions of a second. It means the time of exposure of a light beam to sensitive material of the camera.
  • Light sensitivity (ISO) displays how a film or matrix responds to light output. The higher the ISO number, the brighter the image.




White balance

Another of the most important parameters when shooting is the white balance. BB is responsible for the color rendition of your frames. This is a complex parameter that can be left unattended during normal shooting. Just auto-tune your gadget.





It happens that the photographer is not satisfied with the color rendition in the frames. This means that you should adjust the white balance yourself. To do this, use a special gray card. If not, just photograph a white sheet of paper. Your gadget will take it as a standard. There is another option, in the settings you can choose one of the parameters: cloudy, incandescent, daylight, and so on.

Let's play with aperture: bokeh effect

What does this funny name mean? This is when a person is shown in the photo, and around him you see a blurred background, the outlines of colored circles, dots, snowflakes, colorful stripes. The background looks exquisite, and nothing distracts the eye from it. The bokeh effect is able to make a masterpiece from the most boring image! Let's move on to practice.

Your optics settings

On what, in principle, does the bokeh effect depend? If you need to specifically obtain a figured effect, then you can not do without special filters that just put on the lens. To make the background just blurry, you need to work on the gadget settings.

So, bokeh depends on:

  • from the depth of space;
  • the degree of sharpness of the subject.

The degree of blur is determined by the following parameters:

  • Diaphragm. She plays the most important role here. The more the petals are opened, the more blurred the background will become. Advice! Be careful when setting the most open aperture. If one person in such a picture is likely to succeed, then a group of people or buildings are likely to be blurry. Use the bokeh effect wisely; not everywhere is it relevant.
  • Focal length. The larger it is, the more noticeable will be the effect. If your optics has a zoom lens, then simply twist its circle.
  • The distance to the subject. The closer you are to a person, the greater the likelihood of a beautiful blurred background. Do not overdo it, because the device still needs to adjust the focus. It is optimal if there is a distance of one meter between the person and the background. Then the background will become a masterpiece.

People for whom photography is their bread acquire fast lenses for work. This is expensive professional equipment, but with such lenses the bokeh effect is amazing.

Macro lenses and blurry portrait lenses are also suitable for blurring. The first ones are needed for shooting very small objects.

Bokeh effect and improvised materials

To create a blur effect, it is not necessary to buy many expensive filters and torture the camera with complex settings. Many professionals come to the rescue tools. It could be:

  • polyethylene film;
  • light tissue (chiffon, organza);
  • scarves with an interesting pattern.

These things can be used as a background. Do not forget: it is optimal that there is a meter distance between the background and the object.

There is another option for using a plastic bag. Wrap it around the lens so that there is a small gap in which the subject will fit. You can fix the package in small pieces of adhesive tape.

Cut a circle or other desired shape in a piece of organic glass, cardboard, orgalipta. Such a home-made filter will allow you to make unique photos, only properly attach it to the lens. Cloth or scarves can also be placed around the edge of the lens to get an unusual effect.

Metering in a photograph (modes)

There are three exposure modes in photography. It is a matrix, dot and weighted average. Consider each of them:

  • Matrix is ‚Äã‚Äãalso called multi-valued, evaluative or multi-valued. In this mode, the camera itself measures the exposure in the photo. To work, you need uniform lighting. This is the most hassle-free mode.
  • Center-weighted. The mode makes metering in the center. The edges of the image remain out of work. Great mode for working on portraits.
  • Point. The most difficult mode. A beginner will not cope with it. Similar to the previous one, but only 5% of the image is measured. To work, it is necessary that the subject is well lit and contrasted.

Metering Methods

Metering types were listed above, but they all have a significant drawback. The result does not depend much on illumination, it is affected only by the subject. For example, take two plates with vegetables. One of them will be orange, the second blue. The lighting in the room is the same, and the light evenly falls on the dishes. Using spot metering, we find out the exposure value of each object. Interestingly, the blue plate has a lower brightness, and the yellow one has a larger one. But if you look, it should not be so, because the lighting conditions for the objects are the same.





How light affects metering

The reason for such differences in exposure under the same lighting conditions is the reflectivity of different colors. You can continue the study and change the dishes to white and black. Illumination should not change. Naturally, exposure for a black plate is needed more than for snow-white dishes. Conclusion: when metering, it is not the illumination of the subject that is taken into account, but its reflective characteristics.
