The colorful city of Aarhus in Denmark: touch the story

The pearl of the Scandinavian peninsula is a true treasury that preserves architectural and cultural monuments. A charming country, which is beautiful both in summer and in winter, is visited by a huge number of tourists who know in advance where Denmark is located. On a world map it can be found in northern Europe. Occupying the Jutland Peninsula, it is washed by the waters of the Baltic and North Seas, and by land it borders only with Germany.



In addition, on a world map, Denmark, divided into 14 regions, includes Greenland and the Faroe Islands. Its capital is Copenhagen, and the amazing Aarhus, which will be discussed today, is the second largest city in the northern country.

A bit of history

In the X century, courageous Vikings founded a small settlement, which eventually turns into a developing settlement. Archaeologists have discovered glass pearls dated to the 7th century, as well as the ruins of ancient buildings. And in the heart of the city they found a pagan burial, which was hidden under the church building.



In the Middle Ages, a terrible disaster came to the village, then called Aros: the epidemic of the bubonic plague that was raging in Europe, kills the lives of almost all citizens. Only in the 19th century after the construction of the railway began economic growth, which continues to this day. Now Aarhus (Denmark), located on the coast of the bay in eastern Jutland, is also an important port of the state.

Open-air museum

Its main attraction is the Old Town. A true open-air museum delights tourists who touch history. Receiving visitors since 1914, it serves up to three million people. 75 beautiful buildings, collected from different settlements of the state, were dismantled by pebbles, transported to the city and then reassembled. All buildings underwent extensive restoration.

The museum traveled a lot around the country, but finally settled in Aarhus (Denmark). In a unique village there are workshops, a school, a theater, living quarters, gardens that are always open for tourists who celebrate the unforgettable atmosphere of an amazing place. Museum workers dressed in clothes of past centuries roam the streets of a fabulous settlement. Before Christmas, a fun fair unfolds here, where you can buy original souvenirs.

Cathedral

The charming city of Aarhus is famous for its ancient religious attraction, originally built in the Romanesque style, and then rebuilt in the Gothic. The highest cathedral in Denmark is dedicated to the patron saint of sailors - St. Clement. The height of the nave is 96 meters, and a spacious inner hall accommodates more than 200 parishioners. Here is an ancient organ, numbering six thousand three hundred and fifty pipes. Also, tourists admire the gilded altar created by the brilliant painter and sculptor Notke, and multi-colored frescoes that have not lost their beauty over several centuries.





Cultural capital

The colorful Aarhus (Denmark), bearing the title of the capital of Jutland, is considered an important cultural and educational center of the country. There are a huge number of interesting museums, the exhibits of which acquaint with the rich history of the city and the state as a whole. And in art galleries there are many works of modern masters.

The spacious concert hall, which is the pride of the city, often hosts the most significant cultural events. The Aarhus Symphony Orchestra (Denmark), well known throughout the country, gives performances here. Three modern scenes and excellent acoustics are the main advantages of a beautiful building that attracts thousands of spectators.

The city botanical garden is a favorite vacation spot for citizens and tourists. Several thousand plant species are collected here, but the most interesting is the collection of exotic orchids. The picturesque park, founded in the 70s of the XIX century, is a wonderful corner created for relaxation.





Locals lovingly call Aarhus "the smallest of the largest cities in the world." And tourists who have visited the original city of Denmark understand how fair this comic statement is. Here, modernity and the past are harmoniously interwoven, and the cosiness of the most ancient settlement of the state is combined with a high standard of living and culture.
