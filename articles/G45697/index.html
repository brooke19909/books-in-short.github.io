Holidays in Turkey

Turkey is now a paradise for those who are looking for a budget holiday. Side

Side is the name of a small town. It is constantly inhabited by just over ten thousand people. This settlement is located on the shores of the Mediterranean Sea, from here it is close at hand to Antalya (only 75 kilometers). The climate here is beautiful and very favorable, especially for families with small children: in winter the temperature rarely drops below 15-17 degrees Celsius, in summer it is a comfortable 30 degrees with a plus sign. You can swim in the sea almost all year round, the water temperature almost never drops below 18-19 degrees Celsius. There are two beaches in Side: west and east. Both of them are marked with the Blue Flag - this is an international award testifying that these beaches comply with all environmental standards and have a high-quality infrastructure. The western beach is considered safer, the entrance to the water there is gentle, under the feet - a sand. This contributes to the fact that in the high season there really are a lot of people, especially families with children. The eastern beach is less popular, but it is also very crowded there. Both beaches offer a huge range of water and not only water entertainments, lifeguards are on duty here, and cozy restaurants and authentic cafes are located near the beaches. Also, this region has a very rich history, many ancient buildings and artifacts have been preserved here. In Side is one of the most popular hotels - Orfeus Park Hotel 4 *. Side Attractions

As for the sights, here, as we noted above, there really is something to see. Some people call this place a "city-museum", with which, in fact, it is difficult to argue. Many sightseeing objects have a history of many thousands - some monuments were created by man even before our era. First of all, you should go to the very center of the city - this is a historical district, the so-called old city. Antique buildings with amazing architecture have been preserved here. Some buildings, of course, turned into ruins under the influence of time and various military conflicts, but some of the "exhibits" still delight the eyes of tourists and local residents. Here you can visit the remains of the ancient Greek city of Agora, next to which are the destroyed fragments of the oldest stone building in all of Turkey - the basilica. But even among the ruins you can see Byzantine symbols engraved on the stone, and next to this place is a small bishop's chapel and palace, which were erected a little later. In high season, there are a lot of tourists in these places, and in the ancient city it can be very hot and stuffy, so do not forget sunscreen, a hat and a bottle of cool water.
