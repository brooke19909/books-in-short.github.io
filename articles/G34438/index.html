PCA Members: List of Insurance Companies

The Russian Union of Car Insurers is a non-profit corporate institution, which is a single Russian association, which was based on the principle of membership of partners providing insurance services for car owners. This union acts for the purposes of interaction, and in addition, the formation and implementation of general standards of professional activity in the framework of compulsory insurance. One of the directions of SAR is also the provision of technical analysis of cars in accordance with Russian law. Find out what insurance companies are on the list of members of the SAR.



Russian Union of Auto Insurers and its Functionality

PCA was established in August 2002. Its founders are considered the forty-eight largest insurance companies in the country. The Union‚Äôs activities are carried out in accordance with Federal Law No. 40, which sounds like ‚ÄúOn Compulsory Car Owners Liability Insurance‚Äù.

The Russian Union of Auto Insurers is included in the register of associations of insurance entities and has the status of a professional center. PCA is the first professional association in the insurance market, whose status is fixed by law. Thus, today, seventy companies that are full members and six observer organizations are members of the PCA.

A professional association of current PCA members performs a number of the following functions:

  • Ensuring the interaction of members in the framework of the implementation of the mandatory insurance procedure.
  • The development, along with the establishment of rules of activity binding on the professional association, as well as its members, as well as monitoring their compliance.
  • The provision and protection in government of interests related to the performance of compulsory insurance services by members of the trade union.
  • Compensation payments to victims in accordance with the constituent documents of a professional association, as well as the requirements of the law.

PCA Rights

Members of the RSA on e-CTP are endowed with the following powers:

  • Formation and application of information resources that contain information on compulsory insurance, as well as data on compulsory insurance agreements along with personal details about the victims. Such information must be used in accordance with the law.
  • Protecting the interests of members of the union, because PCA members provide insurance.
  • Fulfillment of the assigned functions of information, and in addition, organizational and technical support for the implementation of the law in the framework of its activities.

Compensation payments

According to Russian law, PCA makes a series of compensation payments to citizens injured in road traffic accidents. This happens in the following situations:



  • In the event that the institution in which the liability of the person responsible for the incident was insured was revoked, or if it was declared bankrupt.
  • In situations where the culprit is unknown.
  • In the event that the liability of the person responsible for the incident is not insured.
  • As part of the implementation of compensation payments, PCA members regularly form a reserve fund, which is based on the amount of three percent of the collected insurance premiums.

Payout Amounts

The amount of compensation for damage to victims when applying to the Russian Union of Auto Insurers does not differ from the general limits for payments under compulsory motor liability insurance. They make up:

  • up to 400,000 rubles in case of damage to property;
  • up to 500,000 rubles in case of harm to life and health.

Payment terms

A professional association of insurers is required to consider the application of the victim within 20 calendar days, with the exception of weekends. During this period, the PCA is obliged to either make a payment (transfer money to a bank account) or send a motivated refusal to the victim.

The term begins to be calculated from the day of receipt of all necessary documents.

Regression to the culprit

The Russian Union of Auto Insurers may file a recourse to the culprit of the accident (a requirement to compensate for losses incurred in the amount of payments to the victim and organizational expenses). This happens in cases:

  • if the culprit of the accident did not have a valid OSAGO policy;
  • the culprit fled the scene of an accident.
