Environmental protection

In this area, environmental protection is measures aimed at reducing the negative impact of people on the environment. These include:

  • Limit the amount of garbage discharged.
  • Creation of national parks, reserves and reserves for the conservation of natural complexes.
  • The ban on hunting, fishing.
  • Limiting emissions to the hydrosphere and atmosphere in order to improve the overall environmental situation.

Environmental protection - this is the protection of land, soil protection. This includes the whole range of agronomic, organizational, economic, land reclamation, technical, economic, legal measures aimed at eliminating and preventing processes that worsen the condition of the land. It is also the fight against cases of violation of the proper operation of land.

Forest protection is also an integral part of the environmental protection system. Activists fight against fires, concentrated logging, plant diseases, windfalls, pollution of forests, the use of individual selection, which impoverishes the species diversity of trees.

Environmental Protection Activities

When developing such a set of measures, responsible specialists should:

  • Analyze current standards and regulations.
  • Decide on the parameters of the sanitary protection zone.
  • Assess the risk of harm.
  • Make a forecast for the development of emergencies, as well as its consequences.
  • Measure radiation, noise, emissions of harmful chemicals.
  • Calculate the amount of gaseous, liquid, and solid waste generated.
  • Identify adverse factors that as a result of the activities of individuals, enterprises can harm nature.
  • In conclusion, draw up a large set of measures to protect the environment, which will also include alternative solutions.

OOS is also actions to eliminate a threat. For instance:

  • Transition to a new type of fuel, which is characterized by less emissions.

  • Organization of a recycling complex.

Environmental project

Organization of environmental protection at the enterprise is the following measures:

  • Protection of the atmosphere (air).
  • Competent use of land resources, minerals, soil cover.

Official All-Russian Website

OOS is an official resource of the Unified procurement information system. In other words, a public procurement website. Tenders (public procurement) is a competitive form for the publication of orders for the performance of work, the provision of services, and the supply of goods for municipal or state institutions within certain periods and volumes. Competition here should be based on three principles: efficiency, fairness and competitiveness.

As a result, a contract is concluded with the supplier who proposed the best conditions for the municipal (state) institution. Thus, public procurements satisfy the needs of the state in a wide range of works, services and goods. The practice of tenders has a beneficial effect on both the country's economy and business development.

Today, both legal and physical (IP) persons can become a party to public procurement. The procedure itself is regulated by Federal Law No. 44 "On the contract system in the field of the provision of services, the acquisition of goods, and the performance of work for municipal and state needs."

Types of public procurement for environmental protection

According to the aforementioned Federal Law No. 44, all public procurements can be found on the OOS resource. It is also he who is the primary source for all other informant sites in this area.

Let's look at the types of public procurement:

1. Non-competitive - from one supplier, without bidding.

2. Competitive:

  • Open: electronic auction, request for proposals, request for quotations, open tender, tender with a limited number of participants, two-stage tender.
  • Closed: closed competition, closed auction, 2-stage closed competition, limited closed competition.

The main criteria for public procurement

All tenders conducted through the bidder must meet the following criteria:

  • Equally fair attitude to the participants - all suppliers have equal rights, equal access to information, equal chances of participation in the tender.
  • Transparency, openness of the process - the customer is obliged to inform about tendering both at the CAB and other media.
  • The economical expense of taxpayers.
  • Responsibility - only strict reporting. In particular, a detailed justification of the fact why this person was chosen as the winner. FAS controls this process. Both the contractor and the customer are responsible for the conspiracy.
