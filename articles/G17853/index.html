Surgut hotels: addresses, descriptions, reviews

Surgut is the administrative center and largest city of the Ugra district. It is a city of oil and gas industry workers, and two power plants, one of the largest in the world, have been built here. The population is about 350 thousand people. In the city there are a large number of young people, for whom more than thirty secondary technical and higher educational institutions are open. In Surgut, there is a large railway station with an oil terminal, two federal highways pass through it, and there is an airport. Comfortable and modern hotels of Surgut have been built for students, oilmen working on a rotational basis, transit passengers and ordinary travelers.



Short story

The fortress, which laid the foundation for the city of Surgut, was founded at the end of the 16th century by decree of Tsar Fedor Ioannovich. At this time, there was an active development of Siberia along the Ob and Irtysh rivers. Surgut received the status of the county town of the Tobolsk province in 1782. At different times, no more than one thousand 300 people lived in the city, and due to the small population in 1926, Surgut was transformed into a district village. In the sixties of the XX century, active geological exploration led to the discovery of several large oil and gas fields. This was the impetus for the revival of Surgut, and in 1965 he was returned to the status of the city.
