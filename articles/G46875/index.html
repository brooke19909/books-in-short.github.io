Great Ustyug. Attractions that delight

One of the oldest cities in the Russian North is Great Ustyug. The first mention of this place can be found in the chronicles of the 8th century. This period is significant because it was then that the advanced detachments of the Rostov-Suzdal principality stepped on the land belonging to the Finno-Ugric tribes. Over time, the village grew and developed. The territory of the modern city stretches for several kilometers on the left bank of Sukhona. Here, in its original form, amazing temples and monasteries, houses of noble merchants of the 19th century managed to survive. Those who decided to visit Veliky Ustyug, the sights will conquer with their originality and magnificence. The main feature of the city is the fact that there are no wooden buildings. Brick construction here began after a big fire in 1772.



What to see, having arrived in the city of Veliky Ustyug? Photos of the sights of this settlement cannot fully convey the beauty of these structures, because interesting places are, first of all, temple buildings. The central complex is the Cathedral courtyard. The first place in this list is occupied by the Assumption Cathedral, the construction of the building of which dates back to the beginning of the 13th century. At the beginning of the 17th century, the wooden structure was replaced by a stone one. In the same period, the bell tower was attached. During construction, strict monumental forms were sustained. The rich interior decoration also pleases the eye: decorative carving, unique painting, stucco molding. The main value of the cathedral is the iconostasis with carvings and ornaments. Nearby is the Cathedral of Procopius the Righteous. Its decoration is a carved gilded five-tier iconostasis.
