Beaded Lariat: workshop, weaving pattern and recommendations

Beaded lariat is a bright and elegant decoration for the neck. Work on it requires patience and perseverance. Regardless of experience, it will be necessary to spend a sufficient amount of time on weaving the lariat. Let's do it together.

Learning to make beads from laris: a master class

The main question that excites many beginning masters is how many beads will be needed for making? The answer is quite simple - it all depends on how long you want to receive the decoration, as well as how many weaving lines your lariat from beads should have.

The weaving patterns and master class provided in this article will tell you how to make this decoration.

Necessary materials and tools

Before you begin, you will need to collect the following materials:

  • hook (up to 1 mm);
  • fishing line or thread similar in color;
  • a needle (you will need it if you start working with a thread);
  • several pins;
  • chipped pebbles;
  • brown beads: 5 g of a dark shade and 30 g of light.

Having prepared everything, we get to work.

The sequence of tying lariates from beads

First, imagine what your bead lariat will look like. The weaving patterns presented in the article will be needed to simplify the work and fully understand the process.





Let's get down to work:

  • Take the crochet threads and make two airy stitches.
  • You will need to string six beads on a thread and slide them to the resulting loops. By the way, if your beads are of a different color, then this is not scary. You can choose a color combination that you like.




  • After that, you should knit another loop. In this case, you need to grab the first bead.
  • In the same way, you need to knit the rest of the beads.
  • Try to crochet tightly - so that the beads are as close as possible to each other.
  • Form a ring. To create it, you need to crochet a connecting column.
  • String 6 beads on the thread again. After that, insert the hook into the loop under the first one. Now you need to move the new bead closer and lay it on top of the first from the previous row.
  • Pull the thread through the two loops.
  • Further, without changing the schemes, you should crochet the obtained harness.
  • Finishing the weaving of the tourniquet, you must draw its ends. To do this, crochet another row, but without beads.
  • Tighten the thread firmly and hide it inward. Trim the excess tip.

Dressing

Making lariat from beads with your own hands is much more valuable than buying it in a store. You did it, and it is priceless. However, in order to make it look richest, we will tell you how to decorate the lariat with stone chips.

First you need to sew a piece of stone and beads. Then again put on the needle a bead and a chip. In order to pass the thread through the three beads of your harness, you need to step back a little. Thus, you should decorate the end of the harness approximately seven centimeters long. Next, we consider in more detail:

  1. Following this, you will need to unfold and, repeating according to the scheme, embroider the decoration in the opposite direction.
  2. Fasten and cut the thread. In the same way, decorate the second tip of the product.
  3. Then you need to wrap the tourniquet with dark brown beads. To do this, lay out the jewelry so that its tips are located at different heights.
  4. Fasten the thread at one end. String the required amount of beads onto the needle.
  5. Wrap the ends of the harness diagonally.
  6. After making two such twists, fasten the thread and go to one of the ends. Tie the selected tip of the harness three or four times.
  7. After finishing the upholstery, you need to decorate it. To do this, you must string a piece of pebble and bead onto the needle.
  8. Pass the needle through the chip, insert it through 3 beads on the cover.
  9. At the end of the work, fix the chip on the end of the bundle and cut the unnecessary thread.

Master class using mosaic weaving style

You will need a monofilament or thread. You can also use a soft fishing line. Preliminarily think about how you plan to tie, sew decorative elements and attach the ends. To do this, you need: needles, pins, rings, beads and separator caps.

  1. String seven beads on a thread. Thread the first with a needle, closing the ring. In the next row put on the bead and string the beads of the previous row, through one.
  2. Put three beads in the second row. You should get a circle consisting of seven beads.
  3. Weave the next row like the second and continue until you reach the required length of the bundle.
  4. Decoration and fastening of the edges is done after weaving, along the entire length. Without changing the scheme, attach the base.

If the number of beads is large enough, then the lariat from the beads will turn out to be empty and will not hold the shape, which often happens with beginners. The beads in this case bulge outward or fall inward. In order to maintain the shape of the tourniquet, weave it of such a width that they hold independently the desired stiffness.





You can braid a tight cord or use as a base not a thread, but a fishing line.

Pay special attention to thick plaits. Often, when crocheting more than ten beads in a row, the lariate loses its flexibility. This indicates that the thread is too tight.

The final stage

Finished jewelry can be embroidered on the surface, both at the edges and the whole, by embroidering relief elements with monofilament. The ends of the harnesses should be decorated brighter so that the harness seems more voluminous and heavier and so that the lariat looks neater and finer against their background.

Such products can look beautiful and aesthetically pleasing or stand out for their originality. Beaded lariat is not only a decoration for the neck, but also a reflection of your personality.
