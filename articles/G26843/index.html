The most famous sights of Dubna

Dubna is a small city in the Moscow region. The status of a science city, a large number of educated residents and city-forming enterprises set it apart from its neighbors. Well, the friendly population, good roads, polite motorists and lovely sights of Dubna make this place attractive for tourists.





History of the city

The ancient Russian city of Dubna, according to the annals, appeared in the 12th century. Until the 14th century, it belonged to the Grand Duchy of Tver, and then Ivan the Terrible himself became the owner of local lands . In 1956, it receives city status after the organization of the Institute for Nuclear Research. Today, the city of Dubna, whose sights are connected with its history, is a dynamically developing center of science and culture, located in the north of the Moscow region.





Lenin monument

This interesting place has something to see. You should start to see the sights of Dubna from the monument to Lenin. This is not an ordinary monument. The monument , 25 meters high, is considered the second largest in the world. It is noteworthy that it was installed back in 1937 on the banks of the Moscow Canal; on the contrary, another monument was erected - Stalin. In the 60s, the latter was blown up, only the pedestal remained.

Castle Museum

Dubna, whose attractions are scattered throughout the city, is proud of its museums. For example, an unusual museum with a rich collection of various castles. Here you can also find other interesting antique items. The information stands contain interesting data on the history of the museum and castles, and the local antique shop offers visitors antiques.

Museum of the History of Science and Technology

The museum was created in 1993. Its main goal is to preserve the history of the Institute for Nuclear Research. Here you can see the models and devices of various technical devices, as well as take a look at the portraits of famous scientists. Monument IL-2

The most interesting sights of Dubna are connected with the history of the city. For example, in 2003, an IL-2 plane was pulled out of a forest swamp near the city, which crashed here back in 1943. IL-2 aircraft were produced by the factory, located on the spot where modern Dubna is located. The plane was restored, and the monument was unveiled in 2008. Today, tourists can visit the famous fighter and see how it looked during the war. Both children and adults will be very interesting and comfortable here.
