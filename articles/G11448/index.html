Heraklion was then settled by the Saracens in 824 A.D. No wonder they chose a country like Greece. After all, Heraklion was protected by a moat, and its port was used as a refuge for pirates. The city in 961 was unlucky. He was captured by the Byzantines. Therefore, the locals - the Saracens - were exterminated, and the city was thoroughly burned and plundered.

The merging of cultural flows and the impact of the Italian Renaissance spurred art on Crete, which is now called the Cretan Revival. Greece is famous for this cultural heritage.



Heraklion was besieged even by the Turks. This happened in the years 1645-1669, and such outrage continued for 22 years. To great sorrow, Heraklion at the end of the siege fell and subsequently joined the Ottoman Empire. The year 1931 is famous for the fact that Greece took over into its "embrace". Heraklion, like the whole of Crete, was annexed to this country.

The rich history of the city has produced a whole series of valuable architectural monuments, most of which are in the archaeological museum.
