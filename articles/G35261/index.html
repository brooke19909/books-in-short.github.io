Lively Piraeus (Greece): Antique Beauty of a Modern Port

The largest Mediterranean port, inherited from the ancient Hellenes, is an economic center that provides maritime communication with almost all the islands of Greece. In the town, the number of inhabitants of which does not exceed 200 thousand, the tourist infrastructure is actively developing, and more and more foreign visitors come to get acquainted with the ancient village, located on the shores of the Aegean Sea.

Resort with developing tourist infrastructure

The colorful Piraeus (Greece), whose history goes back to ancient times, is a rather noisy and lively city that provides tourists with great opportunities for relaxation. The settlement became part of the country in the 19th century. After the construction of the railway connecting the resort with Athens, it became the main port of the state. International deliveries were made through it, and the country's sea gates, which gained a reputation as a tourist center, began to develop economically.



Unfortunately, many travelers do not stop at the resort, but use Piraeus (Greece) only as a transit point. However, it makes sense to take a closer look at the picturesque corner, where the combination of fresh air, amazing nature and the genuine hospitality of the locals will leave a pleasant impression.

Resort located on the lands of Attica

The port of Piraeus is located twelve kilometers south of Athens, in the bay of the Saronic Gulf. From the capital of the state, it can be reached by public transport or metro. Lying along the shores of the Aegean Sea, it is located on the lands of the cradle of ancient Hellas - Attiki. Attica (Greece) is the true heart of a many-sided country where myths and traditions come to life. This region is divided into two parts: Athens with the suburbs, which includes the port, and other areas.

The main source of income for the resort, given its geographical location, is shipping. Cruise ships and ferries arrive daily here. However, in recent decades, the tourism business is gaining momentum, and new hotels, restaurants, entertainment venues and shops are appearing in the city.

Climate and weather

A large number of sunny days and a comfortable Mediterranean climate are ideal conditions for a wonderful holiday on local beaches. The swimming season, starting in May, lasts until October.

The hottest months are July and August, and the best time for hiking and sightseeing is autumn or spring. And even a warm winter will not frighten travelers who want to get acquainted with a city that has been keeping its own face for many years.

Kastella Hill and Area

Piraeus (Greece) is famous for its hill of Castella, which is the highest point in the city. A fantastic view of Athens and the picturesque bay opens from its top, and not a single tourist leaves without taking photos of the enchanting panoramas of the surrounding area. Here is one of the most interesting areas of the resort - Castella, which delights with neoclassical-style mansions that are connected by quaint labyrinths of narrow streets.



Bustling harbor

The center of the city is the port of Piraeus (Greece), at the entrance to which are the ruins of ancient docks and a watchtower, as well as estuaries, where it is always noisy and the endless circulation of people causes dizziness. To look at the fishing longboats, you should go to the colorful estuary - Mikrolimani.

In the harbor of Passalimani (Zeya) you can enjoy amazing landscapes, admire the expensive yachts, more like floating palaces, dine in the cozy taverns, where amazingly prepare seafood, ride on the fast ship "Flying Dolphin".

What else to see?

The hospitable Piraeus (Greece), the sights of which can be transferred several centuries ago, is famous for the square of Corais. This is a wonderful corner for evening walks, romantic and business meetings. Here is the municipal theater, opened in 1880, the city cathedral, made in neo-Byzantine architectural style.



The archaeological museum, near which are the ruins of an ancient theater, will surprise you with collections of bronze sculptures made before our era, funerary objects, and ceramic items. Many artifacts reflecting the rich history of the port are raised from the bottom of the harbor, for example, stone bas-reliefs with images of Amazons fighting the Greeks.

The antique beauty of the modern city

Attica (Greece) is an ideal corner for those who want to get not only a beach holiday at the country's resorts, but also get acquainted with their rich history. The peninsular territory attracts with its mild climate, memorable landscapes and various excursion programs.



Lively at any time, the seafaring center is a city with an atmosphere of eternal celebration. Piraeus (Greece), the ancient beauty of which harmoniously combines with modernity, and the hospitality of the inhabitants and magnificent landscapes will not leave anyone indifferent, lovers of beach holidays and active entertainment will appreciate it. Each tourist who visited the resort will be able to engage in fascinating shopping, taste delicious seafood dishes and appreciate the quiet charm of the surroundings near the bustling port.
