Modern architecture in Dubai

The United Arab Emirates in recent years has turned from the desert into a real oriental tale. Even in the photo, the architecture of Dubai is a delight. Among the hot sands, a city has grown, which in its architectural grace and unique infrastructure is not inferior to any city in the world.

Burj Khalifa Tower

When it comes to the modern architecture of Dubai, first of all we recall this highest tower in the world, whose height reaches 828 meters. This is one and a half times larger than the second tower in height, located in Shanghai, and three times higher than the famous Eiffel Tower. This architectural object is certainly striking in its appearance. A multilevel structure in the form of a three-beam star, crowned with a thin arrow. However, the internal structure of the tower is much more interesting.

One of the unique structures is high-speed elevators that lift people and goods to the highest floor in seconds. The elevator speed is about 10 meters per second, which is the best indicator in the world. On the territory of the Burj Khalifa there are observation platforms, restaurants, business centers, residential apartments and fitness centers.

The tower is made of durable materials, designed for elevated temperature and direct sunlight, which sometimes heats the surface to 50 degrees above zero. Bridge "Three Graces"

Its special place in the architecture of Dubai is occupied by a truly amazing structure - the Three Graces bridge. It is not known for certain which Graces inspired designers to implement this idea, but the fact that the bridge will never have competitors is clear at first sight. The project was created by an architectural agency in Rotterdam, and its designers wanted to combine symbols of unity and beauty in one building.

A tiny marine creature called radiolaria pushed the architects into a form of construction. Before starting the construction of the elements of the bridge, work was done to create bulk platforms on the ocean floor.

The three main elements are created in almost the same form and create a single architectural picture. All towers are interconnected by special jumpers. This is a kind of bridges, inside which are located various residential and commercial premises, entertainment venues and pedestrian zones. The complex is equipped with an independent indoor climate control system.

During the construction of the bridge, the place was not chosen by chance. Three connected Graces symbolize a certain sea gate for travelers arriving in the Persian Gulf.

Palm islands

In 2001, the United Arab Emirates shocked the world with a palm tree archipelago project.

For several years, builders who lived right there on a moored ship poured tons of stones and sand into the ocean water, gradually raising the bottom and giving the bulk islands the shape of a palm tree. As a result, the Persian Gulf coastline increased by almost 570 kilometers, and the islands turned into three date palms with individual names: Jebel Ali, Deira and Jumeirah.

The islands are surrounded by a crescent-shaped belt, which is a symbol of Islam, and in this case also serves as a breakwater, protecting structures from strong waves. These islands are connected to the mainland by a bridge, so that they can be reached at any time of the day or night. On the palm trees 16 leaves. On each of them, architects placed hotels, shops, parking lots and restaurants.

Palm trees - this is the second building on the planet after the Great Wall of China, created by man, which is visible from space.

Sheikh Zayed Mosque

The wonders of architecture are located not only in Dubai. The capital of the Arab Emirates Abu Dhabi is famous for its amazing beauty by Sheikh Zayed Mosque.

The construction of the mosque was completed in the 21st century. She became one of the largest in the world. She takes the sixth line in the ranking, but this does not become less attractive. The snow-white mosque is named after the first sheikh of the United Arab Emirates - Zayed.

The architectural design and construction of a mosque in Abu Dhabi took an average of twenty years. It was decided to design the building in the classic Moroccan style, but some changes were made during the construction process. Subtle borrowings from the Persian and Arabian directions appeared. The external walls of the temple are made in the Turkish style. This mix made the building unique. In the mosque, you can count about a thousand columns, eighty domes covered with high quality gold leaf. The main square is made of white Moroccan marble.

Jumeirah Mosque

It was erected in 1979 in the very center of Dubai. A feature of the construction was a mixture of medieval style with modern building materials. The building can accommodate more than a thousand believers. A drawing of a mosque in Dubai was placed on a local banknote.

The architecture of Dubai is impossible to imagine without the design of the Fisher‚Äôs rotating tower . There are enough projects like this tower in the world, but only she was ahead of her brothers in self-sufficiency.

This unique building is under construction and will be built in Dubai, despite the fact that the city is already famous for its architectural wonders. The David Fisher Tower is a synthesis of conventional housing and equipment that converts wind energy into electrical energy. Opera and cultural center

The most unusual building in the architecture of Dubai will be the famous opera. The project was created under the direction of Zaha Hadid, a British architect with Arabic roots. It was she who brought to life the unusual, conquering with her softness forms in the building of a cultural center of world scale.

The view of the building is very fantastic. It resembles sand dunes, which are common in the Arabian desert. The opera will be located on the artificial island "Seven Pearls". But this does not hinder getting to the cultural center by car, as the island will be connected to the mainland by a bridge. As planned by the architect, up to three thousand guests will be able to attend opera events, and the theater itself is designed for 800 people. The hotel will be located nearby. The building is under construction.

Leaky Tower

Views of Dubai can please even a sophisticated esthete. With each new project that the Government of the United Arab Emirates brings to life, there is one more architectural miracle in the world. This also applies to the amazing "holey" tower, which you can admire in Dubai. "O-14" is the name of an administrative building that looks like a piece of cheese, which houses business centers and office buildings. The construction is unique in that, thanks to the many holes, each square meter is perfectly lit. And if natural sunlight is not enough, then architects know how to solve this problem with the help of modern equipment.

Michael Schumacher Skyscraper

In 2008, the German architectural company LAVA created a high-rise building project, named after the famous Formula 1 driver Michael Schumacher. The skyscraper is under construction and will comprise 29 floors, on which not only residential premises will be located, but also retail outlets, offices, restaurants and bars. According to this project, the creators plan to build seven skyscrapers around the world - in terms of the number of rider wins in various competitions. And the first will be in the United Arab Emirates.

All attention is focused on aerodynamics, and from the side it seems as if the tower rushes out of the water up at great speed. The base of the building looks like a marina, and the apartments on the lower floors are the most expensive, because each has its own exit to its own marina.

The tower has an impressive number of balconies. Due to their vertical arrangement, the structure has such a dynamic look.
