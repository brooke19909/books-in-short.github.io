Why exactly Yuri Gagarin?

Obviously, the choice of the person who was the first to fly into space was lengthy and difficult. Among the applicants were two dozen pilots, who, however, were not considered the best pilots in the country. Since the future cosmonaut himself was chosen by Korolev, he primarily drew attention to indicators such as height and weight, since the dimensions of the first spacecraft were very modest. Another condition was membership in the CPSU.



The preparation of the first flight was complicated by the fact that the Soviet Union had to get ahead of America. Flight

The launch took place on April 12, 1961, the Vostok spacecraft departed from the Baikonur Cosmodrome. Having made one revolution around the Earth, the ship completed its flight, after which Gagarin's cosmic call sign, however, like the astronaut himself, became famous throughout the world.

Since there was a failure in the braking system, the apparatus with the new world hero landed not in a place prepared in advance - 110 km from Stalingrad, but near Engels, in the village of Smelovka. He landed where such a valuable guest was hardly expected, and it is unlikely that some of the local residents knew what Gagarin's call sign was.

Cosmonaut meeting

Since Gagarin landed in an uninhabited area, the first person he met was the forester's wife and granddaughter. A little later, the military arrived at the scene, who secured the descent vehicle and took the astronaut to the nearest part. Already being in the unit, Gagarin transmitted a message to the division commander, in which he said that the flight was successful, and he was safe and sound.





At the same time, a helicopter set off from the airport in Engels to find and take on board Gagarin. They did not find the astronaut at the landing site, but the locals informed them that he went to Engels in a truck. On the way to the city, the pilot saw Gagarin waving his hands, and picked him up to go to the airport together.

He was already expected at the Engels aerodrome so that he could go to the base, report on his successful work and so that from that moment the whole world would know what Gagarin had the callsigns during the first space flight. On the spot he was handed a congratulatory telegram from the government of the Soviet Union. Yuri Gagarin, a world-renowned astronaut, was taken to the control room, and then to the headquarters of the base, at Pobeda so that he could report back to Moscow about the results of his work.

Report

Soon, two aircraft appeared at the airport in Engels, on board which arrived the deputy commander of the Air Force and a team of journalists. While the dispatchers were establishing contact with Moscow, Gagarin talked to reporters and took pictures. As soon as contact was made, Yuri Gagarin personally informed Khrushchev and Brezhnev that the flight completed successfully.
