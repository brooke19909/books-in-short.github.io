Bali - sea, island, ocean?

Bali is an island that is part of one of the provinces of Indonesia. It is also one of the Lesser Sunda Islands. Geography, nature, and the culture of the inhabitants of a small island located between Eurasia and Australia are noteworthy. What else can you learn about this small island?

Bali geography

The coordinates of the island: 8 degrees south latitude and 116 degrees east longitude. Bali has a shape resembling a fish. And even with this he attracts already from the porthole of the plane. In its Indonesian archipelago, Bali is not one of the largest islands, but its importance for the country in economic terms is great. It stretches from west to east, having a width of 120 km. For amazing landscapes called this place Paradise on Earth! And these words are not an empty phrase. A chain of 6 volcanoes of different heights is truly amazing. In this case, one of the volcanoes is considered active today. This is Gurung Agung. He is also the highest point in Bali. In the craters of the frozen giants are mountain lakes. They give rise to numerous mountain rivers, whose waters are used for irrigation. The rainforests that cover the surface of the island seemed to have grown out of adventure films. Closer to the coast are numerous rice fields. They themselves are considered an attraction of the island and attract tourists here.

The waters washing the island

Almost all along the coast stretched beaches equipped with hot sand and pleasant shallow sea. It is curious that by visiting this island, a person will be able to swim in the waters of both the Indian and Pacific Ocean. And to the question, what is washed by Bali, the ocean or the sea, the answer will be: both. In the south and west, the Indian Ocean adjoins the island. From the nearest island of Java in the west, Bali is separated by a strait.

But then what is the sea in Bali? The most interesting thing is that in the north the island is washed by the sea with the same name. It is part of the Pacific Ocean. The coincidence in the names is found here more than once. So, Bali is separated in the east from the island of Lombok by the Strait of the same name.

Climate conditions

The sea washing Bali, although it belongs to the largest ocean on our planet and is located north of the island, it brings warm waters to the coast. Due to its tropical location, the climate on the island is relatively stable in terms of temperature: both in winter and in summer, the thermometer column ranges from +25 to +32 degrees Celsius.

If a person plans his vacation by the sea in Bali, then it is worth choosing a summer period. It is at this time that the weather will be sunny and without unpleasant natural surprises. In winter, tropical showers and storms hit the island. It is worth considering the fact that throughout the year, humidity indicators are high. Therefore, it will be difficult for unaccustomed tourists to breathe.

Where to find your place on the island?

Balinese are always ready to receive guests on their small island. Especially for tourists there are many diverse hotels. Here everyone can find something to their liking: from small complexes with furnished houses made in a traditional style to more modern apartments with unforgettable views of the rainforests and summer blue sea.

Residents themselves also equip their homes so as to receive tourists for a while. This option is suitable for those who want to truly get in touch with the life of the island of Bali, its traditions and culture of the people.

Culture of the nation

People living on the Indonesian island, have become famous throughout the world for their crafts. The traditions of the people are reflected in pagan sculptures and figures carved from wood, in clothes, painted fabrics and jewelry. So, batik (the art of drawing on fabric) here is not only a hobby for local residents, but also a full-fledged craft for earning. Traditional Indian murals are applied in several layers on plain cotton fabric or expensive silk. From the resulting paintings create festive clothes, jewelry and souvenirs for tourists.

The observance of religious traditions is of great importance for the Balinese, therefore, in every city and village, even every family has its own temple. Moreover, separate shrines are built to honor the gods, deceased relatives and close people.
