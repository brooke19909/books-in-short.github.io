What to do if you do not give a loan: reasons, tips and advice

Everyone can face such an unpleasant situation as a refusal to issue a loan. Often this happens for unexplained reasons. What to do if they do not give a loan? How to find out the reason for refusal? How to fix a credit history and remove yourself from all blacklists? Let's try to find answers to these questions together.

Why do banks refuse

Almost every person in his life faced an acute shortage of funds. Delayed salaries, unplanned financial expenses, force majeure situations - these are the most common causes of this situation. It would seem that you can borrow money from a neighbor, immediate family member, work colleague or friend. However, practice shows that recently no one has extra money. Fortunately, the services of Russian banks can quickly solve any financial problems and get the right amount. But not so simple.

Often after the application is submitted, the client is refused a loan. Why is this happening? What to do if they do not give a loan? How to find out the reason for refusal? It should be noted right away that the bank has the right not to explain the reason for such a decision. But money is needed, and another attempt to get it ends in failure. What to do if they do not give a loan? Let's first understand the main reasons for the negative decision of the bank.

Meet by clothes

Paradoxical as it may seem, the appearance and behavior of the potential borrower play an important role. The loan officer must pay attention to the appearance and behavior of his client.

Self-doubt, nervousness, untidiness - all this negatively affects the impression of the applicant and the decision of the bank. If the client is confused in the answers, constantly hides his eyes, looks away, hesitates, then the loan officer will begin to doubt the solvency of the applicant and the integrity of his financial reputation.

Therefore, a personal visit to the bank should be given special attention. Well-groomed appearance, clean clothes of a neutral style, as well as confidence in the eyes can help get what you want.

There is never much money

Low income is another reason why a bank has the right to refuse its client. What if you do not give a loan in this case? First, find out the size of interest rates and the size of the monthly payment.

When applying for a loan, the solvency of the applicant is examined in detail - information on the specified source of income and its level is checked. If the applicant‚Äôs salary is not enough to fulfill loan obligations (taking into account all mandatory payments), the bank will refuse to issue a loan.

But too high a level of income can cause a negative decision of the bank. The fact is that an overly solvent client will be able to repay the loan prematurely, which means that the financial institution will lose its profit.

What to do if they do not give a loan? First of all, you need to look for the cause of the failure. And one of the most common is a bad credit history. Let us dwell on this concept in more detail.

Borrower financial standing

Priority is being given to this moment. The credit history of the client is thoroughly checked, and based on the information received, conclusions are drawn about the creditworthiness of the applicant.

The bank should check if their potential client is sitting in a debt hole. To exclude the possibility of failure, the borrower must make sure personally that his financial reputation is impeccable. To do this, it is enough to send a request to the BCI (credit bureau), after which the client will be able to find out if there are any old debts on it.

It is noteworthy that some banks do not give a loan if there is no credit history at all. They do not have information about the solvency of the client, and they do not want to take risks.

Other reasons for refusal

What to do if they do not give a loan and do not say the reason for the refusal? Trying to determine what was the reason for the negative decision of the bank.

Inconsistency with the age criterion is another reason why a potential borrower may receive an SMS notification with the text "Loan denied." Few Russian banks issue loans immediately after reaching adulthood. However, the young applicant must be 21 or even 23 years old. This is due to the fact that the 18-year-old client is in the so-called risk group (he can be taken into the army, he can study at a university on a full-time basis, that is, it will be difficult to repay a loan to such an applicant).

Banks that make their customers loyal requirements (with regard to age restrictions) most often offer somewhat harsh credit conditions (increased interest rates, short periods, etc.).

A criminal record is an obvious reason for refusal. A person with a tarnished reputation will not be able to get a loan from a large Russian bank. The maximum that he can count on is a loan to MFIs.

A woman with obvious signs of pregnancy will not receive a loan, because after the birth of the baby she will be deprived of a constant source of income.

Do not give a loan. What to do?

It is worth understanding the algorithm in accordance with which the bank decides whether to grant a loan or not. The client submits an application for a loan, indicating the amount of the requested amount and the desired loan term. If a potential borrower knows that his financial reputation is impeccable, he meets the age criteria and has a constant income, you can collect all the necessary documents in parallel.

Upon receiving the application, the bank begins to analyze the data: it checks whether the applicant has a constant source of official income, compares its size and the amount of the monthly payment, and also sends a request to the BCI. The time for considering the application is different: some banks check quickly (within 2-3 business days), others may delay with a preliminary decision for a week. Much depends on the size of the requested loan. So, if we are talking about a small amount for consumer needs, the bank can inform about its decision directly on the day of application. In the event that the client claims to receive a mortgage loan, you can wait a month from the loan officer for a whole month.

What happens next

The applicant receives a notification of the preliminary decision of the bank, after which he needs to come to the office in person to sign a loan agreement. However, in some cases, the client is denied a loan for unknown reasons.

Do not give a loan? What to do with it? You should not call the bank and try to find out why it is refused, since the employees of the credit department have the right not to disclose information about the reason for the refusal. You should try to figure it out on your own. For this, it is necessary to study such factors in detail:

  • Credit history status.
  • Compliance with age criteria.
  • Source of official income and size of the latter.

It so happens that banks do not give credit. What if the cause of the failure is inexplicable? Try your luck at another financial institution.

Perhaps there is more luck.

Why do banks not give loans? What to do? Where to find the missing amount? Sometimes Russian banks suspend the issuance of loans due to the difficult economic situation in the country. This does not happen often, but this factor also needs to be taken into account.

  1. Microfinance organizations. There they issue small amounts (within 50 thousand rubles) for a short period of time (usually up to one month). The interest is slightly overstated, but the requirements are minimal.

How does a bank verify a potential borrower

The very first stage is the client‚Äôs compliance with the declared characteristics. After receiving the application, the loan officer begins to ring the applicant‚Äôs relatives and his colleagues. So he learns whether a potential bank customer is a responsible and solvent person.

Then, the loan application is sent directly to the bank, and the customer information is checked in more detail: requests are sent to the BKI and so-called databases with a ‚Äúblack list‚Äù of borrowers. Thus, the lender will find out if the applicant has large debts, outstanding loans and fraud. The presence of even one of the above items will cause a refusal.

Most banks in Russia use a specific system for evaluating the responses of a potential borrower. It is called scoring. During its application, each client‚Äôs response is assigned a certain number of points, the amount of which determines the reliability of the applicant. Suppose a bank has a minimum score of 100 for a positive decision. If a client scores 90, the loan will not be approved.

They may also refuse to issue a loan due to the disagreement of one of the co-borrowers. For example, when applying for a mortgage loan, the level of income and financial reputation of not only the applicant are taken into account. In this case, the characteristics of the spouse who act as co-borrowers are taken into account.
