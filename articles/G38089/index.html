Brief biography of the pilot

Yuri Gagarin - the man who made the world's first space flight. It is worth noting that for this journey into outer space he received the honorary title of Hero of the Soviet Union. Yuri was born on March 9, 1934 in the village of Klushino. In 1941, Gagarin went to school, but already in October it was occupied by German troops. Soon they drove the family of Yuri into the street, because of which they had to live in a dugout. Gagarin witnessed the most terrible events when his father was severely beaten and forced to work, and his mother chased after the cars that took their children away. After that, Yuri never mentioned the events of the Second World War. After a year and a half of occupation, school studies resumed.

Yuri Gagarin entered the Lyubertsy vocational school and evening school for working youth.

In 1951, he entered the Saratov Industrial College. In 1954, the pilot first visited the Saratov flying club. The next year he managed to finish his studies with honors, he achieved great success, after which he made the first flight. It is worth noting that in the flying club Yuri Gagarin made 196 flights.

Everyone knows and remembers Gagarin. Because he made the first flight into space. But it is worth noting that very few know where Gagarin landed.

Where did Gagarin land?

Every student knows who made the first flight into space. In honor of this event, Cosmonautics Day is celebrated every year on April 12th . Surprisingly, many do not know about the place where Gagarin landed.

On April 12, 1961, residents of the Smelovka village of the Saratov Region heard an explosion in the sky, after which two parachutes descended to the ground. The villagers who witnessed this event did not even suspect that they were the first to see Yuri Gagarin after flying into space.

The village of Smelovka near the city of Engels is the place where Gagarin landed. It is worth noting that six years earlier the pilot graduated from the Saratov Industrial Institute, so the place of his landing became more than symbolic.

After 4 years, an obelisk was installed there in the form of a rocket soaring up. In 1981, right in front of the obelisk, a monument was erected to the first man in space - Yuri Gagarin. Currently, there is also a leisure park.

The place where the great pilot landed is memorable not only for residents of the Saratov region, but also for citizens of the entire Russian Federation and guests of the country.
