What is LTE in a smartphone, what is this system for

The mobile communications market is actively developing, with every day they offer more and more services and applications designed to make life as easy as possible for a subscriber of cellular networks. It is no secret that many wireless consumers regularly use the resources of the World Wide Web on their gadgets . Almost every smartphone now released with LTE support is a mandatory requirement of the modern information age.





A Brief History of Mobile Internet

So, the Internet has become an integral part of any modern smartphone. It helps to check mail, chat on social networks, and just get the necessary information in a short time. Previously, the phone was used extremely rarely as a network access point. This was due to many reasons: the quality of the connection left much to be desired, the speed of the Internet made me pretty nervous, in addition, the price played an important role. Traffic on mobile devices was initially quite an expensive pleasure for the average user of this type of communication. However, nothing stands still. The largest mobile network operators and gadget manufacturers quickly realized all the benefits of introducing and cheapening technologically advanced Internet connection methods for users of mobile devices.





Fourth generation networks

What is LTE in a smartphone? Let's take a brief excursion into the history of the development of network technologies. So, everyone remembers GPRS quite well. This technology for connecting to the World Wide Web required remarkable endurance and was fabulously expensive, so it was not popular. It is being replaced by a new technology called the ‚Äúthird generation of mobile services,‚Äù or 3G. This technical achievement began to be introduced in the 2000s. Its distinctive feature is a two-peer connection, which allows to increase the data transfer rate up to 3.5 Mbit / s. This means the ability to view movies, videos and other traffic-intensive files on a smartphone. In addition, the quality of communication has improved, and in the networks of this generation a quick transition from voice call to the further use of Internet surfing is possible. But they were replaced by more developed networks - the fourth generation, or 4G. This latest technology allows you to access the World Wide Web at speeds up to 100 Mbps - this is what LTE is in a smartphone.

How LTE works

Now let's try to figure out the latest generation networks, their support and the ability to use in various situations. Each generation of communication has been changing for ten years, and new, increased demands are being made on them. What is LTE in a new generation smartphone? This is an opportunity for comfortable use of voice and video communications and, importantly, this is high-speed Internet access. Ceteris paribus, the clear advantages are undeniable: quick download of information, the exchange of large files, a clear picture when viewing online. All this is ensured by multi-peer connection and packet data transmission. However, next-generation networks have a limited coverage area. At the moment, these are megacities, large cities and some capitals of the regions of the Russian Federation. All new generation gadgets have LTE support, but their price is quite high. Chinese smartphones with LTE are in constant demand - as cheaper, but with similar specifications. Now you can imagine what LTE is in a smartphone, and what this generation of communications means for.
