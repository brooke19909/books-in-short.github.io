How to make cranes from paper step by step?

Origami is a national Japanese art. And the crane is one of the symbols of Japan. Paper crane - a classic figure in origami. It is mentioned in books of the eighteenth century. According to an ancient Japanese legend, one who can make 1000 paper cranes makes a wish that comes true!

How to make cranes out of paper? Here we look at it step by step.

History

There is a legend about paper cranes by Sasaki Sadako, a Japanese girl who lived in Hiroshima. 08/06/1945 an American atomic bomb was dropped on a Japanese city . Sasaki at that time was in the house, only a few kilometers from the explosion. She remained alive, although a blast wave threw her through the window of the room. Nine years later, she began to die from leukemia - such a diagnosis was made by doctors. Just before her death, the girl found out about the legend according to which a person who made a thousand cranes from paper can make a wish that will come true! Sadako knew how to make cranes out of paper, but managed to add up only 643 - and died of an illness. Her friends finished the job, and the girl was buried along with a thousand cranes. Since then, in Japan, as well as around the world, the origami paper crane has been a symbol of peace.

  1. Take a square (required!) Sheet of paper. If you have a rectangular, regular sheet, then you need to cut a square out of it.

  2. We fold the square sheet diagonally, unfold and fold it on the other diagonal. Then turn the sheet on the back side. Fold in half. We deploy again, fold them in half again, but on the other sides. It turns out the markup of intersecting lines.

  3. Take our leaf in the middle where all the folds intersect and fold. Bend the corners. According to the rules of origami, if you make a crane, the workpiece is closed angle up. If a flower, then down.

  4. We bend corners on the other hand.

  5. We bend the upper triangle.

  6. Expand all three corners. Then we lift one paper layer from the sharpest end and lay it with a â€œboatâ€.

  9. Crane is ready, almost. The upper triangles become wings. The lower ones will be the neck and tail. Fold the lower triangles in half along, first on one side, then on the other.

  10. Extend the lower parts in different directions - this is the head and tail!

  11. We twist a narrow strip and tuck it between the wings on one and the other side.



  12. We bend the end of a narrow strip - this is the head of a crane.

  13. Gently pull the ends of the wings. The back of the crane will straighten out in the center, add volume to the figure!

Application

Now you know how to make cranes out of paper. Our finished paper crane (or better, a few!) Can be attached to a string and suspended, for example, on a chandelier. You can always use multi-colored cranes as interior details. And also the crane can be presented to someone as a keepsake.
