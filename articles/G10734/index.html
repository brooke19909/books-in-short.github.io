Medvezhye Lake is familiar to many thanks to its healing salt waters. The nearest city is Kurgan, one hundred eighty kilometers to it.

This pond is divided into two parts, they are connected by a small strait. Lake Medvezhye has no drains; its recharge is provided by melt water. It is quite large in area (61 square kilometers), but shallow (the maximum depth is one meter and twenty centimeters).



Mysterious tradition

According to legend, in the waters of this lake, once upon a time, a bear cured its sore paw. After miraculous healing, the reservoir acquired the outlines of this beast and, accordingly, its current name.

People began to study these places in the early nineteenth century. Interestingly, healing mud was discovered purely by accident. At first, the locals were more interested in salt, the deposits of which were found in large quantities in the reservoir.
