A bit about the resort

As we have already mentioned, the Boran Mare Beach Club (Kemer) is located near the village of Goynuk, which is a very popular place located near the famous resort. The village is located only seven kilometers from Kemer. And it is not so far from Antalya Airport to get to it.

In the village of Goynuk, the coast is more rocky, there are many cliffs and gorges formed by the mountains approaching the sea itself. In the village, rest will be interesting not only for families with children, but also for those who are interested in mountain tourism.



In ancient times, local lands were under the control of robbers. And only in the eleventh century did Turkmen nomads appear here. It was they who, struck by the beauty of the coast, gave Goynuk his current name. Currently, the village has a beautiful coast, which is covered from all sides by mountains. The town itself is small, but not without charm. It is planted with pomegranate and orange trees.

The main attractions of the resort are natural landscapes, mountains, grottoes, caves, cozy coves and beaches. Moreover, the main pearl, of course, is the canyon, which is even considered a natural monument. Horseback riding and hiking are organized along the canyon, so everyone can go on an exciting mini-trip.

Resort Attractions

Goynuk is a small village, so there are no special attractions in it. Behind them it is worth going to Kemer. But lovers of mountaineering must visit the canyon. The gorge reaches 350 meters in height, while its width is only six meters, and the length of the gorge is fourteen kilometers. However, not everyone can master a walk in such a place. Part of the road passes through shallow water, so you need to have comfortable shoes that are not afraid of water. Yes, and a swimsuit also does not hurt. In general, a walk can be recommended to those who are ready for the fact that they will have to overcome more than one kilometer on foot.



As for Kemer, it is not difficult to get to it. Between settlements traditional dolmushi ply. In the city itself there are many interesting things, especially for shopping lovers. A couple of times a week in Kemer there is a bazaar with a characteristic oriental flavor. It is worth a visit to purchase a variety of souvenirs, leather goods, suede and ceramics.

Kemer will also be interesting to history buffs. Seven kilometers from the city are the ruins of ancient Phaselis. No less interesting are the ruins of ancient Olympos. Not far from Kemer, an ethnographic park was created in which you can see how the nomad tribes lived.

Tourists are always impressed by Yanartash Mountain, from the depths of which a flame breaks out. She is always shrouded in smoke. There are several explanations for this phenomenon. Experts say that the cause of fire is the accumulation of gases at the surface of the earth. But there is a more fantastic version that the locals will tell you, telling an old legend.
