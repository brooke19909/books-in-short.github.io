Exotic Barbados. Caribbean Pearl Island

Barbados is also called the "pearl of the Caribbean", or "little England", it refers to the archipelago of the Lesser Antilles. This place is very popular among wealthy tourists, because nowhere else in the world can you find such snow-white beaches, a variety of flora and fauna. A huge number of man-made and natural monuments, attractions, a high standard of living, luxury hotels, carnivals, quiet corners, beautiful landscapes - all this is Barbados. The island will appeal to absolutely all categories of travelers, since there is something to do both for lovers of active pastime and those who wish to relax in a cozy place alone with nature.



The beauty of the local landscapes, snow-white, and in some places even pink beaches contribute to the fact that this island is very popular with newlyweds and couples in love. Weddings are held here, honeymoons are held , hotels are always ready to offer special holiday rooms. The island of Barbados will not let anyone get bored. You can go diving on the west coast. Some species of fish cannot be found anywhere else in the world. South and east coasts are suitable for windsurfing and surfing, as strong winds blow there.

By the number of holidays, festivals and carnivals held, few can surpass Barbados. The island is especially famous for the sugarcane harvest festival : it begins in July and ends on Cadment, which falls on the first Monday of August. Also, jazz music lovers gather here annually, athletes come from all over the world to show their strength in sports such as cricket and golf.



You should definitely go to the capital of Bridgetown to get to know Barbados better. The island has many secrets. Here you can find untouched corners of nature, sit in deserted bays and beaches surrounded by palm trees - no one dares to distract from the contemplation of the beauty of local landscapes. After visiting all the historical monuments, it becomes clear how much Barbados has borrowed from England, even the center of the capital is a copy of London's Trafalgar Square.



Barbados is also famous for Harrison's unique caves, which are a gallery of stalagmites and stalactites . The island includes a huge number of parks and reserves, in the south are sugar cane plantations. One of the main attractions are limestone caves, in which there is clean fresh water filtered by numerous layers of rocks .

Absolutely all the beaches are accessible for visiting, because first of all, the island of Barbados takes care of the convenience of the guests. Tours, the prices of which are not so sky-high, are available at any time of the year. The whole island is suitable for a pleasant pastime, although only the southern and western coasts are considered the most developed in terms of tourism. Barbados gives tourists the right to choose: here you can stay in a luxury hotel, participate in daily entertainment programs, go to nightclubs, discotheques, or you can find a nook and shelter there from all the problems and worries, left alone with nature.
