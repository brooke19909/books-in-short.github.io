Also, history is a science about the laws of the development of society. It allows you to take into account the mistakes of the past in order to act more effectively now and in the future.

The approaches



What approaches does science use to study the development of human society? If a person who is in a materialistic position is engaged in it, then it is believed that history studies certain patterns of development, which are limited by some kind of spatio-temporal framework. In the Marxist approach, much attention is paid to labor, methods of production and the implementation of economic activity. In addition, they consider various historical conditions, such as relations between different countries, class struggle, geographical and other studies. There is also a pluralistic approach to the study of processes. He does not recognize the existence of a common cause of development in human history, but he holds the idea that many different factors acted on society. They, in turn, are regulated by different social groups and organizations.

Scientific method



Whatever approach is used by man, there are a number of common features. So, the research uses the scientific apparatus and certain categories. The most important of the latter is the concept of ‚Äúhistorical time‚Äù. In this category, an arbitrary event is characterized in terms of time and place of action. History as a process is seen as a movement between different events. Also, this science, which studies the development of human society, works exclusively with facts. They form the basis of knowledge. It is on them that all concepts and ideas are based. An understanding of historical reality and the development process largely depends on the reliability of the facts. Therefore, let us give them special attention.

Historical fact

This concept is considered in two senses:

  1. The phenomenon that has taken place.
  2. Its reflection in historical science.

There is a close connection between them. So, some facts may provide the reader with too little information to fully understand it. Therefore, they are used with certain "comments" that historians give. The meaning of the content depends on the ideological, theoretical and general scientific views. Therefore, the influence of science on the development of society should not be underestimated. After all, a person forms a science and falls under the influence of feedback. Does this mean that it is impossible to achieve historical truth? No, it is not. People's ideas about the truth are changing. Science develops from incomplete or relative awareness to a more perfect and accurate form of data presentation. But you should not count on absolute truth. After all, as long as society exists, the last chapter of history has not yet been written.

Sources



They are necessary so that history, this science about the laws of the development of society, can develop and improve, providing us with the most complete and accurate information. There are 6 groups of sources:

  1. Written. As such sources are called epigraphic monuments, graffiti, birch bark letters and manuscripts.
  2. Real. These include tools, utensils, clothes, household items, jewelry, coins, remnants of dwellings, weapons and so on.
  3. Ethnographic. The remains and survivals of ancient life, which have survived to this day.
  4. Folklore. This is folklore - songs, traditions, tales, sayings, proverbs and so on.
  5. Linguistic. Personal names, geographical names and the like.
  6. Film and photo documents.
