Sights of Parma: main places

Parma is a provincial Italian town founded by the Gauls before our era. Since then, the city has grown significantly, gained a confident status and recognition not only in Italy, but also in the world. The atmosphere of lazy comfort, elegance and prosperity wonderfully fit into the architecture of its ancient streets. An overview of Parma with its sights you will find further in the article.

A bit about the city

Parma is located in the north of the country within the Emilia-Romagna region. From the very moment of its foundation, the city was passed from hand to hand. He managed to be ruled by the Romans, Lombards, Franks, and even Spain and France. Parma joined Italy only in 1860.





The change of government did not prevent the city from developing as a commercial, agricultural and cultural center. Therefore, the best place for cultural, educational and gastronomic tourism is precisely Parma, Italy.

The sights of the city are ancient squares, churches and cathedrals, monasteries, palaces, beautiful parks and numerous museums. What is the University of Parma - one of the first higher educational institutions in Italy.

Popular not only the architectural attractions of Parma. Many come here for culinary delights. The city produces the world famous Parmesan cheese or Parmigiano Reggiano, as it is called here. The demanded local dishes are tortelli d'erbetta, anolini in brodo and Parma prosciutto.

Parma Attractions

The lion's share of the city‚Äôs iconic spots is located in the Parma-Centro quarter. Garibaldi Square is one of the main squares. Here is the city hall with a sundial of the 16th century, the neoclassical church of St. Peter, built in the first century.

Right next to Garibaldi Square is the Basilica of Our Lady of the Fence. According to legend, it was built after the face of the Virgin Mary appeared on one of the walls of the city. The Parma dukes are buried in this church.

The second most popular is the Duomo Square. It is the symbol of the city - the Cathedral. Outside, it was often rebuilt, but inside were preserved the paintings of Correggio, sculptures of the masters Antelami and Agrate, as well as a mosaic of early Christian times.





Many of Parma's sights are places of worship. Among them, a baptistery of an unusual octagonal shape made of pink marble. Not far from it is the abbey of St. John the Evangelist, including a monastery. Attention is also worthy of the Church of St. Vitaly, the Church of the Holy Cross, the Church of Our Lady near military apartments, the Carthusian monastery.

Palaces of the city

The sights of Parma include magnificent palaces owned by the local nobility. One of the oldest is the Bozelli Palace. He appeared in Parma in the 14th century. It once belonged to the Bergonza family, but was bought out by the Bozelli family, whose property remains to this day.

This also includes the Pilott Palace, which received the name from the name of the ball game common at court. Within its walls is the Farnese Theater, library, archaeological museum, the National Gallery with the works of Da Vinci, Tintoretto and Correggio.





The palace of Ducale, like Pilott, belonged to the Farnese family. The Duke of Ottavio built it in the second half of the 16th century. Part of it was badly damaged in World War II. Twenty hectares of the garden are decorated with fountains, mythological sculptures and antique vases.
