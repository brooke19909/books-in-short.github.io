Fisherman's Bastion in Budapest: description, history, photo. How to get to the Fisherman's Bastion?

The fisherman's bastion in Budapest is a great place for tourism. It is believed that here you can collect a solid catch, as well as look at the true works of art in architecture. Local fortifications are massive and beautiful.

Value

In fact, the Fisherman's Bastion is a place intended not so much for fishing as for receiving travelers and conducting excursions.

Many people are attracted here by beautiful galleries and tall towers. They have an aesthetic function and are not designed for defensive purposes. Despite the fact that the Fishing Bastion (Budapest, Hungary) is undeservedly called a good place for fishing, it is still loved and often visited by tourists. From here you can see the beautiful panorama of the Danube, as well as Pest. Also, this item has many other positive characteristics.





Creation Background

Hungarians very reverently honor the traditions of their native land. The monument to Istvan I - the saint and the first king, who became almost the first full-fledged ruler of the country, means a lot to them.

The local cathedral is also dedicated to government officials whose hands created the foundation for development. It was here that they twice married Matyash the Saint, and also crowned the heads of the country, the ruling already closer to our time. In addition, Franz Joseph I and his wife Elizabeth came here for ceremonies.

Once there was a temple, whose patron saint was the Virgin Mary, but over time it was destroyed by the Mongol-Tatars. After this, Istvan the Great laid down his church, which Bela IV restored after him, when the country was liberated in 1270. Under Matyash Korvin, the state strengthened, so his church became the main Catholic church in the city.





Occurrence

How did the Fisherman's Bastion (Budapest) come about? Its history in the 19th century began to unfold at the height of the Buda hill and is not as long as that of many other architectural structures in Europe. So this place is sometimes called a remake, like many of the local attractions such as Vaidakhunyad Castle.

The creation of this attraction was timed to coincide with the 1000th anniversary of the formation of Hungary, which took place in the century before last. However, the work was not completed by the right time, so the delivery time was shifted to 1905.

A few centuries before the opening, the Fisherman's Bastion had a completely different look. There was a square that was surrounded by a high fortress from different sides. The fisherman's bastion got its name due to the fact that the main product that was traded here was a catch from local waters. In exchange for permission to trade here, city guests had to defend the settlement in the event of a military threat that could approach the fortress. The fishing bastion was gradually losing its attractiveness, as the walls were dilapidated, trade was no longer so lively.

When reconstruction was carried out in the Royal Palace, they also decided to pay attention to this place. We used it for completely different purposes, but the familiar name has not gone out of use. Its name sounds rather formidable and powerful, but the Fisherman's Bastion was not used for military purposes. The photos show that this is a pretty picturesque building, the main purpose of which was to complement the Matthias Church within the framework of a single architectural composition. It has been dealing with it for more than a century.

Ruler Monument

Tourists will be very interested in the monument located here, erected in honor of the first king of Hungary - St. Stephen. The author of the sculpture is Strobl.

This is a magnificent statue of a rider on a horse, towering on a massive pedestal. Stepping closer, you can see the charming bas-reliefs, representing various events from the life path of the ruler. There is his coronation, the beginning of the construction of the temple and much more. This beautiful attraction was established in 1906.
