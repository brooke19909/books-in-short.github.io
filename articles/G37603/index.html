At 33, Pavel Adikaev is one of the most prominent Moscow businessmen - developers. Pavel had a long and fruitful work in the construction giant Mirax Group, for which, according to some reports, Pavel earned about a billion dollars and stood at the very beginning. After the onset of the mortgage crisis in 2008, as a result of disagreements with partners, Pavel decided to leave. And, according to the businessman, this decision was not simple and morally difficult, but there was no choice.

Three years later, Pavel Adikaev founded his own company Intensive Development Group (IDG), which successfully collaborates and represents the interests of the German design company OWPlan Group in Moscow. We met with Pavel in an office on Kutuzovsky to talk a bit about his plans for the future and get some comments on the current situation around the Mirax Group.

- Hello Paul. So the decision to leave MG in 2008 was the right one?

- Hello. I would not pose the question that way. When difficulties came in 2008, the issue of personal gain was far from the first place. Everyone, including Sergey Polonsky and Roman Trotsenko, understood that this was about preserving the company. Let's just say that disagreements arose at the stage of finding a solution to breaking the deadlock. It was necessary to pay off debts, to finish the construction of objects, and we were between two fires: banks and customers.

- What was the subject of controversy? Did you have different views on the situation?

- Yes, Polonsky, who was under the influence of financial director Lutsenko, decided to pay off first with the banks.
