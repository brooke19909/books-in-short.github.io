Which lake in the Chelyabinsk region is better to go to relax?

Chelyabinsk region is a water region. It has almost four thousand lakes, most of which are perfect for fishing and recreation. The largest are camp sites and holiday homes. But small ones are located in cozy beautiful places, they are distinguished by clean water and a rather large variety of fish species.

Meet Lake Turgoyak



Let's go on a journey through the water wealth of the Ural region. It should be noted that almost every lake in the Chelyabinsk region is in close proximity to another. Therefore, having a rest on one, in a couple of hours you can visit another, third, etc. Far beyond the borders of the region, Aydykul, Alabuzhsky, Tishsky reservoirs, Dolgobrodsky reservoir are famous . Vacationers highly appreciate Turgoyak - a lake in the Chelyabinsk region, located in a national park, pleasing to the eye with amazingly picturesque surroundings. Its total area is 26 and a half square meters. km, depth - 19 m. True, in some places the bottom falls quite low - a little less than 37 m. The value of this resource is obvious. The lake of the Chelyabinsk region is unique in the first place because of its transparency of water, it is in second place in Russia. Offshore, the water column is visible to a depth of more than 10 m, and closer to the middle - even up to 17 and a half. Water itself is close in quality to the water of Lake Baikal and is the purest natural source. Has this lake of the Chelyabinsk region and historical archaeological value. On one of its islands ( Vera island ) megaliths are located. Other traces of the sites of prehistoric people were found.

Waterfront Travel



The bulk of the lake in the Chelyabinsk region, reviews from connoisseurs, tourists and lovers of natural nature in general are positive. Good environmental conditions, clean, tasty water that can be drunk even without filtering, valuable varieties of fish freely available for fishing are noted. First of all, this is pike, whitefish, bream, carp and many others. An example is Lake Serebry, declared since 1985 as a regional natural monument. Even more well-known and popular is the Sweet Lake of the Chelyabinsk Region. Around him lies the Kocherdyk Nature Reserve, and the reservoir itself was included in the number of hydrological natural monuments. The water in it is not ordinary, but mineral, healing, at the first sip it seems that it has a sweetish flavor. Because there is no fish and bird nesting here, but there is healing mud. Legends say that, having bathed in Sweet or having smeared the body with mud, you can be cured of many serious diseases of the skin and skeletal system, musculoskeletal system. Water contains alkali in high concentration, therefore, things can be washed in them even without detergents.

Human intervention in the natural environment



Needless to say, that these unique corners of nature are severely affected by human intervention? The Chelyabinsk region, alas, is no exception. Enterprises dump their waste into the lakes. Tourists clog up pristine places with various rubbish. Predatory businessmen catch fish stocks, take out healing mud, and cut down forests on the coastal territories. As you can see, the nature of the Ural region is now vulnerable to the limit. Protect her people!
