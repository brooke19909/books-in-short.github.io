Ski resorts in Italy. Italy ski resorts on the map

Italy's ski resorts are known to winter sports enthusiasts no less than the Swiss Courchevel or the French San Moritz. Places such as Cervinia, Courmayeur, Bormio or Val di Fassa are well-known to all. All these resorts are located on the southern slopes of the Alps. Of course, there are excellent ski slopes and places in the Apennines, for example, in the mountains of Abruzzo (Prati di Tivo, Monte Piselli), but they work only in the winter season. But alpine resorts, due to their high altitude and the presence of glaciers, are accessible to lovers of skiing, snowboarding, tobogganing all year round. And everywhere the guests are awaited by the notorious Italian hospitality, sunny and snowy slopes, tracks of any degree of difficulty and excellent European service.



What place to choose for your vacation

Italy ski resorts on a country map are divided according to administrative provinces. Thus, we can talk about the ski areas of Lombardy, Piedmont, Aosta, Trentino and Alto Adige. Alps are in all of them, but this mountain system is too huge to be monotonous.

The slopes of Lombardy allow you to combine skiing, shopping in Milan and relaxing on the lakes. Dolomites surprise tourists with their colors, rocky spurs. And the peaks of Piedmont and Aosta are the most majestic, in addition, rest in those places allows you to ride in the Swiss resorts.

Ski Areas in the Aosta Valley

In the triangle that forms the state borders of France, Switzerland and Italy, is the province of Valle d'Aosta. The region is small in territory, but it is here that the oldest, most expensive and best ski resorts in Italy are located. All of them are located at a considerable height, at the foot of never-melting glaciers. Around - solid "four-thousandths" - Monte Rosa, Gran Paradiso and Mont Blanc. The latter is the highest peak not only in the Grey Alps, but also the entire mountain system as a whole, as well as the "roof" of Europe.

Here he liked to ski Mussolini, and with him the entire fascist elite. The first lifts were built for them, although the resorts of Courmayeur and Cervinia existed even at the dawn of the European enthusiasm for skiing. The tourist infrastructure of this place continues to develop rapidly - new routes are being laid, new roads are being built. One of them allows you to get directly from the center of the capital - founded by the ancient Romans of Aosta - to the newest fashionable village of Pyla in twenty minutes.

Skating in Piedmont

This is the land of nature reserves, clear lakes and hilly landscapes. Anyone who likes the French Alps will certainly appreciate Piedmont. The heart and unofficial ski capital of the region is the town of Sestriere. It is located at an altitude of more than two thousand meters, and therefore there are no problems with snow cover even in warm winters. Lifts stop working in April.

Most of the slopes here are for intermediate skiers. But there is in the Grande Galaxy region suitable entertainment for aces, as well as snowboarders and heli-ski fans. It offers cross-country skiing enthusiasts - about thirteen kilometers of beautiful tracks. Decent Apres-ski provide 33 restaurants, a nightclub, bowling alley, ice rink, a sports club with saunas and pools. No less popular are other ski resorts in Italy in Piedmont: Bardonecchia and Souz d'Ulsk, Claviere and Cesana.



Ski Areas in Lombardy

The proximity to the world capital of shopping, Milan and large lakes make this region very popular not only in winter. But if you have chosen Livigno as your vacation destination, you will not need to go to Milan to shop. Since this ski resort is also part of the duty-free zone, and all the shops here are duty-free. But freeride in Livigno is allowed only with a guide - the slopes here are avalanche-hazardous.

The most prestigious ski resort in Lombardy is Bormio. Italy hosts here the highest level international winter sports competitions. The town itself is very ancient and has been known since the first century BC due to nine healing thermal springs.





Dolomites

In the Trentino region, you can combine skiing and a board with visits to places of worship such as Lake Garda, Venice, Verona. Tourism infrastructure here is also very developed. Guests are offered dog sledding and sleigh rides, rock climbing, ice polo. Everyone will find Apres-ski to their taste: discos, saunas, gyms, restaurants and bars will create an atmosphere of an enduring holiday.

Italy's ski resorts in Trentino are also popular because of the fabulously beautiful nature. One has only to see how the glare of the setting sun plays on the slopes of the Dolomites - and you will strive here again and again. The most prestigious resort in this region is Val di Fassa. For beginners, Madonna di Campiglio is more suitable . An ideal family vacation awaits you in Cavalese, Val di Fiemme, Passo Tonale, Vigo di Fassa and other resorts of Trentino.

Alto Adige

This area is also located in the Dolomites. The unsurpassed beauty of these slopes and the riot of colors, the instant transition from the snowy peaks to the citrus groves of Limone on Lake Garda make this region very attractive. Before the Second World War, this area belonged to Austria, and this fact still affects the appearance of houses, hotels and punctuality in public transport.

What are the ski resorts in Italy in the upper part of the Adige River region? Reviews mention the Val Gardena ski area. It regularly hosts international freestyle and downhill competitions. The Val Gardena area consists of three resorts - Santa Cristina, Selva and Ortisei, interconnected by free buses and a single ski pass.
