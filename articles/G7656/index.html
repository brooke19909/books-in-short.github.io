Embroidery on a towel: patterns, ideas, step by step instructions

A beautiful and bright towel is in itself an original item in the house. But sometimes you want to express your individuality or give a useful gift to a close and dear person. In this case, it will be too common a present. But there is a way out - embroidery on a towel will make the thing more original and not so familiar.



Embroidered towels for baptism

Kryzhma (that's what baptismal towels are called) plays a special role in the sacrament of baptism. They possess the power of the amulet and carry it in themselves throughout the entire course of a person‚Äôs life.

Embroidery in the form of the name of the child, applied to the towel, enhances the effect of the amulet. Kryzhma will look original, complemented by the embroidered baptism date of the baby. When performing work, you must adhere to the following rules:

  • The name should not take up much space. Embroidery on the towel should be located in the corner or in the center. If it is with a hood, then the inscription can be placed on it.
  • When embroidering a baptismal towel, use gold or silver threads.

Cross-stitch on a towel: patterns and necessary materials

If you wish, you can always update your bathroom or kitchen. It is possible to fill your home with cosiness using embroidered towels made by yourself.

The easiest way to do this is to purchase a ready-made embroidery kit in the store for creativity. However, you can make a separate pattern on the canvas, and then sew it on the canvas. The result is a beautiful embroidery on a waffle towel or on a terry towel, it all depends on your imagination.



The work of embroidering thumbnails does not take too much time, and even a beginner needlewoman can cope with it. For work, it is necessary to prepare such materials and tools:

  • color scheme for cross stitch;
  • mouline thread (they must be of high quality with a stable color);
  • canvas
  • hoop and needle;
  • sewing threads in canvas color;
  • water-soluble marker for marking;
  • towel.

Work Instructions

Getting started, the first thing you need to do is prepare the materials (iron the canvas and make a markup of 10 by 10 cells with a marker). Next, the canvas is pulled on the hoop and begin work. To make the embroidery look neat, all stitches should go in the same direction. First, the row goes through ‚Äúhalf cross‚Äù stitches from left to right. Then you need to go back, completing the same "half-cross" in the other direction. If desired, embroidery can be made more voluminous, for this it is necessary to use thick threads or fold the floss several times.

When the work is finished, the canvas is removed from the hoop, washed in soapy water to remove the marking with a marker, and ironed through the fabric at a not too high temperature. Finally, the ends of the canvas are tucked on the wrong side and sewn to the towel manually with a blind seam.



Terry towel embroidery

Choosing a terry towel for such work, you need to understand that embroidering on it is quite difficult. The fabric itself is not very simple, it does not have a smooth surface, and transferring a sketch to it will be problematic.

Today, a sewing machine is most often used for such work. Although, if you try, make time and effort, then a beautiful embroidery on a towel with your own hands can also be done, while the result will look amazing.

Since a terry towel has a fleecy structure, it is best to embroider it with stitch. Embroidered elements in any case will differ in their texture, so it is best to highlight them with contrasting threads.

Getting to work, the first step is to choose a suitable pattern - it can be initials, an inscription or just a beautiful drawing. In any case, the dimensions of the selected pattern should correspond to the dimensions of the towel itself.

Since patterns are applied to terry fabrics, it is best to choose felt as the basis. Thanks to this, embroidery on a towel will become more voluminous and convex. When using felt, the pattern can be made more noticeable.

The sketch must be cut and sewn to the selected part of the towel with regular stitches. After that, embroidery is done with satin stitch (manually or on a typewriter). In fact, the embroidery technique does not matter - it can be straight or oblique stitches. Care must be taken to ensure that the edges of the sketch are not visible from the outside. Embroidery on a towel will look more voluminous if it is stitched with flooring.



Having tried and made a little effort, you can make an original towel with embroidery, which will become one of your favorite things or a wonderful gift.
