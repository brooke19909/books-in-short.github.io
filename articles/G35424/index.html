1 GB of Internet: is it a lot or a little

A question that does not have a clear answer. For some, this is very small, but for others it is more than enough. It all depends on many factors that should be considered when buying Internet traffic from your operator. To begin with, we‚Äôll clarify this: 1 GB = 1024 MB, and 1 MB = 1024 KB. What is how much

In order to find out what you monthly drain your traffic on, you first need to understand what gigabytes are and how much it is, using examples:

Normal page - depends on how this page is overloaded with photos. If a regular text page weighs about 60-70 Kb, then viewing a social network feed with a bunch of photos and gifs can take 5-10 Mb. By the way, such social networks tend to update themselves, therefore, this will additionally eat traffic.

Listen to or download music - again depends on the file format and duration. Approximately 3 to 5 Mb of traffic will take you.

Watching a movie - depending on the quality, format, duration and compression, the movie size can vary from 8 to 15 GB. If you decide to download a DVD movie, you will have to donate another 1.5 GB of traffic.

Online TV, streaming video and Skype - thanks to the maximum compression, it can take you about 700 MB. In the case of Skype, it all depends on the resolution of the camera.

Everything else - with regard to small chats, acq, Skype correspondence, mail checking, a lot of Internet is not required (provided that you do not send huge files).

How much traffic is needed for a netbook and laptop

How to save traffic

As we noted earlier, no matter what device you use, they all consume the Internet in one way or another without your information. Sometimes even more than yourself. For example, on a Windows update, your computer or laptop can consume up to half of the precious traffic. Therefore, we advise you to disable all possible updates both on a laptop and on a smartphone. Perhaps you can leave only antiviruses.

But not only updates can eat up your traffic, this also applies to applications running in the background, such as Skype, Votsap, mail, weather. If you do not really need them, you can turn off these applications from time to time, which can save megabytes.

What determines the download speed

Each time you try to view a page of a site, when you download this page, files are downloaded from the hosting. In other words, the page loading speed is the speed of downloading it from the hosting to the user's browser.

A number of factors affect your speed, such as:

  1. Data volume.

  2. The speed of the Internet provided by the operator.

  3. Hosting congestion.

Thus:

  • A normal page on a 2G network will load about 50 seconds, 3G - 1 second, and at a speed of 4G - instantly.

  • A song about 5 MB: with a speed of 2G - 8 minutes, 3G - 11 seconds, 4G - 4 seconds, 4G + - instantly.

  • A short video: 2G - 42 minutes, 3G - 1 minute, 4G - 13 seconds, 4G + - 7 seconds.

  • Movie (750 Mb): 2G - 21 hours, 3G - 30 minutes, 4G - 7 minutes, 4G + - 3 minutes.

  • Movie in HD (1.5 GB): 2G - 42 hours, 3G - 1 hour, 4G - 14 minutes, 4G + -6 minutes.
