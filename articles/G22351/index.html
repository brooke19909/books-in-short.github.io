Angra (Brazil): tours, reviews, photos

Brazil is a relatively new tourist destination for travelers from Russia. But the magical name Angra dos Reis has been persistently passed on from mouth to mouth. Translated from Portuguese, this means "bay of kings." What kind of kings arrived on this coast and gave a name to the city? As indicated in the Gospels, these were kings from the East. In Catholic countries they are considered royal monks, in our country they are magi. Angra (Brazil) conquers any person with its beauty. And every traveler arriving on this paradise coast brings him the gift of the love of his heart, as the magi once presented the baby Jesus with gold, myrrh, and incense. In this article we will consider the history of the resort and its attractions. We advise when it is best to come there and which tours to Angra dos Reis choose. Angra dos Reis

This is a small city located on the Atlantic coast. There are three hundred and sixty-five islands in the bay. Close to the sea approach the mountains of Serra do Mar, giving the scenery even more splendor. The coast within the city is not in vain called Green (Costa Verde). The narrow strip between wonderful beaches and mountains is filled with the lush greenery of tropical forests. This place has long been favored by wealthy Latin American tourists. The local beaches (and there are about two thousand) are considered the best in the entire state of Rio de Janeiro. They are made attractive not only by white soft sand, but also by a quiet, gentle surf (which is rare for the open ocean) and a forest that is approaching.

The city of Angra (Brazil) itself is small, with about one hundred and fifty thousand inhabitants. According to reviews, many ancient churches and beautiful colonial-style mansions have been preserved in it.

History of the city

When the Portuguese discovered the coast of Latin America in 1500, they were captivated by the beauty of these places. The kingdom immediately equipped a second expedition. It was led by Gaspard de Lemos. The task of the expedition was to draw up a detailed map of the coast, which would later be called Brazil. Angra became the first landing site for the team of Gaspard de Lemos. And since it happened on January 6, when the Catholic world celebrated the feast of the Three Kings (Magi), then this place was also called Angra dos Reis. It is clear that there was no mention of any city here. Nevertheless, its inhabitants themselves consider the foundation of the settlement on January 6, 1502.

For a long time the coast remained deserted. The pirate ships, taking advantage of the numerous islands and secluded coves, stopped here to replenish fresh water and food. Only half a century later, in 1556, a small settlement of the Portuguese appeared. Now it is the Old Town (Villa Velha) near the island of Zhiboya. Gradually, the area developed. Soon, the city became the center of trade in gold, coffee and sugarcane, which was exported by Brazil. Angra fell into decay at the end of the 19th century, when a road was built that connected Rio directly with S√£o Paulo, making the Costa Verde coastline on the periphery. But in the twentieth century, with the laying of the Rio-Santos autobahn and the popularization of sea tourism, the city revived. Now this resort occupies not only the coast, but also the largest island of the bay - Ilya-Grandi.
