Nuclear mushroom - an ominous symbol of the explosion

This term is called a cloud of dust and smoke that occurs after a nuclear explosion. It would be better, of course, to never know what a nuclear mushroom is. This radioactive cloud is named in this way because of the scientists noted external resemblance to the fruiting bodies of ordinary mushrooms, which can be found and collected in the forest. But mushrooms in the folk art of various countries are symbols of fertility and vitality. A nuclear mushroom, on the contrary, is a symbol of destruction and war.



However, the mushroom cloud is not only the distinguishing feature of nuclear and thermonuclear explosions that have occurred on earth. It is also formed during other non-nuclear explosions of sufficient power, as well as during eruptions of large volcanoes, during severe fires or when meteorites fall on the soil. Its height directly depends on the power of the explosion or impact that has occurred or produced, and on the quality of filling: substances used in the process.

Specifications

How is it formed and what is characterized by this phenomenon? A nuclear mushroom is formed when a dust cloud is raised from the surface of the earth. Nuclear explosion mushroom

What happens after the rise of dust and smoke from the surface of the earth? The nuclear mushroom is already a cumulus rain cloud that has developed strongly in height. It, of course, has a mushroom shape (hat and leg). It is known that with a powerful explosion (up to a megaton) it can be up to 20 kilometers in height! From this cloud, if the explosion was of sufficient power, it usually rains that can extinguish the fires resulting from the explosion.

Radioactive cloud

It poses the greatest danger immediately after the explosion, nuclear and thermonuclear, carried out on the ground. Particles of radioactive dust containing harmful substances act as condensates. And water vapor settles on them, concentrating around in drops. The cloud rises and cools. Inside, droplets of water form, which fall on the soil down as radioactive rains (snow, hail are possible). Such precipitation, fallen from a radioactive cloud-fungus, can cause significant harm to the national economy and pose a threat to all living things.

When is formed

A nuclear mushroom, as already mentioned, does not occur with all types of nuclear or thermonuclear explosions. If they were carried out, for example, in outer space, deep underground or under water, as well as in the earth's atmosphere, then neither a mushroom nor a cloud will form.



Ominous symbol

In modern literature and art, the nuclear mushroom is identified with the ominous symbol of war, and its image is included in some paintings of world painting as the embodiment of evil and threat to all living on planet Earth. In fantastic literary works and films describing the future of the Earth after nuclear wars, this symbol is used by the authors quite often, and always in a negative and sinister way. Indeed, nuclear evil has no future, but only ruins and the past, which people who survived as a result of disasters recall.
