How to choose a dishwasher

If more recently, dishwashing equipment for many of us was considered a very expensive acquisition, now it is in almost every home. A dishwasher, the choice of which is no less acute than when buying a washing machine, deserves great attention. If dirty things can also be taken to the laundry room, then cutlery will never be washed anywhere for you. Almost always, we choose a dishwasher, focusing on the volume and design of the equipment, sometimes completely forgetting about the technical characteristics. In order not to make a mistake in choosing and buying a quality product, you should know how one model differs from another.

The first thing you should know is that the dishwasher you have to choose can be freestanding and built-in. In turn, freestanding models can be either small desktop or large floor. The whole difference lies mainly in appearance. Desktop dishwashers are ideal for a small family of 2-3 people. Machines of this type can accommodate up to 10-15 cutlery, the entire washing cycle lasts no more than 30 minutes, and the water flow reaches 10-14 liters.

However, for a family of 4-5 people or more, the use of desktop dishwashing machines is not rational. It is much more efficient to choose a large-volume dishwasher, which will more economically use detergents, water and electricity. Such equipment accommodates up to 15 sets of dishes and it is enough to include it once a day.

Like washing machines, dishwashers also have different classes of energy consumption, washing and drying.
