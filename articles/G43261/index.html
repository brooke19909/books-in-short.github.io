Lido di Ostia, Italy: beaches, attractions, hotels, reviews

A typical Italian resort town, located on the Tyrrhenian Sea, twenty kilometers from the capital, Lido di Ostia attracts everyone who wants to combine a beach holiday with sightseeing, including those located in Rome.



The name of the city translates as ‚ÄúOstia's beach‚Äù, and it is really famous for its magnificent sandy beaches, so there are always a lot of vacationers. The city was founded in 1884 by its first inhabitants (peasants who came from Ravenna) after draining the swamps. In 1924, the Lido di Ostia-Rome railway appeared, and residents of the capital of the country began to come to the sea en masse. The real tourist boom began in the town after 1945. And today, many tourists visiting Rome prefer to stay in its picturesque suburb - Lido di Ostia.

Climate

As in many regions of Italy, the climate in this town is mild: in July, the average temperature is about +26 ¬∞ C, in January - +7 ¬∞ C. You can relax in this resort both in summer and in winter. Most hotels receive guests all year round.



Beaches

There are not too many attractions in Lido di Ostia. The city‚Äôs beaches more than compensate for this shortcoming. It is very comfortable here at any time of the year. The hottest month is July, when the air warms up to +30 ¬∞ C, the coldest time is January. (+8 ¬∞ ).

The swimming season begins in mid-May and ends in October. The sea in Lido di Ostia is transparent and clean. Water warms up to + 26-27 ¬∞ C in August or September. The best time to visit this resort is autumn or spring: the water is warm as fresh milk, the air is not hot from the heat. Bars, restaurants and hotels work as usual, and vacationers are much less.



In summer, there are always a lot of vacationers, because, in addition to foreign tourists, the inhabitants of Rome like to relax in this place . Many townspeople come here in the winter to take a walk along the promenade and breathe in the sea air.

In the town you can choose a beach from three categories: free, with free admission (you need to pay for sunbeds and umbrellas) and beaches with a paid entrance. There are several free beaches. They are not as clean as paid ones, and there are always a lot of tourists here, which does not contribute to a comfortable stay, therefore, as a rule, guests of the city prefer paid beaches, where excellent service and a lot of space.

Gambrinus

This is probably the most popular beach in the city, famous for its magnificent views that open directly from the coast. You can enter it for free, but you will have to pay a fee for sun loungers, umbrellas and changing rooms. The beach is sandy, the shore is clean and well maintained, the entrance to the water is smooth, the sea near the shore is shallow, which allows families with children to relax here.

On the territory you can visit several small restaurants, which will offer you dishes of national cuisine of their seafood.

Spiaggetta

Amazing paid beach, surrounded by pine trees, emitting a magnificent aroma. Here you will be offered to rent a sunbed and an umbrella, and those who prefer their own bedspread will not be accommodated in the best area - at the bamboo fence, on the edge. On the territory there is a free toilet, shower, cafe.



The city's attractions. Promenade

Both locals and visitors alike enjoy walking on the small promenade. Walking here is a great pleasure. Magnificent landscapes, a light refreshing breeze, the calm expanse of the sea - all this causes a feeling of peace.

Pasolini Memorial

To the north of the embankment is the port, and next to it is a memorial dedicated to the director and poet Pierre Pazolini. The monument was erected in a vacant lot, where this talented man was brutally killed under still unclear circumstances.



Temple of Mary Queen of Peace

The Catholic Church with a preserved ancient altar and a statue of Madonna. Today above it you can see an interesting painting in the Art Nouveau style. It is an active temple.

What to see in the vicinity?

Sightseeing in the city will not take you much time, so we recommend that you go to the suburbs of Lido di Ostia. Guest reviews indicate that in the vicinity of the town there is also something to see.

Very close to the town is the Capocotta beach conservation area with fairly modest vegetation and sand dunes. Vacationers here are usually few. On the northern outskirts of Ostia, you can visit the relict forest of Castelfusano.



History buffs will certainly be interested in the ancient city of Ostia Antica (4th century BC), where archaeological excavations are being carried out today. This unique object is an archaeological reserve. The area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe ruins is about thirty-four hectares. Many fragments are very well preserved. Decumanus Maximus Street leads to the huge round theater. Around it are taverns and residential buildings, baths and ancient shops, public buildings.

Next to Lido di Ostia is another resort town of Fiumicino. In 1961, it opened the international airport. Leonardo da Vinci.

Lido di Ostia: hotels

Judging by the reviews of vacationers who visited the city on their own or as part of a tourist group, there are no problems with accommodation in Ostia. There are quite a lot of hotels, hotels and guest houses. Below we will provide you with an overview of only a few of them.

Barcelo aran blu

The hotel is located in the center of Ostia. It offers modernly decorated rooms with panoramic views of the coast. They are equipped with air conditioning, LCD TVs, a bar. The bathroom is equipped with all necessary toiletries.



Aran Blu is located eleven kilometers from the Ostia Antica Archaeological Site. Fiumicino Airport is less than ten minutes by car. Three kilometers from the hotel is the train station and the popular railway Lido di Ostia - Rome. How to get to this resort town from the capital, we will tell a little later.

Sirenetta

The hotel is located by the beach, a five-minute walk from the railway station. It offers - spacious and comfortable rooms, decorated in a classic style. Some have a balcony, others have a terrace. Bathrooms are equipped with necessary toiletries and cosmetics.



Hotel Sirenetta in Lido di Ostia serves a buffet breakfast each morning. Usually it consists of hot drinks, bread, jam, Italian pastries. And you can have dinner in the restaurant located in the hotel building.

The journey to the archaeological site of the ancient Roman city of Ostia Antica takes about fifteen minutes, and if you want to admire the beauties of Rome, then forty minutes the train will take you to the center of the Italian capital.

Bellavista

This luxury hotel is located on Ostia's seafront. Here you will be offered to stay in one of the spacious rooms with air conditioning, a bathroom, free Wi-Fi. Each room has satellite TV.

Guests can relax on the spacious terrace, which is located on the roof of the hotel, which offers amazing views of the sea. Here you can enjoy drinks as well as shiatsu massage. The hotel provides buffet meals.

Everyone can rent bicycles for free at the hotel and go to the pine forest of Castelfusano or visit the ancient Roman ruins in Ancient Ostia, which is 3 km from the hotel. The hotel provides a shuttle service to Rome Fiumicino Airport upon request. The train station is a five-minute walk away.

La riva

Another hotel located on the promenade of Lido di Ostia. It often stops the inhabitants of Rome, escaping for several days to the sea from the metropolis. They can leave their vehicles in free parking. The rooms are bright and very comfortable, decorated in a contemporary style. Since La Riva is a family-run hotel, a cot can be added to your room (on request).



All rooms have air conditioning, minibar, wireless Internet access, television.

Ping pong

The hotel is in the very center of the city. To the train station, from which trains go to Rome, you need to walk for fifteen minutes. A train ride to Ostia Antica will not take you more than twenty minutes.

All rooms are decorated in a simple classical style with comfortable and high-quality furniture, air conditioning and TVs. If desired, guests can climb onto the rooftop terrace and, sitting on a sun lounger, enjoy magnificent sea views from here.



The partner restaurant, which is located next to the hotel, offers discounts to guests. Breakfast is served in the hotel's dining room with coffee and aromatic freshly baked pastries.

How to get there

From Rome, this wonderful resort town can be reached by metro. When you reach the ground station Piramide (line B), do not get off the subway, and follow the Lido sign to the Porta S. Paolo train station, from which the Roma-Lido electric trains leave every fifteen minutes. The journey will take about twenty minutes.

Lido di Ostia: reviews of independent tourists

We decided to turn to the reviews of this particular category of vacationers, because the vast majority of them are here. Many tourists who visit Rome come to the resort of Lido di Ostia. Having examined the sights of the Eternal City, they independently go to the sea, and since it is the resort closest to the capital, then, of course, the choice stops at it.

Most vacationers say that Lido di Ostia in Italy is fascinating at first sight: compact, homely and very green. It is gratifying that independent tourists do not have any problems with accommodation. There are many hotels here, so the choice depends only on your preferences and financial capabilities. Regardless of the category of hotel, service is always on top. The staff is friendly and welcoming. The rooms are always clean and comfortable.

You can also choose the beaches at your discretion: free, but crowded or paid - more well-groomed and comfortable. The town leaves a very pleasant impression, and many tourists plan to come here again and again.
