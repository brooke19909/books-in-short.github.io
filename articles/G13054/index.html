A bit about chess

Chess is the oldest game that appeared in India. She has a long history. Chess, in its essence, is a logical and sporting game. But she also develops willpower, concentration, and creative thinking.

The game has a 64-cell board and 32 pieces. Cells of black and white alternate with each other, 16 figures of black and 16 figures of white colors. The essence of the game of chess is to checkmate the figure of the enemy king. To do this, you need to think through each move, so for the game you need to think logically and know the rules well.

One game can drag on for several hours or even days, which contributes to the formation of willpower and character. Many people have learned this. Someone has become a famous player throughout the country or the world, while someone has remained an amateur. One of the great players and the best coaches in the world was Mark Izrailevich Dvoretsky, who became famous for his victories in chess games in Georgia, Russia and the USSR.





Mark Dvoretsky: biography

Butler was born in Moscow on December 9, 1947. He received a mathematical education at Moscow State University, but continued his life's path in the chess direction. A few years after graduation, he became a professional chess player.





After victories at the world level, Butler Mark Izrailevich began to write books teaching chess mastery, at the same time he began to train chess players who later became masters of sports in chess and great grandmasters not only in the USSR and Russia, but also abroad. These people include: Sergey Dolmatov, Arthur Yusupov, Nana Alexandria, Evgeny Bareev.





Mark Dvoretsky has written many very useful quality theoretical and practical books. According to them, you can learn independently and achieve good results. Now these books are very popular, they are studied by everyone: from small to great. More than one generation of chess players has grown up on these books, among whom there are champions.

An excellent analytical mindset was endowed with Mark Dvoretsky. The Endgame Textbook, which he wrote, reveals very well the talent for mastering chess techniques, as well as the coaching skills of Mark Dvoretsky. He could quickly find the right solution in any situation, whether it was a life or chess problem.

Achievements of Mark Butler

1973 was a significant year for Mark Dvoretsky in that he became the champion of Moscow in chess. In the USSR Championship in 1974, Mark Dvoretsky took 5th and 7th places. In the same year, in foreign tournaments Polyanice-Zdroj and Wijk aan Zee, he also became a champion.

The butler had just a little before the title of grandmaster, but he decided to teach and train other people, to give them his experience in playing chess. Soon, the skill to transfer experience exceeded his practical skills, and in 1979, 1981 and 1987 he became an honored chess coach of the RSFSR, Georgia and the USSR, and also became an honored FIDE trainer.

Today, Mark Izrailevich is the most authoritative, successful and professional trainer in the world. His theoretical and practical methods are still being studied by his large generation of students.





During his life, the honored master taught chess mastery to Nan Alexandria, Arthur Yusupov, Alexei Dreyev, Alexander Motylev, Sergey Dolmatov, Ivan Popov, Viorel Bologan, Vadim Zvyagintsev, Ernesto Inarkiev, Vladimir Potkin and other chess players. Thanks to this training, they in youth ranks became world, country, and European champions, and also claimed the highest chess title. The butler had the experience of teaching not only to an individual student, but also to an entire national team at once.
