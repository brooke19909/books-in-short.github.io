Restaurants in Bas-Ter, the capital of Guadeloupe

Guadeloupe is a terrific country. It is located on the Lesser Antilles in the Caribbean. Among the islands of the archipelago, the largest is Guadeloupe (a little over 1,400 km 2 ).



From a bird's eye view, this island looks like a butterfly, spreading its wings in the waters of the Caribbean Sea. Butterfly wings have a name: Grand Ter and Bass Ter. This part of the island with numerous rivers is the most beautiful. It is on Bas-Ter that is the active volcano Soufier, at the base of which stands the capital of Guadeloupe Bas-Ter. The city was built in the French style of the colonial period. The main attractions are natural.

Basse Terre (Guadeloupe): National Park

One of the main ones in the capital is the National Park, which many rare birds consider to be their home. In the forest part of the park lives the symbol of the country - raccoon. The park territory is huge - just over 17 hectares. UNESCO declared Bas-Ter National Park a biosphere reserve. The mountain rain forest, which covers the slopes of the mountain ranges, is subject to special protection. The scientific leaders of the park have collected an extensive collection of unique representatives of the flora: rare and endangered species of orchids, hevea, mahogany, tree ferns, etc. Many rivers and streams flow through the park. Many of them flow down from the slope of Soufriere (because of which the water has a complex chemical composition). On its way, rivers, streams and streams form many cascades, waterfalls and lakes. The most beautiful cascade, tourists consider Ekrevis, and the waterfall - Carib.

Pride Bas-Tera - Company-Fermier-de-Gros-Montagne factory

In addition to stunning nature, Guadeloupe especially surprises tourists. Its capital is the place where the Company-Fermier de Gross-Montagn sugar factory operates. It is considered a work of art: giant sugar machines are painted in very bright colors, which reminds the center of Pompidou in the capital of France.

Tourists must visit factories for the production of rum for informational purposes (where, by the way, they offer various types for testing), exhibition venues and beaches.

Fort

The capital of Guadeloupe is proud of the amazing old fort Saint Charles. It was built on a hill in the southern part of the city in 1640 and named after the founder of the city, Charles Wael. The fortress is well preserved to this day. It has a museum of the history of Bas-Ter and the islands. Near the walls of the fortress are the graves of two illustrious military leaders: Admiral Gurbeyr and General Rishpansa. The fortress has a middle name Fort Delgre. Today Fort St. Charles is the most powerful defensive structure on the islands.
