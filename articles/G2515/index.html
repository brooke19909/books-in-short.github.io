Aventurine - a stone of the sun



Aventurine is a stone, referred to in some sources as heliolite, aventurine labrador, sun stone or aventurine (feldspar) spar. It differs from other minerals with a flickering ebb, sparkling in the light of gold, red or green. Real natural stone is quite rare in nature, because on sale you can often find glass pieces that imitate a real natural stone. In the manufacture of fakes, copper, chromium, iron, cobalt oxides are added to the glass mass , this gives the glass an excess of sparkles and is a sign of fake.

Aventurine is a natural stone, and a glass copy appeared in Ancient Egypt, but the secret of manufacture was kept secret. Some sources claim that aventurine glass was accidentally discovered by Venetian glassblowers, and this is the sixteenth century.

As for the natural color of natural aventurine, it can be white, honey, light gray, pink, terracotta, green and even cherry. Blue aventurine comes from Austria, Spain is the birthplace of red-brown, and India is green aventurine. In general, in addition to these three countries, Brazil, China, and, of course, Russia (the Urals) are also famous for the best adventurers. A couple of centuries ago, the most beautiful examples of Russian adventurers adorned vases and candlesticks, glasses and handles of cutlery, not to mention the amazing female jewelry made. Aventurine - a green stone looks very similar to jade. The complexity of the difference leads to the fact that in stores often the first stone is issued for the second. The centuries-old mining of aventurine in China has led to the fact that the mineral was called the imperial stone. It was considered sacred, even the imperial seal was made of it. In nature, this mineral is not always flawless in appearance: it can be iridescent, shimmering, simply luxurious, but it can also be nondescript.

Aventurine is a stone whose properties gave it the name of the sun, making it an ideal material for making jewelry. Especially love him in Asian countries. The hue of this gem depends on the natural impurities contained in aventurine. Hematite particles, for example, give a reddish brown tint, while a greenish tint appears due to the fuchsite content.
