Gaiters - what is it? History, characteristics and manufacturing methods

When it comes to ordinary wardrobe items, such as a skirt, dress or trousers, there are no additional questions with the requirements of clarification of their purpose. Things are completely different when they ask: "Gaiters - what is it?" Let's find out how they look and what they put on.

Gaiters - what is it? History of their appearance

It turns out that this wardrobe item appeared a very long time ago and was completely different in appearance from the modern one. Gaiters were mainly used to protect legs and shoes. They were sewn in the form of a short cover with a clasp. Moreover, their height sometimes did not significantly reach the knee.

So, in ancient Rome they were always worn by gladiators in order to protect the ankle area from possible damage. Later gaiters became a mandatory attribute of warriors. And indeed, for a long time they were considered a purely masculine wardrobe item. They were used mainly as a cover for legs during horse riding.

Over time, gaiters become popular among the masses, becoming one of the fashionable things. A special chic in the late XIX and early XX centuries was to have white gaiters. At this time, they find recognition among women, so they become completely different in style, color, material used and other characteristics. So gradually gaiters from an object to protect shoes from pollution turned into a useful insulating little thing for the feet.

Take measurements: leg circumference in the area under the knee, ankles and length of the product. You can start knitting both from below and from above.
