A little about the history of St. Petersburg museums

Officially, the first such institution was founded in 1709. The honorary right to head the list of museums in St. Petersburg went to the Naval Museum, created under the Admiralty. Models of ships with drawings were presented to visitors. According to historical data, in 1703, by decree of Peter the Great, the foundation was laid for the Military History Museum of Artillery, Engineering and Signal Corps. But the Kunstkamera was the first object that ordinary people were allowed to visit. In 1764, Catherine II acquired the first collections of paintings that formed the basis of the future Hermitage. During the reign of Nicholas I, the building was built where the New Hermitage is located. In 1985, by order of Nicholas II, the Russian Museum was founded, where collections of paintings by Russian artists were presented. If you count all the museums of St. Petersburg today , the list will include more than 200 items, while it is constantly updated.

The Hermitage - the pearl of Russian culture

The city on the Neva is famous for a large number of museums, which are a reflection of the culture and history of this beautiful city. They keep his secrets and wealth, revealing their secrets only to true connoisseurs of art. Among them, one can distinguish the main museums of St. Petersburg, the list of which is opened by the Hermitage. It is the largest center of art and spirituality in our country.





Going on an excursion, you can follow the whole path of development of rich Russian culture and see with your own eyes the delightful works of Russian and foreign masters. Undoubtedly, the main possession of the Hermitage is the beautiful Winter Palace. The first exhibits for the institution were collected by Catherine II herself. Today, the expositions present collections of primitive art and archaeological cultures of the territory of the former Union, collections of ancient antiquities, works of art from Western Europe, the East, Russia, as well as coins and gold items. You can visit the Hermitage at the address: Palace Square, 2.

Museum on the water

We consider other interesting museums in St. Petersburg. The list continues with a unique ship museum, which is a symbol of the city.





On the cruiser "Aurora" you can go on an exciting journey through the waters of the Neva, as well as see many beautiful exhibits that tell the story of this amazing ship. Alexander Nevsky Lavra

The list of museums in St. Petersburg is impossible to imagine without the Alexander Nevsky Lavra . It is the largest sculpture museum in Europe, located directly under the blue sky.





On the territory of the institution there is a delightful Trinity Cathedral, a monastery and an old cemetery, where famous personalities are buried (for example, the wife of A.S. Pushkin and the Decembrists). Here you can also see the graves of cultural figures of our country, including M.I. Glinka, A.P. Borodin, B.M. Kustodiev, P.I. Tchaikovsky, M.P. Musorgsky and many others. In addition, there is a large collection of paintings, sculptures and wonderful architectural monuments that captivate with their beauty and charm. Address: Embankment of the Monastyrka River, 1.

When compiling a list of museums in St. Petersburg with addresses that you would like to visit, do not forget to include the Peter and Paul Fortress in it.





This is a complex of buildings, among which the Peter and Paul Cathedral, which is the tomb of the imperial family in past centuries, is noticeably distinguished. The Cosmonautics Museum is located here, where you can learn the history of space exploration, see a huge collection of monetary units of different times at the Mint, and also visit the Naryshkin Bastion, from where a cannon shot is fired daily at 12. Everyone knows that the most popular among tourists are the art museums of St. Petersburg. A list of the names of these magnificent objects cannot be imagined without the Russian Museum. It was founded in 1895 by decree of Nicholas II, was located in the Mikhailovsky Palace, acquired specifically for him.





By the time of the opening, the collection of the Russian State included about 450 paintings, more than 100 sculptural compositions, about 1000 drawings, as well as more than 5000 thousand objects telling about the history of Ancient Russia. Today, collections of funds include more than 400 thousand exhibits (X-XXI centuries), while expositions are constantly replenished. The museum is famous for the works of Andrei Rublev, Karl Bryullov, Ivan Shishkin, Dmitry Levitsky, Kazimir Malevich. To trace the history of portraits in the works of artists of different centuries, the exposition "Faces of Russia" allows. Detailed information can be found at: Engineering street, 4.
