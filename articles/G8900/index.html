The best shopping centers in Helsinki

It's no secret that Helsinki for residents of the central part of Russia is one of the most popular cities to visit. Almost every Petersburger with a Schengen visa has been to this city or to Finland itself.

The capital is rich in its architecture and atmosphere. There are many attractions here, they amaze with their external diversity.

The city will not leave anyone indifferent. Every year, millions of tourists come here to enjoy the historical masterpieces of the capital. At first glance it may seem that you can explore all the main monuments in one trip, but this is a profound mistake. This place is ideal for tourist excursions. In addition to the famous museums, there are fairs, various exhibitions and more.

In addition, if you are tired of wandering around the city, you can look into many shopping centers of the city. Believe me, there are plenty of them here.

Kamppi Shopping Center in Helsinki

This shopping center is perhaps one of the most famous places in the city. Perhaps due to the fact that it is located on the territory of the bus station. This place can be said to be a city attraction. Here you can go to the shops you are interested in, play bowling or enjoy a cup of delicious invigorating coffee. In addition, in the shopping center there are several grocery stores and a whole food court.

One of the main advantages of this shopping center in Helsinki is the presence of a subway inside the building.

At the beginning of construction, it was clear that this place would become significant for the city. Construction began fifteen years ago, that is, in 2002, and ended by 2006. Finnish projects reacted very sensitively to this and for a long time decided how to ennoble the territory. As a result, the Kamppi area began to include a bus station, from where you can go to other cities in Finland, as well as countries, a huge shopping center, residential areas and a business center.

Address: Suomen tasavalta, Helsinki. The nearest metro station is Kamppi.

Tax-free

Perhaps this is one of the most enjoyable parts of shopping. For residents of other countries there is a special system. You can take a tax free or invoice.

Tax-free is refunded in the event of a purchase in the amount of from forty euros. The percentage of return depends on the amount of the purchase. This can be checked on the official website of Tax Free Finland.

As for Invoice, the refunded amount will be higher, but then you will have to return to the store itself with the presented check from the border.
