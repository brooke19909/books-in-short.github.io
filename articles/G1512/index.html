Ancient side

Where to start your personal excursion program for the guests of the hotel complex under consideration? Perhaps it‚Äôs worth starting with ... a walk. Why? It's elementary, you are in Side. The Side Yesiloz Hotel 3 * hotel itself is located to the right of the chic archaeological site, one of the most spectacular in Turkey.



Ancient Side was a fortified city, protected from both the sea and land. Moreover, despite the earthquake, the walls protecting it from the land side have been preserved well. The central gate erected by the ancient Greeks leads to the main square, which has a horseshoe-shaped outline. The second gate, also of Hellenistic origin, the Roman, having tetrahedral towers, are better preserved. From them leads the ancient central street of Side. Tourists can also see the agora (public area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe city) here. Strangely enough, the guests of Side Yesiloz Hotel 3 * (Side), most of all, write about ancient public toilets (there are 24 of them in Side). Although they are, for obvious reasons, now not used for their intended purpose, they are potentially functional today. Under them (after all, ancient technologies in some aspects are more reliable than ours!) The existing sewage system and water supply with clean running water were saved. There is something to learn for our plumbers, the system has been working "only" for a thousand and a half years!

Unfortunately, the ruined center of the agora is of interest, where you can see the ruins of the temple of Ceres (the goddess of fertility and agriculture), as well as the slave market.

However, the most spectacular surviving ancient structure is the amphitheater in Side. On a scene with a length of 63 m, which can be seen even today, in hoary antiquity, gladiators fought among themselves and with wild animals.

Pamukkale

Visiting Turkey and not swimming in the Cleopatra basin located in Pamukkale is the same as visiting Paris and managing not to see the Eiffel Tower. This is understood by the health resort Side Yesiloz Hotel 3 *. A photo on the background of the Cotton Castle (as Pamukkale is translated) adorns many photo albums of tourists. This unique natural cascade of travertine baths was created by nature itself. Mineral thermal water has healing properties. Externally, the walls of the baths are covered with a layer of crystallized salt deposits. Therefore, the exterior of Pamukkale is original: a delicate pastel-pearl color. An unforgettable sight!

The largest bath, heated by a separate geyser, served as a pool to the ancient Romans. They equipped this place for their baths, providing columns and a portico (unfortunately, these creations of human hands were destroyed by an earthquake).
