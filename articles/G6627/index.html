Appearance and Ergonomics

The device is quite compact, has a thin body. On the front panel in the center is a large lens of the optical module of the device, to the left and to the right of it are a microphone and a speaker. At the upper end there are rails designed to connect to the bracket located on the windshield.

On the right side of the device are an HDMI connector and a microSD card slot. A microUSB port and a jack for outputting analog video to an external screen are placed on the left end. The back panel of the Avtovision Delta New Plus is almost all occupied by a large LCD display. To the right and left of it are buttons to control the operation of the device.

There is a microUSB port on the mount bracket, into which you can connect a complete GPS antenna. You can also quickly turn the device in any direction. This feature is convenient when you need to quickly direct the lens of the device to the side window of the car to fix the process of communication with another road user or traffic police officer.

The ergonomics of the device are well thought out. It will not be superfluous to mention the pleasant design of the gadget. The disadvantages include the excessive shaking of the device when driving on rough roads due to the specific design of the bracket.

How does it take off?

Let's look at how Avtovision Delta Plus copes with its main task - shooting traffic conditions.

In the daytime, in sunny weather, there are no complaints about the quality of the picture output by the DVR. The sharpness is excellent, registration plates of passing and oncoming vehicles are perfectly readable even at a fairly large distance.

In cloudy weather, the output image loses a bit of sharpness, but not critical. State license plates of other road users are clearly distinguishable.

When driving in the dark on a city street illuminated by lanterns, the device behaves best, given the fact that it does not have infrared illumination. Of course, in order to distinguish car numbers on a video image, it is necessary that the distance to them be closer than in the daytime.

When driving at night on an unlit highway, the situation with the quality of shooting is predictably worsened. Despite this, even if an accident occurs, the culprit can be identified by video.
