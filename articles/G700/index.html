Metro station "Zyablikovo". Zyablikovo District

Zyablikovo metro station is located in the south of Moscow. It is followed by Shipilovskaya. The opening of the Zyablikovo metro station took place in December 2011, but the project was considered back in the early nineties. Then they began construction, which soon, however, was suspended due to lack of funding.





Features

The branch at which the Zyablikovo station is located is indicated in light green on the metro map. This Lublin-Dmitrov line is the only one built after the collapse of the Soviet Union. In 2021, new stations will open on this branch. Metro Zyablikovo in the south will remain the final stop. The construction of Lublin-Dmitrov line stations is carried out in the north of the city.

Apparently, the mayors do not consider it advisable to extend this branch to the south. The exits from the Zyablikovo and Krasnogvardeiskaya metro stations are a few meters apart. In the metro itself, you can go from the Lublin-Dmitrov line to Zamoskvoretskaya. The station has two underground vestibules: the north one is located southeast of the intersection of Yaseneva Street with Orekhovoy Boulevard, and the southern one is on the east side of Yaseneva Street.

The depth of the Zyablikovo metro station is a little over fourteen meters. Above the tracks are interchange balconies connected to the platform by two stairs. Construction history

The construction of the Zyablikovo metro station began in 1993, but, as already mentioned, it was soon discontinued. It resumed only after fifteen years. In the spring of 2011, they began to lay a tunnel, which was supposed to be the passage connecting the Krasnogvardeiskaya and Zyablikovo stations. Six months later, a test train set off along the site.

Zyablikovo District

This is a pretty picturesque area. Perhaps that is why it is the most densely populated in Moscow. According to 2017, more than 130 thousand people live here. Like many other areas located outside the Garden Ring, Zyablikovo arose on the site of the village of the same name. In the northwestern part of it - where today beautiful tall buildings rise, once there was the village of Borisovo. In the rest of the territory there were wastelands.

The village of Zyablikovo was located in a deep ravine, where today Guryevsky passage and Voronezhskaya street are located. The village, blooming garden strawberries, was demolished in the early eighties. This place after the collapse of the USSR for several years was a rather bleak picture resembling a garbage can. Gradually, the area was improved. Already at the beginning of the two thousandth situation has changed. Zyablikovo became one of the most prestigious sleeping areas of Moscow.





Orthodox church

In Zyablikovo there is only one church. The Holy Trinity Church was erected in the XVIII century. But in the twentieth century after the revolution, it was almost completely destroyed. The temple is located on the border of Zyablikovo with a neighboring area. It is notable for the fact that before the revolution there was the largest bell in Moscow. Its weight was 5000 kg. The church was closed for a long time, it was returned to believers only in 1990.
