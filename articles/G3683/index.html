How many dinosaurs lived and what died out

Are such terrible dinosaurs as they are described? The word "dinosaur" means "terrible lizard." However, many of these animals did not look like lizards, and they did not at all resemble terrible ones. In the article we will talk about who the dinosaurs are, in what era they lived, how many years they roamed the earth and what is the life expectancy of these dinosaurs.

Who are dinosaurs?

Before touching on the question of how many years ago dinosaurs lived, let us clarify who they are at all. Dinosaurs are extinct terrestrial vertebrates. To date, scientists have about 500 different genera and more than 1000 different species.

Why were these vertebrates called dinosaurs? It happened in 1842. The term was introduced by the English biologist Richard Owen. This was done for the convenience of describing these species of extinct animals. The fact is that the bones of large vertebrates found by archaeologists in different places on the planet were gigantic in size. By this they astounded scientists. It was obvious that the creatures that lived many thousands of years ago on the planet were not just large, but huge. From the ancient Greek "dinosaur" - "scary, terrible."

How many dinosaurs lived and where? Their habitat was vast - the whole planet. The remains of the dinosaurs were found both in South America and in the territory of modern Egypt.

When did dinosaurs live?

We almost came to the main question of this article: how long did the dinosaur period drag on? These animals lived safely on the planet in the Mesozoic, namely from the late Triassic to the end of the Cretaceous. This is from about 225 million years ago to 66 million years.

How many dinosaurs lived

Let's take it. If we subtract 66 from 225, then we get 159. That is, it can be argued that the dinosaurs lived on our planet for almost 160 million years.

Differences not only in species

All the "terrible lizards" were completely different: predators and herbivores, small and large, two-legged and four-legged. The average life expectancy of dinosaurs of various species also varies. Small representatives lived very little, 20-30 years. Large individuals lived 2-3 centuries. It is known that large predatory dinosaurs reached puberty only by 40-50 years.

And how many years ago did dinosaurs live? They appeared about 225 million years ago.

Causes of extinction

Scientists are still arguing about this topic. It is very strange why such a number of rather large and well-adapted creatures died out. The hypothesis about this is quite a lot, the most common are the following:

  1. The fall of a giant meteorite to the ground.
  2. The split of the continents.
  3. Changing of the climate.
  4. The extermination of predatory species, first herbivores, and then their own kind.
