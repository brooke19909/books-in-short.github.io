North Karelia, Finland: nature, recreation, fishing

What is Karelia? Everyone who has been there describes it in their own way. For some, it is a land that smells of tarry forest. Those who have been on vacation on the lakes speaks of the incomparable beauty of places with pointed "eyelashes of firs over the blue eyes of the lakes." Fishing enthusiasts talk about cold transparent ponds rich in fish. Leaving home, each takes with him an incredible amount of impressions, leaving a piece of his soul there in return. After reading this article, you may also want to go to North Karelia.

Finland is near

Everyone who arrives will find a vacation option for themselves. Whether he will be active and sporty in a ski resort, or whether you limit yourself to catching grayling and lake trout, depends on the option of your chosen vacation. In North Karelia, a customs station of Suoperya has been opened, through which you can get to the northern part of Finland, the Ruka-Kuusamo region. The nature of Finnish Karelia is no different from Russian - the same snow drifts in pine-spruce forests. In the lakes there are pike and perch. Olanka Park

All the beauty of the nature of North Karelia can be seen in Olanka Park, named after the river flowing through its territory. In the pine forest, caps of hills rise; under the trees you can see the growing Calypso orchid. In the riverbeds on alluvial (alluvial) meadows in the summer, migratory birds nest their birds and flutter rare species of butterflies. In the park you can meet bears and deer. Lynx and wolverine live in these parts. Walking along the paths of the park, you can go to the clearing with berries and mushrooms that are allowed to collect. In addition to the usual wild berries of strawberries and blueberries, interesting northern berries of the prince (arctic raspberry) and crowberry, belonging to the heather family, with leaf needles grow in the park.

Routes offered in the park

In the park, for the movement and recreation of tourists, there are trails in the spruce forest and bridges over the rivers. On the equipped areas of the park, you can set up a tent for a couple of nights for free or accommodate under a canopy. There are designated areas for making fires, there are braziers and firewood. The park has numerous water and hiking routes. One of them, 80 km long, is the most popular in North Karelia and is available all year. The route is called the Bear Trail. Typically, tourists pass it in eight days. On the trail there will be moss covered old trees of the ancient forest, canyons and stormy mountain streams.

Those who are looking for adventure - they will meet them in the form of six suspension bridges that need to be overcome. There will be stairs, bridges and narrow wooden decks on the way, providing passage in hard-to-reach places. There are forest huts on the Bear Trail in which people walking along the trail can relax and make a fire in a certain place. No less interesting is the one-day route along the Kitka River. He is also with trials in which you need to overcome three suspension bridges and walk on wooden floors in hard-to-reach places, and where necessary, climb rope ladders.

Pielinen Lake

On the east side of Lake Pielinen, there is a small town of Lieksa. It is located in the depths of North Karelia, surrounded by national parks and trekking tracks. Due to the tourist orientation of the city, tourists have places to stay - these are hotels, cottages and campsites. A full rest and a lot of entertainment at any time of the year will also be organized. In Patvinsuo National Park, tourists can hunt for wolverines, lynxes, foxes with a camera in their hands.

It is no less interesting to spend time in Finland in Koli Park, located in the western part of Lake Pielinen. In summer, tourists can go hiking. Trekking routes are represented by various levels of difficulty. The park has the opportunity to ride horses. There are observation platforms.



Situated from the north on the lake, two Finnish towns are attractive for tourists, each in its own way. In Nurmes, you can get acquainted with traditional Finnish life, visit artisan shops. The shallows are rich in whitefish and grayling.

In the "magic land" you can spend the winter holidays with all the New Year attributes - snow, frost, fluffy spruce and Santa Claus, who lives nearby, to extend the child's years of childhood and his faith in magic. Zoo Ranua

The capital of the fabulous country of Lapland is located beyond the Arctic Circle. This is a small town of Rovaniemi with its attractions. Here is the northernmost zoo in the world. In it, the conditions for keeping animals are as close to natural as possible. The zoo has approximately 60 species of wild arctic animals. To get to know the inhabitants of the zoo closer, special wooden walkways have been laid for visitors, which are either above the aviaries or pass alongside them. Everything is provided and done in the zoo so that the human influence is the least, and the animals feel comfortable. Arriving in Finland in the winter and falling into this zoo, you will get a unique opportunity to watch animals that, having changed their fur for winter, often leave their enclosures. The white (polar) bear at this time is in its element.





Finland for adults

What is Finland interesting for adults in winter? Here we will focus on ski resorts with impressive elevations and black slopes. If you are a skier, there are many places in Finland where you can ski. Each resort has trails for beginners and children.
