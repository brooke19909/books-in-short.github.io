Touchscreen and display: what is the difference, the principle of operation and characteristics

Touchscreen is one of the most common types of touch panels currently installed on devices of various applications. They are used on many varieties of equipment, ranging from simple custom smartphones to highly specialized devices in medicine, industry and research laboratories. Why exactly touchscreens have gained such popularity, what is their feature? Consider in our article today.

The difference between the touchscreen and the display

So what is the first element for? The touchscreen (from the English. Touch - touch, screen - screen) works in conjunction with the display, being one of the components of the display module.

It is used as an alternative to classic input devices. The display itself (from the English display, which means "show") is intended solely for displaying information to the user. Thus, on the phone, the difference between the touchscreen and the display is obvious. While one is a governing body, the second is used exclusively to display information at a given time.

Areas of use

Initially, displays with touch panels were used solely to control specialized equipment. After they began to install on devices from the consumer sector. These are smartphones, tablets, laptops and other equipment. In them, touchscreens were used as a replacement for traditional mechanical controls (such as keyboards, mice or game controllers, etc.).

And all these factors clarify the question of what is the difference between a display and a touchscreen. Touch panels are also used to control modern ATMs, payment terminals and other similar devices.

Touchscreen Benefits

The obvious advantages of touch screens include the simplicity of controlling the device, since all user interaction with information is intuitive and takes place directly on the display. The combination of input and output components allows to reduce the dimensions of the manufactured device, which in turn expands the scope of its application. Also, the absence of the need to connect external controls positively affects the use of the device in various conditions. And, as mentioned above, the lack of restrictions in the form of mechanical keys allows you to increase the functionality of the device as much as the imagination of the developer.

Touch Panel Disadvantages

It will be logical that touchscreens, like any technology, have their drawbacks. These include absolute non-repairability in case of mechanical damage, as well as higher fragility. Increased energy consumption is, of course, a rather controversial point, but it is worth noting it, since due to the appearance of an additional source of energy consumption, the autonomy of the device decreases. Also, for speedy typing, this type of control may not be suitable due to the lack of a tactile response; on a smooth screen, it is impossible to type text using blind typing due to the lack of topography of the keys.

As a display for the touchscreen, expensive matrices created using LED or OLED technologies are used. Once, ray screens were also taken as their basis, but now it is already a rarity.

Conventional Displays

At its core, the difference between a display and a touchscreen is that the former lacks controls for the contents of the screen. They are used solely for the purpose of outputting information, for the interaction with which peripheral devices must be connected.

To create them, all the same technologies are used: LED and OLED. There are also ray displays, but nowadays they are definitely not related to touchscreens.
