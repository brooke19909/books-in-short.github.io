Latvia, Jurmala: city history, sights, the date of foundation and reviews of tourists with photos

Jurmala is a popular Baltic resort located on the coast of the Gulf of Riga. The famous Latvian health resort is known for its chic sandy beaches and incredibly healing air. Rest in Jurmala (Latvia) has always been considered not only useful, but also prestigious.

A little bit about the resort ...

Jurmala (Latvia) is rightly called the largest resort in the country. The city has a well-developed infrastructure and a rich cultural life. The biggest pride of the resort are beaches, which are placers of quartz sand. The coast of Jurmala annually attracts tens of thousands of tourists. The modern city is divided into 16 districts. A distinctive feature of the resort is a rich cultural life. Festivals and other entertainment events are regularly held here throughout the year.

The population of the city is approximately 50 thousand people. Jurmala is located just 25 kilometers from Riga.

History of the city

According to archaeologists and historians, people appeared on the territory of modern Jurmala not 750 years ago, according to the first written records dated 1245, but much earlier. During excavations, specialists found objects of labor that were made in 2400-1800 BC. e. (during the late Neolithic period). The fact that such finds are far from unique indicates that ancient people lived here settled. It is known that the settlement was located in the Priedain valley. Primitive settlers chose such quiet places for life, since in them they were more protected from natural disasters.

The status of the resort of Jurmala (Latvia) acquired only after the region was transferred to Russia. The starting point can be considered the year 1783, when the Sloksky district was annexed to Vidzeme, which became part of the Russian Empire. After 20-25 years (in the first half of the nineteenth century), Riga's rich and landowners came into vogue to relax in Jurmala in the summer. Initially, guests stayed in the homes of local fishermen. But the land owners decided to sell the plots at a high price for development. Since then, a real construction boom has begun. The popularity of recreation in the region grew every year.

In 1824, transport links with Riga were established. At first, stagecoaches began to run on the seaside, and then a steamboat was launched. In Dubulti in 1832, the Russian commander Barclay de Tolly built a house for himself. After the steamboat was launched between Dubulti and Riga, the resort's popularity increased even more. Nevertheless, the greatest prosperity came with the opening of the railway in 1877, connecting Riga and Tukums. Gradually, the fame of Jurmala (Latvia) spread to other Russian provinces. Wealthy people combined bathing with therapeutic procedures in equipped baths. By 1845, 3,000 such places were equipped on the coast. Until the end of the nineteenth century, gentlemen and ladies bathed on the beaches separately.

The first sanatoriums can be called summer private clinics, which were opened by doctors. The most famous healers were Til, Maximovich and Nodstr√∂m. After the revolution, medical facilities began to be built on the seaside.

Dzintari Concert Hall

One of the main attractions of Jurmala (Latvia) is the Dzintari Concert Hall. It is very popular not only in Latvia, but also abroad. It is in this complex that all concerts in the country are held. Until 1910, representatives of different genres performed within the walls of the institution.

But later the hall became so popular that the Mariinsky Theater, the Edinburgh Symphony Theater, and the Warsaw Philharmonic performed there. World-famous opera singers regularly come here. The hall reached its greatest prosperity during the USSR period. The most famous singers of the country performed within its walls.

emeri park

The sights of Jurmala (Latvia) include Kemeri National Park. Its area is 38 hectares. Most of the territory is located on the Gulf of Riga. The park was organized in 1997, but it appeared much earlier. The story tells that the forester Kremer lived in this region, who not only watched the forest, but also acted as a guide for all those tourists. The forester built the first house around which the village of Kremer subsequently emerged, where people still go on excursions to this day.

True, experts could not explain how the name of the forester was changed and the name of the Kemeri park appeared. Tourists come to it for sulfur baths and for walks. There are several routes laid in the park that travelers can choose for themselves. Of greatest interest is the peat bog. As for the sulfur baths, they appeared under the influence of dolomite deposits. For this reason, people began to come to the park in the 18th century to improve their health.

Jurmala City Museum

In the city of Jurmala (Latvia) it is worth visiting the Jurmala City Museum. In his collection you can see many exhibits telling about the development of the city from the beginning of the nineteenth century to the present day. There are no ancient artifacts in the exposition of the institution, but there is an extensive collection of old postcards, bathing suits, items raised from the seabed. In general, the exhibits tell about the city as a resort.

Open-air museum

According to reviews, in Jurmala (Latvia) it is worth visiting the Open Air Museum, on the territory of which you can get acquainted with the life of a fishing village. It is no secret that many years ago the resort did not exist, and in its place there was only a small fishing village. The museum has collected traditional utensils and household items. Here you can see real fishing huts. It is better to visit the museum with a guided tour, during which tourists learn a lot about the life of ordinary fishermen in ancient times.

Treatment

Holidays in Jurmala (Latvia) are not only the sea, beaches and attractions, but also a great opportunity to get healthier, because the region is famous for its healing climate, good sanatoriums and mineral springs. Of course, nowadays there are much less health resorts than in Soviet times. But the local air is still healthy and filled with iodine and volatile. From sources in the region, water is produced that has a different degree of mineralization. In addition, there are sapropelic and peat mud, as well as hydrogen sulfide water.

The cost of rest in sanatoriums is quite moderate. Accommodation in a double room is 90 euros (6800 rubles) and more. This amount already includes nutrition and treatment.

In Jurmala sanatoriums they treat diseases of the kidneys, nervous system, heart, respiratory organs, metabolic disorders. Based on the diagnosis of the guests, different procedures can be prescribed - pearl and hydrogen sulfide baths, lymphatic drainage, mud therapy, paraffin therapy, massage and therapeutic nutrition. Some resorts even have their own mineral springs. The most famous sanatoriums are Yaunkemeri and Amber Coast, located in the territory of the Kemeri National Park.

Interesting places to relax with the whole family

It is difficult to find someone who would not hear about a resort like Jurmala in Latvia. What does the name of the region mean? Translated from Latvian Jurmala - it is a seaside.
