Empirical knowledge

Considering the concept of a method in science, we note that the theoretical and empirical method of organizing scientific knowledge is distinguished . Empirical knowledge can be considered as the sum of scientific facts that form the basis of theoretical knowledge. Researchers get them by using two common options: experiment and observation. Let us examine in more detail the concept of the method of empirical knowledge. Observation is a deliberate, special perception of the analyzed object. Among its distinguishing characteristics, we note the following features:

  • setting the goal of the study;
  • search for observation methods;
  • drawing up a work plan;
  • monitoring the studied object;
  • the use of a variety of devices to achieve this goal.

According to the results of the observation receive initial information about the object in the form of scientific facts.

What is an experiment? Consider the concept of a method, the features of its implementation. By experiment is meant a scientific research technique involving the reproduction or change of an analyzed object under certain conditions. In the process, the researcher has the opportunity to change the conditions for its implementation.

If necessary, at any stage of the study may be terminated. For example, you can put the studied object into various relationships with other objects, create conditions in which you can see properties and characteristics of a familiar phenomenon that are unknown in the scientific field.

The basic concept of the method is that with its help it is possible to artificially reproduce the analyzed phenomenon, in practice, verify the accuracy and reliability of empirical or theoretical knowledge. For its implementation, special technical devices are needed.

Devices are devices that have certain characteristics that allow you to receive information about properties and phenomena that are not perceptible to the human senses.

With their help, scientists take special measurements, identify new characteristics in the studied objects. Considering the concept of a principle, a research method, M. Born noted that observation and measurement are associated with a violation of the natural course of the process. In determining new conditions for the analyzed object, a person really intervenes in its nature, but without such actions it will be difficult to examine the object from different angles, to reveal its distinctive features, basic characteristics.
