Population, main buildings

In the future capital of Ingushetia rushes consistently, starting in 1995, and since 2000 - and in the status of the capital, already official. The city during this time grew in length by 4 km, and in width - by 3 km, also acquired a population (in Magas in 2012 about 5 thousand people lived permanently, and more recently, in early 2010, its population was only 524 resident). About 5-6 thousand people come here every day to work. About 90% of all republican state institutions are located in this city, including the residence of the president, ministries and parliament, as well as small units of the administrative apparatus. The Ingush State University is also located here , and by the 20th anniversary of the formation of the republic, the television building was completed. The capital of Ingushetia also has its own republican bank, and a business center has recently begun to actively develop.

The story of Magas

Magas today is a blank sheet, which since 1995 began to be painted in the colors of the state Ingush flag.





It is the only new city that was built in the post-Soviet time on the territory of the Russian Federation, as well as one of the few in the world, founded originally as the capital.

The inhabitants of Ingushetia, talking about Magas, often recall Peter the Great and the city he founded on the Neva. But the circumstances of the formation of these two capitals are fundamentally different. The city of St. Petersburg ‚Äústole‚Äù the status of the capital from the ancient, but quite capable and lively Moscow. The Republic of Ingushetia, after the Chechen-Ingush Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic, whose center was located in Grozny, broke up, was forced to remain without an administrative center at all.

How Magas became the capital

The (former) capital of Ingushetia, temporarily acting as the main city, Nazran could not accept this role, since it was and remains, in fact, a large rural settlement. The Ingush consider Vladikavkaz to be the historical capital, however, as you know, it has long been the main city of another republic, North Ossetia.

The way out of this difficult situation was the construction from scratch of the capital. Other similar examples include Astana in Kazakhstan, but it appeared 2 years later than the city of Magas. 4 km from Nazran in 1995, the garden city grew, right in the field - the cultural, economic and administrative center of this youngest republic in Russia, the new capital of Ingushetia.

Location hypotheses of ancient Magas

Magas, according to legend, was called the capital of the ancient Alanian state (which was an association of mountain tribes). The location of the historic city is not exactly established. Translated from the Ingush language, the name of the capital means "city of the Sun." It is known that in 1239 the troops of Batu destroyed ancient Magas. According to some versions, it was located exactly on the site of the present Ingush capital. But this assumption is not confirmed. There are two solar signs on the flag and coat of arms of the city, which is a reference to the city of the Sun.

There is, however, a more prosaic hypothesis, according to which the lands of the present capital once belonged to the family of Ruslan Aushev, the first president of the republic. Under him, the city of Magas was founded.
