Climatic conditions

In Puttaparthi (India), the weather is hot throughout almost the whole year, and the climate is dry. In summer, the temperature varies between 34-42 degrees Celsius, and in winter - 22-27. The hottest months are from March to July. A more comfortable temperature lasts from November to January.

Precipitation for the year is very small. You can expect them first in July and August, and then in October, November and December. When going on a trip, you need to prepare for the fact that in any case it will be stuffy and hot.

Sathya Sai Baba

Who is this man? This question interests all tourists who are going to the Sai Baba Ashram in Puttaparthi (India).

The news of his death appeared in the press in April 2011. He died at the age of 84. Sathya Sai Baba was not a simple child: while still very young for such an occupation, he could easily write an opera, and also was fluent in English. Later, an event occurred that made it unique. Sathya Narayana Raju (real name Sai Baba) received a bite of a scorpion. His further fate after such an event, of course, was predetermined. After all, this meant that after recovery he would become a healer. And so it happened. Then he came up with a new name.

A unique person, he could shock the most severe skeptics. Sai Baba was able to heal the patient, remove the pain, and also materialize the plan and even predict the future. But the most interesting thing is how he was in life. It is thanks to Sai in Puttaparthi (India) that there are good roads, schools, hospitals, nursing homes, clean drinking water has been provided. And all this was done at his own expense.

This small village is located 50 km from Puttaparthi, not far from the Bangalore-Hyderabad highway. It is recommended to visit the temple of Virabhadra - one of the terrible forms of Shiva. The building was erected in the XVI century, it is very beautiful and well preserved to this day.

Conclusion

Strictly speaking, Puttaparthi will be of interest only to those tourists who purposefully go to the Sai Baba ashram. In the city itself there are no unique attractions and interesting entertainments. There are no temples, no cinemas, no clubs here. Not even industrial production.

You can arrange shopping by going through souvenir and jewelry stores. Also, as an option for active pastime, you can select a bike rental. It is quite inexpensive, but you can independently go on a bike ride to the mountains or the surrounding area. Nearby there are lakes, you can swim and sunbathe on the coast.

Puttaparthi also has two Sai Baba museums. One is located on the territory of the ashram, and the other is next to the city stadium. It is a monumental structure, to which steps of pink marble were laid. According to reviews, there is a pool in which goldfish swim, and plasma TVs hang on the walls.

For those who live in the ashram, their entertainment is organized every evening. As a rule, people meet at the statue of Ganesha and read their prayers to her. Women (usually Russian) especially revere her, and even bring various offerings. There is also a Wish Tree, on which it is customary to hang letters with your requests, and then beat the bell. They say that everything desired in this case will come true. And next to it there is a cafe. In it you can have a tasty snack both at lunch and in the evening. The fee here is fixed and symbolic, while portions and the number of dishes are unlimited.

According to reviews, walking around the city, everywhere you can see hanging banners with quotes from Sai Baba. They are written in different languages, including Russian. Puttaparthi is a kind of city that is ready to accept people from different parts of the world.
