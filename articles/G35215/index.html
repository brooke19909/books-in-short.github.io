Nina Petrovna Khrushcheva - wife of the first secretary of the CPSU Central Committee

Nina Petrovna Khrushcheva has never been an icon of style. She is a simple and sincere woman, with a strong-willed character and a kind heart. The ridiculous and full lady did not at all resemble the role of the first lady, but her husband loved her without memory. This is a story of true love and trust - simple marital happiness.





Nina Khrushchev: biography

Nina was born in 1900 in the city of Vasiliv. At this time, he belonged to the Lublin province. Her parents were simple peasants. At nine, the girl went to a rural school. She always studied diligently, was collected and accurate. She later graduated from the Mariinsky Women's School. During the evacuation, he got to Odessa with him. She worked in the office of the training department.

In 1920, it clandestinely sided with the CPSU (B.). In June of that year, she was sent to the Polish front as an agitator. A little later in the fall, Nina went to study at the Communist University named after Ya. M. Sverdlov. In 1921 she was sent to the city of Bakhmut in the Donbas to teach at a local party school.

In 1922, she almost died of typhus. The woman was persistent and strong-willed. A short time after the illness, she was sent to work: to train teachers in Taganrog. I met Nikita Khrushchev in Yuzovka, where she worked at a party school.





Widower Khrushchev

At the time of the meeting, Khrushchev was already a young widower and had a son and daughter. He got married early. A handsome guy, dancer, harmonist and athlete was an enviable groom. It is not surprising that the girls did not give him a pass. The first wife of Khrushchev is Efrosinya Pisareva. She was a real clever and beautiful. Unfortunately, the woman died of typhus, leaving Nikita with two children. Khrushchev was very worried about the departure of his beloved, but he had to live on. Since he was still very young, and the children needed a mother, he caught up with Nina Petrovna. Her warmth, friendliness, sincerity and at the same time purposefulness conquered Nikita.

Simple communist

Nina Petrovna Khrushcheva worked like everyone else. She did not think that anything was different from any other Soviet woman. Her working day began at eight in the morning, and sometimes she got home no earlier than nine. Nina Petrovna headed the party department at the electric lamp factory.

She rode to work by tram. The woman could not even think about protection. Until the mid-60s, her passport indicated that her name was Nina Kukharchuk. The couple did not see the need to register a relationship. They signed only after Nikita Khrushchev resigned and they needed to register together in a new place.
