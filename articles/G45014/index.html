Denmark ... Sights you must not miss





The island of Funen is located here of extraordinary beauty, which, in principle, can be completely called a tourist attraction. Small towns with gingerbread houses and cobblestone pavements; medieval castles and paradise gardens - all this is more like illustrations for fairy tales read in childhood. It is not surprising that it was on this island in the city of Odense that one of the most beloved storytellers in the world, Hans Christian Andersen, was born. By the way, fans of his talent can visit the museum, open in the writer's house.

New Harbor, or Nyhavn, - by the name itself it is clear that this is a marina. But not ordinary: old ships are collected here. Such a sight will impress both adults and children who love stories about sea adventures and pirates. And on the promenade are cafes, bars, restaurants, where it is so nice to sit and enjoy the surrounding beauty.

Denmark on the world map is a very modest state. However , Kronborg Castle is known all over the world thanks to the immortal work of Shakespeare "Hamlet". It seems that time has stopped here, and if you look closely, in the halls you will see the silhouettes of the inhabitants of the castle. You cannot remain indifferent to this place, here you can see with your own eyes what is often shown in the cinema. Tall walls, a folding bridge, a moat, huge halls, royal chambers, luxurious furniture ... It is worth coming to Denmark just to get to Kronborg.

Culinary traditions





Denmark ... Copenhagen ... Sights ... All this, of course, is great, but acquaintance with the country will not take place if you do not try local delicacies. Danes are well-known gourmets, they prefer hearty, healthy and environmentally friendly food. A variety of spices are used in cooking. Even the wine that is traditionally served at Christmas is generously seasoned with spices. But a special delight in Denmark awaits the sweet tooth. Baking in this country has an amazing taste. Every native has favorite pastries and buns.

Features of local shopping

In Denmark, shops are open on weekdays from 9.00 to 17.00, on Saturdays from 9.00 to 14.00. Sunday at almost all retail outlets is a day off, with the exception of specialized shops for tourists.

As souvenirs from this country, you can bring magnificent handmade silver jewelry, exclusive glass and porcelain items. Prices, of course, cannot be called low, because value- added tax (25%) is included in the price of goods. But for tourists from countries outside the European Union, there is an opportunity to return 20% of the purchase price when leaving Denmark. This is possible provided that the products were purchased at least DKK 300 and upon presentation of a passport and sales receipt.

The history of the Danish flag

In fact, this is an amazing country - Denmark ... Photos, sights and rave reviews from experienced travelers can help to make an overview of it. But a close acquaintance begins exactly when you begin to delve into the details. For example, few people know about the flag of the state. Residents of the country call their flag Dannebrog ("painted canvas"), it traces its history to 1219. And about how the main symbol of the state appeared in Denmark, there is a whole legend. She says that he fell from the sky onto the battlefield, during the battle of the Danes with the pagans who attacked them. The red background of the flag symbolizes the battle itself, and the white cross should remind of the sky as the source of its appearance.
