What is Nazism: differences from fascism and nationalism

Fascism is not synonymous with the word nationalism, so do not confuse these two concepts. You just need to figure out what nationalism and Nazism are.

Nationalism

The next term that you need to know in order to understand what Nazism is is nationalism. It is one of the directions of politics, the fundamental principle of which is the thesis of the supremacy of the nation in the state. This political movement seeks to defend the interests of any particular nationality. But this does not always happen. Sometimes nationalism can form a people not only by the principle of one blood, but also by the principle of territorial affiliation.

How to distinguish nationalism from Nazism?

The main differences between Nazism and nationalism are that the representatives of the second are more tolerant of other ethnic groups, but do not seek to get closer to them. In addition, they, as already mentioned above, can be formed on territorial or religious grounds. Also, this political movement is less likely to run counter to economics, free-thinking and free speech. It knows how to penetrate into the legal field of the state in a qualitative manner and is able to cope with constructive criticism. Anyone who understands what Nazism is should know that under him the state follows totalitarian principles, and there is no place for free thought in it.

Nazism

What is Nazism? The definition of this concept became widely known throughout the world after the end of World War II. It is the Third Reich that is the main example, thanks to which one can understand what Nazism is. This concept refers to the form of social structure of the state in which socialism combines with an extreme degree of racism and nationalism.





The goal of Nazism was to unite in a vast territory a community of racially pure, Aryan people who could lead the country to prosperity for centuries.

According to Hitler, socialism was an ancient Aryan tradition.   • Racism.
  • Militarism.

Thus, one can understand what fascism and Nazism are, as well as nationalism. These are three completely different concepts, which, despite some similarities, are not synonymous. But, despite the facts, many people still consider them one.
