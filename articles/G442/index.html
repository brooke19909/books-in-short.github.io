St Petersburg stations: a brief excursion

An integral part of the transport infrastructure of any city is the railway tracks. St Petersburg stations can be called one of the most important transport junctions connecting the metropolis with the suburbs and other settlements of the country. Every day, every third resident of the city uses the services of railway carriers, therefore, information about the operation of a particular node is often memorized by them. Which stations in St. Petersburg are currently operating? There are only 5 operating stations in St. Petersburg - Baltic, Vitebsk, Ladoga, Moscow and Finland. Earlier in different years, the Warsaw, Okhtinsky and Primorsky stations functioned. In order not to get confused in the transport branches of the city and not to lose precious time, residents and guests of the city should carefully study the information on the most important platforms of St. Petersburg.

Vitebsky Station





Vitebsky Station is the only railway platform not integrated with the metro station. In order to get to the trains, you need to walk about 200 meters. All other stations of St. Petersburg have exits directly to the railway tracks. Vitebsky Station sends both suburban and international flights, such as St. Petersburg - Riga, St. Petersburg - Gomel, Petersburg - Kiev, and many others. The tracks are located near the metro station "Pushkinskaya". It is almost impossible to get lost on the way to the station - on occasion, kind-hearted Petersburgers will certainly tell the guest the way.

Ladoga station is the only transfer station in the city, that is, the only railway junction with through train traffic. Station address: 73 Zanevsky Prospekt.
