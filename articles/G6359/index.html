Ras Um El Cid

It is here, along with other hotels, that Viva Sharm Hotel 3 is located. Reviews of holidaymakers suggest that the resort has one of the most beautiful coral reefs on the entire Sharm coast. Unlike the neighboring Nabq Bay, Ras-um al-Sid does not have a large number of starfish and sea urchins in the water. Magnificent coral reefs attract divers from all over the world. Thanks to its incredible beauty of underwater landscapes and the huge variety of underwater marine life, the bay, which has become their favorite place, is of interest to divers.

Its name is translated from Arabic as "mother's cape." Even the famous researcher and scientist Jacques Cousteau, who has seen many wonderful places, once included this region in the list of the top ten around the world. The coastline of Ras um al-Sid Bay, along which there are many hotels, including Viva Sharm (Egypt), gradually descends to a depth of almost twenty meters. This creates the most comfortable conditions for divers to watch the diverse life of local marine life. In some areas, the bottom drops sharply to great depths, so when bathing in the bay, you must constantly remember to be careful.

Near Ras um al Sid is a real wall of gorgon corals. And in numerous grottoes and caves hiding a variety of marine life. Here you can meet moray eels, lionfish, groupers and schools of beautiful glass fish. During a period of strong currents, fishermen catch tropical horse mackerel near the reefs, often meeting large groups of barracudas during fishing.



sights
