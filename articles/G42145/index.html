Metro station "Alekseevskaya" on Prospect Mira

The metro station "Alekseevskaya" of the Kaluga-Riga line is located on Mira Avenue - one of the central highways of the capital. Over its more than half-century history, it has changed its name several times. It entered into operation in 1958 under the name "World". This toponym echoed the name of the avenue, where its only lobby went. When in 1966 the interchange station on the Koltsevaya Line was renamed Prospekt Mira, I had to think about the fact that the name of the station (Mir), located just after two hauls, somehow looked a lot like it. To avoid confusion, the station received the name "Scherbakovskaya". Thus, the memory of a prominent figure of the Stalin era, who suddenly died in May 1945, was immortalized.

Its current name - the metro station "Alekseevskaya" - the station acquired in the autumn of 1990. It contains a reference to the historical designation of the Moscow region. Under this name, she is known to the modern generation of Muscovites and guests of the capital, often using the metro.

"Alekseevskaya" on the metro map



The Kaluga-Riga line of the Moscow metro is indicated on the metro scheme in traditional orange. "Metro Alekseevskaya" is located on it between the points "Riga" and "VDNH". If we talk about a constructive solution, then this is a three-vaulted pylon station of deep laying. When it was built in the late fifties, an engineering innovation and a new construction technology were applied - the inclined escalator tunnel passed here from bottom to top. In the future, such an operating time was repeatedly used by Moscow metro builders in the construction of other deep-lying stations. Marble and granite of soft colors prevail in the decorative finish of the Alekseevskaya metro station. Dark green marble decorative elements of pylons against a light background look very impressive . The floor is made of red Ural granite, the track walls on the bottom are faced with greenish tiles. There are small benches at the base of the pylons. The room is illuminated in rows of round chandeliers with fluorescent tubes. Escalators are located at the end of the station.

Prospect Mira, metro station "Alekseevskaya"

The station has one ground lobby overlooking Prospekt Mira. Near the exit is Novoalekseevskaya street. The ground part is solved in the form of a semicircular rotunda with colonnades at the entrance and exit. The space in front of the station entrance has traditionally been a lively trading platform. There are also stops of ground modes of transport. You can get to the tram stop and the opposite side of the street through the underpass . Prospect Mira is a wide Moscow highway running north from the Garden Ring.

The metro area "Alekseevskaya" is built up with characteristic high-rise buildings in the style of the so-called Stalinist empire. In real estate circles, apartments in similar houses are quoted very highly. In addition to prestigious housing in the area there are many shops, restaurants and other entertainment venues.
