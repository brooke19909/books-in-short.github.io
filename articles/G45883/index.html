Rest and weather in Morocco in November

Any traveler wants to see an exotic country and not be exposed to dangers, get a high level of service and all this at an affordable price. How to put it all together? The answer is simple: to go to Morocco in a low season. Comfortably, not hot and dry - this is all about Morocco in November. Reviews of tourists show that this time is the best season for sightseeing, walking and enjoying unusual nature in solitude.





Geography

The kingdom of Morocco is located in the north of Africa. Its waters are washed by two reservoirs: from the north - the Mediterranean Sea, from the west - the Atlantic Ocean. The coastline of the state stretches for 1.8 thousand km. Morocco borders by land with Algeria, by sea - with Spain. The nature and topography of the country are very diverse and interesting. In the south of the country stretches the majestic Sahara desert, which is constantly "leading the offensive" on the fertile lands of the state. Throughout the country from the south-west to north-east stretch the mountain ranges of the Atlas. Green coastal plains stretch along the Atlantic coast. Climate

Morocco is located in several climatic zones. The Tetuan region is dominated by a Mediterranean climate, with hot summers and mild, humid winters. The average temperature in this region is + 21 degrees. The Atlantic coast is dominated by a subtropical, continental climate, with long and very hot summers and warm short winters. In the summer, the thermometer is often kept in the region of 35 degrees of heat, in winter, on average, the air warms up to 12 degrees. The largest amount of rainfall in the country falls in January-February, when it can rain every day. In summer, especially in the Atlantic, it can be arid. Weather in November

For those who do not like the heat, but want to see an interesting country, the best choice is a vacation in Morocco in November. Reviews, the weather in which is the main aspect, say that at this time the heat is already falling, but the temperature is very comfortable, which is great for trips around the country and excursions. On the shores of the Mediterranean Sea, on average, daytime temperatures are around 20 degrees Celsius. But in this region in November it often rains, up to 11 days a month. But on the Atlantic coast this is a great time when the air warms up to 23 degrees. Precipitation falls from 1 to 6 days per month. At night, the thermometer drops by 4-5 degrees. The water temperature holds about 19 degrees heat.

The mild and warm weather in Morocco in early November is ideal in order to get to know the distinctive, ancient culture of the country. Tourists coming to this state should not wait for numerous entertainment venues and luxury hotels. What to see

Morocco in November is an excellent time to travel to different places and cities and see everything interesting. First of all, the country is attractive for its natural sites. November allows you to wander along the snow-white coast of the Atlantic Ocean alone. The Atlantic is always magnificent, high waves, the power of water are fascinating, so you can walk endlessly here, and here you can also take great photos. The Atlas Mountains are also amazing, walking along mountain trails allows you to see the untouched, very spectacular nature and magnificent views of the coastal plains. And the third important travel area is the desert. It is best to go here with a jeep tour. This will allow you to see the endless sandy expanses, hold a magnificent photo shoot, as well as experience the full charm of an oasis culture.

Morocco is a country of interesting ancient cities in which the spirit of the Middle Ages and a special atmosphere of exoticism have been preserved. Four imperial cities are required to visit, these are Fez, Marrakesh, Mekns and Rabat. Here, the exoticism and originality of Moroccan culture are in the most concentrated form. Each city has a whole range of attractions: palaces, fortresses, gardens, museums. You can also drive to a small town in the north of the country, to Chawen, which is famous for the blue color of the houses.

Practical information

Visa-free regime gives attractiveness to the country, so you can safely buy last-minute tours. Morocco in November is a low season, so you can safely get a 20-30% discount on a hotel. When traveling with you, you need to take an adapter for electrical appliances, as sockets can be with either 220 or 100 V voltage. Even in autumn, a bright, dangerous sun shines in Morocco, so you need to stock up with UV filters. As well as medicines, as in local pharmacies the choice is scarce. Since the country is nevertheless rather poor, one can encounter battling people everywhere. Sellers try to get the maximum profit from tourists, so the markets are worth bargaining.
