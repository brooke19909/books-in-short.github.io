Difference from the usual types of gold chains - Bismarck, anchor, shell

The technique of chain weaving is to weave individual rings in one product.





For the manufacture of chains in classic jewelry, auxiliary parts are often used. For example, overlapping rings, rose spirals and other elements. In addition, soldering is often used, and the chain that has turned out is subjected to additional final processing - face modification, broaching, or rolling.

"Chain mail" is done only with the help of single rings. Wire for chain weaving can be used along with the purchased material. All structures, patterns and shapes create exclusively metal rings from various materials.

How to choose materials? What metals are bracelets and necklaces usually made of?

The whole process of making jewelry is done manually. For such products, various metals can be used, having calculated the strength characteristics of the future product. It is necessary to take into account the rigidity of the metal, the characteristics of the weaving itself, as well as the dimension of the rings.

The most popular materials are steel, copper, silver and brass. In addition, you can use niobium, bronze, titanium, sometimes gold.

What is the process like? What can it be compared with for a layman?
