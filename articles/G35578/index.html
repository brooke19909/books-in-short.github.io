Hotel TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * (Kemer, Turkey): reviews

TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * is a cozy establishment with an interesting design that combines modern urban trends and authentic village motifs. It is an ideal place to relax with the whole family or with a group of friends.





Location

40 km from Antalya International Airport is the TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * hotel. Kemer, Turkey, Kemal Ilgaz Street, 07980 - address of the institution. It is less than 2 km from the city center. Tourists should definitely visit these attractions:

  • Kemer Sea Pier (1 km);
  • Moonlight Beach (1 km);
  • the ancient city of Phaselis (9 km);
  • Goynuk Canyon (9 km);
  • Tahtali mountain (10 km);
  • Beldibi beach (14 km).

How to get there

If you arrive at Antalya Airport, the easiest way is to take a taxi or book a transfer to get to the TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * hotel in Kemer . In the event that you intend to travel in a rented or own car, you should follow this route:

  • upon leaving the airport territory, turn left and drive about half a kilometer;
  • turn in accordance with the sign for Antalya and continue driving until you reach the sign for Lara;
  • turn left and follow the D400;
  • turn onto Gazi Boulevard and follow along it;
  • when you reach the first roundabout, turn towards Hurriyet and continue on to Akdeniz Boulevard;
  • get to the sign for Kemer Yola and move in accordance with it;
  • at the second roundabout, turn towards Ataturk Boulevard;
  • turn onto Ilgaz street and continue driving until you reach the hotel.

Guest rooms

Despite the fact that on vacation tourists tend to spend as much time as possible by the sea and on excursions, comfort in the room plays an important role in compiling the overall impression of the hotel. Speaking of TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 *, it is worth noting quite decent living conditions. You can choose one of the following rooms for accommodation:

  • Economy is a compact, bright room designed for two guests. There are a pair of single beds that can be installed separately or pushed together. There is also a dressing table with a large mirror. From the panoramic window you can enjoy the courtyard and garden.
  • The standard room is equipped with a large double bed, a dressing table and a lounge chair. Features a private furnished balcony with garden views. The design is dominated by beige and coffee shades.
  • Superior room has more space compared to the standard. It uses natural wood furniture. In addition to the bed, there is also a dressing table, a pair of armchairs and a reading lamp.
  • The family room has two bedrooms. One of them is equipped with a large bed, and the second - a pair of one and a half. Each room has soft chairs for relaxation and desks. The adult bedroom has a balcony.

A pleasant impression is made by the rooms that the TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * hotel offers its guests for accommodation. Photos show an elegant and unobtrusive design that creates a cozy home environment.





Room Amenities

Modern man is accustomed to comfort. Even being far from home, travelers want to feel comfort and enjoy all the benefits of civilization. In the resort complex TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * they will have such an opportunity. Each guestroom features amenities such as:

  • a bathroom with all necessary bath accessories;
  • built-in electric hair dryer;
  • centralized air conditioning system;

The staff of the resort complex TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * monitor the cleanliness of the rooms. Rooms are cleaned six times a week, and bed linen is changed every three days.

Hotel TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 *: reviews, disadvantages

Experienced tourists know that there are no hotels without flaws. Even in the most fashionable establishments you can find some flaws. The TT Hotels Hydros Club 4 * hotel was no exception. Reviews about him contain such negative comments:

  • animators are rather indifferent towards the guests (they seem to do their job, but do not try to interact with the guests in any way);
  • the reception is an open area, and, accordingly, no air conditioners or fans are provided here (while waiting for check-in and check-in, tourists have to languish from the heat);
  • during thematic dinners, the menu is practically no different from the usual one (the only thing that distinguishes one dinner from another is the flags of different countries in the restaurant hall);
  • in the bathroom you will not find either soap or shower gel (of all cosmetic accessories there is only liquid soap and toilet paper);
  • the quality of cleaning in the rooms is far from ideal (sometimes it is limited only to making a bed and removing garbage);
  • the restaurant menu presents too narrow an assortment of seafood, as for a seaside hotel;
  • there is no free ice cream, which was especially a big chagrin for children;
  • at night the noise of music coming from neighboring hotels or nightclubs interferes (the situation begins to subside only at four in the morning);
  • sun loungers on the beach are so densely located that it is almost impossible to pass between them;
  • Before checking in new guests, employees do not check the serviceability of equipment in the room (guests often find problems and have to wait for repairs);
  • in most rooms it doesn‚Äôt catch wireless Internet (mainly this problem concerns the bungalow);
  • the premises have long been in need of redecoration (the paint on the walls is stained and cracked in places);
  • low-quality alcohol of local production (and the prices of imported drinks are unreasonably high);
  • there are many small fish in the sea that painfully bite swimmers by the feet;
  • there are practically no places for walking near the hotel, except for a narrow path by the sea, which for some reason is called the "promenade" (it is not illuminated);
  • in stores in the hotel prices are two times higher than in urban outlets (it is better not to be too lazy and walk a few minutes to the center for shopping);
  • there are no irons in the rooms and floors (apparently this is to ensure that guests go to the laundry room for a paid ironing service);
  • few fruits on the buffet menu (and those that are not always tasty and fresh);
  • even if you leave a tip to the maid, cleaning will still be superficial (the maximum you can count on is swans from towels on the bed);
  • in rooms located in the bungalow, guests often notice ants;
  • children's pools are located in an open area under the scorching sun (there are no green spaces or shades for shade);
  • queues are always lined up for meat and fish dishes in the restaurant (however, if you do not want to waste time, you can still find many delicious dishes in the public domain).
