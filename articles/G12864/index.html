New building or "resale": which is better to purchase?

One of the main issues that many homebuyers have is about the choice between a new building and a secondary. The choice is seriously complicated if the prices of both types of apartments are not particularly different. Each option has its pros and cons, so you need to be very responsible in buying a home. This is an apartment, and the price of some of them can reach hundreds of thousands of dollars.





New building or "secondary housing" - which is better?

Primary housing is an apartment that was not previously issued to anyone. In fact, they often try to sell apartments that are not even built as primary housing.

Note that the sale of apartments in houses that are only being built or planned for development is regulated by law No. 214-FZ. This law obliges the developer to build a house and put it into operation, transferring to the program participants (buyers) the apartments (construction objects) fixed in the contract.

After the house is put into operation , a signature is put under the act of acceptance of the object. At the same time, buyers will have to register ownership of the apartment in the new building. After the certificate of state registration is received, the status of housing will automatically change from primary to secondary.

What is a secondary housing?

Resellers - this is an apartment that belongs to someone, that is, is the property of an individual or legal entity. In this case, the apartment may be private, municipal or state. But it is still secondary housing. Therefore, even new apartments, which were built six months to a year ago, are already considered secondary housing, although in reality they are new. Therefore, initially it cannot be said which is better - a new building or a "secondary housing", because both apartments can be new.





As for municipal apartments, people can live there through a social contract of employment. They do not have a certificate of state registration, but the housing where they live is still considered secondary, since they still have an owner - the municipality.

Criteria for comparing both types of apartments

It is impossible to determine exactly what is better to buy - a ‚Äúsecondary building‚Äù or a new building, because there are no obvious advantages of primary housing over secondary. Everything will depend on the situation and conditions. If you want to make the right choice and finally decide for yourself which is more profitable - a "secondary" or a new building, then consider the following criteria:

  1. Cost. If we talk about the ‚Äúprimary‚Äù, then there is a pattern: the cost per square meter of housing depends on the stage of construction of the object. At the earliest stage, the cost per square meter will be low, and this is the main plus of primary housing. The price of the secondary is always higher, and there is no way to bring down this fixed price.
  2. Dates. When purchasing secondary housing, you can enter it immediately after the transaction. But this is not so with the primary. If the buyer makes a deal and buys housing at the stage of digging a pit, then you will have to wait another two years. If you conclude a deal at the construction completion stage, then the price per square meter will be higher, but you will have to wait only about three to four months.
  3. Investments. When purchasing housing in a new building, it is offered to the buyer for rough finishing. This means that you will have to invest a lot of money in repairs, the purchase of building materials, the purchase of equipment and furniture. When buying an apartment in the secondary housing market, you can often find renovated apartments. And although repairs are most often cheap or old, such an apartment is quite suitable for living. So the question of further investment is relevant when choosing a home.
  4. Range. The secondary housing market is much wider, and there are more offers. Usually the most successful apartments in new buildings are bought at the stage of excavation. By the end of development, the most unfavorable options remain for sale.
  5. Registration. It is impossible to register in a new building, because to obtain a residence permit you must either have a certificate of registration of ownership, or have a relative who is already registered in municipal housing.
  6. Possible income. It has already been written above that the price per square meter rises in direct proportion to the stage of construction. Therefore, when investing in the pit stage, the owner of a future home can make a profit of up to 30% per annum. This is the case if the construction takes place without delay. The cost of secondary housing is not guaranteed to grow, but it is changing due to market factors. However, you can earn money in a secondary apartment, renting it out. Calculating the amount of profit is difficult. It all depends on the cost, area and many other factors.
  7. Mortgage. Resale or new building can be taken in a mortgage. However, in the case of mortgage lending for an apartment in a new building, there are many different difficulties. Banks try not to issue loans for the purchase of apartments that are not yet completed, as there is a risk that they will never be completed at all. One or more maximum banks can issue such a loan, and the conditions may be disadvantageous. Typically, the rate on primary housing is 2-3% higher compared to the rate on secondary.
  8. Legal cleanliness. By purchasing new housing, you can be 100% sure that it is purely from a legal point of view. No one has lived in it before, and the buyer is the new owner. Apartments on the secondary market had owners, sometimes several. And often it is not known what kind of people they were and what they were doing in the apartment.




In addition, new buildings always have fresh communications and engineering systems. On the secondary market, communications can be worn out and require replacement, so in this case this is an advantage in favor of a new building. Also, when you purchase the ‚Äúprimary‚Äù, there is a very low chance that a drug addict or an alcoholic will live nearby.

The risks

Buying an apartment in a new building under construction, there is a risk that the construction company will go bankrupt and the construction will not be completed. In this case, you can not take your money back. Foreseeing this is unrealistic. Practice shows that quite often construction companies delay the deadlines for housing rental, and buyers can not do anything about it. Of course, the government is trying to negate this risk by considering laws on compulsory insurance of developers against bankruptcy. But the risk is still there, and even the penalties for the delay does not stop the builder to drag out the deadlines. Choosing between a new building or a "secondary housing", you need to remember these risks. But if the developer is trusted and respected, then the risk of acquiring housing from him is sharply reduced.

However, there are also risks in the secondary housing market. They are associated with the execution of the contract of sale. This contract may be terminated in court after the buyer has paid all the money. There are also cases when third parties appear with legal title to real estate. In this case, the contract of sale may again be terminated, and the transaction will be declared invalid. In this case, you lose your money and remain without an apartment. Fortunately, all these risks can be mitigated with the help of a competent lawyer, but his services can be expensive.





Pros of a new building

Apartments in new buildings have the following advantages:

  1. They are made of modern materials. This indirectly means that they are more durable and reliable.
  2. They are well designed and almost always spacious.
  3. The layout is comfortable and executed in accordance with modern standards.
  4. The staircases are clean and large. The same goes for the porches.
  5. All apartments have double-glazed windows.
  6. Water and gas meters are everywhere.
  7. There are working elevators, in some houses even freight elevators are provided.
  8. There is parking underground and in the yard.
