What is the principle of metal detectors based on?





Any metal detectors operate on the basis of the principles of the Foucault currents known in the school curriculum. We will not go into the details of the experiments. When the search coil and a metal object come closer together, a frequency change occurs in the generator, which the device reports with an audio signal. If there is a beep in the headphones, it means that something metallic is lying underground.

Search metal detector assembly sequence

A search coil is made from a plywood circle with a diameter of 15 cm: the wire is wound around the template in coils (15-20). Stripped ends are soldered to the connecting cable. A layer of thread is wound around the perimeter of the coil over the wire to secure it.

All details of the circuit are soldered on a PCB printed circuit board in the following order: capacitors, resistor system, quartz filter, signal amplifier, transistor, diodes, search generator. A soldered board is placed in the prepared case, connected to the search coil and mounted on a stick-holder.

The signal from the search coil, reflected by a metal object, increases the frequency of the generator. Amplified by a quartz filter, it is converted by an amplitude detector into a constant pulse, which produces sound.
