Manila Attractions Tours to the Philippines

The state of Southeast Asia, located in the Pacific Ocean, on the archipelago of the same name, has always been popular with tourists from all over the world. A comfortable climate, magnificent beaches, underwater coral reefs, rich flora and fauna attract hundreds of thousands of travelers who dream of real exoticism. Tours to the Philippines is a memorable vacation in a paradise with pristine nature and unusual architectural monuments.

Picturesque capital

The capital of the state is the most beautiful metropolis of Manila, which is located on the island of Luzon. According to tourists, the city is incredibly similar to Los Angeles: the same business districts, modern skyscrapers, expensive sleeping areas, fenced luxury villas. In the economic heart of the country there are incredibly many places that all guests of a colorful city must visit.



History tour

Charming Manila, the sights of which surprise with its unusualness, was originally a Muslim village. At the end of the 16th century, residents disobeying the Spanish authorities were expelled from the territory, and on June 24, 1571, a new district of Intramuros appeared, surrounded on all sides by ramparts. After 24 years, an overgrown city, having survived several terrible wars, becomes the capital of the Philippines.

There was a moment in the history of Manila when the Spaniards lost their dominance and were replaced by the British, who ruled for two years and plundered the main pearl of the archipelago. After signing the peace treaty, the city begins to gradually recover, the first educational institutions, monasteries, temples, private palaces appear.

At the end of the 19th century, the Spaniards ceded their possessions to the Americans, who were very cruel to the islanders. But the most terrible test was the Second World War, during which Japanese soldiers massacred more than 100 thousand inhabitants, and as a result of the bombing, many architectural monuments were destroyed.

Jose Risal Park

Manila immortalized the name of a national hero - a fighter for the country's independence, erecting a monument of bronze and granite near the place where the Philippine patriot was executed. The memorial is guarded by soldiers, and all political figures arriving in the country lay wreaths at its base, where Risal is buried. In 1946, independence of the Philippines was proclaimed at the monument.



Rizal Park is a popular vacation spot for urban residents and foreign visitors. In the stylized Japanese and Chinese gardens, laid out in the park, couples love to walk, and the planetarium, butterfly pavilion and several playgrounds will attract the attention of children. Adults watch the performances of the best martial artists, have picnics in the fresh air and enjoy streaming music from special venues where various groups perform. There is also a three-dimensional map of the Philippines located on the water, and guests of the park are looking at it with curiosity from a three-meter viewing platform.

Ninoy Aquino - main airport

Manila recognizes that tourism is the main source of income, and does everything to make it convenient and comfortable for those who come to glorious land, and the first acquaintance is not the last.

The main gate of the Philippines is the only international airport. Manila is proud of the modern air pier, bearing the name of a senator, who was killed 33 years ago. There are four terminals inside, and when you fly home you will have to pay a small fee for using them. Experienced tourists recommend changing the currency to local pesos in advance and not losing a receipt. A lot of signs will not let you get lost, and free buses running between terminals will quickly deliver to the right place.
