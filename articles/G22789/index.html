This building houses the Cuenca Archaeological Museum, the main attraction of the city

Many tourists visiting the Spanish town of Cuenca cannot pass by a luxurious building guarded by two proud chimeras. This building houses the Cuenca Archaeological Museum, the pride of the city.

History of the archaeological museum



Currently, the whole history of Cuenca is concentrated in this building. The archaeological museum is located in the Garden of St. Philip Nerry. The beauty of the garden is amazing. This is a combination of natural beauty and that created by the hands of people. A walk in the garden can also be a kind of excursion that will help to understand Spain.

The museum was created back in 1871. Then it was located in another building, which easily fit the entire small collection. The visitors were offered ethnographic exhibits, objects found by archaeologists, works of decorative art and collections that could interest numismatists. But a very small amount of time passed, and the collection began to grow rapidly.

Spain was able to buy some of the exhibits, others were found during excavations throughout the country, but a large number of exhibits and collections were donated by philanthropists.

As soon as the number of museum exhibits grew, a new building was needed. And then a beautiful building was proposed on Serrano Street. This building houses the Cuenca Archaeological Museum to this day.

Museum Architecture

First of all, visitors to the museum are admired by two chimeras, who are importantly seated on the sides of a wide staircase. In addition to them, the museum building attracts Doric columns and chicly decorated balconies. All this emphasizes the neoclassical style in which the building is made.

The Archaeological Museum of Cuenca is a huge quadrangle with an area in front of it. On this square, tourists love to take photos. Often here you can see the newlyweds. The museum has courtyards that amaze with its luxury.

The city of Cuenca is striking in its beauty. Tourists are invited to look at the Catholic cathedrals, which now have become not only a place of worship of God, but also architectural monuments.
