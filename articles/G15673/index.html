Multitronics RC-700 On-board Computer: Installation

To install the BC in a standard 1DIN location, it is necessary to insert 4 hooks in turn, tighten them with a screw and remove the console lining. To do this, press the key at the bottom right and, moving the right side towards yourself, remove it. Then connect the cable to the connector on the back of the chassis, install it in the panel, insert and tighten the screws until they touch the hooks of the panel. Return the front panel to the Multitronics RC-700 trip computer . Installation begins with the fact that it is necessary to hook on the left side of the lining behind the chassis. After that, push the panel in the far right to fix it in the chassis.

If the seat meets the ISO standard, disconnect and remove the extra frames, remove the lining, insert the teeth and fasten the adapters. Then attach the brackets, loop connector, install and attach the chassis. Return the lining to its place.

To install the trip computer Multitronics RC-700 under the 2DIN standard, you need to remove the console lining, remove the frames and separate the lower and upper parts. Connect the bottom of the frame to the chassis, insert the teeth and fix the adapters with screws. Attach the brackets to the computer case, fix the cable and insert the BC into the panel. Replace the 1DIN device and lining.





Connect parking sensors

To install the Multitronics PT-4TC, you must connect it to 2-4 pins of the computer connector. The designated side of the parking radar cable should be turned to the edge of the BC.

Connect the Multitronics PU-4TC to pins 5 and 6 of the optional trip computer connector. The cable should be oriented to the main MK connector.

In the Multitronics RC-700 BC, connecting the front and rear parking sensors is simultaneously possible by connecting loops in parallel. Collaboration of different models of parking radars is impossible.

Universal mode

In the 9-pin adapter connector, the blue wire is connected to the nozzle, the white is connected to the K-line, the yellow is connected to the ignition system, the red is connected to the positive pole of the 12 V power supply, the black is connected to the ground, the brown is connected to the dimensions, and the green is connected to the sensor fuel tank, pink - to the odometer.

In the 16-pin ODB-2 connector, the blue wire connects to the J1850, gray to CAN High, black to ground, white to K-line, brown to L-line, orange to CAN Low, red to positive 12 V power supply. K-line contacts, +12 V and ground are duplicated. It is forbidden to connect duplicate contacts in another connector when they are connected in the first.

Connecting the indicated contacts in one connector, it is forbidden to connect duplicate contacts in the other - they must be reliably insulated.

You can read more about the connection schemes of the device with sensors of Nissan, Mitsubishi, Toyota, Honda, Suzuki, VAZ and GAZ cars on the manufacturer‚Äôs website.

Troubleshooting

If there are problems with the Multitronics RC-700, then to restore the factory settings, you must turn off the power or disconnect the removable panel and, when the SET button is pressed, apply power or connect the panel. Confirm reset with SET.

It should be borne in mind that the Multitronics RC-700 BC turns off when driving if the vehicle speed exceeds 100 km / h. This is to ensure traffic safety.

When starting and turning off the engine, malfunction of electrical equipment, voltage surges can occur that can lead to malfunction of the Multitronics RC-700. A message about overvoltage, failure to perform some functions, lack of indication may be a consequence of this. To troubleshoot, turn off the power for 10 s.

Reviews

Multitronics RC-700 user reviews are positive. Buyers appreciated the ease of installation, the availability of a speech synthesizer, speaking out faults and decoding their codes, quick access thanks to a hot menu. Especially useful consider the ability to connect parking sensors, accounting for gas mileage, fuel quality assessment.

Dissatisfaction is caused by the limited service life of the control buttons - after 2 years a single press becomes insufficient, as well as the operation of the HT7533-2 stabilizer in the power circuit. The element works to the limit of its capabilities, and the increased temperature in the summer and heating in the winter contribute to its overheating. The consequence of this is malfunctioning of the on-board computer, overestimated data on air temperature and mains voltage (for example, 16.0 V instead of 14.3 V).
