In the vast majority of cases, the houses in Krasnodar are interlocked two-story or three-story houses, divided into several sections. Thus, a peculiar combination of a country cottage and a full-fledged city apartment is formed. By type, such houses can be:

What are they built from?





It is worth noting some of the most common technologies that are commonly used in the process of building townhouses.

  • Brick-aerated concrete. Such buildings are distinguished by a fairly high degree of reliability, as well as very, very good thermal performance.
  • LSTK (quick-mounted structures). An essential advantage of this type of building is that they are built in an extremely short time at a minimum price.
  • A porous block that can be faced with plaster or brick. The advantage of such houses can be called brick construction, which will definitely add a sense of reliability to the owner of such a building.
  • Monolithic construction. In this case, additional enclosing structures made in the form of external walls are used. The advantage of this technology will be that it is extremely reliable, while being resistant to seismic manifestations. You can also conduct the construction of premises with a completely free layout.

Legal nuances





If you are interested in town houses in Krasnodar, you should remember a few legal points and be sure to pay attention to them if you sign an agreement with the organization.

Initially, you will need to see if your developer has an active building permit. If the construction is not completed at the moment, you can also check the rights to the land and determine what they are trying to sell to you - the building itself or the building along with the land, and how it will all be completed.

Be sure to check the type of permitted use, as there are certain legislative nuances regarding the land on which you can build townhouses in Krasnodar. The feedback from some companies suggests that owners or developers sell such dwellings, despite the fact that they were not built in accordance with applicable law. As a result, certain problems arise. The construction of such buildings is permitted exclusively on land intended for individual or low-rise construction. As an exception, construction of four-section buildings on country lands is allowed if no more than four sections are present in one row. In any other situations, the construction of such buildings is illegal.

In the predominant majority of cases, the sale of townhouses is carried out with a full set of communications leading to the risers in the place indicated in the project. It is for this reason that the finished object should be accepted solely according to the specification of the project attached to the contract.
