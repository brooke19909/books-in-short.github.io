History of the Royal Castle in Kaliningrad

The Kaliningrad region is the only one in Russia that is separated from our vast country by other states. And when you walk around the city, you can find it in a rather strange settlement. Here, there are purely Soviet districts that cause gloom with their dullness, and typical Burger quarters, which are more pleasant to wander around. Kaliningrad is also today a modern Russian port city, but in 1255, when it was founded, the area belonged to Prussia. The Middle Ages are generally a very interesting and at the same time terrible time when the most unexpected events took place. And just then, the fight against the Gentiles was almost the main goal, a noble deed. Therefore, often when mentioning that era, one can hear about the Crusades, in which everyone who was waiting for "spiritual salvation" took part. So, returning to the topic: K√∂nigsberg Castle, on the undertaking of King Ottokar II and Grandmaster of the Teutonic Order Peppo von Ostern, was erected "to defend the Christian faith."

In the spring, work began on the construction of Konigsberg, and, it is worth noting, on a fairly large scale. First, on the site of the previously burnt Prussian fortification of Twangeste, a wooden fortress was built that went into the ground. Further, it was planned to build a stone defensive structure, which would occupy the entire spacious plateau of the Royal Mountain, located at an altitude of 20 m above the level of the Pregel River. In 1256, the Knights of the Teutonic Order fenced off a stream, thus obtaining the Castle Pond, which was extremely necessary in those days, from which the moat was replenished. By the way, it was here that the first mill was built.

In 1260 there was a Prussian uprising - the largest in history. They destroyed all the Christians they managed to capture, and also burned many castles, fortresses and other buildings. A year later, the Prussians reached the Royal Castle, besieged it, capturing and destroying the settlement. The Konigsberg castle had not yet been completed, but it adequately withstood a two-year siege and a fierce battle.

In the following years, the Royal Castle (Kaliningrad) was constantly changing, rebuilding, transforming, becoming larger, more beautiful and stronger. By the XV century it was already an impregnable and quite powerful fortress, which was a big problem to take. However, after some time, the walls ceased to be protection against guns. Gradually, various works of art and valuable books accumulated here. So the castle turned into the cultural center of Prussian land.

In 1525, K√∂nigsberg officially became the residence of the first secular ruler of Prussia - Duke Albrecht. In the era of the Renaissance, the palace, which had already managed to transform, became even more beautiful. The Royal Castle (Kaliningrad) was completely completed and became a magnificent aesthetic residence of the Prussian Dukes.





"Death" of the Royal Castle

The construction founded by the Czech king lasted until 1968. The castle suffered its first destruction during the Second World War. Aerial bombing caused major damage to the building, but, oddly enough, it still retained its beautiful and slightly formidable appearance. In addition, it could be restored. Over the 7 centuries of existence, this structure has been repeatedly modified. Therefore, it combined all the once fashionable styles: Gothic, Renaissance, Baroque and even Rococo. At the same time, its purpose also changed. At first it was a defensive structure, then a castle, then fashionable ducal residences, then a museum complex, and then a place in which spiritual life was concentrated.

Castle today

Today you can see only the ruins of the Royal Castle in Kaliningrad. But even the remains of this historic building haunt the locals, exciting imagination, especially young people. At one time, excavations were carried out here and the possibility of restoring the structure was discussed.

Now in the summer, an exposition is opened, which allows you to inspect large and small building samples and other details. On the observation deck, the real knightly tournaments are held, accompanied by various interesting activities and relevant decorations.





Archaeological site of the Royal Castle in Kaliningrad

Archaeological work was carried out for 7 years, until 2008. Then, due to lack of funding and complete indifference on the part of the Kaliningrad City Hall, the excavations were stopped. But the question of the need to continue conducting archaeological work remains open.

Awaiting Recovery

In 2009, a program was developed to reconstruct the center of Kaliningrad, even a competition was held, but only the development of the territory of the former castle did not take place due to the development of the financial crisis. Then they decided to hold a referendum again, but when the governor and the regional government changed in 2010, this issue was no longer raised.

Today it is known that the restoration of the Royal Castle in Kaliningrad will nevertheless take place. So far, only reconstruction is planned, but the main thing is that the city hall has found a sponsor of the work.
