His son Karl was the eldest of the two sons of the monarch, the younger brother was born later and was named Karloman.

Like the other sons of noble families, Karl was brought up in the saddle. From an early age he mastered the profession of a warrior and ruler. At that time, Karl received an excellent education: he was fluent in several languages, studied ancient literature, played musical instruments. Charlemagne's Goals

Briefly, the achievements of the king of the francs can be described as follows:

1. The imposition of Christianity. Charlemagne extended the borders of his power, imposing Christian teaching among the tribes subjugated by him. The greatest fame brought him victory over the Saxons and Lombards. Strengthening Christianity in the conquered lands, the construction of new monasteries and temples in the image of Byzantium - these are also the achievements of Charles. The most famous and extant temple was the Achaean Cathedral.



The Catholic Church has always been interested in what goals Charlemagne set for himself. History (Grade 6) mentions that at the end of his life this monarch was anointed by the pope and received the title of emperor. This solemn event took place in Rome in the year 800.

2. The revival of the Roman Empire. Charlemagne was able to fully prepare the second birth of the Roman power, because at the end of his life the area he conquered was ten times larger than the original size of the country in which he had once ascended to the throne. This goal was achieved thanks to continuous military campaigns. In total, Karl took part in 53 campaigns, of which 27 were personally headed by him. The ruler divided his country into counties, and on the borders created special territories - stamps reinforced by the army. Constant control over the graphs and margraves was carried out with the help of "sovereign people" personally loyal to Karl.



To fully understand what goals Charlemagne set for himself, it is enough to know that not one of his daughters has ever married. The strict father considered unworthy candidates for husbands all the sons of the rulers of neighboring states.

3. What goals Charlemagne sets for himself in the field of education can be easily traced by the level and number of schools and theological seminaries. Their number has increased several times. The most talented students were prepared for the adoption of the spiritual order. It was during the reign of the King of the Franks that a school was opened where they taught to rule the country, a prototype of the future university. A special decree of Charlemagne created the Palace Academy - a place where they read and discussed the works of ancient writers and modern theologians, practiced writing poetry and guessing intellectual riddles. At the behest of this ruler, annals were written - annual chronicles of the reign of the king, as well as preserved works of ancient art, transcribed by the court clerks of the ruler of the Franks and Lombards. In general, during the reign of Karl, so much was done for culture and art that historians call this time the Renaissance of the Carolingians - by the name of the Frankish emperor.
