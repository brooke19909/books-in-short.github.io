Borodino Field (Museum-Reserve): attractions, description

In the history of wars of any state there are battles that became turning points. For Russia in the war of 1812, the Battle of Borodino took place on September 7th.

It took place on a field of the same name one hundred and ten kilometers from Moscow west of Mozhaysk.

Borodin had the Russian Napoleonic army, conquering all of Europe and now standing at the approaches to Moscow, was hit so hard that the French could no longer recover. This heroic battle was the beginning of the defeat of the enemy invading forces.



History of creation

The events of 1812 left their mark not only in Russian history, but also in the annals of all of Europe. And therefore it is not surprising that the place where one of the decisive events of that war took place was turned into a monument.

The fact that this field is obliged to maintain its glory for centuries, the Russians realized ten years after the victory over Napoleon. Historians describe the chronology of how the Borodino field was turned into a museum reserve.

Opening

The Borodino Field Museum-Reserve has its history since August 1839. On this day, with the participation of Emperor Nicholas I and the participants of the battle before the formation of the fifty-thousandth army on Battery Raevsky - the main reference point of the Russian army during the battle - a monument created by architect A. Adamini was consecrated at the Kurgan height. A few months earlier, the ashes of General P.I. Bagration - one of the heroes of Borodin.



A gatehouse was built near the monument by decree of the emperor, intended for war veterans who were supposed to keep a book of entries coming to see the battle plan and trophies left after it. Description

This historical monument is considered one of the oldest among those based on the battlefield. In 1961, it was renamed the State Borodino Military-Historical Museum-Reserve and included in the Code of the Most Valuable Objects in the List of Cultural Heritage of the Russian Federation.

Its protected area is six hundred and fifty square kilometers. Borodino Military History Museum itself is a territory of one hundred and ten square meters. m. The main object for display is, in fact, the field itself, on which in August for six days the Russian troops fought for Moscow under the leadership of Kutuzov.

The museum in its modern form was opened in 1902. It was a very peculiar and unexpected decision. The head of the Borodino railway station decided to create the Museum of 1812 by placing it in one of the station rooms. Ten years later, the anniversary was celebrated - one hundred years from the moment of the battle. Celebrations were held on a large scale. Monuments were even erected in memory of the particularly distinguished soldiers and officers of the tsarist army.



Thirty-five monuments were also built and the Shevardinsky redoubt was restored . It was this fortification that was the first to take the blow. One of the flushes was reconstructed.

How to get there

You can arrive at the Borodino Field (museum reserve) by car along the Moscow-Minsk highway. From Mozhaysk you need to go west to the village of the same name, then turn left to the main monument.

Electric trains depart from Belorussky Train Station. And by bus, you can arrive at the Borodino Field (museum) from the Victory Park metro station by bus number 457.

Around the central exposition and next to the Spaso-Borodinsky Monastery, comfortable parking lots are provided, designed not only for cars, but also for sightseeing shuttles.

Infrastructure

The State Borodino Military-Historical Museum-Reserve opened its branches in the city of Mozhaisk. This is a local historical and local history center and the House of Artist S. Gerasimov.

Today, through the efforts of the administration, meetings are formed and developed, and funds are preserved. But the most important thing is that normal conditions are created for relics, including those that are exposed in the open.

Familiarity with this historical and cultural site must begin directly from the railway station of the same name . However, the main exhibition of the museum is located in the middle of the Borodino field. It was opened in the pavilion near the Raevsky battery. Called "Borodino - the battle of the giants", the exhibition stores only genuine trophies and artifacts associated with the famous battle. Here, for example, you can see the weapons and uniforms of soldiers of the Russian and French armies, as well as banners and standards, their documents and awards, maps.

The Borodino Field (museum reserve) exhibits a lot of personal belongings of the participants in the battle. The paintings created by veterans and contemporaries of the war of 1812 are also exhibited here.
