Ontario is a province in Canada. Area, Economy, Attractions

Ontario is not only a lake, but also a whole province in Canada. It is the most populous in this country. The province has many amazing sights: museums, parks, lakes, waterfalls and numerous islands. Tourists from all over the world arrive in Ontario to see unique objects and get acquainted with the national culture of the country.





History

Until the 17th century, the lands of the present province of Ontario were inhabited by Native American tribes. These were the Iroquois, Ottawa, Hurons, Algonquins and some other groups. In 1611, the first British visited the territory. A little later, a few years later, the first French settlers landed in the area near Lake Huron . For some time, the struggle for land ownership between the inhabitants of two European states lasted. After 1763, the British retained the right to rule.

A clear division into provinces occurred in the second half of the 19th century. Toronto becomes the capital of Ontario, industry is gradually developing in the region, the first power plants appear. And in the 20th century, the automotive industry became the main profitable industry: it was then that companies such as Ford Motor and General Motors appeared.



After World War II, a large number of people from European countries immigrate to Canada. The population of the country and the province is growing. Today, Ontario is a province populated by representatives of different nationalities and races.

Description

Ontario covers slightly more than a million square kilometers. The territory occupies the central part of the country and borders on some US states. In particular, with Ohio, Pennsylvania, New York and some others. Part of the borders lie along the Great Lakes, that is, they are natural. Ontario is the capital of the entire state - the city of Ottawa.





The elevation differences in the area are mostly small. The relief is mainly dominated by plains and lowlands. As for the mountains, the highest point is Ishfatina peak. It is located at an altitude of 693 meters above sea level.

The types of natural areas that can be seen in Ontario are the tundra, taiga and mixed forests. The climate of the north and south varies significantly. The southern part has a high rainfall, but the seasons are relatively warm. In the northern zones, the climate is characterized by short cool summers and long winters.

Ontario Economy

The province has a very developed industrial production. Ontario is even ahead of Quebec in this indicator. In addition to the aforementioned automotive industry, various types of equipment and plastics are produced in the region, a significant share goes for export to the United States. The mining industry is also developed: the subsoil is rich in salt, silver, granite and marble.

In Ontario, six nuclear power plants have been built to this day. One of the world's largest energy companies, Ontario Power Generation, operates here. It is also worth noting that in the province there are no problems with aviation and transport links. In addition to roads and railways, there are also waterways that run along rivers and canals.

Toronto

The largest, most developed, and most modern city in Ontario is Toronto. It is the business center of the whole country. A transport system is well developed here, and the construction of high-rise buildings is currently underway. The population of the city in 2016 amounted to 2.7 million people, if you do not take into account the inhabitants of nearby settlements. It is in this area that the business and finances of the entire state are concentrated. With the advent of a large number of jobs and an increase in the standard of living of the population, Toronto has become the largest city in the country.



From the 50s of the last century, the construction of the subway began, which today includes 4 lines and 69 stations. In Toronto, there is also the main airport of Canada.

City attractions such as the CN Tower (553-meter TV tower, considered to be the highest on the planet until 2007), the Masonic Temple CTV, the Signs restaurant, which is unusual in that only deaf waiters work in it, have gained worldwide fame.

Ottawa

The capital of Canada is also in Ontario. This is the city of Ottawa, inferior to Toronto in terms of population and area. It lies on the banks of the river of the same name, very close to the neighboring province of Quebec and its village of Gatineau. The city is located in a zone of mixed forests, has several cultural heritage sites on its territory. For example, the gateway station Black Rapids. There are also architectural monuments of the 19th century, interesting sculptural compositions, museums and concert halls.

Lakes

Ontario's attractions also include local lakes. There are quite a lot of them, there are about five hundred thousand.

It is worth starting the description with the reservoir of the same name. Lake Ontario (Canada) is included in the Great Lakes system, most of which are located in the United States. The mentioned reservoir is the smallest in area, and its depth is 244 meters. This is quite a lot, so Ontario is rarely covered with ice even in cold winters. Trout, pike, carp and some other fish species live in its waters. Beautiful and picturesque lakes of the region are also called Nipissing, Simko, Rice, Nipigon. Almost everyone has developed fishing. The names of the reservoirs have been preserved since the days when the Indians lived on the territory.

Museums

Ontario's most popular museum is the Royal Museum (ROM). Its expositions are devoted to history and culture, not only from Canada, but also from other states. In the museum you can see objects found in Africa, the Middle East and Europe. Among the exhibits are skeletons of dinosaurs, Egyptian sarcophagus, works of ancient and modern art. The building is located in Toronto, next to Queen's Park.

There, in Toronto, is the Art Gallery, where visitors get acquainted with the paintings and sculptures of Canadian authors. Visitors who want to learn more about the history of the country must visit the Museum of the Canadian War (in Ottawa). The building houses a large collection of military artifacts, some of which can even be picked up.

If you plan your trip to Ottawa in advance, you can get to the air show, which is annually held by the Canadian Museum of Aviation and Space. And in the museum building, visitors will see some military aircraft. The local museum of nature is also unique: in the early 2000s, its building was reconstructed and now has a glass dome on several sides with several floors.
