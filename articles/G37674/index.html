Battles in the Great

At the end of the fourteenth century, where today several cultural and historical monuments are located, fights were fought between Russian warriors and Tatars. In 1425, the internecine war began. Between the descendants of Dmitry Donskoy in those days, hostility arose for the Grand Duchy. Major battles were carried out by intractable relatives halfway between Rostov and Yaroslavl - where the village with an ambiguous name is located today. This event dates back to the 30s of the fifteenth century. And after a century and a half, a battle took place again on these lands, now between the Russian princes and the Polish gentry.

At the beginning of the 17th century, the militia Minin and Pozharsky stopped in the village of Veliky, Yaroslavl Region . They headed towards Moscow, again to fight against the impudent Poles who had seized the capital. Local residents treated the liberators very respectfully: they shared food, and at night they helped carry out a service for the protection of combatants. Many peasants joined the brave defenders of the Fatherland.

Veliky Selo Kremlin

This is the main attraction. The history of the Kremlin begins at the beginning of the 17th century, when the aforementioned church was laid. It is customary to associate an architectural monument in the Great with significant events near Poltava . As already mentioned, it was thanks to the victory in the battles that one of the close associates of Peter the Great became the owner of the lands near Yaroslavl.

The main object in the Kremlin, of course, is the Church of the Nativity of the Virgin. It is decorated with several domes with a variety of frescoes. Many architectural monuments have been preserved in the Yaroslavl region, but the one that is here stands out against the general background with solemnity and grandeur. It is not for nothing that the locality to which Peter the Great once drew attention, received such a memorable name.

In addition to the church, the architectural complex includes the Holy Gate, the Church of the Intercession of the Virgin, a bell tower and various outbuildings. There are also chapels and shopping malls. Each building is decorated with works of Yaroslavl architecture: pediments, spiers, arches.

In the thirties, the Kremlin was partially destroyed. Today, the area is decorated with flower beds, but serious restoration work is required. The bell tower was built in the middle of the XVIII century. Its height is 75 meters. This is a rather unusual structure with a square base. Behind the church is an old cemetery. Here there are burials that were made more than three centuries ago.





Potato Riot Museum

The name of this institution already indicates that a rather unusual exhibition is presented within its walls. Potato appeared in Russia in the first half of the 19th century. The peasants did not want to engage in the cultivation of an unusual, then still exotic plant. Later, as you know, they appreciated his merits. Potatoes entered firmly in Russian national cuisine.

Museum visitors have the opportunity to learn little-known facts from the history of tubers. In addition, an interactive program operates here, which is very popular today throughout the world. On the ground floor there is a small cafe, the menu of which presents a variety of dishes prepared, of course, from potatoes.

Museum of Local Lore

Great was once the largest village in the Yaroslavl region. At the beginning of the XX century, about four thousand people lived here. Today - less than two thousand. Nevertheless, the history of the village is very rich. It is not surprising that the local museum of local lore has several interesting exhibitions. One of them is devoted to linen business, which was developed in the 19th century. Around the same time, demand for leather goods increased. In the 19th century, not only linen business, but also leather production began to develop. The most famous merchants at that time were Morugins and Butikovs .

Several exhibitions tell visitors of the museum about the life of artisans in the village of Veliky. There is a hall where merchants are shown. The museum also has an exhibition dedicated to the Soviet era.

Other attractions

There are two large ponds in the village. One of them in the eighties received the status of a natural monument. There is an opinion that a blacksmith was once located on its shore. All the soot from the work of the craftsmen went into the pond . Hence the name - Black . The second reservoir is called White. Probably, unlike the first, he was at a distance from the forge. Here is the Bogolyubsky temple, as well as the Svetelka Museum, decorated in the style of an old merchant's hut.
