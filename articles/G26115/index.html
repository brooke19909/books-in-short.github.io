Design

We begin any characteristic of a smartphone with a description of its appearance. This is a tradition, since the buyer encounters the design of the device first of all immediately after picking up the phone. And, as already noted, the Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG (reviews will confirm this) is nothing special.

The front of the smartphone is completely covered with glass, except for the bottom panel. It is intentionally separated from the rest of the screen, and visually this trick is completely justified.





The back cover of the device is made like that of Lumia smartphones: it has a bright color and due to its shape completely covers the side edges of the phone. This solution is budgetary, but fully justifies itself due to the ability to change the lids and thereby individualize the appearance of the device to your taste. Moreover, the manufacturer promises 5 color variations on sale - black, white, red, yellow and blue phones.

Smartphone Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG has a classic layout of navigation elements. Three physical keys are presented immediately below the screen (above the decorative panel mentioned above). They are responsible for switching to the home panel, returning to the previous window, and also opening the ‚ÄúProperties‚Äù tab. In addition to them, on the side faces (and in fact - on the sides of the removable cover) there are sound and lock buttons.

Screen

Regarding the display, the situation arose two-digit. On the one hand, the technical specifications here are not the most enviable: the resolution is 800 x 480 pixels, the diagonal is 4 inches, the density is 233 pixels. The screen operates on the basis of TFT technology, which in itself cannot provide such color rendition as the IPS-matrix.

On the other hand, we see that the reviews on the Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG indicate quite tolerable quality of the image displayed on the display. This is also evident from the way its illumination changes insignificantly when the device is tilted.

Another plus of the screen installed on the model we are describing is the presence of a special layer of oleophobic coating. Thanks to him, firstly, the sliding speed of the finger on the surface of the display increases, and secondly, the degree of susceptibility of the screen to fingerprints decreases. Due to this, they can be seen much less than on phones without such a layer.





Separately, we note the ability to work with color reproduction and the degree of image quality on the screen due to the Splendid application. It is installed by default on all Asus devices, in it the user can change the color temperature, hue, saturation of the display.

operating system

According to tradition, again, ZenUI graphical shell is installed on all ZenFone devices (and the one described by us). It looks somewhat similar to the fifth generation of Android (at least, gradients and the visual design of windows prompt this idea). In fact, Android 4.4.4 was installed on the Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG smartphone. As to whether there is an update to the latest version of the assembly for the device, there is no information.

Performance

The platform on which the device is built does not have a high response frequency and, therefore, cannot provide high enough performance for working with ‚Äúbulky" applications. However, for most of those games that are presented with normal and minimal requirements (in terms of performance), the Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG smartphone (reviews confirm this) is suitable.

Speaking from a technical point of view, we are talking about the Intel Atom Z2520 processor, built on two cores with a clock frequency of 1.2 GHz. The RAM here has a capacity of only 1 GB, of which no more than 300 MB will be available in constant mode.





The amount of internal memory on the Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG is 8 GB. Reviews note the possibility of connecting a memory card, due to which it is possible to expand the volume up to 32 GB.

Battery

The device that we describe in this review has a 1200 mAh battery. Of course, this is not enough for a device with such functionality, therefore, even in the reading mode with the screen brightness set at 25-35 percent, the device will be able to work for no more than 8-9 hours. If we talk about games, this figure can easily be divided by 3. It is not surprising that many users complain in their reviews about the low level of autonomy of the smartphone.

There are several ways to deal with this. The first is to significantly reduce energy consumption, try to install any applications on your smartphone that control it, optimize background processes, and the like. The second is just to use portable charging stations (such as PowerBank) and wear them when you need to increase the duration of the device.

And so, given the small thickness of the case, it is not surprising that the developers chose such a small battery - an element with a larger capacity would simply not fit into the device.

Communication

The phone supports Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, USB; There is, of course, an adapter for receiving a GSM signal in networks of all formats, including a 3G connection (high-speed Internet). In addition, it should be noted the possibility of working with two SIM cards, which will make the mobile device more profitable to use. Sound

The technical description of the smartphone, posted on the official website, claims about a special technology to improve the sound quality of the device, which is called SonicMaster. Allegedly, by default it improves the purity of sound using hardware and software filters.

In practice, again, the situation is somewhat different. As noted, including the reviews describing the Asus ZenFone 4 A400CG phone, the phone sounds lousy before updating the firmware. After it, apparently, some new software audio processors are installed on the device, due to which the sound quality increases significantly. But then again, given the class of the smartphone, hoping for something supernatural is not worth it.

conclusions
