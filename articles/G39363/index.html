Tegucigalpa - the capital of Honduras

Honduras is a large state that is located in the center of Latin America. Honduras is washed by the Caribbean Sea, and in the southwest it borders on the Pacific Ocean. The country is located on a plateau, but most of the territory was occupied by mountains. On the coast of the sea, Honduras is strewn with plains. As in other Latin American countries, gold, silver, lead and copper are mined in large quantities here. Therefore, the price of jewelry in Honduras is low.





The capital of Honduras is Tegucigalpa. This city is considered the main one since 1880, it arose in the 16th century. Today, Tegucigalpa is not only the economic and political center of Honduras, but also the resort of this country. The name of the capital came from Tegushi's own name, and the second part of the word - the galpa - means belonging to a house. Initially, Tegucigalpa was a center for the extraction of gold and silver ores, this continues today. The capital of Honduras today is the largest commercial, economic and cultural center of the country. The peculiarity of this city is that this is one of the few capitals in which even now there is no railway. The capital of Honduras is the most developed city in this country, where most of the largest attractions are located. It is here that the City Council and Administration are located, as well as all the famous museums and libraries in the country.





The climate in the city is most attractive in comparison with other cities of the country of Honduras. The capital has a mild tropical climate, where the maximum temperature in the summer is 25 degrees, and in the winter - 19. The city has a well-developed light industry, leather goods, as well as the tobacco and construction industries. An airport of international importance is also located in Tegucigalpa, it is very important for Honduras, since import and export is carried out only by water and air, there is no railway here yet.

The capital of Honduras has an unusual architecture. There are a lot of stairs on the streets of Tegucigalpa, which are difficult to find in any other city in the world. The streets are lined with bright, colorful houses and buildings. Most of them consist of only one floor. In the center of the city is a small but very beautiful park of Morosana, next to it is a monument. Not far from it is the Cathedral of San Miguel, built in 1785. This cathedral contains the best world relics. The center also houses the old market, where the old church of Los Dolores is located. Already in the 50s of the last century, the Presidential Residence, the Parliament, and the building of the main city theater were erected on Rynok Square.





The capital of Honduras is also a tourist center, where people from all over the world constantly come. This is partly due to the low cost of silverware, which, in combination with ancient monuments and unusual buildings, attracts tourists. All architecture has been preserved since the time when Honduras was a colony. The city also has a special beauty of nature. Tours to Honduras today can be purchased at many travel companies.
