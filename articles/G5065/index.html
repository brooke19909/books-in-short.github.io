Who is Kiselev?

Kiselev Rock is named after the great Russian landscape painter, professor of the St. Petersburg Academy of Arts Alexander Alexandrovich Kiselev. This man was born in Sveaborg, which today is one of the administrative districts of Helsinki, in 1838. Alexander was a pupil of the Arakcheevsky administrative building, and since 1858 he became a student at St. Petersburg University. In the fall of 1861, student unrest began to occur, so the university was closed. So the future artist became a free-coming student of the Imperial Academy of Arts. Mr. Kiselev worked in the Russian capital, but very often traveled throughout the country. He did this in order to find new landscapes for his paintings, which in the best way reflected the nature of different seasons.

A little later, Alexander took up writing the Caucasian species, which gained incredible success. Where is it and how to get there?

Kiseleva Rock is located four kilometers northwest of Tuapse itself, between the mouth of the Aga River and Cape Kadosh. This is one of the most beautiful places on the Black Sea coast. The height of the object reaches 46 meters.

Kiseleva Rock (locals know how to get to it, as well as employees of travel agencies) - the place is gorgeous. So, the first way to travel: by minibus or regular bus from Tuapse to the sign of the same name. Further the path will have to be done on foot. The second method is a little more difficult than the first, but more interesting. It consists of walking from Agoy along the coastline. The whole journey will take no more than an hour. True, sometimes you will have to jump over rocks and wade bends if the water is high. And the third way: on a motor boat, a steamboat or a pleasure boat from Agoy, Nebug or Tuapse. The cost of such a trip includes diving, swimming and a boat trip.

Rock of tears





Once upon a time, the Kiseleva (Tuapse) rock was called the rock of tears. And all because of one ancient Adyghe legend. Once upon a time in the surrounding areas lived a young and beautiful girl, Guash. She fell in love with a dzhigita, a native of the aul, which was located next to her house. He bore the name Dysheek. The couple decided to bond their love with a marriage. But according to tradition, the groom had to abduct his bride. At the peak of the cliff near the sea, that same secret place was assigned. The signal to start the ceremony was to be a fire lit by Guash. On the night of the abduction, the girl decided to test her beloved one last time. She put a flaming lamp on a log and lowered it into the water. When the guy arrived and saw a floating light, he immediately understood the intentions of his future wife. On a horse, he went down to the sea and swam for a log.
