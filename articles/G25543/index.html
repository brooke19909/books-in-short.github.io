Step-by-step instructions for performing a bas-relief on a wall

Abundance in the market for wallpaper and other wall coverings spoiled consumers. They increasingly want exclusivity and uniqueness to create their own unique indoor design. And therefore, original needlework for the home is increasingly being used, the variety of ideas now simply rolls over.

Designers today propose to personally make a bas-relief of gypsum on the walls of their homes, limiting themselves to the usual whitewashing.

  1. Having chosen a drawing (plot) of a bas-relief, the master can fashion it from plasticine on a flat horizontal surface. It can be flying pigeons, swimming swans, flowers. You can use a particularly liked figurine. In the latter case, the thing must be "drowned" in clay or clay so that the outside was exactly that part of the little thing that would subsequently be a protruding part of the bas-relief.
  2. The template, and this is exactly what it is called, with which the master will make an impression, is lubricated with vegetable oil or glycerin and coated with silicone sealant.
  3. After drying, the mold is removed from the template.
  4. Diluted gypsum or alabaster is poured into the mold and left until hardened.
  5. The finished detail of the bas-relief is glued to the wall. If the part is voluminous and heavy, it is appropriate to screw it in some places for insurance.
