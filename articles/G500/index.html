Regensburg Attractions: Overview, Description, History and Reviews

One of the most picturesque cities in Germany is always admired by tourists. The oldest settlement fascinates from the first minute and falls in love with itself forever. The guests of Regensburg enthusiastically note its amazing atmosphere, the hospitality of local residents and architectural monuments, which the capital of the Upper Palatinate district is famous for the whole world.

A city with a long history

The age of the German city exceeds two millennia, and its history goes far back centuries. Founded even before our era, Regensburg, whose sights are becoming the subject of admiration for tourists, remains always young and energetic. Initially, the territory was inhabited by the Celts, who were squeezed by the Romans, who built a defensive camp here.





The 5th century was marked by the fact that the young city turned into the capital of the Bavarian dukes, and later it acquired the status of the largest shopping center in the region.

UNESCO-protected historic center

No one will argue with the assertion that the historic center with ancient buildings, perfectly preserved near the Danube, are the main city attractions. Regensburg, painstakingly collecting its heritage for several centuries, reached the descendants in their original form, and he was not even touched by the bombing during World War II.

Now the medieval center, included in the UNESCO World Heritage List, is considered the largest in Germany.

The color of the Bavarian pearl

Tourists often compare the colorful corner of Bavaria with Italian cities for a similar style in architecture. Regensburg, whose sights will pleasantly surprise its guests, is admirable for its coziness and beauty. Medieval buildings that harmoniously fit into the modern look of the city are worthy of a detailed description.

It is curious that more recently, in the 60s of the last century, the authorities decided to demolish the historical center, and build modern buildings on the site of ancient monuments. However, common sense triumphed, which is unspeakably happy for the guests of the city, which has many mysteries. Now all the masterpieces are being restored, and the gaze of tourists reveals architectural creations in stone, protected by the state.

Small Regensburg (Germany), the sights of which made its appearance incredibly attractive in the eyes of vacationers, is visited annually by millions of foreigners who admire medieval buildings.

A stone bridge

The unique city where every street breathes history is imbued with the spirit of the times. Here is an authentic example of world bridge building - a stone structure that appeared during the Second Crusade. For eleven years, a crossing was built for the knights across the Danube, and the famous creation of engineering was for a long time an example of Gothic architecture.





A real wonder of the world, as the locals call Kamenny Bridge, was several times destroyed, and it was restored from scratch. It offers amazing views of the historic center, and in the evening all romantically inclined tourists who admire reflections in dark water adore walking.

City Hall

The city of Regensburg, perfectly preserved from the time of the German Middle Ages, the sights of which must be visited by every guest, appears as a kind of guardian of history. One of the oldest architectural ensembles is located on the Town Hall Square. For tourists, of great interest is the building, built in the XIII century and until 1860 was considered the highest.

The Old Town Hall, consisting of four wings and a tower, surprises with an unusual entrance portal decorated with a beautiful decor - a sculpture of two stone guards.





Inside, there is a lobby with light floors and dark columns, and you can get into it through the Gothic stairs. The Kaiser’s throne had previously stood in the luxurious imperial hall, and court decisions were announced from the loggia overlooking the square, which were enforced here. The fact is that there was a prison under the town hall, and current visitors are horrified by the instruments of torture, put on public display. Tourists admit that from what they see their blood runs cold.

Gothic cathedral

All visitors to the city are encouraged to turn their attention to religious sights built several centuries ago. Regensburg is rightfully proud of the Gothic cathedral built in honor of St. Peter. The beautiful building crowned by the bell tower is no less beautiful inside: old frescoes, colorful stained glass windows, the preserved shrines and relics of John Chrysostom will amaze every visitor,

Hallhall Hall of Fame

You can’t visit a charming city and not see unique sights reminiscent of its architecture in ancient Greek temples. Regensburg glorifies the great sons and daughters of the nation: it makes sculptural faces of the brightest representatives of Germany.

The idea of ​​an unusual building was invented by Crown Prince Ludwig in the 19th century, and it was he who decided to name it in honor of the shelter of the souls of fallen soldiers - Valhalla.





In 1842, a large-scale construction saw the light, and not a single guest of the city passes by amazing sights. Regensburg is incredibly proud of the huge collection of busts and honorary plaques that cause a natural interest among all visitors who have never seen anything like it.

Sausage cuisine

The wonderful city, famous for its masterpieces of architectural art of various eras, is famous for its delicious cuisine, which is appreciated by the guests of Bavaria. An interesting snack bar located near the Stone Bridge has existed for several centuries. It is considered a real monument to the culinary heritage of Regensburg, because fragrant pork sausages served with sauerkraut and special mustard have been made here for five hundred years.

Locals are convinced that their institution is the ancestor of all the world's bistros, and not a single traveler leaves until he tastes the most delicious dish. Many admit their love for the institution, which is not so easy to get into because of the huge lines of tourists.





Regensburg's world-famous sights with descriptions can be seen in many advertising leaflets of tour operators offering to enjoy a spectacular sight with their own eyes. Those who have already visited a cozy city once say that this is the best place to relax with a special atmosphere that makes the trip unforgettable.

Many cultural events and entertainment for every taste will appeal to everyone who dreams of an exciting trip to Bavaria. It is not for nothing that they say that this small town, in which the inhabitants sacredly honor their traditions, can be studied endlessly.
