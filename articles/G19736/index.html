"Minsk-Arena": photo, address and diagram. How to get to the Minsk-Arena in Minsk?

Minsk-Arena (see photo below) is one of the most high-tech and largest sports and cultural complexes in Europe. In addition, the construction is an outstanding example of modern architectural solutions.





The composition of the multifunctional complex

The main purpose of a modern multifunctional building is to conduct cultural as well as sports events of an international scale. Minsk-Arena includes:

- The construction of the Arena with 15,000 seats for cultural and sports events;

- multi-level parking;

- a velodrome for 2,000 seats and a speed skating stadium for 3,000 seats.

History of creation

The multifunctional complex Minsk-Arena began to be built in the spring of 2006 after the signing of the corresponding decree by the President of the Republic of Belarus A. Lukashenko. About two thousand people worked at the facility daily. They implemented the project of the famous architect Valery Kutsko, an employee of the workshop of the Belgosproject Institute. The building was officially opened four years later, on January 30, 2010. On the ice of the complex, as part of its opening, a match was held for all the stars in the Continental Hockey League.

Currently, the multifunctional complex Minsk-Arena occupies a leading position, being considered the most capacious venue and the leading hockey arena of the European continent. The building makes a huge impression thanks to the high-tech architectural solution. It surpassed the beauty of the external and internal design, as well as the range of services provided, all the sports arenas that were built earlier in the country. Moreover, the Minsk Arena can rightfully be considered a landmark not only in Belarus, but also in Europe.





Description of the ice structure

The Minsk-Arena complex is unique in its multifunctionality, equipment, equipment and technical complexity. There are no analogues to it in the Republic of Belarus today.

During the development of the project, a roof was provided in the building, which was made using French technology. These works were carried out in Belarus for the first time. The roof is cable-stayed. Black cables are pulled around its perimeter. They are called guys. They are the main support for the roof. The roof is made of lightweight material, which, despite this, has the ability to withstand up to one hundred and twenty tons of load. Almost every square centimeter of the roof has special sensors. With their help, constant monitoring of cable-stayed tension is carried out. This information is tracked by both Belarusian and French experts.

Ice Arena

A certain number of stands located in the lower tier of the main structure of the complex have metal steps. Call them blitters. By pressing a single button, the stands are able to slide under the concrete steps along with the seats. The need for this arises when conducting pop and sports shows, as well as concerts. In the free space, a dance floor is arranged and a collapsible stage is set.





Commentators booths are located on the upper tiers, and a little lower designed glazed balconies for VIP-clients for seven hundred seats. At podium A there is one presidential as well as two government lodges. Each of them is equipped with a relaxation room and a bathroom. Banquet service is available in these lodges.

A sixty by thirty meter hockey box is located in the very center of the room. Its dimensions comply with European standards. On the main site of the complex you can play hockey. Minsk-Arena is also designed for other competitions and forums. Often concert shows are organized here.

The Minsk-Arena, the layout of which is very convenient for guests, has four stands. These are A, B, C and D. Spectators can enter the hall through four entrances. If you need to go from one tribune to another, you can use the spacious corridors.

The heaviest octagonal media cube in Europe hangs under the dome of the Ice Arena. Its weight is eighteen tons.

The world-class venue is equipped with a climate control system. Thanks to her, the fans do not feel the cold, even being on the lowest tiers of the stands.

The multifunctional complex Minsk-Arena includes an underground passage. This building connects the central arena with the speed skating stadium. This is the first indoor ice rink in Belarus with a treadmill that is four hundred meters long and nine and a half wide.





At the same stadium are two hockey training grounds. One of them was built according to European standards and has dimensions of 60 by 30 meters. The second corresponds to Canadian sites (60 by 28 meters). The speed skating stadium has two tracks for sports such as curling.

Indoor equipment controls the temperature of ice in various areas. These sectors are designed for figure skating, hockey, curling, as well as speed skating competitions.

Training camps are held on the Minsk-Arena ice tracks. They are attended by skaters of Estonia and Russia, Ukraine and the Netherlands. It was here that the juniors officially trained before the World Figure Skating Championships.

Velodrome

Similar projects were developed in many countries by German experts. However, one of the best cycle tracks is located in the Minsk-Arena complex. In Minsk, a velodrome provides an opportunity to invite those who are fond of other sports for classes. So, there are competitions and training in table tennis and badminton, basketball and futsal, as well as volleyball.

Infrastructure

The Minsk-Arena complex is not only large-scale, but also very comfortable. Here for athletes designed gyms and gyms, comfortable locker rooms, rooms where doping control is carried out. There is also a medical rehabilitation center.





Guests and spectators can visit restaurants and cafes, relax in relaxation areas equipped with TVs and sofas. The complex has cosmetology and massage rooms, a laboratory for electrophysical measurements. Younger guests can have a great time in the children's playroom.

Visitors to the grand building on group excursions held here can learn about the history of the unique project and its features, visit the Walk of Fame, hear information about planned events. At their service - souvenir stalls.

The unique complex offers its visitors mountain air rooms that have no analogues in Belarus. Anyone can use such services. Soon it is planned to create artificial salt caves here.

Guests of the complex should be aware that the Minsk Arena is a non-smoking area.

Entrance halls are equipped with fifty-four wardrobes. All rooms are designed in such a way that fifteen thousand spectators leave the Minsk Arena in just forty minutes.





Tickets for sports or cultural events are purchased at the box office located both in the complex and in specialized kiosks of the city.

Security

The Minsk-Arena complex is equipped with an ultramodern system. Its purpose is to ensure the safety of visitors. This system is designed in accordance with international recommendations and standards.

At the entrance to the complex installed electronic control devices. Spectators apply their tickets to a special scanner, which records the place on the platform and the arrival time of the visitor. Then the guests go through the metal detector frames installed near the entrance.

How to get there

The address Minsk-Arena is 111 Pobediteley Avenue. The multifunctional complex is located on one of the main city highways of the Belarusian capital.

If you purchased a ticket to a sports competition or concert show, which will be held in the Minsk-Arena complex, how to get to it? From the city center this can be done by buses 1 and 44. Trolleybus routes 10 and 28 also pass here. Shuttle taxis can deliver to the Minsk-Arena stop.

If you decide to come by private car, then several parking lots are equipped at your service around the complex. Behind the velodrome, it has many levels. It can accommodate at the same time one thousand eighty cars. Opposite the rostrum In the main arena there is a flat parking for three hundred parking spaces.
