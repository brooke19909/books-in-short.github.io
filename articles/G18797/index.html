Crimea, Livadia: the history of the village

The area on which Livadia is located has been inhabited by people for a long time. In the eighteenth century, on the site of today's Livadia was a small Greek settlement Ai-Yang. Its inhabitants in 1778, by order of the government, moved to the Azov province. When Crimea was annexed to Russia, part of the land of the South Bank was donated to the Greeks of the Balaklava battalion (the Crimean border guard of those years). Almost thirty years, Livadia was owned by Count Potocki. At the same time, by 1859, it was already inhabited and landscaped, becoming a typical southern estate of those years. By this moment, there were thirty yards in which 140 people lived.





A year later, Livadia was acquired by a specific department for the king‚Äôs family. Under the leadership of Hippolytus Monighetti, a court architect, the Potocki Palace was built. In the period 1941-1944. The village was occupied by German troops.
