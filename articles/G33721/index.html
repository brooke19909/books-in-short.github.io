Massandra Palace in Yalta, Crimea: description, history, how to get

Massandra Palace in Yalta is one of the most wonderful sightseeing places of Crimea. The palace impresses with its architectural unusualness, graceful grace and royal sophistication. The story, hidden behind the majestic walls, attracts the attention of many tourists around the world, intriguing with its unusual and entertaining.





Let us virtually visit this pearl of Russian history with you and try to understand why it is so attractive and beautiful.

What is remarkable about the museum?

The Massandra Palace in Yalta is a compact and cozy castle built in the late nineteenth century. It was once the magnificent residence of emperors. Then it was provided for use by ordinary people. A little later, it was considered the state cottage of prominent Soviet politicians.

Possessing such a rich and controversial history, the Massandra Palace in Yalta managed to retain its originality and exoticism, originality and uniqueness, which allows it to still have a huge number of visitors and sightseers.

Where is the castle located?

The charming imperial residence is located in the shade of a dense forest, among a beautiful park, not far from the bottomless expanses of the Black Sea.

Here is a specific address: Massandra Palace (Yalta), Crimea Peninsula, urban-type settlement Massandra, 2 Naberezhnaya Street.

Although the building is territorially assigned to the village, it is practically fenced off from the village by a beautiful dense forest.

What is the history of the Massandra Palace (Yalta) and how is it remarkable?

Royal history of the building

The erection of the Massandra Palace in Yalta took place in several stages.

The first stage of construction ended in 1882, when, on the orders of the landowner Prince Vorontsov, a pretty beautiful house was built, made in the strict style of a knight's castle. The project was developed by the French architect Etienne Bouchard.

A few years after the death of the owner, the house was purchased for the current emperor Alexander Alexandrovich and rebuilt according to the drawings of the German architect Maximilian Egorovich Mesmacher. The second phase of construction began in 1892 and lasted about ten years.

According to the new project, the third floor was added, and the building itself was richly decorated and decorated, turning into a magnificent tower from a fairy tale.





Since then, the palace became the residence of the Romanov emperors. True, Nicholas II rarely stayed in it and almost never slept.

Soviet history of construction

After the revolution, the building was used as a hospital for tuberculosis patients for twelve years. Unfortunately, during this period the palace underwent many negative changes: some decors and sculptures were destroyed.

After World War II, the building served the Soviet citizens for several years as an institute of winemaking. But soon it attracted the attention of the Leader of the peoples and was slightly refitted in accordance with the wishes of Joseph Vissarionovich. For several decades, the Massandra Palace was the state suburban cottage of the first secretaries of the CPSU Central Committee.

The modern history of the castle

In 1992, the imperial residence was awarded the title of State Ukrainian Museum.

And since 2014, the palace has been a Russian museum under the authority of the President of the Russian Federation.
