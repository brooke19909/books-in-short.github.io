Section 4. Walk through the museums





Not everyone knows that, in addition to historical, archaeological and architectural museums, such as Buckingham Palace or the Tower, in London there are many museums with rather curious and unusual exhibits.

We give examples of some of them.

  1. Horniman Museum. This institution is known for the presence of two exhibits - the so-called water. One appeared about 100 years ago, it was made of fish bones and wood. And the second was found off the Japanese coast in the eighteenth century. It was collected from body parts of fish and monkeys.
  2. During the Great Patriotic War, an underground command center was built in Great Britain, where politicians and military leaders continued to manage the army and the country in safety. Three times this center became a refuge for Winston Churchill. The most interesting exhibit in this now museum is his night pot, which stood under the bed during Churchill's days in the aforementioned command center.
  3. The Museum of Natural History also has an exhibit of an elephant man, Joseph Merrick, who lived in the Victorian era in England. Such a nickname was given to him for complex and incurable deformations on his face.

Section 5. The most beautiful cemetery in the world Highgate, London





Great Britain ... The most interesting place, or rather, one of them, is located in North London. Its area is thirty-seven acres. In fact, strangely enough, this sounds to date, this cemetery is considered a historical vacation spot for local residents.

In general, Highgate looks like a classic English garden. Hedgehogs, hares, rabbits, foxes live in large numbers here, and a huge number of trees and bushes grow.

The cemetery is divided into eastern and western parts. You can visit the East only by signing up for the appropriate excursion, while the West is open for general inspection.

Many famous people are buried in Highgate Cemetery: George Eliot, Karl Marx, Malcolm McLaren.

Section 6. Which airport can be considered the worst in Europe?





In northern Scotland is Barra Airport. Why is he so significant? The thing is that this category of air gates can be assigned because of their runways, which are located directly on the beach of the Bay of Tri-Mor. The flight schedule here is, given the ebbs and flows. Night flights and during high tides are not served. But in emergency cases at night, reflective tapes are laid out along the strip and the strip is illuminated by the headlights of cars. And the rest, Barra Airport is no different from the rest: a control room, baggage loading service, arrival and departure terminals - no differences from an ordinary institution of such a plan.

Section 7. Interesting about the UK: what do you think of an overnight stay in prison?

In Oxford, there is a hotel, which was equipped in a former place intended exclusively for the maintenance of prisoners. It is called Malmaison Oxford Castle. Without breaking the law, you can visit the former prison premises, where low ceilings and thick walls are still preserved. Although the rooms themselves are cozy, comfortable and meet the requirements of all, even the most moody travelers.

Once gray dreary corridors are now equipped with furniture by famous designers. And where the former secretariat was located, today there is a bar and restaurant.

The layout of the building is not broken, everything was saved: metal doors, and brick walls, and wooden floors, and prison bars. But now such a situation does not oppress, on the contrary, these elements have become perhaps the key to the reconstruction of a peculiar atmosphere.

And from the small windows that once were for prisoners serving their sentences here the only string of connection with the world, you can see a barbed wire fence.

Section 8. The Road to Nowhere

In Northern Ireland, there is a phenomenon unique in nature - hexagonal basalt columns, which are interconnected, form an incredible landscape resembling a pavement. Only now it was created not by man, but by nature.

This phenomenon is called the Path of the Giants. And it appeared as a result of the activity of the volcano, and the protrusions, in turn, formed when the lava flow was already cooling. In 1986, UNESCO added this unique corner of nature to the World Heritage List.

Section 9. Are you afraid of heights?

Section 10. Unusual attraction

Great Britain ... Interesting facts for children, as well as for adults, are found here at every step. For example, you should definitely take note of the information that recently a new attraction has opened here - the largest trampoline in the world.

This attraction is located in one of the caves in North Wales. The structure itself looks like this: three trampolines of unusually huge sizes are stretched at different levels of the cave. Section 11. The time machine, or Can we go back to the past?





It is simply impossible to list interesting facts about the UK and not to mention this place. Today, East Stratton is an example of classic English landscapes. For three hundred years, from the seventeenth to the nineteenth century, this village was rebuilt and continued its development thanks to three families: Rayotsley, Baring and Russell.

The first bricks houses were once built in this village, and the roof was made thatched. And now this is the calling card of East Stratton. However, one cannot fail to note that today behind a roof with such a coating, especially careful care is needed, which not everyone can afford, and therefore only wealthy people can afford to have such roofs.

Official interesting facts about the UK say that the highlight of East Stratton is that the village still retains the spirit of the Victorian era. The descendants of the creators of this beautiful village left behind a huge park with incredible groves, gardens and sculptures in the Baroque style.
