Attempts in Russia

In Russia, they also tried to invent the very first car, so in 1752 Mikhail Lomonosov was introduced the prototype of the first car. He was engaged in the usual peasant Leonty Shamshurenkov.

The inventor brought to St. Petersburg a four-wheeled independent carriage, which had a pedal drive. He proved that his transport can move at a speed of 15 km / h. Leonty showed Lomonosov the first prototype of a verstometer, which showed the distance traveled by a car.

Using electricity

A little later, the very first cars on electricity began to appear. The first in this business was the Hungarian Yedlik Agnosh, who in 1828 introduced the world to an electric motor. To show his work, the engineer had to create a small machine as a prototype.

Fuel use

It is not surprising that inventors around the world tried to find the ideal transport option that would not require large reserves of coal or rails. So engineers thought of an internal combustion engine. The problem arose only with the use of suitable fuel, which would replace the gas mixture.

A lot of inventors experimented with different fuels and using technologies, but the very first gasoline-powered car in the world was introduced by Karl Benz. So the Benz Patent-Motorwagen model became known. The prototype was so successful that the engineer began producing cars in 1886.

It is now difficult to say whether Benz was an inspiration for someone, but already in 1889 Daimler and Maybach developed a completely new invention, which no longer looked like a horse-drawn cart. Around the same time, engineers worked on the world's first Daimler Petroleum Reitwagen motorcycle.

Production launch

Before the creation of the very first brand of car there was not much left. When the engineers finally decided on the best transportation option, they threw all their forces into mass production.

The first in this area was again Karl Benz in 1888. At the same time, Rudolf Egg was involved in the production of tricycles. Mass production was launched in the USA and France.

The first on the path of the auto industry were the French. They founded the company Panar and Levassor in 1889, which was engaged in the production of cars. Two years later, the world heard about the Peugeot.

The beginning of the 20th century for Europe turned into the active development of the automotive industry. But until 1903, France was the leader. In the USA, there were heroes. In 1893, the Duryea Motor Wagon Company appeared. Behind them, the Olds Motor Vehicle Company became active. By 1902, Cadillac, Winton and Ford were becoming popular.

Despite the fact that the world's automobile production grew exponentially, in fact, everything came to the conclusion that the car was luxury goods and a novelty of fashion. While she could not yet become a useful invention. This was due to the fact that the cost of cars was extremely high, and breakdowns were very common. And the fuel was not so easy to get.

On the way to modernity

Cars have come a long way to becoming what we see them today. It was necessary to conduct a series of experiments, after plunging into the vintage and survive the pre-war era.

The World War II had a great influence on the auto industry. The world saw a pontoon type body, which lost protruding wings, huge headlights and steps. The very first car in the world to be produced in large series was the Soviet GAZ-M-20 Pobeda.

After that, engineers stopped working on fanciful forms and special needs. They delved into the development of more powerful engines and increased speeds.
