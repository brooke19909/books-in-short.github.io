Lutsk: attractions, history, photos

Lutsk is located on the bank of the Stryi River in the Volyn Region . Sights of the city are included in the list of the most beautiful places in Ukraine. Ancient castles, amazing landscapes, unusual parks and churches are an incomplete list of what a traveler expects in the capital of the Volyn region. This publication contains the most interesting information about Lutsk (sights of the city and its environs, history and photos).

From the history of the city

The first references in written sources about Lucescu (as Lutsk was called in ancient times) date back to the times of Kievan Rus. Historians date the founding of the city as 1000. Throughout the entire existence of Lutsk, his fate was determined by different countries and peoples. In the XIV century, the city was considered the capital of the Galicia-Volyn principality, then it became the citizenship of Lithuania, and a little later - the Commonwealth. During the revolution of 1917, a blue-yellow Ukrainian flag was first hoisted over Lutsk . A few years later (under the Riga Treaty) the city came under Polish rule. In 1939, this territory became part of the USSR. All this could not but affect the architecture of the city. Modern Lutsk, whose attractions will be described in this article, is an incredibly beautiful ensemble of different architectural styles and amazing structures.





Lubart Castle

This is a unique building of the late XIII - XIV century, the main attraction of the city. Together with other monuments, the castle is part of the Old Lutsk Nature Reserve. Today this outstanding building is the first and main item on the list of travelers visiting the sights of Lutsk and the region.

Everyone who falls into the castle of Lubart has a unique opportunity to plunge into the atmosphere of the Middle Ages. Here you can visit the museum of bells (one of the most famous in the world), see the towers, various buildings, arches of the ancient gates, which were equipped with drawbridges.
