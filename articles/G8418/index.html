Yuri Dolgoruky: domestic and foreign policy during the period of independent rule

Prince Yuri Vladimirovich Dolgoruky is considered one of the greatest people in the history of Russia. The features of his historical portrait were determined by the desire to conquer as many lands as possible.



A brief biography of the prince

The year of birth of the prince is still unknown, historians do not leave debates about this. Some chroniclers claim that this happened in 1090, others in 1095 or 1097. But one thing is certain: his father was the great Kiev ruler V. Monomakh. Almost nothing is known about the prince‚Äôs mother.

During the life of his father, Yuri ruled the Vladimir-Suzdal principality. When the father died, the reins of Kievan Rus completely passed into the hands of the heir. From this time begins the sole policy of Yuri Dolgoruky.

During his life, he built many cities, including Zvenigorod, Yuryev-Podolsky, Moscow and others. The hero of our article was a very pious man, he wanted to achieve the complete independence of the city of Vladimir from the Kiev Metropolis, but the Church of Constantinople did not allow this.



Prince's Activities

Yuri Dolgoruky, whose domestic and foreign policy was peculiar, ruled alone, without gathering the veche (national assemblies) and boyar advisers. For this he paid cruelly. Due to the violation of centuries-old traditions, many boyars were opposed to him. There is a version according to which the death of the prince is not accidental, but a consequence of the conspiracy of his opponents.

Yuri Dolgoruky, whose domestic and foreign policy was aimed at subordinating the specific principalities formed during the collapse of Kievan Rus, made progress, but also made mistakes. An important factor in foreign policy was the preservation of personal borders and the protection of the state from frequent raids of the Volga Bulgars.



The results of the policy Dolgoruky

Prince Yuri Dolgoruky, whose domestic and foreign policy was competent, but not fully thought out, achieved such successes:

1. The Vladimir-Suzdal principality under the rule of Dolgoruky was well fortified.

2. During his leadership, a number of cities were formed and many temples were built. Some of the temples operate to this day.

3. Yuri initiated many civil wars. In the course of his policy, he could not reach agreement and peace in the principality, for which he cruelly paid for his death at the hands of poisoners.
