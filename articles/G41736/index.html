Few ski enthusiasts know where Livigno (Italy) is located. The map shows that the resort is so close to Switzerland that it is difficult to determine at a glance which state it belongs to. However, Livigno is known not only among avid skiers or snowboarders. Shopaholics around the world also know this place, because by a lucky chance, the villages of this ski area also enjoy the right of duty-free trade. So here, among other things, you can perfectly buy, especially alcohol and ski equipment. What other pleasures does the resort offer? How to get there? Which track to choose? You will learn the answers to all these questions from this article.



Resort History

The ski area itself is quite young, but the villages of which it consists are old. San Antonio, Santa Maria and San Rocco have a lot to see - 16th-century churches with frescoes, patriarchal houses and cobbled streets. As the name implies, Livigno (Italy), there were frequent avalanches. Despising the danger to life, the area was mastered by smugglers. Along with the illegal transportation of goods, they developed distillery cattle breeding. And since the lords had a hand in the development of highland lands, they looked through their fingers at the smuggling pranks of their subjects. And so it happened that even during the reign of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, duty-free trade flourished here. And from the 70s of the last century, ski infrastructure began to develop. Three villages as a result merged into one resort. The ski area continues to ramp up. Every year new ski lifts, hotels and restaurants open, new slopes appear.



How to get to the resort

Remoteness from big cities and international highways is the only minus of the ski area. The resort is 35 kilometers from Bormio and 220 kilometers from Milan Airport. From the capital of Lombardy you have to go by train to Tirano, and from there by bus to Livigno. You can drive up to the Italian resort from Switzerland. Therefore, many consider the starting point of Zurich Airport. Especially easy is the way by your own or rented car - almost all the time by autobahn. But the Forkola di Livigno pass (2315 m) is open only in the summer; at other times of the year, the resort from Switzerland can only be reached through the Mount La Chera tunnel. Only one road - through the Foscagno Pass pass - offers Italy. The Livigno ski resort is located just a few kilometers from the Swiss border, not far from the Zernets resort.



Transport communication inside the ski area

Free buses (green and blue routes) run through the three villages that make up the ski area. They connect Livigno with Bagni Vecchio and Nuovi, Val di Dentro and Pontresina. These beads go with enviable regularity from 7.30 in the morning until eight in the evening. In addition, owners of a six-day ski pass can take the special bus Livigno (Italy) - Engadin (Switzerland) and test their strength on the northern slopes of the Alps. The same ticket gives you the opportunity to ride on the tracks in San Colombano, Santa Caterina and Bormio. But in Livigno itself there are more than a hundred kilometers of pistes. They are divided into three regions: Carozello, Motolino and Val Federia (this last one is on the other side of the ridge).

Trails and lifts

The ski area is divided into two parts by the foot of the valley - Carosello and Motolino. Their height is approximately equal - about 2800 meters. The slopes of medium difficulty prevail on both slopes, but on Carozello there are more opportunities for beginner skiers. But the aces can show the class behind the crest of the ridge in Val Federia and go down to Trepalla. In Motolino, the ski lifts bring the spa guests to the top of Monte della Neve, from where the red and black slopes start. Carosello also has no shortage of difficult slopes. As for freeriding, because of the avalanche danger, this type of entertainment without a guide is prohibited. Violators face a fine of up to 500 –Ñ.
