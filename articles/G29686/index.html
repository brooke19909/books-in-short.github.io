Knitting net: diagram and description

Knitting patterns for knitting needles will help in the manufacture of beautiful handmade products. These models occupy leading positions in modern fashion for many decades, and today they are especially relevant. Light T-shirts, sweaters, tunics and other original things will be an adornment of a summer wardrobe for both women and men. A fragment of the net in the form of a part of clothing is suitable for a stylish model, regardless of the season.

Simple mesh

This is a beautiful knitted mesh pattern, the scheme of which is easy to use even for beginners, will decorate any product. To perform this knitting, you do not need to count the rows and loops, it is enough just to understand the principle of work. The most attractive pattern will look from cotton or linen yarn, but wool and half-wool are also suitable (if this is a part of demi-season clothing). The step-by-step instruction looks like this:

  • The rapport is not limited to a certain number of loops and rows, it is preferable that the number of loops be even.
  • In the first row, remove the edge loop, knit all the loops with the front ones and end with the final wrong one (so that the fabric does not stretch, you need to knit the first and last loop as described above).
  • The second row is completely knit with purl stitches.
  • The third row begins with two loops knitted together front, and one yarn. So continue until the end of the row.
  • The fourth row, like all even rows, knit with purl loops.
  • Repeat pattern from the third row.

Wrong mesh

The difference between this type of knitting mesh, the scheme with the description of which is similar to the previous version, is that in this case the pattern will be viewed on the wrong side. A step-by-step instruction on the job description will make it easy to reproduce the pattern:

  1. Rapport is also not limited to the number of loops and rows.
  2. The first row must be knitted with facial loops.
  3. The second row begins with two sewing together with the wrong loops, then the yarn and the pattern is repeated again.
  4. The third row, like all odd ones, is knit with facial loops.

Torn mesh

The grid with knitting needles, a diagram of which will be presented below, is distinguished by tenderness and lightness. It can be used as a piece of clothing or a summer model. It should be remembered that this pattern is almost transparent, so you can use a cover or lining. The work is performed as follows:

  1. The rapport consists of loops multiple of six plus two hem.
  2. The first row - one front, double crochet, knit two loops with a slope to the left of one front, one front, double crochet and two loops, knitted with a slope to the right of one front.
  3. The second row - one double crochet, split three stitches into two and one (two stretch through one), double crochet, knit two stitches into one with a left slope, double crochet and front stitch.
  4. The third row - one front, double crochet, two loops with an inclination to the left, front, two loops with an inclination to the right, double crochet.
  5. The fourth row - one loop, double crochet, three loops, two of which are stretched into one, double crochet, two facial.
  6. The fifth row - one front, two front to the right, double crochet, front, double crochet, two loops to the left.
  7. Sixth row - two loops in one to the left side, double crochet, one front, double crochet, two in one to the right side.
  8. Seventh row - one front, two loops in one to the right side, double crochet, one front, double crochet, two in one to the left side.
  9. Eighth row - three loops in one, double crochet, three facial, double crochet.

Tight mesh

The grid pattern with knitting needles, the scheme of which is presented below, will be appropriate for warmer things (although summer ones with cotton fabrics will look no less attractive). It can be a beautiful dress, sweater, shawl, cape, etc. Knitting is performed as follows:

  1. The rapport consists of ten loops, fits from the first to the tenth row, then repeats from the third.
  2. The first row is one wrong side loop, two front loops, two wrong ones, one wrong side.
  3. The second row - like all even ones, matches the pattern.
  4. The third row - one wrong side, cross two loops to the left, two wrong side. One wrong side.
  5. The fifth row - one front, double crochet (one front, one wrong side), one broach, two front with a slope to the left, one front.
  6. Seventh row - one front, two purl, crossing from two to the left, one front.
  7. The ninth row - one front, one broach, two loops in one to the left side, one front.

Openwork

Openwork mesh knitting, the diagram of which is presented below, is suitable for summer and demi-season models. A step-by-step instruction is as follows:

  1. The rapport consists of 23 loops.
  2. The first row - two wrong side, double crochet, two together (the first loop turns over), one front, two together for the upper part of the loop, one crochet.
  3. The second row and all even rows are knitted, as the loops look.
  4. The third row - two wrong side, one front, double crochet, three together to swap and knit front, double crochet and one front.
  5. From the fifth row, the pattern is repeated, starting from the first row.

Openwork edge

An openwork mesh with knitting needles, the schemes and description of which are presented in this article, is more suitable for off-season products. It can be made with cotton threads (this will be a more lightweight version), wool mixture or wool. The main condition is the absence of pile on the yarn, because in this case the pattern will not be visible as it was originally intended. The step-by-step instruction looks like this:

  1. The rapport consists of thirty loops.
  2. The first row is knitted with facial loops.
  3. In the second row, the yarn alternates with two front, knitted together front.
  4. The third row - one front, double crochet, five front, three stitches to knit together, turning them around its axis, five front, double crochet, one front and double crochet.
  5. The fourth row is completely knit with purl stitches.

Diagonal grid

Knitted mesh, the description of which is presented below, is universal for any season. The work is performed as follows:

  1. The rapport consists of twelve loops.
  2. The first row - one wrong loop, cross two loops to each other to the left side, two wrong loops.
  3. Second row - one facial loop, two wrong and two facial loops alternate.
  4. The third row - two front knit one front to the right, double yarn two loops one left, two loops one right.
  5. The fourth row is one wrong side, one front, one crossed, two wrong.
  6. The fifth row - one front, two wrong, the cross of two loops to the left.
  7. The sixth row - alternate one wrong side, two front and one wrong side.
  8. Seventh row - one crochet, knit two stitches one to the left, two stitches one to the right, double crochet.
  9. The eighth row is one front, two wrong, one front and one crossed loop.
