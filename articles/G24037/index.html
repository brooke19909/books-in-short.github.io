Marriage to a Peasant Woman

Another interesting fact about Peter 1 concerns his personal life. As you know, the king was married twice. His first wife was a girl of noble birth, while the second was a peasant daughter. Empress Catherine was actually called Martha, the mother and father of the empress were ordinary Livonian peasants, and she herself managed to work as a laundress.

This origin of the wife did not matter to the ruler, she was the only love of his life. It is curious that the autocrat even cared about the opinion of Catherine Martha about the events taking place in the state. He not only asked her opinion on important issues, but often followed the advice received.

The fight against alcoholism

The following interesting fact about Peter 1: the king was one of the fierce opponents of alcoholism. In 1714, Vladyka began to fight the drunkenness of his subjects with his usual humor. He came up with the "award" incorrigible alcoholics medals.





Perhaps world history did not know a heavier medal than that which was invented by the joker emperor. Cast iron was used to create it, even without a chain a similar product weighed about 7 kg or even a little more. The award was presented at the police station, where they brought alcoholics. She was hoisted around her neck using chains. And securely secured, excluding self-removal. The awarded drunkard was to be held in this form for a week.

What to tell children

Some details from the life of the sovereign may seem entertaining and to the younger generation. Many wonderful things would never have appeared in Russia, if not for Peter 1. Interesting facts for children are related to potatoes. The inhabitants of our country were not familiar with this vegetable until the king brought it from Holland. The first attempts to introduce potatoes as everyday food were a failure. The peasants tried to eat it raw, not guessing to bake or cook, and as a result refused this tasty and nutritious vegetable.





Tulips are beautiful flowers, the cultivation of which in the state also began at the request of Peter the Great. The bulbs of these plants were delivered by an autocrat to the country from Holland, where he spent a lot of time. The emperor even organized a "garden office", the main purpose of which was the introduction of overseas flowers.

Rumors of a substitution

The most interesting facts about Peter 1 are not connected at all with potatoes and tulips. They relate to the trip that he took at the age of 26 with the Great Embassy. Eyewitnesses claim that a young man of dense physique left his native land, with a mole located on his left cheek. He showed respect for everything related to Russian culture, practically knew the Bible by heart, demonstrated education and well-readness.

Why did the people decide that after this trip not a real tsar returned? Some contemporaries of the sovereign insist that after a two-year absence, he began to poorly understand the Russian language, to react negatively to everything that is connected with the original Russian customs. In addition, he had many new skills that he could not physically acquire during the trip. Finally, the mole on the cheek disappeared, outwardly he looked like a 40-year-old man.

The childhood of the king

Exciting details are known not only about the years of the reign of the famous emperor. Interesting facts from the childhood of Peter 1 are no less occupied by historians studying his personality. It turns out that during this period the emperor could not live without the noisy games to which he devoted most of his day. He could be so carried away that he refused to interrupt for food and drink.

It was in childhood that the king made friends with someone who throughout his life would be his faithful companion and confidant. We are talking about Alexander Menshikov, who participated in all the childish amusements of the future emperor. Interestingly, the ruler was absolutely not embarrassed by the lack of a good education among the statesman.
