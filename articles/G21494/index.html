Irkutsk is located in the very center of Siberia, 60 kilometers from the famous Lake Baikal. Thousands of tourists from all over the world visit the city every year - Irkutsk is also famous for its unique elements of classical architecture in architecture. The historical appearance of Irkutsk

The location of Irkutsk is extremely convenient for tourists from all over the world to flock here: the local airport is a major international transport hub, and you can also get to the city via the All-Russian Railway. The climate in Irkutsk can be called mild for Siberia - warm summers, dry autumn and spring, frosts rarely last longer than several days.





But tourists are attracted to Irkutsk by something else: the city center still keeps monuments of wooden architecture. Some ancient, rickety houses in which ordinary people live, were built in the century before last. The city administration periodically restores them, maintaining an attractive appearance. And although wood is an eternal material, and sooner or later such houses will have to be demolished, the city does everything to preserve its historical appearance. The largest project is the construction of the "Irkutsk Sloboda", which locals call the "130 quarter". Irkutsk after the implementation of this project received a unique district in the center, which instantly became the most popular place in the city.

The history of the creation of the "Irkutsk settlement"

Irkutsk authorities always had a desire to ennoble the historical ‚Äú130 quarter‚Äù, Irkutsk, to preserve the buildings of the 18-19th centuries, which were badly damaged by time. In 2008, it was decided to build a unique historical quarter at the regional level. To do this, it was necessary to relocate residents from dilapidated monuments of architecture, to clear the territory for new buildings.





The aim of the project was not just the creation of a stylized quarter - the buildings were erected strictly on the site of the lost architectural monuments, the best masters of wooden architecture were invited to decorate. Historians carefully studied materials on the historical development of this area, so that the project as accurately as possible reproduced the architecture of that time. Irkutsk Sloboda is a unique place where modern technologies in construction were used to recreate historical buildings. After the construction of the "130 quarter", Irkutsk, no doubt, has become even more attractive to tourists.
