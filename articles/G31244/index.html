History of the Principality of Monaco

This secluded harbor was originally inhabited by the Greeks in 6 BC. e. Legend has it that Hercules once passed through Monaco, and the Monoikos Temple was built in his honor. Historically, this country was part of France, but in 1215 it became a colony of Genoa by decree of Emperor Henry VI. Grimaldi settled here in 1297, and the ancestors of the family control the principality to this day.

In 1419, the Grimaldi family acquired Monaco from France. Since then, the principality has been protected by Spain, Italy and Sardinia. In 1793, the French revolutionary troops captured Monaco and held it until 1814. Today, a constitutional monarchy is established in the country, but the principality is under the protectorate of France.

Prince Rainier and Grace Kelly





In 1949, Prince Rainier III ascended the throne of Monaco. In 1956, he married the beautiful American actress Grace Kelly. This event was a turning point not only in her professional career, but also in the life of the whole principality. The famous actress at the peak of popularity left the cinema for marriage. This news stirred not only Hollywood, but the rest of the world. This event brought fame to the principality. Previously, they talked about him only as the place where the Monaco Grand Prix is ‚Äã‚Äãheld in the Formula 1 championship. Now the eyes of the rich and famous, riveted to Grace Kelly, turned to a small principality. Having received the title of princess, the actress invested her energy in promoting the arts. This brought charm to a small country and contributed to its economic and cultural development. They had three children in common: Caroline, Albert and Stephanie.

Grace Kelly's sudden death in a car accident in 1982 was a shock that was reflected worldwide. Films and books were written about her life, and death is still shrouded in mystery around which conspiracy theories are built. Prince Rainier III continued to rule Monaco after her death and was a respected monarch. He no longer married and died in 2005, leaving the throne to his son, Prince Albert II.

Current status

The capital of the Principality of Monaco is the city of the same name. The form of government is a constitutional monarchy. The economy rests on tourism, gambling and banking. Lack of income tax attract many wealthy residents. The banking industry and money management generate 16% of revenues and play an important role in the economy. In addition, the country is famous for its casinos, whose visitors come from all over the world to play in elite establishments. Tourism accounts for about 25% of revenues, and the country is proud of its hospitality and excellent cuisine. Climate





Monaco is located on the Mediterranean Sea and is surrounded by France on three sides. Nice is the nearest major city, which is approximately 18 km away. The terrain is quite rocky, located on steep hills that descend to the sea. The climate is mild all year round, the temperature varies from 8 to 26 degrees Celsius.

Monaco is divided into four quarters:

  • Monaco-Ville is an old city located on a rocky cape.
  • La Condamine is a port area.
  • Monte Carlo is the main resort, residential and tourist area.
  • Fontvieille - a new plot built on alluvial land.

Monaco population

More than a quarter of the country's population are French citizens. A smaller but significant number are Italians, Swiss and Belgians. One fifth is the Monegasques, representatives of the indigenous population,

Monegasques are proud of the unique history and position of their country in the world. It is believed that the name Monaco comes from the word "monoikos", associated with both the ancient Greeks and the Ligurians. Ligurians settled on the Mediterranean coast even before the era of the Roman Empire. The coastal road used by the Ligurians later became known as the "Hercules Road." In Greek, Hercules was often called "Hercules Monoikos" or "Hercules." Monegasques have managed to maintain their traditions and dialect for centuries, despite the influence of their much larger neighbors. Their cultural identity is reflected in many local festivals and is part of Monaco's worldwide fame. However, only a small portion of citizens can call themselves Monegasques. The rest are people of different nationalities.

There are more and more tourists who want to visit this country every year. They are probably interested in what language they speak in Monaco. This is a multinational country, but France had the greatest impact on it. Therefore, it is not surprising that French became the official language of Monaco. It is the language of management, business, education and the media.

The native population of Monaco speaks Monegasque, and it is he who is considered traditional. It looks a lot like Italian. Only about 21.6% of the population, who are mainly ethnic Monegasques, speak this language. And although the authorities are struggling to preserve their native dialect, its use is decreasing every year. By the 1970s, the language was on the verge of extinction, but several projects initiated by the government of Monaco helped raise its status. Currently, schools teach this language, and street signs are made in two versions: in French and Monegasque. Another traditional language of Monaco is Occitan. Currently, only a small part of the country's population speaks it.

In addition to the above languages, Italian and English are popular here. This is not surprising, since Italians make up about 19% of the country's population. For some time, Italian was even the official language of Monaco (between 1815 and 1861), when the principality was under the protectorate of Sardinia. Some members of the princely family speak Italian. English is mainly used by citizens of the UK, USA and Canada, permanently residing in the country. The official language of Monaco is French, but English remains the most popular among tourists.

Culture





Throughout history, the neighbors of Monaco (France, Italy and Spain) have had a huge influence on the principality. Therefore, art traces the elements of their cultures. The constitution allows religious freedom, but the majority of the population consider themselves to be supporters of the Roman Catholic Church (about 78% of citizens).

The ruling Grimaldi family has played an important role in promoting culture and art in Monaco. The city boasts exquisite architecture. Visitors will find an amazing array of world-class galleries where they can attend musical performances throughout the year. Many of them are supported by members of the princely family. In addition, Grimaldi has created many charitable organizations, including the foundations of Princess Grace (who also supports the Dance Academy), Prince Pierre (funding for culture and art) and Prince Albert II (protecting the environment).

Monaco Cuisine

Access to fresh vegetables, fruits and seafood has determined the local cuisine. In addition, the food reflects the Mediterranean heritage of the country, and the influence of French and Italian cuisine can be found in many recipes.

Fine dining seafood is served in each of the many restaurants. Among them, cod and anchovies dominate. The warm climate allows you to complement the fish with local vegetables. Separately, it is worth highlighting onions, garlic and olives (or olive oil), which are included in many dishes. As a rule, breakfast is very small, but for lunch and dinner often several dishes are served - this tradition has taken root in Monaco. Reviews about the restaurants are purely positive, because the owners, fearing to lose wealthy customers, maintain the service at the highest level.
