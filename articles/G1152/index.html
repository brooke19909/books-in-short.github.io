Shadow knitting pattern. Scheme and Description

The shadow pattern with knitting needles, a scheme with a description of which will be provided in the article, is not only simple in execution, but also looks very beautiful in the finished product. Such patterns consist of alternating front and back loops in a specific sequence. In this technique, you can perform geometric patterns, for example, zigzag, diagonals, stripes, squares and others. But the possibilities of shadow patterns are not limited to geometry; in this technique, whole pictures can be performed: portraits, landscapes, still lifes.





Let us consider in more detail what such knitting is. Shadow patterns (patterns included) will replenish your collection of simple but interesting knitted ornaments.

What to connect with shadow patterns

Let's figure out what a shadow pattern is with knitting needles. Its scheme is perfect for designing rugs or wraps on armchairs and chairs. The drawing is voluminous, interesting and does not require yarn of different colors. It is well suited for knitting men's clothes. Men do not like bright artsy ornaments, and knitting with a simple smooth surface often becomes boring, here the opportunity comes to help knit a shadow pattern with knitting needles.

Its scheme can be applied to any product - both for children and adults. They can decorate a sweater, cardigan, vest, snood or hat. Another advantage of shadow patterns is that they are double-sided, which is perfect for knitting scarves and stoles.

Yarn and tool selection

Any yarn is suitable for knitting shadow patterns, but the pattern will be more clearly read on a fabric knitted from thick bulk yarn. So the product will look spectacular and stylish, even if the pattern is very simple. You can take the finished thick yarn, or fold the thinner thread in half.

Knitting needles are the size recommended by the manufacturer of the selected yarn. But if you need a dense, inelastic cloth, for example, for a plaid, take one-and-a-half sizes smaller knitting needles. If you take a larger tool, there is a risk that the canvas will turn out too loose, and the pattern is not clear enough.
