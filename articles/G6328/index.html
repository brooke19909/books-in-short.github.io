Making flowers from mastic

Try to remember the school lessons in which you learned to sculpt various figures from plasticine . Follow the same procedure.

  • Spread the cling film on the table and roll the mastic into a thin layer. It can be sprinkled with a little sugar powder.
  • Cut either circles with petals or other necessary details using a sharp knife or a small tin (a glass or similar base is suitable).
  • Form volumetric elements by connecting together the two sides of the petal at the base.
  • Dry the workpieces.

If you notice that during operation, the mastic flower sticks to your hands, add a little powdered sugar. To prevent the mass from drying out, wrap it in film or parchment. Experienced confectioners advise you not to get involved in a large number of parts from mastic, so that the cakes are not harsh. Since milk mastic has a natural creamy yellow hue, you can try another recipe for sweet mass if you want to make a pale blue or pink flower from mastic.
