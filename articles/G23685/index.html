Klaipeda (Lithuania) - a city in which during the Second World War most of the buildings and the fort were badly damaged. Today the city is an important Lithuanian seaport, one of the largest ports of the Baltic Sea, which never freezes.



Quite a bit of history

Klaipeda is one of the oldest cities in Lithuania. Historical studies show that in the first century AD, the territory of this city was inhabited by the Balts - the ancestors of the Lithuanians. In the thirteenth century, Klaipeda became an object of interest for the knights belonging to the Teutonic Order and the feudal lords of Germany. They built a castle on the square of the modern city and became the founders of Memel. Until 1525, this place belonged to its founders.





Then Memel at different times was in the hands of both Prussia and Sweden. After the end of the Seven Years War, he returned to Prussia. Many sights of Klaipeda, photos of which are in our article, have imprints of these times. Memel in the early twenties of the last century found its modern owner - Lithuania. The city received another name - Klaipeda.

The main attraction of Klaipeda



The Memel Castle is rightfully considered to be such - this is the only order castle on the territory of modern Lithuania, which has survived to this day. Klaipeda, the sights of which, like most of its historical buildings, have experienced a lot during its life, this object is protected with special trepidation. Prince of Lithuania Keitut in 1379 completely burned this architectural value. But soon the castle was restored and diligently fortified. In 1455, the building was captured by the Zemaitians, for some time it was under their control. And five years later, the Memel Castle was rebuilt and strengthened according to new defense trends. Such reconstructions and adversities more than once fell to the lot of this object. During the Soviet Union, it was almost impossible to get into the castle, because this required special permission. At the beginning of the twenty-first century, a museum was opened here.
