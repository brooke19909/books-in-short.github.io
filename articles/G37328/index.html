Problems and bug 53

In most cases, flashing mobile devices is easy, and the updated version of iPhone 6 works flawlessly. But sometimes users of iPhone-6 and 6S phone models encounter problems and errors in working after installing version iOS 12. Most often, complaints are related to fast battery loss, poor sound quality, problems in connecting Wi-Fi and Bluetooth, a sensor and level 1 and 3 applications. If the problems persist after rebooting the device, experts advise returning the previous version of the operating system.

Many are also wondering if iOS can be updated to iOS6, unfortunately, at the moment this is not possible. Even in iPhone 6 models, the platform is supported from version 8.0.

In some cases, when updating iPhone 6, an error 53 pops up, which leads to a breakdown of the device. Error code - Error 53 occurs when restoring or updating a smartphone via iTunes, on which the Touch ID module is detected during the download process. Failure is accompanied by a brief message and a denial of work. Most often, iPhone-6 and iPhone-6S are affected.

Apple officials claim that Error 53 occurs in devices with a sensor or other components related to the ability to recognize a fingerprint if they have been replaced. That is, the module is not an element of the factory assembly and is not recognized by the official iPhone programs.

If the sensor has been replaced and does not match other components, when checking the gadget, the operating system detects a mismatch and gives an error. For example, improperly changing the screen will cause a crash. When the smartphone starts, the built-in control sensor checks the authenticity and correct operation of the device, in case of mismatch of spare parts, the device is blocked.

In the official Apple store, there is hardware available to restore the connection between the sensor and the protective controller. After the occurrence of error 53, it is difficult to restore the mobile device. Parts must be replaced with original parts and flashing.
