Knitting on a fork: patterns and possibilities

In order to tie a soft, airy and light thing with your own hands, you can use an original tool called a fork.

This type of needlework is not new, but for some reason it was completely undeservedly forgotten. But such a knitting technique is fraught with many possibilities, the use of which will make home-made things more original and interesting.



What is a knitting fork

Despite the similarity in name to the usual well-known kitchen tool, a needlework fork can be just a piece of hard wire, folded in the shape of the English letter ‚ÄúU‚Äù.

Two needles can be used, fastened together at a certain distance by plates with holes that allow you to adjust the required loop width.

Special forks that are commercially available can also be used.

This tool is so simple that even a child can learn the basic rules for handling it.

Moreover, for a process such as knitting on a fork, patterns can always be found, for example, in magazines.





Knitting technology

For activities such as knitting on a fork, the patterns are actually simple and straightforward.

In the general case, the fork is used only to hold the loops, and the knitting process itself is carried out using a conventional hook. This device is suitable for knitting air and light strips. The specific form of the result will depend on the method of knitting:

- double crochet;

- single crochet;

- a half-column.

You can also knit columns with two or three crochets.

The knitting process in all cases consists in the fact that with the help of a hook the usual conceived loops are knitted, and the thread at the same time wraps around the knitting needles of the frame at a certain distance from each other.



Knitting on a fork with a half-column scheme

First, as usual, an air loop is attached to the hook. Then the working thread is skipped behind the frame. In this case, the hook must be held in the right hand, and the knitting fork, respectively, in the left.

The hook reaches the middle of the thread, moving along the front of the frame, and grabs the thread. Loops fit. Then the frame rotates around itself so that the hook is behind it. It needs to be removed from the loop, moved forward and undressed again.

The working thread is twisted around the frame in the direction to the right. It must be crocheted and knit the next loop. Further knitting continues in a similar way. It is important to adhere to the middle of the frame, otherwise the seam can turn out ugly and uneven.

When the knitting of the strip is finished, it must be removed from the frame and used for further manufacture of the product. But you need to work with it carefully, otherwise loose loops will open.

If you pull on both sides of the two ends of the strip, you can get a decorative finishing cord. It can also be used in a process such as knitting on a fork of a model or pattern that interested the author.

Why use a knitting fork

A fork is a unique and non-trivial knitting tool. With its help, you can get unusually openwork, airy and elegant things, such as knitted sweaters, stoles, patterns.
