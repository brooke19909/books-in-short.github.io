Aegina, Greece: island location, hotels, restaurants, attractions, interesting places, historical facts and events, photos

A rather large island of Aegina in Greece, not as advertised as other resorts of the country, is becoming more and more popular every year among tourists from all over the world. It is the second largest island of the Saronic Gulf. According to legend, the beautiful Aegina was kidnapped by Zeus. This happened on one of the islands that was formerly called Aeona, and today is called Aegina.

Comparison of the island with the pearl in the crown of the argosaronic archipelago is completely justified - it is difficult to find another similar paradise on our planet.

A bit of history

In the distant past, Aegina was much richer than the ancient state of Piraeus (the main port of the Greek capital) and even Athens. The convenient strategic position of the island (between Attica and the Peloponnese) gave Aegina a number of advantages that the locals used very successfully.

Historians claim that for several centuries BC, about a million people lived on Aegina - a figure unheard of at that time. In the ancient state of only slaves, there were more than 400 thousand. Aegina could not feed the population on her own and imported part of the food from outside.

Naturally, such a comfortable existence could not go unnoticed by neighbors, and the state was at the epicenter of regional battles almost constantly. Most successful Aegina haunted the rulers of Athens, and they made numerous attempts to seize this territory. During the rule of the Romans, the island was transferred to each other by the Roman emperors, as a passing banner.

In the Byzantine period, Aegina was part of Hellas. Then the island was conquered by the soldiers of the Ottoman Empire. During the Liberation War (1821-1832), which ended with a peace treaty signed in Constantinople, the island of Aegina in Greece, like other regions of the country, gained independence. Interestingly, the Greeks became the first of the peoples conquered by the Ottoman Empire and gained independence.

Aegina Location

Aegina island in Greece (you can see photos of picturesque landscapes in this article) has a triangular shape and is located between Attica and Argolis in the Saronic Gulf, 27 kilometers from the capital of Athens. Near Aegina there are smaller islands: Agistri, Hydra, Poros and Spetses.

Aegina Square - 88 square meters km, the coastline is 57 km. Administratively, the island is divided into territorial units: Ayia Marina, Vaya, Perdika, Suvala and Marathonas.

St. Nectaria and the monastery

One of the main attractions of Aegina in Greece. The magnificent complex includes a women's Orthodox monastery and temple. It is located six kilometers from the promenade. The monastery was founded by St. Nectarius of Aegina in 1908 and named it Holy Trinity. Here he lived until his death (1920) and was buried in the monastery.

The main monastery church is a magnificent building, built recently (1973). The grandiose building is made in the Neo-Byzantine style and skillfully decorated with amazing mosaics. A steep staircase leads up to the hill from the church leading to the monastery complex itself.

Here is the church of the Holy Trinity - the oldest temple of the monastery. In a small chapel, a marble sarcophagus is preserved, where the body of St. Nectaria, and nearby is a source of holy water. The monastic cell, in which the saint spent the last years, has survived to the present day. Today it is open to the public. The main shrine of the monastery are the miraculous relics of St. Nectaria.

Paleochora

It is the former capital of Aegina island in Greece. The Paleochora was built in the 9th century as a refuge in which the population could hide from pirates. Once the city did not cope with the task assigned to it - in 1537 it was destroyed by the raid of the pirates Hayreddin Ryzheborodogo, who in the West is better known by the name of Barbarossa.

Residents left Paleochora in the years 1826-1827. Since then it has been deserted, but the image of this ghostly settlement is very attractive. You can reach it by car - after passing the St. Nectarius Cathedral, turn left, and after 400 meters you will find yourself in place.

Museums

The island of Aegina not only inherited numerous ancient riches. Its inhabitants were able to keep them in museums, whose expositions are always of interest to tourists.

On the island is the first Archaeological Museum in Greece, located in the house of Kapodistrias. For almost two centuries of history, scientists, archaeologists and museum staff have managed to collect many artifacts from the temple of Aphaia, the sanctuary of Zeus, the Column.

The collection includes bas-reliefs, funerary vases, ancient columns, inscriptions of the archaic period, marble statues. In the capital of the island there is a museum of Kapralos, a Greek sculptor who grew up in a poor family and achieved great success in his work. Holidays on the island

It is no secret that tourists coming to Greece, and to Aegina in particular, are not limited to sightseeing. Fans of beach vacations will appreciate the island's few and well-groomed beaches. There are several, but the most popular are Agia Maria and Aiginitissa.

This is the longest sandy beach on the island of Aegina in Greece (we posted a photo below). The flat coast and shallow water made it an ideal place to relax with the kids. Lovers of outdoor activities can go kayaking, canoeing and rowing boats, take part in sailing competitions or play water volleyball. In the tourist season, there are daily fun parties, music plays. There are several restaurants on the beach. Aegina (Greece) is famous for its cuisine, and on the island you can taste amazing delicacies. They will be prepared for you by experienced chefs.

Aiginitissa Beach

This is the best beach on Aegina island in Greece. The beach offers many activities for a variety of recreation - tennis, beach volleyball, yoga, ping pong.

Aegina island in Greece: how to get there?

From the main port of Athens - Piraeus - passenger ships depart every hour. Tickets can be bought at the port before departure. If you are planning a day trip, come to the port early in the morning to be on the island in an hour and start sightseeing.

In addition to ferries, high-speed high-speed vessels - "dolphins" - go to Aegina. Tickets for them cost a little more than a ferry, but you can halve the travel time. All flights from Piraeus arrive to the western part of the island, to the port of the eponymous capital - Aegina.
