The heart of the city - Kazan Kremlin

The heart of the capital of Tatarstan is its Kremlin. And this is certainly what you need to see in Kazan in 2 days! By the way, it is located very close to Bauman - on the Kremlin street. You can walk on foot, and quickly enough.

The object, created in the 16th century, is under the protection of UNESCO, and a whole collection of pearls of Tatarstan is hidden behind its walls. The soul of any city is that place where traditions of its native inhabitants are carefully kept; where, like the apple of an eye, they protect the culture and honor history. Such a place in the capital of the Republic of Tatarstan is the Old Tatar settlement. And those who have thought what to see in Kazan in 2 days should definitely add this object to their route.

When Kazan was conquered by Ivan the Terrible, it was here that the Tatars were allowed to settle. They settled down and for many centuries passed on from generation to generation the traditions, rites and customs of their people.

Raifa - the abode of purity and inspiration

If you managed to escape to Kazan for 2.5 days, what should you definitely see? In this case, it is advisable to set aside one day for visiting suburban attractions. For example, the Raifa monastery, which is the most important Orthodox shrine. The monastery is located on the territory of the Volga-Kama Reserve, on the lake. You can get to it by bus or private car.

Religious statues located in the courtyard of the monastery are extremely harmoniously combined with flowers of unprecedented beauty, which creates a special atmosphere of elevation and fills the soul with inspiration.

The monks carefully monitor the purity and order in their possessions, and this also contributes to spiritual purification. In the Raifa monastery is the Georgian icon of the Mother of God, considered the patroness of the arts.



Ecumenical temple

You can get by car to the Ecumenical Church, which is located in a village called Old Arakchino. This is another recommendation for those who are considering the question of what to see in Kazan in 2 days.

The temple sheltered seven world religions under its roof, so it can be called a memorial of reconciliation. Believers from different cities and countries come here to honor their shrines and get acquainted with the basics of other faiths.

Sviyazhsk and Bulgar - greetings from antiquity

According to reviews, the centers of ancient culture - Bolgar and Sviyazhsk - are very popular among tourists. A regular motor ship will deliver guests from Kazan to their shores, so that you can not only "taste antiquities", but also enjoy a pleasant trip along the Volga.

The island-city of Sviyazhsk, originating from the time of Ivan the Terrible, will immerse guests in the 16th century. Here is the oldest church in Tatarstan - the Trinity, and will also delight the hearts of the Orthodox Cathedral of the Assumption.

Bulgarians also have something to surprise travelers. This is a city-reserve where tourists can walk directly along the streets of the Middle Ages, excavated by archaeologists. The most beautiful building of the Bulgar is the Cathedral Mosque.
