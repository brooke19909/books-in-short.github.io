Making a passport cover: ideas. Cool passport covers

Handmade souvenirs are becoming more popular lately. If you want to be in a trend, we offer an interesting lesson - making a cover for a passport from different materials with your own hands. This will not only be a great entertainment, but will also create exclusive souvenirs for relatives and friends.





Interesting ideas and design methods

Making a cover for a passport can really be a fascinating creative process, because there are a lot of options for this standard functional element. You can perform the product by the following methods:

  • Knit or crocheted.
  • Sew from shreds.
  • Perform fabric applique.
  • Create from beautiful paper using scrapbooking technique.
  • Apply decoupage to decorate the finished base.
  • Decorate with exclusive stickers or drawings according to your own designs.
  • To make of leather according to your own design.

So, there is plenty to choose from. Each option will be more or less suitable for a particular person. As a gift, it‚Äôs good for a man to make a unique present from leather, and for a teenager who has just received a passport, a cool cover with youth drawings or stickers is suitable.

Scrapbooking

This technique allows you to create very beautiful passport covers. They resemble old family photo albums. Here, on the basis of special paper with a pattern, embossing or other decor, the remaining cut out parts are pasted. Satin ribbons, flowers made of paper and fabric are also used as decorations. You can make the cover design so that it also has a window with a photo of the owner. It‚Äôs easy to come up with different ideas.





This technique is especially suitable for making a gift in a romantic style for a young dreamy girl or a couple in love. Present the bride and groom a set of two covers made in a single concept or complementing each other. By the way, this can become a wedding souvenir for newlyweds, especially since a wife usually takes her husband's name and changes her passport. That‚Äôs the reason for the new cover. Decoupage

Original and beautiful passport covers are easily made using this technique. It is also called a napkin, as for gluing you can use ordinary single-layer table napkins or specialized decoupage cards. Most often they are made with photorealistic images. The work consists of the following steps:

  1. Preparation of the base and its primer.
  2. Sticking images.
  3. Drawing by brush or on stencils of a pattern, inscriptions.
  4. Coating with waterproof transparent varnish.

Before proceeding to the next step, the surface must be thoroughly dried.

Fabric applique

Very creative passport covers can be made from fleece or felt. These materials make it possible to easily cut out all kinds of shapes, sew parts on the front side, or even glue them together. You can make a cover with fun little owls so popular now or offer options with the image of the symbol of the year, zodiac sign, etc. as a gift to friends.





Cool passport covers

This option is relevant for adolescents who have received their first passport. They already feel like adults, but still love to express themselves in extraordinary bright things. You can make cool passport covers in any of the following techniques:

  • Do the same from the fabric.




  • Decorate the purchased cover with original youth stickers that match the interests of the teenager.
  • Draw a finished pattern with acrylics in graffiti style, and then varnish.

As you can see, there can be many options for an original gift for a teenager.
