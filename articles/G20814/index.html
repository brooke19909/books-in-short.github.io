Positioning

To tell you the truth, the development of a phone with a physical keyboard in an age of advanced touch technology is an unexpected step even for Nokia. No, we remember the very successful line of 6300 and the like, but today the release of such a model looks unusual.





The phone, apparently, was created in order to replace the niche of simple, affordable, but reliable devices. Now they are still in demand, and the Nokia 230 Dual SIM, the review of which we are conducting today, is obviously designed to satisfy one.

The gadget is relatively inexpensive - about $ 50. Due to this, it is already more favorably presented than some competing devices from the same niche. A huge plus is the fact that he is a product of the glorious Nokia, which clearly has the most valuable experience in promoting keyboard models among other companies. All this is the basis for positioning the device as an affordable, but functional smartphone.

Appearance

The design of the phone is nothing new. Nokia 230 Dual SIM (Black and White - two available color schemes) has a classic look: a candy bar with a keyboard visually separated in the lower segment of the body, as well as a screen at the top. The display here, by the way, visually seems larger than it actually is, due to the thick edging painted in dark color.





The ‚Äúsegmentation‚Äù logic that we see on the front of the Nokia Asha 230 Dual SIM continues to the rear. Display





The screen has a small resolution (especially in comparison with modern gadgets) of 240 by 320 pixels. This is quite enough to work with a device that has a physical keyboard: it is easy to navigate on it in menus, contact lists, and messages. And although the physical size of the display is only 2.8 inches, visually it seems much higher (due to the dark inserts described at the beginning of the article).

Still, regarding the screen, it should be noted its energy-efficient mode of operation. We learned about him only after getting acquainted with the technical characteristics, but his presence is confirmed by reviews. Nokia 230 Dual SIM is a very optimized phone that can work for a long time on a single charge.

Camera

Like other keyboard devices developed by Nokia, the 230 has two cameras, both for creating simple shots and for working with selfies. The resolution of both matrices is 2 megapixels, each of them is paired with a powerful LED flash, taking photos even in low light.





Of course, the quality of the images leaves much to be desired against the background of the 8-megapixel matrices that have become familiar to us; however, they can make some elementary photos from the rest for posting on social networks.

Those who developed the Nokia 230 Dual SIM (Silver) mobile phone understood that this device would be primarily used as a reliable means of communication. The user here draws his attention to the quality of the connection and conversation, therefore, the phone perfectly holds the signal and works in the current standards of GSM-connection. It should also be noted the possibility of working with two SIM cards. Reviews

Given the popularity of the model presented in this review, we found numerous reviews about it on the network. As for the negative characteristics, they describe the interface and the logic of interaction of the device. In particular, someone is categorically not happy with the firmware of the smartphone, the inability to directly transfer contacts from the phone to the PC, and a host of other minor difficulties that seriously complicate the user's life. There are also shortcomings in terms of choosing a SIM card: some customers ask for help in telling them how to choose one or another card and how to save their choice.

There are also complaints, for example, about the mail hierarchy. According to several authors, the phone does not have a sufficiently organized structure in the memory of letters.
