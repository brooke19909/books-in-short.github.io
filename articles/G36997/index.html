Filling out a declaration on an immovable property: who, when and why should do it

The declaration of immovable property is filled in if it is registered using a simplified system. This is regulated by Federal Law No. 93, according to which the document is submitted in paper or electronic form in 2 copies to the competent authorities. After that, the registration of the erected object is carried out directly.

Under what conditions can a declaration be filed

The first mandatory factor for its adoption by the Federal Registration Service is the availability of documents for the land. This is not necessarily the right of ownership, it is enough just a contract of use or rental.

The second prerequisite for submitting a declaration of immovable property is the completion of construction. That is, a garden house or other structure located on the land should be put into operation. BTI is responsible for this.

And the most important requirement for the adoption of a declaration of immovable property is accuracy and accuracy. Everything should be written with a blue ballpoint pen, in block letters, legibly, without corrections and blots.





How to fill out a declaration correctly

This document indicates all information about the object, starting with its location and ending with information about the owners. The first paragraph of the declaration of immovable property is intended for a detailed description of its address. Information from the subject of the Russian Federation to the house number is entered here.

The second item is the type of object. This can be a home ownership with or without a residence, as well as a farm building or a garage. Separately, the purpose of the object is indicated.

Further, not forgetting to write down the cadastral number of the site on which the building is located, we should proceed to the largest point of the declaration. These are specifications. It describes the nuances associated with the building itself, the materials from which it was built, as well as available communications. The sixth paragraph is information about the owner of the building, including its registration, passport and other data. In the event that the property is owned by several persons, each fills out a declaration on his own behalf, indicating in its seventh paragraph the size of his share.

At the very end, as in any other document, the date of filing and signature is placed in the declaration of the immovable property. By filling out this document and endorsing it, the citizen confirms the accuracy of all the specified information.

For the submission to the registering bodies of information that is not true, administrative liability is provided.

Who should fill out the declaration

In the case of ownership of a country or other land plot on which the completed construction facility is located, the owner shall register his right with the relevant authorities. The easiest option is to file a declaration. Moreover, now there are a lot of legal agencies and specialists providing assistance in this matter. A sample declaration of the property with an example of its completion must be submitted to the Federal Registration Service. To facilitate the procedure, electronic filing is allowed.
