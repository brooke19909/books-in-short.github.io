Handbook for needlewomen: cross stitch pattern "house"; own scheme

Today, many are fond of needlework. Very often for classes, a cross stitch pattern is required. The circuit can be bought at a specialty store. Often they are downloaded from the Internet. But a cross-stitch pattern can also be built with one's own hand. A house, the scheme of which will help to solve the problem, can be of different options.

Ornament decoration products

To decorate products with ornaments, the most primitive cross-stitch pattern is used. A house, the scheme of which is quite simple, can successfully decorate linen or clothes. For this, rapport is repeated as many times as the length of the edge of the product requires.





Usually no more than five to six colors of natural threads are used for such embroidery: silk or floss.

Embroidered landscapes

Many lovers of needlework prefer to make paintings. This requires a cross stitch pattern. The house, the scheme of which is used for work, can be part of the landscape. To make the picture look more natural, the master will need a thread of a large number of shades.

Fairytale house

Embroidery on a fantasy theme will successfully fit into the interior of a children's room. Such may be the image of a fabulous house: the dwelling of fairies, gnomes, elves and other invented characters.





By the way, the children themselves like to embroider, especially the pictures that fit their imagination. This type of needlework brings up attentiveness, accuracy, perseverance. In addition, a feeling of beauty, fantasy is activated in the growing personality.

How to create a pattern for embroidery yourself?

Of course, to perform work on a ready-made template is handicraft. We can‚Äôt talk about the disclosure of creative potential. But to invent and embroider himself what the imagination draws to the master! But how to do that?





The master class will help in this rather difficult task. For sample, you can take a simpler picture. Here, for example, is a fungus in which gnomes have built their home. As you can see, not so many colors and shades are used here.

  • For greater accuracy, it is better to enlarge the picture or draw it in large format. Be sure to apply shades to the drawing, clearly outlining the border of their transition to other colors.
  • Then a grid is applied to the drawing. It can be drawn directly on top of the picture. And you can use transparent paper on which the grid will be applied.
  • Next, the master proceeds to fill the squares with the color that he will use when embroidering. If desired, you can replace, for example, red with green, if that makes sense. You need to completely apply color to those squares in which the selected color occupies more than half of it.
  • The same algorithm is used in relation to all other shades.
  • Then the sample can be removed in the case when the grid was made on a transparent sheet. But it is most convenient to transfer the scheme to a new sheet with a grid, for example, graph paper.

These patterns are not only used for embroidery. They are convenient to use when knitting a jacquard pattern, creating tapestries, laying out mosaics from small square tiles. Therefore, the ability to compose them yourself can be useful not only to embroiderers.
