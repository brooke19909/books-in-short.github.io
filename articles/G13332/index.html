How to check IMEI on Samsung and what is it for?

Almost every owner of a mobile phone is faced with the abbreviation IMEI. If you have not had to deal with it, then this is only a matter of time. Each smartphone or even a push-button old telephone has its own identifier (read IMEI). In other words, this is the number of the mobile device in the operator‚Äôs network.

What is it for?

At a minimum, to identify the device. If someone steals the phone, the owner will be able to contact the police. Those, in turn, will be able to find the stolen phone by checking its IMEI. They can manually dial a combination of numbers to find out the number, or they can determine it using a mobile operator. As soon as the user makes a call, the IMEI of the mobile device automatically enters the operator‚Äôs network. Having learned who the SIM card is registered to, the police can easily figure out the attacker.

To draw an analogy, the IMEI of a mobile phone is like a car's license plate. By this sign, the traffic police can identify who the car is registered with. Same thing with IMEI phone.

How to check IMEI on Samsung?




Usually all IMEI phones are checked the same way, but there are exceptions. For example, take the Samsung Galaxy smartphone , which is now very popular. The first obvious way to check is to find it on the box or in the documentation. It must be indicated. Also, this combination of numbers is recorded in the warranty card. And if it is not indicated in the coupon, then the guarantee will be void. But the third way is to dial the key combination * # 06 #.





The latter method allows you to check IMEI not only on Samsung, but also on phones from other manufacturers. After entering this code, the serial number is immediately displayed on the screen. Exceptions are rare. In 90% of cases, this combination works.

How to authenticate Samsung by IMEI?

Suppose you enter the key combination * # 06 # and the code is displayed on the screen. What to do next? Its presence does not mean anything, because this code is easy to fake. It is more important to determine if a given code matches the phone itself. That is, it must be checked for authenticity.





It is easy to do. On the official website of Samsung there is a special form of identification with which you can easily check Samsung IMEI. Just enter it in the line and click "check". If the code is found and confirmed on the official website, then it means that you have a genuine Samsung smartphone. Otherwise, the answer on the site will be as follows: "Electronic guarantee not found."

If someone offers you to buy such a phone, be sure to find out the serial number and check it. Perhaps they are trying to trick you. Fortunately, now you know how to check Samsung Galaxy by IMEI.

What do all these numbers mean?

Serial number can be 11-20 characters. Most often, these are 15 digits, which mean the following:

  1. The first 6 are the phone model code (the first 2 digits of them are the country code).
  2. The next 2 digits are the code of the country where the phone was collected (FAC or Final Assembly Code).
  3. The next 6 digits are the serial number.
  4. The last digit is almost always 0. Denotes SP (Spare).

Do IMEI change?

Yes, you can change the serial number , so phone manufacturers also have special IMEI authentication services. There are special programs with which they change the serial number, but the developers are not asleep. Every year, manufacturers improve the protection against IMEI changes, but hackers do the same - improve their software by breaking serial numbers. This war goes on forever.

How will IMEI help if your phone is stolen?





If your phone was stolen, then, first of all, you should go to the police and write a statement about the theft. The application must indicate its serial number. Above, we already wrote how to check IMEI on Samsung, and if the phone buyers are really you, then you should have a box or a warranty card, technical documentation.

Law enforcement authorities will accept the application and begin to search for your device. Ideally, they should contact the telecom operators and send them IMEI in order to determine the person who uses the phone. This would speed up the search process. But in reality it will not be so. They will simply personally check the IMEI phones of people who they think are suspicious. The likelihood that in this way they can specifically find your phone is small. Therefore, we recommend that you do not lose your gadget corny. Operators connect to the search for a lost or stolen phone very rarely. This is logical, because police officers should do this.

In the meantime, there is no law that would oblige them to do this. So police officers can check IMEI on Samsung or any other phone, but nothing more. Only in this case will the serial number of your smartphone be saved.

Therefore, we can only recommend that you do not lose your gadget. Alas, the current legislative framework does not allow law enforcement agencies to effectively search for a telephone. In some advanced countries, this is easy for the police, but not with us. Nevertheless, if the mobile was stolen, then go to the police with the IMEI device. There are no alternatives to date. So it remains to hope for law enforcement.
