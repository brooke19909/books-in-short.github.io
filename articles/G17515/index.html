Outdoor antenna for TV: types, description of the best

The difference between urban and rural residents in terms of TV broadcasting is very significant. If the former, as a rule, use TV services through leased lines (cable TV), then the latter is difficult to do without a street antenna.

Types of antennas

To begin with, we denote the main types of these devices, which differ from each other in functionality, appearance and other equally important features. The final conclusions about the appropriateness of the purchase should be made based on personal needs and the geolocation of your home.

Satellite antenna

The now very popular "dishes" do not depend on proximity to the TV towers, because they broadcast via satellite. In addition, the signal strength of the satellite outdoor antenna for the TV is not affected by the terrain.





Even the simplest version of the "plate", such as offset or direct focus, allows you to safely receive an image in high quality. Here you can add a large number of providers, and at the same time a lot of channels.

Despite the obvious advantages of a satellite outdoor antenna for a TV over other devices, it has its own, for some, critical drawbacks. First is the cost. Here you need to spend money on the ‚Äúplate‚Äù itself, and the worse the terrain (harsh climate, the proximity of industrial enterprises that interfere with the signal, etc.), the larger the diameter and more technologically advanced the device is, as well as more expensive. Secondly, you will need to buy a receiver for broadcast processing and, in difficult cases, a convector, to get a street antenna for a TV with an amplifier. And thirdly, for normal broadcasting, a sensible TV that is capable of delivering and ‚Äúdigesting‚Äù a high-quality picture will obviously not be superfluous.

"Polish" antennas

This is the simplest, most unpretentious and cheap device that the sea has on the electronics market. It has a lattice frame with antennae and is perfect for ordinary summer cottages. Using this antenna, you can catch a dozen local channels, including ORT and NTV. In addition, with aluminum wires, a cable and a tool, you can easily make a semblance of the original with your own hands.





Outdoor antennas for a TV of such a plan are placed on the house, roof, poles and other elevations, directing the central part towards the TV tower. The latter should be no further than 30 km from you. If the local topography is diluted with trees, mounds, heaps or other obstacles that interfere with the signal, then the installation of a street antenna for the TV should be carried out as high as possible. In difficult cases, an intelligent amplifier will help to stabilize broadcasting and eliminate most interference.

The internal amplifier allows you to expand the reception area for 80 km, recognizing even the weakest broadcasts (usually with a loss of quality). Just like in the previous case, you can make a street antenna for a TV with an amplifier yourself. The only thing you will need besides the above materials is a high-quality converter. Install such devices at the highest point, that is, on the roof or a makeshift pillar / pole.

Running Wave Devices (Uda Yagi)

Antennas of this class have rather high receiving characteristics and are compact. All the main elements of the device are fixed to one common arrow. In addition, this type of antenna is able to work with several bands, which is very important for remote and inaccessible villages, where only old towers broadcasting at non-standard frequencies are nearby.

Digital antennas

Such devices consist of two parts - a tuner for processing a digital signal and an antenna frame, which is installed on the street. The design of the latter is as simple as possible, because the main responsibility for receiving a signal lies with the tuner. A digital outdoor antenna for a TV is perfect for those who are ‚Äúfriends‚Äù with modern technology, are not afraid of numerous settings / variations in the menu, and whose house is located near the city. Such devices are tuned to receive only the main signal and well ignore reflected interference.

Antenna Types

In total, two types of antennas can be identified - passive and active. The first option involves a separate / external converter, and the second with a built-in amplifier. In terms of signal reception quality, they are not much different, but in terms of service life - significantly.





Active devices installed "as is", that is, without additional protection, will last a little over a year, because due to bad weather and corrosion, the built-in converter starts to fail. Passive models come with an external amplifier, which can be located at home or somewhere in the attic, so the service life of such antennas is much longer.
