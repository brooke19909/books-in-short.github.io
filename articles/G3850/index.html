The city of Lesosibirsk (Krasnoyarsk Territory): history, geography, attractions

Lesosibirsk (Krasnoyarsk Territory) is one of the most interesting cities in Siberia. It is located on the banks of the largest river in Eurasia and is surrounded on all sides by huge tracts of real taiga. When was the city founded? What do its inhabitants do and what interesting tourist can see here?

Cities of the Krasnoyarsk Territory

Krasnoyarsk Territory is one of the largest regions of the Russian Federation in area. About three million Russians live here. The region has a developed mining industry, because the region has significant reserves of some minerals. Among them are nickel, cobalt, gold, graphite and others.

In the southern part of this region is the young Lesosibirsk. Krasnoyarsk Territory is 23 cities with different population sizes. The most populated from this list are Krasnoyarsk, Norilsk, Kansk, Achinsk and Zheleznogorsk. However, only one of the cities in the region has more than one million inhabitants. This is Krasnoyarsk - the administrative center of education called the Krasnoyarsk Territory.





The city of Lesosibirsk takes eighth place in the region in terms of population. What do its inhabitants do? And what may be interesting for this small town to a visiting tourist? Read about it later.

Lesosibirsk, Krasnoyarsk Territory: features of the geographical position of the city

When it was decided to build a new city in Siberia, on the banks of the Yenisei, it was not necessary to think about its name for a long time. Indeed, it is difficult to find another such city in the vastness of Russia, the inhabitants of which would be so close to wildlife. A vivid confirmation of this is his card. Lesosibirsk is located on the left bank of the Yenisei, surrounded by centuries-old taiga forests.





The city has an extremely favorable transport and geographical position. So, through its territory passes the railway connecting the settlement with the Trans-Siberian Railway. The highway, the so-called Yenisei tract, leads to the federal highway "Baikal". 30 km from the city is the mouth of the Angara River, through which connections are made with the Lower Angara. There is a large port in Lesosibirsk itself, which is capable of processing up to one million tons of cargo per year.

Lesosybers themselves affectionately call their city Lessobon and are happy to invite all tourists to themselves.

The history of the forest city

Although Lesosibirsk received the status of the city only in 1975, its biography has its roots much deeper in history. So, back in 1640, the village of Maklakov Lug appeared on this place. The name of the village, as historians believe, comes from the word "wet." The first settlers of Maklakov Luga were engaged in agriculture, fishing, and also hunting.

At the beginning of the twentieth century, the first sawmill (Norwegian Lid) was built here. The products of a small enterprise even went for export. In the 50s, the Soviet government built several large plants in the village of Maklakovo, which were engaged in sawing and processing of local wood. In February 1975, a new city was formed from several villages.





Economics and industry

Modern Lesosibirsk stretches along the Yenisei River for almost three dozen kilometers. It consists of several small residential areas connected by one highway. Thanks to the Clean City company, Lesosibirsk has been clean and well-groomed for more than a dozen years. Since 1971, the company has been collecting and processing household waste.

About 60 thousand people live in the city today. Almost all of them are engaged in woodworking and wood chemistry. In modern Lesosibirsk there are 36 operating enterprises. Their products are exported abroad, in particular to Western Europe. Lesosibirsky woodworking plant No. 1 is the largest producer of lumber and other wood products in the country.





In Lesosibirsk, there are two universities, two vocational schools, a technical school, a museum, a theater, an exhibition hall and five cultural houses.

Conclusion

The city of Lesosibirsk (Krasnoyarsk Territory) is an unusual and interesting settlement. The name itself speaks of the uniqueness of its geographical location. The city stretched along the left bank of the Yenisei. On all sides, its territory is surrounded by green masses of taiga forest.

Lesosibirsk was officially founded only in 1975, although a settlement in its place existed since the middle of the XVII century. Modern city residents are mainly engaged in wood processing. Lesosibirsk can be interesting for travelers. Here you can see several beautiful temples, walk along the unique cedar grove, visit the local forest museum.
