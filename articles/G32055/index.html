The most beautiful places in Rostov-on-Don and the best attractions

Paramonovsky warehouses

The most beautiful places in Rostov-on-Don include Paramonovsky warehouses, which are an obligatory item in the list of attractions for every tourist. The monumental structure now looks more like ruins, but even in this form it is impressive in scale. The walls of the buildings are made of high-quality red brick and look like castle ruins. There are many keys that create waterfalls and ponds. Now it‚Äôs hard to imagine that only a hundred years ago this place was incredibly prosperous. Thousands of people worked in warehouses. The history of the building began in the 19th century. The local port at that time had a huge commodity turnover; wheat and other products were shipped from it to England. The number of shipments grew every day, so the idea came of building a complex of large warehouses on Khlebnaya Wharf.

Buildings were erected in different years: from 1850 to 1980. In the new premises not only grain was stored, but also wool, coal and food. The engineers, under whose supervision the construction was carried out, found an ingenious solution for maintaining low temperatures in warehouses. For cooling buildings used water from local ice-free streams. She was directed through pipes running through the rooms. In such a simple way, it was possible to maintain the optimal temperature for storing products in warehouses, because spring water has a temperature of +9 degrees throughout the year. Now the buildings have turned into ruins.

And yet, even now, this beautiful place in the city of Rostov-on-Don is very popular not only among tourists, but also local residents. It is customary to arrange wedding photo shoots.

Botanical Garden

The list of the most beautiful places in Rostov-on-Don is quite impressive. Among the attractions worth paying attention to the Botanical Garden. In the vast steppes of the region, trees were always appreciated. Therefore, in 1915, after moving to the city of Warsaw University, it was decided to lay a garden in order to be able to study the vegetation of the southern regions of Russia. Thanks to the employees of the establishment, new species of shrubs and trees were distributed. Years passed and the garden became a favorite place for locals. It is difficult to find a more beautiful place to relax and walk. Currently, the park, spread on the banks of the Temernik, is 200 hectares. It is divided into thematic zones, each of which is interesting in its own way. In total, up to 5 thousand plants grow in the garden. It contains an amazing collection of tropical and subtropical flora brought from Australia, Asia, America (North and South). The pristine Azov steppe is also impressive, a section of which has been preserved in the park. The garden has its own museum, where you can admire the huge herbarium and an impressive collection of seeds.

The park has walkways and an ecological health trail. On beautiful clearings set benches for relaxation. Walking along the alleys, you can admire the 300-year-old "dinosaur tree" and petiole oaks. The botanical garden is incredibly beautiful. In it you can not only have a good time, but also take beautiful photos as a keepsake.

Nature reserve

There are beautiful places near Rostov-on-Don. Among them, it is worth paying attention to the Rostovsky Nature Reserve. A unique oasis of wildlife with salt lakes, fields of flowering feather grass, rich wildlife and flora captivates every tourist. Preserved steppes are very valuable for the Rostov region. They are beautiful at any time of the year.

About 400 plant species are registered on the territory of the reserve, among which there are not only rare representatives, but also protected in Russia. The world of animals, insects and birds is no less diverse. Small cormorant, flamingo, eagle owl, pink pelican, steppe bumblebee and others live in the reserve.

The estate Sholokhov M.A.

Of the beautiful places near Rostov-on-Don it is worth noting the estate of the famous writer M. Sholokhov. It is located in the picturesque village of Veshenskaya. The manor is a two-story building, made in bright colors. It is worth noting that it was built in 1942 on the site of an old house, destroyed during the bombing. The beautiful place is unique in its own way, since the furnishings, ancient interiors and every item that once belonged to the Sholokhov family were fully preserved. On the ground floor of the building you can see the dining room, hallway and the office of the writer.

On the second floor there is a large office with access to the terrace and rooms. It was in these walls that the writer once created the famous novel ‚ÄúVirgin Soil Upturned‚Äù and the story ‚ÄúThe Fate of Man‚Äù. Around the house, a garden, a basement, a shed with a glacier and an outbuilding with a Russian stove were restored. Here you can see the writer's cars.

Pavlovskaya fortress

Pavlovskaya fortress is another beautiful place near Rostov-on-Don. It is the first military fortification in the Rostov region, as well as a model of military art. The fortress was designed in the form of a quadrangle, on the sides of which there are bastions. The complex is very beautiful and unique. It was laid in 1697 by decree of Peter I. The place was chosen at the estuary itself, the steep banks of which significantly strengthened the defenses of the fortress.

Dolphinarium

For a family visit, you can also recommend a visit to the Rostov Dolphinarium, which in itself is a landmark of the city. It was opened in 2009. The facility is equipped with three swimming pools, in which performances are held and underwater creatures live.

The building of the complex was built back in the era of the USSR and became the first in the country, as it was designed specifically to accommodate animals. Now everyone can visit the colorful programs with the participation of marine life. In dolphinariums you can also swim with dolphins.
