Section 1. General description of the direction



France is an amazing country of love and romance, which is considered a favorite vacation spot for tourists from around the globe.

"Sights of France" - a photo that is in the family collection of almost every tourist. However, it can be very, very different: someone will be photographed against the background of the famous Eiffel Tower, someone will definitely want to walk along the quiet and narrow streets of provincial towns, and maybe even go to the ocean.

And the country delights with stunning landscapes and monuments, fine cuisine and delicious wine, as well as a peaceful lifestyle.

Sights of France, photos and descriptions of which will be presented below, were not chosen by chance. Often, travelers do not even know about their existence, and numerous tourist booklets provide very scarce information.

But in general, it should be noted that the nature of the country is quite diverse. Here you can see mountain ranges and green meadows, lush forests and calm plains, azure shores and quiet coves. Section 2. The most visited place in Paris: Sacre Coeur Basilica



If you talk about the sights of France, the Eiffel Tower - this is perhaps the first thing that comes to mind. Although, as experienced travelers say, in Paris there are a lot of other, no less, and maybe even more interesting places.

For example, the Sacre Coeur Basilica, built in memory of those who died in the Franco-Prussian war, is one of the symbols and main temples of the state, where faithful pilgrims from French provinces and other states gather.

Interesting information about the sights of France is very often associated with the time of their construction. So in our case: the construction of the Sacre Coeur Basilica lasted more than four decades. Today it is a real masterpiece of architecture. Once upon a time, an unusual mineral mined in the Chateau Landon was chosen for its construction. It becomes white under the influence of moisture. Thanks to this stone, the cathedral has a snow-white appearance.

France ... Paris ... Sights, or rather, most of them, can be seen from the hill on which the basilica is located. From here, a stunning panorama of the entire main city of the country opens. This is a favorite place for walking not only guests of the capital, but also local residents. Especially a lot of visitors on Christmas, when the temple hosts fairs and festivities. The cathedral is striking in its beauty and a huge number of hand pigeons. The main entrance is decorated with two bronze horsemen, and the interior is amazing with a wonderful ceiling mosaic and statues of saints.

Section 4. Normandy



For many travelers, especially those who prefer outdoor recreation, the main attraction of France is not in the capital, but far in the north-west of the country, in Normandy.

These lands are located off the coast of the English Channel, namely from the Bay of Saint-Malo to the Delta of the River Brel. This area is famous for its picturesque landscapes, evergreen valleys and the sky, which during the day takes on various shades - from blue to gray.

The impressionists once loved these places. The great artist Claude Monet lived and created his canvases here . His pink house with green shutters still attracts many tourists.

Normandy is also known for other outstanding creative personalities, including Maupassant, Proust, Flaubert.

The province has a mysterious power and as if created for romance and bohemian life. Normandy is especially popular for its resorts: Deauville, Honfleur, Trouville.

Section 5. Scandola National Park



Interesting information about the sights of France can be found in the southwestern part of the island of Corsica. Why? The fact is that it is here that the Scandola Peninsula is located, which received the status of a national reserve in 1983.

To date, the park is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Access to the territory of Scandola is possible only from the sea and with a special pass. For this reason, tourist visits to the protected peninsula are limited to boat trips along the coastline and photography of local attractions. Excursions are held daily, from June to September.

The peninsula appeared more than 250 million years ago due to active volcanic activity. Tourists are especially attracted by the porphyry rocks, which are millions of years old. The cliffs rise above the sea about three hundred meters and have unusual dark red and purple hues. Sheer cliffs riddled with grottoes.

The sea here is dark blue and crystal clear. Seals, sea turtles, and many species of fish and algae live in the coastal waters of the Girolata and Porto bays. The terrestrial fauna of the peninsula is also rich. There are dozens of rare species of birds, including sea eagles, peregrine falcon and loonie.

Section 6. Attraction for extreme lovers



France is rich and diverse. Paris, the sights of which are known all over the world, is not always liked by lovers of active pastime.

The most courageous and courageous, loving thrill, can without hesitation go to the mountains and take a step into the void. At the highest point of Mont Blanc, the peak of the Aiguille du Midi, at an altitude of about 4 thousand meters above sea level, a glass cabin, called Pas dans le vide ("Step into the Void"), was built. This is the most beautiful observation deck in the world where you can enjoy a beautiful view of Mont Blanc.

The construction of the cabin lasted three years. For the construction, high quality materials and the toughest glass were used, resistant to the most difficult weather conditions and meeting safety standards. At the same time, the authors of the Pas dans le vide project made it possible to make such an excursion to people suffering from acrophobia. To get to the top of Aiguille du Midi, you can use two lifts. Perhaps such a trip will help someone get rid of the fear of heights forever.

The cost of visiting is 55 euros.

Section 7. Tribute to modernity and scientific progress



400 km from Paris, near Poitiers, is the Futuroscope complex. This is a virtual reality amusement park where, with the help of high-tech equipment, special effects and stereo films, you can witness the actions taking place on huge screens. Everything here is dedicated to innovative discoveries and visual technologies. Huge cinema halls equipped with moving chairs, 3D films, a museum of the history of cinema, unusual architecture - all this is the Futuroscope park of the future.

You can admire the beautiful view of the entertainment center from a 45-meter height on La Gyrotour attraction. Inside the crystal of an unusual shape, a huge screen measuring 600 square meters is installed. m. having a height of a 7-storey building. Here is the world's only cinema of the IMAX system, which uses 70 mm film for display.

The dynamic cinema room is designed for thrill-seekers. Armchairs connected to the hydraulic system and a selection of thematic films create the effect of virtual reality. The Coulers Bresil Pavilion, consisting of 9 screens and 9 projectors, provides a 360-degree panoramic view. Here you can independently select the image, being in the center of the system.

For children, a separate playground is organized with various attractions and entertainments. However, Futuroscope is not only an entertainment complex. There are scientific and educational centers where IT-technology specialists are trained and research is being conducted. And in the Palace of Congresses of the park, business negotiations and teleconferences are held.

The park operates year-round. On its territory there is a network of eateries and cafes that offer their guests dishes of French cuisine.
