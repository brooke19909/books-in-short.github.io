Ladoga station in St. Petersburg

The origins of Russian railways are located in St. Petersburg. The northern capital at one time became the founder of the spread in the country of the latest and very promising mode of transport. Despite the fact that many years have passed since the birth of the railway, St. Petersburg is still at the forefront of the innovative movement in terms of expanding and creating new routes of communication, as well as improving their infrastructure. A vivid proof of this is the Ladoga station.

History of creation: Ladoga station, St. Petersburg

Ladoga Station is today a complex transport hub in St. Petersburg. It all started with a small station, the opening of which took place in 1914. Initially, it was called "Yablonovka", as well as the neighboring village. A little later, the station was renamed according to the ownership of the lands owned by Prince Dolgoruky, to Dolgorukov‚Äôs Cottage.





Already in the mid-sixties of the last century, the idea was born of creating a new node on the railway. This thought was prompted by the congestion of existing capacities. However, the plan was not implemented for bureaucratic, political, and financial reasons.

Implementation of the idea

Ladoga station was opened on May 25, 2003. It became the sixth in the city and the only transit railway junction. The Ladoga station was opened on the occasion of the centenary of the Northern capital. However, after the official launch of work on the refinement of some infrastructure facilities were continued. The haste of the events led to the incident with the locomotive (he received damage to the side due to non-observance of the distance between the apron and the extreme rail). However, the youngest station in the city quickly got rid of flaws and became a reliable and safe transportation hub.

Ladoga Station (St. Petersburg) was designed by architect N.I. Yavein. Today, this transport hub, meeting the requirements of high modern speeds, has significant reserve potential.





Specific traits

Ladoga station, unlike other similar structures of the Northern capital, performs a transit function. This refers to the most popular destinations in Murmansk and Helsinki from Moscow. The track capacity of this transport hub is also unique. It consists of an upper and lower level.

Infrastructure

Ladoga Station (St. Petersburg) was built in a difficult urban situation. The small size of the site did not allow the construction of the usual "coastal" type of building. In order to preserve the transit movement of trains at this railway junction, a carefully thought-out three-story building was constructed. It combined the functions of a station-tunnel and a station-bridge. A transport hub can simultaneously accept twenty-six pairs of trains that travel in long directions, and fifty pairs of suburban trains. At the same time, the station is equipped with state-of-the-art electronic equipment. The operation of all equipment is designed to increase safety, comfort, as well as the quality of passenger transportation.





The scheme of the Ladoga station is simple. This complex consists of two structures: the one where local trains stop, as well as the station, designed for long-distance trains. The above buildings are located both on the aboveground and on the underground level.

Lower tier

The underground train station, located in the underground, can be reached by escalator. This moving walkway runs downhill from a subway station above the ground. The suburban station, built at a depth of fifteen meters, is equipped with ticket offices for the sale of tickets. Turnstiles for access to electric trains are also installed here. Not far from them is a waiting room that communicates with the platforms. From the lower tier you can get to the metro station "Ladoga", which is underground.

Middle level





This is a land zone, which is given to city and rail transport. In the area near the station, for the convenience of passengers, bus and trolleybus stops are located. Nearby are taxi rank.

Upper tier

The aboveground part of the Ladoga station greets passengers with its large-scale glazing. From here, you can travel on a long-distance train heading north or east.





An escalator leads to the upper tier of the Ladoga station, which works on the rise. Its beginning is located at the ground lobby of the subway station.

Almost the entire space of the upper part of the Ladoga station is occupied by the Light Hall. Waiting for the arrival of their train, passengers can enjoy beautiful views of St. Petersburg through its transparent dome. In the Light Hall there are ticket offices working around the clock, mail and left-luggage offices, a car rental service and much more. There is a car platform in front of the long-distance train station. It is located 15.3 m above ground level. It can accommodate one hundred personal cars and taxis at the same time.

Remarkable is the architectural solution of the station. The construction of iron and glass is literally striking in its lightness. It seems that it soars above the ground.

At the station, one of the branches of the Baltic Bank operates. In it you can make a currency exchange. Various catering establishments located in the upper tier offer passengers a bite to eat or purchase food before leaving the train.

Planning decision

All three tiers of the station complex are interconnected by stairs and escalators, as well as huge light wells and ramps. The building is literally riddled with passages and transparent elevators, as if soaring bridges and consoles. All this allows you to create a whole gamut of images of the twenty-first century.

In terms of development, the Ladoga station has a rectangular shape. On the sides are two structures with cylindrical shapes. These outbuildings house administrative premises. There is a VIP-hall, as well as a room intended for official delegations, rest rooms for passengers, commandant's office of military communications.

Along the extreme route of the railway is a low block, in which auxiliary services are located. At the same time, this structure serves as a noise shield. This block is separated from the metro by an access area intended for urban surface transport.





To create comfortable conditions for the movement of passenger traffic between the main building of the station and the lobby of the metro station, underground and elevated passages were built to move visitors to St. Petersburg.

Ladoga station has a large track economy. On its territory there are fourteen railway tracks. Eight of them are intended for passenger trains traveling in the suburban and long-distance direction. Six tracks accept freight trains. For rail transport, the middle tier is intended. There are all the ways.

Hi-tech construction

Ladoga Station is equipped with the most modern alarm systems, fire protection, air conditioning and ventilation, as well as lighting, electricity, communications, water supply and many others.

The way to the Ladoga station

You can get to it by metro. This way will be the easiest and most convenient. The station building is adjacent to the Ladoga metro station. Thanks to the crossings, passengers do not even need to go outside. One has only to climb the escalator from the lobby of the subway, and then follow the signs to the Light Hall or go down to the train station intended for commuter trains.





How to get to Ladoga station by land? Trolleybuses Nos. 1 and 22, as well as trams 8, 10, 50 and 64 stop near the complex. More than thirty minibus and bus numbers come from all areas of the city. Directly to the entrance to the upper tier of the station can take a taxi.

How to get to the Ladoga station, if your path lies from the airport? From Pulkovo-1, the route of the 39th bus runs to the Moskovskaya metro station. From it you can drive to the Ladoga. From other railway stations, passengers also get using the scheme of the St. Petersburg metro.
