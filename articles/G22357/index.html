How much is a span? What is the span equal to? What does the word "span" mean?

If we correlate it with other measures of length in Ancient Russia, we get the following picture. A span is the twelfth part of a fathom, a quarter of an arshin or four apex. If we talk about Western measurements, it will be seven inches.

History of occurrence

In written monuments of the history of Russia, a similar term is used for the first time in the twelfth century. However, even its approximate value was difficult to determine until records of pilgrims to the Holy Land were discovered.





In these documents the measurements of the Holy Sepulcher were recorded. Pilgrims, being in Palestine at different times from the twelfth to sixteenth century, called similar numbers. Thanks to this, it was deciphered what the span is equal to. This is how many monks went not just to bow to the shrine, but also to fix it documented to leave data for posterity!

As we will say further, there were three species in all. "Big", "small" and "somersault." The first was about twenty-three, the second was eighteen, and the third was twenty-seven or thirty-one centimeters.





Many people wonder why it is "with somersault." Very simple. This meant that at the starting point we put the end of the thumb, then put at the maximum distance the end of the index finger (it will be about 18 cm). And then, as if continuing to move by inertia, we release the thumb and put the index finger on the side where the nail is. Thus, we measure two or three more phalanxes further. It turns out a kind of somersault.

By the way, the last measure of length was determined by academician Rybakov, studying construction. In Russia, bricks were measured precisely by an inch with a somersault.

Starting from the sixteenth century, it gradually leaves urban use. Remains in the villages (mentioned in the records of the thickness of the snow layer in the fields) and in the religious sphere (the size of the images in the churches was measured).
