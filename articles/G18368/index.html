Features of material for weaving

A true paracord is a nylon cable used in landing troops. According to the military classification, it is divided into several types, each of which is characterized by minimal stretching, tensile strength and the number of threads in the core. Strong, but at the same time surprisingly light, the cord can withstand weight up to 230 kg. The special texture is characterized by the interweaving of many fibers and the content (in some forms) of a strong internal material. For weaving, a hollow paracord is taken. In addition to its intended purpose, this material can be used for the manufacture of key chains, bracelets and other woven products.

Paracord Bracelet Weaving: A Step-by-Step Guide

To create a two-tone bracelet, prepare the following material:

- for braiding - 1.5 m paracord of each shade;

- 0.75 m of cord for the base;

- universal glue (or lighter);

- a needle with a thread;

- scissors.

Perform the work according to the steps below.

  1. Fold the cord for the base twice and tie a knot on the side of the two ends, stepping back from the edge about 4 cm.
  2. Connect the two colored pieces for braiding (workers) using fusion or glue. Suture over the top for strength.
  3. Having placed the working threads horizontally, on top lay the loop of the warp, pulling it up 3-4 cm.
  4. Turn the right side of the working thread horizontally over the warp.
  5. Cross the previous thread with the left end, then slip it under the warp threads and finally pass from the bottom into the loop located on the right.
  6. Pull to the sides of both work threads to form a knot.
  7. Repeat the item in mirror image.
  8. Lead the work, alternating the right and left nodes.
  9. Before reaching about 2.5 cm to the knot on the base, finish weaving and after cutting, melt the remaining cords.
  10. Using the fitting, you can slightly extend or retract the upper loop. After regulation, the bracelet is completely ready.
