The wildest beaches in the world

If possible, go to California, there is one of the most uninhabited beaches in the world - Point Reyes, located in the eponymous national park. To get to the coastline, you have to walk about 5 miles. And for a romantic dinner around the campfire you will even have to get special permission.

There is also a unique place in America, in the state of Florida - Cayo Costa Beach. This is as much as 18 kilometers of the wild beach of the world. To get here is quite problematic, boats and a ferry very rarely go to the island, but secluded conditions are provided.

Do you like islands? Go to Antigua and Barbuda. There are always many tourists and yachtsmen, but few of them go further to Low Bay beach, but in vain. Some 14 kilometers from Palmetto is a beach called Pink. In fact, it is a bulk island, but there are practically no vacationers here.

If you find yourself in Mexico, then Puerto Vallarta is considered a secluded beach . You can get here exclusively by boat, from the bay of Banderas Bay. But the trip to the beach will leave a lasting impression of palm trees and the underwater world.

Another wild beach that can only be reached by boat is Stor, but it is located in Australia and is surrounded by impregnable cliffs, in the territory of Sydney Harbor National Park.

There are still many mysterious and wild places in the world that you should definitely visit.
