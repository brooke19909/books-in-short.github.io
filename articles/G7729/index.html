Tunisia is a small Arab country in northern Africa. Its geographical location, climate and infrastructure allow Tunisia to become one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world. Millions of tourists from all over the world visit the country every year, enjoying its hospitality, the sea, the sun and great landscapes.

Tunisia - oriental tale with a maritime climate

Full name - Tunis Republic. This state received its name thanks to the name of the city - the capital of Tunisia. The country occupies the smallest area in Africa, washed by the Mediterranean Sea. In the neighborhood is Libya and Algeria. Geographically, it is Tunisia that divides the Mediterranean into Western and Eastern. A third of the entire area is occupied by the Sahara desert. Those who like to travel through the desert are attracted by an excursion program that gives tourists the opportunity to get an unforgettable experience from an extreme vacation.



Tunisia has a rich and ancient history. The Carthaginian empire was exactly on this site, the ruins of the legendary city are constantly visited by archaeologists, tourists and researchers from different countries. Later, the country was influenced by Turkey, and from the 19th century - by France. Until now, this country is called "Africa in French" - one of the few African countries that have a special European charm. The country gained independence only in 1956, the republic was proclaimed in 1957. Arabic is recognized as the official language, and the second official language is French. In tourist cities and resorts they speak English and German, sometimes it can be explained in Russian - the staff will understand and, possibly, answer.

Tunisia has a Mediterranean climate, mild - very favorable for the development and prosperity of tourism. This Arab country is very hospitable, the main income Tunisian economy receives from tourism. Sandy beaches, the gentle sea, the sun almost all year, a developed network of transport and hotels of all levels and ranks constantly attract tourists. Since 1961, income from foreign tourism has steadily increased by 30% per year. Then the pace gradually decreased due to political events in Libya, but later the development and growth of tourism resumed. For Russian citizens staying in the country for a period of less than 90 days, a visa is not required. Currency - Tunisian dinar TND, it can be exchanged for dollars or euros at exchange offices located on or near the hotel. If receipts are preserved, the local currency when leaving the country can be changed back at the airport. You can travel inland or around the city by train, bus or yellow taxi. Paid for a trip on the meter, at night there is a night tariff - it is 50% more expensive than the day one.

When traveling around the country, you should follow the usual caution - keep valuables and money in a safe, do not walk with bare shoulders and in shorts, since Tunisia is a Muslim country. Unfamiliar dishes and fruits should be used with caution, it is better to get bottled water, although the local water is of good enough quality - the indigenous people are proud that they can drink it directly from the tap, it is so clean.

National cuisine is distinguished by the widespread addition of tuna; many spices, oils, seasonings, aromatic herbs are used. The cuisine of Tunisia is a peculiarity of the country and a national highlight, it is distinguished by fresh seafood and rare varieties of fish. Almost every hotel offers not only national cuisine, but also European.

Destinations of tourism

From the point of view of travelers, the most attractive and popular in Tunisia are the excursion program, beach vacations and thalassotherapy. The excursion program provides for a real trip to ancient places - here the Romans, Byzantines, Turks, Spaniards and French left their mark. Each of these peoples in Tunisia had its own cities and settlements with the characteristic features of culture and architecture. Many ancient cities with ruins and ruins simply attract researchers and historians. Especially many tourists come to the country to see the ruins of Carthage, the legendary ancient city. A standard tour includes not only the educational part, but also a trip to the Sahara desert.

A beach holiday is attractive for everyone who wants to relax comfortably from the bustle of cities, enjoy the sun and the sea - the whole country seems to be specially created for a fabulous vacation.

Thalassotherapy is gaining in popularity - modern thalassocenters have been built in Tunisia, which are available in almost all hotels and large entertainment centers. All products needed for treatment by the sea are obtained at special local farms and factories, the freshest seaweed comes from the coast, specially purified sea water is piped directly to treatment rooms and pools, and therapeutic mud is extracted directly from its natural location. Diving classes are also popular - for diving enthusiasts there are all conditions, the sea is clear and clean, each major hotel offers not only equipment, but also training courses and instructor services.

Rest in Tunisia - hotels

All possible hotels in Tunisia are slightly different from hotels in Turkey or Egypt. Basically, their construction took place in the 80-90 years of the last century, when land on the country's coast was relatively inexpensive. Therefore, the adjacent areas are large - 15-20 hectares. Hotels, as elsewhere, vary in stardom - categories from 2 to 5 stars are assigned by the Ministry of Tourism of Tunisia. In order to get stars, a hotel must comply with the necessary requirements - to have a certain number of pools and restaurants, a set of services, room size, etc. It often turns out that the hotel only formally corresponds to the declared star rating, and the service between different categories does not differ much. Tunisia 4 * hotels is much better than a 3 * hotel in Egypt or Turkey, but they are inferior to European hotels of the same category. In addition, it should be borne in mind that all hotels in Tunisia, starting from 3 stars, offer an all inclusive - all inclusive. Travelers who used to be in Egypt or Turkey believe that this means round-the-clock service and free services. In fact, in Tunisia, "all inclusive" provides for the opening hours of restaurants - a certain time for breakfast, lunch or dinner. Alcohol is almost everywhere paid, spa centers and procedures too. If the hotel has its own beach, then umbrellas and sun loungers are included in the price, and towels sometimes have to be paid separately. Or leave a special deposit, which will be returned when leaving the hotel. It is worth considering that in the high season there are a lot of tourists, there are practically no free rooms in hotels, and all the free services will have to wait in line. However, one of the most popular destinations is Tunisia. Hotels, prices for accommodation in the country can be very different, for any tourist and at any income level.
