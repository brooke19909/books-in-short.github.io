Kazan-Kirov bus ride: advantages, alternatives, schedule

The length of the Kirov-Kazan route is small by Russian standards. The direct distance between the cities is 315 kilometers, the shortest road - about 450 kilometers, and through Yoshkar-Ola - about 490 kilometers. Those who want to get from Kazan to Kirov or vice versa are faced with the question: what type of transport is optimal? Oddly enough, the Kazan-Kirov scheduled bus may be the best choice, although this vehicle is usually considered the least attractive because of tangible shortcomings.





Bus disadvantages

First of all, this is fatigue. The passenger has to sit all the way, only occasionally stretching his legs at short stops. Although the buses are improving and becoming more comfortable for traveling, there is still not enough space in the passenger seat, there is nowhere to put your legs in, it‚Äôs hard to get a nap. Particularly affected are obese and elderly people.

The noise, shaking, uncomfortable position make hours of travel exhausting, and some people simply can‚Äôt physically endure such transfers, suffering from motion sickness. You can‚Äôt really eat on the bus, it often lacks a toilet, you come across unpleasant neighbors that you have to endure all the way. In short, there are enough shortcomings. But for good reason this type of transport continues to enjoy fair popularity. Sometimes his positive points outweigh the negative ones.





Advantages of the bus

Cost. Most often, bus services attract with their affordable price, compared to other vehicles. For example, the Kazan-Kirov bus, the fare in which is approximately 1000 rubles, is incomparably cheaper than the cost of a plane ticket, a seat in a compartment car, or the cost of gasoline when traveling in your own car.

Oversized luggage. Also, the obvious advantages of the bus include the ability to carry bulk and heavy luggage in the luggage compartment.

Ease of buying a ticket. In order to pay for the Kazan-Kirov bus fare, you do not need to submit documents; this can be done at the ticket office, on the Internet, or even just before the driver leaves.

Nice views. Typically, roads pass through more picturesque places than railways.

Speed. Sometimes a bus gets to its final destination faster than a train or even an airplane. The Kazan-Kirov bus is evidence of this.





Alternatives

A train. Once every two days, the Kislovodsk-Kirov train passes through Kazan, and the Kirov-Kislovodsk flight runs in the same direction in the opposite direction. But this option has two significant drawbacks. The first is travel time: the train makes an impressive detour through Izhevsk, therefore it travels more than 16 hours, which is twice as long as the longest bus trip. The second is the price, which is noticeably higher than the cost of travel by bus. This is especially true for compartments.

Car. It can turn out to be faster and even cheaper than a bus if you go by a large company or find a fellow traveler through Internet services. But not everyone has their own car or friends who will take on part of the transportation costs. Online travel search services are not always suitable for a traveler due to internal reasons: some people are not ready to trust unfamiliar drivers, because you never know in advance what kind of person he is, how he drives, what he is in communication with. In addition, services usually look for travel companions only for the next couple of days, and the place and time of departure or arrival may be inconvenient.

Airplane. The most fantastic and wasteful option. There are no direct flights between Kirov and Kazan, so you need to fly with a transfer in Moscow or Perm. The cost of such a flight looks astronomical compared to a trip by the Kazan-Kirov bus. And most importantly, you can spend more time on such an air route than on a direct bus flight. Therefore, weighing the advantages and disadvantages of the options, correlating the costs of money and time, we can conclude: a bus trip between Kirov and Kazan looks like the most optimal and very reasonable option.
