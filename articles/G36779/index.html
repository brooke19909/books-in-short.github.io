City of Brindisi, Italy: attractions, photos and reviews of tourists

Brindisi is one of the oldest port cities located on the Apennine Peninsula. Currently, it is of interest to tourists in terms of spa holidays and historical attractions. The city has many modern entertainments that are no less in demand than ancient objects.

Brindisi (Italy): description

The city of Brindisi is the capital of the province, in which 90 thousand people live. A beautiful and attractive village is a popular destination for tourists. It is located in southern Italy. There are always a lot of travelers in the city who come here because of the ferry that transfers people to Albania and Greece. It is worth noting that Brindisi (Italy) deserves close attention from tourists, because there are a large number of attractions.

At one time, the city gained fame as a port, which was the gateway to the East. And to this day in Brindisi preserved memorial places that are evidence of bygone historical events.

The city is of interest as a calm and quiet place to relax. How the resort of Brindisi in Italy began to develop not so long ago. And yet, at present, all the conditions for a comfortable pastime are created here. All hotels have equipped beaches, and the infrastructure of the city is so developed that it contributes to a varied vacation.

Tourists are attracted to Brindisi (Italy) attractions, unique Italian cuisine, a large selection of wines and a beautiful coast. The region is considered the largest center of winemaking. Here in every restaurant you will be offered an impressive wine list.

Ancient fortifications

There are so many attractions in Brindisi (Italy) that it is quite difficult to describe all of them. Of greatest interest is the historic city center. To this day, it is surrounded by Aragonese fortress walls, which were built in the fifteenth century by Spanish rulers. Later, the fortifications were rebuilt and changed by King Charles the Fifth. The bastion of San Giacomo, which has recently been restored, is very well preserved. Currently, it is used as an exhibition space. Well preserved bastions of the port of Lecce, Mesagne, Torino Iferno and di Levante.

Swabian castle

One of the most interesting places in Brindisi (Italy) is the Swabian castle, built in 1227 by order of Federico II. It was erected as a defensive structure to protect the inhabitants of the city. The building is a square-shaped building with four towers. The sea covered the castle on one side, and a deep moat on the other. Subsequently, the defensive structure was fortified by Ferdinand of Aragon.

After the departure of the Spaniards, the castle was used as a meeting place. He did not lose his significance under the Bourbons and Savoys. In the twentieth century, the castle became the property of the Italian Republic. During the Second World War, the walls of the castle housed a military base of Italians. And to this day, the magnificent structure is striking in its impregnability. In its appearance, elements of different eras are preserved.

Roman column

The true symbol of the city of Brindisi in Italy is an antique column. Local guides will tell you many interesting stories about it. Researchers believe that once the column marked the end of the famous Appian Way.

It is part of a monumental site that has survived to the present day (it was built during the Roman Empire). For hundreds of years, the convoy was a real reference point for sailors calling at the port.

Santa Maria del Casale

Religious attractions of Brindisi (Italy) include the temple of Santa Maria del Casale, located on the street of the same name. The church was erected at the beginning of the fourteenth century. The construction order was given by Prince Taranto Philip of Anjou.

The temple is an amazing combination of Gothic and Romanesque architecture styles. The building was erected from sandstone. Large blocks of the gray-golden age to this day are the main decoration of the temple.

Tourist house

The city has very unusual sights. Among them, it is worth recalling the House of Tourists. This is the name of an old mansion, recently restored. Currently, the building is actively used for all kinds of celebrations. Photo and art exhibitions, literary evenings and cultural celebrations are organized here.

Other interesting places

The promenade of Brindisi is another attraction. According to tourists, it is worth a visit. On the way to the promenade, you can see many of the city's attractions. On Via Saponea is the Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas, built in the late nineteenth century with the direct participation of Nicholas II. On the opposite side of the street is the Palace of Del Allo, striking a beautiful Venetian facade. Very close to the building is a small square with the temple of Della Pieta.

On Corso Roma, several buildings can attract the attention of travelers. They are made in the style of liberty. This is the school of Perasso and the house of Lisco. And then begins the main pedestrian street of the city - Corso Garibaldi. In ancient times, the promenade was nothing more than a navigable canal. And only in the eighteenth century it was transformed into a street.

City guests can stroll through the beautiful garden located in Piazza Vittorio Emmanuele II. Here you can admire the fountain of the nineteenth century and the monument to Virgil. Not far from the square is the building of the Bank of Italy, erected in the twentieth century, and a sundial of the seventeenth century. No less attractive is the mansion of Dionisi, made in the style of the new Gothic. Two more buildings worthy of the attention of tourists - Via Congregation and Krudomonte.

There are also several attractions on the city's promenade. Here you can see the Virgil Staircase, along which you can climb to the columns of the harbor, as well as several eighteenth-century baroque palaces and the International Hotel, which once hosted the country's government. City beaches

Brindisi is a great place to stay. Here you can relax with the whole family. The region can be safely called one of the best in Italy for family holidays. And the matter is not at all in the institutions, but in the benevolence of local residents. Italians are great guests. Even children willingly communicate with tourists. It is no secret that Italians are famous for their talkativeness and emotionality.

The most popular holiday destination for travelers is the beaches of Brindisi (Italy). The coast of the city is a mixture of rocks and sand strips. The entire province has more than 80 kilometers of beaches. Most of them belong to hotels. But among them you can find very secluded places. These include the "Torre Guacheto", which is a long sandy strip, equipped for a comfortable stay. On the beaches you can not only take sunbathing and swimming, but also engage in water sports, play volleyball.

One of the best stretches of the coast is Lido Risorgimento. The equipped beach has a playground, rental of sports equipment, beach umbrellas and sun loungers. Here you can have a good time in the cafe. The beach is very crowded, so you will not be bored here. This stretch of coast is considered very old.

Lido Azzurro is another good place to relax. A spacious beach can accommodate everyone. The beautiful coastline is designed for swimming in the clear waters of the Mediterranean Sea. If you decide to visit the region in winter, then swimming is possible within the walls of Piscina Marimisti Centro Sport Brindisi. The facility has heated pools that operate year-round. Thanks to the friendly staff, you will certainly enjoy the rest in the center.

City parks

The sights of Brindisi in Italy (photos of some objects are given in the article) include local parks. It is in them that you should visit the whole family. Their selection in the city is quite extensive. One of them is Area Marina Protetta Torre Guaceto. The park boasts deserted beaches and beautiful views. Sandy rocky bays and magnificent landscapes will make your vacation a pleasant one.

In Parco Naturale Regionale Salina di Punta Contessa you can admire the famous lake, which is considered a landmark of the city.

If you want to go in for sports, then you should go to Parco Comunale Cesare Braico park. On its territory there is a large selection of sports grounds where you can play soccer, volleyball or basketball. Here you can also find many playgrounds that impress young guests. The park is very green, and therefore offers family walks on its territory.

Another noteworthy place is Parco Antonio Di Giulio. The park is good for having fun. While parents drink coffee and drinks in one of the bars, children can play on the playground. No less impressive is a walk through the expanses of the park. Its territory is decorated with numerous flowers, fountains, beautiful compositions of trees, in the shade of which it is pleasant to sit in the summer heat.

City Hotels

Travelers will certainly be interested in information about hotels in Brindisi (Italy). There are a lot of similar establishments in the city, since the whole region is focused on tourism. On the territory of Brindisi there are many beach treshki and fours. As a rule, such establishments are built on the first line and equipped with everything necessary for relaxing on the coast.

But you can find city hotels, which are located in old mansions. In addition, the city has more budget hostels and guest houses that provide you with breakfast and bedding. The choice of accommodation largely depends on the purpose of your trip and financial opportunities.

Weather conditions in the region

The weather in Brindisi (Italy) throughout the year can be called comfortable, because the region is located in southern Europe. Summer on the coast is hot and sunny, which attracts many tourists here. Winter in the region is very mild and without snowfall. The beach season here begins in mid-May. In autumn, those tourists who cannot stand the heat come to the coast. At this time, the velvet season begins. The Mediterranean climate of the city is mild. There is always a comfortable weather. In summer and early autumn there is practically no rainfall, and the number of sunny days invariably pleases tourists.

A favorable mild climate and good weather contribute to sightseeing. Ancient temples with stunning architecture and other ancient buildings are worthy of attention.
