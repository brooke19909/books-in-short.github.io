Madeleine Church in Paris and other attractions

Paris is one of the most popular cities in the world. In this city of love, in addition to the Eiffel Tower, there are many other equally beautiful sights. Madeleine Church in Paris is one of the most beautiful architectural monuments of this city.

Construction history

The Madeleine Church is located in the 8th arrondissement of Paris. It is also known as the Church of St. Mary Magdalene. The temple is located on the eponymous square, which is included in the ensemble of the Place de la Concorde.

The construction of the church lasted 85 years due to instability in the politics and economy of France during the late 18th - early 19th centuries. Initially, the construction of Madeleine Church in Paris was supervised by the architect Contant d'Ivry during the reign of Louis XV. Then the construction was continued by Couture, who wanted to take the Pantheon as a basis. During the reign of Napoleon, the construction of the church was carried out by the architect Vignon. Construction was completed in 1842, and the temple was consecrated.

Architecture

Madeleine Church in Paris is similar in shape to a classic Greco-Roman temple. A wide staircase leads to the entrance, and the building itself is supported by massive tall columns in the amount of 52 pieces, and their height is 20 meters. Massive doors made of bronze have murals depicting the Ten Commandments.

Inside the Madeleine church is divided into two parts by semicircular arches. One of them is the lobby, in which there are places for an organ and a small choir. In another part there is a nave. The main difference between the Madeleine church in Paris is that in its interior decoration warm shades alternate, which are expressed in gilding, with cool tones (white marble from which the columns are made).

It is illuminated by natural light that penetrates through casement openings. There are no stained glass windows in Madeleine Church. This creates an atmosphere conducive to solitude and reflection. Also, in addition to natural light, baroque lamps are used. The majestic Madeleine church in Paris is made in the neoclassical style.

The Eiffel Tower

One of the main attractions of Paris and the symbol of France is the Eiffel Tower. Millions of tourists are delighted with her visit. It was designed and built by Gustav Eiffel as an entrance to the World Exhibition, which took place in Paris in 1900.

The Eiffel Tower reaches 324 meters in height and is one of the tallest structures in the world. Tourists can dine in the restaurant, which is located there; take the elevator to the observation deck and enjoy the city view.

The louvre museum

This is one of the most famous museums in the world. The Louvre exhibits more than 300 thousand exhibits. There you can find some of the most famous and great works of art. It was originally the residence of the French kings.

With the relocation of the French court to Versailles in the 18th century, the Louvre became a museum. The first collections were created from the personal acquisitions of Fratsisk I and Louis IV. Constantly in the halls 35 thousand exhibits from a huge collection are exhibited. It is in the Louvre that the famous "Mona Lisa" by Leonardo da Vinci is located.

Notre Dame de Paris

Also known as Notre Dame Cathedral and is one of the main attractions of Paris. This attraction was especially famous after the release of the Hugo novel. Notre Dame de Paris is one of the outstanding examples of the Gothic style in architecture.

Throughout its existence, the greatness of the cathedral fell into decay. But in 1920, the inhabitants of the city carried out restoration to restore its former splendor. Notre Dame Cathedral still delights the French and tourists.

Tourists choose this way of travel also because they have the opportunity to visit not only France, but also other European countries.
