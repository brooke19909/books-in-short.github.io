Embroidery History

Embroidery is a very ancient art of decorating household utensils and clothing. The first embroideries appeared before our era. It's amazing how long people started doing this. In those ancient times, embroidered with wool and even hair. But years passed, humanity developed, and embroidery did not stand still. Gradually, the primitive embroidery techniques became thinner and more beautiful. And nobody used hair instead of threads. Each area has its own patterns, and even certain colors.

There were no patterns for embroidery then, and patterns of embroidery of daisies with a cross and even more so. All patterns and ornaments were invented by ourselves. For example, in China and Japan, it was popular to embroider gold on the finest silk fabrics, but the motifs were mostly natural. In Europe, biblical motifs were often embroidered with woolen threads on hemp fabrics.

Where did you use embroidery before?

The technique of embroidery also changed over time, from chaotic stitches we now see a counting cross. The cross is not just a figure for embroidery. Earlier, and now, he was considered a talisman. That is why patterns, ornaments and charms were often embroidered with a cross. Tablecloths, towels, napkins, curtains were decorated with such embroideries. Also decorated with petticoats and shirts. Each pattern mattered. Usually this is a protection against evil, an evil eye. A blessing or pattern to give strength and courage. On wedding towels embroidered a blessing for the young and a wish for a happy life. Now, ornaments are embroidered only on shirts, but embroidered paintings are more popular in everyday life.

How to make embroidered paintings?

A properly designed picture is no less important than the embroidery itself. There are some very important rules in the design:

  1. The frame should never overshadow the picture itself. She should only emphasize. Since daisies are light flowers, the frame should in no case be black or very dark.
  2. First of all, it‚Äôs worth choosing a frame for an embroidered picture, and not for a sofa, cornice or curtains.
  3. It is important to choose a frame that matches the color or shades of the picture. Compatibility is one of the most important rules in everything. If the embroidery depicts white daisies on a green background, then the frame should have details with the same colors.
  4. Frame and picture size. The balance is important here, small embroidery in a huge frame will not look good.
  5. Each picture needs its own frame. Dressing all the pictures in a room in the same frames is not a good idea.
