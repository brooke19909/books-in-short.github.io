Monastery of St. Gall. Switzerland Attractions

Switzerland is a country that does not have an official religion, but Christianity is the dominant religion in this state. Orthodoxy is represented by a small percentage, while Catholicism (41.8% of the population) and Protestantism (35.3%) make up two large shares.

The perfect example of medieval architecture



Even in our time, a significant part of the population professes Catholicism. Therefore, it is not surprising that so many beautiful old churches and cathedrals founded by supporters of this religion. In the city of St. Gallen is the world-famous monastery of St. Gall, which gave the name to the surrounding village and is the monastery of the Order of the Benedictines. In the Middle Ages, St. Gallen Abbey was one of Europe 's largest cultural and scientific centers. Very ancient, it has been inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List since 1983, which is characterized as "the perfect example of a large Carolingian monastery."

Irish missionaries

The monastery of St. Gall was founded by one of the 12 students of the Irish monk, enlightener and missionary of Columban. The name of the follower Gall. Coming from a prosperous noble family, he devoted himself completely to God. Irish missionaries played a very prominent role in the spread of Christianity throughout medieval Europe. On the way to Northern Italy, Gall fell ill. Columban, considering it cowardice and unwillingness to continue a rather dangerous missionary activity, threw a sick student. Being on Lake Constance, Gall built himself a cell here in 612 in order to remain in solitude and pray. He was offered the bishopric of Constance (615), a city on the shores of Lake Constance, and the post of abbot in the monastery of Lyuksei. He refused everything and died in 627 in Arbon.

First abbot of the monastery

In 1719, on the site of the cell of the righteous, the monastery of St. Gall began to be erected, around which the city of St. Gallen later grew. The first abbot of the monastery was appointed preacher Otmar. He restored the dilapidated cells. Thanks to his efforts, the monastery soon regained its glory as a religious center. Large donations began to be made, and the monastery became a large land owner. The first abbot founded the legendary library of the monastery and art workshops, whose works were famous throughout Europe. There was its own singing school. In the monastery worked major poets and musicians of the Middle Ages. The monastery of St. Gall, through the efforts of the first abbot, also ranked among the saints, eventually turned into the largest Benedictine abbey.





Despite all the wars, the library of the monastery today has about 160,000 volumes. The Song of the Nibelungs is stored here. A unique two-story reading room deserves separate words. It is built in the Rococo style, and above its entrance there is an inscription that is translated from the ancient Greek language as "pharmacist of the soul." The city that grew around the monastery

It should be noted that the capital of Eastern Switzerland, located in the German part of the country, has its own coat of arms, closely associated with one of the many legends about the miracles of St. Gall. It depicts a bear in a gold chain. According to legend, St. Gallus fed a hungry bear and allowed him to bask near his fire. And the beast helped him, as a token of gratitude, build a log cell on the banks of the Steinach River and thus lay the town of St. Gallen (Switzerland). This plot is depicted on an ivory product that dates back to the 9th century. The capital of the eponymous canton, the administrative-territorial unit of Switzerland, the city, is located at an altitude of 700 meters above sea level and is the highest mountain settlement in Switzerland.

World famous St. Gallen textile

Speaking about the achievements of the city, it is necessary to note the successes associated with embroidery and famous lace, known throughout the world. The industry in this city of Eastern Switzerland began with the development of the textile industry. At the time of the emergence of capitalist relations, the textile guild was at the head. From the 9th to the 18th centuries, it was this industry that literally made the city rich.

St. Gallen embroideries and lace are now adornment of products of leading haute couture houses. The highest quality, elite textiles are produced here. A museum of this type of product also belongs to the sights of the city.

The usual history of a European country

The history of Switzerland, of which there is documentary evidence, began with the campaigns of Julius Caesar, that is, from the middle of the first century BC. It is known that before the conquest of these lands by Rome, the Helvetites (the most extensive Celtic tribe) lived here. Therefore, Switzerland has a second name - Helvetia.

After the conquest, Roman colonies were founded in these territories, and in the 4th century the new religion of Ancient Rome - Christianity - penetrated here. Then these lands, like most of Europe, were conquered by both Charles the Great and Napoleon. Switzerland maintained neutrality during the First and Second World Wars, which, for obvious reasons, was strongly pro-German.

The peculiarity of Switzerland

Officially, it is believed that on August 1, 2014, the country celebrated its 720th anniversary. The history of Switzerland as a state began precisely on this day several centuries ago. The most important holiday of the country, the Day of the founding of the state, is associated with an event that occurred in 1291, when the first three communities joined together. Later, Schwitz, Uri and Unterwalden, who entered into an alliance, will be called the cantons, of which there are currently 20 and 6 other half-cantons in Switzerland. These administrative-territorial units are arranged so that, as a rule, people who speak the same language live in them. The borders in which modern Switzerland is located have mainly developed by the sixteenth century. This country is a confederation in which each canton is a state with its own laws, authorities and has the right to hold referenda. On the territory of the country freedom of religion prevails. The democratic system of Switzerland is different from most countries in Europe. An independent policy pursued by the state is also evidenced by the fact that the country has not yet joined the EU.
