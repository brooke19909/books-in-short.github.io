Bali Paradise Travel Reviews

If someone was lucky enough to visit Bali, then consider that such a person was gone. He will only think about where to earn extra money in order to return there. At least, this is exactly the impression when you read the reviews of tourists about Bali. 2013, like the previous ones, was distinguished by an influx of vacationers, and they also left their impressions for us. This exotic place, besides natural beauties, is also distinguished by a calm, cozy and welcoming atmosphere. This is probably why people always want to live like this - peacefully and relaxed. As it happens in Bali.

The beaches of Bali. Tourists reviews

The wide sandy beaches with huge and long waves are a paradise for those who surf. The beaches are clean, quiet and deserted. Of course, there are ebbs with all the ensuing troubles, but this is a problem in almost all countries of this region - Thailand, Vietnam ... But blue water and white sand, as in the advertising picture, are guaranteed to you only if you swim and sunbathe away from party places. And there are a lot of such secluded bays in Bali. Reviews of tourists are advised to talk with locals on this topic - they certainly know where to swim well. They drive there themselves, away from noise and din. Dreamland Beach has been especially praised, where the power and beauty of the ocean is revealed to the delighted guest. Vacationers in hotels write that the quality of the coast depends on who is lucky with the hotel. However, here you can go swimming in the beaches of neighboring institutions - no one is indignant about this. From coasts with developed infrastructure, Sanur is recommended.

Bali Reviews of tourists about the high and "low", spiritual and material



It is no secret that many visit these exotic islands not only with the aim of rest and relaxation, but also to buy things. Moreover, in Indonesia for a couple of hundred euros you can buy two suitcases of things of very good quality, or even well-known companies. However, a large number of people notice that an important factor in choosing a trip to Bali was the desire to penetrate the culture of this people. They did not go to temples to admire bizarre golden sculptures, but they wanted to learn more about Buddhism and its moral values. Many of them complain that upon returning home they lacked not only the sea, sun, sunsets and waves, but also this amazing Balinese culture, smiling locals and their inner warmth.
