Universal pattern: pineapple (hook)

What is a pattern?

The element, which is called the term "pineapple", is used both in a classic form and in a modified form. Improvements relate to its shape, density, size, and patterns that are used to form the individual components of the "pineapple".

Any ‚Äúpineapple‚Äù scheme (a hook is used to create a product) has several main components:

"Pineapples" in flat canvases

This is the easiest way to use the pattern. As a result of knitting, the fabric does not change its shape and dimensions, that is, its width remains unchanged throughout its height.

The specificity of the pineapple pattern is that the paths of its frame form a wave-like pattern. Therefore, creating an even canvas is not such a simple job. It is required to constantly monitor that the "pineapple" scheme is observed. The hook is difficult for craftswomen precisely in that you can imperceptibly cut or expand knitting along the edge.

Pineapple knitted circular fabric

The composition of many schemes with pineapple elements is extremely convenient for expanding paintings. Therefore, it is not surprising that the ‚Äúpineapple‚Äù pattern, crocheted, is so often used for making wide skirts, dresses, flirts, shawls, berets and other types of clothing. As a rule, the expansion of circular canvases occurs with the uniform addition of columns and loops along the width of the entire row. To develop different schemes, various techniques are used:

  1. Increase rapport size.
  2. Expansion of intervals between rapporty.
  3. The gradual formation of new rapports.
  4. Knitting intermediate rows, after which they have a greater number of rapports.

For the pineapple pattern, all of these methods are also applicable. The arc formed by the frame allows you to begin the formation of a new element simultaneously with the completion of the previous one.
