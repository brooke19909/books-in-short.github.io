How to make an accordion out of paper with your own hands?

Want to learn how to make an accordion out of paper? Read - how. The child will also cope with the simplest way, but even an adult can make complex variants in the origami technique only by the pattern. As for the size and appearance of the product, the accordion can be either a very small decorative, souvenir or a life-size model. In a word, here you will find information about options for pursuing children's creativity or hobbies for adults, as well as the possibility of creating attributes for a holiday in school or a preschool educational institution.





Materials and Tools

If you decide to learn how to make an accordion out of paper, then most likely you already have the necessary items for work. Depending on the complexity of the manufacturing method, different things may be required. A sample list is as follows:

  • paper (A4 sheet, roll, printed template) or cardboard;
  • glue;
  • pencil;
  • ruler;
  • paints, felt-tip pens for coloring the product or other decor.

The most important tool here is skillful hands and fingers.

Different ideas

If your child asked you how to make an accordion out of paper, offer him the easiest way by which the child can fold the object himself. If you prefer to work with children, make a more complex pattern. Let the crumbs color the finished product.

If origami has turned into an interesting hobby for you, and you have enough patience, try to implement the most difficult options. They not only look like real ones, but, in general, are unique masterpieces and a great opportunity to show their skills.





If you need to make a natural-sized accordion, you will need colored dark and light cardboard, as well as a creasing tool. Before creating a fold, a guide is drawn on the sheet, which prevents creasing of thick cardboard and gives a smooth, neat line. There are more complex ways where the product is made of several parts with the manufacture of the frame. Both cardboard and paper are used.

The easiest blank option

This product essentially looks like a relief and is made of a small rectangle of paper. The easiest task for children's creativity.





The sequence of actions is as follows:

  1. Cut the rectangle to the right size. Better when the length is much greater than the width.
  2. The kid should make a markup in the form of vertical lines at an equal distance. Moreover, those that need to be folded inward, perform solid, and if outward - dotted or any different hatching or color. So the child will definitely not be mistaken in which direction to bend.
  3. The resulting accordion blank can be used for further creative work. If you want to make a product in the form of a musical instrument, just step back a certain distance to the right and left, and draw buttons and keys in these rectangles.

If the product is created for decorative purposes, you can decorate the upper side with a curly edge made using a special hole punch.

Custom aspect ratio product

Not sure how to make a long accordion out of paper? The scheme does not depend on the length. You just need to take a large sheet and cut off the strip. If there is no appropriate format, it is enough to glue together a few A4 sheets with adhesive tape or with a small overlap with glue. It is better if the seam is exactly the width of the crease.

How to make an accordion out of paper in stages

Let's analyze the sequence of making a paper musical instrument from an album sheet. The scheme of work is as follows:

  1. Lay the sheet in front of you horizontally.
  2. Measure a strip of about 1 cm along the long edge. This will be the seam (glued place).
  3. Fold the sheet in half along the long side. Glue together.
  4. Fold the resulting rectangle in half.
  5. Having departed from the left edge about 5 cm, begin to perform accordion folds. Do them until 5 cm is also left to the right edge.
  6. Carefully open the structure from one edge and blow inward. Straighten the resulting workpiece in such a way as not to damage the creases.
  7. Fold the side elements and draw keys on them.

So, you have learned how to make an accordion out of paper with your own hands. In addition to the usual white sheet, you can take embossed colored paper, and stick the keys from another.

Of course, this instruction shows the implementation is not the most difficult way, but the accordion will look quite realistic. And the child will quickly master the technology. It turns out a good option for children's creativity.

Big accordion for a school event: what you need

To make such a musical instrument you will need the following:

  • Decorative paper or wallpaper.
  • Cardboard (e.g. corrugated).
  • Masking tape.
  • PVA.
  • Glue "Moment" or other quick fix.
  • Pencil, ruler.
  • Clips or clothespins.
  • Details for decoration.

Although manufacturing technology is more time-consuming and will require more time, the materials are almost the same as in the case of a small decorative product.





Instructions for the production of festive props

The sequence of work is as follows:

  1. Cut 4 rectangles from the wallpaper. Take the dimensions you need, for example, 12x40 and 7x40 (two, respectively). Fur will be made from them.
  2. Glue the masking tape on the wrong side of the parts.
  3. Mark the workpieces (strips at an equal distance) along which bends are made.
  4. On internal folds (when viewed from the front side), make cuts about 1.5 cm deep. So that thin wallpapers do not tear further, and masking tape is glued.
  5. For those parts that will be arranged vertically on the accordion (in this example 12x40 cm), you need to make bends and corners from the resulting cuts.
  6. Cut out the rectangles from the cardboard corresponding to the height and "depth" of the accordion (here 12x7 cm). Their number should be equal to the number of folds on the workpieces. It will be a kind of stiffener for bellows.

  8. Do the same with all cardboard rectangles. This will be the top of the bellows.
  9. Also make the bottom side.
  10. When the structure has completely dried out and stuck together, close the resulting "skeleton" with the facade and the back.
  11. Run the side parts in the form of two cardboard boxes of the appropriate size or find the finished ones. These details are also best decorated with colored paper or wallpaper.
  12. For more realism, make on one side the element on which the keys are located. It is easy to assemble it from several cardboard blanks glued one on top of the other (to give thickness), preferably with rounded edges.
  13. Keys and buttons can be drawn or designed as an applique by cutting out of paper. Round elements are easier to make with a hole punch.

The main thing is to understand the principle of creating the structure itself. You can come up with any design.

As you can see, there are quite a few ways to make an accordion out of paper. Choose the one that suits you according to your complexity. Build yourself and teach children. This is a useful and interesting activity.
