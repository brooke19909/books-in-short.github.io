White rock, Abkhazia: photos, reviews. How to get there

Mount White Rock is located in the Crimea and is one of the many attractions of the peninsula. For thousands of years, blown by the winds, it acquired an amazing shape that could not fail to interest filmmakers. Ak-Kai is captured in The Headless Horseman and several other cult Soviet films.



White celebration

Shot from different angles, it resembles an impregnable fortress, if the camera is looking from below, then the head of a cobra blowing its hood when viewed from above at the central ledge. There are a lot of natural caves and faults, canopies and grottoes. And all this is painted white, blinding eyes in the sun. The white rock rises above the valley of the Biyuk-Karasu river (someone calls it Black Water, someone calls it Big Karasevka) by 100 meters, and above sea level - by 325. Of course, most of the names around are connected with the white color of the mountain - nearby there is a village of the same name, the area is called Belogorsky, and the mountain itself is a symbol of the district center.

Ancient temple



The attractive town of Belogorsk is located at a distance of 42 km from Simferopol. The white rock on its western side has aeolian grottoes (indentations blown out by the winds) and two caves - the upper and the lower. The first one is in the center of the western wall. Bob is interesting, such is its name, with its cave signs belonging to the Sarmatians. It is possible that once there was their sanctuary, which was also probably called so: White Rock. How to get to this place? After walking a few meters from the bus stop of the same name, you need to turn onto the first wide street of the village of the same name, which will lead to the ford across the river, but there is a bridge over Karasevka nearby. It is recommended to backpackers in the spring at the ford to stock up with drinking water. Along the entire western wall there is a trail in the middle and the lower cave Bob. The name of the mountain echoes with another place on the territory of the former Soviet Union called White Rocks. Quiet corner
