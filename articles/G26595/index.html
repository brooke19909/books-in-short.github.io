What is Vienna? Whose capital is Vienna? Interesting facts about the city

The word "vein" has several basic meanings. This is the name of a vessel in the body that returns pumped blood to the heart. In addition, Vienna is also the capital of one of the European states. Which one, it will be interesting to know for many.

Meaning of the word vein

Vienna is a feminine noun belonging to the first type of declension. The term is used in anatomy. Veins are blood vessels that pump blood saturated with carbon dioxide from various organs back to the heart. The antonym of the word is artery. The term itself comes from the Latin vena, which translates as “vein”.

In addition, Vienna is a fairly common geographical name. This is the name of the city and county in Austria, a commune in France, as well as one of the Washington metro stations. We offer you to learn more about the capital of the Republic of Austria.

Vienna is the capital of Austria

The capital of one of the most prosperous countries in Europe is located in a picturesque place at the foot of the Alpine mountains. Vienna is located in the eastern part of Austria, on both banks of the Danube (see map below). It was founded by Celtic tribes in the 5th century BC, and in the first century it became an important outpost of the Roman Empire.

Vienna is rightfully considered one of the cultural capitals of Europe. The abundance of Viennese museums, theaters and ancient monuments is simply amazing. The merger of several cultures over the centuries has created a unique atmosphere here, formed the unique architectural appearance of the city.

Vienna is the third residence of the United Nations (together with Geneva and New York). In addition, the headquarters of many other international organizations are located here: OSCE, IAEA, OPEC and others. Today, nearly two million people live in Vienna.

The most interesting facts about the city

To make the story about Vienna as complete as possible, it is proposed to pay attention to the seven most interesting facts about this city:

  • In 2012, Vienna received the status of "the best city to live" on the planet.
  • The oldest zoo in the world, Schönbrunn, is located in the Austrian capital.
  • The oldest newspaper in Europe, Wiener Zeitung, is still published in Vienna. Its first copy was released back in 1703.
  • Waltz was born in the suburbs of Vienna. Today, at least 300 balls are held annually in the city itself.
  • You can safely drink water in Vienna directly from the tap. In the city water supply, it is supplied directly from the purest alpine sources.
  • Croissant (or Viennese bagel) was first baked in Vienna in honor of the victory over the Turks.
  • The capital of Austria has the largest European cemetery, Zentralfriedhof. About 2.5 million people are buried on it. Among them there are also outstanding geniuses of music: Franz Schubert, Johann Strauss, Ludwig van Beethoven.

Vienna is a sophisticated, sublime city, but at the same time very simple. Here you can freely eat a delicious hot dog, perched at the columns of an ancient and pompous building. The atmosphere of this city cannot be expressed in words, it only needs to be felt. That is why hundreds of thousands of tourists come to Vienna every year.
