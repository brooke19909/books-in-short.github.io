The most famous heuristic method is the trial and error method, which consists in the fact that various solutions (samples) of a problem are put forward and considered. Each alternative is examined in detail, analyzed mentally or decided in practice. If it is recognized that this option is unsuccessful, it is determined to be erroneous and a new idea is advanced.

Another heuristic method is the test questions method, which consists in using a list of questions, for example, such:

- How can I simplify an object, object or process?

- What can be regrouped or flipped?

- What can be increased, decreased?

There can be a lot of such questions. But on the one hand, this list should be long so that it is more likely to find the right solution, and on the other hand, it should be short so that it can find an answer in a shorter time and not get confused in a huge number of questions.

The next heuristic method is this called brainstorming or brainstorming. It consists of the following. To solve a problem, a group of people is created from several people to several hundred. I myself conducted ‚Äúbrainstorming‚Äù with the participation of approximately 300 people. At the first stage, various solutions to the problem are generated, including the most unusual and even fantastic ones. The main rule at the first stage is a ban on criticism and the creation of a favorable atmosphere for the influx of new, original ideas. The second stage is criticism and a comprehensive analysis of the proposals made, which, of course, should be recorded at the first stage. After a detailed analysis with clarification of the positive and negative sides of the solution of all alternatives, as a rule, there remain a few of the most optimal ones that can be implemented.
