Hotel Le Bleu Hotel & Resort 5 * (Turkey / Kusadasi / Kadinlar) - overview, description and reviews of tourists

Of all the resorts in Turkey, those located on the coast of the Aegean are best known for their high comfort and clean sea. Le Bleu Hotel & Resort 5 * spread its wide possessions in one of the best resort towns - this is Kusadasi.





Location

This truly amazing place stretches along the azure waters and the cleanest sandy beaches. Once the city was the cultural and historical center of the country, life here always boils in the midst of the summer season and especially captivates young people with a rich nightlife. Families with children choose this place because of their close proximity to major historical sites.

Many national cuisine restaurants offer traditional delights in the form of seafood and famous sweets. In order to have fun, in the middle of the day you should take a walk around the city and go to boutiques with well-known brands, where prices will pleasantly surprise you, and you will be offered a rich and rich coffee in Turkish with an additional bonus to purchase. It is in Kusadasi that you can relax outside the hotel, because life in the city does not stop for a minute and offers a varied pastime for every taste.

Hotel info

Le Bleu Hotel & Resort 5 * is located on the first coastline and consists of the main building with three adjacent buildings. In 2015, there was a complete renovation and re-equipment of rooms for increased modern comfort of vacationers. Everywhere there are balconies with picturesque sea views.

The territory has a good SPA center, many lounge bars where it is nice to start the evening program, a kids club and outdoor pools with water slides. The animation program is different every day, and active tourists will definitely not be bored.

On the territory of the fortress there is a Byzantine tower, which offers breathtaking views of the entire resort and the marina with prestigious yachts. You should definitely go to the ancient city ‚Äã‚Äãof Ephesus for at least one day. Here are concentrated the most important historical sites of the country, protected by UNESCO World Heritage.

The Temple of Artemis is rightfully considered one of the seven wonders of the world. The building is made in the ancient style and attracts high marble columns that rose 20 meters. The main feature was the statue of the goddess, decorated with gilding and jewelry. Until our time, very little has been preserved, but the atmosphere around the temple itself captivates the views of tourists.

The famous Temple of Apollo was conceived as a skillful and majestic phenomenon of ancient Greek architecture. It was built for almost half a century, and it was destroyed in one day by a powerful earthquake. Now antique columns look like an arch, from where a stunning view of the coast opens.

The large amphitheater in Ephesus deserves special attention. The emperors themselves came here, and legends went about the possibilities of the theater stage. The theater was built during the heyday of the Roman Empire and accommodated up to 25 thousand spectators. The construction is well preserved to our days, which will allow us to most reliably estimate the scale of the amphitheater.

The house of the Blessed Virgin Mary attracts many tourists. This is the oldest place with olive trees, where water supply and an underground atrium were discovered. There is a wish wall and vaulted arches, creating a special atmosphere of the architectural complex.
