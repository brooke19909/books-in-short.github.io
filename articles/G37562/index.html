White Lake (Ulyanovsk Region) is a kind of historical monument, because it originates from the time of the ice age. His age is more than 250 thousand years. It was not for nothing that they called him that, the crystal-white sand and the mirror-like surface of clear water fully justify the sonorous name. The nature here is very reminiscent of the Scandinavian countries, it is a world of peculiar vegetation and amazing purity. The air here is truly magical, it fills with strength, vigor and good mood.





Lake dimensions

Its water area is amazing and fascinating. By area, White Lake (Ulyanovsk Region) covers 96 hectares. Its width is 0.9 km and a length of 2 km. Depth varies depending on the time of year and is about 8 meters. Full visibility of the bottom remains at a depth of about 2 meters. Constant water supply is provided by atmospheric and groundwater. It is called self-cleaning, sandy soils and unique microorganisms serve as effective natural filters.

Surrounding nature

The resort area was founded not only due to the magnificent pond. White Lake (Ulyanovsk Region) is located next to a huge array of mixed forest. Its main part is made up of centuries-old oaks and conifers. They not only protect the environment and saturate the air with oxygen, but also contribute to the active healing of nature. Trees are involved in important processes such as regulation of heat and moisture, oxygen production, ionization, and the release of volatile products. If you compare these places with the famous resorts, the White Lake (Ulyanovsk Region) surpasses the famous Kislovodsk in its healing properties. This has been proven by many research scientists.





It is the combination of all these factors - a self-cleaning pond, coniferous forest, sandy soils and therapeutic mud - that creates the conditions for the successful activity of the famous health resort, which is located on the lake. It supplies the establishment with dirt, which has excellent thermal properties and contains a large number of microbes, vitamins, enzymes, hormones and organic substances.
