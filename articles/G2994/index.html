Cultural, historical and natural sights of the Tver region

Tver region has an outstanding natural and historical-cultural potential. This is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Russia. Its historical heritage is unique. The beauty of the nature of the region is fascinating, and the abundance of historical and cultural monuments makes it interesting and attractive. Sights of the Tver region are directly related to the history of the region, with its outstanding and famous people who lived on this earth at different times.

Historical past

No wonder the Tver region is called the heart of Russia. One of the most ancient Russian cities - Tver - has been known since 1135. The great Principality of Tver that arose, being the main center for the creation of the Russian state, existed as an independent state entity until the end of the 15th century. A sufficiently rich and glorious historical past, saturated with interesting events, the modern life of the region was reflected in numerous historical and cultural monuments of history. Despite the significant variability of urban and rural landscapes, among the new life as living islands of history, there remain ancient sights of the Tver region, revealing the beauty of the architecture of Ancient Russia. These are windmills, wooden churches, Slavic mounds dating from the XII-XIV centuries, and many others.

Historical monuments

The national shrine of the Tver land is Nilova Deserts. This is the most famous monastery in the region, founded in the 16th century. The region is also famous for manor culture monuments. So, having been in Torzhok, it is worth visiting the estates Mitino, Nikolskoye, Rayek, recognized as masterpieces of Russian provincial classicism. Noble nests of various sizes and architecture are represented in other areas of the region. These are the estates of Podgorodnee, Kuropatkin and Mikhailovskoye in the Toropetsky district, the estate of the architect A.S. Khrenov in the Bologovsky district and others. The Gornitsa estate located in a small village of the Kuvshinovsky district is interesting for visiting. Here you can see the well-preserved unique stone Vladimir Church.

In Tver itself, among the architectural masterpieces, it is worth highlighting the Tver Imperial Track Palace, built in the 18th century and designed in the style of classicism with the inclusion of Baroque elements. Its main purpose was to provide rest for members of the emperor‚Äôs family along the way from St. Petersburg to Moscow. Now the palace houses the Tver Regional Art Gallery, called the main treasury of the city.

The track palace is located on the territory of the former historical center of the city - the Tver Kremlin. In the City Garden you can see the preserved ruins of the castle moat. The Kremlin itself after the last fire in 1763 was no longer restored.

Among the majority of regional centers in the Tver region, the pretty town of Kimry stands out with its architectural appearance. This is the most striking example of a "town of provincial Russian Art Nouveau."

Museums in the Tver Region

Many interesting museums can be visited when traveling around the area. In Tver itself, this is the museum of M. E. Saltykov-Shchedrin, opened for the 150th anniversary of the satirical writer in the mansion where he lived, holding the post of vice-governor of the city. This is the Tver United State Museum, opened in 1866. Its unique expositions tell about the history, culture and nature of the region. As well as a museum of Tver life and a museum of military traditions, the expositions of which are collections of ancient Russian weapons of the 4th-16th centuries.

The only Museum of Natural History in the region is the Museum of Nature of the Seliger Region, opened in 1986.

In the village of Emmaus, not far from Tver, is the V. A. Serov Memorial Art Museum, a representative of the art of socialist realism. The basis of the expositions are genuine works of the master, telling about his life and career.

In Torzhok, the Open-Air Architectural and Ethnographic Museum and the All-Russian Historical and Ethnographic Museum deserve special attention.
