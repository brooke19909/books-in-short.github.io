Etymology of the word



As in most cases, there is no exact interpretation of the origin of the term. Cicero allowed the formation of the name from the word penitus - living inside. Therefore, Roman poets, in contrast to Russian, in which the term was also incredibly popular, but in a different interpretation, often called these deities Penetrales, or "penetrating." Penates are home deities that are part of the retinue of the goddess Vesta, the guardian of the hearth. According to legend, the second king of Rome, Numa Pompilius, built in the city the first temple of the goddess Vesta. Its hidden central part is called Pen (from penetralia - the internal secret compartment of a house or temple). It maintained eternal flame, and state Penates were kept. The theory of the origin of the name of the gods from the heart of the temple of Vesta is quite acceptable.

Guardians of the Empire

One way or another, but in ancient Rome, the Penates are not only the gods of the home, but also the guardians of the state and the entire Roman people. In the Latin version, this thought was as follows: Penates Publici Populi Romani.

The history of state Penates in ancient Rome is not known for certain. The uninitiated knew little about them; their keeping was shrouded in mystery. It was believed that they were brought by Aeneas from Troy. And what they were, only the priests and vestals - servants of the cult of Vesta knew. But it was believed that the state Penates are the most important shrines of Rome. They guarded the empire and served as a guarantee of prosperity and peace of the whole people.

Lovely, family, home ...



But at the household level, home gods were loved and revered. Clay and wooden images of them were stored in a separate cabinet located at the hearth. It has already been noted that the cult of the Penates was identified with the deification of the ancestors. The housekeepers who care for the welfare of the family, they were dead parents or simply made idols, all united under the understandable name of "native Penates." It is not surprising that the brilliant Russian artist, representative of the realistic school of painting, Ilya Efimovich Repin, having decided to build a manor that would become a family nest, named it after the ancient Roman guardian gods and depicted their figures on the gates of the estate. In 1899, already a famous painter, I. Repin buys a plot of land 50 km from St. Petersburg, erects a house and carefully equips the territory. The choice fell on the village of Kuokkala because since 1898 Ilya Efimovich Repin served as the rector of the Academy, he had plenty of responsibilities. Getting from the village to the duty station was quite convenient thanks to the railway line, and the place itself was secluded and quiet.

Deserved title

Everything turned out as planned: a shelter, where you are always attracted to return, where there are many loving relatives and friends, where success always accompanies you. Soon after the family was justified, the owner received the title of professor and workshop leader at the Academy of Arts. Manor Repin "Penates" fully justified its name. The great painter lived here for 30 happy years.

This was the real house of the artist, he loved every corner here, and each corner had its own name. I. Repin lived here until his death, which happened on September 29, 1930. He was 86 years old. According to his request, he was buried here, on the estate, next to the house and Chugueva hill. The village of Kuokkala was renamed Repino, and now there is the Penates Museum, well-known to every Russian, which was founded in 1940.

Modesty and democratic morals of the estate

Unfortunately, during the war the house burned out completely, the entire estate was damaged. Remained oak, planted immediately after the funeral. After the war, the estate was completely restored, and the new museum opened in 1964. Now it is a federal architectural monument. You can talk about him for a very long time. All the most famous and best people of that time visited the estate of the artist.
