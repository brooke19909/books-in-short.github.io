Raiffeisenbank offices in Moscow: addresses, opening hours, telephone hotline

Raiffeisenbank Austria was registered in 1996 as a bank with 100% Austrian capital. The Central Bank license was issued in 2001, when the institution was transformed into a closed joint-stock company. The history of the Raiffeisen group begins in the middle of the 19th century, when the first mutual aid funds appeared, supporting peasants during the famine. The founder of the group was Friedrich Wilhelm Raiffeisen.

Branch network

The first Raiffeisenbank bank in Moscow appeared in 1996. The merger with Imexbank turned the organization into the largest foreign bank in the Russian Federation. The reorganization procedure was completed in November 2006. And already in December, the bank began to provide its services to customers under the Raiffeisenbank brand. Branches in Moscow, St. Petersburg, Nizhny Novgorod, Khabarovsk today serve 12 thousand corporate clients. In 2013, the credit institution merged its ATM network with UniCreditBank, and after 3 years - with BINbank. So more than 3 thousand ATMs are available to cardholders. The whole network has 200 separate divisions, including additional (98), operational (86), credit and cash offices (6). 9.5 thousand people are engaged in customer service .

In the product line there are also "gold" cards with additional privileges, a high limit. For all service issues, you can contact the offices of Raiffeisenbank CJSC. Remote service

You can use the services not only by contacting Raiffeisenbank‚Äôs offices in Moscow or any other city, but also by connecting remote services: R-Connect Internet, R-Mobile mobile or R-Control. Internet banking was developed in 2002, and the mobile application was released in 2013. Through remote services, you can manage the balance of the account, pay for services and pay off accounts payable. To connect the service, you do not need to look for Raiffeisenbank's offices in Moscow or in any other city. It is enough to indicate the details of the current card on the official website and get a password to enter the "personal account" in the form of SMS.





Achievements

The Internet banking system was updated in 2014. New operations were added to the list of standard operations for transferring funds between accounts: opening accounts, deposits, transferring funds to cards of other banks, working with templates, cost accounting service. Connection to the service has become available through an ATM.

In addition, you can connect R-Connect or change the password to enter the system in just a few minutes at any Raiffeisenbank ATM. Moreover, the Internet service has become free. These updates have led to an increase in the number of customers who have made non-cash transactions: inter-card (including foreign exchange) transfers, currency exchange, and operations with deposits. Convenient functionality customers quickly appreciated. Approximately 30% of users have installed the mobile app. Of all online transactions, 27% were conducted through a mobile application.





SMS banking has also been finalized. Clients have the opportunity to transfer funds between cards and pay for communication services using SMS commands. The average transaction amount for 2015 amounted to 4 thousand rubles.

Restructuring

The decision to reduce the regional network was caused by low development indicators for 2014. Net income declined (as a result of the acquisition of Impexbank) by 62%. The amount of funds in accounts increased by 17%, and for individuals - by 19%. It is important for the bank to remain super profitable. Mortgage, consumer loans and cards are more promising areas of development, according to analysts at Raiffeisenbank. ATMs in Moscow now include not only their own branch network, but also ATM UniCreditBank and BINbank.

The development strategy of a financial institution also provides for the refusal to provide car loans. According to bank analysts, this area is unprofitable in the long run. New contracts have not been opened since March 2015. In all new branches, this service is not provided at all. Terms of customer service under the old contracts have not changed.

According to the restructuring plan, in 2015, branches were closed in the Irkutsk, Volgograd, Chelyabinsk, Ivanovo, Kirov, Belgorod regions. At the same time, Raiffeisenbank's offices were opened in Moscow (6 new branches) and in the Far Eastern regions with a high demand for banking services - Magadan, Vladivostok, Yakutsk, Khabarovsk, Blagoveshchensk and Petropavlovsk.
