The symbolic meaning of deer on embroidery patterns

Each embroidered image on the fabric has its own special meaning. Since Slavic times, women quite symbolically approached the choice of drawing on the canvas. It is not surprising that one of the favorable images of animals was a deer. Therefore, many needlewomen choose it now for their creative work. After all, it is not without reason that deer embroidery patterns remain popular even in our time. You wonder why?

Mythology of the Slavs

The image of a deer played a special role, because the animal was classified as regal. And all because he was considered the personification of the ancestors. In mythology, deer carried the souls of the dead to another world.

Through the resemblance of horns to branches, they were called the Tree of Life. In addition, deer symbolized the sun's rays, bringing abundance to the whole earth.

This animal was revered by mothers and daughters, giving birth to all life in this world. They were depicted on canvases in the form of horned female figures and decorated the homes of the Slavs.

In the modern world, deer embroidery patterns for needlewomen have become interesting in that they are associated with sunrise, the renewal of all life, rebirth, spirituality and communication with ancestors, as well as unborn children.

Symbolism in Christianity

It is no accident that the deer personifies religious enthusiasm and zeal. Quite often, this animal was depicted on baptismal fonts in the form of a drinker at the foot of the cross.

Interestingly enough, the emblem of Christian iconography was precisely the deer trampling the snake - the one who fights evil.

It is worth paying special attention to the patterns of cross-stitch deer, those needlewomen who will embroider this image for themselves and their families. This is important for the spiritual connection between departed ancestors and unborn children.

In addition, the deer is one of the main symbols of Christmas and New Year.

It is also interesting that already in the modern world people began to personify deer with a career ladder, as the animal is filled with strength, aspiration and superiority over itself, which is important in work.
