Hotel Isis Hotel SPA (Tunisia / Djerba): reviews, ratings, photos

More and more domestic tourists have the experience of relaxing on the large (514 thousand km 2 ) resort island of the Mediterranean Sea, called Djerba and belonging to Tunisia. It is known to be located off the coast of Africa. Its fertile territory in the ancient world historically passed from Carthage to Rome, then to the Byzantines. To this day, the island is connected to the mainland by the bulk road built by the Romans. It is now used as the main transport route of the island.

Later, in the Middle Ages, the island became the subject of territorial disputes between Arab pirates, Normans and Spaniards. There was a bloody struggle for it, in which the Ottoman Empire took over.

Nowadays, Djerba is an integral part of Tunisia, which has been actively developing its tourism industry since the end of the 20th century. The local mild Mediterranean climate (average annual temperature ranges from 21 to 27 gr.) Is ideal for spa holidays.

It is in the north-east of the island, in its best resort area, where huge palm trees with an impressive crown adjoin a wide sandy strip of beaches, and the turquoise sea is perfectly transparent, among the dozens of modern hotel complexes, a four-star hotel is discussed, which will be discussed in this article, - Isis Hotel Spa.





Holidays in it (as in most hotels on the island) are preferred by fans of health tourism. The high tourist season lasts from April to November. Well-known in the world centers of talassotherapy (rejuvenation) operate, practicing medical technologies using sea mud, water, algae.

Guests of Hotel Isis Hotel Spa in their reviews often list prices relevant to the island as shopping recommendations. After only being guided in them, you can effectively bargain in local markets. Tourists who visit the Hotel Isis Hotel Spa celebrate excursions to the original Arabian city of Houmt Souk, the remarkably preserved Ottoman fortress of Bord el Kebir, one of the oldest religious buildings in the world - the El Ghrib synagogue, the ethnographic and entertainment complex Djerba Explore, the museum of pottery products in the village of Guellala.

Shopping

Guests of Isis Hotel Spa 3 * (Tunisia) in their reviews often summarize on the topic of shopping. From them you can find out that the best shopping on the island is possible in the markets of the old city of Houmt Souk. It sells handicrafts that Tunisia is famous for: Arabian carpets, handmade jewelry, ceramic products, and national clothes. The second famous market of the island is Midoun.





For successful market shopping, it is important to navigate the schedule and working hours of various markets (there are about a dozen of them). For example, Houmt Souk is open on Monday and Thursday. Summarizing, we say that shopping on Djerba is distinguished more by market impromptu than by brand exclusivity.

Perhaps the only store competing with the markets, besides duty-free islands, is the Al Jazeera fixed-price supermarket.
