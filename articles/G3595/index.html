Pattern pillow toys (cat) for travel

Pillows in the form of animals sew from a variety of materials. In fact, any pieces of fabric available on the farm will do. Even from small shreds you can create a toy using the patchwork technique.

Patterns of pillow toys are very simple. You can draw them yourself based on your own preferences and the desired size of the product. This does not require special knowledge in cutting or drawing skills.

How to make a pillow-toy pattern?

First you need to come up with a design and choose the shape of the future product. For beginners, it is better to restrict ourselves to basic geometric shapes and create an animal from them, such as a cat, as in the image below.





As you can see, the details of the pattern of pillow toys (cats) will be elementary, and additional explanations of the process of cutting and sewing will not be required. Further in the article will be given a master class on making a more complex version of a travel pillow-cat.

Pattern Details

Those who fly airplanes know how uncomfortable economy class seats are. In airport stores, there are always on sale special pillows in the form of bagels that are worn around the neck for a comfortable sleep in the cabin. Often they are made in the form of animals. To build such a pattern of a pillow toy is a matter of 5 minutes, but it will bring a lot of joy to the child, remind you of the house on a long journey and allow you to relax in comfort.





Details on the left are absolutely identical to details on the right, but for clarity of the sewing process, the toy will be cut out from two pieces of different fabric. The pattern must be cut out of paper or special tracing paper. Oversizes at the edges of the parts can be done more if desired.

Choosing a fabric for the cat

Material for home-made toys can be any trim and remnants of fabric. Even a toy made of chintz, due to handmade work, will look unusual and stylish.





When creating a pattern of children's pillows, toys, it is necessary to think through a combination of patterns and textures, fabrics used in advance. Preference should be given to bright, natural and soft materials. As a filler, it is better to use synthetic winterizer and other artificial fillers that do not cause an allergic reaction in children.

How to sew a pillow toy (cat) for a child?

Details of the pattern are laid out on fabric cuts and outlined with tailor chalk or pencil. The lines should be clear and neat. According to the cut, the cat will have a multi-colored body and ears. It is necessary to apply the contours of the allowances, for greater convenience in work.



All edges of the fabric pattern must be processed on an overlock or in any other accessible way. The body parts are folded facing each other, fastened with sewing pins and sewn with a sewing machine, leaving only a small portion of the seam unfinished to turn out and fill the pillow. The ears and tail of the cat are stitched in the same way.





The torso and tail are turned on the front side and filled with synthetic winterizer or cotton. Stuffing the body should begin with the paws and head. For convenience, you may need a long stick or pencil. Then the seams are carefully completed manually from the front side. You need to try to make the stitches as inconspicuous as possible. It is better not to fill the ears, but simply turn them out.





Then you can sew on the ears and tail of the toy. At this stage, it is not necessary to make the seams invisible. A little "sloppy" seam will give the cat a special charm and indicate the manual performance of the toy. On the belly of a cat, you can embroider the name of the child or attach a small label indicating the home address and phone number of the parents.
