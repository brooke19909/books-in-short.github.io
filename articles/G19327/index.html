A brief excursion into history

Useful and tonic properties of coffee have been known for a very long time. But the first coffee maker was presented at an exhibition in Paris only in 1855. And of course, it was completely unlike those household appliances that everyone was used to seeing in their kitchen. It was something very large, similar to a boiler furnace. The machine was controlled by 2 people - a fireman who threw coal into it, and an operator pouring ground grains and monitoring the pressure. The work of the second was more responsible. After all, he ignored the pressure gauge, the car could simply explode. At the same time, the unit was able to produce up to 1000 cups of invigorating drink per hour.

Already much later, in 1974, having gone through many modernizations, units of a more familiar form began to appear in the houses. And, despite the assertion of conservatives that coffee should be prepared in cezve, experts have proved that some types of coffee machines brew this wonderful drink no worse. In addition, the process is almost completely automated.

What is the difference between a coffee maker and a coffee machine

For the majority of people, these are synonyms, but this is not so. A coffee maker requires more human involvement. It is necessary to grind the grains, pour a certain amount into the apparatus, pour water. The coffee machine frees the owner from many actions. The grinding here is automated, many units are even able to remove the spent powder in a separate container, which saves the owner from daily cleaning of the apparatus.

Let's consider what types of coffee makers exist and what are their features.

What types of similar units can be found on the shelves

Coffee makers help a person in the process of preparing a drink, but do not relieve him of some trouble. Today, the manufacturer offers the consumer the following types of them:

  • Drip - with the help of a special heater, water turns into steam, which, having cooled slightly, passes through the ground coffee poured from top to bottom. After that, the finished drink enters the cup at the exit. The horn devices are similar, but in them the powder is pressed into a tablet, and steam passes through it under pressure, which allows you to brew a more saturated and aromatic coffee.
  • Geyser - steam comes from below, unlike the previous version, and, passing through the grinding, it enters a separate tank. These are usually placed on the hob.

There is still debate about which class the capsule unit belongs to. On the one hand, this is a coffee maker, on the other - a machine. In today's article, she will also be assigned her place.

Some tips for choosing a similar device

When purchasing a coffee machine, do not grab the first one you like from the counter. Experts advise spending at least 2-3 days on your choice. And you need to start it not in the store, but at home, with your family. First you need to clarify the preferences of everyone who will use the unit, then decide which functions are necessary and which ones you can refuse, so that the cost is lower. When these issues are resolved, you need to look at suitable models on the Internet, compare prices, read reviews. Acquaintance with the review of models of coffee machines is a very important process. Only in this way it becomes clear which design of the device will fit better into the interior of the kitchen. This lesson can be spent more than one day, because the range of such equipment is really huge.

As a result, the buyer should not only have a clear understanding of what the unit should be. You should go to the store with 3-4 models in your head that were chosen at home. One of them should be purchased. The main thing here is not to listen too much to the words of the sales consultant. Often these people themselves do not understand everything in technology. Their task is to sell a certain model, which will be painted by the buyer as ideal. Unfortunately, more often than not, they manage to confuse the consumer and ‚Äústeam‚Äù him something that does not at all correspond to the chosen functionality and even the type of coffee machine.

After the previously selected model is found on the counter, it is necessary to check with the sales assistant whether there is a certificate of conformity on the device and it is necessary to familiarize yourself with it. The absence of such a document should lead to a rejection of the purchase. The certificate, by law, is provided to the buyer upon request. Of great importance is the warranty period. There is an interesting point here. Some stores intentionally do not put a sale date on the coupon. This leads to the calculation of the warranty period from the date of manufacture, not purchase.
