General Provisions of Marxism

In answering the question of what revisionism is, the philosophical, economic, and political teachings of Marxism cannot be circumvented. This is necessary to understand what the revisionists did not like, why they wanted to thoroughly, thoroughly review the theory prevailing in the circles of social democrats at the end of the 19th century, developed by Marx and Engels.

3 provisions are key to understanding the basics of this system:

  1. Surplus value.
    The cost of goods, according to Marx, is determined by the amount of labor invested in it. This factor is the key. To better understand this phenomenon, he introduces the concept of surplus value - the difference between the new value created in the labor process (profit, rent, taxes, etc.) and materialized value (raw materials, employees' salaries, materials, etc.). In a capitalist economic formation, surplus value is appropriated by capitalists - the owners of the basic means of production.
  2. Materialistic understanding of history.
    History, its course obeys the objective laws of nature, socio-economic relations that have developed in society. The role of leaders, kings is minimal. The study of objective laws allows you to make certain predictions.
  3. Dictatorship of the proletariat.
    Everything is extremely simple here: it is the proletariat, as the main producer of material assets, that is obliged to take control of the basic means of production. Voluntarily capitalists will not give up their dominant position. Here, a revolution will break out, the main purpose of which is the complete elimination of the unjust social order, and at the same time some of the remnants of the past, which hinder the whole of humanity from rushing into the bright future. For revolution is possible only on a global scale.
