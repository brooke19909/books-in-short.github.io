The protest movement in Russia: the origin and the present

After the elections to the State Duma of the VI convocation, a protest movement began in Russia. The protesters did not agree with the results of the elections, argued that the Duma should be recognized as illegitimate, since it does not reflect the interests of most citizens.





The concept

Protest movement - multiple massive anti-government movements of citizens. In the world in recent years, many countries have been covered in which they occurred. Some led to revolutions, others did not achieve their goals. However, almost all of them follow the same scenario. More on this later.





Protest Movement Scenarios

The protests in the Middle East and the CIS look approximately the same:

  1. First, elections are held in which any violations are recorded - In Russia, they were held on December 4, 2011.
  2. They are won by the current political forces - the president or the ruling party.
  3. It turns out the people, dissatisfied with these elections. At this stage, there is active propaganda using reports of all violations in the elections. People are encouraged to think that voting should be invalidated.
  4. Leaders and a symbol of the movement are chosen - In Russia, a white ribbon has become a symbol, but our opposition has not decided on a common leader.
  5. Next, the current government is removed from management, the appointment of people under control in the election commissions, central media, and regulatory bodies.
  6. There are new elections in which the "right" people win.

Our country does not know the last two points in recent history. It is noteworthy, but before the election, no one makes a fuss about the fact that the upcoming elections will be illegitimate. On the contrary, they are silent about the situation, fixing any minor violation. The purpose of election observation is not to build a civil society, to help establish an existing system, but to consciously use violations for future campaigning, to create an image of the illegitimacy of the power that won them. Although the number of violations, as a rule, does not affect the overall voting result.

The protest movement in Russia in 2017





In 2017, there was again a surge in anti-government sentiment. The experience of previous years was taken into account. This time, the organizers refused the call "For Fair Elections" and set as their goal "the fight against corruption." The reason was: the Anti-Corruption Foundation published a film that immediately caused shock in Russian society.

A. Navalny exposed Prime Minister D. A. Medvedev himself, accusing him of creating illegal schemes with state transfers, the damage from which is estimated at hundreds of billions of rubles. The oppositionist applied all known methods of influencing the mass consciousness: he voiced the problem of poverty, insufficient assistance to sick children, problems in the public sector, etc. Mass anti-corruption rallies were held in almost all major cities. The protest movement in Russia currently has a number of features: ‚Äústreet walks‚Äù, flash mobs are used, and minor children are involved.

The origin of the protest movement in Russia (1905-1907)

Let's delve into the story. The birth of the mass protest movement in Russia began after Bloody Sunday, January 9, 1905. A demonstration of many thousands was shot, after which the whole country plunged into revolutionary chaos. Then the state was able to resolve the situation for some time, introducing some political concessions, but in 1917 the authorities signed their powerlessness - the Great February bourgeois revolution took place.
