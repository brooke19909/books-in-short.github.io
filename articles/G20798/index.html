Working conditions when creating batik

Even with the best fabrics, special colors and reserves, it is impossible to obtain beautiful and durable products without proper work organization. The room for creating batik must be bright, ventilated, dry and heated. For work, you will need a convenient table and a large hoop or stretcher, in which the fabric will be fixed. Matter should be well stretched, as sagging can disrupt the outline of the pattern. As a rule, a wet cloth is pulled on a stretcher, and after drying it is well straightened. For painting, you must have a set of tubes (for cold batik), special tubes with a reserve, brushes, patterns, forms for paints, a container for dyeing fabric. For "hot" processing of the material you will need a special tool "chanting". As chemicals that give tissues special effects, you may need alcohol, salt, and urea.
