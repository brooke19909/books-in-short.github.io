Scientific worldview

There are different forms of worldview. The most ancient are religion and mythology. These types of worldviews were a fantastic reflection of reality, which was formed in the minds of ancient people.

However, there is a view of the world, society, nature and everything that surrounds people and happens inside every person who does not accept prejudices and superstitions. The scientific worldview is entirely based on the achievements of modern disciplines and is imbued with the method of scientific research and knowledge. This view of the Universe and the place of man in it reflects phenomena and objects as they exist in reality, without errors, lies and fiction. The scientific worldview is based on the achieved level of knowledge in all sciences.

There are a number of signs that distinguish this view of things from other views.

Mainly, the scientific worldview is an explanation of the facts, comprehension of them in the whole structure of concepts of the corresponding scientific discipline. It reveals the regular and causal relationships of objects and phenomena. The scientific worldview perceives randomness as necessity, and for the single, the general. In addition, it provides anticipation, anticipation of events, as well as disclosure of the upcoming phenomena and processes.

A very significant feature of the scientific worldview is its systematic nature. By this definition is meant a complex of ideas, ordered in accordance with certain theoretical principles.

The generalized system of human knowledge about the phenomena of nature, people's attitude to the basics of natural being, constitutes the scientific and natural aspect of the view of the Universe. The scientific worldview (like everything else) reflects the way a person exists in society. This method contains life positions, ideals, beliefs, principles of activity and knowledge. Worldview is a necessary component of human consciousness. However, it is not just one of many elements. A worldview is a complex system in which knowledge, beliefs, aspirations, thoughts and so on interact. Thus, an integral (to one degree or another) understanding is formed by people of themselves and the world around them.

The worldview, including the scientific one, is an integral formation. A combination of its components plays a crucial role in it.
