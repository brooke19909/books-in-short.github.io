Where to spend time with a guest: the majestic Heraklion

The nearest city to the Oceanis hotel is Heraklion (or, as the Greeks call it, Heraklion) - a place with a rich, fully legendary history. The first information about him dates back to the first century AD. Over the centuries, it, along with other Greek cities, successfully developed and built. In the 9th century, the island of Crete was captured by Arab soldiers. Heraklion was renamed in the Arabic manner to the city of Handak (that is, the "moat" in Arabic). In the 10th century, the Byzantines came here and renamed the city again - it began to be called Megalo Castro ("big fortress"). At the beginning of the 13th century, immigrants from Venice settled on the island of Crete, who named the city in their own way - Candia. The period of being in Venice is considered the time of the highest prosperity of the city, especially in the aspect of culture and architecture. In the 17th century, already under Turkish rule, Kandia once again underwent renaming, becoming Handakas. The city retained this name until the beginning of the 19th century, when it returned its historical name. In 1913, the island of Crete, including Heraklion, returned to Greece.

Heraklion is popular among both Russians and travelers from Western countries (in some tourist catalogs the hotel we are talking about is named as Oceanis Hotel Heraklio 3 stars).
