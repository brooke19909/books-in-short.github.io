Blue Lake in Sergievsky District. Map of Samara region

The beautiful and unusual Blue Lake, located near the village of Staroe Yakushkino in the Samara Region, has a surprisingly regular round shape, which is explained by its formation in a karst funnel. Hydrogen sulfide springs from the bottom of the lake make the water fabulously blue, and the complete absence of life makes it crystal clear.

Mysteries of the amazing pond





Blue Lake in Sergievsky District is a fairly young reservoir by historical standards. He is no more than 250 years old. Samara divers found that the bottom of the lake is constantly changing. In 2013, they opened a gallery with crystal clear water, going far inland. The fact is that this most unusual blue lake in Russia is a source lake, in fact, a kind of griffin of an underground river flowing to the surface and replenishing the Shungut river. And his presence in the active karst region explains the constant changes taking place in the lake. The reasons for these geological processes are being investigated by hydrogeologists.

Legends and beliefs

Blue Lake in Sergievsky District is a regional natural monument. And like any other attraction, besides such an extraordinary one, it is covered with legends and stories. The indigenous people supported an interesting belief, based on the presence near Goluboy, which, in their opinion, contained dead water, a second reservoir that is filled with living water. It is believed that getting rid of all diseases will bring ablution first in dead water, and then in living water . I must say that there are few people who want to plunge into the Blue Lake - after all, the water is very cold.

Local residents are confident in the extraordinary healing properties of sulphurous water, and in the absence of a bottom in this lake. A map of the Samara region, on which more than a dozen water bodies are marked, confirms the saturation of the region with water resources, but the quality of the lake formed in the karst funnel is so amazing that legends have been formed and will still arise at all times. Old-timers say that a horse harnessed to a cart, which they could not find, fell into the water and drowned. There are rumors about sometimes tarred boards surfacing with mysterious ligature of ancient letters, and about many attempts in ancient times to measure the depth of a reservoir.



Road to the blue lake

The starting point will be the city of Samara. Blue Lake is 115 kilometers from it. Start driving on the M-5 highway in the direction of Ufa. The reference point is Sergievsk. From it, on the right side, you need to find a turn to the village of Old Yakushkino and go straight to the bridge over the Shungut River. Then turn left and drive along the river bed. Another landmark is an abandoned farm. It is important to remember that the road is unpaved, and it is better to plan a trip in dry weather, especially if the car is not a powerful SUV. From the last turn at the bridge to the lake no more than 2.5 km. The last landmark on the way to the reservoir can serve as a yellow building trailer, located on a hill opposite the lake.





Pearl of Sergievsky district

Aerial view Blue Lake in Sergievsky District is a bright transparent cornflower blue sharply defined circle, with sloping banks descending to a water mirror and framed by the poor vegetation of the Samara steppes. It is also surprising that through a small isthmus from the reservoir there is another lake with the usual parameters and freshwater inhabitants. And if fishing on the Blue Lake is impossible in principle, then a nearby body of water may provide such an opportunity.
