List of attractions of the Kemerovo region: description and photo

Traveling is one of the most interesting activities of mankind. When you get to another country or city, you are imbued with its atmosphere, you learn something new for yourself. You can get acquainted with the history, culture and traditions of this place, and it will help to do the sights, monuments, museums.

The city of Kemerovo, like the whole Kemerovo region, is no exception. Getting here, you can have a great time and also get acquainted with the cultural side of the region. The sights of the cities of the Kemerovo region are diverse: you can find both natural, and cultural, and historical. It is imperative to spend several days in this area in order to feel the flavor of this beautiful region.

Unique natural places

There are not so many natural attractions of the Kemerovo region, but they are striking in their uniqueness. Among them are: Gavrilovsky caves, Lake Big Berchikul and Shor National Park. Visiting the Gavrilovsky caves, you can enjoy the special aura of this place. They are found in crystalline limestones. Here you can see the virgin nature, untouched minerals. Inside the caves there are galleries: ground and underground. Tourists are better off visiting these places with a guide, then a trip to this wonderful place will be unforgettable.

Lake Big Berchikul is not only a great place for a summer vacation, but also a great natural attraction.

Attractions of the Kemerovo region are fascinating, as you can see for yourself by coming here.

Near the lake you can find mountains, and forest-steppe, and coniferous forest, and taiga, and the river. Silt and sand lie at its bottom. Amazing landscape will amaze any person to the depths of the soul. You can swim in the lake, sunbathe next to it on the beach, pick up berries and mushrooms, herbs and flowers in the nearby forests, and you can also go for a swim in a boat or raft and go fishing.

An equally important natural attraction of the Kemerovo region is Shor National Park. Especially important natural monuments are located here, such as the Saga waterfall, the Nadezhda cave, and the Drinking Elephant rock. Getting into this magical place, you can get acquainted with a variety of plants, various animals. Here, birds, and fish, and amphibians, and mammals. Mostly forests here are deciduous, but conifers can also be found. In this park you can spend the night if you take a tent with you and stop the savages for the night. The main attractions are: rafting, horseback riding, hiking in caves and mountains. It is worth spending a whole day in this Shor National Park, because this is one of the most important attractions of the Kemerovo region.

Museums of the Kemerovo Territory

The main attractions of the Kemerovo region are museums. How else can you get to know a city or region if you don't visit museums? Here you can plunge into the magnificent atmosphere of rituals, traditions, culture and history of this region. There are 13 major museums throughout the region. But I want to highlight 5 attractions of the Kemerovo region - especially interesting museums.

Shor Eco-Museum, Tazgol

This ecomuseum is a museum of ethnography in an open area. It is included in the aforementioned Shor National Park. Here you can get acquainted with both nature and the history, traditions, customs and everyday life of the ancient inhabitants. Important natural monuments of this reserve are: Saginsky Falls, Tsarskie Vorota and Khomutovsky rapids. Saginskiy waterfall is called a stream named Sholbychak, which falls from a height of 15 meters on the stones and flows into a small lake. Even such a small waterfall captivates tourists with its beauty and uniqueness. And under the Royal Gates are meant rocks. According to legend, a certain boy, driven out of the house, went to live in the mountains and with an arrow from a bow broke his hole in a rock the size of a small house.

Red hill

The historical museum of the Kemerovo cities is Krasnaya Gorka. Spending time here is very interesting and exciting. Inspection of historical monuments is accompanied by an entertaining tour of professional guides. In this museum there are great monuments - Barbara, a monument to the Kuzbass miners, an obelisk. There are always exhibitions and expositions. The museum produces various booklets and brochures dedicated to each of the attractions, covers the events of past years. Of the natural attractions here are Gorely Mountain and Marina.

Kemerovo Regional Museum of Local Lore

Kemerovo region is rich in its sights. The local history museum covers the history of the entire region, and military history, and the history of nature. This museum offers its bus tours with entertaining excursions. Sightseeing tours will tell you about Kuzbass and coal, in particular, about the Heroes of the Great Patriotic War of the Kemerovo Region, about giant spiders, butterflies, mammoths and much more. It hosts exhibitions and expositions. The workers of this regional museum are constantly conducting research. Even dinosaurs are studied here. Almost all categories of citizens can visit the museum absolutely free. Interestingly, anyone can hold a wedding ceremony here.

Kuznetsk fortress

Kuznetsk fortress is a magnificent museum-reserve of history and architecture. The expositions represent the history of the war of the Kemerovo Territory. Inside the fortress there are 9 buildings, 8 stone and 1 wooden: a guardhouse, a powder cellar, a soldiers‚Äô barracks, a wooden chamber for storing firearms and others. From the guides you can hear stories about how and where the battles took place, what weapons the military used, about partisans and life in the rear in difficult times for everyone. Here you can also find several archaeological sites and archival publications.

Museum "Archeology, Ethnography and Ecology of Siberia"

Probably, none of the above museums will be better able to consecrate key points, such as archeology, ecology and ethnos of the Kemerovo region, than this one. Now this museum is a division of the university, which has a photo gallery, an extensive library, archive, workshops, vaults and even classrooms. Here, science is very actively developing, archaeological and ethnographic studies are constantly carried out. The exposition of archeology is divided into five sections: the era of stone, the era of bronze, the Scythian, Hunnic and the Middle Ages. There are also departments of ethnography, ecology and zoology. All aspects are considered to the smallest detail. This museum is especially interesting for those who want to broaden their horizons, learn more than can be read in textbooks. The most frequent guests of this museum are students and schoolchildren.
