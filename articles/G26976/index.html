Special World - Izmir, Turkey

Undoubtedly, one of the places that every tourist is definitely worth visiting is Izmir, Turkey. This is the third largest city in the country, but the most important thing is that it is it that is the place where the ancient Greek and Asia Minor cultures merge together. And, of course, it is full of picturesque bays, greenery and warmth, which every tourist needs so much.



Izmir is a living monument of architecture, a museum and just a very beautiful city. Turkey has repeatedly come under the power of other powers that have left their mark on its lands. The historical part of this city is a mountainous region in which the first buildings appeared more than 3,000 years ago. According to legend, the first ruler in this territory was Queen Smyrna, in whose honor the city received its first name. Since it was located on the coast of the Aegean Sea, a lot of locals connected with their Greek neighbors, who left their mark on its history. Smyrna was repeatedly destroyed, but each time the city was rebuilt, and so it survived to this day, and began to be called Izmir.



Turkey today is a country where people come, just to relax from work and fuss. Getting on the Aegean coast, they get silence, and picturesque nature, and a warm climate. Turkey is also a museum country, which has preserved many architectural monuments and other attractions. You can inspect them endlessly. In Izmir, the Velvet Castle deserves special attention, which, according to legend, was the first building on these lands. It is located on the top of Pagos Hill, and from its balconies overlooks the entire bay and the buildings that have been erected here for centuries. And on Konak Meydana Square, the very heart of the city is located, which, incidentally, is very young - it is a chapel tower that was built here in 1901, taking into account all the canons of the Ottoman style.

Undoubtedly, every tourist is primarily interested in the resorts of Izmir. Turkey, in particular that part of it that is washed by the Aegean Sea, is very different from the part that is adjacent to the Mediterranean. There is always calm and quiet, fewer people, and the service and maintenance at the same time at a high level. City beaches are equipped with sun loungers and awnings, and those that are away from the settlement are surrounded by green shrubs and tropical plants. This region is characterized by white sandy beaches that stretch in a wide strip along a quiet and shallow sea. That is why Izmir is an ideal city for families with children.

Turkey, reviews of which I sometimes want to write in poetry, is undoubtedly an inexhaustible source of joy and pleasure. Here an avid historian-archaeologist and a lover of loafing will find joy.
