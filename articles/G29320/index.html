Sicily, Trapani: attractions, photos, holiday reviews

Trapani is a resort in Sicily, which, in addition to everything, is also the provincial capital of the same name. This city is located in the western part, off the coast of the Tyrrhenian Sea and the Sicilian Strait. Trapani in Sicily is very famous for its port. This city is also famous for its many attractions.



What is famous for the city of Trapani

The Sicilian town is very famous on the whole island and in Italy. First of all, this is due to the high level of salt production that is produced here. Throughout the town you can even see unique mills with pointed tops - it is in them that grind this product.



In the province of Trapani there are several vineyards where the best berries grow - they make excellent wine. The drink produced in this province is known all over the world - it is bought at the most expensive restaurants, including in Russia. Also, the city and its environs are famous for the large amount of olive oil and olives produced.

In Trapani, fishing is very well developed. It is here that local lovers of this activity catch valuable species of fish, in particular tuna.

Attractions Trapani

Sicily is an island that attracts tourists with its attractions, among which there are both architectural and natural.

Trapani is a city famous for its cathedral and several so-called palazzos. The most famous among them is the Palazzo Siambra Giudecc, which was erected during the Aragonese reign.

The Basilica of the Annunciation is famous all over the world, which tourists come to see. Here you can admire the creation of the famous sculptor Nino Pisano - the statue of the Virgin. The city of Trapani is relatively small in size, so you can visit all its attractions without even using transport - on foot.

Guests of the city who want to get acquainted with its sights are often invited to visit the Jewish quarter, which is associated with historical events of a long period. Jewish settlements lived on its territory until the 15th century.

While in this city, you should definitely visit its main street - Corso Vittorio Emanuele, where you can admire the luxurious villas that were built back in the 18-19th centuries.



Traditional cuisine

Undoubtedly, all the guests of the city are interested in learning the features of local national cuisine. Here you can enjoy her meals at local catering establishments.

Trapani (Sicily) prepares traditional Mediterranean dishes, a hallmark of which is the frequent use of sauce. In Trapani, culinary experts love to season cooked salads and pasta with pesto alla trapanese sauce. It serves delicious pork sausages, its own version of the Arabic dish couscous, veal rolls, as well as stuffed chop. For dessert, pancakes (especially delicious with honey and ricotta fillings), sesame nougat with honey, and cannoli rolls can be enjoyed at local establishments for dessert.

Trapani is very fond of cooking seafood dishes. Here they are added to pasta, broth, soups, as well as side dishes.

"Procession of the sacraments"

The "procession of the sacraments" is one of the most famous and interesting traditions that are found in the city of Trapani (Sicily). Her roots she goes into the history of Andalusia.

This procession takes place on the Friday before Easter, it starts at 2 pm. Its route begins from the Soul Church in Purgatory and passes through all the largest streets of the city. The people who participate in it depict representatives of the population of different classes and carry with them religious sculptures that are made of wood. Also in the "Procession of the sacraments" are attended by musicians of brass bands, children and local residents who dress up in holiday costumes. Residents of the city carry flowers and various flags, so that the column of walking looks very bright. Such an event lasts exactly one day.



Features

Choosing to relax Trapani (Sicily), you should remember about some of the features peculiar to this island. There are not many, but they are important.

First of all, you should decide on the season, which will be the most favorable for this purpose. The ideal period for this is the time from May to October. It is then that water and air warm up to the most comfortable mark. During this period, there is also a minimal number of rainy days.

If you plan a family trip with your children, you should pay great attention to the features of their accommodation. The fact is that in Trapani (Sicily), far from all hotels provide discounts on accommodation for minors. Of course, when booking a hotel room, you should clarify in advance whether they have the opportunity to provide a separate crib.

Beaches

In this town there are several good beaches that are very similar to each other. They have one name - Lido. Both beaches in Trapani (Sicily) are sandy, and they are equipped with everything necessary for a pleasant vacation for every tourist. Here you can find small bars everywhere where you can quench your thirst with delicious soft drinks. They also have mobile restaurants that operate only in the summer - at the time of a large influx of tourists.



On the sea coast, you can rent a boat and sail on it to the Aegadian Islands, which are nearby. Transportation in this case will be carried out under the supervision of a professional instructor.

Guest reviews

All those who rested in this resort town almost always share their impressions of him.

So, tourists from different countries literally admire the picturesque nature. Architecture experts note the unique combination of buildings made in different styles. The photos taken in Trapani are very beautiful.

How to get to Trapani

All air routes lead to the local Vincenzo Florio International Airport, which is 15 km from the city. From it to Trapani can be reached in various ways. Firstly, the AST bus, which is municipal, runs regularly from the airport to the city center. The fare on it will be 2 euros, and it will take you about 40 minutes to travel.

Secondly, the city can be reached by taxi. Taxi drivers charge a lot for their services. A trip from the airport to the city will cost no less than $ 50, but in this way you can get there quickly and, as they say, with a breeze.

Thirdly, from the city of Naples and from the Aegadian Islands you can sail here using a ferry or boat - they regularly run along the sea surface and through the Sicilian Channel.
