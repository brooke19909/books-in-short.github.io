How to crochet a vest: schemes and description

The vest can be called one of the most popular types of clothing. It is worn to keep warm, or for beauty, it can be made in a simple office style or include crazy bright patterns and ornaments. Knowledge of the general patterns of knitting clothes is necessary to get a really high-quality crochet vest. Schemes and description are also needed, but not necessary, since this product can be connected even with a minimum of information.



Types of vests and their features

The obvious common feature that unites all types of vests is the lack of sleeves. However, today the variety of clothing models and their variations is so great that sometimes the edges are erased. Therefore, you should distinguish a vest from a cardigan, tunic and other items.

As a rule, a crochet vest (patterns and description of knitting can be taken from different sources) is worn on top of some other clothes. For example, a blouse, dress, sundress or tunic. In the classic version, it reaches the waistline, but may be slightly longer.

Vests are solid (without fasteners) and with two shelves. The latter is often left without buttons, locks or belts. They are wide open, so that the shelves act as draperies.

How to calculate button plackets

If the shelves of the vest must be provided with plackets for buttons, their width should be taken into account when calculating the details of the front. The best method for making the planks is to stick them on the finished canvas. However, often the craftswomen prefer to sew a separately tied bar to the shelves.

Buttonholes must be made during the knitting process of the placket. The distance between them is calculated as follows:

  • Divide the length of the strap by the number of buttons.
  • The resulting size in centimeters (A) will be the distance between the two nearest buttons.
  • The first loop should be done at a distance from the edge, which is equal to A / 2. The same distance should be from the last loop to the end of the bar.

The result is five full gaps (A) and two halves (A / 2).
