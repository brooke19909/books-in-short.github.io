Bit of history

The city was formed in 1632 by the Swedes, but in the twelfth century the Karelians here founded a fortified settlement. A century later, the fortress perished: there is an opinion that it was destroyed by the Swedes. But at the same moment the lands of today's Ladoga fell under the influence of Novgorod. On the border of the 16-17th centuries, during the Russian-Swedish war, these lands more than once passed into different possessions. The city in 1783 became a county and belonged to the Vyborg province, while in 1811 it became part of Finland along with the province. In 1893, a railway was opened in Ladoga, after which, by the beginning of the twentieth century, a steamboat connection with St. Petersburg, Shlimmelburg, Valaam and Konevetsky monasteries appeared in this place . Sortavala in 1917 began to relate to the Vyborg province. Today it is a beautiful city that attracts with its historical sights and unusual architecture, which is confirmed by numerous reviews of tourists.



Architecture

Many vacationers in the city leave delighted reviews that Sortavala gives the opportunity to get acquainted with different architectural styles, including classicism, Western European modernism, national romanticism, neoclassicism, functionalism, neo-Gothic, constructivism. In addition, a huge number of wooden structures from the mid-nineteenth century, mainly in the Empire style, have been preserved here. Finland at the beginning of the twentieth century became the leader of world architecture, and at the same time led our country, since St. Petersburg to Helsinki is a stone's throw away. In architecture, the influence of northern Art Nouveau, especially on Kamenny Island , is very noticeable.
