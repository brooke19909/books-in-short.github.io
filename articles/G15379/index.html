Boiling point

September 1943 The Oryol-Kulikovo battle was coming to an end, and Soviet troops actively launched an offensive along the entire Western Front. The Germans were indignant and actively resisted. In parallel with the occupation of these lands, information reached them that there was a secret ‚Äúflight territory‚Äù - anonymous height.

And then there was the question of where the nameless height is. About 17 kilometers from Roslavl of the Smolensk region, a distinctly fortified zone was discovered. She occupied a very important strategic position and was considered a secret object.

For two days, the Soviet army recaptured him from the Germans. Of the 18 guys who went into battle, only two survived.

The Germans were initially confused, rushed through the trenches and dug trenches, but in the end they stubbornly began to take anonymous height. After that, our soldiers nevertheless had the opportunity to distract the enemy - the incident helped them manage to remove the invader from their territory.

To stay alive

One of the survivors was simply dug out of the ground. The brothers found him on the protruding shoes, felt the pulse and immediately began to pull out. The Soviet hero was Eugene Lapin.

After a long period of treatment and rehabilitation, he returned to his military unit. In a short time he managed to get a few more military injuries, to learn and get to Berlin. After the war, he returned to the city of Donetsk, but was soon evacuated with his family.

A completely different story happened with the second surviving hero - Vlasov Konstantin Nikolaevich. He was already listed as dead, and his family even received a funeral notice.

In fact, the Germans captured him, then a prison and a concentration camp in Germany. But he managed to escape with his compatriots. In 1944, was seriously wounded, but survived.
