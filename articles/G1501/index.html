Siberian selection of tomatoes - features and advantages. The best varieties of tomatoes of Siberian selection



It is unlikely that anyone will read such an article for the sake of idle interest. Most likely, this is a person for whom gardening is not just a hobby, but the meaning of life. After all, Siberian selection of tomatoes can be of interest only to purposeful people. You can even say altruists, because growing vegetables in an unsuitable climate for living in Siberia is akin to madness. What will be the result is known only to God.

Right choice

The exception is those options when the gardener chose the variety correctly. Breeders are working tirelessly to create everything that the consumer wants. Genetic engineering has given a frantic impetus to the impossible. As a result, there is plenty to choose from. Moreover, the choice is so great that it is not a sin to get lost. That is why we offer you to consider this text, where we will try to examine in detail the new varieties of tomatoes of Siberian selection.

What to choose from?

Ripening period

So, to begin with, we will discuss the fact that for cultivation in a region with a short warm period, it is necessary to take seeds of exclusively early ripe varieties. It is these plants that can grow in time and bear fruit in a period of no more than three months. It is important that the given growth and development rate is not affected by taste.





Types of seeds

The next thing to consider is that all tomatoes can ripen from varietal or hybrid seeds. Varietal tomato seeds of Siberian selection differ from hybrid ones in that they are able to produce strong plants for the next season. Hybrid seeds require individual processing (crossbreeding), so that subsequently their bushes are distinguished by high germination, yield and disease resistance. That is, hybrid plants are not able to give strong "offspring."

Type of bush

The next indicator that should influence the choice is the appearance of the bush. They are ordinary and standard. The two types proposed by us do not describe the whole picture, but only partially, but the principle is conveyed accurately. That is, the bushes can also be half-stemmed, indeterminate, determinant , etc. You will have to rely on your own capabilities and tastes. Tomatoes of Siberian selection have all kinds in their collection. They differ among themselves, as a rule, by the need for tying. Namely, the standard, semi-standard, determinant ones differ from ordinary tomato bushes by a strong low stalk, bending exclusively under the load of the crop. Ordinary, indeterminate are distinguished by constant growth and plentiful harvest. The length of the bush can reach two and a half meters, they can not do without a garter, even if they do not have fruits. Otherwise, the plant will simply lie on the ground.

Taste qualities

Siberian selection of tomatoes is also distinguished by the quality of the resulting tomatoes. Some are ideal for fresh consumption, while others are exclusively for canning or processing. These recommendations should also be taken into account.





suggestions

So, if you have exactly decided where the bushes will grow, what plants you want to see in your garden, and most importantly - for what purposes you will use ripened fruits, then the choice will be easy, and the end result will be extremely pleasant. We bring to your attention both new varieties of tomatoes of Siberian selection, and already well-established ones.

Let's start with those that are easy to care for, low stems that do not require tying.

Shtambovy Alpatyeva

It is considered the most unpretentious among all types of tomato bushes. It does not require not only tying, but also pinching. Smooth, medium-sized fruits ripen, ideal for pickling.





Shuttle

Like the previous variety, it does not require tying and grafting. The ripening of the first fruits occurs already at 81-100 days after emergence. Tomatoes are not very large, weighing no more than 60 g. The variety is distinguished by high resistance to frost, pests and transportation. They are universal, that is, they are suitable for both fresh and canned consumption. These are the best varieties of tomatoes of Siberian selection.

Hospitable

This tomato variety bears fruit, the average size of which is 500 grams. Moreover, the height of the bush does not exceed 80 cm. The plant is distinguished from others by its maximum resistance to sharp temperature fluctuations and a stably high yield.

destructive force

From the bush of such a plant it is possible to get five kilograms of fruit. In this case, the plant itself does not rise high from the ground. This is a determinant variety of tomatoes. With competent stepsoning, the size of the fruit will reach 150 grams. In principle, beginner gardeners will not have problems with this variety.

Ultra early

Tops the list of "Varieties of Tomatoes of Siberian Selection" by the rate of ripening. Gardeners are able to get the first fruits in 70 days after germination. The plant rises from the ground by only 40-50 cm, and the average weight of the fruit is 100 g. The variety is ideal for canning.





Heavyweight Siberia

This variety will please you with a short stalk, it will not require pinching and will give fairly large fruits. This variety of tomatoes is considered mid-season and shows itself well in open ground. Ideal for canning.

Triumphant

Breeders mark it as a mid-season Siberian standard grade. The height of the bush does not exceed 40 cm, and the fruits - on average - 100 g each.

Fat Boatswain

A distinctive feature of this variety are straw-golden stripes on the red sides. High yield, large fruits up to 180 g and the versatility of use makes it possible for many to give preference to this particular determinant variety.

Bullfinches

After only 95 days after germination, you can get a crop of large fruits (150 g) from a bush with a height of 40 cm maximum. Distinctive features of the variety: resistance to cracking, late blight, ideal for open ground.

Snow fairy tale

The name is not accidental: a bush strewn with ripe bright-colored fruits looks like a Christmas tree. The plant is problem-free, does not require garter and pinching, gives a high yield in cold summer conditions.

Apples in the snow

These are the earliest and most resistant to temperature changes plants from the collection "Siberian Selection of Tomatoes". The bush has small bright red fruits from 50 to 70 g.





Siberian precocious

It is possible to get up to seven kilograms of the crop from one bush, while the vegetables are not very large, about 100 grams each. The variety is cold-resistant, but with medium disease resistance. Ideal for making tomato paste.

Siberian feasts

The ideal variety for canning, giving medium fruits. It is considered premature, not needing to be stepson. In an unripe form it is stored poorly, no more than a month.

Siberian troika

The fruits of such bushes have an elongated shape up to 15 cm long. The average weight of vegetables is 350 grams. About 5 kg of crop can be obtained from each plant. The vegetables are tasty and sweet, the perfect salad grade.

Next, we will tell you about those varieties that have proven themselves in cold climates, have fairly large bushes, that is, indeterminate, ordinary varieties that make up the Siberian Tomato Breeding collection.

South tan

This variety is considered ideal for Siberian greenhouses. It does not apply to early-ripening varieties, rather to mid-ripening ones, but the fruits that you get will differ in respectable size and excellent taste. The average size of the fruit is 200 g, and the main "highlight" can be called an unprecedented sweetness. Tomatoes have a very low acid content.

Miracle of the earth

It is also a marvelous tomato of Siberian selection for greenhouses. Plants reach a height of two meters, and the average weight of the fruit is 1200 grams. At the same time, the quality of vegetables is simply fantastic. Tomatoes are ideal for fresh consumption, have rich pink dessert fruits.





Truffle red

A distinctive quality of this variety are large fruit brushes (10-12 pieces). The mass of each tomato reaches 150 g, and the taste is bright, buttery. Moreover, its stability is striking in that the productivity does not fall even after the frosts. Experts assure that this variety of tomatoes is not affected by late blight. Vegetables torn off in an unripe form can ripen slowly at room temperature until the New Year.

Three fat men

The first tomato crop of this variety can be obtained only 110 days after germination. The weight of the fruit reaches 600 grams. On branches 3-4 brushes of fruit are formed. It is considered one of the best for fresh consumption, that is, a salad variety.
