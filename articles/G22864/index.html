Criteria

Before you start making your old TV with your own hands, you need to consider a solution for several important criteria. On which depends the smoothness of the picture, image quality and color saturation of the frame. These factors include:

  • signal response time;
  • viewing angle (in most modern screens, it is extremely small; and if you do not observe the viewing angle, the image becomes cloudy or darkened);
  • illumination of the room (sensitive sensors of computer displays require rather poor lighting in the room, otherwise the picture on the screen will look pale);
  • the depth of dark tones (which, unfortunately, for personal computer monitors is extremely low and unable to convey the entire depth of black in the frame of a television movie);
  • contrast level (significantly different for these two types of devices; failure to observe the correct contrast settings can cause great harm to the eye of the beholder).
