Bone fish

The overclass Bone fish belongs to the class of vertebrate animals and includes two more classes. One of them is represented by radiant fish. It consists of more than 30 units and 500 families. The second - lobed-feather fish (double-breathing and brush-headed).

Bone fish live in all waters of the Earth - in salty and fresh, from both poles to the equator. They are common both offshore and in the most inaccessible depths of the seas and oceans. Both bone and cartilaginous fish have fins, that is, paired limbs. However, bone-cartilaginous fish have significant distinguishing features. First of all, this applies to the skeleton and head: they have these parts are completely bony. The cover of these fish is exactly the same - the scales formed by bone plates.

The bony branchial arches of these fish (there are five pairs) are located on the sides of the pharynx. On them are gills in the form of drooping petals. The nostrils are paired, in the inner ear there are semicircular canals (there are three of them). The structure of the fish of this superclass is distinguished by a gill cavity covered by a bone gill cover. The swim bladder is located inside the body, in its upper part. The grasping jaws together with the teeth form a mouth. The length of these fish varies widely: from 70 to 500-700 cm, respectively, their weight ranges from several kilograms to one and a half tons.

Bone fish first appeared approximately 400 million years ago. Gradually, compared with other fish and vertebrates living on earth, they reached the greatest variety. Mostly bone fish have a body shape resembling a torpedo.
