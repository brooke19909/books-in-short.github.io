What about late payment?

Based on the rules of MFIs, if there is an untimely or late payment, the borrower needs to pay a penalty. Its size, as a rule, is indicated in the text of the loan agreement and amounts to 20% per annum of the total amount that was late or not received at all. If the debtor refuses to pay the bills, the MFI transfers its credit to third parties for the purpose of further collection. However, the borrower has the right to prohibit the lender from contacting third-party organizations, in this case, representatives of collection agencies. To do this, he needs to write an additional statement. It is his employees of the League of Money who usually attach to the personal file of the borrower.
