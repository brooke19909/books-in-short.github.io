A bit of history

Saransk was founded by archers and Cossacks in 1641 as a fortress. The main purpose of its construction was to protect the central regions from attacks by all kinds of nomadic tribes. Later, a posad was built near the fortress. The first inhabitants of Saransk were not in poverty. The land in the vicinity of this city was fertile and yielded good crops. In the local forests, there were many animals. Therefore, the posad quickly became attractive to other settlers. The village began to grow rapidly and eventually turned into a town.

In 1651, Posad became a county center. In 1774, its inhabitants supported Pugachev, who was going to Moscow after the capture of Orenburg. Moreover, the connection with the rioters was then almost a "traditional" thing for the city. In 1670, he was one of the strongholds of the troops of Stepan Razin.





In 1780, Saransk received city status. It became the center of the Mordovian district much later, after the revolution - in 1928. During the Second World War, the city enterprises produced ammunition, uniforms and vehicles for the front. Also, several military units were formed here, hospitals and orphanages operated. 8 thousand sarans were killed on the fronts of the war.
