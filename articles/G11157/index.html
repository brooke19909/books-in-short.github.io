Smartphone Zte Blade A813: review, specifications, reviews

The ZTE Blade A813 smartphone can be fully called a budget device (about 8,000 rubles). The gadget was originally developed for Asian consumers, and was sold in Singapore and Vietnam under the name ZTE Blade D Lux. The international market and domestic consumers in particular, saw the model on store shelves in early 2016.





Let's try to figure out what exactly the ZTE Blade A813 smartphone can attract the LTE consumer: an overview of the characteristics, advantages and disadvantages of the gadget, along with feedback from the owners and the opinion of experts in this field.

Design

The appearance of the device can be described as classic. The ZTE Blade A813 (ergonomic overview) has smoothed edges, a 2.5 D rounded glass, a familiar power key with a ribbed surface, and a pretty attractive Home button with a built-in bluish tint LED.





A little charm to the smartphone adds an interesting pattern on the back of the gadget in the form of small squares. Otherwise, this is a normal-looking smartphone. ZTE Blade A813 (review of dimensions) is slightly different from the entire mass of competing models in its dimensions, which are slightly larger than the usual for 5.5-inch gadgets: 153.5 x 75.2 x 8.5 mm. Nevertheless, the device sits well in the palm of your hand, and it is quite comfortable to operate it with one hand.

Display

The smartphone received pretty good characteristics for a 5.5-inch display: a resolution of 1280 by 720 pixels (HD), 2.5D glass technology (curved profile), an air gap and a good IPS-matrix. The ZTE Blade A813 has a decent overview of the angles, so you can easily look through a photo or watch a video with friends (no more than two).

Vertical viewing angles are slightly worse than horizontal ones, but this is a disaster for most budget models, so this point cannot be taken into critical cons. In addition, the presence of an air gap between the touchscreen and the main screen does not allow you to work comfortably on a sunny day, but such a design, in case of cracks, scratches or other breakdowns, is easy and much cheaper to replace.

Communication capabilities of the ZTE Blade A813

Communication characteristics at a very decent level. It is possible to work with two SIM cards in micro-sim format. They are located under a removable cover just above the battery. The device supports all formats of international communications, so it feels great in any GSM-networks. There is also support for 3G and 4G streams, which means that there should be no problems with picky SIM cards.

An intelligent GPS module is responsible for navigation, so the gadget can be used as a navigator in a car. The only thing the owners were upset about in their reviews was the lack of support for GLONASS technology, but for a budget model this seems to be enough. There is also an OTG module that allows you to connect various peripherals through a micro-USB connector.

Battery life
