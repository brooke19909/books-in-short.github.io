Common starling as a separate species





In nature, there are 100 species of these birds. But on all continents common starling is found. This is a separate species of the starling family. His homeland is the western regions of Asia and Europe. Usually these birds do not migrate in winter. From the northeastern regions, they fly south only if there is a chance of severe cold weather.

Common starling during the nesting period forms colonies in which there are usually several pairs of birds. They settle from each other at a distance of 10 to 15 meters. These birds tend to move in packs. It may have several dozen individuals.

Starlings feed on plant foods, insects and can even eat the waste left by people. They prefer to get food directly in the air or on the ground. This bird takes out earthworms and larvae from the ground with its beak. Starlings are very fond of fruits, berries, seeds and vegetables. They can very cleverly tear open garbage bags with their beaks to eat leftover food.

Behavior and habitat

Starlings move well along the reed stalks and tree branches. Sinking to the ground, they walk leisurely. When they look for food, they begin to incline their head continuously, turning right and left. These birds are not bad singers. They have a loud, slightly creaky voice. They tend to imitate the singing of other birds and even the voice of a person. They can imitate the bleating of sheep, the croaking of a frog, the clicking of a whip, the barking of a dog. Starlings have learned to portray the noise of a typewriter or the sound of a mobile call. When they arrive in the spring from the south, they sing with the voices of subtropical birds.



Starlings inhabit the flat terrain. They can be found from the lower to middle zones of the mountains. Starlings nest in closed places. This can be a hollow, a deepening of steep slopes, cracks in the rock or an earth embankment. If birds nest in settlements, then they arrange nests under the roofs of houses, under balconies and in various niches.

How many times a year chicks are hatched

Two clutches of eggs a year in starlings happen in the temperate and southern regions. In the northern latitudes, they lay their eggs once a year. In the clutch there can be 4-7 monochromatic bluish, whitish or greenish eggs. Hatching of chicks lasts from 2 to 3 weeks. The same time is necessary for their feeding. Damage to agriculture



In Russia, starlings are often found in house yards. The owners, knowing how starlings look, admire them. The singing of this bird pleases the ear of man. By nature, starlings are very gullible and courageous. In mid-summer, young birds fly out of their nests and begin to roam in the meadows in noisy flocks. In the autumn, from the middle of September to the beginning of October, starling migration begins. Many of them fly away in early November, when the first snow falls. In winter, single starlings and their small flocks remain with a warm pre-winter. During this period, their mass congestion can become a serious threat to agriculture. They begin to peck berries and eat grain crops, thereby causing significant economic damage.
