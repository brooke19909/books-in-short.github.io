Where to go in Brest - the main attractions of the city

It is known to some people due to the fact that here is the famous Brest Fortress, which the Russians selflessly defended during the years of the Second World War. And what other interesting places are there? We decided to give you an overview of the attractions that are definitely worth visiting upon arrival in Brest. Let's start dating right now.

Railway museum

Do you like locomotives and the spirit of romance? Then be sure to visit the Railway Museum. This is a great option if you do not know where to go in Brest. The development of the railway industry in the city began at the end of the 19th century. The city has become an important transport hub on the border of Russia and Belarus. The decision to open a museum with railway equipment was made by the city administration in early 2002. Visitors will be able to see more than 50 models of various steam locomotives here, some exhibits are even used during the filming of historical films. During the tour, you can get into the salon of the legendary trains of the Great Patriotic War, pull the levers and press the buttons of the control panel, like a real engineer. You can also learn interesting facts from the history of the development and formation of the railway in Brest. The museum is open to visitors any day from 10:00 to 18:00, except Mondays. Ticket prices can be checked at the box office.

Address: Masherova, 2.

Winter Garden

The bewitching world of paradise flowers and strange plants is available to all visitors to this place. Only after crossing the threshold, you will find yourself in an amazing tropical world that occupies the entire first floor of the Winter Garden. Huge ferns and orchids with an amazing smell grow here. The second floor is divided into two zones. The first is the desert zone, here visitors can see many unique and funny cacti, African agaves and other exotic plants. The second part of the floor is a real subtropical paradise. Visitors can admire the trees with the fruits of lemon and pomegranate. Wrought Iron Alley

Want to spend an unforgettable, romantic evening with your loved one? The best option would be a walk under the moonlit sky along the forged lantern avenue. This is one of the youngest attractions in the city. The forged lanterns alley appeared in the very center of the city (in Gogol Lane) in 2013. City Hall of Brest decided to open it in honor of the liberation of the city from Nazi troops. The only thing they did not want to invest in the city budget. The administration asked for help from entrepreneurs who supported this idea. In this creative project, they were able to fully express their imagination. The first lantern is located next to the Brest Fortress. It looks like a watch, in the center is a large dial. The next lantern resembles an unusual loom, the idea was filed by Brest sewing factories. Forged Lanterns Alley is one of the most beautiful and interesting sights of the city.

Military relic

Nevertheless, the main attraction of the city is the Brest Fortress. Her defenders at the beginning of the war proved to be courageous and selfless soldiers. For a long time they managed to restrain the fascist invaders at the cost of huge human losses. The fortress is highly respected by both local residents and visitors. If you find yourself in a city, you are simply obliged to go on a sightseeing tour of the Brest Fortress. You can see St. Nicholas Garrison Cathedral, several monuments and obelisks, as well as the Museum of Exhibits.

Address: Heroes of Defense of the Brest Fortress, 60.
