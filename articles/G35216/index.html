Institution mission

Quarks, the Museum of Entertaining Sciences, is a completely new example of a museum view. What does it mean? First of all, the fact that Quarks is no longer just a museum with exhibits, but something more reminiscent of a permanent laboratory.

Physics available to everyone

Quarks, the Museum of Entertaining Sciences, introduces visitors to such areas of physics as acoustics and mechanics, thermodynamics and optics. The settings presented here make it possible to demonstrate the phenomena of nature familiar to everyone and the laws of their development, to learn more about the properties of matter and the laws of its motion, and much more.

Interestingly, the guides here are mostly young and passionate about science. As a rule, these are students of technical universities of the city. The administration of the museum ensures that excursions and shows are interesting and fascinating. Every month, based on customer feedback, the best guide is selected.

Entertaining exhibits and Quark zones

The Museum of Entertaining Sciences contains many different objects that are located in a huge room. Its area is 1.6 thousand square meters. The museum presents to its guests more than a hundred different exhibits, which are located in separate areas. Each of them contains exhibits according to the time of their arrival. Today, three zones are open: blue, red and green.





The first presents visitors the achievements of the era of Antiquity and the Middle Ages. Here you can see interesting technical solutions of Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci, as well as the results of discoveries of other scientists of that time. Of particular interest are the first camera and catapult. Museum guests also have the opportunity to launch the very first helicopter in the world and take a picture with a pinhole camera, which Aristotle mentioned at one time.

In the red zone you can get acquainted with the exhibits of the New Time, which is indicated by the period until 1918. Guides emphasize that this period is one of the most interesting in terms of discoveries and the most diverse inventions. Here, visitors may be surprised to see objects floating in the air, and in practice learn how the Bernoulli levitator works. Maxwell's pendulum will reveal to the guests some patterns in the movement of solids.

The green zone acquaints visitors with the most amazing inventions created during the last century. There are lightnings that occur at will in the plasma ball. You can make your portrait, leaning against a contact base of many cylindrical rods.
