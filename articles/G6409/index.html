Eugene Bogarne: a brief biography

Eugene Bogarne, whose biography will be considered in the article, is the stepson of Napoleon Bonaparte, Viceroy of Italy, General, Prince of Leuchtenberg. He was born in Paris on September 3, 1781.

Origin of Eugene Bogarne





As you might guess, Evgeny Bogarne came from a noble noble family. It was not possible to make a photo of him in those days, but history left us with a number of portraits, one of which is presented above. Alexander de Beauharnais, his father, was Viscount, a native of the island of Martinique (a colony of France located in the Caribbean). Even when he was a young officer, Alexander married Josephine's Creole. After a while, he became a general and a prominent figure in the revolution, but was arrested on a denunciation and died on the guillotine. By this time, Eugene was only 13 years old. Josephine was also arrested, and her son was sent to the artisan's family for re-education.

Studying at a military school





On July 28, 1794, the Thermidorian coup took place . He led to the fact that the Jacobin dictatorship was overthrown. Thanks to this, Josephine was free, and Eugene began to study at the Saint-Germain military school.

Mother Eugene in 1796 married Napoleon Bonaparte, who at that time was a general of the French Republic. In the same year, having graduated from a military school, our hero became Bonaparte's adjutant. The photo above shows two portraits - Napoleon and Josephine.

Eugene accompanies Napoleon on campaigns

When the general went on an Italian campaign (1796-1797), Eugene was constantly with him. He also accompanied him during the Egyptian expedition (1798-99).





Eugene Bogarne was one of the participants in the coup of the Eighteenth Brumaire held on November 9, 1799. The directory as a result of it lost its power. A new interim government appeared, headed by Napoleon Bonaparte, now consul. Eugene also served in his guard, where he was the captain of horse rangers. In the photo above - Eugene Bogarne on horseback.

Career advancement

In 1800, Eugene took part in the military campaign that France organized in northern Italy against the Austrians. At the end of the battle of Marengo (the so-called village located in northern Italy), Eugene was awarded the rank of colonel. And a few years later, in 1804, he became a brigadier general.

In 1804, the coronation of Napoleon took place, during which Beauharnais received the title of State Chancellor. Eugene also earned an honorary title, becoming the prince of the French Empire. However, these awards did not bring real power to Beauharnais. The title and title that he received were only of an honorable character.

Eugene becomes Viceroy. Marriage to Agnes Amalia





Napoleon in 1805 created the Italian kingdom. He became the king, and Beauharnais the vice-king. It is known that at one time (in 1806) Bonaparte even wanted to declare Eugene his heir. To this end, he adopted him. Thus, the status of Eugene has increased. He has now become a monarchist person. Thanks to this, our hero married in the same year (at the request of Napoleon). His wife was the daughter of the King of Bavaria, Agnes Amalie (1788-1851).

In 1807, Bonaparte made Eugene the heir to the Italian throne. He was given the title of Prince of Venice.

Eugene on the Italian throne

Eugene Bogarne was not an experienced administrator. Therefore, as the ruler of Italy, he surrounded himself with many advisers, Italians. During his reign, the administration and court were transformed (in the image of France), and the army was also improved. However, the sending of troops and financial payments made by Eugene at the request of Bonaparte, caused discontent of the local population.

When Beauharnais became the ruler of Italy, he was only 24 years old. However, he managed to lead the state quite firmly. The army was reorganized, the Civil Code was introduced. The country was equipped with fortifications, canals and schools. Despite some dissatisfaction, inevitable in the difficult task of government, in general, we can say that he managed to earn the respect and love of his people.

Participation in the Napoleonic Wars

Beauharnais participated in almost all the wars that Napoleon waged. In the Austrian campaign (1809), he was commander of Italian troops. The outcome of the battle of the city of Salice (in Italy) was unsuccessful. Archduke John of Habsburg won. However, despite this, Eugene managed to turn the tide of events. He inflicted several defeats on John, first in Italy and then in Austria. Beauharnais also won in Hungary, important for the French. We are talking about the battle of Raab (today it is the city of Gyor in Hungary). After that, he distinguished himself in a decisive battle at Wagram (now this is a village located in Austria).

Napoleon called Beauharnais from Italy in 1812. He was to become the commander of the fourth corps of the now French army. Eugene participated in the war of 1812, where he distinguished himself in the battles of Ostrovno (today it is an agro-town located in Belarus), near Borodino, Smolensk, Vyazma, Maroyaroslavets, Vilna (now it is the city of Vilnius, Lithuania), Red.

Eugene Bogarne and Savva Storozhevsky

Many miracles are associated with the Monk Savva Storozhevsky. One of them is considered to be his appearance to Eugene Beauharnais in 1812, during the capture of Moscow by the French. Savva convinced Eugene not to destroy the monastery located in Zvenigorod. In exchange, he promised that Eugene Beauharnais would return home without hindrance. Savva kept his word - the prophet's prophecies really came true.

The reflection of the attack of the Austrian troops

After Napoleon left Russia with Marshal Joachim Murat, Beauharnais commanded the remnants of the French army. He took troops to Magdeburg (today it is a German city). After the battle of Luzen (a city in Germany), which took place in 1813, Eugene was sent to Italy by order of Bonaparte. He had to provide her protection from the attack of the Austrian troops. It is believed that the military operations of Beauharnais in Italy, in the campaign of 1813-14, are the pinnacle of military skill. Only thanks to the betrayal of Murat did the Austrians manage to avoid a complete defeat.

The fate of Beauharnais after the abdication of Napoleon from the throne





In 1814 (April 16) Napoleon abdicated. After that, Beauharnais, the Viceroy of Italy, entered into a truce and went to Bavaria. Beauharnais became a peer of France in June 1815. The Vienna Congress, held in 1814-1815, decided to allocate 5 million francs to him in compensation for Italian possessions. Major rewards

Eugene was awarded many awards. In 1805, he received the Orders of the Legion of Honor, the Iron Crown, and St. Hubert of Bavaria. In 1811, Eugene Bogarne was awarded the Grand Cross of the Order of St. Stephen. And these are only his main rewards.
