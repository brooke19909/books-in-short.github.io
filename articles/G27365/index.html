Tie-break - what is it? Description

Many people ask: tie-break - what is it? Where is it used? We will consider these and other questions in the article. The term "tie-break" ("stop a draw") comes from the English words tie (draw) and break (stop, break). It means a shortened specific game (in volleyball (short set), in tennis (game) and other sports), with which you can determine the winner in a draw.

When is tennis played?

Tennis tie-break - what is it? In solitary tennis, athletes play a shortened game with a score of 6: 6 in games. The game continues until a set of seven points. If with seven points the difference in points of opponents is less than two, then the tie-break continues until the difference of two points appears.





In paired tennis, in addition to short games in a simple set with the same score on sets, a crucial set in the form of a tie-break for the champion can be played. In this case, the rivals compete until they score ten points. The game can last until the advantage of one of the pairs in two points. And it is written as 1: 0 or 0: 1.

So, you already know that a tie-break is a short match. How is he played in tennis? The submitting contestant performs the first serve, then the opponent carries out two innings, after which the change goes through a couple of innings. A tie-break lasts until a difference of two points is obtained. Venues are replaced after every six points.

Volleyball

Next, we will find out how a tie-break is performed in chess, and now we will consider holding a short game in volleyball. In this sport, it is carried out with the score of the parties 2: 2. How is its implementation going?





The fifth (decisive) game is played up to fifteen points with the least advantage of two points. If there is an equal score of 14:14, the game lasts until there is an advantage of two points (16:14, 17:15, ...).

Chess

A tie-break in chess is held when the points of the current champion and applicant in the matches for the title of chess king are equal. According to the rules of the championship, a short game includes four games in fast chess. Each player has twenty-five minutes per game with ten seconds added per turn.

Agree, a tie-break in chess plays a crucial role. But back to the story. If these games are ended in a draw, then the chess players will have to play two more blitz games, each of which will be allocated to the players for five minutes, and for each move - for three seconds. If the score remains the same, then the athletes will play two more blitz games (match). In general, they can play no more than five such matches.
