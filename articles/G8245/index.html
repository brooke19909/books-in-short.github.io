Famous widder

One of the best hotels in the city center of Zurich, Widder is a mixture of townhouses built in the 15th century, expertly designed to house 49 unique rooms.

You may not notice the closed facade of Widder in the Zurich quarter Augustiner, 700 meters from the center, but the location of the hotel is perfect. The train station is a five-minute walk, the financial district is around the corner, and the sights of the old town, including the Grossmunster and Lindenhof panoramic parks, are in front of the entrance. There is a suite with a terrace with a 360-degree view of the chic roofs.

The staff of the best hotel in Zurich behaves in accordance with European traditions: you hardly feel his presence with a smile, and the service is not too formal, but also not intrusive. Its purpose is to make you feel at home.

Facilities include an attractive stone and wood library with books on local history, two restaurants and a bar on the shelves. The gym, backed by huge antique pillars, contains a good selection of the latest Technogym equipment.

Each of the 49 immaculate rooms and suites is unique. For example, room 403 is laced with powerful wooden beams, decorated with cornflower-blue details, and contains a Le Corbusier sofa, and a desk with a beautiful view. At the same time, the A17 family suite boasts Baroque frescoes and the high-tech Dornbracht shower.

The rooms are thoughtfully designed: they have enough light, good soundproofing and climate control, as well as TVs. Bang & Olufsen radios are integrated in the bathroom mirror.

This hotel in the center of Zurich is very popular and must be booked in advance.

LADYs FIRST

The hotel in Zurich, the LADYs FIRST Hotel, built in Art Nouveau style, is located a few steps from the promenade, while staying at it is possible for 100 euros per day.

You can relax near Lake Zurich, just a short tram ride from the sights of the city, 500 meters from the Zurich Opera House.

As the name implies, the hotel gives women a special place, but male people are also welcome. One of the best hotels in Zurich in its price category, it is a stylish place to relax. Many historical details, from high ceilings and mosaic tiles to parquet floors and spiral staircases, have been carefully restored. The interiors are bright and decorated in a calm palette of tones. Previously, this house was a boarding school for girls, but now it has created conditions for women in need of quality relaxation.

You can relax in the Moroccan-style women's spa. This is complete bliss after a day in the city, with a hammam, steam room, sauna and cherry stone beds heated to body temperature.

A kettle, minibar, bathrobes and slippers are standard in all rooms. Bathrooms with bath or shower are equipped with Fairtrade toiletries, and in some cases are housed in a modern glass cube.

Tasty breakfast and health care

Breakfast served in a sunlit room is a good selection of freshly baked bread, homemade pie, boiled eggs, local meats and cheeses, vegetables, cereals, cereal made from birch milk, yogurt, and a gluten-free menu.

Marktgasse hotel

Marktgasse is one of the oldest hotels in Zurich, located in the heart of the city. The 39-room boutique hotel successfully combines modern minimalism with an ancient heritage, warm and welcoming. There is also a lively bar and the exclusive Baltho Restaurant.

The young staff is friendly and competent. There is a library with board games and books. The front desk also serves as a relaxation area with newspapers, coffee and sofas under the beautiful stucco ceiling. A two-minute fitness and wellness center is available to guests free of charge.

The hotel has non-smoking rooms and facilities for people with disabilities. Tourists who visited this hotel recommend taking an adapter to the Euro outlet, note the good quality of the Internet, the dazzling cleanliness of the rooms and excellent breakfasts. Living in this luxury will cost about 300 euros.

Dolder Grand - Zurich's luxury and business card

If you want to enjoy the fantastic scenery and breathe in the fresh mountain air, you need to pay attention to the magnificent Dolder Grand hotel in Zurich, Switzerland.

Majestic towers make this award-winning resort a landmark in the city, while the modern atmosphere is created by two wings designed by Norman Foster. Gallery-quality artworks, a 4,000 m2 spa and a Michelin-starred restaurant are some of its features.

To get to the hotel in Zurich, you need to travel just 15 minutes by tram from the center, take the charming historic funicular from the train station or take the shuttle from the resort to Munsterplatz in the city center, which takes about 10 minutes.

Dolder originally opened in 1899, it prides itself on its distinguished guests, including Winston Churchill, Roger Moore and Sophia Loren. Currently, his reputation as one of the best hotels in Switzerland in Zurich is maintained.

Inside, Swarovski crystal chandeliers, high ceilings and oversized flowers give off a luxurious style, but the entrance to the gallery is more striking. A combination of 19th-century elements such as Corinthian columns and ornate ceilings is an outstanding private collection of works by artists such as Salvador Dali and Henry Moore.
