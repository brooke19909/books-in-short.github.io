What is the most expensive coin in the world: description, classification and photo

Coins are banknotes that are made of metal or other solid material. They have a certain form, weight, dignity (face value). Typically, coins were made and are made by minting to give them the shape of a regular circle.

In Russian history, the word ‚Äúcoin‚Äù came during the reign of Peter I. It was taken from the Polish language. In turn, the word migrated to Poland from the Latin "moneta", which meant "mint", "warning".

A Brief History of Coins

The ancient Romans associated the word "coin" with the goddess Juno, since the temple of this celestial woman, in the city of Rome, was one of the oldest ancient mints.

Ancient Greek legends claim that the mythical heroes were the inventors of the coins. The ancient inhabitants of Rome assured: the gods Janus and Saturn invented metal banknotes. Based on the content of the stories about the life of the Olympians, the very first coins depicting a two-faced god and a ship's nose were minted by Janus, devoting them to Saturn, who arrived in Italy from Crete.

The very word "coin" in ancient Roman - "adviser", "warning."

With this word they called the goddess Juno, the wife of Jupiter. She is also known for warning the inhabitants of Rome of impending troubles. Archaeologists have established that it was at the Temple of Juno (the Roman Capitol) that workshops worked, minting and casting coins from metal.

Oldest coin

Currently, the oldest coin is considered to be a stater. It is made of an alloy containing silver and gold. There is only one image on it - a roaring lion. The reverse is smooth. Such coins are found among the ruins in Palestine. Their circulation in these parts is not accidental, since it was here that one of the centers of trade of that time was located. The age of the oldest statier is estimated at approximately 3200 years.

This money was in circulation for a very long time: from the 5th century BC to the 1st century, it is already our era. They were used in ancient Lydia and Greece, as well as among the Celtic tribes.

The most expensive pre-revolutionary Russian coin

What is the most expensive coin in tsarist Russia? It is considered silver, worth one ruble, dated 1705. It has another name - "Polish thaler." In the difficult period of the beginning of the reign of Peter I, Russia experienced a lack of silver for minting coins, which she really needed for reforms.

At this time, by order of the king, silver coins were put into circulation, the source metal for which (silver) was brought from abroad. Their design is very similar to the thaler of Poland from 1630. In 1705, a ruble coin was minted, which had an error in the date.

What are the most expensive tsarist coins of Russia? It is these silver rubles. For such a banknote with the wrong date, collectors are ready to pay more than one and a half million rubles.

Other "Polish talers", whose date is correct, are estimated at about 400 thousand rubles.
