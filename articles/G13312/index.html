Summarizing

Choosing drives of such a plan, it is necessary to take into account a number of critical characteristics. First you need to determine the amount of memory. Of course, the higher this figure, the better. But here it is important to know that, for example, the same photos can be stored in different formats. And if JPEG encodes the image at 3-4 MB, then the gluttonous RAW is already at 15-30 MB. The same goes for video codecs.

Professional technology uses more advanced formats that are designed for subsequent specific processing in powerful photo or video editors. So it‚Äôs worthwhile to clarify the working formats of the device for which you are buying a memory card.

The situation is similar with the write speed. If you are the owner of a modest smartphone with HD, and not Full HD-resolution, then chasing class 10 is impractical. In this case, 6 or even 4 classes will be quite enough, which will calmly pull the HD-spread at 30 frames per second. Grade 10 is again a professional level, and on budget gadgets it simply will not reveal its potential and will work at half strength.

It is also clearly not out of place to focus your attention on the protection indicator of the memory card. Micro-SD is one of the most fragile form factors. Besides the fact that you may accidentally step on the drive, bend it or dip it in water, it is still afraid of X-rays (hi to airports and other security checkpoints) and temperature changes.

The above manufacturers are trying to provide their devices with the maximum level of protection. Of course, premium drives are higher than budget models. But if you use a memory card in only one device and don‚Äôt worry about it or not, then you should not pay extra for excessive protection.
