History

Surprisingly, taxidermy appeared in ancient times. The first skinning skills were mastered in primitive times. Sorcerers and shamans used animal heads, paws, tails in their rituals.

The great successes in taxidermy were achieved by the ancient Egyptians. Following the owner in the afterlife, his pets were to follow, so in the tomb you can find their stuffed animals.

The history of taxidermy in its modern form dates back to the 16th century. It was at this time that the oldest stuffed rhinoceros to date was made. There was no captured culture in Russia, and the first stuffed animals were brought from Europe by order of Peter I in 1698. This marked the beginning of domestic taxidermy.

In the 19th century, thanks to the development of sciences, taxidermists received new materials at their disposal. This allowed to raise the quality of stuffed animals to a new level.





Taxidermy as an Art

Taxidermy - what is it: craft or art? The work of a taxidermist cannot be put on stream. Making each stuffed animal requires an individual approach and a lot of work. The best works can be seen in museums, at special exhibitions and taxidermy competitions.

A true artist does not just pull the skin of an animal on the frame. He creates the plot, endows the animal with character, repeats its natural plastic. The specialist studies the habits of the animal, because it is important not only to accurately depict the appearance, but also to recreate its dynamics and energy.

A good taxidermist not only knows the anatomy of the animal very well, but also carefully thinks through the composition. Planning can take more than one day. Taxidermy work requires a lot of perseverance, care and accuracy.

Animals have almost no facial muscles, so their character can not simply be conveyed by expression on the face. To make the beast angry, aggressive is quite simple - you need to bare your teeth. But in order to portray the animal as playful, alert, calm, you need to subtly feel the dynamics of the body, to have the talent of a sculptor.





How to become a taxidermy?

Many believe that almost any hunter can independently make a stuffed animal out of their prey. But this is an erroneous opinion. In order to become a taxidermy, you need to acquire specific knowledge and skills.

To make a stuffed animal of good quality, you need to study for more than one year. And the amateur crafts are easy to distinguish from the work of a real master. Products made at a low level resemble moth-bumped skins, and the expression of the muzzle of such stuffed animals can be very comical. If you look at the work performed qualitatively, you get the impression that the animal is about to come to life.

Taxidermy, which began its development as a craft, today is one of the areas of art that does not lose its fans. In order to become a taxidermy, you need:

- be neat and assiduous;

- possess knowledge in anatomy, zoology, good knowledge of animal habits;

- know chemistry;

- have the talent of a sculptor, without this you can‚Äôt convey the grace of a wild animal;

- have the talent of an artist, drawing will help to think through the composition.

As you can see, the craft of taxidermy is difficult to master, and such specialists are quite rare.





Who orders stuffed animals?

Who is the client of taxidermy studio today? Making stuffed animals is most often made to order. The circle of consumers of this rare product is very narrow, but rich. First and foremost, stuffed animals are valued at hunting clubs. They are often bought as a gift to hunters. Such a trophy can become a kind of talisman that brings good luck.

Also, frequent buyers of taxidermy works are interior designers. Scarecrows surrounded by old rifles, paintings with hunting scenes create a unique atmosphere in a country house, and it is difficult to imagine a room with a fireplace without the skins lying on the floor.

You can see stuffed animals in restaurants, bars, cafes. Often they give the name to the institution.

But the tradition of ordering a scarecrow of a deceased pet has not taken root with us. Such orders are extremely rare, and many masters are reluctant to work with cats and dogs.

The most popular trophies are wild boar, roe deer, deer, duck, pheasant, capercaillie, owl, pike, zander, perch.
