Matrix TVs. Types

Various television matrices have personal physical properties. However, they all serve the same purpose - they are responsible for image quality. Liquid crystal matrix

Recent generations of television technology have one thing in common - the principle of their work is based on the use of liquid crystals. These elements were discovered far in the 19th century, however, new types of TV screens using this component were invented recently. These crystals have a unique ability - being in a liquid state, they can hold a crystalline structure.

This phenomenon made it possible to identify the curious optical properties that were used in the new types of LED TV screens to saturate the color gamut of the image and accelerate color modeling. The fact is that each cell that makes up the matrix of crystals is divided into color segments, of which there are only three: green, blue and red. Thus, the image point is a pixel. Pictures on the screen are made up of millions of pixels. This means that in its structure, any modern TV screen consists of LCD pixels, and the way the pixel device determines the type of matrix.

TN

These are more modern types of TV screens. Such matrices are mainly used for designs of not the most expensive TVs. In exact translation, this name will sound like "twisted crystal." By using additional coverage, which allows you to expand the scope of the review, you can find models that have the designation TN + Film, representing them as a means intended to conduct scans.

This type of digital TV has become a representative of the budget line of products. Thanks to the technology used in the production of screens, you can get a very decent image, with nominal color solutions. The advantages of this type of matrix are the affordable price of the products, high color rendering speed and low power consumption.

However, TN technology has one significant drawback. For full completeness of perception of contrast and color, it is necessary to be at right angles to the screen. This is evidenced by consumer reviews. With vertical or horizontal movements of the angle of view, the picture begins to blur.

VA

This name, translated into Russian, sounds like vertical alignment. This development belongs to the specialists of the Japanese company Fujitsu. It has clear advantages over TN technology. The main advantage of this technology is the ability of crystals to interconnect with neighboring ones. This can significantly improve the saturation of the color gamut of the image.

The lack of viewing angle, expressed in the previous brand, was compensated for by the perpendicular position of all the cylinders of the crystals with respect to the rear filter at the time when the current supply to the contacts of the transistors ceases. Under this condition, a natural, black color is obtained.

In this technology, the reaction of liquid crystals to the action of a transmitted voltage is slower than in the previous embodiment. However, this problem is solved using a system designed to dynamically increase the passing current. How does the system work? It has an effect on certain parts of the surface, the reaction of which is slowed down.

This technology allows these types of LCD TVs with VA-matrices to become more acceptable for collective viewing and for use as media in communications at presentations and shows.

Today it is considered the most high-quality development, which is used in different types of LED-screens.
