Current transformer: principle of operation and scope

When working in high-current AC voltage circuits, it becomes necessary to use compact devices with which you can use the non-contact method to control the necessary parameters. For these purposes, a current transformer is widely used, which, in addition to measuring, performs an additional number of useful functions. Such devices are manufactured on an industrial scale and have a wide variety of designs. Distinctive properties of these devices are the measuring range, the degree of protection of the device itself and its design features.



A modern current transformer uses in its work a simple principle, known since the invention of electricity. When working under load in the conductor, it generates an electromagnetic field, which is captured by a sensitive device. The greater the intensity of the field, the, accordingly, the greater the current flows in the conductor. It remains only to recalculate the gain of the device and give the selected signal to the control, control or protection circuit.

Thus, the current transformer plays the role of a frame on the power cable and responds to changes in the supply network. Modern devices consist of a large number of turns and have a good transmission coefficient.
