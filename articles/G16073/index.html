Social environment

Socio-psychological phenomena begin to be determined and appear when the interaction of an individual, group and social environment occurs. What is a social environment? This is all that surrounds any of us in his usual social life. The social environment is an object of mental reflection, which in itself is a mediated or unmediated result of labor.



Throughout his life, a social personality is influenced by all sorts of factors that are caused by the specifics of its environment. Development occurs under the influence of them.

The social environment is nothing more than a specific formation of specific social relations that are at a certain stage of their own development. In the same environment, independently and dependent on each other, there are many individuals, social groups. They constantly intersect, interacting with each other. A direct social environment is being formed, as well as a microenvironment.

In the psychological aspect, the social environment acts as a kind of totality of relations between groups and individuals. It is worth noting the moment of subjectivity in the totality of relations arising between an individual and a group.



With all this, the person has a certain degree of autonomy. First of all, it is about the fact that it can freely (or relatively freely) move from group to group. Such actions are necessary in order to find their own social environment that meets all the necessary social parameters.

Immediately, we note that the mobility of a social personality is not at all absolute. Its limitations are associated with the objective framework that have socio-economic relations. Also, much depends on the class structure of society. Despite all this, personality activity is one of the determining factors.

In relation to the personality, the social environment has a relatively random character. In psychological terms, this accident is very significant. Since the relationship of the individual with her environment largely depends on her individual characteristics.



A fairly widespread opinion that the socio-economic formation is nothing more than the highest abstraction belonging to the system of social relations is true. Note that everything in it is based on fixing only global features.

The social environment of a teenager, adult, and any other person is where the person does not just stay, but receives certain attitudes with which he will continue to live. No one will doubt the fact that our opinion is largely due to certain internal attitudes, which themselves were developed under the influence of the social environment in which we have been for a long time. The most powerful development and intensive fixing of these attitudes occurs, of course, in childhood.

A person does not form himself completely, since a significant part of him is formed by those social groups in which he is a member. Public influence is always great.
