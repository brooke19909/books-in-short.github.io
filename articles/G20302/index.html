How to make a crow out of paper: origami and puppet show

How to make a crow out of paper in different ways? Easy! Even a child will cope with this task, so engage him in creating crafts. Needlework very well develops hand motility.

Origami Technique





Perhaps the most popular technique with which you will learn how to make a crow out of paper with your own hands is origami. What do we have to do:

  1. Take a black sheet of A4, fold one edge to the opposite side. Cut off the excess. There should be a square.
  2. Fold the sheet in half twice and unfold. This step is needed to create fold lines.
  3. Do the same, folding diagonally.
  4. Lay the sheet in front of you like a rhombus. Grasp the edges and join them together along the fold line. It should make a square.
  5. Bend the front side corners to the center.
  6. Bend the triangle remaining at the top. Then return it to its previous position.
  7. Turn the sheet over and along the fold lines begin to gently open the rhombus.
  8. Bend the triangles on the sides to the other side towards the center. Repeat steps 6 through 7.
  9. Now make an inner crease on each side.
  10. Fold the top half of the sheet down the crease. Lift one part up.
  11. Turn the sheet over and fold in half vertically.
  12. Make an internal back fold.

Done!

From improvised materials

Want to learn how to make a crow out of paper and improvised materials? Then take:

  • Toilet paper sleeve.
  • Glue.
  • Scissors.
  • Template.
  • Black paper.

Another variant

To create another crow, take:

  • Paper bag.
  • Black paint or black paper.
  • Scissors.
  • Glue.
  • Template.

What do we have to do:

  1. Print a template and cut out the details.
  2. The paper bag should be completely flat, like a sheet of paper.
  3. Tape the bag with black paper or paint.
  4. Glue the wings on the side at the bend.
  5. Glue the eyes to the bottom, then the beak and scallop.
  6. There should be a tail at the back.
  7. Glue the legs from the bottom to the inside of the bag.
