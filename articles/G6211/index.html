Leonardo da Vinci: where he was born, what he became famous for, interesting facts

Leonardo da Vinci, whose years of life and death the whole world knows, is perhaps the most mysterious figure of the Renaissance. Many care about where Leonardo da Vinci was born and who he was. He is known as an artist, anatomist and engineer. In addition to numerous discoveries, this unique man left behind a huge number of different puzzles that the whole world is trying to solve to this day.

Biography

When was Leonardo da Vinci born? He was born on April 15, 1452. It is interesting to know where Leonardo da Vinci was born, and specifically in which city. There is nothing easier. His surname came from the name of the place of birth. Vinci is an Italian city in the then existing Florentine republic.

Leonardo was the illegitimate child of an official and an ordinary peasant girl. The boy grew up and was brought up in the house of his father, thanks to which he received a good education.

As soon as the future genius was 15 years old, he went to the apprenticeship with Andrea del Verocchio, who was a talented sculptor, painter and representative of the Florentine school.

Once a teacher, Leonardo took up one interesting work. He agreed to write an altar image in the church of Santi Salvi, which depicted the baptism of Christ by John. Young da Vinci participated in this work. He wrote only one angel, which turned out to be an order of magnitude more beautiful than the whole image. This circumstance was the reason that Andrea del Verocchio decided never to pick up his hands again. His young, but incredibly talented student was able to surpass his teacher.

After another 5 years, Leonardo da Vinci becomes a member of the guild of artists. There he began with particular passion to study the basics of drawing and many other compulsory disciplines. A little later, in 1476, he continued to work together with the former teacher and mentor Andrea del Verocchio, but already as a co-author of his creations.

The long-awaited glory

By 1480, the name of Leonardo da Vinci became famous. I wonder when Leonardo da Vinci was born, could his contemporaries suggest that he would become so famous? During this period, the artist receives the largest and most expensive orders, but after two years decides to leave his hometown and moves to Milan. There he continues to work, writes several successful paintings and the famous fresco "The Last Supper".

It is during this period of life that Leonardo da Vinci begins to keep his own diary. From there we learn that he is no longer just an artist, but also an architect-designer, hydraulics, anatomist, inventor of all kinds of mechanisms and decorations. In addition to all this, he also finds time to compose riddles, fables or puzzles. Moreover, interest in music awakens in him. And this is only a small part of what Leonardo da Vinci became famous for.

Some time later, a genius realizes that mathematics is much more interesting than painting. He is so keen on exact science that he forgets to think about painting. In 1516, the life of an Italian genius changed dramatically. He is noticed by the king of France Francis I, truly admired by his work, and invited to the court. Later, the sculptor Benvenuto Cellini will write that although Leonardo's main work was a very prestigious post of court adviser, he did not forget about his work.

What did the great creator leave to the world?

What is famous for Leonardo da Vinci in addition to paintings? Undoubtedly, he was talented in many areas, which, it would seem, cannot be combined with each other at all. However, in spite of all his genius, he had one entertaining character trait that did not fit in with his business very much - he liked to abandon the work he had begun and leave it so forever. Nevertheless, Leonardo da Vinci nevertheless brought to the end several truly brilliant discoveries. They turned the then ideas about life.

The discoveries of Leonardo da Vinci are amazing. What can we say about the person who created the whole science? Do you know paleontology? But it is precisely Leonardo da Vinci who is her ancestor. It was he who first made an entry in his diary about a certain rare fossil that he managed to discover. Scientists are still wondering what was being discussed. Only an approximate description is known: a certain stone, similar to petrified honeycombs and having a hexagonal shape. Leonardo also described the first ideas about paleontology as a science in general.

Thanks to da Vinci, people learned to jump from planes without crashing. After all, it was he who invented the parachute. Of course, initially it was only a prototype of a modern parachute and it looked completely different, but the importance of the invention does not become less from this. In his diary, the master wrote about a piece of linen fabric, 11 meters long and wide. He was sure that this would help a man to land without any injuries. And as time has shown, he was absolutely right.

Of course, the helicopter was invented much later than Leonardo da Vinci died, but the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe aircraft belongs to him. It does not at all resemble what we now call a helicopter, but rather resembles an inverted round table with one leg, to which pedals are screwed. It was due to them that the invention was supposed to fly.

Unbelievable, but it is a fact

What did Leonardo da Vinci create yet? Incredibly, he also had a hand in robotics. Just think, back in the 15th century, he personally constructed the first model of the so-called robot. His invention had many complex mechanisms and springs. But most importantly - this robot was humanoid and even knew how to move its hands. In addition, the Italian genius came up with several mechanical lions. They could move by themselves using mechanisms such as sentries.

Leonardo da Vinci made so many discoveries on earth that he became interested in something new in space. He could watch the stars for hours. And although you can‚Äôt say that he came up with a telescope, in one of his books you can find instructions for creating something very similar to him.

Even with our cars we owe da Vinci. He came up with a wooden car layout having three wheels. All this design was set in motion by a special mechanism. Many scholars believe that this idea was born back in 1478.

Among other things, Leonardo was fond of military affairs. He came up with multi-barrel and rapid-fire weapons - a machine gun, or rather, its prototype.

Of course, Leonardo da Vinci could not help but come up with something for the painters. It was he who developed the artistic technique in which all distant things seem blurry. He also came up with chiaroscuro.

It is worth noting that all the discoveries of Leonardo da Vinci were very useful, and some of his developments are used today. They are only slightly improved.

Nevertheless, we cannot but admit that Leonardo da Vinci, whose contribution to science was enormous, was a real genius.

Water - a favorite element of Leonardo da Vinci

If you like diving or at least once in your life diving to a considerable depth, then thank Leonardo da Vinci. It was he who invented scuba gear. Da Vinci constructed a floating cork buoy that held a reed tube for air above the water. He also came up with a leather bag for air.

Leonardo da Vinci, biology

Genius was interested in everything: the principles of breathing, yawning, coughing, vomiting, and especially heartbeat. Leonardo da Vinci studied biology, closely linking it with physiology. It was he who first described the heart as a muscle and almost came to the conclusion that it pumps blood in the human body. Yes, Vicny even made an attempt to create an aortic valve prosthesis through which blood flow passed.

Anatomy as an Art

Everyone knows that da Vinci was interested in anatomy. In 2005, researchers discovered his secret laboratory, where he allegedly dissected stony corpses. And this, apparently, had an effect. It was da Vinci who accurately described the shape of the human spine. Among other things, it is believed that he discovered diseases such as atherosclerosis and arteriosclerosis. Another Italian managed to excel in dentistry. Leonardo was the first person who depicted the correct structure of teeth in the oral cavity, describing in detail their number.

Wear glasses or lenses? And for that it‚Äôs worth saying thanks to Leonardo. In 1509, he wrote down in the diary a certain model of how and with which you can change the optical power of the human eye.
