Informational messages in journalism and computer science. Mobile info: how to disable

Newsletters, notifications, letters - a very broad concept that can be found in various fields. Therefore, it is worth briefly analyzing its essence and some particular features.

Parsing a concept

An informational message is a concise text containing data that is news to its readers. The reason for its creation is an informational occasion. Such a message can also be defined as informative information transmitted both in writing and in various electronic ways. Most often, the concept is associated with information agencies, radio stations, television, periodicals, etc. Informational publications thus form the informational panorama of the day.





An informational message is also a group of interconnected elementary data elements. It may contain at least two such overlapping components.

The structure of the information message

Speaking of what an informational message is, we briefly touch upon the ideas of its structure by some theorists.

M. Grigoryan believes that the information message makes three factors interesting for the audience. This is the presence of a certain conflict, proximity in space and time. The parameters of the correct informational message should be as follows:

  • unusual factors - the news of an extraordinary event, a person who does not fit into the framework of everyday life;
  • the availability of information about celebrities - not only "at the parade", but also about their personal lives, hobbies, everyday life;
  • affecting a wide audience - any event is more interesting if a large number of people take part in it.

According to Kolesnichenko, any informational publication consists of three important components:

  • core - the element is always at the beginning of the message and immediately reveals all information about the event that interests the reader; it is always placed here that primarily interests a person;
  • details - here the basic information is supplemented by a number of interesting information, circumstances;
  • background - a text that is not directly related to an informational occasion, but helps to understand its essence (statistics, historical background, excerpt from the dictionary, etc.)




Finally, we analyze the position of Tretyakov. He believes that the information message should have at least three substantial layers:

  • basic information - bare facts, details;
  • primary objective commentary - information that places a message in a series of similar ones, determining their relationship;
  • secondary objective comment - here the reader sees the forecast of the development of the event;
  • subjective commentary (optional part) - the opinion of the direct participant, expert, observer, etc.

Information Messages and Examination

Surely, many who have recently passed or are just preparing for the Unified State Exam in Informatics remember this beginning of the task condition from the proposed training tests: "Information message in volume ..." The task specified a certain message volume (say, 5 Kb) and the number of characters in it (randomly - 2560 ) It was proposed to find the number of characters of the alphabet with which this message was written.





To solve it, it was necessary to know only one formula - the power (number of characters) of the alphabet: N = 2 i (N - power, i - bit / character).

Using our values, we calculate how many bits one character "weighs": 5Kb / 2560 = 5 * 1024 bytes / 2560 = 5120/2560 = 2 bits. Now we substitute the value in the formula: N = 2 2 = 4 characters in this alphabet.

Information messages from telecom operators

Many of us are familiar with informational messages not from a computer science course, but as a result of using a smartphone. Here it is pop-up messages of informational, entertaining, cognitive nature that appear on the screen of the gadget. They are usually not stored in the phone. Through such information messages, depending on the telecom operator, the following information can be obtained:

  • daily News;
  • weather forecast;
  • Exchange Rates;
  • horoscope;
  • information about the holidays;
  • review of recent events in the city;
  • new promotions and offers from the operator;
  • entertaining content, etc.




Basically, these messages are promotional in nature. For the most part, they are paid - you can read the continuation of a news or story from a message only for a certain price.

Disabling information messaging on the phone

For most users, this newsletter is not only unnecessary, but also significantly interferes - dozens of information messages can arrive on the gadget per day. The news they contain is not unique - you can find out these data in a couple of clicks on the Internet. The ways to disable such messages depend on the selected operator:

  • "Tele 2". Go to "Tele2-Menu", find "Tele2-themes", then select "Disable".
  • MTS. Open the SIM card menu - you will need MTS services, and then MTS news. Turn off the newsletter on topics that you are not interested in. The command * 111 * 1212 * 2 # will help to completely get rid of information messages.
  • Beeline. In the SIM menu, you will need the "Info Channels" tab. Next, go to "Themes" and cancel uninteresting subscriptions. The team * 110 * 20 # will help to rid yourself of all of them at once.
  • "Megaphone". Here you will also need a SIM card menu, then "Kaleidoscope" and "Subscription" - here you can turn off messages with uninteresting information. You can completely remove information messages by: "Kaleidoscope" - "Settings" - "Broadcasting" - "Disable".
