Turkey, Kemer: traveler reviews

Turkey is a country that has been a favorite vacation spot of Russians for more than a year. And there are many reasons for this, including geographical proximity, the absence of the need for many hours of flight, the climate that you can get used to relatively easily, the presence of a large number of attractions, etc. Among Turkish resorts, Kemer occupies a special place, most of which are admired by tourists. And this is not surprising, because there is a developed infrastructure, beautiful nature, there are many hotels of different levels, and you can have an excellent rest from the beginning of May to the end of October. So, what can Kemer offer (photos, reviews and information are presented below)?

Features

Kemer is a famous Turkish beach resort located on the Mediterranean coast. Until 1910, there were only a few poor villages on its territory that constantly suffered from seasonal debris flow from the mountains. The situation has changed dramatically after the construction of protective structures in the form of a stone belt. He gave the name to the future resort, which began to develop rapidly from the beginning of the 1990s. Since that time, thousands of Russians have visited Kemer hotels, reviews of which are rarely negative.

Today at this resort you can find accommodation options for every taste and budget, therefore it is one of the most affordable places for a beach holiday in Turkey.





Hotels

Holidays in Kemer (reviews see below) are attractive primarily because most hotels operate on the all-inclusive system so beloved by Russians. Moreover, the resort has options for both youth and family vacations. In the latter case, however, tourists should know that Kemer beaches are pebble. This fact makes them not the best place to relax with kids. At the same time, some hotels have loose sandy beaches, however, pebbles still begin at the water's edge, which is a source of potential danger to children.

Excursions in Kemer

Almost all resort hotels at the request of customers can help with organizing interesting excursions to local attractions. For example, the ruins of Phaselis, an ancient city surrounded by the majestic trees of the natural park, are of great interest to tourists. There is an ancient amphitheater and fortress walls, cobbled streets with the remains of houses and public buildings, which are more than one thousand years old. It is worth visiting Olympos, founded in the second century AD, to see the incredibly beautiful Beldibi cave complex, and also to climb the fiery mountain Yanartash, which rises above the town of Cirali.





There are many interesting sights in Kemer itself. Especially in this regard, those who settled in city hotels are lucky. In particular, in the very center of Kemer there is a real colorful oriental bazaar, a clock tower and a monument to the founder of the Turkish Republic and the city itself - Ataturk. There you can see the fountain with a secret, delighting the children, whose jets begin to beat from under the ground when they please.
