The simplest pleated skirt, the base pattern of which consists of a rectangular piece of fabric, is very easy to sew. The main thing is to correctly calculate the footage of the fabric. The total tissue consumption is determined by the formula: the thigh circumference is multiplied by 3. Two-thirds of the width of the material will be used to form folds. Suppose you need to take 3 meters of fabric (hip circumference - 100 cm) and form 20 folds. Divide 300 cm by the number of folds, we get 15 centimeters. This is the total length of the fold, which can be distributed on the product as follows: 5 cm remains on the outside of the skirt, 10 cm will enter the fold. Thus, the entire pattern of the skirt on the wrong side is calculated and plotted. The folds are cleaved with pins, smoothed and swept to the belt. When installing the skirt panel to the belt, the surface rib of the fold is pulled up half a centimeter. This is necessary so that the folds do not separate in the future. During the first fitting, the depth of the folds is adjusted and adjusted at the waist. Then you can grind all the seams on a typewriter.

If you add the upper part with tucks to the fabric with formed folds, you get a skirt in a crease on the yoke. Such a skirt can be worn even by girls with full hips, because the coquette hides the flaws of the figure. You can combine it with any blouses, cardigans, trousers.
