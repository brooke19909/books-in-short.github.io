Translated, the name of the city means "sacred forest." The fact is that in the distant past there was an ordinary fishing village on the site of Ayia Napa, and a very dense forest grew nearby. Once one hunter went for the game, but got lost, he walked through the thickets for a long time until he went to the cave, where he found the icon of the Virgin. The man returned home and told about the find, since then pilgrims went to the cave, and this place was considered sacred.
