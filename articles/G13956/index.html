Phuket Climate: features, monthly air temperature, tips for tourists when to go

Thailand is a state in Southeast Asia with centuries-old culture and traditions, ancient Buddhist temples, a tropical jungle and, of course, snow-white sandy beaches of the Gulf of Thailand. Having been here once, you will want to return again and again.

Features of rest in Thailand

Holidays in Thailand are very diverse, it all depends on the goals that you pursue.

To the northeast is Pang Nga Bay with the famous Fr. James Bond. The islands of Koh Chang, Koh Samui and Krabi have beautiful beaches and quiet hotels, ready to welcome their guests.

Many tourists from all over the world consider the island of Phuket the most affordable and attractive resort in Thailand. The unique shape of the island and stunning nature allow us to confidently call this place the pearl of the Andaman Sea. Once visiting the resort, it is impossible to break away from the beautiful landscape: green groves, coconut plantations, pristine beaches and the blue sea.

Island climate

Phuket is located in the tropical zone, so it has a humid and warm climate. On the island you can relax in the summer and winter. It is especially nice to run away from the severe winter frosts and find yourself on the beach, being warmed by the warm rays of the sun and the cozy climate of Phuket.

Fans of cooler climates can visit Thailand during the rainy season. This does not mean that you have to spend the whole vacation at the hotel. After a short rainfall, the sultry heat recedes, so you can enjoy the cool and refreshing sea.

Two seasons

Many are interested in what climate in Phuket and what are the features. The rainy season is characterized by an increase in rainfall. Powerful waves and winds often appear in this season, therefore it is not always possible to be on the beach.

But even during the rainy season the vital activity on the island does not stop at all. In addition to beach activities, there are many other types of recreation that the clouds will not be an obstacle. And some are waiting for this particular time to go surfing.

Understanding the difference in seasons and stop fearing rainfall will certainly help the perception of why the climate of Phuket is such. The whole problem is that the climate in many regions of Southeast Asia is controlled by monsoon winds.

In the summer season, they blow from the sea to land, and in the winter, from land to sea. So, in the winter months, dry air comes to Phuket, and in the summer months on the island more moisture evaporates over the sea.

The beach season begins in October and ends in April. There is less rainfall in this period, the sea is quiet, there are practically no clouds. The best is the period from the end of December to February. On New Year's holidays, rental housing and plane tickets to Phuket are getting more expensive.

During the beach season, it also rains, but they usually come at night. During the day, rain is extremely rare, and if it does, it lasts 15 minutes and no more. Rainfall in Phuket does not look like cold and gloomy rain in Russia. A little rain after the heat refreshes and helps to cheer up.

In the winter months, the average air temperature is 30 ° -32 °, at night - 25 ° -27 °. The water temperature is also high - 28 ° -30 °. On all the main beaches during the beach season, waves in the water are very weak or completely absent, thus, nothing prevents the rest on the beaches of Phuket. But when sunbathing and bathing, do not forget that the sun is well baked here, it is very simple to sunbathe, including not being under direct rays.

Phuket climate by month and temperature

It is best to consider the situation on the island in more detail. Climate in Thailand (Phuket) for months:

  1. January. The weather at the very beginning of the year is perfect for those travelers who have decided to visit the island with a child. Warm water in the Gulf of Andaman allows children to spend a lot of time in the water without fear of catching cold. Daytime air temperatures often exceed + 30 °.
  2. February. The weather throughout Thailand creates excellent conditions for entertainment, and even more so in Phuket. In the daytime, the temperature approaches + 32 °, which makes being on the beaches at noon not very comfortable.
  3. March. March in Phuket does not cease to delight travelers who have decided to spend the beginning of spring on the island. Air is still warmed up to + 31 ° ... + 33 °, and water - up to + 28 °.
  4. April. In April, the island is very hot. The weather conditions of this month are characterized by high air temperature in the daytime, approximately + 32 ° ... + 34 °, which simply obliges you to follow safety measures.
  5. May. At the end of May, the rainy season begins. Therefore, the number of tourists is declining and package prices are falling significantly.
  6. June. Windsurfers will like June, because monsoons begin in Phuket this month.
  7. July. Entertainment in July can hardly be called comfortable, since it rains almost regularly, often of a continuous nature.
  8. August. In August, the island has a maximum rainy season, combined with high temperature and the usual stuffiness for Thailand. Temperature during the day + 31 °.
  9. September. September on the island is also compounded by strong winds. September is the worst month to travel to Thailand.
  10. October. The air temperature in the daytime approaches + 32 ° C, at night it gets a little cooler than in previous months. The intensity of precipitation is significantly reduced.
  11. November. The weather is normalizing and precipitation is falling less and less. Precipitation in Phuket in November is insignificant, and it rains mostly at night.
  12. December. The best holiday in absolutely all respects in Phuket is December. The weather in this month is characterized by average air temperature (approximately + 29 °), night in the region of + 25 ° promotes energetic relaxation, visiting discos and nightclubs. The chance of precipitation is extremely small.

Phuket beaches

Phuket has gained popularity among tourists with its stunning beaches with white sand. On the western side of the island are the best of them. Island beaches meet the needs of tourists in a relaxing holiday, and in active ways to relax.

Bang Tao Beach

Bang Tao is a luxurious beach with developed infrastructure and a variety of tourist services. Numerous five-star hotels offer their wealthy customers to taste the cuisines of all countries of the world, visit first-class SPA-salons and fitness rooms.

Surin Beach

Surin is a cozy beach that allows tourists to plunge into the world of Thai culture. The hotel complexes are made in a rustic style, which makes the beach more attractive for tourists every year. Patong is Phuket's most visited beach. Young and active tourists will find many exciting entertainments here, it is in Patong that the nightlife is raging.

Karon Beach

Karon Beach is a clean and comfortable beach, which is great for a relaxing holiday with children. Well-developed infrastructure, which allows tourists to find everything they need. Kamala Beach is an almost untouched beauty. The beach attracts tourists who seek at least a few hours to retire and stay away from the outside world.

Useful information and tips for tourists

The climate of Thailand is conditionally divided into two seasons: wet (April-November), when there are tropical showers, and dry (December-March), without precipitation with a temperature of 30 degrees. On the islands, seasonality is more pronounced than on the mainland.

The monetary unit is baht, which are almost equal to the ruble. Exchange points and ATMs are at every step, they set each course. Find more profitable. In stores and large shopping centers accept plastic cards.

24-hour meals at all resorts and very cheap. Do not be afraid to eat in street cafes, no one will poison you. Always bargain, but do not raise your voice. And smile! Thailand is a hospitable country of smiles.
