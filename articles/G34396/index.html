Supsekh village: reviews of vacationers

Supsekh village is located just two kilometers from one of the most popular Russian resorts - the city of Anapa. The opinion of the tourists in this place was simply wonderful. You can have a good time in the village of Supsekh for lovers of beach or outdoor activities.





A bit of history

Vacationers have good reviews about the village of Supsekh primarily because it is located in an unusually picturesque place. The territory on which this settlement is located today began to populate back in the Stone Age. Later, in ancient times, a large settlement was founded here, which lasted for more than 700 years. Mostly Greeks who lived in it were engaged in the cultivation and sale of wheat.

In the Middle Ages, the Adygs settled this territory. In the vicinity of Supsekh settlement, Russian archaeologists discovered burials of the 11th-12th centuries characteristic of this people. In 1479, the Black Sea coast was conquered by the Ottoman Turks. Supsekh, like many other settlements of the Caucasus, went to Russia only in 1829, after the signing of the Adrianople Treaty.

The village today

The modern history of this ancient village began in the late XIX - early XX centuries. During this period, the territory of the Black Sea coast was actively inhabited by immigrants from Russia, Asian countries, and the Mediterranean. Today, more than 11 thousand people of various nationalities live in the village. Hundreds of tourists annually choose Supsekh for relaxation. Reviews available on the Internet about the attractiveness of this place for a summer vacation speak more than eloquently. Many vacationers consider this resort one of the best on the Black Sea coast.

Climate in Supsekh

Summer in this resort, like in any other Black Sea, is sunny, quite soft and very warm. It rains very rarely in Supsekh. In June, the sun at its zenith is 70 degrees above the horizon. The abundance of heat in these places is favorably combined with moderate humidity. Winter in the village of Suspech is also not too cold, and in autumn the air temperature is usually higher than in spring. The first northeast, carrying cold air masses, begin to blow only in early November. Guest reviews indicate that the swimming season in the area opens in late spring and ends in late October. The average air temperature in winter in Supsekh is at -13 degrees, in summer it is about +23. It rains in the warm season mostly only at night.

St. Andrew's Gorge

There is in the resort town Supsekh (Anapa), reviews of which are excellent, and another natural attraction. Near this village is located an interesting, including in historical terms, St. Andrew's Gorge. There are a lot of crystal clear springs in this area. In ancient times, a ceramic water supply system to the Greek city of Gorgippia was carried out from the deepest source of the gorge. The latter under Mithridates Eupator in the Bosporus kingdom was considered one of the most important settlements. At the end of the 19th century, from the same silver source of the Andreevsky Gorge, as in antiquity, a water pipe was laid in Anapa.

Vineyards

At the end of the 19th ‚Äì beginning of the 20th centuries, the lands of the village of Supsekh (Krasnodar Territory), reviews of which are excellent today, belonged to E. A. Prokopovich. It was this landowner who first pitched a huge vineyard in the territory adjacent to the village. Later, various varieties of this crop were grown in Supsekh. However, the best was always considered "Riesling." Wine made from berries of this variety was very popular, including in Europe. To the court of the English kings, for example, it was delivered daily.

The village was associated with this wine by one even very interesting story. In Soviet times, the first secretary of the krai committee of the Krasnodar Territory at the reception with the Queen of Great Britain was treated in that numbers with wine from the village of Supsekh. Being unaware that this wonderful drink was once made on Kuban land, the official got into trouble, asking where he was brought from. Returning home, the first secretary gathered a meeting at which they decided to revive the production of Riesling wine in Supsekh. Which was soon implemented. Today, unfortunately, most of the grape plantings on the site between Anapa and Supsekh are cut down. Those lands on which this culture, traditional for the Caucasus, once grew, were given for development.
