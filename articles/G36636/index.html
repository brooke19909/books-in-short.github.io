Everyone who is interested in the history of the Russian state, sooner or later had to deal with jokes, which some decrees of Peter 1 have turned into today. From our article you will learn about many unexpected decrees of this tsar-reformer, which turned the public life of the country from the end of the 17th to the beginning of the 18th centuries that is called, upside down.

Today, the decrees of Peter 1 are studied in schools and institutes. Some of them are made fun of, while others are perceived as the norm. But this applies to the present time. At the beginning of the XVIII century, these documents were for the majority "blasphemy and devilry."





Some decrees of the tsar, for example, the decree on the right of succession of Peter 1, led to intrigue. Others influenced fashion, economics, and the army. Only one thing remains certain: by tough methods, the king tried to renew the stagnant society of his time.

Succession Order

One of the most significant in the history of the state was the decree on the unanimity of Peter 1. It was published in 1722. The document changed all the foundations of power. Now the heir was not the eldest in the family, but the one whom the emperor would appoint as his successor.

This decree on the succession to Peter 1 was canceled only by Emperor Paul I in 1797. Prior to this, it served as the basis for many palace coups, murders and intrigues. Although initially conceived by Peter, as a preventive measure against the conservative mood of people dissatisfied with reforms.

New Year

We propose to consider the most popular decrees of Peter 1. Perhaps the most famous today are two laws: on the celebration of the new year and on beards. We will talk about the second below. As for the first decree, according to the tsar‚Äôs will, starting from 1700, the reckoning in Russia passed to the European manner.

That is, now the year began not in September, but on the first of January. The chronology was conducted from the birth of Christ, and not from the creation of the world, as it was before. Thus, instead of the fourth month of 7208, it became the first of 1700.

Beards

Probably the most famous innovation of the Russian Tsar after his return from Europe concerned the fashion for beards. Below will be given many decrees of Peter 1, funny and serious. But not one of them caused such indignation among the boyars as this one.





So, at the age of twenty-six, the sovereign gathered representatives of noble families, took scissors and cut off some of his beards. Such actions plunged society into shock.

But the young king did not stop there. He introduced a tax on beards. Anyone who wanted to preserve facial hair was required to pay a certain amount annually to the treasury.

So, for the nobility it was six hundred rubles a year, for merchants a hundred, Posadsky cost sixty, and servants and others thirty. It should be noted that these were very serious amounts for those times. Only peasants were exempted from this annual tax, but they had to pay a penny from a beard for entering the city.

Fashion issues

Many decrees of Peter 1 related to public life. With their help, the tsar tried to give the Russian nobility a European look.

Firstly, having spent significant funds on the construction of St. Petersburg, the sovereign took care of the service time of wooden pavements. Therefore, a ban was issued on metal heels. For their establishment fines were imposed, and for the sale - confiscation of property and hard labor.

The next moment concerned the army. Since Peter the Great was seriously engaged in its updating and improvement, attention was drawn to literally every little thing. About construction and quality

One of the most famous is the decree of Peter 1 on quality. It is not as popular as many funny laws adopted by the tsar, but with its help the Russian army was able to win near Poltava.

So, having discovered that the guns from the Tula plant are not of very good quality, the emperor ordered the owner and the person responsible for the products to be arrested. Then they were sentenced to death by whips and sent into exile. Peter the Great decided to carefully monitor the quality of products manufactured at the plant. For control, he sent the whole weapons order to Tula. Any marriage was punishable by rods. In addition, the king ordered the new owner, Demidov, to build huts for all workers, as the owner had.

No less interesting is the decree of Peter 1 on construction. When the king intended to begin the construction of St. Petersburg, he forbade the construction of stone houses throughout the country. Therefore, all specialists came to work for the Neva.
Thus, the sovereign in the shortest possible time was able to build a city.

Economic decrees

Introduced Peter the Great 1 and economic innovations. We will consider three decrees that have largely shaken the traditional Russian foundations.

So, according to the first decree, the state introduced "opposition to promises and bribes." For such misconduct, a death penalty was presumed. To prevent the reasons pushing officials to commit a crime, the emperor increased the salary of civil servants. But at the same time it was forbidden "all bribery, trade, in a row and promises."

At that time, the medical practice of people far enough even from the basics of this craft was widespread in Russia. Therefore, one of the laws prohibited "the implementation of pharmaceutical and medical activities to all persons who do not have rights to do so."

Beautification measures

Sovereign Peter the Great 1, after returning from a trip to Western Europe, seriously decided what is called to restore order in the Russian Empire. In addition to many other issues, the problems of cleanliness, fire safety and improvement were raised.

Firstly, the law ‚ÄúOn Cleanliness in Moscow‚Äù was adopted. The second decree concerned exclusively shipbuilding and the navy. According to him, when repairing ships and living on them, all waste should be disposed of. If at least one shovel of garbage falls into the water, punishment was imposed. For the first offense, it was in the amount of the monthly salary, and for the second - six months. For the third shovel of garbage into the river, the officers were demoted to rank and file, and ordinary sailors were exiled to Siberia.

A fire safety decree has also been adopted. It instructed homeowners to re-equip all the stoves with a stone foundation. It was also instructed to make brickwork between the wall and the stove, and to conduct pipes in which "a person will climb." It was necessary to clean such a design once a month. For non-compliance with this rule fines were imposed.

Population census

As well as the decree on the succession of Peter 1, this provision was simply necessary for the state. In view of the ongoing military campaigns, the country constantly needed finances to support the army. Therefore, the emperor issued an order to conduct a homestead census.

But this measure did not give the proper result. The landlords did not want to pay taxes "no one knows where," since the country was already tired of the constant war. Therefore, Peter Alekseevich had to conduct such a census several times, since with each new number of yards decreased.

As we mentioned earlier, Peter I in his decrees covered all sectors of society. So often not only men, but also ladies. We give one example.
