How to reanimate a phone‚Äôs battery after deep discharge with a battery

If the battery is fully discharged and has not been charged for a long time, then it may well not respond to normal charging. In this case, you can try to charge it from another battery. For this procedure you will need:

  • Nine-volt battery.
  • Ten centimeters of electrical tape.
  • Two ordinary thin electrical wires.
  • Directly "killed" battery.

Then do the following:

  1. Wrap electrical tape around the wires, leaving the edges on both sides free.
  2. Connect one wire at one end to the plus terminal and the other wire to the minus terminal. You can understand the contacts by marking. Be sure to use two different wires.
  3. Tape the wires with electrical tape.
  4. Connect the other ends of the wires to the plus and minus of the battery, respectively. Be sure to connect the plus of the battery with the plus of the battery, and the minus of the battery with the minus of the battery! Otherwise, a short circuit may occur, which will result in electric shock and damage to both power sources.
  5. Tape the wires with electrical tape to the battery.

After these manipulations, wait until the phone battery is a little warm. This usually takes about a minute. After that, allow the battery to cool and place it on the phone. If the phone turns on, then congratulations - you just found out how to reanimate the phone‚Äôs battery!

Low temperature slightly restores the energy of the battery and allows you to effectively charge it from conventional chargers. By the way, sometimes it helps in the event that the battery just got worse to hold a charge.

How to restore the previous battery capacity

If your battery is not "dead", but simply has become worse to hold a charge, then at home with a few manipulations you can return the capacity to it for a while. To do this, you need this part, a current source with adjustable voltage, a rheostat and a voltmeter.

  1. Connect a rheostat and a voltmeter in parallel to the battery.
  2. Lower the voltage to one volt, but not lower than 0.9 volts.
  3. Make sure that the battery is not hotter than 50 ¬∞ C. If it gets hotter, turn it off and cool to room temperature.
  4. Wait approximately 15 minutes.
  5. Connect the battery and ammeter in series, and the voltmeter and current source in parallel. Connect one contact of the voltmeter to the free pole of the battery, and the other to the contact of the ammeter.
  6. After that, slowly attach the temperature sensor to the battery and set the minimum voltage with the regulator.
  7. Then carefully lift it until the current strength is equal to one tenth of the battery capacity.
  8. Every five minutes, increase the voltage level, and when the current begins to decrease, do it every hour.
  9. When the voltage reaches 1.5 Volts, just leave the battery on charge.
  10. After 5-6 hours or earlier, the current will drop to zero. Disconnect charging at this moment.
  11. Wait about half an hour and put the phone on a regular charge.

Sometimes this procedure should be repeated several times, but the results can be really impressive.
