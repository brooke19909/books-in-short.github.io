The route St. Petersburg - Kaliningrad. Ferry: prices, timetable, duration of travel

Kaliningrad region is a unique subject of the Russian Federation. This is not only the westernmost point of the country. The Kaliningrad region is also notable for the fact that it does not have land communication with the rest of Russia. From the west it is washed by the Baltic Sea. Lithuania surrounds it from the north and east, and in the south this region of Russia borders on Poland. Both countries are members of the European Union and are members of the Schengen Agreement. Therefore, in order to come to Kaliningrad and its region by land transport from the rest of Russia, you need to open a transit visa. There are two ways to get to Kaliningrad without these bureaucratic delays: by air and by sea. In this article we will consider the second possibility. Fortunately, other cities of Russia go to the Baltic coast. How to get the route St. Petersburg - Kaliningrad by ferry, how much it costs and how long such a trip will take - read this article.



Exact route

For a long time, two Russian cities were connected by the cargo and passenger ship Georg Ots. But time passes, and it "has retired." Now, Georg Ots will be moored off the coast of Vladivostok as a floating hotel. But the shipping link between the two ports of Russia continues to exist. But the route of freight and passenger traffic is only conditionally called "St. Petersburg - Kaliningrad." The ferry does not actually leave the city on the Neva, but from the port of Ust-Luga. This village is located in Kingisepp district of the Leningrad region. The port is almost one hundred and fifty kilometers from St. Petersburg . And the ferry arrives not entirely in Kaliningrad, but in the city of Baltiysk. It is forty-five kilometers from the regional center. From St. Petersburg to Ust-Luga can be reached (if there is no own car) by bus. Not only minibuses, but also electric trains run from Baltiysk to Kaliningrad.



Flight schedule Saint Petersburg - Kaliningrad

The ferry runs year-round. But in winter, when the Baltic usually storms, flights are made once a week. During the summer navigation season, sea trips between two Russian ports become more frequent. Transportation during the high tourist season is especially lively. Passengers, railway cars, cars and cargo are currently transported by three ferries: Petersburg, Baltiysk and Ambal. All ships during the busy navigation period travel two to three times a week. The journey is long and takes an average of at least thirty-six hours. But often the gloomy Baltic Sea does not please with a fair wind, and the trip drags on for up to two days. Yes, of course, it will be faster by plane. But many tourists regard the ferry St. Petersburg - Kaliningrad, the photo of which looks like an image of an ocean liner, as an opportunity to make a mini-cruise. The schedule of these ships often changes. Every spring, some changes are made. Therefore, the availability of seats and the nearest exit from the ports should be found in ticket offices.

Baltiysk is a cargo ferry. St. Petersburg - Kaliningrad - the route is long, and the ferry can take on board only twelve passengers to stay in cabins. A two-day trip in the car does not seem very comfortable. For passenger traffic, Petersburg is best suited. On its decks are placed not only goods and cars. Traveling in comfort

Passenger ferry Kaliningrad - St. Petersburg is a wonderful opportunity to see the Amber Region without a transit visa through Lithuania. After all, when buying a ticket and for travel, you only need an internal passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation. Of course, the Petersburg ferry cannot be called a cruise liner. But still the cabins, especially on the upper decks, are very comfortable. Previously, the Petersburg ferry operated flights on the Black Sea, between Sevastopol and Novorossiysk, and only last year was transferred to the Baltic. This summer, most likely, a new route will be introduced. The ferry will depart from Ust-Luga (Leningrad Region) and immediately follow to Sassnitz (Germany). After this, the ship will turn in the direction of Baltiysk. And then from the Kaliningrad region it will deliver passengers to St. Petersburg. This new route, of course, will please those residents of the city on the Neva who have a passport with a Schengen visa.



Reviews

Not many tourists managed to ride on a new ship on the route St. Petersburg - Kaliningrad. This ferry moved to the Baltic recently. But whoever managed, they say that the double and triple cabins are very comfortable and clean. The food on board is good. But if you have motion sickness, do not lean on food. The Baltic Sea is a stormy sea, and the ferry during the storms significantly shakes. But the trip is quite safe, although it may be delayed due to weather conditions.
