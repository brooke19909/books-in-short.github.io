Beach Vacation Reviews

Tours at Club Hotel letoonia 5 * (Turkey, Fethiye) are popular among tourists due to the beautiful beaches. Since the hotel is located on a peninsula, it is surrounded on three sides by beaches, so there is a choice where to go at the moment to swim and sunbathe. It is unlikely that any other hotel complex can boast such an extensive and well-equipped coast. Some beaches are pebbly, while others are sandy. The most crowded and noisy of them is Dolphin. It can be reached by car from the reception. On this beach, it is most convenient to relax with children, since there is a playground, a mini club and a pool for young guests nearby. The Dolphin has amazing water slides that descend directly into the sea. Children just love to ride them.



All other beaches are no less comfortable, in addition, they have fewer people. In general, it is worth noting that on the long coast there is always a lot of space and plenty of free sunbeds, which means that there is no need to take places in advance. In addition, the hotel has well-equipped relaxation areas with hammocks and sun loungers around the pools and in the shade of trees. A wonderful clean coast is the pride of the hotel. It is very good to relax here. Tours and attractions

Fethiye is a very ancient city, as evidenced by some of its attractions. The most famous ancient structures that have survived to this day are the Lycian tombs. These pompous rock-cut gates to another world always impress travelers.

Eight kilometers from the city is another interesting place - an abandoned village. In the eighteenth century there was a Greek village, but later the Greeks were resettled and given the village for Muslims from Macedonia. But they did not take root here and went closer to the sea. Since then, the village has been completely empty, despite the fact that it has 3,500 houses. The old basilica of the nineteenth century has been preserved here. Some tourists believe that the village of Kayakey makes a rather terrible impression.

Experienced travelers recommend not limited to visiting the ancient ruins, but certainly see the twelve islands. To do this, you will have to rent a boat or take a boat trip. Such a trip will allow you to admire the magnificent nature, as well as enjoy swimming in the stunning bays.



All islands, and there are twelve, have completely different sizes. Their beaches are covered with sand and pebbles. There are absolutely no people here, so swimming in clear water is just heavenly pleasure.

The very first island on the way is Knight's. In ancient times, the Rhodes knights used it for defensive purposes. Now there are only a few hotels and villas on it.

Very interesting for tourists Red Island. It got its unusual name due to an interesting optical effect. In the rays of sunset, the coastal sand and pebble strip acquires a reddish hue. There are no structures on the island except the old lighthouse. Its east coast is sheltered from the winds, and therefore is the best place for swimming.

To the north-west of the Red Island is Deliktash, which consists of several nearby islets. Diving enthusiasts love these places, saying that they are much more beautiful underwater.

Another cluster of islands is Yassija. Four sections of land are so close to each other that you can swim from one to another. Between the two of them the distance is only twelve meters. The main beauties of this group of islands are the natural pool on the largest of them, and the sand spit that goes into the sea.

But on about. Since the time of the Ottomans, Zeytin has been operating a small factory producing olive oil. The island is currently privately owned.

On about. Tersane at one time lived the Greeks, but after their deportation the village remained empty. Once upon a time, local convenient bays were used to repair ships. One of them is so small that it seems like a lake on an island.



Together with the children, it is worth visiting the Butterfly Valley, which is located on the west coast of the Belgeniz Bay. You can get to this amazing place exclusively by sea. From Fethiye, ships depart daily here. The valley got its beautiful name thanks to the numerous colonies of tiger butterflies that live here. However, almost all species of these wonderful and beautiful insects live in the region now. Walking through the valley, it is worth getting to the picturesque waterfall.
