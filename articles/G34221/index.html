Thickness gauge: what is it and what is it for

Thickness gauge for cars - a device that measures the thickness of the paint on the surface of the body. It is used to determine the number of layers of paintwork and the presence of hidden defects of the machine. The accuracy of the measurement of the device depends on its type and principle of operation.

Classification

Thickness gauges for first-class cars are characterized by low cost, a limited number of functions and complex operation: you need to learn how to use them.

The second class has wide capabilities, low speed, but their measurement accuracy is higher than that of the first class models.

Third-class thickness gauges combine quality and price. Devices are more expensive than the models of the first two categories, but surpass them in reliability, accuracy and functionality. Among the disadvantages is the need for regular calibration of the thickness gauge and low resistance to negative temperatures.

Professional appliances belong to the fourth class. Such thickness gauges are self-calibrating, surpassing other models in terms of characteristics, functions, reliability, accuracy and ease of operation.

Application breakdown

Thickness gauges are used in various fields:

  • Shipbuilding and automotive industry.
  • Construction.
  • Insurance activity.
  • Expert work.

High measurement accuracy is required in many fields of activity, in particular, in car services. When assessing the scope of work, professionals require instruments that determine the paint coat up to micron fractions. For private use, less accurate thickness gauges are suitable.

Types of thickness gauges according to the principle of operation

Magnetic thickness gauges are the simplest in terms of design and principle of operation. A magnet built into the housing determines the degree of proximity of the metal: the closer it is, the thinner the paint layer. Measurements are shown on the display.

The functioning of electromagnetic devices is based on electromagnetic induction. The sensor of such models of thickness gauges perceives the car body as a closed circuit. The thickness of the coating layer is determined by measuring the density of the magnetic field.

Measurements in hard-to-reach areas of the car are carried out by ultrasonic devices. A pulse is applied to the surface of the body, the magnitude of which is displayed.

Vortex thickness gauges are used in the evaluation of non-ferrous metals. The paint layer on the surface of the body is measured by passing current through the coil wire and its subsequent effect on the magnetic field.

Universal thickness gauges of the combined type and electromagnetic thickness gauges are the most popular types of devices. On sale are ultrasonic models with an accuracy of 0.1 mm; their functionality does not allow to distinguish unpainted areas from overflow of paint. Industrial devices are focused not only on measuring the thickness of the paintwork and have a high cost.

How to choose a thickness gauge

Manufacturers offer a wide range of devices that vary in cost, functionality, and accuracy of measurements. How to choose a thickness gauge and not make a mistake?

What to look for

The fundamental criterion for choosing a thickness gauge is the purpose of its further use. For private use, it is worthwhile to focus on budget devices with the optimal range, duration and working conditions, including technical specifications.

On professional models, you should not save. When choosing a thickness gauge, the number of functions and operating modes, the amount of error, and the sensitivity of the tester are taken into account.

It is advisable to determine the set of necessary functions. Advanced models of thickness gauges are equipped with sensors for determining the degree of corrosion and measuring fiberglass and rubber. Similar functions are required in shipbuilding and construction, but are not needed in the automotive sector.

Specific functions such as the ability to work underwater will not be needed when working with vehicles. In this case, the backlight will not be superfluous: measurements are not always carried out with a sufficient level of lighting.

Automatic shutdown during prolonged inactivity is a useful feature, especially if the device is a volatile type. No less important when choosing is the accuracy and accuracy of the readings of the thickness gauge of car paint coatings. A wide range of measurements, duration of work, resistance to temperatures and climatic conditions are also important.

For use in the cold season, a magnetic thickness gauge is suitable - it does not depend on temperature, humidity and power supply. For work in the room electronic analogues are more adapted.

The duration of the thickness gauge and the battery capacity are no less important. The language selection function is not critical, but desirable: despite the digital display of readings, the menu should be Russian-language and facilitate the work.

The case material of the thickness gauge, in contrast to its color and sound alert, is important. Most models are made of impact-resistant plastic, but a protective case can be supplied.

Second place: CEM DT-156

An indisputable advantage of the combined sensor, which took second place in the rating of car paint thickness gauges, was compactness: the ease of work on bends with a small radius, practically not subject to straightening, is indisputable. Sixth: Elcometer 456

Model of a universal thickness gauge working with all types of metals. The standard error is 3% when working with coatings with a thickness of 0 to 31 mm. A solution with built-in or remote sensors connected via a connector is very successful in practice. The monoblock performed well when measuring on flat and open surfaces, the body with removable sensors - when measuring hard-to-reach areas with complex terrain.

Included with both versions of the Elcometer 456 comes one of three sensors - eddy current, magnetic or universal. Type F sensors are used with magnetic metals. Surfaces from non-ferrous and other metals are measured by type N sensors. Combined FNF sensors are used for non-ferrous and ferrous metals.

Which thickness gauge is better to choose?

Interest in thickness gauges and their assortment actually turns into a very meager choice: if you do not take into account cheap models and expensive professional equipment of high accuracy, then there are only a few brands. So, which company thickness gauge to choose? The list of worthy brands is regrettably small, and three of them are of the same factory: Etari, CHY and EuroTrade, which in fact are not different from each other and are almost identical. An unexpected exception is the Megeon thickness gauges - reliable and accurate instruments.

The devices of the trigger type upon closer examination are not always successful copies of the Etari ET-10 or ET-11 and differ in body color and cost. Therefore, when choosing a thickness gauge, you should rely on the following recommendations:

  • Need a compact instrument with acceptable accuracy, able to go over the body of a car, chosen for yourself or friends, acquaintances? It is worth paying attention to combined or electromagnetic thickness gauges: they have sufficient accuracy, small dimensions, affordable price and are easy to operate.
  • For professional use, handheld combined instruments of the CEM DT-156 type would be an ideal option: it is unlikely to have to inspect cars under snowfall. The popularity of trigger devices is rapidly declining: the inconvenient shape of the body has done its job, and modern multifunctional and compact models have finally replaced them from the market.

And, of course, armed with a reliable thickness gauge, it remains to be right with the car.
