Center of Nizhny Novgorod - Minin and Pozharsky Square

Located in Nizhny Novgorod, Minin and Pozharsky Square is an architectural complex with a rich history. This space is on one of the highest points of the Woodpeckers, right in front of the main gate of the Nizhny Novgorod Kremlin. There are monuments to Minin and Pozharsky, the pilot Chkalov, the Rukavishnikov Museum, the Pedagogical University and a number of unique historical sites on it. Nizhny Novgorod and city guests often walk here. In the middle of the square is a square with a beautiful fountain. From here, the main pedestrian street of the city of Bolshaya Pokrovskaya and descending to the monument to the Hero boat, the Chkalovskaya ladder, installed on the Lower Volga embankment, originate from here.

Nizhny Novgorod Kremlin

Of course, the main attraction of the square, like the whole city, is the Nizhny Novgorod Kremlin. Two of the thirteen towers of which stand here. It also has several museums. The first, if you go inside the Kremlin of Nizhny Novgorod from Minin and Pozharsky Square, an open-air museum will appear. Here are collected samples of military equipment produced at the Nizhny Novgorod factories during the Great Patriotic War. Cars, guns, a tank, a fighter, and even a cabin from a submarine. The Kremlin also has museums in the Dmitrievskaya and Nikolskaya towers, the Arsenal Gallery of Modern Art, the Palace of the Vice Governor, the Museum of Crafts and Needlework. In addition, here above the river, there is an eternal flame with a memorial monument. Closer to the northern part of the wall above the river, at the point from which a very picturesque view opens, stands the Temple of the Archangel Michael. Unlike other Russian cities, the Nizhny Novgorod Kremlin now houses the local government, as well as the leadership of the region and the Volga Federal District.

Since it is located in the very center of Nizhny Novgorod, Minin and Pozharsky Square is the venue for various cultural events. Meetings, concerts, celebrations, fireworks are held here. History of the square

Initially, this place was the intersection of land trade routes. The area was therefore first called Verkhnebazarnaya or Verkhnyaya Posad. Then it became the Annunciation when the Annunciation Cathedral was built. In the mid-eighteenth century, another part of this space next to the descent to the seminary, began to be called Seminar. After the revolution they were united into one and since then it has received its current form and name - Sovetskaya Square. The modern name was given to her in 1943. It appeared after the installation of a monument to Minin and Pozharsky on the square, which was later moved to Balakhna to the homeland of Kozma Minin.
