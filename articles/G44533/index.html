Is there life on Mars? Scientists leave no hope

The planets were named according to the associations that they caused among the Greeks and Romans. The faded color of Venus was associated with sea foam, as a result of which it began to be identified with the goddess of love. Mercury, which moves fastest through the night sky, was associated with the famous messenger of the gods (Hermes in the Greek version). The red-faced Mars could not but cause associations with fires and destruction. For which he received the name of the god of war.

And this planet attracted attention not only in antiquity. She arouses our interest in ourselves today. Perhaps, among all the colder bodies of the solar system, Mars is the most frequent guest in human art. The ancients associated him with the divine essence. And what will science tell us: does life exist on Mars?

Very often, fantastic stories, as noted above, are inspired by writers and directors precisely with scientific ideas and ideas about the boundaries of the possible. For the first time, scientists rationally looked at the question of whether there is life on Mars, back in the 17th century. Then it was discovered through telescopes that the red planet has polar caps and a number of other characteristics similar to those on the earth. So, the first artificial object was launched to the planet in 1962, but control was lost. It was the Soviet apparatus Mars-1. Mars-2 reached the surface of the planet, but crashed during landing. However, the fact that the atmosphere of Mars mainly consists of carbon dioxide leaves serious hopes for finding traces of life in the past. The fact is that carbon dioxide is a product of plant life. And then its presence can be explained if life on Mars really existed once.
