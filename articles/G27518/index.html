Miedzyzdroje

Now consider the Baltic resorts of Poland near the German border. The main cities here are Szczecin and winoujcie. Twelve kilometers from the latter, on the island of Wolin, is the resort of Miedzyzdroje. This town is very ancient. It was founded in 1186. And in the first half of the nineteenth century, Miedzyzdroje turned into a resort. Here the softest microclimate in the Baltic Sea, mineral springs, clean sandy beaches, beautiful nature and developed leisure infrastructure. However, it should be remembered that the entertainment in Miedzyzdroje is designed for a special contingent of tourists. After all, the resort is famous primarily as a hospital. Active tourists can make a trip to the Volinsky Natural Park or visit the ducal castle in Szczecin.



Swinoujscie

This port city is located on the very border with Germany. Seventy-five kilometers separate it from Szczecin Airport. Like many other Polish resorts, which were formerly German for a long time, winoujcie has a developed health resort infrastructure. Here, skin and gynecological diseases, metabolic disorders, problems with the respiratory system and the cardiovascular system are treated with mud, boric, bromine and iodine mineral waters. The pride of winoujcie is the old fort (a copy of the Roman castle of Sant'Angelo) and the spa park, which was established in the early nineteenth century. And the city beach stretches for ten kilometers. The width of the sand strip reaches one hundred meters.

As early as the seventeenth century there were several islets. But over time, the sea and the wind connected them into a scythe, and the fishing villages turned into one resort. In the era of developed socialism on this peninsula were located the closed dachas of party bosses. And even now Jastarnia is not like other resorts in Poland. There is a completely different, unhurried atmosphere. The contingent resting in Yastarn - yachtsmen, avid fishermen, windsurfers. Due to the prevailing south winds, a unique microclimate has developed in Puck Bay.
