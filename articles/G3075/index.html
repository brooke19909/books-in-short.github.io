The currency of this brotherly nation and union state is also called the ruble, but already Belarusian. The difficult political situation of an independent republic doomed Belarusian money to a difficult fate with a large number of repeated devaluations and constant inflation. However, the authorities of this small but bold state in the center of Europe know why they are paying such a high price for an unstable currency. Indeed, the independence of the policies of Lukashenko and his team is simply undeniable. And Belarusian money their weak purchasing power is an echo of the course chosen by the people of the country at the end of the twentieth century, which is aimed at conducting an independent independent national policy by the honestly elected leadership of the republic. However, to survive in the modern world, when even the largest and most developed countries come together to meet the economic needs of their peoples, but at the same time try to realize national ambitions, is quite difficult. One of the conditions for the existence of a small state in the modern system of market relations is the full satisfaction of the economic needs of its own people, which, in turn, is impossible without close partnerships in various unions and associations. Thus, Belarusian money can be called a mirror, reflecting the situation of the economy of the whole country.

Banknotes and denomination



The Belarusian ruble is today the official currency issued by the Republic of Belarus. Due to weak purchasing power, it does not have exchange units and it makes no sense to divide it into smaller parts. The Belarusian ruble is designated, according to the International Organization for Standardization, as ISO 4217 and has a banking code of BYR. Today in the country banknotes with face value of 50, 100, 500, 1000, 5000, 50 000, 100 000 and 200 000 BYR are circulating.
