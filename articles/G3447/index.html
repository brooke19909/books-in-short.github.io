Holidays in Singapore in January - climate features, interesting facts and reviews

In Singapore, there is a feeling of being in a different reality. This is facilitated by the futuristic architecture of the surrounding skyscrapers, gardens and parks buried in eternal greenery, perfect cleanliness in the streets, a humid and warm equatorial climate.

An amazing city from the former British colony in just a few decades has become one of the richest and most developed in Asia. For tourists seeking here from all over the world, this is a beautiful place with unusual strict laws and orders.

"City of the Lion"

As many people know, Singapore is the only island city-state in the world, and also the capital. In the Malay language, the name "Singapore" ("singa" - "lion", "pur" - "city") sounds very majestic and beautiful - "Lion City" or "Lion City".





Occupying a small area on several Malaysian islands, washed by the Singapore Strait, Singapore is ready to receive guests and tourists year-round. This is facilitated by a stably warm equatorial climate. This is especially true for tourists from the Nordic countries who are planning a Christmas vacation on the warm coast.

Christmas in Singapore

When considering Christmas holidays in warm countries, you need to pay attention to holidays in Singapore in January. You can arrange an unforgettable New Year, having got from a cold winter to an exotic Asian summer.

Here, not only celebrate New Year on the Chinese calendar, but also celebrate the holiday from December 31 to January 1, along with European countries. The first day of January is festive, it is a day off. A variety of entertainment programs accompanied by live music are held in the city squares and parks. All this creates a laid-back festive atmosphere, and with the onset of midnight, volleys of festive fireworks are heard high above his head.





Also in Singapore in January, a beach holiday is available. The nearby island of Sentosa is perfect for him, pleasing with its sandy beaches and offering a lot of entertainment.

Weather in Singapore in January

If you are planning to fly to Singapore in the winter , it will be useful to find out what the weather there can be for you. The weather in Singapore in January (according to vacationers' reviews) is quite warm, but this month there may be rains. They can be plentiful, but, as a rule, short-lived. Having spilled in 2-2.5 hours, usually in the afternoon or in the late afternoon, the rains end as suddenly as they began. In Singapore in January, intermittent thunderstorms and showers are also possible. Here their number this month can reach 5-6. So, going to Singapore on vacation in the first winter month, take care of the presence of an umbrella.





The average January temperature in Singapore is about + 30 ... 31 degrees during the day and + 23 ... 24 at night. The wind speed is small - 1.5-2 meters per second. In Singapore, the January temperature of water does not fall below +26 degrees. Moisture values ​​range from 65% during the day to 93% at night. In general, the weather in Singapore in January, like in the other month of the year, is hot and humid.

Places worth visiting in Singapore

Before coming to Singapore in January for the winter holidays, you should think about a vacation program in advance and plan a place to visit. What kind of places these will be, you decide. We will only talk about some of them.

The Museum of Science and Art is not only an amazing architectural building in the form of a lotus, but also a museum that occupies the first place in the world in matters of the interaction of science and art. The expositions are devoted to scientific discoveries in art, ranging from Leonardo da Vinci to modern discoveries. You can visit it at any time of the year, including in the winter (in January, for example).

The famous building from the hotel Marina Bay Sands skyscrapers is worth seeing, as this is a very unusual architectural solution. The image of a skyscraper is a hallmark of Singapore.





The Singapore National Museum is one of the oldest national museums built in 1849. By visiting this museum, you can learn all about the history of Singapore.

Fort Canning is a park located on the hill of Singapore's business center. Archaeological excavations conducted here have uncovered unique finds of an ancient military fortification. So why not go on an excursion to these interesting places in January?

The Singapore Botanic Garden, which occupies a vast territory, will introduce you to unique species of flora, and the Singapore zoo presents a variety of fauna worlds, including some rare, endangered species.

All these places are just a small part of Singapore's amazing sights worthy of attention and study.

Sentosa Island

Among the many islands of the state of Singapore, the main, of course, is the island of the same name. But tourists will be interested to get acquainted with other islands of the country. There are 63 in total. Some are uninhabited.

Probably the main island of entertainment in Singapore is Sentosa Island. Here is the largest Universal Studios attraction and amusement park in Singapore, where you can buy a ticket to visit any attractions all day long. This is a very interesting place, including Christmas.



For an unforgettable experience, the island has a huge underwater oceanarium, a water park, several unusual viewing platforms, a museum of military history and many different theme parks (insect park, bird's world park , beautiful butterfly park, flower park and others), both free and with a paid visit.

Tourists who want to relax in Singapore in January on the beach also need to visit Sentosa Island, as everything here is specially equipped for a beach holiday.

There are several ways to get to Sentosa Island from the center of Singapore, for example, by metro, monorail, funicular or car through the constructed bridge.

Bintan Island

Another resort island to visit is Bintan.



For lovers of wild corners of nature with good beaches, this island will seem like a paradise. Moreover, the service offered here is really at the highest level.

For a relaxing break, there are spa centers offering all types of massage and aromatherapy.

There is a wide selection of entertainment and options for outdoor activities: golf courses, scuba diving and a lot of sports grounds. For fans of shopping, it will be interesting to know that trade on the island is duty free.

You can reach Bintan Island by ferry from Tana Mera Pier in Singapore in less than an hour.

Caution: fines!

Singapore is known for its strict laws and even received the unofficial nickname of the city of fines. However, due to such severity, there is almost no crime in Singapore, it is safe here at any time of the day. To avoid problems, it is recommended that you familiarize yourself with your local policies before traveling to Singapore.

All foreigners staying in the country for more than 7 days are required to be tested for AIDS, for some infectious diseases and mental disorders. Otherwise, you will be fined a thousand dollars and may be arrested for 6 months, followed by deportation from the country.

The penalty is payable to stop or park the vehicle in the wrong place. Smoking, eating in public places is prohibited. The fine is $ 500. Sale of chewing gum under the strictest ban.





Perfect cleanliness in the country is achieved thanks to huge fines. It is strictly forbidden to throw any garbage past the bins. This is not hindered by the lack of police, because cameras are installed everywhere.

From winter to summer. Recommendations

To make acclimatization easier in Singapore, having arrived here from a cold country, it is important to know a few simple rules:

  • after a tiring flight, you need to get enough sleep;
  • first limit your exposure to the sun;
  • it is better to walk in the headdress in the streets;
  • a relaxing massage will help the body quickly acclimatize in a different climate;
  • drink plenty of fluids, especially in heat;
  • take care of protecting the skin with sunscreen;
  • Do not lean heavily on exotic foods and fruits.

After reviewing the information about holidays in Singapore in January, with the reviews of Russian tourists, we can conclude that holidays in this country have their own characteristics. While on vacation in this beautiful city, do not forget about its orders and traditions.
