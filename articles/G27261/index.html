Holidays in Corfu

Among the many Ionian islands, Corfu, which is one of the largest, is distinguished. In its capital city of Kerkyra, approximately 115 thousand people live. An interesting fact is that initially the island bore the name of this city. That was the name of the beautiful nymph that Poseidon stole and brought to live here. In Corfu, she bore him a son, Feak, who later became the leader of the people of the greatest sailors. A dry and hot climate is one of the reasons why tourists choose a holiday in Corfu. It is located between Greece and Italy, which largely affected its culture.

The island is strewn with flowers, olive groves, acacias and citrus plants. Thanks to the climate, Corfu is in bloom almost round. Its beauty inspired many poets and artists to write their works. Holidays in Corfu are diverse and will appeal to all tourists who have visited it. Fans of acquaintances with local attractions can stroll around Kerikir - a city whose history dates back to the 7th century. The influence of different cultures is so strong here that a small town is truly amazing in its diversity. In it you can see a large number of monuments and ancient buildings. For those who are attracted by a beach holiday in Corfu, there are a lot of opportunities. You can simply swim in the Ionian Sea, relaxing in one of the many bays. The island has a large number of classic sandy beaches. Everywhere you can dive with a mask and fins, but scuba diving is best in strictly designated places for this. Fans of mountaineering will be interested in climbing Mount Pantokrator, on top of which you can enjoy views of most of the island. Some climbers noted that in good weather you can even see Italy from the mountain. In Ropa Valley, there is the opportunity to try your hand at playing golf on an 18-hole course. The price of this event is relatively low, and the service is beyond praise.

Those who decide to spend their family holidays in Corfu should give preference to the resort of Paleokastritsa. Here, three bays smoothly pass from the sea to the wooded part of the coast, thereby forming a large number of beaches. In shallow water even children can calmly swim. Young people will be much more interesting in the resorts of Kassiopi and Benitses. It is there that the whole nightlife of the island is concentrated. In addition to clubs and bars, this is a great place to practice various sports, diving, surfing. History buffs will be interested in an excursion to southern Corfu, where the palace of Achilleion is located . It was built for the Empress Elizabeth of Austria. The island has a very large number of fortresses and castles of various eras. Getting acquainted with its architecture, you can immediately reveal some similarities with the buildings of England, France and Italy. Even the most densely populated cafes and restaurants, Liston Street in Kerikir is very reminiscent of the French Rue de Rivoli. In addition, you can visit numerous museums. Walking around the island even without a guide will give you a lot of new experiences.
