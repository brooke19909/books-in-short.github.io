Graphic designation of the ruble. International ruble designation

The national currency of Russia, the ruble, is one of the few currencies in the world that for a long period of time did not have its own unique graphic image. By tradition, most people used the simple letter "P" with a dot at the end for a brief perception of the latter as a ruble designation. In 2013, the National Bank of the Russian Federation decided in the period from November 5 to December 5 to hold an open discussion and vote on the regulator's website to determine the graphic symbol for the national currency. According to the results of the survey, a new designation of the ruble was introduced, but more on that below.

How was the vote?





280 thousand people took an active part in the discussion of an important state issue. More than half of those who took part in the survey not only voted for one of the proposed options, but also justified their choice in the form of detailed comments. The ratings of the voting participants and their feedback were carefully studied and analyzed by central bank specialists. It was on their basis that the final decision was made. The procedure in accordance with which the graphic designation of the ruble was approved was one of the tasks of Russia, in particular the Central Bank of the country, which is defined at the legislative level.

Voting statistics

The selected sign in the form of the Cyrillic letter "P", which is crossed out at the bottom, became a favorite, for which 61% of the participants cast their vote. For the second version of the image, 19% of the survey participants cast their votes. The remaining characters scored 5.5%, 4.5% and 1.9%. Among those who took part in the survey were those (8%) who expressed their disapproval of each of the presented options. Citizens of various age groups and social groups became participants in the popular vote. The greatest activity was observed for citizens of the country, whose age ranged from 25 to 34 years.





Most of those who expressed their opinion (72%) are men. Another interesting fact is that citizens (86.5%) who have incomplete higher education decided to choose the ruble designation. The first 100 thousand participants preferred one of the proposed options on the first day of open voting.

Possible graphic options





The first interest in choosing the designation for the national currency was recorded back in 1998. At that time, about 100 options were sent for consideration, which came from 6 different countries. Then the advantage had a real sign. It is mentioned by historian Ivan Sinchuk and dates back to the 17th century. The obsolete designation of the ruble was presented in the form of the letters "P" and "U", which were written in cursive. The first letter of the symbol was rotated 90 degrees. Most of the proposals were limited to modifications of the letter "P". As an alternative, there were sentences in the form of the letter "b". Introduction of the mark into circulation





The ruble designation in the graphic symbol format was considered back in the distant 90s, but it was not possible to realize the idea at that time. The relevance of the issue in 2006 was due to the fact that in the world economy many currencies of the world have become more significant, which has become a prerequisite for the introduction of their notation. The Russian government was particularly interested in the designation of the ruble as a sign during the appearance of the European currency in circulation. In 2006, during the period when the law on the Central Bank was adopted, the introduction of the image of the national currency became a very specific and relevant issue. The inhabitants of the country saw the real application of the chosen sign in 2014. He appeared on new monetary units minted with mints with a face value of 1 ruble. This sign is planned to be used when packing banknotes and in the future to be used as a protective sign on paper bills and in other formats.

International ruble designation





The international graphic image of the Russian currency, as mentioned above, is now presented in the format of the Cyrillic letter "P", which is crossed out at the bottom. This symbol format embodies the stability of the currency. Elvira Nabiullina, the chairman of the Central Bank of Russia, officially announced that the symbol will now proudly represent the ruble in the international market, in particular when displaying quotes and the actual value of world currencies. Work on the creation of the symbol was carried out for 6 years by a special working group since the adoption of the law on the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, which entered into force on July 1, 2007. Now the Russian currency can stand on a par with other world currencies and compete with them on equal terms and in all branches of the global financial market.
