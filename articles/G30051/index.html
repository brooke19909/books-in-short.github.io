News from Beeline

With the development of the mobile services market, Beeline alone decided not to be limited. Recently, a new, more "advanced" option "Be in the know +" has been introduced. Obviously, it is assumed that it will replace the former, and that it will join the ranks of archival services. That is, it will become inaccessible to new connections.

What is the difference? What does Beeline keep up with Beeline? The description of the new "chip" with the "+" sign states that if you are unavailable, then your subscriber will no longer have to listen to dull beeps or the message "subscriber is unavailable". Instead, he will be asked to leave a voice message. And you will receive an SMS notification that "such-and-such and such-and-such" has left a message for you, and you can listen to it by a specific short number. Moreover, the number for each message is separate, and the message of your interlocutor is stored for at least 24 hours.





If you suddenly delete one of these messages, then there is nothing wrong with that. There is a short number 0646, by dialing which, you can listen to all messages left for you over the last day.

There are limitations. The duration of each message is no more than 40 seconds. And such messages per day, the subscriber can receive no more than 30.

In the event that your failed interlocutor does not want to trouble yourself with a voice message, you will simply be sent an SMS about the missed call.
