"Italian Village" in Crimea

A unique residential complex is being built on the west coast of Crimea. Why unique? Yes, because with such a scope and quality in this part of the peninsula they never built.

The grandiose housing estate "Italian Village" is a whole city. Villas, townhouses, cottages, shops, sports grounds, cafes and restaurants - all on the Black Sea, a stone's throw from the beach. All buildings are designed in a single Mediterranean style that fits perfectly into the surrounding landscape.

Developer

December 2014 is considered the birthday of the Italian Village residential complex in Crimea. The developer is the KSK group of companies, which has already launched many residential, resort and recreational facilities on the South Coast in Alushta. But the ‚ÄúItalian Village‚Äù project is ambitious both for the Black Sea region and for the whole Crimea. It is implemented not only by a Russian construction company, but also by 5 Italian investors who signed the contract during the III Yalta International Economic Forum.

The place of implementation of the project is the urban-type settlement of the Black Sea, Cape Tarkhankut.

The planned building area is more than 10 hectares, of which residential and public buildings will occupy about 70 thousand m 2 . The complex will include hotels of different stardom, cottages, townhouses, apartments, in total more than 4,000 people will be able to comfortably sit in the ‚ÄúItalian Village‚Äù - and this is a whole town!

The developer is also engaged in servicing apartments, providing engineering equipment, real estate services.

Why exactly the Black Sea?

The Black Sea chosen for the construction of the ‚ÄúItalian Village‚Äù in Crimea is not an accidental place:

  1. There is no industry, so the environmental situation is excellent.
  2. Space. If every millimeter is built up on the South Coast, and because of high-rise buildings, the sea is often not visible, then the west coast remains free from mass development.
  3. Transport accessibility. From the airport in Simferopol town. The Black Sea is 1.5-2 hours away by car. A 30-minute drive is the resort town of Yevpatoriya, where guests are entertained by a variety of events and concerts.
  4. A unique air that combines the smells of steppe grasses and the sea.
  5. Climatic features associated with the steppe location of the village. Black Sea.

Well and most importantly - Cape Tarkhankut. This is a wonderful place with the cleanest water and sparsely populated beaches.

Features of Cape Tarhankut

Cape Tarkhankut has long been known to fans of outdoor activities at sea. Beautiful landscapes, not disfigured by urbanization, spaciousness and lightness - this is what attracts tourists here.

In the cleanest sea, the bottom is visible to a depth of 10 m. Photos from Tarkhankut are a visiting card of the Crimea.

Beaches with soft white sand are not crowded as in other Crimean cities, moreover, it is easy to find a secluded bay. The swimming season begins in May and ends in September-October.

Residents of the residential complex "Italian Village" all summer provided a variety of entertainment:

  • diving and cliff diving;
  • kiting;
  • windsurfing;
  • fishing;
  • excursions.

This is where the festival "EXTREME Crimea" is held, attracting extreme people from all over the world.

Complex

Architects from Italy took part in the design of the ‚ÄúItalian Village‚Äù, who proposed to introduce original style features during the construction of each complex. This gives charm and personality to the buildings, but on the whole gives the impression that a village is spread out on the western coast of Crimea, as if by magic moved here from Italy.

The entire construction is divided into 17 phases, apartments are already sold in 6 complexes, named after Italian cities. About them - in more detail:

  1. "Palermo". 3 buildings of townhouses and 5 villas with free planning - a total of 128 apartments. The courtyard is decorated with a fountain. There is a pool, parking and a playground.
  2. Capri. 4 villas in three floors each, and two buildings have 4 floors - a total of 97 unique apartments with a free layout.
  3. "Florence". This impressive 8-story building houses 224 apartments.
  4. Sorrento. 43 apartments in three buildings with a height of 4 floors. A distinctive feature - the complex is surrounded by a garden of lemon trees.
  5. "Tarkhankut Life". These are 100 apartments in 2 and 3-storey buildings, part of the apartments are two-level. Near each building, residents can equip the plots as they wish, setting up a garden or flower beds.
  6. "The sun and the sea." 90 apartments are located in 10 cottages, each with a height of 3 floors.

All apartments, villas and townhouses are located at a distance of 70-300 m from the sea, from their terrace balconies offer wonderful views of the Black Sea. As part of the complex, the Black Sea Hotel has already been opened, which has been hosting guests since 2015.

In the central part of the ‚ÄúItalian Village‚Äù, Italian investors laid an olive alley, which became a symbol of friendship.

Design Features

Potential buyers in LCD "Italian Village" offers economy and business class housing.

The buildings were built in 3 types:

  • monolithic;
  • monolithic frame;
  • panel.

The inner walls are constructed of aerated concrete.

For external insulation, polystyrene foam, mineral wool and facade painting were used, giving each building a unique look. Huge windows are glazed with a German-made 4-chamber profile. The graceful balconies and terraces offer wonderful sea views. In the residential complex ‚ÄúItalian Village‚Äù (Black Sea), the apartments give the impression of southern luxury and spaciousness due to the high ceilings - 3 m.

The roof is covered with terracotta-colored ceramic tiles, which visually supports the Mediterranean style.

At each entrance, an Otis elevator system is installed, and a concierge service is available. Ensuring the safety of residents, the complex is guarded around the clock, video surveillance is conducted.

Property owners are provided with parking spaces.
