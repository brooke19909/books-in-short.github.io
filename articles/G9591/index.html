How many crows live in the wild and in captivity

Birds such as Ravens and Ravens are permanent residents of our cities and parks. They are also heroes of songs, sayings, proverbs and fairy tales, are present in heraldry. Despite the similarity of their names in Russian, the raven and the raven are still different from each other, since they are different biological species. It is believed that in the wild a raven can live up to 30 years, but on average these birds live about 20. These are predators living in a flock. People, observing the intellect of ravens, in the Middle Ages endowed them with magical properties and considered them sorcerers. Perhaps the ability of the ravens to imitate various sounds and even human speech played a role in this? They can very similarly imitate the meowing cats, barking dogs, and now the rattling of a motorcycle. But, despite the dark glory of birds, as companions of witches and sorcerers, that they do not disdain carrion, people very much respect ravens. In heraldry, the raven represents wisdom, foresight and longevity. People often tame them and keep them at home. Therefore, it is known how many ravens live in the so-called captivity: 40-50 years. And this is not the limit, since veterinary medicine has not so thoroughly studied the body of the raven as poultry, so the life expectancy of half a century is taking into account the absence of treatment or with its minimal use.

Since these blue-black birds as pets often passed from father to son and even to grandson, various myths arose about how many crows live. Legends give them longevity: 150, 200 years.
