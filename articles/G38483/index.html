Hogwarts Castle





The real castle in which the pictures were taken is Alnik. It is located in Northumberland, on the border with Scotland. It was in the courtyard of this ancient castle that wizards played Quidditch and learned to fly on brooms.

The story of Alnik is not limited to the Harry Potter movie. Information about him dates back to 1096. He was always known for his interior decoration, art galleries, and a library. He was used to shoot films about Robin Hood, Queen Elizabeth, the knight of Ivanhoe. In the movie saga of the books of Joan Rowling, he turned into Hogwarts.

Where is this magical place, thousands of readers dreamed to know. That is why special attention has been riveted to the castle of Alnik, which has not faded away for many years.

However, the film was not shot only in the pavilion and Alnika. The directors created a collective image of the school, combining many fascinating places.

forbidden Forest

According to the plot, the school of sorcery is located near the forest, in which unicorns and ominous werewolves live. The real location for filming was Black Park, which is located in Buckinghamshire. The atmosphere of the forest did not have to be complemented by decorations, since it is so dense in itself that even on a sunny day, twilight reigns there.

Canteen





Often a dining room appears in the frame. It is in it that young wizards begin and end the school year in each series of film sagas. She is not in Alnik. This is actually Christ Church College's canteen, which is part of Oxford.

This educational institution has a long history. At one time he was graduated by Lewis Carroll - author of "Alice in Wonderland" and a world-famous scientist Albert Einstein. Other equally well-known students were distinguished writers Oscar Wilde and John Ronald Ruel Tolkien.

Library

Oxford provided another filming location for the creation of the Hogwarts School. Where is the book depository in which young Hermione loved to draw information? Everything happened in the famous Bodleian library of a famous university. It belonged to the Duke of Humphrey. Until now, it is considered the oldest and largest bookstore in Western Europe.

Castle corridors





The corridors run by students of the school of magic and wizardry are actually located in Gloucester Cathedral. Their fan arches fascinate with their appearance. The cathedral itself was erected in the 14th century in the southwestern lands of England. It is considered to be the pearl of architecture of both that time and modernity.

In his district - incredibly picturesque landscapes that amaze with a variety of shapes and shapes. It was in these corridors that Harry and Ron saved his girlfriend from the giant troll in the first part of the saga.

Chamber of Secrets





The Lacock Abbey, located in Wiltshire County, was chosen as the premises for the Chamber of Secrets. In addition, scenes were shot here, where Harry heard the voice of Basilisk while serving his sentence.

The abbey is famous for the fact that it was inhabited by an English inventor in the field of photography. His name was William Tabolt. Visiting the convent, you can find many audiences in which the classes of young wizards took place.

Hogsmeade Station

It is from this station that the steam engine takes young magicians to the school of magic. The real place of filming was the Gotland station area, located in Yorkshire. It was there that one of the first railways in England was built.
