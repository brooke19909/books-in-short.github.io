The registration number of the PFR or the FSS is the number assigned to your company (or IP) in the relevant organizations. This individual number is assigned to the organization or individual entrepreneur when registering with the tax authority.

After the organization is created, the Federal Tax Service informs about this procedure one or another territorial branch of the funds that register the organization. But it also happens that a notice from the Federal Tax Service does not come to the funds for a long time.

Reporting deadlines in the FIU

First of all, these reporting deadlines depend on the form of taxation used. The most common STS (simplified tax system) and DOS. In addition, for certain types of activities the Tax Code of the Russian Federation provides for special types of taxation, for example, UTII, imputed income tax . We will consider only the simplified tax system, since this is the most convenient taxation system for small businesses, and freelance is a small business.

According to the simplified tax system, the following reporting deadlines are provided for in the tax calendar for 2012:

- quarterly submit reports to the FSS. The deadline is the 15th day of the month for the reporting period;

- report quarterly to the FIU, until the 15th day of the next calendar month for the reporting period;

- quarterly tax return for the simplified tax system. The deadline is until the 25th day of the next month for the reporting period;

- annually a declaration on the Simplified Taxation System (STS) until March 31 for legal entities and until April 30 for individual entrepreneurs;

- annually until January 20th to provide information on the number of employees in the enterprise.

If an individual entrepreneur does not use the work of hired employees in his activity, then it is necessary to submit a declaration on the simplified tax system once a year, annually information about the absence of employees, and provide information to the FIU before March 1. This is the necessary minimum of information that you need to know in order to legally freelance, paying all taxes.
