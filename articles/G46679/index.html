To sew with tape, you need sharp needles that must pass freely through the fabric, without the formation of puffs. The eye of the needle should be necessarily elongated, since the tape should pass through it easily, slide and not twist. If you work with a tape 3 mm wide, then you will need needles No. 24. And for a width of 7-12 mm, numbers 18-22 are suitable. Well, if you find a gilded needle that is coated with a galvanic compound - this protects it from oxidation and rust.

The most popular among domestic embroiderers are satin ribbons. They can be smooth, pleated or corrugated. The colors and widths are different. The narrowest ribbon is 0.3 mm, and the widest - up to 6 cm. For ceremonial and special occasions, lace ribbons with beads or gathered ones are used in the decoration of clothes.





A variety of fabrics are used as the basis for embroidery. This and the cambric, and kisei, and matting, and satin - they relate to cotton fabrics. From linen fabrics, coarse canvas, a harsh canvas, a fabric with a uniform base or a thin canvas are used. They are used for embroidery with such different materials as tweed, crepe, jersey, silk fabrics. In principle, you can embroider on any surface, if only stitches are kept on it.

A few seams from the lesson "Embroidery with ribbons" A tyutelka in a tyutelka "

With traditional embroidery, at the beginning of work on a thread, the knot is not tied. But when sewing with ribbons, a flat knot is made for fastening. Take 50 cm of tape, insert it into the needle, bending the tip, insert the needle into its center, then, supporting the tape with one hand and pulling it with the other hand, tie a knot. Now consider a couple of seams.





"Loops in a circle." Pull the needle from the inside out onto the face and stick near the point from which it emerged, pull it out to the inside out. There was a loop that needs to be secured with a pin. And then continue the manipulations, moving in a circle. 5 steps - 5 petals, and you can fill the center of the flower with beads or beads.

"Half-loops with an attachment." We stretch a needle with a ribbon on the face. Make a loop and hold it with your thumb. We make a puncture near the former place on the right side and stretch it to the inside. Now, pierce the fabric back in the center of the loop and pull out the needle. Through the loop we stretch the tape and immediately withdraw the needle from the inside up, thereby starting a new stitch.





There are other types of seams that embroider ribbons. Roses, tulips, lilies of the valley, daffodils, butterflies, trees and much more can be created using unique techniques. This is real art, as a flat picture becomes voluminous and vibrant.
