Biography of Marie Antoinette: a place on the throne

Already in 1774, the king of France Louis XV died of smallpox, and the country wished good health to the new king - Louis XVI. The next twenty years were marked by a growing deterioration in the country's economic situation, and considerable blame for this lay on the shoulders of the short-sighted monarch. Contemporaries often characterized him as a person with a good heart and good intentions. However, extremely indecisive. He began his reign with encouraging reforms aimed at limiting the arbitrariness of the nobility, and indeed reducing their impact on the life of the state. Objectively, this estate became more and more a relic of the Middle Ages.





To keep up with the development of its eternal rival - England, France needed to get away from the feudal system of relations, replacing it with a new social and economic order.
