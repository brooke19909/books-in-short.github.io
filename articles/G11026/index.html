Briefly about the city

The city of Denver (Colorado, USA) was founded in 1858. He wore the status of a tent village. This time period is known to all as the Age of the Gold Rush. It was here that the first large gold deposit was found.

The city is named after the Kansas Governor James Denver. This decision had a definite purpose. It consisted in obtaining the location of the governor to the town. However, by this time he had already retired, and the name remained.

Soon they found out that the gold reserves are small, but the deposits in the western part saved him from the fate of the ghost town. Thanks to them, Denver began to develop rapidly.

The year 1861 became decisive for the city. It was at this time that Denver became part of Arapaho County. In the 60s, there was no division into states, so the city became the center of Colorado.
