Scheme of the Leningrad station and other useful information about the object

Leningradsky railway station, located in the capital of Russia, is one of the most popular railway stations. It is here that many Russians and even foreigners board the train to get to the glorious St. Petersburg, as well as to other northern cities.





In addition, there is a suburban service, the terminal station of which is the city of Tver. In order not to have to look for ticket offices for a long time, the necessary apron, relaxation room and amenities, it is recommended that you familiarize yourself with the scheme of the Leningrad station. The following is helpful information.

Landmark according to the scheme

Before the trip, it is advisable to familiarize yourself with the layout of the station so that there are no difficulties. Sometimes it seems that everything will go smoothly. But there are various circumstances that force you to act immediately, for example:

  • traffic jams;
  • accident on the street;
  • a serious train malfunction in the subway;
  • extreme weather events and other unpleasant events.

Therefore, upon arrival, you have to immediately navigate on the spot. The scheme of the Leningradsky station prepared in advance in this article will help.





To make it clear which side to start looking at, it is advisable to return to the beginning of the article, where the image of the station is placed. The fact is that passengers who meet and escort most often use the front door. It is the one through which people come and go in the first photo. This input on the diagram is marked on the left with the inscription "Under 1". To the left of this inscription is an underground passage and exit from the Komsomolskaya metro station.

To get to the long-distance ticket offices, you need to go up a little bit up the stairs to the building. On the left and right there will be glass doors to two cash desks. To get to the long-distance trains (in the diagram on the upper right are marked with long black and white stripes with odd numbering), you should go straight ahead all the time, without turning anywhere. At the very end of the building there is an exit to the platform. If you need to use the left-luggage office, you should turn right at the exit to the trains. As you can see, the scheme of the Leningrad station is not too complicated, you can quickly navigate on the spot.

Suburban traffic in the diagram above is marked on the right by an even numbering of paths (black and white short stripes). You can go to them using the exit from the Komsomolskaya metro station, that is, from the street.
