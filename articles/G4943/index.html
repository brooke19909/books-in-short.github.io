Spain, Alicante: attractions, photos and reviews

Alicante (Spain), the photo of which is presented below, is the second largest city in Valencia. It is located on the shores of the Mediterranean Sea. The city is the capital of the province of Alicante. Sights, reviews of which are the most enthusiastic, are very popular among tourists.



Location

The city is located on the shores of the Mediterranean Sea, or rather, on a rugged plain, close to hills and hills. Speaking about the sights in Alicante, one cannot but mention Mount Benacantil. Its height reaches 169 meters. On it is the fortress-castle of Santa Barbara. The mountain rises above Alicante and gives a very unusual look to the city, which makes it even more popular with tourists.

Alicante beaches

The sights of the city are not limited to the landscape. The beaches deserve special attention. They are located along the entire coast. The following beaches are located here from south to north: El Saladar, Agua Amarga, El Postiguet, La Albufereta, La Almadraba, San Juan. They are also on the island of Tabarca. Cape Cabo de las Huertas is located between the beaches of San Juan and La Albuerta. There are many bays: La Palmera, Los Hudios, Cantalares and Calabarda.

Different route options

The undoubted advantage of the resort, according to many tourists, is the developed transport network. Twelve kilometers from the city center is Alicante International Airport. It is easily accessible by two optimal routes. You can get on the N-332 or A-7 highways. The distance from the airport to the city center does not exceed eight kilometers, and in both directions. Travel time - no more than fifteen minutes. Twice as much time is spent at rush hour. A special bus leaves every forty minutes from Plaza del Maar. A ticket costs one euro. The railway connection is very developed - this is one of the characteristic features of such a state as Spain. Alicante, whose attractions attract annually a huge number of tourists, is connected by railway with many cities of the country. Trains from Seville, Madrid and Barcelona run daily. All the large centers of the province are connected precisely thanks to them.

Alicante. Sights. Fountain

The most famous place in the city is the Square of Stars. A huge number of tourists flock here every year. Guests are especially enthusiastic about the original fountain. It was built in the 30s. last century. The local sculptor Daniel Banyuls Martinez was responsible for his project. This place was called Independence Square until the outbreak of civil unrest in Spain. In the early 50s, it was especially popular. Most of the people here could be met at night. The city administration pleased residents with free performances at the Singing Fountains. Currently, the square has become a gathering place for fans of the Hercules football club. When their favorite team wins, they spend massive bathing in the fountain.



Santa pola

The province of Alicante is rich in many interesting areas. Sights, photos of which can be taken during excursions, especially attract lovers of historical places. Many tourists are attracted by the castle of Santa Pola. It is located in Gloriet Square. This castle fortress was built in the XVI century. Its architecture is designed in the style of the Renaissance. The engineers of Italy were responsible for the project. Construction was completed in 1557. The construction began on the orders of Bernardino de Cardenas. At that time, pirate attacks were considered commonplace. The building was laid so that the sailors could defend themselves from them. The construction is a square fortress. It has two forts and several fortress towers. They are located in the southwest and northeast corners. The first fort served as a palace for the duke. The building itself was built in the military style of the Renaissance. At the southeast and northwest corners are the "Fort of the King" and the "Fort of the Duke." The stone fortified towers are interlinked. External walls with cornices have only forts. On the southwest side is the main entrance to the fortress. It is made as a freestanding stone arch. The entrance itself is built according to the norms of that time and is similar to the letter "L". The outside has no features. The interior is filled with residential buildings for soldiers. They have doors and windows with which you can be in the weapons yard. There is also something to see here. In the arms yard are the Maritime Museum, the Chapel of the Virgen de Loreto and the well. You can get into the fortress towers and forts through the patio. It is square in shape, has rounded corners. Currently, the castle is used as a cultural center.
