History of the city

A centuries-long difficult history has both Greece and its neighbor Italy. Ancona was founded in 390 BC. e., although this is an approximate date. Initially, the city belonged to the Greeks, who called it "elbow" because of the cape of unusual shape. Merchants established a purple factory here. In 178 BC e. Ancona passed to the Romans, its harbor was eventually expanded by Roman rulers, as it was of great strategic importance to the empire.

In the Middle Ages, the port proclaimed itself an independent republic. The emperors of Germany, the Roman Church, the Venetian Republic fought for the right to possess it . In 1532, the authority of the Pope was established in Ancon; during the time of Napoleon, the port again became a republic for a short time. In 1860, the city finally became part of the Italian Kingdom.

Ancona Hills

The city is located on a hilly area, alternating with valleys. Therefore, there are a lot of interesting places, natural, historical and architectural attractions. A line of hills stretches out to the sea from the north side. Here is Mount Cardeto with the park of the same name, numerous fortifications. On this line are the hills of the Capuchins (with a lighthouse) and Guasco (with the Duomo). In the south you can see Astagno with a towering fortress and five bastions built in the 16th century. Ancient buildings are buried in verdure, which gives them even more charm and mystery.



On the southern hill of Santo Stefano is the Pinchio Park, which is interesting in that all its paths and sites have the correct geometric shape. A huge number of evergreen trees grow here. Santo Stefano also has an impressive view of the port. Mount Pelago attracts astronomical observatory located on it. On the Pulito is the Admiralty. On Mount Santa Margarita lies the magnificent Passetto Park, and on its northern slopes there is a convenient path leading to the sea and a swimming pool.

The main attractions

The city is conditionally divided into two parts, one is old historical, and the other is more modern. Ancona (Italy) attractions has a variety of eras. Of greatest interest are the amphitheater and arch of Trajan, preserved from the time of the Roman Empire to the present day. The arch was installed in honor of Emperor Trajan in 115, at the same time the port of Ancona was expanded, it began to be used as a parking lot for Roman warships. The structure is 14 meters high built of Greek marble. Initially, the arch was decorated with three statues and an ornament made of gilded bronze, but to this day they have not been preserved.

At an altitude of 50 m above sea level, between the hills of Cappuccini and Guasco, the most important building of the Roman era is located. The amphitheater accommodated about 10,000 spectators, who were located in three sectors, on 20 rows. The construction was erected in the 1st century BC. e., and was completed in the I century BC. e. Today, archaeological excavations are carried out in this area.

Museums of the city

To get to know Ancona closer, you should look at the exhibits, paintings, various statues. The city has the National Archaeological Museum of Marche and the Diocesan Museum. The first focuses on a collection of artifacts discovered during excavations in the Marche region. Here are collected things from almost every era. In the prehistoric department, finds of the Bronze and Paleolithic periods are collected, there are objects that are more than 200,000 years old. There is a collection that tells about the life of the people who lived on the territory of the city during the Iron Age. There are departments devoted to the Gallic invasion, Greek and Roman, medieval periods.



The Diocesan Museum tells the story of Ancona, dating back to the early Christian era. Here there is a reconstruction of the ruins of the portal of the Romanesque church of St. Peter, a sarcophagus with the relics of the martyr Dasius, dating from the VI century, a huge collection of paintings, statues, objects of worship. The museum has preserved tapestries created by Rubens.
