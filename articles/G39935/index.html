Italy, Veneto: attractions and photos

As all tourists note, the Italian region of Veneto, which is located in the northeast of the Apennine Peninsula, is of great interest to everyone who comes to the country for the first time. A huge number of travelers seek to enter the territory rich in natural and man-made sights.

It is not for nothing that the Veneto region in Italy is called the true treasury of the country. Gathering all the best for tourists, the fertile region is able to satisfy the dreams of vacationers.

All tourists from different parts of the globe are hospitably welcomed by Italy, striking in its beauty. For many years, Veneto has been number one in attendance. Oldest area

The name of the region comes from the tribe of Venets inhabiting the territory of the Adriatic coast. It is believed that the founder of the region was the Roman emperor Augustus, who devoted much time to civil engineering.

After the fall of the once great empire, the main pearl arose - Venice, which became a small but powerful republic, with which the history of the region is closely connected.

Seven Provinces of Veneto

Possessing rich culture and history, it is not in vain that Italy is a unique mecca for tourists. Veneto is an ancient region, consisting of seven provinces, featuring a wide variety of attractions. On the map of the region you can find a huge number of interesting places for guests of the country that can surprise even the most demanding tourists.

Amazing Verona

Another attraction of Veneto is Verona, whose fame was brought by Shakespeare's brilliant play about young lovers. The events described in the work are reminiscent of two houses erected in the XII - XIII centuries. And although no one proved that there really were two warring Italian families, the architectural masterpieces in which Capulet and Montecchi lived were considered symbols of love and fidelity.

Juliet's House

Now the interior of those times when tragic events took place is recreated in the Juliet's House, and a bronze sculpture of a beautiful woman in love is installed in the courtyard. Open to visitors, the Casa di Giulietta Museum is famous for its legendary balcony, from which the lady listened to the sweet speeches of her lover.



There are always crowds of tourists leaving Vandal graffiti and touching the polished Juliet statue. There is a legend that everyone who touched the statue will be happy in love, and each guest tries to verify this from their own experience.

Restaurant, not museum

But with the house where Romeo allegedly lived, everything is much worse. The city authorities several times offered to buy the old building from the owner, but the owner did not want to sell the building, but made a restaurant out of it. Therefore, you can go to a local attraction, but only then, to eat.

Padua and its special atmosphere

No less beautiful is the province of Padova, which houses the oldest university in the country and a large number of architectural monuments. Its capital is a small city of scientists, which will help you plunge into a special atmosphere saturated with ancient history. Preserving several centuries old frescoes made by the great Giotto and the relics of St. Antonio, Padua captivates and gives vivid impressions to travelers.

The beauty of Rovigo

The province of Rovigo, famous for its magnificent landscapes, will allow all tourists to feel unity with nature. On the territory there are many chic villas built back in the days of the Venetian patricians. The evidence of past eras will surprise with its beauty and tell all the secrets that Italy has been keeping for many years.



Veneto: Belluno

The northern province with the Dolomites will delight its guests with natural attractions. Impressive mountain landscapes will make you forget about everything. Virgin beauty attracts a huge number of travelers who dream to stay away from civilization.

History of Treviso

Carefully preserving its ancient history, Treviso will allow tourists to feel the spirit of the Middle Ages. A variety of castles, palazzos, fortifications in the province will charm anyone who comes closer to get acquainted with a charming corner.

Kitchen Features

Continuing the conversation about the national features of local cuisine, it must be emphasized that at all times the eternal and always young Italy was famous for its culinary traditions. Veneto is a true gourmet paradise. All tourists note amazingly delicious food, the basis of which is seafood. Restaurant chefs bake fish and shellfish, grill shrimp and sea bass.

One of the main delicacies is carpaccio with olive oil. Interestingly, the recipe for thinly chopped raw meat was invented in Venice. Risotto is a national treat, and gourmets try its most varied types.
