Where to go in Ramenskoye? Interesting places

The city of Ramenskoye is located forty kilometers from Moscow. The name of the city comes from the word "Ramenie" - that was the name of the estate of Moscow Prince Ivan Kalita.

Presumably, the time of its appearance is associated with the year 1328. Borisoglebskoye Lake, located in the city center, has been known for its chronicles since 1628. The lake received its name in honor of the holy princes Boris and Gleb, who were killed on the way to Kiev by their own brother Svyatopolk.

In the XVIII century. Ramenskoye was listed according to documents as the village of Novo-Troitsk. In the 50s, a large manufactory worked here, which at that time was one of the leading manufacturers of the country.

Ramenskoye received urban status in the 1920s; in 1929, the city of Ramenskoye became a district center. Today Ramenskoye is a developing city in the Moscow region. It has preserved historical uniqueness and significance as an industrial center.

Ramenskoye is surrounded on all sides by small lakes of natural origin. In the west, a few kilometers from the city, the Moscow River flows, followed by the Novoryazanskoye Highway. A railroad passes through the city.

Interesting places

Where to go in Ramenskoye to see the local attractions? There is one interesting monument in the city of Ramenskoye, which is worth a look. 170 years ago, a spinning factory was created in the city.

The main production building and the complex of office buildings today are striking in their grandeur and scope. The architectural complex of Ramenskaya manufactory is of artistic value, as a monument of industrial architecture of the late XIX-early XX centuries.

The owners of the factory, the Malyutin brothers, were not only famous manufacturers, but philanthropists and donors. With their funds, the main temple of the city was built - the Holy Trinity Cathedral. Located on a certain elevation, it looks monumental. It was erected in 1852 according to the project of the architect A. A. Celerov.

Two places of worship attract attention. Although they do not represent significant cultural value, as they were built recently, but they have their own special beauty.

These include the wooden bell tower, which is an ensemble with a wooden church in honor of the Matrona of Moscow.

The church was built in 2001, the bell tower appeared in 2004. The buildings look harmonious: the church is made in the old style with eclectic elements, the architecture of the bell tower resembles the style of old Russian northern architecture. The belfry is two-story, it is allowed to climb.
