The beach "Arcadia", a photo of which can be found in all the tourist avenues of the city, takes several hundred meters, and there is no shortage of tourists until late autumn. That is why I would like to draw the attention of travelers to the fact that you should come here as soon as possible. The traffic intersection here is very convenient. Trolleybus number 13 runs to the coast. You can also get there by tram of the fifth route or city buses No. 195, 168 and 198.

An electric car runs along a special health path from the Langeron beach to the Arcadia. However, many prefer to walk along the sea.

You can get here by taxi at any time of the day. True, it is not cheap.





Section 4. History of education

At the end of the XIX century on the site of the future Arcadia was a rocky beach, absolutely unsuitable for recreation. The founders of the city chose this place for the resort because cliffs and steep slopes lead to the majority of Odessa beaches, and here you could get to the coastal zone by a natural gentle slope.

The Belgian Emil Cambier is considered the discoverer of Arcadia, who built the first restaurant in that place. Then the name itself appeared - "Arcadia". Odessans named this area after one locality in Greece, where, according to the Greeks, peace reigned, idyll, happiness, peace and simplicity of manners.

Gradually, baths began to open there, small beer halls, large new restaurants and all kinds of entertainment facilities were built.

In 1910, the first electric tram went to Arcadia through the reconstructed French Boulevard.

Gradually, the beach "Arcadia", the prices of which "bite" to this day, began to be built up by night clubs and new summer venues. From year to year, the recreation area grows, thrives and continues to be popular with both the residents of the city and its guests.

Section 8. Negative reviews of holidaymakers





One big drawback in Arcadia is the highest prices in the city. There are not so many free recreation areas, and the cost of visiting at least an elementarily ennobled place is 25-30 hryvnias (approximately 88-105 Russian rubles). But this is far from the limit: for a tent or a tent on the shore, you can sometimes pay 500 hryvnias (or 1750 rubles) per day. The price depends on the size and quality of the design, and on the season.

Another inconvenience is the unfinished reconstruction of the alley leading to the sea. Most of the pavilions have not yet been equipped, and now it is hidden by awnings. Benches have not yet been set up along the alley, and tiles have not yet been properly laid near the central pool, which causes a few negative impressions of those who are breathing.

Also, many are outraged that the route of the 5th tram can no longer get to Arcadia, as it was before. But these are temporary inconveniences associated with the reconstruction of the territory.
