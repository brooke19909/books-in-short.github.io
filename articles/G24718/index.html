Hyacinth macaw and its relatives

Hyacinth Macaw is the largest parrot in the world. His body reaches a meter in length, and his tail is sixty centimeters. Such a bird weighs from a kilogram of 200 grams to a kilo and a half. The macaw got its name due to the blue plumage of a cobalt hue. Its black beak is massive and very strong. A parrot easily gnaws a wire, therefore its cage should be made of strong iron rods. The eyes of this species of macaw are dark brown, almost black, with an ocular ring of golden color. Dark gray paws have four fingers, two of them look forward, and two - back. At the base of the mandible, a golden wide strip is clearly visible. The skin on the body is pale yellow.

This type of parrot is common in Paraguay, Bolivia, Brazil. Birds prefer gallery forests, savannas, swamp forests with islets of palm trees. Outside the breeding season, they are kept in a clan of several dozen individuals. Green, hyacinth and red macaw fly to the places of feeding places and clay shallows together. These tropical beauties have a similar diet: fruits, berries, water snails, nuts. On the shallows, they swallow pebbles and clay rich in mineral salts in order to cleanse their stomach and intestines from toxins. Hyacinth giants make piercing sounds, croaking - "Raa-aarr." These parrots are good flyers: they usually travel tens of kilometers from the place of spending the night to places of feeding.

This is a fairly rare bird. In habitats, they are often killed for the beautiful plumage. They become the prey of predators - snakes, eagles, cats. These inhabitants of hot tropical forests live on average about 70 years. Hyacinth macaw in captivity is contained only in zoos. Due to its large size and loud voice, similar to a heart-rending cry, it is problematic to keep such a pet in a city apartment. He needs a very large and extremely durable cage, but it would be better to build a veranda for the bird.
