What is the difference between an ipad and an ipod? Gadget Comparison

The iPad is a brand new device released by Apple, which is also known as the Apple Tablet. This device is considered a hybrid of iPod Touch and Apple laptop, so you can increasingly hear the question of how the iPad differs from the iPod. The company describes the product as "magical and revolutionary." This device can be used to browse the web and read e-books. It supports nearly 140,000 applications. The target audience for the iPad is consumers of previously released Itouch and iPhone familiar with touchscreen technology.

In turn, the iPod is an Apple player, used primarily for playing and storing music, videos, photos, available in four different models - Shuffle, Nano, Classic and iPod Touch. The latter model is also a great handheld computer and can be used to send emails, web surfing and games. So, what is the difference between an iPad and an iPod Touch model and others?





Size and weight

The iPad is 242.8 mm high, 189.7 mm wide and 13.4 mm thick. At the same time, it weighs 0.68-0.73 kg. The iPod Classic, in turn, is 103.5 mm high, 61.8 mm wide and 10.5 mm wide, weighing 140 grams. Other devices, such as Nano, have even less weight. The iPod Touch is a bit larger in size, but lighter than the Classic.

IPad and iPod - differences in storage devices

The iPad has a capacity of 16 GB, 32 GB or 64 GB, while the classic iPod can hold up to 160 GB of data. Differences in specific models vary between 2-4 GB. At the same time, other modifications, including Nano and Touch, have capacities ranging from 8 to 64 GB.

Input and output

iPad has a 30-pin dock connector, headphone jack, built-in speaker, microphone, and SIM card slot on some models. Speaking about how the iPad differs from the iPod, it should be noted that the classic model has only dock connectors for headphones.

Display

The iPad has a 9.7-inch LED-backlit display and a multi-touch glossy widescreen display, while the iPod is equipped with a simpler color LED-backlit LCD. In turn, the iPod Touch has a 3.5-inch widescreen Multi-Touch display.





Battery and power

The iPad is equipped with a built-in lithium-polymer battery that can last up to 10 hours, while the players have a lithium-ion battery that can function without recharging for up to 30 hours (iPod Touch). The time required for charging can vary from 3 to 4 hours.

Wireless connection

iPad can be sold as a Wi-Fi model with Bluetooth and EDR technology, which allows you to connect to wireless headphones and a keyboard, or in a Wi-Fi plus 3G modification with fast Internet access for travel and GPS access. The difference between these varieties is only in a wireless network connection. Differences in Features and Applications

The iPad has a touch screen that allows you to browse sites and open one full page at a time on the on-screen keyboard to help you read and create letters. At the same time, it is possible to simultaneously use various software that allows you to store and share photos. The gadget's screen has a high resolution for watching music videos, movies and other videos, and iTunes will help you listen to your music collection with built-in speakers and wireless headphones. The tablet also has Ibooks for electronic reading and other mandatory functions - a calendar, maps, contacts, as well as special features for people with visual impairment, hearing impairment or other physical disabilities. You can also sync apps from your iPhone or iPod Touch to download them to iPad from your Mac or PC. New applications are being introduced for the iPad for personal and business use.

In the players there are services used to store music and photos, a calendar, notes and some other built-in features. The latest iPod touch is offered with all the extra features besides reading e-books.

Price for iPad and iPod

Summing up the comparison of how the iPad differs from the iPod, it remains to find out the cost of devices. On average, the price of an iPad starts at $ 499, while the iPod price tag ranges from $ 59 (iPod Shuffle) to $ 399 (iPod Touch).

As you can see, despite the fact that these devices have many common features, they also have many differences regarding a variety of characteristics. The above descriptions should help you clearly understand what an iPad and an iPod are.
