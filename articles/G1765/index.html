Some historical facts

Ancient Chernivtsi has long taken its rightful place in the list of Eastern European and Ukrainian cities. Sights of the city, its history, natural beauty, unique architecture, open soul of the local population attract many guests. Historians have put forward the theory that the name of the settlement relates to the populated city of the fortress, which was located on the southern borders of the Galician state. The fortress itself came under the destructive influence of the troops of Burundai, the Mongol conqueror. Therefore, since 1259, the population migrated to the right bank of the river. And exactly a century later the city became part of the Moldavian state.

The first written references to Chernivtsi are found in manuscripts belonging to the Moldovan owner Alexander Dobrom. He issued them in the autumn of 1408 to Lviv merchants. Therefore, the eighth of October is considered the official date of birth of the city. The city of Chernivtsi at that time was located at the intersection of the route from southwestern Europe to the Balkans and Turkey.



Anton - knight Kokhanovsky von Stavchan

This man served as the second autonomous burgomaster of Chernivtsi. He left an extraordinary mark in the history of the city. It is with the surname of this figure that Chernivtsi's assertion as a European-style city is associated. During the reign of Anton, the business activity of the village increased, trade and production began to develop. Chernivtsi won the title of international transport center. A food exchange also opened and the construction of a sewer system was completed.



First attractions

In 1774, approximately 290 families lived in Chernivtsi, and there were only three wooden churches. Only in 1778 the city began to flourish. Just at that time, the Duke Karl von Enzenberg had the chance to lead the military administration. At his invitation, merchants, artisans and industrialists came here. They were supposed to help in the development of trade and production.

Unique city of Chernivtsi! Sightseeing is his calling card. From the mid-19th century until the beginning of the 20th century, many architectural monuments were erected that brought fame to the city. Among them are the town hall, which was created in 1848, the post office dating back to 1855, the Armenian Church, built in 1875. Also in 1877, a Jewish synagogue appeared in the city, and in 1905 a drama theater. Of particular historical importance is the Palace of Justice in 1906 and the premises of the 1908 railway station.



Attractions in detail

First of all, I would like to say a few words about the Chernivtsi Town Hall, which is located on Rynok Square. As soon as she appeared here, she immediately gained the status of the main building of the city. Its construction was led by A. Mikulich, a well-known architect at that time. Active participation in the creation of attractions took regional engineer Marine. The architecture of the house is a departure from the principles of classicism and a direct appeal to the Italian Renaissance. Today, the coat of arms of Chernivtsi flaunts on the facade of the town hall. And the building itself today fulfills its original role - this is the meeting place of the city council.

You should definitely visit the city of Chernivtsi. Its sights are like a part of the city, which seems to live on its own, and cannot exist separately. One of the local advantages is the railway station, built in the Art Nouveau style. O. Wagner was involved in the architectural project. The portal is considered the main element of the decor, the stained-glass window of which is made of a steel frame. He became a symbol of the city gate. At the time when the station opened, its building was in no way inferior to the best analogues of Europe.

Born to crawl can fly

They say that the one who was born to crawl cannot conquer the sky. But everything is changing, especially nowadays. Since the advent of aircraft, anyone who is not afraid of heights is capable of flying. Ukraine invites to soar above the clouds. Chernivtsi have a unique airport. From it flights are constantly made to Kiev, Kharkov, Istanbul, Odessa and Donetsk. The modern history of the local airport begins in the early 70s of the last century. In 1971, reconstruction was carried out here. As a result of the work done, the throughput of the terminal increased to 200 people per hour. And in 1974, the Chernivtsi airport was equipped with an asphalt concrete runway.
