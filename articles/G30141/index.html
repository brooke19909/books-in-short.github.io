Iguazu Falls, Argentina: description, history and interesting facts

South America is rich in stunningly beautiful places. To visit the continent and not see the Iguazu Falls is an unforgivable oversight. After all, they are famous not only on the mainland. The fame of them has long spread throughout the world. Waterfalls are rightfully considered the real pearl of the continent. Tourists note that visiting an amazing place gives incomparable sensations. The grandiose beauty of nature is striking in its strength and power. You can talk a lot about Iguazu Falls, but words cannot convey their beauty. Of course, it‚Äôs better to see it all with your own eyes.

Natural complex of waterfalls

Iguazu Falls is a beautiful region on the border of Brazil and Argentina. It is for their sake that many travelers decide to go to South America. The scale of the natural complex is evidenced by the fact that waterfalls are listed as natural wonders. And that says a lot. Waterfalls are part of the National Park, in which rare plants and animals grow. By the way, the park itself has long become one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

It‚Äôs hard to imagine how many waterfalls an Iguazu complex consists of. And there are 275 of them. The number is really impressive. But no less impressive is their height. If we talk about how many meters Iguazu Falls are tall, then the maximum value is 82 meters. Most of the cascades do not exceed 60 meters in height. But this was far from always.

From Brazil, water flows appear in all their pristine beauty. A fabulous picture is complemented by a cloud of spray, which is always there. The Iguazu complex is twice as wide and much higher than the famous Niagara Falls.

Location of the water complex

There are Iguazu waterfalls on the river of the same name. About 24 kilometers from the confluence of the Iguazu in Parana. The water complex is located right on the border of Argentina and Brazil. Iguazu Falls divide the river into lower and upper parts. They are located in the national parks of both countries.

The Iguazu River itself originates off the coast of the Atlantic Ocean, south of Sao Paulo. It flows deep into the mainland to the west. Its length is 1320 kilometers. In the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe water complex, the width of the river is about four kilometers. And over the entire width of the water falls from a high cliff, which in shape resembles a crescent.

History

The place where Iguazu Falls is located once changed its terrain due to the eruption of the volcano. A huge hollow formed in the earth's surface. Here is such an amazing story. As a result of the disaster, a real world-wide miracle appeared.

No less interesting is the history of the origin of the name of the Iguazu Falls. What does the name of the complex mean in the language of the Indians who lived in these places? Translated from the dialect of guarani, it sounds only as "big water", which describes the water complex as accurately as possible. The first to discover waterfalls was a Spaniard named Alvar Cabeze de Vaca in 1541. He went to the jungle in search of adventure and gold. His detachment landed on the coast of modern Brazil and began to move west. Travelers crossed the Brazilian Highlands and from afar heard a rumble. It was strange sounds that brought people to the waterfalls. The Spanish conqueror was extremely surprised and amazed at what he saw. He called the Salto de Santa Maria water complex, which means ‚Äújump of St. Mary. " But this name did not take root. The traveler reported on his discovery to Madrid. But the royal court was not very interested in natural resources. After all, the colonialists were sent to America for the sake of gold. Therefore, waterfalls have sunk into oblivion for several centuries. They were safely forgotten and not remembered for a long time.

It was only in the nineteenth century that Iguazu Falls in South America was once again discovered by explorer Boselli. Until 1864, the water complex belonged to Paraguay. However, Argentina and Brazil actively claimed the territory. And so a military conflict erupted. Thus began the most bloody war in South America, known as the Triple Alliance conflict. Paraguay lost the fight, so the country was forced to make significant territorial changes, as a result of which the northern bank of the river went to Brazil, and the southern - to Argentina.

The first map of the region was made at the end of the nineteenth century.

Since 1991, the power and strength of water flows has served man. Paraguay and Brazil opened Itaipu's largest hydropower plant. The amount of electricity received is 40% of the energy consumption of the two countries. This power plant can safely be called one of the most outstanding modern achievements of mankind.

Legend

An old legend says that once God wanted to marry a very beautiful Aboriginal woman, whose name was Naipu. But the girl ran away with her loved one in a canoe. An angry God created waterfalls on the river, dooming the lovers to an imminent fall. So the young man turned into a rock, and the girl into a lonely palm. Periodically, a rainbow appears in this place. They say that it is lovers with tenderness hug each other.

The origin of the natural miracle

Legend is a legend, but scientists believe that Iguazu Falls were formed a long time ago, more than 150 million years ago. The reason for their appearance was a powerful eruption of volcanoes. As a result, a tremendous cleft appeared in the earth. In southern Brazil, the river flows along a basalt platform that abruptly ends where the lava flow has frozen. Initially (approximately 20,000 years ago), the waterfalls were at the junction of the Parana and Iguazu rivers , but later, due to erosion, they retreated as much as 28 kilometers to their present location. Scientists say that annually the flows recede by three millimeters.

Description of the water complex

The height of the Iguazu Falls (in meters) reaches 80. But this is the maximum value. Across the entire width of the complex, water flows from different heights. The deepest point of the waterfall is called the "Devil's Throat". In a second, the Iguazu River overthrows from a height of 1700 m3. water. Numerous waterfalls are located both streams and cascades.

During the dry season, tourists can see two separate crescent-shaped streams. The width of each is 732 meters. And in the rainy period, both crescents form a single whole.

Most of the water flows reach a height of 60-70 meters. And only a few - 80. The most famous waterfall is the formidable name "Devil's Throat." So, it has the shape of a horseshoe. Its height is 82 meters and its width reaches 150 meters. The waterfall is located at the very border of Brazil and Argentina. If all its flows were a single whole, then the water complex would be called the widest in the world. But this is not so. The widest continuous waterfall is Victoria in Africa.

Large water streams Iguazu rush down, and then split into a series of smaller ones. And those, in turn, breaking thresholds, form clouds of spray and fog. Sunlight complements the picture, creating a magnificent rainbow, and not just one, but much more.

The name of the waterfalls

An interesting fact is that in different countries Iguazu Falls are called differently. In Argentina, he is called Rivadavia, Belgrano, Arayagaray, Two and Three Musketeers, etc. But in Brazil - Salto Floriano, Benjamin Constant.

National park

The surrounding areas of Iguazu are part of the national park. On its territory guests can see natural beauties and unique animals. Especially for tourists, boat trips are conducted (we will talk about them later in the article).

The national park is located on the border of Paraguay, Brazil and Argentina. It is called "Iguazu". The protected area from Brazil is 180 thousand hectares. This is the largest reserve in the country. And at the same time one of the largest in the world. From Argentina, Iguazu Falls border the country's national park, which occupies 55 thousand hectares.

The nature of the region and climatic conditions are close to the Amazonian forests. The park is dominated by multi-level forests. Here orchids grow on tree trunks. The lush nature of the tropics combined with the beauty of waterfalls makes an indelible impression on tourists. The national park is filled with exotic plants. Over 2 thousand species of plants grow on its territory and about 400 species of birds inhabit.

When is it better to come to the waterfalls?

Waterfalls are beautiful in any season. But still, it makes sense to take into account some features of the local climate. The best time to travel is from January to February. The rainy season lasts from November to March. It was during this period that the river is most full-flowing, and therefore more spectacular. But walking in such a period is not too pleasant due to dampness.

In the winter months, from June to August, the area is cool. It is better to visit the waterfalls in sunny time to fully enjoy the local beauties and enjoy the rainbows. Wandering around under the spray in hot weather is a pleasure. And the photos are incredibly colorful.

How to get to Iguazu Falls?

How and how you will get to the right place depends on which country you want to visit the waterfalls in. In Brazil, you need to get to the city of Foz de Iguazu, which is 1480 kilometers from Rio de Janeiro. Airplanes fly in the town and buses ply. The fare from Rio is about one hundred dollars (5700 rubles). Tourists recommend using air transport. Its cost is lower - ‚âà4000 p. (70 dollars), and the road takes much less time. Travelers arrive at Foz de Iguazu Airport. Buses run from here to the central station. Taxi services are also available. In addition, you can immediately catch a bus that goes to the national park.

In Foz de Iguazu you can stay for the night. The small town is beautiful and green. It has a well-developed tourism infrastructure. Here you can find hostels and hotels everywhere. The cost of living per day in a double room on average will cost $ 150 (8.5 thousand rubles). In a hostel, rooms are much cheaper, and you can rent apartments for two.

From the central station, regular buses run every thirty minutes to the Iguazu Park area. The journey takes about 30 minutes. In the park itself, you need to buy a bus ticket that will deliver directly to your destination.

How to get to Argentina?

According to reviews, in Argentina, Iguazu Falls can also be reached by plane or bus. Of course, air travel is more affordable and time consuming. An air ticket will cost you about $ 200 (11.5 thousand rubles). It is much cheaper than the cost of a bus ticket. Tourists arrive in the city of Puerto Igasu. It is 1340 km from Buenos Aires. In the city you can stay in one of the hostels. The minimum cost of an overnight stay is $ 20 (‚âà1100 rubles).

The road from Puerto Igasu to the national park is about 18 kilometers. Buses make flights every thirty minutes.

How to watch Iguazu?

The water complex is so huge that it can be viewed from above, from the ground, from a helicopter ... And each time you will be amazed by the stunning views. In Argentina and Brazil, boat trips and hiking trails in the park are offered. Admire the Iguazu aerial view is possible only in Brazil.

Air Tours

The Brazilian side offers the best view of the waterfalls. And yet, the scale of natural creation can only be estimated from the air. Therefore, you should go on a tour by helicopter. The flight area is located right at the entrance to the National Park. The flight duration is approximately 12 minutes. The cost of an air tour is 11 thousand rubles. According to tourists, a spectacular sight and indescribable sensations can not be compared with anything. The surging waters framed by the jungle truly resemble the devil's throat.

Boat ride

No less interesting is a boat trip. The trip lasts about three hours. When landing, tourists are given special equipment. The cost of the walk is 4 thousand rubles. On a boat, travelers are brought closer to the waterfalls, so water splashes are felt. Below you can appreciate the magnificence of the falling flows of water.

Boat tour

Boat trips are possible on the Argentinean side. A ticket can be purchased in Puerto Iguazu, its cost is a thousand rubles. Before the trip, you can take a walk in the jungle. Guests are transported by car through the park along the canyon. The sight is amazing.

National park in Brazil

Entrance to the park in Brazil costs 800 rubles. Tourists take the bus through the jungle to the waterfalls. On the way, you can see the park, which is divided into zones for different animals. There is a forest of jaguars, monkeys. Bright raccoons are walking near the walkways.

Of course, the park itself deserves close attention from tourists. However, you must correctly calculate your strength, leaving them at the waterfalls. But you also need to walk along the paths of the park. Local nature and animals will give you a lot of impressions.

The reserve has a bird park. It is so beautiful that it is worth a visit. According to tourists, Iguazu is a modern park that allows you to plunge into the natural world.

Bird park

Representatives of the feathered world from Africa, Australia, Asian countries and Brazil are gathered in the bird park. The park is considered the best in all of Latin America. Its uniqueness is that here you can watch birds in a natural environment. The birds are at arm's length from you, and they are not in a cage. Amazing variety of species. What kind of birds are not here. Especially a lot of parrots. There is also a relict species - a cassowary bird, which vaguely resembles an ostrich.

No less interesting is the room with butterflies. Insects sit on all visitors. Also in the park you can see amphibians, snakes, crocodiles and other unpleasant individuals. The park is very green, clean and beautiful. There are signs on all the tracks, so getting lost is simply impossible. The area is decorated with artificial ponds and ponds with wooden bridges. And all around are fragrant flowers. If you want a snack, you can do it in a cafe. Tourists recommend watching a bird park before the waterfalls, since then there is no strength anymore, and the perception is not so bright.
