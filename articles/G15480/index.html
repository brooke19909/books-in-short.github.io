How to get from Rimini to Venice on your own: ways and recommendations

Venice is one of the most famous and favorite tourist cities in Italy. People come here from all over Europe, and it is not surprising that the transport accessibility of the city is at its best. From Rimini to Venice you can independently get by train, bus or your own car, however, in the latter case, the car will have to be left somewhere outside the city. Since inside of Venice, instead of streets, there are mainly canals, and public transport also runs along them.





Better ride by train

It is best to travel from Rimini to Venice on your own by train, if only because there are a lot of them leaving, and they arrive at one of two railway stations in Venice: mainland Mestre or directly to the city center, to Santa Lucia station. And no parking problems. The average train duration is from 3 to almost 5 hours. Cost - from 20 euros. Based on the fact that the distance between the two cities barely exceeds one and a half hundred kilometers, the travel time seems too long. This happens because all trains transit through Bologna and make a long stop there. There are trains that stand there for about two hours.





Schedule and prices

It is easy to get from Rimini to Venice on their own, they go almost every half an hour or an hour. The schedule is so constructed that the bulk of trains go early in the morning, from about 5-6 in the morning until 11, and in the evening. This is convenient if you want to go on a trip to Venice from Rimini for a day to see the sights of the legendary city on the water. However, if you wish, you can leave at any time - there are routes both night and day. Another thing is that the price can be very different. The minimum price is 20 euros, but depending on the composition, time of departure or some other parameters, it can reach almost 100 euros.

Venice Stations

There are two railway stations in Venice, you can choose to arrive where you prefer. Santa Lucia Central Station is located directly on the island in the city center, and Mestre is on the so-called mainland. Therefore, take a ticket so that it is closer to the hotel. And if you intend to return on the same day, then, of course, it is better to take a ticket to the station of Santa Lucia (Stazione di Venezia Santa Lucia). Here, all the attractions will be literally within walking distance: to the Piazza San Marco and the Palazzo Ducale barely reach one and a half kilometers, to the Basilica - also no more than 2 km. You do not have to spend time getting from the suburbs of Mestre to the center of Venice.

Venice and Rimini are connected by a quite busy railway route, so you can take a ticket from Rimini only one way (only andata). So you can stay longer if you do not have time to see what you have in mind. And to buy a return ticket already upon arrival at the Santa Lucia train station - since trains run around the clock and often.

Travelling by car

To get from Rimini to Venice on your own by car is the best option if you are not accustomed to depend on any schedule. The route is simple - along the coast to the north to Mestre and further east along the embankment to Venice. Although you should not go to Venice by car, because finding a parking lot in this city is still a headache. Yes, and it costs a lot more. If in Mestre you can leave the car in the parking lot, paying from 5 to 15 euros per hour, then in Venice this figure will increase to 25-30 euros if you can find a free seat.

In the city itself, in its central part, there are only two large parking lots: Tronchetto, where the Bonelli bus and Piazza Roma arrive. Although in fact the choice is much wider, but information about this is difficult to find. There is also a Toderini garage, Doji parking lot, Venice garage and others, but this is more for those who have the opportunity to call directly and reserve a place for a car. For parking here you have to pay up to 30 euros per hour. If you leave your car in Mestre, this will not spoil your vacation at all, since the bus service to Venice and the suburbs is very lively, and trains run often.

Driving around Venice by car is also not a great pleasure: the streets are narrow, stone pavements. The romantic air of Venice is still better not to breathe from the car window. You also need to know that a trip by car will require additional expenses, because most of the route between Rimini and Venice will be paid, one way will have to pay about 15 euros. Of course, after a day spent in the medieval atmosphere of Venice, you will most likely forget about all the expenses - you will get too many unforgettable impressions, but it is better to know about them in advance.

Those who rest in Rimini and think about an independent trip from Rimini to Venice should definitely implement these plans - Venice will give you a lot of positive emotions.
