Italy attracts travelers not only with shopping, but also with a great vacation - it is here that there are many picturesque resorts that attract tourists from all over the world. One of these resorts is Bordighera. We will tell you more about it!

Bordighera, Italy: A Short Excursion

Bordighera is not only the most popular resort, the city is one of the oldest in the whole country. It was founded in the fourth century BC - just think about how long it was! The founders of Bordighera were ligurs - it is in Liguria that the city is located. At first it was a small fishing village, but over time, it gradually began to grow and mutate. From the end of the 17th century until the beginning of the next century, the city was considered the capital of a small republic.

Today Bordighera is a wonderful resort, which is famous for its magnificent climate, beaches, hotels and attractions. The weather in Bordighera, Italy, is excellent: the average annual temperature is +17 , which makes staying in this sunny city comfortable any time. The city consists of two parts - the old, historical one, on Cape San Ampelio, where you can walk and admire the buildings that have been preserved from the past, and the new one, where all the hotels and villas are located.

What to see

Below we will tell you in detail about those places in Bordighera that you should definitely include in the list of mast visits to any vacationer in this cozy resort town. And believe me, they will not leave anyone indifferent!

Corso italy

One of the main attractions of the Italian Bordighera is the main street of the town. It is called Corso Italia and simply replete with signs of shops, inviting tourists to themselves. The things that are sold there are one hundred percent Italian, the assortment is huge, so Bordighera can compete in shopping even with Milan, the recognized capital of fashion. Corso Italia is open daily, in addition, lovers of shopping can visit the local market, but keep in mind that it is closed on Sunday.

City wall

In the historical part of Bordighera in Italy there is a city wall - another attraction of the resort town. It has three gates and the parish church of St. Mary Magdalene, erected in the XVIII century. He is famous for the fact that the remains of St. Ampelio are stored in it - it will be described later.

Chapel of St. Ampelio

One of the main attractions of Bordighera in Italy (photo below) is the San Ampelio Chapel, located on a small rocky cape of the same name.

From it you can view the eastern part of Bordighera. Saint Ampelio is the patron saint of Bordighera. According to legend, he arrived in the city in the V century, and the church of his name arose six centuries later. It was partially restored in the century before last, and partially - it was rebuilt (facade and bell tower). Near the altar in the church stands a sculpture of St. Ampelio of the XVII century.

City Hall

In 1870, the city hall was erected on Piazza del Popolo . This building in all its splendor has survived to this day and continues to attract tourists to this day. It was built by the famous French architect of the XIX century Charles Garnier (he is also the creator of the Paris Opera). Nearby is another attraction of Bordighera in Italy - the oratorio of the Apostle Bartholomew, built in the XV century.

Garden of exotic plants

The garden, founded by Giacomo Pallanca at the very beginning of the last century, is located on a steep rocky slope with magnificent sea views. Garden Winter

A huge collection of Mediterranean flora is presented in another stunning Bordighera garden in Italy - the Winter Garden.

There are incredibly many different citrus trees - and, walking slowly along the alleys of the garden, you can enjoy freshly picked orange. In this garden there are a lot of palm trees, olive trees and other no less beautiful plants, in the shade of which it is very pleasant to sit on a fine day.

Interesting facts about Bordighera

In the mid-eighties of the XIX century, Claude Monet worked in Bordighera. Landscapes painted by him in this city are today stored in the St. Petersburg Hermitage.

It was in Bordighera in 1940 that the historical negotiations of Mussolini and Franco took place, the very ones at which the head of Spain refused to support Italy and Germany in the war.

In Bordighera is the winter residence of Margarita of Savoy, built in the 19th century.

The population of Bordighera is just over 10,000.

All beaches of the resort are pebble.
