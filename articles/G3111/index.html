2. Four-armed and two-armed

According to scientists, the very first person on earth appeared 2.5 million years ago in Africa. They called him Homo habilis, that is, a skilled person. Unlike the organ of primitive ancestors, his brain was larger and his mental abilities were much higher. The first man on earth walked on four legs.

Another million years passed, and a man who stood on two legs came to replace him, and from this came his name - Homo erectus - an erect man.

3. The emergence of our direct ancestors

Outwardly, our ancestors were very similar to monkeys, although they had human skills. But with the advent of upright people, the rapid development of mankind began, and about 100 thousand years ago they took on outlines similar to modern human faces and forms. It is they, Homo sapiens, who are our direct ancestors. We were engaged in fishing, hunting, and farming. There was interest in occupying large territories, exploring other lands, and resettlement began in Europe, Asia and Australia.





Life, of course, was difficult, a process of survival took place. To get a piece of meat, you had to fight with terrible beasts.

Names for centuries were given according to the material from which the tools for labor and against predators were made. A little easier when they learned to make fire. From history, we know that there were periods of stone, bronze.

Then there was a division into clans, families, various races and nationalities. As they accumulated, classes of rich and poor appeared.
