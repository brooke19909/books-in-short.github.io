How to crochet a Christmas tree: types of Christmas trees, their description and photos

Crocheted Christmas tree can be a New Year tree decoration, decor for a festive table or a cute souvenir for a friend. The options for making knitted Christmas trees proposed in this article are very simple to implement. These recipes will come in handy for novice craftswomen, as well as those who raise young children. Indeed, co-creation is very popular with many children.

How to crochet a Christmas tree: the easiest way

This method of knitting a Christmas tree can be applied even in cases where the craftswoman does not have a crochet at all or just started training.



In the first case, she can use any tape or tape at her discretion. In the second, you need to tie a long chain of air loops (VP). Before crocheting a Christmas tree , you should think about yarn and a hook. It is better to choose a thread thin and dense, even hard. For example, cotton or linen. And the hook should be small (optimally 0.9-1.2).

The length of the initial chain can be calculated by determining the width of the future Christmas tree and multiplying this number by eight. The width of the knitted ribbon should be no more than 2 cm. Several rows of single crochet (RLS) or double crochet (CCH) stitches can be connected.

The recipe for crocheting a Christmas tree for beginners involves assembling the product. The tape should be laid out on a flat surface, having formed all the bends, and string each layer on the thread. Between the layers you can put simple or tied beads or other appropriate decor.

Crocheted cone tree: workshop

The Christmas tree, the cone serves as the basis for it, is the most universal. It can be executed in almost any color, decorated at your own discretion and used literally everywhere.

Before crocheting a Christmas tree, you should consider filling it. It can be a synthetic winterizer, cotton wool or a cardboard cone. If it was decided to dwell on the latter option, then it must be prepared in advance. The size of the finished cone should be guided in the process. So, when there are all the necessary materials (yarn, hook, scissors, cone or stuffing, decor), you can start knitting a Christmas tree:

  1. Begin by knitting the bottom. To do this, they collect 4 VPs, close them in a ring and knit 6SBN in it.
  2. In each next row it is necessary to increase the number of RLS by 6. It is better to make additions not one above the other, but a little diagonally, otherwise it will turn out not a circle, but a hexagon. When the width of the circle is equal to the size of the bottom of the tree (often about 7 rows are enough), it is time to move on to knitting a conical base.
  3. As the boundary between the two parts, you need to knit a series of RLS without additions, while knitting all RLS for the back wall of the loops of the previous row.
  4. Next, you should perform a uniform reduction of the canvas for 6 sc in every fifth row.
  5. After knitting about half of the tree, you need to insert a cardboard cone inside. If a synthetic winterizer or cotton wool is chosen as the filling, then they should be placed in the tree when it is tied to 2/3.
  6. The last third of the Christmas tree leaf should be reduced by 6 in each row.

So the basis for decoration is ready. It is very important to get a pointed top of the tree. This is possible if, after all RLS are reduced, perform several VIs and only then hide the thread.
