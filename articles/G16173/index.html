The main milestones of his work

Jerry Gionis was born in 1974. Sixteen years later, the young man graduates from high school and goes to college, where one of the subjects was photography. Jerry first appeared in 1989. After that, the young photographer did not let her out of her hands. After studying for only a year, Gionis dropped out of college, and he got a job as a seller of a photographic equipment store. Soon the young man got married. His chosen one was the eighteen-year-old Dahlia.

Photography so captivated Jerry that in 1994 he began giving classes for free in one of Melbourne's photo schools. Things were moving so well that Gionis was soon appointed to the post of manager, and then he led a group of photographers. This was the beginning of a brilliant career. Little secrets

The photographer must speak less and show more, but without a camera in his hands. Every professional should consider five key points:

  • shine;
  • location
  • pose
  • exposure on the face;
  • emotional condition.

Often, weddings are filmed indoors, so you need to maximize the use of light from windows for photos. Lighting on the side gives texture. The photographer needs to catch objects reflecting glare. The body of the bride or groom is usually in a position from the light, and the face should be directed at him. It is very good if the lighting will focus on the cheekbones. Jerry Gionis leaves all sources open, does not close the windows.

Not only the bride is the basis of the wedding photo. The groom should not be shackled either. For a man, the forward discharge of the body is very important in the photo. The chest should be closer to the camera, the stomach is pulled in, the chin rises. A good picture will be obtained near the window, if you select an open light source.
