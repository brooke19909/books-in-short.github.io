Memorial zones of St. Petersburg: Seaside Victory Park

There is Seaside Victory Park in St. Petersburg - a place that attracts citizens and numerous tourists from all over the world. It preserves not only the amazing nature of the Neva Delta, but also many monuments of historical and cultural significance, and also provides ample opportunities for interesting pastime and relaxation. How to get to the Seaside Victory Park of St. Petersburg? To explain this is not so difficult: you need to get on the subway and get off at Krestovsky Ostrov station, cross the road. On the contrary, you will see the main entrance to its territory.

A bit about history

The breakdown of the Seaside Park begins immediately after the end of the Great Patriotic War. However, at the beginning of the XVIII century, these lands were in the possession of the first governor of the city, Alexander Danilovich Menshikov. In the middle of the XIX century, their owners became representatives of the old noble family of Beloselsky-Belozersky. During the years of Soviet rule, it was supposed to make a recreation area for citizens here.

The current Seaside Victory Park in St. Petersburg was conceived as a zone reminiscent of the exploits of Soviet soldiers in battles with the Nazis. The idea is immortalized in a stone stele. The relief depicts a kneeling child, lowering a seedling into the ground as the basis of the future park - a symbol of eternal life.
