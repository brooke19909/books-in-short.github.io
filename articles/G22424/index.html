Indoor antennas for TVs: features, types, advantages and disadvantages

Currently, almost every home has at least one television of domestic or foreign production. To ensure the broadcast of a television signal, he needs an antenna. There are many types of receivers. They are divided by the type of received signal, by purpose, shape and size. The subject of our conversation today will be indoor antennas for televisions. Typically, such receiving devices are used when it is impossible to install a stationary device or to provide temporary needs. Let's see what are indoor antennas for TVs, what features they are characterized.



Such products may differ in the type of received signal (meter and decimeter range), in shape, size, and may be passive and active. Indoor antennas for TVs are significantly inferior in characteristics to stationary outdoor devices. They can provide good signal quality only if there is a television tower under your windows. If it is not, then problems with signal reception begin. Very often you can see such a picture when a room antenna is hung on a metal curtain or pieces of wire are screwed to it, but these measures are not able to ensure a good quality of the received signal. Anyway, on the screen of your TV there will be "snow", running stripes and more.

But if you are still interested in indoor antennas for TVs, then we will continue to consider these products. The shape of these devices depends on the range of the received signal. So, meter antennas have become widespread, they are two telescopic pins that are adjustable in length and inclination. The second common type is decimeter receivers - five or more parallel aluminum conductors connected to a common perpendicular frame.



If you have no alternative, and you want to use only the indoor option, then it is better to purchase a product such as an active antenna for a TV. The indoor active antenna is characterized by a higher gain. However, do not forget that each antenna has its own operating frequency range. There are indoor antennas narrow-band (single-channel), broadband (multi-channel). Each receiver indicates the band in MHz. Typically, indoor antennas have a limited frequency range. That is, some channels can be configured in good quality, others will be in poor quality, and still others will not be able to catch. This should be considered when choosing such devices.



In conclusion, we say that if you need a simple cheap device that quickly and easily sets up, collapses and turns around, then you can use the indoor antenna for the TV. Reviews of these products come to the fact that they are compact and easy to operate, mobile and do not require special knowledge to configure.
