Sound volume: what is the difference between sleep, background and decibel

Sound waves, affecting the human eardrum, cause vibrations of the hairs. The amplitude of these sound vibrations is directly related to the perceived loudness of these waves - the larger it is, the stronger the sound volume will be felt. This, of course, is a simplified interpretation. But the point is clear!



Each person will have his own perception of the same sound power. Therefore, it will be fair to say that loudness is a subjective quantity. In addition, this parameter depends on the frequency and amplitude of sound vibrations, as well as the pressure of the waves. Such factors as the duration of vibrations, their localization in space, timbre and spectral composition influence the sound volume.

In practice, in order to indicate or compare the volume, the decibel is most often used - a derivative of one from bel. This is due to the fact that the increase in sound power does not occur in a linear dependence on the intensity of the waves, but in a logarithmic one. 1 bel is equal to a tenfold change in the strength of the amplitude of the oscillations. This is a fairly large unit. Therefore, for the calculations use its tenth - decibel.
