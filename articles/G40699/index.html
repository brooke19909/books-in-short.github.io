Lefortovo Park in Moscow

Lefortovo Park in Moscow can hardly be classified as a particularly popular holiday destination for both Muscovites themselves and city guests. Nevertheless, this does not prevent him from becoming one of the objects known and loved by many.

One thing can be said with complete certainty: having visited its territory at least once, you will strive to get here again and again, because Lefortovo Park actually knows how to win the hearts of all, even the most experienced travelers.

You can talk endlessly about this area. An interesting story attracts curious visitors who want to hide from the summer heat and find the long-awaited coolness here, outdoor enthusiasts enjoy playing ball or badminton in the warm season, and in winter they ski or build snow castles.

Lefortovo Park in Moscow: the history of creation





Golovinsky garden is actually a huge historical architectural and landscape-park massif.

It should be noted that it is also considered the first stationary and one of the oldest parks in Russia, which is one of the most famous in Europe. For example, now not many people know that it was he who once became the prototype for most parks in St. Petersburg.

Initially, the palace of Field Marshal F. Golovin, the head of the Foreign Ministry, was built here, which, among other things, was also an associate of the great reformer Peter I.

The Golovinsky Palace was built in 1703, and this, no doubt, an important lesson, such as designing and managing work, was entrusted to the then-famous architect D. Ivanov. The latter built the building on the model of the Versailles ensemble.

The park itself got its name in honor of F. Lefort, a famous teacher and friend of Peter I, in the very first years of his reign. They wanted to hold festivities and arrange receptions in the palace, and around it was a garden, which was personally laid by the emperor.

Later, during the reign of Empress Anna Ivanovna (beginning in 1730), the park was called the "Versailles on the Yauz".

It was then that the architect Francesco Rastrelli designed the Annoieff palace and park ensemble in the Baroque style.

It should be noted that Lefortovo Park was considered the largest in Moscow in the XVII century.

Metropolitan Gondolas





Lefortovo Park (Moscow) is truly unique. Once on its territory, a special hydraulic system was built, through which Peter I hoped to get from St. Petersburg to Moscow.

Today, specialists are actively working here, more than 50 archaeological work has been carried out, the bottom of the reservoirs has been examined and about 200 m of the Annihuf Canal line have been covered. The discovery of coastal fortifications built at that far time, embankments on the surface, foundations of arbors, bridges, marinas on the Big Pond and the fountain in front of the Grotto became a real discovery.

Another interesting attraction of the park is the half-gondola. This ancient building with a bust of Peter I was built in 1805 in honor of the place of "rest" of the emperor. During a severe hurricane that swept in June 1904, unfortunately, it was completely destroyed, but it was possible to restore it, returning to its previous appearance.

Archaeological site





Under the supervision of the chief archaeologist of Moscow A. Veksler, archaeological excavations were carried out for three years in Lefortovo Park for the money of the city budget . They provided the specialists with not only a lot of valuable information about the initial layout of the landscape gardening monument, but also the initial data for its future reconstruction. Thanks to the excavations, the historical basis for the restoration of the territory was provided.

Great place to stay

Few people doubt that Lefortovo Park is actually a great place for walking and distraction from the bustle of the city. Muscovites, guests of the capital, mothers with prams, young couples in love and pensioners have a rest in it.

Historical excursions for the guests of the palace and park ensemble, theatrical performances are systematically arranged here, and on big holidays the brass band plays a variety of military music.

Since 1934, there has been a stadium in the park that hosts visitors to this day. Here it is a must-visit for all sports fans.

The foggy future of the park





Today, the question of the future fate of this palace and park ensemble is very acute. Why? The thing is that such a historically valuable architectural object is in very poor condition: it is half built up, partially abandoned, and the original ensemble is completely divided into parts.

From the very beginning, both the park and the palace were one. Now only the green territory can be considered part of the city, and the palace and other ancient buildings are the property of federal authorities and transferred to the military.

To save Lefortovo, a cultural-historical, formerly magnificent ensemble, in the beginning XXI century Under the territory of the park, a huge city tunnel with a length of up to 3.2 km was built. It became the fifth longest tunnel in Europe.

Hope for a better future for the park is also encouraged by the fact that in 2005, by an order of the Moscow government, it was included in the Moscow State Museum of Public Relations (Moscow State United Museum-Reserve). And this means that, most likely, the object will still not be allowed to destroy.
