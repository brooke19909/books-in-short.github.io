Earth satellite general information

It is no secret that the Moon is a natural satellite of our planet. It is one of the brightest in the sky. The distance between the Earth and its natural satellite is more than 300 thousand kilometers. Surprisingly, the Moon is the only object outside the Earth that man has visited.

The Earth and the Moon are often called paired celestial. This is due to the fact that their mass and size are quite close indicators. On the moon, studies have been repeatedly conducted. It is proved that the force of gravity acts there. On the surface of a natural satellite, a person can easily turn a small car.



Many are interested in what moon actually is. It revolves around the earth. Depending on the position of the natural satellite, you can see it in completely different ways. The moon makes a full circle around the Earth in 27 days.

Each of us saw darker or bluer patches on the moon. What is it really? Many years ago it was believed that these were the so-called lunar seas. This concept exists today. But in fact, these are petrified areas through which lava erupted before. According to research, this happened many billions of years ago. Consider below what is called lunar soil.

In 1897, the American geologist first used the term "regolith." Today it is used to determine lunar soil.

Regolith color

Regolith is lunar soil. It has been studied for many years. The main question that researchers from all over the world are trying to answer is: is it possible to grow anything on such soil.

What is the color of the lunar soil? Each of us can safely say that the moon has a silver-yellow color. That is how we see her from our planet. However, this is not at all true. According to researchers, the lunar soil is close to black - a dark brown color. It should be noted that to determine the color of the soil on the territory of a natural satellite, you should not focus on the photographs taken there. It's no secret that cameras slightly distort the real color.



The thickness of the soil on the moon

The uppermost layer of the moon is regolithic. Soil studies are important for the creation of drawings and the further construction of bases. It is believed that the lunar soil arises as a result of filling old craters with newly formed ones. The thickness of the soil is calculated by the ratio of the depth of the so-called sea and its bulk. The presence of stones in the crater is associated with the content of rock formations in it. Chemical composition

Lunar soil contains a large number of chemical trace elements. Among them are silicon, oxygen, iron, titanium, aluminum, calcium and magnesium. Information on the composition of the soil was obtained through methods of remote and x-ray spectroscopy. It is worth noting that there are several ways to study the lunar soil. Their main problem is the separation of attention on the age of regolith and its composition.



The negative effects of moon dust on the human body

Scientists at the National Aeronautics and Space Administration have studied the pros and cons of planned exploration and relocation to the moon. They proved that moon dust is unusually dangerous to the human body. It is known that the so-called dust storms are activated once every two weeks. Scientists have also proven that regular inhalation of moon dust can lead to serious illness.

On the surface of the lungs, there are special fibers on which all dust is collected. In the future, the body gets rid of it with the help of a cough. It is worth noting that too small particles do not attach to the fibers. The human body is not adapted to the negative effects of moon dust due to its small size. Scientists believe that this factor should be taken into account when developing and building bases on the surface of a natural satellite.



The negative impact of dust, which creates storms on the surface of a natural satellite, was confirmed by the Apollo 17 lunar expedition. One of the astronauts, who was part of it, after some time spent on the moon, began to complain of poor health and fever. It was found that the deterioration in health was due to inhalation of moon dust, which was on board with the suits. The astronaut was not faced with complications thanks to filters installed on the ship, which cleaned the air as soon as possible.

The peculiarity is that it will be located on the dark side of the moon. The device will study the geological conditions on the surface of a natural satellite.

Organic matter in the composition of the lunar soil

After one of the Apollo missions, it was revealed that the lunar soil brought from the expedition contains organic substances, namely amino acids. It is no secret that they are involved in the formation of proteins and are an important factor in the development of all living organisms on Earth.

Scientists have proved that lunar soil is not suitable for the development of all life forms known to us. There are four versions of the appearance of amino acids in the composition of the lunar soil. According to scientists, they could be on the moon, delivered from Earth along with astronauts. According to other versions, these are gas emissions, the solar wind and asteroids.



After a series of studies, scientists proved that, most likely, amino acids got into the composition of the lunar soil due to pollution from the Earth, and this was also facilitated by the fall of asteroids on the surface of a natural satellite.

The first flights to the moon

In January 1959, a rocket was launched in the Soviet Union , which launched the Luna-1 automatic interplanetary station onto the flight path to the moon. This is the first device that has reached the second space velocity.

Already in September, the Luna-2 automatic interplanetary station was launched. Unlike the first, she reached the celestial body, and also delivered a pennant with the emblem of the USSR.

In less than a month, the third automatic interplanetary station was launched into space. Her weight was more than 200 kilograms. On its body were solar panels. Within half an hour, the station automatically took more than 20 pictures of the moon using the built-in camera. Thanks to this, humanity first saw the back side of a natural satellite. It was in October 1959 that people found out which moon actually was.

Magma on the surface of a celestial body

In one of the latest studies of the moon, channels with frozen magma were revealed under its upper layer. Scientists say that thanks to such a finding, we can find out the real age of our natural satellite. It is worth noting that to date, the chronology of the appearance of the moon is unknown.



The thickness of the lunar crust is 43 kilometers. Recent studies of the moon have shown that it is permeated by underground channels. Scientists suggest that they formed almost immediately after the emergence of a natural satellite. Almost all the channels are filled with frozen magma. At their locations, higher gravitational fields are present. According to preliminary data, the age of underground channels is more than four billion years. Such a finding is an impetus for further studies of the natural satellite.
