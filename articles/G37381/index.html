What is space? Interesting facts about space

Each of us has heard more than once that the cosmos is something beyond the borders of our planet, it is the Universe. In general, space is a space that extends endlessly in all directions, including galaxies and stars, black holes and planets, cosmic dust and other objects. It is believed that there are other planets or even whole galaxies that are also inhabited by intelligent people.





Bit of history

The middle of the 20th century was remembered by many space race, the winner of which came the USSR. In 1957, the first artificial satellite was created and launched, and a little later the first living creature visited space.

Two years later, the artificial satellite of the Sun entered orbit, and a station called Luna-2 was able to appear on the surface of the Moon. The legendary Squirrel and Strelka visited space only in 1960, and a year later a man also visited it.

The year 1962 was remembered for the group flight of ships, and the year 1963 for the first time that a woman was in orbit. Man managed to reach outer space two years later.





Each of the subsequent years of our history was marked by events related to space exploration.

A station of international importance was organized in space only in 1998. It was the launch of satellites, and the organization of orbital stations, and the numerous flights of people from other countries.

What is he like

The scientific point of view says that the cosmos are certain parts of the universe that surround celestial bodies and their atmospheres. However, this space cannot be called completely empty. It has been proven that it contains a certain amount of hydrogen and has interstellar matter. Scientists have also confirmed the existence of electromagnetic radiation within it.

Now science does not know data on the finite limits of space. Astrophysicists and radio astronomers claim that the instruments are not able to "see" the whole cosmos. This is despite the fact that their workspace covers 15 billion light years.





Scientific hypotheses do not deny the possible existence of universes like ours, but there is no evidence for this either. In general, the cosmos is the universe, it is the world. It is characterized by orderliness and materialization.

Learning process

The first in space were animals. People were afraid, but wanted to explore unknown expanses, so they used dogs, pigs and monkeys as pioneers. Some of them returned, some did not.

Now people are actively exploring outer space. It has been proven that weightlessness adversely affects human health. It does not allow fluids to move in the right directions, which contributes to the loss of calcium in the body. Also in space, people become somewhat plump, there are problems with the intestines and clogging of the nose.





In outer space, almost every person becomes ill with a "cosmic disease." Its main symptoms are nausea, dizziness, headache. A consequence of this disease is hearing problems.

Cosmos in ancient philosophy

Plato and Aristotle tried to show the cosmos as an extremely complete being, an innocent being, an aesthetic whole. The perception of outer space was largely based on the mythology of the ancient Greeks.
