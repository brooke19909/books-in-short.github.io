Do you know how an iPhone differs from a smartphone

The 2007 presentation of his brainchild by Apple revolutionized the world of cellular communications. People saw that in one such device you can combine the capabilities of a computer, media player and tablet.





The first iPhone was a truly revolutionary device, long ahead of its time. No company in the world could compete with him in functionality.

The main feature that sets the iPhone apart from other smartphones is the presence of the latest iOS operating system at that time. Then the concepts of "smartphone" and "iPhone" were one. The only competitor to Apple at that time was the Finnish Nokia, but it could only compete with the Apple company with its design and some features. But the slow and dank Windows Mobile of European phones could not be compared with high-tech iOS. But still, how does an iPhone differ from a smartphone? Indeed, in fact, these are two identical concepts that differ from each other only in the operating system.

iPhone is primarily a style and indicator of status. Having such an apparatus is an opportunity to show your position in society. Apple has always focused on uniqueness and security. Products apple company could communicate via Bluetooth only with each other. Any data should be received on the phone only through the Internet and only through a special application. On the one hand, this is a good protection against the possibility of infecting your device with a computer virus, but on the other hand, especially for residents of our country, this creates a number of inconveniences. And the question of how an iPhone differs from a smartphone, the first thing you can answer: "Style, quality and reliability." But at the time, they were all synonymous words.



In 2008, Google acquired the Android company, which was developing its own Linux-based OS. In November of the same year, its first version was introduced to the market. The emergence of a new system that can compete with iOS has sparked a revolution among mobile devices. One of the main opponents of Apple was the South Korean company Samsung. The advent of smartphones running Android OS has significantly undermined the monopoly of Steve Jobs. And the question "what is the difference between an iPhone and a smartphone" began to take on a sharper outline.

It all started with the fact that Google made it possible for almost any manufacturer to release their own PDA. They rushed en masse to store shelves. Along with expensive, to match the iPhone, models could take the device and much easier for a price not exceeding $ 150-200. The latter sued the Korean company, accusing her of plagiarizing and trying to copy the iPhone. The struggle of the two giants was closely watched by the whole world.





Each company had its supporters and opponents. Disputes erupted not only in courtrooms, but also on many online resources and forums. The reviews that appeared at that time made it possible to understand how the iPhone differs from a smartphone.

But there can only be one answer to this. iPhone is the smartphone.
