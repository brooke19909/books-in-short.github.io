Learning to crochet a beautiful pattern "Rhombuses". Schemes for beginner needlewomen

A hook is a convenient knitting tool that allows you to create amazingly beautiful patterns. Even novice masters, armed with detailed diagrams and understandable descriptions, can easily perform amazing paintings with floral, geometric or fantasy patterns. In this article, we will share beautiful crochet patterns of openwork rhombuses, and provide detailed descriptions of the work process for beginners in knitting. Using our schemes, you can learn how to create unique products - cozy autumn shawls, scarves, cardigans.

Beautiful pattern "Rhombuses" for beginners knitters

This simple and at the same time interesting and stylish pattern is perfect for making a stole, dress or scarf. Crochet rhombuses will turn out at beginner needlewomen. All that is required is to prepare the necessary tools for work, yarn and pre-master how the air loop, half-crochet and double crochet are performed. It is the use of these simple elements that will allow you to get a nontrivial crochet pattern - "Rhombuses".

Let's learn how to perform it using the example of a test sample. We connect the initial chain of 18 air loops. In the first row we make 5 air loops (VP), 7 half-columns with a crochet (PPSN), 5 VP. We skip two loops of the base and in the next do 7 PPSN. Next, we knit 2 VP. We skip 1 initial loop and perform 1 double crochet (1).

In row number 2 we knit 4 VP, 5 PPSN (in the 5 central loops of seven half-columns of the previous row), 3 VP, 1 PPSN in an arch of 5 loops, again 3 VP and 5 PPSN. We finish 3 VP and one half-column. The last element is performed in the third loop of the initial chain of the lower row.

In the third row we create 1 VP, 1 PPSN in the first loop of the previous row, 3 PPSN (in the central loops of a group of 5 PPSN of the lower row), 3 VP. Next, we create again 3 PPSN. Please note that we knit the central one in the loop of the half-column of the previous row, and the other two in adjacent arches. Now we execute 3 VP, 3 PPSN (in three central loops of 5 half-columns of the previous row), 3 VP and 2 PPSN (in the last 2 loops).

Continue to knit

We continue to learn how to crochet a rhombus. The description of the fourth row of the scheme is as follows. At the beginning we make 1 VP, 2 PPSN, 3 VP and 1 PPSN (in the central loop of three half-columns of row No. 3). Next, we knit 3 VP and 5 PPSN, three of which are in the loops of the underlying half columns, and the others in the adjacent arches. We carry out 3 VP, 1 PPSN (to the central half-column from the group of the previous row), 3 VP and 3 PPSN.

In the fifth row, we do 1 VP, 3 PPSN, 5 VP, 7 PPSN (five of them in the PPSN loops, the rest in adjacent arches), 5 VP, 4 PPSN. We hope that difficulties in creating crocheted rhombuses have not yet arisen? We continue to work!

We knit the sixth row according to the following pattern: 1 VP, 2 PPSN, 3 VP, 1 PPSN (in the central loop of five elements of the lower row), 3 VP, 5 PPSN (in five central half-columns of seven), 3 VP, 1 PPSN, 3 VP, 3 PPSN.

Row number 7 is performed as follows: 1 VP, 1 PPSN, 3 VP, 3 PPSN (the central one in the loop of the half-column of the previous row, and the rest in adjacent arches). Next, 3 VP, 3 PPSN (in the three central loops of five half-columns), 3 VP, 3 PPSN (the middle in the loop of the half-column of the previous row, the other two in adjacent chains), 3 VP, 2 PPSN.

Finish the work. Crochet "Rhombuses" pattern is almost ready

Eighth row: 4 VP, 5 PPSN (three of them in three loops of half-columns, two in arches), 3 VP, 1 PPSN (in the central loop of 3 PPSN of the previous row), 3 VP, 5 PPSN (three in three loops half columns, two in arches), 3 VP, 1 PPSN in a chain of VP. The sample is almost finished.

We start the ninth row with 6 VP, then we knit 7 PPSN (five of them - in five loops of the half-columns of the previous row, two - in adjacent arches), 5 VP, 7 PPSN, 2 VP, 1 PPSN in the last chain.

Next, we repeat the pattern of rows, that is, we knit the tenth by analogy with the second, the eleventh with the third, etc. As a result, we get a beautiful, openwork crochet fabric. Rhombuses in a pattern add up to a harmonious pattern. Be sure to learn how to knit according to this pattern, in the future you will be able to use it when creating original stoles, bactus or scarves.

Another good pattern with openwork rhombuses

How nice it is to wrap yourself in a warm soft shawl made by yourself in the autumn cold! She will warm her owner and deliver aesthetic pleasure thanks to a laconic and stylish pattern.

To create such a shawl, Alpaca Royal Alize yarn with a density of 100 g per 250 m is required. It is soft and warm, great for autumn clothes due to the content of alpaca wool in its composition. You will also need a hook for 3, scissors. We will work according to the following scheme.

We knit autumn shawl

We will create a product pattern using chains of 5 air loops and groups of 5 columns with one crochet. In fact, it is quite simple to execute. The main thing is to adhere to the scheme, moving from bottom to top and turning the product.

In the first row we knit 3 VP and 4 double crochet (1), backing two loops. In the second row - first 3 VP, 4 C1H, single crochet (RLS) and a group of 5 C1H. We start row number three with three VPs and 4 1, then we knit 1 RLS (in the third column of the previous row from the third from the end), a chain of 5 VPs, again 1 RLS. We end with a group of 5 C1H. We continue to knit a shawl according to the scheme, using arches from VP and a group of columns, to the required size of the product. As a result, you will get a cozy, beautiful and warm stole with a hook pattern "Rhombuses". I wish you creative success!
