Russian Radio - 105.7 MHz

The second most popular channel and silver on our list, representing the rating of Moscow radio stations , goes to Russian Radio. The main feature of this station is broadcasting exclusively in Russian, that is, there are no Western songs, only domestic speech and music.





All radio stations in Moscow, before getting to the peak of their popularity, went through a difficult and thorny path, and Russian Radio was no exception. His first broadcast was released back in 1995, and initially everything was broadcast in test mode.

A few months later, the first advertisement appeared, and the creators of the radio did not fail, having inserted the phrases of Nikolai Fomenko into each advertising block, which ridiculed our realities in a humorous form. A few years later Fomenko was replaced by Vadim Galygin, and after him Dmitry Nagiyev took hold as a humorous commentator.

In 1997, the Golden Gramophone Award was established. It was recognized by all radio stations in Moscow and Russia. A rewarding ceremony is held every year in the Kremlin, and prize winners are selected based on the results of the radio listeners' voting.
