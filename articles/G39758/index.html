History of balneological resorts

Even the ancient Greeks highly appreciated the treatment on the waters. Then the Romans mastered this practice. They own the term "balneotherapy". Translated from Latin, this means "bathing treatment." The Romans are great lovers of term; they knew a lot about sulfur and other sources, as well as massages and rubbing. It was they who built the first ever balneological resorts in Europe throughout the entire territory of their empire. In the Middle Ages, healing springs were fanned with a halo of holiness, and many monasteries profited from them. Now these recreational areas have specialization. All use the latest methods of treatment and rehabilitation of the body after a disease.
