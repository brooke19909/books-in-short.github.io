Red cave Kizil-Koba in Crimea

The Kizil-Koba Valley ("Red Cave" in Turkic) is located on the territory of the Crimean Peninsula, in the Simferopol District, near the village of Angara. The territory got its name in honor of the main attraction, so beloved by tourists - the cave system with a length of more than 25 km.

The highest of them (145 m) is called the Blue Drop Hall. Kizil-Koba Cave is also notable for the fact that it contains the largest stalactite in Europe, 8 meters long. Its approximate age is 8 thousand years.

The cave complex forms a complex and impassable labyrinth of six conditional floors, however, for tourist (not extreme) trips, a route of only 500 meters is reserved for which people without an escort of a guide are not allowed. Red Cave (Kizil-Koba)

This complex is a natural formation that appeared as a result of the erosion by the Kizilkobinka (underground river) of rocks. It is easy to guess that this object got its name due to its color. The rock forming Kizil-Kobu, calcium bicarbonate, under the influence of water is transformed into travertine (calcareous tuff) of a reddish hue.

As mentioned earlier, the cave arches are uneven in height, so in some passages you have to bend down to the ground. However, there are also very high halls (for example, the Blue Drop Hall), where you can wander with your head held high.





Kizil-Koba is inhabited by bats, which can often be found sleeping in the arches of the cave. They are accustomed to the noise created by people, but do not wake them up specially or shoot with a bright flash, because the reaction of frightened animals is unpredictable.

Cave inside

Atmosphere of the cave complex is set by lighting established along the entire route. It varies in intensity so as to create fancy shadows and at the same time cover obstacles in the way. However, there are not many of them: inside Kizil-Koba it is equipped with paths and railings for security purposes (only an excursion route).





Cave lovers will appreciate the cave, because inside it there is not only stalactite, recognized as the largest in Europe, but also many other sags of very bizarre forms.

In addition, inside you can often see reservoirs formed by the underground river Kizilkobinka, otherwise called Su-Uchkhan. It goes outside with a waterfall tearing off the edge of the tufa platform, visible along the way to the complex. The water in this river is so clear that sometimes in a cave it can be seen only by the characteristic glare on the surface and walls around.

Earlier (about 2.5 thousand years ago) Kizil-Koba was inhabited by ancient tribes, but scientists still fail to associate them with any culture: some call them Kizil-Kobintsy, the second - Taurus, and the third - Cimmerians once capturing the Crimean steppe and the Northern Black Sea coast. However, no matter how many archaeologists try to find something resembling cave painting, to draw more accurate conclusions, while their attempts are unsuccessful.

Visiting Rules

Those who decide to visit the Red Cave should familiarize themselves with the rules that the guide also voices:

  1. Before going to Kizil-Koba, you need to wear warm clothes, which are not a pity to stain (best overalls), since the temperature inside does not rise above 13 ¬∞ C.
  2. To avoid injuries, the visitor should monitor the height of the arches, look under his feet and follow the instructions of the guide.
  3. Any shooting is allowed and is included in the ticket price.
  4. It is forbidden to touch the electrical equipment and leaks inside the caves.
  5. If necessary, it is permitted to use only electric lights, lighting a fire under a ban.
  6. You can not go beyond the equipped route, otherwise the visitor will be fined.
  7. It is forbidden to litter, draw on the walls and do any other harm to the aesthetic appearance of Kizil-Koba.




Path to the caves

Kizil-Koba, a photo of which can be seen in the article, is located on the campsite "Fairy Valley". The car should be left in the parking lot and continue on the road either on foot or in a train for an additional fee (only in summer and winter this type of inland transport is not available and travel by car is allowed).





Next, there is a rather long road along a slope in the thick of various vegetation along the Su-Uchkhan River to a fork (on the right is the approach to the waterfall, on the left is a cave) and turn left, where you need to walk another couple of hundred meters, then climb the bridge and climb up the steep steps to the ticket office, followed by the entrance to Kizil-Koba.

Fairy Valley

Camping (Kizil-Koba has such a camp), called the "Fairy Valley", operates all year round and is a fenced area around the Red Cave, equipped for relaxation and living in gazebos with a fireplace.

Here you can buy food and drinks, visit the tiny zoo, ride the rides, get souvenirs and just sit in a cafe with a cup of herbal tea. In the "Fairy Valley" there is an internal transport that reduces the travel time to the Red Cave. Outwardly, it resembles a children's engine.

Kizil-Koba Cave: how to get

You can get to the Red Cave both by public and by private transport. From Yalta and Alushta to the village of Perevalnoye (Angara), where Kizil-Koba extends, trolleybuses number 52 and 55 go along the Simferopol highway. True, they only reach a turn to the cave complex, after which tourists will have to walk for about 3 km.





From the capital of Crimea, the path is much shorter and starts from the railway station. Trolleybuses and a minibus leave from here. You need to go to the stop "Stadium" (landmark - gas station), there is a sign "Red Caves". In case of confusion, any local can suggest the way.

By car, you should move along the trolleybus route using the Alushta-Simferopol highway. The path will take less than an hour, and its length will be only 25 km.
