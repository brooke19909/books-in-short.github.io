Lilac from beads: a master class. DIY lilac from beads

In the hands of a creative person, any material becomes a work of art handmade. No exception in this regard and beads. From this material, you can create such things that from one glance at them breathtaking. This article offers a master class. DIY lilac made of beads is a simple creation, but a very beautiful creation.

The world of bead crafts

Beads can be used as a stand-alone material and weave, bonsai, embroidery can be made from it, and work from other materials can be finished with separate beads.

Crafts from beads can be a decorative decoration of a room, it also turns out beautiful jewelry.



Bead Tips

This type of needlework is much different from other hobbies. Before you do beadwork, you need to learn the following things.

  • This hobby requires a lot of free time, only then you can calculate that it will turn out a good thing.
  • The beads are very small, and the composition includes, as a rule, beads of several colors, which must be placed correctly, therefore, extreme attention is required.
  • Also need a tendency to perform small painstaking work.
  • The beauty of the work depends on the accuracy of execution.

When working with beads, it is necessary to organize the workplace well, which must meet the following requirements.

  • The table should be properly lit.
  • Remove foreign objects that interfere with work.
  • All tools must be at hand.
  • The material must be laid out in jars to make it more convenient to take.

If you are just starting to weave from beads, then you must strictly follow the instructions, in the future your experience will become an adviser when doing the work.

Options for lilac beads

Living lilacs are short-lived. A few days - and the former splendor as it never happened. If weave this plant from beads, then you can admire it all year round. The master class offers lilacs made of beads in completely different ways. You can start acquaintance with this fascinating activity by working on a small branch, then move on to more complex models and make a bouquet and even a whole bush.



To work, you need to master several basic techniques for making flowers. There are buds consisting of two, six and eight beads of the base color; flowers of five, ten, twenty-three beads; twigs consisting of five or fifteen flowers. At the top of the twigs buds are made, and below are the flowers. The stem of the bud and flower can be made with one or three beads or glass beads in the color of beads.

A simple model of lilac beads

Let's try to make lilacs from beads for beginners. We will prepare everything necessary for the work: beads of lilac, pink and green colors; bugles to match the primary colors; wire, tape, thread; scissors and nippers.

We measure a piece of wire 50 cm long (it is better if it is the same color with beads). We put 5 pink beads on it and make a loop, leaving the end of 10 cm free. The result is a petal. In the same way, on this wire we make three more petals, we connect both ends with bugles, and we get a lilac flower. Such flowers need to be made eight more pieces.

At the next stage, this workpiece is fastened with thick wire and wrapped with tape. It turns inflorescence, which must be made thirty pieces.

Now from the inflorescences we collect a sprig of lilac, arranging them in a checkerboard pattern.

We continue to weave lilac from beads. The scheme of execution of leaflets uses a circular French technique.



We collect more beads on the wire and twist it onto a skein. We twist a small piece of wire with the main 15 times, leaving a free end of 5 cm. We put on it 5 pieces of green beads and braid it with the main end of the wire. After the first revolution we make the second arc. After making 5-7 arcs, we cut off the main piece of wire, fix it and hide the wire in a bead so that it is not noticeable. We strengthen the leaf leg with wire and wrap it with tape. About twenty of these leaves need to be made. From the leaves we form a green twig.

At the last stage, we collect all the crafts. First, we fasten the parts with a thread, then with a green tape. Our lilac bead branch is ready.



Bonsai "Lilac"

You can complete this hack if you follow the step-by-step instructions that the master class offers. Lilac from beads turns out to be very original and unusual.

Take the source materials that were used in the previous craft, add to them some capacity for the stand, gypsum, PVA glue, and paint.

First, we produce individual branches of lilac with leaves and separately leaves. When performing colors, you can use another method that differs from that described above.

To do this, we collect 17 loops of 10-12 beads on the wire, then fold them in half and twist together. We take two such branches, combine them together, and we get a brush of lilacs. Carefully straighten all the loops, giving a beautiful shape. Such branches need to be done about forty pieces.

We continue our weaving with beads. Decorate the lilac with leaves. We prepare them by the method described above, it will take about seventy pieces.

Next, prepare the frame on the stand. To do this, we form the base of the tree from the wire, place the lower end in the container prepared in advance, fill it with liquid gypsum and wait until it hardens. Now we fasten our blanks to this frame. Each branch of lilac from beads forms a beautiful crown of a tree. Leaflets also decorate empty spots on the frame.

When the crown is ready, we cover the frame of the tree, together with the container on which it is fixed, with a mixture of gypsum with glue. Two layers can be applied to make the structure strong. Let us dry thoroughly and cover everything with brown paint.



That's the whole master class. Lilac from beads in the form of a small tree is unlikely to leave anyone indifferent. This method can be basic when working with this material. By turning on your fontasia, on its basis you can perform a variety of work.
