Difficulty choosing

To begin with, we will determine what a sports heart rate monitor is and why it is needed. Such devices are necessary to track the frequency of contraction of the heart muscle, as well as to fix the maximum load on the body. All this helps the athlete during training to properly respond to the data and make appropriate adjustments.

Each sport or physical activity has some kind of optimal heart rate limits. To achieve your goals and to avoid causing harm to your health, it is very important to monitor these limits and ensure that the pulse remains within the designated limits. A similar characteristic is called the target zone.

Target Zones

Such zones are usually calculated according to the Martti Carvonen method. The formula is very simple: MChS (maximum heart rate) = 220 - age. That is, for example, if you are 20 years old, then the critical parameter for you will be 200 reduction / min. (220 - 20), and if 40 years old, then, respectively, - 180 (220 - 40).

Almost all sports heart rate monitors work in real time and notify the user of critical zones. Options are customizable based on your age and specific exercises.

Target areas (% of MES):

  • 50-60% - light loads: walking at a fast pace, warming up, etc.
  • 70-80% - it is considered a fitness zone, comparable to jogging and climbing stairs;
  • 80-90% - sports dancing, aerobics: burning not only fats, but also carbohydrates;
  • 90-95% - skating, skiing, biking and similar active sports;
  • more than 95% - area only for professional athletes: maximum and dangerous load.

It is for the determination of such zones that a pulse sensor is needed. With him, training is much more effective, and most importantly - safer for the athlete.

Types of heart rate monitors

Today, there are two types of heart rate sensors - remote and built-in. In the first case, we have a device that receives information about heart rate and sends it to another gadget for further processing. It can be a smartphone, bracelet or personal computer.

Types of devices with an external sensor:

  • under the earlobe;
  • on the finger;
  • earphone;
  • on the forearm;
  • breastplate.

The most common and most effective option is a chest pulse sensor. Regardless of the environment, it allows you to get the most accurate results. And if earlier such decisions were complained of by a constantly slipping strap, today designers use special materials that can reliably hold the sensor even with heavy sweating.

Sensor Features

A good half of heart rate sensors transmit analog and / or digital signals to the hand, wrist, forearm, or chest. This allows them to work in tandem with more familiar gadgets like smartphones or smart watches. As a wireless protocol, either bluetooth or ANT + technology is used. The latter is more economical and supports a much wider range of mobile and specific devices.

Devices with a built-in heart rate monitor themselves measure heart rate and process the data themselves. For active and frequent training, this solution is not the best option. Such devices are more suitable for single measurements, although there are exceptions. This group includes special rings and fitness bracelets.
