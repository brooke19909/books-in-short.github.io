Already as a gadget, watches find new uses for themselves.

Initially, the watch was designed to determine the time. Gradually, from the huge mechanisms that were located in the tall towers, they moved into houses, and even turned into stylish jewelry through the efforts of watchmakers. A little time passed and as a necessary accessory or gadget for a business person, the clock migrated to the wrists of modern people. Even in the mechanical version, the watch was already able not only to determine the time, but also worked as a calendar, stopwatch, etc. When the mechanical insides of the gadget watch were replaced with electronic stuffing, their capabilities expanded to infinity.

Integrated circuits allowed a variety of devices to be mounted in a small watch case. Alarms and flashlights, heart rate monitors and small radios in various combinations complemented the main functionality of wrist gadgets. With the advent of mobile phones, watches began to lose their functional properties and again began to turn into a stylish and expensive jewelry for both women and men. Through the efforts of designers and watch developers, they have retained their presence in the market and continue to exist both as a stylish classic expensive jewelry on the one hand and as an inexpensive multi - functional device on the other.

As an ornament located on the user's wrist, the watch is a product of world-famous companies; the quality of assembly and the brand mark also affect the price. If the brand name does not shine with historical fame, then the watch-accessory not only turns into a masterpiece of jewelry craftsmanship, but also takes the cost of the material used.
