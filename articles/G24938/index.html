A brief excursion into history

Intense business life and brisk trade are boiling on this northern outskirts of Moscow. There are many modern residential complexes and administrative structures. But not everyone knows that the history of this route is no more than a hundred years old. For a sufficiently ancient city like Moscow, this is not very characteristic. But the highway, known to us today as Altufevskoe highway, was built only in 1914. Just before the start of World War I. By a strange historical coincidence, the existence of this road was owed to Moscow by the German industrialists Borau, whose clan was one of the ten richest families of the Russian Empire. They were interested in the road, because they financed its construction. And its modern name Altufevskoe highway received in honor of the settlement through which it passed. Already in the sixties of the twentieth century, the village of Altufevo became part of Moscow.
