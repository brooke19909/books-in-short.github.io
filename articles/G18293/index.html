In the city you can find places that deserve no less attention and are unique, inimitable and attractive, especially for young people. Cinema

In the Soviet period, the building, a cinema in which in 1935 became known as "Artistic", was reconstructed twice. And today it can simultaneously accept up to 800 visitors in its seven halls.

Lion Bridge

We will continue our acquaintance with the most interesting places of St. Petersburg for young people. Their list includes a suspension bridge built in 1825-1826. according to a project developed by engineer G. Tretter. He is called Leo because of the four cast-iron figures of these animals located here, made by the sculptor P.P. Sokolov. Tourists visit this bridge as often as the famous Bankovsky and Anichkov.

The construction is quite interesting. It attracts the attention of residents and visitors of the city with its cast-iron fence, which has a rather original pattern. In addition, the design of the bridge has certain technical difficulties. So, being suspended, the structure rests on chains that are held with the help of mechanisms inside lions. Its length is a little over 27 m and its width is 2.5 m.

In 1954, the bridge was subject to reconstruction. Lanterns were installed near him, the support mechanisms were repaired, and the sculptures were painted in the color of marble. Snow lions become especially expressive at night. With the advent of darkness, their silhouettes are especially clearly reflected in the waters of the canal.

Even the tourists who are not particularly interested in classical architecture are worth seeing the ‚ÄúLion Bridge‚Äù. After all, this building is remarkable not only for the beauty of its appearance, but also for the legends associated with the history of its existence. So, this miracle of St. Petersburg architecture is considered a very romantic place, and has long been chosen by the residents of the city for dates. It is worth noting that this status has been preserved at the bridge to this day.

There is also an urban legend according to which a pregnant woman who approached the crossing could determine the sex of the child. If the cavalier was the first to cross the bridge, then she must have a boy, and if the young lady is a girl. Also, being near one of the four lions, you should certainly touch his paws. According to the citizens, this will fulfill the most cherished desire. All students of the Northern capital know where the Lion Bridge in St. Petersburg is located. They come to his sculptures before passing a difficult exam.

Where is the Lion Bridge in St. Petersburg on a city map? This is the Admiralty district of the Northern capital. The bridge is a pedestrian, it is thrown over the waters of the Griboedov Canal and connects the Spassky and Kazan islands. Not far from it are the metro stations Spasskaya, Sennaya Ploshchad and Sadovaya.

Museums

What other interesting places of St. Petersburg for youth exist? Travelers interested in unusual areas of fine art are advised to go to the eastern part of the Northern capital, where there are workshops of existing production. They were visited by many famous artists of our time, leaving their creations here. We are talking about the Museum of Street Art in St. Petersburg, opened in 2012. It includes rather unusual exhibition galleries located in the preserved part of the industrial zone. It is impossible to find classic artifacts on display or under glass. After all, everything that is presented in this museum is in the open air and has enormous dimensions. The height of some exhibits can be compared even with the whole house.

The territory of the Museum of Street Art in St. Petersburg has two zones. On one of them is a permanent exhibition. This is the site of the existing production of the plant, which produces laminated plastics. Here you can see the annually replenished collection of monumental murals made by street artists. The second zone is a public area. On its territory, guests are offered temporary exhibitions and various events.

Visitors to this museum in St. Petersburg, which is one of a kind, are satisfied with their pastime. And this is confirmed by their numerous reviews. Monument to the photographer

Where else can young tourists go to St. Petersburg? It is worth noting that the inhabitants of the Northern capital are confident that it was in their city that the photo essay and other types of this art originated. It is believed that the founder of the direction is the German Karl Bulla. When the boy was 10 years old, his family moved to live in St. Petersburg. For twenty years this gifted person managed to go through a difficult but successful path. He started as an errand boy, and after he was able to open his own photo shop and become the head of a company selling accessories for filming. Among Bulla's clients were the most august persons, writers and scholars, artists and politicians. Many of the buildings and sights of St. Petersburg are preserved only in those photographs that were taken by this businessman and his sons.

A monument to a St. Petersburg photographer, made of bronze, appeared on Malaya Sadovaya near house No. 3 in January 2001. It is a sculpture of a man with a mustache, who, holding an umbrella over his head, stands near a high tripod with an old camera, ready to go. At the feet of the owner is an English bulldog. The place for this monument was not chosen by chance. The fact is that it was in this house for more than 30 years that Karl Bulla‚Äôs photo studio was located. Today there is a photo studio. Circus

What objects of St. Petersburg are most popular among young people? One of those on their list is the circus, which can be found on the Fontanka. This is one of the oldest institutions of its kind in Russia. It opened in 1877.

The St. Petersburg Circus on Fontanka, whose poster is very interesting and diverse, is a popular place to visit not only for visitors to the city, but also for its residents. In addition, there is an opportunity to visit the museum, which was opened in 1928. Its exhibits tell about the history of circus art. Summer garden

Many young tourists seek to get to the park created by the tireless Peter the Great. According to one description of the Summer Garden in St. Petersburg, it becomes clear that you must certainly take time to visit it.

The most accessible entertainments here are walking along the paths of the park and exploring the Petrovsky Palace. Also, tourists like to be photographed against the backdrop of beautiful sculptures and fountains, relaxing in the Coffee House with a cup of aromatic invigorating drink.

The landscape of this place corresponds to the style of the French park. These are symmetrical alleys, which, together with plant, neatly trimmed plantings, represent entire labyrinths from the paths. Walking along the paths, guests of the park can admire the graceful sculptures standing along them. Peter the Great himself brought them specially for the Summer Garden from Italy. These elegant statues are nothing but restored copies of ancient compositions. In addition to statues, guests of the park can admire the tall trees growing here.

On the territory of the Summer Garden there are several fountains with a beautiful design. There are also figured lawns, they are decorated with intricate flowerpots. Judging by the reviews of tourists, a pleasant impression of the park is left by benches, arbors and a picturesque pond. With interest, guests of the garden consider the greenhouse and the house located on the territory.

A rectangular two-story building with a high roof also attracts the attention of travelers. This is a small-sized Summer Palace belonging to Peter the Great. To date, the building is presented in its original form. Its facade is decorated with decorative stucco and 29 images in the form of bas-reliefs.
