The foundation of Moscow and its early history

Unfortunately, unfortunately, the early history of Moscow has been lost for us . In this regard, no one was able to give a clear explanation to what event or circumstance the foundation of Moscow is connected with. There is also no answer for what reason the capital was built on this place, and not in any other place of Great Russia.



In addition, the year of foundation of Moscow is reliably unknown. Presumably according to the Pantheon of Russian Sovereigns, Moscow was founded in 880, at the end of the ninth century. According to historical data, it was in this year that Oleg, who was not yet the guardian of Igor‚Äôs throne, but was only Prince of Urmansky, came to the Moscow River, then called Smorodina or Samorodinka. Here, at the mouth of the Neglinnaya River, he laid the town, which was named after the river - Moscow. After that, for more than two and a half centuries, or rather 267 years, nothing was known about Moscow.

It was first mentioned on the pages of the Ipatiev Chronicle in 1147. This year, a meeting of Suzdal Prince Yuri Dolgoruky with his ally, Novgorod-Seversky Prince Svyatoslav Olgovich, took place here. It is worth noting that for a long time the foundation of Moscow was attributed to this particular year. Then Moscow was a small settlement owned by a rich and famous man, Stepan Ivanovich Kuchko, and it was called Kuchkov.



In historical documents, it was noted that the house of Stepan Kuchko at that time was located near modern Chistye Prudy, and in the place of the Kremlin an impenetrable dense forest was noisy. In total, at that time there were six Kuchkovo villages: Vorobyevo, Vysotsky, Kudrino, Kulishki, Simonovo and Suschevo. There is an assumption that Stepan Kuchko was from Novgorod, as his name is quite common in connection with the mention of Novgorod zemstvo.
