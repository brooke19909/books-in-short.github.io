Making origami from a napkin on a table

Do you know that the origami method can be applied to table layout? For example, fold a piece of cloth or piece of paper into a beautiful figure and place it on a plate. We suggest that you familiarize yourself with how to make origami from napkins on the table (photo attached).

Simple flower





  1. Open the paper towel fully (Figure 1).
  2. Fold all four corners toward the center (Figure 2).
  3. Repeat the previous step again (Figure 3).
  4. And bend the corners to the middle again (Figure 4).
  5. Turn the square upside down (Figure 5).
  6. Now fold the four vertices towards the middle on this side (Figure 6).
  7. While holding the corner, pull the tucked part (Figure 7).
  8. Now lift this part to make a petal (Figure 8).
  9. Repeat steps 7 and 8 with the remaining corners to make four petals (Figure 9).
  10. Lift the wrapped triangles up (Figure 10).
  11. You will receive four more petals (Figure 11).
  12. You can leave it this way, or you can still pull out the triangles to form leaves (Figure 12).

A simple origami from a napkin to the table is ready!

Beautiful butterfly





We present to your attention another simple way to make origami from a napkin to a table:

  1. Fold the napkin in half to make a triangle (picture 1).
  2. Tuck the top of the triangle (picture 2).
  3. Now wrap the right side in half (picture 3).
  4. Do the same with the left side (picture 4).
  5. Turn the half of the figure in the opposite direction to make a triangle (picture 5).
  6. Fold the shape to make a boat, as in picture 6.
  7. Pull out the bottom of the figure (picture 7).
  8. Turn the part over (picture 8).

You turned out a cute butterfly, which would be appropriate to decorate the plate both at the children's party and at the adult.

Peacock made of napkins





Such wonderful origami crafts from napkins on the table are simple (photo above), but at the same time they look pretty nice. Operating procedure:

  1. Take one napkin and wrap the right and left corners in the middle (Figure 1).
  2. Then bend the left and right part of the napkin again to the center (Figure 2).
  3. Bend the parts to the middle a third time (Figure 3).
  4. Fold in half the figure that turned out (Figure 4).
  5. Unscrew the edge of the upper triangle and raise one half, as in figure 5.
  6. Now do the tail. To do this, take a long rectangle and fold it with an accordion (Figures 6 and 7).
  7. Fold the accordion and fold the part in half, as in figures 8 and 9.
  8. Fold the edges and straighten the tail (Figure 10).

It remains to connect the tail with the bird and the craft is ready!

Cutlery Case





Since we are talking about table setting, we need to take care not only about the decoration of the plate. Cutlery can also be beautifully decorated. To do this, you can make a case-origami for them from a napkin to the table.

Workshop on folding decorative crafts:

  1. Take a cloth napkin and fold it in half (Figure 1).
  2. Now fold it in half again (Figure 2).
  3. Fold one edge so that it folds in half (Figure 3).
  4. Now turn the second corner under the first one, just so that it peeks out a bit (Figure 4).
  5. Tuck one more corner (Figure 5).
  6. Now fold the part in half with the folds up, as shown in Figure 6.
  7. You got a cutlery case (Figure 7). Put a knife in the farthest pocket, a fork in the second, and a dessert or tablespoon in the third (Figure 8).

This craft can be laid out both next to the plate and on top of it.

Unusual flower





At first glance, it may seem that such an origami from napkins to the table is made quite difficult. But actually it is not. Work does not take much effort and time. The main thing is to carry it out carefully and then the first time in a few minutes you can get a craft that can surprise your guests.

Operating procedure:

  1. Take a beautiful paper towel and, without unrolling, fold it in half to make a triangle (Figure 1).
  2. Wrap the right and left corners to the center line (Figure 2).
  3. Turn the part upside down.
  4. Fold up the peeling edges (Figure 3).
  5. Turn the part back over and spread it in different directions (Figure 4).
  6. You have the first part ready. To assemble a large craft, you will need many similar modules. Make blanks. You will also need paper clips (Figure 5).
  7. Fold each figure into several piles (Figure 6).
  8. Take two modules and connect one end to the third. Substitute one more detail and put on the fifth from above. Thus, assemble a circle in which the last module connects to the last. You have two rows ready. Continue to collect the flower in the same way (Figure 7). If the craft somewhere falls apart, carefully use paper clips.
  9. At the end, straighten the ends and assemble the figure a little so that it has a cone shape (Figure 8).

Such a magic origami craft from a napkin on a table can be placed in the central part as an ornament.
