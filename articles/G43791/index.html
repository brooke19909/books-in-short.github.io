How to make do-it-yourself hyacinth from corrugated paper

One of the most popular hobbies is the production of floristic compositions for interior decoration, clothing items, and gift wrapping. We buy materials

It will help to make hyacinth from corrugated paper with your own hands, a master class placed below, and what you need for work, read the following list:

  • corrugated or crepe paper;
  • floristic wire and ribbon;
  • skewers for barbecue, knitting needles, sticks or frame in the shape of a cone or cylinder;
  • glue;
  • scissors.

You can buy everything you need in a specialized gift wrapping store or departments for needlework and creativity.

Do-it-yourself hyacinth from corrugated paper: a master class

There are two ways to make this flower: from one strip or several. The first option is done faster, so you can start with it.





The sequence of work will be as follows:

  1. Cut a strip of 10 cm wide and about 30 long into corrugated paper.
  2. On one of the long edges, make cuts about half or slightly wider.
  3. Take a kebab skewer, knitting needle or other stick. Wind each small strip on it to make curls.
  4. When the workpiece is finished, you need to wind it in a spiral from top to bottom on a skewer or wire. Fix the end of the strip with glue. If the base is used in the form of a cone or cylinder, it is better to glue along the entire length.
  5. Wrap a stalk in a green floral ribbon.
  6. Cut leaves from corrugated paper, attach them to the stem with glue.
  7. Make the required number of identical or multi-colored hyacinths.

All is ready. You can put it in a vase. If you want to make a colorful flower, in the first step you make two strips of paper in different shades. You will need to wind them on a stalk together.





DIY multicolor corrugated paper hyacinth

This method takes more time, since the flower is made not from one strip, as in the previous method, but from a large number of them. However, it is possible to make hyacinth not monophonic, but with shades, for example, on top it will be light pink, in the middle it will be bright, and below it will be dark. Original look products in which stripes of different colors alternate. The most important thing is to combine tones modeled on natural ones so that excessive variegation and unnaturalness do not happen.





So, to make a multi-color hyacinth from corrugated paper with your own hands (photo above), follow these steps:

  1. Cut from corrugated paper many small strips, a few centimeters wide and about ten in length.
  2. Treat them, as in the first method, by cutting along one of the long sides into small strips. They can be twisted or left straight.
  3. Winding and securing the workpiece from the top to the bottom of the skewer.
  4. Wrap the remaining stalk with floral tape.
  5. Cut the leaves and attach them to the frame.
  6. Do the right amount of flowers.

Put in a vase or use for the manufacture of panels for the wall or other interior decorations.

Another way that hyacinth can be made with a gradual transition of shades involves collecting inflorescences from individual elements. They are made not of strips, but of rectangles with notches, which are folded in the form of "bells".





It turns out quite naturally, but it takes a lot of time.

Bouquet design

You have already read how to make hyacinth from corrugated paper with your own hands, but, in addition, you can collect flowers in an original way in a composition. Use these ideas:

  1. Tie the elements into a bouquet, wrapping with spectacular paper, floral mesh.
  2. Put in a vase, complement the decor of butterflies, bows, ribbons.
  3. Take a basket or a beautiful box, glue a piece of polystyrene inside, stick skewers with hyacinths into it, add decorative twigs, leaves, or you can even use sweets or soft toys.

So you can prepare a great gift for any holiday.

As you can see, making hyacinth from corrugated paper with your own hands will be easy and fast. Using different shades of material, you will create spectacular bouquets and floral arrangements in baskets, pots or containers.
