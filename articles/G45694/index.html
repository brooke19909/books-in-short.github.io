How far is Brazil from us?

Rio de Janeiro is a metropolis to which direct flights do not fly from any city in Russia. Therefore, everyone who wants to relax there will have to make at least one transplant, or even two. A more economical option is to stop in Sao Paulo, from where planes depart for domestic flights to Rio every few hours. Such a trip will take you just an extra hour of time, and finance will cost no more than $ 70. You can also fly through Miami, but in this case you will have to settle many nuances associated with visas and other documents, because you will also cross the US border. And, of course, America as a state will require a much higher fee for flight services than Brazil. Rio de Janeiro, paradoxically, is a city where you can relax cheaply, but in order to get to it, you need to pay a considerable amount.



Visa ... is it necessary for a trip to Rio?

Since recently, Russian tourists traveling abroad have become much more accessible than in the past decades, the visa issue is now much more acute. Climate that allows sunbathing year-round

The weather in Rio de Janeiro is a real find for lovers of summer, which never ends. It is always sunny and hot, both in winter and in summer, especially since there is not much difference between these seasons. Since the metropolis is located in the southern hemisphere of our planet, winter here always has higher temperature indicators, and summer pleases locals with cool evenings and a fresh ocean breeze. The hottest month in Rio is January. The temperature rises above day 30, the oceanic bay warms up to 26-27 degrees. A gradual decline in such heat begins in April-May, and in summer the daytime temperature does not exceed 27 Celsius. Also in summer, the wind rises here, which forms waves ideal for windsurfing. That is why the second capital of Brazil - Rio de Janeiro - is a resort city in which you can perfectly relax at any time of the year.



What if you just arrived in Rio?

The first thing that every tourist who finds himself in an unfamiliar city is supposed to do is get to the hotel. To do this, you can use public transport, namely - by bus, because here they are a dime a dozen. In order to get into the salon, you just need to wave your hand, and the driver will stop, and he will drop you off where you ask. Public transport in the metropolis is very comfortable, and the fare is worth nothing. Buses run in almost all areas of the city, including the narrowest streets, and, as a rule, they do not have to wait long. Also, the resort capital of Brazil, Rio de Janeiro is full of private taxis. Quickly and with a breeze, the driver will take you to the hotel and will be extremely grateful if you leave him a tip. As in a restaurant, subtract ten percent of the amount on the counter and give it to the driver.

Hotel centers of the resort capital of Brazil

A separate topic for all travelers are the hotels of Rio de Janeiro. Brazil, in principle, is an inexpensive country in which you do not have to leave a fortune just to have a human rest. However, in Rio, the cost of temporary residence is fabulously overpriced. The best hotel complexes are erected along the most famous coast of Copacabana, where it is always fun and noisy. Living in such an area, you will be aware of all the events, do not miss a single party and you can make a bunch of new acquaintances. A quieter, but no less expensive and chic place to relax is the Ipanema coast. Here, citizens of the United States, Europe, some Russians prefer to relax. It is also worth remembering that in the winter months, prices in all hotels increase several times in connection with the New Year and the Carnival.



Local cuisine and its features

Special culinary traditions boasts the metropolis of Rio de Janeiro (Brazil). Photos of many culinary masterpieces repeatedly flickered in front of us on blue screens, but their recipes and even taste remained a big secret for us. Going to the east coast in the South Atlantic, you can finally taste all this, and perhaps learn how to cook it yourself. Since slaves from Africa have been exiled to Brazil over the centuries, the state has become a stronghold of Arab and European traditions, supplemented by the customs of local Indians. Therefore, the "name" dish of Rio is feijoada, which consists of beans, smoked meats, spices and many other components. You can taste this miracle in any restaurant in the city, and everywhere it will have its own special, different flavor.



Other gastronomic benefits of Rio

In addition to the unique cuisine, which has incorporated a synthesis of the traditions of the East, Europe and the wild tribes of the Amazon, local drinks also deserve special applause. The resort and very warm Brazilian capital of Rio de Janeiro is a storehouse of various wines, both dry, which will be an excellent addition to a meat dinner, and sweet, nutmeg, which will continue your enjoyment of any dessert. This drink is served here in restaurants, and can also be purchased at local stores. An important place in Brazilian culture is coffee, which is simply masterly brewed here. Even in the beach cafe, where everyone comes, you will cook these fragrant grains so that you never forget their aroma. Do not forget also that in Brazil they still prepare first-class beer. Locals prefer the brand Brama, a drink that is moderately frothy and very pleasant to taste. Well, the "alcoholic symbol" of Rio and the whole of Brazil is Cachasa. This is a high-speed drink that is comparable to Mexican tequila.

Attractions of the resort capital

Explore and comprehend the secrets of this city in a few days you definitely will not succeed. There is almost everything here: museums, and ancient squares, and buildings in the colonial style, and places for outdoor activities. If you decide to walk all of Rio de Janeiro, a map is the first thing you need. You can buy it at any kiosk or store, both in Portuguese and in English. As a rule, hiking starts with the noisy beaches of Copacabana, where there are cafes, discotheques, shops, and even botanical zones. At night, a market opens here, where you can buy everything from spices to jewelry. In the old part of the city you will find colonial buildings adjoined by sculptural masterpieces telling the history of the city and the country. On the outskirts of the city there are favelas - slums in which poor people live and mainly the Rio mafia. New Year and Carnival - the main events of the country

Almost every inhabitant of the planet knows very well what the city of Rio de Janeiro (Brazil) looks like on New Year's Eve. The carnival, which takes place here, gathers not only all the inhabitants of the metropolis on the streets, but also tourists from all over the world. They come here in advance in order to at least look out of the corner of their eyes at the most colorful and noisy action that has ever existed in the world. According to the rules, this holiday should be held 40 days before Easter, in honor of the beginning of Lent. It is, as it were, a symbol of farewell to carnal pleasures, the last day when you can eat meat and perform incendiary dances. However, the Carnival in Rio de Janeiro always coincides with the date of the New Year, which allows you to combine two such significant holidays. In this city, this celebration is the day of the national samba dance, and during it the best choreographic achievements of the year, as well as the most unique and colorful costumes, are demonstrated.

What exactly happens on this holiday?

In fact, preparations for the celebration begin a few days before it begins. The jury, which includes not only local choreographers, but also foreigners, selects dance groups that demonstrate the best skills in the art of performing samba. The chosen ones get to the final, that is, to the Carnival in Rio de Janeiro, where the one and only winner is also determined. Nevertheless, all those dancers who qualify for this finale are, in fact, considered the best, as we can say that they represent their country in the international arena. Every year, for everyone who takes part in this action, costumes are made that are used only once. After the Carnival, decorations and clothes are simply thrown into a landfill, and the city is immersed in its daily affairs and worries, and the dancers will increase their strength to show the best results a year later.
