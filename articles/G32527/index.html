Human resources management is one of the most complex regulatory elements in an organization. After all, employees have their own potential, their own interests, emotions, the ability to make decisions independently or criticize the orders of management. Therefore, it is impossible to predict the reaction to the application of managerial decisions.





For the existence of the organization to be long and the goals set for it to be achieved, it is necessary to create the right management system.

A system is the ordering of all components and their combination into a single whole to achieve a common goal. In other words, it is orderliness and subordination to the main task.

Management includes functions: planning, motivation, organization and control. With their help, the achievement of tasks is achieved.

Management systems are ordered processes of planning, organization, motivation, control. They are aimed at fulfilling the tasks of production and achieving the main goal of the existence of the organization.

Consider them. The managed group includes elements that are involved in the process of creating material and spiritual goods or providing services. These are subordinates. The management group carries out all the functions necessary to fulfill the tasks assigned to the organization, for this it must have the necessary resources: material, labor, financial. Types of leadership

Coordination of work in an organization can take place in four forms:

  • Linear when each subordinate leader is subordinate to a superior. Their actions are coordinated and aimed at fulfilling specific goals. More commonly used for lower levels of organization.
  • Functional. There are several groups of governing bodies, with each responsible for a specific type of activity. For example, one - for planning, the other - for the technical base. However, there are difficulties when several different tasks ‚Äúdescend‚Äù to employees and require their quick completion. The ideal option for the existence of such a system is in combination with a linear one.
  • Line staff. When line managers are created headquarters. Moreover, they do not make any decisions, but only advise, direct employees. They are designed to reduce and distribute line manager responsibilities.
  • Dot matrix. Management occurs both horizontally and vertically. Such structures are used for management at construction sites, where each complex is divided into nodes that have their own leader.

An example of an enterprise coordination structure

Consider an example of a workshop control system in production.

The workshop is one of the main links responsible for the functioning of the entire production. To achieve the goals of the organization, it is necessary to create the right management system.





The director appoints a chief and his deputies to the workshop, who should organize the implementation of tasks received from the senior manager. At the same time, the shop manager must himself control the attitude of personnel to production resources. A variant is possible when this function is entrusted to a specially appointed employee. So, for example, the consumption of raw materials, compliance with safety rules and sanitary maintenance of the workshop are controlled.

The coordination structure includes the presence of team leaders who receive tasks from the master and distribute it among the workers. They also organize their implementation, provide professional assistance, and if necessary, help the master to exercise control.

Modern enterprise management

In the current environment, the coordination of staff requires special skill from the leader. An unstable economic situation and competition lead to this. Therefore, creating modern management systems, the manager must know the principles of their construction.

For an enterprise to function and develop, its products must be competitive. It largely depends on which management strategy will be chosen. For the enterprise, it must be unique - this is the main sign of a successful existence.





For a company to exist for a long time and make a profit, products must withstand competition. To improve the quality you need:

  • Have the necessary resources: raw materials, components.
  • Improve production lines: update equipment, seeking to improve the quality of products.
  • Periodically improve staff skills.
  • To realize products.

The first thing a professional manager should start with is to develop management systems, analyze the enterprise, consider what elements are missing to achieve the goal, and figure out how to achieve them. When developing a development strategy, it is necessary to take into account:

  • long-term goals of enterprise development;
  • resources;
  • technology;
  • management system.

That is, in order to achieve its goals, the enterprise must have all the necessary resources, high-quality technologies that will process these resources, and a well-built management system.

Moreover, the strategy should not be monolithic, but be able to modify depending on external and internal factors. And the task of the management system is to control and timely amend the strategic goals of the organization.

Thus, the effective management of a modern enterprise should be mobile and dependent on environmental factors.

Types of control systems

Management systems - these are areas of management activity that are associated with solving specific problems, aimed at the successful functioning of the enterprise.

There are two main categories:

  • general - management of the company as a whole;
  • functional - management of certain parts of the company.

The management system is a comprehensive collaboration of a common and functional kind to achieve the goals.





There are several forms of control systems, consider some of them:

  • strategic planning;
  • management: company managers, employees, internal and external communications, production;
  • counseling.

With these types of leadership, the company first sets strategic goals, to achieve which you need to be able to coordinate the work of managers. This allows for improved management structure. Coordination of the work of employees allows us to direct their activities in the right direction. At the same time, the company interacts with the external environment: suppliers, customers, and employees.

Types of control systems are also determined by control objects and vary in content. For example, the content can be distinguished as follows:

  • normative;
  • strategic;
  • operational.

Each of these types of management solves only its characteristic tasks.

The coordination system must combine all the positive aspects with which the organization will be easier to develop. Then the strategic goal will be achieved.





The design of management systems takes into account democratic centralism, a harmonious combination of unity of command and collegiality, responsibility, and the creative potential of employees.

Guidelines

The creation of management systems should be based on the following basic principles:

  • optimal separation of the organization structure into separate elements;
  • hierarchical structure with the correct distribution of powers;
  • organic interconnection of all levels of the organization;
  • Arranging goals in order of importance
  • consistency of structure links in the performance of tasks;
  • Efficiency of making managerial decisions, if such a need arises;
  • all stages of product life cycles, hierarchical structure, various management activities must exist in a complex;
  • systematic - all management work is carried out continuously and lasts a long time;
  • you need to learn from the experience of successful production of foreign companies;
  • should use proven scientific methods in the management system;
  • autonomy of subsystems;
  • economic functions - when designing, lay down the reduction of management costs;
  • development prospects;
  • discussion of management decisions and choosing the best;
  • stability and ability to survive in a competitive environment;
  • create comfortable working conditions so that employees can give all their best at work;
  • correctly allocate labor costs to perform specific production tasks;
  • the adaptability of the organization system to external and internal changes;
  • closed management process.

The implementation of the decision must go through all stages: planning, organization, coordination, control.

Important: the management decision must be clear and intelligible, it is necessary to verify that the employee understood it correctly. This will save the employee from unnecessary movements and direct all his potential to perform a specific task.

Manager Functions

To make the implementation of management systems easy and accepted by employees, the manager must perform the following basic functions:

  • Planning.

The manager continuously plans the solutions that are necessary to achieve the main goal of the enterprise. When changing the goal, decisions must also be changed in a timely manner. Planning guides the development of the organization in the right direction and predicts the tasks that employees need to complete.

  • Organization.

To better achieve the goals set for the company, plans, the team works in an organized manner, while it is correctly distributed vertically and horizontally. Each one deals specifically with its tasks, collaborating with other employees.

  • Motivation.

Managers use motivation to encourage workers to better fulfill their responsibilities. It can be of two types: external and internal (psychological).

External - includes the receipt of material goods: bonuses, bonuses, and psychological - moral encouragement, improving the workplace, relationships in the team.

  • The control.

In order for the tasks to be carried out efficiently, the immediate supervisor must observe.

Control includes:

    • tracking planned;
    • verification of intermediate results;
    • comparing the results with the planned;
    • correction of detected discrepancies and deviations from the predicted ones.

The action of these four functions should be carried out in a complex.

Conclusion

Thus, the management system is the orderliness of the basic principles of building, functioning and monitoring the development of the organization. This is done in order to fulfill the tasks assigned to the company. Design and implementation of control systems are of great importance in the successful development of the enterprise. Without a properly built leadership, the existence and development of the enterprise would be impossible.
