The desire of smartphone manufacturers to make their products more attractive to customers has led to the fact that it is now quite difficult to find a device that does not have a built-in digital camera. Dozens of megapixels, complex image processing algorithms, automatic range adjustment ... It would seem that it is enough to select the desired frame and press the button, and the rest will be done by automation.

Unfortunately, this is only partially true. So, for example, an attempt to shoot a building against a bright blue sky will lead to a general excessive dimming, since in this case the brightest element is selected for the reference point, relative to which the remaining parameters are set. If you interfere with the operation of the algorithms and manually specify the exposure, the result may be a building with an acceptable brightness in the picture, but with a white spot instead of the sky. To overcome this, a special HDR mode is used, implemented in almost every modern digital camera, including models in smartphones. By learning to work with him, you can be sure that the quality of the pictures will be perfect. This abbreviation stands for dynamic range extension. What is HDR in the phone‚Äôs camera?

In fact, the principle of operation of this mode is quite simple. HDR shooting assumes that the camera does not take one, but several frames at a time, while focusing on objects with different levels of illumination in the background .

Then the central processor selects pictures with averaged values ‚Äã‚Äãand combines them into one, which is offered to the user. Thanks to this simple method, you can forget about objects that are too bright and not bright enough in one frame - everything is in balance. Since this solution is completely software, for its use it is enough just to install an application that has a similar shooting mode. Note that not all cameras preinstalled in the basic firmware of smartphones have this feature.

The nuances of using

At the same time, it is important to understand that the HDR mode is still not a panacea. Even using it, the owner does not become a professional photographer. The main problem is this: since the final image is formed from several intermediate ones, the apparatus itself and the objects in the frame should be motionless.

Otherwise, an unpleasant HDR effect may occur, in which everything in the picture looks blurry, doubles, etc. When using this mode, it is recommended to use a tripod.

The next feature that should be taken into account is that in some cases, obtaining a picture with an average brightness is inappropriate. For example, the silhouettes in the twilight, according to the photographer, should remain the same vague shadows, and not people in gray cloaks. HDR shooting does not allow to realize this.

And finally, the brightness and contrast of pictures taken in this mode, in general, are slightly lower than in photographs taken in the usual way. Sometimes this is very critical.
