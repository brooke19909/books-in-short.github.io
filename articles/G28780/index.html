Architect Uborevich-Borovsky: features of his work

Architect Uborevich-Borovsky is a Russian designer working in the style of minimalism. During his career, this talented master has implemented more than 40 major projects.

Architect Biography

The architect Boris Uborevich-Borovsky was born in 1959 in a family of repressed. His maternal grandfather was a hero of the Civil War and a famous military leader, and his brother Vladimir is also an architect and cartoonist.



Architect Uborevich-Borovsky in 1982 graduated from the Moscow Architectural Institute, then worked at Mosproject OJSC. In 1993, he founded his own architectural studio and, together with other architects, took the initiative to restore the Moscow Architectural Society. In 2010, he released his first book.

Professional activity

Uborevich-Borovsky (architect) is a modern figure in design art who helps to create special cosiness and make housing more cozy and comfortable.

In his youth, he graduated from the Moscow Architectural Institute, where he acquired all the required design knowledge and skills. The subsequent activities of the master were closely connected with the design institutes of Moscow. 10 years after graduating from the academy, the young architect Uborevich-Borovsky established his own workshop. The studio is mainly focused on the design of country houses in the suburbs and the design of apartments in Moscow.



Today, Boris Olegovich is not only the director of his own workshop, but also concurrently works at Mosproject. He specializes in projects of various urban complexes, public and residential buildings.

The principle of work and features of the master

The portfolio of the architect Uborevich-Borovsky clearly shows that he is a person who strives to move forward all the time. He likes to study space and is looking for a combination of colors.



The master himself prefers to work in a minimalist style, although he sometimes uses other styles for interior design. The uniqueness of his work is associated with tremendous experience, as the master has been working in the field of interior design for a long time.

When arranging the interior, the architect takes into account the proportions of the room, form, color combination, and only after that thinks through and develops the design.

Architect's look at work

The architect Boris Uborevich-Borovsky believes that you need to create the interior in such a way that it is most comfortable for human life. In his works, he tries to look at the arrangement of living space as a designer, to work with the plane and volume.



Distinctive features of his projects are considered laconicism, asceticism and monochrome. He is convinced that the living space should be only a background and emphasize the person who lives in it. However, despite the fact that the arrangement of the living space is only a background, it should be as functional, decent and convenient as possible. The main task of the architect is that he must understand the customer and imbue his interests.

The designer pays a lot of attention to space and its formation. In this case, it is necessary to create a single space of the entire apartment. He tries to remove all the doors and dull walls to make the interior easier and more spacious.

Master about his work

The basic principle of the work of the architect Uborevich-Borovsky is very simple, but at the same time quite convincing. The team of craftsmen works in the principle of minimalism, trying to strictly follow the basic architectural rules.

Corporate identity is always guessed almost unmistakably, as it is characterized by:

  • concise design;
  • graphics;
  • neutral palette;
  • straightforwardness.

However, at the same time, the master claims that he never thinks about how to make a building or interior more conceptual. The most important thing for him is to create an interesting project. Regardless of whether the interior is a penthouse or a residential complex, the workshop always tries to work in a modern style. However, despite the desire to introduce the most fashionable trends in the interior, they are quite realistic and designed for people.
