We travel together. Capital of Canada

The capital of Canada ... Oddly enough, you just ask about the main city of this huge northern country, how often you can hear a lot of incorrect answers. Perhaps the most common is a tirade like: ‚ÄúWell, of course, Montreal! What no? Well then maybe Vancouver? Also no? Toronto?"

Neither one nor the other, nor the third! In fact, the capital of the state is Ottawa. Montreal is the so-called capital of the French province, Toronto can rightfully be considered the main city of the Ukrainian diaspora, and Vancouver is simply a modern metropolis with its size.

Section 1. The capital of Canada. Get to know the city.





Located in Ontario, on the banks of the river of the same name, the capital Ottawa is considered the fourth largest city in the country. Today its population is 875 thousand inhabitants.

And the city was founded a long time ago, in the first half of the 19th century, although until 1855 it had a completely different name, called Beitown in honor of a certain John Bey, who was in charge of the construction of the Kido Canal at that time.

The small village grew and developed, gradually turning into a large administrative center. By decision of the country's government, in 1857 Ottawa became the capital of Ontario, and 10 years later the main city of the country, to which not only the parliament, but also the majority of all federal institutions moved to battle.

Today, scientific and technical centers are developing and flourishing here. Industry is not far behind them, because, as you know, Ottawa can be proud of its second name. The automotive capital of Canada! And this, you see, is a lot.

Section 2. The capital of Canada. What to see first?





The list of local attractions is so wide that everyone can find something to do here.

For example, history lovers in their free time can go to the Military History Museum, the Museum of the History of Aviation and to a modern exhibition of science and technology. Those who prefer to spend as much time as possible in the fresh air should definitely visit the huge arboretum and the Museum of Nature. Art critics will be delighted with the Museum of Fine Arts and the National Center for Painting. The neo-Gothic parliament complex will be interesting for those who are fond of architecture.

Tourists who have come to rest with children should definitely go for a walk in one of the many parks. For example, Gatineau Park, where you can not only stroll among the luxurious old trees, but also sit on the lake. Also in this place, as a rule, magnificent panoramic pictures are obtained.

A good place for walking, especially on a hot summer day, is considered Major Hill Park. It offers stunning views of some of Ottawa 's main attractions - the Notre Dame Basilica, the Museum of Civilization, the National Gallery, Parliament Hill and the Ottawa River itself.

Section 3. The capital of Canada. What events are worth attending?





Every year a huge number of festivals are held here. In total, their number reaches 60. For example, in the summertime, Ottawa, whose hotels are always crowded and rooms are booked for several months, turns into a real capital of music. Tourists tend to attend jazz, blues and chamber music festivals.

In addition, it is recommended to visit at least one of the two real art festivals, Fringe and Ferrari.

In early July, Canadians usually celebrate their country's birthday. And for this reason, mass festivals, fairs, and celebrations are organized, and in the evening everyone who finds themselves in the central Ottawa Square will enjoy a grandiose festive fireworks.
