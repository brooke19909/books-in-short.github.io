What is stereo? Its development and methods of reproduction

In this article, we will examine what stereo is, how such a sound differs from mono, and how to transmit mechanical vibrations from a carrier to speakers, and we also learn the history of the origin of sound recorders and the intricacies of radio technology.

Differences in Sound Recording Methods

To begin, consider the main concept in this area. In acoustics, sound is understood to mean mechanical vibrations in various environments and how they are perceived by animals or people. That is all that our ears hear.

Mono is understood as a sound recording method in which all vibrations are applied to a data carrier with one track and one microphone. Simply put, it is similar to the perception of sound in one ear. In this case, the so-called "sound panorama" is not felt, everything is heard in one plane. This method was used everywhere until the 50s of the last century due to the technical complexity of implementing the recording of each individual instrument and combining the result in one track. Moreover, this recording method is cheaper than others and allows you to phonograph with just one microphone, so it is still used in various fields of activity. For example, in broadcasting.

Unlike mono, stereo allows you to use recording from two or more microphones, which gives a feeling of full presence when listening. There is another way to get this effect, for which a special hardware tool, called a mixer, is used. In this case, the effect of immersion is achieved due to the diversity of the monophonic recordings on different channels. Initially, the first way to achieve stereo was used, but due to the complexity of such sound recordings, from the beginning of the 60s of the last century, the mixing method, also called pseudo-stereo, began to be used everywhere.

Recording History

The pioneer in the field of applying sound to media is Thomas Edison. He invented an apparatus that was able to record mechanical vibrations on a foil with a needle and reproduce the result. This unit was called a phonograph. This invention served as a huge impetus for mono-recording, since what stereo was, at that time no one knew.

Before phonographs, mechanical musical instruments were used. They were able to reproduce melodies, but had great limitations: the instruments were not able to record extraneous sounds, such as a human voice. The first invention in this area is a phono-autograph, which is an experimental device that is not able to reproduce the recording. It was only at the end of the 19th century that T. Edison was able to solve this problem with his invention mentioned above.

Bluetooth stereo playback

But a real technical breakthrough is audio transmission via Bluetooth. This is a stereo sound propagation method through radio communication. A few years ago it seemed that such a stereo could not be reproduced. The range of Bluetooth has a relatively small distance, but it is enough to use a wireless headset. The main advantage of listening to music using Bluetooth technology is convenience. After all, this method of sound transmission frees the user from obsolete and such uncomfortable wired headphones.
