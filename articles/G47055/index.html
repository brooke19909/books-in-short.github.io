Abnormal places

In addition, it has long been noticed that rare species of plants grow in such places. Therefore, now the ridge of remnant towers and the mountain of the same name, the crest of which they are, has become a botanical, archaeological and geomorphological (relief science) and hydrological natural monument. Hydrological because there are many small tributaries of the Iset River in this territory , the largest of them - Semipalatinka - washes the base of Mount Devil's Hillfort on one side. Yekaterinburg is completely dependent on the Iset River - this is its main waterway.

Volcanic origin





The mountain itself has a height of 349 meters above sea level, its crest crowning, stretching along the battlement in the north-west direction, reaches 18-20 meters in the center. Of course, this ridge is a miracle of nature, which does not yet have a clear logical explanation of its formation. As a rule, remnant cliffs (frozen magma that has come up to the top, freed from the soil cover) always have a bizarre and mysterious form, there are a lot of them in different parts of Russia - Pillars near Krasnoyarsk, picturesque cliffs of the Seven Brothers in the Middle Urals, crowning Semibratskaya Mountain (422 m ) other.

The similarity of the Ural objects

In general, the mountain of the same name, with the rocky crest of the Seven Brothers crowning its peak, is very similar to the Devil's Hillfort. Yekaterinburg, like the village of Verkh-Nevinsky, owes its popularity among climbers all over the world to them. The similarity of these remnants consists not only in the same name as the mountains and the crest, they have the same structure (mattress-like) - the rocks of which they consist, are cut into slabs and resemble masonry. And the breed itself is the same - granite. They have in common that both sights are of volcanic origin, they are about 300 million years old.

How to get there



Both objects are located in the Sverdlovsk region, but from the cliffs of Seven Brothers to the regional center - 80 km, and directly at the city line is the Devil's Hillfort (Yekaterinburg). Every citizen knows how to get to this place by car - first you need to get to the Iset station via the Tagil highway (the place is noteworthy because there is a border separating Europe from Asia), from there a good road leads to the Devil's Hillfort , which is in good condition . The cordon at the foot of the mountain serves as a guideline for the end of the road - only further on foot (5 km).

Man-made hillfort

In addition to the natural rock-outcrops of an unusual mysterious form, there are man-made objects known by this name - Devil's Hillfort. Elabuga boasts just such a landmark. This is the name of the stone tower, 7.1 meters high, a monument of Bulgarian culture dating back to the end of the first millennium AD. These are the remains of a mosque-fortress, erected by the princes of the Volga or Silver Bulgaria - a historical state that existed in the X-XIII centuries in the middle Volga region, in the Kama river basin .

The only cylindrical tower that has survived since that time collapsed in 1844. Only a small piece of the north wall remains, which still exists. Through the efforts of the head of the Elabuga I.V. Shishkin, the tower was restored in 1867 and is covered with a low iron roof. A new reconstruction, given the popularity of this place, was carried out in 2007. To the restored tower, a log entrance gate was added, giving the corresponding flavor. Paths are laid, the slopes of a high hill are dotted with benches, because it offers an amazing panorama. The tower itself is covered in legends and is a monument under the care of UNESCO. In addition, she is a symbol of Elabuga.
