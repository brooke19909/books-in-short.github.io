Nokia 5130 - phone review

Today we will discuss the Nokia 5130 phone. The product, released by the Finnish company at the end of 2008, was for its time a significant blow to the market of mobile devices for competitors. At that moment, almost all manufacturers pursued marginality in the release of their developments. In order to squeeze competitors, it was necessary to present an inexpensive and truly functional product. The Finnish company has succeeded in this with its XpressMusic line of phones. The pioneer of the new series was the Nokia 5130 with an astonishing price of 90 Euros, while a similar device of another production cost about 150. Similar solutions ranged from 120-130 Euros. Thus, Nokia 5130 has become the most affordable music phone.

Dimensions, Design and Management



Gamma - blue and red shades. Navigation and software keys - for the color of the case. The front panel is black, glossy. Cover in dark blue and red. The docking is tight, without play, with a hole for the speaker and camera (2 megapixels). Miniature and comfortable size: 107.5 x 46.7 x 14.8 mm, 88 g weight. It fits harmoniously into the hand. At the top there is a connector under microUSB (with a plug), a headset (3.5 mm) and charging (2 mm). On the left are the navigation keys of the audio player with a backlight corresponding to the shade of the phone. On the right are two buttons for adjusting the volume and a slot for a microSD card (with a dummy).

Display, keyboard, battery, memory, performance, Bluetooth and USB



Two-inch screen, resolution - 240 x 320 (31 x 41 mm), supports up to 16 million colors. High brightness. Clear and vibrant colors. TFT technology helps keep the picture in the sun, and tinting protects the screen. But for maximum visibility, you still have to look for a shadow. This is a problem for all small screen phones. The keys are large, tight-fitting, pressing hard. Good or bad is a matter of taste. The lining is glossy, corresponding to the body. Bright white backlighting of numbers and symbols.

In practical use, the device can last about three days, which is a very good indicator. We already got some idea about the Nokia 5130 device. The memory characteristics will be discussed later. On the built-in storage medium of 31 MB, only 17 megabytes are available to the user. But this drawback is offset by removable memory cards, the volume of which is not limited to the manufacturer. JAR file size - up to 1 MB, heap - 2 MB. Bluetooth version is 2.0 with EDR. The data exchange rate is about 180 kb / s. The stereo headset works diligently. The phone is equipped with USB 2.0, which provides data exchange at a speed of 500-600 Kb / s. Support for USB modem and MPT.

Camera, menus and applications



A 2 MP lens (CMOS) of acceptable quality supports resolutions from 160x20 to 1600x1200, and zoom - up to 4. Camera sound is optional. Quick shot mode. The video recording format is 3GP. Resolution is from 128x96 to 176x144. Different visual effects (photo and video) are available. The Nokia 5130 is designed on the 5th generation S40 platform. The standard software package includes a number of topics and three games (Snake 3, Bounce Tales and Rally 3D). There are applications like world time and currency converter, Nokia Sensor, Share on Ovi (file sharing). In the catalog you can download additional software. The default browser is Opera Mini.

Music and general impression of the device



The Nokia 5130 phone has a rather basic headset, but thanks to the 3.5 mm jack you can connect almost any headphone. The music chip endowed the device with excellent sound quality. The speaker is loud. The standard music player is minimalistic and comfortable. The screen displays the name of the song, group, album and its cover, if available. Rewind in increments of 5 seconds and progressive. Various playlist playback modes. Five-band equalizer with several presets for Rock, Pop, Jazz, etc. The ability to compose your own playlists and various filters for sorting audio files. Supported music formats: AAC, eAAC, eAAC +, MP3 and WMA.

Acceptable call quality, loud sound and strong vibration. There are no problems with performing the basic function of a mobile phone. Value for money has no analogues. Long battery life and good musical qualities.
