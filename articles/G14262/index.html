Palais des Iles 4 * (Tunisia, Djerba): hotel description, services, reviews

The corner of Tunisia in question was considered a paradise since ancient times. Using favorable natural conditions, Greeks, Phoenicians and Punians (their common descendants) built colonies here. As you know, the last settlers were expelled from here by the Romans. But after 9 centuries, the Romanesque population of Gabes Bay itself suffered from Berbers and Arabs.

Turning a fishing area into a resort

Former nomads from the bloodthirsty inhabitants of the desert first turned into fishermen, and in the last century - successful hoteliers and restaurateurs ... Fishing ports (in Gabes 60% of Tunisia's fishing industry are concentrated) attract thousands of tourists.

At the service of Western travelers and residents of the former Soviet Union - one of the prominent guest establishments that southern Tunisia can offer is the Palais des Iles 4 * hotel.





The day life of the Roman Meninks

Tour operators recognize its northern part as the main resort of the bay. Among other islands, Djerba is located here. It is the largest in the North African state. For several centuries, it served as the most reliable shelter for the Berber-Libyan corsairs.

The island capital (Houmt Souk - ‚Äúfortress bazaar‚Äù) is the work of the Jews who lived here once. It is with them that the main object of excursions is connected - the synagogue of La Griba (its age is more than 2500 years). Today the temple is the oldest monument of Jewish religious architecture. It remains to add that the Israeli community ceased to exist in the middle of the last century, leaving numerous traces in the local dialect ...

Near the synagogue is Bordj el-Kebir - the "Big Tower" (from it the natives fired from the Ottoman invaders). You can also explore the Tegermess lighthouse (preserved on the territory of the Dzherba Explor ethnic village) and the Gellala pottery quarter (with a museum of folk crafts).

Among the other interesting things of Djerba, all the cafes that serve rice cooked according to the local recipe, as well as shops selling the famous cupcake blanket, should be highlighted. Nature lovers will not pass by a population of pink flamingos. Thanks to them, the Romans called the nearby lagoon the word "meninks."

After the vacationers explore the whole island, they will be taken directly to the Sahara from the hotel ...





Palais des Iles: from Veraclub to 4 *

The hotel Palais des Iles 4 * (Tunisia, Djerba) received its name in a reminder of the good old days when all the countries of the Western Maghreb were a solid French colony. From the language of Jean-Jacques Rousseau and Napoleon, the Palais des Iles roughly translates as "a palace among the islands." Until 2014 (the time of the last reconstruction of the hotel and its acquisition of a 4-star status), the word Veraclub was added to the name. This has been called the fashionable shelter since 1998.
