Some historical information

Questions about the coordinates concerned the minds of people for a long time, even before our era. Prominent scientists in the path of developing a coordinate system were Hipparchus and Ptolemy. These people lived in the second or first centuries BC, but, nevertheless, they could determine the coordinates of stars with separating accuracy . These were the great people of their era, powerful geographers and astronomers. It was they who introduced the concept, which we now call the coordinate system, and from their work it is already becoming clear what geographical coordinates are. At that time, these people did not know that the Earth revolves around the Sun. Hipparchus suggested that the surface of our planet can be considered an ideal sphere, and on its example, he explained various foundations regarding spherical geometry.

What is the axis of the earth

If we take the Earth for a ball, then it becomes clear that it has a line that serves as a stereometric figure. What exactly is it about? This is a line that is a diameter, rotating around which a semicircle creates a whole sphere. What diameter in comparison with the Earth is called an axis. This is an imaginary line, which is not really there, but there is a daily rotation around it, and it is considered that it passes through the North and South poles.

What are parallels?

We continue to consider the Earth as a sphere and determine what the parallels are in this case. If we assume that the planet, like the ball, has a center, then the axis of the Earth will pass through it and will be divided into two equal parts, like the diameter of the radii.

If you draw a certain plane that will be perpendicular to the axis, then it will intersect the sphere in a certain circle, that is, the Earth along a line called a parallel. The parallel with the largest diameter passes through the center of the globe of the Earth, which is a large circle and is called the equator. It divides the sphere into two equal hemispheres. All similar circles that are created by planes perpendicular to the axis are also called parallels, but they are small circles compared to the equator. And the lines passing through the geographic poles will be called meridians. By the way, it is thanks to the equator that our Earth is divided into two parts - northern and southern. Accordingly, there are geographical poles of planet Earth, which are named depending on which part of the world they are located.

Meridian

If we draw a large plane through the axis itself and through the poles, we get a circle, which is called the "full meridian". All meridians are the same in length, as they pass through a straight line and two points on it in different planes. Only their location changes.

The system of meridians and parallels, which are depicted on the map and on the globe, is a degree network.





It is two-dimensional, since it is specified by only two coordinates - the parallel coordinate and the latitude coordinate. That is, what are geographic coordinates? These are two numbers, indicators of latitude and longitude. Such numbers have a dimension in degrees and minutes.

Origin lines

All coordinates are calculated using conventional stereometry.
