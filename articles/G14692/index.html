The largest cities of Bashkortostan



The Republic of Bashkortostan is a subject of the Russian Federation, which received its name in honor of the Bashkir, an indigenous people living in this territory. It is located exactly on the border of Asia and Europe in the southern part of the high Ural Mountains. The Republic of Bashkortostan belongs to the Volga Federal District and ranks seventh in Russia in terms of population - more than five million people. The main industries, especially developed in this region, are petrochemical, chemical, fuel and energy, metalworking, engineering, food processing and agriculture. More than 13% of the so-called gross regional product within the Volga Region and about 2.7-2.9% of the total Russian indicator are produced here annually. The largest cities in Bashkortostan, which are under the most pressure, are Ufa, Salavat, Sterlitamak, Neftekamsk, Tuymazy and Oktyabrsky. Their population is also the largest.

City Sterlitamak

In second place in the ranking of "the largest cities of the Republic of Bashkortostan" is the city of Sterlitamak. It is located one hundred and twenty kilometers south of the capital and is an important center of mechanical engineering and the chemical industry. The population of such a city of Bashkortostan as Sterlitamak is about six hundred to seven hundred thousand people. This is a developed industrial settlement with a well-established transport system and full infrastructure. The economy of Sterlitamak is based on various petrochemical and chemical enterprises.

City of Salavat

The largest cities of Bashkortostan include one of the centers of the so-called South Bashkortostan agglomeration - the city of Salavat. It is located one hundred and sixty kilometers south of the capital. This is a fairly powerful production center, the population of which is steadily approaching the mark of seven hundred thousand. Such cities of Bashkortostan as Salavat are regional centers of the oil refining and chemical industries. Currently, it produces liquid fuel, high-pressure polyethylene, butyl alcohols and nitrogen fertilizers. In addition, the city has factories for the production of metal structures, technical glass, mineral wool and reinforced concrete products.
