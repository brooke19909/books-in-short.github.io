First International: Year of Creation

On September 28, 1864, the International Workers' Partnership was founded in London. The organizers are K. Marx and F. Engels with their supporters. This partnership is the First International.



Education Background

The end of the 19th century was not a coincidence for the creation of such workers' organizations. Many events took place in the world that contributed to this:

  • The bourgeois revolution in 1789 in France.
  • The large development of modern industry in Europe with the growth of factories, factories and, consequently, the number of workers.
  • A radical fracture in transport. 1807 - the invention of the ship, which by the end of the 19th century completely replaced the sailing fleet. Russia and Turkey are the last countries in Europe where they could still be observed. The railway network was growing rapidly.

All these events led to an increase in the number of workers who began to think about their political and economic rights. However, everyone understood that a strong union of workers was needed. A fist that can withstand the onslaught of wealthy capitalists with administrative resources. It was on this fertile ground that the ideological ‚Äúshepherds‚Äù of such ideas, K. Marx and F. Engels, began their activity.

It was they who tried to direct the economic demands of the workers into the ‚Äúright‚Äù political direction.

However, it is a mistake to think that there were two ideologists. Supporters of these ideas were among the highest financial circles in Europe. One is George Odger, secretary of the London Council of Trade Unions. He pushed through the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãrepresenting workers in parliament.

Impulse to the International

The creation of the First International is connected with the first economic crisis of the capitalist system in 1857-1859. Against the background of simultaneous problems in all developed industrial countries, an understanding of global unification among workers has come. It was from this period that the proletarian unions of England and France came to the conclusion of a single international organization. The event in Russia added fuel to the fire . In 1863, Alexander II cracked down on the revolution in Poland. The rebels demanded independence.



Marxists organized general gatherings of workers. They described the allegedly inhuman methods of "Russian punishers" who cut the political freedom of the "peace-loving Poles" at the root. There were no questions about any economic requirements in Poland. This corner of the empire was the most developed in this regard. The central government did not interfere in domestic Polish law.

The method of manipulating public consciousness was also used by the ideologists of the International. They directed the working masses to political demands, which had not happened before. Slogans of war with Russia were shouted out under mass approval. The proletarian began to understand his strength. Rather, they helped him do this.

Russian ‚Äúatrocities‚Äù - a symbol of the union of European workers

On December 5, 1863, British workers turned to the French with a proposal for joint requirements for governments. Objectives - the war with Russia for the independence of Poland.



A year later, in 1864, a joint rally was already held in London, in the hall of St. Martin. Thus, the situation in Russia has become a decisive factor for unification. K. Marx himself, who had never appeared at such events before, attended this rally. He felt a change in the consciousness of the working class, which he realized was a powerful driving force in history.

First Congress: Organization of Planned Strikes

In 1866, in Geneva, the activities of the First International were connected with the organization of the first congress.

It adopted the charter drawn up by Marx, elected the General Council, listened to the reports of the workers. After the congress, the new Council began to lead the strikes of workers. Now it was no longer chaotic disparate performances, but well-planned actions. While the police disperse some demonstrators, others begin to strike on the other side of the city.

Second Congress: the creation of political forces

In Lausanne, in September 1867, a second congress of the First International was convened.

More serious issues appeared on the agenda: the active participation of socialist forces with the mass support of workers in the political life of the country. After him, the bourgeoisie began to show serious fears for their capital and a privileged position in society.

Third Congress: A Call to War

At the Third Congress in Brussels in 1868, ideas were expressed for military defense of their ideas. In fact, the First International called for a class revolution. At the Congress, a resolution appeared "on the manifestation of the greatest activity." You can observe the transformation of the idea from economic requirements to the call of the overthrow of the system in a fairly short period.



Neither the authorities nor the bourgeois could tolerate this. Political persecution begins. The Paris Commune created in France was dispersed. This dealt a serious blow to the International. Supporters across Europe began to be imprisoned, fired from work, etc.

Who needs this?

As the Roman lawyer Cassius said, if a crime occurs, then someone needs it. Indeed, who could need a revolution in a rapidly developing Europe. Paradoxically, the most radical views and calls for war fall precisely at the peak of development. Europeans have never lived in such conditions. The story repeated with our country. It is during the period of the greatest power of the state in the entire history of the Russian Empire that similar forces are activated in our country. However, our society could not cope with such a threat. Why was the first International unsustainable? Did he disappear from the political struggle? This will be discussed later.

First International: briefly on further developments

The First International was not ready to unite in a single revolutionary struggle in Europe. Wise Europeans realized that they needed to follow the path of liberalism, not revolution. After that, the General Council of the International moved to the United States. Its further manifestation will affect our history during the February and then October revolutions. The First International formally existed until 1876, where a decision was made in Philadelphia to terminate it.

It is noteworthy that the First and Second International aimed at the mandatory overthrow of the political systems of rapidly developing Europe. The ideologist of socialism, Bakunin, was just against this. He called only to improve the life and work of the working class.
