Petr Nikolaevich Durnovo: biography

Pyotr Nikolaevich Durnovo was a well-known historical figure of his time, but he gained special popularity after his death, when the sovereign emperor and all his associates became convinced of the truth of the foreteller of the figure. Basic information

Pyotr Nikolaevich was born in 1845 in the Moscow province, throughout his life he led state affairs, for a year he headed the Ministry of the Interior of the Russian Empire. Often he is confused with the famous namesake - Pyotr Pavlovich, who was the Moscow governor since 1905.

Childhood

Durnovo was lucky to be born in a fairly noble and respected family. His father was a representative of an old noble family, revered by a man in the entire Moscow province, as a result of which he was appointed vice-governor of one of her settlements. Mother was the niece of Lazarev, the famous Russian admiral, the discoverer of Antarctica. The Durnovo family was large, but despite this fact, parents were able to give each child an excellent upbringing, literacy and other important sciences, as well as invest in them the foundations of morality and virtue.

Start of activity

At the age of 15, Pyotr Nikolayevich could already call himself the proud title of ‚Äúcadet,‚Äù he graduated from a specialized Moscow school. And after 2 years, he went to conquer the waters of China and Japan, where he spent about 8 years. At the end of the same decade, he decided to return to Moscow and try himself in a slightly different field, but could not completely leave military service, so he combined the two specialties that attracted him, enrolling in the military law academy. Already in the early seventies, he passed the qualification exam and was appointed assistant prosecutor of one of the districts. After some time, he was fired and sent to the Ministry of Justice.

Civil service

In 1873, Durnovo was transferred by a fellow prosecutor to Moscow from Vladimir, and a few years later he received the post of prosecutor - first Rybinsky, then Vladimirsky Uyezd.

At the beginning of the next decade, Pyotr Nikolaevich began to manage the judicial department of the Department of State Police under the Ministry of Internal Affairs. And his appointment in this position only accelerated his already rapid career growth. Literally after 1.5 years, he was appointed deputy director of the same Department, which means he got the opportunity to develop in his activities through business trips abroad. So, for a year he visited France, the German Empire and Austria-Hungary, where he got acquainted with the structure of state structures, in particular, the police, and ways of monitoring anti-government elements in order to apply some of the most successful of them in the Russian Empire.

Upon returning to his homeland, Pyotr Nikolayevich Durnovo will take the post of Director of the Department, where he will stay 10 years, but will be dismissed with a scandal. As you know, the ‚ÄúBlack Cabinet‚Äù was subordinated to the state police - the body that deals with the correspondence of citizens in order to prevent anti-government protests. In 1893, employees found letters from a St. Petersburg lady to her lover, the Brazilian ambassador to Russia. As it turned out, she was a lady of the heart and Durnovo himself, the chief of the Department. He was informed of the correspondence, he reacted very zealously to it, and managed to make mistakes. Namely, he appeared to the woman, threw these letters in her face and whipped her cheeks, and then he turned up to the ambassador and searched his house to find other letters. The Brazilian reported this disgrace to the emperor, and the latter decided to dismiss Pyotr Nikolaevich Durnovo.

But the former chief of police did not remain without a post, he was immediately appointed a senator. And at the beginning of the new century, he received another promotion, becoming a comrade of the Minister of Internal Affairs with famous figures of that time. Also during this period, Durnovo was engaged in philanthropic activities - he took care of work houses and orphanages.

Minister of Internal Affairs

On October 22, 1905, Bulygin A.G., Minister of the Interior of the Russian Empire, was dismissed. Pyotr Nikolayevich took his place, also at the same time a week later he was appointed a member of the State Council and elevated to secret advisers. It is worth saying that he was the last Minister of Internal Affairs, who died a natural death, his subsequent colleagues could not avoid the red terror.

During the 2 years of the first Russian revolution, Pyotr Nikolayevich Durnovo applied rather cruel measures to suppress it. In 1906, members of the Socialist Revolutionary Party decided to kill him, as he supported the activities of the so-called Black Hundreds. But the plan failed after Leontiev, a member of the Socialist Revolutionary Party, shot and killed in Switzerland an innocent Frenchman who looked like Durnovo in appearance.

Resignation and further activities

In 1906, Pyotr Nikolayevich was forced to resign from his post as minister due to internal contradictions, and the whole cabinet, including Witte, followed, since they had one policy. But he did not leave state power at all, remaining to preside in the Council of State. In 1911, he rejected the bill on zemstvos in the western provinces, which laid the foundation for an acute political crisis, and this was extremely unprofitable in a specific period of time, so the emperor ordered him to declare himself sick and refrain from sitting at the Council of State for at least a year.

Key facts

Pyotr Nikolaevich wrote a note on the eve of the First World War, but it still continues to haunt the attention of historians, many publicists, and simply interested people. Almost everything Durnovo warned about came true with the greatest possible accuracy.

Highlights

Pyotr Nikolaevich outlined all the main points of participation in the war, including outlining the balance of power. He absolutely accurately noted that if Russia enters the war on the side of England, then from the rivalry of the two powers (Germany and Britain), the conflict will develop into a world one. ‚ÄúTherefore, the Russian Empire simply does not need to enter into armed conflict, because this will not bring it any privileges, but only exacerbate internal contradictions,‚Äù the retired minister wrote.

Durnovo also noted that an alliance with England could not theoretically bring Russia any benefits, which means that joining it is meaningless and, more seriously, predicts foreign policy problems.

In a note, Durnovo, through a series of conclusions, concludes that Germany and the Russian Empire have no real contradictions and cannot exist, they can very well coexist peacefully. In any case, even if victory is won over Germany, it will not bring any benefits to the state, but only provide an incentive for the emergence of new internal and external contradictions.

Hence, Pyotr Nikolaevich concluded that instead of an alliance with England, Russia needed to draw closer to Germany, establish relations with France and conclude a defensive alliance with Japan.

Durnovo's note to Nicholas ii contained thoughts about the weakness of Russian liberalism, namely its imperfection and the inability to resist the revolutionaries in the event of intensification of internal contradictions in the country. Therefore, it is necessary to stop the opposition at the state level, by the power of the autocrat, as happened in the nineteenth century. ‚ÄúConcessions and agreements with anti-government circles will only aggravate the situation and weaken power,‚Äù wrote Durnovo in the Note to Nikolai.

The author did not even doubt the beginning of new actions against the government in the event of the outbreak of war. They will be caused by the discontent of the broad masses of the population by the highest authorities and the actions of the emperor. Socialist slogans about black redistribution and division of all property will certainly be put forward. The army will be demoralized even in the event of victory, and this will undoubtedly lead to hunger and a production crisis. Russia will be led to anarchy.

The text of the letter to Durnovo to Nicholas 2 contained the above information and position. Accordingly, the emperor could prevent the results of the war, but did not trust the author.
