Temple of St. Sergius of Radonezh in Rogozhskaya Sloboda: tourists photos

The sky-blue church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in the Rogozhskaya Sloboda has long been pleasing to the eye of everyone passing through the old Vladimir tract, which is now called the Enthusiasts Highway.

Rogozhskaya Sloboda

In 1625, the temple in the name of the Trinity Life-Giving already stood on this site. His parishioners were coachmen who lived here in Rogozhskaya Sloboda. During the French invasion, the church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in Rogozhskaya, like the whole settlement, burned out. In 1816, parishioners turned to the Holy Synod with a request to dismantle a dilapidated church and build a new one. Permission was obtained and soon the first building stone was laid in the future church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in Rogozhskaya Sloboda.

The beginning of the restoration of the shrine

Church adviser Gabriel Petrovich Smolyansky, who had invested all his financial savings on this charitable event, was especially interested in the church of God. But before the church was built, in 1834 it burned down. However, church utensils and sacred vessels were saved. They rebuilt the church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in the Rogozhskaya Sloboda according to the project of the famous architect of the recognized master of the Empire style, Fedor Mikhailovich Shestakov. February 17, 1838 the church was consecrated by the Metropolitan of Moscow and Kolomna, St. Filaret Drozdov.

Palekh church painting



The three-story bell tower was built later, in 1864. In 1876, the church was painted by the famous Moscow icon painter Andrei Semyonovich Rogozhkin, as they used to say, in the "Old Orthodox Orthodox style." Now art critics define this style as Palekh. Since the Old Believers lived in the Rogozhskaya settlement, they tried to attract them to Orthodoxy. Icons of ancient letters were purchased in the temple, if they bought new ones, then they were stylized for the 15-16 centuries. It was said that the temple on the Rogozhskaya Sloboda in a collection of remarkable ancient icons could even be compared with the Kremlin shrines. In November 1899, a special place was consecrated in the St. Sergius Church for conversations with the Old Believers, for religious reading and spiritual concerts. Special notes were made for the wonderful choir of the blind, which was considered one of the best in Moscow.

Atheistic Bacchanalia

The revolution and the rampant militant atheism crossed out all the centuries-old works on the improvement of the temple. In 1937, after the arrest of the abbot and active parishioners, the newly arrived atheists chopped old icons with axes. Books and church decorations were burned at the stake. Temple destroyers did not hesitate to burn priceless notes for the blind. What was created and acquired for centuries with love and patience died in a few hours in an atheistic bacchanalia. Only a few old icons were saved. One of them is the miraculous icon of the Mother of God ‚ÄúSatisfy My Sorrows‚Äù, which was transferred to the St. Nicholas Church in Kuznets, which is still there.

Soviet past

Since the late fifties of the twentieth century, the church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in the Rogozhskaya settlement was used for workshops and warehouses. In the late 70s, welding sparks flew into the central aisle, workers swearing and smoking cigarettes, welded the details of the monument to Gagarin, which now stands on Leninsky Prospekt in Moscow. Shower cabins were installed here, where people washed after work. Then no one knew and did not think that all this happens literally on the graves and bones of executed people. The terrible truth about what was happening in the house of God until the fifties was found out only when the church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in the Rogozhskaya settlement was given to believers, and only then a serious restoration began.



Scary finds

Restorers of the temple went down to the cellars, and what they saw there shocked them to the core. Stacks stacked bones, lightly sprinkled with sand, and in the skulls - bullet holes. After a request to the relevant competent authorities, it was possible to establish that the temple of St. Sergius of Radonezh in the Rogozhskaya Sloboda, for which once the state adviser G.P. Smolyansky spent his entire fortune so that the descendants had spiritual joy and comfort, turned into a training ground for the execution of sentences. The bones of the sufferers were buried according to Christian custom. A cross was placed over the mass grave on which it is written: "They were tortured in order to receive the Resurrection." Memorial services are regularly performed over the grave.

Innocent slaughtered

The temple commemorates the new martyrs, whose fate was directly related to the history of the temple. From 1916 to 1918, he served as a psalmist in the church, the holy martyr Joseph Schensnovich, who was shot in 1937. One of the grounds for the execution was the testimony of a witness that Dyakon Skchensnovich forces his own children to attend church. The holy martyr Konstantin Gusev from 1921 to 1927 served as a deacon in this church, and from 1927 to 1934 a priest, he was shot in 1937. In the same 1937 they were arrested and executed:

Shrine revival

December 4, 1991 was a real holiday for all Russian Orthodoxy. Bishop of Solnechnogorsk Sergiy Fomin held a small consecration of the temple. From this time began the revival of the shrine. After several years of hard work, the chapels of St. Sergius of Radonezh and Nicholas the Wonderworker were completely restored. Ancient painting shines again with bright colors. However, the complete completion of all work is still very far away. Very often there is not enough money for everything, so restoration work has to be frozen. According to old photographs, the main iconostasis is being restored. Miraculously, the holy icon of Seraphim of Swarovski, which is written on the wall of the temple, was almost completely preserved. Many fragments of the painting have to be reconstructed.



Special prayers for pregnant women

What else is known for the church of St. Sergius of Radonezh in the Rogozhsky settlement? The baptism of a child, special prayers for pregnant women, holding Christian holidays and ceremonies - all this is available to those who want to visit this holy place. A prayer service for pregnant women in the church on the Rogozhskaya Sloboda is held every Sunday at 13-00. After the service, each young mother can stay and ask her question to the priest. The St. Sergius Church in Rogozhskaya Sloboda is always glad to see new people.
