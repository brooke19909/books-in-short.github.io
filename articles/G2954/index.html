Nokia X6 - mobile phones: specifications, review, prices

Nokia X6 is a Chinese smartphone that has become quite popular. Rather, the device itself is not Chinese, but Finnish. Nevertheless, if we take into account the fact that the Celestial Empire currently has a decent package of documents that allow them to produce certain phones on its territory, then everything will immediately fall into place.

The Nokia X6 8GB case is a separate topic, and a special paragraph will be devoted to it in the article. There are some nuances associated with this part of the device. Meanwhile, we note that the device was available for purchase in mobile phone stores in three variations. The first is just the same model with 8 GB of internal long-term memory. The second variation is a model with 16 GB. It would be logical to assume that the next model will be the device, "on board" which is 32 gigabytes of long-term file storage. The rule of powers of two does not sleep, and this is indeed so.





Equipment

Opening the box in which this smartphone is sold, we can find in it the device itself, an autonomous power source for it (battery), a charger and a USB cable. Also included are wired headphones, an instruction manual and a voucher.

Design

The main material for the manufacture of the phone is nothing more than plastic. Perhaps it is simply impossible to unequivocally answer the question of what is this - a disadvantage or an advantage. Let us dwell on the neutral version and move on to further consideration. Even at the development stage of the device, the manufacturer said that metal elements would also be present. And, as we see, no one deceived us. The metal elements in the case are represented by strips passing along the sides of the phone.

Note that more than once crash tests were carried out, which revealed that the frames on the sides of the device are indeed made of appropriate materials. The screen covers a special glass. Similar materials were used for coating in devices such as the iPhone and iPod. Again, if we return to the crash tests, we can notice: after the impact, the base seems to fall into separate parts. Front side

On the front panel of the device, directly above its screen, is the lens of an additional (as it is called, frontal) camera. Touch buttons we will not see, because they are mechanical. Left side

The Nokia X6 8GB case, as mentioned earlier, is made primarily of plastic materials. Thus, the use of plugs takes on some special meaning. We see it on the left side of the device. The first thought that creeps into my head is probably about a memory card. However, there it was. In fact, unfortunately, it should be noted that there are no memory cards in this model at all.

Thus, there are only two logical explanations for the presence of a stub on the left side: it covers either a charging port or a SIM card slot. In our case, the second option is the right one. So, to replace the SIM card, you do not have to turn off the device. But with bare hands, the user is unlikely to be able to do anything. We need a tool, even an improvised one, with which you can pick up an old card. Tweezers are great for these purposes.

Right side

On the right side of the device is a button that allows you to adjust the volume of the device itself, as well as the music or video being played. There is also a slider to help lock the phone. There are a huge number of device models on the market that have such a control dangling. However, not in the case of our device. Here it is deepened inside the case, which can be called a positive point. But, as the law of conservation of energy says, if there is any advantage, look for a disadvantage. In our case, this is the usability of the slider.

Rear panel

On the back is the main camera lens. The bezel protrudes noticeably forward. Erasing it will be quite simple. This will happen within a few weeks of operation. But for a similar design of the device, this is not something new. This is a kind of flaw. Meanwhile, most experts are inclined to believe that such a shortcoming is expected, and therefore it would be better to write it into the category of expected losses.

Build problems

According to the polls, much more users of this phone are annoyed by the poor assembly of the device: there are backlashes, the back cover is prone to creak from time to time. Whether this is due to its small thickness is unknown. Also, the lid may become loose over time if it is often removed and put back. Without tension, snap it in all the grooves instantly will not work. Each corner must be squeezed separately. A much more successful option would be with a metal plate.

Perhaps, these support points can be distinguished in a superficial review of this phone model.
