What is important to know about Riga

Before visiting Riga, it will not be superfluous to learn a little about its history and features. Riga is the Latvian capital, and in combination - the recognized cultural capital of Europe. It is the largest Baltic city. At the time of Latvia's entry into the Russian Empire, it occupied an important place both in industry, and in commercial and cultural spheres.

Riga is located on the shores of the Baltic Sea. The city has many park areas, squares, ponds, which makes it incredibly beautiful, conducive to hiking.

The climate is temperate, the weather will please both in winter and in summer.

Until 1990, Riga was the most densely populated city in the Baltics. However, after the population was pretty thinned due to emigration.

If you look at the national composition, then the majority of the population is Latvians, a little less Russians live in Riga.

What documents are needed

Those wishing to make a trip to Riga for tourist purposes should remember that Latvia is a member of the European Union. This means that a foreigner will need to obtain a Schengen visa in order to get into the country without problems.

The question naturally arises: what documents are necessary for a trip to Riga?

The first step is to clarify that there are several types of visas:

  • Schengen visa. Upon receipt of such a visa, the traveler also gets the right to move through the territories of all countries that are members of Schengen. Usually, it is she who is considered as a travel visa, which is very convenient.
  • Transit visa. This visa is designed for the traveler to transfer from one aircraft to another when the flight is not direct. At the same time, a visa does not give a foreigner the right to leave the airport.
  • Long term visa. A kind of almost alternative to a residence permit. The holder of such a visa receives the right to stay in the territory of a foreign country for a period of over 90 days and up to 6 months.

If the main goal is to visit the country as a tourist, a Schengen visa is the best choice.

So, to get a visa to travel to Riga, you will need:

  • Fill out the form. If the rest is planned by the whole family, then you need to fill in for each family member.
  • International passport.
  • Passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation. If a minor receives a visa, then a birth certificate must be provided instead of a passport.
  • Insurance policy. It should cover more than 30,000 euros. And it acts on the territory of any country that is part of the Schengen.
  • Two color photos of a standard size.
  • Any confirmation of intentions to visit Riga. For example, it can be airline tickets or hotel reservations.
  • Bank statement.
  • Consular fee receipt.

Additional documents

This list is basic but not exhaustive. Additional documents may be required if a foreigner plans to visit Riga for employment.

How to get from St. Petersburg

There are several ways to get to Riga. For example, some people choose to travel to Riga by car. It all depends on which city will serve as a starting point. From St. Petersburg, a trip to Riga is offered through Pskov.

The roads are lightly loaded, so the path will come out relatively fast. Before departure, you should refuel, as there are very few refuelings after the border.

On the way to Riga, you will have to cross the border with Estonia. The queue needs to be booked through the online service in advance. The procedure may be time-consuming.

How to get from Moscow

If you go on a trip to Riga from Moscow, then the route on the M9 highway is recommended. The distance will be about 950 kilometers. And in time, a trip to Riga from Moscow by car will take an average of 12 hours. The road surface is normal. According to reviews, there are not many cafes and gas stations on the highway. Crossing the border takes a lot of time. With heavy workload up to 8 hours.

There is another option to go on the M1 highway, through Belarus.

Bus ride to Riga

It is not necessary to be torn between a choice - an airplane or a train. A trip to Riga by bus will be for many the most convenient option.

For example, a direct bus runs from St. Petersburg several times a day. Such a ticket will cost about 3,000 rubles, and the transfer will take about 12 hours. You can take up to three bags with you. Comfortable buses with free Wi-Fi and power sockets for charging gadgets are available.

Getting from the capital will be somewhat more difficult. The bus runs once a day, the ticket price is higher, and the travel time will take up to 15 hours.

A few words about cellular communications

In a foreign trip can not do without cellular communications. In Latvia, you can buy a SIM card on a prepaid card, which is easy to find in every kiosk, train stations and even in supermarkets. So calls abroad will be even more profitable.

In Riga, the main mobile operators are TELE2 and LMT.

Free and paid Wi-Fi networks are also widespread in Riga. In many public places you can easily connect and use the Internet.

Gastronomy

When you visit another country, it will be interesting to get acquainted with its gastronomic features. There are many different restaurants in Riga, where they will gladly treat guests with their national dishes, which are sometimes very surprising.

Although the Latvian and Russian cuisines are somewhat reminiscent of each other, it will be unusual for a Russian traveler to taste beetroot soup. Unlike the usual dishes, in Riga such a soup is served cold. It can be found in the menu called aux zupa.

In general, Latvian cuisine is replete with unusual dishes and combinations of products. And yet, after lunch in any restaurant or cafe, satiety is more than guaranteed. What a Latvian onion bug is worth! The unusual dish turns out to be a steak, which is served in Riga with a special onion sauce. And for dessert you can treat yourself to a buberte. You should not be afraid, this is an incredibly tasty semolina, which everyone was probably fed in childhood. Only here they like to combine it with whipped cream, sometimes adding a handful of nuts and cranberry sauce. Few people will refuse such porridge!

The most common product in Latvian cuisine is perhaps fish. It is used here in all possible forms. There are fish casseroles with vegetables, and fried herring, which is served with the already familiar onion sauce, and the same herring, only already baked on charcoal. And also many types of sandwiches, salads. Salmon can be tasted in a variety of forms at every step.

If we talk about drinks, here Riga will also bring a few surprises. Kissels are very popular here. They are so diverse that you can taste both sweet honey and sour, from peas or oats with the addition of yogurt. In addition, bread kvass is very popular.

Among the most famous alcoholic drinks is Riga Black Balm. You can‚Äôt visit Riga and at least once not try it. It is worth paying a little attention to liquors. You can try unusual vodka from tomatoes. Beer in Riga is very excellent and, like the whole kitchen, has its own original features.
