Cubic houses in Rotterdam - a visiting card of the city of the future

Original Holland, which attracts tourists with the scent of freedom, is famous for its lush tulip fields, windmills, and numerous canals. There are legends about the beauty of the medieval architecture of a hospitable country. However, there are modern attractions that amaze with their diversity and have no analogues in the world.

Modern architectural monument

Rotterdam is the second largest city in the Netherlands, in stark contrast to Amsterdam. Famous for its bizarre architectural monuments, it makes a lasting impression. An unusual residential complex, which appeared in the mid-80s of the last century, is recognized as the hallmark of the city of the future.

In the world there are many different designs of houses, but not every one of them attracts attention with a curious design. The cubic houses in Rotterdam, whose photos make you want to see them with your own eyes, are a unique attraction, which is simply impossible not to notice.

Architecture features

To be completely accurate, the form of a real miracle of engineering is more like a box. However, outside the dwellings look like cubes in contact with each other with their faces. Due to the slope, their usable area is much smaller than the actual.

Built cubic houses in Rotterdam, whose frame is a vertical hexagon, look as if they are painted. Carefully crafted colors are perfect for bizarre architecture. The main material used is wood fiber frames and cement. The base of the isolated houses with an area of ‚Äã‚Äãapproximately 100 m 2 is a high pillar-pillar, inside of which there is a metal spiral staircase leading to the premises.

It is curious that each dwelling set on one corner consists of several floors, and on the first, in the spaces between the pedestals, there are offices and shops. All the cubic houses in Rotterdam (Netherlands) are three-level: the lower ones have a living room and kitchen, the second has bathrooms and bedrooms, the third - the so-called atrium (cabinet illuminated by the sun's rays). Thanks to windows oriented to all directions of the world, from which a wonderful panorama of the city opens, there is always a lot of light inside.

Unexpected problems

The interior of the cubic houses in Rotterdam is quite interesting, because inside all the walls are sloping, not straight. In addition, the windows are in the most unexpected places, and the ceiling is supported by a powerful column. Inhabitants of amazing homes suffer from inconvenience, since it is impossible to hang pictures on the walls or nail the shelves, as well as put furniture on them. And all the cabinets, chairs, sofas have to be ordered from designers.

A unique opportunity to get acquainted with a curious object

Tourists coming to the city must visit the cubic houses in Rotterdam and examine them. They admit that no photographs can convey the beauty of an extraordinary sight. Many even become speechless. From the side, the houses resemble an artificial forest that has grown in a metropolis.

Some residents take advantage of the hype that has arisen and create unique museums from their homes. For a small fee (approximately 2.5 euros / 184 rubles), tourists will be able to go inside the strange-looking houses from 11 to 17 hours daily. Visitors are surprised at how unusual everything is here, and even catch themselves thinking that it would be nice to stay here for a week.

Some stay at Stayokay Rotterdam Hostel, which is located in the cabins. Visitors are offered double, four and six-bed rooms, as well as places in a common room for 4, 6 and 8 people. The price of accommodation (from 30 euros / 2200 rubles per day) includes breakfast, toilet and shower - common. Settlement occurs around the clock, even at night.
