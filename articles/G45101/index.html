Hostel, Warsaw: addresses, description of rooms, provided services, photos and reviews

The hostel is a European lodging system that includes a minimum number of amenities for a small fee. Most often, this is a certain number of beds in the room and general amenities on the floor for everyone.

The history of the hostels goes back to medieval Germany, where students from poor families spent the night on weekends in local schools. Further, such overnight stays became public domain and they could be found in almost all towns.

Capsule hostels, which first appeared in Japan, became an innovation in this business. This is an isolated sleeping place in the form of a capsule or parallelepiped inside a common room.

Rules of residence

All hostels have their own routines and rules, which you should familiarize yourself with before checking in. Be sure to pay attention to the gender gradations of the introduction to the room. In some apartments, men and women settle in one room, many may not like this. You can rent a hostel in Warsaw inexpensively both in the city center and on the outskirts. You can also see places for overnight in the private sector.

All low-budget nights in the corridor, kitchen and other common areas are equipped with video surveillance cameras. For the safety of visitors.

Smoking and drinking alcohol during your stay is prohibited. It is necessary to strictly follow the rules of the hostel and clean dishes and pans after cooking.

Features of checking into hostels in Warsaw

Every tourist should understand: no matter how good the hostel is, it is a hostel. The level of comfort and services will vary. Do not count on a comfortable and cheap overnight stay. Most of the European low-cost berths meet all the criteria for price and quality. Therefore, if you want to settle for a penny in a room in the center of the capital, do not expect silk sheets and a marble floor.

Pay attention to the rules of settlement. The hostels do not have the principle "arrived at 02.00 and checked out at 02.00 the next day." Check in at 12.00 and check out at the same, regardless of what time you arrived. You can rent a hostel in Warsaw both by pre-paying it, or simply booking it in advance. It is very difficult to look for an overnight stay in the center of Warsaw without agreeing on it in advance.

When booking a bed, check for towels, slippers, soap supplies (this is not always provided). Be sure to read the administrative letters, they indicate the door codes, as well as the need to leave a deposit.
