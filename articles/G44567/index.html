Alpha is a symbol of birth in the Greek alphabet. In Syrian, Cretan and Sinai, the letter was turned upside down and looked like a horned head of a bull in the shape of a triangle. In various Aramaic, Palestinian and Attic versions, she leans to the side and, in the end, comes to our "A".

Greek alphabet

Currently, the Greek alphabet is used by the Greek diasporas in the Black Sea region of Russia, southern Italy and southern Albania, as well as by some Arumans and meglenorumins, gypsies, Slavs, and Muslim groups in northern Greece.
