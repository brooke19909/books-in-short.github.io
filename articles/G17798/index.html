Semenovskaya Square in Moscow: history, photo

Semenovskaya Square, located in the Eastern District of Moscow, has been known since the 18th century. Here Peter I created his first army, the Semenovsky amusing regiment, which later became, together with the Preobrazhensky Regiment, the foundation and center of the Russian regular army.

The location of the amusing army on the outskirts of Moscow began to be called the Semenovskaya soldiers' settlement.

History of Semenovskaya Square

The area was growing rapidly. Buildings for living, the building of the order, the house of Prince Menshikov were erected. In 1742, the Semenovskaya outpost was erected here. Wealthy citizens liked the well-protected lands; merchants and bourgeois people came here to live and trade. By the beginning of the XX century, the settlement became the industrial outskirts of the city.
