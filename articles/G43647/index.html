Preferred shares: determination, owner rights, dividend amount

Companies issue securities to raise capital. It can be debt receipts - bonds with coupon income and shares. Dividends are part of the net profit due on one security.

Asset definition

People far from the stock market buy securities as a means of additional passive income and investment in the future.

Ordinary and preference shares are a way to engage holders of non-obligation funds in the investment of an enterprise.

The text of the article will contain expressions - elite shares, profitable shares, and elite shares. These phrases indicate one subject - preferred shares.

Cost estimate

The cost of preferred shares is no secret. Information on the current price of one share, on the size of the minimum purchase, on commission fees can be obtained from several sources:

  • from the websites of issuers that placed shares to attract investors;
  • from stock exchange newsletter;
  • Information pages of investment and management companies.

Three types of stock value are used:

  1. Nominal rating. Declared in the prospectus per share. If assets are suddenly issued on paper, then the denomination in rubles is printed on the form. There is no price difference between ordinary and preferred securities upon announcement.
  2. Market price. It develops in the life cycle of paper in the stock market. This is the amount that a person is willing to invest in an enterprise through equity participation. Most often, ordinary shares exceed preferred assets in value. This difference is called a discount. For example, a trading day on the Moscow Exchange on January 22 ended for Sberbank with a closing price of OA at 243.99 rubles, and PA fixed at a closing price of 202.50. Discount - 41.49 rubles.
  3. Book value. Important for legal entities. If the shares are traded on the stock exchange, then in addition to the share capital, the share of PA in the capital is taken into account. The task of balance sheet accounting is for accountants, professionals.

Who rules, the owner

The rights of the owner of a preferred share differ from the rights of holders of ordinary shares by the prohibition of voting at meetings of shareholders. As long as the owner of the preferred shares receives dividends, he does not vote on his shares. Except when a resolution is adopted on the termination of the company. Admission to voting is also made if shareholders discuss issues of reducing the rights of owners of PA.

As soon as the payment of preferred assets ceases, the owner of the expensive assets can approve or protest on the entire agenda.

The company's standards indicate the percentage and cash volume of dividends on PA. Or the order of accrual is determined. If the digital indicator is not fixed, then holders of PA receive dividends on a par with the owners of OA.

The rights and obligations of shareholders - holders of PAs are detailed in the charters of issuers. Therefore, it is useful for a potential acquirer of assets to study the constituent documents. You must know the order of entry and the procedure for exiting the asset.

In the event of bankruptcy of the company on the list of creditors, owners of preferred shares are ahead of the owners of ordinary shares and have a chance of a partial return on invested funds.

Investing and dividends

The charter is the law of the company, which stipulates the payment of dividends on preferred and ordinary shares.

Investing in the capital of a successful organization brings additional income to the co-owner. Previously, the constituent documents of companies indicated the mandatory percentage of deductions from net profit in favor of the owners of elite shares. Now more and more often companies are adjusting the charter, the amendments provide for the determination of the share to remuneration at the suggestions of senior managers.

In a situation where the company has a loss, dividends can be forgotten. But there are more frequent cases when there is profit, and the Board of Directors recommends not paying a penny to the co-owners.

Listing Procedure

When summing up the results of a quarter, half a year or a year, the Board of Directors makes a decision on the remuneration of investors holding shares. Subject to the provisions of the Charter and the dividend policy, the following payment amounts are recommended:

  1. Accrued but not paid.
  2. Counted on elite shares.
  3. Due on ordinary shares.

Payments on preferred shares are made at the same time as on ordinary shares - within 60 days from the date of the decision to pay and no later than 25 days from the date the shareholders register is cut off.

Finances are credited to the client's brokerage account. The shareholder is not required to pay income tax on his own. The issuing company is a tax agent and holds 13%. No dividend payments are made to any social funds. Amounts are received cleared.
