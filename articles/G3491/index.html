Appearance

The design and appearance of the Braun Multiquick J700 is simply gorgeous. The materials used here are metal (stainless steel) and high-quality plastic. Most of the body is made of stainless steel, and everything else is made of plastic. The juicer is sold in only one color and it fits very well with the machine.





On the right side there is a gear shift regulator, of which there are only 2, but this is more than enough. In front is a trailed spout through which juice will drain. The last thing left is a juice container. Its volume is 1250 ml. Additionally, it has a lid and a separator (partition) to separate the foam from the juice. As the experience of many juicers shows, such separators are extremely mediocre in their work, so it is quite possible to do without them.

Juicer at work

Now you can dwell in more detail on how the Braun J700 Multiquick 7 works. Everything is quite simple. When you turn on the juicer, a slight jerking of the case is immediately noticeable due to a very powerful engine. Small and medium apples and pears crawl into the loading compartment without any problems. A grater knife grinds fruits almost instantly, leaving only a small handful of cake from them. Processing 1 kg of apples takes no more than 5 minutes, which is incredibly convenient. He pressed and drank immediately.





As for the speeds, the first spin is done more carefully, but at the second speed, unprocessed pieces, and sometimes pieces, may begin to appear. This can be seen especially well after the filter is slightly healed. The pulp that remains after processing is quite small and resembles a kind of pulp. Some housewives prefer not to throw out the resulting cake, but to let it into the preparation of fritters. Waste-free production, so to speak.





Reviews

Reviews of the Braun J700 Multiquick 7 juicer show that this model is virtually devoid of flaws. The main disadvantage, according to users, is that when the mesh (filter) is clogged, the fruits or vegetables begin to grind worse, and inside, in the compartment for collecting pulp, large pieces and parts begin to accumulate. All this is treated with simple cleaning, so it is not very critical. Some other users complain that the cake (pulp) turns out to be a little damp, which indicates that the extraction is not completely complete. Unfortunately, nothing special can be done about it, the only thing is that at the first speed the juicer works much more efficiently.
