The capital of Algeria. Why is it worth going there?

Algeria is one of the largest states on the entire African continent. What are your first associations when you hear about Algeria? Of course, this is how the locals revere their religion. More than 90% of the population are Muslims. The capital of Algeria is famous for 3 mind-blowing architectural buildings, mosques: the Great Mosque (one of the first built in this city), the New Mosque, Jemaa-Quechua.





In addition, in Algeria you can see with your own eyes the ancient ruins of Roman, Phoenician, Byzantine cities. The history of Algeria originates from the colonization of the land with the French. Local residents started a struggle for the independence of their country. As a result, the capital of Algeria is now divided into two parts: the old and modern cities. Most of the modern part was rebuilt by the French. Here is the vast majority of universities.

By the way, education in Algeria is almost the best in the world. Training is conducted only according to the highest European standards.

Despite the fact that Algeria is a Muslim state, in the modern part of the city there are several church buildings. In addition, there are wide boulevards, theaters, museums.





The capital of Algeria is the historical part of the state. The world famous Turkish fortress is located here. In addition, the capital of Algeria is famous for 3 mosques, which, by the way, are also located in the old part of the city. The historical center of Algeria has preserved the old customs of the country. There are still old one-story buildings here, and the streets are narrow-narrow. In another way, this historical part of the city is called the Kasbah district. The traffic here also has a distinctive feature: you cannot move around on any vehicle here.

In the modern part of the city, you can safely travel by taxi. This type of transport in Algeria is almost the most affordable. On the streets all right next to the sidewalks are cars offering to transport passengers for a reasonable fee. The metro in Algeria has long ceased to function. You can visit it just to be appreciated as a historical monument of the city.

By the way, about the sights. Be sure to visit the Monument to the Fallen in a modern city. This monument was erected in honor of the memory of those killed for the independence of Algeria. In addition, the Kasby area is like one large monument. By visiting it, you will have a complete picture of the history of Algeria, of the religious movement.

Vegetable salads, shorba soup, couscous, grilled swordfish are the cuisine that Algeria is famous for. The capital is literally full of all kinds of restaurants, fast food establishments. As for shopping, in addition to European shopping centers in the modern part, in the old town you can find artisan shops and souvenir shops.



The capital of Algeria is a must-see place. Foreign currency exchange is prohibited throughout Algeria. In addition, loans are allowed only in the capital. If you are planning to visit this state on the African continent, then do not disregard the city of Algeria.
