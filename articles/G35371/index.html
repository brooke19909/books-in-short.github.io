LCD Monitors - Overview, Features, Specifications and Reviews

Buying a good and high-quality monitor is not as easy as it seems at first glance. When choosing, it is worth considering many different parameters, for example, brightness level, backlight type, resolution to diagonal ratio, refresh rate, etc. In addition, the choice is complicated by the large abundance of various models, among which there are extremely unsuccessful instances. In today's review, we will talk about several very good LCD monitors that you can safely recommend for purchase.

LG Flatron W1934S

So, the first model on the list will be the LG Flatron W1934S. Despite the fact that this monitor is already almost 10 years old, it can still be found on sale, although it is already much less common, but nonetheless. This LCD monitor will be a great purchase, especially for those who do not have a lot of money.

Equipment

Now a little about the configuration. The LG Flatron W1934S comes in a medium-sized standard cardboard box. On the packaging you can immediately get acquainted with the main technical characteristics and capabilities of the model. Inside the box, the user will find the actual 19-inch LCD monitor itself, a power cable, a VGA cable, instructions, a warranty card and a driver disk.

Description and specifications

Now a little about the monitor itself. Its diagonal is 19 inches, resolution - 1440x900. At the time of release, this was not the most popular resolution, and many users suffered for a long time trying to set it in the PC settings. The monitor is mounted on a special leg, installation on the wall is possible.

As for the display, it is matte and collects fingerprints, dust particles, etc. very well. The brightness margin is good - 300 cd / m2. In contrast, everything is more than standard - 1000: 1. Color reproduction is good, colors are closer to natural ones. Viewing angles are 170 degrees.

At the bottom of the monitor are buttons for quick setup, as well as switching from 16:10 to 4: 3 format. The power supply is built into the case, does not make much noise. Power consumption - 36 watts, and in standby mode 1 watts.

As noted in user reviews of this model, the LG Flatron W1934S has virtually no shortcomings, except for the power button indicator that is too bright.
