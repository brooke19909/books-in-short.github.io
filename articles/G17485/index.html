Herringbone pattern

We will begin to disassemble patterns for knitting with the option "Herringbone". It is very original. The pattern has a dense texture, so it can be used for knitting many warm things: pullovers, sweaters, hats, etc.





Knitting a pattern is absolutely not difficult, and you can dial an arbitrary number of loops. And, in fact, the whole process comes down to one algorithm: the spoke is inserted in two loops at once. A loop is output through them. The front one is for odd rows, and the wrong one is for even ones. Then one of the loops knitted in this way (right) is transferred to the right knitting needle. The left loop remains on the left, in order to subsequently repeat these actions already with it and the next loop. We knit the last unpaired loop in the row itself, respectively, to the current side - the front or the wrong side.

"Honeycombs"

In the patterns of knitting with knitting needles with patterns for beginners, it is worth adding the "Honeycomb" pattern.



Due to its loose texture, it is ideal for knitting scarves, sweaters and other products requiring softness from the canvas. Therefore, if you plan to knit a scarf or snood, try a honeycomb pattern for it. It fits in this way:

  • Row 1. Alternate to the end of the row the front loop and one untied with a crochet made before it.
  • Row 2. We knit together the front two loops, then make a crochet and remove one loop without knitting it. This sequence is repeated until the very edge loop of the row.
  • Row 3. We knit the facial loop, then remove one untied, after - another facial. So until the end of the series.
  • Row 4. We crochet, then remove the loop, and after - two loops together we knit the front. All this is repeated until the end of the series.
  • Row 5. We knit two loops of the front, then remove one without knitting.

Starting from the sixth row, we knit in the cycle the rows from the second to the fifth.
