How to get from Budapest to Vienna by train, bus, car, boat on the Danube

Distances between settlements in Europe are usually not too big. And especially by Russian standards. Therefore, many of our compatriots who have arrived as tourists in some European city often come to the decision to explore neighboring countries as well. For example, quite often Russians are interested in how to get from Budapest to Vienna.

The main ways

The capital of Hungary - Budapest - is, among other things, a very large transport hub. There are only three railway stations in this city. Of course, there is an airport in the capital of Hungary. It is located near the city. The city of Budapest itself is located on the Danube River, which is navigable. And therefore, a port was built here, including passenger ships.





Thus, tourists who decide to get from Budapest to Vienna on their own can use both rail and air transport. The capital of Austria, like the main city of Hungary, is located on the banks of the Danube. And this means that if you want to travel, tourists can choose the water mode of transport.

Of course, a bus service is established between these two cities. And of course, if desired, tourists can travel from Budapest to the capital of Austria and by car. Highways in both of these states are traditionally good.

Distance between cities

Both Budapest and Vienna are geographically located approximately in the middle of those countries whose capitals are. Hungary shares a common border with Austria in the west. There are no intermediate countries between these two states. Dimensions Austria and Hungary are small. The area of ‚Äã‚Äãthese states is smaller than even in many regions of Russia. Therefore, the distance from Budapest to Vienna is actually quite small. In a straight line, it is only about 220 km. But of course, due to various obstacles, tourists who decide to get to Austria from Hungary will have to travel a little longer.

So, for example, the Danube river between these two cities makes several small turns. For land transport, hills, ponds and forests can become an obstacle. Thus, the real total length of the route from Budapest to Vienna, depending on the chosen mode of transport, can be about 220-300 km.

Flight ticket

Air transport is a good answer to the question of how to get from Budapest to Vienna. Flights between these two cities are quite common. They can be organized by different companies - Airberlin, Lufthansa, Alitalia, etc. The route from Hungary to Austria, of course, is mainly only direct routes. However, in order to save money, tourists can buy a ticket to Vienna by plane and with a transfer. Direct flight to get to your destination from Budapest, travelers can literally 40-50 minutes. The total travel time when choosing a flight with a transfer is about 3 hours.

The cost of a flight from Budapest to Vienna depends primarily on the services of which particular company the tourist decided to use. The price of a travel document for a direct flight is usually 450-470 euros, with a transfer - 180-300 euros.

In order to buy a ticket to Vienna, tourists do not need to go to the Budapest airport. Travelers can also buy a travel document for an airplane flying to the Austrian capital, including online. All that is needed is to fill out a special form on the official website of the selected company. An electronic ticket purchased at such a site is then simply printed on a printer and presented at the Liszt Ferenc Airport. Tourists who decide to fly to Vienna from Hungary by plane never have any problems with tickets purchased via the Internet.





Budapest Airport: how to get there

The Liszt Ferenc Airport Terminal is located 16 km from the center of Budapest. You can get to it by train or minibus. In the first case, you first need to get to the Western station of the city, and then catch the train number 1. From the main bus station of the city, the airport can be reached by bus No. 200E or No. 93. Both of these methods are quite convenient. However, the easiest way, of course, is to get from the city to the place of boarding by taxi. Also, many tourists book a transfer minibus at the airport itself.

Budapest - Vienna Train: Travel Features

By rail, getting to the Austrian capital from Hungary is also quite simple. Only two types of trains run between the two countries - high-speed (Railjet) and regional. A budget trip takes about 4.5 hours in total. By high-speed train, tourists can reach the place in just 2.5 hours.

Tourists who decide to take the Budapest - Vienna train (Railjet) must pre-book a ticket. And experienced travelers advise you to do this as soon as possible. The more days left before dispatch, the cheaper the ticket for the speed train. The booking fee for a trip by rail in Hungary is usually not too high.

The answer to the question of how to get from Budapest to Vienna by regular train is also quite simple. Traveling on a regional train in this country is cheaper than on a speed train. In addition, such trains often stop in many cities less visited by tourists. So passengers of simple trains have the opportunity to look at original life in the outback of Austria and Hungary. You can usually buy a ticket for such a train right on the day of departure.

Railjet trains are just a great answer to the question of how to get from Budapest to Vienna. Such trains depart towards the Austrian capital several times a day. These high-speed trains offer passengers only three types of seats of different price categories: business class, 1st and economy. For customers who have bought the first two varieties of tickets, the convenience is also offered along the way, such as table setting for dinner. All meals at the end of the trip by passengers, however, must be paid. Travelers from economy class can, if desired, visit the dining car on the train.

Near each seat in the Railjet speed train there is a special outlet for Wi-Fi. The price of a ticket for such a train to Vienna from Budapest starts at about 20 euros.

Regional Compositions

Budget trains can go to Vienna either through Gyor or through Bratislava. In the first case, tourists have the opportunity to look at the unusually colorful center of the ancient city, designed in the Baroque style. Tourists usually take tickets on the second route only when they want to explore Bratislava and spend a day or two here.

Regional trains in Hungary are not at all like Russian long-distance trains or trains. In most cases, they are equipped not with shelves, but with soft seats installed in six pieces in one compartment. The seats are located in the wagons of such trains, three opposite each other. Between them, two comfortable tables are provided. The doors in all compartments of the budget train are glass. At small intermediate stations, such compositions usually cost no more than 2-5 minutes.
