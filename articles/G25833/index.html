Historical information

The problem of breathing under water has been of interest to man for a long time. It is impossible to say exactly in which year scuba gear was invented, but it was then that the artist developed a special diving kit, which consisted of a mask, two reed tubes and a diving bell made of cork. Air was supplied to the tubes through this bell, which floated on the surface of the water. Leonardo da Vinci came up with the famous J-shaped tube. Charles Spaulding and Maurice Furnes

Another historical figure who has contributed to the improvement of scuba design is Charles Spaulding. He was a pastry chef and lived in Edinburgh. But he was also an amateur engineer: Spaulding made many useful changes to the bell of divers. He died when diving in the Dublin Bay in 1783.

Maurice Furnes is already a Frenchman who invented respirators, gas masks and improved the underwater breathing apparatus. He equipped the helmets with a one-way valve, took part in the creation of autonomous equipment for diving under water.

Developments of Rukeroil and Deneyruz

Back in the mid-nineteenth century, Rukeroil and Deneiruz tried to create such an apparatus that would lower the pressure when immersed to a depth. They acted independently and were not familiar. Yves Cousteau and Ganyang

The young officer Cousteau married the daughter of the owner of Air Liquid Corporation. His wife's father provided financial assistance to Cousteau for research and introduced him to the company's full-time engineer. Together with Emil Ganyang, an engineer at the corporation, he worked on the creation of a scuba gear in which the breathing system automatically supplied air of the required pressure. Air supply occurred at any depth. It was this development that gave the diver comfort and the possibility of long swimming.





In 1943, equipment was tested on the Marna River. In the summer, Jacques-Yves Cousteau continued to test it already in sea water. After several more modifications, Cousteau brought to the condition the apparatus, which today is called scuba gear. And the name was patented - Aqua Lung.
