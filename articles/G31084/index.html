What is occupation?

In 1907, in the annex to the 4th Hague Convention, the main provisions were formulated on what occupation is, who are the occupiers. This is the armed forces of the enemy, temporarily located in the territory of another state. They are required to comply with international law. An act in 1907 established that the occupation authorities:

  • must respect the laws and customs, the personal property of citizens living in the occupied territory;
  • not to carry out repression, hijacking of civilians;
  • not involve ordinary citizens in military operations, the construction of defensive structures;
  • not to destroy intentionally cultural and historical monuments, works of art, the fruits of scientific activity.
