Characteristics of Neodymium Magnets

But let's first find out what magnets are. They appeared not so long ago. It was possible to buy magnets in the store from the nineties of the last century. They are made of neodymium, boron and iron. The main element, of course, is neodymium. This is the metal of the lantonoid group, with the help of which the magnets acquire tremendous adhesion. If you take two pieces of large size and pull them together, then it will be almost impossible to unhook them.

On sale mainly, of course, there are miniature species. In any gift shop you can find balls (or another form) of this metal. The high price of neodymium magnets is explained by the complexity of the extraction of raw materials and the technology of its production. If a ball with a diameter of 3-5 millimeters will cost only a few rubles, then for a magnet with a diameter of 20 millimeters and above, you will have to lay out 500 rubles or more.





Neodymium magnets are obtained in special furnaces where the process takes place without access of oxygen, in a vacuum or atmosphere with an inert gas. The most common are axial magnetization magnets in which the field vector is directed along one of the planes where the thickness is measured.

The characteristics of neodymium magnets are very valuable, but they can easily be ruined without the possibility of restoration. So, a strong blow can rob them of all their properties. Therefore, you should try to avoid falls. Also, different species have their own temperature limit, which varies from eighty to two hundred and fifty degrees. At temperatures above the limit, the magnet loses its properties.

Correct and accurate use is the key to maintaining quality for thirty years or more. Natural demagnetization is only one percent per year.

Ready-made vertically oriented wind generator

Wind turbines, especially in recent years, have renewed interest. New models appeared, more convenient and practical.





More recently, horizontal wind generators having three blades were mainly used. And the vertical species did not spread due to the heavy load on the bearings of the wind wheel, resulting in increased friction, absorbing energy.

But thanks to the use of the principles of magnetic levitation, a neodymium magnet wind generator began to be used precisely vertically oriented, with pronounced free inertial rotation. Currently, it has proven to be more effective than horizontal.

Easy start is achieved thanks to the principle of magnetic levitation. And thanks to the multipolarity, which gives the rated voltage at low speeds, it is possible to abandon the gearboxes completely.

Some devices are able to start work when the wind speed is only one and a half centimeters per second, and when it reaches only three to four meters per second, it may already be equal to the power generated by the device.

What could be a device of a stationary generator

It is known that an electromotive force through a wire is generated by changing a magnetic field. In the core of a stationary generator, magnetic flux is generated by electronic control, not mechanically. The generator controls the flow automatically, acting resonantly and consuming very low power. Its oscillations deflect the magnetic fluxes of iron or ferrite cores. The higher the oscillation frequency, the stronger the generator power. Trigger is realized by a short pulse to the generator.

Three or single phase

A neodymium generator is usually made three-phase. The single-phase design under load will work with vibration, since a constant current output will not be ensured, which will result in a step-like amplitude.

But with a three-phase system, constant power is guaranteed at any time thanks to phase compensation. Therefore, neither vibration will occur, nor hum. And work efficiency will be fifty percent higher than with one phase.

Coil winding and the rest of the assembly

Calculation of a neodymium magnet generator is mainly done by eye. But it is better, of course, to achieve accuracy. For example, for a slow-moving device, where the charging of the battery would begin to function at 100-150 rpm, it will take from 1000 to 1200 turns. The total number is divided by the number of coils. So many turns will be required in each of them. The coils are wound as thick as possible with the thickest wire, since with less resistance the current will be greater (with a high voltage, the resistance will take all the current).

Usually they use round, but it is better to wind coils of elongated shape. The inner hole should be equal to or larger than the diameter of the magnet. In addition, the optimal magnet will be in the form of a rectangle, and not a washer, since in the former the magnetic field is stretched in length, while in the latter it is concentrated in the center.

The stator thickness is made equal to the thickness of the magnets. For the form you can use plywood. At its bottom and on top of the coils, fiberglass is placed for strength. The coils are connected to each other, and each phase is brought out for connection then by a triangle or a star.

It remains to make a mast and a reliable foundation.
