USA, the city of San Francisco: description of the city, history, photo

The city of San Francisco is one of the largest cities in the United States. It is located on a peninsula between the bay of the same name and the Pacific Ocean. This is a real gem of the state of California, attracting tourists from all over the world, thanks to its beautiful nature and rich history, dating back to the time of the "gold rush". It is a many-faced city with contrasts and color. Let's take a virtual trip to California and explore the sights of San Francisco.

History of the city

San Francisco has a fascinating story. It began in 1776, when travelers from Spain landed on the Pacific coast. They entered the first pages in the history of the city of San Francisco. The Spanish preacher founded the Mission of St. Francis of Assisi, in whose honor the city was named. The Spaniards built a fort called the Golden Gate. At the beginning of the XIX century in the territory of modern California, which was considered Mexican at that time, gold deposits were found. A stream of gold miners poured into open fields. A settlement was established with the current Portsmouth Square and the first street called Calle de la Fundacion (Founder Street).

William Richardson, the first land grantee, built his home on the hill where San Francisco is currently located. Along Grant Avenue, between Clay and Washington, established the first residence. The settlement turned into a city called Yerba Buena, which is translated from Spanish as ‚Äúgood grass‚Äù.

This small town was not given much importance, but the situation changed when a gold mine was discovered. In 1848, during the Gold Rush, Yerba Buena was conquered by the Americans and later became known as San Francisco. It has become the largest city on the Pacific coast. This is the story of the name of the city of San Francisco. True, in addition to its official name, it is called the "city by the bay" and the "city of fogs." The city received its second nickname because of strong summer fogs descending on the city, when the hot air currents in the central part of the mainland collide with the cold ones brought from the Pacific Ocean.

Climate

The terrain on which San Francisco is located is hilly. The water surrounding the city has a significant impact on weather conditions. Among major cities in the United States, the climate in San Francisco is considered the coolest and at the same time the most comfortable. The city has a Mediterranean climate with mild winters, warm summers, and slight temperature fluctuations throughout the year.

The coldest month is January. The temperature at this time in San Francisco does not drop below +8 ¬∞ C. September is considered hot with temperature fluctuations of +13 ... + 21 ¬∞ . In the summer months there is almost no rain, but frequent rains from November to April. Almost no snow falls.

The city of San Francisco is located on 74 hills (allegedly), of which 47 are named. One of the attractions of the city is Lombard Street, the most winding street in the world, designed and built in the 20s of the last century. It is located on Russian Hill. A Popular Place in San Francisco - Chinatown

The largest and most famous among Chinatown USA is located in Chinatown San Francisco. It houses the largest Chinese community located outside of Asia. It originated in the mid-nineteenth century, from immigrants who arrived from the Chinese province of Guangdong. The Chinese, wherever they go, always organized a "city in a city." Entering this area, tourists feel the presence of Asia: shops with inscriptions in the Cantonese language, most of the buildings have pagoda-shaped roofs, all lampposts are decorated with dragons, and the streets with lanterns. There are many cafes and delicious Chinese restaurants serving fortune cookies.

Today's Chinatown was rebuilt after the San Francisco earthquake in 1906, and its architecture is a strange combination of the foundations of Edward and Chinese details. It is traditionally believed that a pair of Chinese lion guardians provide protection; they are common in front of Chinese imperial palaces, government offices and temples. The gate has three entrances. The central one is for high-ranking people, and the other two - located on both sides of the central one - are for ordinary people.

In the quarter there are cultural places for visiting tourists: the Museum of the Chinese Historical Society of America and the Chinese Cultural Center.

Tourism

The city in the bay continues to grow, annually opening the doors to thousands of new residents from around the world. It is truly a vibrant, diverse, and welcoming place to live in America. The city of San Francisco is characterized by extremely low unemployment. Tourism is considered to be one of the main sectors of the economy of this metropolis, so you can always get a job in the tourism business.

What attracts tourists to San Francisco? Most likely not only historical sights, but also a favorable mild climate, the uniqueness and originality of the city. More than 17 million tourists come here for excursions and vacations. San Francisco is in fifth place in the United States for visiting foreigners.

The city is known for its iconic sights, such as the Golden Gate Bridge and the bridge across the Bay Bridge, restaurants with unique cuisine in different parts of the city. The city's attractions

San Francisco has a unique world symbol - the Golden Gate Bridge. It was considered the longest span bridge from its completion in 1937 until 1964. It connects San Francisco with the Marin County and is a total of 3.2 km (2.7 miles). This is the most photographed bridge in the world.

The location of the Golden Gate Bridge is also within walking distance of many other tourist destinations such as Fort Point, Fort Baker and Presidio.

There is much to go for those who are interested in historical and cultural values. These are museums and cultural centers. These include the Museum of Modern Arts, the center of art and culture of Asia, the Jewish Community Center, the Memorial Opera House.

In addition, in the Mission District, you can spend hours walking the streets and viewing painted buildings, walking around art studios, and visiting exhibition venues. The Dolores park area is always full of people. Moreover, it has recreation areas, conditionally divided into hipster, family and gay zones. Depending on the time of rest in the Mission, carnivals (in May), large street fairs (in March), as well as a parade in April in honor of Cesar Chavez are held.

Mysterious Alcatraz Island

Alcatraz Island is considered one of the most visited places in the port city of San Francisco. Why do travelers from all over the world try to get to the island? And the place for him was determined on the island of Alcatraz (translated from Spanish "rock") in the bay of San Francisco, to direct the ships passing through the Golden Gate. But not only the lighthouse was built on the island. Government officials decided that the tiny island, nicknamed ‚ÄúThe Rock,‚Äù would be the perfect place for a federal penitentiary.
