Net Wage Calculation: Gross and Net

Consider how salaries are determined in Germany after taxes.

Net salary is defined as the amount that remains in the hands of an employee after deducting social expenses and taxes. These social security contributions are paid by the employer to the responsible authorities. The amount of contributions to the state is the difference between the gross and net wages.

The average income in Germany for full-time employees is about 40,000 euros per year. However, social contributions and taxes of about 15,000 euros are deducted from this value in the calculation of net wages. The level of social security and taxation is often criticized and especially difficult for the middle class. Particular attention is paid to payroll tax, as a form of income collection. Thus, the average pay to the working population in Germany, taking into account contributions, is about 25,000 euros.

The country has a progressive scale of taxation on salaries, that is, the more payments to employees, the higher the amount of contributions to the state.

Rates can be varied. The percentage of taxes in Germany starts at 14% and ends at 45%.

Calculation by the employer

The German tax system implies that the net payment is calculated by the employer independently. It is he who transfers the social security and health insurance contributions to the responsible authority. Tax deductions are also paid to the local inspection by the company where the employee works. Gross and net salaries are indicated in statements with social and tax contributions paid. To calculate the net amount, the employer uses specialized computer programs and transfers these values ‚Äã‚Äãto the payroll.

Salary class 1

Consider how much tax in Germany on salary is paid in class 1.

For tax classes 1 and 4, the same deductions apply. This category is intended for single or divorced workers. This group of income tax includes even those who are married, who permanently live separately from their spouse or have a spouse who lives abroad.

Employees whose husband / wife died before January 1, 2014 also have tax class 1 from 2015. The same applies to employees who live in a registered partnership or are subject to a limited income tax. Again, deductions from tax class 1 are identical to those from fourth-class income tax.

Consider how much taxes are paid on salaries in Germany in the second class.

The second class from the list is intended for employees who are one of the parents. They are entitled to a benefit, therefore they belong to tax class 2.

Salary Class 4

In the fourth grade, both spouses must receive wages, they cannot permanently live separately from each other and must live in Germany.

The sixth and last salary class in the table is intended for employees who receive income from several employers at the same time. The employee must be deducted the tax by the employer paying the lower amount.

Payroll tax and insurance rates

Income tax of various classes is partially related to whether the employee is legally or individually insured. For employees who are subject to compulsory insurance, deductions for medical insurance in the first of the tax classes are 14.6 percent. Individually insured workers usually have to pay higher percentages in Germany.

It should be noted that in this country there is also a non-taxable minimum of 10,000 euros per person per year. For example, if the total family income does not exceed 18,000 euros per year, they are exempt from all types of payments. That is, the principle is this: the more a person earns, the more he pays.

How does the tax office calculate the amount of income tax?

The tax inspectorate has the task of collecting the amounts of payments in favor of the state. She must ensure that no mistakes are made that result in the state refusing the income to which it is entitled. Thus, the payroll tax is designed to withhold amounts from the wages of the working population. The state receives part of the deductions due to it directly from the source - the employer. He is also liable to the treasury for excessively small amounts of withheld and paid income tax. The reverse situation is also possible when the employee has a claim to the overpayment inspection, which also requires confirmation.

How to return excessive payroll tax?

The employer calculates the amount of taxes in Germany according to two components: official employment tax tables (for allowances, lump sums, etc.) and personal individual tax benefits (for example, class, child allowances). At the end of the year, excessively collected amounts can be returned on the declaration. You can use special software or hire an accountant to resolve this issue on the preparation of the declaration.

If an employee does not want to file a declaration, he should at least ensure that his deductions are accurate and that there are no situations of over-payment of amounts.
