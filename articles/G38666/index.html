Metal detector setup

On the metal detector circuit, certain nodes are marked, which are taken into account, since during further assembly it will be necessary to focus on them. It may also be needed during the metal detector setup process.

The emission of current oscillations by the generator is carried out after connecting the transmitting coil to it. Such oscillations come out of the MC1 chip in the form of a meander.

The current induced by the TX and creating the field is transmitted through the receiving coil. The search coil is balanced with TX along the generated field: in other words, the RX field is subtracted from the TX field. For this purpose, a compensation coil CX is used. Depending on the sensors, its presentation changes: in the case of the DD CX sensor, the coil is virtual, in the ‚ÄúRING‚Äù sensor the CX is real. It is connected in such a way that the direction of current flow in it is opposite to the receiving coil. The balancing of RX and TX is achieved by rewinding from the compensation coil.

The oscilloscope controls the balance, due to which the minimum amplitude is set in all positions of the handle. One end of the compensation coil goes to the production of the tuning loop, which is activated after the amplitude reaches a certain point at which it begins to grow again. TX and RX must be pre-tuned in frequency, while TX should be higher than RX by 100 Hz. You can tune all the coils to the desired frequency by connecting them to the Terminator 3 metal detector generator and the oscilloscope.

You do not need to tune the CX in frequency. When a metal object appears under the sensor, the balance is disturbed, which provokes the current entering the RX, which is then fed to the preamplifier and a synchrodetector, which captures the phases of the incoming signal and outputs them to the amplification channels. In the latter, all the obtained parameters are amplified and fed to the comparator MC8, which compares the received signal levels and activates the sound generator.

The principle of operation of almost all metal detectors is similar to each other, with the exception of some nuances. In most cases, they affect the detuning of the device from the ground. In the case of the Terminator M metal detector, phase detuning.





Checking the instrument board

After soldering all the parts of the circuit, the metal detector printed circuit boards are checked. This is done in order to verify the quality of the solder circuit and its performance.

Verification is performed as follows:

  • The printed circuit board of the metal detector is thoroughly washed out from traces of flux left after soldering. It is advisable to remove all residues, since in the future they can cause breakdowns and malfunctions.
  • The board power is turned on without activating the sensor.
  • The sensitivity knob is twisted until a steady sound signal appears from the speaker.
  • To interrupt the speaker signal, just touch the sensor connector with your finger. Interruption of the emitted sound when touched indicates that the metal detector board has been soldered correctly.
  • After turning on the power, the LED blinks and goes off. When the power is turned off, the diode lights up and gradually fades.




Battery Discharge Indication

When the battery is discharged, the metal detector emits sound signals at regular time intervals. This is accompanied by continuous burning of the LED and a sharp decrease in the sensitivity of the sensors.

The frequency settings of the detector are made using the cable with which the device will be used in the future. The cable length remains unchanged after making all the necessary frequency settings.

Metal Detector "Terminator Trio"

"Terminator Trio" - a two-tone metal detector, equipped with a DD-coil with dimensions of 250 x 300 mm. It is equipped with four settings modes - "Sensitivity", "Volume", "Discrimination" and "Ground Balance" - and a switch between non-ferrous and non-ferrous metals.

Benefits

The advantage of the Terminator Trio metal detector is the confident identification of non-ferrous metal objects. The device finds non-ferrous metal in 85% of all operations, the remaining 15% falls on iron and rusty objects.

Another plus is the absence of false positives. Many analogs react to the edges of dug holes, grass stems or small wires, than the Terminator Trio does not sin.





disadvantages

The only minus of the metal detector is a poor definition of rusty iron. In almost all situations when the device gives a dirty signal, that is, mixing black with an admixture of color or, conversely, color with an admixture of black, there is a rusty metal object.

Of course, this drawback can simply be ignored, but there is a chance of losing some of the finds due to an incorrect signal. You can only distinguish between a clear color signal and a dirty signal when gaining experience with a metal detector.
