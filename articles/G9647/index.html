Modern account system or where Arabic numerals were invented

Originally, mathematics used Roman numerals, of which there were 14, and which did not have a zero, but soon the numbers began to be depicted in the form of letters, since for many peoples they had some hidden meaning. It would seem that with the invention of numbers and the decimal system of counting, the first hours should have appeared. However, it is known that the first hours were used in Babylon more than three thousand years ago, they were a dial with twelve divisions along the edges and a rod in cent. This watch was sunny, so when the sun moved in the sky, the shadow of the rod fell on the corresponding number on the dial. Such watches were used in the daytime, at nightfall they were replaced by a water clock, which is an earthenware vessel with water flowing out of it one drop at a time. The vessel had divisions on the walls, therefore, with a decrease in the water level, one could see which division it stopped at, thus determining what time it was.
