The first time the title "Mother Heroine" was awarded October 27, 1944. This title was awarded to 14 Soviet women. Mom-heroine number 1 was A.S. Aleksakhina. All her eight sons were at the front, 4 of them died, 2 died of wounds, having already come from the front. The second order bearer was the Tula housewife M.M. Ryzhkova. Few people know that A.A. occupies a special place in the history of heroine mothers. Derevskaya. She is the only mother heroine in the USSR who raised 48 children! And the basis of the family was not kinship, but love and compassion. At the time that the First World War went on, she worked in a hospital. There, her fate brought together the Red Guard Emely Derevsky. Soon they got married, but Emelyan was shot by the White Guards.



In 1918, young Alexandra became an adoptive mother. Her adoptive first-born was a ten-year-old Timothy - the brother of the deceased husband. The second adopted child was also a boy, Derevskaya picked him up right on the street. The baby was lying wrapped in diapers near the body of the deceased mother. The autobiography of the Derevskikh family, like a mirror, reflected all the tragic events experienced by the Soviet state for half a century. In the interval between the Civil War and the war against fascist Germany, Derevskaya Alexandra raised 14 children.

In the period 1941-1945. 17 new Leningrad orphans and 18 children from other parts of the USSR acquired a new parental home. In 1950, 36 children were brought up in the Derevskikh house. All children from the Derevsky family grew up as good people. The legendary mother heroine died in 1959, she was 57 years old. The following epitaph is engraved on her grave: "You are our conscience, our prayer is mother."

Medals of the USSR, maternity medal at sunset of the Soviet Union

The last time in the history of the Soviet Union was the assignment of a title to mother heroines November 14, 1991 (by decree of President M.S. Gorbachev). Medals of the USSR (medal of motherhood of the first and second degrees) also went down in history. In just 47 years, 431,000 mothers have been awarded this order.

In the 90s in Russia, mothers of many children were awarded the Order of Merit to the Fatherland or the Order of Friendship. In 2009, the Order of Parental Glory was established. They are awarded to parents raising 4 and more children.
