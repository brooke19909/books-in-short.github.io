Theater in Epidaurus, Greece: photos, reviews, tips before visiting

In the southern part of the Balkan Peninsula is the Greek periphery of the Peloponnese, known for the wonderful ancient city in the north-east - Epidavros. In ancient times, it was the center of the region of the same name, and now it is known for its old ruins and a temple. It is here that a huge number of tourists come here every year to take a look at the Epidaurus Theater. In Greece, he is one of the few surviving and, importantly, operating. It has exceptional aesthetics and acoustics.





Greece is a relatively small country and can be compared in size with the Orenburg or Kirov region. Going round it yourself will not be particularly difficult, given the fact that the transport network is well developed. We will tell you about how to get to Epidaurus, what to see in the ancient city, and also give some tips that are useful before visiting the theater.

About city

The history of the city of Epidavros is rooted in the Mycenaean era. In the old days, he was quite active and had a certain political influence. Colonies were founded on the islands of Kos, Aegina, Tenedos and Nisyros. Decline occurred in the sixth century BC, when the trade conducted by Epidaurus began to decline. Power passed into the hands of the oligarchy, which in the Peloponnesian Wars acted as an ally of Sparta. As a result, the city was repeatedly raided by the Athenians and their allies. In the future, he completely disappeared into obscurity, retaining its significance only as a port at the great temple and Asclepius sanatorium (the ruins in the photo below).





His cult first appeared in these places in the 6th century BC. e. After a hundred years, numerous pilgrims rushed into the city, praying for healing. Already in the 4th century BC. e. the sanctuary at Epidaurus has become one of the most revered and largest in all of Greece. It flourished under the Romans. The pagan temple was closed in the 4th century during the reign of Christianity Theodosius I the Great.

Now Epidavros is a small fishing village. An ordinary tourist can get to him in three ways.

Theater in Epidaurus: how to get there by bus?

Epidaurus has a rather favorable geographical position. The distance from the country's capital city of Athens is only 130-140 km. Nearby are Corinth (60 km) and Nafplio (30 km). You can get to Epidaurus from Athens by public transport. To clarify the availability of tickets and their price should be at bus stations. During the festival season, a special bus travels from Athens. He takes tourists to a concert, and then takes him back to the city. The cost of such a ticket is 23 euros, and the journey takes about two hours.

In addition, you can get to the theater in Epidaurus (photo from the text of the article) on your own or rented vehicles.

From the history of the theater

Description of the theater in Epidaurus should begin with the phrase "very ancient." The antique building was erected between approximately 340 and 330 BC. The creative idea belonged to the famous ancient Greek architect Polycletus the Younger. This was possible to establish thanks to the records of the writer and geographer Pausanias. The theater was erected for entertainment by nobles from Asklepion - a temple erected in honor of the god of medicine, who also served as a hospital. It was believed that theatrical performance favorably affects the physical and mental health of patients.

One of the most ancient theaters of ancient Greece in Epidaurus accommodated more than fifteen thousand spectators and was divided into two parts. There were 55 rows in total, of which 21 were intended for ordinary people, and 34 lower for nobles and priests. The diameter of the building is 116 m, while it has amazing acoustics. In the photo, the plan of the theater, with the words parodos denotes the exits, skene - stage, orchestra - orchestra pit.

Theater today

The theater was opened as a result of excavations conducted by archaeologist P. Cavadias in 1870-1926. Just a few years after his rebirth, in 1938, he already received the first guests. The tragedy of Sophocles Electra was set. In the early 50s of the last century, restoration work was organized in the theater. Since 1955, it became the venue for the annual theater festival. Every summer, performances are held on its stage. Some of them were attended by the greatest Greek and world artists, including opera singer Maria Callas.

The Ancient Theater at Epidaurus is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Hellenic Festival in Epidaurus

The Hellenic festival in Epidaurus is perhaps one of the most famous in the theater world. For a month, a small cozy village turns into a large platform and receives a huge number of tourists. The festival starts on the last Friday of June and lasts until the first August Saturday. Theater groups from around the world come together with plays by the most famous playwrights. Despite the fact that all representations are staged in Greek or Ancient Greek, the audience is going international. Art knows no boundaries, especially when it is created within the walls of an ancient theater, and myriads of stars shine in the sky. The viewer is important atmosphere of what is happening.

Performance tickets





If you want to attend a performance at the Epidaurus Theater, then you should think about it in advance. Tickets have an affordable price, and therefore are snapped up by fans of theatrical art, like hot cakes. Depending on the venue, place and performance, the cost ranges from 10 to 50 euros. There is a discount program for students, seniors and children. You can buy tickets at the box office of the city, in Athens, or on the official website of the festival, paying with their credit card. In Epidaurus, Saturday and Friday are considered theater days.

By the way, a regular visit to the theater during the daytime, along with a visit to the exposition of the archaeological museum costs 12 euros. The place is very popular with tourists, so it is better to plan an excursion in the early morning.

Museum near the theater

Literally a few steps from the theater is the archaeological museum of the city, which offers its guests a rather interesting exhibition. In one of the halls, a view of the classical tholos burial was recreated. Of the most famous and notable exhibits, it is worth noting the column capitals created by the famous ancient Greek sculptor Poliklet, a large collection of ancient surgical instruments found in the sanctuary of Asclepius, gift inscriptions, stone images.
