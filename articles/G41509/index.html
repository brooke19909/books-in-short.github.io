Gaspra, Crimea: rest, hotels, motels

In the luxurious lowlands with oak and juniper forests, 10 km northwest of Yalta lies the magnificent village of Gaspra. Well-known Russians Crimean resort, part of the city Yalta district.

A special conglomerate includes such popular recreation areas as Koreiz, Miskhor, Gaspra. Crimea is rich in magnificent resorts, and every vacationer can choose the one where he will be more comfortable.





From the history of the village

Gaspra (Crimea) appeared in the 5th century BC. In those days, these lands were inhabited by nomadic Taurian tribes. Until now, their necropolises have been preserved here. The village began to actively develop at the beginning of the 19th century. Cottages began to be built on the coast, the local population was engaged in agriculture, tobacco growing and viticulture. The village received special popularity after the Second World War. At this time, he became an urban-type settlement.

Climate of Gaspra

The village is reliably protected from the cold and piercing winds by the Ai-Petri massif. The climate in these places is subtropical, sub-Mediterranean. In January, the average air temperature is +4 degrees, in July - +25. The beach season lasts from May to the end of October.

Gaspra (Crimea): rest

If you want to combine an excellent vacation by the warm sea with treatment and rehabilitation, then you should definitely come to the village of Gaspra. Crimea sanatoriums on its territory has the most diverse, but in this place they are especially good.





Sanatorium "Pearl"

A modern, well-equipped medical facility, with a developed diagnostic and medical base. It is located in the picturesque Ai-Todor Valley. This is the Pearl Rehabilitation Center. Crimea, Gaspra in particular, has a surprisingly mild climate. Thanks to this, the parks in these places are luxurious. It is in them that cottages and dormitories of the sanatorium are buried, the territory of which reaches 17.8 hectares.

Sanatorium "Pearl" is located near the famous architectural complex "Swallow's Nest." It takes 508 vacationers at the same time. Meals are provided three times a day. In addition, guests can use all types of diets, which is especially important in the presence of diseases.

The sanatorium has its own well-equipped small-pebble beach, with terraces in two tiers. When checking in, you must have a passport (for adults), a birth certificate (for children), a spa card.





Sanatorium "Dnepr"

This well-known medical institution is located in the village of Gaspra (Crimea), in the ancient park of the Charaks estate, which was owned by Grand Duke G. M. Romanov.

In this sanatorium for the first time on the peninsula, a technique was developed, and later widely used, for the impact of certain parts of the park and their various combinations for the treatment of certain diseases. According to visitors' reviews, it can be concluded that the Dnipro sanatorium is the best place to stay in the village of Gaspra (Crimea).

Here you can relax and improve your health all year round. The sanatorium has three dormitory buildings, a 4-time food system, custom-made diet menu, medical and vegetarian diets are practiced . It offers vacationers restaurants and cafe-bars, with an excellent selection of drinks, fruits and pastries.

There is a well-equipped beach, which is located at a distance of 150 meters from the buildings. Vacationers go down to the beach in a comfortable elevator, which is located in a rock.

Haraks Palace and Park

The complex is built in a special style that is different from all existing ones. Built by a wonderful master of his craft, N.P. Krasnov, the palace today is striking in its beauty. The palace received its name in honor of the Roman fortress, which was located in these places in the 3rd century BC.

The scale of the project was amazing even during the years of its creation - 46 rooms, a chapel, a church, a greenhouse, a large stable, a kitchen, a park, water supply and sewage systems. Today the Haraks Palace is one of the dormitory buildings of the Dnepr sanatorium.

Church of st. Nina

This structure was created by N.P. Krasnov, an architect known in those days, on the estate of Grand Duke G.M. Romanov. Construction began in 1906, completed in 1908. G. M. Romanov decided to build a temple in the style of architecture of Armenia and Georgia. The Grand Duke approved his decision. The mosaic for the temple was made by an artist from Venice A. Salviati. She adorns the entrance to the building.

The name of the church is directly related to Princess Nina Georgievna, the eldest daughter of the Grand Duke. She was ill with diphtheria at the age of four and survived thanks to an operation she had done in Hamburg on August 6, 1905. This is the day of the Transfiguration of the Lord. The church is named after the saint who performed the miracle of the princess's recovery.
