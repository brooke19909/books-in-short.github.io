How to make a TV out of paper: two simple options

The puppet world fascinates with its similarity to the life of people. Lovers of Barbie and other plastic beauties are trying to recreate a small toy universe that will look like the surrounding environment. Dolls are built at home, they make furniture, they invent household appliances.

Doll life

In the dollhouse must have a TV. How in the modern world without a blue box? Making a TV out of paper, just like a real one, is not difficult. A golden-haired beauty will have this little thing in a matter of minutes, if her masters are armed with the necessary materials and a small amount of patience.

For barbie

There are schemes for making a television out of paper. It is enough to cut them along the contour and glue the parts together.

The finished model must be varnished or with PVA glue and left for some time until completely dry.

There is another way according to the invention of the "blue box" for the little princess.

In order for a Barbie doll to have a TV in the house, the owner needs to get to work. To start, prepare the materials for work:

  • Matchbox;
  • scissors;
  • paper;
  • paints.

Following simple instructions, even children can make a TV on their own:

  1. From the matchbox you need to cut a window in the middle of the front part.
  2. Put the lid on the box and paint it in the desired color. It is not necessary to choose a dark color scheme - blue and pink TV will fit perfectly into the bright palette of the dollhouse.
  3. Roll up some small balls from plasticine - these will be buttons for the blue screen. Stick them on the side. Using the same principle, make legs for the TV.
  4. There are several options for a screen image: attach a specific picture or an animation magnet to the ‚ÄúTV‚Äù. But it can be done differently. Cut a long strip, divide it into small squares and in each part draw various plots or a character who periodically changes his pose.
  5. We insert such a tape into the window window from the "first frame" and slowly pull it towards ourselves. The image moves, the TV "works."

We carry out a television report

For a noisy children's game, there is another way to make a television out of paper. It will be a large, voluminous blue screen, with which the children will be able to tell the news themselves, conduct shows and various programs.

To invent a large TV, you will need the following items:

  • cardboard box;
  • wire;
  • buttons
  • scissors, clerical knife;
  • hot glue;
  • paints.

The procedure is quite simple:

  1. Mark the TV screen on the box and carefully cut it with a stationery knife.
  2. Color the TV with bright paint. Attach the wire in the form of an antenna on top.
  3. Glue the buttons on the side of the screen. These will be the channel switches. Attach buttons to a piece of rectangular cardboard so that the toy TV has a remote control.
  4. TV is ready. To conduct the broadcast, remove the back wall of the box or the bottom.

Now the baby and his dolls have their own TV.
