Fund "Mercury": feedback from members of the fund

The Mercury Fund, reviews of which are almost always positive, is one of the modern investment- type innovative projects . The program combines the advantages of the best enterprises, the main purpose of which is to enrich their participants. The creators of the project Dmitry Vasadin and Arthur Zalyubovsky argue that the system is stable in terms of obtaining passive income. It is fundamentally different from the many other similar systems, as it is completely devoid of flaws that can lead to collapse.

Solution of problems





The Mercury Foundation, which only positive reviews continue to appear, has a unique policy. He takes funds from his depositors and accrues compound interest on them . In this case, the down payment is not returned to the participants of the system. This approach has eliminated the problem associated with the massive withdrawal of money, with the outflow of capital in times of crisis. When the clients of the investment company begin to actively withdraw money, this becomes the reason for their shortage. As a result, payments cease, and the company goes bankrupt.

The participants in the system are people who are aimed not at making a one-time profit, but at creating a constant passive income. People who join the project do not expect the return of their primary capital along with interest. They expect a steady income over the next year. Interest is accrued every day. Money is withdrawn every week. There is a prerequisite for participation in the program - this is a monthly withdrawal of funds. This approach eliminates the likelihood of peak loads on the system, which guarantees its intelligibility and stability.

Sincerity of the creators of the project





Dmitry Vasadin and Arthur Zalyubovsky openly talk about their creation under the name "Mercury". Project Bankruptcy Information

In 2014, very interesting information appeared that Mercury, a mutual assistance fund, reviews of which throughout its entire existence were often good, turned out to be a financial pyramid that collapsed. The report said that depositors gave their money to the fund, while not receiving any guarantees for accruing interest on them. In fact, a certain donation procedure was carried out. The project founders promised to pay at least 1% of the deposit amount every day for a year. 360% against 20‚Äì25% of bank deposits turned out to be very attractive. There were no problems with attracting clients to the partnership. The main participants in the system were residents of Russia and Ukraine, Kazakhstan and Poland, the Czech Republic and Switzerland. The founders of the pyramid had many accounts of international type in different currencies around the world. So, on August 25, 2014, the organization was declared bankrupt. However, this state of affairs did not persist for long. Today the fund is afloat again and continues to actively advertise its activities.

A flexible partnership scheme is working flawlessly so far, but how long this state of affairs will last is very difficult to answer.
