History and location of the town of Auckland

So, for starters, we will talk about the history of the city of Auckland in New Zealand.

Auckland is the largest and most highly urbanized community in the country, with more than 32% of the population. The city is located on the North Island of New Zealand on a huge plateau. Auckland is surrounded by several mountain ranges, three sea bays and a huge number of islands. The town is located on the territory of the Auckland volcanic region, there are 49 extinct volcanoes, which last erupted more than 100 thousand years ago.

The first people appeared here more than 800 years ago. They were called Maori. This territory was suitable for life, therefore, at the peak of development, about 20,000 people lived here, but by the time the Europeans arrived, the number of Maori was small. The recession was due to numerous tribal wars and migrations. Auckland City (New Zealand) - pictured below.

The first European visited here in 1769, his name was James Cook. The city was formed in 1840 by captain William Hobson after the signing of the Waitangi agreement. Auckland began to develop rapidly, since it was through him that the migratory flow of people from the UK and Australia passed.

During the Second World War, the largest naval base in England was located on the territory of the city.

Weather in Auckland

Next, we will talk about the climate in Auckland (New Zealand).

The city is located in the zone of subtropical oceanic climate, which is characterized by warm, humid summers and cool rainy winters. It is worth saying that Auckland is the warmest and sunniest city in all of New Zealand. The minimum air temperature that was recorded here is -0.5 degrees. The absolute maximum in the city is approximately +31 degrees. It rains all year round, peak in winter. 137 rainy days are recorded here annually. This weather is due to the proximity to the ocean. Snow is a rare occurrence for Auckland that happens once every 50-100 years. The last time he fell out in 2011, but melted right in flight.

You can always come to town. The best time, of course, is December-March, when summer comes here. There is almost no rain at this time, and the sun shines incredibly bright.

Sights of Auckland

What to see in Auckland (New Zealand)?

  • Auckland Harbor Bridge

It was built in the middle of the last century. This attraction in Auckland (New Zealand) connects two parts of the city. The Auckland Harbor Bridge is a huge, massive structure that impresses with its beauty and grandeur, it is especially beautiful at night.

  • Golden Mile Quarter. A street where several styles combine perfectly. Here you can see unusual houses in a modern style, and stunning buildings of a classic style.
  • Lookout on Mount Eden. This attraction in Auckland (New Zealand) can show you stunning views of the entire city. It is better to climb the platform in the evening, when the sky is lit up with pink-red flowers of sunset.

What to see in the town?

So, we continue to talk about the best sights of Auckland.

  • Sky Tower. Multifunctional building, which brings together many companies, entertainment and restaurants. Moreover, for $ 30 you can visit the observation deck, which offers a wonderful view of the city.
  • Auckland Museum.

This is the best museum in New Zealand. Here you can plunge into the history of the city and country. To visit this attraction in Auckland (New Zealand), you will have to pay about $ 50 per ticket.

  • Vector arena. The largest and incredibly beautiful arena of the country, where many world stars performed.
  • Auckland Zoo. It contains the rarest species of animals, endemic, living only in this territory. The park is divided into 11 thematic zones. This attraction of Auckland (New Zealand) can be visited daily from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
  • The famous university. This educational institution was opened in 1883, now it occupies 82nd place in the ranking of the best in the world. It has 8 faculties and 6 campuses. The University of Auckland (New Zealand) is the largest in the country.

Best beaches in Auckland

Auckland is located next to the ocean, so there are many beautiful beaches.

  1. Mission Bay. One of the best beaches in Auckland (New Zealand). There is black volcanic sand and untouched nature.
  2. Muriwai. Great beach. If you want a break from people and noise, then you definitely need to come to Muriwai. It is amazingly beautiful. In addition, here you can be alone with nature and yourself.
  3. St. Helier's Bay. This beach is within the city. If you want to meet a beautiful sunset on the ocean, then you definitely need to come to St. Helier's Bay.
  4. Whatipu Beach. A wild and quiet beach with volcanic sand in the vicinity of the city, here you can enjoy the beauty and majesty of nature and mountain ranges.
  5. Mathesons Bay.

A wide beach on the bay, which is surrounded by forests and mountain ranges. Mathesons Bay is a stunningly beautiful place where only the water rumbles, which breaks on high cliffs.

Security and People in Auckland

New Zealanders, in particular, Aucklandians, are very friendly and kind people. It is customary to greet even strangers on the street. Few know the Russian language, but everyone will try to help.

There are no dangerous snakes or poisonous insects. Only sand fleas can bring a little discomfort. There are no infections and diseases, so you can eat even in the smallest and most modest cafes.

Auckland is one of the most liveable cities in the world. It is usually quiet and calm. True, sometimes various meetings and processions take place, where people ask to save the whales, the forest. South Auckland is the most unfavorable area, tourists here are better not to linger in the dark.

Smoking and drugs are prohibited. True, marijuana smokers are not uncommon, but the police are calm about them.
