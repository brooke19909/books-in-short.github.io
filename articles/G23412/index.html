Where are iPhone backups stored?





Many years ago mobile phones entered our life tightly and today they can replace photo albums with family photos, notebooks, music players and many other things that were previously so necessary. It is not surprising that the safety of all the information contained in the smartphone excites each of its owners. Therefore, it makes sense to periodically duplicate them on another medium in order to ensure their existence, regardless of the fate of the gadget. Manufacturers of the famous Apple company in this regard tried to surround their users with maximum comfort, offering a choice of two methods of copying data. So, we’ll explore where iPhone backups are stored with the possibility of their further recovery.



It's no secret that “apple” products are distinguished by a special “intelligence and ingenuity”. The developers of these gadgets are doing everything possible so that, despite the complexity of the created devices, working with their creations for users is as simple and easy as possible. Another distinguishing feature of all inventions operating on the famous iOs platform is their closed nature. For example, the storage methods typical of other companies' gadgets can never be repeated using Apple devices. Therefore, before you figure out where iPhone backups are stored, consider how you can copy data from this device.

The first backup program that users use most often is called iTunes. In addition to the fact that this creation is an online store of music and video files designed specifically for the iOs system, it is also a place where backup copies of iPhone and other “apple” devices are stored. In order to transfer information from your gadget to iTunes, it will be enough to start the process of synchronizing software installed on a PC with an Apple gadget pre-connected to the PC using a USB cable. It is worth remembering that synchronization is possible only with active access to the Internet.



In addition, iPhone backups can be created using iCloud. In this case, the files stored in the “cloud” service will be automatically updated from the device when there is access to the network and when connected to the charger. However, in this case, products that were not previously purchased from the iTunes Store do not sync. It is also worth noting that the most reliable copying, which includes all the contents of the gadget, is made when it is synchronized directly from the PC.

So, after all the necessary manipulations have been made, it’s worth finding on your own computer a place where iPhone backups are stored. It will depend primarily on what software is on the PC. For example, if the iOs platform is used, then you can find saved files in the “Libraries” branch, then open “Application Support”, where you need to find “MobileSync”. When using “Vista” or the 7th Windows, copies will be placed in the “Users” document tree, where, after opening “AppData” and “Roaming”, the “Apple Computer” folder is found. Owners of iPhones running on PCs using Windows XP should pay attention to the Documents and Settings folder, where, by opening Application Data, you can find “Apple Computer”.
