How to roll back iOS 10 to 9: tips, tricks, instructions

Almost everyone is familiar with software updates on mobile devices today. This operation usually does not involve anything difficult. Owners of "apple" phones were offered a new iOS 10 version. However, this software did not impress users very much. Therefore, many people think about how to roll back iOS 10 to 9. What should every owner of an "apple" phone or tablet know about this operation? What are the possible scenarios?

Myth or reality?

To begin with, you have to understand whether iOS 10 can be rolled back to version 9 of the software. Indeed, often after updating this or that content on a mobile phone, it is impossible to return to older versions.



Fortunately, iOS rollback to iPhone / iPad is possible. This operation is accessible to all. It is recognized as official, does not require any special knowledge and skills. However, before rollback, you need to familiarize yourself with some information.

Before reset

How to roll back iOS 10 to 9? Before translating ideas into reality, you need to remember the following:

  1. Backups from the tenth version of the operating system are not suitable for iOS 9.
  2. There are several types of rollback - a "clean" option and with saving data. The second method is not recommended. It causes problems when using the Music app.
  3. Before performing the operation, you need to make sure that iCloud has synchronization enabled. In the corresponding settings item in the "Settings" -iCloud menu, you must enable the corresponding switch. The slider "Media Library" also needs to be activated.
  4. Disable Find My iPhone. If you neglect this step, rollback will be impossible.

Perhaps this is enough. You can think about how to roll back iOS 10 to 9.

Full rollback

Let's start with the most preferred approach. This is a "clean" rollback. A similar method is most often used in practice. It avoids system crashes and malfunctions.



How to roll back iOS10 to 9? To do this, follow certain instructions. It looks something like this:

  1. Launch iTunes on your PC.
  2. Connect iPhone to computer. To do this, use a USB cable.
  3. Launch iTunes. In the "Browse" menu, select the "Make a backup" option. Wait for the operation to complete.
  4. Go to ipsw.me. Select here your phone model along with firmware.
  5. Wait until the corresponding software downloads. After the operation is completed, go to iTunes and click on the Option button in the "Browse" tab (on Windows, this is the Shift button).
  6. Click on Restore iPhone.
  7. Select the previously downloaded iOS in the window that appears.
  8. Complete the update by clicking on the "Restore" inscription.

That's all. Now you can wait for the device to reboot. During OS initialization, you cannot disconnect the device from the PC. In the end, you need to select "New iPhone" and go through authorization using AppleID.
