A little bit about the resort ...

Cyprus abounds with beautiful resorts, but Protaras is an amazing, paradise place. The town became popular due to its beautiful sandy beaches and rocky bays. The resort has built an incredible number of different hotels that are focused on family vacations. The advantages of Protaras can also be attributed to its proximity to Ayia Napa, famous for its entertainment industry. But at the same time Protaras itself is a very calm and quiet place for a good holiday.

The town stretches along Fig Bay . The resort began to develop actively only in the last thirty years of the twentieth century. Since then, much has changed in Protaras, and at present it is one of the main tourist regions not only in the southeast of the island, but throughout Cyprus.



The famous bay has the best beaches on the coast. Here you can spend days without leaving warm and clear water, basking in the smallest sand, enjoying the beauty of the Mediterranean. In Protaras, along the sea you can walk endlessly while admiring the water. Numerous restaurants, cafes and shops were gradually built along the promenade. Protaras is not an ordinary resort. It is distinguished from other cities of the island by a certain leisurely and calm atmosphere. Although the tourist infrastructure has been developing rapidly lately, the natural idyll is still preserved. It is because of this that the Cypriots themselves value Protaras.

Nevertheless, in the evenings, the city dresses in bright lights and comes to life: fashionable melodies are heard everywhere, clubs and discos, restaurants with live music work.

How to get to the hotel?

The most convenient way to get to Protaras is from Ayia Napa, since the town is located only ten kilometers away. You can use a taxi, it will cost inexpensively. Suburban minibuses and municipal buses also run along the route.

Local Attractions

In Protaras, life is slow and steady. This is the charm of the resort. It sets vacationers to complete relaxation and relaxation away from city problems. There are not as many historical sights as in other cities in Cyprus, but still several interesting places can be found. Tourists, for example, can visit the "castles" on Cape Greco, carved in the rocks by the surf. Any person who sees them live simply refuses to believe that this is not the creation of human hands, but simply miracles of nature.

White windmills are noteworthy. Once they served to irrigate the lands of nearby villages. Now they have more aesthetic value.

Tourists will also be interested in visiting the church of St. Elias, built on a rock. In the evening after sunset, the building is transformed by lights of illumination. In addition, a spectacular view of the whole city opens from the cliff; everyone is interested in taking very beautiful photographs in memory of the rest.



Everyone who wants to see the sights of the island can diversify their beach holidays with numerous excursions to nearby cities. It is also worth visiting the cultural center of the whole island - Nicosia.
