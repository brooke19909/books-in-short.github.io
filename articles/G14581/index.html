Monastir and Skanes (Tunisia): the specifics of the resort region

In this North African country, the main youth center, famous for its entertainment and noisy parties, is Sousse. Monastir - like a fly in amber - froze in time. It preserved the sights of the times of the ancient Roman settlement of Ruspin. And Medina (Old City) with its walls and gates looks like it was built not in the Middle Ages, but last year. In Monastir, the main attraction is Ribat, a fort of murabitins - monks who can be compared with the Christian Templars. From the eighth to eleventh centuries, these monks led a life dedicated to God, and in between prayers they fought bravely, reflecting the blows of the enemy. Their fort - an amazing example of Islamic military architecture - is an intricate labyrinth of corridors, towers and secret passages.

The second attraction of the resort are the lagoons in which residents evaporate salt. Sand spits separate them from the sea. And one of them is Skanes. A spit with a length of five kilometers and a width of only three hundred to five hundred meters is almost completely built up by hotels. Skanes Family Resort is the most profitable "four" in Skanes. Why? There is practically no entertainment on the spit - you need to go to Monastir behind them (which is what the three-ruble guests do). Where lodging hotel guests

The stock of guest accommodations is three hundred and twenty rooms, of which only six are excellent suites. But due to the specifics of the Skanes Family Resort hotel and its focus on families with children, it has a lot of family room rooms. Many rooms have balconies. Reviews mention that these are mostly double rooms. Families are lodged in rooms without balconies, which, in principle, is wise, since not all parents follow the children, who only strive to fall out of somewhere. But at the expense of the former veranda, the kids have a separate room where, reviews mention this as a definite plus, there is another plasma TV.

The filling of double rooms and the "family room", in principle, is the same. Only room sizes vary. They have air conditioning, a safe, a fridge, a minibar (the charge of which is chargeable), a telephone, and a TV with satellite channels. The bathroom has a shower and hairdryer. Clean the rooms daily. Maids thoroughly wash the floor and porch, change towels. But the linen is changed twice a week. Family rooms come in two categories. "Kidsen" actually consists of two bedrooms - for parents and children, while the usual "family room" is one room. Reviews warn: the total area in both categories of rooms is the same - thirty-six square meters. Therefore, two rooms in the "kidsen" are very small.
