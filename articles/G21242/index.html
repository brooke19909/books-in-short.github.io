Brugge lace

A very special look that came to us from abroad is somewhat similar to our Vologda lace. This is Crochet lace from Belgium, the pattern of which can even be taken from Vologda. From the earliest times, the craftswomen owned the secret of weaving Vologda lace, there is even a legend about how the craftswomen tried to lure the eastern (Persian kings) for big kalyms, but the ancient tradition and love for the homeland made this technique unique.





To weave it, you need a large pillow stuffed with sawdust or cotton wool so tightly that the lace sheet is held very tightly on it. Cheerful bells and whistles, vivid emotions made not only lace unique, but a unique ritual, the process of its creation.

Bruges lace is woven in a more accessible form; it does not require a pillow or bobbin to create it. Visually, it is very similar to the Vologda. The difference will consist only in the technique of weaving it. If the Vologda lace is crocheted (its scheme is called a ‚Äúcleavage‚Äù) only connects, and the crochet performs an auxiliary role, then the Bruges lace only crochets and weaves. Another feature of Brugge lace is that you can use a thick thread for it (warm wool, acrylic, mohair), the main thing is to choose a suitable hook size so that the loops get the right size and the thread fits neatly. Crochet Pattern: Ribbon Lace

Ribbon lace in the technique of knitting patterns occupies a special place and has a completely different look from the usual options. For the base they take tapes (depending on the pattern and thickness of the tape and select its width), the length is selected depending on the same parameters, in addition, it is important to take into account the length of the product itself. How are finished lace ribbons connected?

The peculiarity of ribbon lace is that the elements of the pattern are repeated in rapports, forming the same lace ribbons. The process of combining them requires attention and imagination. Crochet pattern (ribbon lace) explains how the pairs of branches are connected. As a rule, repeating motifs are crocheted to the same ribbons already connected in several places by knitting with air loops or sewn with a needle ‚Äúfor picot‚Äù. The ribbons themselves form a common pattern in vertically arranged ribbons, but you can adapt the patterns for horizontal and even oblique weaving. The lace lace border is very beautiful: it is used where it adorns the edge of the product.





Irish lace

One of the most beautiful options for creating wardrobe items and accessories (women's clothing, bags, sweaters and blouses) is Irish lace. The finished product canvas is assembled from elements (petals, leaves, geometric shapes), separately connected. A single composition looks symmetrical or asymmetric, thick or openwork at will.

Crochet pattern (Irish lace) is simple. To create lace, it is enough to be able to make elementary loops with a crochet - air and single crochet. Irish lace is formed by creating a track of air loops that are knitted in the second row with single crochet, and then the process is repeated according to the pattern. Connections in elements connected by Irish lace are also crocheted using air loops or stitching.





There is another very original technique that gives things related to Irish lace, tenderness and softness - these are breeches, stitches from the threads of the main color or contrast (depending on the idea), which are sewn by hand with a needle, the seam is made in the style of "Richelieu". You can make a lace product on a net previously connected in any way, and sew the elements knitted using the Irish lace technique after completing the main ‚Äúframe‚Äù of the work. This method is optimal for beginners. It happens that the mesh is made with needle lace, for such a product you need skill and patience.
