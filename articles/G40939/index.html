Vancouver: attractions, interesting places.

Vancouver is a Canadian port city located on the Pacific coast. This metropolis is among the ten best places in terms of quality of life. In this article, we will take a look at photos and descriptions of Vancouver attractions. It is Canada's greenest city.

Some statistics

The city of Vancouver (Canada) is located in the province of British Columbia. Its area is 114.67 km 2 . The population of the metropolis is more than 600 thousand people. According to 2016 data, over 2.4 million people live within the metropolitan area. This metropolis is relatively young, it was founded in the 19th century. Its name is associated with the name of the famous English explorer and navigator George Vancouver. Sights of the city attract many tourists. Therefore, we should elaborate on the most interesting of them.

Stanley park

Perhaps the most striking landmark in Vancouver is the world famous Stanley Park. Both locals and tourists like to spend time here. The park looks very well maintained. There are paths and paths for hiking. Their total length is more than 250 km. The longest trail goes along the perimeter of the park, its length is 8.8 km.

Museum "World of Science"

Science lovers will be interested in visiting this Vancouver landmark. The construction was erected in 1986, and it was intended for the exhibition Expo. Subsequently, the building was used to create the museum "World of Science". It consists of 6 pavilions that are dedicated to various topics. It will be interesting for both children and adults. Throughout the day, museum workers demonstrate fascinating achievements in the field of technology to visitors, introduce them to the laws of physics and other sciences.

Aquarium

No less exciting place for a pleasant pastime is the aquarium. Its discovery took place in 1956. Visitors can see here a wide variety of marine life and other animals and birds. It is especially interesting to watch the pets of the aquarium during their feeding. This landmark of Vancouver attracts many tourists. There are dolphins and sharks, huge turtles, octopuses, sea seals and even penguins. Up to 300 species of fish, about 30 thousand invertebrates, 56 representatives of the world of reptiles and amphibians live in the aquarium. You can also see about 60 species of birds and mammals.

Shopping District - Granville Island

The Falls Creek Creek Peninsula is the Granville Island shopping district. About 100 years ago, it was an industrial center. Plants and factories were concentrated here, as well as several sawmills. Today, the appearance of the district has changed significantly. The central part is occupied by the city market, the area of ‚Äã‚Äãwhich is over 4.6 thousand km 2 . For family shopping, it is worth visiting the Children's Market, which is located nearby. Also in the Granville Island area there are:

Not far from the city market is the Sea Market. Here you can purchase various fishing equipment, materials for the construction and repair of yachts, as well as much more. There is also a thematic museum.

Sun Yat-sen - Chinese Garden

If you strive for unity with nature or just want to have a good rest, enjoying the picturesque scenery, you should definitely visit this attraction in the city of Vancouver. Sun Yat-sen Garden is unique. It is the only Chinese garden that is located outside of China. It was built quite quickly. Construction lasted from 1985 to 1986. The Chinese garden was named after the founder of the Kuomintang party, Sun Yat-sen, who was posthumously named the father of the nation.

Over the creation of the park zone, Canadian specialists and two craftsmen from China worked. The garden is made in the classical style of the Ming Dynasty. Designs do not contain such fasteners as glue, nails and screws.

The garden is divided into two zones: paid, where tours for tourists are held, and free. The bright red roofs of the pagodas stand out beautifully against the backdrop of green spaces. There are small trading shops where you can buy original souvenirs from China.

Suspension bridge "Capilano"

The Capilano Suspension Bridge is another attraction in Vancouver. In Canada, it is considered the longest. Its length is 136 m, and its height reaches 70 m. The bridge is spread over the seething river Capilano and received the same name. The very name of the river is associated with the name of one of the Indian leaders who headed the tribes that once lived on these lands.

The bridge was built in 1889 according to the project of Grant Mackay - a civil engineer from Scotland. As materials for construction, cedar boards and hemp ropes were used. In 1903, reconstruction was made: the ropes were replaced with metal cables. In 1956, the bridge was rebuilt anew. Interestingly, this attraction is privately owned, so to walk along the bridge, you need to buy a ticket: an adult will cost $ 38 excluding taxes, and a child - $ 12. Up to 800 thousand tourists come here every year.

Mount graus

Be sure to visit Mount Graus if you decide to visit the city of Vancouver in Canada. The attraction is quite popular among tourists. The mountain is located quite close to the city, you can get to it from the center of the metropolis by car in just 15 minutes. The height of Graus Mountain is 1251 meters, but the entire tourist infrastructure is located at the level of 275 meters. Using the funicular, you can climb to a height of 900 m. It offers a unique and breathtaking view: the ocean, and the city itself are in full view.

For fans of extreme sports, there are a number of entertainments:

  • descent from the mountain along the cable rope;
  • snowboarding
  • skiing;
  • paragliding and much more.

Gestown District

This area is one of the oldest in the city. Its architecture seems to carry the visitor to another era. Narrow streets with numerous passages and gateways serve as a reminder of Queen Victoria's once-long reign. Cozy patios have small shops and restaurants. The main attraction of the area are steam hours.

Skyscraper Harbor Center

The most famous skyscraper in Vancouver is the Harbor Center tower, whose height reaches 177 m. At 130 m there is an observation deck with a beautiful view of the city. It is from here that you can carefully consider all the beauties of Vancouver:

  • Barrard Pacific Gulf;
  • numerous city parks;
  • island of the same name;
  • quiet bay;
  • various architectural structures.

Other attractions

This is an amazing city that will be very interesting for tourists. In addition to the above attractions, here you can visit one of the largest art galleries in Canada, the building of which occupies an entire block in the central part of the city.

Those who like to soak up the sun should visit Kitsilano Beach. On its territory there is a long pool with heated sea water. The beach is equipped with sports fields where vacationers can play basketball or volleyball. There are also many playgrounds for children.

BBC Place Stadium is another unique attraction in this Canadian city. It hosted the Winter Olympics and Paralympic Games in 2010, held grandiose concerts of such world stars as Michael Jackson and Madonna.
