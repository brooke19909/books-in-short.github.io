Blue Lakes, Chernihiv region. Rest in Ukraine

Ukraine is a rather interesting and attractive country from a tourist point of view. The leisure infrastructure is developing more and more in it. So, not only big cities, but also small villages are ready to receive tourists, including from abroad. What is tempting a vacation in Ukraine? These are low prices, a close road, the absence of a language barrier and the need to open a visa. In winter, tourists are waiting for the snowy slopes of the Ukrainian Carpathians. The most famous ski resorts such as Bukovel, Dragobrat, Yaremche. In summer, you can sunbathe and swim on the coast of the Black and Azov Seas. A timeless vacation will be provided for you by a tour to the big cities of Ukraine. The mother of Russian cities, the throne city of Kiev, the spiritual capital of Lviv, famous for its color Odessa ... In Ukraine there are many Orthodox shrines where pilgrims go all year round, for example, Pochaev Lavra. Fans of antiquity will like the tours of castles of Ukraine and the amazing city of Kamenetz-Podolsky. And you can improve your health at such resorts as Truskavets, Morshin, Nemirov, Shklo. But this article is devoted to only one sight of the country - Oleshnyansky Blue Lakes. This place has long been popular for both Ukrainians and foreigners.



How to get to the Blue Lakes (Chernihiv region)

Three reservoirs are located near the border with Belarus. So close that mobile phones are constantly switching to roaming. Lakes are 205 kilometers from Kiev. You need to go along the Chernihiv highway for about three and a half hours. Having passed the regional center, you should head for the village of Oleshnya. There is no railway station in the village. But from Chernihiv in that direction, three times a day, the train goes to the stop "Grybova Rudnya". The train goes there for about an hour. To get from the "Mushroom Rudny" to the Blue Lakes (Chernihiv region, Repkinsky district), you need to walk along a beautiful road among a pine forest less than a kilometer. An asphalt highway leads to the reservoirs themselves, so there are always a lot of people there on a summer weekend. Come and Chernihiv. After all, the train schedule is very convenient. You can come in the morning and leave for lunch or in the evening.



How lakes formed

It is hard to believe that these ponds are man-made. And even more incredible seems the fact that the Blue Lakes (Chernihiv region) were formed as a result of industrial activity. There was once mined fine quartz sand for the production of glass. As a result, the workings reached the artesian layer. Now, former quarries were filled with clear water from the depths. The smallest quartz sand covers them. The sun's rays, refracting, give the lake water a bright aquamarine hue. Therefore, the reservoirs received the name Blue. The lake is surrounded by a pine forest. In addition to the healing air, drunk with the smells of pine needles, tourists can enjoy amazing water. It treats diseases of the musculoskeletal system. Well water procedures affect the condition of the skin and hair. Therefore, swimming in the lakes can be called "baths of health and beauty."

Tourist infrastructure

Reviews of people who visited the Blue Lakes ten years ago are very different from those left by tourists who visited their careers this year. Off-road driving with all the necessary products, living in a deserted wilderness and toilets under a pine have long sunk into oblivion. Now rest on the Blue Lakes (Chernihiv region) has changed dramatically. You can go here with a tent, as before. But you can install it for 50 hryvnias on an equipped campsite. Entrance to the Big Lake remained free. But when various cultural events and festivals are held, you need to buy a ticket. At Bolshoi there are two dedicated beach areas. One of them is for those who have come overnight. Here you have cottages, and secure parking, and a cafe, and a grill bar, and a wood-fired sauna.
