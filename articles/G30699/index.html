What is fascia?

Since ancient times, proper irrigation has been one of the main components of agricultural science. Showers washed away the fertile soil layer, which is extremely thin in many countries, and there were problems with productivity, which entailed such a disaster as hunger. Water had to be removed from the surface, but in such a way as not to overdo it, otherwise drought is inevitable - another scourge of farmers. Then someone thought of conducting underground channels, from which the moisture flowing at a shallow depth can gradually impregnate the soil. The easiest way to realize this idea technologically included the process of digging a ditch, laying bundles of brushwood or reeds in it and burying this irrigation system. These bundles of twigs are called fascines. A bunch of dried stems is hard to break, but one at a time is easy. Fashina became a symbol of the power of cohesion, and it was for this that the Italian nationalists chose it as one of the symbols of their movement. Fascist ideology assumes the maximum degree of unity of society around the idea and the leader. The hatchet was also depicted on the emblem, but he had nothing to do with peaceful work ...





Under the gentle Italian sun

Benito Mussolini was preoccupied with the fate of the Italian people in the 10s of the XX century, and in 1922 he became the leader of the country. He came to power using democratic means, but gradually suppressed all social organizations that impeded the forward movement, at least in his understanding.
