Crochet round motifs: types, forms, patterns

Crocheted round motifs are the basis of many interesting handmade products. It can be safely stated that the use of such elements helps to create the most beautiful dresses, tops, skirts, bedspreads, tablecloths and other items.

What is a motive, element or fragment

For a beginner who is only mastering knitting wisdom, the appearance of a round pattern can cause surprise or even horror. Where to start, how to switch from one row to another and how not to make a mistake? The answers to these questions are described in this article. Various round crochet motifs, patterns, types and methods of connection are also given.



A motif is a cloth that is crocheted (sometimes knitting) and serves as an integral part of a larger product. Motives can be of different shapes:

  • Round.
  • Square.
  • Triangular.
  • Hexagonal.
  • Asymmetric.

They are also distinguished in appearance: flat or voluminous (multilayer). Crocheted round motifs are used more often than others to decorate clothes or interior crafts. Such fragments are turned into napkins, dream catchers, tablecloths and floor mats.



Experienced knitters can use round motifs crocheted as a basis for the manufacture of hats, berets, bags and other accessories.

The principle of knitting a round motive

As the name implies, such fragments are knitted not in straight lines, but in circular rows. The beginning is always a ring of air loops (VP) or a lingering loop, to whom it is more convenient.

Each new row should begin with the lift. If the first in a row is a single crochet (STB), then there is one loop on the rise. For a single crochet column (STN) - three VP, for St2N - four and so on.

An important point: at the end of each row, a connecting column is required. This means that the last column must be connected to the first so that the row looks even. To do this, the hook is introduced under the loop of the first column, grab the thread and drag through the first and last columns. The connecting posts do not knit, in fact it is a broach.

Simple round crochet motifs: description, photo, diagram

Consider the theory on the example of knitting a fairly simple floral fragment.



1st row: StBN is knitted in the initial ring, * then a chain of 5 VPs is made and StBN * is made again. This element from * to * needs to be repeated seven more times.



According to the scheme, the center is performed with colored thread, which is cut off at the end of the row.

2nd row: the beginning of work with a new thread. * In the center of the arch of 5 VPs, make STB, then knit 5VP and perform STB in the adjacent arch *. The sequence is repeated seven times, ending not STB, and connecting column.

3rd row: 3VP lift, lush column of 2 StN, 3VP, StBN, * 3VP, lush column of 3 StN, 3 VP, StBN *. Repeat six times, complete the row with three VIs and a connecting column.

Such round motifs, crocheted, can be sewn into a whole fabric with a needle or they are connected by chains of air loops. Also popular is the method when the motifs are joined when knitting the last row.

For this fragment: when a magnificent column is ready, the motif is attached to the second connected element with a connecting column, and then 3VPs are already performed and work continues.
