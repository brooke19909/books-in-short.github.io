Voronovo - a manor in the suburbs

Manor Voronovo (Podolsky district) is one of the oldest and most famous in the suburbs. It has an interesting story full of dramatic events, and today it hospitably opens its doors to those who decided to relax and improve their health in the LRC of the Ministry of Economic Development of the Russian Federation.

The history of the estate from its foundation until the 19th century

The manor court on the site of modern Voronovo existed before the Time of Troubles and the Polish invasion. It belonged to the Voronov-Volynsky, who were direct descendants of one of the heroes of the Battle of Kulikovo - the famous Prince Bobrok. During the reign of Anna Ioannovna, the owner of the estate, Artemy Petrovich Volynsky, married to the cousin of Peter the Great, took part in a conspiracy against Biron, so he was executed. As a result, the estate was confiscated in favor of the treasury, but after the accession of Elizabeth Petrovna it was returned to the daughter of the former owner, who by that time was married to Count Ivan Vorontsov.





The new owner of the Voronovo estate has done a lot to make it one of the most comfortable and beautiful corners of the Moscow region. In general, representatives of this genus entered the history of Russia as the founders of new traditions of Russian noble life.

Palace construction

The Vorontsov brothers invited the famous architect Karl Blanca from the capital, who designed and built for them a three-story manor house with magnificent columns, a portico and outbuildings. The result was a real palace that aroused admiration and envy among contemporaries. In 1775, even with great pomp, Catherine the Second was received there, in memory of which two paired obelisks were erected in the estate.

Manor at Count Rostopchin

The squandering of the Vorontsov brothers led to the fact that they had to sell their favorite estate for a huge sum at that time - 320 thousand rubles. Thus, the Voronovo estate near Moscow began to belong to Count Fedor Rostopchin. This favorite of Paul the First lived there almost eight years, during which he spent huge sums on arranging greenhouses, a garden and a horse yard.

As a result, according to contemporaries, Voronovo became the main decoration of Podolsky Uyezd, where they traveled to see the eccentricities of the Count, who planted Mediterranean plants and built a flying ship. This device, vaguely reminiscent of a balloon, Rostopchin intended to use in the war with Napoleon, whom he fiercely hated and considered the Antichrist. However, the ship was never able to fly into the air, and all the funds for its construction were wasted.





Arson and Recovery

When the French occupied Moscow, Rostopchin, who at that time held the post of Moscow military governor, burned his favorite estate with his own hands and left a message to the invaders. In it, he wrote that he himself destroyed his own home so that it would not be defiled by the presence of a hated enemy. Such an act in the spirit of the heroes of antiquity made a strong impression on his contemporaries, as well as an unexpected transition to Catholicism of the count‚Äôs wife - Ekaterina Petrovna.

Rostopchin first spent the last years of his life abroad, and then in Moscow, and almost did not appear in Voronovo, where his son and daughter-in-law settled. They built on the foundation of the old palace a two-story manor house, which was no different from other noble nests of its time.

Voronovo at the Sheremetyevs

In the mid-19th century, new owners appeared at the estate. They were Alexander and Sergey Sheremetyevs, who acquired Voronovo. The estate in this period began to resemble the suburban dwellings of the French rich bourgeois. In particular, an attic roof was built over the house, the south wing was dismantled, the gallery was expanded, and an arcade with a balcony was added to the front facade.

In the last decade before the revolution, the estate fell into disrepair, as its owners: the Sheremetyevs' daughter and her husband - only occasionally visited Voronovo. The estate was nationalized with the coming to power of the Bolsheviks, and in 1949 a sanatorium was founded there. In this regard, the manor house was rebuilt, and a medical building with rooms and other buildings appeared on the territory. In the post-Soviet period, the Voronovo estate was transferred to the Ministry of Economic Development of Russia, and a modern treatment and prevention center was founded there. It is equipped with the most modern equipment, and there are all conditions for recreation for adults and children.





Description of the estate

To visit Voronovo, you should go to the village of Voronovskoye, which is part of the Trinity administrative district of the capital. Only the following buildings have survived on the estate today:

  • a beautiful manor house, which is a building in which architectural styles of different periods of the 19th century are connected;
  • a picturesque Dutch house with decorative vases, white stone details and figured gables, built by Karl Blanck in the style of the Petrine era;
  • the corner tower is the only building preserved from the magnificent horse yard founded by Count Rostopchin, which burned down in the fire of 1812.


Also on the estate you can see the so-called Dutch pond with a small landscaped beach and the Church of the Savior. This church was built in 1762 by the owners of the estate and consecrated a year later with the blessing of the then Metropolitan Timofei. Three decades later, the spouses of Vorontsov were buried in it, but to this day their tombstones have not been preserved. A little later, a baroque bell tower was built at some distance, which, together with the Dutch house, is considered a symbol of the estate.

Horse Club

Throughout its existence, the Voronovo estate near Moscow was the place where horses were breeding. And today there is an equestrian club, which provides training for horseback riding for adults and children. As you know, such exercises have a very beneficial effect on the state of the human body. Doctors even developed a special treatment program for children with cerebral palsy - hippotherapy, with which you can achieve excellent results. Such therapy can also be taken in the LRC of the Ministry of Economic Development, therefore this sanatorium is a great place to relax with children with certain health problems.

Manor Voronovo: how to get there

The LRC of the Ministry of Economic Development of the Russian Federation can be reached both by public and own vehicles. In the first case, you need to get to the Teply Stan metro station, transfer to bus number 508 or minibus number 162 and go to the Voronovo stop. If you prefer to go there by your car, then you should hit the road along the Kaluga highway (about 35 km along the MKAD) to the village of the same name, then turn right and go to the house of the sanatorium of the Ministry of Economic Development.
