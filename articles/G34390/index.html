Resorts of Israel, Red Sea: review, description, characteristics and reviews of tourists

Wonderful mild climate, bewitching nature with its beauty, hospitality of local residents and a lot of interesting places that await both tourists and pious pilgrims - these are not all the "chips" of the country. First-class service and a friendly attitude towards foreign tourists determine the popularity enjoyed by Israeli resorts. But the southern part of Israel is most comfortable in spring or late autumn. In this article, we will pay attention to the country's resorts located on the Red Sea coast. Eilat

Looking at the map of Israel, it is easy to guess which of the resorts of Israel is washed by the Red Sea. Straight, as if drawn by a ruler, the country's borders narrow to the south and touch a thin wedge to the Eilat (Akaba) bay. The Red Sea here, crashed into the northern coast with a wide fjord, is incredibly beautiful and conceals in its bowels a rich underwater world. The city of Eilat is an endless series of resort hotels, beautiful beaches, duty-free shops. It is also one of the country's largest ports, connecting Israel with the Gulf countries . The duty free zone attracts shopaholics and business travelers. Those who have little sun and beach also like Eilat. After all, the border with Egypt can be reached on foot. You can also go to Jordan and Saudi Arabia. Other cities in Israel are far from here. Jerusalem is 307 kilometers, and Tel Aviv is three hundred and fifty-five. And the northernmost city, Haifa, is separated from the Red Sea coast by as many as four hundred twenty-seven kilometers. Therefore, if you plan to go to Eilat, it is best to take a flight to your local airport. Alternatively, you can land in the Egyptian Sharm el-Sheikh.



When to visit Israeli resorts in the Red Sea

Eilat lies in the subtropical climate zone. The sun seems to have settled here forever. Only in winter, no, no and even the sky will rain with rain - so long-awaited in this arid area. December - February in Eilat is a time for lovers of excursions and Christmas sales. In the summer, not all Europeans are able to withstand the extreme heat that prevails on the local coast. Therefore, in June - August it is best to go to the more northern resorts of Israel. The Red Sea is best visited from early March to late April. In the afternoon, the temperature is very comfortable: + 26-31 degrees. Eilat is also good in late autumn, in October and November. Then the air keeps summer heat at + 27-33 degrees. And the sea in the Gulf of Aqaba is the same almost all year round. If you are interested in long swims in coral gardens without a heat-insulating suit, the best time for you is September and October. Then the water temperature in the Red Sea is at around + 26 degrees.



The nuances of Israel

A visa for Russians and Ukrainians is not required to enter the country. Unless, of course, you want to visit Israel for the purpose of tourism. Nevertheless, it is better to plan your trip ahead of time. The fact is that the country has many religious and secular holidays. And at this time, the locals fell on the spas of Israel. The Red Sea is a favorite holiday region. Eilat hotels are stably crowded during the Passover and Rosh Hashanah periods. If you land in Israel on Friday evening, you are unlikely to have a chance to get to your vacation destination by public transport. Here the Sabbath is holyly honored, which begins the day before the rise of the first star. And even more so, you will not leave for Yom Kippur. On Judgment Day, the Israelites only pray and ask God for forgiveness. In this regard, the streets are deserted - not even taxis run.



Eilat resort area

This city is very ancient. It existed back in the days when King Solomon ruled. During the period of the Roman Empire, the port of Isla was noisy here, and a military garrison was located in the fortress. But later the city fell into disrepair and was slowly abandoned by local residents. This continued until the thirties of the twentieth century. Seventy years ago, on the site of chic avenues and glamorous embankments, there was a modest caravanserai for camel drivers. After the proclamation of Israel (1948), the revival of the city began. First of all, the laid highway, which connected Eilat with the capital and other regions of the country, played a positive role. This resort received the status of a city only in 1953. Now in Eilat almost fifty thousand inhabitants live permanently.

Natural Attractions Eilat

The sun, sea and coral reefs - these are three components, because of which tourists choose Israeli resorts on the Red Sea for relaxation. The city of Eilat is sheltered from the cold winds by mountains from three sides. And with the fourth - splashing the blue Gulf of Aqaba. It is unique in that it is the northernmost water area where you can see corals. Yes, what! It is best to admire them by taking a short swim from Migdalor Beach. The reef there comes very close to the shore. And on the beach there are free sunbeds and awnings. The bottom is rocky, and therefore the visibility under water is excellent. And if this doesn't seem enough to you, reviews are advised to go on an excursion to the nearest marine reserve Shmurat Almogim.

The Red Sea has been and remains one of the main attractions of Eilat.
