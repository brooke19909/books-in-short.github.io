Edmonton (Alberta): history, attractions, interesting facts

Edmonton (Alberta) is a large Canadian city. This is the cultural center of the province, in which there are many important industrial enterprises and attractions worth visiting for visitors. So, what can a tourist see in a Canadian town? We will talk about this later.

Historical reference

Edmonton (Alberta, Canada) begins its history in 1795. The city owes its foundation to the Hudson's Bay Company, which laid the fort in this place. Its name comes from the locality of the same name, in which the head of the organization, James Lake, was born. Edmonton received the status of the city only at the end of the 19th century, when less than a thousand people lived in it, and at the beginning of the last century the number of inhabitants increased to eight thousand. When the province of Alberta was officially formed, this locality became the administrative center.





How to get to Edmonton

You can fly here by plane from almost any Canadian airline; there are also flights from European and American carriers. A large number of tourists get to Edmonton through the city of Calgary. This is where many American planes arrive.

If you want to come there by your own car, you also have such an opportunity. However, if the road from the city of Calgary takes about three hours, then in the case when you need to travel from the city of Vancouver, the journey can take about twelve hours, while in winter it can be quite problematic due to the condition of the roads.

Weather

The climate in Edmonton is milder than in other cities in Canada. Spring begins in April, but frost can still strike at this time. The summer season, as a rule, lasts from June to September. Edmonton winter is usually long, but at the same time milder compared to other regions of Canada. Temperature readings fluctuate around zero degrees, but there can be significant frosts.

The main attraction for tourists in Edmonton is the building of the largest North American shopping center - West Edmonton Mall. Here you can find about eight hundred shops and a hundred restaurants. In addition, it is in the "West Edmonton Mall" that there are large entertainment complexes, which are visited daily by a large number of people.
