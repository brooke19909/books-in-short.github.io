Kutyakov Ivan Semenovich: biography and personal life

Ivan Semenovich Kutyakov became famous for his voivodship during the Civil War. At one time, under his command was the 25th Infantry Division. Ivan Semenovich headed the military unit immediately after the death of its previous commander V.I. Chapaev. In addition, Kutyakov Ivan Semenovich, personal life, biography, the achievements of which became the subject of our review, was a participant in the First World War.

Biography: the origins

Ivan Semenovich was destined to be born in January 1897 in an ordinary peasant family, in a small village Shalashi of the Samara province. Now it is the village of Krasnaya Rechka in the Pugachevsky district of the Saratov region. The family of rural workers had 13 children. Ivan's parents worked for the landowners, they did not have their own farmyard, which would help the whole large family survive. Subsequently, they were able to buy a cow and a small house. But by then only three children remained in the family, of which Ivan was the eldest.

By seven years, the future Red Army soldier was put by the parents to the blind as a guide. His salary per day was 7 kopecks. His work continued tirelessly. Soon, he became assistants to the local shepherd and so worked until he was 13 years old. In winter, Ivan Kutyakov studied at a church school, which he graduated from with a creditable certificate. The only one in its class. The reward for his successful training was not only a commendation sheet, but also a book - a rare thing in the house of poor villagers. At 17, the young Ivan Semenovich began to graze rural horses.

A year after their marriage, a son Vladimir was baptized in a young family, baptized as Theodosius. Ivan Semenovich Kutyakov found out about the birth of his son when he was in the army. His first wife soon died of typhus.

Military career

The military service of young Ivan began at age 19, with his call. He was sent to Astrakhan for training in an infantry regiment. After training, he went to the city of Tsaritsyn in the officer rank (non-commissioned officer). Accordingly, he, as an officer, was appointed to dominate the entire unit. At this time, the First World War raged. On it, Ivan Kutyakov moved his subordinates towards the Romanian front.

During the 1917 revolution, Kutyakov Ivan Semenovich, whose photo you see in the article, became a Bolshevik. After short military manipulations, Ivan Semenovich was able to return to his native village. At that time, V.I. Chapaev was the head of the commissariat and the entire military district in the village of Shalashi (Krasnaya Rechka). With him, Ivan Semenovich develops the most friendly relations. A year later (1918), Chapaev headed an infantry regiment, where Kutyakov could easily join. He was appointed head of the foot scouts, because he was in good standing.

After the death of Chapaev, Ivan Semenovich was appointed commander of the 25th Infantry Division, which was originally based in Samara and was called the Samara Division of Zakharov. After this, Kutyakov‚Äôs military career did not end, and for several years he was the commander of various divisions and detachments. The character of Ivan Semenovich, according to contemporaries

From the memoirs of contemporaries Kutyakov, he was an unpretentious person, as well as strict rules, especially in relation to himself. And this is not surprising, given that Ivan Semenovich began his military career when he was not yet 20 years old. Co-workers and friends noted that Kutyakov was a modest and honest man. And also he sought to resemble V.I. Chapaev. He was still distinguished by his undoubted love for weapons and Peter's time. He knew a lot about Peter I and always tried to express his own opinion.

As indicated in the memoirs of his friends, Ivan Semenovich was somewhat embarrassed by his low education, so he studied a lot and capaciously. He tried to cover everything that was possible: books, opera, ballet, museums, with advantage delved into debates and conversations. He never used his official position and did not promote relatives and acquaintances in the service, did not arrange for work. However, he was always hospitable. When they came to visit him in Moscow, he always welcomed, placed in his house, showed the sights. In addition, he did not forget his roots and often visited his relatives in the village of Shalashi. When communicating with him, fellow countrymen noted that he was simple, talkative.





The death of Kutyakov Ivan Semenovich

In the late 30s of the last century, Ivan Semenovich was arrested and, in many opinions, completely unreasonably repressed. Documents about the arrest of the Red Army soldier are still kept in the school museum of the village of Krasnaya Rechka in the Pugachevsky district. After conducting a short investigation, he was sentenced to death by shooting. The execution was carried out a year after the arrest, at the end of 1938. However, according to other sources, official death found Ivan Kutyakov in the second year of World War II, in 1942, September 23. In 1956, Ivan Semenovich was posthumously rehabilitated.
