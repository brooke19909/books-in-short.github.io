Who has the right to rest in Crimea

Sanatoriums of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Russian Federation in Crimea will operate year-round. Not only servicemen and employees of this department can relax in them, but also retired law enforcement officers, as well as his civil servants.

For quite some time, Russian legislation contains clauses stating that employees of the Ministry of the Interior, belonging to a particular group, can receive the most advanced extraordinary preferential sanatorium treatment. These are both the employees themselves and their family members - spouse or spouse, as well as children: minors or have reached the age of majority, but having a disability, as well as those who are studying full-time (before reaching twenty-three years).



The following Russian citizens also have the right to rest in sanatoriums of the Ministry of Internal Affairs for twenty-five percent of the cost of a ticket once a year:

  • retired from the internal affairs bodies with a total experience of at least twenty years;
  • discharged due to disability as a result of injuries received in the performance of official duty, as well as occupational disease;
  • families of heroes who do not receive monthly cash payments, but are eligible for preferential trips to sanatoriums.

Parents and spouses of the deceased servicemen, as well as those who went missing during military missions, have the right to rest absolutely free.

Required documents



Those who come for treatment at the sanatorium of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Russian Federation in Crimea must have a passport, official or pension certificate, as well as a package of documents on the right to benefits. For some categories, a health insurance policy is required. If the ticket should not be used by an employee, then relatives will need to provide confirmation of their connection with an employee or a pensioner.

Children under fourteen years of age are populated with a birth certificate and results of an analysis for enterobiosis, a certificate from an infectious disease specialist or pediatrician.
