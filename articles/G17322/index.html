So, what is the difference between a smartphone and a phone?

For the past couple of years, it was smartphones that received the most widespread popularity among users. However, it is not always even the most experienced seller in mobile stores can easily explain how the smartphone differs from the phone that we are so used to lately. First of all, you need to figure out how the smartphone differs from the phone. A mobile phone is just a communication device capable of transmitting and receiving SMS and MMS messages. Of course, it has a number of additional features, such as the availability of games and Internet access. While a smartphone is a prototype of a computer. After all, its functionality is beyond praise. So, the smartphone supports all audio and video formats, any applications or programs can be easily installed on it, just as if it was a computer. After all, with its help you can watch your favorite movie, the only thing that can cause some inconvenience is the small screen size. Also, you can easily connect to the Internet and view all the files you need online. At the same time, you can use other programs without any difficulty, and you will not have any problems with the congestion of the system.

OS for mobile phones

It should also be noted that it is the presence of a full-fledged operating system that distinguishes a smartphone from ordinary phones. After all, this gives him an unlimited opportunity to simultaneously work with a huge number of various applications and programs. It also provides better work of the entire telephone system.

There are a large number of different operating systems, but phones with windows os are increasingly found. Perhaps, thanks to many years of experience and gained trust, most people give preference to them. But along with this there are the following options for operating systems:

Android One of the most popular modern operating systems currently owned by Google.

Bada. An OS that was developed by Samsung. However, it is more considered an open platform than a full-fledged operating system.

Apple No less popular system designed for devices manufactured by Apple.

And Palm webOS. An OS designed specifically for touchscreen smartphones.
