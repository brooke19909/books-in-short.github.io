The importance of space research

Through space research, valuable data have been introduced into science, which made it possible to comprehend the essence of new natural phenomena and put them at the service of people. Using artificial satellites, scientists were able to determine the exact shape of the planet Earth, by studying the orbit, it became possible to trace the areas of magnetic anomalies in Siberia. Using rockets and satellites, they were able to open and explore the radiation belts around the Earth. With their help, it became possible to solve many other complex problems.

The first spaceship to visit the moon

The moon is a celestial body with which the most spectacular and impressive successes of space science are associated.

The flight to the moon for the first time in history was carried out on January 2, 1959 by the automatic station "Luna-1". The first launch of the Luna-1 artificial satellite was a significant breakthrough in space exploration. But the main goal of the project was not achieved. It consisted of a flight from Earth to the moon. The launch of the satellite provided valuable scientific and practical information regarding flights to other space bodies. During the flight of the "Moon-1" was developed (for the first time!) The second cosmic velocity. In addition, it became possible to obtain data on the radiation belt of the globe, and other valuable information was obtained. The world press gave the name Luna-1 to the Luna-1 spacecraft.





AMC "Luna-2" repeated its predecessor almost completely. The instruments and equipment used made it possible to monitor interplanetary space, as well as to correct the information received by Luna-1. The launch (September 12, 1959) was also carried out using a pH 8K72.

On September 14, Luna-2 reached the surface of the Earth‚Äôs natural satellite. The first ever flight from our planet to the moon was made. Onboard the AMC were three symbolic pennants bearing the inscription: "USSR, September 1959." A metal ball was placed in the middle, which, when it hit the surface of a celestial body, scattered into dozens of small pennants.

Tasks assigned to the automatic station:

  • reaching the surface of the moon;
  • the development of the second cosmic velocity;
  • overcoming the gravity of planet Earth;
  • delivery to the lunar surface of pennants of the "USSR".

All of them were completed.

"East"

It was the very first spaceship in the world of all launched into the orbit of the Earth. Academician M.K. Tikhonravov under the guidance of the famous designer S.P. Korolev developed for many years, starting in the spring of 1957. In April 1958, approximate parameters of the future ship, as well as its general indicators, were known. It was assumed that the first spacecraft will have a weight of about 5 tons and that upon entering the atmosphere it will require additional thermal protection weighing about 1.5. In addition, it was provided for the bailout of the pilot.

The creation of the experimental apparatus ended in April 1960. In the summer, his trials began.

The first Vostok spacecraft (photo below) consisted of two elements: the instrument compartment and the descent vehicle, interconnected.





The ship was equipped with manual and automatic control, orientation to the Sun and the Earth. In addition, there was a touchdown, temperature control and power supply. The board was designed for the flight of one pilot in a spacesuit. The ship had two portholes.

The first spacecraft went into space in 1961, April 12. Now this date is celebrated as Cosmonautics Day. On this day, Yu.A. Gagarin put into orbit the world's first spacecraft. He made a revolution around the Earth.

The main task performed by the first spacecraft with a man on board was to study the well-being and performance of the astronaut outside our planet. The successful flight of Gagarin: our compatriot, the first person to see the Earth from outer space, the development of science was taken to a new level.

Space race

Between the Soviet Union and the United States in those years there was a tacit competition for the right to play a leading role in the conquest of space. The leader of the competition was the Soviet Union. The United States lacked powerful launch vehicles.

Soviet astronautics already tested their work in January 1960 during tests in the Pacific. All the major newspapers in the world published information that a man will soon be launched into space in the USSR, which will certainly leave the United States behind. All the people of the world were waiting for the first flight of a man with great impatience.

In April 1961, man first looked at the Earth from space. "East" rushed towards the sun, the whole planet watched this flight at the radios. The world was shocked and excited, everyone was inseparably watching the course of the greatest experiment in the history of mankind.

The first spaceship "Vostok": tasks

The main scientific tasks facing the Vostok ship were as follows. Firstly, the study of the impact of flight conditions in orbit on the state of the human body and its performance. Secondly, verification of the principles of spacecraft construction.

History of creation

In 1957, S.P. Korolev, in the framework of the scientific design bureau, organized a special department No. 9. It provided for the work to create artificial satellites of our planet. The department was headed by associate Korolyov M.K. Quietly. The issues of creating a satellite manned by a person on board were also investigated here. The Korolevskaya R-7 was considered as a launch vehicle. According to calculations, a rocket with a third degree of protection was able to bring a five-ton load into low Earth orbit.

Mathematicians of the Academy of Sciences took part in the calculations at an early stage of development. A warning was made that a tenfold overload could lead to a ballistic descent from orbit.

The department investigated the conditions for the implementation of this task. I had to abandon the consideration of winged options. As the most acceptable way to return a person, the possibilities of his bailout and further descent by parachute were studied. A separate rescue of the descent vehicle was not provided for.





In the course of medical research, it was proved that the most acceptable for the human body is the spherical shape of the descent vehicle, which can withstand significant loads without serious consequences for the health of the astronaut. It was the spherical shape that was chosen for the production of the lander of the manned ship.

The Vostok-1K ship was the first to be sent. It was an automatic flight, which took place in May 1960. Later, the Vostok-3KA modification was created and worked out, which was completely ready for manned flights.

In addition to one failed flight, which ended in a launch vehicle accident at the start, the program provided for the launch of six unmanned vehicles and six manned spacecraft.

The program implemented:

  • manned space flight - the first spaceship Vostok 1 (the photo is an image of a ship);
  • flight duration per day: "Vostok-2";
  • group flights: Vostok-3 and Vostok-4;
  • participation in space flight of the first female astronaut: Vostok-6.

Specifications:

Equipment

Automatic and manual control, automatic orientation to the Sun and manual orientation to the Earth.

Life support (provided for 10 days to maintain the internal atmosphere corresponding to the parameters of the Earth‚Äôs atmosphere).

Command-logical control, power supply, thermal control, landing.

For human work

In order to ensure the work of man in space, the board was equipped with the following equipment:

  • autonomous and radio telemetry devices necessary for monitoring the state of the astronaut;
  • devices for radiotelephone communication with ground stations;
  • command radio link;
  • software-temporary devices;
  • television system for monitoring the pilot from the Earth;
  • a radio system for monitoring the orbit and direction finding of a ship;

The device of the descent vehicle

There were two portholes in the descent vehicle. One of them was located on the entrance hatch, slightly above the pilot's head, the other, with a special orientation system, was located in the floor at his feet. An astronaut dressed in a spacesuit was located in an ejection chair. It was envisaged that after braking the descent vehicle at an altitude of 7 km, the astronaut should catapult and make a landing by parachute. In addition, there was the possibility of a pilot landing inside the device itself. There was a parachute in the descent vehicle, but equipment for soft landing was not provided. This threatened the man who was in him with serious bruises on landing.

If automatic systems failed, the astronaut could use manual control.

In the Vostok ships there were no devices for manned flights to the moon. The flight of people without special training was unacceptable in them.
