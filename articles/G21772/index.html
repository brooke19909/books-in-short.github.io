Piestany (Slovakia): description, location, treatment, photos and reviews of tourists

One of the most famous resorts in Slovakia is the town of Piestany. His fame spread far beyond the borders of the country. Patients from Austria and Germany, Japan and Israel, Russia and from far Australia come here to treat the musculoskeletal system.

The city of Piestany in Slovakia, which is inhabited by about 30 thousand inhabitants, was founded more than 900 years ago. Today, the famous resort offers various comprehensive programs in sanatoriums and hotels. If you do not need treatment, you can come to this picturesque town just to relax - there are a lot of greenery, magnificent parks and beautiful buildings of the XIX century.

Location

The city of Piestany in Slovakia is located 80 km from Bratislava - the capital of the country, on the Spa island on the river Vag. There are four resort houses (hotels) of the category 2 to 5 * on the island, drinking fountains, hospitals, tennis courts, a pool, a peacock aviary, a golf course, a stage, a well-equipped playground.

You can get to the city of Piestany by road or foot bridge. All guests are greeted by a sculpture of a patient who breaks a crutch. This is a symbol of the resort.

Resort Features

In the resort of Piestany in Slovakia there are no sanatoriums with a mediocre medical base. For example, Balnea Splendid, Balnea Splendid Grand, Balnea Palace, Balnea Esplanade - these are institutions of a general health-improving complex, one of the most modern in European resorts. All four resorts combine warm covered walkways.

Even the two-star Pro Patria has a mud thermal pool. And the procedures for guests are carried out not only in the medical department, but also in the Napoleon balneological center, located next to the sanatorium. The most modernized treatment base has a Thermia Palace sanatorium. Therapeutic procedures are carried out in the balneological center "Irma", which has a pool with thermal mineral water and a mud pool. The most famous and honored doctor, physiotherapist Pavel Makovnik, works in this balneocenter. This is a specialist of the highest qualification, relying in his practice only on scientifically proven methods of treatment.

The resort practices two main methods of treatment - using sulfuric mud and using mineral thermal water. There are many treatment options: taking mineral water inside, bathing in bathtubs, shower procedures, mud wraps, physiotherapy exercises, etc.

In the sanatoriums of Piestany in Slovakia, you can take a long treatment course (2 weeks), and a short (2 days).

Thermal mineral water

It is a healing natural water, the temperature of which ranges from 67 to 69 ¬∞ C. It is characterized as calcium-sodium, sulfate-carbonate with moderate salinity. Most of the sources located in Piestany (Slovakia) are used for medicinal purposes, the rest flow into the bypass of the Vag river.

Healing water is used for drinking courses and water procedures. It normalizes the digestive tract, liver and kidneys.

What is dirt useful for?

Mud treatment in Piestany (Slovakia) gives excellent results thanks to the unique peloid, which has powerful healing properties. Mud ripens in the upstream of the Vag River where thermal sulfuric mineral water comes out. After the extraction of mud, it ripens during the year in special pools to which thermal sulfuric water is supplied. It enriches the composition with minerals.

Aged dirt has an oily sticky consistency. It is a ductile heat-conducting mass that cools four times slower than water. The finished healing mud is painted in steel dark blue. It is used as compresses at temperatures around +45 ¬∞ C.

Indications for treatment

The Piestany spa in Slovakia is recommended for patients with the following diseases:

  • Musculoskeletal system.
  • Neurotic and neurological ailments.
  • Obesity and metabolic disorders.
  • Injuries and in the postoperative period.

Contraindications

If you are going to the Piestany spa, you need to consult your doctor - the available contraindications can violate your plans. The doctor must confirm that you do not have the following problems:

  • Various infections.
  • Vascular and heart diseases.
  • Acute inflammation.
  • Tumors with metastases.
  • Urinary incontinence.
  • Psychoses.
  • Diabetes.
  • Epilepsy.
  • Pregnancy.
  • Addiction and alcoholism.

Healing procedures

Judging by the reviews, Piestany in Slovakia is a unique resort. The complex use of sulfuric mud, thermal water in combination with modern treatment methods gives a stunning effect. After treatment at the resort, patients feel a change in their health status for the better. It's time to talk about how the procedures are carried out in Piestany.

Mud pool

This is a pool with thermal mineral water and a natural bottom, which consists of a layer of mud. Through it, thermal mineral water enters the pool. Its level reaches 90 cm above the level of the mud layer. The water temperature is maintained around +40 ¬∞ C, and in the place where the water enters the pool, the temperature can be higher. On average, the procedure lasts 5-10 minutes. It depends on the prescription of the doctor, who previously conducts a comprehensive examination of the patient. The procedure ends with a shower and a dry wrap.

Partial or full mud wraps

Mud wrap helps to reduce joint tumors, reduce tension in them, improve nutrition of cartilage and connective tissue. In addition, this procedure increases the anti-inflammatory activity and immune processes in the body.

Parafango

Intensive and very effective procedure. It is based on applying pedestrian mud, paraffin oil to the skin. This helps to relax muscle spasms, improve blood circulation, heal wounds and relieve pain.

Pelloid kinesitherapy

The procedure involves physical exercises using pedestrian mud. Fine motor exercises are carried out - the patient performs simple exercises with his hands in a container with mud solution. Such treatment helps to relax tissues, improve blood circulation, relieves pain symptoms.

Mirror pool

It has an artificial bottom. The pool is filled with thermal water (+39 ¬∞ C). The patient plunges into the pool to shoulder level. On average, the procedure lasts about 20 minutes and ends with a dry wrap.

Mineral thermal water treatments

They are necessary for the effect of sulfur on the skin, connective tissue and joints. Water procedures contribute to the expansion of blood vessels, have a beneficial effect on the level of celascone in plasma, and also reduce the number of free radicals. Treatments relax and soothe.

Bubble bath

It is characterized by aeration of mineral water at a temperature of +37 ¬∞ C using compressed air, which is fed to the grid at the bottom of the bath. This procedure is recommended for problems of a neurotic nature and insomnia.

Carbonic bath

A bath is prepared, saturating mineral water, cooled to +34 ¬∞ C, with food carbon dioxide. This bath lowers blood pressure, stimulates the work of the heart and blood vessels, improves blood circulation without heat load. Recommended for diseases of the heart and blood vessels.

Underwater massage

This effective procedure is carried out in a water bath. The temperature is maintained around +38 ¬∞ C. The therapist at a certain angle directs under water a strong stream of water under pressure from a distance of about 15 cm to the main muscle groups. The purpose of such a deep massage is to relax the connective tissues and muscles, partially relax the joints.

Jacuzzi

This is a gentle gentle massage using vortex water, partial massage on the lower or upper limbs or a full body massage at a water temperature of +37 ¬∞ C. The procedure improves blood circulation in the limbs, relaxes muscles and improves joint mobility.

Scottish shower

A very intensive procedure in which the patient stands, and a powerful stream of water is sent to his body under pressure in 3 atmospheres from a distance of four meters. In the first thirty seconds, the water temperature is about +40 ¬∞ C, followed by a stream with a temperature of +18 ¬∞ C, for 10 seconds. Alternation is repeated several times: first for the lower and upper limbs, then for the abdomen and back. The procedure ends with a cold shower. Such a shower improves blood circulation and metabolism, strengthens the immune system, and promotes the rejuvenation of the body.

Hotels and motels

The town of Piestany in Slovakia, whose hotels and resorts are always ready to receive guests, lives its usual life outside the spa centers. Most resorts and hotels are concentrated on the right bank of the Vag River and on the Bathing Island.

The hotel offers 111 luxurious rooms for checking in. Here is one of the best spa centers, cafes and restaurants, its own mirrored pool, games room.

Thermia Palace is located in the south of the Resort Island. At this hotel you can book a stay with treatment or just a room. sights

Of course, treatment at the resort takes a lot of time. However, patients have plenty of time for sightseeing in Piestany in Slovakia. As a rule, guests first of all go for a walk through the streets of the city. Of particular interest are the streets of Winter and Beethoven, on which magnificent houses of the 19th century are located.

If you happen to visit this place, be sure to cross the bridge to the Resort Island. Here you will find a magnificent park with drinking fountains and cozy places to relax. If you have not stayed at Thermia Palace, be sure to inspect this wonderful building.

Of the main attractions of the city should be highlighted:

  • Colonnade Bridge leading to the Resort Island.
  • Monument to a man breaking a crutch.
  • The Temple of God's Heart, located on Winter Street.
  • Balneological Museum.
  • Villa Leger (1899).

The city has an excellent golf club. Many tournaments play on 9 holes, not only amateurs, but also professionals. On the Vag River, you can ride a yacht, canoe, or an ordinary rowing boat. If time permits, take a ride and evaluate Bird Island, on which a huge number of birds nest. If the weather turns bad in Piestany, you can go on an excursion. For example, in Bojnitsky castle with a zoo. Or explore the ruins of the castles Tematin, Bertsov and ahtice.

How to get to the Slovak spa?

Many tourists from Russia have already visited Piestany in Slovakia. How to get to those who are going on a trip for the first time? The most convenient option for Russian tourists is air travel to Bratislava. From the airport of the capital of Slovakia you should take bus number 61, which will take you to the train station. From here every 2 hours a train leaves for Piestany. The road takes about an hour and a half. Private buses depart from the station square in the town and run five times a day.

How to get from St. Petersburg to Piestany (Slovakia)? We asked experienced travelers to share the route. As experienced tourists testify, there are several travel options with a change in Helsinki, Vienna, Bratislava, Moscow. Flights to Moscow fly from Vienna, Rostov-on-Don, Krasnodar and St. Petersburg. The airport is located 140 kilometers from Piestany. Two flights depart from St. Petersburg to Vienna. The average flight duration is 2 hours 40 minutes.

The distance from Vienna to Piestany is 108 km. You can use the local bus. It is very convenient that the bus station in Vienna is near the airport. That is why you can immediately transfer to a flight to Bratislava, which depart every hour.

If you prefer to take the train, trains run from the Vienna main station to Bratislava every half hour. From the central railway station of the capital of Slovakia, you can easily get by bus or taxi to the town.
