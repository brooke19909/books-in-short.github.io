Sokolniki Park: how to get there? Sokolniki Park in Moscow: what to see?

Sokolniki is the favorite vacation spot of the native Muscovites and guests of the capital. And if most of the parks are focused more on youth, then here everyone will find entertainment to their liking. This park is the largest not only in Russia but also in Europe: its area is about 600 hectares. And looking at the pace that this recreation area is transforming, we can say with confidence that this is the most beautiful park. From this article you will find out what Sokolniki Park can offer you (a map of it is presented in the article) and how to get to this wonderful place.

Location and layout

Sokolniki is located in the north-east of Moscow. From the east, the park is bounded by the Bogorodskoye Highway, from the west by the Yaroslavl Railway, from the south by Sokolniki Val, and in the north it adjoins Elk Island.

Sokolniki Park has a radial-ring structure. The Sokolniki circle is located in the center, and from it there are 8 fan alleys that intersect the half-rings Mitkovsky passage and Crossroads. Each alley has its own name: 1st, 2nd, 3rd Raythroughs, the 5th and 6th Raythroughs stretch to the northeast and north, May lane and Sand Alley. And from the southeast, Sokolniki Pavilion Passage adjoins the Sokolniki circle.

History

In the XIV-XVI centuries, at the place where the Sokolniki park (Moscow) is now located, there was a dense forest, in which Ivan the Terrible and then Alexei Mikhailovich liked to hunt. They hunted mainly with the help of falcons. Hence the name of the park. And only in the 30s of the last century the Falcon grove received the status of a city park. We can say that it was from this moment that the cultural life of Sokolnikov began. The area began to be ennobled: well-groomed alleys appeared, and the Krug concert hall was built in the central part of the park. A dance veranda, a library, attractions, the Sokolniki restaurant, a concert stage and much more were opened. This place has become one of the most beloved among Muscovites.

But during the Great Patriotic War, all these institutions were closed. Instead, military enterprises began their work. Rifle and tank divisions were formed here. But as soon as German troops ceased to threaten Moscow, all institutions were reopened. And at the end of the 50s, an exhibition hall was built, since then all kinds of interesting exhibitions and other cultural events have been constantly held here.

Why are Sokolniki good for youth?

Well, firstly, the park has many special lanes for roller-skating and bicycles: you can rent all this. Very often, popular artists perform in the concert hall or on open stage venues. Those who like extreme types of recreation will love the climbing wall, which is located on the right side of the park. Still there is karting. Also at your disposal a tennis court, crossbow and onion shooting gallery, billiard room, pool, various attractions. And in the evening you can light on the veranda of the dances. Well, those who decided to tackle a figure will need street exercise machines that are located behind a large rose garden. It is a great pleasure to play sports in the fresh air. Well, after that you can ride horses in the shady alleys. In winter, young people also love to come with friends to Sokolniki Park. The skating rink will be an excellent replacement for rollers on frosty days.





Entertainment for seniors

For older people, radio discos are held where you can dance to the music of your youth. This is a great pastime that will help return to the days of your youth, and also chat with peers.

Fans of intellectual games can fight with the same clever people in checkers and chess, which are behind a large rose garden. And to improve your health, you can take a walk along the shady alleys and breathe in fresh air or chat with friends at the large fountain. Flower lovers should definitely visit the rose garden.

In winter, Sokolniki Park awaits the elderly, as well as the youth. The skating rink is what you need to improve your health.

What to entertain children in "Sokolniki"?

For the youngest guests, Sokolniki Park is a real fairy tale. There is everything in order for your child to run loose, play enough and come home happy and tired. They are located at a low height, in addition, they will definitely wear insurance for the child, so there is absolutely no need to fear for the baby, and experienced instructors will always be there.

Do you want to introduce your child to wildlife? Visit the mini zoo with him. There you will see moose, squirrels, fluffy rabbits, birds. Would you like not only to entertain the child, but also to teach him some plain craft? Then welcome to the House of Children's Creativity. Here the child learns to draw, sculpt from clay and much more. More than 20 paid and free circles will be provided at your service by Sokolniki Park. How much money do you need to have a good rest in Sokolniki?

To have a good rest in Sokolniki, it is not necessary to have a large amount with you. You can just buy ice cream and stroll along the alleys, sit near the fountain or have fun at the free rides, which are plentiful in the park. Bicycle or roller rental will cost you 100 rubles / hour on weekdays, and 150 rubles on weekends. Deposit - 500 rubles + document. Attractions will cost you from 50 to 350 rubles.
