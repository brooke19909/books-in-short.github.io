Learning Tools in a Modern School

Learning tools include a variety of materials and tools used during the learning process. Thanks to their successful selection, with great success for a short period of time, you can achieve your educational goals. Teachers and students use learning tools during the process of assimilating acquired knowledge.

What are training tools

The teaching tools include various objects and ongoing processes, which are a source of educational information and a tool for perceiving, assimilating and memorizing material proposed as a study. The main didactic task is entrusted to teaching aids: they are designed to accelerate the assimilation of the required volume of material and at the same time contribute to the development and education of children.



All existing teaching aids can be classified as material or ideal. Teaching aids, textbooks, tests, didactic materials, visualization, equipment for laboratories, technical means - all this makes up teaching aids, called material ones. The ideal ones include oral and written speech, painting, music, charts, tables, diagrams, as well as organizing and directing the activities of a teacher, his qualification level, and the methodology for organizing training.

Teaching will give positive results if the learning tools, ideal and material, are used in combination, complementing each other.



Features of modern teaching aids

Achievement of learning outcomes, both personal and subject, is possible only with the use of all existing means of supplying and mastering educational material. In this regard, in the educational process, both traditional methods and modern teaching aids based on digital technologies should be applied.

Today, the main task of the educational system is to teach a person to cognize, engage in search activities, learn, collaborate with others during the cognitive process. A new approach to learning consists precisely in creating opportunities for free access to various information resources, for organizing educational networks and communities.

Therefore, modern teaching aids should include traditional and innovative technologies. Traditional are the use of printed and non-printed media, equipment, instruments and tools for conducting experiments, experiments and practical exercises. Innovative tools - this is all the technology that operates on the basis of digital technology.
