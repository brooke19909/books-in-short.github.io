Dove of Peace (paper craft): scheme

The dove of peace is a symbol of peace and harmony, first created by Ellis Gray from paper. This is a traditional scheme for beginners who try themselves in origami. Professionals went further by creating two-color volumetric pigeons from square and triangular modules. Let's consider in more detail how to make paper birds in two ways.

What are pigeons?

The dove, made in a simple origami technique, is made of a sheet of paper. In various ways, you can make the pigeon stand with its tail down or feathery, with wings folded and spread, with and without paws, with and without a crest, voluminous or flat, in flight and walking.

Where does a simple dove of paper from paper apply? After creating dozens of colorful paper birds, attach them to the fishing line. Get the original curtains or pendant for the lampshade. Extravagant fashionistas make paper bird earrings. Craftswomen use paper models for panels, cards, mini-paintings.

Modular pigeon is a voluminous craft. Moreover, it is possible to make panels from square parts or from triangular ones - a voluminous souvenir gift. The pigeon can be plain, bicolor (beak and legs red) or multicolor to emphasize the shades of feathers. But as a symbol of peace, the dove should be white.

Simple origami: dove of peace

Let's try to make a paper dove that is about to take off. To do this, take a square sheet, repeat the following steps.





  • Fold the square diagonally.
  • Fold the resulting triangle in half, forming a similar small figure.
  • On each side, take one corner of the base and attach it to the other.
  • Get an even smaller triangle.
  • Bend the top on each side to the base.
  • Now expand to a large triangle, where a small figure is marked with folds.
  • Bend this small triangle inside the figure. The side is also bent inward, forming a triangle on the wrong side, and the shape of a boat on the outside.
  • On the other hand, do the same, forming a boat (trapezoid).
  • Next, on each side, fold the wings along the fold line, leaving a small triangle at the end.
  • From this triangle you make the head of a bird. On each side, bend the figure obliquely.
  • Then bend the resulting pyramid and tuck in a corner.
  • Now go to the tail. Fold diagonally a triangle on each side.
  • Bend the figure, where the lines outlined two triangles that fold in half.

Please note that at each stage of the line you need to clearly work out with your hands or a metal ruler. This is the basic rule of simple origami.

Pigeon assembly

Go back to the head. The glue should dry. Now you can make a beak without fear of breakage. Take the pink triangular module and insert in the middle of the head. Next, glue the wings, tail to the body. As you can see, the dove of peace is almost ready. Origami paper craft should be strong, stable, so use the Moment glue.

It remains to make paws of pink modules. Insert five triangles into each other, getting a vertical column. Turn it upside down. From the bottom, on the sides of the module, glue along the triangle. Grease the internal corners of these two parts with glue, put on another module on top, forming a paw.



Similarly, do the second paw. While the glue dries, cut out the eyes from black paper, stick on the sides of the head. You can use store "running" eyes, then the craft will be even more beautiful. Next, glue the paws to the body. Do not try to put the bird on its feet, let the craft dry.

Summarizing

From a simple origami, a dove of peace will turn out faster. Craft from paper in modular technology looks more elegant, elegant, unusual, but requires more time to create it. It is better for beginners to master simple products from triangular modules, and then proceed to create a dove.

In addition, care is needed not only when connecting triangles, but also when gluing parts. For example, in a flying bird, the wings should be convex; if they are made concave, the pigeon will turn out to be unnatural. The tail must be glued so that the bird can lean on it in a standing position.
