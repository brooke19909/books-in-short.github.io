DIY subwoofer box: drawings, diagrams and features

The subwoofer box is a very complex device. Designing and creating enclosures requires specific knowledge of the geometry of sound. To help designers, they created special programs to calculate the optimal geometry and amount of sound-absorbing material.

Programs for calculating the geometry of sound

To create a great sound system, all factors must be considered. This is due to the fact that designing a box for a subwoofer is both a science and an art. To create the most effective speakers, there are several best programs that can guarantee maximum performance and unique design:

  1. Woofer Box and Circuit Designer.
  2. Subwoofer Design Toolbox.
  3. AJHorn 6.
  4. Boxnotes.

Calculation parameters and units

Before calculating the box for the subwoofer, the user selects a program and system of units. All programs released after 2017 support metric units. They easily compare the parameters of various block designs visually on a single graph. Frequency response printouts can be in color or with characters that improve readability with black and white printers.

The tool for designing enclosures helps create rectangular and wedge-shaped boxes, calculate the volume of prefabricated boxes. You can calculate the drawer volume from the size of the mounting windows, or any shape by entering the required enclosure volume.

Box making tool

Woofer Box and Circuit Designer is an advanced tool for creating subwoofer enclosures using MS Excel. The program has a complex mathematical model that can simulate the results of sealing, ventilation or passivity of the radiator. It also has special filters that allow you to simulate a very wide range of shapes.

The goal of this program is to provide the tools necessary for developing a subwoofer using a wide range of available plate amplifiers, crossovers, digital processors and equalizers. Before calculating the box for the subwoofer, the sound response curve of the room is imported into the program, which is saved as a file from any other software and will take into account the imported data in the output results.

Graphs are displayed showing the frequency response, phase, impedance, maximum output, sensitivity 2.83 V, filter transfer function, ventilation rate, group delay (filter, driver and system) and pulse response. All calculations and graphs include effects from the selected filters.

AJHorn 6 Case Modeling

Due to its modular design, AJHorn offers modeling of various types of subwoofer boxes with the same calculation algorithm. This is interesting because the theory of speaker design is not limited to one type of enclosure. An ideal solution to acoustic conditions where boundary cases are included - transmission line, bass reflex, bandwidth and enclosed enclosure types.

Relevant AJHorn projects (hrn files) can be found in the AJHorn installation directory, they provide for the construction:

  1. Frontal shout. Frontloaded Horn is a situation in which the front of the driver creates acoustic power along with a speaker. The back of the driver emits sound in an enclosed or ventilated chamber (RC) with a VRC volume. The length of the closed rear camera with sufficient damping does not affect the simulation result.
  2. Rear speaker. The difference between loading and the front speaker is the discarded rear camera. The driver emits sound right above the cone. Thanks to this modeling ability, old structures (classic rear horns) can be improved if there is still an unused area. This feature allows you to simulate and optimize your device with AJHorn.

Subwoofer Installation Tips

Before installing the speaker in the subwoofer box, it is important to know the impedance so that the speaker can connect to the amplifier correctly. Most amplifiers have separate positive connections for each of the resistances, while a negative connection is common.

Incorrect connection of the speaker to the amplifier, on the contrary, will reduce the volume of sound.

With enclosed speakers, usually the dimensions of the subwoofer box allow insulation inside to absorb sound from the back of the speaker. If the enclosure is not equipped with insulation, fiberglass insulation may be used.

The speakers that are used with sealed enclosures must be sealed in the enclosure. In no case should this be done with silicone or any other liquid, gel or glue. A simple but very effective gasket can be made using foam material, for example, for doors and windows.

Before making a box for the subwoofer, you need to strengthen the mount to the body and it is better to install T-shaped stands. The subwoofer housing must be very securely attached to the car. The subwoofer and chassis can weigh 20 kg or more. In a collision at 60 km / h or during rapid braking at this speed, such a device can exert up to 500 kg of pressure. This is enough to cause serious injuries to passengers.

A good electrical connection is essential to maintaining a healthy system. The best and most reliable way to connect wires is soldering.
