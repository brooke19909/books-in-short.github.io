"Swallow's Nest" in Crimea - a romantic castle in the spirit of the Middle Ages

"Swallow's Nest" has long been a kind of hallmark of the southern coast of Crimea. This wonderful building resembles a knight's castle from the Middle Ages, which sheltered on a small piece of land, on the cliff of Aurora. Millions of posters and postcards have spread all over the world, attracting tourists to Ukraine in order to see this miracle and take pictures next to it. "Swallow's Nest" in the Crimea was depicted on the canvas by many artists, among the most worthy are the works of Lagorio, Aivazovsky, Bogolyubov.



Information about the building has been preserved very little. It is only known that its first owner was a general who participated in the Russian-Turkish war. In those days, many were given land, apparently, this hero came across a patch on Cape Ai-Todor. You need to be a big romantic to decide to build a summer house on a steep rock. "Swallow's Nest" in the Crimea was originally wooden and bore the name "Castle of Love". To whom this architectural creation was dedicated - it remained a mystery.
