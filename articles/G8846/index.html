How to make a crane out of paper? Learn the recipe for happiness



In Japanese culture, origami is not only a form of needlework, but also a cult art. Many crafts have a symbolic meaning, and are not just used as interior decoration. One of the most famous and common figures is the crane. About how to make a crane out of paper, as well as about a beautiful legend associated with this bird, will be discussed in this article.

The legend mentioned above tells that such a craft brings happiness both to the one who made it and to the one to whom it was presented as a gift. There is an old belief that a person who made a thousand cranes will certainly fulfill any desire. This is exactly what the little girl believed, the history of which became known to the whole world after the bombing of the city of Hiroshima during the Second World War. Her name was Sadako Sasaki, and she received the strongest dose of radiation, which led to leukemia. The baby fought desperately for several years with the disease. Once a girl heard about this legend. Learning how to make a crane out of paper, Sadako found hope for recovery, but did not manage to lay down a thousand birds using origami. After this tragic incident, children from many countries began sending similar crafts to Japan in memory of a little girl who struggled with the disease to the last.





Do you want your most cherished desire to be fulfilled? Then, firstly, believe in it, and secondly, read the instructions on how to make a crane from the paper below.

So, we begin to fold the figures, according to the rules of origami, from a square sheet. Bend the paper diagonally - you get an isosceles triangle. Fold it along the line of symmetry and open one of the sides so that it becomes a square. Now your sheet is a rectangular trapezoid, which must be turned on the other side. We turn our product into a basic rhombus shape. To do this, you must do the same steps with an acute angle of the trapezoid. Place the resulting rhombus so that it can open from below. We bend the sides of the first layer to the axis of symmetry. We return them to their original position, and then "open" the upper side of the rhombus up. Iron the sides to the middle, turn the figure over and do the same. The resulting module needs to be further narrowed. Repeat the previous step.
