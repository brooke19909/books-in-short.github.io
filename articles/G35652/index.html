Shirvani Basaev: biography of brother Shamil Basaev

Shirvani Basayev is one of the most famous field commanders of Chechen fighters.

Together with his brother, he took an active part in military operations against the troops of the Russian Federation. He gained popularity as a result of brutal raids on the territory of Dagestan and battles in the territory of the Chechen Republic. The Basayev family was respected by Islamist militants. Almost all of its members took part in the war.

Shirvani Basayev: biography

Born in the Vedeno district in the early sixties. Data on the exact date of birth is very contradictory. In general, most of Shirvani‚Äôs biography is obscured. He graduated from high school and worked in the Vedeno district. Father and mother came from various Chechen teips. Adhered to Islamic traditions and were supporters of primordial nationalism. In addition to the Shirvani, the Basayevs had two more children - Zinaida and Islam.

Presumably, in the eighties, Shirvani moved with his brother to Moscow. There they are engaged in various part-time jobs. In particular, they worked in the field of business. In the nineties, the first participation in the political life of the country. The Soviet state ceased to exist, separatist sentiments began to rise in Chechnya. The future president of Russia, Boris Yeltsin, was popular with such radicals. He promised "as much independence as you can take." That is why during the attempt of the State Emergency Committee to save the country, the Basaevs came out in support of the new government.

Preparation for war

Shirvani Basayev is returning to Chechnya, where a new government is already beginning to form at that time. The first armed detachments were created back in the ninety-first year. They were trained by former officers of the Armed Forces of the Soviet Union, Chechens by origin. The Basaevs immediately joined these formations and took an active part. In an interview, Shamil claims that he and his brother studied military affairs from Russian textbooks.

First war

But they got their first combat experience in Nagorno-Karabakh. By the ninety-first year, relations between Azerbaijanis and Armenians reached the peak of their instability. Ethnically populated by Armenians, Karabakh declared its independence. In response, the Azerbaijani authorities unleashed a war. In view of the fierce resistance of the Karabakh population, the government appealed for support to Islamic organizations. Chechen fighters arrived in Azerbaijan under the leadership of Basayev.

Shirvani Basayev also became known for his participation in the Abkhaz war. In this conflict, the Chechens fought against the Georgian army. Shamil Salmanovich Basayev was presented to several Abkhaz awards. According to various sources, at this moment he was supervised by the General Intelligence Directorate of Russia. Many Chechen fighters said that Shamil was trained in Russia before being sent to Ossetia.

First Chechen

When, after several years of de facto independence of Chechnya, the leadership of the Russian Federation announced the start of a counter-terrorist operation, the Basayev family actively joined the war. Shirvani Basayev received a post in the so-called "army of the Chechen Republic." He became the commandant of the village of Bamut.

It is located in the Achkhoy-Martan district. In advance, the militants began to prepare the defense of the village. All surrounding mountains have been carefully studied. Ambush squads settled in the forests. All approaches to Bamut were carefully mined. The defense line also included concrete blocks that reliably protected the militants from shelling. The battle of Bamut was widely covered in the media. The federal forces stormed the settlement several times in the ninety-fifth, but were never able to take possession of it. The Chechens quickly moved and ambushed the advancing columns.
As a result, the village still managed to take, but only by the spring of next year and after seventeen attacks. Shamil Basayev‚Äôs brother distinguished himself in these battles as a skilled commander.

Battle of Grozny

During the second Chechen war, Basayev Shirvani Salmanovich took an active part in the defense of the capital of Ichkeria - Grozny.

In the winter of ninety-ninth, federal forces launched an offensive on Gudermes. After negotiations, the city came under the jurisdiction of Russia. The siege of Grozny began. Several armies surrounded the village, leaving only a humanitarian corridor for the exit of civilians.

At this time, Chechen fighters actively strengthened the areas of the city. Many areas have been mined. Ambushes were being prepared. An active production of grenade launchers and anti-aircraft installations was established. Which, however, had low productivity due to inept manufacturing. Also used tools such as grenades on small parachutes.

Federal troops regularly bombarded militants with artillery and aircraft. By February, the city was taken.

The leader of the militants Shamil Salmanovich Basayev fled from Grozny with his brother.

The end of the Basaevs

In December of the two thousandth year, the possible death of Shirvani Basayev was announced. However, later many reputable sources have denied this information. The fate of the older brother is still unknown. According to some reports, he moved to Turkey. From there, after treatment, to another Islamic country. The more famous brother, Shamil Basaev, was destroyed on July 10, two thousand and six. On his account were dozens of bloody terrorist acts.
