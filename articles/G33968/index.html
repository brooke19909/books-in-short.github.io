Refund of state duty from the tax: all the nuances of the procedure

Committing certain legally significant actions, you have probably at least once encountered such a phenomenon as the payment of state duty. The Tax Code of the Russian Federation lists all situations when such a fee is required. In general, money will have to be paid whenever you need a service from the state. An example is the appeal to the court.

However, there are also situations where money can be returned to you. All of them are also listed in the Tax Code. We will talk about how to make a state tax refund from the tax in this article.



To whom to write

The general procedure for working with state duty amounts tells us that you need to write a return application to the place where you applied for the service. For such an appeal, you must attach the original document fixing the very fact of payment of the fee by you. An original is required when you are claiming a full refund. With a partial refund, a copy of the payment is sufficient.

Have you paid too much and are sure that you are entitled to a state duty refund? Which tax to apply to depends on which region the authority that has provided you with services belongs to. This means that you will not be able to submit an application in your city if you contacted a notary in a neighboring area.

Exceptions

As in any other rule, there are exceptions. These include:

  • courts of general jurisdiction;
  • Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation;
  • world arbitration court.

Whichever of these authorities you apply for the return of the amount of the excessively paid state duty, the application will have to be submitted to the fiscal authority at the location of the court. At the same time, in addition to the copy / original of the payment, you need to stock up on a court ruling, decision or certificate to confirm the grounds for a refund. Once again, we note that the statement must be written to the tax authority where the court submits its reports, and not to the place where you yourself report.



When the state duty can be returned

The state fee (duty) may be fully or partially refunded in such situations:

  • when it is paid in the amount of slightly more than necessary (Chapter 25.3 of the Tax Code);
  • when the complaint, application / appeal has been returned to the submitter or left without consideration;
  • in case of refusal to conduct notarial actions.

There is another option, when the state duty is not refunded to the payer, but can be counted when the lawsuit is repeated or another legal action is taken. To set off the amount you need to attach a receipt for payment.

Refund of state duty from the tax is also carried out in the event that the judicial proceedings on the claim were terminated or the appeal (claim) was left without consideration. If the parties conclude a voluntary settlement agreement before the arbitration is decided, the amount of the fee may be partially refunded in the amount of 50%. If the parties come to an agreement already during the execution of the judicial act, the fee is not refundable. Half of the amount can be returned even if a citizen voluntarily refuses to register his right to real estate.

Refund of state duty from the tax can also be carried out in such cases:

  • The person who paid the state fee refused to take a legal action until it was started. For example, to travel abroad, you need a passport. You paid the fee, but the trip failed, and you changed your mind about doing the passport.
  • The person who paid the fee was refused documents for refugees.
  • A refusal was received to obtain a passport for departure / entry from / to Russia.

Cases when it will not be returned

However, you need to know that the erroneously paid tax duty is not always refunded.

There are several actions for which a return is not provided even if they were not committed:

  • refusal of state registration of marriage;
  • making any number of corrections (changes) to the registry office papers;
  • name change registration;
  • voluntary fulfillment by the respondent of claims before the start of the consideration of the case in court;
  • when registration of rights, encumbrances (restrictions) on real estate was refused;
  • payment was made for the services of the public services portal, which turned out to be unnecessary.

Compensation Procedure

In accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3 of Article 333.40 of the Tax Code of Russia, there is a certain procedure for the return of state duty from tax. First of all, you need to write the appropriate appeal to the tax at the place of the action, involving the payment of a fee. Such a statement can be provided within three years from the date of collection (payment) of excess money.

The following documents must be attached to the appeal:

  • a copy of the receipt if you are applying for only half the amount;
  • a certificate from the court (determination, decision), which is the motive for the return.

As mentioned above, the state can carry out not only the return of the state duty from the tax by court decision, but also offset it against the future service. The list of documents remains unchanged. Only the application form changes slightly.



What data should the statement contain

There are some differences in the list of data that should be contained in the application of individuals and legal entities.

For legal entities:

  • abbreviated and company (official) name of the enterprise;
  • the actual and legal address of the company, if this data is not the same;
  • the exact amount in numbers and letters (in words) in rubles and kopecks;
  • bank details of the company;
  • motives for which a return is required;
  • Director's signature and company seal.

If an individual applies for a refund:

  • first name, middle name and last name of the applicant;
  • his home address and passport details;
  • the account number of the individual payer and the bank details;
  • refund motivation;
  • the amount of compensation (exact) in words and numbers (with pennies);
  • signature individual.

The timing

According to the law, excessively paid funds can be returned within three years from the date of payment. If you deal with this issue later, nothing will come of it. But the deadline for returning state duties from the tax is much shorter. It is only a month from the date of application. If after 30/31 days you do not receive your money, a penalty may be imposed by the court. It will be paid to you by the tax service for late returns.
