Colombia is a river of great importance. Where is Colombia (river) located? Features of the water stream

Colombia is a river located in North America, in the northwestern part of the mainland. Its length is about 2 thousand kilometers. It flows through Washington, Oregon and British Columbia. The food is predominantly glacial; the nature of the current is fleeting. The full flow and elevation provided good conditions for hydroelectric power plants. It is in these places that it is most efficient to produce electricity.

Fish migration

Given where Colombia is located, the river is rich in fish rising from the ocean. There are many representatives of marine species, for example, salmonids (mykizha, chinook salmon, coho salmon). Often guests are sturgeons, which sail into these waters several times in their lives. After the construction of plants began in 1867, the salmon population decreased markedly. As a result, a law was passed prohibiting net fishing.



The migration of fish is mainly influenced by the constructed dams and dams, which, it is worth noting, is very rich in Colombia. Because of them, the river has a weak current in some areas. This helps to reduce the number of fry in the water stream. Previously, their journey from the ocean to the river took no more than three weeks, but now this figure is at least doubled. Such temporary changes significantly increase the risk of death, which leads to complete disappearance. In the upper reaches of the river there are certain species of fish that never descend into the ocean and cannot get to another part of the water due to dams. This significantly reduces the size of their population.

There is a certain type of fish, representatives of which can live in conditions of constant heat and slow flow. Mostly they eat salmon fry. This is what made the local authorities adopt a law to promote the fishing of these representatives of the fauna.

Ecology

Colombia is a river that is quite polluted. In addition to nuclear waste, other harmful substances and compounds fall into it, in particular, arsenic, pesticides, biphenyls, bacteria. Due to the poor ecology, fish with a huge amount of toxins are found in the pool and in the river itself. This not only affects the number of aquatic animals and contributes to the extinction of species, but also harms the health of the person who uses them. Currently, work is ongoing to improve water quality and restore the natural balance.
