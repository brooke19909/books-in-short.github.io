The concept of the radio, ranges

A walkie-talkie is a communication device operating on radio frequencies. Hence one of the possibilities of their classification: according to the frequency used.

Those who still have Soviet multifunctional products combining a lamp television, above which there is a record player under the lid, and a radio with keys under the television screen, remember that the latter had the inscriptions - HF (short waves), VHF (ultrashort waves), SW (medium waves), DW (decimeter waves). They helped the radio amateur find the right radio range and listen to their favorite program or catch unknown signals.

By the way, on one of these waves on March 19, 1965, an unknown Soviet radio amateur received a signal from the landing capsule Voskhoda-2 with the astronauts Leonov and Belyaev, which saved their life.

So, walkie-talkies can also be distributed on these "keys":

  • Low-frequency, or short-wave (HF).

It uses a frequency from 0.1 to 28 MHz. This is the so-called amateur range. On it, "sitting" tourists, hams.

  • Medium wave (NE).

The frequency from 26 to 30 MHz is used here. This frequency range is usually called ‚Äúcivilian‚Äù because it is intended for mass use by the civilian population both in the city and in the village.

  • The ‚Äúservice‚Äù range (LB) is from 30 to 50 MHz.

It has walkie-talkies of military and law enforcement agencies.

  • High Frequency Range (VHF).

Works from 136 to 172 MHz. It is also not for everyone and involves obtaining a license to use.

  • Ultra High Frequency (UHF).

It assumes a spread from 420 to 473 MHz.

Classification

As can be seen from this classification, for each wavelength range has formed its own audience. And this did not happen by chance, but largely due to the nature of the connection itself at each of the indicated frequencies.

So, low-frequency (from 0.1 to 28 MHz) radios are more applicable on a flat area away from sources of electromagnetic radiation, in other words - power lines. The fact is that obstacles such as hilly or mountainous terrain become an insurmountable obstacle for such waves, since they do not have enough length to go around them. And electromagnetic pulses give a lot of interference in communication.

For the urban environment, ‚Äúcivilian‚Äù and ‚Äúservice‚Äù equipment has been developed, adapted to operate at wavelengths, respectively, from 26 to 30 MHz and from 30 to 50 MHz. But she, of course, suffers from the power lines stretched out in the city, large buildings. Therefore, manufacturers integrate special noise suppressors into it.

Therefore, many users for urban conditions consider optimal high-frequency or ultra-high-frequency radios from 136 to 172 MHz and from 420 to 473 MHz, respectively.





This frequency classification defines all further walkie-talkie group distributions.

By mobility:

  • Stationary: in a special-purpose vehicle (ambulance, police, etc.), and portable: walkie-talkies that they carry with them in the manner of cell phones.

By territoriality:

  • for city and country;
  • for land and water transport;

By the nature of use:

  • amateur and professional.

You can classify radios further, but frequency gradation is the most defining option when choosing the right device.

Historical reference

The invention of a portable radio station is the work of many. Each of them, investing their developments, brought the idea closer to the modern embodiment. In the 19th century, at about the same time, parallel work was going on in different countries around the world, but only Malon Lumis (1872) was the first to patent authorship for wireless communications.

Despite this, until the end of the century, many more patents were registered, which belonged to such well-known scientists as G. Herz and N. Tesla. In Russia, the history of Russian radiotelephony is associated with A. S. Popov.

Mass production for the various needs of the common population, and not the country's military system, received radiotelephones after the Second World War. In the post-war Soviet Union, the emergence of a domestic version of a portable radiotelephone was associated with the name of the inventor Leonid Kupriyanovich and his "cell phone" the size of a palm and a disk dialer (1957). In addition, from the 60s and almost until recently, in the pilot project mode, the Altai system worked in some regions of the country, sharpened to the 150 band, then 330 MHz. But, like Kupriyanovich‚Äôs portable radio telephone, Altai began to be used for the needs of the nomenclature and special services and was not widely used throughout the country.

Currently, in Russia there are single enterprises producing portable walkie-talkies. For example, the design office "Berkut" produces a full range of devices for professional and amateur use. The products of this company find a lot of positive reviews.

In the United States, Motorola, which is one of the leaders in their production to this day, was primarily engaged in these devices. But she now has competitors with equally attractive offers - firms Kenwood, MegaJet, Baofeng and others.
