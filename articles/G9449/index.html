Minsk - St. Petersburg: the road as part of the journey

They say that the road from Minsk to St. Petersburg is a life-long adventure. A long journey through the territories of several countries, a changing landscape and climate, the romance of road trips - all this awaits every tourist who decides to visit the city of Petra, the cultural capital of Russia.





Two capitals

Minsk is the capital of Belarus, where, of course, there are many interesting places that are required to visit. However, this is just the capital of the state. But the city of Petra has a special place. This is also the center, but not the state, although in the past it was St. Petersburg, the cultural capital of Russia. A city built on swamps and bones. A real work of art that can even argue with Misty Albion. An incredible, mysterious atmosphere, a characteristic damp climate and constant rains. Imperial architecture, palaces and monuments of the Soviet era. The Neva River is lead-colored and the embankment recognizable by many people. This is not just a capital, but a real center, a place of concentration of Russian culture, showing the very window to Europe. This city is worth a visit, because all over the world you can no longer find anything like it.





The road from Minsk to St. Petersburg is an adventure. This is an expectation. Silent contemplation of villages and small towns flickering in the window. What to look for on such a trip? It will be just a crime to fly an airplane and deprive yourself of the opportunity to enjoy the smooth transition of the countryside into the urban one, saturated with the spirit of Peter's times.

Vehicle as a source of experience

In our century of rapid development of possible vehicles, we are forced to carefully choose what we will go on. We can travel from Minsk to St. Petersburg by bus, own car, train, and even fly by plane. On such a trip, you should not underestimate the road itself, because this is also part of the experience. Traditionally, the most atmospheric vehicles are considered ground vehicles: cars, buses and trains.

A car or a bus reveals the everyday life of people in different countries, cities and villages. They provide a particular advantage - freedom. The train is deprived of such an advantage. Rail transport is not able to maximize the beauty of nature or provide an opportunity to consider the features of urbanism. Only Minsk trains go from Minsk to St. Petersburg. They are inferior in comfort and service to Russian, but insignificantly. Most importantly, the train will demonstrate to the traveler only the most beautiful - the architecture of train stations and stations, as well as the almost untouched nature outside the transport arteries.

Car

If there is something that can immerse the traveler in the atmosphere of the places in which he travels, it is only a car. Personal transportation gives freedom. The ability to plan a route based on your own desires and surrounding attractions. Nothing limits either the choice of the road or the duration of the trip. Of course, the road by car from Minsk to St. Petersburg is a real live cinema. There will be everything on the route - villages and ghosts of the Soviet era destroyed and abandoned by people - cities in which new buildings are fancifully intertwined with the architecture of Stalin or even imperial times. Real nature, just a few meters from the highway. The beauty of the urban style will always be available for both the driver and passengers. Bus

Traditionally, after a personal car, there is always a bus. He is not able to give as much freedom as the car will provide, but he will not deprive all the beauties that are accessible to drivers of cars. Often, bus routes are built in such a way that the windows will show the sights of various cities. Traveling by bus will be much cheaper. But to lay the route yourself or change it along the way will not work.

Buses from Minsk to St. Petersburg could always boast of comfort. These are modern passenger buses with comfortable seats, air conditioning and large windows with excellent visibility. Almost all tourist buses on this route make long stops in Orsha, Vitebsk, Luga and Pskov. At least a passenger visits 4 cities along the way. There are no transfers on the route, so the traveler does not need to worry about anything. It is necessary to relax and pay attention to the fact that on ordinary days we do not notice. Then a trip with an average duration of 14 hours will pass unnoticed and interesting.





Distance

The distance between settlements excites many, and even scares some, but concern about this is superfluous. Between Minsk and St. Petersburg, the distance will be about 830 km. This is not a straight line distance, but the length of the road between two capitals. Take the train a little further and longer. This is due to the peculiarities of railway transport.
