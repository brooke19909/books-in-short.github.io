Sightseeing tour of the French capital

A journey into the history of Paris begins with the medieval streets of the Latin Quarter, one of the first populated areas of the city. Today there are many cafes, bookstores, here is the oldest Parisian street - Mufftar, which was walked by the ancient Romans. Cluny Palace - the former residence of representatives of the Pope in Paris - now opens its doors to tourists like the Museum of the Middle Ages. You can not ignore the Luxembourg Gardens - a place for dating lovers. And it is also obligatory to visit Notre Dame Cathedral, which became the hero of books and films.





Going to Paris in November, you need to visit the Louvre - one of the largest museums in the world. The museum collection is diverse: it includes paintings and royal jewels, ancient Greek and Roman sculptures, tapestries and household items, unique furniture and much more.





Leaving the Louvre, the Champs Elysees is pleasant to walk to the Arc de Triomphe in the Place de la Zvezda, peering along the way to the shopping galleries or the famous Monoprix department store, Museums of fine arts, discoveries and inventions, music halls or cabarets. The palace of Louis XIV at Versailles impresses even sophisticated guests with its luxury.

The most interesting museums in Paris





To visit the famous world-famous museums, it is good to go to Paris in November. Calmly and without crush you can inspect:

  • The Orsay Museum presents the Impressionists and decorative works in the art nouveau style.
  • The Army Museum owns one of the best weapons collections in Europe and the East.
  • The Jacquemart-Andre Museum holds rare paintings from the era of the Italian Renaissance and the French school of painting.
  • The exposition of paintings and drawings by Pablo Picasso is exhibited in the museum named after him.
  • The Rodin Museum is located in the mansion where the sculptor Auguste Rodin lived. Now his most famous creations are exhibited here.

What a Frenchman does not like to eat!

Paris at the beginning of November smells of croissants and coffee; sometimes it is impossible to walk past tables on the sidewalks. At the same time, both in expensive restaurants and in tourist cafes they sell them at an average price. Even from street stalls, only fresh, delicious dishes are always served here. The French are anxious about food, maybe that's why they remain the most slender in Europe.





Parisian restaurateurs prepare amazing dishes that you can try:

  • in the restaurants of the famous actor Gerard Depardieu;
  • in the gardens of the Palais Royal;
  • in a restaurant on the Eiffel Tower.




However, in restaurants, most often it is necessary to book a table in advance and dress accordingly the dress code. The success indicator of the institution is determined by the number of Michelin stars, their maximum number is three.





Reviews of tourists report that you can have a tasty, quick and inexpensive meal in many Parisian cafes located away from tourist routes. However, some prudent travelers warn that the French themselves eat at 12 and 20 hours, and at this time, institutions are usually crowded.

November events in Paris

  • 1-3 - chocolate festival;
  • 11 numbers everywhere mark the Day of the end of the First World War, Armistice Day, parades and processions are held;
  • third Thursday - the celebration of the young Beaujolais Nouveau wine and the opening of the French Wine Salon;
  • November 28, 135 thousand lights are lit, decorating the capital for Christmas.
  • Antique and art salons, festivals of culinary photography and music are also held in November. At the end of November, Christmas markets begin to open.
