Playa de Aro, Spain: hotels, weather, tours, photos, reviews

Russians are generally not spoiled by the abundance of the sun. That is why rest for our compatriots is mainly associated with a trip to the sea. And for those who are looking for new experiences "without taking off" from the sea, the hotels Playa de Aro (Spain) are perfect. We will tell you about all the features of this place.





Geographical position

On the northern coast of the Mediterranean Sea is the small resort town of Playa de Aro (Spain). The settlement belongs to the province of Catalonia and is located on the northernmost coast of the country, in the Costa Brava region. Climate

The whole life of cities on the Mediterranean coast is connected with this body of water, and the weather depends on it. Playa de Aro (Spain) is located in the north of the country, so the beach season begins here much later than, for example, on the southern Costa del Sol, and lasts much shorter. The climate on the Costa Brava is mild, Mediterranean, there is no such sweltering heat as in the south. The beach season with the opportunity to swim begins here only in June, although you can sunbathe from the beginning of May. They usually stop swimming in October, although there are years when the temperature of the water allows you to do this even in November. The high season in Playa de Aro lasts from July to the end of September. In July and September, the air warms up to 25 degrees on average, in August the thermometer usually shows 30 degrees. Winter in the city is short and rainy, it starts in December and ends in early February.





Hotel base

The resort of Playa de Aro (Spain) is quite young, it grew up on the site of a small fishing village just a few decades ago. Therefore, the hotel base is quite modern, there are many new, comfortable buildings. The city is dominated by 3 and 4 star hotels, there are not many luxury, high-class hotels, and you need to book places in advance much in advance. However, in other places it is worth taking care of the reservation ahead of time, the place is small and has its own permanent vacationers. In addition to hotels, in the city you can find apartments and private apartments, campsites, several hostels. All accommodations are located on three lines from the beach, and in accordance with this, as well as proximity to the city center, accommodation prices also vary.





Features of rest

The main attraction of the resort of Playa de Aro are its beaches, strewn with fine golden pebbles and sand. In total there are 8 places to relax on the beach. Remote beaches, such as Cala Pedrosa, are located in rocky coves surrounded by pine trees, and there is no special infrastructure for them. But here you can perfectly retire and enjoy the silence and clear water.

Gran is the central beach, with a length of about 2 km and a width of about 60 meters. There is everything that a tourist needs for relaxation: attractions, equipment rental service, lifeguards, entertainment for children, cafes, changing cabins. The remaining places are smaller and the infrastructure is poorer. Very popular with tourists is the beach of Sa Conca in the south of the city. There are conditions necessary for a good rest, including parking and a shower. Therefore, when buying tours to Playa de Aro (Spain), you should think about what the soul wants. A noisy but comfortable holiday or a secluded spot close to untouched nature.





sights

In addition to beaches, tourists are attracted to interesting places of Playa de Aro (Spain). Photos of prominent places can be seen in many Costa Brava travel guides. Arriving in this town, you need to see the medieval castle of Benedormiens, which is already 10 centuries old. Today it houses a museum of contemporary art. It is also worth visiting the local church of the Holy Virgin Mary, built in the Gothic style and protected by the state, as a historical monument. The historical center has preserved the buildings and the atmosphere of a traditional fishing town, tourists like to walk along the old streets.

Attractions are also rich in nearby cities, which are easily accessible by bus. You should definitely visit the ancient university city of Girona, in which there are enough attractions for more than one day of inspection.





Things to do

The main thing that tourists indulge in in Playa de Aro (Spain) is a beach pastime. You can diversify it by visiting small water parks operating at large hotels, and the large Aquadiver Park, walking along a long beautiful and cozy promenade. Along the promenade stretches a series of bars, restaurants and shops, which tourists enjoy visiting.

In Playa de Aro, cycling is very popular, there are all conditions for them. You can rent a vehicle and explore the surroundings of the resort. Hikers also find many interesting routes for themselves. The city is surrounded by small mountains and forests, which make it pleasant and absolutely safe to walk. Trips to nearby cities are another mandatory part of the cultural program for tourists in Playa de Aro.

For lovers of vibrant life, the resort has discos and nightclubs. In addition, various holidays, festivals, carnivals are regularly held here. For example, a beer festival or medieval reconstruction. Castell de Aro Castle regularly has a medieval market where you can try and buy local products and participate in workshops on various crafts.

Shopping is another important component of local activities and entertainment. Not far from the city is the famous Catalan outlet La Roca Village, where you can buy branded goods at affordable prices.

Tourists reviews

When choosing a place to relax on the Mediterranean, you should pay attention to the pleasant town of Playa de Aro (Spain). Reviews of tourists say that there is good service and reasonable prices. According to vacationers, the resort is perfect for families with children, excellent conditions are created for them and there is no strong heat, and numerous pools easily solve the problem of cool water.

Practical information

The most convenient way to get to Playa de Aro (Spain) is from Barcelona Airport. There are direct buses from there, which will take places to tourists in a few hours. There, at the airport, you can rent a car and move freely throughout the region. The beaches in the city are free, but you will have to pay a small amount for equipment rental. To arrive in Spain you will need a Schengen visa and medical insurance.
