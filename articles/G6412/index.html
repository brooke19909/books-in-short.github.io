Which reel to buy?

The acquisition of a coil, as well as the metal detector itself, is a responsible matter. Typical models that come with a metal detector are great for a start, but in the future, to work on different terrain, their variations will be required. It is necessary to determine the place of search in order to choose the appropriate size and shape of the coil.

In the ideal case, you should have several different ones, to always be in full readiness, since it is possible to change several places of search in a day. It is not necessary to replace the outdated detector with a new one; it is cheaper to purchase a coil for it. Types of Coils

The coil for the metal detector has two loops:

  • Transmitting - produces an electromagnetic field.
  • Host - monitors field changes. The field begins to deform when a metal object falls under the coil. The resulting distortion allows the operator to start searching for an item.


Coils are of the following types:

  1. Concentric The loops (receiving and transmitting) are spaced as far apart as possible. As a result of this, a symmetrical field is created, which makes it possible to better separate the finds that lie nearby in one wiring. The field has the shape of a cone. The concentric metal detector coil is designed for the entire set of existing finds. They are affected by increased mineralization of the soil.
  2. Mono. It is used on a pulsed metal detector. The receiving and transmitting loops are located nearby. It has the same properties as concentric.
  3. Imaging. A feature of this coil is an additional receiving loop. This allows the detector to most accurately determine the find.
  4. DD. The coil is used to search for non-ferrous metals. She has good sensitivity to small finds. The coil field has the shape of a flat bucket, which provides the same visibility at different depths. Not subject to the effects of high mineralization of the soil.

Coil shape

The detector coils in shape differ in:

  • Ellipsoidal. They well share the goals located nearby. The accuracy of the pinpoint mode on the coils in the shape of an ellipse is higher, and they allow you to search for objects in contaminated areas.
  • Round. These coils allow you to determine the subject at a greater depth than ellipsoidal.

DIY metal detector coil

It is not difficult to make a coil on your own, since it does not require great accuracy in manufacturing.

  1. A mandrel will be required to wind the coil. To do this, take a plank and draw a circle or the Latin letter D on its surface. Small cloves are stuffed around the perimeter of the figure.
  2. For the transmitting coil, take a small length of copper wire, the diameter of which is 0.45-0.6 mm, and wrap the mandrel 25 times. Do-it-yourself receiving coil for a metal detector is done similarly, only a copper wire is taken with a diameter of 0.2 mm.
  3. Tie the coils with a thread every one and a half centimeters and remove from the mandrel.
  4. Impregnate coils using epoxy or varnish.
  5. Dry the product well during the day.
  6. Wrap coils with electrical tape or tape FUM.
  7. Make shielding of products by wrapping them with thin foil.
  8. Wrap a wire without insulation on top of the foil, preferably tinned to improve electrical conductivity.
  9. The detector coil housing can be made of polystyrene foam or expanded polystyrene. For strength, its outer and inner surfaces are reinforced.
  10. Make reel grooves using roller cutter. The depth of the grooves should be such that after they are immersed on top, it is possible to fill the coils with epoxy resin.


So, the homemade coil for the metal detector is ready and placed in the housing.

Quick coil repair

Sometimes, unsuccessfully waving a device, you can hit a coil on a stone or tree roots. The metal detector begins to "scream", and a chip appears on the coil. Such a defect must be filled with epoxy resin, and the detector will still work properly. Experienced search engines advise in a hardware store to buy an epoxy resin in the form of a pencil or two syringes and always carry it with you. If a chip appears, pour it with glue and after a few hours continue the search. Most importantly, you have to wait a certain time for the resin to freeze. It is best to repair the detector coil in the evening, and in the morning place the cleavage with tape for additional protection, and you can work.



For faster repairs, you can use superglue. It freezes much faster. In the backpack, the treasure hunter should always have a tube of superglue, strips from the bicycle chamber (several pieces) and a medical tourniquet. Having noticed the chip, it is necessary to carefully remove the dirt from it, drip a few drops of glue, tighten it tightly with a tourniquet after two minutes, capturing the undamaged parts of the coil, and stick a strip of the camera on the same glue. This, of course, is a temporary repair, but it allows you to use the metal detector for another two days. At home, the coil can be repaired more thoroughly. To keep the product in its proper form, you can buy protection on the coil of a metal detector or make it yourself from improvised means, for example, wrap it with electrical tape or a dense cloth, attach the bottom of a plastic bucket to the outer surface.
