Gravitational anomaly: definition, meaning, features and interesting facts

In the field of theoretical physics, the gravitational anomaly is an example of a gauge anomaly, one of the effects of quantum mechanics. This is the difference between the observed acceleration of gravity or gravity on the surface and the corresponding value of the predicted model of the planet‚Äôs gravitational field. As a rule, there are a number of simplifying assumptions, including self-gravity and rotational motions, due to which the Earth takes the form of an ellipsoid.

Anomaly and gravity

The value of the anomaly, as a rule, is much less than the value of gravity itself, with which the concepts of the total mass of the planet, its rotation and flattening are associated. Thus, gravitational anomalies on the Earth are just local variations of the gravitational field. Nevertheless, these phenomena cause genuine geophysical and geological interest and are one of the typical tasks of geophysics.





Reasons and features

Gravitational anomaly can occur in different parts of the world, most often in the mountains. Lateral oscillations of gravitational anomalies are associated with abnormal density distributions inside the Earth. Gravity measures help to understand the internal structure of the planet. Large-scale anomalies of the gravitational field can be detected from space as a side effect of the gravitational missions of satellites.





Gravity Recovery and Climate

It turns out that man himself can influence processes only subject to nature itself, launching satellites that can detect gravitational changes throughout the Earth. In addition, these changes can be represented in the form of gravitational anomalies of temporal variations. Gravity plays an important role, influencing, among other things, the planet‚Äôs self-regulation and its climatic processes.





Directions of the gravitational field

The gravitational field of the Earth is depicted in two main directions: anomalies of gravity of the map and the map of the Earth's geoid.

  • The gravitational anomaly of the map shows how much the actual gravitational field of the Earth itself differs from the gravitational field of its surface. She emphasizes the difference in gravity on the surface of the Earth. Often this happens in places of unusual concentration of certain landscape forms, for example, the presence of mountain ranges or oceanic depressions.
  • A geoid is a hypothetical surface of the Earth, which is the average sea level in the absence of winds, currents and most tides. A geoid is a useful surface reference material. It defines the horizontals, and gravity acts perpendicular to them.

Obtaining an accurate geoid model has proven to be a daunting task. Data from several dozen satellites, along with surface measurements, had to be combined to create a model of the gravitational field. The identification of large-scale features has fully justified itself, which cannot be said about the features of a local nature, where it is often necessary to accurately describe time-varying gravitational effects, such as those associated with the hydrological cycle.





Remote Sensing and Cartography

These methods provide the critical contribution of many scientific models used in oceanography, hydrology, geology, and related disciplines. Remote sensing and cartography are used for various purposes and tasks, including:

  • measuring changes in the mass of polar ice;
  • measuring water changes on land;
  • definition of shallow and deep ocean currents;
  • determination of sea level changes due to temperature fluctuations;
  • a more detailed understanding of such mass exchange as the atmosphere-ocean;
  • descriptions of the forces that generate the earth's geomagnetic field ;
  • understanding of the internal forces of the planet, the movement of tectonic plates as a result of earthquakes and volcanic eruptions;
  • improved forecasting of natural hazards.

Interpretation of gravitational and magnetic anomalies

Magnetic anomalies are mainly generated by variations of the crystalline basement and magmatic material. Most deposits can be considered magnetically transparent, that is, having little susceptibility.

Thus, magnetic data interpretations can be a useful and productive way of investigating the deepest and often the most fundamental structures.

The gravitational anomaly on a lake or land may be associated with a contrast in the densities of subsurface layers (deep penetrations, faults, river basin boundaries, and so on).

What does GA mean?

The gravitational anomaly, "GA" in abbreviated form, is a general term for describing cases of fixing not quite ordinary gravitational field indicators. This definition explains the moments that arise in cases of contradiction between the generally accepted mathematical model of gravitational theory and the other side, which does not fit into the general framework of the physical nature of gravitational interaction.

Gravity Anomalies

Changes in gravity, which are associated with such a property of the earth's surface as density heterogeneity, do not have an obvious regularity and indicate a certain deviation from the generally accepted values, are called gravity anomalies. As a rule, the difference between normal and abnormal gravity is minimal. However, even minor deviations help to better study the properties of the earth's crust, as well as to search for minerals.

Gravitational anomaly occurs both on the surface of the Earth and in its bowels. An important external factor is the topography. As for the underground causes, these include the movements of the layers vertically and horizontally, as well as changes in the density composition of these layers. A phenomenon such as the gravitational anomaly is important in geology. Gravimetric methods are widely used during the search for deposits of iron ore, oil and gas.
