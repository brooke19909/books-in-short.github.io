How many people were on the Titanic? How many survived and how many people died on the Titanic?

One of the most tragic and at the same time mysterious events of the 20th century is the collapse of the largest passenger liner of its time - the Titanic. Specifications "Titanic"

The Titanic at the time of its creation was the largest ship that ever existed before. Its length was 259.8 m, height - 18.4 m, width - more than 28 m, draft - 10.54 m, displacement - 52 310 tons, weight - 46 330 tons. Moreover, it had a capacity of 55,000 horsepower and Developed a speed of 24 knots, which was achieved thanks to three screws, two four-cylinder engines and a steam turbine. Such dimensions and the presence of fifteen partitions created the illusion of unsinkability.





Now let's find out how many people on board the Titanic could be located at the same time. According to technical specifications, the ship could accommodate 2556 passengers and 908 crew members. That is, even initially it was assumed that in the event of a large-scale catastrophe, less than half of those who could potentially be on the liner would be able to escape. But, most likely, no one even thought that such a disaster could happen on a "unsinkable" ship.

But, of course, the potential capacity of the ship does not yet give an exact answer to the question of how many people were on the Titanic at the time of the disaster. We will talk about this below.

Departure

The Titanic made its first and, as it turned out later, last flight in the direction of Southampton (Britain) - New York (USA) across the Atlantic Ocean. Departure was scheduled for April 10, 1912.





Edward John Smith, one of the most experienced sailors of the time, was appointed captain. He had over twenty five years of command experience.

After loading passengers on the appointed day at 12:00, the Titanic set off on its last journey.

Number of passengers and crew

Now, let's still find out how many people were on the Titanic when he went on his fateful voyage.

According to the official chronicle, the crew of the liner at its exit from Southampton was 891 people. Of these, 390 people of the ship's crew, eight of whom were officers, the rest - maintenance personnel.

With the calculation of passengers, the situation is more complicated, since their number is constantly changing. This was due to the fact that part of the passengers got off, and part, on the contrary, boarded the ship at intermediate stops in Cherbourg and Queenstown.

943 passengers departed from Southampton, of which 195 traveled first class. But by the time it entered the open ocean, the number of passengers had increased to 1317 people. In the first class, 324 of them were lucky to travel, in the second and third there were, respectively, 128 and 708 people. It should be noted that among the passengers there were 125 children.

Thus, we see that, with a total passenger capacity of the Titanic of 2556 people, it was loaded a little more than half in its first and last voyage. It should be noted that the provided number of boats would not even be enough to save all passengers, not to mention the crew.

Among the famous passengers of the Titanic, one can distinguish millionaires John Jacob Astor and Benjamin Guggenheim, journalist William Stead, Assistant to the American President Archibald Bath.

Thus, we answered the question how many people were on the Titanic.

Swimming

As already mentioned, after entering Cherbourg and Queenstown, the liner entered the open ocean and headed along the transatlantic highway to the shores of North America. The Titanic was set at 21 knots with a maximum possible 24 knots.

During the trip, the weather was fine. The voyage itself took place without any special incidents and deviations from the course.

On April 14, 1912, having covered a total of 2689 kilometers of the Atlantic route, the Titanic reached a point near Newfoundland, where it had a fatal meeting with an iceberg.

Clash

Icebergs are fairly frequent "companions" of ships in the North Atlantic. But the "Titanic" was moving, as was believed, at a safe course, at which at that time of the year there should not be ice blocks. Nevertheless, on April 14, closer to midnight, their meeting took place.

It is generally accepted that, due to the darkness, the ship's crew did not see the iceberg. But it is not so. He was noticed by the forward looking when the ship was at a distance of 650 meters from the iceberg.

The command realized that the fate of the ship was a foregone conclusion. The designer said that the ship will remain on the surface for no more than an hour and a half.

Evacuation of passengers

Immediately an order was issued to rescue passengers, especially women and children. The team prepared the boats.

In order to prevent panic among passengers, the true reasons for the evacuation were hidden from them, they said that it was carried out in order to prevent a possible collision with an iceberg. It was not difficult to convince people of this, because, as mentioned above, the strike on the Titanic was practically not felt. Many did not even want to leave a comfortable ship and change boats.

But when the water began to gradually flood the ship, it was already impossible to hide the true state of things. There was panic on board, which intensified after the Titanic began to heel. The number of people saved

In order to find out how many people died on the Titanic, you need to determine two mandatory introductory notes. With the help of them it will be possible to answer this question. According to official statistics, a total of 712 people were saved. Of these, 212 crew members and 500 passengers. The largest percentage of people saved is among first-class passengers, 62%. The number of survivors in the second and third grade was 42.6% and 25.6%, respectively. At the same time, only 23.6% of the team members could be saved.

These figures are explained by the fact that the order was given primarily to save passengers, not crew members. The greater number of surviving people traveling in the first class is due to the fact that the lower the class, the farther it was from the deck of the ship. Consequently, people had less access to lifeboats.

How many people died?

Having determined how many people survived on the Titanic, as well as bearing in mind the initial number of passengers and crew, it is not difficult to answer the question of the number of those killed during the crash.

1,496 people died, that is, more than 67% of the people who were on the ship at the time of the collision with the ice block. Including 686 casualties among crew members and 810 passengers. These numbers indicate a poor organization of salvation for people in distress.
