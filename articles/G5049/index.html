Ontology is a branch of philosophy that studies being. In the classical sense, ontology is knowledge of the extremely general. One of the main questions of ontology is: what exists? The main concepts in this science are being, movement, time, space, the form of being (existential, ideal, material), properties, structure. Thus , ontology tries to describe in the most general form the universe of the existing, not limited by the data of specific sciences and, possibly, not reducible to them. The questions posed by the ontology are a very ancient topic of philosophy, which goes back to Parmenides and other dos to the outskirts. An important contribution to the development of ontology issues was made by Aristotle and Plato.

In the philosophy of the Middle Ages , the ontological problem was central, whether abstract objects (universals) exist. In the philosophy of the 20th century , the following philosophers were specially engaged in ontological problems: Nikolai Hartman, Martin Heidegger, etc. In modern philosophy, the problems of consciousness ontology are of particular interest. What does ontology study? the subject of this science is being, defined as the unity and completeness of all types of reality: objective, virtual, social, subjective, physical. Reality, we are traditional we associate it with matter (the material world) and spirit (the spiritual world, including the concepts of souls, God) and subdivide (materialists) into living, inert and social matter (which entails formalism and a look at the person as an impersonal person in general). To being refers to what can be thought. Its opposite is unthinkable nothing, and also (in the philosophy of Aristotelianism) a still-non-being possibility. In the past century, in existentialism and phenomenology, being was identified with man as the only being with the ability to think and ask a question about being.

However, classical metaphysics understood God as being. People, as being, have will and freedom. Social ontology is the doctrine of the being of society.
