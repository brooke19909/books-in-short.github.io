San Remo beaches, Italy - overview, features, attractions and reviews

San Remo has become famous all over the world for the Italian song festival, although you can still find a lot of interesting things in this city. The city is known for its mild climate, chic casinos, a huge base of cycling. Located on the coast of the Riviera di Ponente, it has a beautiful long coastline with well-equipped beaches and recreation areas. We present you the best beaches of San Remo and its environs, where you can comfortably and safely relax yourself or with family and children.





City Beach San Remo

Within the city there are two main beaches. The first sandy, the most beautiful, is located between two ports - Porto Vecchio, which means Old Port, and Portosole (Sunny Port). It is practically in the very center of the city. From the train station 10 minutes on foot - a pleasant walk through Corso Trento Trieste, a promenade that stretches along the entire coast.

Here you will find some of San Remo's most luxurious beaches in Italy , which can offer all kinds of services. For example, Italian baths, rental of beach umbrellas and sun loungers, swimming lessons, yoga and Pilates classes. There is a field for playing football, playgrounds for beach volleyball, a children's pool. Boat enthusiasts can rent canoes. The sea and the sun awaken appetite very quickly, you can satisfy your hunger in a local restaurant or bar.

San Remo has a long tradition in sailing and in the port area it is noticeable like nowhere else. Here, besides the beach of San Remo, there is a city yacht club, several famous sailing schools. Those who wish can buy or charter a boat for themselves. Tourists love this beach in the city center also because the largest green zone is located nearby - the Ormond City city park and the Nobel Garden with many outlandish varieties of plants and flowers.





Tre Ponti Beach, San Remo

Another of the beaches of San Remo, loved by tourists, is located in the Tre Ponti area. This is the largest free beach equipped with showers. There are two bars and a restaurant, several commercial offices where you can rent beach umbrellas, sun loungers, canoes, pedal boats. The coast is heterogeneous - in some places sand, in some places pebbles. The coast of Tre Ponti is popular with fans of surfing, especially young people love it, but it is also suitable for families with children.

If you like peace and quiet, then you should know that in the high season there is nowhere for an apple to fall, as, indeed, on other beaches of the coast, especially in July and August. By car, it is almost impossible to get to the beaches of San Remo at this time. It is recommended to come by bicycle or come on foot. By the way, here passes the longest cycle track in Europe, which stretches along the coast for 24 km, from San Lorenzo al Mare to Ospedaletti, crossing several beautiful places. Bicycles can always be rented - this type of transport is very popular here. The bike route along the Riviera dei Fiori coastal park is well worth the ride.





Cala del Orsi Beach - Bussana

Bussana is the largest district of San Remo and is divided into three parts: new, old and marine. It is believed that the best beaches of San Remo are located within the sea Bussana, which overlooks the beautiful bay. There are public beach areas and commercial ones. The most popular beach is Cala degli Orsi. According to many tourists, this is one of the most beautiful places on the Italian Riviera. Ospedaletti beaches

Southwest of San Remo, a ten minute drive from the center, there is an Ospedaletti beach area. This is a small suburb surrounded by lush greenery, located in a bay between Capo Nero and Capo Sant'Ampelio. Beach sections here are divided into small sections by cliffs and are characterized by clear water. At least you will find here 6 free beaches and 5 commercial sites. There is a wonderful restaurant that has long glorified the area with excellent cuisine. You can eat on the large beautiful terrace with sea views. Another local attraction is the free beach, located between the commercial baths of Baia del Sole and Byblos, where dogs are allowed to relax.





La Pigna Old Town

You will not be full of beaches alone - you will definitely want to get intellectual pleasure. Therefore, it is worth mentioning not only about the beaches, about the sights of San Remo in Italy, there is also something to tell. Actually, almost all of them are compactly assembled in the old city, this area is called La Pina. In the 11th century there were powerful fortifications that local residents created to protect against Saracens. Ancient buildings make themselves felt - almost the entire area is a maze of narrow streets interspersed with stone stairs and round arches. All these streets will sooner or later lead you to the sea - they, like garlands, cross the terrain from the top of the hill to the coast.





Attractions San Remo

There is something to see in the old town. You can start with a huge stone arch called the "Gate of St. Stephen." This is a 14th-century building and the main gate to the old city. Another attraction is Via Palma, formerly the main street of La Pigny. In 1538, here, in the house of Manard, Pope Paul III stayed during a trip to Nice, and in 1794, the Palazzo dei Conti Sappia Rossi received Napoleon Bonaparte. The gardens of Queen Montenegro Helena are located on a hilltop and rest against the Sanctuary of the Madonna della Costa, which since ancient times served as a guide for sailors.
