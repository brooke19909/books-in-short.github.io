Hotel Cleopatra Annex 3 * (Ayia Napa, Cyprus): description, rooms and reviews

Cyprus was hardly divided between Greece and Turkey, drawing a green neutral line through the entire island. The result was the Republic of Turkey, where Turks reside, respectively, and about twice the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Republic of Cyprus, inhabited by Greek Cypriots. It is in this part that the Smartline Cleopatra Annex 3 * hotel is located.

Cyprus, uniting the traditions of two peoples in its culture, nevertheless, retained its special mentality. Therefore, rest here always turns out to be very colorful. Ayia Napa is considered one of the youngest and most fun resorts in the country. Its name in exact translation means "holy avenue of trees" or simply "holy forest". The word "ayah" is an abbreviated version of the Greek "agia", that is, "holy", and "napa" in the language of the ancient Hellenes meant "many trees in a row."

Once there it was. A dense forest grew on the seashore, then a church appeared in its place, and a village nearby. Since about 1970, she began to get upset and gradually turned into a first-class resort. Since he is still very young, all the hotels here are modern and new.

The same applies to Smartline Cleopatra Annex 3 *. Ayia Napa will be revealed from the best and most memorable sides to everyone who decides to opt for this very budget hotel for Cyprus, since all the most interesting things will be with its guests within walking distance.

Location

For lovers of noisy parties and exciting shopping, the Cleopatra Annex 3 * hotel occupies an unusually convenient position. Ayia Napa, the city and resort where it is located, is located on the southeast side of the Greek part of Cyprus, about 12 km from the city of Protaras, 37 km from Larnaca, where the airport operates, and 83 km from Nicosia.





These and other settlements are connected with Ayia Napa by excellent highways on which regular buses run. Therefore, hotel guests find it easy to visit the capital of Cyprus and its most famous cities on their own, without buying sightseeing tours.

Please note that just across the road from the hotel in question is another one belonging to the same owner. Its name is Cleopatra 3 *, which is very similar to Cleopatra Annex 3 *, which often leads to errors and misunderstandings. The hotel, in the name of which there is no word "Anex", is slightly newer, and the conditions there are somewhat different.

In this article we provide information only about the three-star hotel Cleopatra Anex. It is located in the central part of Ayia Napa, on the street named after the glorious Yuri Gagarin, house 7. True, in Cyprus our astronaut is called in the Greek manner Giouri Kangari, which is written on street signs.

One can say that one of the central streets of Ayia Napa, where there are many bars for every taste, restaurants, cafes, shops with clothes and groceries, night clubs, lies just five steps from the hotel. Also close to Cleopatra Annex 3 * there are offices where you can rent any vehicle. In Cyprus, this is not unimportant for educational vacations, since, owning a car or a bike, you can travel around the entire island without reference to excursion programs. But the beach is not very close to the hotel. Before it you need to either take a bus or walk about 15-20 minutes.

How to get there

The path to Cleopatra Annex 3 * is straightforward. From Russia to Cyprus, everyone does not get on airplanes. The central airport is located in Larnaca, from where Ayia Napa is very close, only 37 km. You can fly to Paphos from Moscow, Krasnodar and Nizhny Novgorod, but then you will have to travel 180 km to Ayia Napa (more than 2 hours), which, of course, is inconvenient.

Arriving in Larnaca, you can get to the hotel on a pre-ordered paid transfer or by public transport. The most convenient option is to take a taxi. On weekdays, it will cost only 12 euros. On weekends, the amount rises to 15 euros. A bus ticket will cost only 7 euros, but this is if you use the services of the company "Intercity".

You can also rent a car right at the airport, which will result in 50 euros per day. In principle, renting a car in the city costs about the same. There is a slight inconvenience, consisting in the left-hand traffic existing in Cyprus. Those who are not used to it will be difficult to adapt in the early days.

In the lobby, for the convenience of guests, there is a lobby bar and tennis tables. For business people, the hotel offers a good conference room equipped with the necessary electronics.

By international standards, this hotel is small, since it provides only 25 rooms for tourists. Their design, as well as the entire hotel, is made in the Art Nouveau style. In other tables, some similarities of shelves mounted in the rear wall of the bed replace, and the table is a shelf mounted in the wall. Thirdly, there are no shelves or a table at all, only a built-in wardrobe and several modern plastic chairs. However, all rooms are very spacious and bright, which is helped by large panoramic windows. View of them on the streets of the city and the hotel pool.

Nutrition

Hotel Smartline Cleopatra Annex 3 * in Ayia Napa has a lobby bar, an a la carte restaurant and a buffet restaurant. Here breakfasts are served, served on a "self-service" system. You can take tours only with breakfast, which is mainly offered by tour operators. You can also take a ticket to this hotel with breakfast and dinner, but this is not very practiced, since there are dozens of restaurants and cafes nearby on the streets that offer all kinds of dishes. Each tourist can choose for himself what is most like it.

The hotel's restaurant is also decorated in a modern style, and to some guests it resembles a dining room. Breakfast here is continental. Sausage, cheese, eggs, butter, bread, jam, cereal, milk, and pastries are offered. From drinks coffee (instant), tea and juice.





Leisure

Cleopatra Annex 3 * is a hotel aimed at young people who like to spend time actively, using the entertainment facilities of the city's infrastructure, rather than sitting in their rooms. Therefore, there is no animation provided, no sports grounds. Guests can entertain themselves in the walls of the hotel in the bar, which is open almost until the morning. In the afternoon, a great time for those who do not want to go to the sea is possible in the recreation area by the pool. It is not very large in area, but deep. Around installed in a sufficient number of sunbeds and umbrellas. For sports enthusiasts, the hotel has a games room with tennis and pool tables, and bicycles can be rented at the reception for exploring the surroundings.

Conditions for children

In principle, the Smartline Cleopatra Annex 3 * hotel (Cyprus, Ayia Napa), as already noted above, is designed for young people and everyone who plans to relax actively. For parents with children there is practically nothing here. Moreover, many Russian tour operators claim that from the end of May until the twentieth of September, guests with children are not accepted here, and some tour operators warn that in Cleopatra Annex 3 * children are not allowed at all.

At the same time, the hotel‚Äôs official website says that tourists with children of any age are always welcome here. Moreover, cots are provided for babies in the room, and payment is not made for children under 2 years old. Older children are only allowed in basic beds with parents or separately, as extra beds are not provided. Therefore, if you decide to go to this hotel with kids, you need to further clarify the information when booking.





Beaches

Cleopatra Annex is a hotel in Ayia Napa, ideally located relative to the center and not very convenient with respect to the sea, which you need to walk for more than a quarter of an hour. This distance can also be covered by bus. The ticket price in them is 1.5 euros.

The nearest beaches from the hotel are two, one on the right side, the other on the left. The first is called Limanaki, which means ‚ÄúPouring‚Äù. The coverage here is sandy, the sea is excellent, plenty of water activities. But for the umbrella and sunbed will have to pay 7.5 euros.

The second, less untwisted, is located in a small bay. It is less crowded, but the water in the sea is not very clear. But on this beach there are tents, there is a wonderful cafe.

The most popular beach in Nissi in Cyprus , which translates as "island". There really is a small island, which can be reached through a narrow sandy strip. Hotel Nissi is approximately 2 km away. A taxi to him costs 8 euros. There is a lot of entertainment (parachute, banana, ping-pong, catamarans, water scooters), excellent landscaping (restaurants, bars, discos, showers, hygiene rooms) and the purest yellow sand, which many call it golden. But there are always a lot of people on Nissi, which not all vacationers like.

Almost immediately after this beach there is another, more calm. It is called Adams Beach. There is a water slide and other attractions, but the entry into the sea is not gentle, but ends with a small ledge.

On all beaches you can see beautiful fish right on the shore. There are even more at a depth, so diving is in great demand in Ayia Napa. But in Cyprus, unfortunately, beach vacations are possible only from the end of spring and somewhere until mid-autumn. The rest of the time it is cool, and in winter it can even briefly decorate the earth with a weak snow cover.
