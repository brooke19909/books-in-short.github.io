The most interesting sights of Kemerovo

One of the most attractive cities in Russia is Kemerovo. It is located in the foothills of the Kuznetsk Alatau on the banks of the Tom River. This is a fairly young city, formed on the site of the village Shcheglova in 1918, which has a three-hundred-year-old biography. Despite its youth, the attractions of Kemerovo make up a decent list. It has a wide selection of places to visit. The Tom River flows through the entire settlement, dividing it into two parts. The central part of the city is on the left bank. There is a wide promenade with excellent views of the Krasnogorodsky hills. The modern city has an international airport.



The sights of Kemerovo are, first of all, the most beautiful architectural structures, including the Znamensky Cathedral. The construction of the building was completed in 1996. It immediately gained popularity among visitors and residents of the city. No less impressive building is the Church of the Holy Trinity, dated 2008. It rises in the park area at the intersection of two avenues - Chemists and Leningradsky. In the immediate vicinity is another religious building - the Church of the Holy Trinity, whose construction was completed in 2008. The temple is a brick one-story building in which the belfry is located.



Continuing to see the sights of Kemerovo, we should mention the Chapel of the Icon of the Mother of God "Joy of All Who Mourn". This is a one-dome church built in the traditional Russian style in 1990. It is located on Cathedral Square and serves as a baptismal. The next architectural building that is recommended to be seen is the Church of Dmitry Donskoy. It rises on Patriotov Street and is a small wooden building with an octagonal dome and a bell tower. And on the Mariinsky highway is the church-chapel of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker. It is a brick tent building of the octagon type on the four.

The sights of Kemerovo can be supplemented by a list of numerous museums, each of which deserves attention. The most popular is the Museum of Local Lore, which is a kind of repository of natural monuments and the history of Kuzbass. The Museum of Fine Arts is considered no less interesting, the collection of which includes more than 5,000 works of icon painting, graphics, painting, sculpture.

Among other attractive places of the city can be noted the Drama Theater. On the left bank of the Iskitimka River lies a beautiful Victory Park. Here you can see many monuments, an exhibition of military equipment and guns. In 2006, a steam locomotive monument (a locomotive of the 50s) was erected. No less popular is the City Garden - a cozy park with various attractions.
