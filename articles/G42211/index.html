History

Numerous excavations by archaeologists confirm the fact that human settlements at the location of the city of Herceg Novi were still in the ancient period. According to one version, Troy was glorified in the legends here.

Herceg Novi was founded in 1382 by the king of Bosnia Tarko I. Initially, the town was called Sveti Stefan. Now this name is another resort city of Montenegro. Herceg Novi received its current name somewhat later in honor of the Duke Stepan Vukchich - another ruler.



The history of the town as a whole is dramatic. However, like almost all Balkan countries and cities. In Herceg Novi, the Spaniards and Turks ruled, the Venetians and the French, the Russians, as well as the Austro-Hungarians. In the twentieth century, the city became part of the Republic of Yugoslavia, and after it broke up, it became located in independent Montenegro.
