15 metro stations on the purple branch in St. Petersburg

St. Petersburg is considered the Northern Capital, but the branching of the local metro is much inferior to Moscow. But its stations practically do not differ in uniqueness and beauty. It’s enough to drive along at least one of the subway lines.

Fifth metro line of St. Petersburg

There are only 5 lines in the St. Petersburg subway. Their official names are used more often in travel booklets and city guides. Citizens call the metro line by numbers. So the purple metro line in St. Petersburg sounds more familiar as the “Fifth Line” or “Line No. 5”.

She connected the Primorsky northwest of the city, through the central part with the southeastern Frunze district. By 2018, 12 stations are located on this line:

  1. "International";
  2. Bucharest
  3. "Volkovskaya";
  4. "Bypass channel";
  5. "Zvenigorod";
  6. "Garden";
  7. Admiralteyskaya;
  8. "Sports";
  9. Chkalovskaya
  10. Krestovsky Island;
  11. "Old village";
  12. "Commandant Avenue."

Technical details of the line

The beginning of full operation is considered to be December 2008. Prior to this, since December 1991, only the northern stations connected to the Right-Bank branch (Chevertaya Line) participated in the transportation of passenger traffic.

The total length of the Frunze-Primorsky line is 18.1 kilometers, and with three new stations it will increase to 20.1 kilometers. Trains run at intervals of 2 to 10 minutes. And the operation mode of all stations is approximately the same: 05:30 - 00:00. And only on holidays can it change.

Interesting Facts

And it is here that the station of the deepest occurrence in Russia is located. This is the Admiralteyskaya located at a depth of 102 meters. It belongs to the purple metro line in St. Petersburg.
