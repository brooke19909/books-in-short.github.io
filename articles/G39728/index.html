Types of information for finding a treasure

Treasures and treasures can be sought with direct and indirect information. Direct information most accurately indicates the path to the treasures, as it is based on the knowledge of their location. For example, a descendant is looking for the treasure of his relative (grandfather, great-grandfather), knowing exactly or approximately its location. In almost all cases, the search is successful. Only the less expected value of the treasure can disappoint, especially if the material costs and the efforts invested in its search exceed the size of the found.





Indirect information is based on assumptions, conjectures, an intuitive instinct that tells where to look for a treasure. In this case, there is no guarantee of complete success, but there is little chance of making yourself richer. Here it all depends on the luck and luck of the treasure hunter. The treasure of Genghis Khan

To find the grave of Genghis Khan - the head of the most extensive of world states, the lord of millions of souls, the owner of countless wealth - the cherished dream of treasure hunters around the world.



Mongolia, Kazakhstan, Transbaikalia, Altai: the place of his burial was kept in the strictest confidence, and the grave itself was razed to the ground. About 2000 thousand people took part in the funeral of the great ruler, but they were literally chopped into pieces by 800 equestrian soldiers from the guard of Genghis Khan. These soldiers were also killed that day. All this was done in order to keep secret the location of the tomb of the ruler. However, the matter was not limited to these bloodthirsty actions. So, the burial area was cleared of any unauthorized persons by specially created patrols. According to one version, a riverbed was laid at the burial site to completely hide the tracks. The expeditions visited the territory of Mongolia more than once in order to find the grave, and therefore the treasure of Genghis Khan, which by the most conservative standards costs millions of dollars. But all efforts were in vain.

A few tips for a beginner treasure hunter

How to find treasures? As practice has shown, the best place to look is a village destroyed during the war or during the revolution.

To determine the location of such destroyed settlements, it is necessary to acquire topographic maps of a large scale. Some of them should depict the area in pre-war form, and part - in the modern. From them you can find out where and what structures were located earlier (chapels, estates, churches). They will not be difficult to find with a compass. In the search area, it is advisable to interview the local population for legends that are worth considering, but believe them unconditionally. Indeed, out of 100 exciting legends, approximately 10 will contain a grain of truth, and in the best case, only one of the narratives will be real. That is, it is important to be able to filter information.

Of course, it is highly advisable to look for treasures with a metal detector by carefully examining the surface.





It is possible that items of interest can be found under the turf. Well, if the history of the object has more than one hundred years, for which a sufficient number of coins could have been lost there. Due to the large amount of metal trash, among which the desired valuable items may be present, it is important that the metal detector has the ability to determine the degree of rejection of the trash, having sound and visual recognition.

Subtleties

In order to know how to find treasures, you need to understand how the hiding places are arranged. Household items (utensils, icons, clothes, samovars) people were put in boxes and chests. They dug a hole near the house, where they hid them. Top pit sprinkled with stones or rubbish. This is especially true for places where the front line passed. A distinctive feature of the wells is the high safety of the items in it.

Attic - a convenient place to find a treasure

Convenience for sheltering values ‚Äã‚Äãwas represented by attics. Attic bookmarks, which are often resorted to by urban residents, have several stages in their history:

  • the beginning of the 20th century, after the First Russian Revolution;
  • 1917 revolutionary coup;
  • The 40s, when a decree was issued on the surrender of weapons by the population and increased responsibility for the propaganda of fascism, which meant the preservation of objects depicting fascist symbols.

The search for treasures in the attic can be greatly facilitated by putting yourself in the place of a person who wants to hide his valuables. Roofing beams, rafters, niches, recesses in the walls and brickwork, any ventilation openings, subtle hidden corners that are easily remembered visually - these are the places that can please the seeker with something of value. The use of metal detectors here will not be effective due to the large number of construction debris and water pipes, creating a strong background. By the way, there is always a void between the ceiling of the room and the attic floor, in which you can easily hide a large suitcase. True, the examination of these places is complicated by the fact that the process of emptying and raising the floorboards creates considerable noise and takes a lot of time. Therefore, it is preferable to investigate such voids in uninhabited houses.

Where to find a treasure besides the attic and the earth? Treasures were often found in rivers and canals.





Over the years, a large number of coins and all kinds of objects have accumulated in reservoirs that can now have historical and artistic value. Many of these treasures were dumped into the rivers in order to get rid of evidence, especially in troubled times. In reservoirs you can find royal awards, sabers, daggers, icons, incense sticks. Extraction of finds requires warm weather, a good tray and a shovel. Soil needs to be scooped up and washed out.
