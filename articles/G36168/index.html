New Year's house from salt dough. Master Class

In the last weeks of December, more and more people begin to prepare for a good and cheerful holiday. Someone comes up with unusual decorations for the Christmas tree, someone invents recipes for New Year's salads, and someone seriously takes up the design of the apartment. If you want to please and surprise your loved ones, then start making original crafts right now. Today we will tell you how to make a house of salt dough with your own hands and decorate it in the style of the upcoming holiday.



Salty dough

This material is familiar to many craftswomen from childhood. It makes amazing home decorations, figures of people and animals, bright gifts and colorful souvenirs. You can make such a dough very quickly, using at the same time a minimum of inexpensive ingredients. To date, there are many recipes for this product, but we propose to stay on the option described below:

  1. Take one glass of fine salt, two glasses of flour and one glass of warm water.

  2. Combine all the ingredients so that you get a firm and elastic dough.

Divide the finished material into several pieces and wrap each with cling film. Put the excess dough in the refrigerator and use as needed. Now you are ready and you can start making crafts. Carefully study the sequence of actions, and then, if you want, make your changes. You can create any look for an original home decoration using imagination and salt dough.

Houses. Master Class

Before you start work, do not forget to stock up on stacks, a plastic cup of the right size, a set of bright colors, toothpicks, a rolling pin, a grater and, of course, a wonderful New Year's mood.



Making the foundation

Roll out the dough using a rolling pin. You should get a thin and even layer 3-4 mm wide. Take a small cup of yogurt, moisten its walls with water and carefully wrap the base in the dough. Using a clerical knife, align the protruding edges and remove any excess. For the roof you can use various materials: paper, cling film or foam. Make a cone of the desired size, dangle it with layers of dough and place it on the very top of the structure.

We make a house of salt dough

Use a toothpick or stack to make the walls look like brickwork. Separately roll a few pieces of dough for the door and window. Draw details of any shape on them and make shallow cuts with a spatula to create an imitation of boards. If you wish, you can make crossbars, handles, platbands or shutters for them. After that, go to the roof. Salty dough New Year's house will look very nice if you work hard on this important detail. So, you can cut narrow strips of dough with a curly edge and lay them overlapping on the roof cone. If you prefer a more original design, then roll thin sausages from the dough and braid them over the top of the structure. Add here a few towers, a pipe or a clock. While the base of our craft is drying up, it's time to start preparing important details.



Colorful dough decorations

You can decorate a house from salt dough with the help of various methods: place on it silhouettes of a snowman, snow-covered bushes, a tree and much more. In order to get a deep and rich color for these figures, prepare a few slices of colorful dough. To do this, take the right amount of product, apply paint to it with a brush and knead it again. If you want to give volumetric texture to the details, then rub the colorful pieces on a fine grater and fix them on the walls or roof. From them you can make a red coat of Santa Claus, a prickly Christmas tree or a fluffy hare.

The final stage

After the salty dough house is completely dry, you can proceed to the final stage of our creative process. Take paints and brushes of different sizes, and then paint the finished craft. Use any colors, do not be afraid to experiment, mix and get new shades. Do not forget to varnish your product at the very end. To make your New Year's house of salt dough look more festive, you can decorate it with artificial snow, glass beads or sparkles. And if you take care of the holes in the window openings in advance, you can put a candle or a pair of LED bulbs inside .



We hope you enjoyed our version of the original Christmas crafts made from salt dough. Self-made houses can be a wonderful gift that children will present on January morning to their parents, friends or teachers. And if you install them under a Christmas tree, on a windowsill or refrigerator, they will complement the festive decor of your apartment.
