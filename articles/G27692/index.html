The foundation of St. Petersburg: historical facts

Officially, the year of foundation of St. Petersburg is 1703 (May 23). In some historical sources, the date is May 16, and this is not a mistake, but simply a date according to the old calendar. Until 1914, the city was called St. Petersburg, after which it was called Petrograd and Leningrad. Petersburg returned its historical name only on the sixth of September 1991.



The foundation of St. Petersburg was marked by the construction of a fortress in 1703 by Peter the Great, which was called St. Peter Burkh.

It was laid on the Ingermanland land, which was recaptured from the Swedes.

The author of the fortress project is Tsar Peter the Great himself.

The northern capital became known by the name of the fortress that Peter the Great gave it in honor of the Apostle Peter.

The foundation of St. Petersburg, which is associated with the construction of the Peter and Paul Fortress, was of particular importance. The first buildings of the city served as the overlap of the delta branches of two rivers - Bolshaya Nevka and Neva. Already in 1704, the fortress of Kronstadt was erected on the island of Kotlin . The foundation of St. Petersburg was of great importance. Peter the Great, establishing a city on the Neva, pursued very important strategic goals.
