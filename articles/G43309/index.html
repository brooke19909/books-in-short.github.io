Rafailovici (Montenegro): recreation, weather, hotels, prices and reviews on the resort

One of the most popular holiday destinations in Montenegro, which has long been loved by many of our compatriots, is the small resort village of Rafailovici. It is located in the Budva Riviera, considered the center of the tourist life of this Balkan state. In the article we will tell you what kind of vacation awaits you in Montenegro (Rafailovici), what hotels and entertainment are available here. We will also find out what was remembered by the rest in this place for tourists from Russia and other CIS countries.





Montenegro, Rafailovici: photos, description of the resort

This formerly modest fishing village, and now popular with holidaymakers from different countries, is located just four kilometers east of the tourist capital - Budva. Assess the location of the village will help the map. Rafailovici (Montenegro) is connected with the nearest village of Becici, and further Budva itself with walking spots. They run along the very shore of the sea, so here you can take exciting and leisurely walks, admiring the picturesque waters of the Adriatic and breathing in the healing sea air.

Despite the fact that the resort Rafailovici (Montenegro) today is quite densely built up, there are no high-rise buildings, but houses dominate with open terraces, twined with decorative flowers of various shades, standing on narrow winding streets that go down to the sea, which give the village a very cozy and, you can say home view.





How to get there

The nearest airport to the resort of Rafailovici (Montenegro) is in the city of Tivat, about 30 kilometers away. The easiest way to get to the hotel from the air harbor is by taxi. Such a trip will cost about 25 euros and take about 20 minutes. Also, upon arrival, you can take a taxi to the Tivat bus station, and from there you can get to the town of Rafailovichi by bus. However, most tourists prefer to use the first option.

Weather

Rafailovici (Montenegro) is characterized by a mild climate. So, the air temperature in July averages + 28..30 degrees Celsius. Rains in the summer, as a rule, do not go often and are short-lived. From November to February, there is quite a lot of rainfall on the Montenegrin coast. January is considered the coldest month. At night, the air temperature can reach 5 degrees of frost, and during the day rises to 11-13 degrees of heat. As for the swimming season, its duration is five months - from May to October. The water temperature at this time ranges from 20 to 25 degrees.





Cuisine and restaurants

In the town of Rafailovici (Montenegro), as in all other settlements of this country, there are a number of restaurants that offer delicious national meat and fish dishes. One of the most popular restaurants here is the Three Fishermen restaurant, located near the beach. Even real gourmets respond extremely positively about it. Many cafes and restaurants are located along the boardwalk. It should be noted that you can safely go to any of the institutions, because in Montenegro, deliciously cooked almost everywhere.





Beach, sea

Rafailovici beach (Montenegro) - Becici - has been considered one of the best in all of Europe for several decades. For the first time, Becici was awarded this honor back in 1935. Since then, the popularity of the beach has only grown, because the fine sand, the clear azure water of the Adriatic, a picturesque view of the mountains and the presence of all the necessary infrastructure do not leave anyone indifferent. In addition, it is not as crowded here as on the beaches of Budva, where at the height of the season literally "there is nowhere to fall an apple."

As throughout Montenegro, the beaches in Rafailovici are municipal. Thus, there is no need to pay for entry. You will have to give money only for renting a sunbed and an umbrella. However, you can comfortably and completely free sit on a sand that is not occupied by sun loungers, spreading a towel brought with you.

Infrastructure and hotel base

Hotels Rafailovici (Montenegro) are represented by two-, three- and four-star hotels. Here you can always find housing in accordance with your tastes and financial capabilities. The cost of living per night in Rafailovici starts from 20 euros and can reach up to several hundred euros. It is worth noting that a characteristic feature of Montenegro is the lack of hotels with their own beaches (with rare exceptions). Indeed, almost all the beaches in this country are in municipal ownership. In this regard, the vast majority of hotels are located on the second and third coastlines.





Four Star Hotels, Rafailovici

The main representatives of this category of hotels are Tanja Rafailovic, Villa Medusa, Hotel Meridian. Tanja Rafailovic is located almost on the shores of the Adriatic Sea and offers its guests excellent rooms with great views. In 2006, a large-scale reconstruction was carried out, and today guests can enjoy the stylish interior and cozy rooms of the hotel. The attendants will be happy to always come to your aid.

The four-story Villa Medusa offers its guests comfortable and cozy rooms designed to create a homely atmosphere for travelers. Near the hotel there is a beach, entertainment, restaurants and shops.

The six-story Meridian Hotel is built on a hill, which offers magnificent panoramic views of the bay and the surrounding area from its balconies and terraces. It offers luxurious rooms and impeccable service.





Three stars hotels of Rafailovici

Most of the hotels in this resort village are represented by three-star hotels. Among them can be distinguished such as "Obala", "Bonaca", "Ponta", "Sumadia", "Villa Dana", "Villa Ratko", "Villa Seca" and others.

Three-star hotel "Obala" offers its guests to stay in one of 97 comfortable rooms. Almost from all balconies of the hotel offers excellent views of the Adriatic Sea. It offers well-developed infrastructure and friendly staff.

Hotel "Sumadia" is perfect for families. It is located near the beach surrounded by beautiful nature. "Sumadia" offers you to stay in one of 120 double and triple rooms, equipped with everything necessary for a comfortable stay.

One of the best hotels in the village is Rafailovici Central Apartments. It is a modern white building with comfortable and cozy rooms, pleasant staff and all the necessary infrastructure. Guests traveling with four-legged pets are also allowed here.

Entertainment and excursions

It is recommended to combine an excellent beach holiday in Rafailovici with an examination of various historical and natural attractions. Montenegro is rich in cultural monuments. So, be sure to visit the Ostrog monastery, which stores the miraculous relics of Vasily Ostrog. Nature lovers will be interested in a trip to the canyon of the Tara River, which is the deepest and one of the most picturesque in Europe. Also here extreme sports enthusiasts can go rafting.

Boat rides are also great fun. The bus will pick you up from the village of Rafailovichi and take you to the marina in Budva or Tivat, where you will transfer to a boat and go sailing along the Budva Riviera and along the Bay of Kotor. Such a walk takes all day. If you wish, you can do it yourself, agreeing to hire a boat at any pier.

Fans of nightlife will be able to find entertainment to their taste in the numerous bars and clubs of Budva. Here you can also find a lot of shops where a variety of souvenirs, clothes and other goods are sold.





Tourists reviews

It should be recognized that tourists about the resort Rafailovici (Montenegro) leave very different reviews. Since many people who plan to spend their holidays here are very interested to find out what kind of impressions the rest of their compatriots left here, we offer you generalized comments of travelers about the village of Rafailovichi.

In general, most tourists agree that this resort village is perfect for a relaxing holiday. There are no noisy discos, as in Budva. However, if you want to get to such institutions, you can even walk or for a couple of euros by taxi. The main drawback of the village of Rafailovichi, vacationers consider crowding. However, in the midst of the tourist season, a similar situation is observed in all resort towns of both Montenegro and other countries. Nevertheless, there are much fewer people here than, again, in Budva.

Tourists liked the local beaches. Unlike Slovenian Beach, here instead of large pebbles you will find a small sand on which it is pleasant to lie and on which it is not at all painful to go into the sea.

As for restaurants and fast foods, there are no complaints about them. The vast majority of tourists remain satisfied with the prices, and the quality of food, and service.

Almost all travelers found their stay in the village of Rafailovichi very calm and pleasant and recommend this resort to their relatives and friends.
