Down scarf: knitting scheme (description)

Despite the abundance of a variety of modern models, patterns and ornaments, a downy shawl is very popular. The knitting pattern of such a product can be extremely simple or extremely complex. Designers make patterns for knitters with different skill levels. Therefore, you can find products that are connected only by facial loops or, conversely, including a number of ornaments.





In general, knitting down scarves with knitting needles, patterns and descriptions of which are designed for beginners, is not difficult. It is only important to monitor uniform density. More complex models require some experience, imagination and knowledge of geometry.

Types of down scarves: Orenburg tenderness

The need for warm clothing led to the fact that he began to actively use the fur, wool and fluff of various animals. Along with fur items of the wardrobe, knitwear containing sheep wool, fluff of rabbits, goats and other artiodactyls is perfectly warmed.

Today, two types of scarves are most popular: Orenburg and Voronezh. The first is a downy cobweb. The knitting pattern of this accessory is usually openwork, and it is made from very thin fleecy threads (hence the name "spider line"). The finished product has an extremely light weight, but has excellent warming properties.

For Orenburg shawls, the craftswomen choose mohair or angora with a thread thickness of about 250 m / 25 grams. In this case, the needles are used quite thick: at least 4 mm.

How to fit classic Voronezh shawls

Traditionally, similar accessories are given a rectangular shape. The center is knitted with some simple pattern

Craftswomen pick up any elementary pattern or even a headscarf to make the central part, and then decorate a down scarf. The knitting pattern can be quite openwork or relatively dense. Unlike Orenburg shawls, Voronezh shawls are heavier and more voluminous.

The fluff of Voronezh goats

For work use yarn from the softest goat hair. The region is proud of its achievements in breeding a certain breed of animals, as well as fluff processing technologies. The hair of such goats is light, soft, silky and the length of the pile.





Masters claim that the quality of fluff unfolds over time. The longer the product is worn, the softer and fluffier the shawl becomes. The knitting pattern does not affect the ‚Äúfluffiness‚Äù, but the esthetic attractiveness of the model depends on it.

Such scarves are not as openwork as the Orenburg ones. They are knitted more tightly. In addition, in the process of wearing, the lint of the fluff is distributed between the loops and fill the gaps, so that the canvas becomes even less transparent.

The value of quality material

You can use absolutely any yarn: if you choose a thin mohair, then the product will turn out delicate and delicate.



When the craftswoman likes a hairy thread, and she wants to get a denser product, you should purchase the appropriate material. Low-quality yarn and materials that belong to the economy segment should be avoided. This is due to the fact that there are a lot of artificial additives in inexpensive yarn, and natural fibers are not processed properly. As a result, the product will be tough, spools will appear quickly, and the overall appearance will not be accurate enough. The search for yarn is the most important preparatory stage. Depends on its results, how high-quality Orenburg or Voronezh down shawl will be. The knitting pattern and pattern in this case are of secondary importance.

The final stage: wet-heat treatment

Given the specific composition of mohair or angora yarn, it should be handled with extreme caution. You can not use detergents with aggressive components (chlorine), and do not try to iron the cloth with an iron.

In order for the knitted product to take the desired shape, and all the loops to be aligned and straightened, the scarf should be washed in warm water (not higher than 30 degrees) with the addition of a mild agent. You can use special mixtures for washing delicate items. For the first treatment, you can just soak a handkerchief in the fabric softener.

Then the product is gently squeezed (not twisted) and laid out to dry. The sharp cloves of the openwork rim should be fixed with pins. In this case, after drying, the product will keep its shape.

In the case when it is not possible to fix the shape of the scarf in any way and it is constantly wrinkled during wear, light steam processing is acceptable. In order not to spoil the mohair, the shawl should be laid out on a flat horizontal plane, pin all the protruding parts and cover with a damp, loose cloth. Chintz or gauze in several layers is suitable. Then you can already steam the product with steam from the iron or even put the iron on the surface of the scarf. It should be set for a split second, immediately removing. You can not carry the iron in any direction, otherwise the scarf is deformed.

In order not to spoil all the work, it is better to conduct experiments with a control sample.
