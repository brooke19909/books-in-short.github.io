Kolpino - satellite city of St. Petersburg

In the first decade of the 18th century, saw mills were erected on Izhora by decree of Peter the Great. Later, the construction of Izhora plants began, around which by 1722 a working settlement had arisen. The city status was assigned to the settlement in 1912. Before the settlement appeared on the earth around the dam on Izhora, the main occupations of the population were agriculture and crafts. In 1722, craftsmen and workers settled on the site of the modern city of Kolpino, the mill commander and craftsmen arrived. Thus, the foundation was laid for the development of new iron and copper industries for the region. Much later, the Izhora plant became the city-forming enterprise .
