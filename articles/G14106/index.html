Night transport in the cities of the world

Not all the capital cities of Europe can boast a similar service for their citizens, as well as tourists. Not everywhere there are round-the-clock clubs, bars, entertainment centers and even shops. But, for example, there are such buses in Warsaw.

But if you carefully monitor these routes, you can see that this often relates to intercity transportation. But Russian large industrial and metropolitan cities are in need of transport that will connect the areas of one city. It was for this purpose that night bus routes to St. Petersburg were created.

This service is comparable only with round-the-clock buses and trams in Germany and Amsterdam, but night buses stop here only at the request of the passenger. There are night buses in Oslo, Vienna, and the famous round-the-clock omnibuses in London.



There is a similar practice in Israel - they launch night buses in 90 settlements during school holidays and holidays on national and state holidays.

Night bus routes are also practiced in many US cities, but the public mode of transport there is not very popular due to the high crime rate.
