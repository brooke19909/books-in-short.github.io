Fiolent (Crimea) - a piece of paradise on Sevastopol land

Fiolent (Crimea) is a cape located between Sevastopol and Balaklava. However, these are not two separate cities, as you might think. Balaclava is a district of Sevastopol itself. Namely, its name means the central part of the city. So if you really want to get there, but there are doubts about the road and the time spent on it, you can not worry - everything is nearby.





Origin

Cape Fiolent (Crimea) has several more names, in addition to this, the most popular. It is often called Partenium (Greek), Felenk-Burun (Turkish). And sometimes even the cape of St. George. But for most tourists it is known under the name Fiolent. Crimea is rich in amazing places, but this cape is considered one of the most beautiful among them. It has a volcanic origin and even from the side resembles the remains of a once erupted volcano. As a matter of fact, it was, but a very long time ago - 150,000,000 years ago.

You should also know that the name Fiolent extends to the territory adjacent to the cape (about 10 kilometers). Despite the fact that this cape was a volcano in the past, it attracts tourists. Sheer cliffs, incredibly clear sea and bewitching views - all this attracts them.





Local beauty

Jasper beach, silence, privacy, tranquility, deliciously smelling flowering almonds, pristine nature, rocky shore ... Fiolent is a real storehouse of natural attractions. Many are interested in the question: "How to get to the beaches if this is a cape?" There is a slight nuance here. If you want to have fun and visit this "paradise", you will have to overcome some obstacles. For example, to go down to Tsarskoye Selo (the so-called one of the beaches), you have to overcome 800 "steps". More precisely, these are not steps, but landslides and cut downs. You need to be careful - follow the road and stick to the tree branches. Although in some places along the way there are even benches. The most difficult thing is not to go down, but then rise back. Everyone is familiar with this pleasant fatigue after the sea, the sun and several hours of beach holidays. So you need to be prepared for this.

Sea views

Fiolent (Crimea) is an ideal place for diving. And just to swim. This indescribable sensation is pure water, into which to plunge after a hard descent in the heat is the peak of pleasure. The snow-white bottom is visible, even if you sail to a place where the depth will be more than 10 meters. True pleasure for aesthetes

In addition to the vast expanses of the sea, bewitching the eye, here you can admire rare plants. For example, a dumb pistachio. Incidentally, a very ancient and even relic plant. It is unique - it perfectly tolerates any weather, whether it is unbearable heat, heavy rain or heavy winter (in Crimea it is rare, but still). Fragrant juniper is, one might say, a symbol of the flora of the peninsula. Some shrubs here for a thousand years! Rocky mighty oak, fragrant jasmine, figs, dogwood - here you can find anything.

The St. George Monastery, the picturesque grotto of Diana, the rocks are full of interesting and exciting places. Many people come here to have a good time. I must say, more than once after that they return.
