Smartphone ZTE Blade L5 Plus: reviews

In today's digital market, there is a huge selection of budget smartphones. Description

For a cost of 5500-6500 rubles, this smartphone looks pretty good. Judging by the reviews of the ZTE Blade L5 Plus of its owners, the performance of the case is reliable and durable. Someone notes that the phone has withstood several falls.

There are no special frills in the design. On the front panel is a standard set of elements for smartphones - camera, speaker, sensors. At the back is the main camera, external speaker and flash. Physical control buttons are installed on the right side of the device.





Available ZTE Blade L5 Plus in two colors - gray and white. Less common is golden.

Display

Reviews of the ZTE Blade L5 Plus showed that the screen in the smartphone is very mediocre. With a diagonal of 5 inches, its resolution is 854 by 480 pixels. The result is that the total pixel density is 200 dots per inch. The result, to say the least, is so-so. Viewing angles also did not please users. Even with a slight tilt, inversion, or even complete disappearance of the image, can be observed. In sunny weather, using a gadget is not possible at all - brightness is sorely lacking. The sensor can recognize only two touches. However, among the reviews on the ZTE Blade L5 Plus, it was not possible to meet those dissatisfied with this factor.

Performance

Computing power in the device provides a budget four-core MediaTek MT6580. Not the fastest processor, but quite economical due to the use of 28 nm technology. The cores in it are Cortex-A7. The maximum processor clock speed is 1300 MHz. The graphics core Mali-400 MP2 can decode hardware video in 1080p at 30 frames per second. In theory, the phone should watch videos in HD-format without ‚Äúbrakes‚Äù.

The claimed internal memory in the phone is 8 GB. Naturally, about 4 are available to the user. 1 GB is operational, which is not so bad for an ordinary state employee.





The total amount of memory can be expanded with an SD card up to 32 GB. True, in this smartphone there are problems with displaying the real amount of memory in the system.

Autonomy

The reviews for the ZTE Blade L5 Plus smartphone in this context are mixed. On the one hand, someone uses his phone only for calls and SMS. Then the batteries of 2150 mAh installed in the device calmly last for 1.5-2 days. Someone is actively using all the functions of the device - watching videos, playing games, and then the battery life "melts" after 5-8 hours.

Interfaces and Communication

The maximum that you can squeeze out of a smartphone is 3G. Supported, of course, are the old GSM standards. Paying tribute to the latest trends, you can use two SIM cards in the phone.

If there are wireless networks in the immediate vicinity, you can connect to them using the built-in Wi-Fi module with 802.11n standard. For communication between devices there is Bluetooth 4.0.

Navigation is presented by foreign GPS. Judging by the reviews on the ZTE Blade L5 Plus Black smartphone, the system works for a long time and is unstable. Even if satellites were found, it is not a fact that the phone will not lose them.





Of the sensors there are a proximity sensor, lighting and an accelerometer.

The operating system on the smartphone is Android 5.1. There are a lot of applications that come with the kit. But with the right approach, they can be ‚Äúcleaned‚Äù a little.

The kit also has a proprietary ZTE shell, which adds a bit of functionality to the interface.





The device is easily subjected to obtaining root rights and accordingly firmware, including one of the versions of CyanogenMod that can be installed on it. The recovery also changes if necessary.

Cameras

The front camera has 2 megapixels. The main one is 5. At the same time, both create pictures of mediocre quality. There is a large amount of noise and autofocus malfunctions. In good light, you can still somehow achieve decent shots. But in the dark or twilight you have to ‚Äúconjure‚Äù a little with the settings. Analogs

Among the closest competitors we can single out the LG K8 K350. With a relatively equal price, this device has the best resolution of 1280 by 720 with the same 5 inches. The version of Android is 6.0 here, which is already an order of magnitude higher. Support for LTE will also give odds to the hero of the article.

Samsung Galaxy J3 2016 costs about 1500-2000 more. However, the kit has a more efficient processor with a frequency of 1500 MHz and a screen with a resolution of 1280 by 720. The internal memory is the same, but the operating memory is 1.5 times larger. The ability to use 4G is also a plus of this smartphone. The version of Android is the same, but it can be updated to a newer one.





A less productive analogue, which is also cheaper - Lenovo Vibe C A2020. Has a screen of the same size and resolution. The same memory indicators, both built-in and operational.
