Positional chess: the northern gambit as the beginning

The board on which the battles unfold is divided into sixty-four equal cells of two colors, usually black and white. There are sixteen figures in each army. There are infantry soldiers, cavalry, fighting elephants, guns and, of course, the king and queen. The names of the figures may be different - all this can be found in any sources devoted to the game. Now about the option of the so-called debut - the northern gambit.

What is a debut? This is the beginning of the party. Usually, the concept of ‚Äúdebut‚Äù in chess covers not only the first moves of opponents, but also the withdrawal of the main pieces to convenient positions - deployment of troops, so to speak.

The northern gambit was first used by the Danish chess player Frome in 1867. True, not very successful - the master lost three out of four games, using this particular type of debut. Well, on the other hand, he entered the history of chess not only as a strong chess player, but also as a developer of a new type of debut.

Until the end of the XIX century, the northern gambit was very popular and was often used not only by masters, but also by amateurs. However, at the beginning of the XX century, the debut was analyzed in detail, and defense systems were built against it, in fact, which nullified all the merits of the gambit.

Now this beginning is not popular and is used very rarely, mainly at the amateur level.
