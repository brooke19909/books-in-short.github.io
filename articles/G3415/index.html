A bit of history

The first video recording from a car was made as an experiment in 1926 in New York. Then the exit of the fire brigade on a call was completely filmed on a movie camera.

In the 70s, the first prototypes of car recording devices appeared - the predecessors of modern DVRs. As it often happens, the military industry gave the first impetus to the development of fixation video devices. These devices were used on military equipment and were intended mainly for tracking.

After some time, the registrar migrated to civilian vehicles. The first devices were bulky and extremely expensive, so they did not get much distribution among motorists.

But progress did not stand still, the gadget was improving, and by the 90s it began to be used everywhere by law enforcement agencies of various countries.

At the beginning of the 21st century, the device for video recording of traffic conditions "went to the masses." Then manufacturers began to supply the device with additional options: LCD display, GPS module, accelerometer, motion sensor. Along the way, the quality of shooting video material also improved.

Legal side

When using the registrar, you should not ignore the legal part of the question. In Russia, a video shot using a car recorder may well serve as evidence of the guilt or innocence of a particular person in court. The same situation has developed in Belarus and Ukraine.

But in most countries of the European Union it is forbidden to use the registrar to a private person. Shooting other cars or people is considered a violation of their civil rights and freedoms.

In the United States, the right to film what is happening on the road is granted only to police and special-purpose drivers.

The main parameters and features that characterize any DVR

All registrars have a number of important characteristics that determine its functionality, utility and final cost:

  1. Resolution of the recorded video. The most optimal resolution is Full HD, in extreme cases - HD. The higher it is, the more small details the image contains. For example, with a low resolution, it is impossible to consider the registration number of the vehicle in front. Such a registrar is only suitable for confirming the very fact of a violation that entailed an accident.
  2. Frame frequency. Currently, registrars support shooting at a frequency of 5 to 60 frames per second. Naturally, the more frames fit in one second of video, the better. Especially if you need to decompose some fragment of the record into separate pictures.
  3. The sensitivity of the matrix of the camera of the DVR. The higher this indicator, the better the video quality when shooting in low light conditions.
  4. Recorder lens viewing angle. The larger the value of this parameter, the wider the camera‚Äôs coverage of the space in front of the car, that is, both the curb and the oncoming lane fall into the frame, and not just the narrow zone directly in front of the car's hood.
  5. Compression ratio. The raw data in their raw form occupy a large amount of space on the memory card, therefore, to reduce the size of the video files, they are compressed using a special device - an analog-to-digital converter. It is important for the manufacturer to find a middle ground: compress the file, but at the same time try to maintain the quality of the video image.
  6. Digital image stabilization. While the car is moving, the recorder is subject to jolting and vibration. To reduce the effect of these phenomena on the recording quality, an electronic stabilization system is used.
  7. Battery. Sometimes it is necessary for the recorder to work offline with the power off of the car network. For such needs, a battery is used in the recorders.
  8. Motion Sensor. This option is necessary when you want to enable image recording only when a moving object appears within the viewing angle of the lens. This function is useful when parking the car in order to record the fact of damage by another moving vehicle.
  9. Cyclical video recording and loss. This function allows you to record video continuously, replacing the oldest files with new ones. It is usually divided into equal parts of time. At the beginning of recording a new file, cheap registrars experience a delay and it takes a few seconds to switch, and it is at this point that an accident can occur. In good devices, this phenomenon is not observed.
  10. Accelerometer With sharp braking or an unexpected change in the position of the car in space, the accelerometer puts the device in emergency mode. In this mode, the video is recorded in a special format that is protected from deletion.
  11. Radar detector. This option helps in finding specialized traffic police devices designed to calculate the speed of a moving car.
  12. GPS module. Allows you to display the speed of the car on the video, and also registers the route of the car in a separate file on the memory card.
  13. Infrared illumination. Allows you to improve the quality of shooting in the dark.
