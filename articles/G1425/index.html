Montenegro is one of the most beautiful Balkan countries located on the Adriatic Sea, surrounded by beautiful cliffs and green mountains. Once Montenegro was part of Socialist Yugoslavia, and all resorts here were organized in accordance with socialist standards. However, everything changed when the country became independent (2006), and also expressed a desire to join the European Union. Over the next few years, it has transformed: on magnificent beaches, as if from sand, comfortable Montenegro 5 * hotels - 3 stars, giant hotel complexes, villas, etc. have grown. Among them you can find hotels belonging to international hotel brands, and this, of course , speaks in favor of the resorts of Montenegro and increases its prestige as a tourist country.



Tourism and hotel business development

Since 2006, the tourism sector as one of the most promising sectors of the country's economy has come under special state control and has begun to develop rapidly. Almost all of the already existing best hotels in Montenegro were reconstructed, and those that spoiled the overall panorama with their appearance were demolished. In order to get closer to European standards, it was decided to build modern conceptual hotels using the latest technology. Soon, new fashionable Montenegro all-inclusive hotels appeared on the first beach lines. Among them you can see both budget three-star, and four- and five-star luxury hotels and complexes. Each of them has its own pool (s), spa, playgrounds for various sports games, restaurants and cafes, a well-equipped beach, etc. Of course, even the best hotels in Montenegro are slightly inferior in service to similar hotels in Turkey or Spain but they were already able to achieve a high level of service for the Croatian and Bulgarian coast. Thus, if Montenegro develops at such a pace, it may soon become one of the most competitive resort countries in Europe.

Montenegro, all-inclusive hotels: prices and services

How much does a room in a hotel of the highest category in Montenegrin resorts cost? This is an expensive pleasure, but it deserves it. In a five-star hotel, daily accommodation costs 80 - 100 euros, in four-star hotels - 20 - 50 euros, in three-star hotels - about 30, but in a two-star hotel you can rent a room for 13-15 euros. By the way, in these Montenegro hotels you can also find quite acceptable service, although they do not work according to the "all inclusive" scheme. Unlike other European hotels serving tourists according to this system, Montenegro hotels "all inclusive" offer tourists non-round-the-clock meals, two meals a day (breakfast - dinner), however, all day long, drinks (alcoholic and non-alcoholic) of local origin and various snacks. In other hotels, the type of food "half board" is most often found.
