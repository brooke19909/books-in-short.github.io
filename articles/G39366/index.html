Botanical Garden of Kiev: them. Fomin, in Pechersk, them. Grishko

The hallmark of the capital of Ukraine are the famous Kiev chestnuts, as well as the magnificent Botanical Gardens. Where, if not in this place, can such a grandiose riot of colors of blossoming flowers, the splendor of powerful centuries-old trees and a grandiose variety of shrubs come together? The most famous of the gardens are the arboretum them. Grishko and them. Fomina.

Botanical Garden M.M. Grishko

Kiev Botanical Garden in Pechersk is directly related to the history of the appearance and development of the Academy of Sciences. The initiator of the creation of the Botanical Garden in the city was V.I. Lipsky, a well-known scientist, florist and first president of the Academy. He was eager to build a garden in the territory belonging to the Goloseevsky forest. This idea never came true, as a famous scientist changed his place of residence. But the name of such a versatile personality has firmly entered the history of the amazing garden.

In 1935, a land plot of 117 hectares was allocated in the Zverinets Garden. The consultant for the arrangement of the botanical masterpiece was Lipsky. After the scientist passed away, his work was continued by V.E. Schmidt. He was also the director of the garden. The Botanical Garden in Pechersk (Kiev) was created slowly. Firstly, it was not sufficiently financed, and secondly, its rather large territory belonged to private owners, who left their usual place of residence with great reluctance.

Despite all the barriers, before the outbreak of World War II, the garden already boasted 1,050 species of plants and a thousand taxa of greenhouse crops. All this happened thanks to the new director Y. K. Gotsik and M. M. Grishko, who at that time headed the Academy of Sciences.

Grishko in the history of the botanical garden





The Friendship of Peoples Botanical Garden (Kiev) was severely defeated during the military confrontations. Most of its plants became unusable, and some were irretrievably lost.

With the arrival of spring 1944, active reconstruction of the reserve began, and in July it came under the direct control of M. M. Grishko. Botanical splendor was given the status of an independent unit belonging to the Academy of Sciences.

Expeditions began abroad to collect a variety of plant collections. Grishko wanted to create a garden that would become both a place for city dwellers to rest and a center of science in Ukraine. For guests, the Botanical Garden opened on May 29, 1964. Today this institution is a carefully protected area, which is part of the nature reserve fund of Ukraine.

Fomin Botanical Garden

One of the oldest Botanical Gardens in Ukraine is the Fomin Botanical Garden of Kiev. The official date of its creation is May 22, 1839. It was during this period that the first tree plantings were made. Today, this garden is considered a division of Kiev University. Shevchenko. Here it occupies 22 hectares of area, which houses collections of more than ten thousand plant species, a botanical museum, a huge greenhouse complex, and also a scientific library.

The garden is known for its exotic exhibits. Here is a large collection of all types of cacti and succulents. The grandiose collection of palm trees is also striking. In the former USSR, they were the largest and oldest.

How the garden of Fomin was created

Ancient Kiev legends say that a long time ago the territory of the modern garden belonged to the sister of Kia, Schek and Horeb - Lybedi. One of the rivers that flow into the mighty Dnieper is called Lybed. And on its left bank, among the ravines and hills, a garden was created.





Beretti, an architect who designed the University of St. Vladimir (now T. G. Shevchenko), proposed to set up a garden in a deserted place near an educational institution. In 1839, permission was granted to build a temporary garden. Only in 1941 he was honored to become permanent.

Botanical Garden (Kiev) Fomin came under the leadership of Alexander Vasilievich in 1914. Fomin ruled it until 1935. During the winter of 1919-1920, when severe frost broke out, a considerable number of plants were saved from freezing by the director and his staff.

Fomin Garden today

The Botanical Garden of Kiev now plays the role of the research department of Shevchenko State University. Here students undergo botany practice. Pupils and young naturalists can take part in special cognitive and scientific excursions that reveal the secrets of nature to them, talk about its significance in the life of each of us.

In 2007, comprehensive restoration work was carried out here: the slopes were strengthened, new species of bushes and trees were planted, greenhouses were modernized.
