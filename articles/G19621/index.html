The best gifts - DIY Christmas souvenirs

For the New Year, undoubtedly, gifts should be thematic, bright and colorful, whoever presented them. That is why it is best to make them yourself, because only in this way you can express not only the festive mood, but also your attitude to the person to whom the gift is intended. All new year souvenirs with their own hands are of particular value, because a person puts part of his soul and warmth into them.

However, preparing such a gift at a more adult age, it is necessary to complicate the task and make the snowflake more voluminous, dense and creative. The best manufacturing technique is to combine curled paper slices. Such elements are collected in any patterns, which as a result form beautiful, voluminous and unusually delicate snowflakes. It is also important to consider the size of the snowflake, because too large will not be combined with every interior.

For the New Year, we present gifts to all our friends and relatives, so our presentations can be either universal or divided into various categories, for example, depending on gender. An ideal gift for a woman for a winter celebration is a figurine or some other trifle. In skilled hands, such things become a worthy decoration of any interior. If we are talking about a gift for a man on New Year's Eve, then it is best to give preference to non-standard culinary presentations. It can be cookies with bows or wishes, or some other unusual dish.



Do-it-yourself main Christmas souvenirs - These are holiday winter cards. If the card is made at home, then it is possible to stick pieces of cotton wool on it, to assemble an original drawing or ornament from them.
A card with embroidery on a cardboard basis will be very memorable. Similar sets are sold in all needlework stores in the country, and it is also worth noting that embroidery can be replaced with crocheted elements. They stick to the base of the card, and the present looks very gentle and elegant.

You can make New Year's souvenirs using empty bottles of deodorants. They are sheathed with fabric and foam rubber or other filler is seasoned under it. As a result, such figures can be turned into any fabulous creature, animal or just in Santa Claus with the Snow Maiden. They will logically fit both under the Christmas tree and on the holiday table. However, unfortunately, such figures cannot be placed on spruce branches due to too much weight.



Homemade balls of threads are the most creative Christmas souvenirs with their own hands, which are very common among needlework lovers this season.
