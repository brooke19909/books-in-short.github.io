Tools and materials

To work on sewing a cover, you will need to acquire materials such as:

  • The material from which you sew the top of the case.
  • Filler - you can use sintepon, sherstepon, foam rubber, etc.
  • Fabric for the manufacture of the inside.
  • Sewing threads matching the tone of the fabric.
  • Pins for chipping the edges of patterns.
  • Scissors, needles.
  • Slanting inlay for edging.
  • Ribbon, colored patches, beads, lace, etc. - for decorating the cover.

Phased work

Before starting work on sewing covers on a typewriter, it will be necessary to create a pattern. A lot of options have been invented for this, but there are several popular ones. The very first step is to take measurements from our equipment - side width and height. The same parameters cannot be due to the fact that all models are different. Therefore, we write down our own and build an individual drawing on them.

In the second version, the manufacture provides only three parts - the back + front and two semicircular details of the sidewalls. We also focus only on our sizes.

The progress will be the following sequence of actions:

  1. We draw the pattern of the cover for the sewing machine with our own hands on paper, applying our measurements, cut it out.
  2. We transfer the pattern to the fabric and cut out from each type of material: the main, laying and lining fabric, not forgetting the allowances for each seam.
  3. Then, using pins, or stitching with large stitches, we connect all the parts. And we do this individually with each layer, leaving a hole so that it is possible to turn the product.
  4. In conclusion, unscrew the cover, sew the hole, and decorate the surface to your taste.

DIY cover for a sewing machine from fabric do-it-yourself

So, at the very beginning of the process of creating drawings, as we noted above, measure all sides of the machine. Making a cover for a sewing machine with your own hands is an absolutely simple task!

We need the height, the width of the bottom and top (here the flywheel is taken for a landmark).

The formula for the length of the main part is as follows: (height * 2) + width of the top. For a loose fit, also add about 1 cm on both sides. Consider every protruding part!

Firstly, we build a rectangular figure with sides equal to the width and height of the machine. Divide the width in half and draw a dotted line across the entire rectangle. On it, we postpone the distance equal to the radius of the flywheel plus the part protruding above it. Using a compass, draw a circle through the highest point. From the bottom, draw lines to the wide part of the circle.

Adjust the length of the main canvas by measuring the side contour, not including the bottom width, and the width is the length of the machine plus 1 cm for a loose fit.

That's all - we built a cover pattern for a sewing machine with our own hands, it remains to transfer it to the fabric and cut it out.

We sew a cover for the sewing machine

We transfer the patterns to the fabric (each type) and cut them out. We sew at the seams, iron them with an iron. Then we fold the inside out to each other and sew, leaving a small gap. We turn it on.

On the bottom edge of the cover you can go with a decorative stitch, fixing all the layers together and giving additional rigidity.

In the upper part, cut a hole for the handle and carefully trim it with an oblique trim.

Iron each seam with an iron - this will give a neat appearance to your product.

You can sew pockets for trifles to the finished case - then everything will be stored in one place.

The beauty of the fabric is ready! Finish to your liking!

Sew a cover for a sewing machine from old jeans

Any of us must have very old jeans hidden on some of the farthest shelves of the closet. Wearing them is indecent, and throwing away is a pity! And why not sew a case for a sewing machine from old jeans with your own hands? Denim is one of the most reliable and durable fabrics, it will last a similar product for a very long time.

So boldly open each seam, cut out details from them according to already constructed patterns. If possible, then position the patterns so that the pockets fall on the sides of the cover. Thus, you get additional storage for scissors, pins, threads, etc.

At the stage of connecting parts, do not be afraid to sew and cut off once again, fitting for the best look. Turn the lower edge inwards and sew.

And voila - we've got a wonderful new thing for a typewriter!

For work it is necessary:
