Description

The city where the museum-diorama "Battle of Kursk. Belgorod direction ‚Äù- Belgorod - is located 64 km from the site of the famous battle that took place near the village of Prokhorovka. The attention of all who come there is usually attracted by a building made in the form of an arc. If you look from afar, it resembles a large white sail, which flutters in the wind. On the pedestals in front of the main entrance to the museum-diorama, a Soviet WWII-IS-3 tank was installed on pedestals, as well as an ISU-152 installation, an anti-tank gun and mortars.

The building houses a huge ribbon-shaped, curved in a semicircle, with carefully written battle scenes and a realistic foreground. On this layout are structures, as well as models of objects, mannequins, figures of various sizes, depicting people, equipment and weapons. Thus, the visitor appears to be at the epicenter of the fighting.

To the right, on the horizon, is the Prokhorovka railway station, and to the left is the small village of Beregovoye. In the very center of the exposition the Oktyabrsky state farm is visible. Further south you can see the railway Belgorod - Prokhorovka. The diorama of the Battle of Kursk is the largest of its kind in modern Europe. Tens of thousands of tourists visit it annually. The total area is 1477 square meters. In addition, it includes a 2-hectare park area.
