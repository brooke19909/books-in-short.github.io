Smart watches: a bit of theory

What are smart watches designed for? At the dawn of its appearance, this device was conceived as a kind of "appendage" to the smartphone. The watch was connected to the phone via the Bluetooth protocol. The more expensive models had a touch screen and even a camera, while the cheaper ones represented a regular watch with additional functions for working with a smartphone. What functions were originally performed by a classic smart watch:

  1. Event Notification. The clock informs you of an incoming call or SMS.
  2. Answer the call. With an incoming call, it is not necessary to get the smartphone out of your bag or pocket, just answer the call by pressing the touch screen of the watch and talk.
  3. View SMS. Ability to view incoming messages on the watch display.
  4. View the contacts in the phone book and the call log of your smartphone.
  5. The ability to control the clock playing the music player of the smartphone using a special application.
  6. The ability to take pictures using the camera built into the watch.
  7. The presence of built-in "sports" applications: pedometer, calorie counter, heart rate monitor.

The functionality of modern smart watches has expanded significantly. Now the gadget can work separately from the smartphone and allows you to autonomously make calls and answer SMS. Many devices even have a built-in GPS module.

Children‚Äôs wristwatch Smart Baby Watch Q80 (analogue is the GW100 watch from Wonlex) has both a communication module and GPS. We will study them more closely and analyze their capabilities in more detail.

Watch Specifications

  1. Sizes and weight. Width - 31 mm, length - 48 mm, thickness - 11.8 mm, weight - 39 g. The watch is designed for a child's age from 3 to 12 years. Of course, on the hand of a three-year-old baby, the gadget will look bulky, but, again, thanks to the wide range of adjustment of the strap and its thoughtful fastening to the watch, the device will fit tightly on the wrist.
  2. Mobile connection. The built-in GSM radio module supports networks from 850 to 1900 MHz. It uses a reduced size SIM card (micro-SIM).
  3. Wireless Modules The watch has a GPS module on board to determine the coordinates. It is noteworthy that there is also a Wi-Fi module, which by connecting to wireless networks significantly improves positioning accuracy.
  4. Display. The device has a very decent TFT-screen with a diagonal of 1.22 inches. In addition, unlike previous models Q50 and Q60, it is touch-sensitive, which greatly facilitates the work with the device.
  5. CPU. There is nothing particularly remarkable. The gadget has an onboard processor MTK2503D with a frequency of 260 MHz. For normal operation of the clock, the processor power is enough.
  6. Protection against dust and moisture. The gadget has entry-level splash protection, that is, with the clock you can completely wash your hands and take a walk in the rain, but taking a shower with them or swimming in the pool is not recommended.
  7. Additional functions. The watch boasts a built-in accelerometer.
  8. Watch support by operating systems. The watch can be used in conjunction with smartphones with Android version 4 and higher or with iOS version 7 and higher.
