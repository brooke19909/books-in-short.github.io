In the light of the recent World Economic Forum, many Russians have again raised the question of where is Davos? Why did the heads of powers, financial movers and leading politicians around the world choose this small town for their meetings? What are they discussing there? And what is more interesting than Davos, besides politics and the global economy? What prevents us from even visiting Davos virtually and getting to know its sights? Where is Davos

The resort is located in Switzerland, in the east of the country. Administratively, it belongs to the canton of Graub√ºnden. World maps designate Davos in different ways. In the local dialect, it is called Tafaas. In Russian, we copy the French pronunciation: Davos. And in the Italian-speaking cantons of Switzerland, the city is called Tavate or (less commonly) Tavau. Davos is located in the intermountain valley, at an altitude of 1560 meters above sea level. The river Landwasser flows through the town. The resort is part of the famous and prestigious group The Best of the Alps. The permanent population of Davos is twelve thousand people. This figure gives him the right to claim the title of the highest city in Europe. The valley is conditionally divided into two parts. The east is called Davos-Dorf, and the west is Davos-Platz. To get from one part to another, you must use the bus. Next to Davos is Klosters - another resort that, together with Courmayeur and Chamonix, is included in the nomination The Best of the Alps.

World Economic Forum (WEF)

For the majority of the public, the question of where Davos is is interesting for one reason: what do financial movers in a small alpine village decide? And why almost every year this resort is stormed by thousands of anti-globalists? The Davos Forum has been convening since 1971 annually, in late January - early February. At this "summit" invited statesmen, political leaders, leading businessmen. The name of the Forum itself involves a discussion of macroeconomic problems. But in fact, the topics that are addressed at the meetings relate to the situation in the world's hot spots , the resolution of political crises, health and environmental issues. The forum is one of the main events of the year, despite the fact that this symposium does not produce any official resolutions. How to get to the resort

Only two express trains go directly to the mountain valley where Davos is located: Bernina and Iced (runs in winter). All other railway routes will be with transfers. If you flew to Zurich airport, you need to get to the city of Kura by train, then to the Landquart station, and then a regional train to Davos. You can use the direct bus to Kura. The approximate price for a second-class train ticket is fifty Swiss francs. The resort has two stations: on the Platz and in Dorf. Between them lies the 4-kilometer Promenade Street, where most hotels and shops are located. Drivers need to take into account that driving along this main resort highway is one-way. In the opposite direction, you can drive along Talstrasse, a quieter street.

Summer resort

The peak season is in winter. But when the snow melts, and with it the prices, a slightly different audience arrives in Davos (Switzerland). Tourists who love mountain biking and hiking will find many well-equipped trails around the resort. In Davos, there is a summer vacation promotion program - "Davos Inclusive". If you stay in any resort hotel for at least one night, you will be given a Guest Card (Guest Card). With it, you have the right to travel freely on all ski lifts and trains, not only in Davos, but also in neighboring Kloster, and you can also join guided tours in the mountains organized by a local travel agency.
