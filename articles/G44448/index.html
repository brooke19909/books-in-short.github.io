Compact active subwoofer in the car: overview, selection, installation

The era of cassette recorders and "tin" sound has long sunk into oblivion. Motorists quickly figured out all the advantages of digital sound and, having tried it once, could no longer return to the usual analogue technique. One of the most important ingredients in high-quality sound is the subwoofer, which complements the standard speakers with full low frequencies.

Connoisseurs of powerful bass equip their cars with impressive and massive speakers. But a good half of consumers do not need such equipment at all and compact models are enough for listening to their favorite tracks. The critical difference here lies only in the maximum volume threshold. And if you are not going to beat the streets passing along the road to work, then the compact active subwoofer in the car will be more than enough.

Today's market can offer a lot of options for such equipment, but not every system can boast of its high-quality component, so the choice must be approached with due care. To completely trust the consultants in the store is not a good idea, especially when it comes to the notorious networkers like Eldorado, Mvideo and others like them. So at least a minimal theoretical base here obviously will not be superfluous.

So, we present to your attention a small overview of compact active subwoofers. Consider the main selection criteria, the advantages and disadvantages of systems, as well as the feasibility of acquiring a particular model.

Difficulty choosing

The choice of a compact active subwoofer in a car is determined mainly by your musical preferences, and not just the speaker quality or amplifier power. For example, models with a bass reflex differ in a sharper attack (open rear volume) and are great for synthetic tracks of the Tynt-Tynts plan.

Devices with a soft attack, that is, with a closed rear volume, perfectly digest the blues, country, etc. directions. The cases in the Band-pass form factor have maximum efficiency and are considered universal, but it will be useful to ‚Äúlisten‚Äù to them before purchase.

Installation

As for the installation of speakers in the car, it is compact active subwoofers that boast a trouble-free installation. The fact is that active models already come with an amplifier, and all you need to do is find a place for the system (usually under the driver's seat) and throw wires to the radio. While for the installation of passive models it is necessary to take care of the additional area.

In addition, compact active subwoofers are noticeably cheaper than their passive counterparts. Of course, the sound with a separate amplifier turns out to be noticeably louder and somewhere even better, but this will result in serious financial expenses, which is unacceptable for a good half of ordinary motorists.

Next, we consider the specific and most popular models of compact active subwoofers, distinguished by a large number of positive reviews from car enthusiasts.
