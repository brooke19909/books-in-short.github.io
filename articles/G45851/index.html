Postcard History

Initially, this was called a postal card in everyday life, in which you could write a message and send it by mail without an envelope.

Then, on one side of the postcard, a picture, illustration or author's design drawing began to be printed. Cards themselves began to be executed on cardboard. Most often these were beautiful greeting cards: on March 8, February 23, New Year's or birthday.

Then cards began to be issued in the form of a book cover, expanding. Now they could no longer be mailed without an envelope. But the name "postcard" was fixed. Now this word meant "congratulations in writing, having a design design."

Drawn cards

Still, the most common option remains traditional, when a piece of cardboard is beautifully framed. Most often, the ideas of postcards on March 8, made with their own hands, are based on such a manufacturing method as drawing.





What is best depicted as a congratulation on the women's holiday? Of course the flowers! For example, a rose will look quite elegant on a postcard. Just do not forget to make the corresponding inscription in the picture.

Master class: draw roses

A card will be very beautiful on March 8 for mom, if you try to draw a flower that is extremely similar to the real one.





  1. First, auxiliary lines are made on cardboard with a simple pencil. This will be a circle with a diameter coinciding with the volume of the lower part of the flower.
  2. A little above the circle draw a horizontally elongated oval, which will be the top of the rose.
  3. From a circle down draw an arbitrary line of the stem.
  4. Next to the first wavy line, make a second one so that the stem has volume.
  5. The lateral points of the oval are connected by concave segments with a circle, forming the silhouette of a flower.
  6. Leaves are marked on the stem.
  7. From the bottom of the circle, the extreme open petals of the flower are marked.
  8. Inside the oval, the petals of the bud should be drawn.
  9. Streaks are drawn on the leaves.
  10. Spikes are placed on the stem.
  11. Below the bud are sepals.
  12. The flower should be painted by applying shadows and making highlights from the light.

Postcard with applique

Anyone for whom drawing is extremely difficult may well use scissors and glue. In order for the card on March 8th to turn out to be beautiful and exclusive for the mother, the master will need old magazines, books, even children's coloring will be useful. You can use unnecessary postcards.

Cutting out the details - flowers, animals, the sun - make up the composition of the future postcard. Then parts of the application are glued. Congratulatory inscription can be made with paints or also cut out of colored paper.





You can use a minimum of detail, for example, only a few silhouettes of flowers and lettering. The eight itself is simply cut out, then the number is slightly bent inward, and glued onto a bright sheet, after having smeared with glue only the top, bottom and middle of the figure. The background will shine through the slot, and the card itself will already be voluminous, and not traditionally flat. By the way, for this it is possible to stick the flowers not completely, but only in the middle, bending inward the lateral, non-glued parts.

Personalized scrapbook postcard with a window and a photograph

People keen on needlework get real pleasure from the process itself. For example, they can make the card on March 8 exclusive, having figured out how to put a photo in it.

This is easy to do if you cut a window on one of the halves. It should be placed in it a photograph of the person to whom the congratulation was prepared, or a photograph where a significant day or significant place is captured.





If you do not insert a whole image, but cut out the silhouette of a person, then from the inside you need to glue a piece of colored paper - this will be the background. The junction of the edges of the background must be masked using the applique. A bouquet of flowers, a smiling sun, a dwarf with a gift in his hands will perfectly fit into the design of the inside of the card.

The window can be cut both round and rectangular. If the card itself is made in the form of a house, then the hole does not have to be cut completely.
