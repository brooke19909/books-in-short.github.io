Varangians: the definition of peoples in Russian history

The concept of "Vikings" in Russia is significantly different from the definitions of Western scholars, who often identify them with the Vikings. Russian historians proceeded from the information contained in the annals of Nestor, although it was written 200 years after the events described.

Varangians in "Tales of Bygone Years"

Varangians in the service of Russian princes

It just so happened that in Russian history almost all the information about the calling of the Varangians to Russia is connected with the "Tale of Bygone Years". But some facts cited in the annals contradict various historical information. For example, three brothers came to rule Russia: Rurik, Truvor and Sineus. Were they Scandinavian Vikings? Probably not.

According to some sources, the Scandinavian Vikings were in the service of the Russian princes much earlier than the time described by Nestor. That is, they were mercenaries. Could Russian princes call their mercenaries?

From this we can conclude that the Varangians meant completely different, presumably Slavic tribes of the Varangians living on the shores of the Varyazhsky Sea. It is known that the Scandinavian Varangians raided the northern lands. In 859, the Slavs built the fortress New Town (Novgorod), which protected the Slavs from Varangian raids. Could they be invited to build the Russian state? Not. After all, it was Rurik who began to rule Novgorod. This is a version of Professor Rybakov.

For all the controversy, who are the Vikings, it must be recognized that they existed and were really attracted to the Russian service. There is confirmed evidence that, starting with Prince Vladimir, the baptist of Russia, the Vikings were often used by princes in the internecine struggle. He seized with their help the throne in Kiev. Yaroslav the Wise to fight the Svyatopolk also appealed to the help of the Scandinavian Vikings.

Varangians in Byzantium

In the Greek chronicles of the 9th century there are references to the Fargans, who served as the guard of the emperor. Since the 11th century, Varangians (Varangians) appear in Byzantine chronicles, who, together with the Rus, Saracens and Franks, are in the service of the emperors. The Varangians were not close to the emperors, they served for food and clothing. They were treated as a people, but did not indicate where they came from.

In the chronicles of the Skilica, they were identified with the Celts. The Varangians participated in all the campaigns of the Byzantines, but already in the XII century, of all the foreigners who were in military service, only they were entrusted to be the personal guard of the emperors.

The Varangians and Slavs in the 9th-10th centuries were in close contact, the Russian princes have the blood of the Varangians. For Novgorodians and residents of Kiev, all who lived on the shores of the Varangian Sea were called Vikings.
