How to attach a card to Paypal when registering in a payment system?

The American payment system Paypal without fail requires information about the account of the payment card user of the system for conducting settlement operations. This information allows you to transfer funds within the system by withdrawing or replenishing (if such an operation is possible for the user's country) the dollar balance of a bank card. Before tying a card to Paypal, it must be remembered that the company cooperates with Russian banks only on the basis of depositing funds into the system for the implementation of various settlement transactions (purchases on eBay, for example). Otherwise, if it is necessary to withdraw funds from Paypal, the cards servicing this operation must belong to a payment account of a bank registered in the USA.



The card is linked to Paypal in stages (if there is a user account in the system). To do this, by entering the menu of your account, you must use the "Profile" option and select the "Add a card" operation in it. After that, select from the list that appears the type of card the user is adding (MasterCard, Visa, American Express or Discover). Then enter the card number in a special cell consisting of 16 digits. In addition, the card expiration date and security code, which is located on the signature line and consists of three digits, should be indicated a little lower. Then you need to specify the address of the account, which is useful for getting rid of unnecessary manipulations in the system. Nothing complicated user expects! Next, you need to go to the bank servicing this card account (or use the online banking service) to find out the statement of the transaction opposite the transaction of the amount of $ 1.95, where the information should be in the form of "1234paypal". The first four digits indicated on the transaction should be entered in the account of the payment system. Thus, the card is verified. And for the user of the system will become available the ability to conduct payment transactions.
