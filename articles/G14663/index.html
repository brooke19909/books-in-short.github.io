Pages of the Past





The history of the appearance of the railway station in Odessa is closely connected with the decision to build in 1863 a railway connecting the port of the Black Sea with central Ukraine.

Emperor Alexander II gave his permission to build a station complex in Odessa. In the summer of 1880, the Society of Southwestern Railways laid the foundation for the building of the railway station.

It was officially opened in 1884. The construction project was created by St. Petersburg architect V. A. Schr√∂ter, and the work was supervised by the famous architect Alexander Bernardazzi.

The building is made in the neoclassical style, with elements of the Renaissance Baroque. During the Great Patriotic War, during the occupation of Odessa in 1944, the station building was destroyed. Later, the project of A. M. Chuprin on the restoration of the structure was adopted. And already in 1952 the building was rebuilt. The last reconstruction was made in 2006.

The main directions of trains





For many, the journey begins or continues from the amazing city of Odessa. The railway station, which is nice to note, works in several directions at once, both local and international.

From the station of the Odessa-Main railway station, trains depart on suburban routes of all directions of Ukraine and abroad: to Russia, Belarus, Moldova.

Apartment Bureau of Odessa Train Station





The apartment bureau of the Odessa railway station allows visitors to rent housing in the city by the day or for a longer time. It is profitable, convenient and safe.

The apartment bureau of the Odessa railway station provides hotels, houses, sanatoriums, as well as apartments for rent for a day or for a long time.

Usually, as practice shows, living in Odessa hotels is several times more expensive than renting a house or apartment in this city.
