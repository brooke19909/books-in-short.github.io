Moomin Park in Finland: opening hours, how to get, reviews

Moomin trolls are known not only in Finland, but also beyond its borders. They are a symbol of this country, and there is no such person who would not hear about these lovely creatures. And in Suomi itself they are so loved that they even created a park of the same name. Now it is one of the main attractions. Moomin Park is a country in which every visitor can plunge into the world of childhood.

The world created by Tove Jansson

These cute creatures, similar to hippos, were invented by the Finnish writer Tove Jansson. Their story began in 1945, when she painted a funny hippo for her younger brother. Then he will become the most famous character in Finnish literature.

And the Pellinki island inspired the writer to create the Moomin World, where the Jansson family rested for several months a year. This place is very cozy, a house surrounded by forest, the sea - all this inspired Tove Jansson. Then the fairy tale grew into a series of books about good, cozy creatures that filmed.

sights

In the Moomin Park in Finland, visitors can find a map guide to the fairy world. In addition to children's entertainment, the island has other attractions:

  1. The Emma Theater is a children's theater in which various performances are shown. They are already included in the ticket price. There are alternating Moomin shows in Finnish and Swedish. Those who do not know the language should not worry: there are subtitles in English and Russian. They also show a piece of music in which language is not important. Emma's Theater is located right at the entrance to the park.
  2. Police station - there is the Moomin police chief who is guarding the bully Haysuli (Stinki). It is periodically brought about by the inhabitants of the island there.
  3. Hemulen's House - there visitors can see a collection of Finnish plants and butterflies. Located near the pier. The house has been restored, and inside you can find various gardening tools that may interest little visitors.
  4. Guests can visit the slightly abandoned house of the Witch and Alice. There is a wooden labyrinth along the road, and a bridge is thrown across the river.

Winter fun

The Moomin Park in Finland is also interesting in the winter season. There you can go snowshoeing, skiing and cheesecake. Everyone is happy to dance to funny tunes. Another winter attraction is ice skating, dog sledding and horseback riding.

Winter park equipment can be rented for free in the park. Of course, there are slides on which both children and adults have fun.

Cafes and restaurants

Visitors to the Moomin Park can take food: there are even specially equipped places on the territory where you can fry sausages or sausages on skewers. Restaurants in the Valley will delight guests with delicious home cooking. For cooking, only natural products are used, and possible allergens are excluded, because most of the visitors are children.

You should definitely go to the Moomin-mum buffet for delicacies. It presents only homemade dishes that will appeal to all guests. The drinks will offer water, milk and soft drinks. But even sweet tooths can find something delicious for themselves: coffee, pancakes, pancakes, various varieties of ice cream - Moomins also love sweets. You can buy popcorn, candy floss, and candy at the Moomin Kiosk.

The entire Moomin theme park is thought out and organized so that little guests feel comfortable. There are also several rooms for mother and child with everything necessary for convenience.

How to get there

The park is located on the southwestern Finnish coast, not far from Turku, but at a great distance from Helsinki. How to get to Moomin Park in Finland? First you need to get to Turku, and then to Naantali by buses (No. 11 and 110), which depart from the market. It is also accessible by boat. This trip will take several hours, but there is the opportunity to enjoy the picturesque scenery.

It can also be reached by car (if guests decide to rent it during their stay in Finland). The park has its own parking, so you can safely leave your car there and go enjoy the beauties. Parking for visitors is free.

Another opportunity to get to the Moomin Park is on a special bus. It leaves from Turku hotels to Naantali every 15-20 minutes. Moomin bus ride: for adults - 6.5 euros (one way) and 13 euros (round trip); for children under 14 years old - respectively 3 and 6 euros.

Schedule

You can clarify it on the official website. The opening hours of the Moomin Park in Finland depend on the time of year. It is open from June 8 to August 25, and in winter the good hippos hibernate and wake up only from February 16 to February 24. You can get to the Moomin-world before August 12 from 10 in the morning, and from August 12 - only from 12 in the morning. The park closes always at exactly 6 pm.

Ticket price

The Moomin country is not far behind other modern parks. And now it uses not ordinary tickets, but bracelets. You can buy them for one or two days. The cost for adults and children over 3 years old is 25 euros, for two days - 35 euros.

There is another kind of ticket - one, it costs 40 euros. It is valid for two days and includes a visit to the farm and reserve.
