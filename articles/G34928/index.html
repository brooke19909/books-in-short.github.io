Types and Types

All instruments of this purpose are divided into types in accordance with the measurements that they produce. In some cases, they even get a similar name. Therefore, if necessary, make a certain measurement, you can immediately determine the device that will cope with this best.

Digital measuring instruments

This type of instrument differs from its counterparts in the way of counting and outputting data, which involves the derivation of a digital value. It is worth noting that this measurement method is highly accurate, because it not only allows you to accurately calibrate the devices, but also to avoid errors when visually fixing the value.



Analog meters

This type of device is equipped with arrow pointers or a special scale. It should be noted that the readings obtained with them are quite accurate, but have a certain error. It is usually indicated directly on the scale with values. There is also a kind of these devices that does not have a scale at all, and the readings of such devices can give only a positive or negative answer. These include indicators that can only determine the presence of a value, and not its size.

Destructive Testing Instrument

This type of instrument is designed to measure the limiting value of the physical properties of objects. Therefore, after its application, the test sample is damaged. For example, after taking a fracture measurement, all parts that pass the test will be broken. That is why a measuring device of this principle of action is used only in production to study the control group of samples of a large batch of products in order to determine their properties and quality.
