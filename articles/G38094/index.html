Good old iPod Shuffle. User's manual

Every year, a variety of manufacturers of electronic equipment release new devices. Most of them are simple and understandable to any modern user due to the fact that they work on a similar basis. However, it happens that a certain model stands out, and its operation causes difficulties for users. One such device is Apple's mp3 player, iPod Shuffle. Instructions for it are absolutely necessary, because the device is unlike other iPods.





About iPod Shuffle

This device stands out for its modern yet archaic design. Nice looking mp3 player is made of quite durable metal. This gives it unprecedented resistance to damage. He is not only not afraid of falling to the ground, asphalt or concrete slabs, but also easily withstands a pedestrian who has stepped on him and even a car that has run over. From such physical influences, the functionality of iPod Shuffle does not deteriorate. The manual does not contain this information in order to protect the device from careless handling. Only minor cosmetic damage appears in the form of scratches and chips. This small pocket player is really capable of serving more than 10 years.





Unfortunately, the device did not receive protection against water and dust according to the IP68 standard. The device comes in a variety of colors. You can choose classic black or white, as well as bright blue or red. The box with the device is extremely small, but still it includes iPod Shuffle 2gb, instructions, Apple's proprietary merchandise and complete headphones. The player is inexpensive, so you should not expect a good headset. These are the cheapest headphones from Apple, the same as those that came with the very first iPhone. You should definitely not expect good and high-quality sound from them. There is only one branded sticker, and it is small.

iPod Shuffle Minimalist instruction

In creating the concept of an excellent mp3 player, Apple pursued several specific goals. This iPod was not supposed to be like the rest of the gadgets in the line. Creating a very budget device as a whole, engineers did not sacrifice the low quality of the product. The metal case protects it perfectly, and a minimal set of buttons ensures reliability. A small player in a minimalistic design with an extremely small set of options and a little functionality is called iPod Shuffle. The instructions for it are as small as the device itself. It fits neatly in a box. But if it is not very convenient or lost - the same instructions can be found on the Apple website.





In order to control the turning on and off of the device, the playback modes and the selection of playlists, two small keys on the top panel are involved. The leftmost key turns the device on and off and has 2 positions. By moving it to the right, you can turn on the player itself, switching a little further - select the mode of random playback of songs. To turn off the player, you need to return the key to its original position. The second key is responsible for the appointment and selection of playlists.
