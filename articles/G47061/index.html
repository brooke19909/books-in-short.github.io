Third Reich

It was generally believed that the Third Reich is the Third Rome, and the Germans living in it are the descendants of the great Romans.

The first Reich was called the state in Europe - the Holy Roman Empire, which included many of the European countries. The basis of the empire was considered Germany. This state lasted from 962 to 1806.

From 1871 to 1918 the period came, which is called the Second Reich. Its decline came after the surrender of Germany, the economic crisis and the subsequent abdication of Kaiser from the throne.

Hitler planned that the empire of the Third Reich would stretch from the Urals to the Atlantic Ocean. The Reich, which was prophesied for a thousand years, fell thirteen.

The Fuhrer dreamed of the greatness of Germany and its revival as a world power. However, the Nazi party was a product of bitterness and chaos.

Already initially all Hitler's speeches were filled with a spirit of violence and hatred. Strength was the only power he recognized. For the Germans, the new order primarily meant the return of national dignity, lost in 1918. Hitler managed to combine humiliation and desire to exalt himself, giving these feelings a new monstrous meaning.

The birth of Nazi ideology. Aryan race

For outsiders, one of the secrets of the Third Reich was such a thing as National Socialism. Hundreds of rituals sprang from nowhere and fascinated millions of Germans.

Darwin's theory led people into confusion. The centuries-old faith in God has been undermined. Occult sects and circles arose throughout the country. Secret societies were created that tried to revive ancient German mythology.

The mystery of the swastika

The Nazi party chose the swastika as its emblem in 1920. It spread everywhere - on buckles, sabers, orders, banners, being a symbol of occultism and esotericism.

Hitler personally designed the sketch of the flag of the Third Reich. The red color is a social thought in motion, the white represents nationalism, and the swastika is a symbol of the Aryan struggle and their victory, which will always be anti-Semitic.

The swastika was a symbol of the basic Nazi dogma, which claimed that absolute will would triumph over the forces of darkness and chaos. In the world of social nationalism, the Aryan race was the bearer and distributor of order. Before the swastika became a symbol of the Nazi party, it began to be used in the form of amulets by the Austrians and Germans. This was during the First World War and took its roots in the teachings of Blavatsky and Guido von List.

Elena Petrovna showed seven characters, the most powerful of which was the swastika. In Tibetan mythology, the swastika is a solar symbol, meaning the sun, as well as the god of fire Agni. The swastika was a manifestation of light, order and fortitude.
