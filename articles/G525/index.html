Where are the Champs Elysees in Paris?

Once in the capital of France - Paris, every tourist wants to see the Notre Dame Cathedral, the Louvre and, of course, go where the Champs Elysees are located. And this is in the 8th arrondissement of Paris, known not only as a business center. It is famous for the most beautiful and longest Champs Elysees Avenue in the world, consisting of many attractions. The Champs Elysees is a large showcase of Paris with its museums, shopping and entertainment centers, premiere cinemas, glamorous restaurants and expensive hotels.

History of Champs Elysees

The first to create the boulevard, the place where the Champs Elysees are located now, was Maria Medici herself. The cultivation of wetlands, which were almost next to the Louvre, began. The architect Lenotre was invited to create a beautiful landscape. First of all, elms were planted on three long alleys, the road was strengthened with pebbles so that the crews could move, and they lit it with gas horns. This is where the story of Champs Elysees in Paris began.

During the reign of Louis XIV, according to the master plan of Andre Lenotre, the prestigious landscape architect of the Palace of Versailles, and with the support of Jacques Hittorff, architect of Place de la Concorde, Avenue Champs Elysees appeared in Paris.

By the end of the 18th century, a wide and well-groomed boulevard extended from the Place de la Concorde to the Arc de Triomphe. The elegance of the style of Parisian life influenced the avenue, as evidenced by the parks, museums, cafes and fashion boutiques that appeared here.

Indeed, the Champs Elysees is the most famous boulevard in Paris. It consists of the northern and southern sectors. The northern side of the Champs Elysees in Paris is a developed infrastructural part from Ronda Poit to I'Etoile, and the southern part is a fully landscaped area that runs from Place de la Concorde to Ronda. The avenue has a width of 71 meters, and accommodates seven lanes that are easily controlled with daily traffic volume - about 84 thousand people.

The first houses on the Champs Elysees

At the end of the 17th century, the construction of the royal palace was completed. And the territory of the boulevard was fragrant in the gardens. Even now they delight the eyes of tourists thanks to the work of the architect Hittorf, who began their construction in 1838. Only at the beginning of the 19th century, where the Champs Elysees are located, development began. Over a century, only six residential buildings were built. In the XX century, ten more were erected.

Over time, the popularity of the Champs Elysees began to grow. And already in the XX century, they become the place of national holidays, annual parades and mass city events.
