Dali (acoustics): characteristics of the best models and reviews

The Danish brand has been known to many audiophiles since 1983, when its products first appeared on the audio equipment market. Today, the company has a strong position in this segment, supplying fans with high-quality acoustics. Despite fierce competition, the manufacturer manages to maintain expressive shades of Dali brand identity. The acoustics of this company was initially focused not on the mass consumer, but on connoisseurs of good sound. Therefore, the efforts of the developers are aimed at the qualitative improvement of equipment in various segments.

Features acoustics Dali

The introduction of new technologies helps to consistently maintain a high interest in the products of a Scandinavian company. In particular, the latest Epicon models have received soft magnetic compounds that eliminate unnecessary variations in Dali speakers. Acoustics in the latest versions are also provided by diffusers made of lightweight paper. In addition, it provides reinforcement with wood fibers, which gives the speakers a heterogeneous surface and reduces resonances. Thanks to this solution, the user can catch even the smallest details.





Proprietary technologies are also applied in the housing. In particular, Dali floor acoustics are made of six-layer MDF, the layers of which are glued together to form a rigid structure. As a result, the case suppresses excessive resonances, and properly curved shapes eliminate the effects of standing waves.

Characteristics of floor models

The Fazon Sub 1 model deserves the most attention in this line. This is not a premium version or even a representative of the highest class, but this subwoofer is an exemplary implementation of high-quality mid-range equipment. So, the Dali floor acoustics performed by Fazon Sub 1 is focused primarily on extracting deep bass. The shape of the device resembles a cube of small size, while the power of the system reaches 90 watts. The equipment is capable of operating in the frequency range from 37 to 200 Hz, and the addition in the form of a built-in amplifier allows you to qualitatively raise the sound level in critical areas.





Use of this system is recommended for multi-channel systems. Thanks to the presence of a woofer and a 16.5-cm diffuser, the low frequencies are played smoothly and evenly, which, however, is characteristic of many Dali models. Acoustics demonstrates the greatest effect in rooms up to 20 m 2 , but with a high-quality addition of powerful amplifiers, a worthy unfolding of potential in larger spaces is also possible.

Characteristics of shelf models

In the assortment of the Danish manufacturer there are several options for acoustics of this type, but the Ikon family is considered the most rational execution. In this case, a balance of cost and performance is observed, which, again, attracts a considerable part of the connoisseurs of good sound. Of greatest interest is the acoustics of Dali Ikon in the MK2 modification. This is not yet a premium level, but not an initial category with basic characteristics.





The user has at their disposal a 2.5-band system with a sensitivity of 86 dB and an ultimate acoustic pressure of 105 dB. The range of supported frequencies varies from 45 to 30,000 Hz, and the distortion coefficient corresponds to 6 Ohms. The range of recommended power for the amplifier is from 25 to 100 W, while it is desirable that the additional equipment was also presented by the Dali brand. Acoustics in combination with the equipment of their own brand, as a rule, provides higher efficiency.

Outboard Model Features

This line also has a lot of good options, including the continuation of the Ikon series. However, it makes sense to turn to another company family - Motif LCR. Acoustics is able to reproduce frequencies in the range of 78-25 000 Hz, while having a sensitivity of 89 dB and a resistance level of 6 ohms. An interesting feature of the model is that it can also be used as a shelf Dali speaker in a small room. However, if desired, the placement can have a large area - for example, if you supplement the system with a 120 W amplifier. The versatility of this equipment is also noted in other operating parameters. The model is suitable for operation as a stereo system, in a multi-channel complex, and also manifests itself effectively as a central speaker. Convenient mounting systems make it easy to cope with installation operations, which is also important during operation.





Dali Product Reviews

Many users note the even, clear and detailed sound that Dali provides. Owners highly value the materials of manufacture, noting their strength, which is combined with a beneficial effect on the reproduction characteristics.

Conclusion

The Dali brand does not belong to the popular brands producing audio equipment. Even among avid music lovers, its name is rather associated with the name of a Spanish painter. This is due to the fact that the company does not seek popularity, occupying its own specific niche. For example, shelf speakers Dali in some segments successfully competes with the largest Japanese and German brands.





Some models can outperform similar versions in quality, but they lose in price. It is the high cost that does not allow Scandinavian products to successfully develop the market. But there is already a question of compromise with quality - the use of expensive materials, which to a large extent and form decent characteristics of sound reproduction, inevitably affects the price.
