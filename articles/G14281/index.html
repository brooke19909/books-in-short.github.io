General information

The Museum of Local Lore of Kurgan originally had 3 departments devoted to the nature and history of the city and its environs, as well as to the achievements of Soviet society. Since 1957, a planetarium has been working there, where children and adults can study the starry sky.

Since the 1960s, when young scientists were included in the museum staff, active work began on the formation of the first museum collections. As a result, in the Soviet period, its funds were replenished with exhibits dedicated to the first Ural communes, collectivization, relics and documents relating to participants in the civil war and WWII.





Museum today

At the moment, KOKM is actively engaged in scientific and educational activities. In 1991, the Museum of Local Lore was transferred to a building that used to be known as the House of Political Education, and the Church of St. Alexander Nevsky was returned to believers. The museum has permanent exhibits of departments of nature and history.
