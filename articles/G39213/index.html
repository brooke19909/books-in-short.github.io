The famous Andreevsky descent in Kiev

It is very easy to feel the spirit of Kiev, to touch its history. It is enough to just walk along its old part and visit places that have become today monuments of culture, architecture and preserving the atmosphere of the past. One of these places of worship can safely be called St. Andrew's Descent. In Kiev, this street has long been one of the main attractions, and locals and numerous tourists are happy to walk along it. What is the history of this place and what is interesting to see here, read in this article.





Street - a cultural monument of the city

Very often, Andreevsky Descent in Kiev is compared with the famous Arbat in Moscow or Montmartre in Paris. And there really are similarities. The fate and creativity of many cultural figures who lived and worked in houses located along the descent is associated with this place. Some of them are museums and galleries today, where you can see the works of famous artists, objects that previously belonged to writers or actors. Let's find out more about these places.

The story of the creation of the famous descent

Andreevsky descent in Kiev was built by periods. Its upper part appeared in the 9-10th centuries, and in the 18th the street was expanded. The descent began to be actively built up from the 19th century. It is interesting that it connects two historical parts of the city: the upper and lower (Podil), which is called the city of artisans. Today it is one of the most beautiful streets in Kiev, which is a meeting place for local residents, an obligatory part of sightseeing tours for city guests, and there is also an active trade in works of art.





Places that will be interesting to everyone

At the very beginning of the street you will see house number 26, in which the businessman Akim Frolov once lived, and today it is the Museum of One Street. Here you can get acquainted with some exhibits related to historical events, the place of which was St. Andrew's Descent. Walking a little further down the street, you will come to the house number 13, where the famous writer Mikhail Bulgakov lived and worked. It is interesting to see numerous interior items and clothes belonging to him, as well as paintings, portraits and some musical instruments. Outside between this and the neighboring house there is a remarkable lantern with a black cat on it, which illuminates this mystical street, and also advertises

local restaurant.

Other attractions

There are other places that will be interesting to visit for those who come to Kiev. St. Andrew's Descent will take you to various museums, art galleries, theaters and souvenir shops. On this street is the Church of the Tithes, the Castle of Richard the Lionheart, as well as the monument to Prone Prokopovna and Golokhvastov.
