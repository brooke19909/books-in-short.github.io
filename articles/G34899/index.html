What is a bomb?

H-bomb

The hydrogen bomb is another scary nuclear weapon. The combination of uranium and plutonium generates not only energy, but also temperature, which rises to a million degrees. Isotopes of hydrogen combine into helium nuclei, which creates a source of colossal energy. The most powerful hydrogen bomb is an indisputable fact. It is enough to imagine that its explosion is equal to the explosions of 3,000 atomic bombs in Hiroshima. Both in the USA and in the former USSR, one can count 40 thousand bombs of various capacities - nuclear and hydrogen.

An explosion of such ammunition is comparable to the processes that are observed inside the Sun and stars. Fast neutrons split the uranium shells of the bomb with great speed. Not only heat is released, but also radioactive fallout. Up to 200 isotopes are counted. The production of such nuclear weapons is cheaper than nuclear, and its effect can be enhanced as many times as necessary. This is the most powerful detonated bomb that was tested in the Soviet Union on August 12, 1953.

Aftermath of an explosion

The result of a hydrogen bomb explosion is triple in nature. The very first thing that happens is a powerful blast wave. Its power depends on the height of the explosion and the type of terrain, as well as the degree of transparency of the air. Large fire hurricanes can form that do not calm down for several hours. And yet, the secondary and most dangerous consequence that the most powerful thermonuclear bomb can cause is radioactive radiation and contamination of the surrounding area for a long time.
