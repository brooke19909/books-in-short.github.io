Hotel Grand Uysal Hotel 4 *, Alanya, Turkey: overview, description and reviews of tourists

Almost at the very foot of the Toros Mountains, the resort town of Alanya, which is rightfully considered one of the best on the entire Mediterranean coast, has spread its possessions.

Constantly developing infrastructure, mild climate and genuine identity of Turkey attract a large number of tourists from all over the world, especially Europeans, to Alanya. It is here that the aparthotel Grand Uysal Hotel 4 * is located.





general information

The comfortable complex is a three-minute walk from the Cote d'Azur. The main feature of the Grand Uysal Hotel 4 * is the availability of apartments equipped for the maximum comfort of guests who prefer an independent vacation.

Living tourists feel apart, as if arriving at the sea, have their own housing, where even food can be prepared on their own. At the same time, the apart-hotel has a busy life and many entertainment events for adults and children.

Grand Uysal Hotel 4 * operates on a convenient all-inclusive system, which offers a varied meal in buffet restaurants, locally-made drinks in any bar on the hotel premises, as well as snacks offered between main meals in the snack bar.





Services

The friendly staff working at Grand Uysal Hotel 4 * will always tell you which services are included in the price and which can be purchased for an additional fee.

Free of charge: Wi-Fi throughout the hotel, swimming pools and equipped sun terraces, animation program, mini-club for children, games room, fitness, darts, table tennis, luggage storage, 24-hour front desk.

For a fee: spa services, billiards.

sights

Grand Uysal Hotel 4 * (Turkey, Alanya) is an extremely convenient option for tourists, as you can get to many interesting places from there. Some of them are worth mentioning.

It‚Äôs not a secret for many that the Ottoman Empire dominated the waters of the Mediterranean for a long time, and the Red Tower of Alanya serves as historical evidence and a symbol of the Seljuk‚Äôs skilful architecture. Initially, the tower was erected to protect the bay and bore the name of the Sultan of Keykubad. The usual name for the building was due to the reddish shade of the brick. Over time, some fragments of the tower collapsed, but this did not affect the holistic view of the building, and the foundation and walls of the tower are still amazing. If you go up to the observation deck, from where a stunning view opens up, you can feel the refreshing sea breeze and take wonderful photos.

Water Planet Waterpark is another place worth visiting. It includes 27 different slides from the most ordinary to completely unusual, twisted into a spiral and attracting only the most daring tourists. The most popular slide is Kamikadze, as well as a pool with artificial waves and many other water attractions that will leave a lot of impressions in adults and children.

Surrounded by the sea on three sides, the fortress of Alanya is a real visiting card of the resort town and shows all the belligerent grandeur of Turkey since the 13th century. Fortress walls stretch along the sea for 6 kilometers, and the building itself from the time of antiquity strengthened the skills of Seljuk architects in the history of construction. Here you can wander around dilapidated objects and explore the most convenient defensive points, which offer a gorgeous view of the rocky coast.





Cleopatra Beach

This is something for which many people go to Turkey, at the Grand Uysal Apart Hotel 4 * (reviews make sure this). In close proximity to the historical relic, at the foot of the ancient fortress, there is a magnificent beach of the Egyptian Queen Cleopatra, who, according to legend, has chosen this place in ancient times. Cream shade, clean sand, azure water, wild nature and old bizarre turrets of the fortress make this place a real tourist attraction, where anyone can make a wish and spend the sunset through the eyes of Cleopatra herself.

Not far from the beach, by the way, is the archaeological museum of Alanya, built in the 20th century. The museum exposition is divided into two main parts: archaeological and ethnographic. In the first part you can admire the ancient products of glass, marble, clay, made by Byzantine and Roman craftsmen. Household items, weapons, clothing of soldiers and women of that era are represented in the department of ethnographic art. Unique floor mosaics of the 2nd century AD will attract the attention of even the most experienced tourists.
