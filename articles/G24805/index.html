Craftswoman's notebook: how to sew buttons

The button is a small but necessary part of the wardrobe. Despite the appearance of hooks, zippers, and Velcro, she stubbornly maintains an honorable place in the leader list of decorative elements of clothing. Oddly enough, she has her own life, her own story, about which few people think and guess.



Why are buttons needed? It would seem that the answer is obvious. For the convenience of wearing clothes. But if you make an excursion into history, you can find out amazing and curious facts.

In ancient times, people wore clothes from hides. About any buttons then there was no question. But walking that way was pretty uncomfortable. The impromptu robe constantly swung open and strove to jump off the shoulders. Then our ancestors began to cut holes in the skins and fastened them with animal bones or wooden sticks. They did not even think about how to sew buttons. But inexorable time flew, new fabrics appeared, and fasteners also changed. In the Middle Ages, instead of bones and sticks, pebbles with drilled holes began to be used, on which loops were thrown. It is these pebbles that served as the prototype of modern buttons. Buttons themselves appeared much later. Initially, they performed the function not of a fastener, but of decoration. Sometimes one button cost more than the whole costume.

It was then that the tradition of attaching this jewelry to clothes arose. Advice on how to sew buttons was passed from mouth to mouth. Seamstresses and maids taught this, both merchants and noble people spoke about this. The buttons were different in shape. However, their main types have survived to our time: on a leg, with two and four holes.

The methods of fastening this small part are still alive. There are certain rules for how to sew buttons:



  1. Always start sewing from the wrong side.
  2. It is enough to make 5-7 stitches so that the button fits tightly on the clothes.
  3. If the fabric is too thick, do not pull the button strongly, let it slightly move away from the surface. This will prevent damage to the product if the button suddenly comes off.
  4. For outerwear and knitwear, it is better to use two buttons. One, decorative, will be located on the front side, and the second - auxiliary - on the wrong side. This will make the seam more durable and help to avoid tearing the fabric.
