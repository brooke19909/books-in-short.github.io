Gustav Klimt: biography, creativity and photo

Gustav Klimt was born on July 14, 1862 in the Viennese suburb of Baumgarten. The boy became the second child in the family of the not very successful engraving artist Ernest Klimt and his wife Anna Finster. Education and development

Due to the fact that Gustav's father was an artist, the boy learned the basics of drawing from him. Then, in 1876, Klimt went to study at the Vienna Art and Craft School, specializing in architectural painting. Already in 1877, Gustav's brother Ernst entered the same educational institution.

Klimt‚Äôs training at the school ended in 1883, and the historical genre artist Hans Makart became a kind of idol and model of art for the boy. During the training, many artists went against the law, but Klimt was not one of them and adopted educational conservatism.

After graduating from college in 1890, Gustav and Ernst and their friend, the artist Franz Mach, worked together and painted frescoes in the theaters of Reichenberg, Rijeka, Karlovy Vary and many other provincial cities of Austria-Hungary. Already in 1898, Gustav Klimt was awarded the Franz Josef Imperial Golden Cross award for his services in the field of art. After that, the artist became an honored member of the universities of Vienna and Munich.

The year 1892 was not easy for Gustav. Father and brother Ernst died, thereby maintaining the family became his concern. Such difficult times did not pass without a trace and reflected on the artist. Association of Vienna Artists

In 1897, Klimt and other artists who departed from the conservatism and traditions that dominated the House of Artists at that time founded the Vienna Artists Association, the Vienna Secession. He remained in the Gustav association until 1908. The main goal of the Vienna Secession is to organize exhibitions for artists who also depart from tradition and write in an unusual style. In this way, the best foreign works were attracted to Vienna, and the work of the group members became popular thanks to the magazine published by the association. The Vienna Secession did not have clear rules and a single style. Naturalists, realists, symbolists successfully coexisted here. The government supported the aspirations of artists and leased them land on which it was decided to build an exhibition hall. The symbolism of the association of artists was Athena Pallas - a symbol of justice, wisdom, art. The biography and work of Gustav Klimt are studied to this day.

In 1894, the artist was hired to create three paintings that embody such faculties as Philosophy, Medicine, and Law. They were supposed to decorate the ceiling of the assembly hall in the headquarters of the University of Vienna. They were only finished in 1900 and received sharp criticism for the overly frank genre that they called "pornographic."

New waves

Klimt was a supporter of new trends, so he transformed traditional ideas into an unusual style for conservatives, with an emphasis on the erotic component. A wave of discontent and criticism, ranging from political circles to religious ones, served as a result of the fact that the paintings did not adorn the main building of the university. This order was the last public and large-scale in the work of the artist. However, these three paintings have not sunk into oblivion; they were acquired by the philanthropist August Lederer.

Most of the artist‚Äôs work was criticized. He portrayed mainly women, if a man appeared in the picture, his face was most often hidden. A vivid example of the most famous painting "Kiss."
