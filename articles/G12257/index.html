Cleveland City (Ohio): Interesting Information About the Town

Cleveland (Ohio) is the second largest city in the state. The metropolis is located in the Midwest, in the northern region of Ohio, on the Kayahoga River and Lake Erie. The name of this place was given in honor of one general.

The history of the area is similar to the fate of many other provincial cities in the continental United States. Cleveland - was formed at the end of the XVIII century and during this time managed to "live" a very interesting life. His successes and moments of recovery alternated with moments of crises and decline. Today, the city is an extremely beautiful place, which welcomes guests with joy and provides as many opportunities as possible for a moderate and peaceful life to its population.

Cleveland (Ohio) owes its development and growth to its geographical position. The arrangement and formation of the railway communication further contributed to the progress of the city. For a short time, it turned into a large industrial center. In the 1920s, Cleveland ranked fifth in America in terms of population. But the Great Depression did not leave the best imprint on the development of the center: industry began to fade, the development of the village stopped. From this moment, the number of inhabitants of the metropolis is constantly decreasing. Today, Cleveland is a province from which representatives of the upper and middle classes are constantly leaving.

Weather features

If you are planning to go on an excursion to Cleveland (Ohio, USA), then you should familiarize yourself with the local climate. In summer, humid and warm weather prevails here. And in the winter time it is quite cold and a lot of snow falls. During the spring-summer period, you can encounter natural phenomena such as hail and tornadoes. The unevenness and amount of precipitation is affected mainly by Lake Erie. So, the eastern region of the suburb suffers from rainfall much more than all other areas.

Due to the arctic cold air that passes from the west through the lake, snow squalls form. But then, in the spring, Erie turns into an incredibly beautiful landscape. Its shores begin to shimmer with bright colors of blossoming flowers and everything is buried in verdure. This magnificence is worth seeing.





Sights of the village

Although not rich, but extremely beautiful is the city of Cleveland, Ohio. The sights here are truly wonderful. So, Public Square is popular, where there is a stone old church and a military monument to soldiers. Civic Center will also be interesting. There are various kinds of government institutions. The legendary Town Hall and the largest community park (Cleveland Mall) can be found among these sites.





Sports enthusiasts especially revere the North Coast District, the region with the largest stadium in the city. In the pier, you can see the most interesting objects, for example, the USS Cod submarine and the ship under the romantic name "William J. Mater."

Where to go

Cleveland, Ohio, is rich in various museums that every tourist is eager to visit. The Museum of Art is world famous. He gained his popularity thanks to the collection of exhibits that represent the art of Asia and Egypt. The collection included over 45 thousand exhibits.

The Rock and Roll Museum of Glory can be found on the shores of a local lake. This ultra-modern complex is built of steel, music, glass and light. Even if you do not like such music, you should still visit this institution.
