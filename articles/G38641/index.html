The history of the game of chess

The beginning of the history of chess goes back into the millennia. Scientists are still arguing about the true age of the game. According to many sources, chess did not appear before the 5th century BC. In India, a game called chaturanga arose, it was very reminiscent of modern chess, but still different from them. For example, the number of players was 4, not 2, and the right to move was decided by the flip of dice, not coins.

In the 10th century, after numerous wanderings around the countries, chess reached Europe, where some changes were made to the game and rules were drawn up. In the XIX century, the rules were corrected again and were finally formed, but the place where the king stands in chess remained unchanged. The world's first chess championship was held in 1886.
