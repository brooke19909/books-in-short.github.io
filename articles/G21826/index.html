The best time to relax

Tourists recommend coming here from November to March. During these months, the weather is most comfortable and pleases with light coolness, and the tropical climate is less prone to surprises. The temperature of air and water is almost the same - about 30 ¬∞ C. In addition, try to plan your trip for December, as this month hosts many interesting festivals. But be prepared for high prices. Hotels in North Goa (Candolim) always increase the cost of living during peak season, so book in advance to receive discounts.

Those who prefer relaxation at affordable prices are advised to come here in February or March. The beaches will be freed from tourists, it will stop raining, the weather will be calm, and the cost of rooms will delight in democracy.

Particular attention should be paid to Candolim beach, which is always clean and well maintained. Shack staff periodically drives away stray merchants. If you order a couple of items from the menu, then umbrellas and deck chairs will be provided for free. In other places, you will have to pay extra 50 rupees - about 45 rubles.

Where to stay

Well-off and demanding tourists are not in vain for their vacation, they mainly choose the northern part of Goa - Candolim. The hotels here have a developed infrastructure and high quality service. Compared to other regions, some of the most famous hotel business brands are represented here: Hyatt, Aguada Hermitage, Radisson, The Taj Holiday Village. Among the budget ones, it is worth noting Marquis, O-Hotel, Lemon Tree Beach Resort and 360 Degree.

In addition, in recent years, holidays in private villas have been gaining popularity among tourists, where a high level of service is presented, which is sometimes in no way inferior to a hotel. The main advantage of such proposals is the availability of their own kitchen and pool. Living in a homely villa is great for couples and families with children.

2-star Hotels in Candolim, Goa

1. Wildflower ("Wildflower")

The hotel complex is surrounded by lush vegetation. In addition to free Wi-Fi in public areas, the hotel offers the following amenities:

  • bar;
  • parking;
  • outdoor pool (open around the clock);
  • honeymoon suite;
  • playroom for children.

Wildflower has villas and 13 double rooms. Cleaning is done daily. Dry cleaning and laundry services are available for a fee. Ladies will have a great time in the massage room, and men in the gym. The complex also has a fitness center, table tennis and darts facilities.

Special conditions: pets allowed.

Cost: from 7350 rubles.

Address: 1709 / A1, Candolim Beach Road, landmark Saipem Sports Club.

2. Silver Sands Sunshine-Angaara ("Silver Sands Sunshine-Angaara")

This Candolima (Goa) hotel, located just 50 meters from the beach and the local market, has 30 rooms. It offers free Wi-Fi throughout, an outdoor pool, a library, shops, a shared lounge, a lounge area and a living room with TV.

Important information: Payment must be made prior to arrival by bank transfer. After booking, the property will contact you with additional advice. Also note that at Silver Sands Sunshine-Angaara, payment is only possible by credit card. Debit cards are not accepted here.

Cost: from 3200 rubles.

Address: Murrod Vaddo, Beach Road Bardez, Landmark Car Park.

3-star Hotels in Candolim, Goa

1. Phoenix Park Inn ("Phoenix Park Inn")

This place is primarily attracted by couples who prefer to enjoy good service and varied food for little money. The beach can be reached in just 15 minutes. The rooms are clean and equipped with everything you need. The luxury apartment is delivered daily with a plate of fresh fruits and drinks. The hotel has two restaurants: Sal and Chapora. When visiting, be sure to order Vindaloo - a spicy lamb dish.

Cost: from 5000 rubles.

Address: Sequera Vado.

2. Sea Breeze Resort

The Sea Breeze Hotel (Goa, Candolim) has become popular due to its high service, affordable prices and a good location. It features a private outdoor pool with sun loungers and parasols, and free high-speed Wi-Fi is available in public areas. Each room has a spacious furnished balcony, where you can spend time with a cup of coffee, enjoying the view and listening to the singing of tropical birds. It comes with air conditioning, a flat-screen cable TV and a private bathroom with a shower and toiletries. Each guest can use the services of a tour desk and go to Fort Aguada, "Monkey Beach" and other attractions.

Please note that when checking in, in addition to a passport, you must present a credit card, since debit cards are not accepted here.

Cost: from 2500 rubles.

Address: Murrod Vaddo, landmark of Taste of China.

4. Alor Grande Holiday Resort

Alor Grande Hotel (Goa, Candolim) is located 800 meters from the local Calangute Beach. At the disposal of guests 70 comfortable rooms equipped with all amenities. Despite being an economy class, Alor Grande Holiday Resort is distinguished by its friendly and helpful staff, clean rooms and well-groomed green areas. When checking in, a bottle of local wine will be waiting for you, and on the day of departure a small gift. Those who wish to always keep themselves in shape can visit the gym or fitness center. The SPA offers a variety of relaxing treatments, including aromatherapy and massage. You can also play chess, table tennis and darts. Those who prefer a more active holiday are given the opportunity to rent a bicycle or car.

Cost: from 2500 rubles.

Address: Behind Delfion's High Mart, Goa 403515.

As you already understood, some of the most luxurious hotel complexes and hotels in Goa are built in this area. Candolim, by the way, also boasts the presence of luxury villas. Things to do

So, what to do after checking into one of the Candolim hotels? Goa is a great place for entertainment and water sports. To make sure of this, it is enough to get to Sinquerim. Here you can go snorkeling, diving, rent a scooter or go on a visit to dolphins.

If you walk along the coast towards Calangute, you will find a clear pond surrounded by giant scarlet lotus flowers.

The resort is the best suited for a relaxing, relaxing holiday. Candolim fans will tell you about the spirit of freedom and incredible energy that reigns here, about the opportunity to hang out, and then find time for meditation and finding yourself. But opponents of the northern part (of which there are also many) will unanimously talk about complete unsanitary conditions, licentious dispositions and poverty. By the way, both speak the truth. One thing is certain: Goa is a truly unique place, and in order to form your own opinion about it, you need to come here at least once.
