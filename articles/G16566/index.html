Khovanskoe cemetery: how to get there, what to see

Among the most diverse sights of Moscow, necropolises are very popular. There are almost 70 of them in Moscow, although, of course, not all of them have, so to speak, artistic value.

History

But a little about the history of this place. Khovansky cemetery is quite young. It was founded only in the year 72 of the last century. Today it is divided into two parts - North and West. The first is older, it has existed since 1978. The second, called "Western", was founded in 1992. Currently, the necropolis is considered the largest in the whole of Europe, its area reaches almost two hundred hectares.

This cemetery is also known for the church complex, where there is a remarkable church of the Baptist John and a chapel of the famous icon of the Mother of God of Vladimir. And since the year 97 of the last century, there has been a site in the cemetery where they are buried according to Muslim customs. It is equipped with everything necessary to carry out the deceased on the last Muslim path according to all Muslim canons.

"Criminal" burials

If you understand how to get to the Khovansky cemetery, visit it at least out of curiosity. The most scandalous burials here are the graves of criminal authorities. One of them, nicknamed Sylvester (according to the passport Timofeev S.I. ), died from the explosion in 1994. The second, buried opposite him, is Ded Hasan (aka Aslan Usoyan), who was shot dead by a sniper last January.



But not everything is so odious. More worthy personalities are buried here. So, the Khovansky cemetery was also chosen as the resting place of Nina Iskrenko (poetess) and Symbolokov (magician, illusionist). How to get to their graves, and in what part of the necropolis they are located, the cemetery keeper will tell you. And they are not the only significant people buried here. Many directors, poets, composers and journalists, outstanding figures of the Soviet Union and modern Russia rest in the cemetery.

Recall that almost 200 hectares is the entire Khovansky cemetery. How to get it to the right part, it is worth thinking in advance. On religious days and funeral days, the route is simplified and more diverse. Then additional express trains are launched, in the role of which is the bus. The Khovanskoye cemetery is accessible "in parts": express trains 2 and 3 go to the central part (from the Teply Stan metro station and Yugo-Zapadnaya station, respectively), and to the northern one - routes 1 and 4, from the same metro stations.
