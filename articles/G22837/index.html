As we already mentioned, the Papillon Zeugma Relaxury 5 * hotel (Belek) is located near the town of Belek, which is considered the young resort of Turkey. In addition, he is known for popularizing golf.



The city is located in the land of cedar, eucalyptus and pine forests, striking in its beauty. Rare birds are found in them, one of which even became a symbol of Belek. This is a tito alba owl. Belek is the most presentable, and therefore expensive resort, because there are few young people. The region itself has wide and beautiful beaches stretching for twenty kilometers. And the swimming season lasts from May to October. It is thanks to the even, clean, with a gentle entrance to the water Belek has become so popular. Its coast is well equipped and leads among other resorts in Turkey in terms of the number of blue flags. The blue sea and white sand complement the most beautiful forests, approaching almost to the coast.

Almost all hotel complexes in Belek have a category of 4-5 stars and are located on the first line along the entire coast. Attractions Belek

Resting in Papillon Zeugma Relaxury 5 * (Belek), you should definitely visit the famous Belek to see all its attractions. Not far from the city are the ancient ruins of Side, Aspendos and Perge. In it you can go horse riding, mountain climbing and many different water sports, among which the most popular rafting. In the park you can see huge carriage-caretta turtles that go ashore to lay eggs in the sand. Several hundred plant varieties grow in the reserve, including pistachios, cypresses, eucalyptus trees.

In Belek, on the territory of the Rixos complex, there is a beautiful Troy water park, which is decorated in the style of the ancient city. In Perga, tourists should see the ancient theater, which is part of the architectural and historical complex. It was built by the Romans on the site of the same Hellenic building. This theater is better preserved than all similar buildings in all of Asia Minor. It has the most beautiful bas-reliefs that have survived to this day. Many of the most beautiful fragments have long been stored in museums in the country.
