Monastir - the history of the city

Nature herself did everything in her power to make Monastir a resort town:

  • It is located among the waters of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • The beaches with pure white sand seem to be inviting tourists.
  • Wonderful climate, not annoying by sweltering heat.

These natural little things make your vacation in this country very enjoyable. Although many tourists are afraid to travel to Muslim countries on beach vacations, as they are new to their laws and traditions, this is not the case in Tunisia.





You can swim in swimsuits here, but it is better not to appear on the streets or in the mosque in shorts and open tops.

Once this town was the residence of Caesar, and its name is familiar to everyone who at least superficially studied the history of the Ancient World. Carthage was part of the Phoenician state. Brave travelers from the city of Tira, led by Queen Didona, who escaped from the treacherous brother of Pygmalion, founded the city, buying cunningly a large piece of land from a local tribe.

At the request of the tsarina, local Aboriginal leaders agreed to sell her as much land as the skin of a bull could cover. Perhaps such a prospect amused them, but they did not know what the clever Phoenician queen is capable of. Cutting the skin into thin strips, she overlaid them with such a large piece of land that it was possible to build a powerful fortress, which the fugitives from the Phoenician city of Tyra did. This fortress was called Birsa, which in translation from Phoenician means skin.

Convenient location of Carthage and its strategic importance as a port on the way of ships from different countries, always made it a ‚Äútidbit‚Äù for those who wanted to seize its wealth.

In Tunisia, with its ancient history, there is something to see, but most often any excursion begins with a visit to the ancient Ribat fortress on the seashore. It was built in 796 by Hartam bin Ayyan and even now one can feel the power of its walls, so strong and magnificent. You can visit it on your own, but it is better to do it accompanied by a guide who will give information about how, and who built this building, about its strategic importance in various historical eras.
