Perseid Meteor Shower

August is called the month of starfalls. And so he was nicknamed not by chance. The fact is that during this period in the northern hemisphere of the planet one can observe a very spectacular meteor shower of Perseid. This stream is not the only one that can be seen from the Earth with the naked eye. However, the Perseid stream is the brightest and most colorful. What is a Perseid stream?

It is a variant of meteor shower created by stone and ice particles from the tail of a comet. It is interesting that the comet itself approaches the Earth only once every 135 years, but its tail flies near the Earth annually, which creates the effect of starfall. Fragments of rock and ice, which are separated from the tail of the comet, fall into the Earth‚Äôs atmosphere and burn here. Thus, a Perseid flow arises, which we see as hundreds of simultaneously falling stars.

Observation history



Actually, we have the opportunity to review this incredibly beautiful natural phenomenon every year. Interestingly, UFO reports from people who do not know about this phenomenon become more frequent during this period. In general, earthlings drew attention to the Perseid stream in ancient times. The earliest mention of it is found in one of the Chinese annals dated 36 year. Often, starfall was also noted by Korean and Japanese chroniclers throughout the medieval period. On the European continent, the Perseids were called the "Tear of St. Lawrence," because they coincided in time with the festival of the same named Catholic Church, which was held in Italy.
