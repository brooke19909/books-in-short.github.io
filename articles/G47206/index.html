The Tunisian city of Hammamet is a rather unique place that has become popular with celebrities and tourists only since the 20s of the last century. Then an aristocrat from Romania named Georges Sebastian decided to acquire a country villa here. The project was executed in the Moorish style, and this house became the most beautiful of all the buildings of those years. Numerous friends who came to the aristocrat immediately fell in love with this wonderful corner, and gradually Hammamet became a favorite place among many celebrities who also decided to build their own villas here.





Today, this city, surrounded by green pomegranate and citrus orchards, numerous palm trees and whole plantations with olives, is one of the most popular and most visited resorts in Tunisia. Everyone can afford to relax here: the abundance of various hotels makes staying in Hammamet affordable for both middle-income citizens and very wealthy people.

There are plenty of places for youth entertainment, but for those who dream of a quiet and measured rest, this is also a great option. Soak up on the shores of the warm sea, see interesting sights, take advantage of the unique wellness procedures of thalassotherapy - all this can be done without problems if you arrive in Hammamet. It is worth noting that many large hotels in the resort are ready to offer their guests separate SPA rooms and their own hospitals.

Hammamet Attractions

Tourists can diversify their beach vacations very easily if they go to explore some interesting place in the resort. For example, an indispensable part of any cultural program in this city is a visit to the Medina (the oldest part of the city), surrounded by a high fortress wall. On its territory there are many old houses, a fort and a real oriental bazaar, which functions today. Lovers of antiquity can visit the ancient fortress called Kasbah and the museum of archeology working here, which contains unique items from the Roman period.

Those who are more interested in all kinds of plants are sent to the stunning gardens of Sebastian, which the city is rightly proud of, because very rare representatives of the flora grow here. For lovers of animals, the doors of the Phrygia Zoo are always open, which occupies an impressive territory of 36 hectares. Over 50 species of various birds and animals are waiting for guests, whose life can be observed endlessly.





Thrill seekers are simply obligated to visit the Cartage Land amusement park, which will transfer all guests to the ancient Carthaginian town. There are attractions for tourists of all ages who are somehow connected with some historical era. All kinds of exciting slides, labyrinths, an artificial river and even a small water park will not leave indifferent any visitor.
