A bit about Tula

Tula is a large Russian city, founded back in 1446. This is a city of gunsmiths. And he is famous for the famous gingerbread. By metropolitan standards, Tula can be considered the suburbs, as the distance from Moscow is only 180 kilometers. However, this is a separate geopolitical region of Russia. The population of the city is about 500 thousand inhabitants.



It was the proximity to Moscow, a large number of historical and cultural attractions that made city hotels popular. The city of Tula is the cradle of talented craftsmen. Residents of Tula are proud of their city and their history.

History of the city and its attractions

Everyone knows the parable of the Lefthander, shoeing a flea. True or not, but the talent of Tula metal processing masters is known throughout the world. Tula weapons glorified for centuries. Since 1714, the arms factory has been producing many interesting items. The city has a special museum where all kinds of different models of weapons, firearms and cold are presented.



Tula is famous for its samovars, which have been produced since 1778. Guests of the city can visit a special museum dedicated to Russian tea drinking. A separate attraction of the city is the Kremlin of Tula - a complex of monuments of past centuries. Necropolis of the Demidovs - a museum dedicated to the memory of the legendary founder of the arms industry Nikita Demidov. The Tula Gingerbread Museum is dedicated to the traditions of printing gingerbread, which originated in the 17th century.

These and other attractions attract guests to Tula. It is for numerous guests of the city that the doors of various hotels are always open. Tula residents always welcome visitors. A variety of service levels, corresponding to the comfort of the price, the possibility of choosing a location and quality of service - all this is offered by Tula hotels.
