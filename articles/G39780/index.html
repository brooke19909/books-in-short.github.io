Baikal - paradise lake

Perhaps some do not know, but Baikal is the deepest lake on the planet. It is located in Eastern Siberia and is a large reservoir of fresh water. Due to its large size, the lake has a wide variety of flora and fauna. Unique landscapes and unusual inhabitants will delight any tourist. All people who like the stunning nature and simply enchanting atmosphere, recommend a vacation on Lake Baikal. The Small Sea is a part of the lake, which is separated by the island of Olkhon. Its length is striking: it is 70 km. Other islands are located in it, such as Zamogoi, Khibin, Izhilhey, Oltrek and others.

Many local residents (and in Russia as a whole there is such an opinion) consider Lake Baikal a sea. This is not surprising, because its length is 620 km and a width of 24 to 79 km. This is impressive, since some seas are not worth comparing with Baikal. Note that in the ranking of the largest lakes in the world, he takes sixth place.

Interesting information about Baikal



A trip to Lake Baikal can be unforgettable. Everything around is for peace and relaxation. In addition, the scenery around is fascinating. The lake is surrounded on all sides by mountain ranges and hills. If you look at Lake Baikal from a satellite, you can see that it stretched in the form of a crescent.

Researchers claim that Baikal is the deepest lake on the planet. It was found that its depth is 1642 meters, which is impossible for an ordinary person. As for the average depth, it is also not small - 744 m. Surprisingly, in addition to Lake Baikal, only two lakes on the planet can at least get closer to the 1200 m mark.

Scientists note that the Baikal waters are saturated with oxygen, and they have very few organic impurities. As for the temperature, it is very low, and this does not allow swimming. In summer, it can be + 8 ... + 9 , and in winter the lake is covered with a thick layer of ice.

Small sea

Lake Baikal consists of several parts. The Small Sea is one of them. It is located on the territory of the Baikal National Park (Irkutsk Region). The Small Sea is connected to the lake through a strait called Olkhon Gate. The coast is mostly mountainous and the depth is 210 meters. In some places there are moving sands.



On the coast of the Small Sea you can see capes Oto-Khushun, Uyuga, Aral, Zunduk and others. The scenery around is amazing! Vacationers can safely go fishing, as perches, graylings, sturgeons, pikes, golomyanks, Baikal omuli, whitefish and elts are found in the lake. Also, tourists can visit settlements, for example, Kharantsy, Khuzhir or Sarma. It is on their territory that the camps of the Small Sea (Baikal) are located.

What to do on Baikal?

Of course, beautiful landscapes, a gentle steppe breeze are great, but entertainment is still needed. Every tourist wants to see something new, experience exciting emotions and feel happy. Holidays on Lake Baikal (the Small Sea, in particular, is a priority among tourists) can be truly exciting. It is proposed to start the trip with a visit to the sources of the Lukovaya Polyana. They are located in the vicinity of Ognuren - the largest village of the Small Sea. Mineral springs are famous for their healing power, because water contains radon and iron, which helps to improve and normalize the functioning of the gastrointestinal tract.



Further, it is worth trying the healing mud of the Cross Lakes. They are famous for treating sciatica and rheumatism. A stunning sight for tourists will be the meadow plain of Zama. It is framed by the mountains of the Primorsky ridge, and after it begins the road to the gold mines of the Lena tributaries, around which there are mineral springs, warm lakes, Kurykan ruins and healing mud.

Rest on Lake Baikal (the Small Sea is the predominant destination) is impossible without a visit to the Aral cave-grotto. And, of course, it is recommended to visit the stone tent, Sarma gorge, Enhok, Kurkut and Mukhor bays. The islands of the Small Sea will also interest vacationers. There are about ten of them, and to look at them, you can go on a boat trip.

How to get to Baikal?

There are several ways to come to Lake Baikal. Mostly, people prefer to travel by car, but you can also take a passenger ship, train, or even plane. The latter option is available only to the nearest cities that have an airport, that is, you will still have to go to Lake Baikal by one of the above ground modes of transport. By boat you can reach your destination in about seven hours. But no matter which path a tourist chooses, the main thing is that he will come to the area covered with legends. And it's worth it!
