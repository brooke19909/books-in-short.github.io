Who is entitled to child allowance up to the age of 18?

Everyone knows that it is impossible to live on social benefits in Russia. But why not take advantage of your rights and arrange all the statutory benefits and payments? Child allowance under 18 is provided to low-income families. To obtain such a status, a family must have an income that does not exceed the standard of living.

Who is eligible for benefits before adulthood?



To receive a monthly child allowance, you need to contact the regional branch of the social protection authorities. Important! You can not go to any branch, but only to the one to which the registration address of one of the parents belongs. The allowance up to the age of 18 should be received by children from low-income families, single mothers, children of military personnel, and those whose parents have been convicted of evading child support. There are separate benefits for children and parents with disabilities, payments for the loss of the breadwinner in the event of the death of one of the parents. All funds are transferred to the bank account indicated by the applicant (or by mail). In addition to cash payments, there are also social benefits for low-income families: providing places in kindergartens and schools, rest on special conditions and treatment in sanatorium-resort institutions.

Child allowance up to 18 years: how much?

The formation of the size of payments is influenced by the regional factor. For example, the minimum for the capital is from 700 rubles per month for each member of the family who has not reached the age of majority.

How to apply for benefits for children under 18?



To receive monthly compensation payments, it is necessary to prepare a number of documents. You will need originals and copies of the passport of the parent (or guardian) issuing the allowance, and the birth certificate (or passport) of all minor children in the family. A certificate of the composition of the family is required (it is done when contacting the passport office, and its validity period is one calendar month). Documents confirming the income (or its complete absence) for each of the parents will also be required. These are: certificates of employment, extracts from work books or certificates from the Pension Fund. If one of the parents does not work, but at the same time receives an education, then a certificate must be submitted from the educational institution or from the Employment Center.



If a child allowance up to the age of 18 is assigned upon loss of the breadwinner or in case of parental evasion of child support, then you will need to provide a death certificate for the parent (or a court certificate). Single mothers will also have to confirm their status with an appropriate certificate. To pay for the care of a disabled child (or HIV-infected), relevant medical documents are provided. If the family with disabilities is not only one or both parents, but also the child (children), then you will need certificates or extracts from the medical history.

When filling out documents, do not forget that a child allowance up to the age of 18 is paid only if the child is a student of any educational institution. If the child does not study, then payments cease on the day they reach 16 years of age.
