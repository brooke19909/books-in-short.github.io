The definition of myth and mythology in children does not cause much difficulty. This is something like a fairy tale, an invention of ancient people who did not know anything, but everyone tried to explain. However, the definition of the word "myth" is not so simple. The foundation

Features of thinking that make mythology a symbolic system of description and understanding of the world can be reduced to the following features:

  • lack of ability to distinguish the real from the supernatural;
  • low criticality, expressed in ignoring obvious contradictions;
  • only the initial degree of development of abstract concepts;
  • sensually specific knowledge;
  • imagery;
  • emotionality.

These and some other features of primitive thinking equate mythology with worldview.

Myth and religion

Mythology and religion are not the same thing. They appear separately from each other, but closely interact throughout the history of mankind. Mythology as a worldview includes religion, as well as primitive philosophy and knowledge. In this sense, it is primary. Myth as a name is expressed in a specific image or in a religious ritual. So he becomes not a familiar story, but a dance, song or other ceremonial action. However, the relationship between the two structures does not end there. So-called cult myths serve to explain rituals. They often appear when the original meaning of the action is lost. Such narrative myths are secondary to ritual.

Myth and Literature

In order to make the definition of the word "myth" more understandable, it makes sense to distinguish between this concept and its relatives. Among them - a fairy tale, a heroic epic, a legend. The first two grow out of a myth. A fairy tale to a lesser extent has an etiological function (does not talk about the origin of something), and is often supplied with conscious fiction. The narrator and listeners are aware of the unreality of the events described. Myth arises in the process of unconscious creation. Its content is perceived ‚Äúas is‚Äù and is never questioned.

The definition of mythology as the ancestor of literature, therefore, is completely fair. On its basis, many famous narrative genres grow.
