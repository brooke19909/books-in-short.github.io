The largest pond in Moscow is Borisovsky. As a rule, the name of this reservoir is used in the plural, as it includes several reaches. Above this body of water along the Borisov bridges passes Kashirskoye highway. To the north of the ponds is the transport artery of the same name in a huge city called Moscow - Borisov Ponds Street.

Section 2. Borisov Ponds. History of occurrence



Studying the history of ponds leads researchers to the time of Tsar Boris Godunov. It is believed that these sources received their modern name on behalf of the king.

In the 18-19 centuries this water body was called the Tsareborisovsky pond.

Near the pond is now the village of Borisovo, which once belonged to Boris Godunov. A monument to Tsar Boris was erected near the springs on the South Bank in 1591 in honor of the victory over the Tatars. Until our time, the monument has not been preserved, in 1935-1940 it was destroyed. There is a version according to which it is believed that he was thrown into the pond.

Near the village, archaeologists are researching the Borisov dam, which was built by Godunov in 1600 to breed fish in order to overcome the effects of a hungry year. In the 17th century, the village of Borisovo was crowded and rich. At this time, a mill was built near the dam, which in the 19th century was transformed into a paper spinning mill.

A wooden church was once built on the banks of a pond, which was replaced by a stone church in the early 18th century. It has not survived to our time, just like the Old Believer church built later in its place : flooding caused the destruction.

Section 3. Borisov Ponds. What do tourists need to know?





The construction of the Church of the Life-Giving Trinity near Borisov Ponds was carried out in the early 2000s in honor of the 1000th anniversary of the baptism of Russia. The complex of buildings of this temple belongs to the Byzantine style.

In the early 2000s, work was done to drain the ponds, clean them and improve them.

Today, on the banks of Borisov ponds you can easily find a quiet place for fishing. Agree, a rarity for such a metropolis as the capital of Russia.

When it snows, in these places children enjoy sledding, and lovers of extreme hardening bathe in ice water.

In summer, in the park near Borisov Ponds, you can ride a bicycle along specially equipped paths and relax in cozy gazebos. There are 2 football fields, tennis courts, volleyball courts and other facilities for recreation.
