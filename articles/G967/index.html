Learning to crochet from scratch: what is needed?

It is best to start training with a fairly thick hook and the same yarn - it will be easier. Note that the hook should be thicker than the thread (optimally - 2 times). Initially, you will need to master the simplest steps: knitting a chain of air loops, single crochet and double crochet.

A chain is the basis of any knitting. To create it, you should make a loop of thread, then insert a hook into it and hook the thread lying on your hand, pull it towards you. Further, by analogy, the hook catches the thread and pulls it into a loop. Continue until the desired chain length is reached.





The single crochet is knitted on the basis of a chain. A hook is inserted into the second loop of the chain from the hook (the direction is from front to back), then a working thread is thrown (or thrown over it), which is pulled through the raised loop. Now there are two loops on the hook. Through them again it is necessary to stretch the working thread.

The double crochet is knitted according to the same principle. Only the hook is inserted into the loop, which is the fourth from the "tool." Before you insert the hook into the loop, a working thread is thrown over it. Next, the resulting loops are knitted like this: first the first two of the resulting three, then two more.

The types of knitting listed above are the basis of any masterpiece created with a hook.
