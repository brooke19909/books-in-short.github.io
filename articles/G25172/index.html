Siena: Attractions of the Italian Town

Between the valleys of the rivers Elsa, Arbia and Merce, beautiful Siena is located on the picturesque hills. Sights of the city are of great interest to visiting tourists, it is necessary to allocate at least a couple of days for their inspection. After arriving in Siena, many travelers get the impression that they were in the Middle Ages. This is not surprising, because the Italian town really stubbornly supports the look acquired in the XIII century. This feature gives it a special charm and originality.



The layout of the city is a little chaotic and fragmented, but not to get lost and visit all the interesting places will help the map of Siena with attractions. All roads converge at the central square of Campo. Its shape reminds the Italians the open cloak of the Virgin Mary, the patroness of the city. It is divided into 9 sectors by strips of travertine and paved with stones of different colors. Campo is considered one of the unique and unique squares of Europe.

The Duomo is a real treasure and an example of Tuscan art, which Siena is so proud of. The sights of the cathedral amaze even the most experienced tourists. The project was designed by the famous architect Pisano, working in the Gothic style. For many years this masterpiece was erected, but in 1348 the structure had to be abandoned due to the plague that began in the city. Now travelers can admire the walls of an unfinished building.



Fans of antiquities will love the Casa di Santa Caterina - the home of the nun Caterina Beninquaza, declared the patron saint of Italy. In the 16th century, Siena acquired many interesting architectural objects. Sights are striking in their wealth and sophistication. An example of the architecture of that time is the Palazzo del Magnifico. The palace was built for the city manager Pandolfo Petrucci. The construction dates back to the Renaissance and is one of the favorite places of the inhabitants of Siena.

The Medici fortress was built in 1563 by the architect Lanci on the orders of Cosimo Medici. Her main task was to prevent the assertion of independence, which Siena so sought. The sights of this place are in the beautiful city garden into which the fortress was transformed. The building offers a picturesque panorama of the surroundings. Today, temporary exhibitions are often held in the fortress. There is also a library and an audio archive.



The Museum of the Hospital Santa Maria della Scala is also proud of Siena. A map of the city will help you navigate and visit all the great historical, cultural and architectural monuments. The museum houses a huge collection of works of art, among which you can find images of noble seniors, pilgrims, patients, emperors, monks. The attention is drawn to the Pilgrim Hall, painted by great artists. Here is the Church of Our Lady with beautiful frescoes.
