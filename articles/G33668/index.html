What to see in Turkey? Sights and unusual places

What to see in Turkey? There are many answers to this question. Turkey is a unique country. No other country has such a huge number of ancient monuments and natural attractions. In addition, 2 of 7 wonders of the world were located in Turkey .





Istanbul

What is worth seeing in Turkey? First of all, Istanbul. This city is distinguished by its uniqueness. What is so special about it? Firstly, Istanbul is located on two continents (in Europe and Asia). Secondly, Istanbul is the capital of three huge empires (Hittite, Byzantine and Ottoman). It is not surprising that Istanbul is now a treasury and a museum city, where you can admire the historical monuments of different cultures and eras, as well as a great many palaces of the Ottoman sultans. Tourists can explore the palaces and plunge into the atmosphere of luxury.





Istanbul is a city of two religions

Istanbul is a city where two religions organically unite. Nearby are mosques and Christian monuments. It is worth admiring the Sultan Akhmet mosque, which is called the Blue (in its decoration, ceramic blue tiles were used), the Suleymaniye mosque, which is famous for its beauty, and the Muslim shrine - Eyyup mosque. Among Christian churches, it is necessary to visit the Temple of Hagia Sophia - the most famous Christian monument, the engineering miracle of its time (the museum is now in the building) and the Church of the Holy Savior, which in its importance takes second place in the world after the Church of Hagia Sophia. In the likeness of the Temple of Hagia Sophia, Sofia Kiev and Sofia Novgorod were built.





Museums in istanbul

Istanbul is famous for its many museums. The archaeological museum in which the sarcophagus of Alexander the Great lies is especially famous . Here you can also visit museums: Turkish ceramics, Turkish and Islamic arts, Turkish carpets, mosaics, aviation, military, marine, fine arts, the city museum, theater, industry and Ataturk house museum.

Bodrum

If you have not decided what to see in Turkey, then go to Bodrum. This city is the cultural capital and one of the main tourism centers in Turkey. Artists gather here, exhibitions are held. Bodrum will be interesting to lovers of bohemia, as there is always a corresponding atmosphere. Sports enthusiasts will also find something to their liking in Bodrum. They will be able to do surfing, yachting, rowing, scuba diving. Fans of history and archeology will be happy to visit ancient monuments telling about the past of the city, the gates of Mindos, the fortress walls of Halikarnassus and Halikarnassus fortress. Previously, in Bodrum was one of the wonders of the world - Halicarnassus Mausoleum. Now at this place is located Halikarnassus Museum, where you can see the mock-up of the mausoleum.



Hilt

What to see in Turkey? You should definitely visit Ephesus - the tourist center of the Aegean Sea region. This city is the record holder for the number of antique works located in it. Despite the small size of the city, a huge number of tourists come here every day. Ephesus has preserved the basic structure of ancient cities. Therefore, visiting this city, you can imagine how the ancient settlements were arranged. Many ancient buildings have been preserved here, the elegance and completeness of forms of which amazes tourists: the ancient library of Celsus, two agora, baths, and dwellings of noble citizens. In Ephesus there is also a house in which the last days of the life of the Blessed Virgin Mary of the Virgin Mary passed. In Ephesus once there was a fourth wonder of the world - the Temple of Artemis. Now only a column recovered from the wreckage is left of it. Despite the fact that now this city is dead, it is constantly filled with crowds of tourists.





Demre (Mira)

Demre - this is the name of the ancient city of the World. The main attraction of the city is the temple of St. Nicholas. Demre is famous for the fact that St. Nicholas lived and did his good deeds here . Here the custom of Christmas gifts was born.

Mount Olympos

This mountain is known as one of the main attractions of the country. It is considered the abode of the Chimera. At the top of the mountain you can see a unique natural phenomenon: flashes of fire appearing from under the ground.





Pamukkale





Pamukkale means Cotton Castle. The city is famous for its white cliffs. Legend says these rocks are a cotton crop harvested by mythical titans. It is also famous for thermal springs rich in calcium. Over the millennia, calcium has formed many cascades, terraces and pools that are striking in their beauty. But not only beauty attracts thermal springs. They are famous for their healing and anti-aging effects. It is believed that Cleopatra came here to preserve her beauty. Cappadocia



What to see in Turkey? Cappadocia is the most unique attraction not only in Turkey, but also in the whole world. When you come to Cappadocia, it seems that you are on another planet. No wonder one of the valleys of Cappadocia is called the moon. Cappadocia is famous for its unusual landscapes, which are unique natural formations: bizarre rocks look like fairy-tale heroes and mythical monsters. Cappadocia is called the land of beautiful horses and the country of fairies. Here horses were bred, which then fell into the legendary horse of Darius. In this area the first Christians hid from persecution. Here, in the rocks of Cappadocia, they cut down entire cities, many of which have survived to this day. Therefore, when traveling to Turkey, you should visit the rocky cities of Cappadocia.

Alanya





What to see in Alanya? Turkey is known for the Byzantine fortress located here on a rock - the most important attraction of Alanya. It is also worth visiting the caves, which are very many in Alanya. The most famous of them are the Maiden Cave (pirates contained abducted women in it), the Phosphorus Cave (with unique lighting) and the Lovers' Cave. The Dalmatash grotto is very famous, in which stalactites, stalagmites and calcareous deposits form bizarre figures. Local conditions cure asthma and other respiratory illnesses.
