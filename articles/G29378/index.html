Tasty hedgehogs made from pears and grapes

Thanks to the master class presented here, each of you will be able to perform such a vitamin composition. Read the description, get the necessary delicacies from the refrigerator and plunge into the sweet creative process.



Preparation of materials for the performance of the composition "Hedgehogs" from pears and grapes

First of all, we select fruits and berries. Cook pears solid, oblong. Fruits from bumps and scratches should not be present on the fruit. In grapes, give preference to dense fleshy large berries.

The stern of vitamin delicacies, for the performance of the figurine you will also need cloves or raisins, currant berries. All elements of the composition will be attached with the help of toothpicks.



Before you start making crafts "Hedgehog" from pears and grapes, take care of the availability of necessary utensils and tools. We will need a large flat dish for setting figures, a cutting board and a knife. Still prepare a pair of clean gloves and a napkin. When the preparations are finished, you can begin to make a delicious crafts "Hedgehogs" from pears and grapes.

Description of the process of performing the vitamin composition

On the pear peel, make an incision in a circle, stepping back from the narrow edge of the fruit 3-4 centimeters. Next, with a knife, gently remove the upper shell of the fetus. Thus, you formed a muzzle to a fruit animal. To make the pear hedgehog crafts stable, cut the peel along the entire length of the fruit from below.

Place the item on the dish. Next, perform the "needles". Pry each grape in the middle with a toothpick. Insert the blanks into the fruit over the entire surface of the back. First, fill the center of the fetus with needles, then the sides to the very bottom. Make a hedgehog eyes from cloves or raisins. Use a currant or dark grape berry as a nose. Hook strawberries or raspberries on the back. Similarly, execute the second hedgehog. Place them on a platter. If you wish, you can cook a whole family of vitamin figures from smaller fruits.
