Openwork lambrequin - a modern type of window drapery (photo). How to make an openwork lambrequin?

Openwork patterns have long been considered an elegant decoration. Thanks to them, the product acquires an elegant and original appearance. This design is found in clothing, furniture decoration, as well as in interiors. The last option over the years does not go out of style. Carved openwork lambrequin is a confirmation of this. These models appeared relatively recently, but quickly came to taste and fell in love with people.





Probably no one will argue that the windows in the room occupy one of the main places in the interior. Therefore, it is extremely important to choose the appropriate drapery for them. Tulle, curtains, cornice make up a single composition. However, simple models, as a rule, do not carry a semantic load, they simply perform a functional role, and in order to give them also a decorative one, you can use an openwork lambrequin (photos of the options are presented in the article).

Bando: familiarization with tough lambrequins

Bando - a certain type of hard lambrequins, which is made by special technology. As a rule, they are given a variety of styles that are filled with intricate, winding and smooth lines.

The basis of such a product is a rigid frame that retains a given shape. Curtains with openwork lambrequin fit perfectly into any interior: classic, Provence, Renaissance, country, etc. Also, with the help of a bando, you can play several effects. For example, a fixed curtain rod with drapery under the ceiling will visually increase the height of the room, and a lambrequin wider than the window opening will give the room additional volume.

The manufacturing technology of such a product is quite simple. An openwork pattern is drawn on a rigid basis, cut out, after which the fabric is glued and decorated with braid. In the article, we will conduct a master class on the manufacture of openwork lambrequins.





What will be needed in the process of work

First of all, in order to make an openwork lambrequin, you need a base (bando). It is of several types: hot-melt and self-adhesive. Next, you need to choose the main type of fabric. Material must be:

  • tight enough;
  • loose;
  • keeping shape, i.e. not stretching.

The next stage of preparation is the choice of lining. As a rule, currently these types of fabrics in stores are presented in a wide assortment.

The purchase of adhesive tape is necessary, since it will be responsible for the quality of fastening the fabric with the base.

And the finishing touch is decor. To do this, use braid or braided cord. Its color should be contrasted with the underlying fabric. This will allow emphasizing the delicacy of the ornament.





When making a carved lambrequin yourself, you must have an overlock and a sewing machine.

Three rules for a successful choice

In order for the openwork lambrequin to harmoniously fit into the overall interior, it is necessary to adhere to the basic rules.

  1. Product form. Here the determining factor is the size of the room and its style. It must be remembered that too intricate and complex ornament can significantly narrow the space, therefore it is recommended to use simple forms on small areas, but on large ones - to give free rein to imagination.
  2. Color spectrum. The palette of colors used in the interior is one of its most important components. Thanks to the game of shades, you can achieve maximum harmony. A win-win solution - the lambrequin of the dominant color. This will complement the overall composition, however, you do not need to try to choose the tone in tone, just stop the choice on the combined shades. Also a good option is contrast solutions. For example, brown curtains - a golden lambrequin. However, there is one caveat: these colors should be duplicated in the interior.
  3. The optimal size. The overall impression of the room will depend on the width and length of the pelmet. If it is very narrow, then the size of the drapery should be from one wall to another, and vice versa, in large rooms, the dimensions of the window side slopes are taken as the basis.




What to pay special attention to when manufacturing

  1. Before sewing, you must take accurate measurements from the window. The length of the lambrequins directly depends on the size of the cornice.
  2. If it is necessary to make a sufficiently wide base, then it is preferable to choose a doubler, as the width of the bardo-pro does not exceed 45 cm.
  3. The pattern is made taking into account all taken measurements, as well as rounding, angles and features of the ornament.
  4. Before cutting openwork lambrequin, it is necessary to make a trial version to assess the quality of the ornament. And only after that you can transfer it directly to the base.




Manufacturing technology

Making the basis for a hard lambrequin is already half the battle. Now you can start decorating it.

  1. In order for the openwork lambrequin to have a corresponding appearance, it is necessary to stick fabric material on the workpiece. First, the front side is treated. The gluing process itself takes place using an iron in two steps: the first - with steam, the second - without.
  2. Wrong side processing. The prepared base is laid on a lining and stitched along the edges with a zigzag seam. For the fortress, you can use adhesive tape.
  3. Trim excess tissue.
  4. Stage decoration. Fringes or braid are ideal for this. It is glued with hot glue.




How to make an openwork lambrequin: the subtleties of work

  • Be sure to leave 1-2 cm to the hem.
  • When using a slanting inlay, an allowance is not needed.
  • The upper edge of the lambrequin is treated with Velcro, with which the product will be attached to the cornice. The tape is sewn 5 mm lower from the fold.

The master class on the manufacture of carved openwork lambrequin is over. Good luck to you.
