Economy class housing "Swedish settlement" in Samara

In the Russian modern construction of apartment buildings, real estate objects included in the complex of buildings have become very common. The advantages of such complexes are, of course, a good profit for investors and developers, as more apartments are for sale. And buyers can choose an apartment with any layout, number of storeys. Many people choose the second or third floor as the best option. These complexes of apartment buildings include the residential complex "Swedish settlement". It belongs to economy class housing, which means that buyers can purchase it with average incomes.

Characteristics of houses

"Swedish settlement" is a residential complex in Samara, it includes ten four-story houses. They are connected to all communications. There is water supply, drainage, gas supply, electricity. The first two houses have three sections offset, which provides a beautiful and unusual architectural design. In the houses of the "Swedish settlement" in Samara, the layout of the apartments is different: there are one-room and two-room apartments with different meters. In one-room apartments, it varies from 31 to 41 m 2 , in two-room apartments - from 54 to 60 m 2 . The remaining seven houses were built in a traditional style. They have one-room, two-room and three-room apartments. In three-room apartments, the area is from 73 to 74 m 2 . The material used for the walls of houses is silicate brick. In addition, they are sheathed with Rockwool stove insulation.

This insulation helped to achieve not only thermal insulation, but also sound insulation, which is considered a big plus in the construction of the Swedish Sloboda complex in Samara. The roof was designed with a gable roof, which reduces leakage and snow accumulation.

These houses have a fine finish: painted walls and laid linoleum. The plus of the fine finish is that you can immediately populate and live. Plumbing is also available, as well as individual heating. This allows you to adjust the temperature of the room itself, hot water supply. There are also meters for water supply, gas supply and electricity.

Property Location

The residential complex "Swedish Sloboda" is located in an ecologically clean area in the city of Samara, not far from the Moscow highway (only 24 km along the street Red Plowman). The territory belongs to the Krasnoglinsky district, a large shopping and entertainment center "Mega" was built nearby.

There are also playgrounds, equipped driveways and parking. Some windows overlook the forest.

Construction took place in turns. The 1st stage was built and commissioned in 2014, and the Swedish settlement in Samara (2nd stage) - in the second half of 2016.

Object lending

The price per 1 square meter is 37,500 rubles. Mortgage lending is provided by Sberbank and Pervobank. You can also purchase an apartment in installments.

Developer

A little bit about the company developer Megastroy LLC.

They carry out their work on the construction of houses since the beginning of 2002. The very first house that the company built was commissioned in Samara at the intersection of Artsybushevskaya and Vilonovskaya streets. In order to free up the site for the construction of their own skyscraper, they resettled the tenants of five dilapidated buildings, giving them seventeen apartments in the property.

The house consists of 4 sections with different number of storeys (from 9 to 14 floors). Architects of this building are the best in Russia - these are Morgun Alexey Grigoryevich and Morgun Dmitry Alekseevich. This building has entered the top ten best buildings in the metropolis of Samara. The construction of the next building, namely an apartment building, the Megastroy company began in 2004. This house was also built on the site of dilapidated buildings, and three families found apartments in a new high-rise building. The house was built with a variable number of storeys.

At the moment, the company Profit LLC, which is part of the Megastroy Corporation, is constructing the Swedish Sloboda residential complex in Samara.
