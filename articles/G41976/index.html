Valuable Soviet coins

In the USSR, coin minting was not established in the 20th century; therefore, many banknotes are of great value to collectors. In addition, due to the instability of the political system, monetary units, if issued, are in limited quantities. Only rare specimens have survived to this day, which every numismatist wants to get.





To date, the most expensive ancient coins of the USSR are 50 kopecks in 1929, issued in a trial run, as well as 20 kopecks in 1934. According to the data, fifty kopecks was preserved only in a private collection, so its cost is at least 10 million rubles. A 20-kopeck banknote at auctions can be purchased for 100 thousand rubles. The value of this coin is due to the fact that there are only 15 copies left, one of which is located in the Hermitage.
