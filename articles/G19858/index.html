The first mention of knitting is dated by scientists around 3000 BC. e. The samples that have survived to this day are unique in their essence - archaeologists discovered items in Egyptian tombs that resemble modern knitwear in the structure of the canvas.

Getting started on knitting

350 g of yarn, as well as 3,5 knitting needles, are required for work. The product is knitted with a classic 2 x 2 elastic band pattern. This is an elastic band whose rapport (rapport is a repetition of a pattern) is 4 loops. Accordingly, you need to dial an even number of loops, which is divided into 4, + 2 edge. The pattern is easy to knit - it is necessary to alternate 2 front loops, 2 wrong loops on the front side, and knit on the wrong side as the loops look (if the last loops are 2 front loops, then they will turn out 2 wrong ones on the wrong side, and so on).

At the beginning of the work, the number of loops is equal to the waist circumference. After checking the density of knitting, it is necessary to find out the consumption of yarn and take into account the density of the fabric when knitting the product. It is worth considering that owners of wide hips will need to make an increase at the hip level several times, and then - several times - decrease. This is necessary so that the skirt fits well both on the waist and on the hips.

Jacquard technique

A variety of patterns for skirts with knitting needles allow you to choose a pattern to your taste. An interesting option that requires only a little practice is a skirt with a jacquard knitting pattern.

Traditionally, jacquard is a Norwegian pattern made in two or more colors using simple geometric shapes. It fits, most often, along the front surface, using a large number of threads of different colors. Now traditional bicolor has long been abandoned, so patterns in the jacquard technique are obtained not in monochrome, but in bright and variegated.

The jacquard can be tied out with a pull of threads on the wrong side of the fabric, while the pattern is read only on the front side. There is also a double sided jacquard option. In this case, the fabric is 2 times denser, since a non-working thread, and if there are not 2, but more, then all of them, is laid in the middle of the loop and picked up when knitting a loop. In the second case, the master must take this feature into account when acquiring yarn - the constant pulling of the thread makes it involved in knitting in almost the entire fabric and greatly increases not only the density of work, but also the consumption of threads. In this situation, yarn must be purchased with a margin.

Most often, knitting uses motifs containing images of deer, stars, snowflakes. Combinations of patterns among themselves allow you to create whole paintings in this technique, with a clearly traced motive or a whole story. Especially popular are sweaters and hats made by Scandinavian patterns.

Jacquard patterns for knitting a skirt with knitting needles contain large and small elements that can alternate around a circle or vertically.

The most exciting thing about working with jacquard is the ability to combine different patterns and techniques for knitting a skirt. You can knit braids with needles and supplement them with a jacquard pattern, your own pattern will also decorate the product well, which can be created using simple programs for creating patterns for crocheting embroidery.
