Hotel "Baltic"

A photo of this hotel proves once again that it is the largest in the Northern capital. Its majestic ten-story building was built in 1979 on Vasilievsky Island. Today, this area is considered one of the most environmentally friendly in the metropolis.

The hotel was built by the Swedish company Skanska. The authors of the project were architects N. Baranov, S. Evdokimov and V. Kovaleva. Two decades later, the hotel was admitted to the ICCA - a member of the World Tourism Organization.

How to get there

For guests from the hotel to the center of St. Petersburg runs a free shuttle. Tourists sending an independent excursion to the sights of the Northern capital can return from the Moscow station by metro, taking the subway from Mayakovskaya station and driving to the Primorskaya stop. Then, transferring to bus number seven or minibus No. 162, drive to the hotel. Travel time is forty minutes.

Arriving in St. Petersburg, tourists who have booked a room at the Pribaltiyskaya Hotel can take a taxi or bus number 39 from Pulkovo Airport to the hotel, then transfer by metro and travel to the Nevsky Prospekt station.
