Consumer basket: composition and price for a month

Is it possible to live on 1000 rubles a week without changing the principles of healthy eating? Experts are sure that it is possible, even if there is no excess of fresh fruits and vegetables in your diet. The Government of the Russian Federation approved a consumer basket, which allows you to leave the prices for most essential products at a low level. What is included in the consumer basket? Half of it consists of food. The rest is spent on expenses for KU, clothes and transportation. The government used food consumption data and WHO data to compile the list. For example, for a whole year an adult in Russia eats about 200 kilograms of potatoes, 120 kg of other vegetables and fruits, 60 kilograms of meat. Based on these data, the following product basket was compiled:

  • bread;
  • sugar and sweets;
  • fish and meat;
  • Fruits and vegetables;
  • potatoes;
  • margarine;
  • salt, tea, spices;
  • eggs
  • dairy.

According to Federal Law No. 227, this kit is enough for the full development and functioning of the human body. The Decree of the Government of Russia determined the average figures of products per year and divided them for each month. And so it turned out a grocery basket, whose cost at the moment is an average of 6500 rubles per month. At the moment, it contains the most flour products, such as pasta, bread and various sweet products. The reason for this is largely how much pasta and bread cost. They can be purchased at any store and they are very satisfying. Their price ranges from 20 to 50 rubles, so people who want to live 1000 rubles a week often include them in their diet. However, the nutritional value of such products is very low, so nutritionists are seriously concerned about the health of people who consume mainly flour.

How much do utilities cost?

Like food prices, apartment service tax continues to rise. Housing and utilities make up the majority of the cost of living. How much do utilities cost? Their cost depends on several factors: the number of officially registered people and the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe apartment. The location of the apartment and regional laws can also affect this amount. On average, the cost of a receipt for a cash register is 5 thousand rubles. For studio apartments, this amount may be less - only 3 thousand rubles. But if the apartment is three- or four-room, and the whole family lives in it, then the expenses will be higher, and receipts will come already in the amount of 6-7 thousand rubles. Therefore, the question often arises of how to reduce the tariff for utilities. You cannot directly affect their cost, but it is quite possible to reduce electricity consumption:

  • Turn off the lights and electrical appliances while you are not using them. The advice is banal, but still effective.
  • Replace the old wiring with a new one.
  • Also, get rid of old household appliances that are 10 years old or more - as a rule, they have a lower class of energy consumption.
  • View discount information. For example, some companies give a discount on the use of electricity at night.
  • Make compensation for housing services. Poor, large families and people with disabilities have the right to do so. The state compensates from 20 to 70% of the amount of utility bills.
  • Set the dishwasher. Oddly enough, this will save you money, because with this device you can reduce water use by almost half.
  • Take a shower, not a bath.
  • Close the taps. Even a small leak can seriously affect the account at the end of the month.
  • Quality windows and doors can reduce heat leakage and lower heating costs.
  • If possible, use gas, not electricity. Electric stoves are much more expensive than gas stoves, although the latter are less safe.
