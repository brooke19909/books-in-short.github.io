History and description of the Lipovaya Gora area in Yaroslavl

In the southern part of Yaroslavl there is the Lipovaya Gora area. Geographically, it belongs to the Frunze district. The article will consider basic information about the area, the history of its name and origin, as well as the current state and cost of housing.

origin of name

According to some reports, a large park with linden trees grew in the territory of the modern district.

District History

At the beginning of the 20th century, the village of Lipova Gora was formed on this site. She was inhabited by the workers of the plant, which was supposed to produce airplanes. However, the revolution did not allow the construction of the plant to end. Under Soviet rule, the plant was completed and began to produce parts for railway transport. The village became part of the city of Yaroslavl in 1933.

The Lipovaya Gora district (Yaroslavl) is located between the radio plant and the railway in the direction of Nerekhta.

The distance from Yaroslavl Central Station to Lipovaya Gora is 12 km; they can be overcome by train in an average of 22 minutes.

In the Lipovaya Gora area there is the Radiy Culture Palace, where a large number of creative groups for children are organized, various entertaining and creative events, concerts, and theater performances are held.

There is also a park, which, unfortunately, is in disrepair.

The cost of housing in the Lipovaya Gora area is lower than in the center of Yaroslavl. This is due to the fact that there are practically no new buildings, and the territory is located quite far from the city center. So, a two-room apartment can be bought for one and a half million rubles. An apartment of 60 square meters can be purchased for 2.5 million rubles.
