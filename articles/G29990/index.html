Rollback to factory settings

In almost 100% of cases, the user himself is to blame for the breakdown of the smartphone. Unsuccessfully installed applications, virus programs, unsuccessful firmware - and you have to figure out how to restore the firmware on Android.

If this happens, you should perform a Hard reset of the gadget. Almost all phones have the ability to quickly call a special menu - Recovery, with which the settings are reset. Usually, these are simultaneous keystrokes of the lock and volume keys (each smartphone model has its own combinations).

You need to do a reset in the following sequence:

  • If the gadget is functioning, then you should transfer all the necessary data to a flash drive or PC.
  • Turn off the smartphone and press the volume and lock buttons at the same time. A black window should appear on the screen with the green Android logo in the center.

How to restore contacts

Perhaps the most important concern of the owners of mobile devices is the search for ways to restore contacts on Android after firmware.

In most situations, this cannot be done if a backup has not been made. However, with the help of some system tools and utilities, you can still recover what was lost.

  • Another way - if all the contacts were transferred at one time to the SIM card. There, in the book settings, you can import contacts from SIM to the phone.
  • Finally, there is a chance to restore contacts using third-party utilities, for example, Android Data Recovery or Super BackUp Pro. But here the chance is far from 100%, although some contacts can still be pulled out of nonexistence.
