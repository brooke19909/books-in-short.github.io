DVB-T2 - what is it? DVB-T2 set-top box. Tuner DVB-T2

DVB-T2 is the standard for digital television. And the prefix T2 means that this is the second generation from the general group. It was created based on the present generation standards in order to increase the overall performance of television networks by 50%. And at the same time their quality and reliability. That this is DVB-T2 will be discussed in this article.

Description

This standard is very different from previous versions. This means that receivers of older versions do not support it. DVB-T2 is characterized by QPSK, 16 QAM, 64 QAM, and 256 QAM modulations. Depending on the application of one or another, the maximum speed of the digital stream can vary from 7 to 50 megabits per second.





The system structure is based on the transmission of a transport stream of the MPEG-TS type. At the same time, several streams can be transmitted simultaneously through the DVB-T2 standard. For this, a special data preprocessing system was applied.

Development

At the dawn of television, the most popular standards were NTSC, Pal, and SECAM. They were responsible for color coding. During the development of television systems, some of them died out, and some live and are still in use. With the global transition of television to digital, these standards are gradually disappearing into oblivion.

The main reason for switching to digital television was the ability to compress data using the MPEG algorithm, thereby significantly increasing the characteristics and quality of the transmitted signal.





Today in the world there are several generally accepted standards specific to each region. DVB and derivatives are used in Europe, ATSC is used in America, ISDB and DTMB, respectively, in Japan and China.

Key Features of Digital DVB-T2

These include:

  • multi-channel multiplexing, that is, combining several channels into one digital packet;

  • display in standard definition, high definition and ultra high definition;

  • 3D television display;

  • display video on demand;

  • TV guide

  • Teletext

  • Dolby Digital sound

  • date and time synchronization;

  • broadband internet access.

DVB-T2 format signal receiving system

This type of signal can be received by a special broadcast antenna, which must be connected to special receivers. They can be used as televisions with built-in modules, or individual DVB-T2 consoles or tuners. They, as a rule, need to be bought separately.

Digital tuner DVB-T2. What does this mean?

When switching to digital television, many users stop at the choice of technical devices for displaying it. The DVB-T2 module may or may not be present on the TV. When buying, you should pay attention to this in modern realities. Of course, most innovative TV models already have a built-in DVB-T2 module. What does this give in practice? This means that when broadcasting digital terrestrial television, the user does not need to buy additional devices such as receivers or tuners.

What gives the transition to DVB-T2

Firstly, it is worth noting the excellent signal quality. Unlike analog, digital television is either there or not. An analog signal may have a smooth transition from a good picture to a bad one.

You can use a much larger number of channels in the same frequency resource. In the so-called package, you can now place several programs at the same time, from 6 to 18.

A distinctive feature of the DVB-T2 signal is the fact that it is not susceptible to noise. That is, in areas where the analog signal was difficult to receive, DVB-T2 will work much more efficiently.





Now you can broadcast the channel in high definition HDTV. The quality of such a picture is really amazing.

The DVB-T2 standard has another interesting feature. It can be taken in motion. That is, receivers, tuners and TVs with DVB-T2 modules can now be installed on vehicles and receive television channels.

Conclusion

So what is this DVB-T2? This is a new innovative standard, which greatly increases the ability to transmit data on television channels. High definition, acceptable levels of signal reception, higher speed, less equipment for reception and many other useful nuances. With the advent of DVB-T2, a new era of television begins. It remains only to wait for the complete transition of all broadcasting companies, as well as users to this single standard.

It is worth noting that in Russia the development of the DVB-T2 standard is proceeding at a significant pace. Many cable providers are constantly expanding the list of channels in HD. Television and radio companies are gradually moving behind them. Now even being in the outback of the Russian Federation, you can use new technologies to watch television. In extreme cases, you can use a satellite dish with support for DVB-T2.
