You are the owner of the iPhone. Suppose you decide to change your phone. At this moment, a very logical question visits you: "How to copy contacts to SIM on an iPhone?" Contact Sync in iCloud

So, the easiest and most convenient way to copy contacts on an iPhone (we can‚Äôt figure out how to copy them to a SIM card) is to let the phone synchronize regularly with iCloud. To do this, check the iCloud settings in your iPhone or configure it if it has not been done before. Go to "Settings", then iCloud and make sure that the service is turned on, and the switch next to the word "Contacts" is in the on position. Now every day, when your smartphone is connected to the mains (that is, it is charging) and is on the Wi-Fi network, it will synchronize with iCloud - a backup copy of the device will be added to your account on the Apple server containing, among other information , information from the address book. Access to the "cloud" can be obtained by going to the official website. To download information to a new phone, you need to restore data from iCloud the first time you turn on by logging in with your Apple ID. Of course, you can download all the information saved in this way only to smartphones with iOS, so it is not universal compared to other features, like copying contacts on an iPhone. Transfer contacts from iCloud

First you need to create a backup of your device in the Apple cloud service. How to do this has been described above. Having visited the iCloud website, select the "Contacts" item there. Having selected all the contacts by pressing the hot keys Ctrl + A (Cmd + A for Mac), click the settings button in the lower left corner and select Export vCard. A file will be created containing all the information about your contacts in the iPhone with the exception of photos. Unfortunately, they cannot be transferred. Now go to your Google account, find Gmail and there "Contacts". Click "Advanced" - "Import ..." and select the file created in iCloud.
