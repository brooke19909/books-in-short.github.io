"Lazy" steam

So, the mop, in the common people "lazy", is nothing more than a banal T-shaped stick, on which they put on a rag designed to mop the floor. The floor is not completely washed with boiling water, of course, but with something close to this approximate.



So what is a steam mop? The feedback and expert opinion is clear - an environmentally friendly and practical device for cleaning flat surfaces.

Operating principle

In a small closed tank, placed on the handle of the device, water boils, turning (according to the laws of physics) into hot steam. It is in this condition that it is fed to a mop rag. As a result, you wash the floor without the use of chemicals, and get an excellent result, since hot water (even if its condition at that time is very conditional) has the ability to dissolve fats and kill germs. The properties of hot steam are fantastic. These are exactly the characteristics of a steam mop.

Customer reviews on this subject, however, are not so rosy. Is it weird? Perhaps, and yet it is. And the reason is banal. Those who are engaged in the promotion of new products on the market, slightly overdid the advertising PR campaign. As a result, consumers are misled on many points.






Advertising myth number 1

For example, leaflets claim the ability of a device to collect spilled liquid. However, to cope with a similar problem at the proper level can only an ordinary rag. After all, the devices do not have a reservoir for collecting water. Although, in fairness, I must say that steam vacuum cleaners have already appeared in the product lines of some large household appliance companies that can simultaneously remove dirt and collect the remaining liquid. In particular, Karcher offers for sale the Karcher SV 1902 model - this is a washing vacuum cleaner and a steam mop. Reviews of consumers who have tested this unit in operation are excellent: only cumbersomeness can be noted among the shortcomings.

Myth number 2

The next myth created by advertisers is the ability to collect dirt, wool and other garbage. There is some truth in this statement. Running on any surface with a damp cloth, we can collect garbage, but not all, but partially. For example, we wipe the kitchen table after dinner with a damp cloth, but we sprinkle the crumbs that have gathered around it on our hands. The situation is also with the device in question. If you want to mop the floor to a shine, then you need to collect dirt and small crumbs in advance. Then the result will be excellent. But after the preliminary garbage collection, an ordinary mop will brilliantly cope with this task.

Myth number 3

Exactly the same situation is with regard to spots that the unit, according to advertisers, is able to clean. If something is spilled on the carpet that cannot stain, but only requires cleaning with a damp cloth (kefir, soup, etc.), then it will certainly help you. But to wipe off stains from coloring liquids (tea, coffee, wine, etc.) will be very difficult.



So where are we going? In principle, to what has been known for a long time: to believe is good, but to think is better. Before trusting sellers, think about what you learned at school. The banal knowledge of physics and chemistry will help you to separate the grain from the chaff.

Claims
