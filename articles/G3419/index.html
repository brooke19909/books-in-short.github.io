Turkey, Kemer, Beldibi

This resort is located west of Antalya, about halfway to the city of Kemer. Administratively Beldibi belongs to Lycia. The village is a narrow strip of human housing, sandwiched between the Mediterranean Sea and the Taurus Mountains, which are very close to the coast in this place. Beldibi History

In the middle of the last century, archaeological research was carried out in the village, during which it was discovered that people lived here in the first century BC. What the locals were doing and what was the name of their village was completely unclear. The village took its present name from the small river Beldibi. Its history until the 1980s is unremarkable. The only thing that kept the inhabitants of Beldibi (Turkey) afloat was the cultivation and sale of citrus fruits. You can still see the remains of rich lemon and orange groves, which are preserved in some places on the territory of the hotels. Since the eighties, hotels have been built here - first for the wealthy residents of the capital. Ten years later, when Turkey secured the image of a country of beach holidays, top hotels for foreign tourists appeared in the village. Archaeological canned pits have remained and bear the romantic name "Caves", but the inexperienced eye can see little in them.
