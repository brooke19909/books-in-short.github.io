Yamoussoukro





This city became the capital for a relatively long time - in 1983, but people still do not recognize it as the main one in the country. The most interesting architectural monument located here is Notre Dame de la Pax, created in the 60s of the last century. This is a huge Christian temple, built on the model of St. Peter's Basilica, located in the Vatican. On the facade of the church you can see more than thirty wonderful stained glass windows. This is what a wonderful attraction the Ivory Coast is famous for. The capital of this state is a real magnet for lovers of exquisite beauty.

Abidjan

This city is often referred to as "Paris, located in West Africa." There is a huge number of parks (the most beautiful of them, of course, Le Plato), interesting places, as well as a large craft market. 500 years ago, Abidjan was an unremarkable provincial town, but his fate changed for the better when a channel called Vridi was opened. He allowed to go directly from the lagoon to the sea. Thus, Abidjan became a very significant port and rapidly increased in size, and the population soon began to reach 3,000,000.

The city center and the luxurious sleeping area called Kokordi are notable for the many architectural monuments left after the colonialists from France. The most interesting place is the Ivory Hotel, built in imperial style. This hotel is the most famous in western Africa. Guests here can indulge in a variety of entertainments: ride on an indoor ice rink with artificial ice, watch movies in a movie theater, play in a casino, swim in the pool, or go bowling. Ivory also houses the most prestigious art store in Abidjan. Walking near this hotel, you can see the temple of Saint Paul. It was built by architects from Italy, and in the beauty of its appearance it is comparable with the most famous churches in the world. The consecration of the throne took place in 1985.
