How to buy a silver bar in Sberbank of Russia

Investment in bullion is one way of investing temporarily surplus funds. Moreover, banking metals are more valued. Read more about how and on what conditions you can buy a silver bar at Sberbank of Russia, read on.

Benefits

Investors who want to save their money can buy a bar of silver. Such an investment is characterized by a high level of liquidity and profitability, but only in the long term. Allocate standard and measured silver ingots. They are made by casting, stamping or electrolysis.





Before investing, you need to consider the following specifics of investments:

  1. A silver ingot that complies with state standards should contain metal without impurities. The highest quality metals are marked Good Delivery.
  2. Investing in precious metals helps protect savings from depreciation as a result of inflation.
  3. A purchase and sale transaction can be concluded in any country in the world. The investor is not dependent on the results of the bank, because precious metals are valued everywhere.

Acquisition

The process of buying precious metals is simple and innovative approach.

Documents are executed in the presence of the client. Absentee registration of the transaction is not allowed. For paperwork, the client only needs a passport.

The sale procedure includes the mandatory weighing of metal on the scales. In this case, the buyer should see the result with his own eyes. Weight determination is carried out with an accuracy of 0.1 grams (silver) and 0.01 grams (gold).

You can only purchase a measured bullion through Sberbank. Silver with obvious traces of contamination is not allowed for transactions. It must first be cleaned.

The metal purchase and sale agreement must be supported by the manufacturer's quality certificate. It is also important to compare the obtained weighing data with the information entered in the certificate.





The entire operation is carried out through the cashier. The settlement documents clearly record the weight, number, sample and number of ingots. The receipt also indicates the date of the transaction and the amount of the transaction. A metal purchase and sale transaction is executed by an acceptance certificate.

Metal demand

The most popular product on the market is a silver ingot weighing 100 grams. The larger the piece of metal, the lower the price of one gram. Accordingly, the most expensive is an ingot of 1 gram, and the cheapest is considered a kilogram ingot. The price begins to decline from the mark of 250 grams. But such an ingot is inconvenient to operate in view of the large difference between the purchase and sale prices. Another important point: when buying metal weighing more than 100 grams, the bank must identify the customer. Small bars can be purchased without identification as a gift to their relatives.

Demand is higher, the lower the weight. It is usually 10%. That is, if immediately after the purchase there is a need to sell the ingot, the market price will already be reduced by 10%. And if you add to this the mandatory VAT, the amount of expenses will increase even more.





The price of a silver bar also depends on how it is made. Stamped metal costs more. It is better to sell a bullion of silver to the same bank in which it was bought. The "native" metal is usually set at a high price, and expertise required when purchasing third-party metal is not carried out. And the question is not even the cost of the service, but the time spent on its implementation.

Storage

Bank ingots (especially with a low weight) must be packed in a continuous non-opening plastic or polyethylene fully or partially transparent case. Sometimes there are even gift wraps of various types. It is also important to check with the bank whether the type of case affects the repurchase of metal at any lending institution. The fact is that some banks redeem bullion only in a certain package or in its original case.

In the same way that damage to an ingot affects its price, so damage to a case affects its liquidity. According to bankers, the ingot loses its appeal. A credit institution may refuse to buy it at all or reduce the price to 20% of the market. If the bank buys such an ingot, in any case, he will need to find an opportunity to sell it. Some credit institutions generally in such cases offer to buy metal at the price of scrap. In the best case, the client will be offered to conduct an examination at his own expense, and only after that a price will be formed.





Metal alternative

Less common in the metal banking market are palladium and platinum. You can buy them far from every bank. The market price is about two times higher than for gold, and the spread exceeds 50%. The payback period for such investments is at least 5 years.

Another metal alternative on the market are precious coins. Their main advantage is collectible value. Silver and gold coins of small weight with a unique design are appreciated in the metal market. They are much better protected from fraud, so there are practically no fakes on the market. The cost of coins depends on the face value and coinage. In their manufacture, jewelry stones are sometimes used in addition to metal. You can purchase these products not only in a commercial bank, but also in the Central Bank.





Investing in coins is a privilege for collectors. To make money on operations, you have to wait more than three years. Tangible profits from the sale of coins can be obtained in 5-10 years after purchase. In the first two years, the bank buys coins at a discount from the sale price. Another important point: when selling coins, it is necessary to provide a contract of sale.
