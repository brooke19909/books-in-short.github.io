Summer blouse (crochet) for women with a pattern of small motifs

This model can be made of any color of thread, but the original version is white. Its feature is that it is made in the technique of continuous knitting. In addition, despite a fairly large number of motives and apparent laboriousness, the product knits quite easily.





The motive itself is not difficult. Like all schemes (hook) for beginners, its scheme may not be fully understood.

The first row is always a ring of air loops. The work is carried out with a thin thread, so the hook should be no more than number 1.5. In some cases, it is considered preferable to use 0.9. Keep in mind that working with such a fine thread is a painstaking and long task. If the hook for some reason hardly enters the loops, then either the knitting density is very tight, or the hook is larger than it should be to work with such a thread.

An interesting effect is achieved through the use of melange yarns. They allow you to experiment with color and create truly unique things.

Description of motive





When the chain is dialed and closed in a ring, the second row is knitted. It consists of a repeating report. This is a double crochet and two stitches. A total of 7 repetitions are done. The number is odd, since the last element is always the lifting loops, which in our case are always three due to knitting the double crochets.

The third row requires special attention and concentration. So after lifting three columns with two crochets are knitted, and then 11 air loops, and behind them are four columns with two crochets. Knitted air loops during assembly of the product will form a connection with other motives. After four columns with two yarn, we knit three air loops, which will also participate in the connection of motifs. Repeat four columns with two crochets and 11 air loops. We tie the motive in a circle. The result is a total of eight repetitions of 4 columns and 4 arches of loops for connecting.

Product assembly features

This summer blouse crocheted for women (with the scheme) is going directly during work. Knitting the second motive, attach it in the process to the first with the help of air loops. Do not use a needle - everything is crocheted here. Remember that it is easier to use a ready-made pattern, laying out connected motives on it. Also, pay attention not to the fact that the sleeves of the model are initially made as separate parts, and then they are sewn in.

Do not be afraid that all the contours are square. This is normal and will not be noticeable. An additional recommendation is to slightly steam off each motive and stretch the finished canvas, rather than single elements.
