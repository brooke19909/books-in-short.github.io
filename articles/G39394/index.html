Name history

This quiet, but at the same time majestic and ancient city is located in the north-west of the Russian Federation. It was founded more than 450 years ago and was called in a completely different way, not Syktyvkar. What area was formed by him at that time, also could not be understood by its name. Since he was called Ust-Sysol, and he was the center of the Veliky Ustyug region.





In the nineteenth century, local residents decided to translate this name into their own dialect and began to call this settlement Syktyvdin, where "Syktyv" in the native language of the Komi peoples means the name of the river on which the city is located, and "din" - its mouth. Thus, it was called for another hundred and fifty years, until in 1931 it was renamed Syktyvkar. What area is adjacent to this administrative center? Until 1936, the subject of the Russian Federation Komi was an autonomous region and only in 1992 it was transformed into a republic.

The population of this region mainly works at enterprises specializing in the woodworking and pulp and paper industries.
