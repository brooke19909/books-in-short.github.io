Webcam: what is it and what is it for?

A webcam is a digital camera connected to a computer. The device is designed for scanning, processing and transmitting images via the Internet in real time. Most often used for online broadcasts and video calls.



Historic Coffee Stream

The first webcam is the invention of the Cambridge geniuses Quentin Stafford-Fraser and Paul Jardetski. The device appeared in 1991 thanks to the love of university staff for coffee. At that time, there was only one coffee pot in the Trojan Room for the entire computer development department. Often, scientists had a long way to go for a cup of this wonderful drink for nothing. So that everyone can see if the coffee is ready without getting up from the workplace, a web-camera was created.

Modern webcam device

The main part in the webcam is the image sensor, or CCD-matrix. It is a semiconductor chip consisting of millions of tiny photosensitive squares arranged in a grid. These squares are called pixels. Basic webcams use relatively small sensors, only a few hundred thousand pixels.

In addition to a CCD or ROM matrix, the camera contains a lens, an analog-to-digital converter, also called a video capture card, a microprocessor and a compressor. Some IP camera models include a built-in web server and RAM.





The principle of the webcam

A standard webcam is a separate device connected to a computer. It acts as follows:

  1. Through the front lens, the camera captures the light and projects it onto an integrated circuit consisting of microscopic light detectors.
  2. The video capture card converts an analog image into a digital one, that is, into a bytecode of zeros and ones.
  3. Compression units compress the digital signal into MJPEG or MPEG formats.
  4. Further information is transmitted to a computer via a USB interface, and then to the Internet.

In the case of IP cameras, connecting to a computer is optional. The device has its own IP address, thanks to the built-in web server, and streaming is carried out directly to the network.

What is a webcam for?

The task of the webcam is to show objects that are out of sight and reach. With its help, you can remotely monitor people in real time. For example, if you are going to another city or the sea, the webcam will show the weather better than any weather forecaster.

90% of users purchase a gadget for video calls, chats and video conferencing over the Internet. To do this, a special software supporting VoIP must be installed on the computer: Skype, ooVoo or Viber. Instead of client programs, you can use the extensions for the Appear.in or Hangouts browser. But on video chats, the range of camera capabilities does not end there:

  1. Live broadcasts of what is happening in the most popular corners of the earth. Webcams in Sochi, New York and near Niagara Falls allow you to travel without getting up from your computer chair.
  2. Security companies and security services widely use this technique for monitoring objects.
  3. Video from a webcam can be recorded, edited, uploaded to the Internet, on forums, in a YouTube playlist, or on a blog. For these purposes, there are many programs, one of which is Windows Movie Maker or Camtasia Studio.
  4. Using web applications, create emails in video format.
  5. Conduct paid training webinars and earn money using the gadget.

  4. Use the face recognition program to enter the account without entering a password.
