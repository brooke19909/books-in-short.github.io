Alanya Attractions

Relaxing in the village of Konakli, be sure to visit Alanya. The city is very interesting for historical sights. His hallmark is the famous Kyzyl-Kul tower. At one time, it was built by order of the Sultan for defensive purposes. For several centuries the tower faithfully served the townspeople. But later she lost her direct purpose and is now a symbol of Alanya. Currently, an observation deck for guests of the city functions at the top of the tower, and a local museum is located inside the building.

No less interesting is the fortress of the city, which dates from the 13th century AD. It has survived quite well to the present day and has the status of a historical monument.

Not far from the center of Alanya is the valley of the mountain river Dim Chai. A dam was recently built here, as a result of which a reservoir appeared. Of course, swimming in it is forbidden, but here fish are bred. And on the coast built cafes and fish restaurants that cook dishes from the freshest catch. There are always many tourists and locals coming to picnics. Dim Tea attracts people primarily by the beauty of nature and the opportunity to relax.

It is worth noting that in Alanya there are not only historical monuments, but also numerous entertainments. One of the most popular places for family visits is the amusement park Sialanya. From the city it can be reached in just twenty minutes.

The park has many attractions for visitors of all ages. Be sure to visit the dolphinarium, which constantly hosts performances by marine life. The park is so huge that it is not possible to see it all in one day. But you can ride through its territory, thanks to the "slow river" attraction. In the middle of the park there is a huge pond, at the bottom of which you can dive and see its inhabitants. Such entertainment attracts a lot of daredevils.
