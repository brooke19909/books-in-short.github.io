Lithuania, attractions: Druskininkai and surroundings

Druskininkai is the largest balneological center in Lithuania with a unique microclimate. In the second half of the 19th - at the beginning of the 20th century, it was known as one of the most aristocratic resorts in the Baltic States, and a place where cultural life was in full swing throughout the tourist season. Today, the traditions of those times are reviving, and the sights of Druskininkai and the surrounding area make the rest on the banks of the Neman even more interesting.





Church

Talking about the sights of Druskininkai and its environs (see photo below), one cannot ignore the local church of the Virgin Mary Shkaplernaya (address: 10 Vilnius Alley). It was built from 1912 to 1930 according to the project of architect S. Schiller and is a magnificent building in the Neo-Gothic style, reminiscent of the church of St. Anne in Vilnius.

A park. A. Chesnulis

Three kilometers from the city of Druskininkai is the small village of Nauyasodes. There is the famous A. Cesnulis Park in Lithuania. One of the most interesting objects of this attraction is a large 4-story windmill, which is also a museum, where the most valuable wooden sculptures by Antanas Cesnulis are exhibited. A few dozen works of the master can be seen in the park directly in the open.

Orthodox Church

Interested in old sights? Druskininkai is the city where the temple of the Icon of the Mother of God is located (address: Liberty Boulevard, 2). It was built on the basis of a private house in 1865 with money and thanks to the efforts of the vice-governor of Grodno, Jacob Rozhnov. This wooden structure is painted white and is decorated with five graceful domes.

Schwenbre

If you go on vacation to the Druskininkai resort, sights and entertainment can be found in the surrounding area. In particular, not far from the border with Belarus (5 km from the city), the ethnographic village of Schwendure is located. This settlement was founded at the beginning of the 17th century, and the traditions of the Lithuanian people are carefully preserved there. Guests of Schwendubre are always eager to see the huge boulder, nicknamed the Devil's Stone. According to an old legend, Satan wanted to block the Neman with him, but dropped it because of his heavy weight.

One adventure park

At 200 meters from the water park is another place that is worth a visit after exploring other resort attractions. Druskininkai offers children and adults a wide variety of activities, including One Adventure Park. There you can stroll along the "air" routes, which are laid at a height of up to 16 m between the crowns of trees and are intended for vacationers of different ages.

Although most visitors to the park are children, fans of extreme entertainment will be interested in One Adventure Park. In particular, they are offered the attraction "Flight of Tarzan" through Nemunas, by participating in which you can get a large dose of adrenaline and experience unforgettable sensations.
