Sights of Krasnogorsk: description, addresses

The city of Krasnogorsk is located near Moscow, to the north-west beyond the MKAD, about 22 kilometers. Until the beginning of the century before last, a small number of villages were located on the site of today's Krasnogorsk. The oldest of them, Pavshino, has been known since the end of the 15th century.





A small digression into history

In the XIX century, large industrial enterprises appeared in these villages, most of which were associated with textiles. The Volokolamsk highway and the railway to Riga were built. The village of Ban'ki turns into a manufacturing village. In 1926, Podolsky Mechanical Plant was moved here. In the circumstances, the country's leadership is considering the possibility of creating the city of Optikogorsk on the site of villages. But the local population stubbornly did not want to take such a name, and instead of Optikogorsk, Krasnogorsk appeared, by the name of one of the villages - Krasnaya Gorka.

The proximity of the city to the capital is one of the ways to get away from the everyday hustle and bustle of the big city and visit Krasnogorsk at the weekend. Moreover, the sights of Krasnogorsk are of interest. They can be inspected with pleasure.

Transformer Concert Hall

It is also worth mentioning Crocus City Hall - the most famous multi-level concert hall, which was built in 2009 and named after Muslim Magomayev.

Crocus City Hall is equipped with the most advanced technical equipment and meets the highest international standards. It is able to accommodate more than 7000 spectators.

Crocus City Hall also hosts demonstrations of fashion collections, Nika film awards, and Ru.TV channel music awards.





What else to visit in Krasnogorsk?

Maybe the Memorial Museum of German Anti-Fascists, which has existed since 1985? This is an outstanding, unique museum, the history of which began in the years of the Second World War. During the battle in Krasnogorsk, the German military were captured, as well as a number of civilians who arrived because of curiosity or with the desire to help the fascist army.

The prisoners were placed in the small village of Brusky - then it was called a camp, and the prisoners - fascists. They worked in factories, built, and after some time their way of life became the same as that of many Soviet workers. In the camp, in addition to German prisoners, there were also representatives of other nationalities. These were citizens of countries that sided with the Wehrmacht. The overwhelming majority of these people, like the citizens of the USSR, wanted a peaceful life, and they had such an opportunity.

An anti-fascist school was created in the village to educate newcomers to the Russian language and to educate those life values ‚Äã‚Äãthat were adhered to in the Soviet Union. On the basis of the camp was created the famous council "Free Germany", which was one of the largest organizations of German anti-fascists. The council specialized in recruiting captives, propaganda on the fronts using loudspeakers, and issuing campaign booklets for Wehrmacht soldiers. The council had its own radio station of the same name, "Free Germany."





Exhibits

Household details of foreigners made up a complete interesting collection of the museum - household items, reconstruction of the rooms in which these people lived, and objects created by the hands of prisoners are exhibited here. These are very interesting and original crafts made in times quite difficult for people.

The estate Arkhangelsk, the city of Krasnogorsk

Manor "Arkhangelsk" - a kind of Versailles near Moscow, an amazing cultural monument, which majestically rises on the high coast of the old channel of the Moscow River. Unique picturesque landscapes delight the eyes of visitors and allow you to forget about the essentials. This is a museum full of interesting exhibits. Here you can see wonderful collections of paintings of the XVII-XIX centuries, as well as a collection of sculptures.

Here is the largest collection of rare books in Russia, including more than 16 thousand volumes. Also here are the Gonzaga Theater and the Apollon Grove. A pink fountain with a gazebo, a monument to Catherine the Great with an architectural frame, as well as an alley with statues leading to the Pushkin monument, and other well-known sights of Krasnogorsk can be seen walking along the estate and enjoying the cleanest air. There are beautiful parks. This is a large parterre, made in the style of the French regular park, subject to the laws of mathematics and symmetry. Children's Tale - Joy Zoo

In Krasnogorsk there is an amusement park and the Otrada zoo, in which for young guests there is a rope park, an equestrian club, a zoo, a playground with a sandbox, slides and carousels, an ice rink. The cafe offers a special menu.





Tours and stage performances can be held here, fishing, themed holidays, classes, special programs and horse-drawn carriage rides can be organized. The zoo is home to a large number of animals: Vietnamese mini-pigs, porcupines, raccoon stripes, sika deer, hedgehogs, squirrels, rabbits of various breeds, Nubian goats, ponies, mini-goats, llamas and noses.
