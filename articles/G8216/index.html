Why does digital television not work?

Probably, each person was faced with the failure of a particular technique, and in recent times, the situation when digital television does not work is relevant. A person turns on the TV to watch the long-awaited continuation of the series or his favorite program, but he receives only a blank screen. And digital television does not work every time for some reason that is not understood by anyone. Either this is not broadcast, or the signal does not pass, or prevention is on separate channels. Digital television also does not work in the event of a breakdown of the antenna or even the TV itself, or maybe the cable goes somewhere. The reasons when there is no television broadcasting are very, very many. This article will help with some.





Main reasons

If digital television does not work due to a lack of signal, this will be immediately understandable, because no channel will respond. So something happened to the antenna. Prevention can not take place at once on all channels, most often on one or in extreme cases - on several. The rest will work, and why digital television does not work - the question is not for such a situation. If the cable has gone somewhere, then the question is legitimate, because silence and a dark screen will be everywhere.

The first thing you can check it, everyone knows, even people far from technology. You need to disconnect and reconnect the cable. If it does not help, then contact specialists and ask them why digital television does not work today. If the user suspects the cause of the breakdown of the TV, there is no sense in independent actions. Only a service center or a private master who can accurately repair TVs will help.

Satellite TV antennas are very powerful, but even they sometimes refuse to transmit a signal, and a frustrated user spends a lot of time with the phone in his hands, asking the broadcasting company why digital television does not work today. Three options

Firstly, it is cable television, when the signal will be distributed directly through the television cable, and it is connected to a single TV. This is a very high-quality transmission, but it is far from accessible in every province, mainly in cities. And the monthly fee is quite high.

Secondly, it is satellite television, when the signal comes from near-earth orbit and is received by an individual antenna ("dish"). This can be done everywhere, and the sound and picture will be quality. The monthly fee is quite high, the equipment is also expensive. And from time to time, the question is nevertheless posed by the user: why did digital television stop working?

Thirdly, this is terrestrial television , when the signal is distributed by ground-based relay stations, and it is received by an individual antenna. There are few costs, but the signal level is much lower, the reception quality depends on everything in the world - here is the weather, and the distance from the relay tower, and the height of the antenna mast, and much, much more. And each user from time to time will spoil the mood from the fact that digital terrestrial television does not work.

Of course, nowadays everywhere, the Internet always helps everyone, where you can always see everything you want. However, this article is not about these cases.





Theory

All of these methods are good enough. But it is necessary, when choosing one of them, to represent, at least in general terms, how they differ. For example, just terrestrial television and digital are completely different. The latter is that digital encoding is used to transmit the image and sound - both the audio signal and the video signal. And for transmission, digital channels are used. Such encoding ensures minimal loss in signal delivery, since no interference to the encoded information is terrible. And if the channels of digital television do not work, then they do not work completely, in general. And if they work, then only in excellent quality. The boundary state and interference are absent here. The exception is only that there is a poor quality of communication. Then the TV, as it were, slows down, turns off and turns on again. And this is corrected only in one way - you need another antenna or you need to raise the existing one higher and deploy it towards the TV tower.

It should also be noted that many users do not know that digital television is always a limitation in the number of available channels. Depending on the region, there may be ten, twenty, the number varies. The principle does not work here: raised the antenna above and caught absolutely everything.
