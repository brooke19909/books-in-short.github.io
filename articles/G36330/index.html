Parents

The full name of the material's heroine is Eileen Carol Pittman. She was born in 1956 in a small American town in Michigan. Mother, Diana, got married early and gave birth to the eldest son Keith at the age of 16. Eileen herself was born two years later. His mother filed for divorce, and Eileen never got to know his father: he was sent to prison for a long time on charges of sexual crimes against children. During the examination, Leo Pittman was diagnosed with schizophrenia. He committed suicide in 1969, when his daughter was 13 years old.

At the age of 4, Eileen Wornos is forever deprived of her mother: she, leaving the children to be raised by her own parents, leaves in an unknown direction and never appears in her daughter's life. The further fate of Diana is unknown.

Diana's parents, Larry and Britta Wornos, took up the education of their grandchildren, and when the daughter disappeared completely, they made custody legal. However, the biography of the future serial killer Eileen Wornos shows: the elderly did not succeed in raising children as worthy people. Eileen's friend, Don Botkins, who, after death, dispelled her ashes, characterized the girl as modest, quiet, self-contained and uncommunicative.

Killer position

At the trial, Eileen changed her testimony several times, motivating the murders in different ways. Initially, self-defense was called the cause. According to Wornos, all the murdered men had sexual intercourse with her or tried to do it, some used violence, others refused to pay for services.

Then the woman changed her testimony, saying that the main target was a robbery. After rejecting hair removal, Eileen said she was ready for death. According to her, she committed the killings, completely controlling herself, in cold blood, experiencing a burning hatred for the victims. "Monster" Eileen Warnos said that she hates life itself and, if she had a second chance, would again commit her crimes. The psychiatrists who examined the woman confirmed that she was absolutely sane and was aware that she had only one way out - death.

After the verdict was pronounced in one of the interviews, with the camera turned off, she said that the place had self-defense, but she was already tired of living in captivity and was dreaming of death.

Protection position

The investigation into the Eileen Wornos case was lengthy and lasted more than 10 years. During this time, the defense did its best to mitigate the sentence:

  • During the Mallory trial, psychiatrists tried to prove that Eileen was suffering from personality disorders and mental instability.
  • Attempts were made to prove that the victims themselves did not have an impeccable moral character. So, the same Mallory himself a few years ago appeared before the court for an attempted rape and was prone to sociopathic manifestations. However, this information was not taken into account.

As a result, the woman fully pleaded guilty, refused protection and received 6 death sentences. Ironically, she did not suffer punishment for the murder of Peter Sims, because his body was never found, but it was the abandoned car of the fourth victim that became the indirect reason for her detention.

Eileen Wornos was executed in 2002 by lethal injection, replacing electrocution. A fact is known: a woman indifferently refused the last meal (according to state laws, everyone sentenced has the right to order food in the amount of not more than $ 40), asking only for coffee.
