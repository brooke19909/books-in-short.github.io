San Gimignano, Italy: detailed description, attractions and reviews

A wonderful country that leaves an indelible mark on the souls of everyone who has ever visited it, known for its color and originality. Charming Italy, which guarantees everyone an exciting adventure, does not complain about the lack of attention of foreign tourists.

A small town protected by UNESCO, year-round welcomes travelers who are delighted at the sight of unique architectural monuments and picturesque nature.

A bit of history

A true treasury with many amazing sights, located in the famous region of Tuscany. San Gimignano (Italy), which vacationers compare with miniature Manhattan during the Middle Ages, rises on a hill that rises more than 300 meters above the Elsa River Valley.

From a small village founded by the Etruscans BC, the main decoration of Tuscany turned into an economically developed city, which gained independence in the XII century. From this moment begins a long period of prosperity thanks to the trade in wine and spices. Around the settlement, fortress walls span more than two thousand meters. To strengthen their protective functions, the construction of towers begins.





It was during this period that two influential families ruled in the village and became rivals. They competed among themselves, figuring out who had more power and money, building up territory with powerful towers that were always higher than those of opponents. Every year, San Gimignano in Italy overgrown with new buildings. And only the decree of the local city council forbade the construction of "skyscrapers" that are superior in height to the building of the main palace.

In the middle of the 14th century, terrible misfortune came to San Gimignano, and half of the inhabitants die from the plague. Weakened by a terrible loss, he falls under the influence of Florence and is forced to submit to her rule. No development took place until the 19th century, when the city, which has retained its medieval appearance, acquires the status of a tourist center in Italy.

History Center

San Gimignano (Italy), whose sights have preserved the spirit of bygone times, is a small city, and therefore you can get acquainted with curious monuments located nearby in a few hours. To get to know the Tuscan pearl better, you should visit the historical center, which transfers to the present Middle Ages. You can move on it only on foot.





The romantic square della Cistern is one of the oldest in the city. Many centuries ago there was a reservoir that supplied water to the whole city. And now in its very center you can see a deep well, which gave the name of the square. Piazza della Cisterna, surrounded by towers, the tops of which were cut off, flows smoothly into the Piazza del Duomo, famous for the city's cathedral.
