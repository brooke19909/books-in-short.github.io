Tunisia Attractions: Overview, Features and Interesting Facts

Tunisia is a country in North Africa that has long been favored by tourists. Most of them come here for the warm Mediterranean Sea, beach relaxation and thalassotherapy. But the country is interesting from the point of view of studying history. In every corner lies the ancient and not very sights of Tunisia.

What to see in Hammamet?

Hammamet - one of the most popular resorts in the country - respectable, European-restrained and conservative. Ancient monuments are adjacent to modern attractions.

The heart of every Arab city is a medina. The ancient fortress wall and mosque may not be the most striking sights of Hammamet (Tunisia), but attention is definitely worth it. You can admire all this for free, not counting the entrance to the fortress, which will cost 7 dinars.

There is a modern analogue of the medina in the city - the Mediterraniya complex. It is an Arabian medina designed by modern builders, behind the walls of which lies a hotel, a souvenir market, cafes and restaurants.

You should look at the old villa of Sebastian, the museum of the history of the city and the water park. The sights of Hammamet (Tunisia) will allow you to spend time in the city is very interesting.

Sightseeing Pearl - Carthage

One of the most visited places in Tunisia is the ancient Carthage. Almost completely destroyed, it still attracts crowds of tourists. The ruins are not concentrated in one place, the most interesting excavation sites are stretched for 6 km. Having a competent guide nearby, you can easily get around at a time.

The closest ruins are to the capital of the country (only 14 km from the center), but excursions are organized here from any resort.

Inspect in Carthage should be the baths of Antonin, or rather, the remaining ceilings and underground premises. Also inspect the amphitheater, water tanks and the remains of the aqueduct. A local historical museum is also a must.

A single ticket that allows you to explore all the excavation sites costs 9 dinars.

Tunis Attractions: Sousse

Sousse is the most noisy and youth resort in the country. In addition to beaches and discos, it is full of attractions.

In Sousse, you must definitely go up to Ribat to view the city's medina and port from a height. Ribat is the oldest building in Sousse, it has come down to our time in its original form. Entrance fee - 7 dinars.

The small but impressive archaeological museum of Sousse can please tourists with a collection of mosaics and ceramics. Entrance costs 9 dinars. The museum is considered the main attraction of Sousse (Tunisia).

If you travel with children or just love animals, be sure to visit the Phrygia Safari Park, which is located outside the city.

The animals are kept in spacious enclosures and walk freely. You can watch lions, giraffes, meerkats, kangaroos, horned animals, turtles. Also on site is a popular fur seal show. After a walk in the park and feeding animals, tourists are invited to look into the neighboring building, to visit the Zulus. There is a dinner and a traditional show for a fee.

What can be seen in Monastir?

Monastir is a quiet city that has retained its authenticity due to the removal of the resort area beyond its borders. The city is located at an equal distance from the main attractions of Tunisia, so it can serve as a starting point for any excursion. However, in Monastir itself there is something to look at.

In the old city you need to visit a mosque of the 9th century, a museum of national costume, a museum of Islamic art and Ribat. Be sure to go up to the observation deck, the panorama that opens is gorgeous.

Another attraction of Monastir (Tunisia) is the Habib Bourguiba Mausoleum.

The wide alley leading to the building with golden domes is surrounded on both sides by a cemetery. Inside the mausoleum, sarcophagi of family members of the country's first president, made of marble. An interesting and small museum of personal belongings of Habib Bourguiba.

If you are not interested in the architectural sights of Monastir (Tunisia), take an active rest. There are developed diving and horse riding.

Picturesque island of Djerba

The island, famous for its olive groves and the most delicious dates, is connected to the mainland by a ferry crossing and a bridge, so there will be no problems with exploring the main historical heritage of Tunisia. For those who do not want to leave the island, there are plenty of local attractions.

El Ghriba Synagogue is the oldest synagogue in all of Africa. Its lining was redone in the 19th century, but the Torah scroll, which can be seen here, is authentic and ancient.

The main natural attraction of Djerba (Tunisia) is a picturesque lagoon where you can see flamingos. It is worth a trip to the crocodile farm, the largest in the Mediterranean. It is interesting to look at crocodiles, feed and touch them, as well as purchase souvenirs.

An eccentric landmark of the island of Djerba (Tunisia) is the village of Erriad. In 2014, this place was visited by famous street-style artists. On the walls of houses they left more than 200 works, against which tourists like to pose.

A place for lovers of peace - Mahdia

Mahdia - these are not crowded and calm beaches, high-quality thalassotherapy and relaxation with children. The main place for walking here is the promenade with cafes and shops. Of course, you need to go to the medina. Behind the walls there are good restaurants with fish cuisine and souvenir shops. In the heart of the medina is another tourist attraction of Tunisia - Skif al-Kahlya (black gate). After going through them, you can see the Museum of Mahdia and climb to the observation deck.

Bordj El Kebir is a massive fortress, built during the time of Mahdia, the capital of the whole empire. Entrance to the fortress costs 3 dinars.

Having departed a little, you can see the lighthouse and the cemetery, as well as the remains of forts.

Tunisia's main natural attraction - Sahara

From any resort in the North African country, a tourist will be offered an excursion to the Sahara, because this desert is the core of the program along with Carthage.

Sugar is not only the smallest sand, dunes, heat and wind. Desert landscapes are very diverse: rocky plateaus, salt marshes and even semi-sandy plains.

In the desert you should see Mount Tembain, the ruins of Fort Tisavar and ancient sources, as well as Zemlet El Bormu - the highest dune. It is best to get acquainted with this exotic landmark of Tunisia as part of a two-day excursion. It usually includes camel riding and quad biking. A tourist with money can ride a motor hang glider. For the night, tourists are accommodated in one of the hotels of Douza. At night, you can even see the inhabitants of the desert: jerboas, scarabs and owls.

If a tourist wants to delve deeper into the life of the desert, he can stay in Duz for a few days and even book a jeep tour, heading right into the heart of the Sahara sands.
