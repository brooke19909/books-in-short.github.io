Highscreen Spider: model review, customer reviews and experts

Over the past few years, Highscreen has noticeably updated its range of models. Moreover, each new smartphone comes with some of its original zest. Take, for example, the Boost 2 SE gadget, which still holds a leading position in the segment of long-playing models.





The object of today's review is the Highscreen Spider smartphone. Let's try to figure out what is the highlight of this gadget, and outline all its advantages along with disadvantages, taking into account the opinions of experts and reviews of ordinary users.

The main feature of Spider is support for fourth-generation networks (LTE). In general, the Spider model is a middle-class gadget with a price tag of 11,000 rubles, a five-inch screen, a four-core processor and a gigabyte of RAM.

Judging by the characteristics, the offer looks more or less interesting, but let's try to find out in the Highscreen Spider LTE Black review how much it matches its price tag. Reviews about the model are very flattering with a total score of 4.0 out of 5 from users.

The gadget comes only in black, and the company has not yet provided for other color solutions. The appearance of the device is standardized - a rectangle with beveled corners, and there are no designer tricks for the Highscreen Spider model.

Build quality

The overall build quality is at a high level: the gaps are minimal, the details fit one to one, squeaks, backlashes and crunches were not seen on the Highscreen Spider. User reviews note a positive reaction to the assembly of the model: the dust does not fill in the cracks, because there are none, the cover is removed without any problems and is also installed in place, fingerprints can be left, but quite difficult.

Display

Highscreen Spider 5 is equipped with a 5-inch touch screen with IPS-matrix, supporting a resolution of 1280 x 720 pixels. The pixel density is quite high and amounts to 294 ppi, so it is visually impossible to see individual points even from a very close distance. If you work with the device at a recommended distance of 30-40 cm, then the picture looks holistic and bright.





The touch screen of the device can support up to five points of simultaneous touch. The sensitivity of the display is good, so the response to touch is true in more than 90% of cases. The gadget has good viewing angles, which is characteristic of the entire Highscreen Spider Black line. User reviews note minor problems with automatic brightness adjustment, sinning on the sensor in the stock firmware, but the manufacturer has already released a fix in 4.4x updates.

In bright and sunny weather, there may be problems with the display of the display, due to the low level of maximum brightness. The contrast of the device allows you to comfortably work with the gadget in a dark room. The indicator is at the level of 900: 1 with an extensive adjustment range, which is very good for an inexpensive Highscreen Spider.

The review of color channels revealed positive dynamics and stability, although the indicators do not reach the sRGB standard. By sight, this drawback is almost impossible to notice, especially since the uniformity of the backlight eliminates any questions about overexposure.

Performance

The device is equipped with a quad-core Qualcomm Snapdragon 400 MSM8926 processor with a maximum clock frequency of 1.2 GHz and 8 GB of RAM. For the graphic component, the Adreno 305 accelerator with one gigabyte of memory is responsible.





Due to its technical characteristics, the smartphone is able to meet the requirements of the average user. If you do not open more than 8-10 tabs at a time, the browser can easily cope with the load without twitching and additional time to switch between tabs.
