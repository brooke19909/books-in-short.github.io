Alcohol in the Maldives: is it safe to drink? Requirements and Interesting Facts

The Maldives is a paradise for everyone who wants to spend their holidays in unity with beautiful nature. This resort is considered one of the best, and most importantly, the safest for tourists, since all the islands are isolated from the outside world.





Privacy, romance, amazing beauty of the ocean and the highest level of service - this is the secret of the popularity of the Maldives for vacationers from all over the planet.

Interesting Facts

This paradise is located right in the equator, and its islands stretch in both hemispheres. At the same time, there are practically no seasonal temperature differences in the Maldives. The resort is always ready to meet new tourists in the hot summer.

In total, the state has 1,190 islands, most of which are only small pieces of land. Only 200 islands are inhabited by people, and only 90 of them are accessible for tourists.

All islands of the Maldives are coral, so it is strictly forbidden to take out corals from the state or damage it in the coastal zone. Violation may result in severe fines.



It will be interesting for tourists that there are absolutely no dogs on the islands. These animals are prohibited in the country, but, fortunately, there are no poisonous snakes or insects on the archipelago, they simply are not found there. Also, a large number of exotic fish live in coastal waters, including the largest - tiger sharks.

Holidays in the Maldives are Friday and Saturday. It is also interesting that the islands are recognized as the lowest state in the world. The highest level above the sea is 2.3 meters.

The literacy rate of the population in the country is 98%, and absolutely all children receive primary education. Perhaps this is also somehow related to the fact that alcohol in the Maldives is not consumed by the indigenous population.

Serious problem

At the customs control upon arrival at the place of rest, tourists learn that the transport of alcohol to the Maldives is strictly prohibited. This rule is strictly regulated by law, and even if alcohol was bought in Duty-free already on the territory of the state, in order to leave the airport, you will have to hand it in or drink it right there. A very pleasant fact for many is that alcohol in the Maldives is confiscated from tourists only during their stay in the archipelago. Immediately after the end of the holiday, all tourists return their drinks, which were previously stored in special free left-luggage offices. Each intruder is issued a receipt with his cell number, from which he will then regain his alcohol.



Knowing how our tourists are trying to save money, customs control conducts a strict search of everyone, all alcohol found is seized. Of course, the savvy of Russian tourists also constantly generates new ways of transporting alcohol to the Maldives.

Of course, at the same time, it is necessary to put on loose clothing so that the contours of the container are not striking to the airport staff.
