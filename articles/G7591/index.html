Dortmund (Germany): attractions of the city

The main religious site is the church of St. Rinalda, dating from the 13th century. After the Second World War, the temple collapsed significantly, but this did not stop the local authorities from reconstructing its original appearance with donations. The facility is located in the historical part of the city near shopping and entertainment complexes.





The construction was rebuilt for several centuries, so two architectural styles prevail in the design: Gothic and Romanesque. The interior adores luxury. The interior is decorated with wooden sculptures of the 15th century - St. Rinald and Charlemagne, as well as statues of other rulers. Here you can see unique works of art dating from different eras.

Also preserved historical elements - the baptismal font. Every weekend and religious holidays in the temple of Dortmund (Germany), choral singing is organized, organ music sounds. Everyone can climb to the observation deck, which offers a magnificent panorama of local beauty.

Westphalian park





Judging by the reviews, this park is a favorite place for walks among citizens and tourists. On its territory is a huge sports hall with a capacity of up to 23,000 spectators. Once there was a football match of the legendary Borussia team. Dortmund (Germany) still remembers all the victories of the outstanding FC, which has repeatedly won champion titles.





Today, celebrations, concert programs, large-scale shows gathering a huge audience are held in the gym. An ice rink was also built in the park. In addition, Westphalian Park is a green recreation area where couples with children often stroll.
