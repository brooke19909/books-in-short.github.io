Church of the Savior of the Miraculous Image



Considering what Dolgoprudny is famous for, the sights of the city, it is worth paying attention to the Cathedral of the Savior of the Holy Face. The famous Orthodox church is located in the central point of the city. The date of its foundation is 1684. It was at this time that Prince Repin decided to build a church on the territory of his own estate. Later, the temple, as well as the territory of the estate, passed into the possession of the Yusupov clan. To this day, tombs of nobles from this family name have been preserved here.

Currently, the Church of the Savior of the Miraculous Image is an active church. Divine services are held weekly in the cathedral. On the territory of the historical complex there is a Sunday school for adults and children.

Monument of the Hungarian-Soviet friendship

The original composition is an architectural structure 10 meters high in the form of a curved arrow. On the inner side of the monument contains a bas-relief of ceramic. It depicts female figures who embody the fraternal relationship between the two peoples. At the top of the monument are sculptures of flying pigeons that symbolize the world.

It is noteworthy that the monument to the Hungarian-Soviet friendship is lined with pyro-granite. The material is unique in its kind and is produced only in a Hungarian town called Pecs.

Manor Vinogradovo

Overlooking Dolgoprudny, the sights of the city, it is worth noting the old Vinogradovo estate. The historical site was previously a house in which the family of the famous landowner R.V. German was living. The estate was built back in 1912.

Attractive architecture has been preserved here not only inside, but also outside. Particular attention of tourists is drawn to the unique interiors, decorated in the Baroque style. There are stone stairs and balconies, carved wooden doors, huge fireplaces.

Museum of Local Lore



What else is attractive for Dolgoprudny tourist? First of all, travelers are interested in the local museum of local lore. This is one of the few large exhibition complexes in the suburbs. The bulk of the exhibits is represented by military installations. And this is not surprising, because at one time the city was significantly damaged by artillery strikes, and many of its buildings were completely destroyed. However, there are many exhibits that reveal the culture of nationalities that once lived in the region.

Monument rocket

The original monument was installed on the city square named after Vasily Sobin in 2012. Thus, the inhabitants of the city honored the memory of rocket scientists. The monument was opened symbolically on the day of the 80th anniversary of the signing of the order on the commissioning of the first airships, the assembly of which is glorified by Dolgoprudny. Today, the rocket monument takes one of the honorable places in the list of the main attractions of the city.

Theater "City"



The building of the central city theater invariably attracts local residents who need to organize cultural activities. Despite its relatively short history of existence, which is 22 years old, the theater not only won the recognition of spectators from Dolgoprudny, but also became famous throughout the region and even abroad.

The repertoire of the theater covers a wide range of topics, from dramatic, comedic productions to children's performances. Today, the number of actors here is more than 30 people. All of them have high skill and are able to intrigue any spectator who visits the theater.

Sculpture "The coachman and the two horses"

The sculptural composition adorned Dolgoprudny in 2007 and is the work of the famous author V.I. Zhbanov. A cultural monument was formed in several stages. Initially, the city residents were presented with horses cast in bronze, and an empty wagon. Then, in 2008, the sculpture was supplemented with a statue of the coachman, who is studying the map of the area. Therefore, local residents often call this sculpture "Lost Coachman".

House of Culture "Forward"



The local house of culture is one of the largest bases in the region where the search and disclosure of the talents of the younger generation takes place. The institution was founded in 1939. At that time, the house of culture had the status of the best cultural and educational facility in the suburbs. But even today, its regular visitors are more than 800 local residents who organize cultural, entertainment and public events, as well as improve the city.

Church of St. Sergius of Radonezh

The famous church was built back in 1893. The project was developed by the famous architect Nikolai Sultanov. The temple is a complex of wooden buildings on a stone foundation, decorated in the old Russian style.

In 1911, the area around the temple was expanded. A school and residential buildings for ministers, a hospital, outbuildings, stables, cells for monks were erected here.

How to get to Dolgoprudny?



The simplest solution that will allow you to get here from Moscow is a train trip. Dolgoprudny from the capital's Savelovsky station runs a whole mass of trains. The following directions should be taken into account: Moscow - Lobnya, Moscow - Iksha, Moscow - Dmitrov, Moscow - Dubna. The trains on these routes depart from the capital station daily at intervals of about 10 minutes. Travel time by train to Dolgoprudny will be only 30 minutes.

You can get here by bus. From Moscow to Dolgoprudny public transport follows routes No. 546 and No. 572. Buses in the directions presented depart from the Altufyevo metro station. The final destination is a stop called the Dolgoprudnaya Platform. If along the way you are lucky enough not to get stuck in traffic, the road will take some 50 minutes.

As for the trip by car, the most logical option is to turn off the Moscow Ring Road towards the Likhachevsky Highway. Further, it is enough to adhere to the main road. Having reached Patsayev Avenue, you can enter directly into Dolgoprudny. Travel time when traveling by personal transport will be no more than 40 minutes, provided that there are no traffic jams.
