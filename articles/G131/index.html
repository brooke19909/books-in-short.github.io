Italy, Tivoli. Aristocratic suburb of the Italian capital

The city of Tivoli is a little away from the main directions, from which our compatriots usually begin to get acquainted with Italy. Most often this comes from simple ignorance. And many do not even suspect that if you turn off a little from well-groomed highways, then a completely different Italy will open up. Tivoli is located very close to Rome. And age is not inferior to the capital of the Roman Empire. This small town is more than three thousand years old. In ancient history, he occupies a very honorable place. In modern terms, we can say that here since the time of the empire, country residences of emperors and other influential people have been located. This is such a suburban area like Barvikha near Moscow.





Italy, Tivoli. sights

The city was badly damaged due to the bombing during World War II and the subsequent uncontrolled development. However, even after these historical cataclysms, Tivoli remains a city with its unique appearance, inscribed in golden letters in the history of world architecture. What makes it unique is a rare combination of architectural masterpieces from different eras, from antiquity to the Middle Ages. Not so much material evidence of the ancient world has reached our days ; only a country such as Italy can be proud of its presence. Tivoli is superior in concentration of architectural attractions to any other place on the Apennine Peninsula. In ancient times, the city was called Tibur. And at present, only historians and architects remember this, for which concepts are built into one semantic series - antiquity, Rome, Italy, Tivoli. Photos of architectural fragments from this city are included in all special textbooks. The most outstanding landmark of the ancient era is the Villa Adriana.





The emperor was not used to denying anything to himself and arranged his country residence on a grand scale. Only by its territory was it comparable with the central part of Rome. And in terms of luxury and magnificence it was much superior. Currently, Villa Adriana is in a ruined state, and this is not surprising, given that it has been plundered for centuries, starting with the barbarians. However, even these ruins make a strong impression. They symbolize the historic turning point between the eras when the Roman Empire ended and Italy began. Tivoli, in many ways, has remained a fragment of antiquity in our time. Among other residences of historical figures, the villas of d'Este and Gregorian should be noted first of all. They are perfectly preserved even taking into account all the restorations and reconstructions and are very significant in their architectural and historical heritage.





Tivoli, Italy. Tours and excursions

When traveling independently in Italy, it is usually customary to devote a separate day visit to Tivoli. For the first acquaintance with the Roman suburbs, this will be enough. However, it is quite possible to spend 200-250 euros on an excursion as part of an organized group, many travel agencies offer similar services. The amount includes the services of a qualified guide and delivery in both directions.
