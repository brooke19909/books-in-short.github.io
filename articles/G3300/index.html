The origins of the drawing "Vitruvian Man"

In very old, but unforgettable times, in the 1st century BC, the Roman Vitruvius created his Ten Books, a work dedicated to the emperor Augustus. But, in addition to gratitude for the help, the books contain valuable advice in the field of architecture.

The books of Vitruvius did not become famous during the life of the author. But the fact that it was he who brought about the aesthetics of architecture and the ergonomics of structures is noteworthy. The latter is extremely important, because in our time, engineers and architects design their creations based on the data of the human body to make the use of structures convenient. In addition, Vitruvius designed the aqueduct - water supply systems that significantly improved life in Roman cities.

And here is one of Vitruvius' Ten Books dedicated to the human body. For the first time, the question was raised about proportions and a certain division, making it close to ideal. This book said that a person fits perfectly into a square shape. The latter is a reflection of the essence of everything earthly. You can also fit a person into a circle - a symbol of the divine. Thus, a person approaches God, and such philosophizing is close to the spirit of that era.

Look again at the depiction of the golden section of Leonardo da Vinci. Pictures, which can be found in many books, provide an opportunity to get acquainted with the code and try to see the clue of the formula.
