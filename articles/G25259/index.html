Crocheted tunics: patterns and description (for women). Crochet summer tunic

Tunic is a universal and modern wardrobe item that has become a favorite for many girls and women. In the broad sense of the word, tunics are called clothes for the upper body, reaching the thigh line. The length of the model, decoration and technique can be completely different.

There are a large number of options for sewing and knitting tunics, which include sleeves or their absence, a dense or openwork fabric, the presence of everyday or holiday decor.

In this article, we will talk about what constitutes and how a summer tunic is crocheted. Schemes and descriptions of various models are provided with illustrations.

What yarn is suitable for knitting openwork products?

When choosing a material for making clothes, it is worthwhile first of all to pay attention to yarn with a high content of natural fibers. Suitable for summer things:

  • Cotton.
  • Linen.
  • Bamboo.
  • Viscose.
  • Silk.

Why do many experienced craftswomen recommend such threads for crocheting a tunic? Patterns and descriptions for women (knitting patterns) also often involve the use of these materials, but the thread is applied a little thicker.

Many models are designed based on optimal and average parameters, and natural materials are suitable for most people. They absorb moisture well, let air through, in such clothes it‚Äôs not hot.

Unnatural threads

In some cases, you can apply blended yarn, in which artificial components are added to natural raw materials:

  • Acrylic.
  • Microfiber.
  • Polyamide.
  • Nylon.

If their content does not exceed 30%, then the material that prevails will determine the performance of the product. These components give the thread strength, elasticity, shine and lightness. In addition, this significantly reduces the cost of yarn.

Tunic assembly

Finished parts are sewn along the shoulder seams 13 cm on each side. Then, the side seams are made, leaving 18 cm cuts for the armhole. Next, the sleeve is sewn along the side and sewn into the armhole.

The connection of parts is best done with a needle with the same thread that was used to crochet the tunic. Schemes and descriptions for women do not provide for strapping, but it can still be done along the edge of the sleeves and bottom.

How to place square motifs?

Of course, you can use fragments of different shapes: triangular, round, hexagons and others. But squares are easiest to model, especially for beginners.

Openwork fragment becomes the main decoration of the tunic crocheted. Schemes and descriptions for women, as a rule, suggest either to make all the canvases from squares, or to combine them into stripes and combine with another pattern.





This model includes a simple mesh and large lace motifs. Fragments are arranged in three rows along the bottom line (typesetting canvas), and on the sleeves they represent a strip.

Details of the front and backs of the tunic are collected from motifs interconnected during the last row of knitting. In fact, this results in a circular canvas, which, when folded in half, has the shape of a rectangle.

Its width should be equal to half the circumference of the chest, and the height - the length of the tunic to the armholes.

Straps are knitted, starting from the front part, and sew on the back. Their width can be any: from one to ten centimeters. The final stage is the binding of the product. It is important here not to overdo it with the complexity of the pattern. If the squares have a rich ornament, the binding should be simple.

How to handle a knitted tunic

Cloths made with a hook are much denser than those knitted with needles. They are less susceptible to deformation, but although stretching does not threaten them, shrinkage is quite possible. Almost all natural materials can sit a little over time, especially after washing in hot water.

Therefore, the basic rules for caring for crocheted tunics relate to washing and drying. Water temperature should not exceed 30 degrees, machine wash is absolutely contraindicated, and it is better to dry such products when unfolded.

If necessary, iron the knitted tunic, this is best done through a layer of thin, damp cloth.
