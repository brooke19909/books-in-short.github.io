Greater Gelendzhik

The shores of a convenient bay were inhabited a long time ago, five thousand years ago. The first settlement of Torikos was built by Greek colonists. Then on this spot stood the Roman military fort of Pagra. In the Middle Ages, this territory was part of Byzantium (port of Eptala). The Khazars captured the Gelendzhik bay, and Genoese watchtowers were erected on its shores. Their fortified settlement was called Mavrolako. Then the Adyghes settled here, and later the Ottoman Turks. With them, Gelendzhik got his modern name. But the name was given to the city by Magrels (Georgians). Gelen Jih means "external fortress." In the end, this territory was captured by Russia. A mild climate, five sources of mineral waters of various compositions, a warm sea - all this led to the fact that already in 1900 the first sanatorium was opened in Gelendzhik. The city became an all-Russian resort in 1907. Gelendzhik, reviews of vacationers in 2015 confirm this, continues to be a health resort now. In addition to water, it is treated with mud that is specially brought from the Taman Peninsula. Gelendzhik repeatedly won in all-Russian competitions as the most comfortable city in the country. Naturally, the resort has grown and has included satellite villages. Now we can talk about the Big Gelendzhik, which includes Praskoveevka, Dzhanhot, Kabardinka, Arkhipo-Osipovka, Betta and Divnomorskoe. With a population of seventy thousand people, the resort annually receives three million vacationers.



How to get to Gelendzhik

In 2010, the resort acquired its own airport. However, Aeroflot airplanes on the route Moscow - Gelendzhik begin to run only from the end of March. From the beginning of the beach season, the local airport also begins to receive UTair and Alrosa liners. You can also consider options for departure to Anapa, Adler or Krasnodar. Buses run from these cities to Gelendzhik. An air ticket in the low season will cost from one thousand seven hundred rubles, and in the high - from three and a half thousand. Moscow-Gelendzhik trains do not exist, since this seaside resort is not connected by rail. But you can get to Novorossiysk. Trains depart from Kazan and Paveletsky stations. A seat in the reserved seat costs 1,700 rubles. Given that you have to spend about a day and a half in the car, it is wiser to take a compartment. A place in it will cost a thousand rubles more. From the Novorossiysk station you need to arrive by trolleybus number 6 to the main bus station. A ticket to Gelendzhik will cost about a hundred rubles. The resort also accepts bus services from Anapa, Sochi, Tuapse, Kislovodsk, Rostov-on-Don, Pyatigorsk, Taganrog. Own car should move along the highway "Don", then in Krasnodar turn onto A146. Passing Krymsk, you need to go towards Novorossiysk. So you will avoid serpentines in the Dzhubga area.



When to go on vacation to Gelendzhik

The time for a comfortable stay begins at the resort from the second decade of May. The deep Black Sea, warmed up over the summer, cools slowly. And the prevailing winds drive away the cooled layers from shallow water. They are being replaced by deep seams. Due to such water circulation in the Gelendzhik Bay, the winters in the resort are extremely mild. Air temperature rarely drops to + 6 degrees. However, in spring, the situation is radically changing. The cooled sea and winds from the Main Caucasian Range cool the air. But the location in the south of Russia makes itself felt, and already from the May holidays, the first swimmers with a squeal open the swimming season. From the foregoing, it can be understood that the best time to relax in Gelendzhik is the so-called velvet period, which lasts from September to the end of October. But the mass Russian tourist traditionally prefers to go south in the summer months. Gelendzhik in July is very crowded. Prices soar, housing without prior reservation is difficult to find, on the beaches - crowds, in cafes and shops - queues. But if you are interested in a wellness holiday in Gelendzhik, then the health resort accepts vacationers all year round. Prices in the sanatoriums are almost independent of the season.

Resorts in the south of Greater Gelendzhik

The name "Dzhanhot" is translated from Adyghe as "happy person". The name of the farm, and now the resort village was not given by chance. Happy are the people who rested here. A thick pine forest comes close to the shore, the small river Khotetsay murmurs, the picturesque rock Parus is seen in the sea. In Dzhanhot, the summer house-museum of the writer Korolenko is located. The village of Praskoveevka lies five kilometers from the sea, but vacationers are also welcome there. Naturally, housing prices there are much lower. Therefore, the option of accommodation in Praskoveevka should be considered if you have a vehicle at your disposal and are interested in a budget holiday in Gelendzhik. 2015 pleased the holidaymakers with rather low prices (starting from 350 rubles per person per night). But not only private owners rent turnkey rooms and houses in Praskoveevka. There is no shortage of resort hotels and recreation facilities. Further south of Gelendzhik are two villages - Krinitsa and Betta. Despite the remoteness from the city, they have established tourist infrastructure - there are recreation centers, pensions, hotels, restaurants and nightclubs. Arkhipo-Osipovka is the extreme southern village of Greater Gelendzhik. He will be interested in outdoor enthusiasts. In the vicinity of Arkhipo-Osipovka there are ruins of a watch tower of the ancient era and dolmens, and in the village itself there is a dolphinarium, water park, museum and garden with relic plants.
