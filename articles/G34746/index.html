Belbidi Cave

Another example of the most visited attraction in Kemer. Excursions here will not only give a lot of enthusiastic impressions, but also provide an opportunity to join the hitherto unknown. After all, on the walls of the cave you can find drawings - no less than many - of ancient people, in addition, here still find various objects, whose age dates back to the periods of the Paleolithic, Mesolithic and Neolithic. Who knows how many secrets her arches and bowels keep? Well, the beauty of the whole cave complex, which is located sixteen kilometers from Kemer, is completely impossible to describe in words.

You can visit Belbidi on your own or book an excursion that will cost you only ten dollars (per person). For this money, the guide will introduce you to the sights and history of the surrounding area, as for the entrance to the cave itself, it is free.

Phaselis

It is impossible to visit Kemer and not visit the oldest city in Turkey. According to legend, Athena herself patronized him. It is believed that it was founded in the sixth century BC, was equipped with three sea harbors and performed mainly a trading function. It was from the hands of the inhabitants of this city that Alexander the Great received a golden crown.

Today, of course, little has been preserved from the city, only the ruins of the walls, the crypt, the temple, and some household buildings remain. But there is a large part of the necropolis, where, according to legend, Alexander the Great was buried. Phaselis is open for visits from nine in the morning. An interesting feature - in the form of payment for entry, dollars are not accepted here. Perhaps this is a kind of tribute to antiquity. So, to get into the territory, an adult tourist must have 14 lire in his pocket.
