Narva Reservoir: where is it located, how to get there, size and depth, recreation facilities, beaches, good fishing and vacationers reviews

The Narva reservoir is located in the Leningrad region, in the middle reaches of the Narva River. This is a great place for lovers of fishing and just a good rest in nature. On its perimeter there are numerous recreation centers and sanatoriums where you can rent a boat and spend leisure time with interest.

Reservoir location, how to get

The Narva reservoir is located about 20 km from Kingisepp, on the border with the Estonian town of Ida-Virumaa and not far from Ivangorod. Choosing a ride along the coast? Stop on vacation at the Narva Reservoir and sit with a fishing rod at one of the fishing bases, the coastline of which stretches for almost 25 km.

Where to stay at this time depends solely on personal preferences. Judging by the reviews of local fishermen, the best bite in the eastern part of the reservoir, where the Narva River flows. In these places, the coastline is indented with many small bays, from shallow to deep. The greatest depth near the flooded channel is 16 m. In most other places, from 1.5 to 3 m.

You can get to Ivangorod by train, following the route Moscow - Tallinn or St. Petersburg - Tallinn. By car via Kingisepp or on the M-21 highway from St. Petersburg. From Moscow on the highway go for about 12-15 hours through Zelenograd, Tver, Novgorod, Kingisepp.

Features

The reservoir is called the Narva Sea, since it occupies the middle course of the Narova River.

Features of the area:

  • There are several small islands on the lake.
  • The rivers Plyussa and Narova flow into an artificial reservoir, on the banks of which there are industrial enterprises and cities.
  • Until the middle of the twentieth century, more than half of the reservoir was a swamp, so there are many peat accumulations.
  • A hydroelectric power station is installed in the water area.
  • About 12.8 thousand km¬≥ of water from various sources flows into the reservoir during the year.
  • The outflow of water from the Narva reservoir occurs mainly through a hydroelectric power station.
  • A dam was built in the south of the reservoir, which regulates the flow of water.
  • Water open spaces and nature allow tourists to relax and fish.

Stay mode

Part of the Narva reservoir is located on the territory of Russia, and part is in Estonia, so a pass must be issued before the trip. It is drawn up and issued free of charge for a month. To obtain it, you must contact the border department of St. Petersburg in the Leningrad Region at 62 Shpalernaya Street. Some travel agencies provide the same service, but according to their own tariff.

The pass allows entry into the border area and temporary stay on water from 8 am to 10 pm. For vacationers with their own water transport, registration, availability of a side number and the Russian flag are required.

Fishing

The reservoir has always been popular with avid fishermen. Valuable fish species from the Baltic Sea and Lake Peipsi swim here for spawning. The fish fauna has about 30 varieties: perch, pike, asp, burbot, ide, rudd, bream, ruff, tench, roach and many others.

Many backwaters and floating peat islands make fishing on the Narva Reservoir not only productive, but also interesting. The shores of the reservoir are very boggy, so fishing takes place mainly from the boat.

According to reviews, most fishermen come here to catch a pike, which is famous for its large size. All the conditions for a large catch are created for zerglik fishermen. Unauthorized gear can be positioned along the edge of drifting islets. Fans of trolling fishing are recommended the area along the old channel of the Narva River.

Regardless of the summer or winter season, fishing in the reservoir is equally good. Naturally, you need to competently approach the choice of place, take into account the specifics of the reservoir, understand the characteristics of the habits of fish and choose the right tactics.

The bottom of the reservoir is strewn with fishing tackle of unsuccessful fishermen, but judging by the reviews, few people left here without a catch and a great desire to go fishing in these places again.

Fishing tips

It will be useful even for professionals to heed the recommendations of the local fishermen.

  1. Valuable advice - do not limit yourself to one fishing rod, take more different gear. There were cases when amateur fishermen lost up to 20 different types of lures in a day.
  2. Another important point to consider is fishing from a boat. Due to the large number of backwaters, the shores of the reservoir are mostly boggy. However, depending on preferences, you can find places for trolling, it is possible to set fire and rise in price.
  3. More experienced anglers prefer to catch predatory fish, pike and zander. Traditionally, they choose deep water, but sometimes they swim to the shore, hunting fry and small fish. Around the floating islands you can catch both small breeds of fish and predatory ones.
  4. If you are going to the Narva Reservoir for the first time, take with you a local fisherman who is at least a little familiar with these places and the habits of the fish that live here.
  5. Local residents will be happy to tell you the best fishing places, taking into account the time of year, tell interesting stories from folklore, and share the secrets of fish dishes.
