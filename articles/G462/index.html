Inconsistency of needs with characteristics

Someone prefers to buy products from exactly one manufacturer, and someone acquires a tool based on their needs and value for money. For example, if you live in a city where power surges are rare, it is better to take a cheaper model. Having tried it in practice, you can make a choice more specifically. So you can save a lot, and also not buy a powerful and expensive stabilizer, which will be "dead weight" somewhere in the pantry.

On the contrary, for completely opposite reasons, people often write very angry reviews. A voltage regulator, bought for a couple of thousand, for some reason burns out after a couple of months when using it for a whole "brood" of tools and home appliances! Amazing right?

Remember that voltage stabilizers for the home (the reviews of which we are considering) must be bought with a margin of at least 30%. Simply put, if the power of consumers, which will be constantly connected to it, is 1000 watts, then you need a stabilizer for 1300 watts, or better for all 1500. In addition, in such cases, you should forget about cheap models that are simply not suitable for so serious loads.
