Macro photography is a process in which the closest photo session is taken of the smallest objects that are not visible to the human eye.

How is macro photography done

First of all, you need to decide what you can take macro shots. For convenience, we note that macro photography is photographing small objects in an enlarged plan. In the end, the subject to be shot in the photo is shown much more than it is in reality. The most favorite subjects of attention of macro photographers are insects, flowers, grass and coins.





Photographic equipment for macro photography

To conduct macro photography, the question immediately arises as to which photo equipment is best for the session? In reality, very good macro photos have the ability to take pictures with the simplest camera, because they also have the necessary mode and the lens capability is very high, but SLR cameras are more convenient.

Their application allows the selection of the required lens and makes it possible to keep all add-ons under control, while achieving the best result. If we talk about mirror technology, then for a specialist, as well as for beginning masters, such a model of a camera as Canon ED or similar ones is suitable.





Macro Lens

If we talk about a compact device, then the choice is small, because the objective part cannot be replaced. Although, if it is possible to purchase a specialized macro lens for the reflex camera, it is recommended to do just that. The most popular is the Canon EF-S 60mm f / 2.8 Macro USM.

Macro exposure

Macro photography is not recommended in automatic exposure. This is considered incorrect. It is recommended to set the aperture priority. It should be remembered that as a result of a photo shoot at a short distance, it should be as small as possible so that the depth of field can be at least a little significant! For example, F / 11 or even F / 22 will be very handy.

Due to the fact that the aperture is closed and there is not enough light coming into the matrix, the shutter speed needs to be increased and the tripod during macro photography will be very useful to maintain the shooting scale.

Macro photography has a little caution, because closing the aperture increases the risk of losing a fraction of the sharpness due to diffraction. This can become noticeable on the f / 9 aperture.

Macro Lighting

To reduce exposure, you need to increase the illumination of the room. On a sunny day, you can do without a flash, but in a dark room or at night with a lack of light when shooting any bugs or at the moment when macro photography of insects is carried out, a flash is very necessary.

How to hold the camera during macro photography

The camera must be kept with you during macro photography. The probability of catching a good shot is often given when this moment is least expected. Because of this, you must try not to leave the camera at home. There is no need to purposefully photograph, hoping to get a good photo frame.

During a walk, a trip to some unusual place or meeting friends, you need to have a camera with you. Maybe at that moment it will be possible to capture a good shot. In this case, a small, roomy, unprofessional camera is best.

During macro photography, you need to pay attention to objects or scenes that are fascinating for a photo shoot. It is necessary to notice objects, think about the possibility of capturing them so that a successful shot is released. You can take pictures on the way to work or school. You can also take photos on the way to the store. If you liked some object, but there is no possibility or time to photograph it, then in a notebook or notepad make a note to yourself that this object will need to be photographed in the future. This may not happen on the same day, or even a couple of months later, when free time appears. It will be necessary to return to this place and make a successful shot.

You need to look at the world through the eyes of a photographer. That is, pay attention to everything that surrounds. It can also be a flower on the window, a surrounding view from it, a vase with the fruits in it on the kitchen table. It is necessary to train the thinking of the photographer.   1. "Shake". It occurs when the camera shakes as a result of enormous exposure. It also turns out when shooting takes place in low light conditions. At the same time, camera shake can affect the camera delay by 10 seconds.
  2. The mobility of the removed object, since not every one of them will be static all the time working with it. And even a small movement can ‚Äúblur‚Äù the photo if you shoot at a long delay.
  3. Auto focus miss. Due to the fact that autofocus systems are not perfect and tend to miss and focus on another object.
  4. Wrong choice of depth of field.
