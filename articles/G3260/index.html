Accounting - an enterprise management tool

Any individual entrepreneur, any organization, be it an LLC, a public company or a closed joint-stock company, will certainly encounter such a concept as ‚Äúfinancial statements‚Äù. Moreover, its provision is necessary for any tax system and regardless of whether there is a profit or not.

What does accounting reporting include and why is it needed? Such words inspire fear in an inexperienced person. But in fact, this is structured information about the financial position of the company. It includes information on the movement of assets, profits, losses of the enterprise, the presence of liabilities. Documents are drawn up for a specific reporting period (month, quarter, half year, year). Accounting statements are mandatory provided to state bodies (tax inspectorate, Social Insurance Fund, Pension Fund), some employees, investors, and contractors. It is necessary for informed economic decision-making, determining the direction of the company‚Äôs development, and analyzing the effectiveness of its activities.

In many ways, specific types of reporting depend on the form of activity of the enterprise, on the tax system. So, the main unified forms are the following: ‚ÄúBalance sheet‚Äù and its annex, ‚ÄúStatement of cash flows‚Äù, ‚ÄúStatement of changes in equity‚Äù and ‚ÄúStatement of profit and loss‚Äù.

In addition to these documents, there are a number of others, no less laborious and voluminous. However, it should be noted that such forms are required for the individual entrepreneurs and organizations who are working on a common system of taxation (OCHO). There are also a number of preferential systems, such as the simplified tax system (USN), the unified agricultural tax (Unified agricultural tax), or the single imputed income tax (UTII). For enterprises enjoying such benefits, the financial statements required to be provided by state bodies are significantly reduced, it is limited to only 2-3 documents for the reporting period. This applies to forms established by the state. But the management of a particular company may require accounting to prepare additional reports for more efficient management of the enterprise.

The opinion of many start-up entrepreneurs is erroneous that if the activities of their company are not carried out, then they are spared the execution and submission of reports. This is far from the case. In such cases, enterprises provide zero financial statements.



The irritation of the directors and accountants of the companies over the huge queues at the tax office or, for example, at the Pension Fund, seems quite natural. You have to lose a lot of time, strength and nerves before you get an appointment with the inspector. Significantly simplifies life electronic financial statements. Almost all forms in all government agencies can be submitted via the Internet. To do this, you need to purchase specialized software or contact the services of an intermediary company. It is worth noting that when sending documents, an electronic digital signature is required, and the data transfer itself is carried out through secure communication channels. This means that all information is under reliable protection.
