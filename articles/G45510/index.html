How to get there. Transport

From the capital by rail or road, you can directly go straight to the Sun Holiday Beach Club 2 * hotel. Tunis-Carthage, the country's main international airport, is 65 km away. Travel time by bus takes about 2 and a half hours. The train goes faster, but costs more. From the airport of Monastir, an hour and a half by car.

From the hotel you can easily reach both Hammamet itself and the neighboring resort village of Nabeul. Near the hotel there is a bus stop and a train station where a local train stops. The latter consists of two comfortable wagons equipped with air conditioning. The cost of a ticket to Nabeul is half a dinar. The timetable is available at the hotel receptionist.

Hotel Description

The hotel was opened in 1986 and is located on the second coastline. The level of service is 2 stars, the number of rooms is 160. The hotel is located in two three-story buildings. Every year before the start of the tourist season, cosmetic repairs are carried out. The surrounding area is decorated in the form of a terrace. Tropical trees create a very pleasant atmosphere in Sun Holiday Beach Club 2 *. Photos are very picturesque. It seems that the hotel is located in the South African jungle.

The hotel features a heated outdoor pool. At the site - umbrellas and sunbeds. For families with children there is a separate pool, a playground, a children's mini-club. The restaurant can provide a child seat. Babysitting services are available at an extra charge. The staff is very attentive, but not annoying. All employees speak English. No pets allowed at the hotel.





Hotel rooms

The ceilings in the rooms are high. Each room has a spacious balcony. The furniture in the rooms includes a double bed, a table, a spacious wardrobe, and a mirror. If you wish, you can put an extra bed / cot or even a bunk bed for children. The floor is tiled. Guestrooms have pool, garden, or sea views.

Bed linen is changed once a week, towels - every day. Daily maids carry out wet cleaning. Air conditioning is open until September 15th. Each room has a TV and a direct dial telephone; international calls are connected at an additional cost. The voltage in the mains is 220 V, 50 Hz.

Chefs prepare mainly Mediterranean and Tunisian dishes. Depending on the type of paid services, meals are organized according to the All (all inclusive) system, or HB (half board). The buffet is plentiful. Baking of exceptional quality - echoes of French tradition. The hotel has two restaurants, a cafe and a pizzeria. The Under Sun Restaurant offers a wide selection of gourmet dishes. Special menus include barbecue dishes. Secret rules of conduct

Although the population of the country is overwhelmingly Islamic, there are no such stringencies as in the United Arab Emirates. Rest on the beaches of "topless" - in the order of things. In tourist areas you can dress easily and freely. However, when visiting ancient Muslim quarters, frank clothing should be avoided.

Security is monitored almost everywhere. Even at night on the beach, the police will ask if everything is in order and wish you a pleasant stay.

Strangers in cafes and bars should not be offered to smoke a cigarette or treat them to alcohol, all the more it is better to refrain from drinking strong drinks together.

It is considered bad form to look closely at Muslim women, to look in the face of a person eating food. Arabs do not cut bread, but break it off with their hands; fatty foods are not washed down with water. Also in Tunisia, it is not customary to eat while standing or on the go and be amazed at the prayers on the street.

During Muslim fasting, Tunisians do not smoke or eat food during daylight hours. Respecting the feelings of believers, during this period it is better not to smoke or eat on the streets. But even at this reserved time, the smiling waiter of Sun Holiday Beach Club 2 * will serve guests and bring mountains of delicious food.





Sanitary standards

The epidemiological situation in the country is quite calm. Infectious and viral diseases, such as dysentery, malaria, amaryllosis (yellow fever), have long been eradicated by the efforts of the authorities. However, it is recommended that you be vaccinated against fever and malaria before traveling. For your own safety, personal hygiene should be followed.
