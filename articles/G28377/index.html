The first resident of Winter is the coachman Nikifor Matveyev. In 1743, by the Decree of the Irkutsk Chancellery, they were assigned from the Bratsk prison to the Ziminsky Stanets to keep the wagon race for seven-wage salaries in good condition.

Throughout the second half of the 18th century and throughout the 19th city, Winter developed as an attractive village. A worker and an exiled people settled here - builders, railway workers, prisoners. In 1878, the village was the center of the rural community. It also included the village of Ukhtuy and Khulgunuy Zaimka. Until now, the museum of local lore has preserved photographs of convicts - builders of the railway, the arrival of the first steam locomotive in 1889, and the wagon - the church.





The year 1891 marked the beginning of the construction of the Trans-Siberian Railway. A locomotive depot, workshops, station, and residential village were built.

The new status of the settlement was assigned by the Soviet government. Since 1917, the city of Zima has stood on the Oka River at the confluence of the Zima River.

Geographic feature

The territory is about 53 square meters. m. The regional center of Irkutsk is located 251 km to the southeast. The relief is a plain slightly inclined to the Oka River, in places heavily boggy, with a high occurrence of groundwater. In the territory of Winter, permafrost lenses, aggressive waters, floaters, heavily podzolized soils lie. Seismic indicators - 6 points.





The sharply continental climate determines the temperature in winter up to -40 degrees, in the summer - up to +30 degrees. The frost-free period lasts 90-93 days. The first frost starts in early September and ends in early June. Precipitation is 44 mm per year, most (60%) in the summer.

Deposits are represented by sand and gravel mixtures, brick loams, coal, gas, rock salt, sand, and limestone.

Economy

Today, the city of Zima is the center of the administrative region, a major station of the Ukrainian Railways. The basis of the economy is formed by the enterprises of railway transport: railcar and locomotive depot, communication distance, track distances, Zima railway station of the Trans-Siberian Railway. All of them are members of Russian Railways.

In addition, there are private sawmills and woodworking enterprises. They are based on the basis of the former concrete products, LDK and small sawmills. The Ziminsky Chemical Plant now belongs to the city of Sayansk and is called Sayankhimplast OJSC.

The Okinskaya poultry farm is one of the largest producers of poultry meat, eggs, livestock products (meat, milk), and grain in the country.

Utilities are provided by ten utilities.
