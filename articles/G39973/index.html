Glory Park

Wonderful nature, a unique panorama of the left bank, monuments, an obelisk that reaches a height of 27 m and, of course, the Eternal Flame are the main attractions of the park. This is a living reminder to each person about the feat and courage of the defenders of the homeland during the Great Patriotic War. The memorial is an alley with the names of the heroes who sacrificed their lives for the freedom of a free people, majestic Kiev always honors their names. The Glory Park attracts and enchants its visitors with a unique panorama: the banks of the Dnieper are buried in greenery, and the gold of the Lavra temples is dazzling with its splendor. Note that this natural complex is very clean, juicy greens grow everywhere. So if you just want to lie on the grass and enjoy the beauties - this place is perfect for you.

A bit of history

The natural complex was founded in 1894, then it bore the name of commandant Anosov. It was this man who raised money for its construction. But the fate of the park is rather complicated - it lasted for a short 5 years and fell into decay, was abandoned. Kievans held concerts here, then broke a cemetery, but nature made its own adjustments. A powerful landslide destroyed this complex. But already in 1957, a memorial of Eternal Glory was opened on this place - on May 9, thousands of people flock here to honor the memory of the fallen heroes. You can visit the Holodomor Museum, where you will discover the long-suffering history of the Stalin era.

One of the most successful viewing platforms in Kiev is located in this park: stock up with powerful binoculars - and you will have an amazing view of the left bank of the city.





Victory Park

In the distant 1965, this park was born, but in 2004 it underwent many changes, and now you can see a beautiful picture that amazes with its grandeur and beauty. The complex is a real symbol of history, which will tell about the glorious exploits of the heroes who defended the country from fascist invaders. In the park you will see a memorial that tells about the main stages of the Great Patriotic War, get acquainted with the monument dedicated to the partisans who gave their lives in the defense of the city. The mound of Immortality is a unique structure that all of Kiev is proud of. Victory Park has become a haven for the memorial, for the creation of which the earth was brought from many corners of the earth where valiant Ukrainian soldiers fought.

But do not assume that in this corner of nature there is nothing more to admire: two wonderful lakes where ducks and swans live, graceful walking bridges, a stone park and many other attractions will appeal to every visitor.

Ferris wheel, attractions and entertainment programs, roller coasters - all these entertainments will bring a lot of pleasure to their guests.

The most modern monument (2013) can be considered a sculpture in honor of the defenders of the border of the Motherland - a border guard warrior with a faithful dog.

Shevchenko park

Kiev parks are very diverse, have different infrastructure and boast all kinds of entertainment. But one of the most comfortable complexes, perhaps, can be called Shevchenko Park. Kiev has created a real masterpiece: despite its small size, this corner of nature has become a favorite vacation spot for citizens. The history of its creation is very interesting: it is located near the university, and when the institution was visited by the emperor of Brazil Don Pedro, he was horrified that such an unsightly wasteland, on which cows grazed, flaunted near the main entrance of the institution. And so this square came into being, which was laid back in 1890.

The famous gardener Karl Christian very skillfully approached the arrangement of space. He was able to create a park that harmoniously connected with the botanical garden and emphasized the greatness of the university. Two alleys merged into a duet in the center of the park, and in 1896 a monument to Nicholas the First was erected here. Walking paths were decorated with sculptures made of white stone, they delighted the eye with their purity and grace. But in 1920, the Soviet government considered them a hotbed of vulgarity and destroyed.

And only in 1939 a monument was erected here to the great writer Taras Shevchenko.

The main attraction is a unique fountain in the form of the Black Sea. The park has become a favorite place for amateurs, real tournaments are unfolding here.
