Unique sights of the Rostov region

One of the oldest settlements in Russia is Rostov the Great, one of the eight main cities of the famous Golden Ring route. The significant past of the region determined its saturation with the most ancient architectural, cultural and historical monuments. Sights of the Rostov region have become classics of Russian and world art.





Monuments of archeology

The Rostov Kremlin, built in the 17th century as the residence of the bishops of the Rostov-Yaroslavl diocese, is considered the most famous monument of the region. It is part of an architectural complex called the Bishop's House. The Kremlin is considered a particularly valuable object of cultural heritage of the peoples of the Russian Federation. The achievement of world-class culture is the wooden and stone buildings of Rostov, belonging to the XVIII-XX centuries, as well as the famous Rostov belfry, each of the 13 bells of which sounds in a special way. Their harmonious chord for many centuries was considered the standard. Rostov ringing was recorded by notes by Archpriest Aristarch of Israel, a famous Campanologist of the XIX century, which ensured their safety. The bells of ancient Rostov fell silent in 1928, but in the 60s of the last century, the old city ringer was able to revive the bells.

No less valuable are such sights of the Rostov region as Avraamiev Epiphany Monastery and Spaso-Yakovlevsky monastery, architectural the ensemble of which, due to the mixing of different styles, seems like a fabulous miracle city, harmoniously flowing into the lakeside landscape.

Cities of the Rostov region also demonstrate a number of interesting monuments of architecture and history. Noteworthy is the main attraction of Novocherkassk - the Ascension Cathedral, which was founded in 1805 at the foundation of the city. Many unique monuments of military-engineering art can be seen after visiting Azov. Don Troy

Sights of the Rostov region are unique and priceless. Many local and alien ancient tribes left their traces in the Don region. So, on the outskirts of modern Rostov-on-Don in the 50s of the last century, the ruins of the most ancient in Eastern Europe Liventsovskaya fortress were discovered. Historians called this discovery a world sensation. The find dates back to the 17th century BC. Liventsovskaya fortress was erected by tribes of the North Caucasian catacomb culture. Excavations were carried out on the territory of the former structure. The archaeological complex of 8 hectares is called the Don Troy. Settlements of people were found here, from the Copper-Stone Age to the Middle Ages, Scythian settlements with burials. Unfortunately, the fortress is on the verge of complete destruction. In January 2014, entrepreneur A. Isakov presented a project aimed at saving a historical monument: they want to make the Liventsov fortress a center of archaeological tourism. After all, this is the only fortress in Europe of the Bronze Age, which is several hundred years older than the legendary Troy, sung by Homer.





Edge culture

Museums of the Rostov Region have an important influence on the development of the cultural life of the region. The unsurpassed collections of Sarmatian and Scythian gold, ethnographic and archaeological collections are demonstrated to their visitors by the Rostov Regional Museum of Local Lore. The Museum of Fine Arts of regional significance is also popular; its collections include unique works of ancient Russian and modern art.



The Taganrog literary and historical-architectural museum-reserve deserves special attention. The Tanais Archaeological Museum-Reserve, located on the outskirts of Nedvigovka, Myasnikovsky District, is unique in its own way. Here are the reconstructions of ancient buildings, lapidary inscriptions - almost the only sources that illuminate reliable information about events two thousand years ago, the history of the Lower Don. Permanent and thematic exhibitions work. A special pride of Tanais is considered to be the unique hall of "Amphora standards", which is an open repository.

Nature reserves and landscapes



The sights of the Rostov Region are not limited to historical and cultural monuments; it is also famous for its nature reserves and magnificent landscapes, many of which are endowed with the status of natural monuments of regional and federal significance. In 16 districts of the region there are 27 nature reserves with various meanings and over 100 protected natural areas.

The Veselovskoe Reservoir and Manych-Gudilo, which are wetlands, are especially notable. The latter is a kind of relic. This once huge pond in prehistoric times connected the Black and Caspian Seas. On its lands in the Repairnensky and Oryol districts there is the Rostovsky steppe reserve, where on pristine sections of the southern steppes you can see huge colonies of various birds. One of its main attractions is the free herd of horses that lives on the island of Water.
