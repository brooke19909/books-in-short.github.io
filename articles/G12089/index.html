APC uninterruptible power supplies. Characteristics and reviews

APC, whose existence legally ended in 2007, has still left its mark as one of the best brands of uninterruptible power supplies. Due to its takeover by Schneider Electric and the prospect of development, it was decided to leave the APC brand itself.

Uninterruptible power supplies

The task of uninterruptible power supply includes maintaining the operability of units, mechanisms and devices from unexpected power outages or power surges.

General design of autonomous power units

The main part of the UPS is the batteries, which accumulate a charge when the power is supplied from the mains and gives the consumer in the event that the circuit is broken for some reason.





To control batteries and power, electronic circuits are used. Also, with their help, the UPS is expanded with various additional functions, such as absorption of noise, radiation and stabilization of voltage surges.

APC Uninterruptible Power Supply Overview

APC devices can be divided into several categories, depending on the conditions under which the device will be operated.

Back-UPS Series

This line of APC uninterruptible power supplies is designed for residential and office consumers. The list of protected devices includes game consoles, computers, and other equipment.





The UPS is capable of providing power to the equipment for some time, protecting it from surges and overvoltages. The design of buildings and their designs are different, making it easy to integrate equipment into the interior.

Presented Series Properties

The APC Back-UPS line of uninterruptible power supplies is characterized by the function of carrying out various actions for self-diagnosis and determination of battery wear.

For interactivity and user interaction, a number of LED and sound indicators are used.





Security systems checked and tested by special services.

BE400-RS

One of the simplest representatives of uninterruptible power supplies 220 V from APC. A maximum output power of 240 W will protect one small household device, such as a laptop or TV.

The UPS is able to stably operate and equalize the voltage in the network in the range from 180 to 266 V.

The battery of the device is lead-acid, with the use of thickening electrolyte, which guarantees protection against leaks. The battery is maintenance-free, that is, when a failure occurs, you only have to change it.

The notification panel is quite ascetic - it has only two indicators - network and battery operation. There are also two special sound signals - for a dead battery and overload.



The APC BE400 - RS uninterruptible power supply has several levels of protection. For example, there is a noise filter, voltage surge limitation and others. If there is a need to protect communication lines, the device has ports for telephone and network - RJ-11 and RJ-45, respectively.

BX650CI

A more powerful and functional uninterruptible power supply 220 V from APC, in comparison with the previous model. Its output power is 390 watts.

The voltage range in which the device is capable of working is 140-300 V. The battery is a similar type to the previous one - that is, maintenance-free and with a thickened electrolyte.

The control and display panel is slightly expanded - there is a notification about normal operation, the battery, its replacement and overload. The sound signal does not differ from the previous model.

BX1400U-GR

A more technological instance of IPC APC. The output power is 700 watts. What should be enough to protect a personal stationary computer and any small device additionally.





The model is capable of operating in the range from 150 to 280 volts. The battery is a standard solution for this line of UPS - maintenance-free with condensed electrolyte.

APC Smart UPS Uninterruptible Power Supplies

This series is designed for comprehensive protection of powerful server solutions and networks. One power supply can handle up to 2100 watts of power.

SC620I APC Smart-UPS SC 620VA 230V

Designed to protect the power supply of entry-level servers, workstations and small network devices.

The maximum output power of this model is 390 watts. The voltage range in which it is able to work is from 160 to 286 V.

On the front side is the power button of the device, an indicator of operation from the network, from the battery, its replacement and overload.





To control the device from a PC, there is a DB-9 port with an RS-232 connector.

SMX 1000I APC Smart-UPS X 1000VA LCD

This representative of the UPS is made in an unusual form factor, in the form of a rack. One of the interesting features of the model is the ability to connect additional external batteries to it, which increase the total battery life of the protected devices.

The output power is 800 W, which is quite enough for use both for domestic purposes and for small offices.

Management is carried out using the console with a liquid crystal display. There are USB and SmartSlot connectors for connecting to a PC.

SUA750RMI1U APC UPS

The model is designed to fit in a 19-inch server rack. Accordingly, this UPS is able to protect small network solutions and configurations.

There are three different ports for connecting the source to a personal computer.

A set of LEDs and sounds is used for indication.

Back-UPS PRO Series

This line is a cross between Smart-UPS and Back-UPS.

Products can be used both in small industrial enterprises and in everyday life. They have built-in systems of protection against voltage surges, noise filtering. Many of their models have built-in support for remotely controlling the network and connecting to a PC for this.

APC Back-UPS Pro 1200 VA

The output power of the model is 720 watts. The voltage range that the UPS can equalize is between 176-294 V. A full recharge cycle is 8 hours.

The kit comes with a USB cable for connecting to a PC, as well as special software.

The unit itself has an LCD display with a large list of indicators and settings.

The most commonly used ports are for securing the network and telephone lines.
