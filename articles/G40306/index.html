Malgrat de Mar

The small old town is located on the very border of the provinces of Costa Brava and Costa del Maresme, just 62 kilometers from the magnificent Barcelona. Once it was a fishing village, and at the beginning of the XX century the population did not exceed 4000 inhabitants. Today it is a popular tourist center with more than 18 thousand people. Malgrat de Mar continues to be actively built up. The town is divided into two parts - historical and modern. They are connected by the Passy Maritim embankment.

In the old town with stone streets you can see several attractions. This is an old market, partially preserving its original appearance, the Church of St. Nicholas, the town hall building, built in 1913 in the Art Nouveau style, an old mansion of the 16th century, which today houses a library, a watchtower, built in the 16th-17th centuries. The new city is lined with entertainment and shopping centers, trendy restaurants and modern hotels.

Malgrat de Mar has a wide selection of hotels and private apartments. The best bars and restaurants are concentrated in the historical part, night clubs and discos are on the coast. The city has magnificent green areas for recreation. Especially popular is the recently opened Francesc Macia Park. The panorama of the resort and its environs opens from the hill, at the foot of which lies Castell Park. You can climb the hill along the paths leading from the park, or by cable car.



Malgrat de Mar forms a single resort area with a developed infrastructure with the cities of Santa Susanna, Pineda, Calella.
