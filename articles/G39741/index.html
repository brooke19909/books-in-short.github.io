What is Brussels famous for? Town Hall Decorating the City

The charming capital of Belgium with an interesting history is among the major cultural centers of Europe. The ancient city is of great interest to tourists who will fully appreciate the wealth of architectural heritage. Each passing era left its mark, and the central square is considered the heart of Brussels from the Middle Ages.

Grand Place is not in vain called the most elegant in Europe. Without exaggeration, the buildings surrounding it with a square are considered to be real masterpieces of world architecture. The historical center that arose on the site of dried swamps, included in the UNESCO World Heritage List, is famous for the city hall Hotel de Ville, which occupies most of the square.

Asymmetrical building

Well-preserved after enemy attacks and numerous fires, the building is crowned with a high sentinel tower, located not in the middle. However, this is not a mistake, but the result of the fact that several architects were engaged in construction at different times.

The most beautiful building of the city ‚Äã‚Äãwas erected in several passes. The left part with the bell tower was built in 1420, after 30 years the right side appeared, which initially turned out to be slightly shorter than the old one, and in 1455 a tower 96 meters high appeared. The Brussels Town Hall is therefore interesting because its building turned out to be asymmetrical. At the end of the 18th century, two baroque hind wings appeared.

Interesting decor

A five-meter-high figure of the archangel Michael, which protects Brussels from troubles, is crowned with gold openwork spire of the tower. The town hall, recognized as a historical and architectural monument, acquired its current form in the 19th century, when it was restored, and then many sculptural objects were added: here you can see the images of saints, horseback riders, heroes of ancient legends, rulers of the Middle Ages, drunken monks. But it is best to once admire the decor with your own eyes.



Architectural pearl

The interior is amazing with special beauty. Tourists die out at the sight of tapestries woven with golden threads, luxurious mirrors with wooden carvings, paintings by famous artists. There is also a magnificent hall in which memorable weddings take place. Therefore, many lovers who want to celebrate wedding ceremonies outside the box come to Brussels for positive emotions.



The town hall, which has historical value, is known for the fact that its architecture harmoniously combines two areas: Gothic and classical styles. This is especially noticeable in the interior decoration of the building, in which to this day there are meetings of the city council. Town Hall: interesting facts

  • The fountains in the courtyard represent the two main rivers of Belgium.
  • The most popular Gothic creatures of the town hall are gargoyles. They are often confused with chimeras, since the differences between these monsters are very arbitrary. This is not just an element of architectural design that performs artistic functions: frightening creatures with a monkey body and wings cover the drains of the structure or themselves remove moisture through an open mouth.
  • Every even year, from mid-August, an observation deck opens, from which the city appears in full view. A wonderful panorama cannot be missed if you want to enjoy the elegant beauty of ancient Brussels. It was then that huge carpets of fresh flowers flourished in the square. A delightful sight attracts thousands of tourists from all over the world.




Even a week is not enough to explore all the main attractions that Brussels is famous for the whole world. The Town Hall Hotel de Ville and the square, where an amazing masterpiece is located, are an excellent opportunity to get acquainted with the culture of the city and the whole country.
