Bavaro, Dominican Republic: beach, hotels, holidays, reviews

Bavaro (Dominican Republic) is one of the most luxurious resorts in the country. It is considered so prestigious that it is even called royal. Here are expensive and exclusive hotels, and the resort itself is somewhat reminiscent of photo wallpaper. Tourists who come here at first cannot believe that it is real - white sand and tropical palm trees, as if in a pristine paradise. Let's get to know these amazing places closer.



Location, how to get

Bavaro (Dominican Republic) is located in the province of La Altagracia, near the famous resort of Punta Cana. It is the most southeast of the country. To get from the airport there is only ten minutes, if you use a taxi. A regular bus can also be reached, and it will take you no more than half an hour. In addition, most resort hotels offer a shuttle service for guests. The distance from the hub of Punta Cana is twenty-five kilometers. The resort of Playa Bavaro stretches for several kilometers to Macau itself. It is in this place that the Atlantic Ocean merges with the Caribbean Sea. The resort of Bavaro is connected with the rest of the country by excellent roads, so if you want to go on a shopping trip or an excursion in a rented car, the trip will be very pleasant.

When is it better to come

The weather at this resort, as well as throughout the Dominican Republic, is very constant. The average air temperature is about 25-27 degrees Celsius. Heat occurs in the spring-summer-autumn period, that is, from April to December. Therefore, some tourists believe that a vacation in the Dominican Republic in March is what you need. At this time, there is still not too much heat and no rain. True, during this period there are many people. The resort can be loaded. Therefore, make sure that you have a ticket to the Dominican Republic on hand. Its price may be still high. In the west of Bavaro there are mountains that protect the area from strong winds. Therefore, the hurricane season is practically absent here. The rains at the resort are strong, but short-term. Description of Bavaro

A quarter of a century ago, these places were completely deserted. Bavaro (Dominican Republic) then was sections of impregnable rainforest with wild beaches. And now it is all built up with luxurious tourist complexes. But the forests of coconut palms, despite the abundance of civilization, now stretch along the coast for many kilometers. In addition, you will not be bored at this resort. Golf courses, tennis courts and horse races - traditional entertainment for aristocrats - will surely appeal to you. And in the evenings you will be whirling to the brim in the rhythms of salsa and merengue, as well as dozens of national versions of these dances. Shops here are expensive, and sell mainly Dominican or Haitian souvenirs. But if you liked something, it is better to go to Punta Cana for shopping - there it will all be several times cheaper. The cuisine of the Dominican Republic (Bavaro is no exception) combines the traditions of Europe and Africa, using local products. Many praise the local rum "Imperial." Bavaro has its own dress code - in churches, casinos, some restaurants and discos will not let you in in clothes with short sleeves or in shorts. And women can go to temples only with their heads covered.



Beaches

Swimming in Bavaro is absolutely safe. The beaches are surrounded by a coral reef. He made the water very calm, protecting the lagoon from the waves of the ocean. There are no sharks or other dangerous inhabitants of the sea. But a lot of colorful bright fish. No wonder diving enthusiasts come here. By the quality of the sand, any Bavaro beach will give odds to any other Dominican resort. The water here is amazingly clean and clear. Fish and marine animals can be seen even at a depth of several meters. And the sand is very fine, soft and silky. You probably will not need special sunshades because the coconut palms growing literally everywhere create a very comfortable shady zone. There are plenty of water activities on the beach. However, in no case do not swim in the open sea - the currents are so strong that you will be carried into the ocean.





What hotels are here

Bavaro hotels are huge palaces with parks and villas. There are no small private hotels. There are about thirty of these large complexes in Bavaro. These are buildings and bungalows hiding in the shade. Most of these hotels have private beaches, and usually they occupy the most delightful stretches of the coast. All hotels, without exception, operate on an all inclusive system. And it can include not only free alcohol, but also expensive cigarettes. Some hotels of the Dominican Republic (including Bavaro) specialize in relaxation and solitude of clients, while others, on the contrary, offer them an active holiday. The most popular hotels are the Grand Palladium Palace Resort and Spa with loft suites, the Royal Punta Cana with a singing fountain, the cozy Lakshari Rosa Hermosa with apartments and the Ocean Blue and Beach with the most beautiful pools. By the way, you can build here only on projects approved by environmentalists. The construction of buildings or structures that could harm the local nature is strictly prohibited.



Attractions in Bavaro

Even in this luxurious resort you will surely want to see the sights. And there are enough of them. For example, the village of Cabeza de Toro. This settlement is built in a typical Dominican island style. Residents here are engaged in art crafts. Jeep rides around Bavaro (Dominican Republic) and boat trips to Saona Island are also popular with tourists. Not far from the resort is the Cave Oleg Bat House Cave, where different types of bats are found. She is also known for stalactites and stalagmites of fantastic colors. By the way, the shopping centers of Bavaro are also of interest to tourists, and not just for those wishing to purchase something. Vacation with children

Have you decided to come to Bavaro (Dominican Republic) with your whole family? Wonderful! After all, it is an ideal place to relax with children. In addition to bathing, sunbathing, wonderful desserts and exotic experiences, you can give kids the opportunity to visit Manati Park. Here they can swim with dolphins, watch shows of dancing horses, talking parrots, trained dolphins and even sharks, watch the most colorful and interesting wild animals and reptiles (iguanas, snakes, crocodiles). Such an excursion will leave them a lot of impressions. Moreover, this natural pearl is located among beautiful tropical plants. It will be interesting here for adults. Bars, restaurants and shops are also in the park. In addition, an ethnic settlement of the Taino Indians, Otra Banda, is organized here. Its inhabitants are engaged in fishing and make souvenirs for visitors.



Reviews for Bavaro

Tourists note that this resort is ideal for spending a romantic vacation or a family vacation. No wonder the Dominican Republic, Bavaro in particular, is a popular destination for travelers who travel to the Caribbean. Travelers praise not only beaches, hotels, the sea, but also the local cuisine, especially seafood. True, at first they are somewhat discouraged by the way the locals communicate, because, speaking English and Spanish, they often use Native American words, so even knowing these languages, you will not always understand what it is about. Still the inhabitants of Bavaro often carry firearms with them - they are allowed here. But you should not be afraid of this, since life here is completely safe. By carrying weapons, locals simply emphasize their high social status.
