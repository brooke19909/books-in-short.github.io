We are in Lycia, gentlemen!



The territory of the resort of Kemer, where the Larissa Vista Hotel 4 is located, is located on a land with an ancient and glorious history. In the XII century BC. e. the Lycian king Sarpedon with his mighty cousin Glaucus became powerful allies of Troy. The great Homer mentioned them more than once in his Iliad. However, even before the epochal battle, pirate bays and hiding places were hidden from view on the rocky shores of this coast. One of them was located in a truly wonderful place, reliably sheltered by nature itself.

Famous grotto

It's about the Dalmash grotto. Quite by chance, industrialists, developing quarries, discovered an underground passage leading into it. The age of this creation of nature, decorated with stalactites and stalagmites, is about 15 thousand years. The "main hall" of this cave is quite impressive: about 15 meters in height and about 10 meters in length. For tourists, the beauty of the grotto is emphasized by special lighting and sounding music. Its unique microclimate is healing, including for patients with asthma. At a constant temperature of +22 0 C, the humidity in the grotto is 98%.

Pamukkale

Guests of the Larissa Vista Hotel 4 * Standard often visit it. Even in ancient times, when Turkey (or Asia Minor, as it was called before) under the rule of the Roman Empire, another unique, thanks to healing mountain springs, healing place was used - Pamukkale, or Cotton Castle. From afar, it stands out from the surrounding green with its pearl-pearl color. By the nature of its mineralogical formation, this is a cascade of travertine baths with healing thermal water of different temperatures. Its main pool, from ancient times called Cleopatra's pool, was used by the ancient Romans, equipping it under their famous baths. Unfortunately, the earthquake destroyed the ancient columns and porticoes decorating it.
