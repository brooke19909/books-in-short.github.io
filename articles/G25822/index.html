Transfer contacts using the Google service

In the event that a personal computer has access to the Network, you can export data through a Google account. Before transferring contacts from your Android phone to Gmail, you need to synchronize phone books.





This is a fairly practical operation, since you can make any changes to your contacts, even when the phone is not at hand. The application not only allows you to work with information in terms of copying and moving, but also helps to restore all contacts in case of loss of the phone or accidental deletion.

The Google Drive service is the easiest way to transfer contacts from Android devices. To do this, just select the Gmail item, go to the "Contacts" tab, select "Phonebook" and export or import data.

Export contacts without an internet connection

If your computer does not have the ability to enter the Network, then there is the option of copying the phone book using wireless protocols. In this case, the Bluetooth module is considered a priority.

  • click "accept" after the request appears on the receiving device and wait for the transfer process to complete.

Transfer contacts using SD and SIM cards

This is one of the oldest and most proven ways to transfer data, but it is used extremely rarely, and there are very good reasons for this. The main disadvantage of this method is that when exporting contacts, restrictions are introduced on the number of characters in the name and the number of contacts themselves.

In order to perform this procedure, just insert the SD card into the phone and export through the drop-down menu. The same actions must be done in the case of a SIM card. Then rearrange the media to another phone or device (any USB modem is suitable) and import the data through the contact settings. The maximum number of transferred contacts in this case cannot be more than two hundred names.
