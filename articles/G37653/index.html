Where is the Adler-Perm train route

The Adler - Perm train route is located on the busiest passenger traffic direction. Many thousands of people flock annually from the northern regions to the Black Sea coast. And most of the passenger traffic traditionally falls on the railway. Air transportation at present cannot compete with it in terms of price parameters. And there is no reason to believe that this situation is capable of fundamentally changing in the foreseeable future.



To the south by rail

The flow of passengers in a southerly direction has a pronounced seasonal character. It almost stops in the winter, but with the beginning of warm days, an increasing number of residents of the northern Urals dream of speeding over the Perm ‚Äì Adler distance as soon as possible. The train, the route of which is indicated in the railway schedule number 353 \ 354, regularly helps to overcome this distance. And after two to three weeks, it gently delivers well-rested passengers on the seashore to their native northern city. The Adler - Perm train route is well known to more than one generation of Permians, and indeed the inhabitants of the entire region. The distance to the Black Sea coast from here is quite large, it exceeds one and a half thousand kilometers. To get there, in the train car you have to spend 2 days and 15 hours. But despite this, the loss of popularity of this railway route does not threaten at all.



The route of the train Adler - Perm

At the height of the season, tickets to Adler have to be purchased long before departure. Of course, not only the residents of the Perm Territory and the northern Cisurals travel to this south by train. The route of the Adler - Perm train passes through a number of cities and large regional centers of the Urals, Volga Region, and Krasnodar Territory. The country's railway network has historically been formed so that most of the trunk lines pass through the capital's railway junction. The majority of routes are also formed there, which ensure the transportation of passengers in a southerly direction. This circumstance explains the large shortage of transport capacities at the height of the summer season. From the large cities of the Russian periphery it is much more difficult to get to the Black Sea coast in summer than from Moscow. And therefore, we can confidently assert that the Adler - Perm train route successfully passed through such large provincial cities as Izhevsk, Ulyanovsk, Saratov and Krasnodar. In these cities, often additional cars are added to the main train. At the height of the summer season, their number sometimes reaches eighteen.



How much is a trip to the sea

The fare on the entire route from Perm to Adler is 3245 rubles in the reserved seat and 6546 rubles in the compartment car. Recently, the railway management has been making active efforts in that direction to make the trip to the sea as comfortable as possible. On the way, passengers are offered hot meals. And in order for two and a half days to fly unnoticed, an entertainment program for adults and children is provided.
