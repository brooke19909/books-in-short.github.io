Who is Democritus

Democritus (lat. Demokritos) is an ancient Greek philosopher. He was born around 460 BC and lived until 360 BC. The main merit of Democritus is the atomistic doctrine, the founder of which he became.

Nobody knows the exact date of birth of this philosopher. Some scientists of those times claimed that he was born in 460 BC. e., others - in 470 BC. e. In this case, it is impossible to say exactly who is right.





Of course, the biography of Democritus cannot be fully described. There are many inaccurate facts. However, with confidence we can talk about the origin of this philosopher from a wealthy family.

Proceedings of Democritus

The name Democritus is associated with the emergence of one of the fundamental theories of philosophy - atomism. This theory combines such sciences as physics, cosmology, epistemology, psychology and ethics. It is generally accepted that this theory also united the problems of the three main ancient Greek philosophical schools: Pythagorean, Elean and Miletus.

Scientists claim that Democritus at one time became the author of more than 70 different treatises. The names of these works are given in the writings of Diogenes Laertius - he wrote more than other scientists about who Democritus is. As a rule, treatises were tetralogies in various sciences - mathematics, physics, ethics, literature, language, applied sciences and even medicine.

Separately, it is worth noting that Democritus was considered the author of the Chaldean Book and On the Sacred Inscriptions in Babylon. This is due to the legend that was created about the training and travels of the philosopher.

Materialism of Democritus

This philosopher is the most prominent representative of atomistic materialism. Democritus argued that the entire surrounding world according to sensory perception, changeable, diverse. Everything consists of matter and emptiness. It was then that the term ‚Äúatom‚Äù was first introduced as the smallest indivisible component of all things. The doctrine of Democritus says that the whole world consists of atoms that move in the void.





This philosopher had his own theory of the appearance of the Earth in the center of a vortex, which was formed from collisions of atoms, different in weight, size and shape. Since the atom is material, indivisible and eternal, there are a large number of atoms, different in weight and shape. They themselves are devoid of content, and together form changeable things due to the constant movement in the void.

Democritus applied the postulates of atomism to the doctrine of life and soul. According to his writings, any living creature has a soul, but each to a different degree. Life and death are the result of the union or decomposition of atoms. Democritus said that the soul is a union of special "fiery" atoms, which, in its essence, is also temporary. Based on these arguments, the theory of the immortality of the soul was rejected by them.
