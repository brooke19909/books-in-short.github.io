How to get from St. Petersburg to Kronstadt. Sights of Kronstadt

Kronstadt is a port city visited by thousands of tourists every year. It is located on the island of Kotlin and several islands of the Gulf of Finland. The one who visits the Northern capital for the first time, is certainly interested in how to get from St. Petersburg to Kronstadt. There are many attractions in this city, once important historical events took place here.

A few years ago, the question of how to get from St. Petersburg to Kronstadt, there was only one answer - on the ferry. The dam was opened in 2011. How can I get to Kronstadt from St. Petersburg today? There are several ways.

By bus

Local residents, answering guests' questions about how to get from St. Petersburg to Kronstadt, often recommend the most budgetary way. You can get there by bus or minibus. By metro you need to get to the station "Old Village", located in the north of the city. The legendary port city runs bus number 101.

You can get from St. Petersburg to Kronstadt by bus No. K405, which leaves daily from the Chernaya Rechka metro station. The passenger, leaving at the Roshal Square stop, sees in front of him an unusual piece of architecture - the Kronstadt Naval Cathedral. Buses depart at intervals of 15-20 minutes, on weekends a little less often.

From the station "Prospect of education" every day from 6.00 to 23.00 bus number K407. You need to get out at the Grazhdanskaya Street stop.

Their southern regions of St. Petersburg in Kronstadt can not be reached without a transfer. Only through Lomonosov. From the station of this city, bus number 175 leaves every day at intervals of 25-30 minutes.

Taxi

This is the easiest way to get to Kronstadt. Fortunately, today, a tourist arriving in an unknown city does not waste time looking for a number by which you can order a taxi. He downloads a mobile application, in a matter of minutes the car drives up to the hotel. The cost of the trip is about a thousand rubles.

Excursions to Kronstadt from St. Petersburg

Kronstadt is a real open-air museum. Many travel agencies offer sightseeing tours of the port city. This is a very convenient way to visit Kronstadt. In just a few hours you can see the main architectural monuments.

Travel companies offer several options for excursions. Traveling to Kronstadt by bus lasts 4-5 hours. It starts in the city center, for example, on Nevsky Prospect. The route includes a segment of the ring road in the north of St. Petersburg, a dam. On the way, tourists see the northern forts, which have long fallen into decay, but once were solid fortifications. Upon arrival in the city, they will explore the Kronstadt attractions, which are listed below.

There is also an excursion program, including a boat trip. This option will cost a little more. If a bus tourist trip costs 1.000 rubles, then an excursion with a boat trip from 1500 rubles.

How to get from St. Petersburg to Kronstadt, we found out. But what is worth seeing in this city for a man if he arrives here on his own?

At first glance, Kronstadt may seem like a stern military garrison. A deceptive impression is created by marinas, docks, ships. However, there are real works of architecture here. For example, the Naval and Italian cathedrals. Attention deserves a cast-iron bridge with a length of about two hundred meters.

The Blue Bridge, one of the main attractions of Kronstadt, began to be built in the late 18th century. The construction was completed in 1801.

Kronstadt would not be on the map of Russia, if not for Peter the Great. Several local attractions are associated with his name. The city has a square named after him. In the center of Petrovsky Park there is a monument to the first Russian emperor. And the oldest park in Kronstadt - the Summer Garden - appeared here back in the time of Peter I.
