Garden paths

Whoever has a summer cottage, you can put beautiful paths out of stones. They will not only serve as decor, but also perform a practical function for quite some time. You can use both natural stones and artificial.

Simple instructions on what can be made of stone:

  • First you need to prepare a picture of the tracks on paper.
  • For convenience, sort selected stones according to size, color and shape.
  • After preparing the foundation, cement is poured onto the marked track, on which the stones are fixed.
  • Pebbles are laid flat or on the ribs, making sure that the stones are as tight as possible to each other.
  • If the material is of different sizes, then large specimens are placed first, and then small elements complete the composition.
  • Care should be taken to create a decorative border.
  • After laying the stones, let the cement dry for 24 hours, after which the entire path is again poured with liquid cement mortar to smooth out all the cracks.

In conclusion, the entire canvas is covered with a film, and until the concrete is completely dry, do not walk along the path. It usually takes 10-15 days.

Artificial stone is a good alternative

In fact, artificial stone is a special material that mimics the texture of natural material. It was widely used in the construction industry. With its help, wall cladding is carried out, both outside and inside. Typical advantages include:

  • in terms of beauty, the material is in no way inferior to its natural counterpart;
  • a light weight;
  • material is easy to process if necessary;
  • high profitability.

Nevertheless, there are disadvantages, among which a high degree of abrasion of the surface.





Only this is critical when the material is used to create tracks. In addition, over time, the coating on the stones fades under the influence of solar radiation. However, they are not so significant.

Original compositions

And what can be made of artificial stone? Such material is ideally suited for creating whole compositions in its suburban area. Only in this case it is worth adhering to simple, but important principles:

  • The structure should be located relative to only one point of contemplation.
  • The contrast between emptiness and fullness must be observed.
  • In the concept of the garden, asymmetry should be clearly traced. Japanese masters at the same time take into account the reflection of objects in the water and the shadows cast by them.
  • The number of structural elements must be odd. Traditionally, the base should be a regular heptagon.
  • It is also important to balance the horizontal and vertical lines of the structure. At the same time, there should be more stones with horizontal orientation, since there are already so many vertical objects on the site (trees, a fence, buildings, and so on).

Using these principles, you can let a dry stream in your garden or equip a small pond with real water. Small waterfalls or fountains look good. By constructing retaining walls, you can not only decorate your garden yard, this solution is relevant for areas with a slope.
