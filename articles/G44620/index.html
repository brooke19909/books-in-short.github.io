Televisions with IPS-matrix: review, rating, selection tips

The leading position in the modern market of electronics and household appliances is occupied by televisions with IPS-matrix. The popularity is due to several advantages of the technology. What IPS-TVs are listed in the ratings of the best and what to look for when choosing them - we will tell in the article.

What is an IPS matrix?

The liquid crystal IPS-matrix was developed in 1996, however, the new technology was widely used only in 2010. The name of the new generation of matrices - In-Plane Switching - is explained by the parallel arrangement of liquid crystal molecules relative to the screen plane.

IPS display technology is constantly evolving. Today, televisions with IPS-matrix have high quality, accuracy and clarity of the image, and excellent resolution.

Technology benefits

The main advantage of televisions with IPS displays is color reproduction: modern models display more than 16 million shades and colors, which allows you to transfer the most accurate and natural image. Other advantages are distinguished:

  • High level of contrast and brightness;
  • The smallest pixel grid among similar liquid crystal displays;
  • High definition image;
  • The maximum depth of white and black.

The strengths of IPS-matrices provided them not only popularity, but also the preference of the vast majority of equipment manufacturers.

IPS TVs

Modern technology markets offer a wide range of televisions with IPS displays equipped with various functions and settings. Such diversity provides a wide choice to customers, but complicates the selection process itself. This can be facilitated by the rating of televisions with IPS-matrix.

Most of the technical specifications of the TV depend on the installed matrix. The same matrix models may vary: manufacturers use different materials and components that affect the quality of the finished product.
