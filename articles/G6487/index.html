Geography of the Dominican Republic

This state is located in the West Indies and occupies several islands of the Greater Antilles archipelago and most of the island of Haiti. The history of the Dominican Republic

The words "Haiti", "Caribbean", "Dominican Republic", "Saona" give rise to the imagination of a resident of cold Russia fantasies about an incredible land with the ever-shining sun, serenity and pleasure. However, the Dominican Republic had to see different times. These lands were discovered by Christopher Columbus in 1492. Prior to this, the indigenous Indian population lived here safely. After Columbus, the Spaniards arrived in the country and occupied this territory, but soon the British and French appeared, who also claimed this paradise. For several centuries, a fierce struggle between the European colonialists took place over the islands, and these or those forces periodically won.

From the beginning of the 19th century, the liberation movement for the independence of Haiti from the invaders began. The island was divided into two states: the Republic of Haiti and the Dominican Republic. But this was not the end of the story. In 1865, the Spaniards were finally expelled from the island. The Dominican Republic was formed on the part that previously belonged to them. But from this moment begins a long series of internal upheavals and the struggle for power.

At the beginning of the 20th century, the republic became dependent on the United States due to the difficult economic situation. Almost the entire 20th century, the island was shaken by military coups, the power of dictators, the intervention of American troops. Only in 1965 the republic was able to defend its rights to independence. Elections were held, but the difficulties did not end. The country was going through times of political showdown and great economic difficulties.

By the beginning of the 20th century, a democratic regime had been established in the Dominican Republic, peaceful times had come, and business, including tourism, began to develop. Since 1945, the country has established diplomatic relations with Russia; since 2006, the flow of our tourists to the Dominican Republic has been steadily growing.





Climate of the Dominican Republic

The country is located in a zone of humid subtropical climate, in this regard, the year is divided into two seasons: dry and wet. The first lasts from October to April, at this time there is comfortable weather, almost without precipitation. In the wet period, it is often short but heavy rains, there is the possibility of storms and floods. Although there is little rainfall in July-August, the weather is rather hot. Light rains are also possible in October-November. Therefore, the high tourist season falls on the period from December to April. Although the rest of the time there are tourists who are not afraid of rains. The peculiarity of the local climate is that there is almost no temperature difference between the seasons. In August, the temperature is around + 30-33, and in winter - around + 25-27 degrees.

What to do in the Dominican Republic

The country has many opportunities for leisure activities. In addition to beaches and the usual entertainment on the beach (water rides, rental of various equipment for games), other types of outdoor activities are also offered here. No tourist can pass the Manati natural amusement park, where you can see various exotic animals and even play with dolphins. The country is famous for its sea fishing. Tourists are transported on a comfortable yacht to places where there is a lot of fish. Travelers are guaranteed not only a catch, but also a lot of vivid impressions.

For lovers of spearfishing and diving to the seabed, excursion programs are organized in the most interesting places of the underwater kingdom. The Dominican Republic is also known for its surfing, yachting, snorkeling. Also, travelers are offered a diverse cultural program, within which they can get acquainted with the unique culture, customs and cuisine of an amazing country called Dominican Republic. Saona is one of the most popular places for excursions in the republic.

Saona Island

Dominican Republic, Saona - this is the embodiment of the best ideas about beach holidays. The largest island except Haiti in the country is Saona. It is part of the national reserve, and only local residents have the right to live here, and even then in a small amount. The island is famous for its pristine and extremely beautiful nature. It was not in vain that many commercials with white beaches were filmed here. The beach of the island is famous all over the world. Here you can see more than 500 species of exotic plants and rare animals: iguanas, manatees, observe dolphins, and in season whales.
