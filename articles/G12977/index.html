Instructions and recommendations for beginners

How to learn to embroider ribbons? For beginners, the following instructions and recommendations have been developed step by step:

Step No. 1. Before starting work, you need to properly process the tapes, cutting them at an acute angle. The tape is passed through the eye of the needle, leaving a tail of about 5 cm, and fixed by piercing the needle at a distance of 7 cm from the end through the center of the tape, forming a small bundle. The length of the tape used should not be more than 30 cm. So it will not get confused and wrinkle.

Step No. 2. Next, fasten the ribbon to the wrong side of the embroidery through a flat knot. This is done by double bending the edges of the tape and puncture the central part of the ribbon tail. The tape is pulled down and tightened.

Step number 3. The main element is a straight stitch, it is important to adjust the tension and make sure that the tape does not twist.



Step No. 4. A popular embroidery element is the French knot, where you need to wrap the tape around the needle 3-4 times and try to bring it to the wrong side of the fabric. The implementation of this element will require special training.

Step No. 5. A straight stitch with a curl begins to be carried out in the same way as a regular straight stitch, and then the needle is inserted on the wrong side to the center of the tape at a distance of the length of the stitch, but do not turn the resulting curl with the wrong side out.

Step No. 6. A curved straight stitch is used in combination with a thread of the same color. It is inserted into the tape at a distance that depends on the length of the stitch, then basting is performed. The needle is removed from the ribbon on the back side, where you need to change the angle between the two stitches to get curved lines in the embroidery pattern.

To learn how to learn to embroider ribbons, first you need to master the simplest stitches. The remaining more complex combinations of this type of needlework are based on simple ones.

Fabric selection

The fabric can be chosen for work at your discretion, since embroidery with ribbons looks beautiful and neat on any basis. The type of fabric and its color should match the planned pattern. Nevertheless, you need to try not to take too dense and stiff fabrics, as it will be more difficult to stretch the tape. If satin ribbons will be used in the work, then they can also be combined with floss threads, which are well suited for sewing stitches.

Necessary tools

Before you learn how to learn to embroider ribbons (photo below), it is important to prepare the necessary attributes. The main tool for embroidery is a thick needle with a large oblong eye. The tape must completely pass through it and not undergo deformation. To tension the fabric, special hoops are used, hoops, which are selected so that the size matches the size of the projected pattern and there is a tension adjustment screw.

In addition to needles and hoops, you must have sharp scissors so that they easily cut ribbons and floss. You will also need markers or crayons for drawing a pattern.

To decorate the finished embroidery using beads, rhinestones, sequins, lace and other decorative elements. For beginners, it is advisable to purchase ready-made kits for embroidery with ribbon, which already include the product design and all the necessary tools. They also contain useful instructions and descriptions.

Ribbon and fabric preparation

The fabric is the basis of embroidery, it must be clean, dried and ironed. The pattern can be applied manually or using a template so that its dimensions do not go beyond the hoop. The embroidery pattern is transferred to the fabric in any convenient way, and then with a screw you need to adjust the uniform tension and fixation of the fabric.

Satin or silk ribbons for work, cut into pieces of the desired length, cutting off the end of each ribbon at an angle. For embroidery, you can use a wide variety of stitches, which can be sent in one or different directions.

Features of embroidery with ribbons

Answering the question of how to learn how to embroider ribbons, one cannot say that it is very difficult. Seriously speaking, one of the main advantages of this charming technique is that it is so simple that even a child can do it. And those who practice different types of embroidery will certainly say that embroidering with ribbons is much easier than with the same threads. It really is.

You should not use ordinary needles instead of those specially designed for embroidery with ribbons, as they can damage the materials and the result will not be perfect.

No need to compare your work with other needlewomen. The technique of embroidery with a ribbon reflects individual mood and emotions, so the same pattern taken as a basis as a result will look different for different masters, as each craftsman brings something of his own to his creation.

The finished product is best not to be subjected to thermal tests. If you wash it in hot water, the paint may fade, and some seams may be irreparably damaged.
