What should be the density in the battery? How to check battery density? How to increase battery density?

Many car owners probably had to deal with the problem of incorrect battery operation. It happens that the car stood for only a day, and it becomes impossible to start it after that. However, even prolonged battery charging does not help. Similar symptoms indicate a decrease in electrolyte density. We will talk about what the density in the battery should be, why it falls, and how to raise it to the desired level, in this article.

Electrolyte and its density



An electrolyte is a solution consisting of sulfuric acid and distilled water. These components are contained in approximately equal parts: water - 1 part, sulfuric acid - 1.25 parts. Indicator 1.25 - this is the density of the car's battery . Battery performance directly depends on this indicator - the higher it is,
the lower its freezing temperature, and it is in a satisfactory working condition. Knowing what the density in the battery should be, you can judge the real state of your device.


Battery Density Measurement

Before checking the battery density, you should get a special device called a hydrometer. It is a device consisting of several rubber and glass elements.

Because the electrolyte is a dangerous chemical compound, before measuring its density, care must be taken about precautions, namely, work in rubber gloves, avoiding liquid in contact with skin and clothing. It is strictly forbidden to smoke!

Open the neck of the can, insert the tip of the device into it and use a pear to collect a little electrolyte so that the hydrometer float floats freely in the body without touching the bottom, side walls and top. Wait until the fluid in the device calms down, and keeping it at eye level, visually read the readings. Perform this procedure with all banks. If the density difference exceeds 0.01 g per cubic meter. cm, be sure to add distilled water or put the battery on an equalizing charge. With a decrease in density to 1.24 g per cubic meter. cm or lower, the battery should be recharged.

How to increase the density in the battery?

The easiest way to maintain the required level of electrolyte in the battery is topping up with distilled water. However, most car owners forget or do not know that it is periodically necessary to measure the density of the battery, because water boils over time, and with it the electrolyte, which leads to a decrease in density, sometimes to a critical point. When the battery is completely
refuses to work, then the burning question immediately arises: "How to increase the density in the battery?"





Using the instructions below, you can independently extend the life of the battery. However, remember that this procedure requires special attention and accuracy.

What to do at a critical density level



If the density index is below 1.18, then this problem cannot be solved by adding electrolyte. In this case, battery acid having a substantially higher density will be required. This process is carried out similarly to the electrolyte addition scheme. If you cannot achieve the desired results on one occasion, repeat the procedure as many times as necessary.
If the battery density is even lower than 1.18, then you must resort to the procedure for a complete replacement of the electrolyte. To do this, you must immediately pump out with a pear the maximum amount of solution. Then, tightly close the vents of the plugs on the battery banks. Place the battery on its side and alternately drill 3-3.5 mm holes in the bottom of each can. Before making another hole, drain the remaining electrolyte from the previous one.

Next, thoroughly rinse the battery with distilled water. After that, seal the drilled holes with acid-resistant plastic (for example, plugs from an unnecessary battery can be used for this).
Having done all the preparatory procedures, you can proceed with the filling of fresh electrolyte. In this case, it is recommended to use a solution prepared independently, the density of which will be slightly higher than that provided for your climatic zone. It should be borne in mind that even a complete replacement of the electrolyte in the old battery will not be able to provide it with the same service life as the new battery.

Tip: if you want the battery to serve you as long as possible, do not forget to charge it in time and check its density periodically.
