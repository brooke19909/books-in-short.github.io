Sousse (Tunisia): Attractions of one of the most fun and noisy cities in the Middle East

Tunisia has prepared attractions for absolutely all tourists, it will be interesting for young people, couples with children, the elderly. Sousse is considered one of the noisiest and most fun cities in the country, travelers who prefer nightlife and outdoor activities come here. But at the same time, the city has a lot of historical, cultural and architectural monuments worth exploring, the beaches here are some of the best in Tunisia. A trip to local shops and markets will not leave anyone indifferent.



Sousse has many interesting architectural structures . Tunisia, whose sights impress even experienced tourists, has a rich cultural heritage. If you select the main objects for visits, then all of them can be bypassed in one day, since significant buildings are located in one place - the Medina (Old Town). Of great interest is the fortress-monastery of Ribat. The building was built in 859, in wartime it was used to repel an attack from the sea, and in peace there were soldiers praying, spreading Islam among the local population. On the second floor, between the columns, there is a footprint, if you stand on it and make a wish, it will certainly come true.



Medina is the historical center of Sousse. Tunisia has accumulated its sights for centuries, in the Old Town you can walk along the narrow old streets and look at the ancient houses. In this place the former Arabian atmosphere is still preserved. Since 1988, Medina has been included in the list of historical monuments under the care of UNESCO. Tunisia has a lot of shrines. The city of Sousse, whose sights do not end with fortresses and ancient houses, can rightfully be proud of the Great Mosque, built in 851. It is notable for the absence of a high minaret, at one time it served as a defensive structure.

The Dar Essid House Museum is one of the most interesting places that Sousse owns. Tunisia, whose sights are surprising, has kept for foreign guests the house of the Tunisian family, built in 928. Today they are owned by their descendants, who agreed to arrange a museum in the building. The house is imbued with the spirit of antiquity, reminiscent of bygone days furniture, chandeliers, canopies, watches, curtains. Near the house there is a high tower, before watching the stars from there, and now there is a small cafe.



Tunis attracts travelers with many interesting places, oriental culture and traditions that have survived after many centuries. Sousse of tourists has mostly positive reviews, because there really is something to see, and a well-developed infrastructure makes the rest comfortable.
