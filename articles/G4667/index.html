general description

The M 6 highway has a huge length of 1880 km. It passes through seven regions of Russia, linking them and providing free communication between them. On the highway there are entrances to the cities of Ryazhsk, Michurinsk, Tambov. The federal road began its history from the middle of the last century, from 1964. It was then that the first section connected Moscow and Tambov.

The coverage of the route is asphalt concrete, the width along the entire length is 8 meters. No dividing strip. The climate is temperate continental. The average temperature in January is -10, and in July up to +25 degrees. Therefore, even if your car does not have air conditioning, the trip will take place in fairly comfortable conditions.

A number of sections on the road require increased attention from the driver. Some have steep ascents and descents, while others have limited visibility. Therefore, accidents on the M 6 highway are a fairly common occurrence.

The main settlements

There are quite a lot of them, almost every kilometer is marked by a large or small city or village. But interest on the highway is of several settlements:

  • Michurinsk is a center of scientific gardening. The city is relatively small, only 100 thousand inhabitants. But on its territory preserved architectural monuments.
  • Tambov is the center of the region of the same name, which was founded in 1636. Today it is a fairly large populated center, where 300 thousand people live.
  • Volgograd - until 1961 it was called Stalingrad. Today it is an important industrial and cultural city. Large enterprises of machine building and shipbuilding are concentrated here, almost a million inhabitants live.
  • Astrakhan - 500 thousand inhabitants live here. The main industry is the cultivation of valuable varieties of fish.
