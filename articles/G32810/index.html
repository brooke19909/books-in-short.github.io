Beach vacation

The most popular in Yeysk is a holiday by the sea. Every year, thousands of Russians seek to soak up the sand on a seashell and swim in the shallow sea in sunny summer. Most of the beaches are concentrated on the Yeisk Spit. There is also a children's and urban central beach. At the very end of the Yeisk Spit there is a very interesting "Youth" beach. It is near it that the underwater current passes, thanks to which the sea is especially deep here. You must admit that Yeysk hotels offer an amazing vacation.

South of the port is Kamenka beach, which is especially popular. Its north side is mostly sandy, and the south is completely strewn with pebbles. The sea here is very shallow. Numerous shallows come to the surface during low tides. They form huge "islands" of sand. The deeper sea begins only a few hundred meters from the coast. Healing rest

Forty kilometers from Yeysk is Khan Lake. It is famous for its healing mud. In addition, there are sources of hydrogen sulfide and iodine-bromine mineral waters. Treatment of people with their help is carried out in the Yeysk sanatorium. Thanks to therapy, metabolic processes in the body improve, radionuclides and salts of heavy metals are excreted, and motor functions improve. These natural preparations also relieve inflammation and have an analgesic effect.

Windsurfing

Do you know that Yeysk people dubbed the "Mecca of Surfers of Russia"? The first windsurfing competitions were held at the Yeysk Yacht Club back in 1999. Since then, the number of fans and participants in this impressive sport has increased rapidly. For the first time, athletes fought for kitesurfing championship in 2003. In May 2006, more than 150 athletes fought for the right to be the first. More than 1,500 guests from all over Russia came to stare at this magnificent sight. In 2009, from May 1 to 9, the Yeisk Cup competitions were held.
