Calella Park 3 * (Spain, Calella): hotel description, services, reviews

Spain is a fairly popular destination for beach and sea tourism. In our article we want to talk about the resort town of Calella and about one of its economy class hotels. Calella Park is a three-star complex in the heart of the resort.

A bit about the hotel ...

Calella Park is located in Calella, 57 kilometers from Barcelona and 48 kilometers from Girona. The hotel was first opened in 1971, and was completely renovated in 2011. The complex has a convenient location, and therefore it is very easy to get to local attractions from it. The hotel is designed for family vacations.

Calella Park 3 * has a good infrastructure. On its territory there is a car rental, exchange office, fax, mail. The property does not accept pets. Wi-Fi is free in public areas and by the pool. Internet is also available in some rooms.

A little bit about the resort ...

Calella is the municipality and city of Spain, located in the Maresme region (Catalonia), the capital of the Costa del Maresme. Calella is a democratic resort that will appeal to supporters of outdoor activities. On its territory there are many hotels built for every budget and taste, shops, restaurants, cafes and entertainment venues.



The first settlements on the site of the city arose in the 1st century BC. However, in those days, the region had an exclusively agricultural direction and supplied the Roman Empire with olive oil, wheat and grapes. Until 1925, Calella was considered a fishing village, and only later acquired the status of a city. Tourism development here took place in the second half of the twentieth century. Currently, the city has become a modern and very popular resort, attracting tourists from all over the country.

How to get to the hotel?

Aeroflot Airlines regularly flies direct to Barcelona from Moscow. And during the high season, additional charter flights are introduced. You can get from Barcelona to Calella on a comfortable modern train. The train station is near the Calella Park Hotel. A round-trip ticket from Calella to Barcelona will cost an adult passenger 6-8 EUR and 4-6 EUR for children. By the way, the railway tracks on the resort's coast are completely invisible and train sounds are not heard, and therefore do not interfere with vacationers in hotels. The fact is that they are in a tunnel inside the rock.

Active tourists who wish to travel around the country and see the sights should rent a car. To do this, it is enough to have a passport, international license and driving experience of more than one year. Rental prices are very different. But on average, it is about thirty dollars a day.

Resort beaches

In summer, the average temperature ranges from + 28 degrees, and in winter does not drop below +15 degrees. The tourist season lasts from the beginning of April to the end of October.

The resort gained popularity due to its sandy beaches, which are limited on the promenade by a shady walkway. The pedestrian zone stretches along the entire city for more than three kilometers and gradually turns into a beautiful rocky coast. Local landscapes, consisting of secluded bays and cliffs, resemble the Costa Brava. Calella Park Hotel is located next to one of the city's municipal beaches. The entire coast of the resort has a coating of golden sand and clear sea water, as a result of which each year it receives such a badge of honor as the Blue Flag for its environmental condition and cleanliness.



It should be understood that the coast is far from one beach. The central part, for example, is always crowded with tourists and very noisy. For proponents of a more relaxing holiday, the area around Sant Polu will be to their heart. The local beach is not crowded, but at the same time very comfortable.

The most remote stretch of coast - Roca Grossa - is a favorite place for divers. The colorful underwater world and beautiful corals in clear clear water make trips on glass-bottom boats with speedboats very popular.

Calella Park (Spain) does not have its own beach, like all other hotels in Europe. On any coast, there are only municipal beaches. Therefore, tourists can visit absolutely any part of the coast, even near the most expensive hotels. Sun loungers and umbrellas during the high season, usually cost 4-6 EUR.
