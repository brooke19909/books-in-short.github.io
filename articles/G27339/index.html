A bit of history

Vancouver Island, a photo of which is presented in the article, is part of a province called British Columbia, which became part of Canada in 1871. At the end of the 18th century, expeditions from the British and Spaniards were simultaneously equipped on the island. In the end, this led to quite logical disagreements. Each side pulled a blanket over itself. Two pioneers, George Vancouver and Juan Cuadra, wanted to give their name to the found land. By that time, relations between the parties had already heated up to the limit. The Spaniards built a fortified settlement and attacked British ships. As a result, it was decided to hold peace talks in order to avoid an increase in the number of victims. Unfortunately, the meeting practically did not lead to anything. But at the end of the land they got a peculiar name - "Vancouver Island and Cuadra."

Time passed, and the influence of the Spaniards in those places was becoming less and less significant. As a result, in the next century, Vancouver Island received its modern name. Interestingly, during the conflict of the parties, the Spaniard, not interested in dividing the land, was able to completely circumnavigate this part of the land by sea.

In the middle of the XIX century, the island began to belong to Britain, a colony appeared there. Its capital was the city of Victoria. But closer to the XX century, having lost its significance, the colony became part of British Columbia.



Characteristic

Vancouver Island, whose area is almost 32 thousand square meters. km, washed by the waters of the Pacific Ocean. The entire west coast is influenced by its waters. In many ways, the climate on the island is formed under the influence of currents and air masses in this area. The eastern side of the land is washed by the Strait of Georgia. But in the south - the waters of another water area. This is the Strait of Juan de Fuca. It separates Washington State and Vancouver Island, and the Johnstone Strait connects to the Pacific Ocean.

Once Vancouver was inhabited only by Indians living in small villages. Now about 800 thousand people live here. The largest settlement is the beautiful city of Victoria.

The development of the economy on the island

The economy of Vancouver mainly relies on the extraction of natural resources. Basically, this, of course, is logging. All of Canada is famous for its vast forests. Vancouver Island is far from an exception to this rule. It is there that the ancient forests that were still in the original British Columbia are still preserved. They remained in impassable corners of the island. The highest Sitka spruce in Canada grows in Vancouver, reaching a height of 95 meters. She and her kindred trees were able to grow to such heights thanks to the surprisingly favorable climate for them.
