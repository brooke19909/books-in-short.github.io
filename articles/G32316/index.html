Panama Canal: year of the official opening of the facility and its historical significance

From time immemorial, mankind has been trying to rebuild the natural conditions of the Earth in order to obtain any benefits. One of such cases of restructuring is the Panama Canal, the year of the official opening of this facility was marked by an event that influenced the development of all seafaring in this part of the planet, despite the negative aspects of construction.

Panama Canal





This artificially created water body is located in Central America. The canal connects the borders of the Atlantic Ocean with the Caribbean Sea. The length of the isthmus at a deep water level is about 82 km, and the width is 150 m, while the vessels pass through three locks along the way, after which they end up in the artificial lake Gatun. Further, after passing a few more steps and Miraflores (a natural water reservoir), the cars go out into the bay. All this way is the Panama Canal, the year of the official opening of which is one thousand nine hundred and twentieth.

The construction of this facility was of great importance for the development of economic relations between the countries of Central America, as well as for the sphere of navigation. The canal allows such huge vessels as container ships and tankers to pass, while saving more than 10,000 km of track. Posting works are carried out by a special service, the time spent on the entire segment, on average, is about 9 hours.

Great construction





The Panama Canal, the year of the official opening of which marked the implementation of the ideas of many minds of the 19th century, began its history at the end of 1882, when its construction began. At the head stood the great French engineer - Count Ferdinant de Lesseps, who at that time had the experience of successfully laying the Suez Canal.

The company laying the waterway was discussed at the International Congress in 1879 in France, after which it received a concession from the Colombian government for a period of 99 years. This activity was based on subscriber loans, with the price of one share at 500 francs.

According to historians, Count F. de Lesseps was a skilled diplomat and orator, but his last creation - the Panama Canal (the year of official opening is indicated above) - had a deplorable outcome for him, possibly due to underestimation of the initial cost of the project. To attract a large number of investors, the engineer presented figures that were almost halved, which, of course, could not but affect the success of the work progress.
