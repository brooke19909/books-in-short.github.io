Activities of zemstvo superiors

On July 12, 1889, the "Regulation on Zemstvo Chiefs" was signed. It began to operate in 40 provinces of our country, in most of which landowners owned land. Zemstvo plots were created in them. This article will highlight aspects of the labor activity of their superiors, who were endowed with great powers in those days.

Background to this post

It is customary to consider the forerunners of the zemstvo chiefs to be world mediators whose main task was to enact the provision on the peasantry adopted in 1861. These people also resolved situations arising from the established order of land relations between landowners and peasants. Over time, the number of global intermediaries has been reduced, their institution has become in need of immediate transformation.

In 1874, this post was abolished and a new one was introduced: county-level peasant affairs. The inconvenient aspects of the work of this organization came to light very quickly. Members of the county presence did not cope with their duties, riots broke out in the volosts, taxes were collected negligently and not on time, cases of embezzlement of money were observed. These prerequisites led to the emergence of zemstvo chiefs (1889 is the starting point of their labor activity).





Special post requirements

The need to introduce a new post was explained by the fact that in Russia there was no power close to the people. Only persons belonging to the local nobility class could enter the institute of zemstvo commanders. Applicants had special requirements. They had to own certain property (land or other real estate). It was possible to become the head of the zemsky district only with higher education.





If there were not enough candidates who met the above requirements, nobles who did not have the necessary education and who had undergone military or civil service were appointed to this post. But in such cases, they should have had a much greater property qualification. The Minister of the Interior had the authority, bypassing all conditions, to replenish the ranks of the heads of zemstvo plots by local representatives of the nobility.

Service Benefits

Zemsky chiefs had the right to wear a special sign on a gilded chain. It was a circle, on one side of which was depicted the coat of arms of Russia, framed by the name of their post. On the other side of the sign flaunted an inscription indicating the day of approval of the Regulation on Zemsky chiefs.





People who exercise power in zemstvo plots also had a special seal, which serves as confirmation of the powers entrusted to them.

Administrative powers

Zemsky chiefs were vested with the following administrative rights:

  • Make decisions on lawsuits and disputes related to land law of peasants.
  • To control the decisions of the bodies of peasant self-government.
  • Manage certain police ranks.
  • To propose your questions for discussion at a meeting gathered in the volost.
  • To approve or temporarily dismiss elders.
  • To monitor the guardianship of orphans and much more.

These powers were valid only in rural areas.

Judicial Powers

Zemsky chiefs did a certain job of a justice of the peace. They could consider specific cases of civilians:

  • Claims for hiring land, not exceeding the amount of 500 rubles.
  • Personal claims for an amount not exceeding 300 rubles.
  • Claims for insults.
  • Claims for the reconstruction of damaged property.
  • Requests to provide evidence base for pending lawsuits and more.

Representatives of this profession were also empowered to judge certain criminal cases. These people were charged with violations of the law, fined up to 300 rubles; criminal acts for which a prison term of twelve months could be obtained.





The law on zemstvo commanders gave these people special privileges in relation to courts operating in the volost. They had the right to control the activities of the judiciary. However, the representatives of this profession were not entitled to cancel the decisions of the volost courts .

Practical work

The status of zemstvo commanders over the years has become increasingly important. Based on them as the main executive branch in the field, important affairs in the state were planned.

In order to control the redistribution of land received by the peasants as a result of the reforms of the 1860s, a corresponding decree was issued. It was valid only in the provinces in which zemstvo plots functioned. In these areas, according to a certain provision, in 1899 the process of collecting salary taxes from the population was streamlined.

An important role was assigned to representatives of this profession in the Agrarian reform. The government in 1906 made it easier for peasants to leave the community. He was given the opportunity to secure for themselves cultivated plots of land. Control over the implementation of this decree in the field was conducted by zemstvo chiefs. The activities of these people led to the success of the Agrarian reform.





The heads of the zemstvo plots in their time were judges and administrators all rolled into one. To date, the importance of their profession continues to receive mixed assessments and be of interest for various studies.
