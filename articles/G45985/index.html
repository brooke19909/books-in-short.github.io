City Chernyakhovsk, Kaliningrad region: description, photo

G. Chernyakhovsk of the Kaliningrad region until 1946 was called Insterburg. It is the administrative center of the homonymous district, as well as the settlement. The city is 80 km away from the regional center of the region, towards the state border with the State of Lithuania. According to the 2016 census, a little more than 37 thousand people live in it. The city is located at the confluence of the rivers Instruch and Angrappa.

Briefly from the story

The lands on which the modern city of Chernyakhovsk of the Kaliningrad Region is located were inhabited by Prussian tribes as far back as the 10th century BC. e. In the 14th century, the Teutonic Order built the castle of Insterburg, the remains of which have been preserved in the northern part of the city to this day. Chernyakhovsk was badly damaged during the Second World War, in 1944 it was bombed by the allies of the Red Army. Now bears the name of the commander of the Third Belorussian Front - Ivan Chernyakhovsky.



Transport

The city of Chernyakhovsk in the Kaliningrad region is, by geographical standards, in the center of the region. A railway line passes through it. The main directions: to Kaliningrad, to Moscow through Belarus and Lithuania, as well as radial gauges to the regional cities of Sovetsk and Zheleznodorozhny. There is also a river port on the river Pregolya. Buses remained from public transport, the trolleybus line ceased operation in 1945.

Population

The city of Chernyakhovsk in the Kaliningrad region is mainly represented by Russians in the ethnic composition of the population (86%). Ukrainians, Belarusians living in this area make up about 4-5%, Lithuanians and Germans - 1%. The population in the last 10 years has decreased due to the migration of people to other cities, primarily to Kaliningrad.

Economy

Not far from the city is the village of Mayevka, where horses of the Trakenens breed, famous in Europe, are bred. They are distinguished by endurance and become winners of equestrian competitions.
