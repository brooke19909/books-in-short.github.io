4K TVs (UHD): what is it, is it worth buying? 4K TV Reviews (UHD)

High resolution is the definition that many people remember when they go to the store for a new TV. The newest word in this area is the 4K Ultra High-Definition format. For many, this is just a set of letters. So, 4K UHD TVs - what is it? The most popular at the moment can be called a Full HD monitor. Ultra HD can be defined as a device that can transmit an image with active pixels. There are, in turn, a huge number of them (more than eight million). The resolution is 3840 x 2160 pixels.



This figure was obtained by doubling the already familiar resolution of 1920 x 1080. This has become the benchmark standard for clarity. Below we consider varieties of screen sizes.

So, at the moment, the resolution for clarity happens:

  • Full HD (progressive scan 1080);
  • 1080i (interlaced);
  • HD Ready (720 pixels per inch).

The latter is the most common of them, because today almost all TV channels and DVDs have this resolution (720 x 480). But this format can be considered obsolete. If you already buy a new TV, it is better not to consider this option at all, even if it will be a large diagonal and at a bargain price. When choosing a television receiver, it is still worth paying attention to the frequency in hertz. It should be at least 120.

4K extension

4K format appeared recently, in 2005. It‚Äôs not so easy for the human eye to see the difference in the latest resolution and the already familiar 1080. It is especially difficult to see the difference if the resolution of 1080 shows, for example, Blu-ray.

The difference is perceptible when watching special demos from manufacturers. To determine the purchase and its feasibility, you need to consider all the advantages and disadvantages of the product you like. So with a high-definition TV.

What are the benefits of the Ultra HD extension?

The huge screen shows with incredible clarity, it is impossible to see individual pixels on it, as on 720p analogs. This is an indisputable plus. The same as the realism of what is happening on the screen: you can feel like part of the movie scene. The saturation of colors and transitions from shade to shade allow you to see the whole variety of natural and artificial colors.

Even if the movie or broadcast is broadcast in the wrong format, a special feature will allow you to adapt the image in 4K. The receivers are equipped with many additional features. In televisions with this resolution, for example, voice or gesture control, the ability to synchronize them with other devices, for example with a personal smartphone, can be installed.

4K adaptation

The broadcasting of channels itself does not yet take place in Ultra-HD, but thanks to the adaptability, you can purchase a screen with this extension and enjoy a clear picture. Almost all 4K UHD TVs combine the adaptation function. What does this give the simple viewer? Nothing but the opportunity to see the smallest details even in long-loved programs.

A new image format opens up new possibilities.

For people whose work is connected with photography, 3D graphics, production of games and, of course, films, the new TV will be a great assistant.

Disadvantages of the latest resolution

And now a little about the minuses that are present while operating such a novelty as 4K UHD TVs. What could it be?

Not wide distribution of content. In our country, few channels have switched to HD, and Ultra HD is not available to everyone, and you will have to pay for it separately. It is included only in paid channel packages, and their selection is small. The price category is high. And although operators have been working on this issue recently, the service is not available to most consumers.

A more complete impression of the new broadcast can be obtained if the devices connected to the TV pass through the cable not now common 1.4 HDMI, but already improved 2.0.

Output 1.4 will be able to do the image conversion, but still part of the extension will be lost. The new 2.0 cable will allow you to watch movies even at up to 60 frames per second, while the modern analogue only reproduces 30. When choosing a new TV, it is advisable to pay attention to the fact that there are several HDMI ports. 4K UHD-TVs, which are only positive customer reviews, are likely to take a certain place in the consumer market, and, apparently, not the last.

OLED: The New Generation

CRT TVs have long evolved into modern liquid crystal or plasma panels. But the top rightfully occupy OLED TVs 4K UHD. What is it? This panel consists of organic LEDs. This technology is very popular among manufacturers and interested buyers, as it is very new in concept. The main advantage of such a panel is a curved screen. Allows this ability to realize many bold decisions. For such a panel, you do not need to create additional backlight, like for another screen, for example LED 4K UHD. The previous generation LCD TV will not be able to technically approach the backlight problem.

4K OLED panels are truly the best combination for the TV to date. Such a screen is quite large (from 55 diagonals and above). The price, of course, is also rather big.
