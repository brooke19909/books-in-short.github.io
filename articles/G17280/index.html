Ottawa's most popular attractions

Today's capital of Canada was originally called Bytown. The city appeared as a village of builders of the Rideau Canal. Since 1855, he was appointed the capital of this British colony, which took as long as 29 years. Then he got his current name Ottawa. You can answer why this provincial small town, and not Toronto or Montreal, was chosen as the capital by looking at its geographical location.



Wanting to find out what Ottawa is famous for, it is worth visiting the Beitown Museum, where the whole history of the capital is concentrated. It is located in the oldest building of the city, built in 1827. In addition to exhibits that are dedicated to the history of the capital, there is also an exhibition of household items of the nineteenth century.

Notre Dame Basilica - Ottawa's main attraction

The Basilica is a magnificent Catholic cathedral, which was erected near the Byward market in the neo-Gothic style.

Ottawa Attractions: The seat of the Canadian Parliament

The monumental building, located on top of the fifty-meter Parliament Hill, is very similar in its style to the architecture of the London Parliament. Its construction was completed 4 years before the country acquired its own statehood. For tourists, the building is open all year, even when the Senate, the Government of Canada and the House of Commons sit in it . In the summer, everyone can look at the ceremonial change of the guard - this gives the place additional splendor and solidity.

Ottawa Attractions: Secret Bench of Knowledge

This bench is a famous sculpture made by Li Vivo, a female sculptor. The monument is located on the street. Wellington, in front of the Canada Archive and Library.

Rideau Canal in Ottawa

The channel connecting Kingston and Ottawa. The total length is 200 kilometers. This is Canada's oldest canal .



Gatineau Park in Ottawa

Known for its amazing scenery, the park. Its area is 360 km. Its attractions include 5 public beaches, Lake Pink, Lask Cave, covered with marble from the inside, and Mackenzie King Manor, which belonged to the country's tenth prime minister. The latter is famous for its mysterious trails, well-kept gardens and picturesque ruins.

Ottawa Attractions: Parliament Hill

This is a grandiose complex of architecture built in the Gothic style. This place houses the Parliament, Parliamentary Library and Senate.

Ottawa Attractions: Peace Tower

She is a symbol of Canada, which rises above the hill of Parliament. The height of its clock tower is 55 m.

Central Experimental Farm

This farm is a research institution of the country. Here are the Ornamental Gardens, the Dominion Observatory, the Agricultural Museum, the Botanical Garden, as well as the Wildlife Garden.



Wakefield Village in Ottawa

This is a favorite vacation spot of the whole bohemian capital. It is famous for local sights and picturesque landscapes, including a covered bridge thrown across the Gatineau River, the McLaren mill, the bungee tower (the highest on the whole continent).
