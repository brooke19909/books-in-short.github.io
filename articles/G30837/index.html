How to make a loop crochet: first steps

One of the most common types of needlework can rightfully be called crochet. With this straightforward device, you can create both simple and complex designs. However, to begin with, it is worth learning how to make a loop with a hook, since it is with it that any pattern begins.

How to make a loop to start work with? A simple step-by-step instruction will help with this.

The first step is the most important.

So, we make the first knot, which will be the starting point for further work. There are several options, and many needlewomen make it their own way, because everyone has a different style - everyone chooses what is more convenient for him. How to make a loop crochet? Instructions for beginners

How to make number one loop?

  • For convenience, we wind the end of the thread in one revolution once around the little finger, while the palm is located with the back side down.
  • After making sure that there is a short tail for insurance, we wrap the thread around the index finger.
  • Then we take the hook, wrap the thread around it and tighten the tight loop, as shown in the figure.
  • Make sure that the knot does not turn out too tight, the thread should slide freely along the hook.

How to hold

In which hand to hold the hook, everyone decides for himself. The method of holding the thread with the little finger and index finger is the most convenient and optimal. This creates enough space to move the hook and maintains the necessary thread tension.





The first loop is ready. What's next?

There is nothing complicated in how to make the first loops crocheted. With it, we stretch the thread through an existing bundle. The main thing is that he should not be too tight. The second loop in size must be done similarly to the first. It should not be too loose. However, it‚Äôs also not worth it. The third loop is done similarly to the second. Thus, something like a thin pigtail comes out. It is also called an air knot or zero level.





Beginning of the first line

So, the pigtail of the desired length is ready. In order to start the first row, you need to go back on the eyelet and hook its half. You now have two loops around your hook.





Then we make a new loop and pull it through the first loop. So, again, two loops on the hook should remain. Catch the thread again and pull it through both loops. Repeat these steps until you reach the end of the line. To finish, cut the long side of the thread and pull it through the loop.





Nothing complicated

In fact, it‚Äôs not difficult to make the first loops with your own hands. Having mastered the simplest, you can proceed to more complex drawings, double or violin knitting. It is also better for beginners to start using light threads, they are best seen stitches and possible errors. How to crochet the first stitches? Practice and practice again. The most awkward movements, which are repeated many times, can be brought to automatism, when you no longer even need to look at the yarn - hands do everything themselves.





Useful Tips

Crocheting, like any other kind of needlework, should bring only pleasure. It is important not only to know how to make a loop of thread, which hook to choose and the thickness of the thread. To make the lesson as comfortable as possible, you need to remember a couple of simple rules:

  1. Proper fit. There should be no voltage during operation. You can comfortably sit in a chair, leaning back in his chair.
  2. If you are right-handed, then the ball should be kept on the left, if left-handed, then on the right. So that it does not roll away, you can put it in some container.
  3. Do not knit while lying down, as well as reading while lying down, this can negatively affect vision. It will not be superfluous to think about good lighting.
  4. Do not be fanatics, do not knit for days on end, take breaks.
  5. There is no need to rush, especially at the initial stage, they say that unhurried and smooth movements with a crochet or knitting needles calm the nervous system.
  6. Train on light threads, it is more convenient to count loops on them.

Time and patience

The so-called pigtail, or air loops, is the initial stage for creating a wide variety of patterns. You can crochet in rows, or in a closed line to create ovals, circles or squares. As a workout, you can knit a small canvas of arbitrary width and height.

To begin with, you can design something useful, for example, a washcloth for washing dishes, well, or a tiny scarf for a doll. It is important that the process is fun. Do not be upset if not everything works the first time. In order for the loops to be smooth, and the movements to be smooth, you need to bring all the work to automaticity, and for this only time and patience are needed.
