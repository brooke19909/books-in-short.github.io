Roslavl: attractions and description of the city

For lovers of calm tourism in the historical cities of Russia, there are many interesting settlements in the European part of the country, where Russia itself came from, it is in them that the spirit of princes, boyars and the real Russian people still hangs. Roslavl (or, as it was originally called, Rostislavl) is one of such small towns in which unique architectural monuments of past centuries have been preserved. What can you visit in this village, where to relax?

What is Roslavl?





In the Smolensk region , 392 kilometers from Moscow, the city of Roslavl is located, with a population of about 51 thousand people.

Its main feature is a rich history that began back in the 12th century. The first mention of the city is connected with the personality of Rostislav Mstislavich - it was he who founded the Rostislavl fortress on the conquered lands back in 1137, which later became the city of Roslavl.

At the beginning of the XIII century, this place became the residence of the prince and his retinue, it was from Roslavl that the Smolensk principality was governed.

For several centuries Roslavl passed from hand to hand, experienced many wars, civil strife, attacks of foreign invaders, only with the advent of Soviet power did the city begin to exist peacefully and develop before the war. Roslavl was by then a major transport hub, and so the Nazis stubbornly tried to break into the city, and they succeeded: for 784 days, Roslavl was in the occupation of the enemy, after which there was a triumphant liberation.

Now Roslavl is included in the list of historical cities of Russia, succeeds in economic terms (developed chemical, heavy, food, light industry), is a major traffic intersection.

Architectural attractions of Roslavl





From 1833 to 1845, the church of the Kazan Icon of the Mother of God was built in Roslavl. The initiative belonged to a local resident merchant A.F. Mukinin, who wanted to build a stone cathedral instead of a wooden one. At one time, the church burned, was desolate, but now it is completely restored and is actively functioning. Address: st. Red Army, 104.

Transfiguration Monastery - one of the main attractions of the city of Roslavl, located on the street. Proletarskaya, 52. It consists of two parts: the Cathedral of the Transfiguration of Our Savior, built in 1811-1819, and the church of Alexander Nevsky (construction in 1857-1872). In addition, the monastery was surrounded by a unique fence, with towers and gates, but only fragments have survived to this day. At the moment, the religious organization operates, and the buildings are being restored.

Interesting monuments





On the street of Engels, 20 there is a monument-steam locomotive - a landmark of Roslavl, which is shown to each guest of the city. Until recently, the L-2012 steam locomotive was put up on a pedestal, but it was removed for reconstruction, and the L-0858 steam locomotive, which was actively used during the war, was installed in its place. In general, the monument is dedicated to the car repair plant, which from the 19th century until today has been engaged in the production of tank cars, spare parts for trains and their repair.

On Bolshaya Smolenskaya Street, 98 there is a water tower that meets all the guests of the city and is visible from many points of the village. In Roslavl, such structures began to appear in the XIX century, the same has been preserved since the XX century.

Where to spend interesting time in Roslavl?

The Museum of History and Art is a landmark of Roslavl, the address and reviews of which can be found on the official website of the organization. The institution was founded back in 1920 under the leadership of Sergei Mikhailovich Sokolovsky. Since then, the museum has increased by 26 thousand exhibits, held hundreds of exhibitions and events.

Main expositions:

  • historical (archaeological finds of the city from the VIII century BC to the XVII century CE: weapons, clothing, household items and culture);
  • dedicated to the Great Patriotic War (exhibits of the occupation period);
  • art (works of local artists, masters, sculptors);
  • documentary fund (important historical documents related to the history of the city from ancient times to the present day).

Address: st. Proletarian, 63.

Another attraction of Roslavl is the Fabrika entertainment complex, where you can relax with the whole family. Located at: st. Krasnoarmeyskaya, 9.

The range of services provided by the organization will surprise even a metropolitan guest: in one building there are cozy hotel rooms with various accommodation and comfort options, a cinema with 2 cinema halls, a banquet hall, a bar-cafe, a sauna, billiards, bowling. Children's programs are held on Sundays.

Thus, the sights of the city of Roslavl of the Smolensk region will be interesting not only to history buffs, but also to ordinary tourists, because there is where to walk, what to see and where to have fun in the evening in good company.
