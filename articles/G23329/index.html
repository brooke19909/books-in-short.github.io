Plowman in Russia: main occupations and synonyms

The main industry on the old Russian land was agriculture. For a long time, various farming systems have been used. In Russia, a plowman, who is also called a walker after a plow, an ancient field worker or a plowman, used subsection, fallow and three-field.

Types of agriculture in Ancient Russia

In the forest zones, a subsection was widespread, according to which a plot of forest was cleared and in its place the soil was cultivated for several years in a row. This period was approximately two to three years, until the soil was completely depleted. Then this land was no longer used for a sufficiently long time, up to forty years.

Fallow was considered a more intensive system of agriculture . Allocate forest fallow and steppe. After deforestation, a plowman in Ancient Russia cultivated arable land for three to four years, followed by a break of ten to twelve years. Field work lasted longer in the steppes, and less land rest. While the arable land was resting, it was used as a pasture for livestock.

Another type of agriculture, which subsequently became the most preferred, is the triple field. According to this system, alternating crops of different crops - spring and winter. For a year, one of the fields was left under steam.
