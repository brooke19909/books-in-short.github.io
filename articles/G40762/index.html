Dzharylgach island (Kherson region): rest, reviews from tourists, how to get

The pearl of the south of Ukraine can rightfully be called Dzharylgach - the largest island in the Black Sea. Officially, it is uninhabited, but the rich nature, unique climate, white sand beaches and calm warm sea during the holiday season attract thousands of tourists.

History

Archaeological finds discovered on the island indicate that its territory was inhabited during the Neolithic and Bronze Ages. The island of Dzharylgach is mentioned in many works of ancient authors, such as, for example, Ptolemy and Herodotus, and the myth of the emergence of the Dzharylgach spit, which connects the island to the coast, arose during the Trojan War. There is also an assumption that the island where Odysseus and his soldiers were captured by the one-eyed Cyclops, this is Dzharylgach. This is evidenced by the texts of Homer, which describes the deserted Goat Island, located near the coast and covered with dense forest, where many wild goats are found. In the northern part of the Black Sea, where, as you know, these events took place, there is no other such island. As for the forest, which is currently not on the island, then translated from Turkic as ‚Äúdzharylgach‚Äù means a scorched forest or burnt trees. There is also a version that the island became a prototype of Buyan, described by A.S. Pushkin in a famous poem.



Geographic features

Dzharylgach Island - Kherson region of Ukraine. It is located near the northwestern coast of the Black Sea, eight kilometers from the city of Skadovsk, and belongs to the Skadovsky district. The area occupied by him is 62 square meters. km, and its length is 42 km. The western part of the island is connected by a narrow scythe with the village of Lazurnoe. On its territory there are many salt lakes, which occupy 8% of the total area. The landscape belongs to the estuary-lowlands. The maximum relief height above sea level does not exceed 0.5 m. The coast of the island is washed by the Dzharylgach and Karkinitsky bays.



Nature

Due to the fact that, starting in 1923, the island of Dzharylgach was part of the Askania Nova Nature Reserve, and since 1927 it became part of the Primorsky Nature Reserve, there it was possible to preserve the amazing, almost untouched nature. Today, there is a national natural park created to preserve the historical, cultural and natural values ‚Äã‚Äãof the northern coast.

Despite the fact that almost the entire territory of the island is a steppe, its flora is very rich. About 500 species of plants grow here, 50 of which are endemic, that is, found only in a limited area. Among them, 7 species from the orchid family.

The fauna of the island is even more impressive. Here you can find 15 species of mammals, including wild horses, red deer, mouflon, roe deer, fallow deer, raccoon dogs. The island has an incredible variety of insects, ranging from colorful butterflies to frightening tourists karakurt, not to mention the clouds of mosquitoes and midges.



Marine life is rich in shrimp, crabs, mussels, various species of fish, as well as the ubiquitous jellyfish. From the shore you can often see dolphins, of which there are 3 species: bottlenose dolphin, guinea pig and white cask.

Climate

The climatic conditions on the island are perfect for families with children. In winter, the temperature does not fall below -2 0 C, and in summer it does not exceed + 25 + 28 0 C. The air humidity, given the marine environment, is quite high, but the cool air flows coming from the continent in the warm season do not allow the climate to be too hot . It rains rarely, the average rainfall per year does not exceed 400 mm. Sea air is saturated with iodine compounds released by seaweed, as well as ozone and oxygen. Mixed with the scents of local herbs, it creates an indescribable, memorable aroma.



Sea

One of the features of the island is its versatility in terms of recreation. The bay separating the Dzharylgach island from the coast is shallow, which allows the water to warm up on the hottest days to 30 0 C. Thanks to sunlight and shallow depth , seaweed form entire underwater meadows at the bottom, attracting numerous marine life in the form of fish and mollusks. There are never big waves on this side of the island. Long sandy beaches of coarse white sand and a shallow depth make swimming in the sea as safe as possible, so this site is the best suited for families with children. Photos of the island of Dzharylgach, with its azure calm sea and white sand on endless beaches, taken in fine sunny weather, resemble images of the beaches of Thailand. On the opposite side, the island is washed by the cool waters that bring underwater currents. The sea is deeper and more uneasy, which attracts fans of surfing, diving, fishing and other entertainment.



Rest on the island Dzharylgach

On the island itself there are no tourist centers, sanatoriums and private pensions, but in the summer there are enough tourists. They come from the coast to sunbathe on deserted beaches, admire the beauty of the Dzharylgach Bay, see animals in their natural environment or simply relax in the bosom of almost wild nature. Some camp for a long time, setting up tents right on the beach. This place has attracted and continues to attract nudists from the countries of the former USSR. They have their own beaches, traditions and rules. Knowledgeable people come here to improve their health with the help of medicinal mud, which they themselves produce near salt lakes.

sights

The only attraction of the island, excluding the sea, beaches, flora and fauna, is the old lighthouse, installed on the east coast of the island in 1902 with the money of S. Skadovsky. It is known that the lighthouse was designed in France by one of the students of G. Eiffel and brought here disassembled. What is surprising is that in appearance it resembles the famous Eiffel Tower. Not far from the old lighthouse is a new one, installed in 1997. Some tourists who come to the island settle down to spend the night in a new lighthouse.
