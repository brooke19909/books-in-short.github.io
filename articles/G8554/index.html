Terms of use

How to use the metro in Dubai? Paying for the metro can be "zero" with cards and tickets. In Arabic, the word "zero" is translated as "fare." Cards are issued in gold, silver and blue colors, but tickets are only red. A significant difference between these payment methods is that the cards are an electronic wallet that can be replenished from time to time. In addition to the metro, they can be used in water and surface buses, in parking lots. The validity of smart cards is 5 years, which is very convenient for the indigenous people of the UAE. Tickets are purchased on only one route for a fixed number of trips. The validity period of one of them is 3 months.



A ticket can be purchased from a metro employee at a kiosk or in a vending machine at the station. A subscription must be bought for each passenger, different people cannot use the same card, because payment is charged depending on the distance. The funds are debited at the exit station. Children under 5 years old can ride for free, but there is a caveat: they must be below 90 cm. Some foreign craftsmen, trying to fool the miracle technique, buy one card, planning to slip into the car. But having missed one passenger, the system no longer accepts the second with the same card, so no one succeeds in such a trick.

Types of cars

Foreigners who come to the UAE need to know how to use the metro in Dubai. All cars are divided into three types: "gold", the usual for women and children. Standard seats make up about 80% of the entire train, they are almost inferior to the Gold class, they have an air conditioning system. The cost of the subway in Dubai is almost twice as high if you ride in a high-class carriage. In every train he goes first. There are no special differences from ordinary places in it, only there are always fewer people, which is connected with the price, and you can also look in the windshield, imagining yourself a machinist or just enjoying the views that open to your eyes.



The Gold carriage is divided by a partition into two parts. One of them is intended exclusively for women and their children, men are not allowed to enter there, so you need to be careful. However, women can ride in the same carriage with men, it is recommended only to adhere to the dress code in order to protect themselves from troubles. To get to the right part of the composition will help inscriptions above the glass doors to the entrance to the train.

Dubai metro stations

The red metro line is 52 km long. A train makes a full circle in an average of one hour. It has 27 stations, and two more are under construction. Most stops are on or above the ground, and only four are below it. The train mainly moves along overpasses at an altitude of 15 m. On the red line, stations 27 through 30 are skipped, apparently they will be built in the future.



The green line is much shorter, its length is 22.5 km, of which 14.6 - on the ground, 7.9 - underground. The train returns to its original stop after half an hour. Now there are 18 stations, two are under construction. The city authorities are doing everything possible for the comfort of guests who have arrived in Dubai. The metro scheme in Russian will help to organize the trip correctly and not get lost. It should be borne in mind that the stations, in addition to names, also have numbers.

Fare

The cost of the metro in Dubai directly depends on which car the passenger chooses. It should be noted that the "Gold" class is almost two times more expensive than usual. The fare also depends on the number of metro zones crossed. For many tourists it will be advantageous to purchase a subscription for one day or month for an unlimited number of trips. If you often have to use the subway, go shopping, see the sights, then this is an ideal option. A 1-day subscription for an unlimited number of trips can be purchased for 14 AED, a one-time Gold class ticket costs from 4 to 13 AED (depending on the number of zones), a one-time standard ticket is from 2 to 6.5 AED. Prices are current for February 2014.

Basic driving rules



In the Dubai Metro, it is strictly forbidden to drink alcohol, and this rule applies both in trains and at stations. It is also forbidden to transport strong drinks, and smoking rooms are equipped with special facilities, as smoking is not allowed in public places. No pets are allowed on the subway, even if they are placed in special cages and bags.

The main differences between the Dubai and Moscow metro

The first noticeable difference between the metropolitan subways of the two states is that trains run automatically in the UAE, and the drivers do not operate them. In Dubai, fare is charged per zone, short trips are subject to a separate fare. There is a maximum charge. If it is debited from the card, then the passenger can continue to travel as much as desired, but for free.

There is a difference in the location of the aprons. In Moscow, it is located in the middle, and trains come from different directions. The metro in Dubai has two different aprons, as trains run in the center. To get on them, you need to climb them or go down on the elevator, escalator or stairs. Before choosing a descent / ascent, you should decide which way to go. In the UAE, the platform is separated from the rails by a transparent wall, which opens only when combined with the doors of an approaching train. It should be recognized that this innovation protects passengers from accidents.
