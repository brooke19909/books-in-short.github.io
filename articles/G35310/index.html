Bank "Moscow Lights": reviews. Reliability of the Moscow Lights Bank

Commercial Bank "Moscow Lights" began its activities in the Russian Federation in 1993, when it received a license to conduct banking operations, and another seven years later - to attract deposits from individuals. A high level of professionalism is evidenced by membership in SWIFT, the Association of Russian Banks and ARBK. For a long time, a financial institution has provided a wide range of services to legal entities and individuals. But in February of this year, he began to have problems.





First Issue Information

In April of this year, it was announced the suspension of deposits in the "Lights of Moscow". The bank explained the problems with technical difficulties. It was announced that a new deposit program was being developed, so the acceptance of deposits was temporarily suspended. Also, the credit institution did not carry out transactions between legal entities and individuals. Despite the official statement, rumors appeared already that the problems were related to the ban of the Central Bank.

In February 2014, a scheduled check of the Central Bank was conducted, as a result of which restrictions were introduced. 12 million rubles - the maximum allowable total amount of deposits attracted to the bank ‚ÄúMoscow Lights‚Äù. The credit institution rating up to this point was rather low. The bank was in the second hundred (172nd place) in terms of assets. For the first quarter of 2013, he received 142 million profit, and for the same period of the current year - 57 million loss. As a result, two branches were closed - in Dubna and Podolsk - due to unprofitability. After this message, the reliability of the bank ‚ÄúMoscow Lights‚Äù in the eyes of many customers has declined sharply.





License revocation

On May 16, 2014, the Bank of Russia announced the revocation of a license from two capital credit institutions. One of them is the Moscow Lights Bank. The license was revoked due to repeated non-compliance with the rules and regulations. At the same time, it was announced that deposits would be paid no later than May 27. This decision was made by the Agency for Insurance Deposits (DIA). For participants of the mentioned system, revocation of a license is an insurance event. Under the agreement, the depositor is entitled to a 100% reimbursement of the deposit, but the total amount of reimbursements should not exceed seven hundred thousand rubles. Former customers of a credit institution began to be actively interested in the registry for insurance payments.

Problems with DIA when returning deposits

Not all former customers of the bank saw themselves on the lists for compensation payments. Many claimed that they were denied payment due to the fact that their names were not in the bank registry of deposits. As it turned out later, transactions were carried out without notifying customers about the closure of accounts. Responsible for these frauds is the Moscow Lights Bank. Customer reviews on the forums confirm this. In the hands of depositors there are extracts that prove that they did not give consent to such operations.





There must always be a way out

Despite the termination of contracts unilaterally, the bank accrued interest on deposits, so some of the funds can be withdrawn. The rest remains to wait for the decision of the DIA. The agency reported that they are actively working to resolve this issue, and there should not be problems with compensation. Applications for insurance compensation may be submitted within two years. This is the average term for liquidation of banks.

Those investors who initially did not find themselves in the registry of compensation payments, lawyers recommend that you follow the following procedure. To begin, write a statement to the DIA for inclusion in the register of insurance payments. Next, you need to write a statement to the police about the situation and file a lawsuit. If within 30 calendar days the DIA does not give an answer, then a second suit should be filed - already with the agency. This procedure should not be neglected. This may be the only way to return the funds invested in the Bank of Moscow. The feedback of former Master Bank depositors, whose license was revoked back in December 2013, only confirms this. Many of the clients still cannot receive a response from the DIA.

The first, after signing the agreements with the client and making the depositor money to the account, transferred funds to the accounts of related companies at night of the same day. What to do to clients with a deposit amount of more than 700 thousand rubles

The claims of customers who made deposits in the amount of more than 700 thousand rubles to the Banks of Moscow should be satisfied first of all. After the license is revoked, the Bank of Russia appoints an interim administration that will manage the credit institution until the court decides to begin the liquidation procedure.

To present their claims, creditors (former bank depositors) must write a written statement, indicate in it the amount and details for the transfer of funds. Copies or originals of supporting documents must be attached to the application. It can be:

  • agreement with the bank;
  • court decision that has entered into force;
  • documents that confirm transactions to an account with a credit institution;
  • account statement indicating the balance, preferably on the date of revocation of the license;
  • other supporting documents.




Money Back Procedure

The provisional administration shall notify the applicant of the inclusion of his requirements in the register (in whole or in part) or of refusal within thirty days from the date of receipt of the application. The latter can be closed no earlier than 60 days after the publication of the notice on the liquidation of the credit institution in the newspaper Vesti Bank of Russia. The exact date for closing the registry will be indicated in the announcement itself.

The procedure for satisfying customer requirements is as follows. First of all, individuals who have entered into bank deposit agreements are served, and obligations arising from harm to life or health are satisfied. Further - the requirements of the Bank of Russia, the obligation to pay severance pay, according to the employment contract. Each subsequent group of requirements is satisfied after full fulfillment of the obligations of the previous one.
