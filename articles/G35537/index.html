Tunisia Attractions

Tunisia is a country with a rich history. A large number of archaeological sites on its territory demonstrate the historical role of the state as an important crossroads of civilizations.

Markets regularly operate in the historical center of Medina, where merchants sell colorful goods and trinkets, while the streets turn into a picture from the Arabian fairy tale ‚Äú1001 Nights. Local weavers and seamstresses offer their handicrafts there. You can watch fishermen in the port, and in restaurants cities taste traditional fish dishes.

The city of Kairouan occupies an important place in the Islamic world. This is due to its history and monuments protected by UNESCO World Heritage. The grand mosque of the city is considered a sacred place. The atrium is open to non-religious visitors. Near the city wall of Suk, numerous merchants set up market stalls, while inviting tourists to buy Islamic national works of art.





The Mediterranean coast of Tunisia has long been a popular destination among holidaymakers from all over Europe. In addition to the endless beautiful beaches, sunny mild climate, the historical Medina attracts many guests with magnificent architectural monuments and local cuisine.

Tunisia Weather Conditions

The weather in Tunisia is warmer throughout the year than in Europe. It can be hot during the day, but when the sun goes down, the temperature drops sharply by 10 or more degrees.

In winter (December, January, February) and spring (March, April and until mid-May), as well as in autumn (October and November), the differences in night and day temperatures are very significant.

In the summer, at the height of the day, the temperature is around 40 ¬∞ C in the shade and this is normal in Tunisia. At night, the temperature drops to 20-30 ¬∞ C.

Europeans should avoid exposure to the sun between 11: 00-16: 00 during the summer months, as significant sunburn and heat stroke can occur.

The most pleasant months to visit PrimaSol El Mehdi Aqua (Tunisia, Mahdia) - March, April and May, although the water temperature is only 17-20 ¬∞ C.

If you want to relax in the pool or on the beach and get the most out of the sun and heat, then you need to choose the summer months. July, August and September are suitable months for visiting Tunisia in case you like hot and dry weather.





From mid-September to half-November, the weather becomes much cooler, but the sea is still pleasant 22-27 ¬∞ C, and rainy days are rare. Therefore, these months are recommended for tourists who do not like the hot weather in Tunisia and basically want to relax near the water. It is also favorable weather for golf lovers.

December, January and February are the cold months to relax. The temperature usually reaches 10-15 ¬∞ C during the day, and at night it is no more than 5-10 degrees Celsius. At this time, often windy and cloudy weather. Older tourists prefer this time because of the mild climate.
