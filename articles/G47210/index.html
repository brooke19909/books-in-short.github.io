What is pheromone and what are its functions?

What is pheromone?

From Greek, this word is translated as "attracting hormone." This substance was first described in 1959 by scientists Peter Karlsson and Martin Lusher. So what is pheromone? The meaning of this term is that the body of a living creature produces a substance containing information that can affect the behavior of individuals around it. Use in cosmetology

So, we found out what pheromone is. Now let's talk about its use in the cosmetic and perfumery industries. It has long been known that sex hormones, or rather, their derivatives (attractants), are added to perfumes. It has been experimentally proven that the use of such substances increases the attractiveness of the opposite sex on an unconscious level. According to the results of the study, people using this substance are much more likely to attract attention.

What kind of research was this?

The experiment involved 100 men and women aged 18 to 47 years who did not know what pheromone was and never resorted to it. For 2 weeks, 50 people used a placebo, and the other 50 used perfumes and perfumes with pheromones. As a result, 74% of subjects experienced a 3-fold increase in sexual desire , and only 23% experienced this in the placebo group. It's about dating, affection, kisses and sex.
