Sights of Ivanovo and the region

The ancient Russian city of Ivanovo, located in Central Russia, is well known not only in our country, but also far beyond its borders. Often it is called the Textile Capital of Russia, Calico, as well as the City of Brides.





From the history of the city

The first settlements on the Uvod River appeared at the beginning of the 14th century. It is believed that the village of Ivanovo is named after the famous John the Baptist. In 1871, it was annexed to the Ascension Posad. As a result, a new city arose - Ivanovo-Voznesensk. It has long been a center of light industry. It was here, at one of the textile manufactories, that the first steam engine appeared.

Many of Ivanov‚Äôs sights are connected with the revolutionary activity of the working people of the city. Spontaneous riots and strikes of workers dissatisfied with working conditions often flashed here. Due to this, in 1905 the first Council of Workers' Deputies was formed here.

Ivanovo sights, photos and descriptions of which are in all advertising booklets of travel agencies organizing trips to this city, should begin to be examined from the Monument to the fighters of the revolution. It was installed in 1975 on the Revolution Square. According to art critics, the composition is ambiguous. It consists of two parts. The first is a dark rectangle symbolizing prison casemates, from which fire breaks out - a symbol of revolution. And inside the granite monument - illuminated crystal, very reminiscent of a beating heart.

The second part of the monument is a sculptural group and is called ‚ÄúRaising the Banner‚Äù. The team that created the monument (artist M. Milyutin, architect E. Kutyrev and sculptor D. Ryabichev) was awarded the USSR State Prize in 1978.

The sights of Ivanov are a lot of museums, ancient churches, houses of manufacturers, magnificent estates. In addition, the unique beauty of nature should be noted. Thick spruce and pine forests, birch oak groves and groves create an atmosphere of romanticism and mystery, give the feeling of a fairy tale and give a little mystical touch to these places.





Sights of Ivanovo and Ivanovo region. Monasteries

History buffs are encouraged to start exploring the sights of Ivanov from the Holy Vvedensky Monastery. The Vvedenskaya church of this complex is located on Bazisnaya street, 23. By the way, at any kiosk selling printed products, you can buy a map of the city, which lists all the sights of Ivanovo with addresses.

This temple was decided to be built at the gathering of residents of Ushakovo and the Pit in 1900. Land for construction was provided by Count Sergei Dmitrievich Sheremetyev. In those days, he owned many lands in Ushakovo. In the spring of 1901, the solemn laying of the temple in Ivanovo took place. Sights, photos of which can be seen in this article, replenished with a new exhibit. The church was built on private donations.





Six years later, in the summer of 1907, construction was completed. The temple was consecrated by Archbishop of Vladimir Nikolai.

The design of the church clearly shows the motives of Byzantine architecture. A three-tier iconostasis was installed, made in the workshop of A.I. Shirokov. The floor is covered with metlah tiles.

In 1909, a two-story house was built in the courtyard of the complex, which in its appearance resembled 17th-century boyars' chambers . It housed the apartments of the watchmen and prosphora. The entire territory of the temple was surrounded by a metal fence with three gates.

In 1938, the religious building was closed. Divine services were resumed only in 1989. It should be noted that the return of the temple to the parishioners was accompanied by tragic events. Four women went on a hunger strike and spent 11 days and nights on the porch of the church, demanding an immediate solution to the issue of returning the temple.

In March 1991, the Holy Vvedensky Convent was opened here, and the construction of monastic buildings began on its territory.

Holy Assumption Monastery

This monastery was built with the blessing of Patriarch Alexy II in May 1998.

At the end of the 18th century in the southeast of Ivanovo was the Assumption Cemetery. In 1815, a wooden church was transferred to this land from the Pokrovsky monastery. A little later, a bell tower appeared, created in the style of classicism by the architect Petrov. In 1924, the temple was transferred to the Renovationists, and in 1933 the cemetery was destroyed. In its place, an amusement park of the Melange Plant was arranged. Citizens often call this place "the park of the living and the dead." The church was transferred to the Ivanovo Electric Networks, after which it was rebuilt and changed beyond recognition.

After the church was returned to the Russian Orthodox Church in 1995, a community began to gather here, choosing the monastic path for itself.

The construction of a new temple can be called a nationwide affair. Both parishioners and philanthropists took part in it, including famous people such as Nikita Mikhalkov, Andrey Bykov, Mikhail Chepel.

The monastery takes pilgrims. Everyone can come here and stay for three days.

Museums

As you know, this city is inextricably linked with the textile industry. During the Patriotic War, local enterprises practically clothed and shod the entire Soviet Army. Therefore, it is quite natural that acquaintance with him often begins with the Museum of Ivanovo Chintz. It is located in the mansion of the manufacturer D. G. Burygin. The building was designed by architect A.F. Snurilov. This building has a complex architectural layout - carved doors, decorative tiles, stained-glass windows and other original decor elements. Some exhibition halls, a library, stock rooms are located in the building of a carriage shed in the courtyard. It was built in 1914. Unfortunately, the name of the architect is unknown.





The central exposition of the museum tells about the history of Ivanovo textiles, about how this production was born in the Ivanovo region. Here you can see a unique collection of fabrics, which began to form back in the late 19th century.

Locals are very hospitable. They are proud of their city. Showing guests the sights of Ivanov, they will certainly lead you to the Ivanovo Art Museum. It is located in the city center, on Lenin Avenue, 33.

The museum exposition is amazing. Here are collected unique exhibits representing the culture of different peoples - from ancient Egyptian monuments of the 21st century BC to modern art. In total in his collection there are more than forty thousand exhibits. The museum is one of the largest in Central Russia.

Here you can see truly unique relics: the two-sided icon ‚ÄúOur Lady of Shuyskaya-Smolenskaya and Nikola‚Äù, the ascetic and restrained main page ‚ÄúDeesis‚Äù, the touching temple icon ‚ÄúThe Prudent Robber‚Äù.

Museum of Military Transport Aviation

Many mistakenly believe that all the sights of Ivanov are associated exclusively with the textile industry. This is far from the case. A museum of military transport aviation is located on the territory of the combat training center for the flight personnel of the Severny garrison.



The building in which it is located has historical value - the legendary Normandy-Niemen fighter squadron was formed here.

Today, the museum exposition is presented at the airport and in the exhibition halls. She acquaints visitors with the history of formations and regiments of military transport aviation. You can see aircraft models, historical photos, maps, interesting documents.

Sights of Ivanovo: Brest region

In the south of the Brest region is the Ivanovo district with a regional center - the city of Ivanovo. It has been known since the 14th century as the village of Porkhovo.

The current city was created in 1940 (Pinsk region). In 1954, it became part of the Brest region. It has been in its current borders since 1965. The population of the city of Ivanovo is 16.5 thousand people. He stands on the Samarovka River, one hundred and thirty kilometers from Brest.

The sights of Ivanovo in the Brest region include more than 125 registered monuments of history, architecture and archeology. Among them are the following:

  • sites from the Bronze Age and the Neolithic;
  • churches - St. Nicholas (d. Druzhilovichi), Peter and Paul (d. Mokhro), Prechistenskaya (d. Lyaskovichi) and others;
  • monuments of architecture of the 14-20th centuries.

They sacredly honor the memory of the victims of World War II in the Belarusian Ivanovo. Sights dedicated to this topic include 84 monuments and an obelisk.



The city and the homonymous district has a rich history. The famous Dostoevsky family comes from the village of Dostoevo, in the village of Vorotsevichi the great scientist, musicologist, ethnographer and artist Napoleon Orda lived and worked.
