The rules of the game of billiards and its varieties

Billiards is one of the most exciting games with an ancient origin, but has not lost its relevance to this day. It guarantees an interesting pastime and hides unlimited possibilities. Among other types of sports, billiards is the most democratic, since it does not set a strict framework in terms of age or physical fitness. All that is needed from the players is to thoroughly study the rules of the game of billiards, learn the basics and develop their own technique, gradually bringing it to perfection.



There are several dozen types of billiards, but the main ones are only five: Russian billiards, two types of pool (sports and entertainment), as well as cannon and snooker. They, in turn, have a lot of variations, each of which is called differently. The general rules of the game of billiards are simple and boil down to the following. The action takes place at a special table: it is called billiard. On it, in a certain order, balls are set, which can be of different colors and sizes. The player is required, holding a cue, in a certain way to hit him on one of the balls, so that he touches the other balls on the table. Before striking, it is imperative that the following important conditions are observed: the cue must move freely in a straight plane, and the position of the body must be fixed and convenient for the player himself. The goal of the game is to score as many balls as possible in special holes. In many varieties of billiards, fines may be imposed on players: for example, for an opponent or an outside observer interfering in a game, for deliberately delaying a game, for touching an outside ball, for incorrect execution of a blow, etc.

And then the variations begin. For example, the rules of the game of Russian billiards are complicated by the fact that the balls in it are relatively large and heavy, and the pockets - on the contrary, are small. Therefore, only experienced billiard players can play the game. But the pool in this respect is more democratic. The rules of the game of American billiards are reduced to a competent choice of tactics and strategy. It is not difficult to score a ball: it is small, and the pocket is almost one and a half times wider than its diameter. Therefore, in this variation of the game, opponents try to create an ideal strike position for the cue ball, as well as in every way make it difficult for their opponent to hit.



Those who are just starting to learn the rules of the game of billiards often make mistakes. They do not fully develop a strategy, do not improve their technique, and put a blow incorrectly.
