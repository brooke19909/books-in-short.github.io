Upper East Side: a walk in New York

The Upper East Side is one of New York's most expensive, lush and fashionable districts. In addition, he is very beautiful.

The symbol of the Upper East Side is a stretch stretching from 5th Avenue to Central Park. On the pages of guidebooks and reference books, this place is called the Museum Mile. The thing is that on this relatively small patch of New York, a huge number of museums and art galleries are concentrated.

Kingdom of consumption

For obvious reasons, the Upper East Side is expensive in all areas of its life. This concerns not only residential and commercial real estate, but also prices in supermarkets and tariffs for the provision of domestic services. The entire 5th Avenue is represented by multi-storey buildings, which are cut with gray green peaks gray sidewalks running along the residences.





Under these canopies, anticipating the entrance to the building, the receptionist and porters are on duty around the clock, who at the right time help wealthy residents to leave the car interior. Wealthy owners of real estate here come either in bright yellow taxis or in their own cars. At the same time, traffic jams on 5th Avenue practically never happen.

Museum ridge

The Upper East Side is a museum home. On the streets of the district are the Whitney Museum, the Guggenheim, the Metropolitan and many other expositions. The most magnificent mansions of the district are located on that part of 5th Avenue, which borders Park Avenue. Everything that is located beyond this intersection looks much more modest.

If you do not go into details, then 5th Avenue is distinguished by a large number of apartment buildings. On the streets of the district rise private town houses and cottages. The upper East Side of New York captivates with an abundance of decorative details that can be seen everywhere: from the decoration of door handles to the design of window spans.

Cooperative movement

Many apartments and houses do not belong to individual individuals, but to so-called cooperatives. All members of the cooperative have the right to vote in the sale of housing. This means that buying an apartment on the East Side, without the support of old-timers, will fail.
