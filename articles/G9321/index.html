Why visit the Marble Canyon?

In the distant eighteenth century, in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe village of Ruskeala (Karelia), such a rare and expensive stone as marble was discovered. Near the local waterfalls, among coniferous plants, were these white-gray monoliths of incredible beauty. Since then, active industrial mining of marble began in Karelia, which was chipped and exported far beyond the borders of the modern republic. This natural material formed the basis for the construction of many temples and palaces, which are now considered a work of art in Russia. Among these, it is mainly worth highlighting the Winter Palace. However, industry in this northern region stopped with the outbreak of World War II, and since then, Ruskeale has remained a huge marble canyon filled with emerald waters of local lakes and waterfalls, which attracts tourists from all over the world.



Many modern citizens of Russia are sure that Karelia is an ideal place for a winter holiday. And they are absolutely right. Well, where else can you see such a fabulous beauty in which nature and human activity intertwine? Marble Canyon is a whole valley filled with the waters of lakes and submarine rivers that flocked here as a result of stone production. Amazing transparency, purity and emerald luster of local waters attract tourists from all over the world.



This marvelous place often hosts excursions, which include boat and boat trips along high cleaved coasts. Visiting the marble canyon, everyone can enjoy the beauty of nature both day and night. In this place, a complex lighting system is mounted on the mountain slopes and under water, so even in the dark, everything is fabulously beautiful. Guides also offer everyone a visit to the grotto through which marble was exported to other regions of the country.

You can get to this amazing place through St. Petersburg.
