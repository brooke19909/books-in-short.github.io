What is the rabbit foot used for? Features of making a mascot

As ancient legends say, the value of talismans has always been of great importance to their owners. Initially, the main character of many beliefs was a hare, but over time, magical qualities spread to the rabbit.

Introduction

Although the first mention of the talismans made from the parts of the body of these animals was found among the ancient Celts, who lived several millennia BC, the spread of the beliefs of the ancient peoples passed very quickly and gained immense popularity. Now it is believed that the rabbit foot brings good luck, opens up new horizons and contributes to the safe resolution of all problems that its owner may have. It is best to hang this amulet on a chain of silver or other precious metal. Then, in addition to the above properties, the fertility, such as rabbits and their wild counterparts, will be added to the owner of the talisman.



Application

If the rabbit's foot is attached to a pram, then this is an excellent protection against diseases, troubles and misfortunes. Also, this amulet is presented to actors who are at the beginning of their career paths. This gift is presented after the first premiere, but it does not need to be carried with you always. It is best to leave the talisman near the makeup, then inspiration and a positive attitude will not leave the actor. If the rabbit's foot is located at the newlyweds' bed, then, according to beliefs, they will soon have a replenishment. In addition, it contributes to a long family life and marital happiness.

History





Of course, such myths should be treated with healthy skepticism, but they are presented not only in European culture and, most likely, have good reason. Indeed, among the peoples of China, South and North America and Africa, rabbits are considered sacred animals. In ancient Greece, the hare and its domesticated counterpart were the favorites of Aphrodite. In the Middle Kingdom, they believed that the jade rabbit that lives on the lunar surface is crushed by the elixir of immortality, and his earthly helpers help him in this. The Indians of North America claimed that these furry creatures helped them open fire, and treated them with great respect. In Buddhists, the rabbit is a symbol of self-sacrifice, because ancient legends say that he offered himself to the suffering people. As you can see from these examples, the veneration of these cute creatures has taken place over the millennia of human history.
