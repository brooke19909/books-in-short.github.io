Iowa is one of the most colorful states in the USA. History and Attractions

The name of this state is related to its Native American origin. About 13 thousand years ago, the territory was inhabited by the Iowa, Missouri and Santi tribes. In the XIII century, France and Spain fought for these fertile lands, and after 100 years, US authorities bought their future staff, which later became one of the main objects of the struggle for the Wild West.

Clashes with the Indians

In the mid-19th century, Iowa became fully American. But the policy of crowding out the Native American Indian population led to armed conflicts in different parts of the state in which a large number of people died. Later, the authorities think about how to attract immigrants to the liberated territory, as a result of which the state is gradually settled by immigrants from Germany and Scandinavia, who subsequently took an active part in the development of agriculture.

Food capital

The state of Iowa, called "corn," was visited more than 50 years ago by N. S. Khrushchev. During the visit, he looked at the farms of local residents and wanted to grow the "queen of the fields" in the USSR.

It is not for nothing that this American territory is considered the world food capital: annually a large crop of oats and soybeans is received here, and the state has long been recognized as the leader in terms of the number of pigs and cattle.





However, economic crises led to the fact that the agricultural region began to pay special attention to industry. The state has several factories for processing and producing canned foods.

Multi-million state

Famous for the vast plains and prairies that make up a large part of the natural landscape, Iowa has a large population. More than 3 million people live in it. The state capital is the former Fort Des Moines, built back in 1843. Now it has turned into a large beautiful city with developed infrastructure and a powerful insurance sector.

This is not to say that the state is extremely attractive for tourists, because there are very unstable weather conditions that lead to storms, tornadoes and floods up to 37 times a year. However, despite this, the state has numerous museums that attract curious travelers, and natural attractions are unusual for their special beauty untouched by civilization.

USA, Iowa: attractions. Law library

The main attraction is an architectural monument, opened in 1886 in the state capital. The capitol with 5 domes is the only one in the country, it houses the working office of the Iowa Governor. Until the 30s of the XX century, this building, causing great interest among tourists, was the highest.

On the 2nd floor of the Capitol there is a law library, the doors of which are always open for those who are interested in issues of law, the history of jurisprudence and international legal problems. It is worth noting that among visitors to this unusual library there are many ordinary inhabitants who have heard about the beauties of this magnificent building. The library has over 200 thousand items and is one of the most significant collections of legal literature in the United States.

You can climb into the central hall, which accommodates 4 levels of galleries, through metal forged stairs equipped with handrails. The ceiling and floor are decorated with bright glass mosaics , and the high walls are decorated with marble panels. This ancient building is rightfully one of the most beautiful libraries in the world.

Religious shrine

The historic site, considered an important religious shrine, is located in West Bend (Iowa). Sights, consisting of 9 grottoes, were created by human hands. Built by a priest cured of illness and offering prayers to the Virgin Mary, the Atonement Grotto now looks like a small block.



For more than 40 years, hard work has been going on to erect a shrine of precious stones and minerals. In an unusual museum is a bronze sculpture of a builder-priest, made by his followers.

Zoo for children and adults

In the state capital there is a unique zoo that allows not only to consider animals, but also to feed them. On a small site there is an aquarium with fish from around the world, cages with formidable lions and tigers, funny monkeys, funny penguins. All inhabitants of wildlife are kept in conditions close to natural.





Playgrounds with various entertainments have been built for children: fascinating labyrinths, high slides, corners for archaeological excavations.

The state of Iowa allows you to immerse yourself in American history and culture, visit the picturesque botanical gardens, take a trip to Fourth Street of Sioux City, famous for hosting open-air jazz festivals. Tourists note the special friendliness of the local population, who carefully preserves the memory of their ancient roots.
