SPL-EXPERIENCE: how to fill out section 1-2?

Consider the example of filling out a declaration if the organization has employees who have retired over the current year. The report consists of a header and 5 sections. Information is placed on a single sheet for duplex printing. Each page is numbered: 001 and 002. The document must be filled in block letters or with a ballpoint pen or on a computer.

The heading of the document contains brief information about the organization: number in the FIU, TIN, KPP, short name of the organization.

Section 1 contains information about the policyholder. Here it is necessary to duplicate the registration number in the FIU, TIN and KPP and note the type of report: initial, additional or purpose of the pension.

Section 2 indicates the reporting period (2017). When to take SZV-EXPERIENCE? At the end of the current year.

Section 3

It contains information about the employees of the company. The section has the form of a table consisting of 14 columns. First, data about the employee is filled in: full name and SNILS (1-5 points), the period of his work in the organization (6-7 points) in the format "dd.mm.yyyy". This period shall not exceed the period for the submission of the report. If it has several periods for one insured person, then each of them is drawn up as a separate line. Breaks in the work of employees are reflected by special codes. Their detailed list will be presented later.

Special cases

When to pass SZV-STAZH if the organization is subject to liquidation? The document should be filled out for the period from January 1 of the current year until the moment when the Federal Tax Service enters data on termination of activity in the register. On the cover page, the report type should indicate ‚Äúsource‚Äù. In addition to information about the organization itself, the report should reflect data on company employees who work on all types of contracts. The SZV-STAGE form must be completed only in the first three sections. The last two sections should be left blank. Reporting is necessary within a month from the date of the decision. Otherwise, the organization faces a fine of 500 rubles. for each employed employee.

For example, the founders of LLC 06/30/17 decided to liquidate the company. By September 11, 17, an interim balance was formed. This means that reporting on all employed persons is necessary until 10.10.17. In this situation, the organization does not have the right to submit zero reporting. If at the time of making the decision the company did not already include employees, then the report should include information on the only founder who was charged and paid salary.





Dismissal

If an employee leaves, then the bookkeeping department needs to compile a report on it of SZV-STAZH in two copies: the first to be handed over to the FIU, and the second to the employee. The document can either be handed in person or sent by e-mail. The main thing is to have confirmation of the issuance of the form to the laid-off employee.

On the day of dismissal, in addition to the report, the employee must be issued:

  • work book;
  • certificate of salary;
  • copy of SZV-M;
  • other documents at the request of the employee.

Up to what date should I take the SPL-STAFF? Ideally, on the day of dismissal.

Penalties

If a person leaves, then the accounting department is obliged to issue a certificate of SZV-STAZH and a new calculation of insurance premiums in his hands. For violation of this requirement of Federal Law No. 27, a fine of 50,000 rubles is provided. Information on such employees is provided in the report at the end of 2017. If a person of retirement age leaves, then SZV-STAZH at retirement must be returned immediately after registration in the workbook.

Reporting to the founder

The report was developed so that all information about his seniority is reflected on the individual account of the insured person. Based on these data, retirement points and the amount of insurance benefit will subsequently be calculated. It is necessary to report on all employees working under labor and civil law contracts. Therefore, a logical question arises: "Do I need to compile a report on the CEO, who is the sole founder of the company?"

If the manager works under an employment contract and receives remuneration for his work, then you need to report on it in the same way as for any other employee.

In practice, directors rarely enter into employment contracts with an enterprise. And such cases cause a lot of controversy among lawyers and economists. From a legal point of view, the director enters into an employment relationship with the organization. So, information about him should be submitted in the report.

From a tax point of view, the information in the report is submitted about the employees on whom insurance premiums were transferred. If there were no payments, then the specified period is not included in the length of service. Why then submit information that does not affect the calculation of pensions? There is no direct answer to this question in the legislation. Some employers adhere to the opinion of lawyers and even in such a situation transfer data about the manager to the FIU.

Another thing is if the contract is concluded, but no payments were made. Then the information on the CEO must be included in the report, indicating the additional condition ‚ÄúLeave without salary‚Äù in column 11.

When a similar question arose with filling out the CBA-M, no consensus was found. In a letter from the FIU No. LCH-08-19 / 10581, the following clarifications were provided. If the organization does not have insurance persons for which insurance premiums would be charged, then there is no need to submit reports. From the same letter it followed that it is necessary to include information about the head of the organization in the document in any case. A similar situation arose with SZV-HUNDRED.

Consider another controversial point. Information on individuals with whom a labor or civil law contract was concluded during the year but no payments were made should be included in the SZV-STAGE report. If an employee has worked in the organization for at least one day, then he must be paid a salary. Accrual rather than payment is the basis for including information in the report.
