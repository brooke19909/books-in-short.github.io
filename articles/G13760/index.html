Orel: tourist reviews, attractions, city history, interesting facts and photos

The date of foundation of this wonderful city is considered to be 1566. Thanks to the initiative of the Boyar Duma, a fortress was founded at that time, designed to protect against nomadic raids of nomadic steppe tribes. But the famous Nikon chronicle says that the founder of the city is Ivan the Terrible, who was at that time the king.

The population is just over 315 thousand people. You can get to Orel by taking a train from the Kursk station in Moscow. Travel time is a little over 4 hours.

General historical information

This city, which has a rich history and high culture, was badly destroyed during the Great Patriotic War. Today it provides tourists with a great opportunity to get to know better Russian culture and its history.

The ancient fortress was located on the coastal territory of the Oka and Orlik flowing into it. The history of the fortress began with the fact that initially it was a defense point on the southern borders of the Moscow state. Then it became the center of the Oryol district. In troubled times, it was destroyed, and then restored in a completely different quality.

At the time of Ukraine‚Äôs accession to Russia, the city of Oryol did not need to perform defensive functions. Everything began to take shape so that as the state grew, the city of Oryol (photo presented in the article) began to turn into an important trading hub. From the time of Peter the Great, Moscow and St. Petersburg began to be supplied with bread thanks to agriculture in Orel.

On the history of the name of the city

These territories abound with numerous archaeological finds. The area even before the foundation of the fortress was inhabited by Vyatichi tribes.

There are three hypotheses regarding the name of the city. One of them is based on a legend telling that during the construction of the fortress a huge eagle flew from a large tree. Then people thought that this was a direct instruction to give the fortress a name by the name of this bird. Another hypothesis is based on scientific research on the transformation of the Turkic word ‚Äúangular‚Äù, characterizing the location of the future city at the confluence of two rivers, into the word ‚Äúeagle‚Äù. The third version says that the name of the city was transformed from a Baltic language word meaning "hay meadow". Such a name could remain from the Golyad tribe, who also lived in these territories.

Be that as it may, and whatever the name of the city, the reviews about Oryol among its guests are mostly positive. Called the third cultural capital of the country, it delights its guests and local residents with an abundance of interesting monuments and historical places.

Reviews about the Eagle are very diverse. By itself, it is very green, beautiful and quite fresh in appearance. Transport

Throughout the city runs the main highway, stretching from the beginning of the Kromskoye Highway on the street. Komsomolskaya to Peace Square. Minibuses, buses, trolleybuses and trams go along it. In general, the city has no problems with public transport, but there are some with the presence of places in them. Often traffic jams occur. After midnight, you have to use a taxi or walk.

On the condition of the roads, the reviews are not very good. Most of them require repair, and the administration does not always have enough money for this.

Housing and climate

In administrative terms, the city is divided into four districts: Zavodskoy (the most central and largest), Zheleznodorozhny, Sovetsky and Severny.

The city is quite suitable for moving and living in it, according to reviews. Where is the best place to live in Orel? The most convenient for this is Zavodskoy district. In Sovetskiy, the main entertainment centers are concentrated. In the Zheleznodorozhny district, mainly private sector houses are located. The most developing and young is the Northern region. It is replete with numerous new buildings.

Summer in these parts is warm (average temperature is + 20-30 degrees), and it is surprising that there are few mosquitoes. In winter it is not very cold (up to -10 degrees) and it rains up to the New Year. Ecology is good, water and air are clean. Things are slightly worse in areas near the railway and industries.

Work

According to the reviews of those who moved, Oryol is not the best place in terms of job availability. Factories are gradually dying, and the remaining enterprises are barely holding on. Mainly in the city are engaged in trade. If its industry was previously developed, as in many provincial Russian cities, now it is going through hard times. Therefore, many have to get a job not in their specialty in order to somehow survive and earn at least the most necessary.

The official average salary is at the level of 25 thousand rubles, although, apparently, this value is overstated. A more realistic figure is 15 thousand rubles.

A little about the city of Orlov

There is another historical city in Russia with a similar name. This is the city of Orlov, which is the administrative center of the Oryol city settlement, located in the Kirov region. The city is located on the high right bank of the Vyatka. The highway Kirov - Nizhny Novgorod passes through the whole city. A distinctive feature of this small town is a straightforward correct layout. Only two main streets are city-forming: Oryol and Lenin (originally Moscow). The address of the administration of the city of Orlov is st. Lenin, d. 78.

Orlov, like Oryol, is one of the oldest cities in Russia. Under this name, he was first mentioned in the annals of 1459, which tells about the campaign of the troops of Prince Vasily the Second, whose purpose was the capture of the Vyatka lands and their connection with the Moscow state. On the site of the current city of Orlov, the settlement appeared as early as the 12th-13th centuries. Since 1923, for some time it was called as the city of Khalturin. This name was given to him in honor of the revolutionary Stepan Khalturin, who is a native of Oryol county.
