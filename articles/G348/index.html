Reasons for the devaluation of the hryvnia in recent years





The main reasons for the decline in quotations of the Ukrainian hryvnia, experts call the decline in production after the change of power in 2014. In addition, there was a significant outflow of capital outside the country. The trade balance of the Ukrainian economy should also be taken into account. All these factors had a significant impact on the decrease in foreign exchange earnings of exporters and increased demand for foreign currency.

It should be noted that after President Petro Poroshenko came to power in the spring of 2014, the Ukrainian currency was in a static position for some time. Many attribute this to the artificial maintenance of the national currency rate until early parliamentary elections in the autumn of that year. Just a few months after the election campaign, the hryvnia devalued by more than 50%. If you look at how much one hryvnia in rubles costs in 2016 and compare with its quotes at the beginning of 2015, you can see an almost identical ratio.
