Petrich city in Bulgaria: photos, attractions and reviews

At the foot of a mountain called Belasitsa lies the city of Petrich. It is located in the most fertile region of Bulgaria - along the banks of the river in the valley of Strumeshnitsa. The city gained special popularity thanks to the famous seer Wange. We invite you on a virtual trip to the city of Petrich in Bulgaria. The program includes acquaintance with the history, appearance of the city today and sights!





Location

Petrich is 23 kilometers from the famous resort of Sandanski. Almost 190 kilometers are separated by Petrich and Sofia. This Bulgarian city is located very close to the border point with Macedonia - between them 20 km. But Petrich separates 13 kilometers from the border with Greece.

Petrich field is the most fertile territory of the country. A variety of vegetables and fruits grow here, in these places lemons and kiwi are grown.

Petrich (Bulgaria): photo and history of the city

The results of archaeological excavations say that Thracian settlements used to be in these places. The first settlements on the territory of the present city appeared in the X-XI centuries, and after another two or three centuries a real regional center appeared here. The ancient city of Heraclea Sintica is connected with the city of Petrich in Bulgaria.

Petrich became part of the Bulgarian state during the reign of Prince Boris the First. The city played an important defensive role in the wars of Tsar Samuel with the Byzantines. The city managed to preserve its incredible Bulgarian character even in difficult times - during the Ottoman rule. It is important to note that 90% of the population in the 16th century were Christians.





When the period began, called by scientists as the period of the national Bulgarian Renaissance, Petrich became the most important center of crafts and trade. In the late 60s of the XIX century, the first Orthodox church dedicated to St. Nicholas appeared here. At that time, the first Bulgarian school was opened in the city. The city took an active part in the national liberation war. The city was liberated from Turkish power during the Balkan War.

sights

The city of Petrich in Bulgaria is not only fertile land, but also a large number of attractions. Some of them are located within the city, some - outside. We offer to consider the most interesting places!

Samuel Fortress

One of the most visited Petrich sights in Bulgaria is Samuel Fortress. It is located 16 kilometers from the city. The story goes: this fortress was built by the Bulgarian king Samuel, who wanted to repel the Byzantine invasion. In 1014, the Bulgarian army was defeated: the troops of Basil II defeated. Byzantine troops captured about fourteen thousand soldiers. The emperor gave a truly monstrous order: all the captives were blinded. For one hundred soldiers, there was only one person who had one eye left - so that he could take his co-workers home. According to legend, King Samuel simply could not bear what he saw and died on the spot.





Today, Samuilova Fortress is a park museum, included in the list of Hundred National Tourist Attractions of Bulgaria. The museum includes 13 small dugouts, one large dugout and the ruins of the fortress observation tower. Above the ruins, by the way, there is a panoramic platform with a memorial and a hall for exhibits.

Nature reserve

Those who have come to Rupita should pay attention to the sources of mineral water. A sign with the inscription that says "Swimming is prohibited" is hardly needed here: the fact is that it is impossible to lower even a hand into the source - the water temperature is 75 degrees! Therefore, it is not surprising that a curtain of steam is constantly standing over mineral springs.

Researchers say: the main reason for the emergence of sources here is the ancient volcanic processes that still occur underground. The village itself (and with it the springs) are located on a volcanic plateau, formerly a crater. Due to the high temperature, heat-loving birds nest in these places, exotic plants grow.

City History Museum

Speaking about the sights of the city of Petrich in Bulgaria, one cannot but say about this museum. Huge expositions, including about 18 thousand exhibits, will be transferred to the past and tell about the brave and courageous Bulgarians. The museum, located in the city center, was originally a small museum collection, but four decades after its founding - in 2006 - it was transformed into the History Museum by decision of the municipal council.





Among the most interesting exhibits, visitors to the museum note a magnificent collection of theatrical masks of the 2nd century, tombstones dating from the third century BC, the decree of the emperor Galerius from 308. It is advised to pay attention to the coins of Philip the Second and Alexander the Third.

Churches

In their reviews, tourists say: the city of Petrich (Bulgaria) is famous for its churches and temples. In 1868, the church of St. Nicholas appeared in the city. They erected it near the old cemetery. Almost all the inhabitants of the city worked at a grand construction site. They donated funds for the construction. In 1924, a wooden belfry was added on the western side, while the women's chamber was enlarged. In 1940, a new bell tower was erected here - more than the old one. In 1955, the Church of St. Nicholas was seriously damaged during the fire, but it was repaired.
