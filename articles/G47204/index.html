From Belarus to Russia: Mogilev - Moscow

Belarus and Russia are not just adjacent to each other, but also communicate closely. Cultural, economic and spiritual ties between peoples have been preserved for many centuries. Residents of both countries often visit each other on business, visiting their relatives, for tourism or business purposes. Moscow and Mogilev, one of the largest Belarusian cities, are connected by a network of roads.





Many Muscovites are happy to go to Mogilev for a weekend to enjoy the beautiful old city.

Distance between Mogilev and Moscow

If you measure the distance Mogilev - Moscow directly on the map, you get about 500 km. But roads rarely pave the shortest path. Therefore, highways stretched for 580-620 km. The railway distance is 640 km.

Air traffic

Unfortunately, between Mogilev and Moscow for a long time there is no air service. Mogilev Airport works only with freight transportation and charters. To use the fastest vehicle, from Mogilev you need to get to Minsk or Vitebsk. Utair planes fly from Minsk to Moscow, and you can get to Minsk by bus, taxi or train. The minimum ticket price is 5 thousand rubles, depending on the season and the purchase date.

Moscow - Mogilev by car

Going to the capital of Russia from Mogilev by car, you can choose one of two routes:

  1. Distance - 580 km, travel time - almost 9 hours. After leaving Mogilev, you need to go along the international highway P-73 to the town of Rebyatki, where you need to turn onto the town of Krichev. From Krichev, take R-43 to the border. After the border in Russia, the highway is called A-130. At the intersection with the M-1 road, turn right; the entrance to Moscow is from Odintsovo. In this case, you can use the toll road M-1 to avoid traffic jams, they call in Moscow near the station. m. "Kuntsevo". Well, you can drive on the free M-1.
  2. The second route Mogilev - Moscow by car allows you to spend about 8 hours on the road, having traveled 620 km through Belarus, Smolensk and Kaluga regions. Having arrived from Mogilev by M-8 to the city of Orsha, the path continues along E-30 to the border. In Russia, the M-1 highway is called the Minsk highway and leads to the capital of Russia, bypassing Smolensk and Vyazma via Mozhaysk. They enter Moscow through Golevo.




Fuel consumption in both cases is approximately the same: when calculating 8 l / 100 km, you will spend about 60 liters of gasoline, that is, about 2 thousand rubles. If you use Internet services and pick up travel companions, travel expenses can be reduced.

Attractions along the way

Between Mogilev and Moscow there have long been stretched lands inhabited by Slavs, so it is not surprising that along the way you can admire a variety of attractions:

  • ecclesiastical: churches, churches, temples, cathedrals, monasteries of Bernardine and Basilian;
  • house-museums: Mironova, Polosukhina, Yanka Kupala, Glinka and others;
  • local history museums in many cities along the way;
  • nature reserves;
  • military museums: armored vehicles in Kubinka, Borodino field, military settlement Doronino;
  • unique objects: mills, water towers, Potemkin's palace, art objects.

Such a trip by car from Mogilev to Moscow will be unforgettable thanks to the impressions received.
