Sanatorium-resort base

Another famous S.P. Botkin talked about the healing effects on the human body of the special Crimean air. However, in tsarist times, recovery in Yalta was available only to nobles and wealthy bourgeois. After the revolution, all the chic palaces, villas and villas were nationalized. Already in 1921, thousands of workers took over Soviet Yalta. Sanatoriums and boarding houses, quickly converted from the estates of the Russian aristocracy, quickly redeveloped to treat a large number of vacationers.

From all over the Soviet Union, people with problems with the respiratory system and musculoskeletal system were drawn into the region. By the beginning of the Second World War, the boarding houses of Yalta, and there were 42 of them together with rest houses and sanatorium-type health resorts, could receive several thousand vacationers at the same time.
