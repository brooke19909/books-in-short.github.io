How much electricity does the multicooker consume: calculations and types of similar household appliances

There are more household appliances in our kitchens every year. There is no escape from this - everyone wants to add comfort, convenience to their life. One of the devices designed to do this is a multicooker. She frees up a host of time for the hostess, allowing her to do other things while preparing food. The problem is that this is an electric device with a heating element in its circuit, which means that utility bills should grow logically when using it. In today's article, we will consider how much electricity a multicooker consumes, and whether to purchase a similar household appliance.

The multicooker has too much consumption: myth or reality?

In order to answer this question, it is necessary to refer to the technical data of the household appliance. Power consumption will depend on the brand and model, however, for example, you can take not the lowest indicator - 1 kW. What is the difference between pressure cookers and multicookers

In principle, one does not interfere with the other. A slow cooker may also have a pressure cooker function. But the differences between them are significant. The main thing is the cooking time. In a pressure cooker, it is significantly reduced due to the fact that the whole process is carried out under pressure. That is why it is forbidden to open its lid during cooking.

Pressure cookers used to be quite common. The lids on them were bolted to the pan or clung to the edges, and the seal was made using the central screw. On top was a valve through which excess steam was vented. However, it often failed, clogged, as a result of which such pans exploded. Despite the fact that modern electric pressure cookers are devoid of such a flaw, many housewives still treat them with caution.
