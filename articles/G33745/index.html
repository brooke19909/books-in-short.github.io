What is the currency in the Czech Republic?

Few people know what the national currency is in the Czech Republic. Many are mistaken in thinking that this country has the euro, since it is part of the Eurozone. But the Republic, although it joined the European Union, did not refuse from the traditional state currency.

Short story

After the collapse of Czechoslovakia in 1993, the Czech krona began to be used in the country, and the Slovak one in Slovakia. At first, the old emblem was simply affixed with the emblem and other state attributes of the Czech Republic. Only after some time new money began to be printed.

In 2008, all coins of the Geller sample were withdrawn from circulation. Only coins worth more than one crown remained.

Description

The Czech currency is divided into 100 Hellers, which were previously issued in the form of coins, but were withdrawn from circulation. Now they are used only for cashless payments.

Today in circulation are coins in denominations of 1 to 50 crowns and banknotes in denominations of 100 to 5,000 crowns.

The currency of the Czech Republic is designated as CZK on the global financial market. The issue of banknotes is carried out by the National Bank of the Republic.

Czech currency exchange rate

CZK is not in great demand on the international finance market, and the country does not play a large role on the world stage. For these, and also for many other reasons, the national currency of the state has a low cost.

The price of the Czech currency to the Russian ruble is about 2.7 rubles to 1 kroon. If we consider the opposite situation, then for one Russian ruble they will give approximately 0.37 kroons.

The ratio of the U.S. dollar to CZK at the beginning of 2018 is approximately 1 USD = 21 CZK. With a return exchange of 1 to $ 0.048.

For one euro you can get almost 25 and a half Czech crowns. Thus, in one crown contains about 0.04 euros.

Exchange operations and cashless payments

The Czech Republic is a developed and modern country, and the financial structure here is at a high level. Bank transfer is accepted in almost all supermarkets, shops, hotels and even in public transport.

There are also many ATMs and branches of financial organizations, so there are no difficulties with cashing out funds. The exchange can be made at the airport, hotel or bank office.
