General Beloborodov Afanasy Pavantievich: biography, photo, family

General Beloborodov, whose biography is described in this article, is Russian by nationality. He was a famous Soviet military leader. Twice he was awarded the title Hero of the Soviet Union. Afanasy Pavlantievich commanded the forty-third army, which liberated Vitebsk from the Germans. Participated in the assault on Koenigsberg. He rose to the rank of general of the Soviet Army. He was a member of the Communist Party since 1926.

A family

On January 18, 1903, in the Irkutsk province, in the village of Akinino (now Baklashi), the future general was born - Beloborodov Afanasy Pavantievich. His family was simple, peasant. Father is baptized like Palladium. But the neighbors renamed it to the more familiar to them - Pavlantius.

Therefore, Athanasius was recorded according to documents under this patronymic. Mother - Lina Konstantinovna. Father - Pavanty Dmitrievich. Athanasius was brought up in a large family. He was the youngest child. Had brothers and a sister.

Childhood

At ten years old Athanasius independently plowed the land, mowed the grass and went to the harvest. Passing cattle, he knew how to recognize edible mushrooms and went fishing. He tidied around the house, helping his mother, and studied with the neighboring kids.



Youth

Youth for Athanasius began with a partisan detachment, to which he joined at the age of sixteen in 1919. Although he did not stay in the detachment for long, he firmly decided that he would become a military man. In 1920, a partisan detachment joined the Red Army and was sent to the 8th Irkutsk Rifle Regiment of the first Chita Division. But soon Afanasy Pavlantievich fell seriously ill and was released home. In April 1920, he returned to his native village.

Education

In 1923 he entered the infantry school in Irkutsk. A year later, it was abolished, and Afanasy Pavlantievich was in the eleventh of Nizhny Novgorod. He graduated at the twenty-sixth. Then enrolled in Leningrad for military courses to them. Engels. He graduated in 1929. Then he entered the Moscow Military Academy. Frunze. He graduated in 1936.

Studying has always been easy for Athanasius. Most of all he was attracted to topography and military tactics. But in mathematics he was weak. But Athanasius understood that he needed it for a military career. And he sat down tightly at the textbooks, excluding even days off for rest.

Return to military service

The future general Beloborodov returned to the Red Army only in 1923. From 1926 he was commander of a rifle platoon of the sixth Khabarovsk regiment in the Siberian Military District. After completing the courses, he was appointed political instructor of the rifle company of the thirty-sixth Transbaikal Division in the hundred and seventh regiment.



During the battle after the death of the company commander, the city of Zhalaynor took command. Under his successful leadership, the railway bridge was captured. Then Afanasy Pavlantievich received his first order of the Red Banner. He was left by the company commander.

Since 1936, the future General Beloborodov, whose photo is in this article, became an assistant to the chief, and then the direct head of the headquarters of the 66th division in the Far East. Since 1939, Mr .. appointed chief of staff of the thirty-first rifle corps. And since June of that year - the forty-third corps.

In 1940, he was in the position of corps commander. In 1941, he became the head of the combat training department on the Far Eastern Front. A few months later, in the same year, already in the rank of colonel, he commanded the 78th Infantry Division.

She was part of the sixteenth army, when the soldiers showed themselves heroically on the Western Front, in the Istrian direction. And the division for courage and excellent combat training was transformed into the Ninth Guards. This was a very rare case when in 3 weeks of hostilities the division so rose in its status.

The document on renaming it to the guards was personally signed by Stalin - People's Commissar of the Soviet Union. Joseph Vissarionovich noted courage, courage, heroism and perseverance of the whole composition. Separate special merits of Beloborodov were noted in the order. He received the rank of major general.

Then his division liberated Istra, and in January 1942 it was transferred to the Vyazemsky direction and became part of the forty-third army. Conducted military defensive operations on the South-Western Front, on the Seversky Donets.



Since October of the same year, General Afanasy Beloborodov took command of the 5th Guards Corps, which participated in battles on the Kalinin Front during the Velikoluks operation. And in 1943 he began to command the second corps, which participated in the battles in the Smolensk region and in the Nevel-City operation. And also in offensive battles in Belarus.

In 1944, Beloborodov, already in the post of lieutenant general, commanded the forty-third army. She broke through the enemy defenses during the Vitebsk-Orshan operation and forced the Northern Dvina. Together with the thirty-ninth army, the Vitebsk fascist group was defeated.

He participated in three operations: Konigsberg, Insterburg and Zemland. Since 1945, Athanasius led the first Red Banner Army. He participated in the first Far Eastern front in the defeat of the Japanese army.

Post-war years

After the war ended, General Beloborodov A.P. commanded the first Red Banner Army in the Far East. Since 1946, the Fifth Guards Division in the Central Group of Forces came under his command, and in 1947 Athanasius led the thirty-ninth army in China. In 1983, he became the head of the military training department of the ground forces for several months. Since 1953, Mr .. was the head of the higher Rifle tactical courses "Shot" them. Shaposhnikov modernization of the officers of the SA.

Since 1954, Athanasius was appointed Chief Military Advisor to the Ministry of National Defense of Czechoslovakia. In the fall of 1955, he commanded the troops of the Voronezh Military District, and in the spring of 1957, he headed the Main Directorate of Personnel of the USSR Ministry of Defense. In 1963 he was appointed to the post of commander of the troops of the Moscow Military District.

Beloborodov was a member of the Central Committee of the CPSU from 1966 to 1971. He was elected deputy of the Supreme Council of the Soviet Union of the third and seventh convocations.



Car crash

In the fall of 1966, Beloborodov (Army General) was returning from the motorized rifle Taman Division and had a car accident. His car "The Seagull" crashed into an asphalt roller. As a result, Afanasy Pavlantievich received numerous serious injuries. He had to lie in hospital for over a year. But then he returned to military service again. And since 1968 he became a military inspector and adviser to the Group of General Inspectors of the Ministry of Defense of the Soviet Union.

Personal life

General Beloborodov Afanasy Pavlantievich married Zinaida Fedorovna Lankina. She graduated from a pedagogical institute and worked as a teacher of chemistry. When, on duty, Afanasy Pavlantievich they moved to live in Port Arthur, got a job there as a director in a Russian school. Zinaida Fedorovna never complained about a nomadic life. Although it was often necessary to live even in dugouts.

In 1930, the son of Alyosha was born. Received higher education. He died in his seventieth year of life. The second son, Vladimir, became a candidate of economic sciences, executive secretary of the association of international organizations, enterprises and associations. He lives in Moscow. In 1941, another son, Alexander, was born to Athanasius and Zinaida Beloborodovyh. But the time was hard, cold and hungry, and he died in infancy. Athanasius never saw his youngest son. In 1966, Zinaida Fedorovna died.



General's death

Afanasy Pavantievich Beloborodov lived in Moscow. He died on the first of September of the ninetieth year. According to the will left, he was buried in the Istra district, at the Snegiri Memorial Cemetery. Nearby is the mass grave of the fallen fighters of his division, who gave their lives for the defense of Moscow.

Everlasting memory

In Irkutsk, opposite the Eternal Flame, a bust of General Beloborodov and a memorial plaque were installed . Streets in Vitebsk, Moscow, Kaliningrad, Irkutsk are named after him. An annotation board has been put in Belarus. In the Irkutsk region, with. Baklash, a memorial plaque is installed and there is a museum of General Beloborodov, and a school is named after him. General Beloborodov is an honorary citizen of Istra, Irkutsk, Krasnogorsk (Moscow Region) and Vitebsk.



Unfortunately, the home of Athanasius Pavlantievich in Baklashi has not survived to this day. But fellow countrymen still remember him. And not only as a general, but also as a patron. After the war, Afanasy Pavlantievich more than once came to his native village and made a lot of efforts to restore it.

For example, the local dilapidated wooden school, thanks to the efforts of Beloborodov, was rebuilt into a brick one. In 2003, a memorial plaque was mounted in its facade, and the educational institution itself has long been named after the general.
