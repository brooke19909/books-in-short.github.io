Renting an apartment - what is it?

Buying an apartment today is a very expensive pleasure, the most expensive apartments are in large cities, which is why many are on the way to renting a house.

Rental of housing is a contract in which the landlord agrees to provide the tenant with residential property for personal use, and the latter agrees to pay for it.

People who intend to rent an apartment should learn to understand the terminology of this market. The parties to the transaction are the lessee and the lessor. Tenant - it can be either a legal entity or an individual to whom the property is transferred for personal use for a certain period of time. Rent an apartment can both residents of the Russian Federation, and citizens or organizations of other states. Landlord - it can be both a legal entity and an individual who provides real estate for use for a specified period. The landlord must be the owner of the housing or real estate, or authorized persons vested with this right.

Each participant in the transaction has its advantages and disadvantages.

Tenant Benefits:

  • the ability to choose affordable housing;
  • capital works or domestic problems (plumbing, windows, electrics) are the responsibility of the landlord;
  • there is no need for additional costs for furniture and household appliances.

Disadvantages for the tenant:

  • psychological discomfort from someone else‚Äôs home (feeling ‚Äúas a guest‚Äù, there is no desire to invest money in arranging life);
  • additional costs associated with the costs of paying for the services of realtors.

The advantage for the landlord is additional income.

Disadvantages for the landlord:

  • problems with residents (small children, animals, unscrupulous payers);
  • care of the apartment (cleanliness, order, redecorating after the change of tenants);
  • possible problems with the apartment through no fault of the tenant (leaking pipes, problems with wiring, resolving conflicts with neighbors).

Rental housing through a real estate agency

The advantage of the agency is that the organization has a huge database for every taste and budget. The future tenant receives a wider selection of offers and can choose a convenient housing by location, apartment filling, price. The important point is that with the help of the agency there is less chance of getting into scammers or finding legal problems, because professionals know all the nuances when renting an apartment.

Real estate agency will take on part of the problems of rental housing, namely:

How to rent an apartment in order not to be deceived

An independent search for an apartment is possible, but it should be remembered that scammers are not asleep! What to look for when renting an apartment so as not to run into trouble? To do this, you need to know the fraudulent schemes and be prepared for them.

In the media, you can come across a lot of ads about renting an apartment. But not always an honest owner is hiding behind an ad. This can be an imaginary author, as well as an owner who can sublet the apartment (that is, the apartment is already rented out). How to check the apartment when renting and what to look for.

Deception schemes:

  1. Sublease. You can come across scammers who rent an apartment in which the owner has settled them. The change takes place without notice to the owner, often for a larger amount.
  2. Apartment for a day. Scammers rent a daily apartment, while placing an ad for rent for a long time. Paying a monthly rent to fraudsters, you may encounter the fact that in the morning the owner may come and you will have to vacate the apartment.
  3. Booking an apartment. Fraudsters advertise on apartments and leave a minimum of contact information. After receiving an advance payment for the reservation, the contacts become unavailable.
  4. Many comers. The scheme is that the apartment is simultaneously rented to several customers on a prepaid basis. Disappointment of residents awaits at the time of settlement.
  5. Imaginary host. From the name of this scheme, it becomes clear that the person who rents the apartment has neither the rights to it, nor the name that he introduced himself to. At the same time, they can show you all the documents for housing and personal data, but they will be fake.
  6. Change in value. The employer may face the problem of increasing rental costs. If an agreement has not been concluded, such a situation can happen to everyone, literally the next month after settling.
  7. Rent without agreement. If there are several owners of the apartment, there is always a chance to lose the deposit if one of them fails. It is possible to insure yourself by writing these items in the contract.
  8. Repair at the expense of rent. The scam is that the tenant is invited to make repairs in the apartment on account of the rent, often the repair costs exceed the monthly payment. Upon completion of the repair, the landlord finds reasons to ask tenants to move out.

Housing rental rules

How to check the apartment when renting so that your choice matches your wishes, you will learn by studying a number of basic rules that lawyers advise to adhere to.

Rule number 1. Use the services of professionals.

Various fraud schemes that may lie in wait for a future tenant were outlined above, so use the services of a real estate agency, where competent specialists will select the option you are interested in, check the legality of the transaction and legally accompany you in all the details. But it should be remembered that not only people can deceive, but also organizations.

Rule number 2. Verification of title documents.

What documents to check when renting an apartment? An important aspect here is the conclusion of a contract of employment, to which are attached copies of all documents of title to the apartment, as well as the passport data of the tenant. The title documents include - a certificate of state registration of the right to an apartment. Pay attention to how many owners per apartment, because there may be several owners. Other owners do not always agree with the rent of the apartment. Ask for a utility bill, which indicates whose name the invoices are in. Registration in the apartment is not proof of ownership - remember this.

Rule number 3. Acquaintance with neighbors.

This rule has several advantages, firstly, neighbors know whose apartment this is, and secondly, you can get a lot of necessary, additional information, for example, how often tenants change, who used to live in this apartment, and much more.

Rule number 4. Contractual relationship.

Be sure to draw up a contract of employment, this is what to look for when renting an apartment in the first place! In it, discuss the amount, property transferred, payment terms, and the term for concluding the contract. If there is no form at hand, you can write a contract by hand, but you need to certify the paper by two parties. The contract is concluded for a period of less than a year with the right to extend, otherwise, such a contract is subject to mandatory state registration.

Rule number 5. Inventory of property.

After the conclusion of the contract, it is necessary to draw up an inventory of the transferred property (or include it as an annex to the contract). An inventory of the property is needed so that there are no questions ‚Äúwhere did the TV from the kitchen go?‚Äù. All property leased is included in the inventory. You can also register the condition of the property.

Rule number 6. Interaction with the landlord.

Be sure to discuss how and when the tenant will check the apartment. The contract should indicate that overhaul and work after an accident (for example, pipe leakage) are the responsibility of the lessor. Otherwise, there may be two options: either you will wait a long time for the owner to correct the situation, or you will repair at your own expense.

Rule number 7. Early termination.

Be sure to specify in the contract the conditions for early termination of the contract from two parties. Usually this is to warn one side or another a certain time before departure.

Room rental in a communal apartment

Renting a room in a communal apartment is possible. Here we consider two options. The first - if the room is privatized, then the owner has the right to do anything with the room under the law. There are certain rules for renting a room in a communal apartment. The owner must obtain the consent of the other residents to rent the room, this procedure is not required, but recommended for a comfortable stay with neighbors. In addition, the rules for using common areas are discussed. For the peace of the neighbors, the conditions for visiting guests are discussed.

The peculiarity of renting an apartment in a communal apartment is its footage. The law establishes that 12 square meters are allocated per person, that is, when two people move into such a meter, the neighbors have the right to appeal to the court for infringement of the rights of other residents.

The second option, if the apartment is not privatized, then the procedure is complicated. In order to rent a room, you must obtain permission from the municipal authority, and it is also mandatory to obtain the consent of neighbors living in a communal apartment.

The contract for renting a room in a communal apartment has its own nuances, because there are third parties involved. The document must register all living conditions, rules of conduct (returning home, driving guests when noise is prohibited, etc.). The agreement also defines the rules for the use of common areas (toilet, bathroom, kitchen, corridor). The consent of neighbors in the form of signatures is desirable to be entered in the contract.

Renting an apartment for a long time

The rules for renting an apartment for a long period include a mandatory check of the apartment for suitability for living. Mandatory verification of window blocks, gas and electrical equipment, the condition of pipes and plumbing. Care must be taken of emergency telephone numbers.

What you need to check when renting an apartment, which is for rent for a long time? Typically, such a living space is rented with furniture and appliances, so an important point is to include all equipment, furniture and utensils in the inventory so that you will not be presented with a bill for a non-existent microwave upon departure.

Be sure to discuss when the tenant needs to provide access to the apartment. It will be unpleasant if in the absence of tenants the owner wants to check the order in the house.

The rules for renting an apartment for a long period dictate paying attention to the terms of payment of utility bills. If you need to pay for them in addition to renting, take an interest, and it is better to personally verify the monthly payments for the apartment. If 8 people are registered in the apartment and no meters are installed on the apartment, you can get unpleasant amounts on the receipt. Therefore, be sure to check all meter readings and record the data in the contract. You can also find out the possible arrears of utility bills, for this you need to contact the appropriate institutions that serve this housing complex.

At the end of the lease, the lessor will check the property transferred to the lessee according to the inventory. In case of damage, the tenant is obliged to compensate for the damage incurred either by compensation of monetary funds, or by independent troubleshooting.

Apartment rental agreement

The apartment rental agreement is the main document that will protect both parties from disputes. What documents you need to check when renting an apartment were written above, but let us dwell in more detail on what the landlord must provide:

  • Certificate of state registration of property.
  • Certificate of inherited property.
  • Gift agreement.
  • Contract of sale.

The documents verify the real estate object (its address), as well as the presence of owners. If there is only one owner, ask for an identity document (passport, driver's license). If there are several owners, then in addition to confirming the identity, it is necessary to obtain the consent of other owners for settlement.

  • The heading of the contract indicates the data of the lessee and the lessor: full name, passport data, indicating the registration. The place of conclusion of the contract and the date of conclusion shall be filled.
