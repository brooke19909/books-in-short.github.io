In the south of the Moscow region, about 80 km from the capital, on the banks of the Oka River (at the confluence of the Lyubozhikha River into it), one of the scientific centers of Russia, the Pushchino science city, is located. Its population is just over 20,000 people. It is also known as the Pushchino Biological Scientific Center of the Russian Federation.

Traditions of antiquity deep

The region in which the Pushchino science city (Moscow region) is located has a very long and rich history. Once, in ancient times, this part of the present suburbs was occupied by the Vyatichi (East Slavic tribal union). Traces of their stay were found on the territory of the city itself, and in a small distance from it there is a very old Teshilov fortress, which got its name from the nearby village of the same name. The first mention of this place is found in the annals of 1147. Manor nature

Later (in the late XVIII - early XX centuries), the owner of these lands, a certain major Artsybashev, built a two-story house. Skillfully using the unusual landscape, the architect was able to design a composition unreal in magnificence. All the beauty of the river and its distant panorama opens before your eyes. Subsequently, the estate changed many owners and underwent several restructures. Before the revolution, it was a whole complex, including a church, a large park, cascading ponds and outbuildings. Nowadays, only a house, a dilapidated horse yard and a glacier cellar are left of the beautiful estate. This site is recognized as a natural monument with springs, a waterfall and many streams.



Art is next to science

Creative people took a fancy to this calm, amazing in its natural beauty corner of the modern world. Here live literary figures, artists and masters of photography. All of them in their creations reflect and glorify this magnificent piece of Russian land. A few years ago, and more precisely in 2009, the Pushchino estate (Moscow region) turned 210 years old. For such a long time the estate has seen a large number of famous and famous people, figures of bygone days and our contemporaries. At different times, this paradise was visited by writer and publicist A.T. Bolotov, composer A.A. Alyabyev, large and outstanding figures of culture and art: I.M. Moskvin, S. Ya. Lemeshev and others. The films The Lame Barin and The Unfinished Piece for the Mechanical Piano were shot on the estate.
