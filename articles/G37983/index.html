Humboldt Wilhelm: biography and works

Wilhelm von Humboldt had a huge impact on the development of culture and literature. His works continue to influence scholars and thinkers of our time. Each educated person considers it his duty to carefully study the works that Humboldt Wilhelm wrote in his time. His thoughts and conclusions still remain relevant for contemporaries of the 20th and 21st centuries. To understand his ideas, you need to delve into his biography, find out in which city William Humboldt was born, where he worked, whose friendship had a special influence on him.





Origin

Wilhelm von Humboldt, as well as his equally gifted younger brother Alexander, came from a noble and wealthy family, which had significant opportunities and finances. They also owned the famous Tegel Castle in Berlin.

Humboldt Wilhelm was born on June 22, 1767 in the city of Potsdam. His father, Alexander Georg, came from a clan of the Prussian bourgeoisie. His grandfather became a noble thanks to his military merits. Mother, Baroness Elizabeth von Holwede has French roots. The oppression of the Huguenots in France forced her family to leave their native lands and move to Germany, to Berlin. When Alexander Georg arrived in Berlin after his retirement, he met his future wife. They had two sons - Alexander and Wilhelm.





Education

The Humboldt family did not spare funds for the education of their children. At the age of 20, Wilhelm Humboldt entered the University of Frankfurt an der Oder, and from 1788 he began to listen to lectures on philology and history at the University of Gottingen. From 27 to 30 he lived in Jena, where he made many acquaintances with famous philosophers and thinkers. Among them, the names of Schiller and Goethe are especially worth noting. Subsequently, he leaves for Paris to study the culture of France - after all, French blood also flows in it. However, he spent a lot of time traveling around Spain and the Basque region.

Professional activity

Humboldt Wilhelm became a significant figure in the political arena of Prussia. At various times from 1801 to 1819 he held important government posts, was plenipotentiary envoy to Vienna, the Vatican, Paris, and Prague. As Minister for Religious Affairs and Education, he managed to carry out a large-scale reform of secondary and higher education in Prussia. He represented the interests of the country in the international arena and achieved great success in this field.

The brilliant education and financial security of his family allowed Wilhelm to enter the circle of prominent scientists and philosophers of his time. This is not the only work in which William Humboldt set forth his ideas and thoughts. Linguistics received in his person one of the reformers and founders of modern concepts.

Due to his broad outlook and high education, William Humboldt was a member of all literary salons. He was often invited to listen to his opinion on a particular occasion, to learn reviews about read literary works.

After the wedding, the Humboldt House became a place of regular meetings of the best minds of all of Europe. Here one could meet writers, scientists, philosophers, and politicians.

Achievements

Wilhelm von Humboldt became not only a prominent political statesman, but also left a significant mark as an outstanding scientist. He defended the interests of his country during the territorial redistribution of Europe, the creation of a new world order. And he did it unconditionally successfully. His work was highly valued by the emperor. He was a skilled diplomat.

After the end of his professional activity and with the advent of free time, he took up the study of languages, their classification, and the identification of common features and differences. He outlined his ideas in his writings, which were published. The depth of research was so serious that its concept formed the basis of a new science - linguistics. Some of his ideas anticipated his time by a hundred years and were confirmed decades later. Based on his findings, a separate science of sounds in linguistics - phonology - has developed.

His education reform helped move the effort to eradicate illiteracy among the population. It was under him that the school began to acquire familiar features. Prior to this, no school system existed.





Cultural heritage

The works of Wilhelm von Humboldt laid the foundation for a new science - linguistics, linguistics. He argued the theses that gave food to the mind to many philosophers and scientists. Until now, linguists discuss and debate about many of his conclusions, agree with something, and argue about something. But one thing is certain - it is impossible to study this science and not know the name of William Humboldt.

In addition to the scientific works that Wilhelm von Humboldt left to his descendants about language, another significant testament was the university he founded, in which thousands of young and gifted people received higher education.

Wilhelm von Humboldt's concept was a revolution in linguistics. Yes, according to the majority of theorists, scientific thought has advanced, and some of the provisions and ideas of the founder of this science are already outdated and become irrelevant. Nevertheless, it will be very useful for every scientist to learn and understand the course of von Humboldt's logical reasoning in the process of creating his works.
