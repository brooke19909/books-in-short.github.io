Description of the Stavropol Territory

The center of the 126th region of Russia is Stavropol. It grew out of a fortress, which was built in 1777 for defense, but soon became one of the main economic centers of the North Caucasus. The population of Stavropol totals more than 429 thousand people. Translated from Greek, it sounds like a city of the cross. Until 1935, it was called the Stavropol-Caucasus, from 1935-1943 Voroshilovsk. Then the city was returned to its original name, but with a slight reduction.
