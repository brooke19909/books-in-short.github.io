Angel Tilda: pattern of a classic toy and its transformation

Dolls in the Tilda technique won the love of needlewomen around the world, because they are so tender, sophisticated, but at the same time funny. Tilda's angel dolls look especially cute . The pattern of such a toy can be simple enough so that even someone who is just beginning to comprehend the basics of sewing a textile toy can cope with it.

Doll Features

Dolls made using the Tilda technique came to us from Norway, they were invented by the craftswoman Tonya Finnanger. Today, these interior toys are one of the most popular in the world. They are bought and sewn on their own. They come in different sizes and depict people and animals, as well as various household items - from cookies to aircraft.

Such dolls are characterized by the absence of sharp bends, sharp corners, they have streamlined shapes, elongated in length. Tilda dolls have small bead eyes that are close to each other, rosy cheeks and a very full decor of clothes - lace, pockets, straps - all elements must be made as natural and accurate as possible.

If the doll depicts a person, then you can use one pattern for different models, only slightly transforming it or even being content with changing clothes and decor. For example, for the toy Angel Tilda, the pattern will be standard for such a product. Only the wings and decor of the appearance will allow to give a conceived personality.





Tilda's basic pattern

To sew a doll using the Tilda technique, you can use the following natural fabrics:

  • calico;
  • linen;
  • thin coat;
  • fleece;
  • cotton knitwear.

In order to sew an angel Tilda toy, you can use this pattern.





It is cut out of paper, all parts are cut into two parts with the addition of 3-5 mm along the contour to the seams. You can sew cut details even manually, even on a typewriter, and most importantly, all seams must be very neat. Before you turn out the finished parts of the doll, the seams must be cut with small scissors. Be sure to do this in those places where the sewing line has corners or bends. This is done so that the seam does not peel off in the finished product, and the doll looked very neat.
