Let's try to determine which mobile Internet is better

The number of mobile Internet users is growing steadily. And each new user thinks about which operator to connect to, whose USB modem to buy. Honestly, the issue is quite complicated, even in Moscow and the region. Although there are not many options, making a choice is difficult. Let's try to figure out: which mobile Internet is better? Firstly, it depends on who needs it. Secondly, from what is needed. And thirdly, from the price of the matter.



The main mobile Internet operators are: Megafon, Beeline and MTS. You also need to pay attention to Skylink and Yota. Its main consumers are those people who need to be constantly online. So, if you need to be able to check mail, read news or see the weather forecast, then any of the presented operators will more or less cope with the task.

Access to the network using modems and mobile phones still remains, to a greater extent, limited and rather slow, but it can cope with a minimum of work. And if you try to achieve something more, then here various problems will begin. We will drop the question of limit and talk about quality. If Megafon in the capital can handle the load, its 3g network has the best coverage here, then MTS and Beeline often do not live up to expectations. But in other cities, there may be a completely different ratio, and the answer to the question of which mobile Internet is better there may be completely different.



As everyone probably remembers, even before the Big Three USB modems, the Internet came from Skylink. At that time, it was relatively expensive and therefore not very common. Now the prices have roughly leveled off, and this operator is in great demand in the region, as it provides a stable result of 3.1 Mbps and provides a wide variety of tariffs. In Moscow, the results are worse.

When considering the mobile Internet (which is better), you must definitely take into account the growing company Yota, which Megafon recently bought. It provides the very first unlimited mobile Internet, while ensuring maximum speed. At the current time, Yota under certain technical conditions can provide a fairly high network bandwidth - up to 10 Mbit / s. Although she has a coverage area so far

uncompetitive, but now, after the takeover of Megafon, it will increase rapidly. In Moscow and the immediate vicinity, high-quality unlimited Internet from this operator costs 900 rubles per month and is a worthy alternative to wired.

When deciding for yourself which mobile Internet is better, it is advisable to read consumer reviews by region. A lot of people criticize Beeline and MTS, but in some places near Moscow, especially in summer cottages, no one but these operators even provides regular communication. And they are also good, at 1.5 Mbps, they give out the Internet, but without interruptions! So everything is relative.
