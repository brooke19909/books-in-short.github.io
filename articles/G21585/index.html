Dense crochet patterns: schemes, description and scope

The claim that crocheting is easier than knitting is highly controversial. However, there is no doubt that the hook opens up many more possibilities. For many knitters, owning crochet skills makes it much easier to do more scheduled work.

Dense patterns

Despite the fact that crocheting is primarily associated with airy openwork, there are situations when you can not do without a continuous fabric. In such cases, it turns out that finding dense crochet patterns with patterns is not so simple.

If you need to perform opaque knitted elements, you can use knitting needles, but this option is not always suitable. The canvas made with knitting needles is thinner and more elastic. In addition, tightly knit a thin thread with this tool is very inconvenient and long.

What are dense ornaments for?

Based on practice, you can specify the scope of the solid patterns designed for crocheting:

  1. Making warm garments. Winter hats, mittens, sweaters, dresses - all this should be connected without extra holes and lace.
  2. Scarves This clothing is made as a separate item, since scarves need double-sided tight crochet patterns (the schemes are proposed below).
  3. Interior items. Blankets, blankets, rugs, some models of pillows need a continuous canvas through which the lining is not visible.
  4. Swimwear and opaque elements of openwork dresses.
  5. In order to "dilute" the openwork pattern. Sometimes a combination of several rows of lace mixed with a dense pattern allows you to create a new unique pattern.

The specifics of choosing yarn for solid patterns

Most types of yarn are suitable for crocheting tight patterns. Patterns are often designed for yarn with a thickness of about 350-400 m / 100 grams. This should be taken into account if the thread chosen for knitting differs significantly in thickness from this figure.

Too thick yarn will cause the fabric to become coarse, overly dense and stiff. In addition, when knitting such products creates a large load on the fingers and they can hurt. To avoid unpleasant consequences and still use a thick thread, you can use a large hook (7 or more) and try to knit freely.

A thread with parameters more than 400 m / 100 grams is thin. For example, the thickness of mercerized cotton is 560 m / 100 grams. Knitting solid patterns with such a thread requires the use of a very thin hook (from 0.9 mm) and tight knitting. Otherwise, the connected fabric will turn out to be openwork and will not fulfill its function.

Crocheted zigzag dense patterns: description and diagram

Wavy patterns are very convenient for the manufacture of solid paintings. Similar ornaments are formed according to one principle: adding loops at the peak of the wave and reducing the same number of loops in the cavity. Zigzags have their own specifics and features:

  • Dense zigzag patterns are difficult to reduce, they create difficulties when knitting according to a pattern (sleeves, neck, relief for the waist). Waves are best suited for knitting even webs.
  • For the correct calculation of the loops, you need to knit a rather large pattern, since a fully wavy pattern is formed after knitting about 5 cm of the fabric.
  • It is necessary to strictly observe the number of added and reduced loops in each row. Ignoring such calculations leads to a gradual change in the proportions of the wave.

Dense openwork patterns

There are such patterns for crocheting, which can be called intermediate between openwork and solid.

These are quite common patterns of crochet patterns, the tight lace of which is formed due to a small number of air loops in the pattern.


This category should include most of the crochet patterns, which are considered solid. Unfortunately, for a swimsuit or skirt, they will not work, but for other products they will be indispensable.
