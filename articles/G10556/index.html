Waterpark "Serena" in Helsinki: description, entertainment, prices. Rating of water parks in Finland

If you are traveling to Finland, we recommend that you include the Serena water park in Helsinki in the route of your trip. His visit will certainly bring you a lot of joy and new experiences, it will remain in your memory for a long time. So, we offer today to get to know this water park more closely, and also to find out what other institutions of this kind exist in Finland.



What is the Serena water park in Finland?

First of all, it should be noted that this water park is the largest indoor tropical center of water attractions on the whole Scandinavian peninsula. Serena is located in a place called Espoo, which is a suburb of the capital of the country - Helsinki. Thus, you can get to it from the city center in just half an hour.

Serena Water Park in Helsinki amazes visitors at first sight. After all, it is located in a cave, surrounded by forest. The entrance to it, as well as restaurants and changing rooms are right inside the cliff. I must say that it looks very impressive.

As regards the price-quality ratio of the services provided, according to this indicator, the water park is one of the best not only in Finland, but also in neighboring countries.

Water Park Hours

Serena welcomes guests almost all year round. So, in the summer, the water park operates seven days a week. In September, it closes for prevention. During the off-season, Serena opens its doors only on weekends, however, during school holidays, the water park is open every day. If you plan to visit this water attraction center from autumn to spring, keep in mind that not all attractions will function, but only those that are not located outdoors. As for opening hours, the water park is open from 12 noon to 8 pm.

Waterpark "Serena": prices

This amusement park rates its services as follows:

  • a day ticket on the All inclusive system will cost you 25 euros;
  • for an evening ticket (from 16 to 20 hours) All inclusive will have to pay 20 euros;
  • a family ticket for two adults and two children costs 94 euros, and for two adults and three children - 117 euros.

However, all food and drinks, with the exception of beer and other alcohol, are free. Children under four years old do not take money for visiting the water park.

Where to stay?

This water park in Helsinki does not have its own hotel to accommodate visitors. Therefore, if you want to live closer to the amusement park, you can rent a cottage directly in Espoo or book a room in Korpilamp, located just half a kilometer from Serena.
