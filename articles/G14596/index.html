What is battery capacity?

The capacity (volume) of the battery, which is indicated among other technical characteristics, shows how long the battery can provide uninterrupted power. The capacity of powerful batteries is expressed in ampere-hours, and small - in milliampere-hours. This value is calculated by multiplying the discharge time by a constant current value.





In modern devices, manufacturers primarily install lithium-ion or lithium-polymer batteries. Battery operation is provided by various chemical processes. During operation, changes in the electrolytic composition, volume, state of the electrodes occur, which indicates the limited battery life.

So, how do you know the battery capacity to timely track the moment of a forced replacement? After all, no one wants the phone to run out of steam at the most inopportune moment.

How to check the battery mechanically?

Battery life is affected not only by the intensity of use. Factors that reduce battery life include sudden changes in temperature or long periods of inactivity. Excessive heat and too severe colds are perceived by the device as negatively as the fact that the battery has not been used for a long time.





Battery swelling is usually quite slow and not always immediately noticeable to the smartphone owner. To check if it is likely that the battery will fail soon, turn the battery on a flat surface. If there is a bend, then the rotation will occur easily.
