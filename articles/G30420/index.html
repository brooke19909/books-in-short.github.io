The Oka is a river that takes its source in the center of the Central Russian Upland, after the confluence of the Moscow River into it, it becomes extremely meandering and remains so until it flows into the Volga in Nizhny Novgorod, gradually expanding as it approaches it.

At the beginning of the last century, the Oka was a full-fledged navigable river, but now its flights are carried out mainly in the spring and at the beginning of summer, during the spill, in other seasons, not in all its sections, the depth is sufficient for large vessels to pass, therefore officially the river is navigable from Kaluga transit - from Kolomna, from the mouth of the Moscow River. In addition, it has many sites with rocky rifts and shallows, which greatly complicates navigation.

Vessels are mainly cruise ships, as the river flows in very picturesque places, attracting many tourists who want to see what it is - the main river of Ancient Russia, the Oka River. Photos of beautiful views along the coasts illustrate the impressions you get on cruises on it, so you should definitely take a camera with you to capture beautiful views.
