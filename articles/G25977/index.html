Where is Mumbai and its main attractions?

Mumbai (formerly Bombay) can rightly be called a city of contrasts. In addition to being one of the largest metropolitan areas in the world, it also breaks records in terms of both rich and poor residents. Where Mumbai is located, there are areas with the most expensive real estate in the world, adjacent to the slums. This city is the record holder for the production of films on the planet, but at the same time it is one of the leaders in crime rates.

Where is Mumbai located?





Mumbai is an Indian metropolis city, which is located on the shores of the Arabian Sea, at the mouth of the river. The capital of the state of Maharashtra. Initially, the metropolis was built on seven islands: Colaba, Mazagaon, Little Colaba, Mahim, Wadala, Parel and Matunga-Sion, which due to dense urbanization, over time they became part of the land. This happened in 1845 due to the reconstruction of the city, which began in 1817. Mumbai, considering all its satellites, has more than 21 million inhabitants, which puts it in fifth place on the list of the largest megacities of the planet. Today Mumbai consists of 7 administrative districts: South, South-Central, North-Central, Western outskirts, Central outskirts, Gulf outskirts, North-Western Mumbai. Mumbai for tourists

The city is visited annually by many tourists, including from Russia. Time in Mumbai differs from Moscow by 3 hours, so difficulties with changing the time zone and acclimatization are possible. It is also worth preparing for the dirt and poor hygiene of the locals. Where Mumbai is located, there is a danger of catching an exotic disease. Therefore, upon arrival, it is recommended to conduct a voluntary vaccination. In Mumbai (India) there are problems with drinking water, so it is worth drinking bottled water only.

The city boasts four beaches. However, their condition leaves much to be desired, and not every visitor will dare to soak up them. But this is a favorite vacation spot of local residents, where there is free entertainment.

Mumbai Attractions



The main visiting card of Bombay is the monumental arch of the Gateway to India. It is located on the outskirts of the Apollo Bunder pier, on the border between the Arabian Sea and the Colaba harbor. The arch is made of basalt and is a popular destination for tourists. The height of the monument is 26 meters. The arch was built in memory of the arrival in India in 1911 of King George V and Queen Mary. On the sides of the arch are two halls with a capacity of 600 people each. Local lovers often make appointments near the Gate.

Another colorful spot in Mumbai is Victoria Terminus Train Station. This is the largest railway junction in Asia, the main building of which is rather eccentric and is a symbiosis of Indian, Victorian and Islamic styles. A unique mixture of architectural solutions does not leave indifferent even the most experienced tourist.


Oddly enough, the Taj Mahal is a tourist attraction worth understanding to understand Mumbai (India). This is a five star hotel located very close to the Gateway of India. The building dates from 1903. Gustavo Eiffel even participated in the construction of the hotel. The seven-story building is made in European traditions, and the furniture and interior were brought from Europe at the beginning of the twentieth century. The hotel has the most memorable event for any Indian citizen. It was here that in 1947 the independence of India was proclaimed.

Today it is a fashionable hotel with the best restaurants in Mumbai, which is loved by both the local elite and world politicians and movie and show business stars. At one time, such famous personalities as John Lennon and Yoko Ono, Mick Jagger, Bernard Shaw and others rested here.

Mumbai on a world map



Mumbai took its place on both the political and cultural maps of the world. This city is an example of how to combine ancient traditions with modern ones. Where Mumbai is, life boils 24 hours a day. The Indian capital rightfully owns part of the world's cultural wealth, both material and in the form of preserved traditions.
