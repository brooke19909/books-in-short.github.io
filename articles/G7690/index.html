Leningrad beauty

The Moika River is one of the most popular reservoirs in St. Petersburg. Its length is about 5 kilometers. In some places, the width of the stream reaches 40 meters. At the same time, the depth of the reservoir is not very large. Its maximum size does not exceed 4 meters.

The embankment of the Moika River for some residents was called the "Russian Venice". This fact is explained by the fact that the width of the reservoir allows you to swim in boats with ease. On both sides, the river is surrounded by old houses with rich architecture, which makes the walk more colorful.

The Moika River, although it is not the only body of water in the city, is of particular popularity. Both tourists and locals come here. This is due to the fact that it flows in the central part of the city, which is always full of people.

Historical reference

The emergence of this reservoir is associated with the XVII century. Then the river originated from the marshland. At the same time, the modern name did not exist, and the locals called the water stream Muay. Translated, this word means "dirty." The choice of such a name is easily explained by the marshy environment. The current - Moika - was invented in 1726, as for most citizens the previous name was too difficult to pronounce.



Later it was decided to connect the channel with the river to another body of water - Fontanka. Thus, an artificial island was created, which is washed on all sides by water. They decided to place a garden on it.

Closer to the middle of the XVII century, a wooden embankment was created, which was located directly on the banks of the Moika, and by the end of the century it was replaced with granite slabs.

In addition, one of the main advantages are bridges thrown across the river. Today there are 15. Therewith, each is distinguished by its design and exterior. All of them look very aesthetically pleasing to the eyes of every passerby.
