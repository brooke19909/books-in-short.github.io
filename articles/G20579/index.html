Quantum physics and its relationship with the reality of the universe

Many are interested in quantum physics and reality, these are such exciting minds of the field of science, where many try to find answers to questions about the origin of the universe itself. Each modern cosmological theory also relies on quantum mechanics, which describes the behavior of an atomic and subatomic particle. Quantum physics has a fundamental difference from ordinary physics.

Using classical physics, the behavior of a material object is described, while quantum physics and reality focus only on mathematical descriptions of observations and measurements. In quantum mechanics, along with the objects of research and the research tool, the element of the analyzed patterns is observers. But since quantum mechanics are used to describe the universe, this is fraught with serious difficulty. Given the definition, each observer is part of the universe. In general, we do not have the ability to imagine outsiders. To formulate a version of quantum mechanics that does not need an outside observer, one of the famous physicists J. Wheeler decided to propose a model, given which, the Universe is capable of splitting into various copies all the time. Each parallel universe has its own observers who can see these specific sets of quantum alternatives, and each of these universes is real.

The basic elements of quantum mechanics are a multitude of integrated circuits (active and passive components electrically interconnected) that perform a certain function, but the trouble of the scientist who used the method of materialistic reductionism does not end there. During this, the theory of relativity is used to describe the general structure of place-time, and quantum physics is used to explain the behavior of a subatomic particle.
