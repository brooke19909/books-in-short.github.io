What to do in Yaroslavl

Even from the school curriculum, everyone knows that the city of Yaroslavl is an honorary member of the UNESCO list, which has preserved more than 800 architectural monuments. Today, the city with a thousand-year history is the center of the Volga tourism.

Here you will see magnificent creations of masters of the last five hundred years: mansions of merchants, shopping arcades, numerous churches with openwork crosses and golden domes. It is in this city that the buildings of almost all styles of Russian architecture have been preserved.

The historical center, where the Parus Hotel (Yaroslavl) is located, is fully included in the UNESCO list. Its main attractions are the Epiphany and Elijah the Prophet churches, Red Square, Assumption Cathedral, Volkov Theater.

In the Krasnoperekopsky district, the industrial center of the city, there is the Peter and Paul Park, the Fedorov Church, the Church of St. John the Baptist, the Church of Peter and Paul.

The Leninsky district is famous for the museum of military glory, and the Frunze district is famous for the temple ensemble in Korovniki.

A remarkable feature of the city is more than three dozen ancient temples and churches, most of which were built in the second half of the 17th century. The richest residents of Yaroslavl did not spare gold on a charitable deed and invited the best icon painters and architects to work.

Where to go with children

For parents who came to Yaroslavl with children, acquaintance with the city does not boil down to visiting churches and churches alone. The parents ‚Äôarsenal includes the Aleshino Compound Museum, Einstein Museum of Sciences, Puppet Theater, Zoo, Dolphinarium, and Circus.

  1. In the "Alesha Compound" the little traveler will be greeted by the heroes of Russian fairy tales - the hero Alesha Popovich with the beautiful Lyubava and the cheerful brownie Shabarsha. Here the child will be carried away by fairy tales, presented with gifts, treated with Russian dishes, introduced to the bird, the Phoenix and the horse Julius, and will even be accepted as heroes.
  2. The Museum of Entertaining Sciences will also be interesting to children of all ages. Here they will be able to lift a pond weight with one finger, boil cold water, build a bridge without nails, curl hair with a ball of lightning and much more.
  3. The Yaroslavl Puppet Theater is considered one of the oldest theaters for children in Russia. Here they stage plays of completely different genres and forms. In his poster - more than two dozen performances for children of different ages.
  4. In the contact zoo, young travelers will be able to feed and stroke rabbits, pigs, lambs, ride a pony.
  5. In the dolphinarium, children will see different tricks performed by dolphins and fur seals, learn about the places where they live, and about the main features of these sea animals.

Walking with children in the fresh air is best on the promenade, where the Parus Hotel (Yaroslavl) is located. It is always fun and crowded here, and the residents themselves recognize this place as one of the best for walking with children.

The Volga embankment of Yaroslavl is considered one of the most beautiful in the country: with magnificent views of the Assumption Cathedral and Mother Volga, musical fountains and lush flower beds.
