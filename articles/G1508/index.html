Church of the Virgin Mary

Vinnitsa (Ukraine), the sights of which surprisingly harmoniously combine unique natural monuments with man-made samples, invariably attracts tourists.



The Church of the Virgin Mary was built in 1745 by the Vinnytsia headman Louis Kalinovsky. It was erected for the order of the Capuchins. The fate of the church is characteristic of all the churches of the former USSR. During its existence, several owners changed, and in 1932 its activities were ceased by the Bolsheviks. During the Patriotic War, when Vinnitsa was occupied by the Germans, the church was repaired, and divine services continued. After the war, the Soviet authorities closed it again and in 1961 they gave the building to a lecture hall, where atheistic lectures were regularly given.

Only after the collapse of the USSR, in 1991, the church was returned to the Catholic Church. It is curious that the Orthodox priests provided substantial assistance in this. Since 1999, magnificent organ concerts have been regularly held here, in which organists from around the world take part.

Church of St. Nicholas

This building is located in the most beautiful place of the Old Town, on the left bank of the Bug River. In the middle of the 13th century a small chapel appeared on this place. It was built by the inhabitants of the Kiev-Pechora Lavra, monk Vladimir and hieromonk Nikolai. They fled from Kiev, captured by the Tatar-Mongols. At night Saint Nicholas appeared to them, indicating the place where the Poklonniy Cross and a small church should have been installed.

At the end of the 18th century, the Uniates captured the temple, but in 1772 it was liberated. Today the temple is under the control of Archimandrite Alexy.



Roshen Fountain

Thousands of tourists annually come to Ukraine to see the sights of Vinnitsa. The Roshen Fountain is the largest light and musical floating sample in Europe. This is a unique hydraulic structure. Its central stream reaches a height of 70 meters, and the expansion in width is 140 meters. The projection screen, which is formed by splashes and water dust, is approximately 16 x 45 meters. The fountain is installed on a floating special platform ninety-five meters long. Water jets beat out of 277 nozzles, 67 pumps pumping water from the Southern Bug provide operation of the structure. Amazing light paintings create 560 powerful lights under water and laser beams. Many visitors to the city who come to see the sights of Vinnitsa, reviews about this miracle on the water, leave enthusiastic. And those who have already heard about the fountain, seek to spend their first evening in the city near it. Its opening took place in 2011. It works daily in the warm season.

Countess Shcherbatova's Palace

We continue to explore the sights of Vinnitsa and its environs. Our next stop is in the town of Nemirov, at the magnificent Shcherbatova Palace. The construction of this structure was somewhat delayed (1894 - 1917). It is still not clear who is the author of the project. The building is made in the neoclassical style: bay windows, colonnades - all this pleases the eyes of visitors. It should be noted the magnificent garden surrounding the palace. Its area is 85 hectares. Rather, it is even a botanical garden, since trees and shrubs were brought here from all over Russia.

Today, the Vanguard sanatorium is located in this palace.



Tulchin City - Potocki Palace

The city was first mentioned in 1607 as the Nestervar fortress of the Bratslav Voivodeship (Poland). In the second half of the 18th century, the city belonged to the Pototsky magnates. The palace of Stanislav Felix Pototsky was preserved in its original form. It was built according to the project of the Frenchman Lacroix. Experts consider him a model of classicism in Ukraine.

During the life of the owners, the palace shone with splendor and luxury: refined stucco molding, gilding, chic furniture. The walls of the palace were decorated with great canvases of famous masters: Rembrandt, Raphael. Three paintings from this palace are kept in the Louvre.

The design was done by the Dutchman Merckx. He carefully thought out the design of each room. The palace was often visited by famous people of that time, luxurious balls were held.
