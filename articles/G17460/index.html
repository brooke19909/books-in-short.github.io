Discovery of Henry Coand

In 1932, Henry (Henri) Coanda conducted an interesting experiment in Bucharest, witnessed by Radu Manicatida. He recalls how his famous teacher and inventor of the world's first jet-powered aircraft project, Henry Coande, demonstrated the experience of a disc that rose up and, upon reaching the ceiling, hovered. At this momentous demonstration, unconventional flight methods were used.

If we talk about these principles in a simplified manner, their essence boils down to the following: if you draw air down and along the sloping surface of a plate (disk), then it moves along the object in question. By drawing in air above the plate, allowing it to flow around and below, the experimenter was able to simultaneously lower the air pressure above the plate and increase this pressure from below, which, in turn, led to the evaporation of the apparatus. This phenomenon is called the ‚ÄúCoande effect‚Äù. According to some researchers, the effect formed the basis of the idea of ‚Äã‚ÄãGerman flying saucers of World War II.

Victor Schauberg

The name of this Austrian nugget from the people associated German flying saucers of World War II. While in a concentration camp, he was forced to take part in the development of a secret "weapon of retaliation." His main merit is the study of the use of water power. The introduction of its development would allow mankind to escape from the predatory plunder of the bowels of the earth with the subsequent destruction of the planet. Throughout his life, the scientist has been the most ardent supporter of the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãharmony between man and nature. He was especially fascinated by the actions of trout, which is able to freeze in the rapid stream of the stream or, if necessary, to retreat against the stream, although according to the logic of things it should be carried away by the strength of the stream. Viktor Schauberg associated this ability of fish with temperature in a stream. He soon conducted an experiment. Heated about a hundred liters of water, pouring them upstream. This and other interesting experiments allowed us to approach the discovery of self-sustaining dynamic flows. According to some researchers, this discovery allowed the creation of flying saucers.

Shriver-Habermol disks - the first devices with vertical take-off

Since 1937, several classified design teams have been formed in Germany. Their goal is to create flying discs with vertical take-off. This was one of the main conditions for creating a combat vehicle that did not need to take off at an airfield. The project was led by Captain Rudolf Schriever. Also involved were Andreas Epp, Otto Habermol, Walter Mitte.

Their office was located in Prague. In terms of secrecy, it could argue with the Nazi missile center in Peenemuende. It was here that the main work was carried out to develop German flying saucers. The original prototype was a "wheel with a wing." Had piston and liquid rocket engines. It looked like a bicycle wheel. This similarity was given to him by adjustable blades located around the cockpit, which serve to select vertical or horizontal flight.

The main defect of this product was the strong vibration caused by the imbalance of the rotor. They tried to fix this problem by making the outer rim heavier, but failed. In the end, the creators concentrated all their efforts on a ‚Äúvertical plane‚Äù, as they themselves christened this German flying saucer V 7. It was developed as a high-tech weapon in a war where Germany obviously could not win: the forces were too unequal. Therefore, the main stake was placed on weapons, which, in terms of their characteristics and operating principles, reached a qualitatively different level.

Description of Reich Flying Saucers

To stabilize the control, a steering mechanism was used. It was similar to the one existing in those days (vertical plumage). The first test model was 21 meters in diameter. Its launch was made in the vicinity of Prague in late spring 1944. She had a horizontal flight speed of about two hundred kilometers per hour.

The next version of the flying saucer, assembled at the Ceska Morava plant, had a diameter of 42 meters. Nozzles placed at the ends of the blades set the rotor in motion. As in previous models, the engine was the Walter rocket launcher. The fuel used was the decomposition of hydrogen peroxide. The cockpit was dome-shaped, around it a wide flat ring rotated under the influence of controlled nozzles.

This car in February 1945 was able to rise to a height of over 12,000 meters and develop a horizontal speed of 200 km / h.
