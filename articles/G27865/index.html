Zhukov Klim, historian: biography and photos

Zhukov Klim is a relatively young historian, but nevertheless quite famous in scientific circles, and not only. He managed to gain fame on the literary field in the field of fiction, as well as historical reconstruction. Some of the historical concepts presented by him are of rather great interest. So who is Klim Zhukov, a historian? The biography of this person is detailed below.





Birth and childhood

Klim Alexandrovich Zhukov is a historian in the future, was born on March 29, 1977 in the city of Leningrad (present-day St. Petersburg). The ancestors of the male line of his father, Alexander Zhukov , came from the Orenburg Cossacks - the second oldest Cossack army in Russia. But the ancestors of the grandmother on the paternal side from time immemorial lived in St. Petersburg, that is, they were local.

Klim studied quite successfully at his hometown school. The turning points associated with the collapse of the USSR occurred just when he was a teenager, which to a large extent affected the worldview of the future historian.

Study

Upon graduation, Klim Aleksandrovich in 1994 entered the Faculty of History of one of the most prestigious and sought-after universities in the Russian Federation - St. Petersburg State University. SPbSU is the oldest institution of higher education in Russia. It was opened in 1724 at the direction of Peter I, and was named the Academy of Sciences. In 1819, the university was granted university status. Such outstanding Russians as Mikhail Lomonosov, Pyotr Semyonov Tien-Shansky, Kliment Timiryazev, Vasily Dokuchaev and others studied and taught in it. The university acquired its real name in 1991.





Naturally, an institution with such a glorious history and reputation made rather high demands on students, and released real high-class specialists from its walls.

Five years after admission, Zhukov graduates from this educational institution with a degree in Medieval Studies (history of the Middle Ages). The graduate thesis is dedicated to a two-handed sword in Italy and Germany of the XV ‚Äî XVII centuries. From then on, we can say that Zhukov Klim is a historian.

In the same year, he enters the graduate school of the Institute for the History of Material Cultures in a department that specializes in Slavic-Finnish mythology. This time, the topic of the dissertation was devoted to Russian armor of the 13th-15th centuries, and the scientific adviser was the doctor of historical sciences, and Honored Scientist of the Russian Federation Anatoly Nikolaevich Kirpichnikov. It was Kirpichnikov who at one time directed the excavations carried out by a group of archaeologists in Staraya Ladoga.

Work in the Hermitage

At the same time he entered graduate school, Zhukov got a job in the numismatics department of the Hermitage. This is one of the largest cultural and historical museums of the Russian Federation. It is located in St. Petersburg, and was founded back in 1852, as the Imperial Hermitage. Here are collected the most valuable exhibits in the field of culture and history.

In 2004, Zhukov transferred to the Arsenal weapons history department. In 2005, he became an employee of the Museum-on-Line holding, which operates under the Hermitage. At the same time, he teaches at the Russian Christian Institute in St. Petersburg, and lectures at the Student Society of the Hermitage.

In 2008, Klim Zhukov left the Hermitage in order to devote himself completely to literature and cinema.

Participation in historical reconstruction projects

Zhukov Klim, a historian, gained wide public fame thanks to his participation in various popular science and reconstruction projects.

He is the head of the Swordsman Reconstruction Club, and the Livonian Order interclub association. He is one of the main organizers of the inter-club association of historical reconstruction "Grand Company", and the Association of medieval BI.





In addition, Klim Zhukov is an official expert of the Guildhall School of Music and Theater, located in the British capital, London.

Scientific works

Quite a lot of works were published by the historian Klim Zhukov. The bibliography of this scientist will be discussed below. It is thanks to these works that he claims to be a man who contributes to modern Russian academic historical science.

In 2005 - 2008, Zhukov published three books on uniforms and military armor of various times in Europe. One of these books is about the Middle Ages, the other is about the Renaissance, and the attention of the third work is devoted to European cavalry. He wrote the first two books in collaboration with experts such as D. S. Korovkin, A. M. Butyagin and D. P. Aleksinsky.

These books were published in Russia in 2008 and 2010, respectively.

The historian Klim Zhukov about science, including history, speaks of an important tool of cognition that is passed on to new generations from past generations.
