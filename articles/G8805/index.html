Panasonic RX D55: specifications and reviews

In the age of digital media, all kinds of CDs, the Internet and all kinds of gadgets, analog media has faded into the background. But finally they did not die. There are also those who appreciate the warm, tube-like, analog sound. A vivid proof of this is the return to fashion of vinyl players, which now cost a lot of money. But what about cassettes? Of course, the sound on these media is not as high quality as on vinyl, but they also have their fans. Along with the era of cassettes, multifunctional devices for their reproduction (tape recorders and decks) have sunk into oblivion. However, there are some signs that cassettes will return soon. Proof of this is the release of the Panasonic RX-D55 radio. And this is in 2014! Consider this universal device in more detail.

A little bit about the company

This company was founded back in 1919. True, then it was called differently. But this does not detract from her venerable age. Over its history, the manufacturer has been engaged in the production of specialized production equipment, all kinds of electronics (including household), the production of mobile phones, cameras, players and the production of many other goods. Cassette recorders made in Japan were especially valued. Then the label of this company was considered synonymous with the highest quality products. And now nothing has changed. Proof of this is the Panasonuc RX-D55 magnetotole. But more on that later.





Around the eighties of the last century, the company mastered the production of multi-functional sound "harvesters", which included a CD player, radio and cassette deck. Such devices have become very popular among potential buyers. Therefore, the company decided to expand its product line. But many years have passed since then. What made Panasonic release a cassette recorder these days? Nobody knows that. But the Panasonic RX-D55 EE K was a real hit. Now consider it in more detail.

Contents of delivery

So, the Panasonic RX-D55 comes in a color cardboard box. Inside - all the necessary pieces of paper, the radio itself, a remote control (by the way, rather big), a wire for connecting to the electric network and batteries in order for the clock to work. You should not expect more from such a product. This is still not a high-tech gadget, but a kind of "greetings from the nineties." Therefore, you will not find anything supernatural in the package. But this is for the better. Brevity is the soul of wit.





Nevertheless, the presence of batteries and a remote control is incredibly pleasing. There is one more feature in the Panasonic RX-D55 delivery set - the instruction manual is completely in Russian. Although in the case of TM "Panasonic" there is nothing surprising in this. In addition to the instructions, there is also a warranty card. The product warranty is one year. This is quite enough for such a device.

Appearance and design

The Panasonic RX-D55 looks like a regular tape recorder from the nineties. The similarity is exacerbated by the presence of a cassette deck on the top panel. There are several color options: black and silver. Which one is better and more practical is for the buyer to choose. But both options are made of durable high-quality plastic. On the front panel there is an LCD display, a CD tray, control buttons and all the necessary ports. This arrangement of the CD player is very convenient, as it eliminates the possibility of damage to the disk.





On the rear panel of the Panasonic RX-D55 is a battery compartment, a headphone jack and all kinds of ventilation grilles. The power cord is also plugged in at the back. The advantage of this radio is also its weight. This immediately suggests that we have a high-quality and solid product. However, the design is a bit archaic. In any case, in 2017, he looks like a blatant anachronism. But the main thing is not the appearance. It is much more important that the radio sound good . Let's move on to its functions.

Radio functions

So, what features can we please the Panasonic RX-D55? We won‚Äôt touch the specifications yet. The device includes a cassette player with the functions of auto-stop, fast-forward and search for the desired composition. It provides playback of any magnetic cassette (even the most jammed one). Also, the radio has a radio with memory for 16 stations, operating in the FM-AM bands. Everything would be very nice if there was still support for RDS - text information sent by radio stations. The radio also has an alarm function that can be set using the remote control.





Below the LCD is the CD player. It is capable of reproducing MP3, WAV, WMA, CD-AUDIO formats. Pretty good set for the radio, made in the style of the nineties. But that is not all. Panasonic RX-D55 is equipped with a USB port, which makes it possible to play music from a regular USB flash drive. Song tags are also supported. And the radio can also be used as an amplifier with speakers for a laptop, PC or mobile phone. To do this, there is a special output on the front panel called Music Port.
