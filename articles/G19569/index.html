The world of crafts and surprises: how to make shurikens from paper

Shurikens are small metal objects, most often in the form of stars or four-, six- and eight-pointed, which were used by Japanese reconnaissance soldiers to inflict hidden attacks on the enemy. There are several options for making shurikens from paper.

  1. First, the simplest: take a piece of cardboard, quite large. Make a stencil of one of the rays of the "stars", circle. In form, of course, they should more resemble a rake or helicopter blades. Their number depends on the type of your shuriken. Cut the rays. Lay them on top of each other and use an awl to drill a hole. Pass a thin wire through it and fasten. Spread the rays "in a single file," one by one. Here's how to make shurikens out of paper in literally 10-15 minutes. Useful advice: paint each blade with some contrasting color. In flight, such a shuriken toy will look very impressive.
  2. The second option is to make an origami craft. Such a Japanese asterisk can be played by throwing it over short distances. Having made a few pieces, arrange competitions for the whole family - it will be fun and exciting.

And now - how to make shuriken stars from paper: a detailed instruction





  • Prepare a few sheets of copybook or other, but rather thick paper. Cut out the squares from them. Then fold each sheet in half and cut - you will get 2 rectangles.
  • Now fold each rectangle again - along, but don't cut it anymore. But bend the corners - inward, point to each other.
  • The next step is how to make shurikens out of paper: fold the blanks one more time, focusing on ready-made lines.
  • Details of the sprocket should be in relation to each other in a mirror image. Therefore, turn one of the blanks from left to right and connect both parts. This is done in this way: the left triangle and the right of the workpiece, which is located below, are inserted under the central edge of each of the main triangles in the upper module.
