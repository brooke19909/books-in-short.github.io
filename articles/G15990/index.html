Essen sights: location, interesting places, city history, photos and reviews of tourists

Essen is one of the beautiful and ancient cities of Germany. It is rightfully considered one of the cultural centers of Europe. There are many beautiful castles, each of which hides a secret. Also in the city there are unique museums, which tourists from all over the world specially go to see. But most of all, this small city is famous for its coal mines. More information about the sights of Essen and the environs of Germany will be described in this article.

Interesting Facts

In the very center of the Ruhr region (37 km from Dortmund and 70 from Cologne) in the north-western part of Germany (the land of North Westphalia) is the small town of Essen. Despite the fact that it is inferior in size to such cities as Munich and Cologne, it is very popular among tourists who come here with pleasure. There are so many interesting things. Starting from the history of the city and ending with its sights. Among the unusual facts about Essen are the following:

  • It has a wonderful climate, stunning landscapes and clean air. The second name of the city is "the green capital of Europe." It is the warmest city in Germany with comfortable winters and moderately hot summers. If you are going to Essen, then the best time for this is from April to November.
  • Initially, there were several blacksmith villages on the site of the city, in the mid-800s. e. St. Mary's Church was opened here. It is from its foundation that the development and formation of Essen begins. The main occupation of the city was coal mining. Since the 19th century, it has become the largest coal center in Germany. The large-scale construction of the railway begins.
  • The architecture of the city was hit hard after World War II. Unfortunately, far from all the sights were restored. Recently, from the industrial city of Essen has turned into a large exhibition and business center with many high-rise buildings.

For those who for the first time decided to see the sights of Essen (Germany), we suggest not postponing the matter, but boldly buy a ticket and go on a trip. So that you do not have problems in an unfamiliar city, pay attention to several recommendations:

  1. Essen is the largest railway station. It is connected with almost all cities in Germany. So if you fly by plane to Dusseldorf, you can catch an electric train and soon find yourself at your destination.
  2. Prices for accommodation in local hotels are quite affordable. In the center you can rent a good room for a small fee. Just remember to book a place first.
  3. There are many different nationalities living in the city, this affected the culture, customs and, of course, gastronomic delights. Zolferain

At the beginning of the 19th century, a mine was built in the city, which for many years became the main coal enterprise of Essen. The first owner was the wealthy industrialist Johann Ganil. It received its name "Zolferain" in honor of the German Customs Union that arose simultaneously with it. Every detail was thought out to the smallest detail. Two architects, Fritz Schup and Martin Kremer, worked on the mine project plan. They wanted to make her unforgettable. When the mine was built, it was equipped with the best equipment of the 20th century, and the coal mining process began. In 1986, she ceased operations. Soon, the German government assigned Zolferain the status of a significant cultural monument.

Today, the mine is a large museum complex, which everyone can visit as part of an excursion group. Here you can see the Ruhr Museum with the remains of animals found during coal mining. But the most interesting is "The Way of Coal." At this attraction you can see all the details of coal production. You can also see the museum of design and ceramics, there are a lot of unique exhibits. In addition, there are various thematic exhibitions. And if you are tired and decide to have a bite, then for your convenience there is a cafe and a restaurant. The Zolferain mine operates every day from 11-00 to 17-00 (break from 13-00 to 14-00). Ticket price: 11 euros.

Villa Hugel

The famous mansion in the Bredenay district of Essen, located on a mountain towering above the Ruhr river and Lake Baldeneysee. He belonged to the dynasty of industrialists Krupp. The construction of the villa lasted about 4 years. The building plan was personally designed by Alfred Krupp. They say that he was very afraid of his ill-wishers, and therefore he tried to make his mansion as close as possible to a fortress. The famous cathedral church is one of the main attractions of Essen. It is located in the very center of the city (in the Stadtkern district, on the Burgplatz square). This church is a wonderful monument of Gothic architecture. It was built in 870 and for many years became the only convent in this county in Germany. Huguenpot Castle

A complex of several structures surrounded by water. It is located in the Ketving district of Essen. It is believed that the castle appeared at the end of 778 and belonged first to Charles the Great, who then transferred it to the ownership of Verdun Abbey. In 1314, for good service, possession was presented to the German knight Flecke von Hugenpot. Now the castle is one of the attractions of Essen, there is a cozy hotel and restaurant. Tourists have a unique opportunity to live in the house of the famous ancient dynasty. All interiors have remained virtually unchanged.
