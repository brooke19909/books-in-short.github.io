The most famous sights of Antalya

Antalya - one of the oldest cities in Turkey, a storehouse of historical monuments. Of course, today it is already a modern city, but many attractions of Antalya have survived to this day.





Hadrian's Gate

One of the oldest monuments is the Hadrian's Gate, erected in honor of the Roman emperor Hadrian, who visited Turkey in 130 AD. e. The majestic and graceful columns of the gates were crowned with Roman capitals. Today, travelers have a unique opportunity to see the preserved sculptural decoration of the cornice and the coat of arms of the famous emperor.

Khidirlik Kulesi Tower

Many sights of Antalya remind of its military past. Among the military fortifications that surrounded Antalya in antiquity, the most famous is the Hydyrlyk Kulesi tower . This building, built in the II century, served as a beacon for sailors. Inside, scientists discovered a huge block, this suggests that the tower previously also performed the function of a tomb.





Termessos

And a few kilometers from Antalya is the ancient city of Termessos, the only one in the region that did not submit to Alexander the Great. According to historians, in the 5th century AD e. the inhabitants left him for unknown reasons. Today, tourists can stroll along the preserved stone roads, see the ancient theater, the temple, as well as other evidence of the life of the ancient city, for example, oil storage tanks hollowed directly into the rock.

Yivli Minaret

Other attractions of Antalya reflect the specificity of the religion of the ancient city. For example, the Yevli Minaret, the symbol of Antalya, erected in the 13th century, is a 38-meter tower, supported by eight columns, at the base of which is the beautiful Byzantine mosque of Eski Jami.





Archaeological Museum

Amazing finds of the ancient city can be seen in the most famous Archaeological Museum of Turkey. Here you will learn the secrets of the gallery of the gods, see firsthand the sarcophagi with ancient sculptures of the Roman Empire, as well as things that belonged to the very founder of the Turkish state.

Karaalioglu Park

Those who want to explore all the sights of Antalya should definitely visit Karaalioglu Park. It is located above the sea and stretches along the coast for hundreds of meters. Travelers can enjoy beautiful views of the city, magical mountains and sea waves shimmering in the sun. Sitting on benches in the shade of exotic flowers, you can hide from the heat and bustle.

Conclusion

Hospitable Antalya, the sights (photos presented above) of which have been preserved since ancient times, are always open for new tourists. Each traveler, finding himself in this mysterious country, will be able to feel the spirit of history. And some may try to unravel the secrets that ancient Turkey carefully conceals. Sights (Antalya are especially generous on them) of the country will certainly surprise the most seasoned travelers.
