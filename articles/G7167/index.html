Upgrade "Lenovo B560". Instructions and recommendations

The Lenovo B560 upgrade can significantly improve the performance of a mobile computing system. Most of these computers are based on low-performance microprocessors and have a small amount of memory. In addition, they were equipped with hard drives, and not high-speed solid-state drives. It is the replacement of these three components in the composition of this laptop model that this material will be devoted to.

Laptop specifications

Before we consider the upgrade of Lenovo B560, here are some typical characteristics of this computing device. For example, one of the models had such specifications:

  1. A Pentium P6100 processor with two cores and a clock frequency of 2.0 GHz, and a third-level cache of 3 MB.
  2. CPU socket PGA.
  3. The amount of RAM is 2 GB (2 modules of 1 GB) of the DDR3 format.
  4. Graphics accelerator HD Graphics.
  5. 15.6 ‚ÄùHD screen
  6. 250 GB hard drive

What can be updated?

As a rule, Lenowo B560 processors are the CPU of Celeron and Pentium lines. These chips have two processing units and operate at low frequencies. In addition, the cache size is reduced in them. Instead of these microprocessors, you can install high-performance i5 or even i7. In this case, the frequencies will increase significantly, and the number of logical cores will increase to four.

Also in such a computer, you can increase the amount of RAM. To do this, it is enough to purchase two new 4 GB DDR3 modules. Another possible way to increase performance is to replace the hard drive with an SSD drive. Actually, the upgrade of Lenovo B560 consists of the fact that you can install new previously listed components in this PC. But the video card cannot be replaced for the reason that its microcircuit is soldered to the system board.

Order of modernization

First you need to purchase those components that are planned to be replaced in a computer. The easiest way to do this is with RAM and a hard drive. Therefore, you need to buy two 4 GB RAM modules specifically for laptops. Their recommended clock frequency should be 800 MHz. You also need to buy a 256 GB SSD-drive of SATA 3 standard. Then, in the off state, unscrew the seven screws on the bottom cover of the laptop and remove the cover - the dummy. In this case, the rest of the body should remain on the computer. Then you can dismantle the RAM modules and install new ones instead. Next, unscrew the fixing screws of the hard drive and carefully remove it. Then you need to copy the image of this drive to a new SSD on another computer. The next step is to install a solid state drive, fix it with screws and install a cover. After that, you need to screw in the remaining screws.

But the upgrade of Lenovo B560 when replacing the processor is more complicated. In this case, you can not do without special equipment. The chip is soldered to the system board, and it can only be replaced using a soldering station. Therefore, in this case, it is recommended to contact a service center that specializes in providing such services.

Conclusion

This article made general recommendations on how to upgrade the Lenovo B560. There should be no problems with the hard drive and RAM modules. But the replacement of the processor must be entrusted to professionals who, with the help of special equipment, will do it easily and simply.
