Evolutionary theory

One of the most exciting issues at all times is the origin of man on Earth. Many theories have been put forward and are being put forward to this day, but not one has been fully proved. This suggests that no one can give an exact answer to the question posed. And yet, who created man?





At the end of the eighteenth century, Charles Darwin suggested that we got our start from an ordinary monkey. Evolutionary theory has become the brainchild of this scientist. He devoted his life to writing a scientific work in which he convincingly proved the origin of man from a humanoid monkey. Charles Darwin made very convincing arguments, highlighted many similarities between ancient monkeys and modern man.

This theory for a long time could not find like-minded people even in scientific circles, despite the fact that it was the only one that had at least some evidence, while others were based solely on assumptions and could be equally false or true. A better version does not exist at this time.

Merits of Darwin, of course, are great. He systematized all living organisms living on Earth, highlighted their similarities. But I did not find one hundred percent proof of the origin of man, which led to the appearance of more and more new concepts, which we will talk about right now.
