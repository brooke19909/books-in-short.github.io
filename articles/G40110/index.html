The status of powerful countries

As noted above, the term "great powers" was introduced into international everyday life after the defeat of Napoleonic troops. The authorship belongs to the German historian Leopold von Ranke. That was the name of his scientific work, published in 1833. At the Vienna Congress of 1814-1815, the status was assigned to the victorious countries of Napoleon - Russia, Great Britain, Austria and Prussia. In 1818, France added to this list. According to many experts, by the beginning of the XX century there were 5-6 great powers on the European continent. The inaccuracy of the number is explained by the fact that the Great Austro-Hungarian Empire collapsed. The Ottoman Empire, approaching the great one, also disintegrated, and Italy, on the contrary, reunited in 1860, became stronger and more competitive. In addition to European countries, the USA and Japan join the list of great powers at the beginning of the 20th century.
