What accommodation conditions does the hotel offer?





The hotel complex Laphetos Beach Resort consists of 312 rooms, including:

  • 248 standard rooms;
  • 24 large family rooms;
  • 40 connected rooms, which offer mainly large families or companies who have arrived to relax together.

It is immediately worth noting that the rooms here are spacious and bright thanks to the large windows. There is also a private balcony with outdoor furniture, where it is always a pleasure to spend time. Depending on the location of the apartment, the balcony overlooks the sea, the courtyard or the picturesque garden.

As for the room itself, there is everything for a comfortable stay, including soft carpeting, a large comfortable bed, a dressing table with a wall mirror, as well as cabinets, chairs and even a desk.

Food for travelers

Like most Turkish five-star hotels, Laphetos Beach Resort Spa Hotel offers its customers all-inclusive meals. In the spacious premise of the main restaurant three times a day are served buffet for hotel guests. In addition, each client of the Laphetos Beach Resort 5 * hotel has the right to light snacks and desserts throughout the day - they are served in local bars and snack bars, of which there are only five in the complex. The hotel also offers a visit to the local Italian restaurant a la carte. But the restaurant works according to the preliminary reservation system, and you also have to pay for the ordered dishes.

The beach here is sandy and pebble, always clean and well maintained. Naturally, a sightseeing vacation is another service that the Laphetos Beach Resort Spa can offer. Turkey is a country with an interesting history and ancient traditions, so lovers of excursions will not be disappointed. After all, the city of Side is considered one of the largest centers of antiquity in the country.

To begin with, you will probably be offered to visit the large Museum of Ancient Art, which contains a huge collection of ancient relics, including many exhibits of the Hellenistic period. In the southern part of the city, almost on the seashore, two sights are located at once - these are the remains of ancient temples dedicated to Artemis, the goddess of the hunt and the moon, and her brother Apollo, which symbolized the sun. Even the ruins of these once powerful sanctuaries are a spectacular sight, especially in night-time lighting.

Another ancient monument is the Great monumental fountain Nymphaeum, which was allegedly erected in the second century of our era. Of course, being in the city, it is worth visiting one more interesting place - the Port Bathhouse, which was built in the third century and today is turned into a museum.
