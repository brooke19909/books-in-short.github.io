Saratov is a large city on the banks of the Volgograd reservoir, the center of the region of the same name. One of the most important cultural, economic and educational centers of the Volga region. Saratov - the capital of the Volga region

The main city of the Volga region was founded in 1590. Scientists still can not unravel the origin of its name. Some associate it with the two Tatar words "sar" and "atav", which in general can be translated as "low-lying island." Others correlate the name of the city with the hydronym "Sarat" of Scythian-Iranian origin.

Modern Saratov is a large city with dozens of working industrial enterprises, a developed cultural sphere and numerous ancient monuments. At the beginning of 2016, the Saratovites received one very good news. Their native Saratov entered the top ten Russian cities for family vacations.

Saratov population: national and religious composition

Geographically, the city is located on the border of Europe and Asia. Historically, it was formed at the junction of several cultures. That is why the modern population of the city of Saratov is multi-ethnic.

The most numerous ethnic group here are Russians (about 91%). They are followed by the Tatars (2%), Ukrainians (1.3%), Armenians (1.1%), Kazakhs, Azerbaijanis, Belarusians and Jews (less than 1%).





Saratov can also be called the center of many faiths. In addition to the Orthodox, there are quite a lot of Roman Catholics and Muslims. The city has eight cemeteries, including Old Believers, Jewish and Tatar.

Social problems

In 2015, Saratov was among the three poorest cities in Russia, according to the results of studies of the Financial University under the Government of the Russian Federation. So, the share of the poor in the capital of the Volga region was 19%. In addition to poverty, the problem of clean streets and courtyards is very acute. Journalists and local residents have repeatedly written that the city in the literal sense of the word "just mired in garbage." True, in recent years the situation with cleaning and landscaping has improved significantly.

Other problems of Saratov remain unsolved. In particular, we are talking about an insufficient number of parking lots, uncontrolled felling of green areas in the city and a careless attitude to architectural monuments.

Conclusion

In this article, we examined and studied the population of Saratov in detail. Its number, as of the end of 2015, is 842 thousand people. In recent years, there has been a positive population growth.

Representatives of various nationalities live in the city: these are Russians, Tatars, Ukrainians, Armenians, Kazakhs and others. Despite numerous urban problems (poverty, unemployment, unsatisfactory condition of streets and the public utilities sector), Saratov continues to live and develop. And the positive dynamics of its population is a vivid confirmation of this.
