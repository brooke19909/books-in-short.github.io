Before knitting the cap with knitting needles, you should calculate the number of loops. For the design sample, we collect 20 loops of the selected yarn and knit 20 rows. Then we measure with a centimeter tape the sides of the resulting rectangle and perform arithmetic operations with the numbers obtained.



For example, the length of the sample - vertical measurement - is 10 cm, and the width is 15. Therefore, 20: 10 = 2. This means that by knitting 2 rows, you increase the length of the product by exactly one centimeter. Next, we calculate the width, so 20: 15 = 1, 44, that is, in one centimeter our sample has almost one and a half loops. And if the head volume of the one to whom we are going to tie the new thing is 56 centimeters, then we should put 56 X 1.44 = 151 loops on the knitting needle.



Having typed a certain number of loops on the knitting needle, we begin to knit according to the drawing. This model is also convenient in that you can try on the product at any time during knitting. When, at your discretion, the moment of rounding of the part comes, we begin the reduction of loops. This process can also be bivariate - either the decrease occurs quickly, and then the cap has a round top, or the decrease in the number of loops occurs slowly, and then the top of the cap is elongated, like a cap.
