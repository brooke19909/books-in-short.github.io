Basic assembly rules

The main goal at the preparatory stage is to bring a 4x4 cube to a 3x3 cube, then assembling a 4x4 Rubik's cube will become much easier and more understandable. To do this, you must first collect the centers of the cube - these are 4 internal squares of the same color.

Important! The 4x4 form does not have a fixed center, that is, you will need to arrange the colors (central fours) yourself. Further, if only the outer ribs are rotated, the arrangement of the centers will not be disturbed.

After you put the centers in place, the same thing needs to be done with rib pairs or corner elements. With the offset of the outer edges, we set the cube so that when the pair of left or right edges of the puzzle rotates, the elements on the edges converge together.
