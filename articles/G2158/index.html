Cities of the Orenburg region: Novotroitsk, Buzuluk, Orsk, Yasny. Short description, photo

Cities of the Orenburg region are one of the most visited areas of the Russian Federation by tourists. It is worth noting the splendor of landscapes, sights, their aristocracy and symbolism. The people living here are quite hospitable and responsive, always ready to help or act as a guide. Do not be afraid to turn to them for help. The cities of the Orenburg region are completely different from each other, they differ in their buildings, history and significance for the state. Among them there are especially memorable settlements, which are described below.

Novotroitsk

The city of Novotroitsk is located in Russia, located in the Orenburg region. The city was built on the banks of the Ural River. Almost ninety thousand people live here. The city was founded in 1920 by Ukrainian peasant immigrants. In the thirties, brown iron ore began to be mined here. Since then, the city began to actively develop ferrous metallurgy. Since 1955, the Ural Steel Metallurgical Plant has been operating in Novotroitsk.



Buzuluk

The city of Buzuluk in the Orenburg region is a locality of the Russian Federation with a population of more than eighty-five thousand people. Several rivers flow near the city, the largest are Samara and its tributary Buzuluk, in whose honor the city itself was named. Its history began with the laying of the Buzulutskaya fortress on the Samara River in 1736. In the sixties of the last century, the development of oil fields began in these places. Since then, the city has been actively developing to this day.



Orsk

Orsk is a large city in the Orenburg region of Russia, the second largest in the region in terms of population (approximately 230,000 people). The Ural River flows through it . Not far from Orsk is another city - Novotroitsk. They are very close to each other and actually have a common border. Cities of the Orenburg region are closely related.

Orsk was founded as a fortress on the banks of the Ural River in 1735. Now Orsk is a large industrial city. Here, non-ferrous metallurgy, mechanical engineering and mining are developed. The city has an oil technical school, a medical college and some other vocational education institutions, as well as branches of some universities.
