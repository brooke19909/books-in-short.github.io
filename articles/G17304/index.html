What is cash discipline

The term "cash discipline" refers to a set of rules dictated by legislative and regulatory authorities in relation to work with cash. Organizations must fulfill a number of requirements in order to continue to function normally without sanctions, fines, tax penalties and other unpleasant consequences of negligence and ignorance.

Cash discipline includes a number of measures to optimize cash management. So, a person with the appropriate education, without a criminal record, with an additionally executed contract on full material liability should conduct operations with money. In the process, he performs the following actions:

  • capitalization and expenditure operations;
  • ensuring the procedure for operations with revenue;
  • control over cash balance limits;
  • adherence to deadlines and collection of proceeds to the bank;
  • registration of primary cash documents;
  • filling out cash statements on the basis of the primary documentation, including filling out the book of the cashier-operator (a sample is presented below).

The combination of these actions is called cash discipline.





Maintaining cash records

The cash management sector involves the preparation of several types of documents that anticipate filling out the book of the cashier-operator. A sample of primary documents - PKO and RKO.

There are several varieties of cash documents, the completion of which requires discipline. This separation is due to the purpose of the operations that they reflect:

  • credit - cash contribution to the cashier of the enterprise;
  • expendable - cash withdrawal from the cash desk for the needs of the organization;
  • accounting registers and magazines reflect the aggregate movement of funds, responsible persons, details of primary cash documents.

The legislation establishes the following forms of documents that must be executed according to all requirements in any organization working with cash money supply:

  • Receipt cash warrant form KO-1.
  • Account cash warrant form KO-2.
  • Magazine of registration of credit and debit cash warrants - KO-3.
  • Cash Book - KO-4.
  • The book of accounting of funds accepted and issued by the cashier is KO-5.
  • Magazine (book) of the cashier-operator KM-4.

Document structure

For the book of the cashier-operating officer, the filling pattern is regulated by the requirements of Federal Law No. 54-FZ of May 22, 2003. It must be stitched, the sheets are numbered, at the end it must be sealed. How is this journal filled

Journal of the cashier-operator - a document containing a number of columns, each of which should contain certain information:

  • cashier shift opening date;
  • department number (if the organization has several employees);
  • surname, name and patronymic of the employee responsible for conducting cash transactions on this shift;
  • serial number of the shift according to the control counter of the fiscal memory of the cash register;
  • indications of the number of sales according to information taken from fiscal memory when transferring the device for repair or checking it;
  • the sum of the cumulative total at the beginning of the work shift (according to indications taken from fiscal memory);
  • Signature of the responsible cashier;
  • signature of the senior cashier supervising the work;
  • indications of the total accumulated amount drawn from the canceled cash register report at the end of the shift;
  • funds transferred to the main cash desk of the organization minus payments on cash account warrants;
  • the number of payments from the cashier on the submitted documents;
  • total payments made by bank transfer;
  • cash deposited in the cash desk of the organization;
  • the amount of refunds on cash receipts of buyers;
  • Signatures of the responsible person, senior cashier and head of the organization.

A total of 18 cells are allocated for each working day. It is worth noting that this form is uniform in Russia, the Republic of Belarus and Ukraine. The filling methods and the information reflected in the register are identical in these countries regarding cash transactions.
