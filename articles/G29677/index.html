The impeccable city of lights and casinos has long attracted millions of tourists from all over the world. It also houses the "Stratosphere Las Vegas" - one of the highest buildings of the state, which accommodates a huge network of all kinds of entertainment: hotels, casinos, restaurants, shopping centers and the like.





History

The idea to build this facility belongs to Bob Stupac, who already owned several casinos throughout Las Vegas. In "Stratosphere" he saw the perfect replacement for his own Vegas World gambling establishment, which is very popular among wealthy visitors to the city. In 1995, Grand Casinos joined the creation of the project, seeing a good potential in this idea. A little later, the creation of Stratosphere Corporation was announced - a public company whose shares could be acquired by anyone. In general, the case was gaining momentum.

The Stratosphere Las Vegas was inaugurated on April 30, 1996, but after a while, the Stratosphere Corporation experienced a series of financial blows, and therefore went bankrupt. Due to such turmoil, the construction of the second hotel complex was suspended, although work on the construction and facing of a couple of floors has already been completed. Economic problems affected the owners of the establishment, where Karl Haykan, who bought the bonds issued, became the main figure. The hotel-casino has come under the complete control of the Icahn Enterprises LP holding owned by the aforementioned Aikan.

Nevertheless, after such powerful shocks for the casino, new horizons have opened. The Stratosphere Las Vegas expanded significantly in June 2001, when the construction of a hotel with 1000 rooms was finally completed. 65 million dollars were invested in the implementation of this project - a considerable amount by the standards of that time.
