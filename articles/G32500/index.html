Everything has changed relatively recently - some thirty-odd years ago, in 1982. Khaosan Road: how to get there

You can come to Thailand in many ways. Most foreign tourists arriving in Bangkok arrive at Suvarnabhumi Airport. How to get to Khaosan Road from the airport? Simply steamed turnip. There are several options for this:

  • Aeroexpress and taxi

A special high-speed line called Airport Rail Line has been brought to the airport, and it is possible to get from Suvarnabhumi directly to the city in literally half an hour. To find this branch is also easy - you just need to follow the signs labeled City Line. You need to buy a ticket to the final stop - Phaya Thai station, and upon arrival there should take a taxi. However, remember: you must always agree with the driver to turn on the meter - otherwise the cost of a taxi will be royal.

  • Express Line and taxi

The Express Line branch runs even faster - it takes you to the right place in just fifteen minutes without stopping. After that you should also take a taxi with the meter on.

  • Aeroexpress or Express Line and bus

At the terminal station Phaya Thai, which can be reached by any of the two options described above, you need to take a bus, not numbers 2 and 59, in a taxi.

  • Taxi

You can leave Suvarnabhumi immediately by taxi, bypassing the railway. True, the trip will come out quite a waybill, so it is better to hit the road in three or four - it will come out much nicer financially. Taxi stands can be found on the ground floor of the airport.

  • Water transport

Do not forget about the river - boats depart from the pier Phra Arthit, where you can make an unforgettable, exciting and full of romance trip.
