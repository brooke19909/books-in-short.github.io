Amalfi Coast of Italy: description, attractions and reviews

There are many beautiful countries on our planet. Undoubtedly, sunny Italy also belongs to them. Magnificent natural landscapes, majestic mountains and volcanoes, a myriad of monuments of history, culture, architecture - all this attracts millions of tourists from all over the world.

But there is really a heavenly place in this southern country. The slopes of the mountains here are covered with orange groves, the colorful houses of local residents with large cozy terraces conquer their originality, and the shady narrow streets lead to an unusually clear and blue sea. It may seem to someone that this cannot be in our time. But they are wrong. Such a place really exists - this is the Amalfi Coast in Italy, located 90 kilometers from Naples and 300 kilometers from Rome.



Amalfi Coast is surrounded by steep mountain cliffs. Their height does not exceed seven hundred meters. From here you can enjoy breathtaking views that conquer at first sight. Numerous shops and churches, villas and palaces are as if carved from a rock. They "float" on each other, rushing upward, from the very edge of the sea. All buildings are surrounded by gardens and terraces, narrow streets are covered with pebbles. Olives, grapes, lemons fill the air with unusual refined aromas.

Climatic conditions

The climate on the coast is Mediterranean, with fairly warm winters (+ 13-17 ¬∞ C) and very hot summers - + 28-30 ¬∞ C. In May, you can already swim, the beach season usually begins in late May.

Where should I go, what to see on the coast?

If you are lucky enough to come to the Amalfi coast of Italy, be sure to visit Amalfi, take a ride here on a high-speed ferry boat. In Atrantri, look at the ancient Benedictine monastery of Saints Judith and Curico.

Do not forget to walk along the Path of the Gods. This is an ancient road connecting coastal villages. To get to her, you need to take a bus to Amalfi, next to Agerola. He will bring you to the place where the trail begins. A fascinating walk will take just over two hours.



Enter the stalactite caves located in the tiny town of Praiano, near Positano. If you will rest in August, then visit the Positano on the 15th. On this day there is a holiday dedicated to the patroness of the city - the Black Madonna.

Italy is very popular with Russian tourists today. The Amalfi coast (tours can be bought in almost every travel agency in our country) guarantees you a wonderful quality vacation both with your family and with friends. We will get acquainted with the resorts of the coast in more detail.

Coastal Resorts

A trip along the Amalfi Coast in Italy will give you great pleasure. According to tourists, this is not only surprisingly beautiful, but also a very romantic place. Cute little resort towns are completely different and distinctive. They are attractive each in their own way, their coves and steep mountain cliffs are picturesque, which seem to touch the tops of the clouds.

Amalfi Coast of Italy: Maiori

This city, located sixty kilometers from Naples, has the best beaches on the coast. Maiori is famous for its famous medieval towers and stone buildings.

The ancient temple of Santa Maria a Mare (XII century) is the main attraction of the city. Its roof is decorated with multi-colored majolica tiles, which gives the structure a special appeal. On the next hill (Pontechieu) you will see the magnificent castle of St. Nicholas.



Sorrento

Russian guides who conduct excursions on the Amalfi coast of Italy always note that Sorrento is the most famous and visited resort. Comfortable vacationers who want to combine a beach holiday with entertainment and sightseeing will feel comfortable here.

Indeed, this small town, symbolized by lemon, is created by nature itself for relaxation. Here, the Amalfi coast of Italy is especially beautiful. This town was visited by Stendhal and Goethe, Mendelssohn and Rossini, Dickens and Caruso. Attracts Sorrento and Italians. Residents of this sunny country are happy to spend their holidays or weekends here.



Cetara

Small and very beautiful city of fishermen. The air of Cetara is saturated with salty wind and freedom. In addition, the town is famous for its untouched nature and the cleanest beach. Peace and tranquility reign here.



The pride of the inhabitants of the city is St. Peter's Church. This saint is the patron saint of Cetara. The church is crowned with a magnificent dome of majolica (XII century).

Amalfi

This city, which gave the name to the entire coast, is located at the foot of the cliffs. For this reason, you can get into it only by sea or through the mountains on a dizzying serpentine. Hiking enthusiasts will enjoy it - you can walk along steep paths for hours, exploring picturesque sights.

The symbol of the city is the magnificent St. Andrew Cathedral (9th century). Norman and Arabic styles are intricately intertwined in its architecture. The portal of the Paradise Courtyard Cathedral (XIII century), made of bronze, is also unusually beautiful. Nice to walk along the Lungomare promenade.



Amazing tourists and the Della Carte Paper Museum. He reminds guests that the old paper mills still work in Amalfi. Tourists will also be interested in an excursion to the Arsenals of the Republic, and a visit to the amazing church of San Salvatore de Birecto, where the doges rest.

Minori

The Amalfi coast of Italy is attractive for tourists because the cities here are very close to each other. Therefore, if you wish, you can move from resort to resort, without spending a lot of time.

On the sandy and pebble beaches of this resort you can relax in the shade of orange groves. From Amalfi, you can get here in just five minutes.
The sights of this town include a well-preserved Roman villa with magnificent mosaics, the basilica of the patroness of the city of St. Trofimena (XII century), the Museum of Ancient Roman Amphoras and Antiquaries, and the magnificent Bell Tower of the Annunciata.



Italy, Positano (Amalfi Coast)

This picturesque resort lurks in a gorge of mountains, hiding from the sea winds. Many tourists believe that Positano seems to be lost in another time. Between the pretty houses, decorated with majolica, painted in different colors, the streets are so narrow that it is impossible to move around on them by car.

The main attraction of the city is the Romanesque church of Santa Maria (XII century). It contains the icon of the Black Madonna, recognized miraculous. This picturesque resort attracts many directors from around the world. More than thirty films have already been shot here.



Fans of noisy parties can have fun here in the disco bar, which is located directly on the beach or in a bright and original dance club.
Montepertuso is especially picturesque - a quarter of the city centered around the Vecchia fountain. On both sides it is surrounded by a cave and a niche, over which the patronesses of Montepertuso are depicted. Nearby is Mount Gamber (510 m).

Praiano

Very close to Positano is the tiny town of Praiano, which is crossed by numerous paths and intricate stairs. This resort is extremely popular among diving enthusiasts. Here is the famous La Boa Center.

Lovers of ancient architecture will be interested in the churches of St. Luke and St. Gennaro (XII century), especially their viewing platforms, which offer stunning views of the coast. On a rocky ledge stands the tower of Torre a Mare, whose history goes back to the Middle Ages. A narrow winding path leads to it.



Ravello

Another colorful town that literally hangs over the bright turquoise waters of the sea. Wagner and Grieg loved to relax here. This is an ideal place for newlyweds - wedding processions are very common on the streets of this city.

Villa Rufolo (13th century) became the venue for the annual music festival. The most important treasure of this town is carefully stored in the Cathedral of St. Panteleimon - a capsule with the blood of a saint. She baked, but once a year the blood becomes liquid.



The central boulevard of the city smoothly passes into the terrace of Richard Wagner. It is not at all surprising that the bewitching sounds of his music are sung here by local musicians.

Salerno

And at the end of our short trip, we will visit the pulsating heart of the coast - the beautiful Salerno, which is famous for its very beautiful promenade, historical center and medical school (active) of the late Middle Ages. Be sure to check out the Archaeological Museum and Pinacoteca, in the territory of the San Benedetto Abbey. And in Areca Castle you can see the medieval water supply system that has survived to this day.

Tourists reviews

The Amalfi coast of Italy, reviews of which are really only enthusiastic, is a magnificent nature, comfortable living conditions, excellent service. Here lovers of nightly entertainment and fans of a quiet pastime comfortably relax. The organizers of resorts from families with children deserve a lot of kind words. Rest of little tourists is thought out here to the smallest detail. And educational excursions are interesting not only for adults, but also for teenagers.

Most tourists note that these small resorts delight in their originality. Bright small cities with very narrow streets seem unusual to Russians. Many note the benevolence of the local population. If you even speak a little Italian, they will tell you in detail how to get to the attractions of interest to you. And of course, the main share of positive reviews relates to well-equipped and well-groomed beaches.
