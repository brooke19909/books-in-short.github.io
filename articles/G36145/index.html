Basic concept

Maslow‚Äôs book ‚ÄúMotivation and Personality‚Äù examines the structure of needs, their basic properties, and the impact on the motivational sphere of a person. Maslow's pyramid consists of blocks, each of which reflects a specific need - from basic to higher. This model is very easy to explain with an example. The basic human needs are physiological needs such as hunger and thirst. Experiencing them, hardly anyone will think, for example, about the philosophy of postmodernism. Therefore, in the first place, the vital needs of the body must be satisfied.

Then follows the need for security - both physical and emotional. It is important to have a roof over your head, to live without a threat to existence.





When these needs are met, a person begins to seek satisfaction of the needs of a higher class - social recognition, sympathy. The need for friendship is followed by the need for self-esteem, that is, a person now wants autonomy and leadership.

The pinnacle of the Maslow pyramid is the need for self-actualization. When all other needs are satisfied, a person can begin to freely realize the potential inherent in him.

So, at every point in time, unmet needs control the behavior. And more often than not, they are never completely satisfied. That is why people who have reached the level of self-actualization in their lives are about 2%, as stated in Maslow‚Äôs book ‚ÄúMotivation and Personality‚Äù.

Maslow's book Motivation and Personality Summary

The first part of the book is devoted to the study of the essence of needs, how their satisfaction affects the development of a person, the formation of his character, and the concept of instincts is once again reviewed. Maslow says that this construct is applicable to humans to a small extent. The next part discusses the concept of mental health, the role of frustration in meeting needs, the question of the acceptability of aggressive behavior. Maslow says that a positive attitude will almost neutralize aggression: therefore, it is always necessary to consider environmental conditions when considering any type of behavior, including destructive behavior.

Next, Maslow considers the actual self-actualizing people, the features of their character. In addition, the third part focuses on issues of love, as well as creativity and its manifestations.
