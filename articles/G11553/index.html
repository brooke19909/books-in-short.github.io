Pekka Eric Auvinen, Finnish mass killer. The massacre in Jokela

Why, then, did a seemingly normal teenager, characterized solely on the positive side, decide on a brutal murder? And what is generally known about this incident that happened in a quiet provincial town of Finland? Consider these issues in more detail.

The reasons for the wrongful acts, which ultimately ‚Äúdare‚Äù the killers, obsessed with megalomania, should be sought in the period of life when they were children. And Pekka Eric Auvinen committed the crime, too, not from scratch. Moreover, the young man prepared for him in advance ...



Pekka Eric Auvinen was a native of the small town of Tuusula (Finland). He was born on June 4, 1989. The parents of the future killer were law-abiding citizens: the mother worked at the city hall, the father worked on the railway, so it would be more logical to exclude the ‚Äúgenetic‚Äù factor. But the "psychological" can not be set aside.

Pekka Eric Auvinen began to experience problems at school. In his youth, he became an object of ridicule from peers, and to restore peace of mind, the future killer often vented anger on younger children. Over time, Eric hated single parents, homosexuals, and those in love. However, he did not show open discontent in relation to the above categories, on the contrary, he was a calm and peaceful teenager, seriously interested in philosophy and history. No one could have imagined that from this exemplary teenager a Finnish mass murderer could turn out. No signs of trouble. And Eric's parents at first did not believe that their offspring had committed the massacre at school.

Preparatory Activities

As already emphasized, the young man premeditated the crime, which he odiously called: "Central Impact." The police managed to find out that he periodically visited the local "Hunting Club", of which he was a member. As a result, he had the privilege of buying firearms. Naturally, the young man took advantage of it. The "Hunting Club" in the person of its chairman helped Eric to acquire his own 22-caliber pistol, which, anyone would think, would become a murder weapon. It's about the Sig Sauer Mosquito model. But it is known that the young man wanted to opt for another model - Birret. However, Eric failed to buy it, since he was under 21 years old.

The tragedy happened in the fall

In the morning of November 7, 2007, Pekka Eric was already in the territory of his native lyceum. On this day, there was a massacre at school. The intentions of the young man were the most serious. He had taken a Sig Sauer Mosquito pistol with him in advance. First opened fire on students on the ground floor. In a panic, the children tried to jump out of the windows to escape. But not everyone succeeded. Then Eric moved to the second floor, where he continued shooting at people. Entering one of the rooms, he alternately aimed his gun at the lyceum director, nurse and students. They failed to survive. Then he walked along the corridor of the educational institution and urged peers to smash the school. Soon the police arrived at the scene.

Law enforcement officers acted as carefully as possible to avoid additional casualties. They did not use force methods and tried through negotiations to convince the young man to give up.

The final

Realizing that it was useless to resist, Eric proceeded to the toilet room, where he tried to commit suicide. The young man put a bullet in his head. At 2 pm, the police found a killer who, although bleeding, was still alive. Then the ambulance took Eric to the clinic, and after a few hours he died.

The tragedy caused a serious public outcry in Finland. Activists demanded that authorities in the country limit the issuance of firearms to certain categories of citizens.

And schoolchildren and teachers who suffered from the hands of a young man were forced to take a long course of psychological rehabilitation. As a result, 9 people were killed and 12 wounded.

Society and government should react toughly to those who are trying to put themselves above others and arrange lynching over innocent people.
