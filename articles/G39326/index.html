Krasnodar Territory, pages: alphabetical list

Before proceeding with the list of villages of the Krasnodar Territory, as a preamble, it is worth talking about the meaning of the word. In the explanatory dictionaries, this is a large Cossack settlement, capable of putting up a squadron (a hundred combat horsemen). During the imperial era in Russia, military outposts were called villages. At that dashing time of raids and Russian-Turkish wars, they defended domestic borders from enemies.





Therefore, in some regions of the country, villages are still preserved. On the one hand, they are considered echoes of heroic history, on the other, the fact of the revival of the oppressed and persecuted Cossacks.

The village as the main administrative unit of the Kuban

Essentially, it is. According to official statistics, in the Krasnodar Territory there are 26 cities, 411 rural districts and 12 villages. But in total 1726 settlements, 11 of which are abandoned. Many of the current cities used to be villages, and to date there are more than 200. But now pay attention to the number of cities and towns. It turns out that the village is one of the main units, and the appearance of the first historian dates back to 1780.





Separately, it is worth noting the year 1783, when, by decree of Catherine the Great, the lands of the Coastal Kuban and Tmutarakan (Taman Peninsula) were included in the Russian Empire. It was then that the Cossack army was required to protect the borders of the fatherland from the adversaries.

Seaside

It is quite obvious that the tourism industry in the cities and villages of the Krasnodar Territory is based on treatment and relaxation on the shores of two seas: the Azov and the Black. And the possibilities in this regard are very great. More than 15 million tourists visit the region annually, and most of them find a roof over their heads in the villages.

They are located in five large resort areas: Sochi, Gelendzhik, Taman Peninsula, Tuapse and Anapa. For a detailed description, from the list of villages of the Krasnodar Territory by the sea, we choose two - one on Azov and the other on Black.

Blagoveshchenskaya

From the entire list of villages of the Krasnodar Territory, its location is the most successful. Blagoveshchenka, as the locals say, not only settled down at three seas: the Black, Kiziltash and Vityazevsky estuaries, but also is part of the resort city of Anapa.





The village was founded by Zaporizhzhya Cossacks, who in 1836 established their own kuren in this territory, and this event occurred on the day of the church holiday of the Annunciation. Hence the name came from. For those who don‚Äôt know, kuren is the home where the Cossack family lives. In a broader sense, the word means a military village under the control of the chieftain.

Blagoveshchensk suffered a lot in its time, but now it is famous for its largest diving center and natural sandy beaches.

Golubitskaya

The list of villages of the Krasnodar Territory is rich in yet another resort territory. Golubitskaya is located off the coast of the Sea of ‚Äã‚ÄãAzov and is divided into two parts: upper and lower. The main attraction is the Golubitsky Lake, known since the time of Catherine the Great by the presence of healing mud.

A farm has been located here since 1879, and in 1907 the territory became a village, which is still known for its three dozen mud volcanoes. By the way, the most popular are located near the central beach.





In the Golubitskaya stanitsa, the largest water park on the entire Azov coast is located, near the Valley of lotuses. Tourists have a belief that a blossoming bud will fulfill a desire, so the main stream of guests is observed in July-August.
