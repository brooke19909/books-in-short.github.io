Origami Tulips

To make such a voluminous postcard, you need to learn how to fold a flower from a square sheet of paper. For work, prepare several multi-colored sheets of double-sided colored paper, cut them into squares of different sizes. Then the bouquet will consist of a variety of flowers. Each square must be folded diagonally, carefully smooth the finger with a finger, then the future flower is bent again in half. It turns out small rectangular triangles. It will turn out to be a do-it-yourself postcard on March 8, if you unbend the last fold back and turn the blank over with the back side. Then it remains to bend the corners of the base up, but not completely. These will be the tulip petals. You can bend the edges a couple of millimeters to give the flower a larger volume.

First, the stems and leaves of tulips are glued to the lower edge. Leaves for bulk before gluing are folded in half. The flowers are held on the glue by folds, the rest of the surface of the cardboard sheet does not touch.
