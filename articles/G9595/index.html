Bobbin weaving for beginners: a master class, schemes and recommendations

An ancient type of folk art is bobbin weaving. In Russia, this needlework was not immediately called lace. Initially, this word was applied to any decoration that adorned a product around the edge. Weaving of bobbins was brought to Russia from Europe in the seventeenth century. Then the word "lace" was assigned to products woven on bobbins.

Types of lace products

Laces can be either measured or piece.





The first ones are measured in meters. They are narrow (1-3 cm), medium (up to 6 cm), wide (more than 6 cm). In addition, measured lace is distinguished by shape:

  1. The seam has a narrow edge on both sides.
  2. An agramant from both edges is serrated.
  3. The edge is serrated on one side and flat on the other.




Piece lace is a complete, whole product: a tablecloth, curtain, cloak, jacket, vest, boa, scarf, collar, cuffs, napkin, etc.

Technical equipment

Bobbin weaving begins with the preparation of tools and fixtures. There are not so many of them:

  1. Roller. It can be done independently. To do this, take a piece of dense fabric. The size of the piece will depend on whether the roller is large or small. For beginners, they are the most comfortable with a size of 40 X 60 or 60 X 80 cm. On the long sides, a hem of 2 cm is made for him. The short sides are sewn together and a cylinder is obtained. A lace is threaded into the binder on one side and tightened. Next, the roller is filled with filler. It can be foam rubber, hay, sawdust. Then it should be covered with a cardboard circle and tighten the second binder. The roller is ready.
  2. Stand. It is needed to give stability to the roller. It can be made from a rectangular piece of foam. The two opposite sides should be twisted and fixed so that a hollow forms in the middle in which the roller can be laid.
  3. Bobbins for weaving lace are wooden sticks that have a ‚Äúneck‚Äù for winding threads.
  4. Pins Needed to secure bound threads. There should be many, at least a hundred.
  5. Knitting hook. Used to grip threads.
  6. Chip - a picture in which dots show the places where the pins are pierced.

Threads. They can be of different thicknesses. It is best to use linen or silk, they give minimal shrinkage after washing the product. During the training period, you can use cotton.





Winding bobbin threads

Patience is what bobbling requires. For beginners, the very first step is winding threads. She will become the training of this quality of character.

Bobbins are used in lacework only in pairs, one thread is wound on two bobbins. This is done like this:

  • You need to take one of the bobbins with your right hand, and the thread with your left. With your finger you need to press the end of the thread to the neck of the bobbin, and with your left hand make several revolutions around it to secure the thread.
  • When it is fixed, rotate the bobbin, winding the thread around the neck. With your left hand you need to hold it, adjusting the uniform distribution along the entire length of the neck of the whooping cough.
  • After wounding 2-3 meters, it is fixed with a loop. After that, the bobbin should hang on a thread and not unwind.
  • Then, without tearing, they unwind another 2-3 m from the skein and only then they cut it off. The free end is wound on a second bobbin and is also looped. In this case, between the bobbins should remain 20 cm of thread.

Thread replacement during weaving

If the thread ends during weaving, then you need to do the following:

  1. Unwind it, freeing the bobbin, and leave the end 20 cm long.
  2. Screw on a new seat a new one.
  3. Secure the end with a knot on a pin, which to stick 5 cm above the place of weaving.
  4. Connect the left end of the old thread with the new one. Wrap a double thread around the bobbin and secure with a loop.
  5. Weave continue with double thread.
  6. After the new thread is sufficiently fixed, the old one can be cut and weaved further than one.

Bobbin weaving: learning the basics

When weaving in both hands is a pair of whooping cough. All weaves are made in two ways to move the tool in hands:

  1. To transfer means to swap them in one hand. This is done with the thumb so that the right bobbin spreads over the left.
  2. To weave means to shift one bobbin from hand to hand. In this case, the right bobbin from the left hand should spread through the left bobbin in the right hand.

Bobbin weaving is of three types:

  1. Weave in half-plaiting: to weave - to weave.
  2. To weave in full braid: to cross - to weave - to cross.
  3. Weave with linen: weave - twist - weave.
