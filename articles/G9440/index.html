Duderhof Canal - a promising waterway

St. Petersburg is a city of canals, bridges and rivers. And each of these objects has its own purpose, its history and its secrets.

Where is the Duderhof Canal located? It flows in the Krasnoselsky district of the Northern capital. It got its name from the river of the same name Duderhofka, which flowed here even before the mid-twentieth century. During the construction of the canal, water was diverted into it from several flowing reservoirs of this region and the Duderhofka riverbed was changed, straightening it and putting it in granite. Thus, the geography and topography of the area were radically changed. In the upper reaches of the Duderhof, it now flows through the Mozhaisk settlement, Krasnoe Selo, Ligovo, Gorelovo, until it flows into the Duderhof canal.

Waterway Artery

The path of this aquatic artery is quite winding. The Duderhof Canal originates from a pond located at the intersection of the Peterhof Highway with Marshal Zhukov Street. When land reclamation work was carried out to lay the canal, the channels of many small rivers were laid along buried channels underground. So, the Dachnaya rivulet was taken to Krasnenkaya, and the rivulets Ivanovka, Duderhof and Novaya were diverted to the Duderhof Canal under construction. Dams in the upper reaches of some rivers or collectors directing underground water for others were arranged.

Along the entire channel, a shaft was poured, which protects when the surge rises. The shores of the canal for its greater length were dressed in granite in the best traditions of the Northern capital. In some areas, they are reinforced with reinforced concrete walls or earthen slopes. And in the area where the canal flows into the Gulf of Finland, they are not fortified at all.





Stages of development

In the 60s of the last century, construction began more actively in the Gulf of Finland. But its shores had a very large difference in height, because the authorities decided to level them. On the southern shore, territory was raised from the Peterhof Highway to the coast. For this, it was necessary to do the diversion of numerous small rivers, directing their waters to the sea. The ideal solution was the construction of the Duderhof Canal.

Conventionally, the channel of this man-made reservoir can be divided into three sectors. The first is latitudinal, 2.5 kilometers long and up to 30 meters wide. The second sector, the meridian, is located almost perpendicular to the first. Its length is 850 meters, it flows along the South Seaside Park. And the third section of the canal is 1.5 kilometers long - from the Matisov Canal to the Neva Bay.

Bridges and piers

The banks of the canal are very picturesque. For the convenience of residents of the surrounding areas, they are equipped with slopes and piers. For the townspeople, this is a favorite place for walking by the water. Six bridges that connect both parts of the city are thrown across the canal (with a total length of 4.87 kilometers).





In the upper reaches, right behind the pond, there is a dam bridge. It serves to control the water level in the canal and in the pond. Downstream is the cable-stayed bridge, which is located at the Walk of Fame. In these places there were fierce battles during the war, so many objects here bear the names of heroes or are associated with military operations. They keep the memory of the feat of the people, who did not allow the enemy to Leningrad.
