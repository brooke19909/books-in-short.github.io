Landmarks of Ireland - the green country of St. Patrick

Ireland is a country of indescribable landscapes, historical places and friendly people. Arriving there, travelers plunge into the atmosphere of goodness and freedom. Oddly enough, but this European state has recently attracted the attention of tourists. Previously, almost no one was interested in the culture, interesting places and picturesque nature of the Republic. Today, the sights of Ireland conquer tourists every day.



To begin with, every visitor should definitely visit the world famous "cliffs" - cliffs that are located on the Atlantic coast. They are also called sheer. In addition, many nature lovers should definitely visit the hills and heathlands, because it is precisely about them that are mentioned in all the legends and traditions of the ancient people of the Republic. The sights of Ireland are fascinating with its diversity, and every tourist will surely find something to his liking. It is worth noting that the most visited place in the country is its capital, Dublin. This is one of the oldest cities in Europe, which means "Black Harbor". Dublin is rich in magnificent architecture, original streets (which are very spacious and lively), beautiful old mansions, public parks and squares. In the city you can easily visit any museum or theater you like, look at the residence of the English Viceroy of Ireland, visit the oldest library (Chester Beatty).



The sights of Ireland can turn the world of man. They are filled with the history of a great nation, which in the shortest possible time was able to create a great state, where there is definitely something to see. Returning to the city of Dublin, it is also worth advising tourists to visit Trinity College, Belfast Prison, an Irish yacht club and, of course, the municipal gallery of modern art. The unusual country of Ireland, whose sights seem to have no borders, is also interesting in the ancient town of Bru-en-Bois, which is rich in archaeological sites of the Newgrange, Naut and Daut burials.

For example, Newgrange is a huge mound that existed long before the appearance of the Egyptian pyramids, but it was discovered only in the seventeenth century. One of the amazing phenomena of this ancient monument is that the sun's rays enter the tomb only once a year (December 21, the day of the winter solstice), and only for 17 minutes. At this time, the entire tunnel (which extends to the burial chamber) is illuminated with light, and an unusual ornament on the stone located there becomes visible. Also, Ireland's attractions include the cities of Cork and Waterford, where there is definitely something to see. Having visited the county of Donegal, you can see the place along which (according to legend) a headless horseman moved.


Tourists will be delighted to visit the Aran Islands: Inishmore, Inishman and Inishir. Ireland boasts many interesting places and places.
