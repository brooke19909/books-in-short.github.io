Types of pockets in clothes

Pockets on garments perform not only the function of storing useful and necessary things. They are decorative elements that give a distinctive appearance. Recently, designers have fantasized more and more about pockets. Variations change and new elements are added, but the main types of pockets themselves remain unchanged. In the article we will consider carefully their known types, what details transform them, how to process and sew pockets at home. In the presented photos, you will consider each element separately.

The main types of pockets

1. patch pockets, that is sewn on top of clothes. The inlet may be located on top or side.

2. Welt pockets are made by cutting the fabric or located in the seams, folds and reliefs of clothing.

According to the shape of the arrangement, the following types of pockets are distinguished: external and internal. There are also complex pockets that combine both types or several varieties of one. Let us consider in more detail each of them separately.

External pockets

These items include patch pockets, which can be simple, with a flap. It makes up one common part and a separate valve sewn alongside.





Perform such an item of clothing is easy. It is necessary to think over the shape and size of the part and draw it on a piece of paper, measuring 1 cm on each side of the hem of the fabric. Looking on which side the inlet will be, we make a larger hem to stitch it in the right place. The patch pocket can have different shapes: square and round, semicircular and multifaceted, rectangular and in the shape of a trapezoid. You can fantasize freely, depending on what clothes they will be on. Consider how they are made in the next photo.

Sewing pockets

Having drawn a template on paper, you need to transfer it to a pocket pattern. Then the fabric is cut out and bent along the contours of the fold line from all sides. The inlet is wrapped twice so that the edges are denser and more durable. The remaining rims are treated on an overlock or using an overlock foot. Then the edges are bent to the middle and ironed well. Many craftswomen use an adhesive pencil so that before sewing, the fold line fits snugly against the main part. You can iron the fold lines with a hot iron through a damp cotton cloth.





After that, mark with chalk the place on the clothes where the pocket will be sewn and place the part on top. You can pre-stitch the element in its permanent place. It remains only to stitch it.

Round pocket

Such an overhead element is usually made either on women's or girl's clothes. First, they draw a pattern on paper in the form of a circle (compasses). Next, a semicircle is cut out, and the template is transferred to the fabric. Do not forget to leave around the edges 1 cm per hem. The inlet with a needle and thread is pinned. The remaining edges must be ironed by making a clear bend line.





Then the bar is cut separately and sewn to the assembled edge from the inside. It remains to stitch the front edge along the front side. In this way, the inlet is decorated and sealed. The finished pocket is attached to the main location on top of the clothes.

Valve Types and Forms

The patch pockets can be different, depending on the designer's idea. Edges are processed by a level or leaf, a turning or a slanting inlay, the separate or bent valve. There are such parts with a lining for strength, but there are simply from one layer of matter. Patch pockets are always made separately, then put together and sewn onto clothes at the end.





Valves can also be of different shapes. The figure above shows only their main types. Now a great many of them have been invented. This is a matter of fantasy master. Also patch pockets can be single or double, in which there is an inner second layer of lining fabric or seal. This is done on soft tissues to give strength to the pocket. Consider now the complex pleated pockets.

Safari pockets

Such a voluminous pocket is attached mainly to sportswear made of cotton. They are comfortable, they can accommodate a large number of things needed in the campaign. Such an overhead product is made by folding the fabric. These pockets are also called pleated. Most of these pockets have flaps that either simply close the inlet or fasten with a button, blocking uninvited guests from accessing personal items.





Such a pocket can simply be with a central internal or external fold, or it can also consist of a fold on the sides, which increases the internal volume of the part. They make it according to this pattern by folding the fabric.





Welt pocket

Such pockets are made from the inside of the product or from the main fabric, or from the lining. A variant of any decorative matter is possible, then the interior finish of the pockets will serve as an additional decoration of clothes.

They can be decorated with stitches, edged with braid, in the form of a connection between the lining and the pick. Basically, this is used in women's products. Welt pockets can have a different arrangement. This can be pockets in the seams or folds of clothing, or cut through the middle of the fabric.

Pocket design

On men's jackets, you can often find the design of a pocket in a frame with two edging. This is a classic option for both jackets and trousers. Often, these details of clothing are often closed with overhead valves, the shape of which depends on the wishes of the master and the style of the product.

Before you make such pockets, you need to duplicate the shelf and both hem with the help of thermal cloth.





On the fabric from the inside, you need to outline the frame of this part. Then it is sewn with copy stitches so that these contours are visible on the front side. Then, in the center of the future pocket in the frame, you need to sew the lining. The product is flipped to the back side and a rectangular frame is sewn along the contour lines. Then a central cut is made with a sharp knife. They do not do it to the end. Not reaching 1 cm to the short side of the rectangle, the cuts go to the corners to the end of the line. Performing these steps, the stitching must be held by hand so as not to cut it.

After that, you need to thread the material of the grinders into the slot and turn everything inside out. Smooth the fold line well and iron it with steam or through a damp cotton cloth. The triangle in the corners also carefully bends inward.

Further separately you need to sew the inside of the pocket from the lining or the main material. Then, from the inside, the lining is sewn first with one edge, then with the other.

It is worth noting that welt pockets can be not only straight, but also inclined, curly and completely vertical. The frame can be fastened with a button or with a zipper. Sometimes make complicated pockets. For example, on the overhead product they make another small mortise pocket in a frame with a "zipper" for storing money or small valuable parts.

Seam pocket

Of all types of pockets, this is considered the easiest to execute. It is enough to cut the inside of the part from the main or lining fabric and sew it from the inside to the hole left in the side seam. But here there are options for complication in order to additionally decorate clothes. If you look at the photo below, you can see that a simple, it would seem, pocket in the seam is decorated with an insert of contrasting fabric.





This option can be used in women's or children's things.

If you will sew clothes yourself, then do not forget that pockets carry not only functional value, but also play an aesthetic role. After all, beautifully sewn pockets will only decorate a thing. So fantasize and add beautiful details, using both overhead, different-shaped parts, and slotted.
