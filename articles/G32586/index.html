Existing types of conditioners

If we consider the types of air conditioners, then we can distinguish household, semi-industrial and industrial solutions. It is worth considering the first option in detail.



Monoblock devices and split systems are customary to distinguish among household categories. Monoblock conditioners can be presented in different forms. The first to consider is mobile. It is a mobile all-in-one with an air duct brought out in the form of a flexible corrugated hose. Different types of mobile air conditioners may differ externally, but their operation is carried out according to a similar principle. The duct is usually displayed through a door or through an ajar window. In appearance, these units are similar to low bedside tables on wheels, which do not require installation. Their main advantage lies in the absence of the need for complex installation. As their disadvantage, one can name the requirement to allocate space on the floor, which should be considered when buying.



If we further consider the types of air conditioners, then we can mention the monoblock unit designed for installation in a window opening. The disadvantage is the loud noise when working in comparison with split systems, as well as the need for a strictly defined installation location. This decision affects the illumination of the room. Low cost is the main advantage of such units. Primitive options work only for cooling, and more expensive solutions can also heat the air.



When considering types of air conditioners, one should not forget about split systems. There is an indoor and outdoor unit, interconnected by copper tubes with a refrigerant, as well as electric cables. This design allows you to take the compressor out of the room, but this is the most noisy and bulky part. Split systems have a number of advantages, among which we can name almost silent operation. You can choose your favorite indoor unit, as well as the installation location. Indoor units can be wall, ceiling, cassette, duct and column. It is worth considering these types of air conditioners.

The most popular solution is wall mounted. In apartments and offices of small sizes they are installed most often. The indoor unit is mounted to the wall in the room, and the external - outside. The ceiling or console air conditioner looks like a wall-mounted one, but looks more like a heating battery. A similar device is mounted to the ceiling, which allows you to direct the stream along it. Cassette columned air conditioner has increased cooling capacity, because it is used for work in rooms with a large area. In the channel solution, the indoor unit is completely hidden behind a suspended ceiling. A special duct system delivers chilled air to several rooms at the same time.

If we consider the types of floor air conditioners, then it is worth saying that they differ only in their power, as well as in appearance. The convenience of such solutions is the uniform supply of conditioned air into the room.
