Kurumoch Airport in Samara

Kurumoch Samara Airport borrowed its unusual name from the village located in the neighborhood. Having begun its work in December 1957, the runway of the Kuybyshev State Airport was the most modern in the Soviet Union and was intended to receive high-speed aircraft.

History of Kurumoch Airport

Since the opening, the Kuibyshev airport has been used to train pilots to operate medium-range IL-18 and An-10 aircraft. In February 1961, the first cargo flight from Kuibyshev to Moscow was carried out. And since May of that year, regular passenger flights were launched on high-speed planes from Kurumoch Airport to Tashkent, Leningrad, Adler, Sverdlovsk and Tbilisi. After 2 years, the air hub began receiving and sending freight and passenger flights on Tu-124 and An-12 aircraft.

In 1970, the airport received and sent about 27,000 tons of mail and various cargoes and more than 700 thousand Soviet passengers. After the reconstruction of the airport facilities in 1986, a new runway was launched, and in 1990 the passenger flow increased to 3.7 million people per year.



In 1992, the Kurumoch airport in Samara received international status and commissioned an international terminal with direct flights to Turkey, the United Arab Emirates, China, Greece, and Israel.

Samara Airport today

During the perestroika period, the airport was part of Samara Airlines. In 2003, it became an independent, one-third state-owned institution. In the crisis 2000s, passenger flow decreased to 800 thousand and only in 2010 approached 1.5 million people. Kurumoch International Airport, according to the IATA KUF code, is today one of the largest Russian airports. Intensively developing, the airport every year expands the geography of both domestic and international flights.

The capacity of the Kurumoch airport per hour reaches 600 Russian passengers, about 150 international and about 50 go through the business terminal. Samara Kurumoch Airport today has the status of an airport of federal importance and is an important link in passenger and cargo transportation on routes within the country, between the CIS countries and foreign countries.

Two runways equipped with all the necessary devices are capable of receiving almost all types of airliners and helicopters.

Where is the airport

The distance from Samara to Kurumoch Airport (the largest in the Volga region) is 35 km to the north, it is located 45 km east of Tolyatti. The nearest settlement to the airport is the village of Birch. From different areas of the cities of Samara and Tolyatti to the settlement of Birch there are several routes of city buses.

How to get to Kurumoch airport from Samara?

You can get to the airport by bus, taxi, minibus, aeroexpress - there are several options. By your own or rented car, you can get to the airport via the M5 highway.

The easiest, fastest and most expensive way is to take a taxi. Some companies that provide ongoing services to air passengers provide the "Travel Companion" service. Having made an order 2 days before the proposed trip and agreeing to travel with an outsider, you can save a lot.



From August 2016, from the railway platform "Kurumoch Airport" to Samara, you can take the express train. Travel time is 1 hour 15 minutes. There are 8 flights per day. The train on the way makes 4 stops. Buses to the Kurumoch Airport building in Samara deliver free not only passengers, but also accompanying passengers. The ticket is bought for cash on the express train and includes baggage.

Near the exit from the airport building there is a bus stop and fixed-route taxis. Traveling from Kurumoch Airport to Samara or Tolyatti takes from 40 minutes to 1 hour.
