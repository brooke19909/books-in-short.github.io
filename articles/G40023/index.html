Historic Kuopio, Finland

Kuopio was first mentioned in 1550 when the Finnish reformer Mikael Agricol pointed to Kuopionniemi for the construction of a church. The city became the center of the province in 1653 through the efforts of the Swedish governor Pietari Brahe. Only in 1775, on the orders of King Gustav III, Kuopio was awarded the status of the center of the province. An interesting fact: following the results of the Russian-Swedish war, the Orthodox Cathedral of St. Nicholas was founded here. Now this building is the oldest in the city. After joining Russia, this region began to develop actively. The opening of the Saimaa Canal also played a large role in this . And the branch of the Savon rata railway doubled the progress.

Sports in Kuopio (Finland)

Absolutely the entire area, spread out by Mount Puyyo - the oldest reserve in Finland. On its territory you can go skiing in the winter or cycling along specially marked routes. Kuopio has over 400 km of cross-country skiing trails. They also love to ride dog sledges and snowmobiles.
