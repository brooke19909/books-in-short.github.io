Deauville, France: photos with descriptions of attractions, what to see, tourist reviews

Deauville (France) is one of the most famous and exclusive Norman resorts. This is a very cozy town on the English Channel, as if intended for relaxation. It is considered the most prestigious and closest seaside resort to Paris in the country. But this town of France is famous not only for bathing, restaurants and aristocratic holidays. Deauville (France): how to get

This resort town is both far and close to Paris. It is 200 kilometers from the French capital. There is an airport in Deauville, this is Saint-Hatier. But only flights from London and Montpellier arrive there. Therefore, there are two ways to get there from Paris - by train or by your own or rented car. You can, of course, consider such an option as a taxi, but it's too expensive. Trains to Deauville depart from Saint-Lazare Station. Drive about two or three hours. It all depends on the class of the train - whether it is an electric train or a high-speed train. A car gets to Deauville in about the same time. It is best to go on the A 13 motorway. It is both fast and free.

Resort History

Glamorous Deauville (France) was built specifically for people of high society to enjoy sea bathing. This was done in the fifties of the XIX century by Charles de Morny. Legend says that his wife, the former Princess Trubetskaya, advised him to do this. And Charles de Morney himself was a relative of Napoleon Bonaparte, although very distant. From a small fishing village, nothing more remarkable, he managed to make an aristocratic resort. Very high-ranking ladies and gentlemen began to come to rest there. It is interesting that in our democratic times, celebrities and rich people also aspire to Deauville. Of course, Deauville was also chosen by movie stars: Belmondo, Catherine Deneuve, Isabelle Adjani ... Boardwalk and Thalassotherapy Center

The wooden promenade of La Planche has a length of about two kilometers. It is located 300 meters from the water. Here is the most prestigious place for walks of citizens and especially vacationers who can make them in any weather. The French come here as whole families and walk their children, grandparents and dogs. The most fashionable clothes on the boardwalk - a kind of "uniform" - are a kind of "sailor suit", T-shirts and thin sweaters in blue stripes. And parallel to the embankment stretches Avenue Eugene Cornush - the most luxurious Avenue Deauville (France). In the reviews, tourists highly recommend taking a walk along the promenade. Here you will be offered various massages, baths, applications from therapeutic muds, as well as creams and ointments based on seaweed.
