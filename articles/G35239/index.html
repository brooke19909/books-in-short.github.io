Exploring a unique edge



It is known that projects were put forward to connect Seliger with the Pola River and Lake Ilmen under Peter I. Ya. E. Sivers, Novgorod Governor-General, wanted to start its practical implementation. The energetic administrator offered Catherine II all kinds of projects for new constructions in the province, which included the connection of the area where Lake Seliger is located, with Ilmen and the Baltic.

The beauty and uniqueness of the region attracted many famous scientists. In 1814, the first Russian geographer N. Ya. Ozeretskovsky visited here. Later he wrote a book that arouses considerable interest among readers and today - "Journey to Lake Seliger."

The first scientific study of the Valdai Upland, where Lake Seliger is located, in 1894-1895. was carried out by D. N. Anuchin, who is considered the father of Russian lake science (limnology). He did a tremendous job. The widths and depths of the local rivers and lakes were measured, and the water temperature and flow velocity were determined. Seliger Attractions



Many natural and historical sights will be discovered during trips around the edge of Lake Seliger. Photos representing the area clearly demonstrate the harmonious combination of architectural works of ancient architects with nature of amazing beauty. On Stolobny Island in the middle of the lake is one of the main Christian shrines of the Nilov Desert. The existing monastery is open to pilgrims and tourists. Here they can see the embankment, Bishop's Quay, Holy Cross Exaltation Church, the oldest building on the island - the Church of All Saints, as well as visit the majestic Epiphany Cathedral and admire the magnificent panorama of the lake, its islands, temples along the banks from its bell tower.



Nilova Desert - a historically established center of the Orthodox faith, but there are others little-known historical monuments that Russia is proud of. Seliger Lake also opens such holy places as the Zhitenny Monastery and the Smolensky Cathedral located in the depths of the dense forest .

The source of the Volga river

The cradle of the great river has long been considered a holy place. Here, by decree of Alexei Mikhailovich, the Transfiguration of the Desert was opened in 1649. St. Nicholas and Transfiguration Churches, located at the source, are the basis of the revived Olginsky monastery.

Museums in the Seliger Region

In the village of Rogozh, there is the Museum of Nature of the Seliger Region, housed in the former Transfiguration Church. Here you can get acquainted with colorful dioramas of natural landscapes, animal bio-groups, the incredibly rich underwater world of the lake and rare, endangered species of animals.

Noteworthy is one of the rather distinctive cities of Russia - Ostashkov. The unique expositions of the local history museum will reveal the history of the Seliger region, introduce you to tools, cult and household items, crafts and crafts, works of art and other rare items from archaeological sites.

Seliger pyramid

On the lake, near Ostashkov, you can see one of the modern attractions of the region - a pyramid established in 1997 with a height of 22 meters. This architectural building by Alexander Golod, according to the author himself, is a source of ionizing radiation that can affect the thickness of the ozone layer of the atmosphere and activate the processes of biocenosis in the environment.
