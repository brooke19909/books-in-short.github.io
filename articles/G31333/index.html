About China

The life and culture of China is very different from Western civilization. In it, the magic of the East intertwined with the achievements of high technology, and all this gave rise to many amazing things that travelers should definitely see.

In China, there are many grandiose and attractive sights, among which there are many natural monuments and reserves. The article describes the most interesting natural attractions of China.

Shilin Park

This national pride of China is located 130 kilometers from the large city of Kunming. The park offers tourists to see the mysterious forests, grottoes, meadows and deep caves. Its territory, occupying about 350 square meters. square meters, divided into 7 sectors. The park in all seasons looks beautiful in its own way.

The local population often spends their leisure time staying in hotels, campsites and recreation centers. It offers vacationers various hiking trails. Torch festivals are organized annually on its territory.

Li River

The length of this amazingly clean river is 426 km. The shores of this natural attraction give inspiration to many artists and poets of modernity and past centuries.

During a trip around Li (Lijiang Province), you can see the Buffalo Gorge, the Yellow Cliff rocks, the Nine Horseshoe Mountains, Sinping City and the Raven Cave. The river waters are plowed by tourist and merchant ships. Sports rafting takes place here annually.

Rice terraces

Excursions in China cannot be imagined without visiting this almost man-made attraction. There are spacious rice terraces in Yunnan province, occupying 16 thousand hectares of land. They are built on the slopes in such a way that useful substances are not washed out of the soil by water.

This miracle was created by the Hani people almost 13 centuries ago. It looks especially impressive in the spring, representing a harmonious unity of natural fantasy and human thought.

Bouncing tiger

The gorge on the Yangtze River (the highest in Eurasia) is located in the canyon of the Sino-Tibetan mountains. In China, they like to give similar non-trivial names to sights. Seen from above, the gorge actually resembles a tiger stretched out in a jump. And according to one legend, it was so named thanks to the tiger, who managed to jump over it.

The gorge is located where mountain peaks reach a height of 2000 meters. The length of the "tiger" is 15 kilometers. The banks of the Yangtze River are inhabited by indigenous people. They hospitably and cordially welcome tourists.

It is impossible to imagine a trip to China without visiting this, without exaggeration, one of the most beautiful places in China, in Hangzhou (the capital of Zhejiang province). The dams and small islands of Lake Sihu are conditionally divided into 5 parts. Mountains with a beautiful emerald hue border the pond on the sides.

Reed Flute Cave

This is a beautiful place not only in China, but also on the entire planet. The cave is located in a rock surrounded by thickets of bamboo, used by the local population for the production of flutes. This place is associated with a fairy tale, and neon gentle lighting complements amazing landscapes.

Reed Flute Cave is tens of millions of years old. Once upon a time there was a natural reservoir in this place, but over time the rocks rose and formed a mysterious cave, which over time became overgrown with stalagmites and stalactites. By nature, amazingly beautiful gorges and grottoes were created in it, which received romantic names: Khrustalny Palace, Pine in the Snow, Dragon Tower, Dawn in the Lion Grove, etc. An additional impressive effect is given by the illumination of the halls in a variety of shades that are reflected in the lake located here.

There is one legend that says that once a poet came here for inspiration, but he was so fascinated by the beauty of the cave that he even stopped moving and turned into stone, which to this day stands in the reed flute cave. Opposite it is a white block, similar to a fabulous waterfall.

About the discovery of the cave

For the first time, people ended up in a cave in about 792. It was possible to find out thanks to the texts and drawings found on the walls of the cave. These inscriptions are made during the period of China's Tang Dynasty rule. It should be noted that time is considered the best for China, since in its development it was ahead of all countries.

Reed flute cave was discovered only in 1940, and since then it has attracted a large number of tourists.
