Park "Fairy Tale" in Domodedovo: description, opening hours, how to get, reviews

Recreation areas in Moscow are many. But the " Fairy Tale " park in Domodedovo compares favorably with the total mass of such places. Here is a collection of unique attractions, the creation of which is based on the motives of both Russian folk tales and modern foreign cartoons.





Fairy tale world

A hut on chicken legs, D Stepa from a work of the Soviet classic, a good dwarf from a foreign fairy tale - all this can be seen in a children's park located in the south of the Moscow region . Here, the magic characters, if they do not come to life, then become almost real. Three heroes and the Serpent Gorynych , Baba Yaga and Ivanushka the Fool - each adult in our country knows these heroes. Therefore, it will be interesting to visit the park not only for children, but also for their parents.

Features and mode of operation

It will be interesting to visit the Fairy Tale park for both preschool children and teenagers who, it would seem, are difficult to surprise with something. Various events, sports and educational games, creative master classes are regularly held here. As in any other park, the Tale has a Ferris wheel, as well as other popular attractions. Namely: a rope park, a contact zoo, multi-level climbing walls and other entertainments.

" Tale " in Domodedovo - a park created according to a new concept. In the design of this zone, everything was done so that young visitors had the opportunity to be in the fresh air as much as possible. A certain part of the rides varies depending on the time of year. Opening hours of the children's park " Fairy Tale " in Domodedovo :

  • Monday to Friday: from 09:00 to 21:00 ;
  • Saturday and Sunday: from 09:00 to 22:00.

A few years ago, access to the territory was free. Today you can no longer visit the " Fairy Tale " park in Domodedovo . For an adult, a ticket costs 100 rubles. For children, of course, discounts apply. The cost of a ticket for a child is 50 rubles.

According to reviews of the Tale Park (Domodedovo), children like it here. According to their parents, in the park, figures depicting fairy-tale characters are present in abundance. They are often installed quite randomly. Let's talk about the main attractions of the park.





Fairytale Gallery

Right at the entrance is a composition depicting heroes from Pushkin's poem Ruslan and Lyudmila. Next is a gallery of fairytale characters. It all starts, of course, with three heroes, without which no such amusement park can do. Near these figures, visitors are happy to take pictures. Children perceive them, as a rule, as static attractions. The children are trying to pile up on Alyosha Popovich‚Äôs horse, and meanwhile the parents capture their children next to the famous hero.

In parallel with the main gallery, where famous characters are represented, there is a small pavilion of curved mirrors. Everywhere there are small fountains; there are also several ponds in the park. Fairy-tale characters are placed throughout the territory. As one of the reviewers said, the organizers probably set out to fill every square meter of the park.

Cafe

In the children's park " Fairy Tale " opened several small cafes. The interior of these establishments is fully consistent with the general mood. These are small buildings, stylized either as a fairy hut or as a small peasant's house. A cafe called " At the forest lake " is more like a hut.

The so-called peasant courtyard, decorated with huge artificial fruits and vegetables, deserves attention. There is also a small fenced area designed for the youngest visitors. Work in the park is still underway, perhaps by the summer of 2018 there will be new attractions. But as already mentioned, everything has been created here so that children can spend time in this recreation area both in winter and in autumn.





Museum of fairy tales and a shoe

The most interesting figures are concentrated here. This open-air museum has a large number of heroes from all the famous Russian folk tales. But you should pay attention to how they are made. This is the result of quite painstaking work. Each exhibit is painted manually.

Society here has gathered international. Along with Masha and the Bear, the Serpent Gorynych and Baba Yaga there is a Swede Carlson and Thumbelina - the heroine of a Danish storyteller. Here you can see the house in which the little man lived, who loved jam above all. And there is also a pumpkin, which at any moment can turn into a carriage. The cost of a ticket for an adult is 150 rubles. For a child - 100 rubles.

Husky Land

This part of the park is an ethnocultural complex that allows children and their parents to get acquainted with the traditions of the peoples of the Far North. In " Husky Land" children have the opportunity to feel like residents of a real northern village, to feel the multifaceted culture of distant peoples. A person, having first heard the name of this complex, will think that this is something related to cynology. And not be mistaken . Professional dog handlers really work on the territory who teach visitors the basics of training.

Theater of children's quests

This complex should certainly be visited by all those who are interested in magic. This is a small building, stylized as an old castle. And it is inhabited by princesses, knights and wizards. Of course, not real. But in children, as you know, imagination works well. They, unlike adults, know how to believe in a fairy tale.

In the park you can make a real trip to the enchanted world. And this is not only entertainment, but also a great opportunity to develop memory, logic, broaden your horizons. In this part of the fairy-tale park, professional actors work, able to hold the attention of small spectators for a long time. The small performances that are shown here are interactive. That is, small viewers can not only observe the events in the life of the characters, but also take part in them.

The building has several floors, many rooms. For the most part, the atmosphere is fabulous and mysterious. But there are rooms that will be of interest primarily to girls. In small rooms, in the interior of which pink shades prevail, real princesses live.

It is easy to guess that a huge number of employees work here. Each of them only plays the role of a fairy-tale character, in reality, he is an ordinary person who wants to get paid on time. Therefore, a visit to the castle should be paid. The cost of an interactive program, which lasts only 45 minutes, is 1 thousand rubles at the weekend. On weekdays, cheaper - 750 rubles.





Clownery

This word was created by the authors of an unusual project. Fish live in the aquarium. Dolphins have dolphins . And in the clownery you can meet a huge number of characters that cause a smile. Here are wax figures, and real, living heroes of fairy tales. But fairy tales are no longer of those Russian folk, in which the hut on chicken legs is invariably present, but foreign. These are Arlekino , Pierrot and nobody known Crystalin. What kind of character is this? What cartoon is it from?





Kristallion is also an invention of the creators of the Clownarium project. The hero who bears this strange name, once upon a time, back in the 19th century, went on a long journey. He has visited many cities and countries. And in every locality, he certainly visited the local children's theater or circus. He collected the most interesting around the world. This continued until Kristallion decided to settle in a children's park near Moscow.





How to get to the Skazka park in Domodedovo ? It is located 15 km from the Moscow Ring Road . From the metro station " Domodedovo " can be reached by bus. It is necessary to get out of the metro from the first car from the center, then turn right from the glass doors twice. At the entrance there is a bus stop number 505, where you need to get to " Summer resident " .
