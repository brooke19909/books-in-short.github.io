An airport

As a rule, guests begin their acquaintance with the city from the only airport located here. "Araxos" is located near the cape of the same name and serves the city of Patras and the western part of the island of Peloponnese. The airport was built in 1941 as a military airfield.

In 1980, it became the country's civilian airport. Although he did not lose his military mission today. The Greek Air Force uses Araxos as an active air base. Patras Airport (Greece) "Araxos" is located 29 km from the city. The planes of three airlines arrive here. Throughput - more than 133 thousand passengers per season. Technical characteristics of "Araxos" allow you to take any kind of aircraft.

St. Andrew's Cathedral is the largest church in the country and the third largest in the Balkan Peninsula. Andrew the First-Called is the patron saint of the city. Here the apostle lived the last years and received a terrible death. The temple was erected on the spot where, according to legend, the apostle Andrew was crucified.



The construction of the cathedral was started in 1908 according to the project of the famous architect from Greece Anastasios Metaksas. He also supervised the progress of construction work. After his death in 1937, the architect Nomikos led the work. In total, construction lasted 66 years, and only in 1974 did the discovery take place that they had been waiting for so long.

A magnificent building built in the Byzantine style. The main dome of the temple is crowned with a five-meter gilded cross. Along the perimeter, it is surrounded by twelve small domes with crosses. This composition symbolizes Jesus Christ and the twelve apostles.

Rich in ancient buildings, Greece. Patras are no exception. The ancient city fortress is one of the most popular and visited attractions, which attracts thousands of history buffs every year.

The construction of this large defensive facility, located on a low hill, began at the end of the VI century a few hundred meters from the sea. Before this place was located the ancient acropolis destroyed by a powerful earthquake.

Ancient Roman Odeon

For many of our compatriots, Greece has become a favorite vacation spot. Patras are especially attractive for tourists because they can combine beach and sightseeing holidays. In this city there are many very interesting places that you should visit in between relaxing on the beach.

The odeon was built in the city in 160 AD. e. It was the heyday of the Roman Empire. Over time, wars and earthquakes buried the once magnificent majestic structure, forming a hill in its place. At the end of the XIX century during the construction of the dam there was an acute shortage of land for the embankment. In this regard, it was decided to take the land from the hill. What was the surprise of the Greeks when, under a layer of earth, they discovered the ruins of an ancient Roman odeon, moreover, well-preserved.



Restoration work was begun, and the entire territory of the odeon, together with the lands adjacent to it, was declared the most important archaeological site. As a result, unique Roman mosaics and other priceless artifacts were discovered. The work was completed in 1956. Greek specialists managed to recreate a unique structure almost in its original form.

Today in the summer, odeon is used as an open-air theater. Concerts are held here, theatrical productions are staged. The odeon is famous for its amazing acoustics. Its capacity is more than 2000 people.

It connects the Peloponnese and mainland Greece. This is, without exaggeration, an impressive and grandiose masterpiece of modern engineering.
