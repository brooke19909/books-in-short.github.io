A Brief Biography of Stephen Hawking

Hawking was born in the UK in a family of doctors. His father was a medical scientist, and his mother worked as a secretary in the same organization. The scientist's childhood passed in the company of two sisters, as well as the adopted son of the Hawking - Edward. Upon graduation from school, Stephen became a student at Oxford, and after graduation he became one of the Ph.D. students at Cambridge University. Hawking has two children from his first wife, and in 1995 he married his nurse. In 2006, Hawking divorced for the second time.

One of Hawking's scientific disputes

But, despite the huge discoveries, sometimes the researcher still lost the argument to his colleagues. Professor Stephen Hawking's mistake was made by him in a dispute with John Philip Preskill. This debate actually triggered one of the most revolutionary studies that Hawking subsequently conducted. The argument itself was as follows.

John Preskill believed that the waves that come from black holes contain some information that cannot be deciphered for modern science. Hawking, however, was an opponent, not accepting this point of view - as an argument, he cited experimental data obtained in 1975. The researcher believed that information cannot be decoded only for the reason that it enters the parallel Universe.

Scientist fears about the future of humanity

The achievements of the scientist in the field of astrophysics research today are indisputable. Many even believe that Stephen Hawking is Newton of the 20th century. However, he shares his concerns about the future of humanity with representatives of other areas of knowledge - political scientists, economists, and sociologists. For example, Hawking is seriously worried about the possibility of a large percentage of unsecured people simply earning a living. Indeed, modern technologies have led to an ambiguous situation: manual and low-skilled labor is becoming more and more automated. As a result, good earnings are only available to the middle and creative class.
