The best beaches of Rio de Janeiro: overview, description and reviews

Rio de Janeiro and the beach are almost synonymous. This is a beautiful, unique city that combines everything that a person needs for relaxation, work and sports. Therefore, millions of people come here every year for entertainment and business. The beaches of Rio de Janeiro are one of the main attractions of the city. There are so many of them that choosing the right one is not easy.





Geography

Not far from the Tropic of Capricorn, along the line of the Atlantic coast, the largest city of Brazil, Rio de Janeiro, stretches out. The settlement was founded in Guanabar Bay, on the plains western coast. The landscape of the city is formed by branches of the mountain system of Serra do Mar. On the one hand, the settlement is limited by the waters of the bay, on the other - by hills and mountain peaks. The beaches of Rio de Janeiro stretch for 30 km. This is the second longest coastline in the city within Brazil. Officially, the city is divided into 34 administrative units, but residents are used to breaking it into four parts: North, South, West and the Historical Center. In the south and west are concentrated beaches of the city, which have their own characteristics.

Climate

Brazil is in the subequatorial climate zone, the average annual temperature in Rio de Janeiro is +23 degrees. It has hot summers and warm winters. Precipitation is distributed evenly throughout the seasons, but July and August are more arid than the other months. The hottest are January and February. The beaches of Rio de Janeiro (Brazil) are attractive because of the comfortable weather here during all seasons. But the highest season in Rio lasts from November to March.





Beach Features

First of all, the beaches of Rio de Janeiro are famous for their high activity of beach life. You can really spend a lot of time here. Festivals, holidays are held on the beaches, there are many cafes, playgrounds and sports grounds, attractions. Along the beaches lined with hotels, casinos, shops. The second feature of the beaches of Rio is a strong current off the coast, in some places it is even dangerous. Part of the beaches is a real paradise for surfing. For Brazilians, the beach is a real place of residence, so the specificity of some places is that there are a lot of people of all kinds. Most often, unemployed or one-time earners can afford to spend a lot of time ashore. Such a number of people leads to high pollution in some areas and to rather high risks of collision with fraudsters and thieves. Therefore, choosing the beaches of Rio de Janeiro and the hotel, you need to understand well how different places in the city differ from each other.





Copacabana

Of course, the most famous beach of Rio de Janeiro is Copacabana. At the foot of the famous mountain, nicknamed Sugarloaf, the oldest and most famous beach in all of Brazil stretches for 5 km. Here in the mornings local people gather who sunbathe, play volleyball, listen to music, spend time in a cafe. There are also a lot of tourists who consider it their duty to visit this attraction. The eastern part of Aproador beach is extremely popular with surf lovers. There is a long tradition of observing from the beach of Copacabana the sun setting in the ocean. After it gets dark, life on the beach becomes even richer, there are many bars, discos, clubs. On weekends, the paved promenade along the beach becomes a pedestrian, and lovers of jogging, bicycles and walks gather here. Every day from 6 p.m. local artists and craftsmen organize a fair of their creations here. Along the promenade are luxurious hotels, the most famous of which is the Copacabana Palace with an elegant restaurant.





Ipanema

Today, the question of what is the name of the famous Rio de Janeiro beach is increasingly heard in response: Ipanema. This is one of the most prestigious places in the city. Geographically, the beach is a continuation of Copacabana. A song about a girl from Ipanema added fame to this place. This beach is a more upscale analogue of the famous Copacabana. In the shadow of the Two Brothers Mountain, the most important part of the beach with white sand, luxurious restaurants and cocktail bars, fashion boutiques, chic galleries and museums is located. Here you can often see celebrities, come here in swimsuits from the best designers in the world. The public on Ipanema is more decent than on neighboring Copacabana, and the level of security is higher.

Urka

The most calm course is on Urka beach, swimming lovers come here. The beach is located at the Sugarloaf itself, on the background of which perfect photos are obtained. Near Urca are the most inexpensive and decent hotels in Rio de Janeiro. Local schoolchildren and students can often be seen on the beach, and a university campus is nearby. The disadvantage of Urka beach is its high pollution. Relatively stagnant water does not take garbage from the shore and therefore swimming here can not always be pleasant; you need to swim away to overcome the dirty lane. The surface of the beach, too, despite cleaning, can be very dirty in the evening. But on Urka wonderful views of the city and the ocean. From the restaurant on Sugarloaf you get great view photos.

Barra da Tijuca

This beach is a real mecca for surfers. There are magnificent waves, and therefore people with boards spend off the coast of Barra da Tijuca almost round the clock. Several schools are open for beginners; on the beach, you can rent any surf equipment. This is the longest beach in Rio de Janeiro, its length is 18 km. Therefore, there is enough space not only for surfers. A big disadvantage of the beach is its proximity to the favelas (poor areas), whose inhabitants also like to spend time in this place. But its length allows you to find a safe and suitable place to relax. In 2016, it was Barra da Tijuca that became the venue for the Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro.





Flamengo

The most authentic view of Brazil is preserved on Flamengo beach. In a cozy bay, traditional nature and buildings have been preserved. Lovers of familiar beach activities come here, they constantly grill barbecue, build sand castles and even hold competitions, play beach volleyball. Flamengo offers the best views of the city of Rio de Janeiro. Here you can find many locals who indulge in their favorite pursuits: sunbathe, fish, enjoy life. Due to the not very high wave, garbage can accumulate at the coastline, and this is the only drawback of this beach.

Tourists reviews

Along with other attractions, the beaches brought Rio de Janeiro the glory of a city that knows how to please its guests.

Travelers who have been to Rio de Janeiro consider themselves lucky. Their reviews are almost always overwhelmed with positive emotions and admiration. We mentioned all the positive points above.

However, tourists say that local recreation has its drawbacks. This is a large amount of garbage in the water and on the shore, and a high risk of being robbed. Teenagers are constantly snooping around the beaches, as well as suspicious people loitering around. In the evening, loud speech, swearing is heard on many beaches, even fights happen. Therefore, tourists recommend choosing beaches and hotels in respectable places. Let the rest come out more expensive, but safer.
