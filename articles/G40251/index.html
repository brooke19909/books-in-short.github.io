HDR

This option needs to be described in more detail. HDR can safely be called a step in the development of TV technology, following Ultra HD, which, it would seem, has conquered everyone recently.

This is a completely new video format. It contains both ultra-high resolution and maximum color and brightness information. It is important to note. that such functions do not work for the overall picture, but for each pixel separately.

Thanks to this technology, which is also used in the OLED55B7V, it was possible to transfer a much more realistic image to the screen, since HDR is what removes a lot of restrictions that other TVs have.

Some do not understand why this technology is needed. In simple terms, home versions of cult films will no longer lose in quality. It will remain at the studio level, at which it was originally.

The features of the OLED55B7V TV acquired with HDR are impressive:

  • The display shows complex scenes where objects are much brighter than on ordinary screens.
  • The wider the palette, the higher the detail. Little things become noticeable even in the lightest and darkest corners of the image.
  • Colors look many times more saturated. This is not surprising, because HDR uses a special color space known as Rec. 2020. It significantly exceeds the standard palette in depth of shades.

Model OLED55B7V boasts the presence of Dolby Vision. But this is an advanced version of HDR! It is used by such world film studios as XXI Century Fox, Warner Brothers, Walt Disney Studios, Universal, etc. The TV correctly supports Dolby Vision, because it is equipped with a special hardware chip.
