Square motifs with a simple pattern.

Let's look at a simple crochet square motif. The scheme is decrypted easily. In the picture you see that you need to start knitting with a ring. It is knitted from eight air loops. Next, the circle is tied with crochets. So that the square has a proportional shape, each row must begin with a lifting loop. The third row consists of alternating a single crochet with five air stitches.





From the fourth row, flower petals will form. Each leaf is knitted from a set of columns: one half column, one column with one crochet, two columns with two double crochets, an air loop, two columns with two double crochets, one column with one double crochet and one half column. Each petal closes with a single crochet. It turns out a clear flower. Now the motive must be enclosed in a square. To do this, from the top of one petal we collect seven air loops, connect to the new top of another petal using a single crochet. Corner pieces suggest nine air loops. The resulting row is tied with crochets. The next one is done in the same way. We knit the last row, alternating a column with one crochet and one air loop.
