How to expand solitaire "Kerchief" of 36 cards

Game rules (36 cards), how to lay out the Solitaire Solitaire:

How to decompose Spider Solitaire into 36 cards

Spider Solitaire rarely uses a playing deck. It can be laid out with one, two or four suits. For the game, take 2 or 4 decks at once, depending on the desired difficulty.





Step-by-step instructions for 4 decks of 36 cards, how to expand Spider Solitaire:

  1. Put cards in 6 pieces in the first 4 rows and 5 pieces in the rest. In total, there should be 54 cards on the playing table.
  2. Put the last row face up - these are the active cards.
  3. The remaining 80 cards can be folded with a deck - a coupon, or can be laid out aside in 8 lines with 10 cards face down.
  4. Cards are laid out on top of each other in order from oldest to youngest of the same suit. Ace is considered the youngest card!
  5. If there are no more options for moving cards, you need to take one of the delayed lines and put one card face up on each row in the game.
  6. In place of an empty column, you can move any card or a continuous combination of them, starting with the oldest.
  7. If in a column it turns out to collect a sequence from a king to an ace of the same suit, such a combination is removed in a pile away from the alignment. The goal of the game is to free the field from cards.

You can play with fewer decks, then the number of rows must also be reduced. So, for 3 decks (36 cards) you need to make 8 columns, one half of which is 7 cards and the second - 6 pieces. Playing fewer decks is not so interesting.
