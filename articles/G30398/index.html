The Mouth of Truth in Rome: photo, description, history, how to get?

The amazing city, nicknamed the eternal, is the most legendary place on our planet. The cradle of ancient civilization really justifies its nickname. It seems that Rome is a separate state that allows you to find out how the ancestors of modern Italians lived centuries ago. In this open-air museum, an incredible number of historical monuments are concentrated, harmoniously blending into the frenzied rhythm of the capital of Italy.

Amazing artifact

A curious attraction, near which tourists are always crowded, is a round-shaped marble slab with a bas-relief depicting an ancient deity with an open mouth. The mouth of the Truth in Rome, shrouded in legends, is in the small church of Santa Maria in Cosmedin on the left bank of the Tiber. Previously, the little-known temple was famous for its unique find installed in the wall of the portico.

Several versions of scientists

A marble disk, reaching a diameter of two meters and weighing more than a ton, appeared around the 4th century BC, but to this day archaeologists do not represent its true purpose. Some experts put forward a version according to which it is not a stone mask, but a sewer hatch. Others claim that the size of the slab is too large, and most likely the decorative stone was once the decoration of a luxurious fountain. But not one of the scientists can tell how the stone bas-relief appeared in the church.

The oldest "lie detector"

If earlier the marble disk performed practical functions, then during the Middle Ages, set upright, it sharply acquired mystical properties and became an important element of the trial. In one of the 12th century ancient manuscripts, the Mouth of Truth in Rome is already referred to as a ‚Äúlie detector‚Äù that you cannot fool. And after two centuries, the modern name is firmly entrenched in it.

Who is depicted on a huge mask of light marble?

According to one version, this is the ancient god Triton, who commanded the depths of the sea. However, art critics believe that, in fact, the gloomy face of Neptune appears on the disc, personifying one of the elements of the world - water. Other sources mention the gods Mercury and Tiberin. Some experts claim that the image carved on a marble mask is a collective image of a faun or oracle.

To establish who exactly the ancient Romans depicted is now impossible. But the inhabitants of medieval Rome believed that the plate depicts Erkol the Winner - a mythical character who could not stand the lie and cut off the hands of all who are soul-breaking.

Marble slab comes to justice

Bocca della Verita - this is the name of an interesting attraction in Italian. The mysterious Mouth of Truth in Rome, according to most experts, is the oldest "lie detector". The tiny church, built by the Romans, was always located near the port market Bull Forum. In ancient times, as well as now, there have always been dishonest people who tried to deceive the buyer or, conversely, steal goods from the seller.

When the scammers were caught, but it was not possible to prove their guilt, a marble slab came to the rescue. The accused, who put his hand into the open mouth of the mystic deity, had to loudly declare his innocence in the crime. It was believed that the stone mouth will bite off the upper limb if a person lies. Thieves immediately confessed to the crime, fearing to lose a hand. And the criminals, who did not believe in the seriousness of what was happening, took out a bloody stump. But most often, the fear of the sinister mask was so great that the accused confessed to all sins on the way to it.

Demonstration Executions

True, in historical chronicles there is a mention of the executioner, who stood unnoticed behind the marble artifact and mercilessly chopped off the hands of liars on orders. Therefore, it is not surprising that the fame of the Mouth of Truth in Rome spread very quickly throughout the city. To intimidate all those who cheated, the authorities resorted to demonstrative executions. In addition, men brought their wives here, suspecting that their spouses were unfaithful to them.
