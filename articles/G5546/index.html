How to finish crocheting? Unique products with simple skills

The number of crochet fans is growing every year. And no wonder! Indeed, in a very short time, using such a simple, at first glance, tool, you can create a lot of beautiful and original products.



There is plenty of information on this topic. Various knitting magazines feature many articles and mini-courses that describe crochet in the smallest detail, with patterns and tips for beginners. To master this craft is not difficult at all.

Where to start and how to end?

Crochet always starts with a chain of air loops. A loop is formed on the free end of the thread. Then a hook is threaded into it, a thread is thrown over it, it is grabbed and pulled back. We got a small chain of two air loops. All subsequent ones are formed in the same way. The base of knitting is ready. Next, continue knitting according to the pattern.

Virtually no explanation is required for the answer to the question of how to finish. Crocheting compares favorably with knitting needles in that at any moment of the process, only one loop remains free. This allows you to not stray and not lose the loop, even for beginners. To close the loop, you need to cut the thread coming from the ball, fully pull through the last loop and tighten. The remaining "tail" should be carefully hidden. That's all the details on how to finish.

Crochet - Bars

The basis of the fabric for crocheting is a column. It can be knitted in three basic versions: single crochet, double crochet and half-stitch.

  • Single crochet. We leave two lifting air loops, insert the hook into the third (from front to back), grab a free thread with it and pull it through the loops. Two loops formed on the hook. In one motion we stretch a free thread through them. The column is ready.

  • Half Column. As in the previous version, we leave two lifting loops. But now we grab the free thread and pull it through all the available loops at once.

  • Double crochet. This species is used to increase the height of the row. We leave three lifting loops (since the column is higher), we put the thread on the hook, insert it into the fourth loop, grab the free thread and stretch it through the two extreme loops on the hook. Repeat the operation several times.

Column one, different ways

Using only these three basic elements, but knitting them in different ways, you can get many different patterns



  • The columns are knitted for the back half-loop of the previous row, while the canvas turns out to be voluminous, with convex and concave rows.

  • If you knit the columns for the front loop, the fabric remains smooth, with flat horizontal stripes.

  • You can also untie the columns for the wrong loops: we introduce the hook for the horizontal jumper located slightly below the top of the previous row. With this method, a pigtail is formed on the inside.

In addition to these methods, it is possible to untie the columns of splinter, from the lower rows, on air loops. Deciding on the most suitable option is quite difficult, especially for those who have just started to master crochet. Schemes and description will always tell in detail all the nuances of manufacturing a particular product.
