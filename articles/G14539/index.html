Troitskaya Fortress: where is it located?

At Cape Taganiy Rog once stood an impregnable and unshakable Trinity Fortress. Taganrog traces its history precisely from the moment of construction of this fortification, which began in 1699 and lasted as long as ten years. A great stronghold has seen a lot in its lifetime, but, unfortunately, only a small part of this ancient building has survived to this day.

Foundation History

In the summer of 1696, when Azov was taken by Tsar Peter I and his mighty army, the sovereign went to these places to pick up a suitable place where you can build a fortress and build a harbor to thoroughly gain a foothold in the newly conquered lands. He liked the Taganiy cape. In the same year, he decides that there should be a fortification.



The fortification was entrusted to the construction of the Austrian engineer and architect Antonio de Laval, who, having arrived at these places, decided to begin construction a few kilometers from Taganrog, and not on the cape, which the king liked. Thanks to this, the Pavlovsk fortress appeared. The Trinity stronghold began to settle down only a year later, after de Leval was arrested and taken to Moscow for interrogation.

The new project was developed by a talented engineer and urban planner von Borgsdorf. All construction work was completed in 1709. The church located on the territory of this building was consecrated in the name of the Holy Life-Giving Trinity. In honor of her, the fortress got its name. Trinity - so they began to call her.

What kind of stronghold was it?

Thus, in just one decade, a real stone city was erected on the cape, blown from all sides by Taganyem winds , which was the first Russian naval base. The construction itself had an interesting shape. The diagram shows that the Trinity Fortress (Taganrog) was built in the form of a pentagon. The photographs depicting the maps of the stronghold that have come down to us since then show that it was surrounded by a large earthen rampart, which had a length of more than three kilometers. There was no special strengthening from the sea side, since the entire stake in the construction was made on the high and steep banks of the cape. On the territory of the fortification there were also a bazaar, a sovereign's yard, a dwelling for ordinary people and a temple. In the center of all buildings was a large area.





The destruction and revival of the fortress

Unfortunately, the further history of this stronghold is as sad as that of many similar fortifications of that time. Troitskaya fortress was demolished to the ground in accordance with the signed agreement between Peter I and the Ottoman Empire. A year later, the last Russian guard left this building. And the Turkish army completely occupied this territory, having plundered everything that remained here.

Then the next quarter century, the Azov lands were ruled by the Turks. Only during the next hostilities in 1736, Azov and Taganrog were again returned to Russia. But the revival of the stronghold became possible only in 1770 - in the era of Catherine the Great. Troitskaya fortress was quickly restored on the remains of the old foundation, and the base of the Azov Flotilla settled in the harbor.





Decline

But after the annexation of the Crimean Peninsula to the Russian Empire, this fortification lost its original purpose. All ships from the port were transported to Sevastopol, and the stronghold was abolished by order of the Russian empress. In 1803, a battalion from Rostov arrived in Taganrog . Therefore, the construction of new buildings for the military began on the territory.
