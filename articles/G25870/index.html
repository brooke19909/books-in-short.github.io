How to crochet rugs from bags

However, their implementation does not require a lot of money. Moreover, you can successfully use the old plastic bags, creating a spectacular and unusual little thing that will certainly decorate any room.

Yarn preparation

Before you start crocheting rugs from bags, you need to prepare cellophane. To do this, we straighten and put several packages in a small pile. We press with a ruler, stepping back from the edge about 4 cm. Experienced craftsmen say that the thicker the bag, the narrower the strip should be. That is, the ruler should be closer to the edge. It is also important to try to pick up packages of the same thickness so that the product turns out to be smooth and accurate. Having measured the required width, we draw a knife along the packet, cutting off the strip. We ‚Äúcut‚Äù the whole package in this way. Then we connect the prepared "rings" into a single "thread". Which we will use to crochet rugs from bags. This is easy enough to do. We take two prepared strips, unfold. We wrap one around the other and thread the tip into the resulting loop. Tighten. Thus we connect the edges of all ribbons. And only then we wind the resulting ‚Äúthread‚Äù into a skein, while slightly twisting it.

How to knit a square rug

Experienced craftsmen note that polyethylene "yarn" is used in the same way as woolen. Therefore, you can safely embody various schemes you like. However, novice needlewomen should still wait a while with the implementation of a complex product. It is better to start with a simple technology, and having mastered it, continue self-improvement. Experts agree that crochet square rugs from packages look most interesting. They are easy to perform, so we offer readers to start with just such a product. For its implementation, it is necessary to prepare a hook and a skein of "yarn". After that, make simple manipulations:

  1. Tie a chain of six loops and close into a ring.
  2. Then knit twelve single crochet stitches. Three pieces on each side of the square.
  3. Between them you need to knit three air loops.
  4. Then we continue to knit triple crochets, and between them - three air loops. In the corners we knit 6 single crochet stitches, making three air stitches between them.
  5. We continue this until we reach the desired size of the square. In this case, you can use various shades of "yarn" and color combinations. Thus, you can crochet a rug from garbage bags or old colored ones.

Make a round rug

Novice masters in various reviews and comments write that round rugs are most easily performed. Because they do not need to comply with technology. It is enough just to add loops in time, forming an even circle. However, experienced craftsmen say that a round rug can start the same way as a square rug. Although it‚Äôs better to do it right. To do this, take a plastic "yarn" and wrap it on the index finger of the left hand. Then carefully remove the loop, tie and tighten, pulling on the initial tip. As a result of the described actions, a middle without a hole is obtained. And the product looks much more interesting. Further crocheting the rug from garbage bags or any other is very simple. The needlewoman knits single crochet or with one crochet from each loop of the previous row. And in order to get a beautiful circle, he lifts two new ones from one loop. This version of the rug is noteworthy in that it is carried out at the request of the needlewoman.

Cooking mat from plastic pompons

The implementation of this original and simple piece of furniture can be done by the whole family. It is also worth noting that this is the only model that does not require knowledge of knitting technology. However, it requires a different set of materials and tools. First of all, it is a skein of polyethylene "yarn". And also scissors, a needle with a thread, cardboard, a mosquito net. Having completed the preparatory phase, we proceed to the creative process:

  1. We cut the cardboard into several identical patterns - strips 3-4 cm wide.
  2. After doing the pompons. We wrap the polyethylene "yarn" onto the template, carefully remove it, knit it in the middle and cut the ends.
  3. Sew multi-colored pompons to the mosquito net, trying to arrange them closer to each other. This will allow you to get the original "fluffy" rug.
