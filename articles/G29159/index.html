DIY dinosaur costume: interesting ideas

Dinosaur costume will appeal to children of any age. It can be worn for the New Year or children's carnival. It is suitable for children who participate in performances and performances. For sewing, you can use fabric, paper or ready-made clothes.

DIY dinosaur costume: master class

Even a mother who does not have sewing skills can easily sew such a model. It will take only one evening to make the costume.

Necessary tools and materials:

  • pants or sweatshirt in green or black;
  • paper for a pattern;
  • a simple pencil;
  • scissors;
  • sewing machine;
  • fabric paint;
  • green felt.




Work Stages:

  1. Pants and a sweatshirt are the basis of a children's dinosaur costume. ‚ÄúCloves‚Äù and decor elements will be attached to it. It is enough to wash and iron clothes.
  2. "Cogs" are created as follows. Draw a template on paper with a pencil and a ruler. Transfer the pattern to the fabric, cut it out. Process the workpiece on a sewing machine. If it is not at hand, you can use a regular needle and thread, but it will take more time. Keep in mind that the workpiece should be 2. One will be fixed on the trousers, and the second - on the jacket with a hood.
  3. Sew the "teeth" to the base. The lines should be even and neat, as they will be visible from the outside.
  4. Decorate a dinosaur costume to your liking. The easiest option is to make spots using fabric dyes. You can also decorate with sequins.

Tail

For a masquerade or children's play, it is not necessary to sew a jumpsuit or a dinosaur costume. Enough to do with the tail.

Necessary tools and materials:

  • a piece of green fabric;
  • filler;
  • lining fabric;
  • contrasting felt for creating spikes;
  • Velcro
  • scissors;
  • needle with thread or sewing machine.




Work Stages:

  1. Lay the main fabric on a flat surface, cut a cone-shaped piece from it.
  2. Making spikes. From the felt, cut a strip of spikes. The edges can be left untreated because the fabric is strong enough. The spikes should be of different sizes. At the top, place the largest, and at the bottom the smallest.
  3. Attach the largest spike to the top of the tail, trim the edges. Sew a strip of lining. Connect with the second part of the tail. A strip of lining should remain in the center. Sew it to the other side, connecting both parts of the tail.
  4. Cut 4 strips from the fabric. This is the basis for belts with which the tail will be fixed at the waist. Sew 2 strips. You will get 2 belts. Sew one edge and leave the other free. Fill the belts with filler.
  5. Connect the wide part of the tail with the free edges of the straps, sew.
  6. Fill the tail with filler, sew the free edge.
  7. Attach the Velcro to the straps so that they can be fixed at the waist.

DIY dinosaur mask

If the New Year is just around the corner, and you don‚Äôt have time to make a costume, make a carnival mask. To create you will need:

  • White paper;
  • cardboard;
  • colored paper;
  • pencil;
  • scissors;
  • glue;
  • color gum;
  • New Year's "rain".

Work Stages:

  1. On a sheet of cardboard draw a muzzle of a dinosaur. Be sure to mark the slots for the eyes. If you do not have artistic abilities, use ready-made templates. Cut the workpiece along the contour, and also make cuts for the eyes.
  2. Glue the cardboard blank onto green paper, cut it out.
  3. Decorate the eye area with yellow stripes and eyelashes.
  4. Decorate the mask along the contour with rain.
  5. Make 2 holes, stretch the elastic.




The mask will go well with a balaclava hat decorated with spikes of dense yellow fabric. If you want it to be durable, use felt or other materials that hold well in shape to create.
