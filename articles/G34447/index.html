How to repair a fur coat from natural fur yourself?

Mink coat - a landmark in the wardrobe, which, perhaps, every woman wants to have. Natural fur products have always been appreciated and were in fashion. The more expensive the thing, the more it needs to be preserved. However, there are cases that are impossible to predict. Even with careful wearing of natural fur products, incidents happen that lead to the need to repair fur coats.



There are various damages, and it is not always possible to eliminate the defect yourself. In this case, you will have to contact the studio for qualified help. But having certain knowledge, you can cope with small problems by repairing the product at home.

Main problems

Repair of mink coats must be carried out extremely delicately so as not to spoil the fur and appearance of the product. In this article, we will consider tips for solving the main problems that occur during the repair process.

It is quite possible to restore the coat to its former appearance on its own, but this is if the damage is minor, in more difficult situations it should be attributed to specialists.

The main problems requiring repair of a fur coat that any woman may encounter are:

  • Divergence or rupture of the dorsal suture of the product.
  • Small holes under the arm.
  • Rupture of a sleeve seam.
  • Seam rupture on the collar area.




Tips & Tricks

The places where the mink coat is most often torn are the back seam and sleeves. This is the most difficult option for finishing work. If the dorsal suture has opened, you need to gently tear off the lined insulation to get to the gap. Next, when repairing a fur coat, you need to prepare and apply a patch on the gap. For these purposes, do not use scissors that will only spoil the fleecy part of the patch. The patch is installed in place of the gap and sewn in the usual line, the skin and fur do not need to be pulled. You can use a wooden mallet if you need to achieve even edges after flashing.

To eliminate the defect of the dorsal suture, you should use a thick needle, since the thickness of the fur in this place is slightly larger than in other places. Sewing is carried out in the direction of the fleecy part of the product.

When repairing a mink coat, one more problem can be encountered - sleeve repair. To sew it up, a difficult task remains to be done - to tear off the side stitch and lining material. The armhole is cut off so that you can see the real size of the gap and its edge. If the fur exploded evenly, it will not be difficult to sew it up on your own, making a patch that is suitable in size.





Bridging small gaps

The sleeve area is difficult to repair, it is complicated by the fact that it is necessary to choose high-quality fur for insertion and carefully sew it on. You can buy it in the studio, but if you plan to remodel your fur coat, as an alternative, use pieces of the remaining fur for repair. If the hole under the armpit is small, you can use special glue and glue the gap. For these purposes, rubber glue, which includes rubber, is ideal. Torn edges are pulled together using thread and a needle, after which a piece of gauze is glued to the place of the gap. This is a reliable way to fix the seam without damaging the appearance of the fur. If the fur coat is repaired correctly, that is, the edges were perfectly fitted end-to-end, the product will look without visual defects.

Fur coat repair along the sleeve length is rarely performed due to rare cases of product rupture in these places. Basically, it breaks under the arm.

Minor repair of a fur coat at the seam

Valuable fur must be properly stored and inspected before purchase. Frequent cases of rupture along the seam should be stitched with thick thick threads, try to turn the fur pile outward so that the seam is not visible. Pass all stitching stitches with the needle by hand, and also to protrude over the edges in the future to prevent the seams from spreading.





When using a sewing machine, repairs are carried out with the line "goat", and the overlocking method of sewing is also well suited. Repair of a mink coat can be done with a contact tape for gluing the seam and at the end stitch with goat-type markings. This method is the most efficient and accurate. Keep in mind that the durability of a fur coat is affected by both the quality of the item being repaired and the stitching seam.
