Microloans. Example





We begin, of course, with the general question of what microcredit is. Surely many of us already understand the difference between the so-called "quick loan" from a simple consumer loan. But better we give an example that illustrates this category.

So, suppose we take a loan to buy a refrigerator. This is an amount equal to our three salaries, which we pay off gradually, giving back some of our earnings, say, during the year. This is a classic consumer loan, which is usually drawn up for some significant, large expenses.

Another example looks like this: we spent our money, although there are still 5 days left before the salary. Money is needed to go to the store and buy products for the arrival of guests. Of course, there is nothing left but to apply for a small loan. Having received a salary in less than a week, we will pay off this debt. This is how a microloan works, or, as it is commonly called, a quick loan.

Registration speed and low requirements

It is called ‚Äúfast‚Äù because of how simple the design procedure precedes it. Instead of collecting various certificates from the place of work and specifying specific information about your property (as in the case of consumer goods), in the case of microcredit, the user just needs to provide a passport. In some situations, only general information is required that you work and, thus, have a constant source of income. As deep as banks, micro-lending organizations do not dig.





The reason for such ‚Äúillegibility‚Äù of companies is that it is beneficial for them to give such loans. If the client returns the money on time, he already overpays a rather high percentage for using the funds (for example, 10% for 10 days is a normal rate). In another case, if the debtor does not return the money on time, he will be dragged by the courts and large financial losses. The conditions for providing microloans are such that the client will return many times more than he initially took. On this and earn such offices.

A financial instrument or slavery?

Actually, in our article on micro loans, this question is asked about what can be considered the activities of offices that initially set a very high percentage for their services.

On the one hand, you can understand the debtors who are faced with the problem of money. People taking a quick loan, and without that, as a rule, do not have significant financial resources. These are not rich representatives of the people, as a rule, living on the same salary (with rare exceptions). The very function of microloans is to help ‚Äúreach out‚Äù to the next payment.

Therefore, when a company asks such a person to pay 10% more, it already worsens his (already difficult) financial situation in order to make his profit. Realizing this, many customers are outraged, leaving negative comments about such offices.

Another issue is the profitability of the company organizing the issuance of micro-loans. Its profitability consists in receiving from each borrower the money that was provided to him at the beginning, increased at the expense of interest. And then, no one forces a person to get a loan under initially unfavorable conditions; and already signed under the contract - please, pay! Therefore, if you look from a practical point of view, the borrowers themselves are to blame for how companies (such as RosMoney) act with them (in case of non-payment). Feedback from debtors about it is sometimes also clearly negative, but "business is business" ...





How to manage a microloan?

Depending on what to do with the money borrowed, they can make you both a slave and their master. If you regularly live on such loans, then most likely you simply do not know how to organize your expenses. On the other hand, by issuing a microloan only in individual, emergency cases, you can save the situation. After that, of course, you need to pay off the debt as soon as possible so as not to incur even bigger problems.

Not everyone knows how to manage borrowed money. The reviews of the debtors describing the RosDengi company confirm this. For example, there are negative comments from those who delayed payment due to the weekend or simply forgot that the "day of reckoning" has arrived. Obviously, such borrowers themselves are guilty of their problems, and for them such a loan can really become slavery. This is not the case with more punctual and prudent clients who carefully monitor the debt repayment schedule.

Therefore, as a small summary for the first part of our article, we can say that debt slavery is not fearful to you if you can properly monitor, manage and manage your money.
