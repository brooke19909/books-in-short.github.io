The heart of the city

Located in the heart of the Rambla (Barcelona), whose photos often appear in foreign magazines, extends for 1.2 kilometers and consists of five boulevards that smoothly transition into each other and have different names. True, this division is purely arbitrary, and they speak about the street in the singular. Considered the soul of the city, it attracts millions of tourists, and locals regrettably go around it because of the huge flows of tourists. A wonderful corner that has not lost its charm for a long time helps to find inspiration and recharge with positive emotions.

The Rambla of the Flowers, or St. Joseph

The most beautiful section of the boulevard is famous for the numerous flower shops that appeared in the 19th century. According to an ancient legend, it was one of the most popular places in the city, as the lively saleswomen were famous for their beauty and sharp tongue. Both ordinary shoppers and womanizer, who flirted with young lovers, were in a hurry here. Later, book collapses appeared near the shops, and this section of the Rambla (Barcelona) was chosen by intellectuals who were vigorously discussing the latest news.



The boulevard inherited its official name from the name of the Carmelite monastery of Sant Josep. Now in its place is the famous Boqueria market, which all tourists dream of wandering around. The oldest attraction, which dates back to the 13th century, is amazing in its size: you can even get lost inside it. As tourists note, this is a rather expensive market, offering a wide variety of products.

However, those who want to save money will have to go deep into Boqueria, where the same products can be bought much cheaper. It is worth remembering that sellers do not allow to try their goods and prohibit taking pictures of them. Rambla of the Capuchins

This part of the Rambla in Barcelona has gained worldwide fame thanks to a bizarre structure decorated with the head of a Chinese dragon who opened its mouth and cast-iron umbrellas on the facade. The Bruno Quadras building delights with ancient Egyptian images, Japanese-style stained-glass windows, palm fans. Now in an unusual house, claiming to be original, there is a bank branch.



An equally famous building is the Grand Liceo Opera House, which, according to architects, was supposed to surpass the beauty of the famous La Scala in Milan. The beautiful building actually competed with many cultural institutions of the world in interior decoration and excellent acoustics. This place was adored by the respectable bourgeoisie, who went to opera performances only in order to declare their status and high position in society. At the end of the 19th century, a young anarchist detonated a bomb in a theater building, which claimed the lives of 20 people.

Next to the Grand Liceo is the Opera Cafe, considered the most popular establishment not only in the Ramblas, but also in Barcelona. It is always crowded, and there is a chance to meet any celebrity, who must leave his signature in a special book, which has grown to three volumes. People come here just to admire the decoration of the halls.



Here you can also see multicolored mosaics laid out by Spanish artist J. Miro on asphalt, included in the lists of street art objects.

Rambla Santa Monica

The last section of the boulevard originates at the old Theater Square. Rambla (Barcelona) is not only unique architectural objects that delight in its beauty. Here stands the former main theater Principal, which could not stand the competition with other institutions and closed. Now the building, waiting for better times, looks very unsightly, and tourists bypass it.

There is also a monument to the explorer Columbus, which is an 80-meter column. The symbol of the city appeared in 1888 and was timed to coincide with the opening of an international exhibition. An elevator is located in the very center of the metal monument, which will take visitors to the observation deck, from where a stunning panorama of the charming Spanish pearl opens.

On the oldest street of Escudelers there is a famous restaurant "Snails", which has a rich history. Famous for its cuisine, it is recognized as a popular attraction. Its halls are decorated with snails made of stone or wood, and even bread is baked in the shape of mollusks.

Once it was a prestigious place where aristocrats walked, and today it has turned into a danger zone for tourists. The Raval district, located where the Rambla (Barcelona) ends, is a slum of the poor and a home of ladies of easy virtue.

Travel Tips

Remember that theft is rampant, and the police will not even come to the rescue in the event of a robbery. Theft of money is not considered a crime, so tourists should be very careful when paying for goods.

Prostitution flourishes here, and the local mafia controls the illegal sex industry. Black ladies who go hunting for men, rob them, adding sleeping pills. Therefore, in the late evenings it is better not to endanger your life. Also, do not get involved in dubious entertainment and play cards for money.

All souvenirs sold in shops are expensive. However, sellers like to bargain, and if desired, reduce the price several times.
