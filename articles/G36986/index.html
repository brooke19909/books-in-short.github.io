New bridge in Novosibirsk. Bugrinsky bridge in Novosibirsk: construction and opening

Novosibirsk is a city with a population of one and a half million, located in the center of the Eurasian continent on the Ob River. It is a major transport intersection of highways connecting the east, west and south of our large-scale country.

The bridge in Novosibirsk became a symbol of his birth as a large industrial center. The city began with the construction of a bridge for the Trans-Siberian Railway. According to controversial data, the choice of a place for the construction of the bridge was argued by N.G. Garin-Mikhailovsky, whose name is currently at the forecourt square in Novosibirsk.

City of roads

Novonikolaevsk, modern Novosibirsk, rightfully bears the status of the capital of the Siberian region. In 1891, it was a village called New Village with a small number of inhabitants. In 1893, a bridge was built here, which became a milestone in the construction of the Trans-Siberian Railway and at the same time the birth date of the city on the Ob. This event predetermined the fate of the modern Siberian capital. Currently, the city is the center of transport routes connecting six federal highways, a river port and an airport of international importance. Therefore, the development of transport links is one of the priority areas in the urban development policy. The city is divided by a water artery into two shores, each of which is a vast industrial zone. Given these features, the system of transport communications should provide for the active construction of bridges.

The new bridge as a way to solve old problems

The problem that any city with a population of one million is experiencing is particularly urgent - endless traffic jams, and sometimes collapses. In the conditions of a difficult Siberian climate with high rainfall in winter, weather anomalies and snowdrifts on the roads, the construction of alternate, secondary and bypass roads is becoming a priority area of ‚Äã‚Äãurban development.



This city, being a major center, is dynamically increasing its resource capabilities. An explanatory note justifying the need to open the third bridge in Novosibirsk directly indicates an urgent need for the construction of new highways and for the liberation of the center from transit intercity and domestic transport.

Geographically, the city is divided by the Ob River into two large industrial zones - the left and right banks. The new bridge in Novosibirsk has become an important link in the creation of a unified transport system, taking into account this fact. It is provided for by the urban development master plan.

The bridge in Novosibirsk is a symbol of the development of the city

The first construction (opened March 31, 1897) is associated with the construction of the Trans-Siberian Railway and actually laid the foundation for the city. The next bridge by historical date was commissioned on January 7, 1932 and received the name Komsomolsky Bridge. It serves to pass the cargo flow in a southerly direction.

Communal Bridge (the official name is October) was put into operation on October 20, 1955. He solved the problem of constant transport links between the two banks of the Ob River.

Prior to its commissioning, communication between the two industrial zones was provided by a pontoon in the summer, and in winter, when a stable ice cover was reached. This bridge became famous not only throughout the former Union, but also in foreign countries. And all thanks to a professional military pilot. Valentin Privalov flew exactly under the bridge at a distance of only one meter from the water, which is why he was subsequently arrested for hooliganism.

In November 4, 1982, the Dimitrovsky Bridge across the Ob opened in Novosibirsk, connecting the eponymous avenue with the left-bank part of the city. It was built using new technology using an all-welded span without the use of bolts.

February 1985 - the launch of the metro bridge that connected the banks of the Ob for the lines of the Novosibirsk metro. It is currently the longest in the world. Another interesting fact was the actual length of the bridge. This is not a constant. Due to the large difference in air temperature during the year, it can stretch (in summer) and contract (in winter) by about 50 centimeters. Especially for this feature there are technical devices in the form of rollers on supports.

In 2007, a bridge was put into operation at the gateway of a hydroelectric power station (HPP). The history of the construction of bridges in Novosibirsk is a milestone in the development of the city as the largest industrial center and transport hub of the Eurasian continent.

In 2014, the third transport highway across the Ob became the famous Bugrinsky Bridge. The design of the fourth and fifth is on the way. And in a strategic perspective, the sixth and seventh bridges are to be commissioned.

The opening of the third bridge in Novosibirsk was the result of a long history. Initially, the object was designated as the Olovozavodsky bridge. Since in the Bugrinsk grove, near which construction was planned, the oldest factory for the production of tin was located. Other variants of the names were "Solnechny", "Nikolsky", "Rainbow" and "Krivoshchekovsky" (they all went through more than one stage of comprehension and various votes). In 2013, it was decided that the name is out of date. And on December 3, the names commission made a unanimous decision on the official approval of the bridge as "Bugrinsky".

Technical characteristics of the Bugrinsky bridge

Four projects were submitted for discussion by the city administration. Of these, two - options for cable-stayed and arched-type structures - received priority. As a result, an arch project was approved. The basis for the adoption of this option was the features of the geographical topography of the area, which the arch bridge in Novosibirsk corresponded to a greater extent. The price of construction has also become a weighty argument in favor of structures with special spans.

The Bugrinsky bridge in Novosibirsk has the following characteristics:

  • arch (70 m - height, 25 m - width, length - 380 m);
  • throughput - 7180 cars per hour;
  • metal supports - 30 pcs.;
  • the number of lanes of the highway - 6;
  • bridge dimensions of the navigable span: width - 140 and 100 m, height - 15 m;
  • the total length of the bridge crossing is about 5480 m;
  • the cost of the bridge is 14,805,462,000 rubles.

Preparatory work

The purpose of the construction is to solve the bypass route for unloading city transport highways from transit routes.

At the preparatory stage, when the construction of the bridge in Novosibirsk took place, a large number of private houses falling into the work area had to be demolished. This process required serious financial investments and time for the construction and commissioning of new homes for immigrants.

Due to difficulties with resettlement, financial shortages and delays in the preparation of space for construction, there was a significant (about 3 billion rubles) appreciation of the initial cost of the project. Nevertheless, the general contractor coped with the task of state scale. And the commissioning of the facility took place according to the approved construction plan.

The third bridge: the chronicle of construction

The project was financed from three sources - the city, regional and federal budgets. The general contractor for the construction, Sibmost, an open joint-stock company from St. Petersburg, proposed the most favorable conditions for the cost of construction.

  • In 2009, preparatory work began on the provision of construction sites by the city. To free the land, more than three hundred houses, ten administrative buildings, garages and a beach in the Bugrinsky Grove were subject to demolition.
  • In 2010, in the month of February, construction began.
  • In December 2012, the construction of thirty bridge piers was completed.
  • April 2014 - the shutters of the arch of the Bugrinsky bridge are closed at an altitude of 70 m.
  • In October 2014, it was opened for operation with the personal participation of the head of state.

In the process of work involved and created jobs for more than one and a half thousand people. The supplies of materials are carried out by five factories for the production of metal structures. Nine research institutes took part in the design process. Large-scale construction required high organization, management and coordination, therefore, it was under close attention and patronage of the government level. Cyclists also tried to "participate" in the construction, who demanded that they have a separate bike path on the new bridge, unfortunately, they were refused.
