How to sew chevrons on a tunic correctly?

A chevron is a graphic sign (image) that resembles the Latin letter V, rotated by various sides. In this article we will find out how to sew chevrons on a tunic without errors. Historical standards

So, we will figure out how to properly sew chevrons on a tunic. Based on history, the element usually belongs to the military circle. But it also finds application in overalls of various enterprises, the uniform of security officers and law enforcement agencies. If you need to sew a chevron on a military uniform , then you need to act according to certain standards. These graphic signs are stitched in a special way to specific places - this is, most often, the left sleeve of a tunic.





We answer the question of how to sew chevrons on a tunic. If there is a pocket on the sleeve, the image is attached at a distance of two fingers from its lower edge. Of course, operating a thread and a needle in this case is very difficult, but the result will exceed your expectations!

Technology

The technology of attaching a graphic sign to a sleeve is simple, but it requires some skill. First you need to measure the necessary interval from the upper seam of the sleeve, then place the chevron in the place intended for it. Next, with the usual pin or needle, the upper point of the mark must be attached to the fabric of the sleeve. This point should be placed in the middle, if its width is meant.

In fact, how to sew chevrons on a tunic, every soldier should know from the first days of service. But we study the technology further ... Now we put on a tunic and become the corresponding side in front of the mirror. We place the lower edge of the chevron on a freely lowered hand so that the sign is held on the sleeve without distortion, strictly vertically. The second needle needs to attach the lower edge of the patch to the intended place.





Next, the tunic must be carefully removed and with small stitches hidden inside, sew the image along the contour. At the same time, the chevron should not form folds. Try to keep it tight against the sleeve. Of course, the color of the threads should match the colors of the patch fabric.
