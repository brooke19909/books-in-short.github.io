Mnemonic - who is this? Word interpretation

Let us turn to the terminology. The most relevant modern interpretation of the word "mnemonics" is a specialist who professionally knows the technique of memorization. This interpretation of the concept of interest to us appeals to another term - mnemonics.





What is mnemonics?

Mnemonics (in terms of sources the term "mnemonics" is found) is a memorization technique. In fact, this is a certain combination of methods and techniques designed to facilitate the storage of information in the brain, to increase the amount of data perceived by a person through the creation of artificial associations.

The term is derived from the Greek word mnemonikon, which in translation means "the art of memorization." Some sources say that Pythagoras of Samos invented it in the 6th century BC.

In addition, it is interesting to know that the name "mnemonics", or rather its original form, is associated with the name of one of the goddesses of the Greek pantheon - Mnemosyne. Recall: Mnemosyne - the mother of nine muses, patronizing memory.

Mnemonics in the modern world

Historically, the memorization technique was formed as part of philosophy. In principle, all modern sciences are rooted in psychology. Mnemonics, on the other hand, was part of rhetoric, and the ancient speakers, in fact, used it to memorize their long speeches. The first recorded data about it are the historians of science date back to 86-82. BC. Their authorship is attributed to Cicero, Quintilian.





Until today, mnemonics has evolved, improved techniques and made them more effective. If earlier it was a tool for remembering structured connected arrays of text, now it can be used to fix any exact information in memory, even if it is considered difficult to remember or not remembered at all.
