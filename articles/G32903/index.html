Busy metropolis

Multinational Shenyang (China) is located in the north-east of the country of contrasts. The administrative center of Liaoning Province, which includes nine districts and three districts, stretches along the banks of the Hunhe River. This is the most important industrial and transport hub, connecting different regions and areas with various routes. The authorities of the developing metropolis, deservedly called the northern capital, care about the protection of nature, paying attention to the numerous green parks that enliven the village.



After 2008, when the Olympic Games were held here, tourists from different countries rushed to the picturesque Shenyang, which received substantial financial support.

A bit of history

Occupying an area of ‚Äã‚Äã3.5 thousand m2, Shenyang (China) is recognized as one of the most ancient cities in the world. More than seven thousand years ago, the first people who were engaged in agriculture and hunting appeared on its territory. In the XVII century, the settlement was captured by the Manchus, who called it Mukden and transferred here the capital of the Qing Dynasty.

In the XX century, Russian troops entered the city, which turned into a stronghold of the Russian Empire. This period is marked by economic growth and cultural revival.

In 1929, Shenyang gets its modern name, and after the war in 1945, he moves to the Republic of China.

Most often they come here in autumn or spring, when there is almost no rainfall.

Mukden Palace - Imperial Residence

Speaking about the main sights of Shenyang (China), one can not help but mention the imperial palace, located in the historical district of Shenhe. The former residence of the rulers of the Qing Dynasty, whose construction began in 1625, is the country's main architectural monument, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.



The well-preserved palace complex, with more than 70 buildings, expanded at the request of the new rulers. Located in the very center of the city, it stands out with a bright orange roof, considered the symbol of the emperor - the son of the sun. So in ancient China, the high social status of the beloved ruler was emphasized. The ensemble, which has historical value, is now a museum that attracts the attention of tourists.

Dongling Mausoleum

The imperial tomb is located in the east of Shenyang (China). In 1629, it was decided to erect a mausoleum that inherited all the features of the construction of the former tombs of the rulers.

The amazing architectural complex, the territory of which has the shape of a square, is made in the Manchu style: the roofs of the buildings resemble a tent. About 30 buildings are surrounded by a giant green park. The tomb of Emperor Taizu Nurhatsi and his Empress is under the protection of UNESCO.

The infamous zoo Shenyang Zoo

The notorious private zoo was founded in 1988. After 20 years, the whole world learned about it from the media after the death of about 500 animals as a result of criminal actions by employees. Zoo workers took home meat intended for animals, and starving predators attacked people.

In addition, it turned out that the Chinese killed white tigers - representatives of an endangered species listed in the Red Book. Employees believed that animal bones helped to heal from all diseases, and healing liquors insisted on them. In 2010, the zoo was closed by decision of the authorities.

Miraculous Object Benxi Cave

A typical object of ecotourism is the deepest caves in the world, located near the colorful Shenyang (China). The karst landmark, which stretches for 5 kilometers, is located in the Bensi massif. It was formed as a result of erosion by water sources of limestone.

There is a wonderful park in front of the entrance, where the sound of the waterfalls is peaceful, and in the pavilions you can buy various souvenirs.



The natural masterpiece consists of three parts, but the latter is closed to the public, as it is a natural monument. The first Bensi cave is a dry landscaped gallery with walking paths lined with paving stones. It surprises with unusual natural formations that are illuminated in the dark by artificial lights. Giant stalactites are very reminiscent of animals, and, as visitors note, the resemblance is actually stunning.

The Bensi Water Gallery is an underground river that tourists ride on boats.

How to get to Shenyang in China

Since the city has an international airport, it is not difficult to get to it from all over the world. Direct flights are operated from Tokyo, Shanghai, Beijing, Seoul, Sydney and other cities. From the capital of our homeland to the metropolis, you can fly in 8 hours, breaking 6 thousand kilometers. The average ticket price is 20 thousand, but we must remember that the price rises in the period from March to October.



In addition, you can get from Moscow to the northern capital of China by train. Travel time is more than five days.
