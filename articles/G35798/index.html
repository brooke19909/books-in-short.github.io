Myths of Ancient Greece. Who killed the gorgon (Medusa)

Myths of Ancient Greece had a huge impact on the formation of European literature, and for scientists, these works of collective folk art to this day are a source of knowledge of the evolution of human psychology. In addition, they are an inexhaustible source of inspiration for artists, poets and musicians. In particular, many Renaissance painters painted canvases on the theme of myth, in which Perseus kills the gorgon Medusa.





Myths: the beginning of the beginnings

According to the myths of ancient Greece, at first only Skotos existed in the Universe, who personified Mhlou. Then chaos arose from it. Together, they gave birth to Night, Darkness and Love (Nikto, Erebus and Eros).

These main gods became the parents of the Earth and Heaven (Gaia and Uranus), as well as deities representing the elements, hecatonheir, titans and titanides. The latter included Rhea and Kronos, from whom the Olympic gods descended. The latter became the protagonists of ancient Greek myths and gave extensive offspring, including demigods and demigoddesses, in which the divine nature was combined with human nature.

Olympus

The ancient Greeks believed that their main deities live on the highest mountain of the country, the top of which is always shrouded in clouds. The real Olympus is located in the north-east of modern Greece, and in ancient times, the border with Macedonia passed along the ridge of this mountain range. There lived:

  • the supreme god Zeus - the lord of heaven, lightning and thunder - and his wife Hera, who patronizes marriage and family love;
  • lord of the kingdom of the dead Hades;
  • goddess of agriculture and fertility Demeter;
  • lord of the sea Poseidon;
  • Goddess of the hearth Hestia.

Then the god-father Zeus gave birth to 4 sons and 3 daughters, who replenished a host of Olympic gods. They became Athena, Ares, Persephone, Aphrodite, Hephaestus, Hermes, Apollon, Artemis and Dionysus.





Demigods

The Olympians, and extremely rarely the Olympic women, did not hesitate to enter into a love affair with mortals. It was from such love that the demigods Achilles, Hercules, Jason, Hector, Pelop, Theseus, Orpheus, Bellerophon, Odysseus, Foreone, Aeneas and Perseus came from. The latter became the one who killed the gorgon Medusa and freed the beautiful Andromeda. All the demigods were heroes with supernatural powers, which they often used to help other people. Moreover, unlike their fathers, they were mortal.
