History of Khryashchevka

Only after the construction of the city of Samara in the 17th century began a large-scale settlement of the right bank of the Volga. The first settlers in these parts were runaway peasants who were looking for a better life. By the end of the 17th century, large monasteries - Novodevichy and Gorodishche were already built on the right bank of the Volga. At the beginning of the 18th century, the entire Syzran Territory was so densely populated that people began to move to the left bank of the Volga.

Khryaschevka, like several other settlements, appeared on the land of the monastery and was originally inhabited by monks. In 1705, it was presented to Alexander Menshikov, but after his disgrace he again transferred to the royal estates.





Since 1851, the village of Khryashchevka has been part of the Stavropol district of the Samara province, which remains to this day. More than 3 thousand people live in modern Khryashchevka, and the main attraction of this place was the Garibaldi castle built here. In Samara and Tolyatti, the local population knows him very well, but the rumor about the Gothic castle has gone far beyond the borders of his native land.

Castle style

Although, in essence, the Garibaldi Castle (Samara, address: Khryashchevka, River Lane) is a hotel with seven VIP rooms, the style of this building was developed very carefully. The owner of the complex wanted the building to look ancient "for real."

Designers had to come up with their own building legend. So, its base and foundation were ‚Äúlaid‚Äù back in the 10-12th century, and the 1st and 2nd floors were completed in the 13-14th century. The third floor was, as it were, ‚Äúbuilt‚Äù by another architect already in the style of the 15-16th centuries, and the final reconstruction of the building, in which the same style was respected, was carried out in the 19th century. To do this, we used the best modern materials, "aged" by the latest technologies. For realism, even such trifles as bas-reliefs on basement stones, similar to those that were found in Denmark at excavations dating from the 10th century, were thought out.





The difference in the "stones" between the old and later masonry also "inspires" the audience that the castle was completed in different eras, maintaining the basic Gothic style. The various components of the building - arches and bas-reliefs - as if were "removed" from the ancient ruins and transferred during construction to this area. Here you can find the motives of ancient Russian architects and the "work" of the masters of the early Middle Ages.

The annex on the west side dates from the 15th century, although stones from the ruins that were as old as the foundation of the castle itself were obviously used for it. Garibaldi Castle (Samara), the photo of which can hardly convey the very atmosphere of this place, nevertheless demonstrates the skill of modern architects who are in no way inferior to their medieval "colleagues".

Port construction and opening

Of course, you can also take a bus to the castle of Garibaldi. Everyone knows how to get from Samara by public transport - with transfers. First, you have to go 2 hours to Tolyatti, and then transfer to the route towards Khryashchevka and get another hour.

But the entire entertainment complex was conceived so that guests could get to it by water. For this purpose, the construction plans included the construction of a port on the shore of the Kuibyshev reservoir.





In order not only yachts and boats, but also oversized vessels, to enter the port, the bay was completely refitted. Now, all yachtsmen and those traveling by water along the Volga can anchor in the waters of the Zhiguli Sea. They will be met by a cozy gazebo with a restaurant, which offers a beautiful view of the Volga. The port opened on June 11, 2014.
