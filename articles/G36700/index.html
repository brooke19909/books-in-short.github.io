From Russia by train

We examined the options for transfers and the use of trains in Europe, but are there any other options, how is it cheaper to get to Prague from Russia? Of course, this is a train. From Moscow on Mondays and Wednesdays, an iron machine leaves for the heart of Europe via Smolensk, Minsk, Brest, Warsaw and Bohumin. The cost of such a route is 200 euros per 27 hours of travel. There is also a 50% discount for children. There are two classes: the first and second, the price of the first is usually 1.5 times higher. It should be noted that baggage is allowed within 36 kg for an adult and 15 kg for a child under 12 years old.

Across Europe

In 37 hours from Moscow you can get to Prague by bus for 107 euros with a two-hour transfer in Riga. The bus is equipped with a TV, music, free Wi-Fi, sockets and drinks. Luggage weighing 30 kg (50 x 50 x 80 cm) can be taken with you. The bus ticket is subject to child and retirement discounts, as well as student discounts using the ISIC card. The bus also passes through Warsaw.

You can also cross the border by car and choose a route in a way that you like. As a rule, tourists cross the border through Smolensk and Belarus, and then follow through Poland towards the Czech capital. The distance between Moscow and Prague is 2000 km, the entire road, taking into account the passage of borders without overnight stays, will take a day. During this time, you will spend 170 euros on gasoline to the point of arrival, plus transit for Polish roads 15 euros. In addition, entering Czech Republic you will need to purchase a vignette on the windshield for Czech roads. Its cost for 10 days is 12 euros, per month - 17 euros.
