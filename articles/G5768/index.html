Pavlovsk reservoir: history and geography

This artificial reservoir was formed in 1960 on the Ufa River as a result of the construction of the Pavlovskaya hydroelectric dam on it . This is the largest reservoir in Bashkiria, its length exceeds one and a half hundred kilometers along the riverbed. It was intended to ensure sustainable water supply in the cities of Ufa and Blagoveshchensk. The Pavlovsk reservoir varies in width from seven hundred meters to one and a half kilometers. The banks of the reservoir are rather steep and indented. On a significant part of the coast, the slopes are covered with dense coniferous vegetation. Pavlovsk reservoir: fishing

The fish stocks of the reservoir have noticeably decreased in recent years, but continue to be quite significant so that fishermen rush to its shores from all the largest Ural cities. Best of all, perch, bream, catfish, burbot, pike, tench and ide are caught here. A characteristic feature of the reservoir is the fact that quite a lot of large fish are found here. You can catch both from the shore and from the surface of the water, in the presence of a boat or other watercraft. Successfully practiced on the reservoir and winter ice fishing. Complications are often caused by flooded logs found throughout the channel.
