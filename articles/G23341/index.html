Abu Dhabi where is it located? Abu Dhabi on the map

Of course, almost everyone had heard at least once in their life about such an Asian city as Abu Dhabi. Perhaps even the average schoolchild knows where this settlement is located.

However, it would be a mistake to believe that it can easily get lost in a string of modern megalopolises of the planet. Many travelers note that there is something in it that subsequently allows you to recognize it, not even from a dozen, but from hundreds of similar ones.

This article will not only tell you in detail about Abu Dhabi (where the city is, what it lives for, and what it is recommended to visit in the first place), the reader will receive valuable tips and advice that will help make future vacations even more interesting.

Section 1. General Information on the Emirate





As you know, the United Arab Emirates is a state consisting of several territorial units.

Unusual to the ear of a Russian person, the word Abu Dhabi is the largest of the seven emirates that make up the whole country. Its area is quite impressive - 80 thousand km 2 .

The capital of the UAE - Abu Dhabi - in a few years has truly turned into an unforgettable place, the greenest city on the entire Persian Gulf coast. Today, many gardens and parks are equipped here, thanks to which the air temperature here is several degrees lower than in the desert around.

Section 2. How to get to the famous resort?

Looking at the map of Abu Dhabi, where the eponymous international airport is located, you can determine it almost immediately. It is in this direction that a modern, wide and beautifully lighted highway has been laid. It will be enough for travelers to cover a distance of 32 km. All terminals of the main air gate of the country are located in one room.

It should be noted that the first and second terminals are served immediately by 12 international carriers, while the third is at the full disposal of the local Etihad Air airline.

You can get to the airport from the capital by taxi, by shuttle bus number 901, which runs every 40 minutes, and by Etihad airline's private shuttle.

It will be useful for Russian tourists to take into account the fact that the S7 (Etihad Air) airliner is constantly leaving from Moscow.

Section 3. The amazing foundation story





Now Abu Dhabi on the world map can be found without any problems. Even a person who is not particularly interested in geography can cope with this task. The magnificent capital of the United Arab Emirates has long been the subject of close attention from not only holiday tourists, but also many business people on the planet.

Of course, the city has gained its present look over the past few years, but according to official figures it was founded in 1760. However, it should be noted that not so long ago archaeologists found confirmation that settlements existed in this place from the third millennium BC. So, it is quite possible to assume that Abu Dhabi (the capital of the UAE) could easily enter the ten most ancient cities on the planet.

By the way, there is an amazing legend about the emergence of the city. Once the Arabs, who lived in one of the oases, went hunting. Soon they met a gazelle and began to pursue it.

The animal looped around the desert for a long time, the hunters followed him until it led them to a spring with beautiful fresh water, which was always worth its weight in gold in this region. The men were so happy that they decided to give the life of a gazelle.

Later, a settlement was formed near the source, which became known as the ‚Äúfather of the gazelle‚Äù, which in Arabic means Abu Dhabi. A new territorial unit has appeared on the world map. So, at least, the legend says, and local residents, as a rule, try to convince tourists that everything was really like that.

Section 4. Location and Climate Features





This emirate is geographically located on an island located on the north-eastern side of the Persian Gulf, 250 meters from the mainland. Three automobile bridges were laid from the mainland to it.

Thanks to the desert tropical climate, Abu Dhabi has become perhaps the hottest city on the planet. There is very little rainfall throughout the year, and the air temperature in summer can reach +50 .

In winter, however, it gets a little cooler. The warmest year of the island is August. The average temperature of the last summer month here is kept at +35.2 .

Section 5. What you should pay attention to when coming on vacation





We can say with confidence that, in general, the United Arab Emirates (Abu Dhabi in particular) is able to surprise anyone, even the most sophisticated and moody traveler.

Many tourists claim that coming to rest in this place of amazing beauty, you should definitely visit its different corners. Those who spend most of their holidays on the beach, as a rule, subsequently regret very much about the time spent.

The United Arab Emirates is actually very diverse and literally in every locality of the traveler, another discovery awaits. For example, the city in the east of the emirate of Al Ain and the region in the west of the island of al-Gharbiya are known for their exotic attractions and the breathtaking world of entertainment.

Take a closer look at the Corniche promenade, visit the Abu Dhabi National Museum, the impressive Al Ain oasis with palm plantations and the region‚Äôs largest zoo. Section 6. The transport system of the capital





It is not surprising that Abu Dhabi, where there is at the same time a huge number of people (both local and newcomers), has excellent transport infrastructure. To move here, wide modern streets with three lanes have been built. It is worth paying attention to the fact that the roads that run from north to south are called Road, and those that stretch from the west to the east - Street.

The best mode of transportation in the UAE capital, Abu Dhabi, a photo of which can now be seen in many travel magazines in the world, is considered a taxi. Public transport here is not very developed and is intended mainly for the transport of laborers and peasants.

Today in the city there are 4 types of taxis: white private taxis, silver new cars, golden taxis and pink taxis with female drivers who transport only women and boys under 10 years old.

All taxi services are equipped with meters and work at standard rates, but you need to agree on the fare in advance with private taxi drivers. The car can be caught right on the street or found in parking lots near shopping centers. In addition, buses run in the village at intervals of 10-30 minutes.

Section 7. Local Attractions





By right, the mosque of Zayed ibn Sultan al-Nahyan is considered to be the pearl of the capital, the famous attraction of Abu Dhabi.

It is one of the six largest mosques in the East. Temple of the founder and first president of the United Arab Emirates. By the way, not everyone knows that in the mosque of Sheikh Zayed his body also rests. Officially and very solemnly, it was opened not so long ago, in 2007, during the holy holiday for all Muslims - Ramadan.

Everyone can visit the mosque, but there is something to see. For example, its arches are decorated with the world's largest chandelier. There is also a huge carpet, which has no analogues in the world. The coverage area is 5627 sq.m. at first glance it‚Äôs even hard to imagine.

The exterior of the main building of the mosque is decorated with 57 domes of white marble. The entire courtyard is lined with colored stone and covers an area of ‚Äã‚Äãabout 17 thousand square meters.

Another no less famous attraction in Abu Dhabi is the park museum called Ethnographic Village. Here is a whole collection of real Arab houses of past centuries. Even now, the workshops of a weaver, a blacksmith, a potter and a glassblower work here. Manufacturing takes place right in front of tourists, and master classes are held with enviable constancy.

Al-Husn Fort Palace, which was built in the 19th century, is also considered a jewel of the Emirates. Beni Yaz tribe in order to protect the fresh water found on the island. The outer walls of the palace were erected by Sheikh Shabut, who made Al-Husn Palace his.

The famous Cornish street stretches along the sea. It is nice to walk here at any time of the year. For tourists who come to rest with children, there is a modern playground, for lovers of hiking special paths are equipped, you can ride a bicycle, scooter or roller skate. But couples in love spend their time in cozy cafes overlooking the coast.

The embankment has earned the world-famous sign - the Blue Flag, which is the guarantor of clean territory and safe swimming. There is a modern catamaran, bicycle and scooter rental service right on the beach.

The Emirates National Reserve - Eastern Mangroves Lagoon is very popular. Pink flamingos and black herons, crabs, octopuses, stingrays and different types of fish live here. The local government carefully preserves these riches of nature, therefore it is forbidden to walk dogs, ride bicycles and scooters in the park. Active travelers will definitely like an excursion on ecologically safe kayaks.

Section 8. What to bring as a souvenir





The United Arab Emirates (a map of the country serves as another confirmation) is located at the intersection of many trade routes, which means that this place can be considered a paradise for shopping lovers.

For example, in Abu Dhabi there are a large number of ultra-modern shopping centers where world-famous brands are sold. But in the markets you can buy a lot of great local souvenirs, exquisite hand-made things, and exotic spices.

Many travelers claim that you can buy absolutely everything here: from branded watches framed with precious stones, to chic outfits, from ancient antiques to high-quality household appliances, from jewelry to textiles and carpets.

All stores basically open from 10.00 in the morning, work without lunch and close in the evening at 22.00. Although it is worth paying attention to the fact that during the month of Ramadan the situation is different: all shops are closed after lunch, and begin to work only late in the evening.

In Al Ain, which is part of the largest emirate of Abu Dhabi, the shopping centers Al Ain Mall, Al Jimmy, and Bivadi Mall are very popular. Not so long ago, another ultramodern shopping center was opened - Foa Mall, which offers not only to buy books, toys, clothes and much more, but also a snack in a cozy, relatively inexpensive cafe.
