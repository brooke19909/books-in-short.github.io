This amazing town, sung in a famous song, will captivate all the travelers who have come to these places. It captivates with its strength and power, embodied in its rich cultural and historical heritage.

Saratov is a wonderful city located on the banks of the great Volga River. Its unique history is famous for its many famous names that have left their mark on this wonderful land. The richness and beauty of this land cannot be expressed in words, you can only admire and enjoy this amazing place.





Museums in Saratov are a reflection of its historical, cultural and spiritual heritage. Each of the museums is unique and keeps the memory of the great people who have contributed to its development. Taking a tour of the museums, you can not only touch and look into the history of this magnificent city, but also enjoy its beauty, wealth and great achievements.

What is significant city?

Saratov is an old merchant city, founded at the end of the 15th century as a defensive fortress. The main pride of the city is the famous Trinity Cathedral, erected at the end of the XVII century. The cathedral is the current spiritual center of the city. Peter I himself visited him during a trip to Azov. In addition, he is a delightful example of ancient Russian architecture. The road going from the cathedral descends to the Volga river bank, where you can take a walk along the promenade or go on a trip by river bus. Victory Park is very popular among residents and visitors of the city. Here you can see a huge collection of military equipment and hear the story of each gun. In addition, the National Village is located here, where you can touch the history and traditions of local indigenous peoples. Tourists will like Kirov Avenue, a kind of Saratov Arbat, where many shops and souvenir shops are located.

The city's attractions

Going for a walk around the city, you can enjoy the ancient architecture of buildings of past centuries. From the embankment of the Cosmonauts a breathtaking view of the Volga River opens, the strength and power of which makes you hold your breath. You should definitely get on the legendary bridge connecting the city with Engels. In the year of its foundation (1965), the bridge was the longest in Europe, its length is 2.8 km. A great place for walking and buying souvenirs will be Kirova Avenue, or, as it was called in the XIX century, Nevsky Avenue. It is here that the main symbol of the city is located - the conservatory. The city is famous for its many museums and theaters. Going to Museum Square, you can see the Holy Trinity Cathedral, where Peter I himself was. In the center of the city is an old church that resembles the St. Basil's Cathedral in Moscow. In the vicinity of the city you can see the remains of the ancient city of Uvek and visit the landing site of Yu. Gagarin.

A bit about the history of the city

During the reign of Tsar Fedor Ivanovich, a decree was issued on the construction of new cities in the Volga region. These cities were to play the role of defensive fortresses. In 1590, Prince Zasekin, together with the boyar Fedor Turov, laid the city between Samara and Tsaritsyn, which was later named Saratov. In the winter of 1614 the city was wiped out by a crushing fire. In 1617, it was again erected on the left bank of the Volga. In 1674, Alexander Shel laid the foundations of the city on Falcon Mountain. In 1676, Tsar Alexei Mikhailovich decided to transfer the city to the right coast of the Volga. Today, Saratov is one of the largest and most beautiful cities of the Volga region. It is famous for its cultural and historical sites, 662 of which are under state protection. 53 of them are of federal importance.

The cultural life of the city

Museums of Saratov will help to get acquainted with the city. They are a reflection of its history and rich cultural heritage. In addition, the city is famous for a large number of art galleries. Saratov is also impossible to imagine without its theaters. The first city theater was opened in 1803. The Academic Opera and Ballet Theater, the Theater of Russian Comedy, the Theater of Young Spectators and others give visitors the opportunity to touch the beautiful. In 1831, the first public library was opened in the city. The city conservatory and the circus are very popular. In addition, the building of the conservatory, made in the Gothic style, is a symbol of the city. The State Circus was founded by the Nikitins in 1873, today it is one of the leading circuses in Russia. In Saratov, annually it is a venue for exciting cultural festivals and competitions, among which the Sobinovsky Music Festival won all-Russian fame.

State Art Museum. A.N. Radishchev

The Radishchev Museum (Saratov) is one of the oldest museums in Russia. Created back in 1885 by Radishchev's grandson - A.P. Bogolyubov, over the years he has put together a huge collection of works of art that has gained popularity not only in our country, but also abroad.





The collection is famous for exhibits from the time of Ancient Russia, ancient icons, sculptures and church attributes. In addition, the works of artists of the 17th-20th centuries are presented here, among which can be seen the canvases by O. A. Kiprensky, K. P. Bryullov, V. A. Serov, M. A. Vrubel and P. P. Konchalovsky. You can also see antique collections, objects of European art and paintings of the Barbizon school of art. There is a library of old manuscripts.

Saratov Regional Museum of Local Lore

The Museum of Local Lore (Saratov) is one of the oldest in the Volga region. It was founded in 1886 on the basis of the city archival commission.





Here is a large collection of archaeological finds, ancient documents and objects telling the history of these places. At the same time, the number of exhibits is constantly growing, even local residents are contributing to the development of collections. The collections devoted to the chronicles of the 20th century, the Golden Horde, as well as the memorial exposition about P. A. Stolypin won great fame.

Saratov Ethnographic Museum

The Ethnographic Museum (Saratov) was opened in 1993 as a branch of the city Museum of Local Lore. It is located in the former Museum of the Ulyanov family, which is a popular city attraction. It is famous for its ancient architecture. At the beginning of the XX century, mother V.I. Lenin. Since the museum does not have its own funds, various exhibitions are held here.

State Museum of K. A. Fedin

The Fedin Museum (Saratov) is located in the old building of the Sretensky School, where Konstantin Alexandrovich studied. Opened back in 1981, today it has the largest collection of Russian literature of the 20th century.





His collections contain exclusive literary works of our and foreign writers. In addition, here you can see old manuscripts, rare old editions, letters, photographs and a huge collection of art paintings.
