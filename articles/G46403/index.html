Swiss ski resort Grindelwald - description, features and reviews

Grindelwald - the ski resort of Switzerland - the best in its region. It is also called the "Village of Glaciers." Local entertainment will interest any tourist, and the landscapes will not leave you indifferent. The article tells how to get to the popular resort, what routes you can try there, and reviews of tourists.





Where is the resort located?

The ski resort Grindelwald is located in Switzerland (Bernese Alps). It is considered the largest and best in its region. The ride height reaches 3300 meters.

Those who have never been to Grindelwald, Switzerland should definitely visit it. In addition to being a great resort, where the whole family is actively spending time, you can relax with your soul after seeing the beauty of the local landscape. Advertising in such places is not needed. Due to its good location and developed tourist infrastructure, Grindelwald is on the list of the elite organization The Best of the Alps.

Who is the resort for? What is in the territory?

The Grindelwald resort is suitable for families with children, inexperienced and intermediate skiers, freeriders and tourists, as well as lovers of restaurants and extraordinary landscapes. There is no spa resort, and nightlife is not abundant.

Sledding enthusiasts will also find entertainment here. Around the resort there are multiple toboggan runs of varying difficulty. A cable car will help you climb the mountain.

In general, in the resort are:

  • more than fifty tracks running on a flat surface;
  • sledding routes;
  • forty-nine lifts;
  • the difference in altitude on the routes is from one to almost three kilometers;
  • Hiking trails
  • routes for toboggans.

For the convenience of skiing, slopes and lifts in Grindelwald (Switzerland) are located as close as possible.

There are also special conditions - children under six are allowed to ride for free with an adult accompanying person (documenting the child‚Äôs age and having received a ski pass at the box office), young people under twenty are given a twenty percent discount, and older people (after sixty two years) - ten percent.

The resort is also proud of its developed tourist infrastructure, including a kindergarten and a ski school for children.





Grindelwald, Switzerland - how to get there?

You can get to the popular resort by car, taxi, hotel bus or public transport with transfers.

First, tourists need to fly to Zurich airport. From here you can order a taxi that will bring directly to Grindelwald, but the cost of such a trip is very high.

Many hotels provide meeting and transfer services. For an additional fee, visitors are expected at the exit from the airport and delivered directly to the room.

The resort can also be reached from Geneva Airport. The journey will take approximately 4 hours. Take the train in the direction of St. Gallen or Luzern to the city of Bern. Next by train to Interlaken Ost, then to Grindelwald.

By road to the destination you can get from:

  • Basel, distance - one hundred seventy-five kilometers;
  • Geneva, a distance of two hundred and five kilometers;
  • Zurich, passing Bern and Interlaken, and having traveled about ninety kilometers.

You can get around in Grindelwald by bus. There are railway stations: one in the center - with the name Grindelwald, and the second - on the way to the top of Kleine Scheidegg, in the Grund valley.





What is a must visit?

The opening of the ski resort takes place in November, and say goodbye to tourists in April.

It is especially beautiful here on New Year and Christmas holidays. The main street along which Grindelwald (Switzerland) is located, at this time shines with colorful bright illumination, decorations are hung all over its length. Favorite places of tourists add electricity: excellent restaurants with excellently prepared food, an abundance of boutiques and hotels.

All this is supported by the owners in their favorite Swiss style - a chalet. Items are made of wood, the tradition is carefully preserved.

Once in the resort, you should definitely do three things:

  • slide off the cold snowy slopes;
  • climb the highest local peak - Jungfrau;
  • listen to the concert of the great Richard Wagner - a native of local.

Interesting activities in the winter are:

  • World Snow Festival - held in January. At this time, artists from around the world are carving sculptures from snow blocks provided by the organizers.
  • World Championship Velogemel (Snowbiking) - held annually in February, giving uniqueness to the alpine village.
  • Snowpenair, an open-air snow music festival, is held in March at the close of the season on Klein Schneidegg.

Ski area and surrounding area

You can ski and sled in Grindelwald (Switzerland) on both sides of the valley. The territory includes blue, red and black tracks with a total length of 213 km. There are 51 of them, the height difference is 1937 m. By the level of difficulty, the tracks were divided almost evenly: 20% - difficult, 50% - medium-difficult, and 30% - for beginners.

For cross-country skiing enthusiasts, 34 km of flat trails are available. Lighting at night was carried out only to some of them.

The resort has over 80 km of hiking trails and 6 tracks 57 km long for tobogganing.

For fans of thrills, a new attraction is opened - First Flyer - it allows you to fly 800 meters down, at a speed of up to 80 km / h. This is a kind of safe "bungee".

In the region of neighboring Wengen, the World Cup is annually held on the famous black Lauberhorn track. Its length is 4454 m. The track is distinguished by insidious slopes and turns closer to the finish line.





Reviews about Grindelwald

Many tourists who have visited a heavenly place dream of returning here. They leave positive reviews about Grindelwald in Switzerland and highlight the following points:

  • tourists are attracted by picturesque alpine landscapes, which are organically complemented by mountain villages;
  • vast ski area, which is equipped with tracks of varying difficulty;
  • the presence of a glacier enables year-round skiing;
  • created conditions for relaxing with the family and training beginners;
  • the atmosphere immerses tourists in a winter fairy tale;
  • hotels of different levels, any tourists can choose suitable conditions for their rest.

But there are also negative points that are mentioned in the reviews:

  • the road from Geneva or Zurich takes a lot of time; tourists would like to get there faster;
  • ski areas are torn among themselves, and there are no ways to connect them, that is, starting a vacation in one zone, it is difficult to move to another;
  • due to competition interesting tracks are often closed;
  • large queues for lifts;
  • few luxury hotels.

Despite the negative aspects, tourists consider Grindelwald a great place to relax.
