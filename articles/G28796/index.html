Colonel Budanov: biography

Colonel Yuri Budanov - a serviceman of the Russian army, a participant in two Chechen wars. In 2003, he was convicted of the murder of a young Chechen girl. Colonel Budanov was sentenced to ten years in prison. In 2009, he was released on parole. In 2011, he was shot dead by unknown persons.

Career

Colonel Budanov was born in 1963 in the Donetsk region. He studied at the Kharkov Tank School, after which he was sent to serve in Hungary. Colonel Budanov, whose biography is quite rich in moving, after the collapse of the USSR continued to serve in the Russian troops.

He participated in the First Chechen campaign. In 1995, he was wounded in the head. In the second Chechen war, he commanded the 160th Tank Regiment. He was shell-shocked twice in the fall of 1999. In January 2000, Budanov became a colonel, and already in March he was arrested, accusing him of rape and murder of a Chechen girl.





The feat of Colonel Budanov: how it was

At the end of December 1999, in the vicinity of the village of Dubai-Yurt, 160 soldiers of the 84th reconnaissance battalion were ambushed in Wahhabi. Scouts asked the headquarters for help, but this was refused. A thousand Arab mercenaries of Khattab literally destroyed Russian soldiers with their fire.

Nearby was the tank regiment of Budanov. He was ordered to stand still and not get involved in battle. The village was considered peaceful, and the authorities forbade the introduction of tanks there. Budanov heard reconnaissance talks with the command. He decided to break the order and help the dying guys.

Budanov gathered a regiment and called volunteers from among the officers. Having equipped them with tanks, he personally led their battle. The enemy was sure that help to the scouts would not come, so a sudden attack by tankers demoralized him. Khattab retreated, and the scouts were rescued. The next morning, all of Chechnya was already talking about this feat. Colonel Budanov for his arbitrariness received a severe reprimand from the headquarters.

Court

In February 2001, hearings began on the Budanov case, which had a great public outcry. The accused claimed that the girl he killed was a sniper and destroyed several dozen of his soldiers in the Argun gorge.

A year later, the court ordered a medical examination. In total, four psychiatric examinations were conducted. One of them showed that the colonel was in a state of insanity during the murder. For this reason, the court sent him for compulsory treatment to a psychiatric clinic. But already in 2003, the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation overturned this decision and convicted Budanov.

Sentence

The soldier was found guilty and sentenced to nine years in prison for murder by negligence, the article about the rape was removed from him. He was also added six years for kidnapping and another five for abuse of power. In total, the court decided to deprive Budanov of liberty for 10 years. In addition, the colonel lost his officer rank and the Order of Courage he had earned earlier .



Liberty

In 2004, Budanov filed a request for early release. It was satisfied by the assembled commission. However, frank threats rained down at Budanov, and he was forced to withdraw his application. When the passions around this matter calmed down a bit, Budanov in 2008 filed a new petition for early release. The application was granted, and in early 2009 he was released from prison.

Memory

We do not undertake to assert whether this person was a hero or a monster, time will judge and put everything in its place. However, his comrades comrade honor his exploits and remember him as a hero. In memory of Colonel Budanov, Russia has devoted many poems to the hands of contemporary poets.





External evaluations, track record and characteristics

At first, his track record was not much different from other similar ones. Colonel Budanov gradually climbed the standard officer ladder. A sharp shift in his career occurred on the eve of the Second Chechen campaign. Lieutenant Colonel Budanov then received a tank regiment under command, consisting of almost a hundred combat vehicles.

Literally immediately, his military unit was sent to Chechnya. There he received the extraordinary rank of colonel. One of his main achievements is that Budanov went through half the war with virtually no loss. Lost only one driver. None of the other commanders had such indicators.
