Mallorca Island, Spain: description, hotels, beaches, excursions, holiday reviews

The island of Mallorca (Mallorca) is one of the Balearic Islands that make up Spain. Translated from Spanish, the name of the island means the largest. About 12 million tourists annually come to Mallorca for holidays.

There are no rivers on the island, fresh water is extracted from underground sources and reservoirs. Despite the limited resources of fresh water, there are 40 reserves in which oaks feel great and green meadows are green.

The capital of the island is Palma de Mallorca. The island has two languages: Spanish and Catalan. Due to the large influx of tourists, every second resident speaks English.

Climatic features and sea temperature

The island has a very mild climate, suitable for relaxation at almost any time of the year. Tourists begin to arrive in April, the season continues until October. The maximum number of tourists is recorded from July to August. Knowledgeable people come to the island in September, when the temperature of water and air is comfortable, and the influx of tourists is small, respectively, and prices are lower.

What to see in Mallorca

Holidays on the island are not only a game of golf and swimming in the sea, but also an opportunity to see the most beautiful places, because in Mallorca there is something to see. This and ancient architecture, unique caves, beautiful water parks.

Drach Caves - Del Drach

This is one of the main attractions of the island located in the eastern part. The total length of the cave is 1.2 kilometers, and from the maximum point, the depth is 25 meters. Inside is Lake Martel, which is huge and recognized as the largest underground water reservoir in the world.

The inhabitants of the island became aware of the caves in the Middle Ages. For inspection by tourists for several years (from 1922 to 1935) arranged passages, laid paths and stairs. They also had electric lighting.

Modern excursions last about 60 minutes, which include a small concert of classical music. Inside the cave there is very high humidity, about 80%, and the air temperature is not higher than + 21 ¬∞ . The cost of the tour is about 40 euros.

Valldemossa and Soller

As a rule, excursions in Mallorca to Valdemossa combine with other interesting places, in particular with Soller. After all, they will make it possible to better understand how the islanders lived in the province several hundred years ago.

Valldemossa is a colorful village on a mountain. Travelers are offered to walk along the winding paths among the ancient buildings, visit the Cartesian monastery, built in the XIII century. Vacationers must visit the Chopin Museum in the monastery, located in one of the cells, where he spent 3 months with his companion George Sand. A visit to the house of St. Catalina Tomac, which is the patroness of the island, is also provided.

The town of Soller is located in a grove among orange trees, rocks and the sea coast. It is here that you can ride a wooden tram, as well as the oldest train, Orange Express. The train path runs through 13 tunnels in the mountains and approaches Palma itself. On average, such an excursion will cost 50 euros, not including a visit to museum complexes.

Cathedral of Palma de Mallorca

This tour in Mallorca is perhaps the most popular, because the cathedral is the main attraction of all the Balearic Islands and the most important religious building in the whole region. Locals call the temple "La Sey", and the official name is the Cathedral of the Blessed Virgin Mary.

The cathedral rises in the most prominent place on the island, so it can be seen from afar. This building is huge, its height is 44 meters. It seems that the structure literally soars in space.

The temple has many stained glass windows, so the inside is always very light. Inside and outside the building is inlaid with stones and precious metals. The cathedral was built in the 15th century. But most importantly, it will be possible to see the main shrine of the temple - the ark of the Holy Cross. The walls of the cathedral often host organ concerts. The entrance to the shrine is 6 euros.

Art Caves

What to see in Mallorca yet? Of course, he will go to the northeast of the island to the caves of Arta, which were discovered back in the 16th century. The most famous priestess of the island, Nurreduna, hid in these caves. According to the assurances of local residents, her spirit still flies here. In the cave you can see stalagmites, one of which is the highest in the world - 22 meters. At the end of the walk, travelers will enjoy a light performance accompanied by classical music. For the tour you will have to pay at least 35 euros.

Flamenco show "Carmen"

In the capital of Mallorca, in a modern concert hall, you can see the most famous show called "Carmen". The concert was compiled based on the works of Spanish artists, but put on a modern dance style. It is on this show that you can see all the passion of flamenco and Sevilla. The cost of visiting is from 53 to 67 euros.
