Pattern: cat Simon (master class). Cat Simon - soft toy

Cartoon Cat Simon from the animated series of the same name for several years as closely entered our lives. But how could it be otherwise if this cat is so real and funny? We suggest you try to make this cute character yourself. Pattern "cat Simon" is attached to the article.

What do you need for sewing?

The list of necessary tools and materials in order to sew the toy "Cat Simon":

  • pattern (large, small, any);
  • paper (preferably denser);
  • pencil, marker, pen (what is at hand);
  • fabric for the "body" of any color (you can use any, but the softest toy will turn out of fleece);
  • white tissue for the eyes (use the same material as for the whole body);
  • black thread for the face (preferably floss or just thicker yarn);
  • any filler (synthetic winterizer, synthetic winterizer, holofiber, grain and so on);
  • thread for sewing (preferably in the tone of body color);
  • tailor scissors (or ordinary);
  • beads for the pupils;
  • pins.

Pattern "Cat Simon"

So, to sew any thing, you need a form. The most common pattern "toy cat Simon" is given below.





It must be printed on any paper using a printer. Remember that the size of the finished Cat Simon toy depends on the size of the pattern. The pattern, enlarged compared to the original or, conversely, reduced, allows you to make a whole family of cats. The easiest way to change the size of the form - when printing from a computer, select page options (for example, set "Print on A4 page").

After listing, we see that the pattern "Cat Simon" consists of three parts: the body, tail and eyes. It is desirable to transfer the printed version to thicker paper (for example, cardboard). This option will continue for a longer period.

Next, you can translate the paper pattern on the fabric:

  1. Cut out all the details of the paper pattern along the contours.
  2. Fold the main fabric in half. In this case, the front side must be inside, and the fabric should stretch to the sides, as in the picture of the pattern. If your toy body color matches the eyes, then take another piece of white cloth (for the eyes).
  3. Attach the paper pattern to the fabric and pin it carefully with pins.
  4. Circle the outline of the paper pattern with a marker, pencil, pen. Unfasten the paper pattern from the fabric.
  5. Connect the two layers of fabric with pins or gently sweep them together.
  6. Cut out the outlined details of the future toy.

Sewing body toys

Pattern "toy cat Simon" is no longer needed. It can be put aside so as not to interfere.

Now proceed to stitching the parts. To do this, it is convenient to use a sewing machine, if it is not there, then you can use a simple needle with a thread.





1. Sew all parts except the eyes, clearly along the marked contours. So the toy will be neat. It is not necessary to leave holes for eversion. The following describes why.

2. Cut out all the stitched details, stepping back from the seam by about a couple of millimeters if you are sewing a toy from fleece fabric, if from another - a little more.

3. Make small cuts on the indentation at the junction of the ears, fingers and paws with the muzzle. This will save the finished product from ugly constrictions.

4. Now you need to turn out a stitched, but not yet finished product. To do this, make small cuts in the following places:

  • on the face, where you plan to sew eyes;
  • on the tail, from the side where it will be sewn to the main body.

5. Unscrew all parts carefully so that the cuts do not open. You can use the back of a pencil or a wooden stick.

6. When all parts are turned out, it is necessary to fill them. To do this, push small portions of stuffing into the body and tail. Stuffing is necessary quite tightly. Particular attention should be paid to the far corners of the ears and fingers, and also do not forget to highlight the belly of a cat.

7. After you have filled and smoothed everything, the cuts must be sewn up. Remember to use thread to match the fabric. Darn should be a hidden seam.





Stitching parts

When all the parts are full and the cuts are sewn, you can completely sew the toy.

Tail

It is sewn to the body on the side where there was a cut for eversion and stuffing. It is best to sew it in a stitch in a circle. So it will be more reliable and aesthetic.

Eyes

  1. We attach two circles to the muzzle and fix them with pins.
  2. Under the circles we put a little stuffing so that the eyes are embossed.
  3. Sew the eyes with a white thread. Sewing is better in the style of "over the edge."




4. We make pupils from beads. In order to have less thread, sew the beads with one thread, then bring the bundle to the place where the nose will be.

Simon's cat's face

Until the completion of the creation of the toy, there is very little left - to make a muzzle. But this step also requires great accuracy in actions, like all previous work.

You will also need a little imagination. After all, your cat can smile, frown, or even show your tongue!

To get a face like in the picture below, follow the instructions below:

Advice

Some practical tips that may come in handy:

  1. From a pattern in A4 sheet size, you get a finished toy about 20 centimeters high.
  2. In order to make a cat-pillow Simon, the pattern will need the same as the one above. Just use more body stuff.
  3. For beauty, you can sew pads on the paws (you need to use the same thread as for the muzzle) and make a puziko.
  4. The above pattern "Cat Simon" is also suitable for making small trinkets or magnets. Just sew a special mount between the ears of the toy or stick a magnet on the back of the cat.




5. You can also sew suction cups to the legs. Thus, you get an original pillow-sucker for the car.
