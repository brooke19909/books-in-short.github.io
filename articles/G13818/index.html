Goa, Panaji. Description, attractions, reviews

On the banks of the Arabian Sea, at the mouth of the Mandovi River, is the picturesque capital of Goa - Panaji. A city with a long history, once the former administrative center of Portuguese India. Panaji cannot be compared with the populous cities of India such as Mumbai, Calcutta and Bangalore. This is a cozy town with small narrow streets and houses preserved from ancient times, a modern port, churches, temples and numerous architectural monuments, each of which is unique and has its own history. The capital of Panaji is rich in interesting places.

sights

For many centuries, the city has surprisingly been combining diametrically opposed cultural traditions and eras. In this unusual place, the culture of ancient India was wonderfully intertwined with the heritage of more modern Portugal.





Residents of European countries who visit Panaji for the first time immediately feel the special atmosphere prevailing in the city. The narrow streets lined with stone, Catholic churches and cozy cafeterias with tables on the terrace resemble more picturesque corners of Europe than the country of the far East. That is why for Europeans, the capital of Goa Panaji is almost the second home.

Spiritual heritage

The city has many amazing places to visit. The Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception of Our Lady of the Virgin Mary is one of the main attractions. This beautiful building with several baroque towers and a belfry was erected in 1541.





Maruti Temple, dedicated to Hanuman, the devotee of Lord Ramachandra, is located in a picturesque place on a hill, from where you can enjoy breathtaking views of Panaji and its surrounding territories.

In the small chapel of St. Sebastian, built in the 80s of the 19th century, it is worthwhile first of all to turn your eyes to the unique crucifix that previously was in the Palace of the Inquisition of old Goa. Jesus Christ, crucified on the cross, has wide eyes, although according to all the canons it is strictly forbidden to depict him like that. Perhaps this sight was to inspire terror in the hearts of sinners who faced imminent punishment.

Another unique place in Panaji deserves special attention - the Mahalakshmi Temple. This shrine, erected in 1818, was the first in India, which the Portuguese were allowed to erect on Goa in the entire three hundred year history of colonization. Today, the abode of the goddess Lakshmi, the owner of all visible and invisible wealth, is considered the main Hindu shrine of Panaji. A huge number of pilgrims visit the temple daily, pray and present it with various gifts.

Unique monuments

An architectural monument - a statue of Abbot Faria - is no less famous landmark of the city. Faria is one of the founders of such an amazing phenomenon as hypnosis. This monument, according to local residents, personifies a trance session.

Not only such unusual creations can be seen in Goa. Panaji is an ancient territory on which many historical monuments have been preserved. You can see them by wandering along the colorful streets of ancient districts with many unique architectural buildings in a special distinctive style. Arriving in Panaji, you should definitely look into the Goa Museum, where numerous exhibits are presented in 15 thematic exhibitions telling about the difficult life and history of the state.

City surroundings

The surroundings of Panaji are also interesting. You should definitely go to the Dudhsagar Falls, which is considered the second highest in India. A distinctive feature of this reservoir is that its waters are not traditionally blue, but transparent white. That is why it is also called the "Milk Ocean". On the way to the waterfall, you can look into the reserve with many exotic animals living freely in their usual habitat.
