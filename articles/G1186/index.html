Interest Rate Types

Fixed interest is considered common. Their feature is that they remain unchanged for the entire term of the mortgage.

There are also floating rates, which depend on the average value of the rates of all interbank markets of European importance. Every six months or a year, the borrower is informed of a new percentage value.



There is also a combined rate that remains unchanged for the first few years of the loan, and then goes into a floating one. Such a rate is beneficial to the borrower because it allows you to accumulate funds until it moves from one to another.

For each user of a mortgage loan, interest is calculated individually, which he will then have to pay.
