Symi Island: Greece friendly and welcoming

It is no secret that many of our compatriots rush to rest in the Schengen area. And the most favorable conditions, interesting excursions and excellent thalassotherapy are provided by Greece. The islands, tours that suit many companies, are the most popular resorts. After all, they are relatively cheap, beautiful, exotic, the climate there is warm, and the atmosphere is a rare combination of oriental hospitality and European service. Moreover, you need to get there by boat or ferry, and this journey itself often gives great pleasure. But there is one place that will especially appeal to those who prefer a typical vacation in Greece.





Symi Island is small (only about seventy kilometers in area), but very autochthonous. White houses with flat roofs, steep cliffs overhanging the waves, beaches (both ocher sand and fine azure pebbles), evergreen pyramidal cypress trees and the spicy smell of dry grass - it would seem surprising? But the human eye is pleased with these landscapes. Indeed, despite the fact that for centuries this land has passed from hand to hand, nature has remained just as calm and pleasing to the heart. Symi Island (Greece) has its own capital. It bears the same name and, as is often the case with Mediterranean cities, is divided into upper and lower parts. The latter - Yialos - is a village near the bay and port. Two ferries from neighboring Rhodes come here, as well as another ship cruising on all the Dodecanese islands (which, in fact, Symi enters).



Tourists recommend traveling here either in the spring (April-May) or already in September-October. In the summer there is too hot, and pastime is an amateur. What can the island of Symi offer us? Greece belongs, as you know, to the Mediterranean, and people come here to sunbathe, swim and swim in clear water. And it is most convenient to get to the best beaches from the city bay by a special boat, which here plays the role of a sea taxi. You can ride around the whole island like that. And you can get from the port to another bay - Pedi - by bus through the upper town of Chorio. The most popular beach is named after St. Nicholas. You can walk from it from Pedi Bay or take a boat from the city port. Moreover, the travel time will be approximately the same. The beach "St. Marina" is famous for its clear water. There are almost uninhabited places with small bays. But everywhere you can find a standard minimum of amenities - umbrellas, sunbeds and a bar.
