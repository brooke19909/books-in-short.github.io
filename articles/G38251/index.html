Pyatigorsk, Stavropol Territory: motels, attractions, the history of the city and reviews with photos

The city of Pyatigorsk in the Stavropol Territory is the second largest. In addition, it is known as the oldest balneological resort in Russia, as well as the place where M.Yu. served and died. Lermontov.

Due to its numerous mud deposits and mineral springs located on a relatively small area, Pyatigorsk in the Stavropol Territory of Russia can well compete with the leading resorts in Germany and the Czech Republic. But these places are good not only for healing. Arriving in Pyatigorsk, outdoor enthusiasts can find many interesting new routes for themselves.

The picturesque city located in the North Caucasus, spread out on the slopes of Mount Mashuk and drowning in picturesque groves, is happy to receive its guests, regardless of their social status and nationality. After all, all of its natural resources in the form of clean mountain air, mineral water and therapeutic Tambukan silt Pyatigorsk allows people to get free of charge.

History from ancient times

Pyatigorsk people settled for a very long time. This is evidenced by household items and tools found in these places by archaeologists. The first written mention of Bish-dag (five mountains) with its hot spring was left by Ibn Battut. This famous Arab traveler mentioned these places, describing his travels in 1334.

Mid 16th century information about the Pyatigors created by Russian chroniclers is dated. In these papers, it is reported that this is the area in which the Circassians live.

In 1773, the Russian scientist Guldenstedt briefly described the Hot Mountain, which is the southern spur of Mashuk. The researcher pointed out the existence of a hot sulfur spring that flows down to the Podkumok river. In those years, this area was ruled by the Turks. But after the adoption in 1774 of the Kyuchuk-Kaynardzhsky treatise, Pyatigorye became part of the Russian Empire.

Foundation of the city

The history of Pyatigorsk began in the 18th century. It was then that a point appeared in this area, part of the Azov-Mozdok fortified line, created according to the order of Catherine II to protect the southern borders of Russia.

In 1780, a fortress was built near the Beshtovy Mountains to strengthen this point. She was named Konstantinogorsk in honor of the grandson of the sovereign. Not far from this fortress, the village of Hot Waters arose, to the healing springs of which visitors began to gather. It is 1780 that is considered the date when the city of Pyatigorsk was founded.

Birth of the resort

In 1803, Alexander I published a rescript that attached particular importance to this area. This document changed the fate of the city, making it a resort.

Throughout the entire history of this region, scientists have repeatedly been convinced that in terms of their diversity, wealth, value and quantity of mineral waters Pyatigorsk has no equal in the whole world.

Some innovations were introduced by the General Manager of Georgia and the Caucasus, General A.P. Ermolov in 1819. By his order in the Hot Waters the baths were rebuilt, and a platform appeared on one of the ledges of Mashuk. A new building for the adoption of water procedures, which was called "Ermolovsky", also grew. In addition, the general, with the participation of the Petersburg Construction Committee, planned the whole area, creating residential quarters in it. In a new settlement, residents of various cities located in the Caucasian province were invited to move in. For this, people were offered some benefits.

Thus, one of the parts of the future Pyatigorsk (Stavropol Territory) and the streets that still exist in the city were formed. Their current names are Bukhachidze, Anisimova, Krasnoarmeysk, K. Marx Avenue and them. Kirov. Soon the first residential buildings appeared in these quarters, and since 1823, construction in the city began to be carried out most actively. Title

The project of the future city was drawn up in 1828 by Giuseppe Bernardazzi. In 1830, it was approved by the Committee of Ministers of Russia. At the same time, it was proposed that General Emanuel call the city of Pyatigorsk, as well as the mountain at the foot of which it was located. Climate

Pyatigorsk is a region of the Stavropol Territory located geographically south of Yalta. But, despite this, the climate of these places is closer to temperate continental. Affects this remoteness of the city from the sea.

Judging by the reviews of vacationers, in Pyatigorsk there are no sudden changes in temperature. The summer here is quite warm. In the hottest month - July, the thermometer rises to an average of +21 degrees. Winters in Pyatigorsk are mild. Even in January, the air temperature does not fall below -4 degrees.

Warm and dry autumn pleases guests of the city. The fine days, which last until the end of November, never upset the cloudy sky and off-season dampness.

Wellness factor

At the very beginning of its history, the resort was founded as a place intended for treatment with hot sulfur springs. It was they who were the first to be discovered in these parts. Today, the guests of Pyatigorsk are invited to improve immediately with three types of natural springs. Among them, not only hot, but also warm, as well as cold. Healing mineral water is produced in Pyatigorsk simultaneously from 23 wells. Additionally, there are 15 more sources. However, these wells are in reserve and will be used only if necessary.

According to their chemical composition, the waters produced in Pyatigorsk (Stavropol Territory, Russia) are divided into three types. Among them:

  1. Sulfide. They are mined in the main group of local sources known since ancient times. Such waters are characterized by high temperature, which reaches +47 degrees. This health elixir is used, as a rule, for balneological procedures in Lermontov, Pirogov and Yermolov baths.
  2. Carbonic. These mineral springs in Pyatigorsk are used for balneotherapy and for drinking. The water in them has medium and low carbon dioxide content. One of the subspecies of this natural healing factor is the Essentuki type. These are cold carbonate waters. Before use, they are specially heated right in the pump room.
  3. Radon. Their sources have poor mineralization. Moreover, in the composition of such waters is a certain amount of radon. Depending on the source, it ranges from weak to high. Apply such water only externally, using when taking a shower, baths and during inhalation.

Judging by the reviews, in Pyatigorsk, vacationers can drink natural medicine for free in nine pump rooms located on the slopes of Mashuk. And also in specialized galleries (on Akademicheskaya St. and on Kirov Avenue).

In numerous sanatoriums of Pyatigorsk (Stavropol Territory), in addition to balneotherapy procedures, which are so traditional for the area, mud therapy is also actively used. For him, natural material is delivered from Lake Tambukan. At the same time, mud is used for various applications and wraps. A small factory has been opened in Pyatigorsk. Its products are cosmetics, which include Tambukan mud.

With the active participation of the Pyatigorsk administration (Stavropol Territory), the improvement of mountain paths located on Mount Mashuk, which are intended for therapeutic walking, is currently supported.

Interesting places of the resort

There are a lot of attractions in Pyatigorsk (a photo with a description of the most popular of them will be presented below). Among such objects are not only museums and monuments familiar to many places, but also unusual territories and buildings. So, in the city of Pyatigorsk (Stavropol Territory) at the foot of Mashuk there is a cemetery. It is sometimes called an open-air museum. The fact is that in this Pyatigorsk necropolis there are a lot of historical monuments. These are the graves of statesmen and warriors, scientists and writers. In addition, the necropolis was the site of the initial burial of Lermontov. As early as 1916, the cemetery was closed and began to serve only for historical and cultural purposes.

Description of the attractions of Pyatigorsk should start with one of its main streets - Kirov Avenue, where the fountain "Tale", which is also called the "Dwarves" or "Grandfathers", is located. The history of this building began in 1902. However, at that time it looked a bit different. Yes, and was called the "Giant" because of its jet of water, which rose up to a huge height. The first version of the fountain did not shine with beauty, because of which they decided to modify it. So a construction appeared here from a picturesque pile of stones with grottoes, as well as with relief images of fairy-tale gnomes. Nowadays, the fountain has been equipped with colored lights adorning it at night.

Quite a few sights of Pyatigorsk (see the description for a photo below) are located in the oldest park of the city, which is called the Flower Garden. He was defeated in the 20s of the 18th century. in place of swamps. Over the years, the park has grown. New flowerbeds appeared on its territory, and trees were planted along the alleys. In addition, a musical rotunda was built in the Flower Garden, a fountain was built and various buildings were erected.

During the years of Soviet power, this park was combined with another, located on Mount Goryachaya. To date, this territory, judging by the reviews, in Pyatigorsk is one of the most beautiful. That is why the residents of the city and its guests love walking here so much.

Right at the entrance to the park is one of the attractions of the city of Pyatigorsk - the Vernissage gallery. Near her stands a monument to the hero of the immortal novel Ilf and Petrov - Kise Vorobyaninov. This literary adventurer is depicted as begging. Vorobyaninov is standing by the large bag, bowing his head and looking longingly at the inverted hat.

Not far from the entrance to the park rises the original building. This building is made of colored glass and openwork metal and decorated with pointed towers. This architectural miracle is nothing but the Lermontov Gallery. She was transported to Pyatigorsk at the beginning of the 20th century. from Nizhny Novgorod, where the building was one of the exhibits of the art-industrial exhibition. The main purpose of the gallery throughout all these years remains unchanged. Within its walls is the State Philharmonic, concerts are held and various exhibitions display their exhibits.

At the entrance to the gallery you can see the old fountain, which is called "Happy Catch." For over 100 years, he has been giving vacationers his cool and interesting sculptural composition, presented by a boy holding fish of impressive size over his head.

Behind the Lermontov Gallery is the building of the Ermolov baths. It was built in 1880. Today there is a gynecological balneotherapy center.

If you walk along the path to the right of the building of the Ermolov baths, then after 50 m you can see the "Diana's Grotto". This is a decorative and rather original construction of the first half of the 19th century, which relates to landscape gardening.

"Diana's Grotto" was erected in honor of the conquest of Elbrus by an expedition led by General Emanuel. This building was named after the ancient Roman goddess of hunting, who preferred to rest in cool grottoes on hot days.

The project for a stone gazebo was created by the famous Italian architects brothers Bernardatsi. On one of the slopes of Mount Goryachaya, a cave was carved, the arch vault of which was supported by prehistoric columns. A semicircular stone bench was set against the wall of the grotto. Near it is a table made of the same material. It is believed that in this grotto Mikhail Lermontov loved to relax. A week before the deadly duel, the poet even invited here to know, having arranged a magnificent ball for his guests. At this event, many of the guests saw Lermontov alive for the last time.

Not far from the "Diana's Grotto" is another one of the main attractions of Pyatigorsk (see photo below). This is a sculpture of an eagle, spreading its wings and holding a snake in its claws.

The bird rises on a pedestal, which is built of stone blocks. There is a legend that tells that a poisonous snake managed to sting an eagle. The bird was saved only thanks to the water located in the source of the Goryachaya Mountain. More than 100 years ago, this folk tradition was reflected in the sculpture. Moreover, the stone eagle is currently a symbol of both Pyatigorsk and the entire region of the Caucasian mineral waters.

Not far from this statue, the gazebo attracts the attention of travelers. It is located on the top of Goryachaya Mountain and has an original roof architecture that resembles the roof of Buddhist temples. That is why the arbor is called Chinese. It was built in 1976.

Returning from the Chinese gazebo to Kirov Avenue, you should go almost to its very end and see the Pushkin baths. This ancient building is made of light brick, has curly masonry and is decorated with bizarre turrets and stucco figures. It was built more than two centuries ago by General Sabaneyev, who at that time was the owner of this land, and were named in his honor. In the 1920s, the bathtub was renamed Pushkin.
