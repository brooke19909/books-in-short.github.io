Tiraspol History

The founding of the city on the Dniester is inextricably linked with the events in Russia in the 18th ‚Äì 20th centuries. The southern edge of the empire was a kind of "crossroads" for many peoples and religions. Preserved this feature and modern Tiraspol. Transnistria until the 18th century was inhabited by Russian Old Believers, Cossacks, Moldavian and Ukrainian peasants. The victories won in the wars with the Ottomans, the conclusion in December 1791 of the Yas peace treaty between Russia and Turkey led to the rapid settlement of the Ochakov Territory with settlers from Russian provinces, Bulgarian, Armenian, German colonists.





On the territory of this region, which became the southwestern border of the Russian Empire, Tsarina Catherine II ordered Count A.V. Suvorov to organize the Dniester defense line and lay the Middle Fortress. The construction of fortifications was carried out in 1792 according to the plan of the Russian engineer of French origin F. De Volan. The first garrison of the fortress under construction was a detachment of 172 Cossacks led by the future hero of the Patriotic War of 1812, Matvey Platov. It was De Volan who proposed to Count Suvorov to place the Tiraspol fortress opposite the Turkish Bender fortress, which is located on the right bank of the Dniester. Since January 1795, the name of the city - Tiraspol is mentioned in official documents.

Where is the Middle Fortress located?

Traces of historical buildings from the time of Alexander Suvorov can be found in the western part of the city. Borodino Square is located on the site of the central parade ground of the fortress, a monument to Russian Field Marshal M. I. Kutuzov is erected. The surviving powder cellar and parts of the earthen rampart of the Tiraspol fortress (Srednina) have been restored and are open for tourists. You can see the layout of the fortification structure, listen to the story of the erection, the history on the territory of the fortress itself or in the Tiraspol Museum of Local Lore.
