Exact sciences - what they are



What are exact sciences? Is it possible to give some clear definitions to them if any knowledge has its own structure and characteristic features, its own theories, laws, and basic concepts. Along with this, the exact sciences are now called those areas in the context of which exact laws are studied (in a quantitative sense), while rigorous and established methods are used that test hypotheses. They are based on logical reasoning, which are then reproduced in experiments. Needless to say, that science and technology are interconnected so much that one without the other practically does not exist. And the reason for this is that this type of science involves more precise measurements, which contribute to the movement of technological progress.

1. Mathematics - in its context, all its conditional sections (geometry, trigonometry and others) are taken into account. The exact sciences include natural:

1. Physics - this science studies the fundamental and general laws that determine the structure of the material and rational world. All its laws underlie natural science, which, in turn, is based on mathematics. But physics can also be called a rather conditional exact science, if you "find fault" with the fact that all research in the category of this knowledge was carried out and formulated under ideal conditions.

2. Chemistry is the most extensive field that studies substances, their basic properties, structure and transformations that occur as a result of various chemical reactions. Just like any other exact sciences, chemistry is based on the fundamental laws to which all studies obey.

3. Biology is an exact science that studies living beings and the features of their interaction with the outside world. All this obeys the laws of chemistry and physics, but the main area for study is directly those aspects of life that include the evolution, growth, structure and functioning of various biological creatures.
