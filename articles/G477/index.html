The only one on the island

And there is something to see. The Vasileostrovskaya metro station alone can become a place for tourists to visit. This station of the Nevsko-Vasilievskaya line is considered one of the deepest - located at a depth of 64 meters. It was commissioned on November 3, 1967 and still remains the only station on Vasilyevsky Island.

Hence the name, although earlier, at the design stages, there were several options: ‚ÄúMiddle Avenue‚Äù, ‚ÄúEighth Line‚Äù. The reason is simple - it is at the intersection of these streets that the station lobby is located.

But on the 7th line near house 34 there is a monument to the scorer-lieutenant Vasily, in whose honor the island was named. According to historical legends, Tsar Peter I personally signed orders to the artillery battery with a note: "Vasily to the island." It is worth mentioning that the action took place in the days of the defense of Russian borders from the Swedes.

And since the station on the island turned out to be the only one for the entire historical building, the name was given to it the only possible one - the Vasileostrovskaya metro station.

Restructuring

Now the passenger traffic on average per month is about 2 million people. This was not in the calculations of architects and builders. For this reason, during rush hours in the Vasileostrovskaya metro station it is a bit crowded. But now experts are working on it.

The interior of the station is concise, convenient - the walls are dressed in white granite, the floor is paved with gray granite inlaid with an aluminum profile, the platform and tunnel leading to the escalators are decorated with blue-green smalt. Convenient wooden benches for tired travelers were installed along the walls.

By City Day in 2016, Metrostroy prematurely completed the repair of the Vasileostrovskaya metro station. The mechanisms of escalators were debugged, drainage umbrellas were replaced with structures made of modern reinforced materials.

The outer lobby was redone, and now there are wide comfortable doors for passengers with limited mobility, all the old obsolete structures were replaced: massive iron supports and a visor, and stained-glass windows and windows were replaced. The color scheme of the Vasileostrovskaya metro station was also corrected - a yellow-canary, more saturated color is noticeable from afar.
