Sergey Shipov: calling and perseverance

Sergey Shipov is a well-known commentator, trainer and writer in the post-Soviet space. He is also a professional chess player, for which he received the title of expert in this field.

Sergey was born in Murom, in the Vladimir region. His family decided to move to Kirzhach, where they started teaching the boy how to play chess. This section turned out to be really good, because, apart from Sergey Shipov, Vladimir Belov and Dmitry Lavrik, who are famous not only in Russia and the post-Soviet countries, but also abroad, left the hands of these teachers. These professionals, including Sergey, are appreciated all over the world.

Shipov was able to prove himself in early childhood, participating in all kinds of youth tournaments. A young chess player was quickly noticed in Moscow, where he was invited to a boarding school, which was aimed at educating real grandmasters. Here Sergey Shipov studied and lived with Yevgeny Bareev and Yuri Dokhoyan, they also achieved great success in this intellectual game.



How Shipov began his chess career

At first, Sergei chose the path of study, decided to get an education, going to study science at the Physics Department of Moscow State University. The collapse of the USSR and the corresponding decline in domestic science contributed to this, after which the chess player decided to build his life around this game.

It took Sergei only a year to get the title of master, because in every tournament he showed the highest class of the game, many envied his level.
