Museum of the Tula gingerbread. Tula City: Attractions

The city of samovars, gunsmiths, accordions and gingerbread, the homeland of lefties - the Russian land of masters. All this is the city of Tula, whose museums and attractions are located in its historical center. The city is extremely convenient for travelers: it is three hours drive from the capital and stands on the railway and road routes of the southern direction of Russia.

Tula Kremlin

It is somewhat different from other Russian similar buildings. The Kremlin is not standing behind the river, but in front of it. It is hard to say that its architecture is amazing.

However, as part of the city‚Äôs fortification system, it was one of the most powerful Russian fortresses. In the entire history of its existence, the Tula Kremlin has never been taken by enemies. Even during the years of the Bolotnikov uprising, only after flooding it, they managed to oust the rebels from the city.

The shape of the Kremlin is quite simple. It is built in the shape of a quadrangle. The foundation with the lower part is made of white stone, and the top is made of brick. The length of the walls is a little more than one kilometer, and the height is ten meters. The Kremlin stands on a base assembled from oak piles. They have a foundation built that goes eight meters deep. Today, all nine towers of the fortress are preserved, of which four are round, and the rest are square.

Weapons museum

In 1989, in the building of the former local cathedral, it was decided to place the collection, exhibits for which the Tula arms factory has been collecting for many years. It is interesting that Peter the Great commanded so, having even issued a decree about it. Today, thanks to this wise decision, tourists have the opportunity to see the most interesting samples created by gunsmiths, from Peter the Great to the present day.



It is believed that it was in Tula that the most complete collection in our country was put on public display. In the museum you can trace the evolution of Russian weapons.

However, not only samples of Tula masters are presented here. The museum also has firearms, which were created in England, Japan, France, Iran, Turkey, Belgium, Germany and other countries. The exhibits from the Royal Guns collection are especially admired.

Assumption Cathedral

It was erected in the center of the Kremlin made of stone. Its foundation dates back to 1628-1629 years. Initially, the Assumption Cathedral was built as a summer temple. Currently, this building is of interest not only in terms of architecture, but also for its artistic value. Among the paintings here you can see rare paintings that depict Ecumenical Cathedrals. In 2012, restoration work began on the bell tower of the Assumption Cathedral. It was decided to completely restore the belfry, which once stood up seventy meters. And twenty-two bells decorated it.



Epiphany Cathedral

If the Assumption Cathedral held services only in the summer, then in this temple they were performed year-round. It was built much later - in 1865, in memory of soldiers who died in World War II. In 1892, it was considered unique, because water heating was carried out in it, which was very rare for Russia in those years. But then, in the Soviet years, the Epiphany Cathedral was placed at the disposal of the flying club, and after that - to athletes.

How to cook Tula gingerbread

No one will undertake to say who and when first made it. The first references to the Tula gingerbread are stored in the museum scribe book of 1685. To our days, special boards have been preserved, on which contemporaries can judge the variety of carriages. They were carved from birch or pear. The tree must be at least thirty years old. For the gingerbread board, only the lower part of the barrel was used, which was cut into pieces about five centimeters thick and dried for almost twenty years.

The edges of the board for the strength of the master were smeared with wax or resin. After the tree was ready, the carver-artist began to apply the drawing. Such boards, in essence, were the form on which the Tula gingerbread was baked.

Description

The first gingerbread in Russia was called "honey bread". They were brought to the Russian land by the Vikings. It happened in the 9th century. It was then in Russia that they also learned what yeast pancakes and dried fruit blasts are. At that time, gingerbread cookies were rye flour dough mixed in berry juice and honey, with the last ingredient making up almost half of the total volume.

Later, roots and forest herbs began to be added to honey bread. And already in the XII-XIII centuries, when various exotic spices began to be brought to the Russian land from the Middle East and India, the gingerbread, having received its current name, almost completely formed into that amazing delicacy that everyone knows today.

Taste variety depended on the dough, its composition, the method of preparation and baking. Spices and additives, which in the old days were called "dry perfumes", played a significant role. Among the most popular were lemon, black pepper, mint, Italian dill, vanilla, orange peel, as well as ginger, caraway seeds, anise, nutmeg and cloves.

By the end of the XVIII century, Tula gingerbread was already known in Perm and Arkhangelsk, in Kursk and Kharkov, in Kaluga, Novgorod, etc. Gradually, they began to establish their own production of delicious mats. Tver gingerbread men had shops in Paris, Berlin and London.
