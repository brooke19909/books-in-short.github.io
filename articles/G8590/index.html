The collapse of the USSR: external influence or internal conspiracy?

Officially, the collapse of the USSR, the date of which fell on December 8, 1991, was issued on the territory of the Bialowieza Forest, and more specifically - in the Viskuli estate. Then the Russian, Ukrainian and Belarusian leaders put their signatures on the Agreement, according to which the Commonwealth of Independent States was created . A little later, on December 21, eight more former republics joined them. Thus, the collapse of the USSR occurred 69 years after its founding.



Now there is no unanimous opinion on the main reasons for the collapse of the Union . Some suggest that this was facilitated by the fall in oil prices on world markets, which was initiated by the US government. Others believe that the betrayal of Mikhail Gorbachev led to this. Still others believe that what happened is the fruit of public discontent with the authoritarian method of government in the country and the protracted socio-economic crisis. It is also widely believed that the collapse of the USSR happened due to a number of technological disasters and military-political defeats of the state. There are other assumptions.



Conspiracy theory today is very widespread. According to her, the collapse of one of the most powerful countries in the world occurred as a result of the consistent and lengthy work of the intelligentsia, which was opposed to the state. Proponents of another theory are sure that it was the Americans who provoked the collapse of the USSR. This process, in their sincere conviction, was intended primarily to change ideological policy. Representatives of the domestic intelligentsia, who were the advanced detachment of everything new, were radically tuned and focused on Western states. In the mid-eighties of the last century, these people in their activities sought to revise historical events, giving them a different meaning. For example, the socialist revolution of 1917 was called a coup. Many believed that they were right.



However, many historians do not agree with the fact that the collapse of the USSR was initiated by external factors. They are led to such a conclusion by the fact that the Soviet political elite, beginning in the mid-sixties, less and less believed in their official ideology and gradually turned into admirers of bourgeois values. Thus, it is very difficult to say for sure that one of the above reasons is the one that caused the collapse of the USSR. There is no doubt that the answer to this question should be approached comprehensively. In other words, the collapse of such a powerful state, most likely, led to the totality of all these events.
