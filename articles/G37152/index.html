Define a tool

There are no strict recommendations regarding what to work with knitting needles or crochet. The product can be made with both the one and the other tool, and even with two options at once - it all depends on the imagination. However, if a novice master plans to tie a sweater with a lowered shoulder, it is wiser to use knitting needles. At the same time, experts give important advice regarding their size.

For a lace or a complex pattern, it is better to choose a tool that is equal in diameter to the thickness of the thread. We get yarn

Another important point that also determines the beauty of the intended product. The most important thing that experienced craftswomen emphasize is not to try to include in the knitted sweater with a lowered shoulder (or another thing) all the known patterns, the types of yarn you like and the available decor elements.

To make the product really spectacular, you should think about the features of knitting in advance. That is, decide what the focus will be on - on yarn, pattern or decor. Inexperienced needlewomen it is better to experiment with yarn - to purchase a patchwork or gradient. As the main pattern, choose an elastic band, garter or hosiery. If a jacket with a deflated sleeve will be framed with lace, it is better to purchase a plain yarn. You can bright or even acid color.

And if the highlight of the sweater is an interesting decor, it is recommended to knit a simple thing. For example, facial stitch and plain yarn.

Take measurements

Knitting any thing is a completely creative process. Rather, the needlewoman can navigate through ready-made master classes and instructions, however, it should be borne in mind that they are designed for a particular person, taking into account the features of his appearance, physique and even age.

Therefore, experienced needlewomen still do not advise beginners to walk along the beaten path. Better to pave your way, because it is not difficult!

Before you start knitting sweaters with a lowered shoulder, it is necessary to take measurements from the person for whom the product is intended. For this:

  1. Prepare a measuring tape, a piece of paper and a pencil.
  2. Invite a model. She should be in tight clothes or underwear - as comfortable.
  3. Measure the circumference of the shoulders. Please note: not the width, but the girth! In order to get the correct value, the model should stand straight, hands pressed to the sides.
  4. Then determine the length of the jacket (from the shoulder to the lower edge), the length of the sleeves (from the shoulder to the cuff), the level of the armhole (from the lower edge to the armpit) and the circumference of the hips (jacket should be free).

How to tie a sweater with low shoulders?

When the hardest part is over, you can get to work. The back and before starting to knit separately, reaching the level of the armhole they should be connected, adding air loops and thereby gaining loops for the sleeves. But first things first:

  1. Type 1/2 of the hips on the knitting needles.
  2. Tie a flat cloth, the length of which is equal to the distance from the bottom edge to the armpits, tear off the thread, do not close the loops.
  3. Put the part aside and, by analogy, tie the same, but do not break the thread, it should continue knitting.
  4. Next, calculate the number of loops needed for the sleeve. Since you should increase the width of the fabric to 1/2 of the circumference of the shoulders, determine the number of additional ones according to the formula: P * = 1/2 of the circumference of the shoulders - 1/2 of the width of the hips.
  5. Now you need to connect the back and front together. To do this, extend the canvas one row and add the calculated number of air loops (P *).
  6. Next, carefully knit 1 loop into 2 parts. Please note - it already fits in as usual, and does not act like a hem. Tie the row to the end.
  7. After that add more air loops, the number of which is also equal to the value of P *.
  8. So, you connected the back and before together, added loops for the sleeves and got the total number of loops equal to the circumference of the shoulders. Next, you just need to knit in a circle, reaching the desired length of the product. The only clarification is that in the last three rows it is better to make a 1 x 1 gum.
  9. Take the hook, on the armhole, dial the loops, transfer to the knitting needles and knit (in a circle) the sleeve to the desired length (parameter A in the diagram above), at the end also adding a single elastic band. By analogy, make a second sleeve.

That's all! The base of the product is ready. You just have to add the side seams, connecting the front and back.
