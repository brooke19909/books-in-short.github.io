Imperial family

Emperors of Japan are considered the descendants of the great deity who illuminates the heavens - Amaterasu. Their position on the throne is so strong that they do not need a surname. Divine origin led to the fact that rivals on the throne of the representatives of the imperial dynasty never had. To this day, emperors, except in Japan, are no longer in any country. Only Japonia retained the titles. Emperors Akihito and Hirohito are representatives of the dynasty, which has not been interrupted since 660 BC. True, the reign of the first sixteen emperors is based only on legends. Emperor Akihito has three attributes of power - a mirror, a sword, and a jasper seal. They are passed on by the father to his son when the prince takes office. Emperor Akihito received them in 1989.

Emperor power

Starting from the XII century, the power of the emperors is only formal. Now Japan is a constitutional monarchy, and Akihito, the emperor of Japan, does not have real powers. He, according to the constitution, is only a symbol of the country, like a coat of arms, flag and anthem. Family life

Prince Tsigunomiya married in 1959, breaking the thousand-year tradition, on the girl Michiko Shde, who did not belong to the aristocratic society.

She was the daughter of a very wealthy and influential businessman, an intelligent person whose family members were awarded the Order of Merit in the Field of Culture. The girl received a brilliant both Japanese and Western education. She became a bachelor of arts majoring in English Literature. She has free English, she plays the piano, in her youth she was actively involved in sports and met the prince on the court. Members of the imperial family did not approve of the alleged marriage, but the community supported the young people. The wedding was traditional and televised.
