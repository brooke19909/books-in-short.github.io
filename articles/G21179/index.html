The capital of Swaziland. Cultural and administrative capital.

Swaziland is a wonderful and amazing state located in southern Africa. The African continent is still largely a mystery to European residents.





Swaziland is a state that combines the traditions of Aboriginal and Europeans. Locals, swazi, are held in high esteem. The government is doing everything to ensure that their family flourishes for as long as possible, and does not disappear from the face of the Earth. As for religion, the majority of local residents still adhere to Christianity, and not traditional beliefs for swazi. However, both religions are equally respected.

Swaziland has two capitals, and so far it is difficult to single out the more official of them. The capital of Swaziland Mbabane has administrative significance. Here is a government building, most banks, various business institutions. It is impossible to say unequivocally that it will be interesting for a tourist to stay here, however it is still worth a walk along the incredibly beautiful streets. Certain kinds of sights can be considered the modern streets of Mbabane Alley, New Alley and Allister Miller. Of particular interest is the history of the emergence of Allister Miller Street. She was named after the first European born in Swaziland. His homeland was the capital of Swaziland Mbabane.





In addition, it is worth visiting Swazi Square - a huge shopping complex. There you can buy a variety of gizmos for a moderate fee. By the way, goods in Swaziland are much cheaper than in other African countries.

Also in Mbabane is the National University.
