Optimal resolution

Just like for a laptop or smartphone, the resolution of the TV refers to the number of dots or pixels that make up the screen. The more pixels, the sharper the screen. Screen size also affects sharpness, because the same number of pixels on a large display will not look so clear. In 2018, the vast majority of sets have a resolution of 3840 by 2160 pixels, a size better known as 4K.

Some companies use the term Ultra HD instead, but this is essentially the same. If you purchase a small TV, say, less than 40 inches, then you should not invest in 4K resolution, because this simply will not be noticeable. However, for something more, 4K is the current gold standard. While 8K is only "on the way" to the buyer and will not be soon on the market. Therefore, the best 40-inch TVs with 4K resolution will please the user for many years to come.

Display technology

Today‚Äôs television screens rely on two competing technologies: the ubiquitous LED LCD and the more expensive OLED. Trying to say which one is best can be challenging, especially because manufacturers add their own settings and proprietary technologies on top of each platform. In addition, as both technologies continue to evolve, they improve their strengths and minimize weaknesses. Both types of screens use LEDs for lighting, but in different ways.

An LED LCD, white LED, or LED pixels are used as backlight to form an image through a liquid crystal display or LCD filter. LEDs usually cover the entire screen, but cheaper sets sometimes limit them to just the edges of the display. LED LCDs are generally sharper than OLEDs, and they also provide more natural control of the light on the screen.

On the other hand, an organic LED or OLED screens let in the backlight and instead individual pixels light up one by one using electric current. This allows the screen to respond faster to input changes. In addition, since the black pixel is turned off completely and not obscured, OLED displays tend to have better contrast than LED LCDs. Finally, they are more expensive to manufacture, which usually leads to higher costs for the best 40 and 43 inch televisions.

Sometimes the common attributes of LED LCDs and OLED screens can provide additional innovation from TV manufacturers in the form of paired technology. Samsung‚Äôs Quantum Dot technology, for example, makes LED LCD screens clean, just like OLED displays. Thanks to these changes, the best LED LCD panels and the best OLED devices stand the same. To really feel the difference between the screens, it is better to make a trip to the nearest electronics store and test them with your own eyes.

Sound and Wireless

Speakers are the main victim of thin TVs. The best 40-inch budget televisions come with two 10 W (RMS) loudspeakers, which typically consume more than 70% of the power. If you need high-quality sound, it is recommended to invest in a home theater or a separate speaker system. Before that, you need to check whether the TV has an optical or SPDIF / coaxial audio output, ideal for multi-channel connection. If a TV with these ports is not affordable for the buyer, you can use the more common 3.5 mm audio jack (headphones), to which most models connect.

Connecting to the Internet on a TV has several advantages: you can access multimedia content, save a tab on your social networks, and in some cases even make video calls. First, you will need to check whether the TV has built-in Wi-Fi or supports external USB keys for connection. This will allow you to connect your home Wi-Fi network and streaming audio / video content on the TV. Most smart TVs come with DLNA or Miracast support, which allows you to stream video from your smartphone / tablet directly to the TV.

Matrix type

An important aspect when choosing the technical characteristics of the best televisions 40 and 43 inches is the type of matrix. In LCD TVs, you can find two types of matrices: VA and IPS. The first of them - the VA matrix - is characterized by a higher contrast, a little deeper than black, and a shorter reaction time compared to the IPS solution. The disadvantage of this technology is the attenuation of colors when viewed at an angle. When it comes to IPS technology, there are deeper colors and wider viewing angles. The disadvantages of the IPS solution are generally relatively less contrast compared to VA.

Manufacturers rarely provide publicly available detailed specifications in which we can only find the type of matrix used. On LG TVs, we usually find the IPS solutions mentioned above. The company is the largest manufacturer. VA matrices are most often found in Samsung devices. When choosing a specific TV model, you also need to pay attention to a number of additional functions.

Currently, the most interesting solution that extends the capabilities of the device is the Smart TV function, thanks to which the TV will be transformed into a kind of multimedia center that provides the ability to browse websites, use e-mail and social networks. Another aspect is the energy efficiency of the device. Currently, the basis of energy is the energy class A +. It is worth paying attention to this parameter, especially when the TV works for many hours a day.
