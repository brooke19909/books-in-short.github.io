Inverter type washing machine motor

The basis of the action of this motor is that the speed control is carried out by an inverter or a frequency converter. It forms an alternating current of the desired frequency. Thus, the rotation speed and the required speed are maintained at the required level.





The quality that the inverter motor of a washing machine is good is the lack of brushes in it. The rotation of the rotor occurs due to the electromagnetic field.

Benefits

Based on the characteristics of a washing machine with an inverter motor, the following advantages are distinguished:

  • since there are no brushes or rubbing parts in the engine, less energy is required for rotation, which increases the efficiency and saves electricity;
  • missing parts will not initially have to be changed during operation;
  • the engine is characterized by reduced noise;
  • set speeds are precisely supported throughout the work cycle.

Energy classes for washing machines

So, if saving electric energy is an important indicator when choosing a washing machine, then you need to look not at the presence or absence of inverter technology, but at the energy consumption classes. They are indicated by Latin letters in alphabetical order, the first of which (with two pluses ‚ÄúA ++‚Äù) is evidence of the most economical machine. Class G, by contrast, can eat the most energy.

So, for example, class A ++ consumes electricity of less than 0.15 kW, and G - more than 0.39.

In addition to the class, energy consumption is affected by:

  • washing program and temperature - the higher the temperature and the longer the selected program, the more energy will be required;
  • congestion - the more laundry is placed in the drum, the higher the consumption;
  • type of fabric - dry and wet weight is often very different;
  • duration of operation - the more time you use the device, the more energy it needs.
