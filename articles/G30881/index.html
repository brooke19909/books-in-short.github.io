Alesund location

So, let's first talk a little about Norway itself, about the location of the country and this city.

Norway is an incredibly beautiful country, with magnificent landscapes and unusual architecture. Norway is, first of all, a unique nature: green forests, mountains, lakes, rivers and, of course, the northern lights. This country is well developed economically, due to the high taxes that go into the improvement of streets, parks and houses, it is very clean, beautiful and comfortable. The standard of living is high, despite the weather, medicine in Norway is free, even the most complex and expensive operations are paid by the state.

So, Norway is the northernmost European country, it is located in the northwest of the Scandinavian peninsula, this state borders on Russia, Sweden and Finland. Norway is washed by two oceans (Arctic and Atlantic) and two seas (Northern and Barents). Most of the country is a variety of mountain ranges, forests and lakes.

The city of Alesund (Norway) is located on the west coast of the country, in the county of Mere og Romsdal. The city is located on seven small islands, the city center is located on two islands, and residential areas are located on two more.

Alesund's story

Let us now turn to the history of the city. It is worth saying right away that Alesund (Norway) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. By the way, this is one of the few remaining cities in the world built in the style of Art Nouveau.

This ancient city was almost completely burned in a fire in 1904, because most of the houses were built of wood. By the way, only one person died during the fire, but 10,000 people were left homeless. For three years the city was completely rebuilt, the construction took place under the guidance of the famous architect of the time, who adored the Art Nouveau style. It is because of this architecture that a huge number of tourists come to the city. By the way, the city center is new and modern.

Alesund Museum

So, now we will talk about the sights of Alesund in Norway. Our list opens with the Alesund Museum, in which you can see how the city looked before the famous fire in 1904.

The museum is located in a beautiful building, which is located near the city center. There really is something to see here, because Alesund was built in the 10th century, and all information about its history is stored here.

Art Nouveau Center

In Alesund (Norway), there is the famous Art Nouveau center, which tells in detail about what the decor looks like, the rooms inside, made in this style.

In fact, this center is very interesting, it is a great place for those who like to plunge into history and culture. The center is located on three floors, there are both temporary multimedia exhibitions and permanent ones, for example, "Craft and Architecture". In addition to information about the Art Nouveau style, here you can find information about the style of Europe as a whole. By the way, there is a cozy cafe nearby where you can have a bite to eat. Guidebooks suggest paying attention to coffee, delicious waffles, locally made preserves and homemade pies.

The center operates from September to May, every day, except Tuesday, from 11:00 to 16:00, in the summer this place is open daily from 10:00 to 17:00.

Atlantic park

This is an incredibly interesting place, which is located 3 km from the city of Alesund. It is believed that this is one of the largest aquariums in all of Scandinavia. The park is located in a picturesque location near the ocean. The park offers stunning views of the islands and the ocean.

This park was built a long time ago, back in 1988. Here you can find a lot of information about the flora and fauna of the fjords. In addition, you can see animals of the deep sea. You can watch them through the large panoramic windows of the aquariums. By the way, water is pumped directly from the ocean. It is best to visit the park at one in the afternoon, at which time workers feed the fish.
