Interesting facts about Hitler. The death of the Fuhrer

April 20, 1889 Adolf Hitler was born into a Catholic family in a small town in the Austro-Hungarian Empire. His father was a customs officer. As a widower, he married a cousin, which made their marriage an incestuous union. However, interesting facts about Hitler do not end there.

Childhood

In the early years of Hitler, his family often moved due to the fact that his father was transferred from place to place. At 8, he becomes a servant in a monastery, sings in a church choir. He admires the self-sacrifice of priests and is inspired by a sense of vocation.





He notices an unusual cross on the facade of the building. Having changed, this cross will turn into a swastika - the emblem of the Nazi party. Hitler will always remember this cross at the monastery as a fateful omen.

Interesting facts about Hitler before he came to power

At the age of 19, after the death of his mother, the closest person, he went to Vienna. He still does not think about the career of a politician and so far only dreams of entering an art academy. However, despite all the ambitions, he is not accepted into the academy. Mobilization. Hitler is 25 years old. And this day, August 25, 1914, will fundamentally change the life of an unsuccessful artist. Previously, to avoid military service, he left Austria. He is now in Germany, in Munich, where he was seized by a seething whirlpool of nationalist sentiments.

Military service

Speaking about this famous man, one cannot fail to mention interesting facts about Hitler in those years when he was just starting his military career. The young man went to war, and the war changed him. For 4 years, Corporal Hitler served as a courier. On the night of December 30, 1918, during a gas attack, he experiences temporary blindness. He is evacuated to a field hospital, and then to Germany.





At this time, an event happened in Hitler's life, which he would call a turning point in his fate a little later. He lies in the hospital, and his vision gradually begins to return. Hitler again goes to the front and serves in the army with great zeal. He becomes an informant, informing his comrades whom he suspects of having connections with the Reds. Higher officers pay attention to his anti-revolutionary ardor. They instruct Hitler to organize the political education of German soldiers returning from captivity.

The path to the heights of power

The German army is reduced to 100 thousand people. Hitler against his will demobilized. He ends up in Bavaria, where extremist movements flourish, and becomes the main political agitator of the German workers' party. In August 1920, Hitler made his political debut with a lecture entitled "Why Are We Anti-Semites." July 1921 - Hitler activates the radicals of his small ultra-right extremist group. They proclaim him a leader and buy him a car as an attribute of a high position.

Hitler changes the name of the party, combining the words nationalism and socialism. His goal is to attract as many supporters as possible and show his anti-capitalist attitude. Party members were called Nazis. In his small headquarters, Hitler draws the emblem of the party - the red flag as an indicator of the socialist orientation of the movement, the white circle - a symbol of nationalism and the swastika - a symbol of the Aryan race and eternal anti-Semitism. From this moment, the long path of the conqueror begins, and interesting facts about Hitler are revealed.
