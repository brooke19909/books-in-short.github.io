Description of Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160)

The rounded, impressive small smartphone with an attractive 3.8 ‚Äùcapacitive WVGA touch screen with a resolution of 800 x 480 pixels is pleasant in appearance, despite the fact that he still could not stand out from a number of competitors. To everyone‚Äôs disappointment, despite the fact that the phone was introduced and entered the market much later than the release of the Android 4.0 OS, the Korean manufacturer chose to use the operating system version 2.3, on the platform of which the original Galaxy Ace also worked. Design

In the 2nd generation of the model, the manufacturer made a comprehensive overhaul, installed a 2-core processor with a frequency of 800 MHz, increased the RAM from 278 to a much more respectable 768 MB. In addition, the smartphone is equipped with a 5-megapixel rear camera with autofocus and built-in LED flash. Also, it became possible to record 720p high-definition video, which allowed the phone to be attributed to middle-class models. A second VGA camera is installed on the front of the model.

The smartphone has a comfortable weight of 122 g and a thickness of 10.5 mm. The characteristics of the Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160) can be improved by expanding the amount of permanent memory by 32 GB using a microSD card. It also offers a standard set of 3G, Bluetooth and Wi-Fi wireless communications, and optionally GPS and HSDPA.

Despite the fact that compared to the first generation model, the screen size of the smartphone has increased from 3.5 to 3.8 inches, the smartphone has become thinner by 1 mm, narrowing to a relatively slim 10.5 mm in its widest part.





And although the model uses a plastic construction, it is a surprisingly strong and well-built device that only bends a little or creaks with great effort.

With the increase in size, the Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160) mobile phone gained weight compared to its predecessor, albeit slightly. Video

Despite the low price and not entirely revolutionary specifications, the video recording on the Samsung GT-I8160 Galaxy Ace II, according to the owners, is characterized by excellent speed, excellent color reproduction and a large number of customizable functions. The smartphone‚Äôs camera is capable of shooting 720p HD video with a frame rate of 30 fps and creating video content without heavy pixelation, blurry movements and weak sound. The built-in LED flash, which can be used to illuminate dark scenes, further extends the video capabilities of a mobile phone, and a set of filters allows you to see the footage in classic black and white, sepia or negative display.

Despite its strengths, the video shot by the Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160) camera, according to customer reviews, is not without drawbacks. With a lack of contrast and color depth of competitors of a higher class, the phone gives a result that suffers from large color blocks, and sometimes the content is more segmented than in real life.

Reviews of the Samsung Galaxy Ace II smartphone (GT-I8160) note the quality of the built-in microphone, which can even catch background noises that add depth and atmosphere to the video without distracting from the main subject. However, like almost all telephone video cameras, in areas with strong winds, distortion of the recorded sound can be a nuisance, since there is no noise reduction system that can prevent it.

Multimedia

Despite the fact that the second generation Galaxy Ace offers its own music audio and video player, it is by no means a phone designed and made to play multimedia content. Rather, it is designed for random playback, rather than constant tough use as a complete replacement for a personal media player.

The maximum amount of ROM smartphone is 4 GB, which is much more than 158 MB of built-in storage of its predecessor. However, only 1.1 GB remains to store applications and data files. Those who want to have large amounts of media content on hand can use the additional microSD memory card up to 32 GB.

A pre-installed MP3 player can be accessed from the centralized application menu or via a shortcut on one of the phone‚Äôs 7 home screens. Battery life

Having surpassed the one-day milestone, which was the limit for most smartphones in 2012, the Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160) lithium-ion battery with a capacity of 1,500 mAh will not keep the user under stress for a hard working day or less weekend with telephone calls, providing 7, 5 hours of estimated talk time and 640 hours of standby time. According to the reviews of the owners, the figures indicated by the manufacturer correctly reflect the reality.

Although the 2-core filling provides a more aggressive and energy-demanding environment for the battery, an increase in battery capacity from 1350 mAh in the original model compensated for the increase in power consumption. A negative consequence of this is a significant increase in charging time.

Connectivity options

In this regard, the smartphone Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160) is a classic example of the uniformity of the models of the Korean manufacturer and a wide range of Android devices, without offering any unexpected additions or omissions.

The usual set of options from 3G and impressive Wi-Fi are backed up by an HSDPA connection at 14.4 Mbps. In addition, Bluetooth 3.0 and the microUSB port are alternative communication methods. The same universal serial data bus is used to synchronize the phone with a PC, charge it, or to transfer files. The smartphone can also act as a wireless access point for other devices that support Wi-Fi.

Advantages

User reviews of the Samsung Galaxy Ace II mobile phone (GT-I8160) note the elegant and stylish design of the device with excellent build quality and pleasant tactile sensations reminiscent of the former market leader Galaxy S2. The smartphone impresses with an elegant finish and a wide range of functions. Since the 2-core processor provides realistic lightness, the integrated 5-megapixel camera has become a hit, equally capable of creating high-quality images and impressive video with a resolution of 720p. In addition, the smartphone is easy to use and not difficult to master.

disadvantages

The attractive simplicity of an inexpensive phone only brightens up the disappointment in the decision of the Korean manufacturer to install an outdated version of the OS. Although not bad, due to the lack of new OS update features, the device seems outdated. The disadvantages of the Samsung Galaxy Ace II (GT-I8160) are long-term charging and, despite the presence of a microSD card slot, a small amount of internal memory.

Conclusion

With the launch of the Galaxy Ace II smartphone (GT-I8160), Samsung has reliably established itself in the mid-range cell phone market, setting a price close to the budget level that many have liked. Not offering the most advanced media device, the Korean company compensates for the small flaws and limited capabilities of the model by improving the proposed functionality. Exceeding expectations in many positions, the Galaxy Ace II smartphone is a good start for beginners.
