Cappadocia





How often do we hear the words-stamps "lunar landscape", trying on rocky terrain, with its original shape and almost devoid of vegetation. There are such corners on Earth.

They are truly original, like Cappadocia, a unique area located in Antalya. Its vertical, pointed upward stone rock pillars, formed over millions of years by erosion of rocks, really leave an impression on the audience.

But the creation of nature is not the only "trick" of Cappadocia. There are also man-made miracles here - vast underground temples - creations of the hands of the first Christians.

In addition, the locals were so assimilated to the stony environment that they began to carve their houses in the rocks.

Pamukkale

The most popular excursions in Antalya are associated with visits by tourists to historical monuments (and there are plenty of them in the territory of Asia Minor, as Turkey was once called). However, modern resort infrastructure has updated another type of excursions. Visits to entertainment and shopping centers are popular with Concordia Celes Hotels 5 * spa residents.

Being in Turkey and not visiting Pamukkale is unacceptable for a real tourist. Did you hear about the Cleopatra Basin? So there it is.

Pamukkale ("Cotton Castle") attracts the eyes from afar with its contrasting color, which clearly distinguishes it from the surrounding landscape. By the will of nature, a whole cascade of elegant travertine baths has formed here with healing mineral water with bubbles of different temperatures. Of course, you should go here, taking a bathing suit with you.

For tourists - this is also a chance to introduce themselves as ancient residents. Indeed, in the largest bath, called the Cleopatra Basin, the Romans erected baths, using the water evenly thrown out by the local geyser. They just built their building on top of it. Unfortunately, the antique frame of the pool (with porticoes and columns) has not been preserved in its entirety until our time. But tourists can see the remains of columns destroyed by the earthquake.

And more sights ...





In the territory of modern Turkey, there were many civilizations. Back in the Middle Ages, Byzantium was located here - a country of the Orthodox faith, in its state structure somewhat reminiscent of the modern Vatican. Until our time, the Byzantine fortress Ich-Kale has been fairly well preserved. Her powerful five-meter fortress fence remained intact. True, the internal buildings of this single architectural complex - churches, towers, pools - are partially destroyed by earthquakes.

Fans of stalactite caves will be able to enjoy the overview of the magnificent structure created by nature itself - the Dalmatash grotto. Constant temperature, high humidity and modern accentuating lighting turn this creation of nature at the age of 15 thousand years into an unforgettable sight.
