Electric circuits, elements of electric circuits. Symbols of electric circuit elements

Electrical devices are very important in the life of a modern civilized person. But for their work it is necessary to comply with a number of requirements. As part of the article, we will carefully consider electrical circuits, elements of electrical circuits and how they function.

What is needed for the operation of an electrical device?

For its functioning, an electrical circuit must be created . Its task is to transfer energy to the device and provide the required mode of operation. What is called an electrical circuit?

So designate a set of objects and devices that form the path of current flow. In this case, electromagnetic processes can be described using knowledge of electric current, as well as those that offer electromotive force and voltage. Types of Elements

Conventionally, they can be divided into three groups:

  1. Power supplies. A feature of this type of element is that they can turn some kind of energy (most often chemical) into electrical energy. Two types of sources are distinguished: primary, when another type is converted into electrical energy, and secondary, which have electrical energy at the input and output (an example is a rectifier device).
  2. Energy consumers. They convert electric current into something else (lighting, heat).
  3. Auxiliary elements. This includes various components without which the real circuit will not work, such as switching equipment, connecting wires, measuring instruments, etc., similar in purpose.

All elements are covered by one electromagnetic process.

Circuit Elements

Symbols of electric circuit elements in the text version cannot be presented, therefore they are depicted in the photo. But still the narrative should be. So, it should be noted that the elements of the electric circuit are divided into passive and active. The first include, for example, connecting wires and power consumers.

The passive element of the electric circuit is characterized in that its presence under certain conditions can be neglected. What can not be said about his antipode. Active elements include those where EMF is induced (sources, electric motors, batteries, when they are charged, and so on). Important in this regard are the special details of the circuits that have resistance, which is characterized by a current-voltage dependence, since they mutually affect each other. When the resistance is constant regardless of the current or voltage, then this dependence looks like a straight segment. They are called linear elements of the electric circuit. But in most cases, the magnitude of the resistance is affected by both current and voltage. Last but not least this is due to the temperature parameter. So, when the element is heated, the resistance begins to increase. If this parameter is highly dependent, then the current-voltage characteristic is not the same at any point in the mental graph. Therefore, the element is called non-linear.

Laws that will be needed when working with DC circuits

Analysis and calculation will be much more effective if we simultaneously use Ohm's law, as well as the first and second laws of Kirchhoff.

With their help, it is possible to establish a relationship between those values ‚Äã‚Äãthat have currents, voltages, electric-electronic components along the entire electric circuit or in its individual sections. And this is all based on the parameters of the elements that enter them.

Ohm's law for a chain section

For us, the current strength (I), voltage (U) and resistance (R) are important. This law is expressed by the following formula: I = U / R. When calculating electrical circuits, it is sometimes more convenient to use the inverse value: R = I / U.

Ohm's law for the full chain

It determines the relationship that is established between the EMF (E) of a power source for which the internal resistance is r, current and the total equivalent of R. The formula looks like I = E / (r + R). A complex chain usually has several branches. Other power sources may be included. Then using Ohm's law to fully describe the process becomes problematic.

The first law of Kirchhoff

Any node of an electric circuit has an algebraic sum of currents, which is zero.

The currents that go to the node, in this case, are taken with a plus sign. Those that are directed from it are with a minus. The importance of this law is that with its help, a relationship is established between currents that are on different nodes.
