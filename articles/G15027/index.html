Sights of Balashikha: description, photo

Sights of Balashikha year after year attract tourists with its diversity. In the city, nicknamed the Eastern Gate of Moscow, there are estates with historical significance, ancient religious buildings, beautiful parks and squares. Many buildings were erected in the era of Catherine the Great. Where to start acquaintance with a large settlement near Moscow?

Sights of Balashikha: estates

First of all, the city is interesting for its many noble nests, which two centuries ago the landowners and princes actively erected here. It is not surprising that the sights of Balashikha are first and foremost magnificent estates. For example, you should definitely visit the former possession of Count Rumyantsev. Unfortunately, the Troitskoye-Kaynardzhi estate was partially destroyed. The working Trinity Church remained intact, the burial place of the former owner, interesting with a colorful jasper tombstone. The temple accepts the afflicted for about two centuries.





Sights of Balashikha are other grandiose buildings. Gorenka Manor became famous in the early years of the 19th century, after it was acquired by Razumovsky. This man was actively interested in rare plants, which prompted him to create a botanical garden. Plants that tourists can admire these days are represented by several thousand species, many of which belong to the category of exotic.

The estate Pehra-Yakovlevskoe will be of interest to those who want to see the most picturesque sights of Balashikha. It is located on the banks of the river, looks spectacular from afar. The main architectural value is the Savior Church, built in 1782.

What churches to visit

Naming the main attractions of Balashikha, it is impossible to forget about churches. One of the oldest religious buildings is the Church of the Intercession of the Virgin, which was first included in scribal books as early as 1624. Several years ago, the church survived the restoration, thanks to which its pre-revolutionary appearance was practically restored.





It is definitely worth seeing the Alexander Nevsky Church, the construction of which was completed in the last years of the 19th century. The construction was seriously damaged in Soviet times, but since the beginning of the current century, its restoration has been underway.

Finally, tourists may be interested in the Temple of St. Michael the Archangel. The style of construction refers to the direction of Baroque. It was created with the assistance of a descendant of Yuri Dolgorukov, this happened in the 18th century.

Interesting museums of the city

Manors and churches are far from all that Balashikha can deservedly be proud of. Attractions in Balashikha are also museums. It is reasonable to start acquaintance with the village with a visit to the Museum of Local History. A study of the exhibits presented in it will give valuable information about the history of the region, tell about how its population lives, starting from ancient times and ending with our century.





The Museum of the History of Air Defense Forces is another pride of Balashikha. Such a place cannot be found throughout Europe. Guests of the city who are interested in military equipment will enjoy his visit.

Balashikha art gallery is also loved by tourists. In addition to professional works of art, among its exhibits you can find the fruits of children's creativity, attractive naive charm.

Natural beauty

Sights of the city of Balashikha, created by mother nature, can not be ignored. Bezmenovsky quarry is a place where residents of this settlement like to gather, spending friendly picnics and swimming in the lake. It is worth to come here for the sake of picturesque views. The lake surface is beautifully framed by forests, the banks are covered with grass. By the way, a little stroll, you can get to neighboring reservoirs. Unfortunately, the beach belongs to the category of wild, there are no amenities.





Vishnyakovsky pond - a reservoir created about 300 years ago. Once upon a time there was a cloth factory. The pond is visited by locals who are fond of fishing. Tourists are primarily attracted by the beautiful landscapes created by nature.

Those who want to take a walk and breathe in fresh air can be recommended to stay at the Kuchinsky Forest Park, bordering Balashikha. It includes 8 forests, meets everyone since 1935. The area is about 2 thousand hectares.

Alleys, monuments

Visitors to the wars that died during the Second World War will be able to pay visitors to the Heroes Alley, bordering the Glory Square. You should definitely take a walk to Lenin Avenue, where tourists will find an original sculptural composition. This monument was erected directly on the Glory Square. The basis of the sculpture is a dead tree, from which the crown was removed. On the trunk carved children's silhouettes, admiring the flock of flying birds.





Visitors to the avenue will be able to see the Memorial Cross, created in 2000. Its establishment is associated with the destruction of the Alexander Nevsky Church, which was subsequently restored.

Humorous sights

Residents of the village are endowed with an excellent sense of humor. There is no other way to explain how the monument to the Janitor, erected in 2007, was included in the list of "Sights of the city of Balashikha." The description of the purpose of this building, which the creators themselves propose, is that the building extols the hard work of representatives of this profession. Near the monument to the Janitor, city guests loved taking pictures.

There are so many interesting places in Balashikha that it is impossible to explore them in one visit. Therefore, all visitors to the village are sure to return again.
