Michael Berriman - a cult maniac of the horror industry

During his life, actor Michael Berriman has starred in more than seventy films. This was made possible thanks to its specific appearance. For the same reason, most of his roles are related to horror films and science fiction. His heroes were mutants, monsters, cannibals and other negative characters.

The appearance that determined the career





Michael Barryman was born on September 4, 1948 in Los Angeles. His parents, Barabara and Sloane, specialized in neurosurgery. From birth, the boy discovered a rather rare disease, which he inherited. It is called anhydrotic ectoderm dysplasia.

From birth, he stood out from the rest with a specific appearance. He has no teeth, nails, hair. In addition, he was a lean physique and tall, which also distinguished him from his peers. Despite the mass of birth defects, Michael not only survived, he was able to become a famous actor.

After graduating from school, the young man did not try to go anywhere.

He got a job as a flower merchant. Appearance influenced the life of the actor, including his career. It is very closely connected with the so-called horror industry.

The cult maniac

Barriman made his film debut in 1975. The adventure film "Doc Savage" appeared on the screens. Most often, Michael Berryman tried on the images of killers, maniacs and other criminals. This is due to his appearance, which does not require a lot of makeup.





The main role, for which the actor was called a cult maniac - Pluto. He was able to realize the image of a maniac in a 1977 film called "The Hills Have Eyes." The director and screenwriter was Wes Craven. The plot of the film tells the story of the Carter family, who went on a trip to America through Nevada. In the desert, they came across cannibal mutants, one of which was played by Michael Berriman (the photo with him became the title poster for the film). Pluto, played by the actor, was one of the two children of Jupiter, who was once thrown out of the house by the owner of a gas station because the boy was born a mutant.
