Laser TV: Overview, Features, Benefits and Disadvantages

The phrase "laser television" sounds technological and modern. However, few know that the development of such TV sets has been going on since the 70s of the last century. Due to the high cost of the created samples and the impossibility of their commercial use, the project was stopped.

Currently, there are already televisions that use unusual laser technology. Such models in technical parameters can be attributed to projection. A laser acts as a light source in them.

In this article, we will talk about laser technologies in television, as well as a short overview of laser televisions that are commercially available, and their main characteristics.

Attractiveness and benefits

To understand the full potential of such television models, we compare the most popular plasma and liquid crystal panels with laser TVs today and highlight the principal characteristics of the latter.

  • High energy efficiency - the technology of using a laser itself can reduce the power consumption of a TV by almost five times compared to LCD counterparts.
  • High brightness indicators - for laser screens they exceed the capabilities of traditional panels by several times.
  • The widest color gamut. Owing to its unique purity, laser beams of primary colors create an amazing amount of color shades that are approximately 1.8 times higher than the capabilities of traditional technologies.
  • Natural black color is created simply by turning off the laser. The resulting saturated black color does not have an afterglow, any side flashes and shades of gray.
  • Longevity of the screen - the pixels on the screen of a laser TV do not degrade and do not fade, as can happen with flat-panel analogs.
  • High resolution - screens of television receivers, which are created by laser technology, are designed initially for Full HD format.
  • Critical viewing angles - this is a concept for laser models, according to the developers, is not relevant, since the image quality on the screen practically does not change even at the sharpest viewing angles. This is not true for traditional panels.

Laser in TV

As mentioned above, laser televisions are very similar to projection televisions, of which there are two types: frontal and rear projection. The laser model refers to televisions with rear projection.

Using technology has greatly simplified the design of the device and get rid of many parts that increase its weight and size. These are color filters, a color wheel, various polarizers, special radiation filters, moving mirrors, and additional optical devices. There is no longer any need for filtering and dividing the lamp beam into different colors. The desired color of the laser beams can be easily projected onto the panel.

After eliminating unnecessary components, the TV turned out to be more compact and light. Power consumption has decreased, while image brightness and color reproduction have improved.

conclusions

After listing a sufficient number of advantages of laser TVs, it is necessary to dwell on their disadvantages. And the main of them is still a relatively high cost. Also, many buyers call such disadvantages as large dimensions and increased eye fatigue during prolonged viewing.

Ophthalmologists say that eye fatigue is directly related to the projected image of such a television at the levels that are maximum for human perception.
