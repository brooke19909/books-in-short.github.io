Theodor Herzl: biography, ideas

Literary and journalistic activities

Since 1885, Theodor Herzl, whose quotes are still used by many Israelis, was engaged exclusively in writing. He composed a number of philosophical stories and plays. The reconstruction of the Jewish state - Herzl caught fire with this very thought. Writer's ideas required support. The development of Zionism

About ten years passed between the humiliation of Dreyfus and the death of the writer. During this period, Theodore managed to establish all the main structures of the Zionist movement. In 1897, the first congress of this community was held in Basel. Every year the number of its members increased exponentially. Jews saw in Zionism a real political movement that could solve their problems.

During the first year of his activity, Theodore tried to enlist the support of the Turkish Sultan (Eretz Yisrael was under his rule). But the long negotiations were unsuccessful. After that, Herzl decided to pay attention to the more far-sighted England. Personal life

Herzl and his family had to pay a very high price for their fascination with Zionism. In 1889, Theodore married Julia Nashauer. But, being a man obsessed, he paid her very little attention. In the family, the spouse had people with mental illness. This affected the fate of the children of Theodore. Paulina (eldest daughter) died due to drug use. Son Hans committed suicide on the day of his sister's funeral. The youngest daughter of Labor spent almost all her life in hospitals, and ended her in one of the Nazi concentration camps. But she managed to give birth to a son. In 1946, Herzl's only grandson committed suicide. Thus, the writer did not have any heirs.

Disease

In addition to the intense struggle for Zionism, Theodor Herzl, whose biography was presented above, participated in fierce verbal fights with opponents. This led to an exacerbation of his heart disease. The situation was complicated by pneumonia. Soon the writer's condition worsened, and he died in July 1904 in Edlah (Austria).



The funeral

In the will, Theodor Herzl asked to be buried next to his father in Vienna. And as soon as the Jewish people have the opportunity, let their body be transferred to Israeli land. The remains of Theodore were transported only in August 1949. Now the writer's ashes rest in Jerusalem on Mount Herzl. The day of the death of the founder of Zionism is celebrated on the 20th day of the month Tammuz.
