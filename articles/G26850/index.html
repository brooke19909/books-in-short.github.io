Political system

German geography includes questions about the political structure of the country. The Federal Chancellor of Germany is its actual leader. Linguistic and Regional Studies of Germany

In Germany, there are a large number of dialects of the German language. The differences between these dialects are so great that when broadcasting news from a television channel in the north, southerners need subtitles for understanding and vice versa. This situation is due not only to the political structure, but also to historical background. Two thousand years ago, various peoples lived on the territory of modern Germany. They were at enmity with each other, each of them sought to capture new lands.





The largest tribal associations of those times are Alemans, Bavarians, Swabians, Thuringians, Hessians, Franks and Saxons. Favorite holidays of the Germans

Despite the fact that the Germans have developed a stable stereotype as a restrained and dry people, in fact they love to have fun from the heart. For example, on the night of May 1, Germany celebrates the Sabbath of Witches. The Walpurgis Night festival lasts until dawn.

National Unity Day is also celebrated as a public holiday - October 3. On this day, there are many festivals, concerts, open-air events.

The new year in Germany is called Sylvester - in honor of the saint who died on December 31, 335. Preparation for the holiday begins in whole months. After the New Year, the Germans celebrate Valentine's Day with pleasure. Germany also celebrates March 8th, Easter and Mother's Day.





German economy

Economics is another of the most important areas that German regional studies are considering. The economy of Germany is the largest in all of Europe and the fourth in a row in the world. German manufacturers are considered a model of quality. The leading sectors of the German economy are mechanical engineering, nanotechnology, the chemical industry, and electrical engineering. Investors from other countries are happy to invest in German companies. After all, the income that they bring, although small, is stable. In total, there are about 45 thousand firms with foreign capital throughout the whole territory of Germany.

Population

The population of Germany is about 82 million people. The average life expectancy for men is 77 years, and for women is 82 years. Germany is a country with a high level of urbanization. Almost 90% of the inhabitants are city dwellers. About 52 million people in Germany profess Christianity, 4 million are Muslims, 250 thousand are adherents of Buddhism, and 100 thousand are Jews. Federal law guarantees freedom of religion, although society is secular and the church is separate from the state.

Each German study guide notes that Germany is a country with a highly developed social security system. There is pension insurance, insurance in case of illness and even unemployment. German homeless people often get dogs for themselves - for their maintenance and feeding they receive an additional allowance from the state. It should be noted that the German unemployment benefit can survive even without having the means to earn money.

Germany is known for its strict approach to legislation. For example, music classes lasting more than two hours a day are prohibited. Exceeding this time equates to noise and is unacceptable to other residents.
