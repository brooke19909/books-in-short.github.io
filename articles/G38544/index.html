Tips and tricks: how to hold a cue

Billiards is an entertaining game that distracts from everyday life and allows you to enter the world of green cloth and melodic rattling of balls. The main thing that a beginner is required to master in this game is to hold the cue correctly. The accuracy of the landmark and the strength of the impact directly depends on these indications, which in turn affects the success of the game. The grip must be strong, but at the same time easy, so as not to hamper your movements. What do you need to look at first? The first and most important is the carpal rest or, in other words, the bridge. Its position should be stable, providing a calculated point and a line of impact on the ball. In the question of how to properly keep the cue in billiards, there are two possible answers. Namely - two emphasis: closed and open. Each of them has both pros and cons.

Open emphasis

With this method, the upper part of the cue (shaft) will have the necessary stability, which will allow to deliver almost any blows. The disadvantages of this technique is the fixation of the cue, compared with a closed emphasis. To build an open bridge, you need to put your open palm on the table and pick it up a little. Next, you need to spread your fingers wider for better stability and put a large phalanx to the index, pointing it up and to the side. Shaft cue will be placed in the recess between the thumb and forefinger.





Closed emphasis

With a closed emphasis, it is better to fix the shaft, this will reduce the evasion of the cue in impact from the planned target. It is carried out in this way. Now back to the question of how to hold the cue. To feel the balance in this position of the hand, you need to move the cue, simulating the blows of different strengths. Make sure that your fingers are in good control of the line of impact, but at the same time do not interfere with the free movement of the tool. It must be remembered that it is better to maintain the correct position of the wrist, which should be on the same level as the forearm, and do not bend it inward or outward. In general, try to keep your hand relaxed and relaxed.

Tips: how to improve the game of billiards

Do not squeeze the cue like a baseball bat, firmly covering it with the whole palm. Do not hold it with just the edges of your fingers. Most of the players easily, but firmly hold the cue with the thumb and forefinger. The other one or two phalanges easily support the cue from below. Talent, years of intensive training, a determined will to win will help offset any technical errors.
