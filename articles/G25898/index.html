The prototype for the appearance of the Owl Egg is Kisling Egg. It was invented by a German specialist in the sensoric-integral development of children, Ulla Kiesling. Tools and materials for creating a cocoon

This thing is certainly wonderful, but there is a significant minus. Its cost is high - not everyone can afford to buy it.

But you can sew Owl Egg with your own hands from materials that are available.

So, prepare the following tools and components:

  • two blankets from padding polyester;
  • soft fabric - terry and fleece;
  • synthetic winterizer;
  • a zipper that can open on both sides;
  • Velcro tape or zipper;
  • threads
  • needles;
  • scissors;
  • sewing machine;
  • a piece of old wallpaper - to build patterns;
  • measuring tape and ruler;
  • pencil.

Sew Owl Egg do it yourself

First, as with any sewing job, you will need to take measurements from your baby. The growth of the child is important - the size of the egg depends on it.

The size of the eggs, as already mentioned above, is different, and the diameter should be a mark in 2/3 of the growth. This is done because a very spacious egg will not give a feeling of comfort; it will not work to take shelter in an embryo position. A narrow device also does not seem comfortable for the baby.

Next, take the wallpaper and build a pattern, focusing on the measurements taken.

Transfer the contours using chalk to a blanket, fabric and synthetic winterizer. There should be 6 pieces of such petal parts from each type of material. We sew all parts together, leaving one seam open. We put all the parts in order - a blanket, synthetic winterizer, fleece, blanket.

Based on the age of the child or your preferences, at the last stage, either a zipper is sewn, which the child can open from the outside or inside, or a Velcro tape.

That's all! Our wonderful Owl Egg is ready for fun and educational games!
