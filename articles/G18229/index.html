Sights. Dagomys (Lazarevsky district, Sochi)

Dagomys is a resort microdistrict in the south of the Lazarevsky district of the resort of Sochi. More than 130 years ago, on its territory, on the orders of Nicholas II, the estate was founded, which was considered the property of the imperial family. However, until the 70s of the 20th century, Dagomys was an ordinary workers' village. The situation changed after the construction of two hotel complexes there, which today are popular among beach lovers. It offers vacationers a well-developed tourist and entertainment infrastructure, as well as chic beaches. Tourists are also attracted by interesting sights.





Tea Houses in Dagomys

Those who prefer to combine beach activities with educational excursions in Lazarevsky district and its environs will be able to visit many interesting natural, historical and cultural sites. For example, in a forest located north of Dagomys, there are houses in ethnic style, located next to the picturesque tea plantations. They are very popular with tourists, as they are treated to delicious pastries and a variety of jams. They are served with fragrant local charcoal samovar tea. There you can see the exposition of folk crafts and purchase original souvenirs.

Caves Glubokiy Yar and Siberian

Do you like surprises? Then go to Dagomys. The sights and entertainment of the resort and its environs will not let anyone get bored, especially if you have mountaineering training. After all, there you can make an interesting excursion to one of the caves equipped for speleo-trekking. In Glubokoy Yar, you will see an underground waterfall and a lake, and in the Siberian one a river, and climb the cable ladder.

Museum of Retro Cars

Will you be bored if you choose Dagomys as your vacation destination? Sochi, whose sights are mostly sports, offers a wide variety of options for excursions. For example, in Dagomys itself there is a private museum of antique cars located at ul. Semashko, 14. There are cars and trucks produced mainly in the first half of the 20th century.





Assumption Mountain

This natural object is present in most photographs, which depict famous local attractions.

Dagomys has repeatedly become the scene of fierce clashes with the Turks. At least, according to legend, the brave Cossack Georgy Uspensky once served there, who did not give the Basurmans any descent. Once, he and his comrades were forced to make a stop at the mouth of the Dagomys River and engage in battle with the Turks. The enemies had to retreat, but it turned out that the Cossacks suffered heavy losses and many wounded. Among them was Old Man Uspensky. Before his death, he asked his comrades-in-arms to put together a coffin with a ‚Äúwindow‚Äù for him, and after his death put his body there and put a stand on top of the mountain. The Cossacks did everything as he wished, and for three days the dead man warned his comrades about the attacks of the Turks. Only after the final victory the body of the father was interred. The Cossacks themselves, in hats, applied so much land to the grave that they poured a whole mountain named after the brave warrior George.

Climbing to the top of this miraculous pyramid for people with appropriate physical training is not difficult, but in the end they will be rewarded with the opportunity to enjoy magnificent views of the sea, the resort and its surroundings.





Cross Desert Monastery

On vacation, you can see religious attractions. Dagomys is a convenient starting point for a trip to the Cross Desert Monastery. This monastery was built in the late 1990s near the resort, in the village of Solokh-aul, in the upper reaches of the mountain river Shakh. Before the October Revolution, hermit monks settled in its place. They also say that even earlier in these parts they were planning to build the famous New Athos Monastery.

At the resort you can explore the Adyghe national attractions. Dagomys is located near the village of Kalesh, from where you will make a trip to two picturesque waterfalls on all-terrain vehicles.
