Laser epilator for home use. Laser epilator reviews

To date, the problem of unwanted vegetation on the body is solved by different options, each of which has its own characteristic features. The most common way is hair removal, which allows you to solve this issue for a relatively long time, but at the same time is a rather unpleasant process. As the most effective laser method has proved itself, which acts directly on the hair follicle, and also does not cause any pain. This option, however, has a negative side - a rather high cost. An alternative way to solve this problem was a laser epilator for home use.

Distinctive features of a home laser epilator

Most women use a standard epilator, the principle of which is to pluck unwanted vegetation without any direct effect on the hair follicle. This option, of course, is more effective than shaving or using depilatory creams, but it is quite painful, especially in sensitive areas of the skin. And after some time, the hair still appears again.

A characteristic feature of a laser epilator is that it acts specifically on the hair follicle. After each application, unwanted vegetation becomes thinner and weaker, and after a certain time, it disappears altogether. In addition, this option does not cause discomfort and does not provoke irritation on the skin.



As for the financial side, the laser epilator, the price of which is slightly higher than the cost of standard devices of this spectrum of action, fully pays for itself. This is evident when taking into account the price list of several courses of the same procedure in the cabin. The price of such a device, depending on the model, is in the range of 5-30 thousand rubles.

The principle of the laser epilator

Each laser can emit rays of a certain type or frequency. Human skin contains the substance melanin, which absorbs radiated energy, as a result of which the hair follicle is very heated and destroyed. Thanks to this, a laser epilator for home use is more effective than other similar procedures.



You can use this unit on any part of the body. It is also great for removing hair in places with extremely sensitive skin - in the armpits, on the arms, hips, in the bikini area and on the face. In addition, the procedure does not cause irritation on the treated surface and allows you to get rid of unwanted vegetation locally.

User guide

Since devices of this kind have similar functionality, the principle of operation of devices of different manufacturers is also similar. As an example, you can use the Rio laser epilator, which consumer reviews are most often found.



The device has technology that protects a person from possible laser contact with the organs of vision and their damage. The device begins to work only when it is in full contact with the skin. In each procedure, a certain part of the body should be processed only once. Clinical studies have shown that about 90% of unwanted vegetation disappears after 7-8 procedures. People with dark hair, however, due to physiology, should use a laser epilator for home use with a course of 10-12 procedures.

Disadvantages of a Laser Epilator

Despite its positive aspects, this unit, however, has several disadvantages. Firstly, not everyone can use a laser epilator at home. The exception is people with dark skin who live in the African continent and the Caribbean. This is due to the fact that their epidermis contains more melanin than fair-skinned people, and the use of a laser can cause a burn.



This type of hair removal is not suitable for people with very fair or gray hair. Here the opposite principle applies - melanin in such a skin, on the contrary, is very small. As a result, the hair follicle will not heat up to the desired temperature, and the procedure itself will be useless. For these groups of people, an alternative is more suitable - electrolysis.

Another disadvantage of a home laser epilator is its small exposure area. Since the device works only when it is in full contact with the skin to exclude the possibility of eye injury, it covers a small area, and it may take several hours to completely treat the body.

Contraindications

Like other means to get rid of unwanted hair on the body, a laser epilator for home use has a number of contraindications. These include skin conditions such as herpes, psoriasis, dermatitis, and eczema. This device should not be used for cancer, diabetes mellitus, tuberculosis, pregnancy and individual intolerance to this procedure.

With caution, the product should be used for varicose veins, skin burns, scratches or other injuries, as well as for acute allergies and the presence of many moles on the treated areas of the body.

Differences between laser hair removal and photo hair removal

Modern cosmetology offers two innovative ways to get rid of unwanted vegetation on the body - laser hair removal and photoepilation. Due to the similarity of these techniques, they are often confused. The difference between these methods consists, first of all, in that the laser acts more locally, pointwise, directly on the follicle, while photoepilation is an intense flash and the release of a huge amount of energy. The latter method is quite fast, since it processes a larger surface than a laser epilator. Reviews, however, show that photoepilation is not suitable for sensitive skin and can cause irritation, itching and redness. You can not use this technique for facial treatment.



Laser hair removal is an innovative method of hair removal on the body, which has been successfully applied at home. It is characterized by a high level of effectiveness, the absence of painful sensations and a wide scope of use. Despite some contraindications and limitations in use, this method has established itself in the market as an alternative to expensive salon procedures.
