Trogir, Croatia: a small cozy town with an abundance of ancient attractions

Four thousand years ago, people took a fancy and began to populate the coast of the warm sea, which we now call the Adriatic. First, a Greek settlement grew up here, which was called Tragurion. Now it has become known as Trogir. Croatia, in which this city is located, is a small Catholic country with a population of just over 4 million people.

The small town of Trogir, popular among tourists from all over the world, is under the auspices of UNESCO. In it, despite all the turbulent events sweeping over it, many buildings of the XIII-XV centuries were preserved. Brought photos from Trogir, Croatia, will warm the soul with warm memories for a long time.





Historical reference

The Greeks are tireless travelers and merchants who were the first to settle on the islet where the historical part of the city is now located, and the coast opposite it. This is mentioned by Strabo and Ptolemy. The town was small, but successfully developed. When these places began to belong to the Roman Empire, then life in it began to calm down. Roman veterans began to inhabit it. They built baths and baths, the remains of which have survived to this day.

When the barbarians destroyed the empire, then Trogir annexed Byzantium. The small town remained to live, because it was on the island. In the 7th century, the Byzantine empire was so weakened that the Croats actively began to arrive in these places, and the town fell under the sway of the Croatian kings. This place was too attractive. Trogir was ruled by Hungarian kings and flourished at that time, and in the XV century it was captured by Venice and began to impose its customs and language. There were wars with her, and the inhabitants of Trogir turned to the Pope for help, demanding that his bishops speak the Croatian language.

In the XVIII century, when Venice lost its position, Trogir annexed Austria. Only in 1990, after the breakup of Yugoslavia, did independent Croatia appear. Trogir is one of its cities. Four kilometers from it is Split with an airport, from where it is easy to get to the tourist town, where pleasant warm weather reigns from August to October.

Beaches of Trogir

In addition, here you can relax on the beaches, swimming, windsurfing or diving. The beaches here are unique and diverse. There are sandy, pebble and rocky platforms. The most popular is Pantan, where you can find pebbles and sand. The beaches of "Okrug" and "Medena" are appreciated for entertainment. Ciovo beach is very picturesque on the island of Ciovo.

Old city

This is a small town of Croatia. Trogir has a length of 500 m and a width of 250 m, and the number of inhabitants slightly exceeds 10 thousand people.





Having passed the short bridge that separates the island from the mainland, tourists enter the city through the North Gate, on which, from the time of Venetian rule, a sculpture of the patron of Trogir - blessed John. In the same period, city walls were built. Entering Trogir, the traveler immediately finds himself in the central square, in the historical heart of the city, which was originally a Roman forum. Further, in all directions lead confused, winding narrow streets paved with stones. Tourist immediately plunges into the atmosphere of the Middle Ages. He will meet on his way more than ten churches and numerous buildings of the 13th century.

St. Lawrence

This is the highest and most visited attraction of Trogir in Croatia. It is by climbing the bell tower of the cathedral, whose height is forty-seven meters, you can enjoy the views of the whole city. It was built and rebuilt for four centuries. This exquisite work of art is the work of various artists and architects. It mixes Romanesque, Gothic style, as well as Baroque.



Palaces of Togir

Noble families lived in the city and built many palaces, including Chipiko. This family ruled Trogir for about three hundred years. Their palace consists of several buildings interconnected.

The Palace of Garanni Fagnola is built in the Baroque style. It has a rich library and is now a museum, in the courtyard of which sculptures are exhibited.

Forts of Trogir

Kamerlengo Fortress got its name from the city treasurer, and a recognizable look - during the reign of the Venetians. Its walls, located in the southwest, were erected from 1420 to 1437. Its main purpose was to deploy the fleet of Venice. Now in this unique place concerts and festivals are held.



Historically important for the defense of Trogir and Croatia, Fort Saint Mark is now the home of Dalmatian music. Today the fortress is also used as a museum.
