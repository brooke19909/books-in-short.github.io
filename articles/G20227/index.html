Where polymer clay is sold, types and properties. Popular brands

The question of where polymer clay is sold is of interest to people who love to create various crafts with their own hands. This material is used by craftsmen to sculpt jewelry or sculptures, flowers and small dolls. Visually, such clay resembles plasticine, to the touch it is as soft, it is well molded, has a characteristic smell. In the work, polymer clay retains its plasticity due to plasticizers. In order for the created figure to harden and be stored for a long time, it must be subjected to heat treatment. At a temperature of 100 to 130 degrees, they evaporate because polymerization occurs, resulting in the formation of polyvinyl chloride.

Varieties of clay

To understand the types of polymer clay, grades and their properties, we first consider the classification according to the method of solidification.

Material is divided into:

  1. Self-hardening clay, which in turn is divided into light, heavy, puppet and the so-called cold porcelain.
  2. Baked, which also has several varieties, namely, liquid, translucent, with fillers and ordinary.

They are sold in hermetically sealed packaging. May have different weights.

Properties of clay of different types

Each subspecies has its own differences. Consider them carefully:

  • As the name implies, self-hardening clay hardens in the open air for several hours. The light mass resembles marshmallows and stretches well and rolls into thin sheets. Most often on sale you can find the popular clay of the Japanese manufacturer ClayCraft Deco.
  • The heavy mass is more like clay. Its properties are also similar to natural. It crumbles in a dry form, and for modeling it needs to be soaked with water. One of the popular producers of this subspecies is the Czech company Koh-i-noor.
  • Dolls are most often made from Darwi. It is well polished and cut in layers.
  • Modena, Modern, Fluer are common manufacturers of cold porcelain. This is a mixture that wrinkles and bends; florists make flowers out of it.

What is baked clay?

Thermoplastics is called polymer clay, which hardens at high temperatures. How to bake polymer clay? Some masters use a domestic oven for these purposes, while others simply cook crafts in ordinary water. Categorically it is impossible to put hardness in a microwave oven!

After opening the package of baked clay, it can easily be kneaded in hands. If it stays a little open and weathered, it begins to crumble and loses its plasticity. Therefore, do not forget to use clay to tightly wrap it in polyethylene.

Consider what are the types of thermoplastics in more detail:

  • Liquid clay is more like a colorless gel.
  • Ordinary clay is sold in bars, has a different color. The color of crafts after firing does not change.
  • Clay with fillers can be of different types. One can imitate metal, the other - natural stone or have bright sparkles.
  • Translucent, which, as the name implies, has no color or is slightly tinted, and after firing, the paint slightly brightens.
