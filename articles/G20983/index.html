Converting a computer power supply to a conventional voltage source

You need to decide what type of power supply your computer, AT or ATX is. As a rule, this is indicated on the body. Pulse PSUs operate only under load. But the device of the ATX type power supply allows the closure of the green and black wires to artificially simulate it. So, by connecting the load (for AT) or by closing the necessary terminals (for ATX), you can start the fan. At the output appears 5 and 12 volts. The maximum output current depends on the power of the PSU. At 200 W, on a five-volt output, the current can reach about 20A, at 12V - about 8A. So without extra costs, you can use a good power source with good output characteristics.





Converting a computer power supply into an adjustable voltage source

Having such a power supply at home or at work is quite convenient. Changing a building block is easy. It is necessary to replace several resistances and unsolder the throttle. In this case, the voltage value can be adjusted from 0 to 20 volts. Naturally, the currents will remain in their original proportions. If you are satisfied with the maximum voltage of 12V, it is enough to install a thyristor voltage regulator at its output. The controller circuit is very simple. At the same time, it will help to avoid interference in the internal part of the computer unit.
