What are the signs for food in hotels?

If you plan to book a hotel, then you will need to choose the option of food. There are several of them. We will tell you what are the signs of food in hotels. We will tell you about each type in detail, so that it is easier for you to decide which one is right for you.

Hotel Food Designations

Type of OB

Such an abbreviation means that you are provided with accommodation, but without food. Food will need to be ordered additionally.





Type of food BB

This option consists of breakfast alone. You will have to pay for additional meals.

Note that in many countries, breakfast is immediately included in the price of the hotel. And in Mexico, for example, it is ordered at will.

Hotel Meal Designations: Breakfast Types

CBF (Continental Breakfast)

He is the most humble. Usually served in hotels (European) 2 * -5 *, sometimes it happens in institutions of the lower category (2 * -3 *). Breakfast consists of buns, butter, eggs and coffee.

ABF (American breakfast)

This is a more nutritious breakfast option than CBF. It is similar to continental, but vegetables, cheese and several types of sausage are added to it. This type is common in Western Europe and in America.





BBF (Buffet)

This type of food is the most popular for breakfast. It is sometimes referred to as BB. This food option is the most plentiful of all. It is found in all hotels of the world. This breakfast includes meat and dairy products, pastries, fruits, vegetables, and local drinks.

Buffet food means that the vacationer can eat as much food as he can, while the number of approaches to the table is not limited. The quality and quantity of food on the table depends on the level of the hotel.

English breakfast

This is the rarest type of food. As a rule, it includes such products: butter, toast, fruit jam, tea or coffee, juice.

What else are the signs for food in hotels?

HB (Breakfast and dinner)

In other words, half board. In some hotels it is immediately included in the price. It is possible to additionally order lunch or dinner, paying on the spot.

DNR (dinner)

There can be two types of food: either a buffet or a regular menu. Some hotels have a limited buffet (for example, cold snacks).

FB (three meals a day)

This type includes 3 meals a day. The first and last meal are presented in the form of a buffet. For lunch and dinner you have to pay extra for drinks.

FB + (extended three meals a day)

The price includes three meals a day with soft drinks.

Brunch dinner

This type of food involves breakfast, smoothly turning into lunch. The next meal is dinner. Drinks are also included.

All Inclusive Meals: Designation of These Types

Mini all inclusive (full board + drinks)

It consists of three meals: breakfast, lunch and dinner. The cost of drinks is included in the payment. You can use them at any time, but the quantity is limited.

ALL (all inclusive)

The price includes 3 meals a day + unlimited drinks. You need to pay extra for an afternoon snack, barbecue in bars, lunch, snacks and late dinner. Hotel services are also included in the price.

HcAL (upper class)

All services that the hotel offers are included in the price.

UALL (UAI) (Ultra All Inclusive)

This type is analogous to All Inclusive, but, in addition, you can eat around the clock in unlimited quantities. You also get drinks (alcoholic and non-alcoholic), both locally produced and from other countries. The price of this type includes the services and entertainment of the hotel.

Now you know what the designations of food types are in hotels, and you can safely choose the option that you like.
