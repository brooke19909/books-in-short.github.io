The author of the idea

In 1929, Barcelona hosted the World International Exhibition. A year before its start, everything was ready to receive and host a unique exhibition. There was not enough zest to make this event memorable.

In June 1928, the young architect Carlos Bougas proposed a rather bold design of a dancing fountain. 460 plans and 70 drawings were studied and approved by the Board of Directors. It took Carlos and three thousand workers less than a year to bring the fantastic idea to life in time for the opening of the exhibition.

The result was the world's first singing fountain in Barcelona on such a scale, where powerful jets of water obediently change their shape in the light of multi-colored rays.

Some facts

The talent of Bouigas was manifested not only in the external appearance of his creation, but also in the engineering component. Every smallest detail has been carefully thought out - from the size of the pool and the number of pumps to the recycling water system designed to save water. In one second, five pumps distill 26 tons of water.

Better to see once

From the day of its creation, the fountain was managed by a team of specialists, musical accompaniment was carried out manually. In recent years, a system has been created that completely replaces man. The arsenal of singing fountains has seven billion combinations of light, music and water.

3623 is the exact number of jets of water that come from wells and strike in all directions, reaching a height of 54 meters, to create the complete illusion of immersion in the magic of Montjuic.

Today the repertoire of the Barcelona Singing Fountain in Spain is represented not only by operas and symphonies of classical composites (Tchaikovsky, Beethoven), but more modern jazz compositions, melodies from cult films and the latest hits.

Particularly colorful shows are held on September 23 in connection with the end of the Mercier festival in honor of the Barcelona saint, at Christmas and some other significant events.
