William Henry Harrison

One of the places in this ranking is occupied by the infamous Henry Harrison, the 9th president of the United States, who became the shortest ruler in the country in history. On the occasion of his inauguration, Harrison prepared a 2-hour speech. The weather that day was rainy and cloudy, and the principled president did not leave the rostrum until he read out the speech completely. He got wet all the way and became seriously ill, and a month later he died suddenly from this. Thus, he became the first notorious president who died right on his post.

Zachary Taylor

The 12th president of the United States, Zachary Taylor was remembered as the most passive ruler. He had little interest in politics, and did not even take the oath of his religious convictions, since the day of the inauguration fell on the "day of rest" by his Protestant faith. Even while serving in the army, he rarely wore a military uniform, and his colleagues, once mistaking him for a simple peasant, were sent to dig the ground. Actually, having been in office a little over a year, he did not receive the proper authority. And in 1850 he died under incomprehensible circumstances, either being poisoned or ill.





Jimmy carter

In 1977, he distinguished himself by giving the Panama Canal to the Panamanians, despite the fact that most Americans believed that it belonged to them.

A little later there was a revolution in Iran, which provoked an increase in oil prices and all the ensuing problems, and this was not the worst moment for Carter. In 1979, students in Iran took the US Embassy in Tehran and put forward their demands, including the extradition of the Shah, who, by coincidence, was in New York at that time. Carter's planned release of prisoners did not go as planned, and as a result failed. Due to the prevailing contempt for Carter, the hostages were released only a year later, when Ronald Reagan came to power.
