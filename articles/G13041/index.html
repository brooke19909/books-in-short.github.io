The confrontation between the USSR and the USA, called the Cold War, primarily affected space exploration. For many Americans, convinced by propaganda that the Soviet Union was a backward agrarian power, it was an unpleasant surprise that the first satellite was launched precisely by the Russians.

Development of PS-1

The first condition of the chief designer S.P. Korolev was the presence of a radio transmitter continuously working on the satellite. It was not possible to realize it immediately. According to eyewitnesses, Korolev rejected many options for the shape of the satellite - cone-shaped, diamond-shaped, square - which caused bewilderment of colleagues, because in space there is no air resistance, and therefore, the form does not matter. When the satellite was launched, colleagues were convinced of the correctness of a brilliant scientist - the satellite was a prototype of the man-made Earth in space.





The beginning of the space age and the role of scientists in it

Initially, the development was ordered by the military. The main condition for victory in the Cold War, they saw the construction of carriers for a nuclear bomb. On August 21, 1957, a military intercontinental missile was launched, after which a serious struggle broke out between scientists and the military. The military department insisted on continuing the defense program, and the people of Korolev suggested using these carriers to put the satellite into orbit. In the end, peaceful goals won, the then head of state, N. S. Khrushchev, put the last point in this confrontation. He received evidence that the Americans were ready to launch their satellite, and this was the decisive impetus that prompted him to lean toward the arguments of scientists.





The day the space age began

The beginning of the space age is October 4, 1957. It was then that from the Baikonur cosmodrome (this is its historical name, it was originally the Tyura-Tam training ground) the R-7 intercontinental rocket was sent on a space trip. It was she who was the carrier of the first artificial satellite PS-1. In addition to it, there was also a head fairing, which serves as protection against atmospheric friction at the start, and the second stage of the rocket is the central engine. Witnesses of this significant event saw it from Earth, since all other parts were simply indistinguishable due to their small size.

As often happens, the success of this significant event for all mankind hung in the balance. During the launch, there were problems, each of which could disrupt the flight. For example, at the 16th second after the separation from the Earth, the fuel supply system failed and an increased consumption of hot began. Scientists took these and many other faults into account during the next launches, and on October 4, 1957 today can be celebrated as the day the beginning of the cosmic era of mankind.





World resonance of a world-stunned event

The beginning of the space era, laid by Soviet scientists, could not hide any means of information warfare. Signals from orbit could catch all the hams of the world. This event was a loud and undeniable refutation of the statements of Western politicians about the scientific insolvency of the Soviet Union, which caused serious damage to the authority of the United States. For a long time, even before the launch of the satellite by the Russians, the American press actively suggested to its readers that the United States of America would soon send the first satellite into orbit. In fact, they only managed to do this on February 1, 1958, and its mass turned out to be 10 times less than the Russian pioneer. The fact that Soviet engineers occupied the podium before the Americans was a real shock for the latter.

The consequences of orbital flight

The beginning of the space era has revolutionized astronomy. After the invention of the telescope by Galileo, this was the next important step in the development of the Universe. The so-called extra-atmospheric astronomy has arisen, scientists have discovered that interstellar space is penetrated by cosmic rays. Black holes and gamma-ray bursts were discovered only due to space research. The launch of telescopes into orbit made it possible to significantly improve their resolution and to see what was impossible to see from Earth. The boundaries of the universe in one fell swoop increased to incredible proportions, something that scientists could imply or assume was clearly confirmed.
