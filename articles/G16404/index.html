How to get from Venice to Verona - different travel options

Many tourists who come to Northern Italy try to see as much as possible during their stay in this beautiful country. Therefore, it is important for them to know how to get from one city to another most quickly and on a budget.

In this article, we will look at different ways to get from Venice to Verona. After all, the "pearl of the Adriatic" is a rather damp city, and in the high season there will not be crowded from crowds of tourists.

Verona, the place praised by Shakespeare, where the prototypes of Romeo and Juliet lived, is also very interesting. And the prices here are much lower.

We will tell you how to get from Venice to Verona on your own. You will also find out where you can get excursions for organized travelers.

Taxi and car

If you want to get from Venice to Verona ‚Äúwith the breeze‚Äù, book a private shuttle. You can also use official taxis. Usually this type of transfer is ordered online - it is safer, and the price is fixed.

The cost of such a trip will cost an average of 250 euros (18 105 rubles) in both directions. Traveling from Venice to Verona by car is approximately two and a half hours. The distance of 120 kilometers is not too big, but during this time you will get to the highway from one city, and then move off from it in another village and get into its central part.

Approximately the same amount will be required for those who travel in their own or rented car. But if you do not regret 8 euros (580 rubles) and go on toll roads, you will be in Verona in an hour and a half. The main road the cars follow is A 4. To get closer to the center, you need to find the Verona Sud exit.

A train

This is the most convenient way to overcome the distance Venice - Verona. " How to get by train from one city to another? First of all, you need to purchase a ticket. Often its price "bites", but the Italian trains are clean, convenient and comfortable. They have a speed of more than 200 kilometers per hour.

Express trains in Venice leave from Santa Lucia station, and arrive in Verona at Porta Nuova station. The first train leaves at eight in the morning, and the last at 20:00.

The cost of the express is approximately 19 euros (1377 rubles) for the second class. But tickets for them need to be bought in advance. However, many tourists prefer the train "Treno Regional." They go slower, but tickets can be bought on the spot, and the price is much cheaper - 7 euros (507 rubles).

Italian electric trains are not inferior to the "expresses" either in terms of comfort or convenience. By high-speed trains you will get from Venice to Verona in an hour with a small, and regional - in two.

How to get from Venice to Verona by bus

This type of transport is preferred by budget tourists. Although, frankly, trains in Italy are cheaper. Therefore, economical Italians prefer to travel by regional trains.

Buses are mainly used by tourists and residents of distant villages. How to get from Venice to Verona with this type of intercity public transport? Buses belong to different carrier companies, and their route depends on it.

Some of them travel through various small villages and narrow paths, and instead of 120 kilometers they wind up as many as 250. Tickets can be purchased from companies whose offices are located at bus stations. Their cost is about 20 euros (1448 rubles), and they reach Verona in three hours (on average).

But lately there has appeared such an option as the Flixbass buses. They go from Mestre station. They make 5 flights a day, and the trip costs from 8 euros (580 rubles). On the road, such buses spend a little more than 2 hours. Tickets can usually be bought online.

Entrance to the museums you will have to pay separately. The trip can be quite tiring, but a vivid experience is guaranteed.

From Venice Airport

If you come to the hub named after Marco Polo, and your final goal is Verona, then you should first get to the suburbs of Mestre. Why? If you ask experienced tourists how to get from Venice Airport to Verona, you will hear that there are a lot of trains from Mestre station to Porta Nuova station.

It is better not to go there by taxi; it will be too expensive. FlyBas express buses run from Marco Polo Airport to Mestre. From their parking lot to the train station is just a few meters.

All trains going to Verona stop in Mestre. There you can choose whether to board an electric train or a speed train.
