Shabby Chic: do it yourself with your own hands

Great time has come for flea market buffs: shebby chic is in fashion again. With your own hands in this style you can recreate truly unique things, the main thing is to learn a few rules.

Where did that come from?

Any designer will say that, despite its essence, shabby chic is truly a young direction. It owes its appearance to the Englishwoman Rachel Ashwell, a big lover of antiques. It was they who inspired the original designer to selflessly use the decoupage technique. Recognize and implement

So what distinguishes from many other shabby chic? First of all, the presence of antiquity. Moreover, the thing may be completely new, but when using special methods, it is brought into a vintage state, so characteristic of the Shebby-chic style. With their own hands, people "age" caskets, chests of drawers, shelves, tables, chairs and much more with the help of a game of paints, varnishes and, of course, decoupage art.

It is worth noting that when designing a room in the style of â€œshabby chicâ€ with your own hands, you should adhere to a single idea, such as Provence or Baroque. And therefore, the images of the village landscape, the drawings in the spirit of "jui", floral motifs - this is exactly what is required to emphasize the "shabby chic". How to do it? Of course, use a special technique, partially modified by the founder. Another distinguishing feature of this trend is its color palette. As a rule, the interior and its objects are painted in pastel shades: milky, pink, yellow, blue and others. In this case, tones can be used either cold or warm. Mixing them is unacceptable.

Shabby Chic Decoupage
