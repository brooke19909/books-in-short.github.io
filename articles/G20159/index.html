Mysterious Olkhon

Tourists who come to enjoy the local beauty, ask themselves questions about where to start the trip and what interesting places to Baikal must visit. Without exception, all guides are unanimous in that you can not ignore the majestic island of Olkhon, shrouded in mysterious traditions. This mystical territory, called the heart of Lake Baikal, since ancient times has become a gathering place for all local shamans. Repeatedly appearing circles of the correct form on the frozen Lake Baikal echo with mysterious rings appearing on the green grass of Olkhon. Scientists are trying to unravel the secrets of the appearance of mysterious formations and are inclined to the version that these phenomena are traces of the landing of alien ships, which from ancient times chose Baikal as a place for their cosmodrome.





It is not in vain that Olkhon is surrounded by a mystical halo, it was here that archaeological expeditions, cave paintings and burials found the parking lot of an ancient man. Rock Shamanka, located on Cape Burhan, revered by local and visiting sorcerers. Once upon a time, a cave cut through it, which became a real shrine. Near the entrance to it, drawings depicting Buddhist deities were found, and ancient inscriptions were preserved inside. Women and children are denied access to the cave due to the concentration of strong energy in it after numerous sacrifices and rituals.

Cape Ryty

The coastal point of the lake, which is part of the reserve and endowed with a sacred meaning, is included in the most interesting places on Lake Baikal, and travelers decide to visit them or not, because in order to see the mysterious cape, the permission of the authorities and considerable courage are required.





Due to the abnormal geological features, a person here feels an otherworldly effect, expressed in hallucinations and prophetic dreams. The cape is completely uninhabited, because not a single settlement has lasted here for a long time. According to beliefs, this cursed place took away all its strength, and a man was literally aging before his eyes. Again, the settlers spoke of luminous objects near the surrounding area. Scientists believe that earth faults interact with solar energy, which is why not a single vessel stopped nearby. And on boats daring to approach, all equipment was out of order.

Baidinsky Caves

Another interesting places on Lake Baikal, endowed with the status of archaeological sites, are located a couple of kilometers from the lake. For thousands of years, water streams flowed through limestone cracks, forming 2 caves. Large in shape resembles a triangle, inside it are 2 halls that are connected by a tunnel. Archaeologists found clay dishes, bone knives, birch bark and other objects of the Iron Age culture under a layer of ice. Inside a small cave located nearby, there is only one hall and 4 dead ends. In it were found traces of ancient sites of local residents who took refuge from the cold in winter, and human bones. And still caves are of great interest to archaeologists.



Cape Mare's Head

Travelers traveling for the first time to enjoy the regal beauty of landscapes should think about their route in order to have time to explore all the interesting places of Lake Baikal. Local guides recommend visiting the peninsula, which is part of the national park and is a natural monument. Cape Mare's head is included in all sightseeing tours. Ancient legends say that once the horse of Genghis Khan stopped here to drink pure water. Visitors to this famous place celebrate the most beautiful grottoes, picturesque landscapes, sandy beaches where you can relax in comfort, while contemplating the unique uniqueness of natural places. The locals considered the peninsula a cult: shamans have long held their rites there.



Mount Ehe Erdoi (Erd)

It is no exaggeration to say that the most interesting places in Lake Baikal are saturated with the spirit of mysticism. Well, the famous mountain, located a few kilometers from the deepest lake, has been playing a huge role in the life of indigenous peoples for thousands of years. It was rightfully considered sacred, because long since the locals held rituals there. Ritual dances, which lasted about a week, were arranged near a hill of unnatural origin of an ideal dome-shaped form. The cult center gathered a lot of people who held hands and thus closed the circle. The ancient peoples believed that through a sacred place they communicate with their gods, and they, in response to actions, send universal grace to the earth. If there were few people, and the circle did not close, it was believed that the year would be extremely unfortunate for residents.

Volcano valley

Speaking about the interesting places of Lake Baikal, one should not forget about the local attractions - the Valley of volcanoes, located far from civilization. Now it is a huge field covered with frozen lava remaining after the eruption. This amazing landscape, reminiscent of the landscape of an alien planet, for a long time remains in the memory of everyone who visited this edge of the earth. You can even climb into the craters of two now inactive volcanoes overgrown with trees. However, guides warn that this is an inaccessible and unusually difficult route that tourists overcome with experienced guides.





It is simply impossible to consider all the interesting places of Lake Baikal in one article. There are many more amazingly beautiful bays, lakes, mountains and cliffs, which are considered cultural attractions of this sacred area.
