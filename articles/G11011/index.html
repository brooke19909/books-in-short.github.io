Baptism of Russia

988 is officially considered the year of the adoption of Christianity in Russia. But, as with all distant events, one cannot say for sure that this is reliable information. In 988, the wedding of the Byzantine Princess Anna and Vladimir the Red Sun took place. Why was he called that? It is for the reason that the ruler introduced the official monotheistic religion. How was the baptism? Of course, baptizing the whole state was impossible. People who believed in pagan gods all their lives could not immediately change their beliefs. Paganism lived in Russia for at least another 50 years after its baptism. Nevertheless, the adoption of Christianity was a great step towards the unification of the country and the establishment in it of a new institution of power. Some historians believe that the Christian religion could be imposed on Vladimir by his grandmother, Princess Olga.
