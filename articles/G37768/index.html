Simferopol: what to see and where to go?

Simferopol is the center and intersection of the Crimean roads, and it is from here that the acquaintance with the peninsula begins. The main attractions are not far from each other, and much can be seen in one day. But if you go on a trip with a child, then look for information in advance where they advise where to go to Simferopol for tourists with young children, with kids who are already at school. Museums

Where can I go in Simferopol if you are a fan of museums? First of all, you should go to the Museum of Local Lore, where more than 150 thousand exhibits are presented that can show the general picture of the nature of the peninsula. A museum of antiquities was opened a long time ago, and on its basis in 1921 the museum was created, it is located on Gogol Street, 14, and it works every day except Tuesday.



The Archaeological Museum, which was opened in 1974, is worth a visit. It contains a large collection of exhibits, divided into three departments. The first represents household items of prehistoric people, the second is related to slaveholding time, and the third presents objects of the Middle Ages.

One of the very first art museums appeared in Simferopol. It was opened in 1921, and the first collection consisted of nationalized paintings. Gradually, the collection was replenished with canvases by European authors, as well as the works of local artists and painters from other cities of Russia.

Historical Buildings

Where to go in Simferopol if you want to get acquainted with historical places that are associated with any people? One of the main attractions is the house of Vorontsov, which is located in the park Salgirka, built in 1826 for General Naryshkin. When the military leader died, his widow sold this house, which began to be owned by the governor Vorontsov. The imperial family, members of noble dynasties, and poets stayed in this house.



In the same park you can see the manor of Pallas, who was a traveler and explorer of the Crimea. It is located on the Salger River. The manor is made in the Turkish style and was built in the XVIII century. At the end of it, the great traveler acquired this property, making it a real cultural center.

Where can I go with a child in Simferopol?

One of the main entertainments for kids can be a children's park, which is located on Kirov Avenue. There are many playgrounds, various attractions for kids and teens, there is also a zoo and an aquarium. Entrance to the park is free, but you need to buy a ticket for the rides, the zoo and the aquarium, the cost can be found at the box office.



Where can I go with a child in Simferopol to be both interesting and informative? Be sure to visit the chocolate museum with the kids, from which everyone will be delighted. You can not only see figures from it, but also taste various desserts, pastries and cakes. The most important exposition are exhibits that are made from different types of chocolate.

Only 6 kilometers from the city is the Denisovskaya ostrich farm, where anyone can get. Few ostriches are breeding in Crimea, but it will be interesting for adults and children to see how the farm looks and where ostriches live. In addition to ostriches, you can see peacocks, geese, horses, donkeys.

Monuments

Where to go in Simferopol to look at historical monuments?

Near the Victory Square you can see the obelisk, which was created as a tribute to the memory of Vasily Dolgorukov-Crimean. Crimea once fought with Turkish troops, and General Dolgorukov played a significant role in these battles. The place was not chosen by chance, because it was near Simferopol in 1771 that the Russian army, led by Dolgorukov, fought with the Turks, and it was then that the enemy was defeated.



In 1967, a monument was erected in memory of the great poet Alexander Sergeyevich Pushkin, which is located at the intersection of the street of the same name and Gorky Street.

The T-34 tank can be seen in Victory Park, it was he who first left the German invaders in the liberated territory in 1944.

Cathedrals

Where to go in Simferopol to see beautiful churches and cathedrals?

The majestic temple rises on one of the streets of Simferopol and is called the Holy Trinity Cathedral, or the Church of St. Luke. Its history began at the end of the 18th century, when a Greek church with a gymnasium was built. A little later this small church was demolished and a large temple was built, which was the center of religious life. In Soviet times, especially in the 30s, they tried to destroy all the churches, but the Greek community managed to preserve this attraction. Here is one of the most famous shrines - the icon of the Virgin "Sorrowful", as well as the relics of St. Luke, who was a talented surgeon.



In Crimea, not only Christians and Muslims live, but also such peoples as Tatars, Scythians, Greeks. However, apart is such a people who live in the city, as Karaites. They profess one of the directions of Judaism and for themselves built a house of worship, which is called the Karaite Kenassa. It is located in the city center, attracts attention with its appearance, because it is very different from traditional cathedrals and mosques.

Crimean Tatars and simply Tatars who profess Islam are also living in Simferopol. One of the main Muslim shrines in the city is the white mosque of Kebir-Jami. Its name translates as "cathedral". It is located on Kurchatov street, house 4, and the date of construction is considered to be 1508.
