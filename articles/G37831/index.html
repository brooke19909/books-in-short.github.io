What is a dime? Coin history

What is a dime? This banknote is a Russian coin in denominations of ten cents. The dime was minted from silver. This coin was used in monetary circulation during the time of tsarist Russia in the period from 1701 to 1917.

The first silver ten-coin coins

For the first time ten silver kopecks were made in 1701 in Moscow. The starting circulation then amounted to 30 thousand copies. In addition to dimes, in 1701 other metal money was made in denominations of half a hryvnia, fifty and twenty-five kopecks.

The last dimes of the era of Tsarist Russia

What is the dime of 1797? In that year, new coins of the Emperor of All-Russian Paul I were issued. The weight of the dime was 2.93 grams, and the issue was carried out in the amount of 48 thousand and one copy. During the reign of the eldest son of Paul I, Alexander, a new ten kopecks were made. This happened in 1810. The weight of such a silver coin was 2.07 grams, and the circulation was 77 thousand 364 copies.
