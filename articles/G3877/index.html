Aushiger thermal springs - relaxation with health benefits

The village of Aushiger is located in Kabardino-Balkaria. It is famous for its unusually beautiful landscapes, as well as unique hot springs. Every year, thousands of tourists from different regions of our country come to these marvelous places to combine a pleasant stay with wellness.



From the story of Aushiger

In the XVII century, this village was founded by the Kabardian nobles Doguzhokovs, who continued the ancient family of Idarovs. They settled at the confluence of the Heu River in the Cherek. In honor of the founders, the village was first named Doguzhokovo (Dyuzhuykyuyu). After the complete establishment of Soviet power in Kabarda (1920), Revkom Nalchik decided to rename Doguzhokovo, like many other settlements of the republic, due to the presence of noble and princely names in their names. So the village began to be called Aushiger, in honor of the mountain that rises above it.



In November 1942, Aushiger was occupied by fascist troops. The village was completely liberated in 1943. In memory of the fallen villagers and soldiers of the Soviet Army who defended and liberated the village, a monument was erected in Aushiger. Local residents look after him, and the older generation instills a respectful attitude towards this modest memorial to young people.

Source History

Aushiger thermal springs were discovered during geological exploration in the Cherek river valley when oil deposits were searched. Geologists and oil workers were very surprised when a hot spring began to beat from drilled wells instead of oil.

Having learned about the healing properties of water from the source, local residents began to dig and equip the pools here. The fame of them quickly spread throughout the Caucasus, and then throughout Russia.
