Port Imeretinsky

The mouth of the Mzymta River is known for having Adler, the largest district of Sochi. Now in this place the cargo port of Sochi Imeretinsky, the first built in the post-Soviet period, was built.





In addition, it is a universal seaport, which is designed as a single complex of wave protection and berthing facilities. It was created primarily to ensure the uninterrupted construction of Sochi Olympic facilities. After the Olympics, it was decided to redesign the port for a yacht marina for 600-700 yachts, and in 2014 the first stage of these grandiose plans was already implemented - a marina for 40 yachts was opened. The port is designed for 800 meters of coastline and according to the plan should have 8 berths.

The new birth of the waterfront

The embankment of the Mzymta River after the reconstruction envisaged by the preparations for the Olympics, solemnly opened at the end of the last month of autumn 2013. The opening was timed to the Day of the city.





You can imagine the joy of the inhabitants of the city and vacationers, given the fact that the last time it was renovated 40 years ago. Modern beaches of a modern resort town

Of course, in Adler there is a beach by the river Mzymta. All Adler beaches have one distinctive feature - they are covered with small round pebbles. It has all the necessary infrastructure, plus dozens of cafes and gift shops. On the beaches, including the Mzymta River, you can do almost all kinds of sea sports - parasailing (parachuting, tied to a boat), diving and windsurfing, you can ride a jet ski, washer and banana. Divers are actively using breakwaters. On the remote coast of Mzymta there is a nudist beach. You can get here from the central areas of Adler by crossing the river. It is located at a distance of 500 meters from the pier.
