Gold galun

It is used to decorate uniforms, as well as in the manufacture of insignia. Gold galun is such a woven braid or ribbon with a dense weft.

For the highest ranks, the galun is made of thread, which varies from 3 to 5% of gilding. Due to this composition, it is very strong and durable.

Historical findings

Historians find a huge variety of different antiques decorated with embroidery and gowns. Even in the time of the Vikings in Scandinavia, weaving from a dragged thread was widespread. Often the embroidery was done in gold, and on the edge was framed by a gimbal from a simple thin wire.

In Hedeby, Birka, Gotland there are gimp products similar to animal images. So, for example, a deer was found, woven from a very thin wire, inlaid with small stones, and the base on which it seems to be standing was made from a gutter. Various pendants are also found, with inserts of mica or leather ribbons. Some positions and knots are woven incredibly beautiful, they are almost impossible to repeat.
