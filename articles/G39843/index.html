How to make a rose from a napkin in a minute

To make a rose from a napkin, you only need sleight of hand and, in fact, the paper napkin itself. The time for making the flower will take no more than two or three minutes, and when you get the hang of it, you can fold them in a matter of seconds. What can be done with these paper creations? Almost the same as with fresh flowers: they can decorate a festive table, they can be presented as

cute presents and much more.

To make a rose out of paper, you don‚Äôt need much talent for needlework. It is enough to just use a simple instruction, and you will definitely get nice flowers. And if you take multi-colored napkins, then a bunch of roses from them will turn out to be more fun and bright.

So, before making a rose from a napkin, you need to slightly clear the table at which you are sitting. A fairly small surface. Now we take an ordinary paper napkin and completely unfold it. Before you lies a square of thin soft paper, from which we are now folding our flower. How to make a rose from a napkin? The very first step is to gently roll one side of this paper square into a thin tube. The width of the rolled up area should be only half a centimeter. When the entire side is evenly curled along the entire length, take a napkin on the corner and wrap the rolled side on two fingers. We do this in such a way that the tube, which was twisted before, could turn out.



Now that the napkin is wound around the fingers to the end, we retreat from the edge of the resulting roll about five centimeters, then squeeze the paper and twist it, forming a stalk. We continue to tightly twist into a tourniquet approximately until the middle of the remaining ‚Äúcuttings‚Äù. The next moment of how to make a rose from a napkin will be the formation of a leaf. So, twisting the stalk to the middle, stop and separate the corner from the remaining incomplete paper. It needs to be slightly turned out and pulled up to the very point where the twisting of the stem was stopped. Having formed a leaf of flower from the corner, we press its base to the handle and continue to roll the paper right to the very end.

That's the whole secret of how to make a rose from a napkin. After you rolled it up, you can straighten the bud a little, give it a more realistic look. You can slightly turn away the corner, creating the effect of a bent petal. The middle can be twisted more strongly with a spiral, the edge is beautifully formed. Do everything as you see fit. You can even drip some pink oil into the middle so that your paper flowers exude a fragrance.



Of course, there are many other options for making roses out of napkins, but they are more complex, require a little more perseverance and sleight of hand. And those flowers that we just learned to twist can be done even while sitting in a cafe with a girl. If a friend is prone to romance, she will definitely appreciate this cute craft. Although, of course, if you are in a serious relationship, it is best to give real flowers, and use paper flowers just as a pleasant addition.
