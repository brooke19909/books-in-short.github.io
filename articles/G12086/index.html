Resort Ada Boyana in Montenegro

In the south of Montenegro is a picturesque island, under the wonderful name of Ada. Every year a huge number of tourists come here, attracted by beautiful nature, warm climate and magnificent beaches.





Resort Ada Boyana

In Montenegro, it is located on the border with Albania. On both sides, the island is washed by the Boyana River, after which the resort is named, and on the third side by the Adriatic Sea. The history of the resort of Ada Boyana in Montenegro

There are no attractions and historical sites on the island. Basically, tourists come here to observe the beauty of nature, breathe clean air and soak up the beach.





Ada Boyana in Montenegro was first mentioned in the annals only in the late 1850s. During a storm, a ship sank at the mouth of the Boyana River. Gradually, the ship's hull was littered with sand, which then formed into a sandy beach of a triangular shape.

On the territory of the island there is a nudist village in which about 100 people live permanently. You can get to the village only through a wooden bridge, which serves as a conditional divider between the modern world and the other, free from everyday rules and conventions. Ways to get to the island

The cost of air tickets from Moscow to Montenegro is different. So, for example, to Tivat from Moscow in September you can get from 4 to 7 thousand one way. To Podgorica, the price is approximately the same: in September it varies from 4 to 10 thousand rubles. Flight time takes from 3 to 5 hours.

The distance from the above cities of Montenegro to the island is the same. Buses run from the bus station of Podgorica daily to the island. The price is about 300 rubles, the trip time will be about 90 minutes. From the airport to the city station, you can get for 500-700 rubles using a taxi. Total: from the airport Podgorica to the resort can be reached in 2 hours, while spending about 1000-1500 rubles.

From Tivat station to the city located on the island only one bus runs, only at 9 am. The ticket price is about 600 rubles. If you got later than the specified time, it does not matter - you can get with transfers. At Tivat Airport you need to walk to the Yadrinsky Way and take any bus going to Budva. The ticket cost will be 200-250 rubles, and only 30 minutes on the road. From Budva, you can get to the island by bus, which leaves every hour. A ticket costs about 500 rubles, and you will have to spend about 2 hours on the road.

What to bring from Montenegro

The most important souvenir from Montenegro is, of course, wine. It is made from red and white grape varieties, and has an exquisite taste. The drink will delight even the most avid gourmet. Prices will especially surprise: the cost of a bottle starts from 200 rubles.





The real pride of the country is cheese. The country is mountainous, so cattle breeding is well developed here. Especially popular among tourists is goat cheese, as well as cheese in olive oil. The cost of 1 kg of cheese starts at 350 rubles.

In addition to the above, ceramic sculptures, a national milk drink, paintings, herbal infusions, elements of national clothes and much more are brought from Montenegro.
