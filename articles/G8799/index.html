Uman. Sofievsky park - a highlight of Cherkasy region (Ukraine)





Modest, but quite interesting, both historically and culturally, the town of Uman is located in the very center of Ukraine, in the Cherkasy region. Not everyone, even the locals, knows that this corner is visited annually by hundreds of thousands of tourists.

Why? What is the reason for this popularity? The thing is that this locality, thanks to its fame, is in many respects grateful to the amazing masterpiece of landscape gardening culture - a national dendrological object called "Sofievsky Park".

I would like to note that today this place is among the most beautiful territories of the world created by man. It surprises and fascinates with its fabulous grottoes, amazing landscapes, mirror ponds and magnificent sculptures.

Sophia Park (Ukraine) is a temple of nature, a beautiful monument of landscape art, one of the most romantic corners of the state, built in the name of love and serving as its symbol for more than 200 years.

How was this place created?





I would like to note right away that Sofievsky Park in the form in which we can observe it today is the result of several generations at once.

Initially, this masterpiece was laid in 1796. Its founder was the landowner Stanislav Pototsky, who owned about one and a half million hectares of land. The serfs, and according to documents that have reached us, the tycoon had about 150 thousand, were actively engaged in construction, successively replacing each other and freeing themselves from other types of work.

How did the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãcreating such a park come about? The thing is that the landowner, already married, fell madly in love with the married Greek woman Sofia, whom he met on one of his many trips. Despite the harsh laws of the time, lovers withstood all the lengthy divorce proceedings and received approval for the marriage of the Empress Katerina herself.

After all the trials that fell out, the couple settled in one of their estates near Uman and began to look for a place to create a corner where they could completely escape from problems and worries. The newlyweds chose a place where the two rivers merged - Kamyanka and Umanka, considering it the most successful for the realization of all that was planned.

The first architect of the park, Metzel, developed the main idea and master plan. Stanislav and Sofia wanted to recreate in this place an illustration for the ancient Greek poems Odyssey and Illiad.

Of course, for all the work required a lot of hands. Even according to the most rough estimates, Sofievsky Park was built by 800 workers who needed more than 10 years to do this.

The park was first opened in 1800, but at that time its construction was not yet fully completed. Work continued on the refinement of the ponds and the laying of alleys. Unfortunately, Count S. Pototsky did not wait for completion, as he died in 1805, having spent more than 2.5 million tsar rubles on his brainchild.

In 1830, the park became state-owned and received the name of the Tsaritsyno Garden. Emperors of Russia took an active part in its further arrangement and visited it at the first opportunity.

In the 80s of the twentieth century, numerous restoration work began in the park . Waterfalls, fountains, ponds, bridges, altanka and statues were restored.

How to get to Sofiyivka





As already mentioned above, Sofievsky Park, the history of which cannot fail to captivate even the most experienced traveler, is located in the Cherkasy region, in the city of Uman. You can get to Sofiyivka in different ways and on any type of transport. A lot of transit bus routes pass through this district center, so you can get here from almost all corners of the country without problems.

By the way, it should be noted that the main entrance to the Sophia Park is located just opposite the main bus station of the city, so tourists definitely will not have to wander around the narrow streets of an unfamiliar city.

You can also go to Uman by rail. Three shuttle buses go from the station to the park gates with intervals of several minutes. In this case, the journey will take no longer than 10 minutes.

But if you want to get in comfort, you should use the services of a local taxi.

Sofievsky park. Visit Prices





Entrance to the territory of the arboretum is controlled by the local administration and costs 25 UAH. for an adult and 15 UAH. for children.

For a fee, you can purchase a schematic map of this place with specially marked routes on it. When a group trip is still recommended to use the services of a guide.

In the central part of Sofiyivka, near the Upper Pond, there are a sufficient number of different attractions, the cost of tickets for which is the most diverse. For example, a bike rental will cost a visitor 50 hryvnias per hour, and you can ride a scooter or roller for 40 UAH. Many people like a walk on an open boat or a tour of the underwater river.

Also in the park there are enough stalls with thematic calendars, postcards, magnets and other souvenir products.

A place for walks of lovers





Sofiyivka is rightly called the most romantic corner of Ukraine. Why? The thing is that the park actually embodies the song of Count S. Pototsky‚Äôs love for beautiful Sofia.

There are white marble statues everywhere, wrapped in branches of exotic trees. Shady paths are ready to give coolness even in the grueling summer heat. Swans and ducks glide along the smooth surface of the mirror ponds, and the crystal clink of waterfalls merges with the singing of amazing birds.

The atmosphere is really romantic. No wonder Count S. Potocki often walked here with his Sofia. Their favorite routes were paths along the Upper Pond, the Ionian Sea, Lake Acheront, the Cretan Maze and the Valley of the Giants.

Legends and tales of Sofievsky park





Believe in signs? Then you definitely need to go to Sofievka as soon as possible. There is simply a huge number of secluded places specially designed for the fulfillment of desires. Touch the stone, lean against the rock, close your eyes while passing under the grotto, and your innermost will come true.

For example, according to one of the legends, if you go dry out of the water under the Great Waterfall, your plan will be fulfilled. By the way, we confirm from a personal example that it is not difficult to do this, since the passage under the waterfall allows you not to get wet.

The second story relates to the Calypso Grotto. She tells that if an unhappy person enters the grotto, he will become happy, and if happy, he will certainly become even happier. In order for all dreams to come true, it is necessary to walk around the stone column in the middle of the grotto with closed eyes three times. Also in the corner of the grotto flows a small fountain into which tourists throw a trifle for good luck.

Another legend tells of the miraculous healing by the waters of the Diana Mirror Grotto at the foot of Venus Falls. Every year sick people come here. They say that the source actually helps many.

Flora and fauna of the old park





According to many, this place actually acts as a real museum of wildlife. For many years, tens of thousands of exotic plants imported from around the world have found their new home here. Immediately near the entrance to the park is the main alley, completely planted with centuries-old French chestnuts and rare species of poplars.

But in the greenhouses Sofiyivka grown seedlings of the most beautiful plants of our planet, some of which can be purchased for a very reasonable fee. Also today, a rose garden operates in the park, in which several hundred different types of flowers are grown.

Various fish are found in the ponds, exotic ducks and majestic swans swim. On the trees they make nests and birds call each other. And in the thickets at any time of the year, you can see funny squirrels.

Hungry? Where can I eat?
