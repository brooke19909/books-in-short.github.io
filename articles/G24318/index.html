How is a hotel different from a motel? What is a motel?

The inn, caravanserai, hostel, hotel, hotel, motel - all these institutions solve one common problem: to give a decent temporary shelter to travelers. However, they vary greatly in infrastructure, level of comfort and service.

Hotel History

Hotel business originated in the Middle Ages. The weary travelers arranged a respite for themselves in the inns and in the roadside taverns with lodging rooms. In these simple establishments, staying for the night, they were content with a simple dinner and breakfast, fed horses.





Small hotels were built on the forks of roads, along trade routes and on the streets of large cities. As the need for people to travel comfortably developed the hotel chain. Hotels began to appear in cities and towns and resort areas of different countries.

In each state they were called in their own way: in Russia - hotels and inns, in Europe and America - hotels, in the East - caravanserais. Nowadays, in most countries, the term "hotel" is used to name these institutions. Motel - what is it

A motel is a type of hotel, the need for construction of which arose at the beginning of the 20th century. The institution is aimed at serving tourists traveling in a personal car. They are erected, as a rule, along highways.





Motel is a simplified name from the word MotorHotel. Such a hotel is a small hotel. It allows tourists to get the necessary respite, gain strength in a short period of time and go on a further journey.

The difference between a motel and brothers

Size and location - the first thing the hotel differs from a motel. Motels are much smaller than hotel and hotel complexes. Their height does not exceed two floors. Moreover, the first floor in some of them is a garage. Motels are built only along busy roads. While hotels are being built in cities and resorts.

Infrastructure is the second difference between a hotel and a motel. The hotel complexes are equipped not only with accommodation rooms, but also with rooms that solve some of the household issues (for example, laundries, hairdressers), a reception, conference and entertainment rooms, a lobby bar, medical centers, and spa complexes.

On their territory there are restaurants, shops, sports grounds, saunas, fitness rooms, pools with slides, bars and other amenities. It all depends on the stardom of the institution. Hotels with few stars offer a minimum of services. In this they look like a motel.

Another difference between the hotel and the motel is that the latter is necessarily equipped with a large parking lot for cars. The windows overlook the car park, which allows guests to keep an eye on the car. The entrance to the institution is located on the parking side, which is convenient for guests.





Number of rooms

The furnishings of the apartments are what distinguishes a hotel from a motel. In the hotel complexes, guests are offered accommodation in rooms of various categories. Tourists settle in apartments from modest standard to chic presidential.

The motel rooms are more than modest. The number of rooms is divided into rooms for smokers and those who are not addicted to a bad habit. They are furnished to a minimum: beds, bedside tables, a wardrobe and a refrigerator are all that are in them. Spartan furnishings are for short breaks. They usually stay for the night.

Service maintenance

The level of service is the last thing the hotel differs from the motel. Four-and five-star hotel complexes are able to provide first-class service. Travelers use the services of beauty salons, spa complexes, gyms, business centers, restaurants.

They organize animation, show programs, excursions, fishing and other entertainment. They have access to beaches, tennis courts, golf courses, billiards and bowling halls. Many hotels serve all-inclusive guests. Among them there are various health resorts, motels, boarding houses and recreation centers.





Motel class is much lower than hotels. They have an almost instant check-in (no more than 5 minutes). In institutions of this order travelers are populated at any time of the day.

Services are kept to a minimum. The staff is engaged in cleaning rooms where guests are accommodated. The rooms have Wi-Fi. Some MotorHotel guests staying a few days may use the laundry room or visit the gym.

Miniature roadside hotels include breakfast. In addition, the fridge installed in the rooms helps to solve the problem with food. In motels, unlike hotel complexes, the security system is not established, they do not have security guards. Roadside mini-hotels set the lowest price for accommodation.
