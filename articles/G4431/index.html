Geographical coordinates and natural features

In the Lomonosov district of the Leningrad region, the Gorovalday lake is spread. It was formed as a result of the separation of the Gulf of Finland by land, a narrow sandy hill.

In the south, the coastal zone is covered with forest (pine and mixed), in the east, the village of Gora-Valdai adjoins the lake. On the north side is a highway.

Lake Horowaldai is a pond that is fed by several large streams flowing in from the forest and drying out in hot weather, and a narrow canal connects it to the Gulf of Finland. The water surface is clean, transparent, not overgrown with plants. Due to the content of peat in the bottom soil, the color of the water sometimes becomes a reddish or yellowish hue. The bottom on almost all sides is sandy, only from the west and east it is rocky, at a depth it passes into silt.

Meaning of the name

Lake Horowaldai got its special name because of its close proximity to the village of Gora-Valdai, whose name is translated from Finnish as "elevated area". In addition, you can hear the second name - Shepelevsky Lake, which is also associated with the name of the nearby village of the same name. The latter was awarded as a reward to General S. A. Shepelev.



Flora and fauna

The forest belt adjacent to the lake on the south side involves a pine forest where a large number of mammals live: wolf, elk, wild boar, fox, bear, hare. The bird population is also diverse: geese, swans, capercaillie, ducks. The territory of the Gorovalday reservoir is rich in mushrooms, herbs, berries (lingonberries, raspberries). In the depths of the lake live ruff, pikeperch, bleak, pike, perch, roach, eel.
