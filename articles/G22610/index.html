Jimmy Savile: biography

Jimmy Savile is a well-known British DJ and TV presenter, after whose death evidence was found of his guilt in sexual harassment and rape of children.





short biography

Jimmy Savile was born in Leeds in a large family, where he was the youngest, seventh child. During World War II, Savile worked in coal mines, and also began to try himself as a DJ. Music played a very important role in his life, but Savile was interested in other things, including sports. He participated in bicycle tournaments, ran in a marathon, and also wrestled on a professional level.

Savile built a stunning career first on the radio, thanks to the mega-pop show Top of the Pops, and then through successful television projects. Savil died in 2011, presumably from pneumonia.





Work with the Air Force

Since 1958, Savile began working at Radio Luxembourg, where he led his own program. Over time, he switches to the Air Force radio, where he continues to work on a variety of music shows. Later, he launches other projects on the same radio, so for many Savile, BBC words become inseparable, since almost all successful projects on BBC Radio 1 belonged to Savil. His success in Britain was so great that only a few years later he became the face and hallmark of the Air Force.

A few years later, a very successful morning show was also launched, where Savile acted as the host of the program. On this show, he used old hits, which was the first program of his kind.





Participation in other projects

In 1987, after 19 years of working with the Air Force, he leaves the company and begins to work with other British radio stations in almost the same format in which he worked for the Air Force. In parallel, he also participated in various television projects, all of which were megapopular in Britain. For his work, he was even awarded the title of Knight of the Order of Malta from the Queen.

In addition, Sir Jimmy Savile launched a series of public service commercials that warned of the importance of road safety. The Clunk Click Every Trip video was especially successful, the words of which, every British, no doubt knew. This later became the basis for his Saturday night show, which was followed by others. In 2007, he launched a new project, which not only repeated the success of previous shows, but even beat him. Almost until the end of his days, Jimmy Savile remained at the peak of popularity.
