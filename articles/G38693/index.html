"Zenith 12 CD": a review of the camera and instructions

In modern technology markets there are a huge number of different innovations in the field of cameras. But is it worth it to make a decent blow to the budget by acquiring modern photographic equipment? Or is it worth paying attention to a Soviet camera, proven over the years? In this article, this issue is discussed in more detail, and everyone can make their choice.

History of creation

All Zenith cameras are Soviet single-lens reflex cameras with small format shooting. They are also one of the first pentaprism single-lens reflex cameras in the world. Produced from 1952 to 1956. Their prototype was the Zorkiy rangefinder camera, which has been produced at the Krasnogorsk Mechanical Plant since 1949.

In the redesigned case, which had a shutter from the "Vigilant", a lifting mirror was installed, which had the ability to create a valid image on the focusing screen.

The Zenit-12 SD camera. Specifications

Zenit-12 SD is a new line in the list of all cameras from the Zenit series.

This new product differs from its ancestors by the appearance of LED indicators. Also, TTL metering has become more accurate than in previous versions.

More detailed specifications:

  • The photographic material used in the camera is a perforated film with a width of 35 mm.
  • The frame size is 24 by 36 mm.
  • The body is made of aluminum alloy, which opens from the rear. There is also a hidden castle.
  • Shutter cock - trigger.
  • The shutter is mechanical.
  • Shutter speed is from 1/30 to 1/500 sec.
  • The sync speed with the flash is 1/30 s.
  • The lens "Helios-44M-4."
  • Mechanical self-timer.

Zenit 12 SD. Instruction manual

The process of creating high-quality images is not very simple. It has its details and some but. Therefore, before you go outside and feel like a professional photographer, you need to familiarize yourself with the rules of shooting so that the pictures are not taken in vain.

  • Exposure

It must be configured in any camera. But on modern models, this is done very easily and simply. On the "Zenith" setting does not take much time, but in some ways it differs significantly.

So, in order to adjust the shutter speed, you need to rotate the shutter speed dial so that the value is set against the index, which is located on the top panel of the camera. During installation, you should feel a disk lock.

The numbers on the scale indicate the shutter speed, which will occur in certain fractions of a second.

You can install it both before and after the shutter.

  • Diaphragm.

In order to configure it and select the required value, it is necessary to set its value against the installation index by turning a special ring. If the camera has a Helios-44M lens, you must set the aperture mode switch to "A" in advance.

  • Sharpness.

In order to achieve sharpness as best as possible, you need to aim only when the aperture is in the open state.

You can also adjust sharpness without the help of the viewfinder. To do this, it is necessary to set the distance from the object and to the film against the large index of the scale "30", and the focus ring must be rotated.

A small index, which is indicated by the letter "R", is used when shooting on infrared material. Therefore, in the case of photographing in this way, pointing at the sharpness of the microraster or the matte surface, it will be necessary to make a small correction, which consists in setting the obtained distance value against the index. It is indicated by the letter "R".

  • The process of creating a photo.

After all the basic details are configured and their correctness and accuracy is checked, you must gently press the shutter button, which will begin taking pictures.

You need to remember one main rule that will help to recreate beautiful, clear and high-quality images. In no case should you press the shutter button sharply. Due to a sharp press on it, the camera will inevitably startle. As a result, you can get a blurry image that will need to be redone.

All the rules for using the camera during shooting are not so complicated, so the recreation of a high-quality image does not take much time and effort.

Camera advantages

Despite the fact that this camera was released a long time ago, even in our time it can compete with the most modern photographic equipment, thanks to its advantages.

  • Ability to set the correct exposure.
  • Thanks to the mirror of constant sight, you can continuously monitor the subject.
  • The lens has a jumping aperture mechanism, which can automatically close when the shutter is released.
  • Thanks to the fully open aperture, maximum image brightness is ensured.
  • Focusing can be performed both on a micro-raster and on a frosted surface.
  • Built-in self-timer.
  • The increased reliability of locking the back cover, thanks to its hidden lock.

Rules for handling the camera

The camera is a fairly accurate optical-mechanical device, which must be handled with the greatest care. Also, you must always carefully monitor its cleanliness, avoid any shock and much more, which can significantly affect the deterioration of the functionality of the device.

Several storage rules:

  • If the camera is brought from a cold into a warm room, then you need to wait a bit, as an instant temperature drop can affect its performance.
  • Optical parts should never be touched by hands, as this can lead to any damage to the surface.
  • It is necessary to regularly wipe the optical coated surfaces with a clean and soft cloth or cotton wool, slightly moistened with rectified alcohol.
  • In case of contamination of the surface of the mirror itself or small elements, it is necessary to clean only with a soft brush (cotton swab). Never use wet cleaning!
  • You need to store the camera in a closed case, while the lens must be closed by a cover.
  • All additional accessories, such as lenses, light filters, converters for the Zenit 12 SD camera, need to be bought only in specialized stores.
  • You need to remove the lens only if necessary, since frequent removal can provoke frequent pollution and unnecessary dust particles entering the device.
  • Charging and discharging should be done only if the camera is completely protected from sunlight.
  • If shooting takes place in cold weather, in no case should you leave the unit in the open air.
  • Any repair work of the camera should be carried out only in special workshops, since only there they can know how to properly disassemble the Zenit 12 SD.

Is it worth buying?

But whether or not to buy this camera is up to the potential buyer to decide. If a person prefers more modern, high-quality, popular and expensive photo equipment, then the answer is unequivocal - no.

But if the company, year of manufacture and cost are not so important, then in this case the answer is yes, it is definitely worth buying.
