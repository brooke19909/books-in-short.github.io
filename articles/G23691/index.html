Classification of pathologies

Women and men see colors differently, dichromants are often found - not able to determine one of the main shades. So, people with pathology of the visual organ are divided into the following types:

  • Those suffering from protanopia - do not see red, the color merges with blue, yellow, green. People with pathology are not able to distinguish a green tint, merging with brown or yellow.
  • Having deuteranopia - the colors are mixed in a different order: green with orange or pink, and red with light green or brown.




Such people are identified using special drawings with mixed colors. If you look closely, a healthy eye will see the contours of a pattern, letters or numbers. For a person with deuteranopia or protanopia, the image will be seen as a solid texture.
