Temple of Hephaestus in Athens

Travelers who have managed to visit many countries recommend that beginners in this business begin acquaintance with history and study of ancient architectural masterpieces from a visit to Greece. The Temple of Hephaestus is very well preserved, there are few analogues in the degree of preservation in the world. He has native columns, pediments and almost the entire roof. Decorations and murals were most affected.





Economics and Monuments

Only in Greece, which is currently experiencing difficulties in the economic sphere, can one see many historical monuments and other interesting places created by our distant ancestors long before the birth of Jesus Christ. In ancient times, the country experienced both prosperity and periods of stagnation, but, unfortunately, mainly on this land there were fierce wars between strangers who dreamed of taking control of this country. The Temple of Hephaestus in Athens still attracts tourists.

All these facts influenced the preservation of a large number of temples and shrines, some of them at the beginning of the XX century, archaeologists literally dug up. Among the small number of buildings that have survived to this day is the world famous Temple of Hephaestus. A large number of preserved written sources speak of the Athenian agora, but the meaning of this word is difficult to find. To understand the significance of the building, you need to tell what this agora was for the inhabitants of Greece at that time. The Athenian agora was located in the center of Athens and served as a venue for gatherings, ceremonies, competitions and fairs. We can say that it was an analogue of the forum among the Romans, erected before the birth of Jesus Christ.

On this place and near it only temples, theaters and other constructions significant for the population were built. The temple of Hephaestus on the Agora was one of them. Now it can be seen by tourists who visit Athens. Interestingly, the temple survived to this day not because of the love of the Greeks for paganism, but thanks to Orthodoxy.





Hephaestus

Myths of Ancient Greece say that Hephaestus was the son of Zeus and Hera. According to legend, during the next quarrel between Zeus and the Hero of Hephaestus they threw him on a volcanic island. Divine origin did not save him from injuries - he received a broken leg and began to limp. The Temple of Hephaestus is a building populated by myths and various stories.

A detailed examination of all the mosaics and images, legends and myths that tell about the life of the gods, we can conclude that all the gods constantly feasted. And only as an entertainment did they come down to people. And only Hephaestus worked without interruption, because he was a blacksmith and had power over fire and volcanoes. He made the best weapons and equipment for the ancient Greek warrior Achilles, who became famous for his vulnerable place - the "Achilles heel." The life of the blacksmith god was difficult and passed by the insanely hot fire hearth. Fans of mythology seek to visit the temple of Hephaestus. Photos of the monument can be found in many guidebooks.





Temple history

According to scientists, researchers who studied various written sources and traditions, this temple was erected during the reign of Pericles. He possessed the ability to convince citizens with speeches, and his talented command and control helped to defend himself against enemy attacks with minimal losses. It is believed that the reign of Pericles is the golden age of Athens. On his orders, this famous building was erected.

It was built over 35 years, starting from 450 BC. It is not difficult to notice that during this time there was the possibility of building several such facilities. But sources tell us that many of the people involved were sent to erect the huge Parthenon. The majestic turned out to be the temple of Hephaestus. Athens is more famous for it.

Temple architecture

The Temple of Hephaestus is one of the few structures that have survived to this day in excellent condition, this is its amazingness. It is located on the hill of Agoraios, the dimensions of the building are 31.7 x 13.7 m. Thirty-four columns and the roof of the building have been miraculously preserved. It should be noted the ionic style of the frieze of the temple where Hephaestus was revered. Eighteen of the sixty-eight metopes are made in the form of sculptures. Metope will tell travelers about the exploits of Hercules and the adventures of Theseus.

Sculptures that are not

The famous thinker Pausanias, who described what he noticed during his wanderings around the world, in his notes tells that in the middle of the temple there were 2 large bronze sculptures:

  • the lord of fire Hephaestus;

Unfortunately, these statues, like many murals and mosaics, are destroyed and stolen by enemies and robbers.

Their opinion is not unfounded, because at that time most of the shrines for worshiping the gods were built in the Doric style.
