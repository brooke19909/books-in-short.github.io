Openwork scarf (hook): diagram, photo, description

On hot summer days, it is absolutely necessary to observe safety measures and protect your head from direct sunlight. This recommendation is relevant for both adults and children. Any headgear is suitable for protection: panama hats, bandanas and scarves.





For lovers of knitting, this is a great chance to show their abilities. Openwork scarf (hook) will be useful to them and their children. A simple fabrication scheme and yarn tips are provided below.

What to knit

Obviously, the thickness of the thread used should not be large, yarn from a long thread of 400-500 m / 100 grams is best suited. In this case, the material consumption will be small, and the canvas will come out very thin and light.

As for the composition of the yarn, here the answer is clear: only natural fibers. Cotton or linen scarf (hook) will cope with its functions best of all. The scheme of almost any openwork pattern will allow you to connect a beautiful canvas.





Many beginner knitters think that acrylic, which is so similar to cotton, has the same properties. This is completely untrue: artificial materials do not allow air to pass through, cause excessive sweating, and can lead to allergies. Even if the cloth of the scarf is tied very openwork, the product can still be hot.

The following is the manufacturing algorithm for the universal size scarf (it is suitable for adults and children):

  1. Knitting a belt: make a chain of five air loops (VP) and knit a narrow long strip with double crochets (STN). When its length reaches 65-70 cm, stop work.
  2. Directly scarf (hook). The diagram illustrates the method of connecting two parts of the product: a triangle begins to knit on the side of the girdle. In order for the scarf cloth to be located clearly in the middle of the belt, you should mark its center and observe symmetry in the work.
  3. The pattern consists in the alternation of "bushes" of four ST and three VP.

How is the beveling performed?

Triangle Knitting Sequence:

1st row: 3VP, 3StN, * 3VP, 4StN *. Repeat from * to * 5 times.

2nd row: 3 connecting posts (to move to the next cell), 3 VP, 3StN, * 3VP, 4StN *, repeat 4 times.

Do you need strapping?

On this work is considered completed. If desired, the craftswoman can tie the scarf with one or several rows of single crochet, but you will have to follow the rules for forming corners: on the ledges you need to knit 3StBN from the highest point, and in the troughs - 3StBN with a common top. The value of the binding is that then a denser scarf (hook) is obtained. The scheme does not provide for this stage, but experienced craftswomen recommend tying (for strengthening) all open edges of any product.
