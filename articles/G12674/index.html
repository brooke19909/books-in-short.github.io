Is it hard to get to the White House in Washington?

One of the world's most famous office buildings is the White House in Washington. All his textbooks on American history adorn his photo. The official residence of the President of the United States of America is one of the most important symbols of American statehood. And besides, it is also an ordinary residential building of a person temporarily at the highest state post.

From the history

The White House in Washington was the residence of all presidents except the very first. During the reign of George Washington, it was not yet built. Construction lasted eight years and was completed in 1800, during the reign of the second president, John Adams. The building is made in the best traditions of European classicism. Its architect James Hoban was faced with a rather difficult task. The White House in Washington was supposed to correspond to the status of its inhabitant and at the same time to radically differ from all the palaces of the ruling European monarchies. Outward majesty was to be combined with emphasized democracy. After more than two hundred years, we can confidently say that the architect coped with his task simply brilliantly. In the history of the White House, fires and reconstructions took place, at the beginning of the twentieth century two side wings were attached to the building. But the overall compositional concept remained unchanged.



About architectural features

The White House in Washington has been emphatically deprived of external luxury. The building is arranged quite functionally. With a relatively low height, it consists of six floors. Two of which are deepened into the base and are mainly used for technical support services. Two more floors are allocated for the office of the head of state and for the personal apartments of his family. There are two expressive facades aimed at opposite sides of the world. But the visible image of the White House is most often associated with a semicircular colonnade in the form of a rotunda in the depths of the park. In addition to the famous Oval Office, where the President signs government papers and receives visitors, the East, Green, Blue and Red rooms and the Dining Room for official events should be assigned to historically significant premises.
