Acoustic system Edifier R980T: specifications and reviews

Although most users install their surround speakers with a main TV, console, or PC, many of them listen to music on a pair of good old speakers. In their opinion, stereo sounds better than 5.1 or 7.1 kits. And in this we can agree with them. Of course, stereo speakers come in different shapes and sizes, and are also made from various materials, but in the end it comes down to needs and, of course, the means allocated to meet them. For example, more powerful and expensive floor models are suitable for larger rooms, while less productive two-way speakers are designed for smaller rooms. With the exception of music lovers, most people will not spend a lot of money on stereo, and since floor speakers take up too much space, more compact models become the obvious choice. Edifier currently produces a large number of active shelf-mounted speakers, but its R980T reviewed here is aimed at meeting the needs of consumers who want to get their hands on low-cost stereo speakers with good sound quality and great bass.

Edifier R980T Design Review

The speakers themselves seem ordinary. They are completely black, except for the red connection points on the rear panel. The front cover is dominated by a foam cover that can be removed with the company logo on its bottom. A standard power cord connection and four audio inputs are provided. Next to them are the bass and volume control knobs, as well as a power switch. At the base of each monitor are red and black speaker cable connectors.

Testing

Users who tested the speakers listened to a lot of music, and also used them for games and watching movies. This allowed them to get a good idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe sound quality of the speakers in various scenarios and gave enough time to check all the tunable parameters to achieve the best performance of the device.

The performance of an inexpensive system was pleasantly surprised. When it comes to games, the Edifier speakers provide the necessary sound volume, an acceptable level of bass and clarity sufficient to reproduce explosions and shots. Similarly, they cope well with more peaceful games. The speakers are able to perfectly convey the eerie atmosphere, full of the sounds of dripping pipes and the creak of fans. Xenomorph sounds more horrific than ever, which is probably the best score for Edifier acoustics.

The speakers provide sufficient volume, and can easily fill a small room with sound. The stereo effect, unless surround sound settings are used, is also excellent. Of course, the vertical stereo pair was designed not only for gamers, it can also play music and, despite the relatively low price, does it extremely well. The speakers are struggling to give out bass, but for a budget system, the sound quality is very commendable. For perfect playback, you will need something else that can transmit the lower frequency range, since low-frequency tests at ultra-low levels did not give anything but a dull rumble, but medium-low frequencies will satisfy the most demanding music lovers.





According to user reviews, speakers are a good substitute for conventional speakers sold with ready-made PCs, and will be the cheapest way to dramatically improve sound quality. Although it is strange to have a sound card that costs more than a speaker system, but if you can buy it in the secondary market, then you should definitely use it - it can also help improve dynamics and tighten bass.

The woofer knob on the back of the main speaker must be carefully adjusted. Turning it too high will have a negative effect on the overall sound quality, and the bass will become less focused and flabby.

Job evaluation

Acoustics Edifier R980T has been available for several years, but, unlike most enterprises in the industry, the technology that underpins the audio system has not changed at all. It is because of this that the speakers are so accessible and show good mid and high frequencies, not subject to distortion, with sufficient bass even for the most demanding consumers. This, of course, largely depends on the signal source, since when connected, for example, to a PC, tablet or smartphone, the sound power is good enough for the dimensions of the Edifier R980T. Advantages and disadvantages

According to user reviews, the strengths of the speaker system are good build quality, crystal clear sound, relatively good low frequencies, the presence of an amplifier, sufficient sound power, which, however, depends on the signal source, dual RCA input, the presence of volume and bass controls, and also low price. However, dissatisfaction is caused by the lack of ability to regulate the HF and the inconvenient location of the control.

So is it worth buying the Edifier R980T?

Users recommend purchasing this model if the budget is limited and you need to update the standard speakers that come with the PC. Speakers can easily fill a small room with sound, and they reproduce surprisingly many low frequencies even without a dedicated subwoofer. In addition to volume, they are unusually clear and transmit the midrange well. Music, games and movies sound equally good. Acoustics do worse with more complex compositions, but it is impossible to be too critical, given the modest price of the speaker system.





This model is probably not interesting for music lovers. But if there is a desire to improve the sound of a laptop or mid-level gaming system without considering the use of headphones, then the R980T will serve well. The build quality is very decent, and although a simple MDF serves as the material of the case, the wood finish will not allow the speakers to stand out noticeably against the background of other equipment and peripherals. Overall, the Edifier R980T is a great way to start your journey on the road to audio excellence. This is just the first, but surprisingly big step for a speaker system at that price.
