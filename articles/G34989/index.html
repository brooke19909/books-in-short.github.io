Mstinsky bridge - a unique engineering structure and the village of the same name

Mstinsky Bridge is a small village in the Novgorod region. The settlement received its name from the railway bridge and station of the same name. This village is very popular among tourists due to the picturesque nature, favorable ecology and local attractions.

Mstinsky bridge - one of the most interesting sights of the Malovishersky district



The Petersburg-Moscow railway was commissioned in 1851. This highway was designed "in a straight line" - without taking into account the features of the landscape. The total length of the road at the time of its opening is 604 versts, while the shortest distance between cities is 598 versts. For this reason, many bridges are included in the highway.

Some of them were a real miracle of engineering for their time. For example, the Mstinsky bridge was the first in our country built with a caisson method. Its length is about 380 meters, and its height above water level is about 40 meters. The bridge was built in the upper part of the bend of the deep-sea river Msta and due to this arrangement it looks even more impressive.

At the end of the XIX century, the building was rebuilt. The existing wooden bridge was replaced with an iron one, which we can observe today. Today, this facility is operational and is included in the high-speed railway. The bridge is guarded and tourists are not allowed to climb it. But anyone can take pictures of an interesting building from a short distance or go down near it to the river.

Village near Mstinsky bridge



In the Malovishersky District of the Novgorod Region there is a small village named after the railway bridge over the Msta River. Today, only about 300 people live here permanently. However, in the warmer months, the population rises to 1,500 inhabitants. The village boasts a fairly developed infrastructure. It has its own school, kindergarten, shops, a post office, a medical center, and a MTS cellular repeater. Agriculture is developed here, in the vicinity there are quite large farms. Some local residents work in neighboring settlements.

Endless possibilities for ecotourism



Novgorod region has a favorable climate. Malovishersky district boasts a variety of landscapes, clean air and good environmental conditions. The village of Mstinsky Most is located at a distance from large industrial facilities. It is surrounded by green meadows, fields and dense forests.

The Msta River is deep and wide. In the warmer months, locals bathe and swim here. Not far from the bridge there are rapids that lovers of extreme relaxation are happy to overcome by kayaking. Local residents and tourists fish on the river year-round. In summer and autumn, in the vicinity of the village you can pick mushrooms, berries and herbs.

As for the accommodation of vacationers, local residents willingly rent rooms and summer houses. Also in the vicinity of the village there are several tourist recreation centers. Many tourists prefer to stop by the Msta River with tents. In the winter season, a ski resort operates on the left bank.

Sights and interesting objects

Mstinsky Bridge and the railway station of the same name, located on the side of St. Petersburg, are very popular among tourists. Today the church is operational, an old cemetery has been preserved near it. On weekends and church holidays, services are held here.

How to get to the Mstinsky Bridge?



From St. Petersburg it is easiest to get to this amazing area by rail. The first destination is Malaya Vishera (electric trains and buses from the Northern capital go here). Next, you need to take an electric train to Okulovka, the Mstinsky Most station will be in about half an hour's journey.

By private car from St. Petersburg you must move along the M-10 highway to the turn to Malaya Vishera. Take the exit and continue straight along the main road. After Malaya Vishera and Burgi there will be the village of Mstinsky Most.

Reviews about the rest in Malovishersky district



Tourists who want to relax on the Msta River should understand that life in these places is rustic. There are no five-star hotels, and even local recreation centers offer fairly modest living conditions. If you are used to maximum comfort, look for another option. The Novgorod region is quite large, and it has modern hotel complexes and resorts.

Malovishersky district is a place for a quiet contemplative relaxation and ecotourism. Here you can enjoy the peace and fresh air. Locals live here almost the same way as a hundred years ago. There is no central water supply and gas supply in the village, instead of them there are wells and gas in bottles. But during the rest you can try real milk, eggs, fresh vegetables and fruits. The villagers gladly sell their own products for a nominal fee, and sometimes treat tourists for free.

If you like this kind of rest, be sure to remember how to get to the station. Mstinsky Bridge (Novgorod region).
