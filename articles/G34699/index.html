Crossed loops

Crossed loops are the basis with which the patterns of ‚Äúarana‚Äù are made with knitting needles. Schemes often contain only an abbreviated description of their implementation: to cross any number of loops in a certain direction. However, not everyone knows how this technique is performed.





Crossing loops will be pronounced if it is tied to the wrong side. That is, to obtain the background, it is necessary to knit the wrong loops in odd rows , and the front ones in even rows. In this case, the tracks themselves are formed on the front side of the canvas with facial loops. Let us consider in more detail the intersection of four loops left and right. The direction in this case indicates which direction the slope will be.

In order to cross four loops to the left, it is necessary to remove 2 loops on an additional knitting needle without knitting, and leave it in front of the canvas. Then, knit the next 2 stitches in the front stitch and, returning to the additional knitting needle, also stitch out stitches from the stitches. The only difference when performing crossing with an inclination to the right is that the deferred loops are left behind the knitted fabric.

After this, the first loop on the left knitting needle is knitted face and stretched through an elongated loop. The remaining loops in girth are knitted according to the pattern.

Bumps
