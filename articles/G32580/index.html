How old is the universe and how is its age calculated?

There are a lot of guesses as to how many years of the universe are at the moment. With absolute certainty, it is now impossible to answer the question of her age. Yes, and it is unlikely to ever be able to find an exact answer to it. But scientists conducted a lot of research and calculations, so now this topic has more or less clear outlines.

Cold Star Computing Methods

There is another way to find out how old the universe is. And it consists in monitoring the glow of the so-called white dwarfs. They are very high temperature celestial bodies of rather small sizes. About the size of Earth. They represent the last stage of the existence of any star. With the exception of those that are gigantic in size. A star turns into a white dwarf after all of its thermonuclear fuel is burned. Before that, she still undergoes some cataclysms. For example, for a while it becomes a red giant.

Dwarfs burn their hydrogen very slowly, so their life span can reach hundreds of millions of years. And all this time they shine thanks to the accumulated energy. And in parallel they cool down. And scientists, calculating the rate of their cooling, determine the amount of time that the star needs to lower the temperature from the one that was the original (as a rule, it is 150,000 K). To calculate how many years the Universe exists, you need to find the coldest white dwarfs. At the moment, it was possible to find stars with a temperature of 4000 K. Scientists, having carefully studied all the data with this information in mind, claim that our Universe cannot be older than 15 billion years.





The study of spherical clusters from stars

It is worth turning to this method, telling about how many years of the universe, according to scientists. These clusters are located in the peripheral zone of the Milky Way. And they revolve around its core. And determining the date of their formation helps to find out the lower boundary of the age of our Universe.

The method is technically complicated. However, in its essence lies the simplest idea. After all, all clusters appear from one cloud. So they arise, one might say, at the same time. And for a certain time, hydrogen is burned in some quantities. How does it all end? The appearance of a white dwarf or the formation of a neutron star.

Several years ago, research of this kind was carried out by astronauts using the ACS camera of a space telescope known as the Hubble. So according to scientists, how old is the universe?
