Where to go in Barcelona: travel tips

Barcelona is one of the most famous cities in Spain. We invite you to get acquainted with its most interesting sights. If you happen to be in a wonderful city such as Barcelona by chance or on business, then our tips will come in handy. This metropolis is not only attractive for museums and cathedrals, but also for its musical atmosphere right on La Rambla Boulevard, where street artists perform and where many tourists dream of going. So Barcelona. Where to go, what to see, travel tips - this is the topic of this article.



Top 5 most famous sights of Barcelona

Immediately define the main objects:

  1. The Holy Family Cathedral is rightfully considered the pride of the Spanish town. This architectural masterpiece with a unique form of structure belongs to Antonio Gaudi. The cathedral remains beautiful at any time of the year.
  2. The monument to Christopher Columbus was erected at the end of the Rambla Boulevard. The monument is located in the same place where the traveler arrived at the end of the fateful campaign, and rises as much as 60 meters above the port. You will be able to climb a small observation deck, where you will have a magnificent view of the sea and the city.
  3. If you are interested in painting, then you will probably be interested to see the Picasso Museum, where there are paintings by this great artist. Basically, it contains paintings of early creativity, but there are later works.
  4. We advise you to pay attention to the Boqueria market. It has existed for several hundred years, and it is here that you can find the most delicious and cheap products in the city. You will be pleasantly surprised by the friendly atmosphere of this place. Here, the buyer will be allowed to try any product and answer all his questions.
  5. The Spanish village was built with the aim of collecting architectural styles of many cities in Spain. It includes many buildings, some structures are made to scale. Workshops of various artisans, grocery and souvenir shops operate in them. There you can watch a flamenco show and have a hearty lunch at a restaurant or pub.

Where to go in Barcelona: local tips

Additionally, the following attractions will be recommended to each traveler:

  1. Park Guell. It is rightfully considered one of the most beautiful and frequently visited parks in the city. Located at the top of the metropolis and offers an amazing view from above. Here you will not only enjoy a walk in the middle of amazing nature, but also relax from the noise of the city. There are also playgrounds for children.
  2. Mount Tibidabo and the Temple of the Sacred Heart. They are several hundred meters above Barcelona. You may already know the name of this mountain from the Gospel. If not, then we will be pleased to remind you that, according to legend, it was here that Satan tempted Jesus with indescribable beauty and power. The temple itself is made in the Gothic style and has a magnificent interior. And on its top stands a statue of Christ.

Top 5 little-known attractions

If time permits, we offer a list of several more objects:

  1. The Statue of Liberty. We are sure that you least expected to see this statue here, but this is so, and it can indeed be found in the Arus public library. Of course, in size it is very different from the original and reaches a height of about two meters. She also has a color that, by the way, is black. And the inscription is engraved on the book, which in Latin means "freedom of the soul."
  2. Temple of Augustus. If you once dreamed of traveling back a couple of thousand years and finding yourself in the era of the great Roman Empire, then this place will easily help you do this. The four oldest columns are all that remain of the Temple of Augustus. But they are rightfully considered the same age as this city.
  3. The Baro de Cuadras Palace will surprise you with its mixture of styles and richness. And the main feature of this building is its many-sided architecture. It exquisitely combines Gothic style and modernism. The industrialist Manuel de Cuadras worked on this palace, and most recently it was open for tourists.
  4. If you consider yourself a sweet tooth, then be sure to visit the Museum of Chocolate. A variety of chocolate figures will delight you. In addition, here you can study the process of its preparation. You can try yourself to cook some goodies at the ongoing master class.
  5. Ravalsky cat fits perfectly into the architecture of the main street. It is believed that if you want to return to Barcelona again, you should definitely touch it with your hand. This sculpture is made by Colombian Fernando Botero and weighs about two tons.




Where to go with children in Barcelona

If you came here with your family, we suggest you visit the following places:

  • Port Aventura Park is the second in Europe after the famous Disneyland. Although it is far away, it will not be difficult to get there. The park combines the scenery of 5 cultures: China, Polynesia, Mexico, the Mediterranean and the Wild West. So very soon you will be divorced from reality and plunge into the fascinating atmosphere of this place. You will also be pleased with a huge selection of original attractions, among which there are water attractions.
  • There are several aquariums in the seaport, in one of them sharks swim.
  • The Wax Museum.
  • At the zoo, your children will be delighted with the dolphin show of other animals.

La Rambla Boulevard

It is always crowded, because this place is considered one of the hallmarks of Barcelona. The length of the street is more than a kilometer. On it you can buy the most diverse and unusual souvenirs. Walking along the boulevard, you will see a statue of the famous navigator and discoverer of land - Christopher Columbus. Many tourists with great pleasure take pictures next to her.





Helpful information

Where to go in Barcelona and what to see, you already know, we suggest you take advantage of some recommendations that can make your stay more intense and safe:

  • Some museums in Spain can be visited for free if you know when they have an open day.
  • Keep track of your personal belongings and do not leave them unattended.
  • It is worthwhile to decide in advance what attractions you plan to visit. This will save not only time, but also money if you buy tickets in advance.

Finally

Where to go in Spain in Barcelona and what to see, each vacationer decides for himself. Most importantly, remember the rules of personal safety, and then your trip will bring you only pleasant emotions.
