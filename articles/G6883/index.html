Granada, Spain - a fairytale city open to everyone!

The fabulous city of Granada, Spain ... What do we know about it? It is located in the southern part of the country near the foot of the north-eastern slope of the Sierra Nevada mountains on stunning hills that calmly descend into the beautiful valley of the river Genil. The first information about the settlements in this place dates back to the fifth century BC. Until 1492, Granada (Spain) had the status of the capital of the Moorish sultanate of southern Spain. It is precisely about this that architectural monuments in the Arabian style and many legends reminiscent of the greatness of the city are reminiscent of it.

sights



Undoubtedly, the pearl of Granada is the Alhambra Palace, built in the 13-15th century on the Golden Hill. The name translates from Arabic as "red castle". It is an ancient fortress surrounded by the stunning gardens of Generalife. Here is the royal palace of the Nazari dynasty. Washington Irving, an American writer and ambassador to Spain, dedicated one of his works to the fortress, entitled The Tales of the Alhambra. The old quarters of Sacramonte and Albaicin are now under UNESCO protection. In Generalif, the summer palace of the Sultan, is the National Museum of Spanish-Muslim Art and the magnificent Museum of Fine Arts. The city of Granada (Spain) is famous for the cathedral, which served as a model for most of the churches of Andalusia. Next to it was built the majestic Royal Chapel with tombs. Fascinating tours of Granada, walks in parks, gardens and charming alleys will give a magical experience.
