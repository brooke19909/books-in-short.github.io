Trial coins

These are ordinary coins, also intended for everyday use, but in connection with certain situations have never been issued. After creating such money, the ruler rendered a verdict on whether to use it or to prohibit circulation because of too large form or expensive material, unprofitability. Basically, they were re-melted again, but there are other options for the development of events. For example, several coins were hidden or they were lost. Now, such rare coins cost unthinkable amounts and are of high value to collectors.

As an example of such coins, two rubles made of silver in 1722, with the image of Peter the Great, should be mentioned. In addition, there is also the Konstantinovsky ruble, which was minted in 1825, currently only about six copies of this denomination are known. Its price is three million rubles!

The Konstantinovsky ruble belongs to the category of ‚ÄúExpensive Commemorative Coins of Russia‚Äù and has an interesting mystical history. Top of the most expensive coins in Russia includes this amazing instance.

There was a hypothesis that it got its name in connection with an error during minting. However, it is still believed that the most reliable version is that this monetary unit was created for the supposed and carefully planned coronation of Konstantin Pavlovich, the crown prince, who refused to rule the country in favor of his brother - Nikolai I.

He denied it in 1819. This fact was kept silent for a long time and was known only to the most native people. Alexander I, during his reign, issued a document that was kept secret and should only be open after he died. As it turned out, it was about Nicholas.

And so the king dies, Nikolai and the whole servants swear allegiance to Konstantin, but for a long time the country was in an unpredictable state. After these events, Alexander‚Äôs manifest was printed and very surprised everyone, but, despite this, the mint unfolded minting of coins, which depicted Konstantin.

Soon everything changed, Nicholas, as his father bequeathed, became king, and the mint was forced to quickly get rid of samplers, because these were expensive coins of tsarist Russia, issued in the amount of six pieces. Now two of them have found refuge in Russian museums, one is in an American institute, the remaining are bought by private collectors.

Coins of Platinum and Gold

Platinum coins were minted in 1836, their face value was 12 rubles, in total there were only eleven pieces. These originals are becoming more and more expensive every year.

World War I and cash flow

In 1908, coins of 25 rubles were issued, now they are considered quite rare and valuable. What is noteworthy, they are made of a gold bar, the weight of which was five kilograms. Nicholas II wanted to give this money as a sign of respect to all his relatives in 1908 on his next birthday. One of the copies was sold in 2011, unfortunately, it was in terrible condition but, despite this significant drawback, the cost of the most expensive Russian coin at that time amounted to 1,900,000 rubles.

There was the First World War, in connection with which the empire suffered from huge losses, there was a lack of valuable raw materials and labor. A decree was issued on changes in the monetary system, in which it was a question of reducing the weight of coins made from copper, since these were expensive coins of Russia, 10 rubles had to be smelted.

The impending revolution prevented the implementation of the plan, but partially managed to mint trial samples. How many of them were actually made, no one knows now, reliable information is missing.
