Anadyr city, Chukotka Autonomous Okrug: description, time, weather

Many cities, which are not so often heard, are located almost at the edge of the world. They are especially common in the north of our country. One of such settlements is the city of Anadyr. It is located in the most sparsely populated region of Russia - in the Chukotka Autonomous Region. Of course, this settlement is of great interest, since life in it is very different from other cities in the country. It is better to get to know Anadyr, talk about its nature, climate, population and various interesting details related to it.





General information about the city

First you need to provide basic information about this village. So, the city of Anadyr is located in a remote part of the country, on the Chukchi Peninsula. It is also the center of the Chukotka Autonomous Okrug of the same name. The city lies almost in the border zone, which also adds to it some specific features. It was founded in 1899, but for a long time it bore a different name - Novomariinsk. The status of the city this settlement received a little later - in 1965.

As you know, our country is really vast, therefore, different settlements are in different time zones. It is interesting to know in what time zone is the city of Anadyr. If we compare its location with Moscow, then it is located far from it in the east. In this regard, the time difference between these cities is very significant. The difference with Moscow is as much as 9 hours. When it‚Äôs 12:00 in the capital of our country, it‚Äôs already 21:00 in Anadyr. By international standards, this time zone is designated UTC + 12.

Thus, the time in Anadyr is very different from Moscow. It is worth noting that the time zone in which this city lies is called Kamchatka time. It acts on several regions - the Chukotka Autonomous Okrug and the Kamchatka Territory.





Local nature

Of course, we need to talk about the nature of the Chukotka Autonomous Okrug and, accordingly, Anadyr itself. Permafrost reigns everywhere here. This means that for a very long time the temperature of the earth's surface does not rise above 0 C. The entire territory of the peninsula is located in a natural zone called the tundra. Forests are completely absent. Of the plants, you can often find Arctic willow, blueberries, cranberries and others, their height does not exceed 20 centimeters. Sometimes plants such as lean birch and bearberry Alpine grow here. Various shrubs are also found, they can often be seen in river valleys. Among them there are some species of willows, birches and other trees. The most common representatives of the local flora are mosses and lichens, here you can find truly unique species that only the Chukotka Autonomous Region can boast. Anadyr is really a very unusual city. Therefore, it will be interesting to visit not only the village itself, but also get acquainted with the local nature.

So, we examined the climate and nature of the Chukotka Autonomous Okrug. Now it‚Äôs worth talking about the population of these places. The city of Anadyr, as is already known, is quite small. No more than 15 thousand people live here. If we talk about more accurate figures, then in 2015 the population of the city amounted to 14,329 people. Interestingly, the number of inhabitants is only increasing. This process began in 2006 and continues to the present. Of course, by the standards of a country where truly large-scale megacities are present, Anadyr is not a leader. In the list it is on the 809th place out of 1114 cities of Russia.

In 2009-2011, some trends were observed here that strongly influenced the demographic situation. At this time, a significant outflow of the population occurred as a result of migration. However, at the same time, a good trend was noted for the city - the birth rate exceeded mortality.

Transport accessibility

Thus, we got acquainted with the data on the demographic situation in this area. Now it‚Äôs worth talking about transport in Anadyr and the region as a whole. Of course, this is an important component of any locality. The transport structure here is represented by several components. Among them there is a seaport, aviation, roads and public transport.

For starters, of course, you need to talk about the port of Anadyr. This is an object that has truly important transport significance. Ships depart from here to Vladivostok, Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, Magadan and other ports. However, the navigation period does not last long, it is 4 months. Thus, transportation is carried out from July 1 to November 1.

As for roads, in the city they are mostly concrete. There is a federal highway A384. She goes from Anadyr to the airport. The length of the road is about 23 kilometers. The city has several bus routes that connect the main important places.

As it became clear, the airport operates here. Anadyr sends and receives planes from various cities. It is worth talking about this in more detail.

Air traffic with other cities

However, it can be difficult to get there, helicopter transportation is organized all year round, in winter you can also use the ice crossing, and in the summer you can take a boat. Regular flights depart from the airport to Moscow and Khabarovsk. Also, air communication is carried out with almost all the settlements of Chukotka.
