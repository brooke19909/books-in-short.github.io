Back to the past

Kostroma is an ancient city that is over 900 years old. Just imagine, a little more, and the city will celebrate the millennium.

Back in 1213, the city was completely destroyed by fire. All its inhabitants, who were still alive, from small to large, were taken prisoner.

Why, who took the inhabitants captive? The history of Kostroma for very young children may well be like this. A prince named Constantine captured the city that belonged to his brother. Of course, after all, Konstantin was an older brother. And while he who is older, he must rule.



Kostroma has always been a tidbit. The princes always wanted the city to belong to them. The princes of Vladimir planted their eldest sons on the throne, hoping that the city could always become a fortress and a place of shelter from enemy invasions.

Times of Dmitry Donskoy

The lands of Vladimir princes gradually expanded. This, accordingly, helped strengthen their power. But this state of affairs did not suit the Horde khans.

And in 1327, the Horde Khan intervened and divided the lands of Vladimir into parts. Novgorod with Kostroma went to Prince Ivan Danilovich Kalita. It turned out that the city of Vladimir was given to another. Kostroma has always been associated with him. And Kalita was the Prince of Moscow.

But under Dmitry Donskoy, much has changed. Previously, the Moscow principality, which included Kostroma, was not in the best position because of the impossibility of communication with the Volga route.

But Prince Dmitry began to use Kostroma bypassing the Volga route. Even a shelter from Khan Tokhtamysh for Prince Donskoy became Kostroma. A brief history of the city for children includes a story about the Kostroma Kremlin.

Kostroma Kremlin

There is an interesting fact in the history of Kostroma when the so-called old city was built on the Volga hill. It was called the Kremlin. Around it were mounds, ramparts and deep ditches. Even today, the remains of former embankments and ramparts are preserved.

Entrance to the Kremlin was through the Spassky Gate with the crossing of the bridge of the same name. A deep moat was dug below.

In the Kremlin itself were located:

  1. Sentry, labial and moving hut.
  2. Jail.
  3. Granaries of the sovereign.
  4. Siege yard and Pushkar yard.


The history of Kostroma even remembers the arrival of Empress Catherine II. She stayed in a streltsy house. But now it is impossible to see him, he, unfortunately, later burned down.

16-18 centuries - Kostroma is growing

History of the city (summary) by years:

- 1551 year. Ivan the Terrible was going on a campaign in Kazan. Kostroma was appointed a kind of assembly point for the tsarist troops.

- 1608 year. Troops and people supporting False Dmitry are gathering in the city. Also this year, in addition to the fight against False Dmitry, the residents of Kostroma go into battle with the Poles, and a little later there was a battle with a detachment led by pan Lisovsky.

- 1613 year. Significant not only for Kostroma, but for the whole of Russia. It was in this city that the boyars from Zemsky Cathedral arrived. Their goal is to put boyar Mikhail Romanov on the reign.

So from this year the dynasty of the Russian tsars of the Romanovs began its glorious path from the city of Kostroma.

- 1613 year. It is also famous for the feat of the Kostroma peasant Ivan Susanin.

- 1648 year. It is interesting in that wooden buildings, which often burned, began to be replaced by stone ones. The history of Kostroma, which briefly tells about the city, is replete with information about fires. And then the Epiphany Monastery became the first enclosed brick.

- 1654 year. A year that was a great test not only for Kostroma residents. This year, the world plague reigned, killing many lives, and simply devastated the city.

- 1672 year. Great fire in the city of Kostroma. The history of the city for children is very interestingly narrated in local museums. Particularly tragic and exciting is the fire that literally destroyed the city.

Guides, focusing on the age of the children, always have in stock stories that tell about the lives of the kids and their parents after the fire.

- 1680 year. The Tatars were brought by royal decree, and a settlement was formed.

- 1719 year. Kostroma is part of the Moscow province.

- 1722 year. When the history of Kostroma is studied at the school, it is said for the 3rd grade that schools were opened this year. They began to accept both boyar children and Posad children .

Third graders will learn with interest that before that, children could not study at all and could not write or read.

- 1760-1761 years. Strengthens, expands Kostroma. The history of the city is famous for its boyars, the Ryltsov brothers, who founded the tannery, the Strigalev brothers opened a linen factory.

- 1767 year. He is famous for visiting Catherine II herself. In addition, in the same year the official coat of arms of the city (Kostroma) was founded and approved.

Kostroma modern

Now it is a glorious, colorful and rich in history city (Kostroma). A brief history of the city is impossible without the coverage of 1913. Then a telephone line was opened connecting Kostroma and Moscow.

The first cars appeared on the roads of the city, light appeared in the houses of citizens. The bright lights of street lighting lit Kostroma.

The history of the city for children is told by interesting facts. They do not need too much information.

It is especially successful to get acquainted with history during theatrical performances. There, the children learn that in 1913 Nicholas II himself and his family arrived in the city. The purpose of the trip was to celebrate the 300 years of the reign of the Romanov dynasty.

Children will be interested to know that only in 1932 a bridge was built across the Volga, on which you can now quickly ride a car.

In the 40-50s of the 20th century, many factories and plants were built and launched. Cinemas, stadiums, clubs are opening. Hospitals and new homes are being built.

And what else is Kostroma famous for? The history of the city, of course, is inextricable with the Snow Maiden. In 2008, the official house of the famous Snow Maiden was opened to the joy of children.

Monument to I. Susanin

Then you can head to the monument to Susanin. This peasant is already known to all children. His feat is always told at school.

Local residents greatly honor their famous countryman, erected a monument to him and even opened a museum named after him.

The monument rises 12 meters high, it is very clearly visible from the Volga. The author of the creation is the famous Moscow sculptor Lavinsky.



For lovers of antiquities and legends, you can organize a trip near Kostroma, to the village of Susanino. It is only 65 kilometers from the center.

There you will be shown all the memorable places associated with Susanin. This is a museum, and a chapel, and Yusupov swamps. They will also show you a stone installed by grateful residents at the site of the death of a peasant.

Nearby is the native village where Ivan Susanin lived and from where he left on his last journey.

Museum of Wooden Architecture

Now wooden houses are very fashionable and look great. But it is expensive, not everyone can allow to build a hut from a real tree. But before the whole city was wooden. But because of this, tragedies and fires often happened.

But Kostroma still rebuilt. But, interestingly, in the whole city there is not a single ancient wooden building. But people wanted their children to know what the city looked like before, how people lived.

Therefore, at least some surviving wooden architectural monuments began to be brought to Kostroma from all over the region.

An unusual museum has been opened since 1958. Its peculiarity is that all exhibits are stored not in the walls of the building and behind glass, but directly in the open air.

Be sure to go there with the children. Tell them that the houses were brought here disassembled. They were assembled on site and furnished inside as they had done before.

The guys will learn a lot of interesting things about various household items, their purpose and even see how a real ancient loom works.
