Construction difficulties

The project of washing the man-made archipelago was a real challenge for man to the forces of nature. The mainland sand, which is so abundant in the United Arab Emirates, was not good. He did not have the right viscosity. Therefore, special dredges scooped sea sand from the bottom of the bay, laid it in the form of a palm tree, and later vibrating machines rammed it to a state of solid soil. So that fatal storms would not destroy and wash the loose banks, Palm Jumeirah acquired a crescent. It was laid out with large stone blocks mined in the Hajar mountains. Moreover, the position of each plate was regulated using a computer.
