A bit of history

The history of Hurghada is amazing because in just 50 years an unknown place has turned into a tourist mecca. Even before the mid-seventies of the last century, Hurghada was not even marked on the map of Egypt. It was a small settlement where search engine oil workers lived and worked, and the only attraction was the military airport. And in just fifteen years, a small village was able to become a leader among resort towns. In nineteen seventy-fourth, the first hotel was built, which was given the name Sheraton (now Le Meridien). But the real heyday of Hurghada began in the eighties, when it was allowed to foreign companies to charter flights. The first tourists from Russia flew here in the ninety-third year. And now this resort is called the winter all-Russian health resort.

Ancient Roman settlement Mons Claudianus

Mons Claudianus is the largest surviving ancient Roman settlement. In addition, it is in excellent condition. The settlement was founded more than two centuries BC. Masons lived and worked here. They made magnificent columns of local granite, which were exported to Rome to decorate temples, palaces and squares. We can still see such columns in the unfinished temple of Venus, Villa Hadrian, Pantheon. This settlement is spread in the desert scorched by the sun between the Nile and the Red Sea. Here you can see the remains of the fortress: residential buildings, baths, stables, workshops and much more.



Monastery of St. Anthony

The monastery of St. Anthony is located forty-five kilometers from Hurghada, near the town of Zaafaran. He is active, seventy monks live in him. It should be noted that this is one of the first monasteries. It was founded in three hundred and sixty-fifth year BC. The main attractions of the monastery include the cave of St. Anthony and the ancient church. There is also a book depository in which there are about two thousand ancient manuscripts. Unfortunately, they are not shown to tourists. You can only look at the tower in which they are stored.



Monastery of St. Paul

Thirteen kilometers from Hurghada stands the ancient monastery of St. Paul. Outwardly, he is very similar to his neighbor, the monastery of St. Anthony. The building dates from the fifth century BC. According to legend, it was built on the spot where the cave of St. Paul was located. If you decide to go on an excursion with a guide, the cost of the trip will be fifty dollars. Together with this building you will also be shown the monastery of St. Anthony.



Oceanarium Red Sea (Hurghada)

The attractions of this resort will delight and surprise even the most demanding tourists. Very lucky for those who are not afraid to go diving: in front of such daredevils all the beauty of the underwater world of the Red Sea is revealed. But do not despair for those who still do not dare to take such a desperate step. Look at the inhabitants of the southern seas in the Red Sea Aquarium. Here you will see sea turtles, hedgehogs, water snakes. Also, stingrays, moray eels and many motley coral fish will swim above you. Due to the variety of tourist facilities, travelers love Hurghada. Attractions here are for every taste.



Giftun Island

This island is rich in caves and canyons. And in the waters washing it, a lot of corals. You will not find such underwater beauties anywhere else. The island is famous for its diverse marine life. This is a real paradise for divers. But due to the constantly cruising boats with tourists, the ecological situation of the island was disrupted. Because now they made entry to it paid. The money collected from tourists goes to maintain and restore the ecosystem of the island.
