Doll Zernovushka do it yourself

The folk doll Zernovushka has many names. She was also called Krupenichka, Pea, Buckwheat. Initially, dolls were made in villages at the end of the harvest in the fields.





The practical and magical role of the doll

In early autumn, the selected part of the collected grain was put in a specially stitched bag. They were especially careful about capricious buckwheat. The bag was called "kitty." He was from the canvas, with ties at the top. He was given the appearance of a chrysalis. The doll was placed in the Red corner of the hut; in the spring, the contents were carefully removed. They were mixed with another grain and sown on the field with the wishes of a good harvest. Later, the craftswomen began to sew such dolls on purpose, decorating them with symbolic embroidery.

Modern attitude to the mascot

After a while, they began to put several types of grain in the dolls at once, as well as other things that were considered talismans. The amulet began to give at Christmas and Christmas time. Each housewife was happy with such a gift as the Zernovushka doll. With their own hands, the craftswomen tried to sew them for all relatives.

The amulet today will be a wonderful gift for people close to you for the New Year holidays or Christmas. The main thing is that the gift should be presented with sincere wishes for good, health and good luck. Next, let's talk about how to make a Zernovushka doll with your own hands.

What was the name of the amulet in different parts of the Russian land

The doll Zernovushka in different parts of Russia had different names. It all depended on which cultures were more valued by the inhabitants. In the southern part they loved buckwheat more and stuffed a doll with it. Accordingly, her name was - Buckwheat. Where millet was most respected, she was nicknamed Krupenichka. In the northern regions, they gave her the name Zernovushka and filled her with oats.

Master class number 1: how to make a doll Zernovushka do it yourself

It is worth noting that making it is not as difficult as it might seem at first glance. Making a Zernovushka doll begins with the selection of beautiful fabrics, because you should like the talisman. Only in this case will it work.

To sew a doll you will need:

  • Two-thread fabric to create a pouch (10 by 12 cm).
  • Cotton for a skirt (5.5x10 cm), for sleeves (3.5x2 cm), for a scarf (12x17 cm).
  • Flax for an apron (65.5 cm)
  • Braid.

Sew a regular bag from a two-thread. Pour the pre-prepared filler into it. It should not be stuffed very tight. Tie on top. Drag it with a thread just above the middle. The little doll‚Äôs body is ready, you can proceed to sewing clothes.

First make a skirt. Sew it from cotton by sewing a rectangle on the short side and put it on the doll. Make an apron from a piece of flax. You can decorate it with embroidery in advance, and then stretch the threads, thus creating a fringe. Tie a skirt and an apron with thread.





Next, we sew the doll's hands. To do this, take two cotton rectangles. We bend and smooth the edges so that it does not spill. For this you need to take two squares of white calico. Fold it two times with a ‚Äúscarf‚Äù, and pull the corners to the middle.

Connect the sleeve and palm by winding them together with a thread. Similarly, make a second hand. Wrap both sleeves around your neck.

Decorate the head. Wind a piece of lace to it. Take a flap of the same cotton from which the skirt is sewn and cut out a triangle. Tie it on your head like a scarf.

How to sew a Grain doll (a very simple way)

This workshop is so simple that even children of primary school age can handle it. Take two types of cotton: white and colored, knitting threads, and filler. From a colored fabric, cut a circle with a radius of 16.5 cm. Thread the needle and sew a circle around the perimeter with simple stitches. Pull it a little and fill the filler. Now you can completely pull off the formed ball and sew it up.





From the white fabric we cut the second circle with a radius of 8 cm. Similarly, we sew it around the perimeter with simple stitches. Tighten the thread a little, fill the ball with cotton, sintepon or holofiber (optional). Sew between your body and head.





We make doll hair. Take thick knitting threads of the right color. Wind them on a jar, making about 20 turns.





Remove them from the container. Attach the double-folded threads to the head and secure with a ‚Äúback needle‚Äù stitch. Make the doll ponytails by wrapping the ends of the ‚Äúhair‚Äù with thin rubber bands. If you wish, you can decorate her outfit with a lace apron, and her hair with ribbons, an artificial flower or a small wreath. Slavic charms were always made without a face.

Than it is possible to fill the Grain (filler value)

Each housewife filled the doll depending on the needs of the family. For the amulet, they took the largest and always integral elements. Inside the talisman, do not put chopped grain, cereal. Each filler had its own meaning:

  • Corn - the health of the smallest inhabitants of the house (children and pets).
  • Peas symbolize friendship and peace in the family.
  • Millet - protection from the evil eye.
  • Pumpkin seeds are laid by those who want replenishment in the family.
  • Sunflower seeds are a symbol of solar energy. Bring health to all the inhabitants of the house.
  • Buckwheat symbolized wealth.
  • Beans - for replenishment in the family.
  • Rice - for cash profit.
  • Pearl barley is a symbol of satiety.
  • Oats were laid for the health of all family members (horsepower).
