What is a geological time scale?

What is a geological time scale ? Why is it created? You will find answers to these and other questions in the article. The geochronological scale (stratigraphic scale) is called the temporary geological scale of the history of the Earth. It is used in paleontology and geology - it is a kind of calendar for colossal time intervals.

Minerals and rocks have been found on our planet that could be witnesses of its creation. The scale of geological time is quite an interesting thing. The subsequent period in the history of the Earth is divided into different time intervals. Their boundaries affect the most important events that have taken place.

The boundary between the times of the Phanerozoic appears through major evolutionary phenomena - global extinctions. The Paleozoic is separated from the Mesozoic by the largest Triassic Permian species extinction in the history of the Earth. Cenozoic and Mesozoic are separated by Cretaceous-Paleogene elimination.

Scale History

How was the geological time scale created? The nomenclature and hierarchy of most of the current geochronological units were adopted in 1881-1900. at the II-VII sessions of the Interethnic Geological Congress. Further, the World Geochronological Scale has been continuously updated.

The periods were named according to various signs. Most often used geographical names. So, the name of the Devonian period came from the county of Devonshire in England, the Jurassic from the European mountains of the Jura, the Perm from the city of Perm, and the Cambrian from Lat. Cambria, denominations of Wales.

The Vendian, Silurian and Ordovician stages are named after the ancient tribes. Names associated with the composition of the rocks were used very rarely. The Carboniferous era is named after a huge number of coal seams, and the Cretaceous due to the popularization of crayon.

The basis of the construction

The geological time scale was created to identify the conditional geognosic age of the rocks. Absolute old age, measured in years, is of secondary importance to geologists.

Earth's life is divided into two main periods: cryptose (Precambrian) and phanerozoic, according to the appearance of archaic remains in sedimentary rocks. In cryptozoic, there were only soft-bodied organisms that did not leave traces in sedimentary rocks. This is the phase of the invisible life.

The Phanerozoic began at the moment of the appearance at the boundary between the Cambrian and Ediacaria (Vendian) masses of forms of mollusks and other organisms, allowing paleontologists to separate the strata according to the finds of fossil fauna and flora.



The Earth's geological time scale has another major division, which is distinguished by the first attempts to crush the history of our planet into the largest time intervals. Then the whole chronicle was divided into four periods: primary, equivalent to Precambrian, secondary - Mesozoic and Paleozoic, Tertiary - completely Cenozoic without a final Quaternary era. The quaternary phase has a special position. This is the smallest cycle, a huge number of events took place in it, the traces of which survived better than others.

Mesozoic era

Even children know what a scale of geological time is. Grade 7, in accordance with the school curriculum, is studying this issue. Students are aware that the Mesozoic is an era of dinosaurs that existed 251-65.5 million years ago. This phase is known for the following cycles:

  • Triassic period: 251-200 million years ago. The diversity of vertebrates has significantly decreased, since universal extinction has occurred on Earth. Crocodiles, frogs, megazastrodons (true mammals), turtles and pterosaurs appear - the first vertebrates that can fly.
  • Jurassic period: 200-146 million years ago. Sea lizards dominate in water, dinosaurs on land, and pterosaurs in the air. Jurassic mammals are very small and resemble insectivores and rodents - just one niche that fell to them after reptiles.
  • Cretaceous period of time: 146-65.5 million years ago. Most dinosaur species reach maximum sizes. Social insects, flowering plants, snakes, real birds, placental mammals appear.
