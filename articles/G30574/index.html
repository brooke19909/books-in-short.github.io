Amazing nearby: Tobol River and its attractions

Russia is a water power. Many large and small rivers, lakes, seas and even ocean waves splash on its shores. Mother Volga, Don-father, gray-haired Irtysh, the majestic Tobol - names can be listed for a long time. Each reservoir has its own history, a glorious biography and many interesting facts that old-timers of those places can share.

Name and geographical coordinates





What is unusual for the Tobol River? First of all, by geographical location. It flows through the two largest states of Eurasia - Russia and Kazakhstan. In Kazakh, its name sounds close to the Russian version - Tobyl. The ancient handsome man pours his waters into the Irtysh, being her left tributary. The Tobol River has an impressive length of almost 1,591 kilometers. No less impressive is the total catchment area. It totals, no less, 426 thousand kilometers. The Tobol River is connected with a huge number of lakes - more than 20,000. Its sources should be sought near Bozbi and Kokpektisay. It was the merger of these rivers that gave rise to Tobyl - on the eastern borders of the South Urals and the Kostanai region (Kazakhstan). Gaining strength, spilling in breadth, the Tobol River flows further through the expanses of Western Siberia.

Natural characteristics





The water supply is mixed. In the upper reaches it is due to the melting of snow and ice. The lower the descent along the channel, the greater the water level in the Tobol River depends on the rains spilling in those areas. The flood season therefore at the source begins in April, the first half, when spring is actively taking over. It lasts until about 1-2 decades of June. In the lower reaches , the water level in the Tobol River is replenished until the first half of August. In addition to natural factors, a complex of reservoirs, the degree of flow / withdrawal of water in them, have a serious effect. With the onset of cold weather, from the end of October to November, Tobol is usually covered with ice. Melting occurs in mid-April. Also, the level of the Tobol River is determined by its left tributaries - Uy and Iset (small), Tura and Tavda, playing the role of important navigable arteries of Siberia. The right tributary of Tobyl is the Ubagan River. The Tobolsk channel is quite winding, and the flow of water is smooth, calm.

Famous settlements of the basin



One of the largest cities standing on the river is Tobolsk (Tyumen region). It was laid exactly at the place where Tobol flows into the Irtysh. The history of the city is connected with the dramatic events of the development of Siberia, the conquest of its expanses by Russian pioneers, Cossacks of Ermak.
