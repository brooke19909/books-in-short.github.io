Budva is one of the most famous holiday destinations in Montenegro. As for history, Budva was founded more than 2500 years ago. Based on this, it can be argued that this resort place is the oldest of those located on the Adriatic coast. Historical evidence suggests that, at different intervals, this territory was subordinate to the Romans, Greeks, Slavs, Venetians and Turks.

Historical sights




As for cultural values, the main ones are in the old part of Budva. The central part of the so-called Old Town is the Citadel, which serves as a fortress. It was erected in the 6th century BC. To date, the fortress has been converted into a museum of history and a theater complex working in the fresh air.

Temple buildings

The narrow streets of Budva are very often built up with churches and temples. The most famous of them are: the Church of Virgin Mary of the 11th century, the Cathedral of St. John, built in the 17th century, the Cathedral of St. Sava, which was built in the 16th century, the Holy Trinity Church of the 19th century. The fortress wall, which appeared in the 16th century, is endowed with a protective function.

Old Town Attractions

The old city is the district of Budva, which indicates the unusual color and identity of the resort. Near the Cathedral of St. John, guests will find a museum complex, which contains all the great finds of archaeologists made in this area. In addition to museums and churches, the town has a large number of cozy restaurants and cafes with Montenegrin specialties. The rock monastery, entirely carved from stone, is another famous highlight of Budva. The height of the cliff is a little more than one and a half kilometers. The monastery is famous for the fact that the relics of St. Basil are stored in its walls. The distance from Budva to the monastery is about 120 kilometers.

The surroundings of Budva





St. Stephen's Island is another beautiful place in the vicinity of Budva. It is noteworthy that before this territory was an ordinary village of fishermen and in a record short time it became a famous resort, which is in no way inferior to the famous Turkish coast. In addition to the fact that this territory has a huge number of beaches (both wild and public), it is famous for its beautiful landscape. This attracts tourists here.

The city is surrounded by a strong wall, which served as a defensive means during the war. All fortifications were built by the inhabitants of Venice back in the 15th century. If you want to capture the beauty of the fortress as a souvenir, you need to climb a high observation deck. The price of this pleasure is about 1 euro. The southern part of the same Old Town will open to vacationers a view of the Church of the Holy Virgin Mary. Also here you can see the famous Citadel, which has a protective function. Nearby is a fort called Mogren. Today it is impossible to find a person who would not agree, if possible, to visit these attractions and not to see all the beauty of this place personally. Among other things, there is the opportunity to visit all the famous Catholic churches, the main of which is the Church of St. John. By the way, icons are kept in this temple, the writing of which dates from the 15th and 18th centuries. Also in the Cathedral is the miraculous icon of the Mother of God.
