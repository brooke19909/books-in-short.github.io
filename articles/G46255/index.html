Nuclear fusion. Cold fusion. Nuclear power

Cold fusion can also be called cold fusion. Its essence lies in the possibility of realizing a nuclear fusion reaction occurring in any chemical systems. It is assumed that there is no significant overheating of the working substance. As you know, conventional nuclear reactions during their creation create a temperature that can be measured in millions of degrees Kelvin. Cold fusion in theory does not require such a high temperature.

Numerous studies and experiments

The study of cold fusion, on the one hand, is considered pure fraud. No other scientific areas in this are comparable to him. On the other hand, it is possible that this field of science has not been fully studied, and at all cannot be considered utopia, and even more so fraud. However, in the history of the development of cold thermonuclear fusion, if not liars, then they were probably crazy.

The recognition by pseudoscience of this direction and the reason for the criticism to which the technology of cold nuclear fusion was subjected was the numerous failures of scientists working in this field, as well as falsifications made by individuals. Since 2002, most scientists believe that work to resolve this issue is futile.

At the same time, some still try to carry out such a reaction. So, in 2008, a Japanese scientist from the University of Osaka publicly demonstrated an experiment performed with an electrochemical cell. It was Yoshiaki Arata. After such a demonstration, the scientific community again began to talk about the possibility or impossibility of cold thermonuclear fusion, which nuclear physics can provide. Some scientists qualified in nuclear physics and chemistry are looking for justifications for this phenomenon. And they do this in order to find not a nuclear explanation for him, but another, alternative. In addition, this is also due to the fact that information on neutron radiation is not available.



The History of Flashman and Pons

The very history of the publication of this kind of scientific direction in the eyes of the world community is suspicious. It all started on March 23, 1989. It was then that Professor Martin Fleischman with his partner Stanley Pons gathered a press conference at the University of Chemistry in Utah (USA). Then they stated that they had carried out a cold fusion reaction by simply passing an electric current through an electrolyte. According to chemists, as a result of the reaction, they were able to get a positive energy output, that is, heat. In addition, they observed nuclear radiation resulting from the reaction and coming from the electrolyte.

The statement made literally made a splash in the scientific community. Of course, low-temperature nuclear fusion, performed on a simple desk, could radically change the whole world. Complexes of huge chemical plants, which also cost a huge amount of money, are no longer needed, and the result in the form of obtaining the desired reaction when it comes is not known. If everything was confirmed, Flashishman and Pons would have an amazing future, and humanity would have a considerable reduction in costs.



However, the statement made by chemists in this way was their mistake. And, who knows, perhaps the most important. The fact is that in the scientific community it is not customary to make any statements to the media about their inventions or discoveries before information about them is published in special scientific journals. Scientists doing so instantly receive criticism, it is considered a kind of bad manners in the scientific community. According to the rules, a scientist who made a discovery is secretly obliged to notify the scientific community about it first, which will decide whether this invention is truly true, whether it should be recognized as a discovery at all. On the legal side, this is considered an obligation to fully maintain secrecy about what happened, which the discoverer must observe from the moment of submitting his article to the publication and until its publication. Nuclear physics is no exception.

Flashman and his colleague sent such an article to a scientific journal called Nature, which was the most authoritative scientific publication worldwide. All people associated with science know that such a magazine will not publish unverified information, and moreover will not print anyone. Martin Fleishman at that time was already considered a fairly respected scientist working in the field of electrochemistry, so the submitted article was due to be published shortly. And so it happened. Three months after the ill-fated conference, the publication was published, but the hype around the opening was already in full swing. Perhaps this is why Nature‚Äôs chief editor, John Maddox, already in the next monthly issue of the magazine published his doubts about the discovery made by Flashman and Pons and that they had obtained the energy of a nuclear reaction. In his note, he wrote that chemists should be punished for his premature publication. There they were told that real scientists would never have allowed their inventions to be publicly disclosed, and those who do this can be considered ordinary adventurers.

After some time, Pons and Fleischman suffered another blow, which can be called crushing. A number of scientists from the American scientific institutes of the United States (Massachusetts and California Technological Universities) conducted, that is, repeated the experiment of chemists, creating the same conditions and factors. However, this did not lead to the result declared by Flashman.





Is it possible or impossible?

Since that time, there has been a clear division of the entire scientific community into two camps. Supporters of one convinced everyone that cold fusion was an invention that was not based on anything. Others, on the contrary, are still convinced that cold fusion is possible, that the ill-fated chemists still made a discovery that could ultimately save all of humanity, giving it an inexhaustible source of energy.

The fact that if the invention of a new method does happen, with which cold fusion reactions will be possible, and, accordingly, the significance of such a discovery will be invaluable to all people on a global scale, attracts more and more scientists to this scientific direction, part of which can actually be considered fraudsters. Entire states are making significant efforts to build just one fusion station, spending huge amounts of money, while cold fusion is able to extract energy in absolutely simple and fairly inexpensive ways. This is what attracts those who want to cash in by fraud, as well as other persons with mental disorders. Among the adherents of this method of generating energy, one can find both.

A story with cold fusion simply had to get into the archive of so-called pseudoscientific stories. If you look at the method by which the energy of nuclear fusion is obtained with a sober look, you can understand that it takes a huge amount of energy to combine two atoms into one. It is necessary to overcome electrical resistance. In the currently under construction International Thermonuclear Reactor, which will be located in the city of Karadash in France, it is planned to carry out the connection of two atoms, which are the easiest of the existing in nature. As a result of such a connection, a positive energy release is expected. These two atoms are tritium and deuterium. They are isotopes of hydrogen, so nuclear hydrogen synthesis will be the basis. To make such a connection, an incredible temperature is needed - hundreds of millions of degrees. Of course, this will require enormous pressure. For this reason, many scientists believe that cold fusion is not possible.





Success and failure

However, to justify this synthesis under consideration, it should be noted that among his fans there are not only people with crazy ideas and scammers, but also quite normal specialists. After the performance of Flashman and Pons and the failure of their discovery, many scientists and research institutes continued to do this. Not without Russian specialists, who also made corresponding attempts. And the most interesting thing is that such experiments in some cases ended in success, and in some cases failed.

However, everything is strict in science: if a discovery occurs and the experiment is successful, then it must be repeated again with a positive result. If this is not so, such a discovery will not be recognized by anyone. Moreover, the repetition of a successful experiment could not be done by the researchers themselves. In some cases, they did it, in others - no. Because of what this is happening, no one could explain, there is still no scientifically based reason for such inconstancy.

Brief information about the Soviet scientist

I.S. Filimonenko died in 2013. He was a scientist who almost stopped the entire development of nuclear energy, not only in his country, but throughout the world. It was he who nearly created the nuclear fusion plant, which, unlike nuclear power plants, would be safer and very cheap. In addition to this installation, a Soviet scientist created an aircraft based on the principle of antigravity. He was known as a revealer of the hidden dangers that nuclear power could bring to mankind. The scientist worked in the defense complex of the USSR, was an academician and an expert on radiation safety. It is noteworthy that some of the works of the academician, including the cold nuclear fusion of Filimonenko, are still classified. Ivan Stepanovich was a direct participant in the creation of hydrogen, nuclear and neutron bombs, was engaged in the development of nuclear reactors designed to launch rockets into space.

Installation of the Soviet academician

In 1957, Ivan Filimonenko developed a cold fusion power plant, with which the country could save up to three hundred billion dollars a year by using it in the energy sector. This invention of the scientist was initially fully supported by the state, as well as such well-known scientists as Kurchatov, Keldysh, Korolev. Further development and bringing the invention of Filimonenko to a ready state was authorized at that time by Marshal Zhukov himself. The discovery of Ivan Stepanovich was the source from which pure nuclear energy was to be extracted, and besides this, with its help it would be possible to obtain protection from nuclear radiation and eliminate the effects of radioactive contamination.





The removal of Filimonenko from work

It is possible that after some time the invention of Ivan Filimonenko would be carried out on an industrial scale, and humanity would get rid of many problems. This was the beginning of the so-called undercover game in the scientific community. The result was a halt in all the work of Filimonenko, and in 1967 he was dismissed. An additional reason for this treatment of a distinguished scientist was his struggle to stop testing nuclear weapons. With his works, he constantly proved the harm done to nature and directly to people, many projects to launch rockets with nuclear reactors were stopped from its supply (any accident on such a rocket that occurred in orbit could threaten radioactive contamination of the whole Earth). His experimental installations are recognized as contradicting the laws of nature, the scientist himself is dismissed, expelled from the Communist Party, stripped of all ranks and generally declared to be a mentally abnormal person.

The essence of the experiments of the Soviet academician

Through experiments, Ivan Filimonenko established that, as a result of the decomposition of so-called heavy water by electrolysis, it decomposes into oxygen and deuterium. The latter, in turn, dissolves in the palladium cathode, in which nuclear fusion reactions develop. In the process, Filimonenko recorded the absence of both radioactive waste and neutron radiation. In addition, as a result of his experiments, Ivan Stepanovich established that his nuclear fusion reactor emits indefinite radiation, and it is this radiation that greatly reduces the half-life of radioactive isotopes. That is, radioactive contamination is neutralized.
