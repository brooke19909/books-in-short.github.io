Turkey ... The resort towns of this country for the past several decades have not been short of guests. Moreover, travelers come here both local and overseas - from near and far abroad. A country rich in millennia-old history with many cultural and architectural sights and the brightest cultural life of Istanbul has always attracted tourists from all over the world.

Within the framework of this article, we will try to clarify in detail what such an amazing country like Turkey can offer us. Resort cities, photos of which can be found in any guidebook devoted to this direction, truly give the impression of cozy, comfortable and relaxing corners of the globe.

Turkey. Aegean Sea Resorts: Kusadasi





Kusadasi is a real gem of the Aegean coast. And there are actually plenty of reasons for this. A lesson for everyone here is for every traveler.

For example, the ancient Genoese fortress of impeccable beauty has long been a symbol of the city. It was erected in the 16th century far from us on the island of Guvergin. And Kusadasi itself is surrounded by ancient cities, including Didim, Ephesus, Miletors and Priena.

By the way, not far from this resort is located the Greek island of Samos, where, with the appropriate permits, you can make a trip.

Kusadasi also has a National Park, home to many diverse species of birds and animals. It is on this island that pigeons, herons and other birds have been flying for many centuries to nest.

In the city today there are first-class hotel complexes, many bars and fish restaurants, shops with oriental antiques. Nightlife is presented by noisy modern discos.

European Resort in Turkey - Bodrum





The city of Bodrum is rightfully considered the largest center of night club life in Europe. Every year lovers of youth parties and discos come here from all over the globe.

Meanwhile, this settlement has an interesting ancient history. For example, it should be noted that the famous philosopher Herodotus once lived in it. And Bodrum was almost completely destroyed by Alexander the Great. After it was conquered by Suleiman the Magnificent, who restored the buildings and arranged the infrastructure. In general, we can confidently say that it is thanks to this person that modern tourists can now enjoy a beautiful resort.

This region has a mild Mediterranean climate. Nearby are the well-known islands of Kos and Rhodes, which you can go to if you wish, since travelers who come here automatically receive a 15-day Greek visa.

Today in Bodrum operates a beautiful yacht club.
