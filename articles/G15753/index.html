Marmaris resort: sea, rest, reviews

Marmaris is one of the most prestigious resorts in Turkey. It is located in the south-west of the country and attracts vacationers with a variety of entertainment and a pleasant climate. In addition, in the Marmaris area the sea is not one, but two of them! After all, it is there that the Aegean merges with the Mediterranean.

Beaches

The main thing in Marmaris is the sea. However, this resort is popular not only among lovers of beach vacations, but is also known as one of the best places in southwest Turkey, where you can go diving and underwater photography.

The main beach of Marmaris is located in the central part of the resort. It is quite long and sandy. Moreover, in some areas at the entrance to the water, sections of pebbles are found. There are many bars, cafes and restaurants right on the beach, so youth companies are also the first choice. As for couples and parents with children, it is better for them to choose the Ichmeler village for relaxation. There is a clean and comfortable beach, where it is relatively not crowded, and there is entertainment for children of different ages.

If you want to arrange yourself a romantic vacation, then go to Turunch. There is a beautiful beach surrounded by forest, with small coves fenced by beautiful cliffs.

Diving

In Marmaris, the sea is clear and even at great depths excellent visibility. In total, there are 50 dive sites in the resort and its environs that abound in ancient ruins. Among the fish and other inhabitants of the underwater world that can be seen in its depths, tuna, octopus, moray eels, crayfish, cardinal fish and others are of particular interest. Some of the diving sites of Marmaris come across fragments of ancient amphorae. In addition, the resort has places that have long been chosen by lovers of underwater photography. For example, in the cave of Basa, you can take beautiful pictures.

Dive centers in Marmaris organize individual and group dives accompanied by an instructor. At the resort you can take a diving course, at the end of which a PADI certificate is issued.





Resort Attractions

Now that we have found out which sea in Marmaris is best for diving, it's time to find out what those who prefer to combine a beach holiday with excursions can see it.

The place most visited by tourists in Marmaris is a medieval fortress. Yes, yes, the same one whose builder they were going to hang. Despite the discontent of Suleiman the Magnificent, it turned out to be more than reliable and perfectly preserved, although over the 5 decades that have passed since the construction of the fortifications, they were repeatedly attacked by enemies.

In addition, lovers of antiquities are happy to visit the castle of Kale, built by the Ionians in the 11th century. Today it houses the Marmaris City Museum. Its exposition presents historical documents, artifacts found during archaeological excavations and works of art created by people who have inhabited this resort since ancient times.

Another Ottoman monument is located on a street leading to Calais. This is the caravanserai of Sultan Hafsa, which was built in 1545. For 5 centuries, it served as a roadside hotel, and today there are cafes in the building serving national dishes and souvenir shops.





Natural attractions in the vicinity of the resort

Which sea washes Marmaris in Turkey? This question can often be heard from tourists. As already mentioned, this resort is a unique place where there are many natural attractions. The main one, of course, is the confluence of the two seas. If you want a unique opportunity to swim immediately in the Aegean and the Mediterranean, then just go along the coast towards the town of Dalaman, where the international airport closest to Marmaris is located. On the way, you will notice that the water off the coast begins to get cloudy. This means that you are at a meeting place for the quiet waters of the Mediterranean and the lush streams of the Aegean.

In addition, tourists are advised to visit Pamukkale. It is often called the "eighth wonder of the world." There, from high rocky ledges, waters rich in mineral salts fall. At the same time, each terrace has a small pool with a water temperature that is held at +37 degrees Celsius.
