Jeremy Bibs: biography and photos

Wreck

In 1911, the British schooner "Beautiful Bliss", engaged in freight transport, fell into a hurricane in the South Pacific. The ship sank, and with it the entire crew of the ship. Only one young young man who was barely 14 years old managed to escape. Fate seemed to show mercy to him. And miraculously, the young man was thrown onto an uninhabited coral island, covered with coconut palms. But this is where the real test begins for him.

Maiden voyage

His name was Jeremy Bibs. He came, apparently, from a simple English family, where from an early age children had to earn their bread. His whole life from an early age was connected with the sea. And the schooner ‚ÄúBeautiful Bliss‚Äù offered him a chance to do what he loved and earn a little money.

From numerous sources it is known that the boy was able to read and loved this business. He was especially fascinated by adventurous maritime stories. It is easy to assume that his favorite work was Daniel Defoe's novel Robinson Crusoe, published two centuries earlier before the fateful day. Who could then predict that this book will play a fateful role in his life ...





Isle

Once on the island, just like his favorite literary hero, Jeremy Bibs was completely at a loss. He and the island were left face to face. It is difficult to imagine what any other child would do in such a situation, but Jeremy, collecting his will, began to gradually settle in new territory. And in this he was helped by the same favorite book, which he remembered in detail. It should be noted his character and thirst for life. Indeed, on the island, except for thickets of coconut and some fruits, there was nothing else.

What helped to survive?

Jeremy Bibs, whose biography is now closely intertwined with the island, built a hut, made a bow and arrows to hunt birds. Fruits became his very first food, they throttled appetite and quenched thirst. Coconut was a favorite treat for young Robinson. He, in addition to delicious pulp and milk, also served as dishes. In his shell, Jeremy collected rainwater fresh water.

He butchered a caught bird and roasted it on a fire. I used sharp stones as a knife. The fire was built using tinder. In addition, he made a fishing rod and successfully caught fish during the tides. Bird eggs served him breakfast. Therefore, having arrived after so many years in his native country, he probably experienced a considerable shock.

Other robinsons

World history already remembers many such heroes. But nevertheless, it is necessary to distinguish between those who become robinsons by chance, and who - of their own free will. The modern robinsons include Ivan Jose and Brandon Grimshaw. The first was discovered in 2014 on one of the Marshall Islands. As it turned out, his boat broke while traveling from Mexico to El Salvador and lost its propeller. He roamed the ocean for 16 months. He ate fish, caught birds and turtles. I collected rainwater for drinking.





The story of Brandon Grimshaw is an example of a voluntary robinsonade. In the 60s, he made a trip to Seychelles for work and fell in love with these places. The entrepreneur chose the least suitable for life island Muen and bought it for 13 thousand dollars. Brandon accepted a hermit life and set out to find someone on the island. Searches were successful. Creole of Rene Lafortuno became the ‚ÄúFriday‚Äù of modern Robinson. They made good friends and set about transforming the island: they planted 16 thousand trees, spent water and began to breed turtles. Among these people, Jeremy Bibs is the record holder. For more than half a century without communication with people, in isolation from civilization, in absolutely unsuitable conditions for life, he was able to survive, and moreover, live to the very gray hair without losing faith in himself.

Today, Jeremy Bibs is a Robinson whose story could well become the source for the script of the film or form the basis of another novel about people with a thirst for life and incredible willpower.
