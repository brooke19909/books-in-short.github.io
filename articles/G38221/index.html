Vintage acoustics: specifications, manufacturers and reviews

Digital products occupy the lion's share of the audio market. Manufacturers strive to offer ever more advanced and technologically advanced systems, working out every characteristic to the smallest detail. But parallel to this process, a special niche of vintage radio technology is also being formed. Representatives of this class have their original appearance, not only external but also internal. It is wrong to think that this is equipment that only imitates the ‚Äúold‚Äù acoustics. Vintage audio system in the modern sense is also a technologically advanced product. Another thing is that it was developed on the traditional principles of operation of models produced 20 or more years ago.





Features of vintage acoustics

If in the production of modern systems the most relevant areas of development are communication technologies and methods for optimizing signal translation channels, then engineers focused on creating the vintage models. In particular, much attention was paid to the manufacture of a paper diffuser, due to which the acoustics reproduce sound. The vintage model involves the inclusion of this component, made according to classical methods. Soviet radio plants, for example, when casting a diffuser, used rosin based on conifers or nitrocellulose varnish. The same technological methods, but in a modernized form, are also used by some modern manufacturers.

Of course, external features distinguish vintage technology. As a rule, such equipment has a small number of analog interfaces, has impressive dimensions and a characteristic design. On the other hand, deliberate repetition of individual aesthetic shades with a reference to the past is not appreciated by real connoisseurs of the segment. Nevertheless, naturally designed acoustics are welcomed, the vintage filling of which also fully reflects its strengths and weaknesses.





Main characteristics

Nominal technical and operational indicators differ little from today's standards. The sound picture is only reproduced with a different quality, but the levels of power potentials, for example, are generally preserved. So, most vintage models include a bundle with a 200-watt amplifier block. This is a full-format massive acoustics, but there are also less impressive systems operating at powers of about 100-150 watts. The frequency range on average is 25-30 000 Hz. Depending on the model, vintage acoustics focuses on the study of a particular area in the sound spectrum. In this regard, analog technology is more prominent and unstable. This factor is also liked by many music lovers, because it creates a feeling of lively natural sound. As for the size, the middle segment offers speakers 10-15 inches, enclosed in wooden cases. The professional category also features 25-inch dynamic heads.

Manufacturers

The greatest interest from audiophiles of different levels is caused by Japanese equipment. Moreover, this country is interesting not with 2-3 brands, but with a whole scattering of companies that have made progress in this niche. If today the speakers of Yamaha, Pioneer and Sony are of interest, then about 20-30 years ago the manufacturers of Diatone, Onkyo and Sansui were trusted in the world. What attracted the Japanese audio system? Each model had its own merits, but in general, developers of different companies sought to expand playback capabilities. In the conditions of fierce competition, the basic sound qualities increased, including the same frequency range, power, detailing, etc. In the world market, the rival to Japanese brands is Dutch acoustics, vintage Philips, German systems Sennheiser and others.





Diatone Model Reviews

This manufacturer produced acoustics in a classic form factor and for the most part in a three-way configuration. A typical representative of the family is the DS-77EX complex. The owners of the device note that its abilities are enough to fully cover the entire frequency range. This is a versatile vintage acoustics from Japan, giving a strong and large-scale sound. What is especially important, Diatone was responsible for the production of accessories for its systems. Today, one of the most acute problems for lovers of vintage technology is precisely the lack of suitable add-ons and components. In this case, finding the right fittings is not so difficult.





Philips Model Reviews

The Dutch company also belongs to small manufacturers and focuses on large-scale projects. One of the most interesting developments of the past years is the 22AH466 speaker system . Modification of the shelf type with a three-way layout scheme. In the frequency spectrum, the model is more likely oriented to vocal compositions. According to listeners, a small 5-inch speaker provides an elegant and enchanting study of notes with minimal interference. In addition, Philips vintage acoustics in this version have special materials. Users indicate the presence in the case of veneer of valuable wood species and the original fabric trim under a plastic rim.





Pioneer Model Reviews

In the family of this manufacturer, the 1988 model is interesting - S-55TwinSD. This is a shelf system based on alnico magnets. The package includes three speakers, complemented by one virtual broadband. Many users have a positive listening experience. In this version, Pioneer vintage speakers have deep and enveloping bass, and two phase inverters add a touch of amorphism. At the same time, it is difficult to call a universal system. Rather, it is recommended for opening upper case.

Where to buy and how much?

It has already been said that connoisseurs of analog sound often encounter problems finding accessories for their systems. But the acoustics themselves are not for sale in ordinary stores. You can search for rare copies in specialized salons, pawnshops or through Internet sites. For example, on the portal with announcements "Avito" vintage acoustics is represented quite widely with coverage of almost all of the above manufacturers. Models of little-known brands, like Diatone, are estimated at 30-40 thousand rubles on this site. Techniques of the same level as Sony and Philips are sold on average for 60-90 thousand, depending on the characteristics of the acoustics.





Conclusion

When switching to vintage technology, some features of its use should be considered. It is important to understand that modern equipment has advanced far in terms of physical strength and reliability due to the use of element base with enhanced protective qualities. But in old-type models, it is necessary to protect the same cases from contact with moisture, and to protect the electromagnetic filling with line filters. As for the choice of a suitable model, the best vintage acoustics is the one that most deeply and gently reveals the frequency range. And if for digital technology the inclusion of third-party colors is a big minus, then vintage analog systems only add sophistication by diluting the sound stream with your own corrections. Some complexes have a completely unique sound, due to the unusual combination of materials in the design. For this distinctiveness, vintage speakers are appreciated.
