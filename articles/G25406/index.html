The Best Poker Books - List, Features and Reviews

Poker is one of the most common gambling games. However, without the necessary knowledge, skills and, of course, practice, achieving great successes will not work. Therefore, it is worth paying attention to the best representatives of educational literature.

However, the market for these books is quite extensive. Many authors offer their own game strategies, many of which may turn out to be losing. The article provides a list of the best poker books for beginners, according to reviews by users of the global network and readers.

The following literature is suitable for both beginners and those who have sufficient experience in this game.

"Poker" by Dmitry Lesnoy

The book was written in collaboration with Lev Natanson. Its main distinguishing feature is a very fascinating form of narration. All features of the gameplay are considered as detailed and accessible as possible. The same goes for the rules of poker itself. In one of the best books about poker, authors offer their readers tactics that are not only mathematically verified, but also verified.

Using these techniques will allow you to get not only enough practice in many varieties of this game, but also help to succeed in full matches.

Harrington on Hold'em

This game-specific tutorial is presented in three fairly large volumes. All of them are recommended for tournaments. It can be noted that these are some of the best books about poker Hold'em. And this title is quite fair. The author is the champion of the tournament in the 1995 world series. He is also the only player in the world who managed to reach the last table for two consecutive years (2003, 2004).

The collection of volumes is recommended for reading to those who seek to achieve maximum results in a particular form of gambling. Of these, one can draw not only authoring strategies, but also tricks and tricks, the success of which is confirmed by eras.

The Little Green Book by Phil Gordon

This literature is a kind of guide. One of the best poker books from the poker library helps players learn to think in such a way as to improve their game as quickly and as better as possible. The author gives not only the usual tips and strategies, but also various materials that help to understand how the enemy thinks and what motivates him to take certain actions.

David Sklansky "Low Limit Hold'em"

This book is also worthy of the attention of readers who want to increase the skill of the game. The author is an extremely successful poker player, more than once became the winner of international tournaments. The practically best book on poker Hold'em provides detailed information on how best to play this kind of gambling.

The publication describes in detail the so-called loose style. He is very popular among professional players. A competent presentation makes it easy to learn even for beginners without any problems. From the book you can also draw tips and tactics that help make the right decisions in various situations. The authors also invite the reader to read detailed reviews of various practical examples of particular value.

Dolly Brunson "Super System. Intensive Poker Course"

This is the next best tournament poker book. She tells in detail about many of the main subtleties of this game. The author gives and analyzes important techniques and strategies that can help both a novice and an already skilled player. Here you can also learn about original and effective techniques.

As a bonus, there is the most complete and accurate poker statistics.

Andrew Sadman "Easy Game"

One of the best poker books for beginners. The publication is presented in two volumes. It talks about the most pressing issues that can arise while playing Hold'em over the Internet on short tables.

The first volume describes the ABS strategy. She teaches how to beat medium and low limits. The second volume gives the nuances of simple and complex game tricks. It also discusses ideas that help you understand how your rivals think.

Not without subjective opinion. The author gives his own criticism of template behavior during the game. He states that all decisions made must be carefully considered. The materials presented will be useful to both novice players and those who already have considerable experience.

"Theory of poker"

Another work by David Sklansky. This book is written with the utmost honesty and directness. Content is almost entirely devoted to game theory. The purpose of the book is the need for training in the thought process, and not copying common techniques and tactics.

During reading, the reader will be able to learn to assess the situation, think through all the possible options and their consequences in order to act with the best results.

The Psychology of Poker by Alan Skoonmaker

Next among the best poker books. Or rather, in the psychology of this game. It teaches the player to correctly and clearly determine the type of each of the opponents. Also, this publication helps to understand the motivation for the actions of a player. In short, this book is intended to teach you how to read your opponents during the game.

Sign Language in Poker by Mike Caro

This book must be read after the previous example. It is an extremely interesting guide to how to determine the psychological portrait of opponents at the table, simply analyzing his actions, behavior and gestures. Extremely necessary for those who prefer to play live poker.

Eugene Gorelik "Hold'em - a game of right decisions"

This book is very popular among Russian poker players. It contains many well-known techniques, tactics and other knowledge that are necessary for any professional or beginner who wants to learn a profitable game. The information provided helps the reader learn to balance, work correctly with statistical information about opponents, and also use the most effective tactics, while analyzing the distribution of forces at the table.

Professional Omaha Poker Secrets by Rob Hollink

The following is a list of top poker books . The publication is dedicated to one of the most popular variations of this game. It is worth noting that the Omaha variety is one of the most difficult species to master. And even professional players may have difficulty trying to learn. The publication contains not only theoretical, but also practical information.

At the very beginning, it tells about the rules and features of the game. The following are examples of strong combinations. The book also demonstrates recommendations on how to draw up the right strategy and get the opportunity to adjust it depending on the conditions of the current game.

Then comes the chapter with practical tasks. Here the author gives an example of a certain situation and invites the reader to make the most correct decision on his own. Then the analysis of the right moves is performed. This content allows you to master the skill as simply and correctly as possible.

Three Nguyen "Let There Be Order"

The next best poker book. It is devoted to rather unusual information, which is not so often found in this kind of literature. Content is about a range of hands. But this, as you know, is one of the main aspects of the game.

Roy Rounder "Easy Poker Math"

Quite an interesting book that refutes the common misconception that making any calculations in poker is difficult. The author provides materials that will allow you to learn how to easily calculate your own chances in the game. All information is presented simply and clearly, so you will not have problems with the development of calculation techniques.

Winning Hard Hold'em by Nick Grajine

In the publication, the author tells about his own many years of experience in the game, thanks to which it turned out to form the right advice and the most effective strategies. All information is described as simple as possible and will be understandable even to those who are just starting to play. With this book, Grajine seeks to teach people not just to play, but to win the majority of hands, thus turning poker into a way to earn money. He advises starting with a small bank, after which, with training and success, it will already be possible to move to larger amounts of winnings.

"Your Best Poker Friend" by Alan Skoonmaker

Quite an interesting book, revered by players of different levels. In it, the author talks about the various psychological aspects of those who play poker. He suggests paying particular attention to habits and distinguishing bad from good. Various methods of how to work with the second in order to achieve victory are given. It also offers ways to control the former so that they do not interfere with the implementation of the strategy and do not spoil the game. The book also describes various ways to determine the emotional mood of all opponents on the table, as well as techniques that help determine whether a person is bluffing or not.
