Old City

Yaroslavl is one of the most ancient cities, thoroughly located on the banks of the Volga River. The first stone in the foundation of the Yaroslavl Kremlin was laid by Yaroslav the Wise even during his reign of Rostov in Holy Russia, around the thousandth year of our era. According to legend, this momentous event occurred on Ilyin‚Äôs day, and the first church built in the city was named in honor of Elijah the Prophet. And the name itself was given by the name of its founder: Yaroslavov city, or (in the possessive form of those times) Yaroslavl.





The city has successfully developed. Monasteries were built here, schools and manufactories opened, and in 1777 Yaroslavl became a major administrative center, having received the status of a province. In Soviet times, the province was abolished, and in 1936 the Yaroslavl region was formed. For the last three years, an influx of population has been observed in the city, the number of inhabitants according to 2014 is six hundred two and a half thousand people.
