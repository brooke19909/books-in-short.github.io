Card suit: history of occurrence, meaning, interpretation





There are many versions of when and where people first started using cards for fun. In one case, their invention is attributed to the Chinese, who began to paint dominoes from cardboard. Others believe that the innovator in this matter was the French king, who was thus able to hide his frenzy during the evening game parties. One way or another, at the end of the 14th century in France, representatives of the lowest classes became obsessed with this occupation, which often harmed both the family and work. Most likely, it was from there that this passion for gambling fun from people came.

What are card suits called?





Everyone knows the answer, but not everyone thinks what they symbolize. According to one of the most common versions, all stripes denote instruments of torture and suffering of Christ during his crucifixion on the cross (directly the cross itself, a spear, a sponge with vinegar and nails). That is why the church has such an unambiguous attitude to this entertainment.

Each suit of cards symbolizes one of the famous elements. Peaks - air, means everything that happens negative with a person at the moment: quarrels, failures, lies, losses. Cross - fire, shows what position a person has in society, has power, or, conversely, is subordinate. Hearts - water, is responsible for the emotional state, love and relationships. Tambourines - land, helps to find out and clarify all business issues related to work, travel, education, etc. These designations are used in fortune-telling with the help of special cards.
