A bit of history

Initially, this territory belonged to the Terletsky family of merchants. The last representative of this kind transferred his lands to the public together with the Novogireevo settlement built by him. Regular visitors to the park know that there are five ponds near the Serebryanka river - Terletsky pond Vostochny, Utiny, Zapadny, as well as Olkhovy and Kupalny. On the old maps, only three of them are visible - Duck, East and West. That was until the seventies of the twentieth century.

Lower ponds located in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Black Stream appeared only in the second half of the last century. At this time, by the decision of the Moscow authorities, Terletsky Park was separated from Izmaylovsky, and there they began to create a recreation area.

The formation of environmental balance

The last owner of the estate, A. I. Terletsky, was seriously engaged in hydrogeology issues. When planning the town of Novogireevo, he thought not only about the convenience of the inhabitants of the village, but also about the ecology of the environment. He dreamed that his ponds would be no worse than German ones.



Noticing the tendency of the ponds to swamp, Alexander Ivanovich began to form the ecological balance of the park. At the beginning of the twentieth century, the property of cattail (many call it reeds, although this is not true) was observed to purify water. Terletsky in Kuzminki dug out a cattail, where there were a lot of him, and planted him in ponds. In addition, a network of canals designed for the treatment of rainwater was laid in the park. The remains of this system can still be seen in the eastern part of the park.

Thanks to the measures taken, by 1910 the water in the ponds was significantly cleared.
