Which multimeter is better to choose for a car? Overview, types, prices

Car operation is usually associated with mechanical operations, even when it comes to serious damage. Electrical equipment requires special attention, so the maintenance of this area is often entrusted to specialists. But also there are simple and often preventive measures that can be performed even by an inexperienced motorist. For example, it can be a test of the voltage at the terminals or a measurement of the current resistance of the on-board network. For such operations, special testers are purchased - multimeters. This is a small device that allows you to take the necessary measurements in minutes. Which multimeter is better to choose is a controversial question, and the answer to it can only be given if individual needs and preferences of the user are taken into account. Nevertheless, there are general rules for such a choice, the knowledge of which will help solve this problem.





Multimeter Overview

The device is a measuring tool of small dimensions, which allows using its functionality to assess the state of the electrical circuit, as well as reflect its specific characteristics. As standard, such devices serve as voltmeters, ohmmeters, and ammeters. We can say this is a set of operations that characterizes a universal multimeter. Which is better to choose for home? Perhaps, in terms of the functional content of this set of operations is quite enough for domestic needs.

However, it will be useful for the car to provide additional functionality. In particular, some of the specialized models of this type also allow you to work with specific on-board equipment. Such devices allow you to determine the performance of the tachometer and speedometer. True, excessive filling with functions is not always a plus to the quality of measurement. Therefore, it is still worthwhile to adhere to standard modifications in which a modern multimeter is produced. Which is better to choose from the point of view of ease of use is also an important issue, which takes into account the nuances of handling the tool. In particular, the usability of the device will depend on its type.

Types of device





Fundamental separation takes place according to the method of management and presentation of measurement data. Morally obsolete, but reliable analog instruments with mechanical controls are still in demand on the market. These are pointer devices that differ in poor functionality, but at the same time they are understandable to most even novice users. But classic testers are being replaced by a digital multimeter. Which is better to choose for a car in this case? It is advisable to focus on models with ticks. Such modifications, in particular, are optimally suited for working with battery packs, since they eliminate the need for additional equipment. The digital model of the multimeter itself is also advantageous in a modern way of presenting data that is displayed on a liquid crystal display. In the improved versions, computer filling is also used, thanks to which it is possible to process the received data.

Key selection criteria





Technical parameters should be the basis for making a choice. This mainly concerns the range of measured values. In particular, using a multimeter with a wide range, the user can determine the current strength in the spectrum from 0.1 ŒºA to 10 A. Also, the resolution that the multimeter provides is taken into account - which is better to choose for this indicator? Of course, the higher the bit depth, the better the measurement quality. This allows you to more accurately evaluate the measured values. Another thing is that small gradation is far from relevant in all cases.

An essential selection criterion is the accuracy of the device. Officially, it is presented as an error coefficient. For example, for digital testers, this value usually varies from 0.02 to 3%. But on the question of how to choose a digital multimeter, the measurement principle itself is important, since low-power equipment is often examined. And in terms of measurement stability, by the way, it is sometimes analog modifications that manifest themselves better.
