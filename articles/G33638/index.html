The value of the national currency and the price of oil are directly proportional.

Many in our country are wondering why the ruble exchange rate depends on oil. Why, if the price of black gold is reduced, the imported goods are getting more expensive, is it more difficult to get out to rest abroad? Why does the ruble depend on oil prices?

The price of the ruble is set depending on the proposals for the euro and the dollar on the Moscow Exchange. However, there are many factors that influence the fluctuations of the currency market. The question often arises, why the ruble depends on oil, and not on gas? Blue fuel also forms the price of the ruble, but to a lesser extent.





The Russian economy and its budget are dependent on the price of raw materials. Half of the treasury income is formed from the profits that the country receives by trading in oil, gas and metals.

The ruble exchange rate depends on the prices of energy resources that form the export of the Russian Federation. They determine the state of the current account of the balance of payments.

From this question, no less logical is formed: why does the ruble depend on oil and the dollar does not? After all, the United States also has hydrocarbons. And their oil fell in price as swiftly as ours.

The fact is that the ruble is a more commodity currency. The United States in this sense makes more money. Indeed, with the development of various sectors of the economy, the national currency is easier to stay afloat. Therefore, the dollar is more stable and less prone to crisis fluctuations.

The reasons for the cheapness of oil

To understand why the ruble depends on oil, you need to understand what factors form the price of hydrocarbons.





Several factors led to the rapid fall in the price of black gold on a global scale:

  1. The main consumer in the global hydrocarbon market is China. The period of intensive growth of the economy of this giant has passed. Now China is not developing at such a furious pace, therefore, it needs less raw materials.
  2. Manufacturers of cheap shale, the United States, dumping world prices for minerals, in turn, monopolists in the market lower prices as much as possible to squeeze out a competitor.
  3. The recent lifting of sanctions against Iran has led the country to increase oil production. However, OPEC members also do not want to slow down the pace of black gold mining.

A surplus has formed in the hydrocarbon market when supply exceeds demand. Now the buyer dictates the price to the market, thus the price is reduced.

Preconditions for rising energy prices

The increase in oil prices, observed some time ago, created certain prerequisites for a more or less stable ruble. In general, over the last period, the national currency is kept in one corridor. This is to the question of why the ruble depends on oil. Such a small stabilization is caused by the recent agreement of the OPEC member countries. Large exporters of black gold pledged to slow down hydrocarbon production.





The situation temporarily stabilized, and the price of oil grew a little, allowing the national economy to breathe a little.

Russian budget revenues fell sharply after the collapse of oil prices. Adjusting the national currency allows you to balance the country's economy for some time.





The longer the ruble is able to stay in one corridor, the less consequences the country's budget faces. The Russian economy is very dependent on imports, so the dollar, being in the range of 55-60 rubles, does not allow high inflation.

In general, the heat in the foreign exchange market has been reduced due to import substitution. This strategy directly stimulated the agricultural sector. Also, there is another tool that the Central Bank of the Russian Federation uses - an increase in the interest rate. A high interest rate strengthens the ruble, as it attracts market participants to keep money in domestic currency.

Why does the ruble depend on oil, and not on gold?

Now in the world there are almost no countries that would tie their currency to gold reserves. Based on realities, one ruble contains about 4 gold kopecks. For example, the US dollar contains about 3 gold cents.

Earlier, when money was minted from alloys containing gold and silver, they themselves were secured, but for almost 50 years, the country's currencies and gold reserves have gone apart.

At the moment, Indian rupee is the leader in terms of gold supply, which is provided by gold at 7%.

Since the domestic currency is weakly backed by gold, there is no need to talk about dependence on gold reserves.
