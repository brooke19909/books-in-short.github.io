City population

Saransk (whose population honors and respects its roots) is the oldest Russian city in the Middle Volga. The first mention of the village dates back to 1641. It was built as a fortified city of the Russian kingdom. The fortress itself was erected on the bank of the Insar River and served as a stronghold of the Ateman serif line on the southeastern outskirts of the state. For its construction, the left bank was chosen: it had a higher slope, which is why it was built there. The fortress was called the Saransk gatehouse. It is from this word that the toponym of such a settlement as the city of Saransk originated, which began to be used in the future. The word "sarah" in translation from Finnish means "swamped."

The first inhabitants of the settlement were the military: Cossacks, gunners and archers. Later, people from nearby villages settled here, and the city began to grow. After 10 years, having entered the Saransk district, it becomes its administrative center. During the reign of Peter I was part of the Kazan province, and later was listed in Penza.

The city stands on the Volga Upland, the average altitude is 125-160 m above sea level, and the maximum varies within 250 m. It is located in the Insar River basin. The city of Saransk is located a little away from federal highways, the nearest major center is Penza.

The composition of the Saransk administrative district

Around the city there are 18 settlements, which together with the capital form the urban district of Saransk (Russia). The total population is more than 330 thousand people.

Administratively, the city is divided into three districts: Leninsky, Proletarsky and Oktyabrsky.



Industry and science

The city has a fairly well-developed industry. It is represented by such sectors: engineering, metalworking, electric power, non-ferrous and ferrous metallurgy, chemical, forestry, food and chemical industries. Large plants employ more than 60 thousand workers. Railways pass through the city; there is an airport of international status.

Saransk today is a large scientific and educational center of Russia. There are more than 10 research institutions and 18 educational institutions, including 4 universities.
