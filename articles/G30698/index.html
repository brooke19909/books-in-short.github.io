Samet Island in Thailand: Overview, Features, Attractions and Reviews

Thailand is one of the most common resorts in the modern world. Its territory occupies part of two large peninsulas - Indochina and Malacca. The geography of the country has created all the conditions for a comfortable holiday for tourists. There are plains and mountain ranges, many beaches and attractions.

What is Koh Samet

The island is located in the Gulf of Thailand. Its total area is slightly more than 13 square kilometers. This is enough to settle the village of Nadan and several hotels on a small plot of land. Batami is paid here (Thai money). You can change them at special points or remove them from an ATM.

The distance from the famous resort of Pattaya to the island is about 80 kilometers. By local standards, this is very small. This piece of land in the middle of the ocean is located quite close to the mainland (a little more than 6 kilometers). This is the middle of the road between Bangkok and the city of Koh Chang. Therefore, on weekends, the island is filled with locals from the nearest megacities.

Samet Island in Thailand is part of Khao Laemya National Park. This is a national treasure of the country. That is why the entrance to this land is paid. Money is collected by soldiers who guard the entrance to the park. By what principle the local military controls the availability of tickets for guests of the island is not known. The easiest way to stay quietly in the Samet territory is to pay 200 baht for an adult (about 420 rubles) or 100 baht for a child (210 rubles). This fee gives you the opportunity to visit all the beaches and the park on the island throughout your stay here.

Climate and weather

Thailand is famous for its climate. You can relax here almost all year round, except for the rainy season. At this time, the number of tourists decreases sharply.

Samet Island is favorable for recreation in that even in the rainy season the weather here is much better than on the mainland. Precipitation falls mainly at night. Therefore, some vacationers are happy to spend their holidays here.

Weather on Koh Samet island in Thailand pleases with its relative constancy. The water temperature remains the same almost all year round and does not fall below 28 degrees. The air warms up on the island to 30-32 degrees, at night the temperature drops to 24-27 degrees.

How to get there

The path to the island will take no more than an hour. At the same time, by ferry you can reach the main pier of the island in 40-50 minutes. This fascinating journey costs 70 baht one way (about 150 Russian rubles). Many tourists choose this option, because during the movement of the ferry you can enjoy beautiful views of the sea and nature.

From the mainland, tourists are delivered to any beach on Samet Island in high-speed motor boats. Travel time is 15-20 minutes. Such a trip costs much more: 200 baht one way (420 Russian rubles) or 350 baht (730 rubles).

All transport to the island leaves from the pier of the small village of Ban Phe. You can get to it from anywhere in the mainland. There are several options: taxi, bus, minibus and transfer. Each will differ in cost and comfort. The cheapest way is a regular bus. However, tourists need to adapt to the time of its movement in this case. Taxi is one of the most common ways of traveling for tourists with children and luggage. Renting a car will cost about 1300 baht (about 3000 Russian rubles). By the standards of the country, this is a little expensive, but for those who first time in Thailand and appreciate comfort, this is the best way to travel.

Transport on the island

Many tourists resting on the island believe that you can move around freely on foot. As a result, this is difficult to do. Although Samet Island in Thailand (the review of which was higher) is small (5 kilometers long and 2.5 wide), there are no footpaths (specially equipped) on its territory. Walking along the sidelines is very dangerous. You can walk along the beach - this is useful and pleasant, however, in some places the rocks block the path, and it is impossible to pass.

There are green taxis with benches inside. Such vehicles often surprise visitors to the island. In fact, this is an ordinary small truck, in the back of which there are benches and handrails are made. The fare between the beaches is from 10 baht (21 rubles). However, this is provided that the taxi is full. If there are no people, you will have to pay from 100 to 400 baht depending on the distance (210 - 850 rubles).

You can also rent a bike for the whole day. If you decide on this type of transport, then you need to rent it near the pier, in other places it will be more expensive (250-350 baht or 550-750 rubles per day).

Koh Samet Island (Thailand): beaches

This small piece of land in the middle of the salty sea has more than 20 beaches. All of them are specially equipped for vacationers. Some are hotel property, others are for general use.

Most of the beaches are located on the east coast. It is more comfortable to relax. On the western side of the island there is only one place for cultural recreation. The northern coast of Samet has three beaches.

The largest and most popular on the island is Sai Keo. It is popularized thanks to the purest turquoise water and soft white sand. The entrance to the water here is uniform and gentle. One of the minuses is a large number of people at any time of the day. The beach is located in the peak resort area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe island. There are many hotels, restaurants and entertainment venues. Therefore, you should not count on a calm, quiet vacation. Also, vacationers notice that often on the beach it smells strongly of gasoline. Perhaps this is due to the numerous transport boats. However, this fact spoils the overall impression of the rest.

For those who love a civilized cultural holiday, but without the hustle and bustle, it is worth giving preference to the beaches of Ao Pai and Wong Duan. They are located right behind the rock with a figure. Moreover, there are several shops and cafes on the territory. It also offers a variety of activities for the whole family. You can take masks for snorkeling, do a full body massage or just enjoy the sea view while sitting in a cafe on the beach.

Ao Cho, Ao Wai and Ao Prao are considered the most beautiful beaches on Samet Island. By the way, Ao Prao - this is the only beach on the west coast. It is so wonderful and beautiful that, resting on it, it seems that you are alone on a desert island. All these beaches are quiet and calm. There is little entertainment and travel services. But the water is just clear. The big plus is that the beaches are in small bays. It offers a beautiful view of the evening sea.

Complete unity with nature can be found on the deserted beaches of Ao Lung Dam, Ao Tubtim (Puds), Ao Thian and Ao Nuan. Here you can find only a few buildings (a couple of cafes and bungalows).

The beaches of the southern part of the island are the property of hotels. Only residents can relax on them.
