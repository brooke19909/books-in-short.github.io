Anna Maria Village 4 * (Greece, Crete): description of the hotel and resort

In our article we want to talk about one of the hotels in Crete. Located in Anissaris, Anna Maria Village is a great place for families.

A bit about the hotel ...

Anna Maria Village is located in the picturesque village of Crete, two kilometers from the city of Hersonissos and 300 meters from the beach. It will be easy for tourists to get to the hotel, as it is 24 kilometers from Heraklion Airport.





The small hotel complex is distinguished by an attentive staff who tries to help their guests in everything. The hotel was built in 1996, and the last major renovation was made in 2003.

Number of rooms

Hotel Anna Maria Village has 31 rooms at its disposal. All apartments are very comfortable and consist of a bedroom, a living room, an equipped kitchenette, a terrace or a large balcony overlooking the garden or the sea.

The rooms are divided into several categories:

  1. For two guests.
  2. For three people.
  3. For four tourists.

The apartments are equipped with air conditioning, private bathroom and satellite TV, safe, hairdryer, mini fridge, telephone, kitchen utensils.

Dining at the hotel

Anna Maria Village offers a varied breakfast in its restaurant.

Venus serves tourists the most popular dishes of world cuisine. The chef uses the best products for cooking. In addition, the restaurant serves food cooked in a real oven.





The hotel has an open-air lounge bar, where tourists can enjoy coffee, drinks and all kinds of sweets during the day. Experienced bartenders prepare the most delicious cocktails for visitors.

The pool bar allows you to relax on the sun loungers and enjoy music and cold drinks.

Hotel infrastructure

Anna Maria Village 3 * has a mini market, boutique, car rental, dry cleaning and laundry facilities on site. The pool has a relaxation area with umbrellas and sun loungers. For children, the hotel has a playground and a special pool. For the youngest guests, a cot and high chairs are provided.

Entertainment and Attractions

Relaxing in Anna Maria Village 3 * (Greece), you should definitely go to Heraklion. It is worthy of close attention from tourists, as it is the historical capital of Crete and its pearl. In addition, Heraklion is a tourist and shopping center of the island with its long history and traditions. The city was renamed by the Turks, but later, in the nineteenth century, it returned the legendary name - Heracles, and later - Heraklion. The city received its name in honor of Hercules, who accomplished a feat on the island. It is the largest city of Crete.

Heraklion has a lot of historical buildings and historical places, these are: Morozini Fountain, Knossos, Nikos Kazantzakis Museum, Titus Cathedral, Archaeological Museum, Kules Venetian Fortress.





Knossos Palace is located five kilometers from Heraklion. Tourists are attracted by its complex architectural form, similar to a maze, and numerous frescoes. An interesting decoration with a picture in the form of a bee was found on the territory of the palace, which became the real talisman of Crete.

No less interesting to the guests of the island is the Morozini Fountain, located on Venizelu Square. It was opened in 1628 by F. Morozini. The fountain is made in the shape of a bowl with four lions that hold it on their backs. Water from the fountain flows into eight bowls on the ground. The exquisite form of construction combines the creative and technical achievements of the seventeenth century.

The Nikos Kazantzakis Museum was founded in 1983. It is located fifteen kilometers from Heraklion. The museum is dedicated to the author of the very famous novel ‚ÄúThe Last Temptation‚Äù, and the translator of such works as ‚ÄúThe Divine Comedy‚Äù by Dante and ‚ÄúGoethe‚Äù Faust. In the halls of the museum you can see the diaries of the writer, rare video and audio materials.

Heraklion Archaeological Museum is dedicated to Minoan culture. It is one of the largest and most interesting museums in all of Greece. An interesting fact is that the museum to this day is replenished with new archaeological finds that are exhibited in its halls in chronological order. Perhaps the main value of the institution is the Phaistos disc, which is the only example of the writing of the Minoan civilization. Unfortunately, it is not possible to decrypt the letter until other examples of this writing are found.

Heraklion also houses the Cathedral of Titus, which is an Orthodox church. It received its name in honor of the patron saint of Crete, who was engaged in the preaching of Christianity in the first century. The relics of Titus are stored in the cathedral itself. The building was built at the behest of Emperor Nicephorus Phocas. An interesting fact is that over the entire period of its existence, the temple has repeatedly changed religion during the conquests of the Turks and Venetians. He was both a mosque and a Catholic cathedral, but still returned to his Orthodox origins. And the minaret was converted into a bell tower.





The most interesting and most beautiful attraction of Heraklion is the Kules fortress, which has survived from the Venetian times. It was built at one time in order to protect the coast from attack by enemies. Then it was destroyed during the earthquake, but restored again. The fortress is interesting in that its walls are built in the rock, which gives it a majestic appearance, making it a very reliable structure.

Shopping

Many tourists buying tours ask themselves what to see and what souvenirs to purchase. Heraklion in this sense is rich not only in sights, but also in markets and souvenir shops. While in the city, you can visit: the central market, Laika market, jewelry stores.

If you want to buy things, then you will certainly be interested in the Laika market, which is a European "stock". Items from company stores that were not sold are brought here. The location of the market is changing every day. However, as a rule, on Thursdays and Saturdays it is located in the very center. The souvenir industry is also developed in Crete. It is here that you can buy local branded products and products: honey and cheese in painted jugs, cosmetics, spices, etc.





The most popular shopping destination in Heraklion is the central market. Once it was the only one in the city, but then small markets appeared on the outskirts, pushing back the flows of the local population. But for tourists, the central bazaar is still the main place of shopping.
