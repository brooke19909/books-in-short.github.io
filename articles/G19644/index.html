The funniest village names

Funny village names are found all over Russia. They amuse with their pronunciation, but this raises many questions. Here is a list of the weirdest place names.

Why is it called that?

The name and patronymic, which respectfully call the settlement, are unique not only in the Skovorodinsky district, but throughout the whole of Russia.

At a certain stage of the construction of the Trans-Siberian Railway in 1909, the need arose to create a large station for trains, which were to continue on the eastward track. As a result, the station was the first stopping point. The name of the Russian explorer Khabarov is connected with the study of the Amur Region, and then the annexation of this territory to Russia in the middle of the 17th century.

When the question arose of naming the new station and the workers' settlement, it was decided to somehow connect it with the famous Cossack Erofei Pavlovich Khabarov. However, the city of Khabarovsk already existed in the Far East by 1880, there was even a village, the name of which turned out to be consonant with the famous surname. Of all the possible options, only Erofei Pavlovich remained. Few of today's youth, who are rarely interested in the history of the Amur Region, guess whose name is still the railway station.

A visitor to a web resource dedicated to Voronezh may be struck by the following inscription: "... Horseradish is the heart of Russia." Who would have thought that a village with a pessimistic name might be famous? At the beginning of the 17th century, young Peter I, full of energy, planned to build a military fleet, for which, in the area where the Bityug River flowed , a ship forest was prepared. Later, the village of Khrenovo appeared here, near which Count Orlov built a stud farm that specialized in the famous breed - Orlov trotters. Apparently, so that the residents did not feel flawed, the derived names of the farm and the river, respectively, Khrenische and Khrenovchanka, arose.
