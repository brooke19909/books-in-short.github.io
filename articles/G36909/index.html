Contour embroidery: features, description and patterns

Various needlework techniques not only help relieve stress and switch to something new, but also thanks to the forms of expression, you can create a real masterpiece. Contour embroidery is used for decoration and is often combined with other needlework techniques. The classic version includes the use of a minimum number of colors. Often these are monochrome works.

Features of contour embroidery

This type of needlework differs from standard embroidery in that it does not embroider a whole motif with a cross, but creates its outline. This gives the finished work lightness and makes it watercolor. Here you can highlight the originality of the implementation, the possibility of application in different areas of life, universality in terms of combination and originality of the plot.

After all, if there is nothing interesting in the needlework magazine, then you can independently draw the contours and embroider the future masterpiece on them. It takes a minimum of time to make an embroidered motif, because the main thing is to convey contour and shape.

In addition to the classic contour embroidery, there is also monochrome. The basis of the execution is the back needle stitch. If at the same time you take the thread in only one addition, then the result will be very gentle and pleasant to look at. Sew a stitch of black thread on white fabric with even elements. Monochrome is intended for tailoring:

  • Floral motifs.
  • Plant elements.
  • Lace on the fabric.

Contour embroidery can look very attractive and interesting, but the choice of materials plays a big role here. The final result depends on the basis of the work.

Work items

By the beginning of the process of contour cross-stitch, you need to prepare so that the result is of high quality. For work, in addition to basic materials, you can use a number of devices that will facilitate embroidery:

  • The cloth. For a small motive, uniform material is selected, it looks more neat and gentle compared to a standard canvas. Even crosses here are less. For large scenes, it is better to use the canvas, this will facilitate the process.
  • Threads. Mouline thread is selected cotton or silk. You can also embroider with wool, but this will add bulk to the work.
  • Hoops and scissors. The former are used to create an even and tight stretch, so the sewn crosses will be neat and beautiful.
  • Water soluble marker. The gadget is used to create squares on the fabric on which the plot will be embroidered. This helps embroiderers to orient themselves in the process of tailoring and not to lose the necessary part of the pattern.

Although you can find many different products in needlework stores, it is better not to save on contour embroidery, since the better the elements of work, the more beautiful the result will be. The main thing is that the purchased materials fit the embroidery plot.

Scheme selection

Depending on where the finished work will be applied, a scheme for the process is selected. Contour embroidery has different variants of embodiment, ranging from primitive ornaments and simple patterns, which are used as an element of decoration in other needlework techniques and ending with complex plots and portrait works.

The choice of the scheme should depend on the skill level of the needlewoman, her skills and speed of completion. To decorate textiles, a floral theme is used, you can embroider small flowers or combine them with abstraction, the same ornaments or interesting patterns. Contour embroidery suggests combining only a few colors in order to convey the essence of the work using simplicity and conciseness, without overloading it with visual moments.

Small paintings or plots that will be framed, often consist of different elements and additional needlework techniques. It can be a counting cross or a smooth surface. Patterns for contour cross stitch can decorate any thing, complement it with simplicity and at the same time sophistication.

Embroidery Tips

Monochrome contour embroidery is best done with silk threads, then the pattern will turn out delicate and refined. You can also use cotton floss, but it will not give the desired effect.

Before embroidering on a uniform weave fabric, you need to wash it so that the base sits a little. If you do this after embroidery, the pattern may look jagged or skewed.

Experienced embroiderers are advised to work gradually, embroidering square by square. If you leave complex sections of the circuit for later, then as a result you can miss a lot of details and make mistakes.

Mouline Secrets

Often on small motifs, some elements are located far from each other. In this case, you do not need to make broaches from the threads, since this is noticeable, and even a sheet of paper under the base will not be able to remove the strips. It is better to fasten the thread with a microstitch, it will be imperceptible, and the embroidery will not deteriorate.

Before starting work, it is necessary to check the threads for molting, if after lowering the water in the room temperature, the threads let out color, it is better not to embroider them. Quality floss will cost more, but the effect of embroidery will continue for more than one year.
