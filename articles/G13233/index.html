DVB-T2 receiver: description, specifications, instructions, models and reviews

Digital television not so long ago, but has already firmly entered our lives. However, not all of us managed to acquire televisions with built-in digital receivers. There are two ways to solve this problem: buy a new and modern TV or install a small DVB-T2 receiver in your home.

The second option is the most profitable and acceptable, because the receivers cost an order of magnitude less than the new "TV box". However, some difficulties may arise here, if you do not know what to focus on at the time of purchase.

DVB-T2 receivers

Today, TV towers, both analog and digital, broadcast in the same range. Therefore, to receive high-definition video channels, there is no need to install a new antenna - ordinary decimeter ‚Äúhorns‚Äù, which we all have for sure, are suitable.

It must also be remembered that not all TVs are capable of ‚Äúreproducing‚Äù a digital signal. Of course, by connecting the DVB-T2 receiver to an old "TV box", you can watch your favorite channels, but you will not notice differences in image quality.

Modern digital receivers are small-sized devices that look very similar to DVD players. Most models have the simplest liquid crystal display on the front panel, control buttons.

On the back wall of such a device are connectors for connecting to the antenna and to the TV, additional slots for flash cards, adapters and other devices, as well as a power switch. Choosing a DVB-T2 receiver, you need to focus on the rear panel.

Receiver Characteristics

The first important characteristic of a digital receiver is the supported video resolution. This setting should match the resolution of your TV. So, if the screen only supports SDTV, then there is simply no need to buy a set-top box that supports high-definition HD video - the TV will still broadcast as before. The same goes for the built-in 3D function.

The presence of the necessary connectors for connecting the console to the TV is the second most important parameter. In the vast majority of equipment, an RCA cable, or ‚Äútulips,‚Äù is used to connect. Some DVB-T2 terrestrial receivers use SCART cables along with RCA ports.

In addition, you can use one of the built-in interfaces to connect to a TV. As a rule, an HDMI port is very often used as such. The presence of all the above connectors allows you to connect two TVs to the same receiver at the same time.

Integrated Interfaces

On the market, you can often find equipment with a built-in USB interface, thanks to which you can connect a flash drive, a phone and, in some cases, even a laptop to the receiver. However, such a DVB-T2 receiver is slightly more expensive than a typical installation. Most often, the described features are used to record TV shows on a flash card or to play audio and video files stored on a flash drive.

The built-in CI slot for connecting a conditional access card, which allows you to view paid channels, has a significant impact on the price. Today, almost all channels broadcast on the territory of Russia are free, but in the future such an opportunity may turn out to be very useful.

Additional functions

The receiver may have a large number of built-in functions that owners are not even aware of. The most popular is the ability to ‚Äústop time‚Äù - TimeShift. But such an opportunity is just a trick that makes the DVB-T2 digital TV receiver more expensive.

The fact is that such a function is available only for satellite receivers. In ‚Äúantenna‚Äù devices, when the ‚Äútime is stopped‚Äù, a screenshot is taken and the sound is turned off, but the transmission itself continues to go.

Another popular feature is the TV guide, which allows you to view the program guide, works slightly better. Its functionality depends on the specific channel. Setting a timer to automatically enable or disable the channel are the only additional functions that work flawlessly.

Connecting the console to the TV

There are two ways to connect a digital set-top box . If your antenna has a ready-made coaxial cable, then simply insert it into the DVB-T2 digital TV receiver, and then proceed with setting up the channels. In some cases, you have to buy a coaxial cable yourself, especially if your antenna has a built-in amplifier.

In this case, you will also need a f-type screw connector. To install it on a cable, it is necessary to cut off the insulation layer, distribute the metal foil and mesh along the contour and get to the copper core. Then you can screw the connector into the receiver.

There are many more ways to connect the TV to the console. You can use tulips, RCA, SCART or HDMI cables. There will be no difficulties here. After combining the devices, it remains only to configure the DVB-T2 digital receiver in the same way as the channel search on the TV is configured.
