DIY Christmas tree from napkins

Of what improvised means are New Year's symbols not made! A do-it-yourself tree made of napkins looks like a work of art. Not everyone will guess what you made a forest beauty from. Moreover, there are several ways to make New Year trees from this material, choose any one you like and start creating.

Napkin Christmas tree

This option will help to decorate the New Year's table. How to make a Christmas tree from tissue napkins, we will now tell. Take a green napkin, fold it four times. Arrange the free angles to face you.





Bend up the first corner, then the second, third and fourth. In this case, each subsequent you must bend to a lesser extent than the previous one. Holding your work with your hand, turn the cloth over to the back. If you want to better fix the fold lines, you can iron them with an iron.

Now you need to fold the inverted napkin three times in this way: first bring the right corner to the left, then the left - to the right. How such a Christmas tree is made of napkins, clearly shows the photo above. In the picture at number ten it is clear that the napkin must be carefully turned over again and further rotated through 180 ¬∞ so that the free corners are directed towards you. Start to bend them from the top. Enter the next corner under the base of the first triangle. Continue to form the tree in the same way, bending the bottom corner last.

Now you can put the Christmas tree on a plate and make a few more, decorating them with a New Year's table. You can arrange the fabric tree vertically, and put on the top a small copy of Santa Claus's hat.





Slender beauty

To make a paper Christmas tree, you will need napkins with a pattern and a sheet of cardboard. Attach a large plate to it, circle it, cut the resulting circle. You can use the compass to draw a circle. Now cut the resulting shape along the radius - that is, from the edge of the circle to its center. Coat one incision on the back with glue, curl a circle to make a cone. Wait for the glue to dry. After that, the do-it-yourself tree from napkins is done on.





Kulechki turn into a tree

Take the same napkins, they will be needed to decorate the first lower tier of the tree. Lubricate one side of the napkin with glue (where the bend is), apply the second side to it, with which the two are joined at an angle. Press down the junction with your fingers - you get a fist. Lubricate the fold line with glue, attach this part to the bottom of the cone. Glue a second cloth next to it, folded in the same way. In this case, their free angles should be directed downward.

After you have made the first lower tier, proceed to the second. It is also better to make it from napkins of the same pattern or suitable in color. Thus, build a Christmas tree consisting of six or more rows of napkins, folded in pads. The top can be decorated with a star cut out of cardboard, or tie a satin bow on it. If you want to make a large Christmas tree, put a cone on a stick and stick its lower edge into a flower pot.





Jewelry work

Also (gluing on the cone blanks) is another tree made of paper napkins. First, you need to cut squares with a side of 1 cm, then collapse each in a certain way.

Take the first small square, put the core from the ballpoint pen in its center, wrap this piece of napkin on it. Then bring this structure to the PVA glue poured into the bottle cap. Lubricate the center of the folded square with a small amount of glue, attach the blank to the bottom of the cone. Glue the remaining parts in the same way - first to the first, then to subsequent tiers. Decorate the tree with paper beads, after that you can admire the finished work.

Fluffy spruce - begin to create

A cardboard cone will serve as the basis for the next beauty. It will tell you how such a tree is made of napkins, a master class. If you made paper boats in your childhood , now this technique will come in handy. Have you forgotten how to do it? Looking at the picture below, you can quickly remember.





Unroll the green napkin first. If it is too soft and large, then do not expand it. Now bend all 4 corners to the center, at this point they should meet. To make this easier to do, first find the middle: for this, bend the napkin one at a time, and then along the second diagonal. The intersection of these lines is the center of the square. This is how the Christmas tree is made of do-it-yourself napkins, but the process is not yet complete.

Creativity Continues

Carefully turn the napkin on the back side and do the same manipulations - bend the four corners to the center. Remember to stroke the folds with your hand. Turn over the napkin again, straighten the 4 resulting petals, give them volume by rounding them. Apply glue to the center of the back of the workpiece, glue it to the lower tier of the cone. In the same way, roll up the second napkin, glue it. After that, proceed to filling the second and subsequent tiers.

It is time to decorate your creation. Cut a strip of 2 cm wide from the red or pink napkin. Starting from the small side, fold the napkin into a circle shape. Put a little glue on the resulting ball, place a paper toy in the center of the napkin, which is already glued to the cone. So make some balls and decorate them with a paper tree.

A do-it-yourself tree made of napkins. You can put it on the table in the office or leave it at home. Each time, looking at her, you will remember how you made such beauty, and be proud of yourself. But these are not all the ways in which a Christmas tree made of napkins is born . One more will be discussed later.





Once a circle, two circles - there will be a Christmas tree

To make such a decorative tree, you will need cardboard or thick paper. Lubricate any of these substrates with a small amount of glue, attach a tissue.

When the blanks are dry, cut them in a circle, make the edge wavy. Make big circles first, then smaller ones. Now in the center of each workpiece you need to make a hole. Carefully do this with a knife or scissors. Put a blank made of cardboard on the table. Lubricate the edge of the wooden stick with glue, attach it with this part to the opening of the first mug, wait until the glue dries. Next, string the prepared blanks onto the stick, so that the bottom is the largest, and the top is the smallest. Glue the star cut from the tissue paper blank onto the crown of the tree. Here is another Christmas tree ready.
