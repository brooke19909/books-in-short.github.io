What to visit in Rome: sights, history and interesting facts

Not every city on the planet can boast as rich and rich history as Rome. He delights and amazes anyone who comes here. Sightseeing in the city is enough for 20 vacations. And many tourists come for a short time. During this time, it is impossible to see everything. Therefore, what to visit in Rome in 3 days, you need to think in advance, and leave the rest of the attractions for the next visits.

The eternal City

The city, in which all roads lead, was built by Emperor Romulus in 753 BC. It was a tiny peasant settlement, which was destined to turn into a powerful power. The ancient Romans were much more interested in war than in art. Simplicity and modesty were most valued. And the heroes of the stories were harsh and disciplined wars.

It is not known whether Rome would become a cultural center, if not for neighboring Greece. After the conquest of this country, the level of education has changed significantly. From Greece, noble patricians wrote themselves highly educated slaves. A flood of philosophers, architects, poets, actors, sculptors and educators poured into Rome.

Noble citizens did not spare money for training. They studied grammar, exact sciences, philosophy, theatrical art. And among themselves, educated citizens spoke Greek.

In Rome, new temples and palaces begin to be built. They are decorated with Greek statues. The Romans absorb the culture of the conquered peoples. At the same time, they retain their own unique spirit. Without exaggeration, we can say that the Roman Empire gave mankind an outstanding culture and made a huge contribution to the development of civilization.

For a long time there is no longer the people who inhabited this power. Despite her power, she was destroyed. One can only try to recreate the lifestyle of the ancient Romans from the preserved monuments of antiquity. Places worth visiting in Rome are everywhere you go.

Public transport

The fastest way to get to Rome is by plane. It is better to book a taxi from the airport in advance. It will be much cheaper than looking for a car directly upon arrival.

It is recommended to rent a car only if you plan country trips. Otherwise, the money will be thrown to the wind. In time to visit most of the sights in Rome, you can also use public transport. But the city is unusually beautiful, so it is best to study it during hiking.

Metro has three branches. It starts working at 5:30, the last train leaves at 23:30, and on weekends the underground services can be used until 1:30.

There are about three hundred bus routes in Rome. They begin to work at the same time as the subway. Only 20 lines function at night. Intervals between buses are 30 minutes.

Tickets can be purchased at special vending machines, tobacco or newsagents, at large stations, and at night routes - from the driver. Tickets are valid on the metro, commuter trains, buses and trams. Depending on the price, travel cards are divided into several types:

  1. Disposable. It makes it possible to use any means of transport an unlimited number of times within 100 minutes. The price is 1,5 ‚Ç¨.
  2. Daily. It has no limits within the validity period. Price - 7 ‚Ç¨.
  3. Single tourist. Suitable for guests who stayed for a few days and have not yet decided which places to visit in Rome. With this ticket you can ride around the city an unlimited number of times on any public transport for three days. Its price is 18 ‚Ç¨.
  4. One for a week. A tourist has the right to use public transport for seven days (for only 24 ‚Ç¨).

Coliseum

What to visit in Rome for the person who is here for the first time? Of course, the Colosseum. The huge amphitheater is one of the most significant buildings of the empire. Its wall consists of three tiers. Once, each of them was decorated with columns made in different styles.

On the first tier was the emperor‚Äôs bed and the place of the patricians. Magnificent statues made by the best craftsmen were installed. In addition, the sculptures were located around the perimeter of the building, and its outer walls were decorated with marble slabs.

The modern Coliseum has lost its splendor, but retained greatness. You can watch it for 12 ‚Ç¨. This price also includes a tour of the forums and the Palatine Hill. Since this is one of the most popular tourist destinations, you will have to stand in a huge line of people who want to visit the Colosseum in Rome.

The most beautiful squares of Rome

City squares are those places in Rome that you should definitely visit. Moreover, it is completely free. Roman squares form an unforgettable and unique atmosphere that cannot be forgotten.

Visit recommended:

  3. Piazza Venezia. Benito Mussolini spoke from the balcony of one of the palaces.
  4. Piazza Navona This elegant square stands on the ruins of an ancient stadium. There is also the Four Rivers fountain.

  6. Column Square is famous for its beautiful palaces, as well as the column of Marcus Aurelius.
  7. Piazza Barberini. Tourists are most interested in the Triton Fountain. You can visit the national gallery of ancient art, which is located in the palace of Barberini.

Pantheon

Among the many ancient temples that you must visit in Rome without fail - the Pantheon. The structure is more than 2 thousand years old. Originally it was a temple dedicated to all the Roman gods. He later became a Christian church.

The main mystery of the temple is its arch - it is the largest, not reinforced concrete dome in the world. How he survived two millennia is incomprehensible. Modern builders claim that the life of concrete does not exceed 600 years.

The temple is illuminated with a hole in the dome, 9 meters in diameter. Through it, not only sunlight but also rainwater enters. It flows into special openings in the floor. You can visit the Pantheon for free.

Underground rome

Dungeon lovers will also find what to visit in Rome. These are caves under a pitchfork, catacombs, mitreriums and aqueducts. These are creepy and mystical places. The most popular are the catacombs of St. Callistus. They are already about 2 thousand years old. Galleries and tunnels form an intricate maze 19 kilometers long. It is located on four levels (the deepest - 20 meters).

The catacombs of St. Sebastian are more modest in size. Here was the most accessible underground cemetery. The walls of the catacombs are decorated with frescoes and mosaics.

In underground Rome you can see not only the ancient buildings. Well preserved bunker built for Benito Mussolini. It is located under the villa of Torlonia and is open to the public.

Best italian dessert

Gelato is a dessert that consists of frozen cream, milk and sugar. It differs from ordinary ice cream in a low content of air and milk fat. The texture is dense and creamy. It melts quite slowly.

Gelato is not manufactured industrially. They are working on its creation manually, in gelatories. It is served immediately after cooking. Each master has his own special recipe.

The most ancient jelateria of Rome is Fassi. The institution was officially opened in 1880. This happened after King Vittorio Emanuel tasted a gelatto from a cart of a street vendor named Fassi. The crowned lady really liked the dessert. He helped the merchant open an ice cream parlor. It is currently located at the Palazzo Fredro.
