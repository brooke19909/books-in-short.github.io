Royal family of Sweden: Bernadotte

By equality and stability, Sweden is perhaps one of the most exemplary democracies in the world. The monarchy and the royal family created by Carl Gustav XVI in this country have rather strong roots and great public support.



The head of state is considered the most important symbol of Sweden. The king is not interested in politics; he does not have any official powers since 1974. All his duties are mainly ceremonial and representative in nature. The monarch, having lost his last right to appoint a prime minister, has become a "trade symbol" of the state, "promoting" his business interests around the world.

The royal family of Sweden is one of the oldest in the world. Monarchical traditions in this country have existed without interruption for a whole millennium. According to the Constitution, the royal family transfers the inheritance of the throne to the eldest descendant of the monarch, moreover, regardless of gender. Therefore, the head of Sweden in the future will be Crown Princess Victoria, and not at all brother Carl Philip, who is younger than her in age.



In the struggle for its existence, the royal house did a lot: including changing the laws of succession to the throne. Today, apart from Carl Gustav XVI, members of the Swedish royal family are also Victoria with her husband, Prince Daniel, Karl Philippe and Princess Madeleine who loves to visit the United States. But the royal house and the royal family are two different concepts. The first also includes other close relatives of the king - his sister Birgitta, as well as the widow of his uncle.

The Swedish royal family enjoys the well-deserved love of their subjects. The king constantly visits the municipalities, makes the so-called "erixgatures" - visits to the province, thereby showing his proximity to the people who answer him with adoration. To see with your own eyes the wedding of the crown princess in Stockholm in 2010, a huge number of people gathered from all over the country.
