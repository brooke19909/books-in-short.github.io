Sandy beaches of Turkey. Top places

Tourists, purchasing a ticket to one of the resorts in Turkey, dream of a beautiful beach and warm sea water. Unfortunately, their dreams do not always come true. Many beaches in Turkey recently are not in very good condition. But there are places where locals prefer to relax. By the way, not a single tourist knows about them. Getting to these beaches is easy. The most important thing is to know where they are located.



The sandy beaches of Turkey, or rather, the best beach of the Black Sea coast, is located near Patara. This is a small village where many Russian tourists are accommodated. According to some estimates, the coastline of this beach stretches for 20 km. Not far from it there are guest houses, the cost of living in which rarely exceeds $ 25 per day. Agree, this is inexpensive for Turkey.

You can relax on the beach of Oludeniz. If you are interested, then its name translates as "Dead Sea". Do not be afraid, the Turks have in mind a slightly different meaning of this phrase - a calm sea. The landscapes that surround it are mountains only. Even during a storm, the sea in this place is always calm, which means it is suitable for small children.



Sandy beaches in Turkey are not uncommon, but Olympos still stands out from their background. This is perhaps the best beach on the coast. It is located near the town of the same name. There are no shops and hotels familiar to major Turkish cities here. But in this part of the country there is a real pine forest. A lot of medieval and ancient Roman ruins will allow you to get acquainted with the life of ancient people.

The sandy beaches of Turkey offer various kinds of entertainment, but the main thing is the purity and beauty of the landscapes. All this you can get on the beaches in a village called Side. Recently, this place began to attract tourists much more than Antalya or Marmaris, and all because the local beaches are clean, and the sea is clear and warm.



About 140 km from Antalya, on the territory of Alanya, there is a beach that is noticeably different from the local ones. It is ideal for those who prefer a holiday in silence, but there are places for noisy companies. You may be surprised, because these two categories of tourists are unlikely to get along nearby. But a twenty-kilometer beach can afford such a separation.

As you can see, the number of excellent beaches on the country's coast is still large. Having spent a little time on the road, you can enjoy the ideal, untouched nature by man, as well as excellent service.
