Church of the Deposition in Leonov. Church of the Deposition on Donskoy

All cultural and educational events necessarily include the study of monuments of ancient architecture. This is important for the assimilation of native culture and instilling spiritual values. But church-temple complexes are completely unique buildings. They can be considered as an architectural heritage, a feat of Russian masters, as a history of the clergy and, of course, as a sacred monastery. Today we will talk about the Church of the Deposition in Leonov and on Donskoy. Their stories are somewhat similar, but in any case, they both appeared to honor the memory of a great event.





History

Its existence dates back to the first century. The famous Cathedral of the Deposition in Leonov from the first half of the XVII century. The first notes about it date back to an even earlier period, 1635, then there was a wooden church on this site. The construction of the stone church, which has been preserved and is still operating today, occurred during the reign of Prince Khovansky.



This was his gesture of remorse for his addiction to alcohol and violation of church rules. After the death of the prince, the Cathedral of the Deposition of the Church in Leonov was closed and did not work for a long time (from 1800 to 1859). This happened due to the fact that the new prince was disturbed by the ringing of bells. Before the opening, it was restored at the expense of the manufacturer Molchanov. He survived the Soviet era relatively calmly, only 12 years (from 1930 to 1942) was closed, the rest of the time he acted. Since 1859, the temple has not undergone reconstruction and reconstruction, so it has survived to our times.

Architectural style

The Church of the Deposition in Leonov was built in the Baroque style, which at that time gained great popularity. The simple geometric forms of the wooden temple were replaced by elaborate, polygonal facades and lush interior decoration. Even today, the temple looks very beautiful and interesting. The entrance to the shrine is a white stone arch with trellised sashes. Inside is a cozy little square, which is especially beautiful in the fall. On the inner walls of the temple you can see the murals of the Position of the Robe of Our Lady in Vlacherna. The whole territory is surrounded by green trees, so it is not easy to photograph the Church of the Deposition of the Deposition in Leonov. Photos are good, that is, fully demonstrating the beauty of the church, only in late autumn, when the foliage flew around. In winter, snow-white walls merge with the background, which somewhat spoils the impression.



Revered relics and shrines

For believers, it is precisely the icons that are the subject of veneration and worship. There are several old canvases that cause special admiration. They are the ones who glorify the Leon Church of the Deposition of the Robe. In the main aisle of the temple, to the right of the Altar there is a revered temple icon of the Position of the Robe of the Blessed Virgin Mary with a particle of the Robe of the Mother of God. On the right is the icon of St. Sergius of Radonezh, St. Dmitry of Rostov, the icon of the Mother of God of Kazan.

To the left of the Altar there is the Revered Icon of the Mother of God of Smolensk, which dates from the 16th century. In total, the temple has more than 50 icons. Hundreds of believers come here daily to offer their prayers to the Lord.





Shrines

The Church of the Deposition of the Virgin in Leonovo has special value. This is a reliquary, in which particles of many saints are located. This is the main pride of the church and the subject of worship of believers. It is located in the right corner of the central aisle, all parishioners have the opportunity to venerate the relics. Legends circulate about their miraculous power, but you can see for yourself that this is true.

Church of the Deposition on Donskoy

His story is very similar to the one that his brother lived. The fate of the temples in Russia often overlap. In 1625, the Persian shah handed over to the Russian Tsar and Patriarch Filaret one of the four parts of the robes of Christ. According to legend, it was in this garment that Christ went to the place of execution. It was transferred to the Kremlin Assumption Cathedral, but a wooden church was built at the site of the great meeting. Construction was completed in 1690. But already in 1713 a project for the construction of a stone church appeared, and on the site of the old building, the Church of the Deposition of the Don was erected. The construction was supervised by Moscow architect Y. Bukhvostov.

What is unique about this temple?

It is more difficult to say how it resembles others. His very birth was already a miracle. In those days, stone architecture was banned, since this material was required for the construction (or rather, reconstruction) of St. Petersburg. Nevertheless, in 1716, on the Don Street in Moscow, a magnificent temple appeared, made in the style of Moscow Baroque. It is distinguished from all other contemporaries by five chapters, a proportional four, completed by the attic. The central cross is crowned with a crown; there is no exact version of this idea. This temple has not been closed since its opening. To this day there are services.

Under his leadership, a fence with gates was created and a northern aisle was added.

The first reconstruction was required in 1923. A strong storm knocked down two church chapters, but the temple still has not stopped its work.
