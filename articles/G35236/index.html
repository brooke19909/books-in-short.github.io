Kazan State Circus: history, description and reviews

Kazan State Circus is a favorite place for leisure activities in the city for both children and adults. The permanent entertainment programs of his own troupe have long been fond of the inhabitants of Tatarstan, and indeed the country as a whole. But this cultural institution is not only surprising for visitors. His building was built in the form of an original architectural structure resembling a UFO. Despite the fact that it was designed in the last century, in the modern world it looks very relevant. We learn a lot of interesting things about the circus from the article below.

Historical facts

More than a century ago, the first circus building was erected in Kazan, namely in 1890. Such an idea arose among the Nikitin brothers, who personally laid its foundation. It was a wooden building. The construction was completed in 1912, at the same time the first performance was given in the arena. Among the invited guests at this solemn event was Fedor Ivanovich Chaliapin.





About ten years later, the Kazan State Circus received official status. In 1966, it was decided to build a new, more modernized building of this cultural institution. The result is a structure that has no analogues in the whole world. The engineer who developed this project was awarded a medal for his work. Thanks to its building, the Kazan State Circus is an architectural monument. The troupe of artists of this cultural institution has always been considered one of the best national groups.

The whole history of the Kazan Circus is kept in its own museum, which was organized near its building in 1995. Here you can see photographs of various famous numbers of Russian and foreign artists.

Description

The doors of this cultural institution are open to visitors all year round, and the numbers of popular artists from around the world make all of his performances successful. Sometimes tickets to the circus are sold out long before the date of the performance.

Significant advantages of this institution are an equipped auditorium, a modern playpen and enclosures for keeping animals. All this makes the circus building an attractive venue for many foreign groups. The circus's own troupe is regularly on tour and gathers its fans in different arenas of the country.

The building operates a school where the younger generation can learn circus art. In the entire history of the educational institution, many celebrities have come from here, among them: Leysan Gayasova, Marat Bikmaev and many other great artists.

Spectator Impressions

Many have loved since early childhood the circus (Kazan). Reviews about his ideas can always be heard only warm and enthusiastic. A beautiful building in the form of a "flying saucer", and, of course, a spectacular and extraordinary program, makes a special impression on the audience.

In the arena, the artists perform in stunning costumes, always excellent musical accompaniment of their own circus orchestra, a variety of special effects, as well as only interesting numbers - all this, undoubtedly, causes indescribable delight among all those who decided to purchase tickets to the circus and watch its magnificent representation.





Cost of visiting

The auditorium seats more than two thousand people. It is divided into several sectors with nineteen rows in each. The ticket price depends on the chosen place, that is, on its distance from the arena. Children under five years old can attend the circus (Kazan) for free. Prices for adults start at 300 rubles in the back rows, and the most expensive places near the arena cost about 900 rubles. In addition, there are places where tickets can be purchased for 400, 600, 750 and 850 rubles.

Contact details

The circus building in Kazan is located on Millennium Square. There are several ways to get there.

  • By metro, for this you need to go to the Kremlyovskaya station.
  • You can take bus number 22, number 28a or number 89 and get to the stop "Circus".
  • Trolleybus number 2 will take to the stop. "Central Stadium", and No. 7 - to the Sports Palace.

For all questions of interest regarding the purchase of tickets, you can call the ticket office at the following number: (843) 292-14-12; and for enrollment in school, call: (843) 292-10-31.





The circus building is open daily from nine in the morning until half past six in the evening. Performances are held three or four times a week, twice a day.
