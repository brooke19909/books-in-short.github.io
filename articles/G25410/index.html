Unique sights of Halkidiki

Halkidiki is a peninsula that is located in the north-east of Greece on the coast of the Aegean Sea. It owes its name to the ancient Greek city of Chalcedon. This area is known for the birth of the greatest scientist of all time Aristotle. In addition, the peninsula has huge tourist potential - the sights of Halkidiki attract travelers from all over the world.

Attractions Halkidiki

Holidays in Halkidiki is a unique opportunity to visit a picturesque place. Imagine green forests, steep cliffs, deep gorges and a clear sea - a true paradise. But this peninsula is not only amazing nature, but also historical monuments that are of great interest to tourists. The sights of Halkidiki are of great historical value.

Meteors

This is the name of the complex of 24 monasteries that were built in antiquity on top of the rocks. Translated from Greek, this word means "soaring in the clouds." From ancient times, hermits from all over the world came to this place. Today, 6 monasteries are open here, each of which is of great historical value.

Mount Athos



Visiting the sights of Halkidiki, you can not forget about Mount Athos, on which there are 20 monasteries (no more construction should be built). But the entrance to these places is limited for tourists. Men can visit Mount Athos only on a special visa, while women will not be allowed to go there. For disobedience, you can get a significant term and go to jail.

Olympus

Mount Olympus is the abode of all Greek gods. Today this place is a national park of Greece. The divine landscape captivates and fascinates. Hiking and cycling trails are available here. Climbing the sacred mountain begins with the city of Litochoro, where you can find an information center.

Platamonas

This is the name of the castle-fortress in the Platamon Valley in Halkidiki. The sights of these places date back to the 13th century. Platamonas can be translated as "Castle of beautiful women." Every year in the summer, the Olympus festival is celebrated here.

Loutraki

13 kilometers from the city of Aridea are the thermal springs of Loutraki. The water temperature in them is always at around +37 degrees. The resort in its properties is not inferior to the famous French sources in the city of Vichy.

Petralona Cave



If you want to see where the oldest man in Europe was found, then your destination is Greece, Halkidiki. The attractions of Petralon are unique. Here were found the remains of animals that are more than 5 million years old! All finds found in Petralon can be seen in the Anthropological Museum.

If you want to visit Greece, do not forget to call in Halkidiki. The sights of these places will not leave anyone indifferent.
