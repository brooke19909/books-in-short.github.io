Relief waves

Relief waves are the easiest to perform. Even a beginner knitter will cope with them, since they use only front and wrong loops. The pattern is dense and quite expressive.

For such patterns, it is best to use dyed yarn. It is important not to iron and steam after knitting . This can lead to loss of relief.

Beginner knitters, who still find it difficult to understand patterns, can be asked to use the description of the ‚ÄúWave‚Äù pattern, knitting with knitting using only the simplest ones - front and back loops.





The rapport (repeating part) of the pattern consists of six loops. In the description, as in the diagram, only the front rows are given. Wrong should be knitted according to the pattern. Do not forget about the edge ones: the first loop of the row should be removed, the last always knit from the wrong side.

First you need to dial the number of loops a multiple of 6 plus 2 edge.

First row: 3 wrong, 3 front. Repeat until the end of the row.

Third: knit 2 loops wrong, 3 front, 1 wrong. Perform until loop runs out.

Fifth: repeat the wrong side combination, 3 facial, 2 wrong side.

Seventh: 3 front, 3 wrong - knit to the end of the loops.

Ninth: repeat 1 purl, 3 facial, 2 purl.

Eleventh: 2 wrong, 3 front, 1 wrong - knit to the end of the row.

After the twelfth row, you should again return to the first and continue knitting until the fabric reaches the required length. If you correctly make the ‚ÄúWaves‚Äù pattern with knitting needles, the scheme of which is given in this section, you can get relief waves located vertically.
