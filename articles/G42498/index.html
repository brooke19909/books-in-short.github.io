Mountains of Tajikistan: description and photo

For many millennia, people have been attracted by mountains. Tajikistan is a land of magnificent glaciers and unconquered peaks, a climber's dream. The republic is almost completely covered by different hills. These are mainly huge mountain systems, occupying 93 percent of the republic. By the way, almost half of the country is located at an altitude of more than three thousand meters above sea level.

Fire ice in the mountains of Tajikistan

The high mountains of Tajikistan have many glaciers. Their total area is almost nine thousand kilometers. Glaciers occupy six percent of the total area of ​​the republic. Snow at high altitude is gradually rammed under its own weight and becomes firn ice. They differ from ordinary coarse grains. Over time, they become denser and large and small glaciers form.



Mountains of Mogoltau and Kuraminsky ridge

The mountains of Mogoltau and the Kuramin Range are located in the north of Tajikistan. And they enter the massif of the western Tien Shan. The mountains of the Kuramin ridge stretch for 170 kilometers. The highest peak is in the northeast. Mogoltau ridge is located on the coast of the river. Syr Darya. The mountains are small, forty kilometers long. They are isolated by the Mirzrabat passage. The height of the Mogoltau mountains is from 320 to 500 meters. The left side of the ridge rises to 1000 m.

Gissar Mountains

Gissar Mountains are located in the center of Tajikistan. They are surrounded by the Ferghana Valley, the Alai River and Surkhobob. The length of the Gissar mountain ranges is approximately 900 kilometers. The highest point of the Hissar Mountains is named after them. Twenty-second Congress of the CPSU. Its height is 4688 kilometers. In the mountains of Gissar there are many passes. The most significant is Anzob. Its height is 3372 meters. Near the mountains is a hundred-kilometer Hissar Valley, adjacent to the ridge.



Pamir

In some countries there are mountains in history. Tajikistan boasts Pamir. This is one of the famous world mountain systems. Sometimes the Pamir is called the "roof of the world." The mountains are in the east. They are divided into two regions: Western and Eastern. The border that runs between them connects Yashilkul Lake and Zulumart Mountains.

Central to the Pamir Mountains is the ridge of the Academy of Sciences. Its height is 5757 meters. And the pass is at the level of Mont Blanc - the highest peak of the Alps. The highest peak of the Academy of Sciences is Ismoil Somoni. This is the highest mountain in Tajikistan, reaching 7495 meters.

The history of the Pamir peak is very interesting. At first he was named after Stalin. This happened in 1931. Then, in 1961, it was renamed the peak of Communism. And in 1999 he received the name Ismoil Somoni. Several small glaciers flow down from it. They merge with their big "brother" called Garmo.



But the Pamir Mountains are not only remarkable for this . Tajikistan has another high peak - Korzhenevsky peak. Its height reaches 7105 meters. In the west, the Pamir is striking in its variety of surfaces. The foot of the mountains is located at an altitude of 1700 to 1800 meters above sea level. In the north, the mountain range is surrounded by the Zaalaysky ridge with a length of 95 kilometers. Through the Kizylart pass with a height of 4280 meters, the highest mountain highway passes.

Ferghana Valley

The photos of the mountains of Tajikistan presented in the article demonstrate all their beauty and splendor. The Ferghana Valley, part of which is located in Uzbekistan, is no exception. It is through it that the famous “Great Pamir Highway” passes. The chain of ridges is located in the north-west of Tajikistan, between the Kuraminsky ridge, Chatkal and Mogoltau mountains. The height of the Ferghana hills is from 320 m on the Syr Darya and its islands, and from 800 to 1000 meters in the surrounding foothills. In the western part is the plain of the Hungry Steppe. Its height is from 250 to 300 m.



Ak-su

One of the splendors of planet Earth is the mountains. Tajikistan has another pearl - Ak-Su. The height of the mountains reaches 5355 meters. The highlands are located 120 kilometers from the city of Khujand. This territory is famous for its extraordinary and majestic beauty of untouched nature. The peaks of the ridge sometimes reach more than 5000 meters. The mountains consist of dense granite with cracks and protrusions. The gorges are very beautiful and easily accessible. On them you can travel on horseback.

Turkestan Range

The Turkestan ridge is located between the Zarafshan and Ferghana valleys. It extends two hundred kilometers. In the north it gradually decreases and ends with the Nuratau mountains. The slopes of Turkestan are strikingly different from south and north. One of them is completely snow-white, and on the other snow lies only at a level of from 3,500 to 4,000 meters. Glaciers, the largest of which is the twenty-meter Rama, are located only in the eastern part.



Fann mountains, or Shakhristan pass

Shahristan Pass 3351 meters high has another name. These are the same Fan Mountains. Tajikistan can rightfully be proud of its amazing mountain peaks. The Fan Mountains are of great height and complexity. In common people they are called "warm."

This name was given to the mountains for the mild climate, which is absolutely not characteristic of the highlands. The top of the Shakhristan pass, Chimtarg, reaches 5495 meters. The Fan Mountains is one of the most visited places in Tajikistan. Here is the largest natural reservoir in northern Tajikistan - Iskanderkul Lake.

One of the attractions of the mountains of Tajikistan - mineral springs, both hot and cold. They have a different degree of mineralization, which allowed the country to master the sanatorium-resort direction in the tourism industry. More than two hundred mineral springs with resort areas organized on their basis help to treat cardiovascular, musculoskeletal, genitourinary and other diseases.
