How to get to Sicily from Moscow without transfers?

Finally, the long-awaited vacation! You are already "on the bags" and it remains to decide how to get to Sicily from Moscow. And best of all - by direct flight, bypassing the loss of time on expectations during transfers. There are two airlines operating non-stop flights from the capital of our homeland as part of the seasonal schedule. Do not forget that you will definitely need a Schengen visa, as you fly to Italy. In Sicily, the airports of Palermo and Catania, as well as Trapani and Comiso, are waiting for their guests. From Moscow you can get directly to only the first two air harbors.

Alitalia

From Moscow Sheremetyevo Airport , Palermo and Catania can be reached with the help of Alitalia. A one-way ticket for a direct flight costs about 20,000 rubles, the time spent on the journey will be 4 hours 15 minutes. The lowest prices for flights are offered in October. Explore the offers of popular search engines by selecting travel dates, see all options by month in a year, and you can save.

If you decide to purchase tickets on this airline, the best solution is to buy them on the official Alitalia website, which has a working Russian version.

Of course, cheap flights can be more attractive to many. If you are willing to spend about 7 hours on the road, you can take a flight with a transfer in Rome. Everyone chooses the best option for himself how to get to Sicily from Moscow: either quickly or cheaply. How much is your time, only you know.

S7 Airlines

Sicily is a wonderful island of excellent cuisine, holy revered traditions, ardent hearts and passions. He is waiting for all lovers of Italian color and wonderful beaches. There is another way how to get to Sicily from Moscow directly - use the services of S7 airline. The flight lasts about 4 hours.

S7 Airlines is a member of the oneworld global aviation alliance; it operates flights on modern comfortable Airbus A320 airliners, which have economy and business class lounges. Catania is already the sixth Italian destination in which the airline operates.

Palermo Airport

Falcone-Borcellino is one of the most popular air hubs. It is located in the north of the island, 35 km from Palermo.

Direct flights to the city of Palermo from Moscow are operated by Alitalia. Great demand for tickets falls in the summertime: June and August, as well as in September. The lowest demand is observed at the beginning of the year: in January, February and March. Ticket price is from 17 thousand rubles.

Catania

The famous beach resorts of the island - Taormina and Giardini Naxos - are located near Catania. This city, built next to Mount Etna, is cleaner and less noisy than Palermo. The place will surely appeal to lovers of ancient European architecture, admirers of mountain trekking, picturesque landscapes and, of course, lovers of sea vacations. After all, it is here that the most beautiful and clean Sicilian beaches are concentrated. Lovers of Italy recognize Catania as one of the best Italian resorts. You have a chance to check it out!

Catania Airport - Fontanarossa

The third airport in Italy in terms of annual passenger traffic is located right at the foot of the beautiful and dangerous Etna. Do you want to witness a bewitching spectacle, because the volcano is considered active?

The length of the runway of the airport is 2438 meters, at the airport there are 20 gates and 6 terapraps. Up to 6 million people pass through the terminal annually. Authorities are striving for an indicator of 20 million tourists annually.

Wind jet

Since 2011, residents of the capital have had another way to fly non-stop, tell how. Direct access to Sicily from Moscow was possible with regular direct flights from Wind Jet. They were carried out from Moscow Domodedovo to Palermo or Catania, the cost of a round-trip ticket was about 15 thousand rubles. But since 2012, the company has not operated flights in any of the directions.

How to get to Sicily from Moscow by train

To be satisfied with the details of the trip, one must also be able to enjoy the road to the final destination.

What can I say, there are tourists who categorically do not tolerate air travel, and for them time costs are not important, the main thing is not by plane. Most often people come to Sicily by train from Rome.

Do you like the sound of wheels? Still would! Knowing how to get to Sicily from Moscow without transfers, you can consider other route options, more inexpensive and interesting. A train from Rome to Palermo is waiting for you. You will spend 12 hours on the road, the ticket price starts from 70 euros.

It is interesting! Through the Strait of Messina, the train is transported on a special platform with rails, this happens at night, when passengers are sleeping. This is a very interesting and unusual adventure. From Messina, the train goes to Catania, from where you can even go to Syracuse.
