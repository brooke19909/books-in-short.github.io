DIY knitted Christmas toys

New Year is a favorite holiday of all people. He is family friendly. In Russia, they began to celebrate it on the first of January under Peter the Great. Prior to that, it was celebrated in March. In the New Year, it is customary to put a coniferous tree, usually a spruce, and dress it up.

The holiday is associated with many rituals, traditions, signs. One of them is to celebrate the new year with a new thing.

You can enter your own tradition in the house: to celebrate the New Year with a knitted Christmas toy.

Christmas toys are small knitted products. The yarn for them should be with a thin thread. Knitted Christmas toys crochet and knitting. They will need 1; 2 and 2.5 mm.

Toys for the New Year tree can be knitted from the remains of yarn, which will always be found in the home of any knitter.

Knitted Christmas toys are voluminous and flat. They can be knitted to decorate the house and Christmas tree. For each New Year, you can knit a new toy. The assortment of Christmas decorations is diverse: the symbol of the year, Christmas trees, bells, Santa Claus, garlands, balls, cones, stars, bird and animal figures, snowflakes, cars, dolls, Santa Claus, Snow Maiden, snowman. Christmas knitted balls and toys are an immense field for creativity. They can be knitted with ornaments, openwork, from boucle yarn, from fleece yarn. Crocheted Christmas tree toys: Christmas tree with balls

Required:

  • Yarn of green, white, brown, red, blue.
  • Hook number 2.

Execution

Knit 5-6 squares of different sizes with green yarn. Knit in any way. The squares should be tight. First, connect the largest square, then smaller, followed by a little less, even less, and so on 5-6 pieces. Each square should be crocheted with white yarn.

Knit a Christmas tree leg with brown yarn in the form of a truncated cone without crochet. Make small balls of yarn of blue and red colors of 15-18 pieces.





Christmas tree assembly

Bend one edge of each square in the middle of the sides of the square and sew on. Sew on the top of the ball. Lay the finished pieces diagonally in descending order. Below is the largest square. On top we put smaller and thus all the figures. The middle of the upper square is at the bottom corner. Sew to each other all the squares. Attach balls on them, evenly distributing colors.

Sew the Christmas tree trunk to the bottom square.

From above make a loop knitted from green yarn. The Christmas tree can be hung anywhere.
