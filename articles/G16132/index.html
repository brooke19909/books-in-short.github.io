Adelaide, Australia: Attractions, Photos and Climate

In the southern part of the smallest continent, on the shores of the sea bay, the city of Adelaide is located. Australia can be proud of this locality, its inhabitants and history. The city today is famous for its athletes, festivals, wine, as well as progressive social reforms.

A city with a beautiful name

Compact, clean, elegant ... This is how Adelaide is most often described. Australia is already considered a prosperous and well-groomed country. Adelaide, in turn, is one of its most beautiful and cozy cities! That is why thousands of tourists from all over the world always want to visit here.





This city appeared on the maps in 1836. Today he bears the name of the Queen of England, the wife of the monarch William IV, whose name was Adelaide. Australia in those days, as you know, was a colony of the British Empire. The "father" of the city of William Light gave Adelaide the correct lattice shape, which has survived to this day.

Today, a little over one million people live in the city. The important administrative and financial institutions of the country are located here.

Adelaide, Australia: Climate and Geographical Location

This city is characterized by a dry Mediterranean climate. Most of the atmospheric precipitation falls here in the winter, but in summer rains are extremely rare, and it is very difficult to predict them.

Adelaide is a city in Australia that is located in its southern part, at the foot of Mount Loft. Snow here can be seen infrequently, and frosts were recorded by meteorologists only twice in the last 100 years.

Population and features of life in the city

How do they live in Adelaide? Australia, as you know, has a high standard of living for its citizens. And the southern city of Adelaide managed to stand out qualitatively even against this background. The city is famous for the whole list of successfully implemented reforms. In addition, local authorities are not going to stop on the successes achieved. So, in the fall of 2015, a bill was submitted to the South Australian Parliament, which should attract additional investment in the region and contribute in every way to social progress in the city.





Adelaide is Australia's fifth most populated city. About 1.2 million people live here. The city is very attractive for labor migrants. Significant diasporas of the British, Greeks, Italians and Vietnamese are formed here.

Adelaide has an excellent layout. The city center has a regular lattice structure of streets with five main squares. Adelaide is surrounded by a dense ring of parks and green spaces. The defense industry, services and research are the main areas of activity for Adelaideans. Here is the headquarters of Australia's largest oil company. And every second car produced in the country is assembled in Adelaide.

Adelaide Australia Attractions

Adelaide can be safely called the most suitable city in Australia for a comfortable and informative vacation. It is bright and clean. Around the city there are many beautiful parks, and not far from it there are beautiful sandy beaches. In Adelaide, you can relax well even in winter, because the temperature here rarely drops below 12 degrees.

Adelaide for Australia is the capital of wine, literature and art. Famous ballet troupes, artists and other artists come here every two years. But on odd years in Adelaide hold a grand wine festival. It is pleasant and easy to walk along the streets of this city: ancient architectural monuments here successfully combine with beautiful new buildings.





The surroundings of the city will please every lover of nature. There is a protected island on which several species of kangaroos live - a symbol of the Australian animal world. The corresponding island name is Kangaroo. Not far from Adelaide, there are limestone caves with ancient Aboriginal drawings. Well, the most popular activities for tourists coming to Adelaide are fishing, golf and horse riding.

In the city itself there are many wonderful restaurants serving national Italian, Spanish, Thai, Malaysian and other cuisines of the world. Extreme tourists may risk taking a stroll along Hindley Street, a street of bandits and drug dealers. However, you should think carefully about whether to go there at all.





There is one more place to visit in Adelaide. It's about the Tandania Museum. This is a unique center for the study of the cultural heritage of Australian Aboriginal people. Unusual exhibits are collected here: musical instruments, products, paintings and other Aboriginal art objects. The highlight of the museum is the fact that only representatives of the indigenous peoples of South Australia work here.

Conclusion

Adelaide (Australia) is a large but very cozy city on the shores of St. Vincent's Bay. It was founded back in 1836. Today you can relax comfortably and safely while visiting interesting museums, exhibitions and festivals.
