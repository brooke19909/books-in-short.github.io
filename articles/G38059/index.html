Station, Samara. Samara, the railway station. River Station, Samara

Samara is a large Russian million-plus city. To ensure the convenience of citizens in the region, a wide transport infrastructure was developed, which includes bus, railway, and river stations. Samara is an amazing place where the main passenger stations are not only the leading transport hubs of Russia, but also real architectural masterpieces.



Railway communication Samara

Kuibyshev railway is one of the largest in the Volga region. The railway tracks are transit, long-distance trains that stop at Samara station. The railway station has a passenger destination, equipped with 12 tracks, as well as 5 platforms. For the convenience of passengers, there are two spacious waiting rooms, which can accommodate up to 2600 people at a time.





Today, more than 16 thousand passengers are accepted by the railway station every day. Samara, therefore, is an important transportation hub through which you can reach almost anywhere in the Russian Federation, as well as to the CIS countries and neighboring countries.

Station History

The construction of a modern station was started in 1996, in the summer. On the eve of Railroad Day, the first stone was laid in the foundation, which symbolized the beginning of the construction of a new transport hub. At the same time, the station was fully functioning. Samara is a station through which both passenger and freight trains passed. For this, temporary technologies were organized that played the role of the station.





1999 was marked by the opening of the first launch complex of the railway station. The contest was a spacious waiting room, which for the convenience of passengers was equipped with an elevator, an alarm system, as well as an electronic scoreboard. Security was provided by more than 150 cameras that made video recordings throughout the entire facility.

In mid-2000, the second building of the railway station was commissioned. The new comfortable building has become a hotel, where every interested passenger at a low cost can rent a room for living.

At the end of 2001, just before the New Year, the third building of the railway station was commissioned. The building houses cozy waiting rooms for passengers, cafeterias, a service center, a cultural zone for those waiting, and a technical department. The administration plans to erect the second stage of the railway station.

Interesting facts about the railway station

  • The modern station building is the tallest room for sending trains on the territory of modern Europe. Height - 100 meters, which was achieved by the construction of a massive building, on the roof of which is equipped with a metal spire.
  • The twin of the building is Matabiau (station). Samara, therefore, has ongoing cooperation with the French city of Toulouse. The agreements were signed between the representative of Russian Railways Sergey Abramov and Sophie Bouissard, who is the director of the French National Railways division.
  • This station allows you to enjoy and learn the history of the Kuibyshev highway. Samara has a long history of the formation of railway transport infrastructure. So, the starting date for the formation of the highway is considered to be 1874. The main historical milestones are presented in the exhibits of the museum, located on the second floor of the railway station.




Where is the station located and how to get there?

The Samara Station address of the exact location is as follows: Komsomolskaya Square, Building 1. You can get to the station from anywhere in the city. There are tram routes towards the station: No. 1, 4, 16 and 23; trolleybuses - No. 2, 4, 16 and 17; buses No. 1, 3, 5d, 14, 22, 37, 50, 52, 77, 128 and 128-72.

Water communication Samara

The river station deserves increased attention from vacationers. Samara is a city located on one of the most full-flowing rivers of Russia - the Volga. This makes river boat cruises possible.

The passenger ship departs on both weekdays and weekends. The minimum number of people to walk is 20. The cruise time is 1.5 hours. The cost of the entrance ticket to the ship "Moscow" is from 200 (children's ticket) to 350 rubles (adult). There are promotions that allow you to swim for free on the Volga (for example, birthday men, newlyweds and children under 5 years old).





Cruise routes Samara

One of the most popular cruises is a boat trip to the Vinnivka pier. It is carried out on the Voskhod-08 high-speed vessel in the period up to 30 minutes. After the boat stops, passengers are invited to visit local attractions, including the Holy Mother of God Monastery.

Perm, Novgorod, St. Petersburg, Volgograd - only a small part of the cities that become accessible to lovers of river cruises.

Samara is a unique city in its capabilities. It is here that both large transport hubs leading to different parts of Russia and neighboring countries are concentrated, as well as excellent tourist infrastructure. Cruises on the Volga, city tours and tours to cultural and historical places are what opens to vacationers who have visited Samara.
