One of the most important elements of the transport system of Russia

Route A114 continues the international transport corridor from Northern Europe to the Asian part of the mainland (Western China). The motorway runs through the territory of two regions of the Russian Federation - Vologda and Leningrad, connects the cities of Vologda, Sheksna, Tikhvin, Novaya Ladoga (Leningrad Region) and other settlements. International, federal and regional significance of the road:

  • shortens the way from Finland, St. Petersburg to Arkhangelsk, Vologda, Yaroslavl, Kirov, Perm;
  • It is part of the road corridor between the northwest and the Urals;
  • connects the transport network of the north-east of the Russian Federation with the Leningrad region, St. Petersburg;
  • gives an opportunity to enter the Murmansk and Arkhangelsk regions, Karelia;
  • connects Vologda and Cherepovets, other settlements within the region.

Modernization of the A114 motorway

The high economic and tourist potential of the South Ladoga, Vologda Oblast and numerous attractions attract people to these regions. The number of cars passing along the Vologda-Novaya Ladoga federal highway is increasing by about 15% annually. The highway is undergoing reconstruction and modernization aimed at ensuring traffic safety and improving roadside infrastructure. Until May 2017, builders plan to increase the number of:

  • lanes, additional interchanges, culverts;
  • sidewalks and pedestrian crossings;
  • lighting devices;
  • road signs;
  • parking lots for personal transport, stops where passengers can take the buses "Novaya Ladoga - Volkhov", and another route transport.

A small part of the route is already equipped with "electric shepherds" - devices for scaring away moose. Devices are powered by solar panels and supply electric charge to metal wires. Tension does not pose a threat to the lives of people and animals, but it frightens off the latter when trying to get out on the road. In the future, the number of "electric shepherds" will increase in those sections of the Novaya Ladoga highway where the migration routes of large wild animals pass.

Natural conditions of the Vologda - Novaya Ladoga road

A freeway is laid on the northwestern edge of the Russian Plain, in a forested area. The relief here is determined by the alternation of lowlands with ridges and elevations. The cities of Novaya Ladoga, Volkhov, Staraya Ladoga are located on the spurs of the Baltic-Ladoga ledge. The lowland plain extends 120‚Äì125 km east of St. Petersburg. The absolute height usually does not exceed 200 m. A lot of lakes, rivers and swamps are the main natural feature of the North-West region. Some water bodies belong to the Atlantic basin, while others are connected with the Arctic Ocean or the Caspian Sea. On the way from Novaya Ladoga to Vologda to the north-west, the road crosses the rivers: Sheksna, Suda, Chagoda, Syas, Volkhov and other watercourses.

The path "from the Varangians to the Bulgars"

The Novaya Ladoga highway is the shortest road from St. Petersburg to Vologda, Arkhangelsk, Yaroslavl. In the Leningrad Region, the motorway approaches the mouth of the Volkhov River. The confluence of Lake Ladoga is located in the center of the coastline of South Ladoga region between the Neva and Svir River deltas (100 km). The history of this land is inextricably linked with the past, present and future of Russia.





In Staraya Ladoga, 15 km upstream from the mouth of the Volkhov River, in ancient times the Volga-Baltic River Route passed - "from the Varangians to the Bulgars and Khazars." A lively international exchange was conducted from the second half of the 9th century. The trade route was used by Russia, the Volga Bulgaria and the Khazar Khaganate. Much later, canals were laid near the coast, connecting the Volkhov with the Neva. The lake-river and marshy region was not suitable for the construction of land roads. The history of the highway in this area dates back to the creation of monasteries in Tikhvin, Novaya Ladoga, Dymi, which were visited by Russian tsars.

How to get to South Ladoga

If you plan to get to Novaya Ladoga by car, then from St. Petersburg you can drive along the Murmansk highway to the Volkhov bridge. Before the bridge there is a turn onto the A 115 highway, which leads to Staraya Ladoga and Volkhov. To Novaya Ladoga - turn onto the regional road.

A convenient option for travel by public transport is through the regional center - Volkhov. At the train stations of the city, located on different banks of the river of the same name, trains from St. Petersburg come. There are also trains stopping from Moscow heading north (to Petrozavodsk and Murmansk). By train from St. Petersburg can be reached in 2.5-3 hours, from Moscow - in 8.5 hours.

Buses and minibus No. 23 leave from Volkhovstroy-1 station to Novaya Ladoga. This transport runs along the left bank of the river. Regular bus "Volkhov - Novaya Ladoga" No. 24 goes in the same direction, but along the right bank. Interval of public transport: buses run after 1 hour, minibuses leave more often.





City of New Ladoga

During the Northern War, Peter I ordered the residents of Tikhvin and Ladoga to move to the vicinity of the old Nikolo-Medvedovsky Monastery, the location of which at the mouth of the Volkhov River the sovereign considered convenient for commerce and shipbuilding. The proximity to St. Petersburg (120 km) and Shlisselburg was also taken into account. For New Ladoga, the year 1704 was a turning point, when the shipyard was laid. After 6 years, the first military vessels equipped with guns went down from the slipways.

Ancient Ladoga began to be called "Old", and the new city quickly grew and settled down. On its territory some buildings from the complex of St. Nicholas Medvedsky Monastery were preserved, a significant part of it was destroyed by German knights in 1538. In total, there are 19 cultural, historical and architectural monuments in the city of Novaya Ladoga (Leningrad Region). Among them are the buildings of the 18th century: the Clement Church and the officer club of the Suzdal Regiment.

Rurik's residence

Staraya Ladoga is the oldest known Eastern Slavic city in Russia. This large village during the Varangian Rurik became a territory, "where did the Russian land come from." Founded Ladoga no later than 753 years, the name of the settlement was given in the Scandinavian language - Aldoga, which means ‚Äúold river‚Äù. The advantageous position at the beginning of the journey from the northern countries to the south and east contributed to the heyday of the city. In 862, he was led by the Varangian Rurik, who was called up to Russia, after some time transferring his residence to the headwaters of the Volkhov River - to Novgorod. In Ladoga, another legendary prince Oleg the Prophet died.

The fortified city flourished before the invasion of the Swedes in 1313, who came on ships on Lake Ladoga. The remains of ancient buildings were found in 1702 by Peter I, who decided to relocate the old Ladoga people closer to the coast. Archaeological excavations on the territory of the ancient fortress have been conducted for more than 130 years. The found rarities and artifacts took a worthy place in the exposition of the Staroladozhsky archaeological museum-reserve. On the territory of the village preserved churches of the pre-Mongol era, other temples and monasteries, ancient mounds and ancient settlement.
