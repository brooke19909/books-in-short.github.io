Shockproof glass for phones: description, features and reviews

Despite the improvement of the screen coatings of modern smartphones, with daily use it is highly susceptible to mechanical stress. Protect it from shock, scratches and cracks allows the installation of protective shockproof glass.

What is it and what does it consist of?

Such an element is made of tempered glass that has undergone chemical treatment. In thickness, it slightly exceeds the standard protective film. According to the characteristics - significantly exceeds. The market offers protective tempered shockproof glass with a thickness of 0.26 and 0.33 mm, and some even 0.5 mm.

The hardness of the glass is 9 N, which is 2‚Äì3 times higher in comparison with different types of film. Commercials often show how tempered glass protects the display from hammering. It‚Äôs immediately clear that this is an exaggeration, but if you scratch the screen with a knife or a key, no trace will remain on the surface.

For the most part, protective glass is distinguished by a five-layer structure, it consists of such materials:

  • a base made of silicone, which is responsible for fixing the element on the surface of the screen;
  • a bonding layer that does not allow the screen to crumble into fragments in the event of an impact;
  • anti-glare layer, responsible for the brightness of the display even in strong light;
  • a protective coating that prevents damage to the screen;
  • the lyophobic layer, which ensures a clean screen, repels moisture and prevents fingerprints and greasy fingers from remaining on the display.

From which we can conclude that the protective glass is not only an obstacle to mechanical damage, but also has a number of other useful functions.

How to choose it?

When choosing a shockproof tempered glass, you should pay attention to the following characteristics:

  1. Price. The cost of protective accessories varies greatly. At the same time, you should not think that only expensive models have the best qualities. But high-quality glasses can not cost about one dollar, the average price range varies from 10 to 30 $.
  2. Strength. The vast majority of tempered glasses have a hardness parameter of 9 N. This indicator indicates the degree of impact that the material can withstand upon impact or for a short time. If there is no corresponding marking on the product packaging, it is better to refuse the product.
  3. Glass or film. It will be more difficult to determine. It all depends on factors that are more important to the user. For example, film gives less loss of screen sensitivity than glass, is easier to install and cheaper. However, the second option has greater transparency and strength, anti-reflective coating, there are no prints left on it.
  4. The material of manufacture. The coating protecting the screen is not always made of glass. In some cases, it can be replaced by a special multilayer polymer. If cracks form on the glass upon impact, then only scratches will remain on the polymer. There are also differences in image transmission. It's about bandwidth. Glass very accurately and vividly conveys the picture, while artificial material makes it worse and dimmer.
  5. 3D glass. If the phone has rounded edges, then when selecting a hardened coating, you should first of all pay attention to the same rounding around the perimeter as the protection. Some manufacturers produce glass with specially designed transparent glue, which allows you to hide irregularities along the surface if you purchased a completely flat part.
