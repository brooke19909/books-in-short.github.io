Some geographic data

The right tributary of the Yenisei is called Podkamennaya Tunguska, as well as the Middle Tunguska, Chulkan and Katanga. The basin area is estimated at 240,000 square kilometers, the length reaches 1865 km. Its source is located on the Upper Tunguska Upland, and the Central Siberian Plateau with the natural region of Tunguska, this river crosses from the Angara Ridge in the south to the Yenisei in the west. In 1908, a meteorite fell 80 kilometers north of the village of Vanavara. Naturally, he was called Tunguska, because everything was named around him - the region, the river, the population. The Podkamennaya Tunguska is navigable for 1,146 km, despite numerous rifts and rapids. Some thresholds even have personal names.

The originality of the river





Stony Tunguska is very diverse. In the upper reaches it has a rather wide and deep valley, then, cutting through the outcrops of rocks (they are called traps), the river forms narrow gorges. Traps, for centuries blown by winds that carry away particles of rocks, form characteristic pillars (Red Coast location). In some places, the current reaches a speed of 3-4 centimeters per second. Like the rivers of the Krasnoyarsk Territory, the described water flow has tributaries. Right are Tetere, Chula and Plague. To the left are Como and Velmo.
