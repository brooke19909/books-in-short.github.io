The specifics of North Goa in India

This is the smallest state in the country. And in terms of population - one of the last. But this is not the specificity of Goa, located in the southwest of the Hindustan Peninsula. The fact is that this state has long been a colony of Portugal. Therefore, it is the most Europeanized territory of India. Catholic churches and monasteries, Mediterranean urban development - such is the surroundings of Goa. Although there is also enough amazing India here. This state has an unofficial border dividing its territory into two parts - southern and northern. The full name of our hotel is Highland Beach Resort 3 North Goa. Therefore, we will talk about the specifics of the northern part of the state. In the seventies of the twentieth century, hippies began to settle here en masse. Later they were joined by the rastamans and downshifters of our day. Until now, their settlements surprise tourists. Here you can attend parties on the ocean, listen to trance-style music, admire their simple way of life. Prices in North Goa are lower than in South. But the resting public is simpler.



Weather in Goa

The state is located in the subequatorial climate zone. Therefore, there are two pronounced seasons. The first lasts from the hottest month of the year - May. Humidity at this time is 75%, so the heat is under +33 gr. very difficult to tolerate. In June it gets colder due to cloud cover and precipitation. There are a lot of them in Goa. Rains pour from a bucket, but, which is nice, only during the monsoon. The last season begins in June and ends in October. Another month the weather in Goa remains cloudy. Sometimes there is a sudden heavy rain. Somewhere in December, the tourist season begins. It reaches its peak on Christmas and New Year, lasts all January and February, and ends in March. Precipitation during this period is extremely rare. Temperatures (at night plus twenty, during the day it reaches thirty-one Celsius) are very comfortable for Europeans, and the sea calms down and does not storm. If you go to Goa, then only from November to March.
