How many volts are in a stun gun, what are its permitted parameters in Russia, and some rules for its use

Nowadays, walking alone in the evenings, especially for girls, is far from safe. Of course, after the 90s of the last century, the fear of the older generation dulled a little, due to habit, but young people sometimes just need a means of self-defense. But which one to choose is a rather serious question. One has large enough dimensions, the other is too dangerous and requires permission, the third can not be used, standing against the wind. In today's article, we will talk about how many volts are in the stun gun, how to choose it and what the law says about its application.

The stun gun: features and possible consequences

It should immediately be noted that not any such device is approved for use by civilians. And the point here is not the magnitude of the voltage, but the power that it possesses. A legal indicator is considered to be no more than 3 watts (for employees of law enforcement agencies 10 watts). However, even a power of 3 watts is enough to immobilize the enemy for 3-5 minutes, without causing substantial harm to his health.

But it is impossible to foresee everything. After all, it is not known whether the aggressor suffers from cardiovascular diseases. In addition, in the heat of struggle, the discharge does not always fall into the safest parts of the body. For example, a shock to the temple (even with 3 watts allowed) can lead to death. That is why it is worth seriously considering before acquiring such a means of self-defense.
