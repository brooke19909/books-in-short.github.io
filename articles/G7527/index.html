Gastronomy

While all of Europe eats with ordinary cutlery, the Chinese remain faithful to their traditions by continuing to use chopsticks. Since the population density here exceeds all possible boundaries, you have to adapt using all possible and impossible products that sometimes so surprise tourists. So, a few interesting facts about China and the gastronomic preferences of its inhabitants:

  • Few people know that the fortune cookie, which many know as Chinese, is actually Japanese! It is especially popular in the United States, where he was brought by immigrants from Japan, borrowing the idea in their temples. The confusion about cookies appeared, probably, during the Second World War, when Americans of Japanese descent ended up in concentration camps and transferred their tradition to the Chinese.
  • The squeamish people of China are no different. Almost everyone can turn it into food products. However, it does not do without quite specific traditions. In the city of Dunyang, for example, there is a tradition - to boil eggs in the urine of young boys. A dish, so unusual for the West, can be prepared up to a day, and, according to residents, it smells in the spring.
  • Cooking in this country reached crocodiles. Not only are bags made of them, but here they are also forced to guard the house, and then eaten. Since local gourmets are very fond of crocodile meat, they are actively engaged in catching reptiles. Since it is not easy to catch a large crocodile, they are mainly caught in small ones and fattened, keeping them at the entrance to the house, which makes the animal actually a guard. But the sad fate of the guard crocodile does not pass by, and when its tail reaches the desired length, it becomes a dinner of omnivorous Chinese.
  • Soup "swallow's nests", in spite of its name, has nothing to do with these birds. And they do it not at all from birds, but use swift nests, which are made of saliva.

Little bit about gloomy

Speaking about interesting facts about China, one cannot fail to mention the attitude of the inhabitants of the Asian country towards death and their excessive superstition.





  • So, as in many countries the number 13 is considered unlucky, and for such a fear there is even a name, so in some eastern countries the numbers 4 are feared. Why?   • In China, there is a tradition of burning paper drawn money at a funeral. Where does she get her roots from? The thing is that the Chinese see the afterlife as a place where spirits live. Once in hell, the deceased must give a bribe to his ruler in order to avoid punishment. Therefore, people who come to the funeral, burn painted banknotes in order to "convey" them to the spirit of the deceased. Often they depict traditional symbols, for example, a dragon. The first Chinese civilizations

The development of China in the Neolithic is divided into two main stages. The first is represented by the Yangshao culture (3-2 thousand BC), the existence of which is confirmed by the find of ceramics. The second is Longshan - from which many dishes of very delicate work were preserved.

Dynasties

Dynasties appeared on the territory of modern China several centuries ago. The very first of them is Xia, whose founder was Yu. The center of the dynasty was in the province of Henan, although it is believed that its influence extended to other regions. Over time, the power of the emperors decreased, and the sovereign's powers began to relate only to spiritual guidance. A very important role in the days of the Xia Dynasty was played by calendars, which Confucius was struck a millennium later. As soon as emperors began to neglect their spiritual duties, the dynasty fell into decay. However, soon a new one was born.



The modern country of China was created under the influence of the Shang-Yin dynasty, which includes about 30 rulers. She was distinguished by totemism and the matriarchal type of power. The goddess of the earth was considered more significant than the god of heaven. The territory of the state was located in a significant part of modern China and was divided into regions. A representative of each of them had to periodically appear in the emperor‚Äôs palace with gifts in order to honor him.

The next was the Zhou Dynasty, which overthrew the rulers of Shang-Yin. Subsequently, this state, which was located on the territory of almost the entire Yellow River basin, broke up into a number of others, which constantly competed with each other.

Chinese empire

In the period from 221 to 207 BC. e. the Qin dynasty came to power , which managed to unite disparate territories into a single whole. This time had a good effect on the entire territory of the modern state of China. The most interesting thing happened with the formation and development of the empire. The feudal system was destroyed, and transport, on the contrary, was developing. During this period, the economy, trade and agricultural products developed. However, the dynasty lasted only 10 years and died with its ruler, after violent uprisings broke out in the state.

The Han Dynasty came to replace the crisis and wars. Over time, the emperors managed, and among the people, Confucianism gained popularity. However, later the Sui dynasty came to power, during which the Great Canal was built. However, the constant war and hostility of neighboring provinces drained the treasury.

Considering interesting facts about China, one cannot fail to tell about the Tang Dynasty, which was able to establish relative peace and stability in the country. During this period, culture and art reached a special peak. Many of the rulers of the dynasty took care of this. This was a powerful state in which not only art was actively developed, but also science and religion. However, the rulers failed to take care of the army and soon overthrew the dynasty due to constant uprisings. A number of dynasties were in power in this territory, including Yuan, Ming and Qing.

New story

An important period in the development of the state was the 18th century, when international relations developed especially, and many Europeans were interested in learning about China, its traditions and people, because it was possible to purchase the best silk, porcelain and much more. A little later, there was a war with Japan, as a result of which China lost Taiwan, Korea and the Pescador Islands. The last Qing dynasty fell in 1911, and a year later the state was proclaimed a constitutional republic. Later Beijing will be declared the capital of the country.



In 1917, China entered the First World War, wishing to return the lands taken away by Japan. But he does not receive support from allied countries. Then the Communist Party is at the helm of the state, and one of its leaders is Mao Zedong, one of the most famous leaders of all time, who has made many changes in the state. The disunity of the country tried to eliminate a number of rulers, but to no avail. Since then, China went through the civil and Korean wars, many reforms took place in the state, and the economy was restored.
