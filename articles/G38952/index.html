The Natural Gate "Sky Gate" (China) is the main attraction of Hunan Province

Discover the unique mountain

Acquaintance with the province begins with a mountain rising almost eight kilometers above sea level, which was called the "Gate of Heaven". China, famous for its miraculous natural masterpieces, provides the opportunity to make the most unusual journey of your life.



To get to the mountain, which will present many surprises for its guests, tourists overcome the difficult, but insanely exciting way. According to the Chinese themselves, Tianmen Mountain itself and the road to it are equivalent attractions. There are stories that during the reign of the Ming Dynasty, the instigators of the peasant uprising hid innumerable riches in the rock, and treasure seekers have come here for several centuries to try their luck, which so far has not smiled at anyone.

Which route to choose?

You can get to the top in several ways. The choice depends on the physical stamina of the guests of China and their love of extreme sports. Those who want to test themselves walk up a long staircase with 999 steps. The guides assure that whoever finds himself at the top, having overcome this difficult test, will find eternal joy.

Those who like to tickle their nerves go on an excursion by car, and this winding road, only eleven kilometers long, will be remembered by everyone. On the "heavenly highway" 99 turns, and the distance between them can be less than 200 meters. A ride on an impressive mountain serpentine with sharp turns will make your heart go on heels.



Tourists who are not afraid of heights choose a cable car with a length of about 7500 meters. The cable car offers incredible views of the beautiful mountains, and it seems that the peaks are within reach. In some places, the road rises sharply, and at these moments, when the cabin is lost in the clouds, the soul freezes with fear and delight. However, whatever path you choose, it will be a real fascinating journey.

Glass gallery over the abyss

On the top of Tianmen Mountain, tourists are waiting for unusual walking paths: anyone will be breathtaking from the sight of the abyss in the transparent floor. The 60-meter-high glass path is safe, but tourists admit that the feeling of emptiness underfoot causes a glow of emotions. Nevertheless, visitors who are afraid of great heights prefer not to refuse to walk along the so-called "heavenly path".
