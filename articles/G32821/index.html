The dollar and the euro are showing steady growth. Why is the euro and the dollar growing in 2014?

The Russian ruble is losing ground, while the European and American currencies continue to grow. Some financial analysts have repeatedly warned of the fall of the ruble back in March 2014 - at the end of the Sochi Winter Olympics. Other experts claimed that the Olympic Games will increase and strengthen the Russian ruble.

To date, none of the forecasts have been confirmed. But foreign currency continues to rise in price. Therefore, the question of why the euro and the dollar are growing this year is becoming increasingly relevant.

The perpetual fall of the European currency contributes to an increase in the dollar. What triggers the growth of the dollar and the euro?

To understand why the euro and the dollar are growing in January 2014 years, should analyze the political and economic situation in the world in 2013.

  1. Last year, the US experienced strong economic growth.
  2. Obama agreed not to send troops into Syria.
  3. The funds invested by Russia in the Sochi 2014 Olympics have not paid off.
  4. At the end of 2013, the Central Bank began to revoke licenses in large Russian banks. This led to the closure of numerous deposits in Russian rubles.
  5. The CBR said it would not support the ruble in 2014.

If we evaluate these factors together, we can understand why the euro and the dollar are growing, and the Russian ruble is falling.





Financial forecasts

Analysts make conflicting forecasts about the position of the Russian ruble this year. Most of them agree that its rate will depend on oil and gas prices. Now the price of oil reaches its maximum, but by the end of the year it should fall. This will certainly affect the exchange rate of the dollar, euro and Russian ruble.

But NATO and Europe continue to threaten Russia with economic sanctions. Such actions explain why the euro and the dollar are growing. 2014 is not an easy year for Russia, both economically and politically. The country is increasingly threatened with exiting the G8. And if this happens, it will be difficult for the Russian ruble to maintain its position in relation to the European and American currencies.
