Largest cities in Germany: metropolitan Berlin

Berlin is the capital of a federal state. It attracts tourists with its many attractions and cultural sites, as well as the unimaginable contrasts between the architectural masterpieces of past centuries and modern buildings. One of the most popular monuments of the German capital among tourists was and remains the Reichstag - the country's parliament building.

Berlin is not just an ordinary European capital. It is a city of art and artists, in which at least 170 different museums work today. Munich - the most promising in Germany

The capital of proud Bavaria has already managed to get ahead of Leipzig, Frankfurt, and even Berlin in many ways. Specialists of the German bank Berenberg have already identified Munich as the most promising city in Germany.

Munich has successfully switched to the so-called knowledge economy. So, about 50% of the able-bodied population of the city is already working at enterprises in science-intensive spheres. And by the number of people with higher education, Munich does not at all know its peers in the whole country. Of course, such a number of educated and qualified personnel cannot but attract huge investments here.





Munich can also be called an international city. Every sixth person working here is a foreigner. To see a specialist from a distant country on the streets of Munich is a common thing.

Hamburg - the city of rivers and bridges

Hamburg is not only the largest city in Germany, but also one of the most beautiful and interesting! However, tourists for some reason very often bypass this treasury of architectural and historical monuments with a stunning urban atmosphere.

Few people know that Hamburg is the most spacious city in Europe. It is much larger than Paris and London. One local resident has about 30 square kilometers of area. In the same city is located the second largest European port, which in itself is a great attraction.





However, the most interesting in Hamburg is its rivers, numerous canals and bridges. The city is often compared with Amsterdam and even with Venice. But there are even more bridges here: 2.5 thousand! Hamburg has another distinguishing feature: the city has no buildings that exceed the 10-story line. So local authorities cherish the unique picturesqueness of urban landscapes.
