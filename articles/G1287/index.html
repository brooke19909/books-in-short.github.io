A little bit about the resort ...

Nessebar is considered to be a true pearl of Bulgaria, since incredible architectural monuments have been preserved in it. It is located on the peninsula, a little north of Burgas. Nessebar is one of the most ancient cities in Europe. It is believed that his story began three millennia ago. Every year archaeologists make new discoveries of ancient cultures. Nessebar is even included in the UNESCO list.

The old part of the city in general can be considered a miracle. She went under water a couple of centuries ago, and on land there was only a small part of it, on which all the temples are concentrated. Until now, the underwater part of the city is clearly visible from the boat in calm weather.

Local Attractions

Relaxing in Nessebar, you should definitely break away from a beach holiday and spend some time sightseeing and just interesting places. As, we have already mentioned, the city is divided into two parts: the new and the old. It is the ancient part that is of most interest to tourists. It is located on a small peninsula and is connected to the land by a small isthmus. The ancient city has a unique atmosphere created by medieval churches, ramparts, the ancient port, old houses with wooden stairs.

It is the churches that are considered the main attractions of the city. Each of them has its own unique architecture. The oldest is the Metropolitan Church of St. Stephen. According to legend, it was built on the ruins of the temple of Apollo. The church has perfectly preserved frescoes, an iconostasis, a department and the throne of the bishop.

One of the most beautiful in Bulgaria is the Church of Christ Pantocrator. It surprises with its rich forms and inlay. Currently, there is an art gallery. Also of interest is the church of St. Paraskeva.



An ancient windmill and a Turkish bath are no less interesting for visitors to the city. Ethnographic and archaeological museums operate in Nessebar. In them you can see coins, gold jewelry, black curly ceramics of Antiquity and the Middle Ages.

In Nessebar there are also natural attractions, for example, the White Cliffs and natural dunes (their height is 7-8 meters). The city has not only historical places, but also entertaining ones. Tourists will certainly enjoy the water parks and Luna Park, they can be reached by free buses. The city has an incredible number of restaurants and cafes where you can eat.

Historical monuments

In the city you can see the Amphitheater, which is characterized by unusual picturesqueness. Here you can admire the beautiful sea view from above. In the evenings, locals often hold concerts here, such as dance and song festivals. Another important attraction is the Turkish Fountain, built during the era of Turkish rule. Hamams have also survived. It cannot be said that these remnants of the past are something incredible, but these are the only buildings that survived after the centuries-old rule of the Turks. In the city you can see the Byzantine baths built in the sixth century. Already in those distant times, a real water supply system was laid across Nessebar, through which residents received water. And in the baths there was a large relaxation room with several pools. It is believed that this place was once visited by Emperor Constantine IV. The baths were so arranged that in different rooms it was possible to maintain different temperatures, which made it possible for people to enjoy contrasting baths. Such ancient buildings are amazing.



We have brought only a few interesting places that active tourists can visit. In fact, there are many more, so you can always find something new, unknown.
