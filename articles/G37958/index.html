The simplest openwork knitting pattern: diagram and description for beginners

Knitting has been popular for decades. Of course, immediately understanding the intricacies of loops is quite difficult, you can even lose interest in this needlework. To prevent this from happening, it is better to start with simple knitting techniques and only then move on to patterns of aran or lace.

The basics of knitting begin with the front and the wrong side. After that, you can try knitting openwork patterns according to simple patterns. By learning to understand symbols and read patterns, you can create amazingly beautiful knitwear.

Openwork Knitting Technique

Openwork consists of a combination of a basic front or wrong side and yarn, from which holes are obtained. Combining these two simple techniques, you can create a huge number of different patterns.

There are several rules, compliance with which will help to understand and knit both a complex and a very simple openwork pattern with knitting needles according to the pattern:

  • It is very important to monitor the constant number of loops in the work. The number of loops must not exceed the number of loops.
  • It is necessary to learn how to reduce loops with different slopes. To make the loop seem to be inclined to the right, you need to insert the spoke into two loops at once and stretch the working thread through them. The inclination to the left fits differently: remove one loop as the front one, knit the next one, then stretch the removed loop through it
  • Before you begin work on an openwork product, you need to tie a control sample. The density of knitting can be different, and such an experience will help to choose the right number of knitting needles.

Openwork mesh

"Grid" is considered the simplest openwork pattern for beginners. Using knitting needles to perform it is quite simple. The pattern consists of alternating knit and double crochets.

To knit a classic "mesh" you need to dial an odd number of loops. Do not forget about the hem, which will not be involved in the pattern, but will give the edges of the sample a smooth appearance.

After a set of loops, knit the first row on the wrong side. This is necessary to get a neat bottom edge of the sample.

Row 1. Remove the first edge loop without knitting. Then 2 loops together front, one yarn. Repeat until the end of the row, finish the front and do not forget about the last edge loop.

Row 2 and all further back rows. Knit according to the pattern or description of the "mesh" pattern. With knitting needles, most of all the yarn is made with purl loops.

Row 3. The sequence of loops is changing. Edge, 1 front, throw a loop, two loops together front. Repeat the sequence ‚Äúyarn, two loops together‚Äù. Finish with an edge loop.

Loop patterns

Among the popular lace patterns, there is one that is ideal for creating youth models of clothing. Knitting according to the scheme of a simple openwork pattern creates the effect of a bit sloppy, elongated loops. For this pattern, a technique is used when the dialed loops are not knitted from the wrong side, but are discarded from the knitting needle.

When performing this pattern, the yarn and the wrong loop are alternated in the front row. When knitting the wrong side, the fabric is done with the front stitch, and the dialed loops are reset. To maintain the shape of the product, between the rows of removed loops you need to knit a denser fabric.

An interesting effect can be obtained if you knit this openwork pattern from two separate tangles of mohair. Moreover, the wrong side of the surface to perform a double thread, and the single loop removed. Sometimes you can choose yarn in different colors, which will further emphasize the unusual model.
