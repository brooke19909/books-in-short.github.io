Jumeirah in Dubai: Description

Until 1960, this land was inhabited by fishermen, Arab merchants, divers for pearls. Then, Europeans who came to these places to work began to settle on the coast. Since 1995, the territory has been actively built up with world-class chic hotels and residences, turning Jumeirah into an elite seaside resort, which has no equal in the world today.

This area begins at the port of Rashid and stretches from its western moorings to the western part of the city, where the Dubai Marina skyscrapers are located. The southern border runs along Al Wasl Road and Al Wasl. The district is divided into three parts: eastern, central and western. Along the coast of the Persian Gulf, the Jumeirah Beach promenade, built up with luxurious skyscraper hotels and very expensive shops, is located.

Jumeirah Mosque

Almost every Arab city has a mosque. Dubai was no exception, where one of the most famous Islamic temples is located - the Jumeirah Mosque. This is a vivid example of modern cult architecture. The mosque was built in Fatimid medieval style. The magnificent dome and two minarets are its special elements. This building looks extremely impressive at sunset and in the evening, when the grace of its forms is emphasized by correctly executed artificial lighting.



What else to see in Jumeirah?

If you come to rest with children, be sure to visit the oldest zoo on the Arabian Peninsula. Rare birds and animals that are protected by law live here. Jumeirah Island

This project in 2001, when the construction of the unique island of Palm Jumeirah was just beginning, many were skeptical. But five years later, when the construction was completed, it was already impossible to deny the obvious: its beauty and originality stopped the talk of spiteful critics.

The place for construction was chosen very well - opposite business Dubai. The island is located at a distance of three hundred meters from land, connecting to it with a multi-lane bridge. Driving along it, tourists find themselves at the base of the "palm tree".



Jumeirah Beach in Dubai

Entrance to its territory is paid, but not for everyone: for adults a ticket costs five dirhams, and children attend the complex for free. The territory of the park is impressive: in impeccable cleanliness, birds fly, flowers and exotic trees grow. Where to live?

This issue should least of all concern departing to Dubai. In Jumeirah, hotels are presented for every taste and financial wealth. Particularly in demand, thanks to a well-developed infrastructure, are hotels near Beach Park. Since the beaches of Jumeirah are recognized as the best in Dubai, thousands of tourists from all over the country come here to relax, swim and sunbathe. We will introduce you to several hotels that are especially popular in the area.

Beach Hotel Apartment

The hotel is located next to the magnificent Jumeirah Mosque and a five-minute walk from the Persian Gulf. It features a rooftop pool with sea views. The apartments of the complex are equipped with modern functional furniture, a living room and a kitchen. The rooms are equipped with modern appliances: flat-screen TV, DVD player, refrigerator. Very close is the Exhibition Center and many popular restaurants and cafes. Guests have access to the gym with cardiovascular equipment and free weight training equipment. Moreover, anyone can swim in the outdoor pool at any time.



Regent Beach Resort

The hotel is located on the seafront in the Jumeirah district, in Dubai, on the city beach. It is a complex of two-story villas, each of which consists of ten rooms. In the vicinity of the hotel there are many shopping centers with restaurants, boutiques and sports shops from world famous brands.

Four seasons

A luxury hotel with its own beach, two outdoor and one indoor pool, a spa and a modern fitness center, is located near the beach in Jumeirah in Dubai (photos you can see in this article). Nearby is the Singing Fountain and the Burj Khalifa Skyscraper.

The resort's rooms are stylishly decorated in a contemporary style with original Arabic elements. Suites have a seating area with LCD TV and a dining area. The magnificent bathroom with a deep soaking tub, rain shower and mirror with built-in TV is decorated with Murano glass lamps.



For the youngest guests, the hotel hosts cheerful educational programs and holidays.

Dar Al Masyaf

The hotel is located fifteen kilometers from the center of Dubai, it takes half an hour to get to the airport from it. The hotel consists of twenty-nine villas. They are made in the form of Arab houses with patios. Nearby are Wild Wadi Waterparks and hotels such as Burj Al Arab and Jumeirah Beach. You can get to the hotel only by water.
