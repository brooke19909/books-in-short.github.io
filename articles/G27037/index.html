Simple braid

To learn how to knit harnesses, you need to understand the basic principle of knitting and crossing loops in a simple braid. Take the braid of three loops as an example. In order to get such a pattern, you need to knit a sample by typing 14 loops on the needles. Two of them are edge, and the rest are part of the pattern. We knit the first three on the wrong side, then 6 on the front and again 3 on the wrong side. Thus knit all the front rows. The inside of the loop is the way they look. After knitting 6 rows, the crossing of the loops begins to form the braids we need.





For further knitting harnesses with needles according to the scheme, you need a knitting or simple safety pin. The first three front loops must be removed, without tying, on a pin. Knitting begins immediately with 4-6 loops. After they found themselves on the right knitting needle, 1-3 loops and the remaining three wrong ones are knitted. Then 6 rows are knitted again as the loops look.

The number of rows in height may vary at the request of the knitter. The number of loops in the braid itself can also change: from 2 to 5, depending on how thick the thread is. If the yarn is thick, then the crossing will look rough, if there are a lot of loops involved. For a thin thread, you can select more of them.

Charting

Knitting harnesses with knitting needles can be quite complicated, so when drawing them up, you need to draw a diagram so as not to stray from the correct location of the loops. Consider the following sample. Any work begins with the initial set. For such a sample, we collect 19 loops, including edge loops. We knit 2 out., 6 persons., 1 out., 6 persons., 2 out. The first and last are edging. Knit 4 rows as the loops look. Then we make a crossover of 6 faces. 4 more rows are knit, as the loops look.





Then they begin work separately on each strip of braids. To do this, after every two rows, a crossing is already made of 3 loops, until 4 crossings are formed. Then the tourniquet is completed by crossing already a large part of the braid of 6 loops. Then everything repeats again.
