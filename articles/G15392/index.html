Valery Khlevinsky - a star of Soviet cinema

Khlevinsky Valery Mikhailovich was born in Nizhny Novgorod on November 14, 1943. To date, he deservedly has the title of People's Artist of Russia. In his piggy bank there are more than a dozen performances, feature films and series.



Childhood

Valery Khlevinsky was born into a family that was completely unrelated to art. Moreover, his parents were deaf and dumb. After some time, the Khlevinsky moved to Dzerzhinsk, where his father was appointed head of the Deaf Society. Mom Valeria took part in the amateur circle in society, he soon began to study there himself.

In 1958, the family moved again. This time to Vladimir, where Mikhail Khlevinsky also leads the deaf community. In a new place, Valery Khlevinsky goes to study at school number 1.
