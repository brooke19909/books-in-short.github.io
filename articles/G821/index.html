How to crochet an asterisk pattern and where to use it?

When knitting lush columns, you can achieve a variety of patterns. An example of what can be crocheted is an asterisk pattern. It turns out to be voluminous, especially if you use thick yarn or take several threads. This pattern is completely independent. It is permissible to choose it as the basis of the pattern of the product, which is supposed to be made magnificent.





How to crochet an asterisk

The chain should have such a number of loops that they fit a pattern with a rapport of 4 loops plus three more. From the first row immediately begins (hook) pattern "star". Moreover, it is formed of two rows, which are constantly repeated.

First row: 4 loops for lifting, in the third from the hook a lush column of three elongated loops, (*) 3 air. A lush column of 2 loops at the top of what was just connected (do not close it). Two more of the same magnificent columns - in the base of the first and in the 7th loop of the stacked chain. On the hook loop from the last two magnificent columns and unclosed, and through them you need to stretch the thread. Then everything repeats from the mark (*) to the end of the row. It ends with a double crochet in the last loop of the chain.

Second row: 7 air loops, a magnificent column of two loops in the fourth lifting loop (do not close). Two more magnificent columns of three loops: the first to the top of the last column of the previous row, the second to the middle of the asterisk. Pull the thread through all the loops. Make three air loops and repeat all of the lush columns listed. Repeat the pattern to the end.





Hat with a star pattern

The beginning of the product is to knit an elastic band that will hold it securely on your head. To do this, you need to dial 4 air loops. Double crochet, insert the hook into the left-most part of the chain and pull the thread. This loop will be the one that will provide the basis for the next column. Then again, pick up the thread and tie the column with a crochet through those loops that are on the hook. Then again, make (**) yarn, stretch the thread through the loop that was made after the column and pick up the thread again, tying the column to the crochet. Repeat the pattern from the mark (**) until you get the length of the strip along the volume of the head. Close the circle.

Further, the cap itself is crocheted. The star pattern should be made in every third column of the rubber band. The number of elongated loops can be varied at the request of the needlewoman. If the thread is very thin, then they can be made 4 or 5. For thick yarn, a fluffy column is permissible to form from two elongated loops. Now you need to knit a pattern (crochet) "star" in a circle until then, until you need to make a decrease. Moreover, the rows are lifted by the magnificent columns themselves without air loops.

Calculate the number of rows that will be required for this. Distribute the number of stars between them so that the last six remain. Reducing stars is carried out in the same way. The first elongated loop of a magnificent column should be tied to its top, and the other two to the next. Then the knitting sprocket continues as before.

In the last row, you need to pair in pairs the two tops of the stars with lush columns. Six formed peaks then pull together the cut end of the yarn. The bottom of the cap is recommended to be tied with a beautiful pattern.

Pattern of stars to decorate the edge of the product

The asterisk can be not only voluminous, but also airy. It turns out to be openwork. It can be easily used for a summer product or tied with the top of a bodice.

To crochet an asterisk pattern, you need to dial a ring of 10 air loops. On it, perform 24 single crochet (this is the 1st row). The second: 6 airs, double crochet in the 3rd previous row, 3 airs, double crochet in the 6th and so on until the end of the circle, a connecting column in the third, a lifting loop.

Third row: air, 2 double crochet and 1 single crochet in the first arch. Single crochet, 2 single crochet and one single crochet in the next arch. Repeat until the end of the circle. Connecting column.

Such blanks need to be made the right amount and sewn to the product. Then crochet the star pattern. In the upper ray, knit two single crochet, separated by an air loop. Between the rays make arches of 4 aerials, and from star to star make an arch of 5 aerials. Tie everything in columns with a single crochet.
