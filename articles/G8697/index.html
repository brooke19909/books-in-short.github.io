Arbat Square in the history of Moscow today

Arbat Square appeared in the last decades of the 18th century, when the Arbat Gate was dismantled in the White City. She very little resembled the modern one. The area was formed at the place where the roads intersected to Smolensk and Novgorod.

Why is it called so

The whole district has been built up since the XV century. It was named Arbat (translated from Arabic - "suburb"). It grew, and in 1808 the architect Rossi built a wooden theater of unprecedented beauty. Performances in it were in both Russian and French. The square was paved, but the fire of 1812 destroyed everything.

Modern landscaping

The square is located in the very center of Moscow, and the view was unpresentable. Arbatskaya metro station had to squeeze along a narrow passage, because for about 20 years there was an unauthorized large two-story building nearby, which blocked the passage of passengers to the very busy station of the Arbatsko-Pokrovskaya line. It was demolished in February 2016. Now they made a pedestrian zone in the square. The improvement of Arbat Square affected not only her, but also the streets adjacent to it: Znamenka, Krestovozdvizhensky and Starovagankovsky lanes. In the pedestrian space of almost twenty-one thousand square meters. m was laid out large granite tiles. Its service life will be more than one dozen years. Put the lights and benches. One hundred and fifty-four main lamps, as well as forty-four additional floor lamps, began to illuminate the area. At the same time, the air wires are hidden underground.

Location of Arbat Square in Moscow

Where Gogolevsky Boulevard, Znamenka Street and Arbat Gate Square intersect, the modern Arbat Square is located. The photograph shows part of it.

One can see the Khudozhestvenny cinema and the detail of the entrance to the Arbat-Filevskaya metro line (the red building at the back). It has a star shape with five ends.

When did the transformation begin

From May to August 2016, work was carried out to change the appearance of the territory (Arbat Square). Moscow needed a modern, in line with the spirit of the time, updated area according to the developed program "My Street". In accordance with it, not only this square was affected, but also four adjacent streets. They expanded the sidewalks, changed the lanes of cars and replaced 33.5 thousand square meters. m of asphalt pavement, arranged convenient parking so that you could walk around the square further. Green spaces are not forgotten.

Thirty-one trees and twenty-five shrubs have already been planted. The area was decorated with benches, flowerbeds, lawns. They created a cozy place for walks of Muscovites and guests of the capital. The adjacent streets, along with Arbat Square, have acquired a modern look worthy of the capital of a huge state.
