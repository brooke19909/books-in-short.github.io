Vietnam, Phan Thiet: attractions, recreation, reviews

Not so long ago, Russian tourists discovered one of the resorts in Vietnam - Phan Thiet. Loved by many, the place is located in the center of the country, and many liked it primarily because of its mild and comfortable climate. Suffice it to say that the average air temperature here is 27 degrees Celsius during the year. Hospitable Vietnamese enjoy treating tourists with original seafood dishes. For visitors - a magnificent sandy beach with convenient access to the sea.





Geographical position

Phan Thiet is located in southern Vietnam, in Binhuang province, on the picturesque coast of the warm South China Sea. The resort area is two hundred square kilometers. The nearest city is Ho Chi Minh City (distance - 240 kilometers).

Infrastructure

Today, many of our compatriots go on vacation to Vietnam. Phan Thiet (Mui Ne - a popular resort) attracts tourists from all over the world. The best hotels are concentrated here, featuring a modern and original design, excellent technical equipment and an excellent level of European service. The beach stretches for 22 kilometers, being the longest in Vietnam. Every year, the infrastructure of the recreation area develops and improves in this country.



Currently, Mui Ne beach is a wide strip, conditionally divided into equal sections between all hotels. This separation is necessary for hotel administrations responsible for the order in the allotted territories. For vacationers, the beach is common, so everyone takes the place he likes. The territory is well equipped, umbrellas and sun loungers are always abundant.

If you cross the road leading from the beach, you will fall into the area of restaurants, cafes, tattoo and massage parlors, numerous shops and tents full of various goods.

If you decide to come to Vietnam, Phan Thiet is one of the best resorts. Here you can have a great time, relax your soul and body, relax and gain strength for further fruitful work. Vietnam Attractions: Phan Thiet

Rest in this hospitable country will be unforgettable for you. For our compatriots, everything here seems original and exotic. Phan Thiet (Vietnam), a photo of which you can see not only in our article, but also in the brochures of travel agencies, is rich in its history. She left her mark on the architecture of these places. In the mid-nineteenth century, there was a massive resettlement of Catholics, as evidenced by numerous architectural monuments. Near the Catholic churches of extraordinary beauty, pagodas and Buddha statues rise here (one is located near the town of Phan Thiet).

Vacation in Vietnam is not only swimming in the sea. This is a unique opportunity to get acquainted with the cultural heritage of this wonderful country. A fascinating excursion to the largest Buddha statue is something for which many tourists make a trip to Vietnam. Phan Thiet, or rather its inhabitants, are also proud of the Chama towers.

And lovers of beautiful nature will certainly be interested in pink and white dunes. However, nothing can compare with the impression that the Red Canyon makes on all visitors. Its walls are a mixture of red clay and sandstone, and this local landmark was formed due to washing out of the soil. This process continues to this day, and after a while the canyon will disappear, so tourists who want to enjoy this natural wonder should hurry up with a trip to Vietnam.



Another attraction of these places is the Lost, or Lost Sea. This lake is located in the depths of the forest on the border of the provinces of Dongnai and Binh Thuan. Local residents called it that because of the size that initially made people think that they really had a sea in front of them, which took about 1000 hectares from the forest. In the rainy season, it tripled. To the east of the pond rises Mount Katong.

Poshanu Towers

This is the legacy of the Champa people. Once they were part of an ancient temple complex. Fairies, daughters of the goddess Po Nagar, are worshiped here. Now three towers are most preserved. Their age is estimated at thirteen centuries. Champa is distinguished by a special architectural style - fake doors, rounded columns resembling Khmer temple buildings. Each year, in the first lunar month, the towers hold the Roch Mbang and Rigi Nuta festivals.

Ke Ga Lighthouse

It is located four kilometers from Phan Thiet. This is one of the most famous places in the small province of Binh Thuan. In 1897, the lighthouse was built by an architect from France, Senavat. The height of the structure is 64 meters. It was recognized as the most beautiful lighthouse in Southeast Asia. An octagonal tower is crowned with a lantern capable of illuminating twenty-two nautical miles.





Mui Ne fishing village

So called their village local fishermen. In the storm season, they found refuge here. Translated "mui" means an island or a cape, "not" - a shelter. Transparent blue waters, bright sun, snow-white sand created a magnificent landscape that is pleasing to the eyes of guests from abroad. In addition to fascinating excursions, tourists can observe the hard daily work of fishermen and take part in fishing.

Town Fantie

The picturesque area near the calm Phan Thiet river was inhabited many centuries ago, but until the twentieth century it remained a quiet unknown fishing town, or rather, even a village. In antiquity, he was part of the kingdom of Champs. Despite its rather modest size, the settlement played a significant role in the history of the country and presented several outstanding figures of history and culture to the Vietnamese people. Economics, transport

Perhaps the most significant drawback of this resort is the lack of an international airport. But this is not so scary. Arriving in Vietnam, you will get to Ho Chi Minh City, from which it is very easy to get to Phan Thiet. It offers trains, buses, taxis (the cost of the latter, by the way, is quite affordable). In the city, tourists prefer to travel by taxi or rented car, which will cost eight dollars a day. A similar service is provided by all hotels in the city.
