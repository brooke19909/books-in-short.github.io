Theater Square in Moscow: history, legends

Theater Square in Moscow was built in the early twenties of the XIX century. It is located in the very center of the city, northeast of another historical attraction - Revolution Square. What is here? Bolshoi and Maly theaters are located on Theater Square in Moscow. This place can be called the cultural center of the capital, and the whole country.





Background

In the fifteenth century, not far from Theater Square in Moscow, shopping malls were located. Around this time, Kitai Gorod was built. In the sixteenth century, the bastions of the famous wall were significantly expanded - the inhabitants of the city were afraid of the invasion of the enemy. Fires in Moscow occurred regularly. One of them, which happened in 1736, destroyed everything in the district.

For the first time they started talking about the construction of the square in 1775. It was decided to equip the area. More precisely, to arrange several squares in the vicinity of the Kremlin and Kitai Gorod. As often happens, the construction was not started immediately, but after many years.

The emergence of Theater Square in Moscow

In 1812, another fire broke out in the capital, the causes of which are known even to schoolchildren thanks to the imperishable work of Lermontov. The capital was not given to the Frenchman, however, he had to pay for patriotism and perseverance with architectural historical monuments. Later, a special commission was convened to restore the city. Osip Bove worked on the project. The layout of the square under the guidance of an experienced and well-known by then architect began in 1816.

Fountain

Once, the Mytishchi water supply pool was located here. Later, according to the project of I.P. Vitali, a fountain was built in its place. At Theater Square, he was perhaps the main attraction, not counting, of course, the world-famous theaters. At the beginning of the 19th century, fountains were more often called "water cannons." In Moscow there were few of them then. And more precisely, the fountain on Theater Square in Moscow became the first public. He depicts four cupids, symbolizing such directions in theatrical and literary art as music, poetry, comedy and tragedy.





After the revolution

In Soviet times, almost all the streets in Moscow and other cities were renamed. This fate has not passed and Theater Square. For more than 70 years, it was called Sverdlov Square. Not only the name has changed, but the very appearance of these places. So, for some time a monument to the political figure Jacob Sverdlov was raised on the square, which had no relation either to the theater or to the history of Moscow. In 1991, the monument was removed.

The Bolshoi and Maly theaters, the Metropol Hotel, the Central Department Store - all these publications play an important role in the appearance of the modern Theater Square in Moscow. How to get here? Of course, on the subway. At the same time, you can use not only the Zamoskvoretskaya line, on which the Teatralnaya station is located. You can also exit the "Okhotny Ryad" or "Revolution Square". Sokolnicheskaya, Zamoskvoretskaya and Arbatsko-Pokrovskaya lines intersect exactly at the place where Theater Square is located.

Legends

The Bolshoi Theater was opened in March 1776. During its existence, he saw a lot, keeps interesting secrets and secrets. A lot of legends for more than two centuries gave rise to the square and the theater located on it.

Five centuries ago there was an impenetrable swamp. According to one of the legends, an unsuccessful doctor once got into it and Muscovites used to treat him not so much as sending them to the other world. For which he paid.





About fires as the main misfortune of not only wooden, but already stone Moscow, as mentioned above. The Bolshoi Theater was built after one of them, one might say, on the ashes. It was called Petrovsky. He stood for only a quarter of a century, then burned down. The worst fire in the history of Theater Square occurred in 1805, on the day when the premiere of the opera Mermaid took place on the stage of the Bolshoi Theater.
