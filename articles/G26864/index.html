Some tips on how to get to Yalta from Moscow

Yalta, without a doubt, is the most popular and favorite resort. In this beautiful sunny city by the sea, millions of people in Russia dream of relaxing. Every season thousands of people strive to get into it, who want to enjoy all the charms of the southern sea city. But how to get to Yalta from Moscow, because planes do not fly here, trains do not go, is it quite problematic to get from Moscow by car?





From Moscow to Yalta by plane

Nevertheless, you can fly here by plane, by train and car. How to do this, we will try to tell you. And so, how to get from Moscow to Yalta by plane? Having joined our country, Crimea faced a big problem - transport links with Russia. The fact is that the path through the mainland through Ukraine is impossible due to recent events. There remains the Kerch ferry crossing, which gives travelers a lot of trouble, so many people prefer air service.

Moscow is located at a distance of 1,500 kilometers from Yalta, not many people are happy with the transfers, so the plane is the most convenient form of transport. There are three airports on the peninsula: in Simferopol, Kerch and Sevastopol. Most passenger traffic in Crimea takes over the airport of Simferopol. The peninsula has a well-developed road junction; from the capital of the autonomous republic, you can easily reach every region of the Crimea.

How to get to Yalta from Moscow? Firstly, you need to take a plane ticket. It is advisable to do this in advance, because at the height of the season it will be difficult to buy it. Planes fly to Simferopol daily, and flights are made by various airlines. If you worry in advance, then round-trip tickets will cost around 8.5 thousand rubles. The flight duration is 2.5-3 hours.





How to get from the airport to Yalta by taxi?

Arriving at Simferopol airport, a person faces the question: "What is the best way to get to Yalta?" Arrived from Moscow without any problems, what to do next? The distance from Simferopol to Yalta is 50 kilometers. You can use a taxi. The cost of this method can reach up to 6000 rubles.

If you came with a company, then the price of a comfortable moving will not be burdensome for you, and if you bargain, you can also get there cheaper. The airport building has a control room where it is possible to order a taxi of any class. You can use the Internet, for example, the Yandex.Taxi program. The service will cost several times cheaper. The fee for 1 km is about 30 rubles.





Trolleybus to Yalta

There are two more ways: the famous trolley bus and a comfortable bus. This, of course, will take more time on the road, but you will have the opportunity to admire the beautiful scenery outside the window. There is a trolley bus stop near the airport. You need No. 55, it goes to Yalta. They leave it every hour. Travel time - 2-2.5 hours. This, of course, is tiring, but very cheap. Only 90 rubles. You can also get to the train station, where trolleybuses also go to Yalta. The frequency of movement is 15 minutes. The train station can be reached by trolley bus number 9, bus or minibus number 115 and 98.





By bus faster

Near the railway station is the bus station "Resort", from which buses run to Yalta. Sending - every 10-15 minutes. They can get to Yalta for an hour faster.

In Simferopol there is no such problem as getting to Yalta. From Moscow and another Russian city, the most important thing is to get to Simferopol. Here, the entire infrastructure works for vacationers. There are no problems with vehicles on the peninsula, so do not be afraid.





Railway

The most popular, affordable, convenient and economical is the railway. It is not in Yalta, but we can arrive at the station of Simferopol, and from it, as we now know, it is not difficult to get to Yalta. The route will go as follows: from Moscow, take a train to the port of Kavkaz, then take a ferry to Kerch, and from there take a train to Simferopol. A bit troublesome. But this is so far the only way to get to the Crimea by rail. In 2018, with the opening of the bridge that connects the Kerch and Taman Peninsulas, this route will be non-stop, but today it is the only railway route. At the Moscow railway ticket office, take a ticket Moscow-Simferopol - this is a single ticket, this includes the cost of services of the railway and sea ferry.

There is another option, how to get to Yalta from Moscow. By rail you get to Krasnodar, Krymsk, Anapa, Novorossiysk, and from there by regular bus to any region of the Crimea. Here you can also use a single ticket, which includes road, rail and sea transport.





To Yalta by own car

You can get from Moscow to Yalta by car, but when setting off in this way, you need to be prepared for hours of traffic jams at the ferry crossing. Although the authorities are trying to solve this issue, but, due to the huge flow of tourists, it is unlikely to be completely eliminated.

You need to leave Moscow along the M-4 highway in the direction of Rostov-on-Don. Further to the village "Kushchevskaya", from where your path will go to the port of Caucasus. Here the ferry service will take you to the city of Kerch, from there - to Simferopol and further to Yalta. The path is not short and difficult, but for lovers of car travel it will not be a big hassle. This way to get to the Crimea is the most expensive.

If you cannot live without a car, you can fly by plane or take a train to Simferopol and take advantage of a car rental that allows you to travel on the peninsula on your own.

From Moscow to Yalta by bus

From Moscow to Yalta, you can travel on a modern bus departing from the bus station. Flights on even days depart at night, and on odd days in the morning. In addition, during the summer, additional flights are organized. The cost of such a trip is 2600 rubles. At the peak of the holiday season, the fare may be higher.
