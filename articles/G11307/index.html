How to find indices in Ukraine





Despite the fact that in recent years we have written fewer and fewer letters and sent parcels mainly using courier services, postal services have not lost their relevance. That is why many are faced with the problem of how to find indexes. This is fairly easy to do, and soon we will find out exactly how this can be done. We will also talk about what an index is and what is the history of its occurrence. In this article, we will cover the index system valid for Ukraine.

What is an index?

Let's start with the very definition of the index, in order to better understand what it is and why it is needed. Later we will analyze the meaning of each digit in it, and also discuss the history of its occurrence. Then we will talk about how to find out the indices for Ukraine.

A zip code is a sequence of different letters and numbers that are added to a postal address and serve to facilitate the sorting of correspondence. In Ukrainian indices, only numbers are used, while, unlike Russia, each set consists of five numbers.

The use of postal codes can significantly speed up the sorting of correspondence, and as a result, the speed of its delivery. That is why it is important to always enter this set of numbers in the appropriate field on envelopes and address lists of parcels.

History of occurrence





After the collapse of the USSR, letters that indicated that the post office belonged to a particular republic disappeared from the indices of most countries.

Before we talk about how to find out indexes, let's say a few words about their occurrence. It is believed that in our country, indices arose in 1932. It was then that they began to use the digital addresses of a particular department, which were set using letters and numbers. Then, in Kharkov, a directory was created containing all postal codes of Ukraine. With the beginning of World War II, the index system was abolished. They reintroduced it in 1971.

The value of the digits in the index

As already mentioned, zip codes always consist of numbers. And before talking about how to find out indices using an address, we‚Äôll look at what each of the numbers or a pair of numbers in it means.

Depending on the country, their number may fluctuate - this is due to differences in the size of the territory, the number of regions and cities. If in Russia the postal code consists of six digits, then in Ukraine only five. Naturally, each of the numbers has its own meaning. Let's talk about what each digit or pair of numbers in Ukrainian indices means.





The zip code is divided into three parts.

So, the first pair of numbers denotes a region. Moreover, to designate one region, not one number is used, but several - depending on its territory. So, for example, indices beginning with the numbers 46 - 49 indicate the Ternopil region.

Further, the third digit in the index indicates the district or province in which the post office is located. And finally, the last pair of numbers indicates the post office. Now we can move on to the question of how to find out the index. Mail for sending is sorted using the index, therefore, this question needs to be considered as detailed as possible.
