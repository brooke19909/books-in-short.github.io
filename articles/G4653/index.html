Badayevsky warehouses in St. Petersburg: history and modernity

Badayevsky warehouses in St. Petersburg are familiar to many people from the history of the Great Patriotic War. What comes to mind at their mention? The fire that destroyed most of the food supplies in besieged Leningrad. Unfortunately, their popularity is too sad.

The Great Patriotic War

The events that glorified the Badayevsky warehouses throughout the world were too sad and severe. Let's recall the chronology of that terrible time. When the Great Patriotic War began, in Badayevsky warehouses most of the food supplies of St. Petersburg were stored. Why did this happen? Before the start of World War II in Leningrad there was a sufficient amount of products. They were stored in different areas of the city.





During the war, the leadership of Leningrad decided to place all the products in one place. The choice stopped at the Badayevsky warehouses. This decision was due to the fact that the products were easier to protect in one place and guarantee their safety. In September 1941, after German air raids, the warehouses completely burned down. The city was virtually left without food supplies, and a terrible famine began. Only after the end of World War II did the restoration of the Badayevsky warehouses begin. Further you will find out why they are so called.

Badayevsky warehouses in St. Petersburg

It is interesting to know when this famous place was built and why it bears such a name. Badayevsky warehouses were built in 1914. They did not belong to their owner, merchant Rasteryaev, for very short time, only a few years. In 1917, the building became the property of workers and peasants. For several years, the Badayevsky warehouses were simply called storage facilities. Only after 1936 did they receive the name that they still have today.





We think that many readers will be interested to know why the warehouses began to be called Badayevsky? Let's make a short excursion into history. So, in 1937, Aleksey Yegorovich Badayev, a Bolshevik, a prominent party leader in the early 20th century, was appointed People's Commissar of the food industry of the RSFSR. For many years he dealt with the food supply of the city, and therefore it was decided to name the warehouses in which the products were stored in his honor.
