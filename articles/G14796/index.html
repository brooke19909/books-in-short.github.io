Tushinsky Park is a natural park. Tushinsky Park - events

The capital of Russia is a huge settlement, a metropolis, in which about 20 million people live together with visitors. In her stone jungle, the only places where you can relax and breathe relatively clean air are parks and squares. One of these oases is the Tushinsky Park, an autonomous state cultural institution.

Location of the park, its components

This cultural object is located on the territory of the Northern Tushino district, between the Khimki reservoir and Svobody Street, in the north-west of the capital. It is a half ring, consisting of various natural elements that cover the Tushino residential area from the east, north and west. These include: Skhodny valley between MKAD and Factory passage, Tushinskaya Chasha, Bratovka valley with Brattsevsky garden, Brattsevo estate, the coast of Butakovsky bay, Aleshkinsky forest and Zakharkovsky garden. All these territories differ in the nature of vegetation and are separated by streets.





Some historical data

Tushinsky Park has its own history, which we set out briefly, in telegraph style. Even in the Bronze Age, ancient people chose the shores of Khimki. Then the Ugro-Finnish and Baltic tribes migrated here, after a while - the Slavs-Vyatichi. The forests were so thick that the settlement was impossible to find, and the tribes lived in isolation, traded and maintained contact with the outside world thanks to the river. Times have changed, forests have thinned, the soil has spoiled. In the XIV century, the villages of Zakharkino and Aleshkino appeared here, their inhabitants have already sawed and smelted the forest, earned money by carriage, and were engaged in various crafts. In 1812, both villages suffered, rebuilding them hard. During the revolution, the monastery and temple were destroyed. In those years, they began to build a reservoir and a Moscow-Volga canal, and a barrack village was set up nearby. And in 1937 the villages were buried under water. Butakovsky Bay appeared on the site of the Grachevka River. After World War II, restoration work was again carried out . Planted trees, built moorings. In the 60s, a park was set up for Muscovites to relax. Tushino entered the line of Moscow, local residents settled on high-rises. Muscovites liked to relax here, and in 1992 the territory received the status of a Park of Culture and Rest, and in 1998 the Tushinsky Natural Park was formed.





What is this resting place now?

Currently, various events are held here and holidays are celebrated. This is a place of attractions, dances, shady alleys and unusual museums. In the southern part of the park, for example, there is a museum of the Navy, and nearby, on the fortified shore, there is an ekranoplan, a boat and a submarine, which also belong to the museum. Behind it is the Zakharkovo orchard, from where there is a descent to the reservoir. Recently, visitors to these places can meet fabulous characters carved from wood. What else is famous for the Tushinsky park? If you walk along the alley, you can get to a small square with a stage installed on it, a small cafe and a descent to the water. At the bottom there is a fountain in the form of a fairy-tale character, known as the Wonder Yudo Fish-Whale. Different sites alternate with ravines. A pond hid in the depths of all this diversity.





More about the Navy Museum

This historical museum complex is primarily the B-396 - a large diesel submarine that was built in Nizhny Novgorod. It is called "Novosibirsk Komsomolets", served from 1980 to 2000 in the Red Banner Northern Fleet, in the Atlantic and Arctic Oceans. In 2003, in the city of Severodvinsk, at the Sevmash enterprise, it was converted into a museum. The boat could sink 300 meters, its length is 90 meters, has one anchor. Sports activities in the park

Children have the opportunity to spend their energy on children's playgrounds, adults on tennis and volleyball courts, in a sports complex, on a mini-football field. Mountain bike lovers independently equipped the track with jumps, potholes and slides. It is located in the northern ravine. The virtuoso pirouettes of cyclists sometimes gather a lot of fans and spectators. Fans of hiking can enjoy walking along the very picturesque coast of Butakovsky Bay, and in Alesha Gardens they can try picking raspberries. During breaks, there are many opportunities to relax on a bench in a shady alley or park, reading books or contemplating the surrounding beauty. And there is something to see, in vain "Tushinsky" - a natural park. Glades, groves, grasses of lawns, ornamental trees and shrubs ... Free services and entertainment, events

Tushinsky Park offers a range of entertainment and services for which you do not have to pay. Every day music plays here. Ideal conditions: in the shade of trees and arbors, you can dance and just listen to the melody. For believers there is a chapel in which prayers are held on holidays, and all other days there is the opportunity to just sit on a bench. Since 1983, the park has a section of winter swimming and hardening "Vivacity". Children from low-income families and children with disabilities use all attractions for free. On weekends, it is especially recommended to visit the Tushino Park. Events held here, you will surely enjoy. And after heavy weekdays there will be an additional discharge. In addition, in 2013, the central square was arranged on the territory of the park: a large stage was installed on it, which allows for various concerts. And here are held - in honor of public holidays - interactive programs and competitions, festivals. On such days there is a particularly high turnout of visitors.
