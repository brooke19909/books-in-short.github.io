Display





ZTE Grand S2 has a high-quality display, whose diagonal is 5.5 inches. The display device contains IPS technology, which reduces eye strain, making reading from the screen more comfortable and safe for the user. The display resolution is 1920 by 1080 pixels. Density - 401 pixels per 1 inch. By the way, on the Grand S2 screens are installed immediately from three different manufacturers. In different batches of devices, the screen manufacturer is different.

For example, previously the company acquired displays manufactured by LG. They read well in sunlight, and there were several times fewer dead pixels. But the products of Tianma and Yassi are much worse. There are already broken pixels and incorrect display of content in the sun.

The user can see the display from which manufacturer is installed in his device. However, this will require root privileges. ZTE Grand S2 supports the glove function. Everything works correctly, but with one caveat: gloves should not be too thick, otherwise thermal sensors will not work. The rest of the display boasts decent viewing angles, when turning the contrast of the picture does not drop particularly. Yes, and this is typical for a display that uses OGS technology.

In the sun, the screen behaves rather weakly. Although the screen brightness is rather big, it is very, very difficult to get a high-quality picture of the display in the sun. If at all feasible. Operating platform and hardware

The core of the phone is the Snapdragon 801 processor. It has four cores. Each of them has a clock frequency of 2.3 GHz. Along with this, the graphics accelerator of the Adreno family, model 330, went into the hardware platform of the device. Its operating frequency is 578 MHz. Supported technology OpenCL 1.2, as well as OpenGL ES 3.0. RAM installed in the amount of 2 GB. This, in principle, is enough for the lion's share of applications. You can free up volume by removing Chinese programs that we absolutely do not need. After that, up to 800 MB can be freed. It should be noted that the interface works really very smoothly. No friezes, no lags.

Work with mobile networks

The device supports working with a 4G mobile network. What actually turns out? In fact, it turns out that 3G works stably everywhere. That is, you can be even in Europe, even in Russia - you will not notice any problems with 3G. This is very important, since many Chinese-made smartphones, without exaggeration, suffer from incompatibility with this standard. 4G works stably both in Russia and in Europe, but not with all operators. There is not much coverage for a decent level for this standard, but Beeline, MTS and Megafon are making some progress in this area.

The device works well with wireless networks through the Wi-Fi module. The signal passes just fine, connection breaks are not observed. Supported OTG function. You can always connect an external USB drive to the device. Having previously formatted it in the FAT32 file system, of course. Otherwise, the external media will not be read by the phone.

Navigation

GPS works no worse. Signals of different types of satellites are steadily perceived. The American GPS system, the Russian GLONASS, and the Chinese Beidou Navigation also work. But with the latter, the device works a little worse than with the first two. The explanation is very simple: Chinese satellites, firstly, are not enough. Secondly, so far most of them cultivate their territory. In Russia, using the phone you can see the work of only 4 Chinese satellites.

But what can not be said and the words of bad, so it is about the speed of the search for satellites. You can find them in just a couple of minutes, or even in one minute. If you compare the operation of the device with the same Mediatek smartphone, then the advantage of the first is immediately evident. The thing is that Mediatek is looking for satellites for 10 minutes, no less. However, he may not find anything at all.
