Electronic transformer: wiring diagram

An electronic transformer is an electromagnetic type device. It consists of an inductive winding, as well as a magnetic circuit. An electronic transformer is used to convert AC. There are devices in various electrical appliances.

Also with their help they collect power supplies. To connect the device using various elements. In this case, the threshold voltage, frequency and current conductivity parameters are taken into account. In order to understand everything, you should consider specific schemes.





Connection diagram via capacitor resistor

Through the capacitor resistor, any electronic transformer can be connected. The connection scheme includes a modulator as well as a transceiver. The current conductivity of the specified element must be at least 50 microns. In this case, the output voltage depends on the number of resistors. In some cases, expansion transceivers are used. If we consider the model for the power supply, then the amplifier is used terminal type. Filters are needed to stabilize the conversion process. Triggers are used phase type.





Connection via two regulators

Only two low-frequency electronic transformers can be connected through two regulators. The connection diagram consists of open type tetrodes. In this case, the ultimate conductivity of the element is 55 microns. Directly regulators are installed behind the relay. Amplifiers are found both operational and toroidal type.

For normal operation of the expander, two connectors are used. The trigger capacity must be at least 2 pF. It is also important to pay attention to the output voltage on the winding. On average, it is no more than 40 V. However, with a high level of negative resistance, this parameter can increase sharply. If we consider the circuit for the power supply, then the thyristor is selected dipole type. In this case, the current reducibility parameter of the element is not more than 45 microns. The maximum input voltage can be 20 V. Contactors are used to connect capacitors.





Using wired stabilizers

Through wired stabilizers, you can connect a high-frequency electronic transformer. The connection scheme involves the use of triggers with a secondary winding. The tetrodes in this case are installed behind the relay. Filters are used to increase the negative resistance. In total, two contactors are required for a 30 W power supply. Resistors are used toroidal type. The output voltage parameter of the elements does not exceed 45 V.

Connection to the diode bridge

A low-frequency transformer can be connected to the diode bridge through one regulator. For this, the tetrode is used with two filters. The current conductivity of the element must be at least 55 microns. All this will significantly increase the threshold resistance. The modulator for the circuit is selected pulse type. If we consider the converter with an amplifier, then the relay must be used only with insulators. In this case, the resistance at the transformer will be about 22 m. The output voltage on the winding will fluctuate around 30 V.

Halogen lamp connection

Only a low-frequency electronic transformer may be connected to halogen lamps. The wiring diagram consists of dipole type resistors. Capacitors are used with a primary winding. Filters are used to stabilize the induction process. In total, the circuit provides two amplifiers. The relay in this case is installed behind the capacitors.

The expander may only be used in an open type. The current conductivity of the element is 55 microns. Thus, the resistance should not exceed 12 ohms. The output voltage parameter depends on the resistors. If we consider models with not a large capacity, then the specified parameter is about 13 V.
