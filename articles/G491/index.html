Alexander Mikhalkov: the history of the noble family

In the large Mikhalkov family, everyone is more or less famous. Not a day goes by in the press or on television this surname does not sound. The Mikhalkovs have their own opinions on all spheres of life - from world problems to cooking.





Great-grandfathers

The modern Mikhalkovs are descendants of the Golitsyn princes. Princess Elizaveta Nikolaevna Golitsyna married Vladimir Mikhalkov, and the family inherits 720 acres of land from the Golitsyns with the village of Nazaryevo. The estate was a dowry from the princes Golitsyn.

Vladimir Sergeyevich Mikhalkov, the great-grandfather of the poet, came from a rather noble family of Mikhalkovs. Their family estate was partially preserved in Rybinsk. The new family began the construction of their own estate. It was a two-story building, decorated in the Gothic style.





This estate became their summer residence, and the family spent most of the time in Moscow. Grandfathers and parents

Alexander Mikhalkov, landowner of the Moscow, Kostroma and Yaroslavl provinces, captain and honorary magistrate, was the grandfather of the poet. Grandmother Anna Miller, his wife, was also of noble origin.

Alexander Mikhalkov and his wife Anna also lived in Nazarievo, where their son Vladimir, the father of a famous writer, was born.

The family of Vladimir and Olga had three sons - Sergey, Mikhail and Alexander (Mikhalkov). The writer‚Äôs childhood passed in the nest of his ancestors near Moscow. But these were difficult revolutionary times.

After the revolution, the estate was nationalized, and the family moved from Moscow to Pyatigorsk. Parenting in the family was Spartan. The governess of German descent was engaged in the education of boys.

Rybinsk

Data on the Mikhalkov family and its noble origin were stored in the family archives of the estate in Rybinsk. After the collapse of the Soviet Union, archives were handed over to relatives and the truth about the life of the Mikhalkovs ancestors became available to relatives.





About the estate in Rybinsk it is known that before the advent of Soviet power many works of art were stored there - paintings and ancient household items passed into the hands of the state and were forever lost to a famous family.

Now the park in Rybinsk has the status of a monument of landscape gardening art. Shady alleys and centuries-old trees preserve the memory of the glorious noble family of Mikhalkov.
