Vnukovo settlement: the unity of history and entertaining modernity

The rural Vnukovo settlement is located in the north-west of the Moscow region. The first mention of him as a settlement appeared in 1852. The small village of Vnukovo, owned by Prince Sergei Ivanovich Gagarin, consisted of 232 peasant souls of both sexes. By 1913, there were 17 courtyards and the apartment of a police officer.

Since November 1925, the village of Vnukovo is already in the status of a village council.

In 2005, the rural settlement Vnukovskoye was formed in the Leninsky district of the Moscow region. And from July 1, 2012, this is already the administrative unit of the Novomoskovsk district of Moscow.

Entertaining places

The most interesting and famous place on the territory of the Vnukovo settlement is the village of Michurinets or Peredelkino. Under the second name, he became known. Built in the mid 30-ies of the XX century at the request of M. A. Gorky and by order of I. V. Stalin, the village was intended as a resting place for the creative intelligentsia of the Soviet Union.

Such celebrities as Bella Akhmadulina, Yevgeny Yevtushenko, Bulat Okudzhava, Boris Pasternak, Korney Chukovsky, Irakliy Andronikov, Lev Kassil, Margarita Aliger and many others lived here. It was difficult to get here even to honored figures of the Country of Soviets. And in memory of those who helped build and live for many generations, there remained street names, house museums and symbols on the coat of arms of the Vnukovo settlement.

Of the historical sights there is the Church of Elijah the Prophet, built after 1670 in the settlement of Izvarino. It is mentioned in the annals of the reign of Ivan IV the Terrible.

Since Moscow and Vnukovo settlement have become one since 2012, you can get there with all possible urban modes of transport. From the metro stations Yugo-Zapadnaya, Troparevo, Salaryevo, Teply Stan or by electric trains from Kievsky station in Moscow.

Through the Vnukovo settlement lie the Kiev, Minsk and Borovskoye highways. There are two railway stations on the territory: Michurinets and Vnukovo.
