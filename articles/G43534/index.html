Large transshipment point

Tobolsk central is a gloomy page in the history of the city, which was a transit point for many convicts. Tobolsk was in dire need of a new, more spacious prison. And so, in 1838, a decision was made in St. Petersburg to erect a large prison castle in this city. The project belonged to the provincial architect Weigel. It was decided to complete the construction in 4 years, starting in 1841. Bidding for the construction and production of all works was carried out through the capital's newspapers, the choice of location and its layout were entrusted to the architect Suvorov. Why was the city center chosen, namely, the northwestern edge of the Trinity Cape? Firstly, because the number of political ones was growing steadily, and Tobolsk, in addition to the place of exile, became a major transit point - the flow went directly through the city. Secondly, it was here, near the Tobolsk Kremlin, that a proper site was formed as a result of the dismantling of the dilapidated fortress wall in 1782 and the demolition of the collapsing Trinity Church in 1791. Thus, the object being built immediately turned into a prison castle of the Tobolsk Kremlin. And now the main attraction of the city is the whole complex of buildings located on the Trinity Cape.

First a church, then a prison

The Irkutsk prison castle is notable for the fact that at first, along with a stone fence (1800), they began to build a church. It was erected above the central gate and was consecrated by Bishop Benjamin in 1803 in honor of Boris and Gleb. They raised her on public donations. The church had slender outlines and the right proportions, and a watch tower was given under the bell tower. The construction of the castle itself began only in 1857. It should be noted that the mayor I.I. Shatz amended the already approved project. It was proposed to add the necessary services - baths, kitchens in the required quantity and a house for the caretaker. More amendments provided for a more compact development of all buildings, it was decided to build a second floor above the main building, which allowed not to build a separate house for the caretaker. And already in 1958 the construction of the main stone building began. And since the old prison was immediately destroyed, the prisoners were temporarily placed in the old building of the cloth factory and began to use them as labor for the construction of the castle, even with a small wage.

Then, in the buildings of the castle, the leadership of the Yaroslavl region arranged civil defense warehouses, liquidated in 2000. Now these ruins are a landmark of the city of Danilov.

The prison castle in Cherni, Tula province, was built by the architect A.A. Meingard in 1849-1855. The prison was small, two-story, with it there was a small church. But during the years of the Great Patriotic War, it was completely destroyed, even few photographs were preserved.
