What is social experience? Concept and essence

Social experience is, if expressed in a scientific style, a stable system of habits, feelings, knowledge and skills that is formed in a person during his life. The topic is not only interesting, but also relevant. One has only to think: a little less than 7.4 billion people live on Earth. And each person in his own way is special and unique. Whoever he is. And he becomes such precisely in the course of gaining social experience. Well, for a better understanding of the topic, it is worth considering it in more detail.





general information

What does social experience mean ? This is quite easy to understand if you think about the meaning of each word individually. Social - means connected with life, society and relations between people. Experience - knowledge gained in the process of observation and participation in certain actions.

For a person to find him, he must be part of society, constantly interact with him. In the course of this, he receives his social role, which also leaves a certain imprint on his understanding and perception of life.

There is such a thing as objectified social experience. This is what is accumulated by all of humanity as a whole, since such experience is gained in the course of joint life. These are traditions, rituals, customs, norms of behavior, moral precepts.

For example, in some African tribes a deceased person is eaten by his family and friends, believing that his experience and wisdom will thus be transferred to them. And there it is the norm. We consider such behavior to be completely antisocial and unacceptable. In fact, we simply have different moral principles and traditions. Different social experience.

This is also reflected in the manner of communication, in subject forms, in ways of thinking. All of the above people unknowingly pass from generation to generation. And so it has been for centuries. If such a practice did not have a place to be, then we would still be primitive people.

Background

The acquisition of social experience is determined not only by the conditions in which the person is, but also by his personal characteristics. Something that contributes to this is laid down at the genetic level. And this is an innate potential. It determines, in fact, the predisposition of a person in development. Is it possible to say that it is already predetermined what we will be? Not. But there is ‚Äúground‚Äù in the form of potential. Whether it will be implemented or not is another question.

Negative dispositions are what limit a person in social development. Worst of all, when because of this the character becomes unbearable.

By the way, such a science as social pedagogy distinguishes bio- and sociogenetic directions. They determine the specifics of the prospects of an individual in terms of its development.

Proponents of the biogenetic trend assure that everything is predetermined by heredity. It depends on what kind of person a person becomes. And adherents of the sociogenetic trend are sure that each individual is dynamic. And he can become anyone.

The second version is believable and has more scientific foundations and life examples. How many cases does our reality know when an absolutely adequate child grew up in a dysfunctional family . About perception

It is worth noting that the formation of social experience also occurs through our reality. And to be more precise, its perception.
