Dues as tax

I must say that the quitrent in Russia appeared in the ninth century and was originally natural. Fixation of its size is planned for the 14th century. Subsequently, commodity-money relations play an increasingly important role, and the rent gradually begins to be paid in money. Best of all, this practice has taken root in the lands of Novgorod. When corv√©e appeared in the 16th century, a peculiar stratification occurred among the peasant milieu: landlord peasants paid corvee, and state and monastic peasants paid money. Since the mid-18th century, it has flourished with an ever more magnificent color thanks to the growth of market relations. More than half of all peasants paid it in all provinces in the European part of Russia (55% in the non-chernozem part and 26% in the chernozem zone). Thus, this quitrent is a tax, the size of which was fixed and paid completely independently of the opinion and desire of the peasants.

Charge History

By the way, it was money rent that was much more beneficial to the peasants, because it provided them with relative independence and independence in managing. Actually, therefore, all attempts by especially cunning landowners to transfer serfs to corvee-land caused fierce resistance from the population. At the beginning of the 19th century, otkhodnika became more and more popular, which served as the main way to earn money to pay a quitrent, the size of which increased by about half (while peasant allotments were reduced). Gradually, a whole system of mixed payments is developing, which included both cash rent and corvee. In 1861, during the liberation of the peasants, the latter was canceled, replaced with money, and from the beginning of 1863, peasants began to pay a mandatory ransom. Paying the rent to the landlords is a thing of the past. All installment payments thus became redemption.
