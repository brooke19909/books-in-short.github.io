Aquarium

The structure of the aquarium is striking in its scope. The park has thirty-five independent aquariums. They contain in full accordance with the natural conditions more than ten thousand units of the rarest fish that inhabit the southern and northern seas. A total of four hundred and fifty species are represented.

Each aquarium has a unique thematic design that tells about the natural habitat of fish that are contained in this tank. The heart of the aquamira is the glass tunnel that permeates the giant aquarium. Its length is eighty meters.

It is laid in the very center of the aquarium. The floor of the glass corridor is a moving walkway, implemented in the manner of a travelator. She slowly and unhurriedly transfers the guests of the aquarium from one part of the tank to another.





The largest aquarium of the water center has a diameter of thirty-six meters. The depth of this tank is five meters! It contains more than four million liters of sea water. This giant aquarium contains the largest inhabitants of the deep sea, such as stingrays and moray eels, sharks and other equally dangerous and terrifying underwater predators.

Inhabitants of the deep sea

The basis of the exposition of the underwater world of the aquarium is the inhabitants of the depths of the Mediterranean Sea. In addition to the fish and marine representatives traditional for this area, in the aquariums there was also a place for the inhabitants of Australian reefs, small fishes of the Caribbean and Red Seas.





In a separate section, the creators of the aquarium have allocated a family of invertebrates. They are demonstrated in all their glory within the framework of a separate stand where you can see with your own eyes oysters, octopuses, sea mollusks, snails and squids.
