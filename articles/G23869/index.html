What is the weather like in November in Malta. Why should you go to Malta in November for a vacation

This is a beautiful island state with a rich history, many ancient buildings and interesting sights.

When to go to Malta?

It is worth approaching the choice of the season of the year with all responsibility. The height of the season begins here in June, when the average air temperature reaches + 25-26 degrees Celsius, and the water warms up to +21 (this is quite enough for most tourists, especially Russians).

The hottest time is August: it is difficult to walk along the street calmly, that is, without streams of sweat all over the body. In the evenings, especially after sunset, the air becomes very stuffy and humid, which can adversely affect the health of some people, for example, those who have certain medical contraindications to being in this type of climate. The season ends for the "thermophilic" in September. Namely, in autumn, you can observe velvet summers, when the temperature of the air and sea is quite acceptable for swimming, but the sun no longer burns with all its might. The weather in November in Malta is already cool, so this mini-season is perfect for tourists who want to explore the island's endless sights or deepen their knowledge of the English language without being distracted by the tempting Mediterranean Sea.

Holidays in Malta in November

It is in the fall that many plants bloom on the island, in addition, it is time to harvest a rich harvest. Therefore, Malta in November does not look like a typical autumn, rather like a real Indian summer. Resting on the island at the height of the tourist season, you can not worry about entertainment. In this calm time, when young people have already left the place to sit at their desks in the new school year, when the sun "keeps itself within the bounds of decency" (the weather in Malta in March is very gentle), when it becomes possible to completely immerse yourself in the Maltese atmosphere - people come here for the purpose of excursion vacations or to improve working qualifications.

This topic is very popular at present. Learning a foreign language in a foreign country provides many advantages, and a certificate of qualification will excel any employee in the eyes of superiors. True, it is worth preparing with great care for a trip at this time of year. For example, with the approach of the evening, the temperature in Malta in November decreases significantly (to + 13-14 degrees Celsius), which gives a feeling of a breeze of wet autumn. Therefore, it is worth taking with you things not only summer but also winter: sweaters, warm pants, sneakers, light boots and windcheaters.

English language learning

Malta is one of the most common places for learning a foreign language (in addition to this island you can go to the USA, Great Britain, Australia, Austria and many other countries). In addition, living here and at the same time studying, the tourist gets the opportunity to communicate with the Maltese in English, so the language barrier just melts. Most often, students come here during the summer holidays so as not to miss school or university classes, have time to sunbathe and have fun in the fun place of Pacheville. However, the weather in November in Malta contributes to a more in-depth study of a foreign language without distraction.

Travel Tips

  • In Malta, you should drink water very carefully. Not because it can be of poor quality, but because it is easy to get a cold (especially in high season). Water should be drunk only warm, in no case cold.
  • In every city there are many souvenir shops where you can buy memorabilia for every taste: from unusual but familiar magnets to incredibly beautiful branded handmade vases.
  • You should not buy things in Sliema (this town is the most popular tourist area, so the prices are high). There are others, for example, Bugibba - the city is no less cheerful, but with prices more affordable.
