Wadi Rum Nature Reserve

This territory is of national importance and among the locals is lovingly called the Moon Valley, as it is a spectacular desert landscape.

Its main attraction, of course, are the deserted sand wastelands themselves, which neither civilization nor time touched. Everything is the same as many centuries ago.

Wadi Rum is famous for its beautiful natural arches, wells, canyons and unique cliffs, which are not found all over the world. This place is especially loved by tourists who prefer an active form of relaxation, are fond of climbing.

Wadi Rum is also interesting for its history. On some of its cliffs drawings are drawn that are already more than three thousand years old. The stunning example of the architectural style that dominates this place many centuries ago can be considered the remains of the ruins of the ancient Nabatean temple.

In the spring, after rains, the desert of the country actually takes on an unusual appearance. Crowds of both local and foreign tourists rush here. Rania Al-Abdullah herself, Queen of Jordan, comes there every year.

A photo of this edge of eternal sand cannot but amaze the imagination of even experienced travelers: a huge area turns green and for some time turns into a beautiful endless thick meadow covered with various colors.

Wadi Rum is best visited as one tour with arrivals in Petra, Dana and Aqaba.

Petra, Jordan: photos and description

It is the oldest city in the country. Once it was the capital of the ancient Nabatean state, which arose in the 7th century BC.

Most of the architectural structures of this place are located directly in the rocks and carved from red sandstone.

In ancient times, it was a thriving village, which was built at the intersection of trade routes. In the rocks were located not only necropolises, but also entire residential quarters.

And outside, in the depths of the canyon, an entire amphitheater was built. Historians and archaeologists believe that the first levels of the structure carved in the rock, like all buildings in Petra, were used for purely ritual purposes. They were built by the Nabataeans before the arrival of the Romans.



Later, when the Tenth Roman Legion was located in these places , the amphitheater was expanded to several thousand places. It was located in the lower city, which, like the upper rocky one, was decorated with various sculptures, frescoes and decorative carvings.

Judging by them, one can imagine how densely populated, active and majestic the city of Petra (Jordan) was once, a photo of which it is simply impossible not to take.

Nowadays, more than half a million tourists come here annually to see the sights of the ancient capital.
