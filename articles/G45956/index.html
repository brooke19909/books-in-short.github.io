Oprichnik - who is this? Famous Opochniki in History

Statehood of Russia went through many difficult stages, sometimes one was worse than the other. The most terrible and dark period in the history of Russia, most historians call the years of oprichnina. Oprichnik is a myth, or did he really exist? A terrible rumor went about these sovereign servants, they said that they were not at all, real monsters, "demons in the flesh." So what can be said about the guardsmen, who they really were and why such really scary stories are told about them?



Forced measures

The appearance of the oprichnina is preceded by a series of negative events for Moscow. The Moscow kingdom during this period waged a bloody Livonian war. The Livonian conflict is one of the largest military companies of the 16th century in the Baltic region, led by large, influential states in those regions - the Moscow kingdom, the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, the Kingdom of Sweden, the Kingdom of Denmark. In January 1558, Moscow attacked Livonia. At first, the Russian troops brought Ivan the Terrible a number of significant victories; Narva, Derpt and many other cities and villages of the Baltic states were conquered.

In connection with the situation, Ivan the Terrible introduced martial law in the country , creating a structure of oprichnikism in the state.

Strict selection

At that time, the country had power not only with the tsar; large feudal lords who divided into eight nests - in a kindred and allotted principle - influenced foreign and domestic policy. None of them acted for the good of their country and, of course, taxes put in their pocket. One serf sometimes had two feudal lords. Only the Yaroslavl princes at that time were about eighty. All these princes did not put a dime in the treasury, which made the Russian Tsar very angry . Since the country already had enough problems, and especially during the war, the tsar needed to solve this feudal problem. On January 3, 1565, Ivan the Terrible announced that he was abdicating because of anger at the nobles. After such a shocking announcement, thousands of people gathered and went to the king to beg him to return to the throne and again lead the country. Exactly one month later, the Russian Tsar announced that he would return to rule, but with the right to execute the boyars without trial, to tax them, to deprive them of property. The rest of the state was supposed to give up the zemstvo. To all this, he added that he was introducing oprichnina in the country. In it, he identified individual boyars, clerks and servants. Therefore, the oprichnik is a person who has certain powers and executes orders directly from the king himself. The Tsar obliged certain cities to contain the oprichnina : Veliky Ustyug, Vologda, Suzdal, Vyazma, Kozelsk, Medyn, etc.





The essence of the oprichnina

The Oprichnik is a person who took on the function of a lightning rod, deprived the prince of power, and a feudal lord in a certain region. Ivan the Terrible acted very cunning, thus killing two birds with one stone. Deprived the arbitrariness of the nobility and distributed to the remaining lands conquered in the Baltic states. Its significance was narrowly targeted and somewhat reminded the function of special forces in our time.





Combat clashes

Since the princes had military slaves subordinate (a detachment of soldiers guarding the interests of their master), to deprive the land of this nobleman was not an easy task. This is where the "black horseman" appeared - the oprichnik. The definition of the word we gave a little higher. The nature of his occupation was essentially the strengthening of the unified power of the king and the killing of those who disagree with this. Often they are characterized as cowardly and vile people. But not everyone was like that; among the guardsmen there were good military leaders and field commanders. There was a case: during the capture of the Livonian city, an army under the command of Prince Tyufyakin stood near the fortress and began to "argue", unwillingness to attack and constant excuses angered the tsar, and he sent there the oprichnik, who, having shown the tsar‚Äôs decree, removed Tyufyakin and him from command of the army assistants, and he undertook to lead the assault on fighters.

Dog head and broom

Modern historians describe the personal protection of the king as follows. A man dressed in all black, with his head trimmed to the saddle and a broom behind his back. The head symbolized that the young oprichnik would sniff out treason, and sweep her away with a broom. This was not entirely true. Yes, the oprichnik was dressed in a black caftan, since they were a kind of order and dressed accordingly. As for carrion - complete nonsense, on a hot day with a severed head is not particularly clear. For the first time, this information came from foreigners who, most likely, drew an analogy with the Dominican monks, in this order the symbol was the head of a dog that adorned the gates of the monastery. Why a dog‚Äôs head? Dominicans called themselves dogs of the Lord. They, like the guardsmen, investigated crimes (against faith), and perhaps this was the reason for the emergence of such an analogy. And the broom was not quite a broom. As a sign of their belonging to the chosen caste of the king, the guardsmen wore a woolen brush on their belts - a broom sweeping treason.
