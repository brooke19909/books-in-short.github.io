Owl pendant

A coffee owl can be made in many ways. It can be sewn, knitted, made in the form of an application.

The first option is quite simple, following the instructions, you can quickly cook up a night bird.

It will be a small coffee owl pendant. To create it will need such materials:

  • White paper;
  • dense fabric of white color;
  • needle, thread, sewing machine;
  • a simple pencil;
  • cotton wool;
  • coffee beans;
  • scissors;
  • black marker;
  • cord and ring for suspension;
  • coffee, black tea, vanillin;
  • hot glue;
  • acrylic paints and brushes;
  • black helium pen;
  • a glass of warm water.

Jar of Owl coffee

DIY coffee owls will help you prepare an original birthday present for friends and relatives. For example, a jar made in the shape of an owl will benefit and decorate the interior.

To create such a present you need to prepare the following materials:

  • a glass jar with a lid that twists;
  • a piece of burlap;
  • scissors;
  • hot glue;
  • Scotch;
  • tight cord, rope;
  • thin cardboard;
  • ribbons and lace;
  • shreds of burlap color;
  • coffee beans;
  • Buttons on the legs or eyes;
  • gray knitted threads;
  • acrylic paints, varnish.

Getting started creating a coffee owl:

  1. From the burlap we cut out a rectangle corresponding to the size of the can. In the middle of the jar, mark the center, cut the oval from the tape and glue it on the marking site. From the middle rectangle we cut out a similar geometric figure.
  2. Gently cover the jar with glue so as not to touch the oval "window". Paste over the prepared burlap jar. The fabric below should be 10 centimeters oversize. These are the future paws.
  3. Glue a thin ribbon along the neck of the can (along the edge of the burlap).
  4. We turn to the limbs of an owl. From an extra piece of fabric hanging from a can, we make incisions: for two legs and a tail.
  5. We twist the legs and hem, trying to give them the appropriate shape. We perform the same procedure with the tail. Fold the base with a triangle and carefully flash it.
  6. We cut out the wings of paper and transfer the pattern to a fabric of a similar color to burlap. Do not forget about the necessary clearance on the fabric to gently glue the edges on the wings. We glue the cardboard base to the fabric pattern and attach it to the "can" owl.
  7. We glue coffee beans on the edge of the window with hot glue, on the "belly" of the owl, glue three grains to the legs.
  8. We glue the edges of the legs and wings of the coffee owl with a thin wool rope.
  9. Wings are decorated with circular patterns of knitting threads or rope. We carry out a number of patterns, combining with a number of coffee beans. The wings will turn out beautiful and voluminous.
  10. We make out the tail with grains of coffee and a woolen edging.
  11. We pass to the head. Cut a rectangle half smaller than it was on the jar. Glue the edges of the burlap to the edges of the lid.
  12. From thick cardboard we cut circles in descending order, at least 6 parts. We stick on each other and on the cover. We fasten the lid to the jar and paste over with burlap. We form the upper edges with pointed ears, we tie them with lace and fluff up the tips of the ears.
  13. From knitting threads we make two pompoms and sew them in place of the eye sockets. Sew buttons instead of eyes, color with yellow, black and white paint. Instead of a nose fasten an oval button.
  14. We decorate the owl with ribbons and beads, fill it with a jar of coffee and the original gift is ready.
