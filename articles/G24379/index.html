The urban type village Krasnogvardeiskoe in Crimea: features of the economy and tourism

Many believe that Crimea is just the sea and vacationers resting on it, monuments of a majestic and formidable history or the magnificent beauty of the south coast. Some people think that Crimea lives only in the summer, at a time when people eager for relaxation and warmth come.

In fact, the inhabitants of most Crimean regions are engaged in a variety of work: they raise animals and crops, look after gardens, work in factories and factories. Such hard workers include the Krasnogvardeisky district of Crimea.

A little about the Krasnogvardeisky in the Crimea

Until 1944, located in the steppe of the peninsula of the village. Krasnogvardeiskoe had the Tatar name Kurman-Kemelchi. After the deportation of the Crimean Tatars, the village was given another name - Krasnogvardeiskoe.

But today in the village people of different nationalities live amicably: Russians, Ukrainians, Crimean Tatars, Greeks, Armenians, Koreans, Gypsies. They work together and celebrate holidays. Agriculture

The weather in Krasnogvardeisky (Crimea) is favorable for the development of agriculture. In winter, the temperature rarely drops below zero, in summer the average air temperature is +27 o . Dry air warms up quickly and retains heat well.

About 160 thousand hectares of land in the region are occupied by agricultural crops: fodder, technical, grain, essential oil, as well as orchards and vegetables. In the Krasnogvardeisky district grow:
