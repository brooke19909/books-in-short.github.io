Single Channel Power Amplifiers: An Overview

Single - channel power amplifiers for a car are sometimes called subwoofer monoblocks. They are designed specifically for connecting multiple subwoofers.

Monoblocks have some distinctive features related to the scope of their application, and are widely popular.

Features of single-channel amplifiers

Many motorists want to buy a monoblock, since it has the following features and advantages.

  • Firstly, these are monophonic amplifiers. When you feed them a stereo / mono signal from the radio, which is collected in the amplifier, you get a mono signal through the subwoofer at the output.
  • Secondly, single-channel amplifiers have a fairly high power. At 4 ohms load, the power per channel is from 150 watts.
  • Thirdly, in such subwoofer amplifiers, there is a mandatory high-pass filter that cuts off all frequencies that are higher than the filter settings frequency. And this is a prerequisite for connecting a subwoofer.
  • Fourthly, in monoblocks often there is a so-called sabsonik filter, which cuts off ultra-low frequencies from a musical signal. This is quite an important thing, since getting into the subwoofer of extra low frequencies can lead to its malfunction.

Choose a candy bar

Single-channel power amplifiers have many parameters and characteristics that must be considered when choosing. The most important of them are:

  • amplifier class;
  • subwoofer power;
  • maximum load on the amplifier;
  • minimum system resistance.

Most of the monoblocks on the market are classified as class D. They have a higher power and smaller size than A / B devices, in addition, they heat up significantly less. But at the same time, the sound from class D single-channel amplifiers is slightly inferior to the A / B analogs in sound quality. Although if the signal is fed to a subwoofer, then there is no difference in quality.

Prices for monoblocks of class A / B are lower, which is why they are a budget option.
