What you need to know about Kemerovo airports in general?

The need for a reliable air-traffic interchange for this settlement arose a long time ago. Undoubtedly, such an important economic, cultural, scientific and industrial center was in dire need of a solution to this problem.





In Soviet times, Kemerovo airports of local importance were considered well-known, with the help of which the main cargo was transported. However, in the early 2000s, it was decided to close them. The reason was the lack of profitability of the content.

The most important airport of the city is named after cosmonaut Alexei Leonov, who was born in the Tula district of the Kemerovo region. This international airport is located within the city, only 11 km from the center in a southeast direction, and was founded in 1960.

It should be noted that in the early 1990s, the airport underwent a massive reconstruction. This was needed to obtain international status. In particular, the terminal for domestic flights was restored and the terminal for international flights was built, and a 3200-meter runway was introduced. It currently allows you to accept all modern aircraft. In 2001, the airport began to operate international flights.

So, today Kemerovo airports are just one transport hub, but tourists do not experience any inconvenience. From here, almost at any time of the year you can fly to Sochi and Moscow, as well as to the cities of Thailand, China, Turkey, Vietnam, Egypt, Greece, Spain.

Characteristics

The airport has domestic and international terminals. In the international terminal there is a VIP-lounge with soft comfortable furniture for passengers to relax. In addition, there is a counter for check-in and control before the flight, a meeting room, a board that displays all the information on flights, a bar with alcoholic drinks, it is possible to watch TV and use the Internet.





In the terminal of domestic airlines, the Kemerovo International Airport is equipped with ordinary waiting rooms, in which there are comfortable seating for passengers, a cafeteria, a left-luggage office, a stall with fresh newspapers, a toilet, and a shower. There is also a mother and child room provided only for passengers with children under 7 years of age and pregnant women with a longer stay.
