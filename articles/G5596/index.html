Therapeutic effect

Lakes with fresh water are oversaturated with living organisms and vegetation, and only tiny brine shrimp survive in salt ponds. It is these crustaceans that, when they die, are mixed with silty sediments and salts, as a result of which, over the course of several centuries, healing mud was formed, which Lake Yarovoye is famous for. Rest on it is favorable for the overall healing of the body and strengthening the nervous system, as well as to achieve a positive effect in the treatment of some intractable skin diseases.

Geographic features

Lake Yarovoye, with a length of 11.5 km, a width of 8 km and a maximum depth of 10.5 m, is located in the Kulundinskaya steppe. The absence of external inflows and drains formed a highly mineralized reservoir environment, due to which the taste of the water is bitter-salty. The lake is fed by underground key sources.

The high shores, in the south-west cut by ravines, in no way interfere with organizing a memorable vacation on the wild savages.

City history

The first mention of the city of Yarovoy can be attributed to 1941, when the memorandum addressed to the Minister of Chemical Industry of the USSR contained detailed arguments justifying the need to create a bromine plant on the shores of a salt lake. As a result, a year later, in wartime, work began on the construction of the Slavgorod Chemical Plant, which needed large reserves of salt water. It was thanks to the defense industry that these territories were considered closed for a long time, and there was no information about the uniqueness and beauty of the local natural resources.

The foundation of the city, next to which is the lake of the same name, dates back to 1943. At that time, a small village was formed on this site, populated by chemists evacuated to the Altai Territory, and already in 1944 the production of chemical products was started. And more recently (by historical standards) - in March 1993 - the town received the status of a city.

Modern realities

Currently, Yarovoye has become a modern, fairly compact town with a population of over 18 thousand people. The lush greenery of public gardens and cozy gardens, unique architectural features and natural healing attractions attract many tourists and people who care about their health to Yarovoye.

Not far from the city there is a tent camp, which corresponds to the ideas of the "savages" about "comfort" and the features of living in such conditions. Located 10 km from Yarovoy freshwater lake ("Teply Klyuch") with a well-equipped shore, toilets, cafes and entertainment venues makes a pleasant variety in the "wild" pastime.

Climatic aspects

The sharply continental climatic conditions are characterized by high average daily air temperatures in the summer. Their level almost completely coincides with the temperature indicators of the southern Russian resorts. The minimum humidity of the climate, due to which the rest on Yarovoy savages becomes the most attractive, contributes to good tolerance of heat during the summer heat.

Over 2,000 hours a year, the sun shines over the territory of the Kulundinskaya Plain, providing the most favorable conditions for sunbathing.
