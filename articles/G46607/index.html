History. Amazing india

Kerala is the state of the country that appeared on the map in 1956. Its merger was facilitated by the merger of Cochin, Travankor and Malabar. All of them were the richest states of India. A year later, the people of Kerala elected a communist government. In this regard, the region began to be considered the very first democratic state in world history.

Kerala, India ( see photo below), and is currently considered to be a prosperous state in social terms. Its territory is densely populated. At the same time, Hindus make up sixty percent of the population, and Christians and Muslims each account for twenty percent.



In shaping the history of the state, a decisive role was played by the strip of the sea coast, stretching over 575 km. The Babylonians, Chinese, Egyptians and Phoenicians as early as the 3rd century BC visited ships in numerous ports of this area and were actively trading here. Spices and ivory at the beginning of the seventeenth century attracted the British, French and Dutch to Kerala. An amazing feature of the history of the state is the presence of Muslims, Christians and Jews here, who adapted to the customs of a foreign country and got along with the local population, helping him establish trade with other countries.

For the precious territory of Kerala, the British, Dutch and Portuguese fought constant wars. Full control of the state was gained only in the eighteenth century. For more than a century, Kerala was a colony of Britain.


Economy

India (Kerala) is currently known worldwide for its exported rubber, spices, coffee, ginger, cashew nuts and black pepper. For commercial purposes, the state allows the cutting of black and rosewood. At the same time, the use of valuable tree species is controlled by the state.

Tourism is the most important source of income for the state of Kerala (India). Photos of resort areas located on this territory indicate the desire of local residents to attract a large flow of tourists. This allows you to create new jobs and boost the state economy.



In Kerala, some areas of industrial production are also developed. These, in particular, include energy, engineering and shipbuilding. They produce fertilizers, chemicals, plywood and synthetic textiles in Kerala. A large percentage of the working population is engaged in the export of coconut fiber, seafood and cashew nuts.

Where can I relax?

If the purpose of your trip is India (Kerala), a map of this country will show the names of beach resorts. One of them is Kovalam. This village is located on the Malabar coast and attracts the attention of tourists from all over the world with its fantastic beaches, which are surrounded by coconut groves.

Kovalam is a natural bay, which is ideal not only for swimming, but also for water skiing, as well as for surfing. Along the coast stretch small strips of coral reefs, between which vacationers are invited to ride on catamarans. However, Kovalam is not only a beach resort. This is a very popular among vacationers wellness and yoga center. Visitors are offered Ayurvedic massage using aromatic oils, as well as special meditation lessons.
