Walter Nowotny: biography

Walter Novotny is the name of a man who went down in history as a fighter pilot during World War II. His life, like a flash, ended just before it began. If it were not for the fact that Navotny fought on the side of the Nazis, one could consider him a hero worthy of eternal memory.

The Second World War

After graduating from the fighter school in Schwechat in 1941, Walter Nowotny was enlisted in the First Air Fleet, stationed in East Prussia. He took part in the Soviet-German military campaign from its first days.

Novotny opened his winning score over the waters of the Gulf of Riga on July 19th, 41, having shot down three Soviet Chaika (I-153). This operation was not easy for him - the pilot had to land his plane on the water and hold out for three days.

The noble act of Walter Nowotny

In addition to the fact that Walter Novotny became the first person in the world to cross the line of 250 downed planes (later his record was broken, and he became only the fifth among the Luftwaffe pilots), this man distinguished himself by yet another, one can say heroic and, of course, noble act . He decided on a bold protest, writing a letter personally to Hitler criticizing the intention to shoot 47 captured pilots who attempted to escape from the camp where they were held.



It is not known whether this letter had any effect (Hitler did not officially respond to the letter), but the act itself indicates that Novotny was able not only to fight well, but also to respect the enemy. He was also a fearless man.

Last flight

On November 8, a German aircraft flew up from the airfield Amher into the sky, which was supposed to intercept the raid of American bombers. From the clouds came the sounds of shooting, one of the "Americans" fell. And then the radio operator heard the illegible words "I'm burning" or "The plane is on fire." So, at the age of 24, the legendary Luftwaffe pilot Walter Nowotny died. His photo forever preserved the image of a young smiling guy who, unfortunately, happened to become a cog in a large bloody meat grinder and leave this world without ever starting to really live.
