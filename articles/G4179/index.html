Arambol, Goa: reviews, photos, attractions

Goa is located on the coast of the Arabian Sea, in the south-west of India. In terms of space, this is the smallest state state. Despite this, it is the richest region of the country. The state capital is the city of Panaji. Vasco da Gama is the largest settlement.



From the history of the state

Goa people inhabited the territory of another 30 thousand years ago. Traces of human presence in these places, which archaeologists have been able to detect, are of precisely such an age. These are primitive tools, cave paintings. The history of the current state began with the landing on this land of colonists from Portugal in the 16th century.

Arambol, Goa (India)

This is the most peaceful and at the same time popular place in the state. Arambol is endless beaches, often wild, a small number of vacationers. This place is ideal for those who prefer to relax in the wild.

This former fishing village is 38 km from the state capital. The area is called North Goa. Arambol is a picturesque and popular beach. In some places it is pebble, in other places it is sandy. Right on the shore you can see small lagoons with fresh water.

Arambol Beach (Goa) because of its isolation is not very convenient for visiting tourists. But fans of great surfing still come here. There are several attractive bays in the north of Arambol.

Behind one of them, the trail leads to a wide strip covered with white sand. From two sides it is surrounded by steep cliffs. At the bottom of this valley is a small freshwater lake that goes into the jungle. It feeds on hot springs. Its bottom is lined with sulphurous mud. This place was chosen by hippies who have been living here for many years. They claim that dirt is very good for human health, so they spend most of the day walking naked around the shallows.



Arambol Beach (Goa), it confirms the reviews of tourists, attracts vacationers with tranquility and solitude.

There is a legend that the famous group The Beatles came to these places to do meditation under a huge tree, which plays an important role in Hinduism - a banyan tree. It can not be confused with any other plant. Its striking appearance is determined by additional roots growing from branches and a trunk.

When to come to Arambol

Despite the fact that this is the most remote holiday destination in North Goa, it is crowded from November to March. In winter, people come here from all over the world who want to take a break from the hustle and bustle of the city, civilization, enjoy picturesque sea sunsets.

Soft waves, coastline, quite wide, palm groves help to relax and rest, both soul and body. The primordial silence of these places is broken only by fishermen, small cozy restaurants that are located around the local central "street", almost at the very water. Not very well-developed infrastructure makes this place charming, semi-bohemian.

sights

Often a question arises for people traveling on vacation to Arambol (Goa): what to see? When you get tired of a beach holiday, we advise you to visit interesting places in the state. We will introduce you to some of them.



Basilica of jesus

This building is located near Panaji. The Basilica is one of the most striking examples of Baroque architecture. It contains the holy relics of Francis Xavier. He was considered the patron saint of Goa. Francis first came to Goa in 1542 and lived here for only 4 months, but after that he visited the place he loved several times. After his death, he was buried in China, but two years later his remains were reburied in Goa. Such was the last will of the deceased.

The basilica began to be erected in 1594, construction continued until 1605. In the same year she was consecrated.

Dudhsagar Waterfall

Arambol (Goa) is striking in its amazing nature. This scenic waterfall can be seen in the Bhagwan Mahavir Nature Reserve. It is located 60 kilometers from Panaji. This is one of the hundred highest waterfalls in the world, the most significant attraction of the state. The waterfall is a multi-level system with a length of 603 and a height of 310 meters.

All tourists visiting Arambol (Goa) come here. Guest reviews state that this miracle of nature makes a huge impression.



The name Dudhsagar is translated as "milk of the ocean." This is due to the milky white color of the water. According to existing legend, on the site of the waterfall in the jungle there was a palace in which the beautiful princess lived. The girl loved to swim in the unusually beautiful lake located nearby, and after bathing she always drank milk from a jug made of pure gold. Once she saw that a man was looking at her through the foliage. Wanting to hide her naked body, she poured milk into the water.

Bondle Nature Reserve

If you decide to relax in the town of Arambol (Goa), the sights of this Indian state will surely interest you. Many tourists say that they received a great impression from visiting the Bondla Nature Reserve, although it is the smallest in the state by the occupied territory.

It is located on a hilly area, 55 km from the city of Panaji. The reserve is located in the jungle, but more like a garden inhabited by many different animals. Its area is just over 8 square kilometers.

The reserve is popular among people who have a rest with children, since it is possible to see rare animals from it, such as giant Malabar squirrels, bison, leopards, Indian bison. Some animals are kept in enclosures in the center of the reserve, while others move freely around the territory (wild boars).

Arambol (Goa) attracts ornithologists from all over the world, since this region is characterized by a wide variety of birds. Many representatives of birds, including the unique Indian peacock, live in Bondla Nature Reserve.



This park is famous not only for its fauna and magnificent landscapes. During the construction of the zoo, the ancient ruins of the temple and stone slabs with Hindu deities carved on them, which were hidden from the Portuguese inquisitors and conquerors, were discovered here. They are of great cultural value.

Temple Magnes

The locals relate with trepidation to historical and cultural monuments. Vacationers in the resort of Arambol (Goa) find interesting places throughout the state.

In the small village of Priol, near the capital, is the largest and most visited temple in the state - Magnes. It was built in honor of God Mangeshi. The main shrine of the temple is Linga Mangeshi - a stone that contains a divine essence. He was consecrated by Brahma.

The temple building is a true work of art. An unusual seven-story tower, which stands at the gates, graceful columns, which are considered the most beautiful in the state, look very impressive. The oldest part of the 400-year-old complex is a small pond located near the main building. The main hall is famous for its amazing chandeliers of the 19th century.

Arambol, Goa: tourist reviews

To date, many of our compatriots have visited this wonderful Indian resort. Arambol (Goa) makes a different impression on different people. Those tourists who are accustomed to relax in comfortable European resorts believe that the infrastructure is poorly developed, there are no hotels that would meet international standards. Despite this, they are very pleased with the magnificent nature of these places.



Arambol (Goa) is a great place for lovers of secluded relaxation. People who are tired of the hustle and bustle of megalopolises feel comfortable here.
