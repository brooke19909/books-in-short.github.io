Church of the Trinity Life-Giving in Ostankino

The church near the Ostankino pond is a monument of old Russian architecture. It is called the Temple of the Trinity Life-Giving. The church is part of the complex of buildings of the Ostankino museum-estate.

The temple was built in 1677 - 1692. The name of the architect is still not known. Researchers believe that it was either the serf Pavel Potekhin, or the architect Stefan Poretsky. The church is made in the style of "Russian patterned".





The composition of its volumes is so complex that it is amazing. Its decorative forms are very diverse. There are elements such as cornice belts, kokoshniks with colored tiles, painted arches.

Over its history, the church at the Ostankino pond has undergone rebuilding and repair several times. At the end of the 19th century, a bell tower was added. At the same time, the nine-tier iconostasis was updated.

At the beginning of the 20th century, Soviet power seized most of the church's values. More than four pounds of silver and utensils were stolen. Then in 1935 the church was completely closed.

The temple came back to life only in 1991. The restoration was carried out. His Holiness Patriarch Alexy II consecrated the main throne of the church. He bears the name of the Life-Giving Trinity. In addition to him, in the church there is also the southern throne of St. Alexander Svirsky and the northern throne of the Tikhvin Icon of the Mother of God. The shrines of the church are the icon of the Old Testament Trinity of 1654, a list with the miraculous Chernigov-Ilyinsky icon of the Mother of God, as well as the Georgian Icon of the Virgin. The rector of the church is Hieromonk Feofilakt. The church is active. Services are held daily. The liturgy takes place at 9 a.m., the all-night vigil at 5 p.m.

Pond in the museum-estate "Ostankino"

The museum-estate is a specially protected natural territory of the capital. Previously, there was the village of Ostashkovo. The first mention of it dates back to 1558. In 1710 it became known as Ostankino.

In 1584, the Ostankino pond was excavated. Trees were planted around him. The construction of the first manor buildings began. At the end of the 17th century, a church was built.





From 1743 to 1917, the Sheremetev family owned the estate. In the XVIII century, its representative, Nikolai Petrovich, built a theater-palace here. Around him was a park. It was organized in a French style, fashionable at the time. Then, from 1791 to 1798, the palace and park ensemble of the estate was built. In 1918, it became the State Museum of the Serfs.

At first glance it seems that the palace is built of stone. But actually it is made of wood. The palace was built so harmoniously, its interiors are so elegant that it fascinates visitors throughout its history.

At the end of the 16th - beginning of the 17th centuries a pond was dug in the floodplain of the Ostankino stream. He was given the same name. But the people still call him Palace and Acterkin. The last name is due to the fact that earlier performances were organized in the estate. The actors in them were serfs. Unable to withstand difficult life and punishment, they drowned in the pond.

He experienced a lot in his story. In the XX century, its shores were lined with stone. The channel of the Ostankino stream was transferred to the collector.





In February 2013, the estate was closed for reconstruction. Also, the Ostankino pond located on its territory does not work. The reconstruction will approximately last until December 2016.
