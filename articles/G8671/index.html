The external and internal decoration of the station is quite modest compared to many other metro stations, which the city of Moscow has. Name history

The station was named after the prominent statesman and party leader Vadim Podbelsky, who at the beginning of the 20th century worked as the People's Commissar of Posts and Telegraphs of the RSFSR, and subsequently oversaw the construction of the Shukhov Tower on Shabolovka. Near the station was located the same street. Podbelsky, however, just two years after its opening, the street was renamed Ivanteyevskaya, and the geographical location of the name was lost.

In the process of city development, at different times, projects appeared to rename the station: "North-East", "Ivanteevskaya" - but not one of them was accepted. Current state

In addition to replacing the name, no more large-scale restoration or design work was done to change the appearance of the station. It remains a fairly simple, functional station, the main task of which was and is to connect the peripheral areas of the capital with other parts of the city. However, it is worth noting that she copes with her role remarkably. Metro management monitors its general condition and appearance. Since it was built not so long ago, the outer cladding has not yet had time to become so old or out of order that it took work to eliminate the shortcomings.
