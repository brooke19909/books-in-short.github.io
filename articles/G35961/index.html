Tariff "Orange", "Tele2" - reviews. Tariff "Orange", "Tele2" - how to connect

Today, your attention will be presented to the tariff "Orange", "Tele2". This plan has interested many customers. After all, the mobile operator interprets it as an advantageous offer. Therefore, it is worthwhile to find out what opinion subscribers who already use this option have about the tariff plan. Do you really need to pay attention to it?



general description

To begin with, the tariff "Orange" ("Tele2") begins to receive feedback from subscribers at the stage of its fleeting description. The thing is that the mobile operator characterizes this option as an anti-crisis.

You can make calls in Russia and the home region for mere pennies. No monthly fee, no cheating. Only a good offer. It is rather difficult to refuse it. In any case, you can strive for this offer before you find out the details of the connection. Maybe they will push you away. What conditions does Tele2 offer to switch to the Orange tariff?

Calls by region

An important point is the cost of calls in the region. All buyers pay attention to this feature. It makes no sense to purchase a tariff plan that does not provide profitable calls within the home region.

What do we get in the end? Tariff "Orange" ("Tele2") receives quite good feedback from subscribers in this area. After all, calls within the home network will cost you only 90 cents per minute. By the way, per second tariffication is used here. In some regions, this tariff offers calls within the home network of only 60 kopecks. The essence of this does not change - less than a ruble per conversation. This feature pleases all subscribers.

By the way, this price applies to all phone numbers within the region. And it pleases. It's time to think about how to connect the tariff "Orange". "Tele2" implies in this case a few more features that will be offered to us.



Messages

Of particular delight for subscribers is the cost of messages within the region of connection. Anyway, in Russia as a whole. If you send SMS to the numbers of your city and region, then get ready to give only 90 kopecks (or 60 each). In other words, now calls and messages are the same. And inexpensive. Subscribers quite often emphasize this feature when talking about the "Orange" tariff.

In Russia, messages are transmitted at a higher cost. For one SMS, give 2.5 rubles. Buyers claim that this is not so much money. Indeed, competitors of "Tele2" quite often the price tag of messages depends on the operator to whom you send the letter. Yes, and it ranges from 5 to 10 rubles. The difference is obvious.

Multimedia messages with their value also delight Tele2 subscribers. Indeed, one such letter costs 6 rubles (in some regions - 4 each). Regardless of where exactly in Russia you are sending it. And on which operator - too. So far, the Orange tariff (Tele2) offers truly favorable terms of use. Many of these offers are attracted very quickly.

Calls in Russia

Do not forget that calls in Russia will also cost something. And for some sociable people this is very important. However, there are no particular complaints either. After all, the tariff "Orange" ("Tele2") is considered anti-crisis.



All calls to this mobile operator in Russia cost 2 rubles per minute. Not too expensive. If you want to call the numbers of another mobile operator in Russia, you will have to pay 9 rubles already. This price tag applies to landline numbers. In principle, subscribers express their satisfaction with the offer. There are no sky-high prices that would scare away the buyer.

For the whole world

Tariff "Orange" ("Tele2") also receives reviews from its subscribers for calls around the world. Few people pay attention to this feature. But, nevertheless, if you suddenly have to call outside of Russia, you will not really think about the cost.

Why? You can call to the CIS countries at 25 rubles per minute, to the Baltic States and Europe - at 45. Satellite communications are probably the most expensive here - 240 rubles per minute. In other regions of the world will have to make calls for 65 rubles per minute. Not so expensive if you think about it. All messages to international operators cost 5.5 rubles. As you can see, the tariff "Orange", "Tele2" (Voronezh, Moscow, Kaliningrad and other regions of Russia) offers very favorable connection conditions. And so many seek to connect it. Subscribers are generally satisfied with their choice.

SIM card purchase

Let's say you like the tariff "Orange", "Tele2". How to switch to it? There are several scenarios. The first one is the purchase of a new SIM card with this plan.



To do this, you will have to contact the nearest Tele2 office. Take your passport with you, and then inform the employee that you would like to buy a room with the Orange rate. A few minutes of waiting - and the case is completed. Check the correctness of filling in the data, select the number of your future phone and insert the SIM card into the mobile device. Now you can enjoy all the benefits that you received after connecting. Nothing complicated. But only this option does not suit everyone. Not really the desire to change the phone number every time you change the tariff.

Combinations

There are several more approaches to solving this issue. How to connect the tariff "Orange" ("Tele2")? First, check that you have at least 40 rubles on your phone. This is the cost of changing the plan. Ready?

Secondly, you have to choose a method of tariff change. You can call 630, and then use the voice robot to complete the action. Not the best option if you want the process to go fast.



In this case, you can use a special "hot" combination. It will automatically transfer you to the "Orange" tariff if there are enough funds in your account. Dial * 630 * 8 #, then press the call key. Wait for the SMS notification with the conclusion. That's all. Now you are connected to the tariff plan called "Orange" from "Tele2". A free transition is carried out if you used the previous conditions of the operator for more than 30 days.
