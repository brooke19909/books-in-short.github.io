Who owns MTS: interesting information. Company History

Have you ever wanted to know who owns MTS? Phone numbers with this mobile operator code are used by many people. After all, MTS is included in the "Big Three" of Russian operators, along with Beeline and Megafon. The customer base of the company has millions of people and has long gone beyond the boundaries of classical cellular communications. Under the auspices of MTS, technology is being created, and the scientific and technical database of data transmission is being developed. The company expands the range of services to meet the needs of customers.

general information

MTS Closed Joint-Stock Company (Mobile TeleSystems) was officially registered in 1993. Due to the rapid growth and active attempts to monopolize the market of mobile operators, MTS is currently one of the largest telecommunications companies not only in Russia but also in the CIS. Now it has in its assets many subsidiaries. Even on a global scale, the company is among the ten most famous.

Specifications

In recent years, MTS has significantly diversified its own business. For example, she began to produce phones under her own brand name (the only minus of most of them is support only for MTS SIM cards). The company has become one of the most expensive brands in Russia. Its value at the end of 2010 amounted to more than two hundred million rubles.

The headquarters of the company is located in Moscow, however, MTS branches are found in many cities. Already in 2013, their number exceeded several thousand. About a quarter of them are flagship stores. This is where you can find branded models of mobile devices. A striking example is the touch MTS 970, released several years ago, as well as the MTS 945 GLONASS, supporting a satellite tracking system.

Despite the current greatness, the path that the company took was not an easy one. Those who own MTS now understand that the stories of their mobile communications corporation as a whole are closely intertwined.

Way of development

Until the nineties of the last century, no one could have imagined that the frequency range of 900 MHz, designed for communication between pilots and the military, would not only be used for peaceful purposes, but would serve to create a mobile communications market.

In 1992, the prototype of the modern GSM system was introduced, and later a competition for the ownership of the GSM-900 license was announced. At that moment, a key event for the company occurred. The victory in this competition was won by the monopolistic association Mobile Moscow, which was later renamed MTS.

The rights to the company at that time were divided between MGTS OJSC (Moscow City Telephone Network), German company DeTeMobil, Siemens and a number of small shareholders. Later, the shares were redistributed several times, but most of them remained in the hands of domestic entrepreneurs, to whom MTS is still owned.

The foundation of the company was laid. A new mobile operator was introduced to the world, and the network coverage, in the 94th, with only one BSS station, began to expand rapidly. The customer base, initially numbering only a few thousand subscribers, has exceeded one million over several years.

This happened for two main reasons. Firstly, the company moved from large centers to regions, actively expanding its services in places where there was practically no mobile communication. A recognizable number was spreading throughout the country. MTS quickly captured the market. The success was affected by the effect of novelty, the lack of decent competition, the expansion of the technical base and the construction of new networks. Those who owned the MTS number found themselves in a winning position.

The second reason for economic victories was the active takeover of small companies that also provide communications services. This facilitated the legal side of the issue regarding the provision of a license for the provision of telecommunication services. So, a significant amount of shares of the Ukrainian operator UMC was purchased. The corporation grew like a snowball, sweeping away competitors in its path.

Guide

In many ways, the development of MTS depended on its leader. It was he who determined the vector along which the company was supposed to move. This is a person for whom the success of the enterprise would be in the first place among all other values.

Leonid Melamed and Mikhail Shamolin

After the global rebranding of the company, as a result of which it acquired its current form, Mikhail Shamolin stood at the helm, replacing Leonid Melamed. The latter did not renounce business and became president of Sistema. But it is interesting not only for this. It is Melamed - the person to whom MTS previously belonged. He proposed to make the familiar red-and-white egg symbolizing the company, symbolizing simplicity of form and promising content.

Shamolin already had behind him not only an automobile and road institute, but also the knowledge acquired at the Russian Academy of Public Administration.

The School of Business (WBS) in finance and management also influenced him. It was she who prepared the bridgehead for his career growth: from a position at McKinsey & Co to a managing director of the Ukrainian Interpipe Corporation. Further, his path lay in MTS, in which he first served as vice president of sales, and three years later became president of the company.

Andrey Dubovsky

In the spring of 2011, he was replaced by Andrei Dubovsky. By that time, the businessman was 45 years old, he graduated from VGIK and worked in the field of telecommunications. Punchy character also led him to leadership positions. After working for some time as the director of one of the branches of the corporation, he headed the company.

World brand

MTS, as mentioned earlier, is more than a mobile operator. It was in his stores that sales of BlackBerry and Apple devices began. It was he who greatly influenced the spread of mobile Internet in Russia and the CIS.

In 2008, the corporation was included in the list of hundreds of the best global brands, and in 2009 it became one of the world giants in the field of mobile communications. By this time, more than 100 million people have become MTS subscribers. The company passed international certification, according to the results of which it was standardized within the framework of ISO.

Despite the status of the most expensive brand in Russia, those who owned MTS actively interacted with the roaming system, reducing prices for their services. At the same time, the company did not reduce the quality of service, remaining one of the most reliable mobile operators.

She began to actively collaborate with banking systems. So, the MTS-Bank service was introduced, which allows using the phone as a payment device.
