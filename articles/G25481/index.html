Memorial complex "Khatyn": history, description, route

The Khatyn memorial complex in the Minsk region of Belarus today is one of the most important monuments of the Second World War.





Brief historical background

The memorial in Khatyn is a tribute to the three million Belarusians who died during the Great Patriotic War. The ensemble is located 54 kilometers northeast of Minsk in the Republic of Belarus.

Until the fateful 1943, Khatyn was an ordinary Belarusian village in the north-east of Minsk. But on March 22, 1943, after a shootout of Soviet partisans and fascists in which a German officer was killed, enemy troops surrounded the village.

Armed Germans locked all the villagers in a huge barn, and then set it on fire. That day, about 160 people were burned alive, among whom there were 75 children. On this terrible day, 6 people were saved. Among them was 56-year-old Joseph Kaminsky. He was able to find his wounded son, but he was not able to snatch him from the hands of death. He had a bullet wound in the stomach, as well as numerous burns.

The history of the village, where the Khatyn memorial complex is now located, is not unique. During the Great Patriotic War, residents of 628 villages and villages of Belarus were burned alive by the Nazis. It so happened that 186 villages from that list were never rebuilt.

After the war, on the site of the former village, the Soviet government erected the Khatyn memorial complex. It was built in memory of all those who died in the lands of Belarus. Soil was brought to build a monument from 185 unrestored villages. Thus a symbolic cemetery was created.

Thus, Khatyn became the 186th village - the site of this terrible cemetery. That is why this place has become one of the most important memorial complexes in the post-Soviet space.

What is the Khatyn memorial complex today?

The opening of the memorial took place on July 5, 1969. He accurately repeats the layout of the former village.

Looking at it, visitors to the complex feel the bitterness and pain of loss. In place of the barn, where the Khatyn burned, a black metal roof was installed, which was on the granary. Next to it is a common grave with a symbolic wreath of memory on which there are words addressed from the dead to the living.

According to the surviving documents, it is known that there were 26 houses in Khatyn. Architects erected a log cabin crown on the site of the destroyed house, inside it there is an obelisk resembling a chimney in shape, which has a bell on top. The silence of the Khatyn field is broken not only by the singing of birds, but also by the hourly reminder of the bell about the terrible events of March 22, 1943.

The symbolic village is followed by the Burnt Village Cemetery. It was there that the urns with land from 185 unrestored villages were delivered. A Wall of Sorrow was erected not far from them. It is made of reinforced concrete block with peculiar niches. They placed commemorative plates with the names of the largest death camps and places where people died in large numbers.

In place of the fourth birch, an eternal flame burns. Nearby is the "Tree of Life", on which is a list of 433 villages. Once they were burned by the invaders. However, after the war they were rebuilt anew.

In 2004, the monument was reconstructed.





Khatyn memorial complex: how to get there?

To visit this historic place, nothing complicated is done. It is enough to contact the tour desk and purchase a ticket. The advantage of this trip will be that you don‚Äôt have to get bored all the way, because the guide will talk about events and battles on the lands of Belarus.

There is another way - a trip in a personal car. In this case, you should go on the M3 highway (Minsk-Vitebsk). Having passed through it, at the 54th kilometer you will come across a sign with the name of the Khatyn complex. Follow it, after 5 km you will reach the place of memory and sorrow.

The Khatyn memorial complex has no address familiar to us. You should be guided as if you are looking for a settlement.





Mode of operation

Just remember that the day off is Monday. For those who wish to attend the photo documentary exposition, it is necessary to take into account that it opens at 10:30 and ends its work at 16:00.

Excursion service for visitors begins at the same time, but ends an hour earlier, that is, at 15:00.

An entrance ticket for schoolchildren and students costs 5 Belarusian rubles. Tickets for the remaining categories of the population - 8 rubles.
