Adapters for SIM cards: convert a micro SIM card to a standard

The familiar SIM card is changing - more and more devices are starting to require micro SIM instead of the usual mini size, which has been used for many years. Trimming a mini-format card to a micro size is not too complicated, but adapters are required to return to the previous setting.





In addition, if you are not careful and try to do the cropping yourself (and then insert the cropped card into the previous shape), you risk damaging the thin connectors on your phone.

Simka's story

The Subscriber Identity Module (SIM) card is used in mobile phones as a memory chip that contains the data necessary for authentication in a mobile network. Early cards only had storage from 16 KB to 64 KB, which was enough to copy authentication data, mobile data settings, as well as some contacts and text messages. More advanced features were introduced with the advent of multimedia messaging. Today, some SIM chips with the same connection format may have integrated microprocessors for added functionality. Modern developers are working to add even Near Field Communication (NFC) to the SIM card chip.

Segment and Card Size

The original SIM card format was exactly the same size as bank cards. Very early mobile phones really used these relatively large SIM cards. What we call SIM cards today is actually in mini format. When you buy a new "SIM card", it may be located inside the plastic form of the old model and be subject to ejection from it. Presumably, this is done either to simplify production or to maintain backward compatibility. Nowadays, it is increasingly possible to find that operators implement micro-SIM in a similar mini format.





SIM card cropping to other formats

Cutting the card to the right size is not a big problem. There is a special cutter for "sims", which allows you to do this most accurately. Much more difficulties begin when you need to use micro on a mini device. For this, there are various SIM card adapters on the market.

Cross compatibility

Most modern smartphones have a tightly mounted SIM card slot, rather than a swivel holder. Some models go even further (for example, Nokia N8) - they have a push-to-eject system. For this reason, such gadgets have very limited space in which the card must stand.

Because of this, poor-quality adapters can easily get stuck on the contact pins and cause damage when you try to remove them. For this reason, it is not recommended to make an adapter for a SIM card with your own hands.

What adapters are on sale?

Two types of adapters are available today. The first is called Outline, and it is just a piece of plastic in the form of a mini-SIM with a hole for micro-SIM. They usually come with an adhesive label to keep the SIM in place.

Adapters for SIM cards of the second type are called Backed. They differ from Outline in the presence of a thin plastic wall on one side of the space with a round hole in the center.
