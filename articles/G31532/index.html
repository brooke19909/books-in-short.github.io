Ancient Greek god Deimos: mythological and serial

Who is Deimos? One of the gods of ancient Greece, terrifying mortals. This is a mythology. According to the series "The Amazing Wanderings of Hercules," Deimos is a blonde type with a nasty laugh.

In this article, we will consider both characters: both mythological and the one that the series talks about.

According to myths

Deimos is the god of horrors. The bloodthirsty son of the god of war Ares and the beautiful goddess of love Aphrodite. Mythology indicates that Ares with Aphrodite is a brother and sister. Accordingly, we are dealing with closely related inbreeding or, in other words, incest. What could be the result? From an illegal union an unpredictable creature was born - Deimos. And according to mythology, he had a twin brother - Phobos, personifying fear.

Deimos had a normal appearance. But the character is such a horror. No wonder the guy was awarded the title of god of horrors on Olympus.

What was the god of horror Deimos, judging by myths? The result of the madness of war and the madness of love. Of course, nothing good could be there. Absolutely unpredictable god. It was impossible to predict what to expect from him in a few minutes.

The bloodthirstiness of Deimos deserves a separate topic. He accompanied his father in all battles and was not inferior to him in the thirst for blood and the ability to wield a sword. Deimos killed for the sake of excitement, hunting instincts worked very well for him. He enjoyed as long as the victim resisted. As soon as she stopped resistance, the god of horror lost interest in her and began to look for a new one.

The main function of the god Deimos was to sow horror and confusion. He took on great responsibility, generously irrigating the earth with blood. As mentioned above, accompanied Ares in the battles. Together with him on the battlefield were Brother Phobos, who was responsible for fear, the goddess Eris - the main by contention on Olympus and Enio - the goddess of fierce war, the eternal companion of Ares.
