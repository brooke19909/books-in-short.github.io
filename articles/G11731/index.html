Budva Riviera (Montenegro): attractions, reviews of tourists

In the central part of Montenegro is the Budva Riviera. It is the sunniest place in Europe and on the Adriatic. Milocer and Budva, Petrovac and Sveti Stefan, Rafailovici and Przhno, Rezevici and Drobnichi, Bulyaritsa and Krstac - these resorts are part of the region.

Budva Riviera (Montenegro) is a popular and world-famous tourist center. It is famous for its beautiful beaches, cultural and historical monuments, which surprisingly harmoniously fit into modern city landscapes. The Budva Riviera occupies a little more than 38 kilometers of the sea coast, which is distinguished by a large number of bays, bays, small-pebble and sandy beaches. The depth of the sea in the coastal zone reaches thirty-five meters. The water temperature in summer does not exceed +25 degrees.



Flora and fauna

The Budva Riviera (Montenegro) is characterized by rich flora and fauna. In the waters of the bay there are such fish as dorada, catfish, brancin and others. Many exotic plants grow here - cacti and palm trees, mimosa and agave. Actively fruit on this fertile land of tangerines and olives, oranges and pomegranates.

Holidays for everyone

The Budva Riviera is an amazing place where everyone can choose a vacation to their liking. Fans of active, noisy and fun pastimes should go to Budva, where the fun lasts day and night. Those who want to relax peacefully with their families will benefit from the small and quiet towns of Becici and Petrovac. It is very calm here, in addition, these places are characterized by a mild and comfortable climate for children.

Tourists, for whom status is important and who are not afraid of expenses, can go to the island-hotel "Sveti Stefan". Those who wish can settle in the luxurious hotel "Milocer" - the former royal residence.

Families traveling on vacation with teenagers should go to the city of Rafailovichi - they will not be bored here. There are many attractions, game centers, youth clubs, so parents with children 10-15 years old like to relax here. The Budva Riviera (Montenegro), the photo of which you can see in our article, does not differ in great distances - you can get to neighboring resorts very quickly. Therefore, during your stay in Montenegro you will be able to get acquainted with many of its sights - take a walk along the quiet streets of the Old Town in Budva, enjoy a vacation in the royal park in Milocer and enjoy amazing fish dishes in the restaurants of Rafailovici.

Budva Riviera Attractions

I must say that the resort cities of this region themselves are attractions. Original architecture, magnificent nature, clean air - all this attracts tourists. But there are also interesting historical and architectural monuments that need to be seen. We will talk about some of them in our article.

Praskvitsa Monastery

An old legend says that the Praskvitsa monastery appeared on this land in 1050. At that time, the old Slavic state of Zeta, which was located in these places, was led by Stefan Voislav.

The ancient monastic cloister got its name because of a curious natural phenomenon. In spring, when streams flow from the mountains, the air is filled with the aroma of peaches. In the local dialect, praske means "peach."



Unfortunately, in its original form the church has not been preserved to this day. In 1812, it was destroyed by French troops, and the monastery was looted. The new building was erected in the 19th century. During the design of the new church fragments of fresco paintings of the old building were used, which miraculously survived. The main painting of the church was performed by the famous Greek icon painter Nicola Aspioti.

Praskvitsa monastery was once assisted by Russian sovereigns. Documents from the Russian Imperial Court are still preserved in the vaults.

The monastery complex has one small temple - the church of the Holy Trinity. Its creation dates back to the 17th century. Toplitz Palace

At the end of the 19th century, the magnificent residence of Peter Karageorgievich, the son-in-law of the Montenegrin Tsar Nikola I, appeared in the New Bar. The magnificent palace complex was later named Toplitsa. It includes the Big and Small palaces, two gardens - a winter and a botanical. Around the palace is a park.



Today, the museum of local lore and various exhibition halls work in the premises of the palaces. In the expositions you can see historical, archaeological and ethnographic collections. They sufficiently familiarize visitors with the history of Montenegro.

Castio Fortress

The Budva Riviera has very ancient historical monuments on its territory. To see one of them, you need to go to the small town of Petrovac. In its architecture, it resembles an amphitheater, since numerous villas, mini-hotels and cottages are built from the very top of the slope to the sea.

Castio Fortress is located on the city beach, in its northern part. At the top of the building today is a memorial. In it you can see a commemorative plaque dedicated to the fallen soldiers of Montenegro and Petrovar during the Second World War.

St. Nicholas Island

The island received its name in honor of the small medieval church located here. It was built in the 16th century. Apart from this church and the ancient graves surrounding it, there is nothing on the island. It is completely uninhabited. Local researchers believe that in these graves rest the participants of the crusades, who died during the terrible plague epidemic.



The island of St. Nicholas is very picturesque. Its entire territory is covered with evergreen vegetation. Various animals and birds live here. Perhaps that is why in recent years people who are tired of noise and fuss prefer to relax here. Here you can retire to small but very cozy sandy beaches.
