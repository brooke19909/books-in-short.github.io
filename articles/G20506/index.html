VSK: life insurance rules

Life insurance is a classic type of service, which includes far from one variety. This category can be understood as:

  1. Survival insurance. Money is paid when clients survive to a certain age or until the expiration of the contract.
  2. Mixed type. It covers two risks, namely death and survival to a specific time period.
  3. Child insurance provides payments to adulthood or to any particular event. As a rule, it includes financial protection from certain accidents.
  4. Urgent insurance. This is the simplest and, at the same time, cheapest form that does not include an accumulation element.

Subjects of the insurance contract: insurer, policyholder, insured person or several, as well as the beneficiary or several.

Policyholder Rights:

  • the contract can be terminated early;
  • its conditions can be changed by agreement with the insurer;
  • the policyholder is also entitled to receive a duplicate of the agreement (insurance policy) if loss occurs;
  • You can replace the beneficiary with the written consent of the insured person.

Policyholder Responsibilities:

  • insurance premiums must be paid;
  • documents confirming the payment of the insurance premium must be kept;
  • in any way possible to objectively record the fact of the appeal, to notify the insurer within 50 calendar days of the occurrence of the insured event (death, temporary disability or disability of the insured person);
  • inform the insurer in writing of significant changes in circumstances that could significantly affect the increase in insurance risk.

Rights of the insurer:

  • refuse insurance payment if events occurred as a result of activities associated with increased risk;
  • upon notification of circumstances leading to an increase in insurance risk, demand a change in the terms of the insurance contract or payment of an additional insurance premium.

Obligations of the insurer:

  • the insurance policy must be issued within the prescribed period;
  • insurance payment must be made within 20 business days from the date of receipt of all necessary documents.

Obligations of the insured person:

  • notification of the insurer within 50 (fifty) calendar days of temporary disability or disability, as well as the provision as soon as possible of all necessary information and supporting documents;
  • strict observance of the rules of life insurance in VSK.

Personal insurance is either compulsory or voluntary. Unlike the first type, the second form is carried out solely on the basis of an agreement between the client and the organization. The most popular in our country are those varieties of services that are characterized by a savings option: it implies child protection, mixed life insurance, the acquisition of a policy for a wedding or other important event in life.

Features of combined insurance

In international insurance law, the complex type of such services is called Comprehensive Insurance. The essence of this product is to combine various options within one policy:

  1. Several types of insurance (personal, property and so on).
  2. Different risk groups.
  3. Levels of protection at various stages of application of the insured item.

The most successful examples are the CASCO and CARGO programs. In the first case, the monetary coverage extends to the car itself, the passengers in it, the driver, as well as to the interests of third parties. As a result, comprehensive motor vehicle insurance may include:

  1. Protection against breakage.
  2. Passenger accident insurance.
  3. Car driver liability policy.
  4. Insurance of auxiliary equipment (for example, electronics).

Under certain clauses, clients may provide for various threats from which the transport will be insured. For example, the machine itself can be protected from minor damage, from fires, accidents or even theft. Electronics in the cabin, respectively, from its abduction or breakdown as a result of an accident.

CARGO Features

The second example is CARGO, which provides comprehensive cargo insurance. It already includes not types of insurance, but takes into account the stages of the subject of the service, which it passes as part of the movement to the recipient. This takes into account:

  1. Risk at the stage of loading and unloading.
  2. The threat of product spoilage along the road (on which it is transported).
  3. Risk factor during storage of goods at a transshipment point.
  4. The CARGO coverage also extends to the installation stages of transported equipment.

Principles of Combined Agreement

The aim of the development of this product is comprehensive insurance of property, liability, cargo or vehicles. Thus, all relevant risks are combined into one agreement on three principles: legality, individualization and wholesale prices. In short, such an agreement is easier to draw up, it works better to protect the interests of the client and is much cheaper.
