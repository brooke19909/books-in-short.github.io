Holidays in Austria: features, recommendations and interesting facts

Holidays in Austria will be a good decision for several reasons. In this country, the infrastructure and conditions for tourism, active and recreational recreation are ideally developed. Austrian nature is incredibly beautiful, numerous mountain lakes are crystal clear and clean. Even in the smallest town, hotels invariably provide excellent service for their guests. The cuisine here is excellent. An extremely extensive and well-developed public transport network, an impeccably arranged system for renting cars and bicycles, which, combined with the highest quality roads, allows you to freely travel around the country.

In Austria, amazing opportunities have been created to spend a beautiful vacation in an unforgettable atmosphere both in winter and in summer, for single travelers, couples or families. The main thing is to choose your vacation option and think about what places I would like to visit.

Types of rest

Austria is a paradise of alpine tourism. Numerous ski resorts will offer pistes of different difficulty levels. Winter activities in selected mountainous areas can be extended until May. In summer, these places are preferred by hiking enthusiasts. For such an active type of vacation in Austria, many marked trails have been developed, also with different levels of difficulty.

The same excellent conditions are provided for cyclists. An incredible number of routes have been laid in the country for family companies and trained athletes. You can choose any bike track if you wish, or spend your holidays in Austria on the shores of one of the many mountain lakes. Another type of relaxation that is acceptable at any time of the year is the numerous balneological and spa resorts that are highly valued by Europeans.

Active winter holidays

Many people prefer to spend holidays in Austria in the winter. Of course, the country where the most popular sport is skiing, and whose athletes are the most successful skiers in the world, and the slopes, jumps, lifts are best suited for this sport, was simply created to spend winter holidays in it. There is another indisputable advantage of the Austrian Alps: the snow here is suitable for sports for a long time, even after the end of the main ski season. Hintertux

Hintertux is the only Austrian resort that offers good snow conditions for all 365 days of the year. The glacier will satisfy even the most demanding skiers. There are almost 90 kilometers of tracks of excellent quality. Hintertux is a paradise for snowboarders and boasts the highest arched springboard in Europe. Here you can see the natural ice palace Natur Eis Palast with giant icicles and interesting ice formations. Hintertux is also known for its Apres skis and the Betterpark summer snow park, open from April to December.

Cycling tourism

After skiing, cycling is the most popular summer vacation in Austria. The network of rental points and routes for cyclists is impeccably developed here. Such points are in all tourist towns and at each railway station, where, having rented a bicycle, you can rent it at any other station.

Upper Austria is the dream of cyclists. Here you can ride comfortably among lakes and rivers. Bikers prefer routes through beautiful alpine valleys, skirting picturesque pastures. And the track along the Danube is a proven classic. The Upper Austrian section of the cycling route runs from Passau to Green and is about 150 kilometers away. Hiking

Hiking in the mountains is a relatively safe, but demanding sport that requires prior preparation. First of all, you should evaluate your capabilities and, according to them, choose a route.

Almost all areas of Austria offer a variety of destinations with standardized marking of trails indicated on tourist maps. Blue color indicates a safe, well-groomed track, suitable for beginners. Red color usually characterizes rocky mountainous terrain with steep climbs and, in many places, steel ropes. Among the red marked trails, there are no really dangerous places, but some skill in alpine crossings is necessary. Black routes - for experienced tourists. These are long rocky hills that require a lot of experience and the right equipment. When choosing your ideal trail, two more factors should be considered: the physical condition of all team members and weather conditions that can be specified in advance.

One of the most beautiful routes is the Waldviertel of Lower Austria, which is suitable for relaxation and easy walks. The area is almost flat, with a beautiful view of the surrounding mountains and granite rock formations. Its culmination is the famous Ysperklamm Gorge, which, in fact, is a 2-kilometer forest abyss with waterfalls. Holidays on the lakes

In summer, Austria is especially beautiful with its pristine lakes, which many tourists prefer to sea beaches. In Carinthia, the dominant part of the landscape is the cleanest lakes with warm summer water. All the waters of this region offer many sports and recreation. Werther See is one of the most popular and largest lake in Carinthia.

Another magical place is Lake Zeller See in Salzburg, with two famous ski resorts on the shore - Kaprun and Zell am See, which are among the centers with the most thoughtful organization of health and sports events. In addition to many advantages, the attractions of the Zell am See resort include the Grossglockner Strasses panoramic alpine road and paragliding flights, which give a relaxing experience in the mountains of Austria. The mountain village of Kaprun is located in the northern foothills of the year-round ski resort - the Kitzsteinhorn Glacier. The water of Lake Zeller See is drinking, there are several beaches along the banks, playgrounds, conditions for lake cruises, windsurfing and other water sports. The water temperature ranges from mid-May to September within +22 ... 24 degrees.

Spa

Austria is known for its thermal springs, which became the basis of its popular spa centers. Many Europeans come to rest in Austria not only for the sake of beautiful alpine nature and sports, but also for water relaxing, therapeutic and cosmetic procedures. After all, Austrian thermal springs are well known for their healing properties and medical indications in a number of diseases of the skin, internal organs and nervous system.
