If you do not care what season things to buy, there is time for shopping, design is not very important, then the answer is clear: go to some outlet in Rome for shopping. The largest outlet in Rome - Castel Romano

This is one of the most important and largest outlets in Italy, owned by McArthurGlen Group, whose similar facilities are available in almost all European countries. The emphasis is placed on the things of famous fashion designers and designers, but, thanks to its purpose, all products here are sold at very substantial discounts - from 30 to 70 percent. As you can see, the benefits are unequivocal. The size of the discount itself depends on whether the item is from the last collection or from the latest. The tax-free feature also works, which allows you to return another part of the money upon returning home.

This outlet was opened in Rome in 2003, it is located at a distance of 25 kilometers from the city center, occupies 25,000 square meters of space, and parking is designed for 2,000 parking spaces. Nevertheless, the main advantage of Castel Romano is its 113 boutiques of leading Italian and world brands such as Calvin Klein, Reebok, Puma, Dolce & Gabbana, Roberto Cavalli, Diesel, Patrizia Pepe, Nike, Pal Zileri, Ermenegildo Zegna, La Perla and many others. Here people of all ages and both sexes will find their thing. The choice is really huge, and the product is no different from what is sold in boutiques in the city center, only here it is half the price. You can buy everything: clothes, electronics, accessories, leather products, cosmetics and perfumes, gifts, household goods and so on. The only problems are with finding shoes of a specific size. Discounts in season can reach fantastic 90%.





Castel Romano working hours, how to get to it

This outlet works from 10.00 to 20.00 from Monday to Thursday and from 10.00 to 21.00 from Friday to Sunday. There are several ways to get to it:

  1. By car. Take the ring road to exit 26, continue along highway 148 to the exit for Pontina - Castel Romano.
  2. You can order a shopping guide with a car, or call a taxi.
  3. Shuttle buses run along the desired route. The first route is from a stop on Marsala Street, next to Termini Railway Station, the second - from a stop near Risorgimento Square, on Via Leone. Round trip costs 13 euros for adults and 8 euros for children 14-18 years old. Tickets are sold by the driver or can be bought at the box office; booking is not necessary in advance. Trains do not run to Castel Romano.

Assortment of Roman Outlets - Lucky or Not?





What can be said here? Everyone who goes shopping in these shopping centers should remember that luck is the case here, like a mushroom picker when going to the forest. The fact that you visited an outlet in Rome does not guarantee that you will return from there happy. The more shopaholics there will be with you there, the more likely it is that the assortment of a certain product of the right size can be both large and small. So if you want to have a good choice, then go to a little-known outlet where there will not be such an influx of buyers. In addition to the assortment, you will also guess at prices.

Some tips for shopaholics

Many of our compatriots over the years have visited outlets in Rome. Reviews are very diverse: both enthusiastic and negative. After listening to them, smart travelers made their conclusions on how not to miscalculate and leave the shopping center as satisfied as possible. First, choose outlets unknown to the general public. Secondly, try to arrive in Rome when there are fewer tourists there - not during the holiday season. Third, set aside more time for shopping. Fourth, try to shop with consultants who already know everything here. Enjoy the shopping!
