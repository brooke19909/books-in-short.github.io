The best hotels of Corfu: description and reviews of tourists

Corfu is the romantic and mysterious name of the Greek island. It has attracted attention since ancient times, and many states wanted to take over the city.

The history of Corfu is inextricably linked with the Greek gods. It is believed that the population of the island are descendants of the ancient Greek gods. According to legends, Poseidon (the ruler of the seas) stole the nymph Kerkyra and settled here with her. Later they had a son, Feak. Initially, the island was named after the nymph Kerkyra, and now it is Corfu.





A little about Corfu

In different eras, power over the island belonged to various states: there were periods of Venetian, French, English, and even Russian-Turkish rule. All this, of course, left its mark on the cultural and architectural heritage of Corfu. In 1864, the city joined Greece.

The waters of the Ionian Sea wash Corfu. Beautiful beaches with turquoise waters, villages with quiet streets in the Venetian style, orange trees, olive groves, clean air - this is only a fraction of what can be seen on the island. In addition to the natural beauties of Corfu, you can admire the monuments of culture and architecture of different eras, monasteries and castles.

What to see on Corfu?

The list of island attractions is simply huge. But there are places that you should definitely visit. This is a new and old fortress, a palace that once belonged to the Austrian queen, the observation deck of Emperor Wilhelm and much more. More about attractions:

  1. New and old fortress of Corfu . The old fortress was built by the Venetians in the 15th century. The most impressive places in the old fortress are the chapel of Madonna Carmini and the Church of St. George. Well, and, of course, you will get unforgettable impressions from the view from the top of the fortress: the Ionian Sea in all its glory and the island of Corfu stretching before your eyes! The new fortress is no less interesting. It is located on Cape St. Mark. The fortress is connected with the city and the Old Fortress by ancient underground passages.
  2. Achilleion Palace . The palace belonged to the Empress of Austria Elizabeth. Here preserved items of furniture and interior of the imperial family. Around the palace are beautiful lush gardens. It overlooks the whole of Corfu.
  3. You can visit an unusual museum in the village of Benitses. This is a private museum of sea shells. There are stuffed crab, lobster, sea urchins. The cost of some exhibits-shells exceeds several thousand dollars.
  4. Kaiser's Throne . A special observation deck built for Emperor Wilhelm. From this site you can see in detail all the attractions of Corfu.
  5. The fortress of angels . This place is also called Angelokastro. Previously, guards were on duty around the clock to prevent the invasion of enemies.
  6. Mount Pantocrator . Its height is 906 meters. From here you can see Albania, and in clear weather even Italy, despite the fact that the distance to them is about 500 kilometers in a straight line!
  7. Inspired by legends, another place in Corfu is Mouse Island . According to legend, he was once a ship of the Odyssey. When Odysseus returned, Poseidon became angry and turned the ship into stone.

The listed attractions are just a small fraction of what you can visit on the island.

Corfu Hotel Prices

Holidays on the island of Corfu are among the elite. Despite this housing you can find for every taste and budget. The cost of budget options will range from 23-33 euros per day.
