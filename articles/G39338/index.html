Some recommendations for operating conditions

Well, so how to connect a washing machine to the water supply? Well, let's get down to describing this process.

First of all, we note that the normal operation of this equipment is possible only under the condition of a constant and uninterrupted supply of cold water under normal pressure. Most household washing machines are not designed to be connected to a hot water supply system, and therefore we will not talk about such cases.

Actually, the connection to the water supply must be carried out using a special hose, which always comes with the machine you purchased. If he simply does not reach the nearest riser with water, visit specialized stores: there are a lot of models of almost any length.

Important! Do not immediately grab a hose of maximum length: first read the manufacturer's recommendations. In low-cost machines, sometimes it‚Äôs far from the most reliable and powerful pump, which can not cope with the load when pumping water.

We connect to the car and water supply





One end of the hose is attached to the machine itself, and you will definitely not have problems with this, since there is a special fitting in it. FUM tape does not need to be used, since the connection in any case is very tight. As for the direct connection to the water supply system, this can be done in several ways at once.

The best and easiest way is to connect the washing machine to the water supply, when the plumbers have already provided for this purpose a separate adapter. In this case, you only need a crane and a supply of FUM tape. Pay attention to the quality of all parts, as builders often manage to save on cheap items.

Cast iron pipes

The most difficult thing is with metal water pipes, since in this case you will have to embed a tap in them, which for beginners will probably be difficult. To do this, you need to purchase a crane, as well as a special bandage. Please note that before tapping it into the water supply system, it is necessary to shut off the water. Having drilled a hole, put a bandage on it, tighten the clamps, and then attach a crane to the resulting structure. You hook the washing machine to its second exit. There is also an option with a crane for three exits.





If the machine is in the toilet, it is best to hook the connecting tap to the inlet of the toilet flush tank. Important! In no case should you break the hose, twist and bend it. All compounds are carefully insulated with FUM tape or something similar.

Since it is your and the neighbor‚Äôs safety in terms of protection against the flood that depends on the crane, you should not save on the quality of products by buying cheap Chinese fakes. It is best to buy taps of proven brands made of brass.

Alas, few people think about how to connect the washing machine to the sewer. Typically, the hose is attached using the plastic bracket that comes with the machine to the edge of the tub or sink. In the toilet, its end is simply sent to the toilet. All this is not too right from the point of view of professional plumbers, since the drains of the same shower stall or even the bath can clog very quickly from the used water, which is full of different fibers and dirt.

Important notes

It is much better and more correct to use special adapters for this purpose, connected directly to the sewer system. It is only necessary to buy a suitable siphon and fix it, not forgetting about some rules.

Important! Be sure to place the siphon only over (!) The curved sections of the sink siphons, since in this case your sense of smell will be reliably protected from far from pleasant smells from the sewer. In no case can you cut the hose into the sewer risers directly: you are not only guaranteed the smell of wastewater inside the machine and the bathroom, but also the effect of the pump, when the water pumped inside the machine will be drained into the sewer at the same time.

Any Indesit washing machine is especially sensitive to this. The manual says that in most models there is simply no valve that would prevent this phenomenon.
