Crocheted Asterisk: Pattern and Description

The hook allows you to create patterns of extraordinary beauty, thanks to which any products acquire a special effect and charm. One of these patterns is the Asterisk. It allows you to create a magnificent canvas - quite dense, voluminous, as if woven from a thousand stars.





If you still do not know how to knit this interesting motive - this article is for you! In it, we will tell you how to perform an "Asterisk" crochet. The pattern design will be simple and understandable for masters with little work experience. Using this motive, you can create original and stylish products - handbags, clutches, bedspreads, blankets for newborns.

Beautiful crocheted asterisk pattern. Scheme and job description

This original, textured pattern is actually quite easy to perform, you just need to practice a little. To create a pattern, take any yarn and a hook. We start crocheting the "Star" pattern. The operation scheme is presented in the figure below.





First of all, let's pick a chain of air loops (any number, depending on the tasks, but a multiple of three, plus 1 VP lift). Next, we knit one row with single crochet. We perform three air loops and begin to knit the stars themselves directly using unfinished columns. We introduce the hook into the second and third air loops, grab the threads, but do not knit. Then we introduce the hook in turn - into the first, second and third columns of the previous row, each time pulling the thread. Now we knit 6 loops on the hook together. We complete the unfinished column with an air loop. The first starlet is ready.





We continue to work on the sample

If you carefully look at the pattern, you can see that our asterisk consists of rays that converge in the central loop. Now we introduce the hook into the third column of the base (into which we knit the fifth ray of the first star), grab the thread. Finally, we introduce the hook into the fourth and fifth loops of the base, pull the thread and knit all six loops together. We complete the element with an air loop. By the same principle, we carry out all the stars with a hook. The scheme of the pattern of the third row: we perform columns without a crochet, one each in the air loops and the center of the stars.

The fourth row - we knit with unfinished columns, just like the second. Next, alternate rows. We get an unusually beautiful pattern of "Stars" crocheted. The scheme and principle of operation should now be clear.

Motive "Asterisk". Decorative element in the piggy bank of your ideas

Beautiful canvases with a starry pattern can be made from individual parts. To do this, knit the required number of stars, connecting them, for example, with single crochet columns or with the help of a sirloin net.





How is the "Asterisk" crocheted? The pattern is not complicated, the job description too! We start with a chain of 5 air loops and arrange it into a ring. We knit the first row like this: we perform 7 VP, then 7 columns with two crochets, between which we perform 3 VP. We connect a number of loops. Second row: we carry out 3 VP lifting and 39 columns with 1 yarn.

In the third row, we need to create eight crochet rays of our starlet. The pattern is as follows: we perform 1 loop of lifting and 1 single crochet. Next, we knit 6 air loops. In the second loop we perform a single crochet, in the third - a half crochet with a crochet, in the fourth - a crochet with 1 crochet, in the fifth - a crochet with 2, and in the sixth - with 3 crochets. The first beam is ready, by analogy we knit another 7 elements.





We complete the motif with a connecting loop, fasten and cut the thread. Ready our smart little star. Of these beautiful motifs, you can make a cute children's blanket, bedspread or sofa pillow.
