What to visit in Crimea? Sights of Crimea

Rest on the exotic Crimean peninsula, undoubtedly, causes unforgettable emotions for every tourist. If you approach the planning of vacations in advance and decide what to visit in the Crimea, then the pleasant experience of staying in this wonderful place will increase many times over. Each village and city of this paradise has its own special and unique sights. Interesting places in the Crimea can be not only in the well-known promoted resorts, but also in modest quiet towns and even sometimes on wild, little-trodden paths.

Architectural monuments of Crimea

The architectural monuments of the Crimean peninsula, with which it is very rich, are perfectly preserved to this day and attract tourists of all ages. It is simply impossible to ignore these historic sites. The palaces and castles of Crimea are beautiful and unique. Resting on the South Bank, it is not difficult to decide what to visit in Crimea, because basically the most famous palaces are in the vicinity.

Crimean palaces

The Dulber Palace of Grand Duke Peter Romanov, which is located in Miskhor, the palace of Count Vorontsov in Alupka, the unique castle on the rock "Swallow's Nest" in Gaspra - all these places are visiting cards of the peninsula.





The equally well-known Yusupov estate in Koreiz not only accepts excursions, but also makes it possible to spend your vacation in old apartments that once hosted members of the royal family, President Roosevelt, Prime Minister Churchill and many other famous personalities.

The romantic Massandra Palace resembles a fabulous tower of children's books and is very loved by tourists. Palace of Prince Golitsyn called "Yasnaya Polyana", which is now a sanatorium, is also very interesting and worthy of attention.

When deciding what to visit in Crimea, palaces and castles should be paid special attention, because there is no such beauty anywhere.

Livadia Palace

South Coast - this is perhaps the most chic and exotic resort on the entire Black Sea coast. The family of the Russian emperor also appreciated this corner, therefore, at the very beginning of the 20th century, in the sunny Livadia, surrounded by the Crimean mountains and beautiful parks, the imperial palace, famous today, was built.





Livadia Palace is a real architectural masterpiece. Millions of tourists from all over the world year-round seek to visit this place. The summer residence of the Romanov family is well preserved, the palace is built of majestic white stone and fits perfectly into the surrounding nature. Excursion to the Livadia Palace will appeal to everyone, without exception. The unforgettable atmosphere of Tsarist times is passed on to visitors, and competent guides who are in love with this palace will tell all the secrets and stories that this beautiful place keeps.

Caves of Crimea

Choosing what to visit in Crimea, you should definitely pay attention to natural creations - caves of Crimea. The amazing underworld of the peninsula is diverse and unique. According to some estimates, there are about a thousand caves in Crimea, however, only some of them are equipped for tourists.

The largest in size and very interesting cave is Red, so named after the characteristic red-brown shade of iron oxide, in which nature painted it.





It is the largest in the Crimea, and part of it is equipped for visits. The excursion route lasts about 500 meters, many unusual places, bizarre streaks and formations, five halls and unusual beauty underground waterfalls open to the gaze of tourists.

"Chatyr-Dag Melodies"

The Chatyr-Dag massif is one of the most interesting places. The most famous cave that is equipped for visiting tourists is the Marble Cave. It was opened relatively recently, and today a huge number of guests and residents of the Crimea have the opportunity to admire the beauties of the underworld year-round.

The marble cave was named because it is located in marble limestone rocks. This cave was created by water, which penetrated here for millions of years. Inside, the Marble Cave looks like a frozen ice palace.





Five unusually beautiful halls will open to the eyes of visitors, and the tour is accompanied by magnificent music that sounds in the underground kingdom in a special way beautifully and exquisitely. ‚ÄúChatyr-Dag Melodies‚Äù is a trip to the Marble Cave, which is definitely worth a visit for everyone.

In addition to these caves, there are other interesting places in the Crimea, which are no less attractive and beautiful.

Cableway to Ai-Petri Mountain

The natural beauties and monuments of the peninsula deserve special attention, because they are so diverse and unique that after visiting them you want to return again and again to this paradise on earth - the Crimea.

What is a must-visit and first of all, everyone decides for himself, but there are places that are especially popular.





The cable car to Mount Ai-Petri is one of such places that all tourists seek to visit. This is a kind of path between heaven and earth. The cable car to the highest point of Crimea is unique, and there are no analogues in the world.

At the very top of the mountain range, tourists enjoy breathtaking views of the entire southern coast of Crimea.

Excursions with children in Crimea

Parents with children in Crimea will not be bored. A huge number of places and excursions are organized specifically for the younger generation. The most favorite excursion route is the Yalta Zoo and the Glade of Fairy Tales.

More than 100 species of animals and birds live and breed in the zoo, in spacious enclosures. The zoo is located in a mountain gorge, stunning nature opens from all angles.

The fairy-tale heroes of the Glade of fairy tales are no less interesting and are waiting for the kids to visit them year-round.

Numerous water parks, dolphinariums, and amusement parks of Crimea are also very beloved and visited by both adult tourists and children.

Museums of Crimea

In addition to natural architectural monuments, the Crimean peninsula is rich in museums. The beauties of Crimea inspired the work of poets and artists who created their masterpieces while in this heavenly place.

For example, the house-museum of Anton Pavlovich Chekhov in Yalta and his cottage in Gurzuf are loved and visited by many tourists - connoisseurs of the works of this writer.

The National Art Gallery named after I.K. Aivazovsky in Feodosia preserves the memory of the best marine painter in the world and presents to all art lovers magnificent works by the artist.





The gallery was opened during his lifetime, in the 80 of the nineteenth century, and today is a unique collection of paintings and other arts, more than 13 thousand copies.

Thousands of tourists come to see the unique underwater museum in Balaklava. The museum was created on the basis of a submarine repair plant located in a mountain 120 meters above sea level. Underground structures are well preserved, historical expositions about the Navy of the USSR are created, which are very interesting.

Various expositions created in various parts of the Crimea can be no less exciting and fascinating.

The sights of the Crimean peninsula can be listed endlessly. On the west bank, there are other charms, on the east - other, and the South Coast is generally a storehouse of virtues. Seascapes, mountain landscapes, monuments of architecture and art, historical museums and other places that Crimea is rich in - all this is very interesting, informative, beautiful and attractive.
