Best Cell Phone for Seniors: Ranking

A telephone for the elderly is more a necessity than a whim. Unfortunately, the reality is that keeping in touch with dear elderly relatives in our busy time is really a big problem, and just giving them your old gadget is not the best idea. Why? What is a grandmother? What kind of phone should an elderly person buy?

Why you need a special gadget

Some people think that giving the old one their old telephone is a reasonable idea. But, unfortunately, this is not so and there are 4 reasons for this:

  1. Functional. Often in our old devices there are too many unnecessary functions for the elderly person, the purpose of which sometimes we ourselves do not know.
  2. The quality of the gadget. Previously, phones with a small screen or small buttons were popular. Older people do not have such outstanding vision that they can easily master such devices. And they buy a new phone due to the fact that the old one is not working so well.
  3. Sound. Often, older people have hearing impairments, and the gadgets that they are given to them no longer have such a powerful speaker.
  4. Aesthetics. Of course, getting a phone is nice. But much more joy will bring the idea that he is new. And do not think that the elderly do not care much, because their age is just a figure in the passport.

Based on these aspects, the so-called grandmothers appeared in the world. What is it?

Taking care of the elderly

Grandma‚Äôs phone is a special phone for the elderly. It has a fairly large screen and buttons. Moreover, such gadgets are usually designed to make calls, send messages and a limited number of functions (calendar, clock, calculator and so on).

Handling such phones is very simple. They do not have unnecessary functions and are intuitive. But how to choose the right model?

Phone Pickup Tips

Before settling on a particular phone model for older people, you need to know what criteria it must meet.

  1. Functionality. This concept means not only making calls, but also sending messages, viewing time and date. Many older people would like to have a flashlight at their side, and possibly a source of music.
  2. Ergonomics The phone should be comfortable in your hand. It should not slip out or be too small / huge.
  3. Large keyboard. It is not so easy for elderly people to get to the small buttons, so the latter should be massive, with clearly drawn characters.
  4. Quality display. It is better that the screen is not only large, but also with high-quality color reproduction. Some people prefer to buy black and white gadgets, as they have higher contrast.
  5. Sound is loud enough. This applies not only to the main speaker, thanks to which a person hears a call. The speaker should also be loud enough to hear what they say on the other side of the device.
  6. Battery. It is important to choose a model that would have to be charged as rarely as possible, so that in a critical situation an elderly person always has a connection.
  7. SOS button. The presence of this button is often the main difference from other devices. It is useful if a person feels bad. In order to dial a loved one, just click on the big button on the phone.
  8. Simple menu. A mobile phone for the elderly does not have to be complicated. It is just a means of communication and nothing more.

Of course, some modern elderly people are good at navigating the Internet and can overpower working with a smartphone. But such units. The vast majority of people do not like equipment, and if you need to get used to the donated phone for a long time, most likely no one will use it.
