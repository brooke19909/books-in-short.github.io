History of occurrence

Many centuries ago, our ancestors noticed that the enemy‚Äôs tooth brings them luck when hunting, and they achieve a love victory with the help of a bizarre plant root. So the story of amulets began, because primitive people were surrounded by so many real dangers that their main task was survival.



From time immemorial, man has been trying to protect himself from misfortunes and troubles. At first, amulets were absolutely everything - cones or roots, the skull of a defeated enemy or the tusk of a dead mammoth. Then they began to serve only such objects that were "talked" by shamans, gaining "magical power."

Over time, people began to assign even more functions to amulets: special images or special inscriptions began to appear on them, intended to avert one particular danger.

Pagan roots

The ancient Slavs did not perceive the world around them quite the way we do today. For them, he was alive and mysterious, filled with magic. Belief in the charms of the ancient Slavs, the meaning of various amulets is a pagan tradition. But it remained in Russia even after the adoption of the Christian religion.

The pagans always in the first place had a fear of powerful and incomprehensible forces. Our distant ancestors, too, as you know, worshiped the elements of nature.

It was they who were called into the defenders, turning into amulets. The ancient Slavs, who interpreted their meaning in their own way, were guarded by the depicted symbols of natural elements. The sown field in the form of a rhombus, divided into four parts, was considered a sign of land and fertility, for example. The symbol of water was wavy lines, fire - an oblique cross, thunder - a six-pointed star.



Pagan charms of the ancient Slavs - this is a whole science. Such protective symbols are found everywhere in our ancestors - on everyday objects, and on clothes, and on accessories. They protected them from injustice and misfortune, from evil spirits and many diseases. In addition, pagan charms of the ancient Slavs could bring love and prosperity to their masters. After all, according to our ancestors, they possessed truly divine power.

Women's amulets

Symbols of the ancient Slavs can be seen on many items inherited by the representatives of the beautiful half from grandmothers and even great-grandmothers. Towel towels, pillowcases or pillowcases, handkerchiefs and pouches embroidered with fanciful designs are found in almost every other house. But not everyone knows that they are the amulets of the ancient Slavs. Our ancestors made them with our own hands. And the patterns that are on them are not just ‚Äúpictures‚Äù for beauty. These are the most powerful and powerful amulets of the ancient Slavs. Cross-stitch always had a sacred meaning - it protected a person from all sorts of misfortunes. True, over the centuries, this occupation has become a regular pastime.

For embroidery of charms, preference was given to the red color of threads. However, in some cases, other shades were used, such as brick, scarlet, poppy, currant, and cherry.

Ancient embroideries were performed with a counting seam: that is, they counted the threads of fabrics before making each stitch. The drawing-amulet was not translated into the material of the craftswoman, but only outlined its size and determined the location.

One of the attributes of women's protective magic was the so-called charms-pendants. They were attached on chains to plates, often made in the form of one or two sun horses, which were considered a symbol of happiness and goodness. Women wore such protective pendants on a belt or on a neck, and also on a shoulder or a headdress.



Until now, archaeologists in separate burials find whole sets of amulets suspended on a common basis on chains. For example, one of them contains a bird, two spoons, a key and a jaw of a predator. They symbolize satiety and well-being, wealth and safety of property. And in combination with the jaws or teeth of predatory animals, they protected from evil, contributed to a successful hunt.

Since ancient times, the Slavs were convinced that the best characteristics of the animal killed by them pass to those wearing animal amulets. Pendants in the form of animals and birds were associated with the properties of these representatives of the fauna. The charm "duck", for example, was a symbol of procreation and a happy family.

Men's amulets

And although a strong half of the Slavic population was less likely to wear amulets or talismans, nevertheless they existed. True, male charms of the ancient Slavs were much simpler. Usually they were various brooches: clasps on cloaks, richly decorated with symbols. Mostly solar signs were placed on them. Some of them were decorated with symbols of earth or rain. The body wards of the ancient Slavs - tattoos - were made in the form of an eight-pointed or ordinary cross, a swastika or an image of animals, birds or fish.

Men also often wore protective pendants with the symbol of the god Perun - a horse, which guarded them away from home, as well as with swords and daggers, representing victory in battle. Amulets and charms of ancient Slavs, such as fangs or claws of wild animals, protected from dangers.

Molvinets

It is considered one of the oldest signs of the sacred culture of the Slavic people. Being an energetically powerful sign of a wide spectrum - like so many talismans of the ancient Slavs - the molvinets was originally used to protect against the evil eye and other negatives generated by the word.

The name of the symbol, as you might guess, comes from the word "say" - to speak. It is precisely on this that the power of the charm of the molvinets is concentrated - to block the flows of any incoming energy that appears as a result of negative emotions or manifests itself in words.

In other words, if someone wanted evil to the Slav or was jealous of his success, slandering behind his back, then evil would return to the enemy. And today, many psychics and esotericists use Molvinets, which protects a person from the negativity born of the word. If the ill-wisher is an energetically weak personality, then Molvinets can not only ‚Äúreflect‚Äù his negativity, but even return it multiplied. This is one of the strongest protective charms of the ancient Slavs. It can be found not only on the patterns of ancient towels, but also in the form of a tattoo on the bodies of the valiant magi.



Over grass

This sacred amulet of the ancient Slavs belongs to the solar emblem. Our ancestors used it mainly in order to drive away from themselves those diseases caused by, in their opinion, evil forces. The same grass is a kind of allegory of the Sun, a kind of one god who gives his life-giving light to everyone on Earth. Grass cleansed all three human membranes - spirit, body and soul. It was used by the Slavs not only as a body amulet. It was fixed on clothes, put on dishes, on weapons or armor. The same grass, being a double fiery sign, protected the Slav from the machinations of evil spirits, did not allow the poison to get into the stomach of the food that was guarded, and kept it from an enemy arrow or spear.

Wedding

Wedding - Slavic amulet, which symbolized the unification of the two genera. Contrary to popular belief, the significance of this Vedic amulet went far beyond the traditional ritual system. In fact, a wedding is a protective pattern that must be woven into the embroidery on the clothes of the bride and groom. In this context, it denoted the merger of the physical and spiritual principles of the couple.
