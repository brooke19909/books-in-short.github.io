Zhukov Vasily Ivanovich: biography, family, scientific activity. Russian State Social University

Soviet and then Russian scientist, academician of the Russian Academy of Sciences, Vasily Ivanovich Zhukov organized the social university of Russia in 2006 and became its first rector. All the activities of this party functionary took place in the field of social sciences and in the field of the Ministry of Education. It was here that Zhukov, Vasily Ivanovich, became an Honored Scientist of the Russian Federation and received a prize from the Government of the Russian Federation.





Start

Born in 1947 in the outback of the Kursk region, Zhukov from childhood loved history lessons. However, he treated the rest of the subjects with full attention, which allowed him to finish school with a gold medal, and Voronezh University with honors. In addition to history, the diploma noted the German language, which was also allowed to be taught, which was Vasily Ivanovich Zhukov, and since 1970 he studied at school No. 64 in the city of Voronezh. After some time, he was already an assistant at the department of the Polytechnic Institute.

This was followed by service in the air defense of the Soviet Army and graduate school at his native university, followed by teaching at the Voronezh Polytechnic. In 1985, the second, doctoral, dissertation was defended on the topic of modern historiography, which made it possible to become a professor at the Department of History of the CPSU of the Polytechnic Institute. Further career quickly went up. Two years later, Vasily Ivanovich Zhukov was appointed head of the Higher Party School (VPSh) department in Moscow, which in 1991 was transformed by Zhukov‚Äôs efforts into the Russian State Social University, where he was awaited by a long rector.

In 2010, a conference was held at which Zhukov said that the rehabilitation of the Soviet historical period was simply necessary.

Naturally, modern "young historians" vehemently objected, including Igor Kurlyandsky and Nikita Petrov, because now it will be difficult to falsify archival documents that incriminate Soviet authorities in terrible crimes, despite the free media outlets with these materials. So far, the Honored Scientist of the Russian Federation has made more than five hundred scientific publications. The main directions of his research were all the same sociology and social history of education in Russia, many topics explored the theory and organization of political activity.

VPSh

As already mentioned above, V.I. Zhukov, among several young (up to forty years old) scientists, was sent from Voronezh to Moscow to step under the arches of the Higher Party School. Anticipating the collapse of a great country, the teacher in February 1991 turned to the Council of Ministers of the RSFSR with proposals to create a special center on the basis of the Higher School of Economics, where they would train highly professional workers for the social sphere.

Moreover, he had already developed curricula and programs for social work, social psychology, social pedagogy, social ecology, which highlighted the problems of politics and economics and gave examples of comparative historical sociology. They met him, and already in April 1991, Vasily Ivanovich Zhukov, whose biography was adorned with the creation of a new university, began to serve as vice-rector of the Russian Higher School of Economics, raising the former MPS to the level of the most prestigious and progressive universities. But the piece was too tasty to leave everything as it is.

But in August 1991 the coup struck, the country ceased to exist, breaking up into a number of independent, but very small, with the exception of the Russian Federation, and weak republics, including the Russian Federation. RSPI was liquidated to create three new universities at once, but the university did not provide for a sociological direction.

The former Higher School of Internal Affairs broke up at the Russian State University for the Humanities (with two main educational buildings, five dormitories, a postgraduate house, two residential buildings, all vehicles, a treasury, a library and much more), Moscow State Law Academy (with correspondence department rooms and hostels along Sadovaya-Kudrinskaya Street) and fill in the carcass!) Poor relatives

Five hundred eighty-six full-time students, sixty graduate students, one hundred and fifty-four teachers were simply thrown out into the street. Documents of sociologists were placed in garbage cans and for the most part destroyed. What was the way out of the situation so ugly? Sociologists could only fight for the right to start all over again.

Here again, the future rector, founder of the Russian State Social University, Professor Zhukov, discovered his remarkable organizational abilities, and he represented the interests of students and teachers in the struggle for the right to receive the desired education and work in the specialty chosen with all my heart. The fight was hard. With pickets, demonstrations, rallies, appeals to the government, courts and the media. There were even hunger strikes. And finally, the case was crowned with success.

Politicians Khazbulatov, Vilchik, Lakhova and some others showed responsibility and foresight, having contributed to the adoption of government decisions, and on October 14, 1991 Zhukov opened the RSSU!
