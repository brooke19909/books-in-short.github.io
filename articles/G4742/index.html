Light jackets

Now consider an example of how to sew a light jacket.

You will need such materials:
- Cloak fabric.
- Non-woven.
- Lightning 60 cm.
- Buttons.
- Threads.
- Sewing machine.

How to sew a jacket? The process begins with sewing the lining. To start, we sew the shelves with picks. Then we sew the side parts to the center of the shelves. After you need to sharpen the tucks. Sew side sections and shoulders. Gently sew on the sleeves.

Jacket top : we sew the center of shelves and side details. Sew tucks. Why sew the shoulder parts, sides of the shelves and backs. Then we sew in the sleeves.

Jacket collar : you need to measure the size of the neck. The size of the finished collar should be 6 cm. Depending on the size of the neck, you need to cut a strip of fabric of this size so that the finished part is 6 cm. But do not forget to add 1 cm to the seams.

In order to sew a zipper, use a helium pen or chalk to mark the place on the right and left sides on the shelves of the jacket. And carefully sew it.

Jacket pocket: cut out the pocket for our jacket of the shape you want. The same shape cut out the lining. We connect both parts. Sew the finishing seam about 1 cm at the top of the pocket. Carefully mark with chalk on the shelf the place where you will sew it. Then carefully attach it.

Belt: cut a strip of fabric 9 cm wide. We put non-woven on the upper part of the belt, bend it along and iron it with an iron. We bend the edges inward and stitch it with a finishing seam.

Jacket assembly : you need to sew on the lining starting from the sleeves. Bottom to Bottom. And so on, neat and even every detail.

Sew the collar to the neck. Wrapping the finishing seam on the collar and shelves. We do the same from the bottom of the sleeves. We bend the bottom of the jacket and also gently hem.

Our jacket is ready. Now you know everything about how to sew a jacket.

Jackets for children

It is also easy to make a jacket for your child. So, let's figure out how to sew a jacket for a boy with his own hands.

To do this, you will need:
- Fabric for a jacket.
- Lining.
- Lightning.
- Rubber cuffs.
- Threads.
- Paper for patterns.





When patterning parts, do not forget that you need to leave 1 cm to the seams. Transfer the pattern to paper.

From the fabric for the jacket and the lining we cut out the following details:

  • Back - 1 pc.
  • Before - 2 pcs.
  • Sleeve - 2 pcs.
  • Hood - 2 pcs.

Tailoring jackets

We connect the back and sleeves. From the main jacket fabric we sew both sides and sleeves at the same time. We do the same actions with the lining.

On the back of the jacket we mark the middle. We connect the seam of the hood with the middle of the back. We put a lining inside the hood. 1 cm wide allowances should remain on the front parts for stitching zippers. Gently bend the remaining edges at the bottom of the zipper.

We fold the rubber cuffs in half and sew them in the same way as the hood.

After that, carefully attach the zipper, while stepping back from the bottom of the jacket 3 cm.
It remains only to carefully hem the bottom of our jacket. The product is ready.

Leather jackets

Let's learn how to sew a leather jacket.

Many people think that a leather jacket is a terribly difficult task. But if you set a goal and approach this question as correctly as possible, then in fact there are no difficulties.

If you have basic sewing skills, know how to work with patterns, then perhaps it's time to try something new. There are some peculiarities in working with skin:

- In no case should leather fabric be wound with a needle, since the puncture places with the needle will remain forever.

- In order to fasten two parts together, you can use superglue or different clamps.

- When working with patterns, you need to take into account the density of the skin. If it is too soft, then more space should be left for the seams.





How to sew a jacket with your own hands

When all the patterns are transferred to paper, all the nuances are taken into account and finalized, you can try to start sewing. Try to lay out patterns on fabric as economically as possible. Use the chalk to circle all the details. This can be done both from the inside and from the front. Since our material is very soft and easy to stretch, all the details must be duplicated with a special fabric - doubler. It can be bought at any specialized store. To give the skin extra volume, glue the skin with foam rubber in the places you need and gently stitch it.

Carefully connect all the details of the jacket. Sew on the sleeves and collar.
From the lining fabric we cut out the same details as from the main one. We connect all the details of the lining. Then very carefully sew the lining fabric to the main product. Better to start with sleeves. Then along the bottom of the jacket, then the shelves and the neck. This is how easy it is to sew a jacket with your own hands.

Winter jackets

If you want a jacket from synthetic winterizer, the question that you need to solve is: what material to choose for tailoring? Basically, such products are sewn from artificial leather or raincoat fabric. Here, of course, there is a difference in price, so decide for a start how much you are willing to spend on fabric.

Once you have chosen the fabric, you can get to work. So, how to sew a jacket on a synthetic winterizer with your own hands?

For such a jacket you will need:
- artificial leather - 2.5 m.
- lining fabric - 2.2 m.
- synthetic winterizer 200 - 2.5 m.
- raincoat fabric - 1 m.
- zipper 80 cm long.
- standard zippers for pockets - 2 pcs.

How to sew a jacket on synthetic winterizer

Sample patterns are transferred to paper. Then we cut out the details of our product from insulation and the main fabric. All parts are interconnected with pins, given the space for seams.





Quilting every detail along the marking lines, marking them on the front side of the fabric in advance. For this, of course, artificial leather is better suited, since making notes on the front side, they are then easily washed off. If you are working with another fabric and cannot make marks on the face, then do it from the inside of the fabric, then sweep, and only then sew along with synthetic winterizer.

To make the side pockets of the jacket you will need:
- 2 details of a pocket from the main fabric, lining and insulation (moreover, you need to take only half the insulation layer, i.e., the synthetic winterizer layer is carefully divided into 2 equal thicknesses manually).
- 2 pieces of strips (to give volume) of the main and of the lining fabric.

For each part, do not forget to leave space for stock - 1.5 cm.

We quilted, and then we sew the details of the strip to the pockets of the insulation. We do the same with the details of the strips and pockets of lining fabric. We connect the details of the pockets from the lining and the main fabric with the front sides and sew them together. Turn finished pockets through the slice.

We sew ready-made pockets to our jacket, bending the edges, in order to hide the lining.

All prepared and pre-quilted parts of our product are neatly joined and sewn together.

Sewing a hood

We cut out the details of the hood from black cloak fabric and sew them. For the inside of the hood, we cut the same parts from another fabric and carefully sew them into the middle of the hood, then turn them out. We attach both lower parts of our hood to the middle of the neck of the jacket.

We cut out the details for the bottom of our jacket and hem. We connect them with the front sides with the bottom of the jacket and sew.

For a hanging loop, you need to cut a part from a fabric in the form of a 1x8 cm strip. Of course, you can buy a finished part, for example, in the form of a chain.

Sew it in the middle during the connection of the details of the grinder and the rebounds.

In order to sew a zipper in the product, we apply our zipper face to face on the left and right parts of our jackets and carefully sew them. Before applying the zipper, step back from the bottom of the jacket 1 cm. This is done so that the finished product does not protrude from the bottom due to the fastener.

In the same way, we carefully sew the lining details of our jacket. Sew on the lining starting at the bottom of the jacket. Then we sew the sleeves from the main fabric and the lining and connect it to the jacket.

Now the jacket is ready.
