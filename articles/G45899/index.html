Griffith Observatory - a unique attraction in Los Angeles

Griffith Observatory is a fairly popular tourist attraction in Los Angeles. It is located on the slopes of Mount Hollywood. Griffith Park, where the observatory is located, is an excellent viewpoint from which the whole landscape of the area opens. From there you can see Hollywood, and the Pacific Ocean, and Los Angeles. She is often shown in various sensational films. Now we will tell you what kind of observatory it is, how they appeared, and why its visit is often included in the program of travelers who make excursions in Los Angeles. The Americans managed to combine two things at once - this is a scientific organization and a popular tourist site. Let's take a walk through this amazing place.

History

Griffith's Observatory and the park itself, where it is located, are called so because it is the name of the person who owned the land. He transferred it to the city of Los Angeles at the end of the XIX century. They say that for a long time he speculated on real estate, but once he looked at the sky from the mountain with the best telescope at that time. The view shocked him. Jenkins Griffith also decided to finance the construction of a planetarium and observatory on Mount Hollywood. The project, whose architect was John Austin, was completed in 1935. Then the buildings of the observatory and exhibition halls were ready. They were decorated in art deco style. The opening of the planetarium was accompanied by a campaign in the press, and the audience just surged into the mountain. In the first years of the observatory, 13 thousand people visited it. Among the exhibits was a Foucault pendulum, proving the rotation of the Earth around its axis, one of Zeiss's first telescopes, as well as a relief model of the moon‚Äôs north pole. Under a huge dome in the center of the building, a planetarium worked. They demonstrated the mechanics of solar eclipses, and also talked about how the solar system works. During the Second World War, the planetarium was used as a simulator for pilots, and in the sixties of the last century - astronauts.

Opening hours and prices

In 2002, the observatory was temporarily closed for reconstruction. She earned it again in 2006. All the buildings were completely renovated, and the planetarium dome was renovated. From the very opening, people were allowed here for free. So bequeathed the founder of the observatory. But tickets for a laser show in a theater are sold separately at the box office. The submission price is seven US dollars. Children under five years old are allowed free of charge, but only for morning performances. Griffith Observatory is closed on Monday. Opening hours in the middle of the week are from noon to ten in the evening. On weekends, it opens from 10 in the morning.
