The center of life of Rotterdam. Old harbor

One of the most ancient ports of Europe has its history since the middle of the XIV century. The Old Harbor is the only place in the city that survived after the bombing during the Great Patriotic War, therefore it is from here that it is best to begin your acquaintance with the life of Rotterdam. Everyone can watch the busy movement of ships on the river and get acquainted with the surroundings during a sea excursion along shipyards, docks and piers. Bird's-eye. Euromast

Studying Rotterdam on a map, it is impossible to imagine the unearthly beauty that opens from the mast tower Euromast, the highest observation tower in the Netherlands. The mast was designed by architect Hugh Maaskant in the early 60s of the last century in honor of the famous flower exhibition held in Holland every 10 years. The height of the tower reaches 185 meters, while its diameter is 9 meters, and the wall thickness is only 30 centimeters. There are several observation platforms in the Euromast tower, a luxury European restaurant with fairly high prices, a cafe with a more affordable price level and two elite hotel rooms. The latter are full-fledged sights of Rotterdam, since you can only book a double room from April to September, and the cost of this service is 385 euros.

Attraction trip to all parts of the world. Bleidorp Pavilions





Bleijdorp is a favorite Dutch attraction with many interesting pavilions. In the realm of cold and ice, you can meet king penguins and even take a walk on moving ice floes right above the floating birds. The pavilion called "Crocodile River" will discourage exotic fish, turtles, crocodiles and amphibians of Africa. In the Oceanarium you can admire the marine and coastal inhabitants and even see how the sharks are fed in the shark tunnel. The butterfly pavilion will delight you with the brightness of the shades that shimmer on the weightless wings of insects that live in the recreated forests of the Amazon. The Botanical Garden will surprise with the pride of the Netherlands, national collections of primroses and bromeliads, and in the Asian part of the zoo, visitors will be greeted by Indian rhinos, bison, Amur leopards, bantenges, lion-tailed macaques, Asian elephants, speckled cats and crested baboons. Interestingly, Bleijdorp is actively involved in the breeding program of endangered species of birds and animals. The local attractions of Rotterdam are rare okapi animals (artiodactyls of the giraffe family), lowland gorillas and bongos (a mammal of the genus forest antelopes).
