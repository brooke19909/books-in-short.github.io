Everything you need to know about Czech spas

Czech Republic is located on the Bohemian Plateau, which is bounded from the west by the Ore Mountains. The nature of this country is striking in its uniqueness. According to one legend, the mythical father Cech was conquered by the beauty of this area during his stay on the top of Mount Rzhip.

A bit of history

Today, Czech resorts occupy one of the leading places in terms of attractiveness among tourists, to a large extent due to the fact that their history goes far into the past.

Bohemia got its name from one of the Germanic or Celtic tribes, which bore the name Fights. On this earth, people lived in the Stone Age, and the Slavs who came here in the fifth century remained here forever.





From the beginning of the ninth century, the Great Moravian Empire began its development and increased influence in Europe. Thanks to Charles 4, under whose rule Bohemia was in the second half of the 14th century, the Czech Republic significantly strengthened its political position. Karl himself became not only the king of Bohemia, but also the emperor of the Great Roman Empire. During his reign significant cultural changes took place. Founded by King Charles, the university is the oldest in Central Europe.

In 1918, the independent Czechoslovak Republic was proclaimed, which lasted until 1990, in which it was divided into the Czech and Slovak Federal Republic. Already in 1993, the Czech Republic and Slovakia became independent states.

Purpose of the trip

Cultural and historical sights, many of which have been preserved in their original form, attract hundreds of millions of tourists annually to this amazing country. Natural landscapes complement incredible beauty by historical monuments and architectural structures, of which there are more than two thousand in the Czech Republic .

Lovers of calm relaxation, natural beauty and privacy will appreciate this marvelous land, located on a ridge of hills.

People who care about their health, with great pleasure, go to the medical resorts of the Czech Republic. High-class sanatoriums with a high level of service are considered the best in the world.

The Czech Republic also attracts sports enthusiasts. An unforgettable experience will remain with you for a long time after staying at the modern ski resorts of this fabulous country.

Medical sanatoriums

Mineral springs, whose miraculous effects on the body have been known for a long time, contributed to the construction of medical sanatoriums. There are about 40 of them in the Czech Republic.





The most popular are Karlovy Vary, Frantiskovy Lazne, Teplice, Marianske Lazne. However, no matter what resort you go to, you will have a wonderful rest with health benefits.

Czech spas, the treatment in which you intend to receive, specialize in many diseases:

  • cardiovascular;
  • digestive system;
  • musculoskeletal system;
  • gynecological;
  • neurological.

In addition to the obligatory intake of mineral waters, treatment includes additional procedures: body wraps, hydrotherapy, massages, herbal medicine and much more. After the whole complex of procedures your health will improve significantly.

Cultural and historical sights

Many cities and buildings here are under the protection of UNESCO. The Czech Republic rightfully takes the leading place in the number of cultural monuments and, thanks to this, is one of the ten most visited countries in Europe.

When you find yourself in the Czech Republic, you get the feeling that you are in the Middle Ages. This is some special world. The richly decorated churches, ancient castles and fortresses (of which there are about 2500), shrouded in secrets and legends, make an indelible impression.





Some of the castles belong to the state, and in some the owners still live and conduct excursions themselves.

Be sure to visit the mysterious world of caves, created by the rapid flow of rivers. The underworld is fascinating with its stalactites and stalagmites.

There is also the opportunity to visit nature reserves, zoos, numerous museums.
