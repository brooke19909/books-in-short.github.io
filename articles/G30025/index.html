Spain, Cadiz. Ancient city in Andalusia

In ancient times, this place on the Iberian Peninsula was officially considered the "end of the world." Such a definition corresponded to what the eye saw - ahead was only the irresistible expanse of the ocean. However, according to modern ideas, only Spain ends here. Cadiz is one of its oldest cities. It is located on a narrow peninsula, deeply extending from the coastline to the Atlantic. In ancient times, such a geographical location was advantageous in terms of fortification.



City of Cadiz, Spain

Currently, it is a fairly significant city within the historical region of Andalusia. Among other things, what happened to him over a long thousand-year history, he is also famous as the most important Atlantic port. In an era of great geographical discoveries, Spanish expeditions set out from here to conquer new lands. And not far from him were the famous Columbus caravels. For many sailors, both Andalusia and Spain ended here. Cadiz was the last port on which you could glance from the side of the ship before leaving for the vastness of the ocean in search of a better fate. However, today Cadiz is, first of all, tourism. And for its successful development, there is everything you need: luxurious beaches, expressive visual texture based on the traditional historical buildings of the city. The unique flavor of the place is given by the southern white-stone architecture and lush tropical vegetation.



Only after a long trip along the Iberian Peninsula, it becomes clear how different the provinces are from each other. And on this coast it is immediately clear that this is primarily Andalusia, only then Spain. Cadiz is a very bright and colorful city, which is typical of Andalusia. Its famous beaches stretch along the coastline for many kilometers. In addition to a luxurious beach holiday, this beach is also a rare expressive place for fans to watch the world through the camera's viewfinder. It is especially beautiful here in the evening, when the sun bends over the waters of the Atlantic. Long evening shadows of medieval fortresses, towers and forts on the shore create unique angles and allow you to make expressive shots. By the way, what we see on many advertising posters is shot here. Through these visual images, Spain is revealed. Cadiz has its own unique energy. The work of such world-famous artists as Salvador Dali and Luis Bunuel clearly testifies to this.



Cadiz, Spain. sights

Heading here, you need to know about objects that should not be missed even with a cursory acquaintance with the city. First of all, it is the Cathedral with an observation deck on the dome and the old baroque church of Oratorio de San Felippe Neri. The fortifications of Earth Gate, which blocked free access to the peninsula in the Middle Ages, and the ancient fortress of San Sebastian are worthy of attention. You should definitely take a walk through the old quarters of medieval buildings, they are located in the central part of the city.
