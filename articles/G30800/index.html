What is the difference between reporting shooting from a regular photo shoot

It has become a long tradition that all significant events, be it an event of national importance or a book exhibition, sporting event or film festival, are covered not only by journalists, but also by photographers. A series of photographs depicting individual moments, episodes of what is happening, are called the photo report or reportage shooting.

Process specifics

Sequential shooting is fundamentally different from the usual. A fundamentally different, first of all, approach to the selection and supply of material. For a photo shoot or traditional pictures, the artistic side of nature is important, i.e. of what is photographed and displayed on the card. Therefore, any photographer is to some extent an artist. He tries not only to show the distinguishing features of the object being shot, but to do it beautifully, with a twist, creating an artistic image.

The photographer carefully plans the composition of future photographs, selects the desired lighting tone, color range. He may ask his models to smile or make a sad look - depending on the plot. And even if the photos are ordered and must meet certain requirements, the photographer has many opportunities for improvisation. Sequence shooting is not like that.
