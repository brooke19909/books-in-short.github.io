How to reduce the size of the photo and not lose in quality?

Modern camera arrays allow you to take photos of incredibly high resolution, which, in turn, increases the size of the image. One could put up with this if most of the photographs taken were not uploaded to the Web. Unfortunately, many sites seriously limit the size of downloaded images or automatically create small copies, which is why the pictures greatly diminish in quality. This raises the question of how to reduce the size of the photo, while maintaining maximum color information.

How is the decrease?

To reduce the weight of the photo, compression is divided into several types: lossless and lossy compression. When reducing the size of the photo without loss of quality, as a rule, the deterioration is invisible to the eye and can be used for pictures with any content, especially for those where it is important to preserve the integrity of the picture. When using this compression format, drastic image resizing is not possible.

Lossy compression is preferably used for small, abstract and medium quality shots. The size can be significantly reduced, but a significant part of the image information is lost.

Usually, the second method is used to crop photos. Weight reduction is achieved by subtracting various garbage data, for example, duplicated or too light pixels, replacing pixels with numerical values ‚Äã‚Äãand cutting out unnecessary information.
