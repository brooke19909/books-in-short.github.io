Fort Shevchenko: attractions and recreation

Fort Shevchenko is located in Kazakhstan, four kilometers from the Caspian Sea. This is one of the major cities of the Mangistau region, which is located on the Mangyshlak peninsula. The territory is adjacent to the coast of the Caspian Sea. The name of the peninsula is translated as "a thousand villages", one of which is the modern city of Fort Shevchenko.

Today, the city has a port of Bautino on the coast of the Caspian Sea. For a long time, local airports worked. They took in light aircraft and helicopters. Now they are abandoned and are used as emergency sites. The city can now be reached by car and water.

A bit of history

The first mention of the city dates back to the mid-19th century. Historical documents speak of the military fortification of Novopetrovsky, which was later renamed Fort Aleksandrovsky, which performed a protective function. His purpose was to protect the Russians from raids by nomads. T.G. served here. Shevchenko, whose name was named Fort-Shevchenko in 1939.

Archives give a description of the settlement. The construction consisted of four powerful bastions, two of the same half-bastions enclosing the outer shaft, and a stone block. All together it looked very impressive and made the enemies retreat.

The city was in the center of the Silk Road. He always attracted many travelers.

What is in the city?

Today, Fort Shevchenko is known as a port city, near which there is a fishing base and a canning factory processing such varieties as stellate sturgeon and sturgeon. There are also remains of a fortress, a museum of local lore, a museum of T.G. Shevchenko, ethnographic museum, Shevchenko monument, Armenian chapel, mass grave of the heroes of the Civil War.

Also here, almost on the seashore, limestone mining was founded. It is white and white, and the water casts blue. A very beautiful sight. Who will visit Fort-Shevchenko should definitely visit the shore and admire.

The holidays of May 1 and 9 are widely celebrated in the city, as well as Nauryz.

Noaryz is a celebration of spring renewal, when residents tidy their homes, plant trees and flowers.





Nearby is the architectural complex of the 16-20 centuries, as well as the Beisenbay necropolis of the 17-19 centuries.

Many tourists visit Fort Shevchenko annually. The sights of the city allow guests to plunge into the atmosphere of past centuries when visiting.

So remarkable is the ancient well existing in the city and clearly showing how water supply was organized in the past.

Mosque

There is a White Mosque in the city with a crescent moon, visible from afar. This is the oldest building. True, now it is destroyed. At one time this building was both a warehouse and a cinema.

There was an Orthodox church in the city, but not so long ago it burned down.

Activities for active

Residents of the city are actively involved in sports. Here, activities such as ballroom dancing, bicycle, boxing, chess clubs, table tennis, and skiing are developed.

On the outskirts of the city there is a very interesting building with arched windows. This is a youth sports school. And initially there was an officer meeting. Also, the city holds regional boxing competitions.

Chapel





Another attraction of the city that you need to see is the white-walled Armenian chapel made of stone behind the forged fence at the foot of the Kurgan-tash. Before, the Armenians were part of the merchants in Fort Shevchenko, grave tombstones also speak about this. It was delivered in the 19th century by Astrakhan merchants. Today it is only an architectural monument with the silence characteristic of the abandoned altars.

Where to relax and settle down if you come to Fort Shevchenko?

Rest you can offer the hotel Chagala Group (Bautino). Oil workers often stop here. Here, comfort and warmth are guaranteed on the shores of the Caspian Sea. Travelers will find a varied cuisine in the local restaurant, high-quality service, friendly staff. The hotel will delight its guests with a gym and sauna, its own small beach.

You can also stay at Shetpe Ecotourism Guesthouse. Here it will be possible not only to have a good rest, but also to see with your own eyes the small business in the countryside in the field of tourism. This is an ordinary residential building in which several rooms have been converted to receive guests. The family living in the house offers tourists a dinner consisting of national dishes, and in the evening you can hear a game of dombra or stories about the life and culture of the Kazakhs.

Festivals

The city of Fort Shevchenko in Kazakhstan is still interesting because many festivals are held here. It will be interesting for tourists to visit them.

The most entertaining, perhaps, will be a festival dedicated to T.G. Shevchenko. In the city, a careful attitude to the memory of the famous writer is still preserved. This is indicated by the exposition of the memorial museum and the monument erected to the author.

Another festival is contemporary music, which gathers a large number of people.

Conclusion

Fort Shevchenko is one of the most interesting cities in Kazakhstan with ancient history, architectural monuments, modern holidays. Tourists enjoy relaxing here on the shores of the Caspian Sea.
