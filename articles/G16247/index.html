A brief history of the city

In the 13th century, just when the Golden Horde ruled, the settlement of Ginestra existed on the territory of modern Odessa. At the end of the XIV century and the beginning of the next century, it was part of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania. Then she was called Kachibey, Kotsyubeyev or Hajibey.

Since 1475, Kachibey passes under the control of the Ottoman Empire and receives the name Adzhibey or Hadzhibey. In 1764, the Yeni-Dunya fortress was built here. But in mid-September 1789 (during this period the second Russo-Turkish war took place), it was captured by a company of major general of the Russian army Joseph de Ribas.

In May 1794, Catherine the Second issued a decree according to which a military harbor was built on the site of Hadzhibey. Joseph de Ribas and Franz de Vollan began to draw up a plan for the creation of the city. The first bricks of Odessa were laid on September 2 of the same year. And the name of the city comes from the Greek village of Odyssos or Odessos.

The main attractions of Odessa were created during the time of the most prominent city governors, who did a lot for the prosperity of the village. Their names are Admiral de Ribas, Count Langeron and Vorontsov, Duke of Duc de Richelieu.

Passage

Anyone who saw the film "Seaside Boulevard", almost took a tour of this amazing place. Indeed, in this movie one could consider many of the sights of the city of Odessa. One of them is the Passage. This is a building of a luxury hotel, which is one of the most beautiful buildings in the city. The Passage is striking in its sophistication of sculptural compositions and architecture itself. Inside the hotel there is a real street with rows of elite class shops. The light inside the building is provided by a high glass roof. It also creates a feeling of immense space. The famous monument to Duke

Sights of Odessa are not only markets and steps, but also monuments to people who have done a lot for the development of the city. This monument was awarded Duc de Richelieu. The sculpture was installed in 1828, and I.P. Martos became its author. This was the first monument that was installed in Odessa.





The statue is cast in bronze and installed in honor of the French aristocrat, who in 1804-1815 was the governor general of the city. Richelieu depicted in the toga of the Romans, he holds a scroll in his hand. And three high reliefs made of brass embody trade, justice and agriculture. Arman-Emmanuel de Richelieu did an incredible amount for the construction of the city, he turned it into a large trading port. With this act, he earned love and recognition among the local population.
