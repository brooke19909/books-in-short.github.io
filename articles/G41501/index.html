What is Minecraft

Before you figure out how easy it is to make a sword from Minecraft out of paper, you need to clarify what this computer game is all about. Minecraft translates as "mining craft." This is an open-world first-person game with somewhat strange simplified graphics that vaguely remind us of Lego constructors. Even the goal of the game is similar, and is to create a variety of things out of cubes. If you are completely unfamiliar with the game, you can register on the site and try yourself in the simplest version, where you are offered an unlimited number of blocks for construction, and you cannot die. But do not get too carried away with computer games, because real life is much more interesting.

Making paper crafts in the style of the Minecraft game is a very exciting activity that will help to spend a pleasant evening with the children. Materials

To make a sword, we need:

  1. Corrugated cardboard or old box.
  2. Scissors.
  3. Pencils or color printer.
  4. Plain cardboard or paper.
  5. PVA glue.

The main highlight of crafts in the style of "Minecraft" - "square". Cut several 5x5 cm squares from thick cardboard and, using them as a pattern, draw a sword on corrugated cardboard. Make another similar detail, cut and glue together with glue to make it stronger. Two more details need to be cut out of white paper or cardboard. Glue them on top and bottom of the sword. Now it remains to give the child colored pencils and let him personally decorate his sword. Similarly, you can make a pick and other tools. Here's how to make a Minecraft sword out of paper.

A sword can be made of wood if the head of your family knows how to carve various shapes from particleboard. Simply transfer the blank of the sword with a pencil to a sheet of chipboard, cut it out and decorate it with acrylic paints. The top of the sword must be varnished.

Another option is to print the presented templates on the printer.

We cut out the patterns, glue them on cardboard and glue the parts together. This option is much simpler and faster, but almost devoid of a creative component.
