Something from the story

Everything once began with a small ancient island (cape), later connected to the mainland.

According to one legend, the city was founded by Cadmus of Phoenician - the son of King Agenor. Budva had to experience a lot in reality. Here is a brief overview of the stages of the history of the city.

It belonged first to the Romans, then to the Byzantines, and in the Middle Ages it became part of the Principality of Diocletes (Serbia). It was also under the rule of the Republic of Venice until the 18th century. The Austro-Hungarians owned the city in the 19th century, after which it became part of Yugoslavia.

The years of World War II were hard times for Budva, when half of the inhabitants died here. It has become the resort capital of Montenegro since 2006.
