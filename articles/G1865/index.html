Waterfalls in the Saratov region: interesting facts and reviews

Saratov region is one of the most famous in Russia. Its area is more than one hundred thousand square kilometers. The population is about two and a half million people. People of different nationalities live well here: Russians, Ukrainians, Kazakhs, Belarusians, Armenians, Koreans and many others. Tourists from all over the world like to come here. There are a large number of cultural and historical monuments. But not only the attractions of the Saratov region attract attention.



The nature of the region is clean air, pine forests, peaks, as if covered with snow and amazing waterfalls. In the Saratov region, you can not only enjoy beautiful views, but also significantly improve your health. One of the famous rivers of Russia - the Volga - flows here. Residents and guests of the region like to relax on its shores.

But not many people know about where the most beautiful places of the region are located, for example, waterfalls in the Saratov region. How to get to them? What other places are worthy of attention? Perhaps the information provided can help you choose a new place to stay.

Beautiful places of the Saratov region

  • The cliff of Stepan Razin. In the Krasnoarmeysky district is the village of Belozerskoye. On the very bank of the Volga rises the famous cliff, which is called the name of the famous Russian ataman. Here Stepan Razin drowned the Persian princess and hunted down rich prey.
  • Moss swamp in the Novoburas district. You can see unique plants and rare animals. Many of them are under state protection.
  • Dyakovo forest. Located in the Krasnokutsky district. In the middle of the steppe there is a unique forest in which the bustard bird is found.
  • Fields of wild tulips. In the Saratov region, there are several areas where these beautiful flowers bloom in spring.
  • Kudeyarova cave. They say that the famous robber Kudeyar hid looted treasures here. Nearby is a dilapidated wooden mill, which was built in the mid-19th century. Separate buildings that are of great interest to people have survived.
