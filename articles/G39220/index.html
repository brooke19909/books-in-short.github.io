Aircraft carrier: progressive ship

This is a huge ship carrying aircraft cargo: airplanes or helicopters. Moreover, up to hundreds of aircraft can be on it. They are the main striking force of an aircraft carrier. For the first time such a surface vessel appeared in the First World War, but the peculiarity of the first-born was that they were remade from ships of other types. For example, a cruiser called Birmingham became such a vessel. For the first time, a plane took off from his deck.

This significant event happened in 1910, and it laid the foundation for carrier-based aviation. At first, such ships were used for reconnaissance purposes, but later they realized the importance of aircraft as a means of bombing. At the dawn of the construction of aircraft carriers, seaplanes were used, since airplanes could take off, but they could not land on deck. For this, hydroplanes were used that successfully landed on the water. History says that the French aircraft carriers before the Second World War were not numerous: or rather, only one, like the United States. The largest number of such vessels at that time was in the United Kingdom (7 units). Subsequently, America succeeded in designing aircraft carriers.

Such ships are divided into the following positions:

  • aircraft carriers;
  • trading;
  • Balloons
  • helicopter carriers;
  • hydro-aircraft carriers;
  • air;
  • underwater.

In addition, there are multi-purpose, shock and anti-submarine. By the type of energy, there are ordinary and atomic models.

The main characteristics of aircraft carriers

Steel hulls of aircraft carriers have considerable strength, because their thickness reaches several centimeters. The length of the huge ships is hundreds of meters: the amplitude ranges from 180 to 342. The draft of the vessel reaches 12-meter depth. The deck width is quite large, which gives the aircraft carriers an unforgettable look. Under the deck are huge docks and hangars for aircraft maintenance. The only elevation on the deck, a kind of "island", is the command post, which includes location systems and antennas. This center is usually located on the starboard side.



The flight deck is one of the most important components of an aircraft carrier. They are distinguished by three types, one of which is not used now. The flat deck, for example, has the famous French carrier Charles de Gaulle. Decks of this kind are used for horizontal take-off. For this, steam catapults are used. Springboard decks are installed on ships containing vertical take-off aircraft. A characteristic feature of such a deck is the combination of the runway and landing strip.

The flight deck, containing two levels, was used until the 30s of the last century. On the nose of such aircraft carriers was the take-off deck, and at the top was the landing deck. But since such a scheme was dangerous for pilots, it was changed by re-equipping it, as is customary in conventional aircraft carriers.

This powerful military formation is armed with submarines, ultra-modern frigates, amphibians, cruisers, destroyers, and, of course, the legendary French aircraft carrier Charles de Gaulle with a displacement of 37 thousand tons. France and World War II

The French are rightly called pioneers of naval aviation. They built hydroplanes, seaplanes and flying boats, and battleships and cruisers equipped with catapults for takeoff. But this was not destined to come true. During the period from 1939 to 1945, the armed forces of France, especially the fleet, lost most of their weapons, and demanded a total recovery. Aircraft carrier Bearn: a gray-haired story

The French Vice Admiral Bourget rightly believed that urgently needed to revive the French aircraft carriers as the basis of the Navy. This man once commanded Bearn, so his advice was valuable and was heard. The admiral believed that the French fleet needed at least 6 aircraft carriers. Subsequently, the government purchased aircraft carriers from England, and in the 1950s shipbuilders in France designed the Clemenceau and Foch aircraft carriers.

But about the warship, the Bearn aircraft carrier, it is worth mentioning especially if only because he took part in the war with the Nazis. The aircraft carrier participated in the search for the German ship Admiral Count Spee. The construction of the ship began in January 1914, and it was launched in April 1920. The draft of the vessel is more than 9 meters and the width is 27. The length of the aircraft carrier was 182 meters. The number of crew was 865 people.

French Navy aircraft carriers began their story with this armored ship. He was armed with anti-aircraft mounts, torpedoes and had on board 40 aircraft. The aircraft carrier was designed from the Normandy hull, while the battleship turbines were replaced by a power plant. After the French surrender, it was rumored that the aircraft carrier had taken all the state gold reserves to Martinique, but this information was not confirmed. Further, until the end of the war, Bearn was transporting aircraft from Canada to his homeland. In 1967, the French flagship of the beginning of the last century was dismantled.

The aircraft carrier is the largest vessel in France, but it is small in comparison with similar American nuclear ships. Mistral: the French station wagon

The landing ship helicopter carrier Mistral differs from others in its multi-purpose application. It can be a hospital, land motorized landing brigades on land, act as a command center, carry and serve combat helicopters. If we discuss the aircraft carriers of France, the Mistral, although it is not an aircraft carrier in full, is also a worthy representative of the French Navy.

Is it possible to sink an aircraft carrier?

This is a very difficult task. Due to the fact that such super-expensive ships carry a huge amount of weapons, then, of course, the protective functions of an aircraft carrier do not quite satisfy the necessary requirements. Therefore, around a huge ship there are always 15 or more ships that provide reliable protection within a radius of 300 kilometers. But still, to sink a carrier is real, although very difficult. The simplest but not very effective method is to storm. To do this, it is necessary to neutralize the security ships, and then try to let the aircraft carrier sink to the bottom, which will be extremely difficult, because it is equipped with a huge number of compartments.

Not the last role in putting an aircraft carrier out of action can be played by sabotage. For example, during the replenishment of reserves, in one of the ports, a group of divers should quietly swim up to the vessel and install a remote explosive device at its bottom. The main thing in this operation is to go unnoticed.



Another way could be torpedoing from the side of a silent submarine. Of course, a huge aircraft carrier will not be able to dodge a torpedo. Only now the Kamikaze submarine is very likely to be immediately destroyed by guard ships.

The greatest effectiveness in the sinking of an aircraft carrier is given by missile and nuclear strikes. It is clear that the latter, due to infection of the territory, and other catastrophic consequences, are applied only in extreme cases.
