Budapest in winter: what to see and try in the Hungarian capital?

Budapest is not the most popular European capital. And it is completely unfair, because this city is distinguished by its magnificent architecture, original Hungarian cuisine and a special atmosphere that is difficult to find elsewhere. What is especially surprising, Budapest is especially beautiful in winter, in cold weather, during the Christmas season.

Therefore, when considering where to go on a winter vacation, be sure to consider visiting the Hungarian capital.

Holidays in Budapest in the winter

In the snowy months, the city becomes even more beautiful than usual. As many reviews of Budapest say, in winter beautiful views begin from the airport itself. All this looks fantastically beautiful and tunes in a festive mood. Another attraction that Budapest can be proud of in the winter is the Danube River. It freezes very, very rarely, on the contrary, it is from December to February that the water here becomes especially stormy. Small crusts of ice, light frost and the whole promenade, shining with purity and freshness - all this is definitely worth seeing. In summer, the river looks very different. Another must-see place is fairs. The central market of the capital is good at any time of the year. Famous seasonal fairs before Christmas help you find the New Year mood and feel the holiday before it arrives.

New Year in the capital of Hungary

Christmas holidays are the center of the entire winter season. New Year and Christmas can transform any city, and Budapest is no exception. Therefore, it would be very helpful to make a vacation just for the holidays. Both wide avenues and small streets are decorated with lights, sales begin in stores. And after long walks, you can do something nice for yourself by going to the famous baths, the water in which comes directly from the thermal springs. Warm pond under

open winter sky will be remembered for a lifetime. So you can see Budapest in winter at least for a visit to the bathhouse. On New Year's Eve, residents gather in the main squares with glasses of champagne, tourists and locals have fun until the morning at parties in bars and restaurants. The hotels also have holiday dinner programs, so even supporters of quiet evenings will be able to find the option they like. Be sure to try the national cuisine. Appetizing goulash or thick ear with paprika, mulled wine and delicious sweets with marzipan are ideal for cold weather. Therefore, Budapest in the winter certainly will not disappoint even the fastidious gourmet. Marzipan sweets are also good as a souvenir, so do not forget to buy them for your loved ones, going home from vacation.
