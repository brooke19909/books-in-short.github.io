Sighnaghi, Georgia: attractions and photos

The small town of Sighnaghi (Georgia) with a population of just over two thousand people will not leave indifferent any traveler. This is such a picturesque area that causes only delight, admiration and the desire to always admire the splendor of nature and the creations of human hands.

Bodbe Monastery

The city of Sighnaghi (Georgia) is located high in the mountains, in the Alazani Valley. Its construction is attributed to the era of the reign of Heraclius II, that is, to the eighteenth century.





Travelers will certainly be happy to visit Orthodox shrines. It is located in two kilometers from the city Bodbe nunnery, built in the fourth century. It was built on the site of the grave of St. Nina, who preached Christianity in Georgia. She converted to the Christian faith of the ruling king in those years, Mirian and his wife Nana, as well as many other inhabitants. Therefore, Georgia is rightfully considered a country that was one of the first in the world to adopt Christianity. The monastery was founded by King Mirian. And several centuries later, in 1617, a school opened under him.





It was one of the best educational institutions of those times, where the richest library was located. Unfortunately, with the advent of Soviet power, the monastery was closed. But, fortunately, he did not suffer the fate of many magnificent ancient temples, barbarously destroyed during the reign of the Communists. And in 1995, the monastery reopened its doors.

Church of St. George

What is worth seeing for those who come to Sighnaghi (Georgia)? Another Orthodox shrine is the Church of St. George. Its locals call SurbGevorg. Initially, this church belonged to the Armenians who moved to these parts by order of Heraclius II. The stone temple is distinguished by severe beauty and durability. In the mid-nineteenth century, the cathedral was closed and gradually fell into decay. Only recently, an old building was transferred to the Georgian Orthodox Church. Inside the temple are not only icons, but also the rarest old books. They can be seen by those who visited Sighnaghi.

City Hall

A magnificent example of architecture is the city hall building. The three-story building, built in the style of Italian classicism with elements of Georgian architecture, decorated with high arcades and a clock tower, is very beautiful and very original. Even ordinary window grilles look impressive, as this is a beautifully crafted openwork forging. Built at the end of the twentieth century, the city hall building blends very organically with the architecture of the old town.

Park "April 9"

Those who came to Sighnaghi (Georgia) must take a photo in the city. And where better to take good pictures? In the center of Sighnaghi is located the park "April 9". It is named after the Independence Day of Georgia, which is celebrated on that day. In addition to luxurious trees, picturesque shrubs, exquisite flowers, emerald grass, the park is unique for its clean and healing alpine air.

City of love

Are you looking for a city of love in Georgia? Sighnaghi locals call it that. Perhaps because it is impossible not to fall in love with this city. It is here that the famous Wedding Palace is located, the orders in which are completely unlike those that exist in other settlements. The beautiful pink building with an open terrace and wide arches leads to a wide staircase surrounded by lanterns.





In this Wedding Palace, you can marry at any time of the day, any day. In addition, those who wish will get married immediately, without any pre-filed in a few days applications. This gives a unique charm to an already romantic event. Therefore, on the way to an unusual palace, you can often meet couples in love who have just completed the sacrament of marriage.

Sculptures
