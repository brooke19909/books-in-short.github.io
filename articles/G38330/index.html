Gudrun Burwitz: biography, photo

The grandmother of neo-Nazism, Puppy, the princess of the Third Reich - so called Gudrun Burwitz. How did the daughter of Heinrich Himmler live her life and what does the once-most famous girl of Nazi Germany do in her declining years?

Biography Gudrun Burwitz

Born on August 8, 1929 in the family of the SS Reichsfuhrer Heinrich Himmler and Margaret Zegot. The family later adopted a boy named Gerhard. He was only a year older than his own daughter, and the girl was incredibly happy about his appearance. Outwardly, she was ideally suited to the image of a purebred Aryan girl: pale skin, blond hair and regular features. Beautiful dresses and braids made her a real doll, for which she received the nickname Puppy from her father. So he called her in letters and at meetings at which she received the attention of Adolf Hitler himself.





Fairytale childhood

Gudrun Burwitz boasts his cloudless and happy childhood. Caressed by the attention of the press and her own father, she was a role model for all the children of the Third Reich. Solemn performances of the Fuhrer ended with the offering of flowers, and it was this girl who most often received attention from Hitler. Relations between parents, as already mentioned, quickly became formal, and the Reichsfuhrer had connections on the side. However, this did not affect young Gudrun. She was still the closest and beloved person to her father. Every day he called his daughter to inquire about her well-being and learn about the events of the past day. Once a week he wrote her long touching letters. This became a ritual, and the girl was looking forward to the treasured envelope.





In addition to love and care, she received a huge number of gifts. Among the many toys, outfits and other pleasant things were even jewelry. To this day, she wears one of these jewelry - a large silver brooch with the image of horse heads. Four animal faces together made up a swastika. This jewel was especially proud of the young daughter of the fascist.

Father's daughter

Heinrich Himmler was very upset by his separation from his daughter and did everything to see his Puppy as often as possible. He regularly sent a military plane to Munich, which delivered his daughter to him for several days in Berlin. There, the girl attended important events with him and spent time with benefit. The girl started an album where she carefully pasted all the photos of her father, cutting them out of the newspapers. Over time, their joint pictures began to be added there.





She colorfully described one of her trips with her father in a diary. It was a tour of the concentration camp in Dachau. With all her childish directness, she described what kind of trees grow there and how wonderful the prisoners draw pictures. There is not a word about the inhuman living conditions and the terrible maintenance of people on the pages of her diary. But she could not see the crematorium pipes and gas chambers. Moreover, the father did not just show her the life of prisoners - he conducted an inspection. Gudrun Burwitz still does not see anything reprehensible in the actions of his father.

He hoped to take Hitler's place and rule the country in accordance with his ideological views. Even after he was kicked out of a leadership position on April 29, 1945 for treason, he still believed in his integrity.

Faithful daughter

In early May 1945, Margaret and Gudrun were arrested by the British. She learned about the death of her father in prison. This news had so much effect on young Puppy that for three weeks she fell into unconsciousness and lay in a fever. She managed to escape from the clutches of death, and endless wanderings in the camps began. They were constantly being transferred with their mother, and she did not have time to properly mourn her father. She categorically denied his suicide and is still convinced that the British killed him. She fiercely hates all representatives of this nation.





In November 1945, mother and daughter appeared before the tribunal at the trial in Nuremberg. Gudrun did not renounce his father and considered all his actions justified by wartime. She was not impressed by the many hours of reports about her dad's atrocities and his contribution to the destruction of innocent people. At that time she was already 15 years old, and she was well aware of what the Holocaust was and how millions of Jews ended their lives thanks to the efforts of her father. The girl confirmed that she supports the views and decisions of the Reichsfuhrer and considers him unfairly accused, while he worked for the good of his country. She still believes that the time will come when her father will be found not guilty and completely rehabilitated.





Hard times

She was strongly advised to change her last name, but she refused to betray the memory of her parent. With difficulty, she managed to get a job in a small atelier, but she did not last long there.

Silent help

In the same year she went to Britain, where she was invited by the English Nazis. The girl willingly poses on the background of posters with the image of Hitler. By that time, she was already in Quiet Aid, which was engaged in supporting the Nazis who escaped punishment. Gudrun is again without work. This did not break the devoted daughter, and a year later she founded the Viking-Jugend.
