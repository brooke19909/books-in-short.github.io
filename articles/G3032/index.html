Pereslavl Kremlin

Judging by the reviews of the sights in Pereslavl-Zalessky, the main one is the Kremlin of the old Russian city.

He appeared in the XII century. At that time, double wooden walls with towers were located throughout the crest of the shaft. Later, the Kremlin was rebuilt several times. He was often captured and robbed, including during the invasion of the Tatar-Mongols.

In the middle of the XVIII century, wooden walls were dismantled due to their uselessness and dilapidation. Today you can see earthen ramparts, the Transfiguration Cathedral of the XII century, the church of Peter the Metropolitan of the XVI century, part of the surviving buildings of the Bogoroditsky-Sretensky monastery, built in the XIII century.
