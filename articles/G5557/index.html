USA in World War I: Historical Facts

Already at the beginning of the twentieth century, the United States was an industrial power that could face off with any European wash. The First World War was supported by America much later than all the allies, however, this allowed it to extract the greatest benefits from this situation. The USA in the First World War behaved more cunning than Odysseus. It will be reasonable to note that this practice was adopted by them and with some variations is used even now.





Trickiest of all

In 1918, July and partly August found German and French-Anglo-American troops fighting bloody near the Marne River. The general offensive of the German troops was the last, since the battle was a failure for them and led to the final defeat. It was then that American troops first took a direct part in this war. Prior to this, there was only economic support, not without a certain benefit for themselves. In the First World War, the United States even overcame the global crisis, which also got the most prosperous of the countries. It should be noted that in 1913 the industrial production of the United States was ahead of the rest of the world, much more steel and cast iron were produced there, and mining was more successful.

If we compare the countries of Europe and the USA according to these parameters, then taken together France, England and Germany did not produce so much coal. The USA in the First World War sharply increased its economic activity. The Entente fought, so she had to experience certain difficulties. The United States, in collaboration with the rest of the allies, was able to double production. It should be noted that it was with their light hand that the mass destruction of people began, never before before: America supplied its allies with chemical and explosives, thus rapidly enriching itself. But they were not in a hurry to introduce their own US troops in the First World War.





Winners feast

So, the United States preferred a judicial role to military feats ("moral judge," according to President Wilson). However, when the denouement became clear, Washington was alarmed. Suddenly it will happen that a peace treaty will be concluded, and at the "feast of the winners" they will have no place. Only in 1917 a decision was made, and finally the United States entered the First World War. This slightly extinguished anti-American sentiment among the allies. Eighty-five thousand US troops entered the battle on the Marne. Death awaited half of them. Allies, it must be said, have lost millions at this point. The objectives pursued by the US entry into World War I are clear.

According to historian Andrei Malov, the Americans traded very actively with all the warring countries, receiving dividends, raising the class of industry, and reducing unemployment. And managed to enter the war, when it was time to share the cake. They managed to participate in this divide. A redistribution of the world took place, further improving the results of the First World War for the United States. After the conclusion of peace, the United States took the most interested part in the creation of the League of Nations, in the liberation of Belgium, in returning France to Lorraine and Alsace, in expanding the territory of Serbia with its access to the sea, in rebuilding Poland. Were concerned about the welfare of other countries? No, hardly.





"Teaching" democracy in any way

The United States firmly took up the entire device of a dilapidated world. Economic policy during the war concentrated more than forty percent of all the world's gold reserves in the banks of the United States, and foreign countries owed them twelve billion dollars - at that time the amount was huge. Wilson and his successors drew up a plan that significantly outlived the creators; moreover, he is still working. The neocons after Roosevelt formulated the results of World War I for the United States: "We are a model of democracy and we must teach it to all other peoples by any means." Already after 1918, the largest countries in Europe owed the United States two generations in advance.

What is happening now? The whole world owes them, and it will not be possible to pay off debts until the very last days of human life. The USA during World War I created a good reserve. Immediately after graduation, American tourists took over Europe, learning how to use the difference in exchange rates. Young Europeans were terribly jealous until the American way of life became the object of blind imitation: technological progress with its poisoned fruits, advertising and gloss. The USSR was the last on this path, exchanging freedom for the Sneakers. After all, freedom is not in the accessibility of shameful things, but in equal rights to housing, education, work, rest. It is easy for a creditor to become not only a trendsetter and a trendsetter, but also a dictator of the aspects of the political economy he needs. Global hegemony. Russia and the United States in the First World War played diametrically opposite roles, and then their fate divorced in two completely different ways - to confrontation.





The League of nations

Since 1914, the United States conducted diplomatic maneuvers in the behind the scenes, creating and playing all kinds of dramatic conflicts, while maintaining a neutral status. And only in March 1917 (April 6 in a new style) did Washington realize the impossibility of further maneuvering. When the United States entered World War I, President Wilson clearly calculated the situation: a powerful blow could be dealt to the pre-war order, where the United States played a secondary, marginal role in the world practice of international relations. Nevertheless, they were not formally attached to the Entente, but remained its associate member. Thus, it was possible to maintain freedom from mutual obligations, purely allied, which expanded significantly in wartime. But to be free in terms of annexations and territorial reorganization of the United States is absolutely unprofitable, which is why the United States entered the First World War.

The Entente was constantly experiencing an ever-increasing need for help from the Americans. And not only finances and weapons, but also troops. Wilson proclaimed the goals of the United States in this war, which fundamentally contradicted the European concept of balancing forces, even at the cost of losing peoples' right to self-determination. Great powers, as the United States believed, constantly violate the principle of self-determination, which means that the world order will not be stable. That is why Wilson proposed the creation of a new, permanent international body, which is called upon to observe collective security and ensure a fair resolution of all international disputes. The basis of the work of the created League of Nations was a certain set of generally agreed principles, among which the self-determination of nations was present. So, the role of the United States in the First World War became dominant, despite the very late entry into it.





London, Paris, Moscow

In planning the creation of the League of Nations, Wilson convinced the Allies that the first organization of this kind was universal and would be able to maintain both the safety of sea lanes for unlimited use by any state of the world and the prevention of any war launched in violation of contractual obligations. Submission of all global issues to a unified public opinion of the world. Paris and London considered the tasks set by Wilson to be far from reality and too abstract. In a word, neither David Lloyd George nor Georges Clemenceau was enthusiastic about this proposal initially. The problems in Europe were much more urgent: military efforts did not increase, because the United States maintained neutrality, things were bad at the rear: strikes, pacifists, and even the Vatican became an intermediary between the warring countries. So it was possible to lose the war.

Regarding Russia, not everything was smooth either. Attempts to revise specific conditions in a future peace treaty have already taken place, and Russia's interests were severely infringed both in Europe and the Middle East. Then the Provisional Government and exchanged diplomatic missions with the United States , trying to get military and economic assistance, plus foreign economic benefits. In Russia then, everything was bad too: the crisis was not only economic, but also political, the complete collapse of the army and the assigned front. Russia has become an extremely unreliable ally. The Entente took control of the situation: England was in charge of sea transportation, France contributed to the combat readiness of the Russian troops, and the United States engaged in rail transport. At the beginning of November 1917, the Provisional Government still saw the bright future of its reign and with might and main demonstrated the desire for war to a victorious end. But on November 7th in a new style with his own signature: "Which ones are temporary here? Slime!" - has come.
