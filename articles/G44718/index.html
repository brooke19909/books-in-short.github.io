DIY snowdrops from paper: a master class (photo)

And in winter, and in autumn, and just when sad, I want spring to wake up in the house and in the soul. It is very easy to do! It is enough to make snowdrops from paper with your own hands. A master class will help to fulfill this beautiful souvenir. All materials are inexpensive and affordable, and even a beginner can learn how to create a cute bouquet.





What is required

How to make snowdrops out of paper with your own hands? A master class and useful tips will be presented below, and now look at the list of necessary materials and devices. The kit may vary depending on the method you choose. So, prepare the following:

  • plain or corrugated white and green paper;
  • sticks, tubes, wire, toothpicks, skewers for making the stem;
  • scissors;
  • glue;
  • yellow paint or cereal (millet);
  • cotton wool;
  • floristic grass;
  • ribbon or vase to decorate a bouquet.

In each case, something additional may be necessary, for example, if you are planning to make a flat postcard, you will need a cardboard base , and special tools will be useful to perform the work in the quilling technique , although you can do with an ordinary skewer or toothpick.

Ways You Can Apply

There are several ways to make snowdrops out of paper with your own hands (a master class of two options is presented below). Bouquets are voluminous or flat, made in the form of an application, which also includes relief elements. The first way is to make a souvenir to decorate the interior, for example, a table, and the second is a holiday gift (postcard).

Choose one of the following methods how to make snowdrops out of paper with your own hands:

  • origami;
  • from plain paper;
  • from corrugated;
  • in quilling technique;
  • applique.


So you get flat or voluminous details that can be decorated on a sheet or turned into a full-fledged bouquet. It is easy to put in a vase or attach to the wall.

As in any other method, first you need to prepare all the materials. If you are going to make many flowers, it is better to cut out patterns in the shape of a figure with three petals from cardboard: one larger for the snowdrop itself, and the second for the sepals. By the way, you can initially cut a round flower with nine petals from a square blank, which you can then cut into three elements. One of them is encircled on cardboard as a stencil, according to which all other parts are already made in the right quantity. Once the templates are created, get to work. The sequence will be as follows:

  1. For each flower, cut out two pieces of white paper from a large template and one from a small blank from a green sheet.
  2. Take the first white blank and wind its petals (each in turn) onto the skewer. This will add volume. Do the same with the green part.
  3. The edges of the second white part should be rounded with scissors, and then with the same tool to tighten up the elements obtained, having previously applied yellow paint to the edges. From this blank, the middle of the flower is made.
  4. Collect the base of a snowdrop: glue white to the yellow part, and green on top.
  5. The design obtained in the previous step is installed on a green stick or a toothpick, which can be wrapped with a strip of green paper with gluing in several places.
  6. Perform the required number of flowers, collect them in a bouquet, decorate with floral grass and put in a vase.

Your decoration for the interior is ready.

Use corrugated paper

This type of material gives a more realistic composition. So, you will make very gentle, almost like real, snowdrops from paper with your own hands. For beginners, the first method from the previous section, and this one, will do. Use any combination of materials and technology. The sequence of work will be as follows:

  1. Cut out blanks of petals, sepals and leaves from paper (white and green). In order for each instance of its appearance to be identical, it is enough to fold a rectangular blank into several layers and cut out the desired shape with scissors. It is clear that each layer will have the same detail.
  2. Glue a cotton ball onto a skewer, wire or toothpick. Dip it in a bowl of millet.
  3. Cut a small rectangle of white corrugated paper and make a carved edge with rounded details that are colored yellow. After drying, wrap this element around the cotton pestle made in the previous step.
  4. Glue the petals and sepals;
  5. Wrap a stick (wire) with a strip of green paper. Decorate the stem with leaves.
  6. Perform the desired number of colors and combine them into a composition.

Such a decoration is suitable both for the interior and for decorating a gift box, bag or, for example, casket.

DIY snowdrops for children: a master class

If you are going to make such a craft with a child, for example, as a gift to your grandmother by March 8, then it is better to choose a simpler method. The application is perfect.

It is easy to decorate a card or the surface of a gift box with it.

Such decor is performed as follows:

  1. The easiest way is to cut flower blanks of the desired shape from paper at a time using one shape, but you can also make individual petals that are glued together.
  2. Prepare thin strips of green paper for the stalk or wire (the desired shade already) or use a colored ribbon.
  3. Cut the leaves.
  4. Stick all the parts on a flat base (postcard, box).
  5. Decorate with additional decor (sequins, beads, bows).




We put flowers for a bouquet or applique

Children will also be interested in making bulk snowdrops from paper with their own hands. Origami will allow you to make such a souvenir, and the kids will start work with pleasure.

To do this, it is enough to fold the shape of a flower according to the scheme from a white sheet and attach to it a wire wrapped in a strip of green corrugated paper. Cut leaves from the same material and stick to the stalk.

Quilling card

In this way, you can make any flowers, including snowdrops, from paper (with your own hands!). The application of twisted parts turns out lace-like and very effective, although everything is done quite simply. You will need strips (about five millimeters thick) of white and green paper.

An ordinary office one is suitable, but it is even better to buy a designer one, for example, metallized. The details of the flower are twisted using a special tool or an ordinary knitting needle, skewer or toothpick. Perform the required number of blanks of the desired shape, combine them with glue into a single composition. Elements are glued both to the plane and in the form of a relief with volume. Many create 3D floristic compositions.
