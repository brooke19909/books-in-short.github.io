Interesting card games for two

Many people like to play cards. This not only allows you to have fun, but also develops logical thinking skills, the ability to analyze the situation, count points, as well as attention, perseverance, memory, because you need to not only be able to correctly add points to each player, but also learn the rules of the game.

"Witch"

Before starting the game from the deck you need to get one of the ladies. After shuffling, the cards are distributed equally among the players. The last unpaired goes to the one who handed out. "Witch" is the scariest card, of course, this is the queen of spades. When playing a card game for two, the players immediately understand who got it, but this does not matter, the situation can change dramatically after the first move.

To begin with, each player searches for paired cards and sets the pairs aside. For example, two tens, two aces, two jacks. Only single images remain in the hand. In such a card game for two, the rules are as follows.

The first player holds his cards in his outstretched shirts to the second player. He draws from the fan one of the cards, any, as he wishes. If he formed a pair, he immediately sets it aside.

Then comes the turn of another player to draw a card. A witch may also get caught. The player who has the Queen of Spades left is losing.

"I believe - I do not believe"

This is one of the most fun card games for two. You can play in a big company. All cards are dealt to the players. The goal of the game is to collect all available fours of cards, for example, if a player has 4 sixes in his hands, he gets rid of them, putting them aside. The winner is the one who quickly stays empty-handed.

The first move is made by the player who was the dealer. He puts 1, 2, 3 or 4 cards in the middle of the table, ‚Äúshirts‚Äù up, and announces what kind of cards these are, for example, 2 ladies. The move is transmitted. The main intrigue is that you can deceive your opponent in every possible way, throwing up completely different cards.

For example, six and eight are laid out on the table, and the player says that he posted two aces. You can believe him, even if you know that he is cheating. In this case, the second player puts his one or two cards, then announces that he also put two aces. Now it‚Äôs the turn to doubt the veracity of the first player. If you turn over the cards, everyone sees that there really are two aces, then the player takes the whole buy to himself. In this case, he can really get aces, collecting all four cards, he lays them aside. The one who first got rid of all the cards wins.

"Drunkard"

"Clabor"

This card game for two is considered analytical, since you need to think over the moves in advance, take risks or pass, depending on the face value of the cards that the player got after the deal. They play it up to 501 points. Before the start of the game, you need to prepare a pencil and a piece of paper, draw a table and write down all the points won in the game. After each move, they are summed up and the total number of points is displayed. The winner is the first to score 501 points.

Six cards are dealt to each player, three more are on the table in front of the players. The rest are put in a deck and a trump card is set, as in the game in "Fool". Point (or 21)

One of the most popular card games for two adults is considered to be the "Point", otherwise called "Twenty-one." This is a simple game, the rules are simple, a lot depends on luck. One player holds a deck of cards and gives out one opponent. He counts the points. He needs to score the number of points close to the number 21. It is better to score less than sort out. If, as a result of the count, the player realizes that he has gone through the cards, then he must definitely say that. Then the opponent automatically wins.

If you are lucky, and when calculating, you get exactly 21 points, then you also become a winner. If you have, for example, 20 points, but your opponent has 18, then you won. There is one more feature. If two aces have fallen, then this is also a victory, although the points are too much. This is called the "banker's point."
