Review of C5 Nokia. Characteristics, reviews

Released in 2010, the C5 was a prime example of producer greed. Although the phone is not bad in its own way, Nokia was able to instantly ruin the positive impression by voicing the price.

Design





Despite the fact that Nokia C5 is an inexpensive device, the appearance turned out to be very solid. In addition to plastic, metal elements are also present in the case. This lends a resemblance to the more expensive E-Series models.

C5 solidity is diluted with a rounded shape and a stroke of the body. The device looks softer and not so striking. On the other hand, the device does not stand out against the background of its brothers.

The user will also like the dimensions of the Nokia C5 00. The device came out lightweight, only 89.3 grams. The thickness of 12.3 mm let me down a little, but for a cellular one this is quite an acceptable characteristic.

The front of the C5 is covered with painted plastic, similar to metal. To protect the display, a panel with a black border is used. A keyboard was placed under the screen, and a front-facing speaker and a speaker were at the top.

The upper end was reserved for the charging socket, 3.5 mm input, as well as a USB connector. Although the manufacturer significantly overloaded the side, this did not affect comfortable use.

On the left side there is only a place for the strap, and on the right there is a volume control and a slot for a flash drive. The memory card goes deep into the device, and it will be very difficult to get it without foreign objects. Problems will arise with the volume control, which is deeply recessed into the body. It will be inconvenient for the user to use the buttons.

At the back of the device, the manufacturer placed the main camera, a flash, two speakers and a logo. The battery cover is completely made of metal. However, the material is very susceptible to scratches. Damage to the cover is immediately apparent.
