Honduras: attractions, interesting places, country history, political system, historical facts and events, photos, reviews and travel tips

Honduras is a Latin American state with significant tourism potential. There is everything in order to have a great vacation. The magnificent nature, favorable climate, the sights of Honduras - all this will captivate even the most experienced traveler. To become a tourist Mecca for Russians, only remoteness prevents the country. So, a flight from Moscow to Tegucigalpa takes at least 28 hours.

Geographical position

To understand where Honduras is, just remember the location of the two Americas on the map. In the very center of the isthmus connecting the continents, it is not difficult to find the name of the country. From the west, its shores are washed by the waters of the Pacific Ocean, and from the east - by the Caribbean Sea. The geographical neighbors of the state are El Salvador, Guatemala and Nicaragua. Local time is 9 hours behind Moscow.

Nature and climate

Most of the country is a mountainous plateau. An exception is the coastal lowlands and valleys of the rivers Ulua, Aguan, Patuka. The ridges formed by lavas and metaphorical rocks are picturesque. Peak Cerro Las Minas is considered the most recognizable peak of Honduras. A photo of a natural landmark towering 2,870 meters adorns country brochures.

Honduran plateaus are covered in rainforests. Deciduous and coniferous trees, shrubs, various types of vines grow in the jungle. The fauna of forests is extremely rich. The jungle is inhabited by a variety of animals: from alligators to panthers and exotic armadillos.

The country is located in the tropical zone. Therefore, the climate is mild. Even in mountainous regions, the average annual temperature is about +20 ¬∞ C. The rainy season begins in September and lasts until the end of January.

State structure, population, religion

The constitution states that Honduras is a unitary state, in which the supreme power belongs to the president. A senior official also heads the cabinet. Legislation is entrusted to the deputies of the National Congress. The mandate of the president and parliamentarians is valid for 4 years.

The territory of the state is divided into 18 departments. The largest cities are the capital of Tegucigalpa and San Pedro Sula with 1,680,000 and 1,300,000 inhabitants, respectively. The total population of the country exceeds 8,000,000 people, among which mulattoes predominate. Indians account for no more than 7%.

Contrary to prejudice, not all people profess Catholicism. There are many Protestants. Ethnic Indians continue to adhere to traditional beliefs. Spanish is recognized as the official language.

Features of the economy

Less than half of Hondurans live in cities. As a result, the main income to the state treasury comes from agriculture. Budget fullness depends on the prices of traditional export products: coffee, bananas, cigars, palm oil, seafood, beef. Tourists who come to enjoy the sights of Honduras help to create a positive balance.

Industry is poorly developed. The main suppliers of goods to the country's markets are companies from the USA, Mexico, Canada, Brazil. The local currency, named after the Native American leader, is extremely weak. Today, 24 lempiers give for one American dollar.

Historical note: main events, interesting facts

The history of the country of Honduras is eventful. Once upon a time, paya, lenka, and other primitive tribes inhabited by Maya Indians inhabited these lands. After the exodus of the representatives of ancient civilization to Yucatan, everything returned to normal.

Europeans appeared here in the first third of the 16th century. Aborigines without reverence reacted to the conquistadors. The leader of Lempira managed to organize armed resistance, brutally crushed by the conquerors.

For almost 300 years, Honduras remained a colony of Spain. The state succeeded in freeing itself from the "guardianship" of the mother country only in 1823. Following the declaration of independence, the formation of political parties began, the national bourgeoisie appeared, and the class stratification of society accelerated. The formation of statehood was difficult. The country survived in the 19th century 12 civil conflicts, constantly fought with its neighbors.

The next century was also not calm. Honduras has gone the way traditional for most Latin American states: from the military junta to moderate liberalism. The geographical position has made the country an object of US interest. Today, it is, in fact, a satellite of continental hegemon.

The main attractions of Honduras

Tourist attraction to the country is provided by man-made monuments created by several civilizations and delightful creations of nature. Several interesting places are worth mentioning separately.

To the south-east of San Pedro Sula is a unique archaeological site - the ruins of the ancient Mayan Indians polis. One and a half millennia ago, Kopan was a prosperous, densely populated city. Huge palaces were erected for powerful local rulers. Majestic temples dedicated to the terrible Indian gods. Some buildings are well preserved, despite the tropical climate.

Tegucigalpa, proclaimed the capital of the state in 1880, is considered a metropolis by local standards. The city has always played an important role in the life of the country, therefore there are many historical monuments and beautiful buildings on its streets. Tourists must see the gilded altar of the Cathedral of San Miguel, the medieval church of St. Francis, the equestrian statue of the national hero F. Morasan. Of great interest to visitors are the expositions of the National Art Gallery and Tegucigalpa museums.

Popular resorts

The lovers of relaxation on the beaches of the resorts of Honduras will delight. Prices for food and accommodation are low, and the level of service is quite high.

Especially popular with tourists are recreations in the vicinity of the city of Tela. The coast here is covered with fine white sand. Jeanette Kawas National Park is nearby.

The resort town of Puerto Cortes is famous for its magnificent beaches and carnival. A colorful festival is held here every year. Travelers from all over the world come to see the colorful extravaganza.

Adherents of underwater fishing have chosen the tropical island of Utila. There are several man-made and wild beaches of incredible beauty. Among the most visited resorts include Trujillo and Omoa.

Roatan Island is a landmark in Honduras that is of particular interest to diving enthusiasts. Coral reefs off the coast of the pearl of the Islas de la Bahia archipelago are picturesque. Graceful grottoes settled moray eels and crabs. Thousands of bright fish scurry between bizarre corals. In the spring, whale sharks migrate here. Comfortable hotels are built on the island. In diving centers, beginners will be taught the wisdom of underwater travel, and professionals will be offered equipment for rent.

Tips from experienced travelers

According to tourists, in the country a Russian with an average level of income will be able to relax perfectly. Travelers who are going to visit interesting places in Honduras will benefit from the advice of people who have already visited the country:
