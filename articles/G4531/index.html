Electron and atom configurations

In the atom are the nucleus and electrons, between which there are forces of an electrostatic nature. Energy will increase as the distance between the nucleus and the electron decreases. It is believed that the potential energy will be zero if it is infinitely distant from the core. This condition is used as a reference. Thus, the relative energy of the electron is determined.

The electron shell is a set of energy levels. Belonging to one of them is expressed by the main quantum number n.





Prime number

It refers to a certain energy level with a set of orbitals, which have similar values, consisting of natural numbers: n = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 ... When an electron moves from one stage to another, the main quantum number changes . Keep in mind that not all levels are filled with electrons. When filling the shell of an atom, the principle of least energy is realized. In this case, his condition is called unexcited or basic.

Orbital numbers

Each level has orbitals. Those with similar energies form a sublevel. Such assignment is made using the orbital (or as it is also called - side) quantum number l, which takes the values ‚Äã‚Äãof integers from zero to n - 1. So the electron having the main and orbital quantum numbers n and l can be equal to starting l = 0 and ending with l = n - 1.

This shows the nature of the movement of the corresponding sublevel and energy level. For l = 0 and any value of n, the electron cloud will have the shape of a sphere. Its radius will be directly proportional to n. For l = 1, the electron cloud will take the form of infinity or figure eight. The larger the value of l, the more complex the shape will become, and the electron energy will increase.

Elementary particles and nuclei

Quantum numbers in elementary particle physics are their internal characteristics, which determine the interactions and laws of transformations. In addition to spin s, this is an electric charge Q, which for all elementary particles is equal to zero or an integer, negative or positive; baryon charge B (in the particle - zero or one, in the antiparticle - zero or minus one); lepton charges, where Le and Lm are equal to zero, one, and in the antiparticle - zero and minus one; isotopic spin with an integer or half integer number; Strangeness S and others. All these quantum numbers are applied both to elementary particles and to atomic nuclei.
In the broad sense of the word, they are called physical quantities that determine the motion of a particle or system and which are conserved. However, it is not necessary that they belong to a discrete spectrum of all kinds of values.
