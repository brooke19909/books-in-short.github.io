Sardinia, Cagliari: hotels, attractions and photos

The small beautiful island of Sardinia has a rich history of conquests, ups and downs due to its unique location. Today it is one of the most popular holiday destinations of both prominent persons and celebrities, as well as ordinary tourists.

The main city of the island - Cagliari (Sardinia) - begins its history from the 8th century BC. e., he managed to survive in all wars to the present day. Only having been here, you can understand that the Sardinians live in a completely different rhythm of time, their leisurelyness gives charm to these places, so many tourists return here to rest, and the rich buy cottages and villas.

History of Cagliari

The founders of this ancient city were the Phoenicians, who built in this place a colony of settlers called Karalis. After 2 centuries, she began to pass from one hand to another:

  • at the end of the 6th century, the island became part of the Carthage Republic;

  • in 238 BC e. he went to the Romans;

  • the entire period of the Punic War - from 218 to 201 BC. e. - Sardinia (Cagliari) was the residence of Titus Manlius, the Roman commander;

  • after the civil wars of the 49-45s BC e. the city received the status of a capital; its inhabitants acquired not only the rights of free citizens of the Roman Empire, but also roads and water supply;

  • in the 5th century AD e. Caralis fell upon the invasion of vandals;

  • in 532 the island was annexed again to the Roman Empire by the emperor of Byzantium Justinian;

  • in the 9th century, Byzantium lost power in Sardinia, and 4 communities were formed here, which were governed by elected judges;

  • in the 11th century, Arabs often raided the island, and in the 13th century, a struggle between Pisa and the Genoese Republic began for it;

  • after the victory of Pisa in 1258, a defensive fortress and powerful walls around the main quarters were built in Cagliari;

  • in 1323, Pope Boniface VIII wrote this land to the Aragonese king, having no rights to it;

  • in 1328, under the law of the new government, local residents were expelled from Cagliari, and at the end of the 15th century the island became part of Spain;

  • when in 1701 a war broke out between the Bourbons and the Habsburgs over who would rule Spain, Sardinia passed to the Austrians, then to the Spaniards, until in 1718 passed to Victor Amadeus, Duke of Savoy;

  • in 1847, at the request of the inhabitants of the island, he was annexed to the kingdom of Italy, and the same laws and privileges as the inhabitants of the continent are legislatively assigned to its inhabitants.





After World War II, the Americans built an air base on the island, which today is a strategic object of NATO. This did not prevent Sardinia (Cagliari and other cities of the island) from becoming the best resort in the Mediterranean.

To evaluate all the sights of Cagliari (Sardinia), one month is not enough, so eventful days are here with a leisurely flow of time.

Amphitheater

The ancient amphitheater, well preserved to this day, was built between the 1st and 2nd centuries BC. e.





Huge by those standards - 6000 m 2 , it was carved directly into the limestone rock. At one time, it accommodated up to 10,000 spectators during gladiatorial battles. But it was also used for theatrical performances, and in the days of early Christianity for public executions.

According to the tradition of that time, the amphitheater was divided into several sectors (according to the estates), each of which led its own entrance. Since the 19th century, it became the property of the capital and is under protection.

Today, theatrical performances, festivals and concerts are held here. All of Sardinia, Cagliari, first of all, comes to fairs held in the ancient walls.

Cathedral

Many pilgrims know Sardinia, Cagliari (many have seen the photo below, it depicts the church in question) thanks to the Cathedral of St. Mary, founded in the 13th century on the basis of a small church of the same name. Since that time, it was rebuilt several times, each time giving an increasingly baroque look to the originally strict Romanesque style. This went on for several centuries, until the next architect ordered to dismantle the baroque jewelry and returned to the cathedral its pristine beauty, which tourists can still admire today.





The interior of the temple is very magnificent, and the preserved medieval frescoes, columns and arches convey the atmosphere of humility and faith, characteristic of the people of that time. Today it is a place of pilgrimage, since the holy relics of the Sicilian martyrs and one of the most important relics of Catholicism - thorns from the crown of thorns of Jesus Christ are in the cathedral.

Botanical Garden

The island of Sardinia (Cagliari), planned at the beginning of the 18th century, received the island only 60 years later, after the Palabanda Valley began to belong to the capital's university. It was students under the guidance of professors who created it with their own hands.

Among the ancient Roman water supply systems and grottoes, more than 2000 species of plants from different parts of the planet grow. The garden is divided into 40 zones, each of which contains certain types of flora. We should also pay attention to the huge herbarium located in this territory. Its uniqueness lies in the fact that some of its specimens are plants that no longer grow on the planet.

Free Wi-Fi is available on site, breakfast is delivered to each room. At the disposal of tourists dry cleaning, luggage storage, safe. Within walking distance are the port, train station, bastion and the University of Cagliari.
