Route Moscow - Prague by car: travel reviews

Traveling by car to EU countries is a special type of vacation. If adventurism, a craving for adventure is inherent in you, then such a trip is exactly what you need. She will bring you complete satisfaction and joy from the trip. Of course, for the trip to go well, you need to carefully prepare for it, as well as for any important event. If you plan to go the Moscow-Prague route by car, you should collect as much material as possible about cities, countries that you have to travel. Find out what rules, orders, customs and traditions are established there so that no unforeseen situations arise. Before the trip, you need to carefully prepare the car, documents, think through from and to your route.

Preparation of documents





If you plan to travel by car to Prague from Moscow, then, first of all, you need to contact the Czech Embassy for a Schengen visa. Another option is to visit a visa issuing center. In order for the Czechs to be calm that you will not be left in the country without a roof over their heads, they will certainly require you to receive fax confirmation that the room in any of the hotels has been booked. By the way, confirmation by e-mail will not suit them, so book a room in advance and wait for confirmation of the operation from there. To do this is quite simple on any of the sites, then contact the hotel and discuss the timing of receiving a fax. It will also require statements from your accounts. You must prove your solvency, at least 50 euros should go out per person per day.

To overcome the route Moscow - Prague by car, the following documents are also required:

  • Passport
  • Technical passport for the vehicle.
  • Rights (driver's license) - better, of course, international.
  • The issued green card.
  • Car insurance.
  • Insurance for all passengers, covering at least 30 thousand euros for each.

Car preparation

Before starting the trip Moscow - Prague by car, you must take care of the technical condition of your vehicle. Go through the running gear diagnostics , electricians, and retool your car seasonally. Check the expiration dates of all drugs in the medicine cabinet for a fire extinguisher. The presence of emergency stop signs, tow rope is mandatory, repair kits, additional splitter cords may be needed. The spare tire must be in good condition. Reflective vests and a RUS sticker will come in handy.





Route selection

Know that by choosing the route Moscow - Prague, the distance by car in any case will have to be covered through Belarus and Poland. Many when preparing the route use the Google maps service. After the start and end points are entered into the route selection, and this is Moscow - Prague, the service will offer you several routes to choose from. How long does it take to get from Moscow to Prague by car? If you do not stop to rest in Poland, then you can overcome the path in about a day.

So, there are two ways to choose:

  • Moscow - Brest - Warsaw - Wroclaw - Prague. This route is about 1950 kilometers, but in fact it takes longer to get there, since the road is laid through many settlements where speed is allowed within 50 km / h, and even less along the mountain section.
  • Moscow - Brest - Warsaw - Katowice - Brno - Prague. If you decide to get on this route, then the only minus is you will not admire the beauty of the mountain scenery. In general, this road is longer, the distance will be two thousand kilometers. But on the other hand, you can drive along the entire route at an average speed of 100 km / h, and on an ideal expressway. Many sections of the road here are paid.

So, before deciding on a route, study what cities you will travel through, what interesting places you can see, where to relax and eat.





Border and customs clearance

So, taking the route Moscow - Prague by car, you need to go onto the Minsk highway and boldly go straight ahead. The track is good enough, and in some places just great. After leaving Moscow in about five hours, you will arrive at the border with the Republic of Belarus. The transport inspection must pay the transport fee and keep the check until the end of the journey. About 600 km to move around the territory of the republic, by the evening you will find yourself in Brest. It is better to stay overnight, and in the morning already cross the border with Poland. From here to her five minutes go.

Before crossing the Polish border, an environmental fee must be paid . Moving is carried out either at the Brest - Terespol point, or to the south, in Domachevo. The car must be changed into any studless coat, even in winter. In no case do not bring radar detectors with you, they are prohibited in the EU countries! Serious problems can occur when they are detected. The goods are allowed to be transported in an amount not exceeding one and a half thousand euros. If there is no problem with this, then you will be sent to the CD corridor, the line there is moving fast.





Travel in Poland

The path from the Polish border to Prague will be no more than three hundred kilometers. It is necessary to purchase a vignette, it gives the right to move freely on local freeways. You will not see any border post anymore, only signs will inform you that you are already in the Czech Republic. Driving through Poland, do not miss the opportunity to visit local attractions, because this country is considered one of the most beautiful in Europe. When planning a trip Moscow - Prague by car, include Warsaw in the route. A hotel room can be booked in advance via the Internet. Highlight the day and visit the most interesting places here.

Traffic rules in the Czech Republic

  • At all pedestrian crossings, a pedestrian has advantages, so be extremely careful.
  • Speed. In the village, compliance with the speed limit is 50 km / h, outside - 90 km / h, if you get on the highway, 130 km / h is allowed. Everywhere there are radars that are invisible to the eye, so if after exceeding you suddenly find a car overtaking you with the inscription "Follow me", expect a fine.
  • Alcohol is strictly prohibited.
  • Low beam is required both at night and during the day. Fog lights are allowed only with poor visibility, fog.
  • Safety belts must be fastened, including in the rear seats. It is forbidden to sit forward to children under 12 years of age and persons below 150 centimeters in height.
  • It is forbidden to use mobile devices without Handsfree.
  • Payment of the fine can be made directly from the police, they accept almost any card.
