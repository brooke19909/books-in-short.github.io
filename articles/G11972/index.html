Why don't they fly to the moon? Reasons for termination of flights

Why did people stop flying to the moon? The answer to this question is not so simple. The course of the history of the development of the lunar plains was influenced by some circumstances, which should be considered in a certain sequence. It is necessary to understand what is reality and what is fiction. Do not forget that the lunar program was developed not only by Russians, but also by Americans. Both projects were abruptly interrupted without specific explanation. Naturally, this caused a lot of questions, and the most important of them - why did they stop flying to the moon? The reasons for such a quick refusal to develop a strategically important project are due to mysterious circumstances.



NASA Success: Moon Race

To better understand why people no longer fly to the moon, you should carefully study the history of the development of this Earth satellite. First of all, it is necessary to mention the race that the two superpowers staged for excellence in this world.

Everyone knows that in that period of history the priority of space exploration was fixed precisely for the USSR. Naturally, the Americans were well aware that their rivals had advanced far ahead in space exploration and it was not so easy to get ahead of them. To reduce the distance, NASA needed to make some kind of breakthrough in space exploration. At this time, the lunar program was created. About 40 thousand employees worked on its development for eight years. Do not forget that about 110 billion dollars were spent on the lunar program. But if there was good funding, then why stopped flying to the moon? The facts were silent for a long time. Until now, some moments of the history of the development of lunar spaces remain incomprehensible.

It is worth noting that the development of Americans in this area were successful. After all, the key link here was Vernen von Braun. This man worked for Adolf Hitler during World War II. It was this specialist who created the legendary V-2.



American Apollo

After the long labors of a large team of specialists, the Americans achieved tremendous success. Werner von Braun created a carrier with sufficient power. However, in finished form, the product possessed simply huge dimensions. It was not possible to transfer it by land. Therefore, the carrier was delivered to the spaceport using water transport. It is worth noting that the Saturn engine had a capacity of 180 million horsepower. When the carrier was launched in nearby buildings, the ceilings showered and all the windows were knocked out.

In 1969 (in March), Apollo 9 conducted space tests of the lunar module, and in May Apollo 10 rehearsed the landing on the moon, descending to a height of 15 kilometers from the surface of the Earth‚Äôs satellite. There was no full landing. On July 2, 1969, a crew was delivered to the surface of the moon in Apollo 11. After that, six more expeditions were carried out with the crew disembarking.



USSR and moon race

As for the USSR, the superpower suffered many setbacks in the lunar race and was much inferior to the competitor. At that time, a team of specialists led by S.P. Korolev and V.N. Chelomei worked on the development of a flight to the moon. However, for a long time, Russian scientists did not succeed in creating a carrier that would have sufficient power.

Some time later, S.P. Korolev died. But it was he who was the key link in the project. As a result of the sad event, the situation was greatly aggravated. It is worth noting that the USSR spent all its forces on a space exploration program. Therefore, the lunar race of opportunities and finances was simply not enough. Of course, the situation improved over time. However, it still remains unclear why they are not flying to the moon now.



The closure of the lunar programs

Why don't they fly to the moon and why were all lunar programs closed? At the end of 1972, NASA stopped conducting research. The lunar program was closed. It is worth noting that the Soviet Union also curtailed all its projects related to the exploration of the moon, without having landed its crew on the surface of the Earth's satellite. After that, no one else tried to resume flights. During this period, a huge number of multi-billion dollar projects were closed. So why did people stop flying to the moon, and why was there such a rush?

Of course, many assumed that the Russians simply lost interest in the program. But to understand the cause of the Americans is very difficult. After all, they were able to achieve success in their developments. Also, many suggest that the reason for the high cost of such programs is contrived. Indeed, at that time, most of the allocated funds was spent on the creation of missiles and launching pads. The cost of one launch was equal to the cost of one bomber. In addition, it is not entirely clear why they are not flying to the moon now. After all, technology has stepped far forward. This suggests that the reasons are more significant than lack of funding or lost interest.

Lunar Anomaly Studies

Of course, anomalies on the moon could become the main reason for the closure of programs to study its surfaces. But first of all, it was necessary to study all incomprehensible and inexplicable phenomena. Therefore, in the United States in 1965, a whole scientific society was created, which was engaged in the study of lunar anomalies. At that time, only highly qualified specialists were part of the team. Over the entire period of the work of this scientific community, many anomalies on the moon have been identified. Many of them were difficult to explain. For this reason, in 1968 a document was created, called the "Chronological catalog of reports of lunar events."

What did they find on the moon?

About 579 unexplained anomalous phenomena occurring on the surface and in the orbit of the moon were indicated here. Among such phenomena, there were:

  1. Disappearing craters.
  2. Geometric figures.
  3. Gigantic domes that can change color.
  4. Colored trenches that can lengthen at a speed of 6 kilometers per hour.
  5. Luminous objects and so on.

Such phenomena could not be explained, but neither the Americans nor the Russians wanted to stop the lunar race. As a result, spacecraft launches began, as it was decided to fly and see everything with my own eyes. At that time, no one paid attention to the presence of anomalies. But why not fly to the moon even after exploring many phenomena?

Years later, it became clear that the guesses were correct. Radio amateurs from Australia and Switzerland were able to catch astronaut talks at a different frequency immediately after landing.
