UK coins: pennies and pounds

The UK numismatic system is extremely complex. All UK coins are regularly updated, and until 1971 they were still not a multiple of ten. This circumstance forced ordinary buyers to carry out a number of complicated mathematical operations in their minds.





The official and unchanged currency of all UK territories for decades is Pound sterling. Today, the British also use pence. Great Britain's pound and two-pound coins adorn Queen Elizabeth II's profile. On the back is a symbolic coat of arms. It is a reflection of several heraldic traditions. The basis of his drawing lay the English and Scottish lions and the Celtic harp.

The cycle has a certain thematic focus. One season may be devoted to metropolitan megacities, the other to bridge structures and famous crossings.

Anniversary Issues

As in Russia, the numismatics of the United Kingdom has its own limited series, the production of which was timed to coincide with a particular solemn event in the life of the country. There are four varieties in total.

Please note that the 1 pound coin (UK) is not available in limited editions. In everyday life you can meet only the limits of 50 pennies and 2 ¬£. Every year, the mint minted in one variety, sometimes two.

In addition to the usual jubilee designs, there are three more types available in the UK. These are large-denominated coins cast from precious metals and the so-called Mandy money. The latter are a special invention of the English royal throne. Representatives of the ruling family give them in the form of alms. In the MM store they accept according to the indicated face value, but collectors are ready to give a fortune for them.





Colonial Numismatics

For centuries, England has been known as an influential marine mistress. These times have sunk into oblivion, but their legacy still serves as proof of the country's mighty past navy. Currently, more than ten United Kingdom autonomies use the pound sterling. They also have a 1 penny coin (Great Britain). However, the land itself is not part of the UK.

Such territories include Gibraltar, the Isle of Man and Jersey, St. Helena, Asuncion, Guernsey and other entities. Many of them issue their own banknotes. Gibraltar officially uses local bills, but their pence (penny) is English.

On the Isle of Man, national and British pounds are in use. Anniversary series are periodically issued.

Back to the past

The first monetary unit, which was cast from metal and used by residents of all territories of the United Kingdom, was a coin of 1 kroon. Great Britain released it in 1526. It was made entirely of gold. Over time, the nominal weight was reduced. At the court of Jacob I, its value was equal to five shillings. And after 1663 she was replaced by a guinea.
