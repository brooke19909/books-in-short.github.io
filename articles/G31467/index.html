Guadalajara, Mexico: description, climate, attractions

Guadalajara (Mexico) is the capital and largest city of the state of Jalisco, as well as the district of the municipality with the corresponding name. It is located in the western part of the country and is considered the 10th largest city by population in Latin America. It was named after the Spanish Guadalajara, whose name in translation from the Andalusian dialect of the Arabic language means "river / valley of stones."



The city of Guadalajara is the cultural center of Mexico, as it is here that hosts international book fairs, film festivals and other major cultural events. Climatic conditions

The climate of Guadalajara is subtropical. Movement to the north in the zone of inter-tropical convergence in summer causes heavy rain, while the rest of the year the climate is quite arid. The highest temperatures usually occur in May (up to +33 ... +39 ). March is considered the driest month, and July is the wettest , since during it more than a quarter of the average annual rainfall falls.

During the rainy seasons in Guadalajara, daytime storms often occur. They bring hail with them. Winter is very warm . Even in January, here the temperature is kept at +1 ... +25 . It never drops below zero. Cold fronts can sometimes bring warm light rain into the city , which usually lasts for several days. Snowfall is extremely rare. The penultimate one was in 1881, and the last in December 1997. This makes the village a real Mecca for tourists who are tired of almost constant cold.

Origin history

The toponym Guadalajara in Mexico changed its location several times. At first the city was in Messa del Cerro , now known as Nochistlan ( Zacatecas ). It was founded here on the orders of Nu√±o de Guzm√°n , who wanted to protect his conquests from hostile natives. About a year later (in 1533), the city was moved to the area near Tonala , and after another 4 years - to Tlakotan . At this time, the Spanish king Charles I granted the authorities of Guadalajara a coat of arms, which can still be seen in the city.

In 1543, the settlement was brutally attacked by the peoples of Porticakes , Kaksan and Zakateto , commanded by Tenamkli . It happened during the war in Mixton . Wicca Antonio de Mendoza had to crush the rebellion on his own after the Spaniards were defeated in several battles. For the sake of peace, he even made some concessions. In particular, he freed Indian slaves and granted them amnesty.

The village in those years barely survived the war, and residents thanked for the salvation of the archangel Michael, who, it is worth noting, is still considered the patron saint of the city. After the reign of peace, Guadalajara was once again moved, this time to the Atemajak Valley. Here she is to this day. The city was officially recognized on February 14, 1550 .





Further story

In 1563, the construction of the cathedral began in Guadalajara (Mexico). In 1575, Augustinians and Dominicans came, making the city a center of evangelism (the process of conveying the Good News of Jesus Christ). In 1669, the villages of Mekhikalsingo , Analko and Metskitan were added to the main part of the settlement . In 1792, a university was founded in Guadalajara on the site of the old college of Santo Thomas . Despite such an early foundation, he began to work only in 1925.

At the end of 1810, Miguel Hidalgo returned to Guadalajara, the remaining capital of Nueva Galicia. Initially, he himself and the army he brought were welcomed by the inhabitants with joy. In those days, the conditions were difficult for the workers, and Hidalgo promised to lower taxes and put an end to slavery. But the rebel army, with its mockery of the city dwellers, especially the royalists, made the people shudder with horror. Despite the fact that Hidalgo fulfilled his promise, the angry royalists attacked the city and won. The commander was forced to flee to Aguascalientes.

The state of Jalisco was founded in 1823, Guadalajara became its capital. In 1844 there was an uprising against the government of President Antonio Lopez de Santa Anna . Soon it was suppressed, but during the time that Santa Anna was hiding in Guadalajara, Jose Joaquin Herrer came to power. In 1864, French troops entered the city, it was recaptured by the Mexicans 2 years later.

From 1884 to 1890, electricity was conducted in Guadalajara, railways were laid, and an observatory was equipped. In 1947, the first industrial park was created. In 1980, the city was divided between different segments of the population. Wealthy people tend to live in Hidalgo and Juarez in the southwest and northwest, and the lower ones in the center. A little later, this state of affairs changed a bit: the poor began to inhabit the periphery of the city, and the middle peasants and the rich moved in the direction of Zapopon .

In 1992, powerful explosions occurred in Guadalajara (Mexico): a mass of gasoline escaped from the sewer system and destroyed 8 km of the street in the city center. According to the official version, 206 died, about 500 were injured, and about 15 thousand people were left homeless. Cash damage ranged from 300 to 1 million dollars. The culprits were quickly found, but soon they were all freed.

Since 1996, divisions of multinational corporations began to open in Guadalajara: Kodak, Hewlett-Packard, Motorola and IBM. This led to an increase in the number of foreigners in the city and capital in the treasury. Conducting important international events, such as the first Cumbre Iberoamericana (1991) , the Third Summit of Heads of State and Government from Latin America, the Caribbean and the European Union (2004) and the Pan American Games (2011) , allowed the village to become the second by economic importance, the city of Mexico.

Guadalajara is also famous for its musical works in the mariachi genre, despite the fact that this style appeared in the neighboring city of Kokul . It all started in 1907, when a group of performers performed on stage at the residence of President Porfirio Diaso . At that moment, visiting was the US Secretary of State John Milton Hay . This made music a symbol of the western part of Mexico, and after the migration of people from a settlement near Plaza Garibaldi , it became a symbol of Mexican identity. Today, Guadalajara hosts numerous festivals, which are played by mariachi musicians . They are traditionally brought together by people from different parts of the globe.
