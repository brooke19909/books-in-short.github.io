One of the smallest and thinnest smartphones at the moment, apparently, is the E52. Nokia tried to, like the E55, have a thickness of about 10 mm, although it turned out to be slightly wider. Available in gray or gold, both options look stylish. Despite the subtlety, the E52 is a reliable phone, unlike some common cheap smartphones.



A few words about Nokia smartphones

As you know, the smartphones of this manufacturer are combined in a linear series of models with some general purpose and functionality. So, the Nseries line is a family consisting solely of smartphones and Internet tablets. Mobile devices included in this line are capable of providing digital multimedia services and have other additional features. All the flagship products of the company are in this series, where most of the advanced technologies and innovations of the Nokia research center are used.

The Eseries line (stands for Executive, i.e., executive) consists of business-oriented smartphones, with an emphasis on improving communication reliability and corporate email support. These phones are for the corporate market. This includes the E52 smartphone that interests us. Nokia has created a device with extended battery life for corporate consumers looking for a well-built phone for their businesses that does not drain quickly. It can also serve as a modem for data transfer using Bluetooth.

The X-, C-, Z-, A-, L- and T-series of Nokia phones are also known.

General impression of the capabilities of E52

"Nokia" on this phone tried to show itself. He has everything here, in a form factor 10 mm thick with a volume of 54 cm 3 and a weight of 98 g. This is very surprising. A tri-band 3G, four-port GSM, Wi-Fi, A-GPS plus magnetometer, Bluetooth, a 3.5 mm standard audio connector and a 3.2 megapixel EDoF camera are all quite impressive, but considering the fact that about half Since the E52-th is occupied by a BP-4L battery, which is usually equipped with the models E90, E61i and E71, the achieved miniaturization level for the Nokia E52 seems even more stunning .

The reviews confirm that the battery with a capacity of 5.5 Wh, a capacity of 1500 mAh and a voltage of 3.7 V can be practically used after charging up to 24-27 hours. This is significantly higher than other phones in its class, released both by Nokia and other manufacturers. If Wi-Fi, Bluetooth are used, and the built-in VOIP client is active, then according to Nokia E52 the instruction informs that the battery can power the phone for up to 24 hours.

Firmware "Nokia" E52

As you know, Nokia E-series smartphones are running Symbian OS. It is used by many large mobile phone brands such as Samsung, Motorola, Sony Erickson and, above all, Nokia. This OS was the most popular smartphone OS in the world until the end of 2010, when the championship was captured by the Android OS. In connection with the transition to Microsoft's Windows Phone OS in January 2014, Nokia stopped accepting new options or changing Symbian software from developers, planning to phase out its use.

At one time, several software platforms were created for this OS, primarily the S60 platform for smartphones. The S60 consists of a set of libraries, standard applications such as telephony and Personal Information Manager (PIM), and Helix-based multimedia players. It is designed for full-featured modern phones with large color screens, which are usually known as smartphones.

From a software point of view, the S60 3rd Edition Feature Pack 2 is very recognizable in E52. The package is very similar to the one in the E75, although in the following months Nokia updated its version of the browser based on the Webkit engine: v7.1. Here it is much faster and has a simpler interface (although there is still no way to copy text from a page or open a new window). New PIM Eseries applications were optimized, Nokia Maps became Ovi Maps 3.0 (complete with unlimited pedestrian navigation), and support for UPnP functionality appeared in Home media.

On the other hand, the absence of both Podcasting and Internet radio is striking. Many users have already demanded their recovery, and the company is considering how the future Nokia E52 firmware will look with these in mind. The latest firmware version v091.004 was released on June 2, 2012.



What's new in photography with the E52?

Nokia in this model, like in the E55, relied on the use of EDoF (increase in depth of field) cameras. This is a new idea, possible only in the digital age. Instead of the bulky and expensive auto focus mechanism, the idea was used that a cheap camera with a fixed focal length can be significantly improved with a custom lens that was designed to focus the RGB light components in different ways. When an image is captured (actually creating three ‚Äúphotos‚Äù, one for each of the RGB components), each part of the photo viewing area is analyzed with a clear definition of the details of the RGB images used for this part, and the corresponding color is formed for all three images. Thus, the normal depth of field for a fixed focus camera expands, and close or very distant objects can be captured quite sharply, which would not be possible with a fixed focus of the camera.

In good lighting conditions, the results can be amazing for such a small camera.

How is sound reproduced in E52?

The mono speaker on its back is, fortunately, crystal clear and loud - a great option for listening to podcasts throughout the house. What about email?

In terms of business software, the Nokia E52 is equipped with the new Nokia Messaging email system, including pseudo-delivery of correspondence from POP3 or IMAP accounts. This is by far the easiest way to mobile email. The full, edited version of Quickoffice v5 comes with firmware.

Application shortcuts work well despite their tiny size.

Third-party applications on E52

Interestingly, a number of third-party applications were fully licensed by Nokia for the E52 and E55. WiPresenter (from Psiloc) allows you to use both Bluetooth and Wi-Fi to control Powerpoint presentations on your computer, while Font Magnifier (also from Psiloc) does exactly what it is intended for, namely: it allows a system-wide font change ( between 50% and 140% of its original size) to make the text more readable (or fit into its large size on the screen) in any application. Psiloc World Traveler (as in the original) is actually freely distributed, but it's nice to see it turned on and providing information about the weather, exchange rates and time. Top Hits Solitaires (from Epocware) is a first-class set of hundreds of variations of card solitaire games. The Advanced Communications Manager (from WebGate) is a call filtering application, and Block Cascade is a Flash-based Gamelion clone.
