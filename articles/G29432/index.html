Moscow defense

The offensive operation of the Nazi forces on Moscow, which began on September 30, 1941, known as the Typhoon, brought them serious success. Near Vyazma, parts of three fronts were defeated, which allowed the enemy to reach the very approaches to the capital. 10/15/1941 GKO USSR announced the evacuation of the city, which caused a real panic in part of the population. But the Germans also needed a respite after the losses incurred, so by November 2 the situation in the Volokolamsk direction had somewhat stabilized. The defense was held by four divisions of the 16th Army (Western Front), including the 316th under the command of I.V. Panfilov.

Vasily Klochkov, whose photo is presented in the article, was a political officer of the 4th company, located near the village of Nelidovo on the Volokolamsk highway. The 316th division formed in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan before the Moscow defense did not participate in hostilities. But the divisional commander, who considers the preservation of the life of fighters to be paramount, conducted exercises, including with an imitation of a tank attack, using a tractor. To create confidence in the possibility of victory over the enemy, already in October he organized raids behind enemy lines, in which Vasily Klochkov twice distinguished himself, represented in both cases to the Order of the Battle Red Banner. On November 16, the 2nd Panzer Division of the German forces attacked the Panfilov‚Äôs positions with the aim of creating a positional advantage on the eve of the expected attack on November 18.

Feat at the Dubosekovo junction

The morning of the 16th in the Volokolamsk direction began with aerial bombing undertaken by the enemy. Fighters of the 4th company of the second battalion, which held the defense at the Dubosekovo junction, counted 35 aircraft. Following the village of Krasikovo in the direction of Moscow came the machine gunners, whose attack was completely repulsed by seven in the morning. But then the tanks went into action. The surviving fighter I.R. Vasiliev recalled how political instructor Klochkov got into his trench. Learning about the number of tanks, he said: "Well, that's okay, one per brother."





His main weapon has always been a draft word and a personal example. On the day of the holiday, November 7, he spoke at a rally of the regiment, preventing panic and encouraging soldiers. In his life, Vasily Klochkov only once visited Moscow, but considered it a matter of honor to defend it. The first tank attack was successfully repelled. Damaged German equipment was smoking on the battlefield, and five vehicles retreated to Zhukovka. But after a while a new party went.

It was then that the political instructor uttered his legendary words, throwing himself from a trench with a bunch of grenades under the armor of an enemy tank, captivating the fighters with his own example. And a few days later in the "Red Star" was described the feat of twenty-eight heroes of the 4th company, who fell at the junction Dubosekovo, but did not miss the Nazis to Moscow. 15 tanks (according to another version - 18) remained on fire on the battlefield, symbolizing the strength of spirit of the Soviet soldier.

Hero Biography

Vasily Klochkov went to his commissar all his life. Born 08.03.1911 in a poor peasant family living in the Saratov province, he knew all the hardships of the hungry 20s in the Volga region. The family went for the best share in Altai, where on the road in Samara, his father, Georgy Petrovich, died. To feed a large family, the mother went to the fist in the service, and Vasily and his brother hired someone. But the boy turned out to be smart, gravitated to knowledge, went through a school of peasant youth. In the Altai village of Lokot, he became a Komsomol leader. Returning to his homeland at age 20, the young man graduated from the construction college and entered the correspondence institute of the People's Commissariat of Trade.





In 1939, Klochkov joined the party, and a year later, together with his wife Nina Georgievna and daughter Elea, he moved to Alma-Ata, where his wife's parents lived. Energetic, active, he soon became the director of the City Industry and Trade, but the war began to break off his career. Day 22.06.1941 with his wife they spent in the mountains. Seeing in the evening the crowd at the reproducer, Klochkov, without hesitation, went to the draft board and returned with a summons in his hands. I didn‚Äôt imagine any other way. In the photo with his little daughter, who was only three and a half years old, he left an inscription, the text of which is known throughout the country today.

Memory
