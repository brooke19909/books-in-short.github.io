Akulovsky Vodokanal: description, fishing

Multimillion-dollar Moscow is supplied with water by several canals and water pipelines, because per day its indigenous and visiting residents spend almost 5 million cubic meters of water. Akulovsky water utility is considered its largest supplier. This man-made hydraulic construction, performing the seemingly most trivial task - to provide the capital with water, from the very moment of its design to the present day is accompanied by secrets and even some mysticism. Previously, people tried to bypass this place; now, on the contrary, many people strive here at any time of the year - to breathe fresh air and enjoy nature. Let us and we take a little virtual walk to him.

Specifications

The Akulovsky water canal, or, as it sounds officially, the ‚ÄúAkulovsky waterworks‚Äù, flows from the Uchinsky reservoir, named after the Ucha River (a tributary of Klyazma). He enters the hydraulic system of the channel. Moscow and consists not only of the water channel, but also of a whole complex of structures that purify water, check its purity and compliance with GOSTs, ditches, protecting the canal from surface runoff, drainage, level controls and other important devices. Its length is small, only 28 km, of which 18.7 km are laid on the surface, and 9.3 - in huge reinforced concrete pipes with a diameter of almost 3 meters. It would seem that everything is envisaged here, so that the Muscovites will be delivered water in the best possible way.

Two words about the history of creation

They decided to build the Akulovsky water canal because Moscow lacked water. In 1935, they developed a plan for watering the capital, according to which a large water ring from the Klyazma reservoir through Mytishchi and other areas to the Moscow River should appear on the maps, approximately at the location of the South Port. For various reasons, this project was not fully implemented, but in a smaller version, namely from the Uchinsky reservoir to the Eastern (formerly Stalin) waterworks, it was implemented. The canal began to be built in 1935 and commissioned in 1937. It was erected exclusively by the hands of prisoners of the Gulag. Only now the whole terrible truth about this grand construction site has become known.

The secret that has been hidden for more than half a century

Previously, few people thought about those whose efforts the Akulovsky Vodokanal was built. In 1990, when exploration of Elk Island was conducted, finally, the recollections of eyewitnesses of this construction site of the century were published. People said that builders were called canal workers. All of them, except for those holding senior positions, were Gulag residents, lived in barracks, worked from dark to dark in any weather, and died in hundreds. Local residents more than once came across anonymous human remains sticking out of the ground. Under Stalin, all who came to work on the construction of the canal wrote a receipt about the non-disclosure of any, even the most insignificant, information regarding the future waterworks. Violation of this receipt was punishable as a criminal offense.

Most of the existing man-made reservoirs for local residents are wonderful places of recreation with camp sites, beaches, pioneer camps. The Uchinskoe reservoir is special in this respect - it is not possible for ordinary mortals to come close to it (more precisely, 200 meters), since the entire area around it is a protected zone. The situation is similar with the waterworks laid out of it. The security zone of the Akulovsky water utility throughout its entire length is also officially listed as a sanitary one, with a strict regime. In an open area, the channel cannot be approached closer than 150, and in the pipe area closer than 50 meters. In fact, where the channel is laid underground, you can move around.
