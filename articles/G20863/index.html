Top of the world

The Himalayan massif is considered a unique place. In this marvelous corner are the origins of the Indus and Ganges. Everest Formation

The Himalayan ridge, according to geologists, was formed during the era of the split of the ancient mainland of Gondwan. The mainland broke into slabs. The Indian plate, moving north, came across a Eurasian fragment. In the zone of joining the plates, the crust was compressed and a huge fold was formed, which the Himalayas called.

The Himalayan mountain system was formed by three grandiose steps that spread from north to south. The lower heights are the "pre-Himalayas", which form the southern step. The mountain ranges here have a height of about 1000 m.

The middle step is represented by massifs rising 3500 m. In the northern part, the height of the mountain peaks ranges from 6000-8000 m. The width of the mountain ranges reaches 80-90 km.

The growth of the Himalayan ridge has not been suspended until now. Scientists assure that the height of the Himalayas increases annually by 3-10 mm. In the mountain range there are 75 peaks exceeding a height of 7,000 meters. Nepalese Himalayas recognized as the highest.





And above all the ridges ascended Mount Jomolungma. Where is its peak located? It rises above the vast Chinese expanses. The highest peak of Mount Everest is surrounded by other giant peaks that form a real "roof of the world", holding the sky above the earth.

Climbing Homolungma

The magnetic attraction of the highest point on Earth is unbelievable. Climbers, year after year, head east to where the Mount Jomolungma is located, where the tip of its giant peak piercing the clouds is located. The temptation to conquer this peak is great, but only a few reach it.

The philosophy of Everest is harsh. The path to its peak is ordered by those who are fussy and hasty, unprincipled and careless. He often turns into a tragedy for them. The first climbers, who began climbing even at the dawn of the 20th century, suffered a fiasco due to poor equipment. For the first time, Mount Jomolungma was subdued by people in 1953.

Climbers are constantly competing in the difficulty of climbing Mount Everest. Some are trying to climb the icy slopes in the middle of winter. Others, intending to climb to the top, refuse to capture oxygen. Emancipated women, united in a group, try to overcome the difficult path without men.





However, only Reinhold Messner can surprise everyone . The unruly mountain of Jomolungma granted him great mercy - to set several records at the same time! He, climbing alone on the northern slope without oxygen, overcame the ascent to the summit in 3 days. In 1992, 32 climbers climbed the peak as part of the Russian Lada Everest team.
