Internet "TTK": reviews, descriptions, features and services

Reviews about the Internet at TTK are of interest to all users who are considering connecting to the services of this company. Currently, it is a large telecommunications company, which is one of the five largest domestic telecom operators. Almost all of its shares are owned by Russian Railways. The company itself is one of the largest providers of backbone communication services for corporations and operators, one of the leaders in the market of broadband Internet access for ordinary users. The subscriber base of Trans TeleCom is almost two million subscribers.

history of the company

Reviews on the Internet in the "TTK" are different. One thing is certain, every month there are more and more users of this company, as it constantly spreads its influence in new regions and cities.

TransTeleCom has been leading its history since 1997, when it was founded at the initiative of the Federal Ministry of Railways. Then the main goal was the construction of a backbone digital communication network, which was required by domestic railways.

For three years, a fiber optic network was built with a length of about 45 thousand kilometers. It covered 71 regions of Russia, almost a thousand settlements. The company officially started commercial operation of the network in 2001, in fact becoming one of the largest providers of Internet access via a dedicated line.

By 2003, the company's revenue had tripled; it had about a thousand corporate clients. In 2008, it became known that "TTK" enters the retail market, starting to offer broadband Internet access to the population. By the spring of next year, the company‚Äôs branches were opened in all major cities of the country with the exception of Moscow. For a long time, the company could not enter the capital's market.

Since 2012, TTK has been providing cable television services. Among the recent large-scale projects, it should be noted the construction of equipment for digital terrestrial broadcasting as part of the implementation of the corresponding federal target program, which was implemented in the Republic of Sakha (Yakutia).

Guide

The Russian Railways Open Joint Stock Company owns 99.9 percent of the shares of TTK.

The direct president of the company is Roman Kravtsov, who has been heading it since 2016. He began his career at the Novosibirsk branch of Rostelecom. Under his leadership, a new management team was formed, which began to carry out a set of measures aimed at improving the operational efficiency of the TTK business.

In particular, to date, it has already been possible to increase its share in the international transit market in several key areas at once. For example, with Azerbaijan and China. In this segment alone, for one year, the company's revenue grew by 650 million rubles.

The chairman of the board of directors is Evgeny Igorevich Charkin, who at the same time holds the post of head of information technology at Russian Railways.

In the market, the company has a fairly aggressive takeover and merger policy. To date, she has already managed to acquire several large providers who worked in one or several regions of Russia at once. In particular, these are Tron-plus, Dartel, Ivtelecom, Informtek, Electro-com, Dars-IP, Maginfo, TV-Kom.ru, Integrated Transport Network "," TeleSOT "," Severlink ".

In addition, the company "TTK" has already managed to take almost 75 percent of the shares of one of the largest manufacturers of fiber optic cable in the country - the company "Transvok", which provides its services throughout the Russian Federation.

mobile connection

Another fundamentally new novelty from TTK was the launch of its own mobile operator based on the infrastructure of Tele 2. The first subscribers who were able to test the quality of the provided communication were customers in the Krasnodar Territory and Rostov Region. Soon, the network began to extend to other regions of the Russian Federation.

Currently, subscribers are provided not only cellular communications and the ability to send SMS messages, but also access to mobile Internet.

During its existence, the operator has expanded its presence in the Ryazan, Novosibirsk, Kemerovo and Chelyabinsk regions, in the Altai Territory.

In particular, the service "Single Account" is offered to subscribers. In this case, you can pay for your mobile phone bill, broadband Internet access from home via a dedicated line, as well as interactive home television.

Structure

If at first the regional branches of the company were directly subordinate to subsidiaries of Russian Railways, then since 2014 the situation has changed dramatically.

The reorganization process was completed, after which the TTK company was transformed into a single federal company with a network of its own branches. There are currently eight of them.

The center of the macroregion of the Far East is located in Khabarovsk, Siberia - in Krasnoyarsk, Western Siberia - in Novosibirsk, the Urals - in Yekaterinburg, Verkhnevolzhsky - in Nizhny Novgorod, the North - in Yaroslavl, the Caucasus - in Rostov-on-Don, the Center - in Moscow.

Services

Internet provider "TTK" in Russia provides various services, they may vary depending on the region. Moreover, both in cost and in description. However, in most regional offices, a single approach is still being implemented with a common pricing and corporate policy.

In particular, now the Internet provider "TTK" provides Russians with high-speed Internet, satellite TV, and also provides package offers of services.

For example, within the framework of the package ‚ÄúOn Top‚Äù, the client has the opportunity to immediately access both the home Internet and interactive television. There are two options for the package: basic and advanced.

The cost of the basic tariff is 470 rubles per month. For this money, the client receives an Internet speed of up to 100 Mbit per second and 107 channels. In an expanded version of the package, the Internet speed remains the same, and the number of channels increases to 138.

It should be noted separately that to connect interactive television, the client will have to additionally purchase a set-top box.

Also, you can connect the Internet and interactive television separately. The tariff ‚ÄúOn top‚Äù when connecting high-speed Internet will cost you 380 rubles a month in the first year after connecting, then the price rises to 430 rubles.

This tariff includes the Home Internet service and Wi-fi router. It is worth noting that the tariff payment system is credit. If in the first year after connecting, the subscriber decides to terminate the contract or change the tariff plan, he will have to pay a one-time payment for connecting this service in full. It also includes the full cost of equipment that was installed in accordance with the contract of sale.

The company offers two main tariffs for high-speed Internet. "TTK 60" (speed up to 60 Mbit per second, cost 400 rubles per month), "TTK 100" (speed up to 100 Mbit per second, cost 450 rubles per month).
