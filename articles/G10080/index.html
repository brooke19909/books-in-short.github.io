What areas of Tyumen do you know?

There are many different cities in Russia: from megacities to small settlements. Many of them began to develop actively in recent years. So, Tyumen has become a large industrial center with a population of 700 thousand people. Its appearance is changing, the development of areas is actively going on, new structures of a social nature are appearing.

City structure

Today, the districts of Tyumen, the list of which will be given below, are transformed into administrative districts. There are only four of them, namely:

  • Central;
  • Leninist;
  • Kalininsky;
  • Oriental.

The youngest area





More than four hundred years have passed since the founding of the city. From a small settlement, Tyumen turned into a large city, becoming an important part of the Transsib transport route. There are a bus station, railway station and airport. New buildings actively flooded the areas of Tyumen. So, in the eastern part of the city the Vostochny district was formed. He appeared not so long ago, in 2008. It includes the territory of the city, located south of the railway, and a small part of the northern shore of Lake St. Andrew. In the Eastern District is located CHP-2.

It is here that you can find the most modern shopping centers, healthcare facilities with new equipment, cultural and sports grounds. According to statistics, 171 455 people live here.

Where did the city begin?

Tyumen's districts developed along with the arriving population. So, the Kalinin district was formed in 1965. It contained the Settlement and the Yamskaya Sloboda. According to the data, 170 thousand people live in it. It is here that there are two airports and a railway station. Also, along the Yamskaya street, an exit to the intercity highway passes. It is worth noting: the regions of Tyumen are unevenly provided with social facilities. Kalinin district boasts a large number of kindergartens, schools, hospitals and libraries. Do not forget about catering enterprises, retail outlets, which are abundant in the district.

Center





Tyumen's districts formed at different times. So, the Central District was formed in 1972 and included territories such as Zarechye and Martovskaya Sloboda. It is noteworthy that the Central District is divided by the Tura River. It is considered to be the center of the cultural and industrial life of the city.

Another district of the city is the Leninsky administrative district, the largest in area and population. Includes the northeastern territory of the city. It is noteworthy that the government of the city of Tyumen is making every effort to improve the quality of life in the city and improve infrastructure. All areas are actively built up with new houses, and new cultural objects appear with them.
