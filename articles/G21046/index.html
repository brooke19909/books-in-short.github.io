How to learn to trade on the exchange: understanding the basics and rules of trading the exchange, tips and step-by-step instructions for novice traders

New investors taking their first steps to learn the basics of trading stocks and other assets should have access to several sources of quality information. Trading training can be compared to the first cycling, where trial attempts to ride and mistakes combined with continued insistence are combined. Ultimately, these actions will lead to success.

One big advantage of trading on the exchange is that it lasts a lifetime. Investors have years to develop and hone their skills. The strategies used twenty years ago are still in use today. Therefore, for new investors who want to take the first steps, the most relevant question is: "How to learn to trade on the exchange?". Below are the main rules that will lead to success.

Open an account with a broker

Find a good online broker and open an account. First of all, you should get acquainted with demo accounts and take advantage of free trading tools and research results offered only to customers. Some companies offer virtual trading, which is advantageous because you can trade with fake money.

Read more info

The various books on the subject provide a lot of information, and yet they are inexpensive compared to the cost of classes, seminars and educational DVDs sold over the Internet. Even articles available on the Web for free can be useful in learning how to start trading on the exchange.

Find an experienced mentor

A mentor can be a family member, friend, former or current teacher, employee of a brokerage company, or any other person who has a fundamental understanding of the stock market. A good assistant is ready to answer questions, help, recommend useful resources and maintain a working mood when the market becomes especially. All successful past and present investors had mentors in their early years.

Various thematic forums can be another source of questions and answers. They can also help in how to start trading on the exchange. Just be careful what you listen to. The vast majority of participants in such resources are not professional traders, not to mention successful market players. Heed the advice of users with a high degree of skepticism, and under no circumstances follow the recommendations for trading literally.

Learn the success story of famous market players

Exploring the greatest investors of yesteryear will provide you with perspective, inspiration and success. These include Warren Buffett, Jesse Livermore, George Soros, Benjamin Graham, Peter Lynch, John Templeton, Paul Tudor Jones and others. Each of them once started trading on the exchange from scratch. Many of them then published books about their success stories.

Read the news and follow the market

International news sites such as Yahoo Finance and Google Finance provide an excellent resource for new investors. For in-depth coverage, see local news only. Monitoring markets every day and reading news headlines will allow you to see all new trends, conduct third-party analysis, not to mention economic concepts and the state of large business. Pulling quotes and observing fundamental data can also be another good source. All this will help you with the question of how to learn how to trade on the exchange.

TV is another way to track the market every day. Even turning on the TV for 15 minutes every day will expand the investor's knowledge base. Do not allow yourself to make a one-sided assessment of what you hear, just watch and listen to all commentators, interviews and discussions. Over time, you will find that many analytical programs may not be very useful, and learn to choose exactly what you need.

Research and analysis fees can be both educational and rewarding. Some investors observe professionals in the market to gain more practical skills, rather than trying to learn new lessons on their own. There are many paid subscription sites on the Internet that can give you a lot of useful information on how to learn how to trade on the exchange.

Take part in seminars and classes

Workshops can provide valuable information about the general market and specific types of investments. Most of them will focus on one specific aspect of the market and how the speaker made success using his own strategies over the years. For example, training is offered on how to start trading futures on an exchange. Not all such seminars require payment. Some classes are provided free of charge, which may be useful.

As with paid subscriptions, be careful with classes and courses. Most of them are quite expensive and are sold with false promises to acquire valuable knowledge. Their advertising move will entice your money, set you up for quick success, and then provide you with a strategy that is not even profitable to start with.

Practice trading through a simulator

How to start trading stocks and other assets on the stock exchange? Using your online broker account, you should start with a small transaction. Do not be afraid to start, even if you use 1, 10 or 20 stocks. If trading with real capital is not possible initially, consider using a stock simulator for a virtual transaction. Various online brokers offer demo trading for classes.

One of the most common mistakes made by traders is to plunge into the whole trade and try to start working with large amounts. This often turns out to be fatal, and because of this, many new investors in the early stages suffer big losses. Proper portfolio allocation is extremely important. This is one of the most significant aspects in how to learn to trade on the exchange from scratch.

What does transaction confirmation look like?

Thanks to the SEC and other regulatory requirements, when you start placing transactions through your brokerage account, you will receive a trading confirmation. It will be sent to you by mail or, if you chose paperless delivery, provided to you in any other downloadable format, such as Adobe PDF, whenever your broker executes a purchase or sale order. Depending on your company, trade confirmation information will vary, but there are a few things that are included in almost all such documents.

What you can find in the trading confirmation from the broker:

  • The name of the investment you are trading with the ticker symbol.
  • How many total shares sold or bought.
  • Cost or selling price per share.
  • The commission that was paid to the company (depending on the specifics, if your broker acted as a dealer for you, you can see the spreads of bonds on securities with fixed income).
  • The execution date of the transaction, which shows when the transaction was placed.
  • Settlement date, which is the day you pay money and receive an investment.
  • Total gross transaction value.
  • The total net transaction value after deducting brokerage fees.
  • The account number on which the trade was placed.
  • The type of order that was used; e.g. market, limit order, etc.

Can I trade without a broker?

There is no doubt that the most popular way to buy and sell investments is to open a brokerage account, many are interested in how to buy shares without a broker. In fact, it is possible. Direct investment provides some advantages and disadvantages that you will need to weigh based on your personal situation.

You can buy shares without a broker by purchasing shares through a direct sale plan from the company. These are situations where companies sponsor a special type of program called DSPP, or a direct share purchase plan. These plans were originally conceived many decades ago as a business way to allow small investors to buy property directly from the company, working through a transfer agent or the plan administrator responsible for processing daily documents and transactions. Most plans will allow you to buy shares without a broker if they agree to either a certain installment amount (deducted from the control or savings account monthly for six months), or they make a one-time purchase, often for $ 250 or $ 500.

Typically, plan administrators collect cash from those involved in a direct stock purchase course and use it to buy company assets either on the open market or just released from the business itself in a predetermined time frame. Just as you receive a report from a bank, a report on direct share purchase plans is received in most cases quarterly, indicating the number of shares owned by you, any dividends you received, and any purchases or sales that you made.
