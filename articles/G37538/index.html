Mainz city in Germany: history and interesting places

The city of Mainz in Germany is one of the oldest territories in the country with a rich history and culture. It was founded before the new era by the Romans. Previously, the city was called Mogontiacium, in honor of the ancient god Mogon. Soon after its formation, it became one of the three centers of the region. Currently, the population of the city of Mainz in Germany is more than two hundred thousand people, and the area is about one hundred square kilometers.

Mainz Tourism

Due to the fact that the city was founded by the Romans, many attractions of the times of the empire have been preserved in Mainz.

The village itself is small and quite cozy - it is an ideal place for family tourism or for those people who love peace and quiet.

There are several ways to get to the city. The first - the fastest, but expensive - is a plane to Frankfurt, and after that the bus is already to Mainz. Fortunately, the cities are only 30 kilometers from each other. The second way is a railroad crossing. Of course, the city of Mainz in Germany can be reached by personal transport.

The city has a fairly large number of hotels, so there will be no difficulties with accommodation. Shopping lovers will also have something to do here - Mainz is full of various shops.

Cathedral in Mainz, Germany

The most famous attraction of the city is the Mainz Cathedral. It was built in the Romanesque style with elements of Baroque and Gothic, which is typical for German architecture. Its construction began in the tenth century and lasted more than two centuries. Its other name is the Cathedral of St. Martin and St. Stephen. It presents many works of medieval sculpture and a collection of religious heritage.

Church of christ

This church was erected much later than the Mainz Cathedral and was built in opposition to it. The Church of Christ is a Protestant church. During World War II, it was badly damaged and its restoration began only in 1952. Now the premises of the church are a place where various exhibitions, concerts, etc. are held.

Gutenberg Museum

It was in Mainz that the first printer Johannes Gutenberg was born . All excursions around the city necessarily go through a museum named after him. Here are Johann's personal items, as well as his printing house and hand press. He began his book printing business in the 1940s. In general, in Mainz, a street is named after Gutenberg and much more, this is not surprising, because he made a great contribution to the history of not only Germany, but the whole world.

Museum of Antique Shipping

There are very few museums of ancient shipping like the one in Mainz in the world, which is why it attracts so much attention. There are warships, ordinary boats and canoes of the Roman Empire. It is noteworthy that some of these vessels were discovered during the construction of the hotel.



In general, the city of Mainz in Germany is of great interest to curious tourists. In addition to the attractions that have already been listed, there are such famous architectural structures as the Theodore Bridge over the Rhine River, an art gallery, a historical city museum and much more. Also, various festivals and carnivals are regularly held in Mainz. One of them is Johannisnacht. The festival is organized in the middle of summer. During it, viewers can visit musical and theatrical performances, pyrotechnic and light shows.

A large number of people come to the city of Mainz in order to look at local universities. The city has a lot of students from all over Europe.

University of Mainz (Germany)

The University of Mainz is one of the most popular and largest educational institutions in the country. The university itself is divided into ten departments, and more than 35,000 students study there.

The university has a rich history. It was founded in 1477 at the initiative of the local archbishop Adolf II. Education was suspended in 1792 due to the war between Prussia and the Mainz Republic. The university restored its work only after the Second World War thanks to the French authorities.

The institution specializes in a student exchange program. Moreover, since 2007, he has been number one in this indicator. More than 15% of students are foreigners, training for them is free, and this attracts many.

Life in Mainz (Germany) is very diverse and interesting, many tourists come here for several weeks for the atmosphere of the most family and bright holiday, on Christmas Eve. For residents of Mainz, this is a special holiday, in anticipation of which the local market begins its work. People come here from all over Germany for various authentic goods: leather goods, Christmas tree decorations, farm products, etc.

Mainz is an ancient city, one of the cultural centers of the country. It has an interesting history, and any tourist here will be incredibly comfortable. That is why the city is very popular and famous.
