Kota Museum in Minsk: address, opening hours, how to get there, reviews

In the world there are a large number of the most diverse museums. They demonstrate exhibits of antiquity, unique finds, works of art and even the mysteries of mankind. However, there are institutions that make adults smile and delight children. One of such institutions is the Kota Museum in Minsk. Let us further consider the features of the organization of the institution, provide surprising facts and talk about such commonplace things as the cost of an admission ticket, the mode of operation, and the address of the museum. In addition, we give especially noteworthy reviews from visitors.

Who is the founder?

The Kota Museum in Minsk is an unusual institution with unique but sometimes boring exhibits. Here visitors are greeted by amazing creatures - purring cats, noble cats and their mischievous offspring.

The founder of the institution is Alla Narovskaya. She also periodically organizes the March Cats exhibitions, which take place annually in Belarus.

However, the reader will never guess who the director of the museum is. In this case, this is the most real cat, nicknamed Jimmy, which has a speck in the shape of a butterfly on its neck. According to its status, the animal is strolling around all the halls without any fear of visitors.

What to look at?

Do not think that the Kota Museum is represented only by animals walking around. Here a large collection of paintings by Belarusian artists awaits visitors. Canvases are also for sale. In addition, works of decorative art are presented. Also, the organizers of the exhibition are planning to soon add sculptures and various works of foreign figures to the gallery.

The main characters of the exhibition

Of course, people try to visit the Cat Museum in Minsk because of its main characters. “Exhibits” are alive, they can not only be caressed and stroked, but also photographed with them. The museum hosts various cultural and educational events. Of course, the institution is still quite young, but already here, in addition to a direct inspection of all objects, you can have fun in the “Cafe Cafe”.

Good intentions

The museum is a real home for a cat in need of care. Here is a catalog that is regularly updated with color photographs of animals, with a description of their character and habits. You can always come, choose a pet and take it to your home.

Kota Museum in Minsk: how to get there

An unusual museum is located in the capital of Belarus, Minsk. According to information from the official website, you can come to see the unique “exhibits” daily. The Kota Museum’s working hours in Minsk are as follows: from 11:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Monday is a day off.

In order not to get lost, you can use public transport. If this is the metro, then you need to get off at the Grushevka station. If you choose a fixed-route taxi, you should wait for No. 1153 or No. 1053 and get off at the stop Khmelevsky Street. You can also use the services of a trolley bus. To do this, you must wait for transport at number 40, 36, 12 or 53 and get out also on Khmelevsky street.

Having reached the desired stop, finally, it will be possible to go to the Kota Museum in Minsk. The address of the institution is as follows: Internatsionalnaya street, 23.

Interesting pastime

"House for the cat" - this is also called this museum in Minsk. It provides great opportunities for families. Any child and even an adult can try their hand at creativity and paint a picture. Of course, all the necessary tools for this will be provided. If you comply with all the requirements and the creation will be on the cat theme, then it will also be exhibited in the museum.

In addition, children like to play koto-checkers, put koto-puzzles, try themselves in the role of a Cat in Boots and read informative books about ... them.

In a small but cozy “Koto-cafe”, which is located here, parents can drink some coffee, and children can enjoy some sweets and drink all this with fragrant cat-tea.

Curious Facts About the Museum

Often visitors are concerned about where the "live exhibits" come from. According to employees, most of the animals in the past were homeless, they were simply taken from the street. Moreover, many were in such a deplorable position that they would undoubtedly die. All cats were examined by a veterinarian, were vaccinated and only after that they had access to the museum.

Do not think that the institution is a shelter for animals. This is far from the case. Today, 15 cats live here. Moreover, each of them with its own character, interesting external data and, of course, a nickname. The most interesting are:

  • Marshmallows;
  • Timosha
  • Michelle
  • Cream;
  • Mr. Red.

All inhabitants eat according to the menu developed by veterinarians. Of course, they do not receive delicacies, but the food used here contains all the necessary minerals and vitamins.

Sometimes people who want to visit the Kota Museum for the first time are frightened and think that a rather unpleasant smell will be felt there. This opinion is due to the fact that in the usual menageries this usually happens. However, cats are very intelligent animals and only go to the tray. To cope with natural needs, a modern filler is used, which completely absorbs the entire smell. In addition, the ventilation system works and copes with its functions perfectly in the museum.

Do not worry about the hygiene component. To protect animals, all visitors in front of the entrance must step on the antibacterial rug and, only after that, wear shoe covers. Hands should be washed with soap and a disinfectant. It is not forbidden to pet animals and it is completely safe. They are vaccinated and regularly examined by a veterinarian.

How do animals feel?

Sometimes lovers of domestic animals worry that cats can tire from a large crowd of visitors. But it is known that these cute pets are able to adapt to excessive attention from adults and children. At the same time, museum staff closely monitor the situation in the halls and recommend treating “living exhibits” with care.

There is a rule that must be observed when visiting a museum. All cats are allowed to be stroked, but it is forbidden to pick them up. Animals do not get tired of the attention of visitors and do not become embittered. In addition, the museum has places of seclusion, where cats can go if necessary. In this case, they cannot be disturbed.

additional information

Visiting the museum "Cats", located in Minsk, you can learn a lot of interesting things about them. For example, adults and especially children are interested in why pets love sleeping so much during the day. Unlike humans, they prefer to spend time in a light nap for up to 18 hours.

According to experts, it is important not to disturb the cat if he falls asleep, because his biological internal clock requires it. At the same time, you can observe such a picture, when literally 10 minutes later the animal is already running around the museum, playing with other relatives.

Visitor Reviews

Kota Museum (Minsk), reviews have accumulated only the most enthusiastic. Most people love pets for their special character, playfulness and creating coziness in the house. Visitors are delighted that the “exhibits” are lively, but very playful and cute. The halls created just a paradise for the living of charming furry creatures. In addition to communicating with animals, here you can appreciate a variety of art objects that are organically combined with the theme of cats.

And finally

The Kota Museum in Minsk is an unusual institution, ideal for families. In addition to entertainment purposes, the staff sets itself other tasks. Here you can choose and take the animal home, learn a lot about their contents and immerse yourself in the atmosphere of art related to cat themes. Of course, certain conditions must be observed. But only in this way can animals be provided with peace and not violate the rules of hygiene. Cats are capricious, but if handled properly, they are very affectionate. It is necessary to recognize their independence and not to worry in vain.

It remains only to mention what it will cost to visit the Kota Museum in Minsk. Ticket price depends on age and status:

  • children and students - 7 little white rubles (about 215 rubles)
  • adults - 9 white rubles (about 277 rubles).

The price includes tea and sweets.
