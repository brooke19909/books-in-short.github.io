Prague Attractions Available to Visitors

Prague is a beautiful atmospheric city with many visitors. In addition to classic buildings, amazing narrow streets or pearls such as Charles Bridge, it offers many interesting non-traditional museums and other places to relax.





Located on the Vltava River, the historical center of Prague attracts millions of tourists every year. The magical Old Town, the royal Hradcany, the charming Small Country are places that are unforgettable and most visited by tourists coming to the Czech capital.

Old Town Square and Old Town Hall

The city received royal permission to build its own town hall, and it was completed in 1338. Of course, a tower is a striking attraction for tourists. Its most popular aspect is the astronomical clock and calendar, in which during the day at certain times windows open and you can watch the procession of the apostles.

The Charles Bridge

Originally, the wooden bridge structure was washed away during the flood and replaced by a more solid stone structure, which became known as the Judith Bridge. It was decorated and further renamed in honor of King Charles IV. Charles Bridge has 30 statues of saints (15 on each side), each with its own history.

On Charles Bridge you can see crowds of tourists who want to touch the plate on the statue of Jan Nepomuk (there is a sign that this is for luck). The statue commemorates the saint, who was the Queen's confessor and was dropped from the bridge for refusing to reveal the sacrament of confession to the king.





Next to the Charles Bridge on the side of the Old Town is a group of buildings, including: The Old Town Water Tower (with clock) and the Bed≈ôich Smetana Museum. This is a great place for a cup of coffee and panoramic shots of Charles Bridge with Prague Castle in the background.

Church of St. Nicholas

St. Nicholas Church is the most famous baroque church in Prague. Construction began when the Jesuits chose the plans for Giovanni Dominic Orsi in 1673 and lasted about a hundred years.

The Jesuit Thomas Schwartz in 1747 built the main organ in it, which has 4 thousand pipes. Mozart himself played this organ during his stay in Prague. St. Nicholas Church in Prague is also the venue for chamber music concerts.

Prague Castle and the Old Royal Palace

The Royal Palace is one of the original buildings in the castle complex, adjacent to the Chapel of All Saints. In 1483, King Vladislav Jagiellon transferred the city court to Prague Castle and began the last large-scale reconstruction of the Old Royal Palace. The complex was added to the hall of Vladislav in the late Gothic style, combined with fashionable elements of the Renaissance. After the fire in 1541, the Sejm and the Church of All Saints were restored.

The exit from the hall of Vladislav passes through the stairs of the riders, specially built for equestrian knights, so that they enter the room on horseback, to participate in knightly tournaments.
