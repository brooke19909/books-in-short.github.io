The story of the creation of reborn dolls

Translated from English, reborn means "reborn, reborn." Logically, these are dolls that look like little newborn babies. The history of their creation goes back to the 90s of the last century. Designers from the USA wanted to create the most realistic dolls. The target audience was considered collectors. The first doll via the Internet was sold in 2002 from the eBay website.

After a few years, not only collectors began to buy reborn. Many women became interested in such babies, each for their own reasons - someone regretted that the children had grown up quickly, someone had no children at all, still others simply harmoniously entered them into the interior of the bedrooms. How to make a reborn doll at home

The first step in the creation is the degreasing of mold. You can buy it in many online stores. You need to do this with a solvent. As a rule, the mold will be made of vinyl. The kit includes a blank of the face, pens and legs. In this case, the body is sewn from fabric and printed. Also in the sets you can see the body of the doll. Such a reborn will be as realistic as possible compared to a real child. As a rule, such molds are used when there is little clothing on the doll (or not at all).

Usually work begins with the head. Holes are made on the blank in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe nose and eyes, if necessary, since they already exist on some molds, or not at all, since the doll can be made sleeping.

At the beginning of the work, the master reborn painter applies several layers of paint to Moldova. As a rule, their number varies from 15 to 30. For this, he uses acrylic or oil paints. They are applied with a sponge, sponge or brush. After each layer, the mold is baked at a certain temperature. Next, a fixative is applied to add a dull color to the skin. It should be noted right away that you can bake the workpiece in the oven, but then it is forbidden to cook food in it. When painting the workpiece for the most realistic effect, you can make slight reddening of the skin.

Next, artificial eyes and hair are fixed. The latter are made, as a rule, from mohair, or use real ones. This process is called rooting. The smaller the doll is made in age, the thinner the hair should be. Do not forget about the cilia (if the designer is going to insert them).

When working with pens, some craftsmen insert artificial nails. Of course, the similarity with a real child rises several times, but the cost of work, of course, increases.

Then the doll should be filled. To do this, you can use the syntep. It does not caking over time, when creasing, it quickly regains its shape, and is affordable. In order for the doll‚Äôs weight to be as close to realistic as possible, the body should be filled with glass granulate.

You also need to pay special attention to doll clothes. It can be either an ordinary diaper for a very young baby, or a larger dress for a girl, and for a boy you can sew some costume. The doll is ready. As you can see, the question of how to make a reborn doll itself is solved quite simply. You can make this toy even out of paper.

How to make a reborn doll out of paper

Such toys are made in a completely different way, unlike a vinyl doll. There is nothing to do with the technology described above. Apparently, there is a reason in the name - everything that is as close as possible to the look of a real baby can be called "reborn" in his work.

How to make a paper doll in literally 10 minutes? To do this, on a white sheet, you need to draw (or rather print for greater realism) the image of the doll, then cut it out. You can also make removable clothes for the resulting paper toy.

Many craftsmen make excellent money on making reborn with their own hands. It is impossible to meet such a doll gathering dust on store shelves. Works are bought mainly through the Internet. The main places of sales work online stores, blogs.

Doll price

On the Russian market, reborn dolls cost an average of 20 to 100 thousand rubles. The price is influenced by many factors: the degree of realism with a real child, the quality of the materials used, the size of the doll, the availability of clothes, etc.

The rather high cost makes such dolls too expensive for many. They should not be allowed to children to play to avoid damage. They serve for aesthetic pleasure. It is impossible to make two absolutely identical dolls, hence another criterion for the high price - the buyer will have an exclusive item.
