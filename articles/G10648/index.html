Italy, Varese: photos and a description of the main attractions

Holidays in Italy are always a busy program, a lot of positive emotions and a vacation spent in pleasure. All travelers usually talk about popular and well-trodden tourist routes. Not a single Italian trip is complete without visiting such famous cities as Rome, Florence, Milan or Venice. Fortunately, Italy is such an amazing country that only these cities can not do.

The topic of our article will be devoted to the small provincial town of Varese in Italy, which is famous not only for its attractions, but also for relaxing on the banks of the magnificent lake of the same name. Let's get to know each other better!

History

The history of this province originates, according to official documents, from 922. The Middle Ages can be described as the interweaving of the events of the Genoese republic and the Lavagna family. Over time, power over the city was transferred from one generation to another, one surname was replaced by another. Despite this factor, by the end of the 20th century, Varese served as a favorite vacation spot for secular aristocracy.

Lake Varese

In Italy, holidays on famous lakes such as Garda, Como or Maggiore are very popular. This kind of pastime is preferred by all local residents, so as not to rush along the sea coast among the crowds of tourists, but to escape from the bustle of the city somewhere in the middle of nature for the weekend. Many travelers completely forget that somewhere in the province of Varese there is the lake of the same name Lago di Varese! But this is a great option for a summer beach holiday, boat trips and yachts. An interesting fact is that Lake Wazere is of glacial origin and is located at an altitude of about 236 meters above sea level. Here you can find quite rare birds: a red heron or a big bittern.

Estense complex

In the central part of the city is the main historical symbol - the Estense palace and park complex. This building primarily impresses visitors with its scale and luxury of the facade, which has survived to this day in its original appearance. At the moment, the Estense complex is a striking example of the Baroque style and the most memorable monument located in the city. As for the park separately, it is worth saying that this is a whole fascinating journey into the world of various sculptures, beautiful fountains and pools. Definitely worth a visit!

Baroffio Museum

Speaking of cultural institutions located on the city territory, it is worth highlighting the Baroffio Museum. Here the main part of the exhibits is devoted to religious subjects, but even if you are not a fan of religion, this place will not leave you indifferent. Once upon a time, a rich baron lived in Varese, collecting all objects of art on religious subjects. Actually, subsequently his entire collection was redistributed to the Baroffio Museum. Today it is one of the most unusual museums in the city that is visited daily by pilgrims from all over the world.

Climatic conditions

The weather in Varese (Italy) is usually mild. Due to its geographical location, climatic conditions are very liveable here: saturated hot summers with periodic precipitation and warm winters. The province of Varese is located in northern Italy, so the climate here is very similar to Milanese. Between May and October, the air temperature fluctuates between 23-30 degrees Celsius, but in winter the thermometer does not drop below 7 degrees. Therefore, you can safely go here in the winter to the thaw in the hope of at least some time to forget about the harsh weather in Russia. In general, you can come to rest in Varese almost at any time of the year, because for Italy there is no bad weather! On the photo below - Varese (Italy) in the spring.

Here you can rent an apartment, a loft or stay in a hotel room on the eve of the May holidays. We hope that our article helped you to form your own opinion about this heavenly corner of the planet.
