Chioggia, Italy: description and photo

How many beautiful cities exist on our planet! And in any country you can see a lot of interesting things, for example, we will go to Italy to Chioggia. What kind of city is it, why is it interesting and how popular among tourists?

Location

In the province of Venice, there is a town that stands on several islands. It bears the name Chioggia, which in Italian sounds like Chioggia.





This small city is located south of Venice, and to get into it, you need to get to the Venetian Square in Rome and take the bus. Or anyone can pay for a boat ticket that will take him to the island.

Chioggia City: Description and History

Chioggia in Italy is called the second Venice or the sister of Venice, because he literally grew up on the water. The city is connected to the mainland by a bulk dam, it covers an area of ‚Äã‚Äã185 km 2 , and only 50 thousand people live in it.

Chioggia appeared during the time of the Roman Empire, although at one time it became a victim of barbaric invasions. In the original version, when the Etruscan people settled here, this place was called Clodia. After this, the names changed several more times - Cluza or Clugia.





The city of Chioggia in Italy has been considered a major port for many years. Every year, ships that came from the south of the country stopped here. It was also an important fishing port, and the rule of the territory passed from one hand to another. The history even mentions the Battle of Chioggia. At first, this city was in the power of the Genoese Republic, then a battle broke out over this territory with the rulers of Venice, who recaptured Chioggia in 1380. Since then, it has been ruled by the Republic of Venice.

No wonder Chioggia in Italy, whose photo is striking in its beauty, resembles Venice. It has the same architecture, the same canals and bridges, but only the area is much smaller and there is no such influx of tourists.

It is interesting that the city was destroyed in the 9th century, but it was rebuilt again, because rich deposits of salt were found here.

Another important event that occurred at this place is the appearance of the Virgin Mary. You can see paintings and small postcards with her image everywhere, they are sold as souvenirs in all shops of the city.

General information

As mentioned above, Chioggia is a small city that is far from the first in terms of population. There are much fewer tourists, but life is in full swing from morning to late evening. This is not the most popular tourist destination, but people come here daily to see this wonderful place, enjoy the atmosphere, and feel how the poor Italians live.





Fishing is the main area of ‚Äã‚Äãearnings of local residents, and here you can always try fresh fish in a cafe or restaurant. In addition, people are engaged in the production of textiles, bricks and steel.

sights

Chioggia is a small town, and one day is enough for sightseeing. You can simply stroll through the streets and look at historic buildings or look into local museums and art galleries.

The main attraction of the city is the Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta, which was built in the XII century, the church of St. Andrew is an interesting and beautiful structure.

But you can start your acquaintance with this wonderful city from Piazzetta Vigo Square, where there is a marble bridge, which is protected by stone lions. If you cross this bridge, you find yourself in the church of San Domenico, where there are several important historical values, including the painting by Vittore Carpaccio.





Chioggia's main cathedral, or Duomo, is located at the end of Corso Street. This is a stately white building with massive columns and a marble pulpit. The altar looks unusual and is decorated with carved cherubs.

The main street is Corso del Popolo Avenue, where all cultural life is in full swing, as well as many shops, cafes and bars, all that any large or small tourist city is famous for, including Chioggia in Italy. Sights are not only ancient buildings, but also the port, which is the breadwinner of many residents. It is very beautiful here, and you can, standing on the pier, meet the dawn and carry out the order.

Chioggia has several museums, including the Museum of the South Lagoon and the Adriatic. They do not work for long, so it is important to plan the time for a walk and for their visit.

Beaches in Chioggia (Italy)

If you came to Chioggia not only to enjoy the sights, but also to visit the beach and see the sea, then you should go to the Sottomarina area, which, in fact, is part of this tourist destination, but in fact has long become an independent city.





There are many beaches in the resort of Sottomarina, they are all covered with fine sand. The large sandy beach is divided into parts where everyone can find something to do. Someone prefers to bask in the sun, someone can rent a water scooter or skis or do their favorite thing - kiting.

The beach stretches for kilometers and is actually divided into several parts, each of which has its own name, for example Bahia del Sole, Bagni Vianello or Playa Punta Canna. Of course, all these beaches are not as high as in the Maldives, and this must be understood from the beginning. But you can always sunbathe, admire the sea and taste fresh sea fish.
