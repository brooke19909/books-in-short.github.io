Masonic conspiracy: history, features

The Masonic conspiracy is the most common conspiracy theory in the world. Hundreds of books and articles are written on this subject annually. Violent discussions are regularly held in the media about the existence of secret societies that rule the world. Proponents of the theory believe that the Masonic trace stretches from the Middle Ages and leaves an imprint on all the events of world history.

The origin of the Masons

In Western countries, the word mason is translated as "mason". The Masonic conspiracy originates in medieval construction artels. Massive churches and temples were erected throughout Europe at the dawn of Gothic architecture. Their size and features of the external decoration required many decades of work. History knows cases when one church could be built for more than a hundred years. Therefore, masons (the common name of architects, engineers and other participants in the construction process) settled right near the construction site and could spend more than half their lives there. Staying in such conditions prompted them to create various brotherhoods and organizations. The first Masons were simple masons who established behaviors in their settlements, hierarchy, etc., thus acquiring the appearance of a community.



Joining influential people

Several centuries passed, and people who had nothing to do with construction began to join Masonic lodges. A lodge is an association of people on a territorial basis. They all submit to the Grand Lodge, which determines the primacy in the local. The rich people of the Renaissance quickly realized that you can use inconspicuous associations of masons to expand their influence. Thus, the Masonic conspiracy did exist in France and a number of other Western states.

Gradually accepting new participants, among whom were even members of the royal families, the Freemasons more and more moved away from the masons. They began to call themselves "architects of life." From the founders there were only symbols - a pair of compasses and a square. Also, the image of the eye in the pyramid is often mentioned as an emblem. In general, the Masonic conspiracy involves the use of diverse occult symbolism by secret societies.





Theory spread

The Masonic conspiracy is a theory according to which secret societies accept prominent public figures, politicians, rich people and other "elites" to influence the world system. The ultimate goal of Freemasons is the complete unification of the world and the creation of a new order, where lodges will play a decisive role. The popularity of such a theory reached its peak in the late 19th - early 20th centuries. Although back in the days of Elizabethan Russia, the first accusations of conspiracy against the Russian people appeared. The empress accused the boyars and some members of the intelligentsia of belonging to Masonic lodges and embezzlement of the treasury.



With the beginning of the 20th century, similar theories were common in France, America, Russia, and Germany. In the United States, the popular belief was that the "founding fathers" (Lincoln and others) were members of the Masonic lodge. This fact is confirmed by real historical sources. A number of US office buildings have a variety of symbols that identify as Masonic. For example, at the Capitol Building, the White House, at Dallas Airport.

Radical nationalists, better known as the Black Hundreds, have blamed Masonic lodges and Zionist sects for organizing a revolution. Who professes
