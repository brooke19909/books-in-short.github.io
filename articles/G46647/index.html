The height of the Petronas Twin Towers in Malaysia and something else

The end of the twentieth century was marked by the rapid development of the countries of Southeast Asia. State revenues grew day by day, making it possible to build fashionable hotels and other buildings with an amazing design. One such country is Malaysia. Petronas Towers, named after the local oil giant, are a real decoration of the capital of this distant state.

Malaysia: Petronas Towers and Their History

The project originated in the nineties of the twentieth century, when the authorities decided to move the hippodrome from the center of Kuala Lumpur, thereby freeing 40 hectares of land. It was decided to erect something reflecting the progress of the Malay people.



To carry out this assignment, Cesar Pelli was called up. He eagerly took up the project, proposing to add two small round extensions to the towers, which increased the reliability of the structure.

The height of the Petronas twin towers in Malaysia was not planned to be a record, but in 1996 it suddenly turned out that there was not a single building in the world that would be closer to the sky.

The official opening took place in 1999, on August 28, seven years after the start of construction.

Skyscrapers of Malaysia: Petronas Twin Towers and their characteristics

The main symbol of the success and progress of the state is located in the heart of Kuala Lumpur. Surrounded by fountains and a park, the complex is truly amazing.



The construction of the project on a national scale was personally supervised by the prime minister of the country of those years - Mahathir Mohamad. He wanted the height of the Petronas Twin Towers in Malaysia to surprise the whole world. In addition, the design was also to be unusual and catchy.

The skyscraper was erected in a Muslim style: the building was made in the shape of an octagon, and at the highest point, the structure is slightly reminiscent of minarets. In addition, it was possible to complete the task of getting into the Guinness Book of Records, although initially no one thought about it. The height of the Petronas towers was almost 452 meters, making the building the tallest in the world for 6 years: from 1998 to 2004.

The total area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe complex, including parks and buildings, is almost 40 hectares. In addition, the construction of the building took 13,000 cubic meters of concrete: the building has the most powerful foundation in the world!

For comfortable movement around the tower, a whole system of elevators is used: 29 in each of them. They move at a speed of up to 7 m / s.

Sky bridge

Even at the design stage of the towers, Petronas Pelli was thinking about how to establish communication between buildings. As a result, he came up with a brilliant idea: to build a bridge at an altitude of 170 meters, which connected the ‚Äútwins‚Äù.



Thus, in 1995, the best engineering minds from Samsung Heavy Industries designed and implemented a unique bridge. It was decided to place it at the 41 and 42 floors of buildings.

The length of the engineering miracle was 58 meters, and the total weight is 750 tons. Despite the powerful technical parameters, the bridge was mounted within three days, since all the blanks for it were previously created in South Korea.

Now, not only the height of the Petronas twin towers in Malaysia attracts tourists with their views, but also a unique bridge, as if suspended in the air. By the way, the first of a number of viewing platforms of the complex is located there.

What to visit in Petronas Towers



Of course, when it comes to this phenomenal building, many want to climb as high as possible in order to capture the eyes of Kuala Lumpur and its environs. The height of the Petronas Twin Towers in Malaysia is so great that you can see any part of the capital. On the other hand, getting to the very peak is not so easy: the number of tickets is limited, but there are many who want to. Thus, it is worth buying the right to visit in the early morning, at 8-30, when the ticket office is just opening. It‚Äôs better not to use the services of third-party companies, as often their tickets are invalid and no one will return the money. It will be interesting to visit the Petronas Science Museum located inside the building. There are many interactive activities for adults and children. Unfortunately, you have to stand a long line at the entrance, especially on weekends.

In addition, all the lower floors of the towers are filled with various supermarkets and boutiques, so there is always something to occupy yourself with.

Beaches, sun, nature, people, technology - the country really has every opportunity to become something more than an exotic exotic!
