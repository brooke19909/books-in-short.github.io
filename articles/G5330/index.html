What information should the check contain?

Considering the types of checks in more detail, one cannot but say about the information that they must necessarily contain. So, they indicate the following information:

  • name of the document (type of check);
  • amount payable;
  • information about the payer indicating his account number (it is from him that the payment will be made in the future);
  • currency for payment;
  • the place where the check was drawn up and the date of its completion;
  • signature of the drawer.

Do not confuse several identical concepts

In addition to securities, other information carriers that are similar only in name take part in the calculations. So, for example, in retail outlets there are cashier's checks. They are a special receipt, printed on paper.

The most common types of checks in the store, supermarket and other points of sale of goods and services. As a rule, they are printed on a special ribbon coming from the cash register.

The following information shall be indicated on such media:

  • name of shop;
  • address of organization or branch;
  • the price of the purchased product or service;
  • legal form of organization;
  • check number;
  • TIN of the cashier or seller;
  • the amount, taking into account taxes and fees (often the amount received and the amount of change issued are also signed);
  • operation time;

However, these types of cashier checks are issued upon receipt of cash from a customer or payment by card. Sometimes they must be presented to receive the goods already paid.

Varieties of cash receipts: what are they?

Depending on their purpose, as well as the variety of goods and services, types of cash receipts are conventionally divided into: paper and electronic. In the first case, the client receives a check printed on paper, and in the second - only its electronic version.

By the way, upon receipt of an electronic form, it can always be smoothly transferred to an alternative paper version. But for this it must first be printed. It is realistic to receive a check in electronic form after payment by card or when using virtual currency in online stores.

What is a sales receipt?

In addition to the above, there are other types of sales checks. In particular, these include paper media that private entrepreneurs use for reporting. In this case, we have in mind the form of a sales receipt, which is an excellent addition to the previously received cash document (receipt). Usually it does not apply to standard forms of strict reporting because it performs an auxiliary function. Nevertheless, it contains the following information:

  • name and type of document;
  • contact details of the entrepreneur;
  • TIN;
  • date of receipt;
  • form number;
  • full list of goods and services;
  • Amount
  • Signature and full details of the person issuing the check.

It is noteworthy that information on each of the purchased goods and services (taking into account its cost and weight) is recorded in such a document. For greater convenience, various types of operations with checks are recorded in the appropriate notebook or cash book.
