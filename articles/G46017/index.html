Laboratory methane production

As a rule, it is synthesized from sodium acetate by the Dumas reaction. 10 g of the mixture, which consists of one part of sodium acetate and two parts of soda lime (saturated solution of sodium hydroxide with calcium oxide in a ratio of 1: 1), is added to a dry test tube. Ethylene Production from Methane

Ethylene is an important raw material for the production of polystyrene and polyethylene. Modern industrial methods for producing ethylene are based on vapor-phase cracking of light oil fractions. A promising alternative to this method could be the production of ethylene from methane. Unlike oil, natural gas, the main structural component of which is CH4, is regarded as a renewable source of raw materials. In this case, the reaction takes place at a temperature of 500-9000 degrees in the presence of O2 and oxides of manganese or cadmium. The resulting gases are separated by absorption, deep cooling, and distillation under pressure.

Under laboratory conditions, ethylene is prepared by ethanol dehydration and ethane dehydrogenation. The obtained ethylene can be used for the synthesis of ethanol, polyethylene, ethylbenzene, vinyl chloride, antifreezes (substances that reduce the freezing point of water in motors) and other organic compounds. In medicine and veterinary medicine, ethylene is used as a narcotic drug, in crop production - to accelerate the ripening of fruits (oranges, lemons, tomatoes, bananas, grapefruits, etc.).

Methane use

In addition to the fact that methane is a valuable fuel, it is also a raw material for the production of methyl alcohol, chloroform, formaldehyde, plastics, etc. Acetylene and other compounds are synthesized from methane.
