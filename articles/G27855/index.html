Mount Kosvinsky Stone: Description

The Ural Mountains are a very valuable and unique natural object. It is here that you can breathe clean air and drink spring water. Admire the majestic beauty of the native land. Get a boost of vigor and energy.

Ural open spaces

In the vast eastern expanses, a narrow belt from the north, from the Yamal Peninsula, to the south, to the very borders of the Orenburg region, stretches the Ural Mountains. They are considered to be the natural border dividing the mainland into two halves: Europe and Asia. Their length is more than 2000 kilometers.





A distinctive feature is the expansion of the massif according to its length: at the top point it is 40-60 km, down to 150. The mountain range is also divided into five parts: the Northern Urals, Southern, Middle, Subpolar and Polar. Scientists claim that this massif was formed more than 3.5 billion years ago as a result of a rupture of the earth's crust.

Rich natural area

Due to its favorable location in the taiga zone, in addition to natural resources, the Northern Urals is famous for its forest. Most of the territory of the Sverdlovsk region is occupied by deciduous and coniferous vegetation. Basically, these are trees of valuable species: cedar, pine, spruce, larch, which allows us to develop the wood processing industry.

Many large rivers flow through the ridge: Vishera, Pechora, Kosva, Lozva. A lot of different fish live in them: herring, grayling, whitefish, taimen, salmon and other valuable species, therefore fisheries are well developed here. But no one can still name the number of small river basins.





General array information

Mount Kosvinsky Kamen is very famous outside the Urals, its height is 1519 m. It is only half a meter lower than its neighbor (Konzhak), therefore it is considered one of the highest on the Ural ridge. Its circumference is 40 km.

The mountain surprises many with its original form, which is presented in the form of a regular cone with a plain at the top and steep slopes. Therefore, in the period of snowmelt, small lakes form on its plane.

I would like to note that on the Kosvinsky Kamen mountain there is the presence of rocky outcrops (sections of the once higher surface, preserved from destruction by arrozia, presented as separate massifs) of a very interesting shape. Simply put - stone pillars.





Surrounding nature

Nature generously endowed the hill with a variety of herbs and flowers, which pleasantly delight the eye with its bright colors.

The main composition of the Kosvinsky Stone massif is presented in the form of plutonic rock - dunite and pyroxenite. The presence of the first, of course, implies a deposit of a precious metal - platinum, and the second - sulfide ores and nickel.

Coniferous forests grow on the lower slopes of the mountain for 900 meters to the top, turning into the tundra with huge boulders of stone placer.

From the south side of the peak, the Malaya Kosva River originates, the length of which is 10 km. It falls into the Kama reservoir.

It is worth noting that the weather in this area is very variable, there are often rains and very few clear days.





Vacation spot for tourists

The small village of Kytlym administratively belongs to the city district of Karpinsk, and it is located on the east side at the foot of the mountain we are considering. Kosvinsky Stone attracts many tourists, and the village is a favorite stopping place for visitors. Its good location - surrounded by the Ural Mountains - provides an opportunity to choose to conquer any of the peaks.

Have you decided to spend your vacation here? Perfectly! I want to say that the road, although not very difficult, but it will take a lot of time to overcome it. Those who have gathered on the road in their own car can drive the coordinates into the navigator and follow the laid route to the very mountain with such a beautiful name - Kosvinsky Stone.

How to get from Yekaterinburg? A bus runs from the North Station in the direction of the city of Karpinsk. This trip takes up to 8 hours. From Perm, the route will be 11 hours long. Then you have to get to the village of Kytlym minibus taxi.

Using public transport, a bus, for example, you can get to Karpinsk central station, and from there - a local minibus to the village.





Types of rest

Vacationers, especially skiers and snowboarders, are very fond of coming here in winter. High steep slopes and a lot of snow will provide a lot of fun and increased levels of adrenaline. You can not just ride, but take part in the competitions that are held here and try to win.

You can also test your strengths in a more active form of relaxation, such as climbing to the top of the mountain.

Kosvinsky Stone, as already mentioned, is replete with the presence of water flows. A large number of the latter opens up the possibility for tourists to travel by boat and catamaran. Rafting along mountain rivers is very popular among tourists. Moving along the surface of the water, it is almost impossible to get off the route and get lost. You are in a good mood and good spirits.





Underground secrets

Locals say that inside the Kosvinsky Stone Mountain is completely pitted with underground tunnels and passages and looks like a large anthill. These walkways are so spacious that not only a car, but also a large truck can drive through them.

It is worth noting the fact that a mysterious construction is still underway there, special transport with construction materials and cement trucks constantly arrives there. In the old days, platinum was mined here, so the horizontal mines dug for this are convenient enough for work. There is also a multi-level security system, and entry there is only possible exclusively by passes.

And another secret is hidden by the Kosvinsky Stone Mountain - a bunker that has been located inside since Soviet times. It is there that the command center for the Strategic Missile Forces (Strategic Missile Forces) and the main center for controlling the air defense system - the "Perimeter" are located. All work there is carried out under special control and in complete secrecy, so we can only guess what is happening there.

The unique material dunite, which is used in the manufacture of refractories, has the unique ability to block scanning radio waves. Therefore, the location of the secret base of the armed forces here is very beneficial for the military.

To ensure the life of the facility, a power line was built, bridges were laid across the rivers to provide easy access to transport to the destination. Near the base, in the village of Kytlym, for the military, who are involved in its protection and maintenance, they built a small town with residential buildings and the necessary infrastructure. The officers and their families live nearby - in the city of Karpinsk.
