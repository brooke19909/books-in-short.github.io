Cozy resort Mishor - reviews on a wonderful vacation

12 kilometers from Yalta is the resort village of Miskhor. Reviews about this village more and more confirm the popularity of this resort. Due to the unique climate, the warmest summer on the entire coast is here. In addition, Mount Ai-Petri protects it from the penetration of cold streams and winds. It is impossible not to note the beauty of the village, which does not leave indifferent any tourist. There are many gardens and parks, as well as pine forests. This makes the resort not only a place where you can relax, but also a kind of health resort where you can treat various pulmonary diseases.



What else attracts tourists to the village of Miskhor? Reviews in 2013 show that its main attraction is the beaches, most of which are covered with small pebbles. All kinds of attractions are installed, there are locker rooms. Many have a paid entrance. The most popular among them is the beach "Hurovsky stones". The beach itself is pebble, but with the help of buns it is divided into several sections. Its territory is well equipped. But the city beach provides its services to vacationers for free. During your stay, be sure to visit the memorable places of the village of Miskhor. Reviews show that the Miskhor Park dating from the 18th century is especially popular with tourists. Its territory is 21.3 hectares. Tourists are attracted by a unique collection of exotic plants, of which there are more than 100 species. This place is one of the largest guest houses. Its name coincides with the name of the park itself. In addition, the Kommunary sanatorium operates on its territory. Among other structures you can see the House of the Union of Theater Workers and the cottage "Miskhor".

But this is not all the attractions that can be seen while relaxing in the village of Miskhor. Reviews show that one of the majestic structures of this settlement is the Dulber Palace. This structure was built as a fortress, so its walls are thick and high. During the revolution, members of the royal family took refuge in this building from reprisal. Today's restored palace delights guests with its snow-white walls with numerous arches and the splendor of silver domes. A particular beauty is given to the building by an ornament of saturated blue and blue against the background of snow-white walls. Around is a beautiful park with rare species of trees and flowers.
