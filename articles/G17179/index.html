Monreale, Sicily: attractions, the best hotels, reviews

Monreale is a small Italian city, a suburb of Palermo, the capital of Sicily. It is located on a hill with stunning views of the Golden Shell - a picturesque valley famous for orange, almond and olive groves.

Translated, the name of the city means "royal mountain." Typically, tourists coming to rest in Sicily, visit Montreal to enjoy the beauty of this ancient city with stunning architecture. There are not many monuments here, but they are all unique.

Monreale Attractions (Sicily) - Cathedral of Santa Maria Nuova

This is undoubtedly the main attraction of the city. A magnificent example of Norman-Arab architecture was laid during the reign of King William II the Good. This monarch reigned peacefully, there was enough money in the treasury, because the construction of the cathedral, founded in 1174, was completed in 1183.

Over the next six years, interior decoration of the cathedral in Montreal in Sicily was carried out. Mosaics used in the design of the temple deserve special attention. We will talk about them a little later. Cathedral of Montreal in Sicily belongs to a special group of monuments of the island, characterized by a mixture of different cultures: local, which has its history from ancient times, and Arabic.

The incredibly beautiful and majestic temple combines two main trends of southern Italian architecture - festive splendor and severe power. Bronze doors adorn the facade of the Cathedral of Montreal in Sicily. They depict scenes from the New and Old Testament. They are opened today only on major church holidays, and on other days you can enter the cathedral through the side door.

In the structure of the Cathedral, its plan and decoration, combinations of Byzantine, Arabic and Norman-French styles are clearly visible. Outwardly, the cathedral resembles the Catholic Church, which seems to be connected with the Orthodox religious building.

The same combination of styles is observed in the interiors of the beautiful temple and its unique mosaic.

Mosaic

The Cathedral of Montreal in Sicily is decorated with the most impressive set of Byzantine mosaics in Italy - 6.5 thousand square meters. m. Unfortunately, today the names of their authors are lost.

Tiny images of glass mosaics in bright colors on a golden background adorn the entire interior of the temple. In the center of tourists' attention is attracted by the huge image of Christ sitting with the Virgin Mary, occupying half the dome. In addition, here you can see the faces of Saints Peter and Paul. No less beautiful are the pictures depicted along the nave.

The figures of saints and prophets, skillfully made of mosaics, adorn almost all the walls of the Cathedral of Montreal in Sicily, which today is protected by UNESCO as a unique architectural and historical monument.

Benedektinsky monastery

Another significant attraction of Montreal in Sicily. The monastery began to be built at the same time as the Cathedral (1174). In March 1176, the first monks who arrived from the monastery of La Cava (Salerno) settled in the monastery.

I must say that in Sicily quite often there was a change of royal dynasties, but despite this the monastery in Montreal has always flourished. By the 17th century, it became one of the richest monasteries in Italy. The pearl of the beautiful monastery complex is the square courtyard of Kjostro, adjacent to the south side of the Cathedral. It is surrounded by elegant arcades and columns, decorated with mosaics and decorated with sculptural compositions. In the corner of the courtyard is a small but very beautiful fountain in the Arabic style, which is an exact copy of the fountain in the Alhambra Palace (Spain).

City garden

If you are interested in where to take a walk in Montreal in Sicily, we recommend that you go to the City Garden. This is a wonderful place where you can wander along the beautiful shady alleys, admire the scenic panorama of Sicily, the Conca d'Oro valley, the Palermo mountains, the turquoise waters of the Tyrrhenian Sea, original plant sculptures from the observation platforms equipped here.

Castellachio Castle

The magnificent building is located five kilometers from the city, on top of Mount Caputo. This ancient defensive structure (XII century) is often called the Big Castle. This is the only castle in the district of Palermo, which has been preserved in its original form - it has never been rebuilt or reconstructed.

The castle was built to protect and monitor the approaches to Montreal by land and sea. You can get to the inner territories of the castle only at the peak of the tourist season, taking part in a guided tour organized by a Sicilian alpine club.

San Martino Abbey

The structure was built on this land in 600, during the reign of Gregory the Great, but in 829 it burned out completely during the attack of the Arab troops. In such a destroyed state, the monastery stood until 1346, when it was not rebuilt. From then until now, the abbey has been operational.
