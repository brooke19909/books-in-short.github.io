The nature and types of investment

Investments in various forms and forms have penetrated so deeply into our daily lives that many people who are not even directly related to investing have a common understanding of what investment is. The nature and types of investments of different authors are slightly different. Let us dwell on this definition of investment:

An investment is an investment of capital in any form (property, money, securities, etc.) in the objects of the economy for profit or for solving any social problems.

Types of investments.

In the economy, investments are divided according to the objects of capital investment into financial and real . Real investment involves investing in the operations of the enterprise and the life of the staff. Financial investments are investments in securities and other financial instruments.

According to the level of investment risk, investments can be of four types: risk-free, low-risk, medium-risk, high-risk and speculative investment. Explanations are only required for the latter type of investment. For speculative types of investments, the maximum level of income is always expected, but money is invested in questionably risky investment projects.

By the nature of participation in the investment process, investments can be direct and indirect. In direct investment, the investor invests money himself, most often in the authorized capital of enterprises, securities, etc. In indirect investment, financial intermediaries are involved in the allocation of money.

According to the investment period, the following types of investments are distinguished: short-term (up to one year) and long-term (more than a year).

Types of investments by ownership: private and public .

And finally, according to regional affiliation, national and foreign investments are distinguished.

  2. Mutual investment funds . For those who are just beginning to comprehend the basics of profitable capital allocation, this investment is the most convenient both in price and in decision making. An investor buys shares that are nothing more than stocks or bonds of any company. The interest is calculated on the amount of investment.
