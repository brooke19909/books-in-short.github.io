On the edge of Europe in Portugal

Portugal is the very edge of Europe, it is located in a narrow strip along the coast of the Atlantic Ocean. There is a mild marine subtropical climate, many attractions that testify to the ancient history of this country. In the past Portugal, the Lusitanian tribe lived here, then Roman rule began, after the Romans the barbarian Visigoth tribes came here, in the early Middle Ages this territory was conquered by the Arabs. She was freed only in the XII century, but came under Spanish rule. Only in the twentieth century did an independent Portuguese state emerge. This synthesis of ethnic groups and cultures gave rise to a unique Portuguese culture, which people from all over the world come to see.

Most tourists arriving here purchase tours to Portugal, choosing an individual or group option for sightseeing and relaxing on the beaches of this country. Here you can see not only historical and cultural monuments, but also wonderful natural sites, which are also the sights of Portugal.

The list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites contains 15 monuments located in Portugal. Two of them are located near Lisbon: the city of Sintra of the 11th century and the monastery of Jeronimites and the Belem tower of the 15th century, built in Manueline power - the Portuguese version of the Renaissance. The monastery was founded by Heinrich the Navigator and its history is closely connected with the history of the Great geographical discoveries in which the Portuguese, together with the Spaniards, discovered the New World. You can also visit the cities of Evora, Porto, Guimaraes, Angra do Heroismo, whose historical center is also on the list of world cultural heritage. There are many castles and palaces left over from past centuries and also representing ancient fortifications and ceremonial residences of kings and nobles.

Portugal is a country in which valleys and mountains are adjacent to each other. For those who are attracted by natural attractions, it is interesting to visit the north of the country with its mountainous terrain, where castles and monasteries are inscribed. There are magnificent parks with various types of tropical plants, a riot of colors and an unusual air filled with aromas. These are the tropical gardens of Monte Palace, Serralvish Park with a magnificent rose garden, the Botanical Garden of the University of Coimbra, etc.

Portugal also attracts with the possibility of a beach holiday in Madeira, which is also famous for its waterfalls, caves and extinct volcanoes. Levada - irrigation systems of the 16th century, a botanical garden and architectural and park ensembles, for example, the palace of Sao Lorenzo, and picturesque villages have been preserved here.

Holidays in Portugal is an opportunity to get acquainted with the original culture and its monuments.
