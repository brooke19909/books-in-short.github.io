Famous sayings. Suvorov about the army, soldiers, tactics.

Very well characterize the historical figures of their statements. Suvorov in this regard is one of the most colorful representatives of his era. He became famous not only for his numerous victories, but also for his well-aimed aphorisms about his homeland, honor, and war. These expressions betray him as a wise man, educated, but most importantly, close to ordinary soldiers who loved and understood their commander. The Generalissimo believed that the main guarantee of success does not lie in the number of troops, but in the art of using it, he argued that one must "fight not by numbers, but by skill."

short biography

When it comes to this illustrious commander, his statements are first remembered. Suvorov was very tagged and sharp on the tongue, although he did not receive professional education. He was born in 1730 in Moscow in the family of a general. The young man was engaged in self-education, he served in several regiments. Subsequently, he participated in seven wars, sixty battles, none of which he lost. The hero of our article was not only a brilliant tactician and strategist, but also an excellent theoretician, wrote books on the art of warfare.





His main principle in the attacks was surprise, which was reflected in his next phrase: "He who won, he surprised." Despite his fame, he was for some time out of favor with the imperial court, although he took part in such major events as the suppression of the Pugachev uprising, the Polish uprising, in Italian campaigns. The famous commander died in 1800 and was buried in Petersburg.

About the army

The versatility of his personality is evidenced by the statements. Suvorov attached great importance to the patriotic education of soldiers. Many of his aphorisms on the Russian people, weapons, allegiance to the fatherland, and the courage of soldiers have been preserved. So, he said: "The Rusak is not a coward." Alexander Vasilievich was convinced of the strength and power of the Russian army, the development of which he attached so much importance. In his opinion, in the case of skillful use of its best qualities, you can always achieve victories. He changed the linear tactics of warfare and began to attach great importance to the tactics of columns and loose battle. At the same time, Suvorov believed that success was achieved through a sudden and decisive turning point in the battle.

At the same time, he attached fundamental importance to the national factor, arguing that "We are Russians, we will overcome everything." Such statements by Suvorov about the homeland indicate that he very well understood the need to maintain a patriotic spirit in the army. The success of his military campaigns is also explained by the fact that there was complete trust between him and his soldiers: ordinary soldiers loved their commander and trusted him. The above statements by Suvorov about the army testify to his understanding of the character of the soldiers, which made him a favorite of the troops. The uniqueness of his personality lies in the fact that he was not only a talented military man, but also well versed in diplomacy, understanding its conventionality: "They lie in the office and beat in the field."
