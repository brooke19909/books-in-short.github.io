Historical reference

The Vourvourou region in Greece got its current name thanks to the Byzantines, who in the distant past called this territory the Land of the Barbarians. In the X century, in the Byzantine era, on the site where the Mother of God Chapel is now located, there was Jeromemnimon Monastery, which later became the courtyard of the Xenophon monastery in Athos.





Excavations around the chapel revealed many valuable artifacts that once belonged to the monastery. In ancient times, the city of Singos was located here. From the name of the city came the name of the bay, washing its coast. This city was first mentioned in the manuscripts of Herodotus. It was inhabited by the Chalkideans, and in the 5th century BC it was part of the Athenian Union. During the Peloponnesian War, the city left the union, but again became part of it in 421 BC, when the Spartans and Athenians signed a peace agreement.

Vourvourou (Halkidiki) is characterized by relatively hot and dry summers and rainy cool winters. The hottest time at the resort is July and August. During this period, the air temperature averages from +25 to +28 ¬∞ C, but quite often the air warms up to + 40 ¬∞ C. However, this temperature is quite easily tolerated, thanks to the sea breezes.

sights

The main feature of Vourvourou in Greece is the unique nature of this area. The picturesque village on the sea is conveniently located among the hills covered with dense forests. Picturesque rocky coves and islets delight vacationers. Near the coast rises the Itamos massif. The fire tower located on its top offers a magnificent view of the Aegean Sea and Halkidiki.

Places of worship

The village has the most beautiful Orthodox churches: St. Theon, the Church of the Virgin, in which the inhabitants of the village annually celebrate the birthday of the Virgin (September 8), the Church of the Prophet Elijah.





Diaporos Island

Visitors to the resort should definitely visit this island. It is not only a unique corner of nature, but also the location of numerous attractions. Here, tourists can see the ruins of the church of St. Andrew. It is advisable to visit other islands of Vourvourou.

To get to know them you can rent a boat or a motor boat. The resort does not require any permits for small watercraft with engines of up to thirty horsepower. Rent a boat will cost you 40-60 euros per hour.





The best beaches of Sithonia

Among the many beaches of Sithonia, there are several that are awarded the Blue Flag. This is an international award given to beaches, the water and coast of which meet the highest standards, and swimming and relaxing on them are safe. There are no Blue Flag beaches in Vourvourou , but they are no less picturesque and comfortable for vacationers. The beaches of Vourvourou in Greece have all the necessary equipment for diving, because the underwater life is no less exciting than the coastal landscapes.

The main beach of the resort stretches along the entire resort. Since this is a bay, securely closed by islands, the water here is very warm and without waves. Caridi

A beach known for its beauty. In fact, these are two bays with wonderful sandy beaches. There are few vacationers in smaller ones. At most, it is usually difficult to find free space. It is shallow, the coast is covered with white sand, huge pine trees on the coast. The bay is framed by bizarre stones.





Agios Nikolaos

Beautiful beach: sandy, wide. The bottom is sandy with a few shells. In the wind there are light waves that beat small jellyfish, but they are completely safe. There are few tourists here. Around there are several villas, apartments and two hotels.

Vourvoudu in Greece: hotels

This resort village has a huge selection of accommodation - there are all-inclusive hotels, villas and apartments. They can be booked in advance or selected upon arrival on vacation by contacting the owner directly - it can be cheaper.

Vergos Hotel 3 *

Surrounded by green gardens, this beachfront Vourvourou resort offers a free beach, a pool with parasols and sun loungers. The hotel has a breakfast room, pool terrace, snack bar. Greek evenings are regularly held here.





The hotel has access to swimming pools for adults and children. Younger guests can play at the on-site playground. The beach can be reached on foot. The road will take no more than ten minutes.

Hotel Rema 3 *

Hotel Rema is surrounded by a huge beautiful garden, which is located in the center of the village, very close to the beach. The cozy rooms are functionally furnished and equipped with modern appliances. The road to the beach takes fifteen minutes of a leisurely walk. It offers guests a swimming pool filled with salt water, a restaurant. Guests can relax on the terrace or work out in the on-site gym. In the mornings, a buffet breakfast is served in the restaurant overlooking the sea. It offers gourmet European and Greek cuisine. Just a mile and a half from the Rema Hotel is the famous Caridi Beach.
