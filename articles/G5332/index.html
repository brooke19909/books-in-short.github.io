Where is Jamaica - Dream Island

In the heart of the Caribbean Sea is located one of the most beautiful islands in the world - Jamaica. This is a country where there is a mixture of several cultures at once: African, Asian, European and Middle Eastern. Despite the fact that almost everyone has heard the name of this country, not everyone knows where Jamaica is located.

The island lies a little south of Cuba and west of Haiti. North shore rocky. It is here that the largest number of beaches are concentrated. The south coast has good harbors, but is bordered by a large number of reefs. A large territory of the island is located on a limestone plateau, whose height reaches 1000 meters. In the eastern part are the Blue Mountain Mountains. In addition, the island impresses with the magnificence and beauty of the numerous waterfalls located on its territory.



Now that the answer to the question of where Jamaica is located is the time to get acquainted with the history of the island. Before the arrival of the Spaniards, Indians lived on it. They lived in small tribes, and their total number was approximately 60,000. During the reign of the Spaniards, the Indians from the island almost disappeared. By the beginning of the 17th century, there were a little less than a hundred of them left. Due to lack of labor, the Spaniards decided to bring Negro slaves to Jamaica. After the capture of the island by the British, an English colony was formed here. They built a fort, near which the city of Port Royal began to grow, which soon gained the glory of the most sinful settlement, it became the capital of pirates.



Once we find out where Jamaica is located, it will not be out of place to find out about tours to this wonderful corner of the world. The island gives vacationers the most diverse experience. Here you can take a walk in the mountains, go on a raft on the Martha Brae River, admire the most beautiful waterfalls. One of the most popular is a trip on a ship to the banks of the Black River. Excursion tours to Kingston, the capital of Jamaica , which begin with the historical part of the city, are also considered unforgettable. Almost all programs include a visit to the Bob Marley House Museum, a trip to the cane plantation at Belvedere Estate, and a visit to the factory producing the famous Jamaican rum.

Recently, the popularity of this island is growing from year to year. Numerous tourists wish to spend their holidays on such a picturesque island as Jamaica. Rest spent here will be remembered by the endless white beaches and turquoise expanse of water. There is practically no bad weather. Those who go to the island for diving, relax on the north coast. Here, the reefs form a peculiar labyrinth of a bizarre shape, along which you can lay your own route. Here you can see beautiful corals, the remains of sunken ships. Grottoes and caves are found everywhere. In addition to diving, the island offers numerous horseback riding and hiking routes that allow you to look into the most forgotten corners of Jamaica. Golf is very popular on the island. There are 10 fields, the best of which is considered the "Trial".



Among the many resorts on the island of Jamaica, reviews of tourists show that the most popular are the following: Montego Bay, Negril, Ocho Rios. Negril is a picturesque resort area that occupies an area in southwestern Jamaica. Here is a superb beach, modern hotels and the ruins of ancient forts. Montego Bay is the largest resort area in Jamaica, which is famous for its vibrant and fun nightlife. A huge number of upscale hotels and equipped beaches are concentrated here. A golf course is available. Ocho Rios consists of a string of excellent beaches, capes, bays, small coastal towns. It is bordered by picturesque mountains, from which stormy rivers descend to the sea. Comfortable hotels rise along the coast.

However, in order to feel the splendor of this holiday, knowing where Jamaica is located is not enough. You must yourself feel the atmosphere of this enchanting island, inhale the smell of fragrant exotic flowers, feel the hospitality of local residents and the unique Jamaican flavor.
