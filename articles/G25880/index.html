Paper products for children: palm-based applications

Your baby is still very tiny, but time will pass and he will become an adult. He, like all people, will be pleased to see some little thing reminiscent of a distant childhood. Let it be paper products, for example, palm-based applications.

It is not difficult to make such applications, in addition, it will be interesting for the child to participate in this action himself. And the process of making the application itself is very useful for children, since, by working with the hands, the child massages the fingertips, and they send signals to the brain, stimulating him. That, in turn, makes all mental processes activate.

So, if you want your child to grow talented, and even get interesting paper products for memory, then get to work.

Together with your child, make an applique of such a cute frog.

Children with great pleasure will do such paper products with their own hands. Schemes and drawings will help to make adults - parents or grandparents.

To get started, prepare the material necessary for the work: newspaper, green, black and white paper, scissors and glue (preferably PVA), a clean rag to remove excess “paste”.

Then make the “patterns” of the appliqué (first on the newspaper, and then transfer to colored paper). Put your child’s hand on the newspaper, circle it with a pencil and cut it out with scissors. Then attach the resulting blank to colored paper and make two such patterns. These will be the frog legs.

Make a head by cutting out a green circle with a diameter slightly larger than the length of the foot. Cut the eyes of the frog oval (from white paper), and in the center of each “eye”, attach black stripes. Glue all the parts. Draw a mouth and nostrils with a black felt-tip pen. The little frog is ready. He smiles sweetly at you! And I’m almost ready to publish my funny croaking! A paper product will be more interesting if you use a thick material.

These cute ducklings can be made from colored paper in the same way.



Prepare details for each duckling: a pear-shaped brown body of a bird, three pairs of wings of red, yellow and orange flowers (the blanks of which will be obtained after the child’s palm is circled), red legs and a beak. Glue the wings, paws and beak to the duckling body. In place of the peephole, fasten the white paper circles. Draw black dots on them with a felt-tip pen. The duckling is ready. He looks at you with a sly look and is even ready to take off, though not very high.

And such an interesting paper product as an autumn tree can also be made using children's hands.



Prepare 12 parts of different shades - red, orange, yellow, crimson, cut the tree trunk from brown paper. Glue these palm-shaped leaflets to the tree trunk.

It seems as if it is kindly waving to us with its colorful leaves and is not at all sad that these leaves will soon fall to the ground.

A tree trunk pattern can also be cut in the shape of a child’s palm. Such an application will be more fun and original.

If you want paper products for children to look smart, use velvet printing.
