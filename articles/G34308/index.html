This is as an introduction. And now - volumetric origami. It can be represented by copyright models of snowflakes, balls and colors of different volumes and configurations. Such crafts were made from ordinary sheets of paper, specially cut and bonded to each other. Particularly beautiful balls presented on the forums are signed by the author and creator and are truly designer work. If you have several of them, then you can create a unique interior in any room - and the room will immediately acquire individuality and comfort. Folk craftsmen also learned to fold sheets of paper in such a way that volumetric inscriptions were obtained. There are no size limits for origami art - therefore, theoretically, you can create an inscription on the entire wall. Agree, your beloved (or beloved) will be pleasantly shocked to see your name, laid out in voluminous letters on the wall in the living room! You can arrange individual cards in the same way. There are no big secrets on how to make voluminous origami: you need to fold a paper sheet so that you get the volume (we learned this in kindergarten) and fasten the elements together. Classical volumetric origami involves the creation of small masterpieces only from paper; however, if you intend to use the product in the future as a toy, it is better to glue the elements together.

Traditional volumetric origami involves the creation of figures from modules. What is a module? This is a square piece of paper folded in a special way. It turns out a triangle, which can be connected to other same triangles using the grooves available in the module itself. The manufacturing scheme of the module is simple, the problem is that you need hundreds to create a figure. So, a volumetric origami swan requires from 500 to 1500 modules of different colors (or white - depending on which figure you are going to create). Do not be alarmed! Even a child can learn how to quickly fold a module.
