The Ancient World is ... The Definition of the Ancient World

In a broad sense, the Ancient World is a designation of the very first and longest period in the history of mankind. It captures the length of time from the beginning of the formation of society (BC 800-1000 thousand years) to the appearance of the first feudal formations (the beginning of our era, the first centuries). This concept covers two huge historical periods, which many have known since school days as a primitive society and a slave system.

The definition of the Ancient World cannot be given in a few words. This concept is capacious, including the events of a huge period of human development. How did it end?

How did it all begin and how did it end? Ancient Rome at first was a small village located on the banks of the Tiber River. After a long period of time, he turned into the capital of a great, powerful power, which captured, one might say, almost the whole world. But over time, he became exhausted in the fight against slaves and conquered peoples. The Roman state fell as a result of numerous attacks by barbarian tribes and again split into many parts. The peoples who seized the territory of the former great empire formed their kingdoms and began to create their own civilization.

This is the final point in the history of the Ancient World - the fall of the Western Roman Empire.
