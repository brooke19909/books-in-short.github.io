Having such a short history, the reconnaissance ship of the Baltic Fleet "Vasily Tatishchev" still managed to go on 22 campaigns along the route across the Atlantic Ocean, the Baltic and North, as well as the Mediterranean Sea. Back to historical personality

A bright start to the development of sciences in tsarist Russia, as well as in Europe, is associated with a small number of names. A few lines from the biography

Tatishchev Vasily Nikitich, whose contribution to the history is simply invaluable, was born on April 19, 1686. Educated in Moscow, graduating from the Artillery and Engineering Schools. He began his career under Peter I as a soldier, participating in the Northern War at the beginning of the 18th century. At the end of the war, Tatishchev began to draw up geographical maps, being carried away for life both with history and geography. Continuing his career in the civil service, Tatishchev receives a referral to the Urals as the manager of state-owned factories. Then he headed the Coin Office for some time. In addition, he was still the head of the Kalmyk and Orenburg commissions. In total, public servants Vasily Tatishchev spent 42 years, ending his career in 1745, five years before his death. Being removed from his post as governor of Astrakhan, Vasily Nikitich was exiled to the Boldino estate in the Moscow Region. Hobbies that have become science

Vasily Tatishchev did not forget about his hobbies in history and geography and directed to their development any opportunities that the life of an official and travel around the country provided him. Any historical written sources, as well as the first Russian maps of the Urals and Siberia, an outstanding historian and cartographer collects. And due to its capabilities, it makes copies of such materials and distributes them in a useful direction. He sends out maps to surveyors to draw up new maps. At the same time, it organizes a search for minerals, personally collects ore samples, forcing, among other things, to describe and make drawings of the deposits themselves. The only wide flow of information allowed Tatishchev to collect extensive and diverse scientific material. The organizer of this work was able to perpetuate and preserve countless information on Siberian geography and archeology, but at the same time on history, ethnography and even linguistics. The scientist combined every business trip with scientific research, sometimes even scientific expeditions. He studied the language, life and customs of the local population, nature and the environment, collecting entire collections of minerals and plants. He carefully examined the Kungur cave and was interested in mineral springs. With such a volume of work and with such organizational skills, few can compare.

Advanced thinking Tatishchev

Everyone knows that people who care about the future always think broadly and thoroughly. Such personalities are always no longer concerned with the problem of daily bread, but with important and global issues. Vasily Tatishchev, who opened the opportunity for understanding Siberia, fascinated by history and science, first of all thought about the descendants and their future. Is it really great wisdom to understand that, developing science, production, construction, military affairs, we need specialists to implement and support all this? Already in the first years of his administration in the Urals, Tatishchev opened schools for teaching geometry and mining. Later, a mining school was opened in Yekaterinburg, which allowed combining theoretical training with the practical application of knowledge at the plant. This was an innovation even for Europe. But even Peter the Great did not fully share this enlightenment approach with Tatishchev.

Retirement and the last years of life

Returning home, Vasily Tatishchev was no longer able to regain his former position and influence. The Empress takes him all the time from place to place, each time moving away from the capital. But in every new place Tatishchev successfully mastered and even began to implement the reforms of his sphere. Later he was thrown to resolve conflicts with Kazakh tribes, Kalmyks, and even sent to the Bashkir rebellion. And the denunciations continue to fly to the capital, and at the insistence of the Senate in 1745, the empress issued a decree on the dismissal of Tatishchev from his post, and also imposed a ban on him to come to St. Petersburg and leave her villages. So Tatishchev, already weakened by disease, falls under house arrest and settles in his estate near Moscow. But a true genius never calms down and does not despair. Boldino becomes like a branch of the Academy of Sciences. Until the last, Tatishchev Vasily Nikitich remained active and incorrigible.
