The Moscow region is the most populated subject of the Russian Federation. On its territory there are 77 cities, of which 19 have a population of more than 100 thousand people, there are many industrial enterprises and cultural and educational institutions, and there is also huge potential for the development of domestic tourism.





Excursion tourism in the Moscow region

On the territory of the Moscow region there are many attractions that will be interesting to those who want to learn more about the history of their country. Moreover, even the "young" cities of the Moscow region deserve attention. Therefore, excursions around the Moscow Region are a great option for spending long weekends for Muscovites and guests of the capital. In order to prepare for such a trip, a tourist will need a detailed map of the Moscow region with cities and information about the monuments located there. history, nature and architecture, and the choice of settlements that should be included in the route of a sightseeing trip around Moscow depends on the personal preferences of the traveler.





City Pushkino, Moscow Region

It is believed that the name of the village, located on the banks of the Ucha River, came from the name of its owner - the boyar Morhinin, nicknamed Pushka - the ancestor of A.S. Pushkin. Today, more than 106 thousand inhabitants live in the city of Pushkino and there are about two dozen industrial enterprises. In addition, Pushkino is a major scientific center. Those who want to get acquainted with the sights of Pushkino should first of all visit Sovetskaya Square of the city, on which there is a large fountain with lighting and monuments to I. Krylov and A. Pushkin. Another beautiful building resembling a fairytale castle can be seen on the Railway Station Square. This snow-white blue water tower was built at the dawn of the last century and is considered one of the most recognizable symbols of the city of Pushkino. As for the ancient monuments, the main one is the temple of Julian of Tarsus, with 12 bells, consecrated in the 16th century. And nature lovers will certainly enjoy visiting the unique Museum of Forest Seeds.





City of Dzerzhinsky, Moscow Region

In 1380, Dmitry Donskoy founded on the lands located southeast of the borders of modern Moscow, the Nikolo-Ugreshsky Monastery. In 1920, the brethren were expelled from the monastery, and a commune of street children named after them was placed in its walls. Dzerzhinsky. That is how the city of Dzerzhinsky (Moscow region) appeared over time. Today, the main attraction of these places is the restored architectural complex of the Nikolo-Ugreshsky Monastery, among the buildings of which the magnificent Transfiguration Cathedral, the bell tower, the Assumption Church and the so-called Jerusalem Wall stand out. In addition, the monastery houses a memorial museum of the murdered Tsar Nicholas II, which presents postcards, photographs, books, portraits, household items and personal items related to the family of the last Russian emperor. Another attraction of the city of Dzerzhinsky is the open-air memorial museum in honor of the creators of the missile shield of our country. On its territory you can see a real ballistic missile, the famous Grad installation and the S-125 complex.





Zheleznodorozhny (Moscow region)

Those who want to know why the village of Sergievka was nicknamed Obiralovka should go east from Moscow. There, 10 km from the capital, the city of Zheleznodorozhny (Moscow Region) is located, which arose around the railway station of the same name, by combining several villages, including the ancient Obiralovka. By the way, in the world famous work of Leo Tolstoy, Anna Karenina throws herself under the train precisely from the platform in Obiralovka. Although the history of the city of Zheleznodorozhny lasts just over six decades, there are several interesting architectural monuments. For example, the old Preobrazhensky temple with the most beautiful porcelain iconostasis, as well as the Troitskoye-Kainardzhi estate with the chapel-mausoleum of the famous Russian commander S. Rumyantsev, deserve attention. And of the modern sights of Zheleznodorozhny, one can note the huge telescope of the Vega Astronomical Children's School.





Podolsk (Moscow region)

Talking about the cities of the Moscow region, it is impossible to ignore Podolsk. Indeed, besides being one of the most densely populated settlements of the Moscow Region, tourists will find many interesting sights there. For example, you should definitely visit the magnificent temple of the Trinity Life-giving in a restrained style, an empire built in the thirties of the 19th century in honor of the victory of Russia in the war with Napoleon. Another magnificent monument of Russian architecture - the Church of the Resurrection of the Word - is one of the oldest buildings in Podolsk. In addition, in the city and its environs there are several old manor estates. These include, in particular, Ivanovskoye, where the Museum of Local Lore is located today, and Plescheevo, where P. Tchaikovsky once visited and worked.

Chekhov city, Moscow region

The first mention of a settlement called Lopasnya is found in a letter dated 1175. It was this village that over time turned into a city, which in 1954 was renamed Chekhov. It so happened historically that in the Chekhov district there are many memorable places associated with the descendants of A.S. Pushkin and with the great playwright and writer A.P. Chekhov, so a trip to these places promises to be very interesting and informative. For example, tourists should visit the Zachatievskaya estate, where a 17th-century temple and a baroque manor house have been preserved. In the same place, in Zachatievsky, you can see the tombs of General A. Pushkin, the eldest son of the great poet and several other members of his family. Other historical monuments of the city of Chekhov include the Anno-Zachatievskaya Church and the post office building, built with the assistance of A.P. Chekhov.
