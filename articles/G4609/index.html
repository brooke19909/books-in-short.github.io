Chenonceau Castle

When walking along the Loire Valley, one cannot help but notice the castle of Chenonceau (France), which was built in 1515-1521. in the style of the early Renaissance and late Gothic and is surrounded by unique gardens.



At different times, this chateau was owned by Catherine de Medici and Francis I. All the owners of the castle were rebuilt several times until it fell into the possession of the Magnet family (1914), which was engaged in the production of chocolate. The reconstruction of the interiors was completed only in 1951. Due to this, the interior decoration of the halls and rooms remained practically unchanged. Today, Chenonceau Castle is the most visited estate in the country. In order not to push around the curious, it is better to come here early in the morning. There is an opportunity to rent a portable audio guide - it will tell you the whole history of these places.

Krusol Fortress

Krusol fortress was built in the XII century in order to protect the river valley. The Rhone. This fortification guarded the borders of France from raids from Italy. At the time the construction began, these lands belonged to Gerald Bastet, Lord.

The fortress was erected on a limestone peak and was an excellent observation and fortified point. She was a key point in the surrounding area. The natural steep slope continued the walls of the fortification. The castle was almost impregnable.

The main building occupied 3 hectares. The first rampart is not included. It was created to protect a small village adjacent to the fortress. She numbered no more than 100 houses.



The Krusol fortress in the XVI century, when the internecine wars began, was in the very center of hostilities. Local feudal lords under the pretext of protecting religion tried to seize large territories. The fortress was destroyed during the fighting, and at the moment lies in ruins.

Chaumont-sur-Loire

Looking at the castles of the Loire, you can not ignore this one. On the rocky bank of the river, between the cities of Blois and Tours, this real gem of palace architecture hid.



The history of this beautiful castle is directly related to the names of Diana de Poitiers, Catherine de Medici, Nostradamus, as well as various princes, with whom he was turned into an exquisite residence. Today, the castle hosts the famous International Garden Festival. Every year at least 30 gardens are created by local artists, landscape designers, and gardeners on the adjacent territory.

Angers Castle

In the historical part of the country, in the department of Maine and Loire, is located the castle of Angers. It got its name from the city of the same name. During its formation, the castle has repeatedly been the cause of the political struggle of France and England. Its massive walls, created in the form of huge columns, withstood many siege actions. This castle was one of the main strategic objects of the country.

It began to be built in the ninth century in order to withstand the attacks of the Romans. In the thirteenth century it was equipped with a complex of fortifications. Then there was a strengthening and expansion of its defensive structures.

The castle was for some time the residence of Rene of Anjou, who organized knightly tournaments and literary festivals there. Due to the constant wars in the sixteenth century, the castle fell into complete decline, and Henry III ordered it to be destroyed. But the fortress was so powerful that it did not succeed in fulfilling its plan, and the work was completed. Soon there was held the solemn betrothal of Francoise of Lorraine and Caesar of Vendome.

The imagination of visitors is still struck by the huge Apocalypse tapestry. This work is several hundred meters long.

The medieval square tower has survived to this day. She was part of the general system of defensive structures. The castle outlines were softened by Renaissance architecture elements - their structure acquired after the purchase of the castle by Charles VIII.

The building was turned into the residence of the king, it was decorated with exquisite stone carvings, and a chapel was also attached for Anna Brittany. At one time, Francis I, Leonardo da Vinci, Margarita Navarskaya were here, Babu de la Burdesiere (the favorite of the king) lived here.

If Castle

The castle, famous for the novel by A. Dumas, is located in the south of France. It was built to protect the city from attacks from the sea. It was ordered to be built by Francis I, although the castle was never attacked, due to which it was able to survive to this day in complete integrity.



Modern Marseille is proud of it rightfully - this is one of its main attractions. So, excursions are held around If Castle, there is a cozy cafe, as well as postcards and souvenirs.

For a long time, this castle was used as a prison, since it was the best place for exile - it was almost impossible to escape from there because of the strong currents off the coast of the island. In the fortress there were cameras with no windows located in the back of the building, although there were other conditions for people with wealth - they were in the upper part of the building, where it was possible to enjoy the seascape and breathe fresh air.

Only in the late nineteenth century did the prison cease to exist, and the castle became a landmark of the country.

Serran Castle

Serran fortress was built in the sixteenth century, but, like many castles in France, the old building was significantly rebuilt in the late centuries. The property originally belonged to the Le Bree family - they asked permission for its construction from Louis XI. The design was done by the famous Philibert Delorm.

After the changes, the spirit of the Renaissance, characteristic of the period of Francis I, still remained here (almost all the castles of the Loire were built in it). The oldest elements of the castle are the corner towers and moats, which are harmoniously combined with the domes crowning the towers and wide windows. The towers at the top are decorated with balustrades.

On the facade of the building, decorated with a triangular pediment, a beautiful contrast is created by white sandstone and dark brown slate.

The castle was able to preserve the interior interiors with a magnificent library and tapestries.
