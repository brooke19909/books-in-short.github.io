The whole truth about the price of the ruble from the position of professionals

Visually, money contains symbols according to the regulations, weight, diameter correspond to the standard. Why then is there such a difference in price for the same coin today? After all, there are no distinctive features: the jubilee coin of that time with Lenin is sold at one auction for a song and the second resource publishes the same face value, but with a price of tens of thousands of difference.

With the naked eye and with poorly published pictures of coins, there is no way to solve this riddle. But turning to the professionals, we get two coins of the same name, dedicated to the anniversary issue, face value of one, but with a completely different appearance.

This is the key to the dual value of the coin. The first category is known as ‚Äúeveryday‚Äù, the coin is dull, and over time it undergoes darkening and staining, both on the front and back.

The second category has obverse and reverse of a shiny glossy shade. Gloss does not fade over time. This category in professional circles is called Proof. But the main differences are in the circulation of the issue.

Paper rubles of the USSR: fantasies of artists

The equivalents of metallic money were banknotes on paper. In this matter, the country of the Soviets was also not inferior in art and artistic beauty. Both minimal denominations of paper money and notes of the highest value had captivating plots, bright colors, but with the obligatory standard: an image of Lenin in various angles. The full face of the leader of the world revolution was used on the first copies of Soviet paper rubles, on a U-turn and was detailed in detail.

Such a design of paper money was used in the formation of the Soviet state, when the imperial stamping or model of execution of disciplines was still not everywhere abandoned. Denznaks were distinguished by a wide and long form, with a detailed portrait of Lenin full face.

But the exploitation of the chervonets showed the inappropriateness of this format, so the mint artists were given the task of replacing it. Such paper rubles with Lenin from the era of the formation of the USSR are of true value to world collectors due to their fine design and rarity.

Soviet paper banknotes: circulation practice

Paper money for everyday use dictated conditions for the convenience of using it. It turned out that large design banknotes are very impractical, wasteful at issue and inconvenient in everyday life. Therefore, special regulations were adopted, standards for paper banknotes for different denominations were established.

For each size of the banknote, a color spectrum was selected, the size, not so intricate, but, nevertheless, the artistic design. Money stamping measures were introduced for practical, cost-effective and easy-to-use banknotes for circulation by a multimillion-dollar population.

The only thing that remained unchanged was the image of Lenin on banknotes. And although the ‚Äúyoung‚Äù full-face of the communist leader has now been replaced by an adult profile, nevertheless, he did not leave a single banknote in the country of the Soviets until its last days.

For collectors at auctions, Soviet paper money represents a different value depending on several criteria: the appearance of the banknote, face value, year of issue, and others. But just like the metal counterpart, they are in demand among numismatists around the world. Collectors love especially large bills of the USSR in good and excellent condition: from fifty rubles. The price is not bad at all.

Ruble and Lenin: conclusion

In the annals of the history of our state, even the Russian Empire, even the Soviet Union, in popular circulation, for the name of money, a short name was used - "ruble".
