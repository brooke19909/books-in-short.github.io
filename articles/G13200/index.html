Kostroma province: counties and its history

The history of the Russian Empire is very interesting. Kostroma province of that time: what do we know about it? What monuments remained after wars and defeats? The city of Kostroma was a significant posad for the Russian state. Transformation accelerated the growth of the economy and culture. The architecture of those times still adorns historic cities.

Geography

The Kostroma province is located in the European part of the Russian Empire. Its borders: from the west, it borders with Yaroslavskaya; in the south - with Vladimir and Nizhny Novgorod. Northern neighbor - Vologda; East - Vyatka.

History

Kostroma province was formed in 1719. It was renamed governorship by 1778. It consisted of 12 counties. The area occupied by the Kostroma province was almost 84 thousand square kilometers. By 1926, the number of counties was reduced to 7. It was liquidated, like the rest of the provinces, in 1929. Subsequently, it became known as the region.

Coat of arms

Initially, it was executed in the form of a shield divided into four equal parts. The first depicts a silver cross, the second and fourth are made in golden color, and on the fourth part - a crescent moon, turned upside down. Such a coat of arms was canceled in 1878 and a new one was adopted, depicting the imperial crown, the Varangian ship and oak leaves connected by the St. Andrew ribbon.





Counties

Kostroma province included many territorial objects. Of these, 12 were counties. How did they differ?

Buysky district was located in the western part. Its area was almost 3 thousand square miles. It was formed thanks to the administrative reform of Catherine II. It consisted of 17 volosts. It was inhabited by more than 70 thousand people.



Barnabas County was formed in 1778. It included 21 volosts. The county town was Barnavin. Based on the census of 1897, the population was more than 120 thousand people. Abolished in 1923.

Vetluzhsky uyezd bordered on the Vyatka and Vologda provinces. Its area was more than 15 thousand square kilometers. It consisted of 21 volosts. About 120 thousand people lived in the territory.

Galich district existed from 1727 to 1929. Located in the central part of the province. It had large estates of boyars and nobles. The structure included 24 volosts with a total number of inhabitants of more than 100 thousand people.



Kinesham County, whose area was 4433 square versts, was divided into two halves by the Volga River. It consisted of 23 volosts. The county town is Kineshma.

Kologrivsky was located in the northern part. On its square there were 27 volosts. The population, consisting of 99% of Russians, amounted to more than 130 thousand people.

Kostroma city

It is located on the banks of the Volga River. Kostroma was founded in the 7th century. The city center still retains part of the architecture of the XVIII-XIX centuries. It has the official status of a historical city. It is the center of the textile industry.

The woodworking and food industries are developing very actively. In addition to flax products, Kostroma is also known for jewelry, because on its territory there are several factories for the production of jewelry made of precious metals.
