City and urban planning

The city differs from other settlements in a number of certain characteristics. Their study is the science of urban studies. But the theory and practice of planning, as well as urban development, is already a field of interest for urban development.





Urban planning is the activity of developing and creating urban systems, their reconstruction and improving the human living space.

What is a "historical city"? What are its characteristic features and how does it differ from the modern one?

The Law ‚ÄúOn the Protection and Use of Immovable Monuments of History and Culture‚Äù interprets this concept as follows: a historical city is a village in which there are a large number of cultural heritage sites. In historical cities, as a rule, numerous cultural objects are located that have a certain value for society: historical, aesthetic, etc. Another distinctive feature of such settlements is that they retain the old (historical) layout of streets and squares.





It is important to note that a historical city is not only architecture and cultural values, but also its population. That is, people with their own set of traditions, customs, outlooks on life. Architecture of the historic city: main features

Architecture is the most useful form of art. Man began to build homes for himself since ancient times. To do this, he used those materials that were available in a particular area. For security reasons, the villages were surrounded by shafts or walls, wicker fences.

The architecture of a historical city in the West is significantly different from the layout of eastern or ancient Russian cities. The latter fit as organically as possible into the natural landscape, closely tied to the surrounding countryside. At the same time, an aesthetic aspect was allotted when choosing a place for the future city. Indeed, in ancient chronicles one can often find similar sayings: ‚Äú... and the place is red, and having loved it, may it burn the city‚Äù.

The architecture of the historical city in the East also differs by certain features. In the center of the eastern settlement, a square with a mosque and a madrasah, a school for the training of teachers and ministers, was being developed. An important place in the layout of the city was also given to the caravanserai - a place for street trading.

Layout of European historical cities

The traditions of urban planning during the times of Antiquity, the Middle Ages and the Renaissance were different. A vivid example of the ancient city in Europe can be considered the Acropolis in Athens. In those distant times, any settlement began with the construction of a magnificent temple on an elevated place. Around this holy hill streets were laid, the location of which was rectangular.

A typical medieval city is a village fenced by powerful fortifications with battlements and moats filled with water. In the center of such a fortified city, a cathedral and the main town hall were built.





In the Renaissance, cities were built according to a new scheme. The streets were perpendicular to each other, and at the end of the most important of them, any outstanding architectural structures or entire ensembles of magnificent buildings were necessarily located. By the way, it was by this algorithm that the Russian city of St. Petersburg was built.

Features of the architecture of the modern city

Modern architecture uses completely new principles and forms of organization of free space. So, in the early twentieth century, new styles in architecture appeared - constructivism and functionalism, which sought to maximize the simplification of buildings and structures.





Today, typical and very similar dwelling houses are being built in cities. If earlier in the cities the spiers of Gothic cathedrals were the highest, today they are office towers made of glass and concrete. New building materials make it possible to lighten all constructed structures.

How does modern architecture affect the emotional and mental state of a person? Many scholars argue that it is extremely negative, emphasizing that she has completely lost her status in the spiritual and aesthetic sphere of human life. Indeed, it is very difficult to enjoy the architecture of the 21st century. Faceless gray buildings, traffic jams, long concrete fences, an acute shortage of vegetation - all this, according to psychologists, creates an unfavorable, aggressive environment for human life.
