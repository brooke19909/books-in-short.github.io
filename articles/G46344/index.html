The most unusual sights of St. Petersburg: description, history, interesting facts and reviews

The majestic St. Petersburg, every corner of which is saturated with rich history and a special atmosphere, will forever remain in the heart of the person who met him. However, it is simply impossible to see the main monuments of North Palmyra, falling in love at first sight, in one trip. To explore all the corners of a fantastically beautiful city on the Neva, it will not be enough for several years.

But there are still unusual sights of St. Petersburg that are of interest to those who are tempted by popular architectural works. Today we look at the work of Peter from a different angle and take a fascinating journey through little-known places, often left unattended by tourists.

Cathedral Mosque

Northern Venice has always been a multinational city in which people who profess different religions coexisted peacefully. This is confirmed by the unusual sights of St. Petersburg - one of the largest mosques in the world and a Buddhist temple that serve as a real decoration of the Northern capital of Russia.



The most beautiful building, which serves as an educational center, where the basics of Islam are taught, appeared in 1913. However, it was closed under Soviet rule, and only 60 years ago the cathedral mosque, located next to Troitskaya Square in the area called "Tatarka", was returned to believers. The bright turquoise dome covered with ceramic mosaic is visible even on a cloudy day, while minarets directed into the sky bring bright colors to the city landscape.

A masterpiece descended from the pages of oriental tales

A magnificent architectural masterpiece, the walls of which are finished with gray granite and decorated with an ornamental pattern, as if descended from the pages of oriental tales. A spacious building appeared near the mosque, where Muslims, before entering the temple, perform a ritual bath. Unusual sights of St. Petersburg can be visited by all tourists subject to certain rules. At the entrance you need to take off your shoes and turn off your cell phone, and women can enter the hall reserved for prayer only with their heads covered and in closed clothes.

Buddhist temple on Primorsky Prospekt

Temples of all religions are comfortably located on the banks of the Neva , and therefore it is not surprising that at the beginning of the last century on the outskirts of the city a so-called Tibetan miracle appeared - datsan Gunzechoynei. It is curious that the architect involved in the project decided to endow the religious monument with the features of the Northern Art Nouveau style popular in Europe.



During the Civil War, a Buddhist manor, which is included in the list of the most unusual sights of St. Petersburg, was destroyed and lost a significant part of the relics.

A place where dreams come true

The three-tiered datsan, erected in compliance with all the canons, is surrounded by a fence, to the rods of which believers and guests tie colored ribbons, and each shred symbolizes someone's dream. Before entering the temple, spinning drums with prayer mantras are installed, and each visitor cranks them in the hope of fulfilling a cherished desire. Mosaic patio

To see the unusual sights of St. Petersburg, not included in the standard guidebooks, is the dream of any visitor to the city. The mosaic courtyard, located on Tchaikovsky Street, is the very place that is better to be seen once. The unique open-air museum was created by the hands of pupils of the Small Academy of Arts, and the St. Petersburg artist V. Lubenko was the ideological inspiration. The ordinary courtyard, which is hiding from casual passers-by, has become a canvas for creative people who decorated it with colorful mosaics.



A wonderful corner shimmering in all shades of the rainbow is a rather young attraction, widely known among local residents. Paths, curbs, benches, ground floors and numerous sculptures are decorated with colorful mosaic panels on a variety of topics. The guests of the amazing courtyard delightedly peer into the creation of human hands, finding something new in bizarre plexuses. The best time to visit the fabulous corner is a bright sunny day, when multi-colored tiles play under the rays of the sun.

Gingerbread house

Without exaggeration, to visit the most unusual sights in St. Petersburg, it is not enough to spend here even a few weeks. To get acquainted with a curious architectural masterpiece, it is worth going to Kolokolnaya Street, where the object of cultural heritage stands - the so-called gingerbread house. Similar to a beautiful tower from a folk tale, it was built at the beginning of the last century. A wonderful example of Russian architecture, despite the abundance of colorful details, looks very harmonious.

Brickwork, multi-colored majolica mosaics, peaked towers, kokoshniks above the windows, floral ornaments evoke a lot of positive emotions among all visitors to the city and delight the eye with exquisite luxury.

John Lennon Street

When planning a visit to the city on the Neva, everyone asks what the most unusual sights of St. Petersburg can be seen. Relatively recently, a non-existent street appeared, a sign of which flaunts in the courtyard of the art center on Pushkinskaya. This is an interesting corner for those who want to get acquainted with the reverse side of North Palmyra and appreciate street art.

The corner, which is not mentioned by any guide, was founded by the Beatles lover N. Vasin, who received an autographed album from John Lennon. Nikolai spent a long time fighting the city authorities, wanting to name one of the streets in the name of his beloved artist.



In the end, the leader of the Beatleman movement in Russia hung a sign in his yard and put a sign pointing to the sky to emphasize the fact that Lennon Street does not pass on the ground. First, plaster bas-reliefs appeared depicting the faces of The Beatles musicians, then they painted a yellow submarine on the wall. A street that has neither on one map, it turned into a Mecca for the Beatles, and for more than five years now astonished tourists have been visiting an informal monument of St. Petersburg.

Museum "Grand Layout Russia"

Finishing to list interesting places in the most beautiful city in the world, we will describe the unusual sights of St. Petersburg for children. Parents who came to rest with their kids think about where to take their children and what to show them. The children will have a wonderful time at the planetarium, watching through the telescope the stars, the huge amusement park Divo Ostrov or the Aquarium, the Mindo Garden, where tropical butterflies flutter and sit on visitors.

However, one of the most interesting places that will be able to interest any kid is located on Flower Street. The Museum "Grand Layout Russia", spreading over 800 m 2 , contains all the significant objects for our country. The exhibition, which was created using the latest developments, is controlled by 40 computers and will not leave indifferent both adults and young children.

Trains and cars are moving here, lumberjacks are felling trees, and firefighters are eliminating fires. Visitors can select various scenarios by launching them using buttons, and the children are especially happy when they independently manage events.
