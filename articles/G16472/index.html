Bath mat

Over time, every housewife accumulates a lot of old and unnecessary things, but many women have the so-called Plyushkina syndrome, save up various trifles, not even allowing the thought of disposing of their ‚Äútreasures‚Äù. A lot of useful things can be made from such rubbish, one of them is a bath mat. To create the product, you will need plastic bags, scissors and a hook, the head of which will be slightly thicker than the material for knitting. Creating a product consists of the following steps:

  1. Material preparation. Handles and adhesions are cut from the bags, after which the source material is cut in a spiral into a tape, the width of which depends only on the manufacturer‚Äôs wishes, the dependence is simple - the wider the tape, the thicker the mat.
  2. The resulting tape is wound into a ball, solely for the convenience of further work, all subsequent tapes are connected in a single strip.
  3. Further, everything is crocheted according to the principle of circular knitting. The size of the mat is regulated only by the amount of materials.

It is possible to produce such decorative elements not only from polyethylene, but also from long rags according to the same principles, the difference in products will only be in care. New ideas for remaking old things regularly appear on home economics sites.

You can get a soft and lush bath mat, bedside area and other needs using old towels, a flannelette blanket or a terry plaid. The manufacturing steps will be as follows:

  • Training. The source material must be cut into individual tapes about 5 cm wide.
  • All the resulting material should be divided into 3 equal parts, sew each part together, the output should be 3 long ribbons.
  • The next step will be braiding.
  • Next, you need to curl the braid so that a circle is obtained, fastening together with threads on the wrong side of the product.

Such a rug can be cleaned in a washing machine, provided that the tapes were well bonded to each other. Such products can be decorated with appliqu√©s, and dermatin, knitting threads and, in general, everything that can be woven can be present as the starting material.
