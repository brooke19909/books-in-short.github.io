Section 4. Brief History of the Country





According to legend, Singapore was founded by the Raja Sang Nila Utama, who landed on the island in the 13th century and named it Singapore, which in Sanskrit means the City of Leo. It was this animal that he met first on the island. Although historically proven that these lands were settled back in the 11th century and were called Temasek ("Sea City"). But all this remained in oblivion, and the official history of Singapore began from the moment the British colony was founded on the island in 1819. The island was at the crossroads of important economic shipping lanes between Europe, Australia and Asia. Singapore soon became the most famous trading port in Asia.

During the Second World War, the Singapore fortress was considered the strongest military base in Britain, but there was no fleet in the fortifications. And in February 1942, unable to repulse the attack, Singapore surrendered. The power of Britain over the island was lost. Having received self-government in 1955, Singapore in 1963 entered the Malaysian Federation, but soon decided to leave it. In August 1965, the island became an independent state.

Now one of the richest countries in Asia is Singapore. Which country adopts laws so that local incomes only increase in volume? Perhaps only this state from all the surrounding.

Section 5. Climate of Singapore





Due to the proximity of the island to the equator, the climate has no seasonality. It is almost always sunny and humid. Precipitation in the form of torrential rains falls daily, especially with the advent of the northeast monsoon from November to January. Thunderstorms can often be observed.

From May to October a haze appears over the island caused by forest fires on the neighboring island of Sumatra, which, however, quickly disappears.

Section 9. Holidays and festivals





Want to attend a holiday heading to Singapore? What country in Asia can please so many celebrations of national scale? Perhaps no.

All holidays here are marked by colorful processions, concerts and fireworks. A revered holiday is the Chinese New Year, when residents give each other citrus trees. These days there is a Chingai parade with dances and boat races, drums are heard on the streets. In May, Chinese churches celebrate the birthday of the Third Prince - the "divine child." Mediums in a trance state cause cuts to the body, sacrificing their blood in exchange for the prosperity of their people.

In August, the Chinese "month of hungry spirits." These days, according to legend, the gates of Hell open, and the souls of the dead come to the living. In the houses they light incense sticks and burn money, and on the threshold and on the windowsills they put bowls of food.

But the main holiday of Singapore is considered to be Hari Raya Poissa, celebrated according to the Muslim calendar at the end of the month of Ramadan. On this day, the fast ends, during which it was forbidden to take food and water during daylight hours.
