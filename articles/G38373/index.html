Prague in December: attractions, reviews

Prague is one of the most beautiful European capitals. Here, each building can be considered a real work of art. The capital of the Czech Republic is beautiful at any time of the year, but Prague is especially beautiful in December. At this time, the city is decorated with lights and snow, locals roam the streets looking for gifts for Christmas, and tourists enjoy the sights. In this publication we have collected the most useful and interesting information about the Czech capital (popular places, entertainment, prices in Prague, weather and much more).

Winter tourism in Prague

According to reviews, Prague in December acquires a special charm. At this time, the city becomes fabulous. The streets are covered with snow, and the facades of beautiful historical buildings are decorated with garlands and lights. That is why today winter tours to the Czech Republic are becoming increasingly popular .

From December 24, the Christmas holidays begin in the country. At this time, the Czechs showered each other with gifts, so prices in Prague are rising sharply, reviews warn. Experienced tourists recommend going on an excursion to the city before the winter holidays or after them. Catholic Christmas in the country is considered a family celebration. At the end of December, all state institutions, museums and entertainment centers were closed. In order to spend an unforgettable vacation in Prague, go here. Reviews are advised in the middle of the first winter month. At this time, the city is already imbued with the spirit of Christmas. In the windows of the houses you can see small Christmas trees and betlems (Jesus creches), historical buildings are decorated with interesting compositions, and trade is boiling around the main squares.





Weather in Prague in winter

Winter in the Czech Republic is mild. Frosts are rare here. In the afternoon, the temperature most often does not fall below 0 degrees. According to reviews, the weather in Prague during the winter months will delight tourists with a comfortable temperature and lack of frost. In December and January, precipitation in the form of snow or light rain is possible here. It should be noted that the winter in Prague, as well as throughout the Czech Republic, is foggy. In the morning, clouds block the sky through which the rays of the sun occasionally break through. Because of this, in the Czech capital in winter there are often problems with traffic.

Prague in December is especially beautiful. That is why travelers love to come here in the winter.

From mid-December, Christmas markets open in the city . Traditional New Year's treats are sold on city squares: waffles, trdlo (the national Czech dessert), mulled wine and much more.

In addition, before Christmas in all city boutiques the discount season begins. At this time, you can buy leather shoes, jewelry, jewelry and branded clothing of Czech origin.

The main squares of the city

  • Wenceslas Square is a favorite place in Prague and a popular tourist attraction. It is located in the very center of the city, so it is simply impossible to pass by such a sight. The most significant city events take place on Wenceslas Square: demonstrations, holidays, meetings. In December, you can see the largest number of local residents. After all, it is on this square that Prague residents gather in order to celebrate St. Miklus‚Äôs Day and Christmas together.
  • Old Town Square is located in the heart of the historical part of Prague. Here is the majority of old buildings: buildings of the 12th century, the city hall of the 13th century, Tyn church of the 14th century. On Old Town Square, you must be on the eve of the Christmas holidays. At this time, she becomes unusually beautiful.




Stare place

The historical center of Prague is called Stare Mesto. Most of the sights are concentrated in this part of the city, so this is where tourists first go. Consider the most interesting objects of the historical center of Prague:

  • The public house of the 20th century is a beautiful example of the architecture of the Art Nouveau period. In this building, the independence of Czechoslovakia was proclaimed in 1918.
  • The gunpowder gate of the 16th century is an interesting sight of Prague.
  • The town square of the 13th century is an ancient center of the country's political life. Like all of Prague in December, this building on Christmas Eve becomes especially beautiful.
  • The Church of St. James is an interesting building of the 13th century. At the turn of the 14-15th centuries, it was reconstructed and today is an excellent example of Prague baroque.




The sights of Prague that everyone should see

  • Charles Bridge is perhaps the most famous landmark of the Czech capital. It connects the historic city center and the Mala Strana area. Charles Bridge was built in the 14th century and for a long time served royal needs. With it, the rulers moved from one bank of the Vltava to the other.
  • Prague Castle is the oldest and largest fortress in the country. It was laid back in the early 9th century and for a long time was the main political and cultural center of the city.
  • St. Vitus Cathedral is the pearl of the Czech capital, an architectural masterpiece erected in the Gothic style. The building, built in the 14th century, today is considered one of the most famous and recognizable buildings in Europe.
