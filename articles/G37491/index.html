Kislovodsk: the history of the resort

For many who decided to come to Kislovodsk, the reviews of vacationers were just a complement to the impressions that still warm the souls of our grandparents, as well as mothers and fathers. Indeed, in Soviet times, this resort was one of the most popular in the country. At least once in their life, everyone could afford to come here on vacation, and Kislovodsk sanatoriums were considered one of the best in the USSR.

The city appeared on the map of the country thanks to the source, which today is known as Narzan. Kislovodsk (reviews of vacationers claim that this mineral water can be drunk without medical advice) even got its name because of the taste of the healing liquid. It has a slight acidity and high carbonation, which is why the ancients called this source the Sour Well.

It is worth noting that the highlanders considered the place with healing water sacred and composed legends about it. The Turks and Kabardians had their own source names, which characterized its miraculous power. It is interesting that at the end of the eighteenth century, before the official formation of Kislovodsk, people from various regions of Russia came to these places to improve their health. And only in 1803, by order of Alexander I, a fortress was built on the site of the Sour Well, the first inhabitants of which were the Cossacks.

Nine years later, the first baths were built near the source, and people began to come in large numbers here, eager for healing from various ailments. Just a hundred years after its formation, the Kislovodsk military fortification received the status of a city, and the aristocracy of the empire began to gather in it in the summer season. Since that time, almost all enlightened people of the country tried to leave reviews about Kislovodsk, because this place quickly became popular and extremely fashionable.
