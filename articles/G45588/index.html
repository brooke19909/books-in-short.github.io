Defense of the Caro-Cannes: history, application, varieties

For an interesting and intense game of chess, both beginners and professionals need to master many elementary and more sophisticated techniques. The key to successful completion of the game, which implies a win, is the correct alignment and mobilization of forces at the very beginning of the game, the so-called debut.





Defense Caro-Cannes is one of the most effective types of debut. This type of balance of power was first applied in the 19th century by the Englishman Horatio Caro and the Austrian Marcus Kann. The latter was able to defeat the then world champion in just seventeen steps, using this technique.

The main principles of this type of protection

The fundamental principles for protecting Caro-Cannes are:

  • king safety;
  • control of the central part;
  • rapid development of figures;
  • resistance to the aspirations of the opponent.

When playing chess, the defense of Caro-Cannes has the character of a half-open opening, which makes it more profitable for Black, giving him more opportunities for counterplay. The most common beginning of the Caro-Cannes defense chess game is based on the move e2-e4 c7-c6.





The defense in question gives Black the opportunity to capture the main line in the opening, thereby gaining an advantage at the end of the game.

Varieties of protection

There are and are popular 6 leading varieties of Caro-Cannes protection for blacks:

  • Panov method. VN Panov is the champion of Moscow, the author of books on theories of debut.
  • Closed circuit.
  • Nimzowicz system. A.I. Nimtsovich - an outstanding chess player, a contender for the championship at the beginning of the 20th century.
  • Standard tactics.
  • The Petrosyan-Smyslov system. V.V. Smyslov - world champion number seven in the mid-20th century chess, three-time champion of Moscow, author of books on chess theories.
  • The debut trap.

Most of these methods during the game go to the French defense. Defense Caro-Cannes is not an aggressive method, but provides reliable protection, which helps Black at the end of the game.

Anatoly Karpov and his defense of Karo-Kann

This technique is often used by positional style players. He showed his effectiveness in parties with the participation of such champions as Capablanca, Botvinnik, Petrosyan. It is a favorite technique of the world chess champion Anatoly Karpov.

The defense of Caro-Cannes for Black involves ensuring the reliability of the position in the center at which the h3-c8 diagonal is maintained for his bishop. Such tactics do not allow to simplify the game, and the game is dynamic. Black can also fight at the same pace in order to further capture the initiative.

Thus, the beginning of the game in the form of the defense of Karo-Kann is an effective debut, especially from the point of view of blacks. If you master this strategy, the number of your chess victories will increase.
