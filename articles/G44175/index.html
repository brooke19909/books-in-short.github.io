Kotka - attractions. Kotka city, Finland

The town of Kotka is modest enough by today's standards. From the first minutes, the sights of this place fascinate literally everyone. Why? Experienced travelers claim that there are actually a lot of prerequisites for this, because in the modern world it is quite difficult to find settlements in which history and modernity, fishing and numerous recreation areas are so successfully intertwined.

The city of Kotka (Finland). general description





Historians claim that its foundation dates back to 1878. It was in that year that the decision was made on the need to create a reliable and superbly equipped seaport for the entire state.

Linguists, in turn, will help to deal with such a name that is quite unusual for a Russian person to hear. In Russian, Kotka means "the city of Orel." A very symbolic and, one might say, even a majestic name, in which the founders love and pride for their small homeland can be traced.

Today, 55 thousand people permanently live in Kotka. A town was built near Russia - only 279 km to the border.

Prerequisites for the construction of the city





Kotka shows its sights very willingly, but all of them are determined either by location or by the fame of a laboring village, which has been engaged in its own special craft for centuries.

This Finnish town is located on the shores of the bay of the same name. Its construction was due to the need to build a port, which would play an important role in organizing the supply of timber from Finland. In order to provide access to sailing ships carrying timber, as well as sawmills, it was decided to lay the port city of Kotka.

From the central regions, logs along the Kumiyoki River were rafted to the Kotka area, where there were many sawmills. After sawing, wood material was exported.

What is modern Kotka

Today, Kotka is of great importance in creating the conditions necessary for the export of wood materials abroad. But that is not all.

Now it is also one of the largest Finnish cargo ports, occupying a leading position in the country in the field of transit traffic. It is also known far beyond the borders of Finland, because ships are dispatched all over the world, and the quality of local wood is valued even across the ocean.

Towards Solitude and Peace





It is believed that only the most tired travelers or finished romantics go on holiday to Kotka. Especially many visitors come here at the very end of summer. Why? The fact is that at this time in the vicinity of Kotka a huge number of mushrooms and berries. You can wander around amidst golden, yellow and crimson trees, admire the breathtaking sea views, marvel at the huge ships and dream with a cup of tea in some local cafe.

There are many hotels in the city. There are city hotels and guest villas. All this at very reasonable prices. Free parking is usually provided.

Real Maritime Museum

In Kotka there is a huge maretarium. This building is the pride of the inhabitants of the city. Many marine inhabitants live here in their natural environment. It is difficult to imagine that today in the Maretarium there are already more than 50 species of fish. And this, of course, is not the limit.

Indoors, two aquariums are placed at once. The large depth is 7 m. However, there are no coral fish familiar to such establishments, and it is not worthwhile to look closely. But the individuals that are found in the Baltic Sea are represented.

Zander, whitefish, pike, roach, tench - this is not a complete list of the inhabitants of these wonderful aquariums. Water for their filling is taken in the Gulf of Finland.

By the way, you can feed large carps and perches directly from your hands, plunging into the depths with special equipment. Not only fish live in the Maretarium. You can also observe here frogs, newts and water snakes, which fall asleep in winter. There is a sea shop, you can buy souvenirs if you wish.

The center has its own pier, which has a museum-icebreaker Tarmo, built back in 1907. This is one of the oldest icebreakers on our planet. Section 9. Center for Wooden Architecture





Finland also has a unique center where you can get acquainted with wooden boats in detail. The goal of the creation of this center is to popularize the culture of manufacturing wooden floating equipment.

In this center, they not only restore, but also make boats. Training of future masters is also held here. It should be noted that everyone is allowed to watch their work.
