Paper airplane - return to school

We all come from childhood - this statement is true. One has only to start making crafts with your child, as soon as you want to make an airplane out of paper and launch it into flight. This is what the former notorious hooligans, and now, perhaps, respectable company managers, liked to do in the classroom. But, however, this is not important. We finish the excursion into childhood, and is engaged in origami.



This hobby came to us from Japan, it is there that the implementation of various paper models is very popular. Perhaps a paper airplane is one of the simplest models that can be made. There are a large number of different options, and consider in detail each of them will not be enough time, so we will focus only on a few. They are not the most difficult and even a child can make such crafts.

How to make paper airplanes

The first model is Fast. Despite the fact that it will be made of plain paper, it can fly far enough through the air. To make it, we need only a regular sheet, bending it in different directions, we get a plane similar to a real one.



So, bend it along and draw along the line of addition, squeezing it on both sides with your fingers to mark the outline. Then we return the sheet to its original state. Next, fold in half across the formed line. At the next stage of execution, from the center to each lower corner, bend the paper down, and then wrap up half of each part. Next, we bend each of the lapels, first internal, then external. They should be the same size. Wrap a part of the wing on each side back and bend each element at a certain distance from the bottom. Lock the addition line and return them to their original position. The last thing left is to bend the wing back parallel to the fold line, and your paper airplane is ready.



The next model is a bomber.

Such aircraft during the war were a real thunderstorm for the enemy, and even in peacetime they look no less frightening. This model looks very serious and deserves respect. It has wings bent from above and is designed for rapid flight. This form allows you to increase flight speed, and this paper airplane looks almost like a real one.

It is performed from paper according to the same principle as the previous one, only has its own structure of sheet folding. First you need to mark the fold lines in both directions, and then bend the two upper corners to the center. Wrap the resulting top at the junction of the corners. At the next stage, fold the sheet in half, and then unfold. Again we bend the corners to the center and bend the sheet along the previously outlined line. It remains to bend the wings at a certain distance from the center of the sheet and, folding each in half, mark the fold line. The last thing that remains for us to do is to deploy the wings to the flight position. That's all. The model is ready. Here are such interesting paper airplanes we got.
