Crocheted napkins from motifs: schemes, description, assembly order

Crocheted openwork napkins will be an excellent decoration for the dining or living area. They will give the interior more comfort and beauty, make it sophisticated and unique.

If you want to learn how to create decorative products and are fond of knitting - this article is for you. In it, we will consider in detail how to make original crocheted lace napkins from motifs.

We will present the working schemes convenient and understandable, as well as give all the necessary descriptions and recommendations. We believe that even beginner craftswomen will not have difficulties in performing these beautiful decor items. Let's get down to interesting creative work soon! To the cause!

We learn how to crochet a beautiful napkin from square motifs. Schemes and process description

For work, you need to prepare a thin thread (100% mercerized cotton with a density of 180 m per 50 g). The color of the yarn can be any, but it is advisable to use gentle pastel colors - peach, beige, "dusty" pink or white. You will also need a convenient hook (recommended size - No. 2.5), scissors, a needle.

Let's take a closer look at how to crochet napkins from motifs. The scheme of work is presented in the following figure.

The manufacturing technology of the product is as follows. First, they knit the first square motif, then the second, third and so on, connecting them to each other in the course of work (the connection points are indicated on the diagram by arrows). The number of square elements the master can vary according to his desire, but for a small napkin, the creation of nine motifs will suffice.

The finished whole web is necessarily subjected to wet and heat treatment, as well as starched to give a good shape.

Step-by-step instruction for completing the first square motive

In order to tie the first lace element, familiarize yourself with the scheme and clearly follow our recommendations. Please note that the following abbreviations will be used in the description:

  • VP (air loop).
  • Joint venture (connecting loop).
  • RLS (SC).
  • C1H (double crochet).

Work progress in rows:

  1. We collect 8 VPs, close the joint venture in the ring, knit 3 lift VPs, use the pattern (3 VP - 1 1) 7 times, complete the row of 3 VP and 1 SP.
  2. We do 6 VP, repeat 3 times (4 1 - 4 VP, 4 1 - 3 VP), knit 4 1, 4 VP, 3 1 and close the row of 1 .
  3. Using half loops, we move to the central loop of the first arch of three VPs, perform 5 VP and 4 C1N, then repeat the scheme (4 1 - 4 VP - 4 1, in the first arch, 4 1 - 2 VP - 4 1 - in the second), we complete 3 1 and .

The first motive is ready! We knit the second by analogy, attaching it in places of pico, in the course of work. Now you know how to crochet beautiful napkins from motifs. An interesting model of napkins made of motifs in the shape of hearts. Learn the basics of working with a hook

If you are a novice master and just learning to crochet napkins from motives, we recommend choosing the most simple and understandable schemes. One of these is presented in the figure below.

A napkin made of "hearts" turns out to be very tender and cute. It will easily revive and decorate the interior of any dining area, will be an excellent decorative element when setting the table for a romantic dinner.

To work, you will need to prepare a thin cotton yarn of several colors (yellow, pink, blue, purple) and a hook of a suitable size.

Learning to knit a heart-shaped motif - the basis of our napkin for a romantic dinner

We start work with a yellow thread with the execution of a magic ring. We knit 1 VP, 1 PRS, 5 VP, 1 1, 1 VP, 1 1, 1 VP, 1 2 (double crochet), 1 VP, 1 1, 1 VP, 1 1, 5 VP, 1 and close first row of the joint venture.

In the second row, we perform 1 VP and in the same base loop 1 RLS. Next, we make 2 VPs and in the arch of 5 VPs of the previous row we form 5 1, between which we do not forget to add 1 VP. In the next 2 arches we knit 2 1, with air loops between them. At the top of the column with two crochets of the previous row, we knit 1 C1H. In the next 2 arches, we again do 2 1 with 1 VP between them. In the arch of 5 VP we knit 5 1. We complete the second row of 3 VP and SP.

In the third row, we make 2 VP and 1 PRS, then we tie the edge using the alternation of 3 VP and 1 PRS (in the arches of the previous row). A series of 2 VP and SP completed. We fix the thread, cut off the tails. So our first "heart" for a napkin crocheted of motifs is ready. We will deal with assembly and strapping schemes later.

Create the second element and combine the motives

To make the second heart, we take a thread of a different color. We knit it by analogy with the first. In the third row we make 5 arches from 3 VP and RLS between them. We knit the next 4 arches by attaching the thread to the arches of the first element. We do the rest of the harness as usual. As a result, we get two "hearts" connected together.

We carry out 2 more motives according to the exact same pattern, joining them to the first in the places indicated on the pattern as we knit. We get the first sector of the canvas of the future napkins.

To tie the edges we use a blue thread. We attach it to the second arch of three loops of the upper right heart using the RLS. We perform * 7 VP and 1 sc through the arch *. Repeat * - * twice. Next, we knit 10 VP and 1 sc (skipping two arches). We work according to the scheme to the end of the row.

In the second row of strapping, we use single crochet columns knitted with a common top - in each arch of 7 VP we make three of these elements, and from 10 - 4. We start the row with 2 VP, and in the intervals between the hearts we knit 3 VP - 1 RLS - 3 VP.

We draw up the third row using the scheme (2 VP - pico from 4 VP - 2 VP) in the spaces between the columns with a common vertex. As a result, we get a beautiful lace trim of the edge.

So the first square element of the napkin crocheted from motifs is ready. With the schemes of beautiful hearts and the binding of the edge of the difficulties should not arise. By analogy, we carry out three more exactly the same squares. And then we combine them into a single piece. We subject to the WTO and enjoy the result.

Now you know how to crochet a beautiful napkin from a motif for a romantic dinner. Schemes with a description, we hope, did not cause you any difficulties. I wish you creative success!

Napkin "Tenderness" from round motifs

We bring to your attention another beautiful crochet pattern for napkins with floral motifs. Such a product will be the highlight of your living room, decorate a table, cabinet or chest of drawers, give your interior elegance and expressiveness.

To work, you will need to purchase white cotton yarn (50 g) and a hook number 0.75 mm. The dimensions of the finished product are 21 * 21 cm. In the manufacture of a delicate napkin crocheted from round motifs, we will use the following scheme.

We knit a large motive napkins "Tenderness"

To create a large round element, dial 14 VPs and close the chain in a circle. Row number 1 we start with 3 VP, then in the ring we do 39 1, for completion we use 1 joint venture.

We knit row number 2 according to the following pattern: "3 VP - 1 C4H (column with four double crochets)." Do not forget at the beginning to do 6 lifting VP, and at the end of 1 SP.

Rows from No. 3 to No. 9 are created using the RLS. In row number 10, we use groups of five columns with two crochets, connecting them at one common vertex. Between these "bumps" we knit arches from 9 VP. With the help of this simple drawing, we form petals for our floral motif. The first element of the napkin is almost ready. It remains in row number 11 to make the arches with the help of the scheme "8 SBN - 1 VP - 8 SBN".

Making a little floral motif

An intermediate motive fits a little easier than a large one. We begin work on it with 10 VPs, connecting them into a ring. In row number 1 we do 3 VP and 23 C1N.

In row No. 2 we use groups of 4 columns with two double crochets connected at the same vertex. Between them we knit arches from 8 VP. Get the petals for a floral motif.

In row number 3, we make the arches with the help of the pattern "7 SBN - 1 VP - 7 SBN". In the course of knitting the last row, we attach an intermediate motive to the main one (the attachment points are marked on the diagram with ‚Äúdouble‚Äù arrows).

Finishing work on a delicate floral napkin

By analogy, we knit all other motifs, attaching large ones at three peaks, and small ones at two. As a result, we get a beautiful openwork napkin.
