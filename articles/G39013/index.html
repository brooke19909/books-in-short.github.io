Lochness Lake and its secrets: reality or just business?



The pond, shrouded in a fleur of secrets, is known to the world for various versions of films about the Loch Ness monster, publications and rumors. But not everyone knows where Lochness Lake is. It is located in Scotland, in the very north of Great Britain. Although Lochness Lake is modest on the map, it is quite impressive in size. It reaches 39 kilometers in length. The surface area is 56 square meters. km The maximum depth is 230 meters. So in such a lake more than one monster could hide quite calmly.

Lake Lochness and its inhabitants

There are legends about the reservoir, associated not only with dinosaurs and dinosaurs. Some evidence suggests the appearance of UFOs in the Lake area. Most researchers agree that the anomalous zone is true here . The first evidence of the existence of a monster in the lake appeared in 1966. Then the director of the Air Force channel filmed on camera, as a huge animal moves on the surface of the water. Experts have proven that this is not a montage or layout. After a while, attempts were made to capture Nessie or his cubs. But not one of the expeditions was successful. Every meter of the lake was combed - and all to no avail. In 1992, the latest technologies of that time were involved. A sonar passed through every cubic meter of water. As a result, the researchers found about five species of various large animals, which are clearly attributed to the long-extinct dinosaurs. Believe it or not - it's up to you, but there is evidence of their presence in the pond.



Lake Lohness, as it turned out, is still not fully understood. Already at the end of the 90s with the help of deep-sea vehicles a cave was found in the water column. Its depth exceeds 200 meters, and according to researchers, it can be the habitat of all the outlandish animals of the lake that are hiding there from annoying researchers. Scientists tried to find out whether the lake is connected through this cave with other water bodies, but reliable data could not be obtained.

Issue price



Many regret that now science and research have faded into the background, and the main place is occupied by commercial interest. Lochness Lake has become a tourist attraction and a way to earn huge sums of money. Every year, many vacationers come here, ready to give money in order to become eyewitnesses of the appearance of a relic monster. But so far no one has seen him. Although on the shore there is a museum dedicated to Loch Ness. It contains all the evidence and evidence of Nessie's habitat in the lake, as well as the main representatives of the flora and fauna. The annual tourist income is ¬£ 25 million. But the saddest thing is that for the sake of excitement and to increase the popularity of the place, businessmen go for outright forgery. Photos and videos are forged (modern technical means allow this to be perfected), false witnesses are bribed. In general, violent advertising is underway . As a result, confidence in real scientific discoveries and evidence decreases. By the way, faking a photo is not news. Back in the 30s of the 20th century, a fake photograph of Nessie was taken by a certain surgeon. Many people believed him, and the photo was considered authentic until the surgeon died.
