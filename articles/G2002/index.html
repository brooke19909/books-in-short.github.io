Embroidery History

Embroidery is one of the most popular types of needlework. Embroidery appeared many centuries ago. And if then during this occupation they spent mostly winter days and evenings, now this type of needlework has become a national treasure. The earliest embroideries were found in China - this country is officially considered the ancestor of this type of needlework. The very first works are dated to the V century BC. e. And they survived. This is surprising given that embroidery is a very delicate work.

Embroidery is divided into several types, namely: stitch embroidery, cross stitch, workshop embroidery and more modern embroidery - ribbons. Perhaps the most common is cross stitch. It all began in antiquity, when embroidering crosses was not yet a form of needlework, but was intended to create amulets. After all, the symbol of the cross itself is a figure of a talisman, therefore, things embroidered with such a sign were considered almost totemic, they averted evil and drove away spirits.

Embroidered items are sold in many markets and in stores. Clothes, bags, wallets, as well as home textiles, such as curtains, tablecloths, napkins, sofa cushions, and, of course, one of the most common types of embellishments at home with embroidery, are decorated with embroidery - paintings. Everything is embroidered on the paintings, starting with their own photos and ending with dreams. There is also a belief that if you embroider an object, then you will definitely have it. There are even special images that are advised to embroider to those who want to marry or conceive a child. They say they are very effective.

Why embroider a lighthouse?

But if it is not possible to specify, or the desire is more vague, then it can be made with the first stitch of embroidery of the lighthouse. For some reason, it is believed that this image will fulfill a desire. Maybe the whole thing is in the lighthouses themselves? They, as a symbol of hope for salvation, have been encouraging thousands of people for centuries in a row before returning home. Lighthouses serve as a guide for travelers, indicating the safest way. But not only at night, as many believe. At night they burn continuously or shine with flashes. In the afternoon they serve as a visible reference. According to their height and shape during the day, sailors distinguish their location by coordinates.

Cross stitching a lighthouse is much easier than stitching. And the picture is much more diverse and beautiful. Stroke does not add as many details as possible with a cross. Given the fact that embroidery of lighthouses is usually made from real places, it turns out just incredibly beautiful and textured paintings.
