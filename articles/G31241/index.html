sights

Cultural life on the coast of the Aegean Sea is closely connected with the ancient exotic of these places.

Ephesus is the city of Artemis. The future inhabitants of Ephesus almost two thousand years ago BC began to reside to the shores of the Aegean. The city is located near modern Izmir. Here is the temple of Artemis - one of the seven wonders of the world of the Ancient World. Since 2015, Ephesus has been protected under the auspices of UNESCO World Heritage.

Pamukkale's natural springs are worth a visit if only for the sake of photography. An unusual natural object, includes 17 hot springs, the temperature of which varies from 35 to 100 degrees. Water constantly flowing down the mountain formed small pools with limestone walls. Due to the healing properties of the springs, the flow of tourists here is inexhaustible.

The Hierapolis Ancient Theater is visible from almost anywhere in the ancient city. It rises on a hill to the right of the Byzantine gate and still surprises with the skill of architects of that era. The theater could accommodate 15-20 thousand spectators. A huge amphitheater with special acoustics located at the hot springs, it was famous throughout the Roman Empire, and even its rulers came here.

The oldest city of Izmir is located off the eastern coast of the Aegean Sea - this is the second largest seaport of the country after Istanbul. This place is interesting for its many ruins, ancient Greek and Roman buildings. Everyone who wants to get closer to the history and architecture of Turkey should go here. It is also a good place to shop.

Oludeniz Bay is completely surrounded by pine forests and delights the eyes of even the most sophisticated travelers. The bay is a haven for yachts and ships, and Oludeniz beach is famous for its beauty and estrangement. There are no hotels nearby, it is forbidden to ride boats here, so the waters and the surrounding nature of Oludeniz are unusually clean and bizarre.
