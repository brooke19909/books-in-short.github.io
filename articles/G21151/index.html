Craft history

Origami as a form of art appeared in ancient China. At first, the inhabitants of the country learned how to make paper, and then came up with an interesting lesson.

Oddly enough, but at the dawn of the appearance of origami, only representatives of noble families were engaged. It was believed that only a select, dignified and intelligent people could own such a craft.

The main rule of art: any figures should be made only from a whole sheet of paper, by repeatedly folding and straightening its individual parts. Valuable advice was handed down from generation to generation, neatly recorded or remembered. Art continues to evolve, and each new generation preserves the knowledge of their ancestors, improving the skills of adding paper figures. Origami bird I had to see many, but, besides this, there are many different crafts in the form of animals, plants and decorations.





The benefits of origami classes

Like any other craft, origami helps develop skills such as mindfulness, perseverance and patience. This activity calms and helps to concentrate. Scientists have long noticed that the performance of small manual work makes the brain work more actively.

You can make an origami bird out of paper yourself, and also teach your children, nephews or grandchildren. By the way, this is a great activity that allows family members to get together, discuss some issues and help each other.

Where to start learning origami art

If you have never before made such crafts, do not be alarmed. In fact, there is nothing complicated, and even a child can perform origami. Bird from paper, for example, will be a great option for those who decide to master a simple and interesting technique.

There are many simple origami figures that can be folded before proceeding with the manufacture of complex crafts. Be careful, try - and soon you will be able to boast a whole collection of interesting paper products.

Folding an origami bird from paper - a symbol of happiness

The basis for the future ‚Äúbird‚Äù will be a paper square with sides 20 x 20 cm. The easiest way is to take an A4 sheet and cut off an extra piece. First, the square is folded in half on both sides, unfold and make similar folds along the diagonal.

Fold lines should be clearly visible on the expanded sheet. This will make an origami bird out of paper easy and fast. Fold the sheet in half and rotate the resulting triangle with the vertex (right angle) towards you. The corners that look to the sides, grab the hand and fold inward, holding the opposite side with your hand. If you get a double square, then that means you did everything right.





Bend the free corners of the upper part of the square to the center. As a result, the upper part will look like a ‚Äúkite‚Äù, the bottom layer is still square. Turn the figure over with the back side and repeat the same procedure using the free corners. Carefully iron the fold lines, this will give the figure the desired shape.

Return the figure to the shape of the square again, pull the bottom corner of the paper and carefully bend the sides inward, do the same with the other side.
