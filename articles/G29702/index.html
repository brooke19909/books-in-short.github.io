Do you need a foot for sewing a slanting inlay?

There are many secrets in sewing. The quality and aesthetics of the finished product made of fabric depends on the craftsmanship and special knowledge of the seamstress. One of the most important skills of a tailor is the edging of sections and details. Particularly difficult is the processing of armholes, necks and other rounded elements of products. For this, a special auxiliary material is usually used - a tape cut along the oblique. The work on sewing it is greatly facilitated by the foot for sewing an oblique bejka, we will talk about it in more detail in today's article.





Beyka: types, principle of use

The use of oblique bei is a well-known practice, it is used both by seamstresses working individually and in large enterprises. There are several features of this tape.

Firstly, the fact that it is cut not along the weft thread or the longitudinal fiber of the fabric, but diagonally and at a strictly defined angle of forty-five degrees. If you make a mistake during the pattern, the tape will eventually lie on the product inaccurately, wrinkle and puff on the details. A special foot for sewing an oblique inlay also requires a perfectly tailored braid.

Secondly, the appearance of such a braid is also specific - for processing it is not enough just to cut out a narrow strip of fabric, you need to attach its edges and iron the resulting allowances. Such a measure will help prevent sprinkling of material, and the appearance of the finished product will be neat and elegant.

An oblique inlay can be done on your own by cutting the material at the right angle with strips, and then stitching them from the wrong side to get the edging of the required length. This is the best option if there is no way to pick up the trim in the desired color.





In addition, such a braid is sold ready-made, with folded edges. Most often these are satin ribbons of various shades, but there are also samples of cotton (plain and printed) and knitwear - this appearance looks like an elastic band, but it is not so elastic and has a hem in the middle, thanks to which the fold is perfectly even. Finished tapes produce the desired width, and they lie as evenly as possible when the foot is used for sewing on an oblique inlay.





How to sew an oblique edging

To process a slice using a slanting inlay, in principle, is not difficult, but it is quite a laborious job. The algorithm of actions, if the seamstress does everything not with a foot for sewing on an oblique inlay, which in some machines comes in an additional kit, but with a universal standard presser, consists of the following steps:

  • Expand the rolled-up collar, attach it with the front side to the wrong side of the product and sew in even stitches at a distance of a little less than one centimeter (7-10 mm) from the cut.
  • Unscrew the tape on the face and iron it.
  • Wrap the inline section inward, then fix it by sweeping it to the product.
  • The last step can be done using a sewing machine, then the line needs to be sewn on top of the first seam, or you can hem the hemline manually using a blind seam.

However, to make this work easier, you just need to find a special device. You can learn how to use the foot for sewing on an oblique beiji from the next section.

A beautiful cut for one, two

The use of additional elements and functions that are attached to standard household sewing machines, significantly saves time and effort of craftswomen. So, the inlay foot greatly simplifies the procedure for processing slices, because it makes it possible to avoid basting the tape to the product, and on the typewriter only one line is made.

The principle of using such a foot is very simple. The tape is inserted into a special hole (to make this easier, you need to cut the edge of the tape at an angle), after which the foot is fixed on the machine. If the original part is used, it is fixed in one motion, however, a special adapter may be needed to combine products from different manufacturers. For example, for a Singer or Minerva sewing machine, the Brother foot may not be suitable for sewing on an oblique edging.





After installing the foot, the edge of the tape needs to be pulled out a few centimeters and, having adjusted the position of the needle (it should go 1-2 mm from the edge of the cut), sew a slanting tape several centimeters to fix the thread. A section of the product is inserted into the slot in the foot and wrapped with an inlay, after which it remains just to lay a line.

Manufacturers

All modern manufacturers of sewing machines offer their customers standard machines with a similar set of functions and additional accessories. The most popular among seamstresses are the brands Singer, Minerva and Brother mentioned above, and Janome machines are in demand.

You can also understaff cars by purchasing the missing parts and accessories separately. The foot for sewing the oblique beige Janome is universal, it is suitable for both native models of this brand and other household machines.

Snail foot

Many seamstresses, especially beginners, do not always know what the foot for sewing on an oblique inlay looks like. They come in several forms, most often there is a snail foot. A special cone-shaped device with slots is fixed on its metal base. These gaps have several functions. The first is to facilitate the movement of tissue inside the foot, the second is to adjust the width of the inlay. If the seamstress uses the finished braid, it needs to be inserted into one of the slots, and when you need to cut the inlay yourself, then a strip of fabric is inserted into the upper hole of the foot.





The standard model of the foot is suitable for an inlay 12 mm wide, it may not capture a narrower tape, and a wide one will wrinkle in the device. Specialized stores of sewing accessories also sell other models of snail legs designed for sewing oblique inks of different widths and even thicknesses. Most often, these are accessories for professional or industrial sewing machines.

Foot with scale

This is a universal device that is compatible with all kinds of sewing machines, provided that you use an adapter. The advantage of such a foot is that it can be adjusted for an inlay of any width using a special bending device.

The ideal option is when the seam passes at a distance of 1-2 mm from the cut. The seamstress needs to control the process and make sure that the needle does not slip past the slice wrapped in a slanting inlay. It is better when the tissue does not move easily inside the foot, but with some difficulty.
