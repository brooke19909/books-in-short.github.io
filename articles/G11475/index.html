Caribbean: Major Resorts



Endless beaches, dazzling sun, unique nature - these are the Caribbean islands. From island to island, volcanic landscapes give way to tropical forests, bananas and sugar cane overgrown with coffee plantations. And all this splendor surrounds the gentle, warm year-round sea. The islands of the Caribbean Sea have a stormy and rich history, countries such as Spain, Holland, Great Britain, France influenced their culture.

The region offers tourists a lot of famous and little-known places for relaxation: from the Dominican Republic and Cuba to Grenada and the Turks and Caicos Islands. Incredibly distinctive Caribbean islands. Life on some around the clock is in full swing, while others are well suited for solitude and quiet relaxation. Many resorts have excellent conditions for water sports: surfing, diving, snorkeling. Russians prefer holidays in the Caribbean islands also because many of them are visa-free for Russia.

Antigua

Due to its location, the island is often called the heart of the Caribbean. In addition to relaxing on the delightful beaches, of which there are more than 350, on the island you can admire old buildings with interesting architectural features, visit museums. The tourist infrastructure is notable for its high-level development: entertainment complexes, medium-sized luxury hotels, casinos, night clubs - for tourists there is literally everything.

Grenada

The island is famous not only for its excellent sandy beaches, but also for interesting sights, botanical gardens and museums. Grenada is often called the island of spices.



Turks and Caicos Islands

For those Russians who choose the Caribbean as their vacation spots, this overseas territory of Britain has become known relatively recently. Now the island for tourists from Russia is visa-free, it was loved by a respectable audience for its luxurious hotels and high-level service.

Anguilla

The serene, small, secluded island, which is only 5 kilometers wide and about 26 in length. Those who need to be alone with themselves, feel unity with nature and peace, can go straight here. Thirty-three sandy beaches, washed by the crystal blue waters, are always waiting for guests.



Barbados

Caribbean islands have their own pearl. It is Barbados - an island often referred to as Little England due to its deep-rooted English traditions. Aruba

An island of cacti and sandy beaches bordered by palm trees. Dutch is spoken here, but many locals understand both English and Spanish. Aruba combines both a relaxing holiday and an active one, which is why it is attractive to many tourists.
