Italy, Verona. Antiquity and the Middle Ages

Verona on a map of Italy takes place in its northern part. It just so coincided that most of the existence of this city became known from the famous tragedy of William Shakespeare. But if the legendary Romeo and Juliet would have lived in some other Italian city, Verona would not have lost very much in its cultural and historical significance. There are almost no unremarkable cities on the Apennine Peninsula . So history ordered, this is such a country - Italy. Verona is one of her unconditional pearls. It is one of the most ancient and historically significant cities. He was famous even in the days of the Roman Empire.



Italy, Verona. From antiquity to the present

Verona is unique in the quantity and degree of preservation of ancient architecture that has survived to this day. Moreover, often these ancient monuments continue to serve people here for their intended purpose. A striking example of this is the open-air theater Arena di Verona. This is one of the largest Roman amphitheaters of the first century. The ancient building of pink marble is better preserved than the Roman Colosseum. Leading opera singers all over the world consider it an honor to participate in performances on this stage. There are several transitional places in this city where Ancient Rome ends and modern Italy begins.

Verona is famous in the architectural world, first of all, for its street Porta Borsari. This main city street has largely retained its appearance since ancient times.

For three thousand years the market square of the city has been fulfilling its direct function. Erbe Market is one of the favorite places of communication of the native Veronians; cozy bars with good wine are located on all its sides. This is a very characteristic Italy. Verona in it is far from a province, but one of the undoubted spiritual, historical and intellectual centers.

The highest and most expressive symbol of Verona is the famous Lamberti Tower. This building adorns the city from the twelfth century; it has survived several reconstructions, rebuildings and earthquakes. This is clearly visible even with a cursory glance at the unique structure with bells at the top. By the way, you can climb the bell tower, the view of the city from there opens up more than impressive.

An expressive architectural environment has been formed here over several centuries on the Adige River embankment. From one coast to the other, you can cross the medieval arch bridge of Pietra. He was badly damaged during the war, but was carefully restored.

Even with a cursory acquaintance with the city, you should not miss the basilica of San Zeno Maggiore. This is early gothic. Not so much came to our days of works of Romanesque architecture of the eighth century.
