Phones with two radio modules on Android

Two SIM cards in a modern smartphone are perhaps the standard that even eminent market leaders such as Samsung do not neglect. Even the conservative Apple has similar developments. Often, such devices are confused with phones equipped with two radio modules, in fact, we are not talking about ordinary phones with two SIM cards. Two radio modules are an opportunity to use each of them at a time. Most devices, unfortunately, only allow each SIM card to be used interchangeably while the second is in standby mode.

Technology: DSDS and DSDA

Almost all, without exception, phones currently on the market work with two SIM-cards in DSDS (Dual SIM - Dual Standby) mode, which implies the alternate work of each of them. Both SIM-cards adopted in the gadget use one radio module for communication, which means that making a call using one of them, it will be impossible to receive a call to the other. The same applies to the Internet, talking on the phone using one SIM card, it will be impossible to use mobile Internet on another.

In the case of DSDA (Dual SIM - Dual Active) the situation is exactly the opposite, cell phones with two radio modules are able to interact with each SIM card in parallel, without interrupting each other's work.





Phones with two radio modules: pros and cons

Of course, such devices are not acquired out of idle interest, they have a number of advantages that attract attention:

  • A phone with two active radio modules allows its owner to never lose touch. Where the SIM card of one operator may be powerless, the card of the other will do just fine, therefore the presence of two simultaneously functioning radio modules favorably affects the availability of the user as a subscriber.
  • Another advantage of such gadgets is a more stable operation of the mobile Internet, which does not turn off even if you talk on one of the SIM cards.
  • Naturally, mobility. Two SIM-cards, coupled with two radio modules forever eliminate the need to have and carry two phones.
  • Last on the list, but far from the last by value plus - the ability to combine the favorable conditions of two different operators. There can be a great many schemes, because each operator has its own conditions. In addition, many people love to travel and certainly will not do without an additional SIM card in addition to the main one.

The rainbow picture described above is spoiled by several drawbacks:

  • Firstly, phones with two radio modules require high expenses in production, components come out more expensive, the assembly and optimization process is much more complicated.
  • Secondly, the radio module is a complex device and at the same time the most demanding in the phone (after the display). Both facts lead to inevitable losses in autonomy, forcing them to constantly live near a power outlet or carry a portable battery with them.
  • Thirdly, there are studies claiming that radio waves adversely affect the human body, leading to the development of cancer. The presence of two radio modules in the phone is the same as holding two phones near the head at a time. Many believe that in this way you can literally fry the brain.




Fall offer

Obviously, the number of phones supporting DSDA is incomparably small, compared to phones that do not support this technology. It is believed that such devices were banned in the European Union due to the excessive radiation of such smartphones that do not meet the standards of countries within the union. There is a certain theory of a conspiracy of operators, allegedly they do not want to see a competitor's SIM card in the same phone next door. One way or another, the market cannot boast a rich assortment of DSDA smartphones, but you can still find a couple of diamonds.

External DSDA Modules

This solution applies to Apple devices. Despite the fact that from time to time there are rumors and patent applications on the Internet related to the support of two SIM-cards in the iPhone, today there is none. The Chinese came to the aid of the destitute with their next know-how, bearing the name NeeCoo Me 2.

The device is a small block in which two radio modules and two slots for SIM cards are installed. The gadget communicates with the iPhone via Bluetooth and allows you to make calls from two different SIM cards. You can make calls even if there is no SIM card in the smartphone itself, which means that NeeCoo Me 2 is suitable for all gadgets running iOS, including the iPad.
