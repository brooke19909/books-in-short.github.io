Is it possible to earn real money on Forex?

It is common knowledge that most Forex traders fail. Studies confirm that 96 percent of market participants lose money and ultimately exit the game. In this regard, the question of whether it is possible to make money on Forex is still relevant today. To understand this, you need to understand the reasons why most traders cannot succeed.





The right investment

First of all, such a reason is low starting capital. It's no secret that most currency traders try to make easy money. However, in order to earn significant amounts, you need to make appropriate investments. Speaking about how to start making money on Forex, you need to use a certain starting capital. According to experts, $ 1000 is the optimal amount for the first trading account. Otherwise, you are simply setting yourself up for potential failure.

Self-mastery

Secondly, risk management is also the key to survival. You may be a very experienced trader, but still not enough to control yourself. When answering the question of whether it is possible to make money on Forex, you should remember a simple rule. It consists in the following: your primary task is not to earn more, but not to lose the available funds. Once your capital begins to decline, your ability to make a profit is lost. Therefore, use a stop loss and move it only when you see real prospects.





Market behavior

Thirdly, following the instructions on how to learn how to make money on Forex, you should clearly choose the direction of trade. If your chosen strategy has not been profitable from the first deals, do not rush to switch to other options. Try to fully analyze your market behavior and find possible errors.

In addition, many novice traders try to capture turning points in currency pairs. However, adhering to such trade, you can quietly continue to move in the wrong direction and as a result lose all profits. Therefore, trading strictly according to the trend is also the answer to the question of whether it is possible to earn on Forex.
