Men's crochet scarf: diagram and description

Once there comes a moment when the needlewoman thinks about knitting a men's scarf . Crochet or knitting - it does not matter, the main thing is to be beautiful, stylish and warm.

Immediately there are many difficulties. Because a strong half of humanity is more difficult to please than kids. You need to choose the right color and soft yarn, guess with the size and pattern.

Below are some options for what a crocheted men's scarf might look like. Moreover, even a beginner needlewoman will be able to cope with the task.





Long longitudinal strip

In order to knit such a scarf with a male hook number 3, you will need one skein of yarn in three colors (100 g per 200 meters). In this case, you get a product with a length of about two meters. If you need to increase the length or width, then more threads may be required.

At the beginning of knitting, you need to dial a chain of air loops. The length of the chain should fully correspond to the length that the male scarf (crochet) will have . The scheme of the further pattern consists of double crochet. Colors need to be changed every two rows.

It is recommended to tie the edges of the scarf in two rows:

  1. First row - single crochet.
  2. The second row is the step of the dog.

Insert a fringe of matching colored threads between them.





Thick and warm scarf

To knit, you need to dial loops across the width of the product. A chain of 47 loops will be needed to make a man‚Äôs scarf (crochet) with a width of 22 cm. Moreover, the knitting is very dense. Therefore, a product about 90 cm long will require 200 g of yarn 500 meters long.

For this scheme, the number of loops of the initial chain should be divided by 3.

The first row is made entirely of double crochet and two lifting loops.

The second row begins with two lifting loops. Then, three relief columns alternate, first in front of the product, and then behind it. The last element is a single crochet.

Third row: after two lifting loops, knit according to the pattern. That is, if in the previous row a column is visible that looks like it was made behind the product, then such a knit. Finish with a single crochet.

Fourth row: 2 lifting loops, triples of embossed columns fit so as to change the pattern in a checkerboard pattern. That is, where the column before the work is visible, it is necessary to do the same after it. At the end of the row is a single crochet.

The fifth row follows the pattern. In the sixth row, the alternation of relief columns alternates again. The seventh row fits in the pattern. Continue knitting from the 2nd to the 7th row along the entire length of the men's scarf.





Simple and stylish

The scheme by which this men's scarf is crocheted is not difficult for beginner needlewomen. And experienced craftswomen may seem incredibly simple. In addition, you can knit both in width and in length. In both cases, the product will look beautiful.

You can add effect and make it stylish if you knit in rows immediately along the entire length of the scarf. And also make each row a different shade. But it is desirable that there should be no more than three.

The number of air loops in the initial chain must be a multiple of four. Each odd row begins with three lifting loops, and an even row with one.

In odd rows, three double crochet columns alternate with an air loop over the missing loop. The last element in the row is another double crochet.

The even rows consist of three air loops above the columns, which alternate with the connecting ones from the air loop of the previous row.

With imitation of elastic

It is suitable for those who love classics. A scarf with longitudinal stripes repeating the pattern of gum obtained by knitting will appeal to every man.
It must be knitted from the narrow side and gradually increase the length.

Getting started: a set of chains of an even number of loops.

The first row is formed from the same double crochet. They require three lifting loops, as for all subsequent rows.

In the second row, the first you need to connect the embossed column with a crochet before work. Then two simple double crochets. Continue alternating these two-in-two columns until the end of the row.

Third row: knit simple crochet columns where they were the same in the previous row. On-site reliefs will be the same, but they need to be knitted before work.

The second and third rows continue along the pattern to the end of the scarf. It can also be trimmed with fringe to create a complete look.
