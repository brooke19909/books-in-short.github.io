Moscow-Murom. Journey to the ancient holy city of Murom

Traveling across immense Russia is very exciting. This article is devoted not only to the Moscow-Murom route, but also to some interesting information. How to get to Murom on your own by car or public transport will be described below. It is recommended before the trip to always clarify all questions, nuances, doubts, since the road will be long.

Sightseeing bus ride

Of course, the best option for visiting Murom is precisely an excursion or a pilgrimage trip by bus, where the guide along the route will tell not only the history of the city, but also show interesting places. And there will also be two or three stops to rest. The Moscow-Murom route is quite long. If you go on a sightseeing bus without traffic jams, it will take about 6 hours. It is recommended to leave Moscow no later than 7 a.m. in order to arrive in Murom at least by 13.00.



What will the trip along the Moscow-Murom route look like? The distance between cities is about 300 km.

How to get there by car?

And again, let's talk about the Moscow-Murom route. How to get by car? You need to leave Moscow along the Enthusiasts Highway. The easiest way will be to those who live in the center, on Aviamotornaya, in Novogireevo. Residents of the eastern and south-eastern districts do not need to go to the Moscow Ring Road. Highway Enthusiasts outside Moscow quietly goes into the Gorky highway (highway M7 "Volga"). There is a direct road to the exit to Vladimir. We drive through Reutov, Noginsk, and Pokrov. The city of Murom is located in the Vladimir region. Approaching the regional center and the famous ancient city of Vladimir, you need to prepare for the fact that you need to stay to the right. The M7 highway ends for us at the next roundabout in the Baigushi and Vyatkino districts. Again go to the right. Everywhere there are signs for Murom. You need to follow them. The road to the city itself is direct.
