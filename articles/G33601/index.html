Gelendzhik: what to see? What to see in the vicinity of Gelendzhik

The city, the memories of which cause warm feelings among many of our compatriots, is located at the Markotkh Range. It is located on the shores of the Black Sea, in the Gelendzhik Bay.



From the history of the resort

People inhabited the lands on which Gelendzhik is located a very long time ago. Already in the 3rd millennium BC, ancient tribes settled here. Over a long history, the peoples inhabiting this area have changed - there was a Greek colony, later - Byzantine cities.

In the 15th century, the territory became part of the Ottoman Empire. According to the Andrianopol Peace Treaty in 1829, the Black Sea coast became part of the Russian Empire.

Climate

This is a rather rare union of the marine climate with the mountain. There are many sunny days in Gelendzhik, and, which is especially pleasant, most of them fall in the summer season. Autumn cooling begins only in mid-October. Often, the velvet season lasts until mid-January. At the end of January and February there are minor frosts. But much more often, winter in these places is the rainy season. The peculiarity of the weather in winter is the cold northeast wind.

In summer, Gelendzhik is dry and hot, humidity is low, coastal winds refresh the air well, so the heat is easily tolerated even by children.

What to see?

What can I see in Gelendzhik? It is a very beautiful and green city. There are many magnificent natural monuments that must be seen by both adults and children. We will tell you about some of them in this article.



Promenade

When people ask, going to Gelendzhik: "What to see in this city?" - the first answer that comes to mind: “The embankment!” Of course, this is the main attraction of the city, the subject of pride of its inhabitants. This is a truly unique architectural structure. The embankment stretches for 12 kilometers and is considered the longest in Europe.

Pine trees, the sea, luxurious flower beds - on the promenade you can feel completely happy, here the soul rests. Conventionally, it can be divided into three parts: northern, central and southern. In the northern part there are magnificent flower beds, which you can admire endlessly.

In the central part is always crowded. This is not surprising - many cafes, restaurants, interesting monuments and fountains are concentrated here. Magnificent pines and deciduous trees save from heat.

Gelendzhik: dolmens

Experts and ordinary tourists have been talking about this city landmark for many decades. Gelendzhik dolmens, whose age is about five thousand years, are still a mystery. There is not a single plausible version that would explain their origin. There are many assumptions, among which there is even a version that this is the creation of aliens.

This is one of the attractions for which it is worth coming to Gelendzhik. What to see in this southern city - everyone decides for himself, but dolmens deserve to be seen by as many people as possible.



Many researchers note the special energy of these places. They are sure that dolmens are built as shrines, objects of worship of believers. The residents of Gelendzhik assure that many tourists come to these places in order to get a charge of positive energy.

Safari Park

Every year, thousands of tourists come to rest in Gelendzhik. What to see, where to go in this city? We suggest you go to the famous Safari Park. It covers an area of ​​160 hectares.

The territory of the reserve is covered with deciduous and coniferous forests. In its southern part is a zoo. Animals live here in spacious enclosures, so they are quite comfortable. There are more than fifty species. Guests can admire and explore the habits of tigers and lions, Himalayan and brown bears, lynxes, raccoons and other animals.

This park can be called a rehabilitation center for animals - some of them are brought by hunters, someone gets here after their owners refuse them. Many animals fall in very serious condition. We must pay tribute to the specialists of Safari Park - people who are not indifferent and very loving animals who do everything to cure their most hopeless "patients".

Come to Gelendzhik. "What to see with the children?" - a question that concerns almost all parents. He will not stand in front of you. Young tourists will be happy to mingle and watch the four-legged inhabitants of the park. It is very beautiful here: on the one hand - mountains covered with forests, on the other - a bewitching magnificent panorama of the Black Sea.





Hippo Water Park"

Thousands of tourists annually visit the popular resort town of Geledzhik in our country. “What to see here?” - a question that faces all vacationers. If you came to Gelendzhik with children, then you should definitely visit the magnificent water park "Hippo". Its main feature is that it receives its guests every day without a break and days off.

The water park has three well-equipped swimming pools, an exclusive playground, seventeen of the most varied slides, two bars, two cafes, secured parking for five hundred cars, a first-aid post.

It should be noted that this is one of the few water parks on the Black Sea coast, where a non-cash payment system operates.

In addition to Behemoth, there are water parks Dolphin and Golden Bay in Gelendzhik.

Blue Bay (Gelendzhik)

So called the small village, which is located thirteen kilometers from the city center. Nearby is a sanatorium of the same name. In the bay is the Institute of Oceanology of the Russian Academy of Sciences. P.P. Shirshova. From here ships go on scientific expeditions to different parts of the world's oceans. It is believed that the bay has the cleanest sea water in Gelendzhik and a healing climate. And this is not surprising - the pine forests surrounding this area infiltrate the air with valuable tarry fumes. Rocky shores, picturesque sunny glades - all this beauty is as if painted by a skilled painter.



Excursion motor ships come here regularly, bringing everyone who wants to visit the perfect beach. In the bay, 260 days a year are sunny.

What to see in the vicinity of Gelendzhik

It is a very bright and friendly city. All coming here are always captivated by the hospitality of local residents. I would like to advise tourists who are coming to Gelendzhik for the first time that it is necessary to see the natural monuments that are unique in this region.

Rock "Sail" and other natural monuments

The rock is located near the village of Paraskeevka, 17 km from Gelendzhik. It is a flat stone stratum 25 meters high and more than twenty meters wide. Moreover, its width is only one meter. The shape of a rock resembles a sail.

Another natural monument located in the vicinity of Gelendzhik is a huge territory (950 ha) planted with Pitsunda pine. This tree is under threat of destruction and is listed in the Red Book of the Russian Federation.

There are many waterfalls in the vicinity of Gelendzhik. Tourists are very fond of excursions to the Janet River. Here is a whole cascade of waterfalls of different sizes. For those who wish to visit such an excursion, a special walking route has been developed.

At the Mikhailovsky Pass you can see and take pictures on the background of the Plesetsk waterfalls. The highest of them is more than 17 meters.
