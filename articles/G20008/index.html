Annuity Payments

The most popular in the practice of lending are annuity payments. Their amount is the same for the duration of the loan agreement. Such a payment consists of two parts: funds that go to pay directly the loan, and interest accrued on the amount of debt. Until the very end of payments, the amount remains unchanged, but initially interest prevailed in it, and by the end - the main debt. And how to calculate loan payments of this type? There is a special formula: P = C * (% * (1 +%) x n ) / ((1 +%) x ( n -1).

In this formula, P is the amount of payment, C is the amount of the loan. Accordingly,% - reflects the interest rate, and n is the accrual period in months. In order to correctly calculate the loan payment schedule, the annual rate can be converted into a monthly rate, and then expressed in decimal. It turns out a monthly payment.



Up to a day

But when drawing up a loan agreement, the schedule is drawn up on certain days, and therefore the annual rate is converted to the daily rate, that is, divided by the number of days in a year. In this case, n increases. When calculating the monthly and daily rates, the difference is small, only a couple of tens of rubles, but it can also carry weight. If you own the construction of tables, you can build an analogue of a bank payment up to a day.

Let us return to the question of how to calculate the amount of payment on a loan. The total amount of debt should be divided by the number of months of planned lending. The received number will be the main payment. The calculation formula is simple - B = S: N , where B is the main payment, S is the loan amount, and N is the number of months. But this is not all, because you also need to learn how to calculate the monthly loan payment, taking into account interest. Here one more value is required - p is the annual interest rate. For the final calculation, you need to multiply the loan amount by the annual rate and divide the result by 12 months.
