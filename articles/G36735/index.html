Texas Hold'em: Combinations

As in any other poker, here the winner is determined by certain combinations of cards. Before you start playing Texas Hold'em, combinations must be learned first. The order of their precedence determines whether you won in a particular hand or not. In fact, these are the main rules of Texas Hold'em.

There are 10 combinations in total, where the kicker is the weakest and the royal flush is the strongest and invincible combination. The first three of them are very rare and almost invincible. Combinations with 3-7 are considered strong, but even the formidable "full house" can be defeated in the game. A pair and a deuce often bring victory, but require care when playing.

  1. "Royal Flush" - "straight flush" from 10s to ace.
  2. "Street Flush" - five cards of the same suit in order.
  3. Four of a kind - four cards of the same value.
  4. "Fullhouse" - a pair and a set in one combination (five cards in total).
  5. "Flash" - five cards of the same suit, regardless of dignity.
  6. "Street" - five cards in order, regardless of suit.
  7. "Set" (or three) - three cards of the same value.
  8. Two pairs - a combination of two pairs of the same value.
  9. A pair is two cards of the same value.
  10. Kicker is the highest card.




Thus, when playing Texas Hold'em, combinations are what determine the superiority or weakness of one player over another in a particular hand.

Beginning of the game

The current Texas Hold'em rules clearly spell out all the stages, from the distribution of cards to the determination of the winner. The game involves from 2 to 10 people. Cards are dealt either by the dealer (in casinos and gambling clubs), or by the players themselves in turn.

The cards in the game are dealt by the player making the last bet. That is, the last one at the table in the current distribution, to determine the position of this player there is a special token - the "button", indicating the last player in the distribution. With each new game he goes to the next player. Clockwise.





The next two players clockwise place the required bets - the small and the big blind. They correspond to the size of the minimum bet at the table. After the cards are dealt, the first bet is made by the player to the left of the big blind.

Circle One

The Texas Hold'em game is a poker game played with two cards. After the cards are dealt, bidding begins, during which each participant evaluates the strength of his hand. Depending on the pleasant decision, the player who divides the auction can choose one of four actions:

Each player, in order of priority, does one of four actions until the bets are aligned or all players fold to the pass, losing to the most aggressive participant.

Texas Hold'em basic rules say that the right decision at this point is crucial for the entire hand, especially for beginners.

Limit and unlimited poker

Unlimited poker is the most popular type of game in which the maximum bet is limited only by the number of player chips. In this kind of card game, excitement and cold calculation are best combined, as even luck can turn away from a very strong professional overnight. When playing Texas Unlimited Hold'em, the rules allow you to go all-in at any time in the game, making even the most powerful opponents nervous. A completely different thing is a game with limited bets.

"Limit Hold'em" implies that a player's maximum "raise" does not exceed one or two big blinds. The maximum possible bet for the entire distribution is only 24 times the minimum bet, and then only if opponents will constantly raise bets. "Limit Hold'em" - the game is calm and measured, very often it is chosen in order to start playing on the Internet.

Tournaments in Texas Hold'em

Tournament rules are somewhat different from regular games. The number of participants also starts with two people, but there is no upper limit. So, in the largest event of 2014 - the main tournament of the WSOP world series in Las Vegas, 6,680 people sat down at the tables. And the total prize fund exceeded $ 65 million.

There are two main differences from a regular cash game:

- Rates increase after a certain passage of time. As a result, the number of blinds in the game decreases over time, and with each new stage the intensity of the game increases.

- The second difference is that the tournament participants can finish it only in two cases: having won or lost all the chips. In the cash game you can leave the table at any time.





Tournament experience on the Internet is very important for beginners, as it allows you to get used to the game with a different number of chips. Tournaments are thought to be more athletic than cash games.

Sit-and-Go Tournaments

Poker bluff

Texas Hold'em rules don't say anything about bluffing. However, without this element a successful game is impossible. Bluffing is good when it succeeds, and can be useful when it is opened.

In the first case, it makes a profit, in the second it allows you to deceive the opponent, if after the exposure you change the strategy of the game.

However, in the absence of sufficient experience, it is still worthwhile to outline some rules. Texas Hold'em does not forgive bluffing mistakes.

  • You need to bluff only from late positions, when all the players have already said their word.
  • Never bet against players who have made "raises" or big bets.
  • Do not bluff often. Only if there is a tangible chance of success, and subject to the first two rules.
  • Never bet a lot, the bet should be less than the pot, but more than half (best 2/3).
  • Do not fight for big banks. Chips lost on bluffs are very difficult to return.

There are several effective bluffing strategies, but all of them are based on experience and a fine knowledge of psychology, and therefore you need to enter this part of the game carefully, without making dangerous decisions in the game.
