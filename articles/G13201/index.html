Savannah (USA): tourist information, attractions, photos

Savannah is a US city located in the southeastern region of Georgia. This is the oldest state settlement founded by the British in the first half of the 18th century. This publication contains all the most interesting information about an unusual city called Savannah (USA).

Interesting facts about the city

  • The city of Savannah (USA) was founded by an English expedition led by James Oglethorpe.
  • It is located on the banks of the river of the same name, which flows into the Atlantic Ocean.
  • During the Civil War in the United States, the city was the most important strategic point of the army of the South. At this time, the army of William Sherman entered Savannah, which had previously destroyed and burned all the settlements north of Atlanta. The small, but surprisingly beautiful town of Georgia, charmed the general. William Sherman decided to give Savannah a citizenship to Abraham Lincoln as a Christmas present. Thus, the city was saved from destruction.
  • Savannah (USA) has straight streets and spacious rectangular squares. Thus, James Oglethorpe proposed to equip the city.
  • Today, Savannah is the most important American port and industrial center.
  • The weather in the city is often rainy.
  • Savannah is known in every corner of the world! After all, it was in this city that the main character of the Forrest Gump film told episodes from his life.




How to get there

If you look at where Savannah (USA) is located on the map, you can see that there are such cities as Garden City, Puller, George Thaum near it. The easiest way to get from these places to Savannah is by car.

In addition, the city has an international airport. From here planes run to the largest American centers. Savannah Airport daily receives flights from Atlanta (the capital of Georgia), Dallas (Texas), Chicago, Washington, Philadelphia, etc.

The railway connection is established on the Savannah-New York line.





What to see?

Savannah is a US city that has something to surprise every tourist. This place is of particular interest in terms of history. After all, Savannah is the first settlement in the state of Georgia, its former capital. In addition, many interesting buildings of the XIX century have been preserved here.

The historical part of the town is located between the coast of the Savannah River and a beautiful park called Forsyth. Here you can see the old city market, the reconstructed Elips Square with a beautiful fountain and other equally interesting buildings. Today, only the architecture reminds of the historical past of Savannah. This part of the city is now the main entertainment center. There are many restaurants, galleries, art sites, as well as an underground parking.

In Savannah, you must visit River Street - the main street and the heart of the city. There are many restaurants and souvenir shops here. And at the end of the street is the famous sculpture of a waving girl, with whom travelers like to be photographed.





Savannah Attractions

  • Telfair Museum of Art is a famous landmark of the Savannah. The building was erected in 1819. It is located in the historic city center. In 2006, a new modern building of the art center was opened here, where every tourist can enjoy the greatest works of art. The author of the new project was the famous architect Moshe Safdi.
  • The historic Bonaventure Cemetery is an unusual place that is definitely worth a visit.
  • The Cathedral of John the Baptist is the main Catholic shrine in the city. The most beautiful temple building was erected at the end of the 19th century. Cathedral of St. John the Baptist built in the Gothic style.
  • Mikve Synagogue Israel is another interesting shrine. This building is unique in its kind. It is built in the Gothic style, which differs from many other synagogues.

Savannah (Georgia, USA) is an amazing place where the past is closely intertwined with the modern. This city will impress every traveler with its interesting buildings and beautiful landscapes.





3 things to do in Savannah

  1. Go around all the famous squares of the city. There are 22 in the Savannah. But the most interesting are Chippewa Square, Chatham, Calhoun, Columbia.
  2. Take a picture with a sculpture of a waving girl on River Street.
  3. Go on a haunted night tour.
