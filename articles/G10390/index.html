What are rear view cameras in the license plate frame for?

The rear view cameras in the license plate frame are designed to transmit images of the space behind the car to a monitor located in the passenger compartment. The presence of such an option makes it easier for the driver to park anywhere. Rear-view cameras in the frame of the license plate is a very reliable equipment that ensures the safety of a car moving in reverse. In the evening, such a device is simply indispensable, because it allows the driver to better view the space.

How to install a camcorder?

Installation of the camera is carried out on the shelf of the car trunk or on the luggage compartment lid. The signal from the device is fed to the monitor via a four-wire cable, two of which must be connected to the reverse lamp, and two should be pulled through the entire cabin to the monitor. Wires can be laid from the bottom of the body, for this you need to disassemble the plastic sills, or under the skin of the ceiling, for this you need to remove the rubber seals and lay the cable.
