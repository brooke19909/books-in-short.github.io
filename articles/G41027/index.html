The image of St. George the Victorious. Coin of modern coinage.

Quite often, the state uses the images of famous personalities to draw attention to an object or idea. This fully applies to the image of St. George the Victorious. The coin with him is known not only to experienced numismatists, but also to amateur collectors.

Gold reserve of the country

The state constantly uses the investments of the population by putting up for sale coins of different denominations made of precious metals. In order to interest the buyer, they display images of various celebrities or famous architectural monuments. At the beginning of the two thousandth, a sculpture of St. George the Victorious was used for these purposes . The coin appeared in different designs. For all the time it was minted both on gold and silver blanks.





In the winter of 2006, the first batch of money with the image on the reverse of St. George the Victorious entered the free market. The coin came out in a circulation of one hundred and fifty thousand pieces. After that, a decision was made to temporarily suspend production and resume it if necessary. With this amount, the government hoped to replenish the gold reserves that the famous Chervonets had provided to it so far. Such a marketing move was quite successful and a little later, Russian citizens were able to see already silver George the Victorious. The coin appeared for the first time in 2009 and quickly went hand in hand.

Execution options

Over the past ten years, the golden "George the Victorious" was minted in two different versions. These were coins in denominations of 50 and 100 rubles. In the second case, the weight of each specimen was accordingly doubled. Each hundred-ruble coin contained 15.55 grams of pure gold. Naturally, its price amounted to 50.5 thousand rubles.





Externally, coins are practically no different from each other. On the reverse is an image of a sculpture by the artist Baklanov, where St. George, sitting astride a horse, strikes with an spear an evil serpent. On the obverse, the coke is usually in the center depicts a double-headed eagle with the inscription under the wings of "BANK OF RUSSIA", enclosed in a ring of dots. At the top is the value of the face value, at the bottom is the year of manufacture, on the left is the name of the valuable metal indicating its exact test, and on the right is the amount of this metal in grams. This money in different amounts was issued by both the Moscow and St. Petersburg mints. Moreover, the trademark of each of them was indicated on the right side of the obverse.
