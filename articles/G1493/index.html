Main characteristics

The processor of the Core i3 model with an index of 2130 underlies the considered mobile device Sony Vaio PCG-71211V. The characteristics of this chip indicate that it belongs to the Intel Sandy Bridge CPU generation. It is equipped with two real computing modules and four logical cores. Its clock frequency is 3.4 GHz. This processor device was produced using 32 nm technology.

Of course, at the moment, such technology is no longer used in new chips, but in 2011, at the start of sales of the laptop in question, this manufacturing process was really relevant. The amount of RAM in the initial configuration is 4 GB, and its type is DDR3 with a nominal frequency of 1066 MHz. The memory controller is part of the motherboard chipset and is capable of addressing up to 32 GB. Again, the number of RAM slots is 2, and this means that as part of this mobile PC, a maximum of up to 16 GB can be set (2 modules of 8 GB each).

This computer is also equipped with a discrete graphics system based on Radeon HD5470 from AMD company with a nominal video buffer capacity of 512 MB. The diagonal of the display is 15.5 inches, and its resolution is HD (1366x768). The type of matrix TN + Film, which underlies the liquid crystal display, and one should not expect phenomenally high quality of the displayed image in this case. The presence of a drive of 320 GB can boast a laptop Sony Vaio PCG-71211V. The characteristics of this component indicate the presence of 8 MB of cache and a rotation speed of 5400 rpm. The software environment in this case is Windows 7 in the Home Edition.
