Let's answer the question, who invented the phone?

Progress is constantly gaining momentum and every year new inventions and discoveries appear faster and faster. Many new devices are entering our life that yesterday seemed fantastic, and familiar household appliances are being replaced with newer and improved ones. Just ten years ago, in every apartment there was a regular color TV with a remote control, which was considered the ultimate dream, now we are witnessing the appearance of flat plasma panels with 3D technology support. Laptops, tablet computers and, of course, phones are so firmly entrenched in our lives that it would be very difficult to imagine it without them.

The standard of our life allows us to acquire numerous household appliances that are now filling our house, but keeping up with the change of generations is becoming more difficult, first of all, because an ordinary person does not know anything about new products.

In the frenzied rhythm of life, it is sometimes useful to stop and look back, to turn your eyes to history, and to remember who stood at the origins. Let's answer the question, who invented the phone? Without a doubt, this is one of the most popular devices among those around us. A man without him now feels literally helpless. But not everyone knows who invented the phone, who first conveyed a few words from a distance.

But who invented the phone in the form that is used now?

Electrical appliance

In 1860, the Italian Antonio Meucci demonstrated a device that can transmit various sounds and speech over wires. He even received a patent for his invention. About a year later, a German named Johann Reis introduced something similar, but equipped with a microphone and speaker. And in 1876, Alexander Bell showed the world a device called the "talking telegraph", combining the ideas of previous designs. He is considered the inventor who contributed, but he is not the one who invented the first phone.

In 1878, a Russian scientist and electrical engineer created an apparatus of an original design that was different from Bell's invention. His device is considered the first Russian phone. In the following years, many scientists worked to improve it, including Thomas Edison.

But answering the question of who invented the mobile phone is somewhat more difficult, because it has become a natural continuation of walkie-talkies and other similar devices.
