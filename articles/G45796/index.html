How to transport the old unit?



Preparation of the old refrigerator for transportation is carried out according to the same rules: disconnect, defrost, free from moving parts, fasten the door. Soviet cooling equipment has one feature: the refrigerator manual warns that they cannot be turned over on their back or on their side. In addition, before moving this miracle of technology, it is necessary to firmly fix the motor-compressor with special bolts. The most reliable way to transport such an aggregate is a vertical position. Soviet technology is a reliable assistant in the household, but it should be handled accordingly. How to transport a refrigerator manufactured back in Soviet times? You just need to follow the instructions and advice of experienced and experienced carriers.

How to transport a refrigerator in the winter?

In addition to the usual rules for preparing and packing the case, the tips on how to transport the refrigerator say that you need to wait time before turning on the unit. A few hours (from 2 to 3) will be enough. Packaging is not removed immediately, but after two hours in the room. During this time, the temperature of all units of the unit will be equal to the room atmosphere. Acclimatization will occur and condensation will not damage internal contacts. This rule applies to any household appliances. If this advice is neglected, an electrical circuit may occur, the appliance and all appliances in the house will seriously suffer. Transportation of the refrigerator is a matter requiring responsibility in both winter and summer.

Is it possible to transport refrigerators in a lying position?



It is not always convenient to put this large item in the car body. Can I transport the refrigerator while lying down? If an old Soviet unit was driven on its side, then you need to give time before switching it on so that the working fluids are correctly distributed inside the system and the condensate dries on the contacts (in winter).

Both old and new refrigerators can be carried in a lying position. To maintain the operability of the equipment, it is necessary to lay the unit on one of the sides and make sure that the door hinges are on top. Otherwise, the door will tear off or the refrigerator itself will break.

After transportation, the appliance should be returned to its upright position and wait several hours before being plugged in. All liquids will return to working condition - and the savior of products will be ready for work again.
