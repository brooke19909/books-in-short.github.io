A bit of history

The first references in historical documents about this city date back to 1032. Already at that time it was quite large and famous, as it was on a significant trade route. According to one version, the settlement was founded by the peoples of Scandinavia. Famous historians argue about the origin of the name of the city today. There are two versions. If you believe the first, then the settlement was named after the river ("Chickens") on which it stands. Opponents of this theory say that the city was designated in honor of the partridges living in its vicinity. These birds are also depicted on the modern coat of arms of the city. In 1095 the Principality of Kursk was founded. In the sixteenth century, a reliable fortress was erected here, and for two centuries the city was an important military-strategic object for the entire Kievan Rus. But already in the eighteenth century, the Tatar-Mongol forces heavily sacked and destroyed this settlement. However, it was restored and played a significant role in the Great Patriotic War. Many monuments of Kursk are dedicated specifically to the battle of Kursk, which significantly influenced the course of the war. Today, the village proudly bears the title of a hero city. This settlement is also included in the list of the cleanest and most well-groomed cities in Russia.

Remember the war ...



One of the symbols of Kursk is the Kursk Bulge Memorial . The open-air museum stretched almost a kilometer along Victory Boulevard. It is impossible not to notice the complex. It includes the Arc de Triomphe, the monument to Zhukov, the Church of St. George the Victorious. Here you can see a sculpture dedicated to the victims of executions, the figure of an unknown soldier and a sign of the city of military glory. There is also its own alley of military equipment. The sights of Kursk associated with the Second World War do not end there. The local House of Officers houses the Museum of the Battle of Kursk. Here you can learn more about how the battle went, see the rewards and personal belongings of the fighters.

Cultural life

The city of Kursk is famous not only as a place of great battles, but also as a modern cultural center. A must-see object is an art gallery named after A. Deineki. Today you can see about 8.5 thousand exhibits. The collection presents outstanding examples of European sculpture, painting and graphics. You can see here objects of folk art. You can get acquainted in Kursk with theater art. For adults, there is the Pushkin Drama Theater . For children and adolescents - the theater of a young spectator or puppet. The city has four large parks. On holidays, folk festivals and various entertainment programs are held here.

Kursk is religious

Many places of interest in Kursk, photos of which you can see, are visible from an impressive distance. We are, of course, talking about the majestic temples, of which there are 33 in this city today. One of the most original buildings in this category is the Catholic Church. It is located on Marat Street, has been operating since 1892. Not everyone knows that once, not yet being world famous, Kazimir Malevich lived in this city. The artist liked to visit this church, and also restored the icons in it. The oldest Orthodox church is located in the Trinity Monastery. The temple was built in 1695, consecrated in honor of the Trinity Life-Giving. From an architectural point of view, of interest are: the Church of the Holy Royal Martyrs, the Temple of the Great Martyr Panteleimon and the Temple of the Archangel Michael.

In its decoration, it is not inferior to the churches of Moscow, it operates today, it is easy to get into the service here. The Znamensky Monastery has been preserved in good condition to this day . Speaking about the sights of Kursk, one cannot ignore the Church of the Resurrection of Christ. This is a relatively small red brick building with rich interior decoration.

What else to see in Kursk?





In the vicinity of the city there are several manor complexes. The most famous among them: Mokva, Feta and Maryino.
