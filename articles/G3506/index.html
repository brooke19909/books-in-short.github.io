LCD "Amber", Balashikha: description, address, developer, reviews

LCD "Amber" in Balashikha - a large-scale residential complex located in the suburbs. The sufficiently high quality of construction and affordable prices make the apartments in these new buildings attractive for many who decide to invest in real estate. In this article, we will talk in detail about this microdistrict, its developer and provide feedback from residents.

About the complex

LCD "Amber" in Balashikha - this is 20 economy class houses built from a gas-silicate block. The technology that was applied during the work is monolithic frame. The houses are insulated with mineral wool.

It is noteworthy that the new buildings in the Yantarny residential complex in Balashikha have different floors - from seven to 25 floors. The developer provides ground guest parking and underground parking for local residents.

Although the apartments are rented without finishing, they are able to attract many with additional space, since the ceiling height in them is three meters.

Readiness

The construction of the Yantarny residential complex in Balashikha was carried out in two stages. In the first, it was planned to put into operation 19 buildings, in the second - another eleven.

The deadlines for the delivery of the Yantarny residential complex in Balashikha for the last houses were set in 2017. In its second quarter they were commissioned. Currently, the remaining unsold apartments are being sold. They are available from the developer, and there are also resale options on the market from individuals or real estate agencies.

A separate emphasis should be placed on the transport accessibility of the quarter. The residential complex was built in the Shchitnikovo microdistrict on the territory of the city of Balashikha. Developer

It is worth noting that in the residential complex is a difficult situation with the developer. In the Yantarny residential complex in Balashikha, the construction company Morton initially carried out all the work. This is a group of companies created in 1994 by Alexander Ruchevov. Initially, the company was engaged in the overhaul of houses in the city of Klin, Moscow Region. Then she received several contracts for the decoration of the facades of houses. Over time, the company's interests expanded significantly. Morton began to specialize in large-scale projects, to comprehensively develop territories. In addition to housing, infrastructure facilities were erected everywhere. In particular, projects were implemented in Putilkovo, Butovo.

By 2013, the company managed to commission more than one million square meters of housing. In 2016, all structures and assets of Morton were acquired by the PIK group of companies, which integrated it into its structure. As a result of the merger, the Morton brand was decided not to be retained. On its basis, a new company was created under the name "Basis". She began to specialize in smart home development. Also engaged in investing in high-tech projects, managing commercial real estate. Perhaps the brand was decided to change, since Morton has recently developed a negative reputation. The company was accused of the fact that during the construction of each object, it creates a new legal entity, so that in case of failure to meet the deadlines for the delivery of houses, and this arose regularly, hide behind a small company.

At the same time, PIK took over the completion of work on all new buildings. This is one of the largest development companies in the country, which implements projects not only in the capital and the Moscow Region, but also in other regions of the Russian Federation. It has been present on the market since 1994, specializing in the construction of houses in the affordable housing segment. At the moment, builders have already commissioned almost 20 million square meters.

PIK's new buildings can be found in ten Russian regions; the company's investment portfolio is quite large and diverse. It is noteworthy that since 2015 the company entered the market with fundamentally new products. In particular, she began to implement plans for monolithic and industrial housing construction, fundamentally changing the approach to the formation of domestic housing among most areas of complex development.
