Gili Islands (Indonesia): description, photos, reviews of tourists. How to get from Bali?

Gili Islands (Indonesia) are familiar to all beach lovers in Bali. Three tiny pieces of land, surrounded on all sides by the vast expanse of the Indian Ocean, look very attractive from a bird's eye view. Well, having met with Trawangan, Meno and Air, I want to stay here forever. Many call Bali a paradise island. Yes, in many ways he is good and even wonderful. Bali is rich in historical and natural attractions. But if you are interested in the beaches and the clear turquoise sea, then it is better not to find the Gili Islands that are part of the Western Small Sunda Archipelago. Bounty embodied advertisement

Our ideas of "heavenly enjoyment" are often based on the video seen on the chocolate bar. Warm white sand, as fine as icing sugar; calm gentle sea, purity and color resembling turquoise, and on the horizon - sapphire; slender palm trees leaning toward the water itself ... Curious tourists who do not imagine holidays without informative excursions, Gili will not suit. But for those who crave passive relaxation on the beach, they are just right. Active divers and snorkelling enthusiasts will also not be disappointed.



The specifics of the Gili Islands

Trawangan is the largest of the trinity. The islands of Gili Air and Meno are much smaller. Accordingly, if you want to alternate beach vacations during the day with fun in the evening, you are on Trawangan. You cannot call this island big, except in comparison with two neighboring tiny pieces of land. But on Trawangan there is a sufficient selection of hotels for every taste and fullness of a credit card, cafes, restaurants and even night clubs. There is even a food market. And if you want to renounce civilization and feel like some sort of Robinson Crusoe on the edge of the earth, then you can go to the islands of Gili Meno and Air. Fortunately, they are only a kilometer from Trawangan. From this island you can see the mountainous Lombok. And from the opposite shore you can admire Bali. Rather, the island itself is hidden behind the horizon, but the towering peak of the Batur volcano is visible in the sky. There are few hotels on Air, but on Meno there are even fewer hotels, so you need to book a hotel there in advance.

Gili (islands): how to get there. The fastest and most expensive option

In fact, Gili is not very far from the island of Lombok. And he, respectively, is located very close to Bali. You can get to the "Bounty Islands" only by sea. But there are options. The ticket price directly affects the time spent on the journey. The more you pay, the faster and with better comfort you will reach the island of Gili you need. The most expensive option is a private transfer. You will be taken from Bali, from the point that you indicate, taken by car to the Padang Bay pier, boarded a high-speed glider and delivered to the desired island. The issue price is one hundred or more US dollars per passenger. But you will be at the place an hour or eighty minutes after leaving the hotel in Bali.

Cheapest option

There are also public ferries to the Gili Islands. "Bali - Lombok" leaves from the same pier Padang Bay. This ferry runs regularly, but slowly. Please note that public transport for local does not provide air conditioning and any amenities (except for the toilet). In Lombok, you should transfer to another ferry, to Gili Trawangan. Boats on smaller islands - Meno and Air - go very rarely. They are ordinary wooden motorboats with additional boards on the sides for stability. But this whole day trip will leave a lot of impressions. A plus of this option is the opportunity to see Lombok. Many tourists traveling in Indonesia are even advised to stay on this island for a night or two. Another advantage is the low price. Two tickets on the Bali-Lombok-Gili public ferry will cost one passenger thirty thousand rupees (or twenty US dollars).



How to get around

To keep the Gili Islands environmentally friendly, local authorities banned all kinds of motorized modes of transport. So if you travel with a scooter, you will have to leave it in a paid parking lot in Bali or Lombok. You definitely will not be put on a ferry or boat to Gili with him. But don't be discouraged. Even the largest of the Gili islands, Trawangan, is two and a half kilometers long and one and a half kilometers wide. You can get around it around the perimeter in an hour with a little. In a good hotel you can always rent bicycles, since the flat terrain of the islands does not turn pedaling into hard labor. Another type of local transport is tuk-tuks pulled by ponies. Rather, this is another attraction for tourists than a way to travel. Moreover, tuk-tuks are quite an expensive pleasure. A trip in a carriage will cost eighty thousand rupees. Boats are the most popular form of transport in Gili. You can ride on a regular wooden junk. For those who are panicky afraid to dive, there are boats with a glass bottom. And between the three islands of Gili from morning to evening scurry carriers.
