IN AND. Lenin

In almost every city of the USSR, a monument to Vladimir Lenin was erected. Sochi, although it is not the cradle of the revolution, the leader of the proletariat proudly shows the current generation a direction towards a brighter future. One can have a different attitude to the events of 1917, but it was ideological revolutionaries who advocated universal education and industrialization of the once technologically backward agrarian country.

We must pay tribute to the authors of the monument to Vladimir Lenin in Sochi - the sculptor Z. Vilensky and the architect L. Rudnev. The founder of the first socialist state does not look pompous and does not belittle the monumental size. Before us on a pedestal of red marble appears the thoughtful Vladimir Ilyich, who is probably arguing about serious things.

The bronze sculpture is located in the Central District near the promenade near the Sochi Hotel. Established on the occasion of the 40th anniversary of the Revolution back in 1957. It is gratifying that the townspeople do not demolish the monuments and honor all the stages of their history.

"Anchor and gun"

The monument is designed to perpetuate the memory of the foundation in 1838 of the city of a new outpost of the Russian Empire on the Black Sea. This is one of the oldest monuments in Sochi, the installation of which took place in a festive atmosphere on 04/23/1913.

The composition is a stylized gun mounted on a concrete base, in front of which a ship anchor is placed. Thus, the descendants are left with a reminder at what a difficult price the region was recaptured from Turkey in 1829.

Yuri Gagarin

The monument to Yuri Gagarin in Sochi is located on the street, also named after the first cosmonaut, in the Central District. Unlike Vladimir Lenin and Peter I, the legendary astronaut visited the city.

Yuri Alekseevich and his family came to rest in Sochi 25 days after the space flight. He actively spent time, met with residents, participated in events. In the park (now named after Gagarin), he personally planted the Himalayan cedar. By the way, the tree has taken root and today reaches the height of a five-story building. Under the canopy of his branches, a bust of Yu.A. Gagarin.
