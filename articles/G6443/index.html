Water lily origami. Various folding techniques

It is always interesting to learn how to add a new origami model. Making a water lily is the best way to develop shape-folding skills. By the way, the water lily model is very beautiful and popular.

DIY Origami Water Lilies

Every lover can think of how to improve their favorite model, or make crafts in new unexpected colors. But there are models that do not need improvement. Another bonus. Origami water lily - a flower that does not fade and does not fade.

How to make such a flower with your own hands? You need to choose the model that seems the most elegant, and learn how to perform all the specified actions according to the scheme.

Water lily made of paper. Most popular model

In this article, we will show how to assemble the most attractive model of a paper water lily. This figure is quite simple in execution. It is necessary to cut identical rectangles from sheets of paper of two colors. For the first row, 8 green ones are used. For 2 and 3 rows - 16 white. Parts are stacked in a specific order. Then 2 or 3 white details are embedded in 1 green. All the blanks are folded parallel to each other and tied in the middle with a thread.

So the foundation is done. From the center, these petals are distributed in equal intervals until a circle is obtained. Then the edges are alternately tucked up, creating flower petals. First, the upper elements, then go to the bottom.





In order to find a suitable paper color for a water lily, it is better to rely on your perception. Above is a classic white flower with green leaves. In the last row, a closing element is added - a yellow core of 2 small elements. After all, sometimes you want to experiment.

Water lily made of paper. Wet Origami Technique

Some lovers prefer the wet origami technique when making flowers. An origami paper water lily made in this way is very similar to a real flower. But working with such paper is more difficult. Paper is selected at a specific density. During folding, the leaves are smeared with a slightly damp sponge or sprayed. But the paper should not be literally wet. Parts so slightly moistened in this way are assembled according to the scheme, and then gently stacked. When such an origami water lily dries up, all those oval transitions, roundings that they wanted to give it are preserved.





But you need to be extremely careful, because wet paper easily tears. And it develops only in one or 2 layers. The volume is attached to the model due to the overlapping layers of paper, and not multiple bending, as in classical origami.

Modular origami

What is interesting modular origami "water lily"? This model, of course, is one of the most beautiful origami. To build such a voluminous magnificent water lily, you need to prepare quite a lot of modules, of which all such crafts are usually built. Green elements are taken for the base of the flower - they need to be made 318 pieces; 300 elements are made for the flower itself.

You can do as shown in the picture: a red flower with an admixture of yellow colors. But it is better to limit oneself to a monophonic flower, until the amateur learns to fold the scheme evenly and without errors. After all, it is desirable that the scheme "settled" in the head, and then freely create your own drawings.





We carry out a green sheet support. It is made of 6 equal rhombuses fastened together. We start with 1 part, with each row we increase their number to 7. From the eighth row, we reduce the number of elements again to one. We apply the same scheme to each of the 6 lower petals. They are combined at the bases with the help of 3, 4 modules.

We proceed to the assembly of the main flower. In the same way as the lower petals were created, we make the first elements of the flower. We connect two parts to the first module of the Serkh, we attach 3 elements of the third row to them. So to the 5th row, and then again combine into one element in the last row.

We add other modules to the petals in free space according to the same scheme. After each row, the elements are slightly bent into a kind of cocoon, so that the shape looks like a real flower. We finish building until the details are over. Another small flower of a different color is sometimes made into the center.
