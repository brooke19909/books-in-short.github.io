Thailand: interesting facts, country culture, attractions, photos

Thailand is a large, fabulous country that every person needs to visit at least once in their life. There are pristine beaches, discos, historical temples and many attractions. In the article we will consider interesting facts of Thailand for tourists. After all, it is in this country that there is much that is surprising, and few people know about it. It's time to open the veil of secrets.

Interesting facts about food

This is an amazing country in which a lot of interesting things are hidden. So, let's look at 10 interesting facts about food in Thailand:

  1. Almost all locals eat fruit with the addition of sugar and red hot pepper. It is even better to add chocolate to the dish, which will give its zest. Some chefs also add salt. Thais consider this combination ideal.
  2. In Thailand, eating fruit is still unripe. As a rule, they are used in a variety of salads. It is considered the most delicious with the addition of green mango.
  3. A lot of such fruits and vegetables grow in the country: eggplant (50 species), mango (40 species) and ginger - there are about 400 varieties.
  4. Locals love to cook such dishes, which combine 4 different tastes - it is sweet, sour, bitter and salty.
  5. Like other eastern countries, in Thailand, chopsticks are served with a dish. Such a custom came to them immediately after China many years ago.
  6. In cafes and restaurants, conventional appliances are served with chopsticks. These are spoons and forks, but the order of use is completely different than that of Russian citizens. A large piece of meat or vegetable should be cut with a spoon, and with a fork, these pieces must be folded into a spoon.
  7. Local residents believe that eating alone is very bad, because trouble will come. That is why, if there is no company at home, they go for lunch (dinner) in a cafe or restaurant.
  8. Thais believe that food should never be thrown away, this can bring starvation for many years to come.
  9. In Thailand, unlike other countries, deep utensils are most often used. No matter what the dish: first, snack or second.

Want to get to know authentic Asian dishes? Then you need to visit Thailand, which offers many restaurants with a variety of delicious dishes with a special, unknown to you zest.

Interesting facts for children

In Thailand, a lot of positive emotions, not only in adults but also in children. Leisure is for every person. So, consider the main interesting facts for children:

  1. Almost every city in Thailand hosts cooking classes for children. Here, kids will learn to combine a variety of ingredients and cook simple, but original dishes. Such entertainment is suitable for school children.
  2. For preschoolers, Thais came up with an original entertainment - riding elephants in the jungle.
  3. Children will never see such big pandas as in the zoo in Thailand.
  4. For middle-aged children, rock climbing was invented. Of course, there is a safety net. But the children will plunge into an amazing adventure.
  5. In Thailand, there is a monkey mountain where animals live on the very top and, most strange, wait for tourists when they bring them some goodies.
  6. Only in Thailand there are shows with elephants and crocodiles, which is another fun for children and a bright palette of impressions.
  7. Children's Day is celebrated on January 13th.

The country has a large selection of interesting and exciting attractions specifically for children.

Moreover, there are many hotels where a nanny is offered for the period of absence of parents. After all, if the child is very small, he should not be taken with him in the heat on excursions.

About tourism

Each country has its own characteristics. The same can be said about Thailand. Interesting facts about tourism amaze visitors. Consider the main ones:

  • The country does not speak elevated tones. This is considered bad form.
  • In Thailand, it is not customary to walk. They drive in tuk-tuk, motobike, taxi or rent a car for a while.
  • From Thailand, it is forbidden to export figurines or crafts with the image of Buddha.
  • The tourist season begins in late October and ends in February. The coldest day when the country is + 28 degrees.
  • Before entering the temple, you must take off your shoes, so you show respect for local traditions.
  • You can export a maximum of 50,000 baht from the country.
  • A visa can be obtained in Bangkok on the day of arrival.
  • The country has left-hand traffic. This must not be forgotten.
  • Most stores open at 10:00, and close at 20:00.
  • In restaurants you need to leave tips, which make up 10% of the check.

Many facts really surprise tourists. You will not have time to get used to the customs and traditions of the country, and your vacation is already over.

About the holidays

Locals call their country "State of smiles." Indeed, they are not far from the truth. Indeed, in Thailand almost every day there is some kind of holiday. As the saying goes: "There would be a reason to have fun."

So, interesting facts about the holidays:

  • as a rule, many holidays are not tied to specific dates;
  • for local residents, a holiday is an important part of life and it is a sin not to mark it;
  • Thais celebrate New Year 3 times - European, local and lunar calendar (January 1, April 13 and February 16);
  • locals celebrate Thai elephant day; it is believed that the country on the map in appearance is very similar to this animal;
  • in Thailand they like to walk on October 8th at a festival dedicated to vegetarians; people subject themselves to various tortures - they walk barefoot on hot coals, do piercings on delicate parts of the body, etc. it is believed that in this way the gods will forgive them all sins in advance, because they have inflicted injury on themselves for the sake of the future.

Thailand is the only country in the East in which local residents are able today to drink and have fun from the heart, and tomorrow, as if nothing had happened to go to work.

About the locals

Thais are very welcoming and hospitable people. However, they have their own distinctive features. For example, locals drink alcohol very rarely, and even then, on major holidays. It is also unacceptable for men with a naked torso to appear on the street. Even bare shoulders are considered bad form.

Buddha is the most revered among the locals. You can talk about him only in a positive tone.

In addition, Thais have one golden rule - never touch anyone's head. This is considered a great disrespect for the person. Never expose your bare heels. For this they can even beat.

About shopping

Of course, many tourists love to go shopping, benches, markets. Therefore, interesting facts about Thailand were selected for tourists, not only about locals or children, but also about shopping:

  1. Discounts are in large stores much more often than in shops.
  2. In Thailand, shopping is ideal a few days before the holidays. Indeed, it is then that tourists are offered good promotions. These days, goods can be bought much cheaper.
  3. The water market is a tourist attraction in Thailand. Sellers and buyers float in canals. Some offer goods, others buy. However, the prices here are overpriced, since the traffic is quite high.
  4. You can bargain with locals not only in the markets, but also in shops, which is a curiosity for the Russian people.
  5. In a large store you can buy not only products and electronics, but even a car. For this there is no need to go to specialized salons. However, car prices are high.

Even after the holidays, many stores still have promotions and discounts until the end of the season. That is, until February.

In addition, locals do not know the rules of punctuation, because they simply do not have commas, colons, semicolons, and ellipses. Thais know only quotation marks and parentheses.
