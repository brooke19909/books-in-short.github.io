New Guinea - exotic for tourists

Papua New Guinea is a state in Oceania, located in the south-west of the Pacific Ocean and has a beautiful, untouched nature, amazing the imagination of rich flora and fauna. Here there are saga palm trees, wild orchids, sugarcane, araucaria, melon tree. In these places you can get acquainted with the marsupial badger, tree kangaroo, crowned pigeons and other types of rare, amazing animals and birds.





Tours

Some travel companies offer trips to the most exotic places on the planet. Papua New Guinea is one of them. Thus, it is possible to travel in time, to see the primitiveness preserved for hundreds of years and to get acquainted with the extraordinary nature of those places. Moreover, the types of recreation in the country offer for every taste: group, wedding, combined or sightseeing. In addition, you can book a tour to the festival of dance shows "sing-sing", to competitions in fishing or windsurfing, visit the exhibition of orchids and be enchanted by a real celebration of nature and beauty.

Climate

As for the climate, it is equatorial in the north of the country, and subequatorial marine in the southern part. The islands of New Guinea have the hottest and humid climates. In winter and summer, the air temperature is about +30 degrees, and at night it ranges from 20 to 25 degrees. The favorable season for visiting the island is the period from May to October. The air on the islands is very hot and humid, hardly tolerated by Europeans. However, the Papuans of New Guinea are confident that these places are a paradise on Earth.

Relaxation

Papua New Guinea has many beaches, characterized by soft sand, warm waters of the Pacific Ocean, friendly natives and a complete absence of blood-sucking insects. Here you can spend serene time. Also, the country has the best diving in the world. So consider both professionals and beginners, who are struck by the magnificence of the local underwater world. Thousands of kilometers stretch lagoons, reefs, submarine plateaus, sunken ships at different times. Tourists call a fascinating trip to this tropical paradise "a journey of a lifetime."
