Sicily, Giardini Naxos: description, features, hotels, attractions and reviews

The population of this resort village totals only ten thousand people, but Sicily is justly proud of it. Giardini Naxos, the reviews of which you will read in this article, is located on the shores of the Ionian Sea between the cities of Catania and Messina. Near the village is one of the most fashionable resorts not only in Sicily, but throughout Italy - Taormina. Therefore, holidays in Giardini Naxos are chosen by wealthy couples who love to travel around the island during the day and relax in a tavern with a romantic atmosphere in the evening. This small village has its own characteristics. In addition to a set of standard resort services (beach, hotels, tourist entertainment industry), there are also a large number of historical and cultural attractions. This is what makes the town so distinctive and memorable.



How to get there

The town of Giardini-Naxos, as already mentioned, is located exactly halfway between Catania and Messina, on the eastern coast of Sicily. For those arriving on this island by air, it is easier to navigate to Fortanarossa Airport as a starting point. In the summer, numerous charters from Moscow and St. Petersburg arrive at this harbor.

If you want to fly to Sicily at another time, then you will be able to get with transfers in Rome, Milan or Venice. Catania Fortanarossa Airport is just fifty kilometers from Giardini Naxos. They are easy to overcome on an excellent highway, on which buses, in particular, run. And for those tourists who arrive to Sicily by sea, it is easier to go to the port of Messina. Reviews say that you can also take a bus from this city to the village.

The story of Giardini Naxos

In antiquity, the entire island of Sicily was a Greek colony. And already then, in the eighth century BC, there was a small fishing village called Naxos on the shore of the Ionian Sea. He was not famous for anything special. Men fished or made pottery, while women spun, made carpets and mats. So, in the flowering of gardens, the village existed until Nero began to rule in Rome . The emperor sent troops to Sicily. But the men of Naxos preferred to fight than to become slaves in the quarries. Women and children fled to the mountains.

Surviving residents founded a new city on Tauro Hill, four kilometers from their former housing. They strengthened it and made it impregnable. The city received its name from the name of the mountain - Taurmin. Then, when the danger was over, some of the residents returned to their former dwellings. Naxos was decorated with magnificent gardens and flower beds, which is why it got its second name - Giardini. At the end of the eighteenth century, the fishing village acquired the status of a city. But the connection with Taormina was not interrupted. Now the resort connects the funicular to this city.



When to go to Giardini Naxos?

Reviews report that it is comfortable to swim in the Ionian Sea near the eastern coast of Sicily from mid-May to the end of October. But the peak of the season (an influx of tourists and, as a result, a jump in prices) falls on the summer months and September. Especially good for relaxation is the first month of autumn. Then the sea and air reach the most comfortable temperatures - + 25-28 degrees. This should be considered for those travelers who want to relax in Giardini Naxos with their children.

But if your goal is to see the historical and cultural attractions of Sicily, it is better to choose May or October for your trip. Winters in the Mediterranean climate of the island are mild, warm, but very rainy. We can say that almost the entire annual rainfall falls from November to February. But even in winter, the water temperature in the sea does not fall below + 16 degrees. Summer is dry and hot. On the coast, the heat softens the sea breeze. But I do not want to go on excursions in the summer months.

Beaches

Giardini Naxos Bay is wide and long, with yellow and fine sand, in contrast to Taormina, which is famous for its small bays. Most of the coast belongs to first-line hotels. But in the very center of the town is the beach of Giardini Naxos - Lido. It is famous for its white and tiniest sand. In the northern part there is another paid section of the coast - La Romance. And in the south of the village there is Rekanti. This beach is municipal, that is, it will only be paid for renting a sunbed and an umbrella.

Giardini Naxos Attractions

This town, as already mentioned, is very ancient. It was founded by the Greeks, or rather, immigrants from Chalkida. Therefore, the resort's visiting card is a sculpture by Niki di Calkis, symbolizing the connection of the inhabitants of Naxos with their historic homeland. I must say that archaeological excavations continue to this day, and new finds replenish the exposition of the Historical Museum with the ruins of the Temple of Aphrodite at Cape Skizo.

In the Middle Ages, Giardini Naxos was surrounded by walls with a gate. From this era, there remained a castle with a tower of Vignazzo. In the town, reviews are advised to see the Church of St. Pankratia with magnificent frescoes and the Cathedral of Santa Maria della Raccomandata, built in the eighteenth century. Tourists also strongly recommend that you take the funicular to Taormina. There is also no shortage of architectural and historical sites.



Excursions

Giardini Naxos is a convenient starting point for independent trips around the island in a rented car. In addition, the island has a well-developed bus service. From the reviews it becomes clear that many tourists used this public transport to visit Messina, Catania, Syracuse.

Having an explanatory guide to Sicily, you can thus save a lot of money and at the same time make your stay full. Judge for yourself: you can go to Catania and Messina for eight euros (price for a round-trip ticket), to Syracuse - for twenty, and to Castelmola (via Taormina) - for seven. A popular excursion to the top of Etna. This volcano is quite high - over three thousand three hundred meters - but you can enter it by car.



What to try and bring?

You should definitely visit the typical Sicilian taverns that Giardini Naxos is so rich in. Travelers' reviews and tips often mention desserts that will remain in your memory for a long time: tubes with cream or ricotta cannoli, candied cassata cake, crushed ice with granite fruit, excellent ice cream is desirable. The most exquisite Sicilian wines will not only decorate your meal, but will also be an excellent gift brought to friends from a sunny island.
