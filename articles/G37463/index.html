Helmets of gladiators: description, photo

Gladiatorial culture has long been of interest to researchers. It is caused not only by the phenomenon of gladiatorial games. They are closely interconnected with the military sphere of Roman culture. The similarities in the practice of sword fencing training and the use of similar types of protective clothing are evident. It is noted that gladiators used helmets similar to those worn by Roman army soldiers in the 1st century. AD The difference between gladiatorial uniforms and the vestments of Roman legionnaires was in weight. Gladiator's helmets (see photo below) weighed 3-5 kg. While the protection of the face and head of the legionnaires was two times less. This was due to the conditions in which the battles were fought. The gladiator had to be protected as best as possible in a short period of time. In war, no one limited the duration of a legionnaire‚Äôs battle.





Back to the helmets of the gladiators. There were 4 species:

  • A helmet with the face of Hercules. The simplest and most functional of all possible. It had a simple angular crest. Jewelery - a minimum. Hercules medallion flaunted on his forehead, symbolizing strength, courage and thirst for victory. The gladiator's face protected the visor from two halves that docked along the line of the nose. The height of the helmet is 46 cm.
  • A griffin head helmet. It was adorned with a silvered image of the Medusa Gorgon. The head of the griffin acted as a comb. On both sides, bushings were found for attaching feathers to them.
  • Helmet with embossed tulle. Very beautiful and original. The relief image gives some grandeur. The plot of the image is simple: the Roman goddess between the barbarians and slaves. Visor from two halves attached to the hinges. It opens like a gate.
  • Helmet with a raised crest. Very similar to the previous option. In it, the comb and visor are decorated with a relief image, and the eye protection looks more brutal. On the sides there are also bushings for feathers.
