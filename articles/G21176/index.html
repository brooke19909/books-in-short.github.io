Such different hand-sewn dolls!

The child learns the world through the game. And that is why textile dolls appeared on earth. They served as toys for children from poor families and were created in the most primitive technique.

But gradually, the manufacture of textile dolls as a separate type of creativity has taken its place in needlework. It can even be called one of the directions in art. Today, there are dolls not only for games, but also for interior decoration. Often they are used as an option for a teapot.





Today, Waldorf dolls, tildes, and pumpkin heads are distinguished. Technicians of their manufacture now lead in the ranking of tailor-puppeteers. Although in its pure form it is quite difficult to meet a specific doll. Manufacturing techniques are intertwined, complement one another. And each master brings something of his own to the work.

A modern master can choose for himself a variety of patterns of copyright textile dolls for work. Based on the patterns, he has the opportunity to create his own technique for making the original crafts in this type of needlework.

Features of the manufacture of tilde dolls

The most important thing for a toy made in this technique is the seam that runs in front. Although now more and more dolls appear, which are called tildes, with side seams. But, taking up the work, it should be understood that the author already many years ago identified the canons of technology that must be observed when sewing.

The second important condition is the natural fabric from which the crafts are sewn: cotton, calico, linen, fleece, flannel. It must be monophonic! Tilda - the fair sex - has a tanned body. Therefore, if it is difficult to find the fabric of the desired color, it can be dyed in advance or after sewing, using both special dyes and blush, powder, slate shavings, instant coffee pulp and PVA glue.

Patterns for tilde dolls

It will help the novice master to get a real textile doll, a master class. The patterns presented here should be downloaded and printed on paper. Then they are cut out and proceed to the most critical part of the work.





First, the body is cut out with the head. To do this, a full-size textile doll pattern is superimposed on a double-folded fabric and encircled. Cut the details with scissors, making allowances for seams 2-3 mm. You should get two symmetrical halves.





Then they cover the details of the legs and arms. The fabric is also doubled, but the pattern is circled twice. You should get 4 parts for the hands and 4 for the legs.

Workshop for making textile dolls

Stitch the parts in pairs, folding them with the front side inwards. You can make stitches manually. But if you can stitch on a typewriter - great! For a tilde, it does not matter how the stitching is done.





It is important to leave a small hole in the workpiece, through which it can then be turned out clearly. Carefully using a pencil to straighten the workpiece, it is stuffed with sintepon, cotton or other filler. After this, the hole is sutured with a hidden seam.

Hands and feet are sewn to the body in the right places. Some in the knees impose a seam. Then the tilde will be able to bend its legs. Just do not in this case, to stuff a lot.

Tilde's face is not very developed. Typically, the master is limited to small eyes, sewing buttons or beads or embroidering a small ‚Äúdot‚Äù. But the hair should be given more attention - this is an important part of the appearance of this tilde.

Waldorf doll

Most often, a tilde is an interior decoration. But the Waldof doll is quite suitable as a children's toy. And all because the face of these cute little babies was worked out with greater accuracy than that done by tildes.





A distinctive feature of these dolls is their protruding nose. Making it is not as difficult as it might seem at first glance. It is enough to first make a blank for the head - to make a ball out of fabric and tightly fill it with filler. Then a bead or a smaller ball is attached to the template - it will imitate the nose. A thin layer of padding polyester is laid on top of the resulting workpiece and the main fabric is pulled. Now it is clearly visible that the nose rises above the cheeks.





The face itself is either carefully drawn with paints or embroidered with mouline thread.





Sometimes masters pay special attention to the fingers on the legs and arms of the pupae. This is true for those toys that portray babies. It will help the master to have such a textile doll doll, a master class.

Pumpkin Head Patterns

And these dolls have their own technique for making the most important part - the head. It is made up of four to six identical parts resembling a flower petal. Here is a full-size textile doll pattern, the head of which is made of 6 parts.





Hands and feet are sewn in the same way as for dolls made using other techniques. But the problem is that a rather large head of the toy rests on a thin neck, may arise in front of the master. Experts advise using wooden skewers to solve it. First, the doll‚Äôs body is pierced with a skewer, leading the sharp end 4-5 cm above the cut on the neck. Then, a ready-made head stuffed with filler is poked at the tip. For insurance, you can use a couple or even three skewers. Its lower end is best broken off and removed at the level of the pupa's waist.
