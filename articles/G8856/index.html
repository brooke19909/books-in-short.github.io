Replacing a keyboard on a laptop with a new one

The keyboard is one of the most fragile components of a laptop, subject to various external factors. In addition, after the matrix, this is the second component of the laptop, which breaks down most often.

If for a stationary computer the problem of keyboard malfunction is easily solved by acquiring a new one, then in the case of a laptop, the problem is most often solved only by replacement.

The keys on a laptop keyboard are prone to sticking and rapid wear due to various small particles getting under the keys. This is facilitated by eating at work, greasy hands, dirt, dust, animal hair.

Another serious danger of a laptop keyboard is spilled liquid on it. If even a small amount of it hits the keys, it is likely that the keyboard will crash.





Here are some reasons to replace the keyboard on a laptop:

  • liquid on the laptop keyboard;
  • loss of one or more keys;
  • violation of the integrity of the conductive mechanisms;
  • failure of the multicontroller;
  • the appearance of rust on conductive plates;
  • getting a large amount of crumbs, dust, or animal hair into the keyboard.
  • display when you click the wrong characters.

Training

We begin the replacement of the keyboard on any laptop by switching off the device. We take out all the wires. Remove the battery (battery).

To carry out the operations required for replacement, we need:

  • tray or tray for bolts;
  • a plastic card or a special trowel for opening;
  • set of screwdrivers;
  • new keyboard.

Replacing the keyboard on your Acer V3 571g laptop with a new one

Turn the laptop over, select a screwdriver and unscrew all the bolts that secure the back cover. Do not forget about the bolts that are under the cover that covers the hard drive and RAM, as well as under the hard drive. We put the laptop and gently begin to pry off the latches by squeezing the front panel of the laptop on which the touchpad is located. Having removed this panel, we unscrew the bolts under it. Only after that we raise the laptop keyboard.

Please note that under the keyboard there is a cable for the keyboard itself and a cable for the power button. Therefore, removing the keyboard completely is only possible after disconnecting them.

We turn over the removed keyboard and unscrew all the bolts on its back. Remove the keyboard tray and take it out. We install a new keyboard and assemble the laptop in the reverse order.





Replacing a keyboard on an Acer V5-551 laptop

A feature of this Acer model is that the keyboard is replaced only in the assembly. It is called TopCase. Its assembly includes a touchpad and a backlit keyboard field.

We proceed to disassemble. First we unscrew all visible bolts. We remove the DWD drive. Pry off with something sharp and remove the cover of the RAM module compartment. Disconnect the speaker connector. Remove the cover. After removal, turn off all visible connectors. Disconnect the cables of the display, backlight, hard drive. We remove the hard drive, a radiator with cooling, a VGA board, a matrix and other components of the laptop. Disconnect the keyboard from the monitor. Dismantling is over. We take the new TopCase and start the assembly in the reverse order.

Replacing the keyboard on a Samsung NP300V5a laptop

Replacing the keyboard seems to be ordinary, but the difficulty is that it is difficult to get to the keyboard. Take her off too. This model is sorted out very strange. To repair the laptop and replace the keyboard, you have to disassemble it completely.

Remove the battery. We remove the hatch of RAM. We unscrew the three bolts under the rubber legs at the corners of the lid. Pull the lid towards you and remove.

This model has this feature: under the first cover removed, only the hard disk and the RAM module are visible. The rest is covered with another lid.

Next, unscrew the screws in a circle. We remove the hard drive and unscrew all the visible bolts. We take out the optical drive.

Only after this we begin to separate the lower part of the body from the upper by pressing the plastic latch on the plastic card. Next, turn off all visible connectors and loops. Disconnect and unscrew the internal components. The disassembly process is very long. Having finally reached the keyboard, remove it from the top panel and install a new one. Now you need to fix the keyboard. It is recommended to fasten the keyboard with double-sided tape for smartphone screens. And in those places where it was fixed with soldered plastic, we fix it with glue. After the glue has dried, you can proceed to assembly.





Keyboard replacement on an HP 15-D008SV laptop

The keyboard for this model is of the Top Case type, i.e. the assembly includes both the top cover and the touchpad.

We begin the dismantling. We unscrew all the fasteners, remove the drive. Disconnect the cables. Carefully disconnect the top cover with a special spatula. We remove the laptop aside and deal with the keyboard.
