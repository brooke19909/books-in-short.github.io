Iron Philips GC 4425: reviews

Electric iron is an indispensable item in any family. Modern models have gone far from massive predecessors that could be ironed only through wet gauze. Irons, which are now sold in stores, are multifunctional units that can handle both the most delicate and coarse fabrics with many folds. These devices include the Philips GC 4425 iron. This model combines many useful functions and options that turn tedious ironing into a comfortable and simple task.

Model description

The body of the iron Philips GC 4425 is plastic, made in white-gray-blue color. The front of the model is elongated and narrowed.

A transparent cover with a sealant is located above the water filling hole - it prevents splashing out of liquid droplets during ironing. The hole itself is enlarged in diameter, which allows you to fill the iron directly from the tap, opening a small stream. The back of the device is expanded. This allows you to stably place it on the ironing board. The long three-meter network cable has a swivel mount. This type of connection prevents the abrasion of the cord, increasing its service life.

Specifications

Power model - 2400 watts. Accordingly, when turned on, this electrical appliance heats up in a matter of seconds. Iron power matters in homes with old wiring. With such indicators (2.5 kW), the load on the network is quite significant and can lead to ‚Äúknocking out‚Äù of traffic jams. The volume of the internal tank is 350 ml.

Steam boost in Philips GC 4425

In addition to the power of the heating element, the magnitude of the steam boost in the Philips GC 4425 iron is very important. The characteristic of such a plan is very important, because it depends on how efficiently the household appliance will cope with rough folds and heavy fabrics.





In this model, the constant steam output is 40 g / min. This means that for a minute of operation with continuous steam, the iron can consume 40 grams of water. Steam boost (one-shot at maximum power) equals 100 g / min in this model - 2.5 times more than in constant mode. This function is used if it is necessary to smooth heavily wrinkled fabric or linen folded in several layers.

Vertical steaming

According to reviews, many buyers are willing to purchase a Philips GC 4425 iron only because of this feature. Not everyone can afford to buy a steamer (for financial reasons or due to the lack of storage).

And it is not necessary to smooth vertically hanging things every day (the function of vertical steaming will be needed in this case - when you need to straighten curtains or a blouse of delicate fabrics). However, this option also has disadvantages, as evidenced by consumer reviews. The Philips 4425 iron is rather heavy and massive, and for a long time it is inconvenient to keep it on weight. Therefore, the option can be useful only if you use it from time to time.

Spatter and steam

The steam intensity can be adjusted. On the handle of the iron there is a 7-position switch. The more the number is set, the more intensive the process of steam humidification will go, reaching a maximum of 40 g / min. In addition, there is a spray function - when a scattered stream of water hits from the hole located in front of the Philips GC 4425 Azur iron .

Spraying and steam boost are controlled by two black buttons arranged in such a way that they are convenient to press while ironing.

Auto shut off

Unlike vertical steaming, this function is not very common in modern models. Although in some families it is extremely necessary. An iron with this function automatically turns off after 30 seconds if the sole is horizontal and after 8 minutes if it is upright. A similar option is implemented due to built-in motion sensors. Thus, the owner can not be afraid of a fire in the apartment if the iron is accidentally left turned on on the board. True, there are automatic shutdowns and cons. Some owners note that the iron manages to turn off while they shift or straighten things on an ironing board. If you take the device in your hands, the motion sensors will work, and it will again start to heat up.



Extra sole

Delicate Fabric Protector - An extra sole for delicate fabrics that fits over the core. This is another interesting feature of the Philips 4425 model . Not all fabrics can be ironed with a pre-heated iron. But at the same time, at low temperatures, a steaming function may be required. The difficulty lies in the fact that a poorly heated iron does this poorly. For intensive steaming, the heating temperature is simply not enough. In this case, an additional perforated sole comes to the rescue. The base of the iron is heated to the maximum, there is intense vaporization. The additional sole does not heat up, and the holes in it do not impede the release of moisture.

Leakage protection

Surely many housewives are familiar with the situation when drops of water flow from the iron onto clean sheets and leave yellow spots on it . Many that is why they prefer to stroke ‚Äúdry‚Äù.

To avoid this situation, manufacturers installed a leak protection (so-called ‚Äúdrop-stop‚Äù) in the Philips GC 4425 02 iron . A special bimetallic plate blocks access to the vaporization chamber at low heating temperatures. As a result, the iron does not leak during operation.

Steamglide

Steam Glide is a technology patented by Philips. Models with such a sole are coated with a durable and smooth to the touch alloy, on which a metal button or lock will not leave damage. A tapered nose is another feature of the Philips GC 4425 iron platform . Reviews say it easily smoothes fabric in hard-to-reach places - such as corners of folds, cuffs or collars. A lot of this contributes to the presence on the nose of the iron holes for the release of steam.

User's manual

The instruction manual describes the operating modes of the iron, as well as the care requirements. Separately, it is possible to note the cleaning of the device from scale. In addition to the built-in cartridge, which operates continuously and does not require replacement, the owners are encouraged to clean with steam every two weeks. To do this, fill the tank and warm up the Philips GC 4425 iron at maximum temperature. The instruction recommends turning off the device after that and lifting it above the sink, press the Calc-Clean button on the body. The rust that has accumulated inside the tank will come out with the steam. If necessary, the cleaning can be repeated. The procedure is recommended to be carried out monthly, if the hostess uses steaming water from the tap - once every two weeks.

Reviews

Since the Philips 4425 iron belongs to mid-priced models, there were many who wish to purchase it. Reviews about this device are more than positive. Mistresses note that the iron is ironed well and they do not have to drive several times in the same place to straighten the folds. This is facilitated not only by the quality of the sole cover, but also by the weight of the household appliance. Not too light so that you do not have to press on it when ironing, but also not so heavy that your hand gets tired of it.



Non-stick perforated sole is another advantage of the Philips GC 4425 Azur iron . Responses of the hostesses indicate that the fibers of the tissue practically do not stick to it in violation of the temperature regime, and those that nevertheless adhere to the surface are easily removed with a soapy damp cloth. Due to the large number of holes, the fabric is moistened evenly. The model has proven itself to be quite reliable, breakdowns rarely occur. An important plus is the two-year warranty provided by the manufacturer. The housewives are also pleased with the modern stylish design, convenient button layout, non-slip ergonomic handle. But not everyone uses an additional nozzle. But automatic shutdown is an absolute plus of the model.
