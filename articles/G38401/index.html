Troy ounce: how much is a gram

To date, there are several different systems for measuring the weight of precious and precious metals. We are used to using grams. And on exchanges and abroad, a troy ounce is often used. This weight system is perhaps the oldest and most accurate today. What it is, how it differs from the usual one, and how to translate it into grams familiar to us - this will be discussed in this article.



Troy Oz: History of Origin

In the old days, the means of delivery of cargo and movement were very primitive, and therefore there was no point in delivering goods on order. In addition, at that time there was still no modern advertising technology. Instead, fair centers operated in medieval Europe , to which all kinds of goods were brought from different places. It was here that at certain times of the year, merchants from different parts of Europe got acquainted with the new products, bought the necessary goods and sold the goods they brought with them.

Since 1150, the most popular European fairs have been those held in the province of Champagne (France). Its center was Troyes, whose name is consonant with the ancient Greek city of Troy, although there was nothing in common between them. Once upon a time, back in the days of the Roman Empire, the tribasic tribe lived here, and it is from its name that the name Troyes comes from. In the 13th century, the richest and most famous fairs operated here, and therefore their organizers strictly controlled trade in order to avoid deception of visiting merchants. In particular, they made sure that the system of measuring and weighing goods was correct and honest. So there was a troy ounce. Now it is very often used in the USA and the UK to measure the weight of precious metals, medicines and cosmetics.

Troy ounce of gold in modern trade

When making bank settlements using this unit, it is customary to round its value to 1/1000. Thus, in practice, a value of 31.103 grams (sometimes 31.1035) is used. On the exchange, this measure of gold is designated as XAU, palladium - XPD, silver - XAG and platinum - XPT.
