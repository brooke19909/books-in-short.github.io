X-Terra 705 Minelab metal detector: instructions, specifications, reviews

In the work of the digger, it is especially important to have at hand not only a reliable and functional device, but also the ability to flexibly adapt the tool to specific conditions. A long time ago, a gradation of metal detectors took shape on the market in terms of productivity, ranges of action and the presence of additional characteristics. The Minelab X-Terra 705 metal detector can be attributed to the middle category, which incorporates the advantages of amateur models and some features of the professional equipment of a modern digger.





Device and construction

The general form factor and layout of external organs are implemented according to traditional principles. In any case, this modification largely repeated the design of the previous version with the index 505. But in the other parameters, we can talk about the serious differences that the X-Terra 705 metal detector received. Minelab is famous for using modern Vflex technology, and in this case, the developers successfully optimized its function saving the sensitive analyzer from interference. The device is controlled by an updated microprocessor. As a result, the quality of metal recognition improved, the search depth and the system of differentiation of minerals increased.

One of the main components of any detector is a coil. In this case, the creators used the universal DoubleD model. Thanks to this solution, the Minelab X-Terra 705 metal detector was also seriously optimized, the detection depth of which, when searching for coins, for example, can now reach 50 cm. However, if necessary, it is quite possible to change the equipment by installing a more powerful component instead of the old coil.





Main characteristics

As already noted, the main element that is responsible for the performance of the metal detector is a coil. It is represented by a standard concentric device, which has a frequency of 7.5 kHz. The armrest can change 4 positions, and the length of the device in different assembly options can be from 1.2 to 1.4 m. At the same time, this model can be classified as semi-professional due to extended scales. So, the discrimination mask provides a corridor from 0 to 20, and the sensitivity is adjustable on a scale of 1-30. And all this with a rather modest weight, which made the Minelab X-Terra 705 metal detector more ergonomic. Weight characteristics vary depending on equipment, but in the standard the device weighs 1.3 kg.





Setup Instructions

The main feature of the model from the point of view of tuning was the appearance of a search mode in the conditions of mineralized soil. Also preserved are the old formats of the device, including manual mode, beach mode and automatic mode. The user can count on automation elements in the tracking format. This search mode is especially effective when it comes to sand formations, the same mineralized soil and other complex soils. Like previous versions, the X-Terra 705 Minelab metal detector has convenient tools for determining the center of the target. This is an activation of the PinPoint function, which also allows you to fix the size of the target. Experienced diggers know that the transition from amateur to professional metal detectors from the point of view of the user is complicated by overloading the option, which can be difficult to figure out. But in this model, thanks to the ergonomic screen and a balanced icon display system, the task of controlling the device was greatly facilitated.





User's manual

The practice of operation shows that the initial knowledge of the characteristics of a particular soil and an accurate understanding of the objects of search are of great benefit than the correct setting of the device itself. Nevertheless, it is desirable to increase the efficiency of the process in all available ways. Work begins with the preparation of the device, which is tuned to a specific type of soil. Next, using the menu with icons, specify the target object or group, and then proceed with the analysis. As the site is explored, the Minelab X-Terra 705 metal detector should be balanced. The manual notes that only automatic mode will completely eliminate the influence of soil interference during the search. However, from an ergonomic point of view, this is not always convenient if the detection tasks and the scanning conditions themselves change.

Maintenance Instructions

It is important to regularly check the accuracy of monitoring and signaling the coil. The basic operating parameters of the device depend on the correctness of its function. It is also necessary to pay attention to the structural condition of the apparatus. The quality of fixing fasteners, the reliability of the installation of modular groups and the state of the display - the functionality of the device also depends on these nuances. In addition, it is worth noting the importance of timely updating the batteries that power the X-Terra 705 metal detector. Minelab does not recommend using old power supplies until they are completely worn out, as there is a risk of electrolyte spilling. Chemically active substances negatively affect the high-quality operational properties of the device and in some cases act, like soil interference. The same applies to some items of inventory that are not related to equipping the detector.





Metal detector reviews

The development of intermediate models with a focus on versatility and multifunctionality is not easy for manufacturers. And yet this model for the most part evokes positive feedback. First of all, an effective system of working with different metals is noted. The device has the ability to distinguish the properties of elements in the search process. All the recorded information goes to the liquid crystal display, which is provided with the Minelab X-Terra 705 metal detector. Ergonomics reviews are key, as this is a weak spot in professional devices, and budget versions lose due to poor sensitivity. In this case, a balance is maintained between seemingly contradictory operational qualities.





Conclusion

The only significant drawback of this model is the cost. On average, the device costs 28-30 thousand rubles, which is not cheap even for semi-professional devices, to which the X-Terra 705 metal detector can be reliably attributed. Minelab offers decent technology solutions and optimized functions with advanced controls for this money, but fundamentally different from previous generations are still not observed. We can say that the developers collected all the successful developments and put them together in one model. The result is an easy-to-use, productive and truly versatile device for a wide variety of treasure hunter operations.
