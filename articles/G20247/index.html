Medieval Avignon (France) - the center of art and culture

The city of Popes, bells, churches, fortresses is called the beautiful Avignon. France is rich in interesting and beautiful places, this town was no exception. It makes a double impression on tourists, because, on the one hand, one cannot but notice the clutter on the streets, ugly graffiti, billboards, but on the other, ancient monuments of architecture, medieval chapels, temples, monasteries make the heart beat faster with delight. Be that as it may, Avignon is considered the most beautiful place in the Rhone Valley, it is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List and it is here that hundreds of thousands of tourists come from every corner of the world every year.



It is difficult to find a more romantic country than France. Avignon (photos of the city's attractions have always been of interest to tourists) is the owner of the largest Gothic ensemble in all of Europe. For seven decades, the city remained the capital of the Catholic world, and all thanks to the French king Philip IV, who made Pope Clement V, move to a new residence. As a recollection of this event, the Papal Palace has been preserved to this day, recognized as the largest building of the XIV century and one of the most magnificent castles in Europe.

Of course, time passed, and the papal throne returned to Rome, but until the French Revolution remained under the control of the papal legate Avignon. France is a very attractive country for tourists, but not everyone comes to see the Eiffel Tower, some vacationers will like a leisurely walk through the quiet and calm town of Provence, exploring architectural masterpieces that melt the mysteries of the Middle Ages, contemplating the surrounding landscapes. You should definitely go down to the St. Beneset bridge, caressed by the waves of the Rhone River, walk through a shady park, broken by Urban V, take a walk along the Courant Square, where you can stop for a snack in one of the many restaurants or cafes.



From Villeneuve-les-Avignon, from the side of the Cardinal Palace, you can discover a new Avignon. France has a rich history that it has passed down to generations through architectural masterpieces. You should definitely walk around the Old Town to feel the real theatrical spirit, you should visit the gardens of Rocher de Dom, from which you can enjoy a magnificent view of the Rhone. Throughout the world, it is thanks to the International Theater Festival that Avignon is known. France has long been a center of art and culture, so there is nothing surprising in the annual celebrations held here for over 50 years.



The holiday lasts a whole month. In July, musicians, dancers, theater troupes and, of course, spectators come to the city of Avignon from all over the world. France hospitably welcomes everyone, performances can go right on the square, in a monastery or church. Famous groups are honored to perform at the most honorable place - in the courtyard of the Papal Palace. Only in this city can you go back to the XIV century and abandon all problems and worries, enjoying the amazing scenery of Provence.
