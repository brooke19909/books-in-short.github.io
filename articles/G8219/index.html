History of the Count Manor

The first mention of the village of Ershovo dates back to the 18th century. The estate was a dowry of Maria Vasilyevna Saltykova, which became the property of her husband Adam Vasilyevich Olsufiev. From then on and until the collapse of the Russian Empire, Ershovo remained a clan count nest.

In 1829, the nobles took the initiative to build a stone Temple of the Life-Giving Trinity. In its place was a dilapidated wooden church. The construction was financed by a family of noble Olsufievs. By 1837, construction work on the construction of the manor house came to an end. The main building of CJSC Ershovo Rest House is the very building in which the landowners lived. It has been preserved almost unchanged.

The church and the house of the nobles were built according to the projects of the outstanding architect of that time, a native of serfs A.G. Grigoriev. His name is often mentioned in conjunction with the name of the mentor - the famous architect Gilardi.

The estate was threatened with destruction in the fall of 1941. The territory of the village of Ershovo was on the front line when the Nazis planned an attack on Moscow. The period of the Great Patriotic War is the saddest page in the history of the holiday home: on the lands of the village one of the borders passed - the stopping place of the fascist invaders.

Two years later, JV Stalin ordered the creation of a special laboratory, which was supposed to operate under the personal supervision of L.P. Beria. The field of activity of its employees was limited by theoretical developments in the field of creating weapons of mass destruction - the atomic bomb.

This department was top-secret. At first it received the name "State Committee", and later became known as the Ministry of Secondary Engineering. In the post-war period, trade union organizations began to protect the rights of workers, including Sredmash. Trade unions represented the interests of the proletariat, including workers engaged in professional activities in the country's nuclear industry. These specialists lived and worked in difficult conditions, they had to stay in classified cities for a long time, work in underground departments, and were in constant contact with radiation. It is no coincidence that one of the first decisions of the trade unions was the creation of a network of medical and sanatorium and resort institutions for the rehabilitation of employees in industrial, military and research industries.

One of the places in which it was proposed to organize a subordinate health resort was the Olsufyevs estate in the Odintsovo district. The rest house "Ershovo" was to the liking and leadership of this project.

The opening date of the health resort is January 18, 1952. The staff of the park hotel celebrate it every year, considering it the birthday of the Ershovo holiday home. On this day, vacationers came here for the first time - employees of the same secret laboratory, people who worked on the creation of the first Soviet atomic bomb and the USSR nuclear reactor.
