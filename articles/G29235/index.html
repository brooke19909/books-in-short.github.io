What is noteworthy is Profsoyuznaya metro station

The Moscow Metro went through several periods in its development. It is curious to observe its genesis from the window of a car, comparing the architecture of stations passing by. There are many masterpieces in the Moscow metro, but there are also more modest underground interiors. Such as the Profsoyuznaya metro station. But she deserves attention. A closer look at her example shows how conflicting trends fought for dominance in metro construction.

From the history of the Moscow metro

The first passengers stepped onto the platform of the Profsoyuznaya metro station in October 1962. It was the penultimate one as part of the launch site of the Kaluga radius from the Oktyabrskaya station to the Novye Cheryomushki station. Moscow in the early sixties was built at an unprecedented pace. In all peripheral areas, new microdistricts grew. For the first time in their lives, people got the opportunity to move from communal apartments to their own apartments. The construction of transport infrastructure did not always keep pace with the construction of housing. This was one of the main reasons forcing to reduce the time for laying new metro lines. And the number of commissioned stations and metro lines in the sixties was very high. At that time, they preferred not to think about the fact that this was largely achieved due to the quality of architecture.



Historical regulation

All the passions surrounding the construction were reflected in such an important historical document as the decree of the Central Committee of the CPSU and the Council of Ministers of the USSR "On the fight against architectural excesses" of 1955. Strategic approaches to construction were radically changed in the country, preference was given to standard projects. This really made it possible to save money, increase volumes and reduce the time required for putting objects into operation. But I had to pay for it by neglecting the aesthetic expressiveness of these objects. This is especially evident in the Moscow metro. The fallacy of the concept of excesses was recognized only after two decades.



Architectural features of the Profsoyuznaya metro station

The station does not make a vivid impression. There is nothing particularly outstanding in the architectural design, or even in the name of the Profsoyuznaya metro station. "Profsoyuznaya St." - indicated on signs in the underpass and on signs on the walls of houses, when exiting the subway. It was in honor of this, not particularly famous street, located in the south of Moscow, that the station was named. The street itself in the early sixties of the last century was in the process of construction and only acquired the familiar look of everyone today. Structurally, the Profsoyuznaya metro station is a columned three-span shallow station. This is a typical project, various variations of which can be found in abundance both in the Moscow metro and in the subways of other cities of the former Soviet Union. There is no ground lobby. The exit to the city, to Profsoyuznaya street, is through the underground passage laid under it.



Attempt to overcome the pattern.

But with all the modesty of the architectural solution, to call it mediocre would still be an exaggeration. The authors did everything possible to achieve individuality within the framework of a standard project. This is achieved by decorative decoration of the interior of the main hall.
