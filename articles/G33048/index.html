Climate

Most travelers to the island leave rave reviews. They can talk about rest in Cuba for hours, describing everything that struck them in a distant land. Surely in such conversations an amazing and very comfortable climate is mentioned. Cuba is warm, but not hot: the average temperature in January is +22.5 ¬∞ C, and in August +27.8 ¬∞ C. High humidity is maintained throughout the year. Monsoon winds almost always blow on the island, and in one direction. This can be seen on the inclined tree trunks.

Population

This is a multinational country where the blood and traditions of the peoples of Spain, Africa, Italy, Britain, Germany, American and Cuban Indian tribes, immigrants from France and China, as well as Jews resettled during the First and Second World Wars miraculously mixed.

The population of the republic is 11.5 million people. In their reviews of holidays in Cuba, travelers always note the benevolence of the local population. Do not refuse the offer to play dominoes with them or share a meal. This can offend them.

When is it better to come on vacation?

Today, many travelers choose holidays in Cuba. Reviews and tips of experienced tourists will help you choose the season to visit this distant country. They say that the most favorable time for a trip to Liberty Island is the period from November to April.

In summer, it is very hot and humid due to the rainy season: torrential rains can go almost every day. In winter there can be a lot of tourists from the northern countries. But windsurfing enthusiasts should choose a summer vacation in Cuba. Vacationers often publish reviews and photos of their success in this sport in tourism magazines. In the rainy season, the biggest waves rise here. Rest can overshadow only the dominance of hurricanes between October and November.

Rest in the winter

It was winter that was recognized as the best time to visit a distant republic. In winter, precipitation is the least, the probability of hurricanes is minimal, and the average temperature in the winter months is +24 ¬∞ C.

If you believe the reviews, a holiday in Cuba in winter is attractive due to the possibility of combining excursion programs and traditional pastime on the beach. Winter is the most favorable and most expensive time in Cuba, because this season the ideal weather for a beach holiday reigns here: the air temperature during the day is around +26 ¬∞ C, and at night it drops only to +21 ¬∞ C.

Great pleasure in winter swimming. On average, the water warms up to +25 ¬∞ C. Quite often in winter the water is warmer than air. This is the best time for a beach holiday in Cuba (reviews confirm this) also because the amount of rainfall during this period is minimal.

In winter, short-term cooling is possible in Cuba - the temperature during the day can drop to +20 ¬∞ C. This is due to northern air currents.

Cuba in December

A huge number of travelers from all over the world dream of getting out of the dull landscapes of winter and dank everyday life. That is why millions of tourists annually fly to Cuba to celebrate the New Year and spend Christmas holidays in the shadow of majestic palm trees instead of spruce. December, January and February are the most stressful times for the country's tourism industry. During this period, prices for tours increase by almost 40%. However, vouchers are still sold out long before the planned trip.

Particular admiration at this time was expressed by guests of the country in their reviews. Holidays in Cuba for the New Year are impressive and leave a lot of bright and pleasant memories. The December holiday on Liberty Island can be divided into two parts - until December 22 and after. Cubans are very fond of the New Year and begin preparations for it long before the holiday. It is very important for them to choose the right outfit: it should be new clothes, with preference given to yellow or red tones, which promise good luck and prosperity in the new year.

Instead of a Christmas tree, locals dress up Araucaria (decorative tree), and at the festive table there is always a roast pig, sweet potato and lots of fruits. You can also try holiday lobsters. They can‚Äôt afford ordinary Cubans, so the whole Mediterranean menu is intended for tourists.

Exactly at midnight, all those present at the festive table, according to a long tradition, eat 12 grapes. This has been the case since the days of the Spanish colonialists. The more sweet berries caught, the better: a ‚Äúsweet‚Äù year awaits you. And champagne, traditional for Europeans, replaces rum here, as well as cocktails based on it.

On New Year's Eve, Cubans always open all doors in the house to spend the old year and let the new one enter the house. Tourists are always surprised by the unusual New Year's tradition of the Cubans - a round around the house with a suitcase. This promises in the coming year a lot of travel and enjoyable adventures. And so that there is always enough money for these pleasures, they put a few coins in a suitcase.

Travel Tips

A few recommendations that make life easier for tourists:

  • In Cuba, two currencies are used - CUP and CUC. Both are not international. Therefore, you will need to change money as soon as you find yourself in the country.
  • In Cuba, gratuities are optional, but sometimes they are included in the bill. They are equal to 10% of the total. If they are not, you can leave 5%.
  • Check your bills at restaurants and bars carefully. Often, their amount is much larger than expected.
  • Public transport in Cuba, despite its low cost, is not the most efficient way to travel. In a taxi, the fare is inexpensive, and bargaining is also possible.
  • English is not widely spoken in Cuba, so learn basic Spanish phrases before you travel.
  • The sun on Liberty Island is much brighter and more intense than in Russia and the CIS countries. To avoid sunburn, stock up on sunscreens.
  • You will need protective creams and sprays against small insects that can ruin your evening beach vacation.
  • You should not drink raw water in Cuba, and ask for alcoholic drinks to be served chilled without ice, since Cuban water contains many harmful bacteria that can be dangerous for tourists.
