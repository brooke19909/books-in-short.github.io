Forum of Trajan

After the death of this Roman ruler, an interesting tradition developed. The Roman senators in greeting each successive emperor wished that "to be better than Trajan." This ruler was born in Spain. He is considered the greatest builder of Rome. It was he who built the cultural center of the city in the hope of strengthening his own popularity and becoming a true resident of the capital of the empire.
By that time, Rome already counted five similar squares, but not one was able to compete with the "brainchild" of Trajan. In size, he exceeded all of them combined. The architect of the Forum was a Greek from the province of Apollodorus. The rich booty that Rome received as a result of a campaign in Dacia allowed him to save on nothing. There were also a market, courts and libraries. But the most important part of the Forum was the majestic column of Trajan.

Photo Description

An amazing building dominated the entire complex of the square. The column of Trajan was decorated with amazingly expressive bas-reliefs that narrated about the military victories of the Roman emperor, in particular about the campaigns of his army during the crossing of the Danube, the seizure of the territory of present-day Romania, etc. Today it also stands proudly above the ruins of the Forum, and its foundations you can see the tomb of Ulpius Trajan himself and his wife.





To build this amazing monument, twenty huge blocks of the famous Carrara marble were brought to Rome.

Column Trajan has a very impressive size: thirty-eight meters in height and weighs forty tons. Inside it is hollow. Only a spiral staircase is arranged in it, which leads to a site built on capitals.

An amazing monument of ancient civilization

I must say that the monument on the column has changed several times. First, an eagle rose on the capitals, then a sculpture of Trajan himself, and only in the sixteenth century did the apostle Peter, standing at his full height, appear here. It is his image that adorns the column today. On the relief tape that runs along the entire trunk of the structure, you can see fragments from the two battles of the emperor and the Dacians. In total, it depicts two and a half thousand human figures, among which Trayan himself is repeatedly repeated. In addition to them, on the relief you can see Nick - the goddess of victory, as well as the Danube - the majestic old man - and other allegorical characters.



History of creation

Column of Trajan was built in honor of the Roman emperor, who made a dizzying career. He began as a simple legionnaire and reached the ruler of one of the most powerful states. Thanks to this talented commander and reformer, the territory of the Roman Empire was able to significantly increase. And the state itself has significantly strengthened its influence.

In addition to an active foreign policy and the construction of fortresses, this Roman emperor built bridges, aqueducts and other civil constructions. The last ancient Roman forum was built in his honor. Unlike the previous five, it was an extensive exposition of victories and weapons of the emperor.

Construction Features

Visually, the triumphal column of Trajan is divided into three parts. Firstly, on the base - the pedestal, then on the directly central part and on the upper protruding beyond its limits - the capital. The diameter of the column is about four meters.

The monument is made of the most valuable varieties of Carrara marble. Twenty blocks were used for its construction, which are installed in such a way that a cavity forms in the inner space. It contains a spiral staircase of one hundred and eighty-five steps leading to a site arranged on capitals. Sunlight penetrates into the column through small windows, more reminiscent of loopholes.

The outer part of the structure is covered with a relief ribbon, which rises in a spiral to its top. The images made on it tell about the episodes of the military campaign of Emperor Trajan. The bas-relief twenty-three times goes around the barrel part of the column. Its total length is one hundred and ninety meters.

At the base is the hall. Here are urns with the ashes of Emperor Trajan and his wife Pompeii Plotina. And on the pedestal to this day, an inscription has been preserved, which scientists consider a model of the Roman font.



Images

The Column of Trajan in Rome, the photo of which every tourist takes with him, is of particular historical value. Her bas-reliefs so figuratively and capaciously convey episodes of the war of the Romans against the Dacians that they simply amaze with the skill of execution.

The column presents the events of two campaigns of Trajan. On the tape, they are visually separated from each other by the winged goddess of Victory, who writes the name of the triumphant on the shield. Numerous military trophies of the victorious emperor were crowded around.

On a relief tape about two and a half thousand human figures. These are soldiers of the Roman army, performing certain actions: building fortifications, river crossings, fighting, etc. Emperor Trajan himself is captured fifty-nine times on a tape: at the head of a detachment, on a certain elevation.

The work of the ancient masters

The figures of the soldiers, the details of their weapons and uniforms, as well as the fortifications on the column, are drawn quite realistically and clearly. There is no perspective on the relief image: both near and far objects are displayed with the same dimensions. In addition, a second relief plan is made at the top of the tape. Thanks to this approach, the tape is very informative.

Such accuracy of all the scenes presented makes it possible not only to better imagine, but also to study the weapons, armor and clothing of the Romans and even the Dacians of that era.

In addition to the figures of the soldiers, on the relief you can see some allegorical drawings characteristic of the art of the Roman Empire of those times. For example, by the figure of an old man one must mean the Danube, and the woman with the face covered with a veil is Night.



At the time of creation, all the relief figures were executed in color. However, over time, their paint faded, and today these images have received the same light tone, which, however, does not detract from their historical or artistic value.

Secrets of Ancient Civilization

Erected in the distant 113 year, this fantastic column rises above Rome for almost two millennia. Over time, its reliefs were severely damaged, therefore, except for a few lower turns on a spiral, the rest are very difficult to see. The column of Trajan is considered one of the main monuments of all those that survived the fall of the Roman Empire. From century to century, historians have been studying its reliefs as a visual aid on the history of wars, where Trajan himself is presented as a hero, and Decebalus, the ruler of the Dacians, is his worthy adversary. Archaeologists even looked at the smallest details from the scenes depicted in order to obtain information on military tactics of armament and uniforms of the Roman army. A lot of effort to study this unique monument was spent by V. A. Chudinov. Column of Trajan in Rome, in his opinion, is dedicated more likely to the famous Trojan war of the XIII century, and not to the emperor-winner. This is evidenced by many facts, including the rate of destruction of the bas-relief, according to which it can be assumed that the construction is only about five hundred years old. However, many experts do not agree with the opinion of the Russian scientist.
