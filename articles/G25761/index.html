Friedrichsburg Gate: history of creation, date of construction, interesting and unusual facts, events, description, photos, reviews and travel tips

At all times, mankind has created various buildings and, it can be noted that over time, the principles of the structure are always improved and improved to this day. For people, in principle, development is characteristic. However, in order to evaluate progress, it is necessary to make a comparison, because everything is known in comparison.

History of creation

Few buildings built in the 16-19th century have survived to our days, and when we look at them, then progress is visible on the face. However, these buildings can not but arouse admiration, because they are unique and have a certain charm and majesty. A striking example is the Friedrichsburg Gate, which was built in 1650.

Initially, according to the decree of Friedrich Wilhelm, the Friedrichsburg fortress was built according to the ideas of the mathematician C. Otter. The historical site was erected in 1657, on the territory of present Kaliningrad to protect the Pregel. This fortress had a bastion type; its topographic shape had a square appearance with four bastions.

Cognitive facts

It is interesting that Peter I visited this fortress in 1697 with the aim of studying the bombardment business. Also, the Friedrichsburg fortress was considered a kind of model for the construction of several forts. The building itself became a fort only in the 19th century, after significant modernization.

An interesting fact is that the fortress did not participate in hostilities throughout its existence, however, it was intentionally destroyed in 1910, as it was declared obsolete and ceased to be a military target. The building itself was purchased by the imperial railway. The ditches were filled up, and the shafts were torn. Railway tracks were laid on the territory, as well as a railway bridge was created.

Nowadays, you can see only the Friedrichsburg Gate in Kaliningrad, which is the only element preserved from the fortress, in addition to the barracks, which was located on the east side. The gates themselves were built according to the design of F.A. St√ºler in 1852. They were made of figured burnt bricks and belong to the neo-Gothic style.

Description of Attraction

A feature of the fortress itself is the bastions. There are four of them. The bastions were named: Pearls, Diamond, Emerald and Ruby. Buildings such as a church, a prison, barns, a Zeighouse, a barracks, a commandant‚Äôs office, and a guardhouse were located in a quadrangular courtyard.

The gates were decorated with false windows in the Gothic style, as well as decorations in the form of serrated parapets. In the center there is an arch, and on the sides you can observe casemates for the garrison. Round towers are located on both sides of the main aisle.

In 1960, the Friedrichsburg Gate was given the status of a monument of urban planning and architecture, despite the fact that before that they were subject to demolition, like the main fortress, due to some damage during the Second World War. When the Koenigsberg was stormed in 1945, the gates were subjected to some destruction. The southeastern part of the guard wall was almost destroyed.

Initially, the gates were not restored, and they continued to collapse. However, in the beginning of the 21st century, large-scale work began on the reconstruction and improvement of the historical monument. After this restoration, the Friedrichsburg Gate became a branch of the Museum of the World Ocean.

Restoration work

For restoration, modern materials and masonry methods were used. During the war, this attraction was destroyed by 25%. Today we have a real opportunity to assess the scale of destruction. Only by 2011, this atypical, one-of-a-kind building, with the presence of emblems on the facade, towers and massive iron gates, acquired the very outlines that were characteristic of it back then, during the construction. Some of the most talented restorers, originally from Kaliningrad, St. Petersburg and Nizhny Novgorod, have made efforts to recreate the Friedrichsburg Gate in its original form.

Interesting fact

The Friedrichsburg Gate was the only gate of its kind that led to the castle of the same name, which was located on the southern bank of the Pregel River, and not to the city of Koenigsberg itself. When the Great Russian Embassy was in the city, the Russian Tsar Peter I underwent artillery training under the name of the officer Pyotr Mikhailov in the Friedrichsburg Fortress. The training itself was conducted by Colonel von Sternfeld, a Brandenburg specialist. When the tsar returned to Moscow, he received a certificate where it was written:

"To recognize and honor Pyotr Mikhailov as being a perfect at throwing bombs, careful and skillful firearms artist."
