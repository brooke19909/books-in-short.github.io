Crochet flowers for beginners knit easily and uncomplicated

For this, a simple scheme is taken, which consists of only two parts: the middle of the flower and the petals (their number is determined by the circumstances).

When choosing yarn for a flower, you need to pay attention to its texture, that is, the thickness of the thread: it should match at different parts of the flower.

Any color is possible for knitted flowers, the main thing is to have a harmonious and beautiful combination.

Crochet flowers: master class

The master class consists of two stages of knitting a flower: the middle and the petals.

  7. The thread from the last petal is connected to the beginning of knitting of the first petal, cut and carefully tucked.

The easiest way to tie a rose

To crochet a simple flower, for example, a rose, you need to dial seventy loops.

1 row: in every third loop a slingshot of two double crochets is knitted, between them there are two air loops.

2 row: in the middle of each slingshot a slingshot of two double crochets, two air loops and again two double crochets is knitted.

3 row: in the middle of each slingshot, nine double crochets are crocheted.

This method is the easiest, the flower crochets quickly, the rose is beautiful, especially if it is tied with a thread of a suitable shade.

There are many ways to tie: simple single crochet, alternating columns with an air loop, walking step, and so on.

Next, you need to collect the rose on a thread, from which the flower was knitted and fastened, after that you can supplement it with your outfit: a hat or hat.

How to tie a bell

As mentioned above, crochet flowers is very diverse.

To tie the bell, you need to take the strings of a suitable color, make a ‚Äúmagic ring‚Äù and tie six single crochet into it.

In the next row, twelve single crochet need to be tied.

Next you need to look at the situation: what size of flower is needed. If small, then you can already knit without adding several rows.

If the flower should be larger, then an even circle is knitted with increments to the desired diameter, then according to the same principle, that is, without increments the desired number of rows.

Having reached the intended size of the flower, we proceed to knit a number of petals: skipping one loop, knit three crochets, two air loops, again three crochets, skip one loop, again the petal, and so on until the end of the row.

You can tie a green cup for a bell, a middle of yellow or white thread, a stalk, leaflets: everything you need for a conceived masterpiece.
