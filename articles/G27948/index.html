Ibiza beaches: where to relax?

Ibiza Island is primarily famous for its night discos. Here life begins to boil only after lunch, and the few tourists who decide to relax in the morning have practically nothing to do. After all, you can even drink coffee no earlier than 10-11 hours. But, despite these features, the beaches of Ibiza are open around the clock. At some, there are numerous parties, and in some places you can swim, snorkel and sunbathe.

The best beaches of Ibiza





The list of beaches in Ibiza has about 32 items. This includes not only small locations, but entire resorts, as well as small bays. All the beaches of Ibiza can be divided into categories:

  • for family holidays;
  • youth;
  • for lovers of outdoor activities;
  • wild and picturesque.

To relax with your family





Family vacations require a safe beach with activities for children. It may seem strange to many, but recently equipped beaches in this way are especially popular with tourists, because modern Ibiza is not only parties.

A great place for a family holiday is the sandy beach of Puerto de San Miguel. It is very crowded. But despite this, Puerto de San Miguel is a great place to relax with children. Clean sand, shallow coastal waters, lots of family fun - what else is needed? It is located on the north side of the island.

And if you turn left, you can get to another beach - Cala Des Moltons (Cala Des Moltons). It is small, but also suitable for families. It is much quieter and calmer here. Nearby is another small sandy and pebble beach Beniriras, where you can not only swim, but also rent a catamaran to get to the rock located in the center of the bay.

On the eastern side of Ibiza, there are two beaches that fall under the category of ‚Äúfamily vacations‚Äù: Aguas Blancas and Cala Nova. The first is divided into two zones - left and right. In the left they prefer to relax in families, and in the right - nudists. There are a large number of quiet and comfortable cafes, showers and sun loungers. And Cala Nova will be a great place for people with little fidgets. Near the coast is very shallow, and near the beach there is a small green hill where you can take walks.

Youth beaches





The most popular beaches among young people are Aguas Blancas, more precisely, its right part, where a large number of bars are concentrated, where nudists and hippies love to gather, Talamanca and Playa d'en Bossa .

Talamanca is a place where people who do not want to live in the city like to stay. They spend the day on a sandy beach, and in the evening go to clubs located in the center of Ibiza, a fifteen minute walk from Talamanca.

Playa d'en Bossa beach is located in the south of the island. Here you can swim and windsurf, and also sit in one of the many bars and clubs. In the southern part there is also another popular beach, Platja Ses Salines. It belongs to one of the most fashionable places. Here, music plays all day, there are many cafes and clubs, and drinks are brought directly to the sunbeds.

Wild and scenic spots





The wild beaches of Ibiza are places to relax and enjoy the views. Whatever part of the island you look into, you will still find yourself in one of these locations.

In the north, all the wild beaches abut the cliffs. It is not easy to get to them; you have to walk along narrow winding paths. Stunning landscapes open from the deserted beaches of Cala Xarraca, S'illot des Rencli and Cala Xuclar. There is pure sand and crystal clear water.

There are two wild places on the east side of the island - Es Pou des Lleo beach and Cala Olivera bay. Es Pou des Lleo is not completely deserted - fishermen's huts were built here. But from here you can admire the picturesque scenery. And Cala Olivera is surrounded by cliffs. You can get here by going along a steep path.

You can enjoy the sunsets on the beaches of the south and southwest of Ibiza. In Cala Conta you can not only spend the sun, but also sit in a cozy cafe. And the beach of Cala Saladeta (Cala Saladeta) as if descended from the pages of tourist booklets: the sand is golden, the water is crystal clear and calm, the views are fascinating.

You can fully connect with nature on the beach of Punta Galera. There are no cafes, sun loungers and other tourist facilities. But you can, just sitting on the rocks, enjoy nature. Also in Punta Galera is clear water where you can swim. True, it is deep enough here.

Outdoor enthusiasts

The beaches of Ibiza have active sports. Everywhere you can snorkel or just snorkel. You can bring the necessary accessories with you or rent.

On the beach of Cala Bassa, located in the west of the island, you can rent jet skis and skis, catamarans, windsurfing. There is also a windsurfing school in Cala de San Vicente. Here you can also rent scuba gear and ride a banana or a boat to enjoy the clear water of Ibiza Island.

Beach: vacation prices





All beaches of the island are free to visit. Except those where private parties are planned. But if you want to rent something (for example, scuba gear), ride a boat, lie down in a sunbed and so on, you will have to spend money. After all, everyone knows that Ibiza is a rather expensive resort. A cup of coffee here costs from 10 euros. Therefore, calculate the budget with a margin if you want to not only enjoy the scenery, but also eat, and also attend a beach party.

You can still list the beaches of Ibiza for a long time. Here everyone will find a suitable place to relax. But most importantly, no one will remain indifferent to the beauty of the island and its lifestyle.
