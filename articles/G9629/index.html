What should be the best iron

Today, hundreds of models of irons are sold in stores. In order to determine which iron is the best, let's look at the capabilities of these electrical devices.



Let's start with the sole. An electric iron may have a sole made of aluminum, ceramics, cermets, titanium, teflon, stainless steel.

The aluminum sole heats up quickly, but is not durable. Over time, scratches and burrs appear on such soles that damage the fabric.

The Teflon-coated sole is durable, non-stick and glides well. When heated, Teflon becomes soft and easy to scratch on metal buttons or snakes. Teflon scratches then turn into burrs.

Ceramic on the sole of the iron heats up quickly, glides well, does not crease things and is easy to clean. Ceramics has a serious drawback - it is fragile.

Ceramic-metal coating has all the advantages of ceramic coating, but is more durable.

The stainless steel sole is strong and durable. Well-polished steel glides perfectly over the fabric.

The titanium sole coating is the most durable and durable. Titanium glides well on fabrics.

The best iron should have a sole covered with titanium, stainless steel or cermets.



The second important characteristic of the iron is power. At high power, the iron heats up faster and steam forms. Irons up to 1,500 watts are suitable for apartments with weak wiring. Irons with power from 1,600 to 1,900 watts are quite suitable for small families. If the family has more than three people and ironing takes several hours every week, then you need an iron with a capacity of 2,000 to 2,400 watts. High-power irons are also available, which consume from 2,400 watts.

The best iron is a device with power from 2,000 to 2,400 watts. Such parameters will provide quick heating, steam generation and will make it possible to use other devices at the same time, without causing network congestion.

The best iron should have an auto shut off function. The device turns off independently after 30 seconds of inactivity in a horizontal position or after 10 minutes of a passive stay vertically.

A good iron should have a steam boost function. This is an intense release of steam through the sole to smooth wrinkles or dry clothes.

Drops falling from the iron onto the fabric cause streaks. The best irons work with anti-drop systems. A system of continuous steam is available in almost all irons.

The iron must necessarily have the option of self-cleaning, when the steam chamber is cleaned of dirt and scale with a strong steam jet. The best irons are equipped with anti-scale systems. Such systems involve the use of removable cassettes or rods.

In the best irons used ball mount power cord. With this mount, the electric cord does not twist at the base and can rotate freely 360 degrees.



This review lists only the minimum set of options that characterize the best iron. When choosing an ironing device, remember that you need an iron, not a computer that can iron. Indeed, simplicity is the key to reliability.
