Mutual Funds: profitability rating. Rules for choosing mutual funds

Every investor wants to know where to invest with maximum profit. One of such tools is Mutual Investment Funds, for short, mutual funds.

Their profitability rating is constantly changing, and depends on many factors. We will try to understand in more detail what kind of financial instrument it is, what advantages and disadvantages it has. Read more about what mutual funds are, their profitability rating, as well as what you need to know before investing, later in the article.

What are mutual funds

A mutual fund is a kind of cash desk of total money, consisting of shares (units) of investors without involving legal entities and banking organizations.

Manages it, or rather tries to increase these funds, the management company with which they enter into an agreement. She receives a certain bonus for this, and investors - an increase in capital.

Good example

For a better understanding, we will simulate a situation: an apple tree is sold in a cooperative garden, which will bear fruit in a year.

One person does not have the opportunity to buy it, he offers friends to invest together in the purchase of an apple tree, with the goal of joint income. As a result, they form a mutual fund and trust it to one person. An apple tree is bought, which in a year begins to generate income. Friends share it depending on the share in the fund. But there is no guarantee that the tree will not dry tomorrow or bear fruit. Friends can invest money and lose everything.

Mutual Funds, the profitability rating of which can change every day, are arranged in the same way. The management company attracts money from customers and forms units from them. It does not mean anything if the name of the fund is associated with any bank, for example, Sberbank mutual funds - profitability, the rating is not guaranteed by PJSC Sberbank.

An investor can also lose his money, despite the fact that the management company is really connected with it, unlike a bank deposit, the profitability of which is guaranteed both by the bank and the insurance company, since all deposits of the population are insured against unforeseen situations

First mutual funds

The first such financial instruments appeared in the USA in 1924.

But during the period of economic crises, the American population did not trust management companies. To carry money to strangers in order to own it is not known what? - So ordinary citizens reasoned at that time period.

Types of mutual funds

Next, we list what UIFs are.

Their profitability rating, as well as risks, directly depends on the type of fund:

  • Bonded. The method of investing in bonds is considered the least risky, but their yield is lower than other types. It is believed that such an instrument is needed to preserve capital without the threat of loss.
  • Promotional. Shares of various companies are acquired with the money of shareholders, on the rate of which both profit and loss depend.
  • Mixed. A kind of golden mean in investment strategy.
  • Venture capital. Investors invest in the purchase of securities of companies that enter the market with various innovative products. More than half of them turn out to be unprofitable, but one successful transaction is enough, and all losses are compensated, and profit will increase.
  • Mortgage. Investors' money is invested in residential or commercial real estate.
  • Hedge The most risky, but at the same time, the most profitable funds. The trust company monitors market fluctuations, and adapts to them, thereby playing for a fall, or growth.
