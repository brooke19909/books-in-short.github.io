Watching the execution, don't yawn

Public execution today is in some places considered an effective remedy against corruption and theft. In some countries, the execution of death sentences is even broadcast on television, as a warning to robbers and bribes. Perhaps such a sight can catch up with fear, but, as a rule, the effect is manifested in an increase in the amount of bribe, which partly compensates for the moral suffering of the still-caught corrupt officials. Even in medieval Europe, it was noticed that it was during public executions in the squares that thieves were most active, cutting off wallets and rummaging through the pockets of onlookers, watching how other crooks were chopped off their hands.

Execution as a pedagogical method

So, execution is a one-time execution of a decision of an authorized instance. This clarification is important because sending to hard labor or imprisonment was not called that. Moreover, in ancient times, the severity of punishment did not matter, its inevitability and timeliness was important. Between the decision and its implementation in educational institutions, for example, a minimum period of time passed. Execution is terror against civilians

This word began to mean the mass execution of civilians with the aim of intimidation. Terror against the inhabitants of the occupied territories was used by German punitive units, the troops of the allies of Nazi Germany and the national units of the SS troops recruited from defectors. Monstrous ethnic cleansings were carried out in Lviv by the Nachtigal battalion, in Kiev tens of thousands of Soviet citizens were shot and buried in Babi Yar by collaborators, in Odessa, Romanian invaders hung the central streets of the city with corpses of hostages. Numerous war crimes were exposed during the trial of Nazi criminals in Nuremberg.
