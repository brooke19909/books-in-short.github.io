Nizhny Novgorod, Chkalovskaya stairs at the Volga embankment

Nizhny Novgorod has a wonderful architecture . The Chkalov staircase is one of the proofs of this. It is also called Volzhskaya. With its help, two embankments are connected: Verkhne-Volzhskaya or Nizhne-Volzhskaya.

About Attraction

This building adorns Nizhny Novgorod. The Chkalov staircase was created in accordance with the project of talented architects A. Yakovlev, L. Rudnev and V. Munts. Its beginning is located in the viewing area next to the monument dedicated to Chkalov. This is precisely why this item found its name.



Also, the Chkalov Stairs (Nizhny Novgorod) is located in close proximity to the St. George Tower, which belongs to the Kremlin. This structure has the shape of an eight. The Chkalovskaya ladder (Nizhny Novgorod) is quite large. How many steps here are of interest to many who first see her.

There are 560 of them, if you count from both sides. If you move up, you can count 442 on the right. Reaching the intersections of the slopes on the sides, you can walk to two sites for viewing. Design

When this land was part of the Russian Empire, as well as in the period before the Second World War, there was the Volga slope. Only then did the Chkalov staircase begin to decorate Nizhny Novgorod.

However, when she was not here yet, people already loved to gather here. Also here guests of the city were called. On the site, which is now occupied by a monument created in honor of V. Chkalov, there was a protrusion of a semicircular shape, from where you could admire the Borskaya floodplain. From here there was an opportunity to get to Blagoveshchenskaya Square, which was later renamed Sovetskaya and then Minin and Pozharsky. There is also a short exit to the beginning of Georgievskaya embankment.





Pause and start work

Plans for the creation of this staircase on the territory of the Volga slope were first visited by A. Shulpin in 1939, when he was chairman of the Gorky city executive committee. At that time, it was a rather spectacular step, because it was the Chkalov Stairs (Nizhny Novgorod) that should have become the link between the city center and the Volga.

The photos show that indeed one embankment is connected to another thanks to this building, which, according to the bold plans of the architects, should have become larger than the Potemkinskaya stairs, which you can see in Odessa.

The project was not implemented immediately, because this was hindered by the war with Germany that began very soon. They returned to development already in 1943, when this territory was recovering from the consequences of the bombing by the German invaders. The idea was further developed and was further advanced. Shulpin made a trip to Moscow, where he presented the project of Yakovlev, Rudnev and Munts - talented architects from Leningrad.





Construction

It was originally planned that the staircase would go down from the hotel complex "Russia". However, she was moved to the monument to Chkalov, who was a friend of the main developer. Thanks to this, the structure in its location approached the city Kremlin of the Middle Ages. After obtaining approval, funds were allocated from the capital, thanks to which it was possible to carry out the construction of a large-scale object glorifying the victory in the battle of Stalingrad.

They also wanted to name the staircase in honor of this city. In 1943, the first stone was laid. The people living here were engaged in construction, as well as the Germans, who were captured. The grand opening was carried out in 1949. A total of 7 million rubles was submitted by the chairman of the city executive committee. As a result, he was arrested in connection with the misappropriation of the budget. After Stalin died, the culprit was rehabilitated.
