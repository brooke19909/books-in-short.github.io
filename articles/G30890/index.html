History

The company appeared in the year of the end of World War II - in 1945. It was then that Max Gruding, who worked for a long time in a company selling radio receivers, resigned and decided to open his own production. Development began with the creation of small devices, analogues of which were already on the market. His radios enjoyed unprecedented success. By 1950, the small company Max began to build up its potential. The production of televisions has begun. The range has expanded significantly. In 1960, equipment was already assembled outside of Germany, in Belfast. Then there was a feeling that this company will always be a market leader, however, due to strong competition, all management plans have changed.

For a long time, Philips bought up the shares of Grundika, and in 1993 the brand completely passed into the ownership of a larger competitor. But the economic crisis did its job - the company was resold again.

Already in the early 2000s, the brand became "independent." Unfortunately, by this period the company had accumulated huge debts, and it had to officially declare bankruptcy.
