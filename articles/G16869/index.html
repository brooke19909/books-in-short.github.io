Blagoveshchenka Village, Altai Territory: description, photo

The village of Blagoveshchenka (Altai Territory) is located on the site of the Kulundinskaya steppe. This territory has for many years become the haven of the Teleut tribe. Kulundinskaya steppe was unsuitable for permanent housing due to bad soil and waterlessness. That is why the Teleut tribe led a literally primitive way of life.



History

By the mid-40s, the territories of the Kulundinskaya steppe began to be actively settled by the first settlers who were not afraid of waterlessness and practically unsuitable for cultivating the soil. It is here, as mentioned above, that the modern village of Blagoveshchenka (Altai Territory) is located. The map of the area will perfectly show where exactly you can find this settlement.

The first immigrants to these places were especially poor peasants. A distant and uncharted land gave many families hope for a good, hunger-free life. However, they were disappointed. Local residents didn‚Äôt really want to share their land with immigrants. This gave rise to a fierce struggle between the peasants. But this did not last long. Soon a government decree was issued that 15 tithes per one peasant soul were allocated to Voronezh settlers.
However, this did not save them from problems. Due to the lack of natural water sources, they had to wander from one land to another. This continued until they reached the territories near Lake Beshtanka. Thanks to the reservoir, this area did not know drought, and the soil turned out to be incredibly fertile. In addition, a trade route to the Yamyshevsky Fair passed nearby.

Residents of the future village earned a living only by farming. They grew cultivated plants year-round. Particularly famous for the future village brought watermelons, which were in incredible demand at all nearby bazaars and fairs.

Location and Economics

The village of Blagoveshchenka is located in the same region of the Altai Territory, not far from two water bodies: Kuchuk and Kulunda lakes. About 300 km - the path from it to Barnaul (western direction).

The main layer of the economy of the village of Blagoveshchenka (Altai Territory) are enterprises of various fields of activity. The largest here is the dairy plant, where the bulk of the processing of agricultural raw materials takes place. Also has its own printing house.



Population and education

The population of the village barely reaches the mark of 12 thousand people. The predominant part of the national composition is occupied by the Russian population.
The village of Blagoveshchenka (Altai Territory) has two secondary schools. The administration is trying to attract the younger generation in culture. That is why there is an art school and a music school. There is also a shared library for children and adults.

Culture and Attractions

The cultural life of the village of Blagoveshchenka (Altai Territory) is represented by the musical collective and theater. There is also a local history museum. Looking into it, you can immediately plunge into the history of culture and life of the first settlers. Employees will tell about military history (participation in wars and revolutions), show exhibits of those years. Here you can also get acquainted with the most prominent representatives of the local fauna.

The weather in Blagoveshchenka (Altai Territory) is generally favorable for life and work.



Transport

The village of Blagoveshchenka is connected with neighboring cities by rail and road. In the regional center is the Novo-Blagoveshchenka station. It is on the way from Barnaul to Kulunda. Motor transport leaves the local bus station around the clock.
