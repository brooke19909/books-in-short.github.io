Church of the Holy Virgin

Holy Devota is considered the patron saint of the Principality of Monaco. A whole legend goes about this girl who lived in the 3-4th century. It tells how a young maiden was sent to prison for adopting Christianity. In prison, she died due to terrible torture. The Christians saved her body from burning and hid it on a ship. The ship on the way to Africa sank near Monaco, and the girl‚Äôs body was thrown to the coast of the country. Fishermen from Monaco buried Devota.

The chapel was erected in the 11th century. It was noticed that people who come to the relics of the saint are healed. Also attributed to the merits of the Holy Virgin are the fact that in the 30s. 17th century, the state coped with the plague; in the 40s from the state were able to expel the Spaniards.

The chapel was rebuilt several times. The result is a full-fledged church.
In Monaco, every year, at the end of January, the celebration of the Day of the Holy Virgin is held, and traditionally a boat is burned on the shore.

Chapel of the Mercy of God

The Chapel of Mercy of God is the oldest building in the country. It was built in the late 30s of the 17th century. Initially, there were meetings of the Brotherhood of the black-headed penitent sinners. The brotherhood tirelessly engaged in charity. In the Middle Ages, it provided significant help to the sick and poor, the plague and lepers.

During the French Revolution, the chapel was destroyed and looted. Later, the building was restored and supplemented by paintings by famous artists and other valuables.

The chapel is also known for the fact that it has real treasures - a marble altar and a sculpture of Christ made of wood.
