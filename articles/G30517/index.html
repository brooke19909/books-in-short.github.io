How was the fire made?

The method of making fire could look as follows. Any of us can use it:

  1. You will need two dried wooden sticks.
  2. In any of them it was necessary to make a small depression (hole).
  3. Place a stick with a hole on the ground and fix it with a knee.
  4. Put the second stick vertically, hold it with the palms of both hands, after putting its end into the hole of the first stick.
  5. Very intensively move your palms relative to each other, forcing the wand clamped in them to make alternating rotational movements in opposite directions.

It is not surprising that during this process the hands of the fire-seeker gradually appeared in the lower part of the rotatable stick, which had to be pressed strongly into the hole. Therefore, each time a person quickly transferred his hands to the starting position and started all over again. This caused some inconvenience. The most primitive discoveries and inventions of mankind in the end yielded special results. Having learned to make fire, people stopped eating raw meat and were able to protect themselves from the cold and predators of that time.

Wheel Invention Version

Our distant ancestors arose the need to move heavy dimensional objects from one place to another. It could be thick tree trunks, huge stones, swimming equipment. Such work could be done not so exhausting, if you use primitive rollers. The inventions and discoveries of primitive people continued to make their lives easier.





Surely, observing the rotating rollers that underwent rough processing, one could come to this conclusion. The load along the cylinder will move smoothly without skidding to the sides if the shape of the roller is slightly changed so that the diameter in the middle part is less than at the edges. This could be achieved by burning with a flame. The result was a kind of "slope."

Gradually, people realized that it was not necessary to lay a massive log, but only need to cut it into rollers of the same width and pair it with an axis. In the future, the rollers began to be manufactured separately until they turned into full-fledged wheels, which were adapted to the wagons. Such inventions of primitive society have allowed us to enjoy car driving.

Background of the invention of writing

At the dawn of its development, millions of years ago, people communicated not only through sounds and gestures. They also tried to communicate something or warn each other by arranging branches or arrows in a certain order, making fires with thick smoke. In a word, conditional signals were given. Over time, warning systems have been perfected.

In different parts of the world, human imagination has been refined in methods of storing and transmitting information. So among the ancient Incas, nodular writing became widespread . Multi-colored knots served as its elements. For this purpose, woolen laces were used, which were knitted in different ways on a special stick. In the described way it was possible to fix this or that law, "write down" a poem or describe a specific event. Nodal writing was used for some time by the distant ancestors of modern Mongols and Chinese. In various fields, inventions and discoveries of primitive people were created. History is the only source of information about this. Archaeologists carefully study all objects, explaining the origin of things that are familiar to us.





About four millennia BC, people decided that it was much more convenient to transmit and store information in drawn characters that vaguely resemble elements of modern writing.

How to create a bow?

The invention of the bow was helped by observing how a bent branch or a young tree behaves. As a modern physicist would explain, the energy of elasticity, freed from the holding force of a branch (tree), ultimately turns into kinetic. Primitive people used this principle in passive hunting with the help of spring snares. Be that as it may, the very first bow could be created in the following way:

  1. Bend the branch, giving the shape of an arc.
  2. Tie it to the opposite ends with woven animal veins or long hair.
  3. After pulling the bowstring all the way and then releasing it, the arrow acquired enough energy to defeat the animal.
