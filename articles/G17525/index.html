Bible legend

The expression "golden calf" is more than one thousand years old. Golden Calfs of Jeroboam the First

This king of Israel erected two golden calves in his kingdom: one in Beit El (Bethel), and the other in the north of the country, in Dan. They were formal symbols of the foot of the throne of Yahweh. The locals worshiped these little bodies as if they were gods; their cult was preserved for a long time.

Some kings went further, accepting foreign cults, but even the most positively evaluated by the Bible rulers of the kingdom of Israel did not depart from veneration of Bethel and Danish calves.
