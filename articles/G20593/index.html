Biography of Sergey Ilyich Ulyanov

What do we know about a man named Sergey Ilyich Ulyanov, whose biography is foggy and indefinite? Born in 1870, he lived in the Ulyanov family until 1886. His parents, like all children, loved and protected him, even spoiled him more than others. Sergey was the antipode to Vladimir. He did not eat meat (he was a vegetarian, which is why he lived 95 years), did not offend animals, was distinguished by a complaisant character. After graduating, Sergei Ilyich Ulyanov in 1886, leaving his parents' house, went to the Ufa province, where he started a family, marrying a girl Zuhra, a local beauty.





Further, the story goes that in connection with the raging typhus, parents did not have the opportunity to come to their son‚Äôs wedding. He was little discouraged about this. By the mid-thirties, being a wax trader, Sergey Ilyich Ulyanov made a decent capital. Having become a wealthy man, he turned into a local "Sultan", marrying three beautiful girls at once. But the family idyll did not last long. Further events began to develop according to the revolutionary scenario.

Activities during the revolution

The revolution of 1905 and the subsequent difficult times required funds. The communist cell could not exist without financial influences. In this difficult period, Vladimir Ilyich remembered that he had a bourgeois brother. Turning to him in a letter, he writes that without the availability of financial support, the cause of the revolution would perish. The brother, quickly realizing the entire supply of wax, brings money to the raging Petrograd.





We conclude from the article that during the Great October Socialist Revolution, Sergei Ilyich Ulyanov (Lenin's twin brother) is at the forefront of a just cause, leading workers and peasants to the triumph of great victories. Moreover, Lenin did not seem to abhor his brother‚Äôs advice , calling their union a force capable of inflating the crucible of a future world revolution.

Post-revolutionary period

At the beginning of the 1920s, Sergei Ilyich returned to Bashkiria, where he was engaged in educational work and campaigning. But again he was not given rest. The brother‚Äôs reign did not last as long as Sergei would have liked. In 1924, the leader of the proletariat suddenly dies, and Stalin, taking all power in his hands, begins to persecute Lenin's former comrades-in-arms.





Seeing himself as one of the first candidates for the Stalinist meat grinder, Sergei Ilyich Ulyanov, whose biography did not correspond to the status of the proletariat, flees abroad. At first he secretly lives in Lithuania, later through the territory of Romania he makes his way to Switzerland. Later, he seems to write that flight is a necessary measure, and his escape served as a continuation of his brother's work - serving the ideas of Marxism.

At the end of the 30s, he was trying to unite by miracle the surviving supporters of the cause of Lenin, scattered throughout many countries of the world. The last refuge of S. I. Ulyanov

Sergei Ilyich Ulyanov, Lenin‚Äôs twin brother, found his last refuge in Cuba. At the end of the 50s, it seems that he was personally invited by Comrade F. Castro.
