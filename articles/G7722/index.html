Train Moscow - Simferopol via Kerch and via ferry. Passenger reviews

In connection with the opening of the border between Russia and Crimea, the peninsula daily seeks to visit hundreds of tourists. The train with the Moscow-Simferopol service, reviews of which are very popular, allows you to travel as quickly and comfortably as possible.

How to get to Crimea from Russia?

At the moment, there are several ways to travel to the sunny peninsula:

  1. By plane, which daily makes several flights from the airport of the city of Moscow and arrives in the capital of Crimea, Simferopol.
  2. Through the ferry, to which the train Moscow - Simferopol delivers.
  3. By train across the Russian-Ukrainian border.

All three methods are currently available to all Russians, but there are some nuances that you will learn about in this article.





Train Moscow - Simferopol via ferry

This method is currently the most convenient and affordable after the flight. The Express-3 marshall transports direct wagons that go through the Kerch ferry. The Moscow-Simferopol train has 7 wagons. Of these, 3 are compartment, 3 - reserved seats, and one - CB.

The route of the train Moscow - Simferopol is movement from the capital with a stop in the city of Krasnodar. It is here that all passengers must transfer to the ferry, which will take them to the city of Kerch, from where the train will continue on its way to the city of Simferopol. Of course, such a trip will seem very difficult for many, but at the moment a project for the construction of a bridge is being developed. Thus, tourists will have the opportunity to use the Moscow - Simferopol train via Kerch without making a ferry transfer.





Since the border between the Crimea and Russia ceased to exist on March 18, 2014, any Russian citizen can freely visit the territory of the peninsula without presenting a passport. You only need to have an internal passport of a Russian citizen with you. In addition, immediately after the referendum, the ruble became the official currency in Crimea, so tourists do not need to spend time exchanging banknotes.

How to cross the Black Sea on a ferry?

The ferry service operates around the clock, providing transport links between Russia and the Crimea. Thanks to water transport, the peninsula is provided with necessary foodstuffs, medicines and various goods.

Any passenger has the right to carry not only hand luggage or baggage on the ferry, but also his own vehicle. In this case, it is not necessary to undergo customs control or become mandatory migration registration. It is enough to simply present a passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation and pay the cost of the ticket for the cargo or vehicle.

When buying a single ticket for the Moscow - Simferopol train, ferry crossing is included in its price. However, if you purchase three tickets separately, you will have to pay 168 rubles for the crossing.





How much does the ticket cost?

In order to make it profitable for tourists to visit the Crimean peninsula, a single ticket was put into operation. It includes train travel and ferry service. In other words, now you do not need to buy several tickets at once to your destination. It is enough to request at the railway ticket office the so-called single ticket to the Crimea, the price of which includes the entire route. The price of tickets (train Moscow-Simferopol via ferry) is about 5 thousand rubles one way.

It is expected that after the construction of the Caucasus-Kerch bridge, the cost of the trip will be reduced, and passengers will not have to leave the cars to cross the strait.





Can Russians get to Crimea through Ukraine?

Today, any Russian can get to Crimea directly through Ukraine, as it was before the referendum. However, at the border, many unforeseen difficulties arise daily, because of which the trains are at customs and passport control for days.

In addition, since February 2015, Ukrainian legislation obliged all Russians to present a passport at the entrance to the territory of Ukraine or to issue a special invitation through relatives. Therefore, today such a route has become completely irrelevant.

The same applies to Crimeans, who are much easier to get to different cities of Russia, using the train Simferopol - Kerch - Moscow. Reviews in this regard are quite numerous. People testify that upon presentation of a passport with Crimean registration at the customs of Ukraine, difficulties may arise. If you need to get, for example, to Moscow in a few hours, you can use not a train, but an airplane.





Train Moscow - Simferopol: traveler reviews

Today you can find reviews regarding the route. Indeed, over the past year, thousands of Russians managed to test the train Moscow - Simferopol. In addition, the absence of a border allows not to delay passengers and provide transport communication promptly.

Many also note the affordable cost of tickets for the Moscow - Simferopol train via ferry, which can significantly save on vacation.

Negative reviews relate to the inconvenience of ferry transfers - this sometimes takes several hours. In addition, passengers would like to see more comfortable trains than those that for the most part run through Russia and neighboring countries.

Thus, the train Moscow - Simferopol, reviews of which are very popular and numerous, are used by the vast majority of tourists. It remains only to wait for the completion of the construction of the bridge from the port of Caucasus to the city of Kerch. This will help to solve several problems at once.





Movement on the peninsula

Passengers who purchased a ticket not to Simferopol, but to Kerch, can continue their journey on any convenient form of transport. In a matter of hours, a bus, taxi or train will take you to any resort city of Crimea.

From Simferopol you can also get to the desired hotel, recreation center or beach from the bus station. Transport on the peninsula is well developed, and buses leave every 5-10 minutes. Moreover, the cost of a ticket in any direction usually does not exceed 350 rubles.

Before the trip, be sure to study the route schedule and their direction, so that upon arrival in Crimea there will be no difficulties. If possible, you can travel around the peninsula by car. This will allow you not to get attached to fixed-route taxis and relax at your pleasure. And guides and a GPS navigator will help you stay on track.
