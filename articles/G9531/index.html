Thailand is a country where the joy of the moment is appreciated

Coming here, I want to "embrace the immense"! Thailand offers such a diverse vacation that it is impossible to distinguish the categories of travelers by type of tourism. People of all ages and religions come here, active travelers and lovers soak up the shore of the Gulf of Thailand, business tourists and shopaholics, the young generation who prefer nightlife and retirees who find paradise, quiet corners in calm and comfortable hotels.





What you need to know when traveling to Thailand? Residents are very religious, love and honor the royal family, kind and smiling, do not rush anywhere, enjoy life and never grumble. In the country there are a lot of temples of Buddha, and near each dwelling (even the smallest) there is a beautiful "house of spirits". Thais do not like to be photographed without permission. Children cannot be stroked on the head: it is believed that the soul of a person is there, it can be frightened off. They can be fined and even sent to prison for crushing or throwing a baht (a banknote of the country with the image of a king). In general, everything is pretty good and cute: an abundance of eateries (the Thais erected a real cult of food!), Funny go-go bars, laughter and music are heard around. The hotel territories are usually calm, and the beaches can be found pristine and uninhabited.

Features of rest in northern Pattaya

This is a fairly calm area, which is often chosen by families with children and tourists who value silence and peace. On the other hand, if you suddenly want entertainment, you can easily and quickly get to the city center. The beaches here are less busy and cleaner.





The main means of transportation in the country are motorbikes and cars. They say that a rental car can be obtained even without a license. There are practically no sidewalks, so vacationers may feel some discomfort when walking around the city. It is best to use a tuk-tuk, where you will be taken at a measured speed at an affordable price to wherever you say. By the way, Thais speak very specific English, which is difficult to understand even for a Briton. The peculiarity of the pronunciation is the excessive softening of many consonants, if you take this into account, you can parse some words. In other cases, sign language and facial expressions will always save us!
