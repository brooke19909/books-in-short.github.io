In this region, not only industry developed. The revolutionary mood of the workers also grew. The turn of the century was marked by large-scale revolts that were brutally suppressed by the Cossacks. The descendants are reminded of those events by the erected memorial complex, called the "Red Talka", and the factory-kitchen, telling about the workers' dreams of a new life. Now it houses a museum and a library. The oldest cities in the Ivanovo region are Yuryevets, preserved miraculously after the flooding of the reservoir, and Ples, modestly spread out on the high bank of the Volga. In the first, the childhood of directors Arthur Rowe and Andrei Tarkovsky passed, the second was glorified by Isaac Levitan, depicting it on his canvases. Crafts have long flourished in these places, the main fishing centers were the famous villages of Kholui and Palekh. In the first, secrets of the local school of icon painting are kept, and in the second, lacquer boxes and souvenir crafts have been painted for hundreds of years.



City Yuryevets, Ivanovo Region

The history of this locality begins in 1225. Today it is the administrative center of the Yuryevets district of the Ivanovo region. Located near the railway station Kineshma (58 km) opposite the mouth of the river. Unzha, on the right bank of the river. Volga (Gorky reservoir). The distance from the regional center is 159 km.

History

Yuryevets is one of the oldest cities founded by Yuri II, Prince of Vladimir, in 1225 as a fortification. Here he received the icon of St. George the Victorious, in whose honor the city was given the name Yuryev-Povolsky. After 12 years, he was destroyed by the army of Batu. Initially, it was a defensive fortress, protecting Central and Northern Russia from eastern raids, eventually turning into an important trade hub. In the middle of the twentieth century, during the construction of the Gorky reservoir, the city was supposed to be completely flooded, but it survived, while nearby villages with churches and other buildings were under water. The fate of the movie-teller A. Rowe and the cult director A. Tarkovsky are connected with these places. One of the streets is named after the latter, and the house where he grew up is turned into a museum. Both of these people spent their childhood here.





Museums of public education, historical and art exhibits and soft toys (located in the neighboring village of Kostyaev) should definitely visit many other places when they come to Yuryevets, Ivanovo Region. Rest will be complete, if you also visit the Gorky reservoir.
