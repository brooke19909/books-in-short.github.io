Headphones Philips SHP2000: description, specifications and reviews

The full-size monitor headphone segment is replete with offers at various levels. A suitable solution can be found by both an avid music lover and an undemanding music lover. The lowest category is traditionally in greatest demand, since it presents inexpensive models. It is due to fierce price competition that large brands are reluctant to produce budget products, but there are very decent exceptions. This is confirmed by Philips SHP2000 headphones, which provide decent sound for a modest fee. Of course, by no means in all respects the corporate model surpasses competitors even from less well-known brands, but the main performance indicators make this choice more than justified.

Headphone Overview





The model has an impressive body, although the weight of the structure is not so great - 225 grams. The design corresponds to standard budget decisions and does not differ in originality. The color scheme in which the Philips SHP2000 headphones are made is also oriented towards minimalism. The buyer can only rely on the black case, which, however, is not so bad. Excessive variegation for this class is not at all characteristic.

You will have to use the model at home, since handling it on the street can be inconvenient. Firstly, the discomfort arises due to the massive structure mentioned, and secondly, a long cord requires a certain dexterity in operation on the go. In addition, among the weaknesses of the Philips SHP2000, many note noise isolation. The materials and the general layout of the parts do not contribute to active noise reduction, so city sounds can seriously spoil the listening pleasure.

Specifications





Operating parameters are most fully disclosed at home. It is difficult to talk about a serious improvement in sound quality compared to other state employees, however, the Philips SHP2000 characteristics presented below may well suit the undemanding user:

  • The lower level of the reproduced frequencies is 15 Hz.
  • The upper frequency bar is 22,000 Hz.
  • Power at the ultimate level - 500 mW.
  • The resistance level is 32 ohms.
  • The headphone sensitivity is 100 dB.
  • The diameter of the membranes is 4 cm.
  • Type of fixation - through the headband.
  • Connection is two-way via cable.
  • The connector is a standard 3.5 mm, complemented by a 6.3 mm adapter.
  • The length of the wire is 200 cm.

Sound quality





Detailed study of the signal at different frequency levels in models costing less than 1,000 rubles. rarely found. In this case, we are talking about 500 rubles. and the listener nevertheless receives some advantages of reproduction. Relatively high-quality sound is observed when working with any sources - from a compact player or phone to a TV with a stereo system. Thanks to the open design of the Philips SHP2000, sound is played as close to natural as possible. True, the same nuance is reflected in sound insulation, which is almost not felt.

The disadvantages of playback include two points - the lack of bass and low volume. Even in compositions with a predominance of bottoms, headphones provide a soft and balanced broadcast, which, of course, is a minus for fans of "heavy" music. Many users lack the saturation and dynamics of the Philips SHP2000. Reviews about the volume are quite contradictory. It is believed that due to the moderate sound, optimal rendering of the middle and upper range is achieved.

Operation Nuances





The simple design, even despite the use of cheap plastic, is reliable and resistant to external influences. The same applies to thick wire. Experienced users are advised not to expose the device to even slight contact with moisture and to handle the cable connections carefully. If one of the speakers stops working, then throwing the device away is not worth it. In this case, a recommendation on how to disassemble Philips SHP2000 headphones without damage will help. To do this, it is enough to move the edge of the ear cushion in the faulty case, thereby providing access to the bolts. Next, the design with the speaker is disassembled. If all connections are operational, then the problem may be in the wire. As practice shows, you can restore a broken speaker by trimming 10-15 cm of cable from the side that is attached to the headphone. The bare wire should then be soldered to the speaker base, and then assemble the body.

User reviews

Among the main advantages that knock this model out of the total mass of inexpensive headphones, many call the sound quality. Again, it is not necessary to compare its ability to play music with the performance of average segment representatives, but the absence of obvious distortions in itself is a plus. Users also note the ear pads that are supplied with the Philips SHP2000 headphones. Reviews, in particular, emphasize the pleasant material from which they are made. Of course, there are critical opinions about this model. Most of the claims relate to noise isolation, which is not able to provide a reliable barrier to third-party sounds. Actually, this is the main factor that limits the use of the device. But the positive aspect of the open design immediately manifests itself - a balanced sound in the upper part of the acoustic range.

Conclusion





Experienced users of inexpensive headphones often note that the main thing in choosing such a product is to find an option with a minimal amount of design flaws. In this sense, Philips SHP2000 can be considered as an example of the optimal ratio of pluses and minuses, which are fully consistent with the price tag. These are practical headphones that will not break in a month and at the same time allow you to hear most of the notes in compositions of different genres. True, bass lovers should better refuse such a purchase. The absence of a volume control and the weakness of low frequencies make the model unsuitable for such tasks. But, in other operating parameters, taking into account the extremely low cost, the development from Philips is very attractive. In any case, when compared with competitors of the same price level.
