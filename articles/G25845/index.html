The best holiday in Croatia: traveler reviews

Holidays in Croatia can be very diverse. There are many interesting places to visit in this country with a warm Mediterranean climate. Local landscapes impress with their beauty. The beaches are diverse. There are sandy, stony, pebble. The water is very clean and in clear weather has a beautiful greenish-blue hue. Ancient cities located on the seashore and green hills will not leave anyone indifferent. All this presents Croatia as a very cozy and picturesque country.

Beach Holidays in Croatia (reviews)

There are 150 different beaches in Croatia. Most of them are pebble. Sand comes across much less frequently, which is far from satisfying. But the sea itself is very clean and warm, which speaks in favor of Croatia. Where is the best vacation with children? Reviews show that, of course, on the beach. It is quiet and calm, there is an opportunity for games and swimming. In addition, the beaches of Croatia are clean and well maintained.

The most popular is Zlatni Rat Beach, which has a length exceeding 500 meters. It is located on an island in the Adriatic Sea called Brac. You can hide from the heat in a forest located near the beach, where tourists have picnic tables. The cleanest beach in Europe is located in the village of Brela. And you can get to it from cities such as Zagreb, Split and Dubrovnik.

Rest on a yacht

You can rent a yacht on the beach in any of the resorts in Croatia. Reviews about the rest on the sea are perhaps the most positive. A boat trip is sure to bring pleasure. After all, the water in the Adriatic Sea is particularly transparent, from the deck of the ship you can see the water at a depth of 50 meters.

A northwest wind blows at sea, so the heat is not much felt. It can be very comfortable and interesting sea vacation in Croatia. Reviews of tourists about him are good. After all, many different excursions are offered. On a yacht you can visit interesting places, see the sights of cities without long pedestrian crossings, as well as visit the islands of Brac, Korcula, Hvar and Pag with wild beaches.

Resorts

There are many resorts in Croatia. In them, you can see architectural monuments in abundance, sunbathe on beautiful beaches, and go on interesting excursions. The medieval city of Dubrovnik is popular. It can be recognized by the large long fortified wall.

Istria is considered the most beautiful region with a mild climate and a picturesque sea coast surrounding the city from three sides. Split is an interesting resort where there are many old streets that deserve attention.

Dubrovnik

This is a very popular resort, which attracts a lot of tourists. The city is very beautiful, the length of its fortress wall is two kilometers. It impresses with its power and grandeur. A tour of this building is necessarily included in a familiarization walk around the city. From the fortress wall you can see many old medieval buildings of the city and, of course, the beautiful blue sea. The city bell tower is especially clearly visible. It stands out from other buildings.

To get acquainted with the history of the city you need to visit the princely palace. It is also worth admiring the interior of the cathedral. And, of course, Dubrovnik is famous for its magnificent Banje beach. It is located next to the walls of the fortress and is covered with pebbles. Tourists and city dwellers like to relax here, play volleyball, take sun baths. Those who prefer yellow sand should visit Uvala Beach. It is calm and clean, and the waves on the sea are suitable for surfing.

Istria

Istria is the largest peninsula in Croatia, which is located in the west of the country. On three sides it is surrounded by the sea, has a very interesting history and a large number of architectural monuments. There is an international airport located 6 kilometers from the capital city of Pula.

In Istria, there are many routes on which you can walk as well as cycle. A healthy lifestyle is welcomed and outdoor activities in Croatia are popular. Reviews of tourists are mostly positive about cycling along the coast. The main attraction for nature lovers is the Brion Islands. These are 14 islands, most of which are overgrown with forests. They have rich flora and fauna.

Central Dalmatia

The nature of Central Dalmatia combines a bright blue sea, pebble beaches and pine forests. The air here is good for children and for those who would like to treat their lungs. After all, it is saturated with volatile, beneficial effects on health. Particularly popular here is the city of Split, which preserved architectural monuments from the time of the Roman Empire. It is worth seeing the palace of Diolectian, which has been perfectly preserved, despite the fact that it stood for more than two thousand years, surrounded by powerful walls.

It is composed of beautiful white marble, which was mined not far from here, on the island of Brac. It will be interesting to look at the mausoleum and the temple of Jupiter, which also miraculously survived to this day. They amaze with the grandeur of magnificent columns, huge halls and arches reminiscent of the era of the ancient gods. Some scenes from the series ‚ÄúGame of Thrones‚Äù were filmed here.

In Central Dalmatia, it is worth visiting the city of Trogir. It is a small, cozy town with medieval streets and ancient dilapidated Roman buildings. It combines Romanesque and Renaissance architecture. It is worth looking at the Cathedral of St. Lawrence, the old town hall of the 15th century and the church of John the Baptist with wonderful carved gates.

Plitvice Lakes

According to some travelers, Plitvice Lakes is one of the most beautiful places on earth. The best vacation in Croatia, according to tourists, must include a visit to this place. This is a national park located in the central part of Croatia. He is the largest in the country.

The most beautiful local lakes were formed thanks to the Koran River. For many centuries, its waters flowed through limestone, forming small barriers consisting of travertine (calcareous tuff). These natural platinum blocked the way to water and thus formed numerous lakes with waterfalls and caves. The national park is included in the heritage of UNESCO. Its territory occupies almost 30 thousand hectares.

There are 16 large and several small lakes located at different heights in cascades, as well as 140 waterfalls with 20 karst caves. The picture is complemented by a unique coniferous and beech forest, which has remained since ancient times. Numerous waterfalls are pleasing to the eye, and in sunny weather you can see many small rainbows here. Walking through the forest, you can enjoy the contemplation of the local rich flora and fauna. Trout is found in the rivers. For tourists, wooden walkways and bridges are carefully laid everywhere. Oddly enough, the nature of this park is striking in its beauty in any weather and at any time of the year.

What time of year to visit Croatia

According to tourists, in Croatia you can have a good time at any time of the year. It has a very mild climate typical of the Mediterranean. The best time for swimming in the Adriatic is summer, when it is hot and you can sunbathe. Holidays in Croatia in June, according to travelers, are mostly beach-based, as it is a bit hot for excursions. If you want to avoid the intense heat and a large number of tourists, you should fly to Croatia either in May or in September.

The average temperature in May is from +23 to +25 degrees during the day. At night it drops a little - up to +18 Àö. Sea water temperature - from +19 degrees. At this time, it is good to walk along the city streets and visit historical places. In September, the velvet season begins in Croatia: the temperature is +25 degrees, and when night falls, it drops to +21 Àö. This creates a very comfortable environment for holidays in Croatia with children. In the reviews of tourists you can find out about the temperature of the sea. She is warm at that time and is comfortable for swimming.

Holidays in Croatia: to summarize

Reviews of tourists about this country are filled with fond memories. Guests who have visited Croatia greatly praise the picturesque nature of this country, well-developed infrastructure, warm and mild Mediterranean climate, a large number of places interesting from a historical point of view. The local cuisine is very tasty, and food is inexpensive. The ecology of this country, according to travelers, is also on top. The sea here is considered the cleanest, and beach lovers will be satisfied with the well-groomed coast.

In addition, the hospitality and hospitality of the locals pleases. In addition, holidays in Croatia are quite cheap for visitors. Among the negative reviews of tourists on holidays in Croatia (which are very few), one can highlight the intense heat in July, a small number of sandy beaches (mostly pebble and rocky) and the presence of sea urchins. Mostly the reviews are positive, and the disadvantages are minor.
