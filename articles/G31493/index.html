Rainier III, Prince of Monaco: biography, children

Monaco is a dwarf state in the south of the European continent, which is mainly known for its world-famous casinos and as the venue for the Formula 1 competition. Since the end of the thirteenth century, he has been ruled by the Grimaldi dynasty, represented by Prince Albert II, who took the throne after his father Rainier III. This monarch, who died in 2005, during his youth became the hero of one of the most high-profile royal novels of the last two centuries.

Parents

The future monarch, whose full name sounded like Louis-Henri-Bertrand Grimaldi, was born in 1923 in the family of the illegitimate daughter of Louis II Charlotte, who four years earlier was officially recognized as the successor to the throne. The fact is that otherwise the throne could pass to his second cousin, Wilhelm von Urach, who fought in the First World War on the side of Germany. The prospect of seeing the Germans as Prince of Monaco did not suit France, which threatened in this case to occupy the principality. Therefore, Prince Louis II went into violation of all laws by assigning the girl the title of Duchess of Valentinua, and also marrying her to the Frenchman - Count Pierre de Polignac. The marriage of Rainier's parents did not last long and was dissolved when the boy was ten years old, due to the homosexual relations of his father, information about which became public.





Rainier III, Prince of Monaco: biography before accession to the throne

The future monarch took a course at the best private schools in Switzerland and the UK, and then received a certificate of completion of general humanitarian education in Montpellier and graduated from the Paris Higher School of Political Science. Upon reaching adulthood, Louis-Henri Grimaldi volunteered to serve in the French army as an officer and took part in hostilities against Nazi Germany in Alsace.

In the role of the crown prince

In the same 1944, his mother, with the consent of Prince Louis II, transferred her succession rights to her son. At the same time, the young man did not leave his military career, for the military merits of Rainier III, Prince of Monaco, he was awarded the Bronze Star and the Military Cross. After the end of World War II, he was sent on a French military mission to Berlin, where he took part in resolving economic issues. In this field, the young man also achieved success, and at the beginning of 1947 the president of the French interim government awarded the heir to the crown of Monaco the Order of the Legion of Honor and the Knight's Cross.

Reign

Rainier III, Prince of Monaco, ascended the throne in 1949, after the death of his grandfather. Since that time, a real golden era has come in the history of this tiny state. It is enough to say that it was under him that the country acquired its modern appearance, and great economic and political transformations were made. In particular, in 1962, Rainier III, Prince of Monaco, initiated the adoption of a new, progressive constitution of the country, and in 1993 this state became a member of the UN with all the ensuing rights. In addition, thanks to his wise policy aimed at increasing the tourist attractiveness of the principality, the Monte Carlo coast has become one of the most prestigious resort areas in Europe.

Grace Kelly Before Marriage

The extraordinary beauty of a young girl from Philadelphia helped her become a model first, and then a sought-after movie star. At the time of the meeting, which took place in the spring of 1955, Grace was at the zenith of fame. She recently received an Academy Award, but, oddly enough, she was completely alone. The young people liked each other almost at first glance, and their engagement was soon announced.

The death of his wife

Grace Kelly and Prince of Monaco Rainier III, whose children themselves have long become parents, lived together for only 26 years. In 1982, the princess, along with Princess Stephanie, was in a terrible car accident and died as a result of her injuries. The daughter of the spouses received serious injuries, but she still managed to save her life. According to investigators, the princess, who on this day refused the services of a driver and drove a car herself, lost control due to a stroke. As a result, the car fell off a cliff. Although the accident happened early in the morning, the necessary medical equipment was delivered to the hospital, where Grace Kelly was brought, only in the evening. The precious time was lost, and the next doctors told the family that even if the princess remained alive, she would be paralyzed forever and could never return to normal life. Then Prince Rainier, after consulting with his older children, decided to turn off the life-support equipment.

So one of the most desirable and charming women of the planet died, the memory of which is still alive today, after more than 35 years from the date of her death.





Son

In 1958, Grace Kelly gave birth to a son, Albert. Rainier III, Prince of Monaco, was most rejoiced. The height, weight and appearance of the baby he was interested in much less than gender, since he had long dreamed of a son. Since childhood, the boy was fond of sports and participated in the Olympic Games five times as a bobsledder. In 2006, he visited the North Pole and even took part in the Dakar rally. In 2005, Prince Albert II inherited the throne, but remained childless until recently. Only in December 2014, his wife Charlene Wittstock gave birth to a twin monarch: a boy and a girl. According to the laws of the principality, after Albert II, the throne will pass to his son Jean.
