Crimean embankment in Moscow

Crimean embankment is located in the Yakimanka area, on the right bank of the Moscow River. This name was not given a name for this place. As a continuation of the Pushkin embankment, Krymskaya originates from the Crimean bridge. Then she runs along the "Museon" - an art park. At its end, the Crimean embankment passes into Yakimanskaya.

History

Crimean embankment appeared in the 19th century. Initially, she had several names. It was the Crimean dam, travel from the Crimean bridge, as well as the rampart from the Crimean bridge. In the 30s of the last century, the embankment was dressed in granite.





In the 70s, a building of the State Gallery was built near her. Currently, it houses the Central House of Artists and some rooms of the Tretyakov Gallery. In the 90s, the Museon Park was formed around the building of the art gallery. The embankment itself in those years was a small street. It was located between the fence of the Museon and the river and was a parking lot for employees of local offices.

Crimean bridge

This building is on the list of the largest suspension structures available in Europe. The bridge is a pedestrian-car and is considered one of the most beautiful in Moscow. In the place of its location in the old days was the eponymous ford, which the Tatars used during their attack on Belokamennaya. The prototype of the modern building was the Nikolsky floating bridge, which was built at the end of the 18th century. from wood. Further, this crossing was replaced by a metal structure, and in the third decade of the 20th century. erected a modern bridge.

Crimean shaft

Sightseeing walks do not ignore the most famous of the local streets. This is the Crimean shaft. The street has not only a number of attractions, but also a rich history.

The Crimean shaft was formed in the 19th century, in the 20s. Before on this place was the Earth rampart. In the old days, the chambers of the Crimean Khan were located on this street. He stayed in them during a visit to Moscow. Crimean baths were also erected here. All buildings of that time were made of wood. During the reign of Soviet power, the street began to take on its modern look. In the 20s of the last century, an agricultural exhibition was organized here, on the site of which there is now Park them. Gorky. Today, this green island in a green metropolis is an ideal place for hiking, jogging, rollerblading and other interesting and useful leisure activities.

Central House of Artists

Crimean embankment in Moscow gives art lovers the opportunity to join the eternal. The fact is that here is the Central House of Artists, next to which is part of the halls of the Tretyakov Gallery. Almost a million people visit the CHA annually. Here you can see exhibitions of contemporary art and design, as well as thematic fairs. The Tretyakov Gallery will delight the work of artists of the 20th century. Here are the paintings of Chagall and Konchalovsky, Malevich and Kandinsky, etc.

The ideal place for walking is the Crimean embankment. The Museon Park, which is located near the Central House of Artists, is part of it. On its territory, directly under the open sky, there is a museum of sculpture, as well as a spacious venue for organizing all kinds of lectures and a summer cinema.

Reconstruction

The Crimean Embankment in Moscow has become a cultural leisure center since 2013. After the reconstruction, it represents an excellent pedestrian zone and a beautiful landscape park. Prior to this, the embankment was a nondescript street located between the river and the park. However, the reconstruction completely changed it. Currently, this pedestrian zone, well-equipped with all modern requirements, can rightfully be considered the most romantic and cozy corner of the capital.



The space under the Crimean bridge was also reconstructed. About 15 wooden benches were put here, the cases of which are equipped with a backlight system. Before them is a small scene. This space, which remains dry even during rain, is intended by architects for performances by street artists, as well as for film screenings.

Vernissage

An amazing and interesting place is the Crimean embankment. The reconstruction of this zone also affected the vernissage located on it, which until 2013 was the longest in Europe. Artists exhibited their paintings here in the open. Before, you could not just walk here. The embankment served as the place where the paintings were purchased. Among the visitors of this vernissage, there were often famous athletes, politicians and stars of show business.

After the reconstruction, Vernissage on Krymskaya Embankment is a closed pavilion with a length of two hundred and ten meters. The exhibition complex is designed for ninety-six artists. And places in it are provided absolutely free of charge.

It should be noted that the wave became the basis of the architectural composition of the renovated embankment. Its shape can be seen in the roofs of the "Vernissage", and in bicycle and pedestrian paths, in benches and artificially constructed hills. The theme of the wave is especially visible from the Crimean bridge and emphasizes the proximity of this place to the river.

The site arranged in front of Vernissage looks unusual. She, unlike a regular lawn, is not planted with marigolds and petunias. It grows interesting decorative and wild flowers, herbs and cereals. All plants have different heights and create a common unusual composition. Nearby grow European maples and lindens of various species. Ornamental berries are planted here. Basically, they are represented by hawthorn.

Dry fountain

Crimean embankment never ceases to amaze its visitors. If you go along it to the Central House of Artists, you can see the jet fountain. It is equipped with a system that allows laser projection of images. Such fountains are also called dry. The reason for this is the location of the bowl of water. It is built below the ground. In this regard, it seems that jets of water hit directly from under the asphalt.



The dry fountain on the Crimean embankment is not the only one in Moscow. A similar one was opened in July 2013 in Babushkinsky Park. However, the fountain on the Crimean embankment is much more spectacular. In the park, this building has a very modest size. On the embankment, a whole alley of similar fountains is arranged. Their jets not only have a backlight, but also hit at different heights and at different intervals.





Rest zone

An interesting place is the area between the Moscow River and the dry fountain. In this place on the Crimean embankment, a beautiful and cozy recreation area was arranged. Garden furniture is placed here - small tables, tables and chairs. All of them are between the rows of trees. This solution helps create the most comfortable conditions for vacationers. If you wish, you can conveniently sit near the fountain or sit not far from the river.

Pedestrian waves

Crimean embankment has a clear delineation of zones. At its beginning from the bridge is the Vernissage site. This is followed by a dry fountain zone, followed by a place of artificial hills and waves.



The river theme is set by the roof of the Vernissage. Further, it continues with paths that, after a dry fountain, look like real waves. Here vacationers walk and cyclists ride. Moving along such tracks is very invigorating. They are especially loved by bicycle extremals and those whose hobbies are connected with jumping. As for the pedestrian waves, their height reaches thirty centimeters. Bicycle paths in some places rise to one and a half meters.

Puddle

A little away from artificial waves, another fountain was built. It has an overflow type and is called "Puddle". In this fountain, you can wet your hands or feet, sitting right on the bench. Everything is like in a real puddle. The only difference is that the water here is constantly updated and, therefore, clean. But in any case, you need to wet your feet or hands in an artificial puddle only in good weather. Along the edge of the fountain backlights are arranged.

Artificial hills

A section of the Crimean embankment, which begins after the Puddle fountain, is equipped with individual hills. Moreover, they have different landscaping. On some hills you can see a regular lawn and growing trees. Others have the appearance of a forest edge, while others grow tall herbs. Especially spectacular are the hills that are not far from each other. It seems that you are not in the center of a huge metropolis, but at the edge of the woods. This effect is enhanced by original lanterns-lamps made in the form of giant mushrooms.



At the end of the Crimean embankment, where it goes into Yakimanskaya, a pavilion with a cafe and video rental was built. It is notable for the fact that you can ride on its roof by bicycle or go. In winter, it easily turns into a snow slide for skiing.

Walking along the Crimean embankment is not only pleasant, but also safe. The whole territory is highlighted by lanterns, which are built in the form of the letter "G". Some light bulbs on the embankment are mounted directly into the paving slabs and railings.



In every zone, every hundred meters, you can see strolling stewards or security guards. They will always come to the rescue if necessary.

In the cold season on the promenade, you can warm up in any of the cafes working here, and in the summer lie down on artificially arranged hills, planted with mosses and spikelets. Enjoy reading a book in the shade of trees.

River walks

From the pier, a magnificent view of the Crimean bridge and Park them. Gorky. Here you can start your journey by river bus or motor ship. During the walk, vacationers will be able to admire the magnificent views of Moscow and breathe in the fresh air. Moreover, this can be done during navigation, which begins in early spring and ends in late autumn. Great pleasure and night walks. During these trips, Moscow appears in all the splendor of advertising lights and illuminated buildings.
