Chalky quarries of Belarus - description, photo, history of education

Chalky quarries of Belarus are exotic places that do not fit their usual classic Belarus into its usual beauty. The lakes filled with turquoise water will not leave you indifferent, and the white chalky shore will make you feel like a tourist somewhere in a European resort. Many lakes are surrounded by trees, the surrounding fields are covered with green grass. The water in the reservoirs is clear, azure. Everyone who visited the chalk lakes at least once in their life was fascinated by the beauties formed by the hands of man and nature.

Career education

The chalky quarries of Belarus are natural objects formed as a result of the extraction of chalk. They reach 50 meters in depth. Quarries are filled with groundwater and rainwater. Each such artificial lake has its own color: bright blue or green-blue. This color scheme appeared thanks to the minerals and chemical compounds contained in the water.

The shores of such lakes are steep, covered with dense vegetation, but with a tendency to collapse, therefore it is forbidden to swim there, but some daredevils sometimes allow themselves to swim in such a lake, referring to the fact that the density of water here is higher than in ordinary reservoirs, and the risk of drowning is minimal . But there is an opportunity to find a shore more or less suitable for swimming. Although you should not forget that if water is ankle-deep for you, this does not mean that after 1-2 meters you will not stumble on a cliff, in addition, there is a lot of silicon at the bottom that can be injured.

Where are the chalk quarries in Belarus?

Many people who want to go on chalk quarries will be interested in answering the question of where they are.

Their total number is about a hundred, or even more. However, only three chalky quarries of Belarus received wide fame (precisely for the color of the water):

  • Volkovysk;
  • Krasnoselsky village;
  • Grodno at the village of Pyshki.

Finding them is not too difficult, as large trucks carrying chalk are constantly driving along the roads. Highways and cars, respectively, are white from chalk dust, so you won‚Äôt get lost.

Careers Blue and Birch

And 10 kilometers from the regional center of Klimovichi in the Mogilev region there is a blue quarry, its banks are overgrown with trees, there is a fish that is caught by local fishermen.

The blue quarry was formed about 30 years ago only thanks to the springs that scored here after the completion of the chalk mining.

Birch is another man-made, chalky quarry. According to tourists, the rest there is even better than in the Krasnoselsky village. The shore is flat, which is why it is more like a natural formation. Spring water, an incredibly beautiful shade.

Holidays in the chalky quarries of Belarus are in principle prohibited, since steep banks are dangerous, every year about 30 percent of swimmers die in these careers, mostly diving enthusiasts. But ordinary tourists do not miss the opportunity to smear themselves with useful mud, sunbathe on the slopes and swim in the emerald green water.

Over the year, these places are visited by 100-130 thousand tourists from abroad. They are not even stopped by the fact that they have to go on their own. They take tents, things, food and just go to look at these wonderful white cliffs and emerald water. Although Belarus is famous for its cleanliness and order, this does not apply to the chalk lakes, since they do not belong to the tourist destination. Accordingly, nobody cleaned up and cleanliness there.

Everywhere trash, plastic bottles, bags, wrappers, in general, are all that visitors leave behind. Therefore, in order to get well and relax, you will have to work hard to find a cleaner place.
