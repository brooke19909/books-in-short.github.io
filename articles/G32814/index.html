In an authentic Arabic style, the hotel built Marina Plaza Hotel Tala Bay 4 *. Here you can not only enjoy the Red Sea and the hot sun, but also get to know the local culture better. This is a real oasis in the middle of a lifeless desert.

Tours to Jordan have been very popular lately. This is a unique kaleidoscope of unusual Arabian culture, beautiful traditions, untouched nature and, of course, the warm Red Sea. Sufficiently convenient location boasts Marina Plaza Hotel Tala Bay 4 *. It is located 250 meters from its own well-kept beach. It is 15 km from Aqaba, as well as 25 km from the nearest international airport. About an hour's drive from here is Petra and Wadi Rum.

Description of rooms

Comfortable and cozy rooms await travelers at the Marina Plaza Hotel Tala Bay 4 *. The following categories of accommodation are available to you:

  • Standard rooms are compact in size and have a cozy home-like atmosphere. Two one and a half beds, if necessary, can be shifted. Opposite there is a dressing table with a mirror, as well as a bedside table with a TV. It is also worth noting the presence of your own balcony with plastic chairs and a table. One of the rooms of this category is equipped specifically for the comfortable stay of guests with disabilities.
  • Junior is a huge apartment, which in size and functionality can be compared with a full-fledged city apartment. The bedroom has a large bed and a folding armchair. As for the living room, it is also equipped with upholstered furniture, which can serve to accommodate additional guests. There is a kitchen area with cooking appliances and a dining table.
  • The suite is a spacious studio. A large double bed, a couch for relaxation create an atmosphere of comfort. And for a special mood, warmth and coziness, original decorative elements are responsible.




As for the interior solution, it will appeal to lovers of authenticity. Someone may find the design too clumsy and pretentious. Nevertheless, true connoisseurs will see in this brightness an element of the eastern tradition.

General impression

As mentioned above, Jordan is a rather specific tourist destination that has not yet been fully explored by domestic travelers. Of great interest is the historical and cultural heritage, as well as natural and recreational resources. As for the service, it remains a bit in the shade. Although, the prices for accommodation and recreation are much higher than in Egypt and Turkey. This direction is chosen by real travelers who seek to learn something new, and the hotel for them is a secondary factor.

The first thing that catches your eye upon arrival is architecture and a luxurious area with a dense green garden. However, the euphoria passes almost immediately after you go inside. Here you will find shabby walls, outdated furniture and so on. However, the impression evens out a bit when you get into the room. The "tired" interior and equipment is more than compensated by the design decision and bright decorative elements. In addition, the rooms have everything you need for living.

Jordan is a fairly expensive resort. This hotel is located far from urban infrastructure, and therefore the prices for accommodation and recreation here are even higher. One can say that they are unreasonably high, because neither the quality of service, nor the range and taste of food, nor entertainment are impressive. Perhaps only the beach deserves attention (which, incidentally, is located quite far from the hotel). As for excursions, they are several times more expensive with a hotel guide than with local travel agencies.

Given the tense situation in the region, resort hotels are heavily guarded. Outsiders are not allowed into the territory. And even the residents of the hotel are searched every time they return to the hotel after a walk, tour or visit the beach. Upon arrival, the luggage is screened on an x-ray machine. Despite the fact that these events are not the most pleasant, they can be considered a kind of payment for safety and a relaxing holiday.
