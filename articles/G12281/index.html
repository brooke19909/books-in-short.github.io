A bit of history

The first mention of the city dates back to the beginning of the 12th century, when fishing huts began to be built in these places.





In 1497, only 5 residential buildings were erected in Calella, another 9 were built in neighboring Valdeour and Capaspre. In the 16th century, the central part of the modern city began to take shape: the market square, the streets of Mar, Batelier, Iglesia and the Salvador quarter. The Pope approved the creation of a local church parish in 1525. A few years later, the construction of the temple began. In 1564, the church was consecrated. Spain, Calella in particular, began to develop in the tourist direction in the 60s. Local people began to abandon their traditional occupations - fishing and textile production - and began to pay more attention to tourism.
