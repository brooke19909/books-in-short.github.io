How to make a claw out of paper quickly?

Many have thought about how to make a claw out of paper. Perhaps it should have been a detail of a Halloween costume or New Year's carnival. Some youth movements support the wearing of such "accessories" in everyday life, but do not want to immediately spend money on expensive models made of metal or polymer clay, until they get used to the unusual decoration. But more often than not, on how to make a claw out of paper, mothers think on the night before the matinee in kindergarten or school.

Second way: we will cut and glue



How to make a claw out of paper to make it look more natural, suggest designers-decorators of old Hollywood horror films. For this method, you will need paper by the number of necessary products, very sharp scissors and ordinary glue stick. To create claws, perform the following steps:

  • cones of suitable sizes are harvested so that they do not fall off the fingers;
  • the edges of the base are neatly trimmed, and all joints are glued so that the structure does not fall apart during operation.

This method differs only in that it does not require origami manufacturing skills, which means it is suitable even for children. Caution: babies should not work with scissors or chemical glue sticks unattended.

The third way: we will glue, but not cut

For this method, you will need a very high-quality glue that will not spread or greatly impregnate the base. How to make a claw out of paper without scissors can tell ordinary school teachers and educators from kindergartens. The manufacturing scheme is almost the same as in the first two cases. You need to make a small cone out of a whole sheet and try it on your finger, after which all the protruding "extra" corners are simply bent inward and glued. It is necessary to wait until the structure has completely dried, otherwise it may stick out or stretch when trying on.



If you understand how to make a claw out of paper in one of the listed ways, it will be possible to produce complex models with separate phalanges. For them, they simply make several cones and cylinders, gluing them together or folding in the origami style. Thus, it will be possible to provide their "clawed" characters with the necessary accessories quickly and very cheaply.
