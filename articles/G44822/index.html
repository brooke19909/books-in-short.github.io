The world around us, or the Capitals of Europe in a brief outline

Everyone loves to travel. Because this is the best way to get to know the world. To see places where I have not been, to understand how people live and to realize that you want to see a lot more. Now people have the opportunity to travel around the world. And many use this chance.

You can start traveling from any European capital. The main thing is to decide which is closer to you. Fairytale, like on a magic postcard, Prague or the bustling Madrid. Paris, which is considered the most romantic city in the world or austere Berlin. Choose!

All the capitals of Europe are somewhat similar to each other, but at the same time completely different. If you look at the map, it becomes clear that development in European countries went along approximately the same path. Feudal wars raged in each country , thanks to which we have the opportunity to visit medieval castles. And kings dreamed and embodied their dreams in magnificent palaces, which can be seen not only on a postcard. But in each country there is something of its own that attracts the eye and makes your heart sank.

Discovering more and more European capitals, very soon you will highlight the cities in which it is comfortable and calm, as well as those that you want to visit again and again. All people are different, and each will be close to something different. Someone has dreamed all his life to see the world from the Eiffel Tower, and someone wants to see bullfighting in Madrid. Someone will endlessly wander along the bridges of Amsterdam, while someone will be fascinated by the masterpieces of the Louvre.



All the capitals of Europe are fraught with centuries of history, for the study and knowledge of which life will not be enough, but, nevertheless, it is worth a try. Take, for example, Hungary and its Budapest, formed from three cities, each of which has its own roots and traditions. And the "Eternal City" Rome? Every stone in it is steeped in history and it is worth turning a blind eye as the Roman emperors stand before the mind's eye , applauding the gladiators who perish in the arena.
