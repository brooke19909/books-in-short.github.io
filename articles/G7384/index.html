Probably, the most familiar work for many craftswomen was knitting openwork scarves with knitting needles. Schemes and descriptions of most patterns are designed for vertical or horizontal ornaments. The classification of scarves as such does not exist. They can be conditionally divided into the following:

  • Knitted in a simple one-piece pattern.
  • Made up of fragments.
  • With a composition of several ornaments.

The first method of making a scarf is quite simple: one rectangular piece is knitted according to the scheme to the desired length. It is not easy, but real to make a scarf cloth from separately connected motifs. Openwork scarf: a pattern for beginners

Among the interesting and attractive ornaments, which are also quite easy to knit, the cobweb pattern occupies a leading position.



Almost any material is well suited for such a product. This scarf is openwork, knitting (the scheme and description will follow), knitted, looks great if you knit it from thin mohair, angora, wool , cotton or even viscose. Depending on the composition of the yarn, this accessory will be a warm addition to an autumn coat or an interesting wrap on your shoulders for cool summer evenings.

It should be noted that viscose allows you to get very beautiful and elegant products, but it is quite difficult to work with it because of its silky and smooth surface. Therefore, cotton and linen will be preferable if you need a summer stole, shawl or openwork scarf with knitting needles (patterns and description of patterns can be with a large number of large holes).

Preparatory Activities

To knit a cobweb pattern, you need to dial such a number of loops that is a multiple of four (since there are exactly so many loops in the rapport), plus two pieces for the edge. The exact number of loops should only be determined by measuring a control sample. The peculiarity of this ornament is that in each front row nakida are formed, which are knitted in the wrong side. The resulting holes significantly expand the canvas, and it is very difficult to determine by eye how many loops will be needed.

In addition, knitting a sample allows you to see on a reduced scale how the color and texture of the threads are combined with the selected pattern. In extreme cases, it is possible to choose a different yarn to get the desired openwork scarf with knitting needles. A set of loops and getting started

When the required width of the product is determined, and the number of loops is calculated, it is time to proceed to knitting the fabric:

  1. It is better to collect loops by the traditional method. It is important that both ends of the scarf are the same (both typesetting and the edge with closed loops). Perhaps at the end of the work you will need to complete the crochet.
  2. The first loop (edge) should always be removed without knitting.
  3. Next, to the end of the row, you need to repeat this sequence: 1 wrong loop, 2 facial.
  4. In the wrong row, all the loops are knitted in a pattern.
  5. After the edge loop, the yarn is made, then three loops should be knitted together, 1 wrong side. Repeat the sequence to the end of the row.

The third (front) row becomes the only one that forms the pattern; therefore, only it is repeated further. The inside of the loop knit in a pattern.

When the product reaches the planned length, all the loops are closed with a hook or knitting. Performing this action, you need to ensure that you do not tighten the canvas, otherwise the end will differ from the typesetting edge.

Patterns: rules of use

Considering the options for ornaments made up of different patterns, you can notice a certain regularity: between the openwork sections, harnesses (braids) are most often located, even longitudinal posts from front loops or a combination of both.

If the craftswoman decides to knit an openwork scarf with such a pattern (the diagram and description require mastery of the basic knitting techniques), she should be careful and carefully make all the calculations. It is necessary to arrange the pattern so that there are continuous sections along the edges of the scarf. The number of openwork stripes can be even or odd, it does not matter.

It is advisable to provide a narrow bar at the beginning and at the end of the product.
