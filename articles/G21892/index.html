Overview of the most interesting places

Nice is a big city, it is located along the Bay of Angels. From the north and west winds it is protected by the Alps. Therefore, winters are mild, and summers are dry and hot. Its population is more than three hundred and a half thousand inhabitants and annually receives more than 4 million tourists. Nice has a university, several business centers, many museums, the National Theater, opera, concert halls, a conservatory, and a regional library. Arriving here, you need to walk along the endless Promenade des Anglais, lined with palm trees, admire the palaces, hotels and the turquoise-colored sea, and then wrap yourself in a lively cheerful old city that preserves the features of Italian architecture. Its streets are narrow and winding. The walls of the houses are painted in warm reddish-orange colors. There will be many baroque churches.

What aromas exude open flower shops and cafes! And how many fruits are here! Further on the street are picturesquely exposed for tasting shrimp, oysters, lobsters! Church of the Immaculate Conception

The Notre-Dame-du-Port Church has been in the port of Nice since 1853. Her neoclassical style is successfully complemented by Corinthian columns. As in any Catholic church, there are benches for parishioners, and the walls are painted by the artist E. Costa.

Even such splendor was once just a port ward, which was surrounded by poor shacks and fisherman houses. Undoubtedly, before each going out to sea they came here with prayer, and upon returning with modest gifts. Now this is a solid area in which a respectable audience lives.

Building on a hill

Nice Observatory is located on the hill of Mont Gros. It was built by architects G. Eiffel (dome) and S. Garnier (building), it belonged to the University of Sorbonne.

At one time, the observatory was desolate, but after it was put in order in 1988, it became one of the world centers for the study of space. It hosts interesting excursions.
