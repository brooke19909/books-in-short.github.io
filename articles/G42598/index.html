Getting started on a shawl

Now let's try to consider in detail how to crochet a shawl. From the corner, it will expand until we get the desired size. And despite the strict geometric elements in the diagram, it will look quite impressive.




We begin by completing a chain of 4 air loops. Then at the base of the first loop we knit 3 crochet posts. The beginning of the future shawl has been laid. This is the so-called angle from which our shawl begins.

Next, we perform 3 air loops for lifting, and at the top of the last column of the previous row again knit 3 double crochet. Thus, we will add loops at the beginning of the row. Then 9 air loops. And we finish the row again with 3 double crochets in the first column of the previous row. That is, we complete the series also by adding loops.

When performing the next row, we will start the first square. At the beginning of the row we will make 3 air loops for lifting and 3 double crochets for adding. Then 5 air loops, a single crochet into an arch formed by a chain of air loops of the previous row, again 5 air loops and we end the row by adding - 4 double crochets.

Further crocheting of the shawl from the corner proceeds similarly, strictly following the pattern and not forgetting the symmetrical addition of loops.

Shutdown




Following the pattern, we continue knitting until the desired size of the product is obtained. As a rule, the shawl ends with a hook connected with the corner, simply fixing the thread at the end of the row. If necessary, or to give the product the most expressiveness, you can tie it in a circle. To do this, you can use ordinary single crochet or a more complex scheme. This will give the shawls a finished look, and also be able to mask minor errors. You can also decorate the shawl by evenly attaching pre-made brushes.
