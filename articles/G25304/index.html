Dollar coins





Studying the question of how the dollar looks, it is worth paying attention to coins. One cent coins have been minted since 1973. They are presented in many formats. Since 2010, 1 cent has been decorated with a shield with 13 vertical stripes, indicating state and national unity, on the reverse. Obverse adorns the image of Abraham Lincoln. The 5-cent coin was issued from 1956 to 2003. The obverse adorns the portrait of Thomas Jefferson, and the reverse depicts his estate in southern Virginia. The smallest coin has a value of 10 cents. It is called 1 dime. The obverse was decorated with a portrait of Roosevelt, and the reverse with a torch and oak with olive branches.
