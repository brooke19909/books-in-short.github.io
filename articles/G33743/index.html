History of Heraklion

Heraklion is considered one of the oldest Greek cities. He appeared on the site of a small harbor, near the center of Minoan civilization. For the first time, Strabo mentions him in his famous work entitled "Geography."

True, according to archaeological finds, it is still impossible to assert that Strabo‚Äôs speech is about a city that was located on the site of modern Heraklion. In the IX century, he was captured by the Arabs, who held their power here for almost a century and a half. For a reliable defense, they built a powerful wall, which was surrounded by a deep moat.

In the X century, the city was conquered by the Byzantines. The city was looted, and all the wealth stored in it was taken out on 300 ships. After 250 years, the crusaders came here to establish a new government in Heraklion. In 1204, the city was sold to the Venetian Republic, which established its rule here.

It was the golden age of Heraklion. Many magnificent buildings, beautiful fountains, remarkable squares and architectural monuments appeared. After the Turks settled in Constantinople, many Byzantines moved here, contributing to its further development.

In the middle of the XVII century, the Turks began a protracted war for the city, which lasted for a quarter century. During this time, the Turks lost more than one hundred thousand people, about 30 thousand defenders of the city died. The fortress was commissioned only in 1669.

Heraklion acquired its historical name only in the 19th century. The city is named after Heracles. In 1898, the island of Crete gained independence for a short time, and in 1913 reunited with Greece.

Weather in Heraklion





Heraklion has a Mediterranean climate. Due to the fact that the city itself is located on the island, it is possible to mitigate temperature fluctuations as much as possible. Due to this factor, summer is much cooler here than in most other cities, where the same climate is predominantly observed. Therefore, the heat is much easier.

At the same time, winters are warmer and milder. The most comfortable place to relax in Crete is from May to October. During these months, the weather in Heraklion has a good rest. Already in May, the average air temperature is 23.5 degrees. In June-August, it reaches a mark of 27-28 degrees and in October does not decrease below plus 23.

It is coldest here in January and February, on average, the thermometer is held at plus 15 degrees.

About 500 millimeters of precipitation falls in Heraklion annually. And the least in July and August - about one millimeter per month. The rainiest month is January (more than 90 millimeters of precipitation).
