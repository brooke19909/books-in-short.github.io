Strikes in History

In Russia, strike committees appeared in the eighties of the nineteenth century. In 1905, a strike committee of the railway was organized in Mordovia, which established complete order at the station. According to historical data, this committee made a huge contribution to the course of the 1917 revolution.

Strikes were subdivided into mass strikes (several organizations participate), partial strikes (only a part of the workers take part), local strikes (one enterprise), general strikes (the whole country), offensive and defensive.

What is the strike and when they were - very well laid out in history textbooks. For example: the strike of 1835 in Kazan, 1885 in Orekhovo-Zuev, 1896 in St. Petersburg.

Police and tsarist forces tried to crush various strikes, but in vain. They continued until the advent of Soviet power. Then they were strictly prohibited. But in the 1980-1990s, strikes were revived.





Strikes in the modern world

The basic law of the state, the Constitution of the Russian Federation, secured the right to strike (2001).

In a word, one can easily explain what a strike is. This is an expression by workers of their requirements and wishes.
