Sale of property of debtors by Sberbank - auctions, procedure and recommendations

When applying for loans in large amounts, it is often required to provide a security in the form of movable and immovable property. This object becomes the property of the pledge holder. In case of violation of the terms of the contract, the formation of debt, the bank sells such objects at attractive prices. How is the sale of debtor property by Sberbank?

Definition

Pledged property is considered to be objects transferred as security for obligations taken. This can be not only real estate, but also special equipment, equipment, vehicles, goods in circulation. In case of formation of overdue debt on the loan, the object becomes the property of the bank, which then deals with its implementation. Objects are exhibited at interesting prices. Their implementation allows the company to settle obligations.

Most often in Russia, Sberbank and other financial institutions sell their mortgaged property through an auction. The initial price for the object is very attractive, because there are many who want to participate in the auction. All participants are pre-accredited.

Bidding is carried out on an electronic platform. To participate in the process, accreditation is required, which is accessible even to individuals.

Sberbank exhibits property of debtors and bankrupt on its electronic site RAO OJSC. She works around the clock. Potential buyers first look at objects on the platform. If they find interesting offers for themselves, then they need to contact a bank representative and fill out applications. After their approval, the organizers inform the participants of the bidding time. Notifications of all objects of interest are sent to the email of each participant. All bets must exceed the initial price set by the bank.

Sales of vehicles

A collateral object can be not only real estate, but also motor vehicles. Realization of cars can be carried out in two ways. Or the owner himself puts up a vehicle for sale in order to pay off obligations in a short time. Or a financial institution is engaged in this in the same way as the sale of property of Sberbank debtors. Bidding is also carried out through an electronic platform. If the borrower himself puts up ads for the sale, then the transaction is executed much faster.

After paying for the object, you need to do the paperwork:

  • Requirement for the seller to issue the vehicle.
  • The act of bidding.
  • Decree on deregistration.
  • Power of attorney from the owner of the vehicle.
  • To remove an object from the register in the Ministry of Justice.




Scheme

Everyone who wants to buy a car submits a preliminary application and pays for participation in the auction (held once a month). It exhibits all collateral objects of the bank. The process is the same as the sale of collateral. Before the auction, the judge invites the borrower to repay the debt in order to avoid the sale of the property. If he refuses, then bidding begins.

Recommendations

Sometimes financial institutions artificially inflate the value of the property to the full amount of the loan. In order not to overpay the bank, you should study the object in detail at the official sites: Sberbank AST and RAD before the auction. The same information can be obtained through electronic bidding operators. The VYKUPAI.RU, Zalog24 and RuVin portals contain all information about collateral objects, their status and prices. It also exhibited technical specifications and photographs of objects from different angles. Legal aspects of the transaction will help clarify the consultation of a lawyer (1-2 thousand rubles).





What you should pay attention to?

Before selling, the vehicle undergoes maintenance. The buyer receives a car with a warranty period. Banking institution is engaged in paperwork. The cost of the object is 20%, and sometimes 50% lower than the market. If the owner does not know about the confiscation of the object, then perhaps the vehicle is listed in theft, and the transaction itself can be challenged in court. Very often, such objects fall at an auction from owners who are not serious about buying. To protect yourself from possible problems, you need to make sure that the alienation of property was carried out legally.

What do potential buyers need to know?

At the end of the auction, the winner must redeem the item within 10 days. If he does not have enough funds, then he can take a loan from the bank on favorable terms. Only after paying for the property does the process of paperwork for the property begin. The winner of the auction becomes the owner of the object. In practice, not all bidders buy objects. The fact is that the opportunity to evaluate the goods appears only after the auction. This is a big drawback of this implementation method.





Market situation

The last two years there has been an imbalance in the banking sector. Loans are getting less and less, and the number of collateral received on default loans is growing. At the same time, the number of positive refusals from property increased. Borrowers themselves want to quickly repay the debt, so they put up objects for sale voluntarily. According to the Federal Service of Bailiffs, in 2015, 20 times more real estate objects were put up in comparison with 2014. The situation is also with motor vehicles.

Due to the growing number of debts, banks began to use new sales schemes. VTB went under the hammer, announcing the sale of hundreds. Half of the objects were sold at public auction and the same amount through the "Unified electronic trading platform." Moreover, the auctions were held at different times.

Bidding is not the only way to sell objects. Raiffeisenbank searches for customers on its own. The most illiquid objects are sold through real estate companies. DeltaCredit and Rosbank operate according to the same scheme.

Clients of Otkritie Bank also agree to sell the objects in pre-trial procedure. The financial institution‚Äôs website provides a list of collateral. It can be purchased by both an individual and an individual entrepreneur or company. If there are several buyers for an object, then the one who offers a large amount wins. However, the auction in its pure form is not held.





At Absolut Bank, problematic objects are sold at face-to-face auction, the minimum number of participants is two. Each of them must make an advance of 5% of the value of the object. This amount is sent to the winner on the deposit from the purchase, and the funds are returned to the loser. According to statistics, during auctions, lots are getting more expensive by 20-150 thousand rubles.
