Credit card grace period

Any self-respecting bank today issues credit cards to its customers. Their main feature is an interest-free or grace period for using the institution‚Äôs money, or grace-period.

What, in fact, is the advantage?

A grace period is a time period during which money borrowed from a bank can be returned without interest. The main condition is to do this in full and before the end of the agreed period.

It would seem that a service that is absolutely unprofitable for the bank is a grace period for lending. In fact, today there are a lot of optional people. They pay interest and fines, providing banks with a profit.





A responsible borrower, having wisely weighed all the pros and cons, will easily use the money of the bank, and will force his money to work for himself (having drawn up, for example, a deposit at a good interest).

Calculation Features

Most consumers of banking services do not always delve into the issue and understand the grace period system. In fact, it is not difficult. Today, banks divide the grace period into two parts. The first is about 30 days and allows you to make transactions on credit. The second, about 20-25 days, is intended to repay the loan. As a result, it turns out that the grace period can last up to 55 days (it depends on the day of purchase).

There are only two lending options offered by banks: the period of privilege begins either from the moment of registration of the purchase, or from the date known by the bank.

If the privilege starts from the date fixed by the bank

If there is a card with a grace period of this type, the term usually starts counting from the first day of the month or from the moment the card is activated, and it lasts an average of 30 days. Moreover, all transactions are summed up, and before the end of the announced period, they must be credited to the bank account.

Such a concession is considered unstable, since, for example, a purchase made closer to the end of the month has a shorter interest - free loan term . But then the number of payments from month to month is the same. For consumers who make purchases often, this calculation option is most convenient. It is enough to remember the beginning and end of the grace period and the date of making the monthly payment. Typically, banks, in accordance with the terms of the grace period, send a monthly electronic statement to the client or an SMS message indicating the amount of the debt and the time it should be paid to the bank.

If the privilege starts from the moment of transaction

In this case, it would seem that everything is simpler. The period of interest-free repayment of the bank‚Äôs money starts from the day of purchase and lasts an average of 30 days (depending on the particular institution). But if the cardholder with a grace period makes many small purchases, there is a chance that he will simply get confused in both the amount of return and the dates.



Probable problems

They can occur if the client at least once forgot to pay off the debt or make the necessary monthly contribution. In this case, the bank will accrue interest on both the remaining loan term and the preferential term. To pay off debt, you can use one of three methods:

  1. Deposit the amount agreed by the bank in any of its branches.
  2. Put cash at any creditor bank account at any ATM.
  3. Make a cashless transfer in any convenient terminal.

Here you need to take into account one caveat. Only money deposited in the issuing bank (first option) will be credited to the account on the same day. Payments from other banks can go up to three business days.

There is also such a condition of the institution that issued the credit card, as a mandatory payment. This monthly payment is stipulated in the contract, and usually does not exceed 10% of the loan amount. Ignoring these payments, even if the borrower pays interest, threatens with penalties and blacklisting.
