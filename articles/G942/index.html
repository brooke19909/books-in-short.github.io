The GS B211 satellite TV tuner was introduced in 2014, becoming the most popular receiver in the Tricolor TV equipment exchange program. The model was created specifically to replace old receivers that do not support the ‚ÄúHD Maximum‚Äù viewing quality.

Appearance

The lack of a display has reduced power consumption. A power supply removed from the housing reduced heat and simplified replacement in the event of a failure.

The GS B211 receiver, the photo of which is presented here, is produced in a black compact glossy plastic case measuring 110 x 175 x 30 mm. The side parts of the body are rounded, which gives it a modern look. On the upper and lower surfaces are ventilation holes. On the front panel there is only one button for switching between the operating mode and the standby mode of the receiver, which is indicated by green and red backlighting, respectively. On the side of the case is a slot for installing a conditional access card.





The glossy plastic case and the lack of a display, which distinguishes the GS B211 Tricolor receiver, customer reviews call the main design flaws of the device. The censure is caused by the fact that the case attracts dust, gets dirty very much, scratches are clearly visible on it.

On the rear panel are located:

  • input antenna connector LNB;
  • USB port
  • HDMI connector
  • CVA composite video output CVBS;
  • Stereo RCA output;
  • remote control signal receiver connector;
  • connector for a 12 V power adapter

The RF modulator, which the GS B211 receiver lacks, was also not left unattended by consumer reviews. Without it, viewing on TVs only with an antenna input has become impossible.

The remote infrared receiver allows you to receive signals on a TV tuner, which is located in a place where there is no direct line of sight to the remote control.





GS B211 receiver: specifications + reviews

The main processor AmberS2 was the first processor for satellite receivers, fully manufactured in the Russian Federation. It uses system-in-case (SIP) technology, which is used in consumer electronics. Designed and manufactured at GS Nanotech, which is part of Technopolis GS.

A SIP system is a combination of several functional components in one module. In this case, combined:

  • STH206 processor;
  • GS Lanthanum cryptographic microprocessor;
  • RAM SDRAM DDR3;
  • flash drive NOR Flash.

Designed for low-cost, high-definition (HD) devices and supports secure data exchange technology. The processor has improved image quality at a minimum cost, which has found application not only in satellite receivers, but also in security systems, medicine, etc.

256 MB RAM and built-in 128 MB flash memory are another improvement that the GS B211 Tricolor receiver has undergone. User reviews are extremely positive, since such an innovation has allowed to increase the number of connected Tricolor TV services.

The flow rate is from 2 to 45 Msv / s, QPSK modulation and 8PSK.

Increasing receiver sensitivity is another innovation that the GS B211 satellite receiver boasts. Feedback from satellite TV enthusiasts confirms improved reception stability in adverse weather conditions. Of course, the right choice of the antenna and its high-quality tuning play an important role here.

The signal coding system DRE Crypt 3.0 is used.





The ability to record transmissions to an external flash media is another feature that the GS B211 receiver has. Customer reviews on this subject are mixed. On the one hand, this is what many consumers expected, and on the other hand, it is depressing for users that recording movie channels has remained unavailable. Moreover, the record can be viewed exclusively on the device that produced it.

Support for DiseqC versions 1.0 and 1.1, which was acquired by the GS B211 receiver, is positively described by the owners. It allows you to independently select and configure satellites, scan open FTA channels of other satellites in the presence of additional LNB converters, which you could only dream of before.

Firmware update

  1. Turn off power to the receiver.
  2. Place the FAT32-formatted flash drive into the USB port of the satellite receiver. The b211.upd file should be in the root folder of the drive.
  3. Connect power to the receiver. At the end of the download, you need to make sure that there are no messages.
  4. Wait for the request and confirm the software update.
  5. After the download is complete, the satellite tuner will reboot automatically.
  6. Turn off the power. Disconnect the flash drive and write the b211_lcs1_app.upd file to it.
  7. Connect the flash drive with the b211_lcs1_app.upd file in the root folder to the USB port.
  8. Turn off the power of the receiver. At the end of the download, make sure that there are no graphics on the screen, including prompt messages.
  9. Wait until the update request appears and click OK to start the firmware update.
  10. At the end of the update procedure, the device will reboot automatically.
  11. Remove the flash drive from the USB port after the reboot is completed.
