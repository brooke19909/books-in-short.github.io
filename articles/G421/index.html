Park Hotel "Potemkin", Pushkin (St. Petersburg): photos and reviews

The famous Tsarskoye Selo, so named because it was here that at one time was the residence of Russian sovereigns, was known to all. Today it is a small settlement near St. Petersburg, renamed in honor of the great Russian poet, who studied and even lived for some time in Pushkin. Five parks give a special charm to the city.



Tourism

The city of Pushkin is literally immersed in greenery. This is what tourists who come from all over the world look at the open-air museum reserve note in their reviews. Since the time when it was called Tsarskoye Selo, the city has a peculiar symbol - it is the Golden Gate, the direction of which indicates the Catherine Palace. It is beyond its walls that tourists become participants in Russian history. This is where the famous Amber Room is located. Most of all, it is not the palace itself that strikes you, but its parks - Alexandrovsky and Catherine. On the territory of the latter are located the Grotto pavilion and the amazing Cameron Gallery. During walks in the parks, tourists can see the Turkish baths, the Girl with a Jug fountain, numerous monuments that tell about victories over enemies during the reign of Catherine.

It is an amazing opportunity to get acquainted with history and the opportunity to take a walk and see the residence of Russian tsars that leads tourists to the former Tsarskoye Selo (the city of Pushkin) who stay at hotels in the city, including the Park-Hotel Potemkin. Photos, a lot of impressions and, of course, pleasant memories, everyone who comes here takes with them.

Description

This modern hotel complex offers guests not only comfortable rest, but also receive the most diverse range of services, including the possibility of holding business events at the highest level. Park Hotel Potemkin (Pushkin, St. Petersburg) is located within one of the most beautiful suburbs of the Northern capital. Many people come here to see local attractions, walk along the alleys of beautiful park ensembles, see the royal palace and just get fresh impressions.



Park Hotel Potemkin (Pushkin) is only a quarter hour drive from the airport. The center of St. Petersburg can be reached in just half an hour. The hotel has its own green area, nearby is the beautiful Babolovsky Park. Within walking distance - a huge Catherine ensemble with a beautiful palace.

How to get there

You can arrive at the Potemkin Park Hotel (address: Krasnoselskoye Shosse, 85) by bus or by shuttle bus from the railway station of the city of Pushkin, where an electric train runs from Vitebsk Station. Getting to the hotel complex is convenient from the Moscow metro station.

Reviews

A great place ... That's what many of those who came to rest in the Potemkin Park Hotel say so. Reviews of most guests indicate that there are not only comfortable and always clean rooms, but also well-working staff.



The park hotel looks equally pretty both from the inside and the outside. He has a fairly large well-groomed area. In the evenings you can stroll not only through it, but also through the nearby Babolovsky park. Many sights are within walking distance, including the famous royal palace.

As for the work of the staff, the maids work quite harmoniously: the rooms and public areas are always clean, towels and bed linen are changed regularly. Some reviews express dissatisfaction with the fact that employees at the registration desk do not speak English, which causes difficulties in communicating with many foreigners who come here to relax and see local attractions.

Meals by almost all tourists are rated at a solid "five". Breakfasts always consist only of fresh products, besides the buffet is constantly replenished, therefore, even arriving at the very last minute, you can get everything that was set at the time of opening.

The hotel pool is large and always clean. The water temperature in it, however, is somewhat high, but this is a matter of taste. Some in their reviews regret that the use of the sauna is not included in the price, and customers also complain that geographically it is far from the pool.

Many consider it a big plus that right from the hotel you can stroll to the palaces. After the noise of a big city, such a promenade seems like a fairy tale. In fifteen minutes, through Babolovsky Park you can walk to the Chinese village, and from there go to the Alexander or Catherine ensembles.

One of the drawbacks of this park-hotel in their reviews, some tourists call poor soundproofing.
