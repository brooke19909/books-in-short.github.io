History and Modernity

The first mention of Poprad is found in the chronicles of 1256. In those days, it was a group of small villages. They gradually grew and eventually united, reaching the status of a city. At this time, almost all the inhabitants of this place spoke German.

In the 14th century this and other Spissky settlements were transferred to Poland for debts. As part of this state, the city was for four centuries.

In 1871, a significant event took place in Poprad. Railways were laid here. This gave the village an accelerated growth rate.

After Poprad became part of Slovakia, it reached the peak of its heyday. Today, it is rightfully considered the most prosperous locality in the Pre≈°kovsk Territory.

The city continues to grow steadily. This is indicated by the expansion of the construction of apartment buildings. In addition, the quality of tourism business services and various services is constantly improving here. Particular attention is paid to the institutions and colleges of Poprad. Slovakia, like all other European states, is proud of its education. In this city, higher schools are represented by their departments. In addition, employment opportunities are constantly expanding here, and the conditions for recreation for its residents are improving.

St. Aegidius Square

This place must be visited by all travelers who have arrived in the city of Poprad. After all, St. Aegidius Square is the heart of this place. It contains the most significant sights of the "Gateway to the Tatras". This is the Lutheran Church, the Church of St. Aegidius, as well as the Podtatran Museum.

The square is the brightest place in the city. Here the roads are paved with tiles, and a palette of all kinds of shades and colors was used in the coloring of the houses. In addition, a variety of shops are located on the square. In them tourists will find an abundance of clothing produced by well-known brands, as well as souvenirs, home crafts and various goodies.

Interestingly, according to historical information, St. Aegis is an ancient Greek saint and hermit. He lived in the 7th century AD.

Church of St. Aegis

The date of construction of this building dates back to the 13th century. The church was originally conceived in the Gothic style. However, it underwent multiple destruction and was subsequently restored and transformed. As a result, today its external appearance corresponds to the Baroque style.

The Church of St. Aegidius in Poprad, Slovakia (photo can be seen below) is the main cultural monument of this village. The building is located on its main square.

Every year, this holy place is visited by more than one thousand tourists. Constantly come to the church and the townspeople. Visitors can go to the observation deck of the building, which offers magnificent views of the surrounding area.

High Tatras National Park

As part of the possession of Poprad is a unique natural area. This is the oldest national park in Slovakia - Tatra. Its history began back in 1949, when vast territories were allocated to protect flora and fauna in the highest mountain range in Europe, which is located north of the Alps. Today, many mountain and alpine plants grow here, including Alpine edelweiss. Images of this flower can be found on many postcards sold in the Tatras.

Among the rare representatives of the fauna, a brown bear, golden eagle and some other species live here. The most valuable of them is chamois. She is a symbol of the Tatras.

On the territory of this national park is the highest point in Slovakia. It is located on the Gerlachowski-Shtit mountain, whose height is 1655 m.

The park has more than a hundred lakes, as well as several waterfalls. There are many caves here. However, for safety reasons, only one of them is available for tourists - Belyanskaya.

Podtatransky Museum

He invites history buffs. The year of foundation of the museum is considered to be 1876. Its building is located in Spisskaya Sloboda.

Podtatransky Museum presents to its guests unique objects. Among them: the skull of a woman of the pre-Neanderthal period, stuffed animals, various sculptures and household items.

The museum has a library. Her collection contains rare ancient books, including educational ones. This, for example, grammar in Arabic, Persian, German and Turkish, the publication of laws, etc.
