Therefore, it is best to choose a comfortable bus where you can take a tour of the central part of the metropolis. Mount Corcovado

Rio de Janeiro is a city that Brazil is very proud of. The sights of this metropolis are so diverse that in order to see them all, you will need to climb to the top of Mount Corcovado. At an altitude of 710 meters is the Statue of Christ, which was built in 1931. Its height is 125 feet and its arm span is 30 meters. From this place the whole huge city is visible at a glance.

Sugar head

You can also look at the Rio panorama from a slightly different angle - from a mountain called Sugarloaf. Its top can be reached in a few minutes by cable car. The first stop is Mount Urka. There is an extensive platform where numerous bars and restaurants are located, in which the amazing Mulatto Show takes place. Further, directly on top of the Sugarloaf, there is a smaller observation deck. From it, beaches, mountains, a statue of Jesus Christ, the island are perfectly visible.

Iguazu Falls

Iguazu is a real miracle created by nature. These waterfalls are located on the border of three countries - Argentina, Paraguay and Brazil. The Iguazu River is the border between Argentina and Brazil, and it flows into the Parana River, which separates Paraguay from Brazil. Waterfalls formed at the place where these two rivers meet, forming huge cascades of water. Sights of Brazil (photos of the waterfall are simply mesmerizing!) Without Iguazu it is difficult to imagine.



Amazonia

To get to the jungle of the Amazon, you must first fly to the airport of Manaus. The population of the town consists of 80% of the Indians, which gives this place a special charm. In this locality you can visit the Museum of the Indians, the Palace of the Palaciu Negru, the Museum of Man, the Church of San Sebastian, the Museum of Numismatics, as well as numerous floating markets. Not far from Manaus is an amazing place where the rivers Rio Negro and Solimoes merge. It is noteworthy that the waters of these two reservoirs do not mix, but flow over hundreds of kilometers with colorful ribbons.

30 km from Manaus is a tourist attraction - a typical Native American village, extremely popular with tourists. The flora and fauna of this region is amazing and have no analogues on the planet. Here you can admire the water lilies, whose leaves in diameter reach more than one meter. The flora of this area has been studied so far only by 30%. Many substances that are in local plants are considered medicinal and are widely used in world pharmacology. More than 1800 species of birds, 1500 of fish and 250 of mammals live in the forests of the Amazon.



All the natural attractions of Brazil can show the country in a completely different light. The Amazon River with its tributaries is 20% of the fresh water reserves of the entire planet. Terrible piranhas, a surprisingly beautiful pink dolphin and a huge bull fish live in the basin of this river. Hunting alligators, canoeing along the numerous arms of the Amazon, traveling in the thick of the jungle, getting to know local customs - all this will give unforgettable emotions.

Despite the fact that this country has a unique nature and unusual architecture, the main attraction of Brazil is its inhabitants. They are always friendly with tourists and are ready to show guests the beauty and charm of their country.
