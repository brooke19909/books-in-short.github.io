Asturias, Spain: attractions, photos, reviews of tourists

Spain is one of the most visited countries of the European continent. However, travelers, as a rule, choose the brilliant Madrid, Barcelona with its masterpieces created by the ingenious Gaudi, and resorts located on the Mediterranean coast of this country for recreation and excursions. As for the northern regions, the relatively cold climate frightens away guests who associate Spain with the sun and luxurious beaches. It is not surprising that even those who quite often visit the Iberian Peninsula, they are practically unknown. To such "terra incognita" applies Asturias. Spain is an ancient country, and Asturias is its historical pride.





Where is some geographic information located?

Asturias (Spain) is a province and autonomous entity on the coast of the Bay of Biscay, which borders Castile-Leon in the south, Galicia in the west, and Cantabria in the east.

Its administrative center is Oviedo. Together with the cities of Aviles and Gijon, it forms a kind of triangle, inside which the bulk of the inhabitants of the region of Asturias (Spain) are concentrated. Reviews of tourists indicate that it is in these settlements that the best hotels and entertainment venues in the northern part of the Iberian Peninsula are located.

Many short and shallow rivers flow through the principality from south to north, towards the Bay of Biscay. Their energy is used to generate electricity at numerous hydropower plants.

Oviedo

As already mentioned, this city in Spain is the center of Asturias, with 220,000 inhabitants. It is the economic, cultural and administrative capital of the principality.

The city has a long and eventful history that began more than 1300 years ago. On the tourist map of Spain, Oviedo is marked as a preserve of pre-Roman architecture in the open. Among the most noteworthy old buildings can be noted:

  • Church of San Tirso;
  • the Cathedral of San Salvador, in which the kings of Asturias are buried and three of the most important relics of Iberian Christianity are stored;
  • 14 pre-Roman churches built on Mount Naranco, which are included in the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

In addition, tourists will be interested in visiting the Archaeological Museum of Asturias (address: Calle San Vicente, 5, 33003, Oviedo), which is located in the former monastery of San Vicente.



Thousands of pilgrims pass through Oviedo every year. They follow the path of the pilgrims who, since the early Middle Ages, visited the Christian shrines of northern Spain every year. Travelers who have previously received the blessing of the Catholic Church move along the route, the final destination of which is the city of Santiago de Compostela. Interest in such pilgrimages revived thanks to the writer Paulo Coelho, who described the adventures of pilgrims in his books. In each monastery, they are issued a certificate of attendance. In many families, they become relics passed down from generation to generation.





Gijon

This city in Spain, in Asturias, also deserves the attention of tourists. In terms of population (277 559 people), it even surpasses Oviedo. There is a port in Gijon, through which most of the goods entering the region pass. The city appeared in the early Middle Ages on the site of a small fishing village that existed as far back as the 5th century BC. e. Its heyday was associated with the development of navigation and maritime trade.

Although Asturias is not one of the popular tourist regions of Spain, during the relatively short beach season many people come to Gijon to relax for little money. It offers comfortable beaches where you can soak up the sand brought from the Sahara desert. To attract tourists in the summer in Gijon, various holidays are organized. Among them is the San Juan festival, during which bonfires are lit on the beach. Incendiary music is played there all night, and the inhabitants and guests of Gijon indulge in unrestrained fun.



The main attractions of the city include:

  • Roman baths, built in the second century AD, with a heating system and ancient frescoes;
  • the existing bullfighting arena, built in 1888, which seats 10,000 people;
  • Revillahihedo Palace, which houses the Center for International Art.

Aviles

If you are interested in Asturias (Spain), reviews of tourists often recommend visiting this city.

Aviles is just 14 km from the international airport. It was founded in 1085, under King Alfonso Six, at the site of a settlement known since the Roman period. Although many monuments of medieval architecture have been preserved in Aviles, from the mid-20th century it was impossible to lure tourists there. The reason was the terrible environmental situation caused by the rapid pace of industrialization. Aviles was not even awarded the title of the dirtiest city in Europe.

Only in the mid-1990s did the Aviles authorities begin to attempt to create normal conditions for the life of citizens. For this purpose, some industrial enterprises were closed, while the rest of the modernized systems that ensure their safety for the environment. However, the turning point that changed the history of the city was 2006, when during the festival organized by the Prince of Asturias, the great architect Oscar Niemeyer presented the city with a project of the Cultural Center.

After 5 years in Aviles, a structure appeared that could become one of the main attractions of any European capital.



With the influx of tourists who want to see a new architectural masterpiece, the city's economy has received a new development vector, especially since there are many other attractions that are of great interest to lovers of history and ancient architecture. These include:

  • the palace of Llano Ponte;
  • Temple of San Nicholas de Bari;
  • the palace of the Marquis Ferrera, built in the 17th century in the Baroque style;
  • Valdekarsan's house;
  • Ferrera park, etc.

Asturias (Spain), whose photo is striking in the wild beauty of magnificent mountain and coastal landscapes, is considered one of the most environmentally friendly regions of this sunny country. Kitchen

Peculiar gastronomic traditions are another reason to visit this country. The main authentic delicacies of these places are various cheeses with a delicate taste (about 100 varieties are known in all!), Fabada, which is a bean dish with black pudding, as well as sweet rice pudding. Cinnamon, raisins and saffron are added to the last. Dessert dishes also include special casadielles pancakes, which are filled with a mixture of various finely chopped dried fruits mixed with grated almonds, hazelnuts or chestnuts. Biscuits with cherries are also popular, considered one of the main treats, which are hosted by the owners of Gijon on holidays of their household.

In addition, Asturias (Spain) is famous for environmentally friendly products that are delivered to supermarkets not only throughout the country, but also in neighboring states. This is especially true for Asturian cheeses, which are produced by mixing cow's, sheep's and goat's milk. As for drinks, the residents of this region prefer magnificent cider to wine, which is prepared according to a special recipe and poured into glasses, holding a bottle over their heads.

Climate

Asturias are not as hot as in other regions of Spain. This circumstance is the reason for the relative economic backwardness of the region in comparison with other provinces of the country. After all, the basis of the welfare of the kingdom is beach tourism, the level of development of which Asturias (Spain) cannot boast. The climate there is oceanic and humid. Summer is usually warm, with frequent rainfall, and in winter the province is quite cold. This is especially true in mountainous areas, where snow often lies from early autumn to mid-April, and sometimes longer. As for the lowlands, in the winter there is wet and frequent rains.

The maximum air temperature recorded in Asturias in the summer is +36 degrees, and the minimum - 10 degrees below zero.

How to get there

Due to the rugged terrain, Asturias (Spain) has always been an inaccessible area. At the moment, the main artery that connects this region with the center of the country remains the Roman road Via de la Plata (the modern name is the A-66 motorway). She passes through the Cordillera passes. As for rail transport, you can get to Asturias by trains, passing through the cities of Leon and Santander. In addition, Asturias can be accessed through the port of Gijon. It is one of the main maritime harbors of the Bay of Biscay, and ships stopping there regularly make flights from Northern Europe to the UK and Ireland.

If the purpose of your trip from Russia to the Iberian Peninsula is precisely Pong (Asturias, Spain), those who have already been there will be the best ones to tell you how to get here. As a rule, they advise you to use air transport. Asturias International Airport is located in Santiago del Monte. From there, on the N-634 motorway, you can reach your destination in 1 hour 50 minutes.



Pong (Asturias, Spain): how to move there to live

Emigrating to the Kingdom of Spain is a difficult task. You can legally enter the country in the following cases:

  • to marry a citizen of Spain or another state with a Spanish residence permit;
  • to study at a university with the design of a student residence;
  • get refugee status
  • travel to Spain for family reunion;
  • open your own business in the country;
  • Receive an invitation from a Spanish employer
  • buy property.

In addition, many are trying to settle in Spain illegally, having entered there on a tourist visa. I must say that this option rarely works, and very often the "emigrant" is not only expelled from Spain, but also loses the opportunity to visit any Schengen zone states in the future.

Festivals in Pong

As in other Catholic countries, almost all cities in Spain regularly host various religious processions and holidays. However, in each village they have their own flavor. Ponga (Asturias, Spain) is no exception. Moreover, it is there that you can celebrate Christmas and New Year in an unusual way. On January 1, the El Girria Festival begins in the town. According to an ancient legend, on this day an ancient spirit descends from the mountains and occupies the body of a single young man. The latter begins to run through the streets and burst into houses, trying to kiss young women and girls who will meet on his way. He is accompanied by a crowd of onlookers who want to stare, what resistance will be exerted by the arrogant mountain spirit by local beauties.
