The origin of the word "exchange" as a designation for a place for drivers

By the way, today few people will tell you how in the old days the street parking of cabmen was called. And this is not surprising, because the word "exchange" today has a completely different meaning. This is not even just a gathering place for merchants, but a very serious institution where shares are bought and sold (trade exchange) or employers and future workers meet (labor exchange).

It should be noted that in Russia the word "exchange" as a designation for a place for drivers did not take root immediately. Most likely, it was introduced after the reforms of Peter the Great, because this very word is of German origin.

So in the official documents of Tsarist Russia, those places on the streets of the city were named where the cab drivers were supposed to stop. City authorities thus controlled these workers. One more name for this place has taken root among the people - the ‚Äúdeck‚Äù.

The fate of this expression

By the way, this concept has not taken root among the people. After the 1917 revolution, it completely disappeared from all documents. The exchange began to be called exclusively by enterprises of economic or social significance.

Already after 20 years, few people could answer the question of how the street parking of cabmen was called in the old days. And the cabmen gradually became unnecessary. They were replaced by cars, buses, trolleybuses, subways.
