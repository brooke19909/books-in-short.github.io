The best district of Odessa

Odessans adore their city and are unlikely to say which areas of Odessa are better and which are worse. Each of them is unique and not like the other. Some people like only the center, others grew up in the village of Kotovsky and will never exchange it.

All areas of Odessa have different criminogenic and environmental assessments. From some parts of the city you can get to the sea in 10 minutes, and from others you need to get a few hours. Let's look at the pros and cons of several popular housing estates in Odessa.

Kiev district

Located close to the sea. The area is considered clean and cultured. Here is a convenient traffic intersection. Such tidbits, places as the village of Chornomorka, Kovalevsky's Dacha and part of the Big Fountain are included in the Kiev region .

Tairova village

An area with well-developed trade. There are three large markets where people from all over Odessa go shopping. The area also has cinemas, shopping centers.





Big Fountain

This is the resort part of Odessa. Housing prices in the area are the highest in the city. There are beaches throughout the area that are packed with tourists in the summer. This part of Odessa has the largest number of night clubs. The downside of this district is a small number of schools, there are no supermarkets, roads that lead to the private sector require repair.

Tourists and city dwellers also love to stroll through numerous and chic shopping centers, clubs, boutiques. Apartments on Deribasovskaya, Greek, Catherine are rented all year round. Rental prices are high, despite this, even in the low season, tourists and business travelers always live here.

Fantastic sights of Odessa

Some areas of Odessa have such sights that the human brain simply refuses to believe in. In the very center of the city you can see an incredible building, which has the name of the house-wall. Imagine you are standing at an old house, built during the time of Tsarist Russia. There is nothing surprising here. You see how a woman hangs clothes on the balcony, and then you notice that this building has nothing but a front facade wall! One would think that this is a living sculpture and the inhabitants of Odessa are joking with visitors so much, but no! The lady picks up a basin and leaves the balcony for the door.





The whole secret is that the wall of this building was built at an acute angle to the facade. Why the architect so decided to joke is unknown. There is a version that the developer did not have enough budget to complete the project, and he found such an interesting solution.
