Slovenia: rest and treatment. Photos and reviews of tourists

The Balkan Peninsula is the cradle of not only many cultures, but also civilizations. It attracts tourists from all over the world with its unique identity, the beauty of nature, the warm Adriatic Sea, thermal springs and resorts, rich history and amazing gastronomy.





Compared to other countries of the peninsula, holidays in Slovenia can hardly be called popular, but from this it acquires its own special charm. There are not a huge number of tourists and advertised places, but silence and solitude are everywhere. Resorts are affordable and are great for families with children, as well as for young or older couples.

about the country

The ancestors of the Slovenes lived in the modern territory of the country already in the 6th century. After another hundred years, they formed one of the first Slavic states - Quarantine. Subsequently, it broke up and in different years was under the protectorate of the Franks, was part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. The independence of the country was proclaimed relatively recently - in 1991. Now it is a developing state with a population of just over 2 million people.

Geographical location is one of the main advantages Slovenia can be proud of. Rest here can be very diverse: from active sports to exclusively wellness. The country is located in the Alpine-Danube region, in the northwest it is limited by the majestic Alps, in the northeast - by the Pannonian lowland, in the south - by the Dinar Highlands and the Adriatic Sea in the west. The mild climate, the abundance of beech, coniferous, oak forests (more than half of the entire territory), together with a rich historical and cultural heritage - all this creates ideal conditions for a comfortable stay.

It is hardly possible to tell in the framework of one article about all the resorts of Slovenia, the rest of which is possible year-round. Therefore, we draw your attention to the most popular and interesting places.

Portoroz - Port of Roses





This is exactly what the name of this resort town, the most famous in Slovenia, sounds in Italian . Portoroz is located near the capital (145 km). You can get to it from Ljubljana by plane and bus. Around it is surrounded by the small village of Lucia, the medieval city of Piran and, of course, the sea. An interesting fact is that the country's only Thai medicine salon and a thalassotherapy center welcome guests here. If you choose excursion, beach vacation and treatment in Slovenia, then Portoroz is perfect for this, harmoniously combining all these aspects.

The climate of the resort town is similar to the Crimean one: with mild and snowless winters, dry and not too hot summers. One of the main pluses is the presence of a bulk municipal sandy beach. It is opposite it that the majority of the city's hotels are located.

Portoroz has been known as a medical resort since the 13th century, when healing Benedictine monks discovered healing salt. Modern medical centers of the city use it to this day, as well as royal and salt water, medicinal sea mud and thermal mineral water. If your vacation in Slovenia includes Portoroz, then you should be aware of the indications for visiting this resort. Experts recommend choosing it for people with problems of the musculoskeletal system, diseases of the skin, respiratory system, gynecological and neurological, as well as in case of overstrain, chronic fatigue.

Koper

A small resort town is next after Piran on the Adriatic coast of the country. With a length of 30 km along it, it is the largest in terms of population. Once upon a time there was an ancient settlement of Aegis in its place, and it got its modern name from the Roman Caprice. The heyday of the city reached during the Venetian Republic, becoming the largest port in this area. Since that time, the most significant and recognizable architectural monument - the Pretoria Palace, erected in 1464 - a monument of Venetian Gothic, has been preserved. The most ancient building is the Ascension Rotunda (12th century). It is here that the closest relationship between Italy and Slovenia is especially evident. Holidays in Koper pleasantly combines a historical and cultural program, clean beaches and excellent gastronomy. The city hosts a large number of folklore and music festivals.

Isola





Seaside provincial town with developed infrastructure is ready to receive tourists all year round. It is interesting that earlier it was a small island, and only in the 19th century it was connected by an artificial isthmus to the mainland. The basis of Izoly's economy is tourism, fishing, and related industrial processing of seafood.

If you have chosen Slovenia for your vacation, you can recommend relaxing with young children in Izola, a quiet and calm town with a rich history and a great beach. In addition, this place is perfect for sailors and windsurfers. The basis of transport links is the seaport. From it you can easily get to other European cities, including the nearest Venice.

Bohinj Lake





The largest lake in the country with glacial origin. In width, it reaches 1 km, and in length - 4.2 km. Located in the territory of the Triglav National Park at an altitude of 525 m above sea level. Clear waters are rich in fish, shellfish and are a popular vacation spot both for a purely beach-bathing and sports (fishing, climbing, mountain hiking, biking, etc.). All necessary equipment and gear can be rented on site. In the summer months, the average temperature is +22 ¬∞ C. You can get to the lake from Ljubljana by bus, to the neighboring lake Bled the distance is only 28 km. Holidays on the lakes in Slovenia are unforgettable and full of pleasant memories. Beautiful landscapes and clean air will fill you with strength and give you a good mood for many months.

Lake bled

The most popular tourist destination is the beautiful Lake Bled. It is located near the border with Austria and Italy, in the Julian Alps, to Ljubljana - 45 km. On the shores spread the eponymous town. The picturesque mountain lake at an altitude of 501 m above sea level is fed by the purest mountain springs. The water temperature in the summer months reaches + 18-24 ¬∞ C, in winter it partially freezes, and then only in severe frosts.

Winter holidays in Slovenia

Slovenia is beautiful at any time of the year, and that is a fact. Moderately hot summer is replaced by warm autumn and mild winter. The proximity of the Alps contributes to the prosperity of winter sports and specialized resorts. There are a lot of them, but we will focus your attention on the most popular.

  • Kranjska Gora is one of the main ski centers in the country. It is located in the valley of the Sava River, surrounded on the sides by the Julian Alps and the Karavanke mountain range, about 2 km to the Austrian border. Here you will find a typical alpine flavor with cozy little houses, green meadows, as if lost in the mountains and frozen in time. Service at the highest level. The resort offers not only climbing, skiing, snowboarding and hiking, but also pay attention to the nearby Triglav Nature Park and the Zelenka Nature Reserve.
  • Bovec is the only resort in Slovenia whose routes run at an altitude of more than 2000 m above sea level (photo below). The place is located in the western part of the natural park mentioned above, 136 km from the capital. The ski season lasts from December to the end of April.
  • Maribor Pohorje. Located 17 km from the border with Austria, this ski resort is the most popular in the country. The city is famous for its developed tourist-oriented infrastructure. High-quality ski runs, of various difficulty levels, a modern ski lift system and a variety of winter activities.
