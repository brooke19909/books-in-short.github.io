Do not lose when selling

Not only newcomers to the numismatics business, but collectors also figure out where to sell coins. You can do this in several ways: through a pawnshop, through a bank, at auction, through resellers.





Selling through a pawnshop is an option for accelerated implementation. They operate with a different set of services. Some institutions accept coins as collateral as a separate category of valuable items. Other lending institutions will accept, without the purpose of returning and repurchasing collateral in the form of scrap or jewelry, depending on the condition of the coin.

Selling through banks has its difficulties. They accept valuables in impeccable condition, preferably in bank packaging. Experts verify the authenticity of the coin and its trading condition. If the checked copy was just taken out of the package, the bank will reduce the purchase price by 3%. In the presence of external damages in the form of spots, scratches, chips, violation of the form, the bank will reject the offer to purchase.

Remember also that there is a price gap in sales transactions. It is highly probable that in a short period of time (month, year) the purchase price of the bank will not exceed the sale price of the same.

Coin trading auctions are organized on the Internet. The company organizing the auction is an intermediary and earns commission fees. The intermediary is interested in the sale of the product at the maximum price. In order not to become the owner of a high-quality fake, choose an auction with the presence of high-level specialists with successful sales and purchase experience. The convenience of an auction on the Internet is that you don‚Äôt have to spend energy searching for a real buyer and risking the safety of material values.

Where to sell coins is up to the seller of valuables.

Track the period from the time of purchase to the time of sale. Income from the transaction is subject to personal income tax at a rate of 13%. But if you own a valuable monetary unit for more than three years, the seller is exempted from paying income tax on individuals and from filing a declaration. If the tenure is less than three years, but the amount of income from sales transactions is not more than 250 thousand rubles, or the sale price is lower than the purchase price, then the tax is also not paid, but the obligation remains to file a declaration and the right to demand a tax deduction. Evasion of filing a declaration entails a fine of one thousand rubles.
