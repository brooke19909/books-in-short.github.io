Sights of Lebanon: photos and description

On the shores of the Mediterranean Sea is a mysterious country that reveals to tourists the mysterious world of the East. It is so small that it can be crossed by car in one day. The state bordering Syria and Israel has an ancient history, and the main attractions of Lebanon will be described about it, the description of which is found in all tourist avenues.

Acquaintance with a small country with ancient history

Unfortunately, the country in the Middle East is not associated with Europe among many foreigners. Travelers rarely choose this route, but in vain. This is the only Islamic state in the world where oriental customs are combined with European freedom, and a rich nightlife will appeal to everyone who is used to round-the-clock entertainment.

An original country with a rich culture will surprise even experienced tourists with a unique architectural heritage. The archaeological sights of Lebanon cause awe and delight, and their authorship is attributed not to people, but to aliens from other planets, so you can not miss the opportunity to admire unusual structures.

It is worth noting that this is a country whose beauty of landscapes causes the only desire to stay here forever. Inspecting the picturesque corners is best in solitude, so as not to break the fragile unity with nature. In a matter of minutes, you can move from winter to summer, and after skiing down the blinding whiteness of snow, get into the warm Mediterranean waters.

Some facts about Lebanon

This is a very small state, and the total number of Arabic-speaking residents is approximately four million. On its territory, long before our era, developed civilizations existed, so here you can get acquainted with various evidences of past eras.

The emblem of the country depicts Lebanese cedar, which grows only in this area and is considered a national pride. In ancient Egypt, coffins were made from it, and the Phoenicians used to build ships.

Recognized as a true paradise for all archaeologists and historians, Lebanon is repeatedly mentioned in the Bible. Sights, photos of which cannot always convey their amazing beauty, enjoy well-deserved attention of all vacationers who dream to join the beautiful.

Byblos - the oldest city on the planet

So, what to see in Lebanon for foreigners who first got into a magical oriental tale?

It is believed that the oldest city on Earth is located here, and such is Byblos. Ten thousand years ago, the first inhabitants appeared on its territory, who built temples, citadels, theaters. Tourists who visit the unique sights of Lebanon admit that they felt the atmosphere of an ancient city, in which different traditions and cultures were mixed.



Byblos is interesting because several centuries ago the first graffiti appeared in it, and Ramses II himself was the artist.

Baalbek, where the giants lived

No less mysterious is Baalbek, located 80 kilometers from Beirut. Called the "city of the sun", it was destroyed by strong earthquakes, but even the ruins of ancient buildings silently narrate the scale of buildings and the unique talent of workers.



Some temples, partially preserved to our days, were built over two thousand years, which is difficult for modern tourists to understand. The creation of massive structures is attributed to representatives of extraterrestrial civilizations, since ancient masters alone could hardly have erected giant structures. One stone block weighs more than a thousand tons, and famous temples of Jupiter and Bacchus are made of such monolithic blocks.

Miraculous monuments of the state

Natural sights of Lebanon often become the main reasons for visiting a picturesque country.

Stretching for nine kilometers, two caves of Jaita are a miraculous miracle, which is located in the eponymous settlement. The lower galleries can only be reached by boat, as they are filled with an underground river. The upper ones are interconnected by numerous tunnels and walkways, and the largest hall reaches 120 meters in height. Without exaggeration, we can say that this is the most popular miraculous monument in Beirut.



Near the city of Tannorin there is a real natural phenomenon that falls from a height of 255 meters into three caves overhanging each other, which appeared in the Jurassic period. Baatar Waterfall, whose age exceeds 160 million years, was opened in the 50s of the last century, and has since been popular with tourists. But he gains the greatest power in the winter, when the snow melts, and cascades of water through the underworld fall into the abyss.

When it comes to the most beautiful sights of Lebanon, one can not help but mention the Pigeon Rocks, which got its name due to the fact that from time immemorial birds have been nesting on them. They attract not only admiring vacationers, but also professional photographers who emphasize the uniqueness of this quiet corner in their work. Two cracked islands, lost in the turquoise sea, are considered to be a true decoration of Beirut.



We tried to talk about the main historical and natural attractions of Lebanon. The list of places that you need to touch at least once in your life can go on for a long time: the soap museum in Sayda, the Mussalayha castle in Batrun, the settlement of Bsharre, where monasteries are hollowed out in the rocks, the resort of Jounieh with Mount Harisa and many others.

The original country carefully preserves its historical heritage, and tourists seem to be transferred to the past, becoming participants in important events. It is impossible to inspect all the monuments of Lebanon even in a week, so you should think about a long rest on hospitable land.
