As the chronicler Cyril Murom mentioned?

The city of Murom was first mentioned in the annals of "The Tale of Bygone Years." Only with the help of it was it possible to find out the origin and name of this city. A long time ago, on the site of the city lived the tribes of the Finno-Ugric Slavs, who called themselves "Murom". In 988, this city passed under the control of the son of Prince Vladimir Gleb. Tourists are interested in the Transfiguration Monastery, which is called one of the oldest in the whole country.

In the ancient chronicles for 1024, the name of the city of Suzdal is mentioned , as well as the uprising of the Magi, which glorified this city. There are earlier records telling that this settlement was created from the merger of several villages. An interesting fact is that on the territory of this city there are a huge number of different monuments, and only 10 thousand people live there, but more and more tourists come there every year.

Smolensk is our hero

The hero city of Smolensk with a population of 330 thousand people is first mentioned, like the city of Murom, in the "Tale of Bygone Years" in 946 as a settlement in which Krivichi lived - a mixture of East Slavic tribes. According to the annals, Prince Oleg, who captured this city and made it his own, set there to rule his son Igor, who could not cope with his task because of his young age.

Veliky Novgorod can rightfully be considered one of the most famous cities in Ancient Russia, as well as one of its centers from the beginning of the founding date of Russia. Until 882, it was its center, until it became the city of Kiev, located on the territory of Ukraine. St. Sophia Cathedral is the oldest attraction in Veliky Novgorod along with the Novgorod Kremlin. All tourists traveling along the Golden Ring visit this city, because only there you can feel the very atmosphere of ancient Russia. At present, 200 thousand people live there.

Independent Russia

In 1990, when the first president Boris Yeltsin was elected, and the country itself gained independence and became the Russian Federation, it can rightfully be considered the Day of Russia, and the date of the founding of the state is June 12. Having passed through the thorny path, the state turned out to be completely free and began to proudly bear the name - Russia. Since 1991, June 12 has been a non-working day. At present, the president of the country issues state bonuses in the Kremlin. Across the entire territory of the country, it is customary to consider the date of the foundation of Russia as the City Day, which means that at the end of the day festive fireworks are thundering throughout the country and solemn events are taking place.
