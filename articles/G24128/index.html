Bodrum Attractions Turkey, Bodrum

Bodrum resort is located in Turkey. On its territory, natural beauties, historical monuments and modern infrastructure are successfully combined. Comfortable guest houses, unique villages on the coast, amazing in its beauty and transparency waters of the Mediterranean Sea, underwater cliffs, magnificent caves - Bodrum is famous for all this. Attractions of the popular resort are discussed below.



St. Peter's Castle

It was built in the fifteenth century. Building material - green granite. He was brought from the western part of Bodrum. In ancient times, the castle served as the seat of fifty knights and one hundred and fifty ordinary soldiers. Their task was to defend the village. The fortress is distinguished by thick double walls. It has several towers - Italian, German, English and French. The latter is the highest - 47.5 m. The walls of the structure are covered with evergreens. In addition, knight's arms are preserved on green granite .

The castle was officially recognized by the museum in 1960. You can get into it through a long wooden suspension bridge. The Museum of Underwater Archeology is open on the territory of the fortress and a rich collection of sarcophagi is presented.

Amphitheater

What else is interesting in Bodrum? The resort's attractions allow you to get acquainted with the most ancient monuments of architecture. So, the Hellenistic amphitheater was erected as early as three hundred years before our era. Its capacity at that time was considered simply huge - 13 thousand people. The steps of this unique structure were hollowed out right on the slope and faced with marble. The best preserved to this day is the lower tier. As for the upper ranks, they were seriously affected by earthquakes.



The scene is in the southern part of the theater. This is a two-story structure in the shape of a rectangle. In 1973, the oldest architectural monument acquired the status of a museum. This happened after excavations were carried out on its territory. Not so long ago, Turkcell and Ericsson specialists completed work on a joint restoration project. Many travelers who come to Bodrum seek to see this place. Sights of the resort have long attracted lovers of antiquity. Thanks to the stories of experienced guides, walks in memorable places will become even more informative and interesting.

Mindos Gate

In ancient times, they served as the entrance to the city from the west. Initially, three towers towered above the gate, and a fifteen-meter ditch was dug in front of them. At one time, Alexander the Great tried to pass through the Mindos Gate . This ruler was very interested in Turkey. Bodrum was not fortified enough to resist a powerful army for a long time. As a result, the soldiers of Macedon broke through the fortification and mercilessly dared everything in their path. During modern archaeological excavations near the gates, it was possible to find the burial places of the Romans and Greeks.



Restoration

In the twentieth century, the Mindos gates and towers underwent a major overhaul. Nevertheless, today tourists can see only the ruins of a once magnificent structure. Local authorities took this facility under their care. Minor restoration work is carried out as needed.

Mausoleum

The impressive marble tomb of Tsar Mavsol in Helicarnassus (formerly called Bodrum) has no equal in the whole world. The uniqueness of the mausoleum lies in the fact that this is the only building of the Seven Wonders of the World, fragments of which have survived to this day.

This construction began to be erected shortly before the death of the king (he went to another world in 353 BC). Turkey (Bodrum including) has not yet seen such a large-scale and luxurious project. Many ancient authors describe the mausoleum as a huge tombstone made of white marble with ionic columns. The dimensions of the building are also impressive: the perimeter is 30 by 40 m, the height is 45 m. The tomb had two floors, they were crowned with thirty-six columns. The ashes of the royal people were stored in golden urns. At the entrance to the mausoleum were large stone lions.


The monumental building was destroyed by the 1304 earthquake. In the sixteenth century, fragments of the mausoleum were taken out by the Knights-Johnnites for the construction of St. Peter's Castle. The first excavations at the site of the ancient tomb started in 1850. They were led by Charles Newton, an archaeologist from England.

Gyokova Bay

This bay with its cozy bays, wooded islands and beautiful beaches is called the treasure of Turkey. From Bodrum, tourists can go on a multi-day cruise on the Gokova on a yacht. One of the most interesting stopping points is Cleopatra Island. On it is a beach covered with unusual sand, as if consisting of millions of pearls.

Kara Ada

This picturesque island is located six kilometers from Bodrum. It is popular mainly due to its hot springs. The healing properties of local mud and water are confirmed by doctors. People come to Kara Ada who want to get rid of skin ailments, arthritis or radiculitis. There is a legend that Queen Cleopatra retained her beauty and youth thanks to the amazing mud of the Black Island.

Twenty-four water slides are installed on its territory and a man-made river flows more than three hundred meters long. Bodrum beaches

The terrain on which the city stands is hilly and rocky. For this reason, you should not count on wide sandy beaches. Most of them are pebble. Some beaches were created artificially. To do this, a sufficient amount of sand was poured on the pontoons. One of the main difficulties is that you have to overcome the difficult path along the steep stairs to the beaches. Only youth, who make up the bulk of vacationers, do not pay attention to this.





Bodrum's beaches are not empty even at night. Here incendiary discos and foam parties are held. The best place on the coast for young people is Gumbert Beach. Here they rest fun and actively. Among other popular options, we note the following:

- Ada Booazy. It features clear water. You can only get to it by catamaran, boat or canoe.

- Bitez. This beach is sandy. It is ideal for lovers of secluded relaxation.

- Gumusluk. Swimming with a mask near this beach, at the bottom of the sea you can see the remains of ancient structures that once connected the island of Tavshan and the mainland.

- Akyalar. This place is especially loved by surfers.

- Karaada. On the beach, you can not only soak up the rays of the gentle Turkish sun, but also improve your health. Healing mud and mineral springs will help.

Interesting vacation

Do you dream of spending your vacation in this city? What other entertainment does Bodrum offer? The sea, beaches and discos are not the only things that attract many tourists. You will not be bored at the resort thanks to an interesting excursion program. In the vicinity of the city there are many distinctive villages with snow-white houses, buried in lush greenery. During such trips, you can not only buy souvenirs, but also learn more about the peculiarities of the life of the Turks. During long excursions it is proposed to visit such iconic historical places as Ephesus, Dalyan, Pamukkale, Kos island (the place where Hippocrates was born).

Conclusion

One of the best places for a long-awaited vacation is Bodrum. The sights of the resort can be explored both independently and with guides. In any case, the opportunity to explore the monuments of antiquity and plunge into an unfamiliar culture will leave a lot of impressions.
