A group of stars united by one name. Constellations and the most remarkable facts about them

Constellations are guides that have accompanied a person since ancient times. According to them, people could navigate along the way. Once the constellations were those figures that formed stars among themselves. Now so-called certain territories of the celestial sphere. In 1930, the official number of constellations was fixed - 88. Of this number, 47 were discovered and described BC. But the names that they were given then are used up to the present day.





The number of stars in the visible sky

A group of stars, united by one name, is called a constellation. Thanks to the observations of astronomers, it was possible to find out that their location in the sky is gradually changing. The night sky creates the illusion of stars randomly located next to each other. About 3 thousand stars are located in the visible part, and about 6 thousand stars in the whole sky

How to get the names of a group of stars

For ancient astronomers, a group of stars, united by one name, served to ease the search for less bright luminaries. Bright stars united in certain groups. Usually they received the names of various animals. For example, Scorpio, Ursa Major and Ursa Minor, constellations of Upper and Minor Dog. Also, a group of stars, united by a common name, could get a name in honor of one of the heroes of ancient myths - for example, the constellations Perseus, Andromeda and others were named. Since the beginning of the XVIII century, the brightest of the stars of some constellations began to be called the letters of the Greek alphabet. In addition, about 130 separate bright stars got their names. And for luminaries of lower brightness to this day, researchers use letter designations.





Constellation observation

The world around us, stars and constellations will be especially interesting to the attentive observer. If you watch the night sky for several hours, you can see how the entire celestial sphere, including the stars, moves smoothly, as if it rotates around an invisible axis. This type of movement is called daily allowance. The stars in the firmament move from left to right. Stars, like the moon and the sun do, rise in the east. They reach their maximum height in the southern part. Sunset is from the west.

The largest constellation in the sky

The largest group of stars united by one name is the constellation Hydra. It is located in the southern hemisphere. The name of this constellation translated from Latin means "water snake." Hydra was discovered by the ancient Greek scientist Ptolemy in the II century BC. e. There is a famous myth according to which the constellation of Hydra is identified with the snake brought to the god Apollo by the raven: next to Hydra is also the constellation Raven. According to myth, Apollo sent a raven to get water. The raven brought the water snake as an apology for returning too late. The ancient Greek god was very angry and threw a bird, a bowl and a snake into the sky in a rage, where they turned into constellations - Raven, Crater and Hydra.

Constellations of the zodiac

The universality of the names of stars and constellations, around which the Sun makes its visible year-long path, was called the Zodiac. There are thirteen such constellations in total, but researchers use twelve of them by the number of months in a year. The division of the sky into 12 spheres appeared in ancient Babylon in the 5th century BC. e. Many people associate the Zodiac primarily with astrology. But in fact, the signs of the zodiac belong to the field of astronomy. These constellations lie on the line of the ecliptic, along which the Sun makes a visible journey 365 days a year. It lingers around each constellation for about the time that we call the month.
