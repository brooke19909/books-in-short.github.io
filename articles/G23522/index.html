Medium format cameras: rating, review of the best models, shooting features and selection tips

The history of photography began precisely with medium-format cameras, which made it possible to take large, high-quality pictures. Over time, they were superseded by the more convenient and cheaper format of 35 mm film cameras. However, now the use of medium format cameras is becoming more and more popular, even the first digital analogues have appeared. You can read more about the nuances of choice, the best manufacturers and places of purchase of professional film cameras in this article.

Medium format in photography

In the last century in the world of photography there were many different formats. But only two took root and became widespread - 35 mm and medium format. At first, all photographers used medium-format cameras, but over time, most of them switched to 35-mm film, since cameras with it were much more compact and convenient. In addition, large format cameras didn‚Äôt have automatic parts, and people had to manually rewind and rewind the film, as well as measure exposure.

  • Modern (digital) SLR cameras.
  • Dual Object SLR Camera (TLR).
  • Mirror frames with one (interchangeable) lens: SLR.

Differences between conventional and medium format cameras

The term "medium format" refers to a class in a camera that has a film frame measuring 4.5 by 6 or 6 * 9 centimeters. Medium format differs from ordinary cameras, first of all, in quality. Due to the large size of the matrix and the frame of a larger area, the camera captures more information and the pictures are more detailed. Initially, medium format cameras were used by professionals, it was from 6x9 film that they printed material for magazines and posters. The question is, why are medium-format cameras now so popular again? The answer is simple - no matter how hard the developers of digital cameras try, they still fail to achieve the quality of conventional film cameras. The camera, released in the last century, shoots with a resolution of 50 megapixels, which exceeds all the indicators of digital analogues. Of course, this is the picture that will be chosen for printing large banners, because it has simply amazing detail with minimal distortion. True, and the camera for this should be good, you should take into account some of the nuances of shooting. The medium format has many advantages and a couple of minuses. The pluses include:

  • Great information capacity. A larger frame captures more details, which means that the graininess of the image decreases, but the smoothness of the transitions increases.
  • Accurate colors and shades. Many modern photographers try to achieve "film color" in digital photographs. The medium format camera allows you to create natural colors that do not need to be adjusted in any way.
  • Focal lengths on such cameras, as a rule, are greater than on small-format ones.

However, medium format cameras also have disadvantages that often stop photographers who want to purchase them:

  • The high cost of technology.
  • A lot of weight.
  • Lack of automatic settings.
  • The high cost of consumables (film, development).

Digital Medium Format

A rare photographer will remain indifferent to medium-sized cameras. As in the case of film cameras, you get in the transition from the usual 35 mm to the medium format - this is color accuracy and high image detail. Modern cameras consist of several parts:

  • the camera itself;
  • a digital backdrop that allows you to convert an optical image into a digital one.

Instruction for use

Many photographers ask where to start? Medium format cameras have a completely different device than the usual 35 mm. Each brand has its own nuances, but general operating principles can be distinguished.

  • First of all, if you have a film camera, you need to choose the right type of film. 120 type fits almost all cameras. Medium format cameras are not equipped with automatic ‚Äúscrolling‚Äù of the film, so after you fill it in, you will need to scroll it to the end yourself.
  • Then you need to set the correct settings. Medium format cameras are usually not equipped with a built-in exposure meter, so it can be purchased separately or be guided by the standard table for the ISO ratio and aperture values.
  • After you have shot, you must manually scroll forward.
  • In the end, with the utmost care, you need to remove the film and give it to print or scan. Modern film scanners allow you to transfer all information to a digital version in high resolution, so most photographers prefer this option.
