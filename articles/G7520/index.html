How to choose a Power Bank: tips and reviews

Developers of smartphones and in general mobile gadgets focus on the operational qualities of devices. Display characteristics, camera capabilities, operating system, communication functions - these are the aspects that primarily interest users. However, the convenience of handling the phone in terms of charging also becomes a serious factor in the success of the models. And while the manufacturers of the same smartphones seek to optimize the energy consumption of products and increase battery capacity, third-party manufacturers offer an alternative solution to the problems of fast consumption of the power source. Therefore, more and more often you can meet the question of how to choose Power Bank? This is a special device, which, in fact, serves as an independent energy storage device. Connecting to such a unit allows you to charge your mobile device even without access to an outlet.





Power Bank Drive Overview

Externally, such devices resemble small disks with one or more connectors. This is a traditional form factor, but there are a variety of cases. For example, models in the form of tubes, cubes, various shapes, and even stylized versions similar to popular characters are also common. At the same time, the question of how to choose a good Power Bank from the point of view of the characteristics of the case should rather be based on the type of material used. Today you can find models made of metal, polycarbonate and plastic. Obviously, metal, in particular high-strength aluminum, is the most reliable, and polycarbonate is lightweight and practical. Plastic versions are good at a low price, but they should be protected from mechanical stress, since they are short-lived under harsh operating conditions.

The internal filling is a lithium-ion battery with a large supply of energy. Actually, the question regarding which Power Bank to choose should be decided on the basis of the volume, organization of content and methods of transferring this resource.

Volume Choice





The main parameter that determines the benefit of this device is the capacity. It depends on how many times one charge of this battery can replenish the smartphone battery. Measured volume in milliamps / hours (mAh). It should be matched with the needs of the target device. For example, if the question of how to choose a Power Bank for Iphone is being decided, then a capacity of 5,000 mAh will be enough. The standard model of the gadget has a battery of 2,000-3,000 mAh. That is, the energy storage is enough for 2 cycles with a small one. But another aspect is important here. The fact is that not always the user can be limited to 2-3 cycles. Sometimes a larger unit is also required, even if they plan to service an undemanding phone. For example, on a long trip for several days, the number of cycles can be increased to 5-6. Therefore, the potential of the drive must comply with these requests.

How to choose Power Bank by current?

Each user of traditional chargers gets used to the speed of energy replenishment over time. In most cases, it does not matter, since the session is carried out at home without haste. Much more important is the ability of the device to stably hold at least the declared charge. However, it is the speed of energy replenishment that can be important when working with storage devices. This indicator is affected by current strength. The number of amperes determines how long the gadget's battery will charge. For entry-level devices, models are recommended whose power reserve is at the level of 1 A. This, in particular, is enough for servicing smartphones. If you plan to work with tablets, then you should focus on 3-4 A. Such a distribution of current strength by the type of target devices will provide an optimal charge time of about 30-40 minutes.

Compatibility nuances

If the current strength mainly affects ergonomics when handling the power supply, then voltage and connectivity will be of fundamental importance from the point of view of compatibility. In terms of voltage, it is important that the gadget is serviced by a drive whose voltage potential is within the acceptable range for a particular model. For average phones and smartphones, this figure is 5 V. Now we can move on to the question of how to choose Power Bank, taking into account the interface. In this regard, you should rather pay attention to the equipment of the mobile device itself with USB and micro-USB connectors. The vast majority of external batteries interact with devices through these interfaces. Another question is that their number may be different. That is, 2-3 ports will already allow you to simultaneously energize both the phone and the tablet, and maybe the action camera, which is suitable for other characteristics for such a charge.





Additional functionality

I must say that banks are mainly focused on the fulfillment of a single task - recharging the charge on mobile devices. Nevertheless, to attract interest in their products, many manufacturers are experimenting with the inclusion of additional features. So, for the convenience of tracking charge in modern models, a digital indicator is provided. In addition, there are models equipped with LED flashlights. Before choosing a Power Bank with additional functionality, it should be borne in mind that it will take energy to provide the same indication and flashlight.

What else to consider in choosing?

Unfortunately, even the acquisition of a power bank with optimally suitable characteristics does not guarantee the fulfillment of the charger function in full accordance with expectations. The question of which company to choose Power Bank has already been considered - in terms of reliability, preference should be given to Rombica, Inter-Step, etc. But even in choosing among these products, you should prepare for two unpleasant nuances. Firstly, the capacity of the purchased copy may be 10-15% lower than that stated in official documents. This is not due to dishonesty of manufacturers, but may be due to improper storage and transportation of the device. The second aspect is that as you operate, the power bank will lose its potential. Even the best models lose an average of 15% capacity. You should also be prepared for this. A decrease in capacitive potential is typical for all models and it is almost impossible to prevent this process. However, it is possible to minimize its intensity, if preliminary, if possible, to perform frequent recharging sessions. By the way, the loss of energy in the idle mode is also typical for power banks, so you should not count on the effectiveness of the device if it has been idle for a long time.
