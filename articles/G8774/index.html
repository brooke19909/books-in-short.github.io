A collection order is a settlement document that facilitates the repayment of certain obligations to the bank.

A collection order is a settlement document by which a certain amount of money is debited in an indisputable manner from the payer's accounts. In other words, collection payments are a kind of banking transaction that facilitates the receipt by the banking institution on behalf of the client on the basis of certain settlement documents of the due funds on behalf of the payer. In this case, we are talking about paying for goods and services received by such a payer when crediting them to his bank account. Collection can be defined as an order of a creditor (seller) to his banking institution to receive from the payer (buyer) directly or through another bank a certain amount of money or confirmation of payment of this amount on time.

Areas for the use of collection orders



Collection orders are applied:

- upon collection of funds in an indisputable manner established by the relevant legislation;

- when collecting debts to the budget by regulatory authorities;

- upon collection under executive documents;

- within the framework of the main contract stipulated by the parties, with the condition that the banking institution responsible for servicing the payer is entitled to write off his funds without his order.
