Historical background of Penza

The city of Penza deserves attention. This is the cultural and historical center of the Volga region. Its history dates back to 1663, when a fortress was built on the banks of the river of the same name by decree of Tsar Alexei Mikhailovich. In the shortest possible time, the settlement turned into a city that became the center of the Penza province in the Kazan province. In 1796, this city acquired the scale of a province. In 1801, Penza was appointed the center of the Penza province. After the revolution and civil war, the city became the capital of the Tambov region, and with the formation of the Penza region, it was "re-qualified" in its regional center. Now more than 530 thousand inhabitants live in Penza.
