Definition





Etymology is a branch of linguistics that studies the origin of words. It is also a research technique used to identify the history of the appearance of a word in the language and the very result of such a study. This term originated in ancient Greece, and until the XIX century it could be used as the meaning of the word "grammar".

When answering the question of what is the etymology of a word, it is worth mentioning that this concept very often means the very origin of the morpheme. For example: "In this case, you need to find a more convincing etymology," or: "The word copybook has Greek etymology."

Now briefly consider the formation of this science and what methods for research it uses.

History





Even in ancient Greece, before the etymology as such, many scientists were interested in the origin of various words. If we consider the later ancient times, then etymology was considered one of the parts of grammar, respectively, exclusively grammarians were engaged in it. So now we know what etymology is.

In the Middle Ages, no significant changes were made to the methods of studying etymology. And before the advent of a method such as comparative-historical, most of the etymologies were very doubtful. Moreover, this was observed both in European and Slavic languages. For example, philologist Trediakovsky believed that the etymology of the word "Italy" comes from the word "udalia" because this country is far removed from Russia. Naturally, because of such methods for determining the origin, many considered the etymology to be an absolutely frivolous science.

Comparative historical method

Thanks to this method, the etymology was able to very accurately explain the origin of many words. Use it in our time. Its essence lies in a combination of techniques that prove the affinity of some languages, the origin of words and reveal various facts from their history. It is also based on a comparison of phonetics and grammar.
