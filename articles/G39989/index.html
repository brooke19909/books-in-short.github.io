Some historical facts about Kamensk-Shakhtinsky in the Rostov region

From the end of the XVII to the beginning of the XX centuries. here was the village of Kamenskaya, which was part of the Great Don Cossacks. It was originally located on the banks of the Malaya Kamenka River, then on the left bank of the North Donets. In the spring, full-flowing rivers often flooded the village, and after the Patriotic War of 1812, the stanichniki were relocated to the higher right bank of the North Donets at the request.





During the Second World War (1941-1945), almost the entire young population of the village participated in heroic battles against the Nazi invaders. Now in Kamensk-Shakhtinsky you can see many monuments and memorial complexes that perpetuate the memory of young fighters.

In the north-east of the village, which stands on the North Donets River, are picturesque spurs of the Donetsk Ridge. Today, about 100,000 people live in the city of Kamensk-Shakhtinsky, Rostov Region. Most of the population is engaged in coal mining, many work in factories. Young people have the opportunity to receive higher and secondary specialized education after school. Cultural and leisure activities are offered in the theater, museum or amusement parks.

Natural and climatic conditions of the region

The Rostov region is a beautiful plain expanse with vast meadows and endless fields. The landscape of Kamensk-Shakhtinsky (Rostov Region) is also interesting for its unusual cliffs and mountains, which form bizarre forms near the numerous tributaries of the Don and forest streams. There are wonderful nature reserves in these places, unique salt lakes, which are the remains of the oldest water areas.





The area lies in the steppe zone. The climate here is temperate continental: it is very hot in summer and cold in winter. As a rule, there are no severe frosts, and the snow cover is unstable. Summer weather lasts for four months (from the second half of May to mid-September).
