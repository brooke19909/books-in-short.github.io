Construction history





This work of art was invented by David Macrs and his wife Julia Barfield. Alas, the project did not win in the competition "Building for the new millennium." Undoubtedly, they expected that someone would order it all the same, but eventually decided to build it on their own, that is, to their own savings.

Financial support was provided by neighbor Bob Eling. With permission for the London Eye, architects began construction. But under the contract, as already noted above, the Eye was supposed to stand for only five years, and in 2005 it had to be removed without fail.

It is known that parts of the Ferris wheel were transported along the river to the very place of construction on special barges. After completion, it was raised to its place, but according to a special technology, it was raised only two degrees per hour.

The construction of the London Eye is very heavy, metal materials in total weight of about 1700 tons.

Design and construction





The London Eye is a ferris wheel located in a very good location in the city center on the banks of the River Thames. There are only 32 booths for passengers on it, each booth-capsule weighs a little more than 10 tons, has a round shape in the shape of an egg. Such an amount is far from accidental; it represents the suburbs of London.

The rounded rooms are air-conditioned and can accommodate about 25 people. A full revolution of the wheel takes about 30 minutes, it almost never stops, because thanks to the low speed people can enter the cabs without difficulty. The wheel can only be stopped for the elderly to protect them when entering or exiting the capsule. Externally, the structure looks like a wheel from a bicycle, as it is also supported by spokes.

In 2006, they decided to replace all the lighting with LED, which could be controlled by special devices.
