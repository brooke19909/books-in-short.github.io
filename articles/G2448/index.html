Openwork crochet jacket: diagram and description. Openwork patterns

It is very simple to crochet an openwork jacket. A diagram and description is all that is needed for the job. This beautiful and truly feminine wardrobe item is combined with many things and will be a good alternative to the usual jackets and turtlenecks. Depending on the model, the jacket can be either with sleeves or without them. In many ways, the design of the model depends only on the fantasy of the fashionista.
Knitted openwork jacket is combined with both jeans and a skirt. There are several ways to connect it, but no matter which one you choose, the finished model will amaze you with its beauty and grace. All that is required is attentiveness and a little patience. Below are detailed diagrams and descriptions for models.

Crochet red openwork jacket: diagram and description

One of the easiest ways to connect a complex and time-consuming thing is to use motives. It is much simpler and faster than knitting with a single piece of fabric. The fact is that over time, the pattern is remembered and the execution of one motive requires much less time and effort. Many crochet openwork patterns consist of several basic elements, therefore, having mastered one, we can confidently move on to the next. One of the most beautiful models is a red jacket of square motifs.



Of course, the color can be changed, but in this version this model looks really festive. To work, you need a 1.5 hook, as well as a red cotton thread. Melange yarn is not suitable here, because the motive itself will be lost.

Description of motive

The model will need 60 motifs - 30 for the front and back with sleeves. Such a summer jacket can be knitted in a few evenings. The basis of the motive is, as always, a ring of air loops. In this case, there are six. The next row is 11 half-columns. The role of the 12th half-column will be played by the lifting loop. That is, 2 half-columns are tied out of each loop. The next row is 11 pairs of double crochet knitted from one half-column, plus one double crochet and lifting stitches. That is, there is a circular increase in the number of loops in a row. In the next row, we form the triangular parts of the motif, knitting 7 elements of 3 columns with double crochets connected by one vertex, plus two double crochets and lifting loops. Between these elements - 7 air loops. The next row - 10 half columns in each arch of the previous row. The sixth row requires special attention.





In it, the square shape of the motive begins to emerge and form. The report consists of a corner part and a part that will become the side of the square. So, we start from the side. From 5 half-columns of the previous row, three crochets, three loops, and again 3 columns from the same loop are knitted. Then - 4 loops to go to knitting the corner part. Again from the fifth half-column - three columns with crochets, 9 air loops, three columns with crochets from the same loop of half columns. And so it is repeated three more times. The seventh row also consists of reports for the corner and side. So, for the side part a number of elements are knotted, as indicated on the motif scheme. These are simple elements and any needlewoman will master them. The corner part is also knitted according to the pattern. Here the technique is a little more complicated, and a new element appears - three double crochet with a common top for all of them. This row is based on an arch of 9 air loops of the previous row. Almost all openwork crochet patterns end with arches from air loops in one form or another. This motive is no exception.

Build Features

This model consists not only of full motives, but also of the corner element shown in the diagram. Such a summer jacket can be assembled both during operation with the help of a hook, and with the help of a thread, when all 60 motifs are already connected. Of course, the model for which only the hook is used looks more reliable and accurate.





In addition, related motives will not blossom and will not disconnect. It is most convenient to assemble a knitted openwork jacket according to the finished pattern.

Shrinkage and features of motives

Most motifs tend to curl, so it is recommended to steam them before connecting them to each other. After steaming, you can either put them under the press or stretch them on needles. From this crocheted openwork jacket, the diagram and description of which is presented above, it will be better to sit when worn.
An important point is the shrinkage of the canvas. Natural threads are subject to it. In order not to make a mistake with the number and size of motives, a trial element is knitted, and then it is stretched and dried. Preliminary measurements are taken on the number of loops and the size of the element. The degree of shrinkage is determined by how much the element has changed its size.

Options for connecting motives

Motives can be connected in several ways. One of the most common among beginner needlewomen is simple stitching. This method has a significant drawback - the threads wear out over time, and the product begins to literally crack at the seams.





This is especially noticeable when a conventional bobbin thread is used to connect elements from a thick thread. Another way is to connect using an irregular grid. This is a little more complicated and not always suitable. So the motives of Irish lace are combined, as well as the motives in weightless and very openwork products. Finally, the third option is the connection in the process of working on arches from air loops.
