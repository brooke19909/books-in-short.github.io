Location of Oryol

It is located on the Central Russian hill, which is located in the European part of Russia. In order to understand where the city of Oryol is located on the map, you need to look south from the capital (Moscow), the distance is approximately 380 km. It is more than 1000 km distant from St. Petersburg. Near the Oryol region are Kaluga, Tula, Kursk, Bryansk and Lipetsk. They have common borders.

Oryol is a city that is the center of the region. Its length from south to north is about 150 km, and its width (west-east) is more than 200 km. Oryol Oblast is the smallest in Russia. It also has the smallest population.

Population

The eagle, whose population is decreasing every year, is considered to be a primordially Russian city. The indigenous people, of course, are Russians. In a percentage ratio of about 95%. However, other nationalities also live on the territory, although there are less of them - 5%. Ukrainians in Orel - less than 2%, Chechens, Azerbaijanis, Belarusians and Armenians - 1%, other nationalities - 2%.

By the beginning of 2000, the population was about 330 thousand people. However, by 2014, this figure had decreased by approximately 13,000 (317 thousand).



Cultural heritage

"City on the Oka" - so affectionately their beloved land is called by those who consider themselves to be the indigenous inhabitants of such a beautiful city as Oryol. The population here honors its history and constantly supports the cultural development of the regional center. Residents respect the writings of Russian writers, such as Turgenev, Bunin, Fet, Andreev, Rusanov, Granovskaya. After all, these names are known to everyone. In Orel there is a famous cultural monument - the Noble Nest (Turgenev's estate). Here the largest source of the Volga originates - the Oka River. The city is also famous for the fact that in 1943, on August 5, the first salutes thundered in Orel and Belgorod in honor of the liberation from German occupation. This indicates the courageous character of the inhabitants.

Eagle is considered a "green" city. Both in the center and beyond, the level of vegetation exceeds 7% of the occupied area.

Monuments

A distinctive feature of the city is its literary heritage (this is the birthplace of famous writers mentioned above) and architectural monuments. In the heart of the city - on Karl Marx Square - stands a mighty horseman. This is General Alexey Ermolov. The sculpture was erected in 2012. In the Northern region there is a monument to Alesha-Stalevar. In the commercial part of the city, opposite the central department store, is the Tankers Square, which is guarded by an honor guard. In memory of the fallen soldiers, an eternal flame burns.

Eagle Areas

The city territory is divided into 4 districts: Zheleznodorozhny, Zavodskoy, Severny and Sovetsky.

  • Sovetsky district is the central part of the city. On its territory you can see the sights, heritage, squares, streets, shops and squares. It hosts entertainment events, fairs, youth competitions. This area is considered privileged, about 81 thousand people have housing here.
  • Zavodskoy district represents a vast territory with a high population density (number - more than 107 thousand people). Here is the industrial core of the city: factories, workshops, factories.
  • The northern region is considered the youngest. There are many not only factories, but also budgetary institutions. It occupies the third place in terms of the number of inhabitants; about 68 thousand people live here.
  • The railway district of Orel is famous for its main attraction - the station. The symbol of the city - a huge eagle, spread its wings - greets residents and guests opposite the main entrance. The population in this area is about 63 thousand people.
