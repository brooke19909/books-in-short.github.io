Large crochet napkin: pattern, knitting principles, application

Crocheting napkins is an old good tradition, which many craftswomen still support. The popularity and prevalence of such a decor greatly simplifies the search for the desired pattern and description for it. However, there is a flip side to the situation: crocheted napkins are so ‚Äúboring‚Äù to knitters that they have become a kind of synonym for banality. Types of Wipes

Despite the fact that the openwork circle always becomes the first association with the word ‚Äúnapkin‚Äù, there are square, pentagonal and hexagonal, rectangular, diamond-shaped and many other forms of napkins.

In the modern design, a large crochet napkin (the pattern can be relatively solid or openwork) most often remains round, but squares are also often found. I must say that decorative elements with right angles are easier to place on flat surfaces.

The basic principle of knitting napkins

The work of knitting almost any napkin starts from the center and, gradually expanding the fabric, ends with the widest range. The only exceptions are products assembled from several separately related motives.

Like a small and a large crochet napkin (the scheme can be of any shape) provides for the presence of several main components:

  • Relatively dense center.
  • Openwork main canvas.
  • Strapping.

Depending on the specifics of the pattern, the main canvas of the product may include some elements. For example, they often use the technique of duplicating stripes of a pattern. They are located at different distances from the center, interspersed with very openwork or, conversely, solid sections. This distribution of ornament allows you to effectively expand the canvas in compliance with the overall harmony of the product.

Large crochet napkin: diagram and application

Such a large decorative element, like the one shown in the photograph below, has an extremely wide scope.

Description: crochet napkins

Begin work with a set of chains of eight air loops. In the second row perform 16 single crochet, and then continue knitting in accordance with the scheme.

The central part of the pattern contains the very popular pineapple elements. They are located in several rows and are very decorated with a napkin. "Pineapples" are well suited for expanding canvases, so they are so often used in napkin schemes. The final part of a large napkin

The main decor of this large napkin is a wide border that runs along its edge. The peculiarity of the scheme is that several patterns are combined here:
