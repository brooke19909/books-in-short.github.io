What is the currency in Cuba?

This question is always relevant for any tourist. The national currency of Cuba is the Cuban peso, which is equivalent to one hundred cents.

As for the face value, there are the following varieties:

  • peso banknotes: 1, 3, 5, 10, 20, 50;
  • peso coins: 1 and 3;
  • cents coins: 1, 2, 5, 20 and 40.




Also in the country there is a special type of peso - convertible. It is equal to the dollar at the rate of 1: 0.9 and was created specifically for tourists. Convertible differs from the usual peso by the inscription ‚Äúconvertible‚Äù. Convertible pesos can pay for the purchase of goods, taxis, transport, tax departure.

Tourists can pay with US dollars only in some large hotels and restaurants. But there is always a chance that they will refuse to accept the currency.

The currency of Cuba and its exchange

In Cuba, you can make currency exchange at banks, exchange offices and in many hotels. As a result of the exchange of any currency, a tax of 8% is levied.





When exchanging US dollars, a commission is provided, the amount of which is 10% of the amount of exchange. When converting another currency, this fee is absent.

As a result of the exchange, instead of the euro, British pounds, Canadian dollars, Swiss francs, you can get a convertible peso.

Opening hours of Cuban banks: from 8:30 (some from 9:00) to 15:00 (sometimes until 15:30 or 16:00), with a lunch break from 12:00 to 13:00 on weekdays, from 8 : 30 to 10:30 on Saturday. Other exchangers for the most part operate around the clock.
