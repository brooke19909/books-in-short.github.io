General Pershing: biography and photos

General Pershing is one of the most prominent commanders in the US Army. His experience is being studied by the military around the world, quoted by American presidents, and his memoirs were awarded the Pulitzer Prize. He is an icon and role model for American officers. Recently, Donald Trump in his Twitter said in all seriousness that he was ready to apply General Pershing's best practices to combat Islamic radicalism. What methods does the US president talk about and why have serious historians and researchers ridiculed Donald Trump's words?





black Jack

It is necessary to briefly dwell on the milestones in the biography of the famous American military leader. A native of Missouri. Born September 13, 1860. His father managed to give his son a good education, and the guy before entering West Point managed to work not only on a family farm, but also as a teacher. However, a military career attracted a young man more.

Enrollment at the West Point Military Academy in 1882 was crucial not only in the life of one person, but also in the fate of an entire nation. Teachers noted the unprecedented zeal and diligence of the cadet - perhaps, it spoke of the blood of German ancestors. Be that as it may, he graduated from this famous educational institution and was assigned to serve in the infamous 6th Cavalry Regiment.

The Indians of the Sioux and Apache tribes defended their independence in an unequal struggle against the greedy white colonizers who staged the genocide of the indigenous population. The command noticed the participant in punitive operations and promoted to the rank of first lieutenant, which corresponded to the rank of lieutenant in the Russian army. Along with the promotion, a transfer to the 10th Cavalry Regiment followed, consisting mostly of African Americans. The treatment of the privates was harsh - hence the nickname Black Jack stuck.





10th Cavalry Regiment in the Spanish-American War

The Cubans rebelled against their Spanish exploiters. The American government suddenly flamed up with a desire to help the Cubans - too serious were the interests at the frontiers of American business.

Most US wars began with provocations. The Main cruiser exploded on February 15, 1898, and judging by the reports of Russian military intelligence, not as a result of a mine. The explosion literally split the Main in two - that is, it was fired from inside the ship. Nevertheless, American newspapers presented everything to their citizens in the right light. Boiling with righteous anger, society demanded to punish the "vile Spaniards", and therefore the troops were sent, which included the future General Pershing.

Despite the fact that the 10th Cavalry Regiment performed well in the famous battles at El Caney and Kettle Hill, the overall picture for the US Armed Forces was deplorable. The Americans simply did not expect the catastrophic losses that they received during the hostilities. Tropical diseases also pretty squandered personnel. The stupid paradox of that war was that the Americans were ready to give up, but they were overtaken by the temperamental Spaniards. They got the 1st place in the race "who will have time to capitulate first."

Conclusion

General Pershing stood at the origins of the creation of the modern mechanized army of the United States. Having burned himself during his operation in Mexico, he attached great importance to the loyalty of local authorities during campaigns on their territory. His name is overgrown with an incredible amount of myths and legends that political leaders and some fairly well-known media characters shamelessly use for cheap populism. One thing is indisputable: General Pershing is a soldier who honestly performed his military duty, whose victories adorned the poor military history of the United States.
