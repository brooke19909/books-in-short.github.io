First idea: bright beads

How to make hand-made markers to make them bright and comfortable at the same time? Of course, use improvised materials. We will take this advice and collect all the necessary components of the house, thereby reducing the amount of accumulated stocks. You will need:

  • A lace with a cap, such as those used on key chains, or any other dense rope.
  • Beads of different colors and sizes.

The manufacturing process is so simple that these markers can be made in five minutes:

  • Prepare a rope or cord in advance, fixing one of the ends so that the beads do not fly off.
  • From the open end string the jewelry in the form of beads, beads and others.
  • Put the second bracket on the back of the marker, it can be made of an iron ring or use hot glue. Our knitting markers are ready. Do it yourself with your own hands.

If you absolutely do not have time to create the first option, then we offer you just a wonderful alternative, the result of which cannot but please the eye. We will make bright markers from what is on each desktop.

  • Pins or paper clips: it is best if they are large so as not to hamper the knitting process.
  • Spray paint, acrylic paint, nail polish.

Some more tips

So we told you how to make DIY knitting markers. The master class is simple. Even small children can master it, so feel free to bring them to this matter.

It is important to recall that knitting markers (it is very simple to make them with your own hands) is only the first step to an ideal result, because practice also plays a huge role.

But we can still advise you a couple of devices to facilitate this process:

  • Automatic row counter. It works by clicking, thereby counting their number. Indispensable in the event that you are interrupted during the knitting process or knit on the road, and an ordinary craftswoman would greatly simplify the life of the device.
  • Tips for knitting needles. A very useful detail if you wear knitting with you, as it eliminates the possibility of slipping loops from the knitting needles. Cheap but cheerful.
  • A large pin for knitting patterns when you need to put off a large number of loops before or after work.
  • A holder for the ball, which can be made from an ordinary bucket with a lid and a small hole. This alternative is very convenient and easily fits into any interior.
