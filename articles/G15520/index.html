There is still no definite answer to the question about the reasons for the collapse of the Soviet Union. Expanding the borders of the community

The agreement, signed in Minsk, did not oblige anyone to anything, and this is the main reason why almost all the former Soviet republics soon joined it, except for the Baltic states, which suddenly felt their own European essence. So, in a historically short period, 12 countries joined the CIS. The list of parties to the agreement, in addition to the founding countries of Russia, Belarus and Ukraine, included Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Tajikistan, Kyrgyzstan, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Moldova and Georgia, which joined after some thought.

In a sense, Russia and the CIS at the first stage were related in the same way as Great Britain and the countries of the British Commonwealth after the collapse of the colonial system. The difference, however, was significant. The "popular fronts" and nationalist movements that came to power in many former Soviet republics fanned the "Russian occupiers", sometimes turning to real pogroms, and the Russian leadership at that time looked at what was happening with a strange expression on their face, seemingly approving, and slightly condemning these "fruits of democracy and the rise of national identity." CIS today

The inefficiency of the Commonwealth of countries that previously formed a single whole is proved simply. Entering or leaving it does not entail any legal or economic consequences and can only serve as a symbol of protest, as in the case of Georgia, which left the CIS in 2008 after the August war, offended by the fact that the Russian army intervened in the conflict in South Ossetia . It is unlikely that such a "revenge" puzzled the leadership of the Russian Federation, and other participants in a reputable international organization, at least there was no sharp reaction.

Customs Union - an alternative to the CIS

To implement plans for serious economic cooperation in mentally, politically and geographically close countries - the former republics of the Union, another structure has been created, with much less vague principles of membership and much more effective. From the USSR, the states inherited powerful and high-tech production of aerospace, nuclear, energy and engineering sectors, originally built for unified programs. The customs union allows them to be used at full capacity, avoiding bureaucratic barriers to the benefit of all participants in this economic interstate association.
