How to extend the life and battery level of a laptop: tips

The article contains the most important points on the methods of preserving the laptop battery level for users of all levels. What happens if I recharge my laptop battery? The answer is as short as possible: nothing. If you forget the laptop on charge after fully charged, nothing will happen to it.

Lithium ion batteries

Most modern computers run on lithium-ion batteries. They can be recharged hundreds of times without affecting the long-term battery life. Inside there is a cycle that stops the charging process when the battery is fully charged. Without this cycle, it would have warmed up during charging and could easily burn out. Such batteries should not heat up, if this happens, it means that you have a defective product.

Nickel cadmium

The older generation of laptops runs on nickel-cadmium batteries. They require more frequent repair and replacement than lithium-ion. Once a month, the battery should be fully charged and discharged, this helps to prolong its existence. As in lithium-ion batteries, if left on charge after fully charged, this will not affect their service life.

Batteries on MacBooks

Saving energy in Windows 10

In Windows 10, the power saving mode is automatically turned on when the laptop battery level reaches 20%. Depending on the settings, the screen brightness is lowered to preserve the charge level for longer. To check this, go to "System and Security" in the control panel, then in the power settings. All changes can be made manually. To save the battery, it is advisable to keep the bluetooth off at all times. If you do not use the Internet, you can temporarily put the laptop in the ‚ÄúAirplane Mode‚Äù state, which will significantly save the device‚Äôs charge level.

Extended Battery Life

To extend battery life, leave your laptop charged for 12 hours after use before you buy it. Lithium-ion can last longer if the battery level is constantly kept between 20-80%. Although constant charging does not greatly affect lithium-ion batteries, it is still advisable to remove it if the laptop is constantly connected to the network.

If you are not going to use the laptop for a month or longer, then remove the battery. If it is not removed, then the battery level should be left less than 50% before closing. If the battery is not charged for a long time, the battery may be damaged. High temperature extremes should also be avoided. Do not leave the computer in a closed car in hot summers in the sun or in too frosty winters.

Extended Battery Life

To extend the battery life between charges, you can:

  • reduce screen brightness;
  • set the time for sleep mode and the screen off timer for a short time;
  • disable Wi-Fi and bluetooth;
  • close unnecessary tasks in the "Manager";
  • close unnecessary tabs in the browser;
  • Do not block the air access to the cooler (the battery dies quickly when heated);
  • remove unused USB cables and drives;

No battery has a lifetime warranty, so from time to time old batteries should be replaced with new ones. If the old one is still able to hold 15-20 minutes of charging, you can take it with you on long trips to replace it with another in case of emergency.

Follow the tips to save battery life, avoid using a laptop connected all the time to the network, then you will notice how amazingly long the battery lasts.
