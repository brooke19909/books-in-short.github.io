Tours in Thailand in August: weather, holidays, reviews

Thailand is an amazing country with many smiles. You will agree with this as soon as you step onto hospitable land. Thais are one of the friendliest peoples on the planet. Therefore, if you have planned a vacation in this amazing region, you can be sure that your vacation will succeed. But if you gathered in Thailand in August, be sure to check out the weather at this time. And she may disappoint you a little.





Weather in August

This time is not considered a great season to relax. And if you are going to Thailand in August, be prepared for the fact that you can meet a series of cloudy days. However, this is not always the case. Sometimes rains are not observed at all. And tourists enjoy a wonderful vacation. In other cases, rain will wet guests once a week. But, of course, it is impossible to exclude those cases when there is much more rainfall. Still, August in Thailand is a season of heavy rains.

In other words, this time is not at all the most favorable for relaxation. However, one should not consider the trip to Thailand in August as terrible as some tour operators describe. As mentioned above, a series of beautiful dry days often falls out at this time.

You will not meet in August the terrible heat that occurs in winter or spring. Dangling clouds and dense clouds protect from scorching heat. This weather is very conducive to the study of attractions, excursions. Beach vacation

If you go to Thailand in August, then you will have a greater chance of getting acquainted with the flag system that characterizes safety on the beach.

At this time, the southwestern monsoons dominate. Therefore, a squally wind often rises, bringing with it a storm. The Andaman Sea is simply amazing with the height of the waves. Of course, swimming at this time is strictly prohibited. Even entering the water is not recommended. This is signaled by red flags. They cover the entire coast.

If you see a red flag, do not neglect this warning. Ignoring the ban is a great frivolity that can result in death. Often bad weather is accompanied by a reverse course. Its speed is capable of reaching 2-3 m / s.





If you notice a blue flag on the coast, then be careful. It signals the activation of marine life. Most often we are talking about jellyfish. Of course, their bite is not fatal, but it can provide a serious burn. He is accompanied by severe cutting pain. And later, an allergy can develop.

Despite the cloudy weather, you will be able to get a great tan. After all, the ultraviolet suck "does not sleep." In addition, the sun periodically peeks through a veil of clouds.

The most suitable for swimming in August is the east coast. It is framed by high cliffs. They protect from the strongest winds. Great places to relax are Nai Harn and Kata.

August is the best time for fishing. Inveterate anglers can enjoy a rich catch of tuna. The hunt for such fish is carried out on high-speed boats and motor boats, far from the coast. Therefore, before taking such an adventure, make sure that you are not peculiar to seasickness.

In addition, having arrived in Thailand at the end of August, be sure to visit the picturesque islands. Of course, if the weather allows. In season, such a pleasure costs tourists a fairly large amount. In August, travel will be less costly.

Holidays, festivals

Tours to Thailand in August will delight guests with wide walks. On August 12, the Queen's Birthday is celebrated on the island. This holiday coincides with another important date - Mother's Day. That is why this event is celebrated in every home. Thais start preparing for the holidays in advance. A few days before the event, residents begin to decorate the facades of buildings, streets.

The festival begins with concerts taking place in different parts of Thailand. And in the evening, volleys of magnificent fireworks bloom the dark expanse of the sky.

True gourmets can enjoy delicious meals by visiting the Seafood Festival in Thailand in August. On the court of exotic food lovers, many delicacies of various cuisines of the world are presented.
