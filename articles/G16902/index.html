Rublevskoe highway in Moscow

Rublevskoe highway is located in the west of Moscow and the region. Nearby is Kutuzovsky Avenue, as well as the Obvodnaya road. The bridge crosses the Moscow Ring Road. We learn a lot of interesting things about this highway from the article below.

Where there is an opportunity to get

Leaving Rublevskoye Shosse, you can get to Kastanaevskaya, Krylatye Holmy Street, drive along Osenny Boulevard. In addition, there is access to many other popular points.



Moscow is very busy in terms of transport. Rublevskoe highway appeared in 1903. A water supply station was built here. Therefore, a large road was needed here, which was immediately built. Rublevskoe highway started from the water intake and ended with a reservoir, which is located in the Sparrow Hills area. Since 1912, a bridge has been operating here through which you can cross the Setun River. In former times, Rublevskoe highway led to the cottage where Stalin himself lived. Nearby are Davydkovskaya and Minskaya streets.

After entering the capital

A redevelopment was carried out, as a result of which the territory near Moscow State University, the stretch of road between the Vorobyevsky highway and Michurinsky Avenue from 1950 to 1953 was eliminated. The changes significantly affected the Rublevskoe highway.

The map of Moscow from now on included this territory in 1960. Universitetskiy Avenue was enriched by a piece of the road between Michurinsky Ave and Mosfilmovskaya Street. Changes also took place in 1965, when paths were cut near the Kuntseva station using the Filyovskaya metro line.

Since 1966, the area between Davydkovskaya Street and Mozhaysky Highway turned out to be part of Slavyanskiy Boulevard. If you look at modern drawings, you can pretty clearly see what this road was half a century ago. There was a straight passing alley located at the intersection of the direction of the Moscow Railway going to Belarus. You can even see an indication of the point where the passage previously operated. Then the route was sent more north along the bridge.





History

Beginning in 1973, in the northern section, if you deviate slightly to the south, you can stumble upon the Aminevskaya road. In the old days there was a territory limited by Kutuzovsky Prospect and ul. Kastanaevskaya. It was also called the Starorublyovskoye highway.

Since 1987, on the street Olof Palme was replaced by the name of the piece passing between Mosfilmovskaya and Dovzhenko streets. The road was reconstructed from 1987 to 1989. Three lanes appeared for the movement of cars. From the center of the capital you can get to the Moscow Ring Road. The former two-way system was no longer used after expansion. There are interchanges between the Rublevo-Uspenskaya road and ul. Krylatskaya. Since 1996, the road has expanded somewhat. 2012 and 2013 noteworthy in that the highway operation system was comprehensively reconstructed, namely, an overpass appeared at the point where ul.
