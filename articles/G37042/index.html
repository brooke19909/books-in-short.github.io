Historic Sites of Valdai

About halfway between St. Petersburg and Moscow is the old town of Valdai. In the Russian chronicles, this village was first mentioned in the twelfth century, but Valdai received official status only in 1770. Its historical development to a large extent determined its geographical position on the main route between the two Russian capitals. Many sights of Valdai are also associated with the ancient postal route passing through the city. Many famous people who left their mark on Russian culture stopped here. Reflected in Russian literature and the city of Valdai.



Valdai, attractions and museums

The historical appearance of the district Russian city of the middle zone is well preserved to this day. Revolutions, wars, industrialization, and perestroika did not affect him so much. The layout of the streets still clearly shows the layout of the eighteenth century. Listing the attractions of Valdai, it should be noted founded in 1654, Virgin Svyatozersky Iver Monastery. It is located on one of the islands of Lake Valdai. The latter was renamed the Holy in the seventeenth century, but the new name did not take root. This Orthodox monastery was founded by Patriarch Nikon himself in the image of the Iveron monastery on Mount Athos. For many years, it has been a center of spiritual attraction and typography. On the territory of the monastery were workshops of various artisans. The district town of the Novgorod province of Valdai was famous for their talents.
