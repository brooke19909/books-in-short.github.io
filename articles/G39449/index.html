How to finish baubles and make fasteners?

Baubles are interesting accessories that not only the younger generation wears today, but also adults, quite respectable, people. You can make them from a variety of materials - from beads and beads, ribbons or multi-colored threads. Learning to weave is not at all difficult. But sometimes the question arises of how to finish the baubles correctly.

Do it yourself lock





You can sew a button on almost any bauble and make a buttonhole on the other end. If the bracelet is very wide, you can make several locks of this type, placing them at an equal distance. Another way to finish the baubles is to use ready-made fittings. Simply select a lock that is suitable in shape and size in the store and complete the product. For flat products, the end with a regular velcro for sewing is also suitable. There is another interesting idea - to weave a buckle into the bracelet and fasten the second free end of the product to it.

How to finish baubles without locks?



If you are going to tie a bracelet on your hand and not take it off, just braid two braids and tie them together on your wrist. You can leave the ends of the threads loose, tie a couple of knots and fix the bracelet on your hand. If you don‚Äôt know how to finish a bauble made of floss and fix it, try to make it solid. In this case, the beginning is intertwined with the end of the product so that the palm passes through the bracelet. The bauble is removable, but on the wrist it will be located freely. In order to combine the beginning and the end, it is enough to fold the free ends of the product together towards each other. Next, you need to take a new thread, fix it at the beginning of this section. Then just wrap the bracelet or tie it with decorative knots. Such a fastener will be very durable, it is suitable for both volumetric and flat bracelets. Starting to weave a bauble, be sure to leave at least 10 cm of spare thread or ribbons. It is with these ends that you can fix the finished decoration. If the lock needs to be woven, pass the initial thread (or threads) through it and begin to string beads or knit knots according to the scheme. There is another interesting idea about how to finish a bauble of ribbons: tie the ends with a knot and leave the hang down free. This fastening looks most effective when using thin ribbons.
