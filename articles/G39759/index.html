Cardigan History

Cardigan began its history with a man. In 1854, during the Crimean War, British General James Cardigan added warm knitted button-down sweaters to his soldiers‚Äôs wardrobe. They were without collars and worn under soldier's uniform. These clothes reliably protected from winds and frost.

The legendary Coco Chanel breathed new life into this forgotten men's clothing item. In 1918, she created the first suit for women, which consisted of a skirt and a cropped black cardigan with patch pockets. This innovation captivated the fairer sex. Since then, cardigans have remained in the wardrobe of women and are still very popular.

What is needed

Of course, it is necessary to choose high-quality yarn, knitting needles, a pattern and buttons (if their use is necessary). Yarn should be of high quality. The general appearance of the product and your image as a whole depend on this. Experts recommend giving preference to natural yarn (cotton, wool). When choosing yarn, pay attention to the label. On it, the manufacturer indicates the recommended size of the needles.

It is better to study the scheme and knit a pattern in advance. If you are satisfied with the result, you can start making calculations for the future product.

Cardigan Length

Deciding to make a knitted cardigan with knitting needles, diagrams and descriptions are not enough. It is necessary to determine the length of the product. So, in what images is it better to use cardigans of various lengths.

  1. Cropped cardigans are ideal for office and strict silhouettes. They go well with sandals or low shoes at medium heel height.
  2. Knee-length cardigans are versatile. They are suitable for any height of the heel, they will look beneficial even with moccasins and ballet shoes.
  3. The maximum length will look perfect with over the knee boots or stilettos.

Some models are deservedly called a knitted coat. And no wonder, because a large, dense and warm knit warms perfectly in the fall. And if you sew a lining with insulation, it really can replace the autumn outerwear.
