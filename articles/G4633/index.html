Piazza di Spagna in Rome: photos, hotels, how to get

Piazza di Spagna in Rome is one of the most famous and picturesque in the Italian capital. Her name in her native language sounds like Piazza di Spagna. There are many attractions in this square. This is the fountain of Barkaccia, the temple of the Trinity on the mountain, a statue of the Immaculate Virgin. Piazza di Spagna in Rome: how to get there and what's next

It is located in the city center, in an area called Campo Marzio (Champ de Mars). Metro line A leads here. Next to the square is Via Condotti shopping avenue, as well as Happy Street, where Nikolay Gogol lived and wrote the first volume of the novel Dead Souls. And in the first coffee house in Rome, they enjoyed Stendhal, Goethe and Andersen. At one of the corners of the square is the Palace of the Propagation of Faith - the property of the Holy See. In the 16th century, it was the private residence of Bishop Amelia. Now there is the Missionary Museum. From here it is easy to get to other interesting places in the Italian capital - for example, the Trevi Fountain and Villa Borghese.



Palace and Column

Piazza di Spagna in Rome got its modern name after the embassy of this country was opened here. Since 1620, there has been a palace. It is called the "Palazzo of Spain." The kingdom's ambassador to the Vatican lived here. At that time, the palace was on the periphery of the city, but then gradually turned out to be in the center. Before him, in the nineteenth century, a marble column was built in honor of the adoption by the Roman Curia of the dogma of the Immaculate Conception. At the top of this pillar is an 11-meter bronze statue of Madonna by Giuseppe Obichi. She is surrounded by images of Moses, David, Isaiah, and Ezekiel. Every year, the pope arrives here, decorates the head of the sculpture with a wreath with the help of a team of firefighters and stairs, and also says a special prayer.

Fountain

In the center of Piazza di Spagna in Rome is the "Boat". "Barkaccia" is the name of a small, but very famous fountain. It is also built in the Baroque style, and its author is one of the most outstanding sculptors of the time - Bernini Sr. Ordered the fountain Pope Urban Eighth. The history of this architectural structure is interesting. In 1598, such a severe flood occurred in Rome that the area was completely flooded, and in the middle of it a boat ran aground. The sculptor placed a longboat in the center of the fountain. It is as if drowning in the waters. This was done as a reminder of the disaster. Jets flow from the stern and bow of the sculpture. Water in the fountain comes from the ancient aqueduct "built by the slaves of Rome," as Mayakovsky wrote. It is called Aqua Virgo.
