Surrey, United Kingdom: photos, attractions

The main territorial and administrative units of Great Britain are counties, which can be compared with regions in Russia. Each of them is of particular interest to travelers who want to get to know better the country of centuries-old traditions.

A cozy place located near London

Surrey is a small county located in the southeast of the state, which includes 11 administrative districts consisting of dozens of cities. It is no coincidence that its name translates as "southern ridge", because the picturesque hills of the North Downs mountain range divide the territory into two parts. Surrey is only half an hour from London, and tourists often set off for a new experience in a quiet corner famous for pastoral landscapes.

Prosperous county

The rural suburbs and to this day retains vast arrays of pristine nature. More than 70 percent of its territory is located in the so-called green belt of England, and this circumstance imposes certain restrictions on construction in the county of Surrey. Nice thatched-roofed houses, old cottages, Georgian buildings, and modern, spacious residences with tennis courts and pools evoke a gamut of feelings among holidaymakers enjoying the romance of the English heartland.

For several decades, a cozy corner has been at the top of the list of the best places to live in the country. It is no coincidence that wealthy British buy real estate here, because the inhabitants of the green oasis, whose number has exceeded one million, are guaranteed a high quality of life and its safety.

The county with the highest concentration of millionaires is one of the most prosperous. The offices of world famous brands such as Canon, Toshiba, Toyota, Procter & Gamble, Colgate, Palmolive are located here.

Marvelous park

Another beautiful garden can be found in Surrey, UK. Luzley Park is a series of emerald "rooms" that are fenced off by hedges from yew. A picturesque attraction that appeared around a luxurious mansion built in the 16th century, unfortunately, until recently could not please the eyes of tourists. The declining park, divided into several parts, was restored only in the 90s of the XX century.

Visitors to the Rose Garden are delighted to see wonderful flowers brought from different parts of the world. And the greatest admiration is the metal gazebo with a huge pot, twined with dozens of species of the Queen of Flowers. Plants blooming in various shades of a snow-white palette are planted in the White Garden, and rare species of plants can be admired in the decorative garden.

Ancient landmark

It is worth mentioning the village of West Clandon (Surrey, England), because it was here that a giant park with trimmed shrubs and trees is spread. The surrounding modest mansion in the Palladian style, it is famous for its picturesque grotto, located in the shade of plants. The project of a beautiful garden with hedges, a circular pool and statues was developed in 1781.

And the main highlight of Clandon Park is recognized as a unique prayer house belonging to the Maori tribe and brought from New Zealand. Decorated with bizarre patterns, it is the only structure of its kind in England.

Abbey ruins

In Farnem, a small town in Surrey, centuries ago, the Cistercian Abbey of Waverley was located, which existed until the middle of the 16th century. Founded in 1128, it was surrounded by canals of a local river. After several floods, Henry VIII ordered the dissolution of the monastery, which received serious damage.

Now you can admire the mysterious ruins lying in the thick grass. The ruins declared a conservation area are protected by the state. They are definitely worth a look, because they not only look like sets for historical films, but really served as the location for the filming of numerous films.

Interesting Facts

Surrey is considered one of the richest. When reserves of black gold were discovered on its territory, local residents opposed oil production, wishing to preserve the pristine beauty of their beloved corner. In addition, they were supported by well-known geologists, concerned about a series of small earthquakes. They are afraid of abnormal seismic activity, which was the result of human activity.
