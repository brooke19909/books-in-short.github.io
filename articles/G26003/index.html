Sights of Serbia - places that you cannot forget

Sights of Serbia ... What do we know about them? After all, not so long ago this state was not at all considered a popular tourist destination, and a few travelers, if they got here, are more likely by chance. And very much in vain, because it is here that there are many historical monuments. Despite numerous wars, most of them, by the way, are in good condition.

It is safe to say that medieval monasteries, ancient buildings in cities and unique wildlife complexes - all this is of great interest to tourists today.

Section 1. Sights of Belgrade (Serbia)



It is unlikely that anyone will decide to challenge the fact that the city of Belgrade is rightfully considered the heart of Serbia. It is filled with an amazing historical atmosphere. Avenues, squares, alleys - everything in this village can tell inquisitive travelers about the heroic past.

In the center of Belgrade, you can see the oldest European fortress made of white stone and bearing the name Kalemegdan. Worth a visit and located inside the War Museum. The medieval gate has survived to our time. Today on the territory of the fortress are Muslim graves and Orthodox churches.

Not far from the ancient fortification is the old part of the city. It is here that not only museums and palaces are now concentrated, but also presentable restaurants. The colorful quarter of Skadarlie, enchanting with its ancient atmosphere, the Ada Siganliya park, the remains of fortifications, the construction of which dates back to different historical eras, is only part of what a magnificent city can offer its guests.

Section 2. Sights of Serbia. The surroundings of the capital



You should definitely visit the surroundings of Belgrade. Why? Yes, because here travelers at any time of the year and in almost any weather will definitely find something to do. For example, not far from the capital, in the village of Smederevo, there is an amazing plain fortress of Brankavich (the largest fortress of this kind in all of Europe).

In Kragujevac is an old cathedral, once built in the Byzantine style. In the medieval monasteries of Mileshev and Zhich, tourists will definitely feel the amazing atmosphere, which is distinguished by special silence and harmony.

Priepolye Monastery is a place of storage of the relics of St. Savva from Tarnov. Here you can also see the "White Angel on the tomb of the Lord" - the world famous fresco.

Section 3. Sights of Serbia: Nis, Novi Sad and Subotica



The city of Nis is remarkable for its amazing fate, which was due to its unusual location: at the crossroads between western and eastern countries. And modern Serbian children are still enthusiastically listening to fairy tales and legends associated with this region and preserved to this day.

Ancient monasteries, amazing world-famous buildings (The Death Gate, Oars Tower), the early Christian crypt in the vicinity of the city, the Byzantine and early Christian basilicas, thermae - all this annually attracts many travelers keen on studying the past.

But the amazing sights of Serbia do not end there. In the capital of Vojvodina, the city of Novi Sad, is the Petrovaradin fortress.

The city, as it were, is specially organized so that travelers would like to visit it again and again. Currently, in this village there are magnificent high-class hotels, equipped in accordance with all the requirements of modern tourists, and you can have a tasty and inexpensive lunch in one of the restaurants decorated in a medieval style.

In Novi Sad, it is recommended first of all to visit the museums of the Revolution, Painting and Archeology. Of great interest is also a visit to the Franciscan monastery of St. Ivan Kapistran and the Petrovaradin Military Hospital.

In Subotica, which is located in the north of the country, not far from the Hungarian border, half of the population, except for Serbian, also speaks Hungarian. However, English is also very common, especially among young people. At the gates of the city, the incomparable steppe of Pusta begins. No wonder the city of Subotica attracts the attention of many tourists. The impression will be left by visiting the Old Town Hall, the art gallery in Lenin Park, the Episcopate of Subotica, the Gothic cathedral, the Gradska Kuch complex and the Reichl Palace.

As you yourself understand, for those who want to not only get acquainted with history and architecture, but also just have a good rest, a country like Serbia is the best suited, the sights, photos and souvenirs of which will not leave indifferent even the most experienced traveler.
