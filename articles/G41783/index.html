Beautiful cities of southern Italy

Southern Italy is a unique territory with its own history and culture. It includes not only the southern regions, but also several large islands. Everyone who begins to study this country will find out that all Italians are divided into northern and southern. After delving into the culture of Italy, you can make sure that these two populations are really very different from each other.

The only thing they are similar to is the tourist value for visitors. To visit the country and not be inspired by the spirit of its southern territories will be a great loss for everyone. Therefore, it is necessary to determine which cities of southern Italy need to be visited.

Naples - the most important city of southern Italy

This large, noisy, vibrant and contradictory city, filled with historical sights, does not leave indifferent any tourist. Among all the names of cities in southern Italy, it is Naples that is most often heard by most visitors. Therefore, each guest of the southern coast first goes there.

Naples is a sunny, warm climate, hospitable residents, as well as ancient architecture. The most beautiful part is its eastern territory. It is there that overlooks Pompeii - the remains of the ancient city, buried under the volcanic ash of Vesuvius. You can look down on Naples, as well as enjoy the ancient architecture from the Kamaldoli Monastery, which is located in the northwestern part of the city on the Flegrei Fields.

Positano is an Italian summer resort town

It is located just 60 km from Naples on the coast of the Gulf of Salerno. This is a small but very picturesque city in the south of Italy, which looks more like a cozy village. Positano is completely shrouded in greenery, due to its favorable location on a cliffside it offers its visitors stunning views of the sea. This beautiful resort is located at the very base of the Mediterranean mountains, hiding it from the cold winds of the north. In Positano you can find everything that is so necessary for a cultural holiday. Medieval streets, old colorful houses, going down to the coast, picturesque views, beautiful nature - all this makes the city a real paradise for every tourist.

Thinking about which cities of southern Italy to visit for a beach holiday, it is necessary to pay attention to Positano. After all, most of the country's guests choose this resort because of the white beaches. It is also important that the traffic in the center of Positano is fully equipped in the pedestrian zone. This gives the city coziness and recalls its ancient origin.

Puglia region and the city of Bari

Italians call this region the pearl of southern Italy. Its capital is a town called Bari. Apulia is washed by the Ionian and Adriatic. In addition, this region has a very long coastline. The whole territory is dominated by flat terrain, which effectively echoes the coasts and cliffs of rocks.

Bari is considered not only a resort town in southern Italy, where you can relax on the beach, but also its most important historical and cultural center. It is there that the relics of St. Nicholas are located. All tourists celebrate the amazing cuisine of Bari, which is famous throughout the Mediterranean. Therefore, visiting this town, you can enjoy not only the most beautiful views and medieval architecture, but also gastronomic delights.

The unique town of Alberobello

This settlement is located in southern Italy near Bari County. Alberobello is known worldwide for its unique architecture, which makes it unique among other cities in southern Italy. It was built by farmers in the XVI century. The main feature of the city are considered local buildings, called "trulli". Most tourists come there just to look at these small, but amazing and beautiful houses.

At the moment, the number of residents of Alberobello consists of 11 thousand people. The annual flow of tourists allows local residents to make good money by providing guests with accommodation, selling souvenirs and groceries. The cost of living in a hotel room of the city is at least $ 80 per day.

Calabria Region

It can be called one of the most environmentally friendly and picturesque areas of Italy. Due to the poorly developed economy in Calabria, the cleanest sea, snow-white beaches and dense pine forests. They go to the cities of this region for unity with nature and, of course, for the sake of a beach holiday.

Tropea is considered one of the most amazing and beautiful cities in Calabria. It is very small and located on a cliff of rock. Tropea was founded by the Greeks, and now is one of the most visited Italian cities.

You can enjoy the sea coast in Soverato. This city of southern Italy is located on the coast of the Ionian Sea. The stunning wildlife of the mountains, high cliffs and white beaches make Soverato attractive to all visitors to the country. Near the city there are a huge number of small villages with wide sandy beaches. Fans of a quiet holiday can choose the most affordable and picturesque place to spend time.

Islands of southern Italy

You cannot visit all the beautiful cities of southern Italy without going to explore the most picturesque islands of this part of the country. Traveling through them can be the final part of getting acquainted with the culture and sights of Italy. The islands are always clear sea, mild climate and healing air. In addition, it is in these areas of southern Italy that tourists can enjoy hot thermal springs that attract not only Italians from all over the country, but also lovers of such procedures from around the world.

When planning to visit the islands of southern Italy, first of all, you need to go to Sicily. This is the largest island in the Mediterranean, washed by three seas at once and includes many more small islands located nearby. An unforgettable trip will be a visit to the resorts of Sardinia. It is there that a beach holiday is perfectly combined with interesting excursions. Most of the islands of Sardinia contain a huge number of archaeological and natural reserves.

Fans of healing holidays with their families will love the island of Ischia. It is located in close proximity to Naples. But unlike the bustling city, this island is very quiet and calm. Therefore, it can be safely recommended for families with children. To enjoy the warm water of the sea, it is better to go to Ischia from May to September.

Capri is considered a more lively and bustling island of southern Italy. This beautiful place is suitable for young companies who want to not only lie on the beach, but also lead a vibrant nightlife. There are two cities on the island: Capri and Anacapri. In Capri there are a lot of bars, restaurants, shops and all kinds of entertainment venues. Anacapri will appeal to those who prefer a more secluded and relaxing holiday.
