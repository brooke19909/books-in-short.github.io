Egyptian writing icon: what is it called and what does it look like

Ancient Egypt. Only one name of this country evokes a feeling of mystery and mystery, mysticism and some kind of magic. Many legends and myths, riddles and adventures are associated with this edge of the deep Nile. Who just did not want to seize the legendary treasures of the pharaohs? Who did not dream at least once in his life to visit the cradle of human civilization and touch the wall on which the Egyptian letter icon is knocked out? Nile Valley Culture

This country is very different from other ancient civilizations in its mythology, worldview, religion and culture. The Egyptian letter icon is also very different from the letters of the ancient world, Phenicia, Mesopotamia. Until now, scientists puzzle over the true purpose of the monumental pyramids. Writing system

High culture in the country required the development of writing. And it appeared in the Nile Valley around the fourth millennium BC. First, each icon of the Egyptian script displayed the subject that it signified. For example, the sun was depicted in a circle with a dot in the center, water - in several wavy lines and so on. But it is difficult to convey the name of a god or pharaoh with an image. Therefore, the Egyptians found a way out - they divided the word into syllables and for each word they selected the appropriate character. For example, if a person needed to write the word grain (peret), then the first syllable was the icon of the Egyptian letter designating the house (per).

It is worth noting that the system was quite complex, so the scribes enjoyed special honor at the court of the pharaohs. But for this they had to study long and hard.



Materials for books

As mentioned earlier, the icon of the Egyptian script has a simple name - a hieroglyph, and the system itself is called hieroglyphic. Now I want to dwell in more detail on what the inhabitants of the Big Hapi Country used to create their books. The letters were knocked out on stones and rocks, on the walls of temples, palaces and tombs, on pottery, metal plates, wooden planks, and canvas. But the most common material was papyrus, which grew on the banks of the sacred river.

What are the hieroglyphs talking about?

An interesting question is why the Egyptian writing icon was needed. In ancient Egypt, they recorded not only household expenses, for example, how many slaves were involved in construction, how much and what kind of food they were given, how many coins were paid for one or another product. With the help of hieroglyphs knocked out on stones, people conveyed to us their aspirations and hopes, spoke about their beliefs, about sadness for a dead person. There are also literary works, fairy tales, heroic stories about distant countries, about the hard work of the common people. Often, archaeologists found letters stating the cruel grievances and injustices that poor people are forced to endure from the nobility. But most often with hieroglyphics they recorded hymns to the gods, wishes for the dead, songs that helped the soul go into the afterlife.



The most difficult decryption

It is worth noting that the Western world did not know for a long time what each icon of the Egyptian script means. The answer to this question was found late, after the reign of Napoleon Bonaparte. A young and talented commander, whose idol was Alexander the Great, dreamed of conquering the planet. He carried music with scientists and artists, researchers and historians. So Europe became acquainted with the culture of Egypt. Then the Rosetta stone was discovered , which helped Jean Champollion, a genius expert on ancient languages, solve the mystery of hieroglyphs. Years of painstaking work - and dead stones spoke from the depths of centuries! And today, everyone can independently read mysterious letters.
