TOP tours of Golden Prague

From ancient times Prague was considered not to be a craftsman and merchant city, favored by artists and scientists, a city of aristocrats and the poor. Prague was the capital city of Czech kings.

A tourist of the Prague Troika hotel, who is going to get acquainted with the sights of the City of a hundred spiers in 1 - 2 weeks, naturally prepares a plan for their visit in advance. Let's try to compile it by analyzing the reviews of guests of Libuse 3 * (Prague).





It is logical to begin your acquaintance with Golden Prague with the objects of the road, called the Royal Way. Its length is 3.8 km. It starts from the Republic Square, continues along Tseletnaya Street, passes along Old Town Square, and then goes along Karlovaya Street to Crusader Square and ends at St. Vitus Cathedral - one of the main relics of the Czech Republic. Imagine: 23 Czech kings and 27 queens have walked this path at different times!

You should also see firsthand Vysehrad (coming out at the metro stop of the same name), the first-born center of the Czech state created in the 10th century. There is something to see here (Peter and Paul Basilica, casemates, gallery, Gothic dungeon, cemetery. In fact, it is a carefully preserved fortress with the best panoramic view of Prague.

It is advisable to visit the Old Town Square at noon to see and hear a real man-made miracle - the Prague chimes. There is also a monument to Jan Hus, the town hall, the Church of St. Nicholas, the Gothic Church of the Virgin Mary.

About Currency Exchange

Tourists leaving for Prague do not have to worry about purchasing crowns at home. Tourists residing in Libuse 3 * have no difficulty in changing their currency. The exchanger is located very close to the hotel. Its exchange commission is 50 kroons or 2% of the amount exchanged.





In addition, the whole center of Prague is literally dotted with exchangers. However, among them should be chosen. Do not fall for the course that you see on the nameplate of the exchanger. In fact, it is the most profitable among those offered to customers. Czech bankers have learned to change different amounts of currency at different rates. Therefore, before a transaction, you should definitely ask the cashier, for example, what amount in kroons he will give out when exchanging 50 ‚Ç¨. Tourists from other countries who are in the capital of the Czech Republic, for the sake of completeness, try to try the dishes of the local cuisine. They are usually tasty and satisfying. Guests of the hotel under consideration are no exception. What did they taste in the restaurants of Prague?
