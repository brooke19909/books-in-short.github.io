How to get from Florence to Venice - features, methods and recommendations

Italy is a European country that is famous as the birthplace of pasta and pizza, delicious wine and fashion trends. Italy has incorporated all natural objects: seas with picturesque coasts, lakes, mountains, dangerous slopes for ski slopes. One of the most famous cities of this country is Florence and Venice. But how to get from Venice to Florence? First, understand why.

The beauties of Florence

Narrow medieval streets with fortresses have been preserved here. However, the dominant architecture of the Renaissance - the heyday of the city. There are three main attractions:

  1. Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore.
  2. Giotto's bell tower.
  3. Signoria Tower on the Palazzo Vecchio, which belonged to the medieval city hall.

The historical center is Piazza Signoria with a collection of sculptures: "David" by Michelangelo, "Perseus" by Cellini. It was on this square that the most important and major events of the city took place. The main cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore is the third largest in the world. Next to it rises the bell tower of Giotto, decorated with multi-colored marble.

The most famous church is the gothic basilica of Santa Croce. Florence has a picturesque promenade with ancient bridges. The oldest of them - Ponte Vecchio - houses jewelry shops.

Florence retains its historical appearance, so it is an open-air museum city.

Inimitable Venice

Venice is built on 122 islands connected by 400 bridges. Monument city founded in the 5th century BC, where all the buildings are monuments of architecture.

Most of the streets are narrow, you can rest your hands on the opposite houses, and the height of the building is 7 floors. There are no coasts at the canals; in this regard, houses stand in the water. A large canal 4 km long runs through the whole city. It flows to the central square - San Marco. Here are the Basilica di San Marco Cathedral and the famous Palazzo Ducale Palace. A huge number of pigeons live on the square, who were brought from Egypt specifically as a gift to the Doge's wife.

In St. Mark's Square you can also see the Doge‚Äôs Palace itself, a magnificent building for noble people. Nearby is the Bridge of Sighs, which is made in the Baroque style. The name of the bridge is related to its history. Here the prisoners were taken to execution.

Today, scientists often argue that due to the number of canals and the structure of the earth, Venice can go under water for several decades. However, this fact attracts tourists even more.

Distance between cities and ways to get there

Venice and Florence are the two main points on the map of Italy, so the question of how to get from Florence to Venice should be resolved. Cities are separated by 270 kilometers, which can be overcome in different ways:

  1. By plane.
  2. By train.
  3. By bus.
  4. By car.

Depending on the budget, convenience and travel time, you can choose the most suitable option for you. This information is valid for two directions: Venice-Florence and vice versa.

Airplane: is it worth it?

How to get from Florence to Venice? Of course, buy plane tickets.

Venice has an international airport, but there is no airport in Florence itself, it is a couple of kilometers in the vicinity of Pisa, since it is the main airport of Tuscany. But do not rush to look for tickets; there are no direct flights between cities. The fact is that travel time by plane takes about half an hour.

Although it is possible that there will be non-stop flights, since a solution with a stop increases not only travel time to three hours, but also the cost of the ticket.

But if you decide to still use this type of transport, then we present to you the flight schedule for the direction "Florence - Venice".

11:50 - departure from Florence, at 14:30 arrival in Venice. Travel time: 2 hours 40 minutes with a change of 45 minutes in Rome. Airline: Alitalia SPA Ticket price - 9 000 rubles, this price does not include baggage, only hand luggage.

There is another plane from this airline with the same route. At 15:25 it flies out from Florence, at 18:45 it lands in Venice.

Of course, this option is not very convenient and for a budget tourist is not suitable. So how to get from Florence to Venice on your own?

On rails we rush towards a fairy tale

The most optimal and popular mode of transport is the train. Many tourists, and Italians themselves, choose the Florence - Venice train. Reviews of tourists are mostly positive, they highlight the price, speed and comfort. But more about everything.

More than a dozen high-speed trains run between cities daily. Namely, from 8:30 to 19:25, 14 non-stop trains. Travel time is 2 hours. Ticket price - 20-30 euros (1500-2000 rubles).

How to get from Florence to Venice by train? There are several transport companies that travel along this route. These are Trenitalia and Italo.

The first option is a state-owned company that provides trains with and without transfers. Of course, it is most profitable to buy a ticket for a high-speed train. Its travel time is 2 hours 5 minutes, and the cost is 30 euros. There are three classes of tickets: base, economy and super-economy. SuperEconom does not imply a change or cancellation of the reservation. Economy allows you to change your reservation before departure. Buying a base class ticket, you have all the privileges. The price of these ticket classes varies depending on the selected train and seasonality. And the gap between prices is 10-20 euros. Also on the trains of this company there is a division into economy and business class. The business class is usually 10 euros more expensive and includes, in addition to space, drinks and snacks.

Italo also provides the same ticket division with different privileges for changes and cancellations. However, you can change the data in the budget ticket itself. For example, you can change the name for one euro. Also, tickets are divided according to the comfort of movement, width and fabric of seats. It is important to note that in the wagons of trains of this company there is WI-FI.

Wheel in Italy

As you know, for a budget tourist, the most profitable mode of transport is a bus. Therefore, if for you the price of a ticket comes first, how to get from Florence to Venice, you now know by bus.

Flixbus provides the most convenient and cheapest options for getting from one city to another. If you book tickets on the company's website in advance, you can track the price. The minimum is 10 euros (about 730 rubles) for 4 hours of travel. Of course, the price of a ticket can vary from date and seasonality. In this regard, we advise you to consider tickets in advance. The ticket price also includes baggage allowance of not more than 20 kg and hand luggage not more than 7 kg. If you want to book a seat in advance, then you will need to pay 1.5 euros (about 100 rubles). For extra baggage - from 2 euros (about 150 rubles). Changes to the reservation can be made at any time 15 minutes before departure. And also in buses there is WI-FI. In addition, there are preferential rates for children and students. Does it make sense to drive?

If you like traveling by car, then you have two options. To come initially to Europe by car or rent a car in a special center. However, be prepared to pay for toll roads in Italy, entrances and parking in cities. In addition, it is necessary to take into account gas prices, which in Italy range from 1.5 to 1.7 euros (100-120 rubles).

There is an option how to get from Florence to Venice by car, less expensive and more comfortable. You can use the BlaBlaCar service. This site allows you to find a travel companion and get to the right place. After registering on the site, you are looking for the desired route and date of travel. The system will show you people who will travel that day that day and are ready to give you a ride for a fee. The price of a trip from Venice to Florence is from 800 to 1500 rubles. You can also choose a driver according to the reviews and conditions of the trip: smoking, pets, luggage space and more.

It's important to know

For a trip to Italy, it is important to take with you primarily a passport with a Schengen visa. Since you will walk a lot and the city streets are covered with paving stones, take comfortable shoes without heels. It is also important to bring medicines, sunglasses, hats, especially for traveling in the summer. The temperature in Italy in the summer months can reach 50 degrees.

Pay special attention to your belongings, as in the seasonal months, thefts of tourists with bags, cameras and telephones become more frequent.

Of course, it is important to come to Italy with a good mood, an empty stomach and an empty camera memory card.

Which Italian cities can I go to

Near Florence there is the city of Pisa, famous for its architectural ensemble and leaning tower. Getting from Pisa to Florence will not be difficult, and a walk along the tranquil streets of Tuscany will not leave you indifferent.

Also the city of the Middle Ages with an interesting history, an unusual building is Siena, located south of Florence. The city attracts with its colors, the beauty of buildings and squares. It is unusual and bright.

Of course, all roads lead to Rome, and it is impossible to come to Italy and not see the capital in order to view the ancient excavations, visit the Colosseum or just walk along the streets.
