DIY hut on chicken legs

Need an article "Hut on Chicken Legs"? With your own hands it can be done in many ways. The main methods and materials are presented below. Choose any option and complete the task.



Where to begin

First of all, it is worth determining what your hut on chicken legs will be of. With your own hands you can make it from different materials. The basis, that is, the house itself, can be made in the following ways:

  • from cardboard in the form of a painted box;
  • from paper (twist log tubes and assemble walls from them);
  • to collect from natural "building materials" (sticks, bark, straw will do);
  • sew or bind.

Choose any suitable method. Do-it-yourself hut on chicken legs is easy. The construction of the "foundation" is often a problem. It should not only have the appropriate shape and design, but also be stable and support the weight of the craft.

Chicken legs can be made both flat or voluminous. In the first case, they are performed by cutting shapes from paper, polystyrene, cardboard, wood. Templates are connected to the house with the help of rods, sticks, wire. Volumetric (more natural) chicken legs can be done like this:

  • mold from plasticine;
  • tie a frame of sticks or rods;
  • wrap sticks, tubes, wire with thread;
  • use driftwood;
  • decorate the metal frame with satin ribbons ;

  • assemble from beads, beads, beads on a wire frame.

Do-it-yourself hut on chicken legs out of the box

This is the easiest option. This craft is made as follows:

  1. Take a box of the appropriate shape.
  2. Paste the white paper over the base.
  3. Paint the surface in the form of logs.
  4. Draw doors and a window or glue separately made elements;
  5. The roof can be glued on your own or you can also use part of the box, cutting off the extra edges. It can be removed or fixed.
  6. Follow the chicken legs in any of the ways presented above.

Even a preschooler will cope with this option.

House of paper logs

A toy hut on chicken legs can be made in a more complicated way, also using paper. This craft will look spectacular, as the surface of the walls has a volume. The hut is made like this:

  1. Cut the paper of whatman paper into rectangles. Their length should correspond to the dimensions of the structure.
  2. Roll the workpieces into tubes and glue the joint with glue.
  3. You can leave the logs without sidewalls or prepare circles (in double quantity) and stick them on the ends of the resulting cylinders.
  4. Connect the ducts to make walls.
  5. Assemble the frame.
  6. The roof can be made from a flat sheet of paper, bent in the middle, or from the same tubes, gluing them with ticks, more precisely, connecting two planes at an angle.
  7. Paint the hut. This action can be performed before curling the tubes or using colored paper. It is easy to make an imitation of wood by purchasing the appropriate self-adhesive film, which you need to cover the Whatman paper before making the logs.
  8. Draw windows and doors, glue them on the walls.
  9. Do chicken legs in any suitable way from the above.

Paper Vine Weave

The original and decorative hut on chicken legs will turn out if weave it from newspaper sticks. This method is a cheap and easy alternative to willow vine. Sticks are made by twisting newspapers. To build a hut, you will need a box that will be used as a base. You will braid her.



First you need to make the "floor" of the house, and then fix the frame elements with clothespins or paper clips at the top of the box. Braid the racks, bypassing them with the working tube at the back and front in turn. When the "vine" ends, grow it to the required length. The joints of the tubes are better to glue and hide inside the product. You can paint the hut after weaving or you can cover the paper with the right color at the very beginning, even before curling. The roof, legs and decor can be made in any convenient way.

Natural materials

Crafting a hut on chicken legs can be made from what you find in the forest. The following materials of natural origin are suitable:

  • branches
  • bark;
  • moss;
  • driftwood;
  • bumps.

Natural materials are glued onto a cardboard base or made directly from them. For example, a box can be glued with branches, bark, and from spruce cones that did not have time to open, assemble the walls using PVA or a thermal gun. By the way, in order for the cones to retain their shape, they need to be applied with a layer of adhesive solution as soon as you collect them and bring them home.



From the bark and moss, you can make a roof, and driftwood fit as chicken legs. Such a craft will look most natural, corresponding to its fabulous image.

You can combine different techniques and also get very original souvenirs. For example, moss is added to the walls of paper tubes, and twigs are glued to the cardboard roof.

If you do not use a needle with a thread, but stick the rags on the cardboard base, then even a child will cope with this task. You can use any fabric. Crafts from felt and fleece look original. In the form of a fabric applique, flat crafts are performed, but you can sew any volumetric parts, including logs or their halves.

By the way, if you make it removable (for example, with zippers), then the internal space can easily be used for gaming purposes (folding, hiding small items).
