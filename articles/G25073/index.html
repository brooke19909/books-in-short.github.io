The philosophy of the Renaissance or the first manifestations of anthropocentrism in philosophy

From the beginning of the 15th century, a transitional era begins in the history of Western Europe, which creates its own brilliant culture. At this time, the collapse of the feudal system and the development of the capitalist system began: magnificent cities in Italy developed, one after the other, the greatest historical discoveries took place - firearms, the beginning of printing, cartography and geography as scientific disciplines appeared. In mathematics, symbolic notations are introduced, medical chemistry continues its development in the knowledge of the chemical phenomena of the human body and the study of medicines. Astronomy is achieving great success at this time, Vasco da Gama opens the sea route to India, Columbus finds America, and Magellan proves the sphericity of the earth, setting off on his first round-the-world trip.

However, the most important discovery at this time is the overthrow of the dictatorship of the church, a new philosophy of the Renaissance is being formed, and it was this that served as a special impetus to the flourishing of culture. The feudal asceticism of the ghostly world was forever buried under new trends, the basis of which was the anthropocentrism of the philosophy of the Renaissance. It was as if a rational man had regained consciousness from a long sleep and was ready to create for the good of his Motherland; it was not by chance that the creative formation of the greatest Italian poets of that time took place at that time: Petrarch, Erasmus of Rotterdam, Rabelais, Boccaccio.

The philosophy of the Renaissance was formed under the influence of the greatest people of that time, talented artists, architects and sculptors made their contribution to the development of the new empire. The whole philosophy of the Renaissance is saturated with recognition of a person as an independent person and affirms his right to the manifestation of his abilities and free personal growth. The first stage of the Renaissance passed as a freethinking, opposing the spiritual domination of the church and medieval scholasticism. The ancient philosophical heritage is assimilated and restored in full, the philosophical schools are opened again, so unjustifiably forgotten in the Middle Ages. Freed from the scholastic shell and rethought the works of the great ancient philosophers - Plato and Aristotle.
