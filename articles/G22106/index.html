Meet Madeira!

A map of Portugal with resorts and amazing places to visit will be incomplete if you do not mention another wonderful place of this country - the island of Madeira. The Portuguese call it the place of endless spring. It smells of forest, fish, honey and flowers. The sky above it is devoid of clouds, and the whole island looks like a huge outlandish garden.

The capital of this autonomous region of Portugal - the city of Funchal - descends in a huge amphitheater from the mountains directly onto the ocean. There are several famous botanical gardens in and around the city, including an orchid garden. And if you climb the cable car to the top of Monte, then your eyes wide open with delight will see a stunning picture: a city hugging the ocean. By the way, you can go downstairs on wicker sledges, which for many years served as almost the only means of transportation for local residents.

Portuguese bullfighting

Describing holidays in Portugal in September, one can not help but mention bullfighting. From spring to autumn in the cities of Santarem, Corusa and Vila Franca di Shira annual fairs are held with a unique spectacle - bullfighting.

You can immediately notice that in Portugal, bulls are not killed in the arena. In this country, the sight mentioned is primarily a demonstration of courage, dexterity and excellent dressage. By the way, there is another feature in the Portuguese bullfight, there are no professional toreros in it. In the arena in their role you will see ordinary clerks, waiters and workers.

The most western coast

This country even stands out on the map, showing the whole world its individuality, dissimilarity to the rest, an old and so understandable Europe. Turning away from the continent, Portugal peers into the Atlantic Ocean.



Holidays at sea here can not be imagined without a visit to Cape of Rock - the westernmost point of Europe. By the way, tourists who have been here are given a certificate of this.

And goodbye, Sintra

Incidentally, despite the small number of inhabitants (there are just over 30,000 in Sintra), the city is the recognized cultural capital of the country.
