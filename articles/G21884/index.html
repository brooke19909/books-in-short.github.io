Hard pelmet with your own hands. Patterns of hard lambrequins

Do you dream of new do-it-yourself curtains? Can't choose a model? Do you need to close the fastening elements of the curtains to the cornice or defects in the window opening? Sew a hard pelmet. To make it real at home. The necessary materials are available, but the technology is not so complicated. Even a beginner can master it.





Types of lambrequins. Advantages of hard

There are several options for this decorative element of curtains:

  • plain;
  • hard;
  • soft;
  • combined.

The first is a strip of fabric, gathered in folds on a curtain tape or manually. Hard pelmet has a fabric front side and a dense non-woven back or inside.



Soft consists of a certain number of alternating sequentially complex elements with folds - swag, de frill, chill. Combined is a combination of the two previous options.

The advantage of a hard lambrequin is that with a fairly simple sewing technology, a very beautiful, effective decorative product is obtained. This effect is due to the ability to make a complex curly hem, which will be fixed.

What is a bando

Do-it-yourself hard pelmet is made on the basis of a dense non-woven material. You can use the following:

  • bando;
  • non-woven;
  • doublerin;
  • proclamin;
  • wooden frame.

The first is best. Bando is self-adhesive, hot-melt, with one or two sticky sides. May vary in density. It is convenient and easy to work with. Compared to a wooden frame, it is lightweight and the lambrequin is easily attached to the cornice using a regular Velcro tape.





How to sew a hard pelmet

So, you have decided to decorate your interior with this decorative element. The procedure will be as follows:

  1. Make basic measurements.
  2. Draw a diagram of what you want to see.
  3. Prepare materials in the required quantity.
  4. Make a pattern on paper and cut it out.
  5. Put it on the bando, circle without allowances and cut it out.
  6. Attach a non-woven fabric template with the adhesive side inside out of the fabric, which is best fixed on a table or ironing board. On top, leave an allowance equal to 2cm, with all the rest 1.5cm is enough. For the best sizing, turn the product over and go with the iron on the front side.
  7. Make a bando lining from the same fabric. To do this, fold the flap with its sides inward so that the fold line runs no closer than 5 mm to the most protruding element of the lower edge.
  8. Chip both layers with safety pins so as not to damage the adhesive base.
  9. Sew side seams and the bottom edge 3 mm from the bando. The value depends on the thickness of the nonwoven. Do not cover the top edge.
  10. Cut off excess fabric, leaving from 0.5 to 1 cm to the seam.
  11. On arched parts, carefully make scissors with notches, cut the corners so as not to damage the stitch.
  12. Turn the product over the top edge to the front side.
  13. Walk the finishing stitch along the contour.
  14. Iron the top edge allowances inward.
  15. On the wrong side of the lambrequin, sew on Velcro tape, the counterpart of which is glued to the cornice.




By following all the steps in sequence, you can make a beautiful hard lambrequin (photo above) of any shape.

Product outline

Before you begin to sew or even perform a pattern, draw, or rather draw on a scale, the outline of your curtain with a hard lambrequin. It will allow you to see how the element will look, and will also help when building a template by size.

Patterns: do it yourself and use ready-made

To sew a hard lambrequin with your own hands, you will need a template according to which details from the bando will be cut.

If you intend to perform a simple-shaped lambrequin, it is easier to build a pattern on your own in a 1: 1 scale.

It makes sense to use ready-made options in the case of repetitions of some module or a very complex asymmetric form, which, due to a lack of skills, cannot be drawn by ourselves.

If you decide to make a pattern with your own hands, prepare the following materials:

  • a long strip of paper corresponding to the dimensions of the lambrequin. You can use unnecessary wallpaper, graph paper, Whatman paper, glue a format from office paper for a printer or newspapers. The latter option is not very good, because the paper is too thin, and the pattern will have to trace the outline of the product on the bando.
  • pencil;
  • a ruler;
  • square;
  • a compass if you have arcs or semicircles;
  • scissors or knife.

You need to build a pattern like this:

  1. Draw a rectangle corresponding to the maximum dimensions of the lambrequin in width and height, respectively.
  2. Build the axis of symmetry, if the shape is like this.
  3. Set aside the necessary distances to the left and right from the executed center line and build figures.

If you previously made a diagram on a scale, then you can simply measure from the lower left corner the lengths that you take from the diagram, multiplied by a scale factor. For example, in the sketch you have 3cm, with a scale of 1:10, the pattern will be 30cm.

If your bottom edge is made in the form of repeating curly modules, it is enough to build one on another sheet, and here just circle it several times.



Having mastered the creation of patterns of hard lambrequins, you can make your own templates of any complexity. You do not have to review many images on the Internet, wasting time. An idea came up, they took and implemented first in the pattern, then in the material.

Decorative finish

Any curtains that have a hard lambrequin (photo below) look stylish and very original due to the shape of the part. However, additional decoration will also come in handy. Decorate the edges of the finished product as follows:

  • fringe;
  • braid;
  • cord;
  • bugles.

Usually, this is done from below. The surface of the part can also be decorated with a sewn pattern from the above elements or use other objects, such as:

  • bows;
  • flowers
  • butterflies and dragonflies;
  • beads;
  • eyelets;
  • sequins.

The choice of decor is determined by the fabric, shape and model of the lambrequin. If it is sewn from several multi-colored details of complex shape or the fabric has a contrasting bright pattern, additional decor is not required.





How to make an openwork drawing

If you do not like to overload space with dense fabrics, but at the same time want to do something original, make an openwork hard lambrequin with your own hands. The principle of operation and technology are the same with what is described above. The difference is in the template used. In this case, it is not just a rectangle with a curly edge, but a carved decorative pattern.



If you know how to draw, then you can easily design an ornament yourself. You can also find on the Internet any vector (outline) image. If the file is in this format with the extension * .cdr, * .ai, * .eps, you simply scale it without losing quality in the appropriate editor. For such a service, you can contact an advertising agency that offers large format printing. There you will make a template on a scale of 1: 1.



It will be difficult to make such a pattern from the jpg file, since with an increase in the contours it will turn out blurry, but if you try, everything will work out with such a pattern. It is worth paying attention that the ornament should not have too many small and complex elements. It will be difficult to perform in the material, especially if you are a beginner.

In an industrial way, openwork lambrequins are made on special equipment for laser cutting, when the drawing is performed immediately from a two-layer base. You will have to do all the operations manually.

So, you have learned how to make a hard lambrequin. This is not difficult if you follow the sequence of actions. Having correctly calculated the sizes, you can easily build a pattern yourself or modify any ready-made template. Design, sew, decorate your interior.
