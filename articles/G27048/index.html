How to disable auto-update on iPhone and Android

Today we will try to understand in detail the question that surely worried every user of a modern smartphone and / or tablet: how to disable auto-update on an iPhone or Android? But everything is quite simple, if you know all the basic functions of your gadget.

Auto Update Concept

Auto-update is a special function of almost every modern smart device, which involves automatically downloading updated versions of its software (applications or operating system and its components).

Why disable auto-update?

The software automatically checks the network for available updates. If it detects them, updates will be installed without owner intervention.

However, there are cases when this function creates only unnecessary problems for the user. You can consider many different examples of such cases, but all of them certainly require a solution to the following question: how to remove auto-update on an iPhone or Android anyway?

For example, if the device runs out of memory, installing application updates may cause some inconvenience. Another example can be given: when you use mobile data, automatic downloading can inadvertently squander a large amount of money from the balance of your phone, because your 3G connection will be used for this.

Let's try to solve this problem.

How to disable auto-update on an iPhone?

iOS provides that you can both turn on and off automatic updates freely. If they are included, then you can choose whether to use or not use mobile data to download applications.

Disable automatic updates for specific applications is not possible. This means that either all applications will be updated one at a time, or not a single one. If you want to learn how to disable auto-update on an iPhone, then simply follow these steps:

  • Go to the "Settings" menu.
  • Open the "iTunes & App Store".
  • To disable automatic updates, you must remove the green switch next to the item "Updates".

How to disable auto-update on Android

To get rid of annoying updates on the Android operating system, you can use the following algorithm:

  • Go to the "Settings" menu.
  • We find the item "Applications" or "Application Management".
  • In the menu that opens, you will see a collection of applications installed on your Android device. It is necessary to find in it an icon called "Software Update", "Application Update", "System Updates" or with another similar name. There is no single name, since on devices from different manufacturers or in different versions of Android, this item is indicated differently. Most often, it is located at the very bottom of the menu that opens.
  • Next, just click the "Disable" or "Force Stop" button.

It is worth noting that when you reboot the system or check for updates manually, the auto-update function may turn on again, which means that you will need to perform the procedure again.

Among other things, auto-update applications on Android can be disabled or limited in the Play Market. For this:

  • We go to the Play Market.
  • Open the "Settings" item.
  • We find the menu "Auto-update applications." Here you can choose one of three auto-update states:
  1. Never.
  2. Always (both over Wi-Fi and over 3G).
  3. Only via wifi.

Thus, in this article we examined in detail a rather relevant issue among most users of modern gadgets: how to disable auto-update on an iPhone or Android. We hope the article was helpful to you.
