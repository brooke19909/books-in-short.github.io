Sights of Essentuki: photos and description, the most interesting places, reviews of tourists

Surely a large part of the Russian population after a stormy and prolonged feast immediately recalls the mineral water "Essentuki-4" or "Essentuki-17." But few people think that Essentuki is not only the name of water, but also a resort town. It is located in the south of our state, in the Stavropol Territory, at the northern slope of the Caucasus Range. The healing waters were discovered by the Volga Cossacks in the 19th century. It was not quite a pleasant taste and smell liquid, so the Cossacks did not drink it, but the horses consumed it with greed.

Climate and ecology

The climate in the city has a bright seasonal severity and is characterized as continental. In summer it is very hot and dry, and winter is snowy and quite cool. The offseason is short.

From an environmental point of view, the city is clean, since there are no enterprises that can pollute the air and the environment. Although there are certain problems with car exhaust, radiation from rocks, natural dumps, but in general, not everything is so bad when compared with other cities in Russia.

sights

Essentuki is a resort where people go to improve their health, drink healing mineral water. Along with this, the city has many interesting sights, ancient and unique buildings, holy places that are worth seeing. And for those who came to the resort not only for treatment, there are many night clubs.

Mud clinic named after Semashko

This is one of the most important sights of the Essentuki. The building began to be built in 1911, and the doors of the establishment opened in 1915. The structure was erected from the local dolomite.

The hospital resembles the ancient Roman baths, and stone lions are installed near the entrance. There is a fountain in the courtyard. Inside the building there are antique figures, the walls are decorated with bas-reliefs with the faces of the Greek gods.

The hospital has 2 parts, for men and for women. After all, that was exactly what was established in the Roman terms.

The treatment is based on the use of hydrogen sulfide mud from Lake Tambukan. It is recommended to visit the hospital, even if there is no indication for treatment with mud. After all, this is a real museum of pseudo-antique architectural style.

Treatment park

This green zone in the city was defeated in 1848. Then the governor of the Caucasus was Count Vorontsov, who issued a decree on the landscaping of the settlement. 60 hectares of land were allocated for the park zone, planting began already in 1849. Two companies of soldiers were employed at the works, which planted more than 7 thousand seedlings.

Due to geographical features, the park is conditionally divided into Lower and Upper. Then they arranged the main entrance, laid out paths for pedestrians.

With the advent of Soviet power, landscaping work continued. They built baths and an inhalation. However, in 1942 the city was occupied, and the park was badly damaged. Many medical buildings were destroyed. At the end of the war, restoration work began.

Now on the central avenue is the main pavilion with the water source "Essentuki No. 4" (Upper Park). Next is the Health Road, along which you can get to the Lower Park and other sources.

In the park there is a pavilion of physiotherapy exercises, an aerosolary, an inhalation and a pavilion of mechanotherapy. There are also catering establishments with healthy meals, a dance floor and table tennis tables.

The park has many cascading slopes, fountains and gazebos, a natural abundance of vegetation. And there is also the beautiful Theater-Park building in Essentuki. It was erected in 1901 and attached to the drinking gallery No. 17. Once upon a time there were concerts here. In fact, it was the only concert hall in the city for 100 years. Mayakovsky spoke here and Komissarzhevskaya Vera shone. In 1980, the concert hall ceased to function, and after reconstruction, a hotel, shops and a restaurant were opened in the building.

Museum of Local History V.P. Shpakovsky

Within the walls of this institution you can learn about the history of the city, how the resort developed. In some rooms, exhibits of flora and fauna of the region will be of interest. There are objects found during archaeological excavations and which are the best way to tell about the ancient settlements in this area.

The Yessentuki Museum of Local Lore opened its doors to visitors in 1963, for a long time it was a folk museum and after winning a museum review in 1978, it became a state museum.

The building itself is a monument of Russian architecture.

St. George's Convent

This is a fairly young attraction of the Essentuki and the surrounding area, but truly magnificent. It is located 35 kilometers from the resort (Mineralnye Vody - Kislovodsk highway). You can also get here by train, get off at the Beliy Ugol station. The shrine is located on Mount Dubrovka, at the very top there is an observation deck from where a magnificent view of Elbrus, Beshtau and Mashuk opens.

People come here to pray and simply admire the local beauties. The pastries of the nuns living here are very popular.

The sculptural complex of Christ the Risen

Another temple complex is listed in the list of attractions and monuments of the city of Essentuki. The dominant structure is the sculptural image of Christ, whose height is 22 meters. This is the highest sculpture in the whole country. For example, the sculpture of the Savior in Rio de Janeiro rises 38 meters, and the highest such building is in Poland - 52 meters.

The complex was built in 2013, it includes not only sculpture, there are chapels and temples, a playground, parking. The doors of a small zoo are also open for visitors. You can get to the complex by public bus number 10, which departs from the train station.
