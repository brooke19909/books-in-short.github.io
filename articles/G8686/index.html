Dominoes Definition





This is a logical game where they build a sequential chain of knuckles ("bones", "stones") that touch each other in half with the same number of points (points).

Bones

The domino tile is a rectangular tile with the correct geometry - its length is equal to the width multiplied by two. That is, these are two connected squares, on each of which points (points) are drawn: from zero to six. This is a kind of flat reincarnation of the dice.

In a regular set of dominoes, twenty-eight stones. It is made up of combinations with repetitions of two by seven numbers (0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6). But there are special sets in which there are points up to nine or more on the knuckles. In general, the number of bones in a set is calculated using the formula (n + 1) x (n + 2): 2, where n is the largest number of points. That is, for a common set, the formula looks like this: (6 + 1) x (6 + 2): 2 = 28.

They make bones from simple or ivory (hence the name), metal, plastic, wood.





The history of dominoes

From India and China in the eighteenth century the game was brought to Italy. It has changed somewhat and has taken root very firmly in monasteries, where it was forbidden to play card games, so the monk Domino invented this game (legend).

There is another option because of the white dots on a black background, the game began to be called dominoes, like a suit of Dominican monks.

From lyrical reasoning, let's move on to practice and figure out how to play dominoes.





General rules

Two, three and four people can play. The rules of playing dominoes together differ in that each player gets seven dice in his hands, if there are more than five players.

The remaining bones (points down) are laid aside - this is a "reserve" or "bazaar". They turn to him when there is no necessary element for the move on his hands.

The participant who has the smallest double (the bone with the same points 0-0, 1-1, 2-2, etc.) begins the game. If no one has it, then they are looking for a bone with the smallest value, for example, 0-1 or 1-2.
