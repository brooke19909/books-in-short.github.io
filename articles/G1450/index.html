Tourism, Iran: rest, sea, reviews

No matter how people strive for the latest technology and entertainment, they will always be attracted by the secrets of ancient civilizations. Tourism centers such as Egypt, India, China or Israel attract travelers not with their modern megalopolises, but with the sights that were created by great masters more than 2 thousand years ago. The emergence of ancient kingdoms and their decline, the monuments of culture and architecture that they left behind are an interesting excursion. Only countries with such a long history can offer it to guests. But not in all states with a past tourism is sufficiently developed. Iran, for example, occupies only 0.002% of the volume in this industry. But the changes taking place in the country today allow us to hope that it will occupy an honorable place in the lists of world tour operators.

From nomads to empire

For a long time there was no tourism in Iran. The power was closed to the public. Only now she began to gradually open up to people and appeared before her guests as a proud, wise and hospitable host who honors her ancient traditions and loves to be respected. The name of the country comes from the word "Ariana", which translates as "country of Aryans." This large tribe inhabited this territory in the first millennium BC. e. Initially, these were nomads and herders, which spread from Central Asia to North India. Only in the VI century BC. e. the political world recognized them as Persians - tall, strong and brave warriors. In the records of Herodotus they are mentioned as people who are indifferent to gold, who abstained in food and do not drink alcohol.

An unknown country was able to conquer half the world in just a few decades. Before them fell Egypt and Babylon, Medes and Sippar, and the power of the Achaemenid clan - the rulers of Persia - was established from the Aegean to India itself. Thus, the great Persian Empire lasted more than two centuries, until it was conquered by Alexander the Great.





Today, a traveler, choosing a vacation in Iran, can get acquainted with the ancient monuments of Persian culture by visiting the archaeologists found in the capital of his great kings, the most ancient of which is Pasargada. It was in this city that the famous Cyrus ruled over everything. Persia had to endure both the ups (during Nebuchadnezzar and during the reign of the Sassanids) and the fall, after it was conquered by the Arabs who brought a new religion - Islam. With their arrival, not only the ancient state, but also the centuries-old Iranian culture disappeared.

Iran today

Unfortunately, the long-term sanctions against this country and its positioning in the press as a terrorist center contributed little to tourism development here. Iran was forced to re-establish relations with the civilized world. In order to become a popular tourist attraction, the country has everything necessary - a rich cultural and historical past, a unique landscape and good climatic conditions. At the government level, a decision was made and a concept for the formation of a new industry for the state was developed.





Today, tours to Iran are organized to cities such as Qom, Isfahan, Shiraz and Mashhad. The infrastructure necessary for a comfortable and interesting stay has been created here. Opening of tourist routes is also planned in other regions of the country. Implementation of the plans is expected in the near future, since this business is 70% owned by private capital, and he loves the quick return on investment. Already, the world's leading tour operators have developed programs that include pilgrimage, visits to cultural and historical sites, beach and even ski tourism. Climate

This country is able to surprise even the most sophisticated traveler, as the climate is one of the attractions that Iran has. Reviews about it directly depend on exactly when and where the tourists rested. So, in the summer, guests of the northern part of the country will feel great. Here they expect a mild climate and even night frosts in areas located closer to the mountains. Whereas at the same time on the shores of the Persian Gulf, moist forty-degree heat makes tourists spend more time in places where there are air conditioners.

In general, Iran has a dry and hot environment. But the presence of several climatic zones allows travelers to navigate with the choice of a place of rest at a convenient time of the year for them. The best are the spring and autumn months. It is important to remember that Iranians do not welcome too bright and short clothes. Therefore, in order to avoid unpleasant moments, those who like to go in shorts or turn around in a pair, should find another place to relax.

Traditions

When spending holidays in Iran, you should familiarize yourself with local traditions in advance. This country is extremely religious. The people here honor their rituals and demand the same from foreigners. For example, during Ramadan, you should be prepared for the fact that many restaurants and shops change their schedule and open later in the evening. The most emotionally powerful ritual among the Iranians is a ten-day mourning for Imam Hussein, who suffered martyrdom from the illegally seized power of the ruler Yazid. These days, the whole country wears black clothes, and theatrical performances of tragic events are held on the streets.





Also, believing men pass in a mourning procession, torturing their flesh with chains, and women stand along the road and cry. In this way, the people personifies the remorse of the inhabitants of the city of Kufa that they did not want to come to the aid of the imam, but bowed to the illegal ruler. The presence of foreigners at these ceremonies is not welcome. Therefore, you should know in advance exactly when this event will take place and look for tours to Iran at another time. But it may be interesting to celebrate Novruz - the ancient Persian custom of New Year's Eve. It lasts 13 days and is accompanied by festivities.

Iranian cuisine

Food in the Iranian culture takes the same place as religion. They honor their national cuisine, which is considered one of the oldest in the world. The basis of local dishes are rice, bread, lamb, poultry and vegetables. Sour-milk products and world-famous oriental sweets are also popular. Numerous cafes and restaurants provide a wide selection of dishes, and in some tourist centers there are even establishments with a vegetarian menu, which is completely unusual for the local population.





Travel around the country

The most popular form of transport in Iran is public. He always goes on schedule, comfortable and can deliver anywhere in the country. Taxis are no less in demand both in the city and between different settlements. It‚Äôs worth the trip quite inexpensively, since gas prices in the country are extremely low. Foreigners can rent a car while traveling. Iranians monitor the condition of their roads, so it will bring real pleasure. Also in demand are air, rail and sea transport. The ports of Iran successfully connect the country with neighboring states, and some of them, for example, Enzeli, are called the "gateway to Europe." It is from it that ships can pass along the Caspian Sea to the Volga-Don Canal, which is connected to the ports of the Black Sea.





Currency exchange

Even a sophisticated tourist, the problems with currency exchange in Iran can be confusing. There are no special points in cities and towns. Therefore, it is better to conduct a currency transaction immediately at the airport, where they are. You can use the services of banks, but their work schedule is extremely incomprehensible to foreigners. There are illegal "exchangers" in the markets. True, for such actions can not only be fined, but also put in jail for three days. In addition, you can just meet scammers and be left without money.

Bank cards are accepted only at banks and hotels. At the same time, only cash is taken in stores. Since real tourism is just beginning to develop in the country, Iran has not yet provided comfortable exchange conditions for tourists. Some stores and bazaars accept euros, pounds sterling, US dollars and even Chinese yuan. And sellers know not only the language of Iran, but also English.

Memo to tourists

When choosing Iran for a vacation, one should take into account local traditions: from the selection of a wardrobe to the voluntary refusal of alcohol for the entire duration of the trip. And you should think about it immediately after boarding the plane. If tourists smell of alcohol, then customs clearance can drag on for many hours. This country is also unsuitable for families with children, as the local infrastructure is not provided for toddlers.
