Lake Taupo in New Zealand: description, sizes, flora and fauna

One of the attractions of New Zealand is Lake Taupo, located on the North Island in the province of Auckland, a region of Waikato. On the shore of the lake there is a small town with the same name.

The lake has an ancient volcanic origin and is a popular tourist center, attracting vacationers and inquisitives.

A bit of history

Looking at New Zealand on the map, it is easy to notice that the lake is located almost in the center of the island.

If you look from a bird's eye view, you can see that the lake is an extensive circus-shaped basin, the so-called caldera, which was formed after the eruption of the volcano Oruanui.

The strongest eruption occurred approximately 27 thousand years ago, seriously changing the landscape of the island. According to the research of modern scientists, the last eruption of Oruanui, due to which a lake formation appeared, had an apocalyptic level of danger. During the explosion of the inner part of the volcano, more than 1200 km 3 of magma particles and subclastic materials rose into the air. Ashes covered the entire island center, ashes fell even on the central part of New Zealand and Chatham Island, which is located at a distance of 1000 km from the island of North.

After the eruption, the deep (almost 200 m) caldera at an altitude of 368 m above sea level was gradually filled with fresh water from underground sources, rivers that changed their course, and rains.

The water in the lake is not cold, because the proximity of the magmatic layer causes the activity of geothermal sources, they create warm strong currents at the bottom of Taupo.

Lake Taupo can be compared with Baikal, because in the southern hemisphere of the Earth it is the largest storage of fresh water. For the Maori tribe, the natives of these places, the lake was a sacred place.

Natural features

The flora and fauna of Lake Taupo are no different from New Zealand.

Some of the lake shores are densely overgrown with beech and coniferous forests, greening at any time of the year. The undergrowth is made up of thickets of ferns and shrubs. Most plants are endemic. The symbol of New Zealand - silver fern - is found in the vicinity of Taupo.

The lake is inhabited by natives - tyulka, trout and northern kour - crayfish, as well as brown trout, rainbow and brook imported from the USA and Europe in the century before last. The trout has taken root, and now it is not difficult to catch a 10-meter individual. A small smelt, also brought in, is feed for trout.

But there are few mammals in the vicinity of the lake. A thousand years ago, only the fur seals and sea lions inhabited the coast of the islands of New Zealand, and bats lived inland. European immigrants brought ferrets, ermines, rabbits, possums, which actively bred on fertile land. European hedgehogs have also taken root here, adapting well to local conditions.

In the forests around Lake Taupo, kiwi, kea birds, owl parrot, wingless sultanka live. There are reptiles that are non-toxic and harmless to humans - these are hatteria and skink. There are no snakes not only near the lake, but on the whole island. Unpleasant consequences can be in a person only after meeting with a poisonous spider cathirp.

Weather

The subtropical marine climate on Severny Island, where Lake Taupo is located, is stable, there are no sudden changes in weather.

Summer temperature does not exceed +24 ... +27 , winter is mild, frosts below -10 are rare. Long rains are not uncommon in winter and spring.

The lake as an object of tourism

During the year, about 2 million tourists visit Lake Taupo, staying in the town of the same name. Of course, most people come for great fishing. The lake and rivers are teeming with fish, fishermen with excitement catch trout and eel. A fishing license is inexpensive - 97 New Zealand dollars. The fish is immediately cooked and served on the table. Fishermen can rent yachts and fishing equipment.

What else do tourists offer:

  • surface of the lake;
  • speed boat trips;
  • water skiing;
  • a visit to the Valley of the Dinosaurs (30 models of extinct animals in full size are presented);
  • rafting along rivers flowing into the lake;
  • bungee jumping into the Waikato and Waikato rivers;
  • excursions to the shrimp farm and to the local observatory.

Every year, cycling fans cross the lake in New Zealand. For this, many cycling routes of varying complexity have been laid.

Finally, Taupo attracts those who wish to swim in the crystal clear water, as if poured into a huge volcanic bowl, to have a picnic or to look into a restaurant. Tourists relax and contemplate the water surface, sitting on the banks covered with emerald grass.

Near the lake there are a variety of attractions, natural and historical-cultural. It is worth visiting them to get a complete picture of the place where Lake Taupo is located.

In the city of Taupo, you can look into the lake museum to find out how the Maori tribes lived on the lake shores, what crafts they did, how the Europeans mastered these lands. The museum displays interesting exhibits: real canoes, tools, weapons, tackles, the Maori dwelling was reconstructed. There are stuffed animals and birds, including the extinct moa. The museum has a small art gallery and a botanical garden.

By boat or boat you can reach the northwest side of the lake. Here, on the cliff of Main Bay, a huge, 10 meters high, formidable face of the leader Ngatoraruanga is carved. This is the glorious Maori leader who lived a thousand years ago. The natives believe that as long as the bas-relief is intact, the volcano does not wake up and bring harm. The image looks ancient, although actually carved at the end of the XX century.

Volcanoes

Taupo is part of a seismically active volcanic zone, so Toganriro National Park is located on its shore.

On the territory of the park are the sacred mountains of Ruapehu, Tongariro, Ngaruhoe. These volcanoes were considered as sleeping, only Tongariro occasionally breathed multi-colored fumes. In 2010, Tongariro stepped up, spewing ashes and stones and scaring the locals.

The ski resorts of Fakanapa (43 pistes), Turoa (17 pistes), Tukino (characterized by gentle slopes) are open on the slopes of the volcano Ruapehu.

Specialists of the Institute of Geology, which is located on the territory of the park, study the Taupo volcano, and also show the visitors of the observatory the activity of volcanoes and geysers. Tourists are invited to create a computer simulation of the zone of volcanic activity and try on themselves earthquakes of various strengths.

How to get there

Lake Taupo (New Zealand) can only be reached by air. Airplanes from Wellington, Auckland, Australia arrive daily at Taupo Airport.

Sightseeing is possible by shuttle bus. He leaves every hour from the city center from the stop marked i-SITE, and takes him to all tourist sites. Ticket price 15-25 USD (1000-1675 rubles).

Another option is to rent a car, this will cost 60 USD / day (4000 rubles).

It is more convenient to walk around the city on foot, on a motor scooter, and a segway.
