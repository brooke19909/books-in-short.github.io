The appearance of the crown

At the time of joining the European Union, the majority of the country's inhabitants voted to keep Swedish krona in use, but not to have the euro.

Swedish krona is the monetary unit of the same name country. At the beginning of the XVI century, the means of payment in this state were called Riksdaler. They became money at the very beginning of the XVII century, while the cost was not indicated and was prescribed by hand.





The end of the 19th century was marked by the emergence of the Monetary Union of the Scandinavian countries. It also included Denmark and Norway. So there was a krone, a new name for the currency of the Swedes. The start of the First World War put an end to unification, the Union collapsed, but the names of the country's national currencies remained.

Translated "crown" is a crown. Therefore, it is depicted on most coins of this country. It is believed that this money is one of the most beautiful.

In the 50s of the 20th century, the exchange rate of Swedish money was tied to the value of the US dollar. At the end of the 80s of the 20th century, the National Bank formed the national money rate based on the indicators of a multicurrency basket.
