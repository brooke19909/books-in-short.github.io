Getting ready for the carnival: royal paper crown

Children of kindergarten and primary school age are happy to participate in holiday mornings. And they love to dress up in costumes of princesses and princes. One of the main details of the outfit is the crown.

And now from theory to practice. Let's make a crown stencil from ordinary, classic type paper. Take a piece of paper, a simple pencil, an eraser, scissors, a centimeter. Measure the diameter of the head. Divide by 2. On the Whatman paper, set aside 2 pieces, equal in size to half the size. Add a few more centimeters to the ends - for gluing.

Measure the width of the base of the crown and make another measurement - for height. Using triangles or a ruler, draw the teeth. Try to make them the same size. When one piece is ready, carefully cut. Attach the stencil to the second part of the princess paper crown blank. Circle and cut. Connect the halves with tailor pins and try on the person for whom the dress is intended. Correct if something is wrong.
