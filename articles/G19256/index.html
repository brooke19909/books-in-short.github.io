Season features

So, is it worth going to Rome in the fall? To begin with, this time of year is considered the period of the "low" season, however, it is called such according to Italian criteria. This city is a place that is visited simply by a huge number of tourists, so do not expect that on the street and in cultural institutions you will not meet many people. On the contrary, it must be borne in mind that in November even more guests are traveling to Rome who want to save on prices for hotels and airline tickets that have fallen traditionally in the fall. Perhaps their cost will be really less compared to the spring season or the end of summer, however, be sure that you will have to stand in line for the main attractions of the city for a very long time.





Rome: November weather

If on October days nature was supportive of Italians tired of the summer heat, then November is a whimsical time. For example, in Rome it is sometimes really cool, damp and rainy. Already often at this time thick clouds gather over the city. Meteorologists say that you should expect at least fifteen days this month to be cloudy. And how then to pack your bags, going to Rome? Although the weather in November promises to be rainy, the precipitation is short-lived, which is why the clear sky and the sun can still please tourists. However, it is better to wear warm clothes when you leave the hotel room in the morning, because the outside will be guaranteed to be damp and cool (no more than 10 degrees, as a rule). By the middle of the day, the temperature will rise slightly (up to 16 degrees), and if the weather is warm, then you can even count on as much as 18-20 degrees of heat. And yet, no matter what the weather conditions are foreseen, in no case do not give up umbrellas or raincoats - they can come in handy at any time. After twelve o'clock in the afternoon it can get colder. It gets dark in Rome this season at about four o'clock in the afternoon, and after an hour the city will be covered with pitch darkness, and the lights of lanterns and lamps will light up everywhere.

Festivals and festivals in Rome

Holidays here begin to be celebrated already from the first days of November. Therefore, those who bought permits to Rome at this time were lucky: they would certainly be interested in familiarizing themselves with local customs. All Italy celebrates All Saints Day. This Christian festival has pagan roots, so participating in events will be especially interesting for those travelers who are interested in history. These days, it is necessary to recall the deceased family members, therefore, in the church, as a rule, memorial services are held. In the country, the holiday is considered a public holiday, so many shopping centers, shops and cafes do not work. The next day, on the 2nd, the Romans will buy flowers and go to the cemetery. The fourth of November is considered the Day of National Unity and in Remembrance of the Soldiers Who Gave Their Life during the First World War. Also, fashion shows are held in the first week of the last autumn month, and a new season is opened at the opera house. November is the opening hours of the international festival of sacred music performers, which is opened on the territory of St. Peter's Basilica. However, concerts are given not only there, but in many churches and basilicas of Rome. So, as they say, you are welcome!
