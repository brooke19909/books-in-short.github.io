Thick and openwork female hats (hook)

In practice, any knitter inevitably comes the moment when you have to learn how to knit women's hats. A hook for such products is preferable to knitting needles, since it provides the opportunity to create a rather rigid and dense headpiece.

Semicircular hat

Models such as the one shown in the following photo are suitable for women and girls with an oval or round face.


It should be noted that such a product cannot be worn if there is a bang, as it is pressed to the forehead and deformed.

This women's hat, crocheted, perfectly repeats the shape of the head and can be decorated with almost any decor. Knitted flowers, embroidery, appliqu√©s, beads and other elements can be placed around the entire circumference of the product or on the side.

For this model, a half-woolen thread was used, the thickness of which was 300 m / 100 grams. Women's hats connected in this way (a hook is needed No. 3 or No. 3,5) become indispensable for spring or autumn. They can be worn even in winter, if the air temperature does not drop below zero.

Flat part of the product

The diagram shows the knitting order of the flat part of the cap. The dotted lines going from each ‚Äúbush‚Äù of the bottom to the ‚Äúbush‚Äù of the new part are designed to draw the attention of the craftswoman to the fact that the even part is an extension of the bottom, but without expansion.

In fact, you just need to knit the fabric without adding new elements. Often, women's hats (hooks) are about 18 cm high. This is the distance from the crown to the lowest point. In this case, the width of the strapping should be taken into account.

This means that a flat part of the cap should be knitted until the product is equal to 18 cm minus the width of the harness (3-4 cm).

The bar at the bottom of the cap consists of single crochet posts. It is designed to keep the cap fabric from deformation and fix the product on the head, preventing it from falling.

Hat of motives

These models of hats are suitable for relatively warm weather, with the exception of products with a hemmed lining.





The manufacturing process for such caps is as follows:

  • A flat bottom is knitted according to any pattern involving the formation of a circular web. You can use the description of the previous model.
  • Separately perform square motifs. They can be large, as in the photo, or smaller, as in the diagram. If small squares are used, then they are placed in two rows.
  • Sew the squares together, then sew them to the bottom.
  • Perform cap binding that combines all the squares.
