History

Already in antiquity, caves (Miskolc-Tapolca) were familiar to primitive man. The baths, whose history of mass visits began in 1959, were opened after lengthy preparatory work.

It is known that the first baths in Miskolc-Tapolz were built by the ancient Romans. Documents indicate that in the 13th century. here stood the monastery of St. Peter (Benedictine), which was destroyed in the 16th century. Ottoman Turks. But the land, as before, was in the possession of the church, and in the 18th century. baths were built on the local thermal springs. But in those days, pools existed only outside.

In 1934, Miskolc-Tapolca was officially declared a resort. Active construction of hotels and infrastructure development began. In 1939, the old building was demolished. The construction of the next bathing complex began. At that time, a previously unknown thermal spring with a water temperature of +31.5 ¬∞ C was discovered. After 2 years, in May 1941, a new thermal complex was opened. And in 1959, the ‚ÄúBathhouse in the Cave‚Äù began to function, which is modernized every ten years. The last reconstruction of the pool was carried out in 2012.

On the healing properties of water

The healing properties of the bath are due to the favorable climate of the cave, as well as the healing composition of its warm karst waters. The local water is of the hydrogen-lime-carbon type, it contains: bromine, iodine, magnesia-metaboric and metasilicic acid, calcium and radon. The thermal water in Miskolc-Tapolca is not as dense as in other therapeutic baths. The concentration of salts in it does not exceed 1000 mg per 1 liter, so it is very much appreciated by many.

About sights

The center of the old town is the Town Hall Square, a typical, according to reviews, harmonious and very beautiful example of the Hungarian buildings of the 18-19th centuries. The main architectural style of the building is Baroque, according to which all buildings are light and elegant. The local temples are not only the property of believers, but they are also a magnet for many tourists.

Miskolc is one of the most important Protestant centers in Hungary. On the high hill of Avash, the largest Reformed church was built. There are also temples representing other religions: the Orthodox with its magnificent iconostasis (88 icons), a synagogue with an established memorial dedicated to the victims of the Holocaust, the current Catholic parish church.

In the west, in the vicinity of the city, is located Dioschier Castle, whose history dates back centuries. The castle remembers many famous battles. At one time, he was the favorite residence of King Lajosh the Great, who equipped him well and luxuriously decorated in the 14th century. Today, a historical panopticon operates here year-round. Each of the four towers of the castle presents a certain exposition. In the summer, there are many festivals, holidays, historical theatricalities.

In the vicinity of Miskolc there is also the famous climatic resort of Lillafured. It is located in a beautiful picturesque place surrounded by mountains. The most famous building here is the wonderful Palace Hotel (construction date - between 1927 and 1930).

Miskolc also has a good zoo with a wide variety of animals. Travelers with small children come here with pleasure.
