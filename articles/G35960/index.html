Croatia beaches are waiting for you

It is generally accepted that Croatia has a very rocky coast, and there are no such beautiful sunbathing areas, as, for example, in Spain or Italy. And indeed, even in the fashionable five-star hotels of this country on the Adriatic coast, the beach is often a concrete platform with gangways into the water. But do not generalize and label: that, they say, they go to this country of the former Yugoslavia only for excursions in cities and national reserves, but not for sunbathing. Here we will tell you where the best beaches of Croatia are.



The rugged coastline has already created the conditions for the appearance of secluded coves, hidden from prying eyes of bays and harbors. In addition, there are many islands: small, deserted and just the smallest. It is clear that not all rescue workers are on duty and there are various entertainments. But they will surely appeal to those who appreciate romance or just want to relax from the hustle and bustle and be closer to nature. Most of the bathing areas in the country are pebbled or are rocks covered with platforms. Therefore, those who do not accept pebbles in any form, need to know where in Croatia there are sandy beaches. These are, first of all, islands: the sea lays deposits of frayed shell rock on Hvar, Korcula, Krk, Lopud, Murter, Pag and others. But there are beautiful sandbanks in Split (Bakvice beach), Dubrovnik (Lapad). They are well equipped: they have a cafe, showers with fresh water, toilets, dressing rooms, rental umbrellas and sun loungers, playgrounds. What pleases, without exception, all recreation areas in the country are free, even in upscale hotels on the first line.



Pebble beaches of Croatia are considered healing and are very popular. Areas with very small pebbles, which pleasantly massage the feet, are especially appreciated. The most famous among this category of beaches is Zlatni Rat (Golden Horn) near the Makarska Riviera, on the island of Hvar. Other pebble recreation areas near Makarska are not inferior to it: Brela, Baska Voda, Podgora, Tucepi. Here you can relax even with very young children. After all, pebbles on these beaches are as fine as sand, and the entry into the water is very gentle. There is enough space under the sun for everyone - the Makarska Riviera stretches for 40 km.





The city of Dubrovnik is also considered a resort where the best beaches of Croatia are located. We advise you to visit Banje and Lorum rest areas. In Rovinj, Grveni Otok beach is a great place for swimming.

Croatia is proud to be one of the first in Europe to accept nudists. This fact makes the country very popular among Danish and German tourists, big fans of the naturist lifestyle. However, one should know where the nudist beaches of Croatia are located. These are thirty official recreation areas, among which there are even naturist hotels and campsites. The oldest of these resorts is the island of Koversada, near Istria. On many ordinary The beaches have dedicated areas for naked tanning fans.
