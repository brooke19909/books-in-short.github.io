AeroCool VX-500 power supply: specifications, description, principle of operation

The power supply is a very important component in any computer. It determines what power components can be installed in the computer. Many manufacturers produce quite interesting models. And the AeroCool VX-500 power supply is one of the most interesting instances. It is not very expensive, but it has a lot of advantages over competitors' products. Let's take a closer look at this block. But first, a few words about the manufacturer.

A little bit about the company

AeroCool was founded in 2001. The main industry of the manufacturer is the production of components necessary for the modification or self-assembly of a personal computer. The components manufactured by the company include power supplies, system unit housings, coolers and fans, water cooling systems and much more. The company is already quite firmly established in the first lines of the rating among its own kind and is not going to give in to the palm. Its products are chosen by advanced users along with such masters of the genre as Salman and DeepCool. In this regard, products such as the AeroCool VX-500 power supply are very interesting. Specifications

So, let's move on to the boring numbers. The rated power of the AeroCool VX-500 power supply is about 500 watts. Maybe one hundredth more or less, but these are trifles. Such power is quite enough to build a home computer with fairly average specifications. The unit provides a current strength of 0.3 to 20 amperes. This is enough to power any component of the computer. The unit is equipped with one cooler with a blade diameter of 120 millimeters. With its power, such a cooling system is quite enough. The energy efficiency of the unit is also at a high level. It belongs to the type of "Gold". This means that the power supply provides good power with minimal power consumption. And in the current conditions (with the high cost of electricity) this is very important.

How to choose the right power supply?

The answer to this question is not as simple as it seems at first glance. To select the "right" block you need to know quite a lot. First of all, you need to calculate the total power consumption of all components of the computer. These include: motherboard, processor, RAM, hard drive, disk drive, video card, sound card, network card. Only by knowing the power consumption of each component individually and putting them together, you can understand what power supply you need to choose. It should be noted right away that for powerful gaming computers you need to build on a 750-800 Watt figure. You also need to consider the possible procedure for upgrading (improving) PC components. More powerful components require more energy. Now about energy efficiency. It is better to purchase blocks marked "Platinum". With a large specific power, they consume very little electricity. Gold-marked blocks are also pretty good.

And what is the AeroCool VX-500 power supply capable of? Its power is only 500 watts. This means that it cannot be put on all gaming machines. With its power density, only office PCs and medium-power computers can do it. But under the latter, you also need to carefully calculate the power consumption of the components. But for not particularly loaded office machines, this unit is ideal. There will also be room for an upgrade. As a result, a weak PC can be turned into a good multimedia center. This block also has the Gold marking. This, of course, is slightly worse than Platinum, but its energy efficiency is at a fairly high level. So for those who do not need extraordinary power, this power supply is ideal.
