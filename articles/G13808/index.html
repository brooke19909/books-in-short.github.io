Infrared heaters: technical specifications. How to choose an infrared heater

Infrared heaters have long proven themselves on the positive side. First of all, they are economical. Almost all models have a regulator that allows you to adjust the temperature. With proper use, energy consumption can be reduced to a minimum.





Installation of infrared heaters

Many in our time are preoccupied with the question of choosing a place to install an infrared heater. On average, this device has a length of about 80 cm, which is a lot. Today, manufacturers can provide floor, wall, and ceiling devices. The last option is in greatest demand. When installing the device on the ceiling, you can significantly save space in the room. Additionally, the air will be better warmed up. However, such infrared heaters also have disadvantages. First of all, they are associated with poor fasteners and the lack of installation at low ceilings.

Wall mounted devices also have their fans. The main thing is to choose a suitable place. There should be no flammable materials nearby (including on the wall itself). Furniture, in turn, must be located at a safe distance. Damage to the infrared heater if installed incorrectly can be significant.

The last type of installation is called floor. It is most common because it is the simplest. As a rule, a tripod or a small stand is included with an infrared heater. With it, you can easily adjust the height of the device. You can also quickly rearrange the infrared heater to any other place. And wall and floor installations require reliable installation of the device using brackets. Additionally, metal hooks and dowels can be used.

Cons of infrared heaters

Of course, any infrared heaters have disadvantages, and in some cases even can harm human health. Most often this is due to the wrong choice of a device that is not suitable for a particular room in terms of its characteristics. The second problem is improper installation of the heater. Certain problems may also occur when using these devices for too long. The first nuisance is the drying out of the skin. You can feel the harm of an infrared heater on yourself if you stay too close to a working device for a long time. The body will begin to actively lose moisture, dehydration will occur.

In some cases, powerful infrared heaters can cause damage to whole cells. This process is called denaturation. It is very harmful to look at radiation for a long time. This can result in damage to the retina. The last problem is the low installation of ceiling heaters. The distance between the device and the person‚Äôs head can be critically small. In this case, exposure to infrared rays often leads to headaches.
