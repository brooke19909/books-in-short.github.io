Tampere: attractions, overview, photo and description

Tampere is the largest city in Finland, which ranks third in size. On all sides it is surrounded by numerous lakes. By popularity, the city is second only to the capital - Helsinki. But there are no less excursion routes. What to see in Tampere (Finland)? In our article we will talk about the most interesting places in the city.

Nature Park

The oldest attraction of Tampere is the highest coastal hill in the world - Pyyunikin Harju. Its length reaches 200 kilometers and a height of 80 km. In ancient times, there was a glacier at this place, the height of which in some places reached several kilometers. Now at this place there is a natural park called Pyyuniki, on the territory of which there is a cafe and an observation tower. On the rocky cliffs, you can see the mark reached by Lake Ankulus 9 thousand years ago. Many sightseeing tours of the city begin in the park, which can be safely called an outstanding landmark of Tampere.

City Hall

Another historical landmark of Tampere is the Town Hall building, towering above the central square. The Renaissance building was erected instead of the old wooden one in 1890. It is traditionally located in Finland for the city administration. The interiors of the building are very beautiful and at the same time restrained.

Sometimes events are held in the halls of the town hall, and on Independence Day local residents gather here to hear the festive speech of the city leadership.

Nyasilinna Palace

Among the attractions of Tampere (photos are given in the article) there is another outstanding building, built in 1898 on the outskirts of the city. The palace was designed in a neobarochny style. For many years a regional museum functioned in it. In 2012, specialists from Tampere began to establish contacts with Hermitage workers to open a branch of the St. Petersburg Museum in their city. According to experts, temporary exhibitions of world-famous works will help attract many tourists and art lovers to the city.

A simple project would not stand out among other similar designs, if not for one circumstance. Throughout the bridge is decorated with bronze sculptures that depict figures of representatives of an ancient people who once lived in these places. Moomin Trolls

If you travel with children, then you should pay attention to the children's museum of Moomins. In the world there is only one such institution, and therefore it is an interesting attraction of Tampere. Photos and descriptions of the museum allow you to evaluate its exposure. The exhibits of the institution are the heroes of the works of Tove Jansson. They are located on the ground floor of the city library building.

Children will enjoy traveling through the gloomy halls, which have a fantastic atmosphere. The museum's collection consists of 2000 drawings, book illustrations, sketches made by the hands of the writer herself. Here you can see dolls - the heroes of the Moomin Valley, scenes from their lives. The main pearl of the museum is a large house of five floors, on which rooms are located. The interior of the building can be estimated using a computer program.

Museum of espionage

According to tourists, in Tampere it is necessary to visit the only espionage museum in Europe. Within its walls, guides will very interestingly tell about famous and not very famous spies, about working methods and technical means, about historical events and other interesting facts.

Museum employees offer guests to try their hand at espionage, allowing you to listen to a conversation or change your voice. In general, the exposition of the institution is very exciting and informative. It will be interesting to visit the museum not only for adults, but also for children.

In Tampere, the whole family can visit the amusement park, open all year. Its area reaches 1 thousand square meters. m. On its territory you will find a wide variety of modern attractions and all kinds of entertainment. There is also an aquarium, a zoo and a planetarium.

Lookout tower

In Tampere, in the city center, there is a viewing tower 168 meters high. It was erected in 1971. The tall structure allows guests to enjoy the surroundings. The observation deck and cafe are at a height of 120 meters. A high-speed elevator lifts guests up.

But if you wish, you can use the stairs, which has 700 steps.

Hameenkatu street

In Tampere, as in any city, there is a main street on which there are shopping centers, boutiques and shops. Walking on it, you can buy cute souvenirs or handmade products. Here you will also find branded shoe and clothing stores. Even if you are not interested in shopping, walking along the street is still interesting. It houses the famous Kauppahalli food market. The main street hosts a colorful festival of light.

Aleksanteri Church

In 1881, the city of Aleksanteri was consecrated in the city. The temple received such an interesting name in honor of the 25th anniversary of the reign of Russian Emperor Alexander II. The church is made in the Neo-Gothic style. In height, the structure reaches 57 meters, and in length - 60.

Throughout the history of its existence, the temple has undergone several reconstructions, which gave its appearance elements of national romanticism. The external and internal decoration of the temple is decorated with Finnish artists.

Kaleva Church

Kalev Temple was built in the sixties of the last century. The building of the Lutheran church is very different from traditional views. The temple has a modernist design. The building is designed for a thousand parishioners.

Ideal lighting is organized inside the room, which gives an optimal combination of shadow and light. The acoustics of the hall are such that they provide an amazing sounding of the choir.

Art Museum

The art museum was opened in the city in 1931. Its exposition introduces visitors to nineteenth-century Finnish art. The funds of the institution include more than seven thousand engravings, sculptures, drawings made by more than 670 artists.

Among the works of the museum are creations of Carlo Vuori and Gabriel Enberg. Within the walls of the institution are the works of not only famous masters, but also novice artists.

What to see in the city in one day?

According to tourists, one day is not enough to explore the city. But in a few days you can rush to see all the most interesting sights of Tampere. It is simply impossible to feel the spirit of this amazing city in one day. And yet, if you have only 24 hours to go, then you need to start the city tour from the central square. Just on it are the most interesting buildings in terms of architecture.

Tour guides usually pay attention of tourists to the Cathedral, Kaleva Church, the Old Church. It is also worth a visit to the museum of espionage, dolls, police, or Moomins. Any of these institutions will not leave you indifferent. If you have time and energy left, you can go to the Koskikeskus shopping center or look at the market to buy delicious souvenirs. In general, for a one-day walk, you need to choose objects located in the city center.
