Harry Cooper - the legend of cinema

The famous American actor inspired more than one generation of Hollywood directors with his work. His manner of holding on and speaking was replicated by the boys, portraying dashing cowboys. And the style of clothing, elegant and at the same time brutal, became the standard of taste and wealth. Harry Cooper was at the forefront of cinema and managed to work in silent cinema, with the advent of sound he did not lose popularity, like many other actors of that time, but on the contrary, became one of the greatest stars in Hollywood. Widely known for his Oscar-winning roles in Sergeant York (1941) and at Exactly Noon (1952), this amazing person still delights people. Childhood

(Gary or Harry) Frank James Cooper (1901-1961) was born in the city of Helena, Montana, in an English family. His father served in the Montana Supreme Court and owned a ranch north of the state capital. James's mother was never enthusiastic about village life, and her main desire was to send her two sons to study in Britain, so that the boys could get the right education. This became a source of constant contention between the spouses, but in the end the father gave up and allowed to take his sons to England. However, when World War I broke out, their mother hurriedly sent them back to the States.
