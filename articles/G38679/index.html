The best attractions of the Kostroma region

When traveling, I definitely want to see and learn as much as possible interesting things. And if the trip is supposed to be on holidays or weekends, then you have to plan an interesting and compact route. The path from Kostroma to the vicinity of Chukhloma will be impressive.

Kostroma is one of the points of the Golden Ring of Russia. Here is the largest number of attractions in the Kostroma region, and it will take at least a day to explore at least the most significant of them. Galich, Kostroma Region: Attractions

The city stretched along the largest lake in the Kostroma region. He best preserved his epochal primeval. Many beautifully preserved temples and centuries-old buildings, the mysterious history of the city, its once distinctive culture preserve a special atmosphere and make the appearance of Galich unique.





The city became famous for its original architecture, made in different styles. Therefore, it is better to start the inspection from the center, where wooden and brick public buildings and mansions of the century before last are perfectly preserved. Galich's heart

Shopping arcade - a colorful decoration of the city in the style of classicism, located in its very center. The ensemble of buildings was designed by the best architects in the Kostroma province : N.I. Metlin and P.I. Fursov. The construction of the main and additional buildings was carried out in the years 1820-1830, and at that time it was a grand trading facility for the county town. Both rows, Upper and Lower, look impressive, slightly inferior to Kostroma. The complex deservedly belongs to the famous sights of the Kostroma region. The photo of the commercial buildings, taken from various angles, makes a completely different impression. Today, trade in the ranks continues, and for the guests of the city, perhaps, a delicious smoked fish of Lake Galich will become a pleasant purchase .





Other attractions

If time permits, then you should definitely go up to the Galich hillforts - the Baltschug Hill and the Shemyakin Hill, connected by a staircase. A view of incredible beauty will open from here. The lake and the remains of the defensive ramparts with ponds, cathedrals and part of the city will be down at a glance.

On the street Lunacharsky in a unique exhibition of the museum of local lore you can see exhibits of the Galich region from the 18th to the beginning of the 20th century. Everything is collected here: from clothes and utensils to paintings and stamps. The museum also has a selection of old documents, photos and descriptions of the sights of the Kostroma region of bygone times.

Galich temples

Behind the shopping arcade is the oldest stone church in the city: the Church of the Epiphany, the first written mention of which dates back to 1635. The wooden temple, after the blessing received in 1680, was recreated in stone. Since then, his appearance has not changed. The church belongs to the most important historical sights of the Kostroma region.

An elegant church complex of the mid-18th century, consisting of two temples: Introduction and Basil the Great, is located in Ryboda Sloboda. The first, with its characteristic wooden porch, has been restored, while the second is underway. Visitors note the presence of a particularly cozy and warm atmosphere in the complex. In addition, Rybnaya Sloboda is a picturesque area adjacent to the lake, and it is quite interesting to walk here.

Galich pearl

Being in this city, one cannot help but see the female Paisiev Assumption Monastery. Situated on the Fox Mountains, he ascends over Galich. Its architecture is impressive: as if created from pink opal, the temple seems weightless, soaring in the sky. From the middle of the 14th century, the moment of its foundation, the monastery was rebuilt in the middle of the 17th century, when in the place of wooden buildings, the Assumption Cathedral was first erected, and then the Trinity Church. This object, significant in Orthodox and architectural terms, is one of the main attractions of the cities of the Kostroma region. Chukhloma

Time seemed to stop in this town surrounded by forests and swamps, conveniently located on the shores of Lake Chukhloma. Streets with wooden huts picture down the hill to the very shore. It seems that you are transported into a quiet Russian outback for a century and a half ago. Indeed, almost all brick buildings in the center and a considerable part of wooden houses belong to this period.

The once flourishing agriculture, brewing business, the Chukhloma carpentry known throughout the region, the famous merchant dynasties - everything has sunk into the past. Only the old architecture remained a witness to past prosperity and prosperity. Brick buildings in the city center are former public, commercial buildings and merchant houses. The rest of the Chukhloma huts are built of wood, many hundreds and a half years old, and many of them are decorated with carvings.

Chukhloma attractions

The main subject of attention of these places is the Abrahamiev Gorodetsky monastery, the beginning of which in the 14th century was laid by Abraham Chukhlomsky, a disciple and follower of Sergius of Radonezh. The monastery, rich and vast before the revolution, was considered a miraculous and revered place. Many members of eminent families, including the Lermontov clan, are buried in the cemetery near its walls. Having fallen into decay and significantly destroyed, the monastery began its restoration in 1991. On the territory you can now see the remains of the ruins. This beautiful and important complex for Russian Orthodoxy rises above the lake in the village of Nozhkino, 11 km from the city.





Other attractions of the Chukhloma of the Kostroma region: the well-restored active Assumption Church of the first half of the 18th century and the earthen ramparts of the mid-15th century, framing the territory of the city park. And of course, the local pearl is of glacial origin, Lake Chukhloma.
