The capital of Armenia. History, sights, population.

Yerevan is the capital of Armenia. Most historians believe that the year of foundation of Yerevan is the year of foundation of the settlement of Erebuni, that is, 782 BC. Erebuni was located in the southern territory of the modern capital of Armenia. Yerevan is located in the Republic of Armenia, in its central part. The upper part is surrounded by picturesque mountains, and to the south it is located on the banks of the beautiful Hrazdan River, which flows through the canyon and divides it into two parts. The climate in Yerevan is continental. Summer is dry and hot, and winter is snowy and cold.





Yerevan population

Armenians make up the majority of the population of the capital. Ethnic minorities also live in Yerevan - Kurds, Russians, Iranians and Azerbaijanis.

How to get to know the city?

It is better to start exploring the city from the hill on which the ruins of the Erebuni fortress are located. This hill is located in the southeastern part of the capital, between the districts of Vardanesh and Nor Aresh. During excavations, archaeologists discovered that on this hill there are about a hundred hectares of ancient buildings, which were covered with a thick layer of soil. Almost in the center rises a hill with the buildings of the citadel.

The capital of Armenia and Soviet power

In 1924, the Soviet government decided to reconstruct Yerevan. It was carried out according to the project of Tamanyan, who created a special national style using elements of church architecture. During the reconstruction, the capital of Armenia changed its appearance. Almost all previously built buildings were demolished. New streets were created, electricity, sewage and running water were laid, and the surrounding hills were planted with trees. Now the central square of the Republic has become the architectural center of Yerevan. Other major architectural projects include the Opera and Ballet Theater, the Brandy Factory, numerous monuments and monuments, the most significant of which are the monument to the victims of the genocide and Victory Park.

Last years

Over the past years, the ancient capital of Armenia has undergone many changes. The city acquired a young and fresh look, the construction of the Northern Avenue, which connects the Republic Square and the Opera and Ballet Theater, has been completed, many supermarkets and hotels, cafes and restaurants have been built and renovated. The Moscow cinema has been repaired and now residents of Yerevan can once again enjoy the masterpieces of world cinema. Now the capital of Armenia has many music and karaoke clubs.





New playgrounds for children and entertainment facilities have also been built in new Yerevan. The largest of them is an amusement park, as well as the Jrashar water complex. On the territory of Avan, which was previously deserted, Play City was built. There is a bowling alley, a go-kart track, off-road for a buggy, a children's labyrinth, a paintball training ground and much more. Now the capital of Armenia offers not only cultural, but also entertaining vacations.
