Portable speaker JBL Charge 2: specifications, description, reviews

One of the most popular brands of audio equipment in world markets is the JBL brand, created in 1946 and owned by Harman International. The company produces a variety of devices, ranging from car subwoofers to headphones and portable speakers. The latest model in the lineup was the JBL Charge 2 portable speaker.





Specifications

  • Using the Bluetooth 3.0 protocol to wirelessly connect up to three devices at the same time.
  • 3.5 mm TRS connector for wired connection.
  • Ability to work in wireless headset mode.
  • Two passive emitters.
  • Two wide-range speakers.
  • The maximum noise level is 80 dB.
  • The frequency range is from 75 to 20 thousand Hz.
  • 6000 mAh integrated battery
  • The ability to charge third-party gadgets via a USB connector.
  • Light weight - 602 grams.
  • Compact dimensions - 184x76x76 mm.

Contents of delivery

Together with the portable JBL Charge 2 speaker, only the power supply unit, interchangeable plugs and instruction manual are supplied. No additional accessories are implied.





JBL Charge 2 Wireless Speaker Design

The appearance of Charge 2 is practically no different from the previous model and is focused only on mobile and ubiquitous use. The original JBL Charge 2 looks like a cylinder, making it easy to attach, for example, to the bike frame using a standard holder.

A large battery negatively affected the mass of the column: it exceeded half a kilogram. Perhaps this characteristic of JBL Charge 2 is the only one that can cause inconvenience to the user.

The case is mostly made of rubberized plastic. One of the distinguishing features of JBL Charge 2 is the well-thought-out form of the case, in which the speakers are covered with plastic grilles recessed into the main material. Passive emitters are placed at the ends of the column. During operation, the emitters vibrate very noticeably.





On the right side of the case are small legs that allow you to set the column in a vertical position. In acoustics JBL Charge 2, a characteristic that indicates the battery level is a special indicator located under the volume buttons.

Three connectors are hidden under the grill located on the back of the speaker: for charging third-party devices - a high-amp USB input, for connecting sound sources - TRS 3.5 mm and for charging the gadget - microUSB.

Due to the elongated base, the column does not have sufficient stability, which is why it is easily tipped over. Despite this, in the reviews, users note that this does not threaten the acoustics, since after several swings its speakers remain upward.

Column Measurements

Many users in their reviews conducted various tests of JBL Charge 2 characteristics. For this class of acoustics, the frequency response graph is acceptable between 75 Hz and 2 kHz and from 5 kHz to 12 kHz. It should be noted separately that the schedule reaches an acceptable level quite early.

The maximum sound pressure, which is often measured by specialists, is one of the important parameters of acoustic systems. The check is carried out simply: the column is placed on a table made of chipboard so that the main speakers are directed towards the measuring microphone. At a distance of a meter from the speaker system, a measuring microphone is mounted at the same height and connected to a spectrum analyzer.

The speaker system is synchronized with the smartphone with the maximum volume level. During the test, the average dBA readings for half a minute of sound are recorded in any player of the so-called pink noise.

According to the results of tests, we can say that the portable speakers JBL Charge 2 is practically not inferior to its counterparts in quality and sound volume.





Sound

JBL speakers have excellent sound quality: even for fakes JBL Charge 2, the sound is more open and louder than their original competitors - for example, Sound Blaster Roar. Lower and upper registers are more detailed, which allows you to convey the nuances of the sound of various musical instruments.

The JBL Charge 2 manual notes a feature such as simultaneous synchronization of up to three third-party devices. The gadget that was connected first remains the priority: the rest do not enter until playback on it is completed. By pressing the corresponding key, priority is transferred to the device that is currently connected to the column.
