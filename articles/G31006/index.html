Castling in chess - how to do everything according to the rules

Castling in chess is a double move performed by the king and the rook, which never went for the game.

In a chess game, they often do a similar trick. The essence of this is that the king is moved towards the rook, and the rook is moved to the cell previously occupied by the king. Each side can make only one such move during the game.



Castling not only makes it possible to hide the king in a safer place on the flank, but also provides a chance to group two rooks in the center or on the flank at once. This can be a powerful offensive weapon. But even without this, the rook becomes in the center of the vertical. There, her position is more advantageous.

How to make a chess move?

According to the technique, castling in chess is a double move, which can be made by pieces that have never been played throughout the game. The first movement you need to move the king to the side to the rook immediately by 2 cells. Next move we put the rook in the place recently occupied by the king.

Although the movements of two figures take place during castling, it is considered to be considered as one move. First of all, the rule works: touched - go. Therefore, you need to start castling with a rearrangement of the king into 2 cells, and not a rook. This is an important aspect in technology, which sometimes results in violation of the rules.

Castling is performed in any of the directions. It makes no difference whether you move towards the queen or on a short flank. When creating a short castling, the king is placed on the position of the horse, and the rook replaces the elephant. If you use its long version, then the king will find himself in the position of an elephant near the queen, in the position of which the rook will fall.

Castling Prohibitions



Castling in chess is prohibited in the following cases:

- the move is excluded if the king or rook has previously walked, or a rook and pawn is vertically castled;

- it is forbidden to do such a chess trick if the king is under attack, that is, a check is assigned to him;

- if other pieces are placed between the king and the rook, the move is prohibited until the opening of the clear fields.

Chess etiquette - what is it?

It is worth following some rules of the game of chess. Castling is performed with one hand (like all moves). This gives a certain elegance to the game. By itself, it represents a single move, because at first the king moves, and then the rook. All this is done alternately and one of the hands. In competitions, there is a rule that if a player takes both the king and the rook, then it is necessary to make the last castling. If you accidentally touch, and you are not going to carry out this trick, then you need to make any other of the moves king. In such cases, if the participants touched the rook, then they must make a move with it. Only castling will not work.

The rules describe such options when the playing participant castles in violation of the previously specified rules. In this case, the game stops, the move is canceled, and the pieces are put in the initial places. The participant is obligated to perform any other movement by the king.
