Sadovo-Kudrinskaya Street (Moscow): description, history, attractions

Each street of the historic city center is something remarkable. Sadovo-Kudrinskaya, one of the most important transport and pedestrian arteries of the capital, will not be an exception here. Let's get to know her better.

general characteristics

St. Sadovo-Kudrinskaya is one of the largest Moscow streets, belonging to the northwestern circle of the Garden Ring. You can walk along it from Malaya Bronnaya and Kudrinskaya Square to Bolshaya Sadovaya. Geographically located in the Presnensky district of the capital (CAO). The total length of Sadovo-Kudrinskaya is 0.85 km.





The nearest metro station to the street will be Barrikadnaya. The situation with the indices today is observed as follows:

  • 121069 - houses 26/62: pages 1 and 4.
  • 123286 - houses 9: p. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 13, 15.
  • 123242 - all other odd buildings.
  • 123001 - all other even buildings.

The main attractions on this street will be the Moscow Planetarium and the Filatov Hospital.

Location

As we have already noted, Sadovo-Kudrinskaya is part of Ring B (the Garden Ring), a large circular road in the center of the capital. The street originates at Kudrinskaya Square, then goes to the northeast. On its inner side, Spiridonovka and Malaya Nikitskaya adjoin Sadovo-Kudrinskaya.





Further from the center, Malaya Bronnaya overlooks it, and from the outside - Krasina Street. After that, the highway passes to the Bolshaya Sadovaya.

Name Origin

The name of Sadovo-Kudrinskaya in Moscow is double:

  • The first part indicates involvement in the Garden Ring.
  • The second part speaks of the beginning on Kudrinskaya Square. The latter received its name by the name of the village of Kudrino, as well as by the Kudrina settlement (Patriarchy, it was run by the Novinsky monastery). Once these historical sites were in place of the modern square.




sights

If you decide to walk along the street. Sadovo-Kudrinskaya in Moscow, you will find a lot of interesting things here. For example, on the inside even side you will see:

  • Building No. 2 - Kahn's apartment building, built according to the Schechtel project in 1901
  • Buildings No. 4-6 - the mansion of V. I. Schubert with the left and right wing. Architects - Afanasyev, Zagorsky. Construction: 1873-1892 The doctor A. Y. Korneev lived in the left wing. He was also rented by Anton Pavlovich Chekhov in 1886-1890. Today, the house-museum of the legendary writer is arranged here.
  • Building No. 8 - Agafonov's apartment building.
  • House number 18 is the mansion of Popova (Kapkanschikova). Today it houses the Moscow Union of Public Engineering and Scientific Associations.
  • Building No. 20 is a tenement house in which the architect I. A. Fomin lived at the beginning of the last century.
  • Building No. 21/22 - here is the headquarters of the movement "The Essence of Time" and a theater studio called "On the Boards".
  • Building No. 24/26 is Skopnik's former apartment building. And today it is the building of the RAS Astronomical and Geodesic Society.
  • Houses No. 28-30 are buildings owned by the Soviet Ministry of Defense. Here, at one time, the military conductor Chernetsky, the surgeon Elansky, the architect Rudnev, the military leaders Eremenko, Zakharov, Grechko, Shtemenko lived.
  • Building number 32 - the former apartment building of Sidamon-Eristov, built in 1911-1912.




Now let's move to the odd side of the street:

  • House number 3 (building 1) - The fourth female gymnasium in Moscow. It was here that the famous poet of the Silver Age, Marina Tsvetaeva, studied.
  • Building number 3 (building 2) - today there is a center for forensic medical examination. And earlier there was a school, one of the students of which was Vasily, the son of Joseph Stalin.
  • House number 5 is a planetarium built in 1927-1929. designed by architects Sinyavsky and Barshch.
  • Building No. 7, Building 13 - an architectural monument of federal significance: the Meshcherskaya-Buturlin estate (XVIII-XIX centuries).
  • House number 9 - it was here that the first Moscow Real School (1899) was located. Then he was replaced by the Higher Party School. Today here is Moscow State Law University named after Kutafin.
  • Building No. 11 - Union of Disabled Persons of Russia, Federal Antimonopoly Service.
  • Building No. 15 - Protkova's mansion (1809-1841).
  • House number 15, building 9 - Temple of Sofia and Tatiana, the holy martyrs.
  • Building No. 17 is the mansion of A. V. Demidov. Today is the Pakistan Embassy.
  • Building number 19 - the former apartment building Pestrzhetskogo.
  • Building No. 21 is the house where the writer N.V. Toman lived.
  • Building number 23 - the former apartment building Chizhikova. Today, here is the Moscow Central Youth Library named after Svetlov.

This concludes our acquaintance with one of the most interesting and beautiful metropolitan streets - Sadovo-Kudrinskaya. As you already noticed, a walk along it will be culturally useful, since almost every house has its own story, a rich past, which is worth it to take a closer look at the structure.
