What to see in the Crimea?

Crimea is a real open-air museum that stores a lot of wonderful sights and unique natural monuments. It makes no sense to list famous places on the peninsula, because more than a dozen pages will do this. Nevertheless, it is worth listing the most significant objects, from which it is breathtaking.

Of course, the sea and the walls of the hotel get bored over time, which is why there is a persistent desire to visit beautiful places. The Swallow's Nest, a miniature castle located in the village of Gaspra, has long been considered the symbol of the Crimea. From the outside, he delights in his incomparable forms, towering above the stormy waters of the Black Sea. See the photo below.

The list of interesting places is worth adding Ai-Petri Mountain with its famous "battlements" and the Uchan-Su waterfall, countless monuments of Sevastopol, as well as Crimean palaces, among which Vorontsovsky, Massandrovsky and Livadia are especially notable. Finally, the Muslim architecture of Bakhchisarai also attracts to a closer acquaintance with the local culture. While in Crimea, be sure to take the time to visit the above attractions - you will be sincerely delighted with what you see.





How to get there

The peninsula can be reached in several ways: through the sky, sea or land. So, in the republic there are 3 airports, including 1 international, located in Simferopol - the capital of Crimea. Plus, trains and buses constantly run here. Having reached the railway station of the capital, you can use the trolleybus line, which will take tourists to many cities and villages of the peninsula (Alushta, Partenit, Yalta and the like). An interesting fact is that this is one of the longest trolleybus lines in the world. Finally, from the Black Sea ports of Russia, you can easily get to this paradise, washed by the mirrored waters of the Black and Azov Seas.
