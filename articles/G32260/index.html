Kirzhach - a river in the Vladimir region

Kirzhach is a river in Russia. It flows through the territory of the Vladimir region. In its lower reaches there are sections that are the border with the suburbs. The river is 78 km long; it is a left tributary of the river. Klyazma.



Source

The source is considered to be the confluence into one of two rivers - the Small and the Big Kirzhach. The first water stream flows from the Berendeyevsky swamps in the Alexandrovsky district of the Vladimir region. The length of the river is 69 km. On its way, it is littered with snags and fallen trees, but is popular with kayaking enthusiasts. East of the Small, parallel to it, flows the Bolshoi Kirzhach River. The length of the water stream is 55 km. Like Small Kirzhach, it is available for rafting.

Near the village of Ivashevo, both rivers merge into one, continuing their flow in a southerly direction, until they flow into the waters of Klyazma, 10 km from the city of Pokrov.

A bit of history

For the first time, a description of the river and its sources appeared in 1852 in documents of the General Military Department. It is known that in the period from VII to IX centuries. Finno-Ugric tribes lived on its shores. Kirzhach is a river, the name of which, most likely, came from the Moksha (Meryansky) dialect. The word kersh meant left.

Characteristic

The source of the river is located at an altitude of 137 m, to the place where it flows into Klyazma, it drops to 115 m above sea level. The channel of the watercourse twists steeply in some areas, the direction of water movement is from north to south. Kirzhach - a typical plain river, has a slight slope (0.6 m per km), the flow is calm. The bottom and coast are sandy. The right coast is high and hilly, while the left is more gentle and low. Forest plots are often found on both banks of the river, but meadow vegetation is more common in them. At the source, the width of the channel is small, with the fastest flow. There are often obstacles in the form of fallen trees on this site. Further downstream the river diverges to an average size of 10-20 m. The maximum width of the river. Kirzhach is observed at the place where the Sheredar tributary flows into it, and reaches 70 m. In the lower reaches, it has a rather large swampy valley with numerous old people on the way. Kirzhach is a river, the average depth of which is 1-1.5 m, and the maximum reaches 4 m.

Fineevskaya hydroelectric power station

On the banks of the river there are no sights, at least architectural and historical. The only building that could attract attention are the ruins of the Fineevskaya hydroelectric power station. In the first half of the XX century, this hydroelectric power station was autonomous and completely provided electricity to nearby collective farms, state farms and settlements. However, in the post-war period, in rural areas it was forbidden to use one's own sources; it was necessary to connect to state power systems. So Fineevskaya hydroelectric power station ceased its activities. And in 1967 it was completely dismantled. Now only the ruined walls are left of the former hydroelectric power station, although it is considered a local attraction.



Flora and fauna

The beauty of the local nature will delight Kirzhach (river). Vladimir region in this area is covered with mixed forests. Broad-leaved and small-leaved plantations (mainly aspen and birch forests) spread along the banks of the river. Broad-leaved forests are the natural wealth and the most important raw material resource of the region. In them you can find a large number of berries (blueberries, currants, lingonberries, cranberries, etc.), mushrooms and medicinal plants (St. John's wort, mint, nettle, rosemary, etc.). Among the fauna in this region live hares, ferrets, badgers and foxes . Sometimes large mammals come to the banks of the river: elk, roe deer, sika deer. Of the birds there are black grouse, hazel grouse, partridge, geese and ducks. Hunting in the region is allowed only under licenses and in the time allotted for this purpose.

How to get there

The most convenient way to get to the shores of the Kirzhach watercourse is by rail. The railway track runs west of the river for almost all of its movement. On the banks there is a large settlement - the city of the same name Kirzhach, as well as a number of other small villages: the villages of Savino, Ilkino, Lisitsino, Ileikino, Fineevo. There is a bus service between them. Residents of Moscow can go here from the bus station (metro station "Schelkovskaya"). Buses depart on schedule with an hour difference. There are also minibuses.
