Sights of Sukhum: photos, prices and reviews of tourists

Probably no less than Egypt or Turkey, potential vacationers mention Abkhazia, deciding where to go on vacation. Even in pre-perestroika times, tourists flocked here to climb the mountains, swim in the transparent sea, enjoy the clean air. And the resort history of this region began much earlier.

Sights of Sukhum

Millennia ago, Abkhazia hosted colonies of ancient Greeks on its lands, then the Romans erected their fortress. And they chose for their location the place that today is known to all as Sukhum, the capital of the republic. He received the status of the city after 1840. The name comes from the Turkish Sukhum-Kale. That is what the fortress they built was called. Su is water, and hum is sand. Both the first and second are very many. And excellent quality. Those who have been here will confirm this with their reviews.





Sights of Sukhum should not be sought. The city itself is unique and inimitable. On the outskirts, where private houses are located, a familiar aroma is felt. This laurel bushes are located on the side of the road. The whole city is hidden in greenery, and so many palm trees are hard to meet elsewhere.

Promenade

Fans of walking can not pass one of the picturesque places of the city - the promenade of Makhadzhir. She is over a hundred years old. And here, during his stay, the great Russian poet Sergei Yesenin liked to pace. Now, groups of vacationers enjoy the view of the sea on cozy verandas, which are created in complete architectural harmony with the historical appearance of the embankment.





There are numerous mansions, hotels, restaurants and cafes. The former Rossiya Hotel and the Shipping Company are the oldest buildings located here and recognized as the sights of Sukhum.

Botanical Garden

You can not ignore the large Botanical Garden with a bicentennial history. Ticket price is 140 rubles. In the botanical garden, a rich collection of unique plants constantly accepts new pets. Today, the fund has more than five thousand green spaces, shrubs and trees. And after most of the garden was destroyed during the Georgian-Abkhaz conflict, the first question arose about its restoration. Tourists enjoy walking along the sandy paths hidden in the dense thickets of the most exotic representatives of the flora. And the Caucasian linden, which celebrated its 250th anniversary, many make a wish.
