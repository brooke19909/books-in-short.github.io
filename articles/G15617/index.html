Improved laser signal detection algorithm

Changes to the radar software made it possible to detect the Vitronic PoliScan photoradar laser system. A similar device is made in the form of both a portable tripod and a stationary complex.

A feature of Vittronic PoliScan is that the speed of a moving vehicle is determined by a narrow laser beam. The vehicle speed is recorded at a distance of 15‚Äì75 m from the device. But this is in theory, in practice, the distance is 40 m.

Due to the narrow beam, the radar detector cannot pre-warn the driver about the presence of such a complex on the highway at a great distance, therefore, after it is detected, the driver has only a few seconds to reduce the speed. A plus is the fact that such complexes practically do not occur on the territory of Russia.





Changing GPS Databases

The updated firmware version of Sho Me G 900 STR uses a two-point base to identify tracking devices. The first is the location of the camera or radar, the second is the entry point, which is located at a certain distance from the tracking device. For each stationary complex, this distance is individually calculated by highly qualified specialists on the basis of data collected in practice and the conditions of the terrain in which the radar is installed.

The changes made to the firmware were aimed at eliminating the false positives detected by users of the radar detector, which detected complexes located on parallel streets. And also on the adjustment of informing the owner of the vehicle about approaching various video and photo fixation complexes, depending on the range of these complexes. Thanks to this, the need for further use of the ‚ÄúDist.‚Äù Parameter has disappeared.

Specially for the new firmware of the radar detector, point-to-point databases were released that can only be used with new software. And the new ATO parameter allows the user to independently select the base for further use.
