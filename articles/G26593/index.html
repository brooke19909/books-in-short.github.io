Restoration of a favorite chair is a wonderful gift

Often a certain thing is so liked that a person does not want to part with it, even if it has already become unusable, has lost its external beauty. This may be a dad's chair.

If this is the case, then do not puzzle for a long time about what kind of crafts the father needs to make for his birthday. After all, it‚Äôs enough just to update his favorite chair!

Of course, this work is laborious and will require some skills from the master. First, you need to disassemble the chair, removing the upholstery from it and removing the thinned foam. All joints of parts should be strengthened with carpentry glue or metal corners.

If any parts are no longer subject to restoration, they should be replaced with new ones. Something you can buy in a building materials store, such as legs, but you have to do something yourself. By the way, to the joy of the parent, you can replace ordinary legs with wheels.

Then a new foam rubber is laid on the backbone of the chair, cut exactly according to the old patterns. Although in some cases it is enough to make ‚Äúpatches‚Äù by putting pieces of new material on dents. You can make additional pillows that can be put on top of the old upholstery.





Every dad should have a favorite chair.

And if the head of the family does not have his own "throne", then this is just a shame on the family! And this slip should be urgently corrected.

Making a chair for daddy is not at all as difficult as it seems. You can use several options.

It is easy to make a chair without a wooden base at all. Two rectangular pillows with foam inside are sewn together with a ‚Äúbook‚Äù. Such a chair is installed directly on the floor next to the wall so that there is an emphasis on the back.

It is more difficult to make a chair with a wooden base. In this case, the skill of carpentry is required. Although to simplify labor, you can take the old chair as a basis.

  1. First, it is checked for strength, the joints of the parts are strengthened - glued or fastened with the help of "corners".
  2. Then the legs are partially sawn off, because the chair will have a fairly high seat.
  3. Details are cut from plywood, which will serve as armrests. They are stuffed with one edge on the side of the seat. The other edge, adjacent to the already nailed side, is attached to the back.
  4. Furniture foam is glued to the seat, back and sides. Moreover, it is necessary to build a roller in the upper part of the armrests, applying in this place not one, but several pieces of packing.
  5. The cover on the chair is cut separately. Measurements should be done after laying foam.
  6. The fabric on the chair should be fixed from the bottom, pulling the edges with twine and knotted.
