sights

The island of Rhodes has a rich history and a huge number of attractions. The inhabitants explain even the origin of its name by two myths. One is associated with the Greek gods, and the second with the first settlers. But you will certainly be more interested in hearing them from the Greeks! Now we list the most popular local attractions:

  1. The residence of the Catholic Order of Hospitallers is the Rhodes Fortress. With her beauty, she conquered many people, one of which is the Italian dictator Mussolini. In the 30s of the last century, he rebuilt it for King Victor Emanuel III. Inside the fortress, the ancient square of Hippocrates, dotted with restaurants and tourist fairs, has been preserved.
  2. Valley of Petaludes. It is an interesting natural refuge for butterflies. In the summer months there are so many that it seems that they are everywhere!
  3. Lindos and the Acropolis are the ancient ruins of ancient cities with architecture erected before Christian times. They are striking in their scope. There is also the Olympic Stadium - the last surviving analogue of the Olympia Stadium!
  4. Natural Park "Seven Springs". It is listed by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site.
  5. The medieval castle of Castello and the Church of the Assumption of the Virgin.

All this attracts tourists from all over the globe. Nowhere else in the world can you find such a large number of attractions on such a tiny piece of land!
