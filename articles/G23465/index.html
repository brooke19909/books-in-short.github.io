Helsinki Tallinn Ferry - all options!

The distance from Helsinki to Tallinn is only 80 km, but for tourists there are only 2 convenient options. The first is to fly by plane. This is undoubtedly fast, the journey time is only 30 minutes. But, on the other hand, ticket prices "bite" - from 110 euros per person. Therefore, the Helsinki ‚Äì Tallinn ferry is the best option. It will not take much time (from 1.5 hours), moreover, it is comfortable and exciting. This route is very popular. Ferries depart several times a day, and more than one company serves this line.





Linda Line

Linda Line Express is the fastest and cheapest option on the Helsinki-Tallinn route. Prices range from 19 euros and do not depend on the time of departure. You will spend only 1.5 hours on the way. Among the minuses can be called the lack of a car compartment. The company is represented by the vessels Caroline and Marilyn. Each ship has bars, shops and business centers.

Viking Line

The Viking Line Helsinki-Tallinn ferry is an opportunity to get to your destination with comfort and at reasonable prices. All ships operate duty-free shops. In the evenings, exciting programs and great restaurants await you. Viking Line is the first company to abandon fixed prices. The company's dynamic prices depend on the time of departure and loading of the vessel. The cost is from 24 euros. The ships also have the ability to transport a car.

Tallink Silya



This is a rather famous Baltic Sea ferry company . Many Tallink Silya ships have a high degree of comfort. Three ships of this company ply the Helsinki-Tallinn route: Star, Superstar and Silja Evropa. The first two ships differ in affordable prices from 19 euros and run three times a day between these European capitals. The ship "Silja Europa" is a luxury ship offering exquisite service and plenty of entertainment. Prices - from 77 euros in low season.



How to save on travel

Choosing the Helsinki-Tallinn ferry you should know how to save. This applies not only to budget vessels, but also to more expensive options.

  1. Buy tickets during the low season, usually from Sunday to Thursday. Prices also depend on the time and month when the route is planned. For this reason, it is necessary to know the prices and timetable of the Tallinn-Helsinki ferry in advance. This will save you a lot.
  2. If you want to buy tickets for the Helsinki-Tallinn ferry and vice versa, then get round-trip at once. This way you can save up to 10%.
  3. 10% discounts are also provided to students and retirees upon presentation of the relevant documents.
  4. Traveling as a group is beneficial, you can count on bonuses.
  5. Dynamic prices provide a unique opportunity to buy a ticket at a very low price, provided the vessel is lightly loaded.
  6. If you often travel on the ships of one company, you can immediately purchase a serial ticket for 10 trips.
  7. Special offers are another way to get the cheapest tickets. Such promotions are usually carried out when making an online reservation. The downside is that the ticket is not refundable.

Thus, the 2013 Tallinn-Helsinki ferry is the most affordable and convenient option for moving from the Estonian capital to the Finnish one.
