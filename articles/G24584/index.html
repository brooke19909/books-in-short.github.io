What is an anime?

Today there are few people who have never heard of anime. This has become part of the lives of many teenagers, children and even adults. Anime comes from the English word Animation (animation) - this is a Japanese animation with easily recognizable images: big eyes in half faces, incredible hairstyles, bright colors.

The first anime began to appear in the early 1900s. By the beginning of World War II, the style of drawing was controlled by the government, so a technique appeared that was as different as possible from Western cartoons. Over time, Japanese culture has penetrated the west, winning fans around the world.

Bright and beautiful characters from Japanese cartoons won the hearts of millions of people, including needlewomen. The art of embroidery allows you to see your beloved hero nearby, create it with your own hands. High-quality work will become an original bookmark, decorate clothes or successfully fit into the interior of the room. Consider the anime cross stitch patterns.

How to make an embroidery pattern yourself?

Such schemes are not widely distributed on the Internet or sold in needlework stores. If you cannot find the desired sample, you can do it yourself.

Tips for Beginners

The theme of anime patterns for cross-stitch will be preferred by teenage girls rather than adult and experienced needlewomen, so there are some tips for beginners. They will help to enjoy the work.

The main set of tools for embroidery:

  • Thread floss.
  • Hoop.
  • Canvas.
  • Scissors.
  • Lamp.
  • Needles.

Recommendations:

  1. Canvas "Aida 14" is most convenient, since it has medium-sized crosses.
  2. Canvas needs to be bought with a margin. Cutting off the desired piece from it, it is desirable to overcast the edges.
  3. Mark the fabric into 10 * 10 squares.
  4. Start embroidering small patterns, immediately do not undertake large ones.
  5. Prepare a well-lit place for embroidery, where it is comfortable to sit, enough light, and everything you need (threads, scissors, needles) will be at hand.
  6. Wash hands before starting work so as not to stain the picture.
  7. It is better to embroider with a full cross, other methods are more difficult.
  8. Do not make knots - this will affect the beauty of the wrong side and the durability of the finished work. A loose thread may come out at the first wash.
  9. Sew even crosses. Do not take too long a thread, otherwise it will be confused.
  10. The top stitch should look in the same direction. The lower half-cross is embroidered from the lower left to the right (looks like this: /), and the upper from the upper left to the lower right (looks like this: \). The process is better not to get confused.
  11. It is more convenient to start embroidery with the color that is most.

Embroidery should bring joy, so you need to do the work at your own pace, without competing with anyone. The main rule is to have fun. The finished picture can be framed in a baguette by contacting the workshop, or make a frame yourself.
