Extraterrestrial beings from a scientific point of view





Scientists believe that on planets where most of the surface is occupied by the ocean, aquatic individuals similar to those that live on Earth can live. Where there is strong gravity and a dense atmosphere, large, strong and aggressive creatures are likely to dwell. On ice-covered planets, perhaps only bacteria can live.

The molecules on Earth are built primarily from carbon. As a result of this, some scientists suggest that life on other planets is possible only if carbon is present on them.

So, American and British scientists suggest that they will soon find a planet similar to the Earth, which revolves around a red dwarf (a cold star that produces much less light from the Sun). Such a hypothetical planet was called Aurelia.

It is believed that the beings of such a planet are similar to the earthly senses: eyes, nose, ears, skin, and taste sensors. They have a brain and a data center, a long neck and legs.

Another hypothetical object of study is the Blue Moon, a satellite of the planet like Jupiter, completely covered by water with a dense atmosphere. What do aliens look like there? Hypotheses are put forward that due to excess oxygen, huge flying creatures with a wingspan of up to ten meters can live there.

In 2010, however, scientists discovered that there are organisms that do not need oxygen to function. Instead of a carbon atom, organic structures there most likely form silicon, which in many respects resembles carbon in its properties. Silicon-based life is thought to be much less diverse than carbon-based.
