Operating leases are usually short-term. It lasts less than ten years, which makes it attractive when the liner is needed to launch a project (company) or a trial extension of the official carrier.

Depreciation

In operating leases, aircraft equipment is not fully depreciated during the lease. After its expiration, it can be rented out again or returned to the owner. On the other hand, the residual price of the airliner at the end of the lease is important to the owner. The owner may ask that the returned equipment undergo maintenance (for example, C-check) in order to rush the transfer to the next operator. As well as in other leasing areas, a security (guarantee) deposit is often required in an air lease.

Special shape

So what is wet leasing? This is a special type of operating room when an airplane is leased with the crew. That is, when an aircraft, its crew, insurance (ACMI) and maintenance, one airline (lessor) entrusts to another or another type of business, acting as an air travel intermediary (lessee), which pays for the administration on an hourly basis.





The tenant supplies fuel, the payments also include taxes, airport fees, any other duties and so on. Its flight number applies. Wet leasing, as is customary, lasts from 1 to 24 months. A shorter lease is considered as a charter short-term flight on behalf of the client.

Commercial aircraft

And yet, what is aviation leasing for? It is used due to the high cost of airliners. For example, the Boeing 737 Next Generation in 2008 cost about $ 58.5-69.5 million. It is used by Ryanair and Southwest Airlines. In general, few airlines can afford to pay for their air fleet in cash, as they have low profits.





Commercial steel birds are bought by airlines using more complex methods of financing and leasing (by attracting borrowed capital and obtaining loans). The most popular schemes for allocating commercial aircraft are financial and operational leasing, and secured loans. An example of a wet lease agreement can be obtained from any relevant company. There are other options for paying for aircraft:

  • bank loan or finance lease;
  • real money;
  • operator leasing and sale or financial leasing;
  • manufacturer support;
  • tax leasing;
  • EETCs (Trust Certificate for Equipment).

These schemes primarily relate to tax and accounting. They include interest, amortization of tax deductions and operating expenses, which can reduce tax liabilities for the financier, operator and lessor.

Private airliners

Private jet leasing is identical to a car loan or mortgage. A basic transaction for a corporate or small private jet can be done this way:

  • the borrower provides basic information about the expected aircraft and about himself to the creditor;
  • the lender determines the price of the aircraft;
  • the lender searches the property by the registration number of the board to find problems in the property right;
  • the lender prepares materials for the transaction: collateral agreement, bill of exchange, guarantee from a third party (if the borrower is less creditworthy).

At the conclusion of such a transaction, credit documents are drawn up, ownership and cash are transferred.
