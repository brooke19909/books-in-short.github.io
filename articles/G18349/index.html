G. Abakan: area, facts from history

In 1675, the first Russian fortress was built on the island of Sosnovy at the mouth of the Abakan River , which lasted until 1679. The Cossacks who built it were then the very first inhabitants of it. Families of immigrants from Russia settled near the village of Ah-Tigey, in this very fortress. They were engaged in agriculture.

For local residents, they became an example for the desire for a settled life. Thus, this village began to grow rapidly and turned into a village with the name Ust-Abakan.

Subsequently, the village (1822) was transformed into an administrative center (Kachinsky Steppe Duma). The latter was one of the four entities that united the tribes living at that time between the rivers Iyus and Abakan. Such thoughts (and councils) existed until 1912, after which the Abakan volost was created.

Curious and noteworthy is the history of the emergence of such a locality as Abakan. What region of Russia does it belong to?



After the October Revolution and the next reform of the administrative structure of the whole country in these places, the Khakass district of the Yenisei province was created. Ust-Abakan village became its center, which in 1931 was renamed the city of Abakan.

Now this city, as noted above, is the capital of the Republic of Khakassia (since 1991). He is famous for the fact that during the history of the 20th century he was considered the production center of the southern part of the Yenisei Siberia.

General description of the city

Where is the city of Abakan located? What is the region of Siberia? Eastern, slightly south of the city of Krasnoyarsk.

Geographically, the city is located in the very center of the basin called Khakassko-Minusinskaya. It has the shape of a bowl, the faces of which in the west are the Alatau Mountains (Kuznetsk), in the south - the Western Sayans, in the north and east - the Eastern Sayans. Almost in the middle from south to north, this basin is cut by the great Yenisei River, into which the considerable river Abakan flows. Just at the confluence of these rivers, this peculiar city of Abakan is located.
