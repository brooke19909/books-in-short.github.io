History of Bulgaria. First kingdom

The ancient history of Bulgaria is exceptionally rich. In this small country, various ancient nationalities managed to visit. Byzantines, Greeks, Thracians, Romans and other nations left their mark on this land.

The history of Bulgaria began at the end of the 5th century AD. The Bulgarian tribes included Utigurs, Kutrigurs, Ugori, Barsils, Balanjars, Savirs and others. These nationalities led a nomadic lifestyle in the territory from the Lower Danube to the North Caucasus and the Caspian Sea. Part of the Bulgarians (Kutrigurs and Onogurs) in the 630s formed an association. The history of Bulgaria began with Great Bulgaria, which fell thirty years after its founding. This happened as a result of the invasion of the Khazars.

Having settled on the lands of the future Northeast Bulgaria, they moved to the southern territories. There they settled in the Stara Planina region.

Over time, the Proto-Bulgarians penetrated deeper into Thrace. In the 7th century, the history of Bulgaria was marked by the founding of a state with its capital - the city of Pliska.

Over the next three centuries, the state government was engaged in repelling the attacks of Byzantium. The Byzantine Empire tried to destroy its new neighbor, but success in this matter has not been achieved.

From the 8th to the 9th centuries, the history of Bulgaria was marked by the annexation of the South Balkan region to the Rhodope Mountains, territories stretching south of Stara Planina, the Morava and Timok river basins. As well as the territory of the future North-West Bulgaria. In addition, part of Western and all of Central Macedonia joined the state.

The unification of lands continued under Boris 1. At that time, cities such as Prilep and Ohril were annexed. At the same time, Bulgarian power spread to certain territories of future Albanian lands. The territorial transformations of Boris 1 were consolidated by his son - Simeon.

As a result of the victories won by the leader of the new tribe of Turkic Bulgarians Khan Asparuh, the first kingdom of Bulgaria was formed. The history of the existence of the state was marked by the adoption in 864 of the baptism of Boris 1. Thus, the kingdom entered the circle of Christian countries.
