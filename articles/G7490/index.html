Lake Iseo (Italy), whose photos will be presented in this article, is the smallest and most beautiful in Lombardy. This place is rarely visited by tourists (guests of the country). More often Italians come here. This area is beautiful, like any other in Italy, and you can admire it endlessly.

Geographical position

The location of the object is northern Italy. Lake Iseo is located in Lombardy - a picturesque region, comfortably stretched between Brescia and Bergamo. This area is well developed, and visiting the lake and the city of the same name is considered prestigious. We can say that the region is fashionable in Italy.

A small lake, rarely visited by tourists from other countries - why did it become popular among "friends"? The fact is that its waters are washed by the most famous in Europe and the highest inhabited island (600 m above sea level) - Monte Isola, which translates as "mountain island". Also on the territory of Lake Iseo there are two more small islands - San Paolo and Loreto.

The river Olo flows through Iseo, and its banks are rocky and steep. However, this is not surprising for a country like Italy. Lake Iseo fills the basin of an ancient glacier. Attractions in the area

In the area where Lake Iseo (Italy) is located, there are many attractions . First of all, tourists cruise around the three islands. These are the main attractions of the Pre-Alpine zone (Monte Isola, Isola Loreto and Isola San Paolo).

Monte Isola is divided in half by the mountain. One part is forested, with steep slopes, and the second is more even and free from thickets. People live here. You should see this magnificence: small houses with spacious terraces, surrounded by olive trees and vineyards! At the very top is the main attraction - Madonna della Cheriola - a chapel resembling (like many other objects in the area) the medieval period. Noteworthy are the villages with villas - one more beautiful than the other. For example, in Sincignano there is the Church of St. Michael of 1648, and nearby - Oldofredi Castle. In Karzano, it is worthwhile to devote time to visiting the church of St. John the Baptist, and in Massa - the church of Saints Rocco and Pantaleon.

On the island of Loreto are the ruins of the town of Iseo, abandoned in the 16th century, and the ruins of an ancient fortress with two towers and a chapel were also found here. In general, it is a very picturesque island.





Italy, Lake Iseo - how to get there?

The easiest way is to fly to the city of Bergamo (by plane), then take the bus at the bus station opposite the railway and get to the city of Lovere, in the north of Iseo. You can also get to the lake by train from cities such as Milan and Brescia. If you have a car, you need to follow the A4 highway from Milan in a northwest direction.
