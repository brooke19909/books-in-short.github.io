Rest in the village of Morskoye, Crimea: reviews, description, features and interesting facts

In the south-east of the Crimea is the village of Morskoye. Since 2014, it belongs to the Sudak urban district and is part of the Russian Federation. This place has an ancient history, as it was very convenient for people.



Some interesting historical information

These edges were inhabited about 1700 years ago. But after 300 centuries, Genoa, competing with Venice, occupied this area to put its trading posts leading to the Middle East. And a century later the lands began to belong to the Ottoman Empire, there were only about three hundred people living there and fifty-six courtyards stood.

The village was gradually growing. At the end of the XVIII century, it became part of the Russian Empire. After numerous disturbances, only forty-two yards remained. In the seventeenth and nineteenth centuries, he was changed names and postscript to various regions and provinces. The village, which has long been called Kapsikhor, however, grew and was inhabited by the Tatars, who had a mosque and two trading shops, and about 500 people gathered residents. They actively cultivated grapes because they had their own land.

After World War II

When the revolution took place, the population was already about 1,500 people. Then the Second World War came to Crimea, and in 1944, after the liberation of the peninsula, all Crimean Tatars were resettled in Central Asia. Residents from different regions and republics of the USSR began to gather on free lands. These were mainly collective farmers from the Krasnodar and Stavropol Territories, and then from Ukraine. In 1945, Kapsichor was renamed. It became known as Marine.
