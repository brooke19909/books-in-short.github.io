Chocolate Museum (Bruges): Belgium's sweet tradition

France, Switzerland, Belgium - three countries that in the minds of many Europeans are associated with chocolate. We invite you to virtually visit the Chocolate Museum in Bruges - a city that is not without reason called the "sweet capital" of Belgium.

The country of delicious sweets

172 thousand tons. An impressive number! That is how much chocolate is produced in one year in Belgium. Moreover, almost half of the delicacies produced in the country are eaten by the Belgians themselves. There are 16 chocolate factories and about 2,000 shops in Belgium where you can buy various sweets.





Belgium was not accidental at all as the European center for chocolate production. In the early twentieth century, the state took over the Congo colony in central Africa. It was she who began to supply the Kingdom of Belgium with cocoa beans of excellent quality.

According to many experts, it is Belgian chocolate that meets all the requirements and quality standards that are put forward for this product. Therefore, you should definitely try it. Well, fans of millions of delicacies loved by all means should definitely visit the Chocolate Museum.

Bruges is a city in the north-west of the country that has long been recognized as the chocolate capital of Belgium. Here, in almost any cafe, tourists will be offered pralines, chocolate figures and various types of local sweets.

Museum of Chocolate, Bruges: photos and description

One unknown Belgian pharmacist tried for a very long time to create an effective and effective cough suppressant. But all his attempts were in vain. However, without suspecting it, the doctor suddenly made the greatest discovery. He managed to get a completely new product - dark chocolate! This fascinating story for all tourists will tell the Chocolate Museum. Bruges is a city of a thousand sweet stories that he is ready to share with all his guests.

The museum will tell tourists interestingly about how cocoa beans turn into chocolate. Yes, and show this amazing process! In addition, in the halls of the institution there are many interesting and sweet exhibits. For example, a sculpture of the Virgin Mary made of white chocolate or a huge two-meter chocolate egg.

The Chocolate Museum (Bruges) is an important source of cultural, geographical and historical knowledge about this product. It even has its own library with relevant literature and recipes.
