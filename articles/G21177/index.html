Metro station "Tulskaya". Its features, ground infrastructure

A huge role in the Moscow city transport system is played by the metro. It has many stations, including the Tulskaya metro station. Consider its features, architecture, design and attractions located in close proximity. How is it remarkable and what streets can you get off at this station?



Station history

Tula metro station was opened in 1983, on November 6. She became part of the first section of the Serpukhov line "Serpukhovskaya" - "South". Description

Currently, this station is part of the Serpukhov-Timiryazevskaya line. It is surrounded by such stations as Serpukhovskaya and Nagatinskaya. Metro Tula is a single-vault station, which has a shallow location. Its depth is only 9.5 meters. It was built according to the project developed by famous architects V.P. Kachurinits, N.G. Petukhova and N.I. Shumakov. A monolithic reinforced concrete structure was used as the main material. According to similar projects, several other stations were built located near the Tulskaya station. It has only one platform and two vestibules underground, which are located between Kholodilny Lane and Bolshaya Tula Street. It is the last shallowest station on the south side.

The lobby is dedicated to the armourers of Tula. Its track walls are covered with white marble, and the floor with gray granite. The main decoration is the original lamps in the shape of a rhombus and a carved ornament. They are installed at the junction of the arch and track walls. In addition, the edge of the vault is decorated with a peculiar ornament on concrete, consisting of geometric shapes. Along the center of the platform are benches with pointers located on them. This is a station without track development.



Exit to the city

The Tulskaya metro station has no ground lobbies, so in order to get to its platform, you must go through the underpass. From the northern lobby you can go to Bolshaya Tula Street, to Serpukhov Val and Danilovsky Val Streets. From the southern lobby you can exit onto the ZIL platform of the Paveletsky direction of the Moscow Railway. The famous Yerevan Plaza shopping center and ground transportation stops are also located here. The rise to the surface is carried out using escalators.
