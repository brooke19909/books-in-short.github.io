Where to find the simplest cross stitch patterns? Embroidery for beginner needlewomen

Along with new fashionable hobbies (decoupage, weaving from rubber bands or leather cords, temari, knitting without knitting needles and hooks), more traditional types of needlework are also gaining popularity. Cross stitching was no exception - the simplest type of decorative embroidery, with which you can both decorate household items and create real works of art - large-scale paintings. If large projects are not yet within your strength, start small: the simplest cross-stitch patterns will help you fill your hand and understand the peculiarities of the direction of stitches and fastening of the thread.





How to choose a design

Many lovers of unique home-made things keep detailed blogs and diaries where they describe the embroidery process in stages, from the development of the scheme and the selection of materials to the design of the finished work. In these blogs you can find interesting ideas for using small embroidered designs to create unique diaries, book covers, bookmarks, home textiles, needle cases, glasses or tool cases , children's or women's handbags and other accessories. Of course, not all craftswomen develop simple cross-stitch patterns - someone creates projects of truly artistic paintings and willingly shares them with those who want to create the same beauty.

Ready Kits

Sometimes experienced needlewomen forget that readers of their blogs often have only basic skills and knowledge in working with fabric, canvas and thread. In small projects, there are quite complex seams (for example, a chain stitch or an abundance of back-needle stitches, which requires painstaking work) or expensive decorative elements, starting with curly wooden or plastic buttons and ending with colored bells. Moreover: it may seem to some that they cannot afford to master this type of needlework as cross-stitch. Simple schemes of flowers, animals and miniature landscapes remain unfulfilled dreams.





Meanwhile, domestic manufacturers began to take into account the wishes of different consumer groups. Children's sets for embroidery with threads and beads, sets for visually impaired, complex projects for craftswomen containing satin ribbons appeared on sale. And, of course, the simplest cross - stitch patterns for beginner needlewomen. Some finished designs are only 10 x 10 centimeters, and some are even smaller. Such miniatures are the best choice for those who are not yet confident in their skills.

Create by yourself





Oddly enough, to develop your own small format design for every woman. To do this, you need only checkered paper and a ballpoint pen or a simple pencil (as an option - colored pens or even felt-tip pens). In a regular school notebook, you can draw any shapes and ornaments that your fantasy tells you, and then bring them to life on the canvas. In this way, even the most skilled bloggers develop simple cross-stitch patterns. Try it and you will succeed.
