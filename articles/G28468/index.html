How to choose a camera: an overview of the best models and reviews about manufacturers

This article is intended to help those who are going to purchase (but do not know how to choose) a camera. Experienced users can also find useful information on the most popular alternatives.

How to choose the right camera: required features

You should not buy a camera if it is deprived of at least one of the following features that have become standard in modern models.

  • Image stabilizer. Shaking hands can ruin the frame, but the best cameras are equipped with optical stabilizers for a clear shot. The cheaper models use digital correction, but it is not so effective.
  • LCD screen. A good display should be bright and clear so that you can view the photo or endorse even in strong sunlight. The standard is a 3 ‚Äùdiagonal screen.
  • Manual setting. In the price range above 10 thousand rubles, even basic digital cameras provide, at least partially, manual control, and high-end models give you the ability to fully control focus, aperture, shutter speed, etc. Some cameras allow you to customize user functions.
  • Strong optical zoom. Most modern digital cameras provide adequate optical zoom.
  • HD video. Many modern cameras can record high definition movies. However, some models only provide 720p recording. High-performance cameras can usually record 4K video.
  • Timer. All digital cameras allow you to set the time delay so that the photographer can join the group being shot.
  • Wireless connection. If you need a simple way to backup or transfer photos to your smartphone or tablet, then before choosing a camera, you need to check whether it supports Wi-Fi wireless. Some models also allow connected devices to remotely control the camera.
  • Autofocus Most digital cameras can automatically focus on the subject.

What to decide?

Cost. For 11 thousand rubles, you can purchase a compact, convenient camera for quick shooting with limited creative control. If you need a higher image quality, longer zoom or the ability to change lenses, the price will increase to 14 thousand rubles. and much higher with the best SLR cameras.

The size. Modern cameras can fit in your jeans pocket or jacket. Interchangeable lens compacts are usually larger. Such devices weigh about 140 g, so they do not burden a backpack or jacket too much during outdoor adventures.

Is protection necessary? Good cameras, as a rule, are made not of plastic, but of durable light metal. The best of them are sealed to protect against dust and rain. But you can find surprisingly durable and waterproof cameras cheaper than 28 thousand rubles. The quality of photographs will not be professional, but sufficient to please the photographer himself.

How many megapixels do you really need? Some of the best digital cameras have only a 12 megapixel image sensor. Some offer 24 megapixels or more, which provides high definition details when printing large photos. However, practice shows that not always everything works in this way, so you should not judge a camera by this indicator only.

Are you planning to shoot moving objects? If so, then before you select a digital camera, you must check the shooting speed. The fastest cameras capture 16 or more frames per second. Again, you should not judge only by the numbers: the best models are equipped with fast processors that can quickly cope with such a data stream, but many make you wait until the data is written to the memory card.

Will special effects and modes be used? Even cheap cameras provide at least a few of these features. For example, you can give the photo a nostalgic shade of sepia or make it look like a miniature model.

Is shooting in RAW format necessary? Most basic digital cameras record images only in JPEG files that are pre-processed in the camera (sharpening, smoothing granular noise, etc.). Some photographers prefer to shoot in RAW format and later process photos on a PC, thus gaining full control over the editing process. Advanced digital cameras allow you to record both RAW and JPEG files.

How to choose a camera by type?

Basic level digital cameras. They are in the price range up to 14 thousand rubles and are suitable for beginners or those who need a pocket-sized, simple camera that does not greatly burden the family budget. Some models of this type are truly tiny (slightly larger than a business card and less than 2 cm thick) and inexpensive. Although not a single camera in this category matches the professional quality of the best cameras, with the right choice you can get decent shots. Will photos be better than on a smartphone? Probably not, but even a cheap camera has more zoom (and battery life) than any phone.

Protected Models. When floating or climbing rocks, boating or skiing, I don‚Äôt want to risk my smartphone, so shockproof and waterproof cameras are useful here, the characteristics of which will make it possible to take pictures without worrying about anything.

Digital cameras with super zoom. This is another type of camera that smartphones cannot match. The longest telephoto cameras of this type with 83x zoom allow you to study lunar craters in detail. They are great for leisure and sporting events, as they take everything from wide angles to extreme close-ups.

Mid-range digital cameras. These work tools provide better photos and can handle difficult situations - dim light, close-ups, and fast movement. They provide more control over the settings so you can be creative in your shots.

Compact cameras with interchangeable lenses. Also known as hybrid or mirrorless. They allow you to change lenses like bulky SLR cameras, but much less. Can match DSLR quality.
