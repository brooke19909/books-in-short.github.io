The first Russian resort

The first health resort in Russia was the Marcial Waters sanatorium, founded on the orders of Peter the Great in 1719, which he himself came to treatment in this region several times, and many noble people of the country began to visit these places with his "light" hands.

These places were truly popular only in Soviet times, when modern buildings were built, the chemical and healing properties of the water at the source were fully studied, and wellness programs for various types of diseases were developed.





Located in a quiet beautiful place in a pine forest, in the immediate vicinity of the unique reserve of rare Karelian birch, surrounded by lakes, the sanatorium is a balneological resort, well known in Europe.

Not all SPA hotels in Karelia in the forest can offer such a level of comfort and service as in the Marcial Waters sanatorium.
