Who sponsored Hitler: historical facts, truth and fiction

It has become a tradition to explain Hitler's rise to power with his extraordinary oratory, political will and charisma. The Versailles Treaty, which became the result of the defeat in the First World War, also points to the difficult economic situation in the country. All this is fair, but it is important to consider that no political ambitions can be realized without serious funding. That is why the question of who sponsored Hitler so worries researchers.

Where did the money flow to the party cashier?

It is difficult to disagree with the fact that without the sufficient material resources necessary to pay for very expensive propaganda events, the German National Socialist Workers' Party (NSDAP) would never have been able to come to power, and would be forever lost in the variety of other political movements of that time. For all serious scholars of the history of National Socialism, this fact is undeniable, but when they answer the question of who sponsored Hitler, they cannot come to a consensus.

There is an assumption that at the initial stage the fascist party was financed by the leaders of the Reichswehr - the German armed forces limited in accordance with the terms of the Versailles Peace Treaty. According to another version, millions of stamps were transferred to party accounts by German industrialists, who were expecting, after Hitler came to power and the start of total militarization of the country, abundant government orders. Supporters of both hypotheses are probably right, since the funds spent by the future Fuhrer on the way to the heights of power were truly enormous.

At the same time, he publicly named the two largest American corporations - Ford and General Motors, as well as the Governor of the Bank of England - Norman Montague. According to him, both the leaders of these industrial giants and a number of representatives of the British financial oligarchy, in pursuit of their interests, were sponsors of Adolf Hitler, and, therefore, should be responsible for the disaster.

These revelations were extremely disadvantageous to the leadership of the countries that were part of the anti-Hitler coalition.
