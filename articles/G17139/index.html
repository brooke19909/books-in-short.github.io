The capital of Abkhazia - Sukhumi

Abkhazia is a very ancient country with a unique history, which means that there is something to see. In each town of this not yet recognized republic, there are many unique architectural monuments, historical and cultural attractions. Excursions in Abkhazia include the stunningly beautiful Lake Ritsa, the Abaat fortress in Gagra, Seaside Park, and other interesting natural monuments that you must see. In addition to a resort holiday, Abkhazia also offers active, for example, jipping, an unforgettable experience that will be remembered for a long time.





The sun, gentle breeze, fruit, the purest Black Sea - all this has the potential to spend holidays in this wonderful country. At the same time, rest in Abkhazia by a savage has several advantages. Firstly, the cost of it is much lower, and secondly, it is more comfortable to relax.

The capital of Abkhazia - Sukhumi - is a charming resort town, completely sun-drenched and lost in exotic flowers and plants. The main street of this city is its embankment bordering palm trees, magnolias, eucalyptus trees, oleanders, camellias.

The climate in Sukhumi is subtropical, humid and warm. The sun shines for more than eight months a year, and the air here is so clean and transparent that even the slopes of the Adjarian mountains are visible in the early morning.



The capital of Abkhazia combines straight straight streets built in the style of past centuries with the emerald luxury of parks, shady alleys, boulevards with blooming mimosas and acacias, as well as snow-white arrays of residential complexes.

The entire coast of the city is wide beaches with sun loungers, umbrellas and a large number of vacationers.

Over the course of almost three centuries of history, the capital of Abkhazia has repeatedly been destroyed and rebuilt. Today's city is a model of urban development of the last century.

The capital of Abkhazia received the glory of one of the best health resorts during the time of Tsarist Russia, when numerous health resorts began to be erected here. After all, the climate of Sukhumi has always been considered optimal for the treatment of many heart and lung diseases.

Historians believe that the ancestor of Sukhumi is Dioskuria - the legendary city that was founded by the ancient Greeks and was part of Colchis. It existed for only five hundred years, and once disappeared without a trace under the Black Sea waters. Today it is difficult to say whether this is a legend or a historical fact, but the fact that at the beginning of our era the Romans built a small fortress Sebastopolis in its place, which became the property of the Byzantines, is known for certain. And these are only the first pages of the history of Sukhumi, and today it is already in the tens of centuries.

The city recently turned 2500 years old, and this is what makes it attractive enough for historical tourism. The capital of Abkhazia and the surrounding areas have preserved many monuments of the ancient and Roman periods, such as the palace of Tsar Bagrat, the ruins of the Sebastopolis fortress, as well as the Beslet bridge and the ruins of old fortresses, temples and monasteries.
