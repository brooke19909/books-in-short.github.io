Do not confuse with Alexei Grigoryevich Bobrinsky

In the course of research of historical sources about the graph, a collision was found with the namesake of the hero, with whom he is often confused. We are talking about Alexei Grigoryevich Orlov - the son of Catherine the Second and her favorite Grigory Orlov. He was born before Catherine came to power, so he was immediately taken away to live in another family. The boy never bore the name Orlov, he was named Count Bobrinsky. Not remarkable for anything. Adventurer and conspirator: killed or not killed?

This, of course, is about Peter the Third - the non-merry wife of Ekaterina Alekseevna. This story is known to all and repeatedly beaten. Most often in the biography of Count Alexei Orlov, the main point is precisely this episode (in vain, I must say). The Orlov brothers were part of the party of young military men from the guards regiments formed around Grand Duchess Catherine Alekseevna. Their goal was simple and clear: to give power to Catherine, and to excommunicate the rightful heir from the throne once and for all. The main coordinator of the entire palace plot was none other than the middle brother Alexei Orlov. Catherine gained power thanks precisely to his courage and composure. The Empress became the eternal debtor of the Orlov family. It should be noted that in the end this fact of happiness did not bring any of the brothers.

It is one thing to put the Grand Duchess on the throne. It is completely different to deal with the legal emperor. Alexey Grigoryevich was a member of a small initiative group dealing with this slippery and reputation-hazardous issue. The abdication of Peter the Third was torn out. But in the matter of the murder of the emperor, there is continuous fog, stereotypes and historical cliches.

Foes loved to call Alexei Grigorievich regicide. They did this often and with pleasure: "he was a regicide in the soul." The main argument for a long time was the famous note by Alexei Orlov to Catherine, in which he allegedly confessed to the murder of the emperor. But it wasn‚Äôt just a letter, but only a copy of it, which was in the hands of the Oryol detractors (there were many of them). Modern historians are inclined to the version of a fake document and to the fact that Alexei Grigoryevich Orlov had only an indirect relation to the death of Peter the Third.

But what can be said with accuracy is about the many services that no one else could do for Catherine. By the way, Alexey Orlov has never been an imperial favorite.

Secret missions

Here is just one example of missions that few people know about. Three years before the Chesme epic, Count Orlov arrived in Moscow on the secret order of the empress. He needed to investigate the numerous riots that occurred in different places in central Russia. The audience in the investigation was not easy - it was the Don Cossacks, who established contacts with the Tatars who lived in the neighborhood. Their joint plans had many unpleasant and even dangerous intentions - to raise, for example, an uprising in Ukraine.

The concentration of Tatars at the borders became critical, the danger of rebellion with secret support from Turkey took on a threatening character. Such a situation could lead to an armed clash with Turkey, which was highly undesirable in that political situation.

Alexey Grigorievich faced the most difficult task: to nullify the risk of war with the Turks, put out the Tatar unrest, understand the behavior of the Cossack hetman. He wandered around to different places, collected information, made conclusions, met the right people, and eventually eliminated the political crisis.

Few people know about these details. And they characterize Alexei Grigoryevich as an international strategist much more fully and more reliably than an international conflict with Princess Tarakanova.

It will never be possible to find out if he was sincere in his new "Greek" ideology. Or a beautiful case with the restoration of ancient culture (at the same time as Egyptian) was specially invented to cover a simple and clear imperial goal - Russia's access to the Black Sea. One thing is clear: Alexei Grigoryevich wrote a bright and non-standard page in Russian military history. That's how it was.

To send a military squadron whose actions would be supported by local uprisings of the Greeks and other discontented - that was what had to be done immediately in the light of the situation. To implement the plan, Orlov proposed himself, with which Catherine immediately agreed. The mission included more than just fleet command. Chesme victory and Oryol genetics

It is useful to recall that July 7 is the Day of the military glory of Russia in honor of the victory of the Russian fleet over the Turkish precisely in the Chesme battle. In most sources, this naval battle is described as a heroic brilliant victory for Russian weapons: four ships and a firebrand attacked the Turkish fleet at night and set it on fire. The Turks were almost completely burnt out, and the Russian squadron remained intact, preventing the Basurmans from extinguishing fires and escaping.

In fact, everything did not happen, and this is most interesting. The plan and the campaign, as well as the battle itself, were, of course, an adventure of clear water. The first squadron (and there were two) barely made it from Arkhangelsk to Gibraltar, with breakdowns and the loss of several ships. The sailors were mowed by illness, now almost half of the crew consisted of Danes hired in Copenhagen. As a result, the first to reach the destination was only one "Saint Eustache" with a broken mast. Gradually, another six ships pulled themselves together: the squadron was re-emerging. The Turks could kill her in two. But then they did not understand that this battered camp was the Russian navy. Lucky so lucky. And the Russian "camp", having waited for the second squadron, vigorously carried out an amphibious operation and occupied several coastal towns with the help of the rebellious Greeks. Hopes for Greek patriotism did not materialize, but Alexey Orlov was able to draw conclusions and, more importantly, quickly rebuild. The main event was a naval battle.

It is believed that the Chesme victory of Count Orlov was a pure coincidence due to the catastrophic chain of fires on Turkish ships. It is possible that it was so. But who brought both squadrons to difficult and distant lands, didn‚Äôt abandon the fulfillment of the original plan, was not afraid to engage in battle with the Turkish armada, twice the power of the Russian fleet?

Courage, dare, perseverance, impudence - a long list of Oryol family qualities that helped with Chesme. Indeed, in the event of a defeat, Orlov had nowhere to go - either to fix it or hide. Then he put his life at stake. Yes, and his fleet.

The political benefit came out huge. The Turks became extremely cautious, Europe was quiet, and Russia got access to the Black Sea. With the personal career of Count Orlov, everything was also in order: in addition to the usual privileges and awards, Alexei Grigoryevich received a solid addition to his glorious surname. He became Orlov-Chesmensky.

Moral and State Security Considerations

The fact is that by the time the next impostor and pretender to the Russian throne appeared in Italy, Catherine had already received a dose of the strongest allergens in the form of the same "children of Lieutenant Schmidt", the main of which was Emelyan Pugachev. Therefore, Orlov was entrusted with tough and decisive action: to approach the Italian Ragusa as part of the squadron and, if necessary, force him to issue an adventurer, threatening to shell the city from the sea.

But Alexey Orlov solved the problem differently, making life difficult for himself to the very end. He offered the Tarakanova hand, heart, and, most importantly, support in winning the throne. So he lured the princess to Russian territory - the deck of the ship. She was arrested and placed in a dungeon where she died of a fever. By the way, Princess Tarakanova was never an innocent sheep, and the danger to the integrity of the state was due to a conspiracy with some foreign powers that be.

You can have a different attitude to the chosen method of solving the problem. You can reason from a moral point of view, or you can - for reasons of state security. In any case, Count Orlov executed the order quickly and bloodlessly, in the most efficient manner from the state point of view.

Oryol trotters

The following year, Alexei Grigoryevich resigned and left for his native Moscow. By that time, the elder brother Gregory was one of the empress‚Äôs favorites, and the Orlov clan lost its influence.

Alexey Orlov, like any outstanding person, never got bored: he always had a lot to do. But the main was a very ambitious task. He decided to breed the Russian breed of running horses. He accomplished this task brilliantly: the famous Oryol trotters appeared at the Moscow stud farm - the beloved brainchild of Alexei Grigorievich.

Oryol trotter is an integrated breed. It was a difficult cross. The English, Danish, Arab and German breeds were selected so that the Oryol trotter had special and unique qualities. These are light-duty sports or walking horses with excellent running trotting abilities that are inherited.
