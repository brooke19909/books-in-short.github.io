What is a pictogram in antiquity and in our time?

What is a pictogram? This is a certain sign (or record), indicating the most important and most recognizable features of an object, phenomenon, object, to which, in fact, it points. Most often, this is a pattern that has historically been formed over the centuries. Today, pictograms are defined as a highly specialized and secondary role. This, for example, road signs, icon-elements of graphical interfaces, etc. Let's talk about this in more detail in our article.

What is the pictogram in early writing?

This is an ordinary image that was used as a symbol. The fact is that the icon usually corresponds to a specific object. In this case, its purpose is to provide the most specific information, which emphasizes the typical features of this subject.



What is a pictogram in pictographic writing?

This is a characteristic type of writing, the signs of which are also intended to indicate the object depicted by them. Pictograms and their meaning in such letters have been used since ancient times - at the dawn of the appearance of writing itself as a process of non-verbal communication between ancient people - and were the prerogative of such cultures as:

  • Egyptian
  • Mesopotamian;
  • Chinese
  • Aztec and others.




Today pictographic letters are very popular in the Dongba language. By the way, it is owned by elderly residents of the Nashi people living in the Tibetan foothills.

What does the pictogram mean in the design hypostasis?

Who, if not professional designers, should know that an easily recognizable and stylized graphic image should be as simple as possible. Pictograms in design pursue this very goal, since they serve to facilitate the visual perception of an object, action or phenomenon in the eyes of the consumer. Here they enhance the characteristic features of the object that they depict. For example, this is a red X, denoting a hospital, this is the letter "P", signifying the entrance to the parking lot, these are triangular little men indicating that we have a toilet.



What is a pictogram in computer science?

  • This is an icon or element that is an integral part of the graphical user interface.
  • This is not a very large image on the screen of a monitor (or TV), which serves to identify an object (for example, a file, program). This also includes the logos of the TV channels (for example, the white unit in the upper right corner of the First Channel) and the favicon of the tabs of certain sites.
