Ivan the Terrible, on the one hand, is a wise reformer, an outstanding and talented figure of the Moscow state, on the other, a bloody tyrant, a real murderer who subjected his subjects to monstrous repressions. How did the formation of the personality of one of the most extraordinary rulers happen, which had a noticeable impact on the course of Russian history?

The baby was named Ivan in honor of the predecessor of Jesus Christ - John the Baptist. The name of Ivan was also the grandfather of the little prince. His father, Prince Vasily, lived only three years after the birth of the heir.

It is rather difficult to briefly describe the thirteen years that have passed between the coronation and the wedding to the kingdom. Ivan was an 8-year-old boy when his mother Elena Glinskaya died. Feelings of abandonment and loneliness developed in him the habit of constantly looking around and listening.

Ivan the Terrible, surrounded during ceremonies by royal luxury and subservient humility, in everyday life fully experienced shyness in everything, indifference and neglect on the part of the boyars and princes. Children's hardships were exacerbated by the fierce struggle for power of the boyar groups, accompanied by killings, intrigues and violence.

The childhood of Ivan the Terrible became the time when unattractive features of his character were formed: secrecy, suspiciousness, pretense, duplicity and cruelty.

At 12, an embittered teenager tormented and mutilated animals, throwing them from high towers, at 13 gave the order to kill one of the boyars. According to a contemporary, from the age of 15 the future tsar has a new fun - to ride with young men on horses in the streets and markets, to beat and rob ordinary people.

Ivan the Terrible became the king of all Russia in January 1547, when a wedding to the kingdom took place in the Assumption Cathedral - a solemn ceremony borrowed from Byzantium. The future sovereign was dressed in royal clothes during the wedding and laid a crown on him. Thus ended the childhood and adolescence of the first tsar of the Russian kingdom.
