Smartphone Lenovo A516: reviews, photos, features, an overview of new features

Just three color options for the case are offered by Lenovo for this model: pink, white and gray. The first option is best suited for the weak half of humanity. CPU

The central processor is one of the bottlenecks in the Lenovo A516. Reviews indicate a low level of its performance. And it's hard to argue with them. The MTK6572 CPU is built on the basis of the "ARM A7" architecture and consists of only two energy-efficient cores that cannot boast of high computing power. The clock frequency of each of them can reach a decent 1.3 GHz with a maximum processor load. Resource-intensive tasks, such as 3D toys, will not work on it. But for the remaining cases, its capabilities will be quite enough. You can watch the same film in the * .avi or * .mpeg4 format without any problems. A similar situation is with listening to audio clips with the extension "* .MP3" or surfing the Internet. But, again, the manufacturer is positioning the smartphone as an entry-level solution, and it is with the tasks of this level that it can cope without problems.





Graphics and screen

The main advantage of this device is the rather large screen size, the diagonal of which is an impressive 4 and a half inches. It is based on a very high-quality IPS - a matrix capable of displaying more than 16 million colors. Moreover, the display resolution is 854 by 480. To ensure the proper level of performance of the graphics subsystem, the Mali-400MP adapter is integrated into the device. This is a fairly productive solution that can easily cope with any task, unlike a central processor, whose processing power leaves much to be desired. The graphics system can process up to 5 touches to the display surface at the same time.
