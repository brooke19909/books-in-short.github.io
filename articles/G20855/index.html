Zone Creation History

‚ÄúBaikal harbor‚Äù is a special economic zone of tourist-recreational type, created by the decree of the Government of the Russian Federation in 2007. Geography

The territory of the zone is located within the boundaries of the municipality "Pribaikalsky District" (the central part of Buryatia) on the eastern coast of Lake Baikal. The coastline of the great lake stretches along the SEZ for about 60 km - from the village of Gremyachinsk to Cape Katkova on the border with the Barguzinsky district. From the south, the SEZ adjoins the valley of the Chaim River, which stretches in these places. On the territory of the tourist zone are Lake Kokotel and - south - Mount Bull. The settlements located within the zone are Gremyachinsk, Goryachinsk, Turk, Istok, Cheryomushka, Yartsi, Kokotel.

Specialization

The specialization of the SEZ TRT ‚ÄúBaikal Harbor‚Äù was to be the development of ski tourism, family vacations, spa, treatment with Eastern medicine, the organization of cruises on Lake Baikal. Today the zone includes five objects: Turk, Goryachinsk, Sands, Mount Bychya, Bezymyannaya Bay. They planned to build:

  • in Turku - the shopping and entertainment center "Fishing Village"; a yacht club with a marina serving the infrastructure;
  • in Sands - a tourist and recreational complex consisting of several areas of entertainment and recreation;
  • on Mount Bychye - an all-season ski resort;
  • in Bezymyannaya Bay - a center for family recreation;
  • in Goryachinsk - spa resort.

Construction

The first stone in the construction of the SEZ was laid in July 2009. The ceremony was attended by A. Kudrin, Minister of Finance of the Russian Federation, V. Nagovitsyn, President of the Republic of Buryatia, A. Alpatov, head of the RusSEZ, as well as resident companies. At the end of 2010, the first phase of the tourist zone infrastructure was put into operation: the embankment, power supply facilities, domestic sewage, and on-site water supply networks. In February 2011, the work of treatment facilities and electrical substations began. The Ministry of Economic Development of the Russian Federation concluded an agreement on the development of a tourist zone with nine residents of the SEZ. It was planned to attract investment in the amount of 36 billion rubles.

Problems

The further history of the Baikal Harbor is fraught with several scandals. In 2014, it became clear that the construction was failed, because after 7 years of the more than 20 billion budget funds invested in it, only one fifth was mastered, and despite the considerable amount of money spent on the development of the zone, tourists who came to the lake continue to rest "Savages" or rent houses in villages where the infrastructure is completely lacking.

Rebirth

It seemed that the construction of the SEZ could put an end to. But everything changed with the change of leadership of the republic and the advent of the general director of the tourist zone A. Togoshiev. Currently, the SEZ is being transferred to republican ownership. The issue of attracting new effective residents and terminating agreements that violate deadlines is also being addressed. At two sites - in Turk and Sands - the infrastructure necessary to start work has already been built. It is planned to complete the construction of the Baikal Harbor by 2021. Currently, over 22 billion rubles have been attracted to the project. state and over 47 billion rubles. private investment.

Future key water tourism center

Today, although Turka is not yet a particularly promoted and attractive place to relax, many tourists believe that you should visit it without fail - at least just stopping along the road. Climb the lighthouse, walk along the promenade, swim in the river of the same name - the water in it is much warmer than in the open lake. The weather in Turka is quite consistent with the mild climate, which allows strawberries and raspberries to be grown in local farms without warming. In the vicinity of the village there are extensive plantations of mushrooms and berries: blueberries, lingonberries, cranberries.

The port in Turku was the first facility from which the construction of the Baikal Harbor SEZ began. Someday, as planned, a real water tourism center with a hotel complex, a cottage village, a yacht club and much more will certainly grow here. In the meantime, in this village with about 1,400 residents, vacationers have to be content with guest houses of various levels: from institutions with simple and uncomfortable rooms to hotels with luxury rooms.
