Order of Lenin: description of the award and history of the order

The world of orders and awards is multifaceted. It is full of varieties, design options, history, award conditions. Previously, people, money, fame, their own interests were not so important. The motto for everyone was this: first, the Motherland, then their own personal life. This article will focus on the Order of Lenin.

Where are its origins?

Perhaps more than one person is interested in this. The Order of Lenin first appeared in 1926 (then there was already the highest award for military personnel - the Order of the Red Banner). The state adoption of such an order was to reward the commanders and soldiers of the Red Army and Navy. He had to replace with himself all the highest awards, as well as those that were lower in the hierarchy. Initially, they wanted to call such an order the "Order of Ilyich."

Just think how happy the person who was awarded this order was. It was decided to issue him only for special military, labor and revolutionary services to the state. Since the advent and existence of such a state promotion, it has undergone many species changes.

Order of the Lenin Banner

So the order was sometimes called because it was approved and approved along with the Order of the Red Banner. For the first time, the citizen was awarded the Order of Lenin for special services to the state on May 23, 1930. Such information was posted in the newspaper Komsomolskaya Pravda. The next awarded, a year later, were servicemen who distinguished themselves after putting out a fire. In 1934, for the first time such an award was awarded to foreigners. In the same year, a new title of Hero of the Soviet Union appeared, so citizens who were awarded this title were also awarded the Order of Lenin.

Order of awarding military personnel

In this case, the military had its own special procedure for awarding the award. It has also been approved by government agencies. And there were significant differences from the usual, general order. This issue was especially acute during the war years. Namely, when the award was issued, and the person at the end of hostilities was obliged to prove that he was handed it to the commander. Although there was no Decree of the Presidium on her appointment. In the pre-war time, the situation was different - there were few awarded, so there were no special problems.

After a while, the right to reward lay on the shoulders of the commanders. Among the awards there was a hierarchy of its own, everything was listed in a special table. In addition to the Order of Lenin, servicemen were awarded "for the defense of Leningrad", "Partisan of the Great Patriotic War" and so on. Subsequently, the adopted legal acts were changed and supplemented, which is happening to this day.

Varieties of the Order

Types of the order was enough. Each was issued under its own identification number. So, at number 170, a silver medallion was issued , and on top of it was a gold rim. It depicted Lenin, as well as the inscription "USSR", a sickle and a hammer. And of course the word Goznak. In the thirties, a golden screw order with a bright red enamel banner was also issued. Such awards were in several versions. In the forties, up to the nineties, suspended models were in use. The medallion was fastened by the ear to a special ribbon. Despite this, such orders were also issued by different mints. In the manufacture of them there was one interesting detail - the individual parts were then fastened together.

Various alloys were also used and applied: platinum, silver, gold. And the weight naturally varied. And it was legislatively fixed how and how this order was to be made. Among the indicators: length, width, weight, material, height, diameter.
