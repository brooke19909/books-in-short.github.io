Stay in a cold environment

The time that a person can spend in water is an hour or a half. During this short period, death occurs due to shock, difficulty in breathing or cardiac arrest. It would seem that the physical capabilities of a person simply do not allow expanding this line. But there are other facts.

During the Second World War, a sergeant of Soviet troops sailed in cold water for 20 kilometers, thereby fulfilling his combat mission. It took the soldier 9 hours to cover such a distance! Does this not say that the world of human possibilities is much larger than we imagine ?!

This fact is proved by one British fisherman. Within 10 minutes after the shipwreck in cold water, all his comrades died due to hypothermia, but this man lasted about five hours. And after he got to the shore, he walked for another three hours barefoot. Indeed, with regard to the cold environment, human capabilities are much wider than is commonly believed. And what about other areas?

Hunger, or How long you can live without food

There is a general opinion of experts that a person without food will survive for about two weeks. However, doctors in some countries have witnessed astounding records that help to realize the fantastic potential of the human body.

For example, one woman went hungry for 119 days. During this period, she received a daily dose of vitamins to support the work of internal organs. But such a 119-day hunger strike is not yet the limit of human possibilities.

In Scotland, two women registered at the clinic and began to starve to lose weight. It's hard to believe, but one of them did not eat food for 236, and the second for 249 days. The second indicator has not yet been surpassed by anyone. The resources of our body are really very rich. But if a person can not eat for so long, the question arises of how much he can not drink.

Is water life?

They say that without water a person can stretch no more than 2-3 days. In fact, this indicator depends on the individual capabilities of a person, his physical activity and ambient temperature. Scientists argue that in optimal circumstances, you can survive as long as possible only for 9-10 days. Is it so? Is this the limit?

In the fifties, a man was found in the city of Frunze, who suffered a head injury and lay without assistance for 20 days in a cold and deserted place. When they found him, he did not move, and his pulse was barely palpable. However, the next day, the 53-year-old man was already free to speak.

And another case. In England, during the end of World War II, the ship sank. The steward of this ship, wrecked in the Atlantic Ocean, escaped on a boat and stayed on it for four and a half months!

The manifestation of strengths



Many people with disabilities are a good example to follow in how to pursue goals and not give up, despite great obstacles. Human development in such difficult conditions not only gives its results, but also tempers the character. So, among the disabled there are a huge number of excellent writers, poets, artists, musicians, athletes and so on. History knows many great people who have achieved success in various fields of activity, although they were sometimes considered inferior. Here is just one example. Pauline Gorenstein was a ballerina. After she contracted encephalitis, she was paralyzed. The woman lost her sight. Despite all the troubles that arose in connection with a serious illness, the woman began to engage in artistic modeling. As a result, her few works are still among the exhibits of the Tretyakov Gallery.
