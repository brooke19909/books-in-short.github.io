Village Museum Vyatskoe (Yaroslavl region): history, attractions and interesting places

The village of Vyatskoye (Yaroslavl region) is a tourist brand advertised today. Travel agencies positioned it as an open-air museum, a model village or a village that really wanted to become a city. Now every year a huge number of tourists come here to see the monuments of antiquity, the true face of the Russian hinterland and touch the ancient Orthodox shrines. Is it worth spending time on this trip and what sights in Vyatka can you see?

Village history



The first references in historical sources to the village of Vyatka date back to 1502. At that time, this settlement was considered the center of the metropolitan Vyatka volost. One version of the origin of the name of the village comes down to the derivative of the city of Vyatka, the road to which passed nearby.

In the 17th century, Vyatka was the patrimony of Patriarch Filaret. And a century later, the village is known as a large shopping center, which regularly hosts fairs. In the 19th century, the village of Vyatskoye in the Yaroslavl Region also gained fame as a major center for growing cucumbers and their subsequent harvesting. Local ambassador vegetables are sold at local fairs and transported to other regions, in particular to Arkhangelsk. Wealthy merchants and craftsmen / craftsmen of various specialties lived in the village, once the Vyatka was considered the center of the Old Believers, and then the center of revolutionary propaganda.

The subsequent history of the village is typical of the Russian province - the peasant militia against the Soviet regime in 1917-1922, the suppression of the uprising and the creation of the collective farm, as well as its liquidation in connection with bankruptcy.

Open-air museum



Today Vyatka really looks much more prosperous than the neighboring settlements. However, one should not think that the village was preserved in some mystical way or was specially preserved. In 2007, the reconstruction of Vyatsky began in order to turn it into a tourist center by the Yaroslavl Investment and Financial Company Limited Liability Company. Impressive work has really been done to date. The central streets delight with restored facades of ancient buildings and clean sidewalks. The village has ancient churches and chapels, holy springs, as well as many museums. Organized tours are organized here, but many tourists prefer to go to Vyatka on their own. Tickets to all museums can be bought at the entrance, there are signs and signs on the streets for independent sightseeing.

What to see in Vyatka?



In the center of the village you can see the complex, consisting of several beautifully restored mansions. This is the main hotel, restaurant, souvenir shop and some related organizations. According to tourists, the spirit of antiquity can be felt inside these magnificent buildings. However, the prices for accommodation and the restaurant menu are quite high and not at all provincial. It is not surprising, because Vyatka is very popular, including among guests from other countries. There are about 10 museums in the village today. Tourists who decide to visit the Vyatka (Yaroslavl region) are also offered sightseeing walking tours of the village. If you go for a walk around the village on your own, you can feel its real atmosphere. This is not only beautiful scenery for visitors, but also a settlement quite usual for its type, in which the most ordinary people live.

The village of Vyatskoye, Yaroslavl region: attractions

In 1750, the Church of the Resurrection of Christ was built. Today this church is operational, and its bell tower is the tallest building in the village. Once the village of Vyatskoye in the Yaroslavl region boasted two temples at once. Today, the Church of the Assumption of the Virgin, built in 1780, is in ruin. However, the local population does not lose hope for its restoration. The village of Vyatskoye (Yaroslavl region) is a large religious center, there are also holy sources and small chapels. Other attractions include the monument to Alexander II and the Walk of Fame, dedicated to soldiers who fell during the Second World War.

Museums and tourist attractions



The village of Vyatskoye (Yaroslavl region) is a real paradise for lovers of organized excursions. In addition to historical architectural sights and monuments, you can visit many different museums. There is an exposition in the village dedicated to Russian enterprise, and a collection of angels. The museums will allow you to plunge into the everyday life of the past: a trading peasant, "The Number of Merchants of the Urlov Brothers", "Russian Bathhouse in Black" and an exposition of Russian amusements. But the list of interesting places in the village does not end there. Here you can visit the Polytechnic Museum, the Museum of Kitchen Machinery, as well as the Museum of the Returned Shrine and the exposition "Children's World". Choose the most interesting excursions or come to the village for the whole weekend in order to have time to see everything. The village museum Vyatskoye (Yaroslavl region) is a unique place where you can not only relax from the bustle of the big city, but also get a lot of pleasant experiences.
