Park "Curonian Neriya"

This famous park is located in the northern part of the spit. It covers an area of â€‹â€‹26,500 ha, the most unique part of which are the dunes. For dozens of kilometers, wherever you look, there are sandy hills that environmentalists and enthusiasts are trying to keep from destruction.

The landscape of the spit is truly unique, it is not for nothing that it was chosen by millions of birds migrating to the north and back. Ornithologists count up to 20 million birds per season, among which rare species are found.

Watching them is another type of ecotourism. Hundreds of people climb dunes or specially equipped observation towers to monitor the behavior of birds.





The most popular is the Parnigio dune, which is rightly called the ornithological observatory. Migratory birds should be observed from March to May, but the most impressive thing is their return from August to November, when their grown chicks join them.

The boars living here are no less enthusiastic about travelers. The Curonian Spit (Lithuania-Russia) is not only their homeland, where they are not in danger, but also the opportunity to chat with people who have these semi-wild animals asking for food. Well, where else can you find a boar standing on the road in the hope that the car will stop and something tasty will fall over to it?

Another reason to come to these parts is the opportunity to observe the oldest colony of cormorants and herons that settled here during the Middle Ages. During this time, hundreds of generations of birds have been replaced, and they continue to live where they are protected.

You can learn about the local landscape and its changes over a thousand years, about the plants and animals that have been here and have disappeared, and about those that grow today, in the Curonian Neria Museum (Curonian Spit). The border with Lithuania divides the reserve into 2 parts, but does not make it less unique.

Historical Museum of Neringa

Once upon a time, the Curonian people inhabited the spit. They had their own legend about the appearance of these places. One lord had a daughter Neringa, who was a giant. Her mission was to help fishermen during a storm, for which she had to go into the sea and pull ships to the shore for anchor chains. The travelers who got lost in the forests, whom she led to the nearest villages, also knew her kindness.

Once people managed to anger the god of the winds, who sent such a force to a hurricane that Neringa could not go through the huge waves to the ships. Then she collected sand in her apron and began throwing it into the sea until there was land formed on which people sheltered. In honor of their salvation, they named the sandy shore of Nering, where the city of the same name later appeared.

The Neringa Historical Museum tells in great detail about the events from the first settlement of people here to the present day. More than three hundred years ago, the local population, not thinking about the consequences, began to cut down forests for the construction of ships, and when their houses began to be absorbed by the dunes one by one, a decision was made to landscaping the area, which continues to this day. For this, herbs and plants with a strongly interwoven root system were planted. The net from the roots stopped the sand, and later trees were again planted on the dune-free lands, which are now protected and cut down only when there is a danger of falling from old age.

To preserve the thin layer of the earth and the dunes themselves, the reserve has installed flooring, which is not recommended to be left. The weather on the Curonian Spit allows you to walk here at any time of the year, although in winter fishermen are more common than tourists.

Nida

This city, once a former Curonian settlement, is today the capital of this region. It is simply amazing how people were able to maintain the flavor, life and homes of the people who once lived here. For many tourists, the Curonian Spit (Lithuania), whose sights differ even from what can be seen on the mainland, is an island of a completely different life.

Every house is original here, and the local architectural style is not found anywhere else - neither in Lithuania, nor in Germany, nor in Finland, nor in Latvia, countries, natives of which became the future Curonian people with their own language and culture.





Each Nida building has its own carved decoration and platbands. The Curonians were not only fishermen, but also woodcarvers and raven catchers. You can find out about their lives in the small museum "Fisherman's Life". In Nida, even cemeteries are a attraction.

The chicken had its own custom of burying the dead. Instead of a cross in the head of the grave, they placed crickets at their feet - carved wooden posts with different shapes of tops. They were buried to the depths of the grave, since this people had a belief that during the Last Judgment the resurrected could get out of them, grabbing the pole.

Admirers of Thomas Mann can visit his house-museum, and amber lovers will find a whole gallery of this beautiful sun stone.

Maritime Museum

If the weather on the Curonian Spit has turned bad, then the day can be spent in an interesting Maritime Museum - an aquarium. It is located in the 19th century bastion fortress, built by the Germans in the Smiltine region. Among the presented expositions there are stands dedicated to marine life, the history of shipping and shipbuilding, and the place of gun sites was taken by anchors that were brought here from all over the country.

In redoubts are aquariums, in which 40 species of marine inhabitants feel great. In the place of the former fishing village, near the bastion, an ethnic village appeared, where houses, utensils, and household items of Pomeranian fishermen living here, even their ships and boats on which they went to the Baltic for fishing, were reproduced.

The inhabitants of the aquariums are representatives of Lithuanian rivers, lakes and the sea, as well as guests from the tropics - catfish, chub, grayling, sabrefish, eel and exotic starfish, a huge freshwater moray eel, sea urchins and a collection of corals.
