Phone with AMOLED display: model overview

Quite a few manufacturers of mobile gadgets are starting to give up quite good IPS-matrices in favor of AMOLED displays. Which phones they are installed in is pretty easy to find out. Just look at the screen on in total darkness. If there are at least some glimpses of light, then you have the usual LCD-backlight with IPS technology. Phones with an AMOLED display have a completely black screen and do not light up. This is one of the most remarkable features of this technology.

Samsung is considered to be a pioneer in this area. It was she who showed and told in detail what the AMOLED display on the phone is. Samsung and its engineers were able to design an active matrix using organic LEDs. The abbreviation AMOLED is precisely the decoding of this technology (Active Matrix Organic Light-Emitting Diode).

Difficulty choosing

Phones with an AMOLED screen have a number of undeniable advantages and there are practically no at least some significant disadvantages. That is why many manufacturers are moving their new models to them.

Advantages of AMOLED technology:

  • noticeably less energy consumption in comparison with conventional matrices;
  • more varied colors;
  • reduced response time;
  • maximum viewing angles;
  • high and at the same time comfortable for the eyes contrast;
  • the ability to work with ultra-thin cases.

Also on sale you can find phones with Super AMOLED-screen. The prefix "Super" was added by Samsung marketers to incite interest in new models. In principle, as such, there is no difference between a regular phone and a phone with a super AMOLED display.

The only thing that can be called more or less significant is the elimination of the air gap in the gadgets marked ‚ÄúSuper‚Äù. This gave an impetus to an even greater decrease in the thickness of the case, and that‚Äôs all, and the backbone of the technologies has remained as it is, and there are no other cardinal changes. So for those who do not want to overpay the extra money for a millimeter of thinness, it is not at all necessary to buy phones with a Super AMOLED display.

Some experts even track negative aspects in new screens and describe in detail flaws in thematic forums. The fact is that smartphones with a Super AMOLED display use a sub-pixel dot layout of the PenTile RGBG standard. This allows you to significantly increase the life of the screen, but at the same time, the picture on the output worsens.

A good half of manufacturers, including Samsung, preferred to remain silent about this feature in their brochures, but soon corrected the situation by releasing all the same phones with Super AMOLED, with the only difference being that the new gadgets support the familiar RGB RGB format. The latter is just used in conventional AMOLED devices without the Super prefix. So be sure to pay attention to the variety of the standard RGB before buying a smartphone.
