The capital of South Korea: come, you will not regret it!

What is Seoul? What is the capital of South Korea? This is a riot of exotic colors, centuries-old history, reckless nightlife, growing every second business.

Seoul is a tradition and exotic, mixed so closely that no one can understand what came to the city from ancient times, and what originated recently.

Tourists in Seoul have something to admire. History buffs who have traveled to the country to appreciate the sights of South Korea should begin their journey around the city with a tour of its historic center.

Here you can see the incredible beauty of palaces and temples built in the fifteenth century. Particularly striking in their luxury, brightness and wealth are Changengun and Chandokkun. The area around these buildings is a number of eastern parks with an abundance of exotic flowers, tropical greenery.



Nearby is the residence of the rulers of Gyeongbokgung, the remains of the fortress wall that surrounded the city in time immemorial. It is decorated with mystical animals: if the wall did not allow enemies into the city, then animals and birds protected it from evil spirits.

After the historical center, you can set off to explore the sights of Seoul located in other areas.

In the center of the metropolis is Mount Nasman, where among the luxurious parks are museums, cultural centers, shops and other places pleasant for tourists.

And in the very center of the mountain, right under the open sky, an old village was sheltered. In this museum, the life of Koreans is recreated to the last detail.

The capital of South Korea can be proud of a non-hot (unlike other tropical cities) climate. Therefore, you can spend the whole day on the mountain: in addition to the above, there is a huge aquarium, architectural fountains and a very rich exhibit of the botanical garden.

Want to see local life unadorned for tourists? The capital of South Korea invites you to visit the Kanbuk area. The most ordinary people live here, but a very unusual market is located here. It is on it that most of the goods are sold (from minor souvenirs to washing machines and other equipment), which then fall into Europe.

Are you tired? Hungry? The capital of South Korea can feed tourists with national food, Japanese dishes, please European or American cuisine. To taste all this, you have to move to the Hongdae area. Here you can meet young people from all over the world. Hongdae is a student district and very laid-back.
