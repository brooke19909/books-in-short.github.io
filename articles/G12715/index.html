How to crochet a half-column (connecting loop)

A half- column , a connecting column, a half- column without a crochet, a hook, a blind loop, an attach, a connecting loop, a blind loop - these are all different names for the same element of crocheting. It is a tight, dense, low loop intended for closing circles, connecting rows or transitions to knitting other loops, without breaking the thread.

This knitting element is used to close the rows in direct or circular knitting, to carry out the necessary reductions at the edges of the product, to stitch together work items, and also to connect motifs. With a half-column, fringes of details, collars, necks, armholes, sleeves, sides of square or rectangular motifs are conveniently and efficiently designed. With the help of a connecting column they perfectly seal the edges, eliminate stretching and other possible defects. Being able to properly knit a connecting loop is an important skill for every needlewoman. A blind loop allows for a very neat and durable connection. In this article we will tell you how to crochet a half-column. This is nothing complicated, and you will surely succeed!
