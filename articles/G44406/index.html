How does a microwave work and how was it invented?

The last century is a century of discoveries and innovations. Many devices were invented that are now firmly in our everyday life. And one of these inventions is a microwave. A completely new approach to cooking delicious and healthy foods. But how does it work? Oddly enough, the stoves owe their appearance to the military industry. It was in radars for the first time that they began to use ultra-high frequencies to detect targets. In the early 30s, in one US laboratory, employees accidentally prepared the first dish using a microwave generator. This dish turned out to be sausages. In fact, the invention of the microwave is the work of thousands of scientists. Today, all furnaces operate at a dedicated frequency - 2450 Megahertz. According to international agreements, stoves of all manufacturers must work precisely at this frequency, so as not to interfere with radar stations. The basis of any stove is a magnetron, its supply voltage is about 4 thousand volts. The mains voltage is small, so an increase is required, which occurs due to the use of special transformers. A magnetron with a power of 850 watts necessarily has a radiator and a fan that turns on during operation and blows the device.
