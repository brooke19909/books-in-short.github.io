How to keep IP accounting independently: step-by-step instructions. Bookkeeping IP for beginners

Even at the registration stage, an entrepreneur should decide on a tax system for his future enterprise. After this, another important issue should be resolved: who should be entrusted with bookkeeping. IP has several options for solving it. Let's consider some of them.



Bookkeeping

IP has several possibilities:

  1. Conclude an agreement with a specialized company for reporting support. This method is considered the most costly financially. However, this way out of the situation completely eliminates the need to delve into the reporting by the owner of the enterprise. Experienced specialists of competent companies accompany the reporting from beginning to end, represent the interests of business in various instances.
  2. Hiring an incoming accountant. This option is not as expensive as the previous one. However, in this case there is a certain risk. The choice of an experienced specialist will lie solely with the entrepreneur. Today it is quite difficult to find a person who can work at home and perfectly cope with the tasks set. It is not so easy to determine at first glance the amount of knowledge and competence of a specialist. In such cases, it is advisable to use the recommendations of friends or business partners.
  3. Accounting independently. Many entrepreneurs do this, whose enterprises use special taxation regimes. Accounting IP on the simplified tax system and other systems has a fairly simple scheme. Unprofessional in this matter may well cope with it. Moreover, there are a variety of automated services that detail the accounting for IP (eg, online accounting 1C: BusinessStart specially for entrepreneurs without an accountant)

The latter option has a number of undoubted advantages for entrepreneurs.

Basic concepts

Before explaining how to keep an IP accounting independently, a step-by-step instruction includes an explanation of the key elements of the system. Management accounting is an analysis of the information given in the financial documentation. Employees

If there is staff, the entrepreneur must keep personnel records of employees. He acts as their tax agent. The entrepreneur calculates and withholds income tax from employees, pays insurance premiums to the Social Insurance Fund and the FIU. For personnel, the IP submits reports:

  1. In IFTS on the average number of employees and their income (f. 2-PIT). The first document is submitted until January 20, the second - until April 1.
  2. In the FSS. The annual and quarterly statements for f. FSS-4 until the 15th day of the month that begins after the end of the reporting period.
  3. In the MHIF and the FIU. Form RSV-1 is submitted to these bodies by the 15th day of the second month from the end date of the year and each quarter.

If an entrepreneur works alone and does not act as an employer, he needs to pay fixed medical and pension contributions "for himself."

Important point

When using UTII, an entrepreneur must submit reports every quarter before the 20th day of the month from which a new reporting period begins. Tax is payable by the 25th.
