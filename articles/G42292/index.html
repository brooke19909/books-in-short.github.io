Contraindications to sanatorium treatment

Sanatorium "Voskhod" (Feodosia) does not accept patients with acute stages of chronic respiratory diseases and complicated acute processes. With malignant neoplasms, often recurring or heavy bleeding, with all forms of tuberculosis (active stage). With all acute infectious diseases until the end of the isolation period. With diabetes mellitus (decompensated and severe), thromboembolic disease, cirrhosis, myocardial infarction and all forms of jaundice. As well as pregnant women for more than 26 weeks.

Guest reviews

During the summer season, thousands of people come for healing and treatment to the sanatorium "Sunrise" (Feodosia). Customer reviews highlight the pros and cons of this health resort. The advantages of the sanatorium include affordable prices, polite staff, first-class medical care, beautiful resort architecture, and sights of Feodosia. The disadvantages include the modest atmosphere in the rooms (however, the administration annually improves the material base), the lack of a heated pool (which would increase the season by two to three months). One of the main disadvantages is that the resort does not have its own private beach. As a result, at the height of the season, thousands of people gather here, hence the unsanitary conditions. In addition, the mass of merchants who go between vacationers and offer sweets, pickles, drinks, ice cream and so on spoils the whole impression. Given that most visitors to the health resort are on a diet, this "provocative" behavior of traders can nullify all the efforts of medical personnel.



History of the Feodosia resort

The history of this city clearly distinguishes four main stages. The first of these is the birth of the resort, which began in the first half of the nineteenth century and continued until the Great October Revolution of 1917. The second stage captures the period of the formation of the young Soviet state - 1917-1941. The third stage of development of the Feodosia resort begins after the end of the Second World War of 1945 and continues until 1990. The fourth period falls on the time of the emergence of independent Ukraine and the destruction of the USSR. Probably, after some time, the fifth stage will also stand out - the reunification of Crimea with the Russian Federation, but today it is too early to talk about it. Although the changes are visible today, the Russian government and the new administration of the peninsula pay great attention to the development of Crimea, the restoration of its status as a world resort. Today, large sums are invested in restoring the destroyed infrastructure and creating new facilities. Perhaps next year Crimea will change so much that it can compete with most resorts in the Mediterranean.
