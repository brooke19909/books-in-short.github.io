general characteristics





The climate of Madagascar is characterized by pronounced monsoon and tropical features. As such, there are no prolonged rainy seasons on the island. The coldest areas are located in the mountains. Frosts occur at the tops of ridges. The weather in these parts is strikingly different from that prevailing on the African continent. It is formed by anticyclones arriving from India, and air masses coming from Southeast Asia.

Seasons





The climate of Madagascar has four seasons. The average daily winter temperature is 24 ¬∞ C. In this case, the ocean warms up to 30 ¬∞ C. Hot days are accompanied by heavy rains. Showers are in the eastern part of the island. The center has a high level of humidity. And only in the south the bright sun almost always shines in January.

In spring, the ocean cools down. He begins to worry, and the volume of plankton increases markedly. Easy prey attracts flocks of predatory fish to the coastal zone. From March to mid-May, rains continue to rain in the east and in the center of the island. In these parts of Madagascar, the climate differs from the weather at the resorts with an abundance of foggy days.

Summer nights in the island's capital are cool. The air cools to 10 ¬∞ C. Rains give way to strong winds that attract hundreds of windsurfers on the coast. In August, lagoons remote from tourist recreations become the venue for whale mating games.

In autumn, rains begin again, gray clouds cover the sky. The relatively dry period lasts until the end of October. The climate of Madagascar during the off-season is characterized by an abundance of rainfall.

Factors





The variety of temperature conditions is explained by the geographical position of the island. It lies between 12 and 25 ¬∞ south latitude. At this point, the transition from dry subtropics to moist equatorial conditions is most pronounced. Plus, the mountain ranges that divide the island are perpendicular to the vector of weather-forming cyclones. Open areas are not protected from the effects of moist monsoon currents that come from the Indian Ocean.

The description of the climate in Madagascar confirms that the part of the island closed to the winds does not receive such a volume of precipitation. Winds blowing from the African continent are characterized by a minimum concentration of moisture. The mosaic nature of the temperature zones is due to the length of the island. Even the weather conditions of the open coastal areas, which occupy the opposite ends of Madagascar, have a lot of differences. The difference in air temperature can reach 5 ¬∞ C.

The higher the mountains rise, the colder it becomes on their slopes. When the thermometer in Tuamasin shows 24 ¬∞ C, in Antananarivo it is held at 17 ¬∞ C. Arid

Territories with an arid type of climate include the regions of Murumbe, Sainte Marie and the vicinity of Tulear. The main destructive factor that causes great damage to the flora of the coast, are tropical cyclones.
