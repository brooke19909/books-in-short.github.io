Rock origin

A very interesting question - where did this unusual beauty come from and how? It was obtained as a result of weathering and erosion of Paleogene and Cretaceous sandstones and limestones. This process is an example of a well-known kuest relief. The weathering of the mountain in the upper part created oval niches, grottoes, and pillars.

Below, weathering products are constantly accumulating - erosion hollows, bulk blocks, scree. Some plants inhibit erosion in some places. This is a thicket of meadowsweet and rose hips. All this contributes to the beauty of the monument White Rock (Crimea). The photos posted here convey only part of this magnificence.

The history of this cultural monument, excavations

In the sixties and seventies of the last century, at the foot of the rock, on its northern side, the Crimean Paleontology Expedition excavated twenty sites of a man of the primitive Mousterian era. Found a lot of silicon spikes, scrapers, knives. Near them were found a sufficient number of various bony remains of the disappeared fauna of the peninsula: onager, wild horse, primeval bull, saiga, red deer, giant cave bear, mammoth and others. During excavations, a fragment of the skull of a Neanderthal, an adult, and later the remains of a child of the same time were first found here.

The reason is the favorable territory of the White Rock (Crimea) object for housing: river water, many sheds and grottoes, deposits of the necessary flint. Even a high precipice brought benefits - it was ideally suited for hunting many animals. Already in our era, in the first centuries, Sarmatians lived here in a cave. According to some assumptions, it then served as a sanctuary, a kind of temple. Tamgas were found - signs belonging to the clans of that time, which were carved in stone and this confirmed the ownership of these territories.

Scythian mounds, caves and place of execution

Many mounds are located on a plateau. At the foot of the cliff in the Middle Ages lived Shirin, the leader of one of the rich Tatar clans. Access to the upper cave is very difficult, since the entrance to it is located at a distance of 52 meters from the sole in the form of a circle-shaped hole and 49 meters from the edge of the cliff. There are three legends dedicated to Altyn-Teshik:

  1. This cave is the den of a serpent who was a werewolf. He abducted a handsome man in the area and brought them here.
  2. This cave stretches very far - to Feodosia.
  3. Here the robbers hid a chest of gold.

On the rock, an older clan was elected; here Murza, dissatisfied with the khan of Crimea, gathered. In the Middle Ages, Ak-Kaya was a famous place of execution. In the eyes of Bogdan Khmelnitsky, who sometimes visited here, they threw prisoners off a cliff in order to make the hetman pay the ransom on time. The headquarters of Suvorov was here in 1777. In 1783, the Crimean aristocracy here took the oath of loyalty to Russia.
