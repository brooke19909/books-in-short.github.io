Philips W536: repair

Like any other smartphone, a malfunction may occur for various reasons. Sometimes it becomes necessary to reset to factory settings. How to do it on Philips W536? The following steps need to be done.

  1. Turn off the phone.
  2. Press the volume keys and turn on the phone at the same time.
  3. Hold them until the recovery menu appears.
  4. When the menu appears on the screen, you need to release the keys.
  5. You need to navigate the menu by pressing the volume keys, and you need to confirm the selection with the power key.
  6. Moving through the menu, you must select the data reset and factory settings.
  7. If you are asked to delete all information, you need to confirm that you want to delete everything.
  8. Next, select the system reboot item. The phone will reboot, and you will receive it in the state in which it was purchased at the store.

Remember ! You will lose all the data that has been saved on your phone! Save important information before deleting everything. This can be done by copying everything you need to a memory card, to a computer or to the cloud.





Disassembling the phone

Sometimes a question arises from a Philips W536 user how to disassemble a phone. In order to do this, you need to have special screwdrivers for working with mobile devices and a small shovel that a bank card can replace. We remove the back cover and get the battery, memory card and SIM card. On the phone case you will see five bolts that must be carefully unscrewed. Why neat? Because a mobile phone is a very delicate device, and it is necessary to handle it with repair very carefully. After you unscrew the bolts, you need to slip a card or a shovel into the slot formed at the bottom of the phone. Moving the card gently around the entire perimeter of the phone, you need to raise the central part of the phone. As you lift you will hear faint clicks. Do everything very slowly, because if you are careless, there is a danger of breaking the loop of volume control or some other wiring at the top of the phone. After you picked up the free part of the phone, the motherboard of the phone will open for you. It is fastened to five more bolts: three bolts at the bottom and two at the top. We unscrew all the bolts. After that, carefully remove the holders with the fingernail from the loops that go to the screen and other sensors, and raise the motherboard. That's all. The phone is completely disassembled.
