Construction history

The construction of the first Moscow City building was started in 1996. It became the ‚ÄúTower 2000‚Äù, which was commissioned in 2001. Location

The Empire Tower (Moscow-City) is located in convenient transport access from all areas of Moscow, on Presnenskaya Embankment. There is direct access to it both from the Moskva River and from the pedestrian zone of the MIBC. Due to this circumstance, the Imperia skyscraper has an advantage over other objects in the Moscow-City district. The Empire Tower is a five-minute walk from the Bagration pedestrian bridge and from the Vystavochnaya metro station. In addition, from there you can easily get to the Afimol shopping center and further to other objects of the Moscow-City district without having to go outside, which is important, especially in the cold season.

Description

The Moscow City Empire Tower stands out architecturally from neighboring structures, although its dimensions are not as impressive as, for example, the height of the Mercury City Tower or Eurasia. It is made in hi-tech style and is an ellipse inscribed in a rectangle with mirror glazing. The skyscraper is slightly tilted north to provide better daylight inside the building. Most of the common areas are finished with expensive materials such as precious wood, metal, marble and other natural stones.

Excursion. Empire Tower, Moscow City

On the 58th floor of the building there is an observation deck with a magnificent view from a height of 239 meters above the ground. This premises is supposed to be rented out or sold for apartments in the future. There, high-speed elevators moving at a speed of 7 m / s can be reached during a tour lasting 50 minutes. On weekdays, such guided walks are organized at 18:00, 19:00, 20:00, 21:00, and at 22:00 you can visit the observation deck without an accompanying person. As for the weekend, excursion groups are allowed into the building every hour from 11:00 to 23:00.

The cost of visiting the Empire Tower for children is 400 rubles. As for adults, on weekdays and weekends after 14:00 they can visit a skyscraper for 700 rubles, and on weekends in the morning - for 500 rubles. Since there are always many who want to visit the 58th floor of the building, it is recommended to book an excursion in advance. From the observation deck of the Empire Tower, a magnificent view of such objects as the main building of Moscow State University, the Ostankino Tower, the building of the Academy of Sciences, the Cathedral of Christ the Savior, the high-rise on Kudrinskaya, the government White House and many other ancient and modern sights of the capital opens up.
