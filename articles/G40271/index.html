What is an enterprise balance sheet?

Document structure

In the balance there is an asset and a liability. These are two equal parts. In one of them, the composition of the funds is shown, in the second - the sources of their formation. Sources of information

As these are accounting accounts. As a rule, their name coincides with the name of the accounting object. There are two parts to any account: debit and credit. This division is due to the movement of funds - a decrease or increase. Each account has an initial balance - a balance for a specific object, and subsequently its decrease or increase. Thus, you can set a final balance at any time. To do this, an increase is added to the original balance, and the amount of reduction is subtracted from the resulting balance.



Account classification

Elements on which household funds are taken into account are divided into 3 groups. The first includes active accounts. They reflect the types of households. funds. Explaining what are the assets of the balance sheet, it should be said that the balance of such accounts can only be debit. This is due to the fact that the decrease in funds is limited by their size. The second group is passive accounts. They keep records of the sources of formation of households. funds. Their balance can be only credit. The third group is active-passive accounts. They keep records of both types and sources of funds. This group includes accounts for settlements with legal entities. The latter may act as debtors or creditors. In the first case, the accounts will have an active structure.

Subaccounts

These are intermediate links between analytical and synthetic accounts. With the help of subaccounts, a grouping of relevant indicators is carried out. In particular, the data of analytical accounts are used to obtain additional information about the objects registered.

Classification of balances

Separation is carried out according to the following criteria:

  • Compilation time.
  • To sources.
  • The amount of information.
  • The nature of the activity.
  • Form of ownership.
  • Object of reflection.
  • The cleaning method.

In accordance with the time of compilation allocate balances:

  • Current.
  • Introductory.
  • Liquidation.
  • Unifying.
  • Separating.

According to the sources, the balances are divided:

  • General.
  • Inventory.
  • Book.

Depending on the amount of information, the balance can be consolidated or single. According to the nature of the activity, documentation on the main and non-main types is distinguished. In accordance with the form of ownership, there are balances of cooperative, state, private, joint, public and mixed organizations. Their difference lies in the source of the formation of funds. Depending on the objects of reflection, separate and independent balances are distinguished. According to the cleaning method, gross and net documentation is distinguished. Also in accounting, an alternative balance is used. This form is used to obtain evidence of the completeness and reality of the reflection of manufactured products, services rendered or work performed. That is, what is an alternative balance? This is the way by which used materials are shown for accounting for the production of a unit of goods and the actual output of products.

Categories by compilation time

When combined, the corresponding documentation is compiled.
