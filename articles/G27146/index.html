Where to spend a vacation: Tunisia or Turkey?

There are countries that welcome Russian tourists with hospitality. Those who prefer a beach holiday go there . Tunisia, Turkey and Egypt are washed by the waters of the warm gentle seas, and the sun almost always shines there. Unfortunately, the Egyptian authorities have recently been unable to agree with the people, and many refrain from tours to this state.

For a relaxing holiday with your family or a fun trip as a friendly company, one of the two countries can be perfect: Tunisia or Turkey.

Both countries offer beach vacations on the shores of warm seas, tours cost about the same, but the coincidence ends there. Countries are fundamentally different, so it is important to choose a place for you.



Which is better: Turkey or Tunisia?

Turkey has long become a favorite and familiar place of rest for Russians. They go over the warm sea, good service, entertainment and excursions. The flight takes little time, a visa to the country is not needed, and buses with Russian-speaking guides are waiting at the airport.

You can also go to Tunisia without a visa. Communication with local people is mainly in English or French. The level of service is somewhat lower, but the food here is excellent. Tunisian cuisine offers tourists a wide range of delicious and delicious dishes.

Consider the pros and cons of both countries and choose which is better - Tunisia or Turkey.

What is worth visiting Tunisia for?

The country is so original and exotic that it would be a pity to spend all time on the beach. Although many people come here precisely for the sake of pure white sand and a gentle smooth entrance to the sea. The infrastructure is more developed in noisy Sousse, the coastline near Hammamet is built up with boarding houses and health centers where you can take a course of thalassotherapy.

In the south is the resort of Madhya, its beaches are a real treasure. Soft velvet sand, wide sea space and remoteness from noisy centers make this place ideal for a relaxing pastime.





In Tunisia there is an opportunity to go on an amazing excursion to the Sahara, visit ancient Roman settlements, see the unique fortifications and the majestic ruins of Carthage.

The country has long been a French colony, and Arabian hospitality is intricately intertwined with Parisian chic. People are more restrained and friendly than in Turkey, and our tourists are comfortable in their company. Despite the fact that the state is Muslim, here you can relax, have a drink, and sunbathe naked.

Cons Recreation in Tunisia

Service is poorly developed. Salaries of staff are low, and hotel workers are not shy about asking for a tip. It is better not to choose three-star hotels for rest, acceptable conditions start from 4 stars.

Excursions, although interesting, are the same in every city. Therefore, Tunisia is often called the "country of one trip."

Men love to get to know each other and can be very intrusive. Although they behave less vulgar and licentious than the Turks.

July is a very hot month, in August a large number of jellyfish accumulate off the coast, and in October a wind from the desert begins to blow, carrying thousands of grains of sand with it.





Why go to Turkey?

The country is very well located, its nature is delightful and diverse, and the coast is washed by the waters of four seas.

In Turkey, you can relax for a penny or, conversely, spend a lot of money in the most respectable hotels. The all-inclusive system is universally developed, and you can spend your vacation without leaving the hotel.

Excursions are the most diverse: ancient cities, chic waterfalls, hot springs, magnificent palaces and the famous Cleopatra bath.

There are quiet respectable villas, and vibrant night discos, and places for families. The infrastructure is well developed, the service is up to standard, the animators work from morning to night. Cons of relaxation in Turkey

Hotels of low price category may be unsuitable for recreation. The food offered is not always tasty and does not differ in particular variety. There are a lot of Russians here, in some cases this is a minus.

A separate topic is the relationship between Turkish men and our tourists. Of course, not all Turks are the same, but very intrusive types are often found.

There are few sandy beaches, mostly they are rocky or pebble. Vacationers with young children will have to be content with pools.

In August there is terrible heat, and in October it is already cold.
