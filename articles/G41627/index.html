Stuttgart (Germany) - a city of unique architectural structures and the automotive capital

It is located in the valley of the picturesque Neckar River, it is a modern industrial and cultural center of the country. The city has a huge number of museums, architectural monuments and many other attractions, Schwab and Schiller lived here , the great Hegel was born . Stuttgart on a map of Germany is easy to find, it is located east of Bavaria, near the border with Switzerland and France.

Architectural Attractions

Stuttgart (Germany) is rich in various architectural structures that have been preserved in their original form since the Middle Ages.

One of the first attractions that you meet upon arrival in the city is the Main Train Station. Its building was built at the beginning of the last century and is a huge dome with a red brick tower, above which the Mercedes star rises - the symbol of the city of Stuttgart (Germany). Photos taken here, among ancient houses and streets, transfer to the world of kings and princes of past eras. Schlossplatz, or Palace Square, will surprise with buildings built in the era of classicism, the New Palace, which was built in the last century. The city of Stuttgart (Germany) has the largest theater in the world, and the modern planetarium, which represents a unique modern architectural structure, receives more than two thousand visitors annually, in addition, various space painting exhibitions, courses and seminars on astronomy are held here.


Car museums

Stuttgart is the automobile capital of Germany, in this place is the Mercedes-Benz Museum, which is visited by about half a million tourists from around the world. The museum features one hundred unusual exhibits of this outstanding car. But high-speed cars can be seen in Stuttgart-Zuffenhausen, where the collections of the famous brand "Porsche" are collected. The exhibition allows you to see all the features and details of the legendary German car.

Swabian sea

One of the main attractions that the land of Baden-W√ºrttemberg has, the capital of which is Stuttgart, is Lake Constance or, as it is also called, the Swabian Sea. This is the second largest lake in Europe, and in its beauty and uniqueness has no equal, it can safely be called a real German paradise. Along the picturesque shores of Bodensee are medieval castles and palaces, green gardens and fabulous fields, and in the distance you can see the snow-capped peaks of the Alpine mountains.

Rest in the city

Stuttgart (Germany) is a large cultural and tourist center with many modern and comfortable hotels and high quality services.

Visiting interesting excursions will lead you into the world of ancient legends and myths; it is convenient to get to the nearby castles from the city, which once served as residences for kings. Not far from the Lower Park there is a wonderful botanical garden, in which there are pools with healing mineral water. Restaurants and bars will allow you to spend a romantic evening, taste national cuisine and enjoy the taste of German beer.
