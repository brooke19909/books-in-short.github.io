Types, description and patterns of ribbon lace. Crocheted knitted ornaments and patterns.

Many knitters, gaining experience and trying their hand at creating simple products, over time show a desire to take on a more difficult task. In the world of needlework, the most difficult to perform are paintings made up of separately connected fragments. All the skills and abilities of the craftswoman are needed here. Indeed, such knitting and crocheting (with or without a pattern) is significantly different from the traditional manufacture of a flat cloth.

Types of typesetting cloths

Products connected from many ornaments for various motives look very attractive and spectacular. It is not surprising that there are quite a few techniques that make and knit knitted elements. Among the most common are:

  • Irish lace (motifs on the grid).
  • Comparison of geometrically correct motives of the same shape.
  • Romanian lace lace (the cords are crocheted, and the joints and lace are made using a needle).
  • Canvas made of lace ribbons.




For the manufacture of products, one of these methods can be used, their combination or a combination of these techniques with a continuous canvas.

The secrets of knitting ribbon lace

You should know that it is undesirable to try to connect openwork ribbons from fragments during knitting. Although many magazines offer just such a way of matching elements, it almost never pays off. Often, a connection error is detected when a large number of semicircular motifs are already connected, and they all have to be dissolved.

In addition, it is much more convenient to lay out the finished stripes on the pattern and plan their location than to get a canvas of tapes that is not suitable in size or configuration. An important feature that the patterns of ribbon lace crocheted are their asymmetry. It is better to combine elements into the whole canvas in pairs. Each pair should include tapes that mirror each other. If all fragments are directed in one direction, it will not be possible to avoid a skew of the canvas.
