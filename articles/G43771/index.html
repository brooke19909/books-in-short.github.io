The narrowest street in Prague - Vinarna Chertovka

Prague attracts tourists with an abundance of museums and historical monuments. If you look at the interactive map of the city, you will notice that the sights here are located literally at every step. The narrowest street in Prague deserves attention. Where is it located and what is its width?

Description of an unusual attraction



In Prague, the narrowest street is also the least populated. The thing is that its width is only 70 cm, and two people here simply can not separate. The name of the street is Vinarna Chertovka, she got it from a nearby restaurant with a wonderful liquor store. The institution was named in honor of the nearby Chertovka River. Here is the winery of the same name.

The narrowest street in Prague stretched about 10 meters. In fact, these are just a few steps between neighboring buildings. If we turn to history, Vinarna Certovka received its “status” relatively recently. Initially, the narrowest Prague street was a fire passage between two detached houses. Its main task was to ensure the rapid evacuation of people in case of fire and to prevent the spread of fire too quickly.

It is worth noting that once in Prague there were many such "streets". Only a few have survived to our days, and, undoubtedly, the Vinar Devil is the most famous among them. It is worth noting that officially this "street" and today has the status of a fire passage. All identification marks and advertising in travel brochures are designed solely to attract the attention of guests from other countries and cities to this attraction.

Why do you need a traffic light on the narrowest Prague street?



Scary stories are often told to tourists about how someone got stuck while visiting the narrowest street in the city. In some urban legends, a full lady suffered, while in others it was a obese man. You can hear a story about how two people met in the middle of the road.

Of course, before you start the descent along the old steps, it makes sense to evaluate your own dimensions. Remember that the width of the passage is only 70 centimeters. But meeting with someone before Vinarna Certovka is left behind, you are unlikely to succeed. The thing is that now it is the narrowest street in Prague with a traffic light. Representatives of the fire department ordered him to deliver. The traffic light looks like an ordinary pedestrian. If the green light is on, you can safely move. And when the red lights up - you need to wait until the street is vacated. Traffic lights are manually controlled by a special button. Remember to click on it when you start moving.

How to get to Vinarna Certovka street?



Where is the narrowest street in Prague? The most striking landmark is the famous Charles Bridge. From it to Vinarna Certovka is only about 100 meters. Just walk towards Hradchan, then turn left. The street you are interested in starts from Luzickeho seminare street. If you plan to have lunch or want a glass of something tasty with national snacks, the narrowest street in Prague will lead you to an inexpensive and very good restaurant of the same name. The institution is open daily from 11.00 to 24.00. Restaurant Vinarna Certovka is famous for its national cuisine, as well as traditional wines and beer. Some travel agencies offer organized tours of Prague with a visit to the narrowest street. This is a great option for those who like guided walks and are poorly oriented in the city.

Reviews on Vinarna Certovka Attractions



Vinar Chertovka is described by tourists in completely different ways. This attraction makes an indelible impression on someone as an atmospheric and unusual place. Some tourists also note that the most common fire passage is overly advertised by guidebooks. In any case, it makes sense to visit him when walking around Prague. This Prague street is a great place to create vivid photos that will become a real decoration of your holiday album. Vinar Chertovka will not like only those who suffer from claustrophobia and are afraid of enclosed spaces. Fans of all unusual and original architectural objects will undoubtedly like this street. You can visit this attraction at any time of the day. In the evenings, Vinarna Chertovka is brightly lit by lamps.
