Multi cooker-multi-kitchen Redmond RMC-FM230: reviews, overview

Redmond RMC-FM230 is the latest multicooker that opens up new possibilities for you. The innovation in the form of a rising heater allows you to use the appliance as a burner.





Redmond RMC-FM230: Technical Overview

It would seem that multicookers can no longer surprise you. However, the Redmond RMC-FM230 proves the opposite. Now it is not just a multicooker, but also a mini-kitchen stove. If we talk about the technical characteristics of the device, then they look as follows:

  • the crock-pot has a power of 860 watts with a permissible voltage of 240 V;
  • the device is equipped with class 1 protection against electric shock;
  • metal 5-liter bowl with non-stick ceramic coating; 3D heating function is provided;
  • a removable inner cover as well as a steam valve facilitate the cleaning process;
  • 23 automatic cooking programs;
  • optimal food temperature can be stored up to 24 hours;
  • management is carried out by means of the LCD display and membrane buttons;
  • lifting heating element;
  • metal and plastic case.




Equipment

In the factory packaging, in addition to the Redmond RMC-FM230 multicooker itself, you will also find:

  • 5 liter bowl; special container for cooking steamed dishes;
  • a small frying pan with non-stick coating;
  • measuring cup for bulk and liquid ingredients;
  • scoop, spoon and tongs, as well as a special holder;
  • electric cord;
  • instructions;
  • brochure, which collected 120 of the best recipes for this model.

Appearance and design features

The design of the Redmond multicooker RMC-FM230 is standard, as for devices of this manufacturer. The lower part of the elongated body is made of metal, and the upper panel and the cover are made of glossy black plastic. For ease of transportation, a handle is provided.

On the back of the lid there is a removable valve that provides condensate collection. The heating element can be easily lifted using special forceps or manually. It is also worth noting that, in contrast to previous models, the power button is removed separately, which makes the operation of the multicooker even more convenient.





Device management

The Multicooker Redmond RMC-FM230 is controlled by a white backlit liquid crystal display, as well as 8 membrane buttons. Pressing is accompanied not only by a click, but also by a clear sound signal. The screen has a digital clock, indicators of the selected mode, as well as the functions provided in this device.

It is worth noting that the buttons "+" and "-" are located differently than on other similar devices. As a result, confusion may arise in the initial operation of the device. Nevertheless, this drawback cannot be considered significant, because users quickly get used to this feature. By the way, by simultaneously pressing these buttons, the device lock function is activated.

One of the innovations can be considered detailing of cooking programs. This means that before setting the cooking time, you must specify the type of product that will undergo heat treatment. An individual temperature regime will allow preserving the taste and nutrients.

A nice innovation is non-volatile memory. So, if you suddenly turned off the light, the crock-pot will continue to work according to a pre-set program for another quarter of an hour.

Of great interest to experimenters is the "Master Chef" function, which allows you to change settings directly during cooking. It is also worth noting the ability to memorize the performed manipulations in order to use them later as a template program. So you can create truly perfect dishes.





Multicooker Care

For trouble-free and quality operation, the Redmond RMC-FM230 device needs careful maintenance. The multicooker multicooker has a glossy black body, on which all dirt is clearly visible. That is why the device is recommended after each session to wipe with wet and dry wipes.

The bowl can be washed both manually and in the dishwasher. In order not to damage the non-stick coating, it is worth using only a soft sponge, as well as gentle detergents. If the pollution is strong, then you need to pour warm water into the container and leave to soak.

Particular attention should be paid to cleaning the internal removable cover. First you need to press on the plastic clips, and then disconnect the aluminum disk from the main surface (do not make significant efforts so as not to damage the device). Wash the inner lid under running water with a sponge and a special tool, and wipe the plastic surface with a damp cloth and wipe it dry. A removable steam valve also needs regular flushing.

It is worth noting that with careful operation and following all the requirements of the instructions, pollution of the working chamber is almost impossible. However, if this happens, immediately unplug the appliance from the mains. The sponge with which you will wipe the surface should be soft and slightly damp. The use of detergents is not welcome.





Multicooker Redmond RMC-FM230: reviews, advantages

A slow cooker is one of the most popular kitchen appliances at the moment. Already many managed to try out such a novelty as the Redmond RMC-FM230. The reviews for this device contain the following positive comments:

  • very detailed instructions, thanks to which even a child can cope with device control;
  • the possibility of using the device as a burner (thanks to a rising heating element);
  • the kit includes a small frying pan with non-stick coating;
  • a huge number of automatic modes that allow you to quickly cook the most popular dishes;
  • the "Master Chef" function allows you to record individual cooking programs, which eliminates the need to change settings during operation;
  • stylish design will make a multicooker an adornment of your kitchen;
  • due to the fact that the buttons are membrane, dirt does not clog under them;
  • a removable inner cover greatly simplifies the process of cleaning the device from contamination.




Multi-cooker-multi-kitchen Redmond RMC-FM230: reviews, disadvantages

It is worth noting that there are a lot of shortcomings in this multicooker. From customer reviews, you can find out the following:

  • poor-quality display with poor viewing angles and not the best backlight;
  • the size of the pan allows you to cook only small portions;
  • in many devices the frying mode does not work (the multicooker gives an error and turns off);
  • the network cable does not fit snugly into the socket and falls out at the slightest movement of the device;
  • milk constantly runs away, which is why you have to wash not only the entire slow cooker, but also the kitchen;
  • even if you clearly follow the recipe from the brochure and use the appropriate mode, the pastries are not prepared;
  • on the side of the multicooker there is a large paper sticker, which deteriorates during operation, and it is almost impossible to remove it;
  • raise the heating element is quite problematic;
  • over time, the bowl changes color (despite the fact that the manufacturer does not consider this a marriage, the products begin to burn and acquire a specific flavor);
  • in the kit there is no lid for the pan;
  • when using the cooking program, the meat remains raw (you have to re-process it).




conclusions

The Multicooker Redmond RMC-FM230 can be considered an innovation in the field of kitchen appliances, because it can also be used as a stove. This is a great option for students who live in a dormitory, as well as for summer residents. The rising heating element will allow you to boil the kettle, cook, fry or stew foods.

But, despite the grandeur of the idea, its implementation is lame. Users note that when raised, the heating element works poorly (but for small portions this will be enough). In addition, many modes are programmed illiterate, because of which dishes are obtained unfinished or, conversely, burn up.
