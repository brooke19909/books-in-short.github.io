Sights of Argentina: description, photo

There is a country that can make a lasting impression on art lovers, and adventure lovers, and tourists looking for comfortable conditions for relaxation. The sights of Argentina can not be seen for a month stay in this amazing state. Therefore, travelers come back here again and again.

Argentina Attractions: Buenos Aires

Familiarity with the country is best to start by visiting the most interesting places in the capital. It is here that the most striking of the sights Argentina possesses are concentrated. Buenos Aires, nicknamed South American Paris, is also known as a city that never sleeps. It is easy to get into the business mood of the capital by exploring the Puerto Madero quarter, located near the harbor. The architecture of the buildings belongs to the high-tech direction, tall skyscrapers are adjacent to luxury yachts moored to the shore.





Many sights of Argentina allow visitors to get acquainted with its history. A visit to May Square will provide an opportunity to enjoy charming views. You should definitely look at the majestic main cathedral, the town hall, admire the Pink Palace. The balcony of this building was used by Evita Peron, speaking with her illustrious aria. Here, tourists can stroll along the street on July 9, recognized as the widest in the world, including 20 lanes.

The sights of Argentina are also Palermo, it was there that the family of the famous Che Guevara lived about 50 years ago. The La Boca quarter, famous as the birthplace of tango, is also noteworthy. The variegation of the roofs and walls of buildings is associated with tradition, the founders of which were sailors. They used ship paints of all colors of the rainbow to decorate houses.

The most famous wine regions

Almost all the guests of the country try to get to Mendoza without fail. Not surprisingly, this wine region is recognized as the most important among those possessed by Argentina. The country, the sights of which cannot be fully explored in a year, attracts tourists with exquisite alcoholic drinks. About two-thirds of the local wine is made in Mendoza.





Visitors to the wine-growing region will be able to learn the basics of winemaking, learn fascinating details related to the history of this art. You should definitely visit Clos de Chacras, this wine restaurant has been operating for over 100 years.

Cities of Argentina

Buenos Aires is not limited to the list of settlements in the country that are required to visit. San Carlos de Barichole is a city whose guests can have a great time, regardless of the current season. This place becomes especially lively in winter when skiers from different parts of the world flock here. In the warmer months, you can wander along the slopes of the mountains, ride a horse or prefer a mountain bike. All conditions are created for travelers who are fond of fishing.





Colonial architecture awaits tourists in Salta, one of the most colorful cities that Argentina possesses. The country, whose sights are distinguished by its originality, provides its guests with the opportunity to ride a "train in the clouds." This amazing transport runs at an altitude of 4000 meters, transporting travelers from Salta to a small mountain village.

What else to see?

Just a crime - to visit Argentina and not see the famous Tierra del Fuego. Visitors to the archipelago will be able to ride along the Beagle Channel, getting acquainted with the marine inhabitants, visit the southernmost city in the world, which is considered Ushuaia. It is easy to relax from the city noise in the steppe - pampas, where you can taste traditional local dishes and ride horses.

Argentina is a state that deserves to come back here again and again.
