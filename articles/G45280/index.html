A metal detector for searching for gold: models, characteristics, principle of operation

Not all metal detectors are suitable for searching for gold. The standard model includes a transmitter with a sensor, a small generator and a control unit. Microcontrollers in devices operate at different frequencies. The main parameters of the detectors include the maximum detection distance, accuracy and resistance. Also, do not forget about the operating frequency.

Modern models are made with high-quality transmitters that are able to work in different modes. To learn more about detectors, you need to understand how gold is searched by a metal detector in streams.





Principle of operation

A standard metal detector for searching for gold works by generating a short-wave pulse from the transmitter. A microcontroller is used to receive the return signal.

The control unit processes the data and displays information. Devices differ in indicators. The resistance level is measured by the sensor. Ultimately, the user receives information about the presence of metal in the ground.





Model selection

Which metal detector to choose for the search for gold? In the market, the user is able to find many compact detectors. They are typically manufactured with a single transmitter. They have a low sensitivity, and they are not able to work with high humidity. The undoubted advantage of professional detectors is low weight. Thus, the user is able to carry the device for a long time, and not make much physical effort. The devices are powered by lithium batteries of different capacities.

Some modifications are made with holders. The maximum search distance is about 50 mm. Many models are equipped with a protection system. Filters for modifications are used with a resistance of 60 Ohms.

If we consider the Pirate metal detector, then its working humidity is 55%. The error coefficient is not more than 0.2%. Good models come with covers and are easy to transport. It is also worth noting that it is worth choosing devices with high-quality handles on the belt.

20 kHz devices

Inexpensive metal detectors to search for gold at 20 kHz are able to work on soft soil. Their working distance is approximately 55 mm. Some modifications are made on two sensors. Sensors in devices are used with racks, and without them. The handles of the detectors are usually rubberized. Some devices are equipped with electronic control units that operate on battery power.

30 kHz metal detectors

30 kHz detectors can be manufactured with multiple transmitters. Filters are most often used at 4 mV. They have good conductivity, but problems may arise on hard soils. The ripple level in this case depends on the control unit. If we consider high-quality modifications, then they have an electronic control unit. It works with voice notification.

The limit resistance index for detectors starts from 45 Ohms. Many devices have fuses, and lithium batteries are used. Very often there are searchers with a vibration signal. If necessary, you can connect headphones. Professional detectors are made with wide handles and clamping straps. Devices differ in security. If we consider models with one transmitter, then their error coefficient is quite low.





Low sensitivity devices

The operating frequency of the detectors is on average 10 kHz. Many models are manufactured with a single transmitter. Typically, it is installed as an integrated type. Detectors are equipped with a standard display system. It is also worth noting that there are decent models, but they have large dimensions.





High sensitivity detectors

High sensitivity devices are suitable for finding gold in places of high humidity. Experts often choose them also for good conductivity. Experts say that only models with integrated sensors should be preferred.

The optimum working distance is 55 mm. In this case, the resistance coefficient must be no more than 45 Ohms. The last to be evaluated is the stand and the holder itself. The permissible humidity for this type of detector is 75%. Batteries are used in high capacity because the devices consume a lot of energy.
