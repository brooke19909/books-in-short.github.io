Saint Tropez, France. Cote d'Azur

The small town of Saint Tropez (France, province of Provence) until recently was an ordinary fishing village, which today has become a real epicenter of social life. In the vicinity of this small, even by modern standards, town, whose population is about six thousand inhabitants, the most famous and richest people on the planet prefer to settle.



History of Saint Tropez

The first mention of the settlement in the territory, which later became known as St. Tropez, dates back to the 2nd century BC. e. The city owes its name to Saint Great Martyr Tropes, who lived in the 1st century, one of the first Christians who suffered for the faith. According to legend, by order of the emperor Nero, Tropes was cut off his head, and his body was put in a boat, which was nailed down to where the city is now located. After the fall of the Roman Empire , the pirate emirate Fraxinet settled on the Mediterranean coast, which included St. Tropez. France began to own these territories only in the 15th century. The locals still celebrate the victory in the naval battle held in 1637 off the coast of St. Tropez, when only 4 French ships completely defeated the Spanish squadron, consisting of 22 galleons.

At the beginning of the 20th century, famous painters began to visit here. The French neo-impressionist painter Paul Signac created his pointillism technique precisely in St. Tropez (France, as you know, is the birthplace of many stylistic trends in painting), and Henri Matisse drew his creative mood here. What is famous for St. Tropez today

Perhaps the main thing that attracts tourists here is the sea. It is as warm here as it is on the entire Cote d'Azur. The swimming season in St. Tropez lasts from May to September. Heavily rugged, and therefore unusually picturesque, the coastline stretches for many kilometers. Here everyone will find a place to taste, as much as possible corresponding to his desires and possibilities. Numerous St. Tropez hotels are also located here. France, in particular its Cote d'Azur, has always attracted many lovers to soak up the sun and swim in the warm waters of the Mediterranean Sea.

In addition to the beaches, in Saint-Tropez you can visit the Annunciation chapel, built in the XVII century, the local Maritime Museum, the Butterfly House (in its collection there are more than 20 thousand samples) or the Museum of Modern Art. You can simply stroll along the narrow, filled with various fashion boutiques, craft workshops, city streets of St. Tropez. France, as you know, is a trendsetter and is rich in such establishments. You can also go to nearby villages to admire the magnificent views that attract artists from around the world.

Do not forget that the country in which Saint Tropez is located is France. On the map of this small town it is easy to find many establishments that are very popular among fans of noisy entertainment and fans of gastronomic delights. In numerous cafes and restaurants you can taste real culinary art and taste the best varieties of famous French wines.
