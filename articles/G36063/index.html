Bali - Island of Dreams

Bali is a small Indonesian island located between two oceans. From the south, its beaches are washed by the warm Indian Ocean, from the Pacific North. It is a famous resort in the whole world.

The length of the island is about 145 kilometers. But Bali is not only an island with pristine beaches and magnificent sunsets. The mountain range, which stretches along the entire island, is a seismically active zone. Volcanic eruptions here can happen at any moment. In 1963, a major eruption of the Agung volcano claimed the lives of almost half the population of the island, destroying residential and commercial buildings to the ground.

Tourists love the island for its stable warm temperature throughout the year, its magnificent nature and warm ocean. Abandoned hotel

The story of an abandoned hotel in Bali is known throughout the world. Tourists have heard about this unusual place and often ask local residents to take them there. However, the Balinese themselves do not really like to talk about this gloomy place and try to avoid not only visits, but also conversations about it. There is an opinion that the ghosts of those people who, according to legend, disappeared in an unknown direction in one night, roam around the hotel at night.

An abandoned building is located fifty kilometers from Denpasar. This is the former Bedugul Taman Rekreasi Hotel & Resort, and is located at an altitude of one and a half thousand meters above sea level. Every tourist would like to visit it on their vacation.

One of the most popular and widespread versions of why the hotel has turned into an old, forgotten building, is as follows.

In 1993, the son of Indonesian President Tommy Suharto began construction of a magnificent hotel. The building was to become the residence for the presidential family, as well as increase the flow of tourists to Budugul. Since the president‚Äôs son was a successful businessman, it was planned to invest large sums in the construction to give the hotel a truly royal luxurious look. The best foreign architects and designers were invited to realize all the ambitious ideas, and only natural marble, wood and silver were used as materials.

Construction was abruptly stopped in 2002. As tourists around the world suggest, the blame for the terrible terrorist attacks in Kut. Many people died, and tourists for a long time forgot the way here. An abandoned hotel in Bali has become a symbol of those terrible events.

The legend of the Chinese millionaire

The most fantastic legend about the appearance of a huge hotel is the legend of a Chinese businessman. It is said that at the end of the twentieth century, a Chinese millionaire who once visited Bali fell in love with this island with all his heart. But he was a man who was used to working, and instead of resting and doing yoga, he developed a business plan and began to build a hotel of enormous proportions at an altitude of 1,500 meters. Another, mystical version, reports that the hotel was still open and even received several thousand tourists. But in one night all the people who were on its territory disappeared inexplicably somewhere.

When the difficult period began, the hotel was put up for sale. No buyer was found, and he turned into the only abandoned hotel in Bali, where people disappeared.

What an abandoned hotel looks like now

The full name of an abandoned hotel in Bali is Bedugul Taman Rekreasi Hotel & Resort. Despite the fact that more than fifteen years have passed since the construction was stopped, the hotel is striking in its scale.

During construction, great attention was paid to the quality of materials. And not in vain. Both the interior and exterior of the hotel is in excellent condition so far. If street decor elements begin to collapse under the influence of wind, prolonged rains and fogs, then only a layer of dust can contribute to destruction.

There are no guards at the abandoned hotel. Nobody watches the garden, so lush island plants almost filled all the paths with themselves, sprouting between stones and slabs of marble.

Pool baths were never cleaned and began to collect rainwater. Water does not go anywhere, so over time the water in them turned into a dense swamp slurry, which was chosen by persistent marsh plants.

Balconies, railings and stairs are everywhere entwined with various kinds of ivy, the branches of which are so intertwined that they can withstand the weight of an adult. The steps were covered with moss. Long tentacles of perennial vines hang from the roofs, and wild birds have chosen places under the flooring.

The main entrance to the hotel is closed by a makeshift fence with barbed wire. Some tourists report that sometimes at the entrance you can meet a gatekeeper who will open the main gate for you to enter by car for just a few cents.
