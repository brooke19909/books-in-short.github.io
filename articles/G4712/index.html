Hotel Residence Hotel & SPA (Russia, Repino) - overview, description and reviews of tourists

The village of Repino is located in one of the most picturesque places of the Karelian Isthmus. It is separated from St. Petersburg by a distance of 45 km. The uniqueness of the microclimate, sandy beaches on the coast of the Gulf of Finland, the proximity of a magnificent pine park, the presence of a modern highway and a slight distance from the city are factors that have long attracted many residents of the Leningrad region. In Soviet times, many boarding houses and sanatoriums were built in Repino, the rest and treatment in which were very popular among Soviet citizens.





Modern Repino has all the necessary infrastructure for an excellent vacation in the countryside at any time of the year. Guests here are given the opportunity to stay in modern hotels, visit museums, restaurants, and all kinds of entertainment venues. Every year, the coast attracts more and more people who want to relax. Today, to spend leisure time, enjoying the incredible beauty of these places, not only residents of the North-West region and the capital, as it was before, come to Repino, but also guests from other regions and countries. Among the variety of options for accommodating visitors , the Residence Hotel & Spa is very popular.

Penates: Museum-Estate

Lovers of attractions pay special attention to the house located at ul. Primorskoye Shosse, 411. The estate was once owned by the famous Russian artist Ilya Repin. A fancy two-story building under a glass roof was built according to the project of the owner himself. Pyramidal lanterns and many large windows, verandas, light openings made of glass on the ceiling, through which natural light enters the artist‚Äôs workshops, are noteworthy.

In fact, from this house in 1899 the history of the resort began. It is known that the small manor acquired in those days by I.E. Repin soon became the favorite resting place of the intelligentsia of St. Petersburg. The best representatives of Russian culture liked to come to Kuokkala, among which the names of M. Gorky, V. Mayakovsky, F. I. Chaliapin, S. Yesenin, I. A. Bunin and others are named. The artist himself has lived in the estate for the past thirty years, here and buried. The house became one of the first memorial museums in Russia.
