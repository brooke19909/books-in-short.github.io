Tour of 27 waterfalls in the Dominican Republic: photos, reviews and best tips before visiting

If you think that rest in the Dominican Republic comes down to lazy lying on the beaches, eating fresh fruits and cocktails, swimming in the warmest sea, you lose a lot, because there is such a unique phenomenon as 27 waterfalls in the Dominican Republic. Get to know them better.

Dominican Republic

Dominican Republic is one of the most popular tropical states in the Caribbean. In search of the sun and entertainment, so many tourists flock here during the year that they can populate all European cities, from small to large. The clear sea, many kilometers of white sand beaches, comfortable hotels and delicious food - this is what this wonderful tourist destination is associated with.

The Dominican Republic has not only magnificent nature, but also a rich history. The island was discovered in the fifteenth century by Christopher Columbus. At different periods of its existence, it was controlled by the Spaniards, the French, the British and the Americans. The country has long been economically dependent on foreign countries, especially the United States. The Dominican Republic survived many uprisings and wars, but by the middle of the twentieth century, through the holding of popular elections, it turned into a republic and to this day has good diplomatic relations with many civilized countries of the world.

Waterfall path

27 waterfalls in the Dominican Republic are located in the Puerto Plata area, near the city of Imbert. To the sights, several excursions of various types are organized daily: VIP, individual and small groups. It is not recommended to take young children with them, for whom this walk in the heat will turn into a real torture. If you are tormented by the question of how to get to 27 waterfalls in the Dominican Republic quickly and without much loss of energy in the heat, there is only one answer: a taxi. The cost of a one-way road will be approximately thirty dollars, but the car will take you to your place in just twenty to thirty minutes.

After arriving at the main location, tourists are greeted by local residents and offer a light breakfast, since they will have to leave home or from the hotel early in the morning. After this, preparation for the excursion will begin, which includes mandatory instruction, as well as a life jacket and hard hat. The ticket price also includes the services of an experienced guide.

Precautionary measures

As one good Russian proverb says, safety precautions are written in blood. Of course, we are exaggerating, but in the rainforests, where 27 waterfalls are located in the Dominican Republic, precautionary measures will never be superfluous.

Before leaving your home, check to see if you have any basic documents with you. Leaving them at the hotel is, of course, scary, but no one knows what might happen along the way. Take medical insurance with you and your family members. Wrap the documents in a waterproof bag so that they are not inadvertently wet.

Be sure to bring drinking water and hats with you. In the heat, these are the first means of necessity. Leave your bank cards in the room and use only cash, exchange them in advance at the bank or at the hotel staff.

If you suffer from any phobias or know what may happen, for example, a panic attack with you or someone from your loved ones, be sure to warn the guide. Experienced experts will tell you the best option for entertainment and will be ready when an emergency occurs.
