Technological cemetery in Chernobyl: photos

The equipment cemetery in Chernobyl is one of the largest technogenic dumps remaining in the territory of the former Soviet Union, which makes us think a lot. Admittedly, she is not the only one. After the collapse of the USSR, a lot of military equipment remained in small republics. It became too expensive for the new authorities to keep it, so they simply wrote it off and left it to rust on the street. But with this cemetery a completely different story. In this case, the equipment was specially thrown into the danger zone in order to protect people from the effects of radiation that these machines were contaminated with.

Chernobyl accident

The equipment cemetery in Chernobyl is one of the consequences of the Chernobyl accident, which we can observe today.



Perhaps the date of April 26, 1986 was remembered by the whole world. It was on this day that the largest technological disaster in the world occurred. The accident at the Chernobyl nuclear power plant immediately claimed many lives, but its consequences in the long run turned out to be much more destructive. For many years, those who were somehow connected with this accident or its liquidation, struggled with diseases and ailments caused by radiation. The harm to human health caused by this accident, according to scientists, will fully affect the next several generations.

Liquidation of an accident

To eliminate the consequences of the accident, more than half a million people and a huge amount of equipment were involved, which now can not even be counted. As soon as possible, more than 100 thousand residents were evacuated from the territory of 30 kilometers surrounding the nuclear power plant. These places have become uninhabitable forever.





The authorities promptly decided to build a sarcophagus, which was supposed to bury the remains of radioactive substances, as well as the ruins of an exploding nuclear reactor. For this, a large number of construction equipment was involved, which also turned out to be contaminated.

What equipment was in the cemetery?

Today, a large number of photographs are available, which depict the cemetery of equipment in Chernobyl. These are buses, trucks, military armored personnel carriers and even airplanes. The personal cars of local residents were also abandoned, since during the evacuation only the most necessary things had to be taken. In addition, most people believed that very soon they would be able to return home again.





The cemetery of abandoned equipment in Chernobyl clearly demonstrates that everyone who could be involved in the shortest possible time participated in the liquidation of the accident. First of all, this applies to military and power structures, for example, to fire services. There were many volunteers who came on their vehicles. Today, disassembled cars look especially eerie in the cemetery of radioactive equipment in Chernobyl. It turns out that people, risking their lives, took them apart for parts to resell, without thinking about the fact that individual parts are most likely radioactive.

Impact of the Chernobyl accident

How did the Chernobyl disaster affect people and the environment? It is difficult to answer this question even 30 years after the tragedy. Obviously, many diseases are younger today - heart attacks are increasingly occurring with very young people. It is possible that this is one of the consequences of an accident at a power plant. An even more obvious consequence is a surge in cancer. After all, radiation seriously affects oncology. In addition, she had a negative impact on the animal world. In the restricted area, the population of individuals has declined significantly.

The cemetery to which this article is dedicated perhaps best illustrates the helplessness a person experiences against atomic energy.
