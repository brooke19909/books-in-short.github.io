Memorial Day of the young anti-fascist hero - February 8

Since 1964, the Day of the Young Anti-Fascist Hero has been celebrated around the world. It was approved by the UN International Assembly in honor of the guys who died at an anti-fascist rally in 1962: fifteen-year-old Parisian Daniel Feri and Iraqi fighter against violence in his country Fadil Jamal, who died from torture in a prison in Baghdad in 1963.



Both boys died on February 8, one year apart. And 21 years earlier, similar tragedies occurred in different countries of the world on this very day. In France, five brave underground boys from Paris were tortured. In the Soviet Union, participants in the Krasnodon organization Young Guard were shot

It was these fateful coincidences that served as the day on February 8 and became Memorial Day for the young anti-fascist hero.

Memorial Day of the young anti-fascist hero (February 8)

The war has a childish face - everyone knows that. But how many know how many times children and war intersected?

On February 8, Russia recalls Soviet boys and girls who, shoulder to shoulder with adults, defended the country during the Great Patriotic War.

There were so many of these young heroes that memory could not save all the names. Famous and unknown little heroes of the Great War, they fought in thousands and died on the fronts and in the occupation. They fired from one trench: adult soldiers and yesterday's schoolchildren. They blew up bridges, columns with fascist armored vehicles, covered their comrades with their breasts.





They became fearless underground, committing dangerous sabotage and helping to shelter wounded soldiers. Every day they risked their lives, and not everyone managed to survive in a meat grinder of a terrible war.

And on land, and at sea, and above the clouds ...

Pioneers and Komsomol members, urban and rural, these boys and girls all over the world glorified the heroism and unbending courage of the Soviet people. Young patriots smashed the enemy both on land, and at sea, and in the air.

Twelve-year-old Boris Kuleshin since 1942 fought in the Black Sea Fleet, in the destroyer "Tashkent". During air raids, the boy brought shells to the guns, and during the lull he looked after the wounded.

Arkady Kamanin - the famous "flyer", at the age of 14 he was appointed to the post of pilot of the 423rd air squadron. He fought on the 1st and 2nd Ukrainian fronts, on the Kalinin front. Before reaching adulthood, the young warrior was twice awarded the Order of the Red Star and the Order of the Red Banner.

Leonid Golikov, scout of the partisan detachment, which operated in the Pskov and Novgorod regions, participated in more than 20 battles, and was awarded many orders and medals for courage and bravery. Lenya received the highest distinction posthumously, he was awarded the title Hero of the Soviet Union.

Little heroes of the big war

It is impossible to list all of our early adult soldiers of World War II. But at the mere thought of what they accomplished in the name of victory in their 12-17 years, pride for the country that raised such "eagles" is overwhelming.

Bitterness burns our hearts from the awareness of how short their lives were, how absurd to die at the age of 14, and not having time to grow up. It seems that nowhere in world history has such a mass heroism of children and adolescents been recorded as in Soviet Russia during the Great Patriotic War.



On February 8, the feast of the young anti-fascist hero, the whole world will freeze in a single sigh about the heroically dead boys and girls. They lived in different countries, spoke different languages, but performed the same feat - they fought for the liberation of their land.

They strive to provide as much historical information as possible about the days of the great battles and the unparalleled courage of the little heroes of the great war.
