Coins of Brazil: Flights, Cruzeiro, Cruzado, Reals and Centavos

Brazil is a unique country in the sense of "moneymaking." In most countries, the name of the national currency is kindly, but in the largest country in Latin America its name was easily changed.

Flight One

Well, what do you want from a remote colony, where there was always a shortage of cash money supply? Now it seems ridiculous, but the first coin minted on the territory of Brazil in 1652 was the Dutch guilders and stovers from the Dutch illegally trying to settle on these shores.

And the first flights were de facto coins of different states, which were in the hands of the Brazilians. Most of them were Spanish realities, and so the plural form of the word in Portuguese came out - "flight". That is, a "flight" is a "real". Foreign coins were made their own primarily by means of minting.

The first mint in Brazil began operating in 1694, and it was then that the flight began its official history. In 1822, Brazil gained independence and completely separated from the Portuguese banking system: flights gained full legitimacy, but their real cost was very low. In monetary terms, these were steel coins, stylistically continuing the series of the last Cruzeiro. Denomination: one, five, ten, one hundred cruzado and one, five, ten, twenty, fifty centavos.
