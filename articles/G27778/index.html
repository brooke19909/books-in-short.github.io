Hyde Park and the Royal Ones

It is hard to imagine England without this worldwide attraction, open to visitors 19 hours a day. But once the beloved Hyde Park, whose photos are striking in its beauty and majesty, simply did not exist. This territory belonged to Westminster Abbey. The history of the now popular park began in 1536. This cultural complex owes its existence to King Henry VIII. The following royal people made a special contribution to the development of the national park.





  1. Henry VIII. It was he who took Hyde's possession from the abbey and turned it into a hunting ground. This was in the first half of the 16th century.
  2. In the XVII century, King James I decided to open the park, which at that time was the largest of all existing, for the public.
  3. Charles II made sure that this place became one of the most popular in London. The promenade along the shady alleys, and especially along the Rotten Row road, has become a favorite pastime of the English aristocracy.
  4. Under Queen Caroline Hyde Park in London lost part of its territory - Kensington Gardens were separated from it.

What to do in the famous park

If you decide to visit Hyde Park in London, you can be calm: it will definitely not be boring.

  • Lake Serpentine will joyfully open its arms to you. There you can fish and even swim. But even more unusual sensations will give a ride on a boat. It can always be rented.   • The special Rotten Row is designed for horse riding. In general, if you like horses, you definitely need to visit this amazing place. There is also a racing circle, and a riding school.
  • For sports fans, too, have their own surprises. Feel free to head to its southern part. There you will find sports fields for rugby, softball, cricket. Or head west, where you will be greeted by a sports center with tennis courts and bowling alleys.
