Mako-satin: characteristics and features of the fabric

The variety of fabrics is so great that you can just get confused in their types. However, not all materials are valued equally. The most popular and high quality are fabrics made from natural fibers.

Advantages of Cotton Fabrics

A striking example of quality material is cotton. It is environmentally friendly and practical. Various types of fabrics are made from it, which differ in density, quality, cost and strength. One of the highest quality cotton fabrics is mako-satin. Its main purpose is sewing bed linen. However, this type of material can be used for other purposes, for example, for the manufacture of blouses, light scarves and other products.

What is the peculiarity of mako-satin fabric?

When buying bedding or underwear, people need not only design, but also the quality of the fabric from which the products were sewn. Naturally, natural matters are most valued. But few people know what mako-sateen is. What is this fabric? Reviews say that this type of matter is 100% natural. Is it really?





Satin is called a fabric made of cotton threads. This is actually a fallacy. Satin is not a material, but a way to weave cotton threads. Mako-satin is a weave of the finest fibers of yarn. The ratio of threads in this material is 4: 1, as a result of which the fabric acquires a pleasant shine and smoothness. It is also characterized by increased wear resistance and a dense surface.

How is mako satin made?

What kind of fabric, we have already found out. But what is her secret? To create such a high-class material, it is necessary to observe the following parameters during its production:

  • The purity of the raw materials.
  • The fineness of natural fibers.
  • Satin weave technology .

This material is made from Egyptian cotton. Mainly used Mako variety (Mitafifi). This cotton is characterized by especially thin fibers with a length of 40-55 mm, the twisting of which results in the thinnest fabric of increased wear resistance and density. Raw materials are grown for production in the Nile Valley without the use of any chemical additives and pesticides.





The purity of the material depends on the quality of the dressing. In the production process, makosatin is subjected to various stages of processing. So, to give shine to the front of the fabric, the cotton fibers are twisted. Density of the material is imparted by treatment with an alkaline solution. The paintings are dyed in a reactive way, during which a coloring substance envelops each of the fibers and penetrates deep into it. Due to this, fabrics acquire saturated colors that do not fade in the sun.

Main advantages

What are the benefits of mako satin? Reviews eloquently say that bedding from this material is unusually light, smooth, delicate, durable and silky. In terms of appearance and quality, this material is practically inferior to silk products, but this fabric is much cheaper.





The main advantages that characterize mako-satin include:

  • breathability;
  • durability;
  • simplicity of leaving (material is easily washed off from the majority of types of pollution);
  • the fabric practically does not crease and perfectly holds its shape;
  • Does not fade during washing and leaves no traces of paint on other surfaces;
  • bed linen from this fabric gives the same tactile sensations as natural silk;
  • not electrified;
  • does not accumulate and does not create dust;
  • is hypoallergenic material;
  • absorbs water well and dries quickly enough.

How to care for the fabric?

After buying bedding from this material, it is recommended to wash them before use, while the duvet cover and pillowcases should be turned inside out. If there are buttons and zippers, then they must be fastened.





For washing, it is recommended to choose non-aggressive products that do not contain bleaching components. Subject to this paragraph, even after 200 washings, the material will look new.

Bedding from this fabric is considered to be elite, as a result of which the first wash is recommended to be carried out at a temperature of no more than 40 ¬∫, then the water can be heated up to 60 ¬∫.

It is not necessary to wash natural fabrics in the same container with polyester products, because the material from this can lose its aesthetic and tactile qualities. Also, do not lay white and colored linen in the washing machine at the same time. After drying, the fabric does not need to be ironed, since it holds its shape well and practically does not crease.





So what is mako-satin? What is this fabric? Customer reviews reveal all the positive aspects of this material, but the words are not able to convey the sensations that bed linen made from it gives. Sleeping on such a bed is the greatest pleasure, and the variety of colors will help you choose a set that matches the tone of the overall design of the bedroom.
