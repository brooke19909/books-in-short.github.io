Fedor Shcherbina and Vladimir Korolenko

The dzhanhot that stretches along the mountain beauty Hotetsay (by the way, another translation of the unusual name "river flowing along the beam") has a rich history. In this place once lived Adyghe tribes. But if we talk about a farm, then its appearance dates back to the end of the 19th century.

It is noteworthy: the writer V. G. Korolenko lived and worked on the same slope of the gorge in the estate (at the dacha) of his younger brother for some time (years of life 1853-1921). Korolenko Sr. chose a site for construction with the help of the Zemstvo leader, historian, author of the book "History of the Kuban Cossack Army" F. Shcherbina.

The latter already had land on the opposite slope (the Shcherbin brothers were well-known landowners in the area), he built the estate there. In the summer, the Korolenko Museum-Estate is actively visited by tourists from different parts of Russia, who have chosen the coast of Dzhanhot for recreation.

Between Janhot and Paraskoveevka

In high season, the air temperature is comfortable: plus twenty-five degrees (water - plus twenty-three). Winter is mild, only occasionally does the thermometer drop to zero. You can swim in the sea for five consecutive months: from mid-May to the very "bottom" of October.





The resort village of Paraskoveevka is located very close to Dzhanhot. If you look at the map, all places for swimming are located between these settlements. The sloping shores, ready to shelter bathers, are among the cleanest in the whole region.
