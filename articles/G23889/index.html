Fleece mittens: pattern. How to sew mittens with your own hands

With the advent of winter frosts you always want to wrap yourself up warmer. Mittens from fleece, the pattern of which will be presented below, as well as a description of sewing gloves from fur, will be a good material for a piggy bank of useful tips for needlewomen.

Of course, it‚Äôs easier for someone to take knitting needles or a hook and a skein of thread and knit mittens, but a sewn product will be much warmer and more reliable, especially if you use warm, dense fabric.





Material selection

Sewing fleece mittens with your own hands is the surest solution for warming your hands in cold weather. It is this fabric that will protect against frost, and will make it easy to sculpt snowballs, if you suddenly want to remember childhood.

You can also use natural or artificial fur. Here the choice should be based solely on personal preferences. Fashionable women and animal defenders will appreciate mittens made of fur with a long pile, for example, white.

Mittens made of rabbit or goat fur will look great, but a mouton will not work for this purpose, it is too dense.

The whole charm of mittens is that for their manufacture you can use old coats with warm fur linings or soft cashmere, as well as the remnants of fleece after sewing larger items.





How to build a pattern

It should be said right away that excellent mittens will come out of fleece, the pattern of which is done individually. It is being built in stages, detail after detail:

  1. Place your hand palm down on a piece of paper and circle around the outline without giving your thumb, stepping back about a centimeter for allowances for processing slices and stitching cut elements.
  2. Next, you should put your hand on another sheet and circle only the thumb, while outlining the line of its articulation with the palm.
  3. After the articulation line is marked in the first drawing, and its mirror reflection is drawn to the finger detail without highlighting the articulation line.

Mittens made of fleece, the pattern of which consists of three cut elements, are the most convenient, as they do not hamper the movement of the finger. But if the fleece stretches well, then you can make a template by simply circling your hand along the contour.

Also, for sewing, you need a strip of knitted fabric, from which cuffs should be cut out, which are a rectangle with sides equal to the circumference of the arm and double the height of the cuff.



Cutting products

When calculating the fabric, it should be borne in mind that material will be required not only for the top of the product, but also for the interior design, i.e. the lining. For this purpose, it is also best to use fleece.

Given the lining, it is necessary for two mittens to cut out two mirrored details of the fingers and palm elements from the main fabric and from the lining. Fleece mittens, the pattern of which is described above, can be sewn without a cuff, but with a wide dense elastic band inserted along the wrist line.

All details should be laid out on a fabric folded twice inward, stabbed with tailor pins and cut out, stepping back from the paper about 0.5 cm.





Product assembly

So how to sew mittens? The pattern is designed, the details are cut out of the material and now you can begin to assemble the product. First, the parts are sewn along the joint line of the thumb with the palm of the hand. After they connect the back of the palm to the other side of the finger, and then all the elements are collected along the contour of the mitten. In the same way, the lining part is assembled, leaving the seam near the fingertips open. After folding parts from the main fabric and lining along the line of the wrist and close it. Here is the time to insert a cuff or elastic into the seam. They turn the product through an open hole and carefully sew it manually with a blind seam.



How to sew mittens, the pattern of which with a whole-cut thumb? No difficulties should arise here. Cutting and assembly is carried out on the same principle. But it should be noted that on each smooth part of the slices, it is necessary to make notches for allowances that will eliminate the tightening of the seams of the product.

Sewing mittens from fur

The pattern of mittens from fur is no different from the construction of a fleece mitten described above. Here the same allowances for seams, and the assembly steps are similar. The only thing to consider is a pile bent into the finished seams, which should be removed with a hook or a gypsy needle.

The pattern of fur mittens is transferred to the canvas from the wrong side and is cut with a sharp tailor's knife or scissors with thin blades. In this case, the fur pile should be carefully separated so as not to cut it off. Only under this condition there will be no bald spots on the finished mitten. The pattern of fur mittens must be with a sewn thumb. Otherwise, such a product will be inconvenient.

The lining part for fur mittens can be made of fleece or a thinner knitted fabric. Dress fabric for this purpose is not suitable. Here you need to make it convenient to move your fingers and the product does not hold your hand.

The best solution for decorating the wrist of a fur mitten is an elastic band inserted at a distance of about 5 cm from the edge. In this case, the product itself must be of sufficient length to go under the cuffs of a jacket or coat.





Decoration

The developed pattern of mittens made of fur or fleece is half the battle. It is also not difficult to cut the product from the fabric and assemble them together. And to make the product interesting, you should consider decorative elements for mittens. It can be stripes in the form of inscriptions or fabric flowers or laces with fur buboes.

Exclusive embroideries and appliqu√©s add a special charm to the product. Also, do not forget about the possibility of combining different colors of material and fur, which will make handmade mittens even more interesting. One can only imagine those white mittens of white fur with a large pile with dotted splashes of black, as on the fur coats of kings in a fairy tale. Or bullfinches embroidered on a fleece mittens with a sprig of mountain ash. There is no limit to fantasy.

To make the mittens more practical, the top layer is best made from a lining, water-repellent fabric, and inside from fleece or sheepskin on a knitted basis. These mittens are perfect for children, as they can endlessly sculpt snowballs without fear that the pens get wet and stick.
