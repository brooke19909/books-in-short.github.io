Alloy Steel Production

Ferrous metallurgy uses molybdenum powder. Where is it used? For alloying steels and cast irons. Adding this element to the composition of the alloys significantly improves their quality. Increases elasticity, wear resistance, resistance to shock. About 0.5% of molybdenum is added to structural steels, from which their structure becomes fine-grained and more uniform, the fragility of the final products is reduced.

Adding other substances allows you to get special compositions that are widely used in various industries. Alloys containing cobalt and nickel (50-60%), as well as chromium (about 20-28%) are alloyed by adding molybdenum. Where is such material used? The answer lies in its special properties - high heat resistance. It is used in the manufacture of skin parts for aircraft and missiles.





The use of alloys with molybdenum

When niobium, titanium and other heavy-duty metals are added to molybdenum, the heat resistance of alloys increases. Such a composition can be used to create parts for gas turbines and combustion chambers in rocket science.

In alloys containing a large amount of molybdenum (17‚Äì28%), corrosion resistance increases. They are not afraid even of interaction with any acid (except hydrofluoric).

The refractory properties of molybdenum and its use in the creation of special pipes are of great importance in nuclear energy. Such products can withstand molten lithium. It acts as a coolant in uranium reactors. In addition, molybdenum itself in the form of the Mo-99 isotope has found application as an indicator in the nuclear industry.

Due to the refractoriness of molybdenum, molds are created from it for casting parts from copper, aluminum and zinc. The high strength of the metal allows processes to be carried out under high pressure.

Application in other industries

Molybdenum has found application in the oil industry. There it is used as a catalyst capable of purifying products from sulfur impurities. Lubricants are made from aluminum disulfide. They stabilize the operation of various devices and protect surfaces from mechanical stress at high temperatures. This lubricant also has anti-corrosion properties.

In the manufacture of paints and varnishes, where molybdenum and its oxides are used, stable pigments of yellow-orange tones are obtained. Synthesis of artificial fibers also does not pass without this substance. To increase the nitrogen content in the soil, microfertilizers are used, which include molybdenum.

Molybdenum has a significant role in the human body. He is involved in the synthesis of hemoglobin, nitrogen and purine metabolism. Responsible for the absorption of iron and vitamin C, is a powerful antioxidant. The trace element has an oncoprotective, anti-aging effect.
