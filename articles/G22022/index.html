Physiological features of songbirds

Depending on the shape of the beak, all songbirds are implied in two large species: granivorous, which have a thick beak, and insectivores, which have a thin beak. The subtleties of the content of the songbird will depend on the type of feathered friend.

Therefore, when a person picks up a bird in his hands, care should be taken to ensure that the bird's breathing is normal and its breathing movements are not restricted.

Maintenance and care

Songbird forest birds are a sociable population. In order to properly keep them in captivity, you need to make pairs, and even better whole groups. It is known that catching and keeping songbirds requires special knowledge, so you should first study a little the material that is in this article, and then make feathered friends.

It is recommended to choose the correct cage for such birds. You can‚Äôt use tall and narrow cells, as a bird usually cannot normally exist under such conditions. Both round and angular cages are not suitable for keeping songbirds. An ideal cell would be a one that uses a stainless steel grill and a pallet made of any artificial materials. The tray should be carefully inspected during each cleaning, as it should not have cracks or cracks.

Catching and keeping songbirds always requires training. First of all, you should take care of the room and the place where the cell should stand. The room should be ventilated and bright. It is desirable that the cage was near the window, but one of the walls of the cage should adjoin the wall so that birds could snuggle up to it at any moment and calm down.

If the cell is very large, then you can make small houses from plants in such an enclosure, but you should only pick up non-toxic flowers. You can set the cage so that the shadow on the cage falls from a shelf or from a tall flower. Birds are sensitive to lighting. If the change of day and night does not occur naturally, care should be taken to cover the cell with a dark blanket at night.

It is worth taking care that there are sticks in the cage. They should be of different sizes and different sizes. They can be made from a variety of non-toxic wood, such as birch or maple. Most often, birds use them either for roosting, or sharpen their beak about them. There should not be any glass inserts in the cage.

It is worth taking care of the bedding for birds. It is better to use sand litter, but it is desirable that the grains of sand be of different sizes. The sand should be coarse, and the pebbles in its composition often eat birds to improve digestion.

In the cage, bathing bathtubs are also necessary, which can be hung directly on the cell walls. Bathing bathtubs, like drinking bowls, need to be cleaned every day and always monitor their cleanliness. You can‚Äôt put drinking bowls and baths under roostes, as then droppings will fall into them. The cage should have houses for breeding offspring.

Feeding

The maintenance of forest songbirds requires the competent and proper distribution of the feed ration. The food ration of such birds should consist of three parts of grain, one part of soft food, which is enriched with protein, and green food. If protein supplements are not taken into account, then this can lead to the development of protein deficiency.

It is best to use pre-germinated grain feed, as it has a lot of vitamins and nutrients. It is not difficult to prepare it: you need to take the grain and soak it for a day, then leave it covered with a wet cloth for another day and wait until green seedlings appear. But you can‚Äôt keep such a grain for a long time, as it quickly deteriorates. It is necessary to try to acquire that seed mixture for germination, which did not pass radiation.

You can not cook it at home, but purchase ready-made food in which there is enough protein. Songbirds are not allowed to give cabbage.
