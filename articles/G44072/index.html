Application: do-it-yourself paper dove

Looking for new ideas for creative activities with children? The application "Pigeon" from paper can be made together with the child, if you choose a simple way of working. Create interesting decorations. Teach children artistic skill. This is a useful, enjoyable and interesting way to spend time.





Options and Ideas

The application "Dove" made of paper can be presented in various designs:

  • From a glued flat image.
  • With volumetric parts obtained by folding and gluing additional elements (tail, wings).
  • From paper with a textured surface formed from napkins.
  • Of the elements in the technique of quilling.

As you can see, there are several manufacturing methods. Little children can also cope with the first if you prepare a cut out template for them. The latter method is suitable for older children or for co-creation with parents.





Where to use paper birds

If you approach the work creatively and with pleasure, you will get a very beautiful application "Pigeon" from paper. With your own hands you can make a postcard, a decorative panel for decorating the interior, decorating a gift box using this idea. The subject matter is suitable for arranging congratulations on Victory Day, and, for example, for Mother's Day. A pigeon, as an addition to a bouquet of flowers, may appear on a card for the spring holiday on March 8. At home, you can offer your child this option for making a gift to someone from relatives or your acquaintances for the wedding. These paper birds - symbols of peace, kindness and love - will be able to decorate any holiday.

Preparation of materials and tools

The application "Dove" from paper will require the preparation of the following:

  • white sheets;
  • napkins;
  • pencil and eraser;
  • printed on paper template;
  • scissors;
  • PVA glue.

To work in the quilling technique , strips of paper about 5 mm wide will be needed, as well as a tool for twisting them. For this purpose, you can use a knitting needle, a toothpick or a skewer. As you can see, there is nothing complicated, so the work can be completely entrusted to children. You are required to prepare templates - draw a contour or print a picture.





A simple option for children's creativity

The simplest dove of the world (paper application) can be made as follows:

  1. Take a sheet of white or color cardboard or paper as the basis (background). Leave it flat or bend in the shape of a folding card.
  2. Prepare patterns of birds cut out of white paper in any way.
  3. Stick the pigeons on the base.
  4. Draw or glue colored eyes and a beak; you can also glue a wing or tail detail on top.

It is good to supplement such an application with simple flowers, an image of the globe, children's hands, or little men holding hands.

For older kids, it‚Äôs worth complicating the job:

  1. Prepare napkins and tear them into small pieces. The smaller the particles, the more accurate the application.
  2. Put the resulting mass in a plate or other similar container and pour a small amount of water.
  3. When the wipes are soaked in liquid, wring out the excess and add PVA glue to the mass.
  4. Stick napkins on a pre-cut dove pattern.

After drying, the surface of the bird will turn out textured, slightly voluminous. In this way, you can even form something similar to the relief, showing the shape of the wings. By a similar principle, it is easy to work with crumpled paper.

Paper dove: applique (volumetric)





To get this option, use one of the following methods. Method No. 1 consists of the following steps:

  1. Prepare a bird template.
  2. Cut squares from white napkins (choose the size yourself according to the dimensions of the bird).
  3. Twist the resulting blanks in the form of cone pads. To do this, use a regular pencil.
  4. Dip the narrow parts of the parts in the PVA so that the napkin is not wet and stick to the surface of the pigeon blank.

So you get a bird with a very delicate openwork plumage.

Method number 2 is to prepare or create your own appropriate template for a pigeon, from which you can cut the overall shape, and then, using the folds of the parts, create a semi-volume bird figure. The surface of such a product, by the way, can be decorated with any of the above methods. To get a more realistic image, make individual parts of the plumage in the form of scales with paper cut along the perimeter of the edges of paper. Stick them all over the surface of the bird. On the wings, the details may be larger, on the breast and trunk - small. This option, of course, is quite complicated, but older children will cope with it.

Quilling applique

This method is also not easy, but the products are very effective. They look lacy, airy, so they are quite suitable not only for children's creativity, but also as wedding decorations. As a blank, you can take any pattern of a dove of peace from paper. Application can be performed both on a flat surface of a wall, postcard, and on a volumetric one, for example, on a glass, a bottle of champagne, a vase, an arched structure.

To make simple doves in this technique, it will be enough to twist just a few details: a head in the form of a circle, a body, wings and three details of a drop-shaped tail. If you want to create a real masterpiece, you have to work hard, because each element of the body can be assembled from a large number of openwork twisted blanks.

As you can see, the application "Dove" from paper is performed in different ways. Choose the right one according to the age of the child and his ability to work with materials. Create creative works together with children and teach their independence.
