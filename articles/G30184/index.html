Education as a sociocultural phenomenon and pedagogical process

Education can be viewed as a sociocultural phenomenon from the point of view that this process was formed and directly disseminated by society. After all, it is continuously associated with the life of people, their skills, which play an important role in the progression of human development.





The concepts of education and the pedagogical process

As stated in many scientific works, education is training and enlightenment, as well as students gaining socio-historical experience. Here you can add the assimilation of certain skills by them. Moreover, all informational materials indicate that education as a sociocultural phenomenon appears in the form of a phenomenon created by society, and regulated by it. In the legal norms of the Russian Federation, the term is considered as a complex phenomenon, which includes not only the learning process, but also the education of a new generation.

That is why it is safe to say that pedagogy is a science about the features of education. It covers a wide range of methods of forming personal qualities in a person and consolidating knowledge. The very concept of the pedagogical process is very simple - it can be represented as a focused and regular interaction between the teacher and his students.

Education as a public value

For the formation of society requires certain norms, principles and requirements. In accordance with them, society is developing, undergoing constant changes in accordance with the fluctuations of the surrounding conditions - technical, natural, historical. Education as a sociocultural phenomenon and the pedagogical process is considered the most important component of socialization. It is aimed at the comprehensive development of the personality, the formation of stable moral qualities in a person along with his physiological formation. The vocation of this phenomenon is to educate the state, so to speak, an ideal citizen that meets all modern value guidelines.

Depending on how developed the education system is in the country, the level of cohesion of its society is determined. And vice versa - the particularities of the political system, the economic and political situation have a direct impact on the image of the humanistic ideal, which the corresponding schools strive to recreate.

Education Functions

Any activity is always aimed at achieving a goal. This also applies to the educational process, since it cannot exist on its own, but only if society needs it. Therefore, this phenomenon has two main functions: training and education.

In the first case, the purpose of educational activity is to help the child acquire certain knowledge, master skills and form skills, as well as determine life guidelines and future profession. Thus, the foundation is being built for the development of a full and diverse personality in the future. The learning function reveals education as a sociocultural phenomenon in the context of a process that is significant for the state and humanity in general.

Parenting serves to give the child motivation to obtain the necessary knowledge. Indeed, not every student understands the importance of the learning process for his future. Therefore, the task of the teacher is to push him to this.





The main paradigms of the educational process

To begin to consider this issue, for a start it is worth describing its essence. A paradigm is a set of specific attitudes, theoretical or practical methods adopted in the scientific community as a model for actions in a certain field. Such standards are also available in the educational process. Scientists distinguish four types of paradigms of this phenomenon:

  • Cognitive (focus on the development of thinking).
  • Personally oriented (individualization of learning).
  • Functionalistic (compliance with the moral standards of society).
  • Culturological (perception of a person as a subject of culture).

Education as a pedagogical process

It is the activity of educating a socially significant personality that defines education as a sociocultural phenomenon. The pedagogical system and the process of its application are aimed at achieving this goal. As a result of purposeful and systematic interaction of the teacher and his students, the knowledge necessary for the further development of the personality is formed. It is this contact of the teacher with his pupils that is considered within the framework of the pedagogical system.

This concept cannot be limited to any specific structural components. The pedagogical process is a mixed system in which a huge number of elements of education are placed. Moreover, no matter how independent any of these components may be, it will still be considered secondary. The pedagogical system cannot be equated to any substructure ‚Äî rather, it acts as a connecting link.

Patterns of the pedagogical process

Education as a sociocultural phenomenon and the pedagogical process is characterized by certain laws, namely:

  • Flow dynamics.
  • The interconnection of theory and practice in learning.
  • The unity between cognition and teaching.
  • Personal development in the process of training education.
  • Manageability.
  • Stimulating students for activities.

Pedagogical process and its elements

Structurality is an important feature that distinguishes education as a sociocultural phenomenon and the pedagogical process. Briefly about this, one can say as follows: this phenomenon consists of various components that are generally combined into one combined system. The structural elements of the pedagogical process include:

  • Determining the goals and main tasks of future educational activities.
  • Establishing contact between the teacher and his students.
  • Correct application of teaching methods, tools and forms.
  • Creation of such conditions in which students will be most comfortable to perceive information.




Complex of educational institutions

If the state seeks to create an ideal society, then it should use the means to achieve this goal. As such, there is a complex of diverse educational institutions. In each of them, certain training programs and educational directions are presented. In this interpretation, education as a sociocultural phenomenon and universal value acquires a certain state or, rather, organizational and legal meaning.

There are several types of educational institutions:

  • Preschool.
  • General education.
  • Special
  • Professional.

The first type of organization is represented by kindergartens, nurseries, early development schools and other similar structures. In such institutions, an introductory program for raising children is carried out, which is largely aimed at the psychological preparation of the child for further education.

General education of citizens of the state is carried out in schools, gymnasiums and lyceums. As a rule, they are divided into 3 stages: initial, main and secondary.

Special institutions (boarding schools, correctional schools) are designed to enable children with physical disabilities to become full members of society. This just clearly reveals education as a sociocultural phenomenon.

Briefly about professional educational institutions: they make up for the shortage of necessary personnel in the country by training certain specialties. These include institutions of primary (vocational schools), secondary (colleges, technical schools) and higher (universities, institutes, academies) level.





Education is an integral part of human society. This fact defines it as a sociocultural phenomenon. That this is a phenomenon generated by humanity, or an instrument for its existence, is a rather controversial issue. But, in fact, both options are correct.
