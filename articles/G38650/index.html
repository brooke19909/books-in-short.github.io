Car radio "Pioneer 88": description, specifications, settings, instructions and reviews

With Japanese equipment in the domestic market, there have always been some difficulties for Russian-speaking users. After all, it is impossible to achieve a balance between cost, functionality and quality with such equipment - one criterion will constantly not meet. In this article, the reader will get acquainted with one of such unique devices - a radio tape recorder for the Pioneer 88 car. The gadget has an affordable cost in a budget class and has unrivaled sound quality in terms of functionality. Description, characteristics, settings, instructions and reviews of the owners will become for the potential buyer a guide to the market of automotive accessories for multimedia.





Specifications

The radio tape recorder is the head unit, which means that all the declared functionality is supported at the hardware level and does not require the installation of additional control systems. The front panel has a motorized mechanism that can automatically adjust the position. The display and buttons of the radio recorder "Pioneer 88" have a built-in backlight. The device is equipped with a wireless remote control and has hardware support for connecting a wired system (for example, for a steering wheel).

The maximum output power of the car radio amplifier is 200 watts (50 watts per four channels). Built-in automatic volume control with noise detection system. In active mode, the device has high and low pass filters. Equalizer settings are also carried out automatically, at the software level.

The main function of the radio

For the car multimedia device "Pioneer 88" the characteristic of the radio is a priority, because it is the basic functionality for all radio tape recorders. The gadget has a proprietary tuner created using D4Q technology. This controller is famous for having an intelligent system and is able to independently reduce the noise level. And in difficult conditions, the well-known technology is able to prevent the appearance of interference altogether.





Flexible sensitivity adjustment and the data system for RDS radios improve the quality of the search for radio stations, allowing the user to independently determine which sound they like best. 4-digit sensitivity adjustment. On the one hand, the convenience of tuning is obvious, but there is also the flip side of the coin - the search for channels is time-consuming.

Tuner tuning recommendations

The instruction "Pioneer 88" recommends that all owners of the device use the automatic search for radio stations. Walking through the range specified by the user, the tuner will find all the channels that have a powerful signal. Radio stations with a weak signal will have to be searched manually. Many professionals claim that the quality of the radio signal is directly proportional to the antenna power for the radio, and recommend installing a pin design to improve reception quality.

The cradle of quality sound

The digital audio converter, popularly referred to as the "processor" or "DAC" in the Pioneer 88 radio is the main component that is responsible for sound quality. The Japanese decided to entrust the work of their device to the well-known microelectronics brand, Burr-Brown. It is the new generation technology called Advanced Segment DAC that provides the car radio with unsurpassed sound quality.

However, the manufacturer used the CD-receiver of its own production, which caused a stir in foreign markets during the presentation. Everyone knows that the Japanese brand Pioneer seeks to support only licensed audio data formats. The model of the radio tape recorder participating in the review is completely devoid of restrictions: CD-Audio, MP3, WMA, WAV, AAC - all popular standards, including support for ID3 tags, are supported on the hardware level by a multimedia device.

CD Management

As for the Pioneer 88 radio, the price of which is 10,000 rubles, many owners note too complicated management when working with CD-carriers. Such extensive functionality is more needed by those who use a professional speaker system installed in the car. Many owners in their reviews of the radio recommend all newcomers not to use the instructions, but to determine the operability of a CD player intuitively.





There is logic in such tips, because for most users, four skills will be enough: insert a disc, start playback, switch to the next track, remove the disc from the tray. Fans of the finer tuning of the player can use the independent study of the operating instructions for the device.

CD Guidelines

The user guide provides information on how to set up the Pioneer 88 and how to use it conveniently. However, on the pages of the manual, the reader will not find a list of standard errors made by many owners of the radio. The fact is that there are several quick ways to break the device with incorrect user actions.

  1. Removing a CD from the tray should only be done after the rotating spindle has completely stopped. That is, the user must stop playback and wait 5-6 seconds before pressing the "EJECT" button. Failure to comply with these requirements leads to the fact that over time the tray stops returning the disk after pressing the eject button.
  2. To insert a CD-ROM face down, even for sporting interest, is not worth it. Just one pothole on the road will be enough to damage the laser reader.
  3. No need to experiment with DVDs, inserting them into a device that does not understand a denser amount of data. The radio processor from such actions can go crazy (a general reset to the factory settings helps).

More features with a Japanese representative

Car radio "Pioneer 88" is completely devoid of support for USB-devices. That is, the owner will not only not find the corresponding port on the front panel of the device, but also will not be able to find the necessary wiring on the system board of the radio. Naturally, for many car owners this is a huge drawback. But there is one loophole that many owners are quick to share in their reviews. The radio is equipped with an analog audio input, which is implemented by the Mini-Jack 2.5 interface.





The manufacturer in his user manual practically did not pay attention to this feature, indicating only that the radio supports operation with iPod. In fact, having purchased an adapter for the Pioneer 88 (Mini-Jack 2.5 to 3.5) on the electronics market, the owner can play music in the car from any storage medium, including a laptop, mobile phone or tablet. It is clear that this option will not replace USB devices, but still it is more interesting than using obsolete CDs.
