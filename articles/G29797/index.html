What is a shuriken? How to make it yourself?

Shuriken is an ancient Japanese weapon. It is designed for hidden wear. In Russia, the shuriken is better known as "Asterisk", although the name is rather generalizing. Real throwing weapons can take many forms.

These are pointed or curly stars with a different number of rays, and throwing knives or blades, and sharpened coins, and even nails.

How to make paper shuriken

For children's games, not real, but paper shuriken is more suitable. If it is painted, for example, with silver (or other paint), then the weapon will look like real.



  • First, a square made of paper needs to be cut into two equal rectangles. Remember: the more accurate the work, the more beautiful the finished shuriken will be. How to make cuts smooth and parts smooth? Use sharp scissors and a ruler.
  • One of the rectangles is bent again in half, and the corners of the resulting figures are bent in different directions.
  • At right angles to the first folds, the second ones are made. You should get two triangles (rectangular and isosceles).
  • The same, but in mirror reflection, do with the second rectangle.
  • The resulting triangles overlap. The corners of the lower wind up under the upper triangles. Then the figure flips over and the action repeats. The simplest four-corner shuriken is ready.
