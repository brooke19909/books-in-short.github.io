History

According to researchers, people settled on Samui in the 6th century. AD These were the Chinese fishermen who built their village here. However, the first mention of the island in the history of the Middle Kingdom can be found only 500 years ago. These are documents proving the fact that trade flourished between China and Samui. This is also confirmed by the Celestial ships found near the island with ceramic products.

Initially, only fishermen settled in Samui. And the island was a continuous jungle, since the villages in which the local population lived were, as a rule, on the seashore. People got to each other in boats, having traveled along the coast. The development of the island was facilitated by Buddhist monks who called on the people to build earthen roads.

For a considerable period of time, the inhabitants of the island earned fishing and collecting coconuts with the help of specially trained monkeys for this. Buyers came for these goods not only from China, but also from mainland Thailand.

The almost complete isolation of the island, which had the only connection with the outside world in the form of a ferry, which in 10 hours transported people to the nearest major city of Thailand - Surat Thani, came to its end only in the second half of the 20th century. In 1967, the Governor of Samui made a proposal to the Thai government on the need for road construction. This was the turning point that changed the history of the island. Roads have been built here for 6 years. The work was greatly complicated by the relief, in which impassable jungle and hills abounded.

The construction of the 52-kilometer ring road was completed in 1973. This moment was the beginning for Samui of its history as one of the best and most popular resorts in Thailand.

The first tourists who came here in the early 70s settled in ordinary huts, standing on the numerous beaches of Samui. Here vacationers were waiting for a real paradise shelter from the bustle of the city and tedious everyday life.

Already in the early 90s, the island was swept by a tourist boom. The largest beaches of Koh Samui have become the main resorts of Thailand. Numerous hotels began to be erected here and the tourist infrastructure with entertainment facilities, restaurants and shops got its rapid development. Migrant workers rushed to the cities of the island. They came here from almost all over Thailand. Together with tourists, they increased the population of the island several dozen times.

Today, the basis of the economy of Samui is precisely the tourism business. The development of this sphere was not prevented even by the disastrous tsunami of 2014 and the flood of 2011, which left most of the sights and beaches of the island under water.

Climate

Koh Samui is located in the tropical zone. Its territory is characterized by rains, the frequency and power of which depend on the season. Precipitation is distributed very unevenly throughout the year. In this regard, one month on the island is quite dry, and the second wet. The winds in Samui are not strong, but mostly gusty, while warm.

The average air temperature is +30 degrees. No less warm are the waters of the bay washing Samui. Their temperature reaches +27 degrees. In this regard, a very good vacation on Koh Samui with children. Reviews of tourists say that children can splash around in such water for a very long time without the risk of catching a cold.

High (dry) season

Having chosen Koh Samui for his vacation, a tourist needs to decide when exactly he is best to spend time here. There are three seasons on the island - dry, hot and the rainy season. All of them differ from each other in their climatic features.

During the high season, Samui is dry. It rains on the island, but there are very few. Moreover, they most often take place at night. If it rains during the day, it ends very quickly. The high (dry) season, which begins on Koh Samui in mid-December and ends in March, delights vacationers not only with its comfortable temperature, but also with warm gusty winds, due to which small waves suitable for surfing are created on the sea.

This is the time when a lot of tourists rush to the island. It is impossible to meet such a crowd of visitors at other times of the year.

Particularly favorable in high season holidays on Koh Samui with children. Reviews of tourists say that for young travelers this period is the most comfortable. After all, air at this time only warms up to +27 degrees, and the water is just as warm (its average temperature reaches +25). Clear days and a small amount of rainfall allow vacationers with children to swim, walk in gardens and parks, visit attractions, etc.

However, due to the large influx of tourists on the island, prices increase by 10-20%. They are enhanced not only by hotels and various resort establishments, but also by airlines.
