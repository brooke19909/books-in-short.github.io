The capital of Cameroon, why I decided to visit it

Cameroon is Africa in the broadest sense of the word. As it seemed in childhood, there are sharks, and gorillas, and large crocodiles. The heat, savannahs, equatorial forests, mangroves, the traditional African lifestyle, a simple way of life and a very weak belief that in the near future, the country will face grandiose transformations.

There was indeed a myriad of shrimp in the coastal waters. From the west, Cameroon is washed by the waters of the Atlantic Ocean, and the countries bordering it are Nigeria, Chad, the Central African Republic, Gabon, the Republic of Congo and Equatorial Guinea.

The territory of Cameroon is quite impressive, in size it can compete with Spain or with the state of California. Unlike many other, even African countries, you can find yourself in completely different natural areas.

The northern part of the country is stretched towards savannahs and semi-deserts, above which the famous Sahara desert is located . In the south, on the contrary, it is very humid. The coast of the Atlantic Ocean of Cameroon is one of the wettest places on planet Earth. Immediately, in the southwestern region of the Republic, not far from the coast, is the highest point of the country (4040 m) Cameroon. The volcano on which it is located is active, it periodically reminds itself. In 2000, he erupted, leaving huge gaping craters on the peaks.

"Africa in miniature" - it is precisely about Cameroon that they say so most often. Here more than 750 species of birds are found, almost all types of animals that are supposed to live in Africa live in the savannahs: rhinos, giraffes, lions, antelopes, leopards, ostriches and others.
