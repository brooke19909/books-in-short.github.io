How to crochet a sheep (diagram and description): tack

First, we take that yarn, the color of which will be the main color in the sheep, and we knit a circle of suitable diameter from it. We use single crochet columns. For this, any scheme that allows you to connect a circle is suitable. For example, such.

The number of rows that you will get based on the results of the work will depend on the desired size of the future tack, as well as on the thickness of the yarn used. If your yarn is not thick enough, then fold it in half, or maybe three times. After all, the purpose of the tack is to protect your hands from the heat of heated dish handles. And this is impossible if the thickness of the material is insufficient for these purposes. After the last circle is created, we begin to tie it with ‚Äúfans‚Äù. A fan should consist of five columns, each of which has one double crochet. The wavy edge will imitate sheep curls. You can leave it in this form, or you can tie the finished part with contrasting threads, using columns without a crochet for this. In the process of tying, you must not forget that any tack should have a loop for hanging it. To create it, simply tie in the middle of the work a chain consisting of air loops in the amount of, for example, fifteen pieces. That's all. Our main item is ready.

We knit a muzzle for a sheep-tack

To make the lamb's muzzle look good, it is better to take yarn different from the one from which the main part is knitted. Now we knit a circle as large as the face should be. These are approximately three or four rows. As soon as the work is completed without breaking the threads, we create ears from the air loops, and carefully hide the ends of the threads on the wrong side.

This is the easiest way. But you can tie the oval ears separately, using columns without a crochet, and then sew them to the muzzle of a lamb. The hairstyle can be made in the form of a cloud from a contrasting thread, and to attach this hairstyle to the head. In knitting, use the same technique as when tying the main part.

Create the legs

Now it was the turn of the legs. They can be made very simple. Tie chains of air stitches of the required length, one chain for two legs. From both edges we attach a bead. Just put it on a chain and tie a knot. And now it remains to simply attach these legs to the bottom. Pull them into any hole and tie. Each sheep should end up with four legs. It remains only to glue the eyes.

That's all. We studied one of the options for crocheting a sheep. The scheme and description of creating a tack will certainly help even beginner needlewomen.

Knitting torso

To tie a torso for a sheep, which will also be a dress, you will need yarn of two colors. One of them is the color of the body of the sheep, or simply white, and the second is the color of the dress. The body fits in according to the same principle as the head, only of a larger size. Start knitting from the bottom, with the thread that will denote the body. Having knitted somewhere one fourth of the whole body, attach a thread of a different color and knit to the end. Stuff your torso as you knit. As soon as the work is completed, attach a thread of contrasting color at the place of transition from one color to another and tie a pair of rows. After that, knit an openwork skirt according to the scheme.
