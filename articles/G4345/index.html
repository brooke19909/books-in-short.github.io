Pay attention to the neighboring countries - Ukraine, Abkhazia, Kazakhstan. To enter there, you do not need a foreign passport, a sufficient domestic Russian. If you are a resident of Vladivostok, then it will be more convenient for you to go to the beaches of China than Crimea.

The most important advice for those who have not decided yet and are considering where to go by car to the sea: avoid large resort cities. The people of darkness, the traffic on the streets is extremely confusing, parking is paid or absent. Using your mobility, choose small villages. Often these are satellites of big cities. For example, Uchkuevka on the north side of Sevastopol or one of the countless resorts of Big Yalta, Small Lighthouse or Satera near Alushta. These are quiet, one might say, family villages, where there is not much entertainment on site, but

you are on wheels, and you can reach water parks and museums in minutes.

Some wildness of these small villages has a beneficial and most direct effect on housing prices. Locals do not live in small apartments in high-rise buildings, but in spacious houses with beautiful and well-kept gardens. There is where to put the car. In resolving the issue: "Where to go by car to the sea?" not the least role is played by the factor of purity of water and air. And the nature in such places is simply amazing. On the Mangyshlak peninsula in Kazakhstan there are simply unearthly landscapes, as well as on the Tamansky, where you can alternately swim in the Azov and Black Seas.



If you travel with children and you care about the level of service, where to go by car to the sea? We recommend Crimea, the Northern Black Sea region - towns in the Kherson, Nikolaev and Odessa regions. In Abkhazia and Kazakhstan, service - alas! - leaves much to be desired, although the rest is more budgetary than in Ukraine and especially in Russia. If you are a young romantic and the level of amenities cares you least of all, we advise you to ride savages across the Crimea. From Cape Tarkhankut to the opposite end of the peninsula, there are many beautiful places where civilization and land privatization have not yet reached.
