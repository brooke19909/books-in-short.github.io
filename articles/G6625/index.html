What requirements should a 3 star hotel meet?

Each "star" assigned to the hotel, by European standards, is a measure of the level of service provided by the hotel to its customers. And although there are some general criteria, each country in Europe assigns some additional conditions so that its hotels differ from hotels in other countries. In this article we will talk about the general criteria that a 3 star hotel should meet, and also pay a little attention to the distinctive features of hotels of this level in some individual countries.

Each 3-star hotel receives its rating due to the number of rooms and places in them and their size, as well as the comfort of the proposed conditions. Nothing in the room should prevent the client from freely placing himself and arranging things in the order he needs. Such a hotel must have a shower in every room, equipped with hot and cold water, a hairdryer and other amenities. Telephone communication works both internal and external. The guest should not have problems with telephone conversations without leaving the room.

The classification of a 3-star hotel places great demands on the equipment of the hotel. The elevator in this hotel runs smoothly. And in some of them there is a special freight elevator. The hotel infrastructure is required to have, in addition to the dining room, where all guests eat, a restaurant and cafe, where there should be an internal passage for customers and a separate entrance for staff.
