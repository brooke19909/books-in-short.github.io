Key notation

Battery capacity is measured in milliampere hours (mAh). The larger the mAh battery, the higher its technical capacity. But the phones with the highest number of mAh are not always the phones with the best battery life.

This may depend on how efficient the processor is in combination with the software, as well as how efficiently the owner uses his phone. Expensive devices are often faster and have better screens, but this will mean that the battery life is much lower than that of the middle class segment.

What to pay attention to?

Phones with great looking QHD displays run out of battery faster than phones with lower resolution displays. Therefore, you need to prioritize - if you need to increase battery life, you have to be content with mid-level gadgets. Fortunately, this means that the phone will cost much cheaper than the flagships and the latest innovations from popular manufacturers.

Many phones today, but not all, have fast charging. Often, cheaper mid-range smartphones have a long battery life, but do not charge quickly. Many users say that fast charging reduces battery life.

What do the results show?

Experts often use the Geekbench 4 battery testing feature to get the time in minutes and seconds, but this is not an indication of how long the phone will actually work.

In all tests, the screen brightness is set to the same level to ensure an equal metering level (120 cd / m2). The phone discharges the battery completely and the screen is set up so that it does not fade or rotate. Thanks to these settings, you can get more accurate test results on different models.
