What is the current currency in Cyprus and what money should I take on a trip?

Travelers going on a tourist trip to the fabulous and mysterious Cyprus, in addition to finding places to live and drawing up a cultural program, think about more pressing issues. This island has its own culture, customs, which it would be better to know about in advance. You should also clearly understand what currency in Cyprus, so that you can make purchases with minimal losses. It should be noted right away that the island is divided into 2 parts - Greek (southern) and Turkish (northern). And although tourists can move from one to another quite freely, life in one and the other is quite different.





What money is better to take with you

Since 2008, the euro has been officially introduced in Cyprus as the main currency. Therefore, going on a trip to this country, it is enough to have them with you in sufficient quantities. As in any other civilized state, here in shops, restaurants and other places of mass visits plastic cards are accepted for payment. They, in turn, can be opened in any currency, if it differs from the euro, then conversion will simply happen.

Neither Russian rubles, nor Ukrainian hryvnias, nor other money of the countries of the former USSR here are accepted even at special points and banks, so there is no point in taking them with you. American dollars and British pounds in the store and on the market are also unlikely to be taken for payment, but they will certainly be exchanged at banks, so if you have this money with you, the tourist will certainly not be lost. In the north of the island, you can pay with Turkish lira.





What currency was in Cyprus before

Before joining the European Union, there were bills and coins. The currency of Cyprus was a pound of 100 cents. At the same time, many shops (especially in the northern part of the island) willingly accepted Turkish liras and euros. Therefore, to the question: "What currency to pay in Cyprus?" there was no single correct answer. The one that was available.

Modern monetary policy of the island

Unfortunately, many tourists do not know exactly what currency is in Cyprus, and they think that pounds here are still the main bargaining chip, and therefore try to acquire them in advance in the required amount. In fact, the population, of course, had both banknotes and coins, but now they can be used only as a souvenir. In the territory occupied by the Turks (the northern part of the island), the lira is still in circulation. Therefore, having it available, you can have dinner without any problems, pay for the purchase of souvenirs or products. True, tourists usually visit the Greek part. So the correct answer to the question: “What is the currency in Cyprus?” - all the same, the euro. Banknotes here are the same as in other states of the Commonwealth. Exchange operations are best done at banks, rather than at hotels or other points. Otherwise, you can significantly lose course.
