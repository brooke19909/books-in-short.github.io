Lido di Camaiore, Italy - description, attractions, interesting facts and reviews

Italy is an amazing country with a rich culture and beautiful nature. On its coast there are many resorts that deserve the attention of tourists. In our article we want to talk about Lido di Camaiore. This small town is located between Marina di Pietrasanta and Viareggio. Despite the modest size of the town, the resort is very popular not only among local residents, but also among tourists from around the world. The gentle sun, warm sea and stunningly beautiful landscapes - all this is Lido Di Camaiore. You can rest assured that you are guaranteed an unforgettable vacation.

The perfect resort

Lido di Camaiore can rightly be called an ideal resort for the whole family. Here are the most beautiful villas, immersed in emerald greenery, landscaped areas with fountains, shady gardens and alleys on the coast. In the summer, the resort has a stunning Luna Park, where you can have fun not only for children, but also for adults.





You can walk along the coast, admire the sea, and then sit in a cafe surrounded by palm trees and flowers. During the high tourist season, the town is transformed. At this time, exhibitions, fireworks and various concerts are held on its streets and squares daily.

What is the secret to the popularity of the resort?

Lido di Camaiore, unlike other brilliant resorts in Italy, is completely devoid of pathos and brilliance. The quiet and calm town of the Tuscan Riviera is rather interesting not for lovers of glamor and social events, but for couples. It is not customary to bring expensive costumes and furs here. But relaxing with children is very comfortable. In general, the resort is popular with all lovers of a relaxing and quiet holiday.

What so attracts guests to Lido di Camaiore (Italy)? Of course, stunning beauty and peace. The whole city is buried in orchards of fruit trees. Numerous flower beds fragrant around. Tourists love walking along ancient shady ponds.



Fountains in the town square give guests the desired coolness in the hot summer days. The pride of the resort is the promenade that stretches along the coast.

The beaches of Lido di Camaiore

The resort has gained popularity among tourists precisely because of its beaches. Lido di Camaiore belongs to the beach region of Camaiore. The local coast is incredibly popular due to its golden soft sand. Well, beautiful seascapes complement the picture of the resort. It is worth noting that no other European resort can boast of so many comfortable beaches.

An interesting fact is that the ideal cleanliness of the coast is supported by a kind of cooperative of owners.

Reviews on the beaches of Lido di Camaiore

The beaches of the resort are beautiful and comfortable. All of them have excellent sand cover and a comfortable gentle entry into the sea. According to many tourists, the best place to stay with children is simply not to be found. The holiday season here begins quite early - back in May. And it continues until the end of September. Sea temperature in this period ranges from 26-28 degrees.



Tourists are attracted not only by the incredibly comfortable temperature of the water, but also by the arrangement of local beaches. Restaurants and Cafes

Reviews about Lido di Camaiore (Italy), one way or another, always come down to talking about food. Indeed, the amazing local cuisine is worthy of enthusiastic words about it. Relaxing at the resort, you should definitely visit one of the local restaurants or cafes on the waterfront. In each of them, they offer delicious seafood dishes and wonderful Tuscan wines. Numerous tourists visiting the resort are interested not only in the beaches of Lido di Camaiore, but also in the sights. And there are a lot of them in the city. If you are interested in excursions, then you should know that there are places interesting from the point of view of archeology, both in the city itself and beyond. Not far from the city of Lucca overlooks the picturesque spur of Camaiore, which is considered to be the main natural monument of the region. Tuscan archipelago

The Tuscan archipelago has seven islands: Pianosa, Montecristo, Gorgon, Giannutri, Giglio and Elba. The largest of them is the famous island of Elba. It is he who can rightfully be called the treasury of all kinds of attractions.

But the rest of the islands are also worthy of the attention of vacationers. So, for example, on Giglio, in addition to stunning landscapes, you can admire the picturesque streets of local villages. Fans of diving will certainly appreciate the beauty of the Mediterranean underwater world, especially those places where archaeological searches are still being carried out.



Montecristo Island has been a nature reserve since 1971. This is the wildest and most unpopulated area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe archipelago. There are no buildings on the island, except for a villa of the era of the XIX century, which once belonged to a certain Englishman.

Elba

The island is quite small. Its length is only 27 kilometers and a width of 18 km. Elba is famous for its stunning beaches, of which there are more than 70. All of them are framed by stunning cliffs and diverse Mediterranean vegetation. No less impressive and underwater world of the island. Along the coast stretch "underwater gardens", which are always of interest to divers.

The largest beach is called Barabarka. The water in the local lagoon is crystal clear. It offers beautiful views of Stella Bay. Tourists should definitely visit the beach of Le Gyaye, on which pebbles are pink.



On the island there is a cave in the rock, which is also worth a look. Inside it you can find a pebble beach and a fresh spring.

The female representatives on the Elbe are attracted by the thermal springs of San Giovanni, which are known for a wide selection of cosmetic, therapeutic and preventive procedures. They treat cellulite, acne, psoriasis, arthrosis using sea mud and medicinal algae, rich in iodine and organic sulfur. In summer, the Algotherapy Center, popular in Europe, operates, practicing sunbathing with healing algae.
