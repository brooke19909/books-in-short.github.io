What is the difference between velvet and velor, their scope, photo

Velvet and velor materials are very similar in appearance and purpose. What is the difference between these fabrics, what is the specificity of their use in practice? You will learn about all this from this article. After all, you often have to see these materials as upholstery on sofas, corners, soft chairs, poufs. They also sew fashionable home clothes: bathrobes, tracksuits. Both that and other fabric is dense, durable and gained the title of "royal".



Meaning of Terms

The word "velvet" appeared in the Russian language for a very long time. It was also mentioned in Domostroy. It called velvet silk fabric with a thick pile on the front side. The original source of this term was the Turkic barrakan. It had the meaning of "fleece fabric." The modern definition of velvet is fabric made from cut pile using the method of weaving threads.

What is the difference between velvet and velor, are they synonymous? The word "velor" was formed from the French velours, meaning "hairy, hairy." A feature of velor is that for its manufacture woolen yarn is used. The material, the structure of which includes a whole pile - is velor.

Pros and Cons of Velvet

Being an expensive fabric of natural origin, velvet has a large number of advantages:

  • does not cause allergies and skin irritations;
  • does not have the ability to stretch;
  • holds heat well;
  • not covered with dust;
  • perfectly drapes, shimmers and shines;
  • has a long durability;
  • has a noble appearance.

Among the shortcomings of the material, it is worth highlighting the following:

  • not suitable for clothes for overweight women, because it creates the effect of volume;
  • Needs careful care;
  • has a high cost.

Velvet is made from cotton, silk and wool threads. He has a high pile, which is also called plush. Varieties of velvet are velvet and plis.



Advantages and disadvantages of velor

Many needlewomen wonder how velor differs from velvet. Photo articles demonstrate the nobility of these fabrics. Many people prefer velor products because it is a pure-wool material. It is very soft and pleasant to the touch. It is necessary to highlight the following advantages of this fabric:

  • keeps heat well;
  • refers to "breathing" tissues;
  • It does not have the ability to stretch and wrinkle;
  • gives a pleasant sensation when touched;
  • has an affordable cost.

There are very few disadvantages of velor:

  • easy to get dirty, difficult to remove stains;
  • Needs constant care and attention.

Velor is made from wool or cotton by spinning. Velor pile can be smooth, embossed or in the form of drawings. Velor is of two types - looped and split. Looped is considered a knitted fabric and is used for sewing clothes. Split is made of wool and has a longer pile.

Scope and Care

So you found out how velvet differs from velor. Now it's time to learn about their application. Velor is used for sewing children's things, home clothes, tracksuits, pajamas. Evening dresses are sewn from patterned velor. It is suitable for modeling berets, hats, coats. This material sometimes serves as upholstery for upholstered furniture. Special car velor tighten the seats. Jacquard fabric is suitable for bedding. Sometimes it is used as a lining material.



Lovers of exquisite outfits sew evening dresses and puffy skirts from velvet. It is also great for suits, wide trousers, coats, and bodysuits. Velvet is often decorated with shoes, boots, sandals. This is an excellent material for tailoring accessories: bags, clutches, headbands. Velvet serves as upholstery for furniture, for the manufacture of chic curtains.

Velvet is a more moody fabric. For its worthy appearance care is necessary. Velvet is regularly cleaned to remove dust and lint. Velor is not so whimsical. Sometimes it‚Äôs enough to remove dirt from the velor surface with a clean, damp cloth.

Velor and velvet items must not be washed with bleaches or other chemicals. Such clothes should not be twisted and squeezed strongly. The drying process can be accelerated with a hairdryer, but not with an iron. Make sure that products from velvet and velor are not dried in direct sunlight, it is better to choose a shadow. Ironing such things is also undesirable. If you really need to straighten wrinkled clothes, walk on it with a slightly hot iron, turning inside out. It is best to align the creases with steam. Dust on velvet and velor furniture is collected with a vacuum cleaner.

Small differences between these two fabrics do not interfere with calling them related. Velvet and velor items - a sign of good taste of their owners!
