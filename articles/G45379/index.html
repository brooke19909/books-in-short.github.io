City Minden, Germany: description, attractions with photos

For the first time this wonderful German town was mentioned in the so-called imperial chronicles in 798. In them he was listed as the seat of the imperial assembly of Charlemagne. Around 800, the emperor founded a bishopric in this city. In 977, the settlement was granted customs law, the right to a coin charter and free trade. Today this city (population 84 thousand people) is the center of the East Westphalian region, as well as the historical and political center of the land of Minden.

The article provides information about the small town of Germany - Minden: photos, description, attractions.

Geographical position

At this point, the Weser River erupts between the low ridges of Vienna and Weser, and then leaves this mountainous country, after which its waters enter the territory of the North German Lowland. Minden is located just north of that breakthrough.

The city of Germany, Minden, extends on both sides of the flat part of the Weser, but its southern regions (Ducen, Heverstadt and Haddenhausen) occupy the northern slopes of the Vienne ridge. The city center is located about five kilometers north of the mountains, on the border of the Middle Weser Valley and the Lubbeck Loess Plain. The section of these two natural complexes is clearly expressed in the relief of the city. He divides it into lower and upper Minden.

The city is located 40 kilometers from Bielefeld, 55 kilometers from Hanover, 60 km from Osnabruck and 100 km from Bremen. The highest point in Minden (Germany) is in the Haddenhausen area (180.5 meters above sea level), and the lowest is in the Leteln region (40.3 meters). The 42.2 m mark is indicated on the town hall. Minden has often been and is still being flooded due to its location on the Weser floodplain.

History of the city

Minden (Germany) was founded around 800. Before the Peace of Westphalia, it was, as noted above, the center of the Catholic Diocese of Minden. Subsequently, Minden became ruled by the Principality of Brandenburg-Prussia and expanded to a fortified city. At the same time, it turned into the administrative center of the Principality of Minden, and in 1719 received the status of the center of the Minden-Ravensberg region. Minden became the center of the administrative district of the same name in 1816.

Currently, the city is famous not only for historical monuments, but also for its unique bridge, thrown at the intersection of the canal of the Central German River Weser. There are also numerous buildings in the Minden era of the Weser Renaissance, as well as a cathedral, which is an important architectural landmark.
