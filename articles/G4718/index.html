Village History

The coastal edges of the sacred lake at the source of the Angara began to be inhabited more than three hundred years ago. Initially, it was the winter houses of the Shamshurins and Natyaganovs, then a postal station, and in 1726 a water crossing across the lake was established here. The first mention of the settlement is found in notes of travelers of the 18th century. Numerous larches growing on the nearby Larch cape gave the name to the settlement that originated here - Listvyanka. Lake Baikal from the moment the village emerged becomes navigable, which was also greatly facilitated by the merchants Sibiryakov, Shigaev, Shishelov, Dudorovsky, Potapov and others.

Gate to Baikal

Listvyanka quickly grew, new marinas, warehouses, offices, hotels appeared. She became the main marina on the great lake. It is often called the gateway to Lake Baikal.

Sights of Listvyanka

Often, sights are surrounded by mysterious traditions and myths. This can be said about the St. Nicholas Church located in the valley of the Cross of the Cross, which the people nicknamed the "Traveling Church." Everyone knows the history of its creation in the village of Listvyanka. Baikal is famous for such monuments of nature and history. The church, according to legend, was erected with the money of the Siberian merchant Xenophon Serebryakov. At the beginning of the XIX century, he happened to suffer a disaster while sailing across the lake. He desperately prayed to St. Nicholas, the patron saint of sailors, for salvation, promising that if he could survive the storm, in gratitude he would build a temple on the shore of Lake Baikal. And a miracle happened! Serebryakov set up a chapel at the source of the Angara, and after the death of the merchant, his wife built a church in the village of Nikola. In 1848, the church was moved to Listvyanka. In 1956, the temple was returned to Krestovaya Pad due to the construction of the Irkutsk Hydroelectric Power Station.





The legend of old Baikal and his meek daughter Angara is one of the most popular legends that can be heard in Listvyanka. Its sights, must-see, include the so-called Shaman-stone. This is the same piece of rock that was thrown in a fit of rage by Baikal after his beloved daughter Angara. Once, dancing between the mountains, she saw the mighty handsome Yenisei and fled from her father to her lover. The stone shaman is still considered sacred, and shamanism adherents claimed that the spirits of the lake inhabited the rock.

When sightseeing, you should not ignore the Baikal Museum, which holds unique expositions representing the flora and fauna of the lake, as well as the private art gallery of Vladimir Plamenevsky, which is also a creative summer residence for artists working here in the summer. In Listvyanka, a zoo and an aquarium with seals are also interesting.

The observation deck, located on Mount Cherskiy Kamen, offers beautiful views of the lake and the village. A cable car leads to the top. In winter, lovers of snowboarding and skiing gather on the mountain.

Pribaikalsky National Park

Listvyanka village on Lake Baikal is located in the territory of the Baikal Park. It covers a significant part of the western shore of the lake, including the island of Olkhon, and is one of the five largest national reserves of the country. In Listvyanka, the visitor information center of the park is concentrated. Its animal and plant worlds are extremely rich and represented by rare species, as well as endemic.

On the territory of the park there are more than 700 ethnographic monuments belonging both to the Paleolithic era and to the present. 52 objects are given the status of natural monuments. The most famous are the cave paintings of the Sagan-Zaba cliff.

A special attraction of the park is the Circum-Baikal Railway, which surpasses all other routes in the world in terms of saturation with engineering structures. Not only its architectural sights are interesting, but also its natural objects - amazing cliffs with outcrops of rare rocks and capes with bird nests. In order to see them, you just need to go on a trip around Circum-Baikal on a tourist train.

Sandy Bay

Peschanaya Bay, striking with its unusual landscapes, is considered to be a peculiar landmark of the park. It is here that you can see a natural phenomenon created by the power of the wind. These are the famous stilted trees standing on their roots at a height of 3 meters above the surface of the earth. Their exposure occurs as a result of blowing sandy soils by the winds. However, it still feels like the trees are resting and will soon continue their long journey again.

Unusual picturesqueness to the amazing natural ensemble of Peschanaya Bay is given by the so-called remnant rocks Hat, Buddha and others, rising above the taiga cover of the Primorsky Range.
