Zoo (Yaroslavl) - a unique object for the protection of wildlife

The tradition of keeping wild animals at home comes from ancient times, and the pharaohs of Egypt and the emperors of China sought to start their collections. However, their goal, like many modern institutions, was the desire to satisfy curiosity in learning new information, and simply the need to have your favorite pets.

Benefits

The zoo (Yaroslavl) differs from other Russian organizations in its conceptual component. The fundamental factor in its construction was the awareness of the need to create animal conditions similar to the natural environment. Unlike cell-type organizations, the landscape version of the institution allows all pets to feel comfortable and meets their needs as much as possible.





Zoo (Yaroslavl) provided the best conditions not only for living animals, but also for their reproduction. Missions

In addition to the main function of preserving the gene pool of rare and endangered animals, employees of the Yaroslavl Zoo conduct enormous educational work aimed at developing spiritual values, aesthetic perception of the world, and increasing the level of environmental literacy. The research carried out here contributed to the creation of a modern educational base for students of Russian universities. In the near future it is planned to open zoological and ecological circles, because schoolchildren and students like feeding and caring for animals, studying their biological characteristics and psychological features.





Yaroslavl Zoo makes a significant contribution to nature conservation. Programs are being introduced in captivity for black stork, osprey, snake-eater, peregrine falcon, golden eagle and other rare animals, which will allow the restoration of natural populations in the future. The zoo (Yaroslavl) is ready to become a home for European mink, muskrat, lynx, gray crane, boreal owl and other endangered representatives of the fauna.
