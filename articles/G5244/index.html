Atlantis Hotel, Yalta: reviews of tourists

If you dream of a trip to the Crimea, pay attention to the Atlantis Hotel. Yalta is one of the most popular resorts of the peninsula, where you will find many attractions, vibrant entertainment and quality service.

Convenient location characterized by the hotel "Atlantis." Yalta, Crimea, Komunarov street, 7A - his address. From here to the sea will have to go about 10 minutes. Simferopol Airport is just over 100 km away. And if you want to walk around the resort Alushta, then you have to overcome 30 km.

Resting here, be sure to visit the following attractions:

  • seaside park (1 km);
  • Catholic church (1 km);
  • Lesya Ukrainka Museum (1 km);
  • Chekhov Museum (2 km);
  • Livadia Palace (2 km);
  • Yalta Zoo (3 km);
  • Swallow's Nest (6 km);
  • Nikitsky Botanical Garden (7 km);
  • cable car (9 km);
  • Vorontsov Palace (11 km).

Hotel Policies

Certain rules of residence apply in the resort complex "Atlantis" (hotel, Yalta, 4 stars). Tourists should pay attention to the following points:

  • for registration at the hotel, the guest must present a valid identification card, as well as the birth certificate of minor children, if any arrive with him;
  • Children under the age of 3 years stay in the room free of charge (if possible they are provided with special cots);
  • one guest can check into a room of any capacity subject to full payment; upon check-in, the guest is required to make an advance payment for the entire expected period of stay at the hotel;
  • in case of early booking, the hotel administration reserves the right to demand prepayment;
  • check-in of guests starts after 14:00, and eviction is carried out before noon;
  • late check-out is subject to an additional charge of 50% of the room price, and after that - 100%;
  • early check-in will be charged at 50% of the total cost of living in the room;
  • if the guest leaves the hotel before the due date, the administration may deduct a fine in the amount of the cost of a daily stay;
  • the removal of food from the cafe is prohibited (the exception is in cases when, due to health reasons, the guest is delivered food to the room);
  • non-registered guests can stay in the rooms until 23:00.

Negative reviews

Reviews about the Atlantis hotel in Yalta contain a sufficient number of negative comments. Among them, it is worth noting the following:

  • there are no partitions between balconies, which causes some discomfort;
  • no towel dryers and bathing suits, no clothesline;
  • the refrigerators in the rooms are too noisy;
  • the rooms do not have kettles and sets for making hot drinks;
  • there are no such popular beer snacks in the restaurant menu as shrimps, chips, crayfish and others;
  • the most expensive rooms with sea views also face a noisy road, which is why it is impossible to stay in the room with an open balcony;
  • to get to the sea about a quarter of an hour;
  • an inclined road leads to the embankment, returning along which is quite problematic;
  • as an extra bed, an uncomfortable folding sofa is used, which is located directly under the air conditioning;
  • breakfasts are repeated day by day;
  • Inflated prices for complex meals;
  • after 18:00, when the water park closes, the hotel becomes too quiet and boring.

General impression

Most guests agree that the institution fully justifies its four-star status. It manifests itself in a beautiful designer interior, and in high-quality service, and in delicious food. The rooms are spacious, you can comfortably move around them without tripping over furniture. In addition, one cannot but rejoice at the presence of its own water park, which guests can use without any surcharges.

One of the most significant drawbacks is the location. Of course, for tourists who want to get acquainted with the sights of Yalta, it is quite profitable. But those who came to Crimea for sea vacations complain of an inconvenient inclined road, on which it is rather difficult to return. In addition, the hotel does not have its own beach. But guests can use the private territory of a neighboring sanatorium, which eliminates the need to visit the crowded public coast.
