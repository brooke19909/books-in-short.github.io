China cheap mobile phones: review, specifications and owner reviews

Every year, smartphones are becoming more powerful and high-tech. The amount of RAM is increasing, the capabilities of the processor and display are growing, and the physical amount of data on the disk is expanding. All these changes occur evenly, so we do not notice them. However, it is enough to conduct an elementary comparison to understand: over the past few years, smartphones have become "smarter" by dozens of times. And what used to seem like science fiction turned out to be a reality today.



Along with the growth of opportunities, the availability of smartphones also increases, and their price decreases. Today, if you get cheap mobile phones, you will notice that they can solve much more problems than they did in previous years. And, accordingly, the capabilities of the user with such gadgets also increase significantly.

Since the Chinese industry is currently the undisputed leader among low-cost mobile devices, we will devote this article to describing Chinese cheap mobile phones, as well as some models of manufacturers from other countries.

Price quality

First of all, it should be said that inexpensive devices can be such for two reasons. The first is the low build quality, as well as materials and modules used in the construction of the smartphone. The second is the cropped functionality of the model.

If you want your phone to be capable of a lot, but do not want to overpay, you should pay attention to cheap mobile phones from Chinese developers.

This, for example, brand Lenovo, Zopo, Elephone, ZTE, as well as many less popular (or nameless) companies. They differ in that they have quite serious technical specifications: a powerful 8-core processor, a 16 megapixel camera, and so on.



Most of these devices, of course, have "fake" data for the reason that even the flagships do not equip such a "stuffing". This is difficult because of the difficult layout of such devices, and because of the inability to optimize the smartphone properly.

Chinese cheap mobile phones are of poor quality, which is why it would be foolish to expect stability from them. Some models released by the manufacturers listed above are unstable, so we would not recommend buying such a device.

Functional

The second indicator that could be sacrificed if you are interested in cheap mobile phones is a set of functions. A smartphone, simply put, will be "dumber", will not be able to perform a number of additional functions, but at the same time its work will be more stable.

These include keyboard devices that are not equipped with a touch screen. An example is Fly phones. They have a low cost, somewhat truncated functionality, but show excellent performance in terms of reliability. Old but cheap Nokia mobile phones demonstrate the same thing.
