All About Top Award

The main award of the USSR - the star "Hero of the Soviet Union" - appeared in 1939. At first, it was an additional insignia for those who received the highest degree of distinction. Then it was called in a different way: "Golden Star." It is made of gold, sample 950, and on its reverse side is written ‚ÄúHero of the USSR‚Äù.

A gold medal was given for special merits and for accomplished feats. Heroes were called those who shot down planes (at least 15 pieces), saved people. Air shooters-bombers could get the "Golden Star" for 8 enemy aircraft shot down in the air.





The youngest Hero of the Soviet Union is partisan Valentin Kotik. He was 14 years old at the time, but he was a brave pioneer. In 1943, Kotik was able to kill the officer and raise the alarm. Thanks to him, enemies were discovered and defeated.

First Hero of the Soviet Union

Anatoly Lyapidevsky - the famous Soviet pilot. He was a major general in aviation. Today, almost no one remembers about him, but in vain. After all, he was the first Hero of the Soviet Union. Anatoly Lyapidevsky received the Golden Star medal - ‚ÄúHero of the Soviet Union‚Äù - had 3 Orders of Lenin and many other awards. He received a star in April 1934 for saving the Chelyuskin polar explorers. He looked for them, having made 29 flights in bad weather (there was a terrible snowstorm ). In March, he still found them, put the plane on a thin ice floe and saved 12 people, including women and two children, and then he participated in the Great Patriotic War, where he received the rest of his awards.

Many believe that the first Hero of the Soviet Union passed away too corny. Such a difficult and thorny path went and survived. And then he was at the funeral of a colleague, where he caught a cold. They could not cure him, and on April 29, 1983 he died.

In honor of A. Lyapidevsky, the USSR postage stamp was issued in 1935. In Russia and Ukraine, many streets are named after his last name. A memorial plaque was installed at the school where the first Hero of the Soviet Union studied , and in 1990, a monument was erected in his honor in the village of Belaya Glina.





Heroes of the Soviet Union: women

There were few of them, only 95 people who were awarded this title. Some women - Heroes of the Soviet Union were even able to get the title twice. Some were awarded posthumously, others live today. Let's remember who had the Golden Star Award of the Hero of the Soviet Union.

The first woman to receive the high title of Hero of the USSR is Zoya Kosmodemyanskaya. She was awarded a posthumous medal. Zoe managed to burn the Germans' connection, so that they could not interact with their units. The next time, Zoe also tried to arrange arson, but she did not succeed. She was caught and brutally tortured. However, Zoe did not even say her name. She turned out to be a real partisan. When she was led to the gallows, all battered, in blood, she walked with her head held high. When she was being prepared for hanging, she managed to shout that the Germans would not win the Soviet Union, and her comrades would avenge their fighting girlfriend. And so it happened. And after her received high rank and other heroic women.





Maria Baida - worked as a sanitary instructor in the second battalion. It was a 514 rifle regiment.

Gnilitskaya Nina - was a scout in the 383 rifle division.

Kovshova Natalia - was a very good sniper in the 528 rifle regiment (Red Army, awarded posthumously).

Tatyana Kostyrina - junior sergeant, an excellent sniper of 691 rifle regiment.

Elena Stempkovskaya - junior sergeant, awarded posthumously. She was a radio operator in the 216 rifle regiment.

Maria Semyonovna Polivanova - a Red Army soldier, was a sniper in the 528 rifle regiment.

Svetlana Savitskaya - she was awarded twice. This is the first female astronaut who went into outer space. Svetlana Savitskaya - major in aviation. In 1993, she retired.

All these women are Heroes of the Soviet Union who are worthy of respect. After all, they have passed a very difficult and glorious path.

What privileges were given to citizens who received an honorary title?

Today, there are certain privileges for citizens who have this title. Benefits for Heroes of the Soviet Union that were during the USSR:

1. They are exempt from various types of taxes, fees and other deductions to the budget.

2. In medical institutions, Heroes of the USSR have the right to be treated free of charge.

3. Free travel on all types of city and suburban transport (taxi is not included).

4. Their state should provide free medicines for home delivery (if the doctor has made the necessary conclusion).

5. Free treatment and prosthetics (only in state dentistry).

6. Every year they should be given a free ticket to a sanatorium or dispensary.





7. Heroes are entitled to benefits for utility bills and housing.

8. They have the right to receive telephone communications without a queue.

9. Children of heroes have the right to provide relevant documents to the funeral service in order to bury their parent at the expense of the state.

10. If the Hero has died, and his child is studying full-time, then the state is obliged to pay the child a cash reserve.
