Our hero today is chess player Sergey Karjakin. Biography and features of his activities will be discussed in detail below. This is one of the most titled chess players of our time. At the age of 12, he became the youngest grandmaster in world history. To date, many achievements have been added to this. Among them are the winner of the World Cup and the Olympic champion.

Biography

We have already briefly described how remarkable chess player Sergey Karjakin is. His biography in all details will be considered below. Our hero was born on January 12, 1990 in Simferopol.





The mother of the future grandmaster claims that he became interested in chess at the age of five. However, at that time, parents did not attach any serious significance to this. However, the family helped the child to the best of his ability, and our hero himself showed enviable tenacity. Often he played one for two participants. As a result, he lost and was upset because of this.

His first successes were victories in the championships among the children of Ukraine, as well as Europe. He was noticed, after which he was invited to train at the Kramatorsk chess club. There he spends two years, it is during this period that he receives the title of Grandmaster and falls on the pages of the Guinness Book of Records.

In 2009, chess player Sergey Karjakin was already known in different parts of the world. At this time, the grandmaster decides to move to Russia. Chess Achievements

By the age of 26, our hero has won many titles. He became the winner of the 36th Chess Olympiad in the national team of Ukraine.
