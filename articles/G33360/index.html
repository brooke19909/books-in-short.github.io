Another name for rocket, military

If a professional military, classified rocket launcher was asked about the name of the spacecraft Yuri Gagarin for real, he would answer (observing the privacy regime, and only to those who had the "first clearance"), which is correct - R-7. Because it was this ballistic missile that was the carrier that launched the famous Soviet pilot into orbit. But just asking such questions was fraught. Tricky questions to Gagarin and his witty answers

On April 16, 1961, the world's first astronaut came to a press conference at which correspondents of all news agencies could ask him any, even the most tricky questions. At that time, they knew only what the name of the spacecraft Yuri Gagarin was called, the weight of its payload (5 tons) and a few other parameters announced earlier by academician Keldysh. The hero smiled charmingly, willingly and witty answered all the questions of the interviewers, but did not provide new technical information. Until 1968, when the Vostok lander was an exhibit of the Exhibition of Economic Achievements, even the shape of the inhabited compartment remained a secret, not to mention sophisticated devices that ensure vital activity and landing of the capsule. There were reasons for such a veil of mystery. The Americans literally stepped on their heels, but they were always two or three months late, which greatly upset President Kennedy and other important people in the White House, and not only that. Even insignificant at first glance details could lead overseas designers to the right direction, and priority would be lost. Experts from the United States made assumptions about the layout of the "East", but, as time has shown, they were all incorrect.
