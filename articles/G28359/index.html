Manchester city. From the foundation to the present day

The city of Manchester (England) belongs to the largest trade, financial, industrial and transport center of Lancashire (52 cities, 2.5 million people).



Of course, the port function, the presence of an airport of international importance (fourth in the world in terms of congestion) and a railway network, launched back in 1830, are of great importance.

The history of the city began in the X century, when capital buildings began to appear in the places of the Celtic settlement. The industrial revolution in the XVIII century gave impetus to the more rapid development of the settlement. The city of Manchester quickly became the center of the cotton industry, equal in importance and importance to London, Liverpool, Birmingham.



Today we can already talk about the centuries-old history of this pearl of England and the world. Many architectural monuments are open for visitors to the city , including masterpieces such as the cathedral (1422-1520), the Athenaeum (classicism, 1837-1839), the Exchange building (1806-1809), the neo-Gothic Town Hall and, of course, the Art Gallery with its a unique collection of the period of the XVII-XIX centuries (all galleries, museums and exhibitions more than fifty).

The other side of Manchester is represented by ultramodern areas, vibrant shopping centers, skyscrapers and business lounges, many shops, youth clubs and restaurants (Exchange Square, North Quarter). Among the sports facilities that attract particular attention are Old Trafford (the legendary stadium of the famous Manchester United club), a cricket club and a velodrome.



New sights appeared on the map: Trinity Bridge, thrown over the waters of the River Erwell and leading directly to the Salford quarter.

Those who prefer to combine business with pleasure will enjoy visiting the Urbis (Cultural Center), Manchester University and the Old Library.

The educational network covers about two hundred institutions of various formats and directions (classical schools, specialized colleges, universities, etc.), and the history of some of them dates back several centuries. City University, for example, is the largest and most popular in the United Kingdom - 37 thousand students study here.

Music lovers (both modern and classical), who appreciate excellent performance and clear, filling sound, should visit at least one of the theaters: Apollo, Opera, Palastheater, Contact.
