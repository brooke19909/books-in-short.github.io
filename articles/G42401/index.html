EMA indicator: description, how to use?

Indicators on the Forex exchange serve to make life easier for traders. The most famous of them is the EMA indicator. It allows you to predict the trend and smooths quotation data. In conditions of increased volatility, this is important.

EMA is the most respected indicator.

The most beloved and popular tool among all investors is moving averages. At the same time, it does not matter whether the capital is large or small, this indicator will only bring benefits on any exchange asset and timeframe.

The EMA indicator is part of many trading strategies and makes it possible to filter out false signals in many cases. He has a keen reaction to market fluctuations and is therefore used by most traders in their techniques.

The last price of the product gives the most accurate reflection of the position of players on the Forex, which formed the basis for calculating the formula of the indicator rsi ema signals. The final value of an asset is more important than its other values, since the previous ones are not so significant.

EMA tool calculation

In order to calculate the necessary point on the timeframe, you should add part of the real closing price to the previous price. In practice, it looks like this:

EMA (t) = EMA (t-1) - EMA (t-1)) + 2 * (P (t), where:

  • EMA (t) - exponential for a particular cycle;
  • P (t) - price at which the previous Japanese candlestick closed;
  • EMA (t-1) - the size of the previous segment to be measured.

Many traders do not study the rules for calculating the indicator, but only remember when it is worth using EMA, and when not. Ways to use the EMA indicator

To understand how to use the EMA indicator, you should understand the principle of its operation.

Breaking through the EMA price is the most popular indicator application. So, a buy signal is when the price crosses the EMA from below, and when it crosses above, when it sells. This principle is explained by the fact that the price of the pair broke its average, and, accordingly, a new trend appeared on the market.

To determine the moment of entry into the market, most traders use the fast and slow moving periods of 21 and 100. In this case, you need to focus on the intersection of several EMA indicators with each other.

Moving Average EMA with a period of 50

  • The angle of inclination suggests a trend.
  • In vertical movement, indicates the presence of a side trend.

Is resistance / support:

  • After the EMA breakthrough, the price most often comes back again and pushes off again, either from the resistance level in case of a bearish trend or from the support level in case of a bullish one.
  • When using an EMA with a long period, these lines can be used on the charts as resistance and support levels (just by themselves).

With this approach, it is very simple to use the indicator:

  1. If, with an uptrend, the price value has dropped and reached the support level, then you can enter the purchase.
  2. If during a downtrend the price level has risen and touched the resistance level, then you can enter the sale.
  3. Conducts a cross analysis of only fast or slow lines.
  4. The intersection with the asset makes it clear that there is a change in market position.
  5. The intersection of slow and fast movings is a signal of sale or purchase.
  6. Helps analysis when crossing lines that differ by their construction method. Such as EMA + WMA + SMA.
  7. Used with technical analysis indicators inside models.

The difference between EMA and other MA

Moving simple is like a trip in which you have to either go in a straight line, then climb uphill, then dive to the bottom of the sea, or fly by plane from one continent to another. And only the average time spent on the whole journey is taken into account.

Moving weighted is more like moving along stairs. More important is the place where the price is located or the step where you need to go up. The steps that have already been passed are no different from those that lie ahead.

The EMA indicator can be compared with climbing uphill. Sometimes the path is direct and simple, sometimes it turns into a serpentine, sometimes gentle, sometimes steep, but only the place where the price is located now matters. The distance traveled is completely irrelevant.
