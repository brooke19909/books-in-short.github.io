Types of policies

Most often, a vehicle insurance contract is concluded immediately after the purchase of a car and is renewed annually. An exception may be situations when the age of the purchased car exceeds 10-12 years: in such cases, some companies may refuse to service at all. Today, two types of policies are most popular:

  • OSAGO - involves covering losses to the owner of someone else's car that suffered from your negligence;

One important nuance: in the event of an insured event, it is impossible to receive payments under two policies at once, so if you have both compulsory motor third-party liability insurance and hull insurance, then in case of an accident you will have to choose where exactly you will demand compensation for the losses incurred.

When acquiring a particular auto insurance policy, the vehicle owner first of all searches for the most favorable conditions for himself, and the price of the service in this case plays an important role. What to do if you are refused

Based on Russian legislation, you are not at all obligated to pay for additional options. Therefore, if, for example, Rosgosstrakh refuses to insure a car without special stages, and there is no time or desire to change the company servicing the policy, you can try to influence the insurers by law. As a rule, people making out insurance do not pay attention to overpayments, but if this is a matter of principle for you and a simple conversation with company employees does not bring any results, contact the court or the antimonopoly committee.

In order to accurately win the case, you need to prove that managers force you to pay for an additional service that you do not need. For this, a written refusal of the company is suitable, in which the impossibility of concluding an agreement without life insurance is indicated as a reason. If you refuse to issue such paper, then the fact of refusal can be recorded on the phone or voice recorder - this can help prove your case. Most likely, you will win the court, and all the overpaid amounts will be returned to you, but you will have to pat your nerves pretty well.



In addition, there is a possibility that offended insurers will pass on information about you as a negligent client to their partners, and it will become problematic to issue a policy in the future. If this happens to you, immediately file a complaint with the antimonopoly office, the prosecutor's office and the court. Beat all the bells, the truth is on your side, because any specialized company is obliged to insure the car without additional insurance, and there is no law that forces you to pay for what you do not need.

CASCO

Another fairly popular type of policy is CASCO. In this case, it is possible to insure a car in addition to compulsory motor third-party liability insurance, although this is not mandatory. In the list of risks under this agreement, you can include almost anything:

  • hijacking;
  • damage in the event of an accident;
  • broken glass from a pebble flying out from under the wheels of the car in front;
  • cut wheels;
  • the fall of any foreign objects, such as an icicle or brick;
  • dents or scratches of unknown origin;
  • any other risks.

In this case, it does not matter who is to blame for the occurrence of the insured event, the payment will still cover losses. It all depends on the cost of the policy - the more all kinds of risks are included in it, the more expensive it is. So, as you know, CASCO is not cheap, often the cost of a car insurance is 10-15% of the price of the vehicle itself. However, despite the high cost, the CASCO policy is very popular, especially among novice motorists.

In this case, it is rather difficult to answer the question ‚Äúhow to insure a car without additional insurance‚Äù. Due to the fact that CASCO is exclusively voluntary, insurance companies try to set as many additional requirements as possible. Unfortunately, it is quite difficult to deal with such phenomena - it is easier to change the insurance company.
