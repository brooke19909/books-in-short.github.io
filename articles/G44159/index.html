Mixing console: overview, features, purpose, selection

A mixing console is an electronic device for working with audio signals: summing, controlling, processing, correcting and converting several audio sources into one. More modern models are equipped with equalizers, filters and effect processors. They surprise with their versatility and compactness, the ability to control through a computer. At the same time, it is possible to save all the settings for the compositions, which undoubtedly makes the remote control an indispensable tool for sound engineers. The remote control is the main component of concert sound, removing noise and tuning incoming signals.

Application

Depending on the areas of use, mixing consoles are divided into several main types:

  • Studio
  • concert
  • broadcasting;
  • DJ

Of course, this classification is very conditional, since there are varieties of such devices used exclusively for musical accompaniment and vocals (for example, for karaoke parties) or simple and inexpensive remote control switches for home use.

Varieties and types

There are digital mixing consoles and analog. Each of these species has both supporters and opponents, since both options have obvious advantages and disadvantages.

For example, analog signal processing is considered more preferable by professionals, since in digital models signal processing is carried out in two ways - to digital and vice versa, and this definitely affects the quality of the processed sound.

Also mixing consoles come with built-in and separate power amplifiers. A remote control without a power amplifier has such significant disadvantages as cumbersomeness and the need to pull cables to the speaker system, which is not always convenient. The advantage of remotes with built-in amplifiers in their mobility and operational work.

Choose

Before you connect a mixing console, you must also know that they differ in the number of outputs and inputs. Studio and concert mixing consoles for professionals usually have more than 6 Aux buses, at least 32 inputs, at the inputs there is a powerful equalizer and 4 or more subgroups. In addition, they feature long-stroke and high-precision faders. And compact mixers have more meager equalizers, a small number of channels and often lack faders.

According to the type of power, the mixing consoles are divided into active and passive. The former are equipped with built-in preamps, which makes it possible to work even with a weak signal, amplifying it to the required level, while the latter consists of elements that do not require power sources for operation.
