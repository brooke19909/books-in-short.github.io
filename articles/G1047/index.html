St. Petersburg: Zoological Museum (history of creation)

This institution, like many others, began its existence with the light hand of Peter I. The museum was founded in 1714. At first it was a Kunstkamera, for which they collected a small collection of outlandish animals. Interesting exhibits are constantly replenished. By 1832, there were so many of them, they were of such scientific value that the Kunstkamera was given the status of a museum.

And it became known as the Zoological Museum of the Imperial Academy of Sciences. Six years later, it was opened to visitors. The first director of the unique institution was Academician F.F. Brant, who made a significant contribution to the creation of the museum fund. Many scholars gave their collections - this is A. Middendorf, and C. Baer. Such legendary discoverers as Miklouho-Maclay and Nikolai Przhevalsky constantly brought more and more exhibits from their expeditions.

From year to year the collection increased, the place was sorely lacking. In 1896, the museum moved to the building of the southern warehouse of the Exchange. Five years were needed in order to equip new premises and prepare expositions.

In 1901, the Zoological Museum in St. Petersburg was inaugurated. The ceremony was attended by Nicholas II himself and his family members. After the revolution, in 1930, the museum became part of the Zoological Institute of the Russian Academy of Sciences.

It should be noted that in essence it is still a laboratory in which research is conducted in the field of biology and zoology. At present, the Institute‚Äôs exhibition department includes two biostations, twelve laboratories, a huge library and its own information and publishing department.

There are also exhibits representing Darwin's evolutionary theory. Here you can admire rare animals. Amazing exhibitions are accompanied by fascinating stories from experienced guides. Arriving in St. Petersburg, the Zoological Museum is worth a visit.



Exhibits

Not a single visitor is indifferent. The museum features a large number of mollusks and corals (the Invertebrate Animals department), marsupial mammals from Australia, and rare species of tropical birds. Thanks to the unique processing of stuffed animals, all the exhibits look completely alive. This museum has the richest collections (the best in the world) of cetaceans and the Palearctic fauna. The mummies of small mammoths, which were given the names Dima and Masha, are very popular among guests. Attention is also drawn to the skeleton of a woolly mammoth and the southern elephant found in Kolyma, on the banks of the Berezovka river. No less interesting are the remains of mammals that inhabited the earth in the fortieth century BC.

Opening hours and address

The institution is located on the University Embankment, building 1. This place is very popular, many rush to visit the Zoological Museum. Opening hours: daily except Tuesday. Excursions run from eleven in the morning until six in the evening.
