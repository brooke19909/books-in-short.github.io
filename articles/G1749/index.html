Three-dimensional crochet patterns: description and schemes

A cloth made by a hook almost always turns out to be a little denser than the one that is formed when working with knitting needles. This is primarily due to the fact that when the columns are formed, the thread is subjected to repeated twisting, as a result, the thickness of the web becomes much larger than the thickness of the thread. There are many methods to soften the knit, make it more loose and soft. However, this is not always true. In some cases, obtaining a dense and even hard fabric is the ultimate goal of the knitter. It is for such situations that numerous volumetric crochet patterns have been developed.



Where volumetric ornaments are used

The list of products that are knitted with the help of such patterns is very wide, here are clothing items, and accessories, and decor for the interior.

In clothes, voluminous crochet patterns are necessary for knitting cardigans, collars, cuffs, berets, hats, scarves and many other items. However, applying the scheme in order to manufacture a coarse web, the purpose of the product should be taken into account. It is very easy to make a mistake and get heavy, bulky and completely unattractive chain mail instead of the planned large knit sweater. If you are going to make any product larger than mittens, it is advisable to tie a control sample.

Techniques through which volume patterns are formed

In fact, there are a lot of such ornaments, and there is no sense in describing them all. However, three-dimensional crochet patterns can be combined into several main groups:

  • Tied from a thick thread.
  • Openwork with volumetric elements.
  • Solid with convex fragments.

Often, the volume attached to crocheted patterns is given by such techniques:

  • Lush bollards.
  • Embossed posts.
  • Knitting with the capture of only one "pigtail" loop of the previous row.
  • Single crochet lining.

Crochet three-dimensional patterns: embossed rows

Almost all patterns intended for a hook can be equipped with volume rows. They can emphasize existing lines of the ornament or form new ones.

Similar patterns of volume crochet are performed in two ways:

  1. Tying any pad. This method can be used to make a circular or even row. When knitting along the fabric, a dense and thick thread or wire is laid. Then the column is formed so that the working thread completely hides the lining (it appears inside the row). The resulting series comes out quite embossed and stands out against the background of a flat canvas.
  2. The second method is associated with a special way to capture the loops of the previous row. When knitting columns, the hook should not be inserted under both ‚Äúbraids‚Äù of the bottom row, but only under one of them. As a result, a kind of scar forms, which runs across the entire canvas.

Canvas made of thick yarn

The elementary way, thanks to which it is easy to obtain a three-dimensional pattern, is the use of a thick thread for knitting. We are talking about yarn with a thickness of at least 100 grams / 100 m.

Today, the assortment of many traditional and online stores is striking in its diversity. Here there is thick wool, and acrylic, and cotton, and even lace yarn from knitted fabric. The use of such unconventional materials allows you to create truly original and unique products.

A feature of knitting from thick yarn is the use of a very large hook (7-20 mm). A complex pattern is no longer necessary, since it will still not be visible. It is better to pay attention to the accuracy of knitting and maintaining a uniform density.
