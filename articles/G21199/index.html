The invention of the telephone is a landmark event for civilization

The history of the invention of the phone has more than one hundred thirty years. In everyday hustle and bustle, taking advantage of the many benefits of civilization, a person almost never thinks about how life would have developed without some objects that are familiar to us. And she could be completely different.



The invention of the telephone began with the work of Alexander Bell (1847-1922), a teacher at a school for deaf children in Boston, USA, who in 1875 created a device that can transmit human speech over a distance. In February 1876, Bell patented his device. And in 1878, the first communication session took place, Bell talked on the phone with his assistant. The creation of such a device became possible after alternating current and electromagnetic radiation were discovered. Alexander Bell used these two natural phenomena in the work of the new device.

The invention of the telephone provided a leap in the development of communications and set the transfer of information to a new level. In addition to Alexander Bell , thirty more inventors, including Thomas Edison, claimed to be the discoverer, but none of them documented their invention.



It all started with the fact that in 1866, after several unsuccessful attempts, a telegraphic transatlantic cable was laid connecting America and Europe. But he did not bring the expected profit, and the owners of the cable offered to pay a large bonus to someone who would find a way to transmit messages in a more efficient way than a telegraph. The result of these searches was the invention of the phone. The principle of its operation was as follows: the pulses created by human speech fell on a metal membrane and transmitted through electric wires, fell into a receiving device, caused vibrations of the membrane, where they were again converted into speech. This connection is called impulse.



The phone very quickly took its place in the life of all mankind. And almost until the 70s of the last century, the principle of operation of such devices remained unchanged. In 1973, Motorola engineer Martin Cooper made a call using a mobile device. It was a prototype phone presented by the company on March 6, 1983.
