Charming Cordoba (Argentina) that will surprise even the most discerning traveler

The homeland of tango, famous for its wonderful landscapes and unique architectural monuments, hospitably welcomes tourists in a hurry to get to know them. Argentina is a charming country whose diversity does not have time to be surprised. Rainforests and magnificent glaciers, endless pampas and magnificent waterfalls, ski resorts and snow-white beaches attract travelers from different countries.

A city that will not let you get bored

The country's second largest city is located in its very heart. A bustling metropolis, where life does not stop for a minute, resembles a busy anthill. Charming Cordoba (Argentina), spread on the left bank of the Sikuya River, on the Pampas Plain, is a scientific and industrial center, which is no less valuable than the capital of the state.

A bit of history

Cordoba, which has grown over time, has existed in the country for several centuries. Before the era of colonization, the Indians lived here, and then the Spanish conquistador Jeronimo Luis de Cabrera in 1573 opens this part of Argentina to the whole world. Gradually, the settlement, named after the city of the same name in Spain, turns into the administrative center of the region. Authorities pay special attention to the city, where the Jesuits arrived are engaged in educational and cultural activities, contributing to the development of Cordoba in Argentina. Temples are being built here, educational institutions are being built, sewer and water supply networks are being laid, and street lighting is appearing.

In the middle of the 20th century, the village turned into the main industrial center of the country. The capital of the province of the same name is famous for its developed tourist infrastructure.

Jesuit quarter

Vacationers are interested in the architectural attractions of Cordoba (Argentina), which we will talk about. The main city monuments are associated with the Jesuits and are located in the quarter named after them. The unique complex, protected by UNESCO, includes a church, the Montserrat school, colorful residential buildings and a national university.

The Jesuit quarter, which is the hallmark of the city, is famous for its unusual structures, as Catholic monks from all over Europe came here, bringing with them new ideas for construction. And now it is a colorful illustration of the merger of European culture with the local.

The oldest educational institution in the country

For many years, a university has existed on the territory of the Argentinean pearl. Now it consists of 12 faculties, several institutes and an observatory. In the two-story mansion, which looks rather modest, there is also a museum, the exhibits of which tell about the history of the institution, where the Jesuits taught theology and philosophy at the beginning of the 17th century.

And in the courtyard there is a monument to one of the founders of the university - Fernando's father.

Cathedral

Connoisseurs of luxurious architectural works will not be disappointed with an exciting trip to Cordoba (Argentina). The majestic cathedral building delights with amazing beauty and a mixture of styles.

The construction of the national historical monument began in 1580, and it opened its doors to parishioners after 129 years.

Church of the Sacred Heart

Another religious monument is worth visiting. The Church of the Sacred Heart is a bright landmark of the city. Appearing in the 30s of the last century, she delights in her appearance. The majestic temple has two towers that symbolize the duality of man, and the high spire crowning a real work of art is his soul.

Ongamir Grottoes

When it comes to natural attractions, one cannot but mention the caves of Ongamir, which served as the habitat of the country's indigenous people. A miraculous miracle is located 120 kilometers from Cordoba in Argentina. There were fierce battles between the Indians and the Spanish conquerors, and the last representative of the local population died in 1574.

Three caves, dating back to the Cretaceous, give surprises to scientists who find bead jewelry, quartz arrowheads and other artifacts.

What to see in the province of Cordoba (Argentina)?

The province, whose administrative center is Cordoba, is one of the most visited regions. And this is no coincidence. We present the most interesting places that you should definitely get acquainted with:

  • Villa Maria is the third largest settlement in the province. This tourist paradise is famous for its green oases, a huge concert hall, interesting museums and the Rio Tercero river embankment - a favorite place for walking.
  • Miramar is a ghost town. Located on the shores of the salt lake of Mar Chiquita, it looks unusual and evokes thoughts that nothing lasts forever. The fashionable resort, flooded with water in the 80s of the last century, survived a new tragedy 15 years ago. Dilapidated hotels and restaurants look intimidating, and tourists note the oppressive atmosphere prevailing here.
  • La Cumbresita is a tiny but picturesque village hiding high in the mountains. Reminiscent of alpine villages, it attracts those who are tired of the noise of the big city and want to relax in silence in the bosom of pristine nature.

Tourists reviews

Travelers who enjoyed the special atmosphere and beauty of the city of Cordoba in Argentina, admit that the narrow old streets and magnificent masterpieces of architecture seem to be transferred to past eras, revealing the secrets of a rich history. Vacationers admire the beauty of masterpieces of architecture that have absorbed the best European traditions.

Excellent conditions have been created for guests here, and local residents are always ready to share with tourists interesting facts about the country and beloved Cordoba.
