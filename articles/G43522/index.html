Hotel Marins Park (Yekaterinburg): reviews, prices, description

Do you know where the Marins Park Hotel is? Yekaterinburg - here you need to look for it. In this amazing metropolis, the administrative center of the Sverdlovsk region. After Novosibirsk, Moscow and St. Petersburg, it is the fourth most populated city in Russia. In general, it is the largest cultural, administrative and scientific-educational center of the Urals.

Yekaterinburg is also considered one of the most impeccable cities in the Russian Federation. This is one of the most impressive logistics and transportation hubs in the country: the Trans-Siberian Railway and six federal highways pass through the metropolis, and there is an international airport. Yekaterinburg is called an important industrial center: the optical-mechanical industry, heavy engineering and instrument making, food, printing and light industry, metallurgy are highly developed here, there is a military-industrial association.

This city is the leading center of the Ural Federal District. Here is the headquarters of the military Central District, the Presidium of the Ural Division of the Russian Academy of Sciences and thirty-five regional authorities. Sometimes this metropolis is called the "Capital of the Urals."

Today, the tourism industry is showing the highest development dynamics. Both Yekaterinburg and its environs own huge reserves, interesting for travelers. Indeed, in these places there is a huge number of natural monuments and sites for outdoor activities. Interestingly, in 2007, the Ural capital was visited by 675,000 travelers.

Everyone knows that now business tourism brings the highest income. Indeed, 87% of travelers arrive in Yekaterinburg to participate in conferences, international and Russian forums, and exhibitions.





By the way, the city is located on the land border of Asia and Europe. Therefore, cognitive-cultural tourism is very popular in this metropolis. At the seventeenth kilometer of the Moscow tract, a monument is installed. It denotes a borderline makeshift line dividing the two parts of the world. Two stones are placed in the foundation of this monument: one of them was delivered from Cape Roca (extreme European point), and the other from Cape Dezhnev (extreme point of Asia). It is at this place that they intend to create a cultural and entertainment complex.

In the metropolis there are monuments of the XVIII - XX centuries: ancient merchant estates, Soviet architecture and modern buildings. Many foreigners come here to visit the holy places: Ganina Pit and the Temple on Blood. Some tourists enjoy visiting the local ski centers, climbing and caving. Other visitors are engaged in local history and ethnographic tourism, fishing and rafting on the Chusovaya River.

In Yekaterinburg, one of the largest temples in Russia, the Temple on Blood, was recently built. A stone's throw from him is our wonderful hotel. The temple was erected in 2003, on the site where on July 16 at night in 1918 the Russian autocrat Nicholas II and his family were shot. The temple was consecrated on July 16, 2003.

Currently, the pilgrims visit the sanctuary, not only from Russia, but also from other countries. The church attracts the attention of many travelers arriving in Yekaterinburg, including representatives of art, famous politicians and so on.

Today the Temple on Blood is not only a functioning church, but also a museum complex. Here is a collection of objects that tell about the last months of life of Nicholas II and his household.

Between the built buildings of the church and the Patriarchal Compound there is a wooden chapel of the Rev. Martyr Elizabeth. This structure was repeatedly set on fire and subjected to vandalism, but survived. As a result of the arsons, the chapel fell into a dark color. In front of a wooden house on the ground lies a huge marble stone with an inscription that tells about the murder of the royal Romanov dynasty.

The Patriarchal Compound was erected and consecrated along with the temple. It includes the patriarchal chambers, the library, the house chapel of St. Nicholas, an incredible number of halls and rooms for organizing a variety of church meetings.

"Red line"

You must admit that Hotel Marins Park enjoys considerable popularity! Reviews, description - all testifies to this! But now the conversation will be about the "Red Line" - a walking tourist route in Yekaterinburg. It was created for independent travel of the townsfolk and guests of the metropolis in the historical center. The residents of Marins Park can also take such a walk.

The route of traffic is painted in red on the sidewalks of the central part of the metropolis. Volunteers designated all points of interest by numbering, which was also applied to the sidewalk. This nuance allows tourists with a guide to navigate the city without the help of a guide.

It is interesting that the length of the Yekaterinburg Red Line is six and a half kilometers. Near each attraction on the asphalt there are telephone numbers, by calling which you can listen to the history of the object in Russian or English. The text is told by musicians, English polyglots or journalists.
