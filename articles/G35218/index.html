Nokia E6: specifications, review and reviews

One of the most popular phones on the market, Nokia E6, became known to the world community due to the conflict between the Finnish manufacturer and the Canadian giant Blackberry. The fact is that smartphones are identical not only in appearance, but also in functionality. A significant difference in phones is only in price - Nokia is 5 times cheaper than the Canadian representative. In this article, the reader will get acquainted with the infamous smartphone closer, and the technical specifications, review and reviews of the owners will help a potential buyer to choose a phone on the mobile device market.

Specifications

The Nokia E6 smartphone , the price of which is about 10-12 thousand rubles, has all the modern functions useful for both work and leisure:

  1. TFT multi-touch color touch screen with 16 million colors. Its diagonal is 2.5 inches, resolution is 640x480 dpi. There is a technology for automatic screen rotation, and the display itself has a glass that is resistant to mechanical damage.
  2. In the classic case of the phone there is a full-sized QWERTY-keyboard. Accordingly, control can be carried out using mechanical buttons.
  3. The device works in all known networks of the GSM standard, and also supports 3G. There are built-in modules Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, GPS.
  4. The single-core processor operates at a frequency of 680 MHz. The RAM capacity of the Nokia E6 is 256 MB. 8 GB non-volatile memory can be expanded with memory cards up to 40 gigabytes.
  5. The battery capacity is 1500 mAh, and this volume is enough for the phone for continuous conversations for 15 hours.

Functionality for work and entertainment

For convenience, the smartphone supports voice dialing and control, and there is also a built-in speaker for speakerphone. Light and proximity sensors allow the phone to save energy, and the built-in accelerometer copes with the role of a compass.





Nokia cell phone, apparently, claims to be in the niche of youth devices, at least this is indicated by its multimedia capabilities:

  1. The built-in camera has a resolution of 8 megapixels. In addition to the built-in flash, the camera is equipped with digital zoom and can recognize faces.
  2. Video recording is carried out according to the H.264 standard with a maximum resolution of 1280x720 dpi (the frequency does not exceed 25 frames per second).

There is a front camera as additional functionality, Geo Tagging is supported (adding location information to photos). The phone is capable of playing audio in all popular formats. At the hardware level, the smartphone has an FM radio.

Inside you can find not only a smartphone and a charger. The buyer will be pleasantly surprised by the branded phone case and excellent quality headphones.

Operating system features

According to manufacturers, the phone has an innovative Symbian Anna operating system. The manufacturer assures that the changes affected not only the appearance, but also are directly related to performance (less resource intensive). But, as the owners note in their reviews, they did not notice much difference between the devices of the previous generation and E6. And for most users, the main criterion for the purchase was full application compatibility for the Symbian platform. Expectations and Opportunities

The presence of a high-resolution camera in an inexpensive smartphone definitely attracts the buyer, however, a close acquaintance with the functionality of the device leads to a completely different result. Firstly, the camera does not have 8 megapixels announced - in fact, a 5 megapixel sensor is installed. Built-in programs for Nokia perform the usual interpolation of the image, providing the result of processing to the user. There are complaints about the photosensitivity of the matrix - white balance and shades of gray are unfamiliar to her, therefore, in poor lighting, it will not be possible to achieve a high-quality picture.





But the lack of autofocus did not affect the quality of the photos. The camera does an excellent job of measuring distances and captures the smallest details of objects. The better the lighting on the street, the better the user will receive a photograph. To color reproduction in good lighting, too, no complaints.

Information Entry and Management

The keyboard buttons of the Nokia E6 phone, as noted by users, have a fairly rigid course, which completely eliminates accidental pressing when entering information. The keys themselves are slightly convex upwards - this form does not even allow you to press several buttons with your thumb. As befits any full keyboard, the smartphone has a long space, an upper-case letter, and an alternative command call (Sym and Ctrl buttons).





The manufacturer implemented in the device a full keyboard backlight, which is controlled using a light sensor. All buttons of the smartphone are illuminated evenly, without any distortion. In general, the keyboard of this phone is impeccable. With it, it is very easy to type large amounts of text with both one and two hands. The small pad present under the keys acts as a palm rest.

Finally

A rather interesting model was released by Nokia to the world market. On the one hand, this is low-quality plagiarism for a more expensive phone, and on the other, the cheapest smartphone with a QWERTY keyboard, which has a lot of useful functions. For example, in a Nokia E6 phone, the firmware is downloaded independently when the device is connected to high-speed WiFi networks, as is implemented in many Android devices. In general, the advantages still prevail over the shortcomings, and new owners continue to appear on the smartphone daily.
