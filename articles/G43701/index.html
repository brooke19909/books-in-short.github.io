The choice of yarn and pattern for warm things

Almost all types of threads are suitable for knitting stoles with knitting needles. The following types of stoles can be distinguished:

  1. Dense and warm. For such things, material with a high content of wool or mohair is selected. As a rule, the thickness of the thread should be no more than 300 m / 100 grams.   2. Openwork and warm. For such stoles, a thin mohair or angora with a thread thickness of at least 500 m / 100 grams is traditionally selected. In combination with an openwork pattern, such materials make it possible to achieve lightness and grace of the finished product.
    3. Mohairs of Italian manufacturers are considered to be of the highest quality, since they contain at least 75% wool of fine-wool goats and only 25% artificial fibers (cotton, rayon or nylon). Turkish analogues include about 50% acrylic, which leads to the appearance of pellets, the formation of lumps of mohair on the threads and other troubles.

Cotton and viscose products

Working with yarn without wool or mohair, you can get the following types of stoles:

  1. Dense but light. These stoles are suitable for cool summer evenings. They are often taken with them to the sea to protect themselves from the wind. Such products are knitted with a small pattern of thin cotton threads.
  2. Openwork and light. It is these stoles that become the main decoration of evening dresses. Thanks to silk or viscose yarn, products acquire an attractive shine. The pattern here is suitable for almost any, most importantly - the accuracy of the knitter. In order for the palatine to become a really high-quality thing, it is necessary to observe the same density and not make mistakes in the ornament.

The edge design is also of no less importance: you can make a border simultaneously with the manufacture of the main fabric or crochet the finished fabric.
