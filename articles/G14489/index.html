Corrugated paper irises: master class and recommendations

This beautiful flower can be seen in the garden, greenhouses and on the street. Let's try to make irises from corrugated paper together and enjoy their beauty all year round. The master class proposed in the article will help you understand this simple and interesting process.

Necessary tools and materials

Before you decide to make such beautiful flowers, it is worth stocking up with the necessary materials listed below. And also see how irises are made from corrugated paper. The master class proposed in the article covers the sequence and nuances of your future work. For her you will need:

  • a sheet of plain paper;
  • corrugated paper;
  • glue;
  • a delay;
  • scissors;
  • knitting fluffy thread;
  • tape tape;
  • stem stem.

We begin to make irises from corrugated paper: a master class





If you have prepared everything you need, then proceed to the implementation. The process will not be complicated:

  1. To make irises from corrugated paper, you must first prepare the templates: the most pointed - a small petal, the middle - teardrop-shaped and large - rounded.
  2. To make iris of natural colors, you need to take paper in blue, blue or purple. Cut the blanks of the required shape into 3 pieces and gently stretch them over the edges. Please note that corrugated paper is not stretched in four, but only in two directions. As a result of this, outline the template so that it is possible to stretch it as far as possible along the side contours.
  3. To create the correct shape of the flower, you need to attach a thin wire to each petal, leaving tails at the base.
  4. It should be noted a feature of irises - yellow fluff on large petals. The cannon can be made using finely chopped fluffy thread.
  5. Next, apply a strip of glue in the middle of the three large petals and sprinkle the fluff on top. Make the iris more natural by painting its petals with light paint. Strokes should be light and superficial.




Thinking about how to properly assemble irises from corrugated paper? The master class explains in detail the sequence of necessary work. The main thing is to follow a clear chronology of the description, and the work will be easy and interesting. So, let's continue:

  • First, connect the smallest petals to each other. Then between them add the middle ones, and attach the largest ones from the bottom.
  • The final stage is the formation of a flower with a stem. Wrap the remaining ends of the wire and the rod with green tape.




If you do not have corrugated, then you can use crepe paper. The technology for making iris will be similar. The crepe material is thinner and the petals are sleeker. Therefore, iris from crepe paper will come out gentle and beautiful.

The use of various materials in the manufacture of a bouquet of flowers affects the final result. Experiment, do not stop at certain materials, and perhaps you will discover something new in creating beautiful bouquets of flowers.

Make flowers
