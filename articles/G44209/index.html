Pinezhsky caves: location, description of how and what to get, tips for tourists

The greatness of karst caves attracts many cavers. However, lovers of beauty and amazing sensations, too, only after seeing Pinezhsky caves in the photo, are eager to visit there. The reserve is a state-protected zone, and therefore its wealth and nature are still not destroyed, but beautifully ennobled. Understanding that to forbid those who wish to visit it is to provoke them to illegal entry, an excursion was organized. What is included in the tour? Why are the caves so beautiful? What else does the reserve keep in itself?

Rich Pinezhye

This region has special beauty because of the forest. About 505 species of pines, deciduous trees and spruces grow in it. In addition, here you can find about 40 species of edible mushrooms! Aphyllophore is distinguished by special beauty - they grow from the bark of a tree and hang from it. They give the feeling that a tourist who wandered here was in a fairy tale.

The flora and fauna of the Pinezhsky reserve is distinguished by its history. Researchers are especially looking at Sotka, namely, the section of deciduous trees near the reservoir, which at this time is approximately 200-300 years old. The age of the trees allows us to trace which years were especially arid and unfavorable for the vegetation of this region, and which were fruitful.

The Sotka River is also distinguished by its inhabitants: salmon, grayling, whitefish and many more common fish species found their homes here. Thus, the fauna helps preserve the unique conditions for the growth of endangered plant species. Among vascular, and 213 of them are known to be endangered species, in the reserve you can find 30% of plants that quietly exist next to each other.

Caves - pearls of the Pinezhsky region

The pride of the reserve can be considered Pinezhsky caves, going deep underground and forming among themselves a curled network of galleries and narrow passages. In total there are 467 in this region, and in the reserve itself - 91. Ten of them are 1 km long and are both independent units and interconnected, forming a network of exits and passages.

Exits are most often located on the side plots of land near the coast of Sotka, have a horizontal arrangement. Vertical happens, but rarely. The tunnels are also often located horizontally, sometimes going diagonally down. Depth ranges from 10 to 30 meters. Here, in abundance, you can find tunnels connected in labyrinths, galleries and halls, which have their names according to external signs or historical incidents.

The age of some Pinezhsky caves reaches 8 thousand years. Almost all are formed in tectonic cracks periodically filled with water. So, the formation of underground passages was facilitated by spring floods, flooding individual halls entirely, lakes inside the cave, underground streams and even several waterfalls.

Golubinsky failure

The longest, but at the same time, safe, is the Golubinsky failure - a cave located on the territory of the Golbinsky karst massif. It reaches 1620 m, throughout its entire length showing tourists its beauties in different halls in height and width in tunnels. The beauty and uniqueness of each cave is formed by sediments, which are abundant: carbonate sediments, stalagmites, crusts, stalactites, stalagmates (interconnected growths of stalactites and stalagmites).

Especially beautiful can be called ice formations: through a transparent screen you can see the fantastically distorted relief of the cave. Ice ridges and crusts hang from the arches of the halls along with stalactites, and the icefall reminds us that in its place there was a real waterfall, frozen due to cold temperature. Overhead, almost all the time there are crystals, fantastically shining under the rays of light.

How to get there on your own?

You can visit the reserve with a guide, but there are tourists who want to make an independent trip to the Pinezhsky caves. How to get to them is of interest to many who have not been to these parts, but who dream of seeing their beauty.

On almost all maps you can find Pinega of the Arkhangelsk region (Russia). Because autotravel to Pinezhsky caves are real and feasible. There is an asphalt road to Pinega , on which both a bus and a passenger car will pass well.

There are two ways to go:

  • The first - turning from Arkhangelsk, get to the village of Pinega, then Golubino, and in Krasnaya Gorka there is a guest house where you can stay.
  • The second method is bypassing Arkhangelsk, via the M8 highway towards the village of Kholmogory. You don‚Äôt need to drive to the village itself, but you need to turn to the village of Ust-Pinega. It is across the river. Ferries go in summer, and in winter - ice crossing. From there, you can already reach the village of Pinega, Golubino and Krasnaya Gorka.

General tips for "beginner cavers"

The caves have high humidity, and these are not the cleanest places - you should wear clothes that no one will grieve if they accidentally tear or get dirty. In addition, the temperature in Pinezhsky caves is kept at 2 ¬∞ C all year round, which is why a scarf, a warm hat and gloves should be with you. It‚Äôs best if a second pair of warm clothes is prepared nearby at the base. After long hours of travel, you will want to wash and keep warm.

Flashlights are an important part of the equipment of cavers. They must be powerful and reliable. It will be useful to take spare batteries. The boots should be waterproof, because if they are wet in the cave, the risk of freezing faster increases, and the victim will still have to wait for the road back covered with snow.

It is best to visit the cave in winter and the first month of spring - stalactites are impressive in size, while the rest of the time the ice melts, and such splendor is no longer observed. Late spring and summer are fraught with the fact that many caves will be flooded and most of the colorful places can simply not be seen.
