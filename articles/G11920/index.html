Athlete, teacher, inventor

Basketball's father was born in 1861, in Ontario, Canada. Now in the town of Mississippi Mills there is a house-museum of the inventor of basketball. Since childhood, he was fond of sports, especially game types. If hockey at that time was as popular as it is now, a center forward would probably have appeared in one of the Canadian teams, whose shirt would have the name Naismith. James, while studying at the University of Montreal, played other games, mainly in the form of football - in European (called in North America sokker) and Canadian.



The young man had an innovative mindset and an inventive streak. Canadian, like American, football has English rugby in its ancestors and is a supercontact game with increased injury risk. Bachelor of Physical Education

After graduating from university and becoming a certified teacher of physical education, James Naismith moved to a neighboring country. Since 1891, he began working in one of the colleges organized by YMKA (YMKA - Young Men's Christian Association) - a youth Christian organization in the city of Springfield, in the state of Massachusetts. In addition to gymnastics, he taught anatomy.



As a teacher, James Naismith was considered a true professional. He sought to introduce students to the sport, trying to make classes interesting, without turning into boring and monotonous exercises. In the summer, it was easier to organize outdoor competitions, where students played baseball and soccer, in the winter it was more difficult to maintain interest in gymnastics in the gym. College Director Dr. Luther Gulick asked James to come up with something. And better not as aggressive as rugby or American football. The creative nature of a talented physical education teacher once helped Naismith find a brilliant solution.

Soccer ball and two peach baskets

James Naismith invented a game with a ball indoors to dispel the boredom of doing gymnastics or running monotonously around them. They say that the rules of the new game, relevant in general terms today, were formulated within an hour. Once he noticed how some of the students from the college rugby college team are trying to play in the hall, using a large box instead of a goal.



December 1, 1891, dividing the students into two teams, he proposed to hit the ball in a wooden box located on the floor in the center of the gym. The case ended in a heap, when the defenders got up around the box and completely blocked it from the rivals. A gallery was located around the perimeter of the hall, and James got the idea to nail two empty baskets to its opposite sides in which fruit was delivered to college. The first basketball players were supposed to get into them with a leather ball for European football, gaining points.

First rules

Understanding what sports game James Naismith invented is very simple. Today, a durable metal ring equipped with a synthetic mesh should support the weight of two-meter athletes, but is still called the basket - in English basket. A modern ball made of a unique polymer is a product of complex technologies and high design. It does not look much like the football leather that Naismith found the most suitable, but it is still called in English ball - a ball.

The rules of basketball for over a hundred years have changed dramatically, making this game the fastest in the world. But the first, original, were published on January 15, 1892 in a school newspaper called the Triangle. This day is considered the official birthday of basketball - a game that today is played by about 300 million.

Ad-Free Popularity

The new game - fast and exciting, which could be played both outdoors and indoors, with simple and clear rules, immediately liked the youth. Naismith began to be contacted from various educational institutions from all over America with a request to send basketball rules. In 1892 a book was published in which they were formulated in 13 paragraphs. The main provisions of the rules are unshakable for the current generation.

Olympic sport

In 1898, the first professional league appeared in the United States. Players received $ 2.5 for home games and $ 1.25 for away games. Less than a hundred years later, one of the star players, the Washington Bullets NBA team, Juvan Howard, signed a contract according to which he received $ 100 million over seven seasons.

A basketball show was held at the 1904 Olympic Games in St. Louis, and the first official tournament was held at the Berlin Olympics in 1936, at which the U.S. team won Canada 18: 9 in the final. The guest of honor, who made a symbolic face-off in the final match, was the inventor of the game himself. Invited to the Olympics by the top leadership of the IOC, he was able to appreciate the magnitude of the popularity that his brainchild gained throughout the world.

Intravital and posthumous fame

Naismith died on November 28, 1939, surrounded by five children and many grandchildren.
