Who is the merchant? Russian merchant

A merchant is not such an ancient profession as a hunter, but still a rather old specialty in the field of entrepreneurship, that is, activities aimed at the systematic receipt of profit from trade.

Foundation basis



In Russia, merchants were already in the IX century. In those days, the treasury of the state was filled mainly due to tribute levied on conquered peoples. The second item of income was trade. She was also the engine of progress. Cities were built mainly along the banks of the rivers that served as trade routes. According to historical information, the Scythians had no other roads at all. The coastal cities became first shopping centers, and then crafts developed in them. In ancient Russia, a merchant is not only a merchant. Athanasius Nikitin, whose monument was erected in Tver, in his homeland, was a great traveler who traveled "beyond the three seas", and a discoverer, and a diplomat. And the famous legendary Novgorod merchant Sadko also went to the bottom of the sea.

Rich and kind



The merchants developed and gained strength, the best representatives of this estate were promoted to the appropriation of the nobility for their special merits to the fatherland. For example, the Rukovishnikovs. The Moscow dynasty founded a noble family, and Ivan Vasilyevich (1843-1901) rose to the rank of Privy Councilor. The Novgorod dynasty, the founder of which was a resourceful peasant, already in the third generation began to belong to the upper class. The motto of this family was the words "sacrifice and guardianship." The same can be said of a very large number of Russian entrepreneurs. This is the special mentality of the domestic merchant. The Russian merchant is in most cases a benefactor and patron. The names of the largest merchants-patrons, the memory left by them occupy a special place in Russian history. Who does not know the merchant Tretyakov, the founder of the art gallery of his name.
