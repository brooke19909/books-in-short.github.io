Sightseeing in Lloret de Mar (Spain). What to see in the Spanish city of Lloret de Mar?

Emerald Pyrenees spurs, impregnable cliffs, amazing bays, pebble and white sand beaches characterize the north-western coast of Catalonia, recognized as one of the most picturesque places in Spain. The coastline here is indented with small bays, unsuitable for the construction of ports, which allowed to preserve the amazing landscapes of untouched nature. One of the most famous resorts in Catalonia is Lloret de Mar, famous for its magnificent beaches and vibrant nightlife. A variety of variations of spending free time is ideal for a busy vacation. And the natural, cultural and historical sights of Lloret de Mar will greatly enrich the experience.

Beautiful, interesting and fun.



The Mediterranean coast of this part of Spain is called the Costa Brava, it extends from Blanes to the border with France. The secret tourist capital of the region is the resort of Lloret de Mar. Lloret de Mar is the most interesting place for those who are attracted by the combination of beach holidays and travel. The proximity to the Catalan Pyrenees, bordering France on the north, also plays a significant role.

Resort beaches

The area from Canyez Bay to Santa Cristina Bay belongs to the resort of Lloret de Mar. Its beaches are distinguished by crystal clear sea, clean sand and Blue flags, indicating the quality of services according to international classification. Lloret, Fenals, Santa Cristina, Canyelles, Bans Bay and Boadella turn the resort into the best place to relax, surrounded by picturesque cliffs, fantastically rugged surf.



The Fenals beach is quite popular. The watchtower of the castle "Sant Joan", built in the 10th century and served as a shelter for local residents from pirate raids, gives a special flavor. The castle was built on a mountain between the beaches of Fenals and Lloret. As you ascend to the watchtower, a breathtaking view of the port of Blanes opens up. No less wonderful is spending time in the nearby wonderful park of St. Clotilde, designed in the style of the Italian Renaissance in 1919. This is a favorite place for walking residents of the resort and its guests. Here you can admire the sea views, quiet ponds and sophisticated statues of Maria Llimona. Not far from Santa Cristina Bay, there is a branch of the Jardi Tropical Pinya de Rosa, the famous botanical garden in Blanes, famous for its magnificent collection of cacti.

Particularly admired by tourists is the nightlife of the resort. Bright colors of the night, laser shows, incendiary music of numerous night clubs, bars, restaurants as if envelop Lloret de Mar. Photos of the city at this time of day allow you to feel the drive of nightlife, recharge the energy of dance and fun. Truly, this is one of the most rave resorts on the coast of Spain. Amusement park

Water park rides are designed for the whole family. Water World is one of the best Costa Brava entertainment complexes. Vertical slides, famously twisted serpentine pipes, one of the highest towers in the world for bungee jumping and other attractions will leave a lot of positive impressions. For the younger generation, an entire island is provided with shallow pools and small slides, where children have fun under the supervision of an experienced animator.

In the very center of the city is located the equally popular water park Marineland. Here, fascinating shows with sea animals take place, you can take a walk around the mini zoo, visit cozy restaurants, have fun on the rides.

Maritime Museum

Museums in tourism programs often occupy a special place. When visiting the sights of Lloret de Mar, it is worth paying attention to the Garrigue's house, built in the middle of the XIX century. Initially, the Garriga brothers conducted their business here, selling building materials. Now the building is converted into a Maritime Museum. This is the resort‚Äôs unique and most visited museum, representing the entire course of the development of the history of maritime affairs in Catalonia. The museum displays not only ancient ships, simple fishing boats, but also modern liners, navigation and ancient maps from the time of great geographical discoveries. If desired, all visitors are given the opportunity to get acquainted with marine navigation devices, diving equipment, visit the sailor's cockpit and even the captain's cabin.





Pearl of the Costa Brava

Lloret de Mar can be safely called the pearl of the Costa Brava. And that is true. On the coast, the resort occupies the most favorable position. Bizarre cliffs, numerous bays, calm clean beaches attract and captivate tourists. Young people are attracted to night clubs, discos of various directions and dance programs. It is here that the only casinos on the entire coast and the bullring located on the Vidreres road are located. Not deprived of impressions in Lloret de Mar and lovers of antiquity. Most of the towns in these places were once small fishing villages with a long history and keeping evidence of the presence of ancient Greeks, Romans, Phoenicians, Franks, Visigoths and Moors. The sights of Lloret de Mar fit organically into the general landscape, each of the historical sites deserves attention and carries with it a fascinating history and a certain riddle.

What is worth seeing?

The Old Town is especially popular with tourists, where such architectural and historical monuments as the Monastery of St. Cristina, built in the 14th century, and the Church of San Roma, built in the 16th century and which is a kind of example of the Gothic style, are located.

To this day, in the church, called after the "Old Virgin" after 1522, preserved frescoes and a bell tower in the Romanesque style.



Among the attractions of the resort stands out the medieval castle of San Juan, dating back to the VI century. Until the 17th century, he defended the city from naval conquerors. Now the castle has a museum complex.

A unique symbol of the city is a monument to the wife of a sailor, called the Virgin Lloret, or Don Marinere. It was established for the millennium of the city, which was celebrated in 1966. The rock, on which you can see a sculpture by Ernest Maragall, is located on Lloret Beach. The monument is a reminder of the fishing past of the famous resort. For many centuries, men went to sea, and women waited with hope and anxiety for their return to shore.

Sculpture museum

The open-air sculpture museum is the cemetery in Lloret de Mar. At the same time, ancient tombstones and familial luxurious crypts look beautiful and sad, many of which were created by Catalan architects Josin Puzh-i-Kadafalk and Bonaventure Conil. The cemetery is divided into six sections. Walking along its quiet alleys, many tourists visiting architectural monuments, made in the style of the Art Nouveau school of Antonio Gaudi. The project of the cemetery, one of the most beautiful in the world, was created in 1896 by the architect Joaquin Artau.
