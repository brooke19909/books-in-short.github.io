What is an asymmetric grid

Even a person who is not dedicated to the intricacies of the craft, it is clear that any wearable thing should be at least to some extent durable. The methods of fastening, which connect the elements of Irish lace, do not differ in particular variety. This is stitching in cases where the motives are adjacent to each other closely. If a certain distance is provided between them, then the so-called arches from columns or air loops with a different number of yarn are used. In general, the entire mass of intermediate connection lines forms a whole network, which is conventionally called irregular, or asymmetric. This system is the main feature that distinguishes Irish lace. Schemes, of course, exist in specialized albums, but it‚Äôs extremely difficult to copy them exactly, and to no avail. Get to the point

So, we settled on the fact that a suitable basis was found, for example, an old T-shirt corresponding to a given size (a foam rubber or a pillow will also work). Where to begin? The Irish lace technique for beginners allows you to do without tedious calculations of the number of loops, everything can be done by eye, but there is one important feature: in order to make the stitching and joining places less eye-catching, it is best to work from the inside out. How to create a network

Now about the main thing, that is, how to do it. No special skills other than those owned by Russian craftswomen are required. It is important to learn the connection method, that is, the creation of the said irregular grid. We decided on those features that distinguish Irish lace. The master class involves a description of the sequence of operations, that is, manufacturing technology.

The steps to create an arch consist of the following steps:

- Closer to the edge (a couple of threads from it), a working thread is introduced into the canvas.

- The thread is pulled towards itself through the loop, and its end is pulled into a small knot, the tip of which is hidden inside the canvas.

- A chain of several loops is being dialed. It is applied to places of possible articulation in such a way as to find out where to fix it. The result should be pleasing to the eye, so the arch cannot be too short, but you should not get carried away. The best assistant and adviser in this matter is their own taste.





- After determining the place of the future connection, two double crochets are added to the column and two air loops are knitted. All this clings to the canvas of the next lace element, creating a strong bond.

- When creating other connecting elements-columns alternating with chains, their value, measured in loops, should be varied depending on the geometry of the elements. The main thing here is to start, and then everything will go by itself.
