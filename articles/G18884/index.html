Soilmen - who are they? Representatives, Views and Key Ideas

The main ideas of the soil workers are considered a direct continuation of the concept of the Slavophiles. They arose and began to spread in the second half of the 19th century. Especially actively studied are the main topics of soil cultivars in the literature of the 10th grade . Let us further consider the features of soil cultivation.



General information

The views of the soil workers are reflected in the works of such famous personalities as Apollo Grigoriev, Fyodor Dostoevsky, Nikolai Danilevsky. In the writings of the first two, the concept was especially pronounced, according to which history was presented as a spontaneous process that could not be rationalized.

Soil farmers are people who proclaim faith in the people, soil, soil.

Theory Background

In the middle of the 19th century, the idea that every culture, every nation was unique was widespread. They cannot be reduced to a single world model.

In Western European philosophical thought, this idea is reflected in the works of Rickert, and in Russian - in the works of Grigoriev and Pogodin. The latter was the first to express the idea that Russia and Western Europe belong to different types of cultures. Pogodin regarded Europe as the heiress of the Western, and Russia as the Eastern Roman Empire.

Grigoryev called his worldview organic. His ideas were based on the fact that world life is developing in the aggregate of "cycles". They have their own image, form. Grigoriev considered each era to be an "organism", as well as a single nation. He fixed several terms and ideas, which subsequently found expression in the concept of cultural-historical types created by Danilevsky.



The essence of ideas

Several distinguishing features of the concept proposed by the soil workers should be highlighted . This is , first of all, recognition of the special role of Russian people. According to the theory, the people fulfill a mission to save all of humanity.

An educated society must become closer to the people. This is exactly what the soil workers promoted in their writings. In this case, rapprochement should occur on a religious and ethnic basis.

The main ideas put forward by the soil workers are :

  • Elimination of serfdom and bureaucracy.
  • Development of trade, industry.
  • Freedom of the press and personality.

The ideas of representatives of soil cultivation were close to the concept proposed by the Slavophiles. The soil workers, adopting the culture of Europe, simultaneously exposed the West in its lack of spirituality, bourgeoisie. They rejected materialism, socialist and revolutionary ideas, opposed them to Christian ideals.

Cultural diversity concept

It was first formulated by Danilevsky. He developed a system of types of cultures and explained the mechanism of their appearance, development, change in the framework of various historical events. In his studies, he relied on the laws of nature.



Danilevsky considered the cultural-historical type to be peculiar and independent plans for industrial, domestic, social, artistic, scientific, religious, that is, in general, historical development. He argued that the essence of world history is the formation of 10 types of cultures:

  • Assyro-Babylonian;
  • Romano-Germanic;
  • Iranian
  • Indian
  • ancient Semitic;
  • Chinese
  • Egyptian
  • New Arabian;
  • Ancient Roman
  • ancient Greek.

However, he also identified 2 types of cultures that existed in pre-Columbian America: Peruvian and Mexican. According to Danilevsky, they did not have time to develop, since they were destroyed by the conquerors of Spain. Slavic type, according to the ideas of the author, is in the process of becoming.

Opposition to Eurocentrism

The achievement of the soil workers can be called the fact that they formulated a concept that does not correspond to the universalist theories of human history. At the same time, the latter had a pronounced Eurocentric character.

The concept of Dostoevsky

As mentioned above, the activities of the soil workers were aimed at bringing the educated population closer to the common people on the basis of religious and ethnic values. At the same time, individual representatives began their research with questions of the relationship between man and God, the world and God.

Dostoevsky, unlike many of his contemporaries, believed that outside the divine idea as a higher principle, moral foundations in a man himself, developing only at the expense of his own forces, are conditional. Only religion, according to the author, fully substantiates morality.



Dostoevsky said that Christianity gives its moral principle and orders to believe that it is normal, single, and all conventions are absent. In his works, Dostoevsky studies all the negative consequences that the rejection of God entails.

Theory of Man

According to Dostoevsky, man is a great secret. He says that there is nothing more expensive and more significant than man, but at the same time, there is nothing worse than him. Dostoevsky ridicules rationalistic ideas about a person according to which the behavior of an individual is determined by considerations of profit and reason.

The writer believed that man is an irrational being. Accordingly, it is impossible to know its nature in a rational way. A thought will not be able to embrace the whole person, since there is always an irrational remnant that makes up that very secret meaning. In the individual, according to Dostoyevsky, an act of creativity is hiding, which brings joy and suffering to human life. However, they cannot understand, the mind cannot redo them.

European socialism in the works of Dostoevsky

The writer considers European socialism as a product of Western European culture. He says that the key to learning is the economic problem.

Meanwhile, according to Dostoevsky, the problem of socialism is more atheistic. He says that the new European teaching eliminates Christianity and cares, first of all, for daily bread.
