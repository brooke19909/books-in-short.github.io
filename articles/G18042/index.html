City of Gijon, Spain: attractions, weather, reviews

The city of Gijon in Spain is located on the coast of the Atlantic Ocean. It is part of the autonomy of Asturias. For a long time Gijon was the industrial and commercial center of the country, with a well-developed metallurgical industry and large reserves of natural resources. Towards the end of the last century, tourism began to develop actively in Gijon, which significantly replenishes the city budget.



The history of the city of Gijon

On the land where the city is today, the first settlement appeared in the VI century BC. In the 1st century BC e. it was subdued by the Romans. The influence of Roman culture and today is clearly visible in the architecture of the city, especially this applies to the mansions between San Lorenzo and the marina.

After the collapse of the Roman Empire, Gijon was captured by the Visigoths, and then by the Arabs, who established their rule until 722. Legend has it that at this time the head of the tiny kingdom of Pelayo opposed the Arab governor. As a result, Pelayo was forced to flee to the caves of Asturias. It was there that the Madonna appeared to him and convinced him of the need to fight and defend the Christian faith.



Was the phenomenon of the Madonna or not, but in 722 the army of Pelayo defeated the Arabs in Kovadong. This event was the beginning of the reconquest of the Iberian Peninsula by Christians. In 1395, a terrible fire almost wiped out Gijon in Spain from the face of the earth. However, it was restored quite quickly.

In 1480, the city began to build a port on the north coast. In the middle of the XVI century it was fortified, and at the end of the XVIII century, active trade with the West Indies was already conducted through it. Today it is one of the main ports of the country.

Gijon in Spain: sights of the city

It's hard to believe, but just a hundred years ago, at the place where the modern city is today, there was a tiny fishing village. Modern travelers know Gijon in Spain as an actively developing resort, the country's cultural center with many interesting objects of history and architecture. There are ancient places of worship and museums, parks and squares, sports facilities and many other interesting places. It is located in the fishing village of Cimadevilla, on a peninsula that bisected the port. Most of the local streets are lined with cobblestones. Many buildings have been restored in recent years. Museum of the people of Asturias

One of the most interesting cultural centers in Gijon (Spain) is the Museum of the people of Asturias, which acquaints visitors with the culture and features of the local population. This ethnographic museum began work in 1968 and during this time has earned recognition not only from the townspeople, but also from many tourists.



The museum has an unusual layout - it is located in the park and consists of several separate pavilions, each of which has its own unique collection.

Hoveljanos House Museum

Tourists who love excursions are advised to visit the Hovelanos House Museum. This is a beautiful palace-type building, which for many years belonged to the family of the famous Spanish writer. The museum was opened in 1971, and today it houses a large collection of works of art. Conferences, concerts and other city events are held here.



Isabel Park

In the mid-20th century, the architect Ramon Ortiz created a landscape park in the city, which he named in honor of Queen Isabella I of Castile. Today, the park territory occupies fifteen hectares. There are a lot of fun rides, colorful flower beds. Shady alleys are decorated with sculptures. The picturesque pond is especially attractive, on the banks of which peacocks are important for walking, swans, ducks and geese nest.



Aquarium

Has its own aquarium Gijon. Spain boasts many similar structures, but this one is unusual in that it is located on the Poniente beach. Four thousand underwater inhabitants are represented in it - from otters and penguins to sharks, which are kept in fifty tanks. Here, more than twelve separate underwater environments have been restored - from the Bay of Biscay and the Asturian rivers to the tropical oceans.

Gijon Workers University

Fans of architectural monuments will be interested in the working university of Hijon in Spain. It is considered the main architectural attraction of the city. The university was founded in 1946 and was intended for children from families of coal mines.



Today it houses the faculty of the University of Oviedo, the Center for Industrial Creativity, the Higher Stage School and the Conservatory. The height of the main tower of the building is 130 meters. On a high bell tower is an observation deck, which offers amazing views of the city and surroundings.

Museum of Roman Baths

Gijon in Spain is able to impress even sophisticated connoisseurs of historical monuments. Specialists attribute the construction of baths to the I-II centuries of our era. In the VI century, they were still used as living quarters, and in the Middle Ages a necropolis was organized here.

Picturesque ruins are preserved to this day, in which you can see unique murals. The heating system was also well preserved.

In 1969, a large-scale reconstruction took place at the stadium, during which for the first time in Spain the stands were covered with a visor.
