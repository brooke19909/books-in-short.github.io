Glorious land

Saba is one of the ancient states that existed from the end of the second millennium to the III century AD. It is also called Sava. It was located in the south of the Arabian Peninsula and took the place of modern Yemen.





This land was famous for fertile lands and an abundance of sun. A huge dam was built near the capital Mariba, which provided the earth with water, turning the barren desert into a blooming oasis. Initially, this state served as a kind of transshipment point. It was here that goods came from Hadramaut and went on to Egypt, Syria and Mesopotamia. In addition to profits from transit trade, Saba received income from the sale of his incense, which was very much appreciated.
