Schemes of daisies crocheted. Crochet patterns: diagrams and description

Knitted flowers are used to create napkins, jewelry, artificial plants, topiary, etc. At the same time, simple flowers with a simple description are popular. Masters currently offer a wide variety of crochet daisy patterns.

Flat flowers

Such daisies will make a napkin, tablecloth, top, blouse, bag. They will perfectly decorate pillows, bedspreads, any clothes. Their size depends on the circumference of the core and the length of the petals. To get a delicate look, use thin yarn. If you need to increase the size of the chamomile without changing the scheme, just take the strings thicker. From thick yarn, flowers hold their shape, but look implausible.

Now sew the floral elements together or tie them with connecting loops.



Crochet daisy patterns for brooches

Use thin threads (cotton seed yarn, "Iris"). Knitting begins with petals.

  • Dial twelve loops and another lift.
  • Knit twelve single crochet stitches (RLS) on them.
  • Tie the resulting petal with connecting posts on all sides.
  • Again, collect 13 loops and knit a petal.
  • Create sixteen loops.
  • Next, piercing each petal at the base with a crochet, knit with connecting posts and close in a circle.
  • The next rows are knitted in the same way, reducing the columns and filling the circle.
  • Now, from the yellow yarn, knit the RLS core (connect two circles, filling with synthetic winterizer).
  • Sew a circle on the petals.
  • Also sew the same circle with the petals for the bud.

Now proceed to crochet the leaves. Schemes and sheet description:

  • Knit twenty-two stitches of sc.
  • You tie the sheet with ‚Äúwaves‚Äù: a connecting column, two sc and one double crochet.
  • Then repeat the waves throughout the petal.
  • Knit three petals for a bud and a flower.

Collect brooch

Continue crocheting. Daisies in a bud are collected from two circles with petals (also twelve). For the bud, the yellow middle is not filled with padding polyester, it remains flat. A wire is attached to the sepal side in the middle.

From green yarn, with crochets, knit a circle the size of a daisy core. Pull the sepals through the wire, sew with a flower so that the petals are folded into a bud. Tie the stem with columns, fastening with a sheet.

For the opened flower, also knit a round sepals. Sew two sheets with a bud on the wrong side, and attach the petals to your face. Glue the pin to the sepals. Correct the bud - and the brooch is ready.

Pay attention that the petals and leaves keep their shape, they can be starched, fixed with PVA (grease liberally with glue from the inside and left to dry in a certain position), sew in a thin wire, knit from dense yarn. Choose your way.



Crocheted daisies: schemes and description

Decorative flowers turn out a little differently . For a flower, gain ten loops in a circle. Next, knit sixteen sc. In the third row, alternate sixteen "flare" columns with two loops. It is on the loops that the petals will be individually knitted according to the scheme:

  • three CCHs;
  • along the edges of the slingshot (two columns with one base), in the middle of the CCH;
  • slingshot (P), three columns, P;
  • seven columns without slingshots;
  • knit two rows without changes;
  • along the edges of the sheaves (two columns with one top), in the middle three CCHs;
  • sheaf, column, sheaf.

Thus, the whole chamomile is crocheted, a sepals master class:

  • make eight loops in a circle;
  • dial sixteen CCH;
  • knit sixteen P;
  • alternate P with three CCHs;
  • now, in turn, knit a slingshot and four columns;
  • form twelve sheaves of five columns.

It will take seven sheets: three small and medium, one large. For a large sheet, dial six loops (taking into account the three lifting loops). Knit two double crochet stitches. Turn over the work.
