Which is more - Mars or Earth? Comparison of the sizes of Mars and the Earth

Since ancient times, mankind has turned its eyes to the stars. But if earlier people turned to celestial bodies only as higher beings, capable of influencing their life with their wonderful properties, now these views have a much more pragmatic character.





Mars in antiquity

The first name the planet received was Ares. So in honor of the god of war they called the red planet, reminding people of the war, the ancient Greeks. At a time when nobody was interested in what was more, Mars or Earth, power was everything. That is why the Greeks were replaced by the ancient Romans. They brought their ideas about the world, life, their names. They also renamed the star, symbolizing evil, cruelty and grief. It was named after the Roman god of war, Mars.

Many centuries have passed since then, it has long been clarified that more, Mars or Earth, it became clear that the planet is far from being as cruel and powerful as the ancient Greeks and Romans imagined, but interest in the planet did not disappear, and every century intensified.

Life on Mars

The first sketch of Mars was made public in 1659 in Naples. Francesco Fontana, a Neapolitan astronomer and lawyer, laid the foundation for a whirlwind of research that has hit the planet through the ages.





Giovanni Schiaparelli in 1877 went around the achievements of the Fountain, making not just a drawing, but making a map of the entire planet. Taking advantage of the Great Confrontation that was taking place, which allowed him to take a close look at Mars, he discovered some channels and dark areas on our solar system neighbor. Without wasting time thinking about which planet is larger: Mars, Earth, humanity decided that these were products of an alien civilization. It began to be considered that canals are irrigation systems that aliens sent to irrigate vegetation zones - those dark areas. Water in the canals, according to the majority, fell from ice caps at the poles of the planet.

The scientist who discovered all these geological objects did not initially mean anything like this. However, over time, under the influence of the enthusiasm of the majority, he believed in such a popular hypothesis. However, already in 1907, a geographer from Great Britain in his book "Are Mars Inhabited?" refuted this theory using all the studies available at that time. He finally proved that the life of highly organized beings is impossible in principle on Mars, despite the fact that Mars is larger than the Earth or smaller.





The truth about channels

They confirmed the existence of direct, like arrows, channels of the planet‚Äôs images in 1924. Surprisingly, most astronomers observing Mars have never seen this phenomenon. Nevertheless, by 1939, by the next Great Confrontation, about 500 channels were counted on the images of the planet.

Everything was finally clarified only in 1965, when the Mariner-4 flew so close to Mars that it was able to photograph it from a distance of only 10 thousand kilometers. These images showed a lifeless desert with craters. All dark zones and channels turned out to be just an illusion caused by distortion during observations with a telescope. There is nothing like this in reality on the planet.

Mars

So still, what's more: Mars or Earth? The mass of Mars is only 10.7% of the mass of the Earth. Its diameter at the equator is almost two times smaller than the earth - 6794 kilometers against 12,756 km. A year on Mars lasts 687 Earth days, a day - 37 minutes longer than ours. There is a change of seasons on the planet, but no one would rejoice in the onset of summer on Mars - this is the harshest season, winds of up to 100 m / s walk around the planet, dust clouds cover the sky, blocking sunlight. However, the winter months also can not please the weather - the temperature does not rise above minus one hundred degrees. The atmosphere consists of carbon dioxide, which in the winter months lies in huge snow caps at the poles of the planet. These hats never completely melt. The density of the atmosphere is only one percent of the earth.

The results of the study prove that once fast streams of water flowed along these rivers.

Research

In the 20th century, not only unmanned space stations were sent to Mars, but Mars rovers were launched, thanks to which it became possible to obtain soil samples from the red planet. Now we have accurate data on the chemical composition of the atmosphere and surface of the planet, the nature of its seasons, and we have photographs of all regions of Mars. NASA's rovers, reconnaissance satellite, and orbiter have a busy working schedule, in which there is literally not a single minute until 2030.
