Ionian Sea (Greece). Historical sights



As mentioned above, the most famous are the islands of Lefkada, Corfu, Ithaca and Kefalonia. Some of them are actually worth telling in more detail.

Snow-white Lefkada (white sand and white rocks gave the name to the island) is connected to the mainland by means of a floating bridge. This island, as a rule, is visited by all tourists. Especially it attracts those interested in the historical past.

From the porthole of Corfu, the island of Corfu seems completely green; when approaching, the landing strip is not even visible. The international airport on this island is the smallest in Europe. In the main city of the island you can see the ruins, which in ancient times were the magnificent temple of Artemis. Historians believe that this building dates back to the VI century BC. You can see the fortress of the XII century, as well as the Cathedral of St. Spyridon (XVI century).

Greece ... The Ionian Sea ... Hotels here never lack tourists. As a rule, rooms are booked in advance. For several years now, such hotels as Albatros Hotel, Kontokali Bay Resort & Spa, Calavier Hotel, Agrepavlis Villa and Aquis Mon Repos Palace have been especially popular.
