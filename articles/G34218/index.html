The first row, from which the scheme of the ‚ÄúScale‚Äù pattern begins (crochet), is knitted like this:

  • double crochet (below will be denoted as CH column) in the 4th loop from the hook;
  • two air;
  • two columns of CH in one base from the seventh loop of the initial chain;
  • continue to alternate the air and columns of CH in every third loop to the end of the row.

All odd rows will be considered purl. Even - facial.

The pattern of the "Scale" (crochet) continues the second row. It starts with three air loops. Then comes this knitting:

  • on the right column of the pair of the previous row, you need to immediately connect 4 columns of CH;
  • air loop;
  • 4 columns of CH on the second of this pair;
  • air loop;
  • repeat the work on each odd pair of columns of the previous row (scales fit through one);
  • in the last such pair, you need to connect the connecting column.

The crochet pattern of the ‚ÄúScale‚Äù pattern continues in the third row. It is performed exactly as the first, but you need to remember to make a climb of three air loops. The same applies to all other odd series.

The fourth series of work is similar to the second. Only need to tie every even pair of columns of CH. And in the first it is necessary to make a connecting column. Then all the work is repeated from the first row.

Openwork pattern "Scales"

So, we start crocheting. The "scale" pattern (diagram and description) starts with a set of chains. Moreover, the number of loops should be divided by eight. In the first row you will need to make one loop for lifting. In the first, connect the connecting column. In the fifth loop, perform nine columns of CH. In the ninth - connecting (from this moment the pattern is repeated, then nine columns of CH will again go). You need to complete the row on the next connecting column in the last loop of the chain.

Second row: with a lifting loop and a column of CH from the base of the lifting; two air; a connecting post in the fifth of nine connected in the previous row; two air; in the connecting column between the two scales of the previous row, make two columns of CH, separated by one air; two aerial and connecting to the top of the fifth. Continue this pattern to the end of the row, which must be completed with two columns of CH in the last loop.

The pattern of the "scale" (hook) continues in the third row:

  • three lifting loops, 4 columns of CH at the base of the lifting chain;
  • a connecting column in the same previous row (located at the top of the scale);
  • 9 columns of CH in the arch from one air loop obtained in the previous row;
  • continue this drawing to the end.

The fourth row repeats the pattern of the second. Only here it is not necessary to make an ascent, but to start from the moment when the connecting column is tied to the top of the scale.
