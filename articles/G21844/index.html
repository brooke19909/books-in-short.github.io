Fire in rome

Where revelry, disaster is inevitable. The best part of the people of the empire was exterminated, the people became impoverished and sank. The result is this: in 64, Rome caught fire. It all started with shops clinging to a circus. Everything that could burn was burning, but almost everything could, since Rome was then basically a wooden city. The streets blazed for six days, then the fire was stopped, but not for long, it flared up again, and another three days blazed. Of the fourteen regions of Rome, only four survived.





Nero inspiredly watched this colorful spectacle and sang songs about the burning Troy. For this, the people accused him of arson of Rome. This is how the biography of the Roman emperor Nero was overgrown with terrible details. Most likely, this is slander, since the emperor has accumulated many ill-wishers. New rome

This time the city was rebuilt according to good architectural and engineering plans: the streets became wide, the houses became stone. Beautiful squares with colonnades, fountains, pools spread everywhere. Construction went quickly, Nero spared no means to restore Rome.

And the new imperial palace surpassed in size and beauty everything that existed hitherto, not only in Rome. It was incredibly magnificent: several huge buildings, distant from each other, but united by colonnades, with artificial ponds, meadows, olive groves and vineyards in the territories between the buildings.

A statue depicting Nero in the form of the sun god adorned the main palace. The "Golden Palace" was called by the Romans this grandiose project of architects Celer and the North. It is a pity that he did not survive to this day, after a dozen years, he also burned down.
