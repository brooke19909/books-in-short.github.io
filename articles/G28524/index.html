A brief history of Greece. The origin of the country

The path of this state is long and difficult. On the modern territory of the country, civilization appeared one of the first in the world. Already in the third millennium BC. e. there were several different cultures: Mycenaean, Cretan and Peloponnesian. For 1500 years, the Cretan was considered the main of them, and later the palm passed to the Mycenaean.

Greece survived many rulers, was ruled by different states, but today it is an independent, unique, colorful country. For a century and a half of its history, before falling under the rule of Rome, the state managed to survive the monarchy, the republic, and even tyranny. In 146 BC e. Greece came under the rule of Rome, and in the 6th century AD e. it became part of the Byzantine Empire and was it until the 13th century.





Development

Interesting facts about Greece also relate to the Crusade, which took place in 1204. During it, the Byzantine Empire collapsed, and the territory of modern Greece was divided into certain counties, the most powerful of which was considered the Athenian.

In 1460, most of the country fell under the rule of the Ottoman Empire, and two hundred years later, the Turks also expelled the Venetian merchants. In 1669, Crete was annexed to the territory of the Ottoman Empire.

Independence

In 1821, the first war for the independence of Greece began, which at the end of the confrontation became known as the Greek Kingdom. Later, in the same century, the country continued to fight for its independence. There were several more wars, as a result of which Greece managed to get back Crete and Macedonia.

In the 20th century, after the Balkan wars, in which Greece also participated, some more lands moved to it. In 1967, "black colonels" came to power in the country. In 1975, a new constitution was adopted here, after which the position of the state was relatively stabilized.

Legends

Ancient Greek culture throughout the world is famous for its legends on a variety of topics. They tell about the gods who lived on Olympus, and about the ancient heroes. The myths about Heracles, whose father was Zeus himself, the main god on Mount Olympus, are considered one of the most famous . An important role among the legends of Greece is played by the cycle of the Trojan War, which tells about all the events of the confrontation caused by the abduction of Helen.





Hercules

The most interesting facts about Greece and the myths of this state primarily relate to the gods of Olympus. The most important god, as mentioned above, was Zeus, and his wife was called Hera. But in the legends, Zeus was not distinguished by devotion and monogamy, so he had many children from different women. He did not deprive his earthly girl Alkmene of his attention, as a result of such a union, the illustrious Hercules was born. Before he was supposed to be born, Zeus said that his child would become the lord of all earthly nations. But Hera, the wife of Zeus, did not like this statement, and she delayed the birth of Hercules, so that the grandson of Perseus was born first, to whom Zeus's son would have to obey.

As soon as the child of God and the earthly woman was born, Hera began her persecution. For example, when he was still quite a baby, she sent two snakes to him that were supposed to kill him, but it turned out the other way around - the child himself strangled them. During his life, the hero accomplished a dozen exploits that glorified his name.
