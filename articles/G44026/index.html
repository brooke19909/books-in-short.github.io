Grace period - what is it?

The grace period, or grace, on a credit card is a time interval that allows the owner to use the bank without interest. The terms of its observance include expenditures without cash withdrawal and depositing funds to the card before the renewal of the limit.

In other words, the owner of the credit card, in order not to pay interest, must pay by card, and not withdraw cash from it. After making a purchase, the customer must deposit all funds until the end of the grace period.

What is a credit card grace period? When defining the term, the main are 2 conditions:

  1. Payment by card.
  2. Renewal of the credit limit.

Failure to comply with at least one of the conditions deprives the client of the opportunity to pay with borrowed funds without interest. The credit card grace period is renewable. After repaying the debt, the user can again make purchases by bank transfer. This will be the beginning of a new grace period.

Features of the grace period

Free credit card use is limited. This is its distinguishing feature. In addition, it is floating, which means the absence of a fixed grace period.

The grace period depends on the start of purchases. It can be minimal, for example, 20 days, as in Sberbank, or reach 100 days without interest (Alfa-Bank).

The end of the period is the day of repayment of the outstanding balance. Banks that offer credit cards to their customers, indicate in advertising only the maximum period, for example, 50 or 100 days without interest.

How to calculate a grace period by credit card: instructions

Calculation of the grace period is a simple task, but necessary for all cardholders. If the client does not know when it is better to make purchases, he risks becoming a debtor. To avoid this, you should study the features of calculating the grace period.

How to calculate the grace period by credit card:

  1. Find out the reporting date.
  2. Remember the day of purchase.
  3. Find out the end date of the grace period.
  4. Subtract from the maximum period of the period the number of days after which the customer made the purchase.

Managers recommend making purchases on or immediately after the report date. In this case, the grace period for a credit card will be maximum. If a client pays with a card a month later, a day before the date of the report, this period will be less by 30 days (1 month is deducted from the maximum period).

Grace extension

Cardholders are not always able to repay the entire amount of debt during the grace period. But paying interest in this case is not necessary. There is an easy way to extend your credit card grace period.

To renew the limit, you need to fully pay off the debt. But a partial deposit of funds in the amount indicated in the SMS from the bank can save the card holder from the need to pay interest. This will extend the period without paying the full amount of debt and interest repayment.
