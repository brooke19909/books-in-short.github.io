Which navigator tourist to choose

A tourist navigator is a device that will be useful not only to lovers of hiking and travel, but also to simple mushroom pickers, hunters and fishermen. In general, to everyone who likes to get out into the countryside. It is the key to confidence in the correct route.



Orienteering skills and the ability to work with topographic maps, of course, are key for any inveterate tourist. Without them, you just can not do on long and long trips. And if you don‚Äôt have such skills, then even a simple walk through the forest can end in disrepair. But there is a way to help novice travelers or simplify the life of already seasoned tourists. This is a tourist navigator.

What to look for when choosing a navigator

Battery capacity

This is the most important factor that you should pay attention to when choosing a device. It must be clearly understood that in the wild, the tourist navigator cannot be recharged, therefore, the large battery capacity is not just a technical indicator of the device, it is first of all confidence in its long continuous operation. An alternative can be battery powered navigators, since spare sets of power supplies can be taken with you.

Ability to edit existing maps and download new ones

New updated maps may contain additional important or simply interesting information, which may turn out to be quite useful during the trip. If the tourist GPS navigator supports editing, then such functionality can be very useful. For example, when climbing a mountain, excess equipment can be left at some specific point, and its coordinates can be entered into the device. Or simply mark the coordinates of places on the map due to various reasons.

Moisture and shock resistance

With this point, and so everything is clear. During a walk, trip or hike, the device may fall, or it may rain, or something else may occur that will adversely affect the functioning of the navigator.

Control

Of course, in this matter, everyone decides what is more convenient for him: mechanical buttons or a touch screen. But keep in mind that touch screens consume much more energy, which means they quickly discharge the power source.

Additional features

Modern technologies, and with them the capabilities of devices, do not stand still. So, the tourist navigator, depending on the model, can have a built-in radio, camera, flashlight, games, mp3 player and much more. All this, of course, is good. But at the same time, the operation of each of these options additionally discharges the battery or batteries.

To date, there are a sufficient number of manufacturers of such equipment that offer a variety of models with different characteristics. Among a large number of devices and brands, outdoor enthusiasts separately highlight Garmin touring navigators. A wide range of products allows you to choose the best option for everyone. And the reliability of the equipment, proven, so to speak, in the field, gives its owner a guarantee in the exact following of the route.
