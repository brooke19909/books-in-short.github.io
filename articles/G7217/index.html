A little bit about the company

The Soviet manufacturer of radio electronics "Vega" was located in the city of Berdsk of the Russian Federation. In principle, there is a plant now, but things are going badly from it. But that's not the point. Previously, the company was called the Berdsky Radio Plant. She was engaged in the production of consumer electronics: amplifiers, cassette decks, turntables, speakers. A little later, the plant mastered the production of CD players. Until the 90s of the last century, Vega products were in almost every home. Her amplifiers enjoyed wild success. Appearance and design

Let's start with the appearance of this amplifier, because it is too early to go to the technical specifications. Usually Soviet devices were heavy and bulky. It's better to keep silent about design. The second design feature is the buttons for switching the playback device. They are quasi-sensory. This means that there will be no familiar click (as in other Soviet amplifiers). Additional options

What else can the Vega 50U-122S amplifier boast? Its characteristics are far from the main thing. The most interesting additional features of this model. What else is characterized by Vega 50U-122S? The amplifier is equipped with a four-band equalizer. It helps make the sound better. It is possible to make the device work in mono mode. Amplifier repair

The Vega 50U-122S amplifier is a fairly reliable device. But he also has the property of failing. But repair of the Vega 50U-122S amplifier does not present any problem for those who are even a little versed in radio parts. What most often fails in this device? Usually these are diodes in the bridges KD202D and KD202K. Due to the voltage drop, they burn out and the amplifier in the future simply refuses to turn on. Replacing them is not difficult. They are in any radio store. First you need to ‚Äúring‚Äù the diodes with a tester to make sure that the problem is in them, then remove the damaged components and solder the others in their place.





However, this is the simplest malfunction. But what if the amplifier turns on, but there is no sound? There may be several problems. However, the most common is the failure of a capacitor. To confirm this hypothesis, you should ring the capacitor with a tester, identify the faulty one and replace it. But remember: never do anything with amplifier boards without a circuit.
