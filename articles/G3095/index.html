Palmarinha Resort 3 * (India, Goa, Calangute): hotel description, reviews of tourists

Of all the states of India, Goa is the smallest. By and large, this is a narrow strip of land that separates the mountains from the sea. However, the nature of the foothills is so amazingly diverse that to go to Goa is considered a golden dream of every traveler. Lakes and waterfalls, islands covered with mangroves, palm groves, which are home to a huge amount of amazing living creatures - all this made this exotic destination popular for recreation.



India, Goa

Hotels in this state are very different from each other. Geographically, the region is divided into North and South Goa. The first, characterized by a high population density, offers tourists accommodation in a few hotels, mainly having a category of 2-3 stars. Due to the fact that the beaches in this part of the Indian coast are covered for the most part with grayish unsightly sand, and the sea is by no means transparent, rest in the region is considered very inexpensive. More lovers of economical holidays come here, as well as foreign students and the poor local population.

Rest spent here is much cheaper than in the south. North Goa is famous for its Arambol beach, which was once chosen by hippies for its natural beauty - virgin sands and stunning sunsets, which can be observed in the month of February.

In South Goa, the sea is cleaner and the sand is golden. There are expensive hotels, buried in the greenery of gardens and with a minimum of 4-5 stars. Here, the rest is relatively expensive, especially judging by Indian standards. Here you can meet the local elite and wealthy foreigners, including Russians.



Hotels

As in any resort in the world, in India, and in Goa in particular, a place to stay must be selected taking into account many factors. This is the location of the hotel, a set of services provided in it, a food system, remoteness from the beach and much more. Despite the fact that Goa is the smallest state in India, there are a great many hotels here, so their selection must be approached with great care.

North Goa, a beach that became popular thanks to hippies, has changed a lot since the late sixties of the last century. On its largest beaches - Vagator and Calangute - annually a huge army of youth gathers, for which neither expensive apartments nor developed infrastructure are important. Five star hotels in North Goa are a rarity. But it is full of hotels for two or three stars with a small territory and low prices. Among them, Palmarinha Resort 3 * occupies not the last place.

general information

This hotel is a good option for those who choose their vacation in an exotic country such as India. Palmarinha Resort 3 * is relatively close to the best Calangute beach. Many tourist sites - restaurants, nightclubs, etc. - are just a ten-minute walk away. According to reviews of tourists, the hotel has a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.



Location Palmarinha Resort 3 * was chosen quite well. It is located in the very center of Bardez in North Goa, fourteen kilometers from the capital, in a small but very green area. The nearest international airport is fifty-five kilometers away, and Thivim Railway Station is a fifteen-minute drive away. Located along Calangute Mapusa Road, the hotel is a kilometer north of attractions such as St. Alex Church.

Reviews

Those traveling to Goa for the first time need to be aware that this is not Turkey or Egypt with their all-inclusive system. India, and especially Goa, is a country with a completely different mentality, with other people and other ideas about the rest.

Judging by the reviews, the hotel Palmarinha Resort 3 * appealed to many Russians. The staff speaks not only Hindi, but also English. The rooms are very comfortable, they have everything you need for a carefree stay, including a refrigerator. Plumbing works without interruption.

The food is quite varied, one breakfast per week may not be repeated. Lots of local sauces, vegetables and fruits. Many Russians chose to eat in shekels - a cafe located on the beach, paying only breakfast for their stay with the hotel.

Among the shortcomings, tourists note a certain remoteness of the beach, as well as paid Wi-Fi and the lack of Russian channels on TV.
