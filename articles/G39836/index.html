Sights of Porvoo, Finland: description, history and interesting facts

Porvoo is a city in Finland, considered the most beautiful in the state. He is second in age in the country. From Helsinki, they arrive here in an hour by car or cruise ship.

About city

On the way to the center, tourists observe the picturesque coastline, neat houses, cobblestone pavements of the medieval type, alleys. Rich in sights of Porvoo (Finland). This place is one of the finest decorations in Finland.



The charter appeared at the city in 1346. Then it was owned by Magnus Ericsson, the Swedish king. It's cozy here now. Porvoonjoki Riverbanks are decorated with ocher barns. Tourists walk along cobblestone pavements along the old part of the city, where the city cathedral is located.

The attention is drawn to the museum of the poet Runeberg, opened in 2004 after the general reconstruction was carried out. The city has long had the status of a major center of trade. This position has remained with him to this day.

In the fall and at Christmas, fairs are visited by a large number of people. Visitors try delicacies, buy works of masters sharing their best practices. Each seller dresses in traditional attire.

City Museum

Tourists are interested in the sights of Porvoo. History is perfectly presented in the City Museum. It is located in the Old Town Hall, built in 1762-1764. Works of applied and artistic art are in abundance.

On the territory of the Old Town, this building is one of the main ones. It has two floors, an attic, a wooden clock tower, located at the highest point of construction. Until the end of the 19th century, the building was used for administrative purposes. In 1896, museum associations opened exhibits here, and the building itself was saved from destruction. In 2011, an exhibition appeared on the subject of the history of antiquity and the Middle Ages.

Hill Merchant House

The sights of Porvoo (Finland) attract a constantly large flow of tourists to the city. They learn a lot about history and traditions by visiting the walls of a house belonging to the merchant Holm. The construction is dated 1762 year. It is made of brick. There used to be a wooden structure, but in 1760 a fire destroyed it. The merchant's family lived here at the end of the 17th century. The owners of this museum tried to preserve the features of life and decoration as much as possible.

The bedrooms are located upstairs. Down below, deals were made, paperwork was conducted. The maid lived in the attic. Being here, visitors visually imagine what Porvoo (Finland) was several centuries ago. Sights of such a plan transfer a tourist to antiquity.

A bakery was opened here in the 19th century, and at the beginning of the 20th century - a museum complex. Now this is a place of frequent exhibitions. Exhibits often change. The museum includes two houses of a residential type in the neighborhood, household buildings. People are not allowed there. The shop sells souvenirs.
