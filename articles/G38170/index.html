Geography

The ancient city of Meissen in Germany is located on the River Elbe, at the confluence of it with Tribisch. The nearest major city is Dresden. These settlements are separated by only 25 km. Meissen is located on a hill and the adjacent plain. From the capital of the country it is separated by about 150 km. Therefore, Meissen is very convenient to visit, a trip to it can be combined with a visit to Dresden or Leipzig.

History

Meissen (Germany) dates back to 929. At this time, the German king Henry I, nicknamed the Ptitselov, ordered the construction of a fortress Misnia on the hill to protect against aggressive Hungarians who were actively expanding their holdings. In 968, led by Meissen, Margrave was formed with its own bishop. The fortified fortress began to attract merchants and artisans who began to settle under its walls. The convenient location on the way from Prague to Leipzig contributed to the rapid growth and prosperity of the city. In the XI century, makgrafsto departed to the Vettin family. This royal family is today represented among the monarchs of Europe by the Windsor branch. In 1250, the city, which resisted the attacks of the Tatar-Mongol yoke, began to build a Catholic cathedral in the style of flaming Gothic. Meissen gradually strengthened, became more influential. In 1423, the local duke received the rank of elector, and this further strengthened the importance of the city. In 1470, the first castle in Germany was erected here.

At the beginning of the XVII century, Augustus the Strong decided to establish porcelain production in Meissen - a very profitable business at that time. And the factory was located in the castle of Albrechtsburg, in which no one lived anyway. The factory worked in the castle until 1864, until its own building was built for it. At the end of the 19th century, Meissen became a large industrial and commercial center. Two World Wars spared the city, and it preserved many historical buildings. Today Meissen is known as the city of china. This is the main local enterprise. At the beginning of the XXI century, Meissen was badly affected by the flood, but now the city is put in order.

sights

Today, tourists are attracted to Meissen by Albrechtsburg Castle. In Germany, this is exactly the iconic place where the famous porcelain appeared. However, there are other interesting sights in this city. Although the object number 1, of course, is the castle. Today it has been restored, and it houses the Porcelain Museum. The second most important attraction is the Meissen Cathedral. Together with the castle, it forms a unique architectural ensemble of the city. The cathedral is considered an example of the purest German Gothic and is included in all world directories telling about this style. Another attraction is the market square, or rather, the Town Hall building, built in the 15th century. The deer house, a market pharmacy and residential buildings of the 16th-17th centuries attract the attention of the square. Also distinguished by their special architecture:
