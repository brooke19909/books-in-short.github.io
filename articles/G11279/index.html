Car recorder DOD F900LHD: reviews and features

Inspectors of the traffic police - the owners of the road. But if controversial situations arise, a law-abiding driver can prove his case with the help of a witness who is always there. So for many, the F900LHD has become a car DVR that captures everything that happens on the go.



The data recorded by this technical device can often be seen not only on YouTube video hosting, but also in special television projects devoted to traffic situations.

Recently, counterfeit devices manufactured by DOD have become more frequent. The appearance of cheap copies seriously spoiled the image of the F900LHD. Reviews about the registrar are increasingly negative. In order for you not to join the ranks of offended users, buy the device only from official DOD representatives. Addresses and phone numbers of specialized centers and online stores that officially sell original certified products can be found on the Russian-language page of DOD Tech, in the "Where to Buy" section. It is noteworthy that this resource pleases its readers with a page under the heading "Where it is not worth buying." I would also like to see the interactive section on the F900LHD. User reviews would give an opportunity to evaluate the original device to those who are going to purchase it. Nevertheless, it is gratifying that company representatives in Russia published their contact details, using which you can tell where the fakes were bought.



The original Taiwanese DOD F900LHD, an overview of the possibilities of which is brought to your attention, is even known today by judges who are involved in cases involving abuse of authority by traffic cops.

The technical device is single-channel, that is, it allows you to record events through only one camera.

The DVR is equipped with a built-in microphone and a rotating display.

The wide-angle lens gives a good view of one hundred and twenty degrees. Whatever the overall writing about the F900LHD, reviews about the lens are more often positive. This optics allows you to clearly capture everything that happens near the car, and when shooting long distances only slightly distorts the image at the edges. But this is the flaw of all wide-angle lenses. The creators of the registrar as much as possible compensated for this minus of optics using electronics.

Externally, the device looks like a video camera. This is probably why the F900LHD reviews are often erroneous. Some classifies it as a camcorder, although in this model there are the main distinguishing features of a car recorder: recording starts as soon as external power appears, and is cyclical.



The recorder screen can be rotated three hundred and sixty degrees. Optics unfolds a hundred and eighty degrees.

The lens of the device has a good depth of field. A seven-millimeter lens works in the range from ten centimeters to infinity.
