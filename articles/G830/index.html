The principle of the formation of braids

Any harness connected by knitting needles is formed by moving several loops. More precisely, the loops do not just move, but swap with adjacent elements. For example, a classic bundle of two strands is performed as follows:

  • The loops of the first strand (P # 1) are removed from the left knitting needle to the auxiliary knitting needle.
  • The loops of the second lock (P # 2) are knitted with facial ones. At the same time, Pos. 1 remain before work.
  • Pos. 1 is transferred to the left knitting needle and is also knit with facial ones.

The formation of more complex braids

It should be noted that both the first and second strands can contain absolutely any number of loops: from one or more.

Traditionally, braids knitted with facial loops are placed on a canvas made with purl loops. More complex ornaments involve interlocking locks of strands with base loops. That is, instead of P # 2 there will be purl loops of the base.

Any patterns with a component such as a ‚Äúbraid‚Äù will be derived from the classic harness.

Sequence of steps

First of all, the required number of loops (a multiple of one rapport) is gained on the needles. Then knit all the loops facial (first row). The second row, like all the wrong ones, is performed according to the drawing. In the third row, weaving loops begins - so the ‚Äúbraid with shadow‚Äù pattern is knitted. Seventh row: the loops of the first and second strands are knitted with facial loops, Pr No. 3 is removed to the auxiliary knitting pin or pin and left behind the working knitting needles, Pr No. 4 is knitted, then Pr No. 3 is returned to the left knitting needle and also knit.
