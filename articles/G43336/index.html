Sho-Me 525. Anti-radar "Show-Mi": a review of the model, customer reviews and experts

The fines for traffic violations are higher and higher every year. Even for headlights turned off during daylight hours, drivers will have to pay several hundred rubles, to say nothing of larger violations. Therefore, they go to all sorts of tricks, trying to minimize contacts with the traffic police. Some try to follow the rules, others use a radar detector.





A radar detector is a special device that notifies the driver that a radar is used nearby to determine speed. Thus, the owner of the vehicle manages to slow down and passes a controlled section of the road without any problems. In addition, the function of the radar detector is to create interference that precludes accurate determination of vehicle speed.

Among existing products, a significant share of the market is occupied by South Korean models under the show-Mi brand. For 15 years now, the company has been launching devices on the CIS market warning of speeding violations, which have been repeatedly recognized by experts as the best in their segment.





One of the most popular models produced by this company is the Sho-Me 525 radar detector. Let us consider in more detail its characteristics and features, its advantages and disadvantages, taking into account the opinions of customers.

Description

This is a budget radar detector used to identify all types of radars in the service of the traffic police. The device distinguishes between the work of Iskra, Falcon, Binar, Vizir, Radis, Arena, Chris-P and more modern laser video detectors for violations of Amat and LEADS. This is a compact model that does not take up much space on the windshield. The length of the Sho-Me 525 is 10.6 cm, the width is 6.7 cm. Weight is only 115 g. Unlike many models on the market, this device is capable of detecting one of the most formidable radars - Strelka, which it only automatically measures the speed, but at the same time conducts video recording of the car with the determination of its number.

Ranges



Sho-Me 525 operates in all known ranges (X, Ultra X, K, Ultra K), including those that are rarely found on Russian roads (Ka, Ku). The radar detector can also recognize modes with short-term operation of the radar Instant-On, POP, F-POP. As mentioned earlier, it detects radiation from laser detectors. The viewing angle, like most modern detectors, is 360 degrees.

Modes

As you know, the abundance of different-frequency radiation within the city is simply huge. A large number of signals can easily confuse a sensitive radar so that it will fire at every corner. That is why some models, including the Sho-Me 525, are equipped with the ‚ÄúCity‚Äù mode, in which the sensitivity of the devices decreases, thereby reducing the number of false positives. In this model, 2 such modes are set right away, tuned to different levels. Conversely, on a country road or motorway, when the probability of catching an extraneous signal is extremely low, the sensitivity of the radar detector should be maximized in order to catch the signals issued by the traffic police speed locks. It is for working in such conditions that the ‚ÄúRoute‚Äù mode is intended.

Model Features



This model uses the new Intelligent anti-falshing algorithmTM. As a signal receiver, superhetardine, a processed hetordin and a mixer are used, which made it possible to improve the detection characteristics of short-pulse signals. A biconvex sensitive capacitor lens is used to detect laser signals.

Appearance

The Show Mi anti-radar comes in a plastic case, completely black or with silver accents. On different sides of the case are the control buttons for the device:

  1. Volume on / off button (located on the side).
  2. Display with LED icons (located at the end).
  3. Antenna and lenses for receiving laser signals (from the other end).
  4. Dimming the panel brightness (on the upper plane).
  5. The button of inclusion of the City and Silent mode.
  6. Input for 12 V power cord (side).

Several symbols of different colors are arranged in a row on the display, which help to navigate in the selected modes and ranges of determined speed locks.

Character Values

  1. The P / L indicator, illuminated in yellow, indicates that the Sho-Me STR 525 radar detector is on. If it blinked during operation, it means that the device caught the signal of the laser speed lock.
  2. The X indicator is highlighted in red - the device in the X range works nearby.
  3. The Ku icon lights up green - the device has caught a Ku-band radar.
  4. The K indicator lights up yellow - there is a device nearby operating in the K range.
  5. The letters Ka glow in red - the Sho-Me 525 radar detector has caught a device operating in the Ka range.
  6. The letters St lit up in red on the display - in the range of ‚Äúvisibility‚Äù of the device, the Arrow video recording system was detected.
  7. The C1 icon is highlighted in red - the City 1 mode is on.
  8. The C2 icon is highlighted in yellow - City 2 is on.




The color designation of the range of operation of the recognized radars does not limit the capabilities of the Sho-Me 525. The instruction states that when a radar is detected, an audible alert is activated. If two or more signal sources are detected, priority is given to laser signals and only then to radar signals, including the Strelka complex.

Equipment

Complete with the device are Velcro, power cord and bracket for mounting on the windshield with suction cups.

Mounting methods and inclusion

There are 2 ways to mount the Sho-Me 525 radar detector. The instructions say that it can be installed on the dashboard with Velcro or attached to the windshield with suction cups.

In the first case, the protective film is removed from the Velcro . One stick on the bottom of the radar detector, leaving the serial number open. The other is attached to the dashboard at a previously selected and cleaned place from dirt and dust.

In the second case, put the suction cups on the bracket and attach them to the windshield (the bracket can be bent if necessary). They don‚Äôt put on the device, insert one end of the power cord into the radar detector and the other into the cigarette lighter. In this case, the radar detector will turn on, and the self-testing phase will begin, which can be determined by a series of sound and light signals. At the end of the self-test, the P / L icon will turn yellow on the display.

Volume control

Since an excessively loud sound signal can interfere with the driver or annoy, there is a need to make it quieter. Conversely, a person with a hearing loss is required to set it to the maximum in order to respond to the signal in time. You can adjust the sound volume using the side wheel: for this, you need to turn it left and right until it starts to arrange. If necessary, you can completely turn off the signal or turn on the automatic muting mode.

Display brightness control





The display backlight in this device can be adjusted in 3 positions:

  1. Dim (dimmed brightness) - the mode is recommended for use in the evening, at night or on a cloudy day.
  2. Bright - Used in daylight.
  3. Dark - the display turns on only when the Sho-Me 525 radar detector picks up a signal.

Switching from mode to mode occurs when you press the Dark button.
