339 rifle division: composition, features, awards and interesting facts

The 339th infantry division played a significant role in the victory over Nazi Germany. This unit was one of the most combat-ready on the Crimean and other fronts. Fighters took part in many decisive battles of World War II.



They liberated Soviet land from the Caucasus to Lviv and invaded Germany. For military merit, the division bears the honorary title "Red Banner".

Creature

339 rifle division was created at the beginning of World War II. Immediately after the Nazi Germany attack on the Soviet Union, mobilization began in the country. New units were created, which afterwards often immediately rushed into battle. In September, the mobilization center of the new division subordinated to the ninth army moved to Rostov. 339 rifle divisions were assigned the role of a reserve connection. The fighters were trained in Novocherskassk. Mostly recruits consisted of the local population. Therefore, the division was supposed to be in Rostov-on-Don. The command of the military district devoted special attention to the formation of units. Armament and some tactical decisions took into account the features of the steppe area.

Composition of 339 rifle divisions

In total, the division had 16 divisions. This is taking into account various logistics and service groups. Many battle regiments bore the names of their cities. The core of the division were three rifle regiments. They were armed with rifles, PPSh machine guns, machine guns, hand grenades and mortars. The cover was provided by an artillery regiment equipped with howitzers and multiple launch rocket systems. Also, the 339th rifle division included a separate anti-tank division.

The structure included a reconnaissance battalion, a chemical protection company, and sappers. Other units performed auxiliary functions: transportation, food delivery, provision of medicines and so on. The division was commanded by Alexander Pykhtin.

Baptism of fire

After the failure of the defense of Kiev, the Germans quickly advanced to the East. By the fall, they had already launched an offensive in the Crimea.





Kharkov was besieged, advanced units overlooked the Donbass. In early October, Soviet divisions covering the Rostov area were surrounded. As a result of the fighting, the eighteenth army suffered a serious defeat. The southern front was liquidated. The catastrophic situation developed in all directions. Under the threat of occupation was Rostov-on-Don, Voroshilovgrad (Lugansk) and other settlements. In order to somehow delay the advance of the Nazis, the command threw all reserves into battle.

As a result, the 339th Infantry Division was introduced to hold the line of defense. At that time, stopping the offensive was an extremely important strategic task. On other fronts, the situation was similar. Therefore, the soldiers of the division were thrown into battle practically from the echelons. Weapons were issued only upon arrival at the front line. But from the rear, replenishment slowly approached. An acute shortage of anti-tank guns forced the command to issue exhibits from museums to the soldiers. So, with the weapons of the Civil War 339, the rifle division went into battle.

Counterattack

After breaking through the front, Soviet troops were rapidly retreating. The command ordered to cover Rostov-on-Don. 339 rifle divisions were ordered to gain a foothold in the suburbs. However, the situation was developing rapidly. Inspired by the Germans attacked the city with huge forces. Therefore, the command decided to leave Rostov. A few days later the Germans entered it.

On November 5, the Red Army launched a counterattack.





From several fronts, the forces of three armies, Soviet troops launched an attack on Rostov. The 339th division stormed the city with special zeal, since a significant part of the personnel came from these places. On November 27, the German defenses were broken. The forces of the two fronts struck towards each other, trying to surround the German group. Two days later the city was liberated. The success of the operation greatly encouraged Soviet soldiers throughout the country, as this was one of the first successful offensives. The soldiers of the 339th again took up the defense along the Mius river.

Retreat

However, everything changed in July of the forty-second. The Germans launched a large-scale offensive. The division began to retreat. After the defeat of the Southern Front, it was transferred to the subordination of the forty-seventh army. By the end of the summer, the division took up defense in the Caucasus.

The fighting took place in extremely difficult mountain conditions. Although the personnel of the Rostov division was located on a relatively flat area, the new climate affected the health of some fighters. The German offensive continued until winter. All this time, the soldiers held a stubborn defense.





But the fate of the front was not decided here, but near Stalingrad. After the defeat there, German troops began to retreat rapidly. Fearing the environment, they left the Caucasus and the Kuban. After this, a large-scale counterattack of the Red Army began. The soldiers of the 339th division liberated Taman and Kerch.

Liberation of Crimea

An airborne operation was undertaken to transfer to the peninsula. Soviet troops landed in the Kerch port and immediately rushed into battle. As a result, units of the Wehrmacht and the Romanian armies suffered a crushing defeat and retreated. The distinguished soldiers of 339 rifle divisions were awarded orders and medals.

After the liberation of the city, preparations began for a large-scale offensive on the entire peninsula. Division soldiers participated in the offensive from the first days. In April, Soviet troops took Sevastopol into the ring and began to prepare for its attack. However, numerous assault attempts were unsuccessful. On May 5, a decisive offensive began. After four days of heavy fighting, the Red Army still managed to free Sevastopol.

Offensive on Germany

After the complete liberation of Soviet land, the soldiers of the 339th division began to liberate western Europe.





As part of the Belarusian Front, they participated in the defeat of the German armies that occupied Poland. As the Soviet soldiers retreated to the forty-first in the Donbass, the Germans fled from them in the forty-fifth. Every day, the Red Army advanced several tens of kilometers. In less than a month, almost all of Poland was liberated, and the advanced units reached Oder. The Soviet fighters performed some operations together with the Polish partisans.

Storming Berlin

The last operation of the division ended the war.





On April 16, Soviet troops went on the offensive. Bloody battles continued for twenty-three days. On May 8, Berlin fell, the Second World War in Europe ended. The 339th infantry division met the end of the war on the Elbe along with the troops of the United States of America.
