Poker combinations for beginners

So, you decide to learn how to play poker. The first task for you is to learn poker combinations. It will not be difficult, since there are not so many of them, and even more so, they are remembered very simply.





Consider all the poker combinations that exist:

  • the rarest and at the same time the most successful combination is called a royal flush. This is a combination of five suited high cards;
  • the lower combination in terms of play is a straight flush. She plays when the player has five suited cards in a row;
  • square. Such a combination occurs much more often than the two previous ones. It represents four cards, necessarily peer-to-peer;
  • full house. In this case, the player must have two cards of the same rank and three other cards in his hands. For example, three kings and two sevens;
  • flash. A flash is any sequence of cards necessarily suited;
  • straight. This combination is played when the player has five consecutive cards of any suit;
  • troika. In this case, the player must have three peer cards in his hands;
  • two pairs. This combination combines two cards of the same rank and two more completely different ones. The suit is not taken into account;
  • couple. This combination is very simple. It consists of only two peer cards;
  • "senior" card. This is not even a combination, but simply the outcome of the game. If no player has played any of the above combinations, then the player is determined by the seniority of the cards.




Poker combinations are simple and fast to remember. But it is important to remember that poker has several varieties. These are horse, draw poker, stud, Texas Hold'em, Omaha, double discarding, draw poker, lowball from two to seven, and badugi. All combinations in poker are the same for any kind. Only strategies and rules differ.

One of the most popular variations of club poker is Texas Hold'em. This is due to the fact that the game optimally correlates the general and hidden information. Texas Hold'em combinations are standard.
