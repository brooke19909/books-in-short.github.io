Postage stamp - what is it?

As soon as serious postal services began to function in the world, their organizers immediately asked the question: "How can I determine if a particular postal service has been paid?" And so the postage stamp appeared - a special sign with its face value, which is a fact of confirmation that a particular postal service has been paid.





A postage stamp is a small piece of rectangular paper with a specific (notched) border. It usually contains information about the face value of the brand itself, as well as the postal service number. On any brand there is also a certain drawing with an inscription, thanks to which these products have become an object of interest for collectors.

Types of Postage Stamps

There are several main types of postage stamps:

  • official (state-established sample);
  • unofficial;
  • stamps of private postal services.
