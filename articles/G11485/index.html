Modern valuable coins of Russia: rare and jubilee specimens

Our planet is inhabited by a huge number of people. And almost every third person has a favorite hobby, which is dedicated to free minutes from work. At the same time, sometimes a favorite pastime becomes a way to get additional or basic income.

Numismatics as a way to generate income

For example, numismatics. The habit of collecting old and modern, but no less valuable coins from Russia and the whole world, acquired in childhood or over the years, can eventually turn an ordinary person into a millionaire. Of course, true numismatists will never wish to voluntarily part with their collection. There are cases when, even in extreme need, collectors were ready to starve, but did not want to part with their treasure.





In almost every country where there has been or is minting, there are valuable coins. Russia owns a vast territory and a long exciting history of development. It is the coins of ancient minted yards (imperial, royal) that are used by numismatists around the world as a priority. Collectors are ready to lay out a rather round amount in order to get another rarity in their storehouse.

The development of engineering and technology has allowed people with similar interests to unite. A positive point was the simplified search for the seller or buyer of the product of interest.

Commemorative coins

This list, which includes valuable coins of Russia, does not end there. There are several more species that will be interesting to numismatists. These include defective and valuable commemorative coins of Russia. The last category includes:

1. Two-ruble coin of 2001, on which Yuri Gagarin is depicted on the reverse side. Moreover, only those specimens on which the minting place is not indicated are of value. The absence of a label looks to the right, from the very edge of the number "2". If there is no sign, the value of the coin automatically flies up to 4700 rubles.

2. In 2010, in honor of the beginning of a new phase of life in Chechnya, the Bank of Russia issued a jubilee coin with a face value of 10 rubles. This banknote is made from brass and cupronickel. The cost of such a coin is 2650 rubles. Moreover, the price of it is growing annually.

3. A ten-ruble coin issued in 2010 by the Bank of Russia in honor of the Yamal-Nenets Autonomous Okrug of the country. Made of an alloy of brass and cupronickel, it gradually increases its value. At the moment, the price of it is about 9000 rubles.

Be careful: maybe in a bank where you put a trifle, a coin is hiding that can fulfill your cherished dreams.
