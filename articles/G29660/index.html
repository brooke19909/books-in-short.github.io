Bitsevsky park: the charm of a forest fairy tale

Bitsevsky Park (forest, forest park) is a well-known territory in the south-west of Moscow, occupying more than 2,200 ha. A specially protected zone was created in 1992, with the initiators of the reserve organization being residents of microdistricts located on the territory of Chertanovo, Zyuzino, Konkovo, etc.

The territory occupied today by Bitsevsky Park has been known to historians and archaeologists since ancient times. Finnish-Ugric tribes lived in the local forests, later the Vyatichi tribes, and after them the first villages appeared around the forest on the Bitsa River.

Moscow. Bitsevsky park: tasks of the forest reserve

Forest park zone is a place that carries the most important environmental load. His tasks are as follows:



  • Protection and monitoring of the natural environment, flora and fauna.
  • Preservation of the natural landscape and climate.
  • Development of the latest environmental practices and their implementation.
  • Environmental education of the population.
  • Restoration of ecological balance and leveling of harmful effects caused by human activities.
