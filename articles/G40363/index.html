How to learn to knit: simple instructions for beginners

It's nice to put on a warm soft sweater in the cold season and clutch a cup of tea in your hands, watching the snowflakes soaring quietly outside the window. And you can get double pleasure if you create this sweater yourself. But if you do not know how to do this? An article on how to learn how to knit, for beginners will be very useful and will help greatly facilitate this process.



Materials and Tools

If the decision to get to know this fascinating hobby is made, then the first thing you should learn about is materials and tools. To learn how to knit from scratch, you need to at least roughly know what these knitting needles are and what types they are.

The simplest are straight knitting needles. They are always sold in pairs and are numbered accordingly according to thickness. As a rule, manufacturers specify the recommended thickness for any type of yarn. On such knitting needles you can knit almost everything.

Stocking knitting needles differ from straight ones in length and in that they are represented in the set by five pieces. They are intended, as a rule, for knitting socks and hats. Circular knitting needles combine the virtues of the first two types. They differ in that they are interconnected by a special "cable".



It is worth noting that the knitting needles themselves are made of various materials: wood, plastic, metal, and in the process of creation the needlewoman herself determines the best option for herself.

For beginners, the best option is to purchase woolen, semi-woolen or acrylic yarn. These types are not so capricious in work, as, for example, cotton or cashmere. And in order for the first stage of the How to Learn to Knit Knitting lesson for beginner needlewomen to succeed, it is best to make a yarn-knitting pair of circular knitting needles with a marking of 3.5 mm and half-woolen yarn of the corresponding thickness.

First steps

In the development of how to learn to knit, it will be useful for beginner needlewomen to master three basic techniques: a set of loops, wrong and front loops.

Recruitment is quite simple. A small piece of thread is clamped with the middle finger, ring finger and little finger to the palm. The thread coming from the skein wraps an index finger around the outside and is wound behind the big one from the inside. Then it again returns to the center of the palm and clamps along with the segment left. Next, two knitting needles are taken into the right hand and wound under a loop near the thumb, ‚Äúdive‚Äù into the loop at the index and are brought out again through the loop at the thumb. The resulting loop is pulled by the movement of the spokes "on itself".

As soon as the required amount is typed, you can begin to master the facial loops. To do this, take the knitting needle with the loops in the right hand, and the free one in the left. The thread should be on the knitting needle. A loose knitting needle fits into the stitch loop from its inside, grabs the thread and passes it through the stitch, forming a new loop on the left knitting needle.

In order to master the wrong side, the same actions should be performed, only the thread should go from the inside.



How to read schemes?

Once the skills of recruiting and knitting the main loops are obtained, you can begin to create small masterpieces. This can be done by studying the scheme. As a rule, they are given in graphic or text form. The last option for beginner needlewomen is preferable, since everything is painted there accessible.

But if there is only a graphic diagram, before proceeding with the knitting, it must be read. And they are read in the Arabic manner, that is, from right to left. In addition, you should carefully study the numbering: if only odd numbers are indicated in it , it means that only front rows are present in the scheme, and the wrong ones must be knitted with the exact opposite. It is also worth remembering that the first steps are best done in schemes where there are symbols, and they are mainly composed of front and back loops.

These simple tips can be given to those who are interested in how to learn how to knit. For beginner needlewomen, they will be very useful.
