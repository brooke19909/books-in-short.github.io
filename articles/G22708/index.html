"Kauni" (yarn): description, composition, manufacturer, types and reviews

Yarn for hand knitting has recently gained great popularity among needlewomen around the world. And no wonder, because with your own hands you can create exactly the thing that will fully meet expectations, will become unique. The article is about one of the famous products for knitting, yarn "Kauni".





"Kauni" - yarn for aesthetes

Want a light and airy thing for fall or winter? It's just with Kauni. Foreign-made yarn helps to create truly colorful and stylish things that are relevant for bold fashionistas. A huge selection of colors allows you to stand out from the gray crowd.

Many buyers are attracted to one of the main varieties of Kauni - Artistic yarn with smooth transitions of colors. This is not only beautiful, but also convenient - you do not need to buy several different shades of thread, take a long time to think about how to make a smooth transition. Everything is ready in one skein "Kauni".

Composition and properties

Yarn "Kauni" - 100% high-quality sheep wool. Like all types of wool yarn, it is more prickly to the touch, but very warm, so it is suitable for knitting winter clothes. "Kauni" is very light, the products are almost weightless.

Threads are twisted in skeins for transportation. But for work it is better to untwist them and make a ball.

The yarn is quite resistant to wear. With proper care of the thing (washing in cool water by hand or in delicate mode in the machine and drying in a horizontal position), it lasts a long time.

Colors do not fade; for a long time they remain as bright as they were originally.





Main characteristics

Yarn "Kauni" is sectionally dyed and plain. The thickness of the threads also varies: per 100 grams of 800 meters single thread and 100 grams of 400 meters double. The first is best used for more airy and lighter things - scarves, shawls, the second - for thick ones. Sometimes they are combined in one product. So it turns out a piece of clothing with "breathable", openwork inserts.

Sections in colored yarn are different. Sometimes their length is small (where there are less than 4 colors, for example, Artistic Rainbow) and very long, reaching 100 meters (Artistic Lavender), which allows a very smooth transition of color.

What to bind?

As already mentioned, products made from Kauni yarn are much lighter (1.5-2 times than the usual things from wool and synthetics). It is suitable for machine and hand knitting. For example, for knitting a shawl or stole you need thin knitting needles No. 2-3.5 or a hook, for sweaters - knitting needles No. 5-6.

From 80 grams of thin ‚ÄúKauni‚Äù you can get a small scarf, from 150 grams - a shawl. From a thicker yarn (400/100) this item will require up to 200 grams. The rest of the yarn consumption is as follows:

  • a thin women's jacket of medium size (M) - 210 g;
  • sweater of the same size - 300 g;
  • men's sweater (48 size) - 400 g;
  • medium length dress - 400 g;
  • small crocheted plaid - 800 g.

The use of larger knitting needles, openwork viscous reduce material consumption. Tight knitting, braids and the presence of collars - increase.

As you can see, this yarn has a very wide range of applications. From it you can knit everything you need - from hats and scarves to skirts and cardigans. Especially popular in recent years are knitted accessories - belts, handbags, jewelry.

Yarn "Kauni": reviews

Reviews of customers who have tried Kauni most often relate to the abundance of flowers and the naturalness of the thread. Warmth, originality of things - this is what you often hear about this yarn. They also say that the drawing of the thing retains its shape even after washing and prolonged wear.

Owners of sensitive skin notice that the yarn stabs the body. To get rid of this, it is recommended to add a little conditioner and glycerin to the water when rinsing things. To prevent the villi from sticking together, fluffiness remained, while rinsing, you can also add citric acid or a weak solution of vinegar. It is better to dry the finished thing in a horizontal position, fixing its edges so that it retains its original shape.
