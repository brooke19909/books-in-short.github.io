How to make a Freddy mask out of cardboard

First you need to make the basis for the mask in the form of a half volume oval or ellipse. First, cut strips 2 cm wide and 42 cm long. Measure the circumference of your head with a tape measure. Cut one strip according to this volume and glue it into a circle. Fix the remaining strips in a semicircle to the base, which corresponds to the volume of the head. This is convenient to do with a stapler.

Similarly, make the shield oblong shape. He will cover his face. It must be cut into strips from below. They should reach the place where you plan to begin to expand the muzzle. Tape the mask with paper, old newspapers or magazines.

Freddy's eyes

Cut a suitable sized plastic ball into two. Tape it with white paper. Cut holes for the eyes in the shield and glue the mask again.

Lower jaw

Turn a small saucer over and place cardboard around it, connecting it into a ring. Fill the circle with newspaper strips from one edge to the other. Form gums from the newspaper.

Make Freddy's hat using a cylindrical base. For example, a small flower pot. Paste it with white paper to paint it later.

To form the jaw, take two toilet paper bushings. Attach them to the shield under the holes for the eyes and glue. Glue the lower jaw again for strength.

Attach the fields to the hat. Glue the upper jaw again. Shape the gums. Try on the lower jaw.

We make teeth out of matchboxes by wrapping them with paper. Make cuts in the jaws and insert them. We proceed to the formation of eyebrows. They can be made of tubes, twisted personally from paper. Make them and cut them into two. Glue your eyebrows.





Paint the hat black. The mask itself is painted brown inside and out. We make the nose black, and the teeth white. We attach eyeballs to the back and draw the pupils. Draw Freddy's ears on the cardboard and stick them. So we figured out how to make a Freddy mask.
