Perception is what?

Every day we are attacked by various irritants: faces, news broadcasts, buildings, advertising, conversations, even if we take into account what we hear or see from this a very small part. I wonder why this is happening. The answer is because of perception. You should know that perception is a special process of obtaining all incoming information through the five senses of a person, awareness and giving it specific meaning. It is caused by the influence of 3 components:

- physical characteristics of various stimuli;

- the ratio of such stimuli with their environment;

- special moods of the individual.

It is the last component that makes our perception individual. After all, each personality considers the existing stimuli in its own coordinate system. They often depict things that can be associated with the outside world. But the total number of all stimuli affecting us complicates the whole process of perception even more. We can absorb some of them completely, others only partially, some correctly, and some incorrectly. In the end, we neglect some stimuli and choose others, because we are simply not able to perceive all the information coming to the brain at once.

We select all impressions on two levels: external and internal. Internal selection always occurs for physiological and psychological reasons. Psychological selection, due to our feelings, experiences, or interests, makes us completely distort specific incentives or ignore them at all.

When perception occurs (this is a prerequisite), motor components take part in it (eye movement and palpation of objects; speaking or singing of appropriate sounds). So perception will most correctly be designated as the perceptual activity of the subject.

Types of perception

All people feel the surrounding world in different ways, but, nevertheless, they distinguish 3 main types of perception:

  • auditory (perception of this happens with the help of sounds);
  • visual (through pictures, images);
  • kinesthetic (using tactile sensations, feelings).

People visuals basically see the world, people audio hear it, but kinesthetics feel it. These three types of perception help us make decisions, suggest how to respond to certain situations that occur around us. Therefore, they can even be called types of thinking.

Naturally, the audio / visual / kinesthetic system seems a bit simplified. After all, no one can perceive reality one-sidedly, with the help of just one type. A person, as it were, switches from one type of perception to another and vice versa (which depends on what is happening around the situation).
