One of the most famous, distinguished chemical scientists who made an invaluable contribution to the development of organic chemistry, was the Russian man A. Butlerov. We will consider his works, merits and results of the work briefly in this article.

The theory of the structure of organic compounds A. M. Butlerova

The XIX century is marked by the rapid development of almost all sciences, including chemistry. In particular, extensive discoveries on carbon and its compounds continue to accumulate, organic substances strike everyone with their diversity. However, no one dares to systematize and streamline all this factual material, lead to a common denominator and identify common laws on which everything is built.

Butlerov A.M. was the first to do this. It was he who owned the ingenious theory of the chemical structure of organic compounds, the provisions of which he spoke massively at the German conference of chemists. This was the beginning of a new era in the development of science, organic chemistry embarked on the path of intensive development.

The scientist himself went towards this gradually. He conducted many experiments and predicted the existence of substances with desired properties, discovered some types of reactions and saw the future for them. He studied the works of his colleagues and their discoveries a lot. Only against this background, through careful and painstaking labor, did he manage to create his own masterpiece. And now the theory of the structure of organic compounds in this section of chemistry is practically the same as the periodic system in inorganic.





Discovery of the scientist before creating the theory

What discoveries were made and theoretical justifications given to scientists before the theory of the structure of organic compounds by A. M. Butlerov appeared?

  1. The domestic genius was the first to synthesize such organic substances as urotropin, formaldehyde, methylene iodide and others.
  2. He synthesized a sugar-like substance (tertiary alcohol) from an inorganic substance, thereby inflicting another blow to the theory of vitalism.
  3. He predicted the future for polymerization reactions, calling them the best and most promising.
  4. Isomerism was explained for the first time only to them.

Of course, these are only the main milestones of his work. In fact, the many years of painstaking work of a scientist can be described for a long time. However, the theory of the structure of organic compounds has become the most significant today, the provisions of which will be discussed later.

The first position of the theory

In 1861, the great Russian scientist at the congress of chemists in Speyer shares with his colleagues his views on the causes of the structure and variety of organic compounds, expressing all this in the form of theoretical provisions.

The very first point is the following: all atoms within a single molecule are connected in a strict sequence, which is determined by their valency. In this case, the carbon atom exhibits a valence index of four. Oxygen has a value of this indicator, equal to two, hydrogen - unit.

He proposed calling this feature the chemical structure of matter. Later, the notation was used to express it on paper using graphical full structural, abbreviated and molecular formulas.





The phenomenon of the connection of carbon particles with each other into infinite chains of different structures (linear, cyclic, branched) also applies.

In general, Butlerov‚Äôs theory of the structure of organic compounds determined by his first position the significance of valency and a single formula for each compound, which reflects the properties and behavior of a substance during reactions.

The second position of the theory

In this paragraph, an explanation was given to the diversity of organic compounds in the world. Relying on the carbon compounds in the chain, the scientist suggested that there are unequal compounds in the world that have different properties, but at the same time completely identical in molecular composition. In other words, there is a phenomenon of isomerism.

The theory of the structure of organic compounds by A. M. Butlerov not only clarified the essence of isomers and isomers with this provision, but the scientist himself confirmed everything by practical experience.

So, for example, he synthesized a butane isomer - isobutane. Then he predicted for pentane the existence of not one but three isomers, based on the structure of the compound. And synthesized them all, proving their innocence.





Disclosure of the third position

The next point of the theory says that all atoms and molecules within the same compound are able to influence the properties of each other. The nature of the behavior of the substance in reactions of various types, the manifested chemical and other properties will depend on this.

Thus, on the basis of this position, several classes of organic compounds are distinguished that differ in the type and structure of the functional determining group.

The theory of the structure of organic compounds by A. M. Butlerov is summarized in almost all textbooks on organic chemistry. After all, it is she who is the basis of this section, an explanation of all the laws on which molecules are built.

The value of theory for the present

Of course, it is great. This theory allowed:

  1. to unite and systematize all the factual material accumulated by the time of its creation;
  2. explain the laws of structure, properties of various compounds;
  3. give a full explanation of the reasons for such a wide variety of compounds in chemistry;
  4. gave a start to numerous syntheses of new substances based on the provisions of the theory;
  5. allowed to advance views, to develop atomic-molecular learning.
