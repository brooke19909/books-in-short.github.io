Moscow-Silver Ponds: Traveling in the Moscow Region

Silver Ponds - the farthest urban district of the Moscow region. Moscow - Silver Ponds - a short distance. The capital with a regional center is divided 180 km.

History

An ancient settlement known since the 16th century. In these places, battles with the Crimean Tatars and battles of the Time of Troubles took place. From here the Soviet troops launched an offensive in December 1941 in a westerly direction. Here was born military marshal Vasily Chuykov and many other personalities who made a significant contribution to the history of the country: the heroes of the war of 1812 and the Decembrists, actors and composers, writers and athletes.

At different times, the Silver Ponds belonged to the princes Serebryany-Obolensky , Cherkassky, and the Sheremetyev dynasty.

At the beginning of the XVII century, Ivan Bolotnikov set up a stronghold here. Later, supporters of the Zemstvo militia, Prince G.V. Volkonsky, governor N.D. Pilyemov, I. Zarutsky, and Y. Ryndin besieged Silver Ponds, but did not achieve the desired result.

Here you can always find natural vegetables from personal plots and the freshest animal products. There are no large industrial enterprises, the city is simply surrounded by greenery. Dense forests, spacious fields and full-flowing rivers can be safely called natural attractions. Creative personalities have a special love for magnificent landscapes; they will appreciate this magnificence.

Three rivers flow in the area: Kudesna, Mordves, Berezinka.



Neighbors

As already mentioned, the distance Moscow - Silver Ponds is 180 km. In the neighborhood are the Zaraysk and Kashira districts of the region. Neighbors can be called the Tula and Ryazan regions. Silver Ponds during its existence and due to the peculiarities of its geographical location belonged to the Tula region (1939) and was part of the Stupinsky district of the Moscow region (1963). In 2005, the working village was transformed into an urban settlement.



Infrastructure

The population of Silver Ponds is 10,000. There is everything necessary for life: cafes, shops, a post office, banks, beauty salons, a culture house, temples, schools, kindergartens, an art and music school, an Olympic reserve school and two sports schools, a children's creativity house, a hotel.

sights

Among them are a monument and a bust to Marshal Chuikov, his house museum, a monument to the leader of the world proletariat V.I. Lenin, a stella of fallen soldiers and a monument to the fallen in hot spots.

In the cultural center "Heritage" there is a museum room of the national artist Sergei Dmitrievich Stolyarov.

The city has two magnificent temples: the Church of the Sign of the Blessed Virgin Mary, built in the early twentieth century (more precisely, in 1914) and the church of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker, built in 1835. Restoration work is underway on small churches throughout the area.

How to get there?

The Moscow-Serebryanye Prudy road will lead to the M4 federal highway Don, the village is located not far from it. The federal highway M6 "Caspian" runs through the district , leading to Astrakhan, and the Kashira-Serebryanye Prudy-Kimovsk road.

Silver Ponds has a train station and a bus station. Therefore, for those who like to travel by public transport, you can get here from the capital by train or bus. Travel time is approximately three hours.

By train

The first train through Silver Ponds passed back in 1898.

The distance Moscow - Silver Ponds can be overcome by train from the Paveletsky railway station, but, unfortunately, the route is indirect - you will have to change trains at Uzunovo station. The road will take 3.5-5.5 hours.



It can be reached by passing southbound trains coming from the Paveletsky station. Their number increases during the holiday season. Travel time is three to four hours. The fare will cost 400-500 rubles.



By bus

The most accessible form of public transport is the bus. 10-11 buses leave from Krasnogvardeiskaya metro station on the route Moscow - Serebryanye Prudy every day. Ticket prices range from 390-430 rubles. Travel time is three hours.

Bus Moscow - Serebryanye Prudy arrives on Sovetskaya Street, from where you can get to any part of the city on foot or by taxi. The fare for a taxi ranges from 200 rubles.



From Krasnogvardeiskaya to the bus station three minutes walk. Exit towards the first car from the center. When exiting through the glass doors of the metro, turn right, go along the underpass about five meters to the left and climb the stairs to the city. On the left side in the direction of travel is the Krasnogvardeiskaya bus station, from where the bus leaves for Serebryanye Prudy. Address for motorists: Nut Boulevard, possession 24, building 1g.

Bus tickets Moscow - Silver Ponds can be bought online or purchased directly on the spot.

The first bus leaves in the morning at 9:15, the last - in the evening at 19:45.

There is no clear answer to the question of how to get to Serebryanye Prudy from Moscow. Everyone can choose a convenient way to travel.
