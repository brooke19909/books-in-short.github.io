Margaret Island, Budapest: how to get, attractions, description of hotels, recreation and entertainment

It is located in the very center of the city, separating the two parts of the capital - Buda and Pest, connecting to them with bridges, one of which is called Margarita, and the second - Arpad. History

The history of the island, located on the Danube River, began with the name "Hare". That is the name he bore for a long time. The Hungarian king Bela IV decided to give his beloved daughter Margit to the Dominican monastery, located on the island, when the kingdom gets rid of the Mongol yoke.

After this happened, he fulfilled his vow and gave his 9-year-old daughter to the monastery to serve God. At the age of 28, Margaret died. She was revered among the people as a righteous woman and was very loved by him. After some time, in memory of the righteous Margaret, the island "Hare", it was decided to call her name.

Dominican monastery

This monastery existed in the XII century and was built in the Romanesque style in the name of St. Michael. The chapel and the sacristy on the south side were attached in the middle of the 15th century. At the same time, when the Turks invaded Hungary, many buildings in the capital were destroyed, including the monastery.

However, at the beginning of the 19th century, the ruins were excavated, and the restoration of the temple began. At the end of construction, an old bell, which was made in the 15th century, was installed on the church. Currently, the temple is active and attracts not only tourists, but also believers.

On one of the alleys of the monastery park is the grave of the very Margit, in whose honor the island is named. On her grave you can see a large number of flowers, this is due to the fact that there is a belief associated with Margit. It is believed that she blesses all the lovers who bring her flowers.

Island Baths

The baths on Margaret Island in Budapest are represented in large numbers. They are divided into two categories - a classic bathhouse and a modern one. The main difference is that the classic ones are treated, not entertained. They work year-round, and the ticket price is higher than in the modern one. Classic baths include:

  • Gellert
  • "Sections";
  • "The Sly";
  • "Rudash."

In the thermal pools of these institutions they treat various types of diseases, for example, joints, strengthen bone tissue, and recover from a fracture. And also eliminate problems with the skin, respiratory tract and much more.

The fact is that the water in these baths has a rich composition of various useful salts, minerals and other substances that help with ailments. Hungarians adopted the use of thermal pools from the Turks, Turks from the Byzantines, and the latter from the Romans. And so the traditions of restoration and treatment from those ancient times have come down to our time.

Bathhouse "Palatinus"

This institution opened in the distant 1919 and became the first open-air bathing beach. Every year the number of visitors increased, and it was decided to expand the territory. Currently, the pool has several pools and water attractions. Large areas for sunbathing and relaxing, cafes and restaurants.

Water is supplied to the Palatinus pools from thermal springs, while it first passes through a special high-tech filtration system. The pool has several pools for various purposes:

How to get to Margaret Island in Budapest?

You can get to Budapest from Moscow or St. Petersburg by direct flight by plane. Arriving at the Budapest airport, named after the composer Ferenc Liszt, you should use public transport or a taxi.

If you decide not to take a taxi, then you can get to Margit Island by bus number 914. For the convenience of guests of the capital, they go at intervals of 20 minutes. Travel time will be approximately 45 minutes, however, it will fly by unnoticed, because outside the windows there will be beautiful landscapes of the ancient city.

It should be noted that transport will not take you to the island itself, but to the West Station, from which you will have to walk about two kilometers or take a taxi. You can also use trams number 4 or number 6, which will take you to the bridge connecting the island with the city. In fact, an hour and a half after arriving in Budapest, you will find yourself on the wonderful and bewitching Margit island with its beauties.
