Sights of Amsterdam: photos and description

A unique city with an intricate system of canals successfully combines modernity and centuries-old traditions. The charming capital of the Netherlands holds many monuments that will appeal to tourists. It combines the amazing beauty of nature, the luxury of ancient architecture and tolerance for human vices.

A city grown from a fishing village

The vibrant Amsterdam, whose attractions carry over to past eras, has a special energy. It attracts millions of travelers seeking to get acquainted with the city, destroying all stereotypes and giving a breath of freedom.

The first mention of the Dutch pearl dates back to 1275. At that time, she was just a small fishing village, grown on the banks of the Amstel River. Residents built a dam to prevent flooding, and gradually the settlement turned into a port city, with 90 islands separated by canals.

The modern capital of the Netherlands

And now Amsterdam, the sights of which deserve a detailed story, is a densely populated European city that has retained the charm of the Middle Ages. More than 750 thousand people of 175 nationalities coexist on its territory. The local population prefers to live on houseboats, moored along the promenades. In such dwellings, more like barges, there is electricity, gas, sewage. A true tourist paradise attracts vacationers from many different countries, because you can come here several times and always find something interesting.

Excursions - an ideal option for exploring the city

It is simply impossible to get acquainted with the main attractions of Amsterdam in one day . For guests visiting it for the first time, it makes sense to start with a sightseeing tour of the capital, lasting two hours. Tourists will travel to memorable places on a comfortable bus, and everyone will be able to get off at any stop, and then again continue an exciting journey on the same ticket.

However, the most impressive way to get to know the amazing city is a waterway cruise that spans the historic center. Excursions differ in time and route, and a tour of the monuments is accompanied by explanations of the guide, and a glass of sparkling wine. If local residents prefer to travel around the tourist center on bicycles, then foreigners choose cruises through numerous channels. You can‚Äôt assume that you got acquainted with the city if you were not on boat excursions that take place both day and night.

Surrounded by water, it is compact in size, and in just a few hours you can swim past the main attractions of Amsterdam, connecting the heritage of different eras. A canal cruise inspires tourists so much that they rent private boats and embark on their own journey, becoming a captain exploring the waterways.

Foreigners love to walk, because a busy city is a wonderful place for such walks, and you can get to the objects of mass pilgrimage of tourists by public transport. It is best to purchase a city guide that will offer you to follow a special route or build your own, choosing the most interesting places.

Amsterdam main square

Acquaintance with the historical center, where the main attractions of Amsterdam, preserved from the Middle Ages, are concentrated, begins with Dam Square, where it is always crowded. It was here that Northern Venice was born. For more than seven centuries, it has been the heart of the Dutch capital. Derived from the word "dam" (dam), it appeared on the very spot where the inhabitants erected an embankment that regulates water flows and protects the territory from the elements. Once upon a time, houses on the square were built of wood, but after numerous fires in the 13th century, they began to be built of stone. Architectural works of various eras and styles look very harmonious, and it is interesting to watch how historical objects combine with multi-colored windows of fashion boutiques.

Royal Palace

A rectangle connecting two old streets, Dam Square is always full of tourists. Here is the Royal Palace - its main building and the main attraction of Amsterdam (photos and description are presented in the article).

The architectural miracle that appeared in the XVII century, stands on wooden stilts that do not allow a three-story building, decorated with allegorical figures, to descend into the swamp. Built from yellow sandstone, it looks dark because the porous material has absorbed a lot of dust over several centuries.

New church

One of the most iconic buildings, which are a religious attraction of Amsterdam (photo can be seen below), was built over 70 years. Numerous fires completely destroyed the church dedicated to Saints Catherine and Mary. And it was restored for a long time. Made in the Gothic style, it is famous for its beautiful altar and multi-colored stained-glass windows that cause admiration.

In the temple, the monarchs of the Netherlands are married and crowned, but services are no longer held here. But everyone will be able to attend concerts of organ music.

Obelisk dedicated to the victims of the Second World War

In the square rises a national monument - an obelisk 22 meters high. Glorifying the heroes who died during the Second World War, it is lined with calcareous tuff of a snow-white shade. The attraction of Amsterdam, the photo of which guests are sure to take, evokes mixed feelings. The stele is decorated with bas-reliefs depicting male figures chained to each other, symbolizing the suffering of the people, and dogs howling at their feet - devotion and despair. The monument is crowned with a sculpture of a woman holding a child and surrounded by pigeons. She personifies victory and a new life, as well as liberation from fascist oppression.

City of Contrasts

The city of Amsterdam, the sights of which made its appearance unique, is limited by territorial space. Man conquered the land from the sea, and the abundance of water determined the architectural originality: there are almost no high-rise buildings, and only the pointed spiers of the churches rise above the capital of the Netherlands.

In fact, it is a city of contrasts: here art connoisseurs and freedom-loving hippies, adoring dope, peacefully coexist. This is a respectable and at the same time liberated city, where drugs are sold, gay marriage and prostitution are allowed. It is no coincidence that for many people Amsterdam (Netherlands), whose sights reflect its cultural and historical development, is associated with forbidden pleasures.

What do the guests say?

Reviews of tourists about Amsterdam, the sights of which reflect its diversity, are full of enthusiasm. Travelers agree on one thing: this city was created for entertainment, not for living.

Guests are advised to rent a bicycle to get acquainted with the main monuments, because some of them are hidden in the most hidden corners. As vacationers admit, cute houses, tilted in different directions, immediately attract attention. Since the tourist paradise was erected in a swampy area, all structures were built on stilts, which rotted over time, and houses were skewed.

Of course, Amsterdam cannot be imagined without a network of picturesque canals forming concentric belts around a settlement. This seems surprising, but longboats and boats moored off the coast are a haven for local residents, as housing has always been a big problem. More recently, floating hotels on the water appeared in the city, which began to enjoy unprecedented success, since living in them is much cheaper than standing on land.
