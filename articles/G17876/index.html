Instructions on how to put a ringtone on iPhone

iPhone is a fashion device from Apple, the lucky owners of which are many people who monitor the development of information technology around the world. The iPhone has a lot of advantages, one of which is its own iOS operating system and a large number of applications for working with different files. IPhones, like other Apple devices, work with the free iTunes program, a media player that allows you to not only play audio and video files, but also organize them. Many buyers buying this phone for the first time are concerned about how to put a melody on an iPhone. By and large, this is not a problem at all - you just have to try a couple of times, the habit of handling new functions is developed quickly. Let's consider in more detail how to put a melody on an iPhone.





In order to put a melody on the iPhone, you can use the existing library of ringtones or diversify it due to the tunes downloaded from the Internet or from a desktop computer. For iPhone ringtones, files with the extension .m4r are created, lasting up to 40 seconds: they are converted using special applications or online Internet services. To download ringtones from collections that are freely available on the Internet, you can use the wi-fi wireless interface, and for transferring from a stationary or any other computer, you can use a high-speed USB serial port, which is connected via a cable. If the firmware of the iPhone belongs to versions 5.0 or newer, you can also connect to the computer via wi-fi.





After connecting the iPhone to the computer, you need to open the iTunes media player. Further steps on how to put a melody on an iPhone are also quite simple. To set a ringtone directly to a call or message, you need to go to the "Settings" menu, select "Calls" (or "Messages"), go to "Ringtones" and select a ringtone from the already generated list.





In general, the ability to set a ringtone to your taste not only allows you to use your favorite music, but also select individual melodies for different groups of people or individual contacts. One song alone can determine the caller and the importance of the call itself.
