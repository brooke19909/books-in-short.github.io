Great chemists of the world and their work

Chemistry is the most important science, which is already used mechanically in the modern world. A person does not think about the fact that he uses the discoveries made by scientists in his time in everyday life. Cooking according to ordinary and unusual recipes, working in the garden - feeding plants, spraying, protecting against pests, using medicines from a home medicine cabinet, using favorite cosmetics - all these possibilities were given to us by chemistry.

Thanks to many years of work, the great chemists have made our world just that - convenient and comfortable. More information about some of the discoveries and names of scientists can be found in the article.



The formation of chemistry as a science

As an independent science, chemistry began to develop only in the second half of the 18th century. The great chemists, who gave the world many interesting and useful discoveries in the field of research on chemical elements, made a huge contribution to the formation of the world in its current form.

Thanks to the work of scientists, today we can use a lot of advantages in everyday life. Chemistry became a strict discipline only with the help of painstaking work and a clear distribution of the basic concepts in science that the great chemists have been performing for a long time.

Discovery of new chemical elements

At the beginning of the 19th century, the scientist Jens Jakob Berzelius lived and worked in Sweden. He devoted his life entirely to chemical research. He received the title of professor of chemistry at the Medical and Surgical Institute, was listed in the St. Petersburg Academy of Sciences as an honorary foreign representative. He was president of the Swedish Academy of Sciences.

Jens Jakob Berzelius was the first scientist to propose using letters to name chemical elements. His idea was successfully picked up and used to this day.

Oil research and the invention of coal gas masks

Academician of the USSR Academy of Sciences Nikolai Zelinsky became the founder of petrochemistry and organic catalysis, created a scientific school.

Studies of the origin of oil, discoveries in the field of hydrocarbon synthesis, the reaction of producing alpha-amino acids are the merits of Nikolai Dmitrievich.

In 1915, the scientist created a coal gas mask. During gas attacks by the British and Germans in World War I, many soldiers died on the battlefields: out of 12,000 people, only 2,000 remained alive. Nikolai Zelinsky together with scientist V.S. Sadikov developed a method of calcining coal and laid it the foundation for the creation of a gas mask. The use of this invention saved the lives of millions of Russian soldiers.

Zelinsky was awarded three times the State Prize of the USSR and other awards, the title of Hero of Socialist Labor and Honored Worker of Science, was appointed honorary representative of the Moscow Society of Naturalists.



Chemical industry development

Gas reactions and cement chemistry

The outstanding French scientist Henri Louis le Chatelier became a pioneer in chemistry in the study of combustion processes, as well as the study of the chemistry of cements.

The processes taking place in the reactions between gases, also became the object of study of the scientist.

The main idea, which was the red line in all the works of Henri Louis le Chatelier, is the close connection of scientific discoveries with problems that are becoming priorities in industry. His book "Science and Industry" is popular and now in the scientific community.

The scientist devoted much time to researching reactions occurring with mine gas. All the processes that can occur with gas - ignition, combustion, detonation - were studied in detail by Henri Louis and he also proposed new methods of metallurgical and heat engineering calculations. The scientist won recognition and fame not only in France, but also around the world.
