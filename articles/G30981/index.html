Leaders of the USSR

In this article you will find out what policies the leaders of the USSR conducted, about their achievements and the desire to make the country better. Let's look at two prominent representatives who went down in history: Brezhnev L.I. and Gorbachev M.S.

Leaders of the USSR


Leonid Ilyich Brezhnev

There were Soviet leaders who provided people with a good and dignified life. One of them was Brezhnev. The state Soviet and party leader was born in the village of Kamenskoye, in the family of an ordinary metallurgist. At the age of fifteen, he began working life. After graduating from the Kursk Land Management and Land Reclamation College in 1927, he worked as a specialist in the Orsha District of the Kokhanovsky District. In 1923 he joined the Komsomol, and in 1931 became a member of the CPSU. After graduating from the metallurgical university in Dneprodzerzhinsk, he worked as an engineer at a metallurgical plant. Since 1964 he became the first leader of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union. From 1960 to 1964 he was one of the chairmen of the Soviet Union. Since 1977 he became Marshal of the USSR. The General Secretaries of the USSR, which were before Brezhnev, flattered about his policy.

World political, state and public figure, President of the USSR Gorbachev was born in the village of Privolnoye, March 2, 1931, in a peasant family. At the age of sixteen he received the order. After graduating from school with a silver medal, in 1950, he entered the Moscow State University. M.V. Lomonosov law school.

He took an active part in the Komsomol organization of the educational institution, and in 1952 he joined the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, where he was eventually Secretary General from 1985 to 1991. He was the first and last president of the Soviet Union. Since 1993, he was one of the members of the council and co-founder of one of the leading newspapers. Many awards and honors were earned by this man. In 1990 received the Nobel Prize. The leaders of the USSR were distinguished by their assertiveness and confidence.
