Celtic pigtail step by step

One of the most common Celtic patterns is a braid. You can tie it with the wrong side. Regarding the example described below, you can change the width of the product, as you wish the knitter, simply increasing the number of loops in the fabric.

  1. First, make three front loops on the first row, end the line with two wrong loops, and between them repeat the cycle of 4 wrong and 6 front loops as many times as necessary for the intended width.
  2. The second row begins with two facial loops, ends with three wrong loops, and between them repeat the cycle of 6 wrong loops and four facial loops as many times as necessary.
  3. The third row begins with three facial loops and ends with two wrong ones. Between them, the following cycle is performed: 4 wrong loops are made, then three are removed on the auxiliary knitting needle, and three more are knitted with the facial knitting. Further, the previously removed loops are knitted with facial ones. How many times the cycle repeats is determined by the width of the product.
  4. The fourth row begins with two front loops and ends with three wrong loops, and between it repeat the necessary number of times 6 cycles of 6 reverse and 4 facial loops.
  5. The fifth row consists entirely of a cycle, the number of repetitions of which depends on the size of the thing. The cycle is as follows: three loops are removed on an additional knitting needle, then two are knitted on the wrong side and the removed loops are returned, making them facial. Two more loops are removed, three are knitted with facial ones, and deferred ones are purl.
  6. The sixth row begins with three wrong loops, ends with two facial loops, a cycle of 4 facial loops and six wrong loops is made between them.
  7. In the seventh row, the beginning will be two wrong loops, complete with three facial loops. A cycle is made between them: three loops are removed on an additional knitting needle, three more are knitted with facial ones, then the delayed loops are also knit with facial ones, and four are made from the wrong.
  8. The eighth row is knitted similarly to the sixth.
  9. On the ninth row, first, two loops are removed on the third knitting needle, then three loops are made facial, the deferred are knitted on the wrong side. The next three loops must be removed on an additional knitting needle, knit two wrong seams and return the removed loops with their faces. This cycle is repeated from the beginning of the row to the end.
  10. The tenth row coincides with the fourth.

When knitting further, it is necessary to repeat rows 3-10. The resulting braid will be dense.
