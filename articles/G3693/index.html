Spain or Italy: pros and cons of resorts, travel tips, an overview of attractions

Spain and Italy - between the two states there is actually a lot in common. Both countries are located in southern Europe, have wide access to the sea and are at approximately the same level of socio-economic development. In this article we will try to figure out where it is better to go - to Spain or Italy. We will talk in detail about the pros and cons of spa holidays in both countries.

Spain or Italy: where is the best place to relax?

Geography and climate

Both countries are located in southern Europe, at approximately the same latitude. Italy is washed by five seas and boasts the longest coastline among European states (about 8000 km). The length of the Spanish coast is more modest, but Spain has another significant advantage - wide access to the Atlantic Ocean.

The climate of Italy is extremely diverse. In the north of the country - temperate, in the south - subtropical Mediterranean. The climate in Spain is slightly hotter and drier than in Italy (especially in the central regions of the country, which are very remote from the sea). If we talk about landscape diversity, then it is equally characteristic of both countries. And if Italy‚Äôs natural beauties are impressive, then Spanish landscapes are more likely to produce a mesmerizing effect.

Food and entertainment

Italian cuisine is known throughout the world. Who hasn't heard of pizza, lasagna, or risotto? In any local cafe, these dishes, along with the famous Italian pasta, form the basis of the menu. Spanish cuisine is less famous, but no less diverse. Staying in this country, you should definitely try paella, tortilla or gazpacho. Spanish wines are also noteworthy, especially Merlot, Rioja and Cabernet Franc.

In entertainment, of course, Spain wins. Here you will definitely be advised to visit the bullfight (a bloody and ambiguous action) and watch a bright and colorful flamenco show. In addition, in Spain a number of unusual festivals are held annually (for example, La Tomatina or Las Fallas). In Italy, entertainment is more refined: here you can visit the opera festival in Verona or the famous Venice Carnival.

Shopping

In shopping tourism, without a doubt, Italy leads. Here you can purchase branded items and leather products of impeccable quality. The most recognized centers of Italian shopping are Rome, Milan, Florence and Turin. As a rule, people go to Spain in order to update their wardrobe with clothes of inexpensive and lesser-known brands. In Spain, it is also worth paying attention to leather goods (in particular, bags and belts).

After all, it was here that such universally recognized masters as Michelangelo, Raphael, Botticelli were born and created. Nevertheless, Spain will find something to surprise lovers of antiquity. It is enough to recall only Barcelona and its unrivaled architect - Antonio Gaudi.

Holidays in Italy: pros and cons

If you have not yet decided where to go - to Italy or Spain, we suggest that you familiarize yourself with the main advantages and disadvantages of relaxation in both countries. And we will start with the state of pizza and spaghetti.

So, the main advantages:

  • The richest historical and cultural heritage.
  • Stunning abundance of museums, castles and architectural monuments.
  • A large number of thermal springs.
  • The proximity of the mountains and the sea.
  • Great shopping.

The main disadvantages:

  • Not always high quality hotel services.
  • Problems with petty crime (especially in large cities).
  • The work of public transport in Italy is far from ideal, there are problems with garbage collection.

Holidays in Spain: pros and cons

And now let's look at the main advantages and disadvantages of holidays in Spain. The main advantages:

  • A wide selection of magnificent beaches - sandy and pebble, comfortable and wild.
  • A diverse hotel fund with high quality service.
  • Incredibly picturesque landscapes and fine wine.

The main disadvantages:

  • The Spaniards are quite noisy and cheerful people. Therefore, if you expect rest from rest, then it is better to go to a remote provincial area.
  • There is a risk to say goodbye to your wallet in this country, especially in big cities.
  • Many Spanish hotels have problems with sound insulation (due to the very thin partitions between the rooms).

Useful tips for tourists

In addition to our article, there are seven useful tips for those who are going to relax in one of these countries:

  • In Spain, sea water, as a rule, warms up well only by mid-June. You should not go here before, especially if you are traveling with children.
  • It is not worth going to Italy in mid-August. At this time, here comes the period of long weekends and seasonal holidays, which is associated with the local holiday of Ferragosto.
  • For children and families, beach holidays are more suitable for Spain.
  • Spain ranks 6th in the ranking of countries for Environmental Performance Index, while Italy is only 29th.
  • In Spain, only a few locals speak English, so it is recommended to take a phrase book with you on a trip. In Italy, this problem is not so acute.
  • If the purpose of your trip is shopping, then feel free to go to the south of Italy (to Verona, Tuscany, Milan).
  • The best time to visit Italy and Spain is spring or autumn (of course, if you are not interested in a beach holiday).
