What are humic acids

These are compounds having a long molecular chain. Humic acids are contained in the soil, in peat, in plants. Oxidized coals also contain this substance. You can find it in small quantities in fried meat, tea, coffee and bread crust.





If we talk about natural humic acids, they have a rather complex structure, so their molecular weight can reach 2500. Humic acids practically do not dissolve in water, with the exception of fulvic acids.

The formation of these compounds occurs in the process of humus formation. For scientists, it remains a mystery why the decomposition of organic substances in the soil does not end with the formation of water and carbon dioxide as final products, and at a certain stage, simple decomposition products turn into humic acids.
