History of Alfa Bank

Alfa Bank was founded in Moscow in 1990. It provides the population with all possible financial services, both to individuals and legal entities. The main branch is located in the capital of Russia - Moscow. This institution provides jobs for more than 20 thousand people. In 2014, Alfa-Bank won the tender, after which it included the assets of the public joint-stock company Baltic Bank.

The main shareholder of the financial institution is the Russian holding company AB Holding, which owns 99% of the shares. The remaining 1% of the shares belongs to a Cypriot company.

Alfa-Bank branches in Moscow are included in the 10 most significant lending organizations by decision of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation. And also the banking group does not lose the position of one of the largest banks in Russia in such parameters as total capital, client funds and loan portfolio.
