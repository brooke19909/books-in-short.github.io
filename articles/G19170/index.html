Chair bag: pattern. Bean bag: sewing instructions

Frameless armchairs are fashionable and comfortable furniture. They are especially in demand in the children's room. After all, such a chair is safe, comfortable, convenient and easy to adapt to any body shape. It is not surprising that such furniture was to the taste of the adult population. The stores offer a large selection of different bean bags. But much more fun and cheaper to sew such furniture yourself. The pattern will help you with this. The bag chair is quite easy to make with your own hands. The main thing is to make a little effort.





What is a bag chair?

It is also called bin-bag. The chair bag is an amazing piece of furniture, frameless design. It has an almost unique property. This furniture is able to take any form. It can serve as a bed, chair, ordinary chair.

Frameless furniture is made in the most bizarre and unusual forms. Below will be considered the 3 most common options. To make it easier to sew, a pattern will be given. The bag chair can take the form of a pear, a drop and even a soccer ball.

Material selection

To sew the product you will need 2 types of fabric. After all, the chair bag consists of an inner and outer cover. Before choosing a material, decide on a model. Since each species has its own pattern. It is better to sew a bag chair from integral cuts of fabric. That is why initially select a model and calculate the required amount of material.

For an internal cover it is recommended to choose very strong fabric and inexpensive. The filler will fall into it.

The following materials can be used:

  • satin;
  • chisi (this fabric is used for the manufacture of workwear);
  • raincoat or mattress textiles;
  • polyester material (it is durable and does not leak water).




How much average tissue will it take? For example, you selected a pear chair pattern. The ready-made bag chair will have the following dimensions: diameter - 90 cm, height - 120 cm. This structure requires about 2.5 m of fabric, the width of which is 115‚Äì122 cm.

For an external cover, choose a fabric that harmoniously fits into your interior. The most commonly used materials are:

  • flock;
  • shenill;
  • faux suede;
  • velours;
  • jacquard;
  • faux fur or leather;
  • tapestry.

Features of the cut

Your soft chair bag will serve more than one year. That is why the choice of upholstery fabric should be approached very carefully. In addition, it should be possible to remove the top cover for washing. To do this, be sure to sew a zipper when sewing.

If the fabric selected for the upper cover does not allow air to pass through, then a special air valve must be provided. Otherwise, the chair can tear easily.

Product Filler

This is another important point that should be decided before stitching a bean bag chair. After all, the feature of the product lies precisely in the filler, which allows the frameless model to take any form.

So what should be inside the chair? The best filler is foamed polystyrene. This material has a long service life, does not get damp, and does not cause allergies.





Tools and materials

Regardless of the model you choose, you will need:

  1. Graph paper. It is necessary to create a pattern.
  2. Fabric for inner and outer cover.
  3. Lightning - 2 pcs.
  4. Pencil, ruler, crayon, scissors.
  5. Reinforced threads.
  6. Filler - for one chair you will need about 300 liters of filler. This volume weighs 1.5 kg (so do not buy 10 kg packs of polystyrene foam).
  7. Sewing machine.

Bean bag: "pear"

This model is very popular.

A master class will help to sew such a chair bag:

  1. Below is a product pattern. It must be transferred, according to the dimensions, on graph paper. If necessary (if the chair is sewn for a child), you can proportionally reduce the pattern.
  2. According to the prepared patterns, we cut the material. 1.5 cm should be left for the allowances. You should have 6 wedges of the main part, 2 - the bottom, 1 - the upper part. All parts are ready for the outer case.
  3. Repeat the procedure on the fabric selected for the inner cover.
  4. Details must be swept away. At this point, consider placing a zipper. It is recommended to sweep it in the lower part of the product. So she will not spoil the appearance of the model. Zippers should be on both covers. This will not only promptly wash the upholstery, but also add filler if necessary.
  5. Now we sew parts with reinforced threads using a sewing machine. All bastings should be removed. To give the product durability, two lines should be laid. Edges are best treated with an overlock.
  6. Now fill the inner bag with filler. This procedure is best done without children and animals. Polystyrene balls can easily electrify and can scatter throughout the room. They are very dangerous if they get into the respiratory system! If the balls still scatter, collect them using a vacuum cleaner. But to avoid this, it is recommended to fill the filler through a watering can, a paper funnel or a plastic bottle with a cut bottom.
  7. Do not fill the bag to the eyeballs. Remember that the beauty of this design is that it perfectly adapts to the shape of the body. Therefore, the filler must be filled in 2/3.
  8. Put on the top bag. Button the zipper. The soft chair bag is ready. You can enjoy your labors.

  3. Before proceeding with the assembly of the product, it is recommended to process all the parts with an overlock. It‚Äôs even better to cut the edges. Of course, this will take a lot of time, but such details will certainly not crumble.
  4. Now you need to sweep the product. Difficulties may arise with lightning. After all, the clasp simply does not fit on one face. Therefore, the lightning should be placed along the curve.




You already know how to sew a chair bag and fill it with filler. Therefore, this procedure should not cause difficulties for you.

Bean bag: "drop"

This is another kind of frameless furniture that may interest you.

The pattern below is transferred to graph paper. The bag drop chair is made as follows:

  1. The pattern below is transferred to graph paper.
  2. When laying out the details on the fabric, be sure to consider the shared thread. This will protect the model from skew. On one cover you need: 2 sidewalls, 1 bottom, 1 top.
  3. Now sweep away the details. Mark the zippers.
  4. Work the slices and sew along the bastards.
  5. Fill the filler. Button the zippers.
