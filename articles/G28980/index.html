V.I. Chapaev Museum - a living history of the hero and his exploits, preserved for posterity

Vasily Ivanovich Chapaev - the figure is truly amazing and legendary. The man who actually existed became not only a symbol of courage and military courage, but also a hero of jokes and fictional stories. And all because he was from the people and close to everyone, and his astounding heroism is nothing more than a love of the motherland. It is not surprising that museums and memorial complexes dedicated to Chapaev are scattered throughout the cities of the former Soviet Union - in the places where he was born, showed courage and died.

This is the largest and most important museum complex dedicated to the figure of Chapaev. The house was recreated exactly as it was during the lifetime of the owners. It is interesting that the house stands on the same place as before, only earlier it was in the village of Budaika near Cheboksary, but over time the village merged with the city, and today Chapaev Street, almost the very center of the city, lies in its place.





Museum in Balakovo

This is the place where the future hero spent his youth, it is considered his second homeland, it is not surprising that there is a Chapaev museum here. Balakovo, located on the Volga, became the haven of a family that escaped from the hungry and impoverished Chuvashia. From here, Vasily in the 14th year went to the front.

The museum was created by the forces of fellow countrymen and colleagues of the hero, and many objects for its arrangement were transferred by the children of Chapaev Claudius and Alexander. Today, in a small wooden house, family items, photographs and typical household items of that time are stored. In addition, here you can see the medals of the eldest son Chapaev and things of his father's comrades-in-arms. Of great interest to visitors is the personal belongings of Maria Popova, who served in the Balakovo regiment. Her handbag, dress and glasses are interesting because Popova became the prototype of Anka the machine gunner, the heroine of the famous film about Chapaev.
