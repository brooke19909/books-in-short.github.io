Comparative analytical balance. Enterprise balance sheet is an example. Analytical balance

The analytical balance is usually used to study the structure and dynamics of changes in the financial position of the company. Get it is not difficult. For this, the initial balance is used, in which individual articles are condensed and indicators of dynamics and structure are supplemented. At the same time, the structure of the initial reporting form is usually retained: current and non-current assets, borrowed and own capital, and equality of the total assets and liabilities are highlighted. The analytical balance is useful in that it allows you to systematize the calculations made, to determine what changes have occurred in the property status of the organization, to draw conclusions about which sources made it possible to open the inflow of new funds and in which assets the finances received are invested.

The role of analytical balance





The analysis of the comparative analytical balance provides data for the assessment of a number of points:

- determination of the value of current and non-current assets, changes in their ratio, as well as sources of their financing;

- Articles that are growing at a faster pace and the degree of their influence on the balance sheet structure;

- the share of assets belonging to receivables and inventories;

- distribution of borrowed funds by urgency and other parameters;

- determining the size of the share of equity and how much the organization depends on them.

The analysis of the analytical balance pays particular attention to the elements whose specific gravity is the largest, as well as to those whose share changes stepwise. Horizontal and vertical analysis of the dynamics of the structure and composition of the property makes it possible to set the size of the relative and absolute increase or decrease in the existing property of the company, as well as its individual types.

Methodology for the formation of a comparative analytical balance

The analysis is usually performed by one of the methods proposed below:

- directly on the balance sheet, without first changing the composition of the balance sheet items;

- by means of constructing a sealing comparative balance, where some elements of balance sheet items that are uniform in composition will be aggregated;

- an additional adjustment is made to the data on the inflation index with further aggregation of articles in the required economic sections. Obtaining a comparative economic balance is allowed from the source using the procedure for compiling individual articles, as well as adding dynamic and structural indicators.

The convenience of this method lies in the fact that it systematizes and brings together the calculations traditionally carried out by analysts when they first familiarize themselves with the balance sheet. Analysis of the comparative analytical balance allows you to cover a huge number of indicators that demonstrate the statics and dynamics of the state of the organization in terms of finances. This includes indicators of vertical and horizontal analysis, directly from which you can get data and characteristics of the current state of the enterprise in terms of finances. These include:

- the reflected total value of the property of the organization, which is in the final line;

- the value of the immobilized assets, which is equal to the result of the first section;

- the cost of mobile means, visible as a result of the second section;

- the value of stocks, that is, material circulating assets;

- the size of the company's own funds, presented as a result of the third section of the balance sheet;

- the amount of borrowed funds, which is visible in the sum of the results of the fourth and fifth sections;

- the amount of working capital, visible in the difference in the results of the third and first sections.





Net working capital is seen as a separate position in the aggregated balance sheet and is defined as part of the current assets that receive financing through invested capital. This indicator is a characteristic of the degree of liquidity of the enterprise, which makes it of particular importance. Net working capital is the difference between current assets and liabilities. The greater the magnitude of this indicator, the more stable is the financial position of the organization.

The analysis of the analytical balance should be carried out with a mandatory attention to the change in the specific weight of the amount of working capital in the total value of the property, the growth rate of equity and borrowed capital and their ratio, as well as receivables and payables.

Stable financial stability of the enterprise is accompanied by an increase in the share of working capital, as well as high growth rates of equity in comparison with borrowed. At the same time, payables and receivables are approximately at the same level.

The essence of the horizontal analysis of the enterprise is to build the required number of analytical tables, where the absolute balance sheet indicators should be supplemented by indicators of dynamics. The analyst determines the degree of aggregation of indicators. This is another important way to determine the financial position of the company and its sustainability.

Very often, for an external analysis, the standard balance sheet is completely insufficient. In this case, the use of sealed is allowed.
