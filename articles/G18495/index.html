To Crimea by car: reviews, routes, attractions

Traveling by car is a very special kind of travel that not everyone loves. Someone prefers airplanes and trains, which can significantly save time and literally transfer tourists from one point of the world to another. The car is somewhat limited in range, but travelers have practically no chance to miss some sights, and even vice versa - they can find the most picturesque corners and go on any excursions, because in this case they are their own owners throughout the trip.



One of the main goals of this category of Russian tourists is to travel to the Crimea by car. 2012 and 2013 showed that, despite the fact that so many can afford to travel to distant countries, the Crimean peninsula is still very popular. The reasons for this are simple: it is quite close to those who live in the European part of Russia, lies in the subtropical climate zone, and interesting sights are located here. Of course, the Black and Azov Seas, as well as the mountains also add to this place attractiveness. That is why more and more tourists are choosing to travel to Crimea by car. Reviews and impressions are most often enthusiastic. Even those who have been there more than once are satisfied.

The road to the Crimea by car is not difficult. Most often, travelers use the E105 or M4 Don tracks . Opinions differ: on E105 the route is shorter, but the quality of the route leaves much to be desired, so more time can be spent.
