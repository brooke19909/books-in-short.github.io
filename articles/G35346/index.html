Two-tone patterns with knitting patterns. Simple and lazy patterns.

The easiest way to knit a beautiful, bright and fashionable thing without mastering the complicated knitting techniques is to learn how to knit simple two-color patterns with knitting needles according to the patterns. Schemes in this case are an elementary combination of colors among themselves, without fanciful knotting of drawings. The pattern is obtained through the use of two or more colors of yarn.

Two-tone knitting patterns : patterns for children's things

Children's things differ from adults not only in size, but also in greater brightness and expressiveness of colors. The combination that the child will happily wear is often unacceptable to an adult. Children love catchy, vibrant and contrasting colors, while adults use them for detail and to give the image a special sound.

Lazy two-color knitting patterns: diagrams and description

False ornament, lazy ornament, lazy jacquard - these are all simple lazy patterns. They are called so because to use them you do not need to have rich experience and a high level of skill. Knitting with such patterns allows you to spend an order of magnitude less time in the manufacture of the product, in contrast to knitting in the usual, often more complex ways. The peculiarity of creating lazy patterns is that there is no color change inside the knitted row, and the introduction of a new color each time occurs at the beginning of the knitting of the row. The lazy pattern is knitted in two rows in the same color, however, the correct alternation of the removed elongated loops creates a unique beauty. This type of knitting is preferable for beginner needlewomen, because it avoids frequent mistakes: a tightened fabric, uneven loops that are obtained when knitting classic patterns, where the color of the thread changes inside the row.

How to knit lazy patterns? Basic rules and features:

  • As mentioned above, the most important rule is to knit two rows (front and back) with one color of thread, you need to change it at the end of the second row.
  • In the front rows (they are always odd in the diagrams) when removing the loops, the thread remains FOR work and, accordingly, in even rows, the thread passes before work. This allows you to leave all the broaches on the wrong side.
  • In the wrong rows, the loops are knitted according to the pattern, most often leaving them wrong (but in some patterns there may also be front loops on the wrong side), while the removed loops are simply thrown over, they are not knitted.
