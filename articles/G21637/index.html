Holidays in Istanbul

Istanbul is one of the most beautiful cities in the world. This Turkish metropolis combines the oldest buildings with new business centers. There are a huge number of attractions without which it is impossible to imagine a vacation in Istanbul. Numerous palaces, mosques, monuments - all this and much more is opened to tourists who come here.

Not one vacation in Istanbul is complete without a visit to the museum of Hagia Sophia - Hagia Sophia. According to ancient legend, the basilica was first created on this site in 325 by Konstantin, but already in 404 it was burned. Justinian later began her recovery. He wanted the church to amaze with its magnificence, therefore, on his order, silver, gold, precious stones, ivory were brought for its decoration. After five years of ongoing work, the church was ready, and Justinian consecrated it. Unfortunately, several years later an earthquake occurred that partially damaged the temple. Then came the long years of wars, the invaders plundered and destroyed the church, until in 1453, Sultan Mehmet II decided that now it would be a mosque. To the main building of St. Sofia was attached 4 minarets. After such a reconstruction, it became known as the Hagia Sophia Mosque . In 1935, it was abolished and became a state museum, where the memory of two religions is preserved. So in one building are Christian icons and Muslim paraphernalia. Almost all tourists spending their holidays in Istanbul come here to see one of the most beautiful places on earth.

Another unique monument of ancient architecture can be called the Basilica Cistern, which is located a hundred meters from the church of St. Sophia. The beginning of the construction of an underground reservoir dates back to the 6th century. Its original function was to provide fresh water to temples and residents of Istanbul. For several centuries, the tank stood untouched, which led to its desolation. In 1987, it was open for tourist excursions.
