Long history

Sanatorium "Electronics" in Sochi was founded in the second half of the twentieth century by decree of the Ministry of Electronic Industry. According to the project, the sanatorium complex was designed for 500 places. It was developed by the Moscow Institute. But at the beginning of the 20th century, the Black Sea coast was an abandoned land. In those years, the coastal strip looked like impenetrable American forests, which were described in the stories of Fenimore Cooper and Mine Reed. The banks were hills and ravines, they were covered with dense forest. Trees were covered with ivy, and creepers descended from the peaks to the ground. It was difficult to go through such forests, because thorny bushes grew below. A lot of blackberries grew in these forests.





A little later in these places there were paths, various sites on which one-story buildings were erected. All this appeared thanks to the Russian geographer and botanist Andrei Nikolaevich Krasnov. From various parts of the planet, he brought in various seedlings of trees and shrubs. In 1901, Krasnov bought an inexpensive plot in Sochi (Hosta). Sanatorium "Electronics" after a few decades, and began to build there. But before that, a domestic scientist planted more than 200 species of plants on the principle of building the Botanical Garden in Batumi. Thanks to Krasnov, varieties of tea, lemons, oranges, and so on, already familiar to Russians, are growing on the Black Sea coast. His love of botany has transmitted many valuable gifts to the modern generation.
