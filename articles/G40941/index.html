Term concept





Insurance is a special type of financial relationship in order to create and use cash savings, which serve to compensate for damage in the event of various kinds of dangers and are paid to individuals or legal entities.

In a narrow sense, this is a relationship between two parties aimed at protecting the insured person or his property at the expense of monetary funds, which were formed from paid insurance premiums.

In a broad sense, it includes the types of activities that subsequently formed this material protection.

The normative legal acts on the basis of which these relations operate are the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (part two, chapter 48) and the Federal Law "On the organization of insurance business", which describe the detailed aspects of this term. Based on this Federal Law, one can understand the following: insurance is the relationship of two counterparties in which certain amounts are paid by the contract by one or another party.

Parties and their responsibilities





In this relationship, there are usually two participants. The first side is the insurer (the organization may be private, joint-stock and state). The second - individuals and legal entities.

Obligations of the insurer:

1) Compensates to the other side for damage when a suitable event occurs (natural disasters).

2) Introduces the individual and legal entity with the rules and nuances that are related to this topic.

3) In addition to the indemnified damage caused by the insured event, it also reimburses other expenses.

4) Does not disclose information that he learned about the second party to the contract (these include health status, property status and much more).

5) After written notification, the policyholder may replace the beneficiary with a completely different person.

6) In case of early termination of the contract he warns the other party in advance.

Policyholder Responsibilities:

1) Pays contributions on time.

2) Communicate with circumstances known to him that are of great importance for determining the reason for the formation of the insured event.

3) At the conclusion of the contract, this party continues to take care of the safety of property.

4) Immediately informs the insurer about changes in circumstances upon conclusion of the contract.

5) As soon as possible notifies the other side of the accident.

6) Provides the insurer with the right of subrogation (change of the creditor in the insurance obligation).

7) In case of early termination of the contract notifies the other party of its intention.

Minimum MTPL Term

Many car owners understand that it is forbidden to drive on roads without compulsory motor third party liability insurance. It is because of this that citizens conclude contracts with different companies. According to the legislation in force on the territory of the Russian Federation, the minimum MTPL period can vary in three categories.



The first option is for owners whose vehicle has passed state registration outside of Russia. For such citizens, the minimum period is from 5 to 15 days.

The second option is for people who are going to the place of registration or to a vehicle inspection. The minimum period is 20 days.

The third option is the most common. The contract is concluded if you have registered with the traffic police. The minimum period may not be less than 3 months.

In the event that you decide to insure your car for one quarter (season), the cost of the policy will be 30% of the annual amount. Typically, these types of OSAGO policies are issued by car owners who use the car not all year round.

Brief information about CASCO

This type of insurance is considered to be voluntary. If there is a CASCO policy, a citizen can receive monetary compensation for hijacking a vehicle or causing harm to the health of the driver and passengers. The final settlement will be provided by the organization you contacted. For example, if you decide to conclude an agreement and get a policy, select a company, for example, MSC (insurance company). The addresses of the branches in your city can be found on the Internet.

When calculating CASCO, the following parameters are usually taken into account:

  • car model;
  • year of manufacture of the car (usually only new vehicles are insured);
  • driver's age (for older people it usually goes more expensive);
  • experience in driving a vehicle (beginners pay more than experienced drivers);
  • the presence of a franchise (with its use, the policy will cost less);
  • maintenance of the car in a guarded parking lot (in this case, the fee will be less);
  • the presence of an anti-theft alarm system;
  • The region in which the vehicle will be used.
