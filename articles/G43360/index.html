What is NFC? NFC on the phone - what is it? NFC technology

At the moment, there are various concepts in the mobile industry that many have never heard of. Therefore, it is quite natural to need to understand some of the nuances. So, if you come to the question of what NFC is, then you had to come across in some form the mention of this concept somewhere. It is worth understanding it as much as possible.





NFC (Near Field Communication) is a technology of high-quality wireless communication with a short range (not more than 10 centimeters), which allows contactless data exchange between a pair of devices located nearby: for example, between a plastic smart card or cell phone and a reading terminal . NFC technology is based on RFID (Radio Frequency IDentification). This is radio frequency identification, which is a method for identifying objects in automatic mode. In this case, a radio signal is used, by means of which the data stored in transponders, often defined as NFC tags, are recorded and read. In general, we can say that we are talking about technology for transmitting information through a radio channel that supports active and passive devices. For example, Sony NFC key fobs do not need any additional power to ensure their operation, they do it completely passively.

Technology features

So, if we talk about what NFC is, then it is worth considering the three most popular options for using this technology in mobile phones:

- a reading mode in which the phone reads a passive tag, for example, for interactive advertising;

- emulation of cards, in which the gadget is able to "pretend" with a card, for example, payment or pass;

- P2P mode, in which two phones are paired for data exchange.

Most often, NFC-technology assumes that the carrier of the chip is a mobile phone, which is as massive as an individual device, and at the same time completely inseparable from its owner. In this case, it can be used as a means of payment, which is permissible if there is a virtual wallet, key, means to identify the owner, ticket, bonus card, and much more.

Scope of application

So, NFC in the phone - what is it? Answering this question, it is worth noting that at the moment such solutions have become increasingly widely and actively used in many areas. For example, through this technology, electronic tickets are booked and sold, paid for car parking and public transport. NFC tags are very actively used in the areas of entertainment and services, in the field of security and access control.





Bluetooth difference

The two technologies are similar in principle, but there is a very big difference between them. If we consider what NFC is, then it is worth noting that the main advantage of this technology is a short connection time of one tenth of a second. The short range makes this method of data translation significantly more secure. However, NFC supports 424 Kbps, which is much less than Bluetooth.

Modern stage of development

Contactless payment technologies have proven to be very advanced at the moment, and this has led to the emergence of cards such as MasterCard PayPass and Visa PayWave, which are equipped with built-in antennas and have an NFC function. This market has become so developed that now companies such as MasterCard, Google, Sprint, Citibank and First Data have formed a service called Google Wallet, installed on a number of Android smartphones. Using this application, you can easily turn your gadget into a credit card that allows you to pay in any terminal that supports PayPass.





What is NFC and how are labels used?

Labels in this case are small programmable information areas that are built into billboards, posters or on shelves with products in retail stores. If you touch any of them, you can get some additional information in the form of web addresses, maps or movie clips.

The process of working with labels involves a certain sequence of actions to obtain the information embedded in them.

What you need to do to scan tags

First you need to make sure that the NFC function on your phone is turned on and its screen is active. The phone should be placed above the tag so that it touches the NFC detection area. Next, your device will scan the tag, and after that it will show the content received from it. You should touch the contents, and then you open the label.

So, if we talk about what NFC is, then it should be said about the presence of certain points, which can be called the "dark side" of this technology. The origin of the future

So, if we consider the NFC technology (how to use it is already a little understandable), then it is worth saying about who or what it owes its appearance to. In 2002, companies such as Sony and Phillips teamed up to develop a completely new radio standard, which was named. Prior to this, attempts were repeatedly made to formulate technologies of this kind: Phillips created MIFARE technology, and Sony came up with a similar development called FeliCa. Despite the fact that these technologies had a lot in common, they turned out to be incompatible with each other. The created standard was designed to absorb all the advantages of past developments, as well as open up opportunities for its application in practice.

Speaking about what NFC is, it is necessary to mention that since its inception and development, this technology has been focused on the implementation of interaction between different electronic devices between which there is no wired connection. As examples, it is appropriate to indicate personal computers, PDAs, mobile phones, video cameras, as well as other gadgets.

We can say about such a feature of the interaction of devices working with the support of this technology, such as quick initiation of communication between devices after they are brought to each other at a fairly close distance. After the connection is established, an attempt is immediately made to transfer data between the devices.

For example, if you bring a working camera to the TV, provided that the NFC module works in both gadgets, the image transfer process will immediately begin. If a mobile phone or PDA is in close proximity to a personal computer, this will allow you to instantly start synchronization of the address book or some other documents.

NFC technology is implemented in the form of a chip operating in passive or active mode. The first option involves using the device as a pass or subway card, and the second - receiving information from passive devices, as well as sending it.
