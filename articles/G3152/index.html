Who is Cthulhu? Myths and reality.

Let's look at the question of who is Cthulhu? Cthulhu is a deity belonging to the family of the ancients; he is first encountered by the writer Howard Lycraft. In his books, he describes this creature sleeping on the bottom of the Pacific Ocean. Outwardly, Cthulhu is a mixture of man, dragon and octopus. His body is covered with scales, on the back are the rudiments of wings, and his head is dotted with tentacles. With its gigantic size, Cthulhu is like a huge sorrow.





Cthulhu has the ability to penetrate and control the human mind, but given that the monster is under water, his superpowers lose their power, but nevertheless, it can penetrate people's dreams.
