Stalin's skyscraper on Barrikadnaya: the glorious history of an elite house from the USSR

The Aviators House, or the high-rise on Barrikadnaya, is one of the symbols of Moscow. There are many legends about seven Stalinist skyscrapers built in the post-war years. Despite their venerable age, these buildings today amaze tourists, and many indigenous Muscovites would be happy to purchase apartments in them.

History and description of the Stalinist skyscraper on Barrikadnaya

In January 1947, I. Stalin signed a decree of the Council of Ministers "On the construction of high-rise buildings in Moscow." Kudrinskaya Square (formerly Uprising Square) was chosen for the construction of one of the magnificent buildings. For the simultaneous construction and similar architectural forms, these houses were later poetically called the "Seven Sisters". The building on Kudrinskaya Square will go down in history as a skyscraper on Barrikadnaya, by the name of the nearest metro station.





The main architects of the project are A. A. Mndoyants, M. V. Posokhin. On the common ground floor, the main tower was built on 24 floors (total height - 156 meters with a spire) and two symmetrical side buildings with 18 floors each. You can get inside through three large porches and many small ones. The high-rise on Barrikadnaya was built as a residential building (in total there are more than 450 apartments), except for the basement floor. The building has underground tiers on which, according to the original design, a parking lot and a bomb shelter were located.

Timeless luxury

After putting into operation, the house received the following address: Kudrinskaya Square, 1. The first tenants moved into their apartments in the spring of 1955. Only a select few could get their own square meters in such a house. Mostly apartments were distributed between test pilots, aviation industry workers and members of the USSR Council of Ministers and the Central Committee of the CPSU.





The luxurious decoration of public spaces has partially survived to the present day. In large porches and floors - stucco, parquet and chic oak panels, colorful stained glass windows and mirrors. The facade of the building is decorated with sculptures and bas-reliefs, demonstrating the desire of citizens of the USSR to work, creativity and defense.

High-rise on Barrikadnaya is a worthy example of elite housing for its time. All apartments had a telephone connection with a concierge, sewer pipes were equipped with devices for shredding garbage, the building had its own boiler room. On the ground floor there were public institutions necessary for life. Here was the famous grocery store "Deli number 15", an ice cream parlor, its own post office, a hairdresser and many other organizations. Often the famous Soviet skyscraper "starred" in the movies and was mentioned in literary works.
