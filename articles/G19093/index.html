What are underwater cameras?

A huge number of different models of different price categories and with different characteristics are presented on the market. All this seriously complicates the choice. And even professionals have a hard time. Usually, experts in the field of extreme photography distinguish two categories of underwater cameras:

  • Chambers for shallow water. Those with which it is not recommended to shoot at a depth of more than 10 meters, the time the camera operates under water varies from 20 to 60 minutes. Their mission is to capture an intense rest. The case of such devices is sealed, which saves the equipment from moisture, dust and shock. The camera is suitable not only for underwater shooting, but also for work in rain, snow and wind. The disadvantage of shallow water machines is that in order to get high-quality and clear pictures, the water must be crystal clear. Otherwise, the photos will be blurry and not sharp.
  • Cameras for depth. This technique belongs to the professional and is as much as possible protected from moisture and blows. Designed for depths of up to 60 meters, however, some cameras are capable of more. The advantage of such cameras is their long operating time in extreme conditions. On average, the duration of the shooting can be from an hour to two. Due to the high technical characteristics, the pictures are obtained in high quality and with high resolution. Professional underwater cameras come with specialized filters, flashes and lighting equipment. Such devices are well protected not only from water pressure, but also from mechanical damage.

If the purpose of buying a camera is to capture a bright vacation, then take a closer look at cameras designed for shallow water.

What to look for when choosing?

First of all, you should decide for what purpose the device is bought. When choosing, you should pay attention to such factors:

  1. The most important thing is depth. It depends on this parameter how deeply it will be possible to immerse the camera in water.
  2. The second most important parameter that affects the quality of images is the lens. Orientation is at the capture angle. In order to regulate the process and pamper your loved ones with high-quality images, it is recommended to purchase a camera with removable lenses or pay attention to models that differ in a wide range of capture angle values.
  3. In order for the photos to turn out to be of the highest quality at a depth exceeding 15 meters, you will have to stock up on additional flash. Stationary flash output is only suitable for shooting at depths of up to 10 meters. With all this, shooting is recommended in the daytime or spend money on a professional flash.
  4. The quality of photographs depends on the optics and resolution of the camera‚Äôs matrix. For example, for amateur photos at shallow depths, a value of 10 megapixels and ordinary glass lenses are suitable. If you need professional images, then the appropriate resolution is from 16 megapixels, and the optics should be professional.
  5. Pay attention to such a parameter as the strength of technology. After all, extreme photo shoots carry the risk of a device falling, for example, from a two-meter height. It is important that the camera continues to work stably. You can learn about the strength of the camera from the technical passport, which indicates the strength coefficient.
  6. The interface, as a parameter, is important only for convenience and does not carry any practical benefit. Choose a camera with a simple interface, because shooting under water is very dynamic, which is why you will have to quickly respond and set the necessary parameters.
  7. For shooting under water, an image stabilizer is vital.
  8. A fairly important parameter is the battery operation. Even when shooting on land, it is very disappointing when the battery sits at the most inopportune moment, what can we say about shooting the underwater world. Do not forget that when using an additional flash, the battery will drain many times faster, so the battery power is quite important when choosing a high-quality camera. If anything, a spare battery will always come to the rescue.
  9. In addition to depth, it is important to pay attention to temperature indicators at which the camera will function perfectly.
