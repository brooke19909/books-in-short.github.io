Crafts from natural material in kindergarten: interesting and exciting

Crafts from natural material will help develop the general and fine motor skills of the child, imagination, and broaden their horizons .

In kindergarten, kids love creative activities. Lessons help children look at familiar things with different eyes. They teach to create, see the beauty and functionality of the simplest objects around them. Contribute to innovative thinking and finding new solutions, develop creative abilities. Lay confidence, observation, respect for nature. Educate the ecological culture of preschoolers.

The collection of materials is best done with the children. Cones and feathers, roots, twigs, pebbles, leaves, seeds are useful. It is important to show and teach how to properly dry and store the collected materials.





Crafts from natural material in kindergarten must be done at a table in a well-lit place. So that children do not get tired, periodically carry out physical. minutes, exercises for the eyes, telling poems and riddles about trees, flowers, animals. Classes unite children in a team, make them more united. Joint work contributes to the development of communication skills in children.

Common crafts from natural material in kindergarten: from leaves, from cones, from shells, from seeds and straw. All kinds of applications, compositions, collages, prints. Acorns are ideal for creating voluminous animals and dollware. Needles are used for the legs and needles of hedgehogs. Nuts are used as rattle toys. Birch bark is one of the most durable and popular materials since ancient times.





Dry branches go as arms and legs of men. The grass becomes brittle when dried, so it only fastens the details. Moss (as an addition to the background) - for the whole craft. Rosehips and mountain ash are attached with matches, toothpicks. As a result, gnomes, bright beads and other unique little things appear. A lot of options!

Cognitively draw using these materials. For example, stick the first halves of different leaves onto cardboard, and invite the child to finish the missing parts on their own.
