Sights of Tatarstan - from antiquity to the present day. What to see in Tatarstan - in Kazan, Sviyazhsk and other cities of the republic?

Tatarstan is today considered one of the fastest growing regions of the Russian Federation. It is also famous for its multifaceted and rich culture. The Republic of Tatarstan gained rapid popularity after the Universiade in 2013, in which athletes from around the world took part. The capital of the Republic of Kazan is a city with a long history, as well as a cultural, commercial and economic center. To see the many attractions of Tatarstan, hundreds of travelers come here annually. And here definitely there is something to see!



Short characteristic of Tatarstan

It is located at the confluence of the Kama and Volga rivers . The Tatar Autonomous Socialist Republic was created in 1929, in 1990 it was transformed into the Republic of Tatarstan as part of the Russian Federation. The capital Kazan is an ancient year, which was founded in 1005.

About 3.8 million people live in this region. Moreover, 75% of them are residents of cities. There are two official languages ‚Äã‚Äãin Tatarstan - Tatar and Russian. In total, 115 different nationalities live here. The president is considered the head of the republic. Tatarstan has its own anthem, the Cabinet of Ministers, the Constitution and the State Council.

The geographical position of the republic

The region is located on the East European Plain, in the forest-steppe and forest zones. The climate of the region is temperate continental with rather pronounced seasons. The sights of Tatarstan are best visited from April to October, when the snow no longer lies and pleasant warm weather has settled. Snow usually falls in mid-November and lasts until the end of March, and sometimes until the beginning of April.

In addition to such large rivers as the Kama and Volga, approximately 500 small rivers flow in the region. There is also a reservoir: Kuibyshev, Zaim, Nizhnekamsk and Karabash. About 15% of the territory of Tatarstan is occupied by deciduous forests. Over 1,500 valuable natural objects are protected, the largest of them are the Volga-Kama nature reserve zone and the Nizhny Kama National Park.



Sights of Kazan

Kazan is rightfully considered the pearl of the republic. Here are a myriad of historical monuments. Nevertheless, the Kazan Kremlin, which is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List, is considered to be the visiting card of the city.

The view of the Kul-Sharif Mosque and the Kremlin from the side of the Kazanka River is perhaps the most famous image of Kazan. The capital of Tatarstan is a unique color of cultures of the West and the East, Christianity and Islam. Mosques here harmoniously coexist with Orthodox cathedrals.

An unforgettable impression is made by a walk along the historical quarter of the city - along the streets of Bauman, Tukay and Gorky, the Bulak Canal, the Old Tatar settlement. One of the most important modern symbols of the city can safely be called the Millennium Bridge, which was built in part of the 10,000th anniversary of Kazan. The Kazan Circus, which in shape resembles a flying saucer, may surprise . The Riviera water park also gained great popularity. Quite often, tour operators offer bus tours to the capital of Kazan with a mandatory check-in to this institution.

The sights of Kazan do not end there. The city has a lot of interesting places to visit. This is Chatyr-Tau Mountain (the highest mountain of a single mountain range in the region), the Ascension Monastery, Kara-Kul Lake, etc.

Museums

To learn more about this region, it is worth visiting the museums of Tatarstan: the Museum of Military Glory, the Circus Museum, the National Art Gallery, the Fire Protection Museum, the Museum of the 1000th Anniversary of Kazan, the Museum of Islamic Culture, the Botanical Museum, the Vasilyev Art Gallery and other no less fascinating institutions .



Orthodox shrines

The most important Orthodox shrine of Kazan and Russia as a whole can be called the Church of Our Lady of Kazan. Here are the most revered icons in Orthodoxy. The Peter and Paul Temple, which proudly rises above Kazan, is considered no less picturesque.

The Epiphany Cathedral is one of the most important symbols of the capital of the republic. The city also houses the ancient temple of the Yaroslavl Miracle Workers, which is located on the Arsky cemetery. In addition to all these shrines, you can visit the monasteries of Tatarstan: Zilatnov Assumption female, Raifsky and Ioanno-Baptist male and others.

Not far from Kazan, in the village of Voznesenskoye, there is a unique temple of the Mother of God of the Tikhvin Icon. Previously, there was a fairly well-known image of "All Who Sorrow Joy." In the village of Borisoglebsk there is the church of the Great Martyrs Boris and Gleb. It was erected in 1600. In 1888, her building was again restored after a fire.

Sviyazhsk

The city of Sviyazhsk is a picturesque place of Tatarstan and a monument of local importance. This fortified city was founded by Ivan the Terrible himself in 1551 at the confluence of the Sviyaga and Volga rivers . During the years of Soviet rule, hundreds of thousands of prisoners were exiled here. After the creation of the Kuibyshev reservoir, Sviyazhsk turned into an island. You can get to it by a dam by car or river transport.

Since 2011, a grandiose program has been launched to turn this place into a museum reserve. Now in Sviyazhsk work is underway on the reconstruction of various monuments, some of which were founded in the 16th century.

This island island is considered one of the most popular holiday destinations for both tourists and residents of the republic. Various contests, festivals, festivals are often held in Sviyazhsk.

Bulgar

We continue to closely study the sights of Tatarstan. On the banks of the Volga lies the picturesque city of Bolgar. It was built in the place where the ancient Bulgar was previously located. At that time, the city was a major economic center and the largest settlement of the Golden Horde. Bulgar was destroyed in the 15th century.

The most remarkable construction of the town is the settlement of Bulgar. This complex consists of numerous monuments of the 13-15th centuries: the Small Minaret, the Cathedral Mosque, the Khan's tomb, etc.



Elabuga

Elabuga is one of the oldest cities in the republic, whose age has long exceeded one thousand. True, the exact date of its occurrence has not been clarified, it is only known that it was founded at the turn of the 9-11th centuries. The main historical monument of Elabuga is the Devil's Hillfort. All the main attractions of the village were combined into a museum reserve. It included memorial museums of various figures that are related to Yelabuga (Durova, Ankylosing spondylitis, Tsvetaeva). There is also an art gallery of Shishkin.

In general, Elabuga is a very beautiful city, but the rapid economic development has somewhat spoiled the characteristic architectural ensemble, which should be saturated with the spirit of antiquity.



The sights of Tatarstan are a symbiosis of the past and future, East and West. To feel the unique atmosphere of the region, you must definitely visit here!
