Where the glyph meets

The statement that the glyph is the same graphic image as any component of information expressed in written form will be completely true. However, this is only partially true. If we draw an analogy with the letter, then it, being an integral part of the word, does not have an independent meaning, representing only a sound expression, which, only combining with other elements, is able to convey the content of the concept.

The glyph, on the other hand, has a specific meaning in itself, and in connection with this, the requirements for writing it are quite high and canonized in a certain way. This is confirmed by numerous archaeological artifacts discovered around the world: in Egypt, in the city of Kopan, in Greece, Turkey and in the mountains of Ingushetia. Petroglyphs carved in stone by ancient masters are valuable sources of information, thanks to which we can now get to the bottom of the mysteries of history.

There are characters carved on stones and carved on a wooden plane, they can be in the form of a pictogram or logogram.

It is also known that a glyph is such an image of a font that gives it a certain exclusivity, which is sometimes necessary in typography. In addition, symbolism is indispensable in creating computer programs. All kinds of graphic symbols are widely used in them, both complex, for example, an icon, and elementary ones - letters, numbers, periods, commas, etc.

Sacred meaning

At first, the researchers suggested that the glyph is the same writing element as all that were previously discovered and used by ancient people to transmit information, including everyday content. However, having studied in more detail archaeological finds with signs embossed on them, scientists realized that these symbols not only carry some independent meaning, but also have a certain power that goes back to ancient magical rituals, and have nothing to do with everyday life.

The oldest types of writing, their heyday and decline can be traced on the example of Egypt. Initially, pictography prevailed there, as evidenced by samples with reduced images of the object. After some time, the symbols acquired a broader interpretation, and the glyph of the Sun became not just the name of the star, but also indicated the light or dark time of the day. That is, if necessary, to inform about an event that happened in the afternoon, it was possible by depicting the sun, and during a night incident they painted a lunar sign.

The heyday of glyphs in Egypt can be considered the period of Roman rule, when there were more than 6,000 characters. However, they were used for the needs of priests who wrote secret texts with signs.

For everyday needs there was an hieratic cursive. The complex system of hieroglyphs remained in the past due to Christian influence.

Glyph can be used both in the singular and in the kit, being a compound. In addition, as a unit of glyph graphics, it is used in linguistics as a complementary component to expand the meaning of the main character.

There is still such a thing as an allograph. This is when a couple or several glyphs are equal in essence, but differ in graphic performance. They can be used in different pieces of text. Drawing an analogy with the alphabet, we can say that this is a different spelling of the same letter.

What ends the argument with the gods

Among the many variations of the already known sacred symbols, a special place is occupied by the glyphs of Arachne. The story of their appearance is reflected in one of the myths of Ancient Greece, which speaks of a duel between the goddess Athena and the unsurpassed weaver from the mortal world Arachne. The girl really was an outstanding craftswoman, and there were no peers in the art of weaving.

And she was extremely proud, and it was her quality that prompted the weaver to challenge Athena herself. The competition ended sadly: the goddess tore apart the canvas woven by Arachnoy, while hitting the girl with a shuttle on her forehead. The weaver could not survive such humiliation and decided to take her own life by trying to hang herself. She almost succeeded: she reached the limits of Navi - that was the name of the kingdom of the dead.

However, Athena returned the proud woman to the world of the living, having determined her punishment: to become a spider, to hang and weave forever and ever. And the knowledge brought from Navi by the weaver Arachna, has since been used in magic in the form of necroglyphs.

Runic characters

The origin of the runes is Scandinavian, and before the establishment of Christianity, there were two types of glyphs: the younger and the older futark. It was a kind of alphabet, but intended for polar purposes. The runes of the younger futark were known to all literate inhabitants of the Scandinavian countries: it was used in the preparation of contracts, letters, business accounts and other household records.

The runes of the older futark were accessible only to the initiates who knew their sacred meaning and used it for ritual purposes. Then, the ancient knowledge transferred to the initiates (according to myths) by the god Odin was lost. And only in our time they were restored by runologists.
