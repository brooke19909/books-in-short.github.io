Openwork socks with knitting needles: patterns. Patterns for socks

In vain, many consider knitting socks an uninteresting and dull occupation, completely unrelated to creativity. This is not true! With a little skill, diligence and patience, you can create not just socks, but a real masterpiece, emphasizing the old truth: beauty is everywhere.

Even knitting socks can be approached as creating a favorite accessory or an exclusive gift that will be welcome. So, the topic of this article is knitting openwork socks, or rather, choosing the right pattern.

Yarn Selection Tips

It's no secret that openwork patterns, even the simplest ones, are always exquisite and do not like thick yarn. Therefore, the thread for knitting should be medium, closer to thin. The reference will be the footage of a hundred-gram skein: the length of the thread in it optimal for good lace is 500-600 meters. Socks made of yarn of this thickness will be moderately thin and warm. The composition of the thread can be any, it all depends on your own preferences, but experienced craftsmen recommend choosing high-quality wool yarn with the addition of polyamide fiber to strengthen the product and extend the life of the product.

Today there are special series of sock yarn produced by many manufacturers, with the composition of 70-75% of wool and 25-30% of polyamide. Note that the masters in this category are supportive and respond very positively to it. Material consumption - about 100 grams per pair of socks.

Necessary tools

They perform work on five toe needles (they are also called needles, since there are no clamps on them). For non-thick yarn, the tools should also be proportionate, so needles No. 1,5-2 are the most suitable option. In the process, special markers may be required, which can serve as office paper clips, multi-colored remains of yarn, etc.

Overlaps, plaits and braids in knitting openwork socks

Openwork patterns when knitting socks are often complemented by various weaves - braids, medium-sized arana, elongated loops and other decorative techniques.

You can give many examples of the successful combination of lace and braids, but to talk about all the drawings in the same article is impossible, and not necessary. The models shown in the photographs clearly illustrate the possibility of combining various weaves with lace inserts or stripes.



Often there are small braids, in the intervals between overlaps of which the masters make openwork holes. Sometimes narrow aranes are used, which add extra density to the product and contrast with delicate textured paths.

In a word, patterns for socks can be made in different variations. Another important rule: the work must be done carefully, trying with equal force to pull the working thread so that the canvas is even and equally dense. Subject to these simple conditions, openwork socks with knitting needles, patterns of patterns for which we examined, will become original and unique.
