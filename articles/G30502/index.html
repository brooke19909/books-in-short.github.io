Roses from satin ribbons - an extravagant addition to the home interior

Roses from satin ribbons always look very bright and original. They look like small works of art. With their help, you can easily revive the home interior.



Roses look very extravagant on the curtains in the kitchen, the curtains in the bedroom, but most of all they are suitable for decorating a children's room. Little girls will be delighted with such beautiful and bright flowers attached to pillows, bedspreads, tablecloths. In addition, gathered in floral bouquets, roses will look great on summer dresses, hats and purses.

There are several ways to make roses from fabric. The most relatively simple is the method of folding roses from satin ribbons. The resulting flowers, as a rule, are decorated with gift bouquets, boxes and even cards. They can also decorate a headband, hairpin or ribbon on the head.

So, the process of making a flower is quite simple, but it requires perseverance and accuracy. If you interrupt it without collecting the rose to the end, the resulting blank will blossom. In order to make a medium-sized rose, you will need a satin ribbon about a meter long and five centimeters wide. If the atlas was not rolled up, then it should be ironed. If this is not done, the flower will turn out crumpled and sloppy.



Fold the tape in half in length. Rotate the lower end at right angles to the other half of the tape. Bend it so that it gently rests on top of the formed corner. Now the second end of the tape was below. Fix the resulting square with your index finger to avoid blooming and deformation. We shift the lower end up, bending it at the intersection of the ribbons. In such a simple way, fold the entire ribbon until there are tips no longer than five to six centimeters in length.



Do not forget to constantly keep the resulting column. If he slips out of his hands and at least slightly blooms, everything will have to be done anew. So, in the end you should get some kind of pigtails. Next, we grab the place where the tapes intersected with the thumb and forefinger and gently pull down at one end, while not letting go of the top edge. The satin column will be shortened. At the same time, it is necessary to ensure that the end of the ribbon does not slip through the fingers, but, being pulled into a bundle, forms a core. If you pull it hard, the column will open and the rose will not work. The shape of the rose from satin ribbons is largely dependent on the case. All flowers turn out different.



As soon as the rosette (from your point of view) becomes final, fasten its base with a thread, and best of all, sew so that it does not open. If you tighten too much, then it will turn out not very magnificent. In this case, it will have to be dissolved, ironed and everything done again. Roses from satin ribbons can be decorated with transparent nylon fabric or small flowers and leaves.



Having mastered such an easy way to create flowers from fabric, it will be possible to proceed to more ornate techniques. For example, Japanese Kansashi ornaments include satin ribbon flowers. Their patterns are very simple, but the result is original and exclusive hair clips and headbands. In ancient times, jewelry made of satin ribbons was very much appreciated among secular ladies at the royal courts. Court fashionistas decorated their outfits with separate large flowers and bouquets of small roses.
