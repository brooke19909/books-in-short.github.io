To tie such an envelope for a newborn with knitting needles, schemes are not needed. An envelope is knitted on 4 knitting needles in a circle, the 5th knitting needle is a working one.

Yarn - 100% merino thread, knitting needles - from 20 numbers.

Knitting begins with a set of 20 loops and distributing them on 4 knitting needles. It turns out 5 loops per needle. There are no hem loops in circular knitting. In order not to get confused, the first loop is marked with a marker. Having reached the marker, making a circle, they understand that the series is completed.

After tying 15 rows, 8 loops are closed and they continue to be knitted on two knitting needles with classic rotary knitting. So they knit with a front smooth surface another 10 rows. At the end, the last row is closed and the sides of the rectangular valve, obtained at the top, are sewn from the wrong side.

Simple summer envelope

Summer envelopes for discharge for newborns do not have to be openwork.

An unusual envelope looks good in simple loops that do not distract from the shape of the canvas.





Such an envelope is knitted with thin knitting needles of the front or garter stitch. The height of the product should be equal to the height of the child + 25 cm for the formation of a cape on the head, and the width - 3.5 girths of the body - about 70 cm.

A rectangle is knitted, the length and width of which correspond to the measured parameters. Then it is necessary to tie a strip 3 cm wide and 50 cm long. It will act as a belt, which fixes the envelope. When the strip is ready, it is sewn to the corner on the right side.

The kid is wrapped in such an envelope, as if in a cocoon, and bandaged with a belt.

Envelope of motives

Envelopes for discharge for newborns, knitted from motifs, look unusual and beautiful.

First you need to choose the motive from which the envelope will be knitted. Then you need to tie the test sample. The sample must be washed, dried and steamed - the yarn after heat treatment can behave unpredictably, which will affect the appearance and size of the product.

Then, based on the obtained parameters, a pattern is drawn. It is a rectangle made up of motifs. On the pattern, you need to calculate how many motives you need to knit to get the product of the required size. For a children's envelope you will need 100 motifs measuring 10 x 10 cm + 25 motifs to process the hood.

When all the parts are connected, the parts are first stitched together, and then the fabric is laid out and stapled like a regular envelope.
