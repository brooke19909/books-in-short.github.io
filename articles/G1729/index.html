In Thailand in September: tours, weather, holidays, reviews

Vacation in September? It's time to go to Thailand - an exotic and unpredictable country. In September, the rainy season ends, every day there is less and less rainfall, and the sea becomes calm and transparent. This month is ideal for both lovers of beach lounging and those who wish to enjoy sightseeing tours. In other words, if you decide to go to Thailand in September, you will surely enjoy this trip.

Thailand - a country of contrasts

Any exotic country for a novice tourist can be called a country of contrasts. But Thailand has surpassed everyone else in this respect. Against the backdrop of modern fashionable skyscrapers, you can easily see the old shack of a poor Thai, in which there is not even furniture. Thailand is the only country where there is no division into rich and poor. Here everyone is equal and there is no social inequality. We advise you to go to Thailand in September. You can not only feel this unusual atmosphere, but also enjoy the magnificent weather.





Thailand Attractions

Thousands of tourists from all over the world come to Thailand every year. Here everyone will discover something extraordinary. Going to Thailand in September, you can get under heavy rains, but this will not stop you from enjoying sightseeing trips. Many beautiful exotic places and a wide variety of entertainments will not leave indifferent even the most fastidious guest.

Royal Palace

This is a place that no self-respecting tourist will pass by. For Thais, this palace is a kind of shrine. Its construction began more than two hundred years ago and lasted a long time. The complex was periodically completed and rebuilt. Today it is a huge territory with towers, greenhouses, galleries and lakes. The length of the palace wall is about two kilometers. You can move around the castle at any time of the day, while respecting decency in clothing. It should be as closed as possible. The whole territory is divided into two parts. One of them is intended for tourists. The second is for members of the royal dynasty. Despite the fact that none of the members of the royal family has been living here for a long time, it is strictly forbidden to enter this part of the castle.





Temple of the Emerald Buddha

The temple is located at the royal palace and gathers many tourists and pilgrims all year round. This sacred place for Thais is shrouded in legends and legends. On the territory of the temple you will see a large number of statues belonging to different eras. Real professionals will appreciate the library located in the Temple of the Emerald Buddha. Books in this library have been collected for more than one hundred years. It is amazing that the main Buddha statue, after which the temple is named, is very small and not made of emerald at all. This statue is changed by the emperor three times a year in various clothes.





Floating market

This market in Thailand is not only the most unusual and exotic, but also the most expensive. Prices are much higher than in stores, but the atmosphere prevailing here is worth it. Boats sell mostly tropical fruits and souvenirs. If you are planning to organize your vacation in Thailand in September, be sure to visit this unusual European market. Fans of bargaining will surely find a worthy opponent here and will be able to significantly reduce the price.





Tiger Temple

The name of the temple is associated with an ancient legend. It is believed that once in the cave, where the temple is now located, a tiger lived. He kept the whole district at bay. But once the tiger left its home, and since then Buddhist monks have been living and praying here. There is an observation deck near the cave, but getting to it is not so easy. To do this, you need to overcome a steep high staircase. It is customary to take a liter of drinking water with you and fill it with a vat that stands on the site. This water is for those who have not been given such a lift.





Buddha's Magic Garden

This sacred place is associated with the name of the farmer Nim Thongsuk, who at the age of 77 decided to create this unusual garden. Over the past 15 years of his life, he has created a variety of sculptures of mythical creatures, animals and people. In addition to sculptures in the garden there are small houses - a haven for wandering monks. In the depths of the garden, among the stone sculptures entangled in vines, there is a crypt where the farmer and his wife rest.





Khao Khanab Twin Cliffs

If you decide to go to Thailand in September, be sure to take a boat trip along the Krabi River. It offers a magnificent view of the twin rocks of Khao Khanab. Unusual name due to the striking symmetry of their location. In the rocks there are a lot of caves to which wooden platforms lead. Once in these caves were found the remains of people. According to scientists, they tried to escape here from a flood, but died of starvation.





Koh Samui

Locals call this island the diamond of the Gulf of Thailand. Going to Thailand in September, it is worth choosing the island of Koh Samui for a stop. The rains at this time of year here practically stop, and tourists enjoy basking in the rays of the tropical sun. Lovers of outdoor activities will be offered diving or water skiing. Very often surf lovers go to Thailand in September. In windy weather, this is the perfect place to practice this sport. Many spectators usually gather on the shore from those who themselves have not yet decided to try to conquer the waves.





Park "Safari World"

The most popular park of its kind in the world. All animals here are in their natural habitat. Throughout the park laid car routes for tourists. Movement in the park is allowed only on a specially equipped car, which ensures the safety of tourists. The park is divided into three sections: savannah animals, marine inhabitants and birds. An entertainment town is equipped for the youngest visitors.

Naturally, this is not all the attractions that can be seen in Thailand. It is simply impossible to list all the beauties, and to describe is unrealistic. It is worth visiting Thailand to see all this with your own eyes.





When is the best time to relax in Thailand?

If you have chosen Thailand as your holiday destination, then September is ideal. This month, seasonal rains cease, the influx of tourists subsides. If you go to Thailand in September, the prices will pleasantly surprise you. The cost of vouchers for seven-day tours from 27,000 rubles per person. This is an order of magnitude lower than at other times of the year, when there is an influx of tourists.

Tours in Thailand in September

Many Russian travel companies conclude agreements with local hotels and provide tours to Thailand at any time of the year. Especially great demand for holidays in Thailand in September, because the tropical rains have already stopped, but the air and land have not yet had time to become very hot.



The most popular hotel among Russians is the Royal Cliff Beach Resort, located in Pattaya. It attracts our tourists with a low cost of trips, convenient location and good service. In this city there are several more hotels that are no less popular among our compatriots. A large influx of tourists from Russia recently did not go unnoticed by the organizers of the holiday. More and more often cafes and restaurants with Russian cuisine began to appear on the streets of Thailand, and even Russian karaoke bars have recently opened. Therefore, you can safely plan your vacation in September in Thailand. The weather here, unfortunately, is not always predictable, but hotel owners will probably offer you ways to diversify your leisure time even in the rain.
