The results of the Second World War: briefly and on the case

The results of the Second World War are very difficult to describe briefly. The war itself affected the fate of many millions of people and many states. Below we will try to tell as concisely, clearly and concretely as possible what were the results of the Second World War. She radically changed the fate of many countries in Asia, Europe and America.





By its results, the war for a long time determined the geopolitical position and further fate of European countries almost until the end of the twentieth century.

The results of World War II: briefly and clearly

Of course, the most important outcome was the defeat of fascism and the restoration of the sovereignty of the countries captured by fascist Germany and its allies. The state machines of militarism and fascism were completely defeated. The military power of the USSR was actually recognized by the Yalta-Potsdam system. The alliance acquired critical importance as a world power to be reckoned with.

Naturally, the Soviet Union, which accounted for 90% of human losses, gained immense moral authority. The masses of European countries began to see in him the guarantor of democratic transformations in the world. People were confident that the Tehran, Yalta and Potsdam Conference laid the foundation for the consent and cooperation of world powers. In addition, a powerful anti-colonial movement began in Africa and Asia. By the end of the war, Lebanon, Syria, Vietnam, and Indonesia had proclaimed their independence.





In a nutshell, these are practically the decomposed results of World War II point by point.

Conference Results

At the conferences of the countries of the anti-Hitler coalition , fateful decisions were made on the structure of the post-war world in Yalta and Potsdam. In Germany, democratization, demilitarization and denationalization were carried out. Also made changes to the outline of the borders of some European countries.

In particular, Czechoslovakia again restored its borders since 1938. At the initiative of the Soviet government, Poland received part of the German territories. And in 1955, an agreement was signed on the restoration of independent Austria. In October 1956, the USSR and Japan signed a declaration on ending the state of war and restoring diplomatic relations. This all created the prerequisites for a lasting democratic peace.





However, such results of World War II did not live up to expectations. In short, the real world was still very, very far away.

Conflict between former allies

Contradictions arose between the Western countries and the USSR as to what the post-war world should be like. The coalition broke up, which was able to unite countries with different geopolitical goals during the war.

Western states, accustomed to dominating the world, did not want to perceive the USSR as an equal player in the political arena. However, the Yalta-Potsdam system was able to keep the world from global war for half a century. The war has demonstrated that a coalition can only be successful when the allies trust each other. Only taking into account economic, political and cultural factors can make a coalition effective. All these are the results of World War II.

Historical lessons

Wars happen because descendants forget the historical lessons of previous wars. The first lesson that the war taught was that the past has a great influence on the long term. The future originates in the past. For example, the Versailles peace, which consolidated the results of World War I, essentially created the conditions for a future global confrontation.





The mistakes made by the leadership of the victorious countries were expensive. This was shown by World War 2. Its results, including this one, should serve as a lesson for the rulers of the future.
