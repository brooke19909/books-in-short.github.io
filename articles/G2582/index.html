Gazprom Bonds - Protective Asset

In a crisis state of the economy, people have a problem of saving earned money. There are several options: investing in real estate, buying currency, replenishing strategic food supplies, and placing them on bank deposits.

In addition to the above, there is another possibility - the acquisition of debt receipts of reliable companies. Long-term securities provide an opportunity to receive a small but regular income annually.

One of these securities are Gazprom bonds.



Issuer Rating

PJSC Gazprom is a company in the energy sector. Types of production activities:

  • mineral exploration;
  • handling of oil and gas raw materials;
  • processing and sale of hydrocarbon products;
  • production and sale of electric and thermal energy.

PJSC Gazprom is a leader in the top three global public commodity companies according to Forbes magazine.

The Government of the Russian Federation controls the activities of PJSC Gazprom.

The company's reliability rating and forecast for 2017 are:

  • Dagong: AAA; stable;
  • S&P: BB +; positive;
  • Fitch: BBB-; stable;
  • Moody`s: Ba1; stable.

Alternative to deposit

The deposit is more familiar to the consumer. Compare the proposed type of investment with a deposit.

Entrance to the deal. A deposit in some banks can be opened from one thousand rubles. Typically, debt securities are issued in denominations of 1 thousand rubles. But they are traded on the stock exchange most often below face value with a lot of one paper. That is, you can invest less than a thousand rubles.

On deposits, the client makes a profit in the form of declared interest. The yield on a Gazprom bond consists of coupon payments plus the difference between the face value and the acquisition price. Suppose a customer bought one paper at a price of 95 percent of the face value, that is, for 950 rubles. Then, upon repayment, he will receive a profit of 50 Russian rubles. This is a plus for coupon payments.

As a deposit can be closed at the request of a client, a bond can be sold on any day. But in case of early termination of the deposit agreement, the depositor usually loses interest. And when selling a bond, a coupon profit is accrued to him for the time of holding the security.

Often, for operations with a deposit you need to visit a bank. The client can perform all actions with bonds on his own, concluding an agreement with a broker and setting up a trading program.



Ruble bonds: price, chart, yield

The denomination of ruble bonds of Gazprom is 1000 rubles.

There are several ruble issues in circulation. For example, the BO-19 and BO-20 series with a maturity date of 2043. The yield on them is determined by the issuer based on the percentage of the coupon period K TP :

To TP = (CPI - 100)% + 1%,

where CPI is the consumer price index.

Coupon jackpot is directly related to the current inflation index. During the existence of the BO-19 series, the percentage of the coupon ranged from 7.3 percent to 17.4. Recently, revenue has been 8.3%.
