Is it legal to put xenon in fog lights?

To understand which lamps are allowed to be installed in foglights, it is immediately worthwhile to understand the features of the PTF itself.

Fog lights are the same external lighting device of the vehicle as the main ones, despite the fact that they play the role of additional lighting. Therefore, they also have a designation of the type of lamps used. For example, if the letter ‚ÄúH‚Äù is present on them, this means that for this type of headlights only halogen lamps should be used. If the letter D is present, the car owner should know that gas discharge lamps, that is, xenon lamps, are provided for lighting.

Thus, it is illegal to put xenon in fog lights that are designed to use halogen lamps. This violation may result in punishment in the form of deprivation of rights for six months or more. By the way, this rule also applies to headlights.

However, those car owners who equipped their car with a PTF specifically designed for xenon, or purchased a car with such headlights, need not worry.

The fog lights that are equipped with foreign cars at the factory belong to class D. The difference is the absence of a reflector in them and the presence of a special lens that collects scattered light into the beam, thereby ensuring even illumination of the roadway in front of the car.

Xenon Installation Rules

The installation process of xenon lamps is practically no different from the installation of halogen lamps. The only difference is that the latter "feed" directly from the car's on-board network. And in xenon lamps, power is supplied through the ignition unit.

The main problem when installing xenon is the output of high-voltage wires through the back cover of the headlight. To do this, you need to drill a hole in it, the size of which will correspond to the size of the anther that comes with the lamp. Through the hole wires are output. After that, it is closed with anther, which keeps the headlamp tight.
