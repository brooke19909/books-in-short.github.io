Interesting and practical crochet felt slippers

One of the most common drawbacks of knitted indoor slippers is the quick wear of the sole. No matter what coating the floors in the house are equipped with, after a month or two, rubbed areas appear. As a result, the sole has to be changed or get rid of slippers at all. Slippers on felt soles are a great way out. They are crocheted quite simply, you just need to find the right materials.

What to use for the sole

Simple felt insoles serve well as a sturdy base for indoor shoes. They can be bought almost anywhere, especially in winter.

Another option is to buy felt and cut out the sole of the right size from it. This method is convenient if you need to immediately produce several pairs, since the cost of buying a sheet of felt exceeds the cost of insoles.

Attaching slippers to the sole

Some craftswomen prefer to reduce the amount of work for themselves and to impose slippers directly on the sole. To do this, holes should be made in the felt. This is best achieved with a device for working with leather products. Sometimes an awl helps, but such holes are not large enough.



It is required to choose a suitable diameter of the holes and make them at a distance of one or one and a half centimeters from each other. This distance depends on the thickness of the yarn used: the thinner it is, the closer the holes should be.

If there is no special device, you can get out of the situation by performing a buttonhole stitch along the edge of the sole (in this case, the first row will consist of single crochet stitches sewn into arches formed by a buttonhole stitch).

The next step is to proceed directly to the manufacture of the main fabric of the product. Slippers on a felt sole crochet begin to knit from the "bushes" of 3-5 columns with a crochet. The basis of each "bush" is a hole made. A different number of columns may be required, the thicker the thread, the fewer there will be.

When the first row is finished, you can proceed to the next step.

Sides of slippers

Further knitting should continue smoothly, without additions or cuts. The optimum height of the canvas is four centimeters. The color of the yarn does not matter, but the pattern is important. Only tight knitting is suitable for the sides of the slippers. Otherwise, the fingers resting on the toe will deform the crocheted slippers. There is no scheme as such, the work takes place in circular rows with simple single crochet or double crochet columns.



Crocheted felt sole slippers: sock shaping

The photo above shows how to start knitting the upper part of the toe of slippers. In the first row, 4 columns are made in the center of the toe, then the number of columns is increased by one on each side in each row. The work goes in straight and reverse rows, with the simultaneous attachment of this canvas to the sides of the slippers.

Thus, each row increases the number of columns by two. When the length of the sock becomes equal to 10 cm, work should be stopped. The depth of the product can be made more or less than the specified size. The height of the sock is determined by fitting.

Semi-circular toe for slippers

An interesting way to decorate slippers on felt sole, made crocheted, is to make a toe of a semicircular shape. This part is knitted separately, and then sewn into the right place.

In fact, any ornament can become the basis for such a sock. The main condition can be called the absence of large holes, as this will reduce the level of comfort of the product. A floral motif or just an openwork semicircle will decorate slippers with a crochet. Patterns of fragments can be obtained from patterns of circular motifs.



Completion of work: pagolenka formation

The upper part of slippers is also knitted in circular rows. The first row always consists of single crochet, then you can apply some kind of pattern.

The columns of the first row of pagolenka pass along the last row of the side of the slippers, as well as along the open side of the toe. Having closed a circular row, it should be knitted exactly without additions.



The height of the pagolenka is determined only by the personal choice of the craftswoman or the person who will wear crocheted knitted slippers. Schemes and description of the pattern for pagolenka can be used absolutely any.

However, it is worth considering that short slippers are easier and faster to put on, they are more convenient than high ones, and their backs are not trampled. On the other hand, a long pagolenok is much warmer.

As an alternative to the described method, you can knit slippers in any convenient way, then fix the felt on the manufactured sole and sew it tightly. Thus, you get home-made shoes with a knitted insole and felt sole.



Crochet slippers for children are knitted almost the same as for adults, they only reduce proportions and choose more colorful colors.
