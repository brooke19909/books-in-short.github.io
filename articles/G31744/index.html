Hall sensor in a smartphone - what is it, what is it for, principle of operation

A modern smartphone can be so multifunctional that its owner is not always aware of all the characteristics and capabilities of his device. For example, do you know what a Hall sensor is in a smartphone? How does it work and what is it for? We suggest you learn more about this feature!

What does a Hall sensor mean in a smartphone?

We already know why the gadget needs an approximation module or a gyroscope. But what is a Hall sensor in a smartphone? It is a determinant of position whose action is based on the Hall effect. This device detects both the presence of a magnetic field and measures its intensity.

The sensor and the effect itself is named for the famous physicist E. Hall. It was this scientist who established that when a conductor plate is placed in the center of the magnetic field through which alternating current flows, Hall voltage (the transverse potential difference) will appear in it (the field).

In the described case, the electrons in the conductor are deflected strictly perpendicular to the direction of the magnetic field itself. Hence, their density on different parts of the plate will be excellent. It is this potential difference that the meter fixes.

And what is a Hall sensor in a smartphone? This is an even simpler device - it is designed to determine only the presence of a magnetic field, without measuring its strength. In addition, the gadget is probably also equipped with a magnetic sensor, which allows you to use your smartphone as a compass.

Where is it used?

We have established what a Hall sensor is in a smartphone. However, gadgets are not the only field of application of the invention, which also differs in the possibility of contactless control of any device.

I must say that the Hall effect was discovered relatively long ago - in 1879. And for the first time they put it into practice only 75 years after this event. It turned out to be useful for cars - the sensor was used to measure the angle of the crankshaft, camshaft. In older models of cars, the Hall sensor detected the moment of spark formation.

Further along the path of progress, the device began to be used in complex systems:

  • proximity switches;
  • systems for reading magnetic codes;
  • devices used for contactless determination of current strength in conductors;
  • liquid level meters;
  • ion rocket engines.

In addition, it was found that the Hall sensor is able to replace magnetically controlled sealed contacts - reed switches. They have a wide scope: microelectronics, security alarms, keyboards, elevators, headphones.

Why is the Hall sensor in the smartphone?

We found out that this device determines the presence of a magnetic field. But then why do we need a Hall sensor in a smartphone today? Everything is simple - it determines whether a smart cover with a magnetic fastener is open or closed. If the magnet is far (the sensor "does not see" it at a certain distance), then a command is given to turn on the display. If the fastener is close (which means the user has closed the cover), then the sensor signals the system that the screen needs to be put into sleep mode.

Removed the cover of the cover - a command from the sensor to display complete information on the display.

Other features in smartphones

Interaction with magnetic caps is the most common sensor application in modern gadgets. However, it should be noted that it was successfully used in earlier models of smartphones:

  • The digital compass function operated thanks to the Hall sensor. And today it is used by navigation applications for a general improvement in positioning and higher accuracy in determining the motion vector.
  • Activation / deactivation of the backlight when opening / closing the clamshell device. Here, the action is similar to the current situation with magnetic covers.
