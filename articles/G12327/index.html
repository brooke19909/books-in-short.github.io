Tourist Attractions Corfu

One of the best tourist destinations in Greece is the island of Corfu. Corfu Residence 4 inspires tourists to explore local attractions beyond stunning beaches, delicious restaurants and bustling bars.

Kassiopi is the largest village in the northeast, known for its picturesque surroundings and ancient architecture. On its territory is Mount Pantokrator, with hills covered with olive groves, vines and citrus plantations. It will be interesting for guests to stroll along the narrow streets of the village, knowing that the great Cicero and Emperor Neuron once walked here. Here you can also have a tasty meal in traditional restaurants and cafes or make an unforgettable selfie on the background of the port or beaches.

The Panagia Kassiopi Tissa Church is an interesting place to spend a few hours there. It is also worth visiting the Byzantine castle, which was once used to protect the city and port from attack.





Kerkyra is the Old Town of Corfu, filled with charming historical narrow streets where you can wander. Any tourist map here will be irrelevant, the route for research will be found by itself. No matter which direction you take a walk, guests will still be minutes away from the coast. Many visitors to Kerkyra recognize the strong Venetian influence on the city. This is noticeable in the architecture of local fountains and houses, unlike the rest of Greece. The entire territory is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Here you can visit interesting souvenir shops, restaurants and taverns.

Achillion Palace is another attraction of the island, which was built by the Empress of Austria, Elizabeth of Bavaria, to forget her personal tragedy - the death of her son. She was deeply attached to Corfu and obsessed with his beauty. The Italian architect Raffaele Caritto built this palace, inspired by the legends of the hero of antiquity Achilles. When the empress was killed, German Kaiser Wilhelm II acquired this castle as a private property, preserving beautiful gardens and architecture until it was plundered during the First and Second World Wars. The building was both a military headquarters and a meeting place at the highest level, and only very recently became a museum.

Paleokastritsa is a village in the northwest of Corfu, located between the hills of olive groves and citrus orchards. Like most Corfu sites, this is an incredibly picturesque place in Greece, where you can visit various souvenir shops. Popular spots in Paleokastritsa include the 12th-century Monastery of the Virgin. Here you can visit the museum and the nearby village of Lacones, where you can enjoy a good breakfast. Water sports are very popular in Paleokastritsa. It is easy to rent all the necessary equipment. Energetic tourists will enjoy canoeing around the beaches on a great sunny day. You can also go to the famous caves of Paleokastritsy, where you should go diving.
