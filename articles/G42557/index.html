Yarovoy hotels: overview

On the shore of Lake Big Yarovoye in 1942, the construction of a chemical plant began. In 1943, the bromine plant was evacuated from the city of Perekop. After the unification of these enterprises, the development of the chemists' village, called Yarovoye, began. The town received the status of the city in 1993. The population is approximately 18 thousand people and may increase by an additional 10 thousand in the summer season due to vacationers. They are attracted here by the presence of salt and fresh lakes, water and mud procedures that help in the treatment of skin, neurological and gynecological diseases.





For vacationers and travelers in the Altai Territory, Yarovoy hotels have been built. Many locals also rent their homes and apartments.
