Which sea in Thailand and which ocean does it belong to?

The Kingdom of Thailand is located in the southwest of Indochina. Also, its territories cover the northern part of the Malacca Peninsula. Looking at the political map, one can easily determine that the state borders on Laos and Cambodia in the east, Myanmar in the west, and Malaysia is its neighbor in the south. But the question of what kind of sea is washed by Thailand, tourists coming to this country for a vacation answer differently. Those who visited Krabi, on the islands of Phuket or Pee-Pee, claim to be Andaman. And those who walked in the nightclubs of Pattaya or soaked up on the snow-white sand of the beaches of Samui stand their ground: the Gulf of Thailand of the South China Sea.



No less confusion with the larger waters washing the tropical kingdom of Thailand. Which sea or ocean is surrounded by its cordons? The Gulf of Thailand through the South China is connected with the Pacific. But the Andaman Sea belongs to the Indian Ocean basin. Each of these reservoirs has its own salinity, its fauna and flora. Density and transparency, and even the color of the waves, are also different. Mainstream trade winds bring storms to the western coasts of the Northern Hemisphere. This means that in the Andaman Sea, there is more excitement than in the Gulf of Thailand.



Which sea in Thailand is preferred by travelers on holiday? If you are interested in sunbathing on wonderful beaches and swimming in warm water, then there is no significant difference. Due to the fact that the country lies in the subequatorial zone, the waters of all the water areas of Thailand warm up to thirty-five degrees in the hot season. And even in the rainy season, when a large amount of celestial moisture enters the seas, the water temperature does not drop below plus twenty-five. This leads to the incredible wealth of the animal and plant world of coral reefs.

By the way, what kind of sea in Thailand do snorkelers and divers prefer? Experts say that both areas are suitable for diving and scuba diving. The Gulf of Thailand has the advantage that the water in it is more transparent than in the Andaman Sea. But it also has the disadvantage that its salinity exceeds the concentration of water off the western coast of Thailand.



Again, purity and transparency are quite different concepts and often depend on the bottom. For example, in Pattaya, which is located on the shore of the Gulf of Thailand, the water is muddy due to the suspension of sand and silt. And in Krabi, the sea has a greenish tint due to the large number of rivers that also bring bottom sediments. If you dream of crystal clear and transparent water - which sea in Thailand to choose? Any! Just go to the islands, and in the dry period (December-April). Off the coast of Phuket or Pi-Pi in the Andaman Sea, the water is azure and clear at 60 meters. The same can be said about the waves washing Samui, Phangan, Tao, Koh Kut, Koh Chang and other smaller islands in the Gulf of Thailand.

The kingdom of Siam until the 80s of the last century was a tabula rasa for European travelers. But now tourism is the main source of state revenue. Infrastructure and service are rapidly developing here, and in recent years they are in no way inferior to the European standard. Therefore, no matter what sea you choose in Thailand, you will not be disappointed. On the contrary, you will want to return to this tropical paradise again and again.
