Stages and stages of development of the team: methodology

All people living on our planet are united among themselves in a variety of numerous stable and not very common. They are called groups. Such communities are different, namely: social, small and large, formal and informal, conditional and real, labor and educational, etc. In addition, groups of low and high level of development are distinguished.





The first of them is characterized by a lack of cohesion, a clear separation of leaders and personal relationships. A group of a high level of development is usually called a collective. This community has those properties that are absent in the formation at a low level of its development.

Team concept

The path that a low-level group goes to the very top step of its development is individual. But in the end, such a community has a significant impact on the individual.

Team Signs

A group of people reaches their high level of development if there is:
- common goal;
- cooperative activity;
- interdependent relationship of responsibility;
- General management, which may be either one of the most authoritative members, or governing bodies.



The team has various types of relationships:
- personal, the basis of which is sympathy, antipathy and affection;
- business, necessary for the joint solution of tasks.

School team

Of particular importance in the formation of personality is the educational group. It is created at school and formed from students on the basis of common aspirations on the path to success, as well as on the basis of normal social relations. In such a team there is a high organization of interpersonal relations and self-government. The formation of such a community activates students in increasing focus, forms their culture of behavior and positive interpersonal relationships.



The school team includes the following elements:
- primary (classes);
- temporary (circles, sports sections);
- formal (school self-government body, student committee);
- informal.

Educational facilities

The formation of the personality of the student in the school team occurs through:
- educational work;
- extracurricular activities;
- labor activity;
- cultural and educational and social work.

In order to form a healthy school team, you must:
- bring up a student asset that helps the teacher and positively affects all his classmates;
- properly organize sports and health, educational, labor and educational activities;
- clearly formulate pedagogical requirements.

Stages of the formation of the team

The great teacher A. S. Makarenko formulated the basic law by which the community of pupils should live. Its basic principle is movement. This is a collective life form. Stopping always means his death.

The main principles of the collective, according to the great teacher, are dependence and publicity, as well as perspective. An important role is played by the parallel actions of all its members.

Makarenko also revealed the stages of development of the team. They consist of four stages, the first of which is becoming. It occurs during the formation of a class, circle, or group into a collective or socio-psychological community, where relations between students are determined by their goals, objectives and the nature of the joint activity. In this case, the main organizer is a teacher who puts forward certain requirements for children.



At the second stage of collective development, the influence of the asset is enhanced. These are students who not only fulfill the requirements of the teacher, but also present them to other classmates. At this stage of development, the collective acts as an integrated system in which the mechanisms of self-regulation and self-organization are already developed and begin to work. Moreover, this community is an instrument for the purposeful education of positive human qualities.

At the third stage of development of the collective according to Makarenko, flowering occurs. The community reaches such a stage of its development when the demands of its members for themselves become higher than for their environment. All this indicates the achievement of a high level of education, as well as the stability of judgments and views of students.

Being in such a collective, the personality has all the prerequisites in the formation of its morality and integrity. The main feature of such a community at this stage of development is the presence of common experience and the same assessment of certain events.

The fourth stage of development of the group is a movement. At this stage, students, relying on the already learned collective experience, make certain demands on themselves. At the same time, the basic need of children is compliance with moral standards. At this stage, the upbringing process smoothly passes into self-education.

All stages of the development of the collective according to Makarenko do not have clear boundaries. Each of the subsequent steps is added to the previous one, but does not replace it.

Describing his theory of the stages of collective development, the great teacher paid great attention to the traditions created by his members. These are sustainable forms of community life, helping to develop a line of behavior, as well as decorating and developing school life.

According to Makarenko, to unite and captivate the team is capable of a goal that can be close, medium and distant. The pillar of the first of these is personal interest. The average goal is determined by complexity and time, and the distant is the most socially significant. A similar system of prospects should permeate the entire team. Only in this case will its development proceed naturally.



Considering the stages of development of the children's educational team, Makarenko also put forward the principle of parallel actions. What does he mean? At one stage or another of the development of the collective, each of its members is under the simultaneous influence of the educator and his comrades. Sometimes the punishment of the guilty can be too harsh. That is why this principle of advice Makarenko use with caution.

According to the theory of a well-known teacher, the following features are characteristic of a fully formed collective:
- constant vigor;
- friendly unity of all members;
- self-esteem;
- motivation for ordered action;
- a feeling of security;
- emotional restraint.

The development of the theory of Makarenko

The characteristics of the development stages of the collective were also considered in the works of Sukhomlinsky. This teacher, working for many years as a teacher and school principal, on the basis of his own experience formulated a set of principles, thanks to which a highly organized group of students is formed. Among them:
- unity of pupils;
- initiative;
- amateur performances;
- the wealth of relations between students and the teacher;
- harmony of interests;
- the leading role of the teacher, etc.

The stages of development of the collective are considered in their works by A. T. Kurakin, L. I. Novikov and others. Moreover, they have a completely different approach to this issue. These authors believe that not only requirements can rally children at the stage of development of the student body. Other means help in this.

Recently, there has been a clear tendency to understand a group as a group whose members have a high level of development. Moreover, such a community should be distinguished by integrative activity, cohesion and a single focus. The most significant quality of the group, according to modern authors, is the level of socio-psychological maturity. It is this feature that is the main prerequisite for creating a team group. What are the main stages of its formation?

At the first stage of development of the group, a conglomerate occurs. This community consists of children previously unfamiliar with each other, who were gathered at the same time in one space. The relationship of the guys at this stage is usually situational and superficial. If you assign a name to such a group, it will be nominalized. In the event that the members of such a team do not accept the conditions and goals set for them, then the transition from the conglomerate will not happen. Such cases are not uncommon in school practice.



If the initial unification nevertheless happened, then the collective accepted the status of primary. In this case, the group goes into an association, where the goal for each of its members is designed by the task. At this level, the first bricks in the formation of the team are laid. With joint activities, the group moves to a higher level of organization, changing interpersonal relationships.

In the presence of favorable conditions there is a change in the stage of development of the children's team. At the next stage, a cooperation group is formed. Such a community is distinguished by a successful and actually functioning structure of the organization. Also in this case there is a high level of group cooperation and preparedness. All relations between the members of the cooperation group are business in nature and are aimed at achieving the goal.

Lutoshkin's concept

According to this author, there are the following stages of development of the student body:
1. The first stage is the nominee group. This community exists formally and has joint activities and time. If this is observed at school, then such a team is called an unfriendly class.
2. The second stage is an association group. It arises when all its members have the same goals.
3. At the third stage of development of the team according to Lutoshkin, a cooperation group arises. It is characterized by unity of goals, high cohesion and a single approach to solving the tasks.
4. The fourth stage is the creation of an autonomy group. This community is distinguished by internal unity, developed self-control and high preparedness for solving the problem.
5. The fifth stage is characterized by the emergence of a collective group. This is the highest stage of development of the community, all members of which are connected by a single goal, as well as by activities on the path to achieving it. In addition, in such a group one can observe moral and psychological unity, high preparedness, and a perfect organizational structure.
