Excursions to Chatyr-Dag caves in Crimea

In the Crimean mountains, which in the past were called Tauride, about eighty caves were discovered. And what panoramas open to those who climb the upper plateau: forests and valleys covered with lavender, the sea and the immense expanse of the sky, where it seems that you can reach the birds by hand!

Mountains and caves are especially popular with tourists. Chatyr-dag is a mountain range in the south of the peninsula. The name translates as "Tabernacle Mountain." In the fifth century BC, hunters and herders settled in the grottoes. Now it is a popular tourist attraction. In particular, such caves as Marble, which was included in the 5 most beautiful caves of the Earth, Emine-Bair-Khosar (Mammoth, or a funnel at the hill of Emine), Emine-Bair-Koba (red-brown funnel of Emine), Thousand-headed. Two first caves are fully adapted to the needs of tourists. Chatyr-dag with magnificent stone icicles flowing down from the ceiling, and growing cones and pillars from the bottom will delight even a sophisticated tourist.

Magic caves

A complex system of halls and galleries was discovered by cavers in 1987. A year later, a tourist center was created, which is still active today - Onyx Tour. This center is made available for visiting Chatyr-Dag. The marble cave is equipped with paths with handrails, lighting, musical accompaniment. Experienced and beginner guides work in it. In the Marble Cave there are several halls that are cool, so the tourist needs to stock up on warm clothes and shoes.

The route "Chatyr-Dag Caves" is very interesting. The tour is thought out so that it is suitable for any age. Chatyr-dag will now begin to tell its tales, having led tourists along a 10-meter passage. The mystery begins from the entrance: silence, cold and dusk reign here.

Crimea, Chatyr-Dag caves

Not only the Marble Cave is famous for the mountains of Chatyr-Dag. Emine-Bair-Khosar Cave has retained in many ways its natural appearance. It is divided into three floors. Tourists can visit only the first, but this is enough. It contains the skeletons of prehistoric animals that fell into it.

The photo shows the skeleton of a mammoth, which is located in a cave from the Ice Age. Inside there is a lake filled with pure water, as well as the Hall of Idols. These are stalagmites incredibly grown in height (10 m). One of the stalactites looks like a Monomakh hat. There is also a hall of the Dead Sea - a lake with waves without water.

The thousand-headed, or Binbash-Koba cave, thanks to the barbarians, is not very well preserved. The entrance to the cave is narrow and cramped. Then it turns into a small hall, bypassing which you can get big.

A sad local legend is associated with it. According to legend, when the enemies came, all the inhabitants here hid. Girls went out later for water. She spilled, and beautiful flowers grew along the path. Enemies came for civilians and destroyed them all. More true is another story. For centuries, ritual burials have been held here. Hence, many skeletons and skulls. They cannot be found now. As far back as the century before last, their sale for souvenirs began.
