Crochet poncho methods: patterns and description

Comparing knitting techniques with crochet techniques, many craftswomen note the obvious advantage of the latter. Crochet ownership simplifies many fairly complex tasks: knitting hats, mittens, children's clothing, toys, and even home decoration.

Poncho - clothes for all time

According to historical data, the so-called clothing of the Indian tribes of South America. This wardrobe item quickly became very popular and fell in love with many people around the world. Today, the fashion for traditional types of poncho has passed a little, but its latest modifications have won general recognition.



In order to crochet a poncho, patterns and descriptions are desirable, but not necessary. This product allows you to use completely different yarn and knitting techniques. The choice of material and pattern depends on the purpose of the clothing: decorative or warm.

Also, the size of the product will affect the amount of yarn. Crocheting a baby ponch for a girl will require significantly less yarn than an adult long product. When knitting warm patterns, it should be taken into account that yarn with a thick thread has a very small footage. Therefore, it is worth focusing not on the weight of the purchased material, but on the total length of the threads in all skeins.

The most common types of poncho

All existing types can conditionally be divided into two types:

  1. Solid warm products.
  2. Openwork decorative capes.

The first should be knitted from yarn containing at least 30% natural fibers of wool, angora or mohair (optimally 50%). Of course, you can use acrylic, but it will not warm, even if the canvas comes out dense and without holes. Rather, artificial fibers will cause the poncho to be either too hot or too cold.

Openwork poncho for girls, crocheted, will hold its shape and be worn for a long time, if you use cotton, linen, polyamide or viscose.

Round knitting

The classic version of knitting poncho - round. They start knitting from the neck, the dialed chain of air loops is closed in a ring and two loops are marked with colored thread at an equal distance from each other. Next, knit circular rows, adding loops at these two points.



The general principle of adding is three loops from one loop of the base. This forms a right angle (90 degrees), more precisely, two right angles. Successive expansion will allow you to get a canvas of a specific shape. It meets the characteristics of human anatomy as best as possible and avoids the formation of too wide a canvas.

Short Poncho: Drape and Cape

The modern modification of the classic poncho is a short drape. It can be knitted from warm wool yarn, have fur, fringe and pompons as decor. Pelerines are also knitted with the use of openwork patterns and thin threads. They are decorated with beads, rhinestones or knitted flowers. Quite often, knitted drape is used for wedding attire or on summer days to protect against solar ultraviolet radiation.



Crocheting a short poncho (the patterns and description are very important here) is carried out round-the-round, by return rows or by compiling individual fragments. The photo above shows the options for openwork decorative poncho. Like other types of poncho, the drape can be circular or in the form of an open canvas with a fastener or ties.

Crocheting a poncho for beginners should not be considered an overwhelming task. The correct selection of models and yarn, as well as careful calculations of loops and rows will help create an excellent functional wardrobe item.
