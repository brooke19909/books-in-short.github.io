Beach holidays and weather

Like any resort, Lecce has a high season, from the first decade of June to the end of October. The sea warms up best in August. It was at this time that different-sized vessels were going to the coast of the Lecce region - from light yachts to solid liners. In high season, beach vacations can be combined with sightseeing tours. For its beauty, incredibly azure color, crystal clear water and the whiteness of local beaches, the Lecce region is rightly called the Italian Maldives. It is difficult to disagree with this, although many bathing areas are at some distance from the city itself.

How to get to Lecce (Italy)

To get to this resort, tourists will have to take a short trip. Lecce does not have its own airport. The easiest option would be to get to Salento or Bari. In high season, direct charter flights from Moscow fly here. This is a very convenient way for non-stop being on the heel of Italy. Those who have an international driving license can immediately rent a car at the airport in the same Bari and drive to Lecce in two hours. You can go to the resort and by train.
Another way is to get Brindisi with a transfer in the capital of Italy. From here, shuttles run to Lecce directly from the airport. The first departure at nine in the morning, the last at twenty minutes to twelve. Travel time is a maximum of forty-five minutes. Ticket price - eight and a half euros.

This city has many options for living. The opportunity to live in a former synagogue will be given to tourists who have chosen Palazzo Persone. This hotel has just six apartments. sights

The city of creativity, aesthetes and hedonists, many call Lecce. The attractions of this small village are numerous, but at the same time they are quite compact. In order to see them, tourists do not need to carefully think through routes. Only a few hours are enough to go around the entire Old City, if, of course, the weather allows. Lecce, Italy attracts travelers with its history, legends about the temples and houses of the aristocracy, which can be seen literally at every step. Its main attraction is the Basilica Di Santa Croce - a real triumph of baroque. The facade of this temple perfectly fulfills the function originally laid in it - it frightens and pacifies. A pile of scrolls and birds, half-naked female figures and angels will not hurt to notice the sculptural images of Turkish warriors instead of Atlanteans, traditional for Baroque. This is how architects glorified the victory of the Holy League over the Ottoman army. The center of the city is Piazza del Duomo. On this square are the bishop's palace, as well as a library and a seminary. All of them belong to the work of Giuseppe Chino. In the seminary today is a museum where the gifts of parishioners are exhibited.



The excursion route is best to start directly from the train station. the boulevard from which leads directly to the cathedral, located on the same square of the Duomo. It is considered one of the most outstanding cathedrals in all of Italy. Built in the twelfth century and completely reconstructed in the middle of the seventeenth century, the temple thanks to its seventy-meter bell tower is visible from afar.

Not far from the square is the church of St. Irina. In the adjacent monastery, interesting events are held annually. The most beautiful and original of them is the Christmas exhibition ... of nativity scenes. Tourists should definitely see another Lecce square, named after St. Oronia. Here is the Roman amphitheater of the era of Emperor Augustus. Another attraction is the castle, erected on the orders of Charles V. It was supposed to protect the city from Turkish raids by the Turks.
