A bit of history

The village of Altai Altai Territory was founded back in 1808. At first, this settlement was the center of the Altai volost, later it became considered a large merchant village. Here, trade and small industry were well developed.



If we talk about people who lived on this earth in those distant times, then, according to the records (which, however, it is said only about male souls), in 1857 511 men lived here, in 1882 - 822. At the end of 19 century, people began to move to the village of Altai, Altai Territory, so the settlement grew rapidly.



Already in 1893, there were 519 yards and 3082 inhabitants on this land. A volost government building was built, two churches (one of which was Orthodox, and the other a single-faith), a school and a school. Also, oil mills and tanneries, a wine warehouse, mills and several trading shops worked here.



According to a census conducted in 1926, the village of Altai in the Altai Territory was home to 7595 residents. After almost half a century, the settlement received the status by which it was now considered an urban type settlement.

A bit about phytotourists

Such an interesting and unusual direction appeared here thanks to the Biolit association, which opened in the vicinity of the village. It is a large arboretum, plantations of which are located in the Altai foothills. More than 70 types of medicinal and ornamental plants are grown here.





Phytotourists are just the guests of the arboretum. People are interested not only in these plants, but also in the products that are made from them. And here they get the opportunity to acquire these crops, get comprehensive information on each plant.

In addition, the village has a museum of local lore, which contains a whole collection of paintings and interesting exhibits.
