Antenna Operation

The satellite antenna is a ‚Äúplate‚Äù that collects a signal from space in its center and amplifies it to a certain stability. To receive high-quality broadcasting from remote satellites, you need to install a large diameter antenna. A converter is installed in the center of each ‚Äúplate‚Äù, which picks up the signals, further converting them into sounds and an image, transmitting them to the receiver. The latter is installed directly near the TV and plays the role of a receiver. It finally decodes the signal and transmits the resulting image directly to the screen. The advantages of satellite broadcasting

Among the advantages should be highlighted:

  • superior quality of transmitted image and sound;
  • unlimited choice of channels (almost all television stations available today broadcast on satellite frequencies);
  • a large number of free channels;
  • the ability to record broadcasts;
  • wide availability (regardless of region of residence);
  • low cost of equipment;
  • the ability to view the program guide directly in the system options.

If there were fewer items on this list, perhaps television would not have gained such popularity today.

disadvantages

The main disadvantage is that, no matter what channels are broadcast on which satellite, in bad weather the signal will disappear. Weather dependence is especially evident when the sky is very cloudy, it rains or snows. The signal quality also depends on the direction of the antenna; its converter must be facing south, since all satellites are located in the equator area.

If there is an obstacle in the space between the antenna and the satellite, the signal will disappear. A vivid example: braiding the converter with grapes, flowers or the appearance of a new tree.

Also, the receiver must be serviced periodically, since the channels change encodings from time to time and disappear from the screens.
