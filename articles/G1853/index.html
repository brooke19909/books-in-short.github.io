Cities of Indonesia: capital, large cities, population, resorts overview, photos

What do we know about Indonesia? For the average Russian, this state is associated with an expensive resort country. Names such as Bali, Lampung, Sulawesi, Riau, caress the ears and inspire associations about the paradise islands, bungalows on stilts over the turquoise lagoon and the like.

We also know about Indonesia and other less pleasant information. This island state lies in a zone of increased seismic activity. And he is often trembled by typhoons and tropical cyclones.

There are cities in Indonesia with more than a million inhabitants. And this is not only the capital. There are fourteen million-plus cities in Indonesia, according to the latest census of 2014. We will talk about some of them today.

Special status

According to the current legislation of Indonesia, the city (cat) in the country is a special administrative unit. They are equated to the territory of the third level.

That is, in fact, they are administratively equal to the district (kabupaten). The city municipality is headed by a mayor, who in Indonesian is called a valikota.

This autonomous administrative-territorial unit has a legislative body. It is called Dewan Pervakilan Rakyat Daer, which can be freely translated as a Regional Council of Representatives of the People.

This municipality body is elected. It may include city residents. The status of a "cat" in Indonesia has ninety-eight settlements (as of 2013).

In medieval Europe it was said that "the air of the city makes a man free." After all, the townspeople were not serfs. The man who lived in the "villa" for more than a year got rid of feudal dependence.

Of course, in Indonesia there is no serfdom. But the citizens in this country still differ in status from rural residents.

Jakarta

Let's start our review with the capital of Indonesia. The city of Jakarta differs from the rest of the cat in its administrative structure.

He has the status of the second, not third level. That is, Jakarta is equated to the province and is governed by the governor. But it is called the Special Capital District.

In fact, Jakarta consists of five cities, which are simply called: Central, Western, Eastern, Southern and Northern. These administrative units have somewhat reduced rights compared to other cats. They do not have legislative bodies. Also, mayors are not elected by residents. They are appointed by the Governor of Jakarta.

The capital is a kabupaten administrative area - a special region, which includes not only five cities, but also several islands off the coast that have no buildings. It should be said that the number of people in Jakarta in 2014 exceeded 10 million.

The largest administrative cat is Oriental. 2 million 842 thousand people live there. And the smallest number of inhabitants is Central Jakarta (953,000).

Capital for tourists

Let us give some more attention to the main city of Indonesia. Many foreign tourists flying to the paradise islands land at Jakarta International Airport. But most do not stay here. How many of them immediately resort to the help of local airlines and go to resorts! But they lose a lot.

This 10 millionth megalopolis on the island of Java can win the heart of any tourist. In Central Jakarta, in addition to diplomatic missions, there is the largest mosque in Southeast Asia - Istiklal.

The southern part of the capital is the most luxurious. There are skyscrapers, concentrated fashion stores. In Eastern tourists there is nothing to do. There is only one attraction - the Mini Indonesia Park.

The northern district adjoins the sea. Although the beaches here are of dubious cleanliness, there is an amusement park called Taman Impian Jaya Ankol.

And finally, the main natural attraction of the capital is the Thousand Islands. This national park fully lives up to its name.

It will also be interesting for tourists to visit the local Chinatown and Western Jakarta, where the spirit of Dutch colonialism is still preserved.
