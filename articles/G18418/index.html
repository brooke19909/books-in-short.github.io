A bit about the city

The city of Pushkin is known for its beauty, an abundance of parks, clean air and a lot of attractions. It was here, in Tsarskoye Selo, that the royal residence of Elizabeth Petrovna was located from the beginning of the eighteenth century. Later, Catherine the Second in the outbuilding of her own palace founded the Imperial Tsarskoye Selo Lyceum, in which the young poet Alexander Sergeyevich Pushkin lived and studied for six years. In honor of which in February 1937 the city was renamed.





The city, being the royal residence, always had the status of one of the most comfortable corners of Russia. Already at the end of the nineteenth century, he was the first in Europe to be fully electrified, then there was a telephone connection earlier than everywhere. The infrastructure, roads and urban development plan were carefully thought out.





During the Great Patriotic War, the city was occupied by the Germans and was badly damaged. Since the mid-twentieth century, restoration work has been carried out in palaces, parks and estates, and historical buildings are being restored. Work has already been done to replace sewage treatment facilities, residential buildings have been restored, new residential areas have been built, a number of industrial enterprises and a university have been opened.

Today it is actively developing and can rightfully be considered one of the best places to live in the city.
