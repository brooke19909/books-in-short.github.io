The sights of its capital - the city of the same name - amaze even the most experienced travelers. No wonder Sharjah in 1998 recognized the UNESCO organization as the cultural center of the entire Arab world. The city received this status deservedly as a reward for loyalty to culture and art, and respect for its architectural heritage. And today restoration work is underway to restore the ancient buildings, new, unprecedented beauty objects are being erected.



The real museum treasure is Sharjah. The sights of the Middle East are striking in wealth. There are a lot of museums in the city: Arts, National Heritage, Archaeological, Scientific, Historical, etc. If it is not possible to visit them all, then you should opt for the Maritime Museum and the Museum of Fine Arts. The first one was opened relatively recently - in 2009, but became one of the main objects for which Sharjah is visited. The sights of the museum introduce visitors to the marine life of the Arabs. The exposition contains items used for fishing, collecting seafood, trade, there are wooden sailboats.



The Museum of Fine Arts was opened in 1997, it gained popularity not only among local residents, but also guests of the emirate. Here a huge number of the best works of fine art of the Arab world is collected, there are works of foreign artists, exhibitions are often held. King Faisal Mosque is one of the grandest structures that Sharjah is so proud of. Sights of other cities fade in comparison with this architectural miracle. The mosque is considered one of the largest in the Middle East, it holds up to 3 thousand believers.
