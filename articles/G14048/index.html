The House of the Noble Assembly - the focus of high society

The foundation, the state-forming foundation of pre-revolutionary Russia was the nobility - the highest, privileged estate. The self-governing body of this estate, which appeared in 1766, acquired legislative force in 1785 from the moment the "Charter of the Nobility" entered into force.

Beautiful and durable

The noble assembly was also called "noble", by the very name making it clear that this is a self-governing body of people with blue blood.

Naturally, to hold meetings that took place once in three years, an appropriate atmosphere was needed. The decoration of society required a decent frame. The houses of the Noble Assembly in the capitals and main cities of the provinces were erected in the centers; The best craftsmen from all countries were invited to build them.

Demanded after the revolution



Buildings were built for centuries, so many of them have survived to this day and are the main attractions of the central provincial cities. The houses of the Noble Assembly in such cities as Smolensk, Vologda, Vladimir, Ufa, Kostroma, Tver and others are decorations of these settlements and historical and architectural monuments of these regions. Cultural objects are still located in them - where is the philharmonic society (St. Petersburg, Smolensk), where is the Academy of Arts (Ufa). For many years in Moscow, in the building of the Noble Assembly, the House of Unions has been located (it was known under this name).

Center for cultural and social life

Needless to say, what role did the House of the Noble Assembly play in the cultural and social life of both capitals. The most famous celebrities performed on the stage of these houses, the most refined and brilliant balls were also given here. The Moscow building was built by the most famous and sought-after domestic architect Matvey Fedorovich Kazakov, who rebuilt the entire central part of Moscow. New buildings, including the House of the Noble Assembly (in Moscow it was officially called the House of the Noble Assembly), were erected in the style of early classicism, known as "Palladianism" (in honor of the architect from Italy Andrea Palladio).

House of the Noble Assembly of the Northern Capital

Maybe because these houses were built for some important and joyful events, they did not suffer the sad fate of destruction and oblivion. This also applies to the famous building in St. Petersburg, which now houses the world famous Concert Hall of the D. D. Shostakovich Conservatory - one of the cultural centers of Russia.

When did the Noble Assembly House appear in the northern capital and by whom was it built? The building, part of the ensemble of the Square of Arts, was erected according to the design of K. I. Rossi. The building was built by architect Paul Jacot in 1834-1839. One of the main advantages of this building is the hall with amazing acoustics. It is not surprising that there is a philharmonic society in it. Three floors were originally counted by the St. Petersburg House of Noble Assembly.

The year 1901 went down in the history of the building with a superstructure of the four attic floors located above the crowning cornice. This reconstruction, which affected both the main staircase and the exterior of the building, was carried out by the architect A.P. Maksimov according to the design of V.A.Shreter. In 1948-49 (after the war), the architect I. G. Kantsyug, who led the restoration work, restored the building according to the drawings of K. I. Rossi.

Years of Soviet Power

When the Noble Assembly House in St. Petersburg was created, intended for social gatherings, no one thought that the building would become famous around the world. The St. Petersburg Academic Philharmonic Society, which opened on June 12, 1921, will thunder not only with the shameful process in the case of Metropolitan Veniamin and 86 Orthodox priests, but also with the great performance of the D. D. Shostakovich Seventh Symphony, played here on August 9, 1942.

This performance became a symbol of the invincibility of the besieged Leningrad. For many years, the orchestra has been led by Yu. Kh. Temirkanov. VV Putin called the St. Petersburg Philharmonic "a prayer place."
