Life of George, Duke of Kent

The ruling dynasty of Great Britain has existed for more than a century. During this time, many members of the great house became famous all over the world not only due to their family name, but also their contribution to culture, charity and the development of military affairs.

As a teenager, the young man graduated from college for Royal Navy officers.

Learning and becoming a person

George of Kent was the fourth son of the ruling king, so it was the brothers who influenced the young man's decision to connect his life with the naval affair. Edward and Albert, who will become rulers of the UK in the future, but at different times, inspired the younger brother to try his hand at this craft. Upon graduation, Prince George of Kent remained a member of the Royal Navy.

A representative of the dynasty also held public office in the ministry. The young man worked on internal affairs within the state, and also resolved the difficulties encountered abroad. It is Prince George who is considered the first heir to the great house, which officially worked in government agencies.

At the age of 37, George, Duke of Kent, was chosen as the head of the great Masonic lodge, which is located in England and is the oldest and most numerous organization of this nature.

Marriage and family

On November 29, 1934, George, Duke of Kent, was married by the sacred bonds of marriage with the heiress of the House of Glucksburg, with the Princess of Greece and the Danish Marina. The newly made spouse also received the title of husband after marriage. Three children were born in this union:

  1. Prince Edward The son followed in the footsteps of his father: he earned a military rank, became the head of the Masonic Order and the Duke of Kent after the death of his father.
  2. Princess Alexandra, Honorable Lady Ogilvy. The only daughter of George and Marina. Engaged in royal duties. In the 60s, she represented the Windsor Dynasty when traveling to Australia, Canada and several other countries. The title of Honorable Lady Ogilvy was obtained through marriage.
  3. Prince Michael Just like his father and older brother, the young man served in the army. At one time he worked in intelligence, but later voluntarily resigned. Unlike his sister, the prince rarely represented royal interests at diplomatic meetings. Currently, Michael of Kent is a businessman and head of many of his own companies around the world.

Unusual facts

The duke's circle noticed George's hobbies and inclinations. Known fact about the bisexuality of men. For a long time he was friends with Sir Noel Coward, a playwright and actor. Relations between men were purely friendly.

In the biography of the duke, there is also another fact - drug use. Georg was not a drug addict, but was convicted several times in an inadequate condition.

The year 1939 was difficult and difficult, as the world was immersed in war. The duke did not sit back in safety, but opposed the aggressor countries of the Second World War as rear admiral, that is, on the front line of the fleet. A year later, in 1940, George joined the Royal Air Force and continued defensive activities.

The Prince's History in World Culture

The personal life and biography of George, Duke of Kent, attracted many contemporary filmmakers and writers. Based on verified facts, the documentary "The Lost Queen's Uncle" was shot, since the man was the uncle of the ruling Queen of Britain Elizabeth II. In addition to the film, the world saw the printed work of Jeffrey Corrick's "African Nights", a story about the personal life of the Duke.

Death of a prince

Georg Edward Alexander Edmund tragically died on August 25, 1942 in a plane crash. A single version of the death of the Duke has not been put forward. Someone believes that the crash was not an accident, and someone blames the technical malfunction of the aircraft, because of which the pilot was unable to gain the necessary height. Buried in a suburban family residence.

Official emblem

As a representative of the great house, the duke has his own coat of arms. The emblem of England is depicted on two parts of the canvas, Scotland on the third, and Ireland on the fourth. On different sides are a crowned lion as a symbol of England, and a unicorn - a sign of Scotland. At the very top is a small leopard, and under it is a crown, so that it looks like an animal is walking along the personification of the power of the rulers. Below is a green lawn.

The life of the Duke George was filled with both difficulties and wonderful bright moments, because the man made an invaluable contribution to the development of the state.
