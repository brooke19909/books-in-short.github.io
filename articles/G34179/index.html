Ancient Nimes (France): a touch of ancient history

One of the oldest cities in the world is famous for its well-preserved ancient Roman structures. Modern Nimes (France) is a popular tourist center that welcomes guests all year round.

Climate and weather

The Mediterranean climate of the city of Nimes with hot summers and mild winters, when the temperature rarely drops below 6 degrees, is comfortable for people. However, it is worth considering that strong winds blow here, the speed of which exceeds 100 kilometers per hour. Most often they dominate in autumn and winter, and most foreigners come here from April to September.

Some facts about the ancient city

The cozy city of Nimes, located in the picturesque valley of the Rhone River, is the former capital of the Gallic tribe, which was captured by Roman soldiers in 121 BC. The valiant emperor Augustus founded a city on this site that began to develop economically. Unfortunately, after only a few centuries, the largest population center of the country was attacked by the Visigoths and Arabs who plundered it.

The main industries of the city, located in the south of the state, on the border of Provence and Languedoc, are wine and textile. So, Nimes (France) became the place where denim was first released - denim.

Jardins de la fontaine

The delightful garden of the Fountains, which is a favorite place for walking, originally supplied the city of Him with drinking water. Later, the luxurious corner turned into a huge complex with a theater, baths, a temple and an imperial palace. In the middle of the XVIII century, the authorities decided to build a park on this site, and the ancient Roman statues, elegant fountains and double staircases very successfully fit into the classical style of a green oasis.

Pont du gard

The colorful Nimes (France), whose sights reflect its rich past, is incredibly proud of the oldest aqueduct more than 275 meters long, built in the 1st century AD. The two-story high construction of the Pont du Gard was part of a multi-kilometer water supply, from which life-giving moisture entered the city. Later, the excellently preserved aqueduct was used as a bridge for crossing horse-drawn carts .



Bullfighting and Theater Activities

Residents of the administrative center of the Gard department have long been passionate lovers of bullfighting, and it is no coincidence that five-day festivals are held in the arenas of the city, including the amphitheater, at which bullfighters from around the world show their skills.

The French pearl also adores theater performances, dedicated to various periods of history. In the evening, when the heat subsides, tourists continue to have fun in nightclubs and theme bars, tasting the best wines that ancient Nimes (France) is famous for all over the country.

The modern city, the architectural monuments of which it is very difficult to describe in one article, is a paradise for history buffs. In Nimes, which reverently keeps the memory of the past, time has stopped, and every tourist unwittingly becomes a participant in important events, getting acquainted with ancient sights.
