Shipwreck in the Tsemess Bay

Tsemess Bay (Novorossiysk) is located in the northern part of the Black Sea coast. It became part of Russia in 1829 as a result of another war with the Turks. In the mid-80s of the last century, a clash took place here, which claimed more than four hundred human lives.





Geographical position

Tsemess Bay was named after the river, which originates on the slope of Mount Gudzeva. There is another one-root toponym - Tsemesskaya grove. Near the western part of the bay is the Abrau Peninsula. On the right is the Markotkh Range. The coastline of Tsemess Bay is 15 km long. Width - 9 km. From the north-west of the bay is the island of Sujuk, and from the south-east of Doob. The average depth of the Tsemess Bay is 24 meters. The maximum is 29 meters.

Tourism

Vacationers who prefer to get to the Black Sea coast in their own car, at least once passed the Tsemess bay. It is located very close to Gelendzhik and Kabardinka, located a few kilometers from the resort town. The beaches of the Tsemess Bay will not please everyone. There is almost no infrastructure, not crowded. However, the places are picturesque, which is confirmed by the photos of the Tsemess Bay, which can be seen in this article.





Ship flooding

In Novorossiysk and its environs, many important historical events took place. One of them is the destruction of the fleet (1918). Then, an agreement was concluded between the Soviet government and Germany, according to which the ships of the Black Sea Fleet had to be transferred to the enemy. Captain 1st Rank Tikhmenev received an order to send ships to Sevastopol, where they will pass to German troops. At the same time, a secret order was received to flood them.

Tikhmenev thought for a long time. Ultimately, he decided to bring the ships to Sevastopol. Many officers did not agree with him. On June 18, almost all ships were destroyed with torpedoes. Two years later, the rise of sunken ships began in the Tsemess Bay. Some of them even managed to be restored, for example, Kaliakria.
