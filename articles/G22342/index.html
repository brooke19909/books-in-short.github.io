How to close the loop with a needle: closing the gum, closing the loops when connecting the paintings

Knitting the main fabric is far from the most difficult process. At the end of the work, each knitter raises the question of how to close the loops correctly and beautifully. It is necessary to do this so that the work has a beautiful and neat edge that can no longer be processed, or vice versa - it will be convenient to continue working with it.

The easiest way to close the hinges is to use knitting needles or a hook. These methods are good, but not all needlewomen are happy with the results. Sometimes the loops can stretch unevenly, the hook is difficult to put into work, and the needles slip, which threatens the loss of loops.

Closing loops with a needle

To make the edge of the product neat and beautiful, you can close the loop with a needle. There are many options for using needle closure :

  • the simplest and most common - pulling loops through one another to complete the edge of the canvas;
  • closing loops in the middle of work;
  • closing gum loops, openwork edges, embossed areas.

To understand how to close the loop with a needle efficiently, you must first familiarize yourself with the theory.

The connection of two paintings

It is not difficult to understand how to close the loops with a needle on a simple canvas and connect the two sections. It is necessary to perform a number of operations:

  • the thread passes through the first loop of the upper fabric;
  • the needle is removed through the first loop of the lower fabric and the loop wraps itself with the same thread;
  • now again the needle picks up the first loop on the upper canvas, is threaded down, clinging to the lower loop;
  • the operation is repeated until the end of the row;
  • at the end, the thread is pulled to form an even seam.




If you close the loops with a needle in this way, the edges of the canvases will join tightly, giving the impression that the product is connected with one large canvas. The seam is masked and does not stand out from the general row. It is important to ensure that the tension of the thread that sewed parts of the product corresponds to the tension of the thread during knitting.

Simple row closure

To close a simple row with a needle, the needle repeats movements similar to working with knitting needles:

  • cut the working thread, leaving the tail about 3-4 times longer than the connected row;
  • the end of the thread is threaded into the tapestry needle. This needle with a blunt short end and a wide eye - it does not tear the thread and does not separate the fibers when stitching;
  • the needle is inserted so that the first two loops on the left, working needle are caught;
  • the needle is withdrawn inward, the tip to itself;
  • now the needle is inserted with a point into the first loop on the left needle, as if the work is done with a needle;
  • the thread on the needle is slightly tightened.

The manipulation is repeated until the entire row is closed.





This closure of the loops allows you to get a smooth finished edge, which does not stretch during operation of the product.

Closure of gum loops

The most commonly used element to complete the knitting of a product is elastic. It can be different - classic English, 2 x 2, dense, openwork. The question of how to close the gum loops with a needle must be carefully studied by each knitter. Using this method has several advantages:

  • the closed row is elastic;
  • hinges do not constrain the canvas;
  • connected loops look at each other according to the figure, without turning in the opposite direction.

The type of product in the future depends on how to close the loops with a needle when knitting, accordingly, it is necessary to practice before working with the finished thing.

The needle tip unfolds and sticks into the middle of the fourth loop from the front to the wrong side.

Now the fourth loop is considered the first. The sequence is repeated until the loop ends.
