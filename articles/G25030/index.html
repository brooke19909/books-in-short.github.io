AAA batteries: which are better, technical specifications, classification, instructions for use

Batteries gradually replace conventional disposable batteries when used in a wide variety of household devices. Once you buy a set of rechargeable power supplies and a charger, you will save significant money (compared with the regular replacement of standard batteries). Classification and dimensions

The battery classification defines a clear correspondence between the size and shape of a product and its alphanumeric or alphanumeric designation (or the so-called form factor). The "little fingers" batteries called 44 "long" with a diameter of 10 mm according to the American standard are called in common people by three Latin letters - AAA (in the international standardization system - HR03). Accordingly, AA (HR6) batteries are used instead of disposable ‚Äúfinger‚Äù batteries with a length of 50 mm and a diameter of 14 mm.

Varieties

Depending on the chemical composition of the technological elements included in the technical device of the AAA battery, such products are:

  • Lithium Polymer (Li-Pol). A design feature of such products is a micro-USB connector installed on the case, through which they are charged using the cord supplied. Such batteries have not yet been widely used because of their very high cost. For example, a set of two batteries (400 mAh each) Rombica Neo X3 costs about 1000 rubles. The pluses include increased voltage - 1.5 V.
  • Nickel Cadmium (Ni-Cd). These rechargeable AAA batteries have been popular for quite some time. However, taking into account the highly toxic characteristics of cadmium and its derivatives, as well as the increased modern requirements for human environmental safety and the environment, almost all the leading manufacturers of this battery segment have abandoned their production.
  • Nickel Metal Hydride (Ni-MH). They are the most popular today and are perhaps the best AAA batteries. The choice of these products is very wide both in terms of manufacturers represented on the Russian market and in terms of price and technical indicators. Therefore, it is precisely about them that will be discussed in this article.

For information! AA and AAA lithium-ion (Li-Ion) batteries are no longer available at all.

AAA Battery Specifications

AAA batteries are characterized by:

  • Operating voltage: 1.2 V.
  • Capacity: 550 to 1100 mAh.
  • The form factor is AAA (HR03).
  • Overall dimensions: length - 44 mm, diameter - 10 mm.
  • Weight: 12-15 grams.
  • The number of full charge / discharge cycles guaranteed by the manufacturer: from 500 to 3000.
  • Uninterrupted service life: from 3 to 10 years.
  • Self-discharge level.

Application area

Rechargeable batteries are used in a wide variety of devices:

  • digital cameras and flash units;
  • video cameras;
  • voice recorders;
  • portable radios and multimedia music centers;
  • wireless game console controllers;
  • hair trimmers;
  • cordless phones;
  • wireless mice, keyboards and headphones;
  • portable CD and MP3 players;
  • children's electromechanical toys (including radio-controlled);
  • miniature flashlights (on incandescent lamps or LEDs);
  • electric shavers, epilators and toothbrushes.

The choice of AAA battery capacity directly depends on the energy consumption of a particular device.

Advantages and disadvantages

The undoubted advantages of nickel-metal hydride batteries (compared to disposable batteries and rechargeable products based on Li-Pol or Ni-Cd) are:

  • Saving money (the kit purchased once will last for many years).
  • The energy intensity of modern AAA batteries often exceeds that of standard batteries.
  • Modern rechargeable Ni-MH products (unlike batteries) almost do not reduce the amount of energy delivered even at high discharge currents.
  • These products (compared to Ni-Cd) have almost no effect of the so-called memory, so you can put them on charge without waiting for a full discharge. In this case, there is no deterioration in performance.
  • Full environmental safety of materials used in the manufacture. This allows you to operate them without causing any harm to human health, and also greatly simplifies the process of subsequent disposal.
  • Large selection of products.

The main disadvantage of such products is that manufacturers do not recommend using them at sufficiently low temperatures. Only some models (usually with the Pro index) are designed to operate at minus 20 degrees.

What to look for when choosing

When choosing batteries, you should pay attention to several basic aspects:

  • First of all, it's capacity. The larger it is, the more naturally your device will last longer until the next recharge. However, the process of restoring the performance of high-capacity products will take longer.
  • If you use the device (with batteries installed in it) often enough, then you can safely purchase standard batteries. But if you turn on the device from case to case, then buy products with a low self-discharge current. Then, after taking out the flash from the shelf in half a year, you can be completely sure of its performance. Conventional batteries in this period will be discharged to zero.
  • The number of reload cycles excites mostly very zealous owners. Even with a minimum value of this indicator (500) and very intensive operation (recharging every 2 days), the most budget GP batteries will last at least a year and a half.

Important! You should not save too much on the charger, as this leads to a decrease in battery life. It is best to purchase products with the function of automatic disconnection from trusted manufacturers.

The final conclusion, which AAA batteries are best for your CD player or a radio-controlled car of your beloved child, will help you to carefully read the instruction manual (which often indicates the recommended capacity) and consult an experienced sales assistant in a specialized store.
