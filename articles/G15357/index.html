History of Citibank

Citi Bank began its development many decades ago. As early as 1916, during the October Revolution, the first branch was opened. The main goal of the institution at that time was to accept assistance and provide resources to the state in wartime. The first branch was founded in Petrograd, then another branch in Moscow. In 1919, another branch was opened in Vladivostok. Due to difficult times, the crisis of those times, in 1920 all three branches were closed.

Later, Citibank again took off and resumed work in Moscow. It began in 1973. Due to difficult times, in 1979 the department was again closed.

Citibank did not want to leave the financial market of the Russian Federation, so in 1992 it resumed work. The first year a banking institution existed exclusively for the international financial relations of Russia with other countries. Nevertheless, already in 1993, the organization received permission to conduct business and activities in the banking sector. This bank was one of the first to appear in the Russian Federation. Initially, the main task of the institution was to provide services to legal entities, as well as interbank currency trading on exchanges.

The impetus for the development of the structure occurred in 1995, when the bank contributed to a major transaction for a developed and profitable company. After that, the bank became popular and was heard by many. In 1996, an office was opened in St. Petersburg. It was here that the bank actively began to position itself as a financial structure that provides great opportunities for its customers. As before, corporate clients remained the main group.
