Where to go in winter in Moscow? Short tour of the most interesting places

When we look for where to go in Moscow in winter, most likely we are talking about ice rinks, artificial ski slopes and tracks. In fact, there are much more winter entertainments in the capital, and all of them are not as monotonous as it seems. Beautiful places in Moscow in winter are found at every step, starting from Red Square, surrounded by unique buildings, ending with parks that are located on the outskirts. Well, let's not hesitate and right now we will go on a virtual winter tour of the capital.

The most famous place - Gorky Park

If on the day off the question of where to go for a walk in Moscow in winter, got an edge due to the lack of ideas, go to the world famous Gorky park. Here, in addition to attractions, a huge ice rink is functioning. 15,000 square meters are divided into different zones. In one of them, trained instructors teach young children to stay on the ice, ride back and forth, and spin in place. Girls most often go to the dance rink. There is a full-fledged disco under an open winter sky, with incendiary music and light shows. Sports enthusiasts do not miss the opportunity to visit a hockey rink. Amateur competitions are held here constantly, and anyone can take part in them.



We rush towards the wind

One of those places where to go in winter in Moscow will be not only fun, but also very original, is Tushinsky Park. In addition to walking promenades along the picturesque alleys, horse riding is also offered. Instructors will teach beginners the basics of riding, tell them how to behave on a horse, how to subordinate it to their will. If you already have experience in equestrian sports, then you can safely rent a horse and drive it in specially designated areas of the park. In fact, a winter horse ride is fundamentally different from a summer one. Impressions are much greater due to the indescribable snowy beauty that surrounds you.



Visiting a fairy tale

In our capital, equipped with one of the most beautiful places in the world - the Hermitage Garden on Karetny Ryad. Adults, choosing a place where to take a walk in Moscow in winter with their children, often forget about this eighth wonder of the world. The park consists of picturesque alleys that are surrounded on all sides by trees, shrubs and lanterns. In winter, all plants are covered with a dense layer of snow, and LEDs create a fabulous atmosphere. There are restaurants in the park where you can sit and relax with the whole family. Also in the Hermitage are two ice rinks - with artificial and natural ice. Choose any and enjoy skating at least all day.

For ski enthusiasts, Kolomensky Park is ideal , where trails stretch for five kilometers. Also, tracks are equipped in the vicinity - in Rumyantsevo, Romashkovo, Tsaritsyno, etc.

Open-air museum

Winter in Moscow parks begins with the first frosts, and to the greatest extent it manifests itself in Sokolniki. It is here that before the eyes of residents and visitors of the city appear beautiful ice figures, which are often called museum exhibits that are stored in the open. Among them, you can find everything you've ever heard of. These are the heroes of new and old cartoons, compositions from ice plants, miniatures of fairy-tale palaces and even entire medieval cities. Having admired the sculptures made of ice, you can go for a walk along the snowy alleys of Sokolnikov. This place is ideal for family walks and romantic meetings.



To the cottage to Santa Claus

Incredibly beautiful places in Moscow in winter can be found in the Kuzminki-Lublino park, which differs from many others in its vast territorial scale. There is almost everything that can interest a child and even an adult. An artificial ice rink was opened in the park, and skates for hire and sharpening are offered. There you can rent ski equipment and go for a walk along special tracks. Here equipped with beautiful ice and snow slides. The park has many buildings, many of which serve as cafes. Some have musical and literary evenings. Choose your favorite program and go to a meeting with a fairy tale. In addition, in one of the estates is the residence of Santa Claus. Then he gives gifts to the kids and tells them the New Year parables.



On the old streets

When it snows, the center of Moscow is especially beautiful. Where to take a walk in winter without traveling far beyond the Garden Ring? There are plenty of places! Firstly, in the city center there are countless skating rinks. This indicator changes annually, but on average their number approaches 30. If the weather is snowy, you can get new impressions simply by walking along the city streets. Old Arbat, Prestizhenka, Ostozhenka and many other streets, which are surrounded by ancient museum houses, will be an excellent route for walking. Walk along the alleys and at the same time ride on skates at Chistye or Patriarchy Ponds. It is always very beautiful here, and it is likely that it is in such places that two times combine - the past and the present.
