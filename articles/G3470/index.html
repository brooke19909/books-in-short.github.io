Images - where are they from

Few people know that the monument depicted in the "five thousand" has a difficult fate. In the competition for the development of a sketch, three rather famous sculptors took part in it - M.M. Antokolsky, M.O. Mikeshin and A.M. Opekushin, who defeated the remaining applicants. At the end of 1890, a finished statue was delivered from St. Petersburg to Khabarovsk, after only 35 years the monument was demolished and sent to the museum, where it lay for many years, and in wartime it was sent for re-melting. Only at the end of the 80s, at the expense of caring people, the monument was restored, and in 1992 its grand opening was held.

The bill "5000 rubles" has a very beautiful back - it shows a bridge over the Amur River - a monumental two-tier structure with a separate road and railroad tracks. The construction of this masterpiece began on July 30, 1913, and it was planned to finish it in just 26 months, which is simply unbelievable for such a huge. In Warsaw, special metal farms were made, which were then transported through Odessa to Vladivostok (by sea), and there they were loaded onto special platforms and delivered by rail to Khabarovsk. The bridge was almost assembled, 18 huge farms took their places, but the First World War violated the grandiose plan. The German cruiser Emden in the autumn of 1914 sank a ship with the last two bridge farms in the Indian Ocean. So the last two designs had to be re-ordered in Canada and shipped to Russia. Already on October 5, 1916 this magnificent building was ready - the bridge was open for traffic. Interestingly, at the time of opening, the bridge across the Amur was the largest railway construction in the Old World, and more than 13.5 million rubles were spent on its construction.

How to determine the authenticity

Of course, it would be nice to always have with you devices that help confirm the presence of signs of the authenticity of banknotes, but in practice this is not possible. In addition, the proper protection of bank notes should provide an opportunity to distinguish between falsification of all categories of the country's population, including visually impaired citizens. So "5000 rubles" is a bill, the signs of the originality of which can be determined not only visually, but also by touch:

  • the coat of arms of the city is applied with OVI paint with a variable optical effect - when the viewing angle is changed, the color becomes green or crimson;
  • there is a field with hidden stripes MVC - if you look perpendicularly from a distance of 30-40 cm, this part of the banknote is perceived as plain, and when tilted, stripes are visible;
  • the height of the digits of the serial number located on the right gradually increases;
  • there are watermarks visible in the light: on the right is the head of the monument, and on the left is the number 5,000;
  • the emblem of the Bank of Russia applied to the banknote has a polarizing effect;
  • there is a digital value made by perforation;
  • a certain element located to the right of the inscription ‚ÄúBank of Russia Ticket‚Äù is made by a print on an unpainted field (embossed without embossing) ;
  • Microtext is applied - with a very close look, you can see continuously repeating signs "5000" and "CBRF 5000";
  • a metallized thread of a diving type, having a width of 3 mm, five times comes to the surface from the back of the bill;


In addition, there are several more signs that are quite difficult to see with the naked eye.
