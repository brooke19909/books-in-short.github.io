Integrated amplifier - what is it? Types, classes and application of integrated amplifiers

Anyone who understands at least something in the world of sound should be aware that without a high-quality amplifier, there can be no talk of any good sound. A user may have a speaker system worth several thousand dollars, a vinyl player and a top-end CD player. But without a good amplifier, this whole system is useless. Therefore, integrated amplifiers are needed. What it is? Let's try to figure this out. But first, let's understand this: amplifiers are divided into integrated, two-block and three-block. But the first ones are the most common. They can be easily used in any conditions and do not take up much space. Because they are so popular.

What is an integrated amplifier?

So, what is an integrated amplifier. This is a device for amplifying sound, which includes a preamplifier, distributor and the power amplifier itself. All this is integrated into one housing (and therefore integrated), in contrast to two-unit and three-unit systems. Amplifiers of this type can provide high power density and fairly high-quality sound. At the moment, these are the most common devices. It is understandable. They are much more convenient to use in homes, offices and apartments. Integrated amplifiers have their own categories: "A", "B", "AB", "C" and so on.

The highest (and most difficult) is considered category "A". These devices have unrivaled sound quality and are very expensive. But not as expensive as their two-block and three-block ‚Äúcolleagues‚Äù (which became the lot of professionals and audiophiles). According to the type of power supply, amplifiers with an integrated unit and models with an external power supply unit (which is located outside the case) are distinguished. The second option, of course, produces a cleaner sound (in theory), but with it more fuss. Therefore, most prefer amplifiers with a built-in power supply. Now consider the main categories of integrated amplifiers.

Categorization by category

Classes of integrated amplifiers we have already considered. Now it is worth paying attention to the main categories of these devices. There are only three of them. And they differ in the type of components used. Naturally, the sound quality is different in each category. So, here are what types of integrated amplifiers are currently available:

  • Tube According to many, it is these amplifiers that produce the cleanest, highest quality and "correct" sound. Their total power cannot be high. And they are very expensive, since their main component is expensive lamps.
  • Transistor Here, transistors are responsible for amplifying sound. They can provide high power density and very good sound quality. It is these models that are most popular at present.
  • Hybrid. These devices combine several elements: lamps and transistors. The former are responsible for the ‚Äúcorrect‚Äù and high-quality sound, while the latter enhance it.

It is these types of integrated amplifiers that currently exist. The integrated amplifier circuit, whatever type it may be, suggests that all its components are enclosed in one enclosure. This is the main feature of this type of device. However, there are still such things as receivers, which are also formally considered amplifiers. But they have certain differences.

Many users do not understand the difference between amplifiers and receivers. Moreover, they believe that these are the same devices. Yes, they are both designed to amplify the signal. But the circuit diagram of the devices and their filling is fundamentally different. In order to realize this fact, users need to explain what exactly are receivers and how they differ from amplifiers. This will be discussed in the next chapter. Let's start with the most noticeable differences, and then move on to the less obvious things.

Amplifier and receiver. What is the difference?

The integrated amplifier and receiver are fundamentally different devices. The AV receiver is not just for signal amplification. Although such an option is in his arsenal. But it also includes a separate DAC for audio processing and has a built-in tuner. But most importantly, the receiver is designed to amplify 6 or 7 channels (depending on configuration), and the amplifier works with only two channels. And this is the main difference. It is also worth noting the difference in price. Since the receiver has a huge number of high-tech components, it will be much more expensive than an integrated amplifier of the same class. Nevertheless, receivers are actively purchased to install a home theater system. For this, such devices are perfectly suited. But in terms of providing high-quality stereo sound, they are not very. Here the leaders are integrated amplifiers. Now let's look at some of the most interesting models of integrated amplifiers and see what users say about them.
