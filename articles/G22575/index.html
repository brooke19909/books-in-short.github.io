A bit of history

The company was founded in Japan in 1946. Then it was called TTK. This is an abbreviation. The full name of the new firm was so unpronounceable that poor Americans broke their tongues in vain attempts to pronounce it correctly. Then the company management decided to change the name to Sony, which inexpressibly pleased the Yankees. And at that time, the American market, along with the European one, was a priority for Sony. Therefore, the change of name played a very large role in the formation of the future giant. Sony began its glorious journey with the release of tape recorders with very high-quality sound. Then came the turn of compact receivers that fit easily in your pocket. The latter have become very popular in the USA and Europe.





The manufacturer began to produce mobile phones back in those days when the ball was ruled by keypads. In tandem with the famous Scandinavian company Ericsson, the manufacturer managed to create a legend phone. It's about the Sony Ericsson K750. It was an exceptionally successful gadget. In the middle of the 2000s, the first smartphone of the company under the Xperia brand was released. But there was no question of any Ericsson. Only Sony. The device turned out to be very successful.
