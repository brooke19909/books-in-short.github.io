Crochet Techniques: Pattern Features

Many crochet work is done in several of the most common techniques. And, accordingly, the schemes are also compiled and read in different ways. Below are the most popular crochet techniques.

  1. Crochet loin technique. Its peculiarity is that its basis is the alternation in a given order of only two simple elements: an air loop and a single crochet column. Therefore, crochet patterns here are very simple and read no more complicated than cross-stitch patterns.
  2. Openwork crochet technique. This species is the most common and variable. Almost all garments of stunning beauty, lace tablecloths and napkins, shawls and wraps are connected exactly with the technique of openwork knitting. Patterns of openwork crochet can be either simple or complex, they often contain all the elements of crochet.
  3. Technique "Irish lace". A distinctive feature is that the products made in this technique consist of separately connected elements that are connected together using a mesh of air loops. Knitting patterns for these elements are very similar to openwork knitting patterns .
  4. The Bargello technique. A very interesting look that imitates Florentine embroidery with its patterns and color combinations. Used to create various plaids, bedspreads and some items of clothing. It is distinguished by the monotony of patterns, so knitting patterns are very simple and understandable.
  5. Freeform. It is very similar to the technique of Irish lace, however, the name itself (in translation ‚Äúfree form‚Äù) indicates that all the elements - scrambles, which make up a single product, are made in a free manner, from yarn of different textures and using various techniques. The main difference is that the elements are stitched or joined together during the knitting process, and are not fastened with a net. The schemes in this technique are also not specific and can be adjusted by knitters independently.

How to read them correctly

There are some rules for reading patterns that can help you master any crochet technique, even if you're still a beginner.

  1. Understand the conventions of all the elements in the diagram. See in advance how unfamiliar elements are performed.
  2. Crochet patterns are always read from the bottom up. If it is a circle, then reading starts from the center.
  3. Remember that the rows are read by the "snake" in turn: first from right to left, and then from left to right.
  4. A section of the circuit, bounded on both sides by arrows or lines, is called "rapport." It means that it must be repeated as many times as necessary.

Openwork crochet: patterns of patterns with photos

One of the most common and embossed crochet patterns is the fan. They can knit warm shawls and light capes, baby rugs and bedspreads on the bed. If you create a fan with swivel rows in a straight line, then you can decorate the hem of a skirt or sundress.

Above is a knitting pattern for shawls with a fan pattern. The triangular shawl is knitted in rotary rows (they are indicated in the diagram in different colors). Knitting begins with a chain of 6 air loops, closed in a ring. Make three lifting loops, two single crochet stitches, an air loop, three double crochet stitches, and then follow the pattern.

To get a square, knitting must be done in a circle. The colors of the rows of fans can be alternated - so the plaid or shawl will turn out more vivid and interesting.

Original ideas for combining techniques in one product

For example, using sirloin, you can tie a name or a favorite saying, pick up an openwork border and get a very unusual present. Useful tips for needlewomen

In order to avoid mistakes in the knitting process, it is necessary to adhere to the following rules:

  1. Choose yarn and crochet correctly before starting needlework. If you find a ready-made description of the product, please note that before the description of the knitting technique it is always indicated which types of yarn and which hook number is suitable for the product you have chosen.
  2. Always keep an eye on how many lifting loops or pivoting loops you make at the beginning of each new row. If their number does not match, the knitting fabric is skewed and the product will lose its appearance, so study the crochet patterns very carefully.
  3. If the size of the future product is very important to you, then before proceeding with it, knit a sample. So you can estimate the density of knitting and make the necessary calculations.
  4. It is not necessary to iron the finished product with an iron, it is better to simply sprinkle it with water and steam through gauze. And those with a relief pattern are best steamed on a pillow.
  5. When making calculations, remember that the finished product is almost always stretched when washing or steaming. Therefore, it is better to make it shorter by 1-2 centimeters.

Use your skill and imagination, do not be afraid of experimentation, and do-it-yourself things will delight you and your loved ones for a long time.
