Kuzminki metro station (photo). Which branch is this?

The Kuzminki metro station is located between Tekstilshchiki and Ryazansky Prospekt, two other Moscow metro stations, in the eponymous district of the Russian capital. It belongs to the Tagansko-Krasnopresnenskaya, one of the busiest lines in Moscow. Its opening took place in 1966, it was named after the area in which it is located. Since then, the metro station almost all the time honestly and diligently performs its function - the transportation of Muscovites and visitors.

Brief description of the station

The station itself has only two underground vestibules leading to the underground passages, which are located under the large Volgograd Avenue. If you are interested in where you can go thanks to this, then you should know: through the lobby No. 1 you can go to the avenue and Zelenodolskaya street, through No. 2 you can go to the streets of Marshal Chuykov and Zhigulevskaya, as well as the same avenue. The Kuzminki metro station is a pillar, three-span shallow-laying platform.

Its depth is only eight meters. It so happened that it is the busiest station in the Moscow metro. Now, when the extension of the metro was built for Vykhino, nothing has changed. For partial unloading of Kuzminka, especially in rush hours - in the mornings, specially empty trains that do not stop at Vykhino and Ryazansky Prospekt are specially sent here.
