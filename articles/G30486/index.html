Oschadbank branches in Kharkov: addresses, phone numbers, business hours. Oschadbank of Ukraine

Oschadbank is the largest financial institution in Ukraine with an extensive network of 4,000 institutions. The state financial institution is under the control of the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine.

History

The State Savings Bank of Ukraine is the successor to Sberbank of the USSR. It was registered on December 31, 1991 in the NBU under No. 4 as an independent institution. By a resolution of the Cabinet of Ministers, in 1999 the financial institution was renamed OJSC Oschadbank. Today, the head of his board is Andrei Pyshny.





Financial performance

Oschadbank of Ukraine is a universal institution that focuses on creating favorable conditions of service, expanding the list of products and its presence on the market.

In 2013, he ranked second in the rating of credit institutions after Privatbank, and today it is a symbol of stability and reliability. According to the main financial indicators of the market, the named bank is among the market leaders and belongs to the largest financial institutions of the country.

Its authorized capital is 34.9 billion UAH. Own funds - 8.6 million UAH., The volume of deposits - 122.2 billion UAH. According to the results of the second quarter of 2016, the bank received 252 million net profit.

Oschadbank of Ukraine has the largest network of branches in the country - 4,000 units. A financial institution lends weakly to individuals, but accepts deposits and provides loans to state corporations. The set of services includes:

  • cash investing;
  • operations with metals, etc.

Banking News

The events of 2014 influenced the situation in the banking market. Many lending institutions needed the help of a regulator. The National Bank has developed individual recapitalization programs and provided funding. To date, the twenty largest institutions in the country have been capitalized. The full program was implemented by two state institutions - Oschadbank and Ukreximbank, as well as Aval, VTB and UkrSibbank. Ukrsotsbank, OTP, Pivdenny and Prominvest managed ahead of schedule.

The share of capital in the structure of sources of financing for all of the listed credit institutions reached 5%. The adequacy of regulatory capital in the entire system as of August 1 was 13.84% with a minimum of 10%.

According to the plans of the NBU, the next stage of system restoration is the specific weight of 7% by the end of 2017, 10% for 2019. Of the twenty largest financial institutions in the country, Ukrgasbank, ING, Agricole Bank and Citibank do not need financing at all, and Fidobank and Khreschatyk have already gone bankrupt.





A new market direction - a bank in the phone

The largest public financial institution OJSC Oschadbank back in 2015 served customers through 4949 branches. Today, only 4559 points are actively working. As a result of modernization, 390 branches were closed or restructured. And this is not the limit. Until the end of 2016, there will be about 500 new branches in the country, and by the end of next year - 750.

The reality is that the financial services market is gradually moving to digital communication channels. Competition is now carried out not between bank branches, but between terminal networks, online services. The transition of the form of work to a digital service channel leads to an unreasonable increase in the cost of supporting the work of departments. The management strategy is changing. After 2-3 years, most of the branches will no longer be in demand.

In fact, the customer‚Äôs need for live communication will only be about informing about new banking products. Even cash transactions are gradually moving to iBox terminals.

Ukraine picked up the global trend. Maintaining bank branches is expensive. Therefore, there is a massive transition to self-service. A vivid example of this is Privatbank. In each department, employees teach people how to use self-service terminals.

Ukraine is a country of cash. This situation will not change in the next twenty years. Therefore, to work with money, people will need at least terminals and Internet banking.





Oschadbank and Ukreximbank will sell the EBRD

As part of the banking sector refinancing program, the issue of selling up to 20% of the shares of two state-owned banks by 2018 is being considered. But if back in 2015, these conversations were related to the fact that both banks were facing default, today this step is considered as a new development strategy. The entry of the EBRD and the International Finance Corporation into state-owned banks will improve the quality of asset management. Oschadbank, which is engaged in lending to legal entities, and Ukreximbank, which finances export-import operations, will switch to work with the population.
