Krasnodar Territory, Ashe: the private sector. Photos, prices and reviews

The small but very attractive Black Sea village of Ashe is conveniently located just seven kilometers northwest of the famous resort of Lazarevskoye.

Bliss and tranquility

The area is simply surrounded by greenery and fragrant flowers due to sunny weather, which does not leave Asha for 280 days a year. The aromas of the spicy sea breeze and the purest mountain air pacify, give harmony and envelop, like a soft cloud, returning people to the bosom of nature.





No European sophistication, Turkish abundance or Egyptian mystery can compare with the native soft subtropics of our Krasnodar Territory.

How to get to Ashe resort

If it is unacceptable for you to be on a long trip, you can buy a plane ticket to Adler, Krasnodar or Gelendzhik. From cities to get to the village of Ashe is not difficult. Rail transport goes to the Lazarevskoye station, the distance of seven kilometers to the resort of Ashe can be overcome by regular bus or taxi.

Since the location of the village of Ashe - Krasnodar Territory - the private sector will always offer inexpensive housing. Therefore, as part of a budget trip, you can not spend money on airline tickets or rail and go to Ashe in your car. The path starts from the M4 highway and goes to the M7 highway, which will lead to the destination.

Cozy apartment with sea view

For everyone there is accommodation to taste in Asha - a private sector or a boarding house with all amenities - the choice is yours. One thing is for sure: Caucasian hospitality is not a legend, and a tourist will certainly become a dear guest in any house or hotel. It is not customary to book expensive hotels with a daily cost of accommodation starting at 4000 r. per room.





The bulk of tourists makes their choice in favor of apartments, cottages and small houses. For everyone who comes to rest in Asha, the private sector provides a choice of housing for every taste and at affordable prices.

History of the area

The village was named after the mountainous rocky river that flows into the Black Sea. The place of the confluence of the river is the village of Ashe. The private sector, in which the indigenous Black Sea Circassians still live, they are also called Shapsugs, pleases modern tourists with an extensive offer of housing, cozy cafes and souvenir shops.

The legend of the murdered old people and conscience

At first glance, the peaceful and cozy town of Ashe has a terrifying story about the Rock of the Old Ones in the Ashe Valley. Local residents of the valley collect the famous "drunk" honey from rhododendron, which causes drowsiness. Ashe is famous for this honey; the private sector everywhere offers to buy it at relatively low prices. The legend is associated with ‚Äúdrunk‚Äù honey.

The ancient warlike Shapsug tribe was famous for its cruelty. Not a single old man was in this tribe. All weak and burdensome families, according to the law in force in society, were thrown from a cliff into a river. One warrior could not kill his father, despite the fact that he asked him to. The son hid his father in a cave and carried him water and food. Once upon a time enemies attacked the soldiers. Father saved the tribe by letting the enemies of "heady" honey get drunk. The enemies fell asleep, the soldiers loaded them into galleys and sent them down the river. Since then, the high cliff has been called the Rock of the Old Ones, and the Circassians have gained respect for the older generation.

Reviews
