What is a Kiwi Wallet?

Qiwi System is a multi-platform payment service used internationally. The group was formed in Moscow in 2007, and the eponymous bank was later created.



Since that time, the system has acquired tens of millions of users around the world. The wallet is widely accessible and has a variety of feature sets, so that the use of the payment system has become commonplace for most people.

Qiwi electronic payment system gives its users access to a huge number of transactions. Using the service, you can pay for any services and goods on the Internet, make utility and other types of payments, deposit funds to accounts and cards in any banks, pay loans and replenish credit cards.

Those who are familiar with freelance and other types of online earnings know that many exchanges withdraw wages to this electronic payment service. And this is not all the features of the system.

Wallet holders can use cash, as well as prepaid cards and other non-cash methods for transactions. Access to the service is provided from several platforms. Initially, the Qiwi Wallet could be replenished in cash using special terminals located at retail outlets and stores. Also, access to the system was provided by a computer: a user could log into his personal account using any browser and replenish his account using a bank card of another bank.





With the development of mobile technologies, developers have created a special application available for smartphones on any software, whether it be Android or IOS.
