Crochet tunic: scheme, description, types and methods of knitting

A tunic is a type of clothing that resembles both a short dress and a long sweater. Often, these wardrobe items have a slightly loose or very wide cut, their length reaches the hips. They can be worn by children, girls and women, regardless of age, since there are enough varieties of models of tunics. Their choice is dictated only by personal preferences and good manners.

Many people like these clothes for the opportunity to hide the flaws of the figure, but it is worth remembering that the tunic always shifts the emphasis to the legs.

Types of tunics

Knitters who prefer crochet weaving have a particular passion for tunics. How does a tunic fit? The scheme may not be useful if you select a familiar pattern, then the thing fits quite simply.

Of course, for beginners, everything seems complicated, but the cut of this garment lends itself to simplification. If desired, it can be composed of only squares without sophisticated armholes, rounds and necks.

There are several main types of tunics:

  • Warm (continuous knitting).
  • Decorative (openwork pattern).
  • Beach (very large openwork).




Most often, the object of creativity is a decorative tunic crocheted. The scheme and description of their models will allow the needlewoman to create a wonderful thing. To get a product pleasing to the eye, it is not in vain to spend time, effort and yarn, it is advisable to plan the model, color and general appearance in advance. Such a tunic can be worn daily or worn on holidays.

Knitting a warm product

Tunic for the cold season is knitted from warm yarn. By the way, the pattern does not have to be very dense. The degree of its openwork depends on the plans of the craftswoman: to wear a tunic as a sweater, vest or as an element of decor. The beauty of tunics is that they can be worn on top of other items of clothing: golfs, T-shirts, tops, blouses. A warm crocheted tunic, the scheme of which provides for the presence of lace, can be knitted from smooth or fluffy yarn. The hook number is selected according to the thickness of the yarn.

The optimal composition can be considered from 30% to 80% of natural fibers (wool, angora, mohair) in the composition of the thread. A lower content will lead to rapid wear of the product, its deformation (tension, shrinkage), the appearance of spools and the loss of a neat appearance.

A dense tunic crocheted (almost any scheme can be used) can be connected either with an even continuous web or consist of separate fragments.

The subtleties of combining patterns

A feature of complex products is the ability to combine several patterns. In this case, there are three of them. Often when trying to combine more than two patterns in one product, there is a risk of getting an unattractive and clumsy combination. The same model was developed very successfully. Here, square fragments are used to form a canvas with even geometric shapes. These are stripes on the bottom of the product, on the chest and on the sleeves.

The second pattern - openwork "bushes" - is used for knitting the middle part of the tunic and its sleeves. Triangles (the third element) perform a dividing function. The most interesting thing is how the developers of the model approached the design of the most difficult areas: the neckline, the sleeve ridge and the armhole. A simple net was used for their knitting, which is why the tunic was crocheted (the net pattern can be the simplest) only won. A grid with cells of arbitrary sizes was also used to fit the motif into the upper part of the sleeve.
