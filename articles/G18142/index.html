Petrovsky lake in Russia: description

Petrovsky Lake in Russia is not one, there are several. They are located in the Pskov, Leningrad, Moscow regions and in the Altai Territory. They are all different. Let's look at each of them.

Petrovskoe (Uzskoe) lake

The lake, located in the Pskov region, officially has two names: the first is Uzskoye, the second is Petrovskoye. The flowing body of water, the coastline is indented, there are many shallow areas overgrown with vegetation. Refers to the basin of the river. Ties. There are practically no beaches. The bottom is silty. The shores are very steep, low and sloping. Many islands and bays are characteristic of such a reservoir as Lake Peter. Where is it located in the Pskov region? In the Dedovichi district, between two similar reservoirs: lake. Gorodnovsky and lake. Lokno. Uzskoye occupies 2.3 km 2 of the Sudom Upland. The average depth in the pond does not exceed 5 m, but in some places it can reach 10 m. It feeds on precipitation and underground keys.

The lake is surrounded by forests and meadows, a little further there are fields for the irrigation of which water from this reservoir is used. There are practically no tourists here, but there are a lot of fishermen. This is easy to explain - Petrovsky Lake is rich in aquatic vertebrates. Along the coast there is pike and perch, bream and bleak - in depth. Previously, there was a lot of zander, however, due to siltation, its population decreased significantly.

There are two settlements in the lake area: the village of Uza (directly on the coast) and the village of Vyshegorod (1.5 km south of the reservoir).



Petrovskoe (Kuchane) lake

In the Pskov region there is another Petrovsky lake, which is called Kuchane. It is located in the Pushkinogorsky district. The dimensions are medium (area - almost 175 hectares). Refers to the basin of the river. Soroti. The pond is shallow, the average does not exceed 2 m. There are pits closer to the center. The depth in them reaches almost 4 m. It is practically not used for tourism and fishing, since one part of the reservoir is located on the territory of the Mikhailovskoye Museum-Reserve, and the other is a part of Pushkinogorye. The bottom is mostly muddy, but in some places stones and sand are found. The banks are gentle, the approaches to the water are steep. Wetlands are often found. The pond is surrounded by forests and meadows. Vegetation under water is well developed, this creates favorable conditions for many species of fish. In large quantities, bream, roach, pike, perch, etc. are found here. The villages of Petrovskoye and Mikhailovskoye are located near the lake.
