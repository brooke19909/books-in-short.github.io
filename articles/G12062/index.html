Vitamins are Organic Essentials for Life

Vitamins are organic substances that (for the most part) are not synthesized in the human body (or are formed in small quantities), so they are indispensable in nutrition. There are 20 known vitamins that affect human health. The amount of consumption depends on the gender, age and level of physical activity of a person.

Vitamins are organic substances necessary for human health. Their deficiency leads to serious consequences.





Lack of any vitamin in food can cause various diseases.

Vitamins are organic substances that affect life, participate in the synthesis of hormones, metabolism, contribute to the normal functioning of the body.

Almost all vitamins are well studied, their role in the life of a living organism is determined.

Vitamins 2 types

Vitamins are organic substances necessary for the formation of enzymes. Therefore, the high activity of enzymes depends on the amount of vitamins received in the body.

Fat-soluble vitamins are absorbed only with fat. Vitamin A intake, for example, is best combined with sour cream or oil.

Water-soluble vitamins do not need fats.

Excess and disadvantage

Vitamins are organic substances that cannot be replaced by anything in the body. Most of them enter the body with food. Depending on the amount of vitamins consumed with food, several conditions can be distinguished:

  1. Avitaminosis.
  2. Hypovitaminosis.
  3. Hypervitaminosis.

The first condition is characterized by metabolic disorders, which is caused by a prolonged deficiency of any vitamins (or one vitamin) in the body. Vitamin deficiency leads to serious illness or death.

The second condition is characteristic with a reduced intake of vitamins. Hypovitaminosis is manifested in rapid fatigue, decreased performance, vision in the dark, peeling of the skin, and a decrease in immunity.



The third condition, characteristic of prolonged intake of excess vitamins, leads to a violation of metabolic processes and body functions. Typically, this condition manifests itself with excessive intake of fat-soluble vitamins (for example, A and D). These vitamins are able to accumulate in fat depots.

Vitamin and mineral complexes

Vitamins are complex organic substances that must be used in a balanced way, without excess and lack. There are many different options, both in conjunction with minerals, and separately.

In the modern world, every day new data about vitamins and their effect on a living organism appear. It is believed that they are able to improve the internal environment, increase the functional abilities of a person. Therefore, modern dietetics considers the intake of vitamins as a means for the prevention of diseases.
