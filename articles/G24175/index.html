On a map of Hungary, Szeged can be found in a southeast direction from Budapest. The distance from this city to the capital is 160 km. Very close to Szeged are the borders of Romania (20 km) and Serbia (10 km).

What is the prevailing weather in Szeged (Hungary)? The climate in the territory of the city is temperate continental. Due to this, there is a very mild winter, warm summers and even rainfall. The average temperature during the year in Szeged is +10.6 degrees. In the coldest winter month of January, it reaches -1.8, and in hot July and August - + 20.8 and +20.2, respectively. History

The city of Szeged (Hungary) originated in ancient times. Historians know about the small settlement of Partiskum, which was located in these places in the Roman era. In subsequent centuries, these lands were settled by the Slavs. In the 9th century Hungarians to this territory. In the 13th century the city was ravaged and destroyed by hordes of Mongols.

However, life went on. The city was rebuilt. In 1543, he became part of the Ottoman Empire. After exile in the 13th century. from the lands occupied by modern Hungary, the Turks, the Habsburgs gained power over Szeged.

In 1879, the city was destroyed again. The reason for this was not the foreign conquerors at all, but the water of the seemingly calm and quiet Tisza River. It spilled with such force that it actually wiped the city from the face of the earth, leaving only 500 out of 3,000 houses to stand.

The news of the tragedy reached Vienna. Emperor Franz Joseph decided to completely restore, or rather rebuild Szeged to become more beautiful than before. And the intended was fulfilled. Moreover, during the construction of Szeged, large European cities were taken as a model. New Synagogue

Just 10 minutes walk from the center of Szeged (Hungary) is Yoshika Street. Here is the New Synagogue. This building was built in 1903 according to the project of architect Lipot Bauchmorn.

It is one of the most beautiful synagogues in the world. In its appearance, elements of the Mediterranean, Moorish and Arabic styles were perfectly combined. The entire structure is 48 m long, 35 m wide and 49 m high. In the summer, the New Synagogue hides behind the foliage of trees in the surrounding garden. When viewing a building from the street, travelers can see only the dome, which is decorated with bas-reliefs, turrets and cornices.

No less exquisite is the interior of this building. Blue and golden tones, as well as ivory elements, are used in its interior decoration. The huge glass dome, which depicts the sky, as well as the altar, for the manufacture of which Jerusalem marble, white gold, silver and other precious metals, as well as stained-glass windows made of Venetian glass, cannot shake its beauty.

On weekdays, the synagogue is open from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. for visitors. Currently, it is used as a concert hall, with a capacity of 1300 people.

Church of the Vow

On Cathedral Square, tourists can admire the magnificent architecture of the Cathedral of Our Lady. It is also called the Church of the Vow. Where does the cathedral get such an unusual name from? The fact is that after the flood that happened in 1879, the inhabitants of the city took a vow. They decided at all costs to build a magnificent Catholic church glorifying the Virgin Mary, the patroness of Hungary. The temple began to be erected in 1913 according to the project of the architect Fridbesh Shulek. However, a year later, construction was suspended due to the outbreak of World War I and the ensuing economic crisis. Work continued only in 1923. Construction was completed in 1930. Then the cathedral was consecrated.

According to experienced tourists, the architectural appearance of the church is very interesting. It mixes elements of the Byzantine oriental style, as well as Romanesque and Gothic. The main facade is decorated with two slender belfries, each of which rises above the ground to a height of 91 m.

The interior of the temple is replete with statues and reliefs. Decorate it and mosaics. One of them is located directly above the altar. It depicts the Virgin Mary in the national costume of Hungarian women and in Szeged slippers. The third largest in the country is the 9040-pipe organ located in the cathedral.
