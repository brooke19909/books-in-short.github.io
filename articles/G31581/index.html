How does the voltage comparator

The voltage comparator is a rather interesting device. How does he work? Thanks to what can he fulfill his functions? It is impossible not to note their significant importance in the numerous household appliances, which are in large numbers in every home.

general information



To control electronic circuits, a large number of different devices are used. They allow you to branch and tune the signals. To compare two different pulses, a comparator is used. What is he like? A voltage comparator is a device that compares two different voltages and amperage, and on the basis of the received data it produces a final power signal. With its help, it indicates the higher value obtained and indicates the ratio of the obtained parameters. The device has two analog input terminals that can process a negative and a positive signal. They also have, like the ADC, one binary digital output. What is its functioning based on? To ensure operation, a voltage comparator on transistors is always created.

What is used in modern times





Initially, integrated voltage comparators were widely used. For the characteristic features of their work, they were called high-speed. They require the presence of differential voltage within a certain range, which is much lower than the voltage in the power supply. Such devices did not allow the presence of external signals that would go beyond the established framework. Modern analog-digital voltage comparator has a transistor input. As a rule, the potential signal for it should not exceed a value of 0.3 V. Often you can meet the voltage comparator on the microcontroller. For such cases, products of the companies "Microchip" and "Atmel" are used. If you have to deal with a stereo comparator (they are also called ultrafast type comparators), then the required threshold does not exceed 0.2 V. It should be noted that the range used is limited by the specific input voltage.

And it's all?

Of course not! There is also a voltage comparator on the operational amplifier. It is a device in which the difference between the input and the high signal resistance is extremely finely balanced. Due to this, they are used in cases where it is necessary to ensure the operation of circuits with a small voltage. Also, devices that use operational amplifiers are often also called a video spectral comparator. Theoretically, it can work in a configuration where an open loop is provided (that is, there is no negative feedback). The device in such cases is used as a low performance comparator.

What are the disadvantages of comparators with operational amplifiers





They have such negative points:

  1. So, their main purpose is to work in linear mode, when there is no negative feedback. Also their feature is a fairly significant recovery mode.
  2. Almost all operational amplifiers have internal compensation capacitors, which limit the slew rate of the output voltage when generating high-frequency signals. Therefore, the use of such schemes leads to a slight delay in the pulses.
  3. And finally, the comparator does not have internal hysteresis.

Because of these shortcomings, the use of a device to control different circuits often dispenses with various amplifiers. The one exception is the use of a generator.

Using



The operation of a voltage comparator is often used during production processes where there is a limited output voltage. But it is necessary that it interacts well with digital logic. Therefore, they can often be found in a variety of thermal devices. An example is a temperature switch, a temperature controller, and more. They can also be used to compare signals and impedances for devices such as a stabilizer, timer, and more. In household devices, almost any microcircuit contains them. A voltage comparator can be found in the microwave, oven, and many other modern pieces of technology.
