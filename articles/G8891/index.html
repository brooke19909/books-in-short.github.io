How to find out debt from bailiffs?

Every person at least once in his life got into debt. If the amounts are small, then they can still be somehow remembered. But when money needs to be returned to several authorities at once, confusion can begin. If you do not pay off the debt on time, then at the most unexpected moment it can come out sideways. For example, during the registration for a flight, it turns out that the bank wrote a statement of claim against you and won the process. How to find out debt from bailiffs?





Who can not be allowed abroad?

In Art. 67 of the Law on Enforcement Proceedings, it is said that persons who have debts of alimony, loans, taxes, fines, payments for housing and communal services, who, without justifiable reasons, have delayed the repayment period, can be entered into the FSSP database. Based on these data, they will be restricted from leaving the Russian Federation. However, a separate court decision is not required. For an individual entrepreneur, the decision of the tax service on debt collection in itself is an executive document. On its basis, information can be entered into the FSSP database. Therefore, it is worthwhile to know in advance the debt from the bailiffs.

Moreover, the basis for initiating enforcement proceedings may be any administrative violations: non-return of money to the tax, traffic police, border, veterinary service, customs, labor inspection. Any penalty must be paid within 30 days from the day the provision comes into force (Article 32.2 of the Code of Administrative Offenses). Then it should be presented to the body that issued the decision, documents confirming the fact of payment. If this was not done, then all materials will be sent to the bailiff. Up to this point, you can safely travel outside the borders of the Russian Federation. And in order not to worry about this at all, it is worthwhile to find out the debt from the bailiffs in advance.





Limitations

After the decision is made, the debtor has five days to voluntarily pay off the debt. This right is spelled out in the document itself. If there is no answer, then the executive service may engage in compulsory collection of debts, for example, seize property. If the debt is not repaid voluntarily, the offender does not have the right to travel outside the country. But if measures are taken to enforce debt collection, then such a ban is not always legal.

Example

The individual entrepreneur owes the bank 5 million rubles. The bailiff recovered the debt from the property - three cars registered on a business entity. It was found that, say, KAMAZ IPs were not delivered due to fraudulent activities, and hatchbacks were stolen and put on the wanted list. Then the bailiff decided to recover the debt from the income of the individual entrepreneur. It was found that a person has a source of cash in the form of wages at the place of work of another entrepreneur. For 6 months, you can recover 50 thousand rubles in favor of the bank. Since the debt has not been fully repaid, the bailiffs may prohibit the offender from leaving the Russian Federation. But in this case, you can go to court and appeal this decision. But it is better to know the debt from the bailiffs in advance.





Amount does not matter

The legislation on this subject does not spell any restrictions. Even penny debts can become the basis for the inclusion of the debtor in the "black list". But tax authorities rarely apply with a writ of execution if the amount does not exceed 1,500 rubles. The cost of paperwork may not pay off. But there were cases when the control system allowed individuals with debts of several thousand rubles to leave the country. Failures happen, but rarely.

The Federal Law also does not indicate the period for which the ban may apply. Order No. 36 of the Moscow Office of the FSSP states that it cannot exceed 6 months. And then what? If the debt is not paid, it is possible that the relevant authority will apply for a second order. Therefore, it is worth knowing the debt from the bailiffs in advance and paying off the debts.

How the ban mechanism works

After the execution of the decision, the bailiff must forward it to the debtor, as well as to the Migration Service. The latter makes the data in the list of "travel abroad". If such a person does not have a passport, then it will be impossible to obtain it until the debt is paid off. If there is a document, then a request is sent for its seizure for the duration of the ban. The bailiff has no right to do this on his own. In practice, passports are extremely rare. And if the document was not taken from the debtor, then the court may not recognize the decision on the ban as legal.

There is a very important point in the procedure - non-receipt by the violator of the decision to impose a ban. If the person does not live at the indicated address or avoids receiving the message, then the sending is still considered valid. Therefore, it is worth worrying in advance about free movement around the world, especially if you know that enforcement proceedings are being conducted against you. When to check debts? At least one month before the expected departure date.





How to find out if a bailiff has a debt?

Contact the Federal Bailiff Service (FSSP). Their branches are located in almost every city of the Russian Federation. But in order to get the necessary information, you will have to visit the office during reception hours. At the same time, it is likely that on the day you contact you will not receive all the necessary information. If the line is large, and you arrived at lunch, you may not have time to see a specialist. Those who nevertheless decided to personally visit the branch of the FSSP need to remember that throughout the Russian Federation this structure accepts citizens on Tuesdays (from 8:00 to 16:00) and Thursdays (from 16:00 to 19:00).
