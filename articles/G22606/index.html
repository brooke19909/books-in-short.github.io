A master class on how to make a mouse out of paper so that it is as realistic as possible:

  • You will need to take a piece of square paper. It is best to choose two-tone leaves: white on one side and gray on the other.
  • Lay it in front of you with the white side up and so that it is a rhombus (picture 1).
  • Fold the leaf in half - connect the top with the bottom. And then spread it out.
  • Now you need to bend the top and bottom to the fold in the middle. Do as shown in picture 1.
  • Make two more folds. To do this, wrap the top and bottom of the new figure to the center (image 2).
  • Now you need to turn the right side of the right part of the part to the left as shown in Figure 3.
  • When all the elements are wrapped and new fold lines are formed, you need to turn the edge of the upper triangle under the bottom (see picture 4).
  • The left triangle also needs to be turned back (Figure 5).
  • Now fold the edges to the left again, as in image 6.
  • Fold the shape in half horizontally.
  • Unscrew the triangles from above (picture 8).
  • Make the ears as in image 9.
  • Now you need to make a ponytail. To do this, collapse the right side, as in picture 10. Then turn the tail inward and fold it up again (see illustrations 11 and 12).
  • Fold the tail in half and flip the figure on its side (picture 13).
