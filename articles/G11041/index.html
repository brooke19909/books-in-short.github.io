What does refurbished mean?

Although the word refurbished, which in English means ‚Äúrefurbished‚Äù or ‚Äúrefurbished,‚Äù may conjure up not-so-encouraging pictures of retailers and manufacturers resurrecting defective products, this term covers many products. Sometimes a customer submits a purchase in order to receive money without even opening the box or never using the purchase. Although nothing bad happened with the equipment, it can no longer be sold as new and should be offered as repaired, often at a great discount (from 15% or more).

Other reasons goods get a refurbished tag include:

  • Damage to the delivery or external defects: minor scratches or dents in the delivery will force you to return the purchase to the manufacturer.
  • Demo samples. Demonstration goods used at trade shows, retail stores, or reviewers may be re-packaged as remanufactured.
  • Open box. Opened packaging can mean anything. From a technical point of view, this should not be a problem, and the product is no different from the new one, just the box was opened.
  • Manufacturing defects. If a defect was discovered, the manufacturer could replace the defective parts and sell the device or send it to retail as a repaired one.

What is meant by refurbished on a product is in most cases unknown. If the buyer just changed his mind and returned it, then in fact he is new and will allow the new owner to save a significant amount. But in the case of open packaging, the employee of the return department could simply stick such markings on the equipment that was returned for the reason that it did not work or had a large defect. And then the new buyer was very unlucky.

So what to do to someone who wants to save money but is scared of repaired products? It is best to purchase goods from a reputable retailer that provides a generous warranty.





Where to buy refurbished electronics?

According to customer reviews, it is advisable to purchase such goods directly from the manufacturer (manufacturer refurbished), thus insuring himself that he carefully tested and tuned the device, or from an authorized dealer who receives the products directly from the factory, for example, from Amazon or Newegg (seller refurbished) , which means "restored by the seller").

What to look for when buying returned equipment?

Not all refurbished products are the same. There is a sale of equipment restored by third parties, but you should look for electronics, the repair of which was carried out by the manufacturer itself (manufacturer refurbished), which means extending the manufacturer's warranty to it. The equipment is carefully checked and brought to original standards.

Warranty

Many retailers and manufacturers of remanufactured products offer a 90-day warranty. As mentioned earlier, for peace of mind it is better to look for the full (for example, for a period of one year), if, of course, you can find one. Unfortunately, the extension of the guarantee for such goods is usually not applicable, but it is necessary to ask the seller whether it is possible to increase its duration, not in order to buy it, but for the reason that this is a sure sign of the seller‚Äôs confidence in the quality of the technology he is selling. .

Before buying, you should definitely make sure that there is at least some opportunity to return the goods.

Accessories and other accessories

You must make sure that you have all the chargers, cables, cords, manuals, software, drivers, and everything else that is included with your device. You should also check the full description and list of specifications of the purchased equipment and make sure that this is exactly the thing that the buyer is counting on - a simple substitution of the model number, for example, may mean that he is facing a completely different product.

Refurbished products: is it worth it to buy?
