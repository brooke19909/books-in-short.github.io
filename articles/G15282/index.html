Crucifer family

The temperate zone of the Northern Hemisphere - it is here that the cruciferous family, with more than three thousand species, is mainly distributed. It includes annuals, biennials, as well as perennials with a similar structure.

The cruciferous family got its name because of the cross-shaped petals in the flowers of its species. Other common symptoms are four sepals that make up the calyx, four petals that make up the corolla, stamens (two short and four long). Inflorescence brush is also a characteristic feature of all plants belonging to the family. Crucifers form fruits - pods. As for the leaves, a basal rosette is formed from them or they are attached to the stem alternately. The root system is the core, in some members of the family a root crop is formed. The erect stem is often covered with soft or hard hairs.

Plants are pollinated mainly by bees and bumblebees, flies and butterflies. This suggests that members of the family are wonderful honey plants with a strong aroma.

Vegetable, oilseeds, fodder, medicinal, ornamental plants, as well as weeds - all of them are members of this family. Cruciferous weeds are a serious headache for agricultural workers: often a special treatment regimen is required in order to destroy them.
