How did the name



How did the area called Divnogorye arise? For many years now, the Voronezh region has been the subject of close attention of various kinds of scientists, mainly ethnographers and cultural scientists. It was possible to establish that the territory received such a name for a reason. This happened thanks to the chalk pillars of the remnants, which the locals called the "diva" (miracle). For the first time, the name was recorded in writing in 1389. Ignatius Smolyanin, who accompanied Metropolitan Pimen, made detailed notes about the path traveled.

Famous monument of architecture

It can be argued that, in general, our country is famous for its cathedrals, temples, and simply magnificent buildings known throughout the world. Divnogorye (Voronezh region) is no exception. The monastery, called the Assumption - is another confirmation of this statement.

The construction is located 18 km from the city of Ostrogozhsk and 7 km from the city of Korotoyak, exactly in the place where the chalk mountain and unusual pillars are located. Historians managed to find out that the monastery was founded around 1640 by hieromonk Joasaph and Xenophon. In 1826, the building was almost completely restored, and a little later it was decided to replenish it with icons and property from the Ascension Korotoyak monastery.





Today, the main shrine of the Cathedral Assumption Church of the Divnogorsk Monastery is the Sicilian miraculous icon of the Mother of God, which is depicted on the throne with a baby. Experts say that it was once performed in the Greco-Byzantine style. Ordinary residents first saw the shrine only in 1092. According to legend, after the capture of Constantinople, the icon was transferred to Russia, and the Divnogorsky monastery already received it from its founders.

In addition, inside the chalk pillar, on the upper slope of the mountain, there is a carved church of St. I. Forerunner. An altar rises above the building at a height of several steps. The throne is carved in stone. There is a cave on the right side of the entrance. Here everyone can see the remains of the founders of the monastery.

Those who follow south from this holy place for every Orthodox person will be able to look at another church, or rather, what is left of it. Now there is only a chapel.

Natural monuments

The necropolis and the ruins of a medieval fortress - the Mayatsky settlement and the Mayatsky pottery complex - are still preserved in the reserve. These ancient monuments date back to the 9th-10th centuries.

Unfortunately, most of the chalk pillars were destroyed during the construction of the railway, but today there are 5 more such unusual natural monuments in Divnogorye that can be accessed by a special wooden staircase.





Visitors enjoy climbing Mount Shatrishche, located 7 km from the railway platform. The Kazan Church was once located in this place. Now it is abandoned, organized excursions are not conducted here, so only the most desperate travelers can visit such a mysterious place.

In addition, not everyone knows that the Museum also includes the Cretaceous Canyon - a deep and winding ravine with sheer walls. Bypassing it, you can perfectly consider the restored settlement from the time of the Salt-Mayak culture.

Flora and Fauna Divnogorye



Geographically, the reserve is located on the edge of the northern steppe zone. A lot of time on its territory people were engaged in farming, mainly related to sheep breeding. That is why the vegetation of the reserve was destroyed. However, due to the fact that cattle grazing is prohibited here today, and the number of people has decreased significantly, the grass cover has already been partially restored.

More than 250 species of petrophytic and xerophytic plants grow here. Botanists of 40% of the vegetation of Divnogorye are classified as calciphytes (small lovers). On the surface of the plateau, the Kozo-Polyansky breaker and gelatinous algae-algae are often found - plants that are considered quite rare in the Russian Federation. In 1956, a forest protection strip of ash and maple was planted in the center of the plateau.

Occasionally, on the territory of the reserve you can see the golden eagle and bustard listed in the Red Book. There are also a lot of bee-evers and eagle owls, hares and foxes. Bats live in crevices.

Local features

Many travelers argue that visiting Divnogorye is best in the warm season, when the heat is not yet felt so much. For example, in May, June or early fall. In the summer in the reserve it is stuffy, in winter it is cold and windy, therefore walks in the fresh air are unlikely to bring the expected pleasure.

Both the administration of the museum-reserve and many regular guests claim that the April flood of the Don is a sight that is truly mesmerizing. Floodplain meadows fill up with meltwater, and relict trees and grasses begin to bloom wildly.

In winter, local residents and guests of the Voronezh region enjoy sledding from the mountains and organize real festivities.

It is recommended to stay overnight at a local hotel, which not only meets all the requirements and requirements of modern travelers, but also has a spacious and 24-hour guarded parking lot.

It is worth paying attention that during the tourist season in Divnogorye various exhibitions, festivals and concerts are held.
