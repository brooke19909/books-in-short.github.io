Safety information

This should be kept in mind when diving. In the magnificent underwater world, you cannot completely let euphoria take hold of you. Novice divers should be guided by some simple rules.

  1. Do not submerge at night. Underwater inhabitants at this time are most active, and people, due to the limited view, can not fully control the situation.
  2. In the area of ‚Äã‚Äãcorals, water should be entered exclusively in special shoes. Why? The small fragments of coral at the bottom are very sharp. Also, sea urchins - fish whose body is covered with sharp needles - bring inconvenience to vacationers.
  3. Do not touch anything in the water. Behind the charming appearance of a calm and non-aggressive marine essence, sharp teeth and a lightning-fast reflex reaction can be hidden.
  4. The instructor's instructions should be meticulously followed and ambiguities clarified directly with him.

Excursion to Giza

In addition to a beach holiday, by the way, according to experts, the best in the world according to the price / quality criterion, Egypt is a country of interesting excursions, a real storehouse of history, a treasury of the oldest architectural structures of mankind. Near Cairo, on the deserted mountain plateau of Giza, there are the most famous great pyramids in the world dedicated to the ancient pharaohs: Cheops, Chefren, Mikerin. Here, at the foot of the plateau, stands the famous Sphinx, as well as burial churches. The cost of the excursion to these pyramids ranges from $ 75 to $ 100. The road to them from the Movenpick Resort Soma Bay 5 * hotel (Soma Bay), if you go by bus, takes about 7 hours. The standard excursion time is from 1 00 to 22 00 . Tourists traveling to the pyramids are advised to bring a hat, sunglasses, sunblock, water, a pillow from the room, warm clothes.

Abu simbel

Perhaps the next most popular tourist destination after the pyramids is Abu Simbel. It is located south of 280 kilometers from the city of Aswan. This is a two-part temple. It is carved into the rock during the period from 1313 to 1218 BC. e. This name in Arabic sounds like "Father of bread." The fact is that from the bend of the river in the bas-relief the ancient masters carved a stone man in a stone with an apron of a pointed shape. It was in the apron that the sailors identified some semblance of bread measure. Hence the name ...

The first part of the temple is dedicated to Pharaoh Ramses II. The second (it is much smaller) is dedicated to the goddess Hathor, who favors the Egyptian queens. It was built in honor of Ramses' beloved wife, Queen Nefertari.

The rock, consisting of sedimentary fine-grained sandstone, has long been called sacred in Egypt.
