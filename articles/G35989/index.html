History of Baden-Baden

We have already mentioned that the city owes its glory to the ancient Romans - great lovers to soak in the baths. At the dawn of our era, they built on the shores of Os a bath for wounded veterans. Later, a settlement grew up here, which became known as Civitas-Aurelia-Aquensis. The city became truly famous in 214, when it was visited by the emperor Caracalla. He really liked the local thermal waters. Imperial baths were built for Caracalla.

In the early Middle Ages, the baths were forgotten. But already from 1306, they were again put in order by the rulers of the burg of Badon (as the Roman Civitas-Aurelia-Aquensis began to be called in the local dialect). In the Renaissance, when doctors began to prescribe ‚Äúsluggish" patients and women who could not conceive a child, treatment on the waters, the city became so popular that a resort fee was introduced here (by the way, the first in all of Germany). The presence of mountains attracted pulmonary patients. In the nineteenth century, the resorts of Baden-Baden became the "summer capital of Europe", where the whole high society flocks. A casino is being built here (the first in Germany) and other leisure infrastructure.
