Humidifier: benefit or harm? Humidifier: instructions, reviews, price

Wellness is simply not possible without fresh, clean air. Today, through our windows and windows, a mixture of gases with dust and pollutants comes into our homes that is hardly useful. As additional sources of pollution in houses, modern materials for construction and decoration, household chemicals and gas appliances often act. To ensure effective cleaning, it is required to install a purifier and a humidifier at home, which will not only eliminate unnecessary impurities, but also raise the humidity level to the optimum value. These are special climatic devices, without which the life of a modern person is greatly complicated.





Home Air Purifiers

The air purifier is designed to rid indoor air of poplar fluff, dust, tobacco smoke, dangerous bacteria, allergens, odors and harmful impurities. In terms of design, this is usually a device that includes an engine and a set of filters that allow you to achieve the highest quality air purification from all kinds of impurities. Modern devices can have several fan speeds, so that the performance is regulated quite flexibly. The cleaner can be floor or suspended. The humidifier-ionizer, in contrast to it, has only a desktop version.





Device types

Devices that are installed under the ceiling are more focused on cleaning the air from all kinds of volatile pollutants, such as tobacco smoke, and they do not do very well with ordinary dust. Power can be up to 2000 watts and is a fairly important indicator of work efficiency. This parameter should depend on the size of the room in which the device is installed. The purifier and humidifier should not be excessively powerful, as this will cause increased consumption of electric energy.

The instrument housing has a certain set of filters, the quality and quantity of which have a direct impact on the degree of work efficiency and cost. Cleaners use such filters.





Mechanical filtration

That is, pre-cleaning. This is the simplest type, since it is a fine mesh on which large dust particles, poplar fluff, and animal hair settle. This is the necessary protection of the engine of the device from all kinds of pollution. The filter does not need to be replaced; it only needs to be washed periodically in cold water.

Carbon filtration

Such filters are used to absorb odors, remove tobacco smoke and all kinds of contaminants. They are usually used in combination with others, as in an environment with high humidity they are usually ineffective. The carbon filter is replaced every 4-6 months, otherwise it becomes a source of pollution in the room itself.





Complex devices

- HEPA fine filters trap approximately 99% of the particles entering them. This type is necessary to ensure the most effective cleaning, especially for people suffering from allergies. As the filter becomes dirty, it must be replaced.

- Electrostatic (ionizing) filters - plates of metal, which are supplied with voltage, designed to create an electrostatic field. A humidifier-ionizer works according to a similar principle. Dust particles receive a positive charge, and then settle on positively charged plates.

- Lamps with ultraviolet radiation are used as additional cleaning; with their help, viruses, bacteria and fungi are effectively destroyed.

Traditional moisturizers

Their work is based on the principle of ‚Äúcold‚Äù evaporation, which is natural. Water is poured into the tank of the device, after which it falls on special evaporation elements. The fan drives the air through them through the room, and moisturization occurs in a natural way. These are quite economical devices with a low noise level, as simple as possible to operate. Since this type of evaporation is a self-regulating process, relative humidity reaches a certain value for a specific temperature. At higher temperatures, the evaporation rate increases. You can increase the efficiency of a classic evaporator by installing it next to some kind of heat source.





Steam humidifiers

This type of appliance works on the principle of ‚Äúhot‚Äù evaporation. Using a pair of electrodes, the water in the humidifier is heated to a maximum temperature, after which it passes into a vaporous state. When the water is completely evaporated, the device automatically turns off. For this type of device, the advantage is high performance and the possibility of increasing humidity up to 60% or more. At the same time, a steam humidifier, the instruction to which says about the need to regularly fill it with water, so that work is carried out continuously, is not equipped with a built-in hygrostat and consumes quite a lot of power. Since the output is quite hot steam, you can not use such a device in a children's room. However, such a device is appropriate to use for inhalation and aromatherapy. To do this, put a few drops of fragrant oil into the humidifier nozzle.

Ultrasonic Humidifiers

These are the most effective and popular devices from this category. Then water is supplied to the plate, which vibrates at a high frequency, as a result of which a cloud forms above it. And the fan drives air through it into the room. Using this unit, you can generate wet and cold fog at home. Consider such a humidifier. Is the benefit or harm derived from its use? The built-in gyrostat is designed to control the humidity level and maintain it within certain limits. For him, it is best to use distilled water. This type is characterized by maximum performance, safety, low power consumption and low noise. Most buyers say that such an air humidifier is optimal for them, the price of which significantly exceeds the cost of other types. Usually the simplest models cost 500 rubles or more.





Climatic complexes and "air washing"

This is a combination in a single humidifier housing with a cleaner. Both devices can be operated separately or simultaneously. Among the disadvantages that such a humidifier has: the price is several times higher than the cost of traditional models, large dimensions, as well as the inability to maintain a humidity level of more than 60%.
