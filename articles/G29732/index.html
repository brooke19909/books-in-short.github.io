History, features and main attractions of Mtsensk

Mtsensk is one of the oldest cities in the Oryol region, where ancient bell towers are adjacent to the panel houses of the Soviet era. He is exactly a year older than Moscow! The first written mention of this city dates back to 1146. In the article you will find information about the main attractions of Mtsensk with photos and detailed descriptions.

Mtsensk: exploring the city

Mtsensk has been known since 1146. This is one of the oldest cities in the Oryol region, which managed to visit both a military fortress and a shopping center. Today it is a small city of regional significance with a total population of approximately 40 thousand people.

The modern layout of Mtsensk was formed at the end of the XVII century. The Zusha River divides this city into two parts. On the left bank are the historical quarters of old Mtsensk, and on the right - the administrative center with predominantly Soviet buildings. The main transport artery of the city, Mira Street, unites both parts.





Mtsensk lies in the zone of temperate continental climate. Summer here is quite hot, and winters are cool and snowy. The natural landscapes in the vicinity of the city embody the classic Russian landscape, consisting of lakes, forests, lush meadows and birch groves.

Below you will find descriptions of the sights of Mtsensk, as well as find out what souvenirs you can take home with you from this marvelous town.

Orthodox shrines of Mtsensk

The main attractions of Mtsensk are its temples and monasteries, of which there are at least a dozen. So, in the northwestern part of the city, the ruins of the 16th century Peter and Paul Monastery are preserved. On the high bank of the river there is a five-headed beauty - Vvedensky Church - the oldest monument and the most famous landmark of Mtsensk.





On the corner of Karl Marx and Pionerskaya streets you can see the Exaltation Church. It was built in 1818. It was within the walls of this church that the body of Alexander I, following from Taganrog to the capital, resided. Another beautiful temple of Mtsensk is located on Komsomolskaya Street. This is a massive brick church of St. George the Victorious. In the 1930s, it was closed and until today is not used for worship.

Unfortunately, not all the ancient temples of Mtsensk survived the Soviet period of history. So, in 1930 the most beautiful Nicholas Cathedral was blown up. In its place, on Mount Samorod, today stands the modest chapel of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker, built in 1996.

Walking along Mira street

Central in the city is Peace Street. It starts from Oryol Square on the left bank of the river, crosses Zusha and rests on Turgenev Street. It adjoins the central square of the city - Lenin Square. A large number of architectural and historical sights of Mtsensk are concentrated on Mira Street.

Right after the bridge over the river, Mira Street runs along the main city park of culture and recreation. It was laid in the first half of the twentieth century and is a monument of landscape gardening art. Today you can ride horses or have fun on children's attractions. The park often hosts various musical and theatrical events.

On the street of the world, several ancient merchant houses built at the turn of the XIX-XX centuries have been preserved. The most beautiful among them is the house at number 17 with an elegant brick decor. On the same street is another interesting architectural landmark of Mtsensk. This is a white three-story building of the city administration in the style of constructivism. The building is crowned with a spectacular corner clock tower and a huge bas-relief.
