National Museum of Sweden

It has been under reconstruction for several years. It will be opened again in October 2018. The history of the collection presented at the National Museum of Sweden begins in the 16th century, during the time of Gustav Vas. In the first decades of its existence, the collection was replenished by trophies.

In the forties of the 18th century, the Swedish envoy acquired several French canvases for the collection. After the death of King Gustav III, the collection of paintings was transferred to the National Museum. The pearl of this collection is a painting by the Dutch artist Rembrandt "Conspiracy of Julius Civilis."

Skansen

This is one of the most beautiful places in the capital of Sweden. Skansen is an open-air museum. Located on the island of Djurgarden.

This museum was founded in 1891. 80 years before its opening, a Swedish merchant built a pavilion on the island with a beautiful view of the city. Here he set up a magnificent garden. These places are called ‚Äúskansen‚Äù, which means ‚Äústrengthening‚Äù. The fact is that there was a small fortress nearby.

In 1891, these lands were acquired by the director of the Museum of the Northern Countries. Then the idea came up to create a cultural complex, which had no analogues in Europe. This is the first ethnographic museum in Sweden, which contains architectural monuments. Here you can see a forge, a glassblower workshop, a medieval bakery.

Today, the museum has collected more than a hundred estates and houses of the 18-19 centuries. Here, the atmosphere in which representatives of various social strata lived is preserved. Employees of the museum, dressed in costumes of the past, tell visitors about interesting events from the history of Stockholm. Various holiday events are regularly held here. One of them is Swedish Flag Day.

The construction of the building, in which the Museum of the Northern Countries is located today, continued until 1907. The building looks like a Danish castle Frederiksborg. Royal Palace

This landmark of Stockholm is the official residence of the monarchs. Located on the main promenade of Stadsholmen island.

In the Middle Ages there was a castle on the site of the palace, designed to protect the waterway. With the construction of this building began the history of the Swedish capital. The gloomy castle was replaced by a magnificent residence under King Johan III. But by the end of the XVII century, and it seemed to the mayors obsolete. The building was demolished, a baroque palace was built in its place, which burned down in 1697. The construction of the new residence, which is now included in the list of the main attractions of Stockholm, continued until 1754.

Stockholm City Hall

This is another attraction that is included in most tourist routes in Sweden. The town hall was built in 1923. The author of the project is the architect Ragnar Estberg. The beauty of the building can be appreciated from the promenade, but you can not visit it at any time. Important political negotiations are held here and city government meetings are held. There is also a museum in the town hall.

The height of the tower - the main part of the structure, 106 meters. In the construction, more than two million bricks were used. The museum exhibits sculptures, here you can see rare in beauty works of mosaic art. The main exhibit is a statue of St. Eric, the Swedish king who ruled the country in the 12th century.

From the museum, a staircase leads to the Round Gallery, where sculptures depicting historical figures and biblical characters are installed. The observation deck of the town hall is often called the Copper Tower. There are nine bells here, the largest of them weighs about three tons. Guided tours of the town hall are held from May to September, from 9:00 to 16:00.

German church

The temple is located in the Old Town, which in the Middle Ages was inhabited mainly by Germans. Hence the name. Every Sunday, church services are held in German. It is worth saying a few words about the history of this temple.

In the 15th century there was a building on the site of the church, which was occupied by a German public organization. In the eighties of the XVI century it was rebuilt into a church. A large number of Germans then lived in Stockholm, among them, as a rule, there were traders and artisans. But they did not have their own temple.

In 1558, King Gustav allowed religious meetings to be held in German. In 1571 a separate parish was organized. Pastors from Germany served here. In 1607, the building in which the church is located today was transferred to the Germans. The construction was expanded, supplemented by a bell tower. The opening took place in 1642.

Church of St. Nicholas

This is the oldest temple of Stockholm located in the historical center. The building is an example of neo-Gothic architecture. Located near the Royal Palace, the facade faces the Royal Square. For the first time this church is mentioned in documents of 1279. Since the XVI century, the cathedral became Lutheran, in the XX century it received the status of the cathedral. This is where the coronation of the Swedish kings takes place.

Grena Lund

Stockholm's most famous amusement park is located on the banks of the Djurgarden. This attraction of the Swedish capital appeared about 130 years ago. At first, there were several attractions in the park established by a German merchant in the territory he rented. This place was liked by the residents of Stockholm. There are new rides. The park has become a permanent venue for various celebrations.

Popular objects of Grena Lund are a room of laughter, a tunnel of love, a roller coaster. Most of the attractions are Scandinavian-style buildings. The park is open every day from 11:00 to 23:00. Children under six years old have free admission. The ticket price for adults is 70-80 kroons (depending on the time of year).
