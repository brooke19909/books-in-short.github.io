Entertainment

Apart from the sea and beaches, Hua Hin definitely has something to do. For example, many tourists take a few days off specifically for sightseeing. What are the most interesting?

Train Station

No matter how strange it may sound, but for Thais this place is almost sacred. It was built in 1911, when the king chose this city as his residence and made it a real resort. In 1920, a special waiting room for the monarch's family was equipped in the station building. They now rarely visit here, but tourists really like this place.

Cicada market

If you do not know what to do with yourself on the weekend, the best solution would be to go to Khao Takiab Street. It is here that the main Hua Hin party place is Cicada Market. Theatrical shows, open-air galleries, accompanied by the music of local bards, souvenir shops and masterpieces of Thai, European and Chinese cuisines. And you can buy all this not for money, but for tickets that are purchased at the entrance.

Night market

By name, it is clear that this place opens only at night. More precisely, from 5 pm. However, the assortment is rather mundane: clothes, shoes, a large number of souvenirs. Nearby there are many cafes and food courts where you can try various local dishes. Seafood is cooked right before your eyes.

Wat ampharam

Another attraction of Hua Hin, enjoyed by the Thais only because of its connection with the royal family. If you want to feel the local flavor with might and main, definitely visit this complex from the temple, monastery and columbaria.

Pala-U Waterfall

This nature-created Hua Hin landmark is located in Kaeng Krachan National Park. Pala-U is the highest waterfall in Thailand. Climbing 16 steps, you can see the chic landscapes of Hua Hin. Nearby are the jungle and pristine nature. Not far from here you can meet elephants.

Royal Palace and its history

Perhaps the main attraction of Hua Hin is the palace of Marukhataivan. It was erected in 1923 by order of the monarch of Rama 6. Initially, the castle was located on the beach of Chao Samran. The result did not satisfy the ruler, and the building was demolished. They decided to build a new palace from teak in the Cha-Am district. From the area to Bangkok there is a railway, around a beautiful forest landscape, mangrove trees and a fresh sea breeze.

The king personally designed almost the entire design for his palace. Majestic high ceilings, good ventilation system, niches with water to repel ants. To complete the project, the monarch hired Italian architect Ercole Manfredi.

The ruler used the palace as a summer residence for the royal family until his death in 1925.

Marukhathaivan is also called the Palace of Love and Hope. It consists of three parts with long corridors between them. One leads to the beach, the second to the king‚Äôs private room, while the third leads to the women's section.

Phisan Sacorn - King‚Äôs private quarters: study, bedroom, bathroom, refectory and dressing room. Rama 6 was a poet and wrote most of his poems in the Teak Palace. In the Smundra Biman section, the wife of the king lived. Sewakamart has work rooms and a theater.

After the death of Rama 6, the castle remained empty. Now this is the main attraction of Hua Hin. Having visited her, you can see some household items of the monarch, for example, his bed, sofa, desk, pencils and paper.

Tourists come here daily, except Wednesday, until 5 pm. When planning to visit the palace, do not forget that they are not allowed in short shorts, skirts and T-shirts here. You also need to take off your shoes in reverence for the ancient traditions of Thailand. At the entrance they will give out a bag where you can put your shoes. The tour includes a visit to the palace itself, as well as a walk through the surrounding areas. Shooting is prohibited here. For a more comfortable walk you can rent a bike.
