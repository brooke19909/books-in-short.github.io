The most expensive hotel in Moscow opposite the Kremlin

Baltschug Kempinski is one of the most popular hotels in the capital. It is located near the Kremlin and the view from the windows of the rooms in the evenings is simply mesmerizing. The Tretyakov Gallery is 10 minutes away. Baltschug Kempinski has its own indoor pool, restaurants, spa centers, and gyms.



All rooms are equipped with classic furniture, plasma TVs, air conditioning and spacious bathrooms. For a day of stay in this hotel guests will have to pay from 11 to 170 thousand rubles. Due to its unusually favorable location with regard to the capital's attractions and the highest level of service, this hotel is very popular among foreign tourists and celebrities.

Four Seasons Hotel Moscow

This hotel is 75 meters from Red Square. Here a day of stay will cost tourists from 20 to 65 thousand rubles. The rooms have a classic design. They have furniture made from natural materials.

Numerous services are presented in one of the most expensive hotels in Moscow on Red Square (as guests call the hotel because of its location within walking distance of the famous Russian Square):

  • Indoor and outdoor pool.
  • Gym.
  • SPA complex.
  • Turkish sauna.
  • Massage room.

There is also a business center with all the necessary equipment. Near it there is a spacious conference room and a restaurant, where after negotiations you can organize a banquet.

Hotel "Metropol"

This institution is located in the capital near the Bolshoi Theater. Over the years, the hotel has been hosting guests from different parts of the country and abroad. The rooms are made in modern style. This trend is supported in the general atmosphere of the institution.



The staff here speaks several languages. Service is established at the highest level. The rooms were designed by experienced professionals, so all rooms are cozy and comfortable.

Guests have the opportunity to visit the fitness room, pool, spa treatments, massage services. The price includes a rich breakfast buffet with European dishes.

Metropol can also be called the most expensive hotel in Moscow. A room per day costs from 10,000 to 110,000 rubles.

"Ararat Park Hyatt"

One of the original hotels is located near the Central Department Store and the Children's World. In 2012, all its rooms were restored with the help of the famous designer Tony Chi. They have a spacious terrace with views of the Kremlin and the State Duma. Here guests often like to dine. Suites have living rooms and offices. Each room has its own bathtub and shower.

The high cost of living (per day from 20,000 to 110,000 rubles) is determined by excellent service and maintenance. The hotel has a swimming pool, gym, Turkish bath, massage rooms. The restaurants serve dishes of European, Japanese, Armenian cuisine.
