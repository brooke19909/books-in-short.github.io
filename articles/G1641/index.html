History of Creation and Management





Amusement park ... Spain ... Port Aventura ... Did architects and designers know how popular their brainchild would be after a few years? Most likely, they did not even guess.

Let's delve into the story. As it became known, it was designed and built by two British companies - Anheuser-Busch and Tussauds Group.

In 1995, the American corporation Universal Studios took part in the creation of the park, which in 1997 bought back the controlling part of the park. After that, the name changed a little, and it became known as Universal's PortAventura.

Later, in 2000, the Costa Caribe water park and two hotels were built here, and the park again changed its name to Universal Mediterranea.

Universal Studios NBC Universal began distributing shares of the park in 2004, and after their full sale in 2005, a controlling stake was received by La Caixa, a well-known company at that time, which returned the park to its more familiar name.
