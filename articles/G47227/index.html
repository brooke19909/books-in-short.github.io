Jasmine Hotel, Prague: overview, description and reviews of tourists

The Czech Republic is a very popular European country for tourists. Travelers love the local architecture and original cuisine. Choosing a place to stay in Prague, many people want to save, because they will spend most of the time in the city, and not in the room. Such tourists often choose the hotel "Jasmine" (Prague), which offers very inexpensive apartments.

Brief information about the hotel

For tourists, the hotel opened its doors in the distant 1990. It consists of several small but cozy buildings, the height of which is only three floors. The appearance of the buildings reminds of provincial hotels built in small towns. The roof of the hotel is tiled red. The complex offers 75 rooms in which there is everything that tourists need for a comfortable stay. Following European standards of service, the hotel staff begins to check-in at exactly 14:00 local time. Leave Hotel Jasmin 3 * (Czech Republic, Prague) before 10:00.





The hotel is located in a quiet area, so it is well suited for tourists with young children. Dogs are allowed. But vacationers must notify the hotel of their arrival in advance. Pets are also available at extra charge. When checking into a room, you will definitely need a photo document that confirms your identity, as well as a bank card. Visa and MasterCard are accepted for payment of accommodation and additional services.

Hotel location: distance to the center of Prague and the nearest attractions

Guests of EA Hotel Jasmin 3 * (Prague) can enjoy a good sleep, because the hotel is located in a quiet area. It is located away from large night clubs where noisy discos are held. The historic city is 7 km away. The center can be reached by metro in 15 minutes. The hotel is built on a busy street near the bus stop. The nearest metro station is just 800 meters away. Ruzyne Airport is 13 km away. Tourists coming to the Czech Republic can call a taxi, arrange a transfer or rent a car.

Near the hotel is a large O2 arena, which often hosts celebrity concerts and sporting events. You can get to it by bus. You need to drive only 2 stops. There is also a pizzeria nearby. Liben , the oldest district of Prague, is 2 km from the hotel. Charles Bridge, Wenceslas and Old Town Squares, the Old Town Hall are 5 km away. And the old Prague Castle is 6 km away.

All rooms have their own bathroom with shower, toilet and washbasin. The hotel overlooks a busy street and the garden that surrounds the building. Maids clean the rooms every day. Guest reviews are negative

Like any other place to stay, the Jasmine Hotel (Prague) receives bad reviews. Comments tourists describe the following disadvantages of the hotel:

  • The furniture in the rooms is very old. The beds creak terribly, and the bedding smells damp.
  • Internet almost does not work in the rooms. In the evenings, it is generally impossible to connect to it.
  • You need to get to the metro by bus. The hotel has a very uncomfortable location for those who do not have their own car.
  • There are no elevators in the hotel, so some guests had to carry their luggage to the third floor on their own.
  • When staying in a double room, only one key is issued.
