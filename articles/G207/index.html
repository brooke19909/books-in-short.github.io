Phillips school anxiety level diagnosis technique: method goal and instruction

The technique of diagnosing the level of school anxiety of Phillips allows you to timely identify the condition of the child. Having information about the impending danger, the need to take certain measures, the psychologist begins to work with the student. We will identify the main reasons for the appearance of such a condition in children, the methodology of the study, and the specifics of the condition.





Anxiety characteristic

A technique for diagnosing the level of school anxiety of Phillips is necessary for the timely detection of the condition of the child. Depending on the natural and acquired qualities, it is characterized by a vague sense of danger or aggressiveness. The following parameters can be considered as additional symptoms of anxiety: sweating, nausea, headache, palpitations, chest tightness. The level of anxiety among students differs quite significantly. The child is not able to stay in one place, very worried. Fear is a reaction to external danger.





Types of Anxiety

The technique of diagnosing the level of school anxiety of Phillips allows you to identify its different levels:

  • neuroendocrine form of hormonal changes;
  • motor-visceral level (twitching of hands, knees);
  • conscious understanding (the child feels only bodily discomfort).

Anxiety components

The main component of this feeling is redness, palpitations, shortness of breath, sweating.

The child realizes that he is nervous or afraid of a certain moment (situation). The methodology for diagnosing the level of school anxiety of Phillips also determines the feeling of shame, the fear that someone will see the actions of the child. Due to increased anxiety, the student appears distracted, there are problems in learning, and the concentration of attention decreases.





Types of Anxiety

At a low level of intensity, anxiety is vital for a person. This feeling helps to take certain actions to mitigate or prevent danger. For example, high school students put a lot of effort in order to prepare for the exam. Normal anxiety stimulates the growth and change of the child, helps him move from one age to another.

Excessive anxiety is an ambiguous response to stresses and problems that arise in school life. If anxiety is normal, the child's internal mechanisms are turned on, which are aimed at eliminating the source of danger. For example, the fear of passing exams stimulates active preparation for them. Pathological anxiety causes maladaptive and unconstructive actions. The child does not cope with threats, he is not confident in himself, his strengths, avoids social communication. If you do not provide such a student with timely psychological help, he may develop various phobias. In children, agoraphobia is formed, implying a fear of loneliness.

The Phillips technique helps diagnose the level of school anxiety). Processing the results allows the psychologist to identify the problem, select ways to reduce fears. If it is not possible to get rid of fears by external methods (Erickson hypnosis, cognitive psychotherapy), the doctor prescribes certain drugs to the child.

Anxiety disorder is considered a diagnosis of neurotic disorder when a child without discomfort feels discomfort and nervousness. This condition is revealed by the method of diagnosing the level of school anxiety of Phillips. The objectives of the study are to assess the psychological comfort of children.





Portrait of an anxious child

A schoolboy with fear refers to all the events taking place around him. He is timid, avoids communication with adults, peers. The child feels completely helpless, he is afraid to engage in various activities. He makes high demands on himself, is self-critical, has a low level of self-esteem. The schoolboy is convinced that he is stupid, clumsy, ugly. Children with increased anxiety need the approval of adults, they need constant praise and encouragement. With the manifestation of anxiety, weakness occurs in the legs, dry mouth, palpitations speed up. Anxious children have somatic problems such as dizziness, shortness of breath, and abdominal pain.
