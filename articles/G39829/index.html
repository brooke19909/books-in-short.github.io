Vichy city, France: photos, attractions

The city of Vichy is an unusually beautiful resort town, which has long been a resting place for the monarchs of France. For the most notable people, magnificent museums and theaters were built here, picturesque green park areas were laid out. In addition, Vichy is famous for its unique thermal mineral waters.

General information about the city

A city in France Vichy is known all over the world thanks to the famous cosmetics brand of the same name, which is produced by the L'Oreal Corporation. This is the first association that occurs in the head when mentioning the name of a city.

A deeper look at the history of the development of the city opens up an interesting topic of the ups and downs of a small town located at a distance from the more popular and popular tourist routes in France.

Location

The beautiful and cozy city of France Vichy is located in the center of the country. Through it carries a fairly large river Allier.

The resort town has a fairly convenient location, as it is easily accessible from Paris. The train takes only 3.5 hours. An airfield for private jets has also been built here, which creates additional conveniences for vacationers.

A brief history of the popularity of the resort

Located in the very center of the state, the resort town has been known for several centuries. The extraordinary miraculous properties of local water were appreciated by the ancient Romans, who had already settled in this paradise. Today, the thermal water of the town of Vichy (France) is one of the most valuable sources in the world.

The resort became especially popular during the reign of the French king Henry IV, who preferred to rest here, and not in other medical resorts in France. Fell in love with the city and Napoleon. Thanks to him, some luxury and sophistication appeared in these places. The former village with mineral springs under Napoleon was transformed into a respectable place for recreation for all the nobility of Europe.

Attractions Vichy (France)

In the city itself and its environs there are many attractions that you should definitely see.

One unique park is located an hour and a half drive from Vichy. On its territory there are several volcanoes, however, sleeping. The park, opened in 2003, annually gathers many tourists who dream of visiting inside the crater. A tour of the area takes time all day.

And at the San Necter cheese factory, located near Vichy, tourists are embraced by a real delight. It is interesting to visit the cellars in the rock (in grottoes and caves), with huge cheese heads stored in them. The end of an eight-hour tour is a cheese tasting.

The surroundings of the city of France Vichy are adorned with wonderful architectural structures. Here you can see two medieval castles (Olterib and Busse) with parks and collections of ancient objects. Previously, they were the residence of the monarchs, but now they are open for excursion groups. In addition, in Olteribe there is a unique collection of old carriages.

140 kilometers from the resort town is the fortress city of Sharu. This is a fabulously beautiful French village, which is the habitat of artisans living in it according to the technical capabilities and laws of the XIII century. There are museums, an old factory (produces oil and mustard), a soap factory and wine cellars in San Pursan.

In the city of Vichy itself there are museums and theaters, including the opera. You can test your strength in aristocratic sports (horse racing, golf, tennis).

In conclusion about the Vichy regime in France

Vichy regime, or Vichy France - the period of the occupation of the country by Nazi Germany, which arose in southern France after the fall of Paris in 1940. This regime lasted from July 1940 until the complete liberation of France by the Allies. Its name comes from the name of the city we described, where in the summer of that year the National Assembly decided to transfer power to Marshal A.F. Peten.
