The best places to stay in Finland in winter: recreation centers, reviews of tourists

Finland is Russia's northern neighbor. This state is very popular among tourists for many reasons. The country is beautiful in nature. About which resorts are considered the most visited, read the article.

Why is vacation in Finland popular in winter?

There are many reasons why many tourists choose a vacation in this state. So here they are:

  • Beautiful landscape. The relief of Finland is characterized by the presence of hills and mountains covered with snow.
  • Skiing. The landscape allows you to take part in various competitions in snowboarding and other winter sports. So, everyone can ski or cheesecake. If you are brave enough, you can take part in a snow safari with a team of sleds, snowmobiles or ATVs. Winter fishing is another entertainment.




  • Long snow season. Holidays in Finland in the winter are chosen by many tourists, since the winters are quite warm.
  • Developed infrastructure. In this country there are a huge number of museums and amusement parks. Everyone can visit the spa. Holidays in Finland in winter are very popular for another reason. Local hotels and tourist centers are considered very good. The service here is of high quality.
  • The ability to travel around the country. Everyone can visit such Finnish cities as Helsinki and Tampere, and this is only a small part of the local tourist "capitals". Well, of course, do not forget about Lapland - a resort where you will get the New Year's mood.

Weather

Finland is located in northern Europe, a third of the state is located beyond the Arctic Circle. The day lasts several hours, from 10.00 a.m. to 15.00 p.m. The influence of the Gulf Stream affects temperature, however, it cannot be said that Finnish winters are cold. They are moderate. On average, the temperature of the coldest month is from -14 to -12 degrees Celsius in the northernmost points of the country.

The air in Finland is dry, so the cold is easily tolerated. The formation of snow cover begins in December, lasts until April in the southern regions of the state. In the northern part, snow lies until May. Since holidays in Finland in winter are often accompanied by westerly winds, when choosing outerwear, you should give preference to windproof models.

How to get there

There are several ways to get to Finland.

  • By plane. To get to this state, you should make a flight on the route "St. Petersburg - Helsinki" or "Moscow - Helsinki". An average ticket costs 3,600 rubles. If you book in advance, about a month before departure, their cost will be 2700 rubles.
  • By train or ferry. For example, from Moscow the express train "Leo Tolstoy".
  • By bus. For just 500 rubles you can get from St. Petersburg to Helsinki.

A very developed transport system operates inside the country, therefore, problems with movement around the state usually do not arise. Most often, bus routes are preferred, although the main cities are connected by rail.
