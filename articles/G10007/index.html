Description and specifications

The jubilee 10 rubles "Chechen Republic" were issued by the St. Petersburg Mint on October 1, 2010. The coin was dedicated, as its name implies, to the Russian Republic of Chechnya. The coin has a diameter of twenty seven millimeters. It is composed of two metal discs made of brass and cupronickel. For this reason, it and similar coins are called bimetallic. The circulation of 10 rubles "Chechen Republic", as already mentioned above, is one hundred thousand pieces.

The designation of the face value of the coin, 10 rubles, is located on its front side (obverse). At the same time, if you reject the coin to the side, then the hidden number of ten and the inscription "rub" will appear on the number zero.

The back or the wound side (reverse) has an image of the coat of arms of the Republic of Chechnya. The symbol of Unity and Eternity is placed in its inner part.
