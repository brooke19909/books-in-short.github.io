Shkaret Park, Mexico, Cancun: description, history and interesting facts

Tourists who come to Cancun must visit the ancient Mayan pyramids, go in for deep sea fishing, shopping, take anti-aging and wellness treatments in SPA centers, etc.

History

Translated from the ancient Mayan language, the word "rakret" means "small bay." Today Xcaret is the most popular unique national park not only in Cancun, but throughout Mexico - on the Caribbean coast of this country. When in 1984, a local architect named Miguel Quintana Pali, acquired a plot of land in the Riviera Maya, his plans were only to build his own house. However, in the process of preparing the site, he unexpectedly stumbled upon the amazing cenote craters and underground rivers.

These were the failures resulting from the collapse of the arches of the limestone caves located here.

Pali, after consulting with specialists, decided to open a large park with friends. Thus, on the territory of one of the ceremonial centers of the Mayan tribe, Xcaret arose. Today, there is an eco-archaeological park with numerous monuments of the ancient culture of this amazing civilization. Tropical nature, animals that live almost on the wild, beautiful beaches and vibrant evening performances, set on the theme of the history of Mexico, in which about two hundred people take part - all this is Shkaret. There are many predators. The most dangerous animals, such as cougars, jaguars, etc., live in enclosed enclosures behind a deep moat and a low stone fence.

Description

So what is this amazing ecological park? It is almost impossible to answer this question in one word. This is the botanical garden, in which there is a farm for growing orchids, as well as a huge variety of beautiful trees, shrubs and exotic flowers, and a zoo where you can see an incredible number of unique butterflies and reptiles, predators, birds and other representatives of the animal world.

Shkaret is a huge theater, in different corners of which you can participate in various ceremonies, get acquainted with the dances of the Indians, and in the evenings watch vivid performances in the tent. But first of all, tourists come to an amusement park, where there is a long (more than half a kilometer) underground river, along which travelers travel exclusively in life jackets. Here visitors are given the opportunity to swim in the aquarium with coral fish or in a large pond with dolphins. Fans of extreme sports will enjoy rafting in the park jungle or climbing a rotating platform with a panoramic view.

In addition, you can visit the tortoise farm and the butterfly park, ride on the sand of the beach or go snorkeling, which you can rent. That's why tourists come all day to Shkaret Park (Mexico). The reviews of our compatriots indicate that the vast majority will forever remember visiting this unique place.
