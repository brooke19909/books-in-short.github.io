Museon Park: how to get there and what to see?

In the capital of our country there are many interesting places and all kinds of attractions. Often, even the native Muscovites do not know all the museums and parks of their native city. And this is a great reason to walk more on weekends and discover new places. One of the interesting ideas for organizing weekend activities in Moscow is a trip to the Museum Museum. How to get to this attraction?





Museon - what is it?

This place is officially called an art park. Among the people, he has one more name, the national name is the cemetery of sculptures. Indeed, the history of the creation of the park begins in the 90s of the last century. At that time, it was decided to get rid of some of the monuments glorifying Soviet power, leaders and figures of a bygone era. In 1992, a landscaped green area was allocated for the transportation of statues, located not far from the Tretyakov Gallery and the Central House of Artists. The Museon (art park) is located in a relatively small area - only 24 hectares. Today there are 700 sculptures and compositions. What is striking, the monuments of Soviet culture here peacefully coexist with the works of modern authors.





Museon Park: how to get there by public transport

A walk among the original monuments is not difficult to combine with excursions to the historical center of Moscow. The nearest metro stations to the park are Kropotkinskaya and Polyanka. The distance from them to the attractions of interest to us is about the same. But if you want to see as many interesting things as possible, it makes sense to go to Kropotkinskaya. So, we arrive at the necessary station, but where is the Museum Museum, how to get to this place? Tune in for a short walk. From the subway you need to climb into the city using the exit to the Cathedral of Christ the Savior. Next you need to get to Volkhonka street. If you find the right way, the Cathedral of Christ the Savior and the square will be on the right. Next, you need to go through the territory of this cult religious building and then move along the pedestrian bridge across the Moscow River (Patriarchal Bridge).

Next, you need to focus on the monument to Peter I, from afar you will see the abundance of greenery at its foot, this is the Museum Museum. Do not be afraid not to find the right place. Near the monument to the great sovereign, beautiful flowerbeds and lawns begin, here you can see the cafe "Shardam". As soon as you read this sign, you can be sure that you are there! For those who are afraid of getting lost, we recall that the Museum Museum has the following address: Moscow, Krymsky Val Street , ow. 2.

What else to do in the park?

"Museon" is not only a huge exhibition of sculptures, but also a comfortable relaxation area. The landscape design of the park deserves special attention - there are fountains, alpine slides and very beautiful flower beds. There is also an open-air cinema and photo exhibition in the recreation area. From time to time, music concerts and other cultural events are held here. In the warmer months, yoga classes and dance classes are held in the recreation area. Be sure to come to the Museon Park, you already know how to get there. No one will be bored here, and everyone will find something to their liking.
