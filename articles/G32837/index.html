Evolution

Culture and society developed from simple to complex, from wildness through barbarism to civilization. A concept such as progress has become an integral part of evolution. What is the evolutionary nature of the development of society? In organic development, which includes a number of concomitant changes in a certain direction. Evolution is a sequence of interconnected phenomena aimed at transformation. This is a process of continuous transition from a lower level to a higher and more complex one as a person gradually increases his control over the environment.

Revolution

The revolutionary theory of development is manifested in a sudden, radical or complete change, a basic reorientation and reorganization. This is a fundamental change in a political organization or government, a renunciation of the constitution, or an unplanned overthrow of one ruler by another. A revolution may involve both violent and non-violent acts.

These are profound qualitative changes in the development of various phenomena of nature, society or knowledge. When answering the question of what distinguishes evolutionary and revolutionary development, it is important to note that this is a sharp interruption of gradual development, with a radical breakdown of everything old. As history shows, such leaps often lead to a slowdown in development.

What is the evolutionary nature of the development of society?

From the point of view of anthropologists and other scholars, culture as a whole develops in a uniform and progressive manner. Evolutionists, relying on Darwin's theory of evolution and natural selection, sought to track the evolution of culture over time. It was believed that most societies go through a certain series of stages of development. What is the nature of evolutionary development expressed in? One of the schemes proposed by the philosopher Montesquieu in the 18th century consisted of three stages: hunting (wildness), shepherding (barbarism) and civilization. The division into evolutionary stages, from primitive to civilized, is fundamental to the new ideas of nineteenth-century social evolutionists.

Evolution theory

Based on Darwin's theory and cultural, historical, and archaeological evidence, social evolutionary theorists created competing patterns of overall social and cultural progress, and they also explained the origins of various institutions. This includes religion, marriage, and family. Summing up all of the above, we can say that the evolutionary development of society is a change through the gradual adjustment of the existing system, in contrast to revolutionary measures, with the help of which a complete or partial replacement of the existing order takes place.
