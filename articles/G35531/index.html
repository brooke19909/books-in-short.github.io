Mezhdunarodnaya metro station, St. Petersburg - one of the new stations

At the end of December 2012, residents of the St. Petersburg region of Kupchino received the long-awaited gift - a new metro station. "International" in St. Petersburg was the last on the 5th Frunze-Primorsky line (purple).

How was the station built

The construction of the station began in the late 80s. Then funding stopped, for a long time the fenced area stood idle, surrounded by a spontaneous market. The active construction of the Mezhdunarodnaya metro station in St. Petersburg began in 2011 and ended a year later.

At first it was planned to make a ground-type station, but the state of the soil did not allow to implement such a project.

Metro "International", St. Petersburg

"International" is the 67th metro station in St. Petersburg. Project architects - N. Vinogradova, Y. Romanova and N. Romashkin-Timanov.

The plans of the St. Petersburg authorities to extend further the Frunze-Primorsky line and build 3 more stations beyond the "International". It is assumed that the deep-seated station will become an interchange and connect the 5th line and Koltsevaya.

Lobby design

According to St. Petersburg tradition, the lobby of the Mezhdunarodnaya metro station, St. Petersburg, the Kupchino district, is small. Ticket vending machines are installed in the lobby, there is an information desk.

A pleasant surprise is the presence of a stand that displays information about the nearby streets - Bucharest and Bela Kun. The history of the Romanov village is briefly described, on the site of which the Kupchino district arose, as well as the Farforovsky overpass and the neighboring village named after Shaumyan.

The mosaic decorates the walls of the lobby, which was performed by A. Bystrov. This is a well-known artist in St. Petersburg, the head of one of the workshops of the Institute. Repin.

The laconic palette of blue and white colors fits well with the ensemble of the St. Petersburg metro.

4 escalators descend to a depth of 65 m, the travel time is about 3 minutes.

Interior decoration

The "international" metro station in St. Petersburg is both strict and luxurious.

The underground part is bright and airy. The floor is gray and the walls are white marble. Two rows of columns separating the central hall from the landing platforms are lined with antique gold-colored brass sheets. Each column is surrounded by three rows of handrails made in the same style.

The decorative decoration of the underground station is a panel from the Atlas mosaic, located at the southern end of the central aisle. Near the panel there is a place for creating a transition to the Ring Line. Already installed 2 stairs with a handrail of golden color, as in the hall, but so far the stairs end in a dead end.

The hall is well lit with fluorescent light, giving the impression of a fine day. Lamps, according to the tradition of the St. Petersburg metro, are located behind the cornices.

The underground metro station is equipped with information stands, comfortable oval benches are located on the platforms.
