Capital of spain

Spain is one of the most beautiful centers of Europe, the main cultural and entertainment centers, which annually attracts millions of tourists from all over the globe. Some visit the country to stroll through the winding streets of the city, enjoy the unique architecture and imbue the masterpieces of the great masters of art. Others prefer to relax on the sunny sandy beaches with a glass of a pleasant cocktail, enjoy the festive atmosphere in local tapas bars. Whatever direction tourists choose: sightseeing or relaxing on the beach, Spain will combine diverse preferences!



Cities in Spain differ from each other based on their economic affiliation. For example, Barcelona is the largest industrial center; Alicante - a place of fishing and export; the city of Salou is popular with sea resorts and is one of the tourist centers of Catalonia; Madrid, Valencia, Lloret de Mar, Granada attract tourists and travelers with their magnificent beautiful resorts and beaches. Thus, everyone who wants to visit this amazing country will be able to choose for themselves that city and that vacation that will leave a lot of pleasant memories and unforgettable impressions.

The capital of Spain is the city of Madrid. This amazing place is called the very heart of the state. A majestic and incredibly beautiful city with stunning castles and palaces of various styles and eras, with wonderful fountains and fascinating lush green parks. Thousands and even millions of tourists visit this amazing place to see the ingenious masterpieces of art by great masters such as Titian, Goya, Velazquez, Rembrandt. The capital of Spain, Madrid, will leave unbelievable impressions of its historical and cultural heritage, incendiary and colorful holidays in the hearts of its guests. All of them are filled with joy and optimism.



The capital of Spain is a rather diverse city with wonderful and original architectural monuments. It should be noted that some of the hotels and buildings of Madrid in all its splendor are attractions. These include the world famous and luxurious Prado Museum. Initially, it was called the Royal Museum of Sculpture and Painting, which was located in a building constructed in the eighteenth century, and was the cultural heritage of all of Spain. The architect of this structure was Juan Villanueva.

At the moment, the museum contains the greatest collection of masterpieces of the most famous masters: Rafael, Botticelli, Velazquez, Rubens, Goya, Titian, Durer, Rembrandt, Ribalt, Zurbaran and other eminent and brilliant masters.
