LCD TVs: an overview of the best models, advantages and disadvantages

Technology development is massive. In recent years, it has gained momentum, and it is no longer easy for everyone to understand the new products that appear on the market. Everyone has long known LCD TVs, but few can explain the name of the device. In fact, the technology appeared long ago, not to mention when liquid crystals became known.

Abbreviation

So, as you already understood, today we will consider televisions with a liquid crystal screen. If you ever had to choose a similar technique or monitor for yourself, then you have often come across incomprehensible abbreviations such as LCD or LCD. What does it mean? And if you can easily guess that the LCD is a liquid crystal, then not everyone knows what LCD is.



The last abbreviation is also related to our topic. In English it stands for liquid crystal display, which in translation again brings us back to the liquid crystal display. That is, LCD and LCD are one and the same. Therefore, when choosing a TV, do not look for the difference between these concepts.

What is it?

This kind of screens first appeared in simple electronics. It was used in the manufacture of thermometers with a screen, players, watches, telephones, etc. This type acquired a monochrome display, and received multicolor thanks to the RGB triad.

Now this display is used in a variety of devices. These are computer monitors, laptops, televisions, telephones, cameras, e-books and much more.

Over time, a display with a matrix appeared. Thus, a tool for controlling thin-film transistors was added to the screen. And to the abbreviation LCD began to add TFT and other types of matrixes.

Development

The LCD TV did not appear immediately. Back in 1888, liquid crystals were found. Already after 40 years, a technology was created that began to be used in modern displays. The first attempts to create such a screen were made in the 70s of the last century. RCA was able to produce a monochrome product based on this material.

This gave an impetus to the active use of new items in various electronics. A color version of the LCD appeared in 1987. Sharp, now known for its TV models, has released a three-inch color screen.

The development of screens for laptops has also contributed to the development of LCD displays. After applying the new technology, flaws were noticeable. Initially used black and white matrices. When they decided to try the color version, a lot of problems got out. The static picture looked tolerable, but the slightest movement blurred the image.



So active matrices appeared that could correctly display moving objects on the screen. Soon, the mass production of monitors began. Already at the beginning of the 21st century, televisions began to appear, but small sizes: up to 15 inches.

How it works

Explain how the LCD screen works is not easy. Especially people who are not technically savvy. In order not to go into details that will completely confuse everyone, let's start with the elements.

Obviously, the display consists of several parts. They are conditionally represented by layers. There is an LCD matrix. Previously, it was a flat bag with glass plates. Between the layers were liquid crystals. At the beginning of the 21st century, all internal elements were replaced by more flexible ones made using polymers.

The display has a light source that illuminates the picture. All this is enclosed in a housing, and the electricity comes from the wires.

Naturally, this is not the end of the screen. The LCD matrix can also be represented in parts. Its pixel has transparent electrodes between which there is a layer of molecules. All this works with a pair of polarizing filters.

If the whole structure is at rest, that is, without applying voltage, the molecules are in an amorphous state. If electricity comes in, they line up toward the electric field, distorting their original structure. A thin-film transistor is responsible for the color in the screen. It works on the RGB color scheme. Each pixel is born out of three color cells. The color difference is affected by the magnitude of the electric charge.

The LCD panel weighs a little and takes up less space on the desktop. New products have lost focusing issues. Thanks to the use of technology, flaws were also lost with clarity at high resolutions. Almost all LCD monitors have become digital. Therefore, the graphics card does not need to work with digital-to-analog conversions. But even in this case, manufacturers have learned to get out of situations with unwanted artifacts if the monitor is connected to VGA.

Care

Any technique requires care. Therefore, you need to watch TVs. In addition, the situations are different. On the Internet, people often ask how to wipe the LCD screen of the TV. Someone easily copes with a wet rag, and then complains about problems with the device. To do this, it is better to read additional information.

There are several options for how to effectively clean the model of dirt and dust. The easiest is to purchase special napkins. They help eliminate static electricity, do not cause damage and are free from abrasive substances.

You can use simple dry wipes or microfiber wipes. The latter copes well with dust, stains and stains. You can moisten them with water.

The stores may offer a special spray. Such a liquid does not have alcohol or allergens. In addition, according to the manufacturer, it covers the equipment with a special protective layer.
