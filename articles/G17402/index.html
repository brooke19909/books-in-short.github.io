The village of Lermontovo, Krasnodar Territory - all the possibilities for a summer vacation in one place!

On the Black Sea coast of Russia there are many places of amazing beauty. One of them is the village of Lermontovo, Krasnodar Territory. This is a fairly quiet and calm village, which is distinguished by a well-developed infrastructure. There are all conditions for relaxation, capable of leaving exceptionally pleasant memories.





Resort Information

According to some sources, the village of Lermontovo in the Krasnodar Territory was already a well-known place for recreation and treatment in the late nineteenth century. However, he appeared on the map of our state only in 1958, at that time more than 500 people lived here. However, already in 1999 the population of the village amounted to 1143 people. The village is located at the mouth of the Shapsuho River, on the coast of the Tenginsky Bay. The village of Lermontovo of the Krasnodar Territory is surrounded by virgin forests, in which rare plants and animals are found. A favorable environmental situation in the area continues today, despite the development of tourism.

Sights of the village

Going on vacation, everyone wants to see something unusual, take beautiful photos as a keepsake and get a lot of new impressions. The village of Lermontovo (Krasnodar Territory) offers attractions for tourists to explore mainly of the natural type. In its picturesque surroundings you can see the turbulent mountain rivers and waterfalls. There are few historical architectural monuments in the village itself. However, local travel agencies offer day trips to neighboring cities. Due to the favorable geographical location of Lermontovo, it is not difficult to get from here to Tuapse, Dzhubgu and other neighboring settlements on your own.





One of the most interesting tourist attractions in the area is the Black Sea water park. It is because of this complex of water attractions that many tourists choose the village of Lermontovo (Krasnodar Territory) as a vacation spot.
