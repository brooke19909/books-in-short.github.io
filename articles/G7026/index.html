The best amusement parks in Germany: rating, review with photo

The question of where the whole family can relax in Germany is asked by some Russians. The answer is simple: choose an amusement park that can amaze not only with stunning attractions, but also impress with its beauty and unique design. The best amusement parks in Germany keep their doors open all year round. There are many similar institutions in the country, which are referred to as world leaders in this market. In children's amusement parks in Germany, you can spend days full of interesting events and vivid emotions.

The rating is compiled according to the popularity of the parks, from the most famous to the least popular.

1. Hansa Park

This amusement park in Germany is located in Sierksdorf, where a resort on the Baltic Sea is open. It got its name thanks to L√ºbeck, which is very close by. It is the capital of the Hanseatic League. The territory of this complex is divided into eleven branches, each has its own theme. Hanza itself was a community of European urban settlements of the Middle Ages. On 46 hectares of the coastal zone there are more than a hundred attractions for visitors of all ages. The local roller coaster rails are 1.2 km long. Their speed can reach 127 km / h.

On the territory of the complex there are also buildings in the spirit of the famous sights of the cities of the Hanseatic League, a rope park, a room of mirrors and open-air venues, where spectacular shows are held, children's master classes are organized. Younger visitors can compete, take a dip in the huge pool with balls, or follow in the footsteps of James Cook and make their way through the thick Tahiti jungle.

This amusement park in Germany is open from April 1 to November 1 from 9 to 18 hours. Standard prices start at 28 and end at 35 euros. If the visitor is not yet four years old, there is no need to pay for his entrance to the territory of the entertainment complex. The only thing needed is a confirmation of his age by any documentation.

2. HeidePark

The park is located in Soltau. This is one of the most famous and popular amusement parks in Germany. It is divided into 4 thematic zones. More than five dozen different sights are constantly functioning in it, and entertainment is provided for the youngest customers. These, of course, include the Driving School, in which young adherents of four wheels learn to be car drivers.

In the area associated with the Wild West, you can make rafting in a tree trunk, as well as take a break from swimming in treadmills. A couple of years ago, another country appeared at the disposal of the guests, created based on the cartoon "How to Train Your Dragon." Numerous trampolines, slides, carousels and Viking boats are open in it.

On the territory of the institution there are hotels; here you can relax in the spa or in tents. Near the hotels there is an entertainment center. The complex operates from April to the end of October, on weekends and during the period July-August, it operates from 10 to 18. For entrance fees are paid in the range of 40-50 euros. Again, the older the person, the more he needs to pay. Children under 3 years old are skipped without payment.

3. "Fantasy Land"

About 20 km from Bonn and Cologne is one of the largest amusement parks in Germany, Fantasy Land. It has 7 thematic zones, that is, various places and magical lands. There is Mexico, China, Africa, Berlin, Fantasy and so on. The park is famous for the fastest and longest family slide in the world called Raik. For little visitors, special platforms have been prepared here. Different types of events are recommended for older children, from quiet carousels to huge cars chilling.

As in almost all establishments of this type, for safety reasons, restrictions on minimum growth apply to each of the attractions.

It is noteworthy that this entertainment complex operates in the winter. From April to July and from September it works from 9:00 to 18:00. In July and August it is available to visit until 20:00. From November to January it can also be visited until this time. Persons under four years of age are included in it without cash fees. In different seasons, entry to it will cost differently, but prices range from 30-50 euros.

4. Belantis Park

This park is located in Saxony, a couple of tens of kilometers from Leipzig. This is the largest such facility in the east of the country. It has 8 zones, decorated in accordance with different themes, where visitors find more than six dozen colorful objects and attractions, including boating, attractions in dark catacombs and roller coasters. This is a real celebration for thrill-seekers. Children, of course, will not be bored here either.

Zones

Waiting for visitors are fabulous open-air carousels, jogging paths, shallow pools. Here unique slides for children of any age are realized. It is also complemented by carousels, incredibly colorful shows for young guests. Among the attractions for people of any age, there are also trips on the Cobra train in the Valley of the Pharaohs, along a forest trail or walks on the WildWestExpress train in the Wild West. An amazing adventure will also be participation in archaeological excavations in the shadow of the Egyptian pyramid.

Entrance to this amusement park in Germany will cost around 35 euros. It will be most profitable to buy it at the same time for 3 family members: then it will cost at a discount. Persons under four years of age are admitted without tickets. You only need to document their age. In the period March-June and September-October it can be visited from 10-17, and at the height of the summer season - from 10 to 18.

5. "Europe"

The amusement park "Europe Park" is very famous in Germany. He is the largest in the country. There is a place in the town of Rust in the south-west of the country, right on the banks of the Rhine, along which the German-French border passes. Its area totals more than 90 hectares, and Europa-Park itself consists of as many as 18 thematic sections, in which dozens of amazing attractions work. Each of the branches is dedicated to a separate country or region of Europe. Park service provides additional amenities for families with children. The complex has a special area for parents with children. When visiting this amusement park in Germany for children, you should use the proposed BabySwitch option: while one parent stays with the children, the second one can enjoy the rides and walk around the complex without standing in lines. Thus, this complex is most convenient for family people.

Prices

Tickets to this amusement park in Germany are sold for no more than 50 euros. The final cost depends on age: the younger the client, the less the entrance fee. Persons under 4 years of age are admitted here without payment (the age of the child, as elsewhere in such institutions, must be documented). It is noteworthy that this entertainment complex operates both in winter and in summer. During Christmas, the entertainment area is open from 11 to 19.

6. "Legoland"

The park is located in Bavaria. It is the fourth largest Lego amusement park. It is divided into eight thematic zones where you can see numerous accurate models built with a huge amount of detail (whole cities, individual objects, invented or real, for example, Neuschwanstein Castle).

Here you can have fun until you drop on more than 50 attractions designed for different age groups. More can be found on the amusement park website. Each site of this company is unique in its own way, there are new facilities there, so visiting places is always interesting.
