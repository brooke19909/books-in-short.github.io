Factors of personality socialization: everything is important

Few people today are able to not depend on society. It is no coincidence that solitary confinement is considered one of the worst forms of punishment. We are attracted to others, but does interaction always work out well? Of course, in each case there are features, but in general the success of our communication depends on whether adaptation to the norms of society, that is, the socialization of the individual, has occurred. In this article you will read about the factors of socialization of the individual.

In general, this process is considered from two sides. A person is affected by his environment and significant people from this environment. Let us consider in more detail all the factors.

Let's start with microfactors, they are especially relevant in early childhood.

The first factor is socio-emotional development. A small child goes through a stage at which he absolutely needs to solve communication problems. This is as important for the baby as it is for the student to grow intellectually, solving mental problems. What does the baby need to be able to do? Form a balance between independence and discipline. And also he must learn to acquire the qualities of the environment around him. This is mainly due to the tendency of the child to imitate the qualities of peers or adults that are interesting to him.

Authoritative adults can be family and parents (second factor). Of course, families are different nowadays, new forms are emerging. However, the importance of a small immediate society remains the same. In each family, different roles, patterns of behavior and expectations regarding the baby are created. Often different parents are surprised at each other's approach and requirements. The third factor is teacher stimulation of personality development.

At the stage of secondary and high school, maximum importance is gaining popularity among peers. Therefore, many teenagers begin to learn worse - the ability to build relationships becomes subjectively more important than the knowledge given by the teacher. That is, the fourth factor is the influence of peers. Paradoxically, a teenager wants to belong to an authoritative group, this helps him to express his personality, because only the group has someone to demonstrate skills.
