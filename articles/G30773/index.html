The social struggle has always been interesting to researchers, and the history of Ancient Rome has become the center of research scientists in the Renaissance. The works accumulated since then cover almost all aspects of the political, cultural, and economic development of the tsarist, republican, and imperial periods. As a result of the class struggle between powerless plebeians and omnipotent patricians, a new post was formed, designed to protect the lower layers of the population.

The origin of the Institute of National Tribunes





The people's stands are an elected post, the main purpose of which was to ensure legal protection of the common people from the arbitrariness of the authorities.

Plebeians are a practically disenfranchised category of the population that was formed as a result of social struggle. They were entitled to a small piece of land. But it was precisely on the plebeians that the Roman imperial power sought to rely on; they wanted to make them the main force of the army. For the first time, a mixture of the lower and upper layers of the population occurred during the reign of Servius Tullius. He carried out a reform of the administrative system - in place of tribal tribes came territorial. The male population was divided into 6 categories, regardless of origin. In the tsarist period, the election of authorities was virtually eliminated, but with the establishment of the republic, public assemblies began to gather. Century commissions replaced the king and had similar powers. Patricians monopolized power in the Senate. At the same time, the plebeian struggle for rights escalated - they wanted to get equal opportunities with the patricians in public life, sought to limit the debt percentage. In 494 BC e. - in protest against the Senate - the plebs gather on Sacred Mountain. This was the first secession. The Senate made concessions and allowed the organization of a magistrate and the post of defenders of popular rights.

Powers and Responsibilities





The main task was to protect the rights of the common population - the plebs. The stands could affect the decision of the magistrate, the Senate. They did not have power only over censors and dictators. Their rights were expressed in the ability to veto, fine magistrates, ordinary citizens for various violations. Over time, they received legislative rights.

The doors of his house should always be open to the people - the people's stands, a sacred person in Rome, was obliged at any time to provide support and protection to those who needed it. We can say that he was attached to Rome - it was impossible to leave the city for more than a day.

This position was the center for the development of democratic ideas and aspirations. But they had to work harmoniously, otherwise the confrontation caused stagnation and the inability to make a single decision.
