Volgograd: the most interesting information

Over its long history, the city has changed three names. Initially, in the XVI century it was the watchdog city of Tsaritsyn, whose main function was to defend the Volga route from endless raids by local tribes, formed and settled in the surrounding lands after the collapse of the Golden Horde. The toponym "Tsaritsyn" is not associated with the monarchy, but comes from the Turkic words "sary-su" and "sary-chin." This combination translates as "yellow or red water."

From the 18th to the 19th centuries the city grows and receives the status of industrial, and then (after the construction of the railway in 1862) and transport hub. In the period preceding the revolution, many beautiful churches and cathedrals, palaces and monumental noble structures were built in the city, large-scale residential complexes were erected, and a significant population growth was noted.

During the revolution, Tsaritsyn was a long struggle between the red and white. In the near future, the Soviet government will appreciate the merits in these battles of Joseph Stalin and rename the city in his honor. From 1925 to 1961, the city was called Stalingrad, the Tsaritsa River was called Pionerka. It was a time of total eradication of everything that at least remotely related to autocracy. With the construction of the Volga-Don Canal , the status of the most important transport hub was finally entrenched in the city.





After the terrible war years and the infamous battle of Stalingrad, even more difficult recovery years came. Exhausted by the war and half-starved people literally from the ashes revived their beautiful city. After the death of Stalin, Khrushchev initiated the so-called ‚Äúde-Stalinization‚Äù process, during which the city was again renamed according to the name of its main river.

Types of tourism in Volgograd

In order to understand the purposes for which travelers travel to the city, it is enough to consider in detail the nature, climate and areas of the city. The climate in Volgograd is sharply continental: frosty winters, hot summers. You can relax here all year round: at the tourist camps ice fishing and winter fun are offered, and in summer beach vacations are popular on the banks of the Volga and its tributaries (Tsaritsa, Mokroy and Sukhoi Mechetka). The vegetation and fauna in the vicinity of the city are quite diverse. On the high banks of the Volga, you can find oak groves, spruce forests. For hunters there are lands where you can shoot game, hares, wild boars.

The cultural and historical significance of the city, as well as its heroic military past, make it possible to talk about educational and sightseeing tourism. Volgograd is spectacularly distinguished by the panorama from the main river. The metropolis is expanding along its shores, today there are 8 administrative districts.

During the years of the USSR, more than 200 educational institutions were built in Volgograd. Today it is a scientific and educational center. Students come here to study, in universities they hold conferences, seminars of intercity and international levels.

A developed industry, a large number of enterprises and commercial organizations attract partners from all over the country. Consequently, a high level of business tourism in the region can be noted. One of the most popular accommodation facilities in the city is the Ring Premium Business Hotel.
