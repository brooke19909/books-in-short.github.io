The mistake of archaeologists

The palace at Knossos had a special purpose. It focused on political, economic and social life. This is evidenced by many finds.

The first studies began back in 1878. They were conducted by the Greek Minos Kalokerino. He was interested in a strange hilly area. The man began work and even found several significant objects in support of the theory that the palace lies underground. But the excavations had to be stopped due to the ban of the then Turkish authorities.

Arthur Evans continued the business in 1900. The Englishman bought up the lands on which he believed there were chambers. The goal was to learn as much as possible about the period from 1400 BC. e. Therefore, all subsequent layers, which would tell about the further history of this part, were destroyed. But this was not the only mistake of the archaeologist who discovered Knossos. This is a city that not only lost several centuries, but did not preserve its original appearance. During the excavations, Evans restored the found palace, guided by his ideas.

The genius of architecture

In fact, the Knossos palace is a lot of rooms in which it is very easy to get lost. The huge multi-story building with more than a thousand rooms for various purposes was stunning. Such a system, according to scientists, arose because every year something was being built to the wards. The halls were connected by stairs and cunning corridors. Architects did not think about aesthetics.



In addition to amazing planning, the palace had high-tech features. So, for example, the complex had plumbing and sewage. With the help of some pipes, water entered the building, others filled the baths, and others removed the slops. In the construction there were not only toilet rooms, but also toilet bowls.

The total area is 24,000 square meters. On this territory there was a theater, warehouses, workshops, rooms for worship and, of course, the luxurious chambers of the royal family.

Museum of the past

You can enter the territory through the Western courtyard. From here begins the tour. There you can go to the main staircase that connects four floors. The path along the perimeter runs in a circle. The first stop is the courtyard, which is divided by three roads. He once served as a prayer book. This is evidenced by the altars.

One of the most ancient premises is the kulures. These are large pits that could fulfill the role of both a warehouse for grain and a place for remains after the sacrifice. The throne room with the stone throne of the ruler is popular among the guests. Many walls of the corridors and rooms were decorated with frescoes, paintings and expensive metals.
