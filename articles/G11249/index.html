SJCAM SJ5000 Plus: instruction manual

On the front panel there is a Mode button, which is used to turn on the device and switch between recording, video playback, photo display, parameter settings. Pressing it during recording makes the video erasable. There are also two indicators in the form of narrow horizontal stripes: the lower one lights up when the camera is turned on, and the upper one, green, when Wi-Fi is activated.

The top button on the right side panel is used to turn Wi-Fi on and off, and the bottom buttons are for scrolling through the menu and for enlarging. On the opposite side are the HDMI connectors, micro-USB and microSD card slot.

The button at the top serves to start recording. When pressed during recording, an image is captured with a resolution of 2 megapixels (1920x1080).

The batteries last for 55 minutes with the screen and wireless connection turned on. After the SJCAM SJ5000 Plus firmware was improved (version 1.7), the battery began to work for 15-20 minutes longer.

The maximum frame rate for files recorded using the SJ5000 Plus is 24 Mbps (1080p 60 fps) versus 15 Mbps in the SJ4000. This means that files are less compressed, contain more information and are of higher quality. Obviously, they take up more space on the memory card.

In the menu, you can adjust the microphone volume, which should be set to 100% when using a waterproof case and at a level of 50-60% without it, otherwise the sound will be loud and distorted.

Two new sensitivity settings have been added to the SJ5000 Plus in addition to the ISO 100, 200 and 400 in the SJ4000, namely 800 and 1600 ISO for shooting in low light conditions.

If the camera is turned off by mistake during video recording, you can restore it using Recovery Movie if the file size is not too small.

Sensor and optics of action cameras

The SJ5000 made a big leap in quality compared to the previous SJCAM SJ4000 model, whose 12-megapixel resolution was created by simply enlarging the file created by the 2-megapixel sensor. Two basic models - SJ5000 and SJ5000 WiFi - are equipped with Panasonic's new 14-megapixel image sensor, and the SJ5000 Plus and even more - 16 megapixels. The number of pixels says a lot, but it is worth recalling the requirements for the design of the optics of action cameras.

Small overall dimensions and light weight are decisive factors. GoPro aesthetics mean an ultra-wide angle view of action cameras with a fisheye effect. This type of lens has a deep focus, so you can have a lens with a constant focal length so that the depth of field is maximum. Such lenses for large SLR cameras are too heavy and expensive. GoPro-style devices use a small and inexpensive optics option with the same small image sensor size. The SJ5000 Plus has 1 / 2.33 "Panasonic MN34120PA CMOS. Its dimensions are tiny - 5.8x4.3 mm, like the iPhone 5 sensor, and much smaller than that of a digital SLR camera.

For this reason, any assessment of image quality should be realistic and take into account the physical limitations of the device. Even the best camcorder, due to the small size of the sensor, is invariably subject to more noise, especially in low light, high sensitivity and limited dynamic range.

In ideal conditions, you can create an impressive picture on your mobile phone.

A wide-angle view of action cameras unequally displays all objects: objects at an average distance seem very distant, and photographing in the vicinity significantly distorts faces.





How to improve the video?

Professionals do not tolerate when the sky is not visible on the video. Most of the material captured on action cameras using the default settings has terrible cloud spots or strange shades of color where the blue sky should be. This is because the small sensor cannot handle the high dynamic range of many scenes, and the factory settings are designed to correctly transmit the main subject, giving the rest to chance. In order to achieve the desired result, you should set the maximum exposure, which does not distort the sky, and bring the middle tones and shadows in post-processing. For example, with an exposure of -1.0Ev, AWB, and ISO 100, the sky looks good, the clouds are detailed, and realistic blueness is visible. At the same time, the shadows and the middle are too dark, they must be made brighter.

Unfortunately, sometimes processing 8-bit video can lead to layering and excessive noise, but adjusting the SJ5000 Plus files did not have this effect.

Light weight

The first impression of the pictures is that they look grainy and noisy. After processing by the graphic editor, halos of sharpness and other defects remain visible. According to users, the inclusion of sharpening in the menu could largely prevent this at the post-processing stage.

Buyers are pleasantly surprised by the work of an inexpensive lens, the price of which does not exceed $ 3. Image detail reaches the edges of the frame, and there really is no need for an aggressive level of sharpness embedded in the camera. Not surprising was the high level of chromatic aberration. Its blue-yellow variety is clearly visible in the pictures. The photo editor is able to remove it, but purple halos at the border of areas with high image contrast are much more difficult to remove.

Like all cameras with a small sensor, the SJ5000 Plus has a limited dynamic range. When using the default settings, the device tends to ‚Äúinflate‚Äù bright areas of the image, as a result of which the sky turns white and extraneous colors are present. With an exposure set to -1.0EV, the sky looks pretty good, but light buildings still stand out a lot.

Thus, with the use of light post-processing, quite suitable photographs can be obtained, provided that measures are taken to prevent overexposure. Unfortunately, the professional use of the camera is hindered by aggressive image sharpness, which limits the size of the image when printing, but its quality is sufficient to display ultra-high definition on a TV screen.
