Ultrasound apparatus: principle of operation, use in medicine and in everyday life

Modern technology does not stand still. Until recently, no one had any idea about the presence of ultrasonic waves with an almost unlimited range of applications. In our time, the use of an ultrasound apparatus is common not only in medicine, but also in cosmetology. In addition, this device can become indispensable in everyday life and household! But first things first.

Operating principle

How does this device work? Why, thanks to the ultrasound therapy apparatus, can the doctor see the image of a certain organ, and the cosmetologist can cleanse the face and improve blood circulation in the capillaries of the skin?

The operation of the ultrasound apparatus is based on the creation of an ultrasonic wave oscillating with a frequency of 1-25 MHz. When this wave of ultrasound hits various organs and tissues, each individual cell reflects it in its own way, transmitting the signal back to the sensor, after which it is transmitted to the monitor of the device. It is on this principle of action that its use in medicine is based.

In cosmetology, the reflecting ability of tissues is not used, but the ability of ultrasound to literally push the necessary substances into the skin with its vibrations. Before the procedure, a special cream gel is smeared on the skin, which cleans the pores under the action of an ultrasonic wave.

Ultrasound from mice is an innovative tool that allows you to clean the house from them once and for all. Medical use

The most widespread ultrasound device has acquired it in the field of diagnostics. The ultrasound diagnostic method is highly informative and allows you to obtain the following data:

  • localization of the pathological process;
  • the size of the organ or foreign entity;
  • organ wall thickness;
  • contours of an organ or pathological formation;
  • location relative to other organs;
  • the presence or absence of fluid in the cavities;
  • tissue density or, in the language of ultrasound diagnostics, echostructure.

Application in cosmetology

An ultrasound apparatus for the face is a device of much smaller size than that used in medicine. It fits in your hand and is lightweight so as not to cause fatigue on the hands of the beautician. Ultrasonic waves beat off from a small metal plate located at the end of the device. They drive in a product previously applied to the face, cleansing the pores, exfoliating dead cells. It also improves blood circulation in the capillaries and the outflow of lymph from the skin, increasing the intensity of metabolic processes and rejuvenating.

Main effects

The ultrasound apparatus provides a comprehensive effect on the skin of the face, namely:

  • intensive cleansing;
  • peeling, which allows the body to get rid of keratinized epithelium and microparticles of dirt;
  • massage that improves cell regeneration;
  • bactericidal effect, which is the destruction of bacteria and microorganisms located on the skin of the face.

Note! If you experience unpleasant sensations on the skin of the face, you need to reduce the frequency of ultrasound or increase the amount of gel applied to the face.

How to choose an ultrasonic scrubber

Ultrasonic equipment for face cleaning is an extremely popular product in the beauty market due to the high demand for it. After all, cleansing the skin with the help of an ultrasound therapy apparatus is almost the most common procedure in cosmetology.

It is with high demand that such an abundance of ultrasound devices on the market is associated. Each of them has its own characteristics and nuances of application. However, in general, two main types of devices are distinguished: portable and stationary. Stationary is more effective and provides a deeper cleansing, it is focused on professional salons and has a higher cost. Portable is cheaper and more suitable for home use.

Contraindications

Whatever unique properties the ultrasound has for cleansing the skin of the face, there are a number of contraindications to its use:

  • the period of pregnancy and breastfeeding;
  • the presence of acute infectious diseases;
  • high blood pressure;
  • endocrinological diseases (diabetes mellitus, thyroid problems);
  • the presence of neoplasms;
  • age less than 18 years.

Also, you should not carry out this procedure with a superficial arrangement of the capillaries of the face, that is, very close to the skin, since ultrasound can cause their damage and the appearance of small hemorrhagic rashes.
