Aleksin (Tula region). City Aleksin (Tula region): attractions, recreation

The wonderful and amazing city of Aleksin in the Tula region, according to legend, was created at the very end of the XIII century. Its founder was the son of Alexander Nevsky, the first Moscow prince Daniil Alexandrovich. But official sources indicate that the city was founded in 1348. This date is recorded in the Nikon Chronicle. Historical treatises claim that the name of the settlement comes from the name of the son of Prince Daniel, Alexander. So Alexin appeared. Tula region acquired a settlement with this name in 1298. Some believe that it was named after Metropolitan Alexei. It was to him in 1354 that the city was given up for maintenance.



Amazing story

Despite its not too large size, this city has an amazing fate. It was founded at the height of the Golden Horde yoke. Like the state, Aleksin was prepared for many tests. Through them, he successfully passed, repeatedly succumbing to destruction and, like a phoenix from the ashes, reborn again. The city of Aleksin (Tula region) is the great history of a small settlement, which, despite the obstacles, has grown into a magnificent settlement with a population of more than 65 thousand people.

Active development of the city falls on the XIX and XX centuries. It was then that he took place as the center of the metallurgical and sawmill industry. In the 20s of the last century, in Aleksin, not far from the pine forest, the first summer cottages began to settle in. The city was a favorite vacation spot of A.P. Chekhov. Here at different times and for various reasons, there were Pasternak, Zhukovsky, Polenov, Richter and other no less famous personalities.

Flora and fauna

Aleksin (Tula region) is a marvelous land surrounded by wonderful nature. This is perhaps one of the few places in the region that has not been contaminated with Chernobyl radiation. Therefore, experts recommend coming here for a wellness purpose. The main natural asset of the region is the Oka River, which divides Aleksin into two parts. But this is not the only body of water, there are many of them in the region. Here, not a single summer passes without joyful bathing, and the children look forward to the start of the beach season.

The fragrance of nature, its coniferous, deciduous and mixed forests, trees dating back more than a hundred years, wide shrubs call tourists to visit Aleksin. They attract to themselves, fill a person with energy, help to cure many ailments and give an incredible feeling of unity with nature.



Aleksin (Tula region), whose photo colorfully confirms all of the above, attracts not only people. Various species of animals live in the local forests. For example, you can easily meet a wolf, an otter, a wild boar or an elk. And squirrels, gophers and muskrats became almost the best friends of the inhabitants of Aleksin. So if you are not afraid to fall into the clutches of a predator or dream of feeding a fluffy and playful squirrel, then you are welcome to an amazing and fabulous city.

Famous personalities in the life of Aleksin

Like any other city, Aleksin (Tula region) was overgrown with legends associated with historical figures who previously lived here. So, not far from the city itself is located the picturesque village of Kolyupanovo. Once upon a time, blessed Euphrosyne lived here for quite some time. People came to her from different parts of the region asking for help. The woman was perfectly able to heal from various ailments. The old woman loved to walk near the Proshenka river. Here in a quiet and secluded place, not sparing her own hands, she dug a well. The people who arrived at her, she ordered to drink water from this well.



Euphrosyne died, and in 1885 a small chapel of wood was erected above her pond. And when the feast of the coming of the Holy Spirit came, the chapel was consecrated. At the beginning of the last century, the chapel was demolished, and a new one was built instead. Over the years, this place has deteriorated, no one was engaged in its restoration. But people continued to believe in the power of the healing source. They continued to come here and ask for healing from Saint Euphrosyne. And, oddly enough, they received it. Only after many years the chapel was reopened.

He withstood them, will withstand all that is difficult to overcome today. He draws his strength and energy from the surrounding nature and the majestic Oka.
