How the rent is calculated: structure, calculation rules, what the calculation is made of

How is the rent calculated? This issue is most important for many owners of real estate, as laws often change and tariffs are systematically raised. There is also dishonesty of various companies. In order to pay the bills correctly and not overpay, you need to know the principle of calculating the rent.

Rent

The owner has an obligation to pay the rent immediately after the acquisition of housing. A person enters into an agreement with a service provider (for example, water, electricity), as well as with a company that maintains communications in good condition.

Calculation of the commission according to the number of registered people living, as well as depending on the area

The calculation of the rent by the number of registered people is carried out using the average rates. The list of such norms includes: garbage collection, water disposal, hot and cold water, electricity, as well as gas. The last three items will be included in the list of norms if the owner does not have meters. If you install the counters, then in the future the costs will be significantly reduced. If the norms depend on the number of people, then the calculation of the rent will be carried out from the number of people registered in this territory, regardless of how many live in the apartment. That is, two people can be registered in the apartment, but actually three people live. Payment will be made for two people.

Availability of counters

How is the rent calculated if the homeowner has installed meters at home? Initially, it is necessary to monitor their evidence. In order to pay the rent, the owner must submit the meter data every month, always at the same time. Usually, everyone takes testimony in the last days of the month. In order to find out the amount of payment, you need to calculate the difference between the readings of the previous and current months. The resulting difference should be multiplied by the tariff set in the region.

Usually, the use of counters is the most profitable, since the data on it are less than the calculations according to the norm per person. Payment can be made using meters for these types of services: cold and hot water, electricity, heating, gas. Payments if no one lives in the apartment

How is the rent calculated if no one lives in the apartment? Initially, the owner must prove that no one lives in the apartment. And for this it is necessary to issue a certificate in the housing office. Also, if the owner registered in the apartment, but actually lives in another place, then you need to submit a document that can confirm this. Lack of counters

If the apartment does not have meters, then the calculation of the rent is based on standards that are multiplied by the number of registered people. In this case, payment will be made regardless of how much in reality the various resources were used. Typically, the amount received in this embodiment is much higher than the actual price.

Payments in a communal apartment

The calculation of rent in communal apartments has its own characteristics. The tax, which depends on the quadrature, is calculated separately for each room. Also, electricity meters should be installed on all rooms separately. Owners of communal apartments must pay for common areas. The amount of payment will be divided among the owners proportionally, depending on the share. And the use of water and electricity in the common room is calculated separately and divided equally. Thus, each owner pays a certain part of the total tax.

Payments in new buildings

Who should pay the bills in new buildings? When the house is still under construction, the developer must pay all incoming bills. After the house was taken into operation, the developer also continues to pay tax. But as soon as they buy apartments in the house, the owner has an obligation to pay bills. This obligation comes from the moment of signing the agreement on the sale of real estate. The obligation arises regardless of whether the new owner lives in the apartment or not. The amount of payment can be reduced by submitting certain information.

Penny

Penalty is a fine that accrues to the owner due to unpaid bills on time. The delay begins on the next day by which the payment was due.

How is interest on rent calculated? The fine is 1/300 of the total refinancing rate of the Central Bank of the country. If payments have not been received within four months, then the penalty will be 1/170 of the rate. Rates should be specified on the official website of the Central Bank, as they vary. Typically, a fine is charged for each overdue month. It will be possible to stop the penalty only if the debt is fully paid off.

It is worth noting that the penalty is not an obligation for the supplier company. Penalty is his right. That is, companies may not accrue it. Of course, they will not refuse additional profit in the form of a fine. If a person has a life circumstance due to which he cannot pay in full, then he must contact the office of the company with a statement of delay or installment plan. Cases can only be respectful. For example, the birth of a child, the loss of a breadwinner, dismissal, illness and so on.

Subsidies

The country's legislation allows citizens to apply for a subsidy on a rent. But this service is not provided for everyone. The list of citizens who can receive the subsidy is presented below:

  • owners;
  • persons who live in buildings owned by the state fund;
  • persons who rent a house, while it is necessary to submit a lease;
  • persons living in a cooperative.
