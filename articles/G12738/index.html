A bit about the resort

As we have already mentioned, Club Zigana 5 * HV (Turkey, Kemer, Beldibi) is located near the village of Beldibi, which is currently a popular beach resort in Turkey. The town is an elongated coastal strip, which is built up with hotels, shops and restaurants.

The village received its name in honor of a small rivulet. Archaeological excavations have shown that the settlements in this region were still in the first century BC. However, later the town was going through hard times. The situation changed in the eighties, when the first hotels began to be built here. And ten years later, the resort also acquired luxurious high-class hotels.



Beldibi is located just 25 kilometers from Antalya. The coast of the resort is very unusual. It stretches in a thin strip between the slopes of the mountains and the sea. Therefore, the town consists of several streets stretched along the coast.

The beaches of the resort are quite large pebbles. But some of the hotels for their vacationers covered the beach with fine sand and built bathing piers. This also applies to Club Zigana 5 * HV (Turkey, Kemer).

It is worth noting that Belbidi is a beach resort, and therefore the main attraction here is the beach and the sea. And you will not find other attractions here. Not far from Beldibi are the ruins of ancient Phaselis. At one time, it was founded by colonists from Rhodes. Later, the city survived the heyday and was considered a very large trading and seaport, and a military center. From the past, only ruins remained in the city.

Nearby, near the village of Goynuk, there is a canyon, which only trained people can walk on. A pedestrian walk is quite long, and in some places the road leads through shallow water.

To see something else interesting, you should go to Kemer.

Nutrition Reviews

What about food in Club Zigana 5 * (Kemer, Turkey)? Reviews in 2017 indicate that the food in the restaurant is quite monotonous for a five-star establishment. On the tables there is a very small selection of meat dishes. This is usually chicken. As for fruits and desserts, they are also very few. The hotel positions itself as an institution that accepts couples with children. This means that the restaurant should have a special menu. Unfortunately, at this time the kids are rushing to a mini-disco.

As for drinks, the concept includes only local alcohol. The restaurant staff is fast enough, so there are no complaints. But as for food, tourists consider its quantity and quality to be inappropriate for the five-star level of the hotel.

Excursions and trips

Resting in the hotel, of course, it is worth allotting time for all kinds of trips. A mandatory item in the program should be Kemer, which has a lot of interesting things. And in the immediate vicinity of it there are many attractions. You can get to the city on dolmushi, which regularly run between villages.

Almost all vacationers seek to buy gifts and souvenirs in Turkey. From Kemer, as a rule, painted robes, hookahs, and homely pointed shoes are brought. Handmade carpets, fur and leather products are especially popular with tourists. Ceramics, tea sets and world famous sweets can also be brought from Kemer. Textiles, souvenirs and desserts are most profitable to purchase in the markets.

The largest number of shops in the city is located on the main pedestrian street - Ataturk Boulevard. Here you can buy gold jewelry, fur and leather products in stores.



Each tourist, relaxing in Turkey, seeks to visit the local market, since it is here that you can feel the real oriental flavor. On the main street of the city there is a grocery market. It sells the freshest fruits, vegetables, olive oil, nuts, spices, coffee, honey. Also in the center on Tuesdays you can visit the textile bazaar. On it you can buy carpets, bathrobes, shoes.

For those who are interested in historical monuments, there are a lot of interesting things in Kemer. One such place is Phaselis. The ancient city was founded in the seventh century BC. Throughout his long history, he experienced a variety of times - from falls to take-offs. Phaselis was once a major port. His prosperity lasted more than 300 years. Later, the city lost its significance. The ruins preserved on the site of Phaselis belong to the Byzantine and Roman periods. Another historical monument is the ancient Olympos, which now became part of the National Park.

No less interesting is the historical park called Yoruk. It is located near Kemer. The ethnic village provides an opportunity to see how a nomadic tribe lived, how people's dwellings, clothes and household items looked. Once in the village, as if transported for centuries into the past, everything looks so realistic.

An interesting place to visit is Mount Yanartash, which is always shrouded in fire and smoke. Currently, scientists believe that this phenomenon is due to the fact that the earth's surface has accumulated gases, which, when exiting to the surface, ignite. However, local residents prefer to believe the legend, which says that the fire spews out the defeated monster, locked in the mountain. The fire on the mountain does not go out day and night. Of course, in the dark, the sight looks simply amazing and bewitching.

Not far from Olympos and Kemer is a complex of Beldibi caves. A stunningly beautiful place is surrounded by coniferous forests, and somewhere in the depths a river flows. Having crossed it, you can reach both the caves themselves and the waterfall. In ancient times, caves were a reliable shelter for animals and people. In the middle of the last century, they were well examined by experts who made many amazing finds - household utensils, cave paintings and tools. Here you can feel the spirit of antiquity and feel like a real explorer.
