Attractions in the park "Magic World" in Ufa

The "magic world" actually exists. In this fairy-tale world, you can have a wonderful rest for children and adults and participate in interesting contests. It is located in the center of the capital of Bashkiria - Ufa. The Magic World Park is open to all comers, and on the eve of the opening of the winter season there will be many interesting attractions.

Rides

The Magic World Park has been operating for a long time. We can say that he is an old-timer in the capital. There are so many kinds of attractions in it that visitors with children just run their eyes and think about where to go with their children. Convenient park for relaxing in that it is divided into zones. There is a park area reserved for the smallest. For adults and older children, a large area with many attractions. There is a place in the park where two summer cafes with the original names "Bison" and "Cheburechki from Uzbek" are concentrated, and two stationary year-round restaurants "Cloudberry" and "Cloudberry at the Cottage".





The park has hours of discounts. In the so-called nanny hours on weekdays from 12:00 to 15:00, when for the price of one you can visit three attractions. And by the way, since there is face control at the entrance, there are neither drunk nor homeless people in the park. There is a park "Magic World" in Ufa at the address: 79/3 October Avenue.

Children's area

A children's area with many sandboxes and at least a dozen attractions. There are all kinds of trampolines, trains and carousels in the area for kids. "Merry Kangaroo" is one of the favorite attractions of young lovers of heights, where they fly up with a squeal, but without fear in a six-seater cabin. The design of the park, and especially this area for kids, is fabulous. There are playgrounds with inflatable slides in the park. Entrance to them costs 100 rubles, but time is unlimited. In the park there are a lot of figures of fairy-tale heroes, and the attractions are decorated in the form of fairy-tale castles.
