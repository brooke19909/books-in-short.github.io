Who is the statistician? Social, economic and legal statistics

In modern conditions of development of society, interest in statistics as a science and its widespread use in practical activities has significantly increased. Today, no one can deny the importance and underestimate the role of statistics in public life. Statistics contribute to the formation of an adequate understanding of the current state of affairs in the country. Due to this, in the event that any deviations or inconsistencies are detected, it becomes possible to take a number of corrective measures in time and significantly improve the situation.

Interpretation and meaning of the term

The term "statistics" comes from the Latin word status, which implies a certain state of things. This concept was first used in 1749 by a scientist of German origin G. Achenwal, who mentioned it in his book on the conduct of public affairs. To date, the term is used in three main meanings:

  1. Statistics is a science that studies the processes and phenomena of public life, reveals the laws of development of these phenomena and explains the existing relationship between them.
  2. This is a branch of knowledge that studies the phenomena occurring in society from a quantitative point of view.
  3. This is some data presented by organizations under the guise of financial statements.

The subject, object and tasks of statistics

Each science has its own specific features, its own subject and object of study. Statistics are no exception. Her subject are:

  • social and economic phenomena occurring in public life;
  • the quantitative side of socio-economic phenomena, taking into account the place and specific time.

The objects of study of statistical science are:

  • society;
  • social processes and phenomena;
  • the impact of the activities of society on the state of the environment.

As for the main tasks of statistics, the following should be noted:

  1. To identify and analyze all the socio-economic transformations taking place in society.
  2. Investigate and evaluate the effectiveness of social production.
  3. Timely provide the government with reliable and reliable information.




What is economic statistics?

Economic statistics is a very important branch of statistical science, which is engaged in the study of economic processes that occur in society. Its purpose is to conduct an adequate analysis of the conditions for the functioning of the economy, the laws and patterns of development of society. This goal is realized by collecting information, its processing and analysis. For this, a system of quantitative indicators is used, which are closely interconnected with each other. Thanks to the data obtained by economic statistics, it becomes possible to ensure a constant quantitative characteristic of various economic processes, as well as the economy as a whole.





What is social statistics?

Social statistics is an equally important industry that studies social changes that take place in society. Its subject is society in all its manifestations and the quantitative side of the totality of social processes and phenomena. The main goal is to develop and use effective indicators with which you can identify the dynamics of the development of social conditions of the population and the development of society as a whole. What is legal statistics?

Legal statistics is another branch of statistical science, the subject of study of which is the quantitative characteristics of legal processes, as well as immoral manifestations in society. There are 3 main branches of legal statistics: criminal law, civil law and administrative law.

Criminal law statistics have as a subject of study the patterns in which there are movements of crime, criminals and punishments applied to those who have violated the law. After the court has passed one or another verdict, statistics are used to record the sentenced or convicted.

Civil law statistics keeps records of lawsuits-plaintiffs and defendants-decisions. All information about them at the end of each court case is entered by the judge in special form cards.

Administrative law statistics study the laws governing the movement of administrative offenses, the people who committed them, and the measures applied to offenders.

Who is the statistician?

The question arises of who such statistics are and what they do. Initially, it should be said that statistics is primarily a profession. Today, this profession has attracted the attention of an increasing number of young people who, after graduation, decide to devote themselves entirely to the study and development of statistical science. They are well aware that a statistician is a specialist whose work is devoted to the processing and study of quantitative indicators of social processes and phenomena, the degree of their change and development. He is an employee subordinate to state authority, or an employee of the statistical departments of various enterprises and organizations. It should also be emphasized that the statistician is an expert whose activities are aimed at collecting, processing and analyzing the totality of information about the state and the events occurring in it. His immediate responsibilities include the following tasks:

  1. The collection of information and the preparation on its basis of reporting on certain indicators.
  2. Verification of the realism of the data collected and their comparison with indicators of the past period.
  3. Systematization, processing and analysis of data.
  4. Compilation of various kinds of certificates, based on the totality of the data collected and processed.

The subject of statistics is symbolic systems, that is, numbers, different tables and graphs, formulas, documentation. The main goal pursued by him is the analysis of statistical data, their systematization, as well as the comparison of laws from a quantitative perspective.





Role in public life

The role of statistical science and statistical accounting in the life of society cannot be underestimated. Statistics give a real picture of the state of the economy, scientific activity, the level of culture of the population, the welfare and well-being of society in a certain period of time. In addition, it becomes possible to monitor the implementation of various national economic programs, identify inconsistencies, deviations from the intended plan, and compare development indicators of various states. Moreover, statistics are of great importance not only for the present, but also for the future. Thus, they can be the basis for the scientific planning of tomorrow's development of society and its progress.
