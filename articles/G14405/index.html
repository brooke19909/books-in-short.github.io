History of Valaam Island





In the northern part of Lake Ladoga, the largest in Europe, the rocky Valaam rises. Karelia with the peoples inhabiting it since time immemorial gave it a name that translates as ‚Äúhigh, mountainous land‚Äù.

According to legend, the first appeared here the Apostle Andrew the First-Called as an enlightener and distributor of Christianity. He erected a stone cross, on the site of which now stands a monastery.

Nine hundred years have passed. The missionaries from Greece arrived in Karelia on Valaam: German and Sergius. Thanks to them, a brotherhood of monks appeared on the island. It was they who built the Valaam Monastery at the beginning of the 15th century. Monks from other places slowly reached here. They put out farm buildings and other structures made of wood. After a hundred years, the monastic brotherhood has grown to a thousand people. By the standards of the time, it was rich. The Swedes regularly robbed him, killed monks, burned temples, manuscripts and books. The place is empty.

Recovery

By decree of Peter the Great, the monastery was restored. In 1720, the wooden Transfiguration Cathedral was built, which was consecrated in the name of St. Andrew and the Theologian. Household buildings and buildings for cells grew. The Assumption Church was built ten years later. The fire of 1754 destroyed everything. The monastery began to be restored with the help of Elizabeth Petrovna eleven monks. A new construction began: they put the Church of the Transfiguration, St. Nicholas, the Assumption and Gate Church, new cells. So the difficult history of Balaam flowed in Karelia.

Acts of the Rector

Rapid stone construction began during the abbess Damascene, who came out of the peasants.

This new abbot, a strong and extraordinary personality, invited experienced architects from St. Petersburg. Valaam literally blossomed in Karelia: gardens and vegetable gardens were planted, livestock was raised, monks were engaged in fishing and handicrafts. They had bakeries, a pharmacy, a hospital, a bathhouse. Surplus went on sale. For more than forty years, the monastery was run by father Damascene. The rules under him were strict: in the care of the elders were young monks and novices. All church services were required to attend, as were many hours of labor obedience. No one could voluntarily leave the island, which since the XVI century was at the same time the place of exile of the guilty monks.

Development from mid-19th century to mid-20th century

For over a hundred years, the archipelago was part of the Principality of Finland. It, in turn, was part of the Russian Empire. The next abbot was Jonathan II (Ivan Dmitriev). He completed the construction of a majestic stone two-story light five-domed Transfiguration Cathedral with a belfry.

His efforts put in order the huge library of the monastery. Water was laid on the island. After the revolution, Finland became an independent state, where most of the churches are Lutheran. The monasteries of Balaam turned out to be a "national minority." To attract the flock, the monks began to serve in Finnish. Their number has decreased. The remaining brethren accepted Finnish citizenship. During the Finnish War, the island was bombed, and the monks, taking all the valuables, moved to the continent. In the town of High-balance, they created a monastery called "New Valaam". He became the center of Orthodoxy in Finland.

On Soviet Valaam in Karelia, a mundane life was going on. The monastery formed a school of young and boatswain. In 1940, Finland again occupied the island, but in 1945, after the war, he again moved to the USSR. Five years later, the monastery housed a house for the disabled of World War II and labor, which closed in 1984.

New life of the old monastery

Due to the beauty of the nature of Karelia, the island of Valaam from the 60s on the boat began to visit tourists. In autumn 1989, the monastery was transferred to the Leningrad diocese, in December, on the day of the Apostle Andrew the First-Called, six monks arrived on the monastery ground. They had a lot of work to put in order not only the monastery in Karelia (Valaam), but also to re-develop the lands that were transferred to them, the restoration of fifteen hermitages. This requires an amount of approximately 370 million rubles. In total, 15 million were received.

The lands that were given to the monastery are not suitable for agricultural purposes, while the good ones are empty. The monastery contains 23 cows, and could even be one hundred and forty, as it was in the XVIII century. Monks donate milk free of charge to school and kindergarten, and at cost to the hospital. There is not enough food for dairy cows; they have to be purchased on the mainland. The monastery has a lot of problems.

We give four examples. The question of the transfer of old bakeries, which made delicious bread on wood, was not resolved. He did not get stale for several days. Tourists filled the forest with trash. If they were rented to monks, and no one wants to solve this issue, then everything would be cleared.

There is also no decision to create a National Park on the island, which can fully preserve nature. And plants and animals are unique here: relict coniferous forests, oaks, ash, larch, chestnuts, arborvitae, cedar groves. On the shores you can see colonies of terns and gulls, as well as rookery of the Ladoga seal. Nobody wants to build a plant for processing solid waste (glass containers, bags, polyethylene) and engage in their removal from the island, as well as a system for treating fecal effluents that cause hepatitis and dysentery.

Monastery complex

Valaam Monastery in Karelia was completely formed in 1862. Bricks were made on the island, from which everything was built. From the bay along the granite staircase that passes through the larch alley, tourists and pilgrims approach the monastery. Before them open the gate with the church of Peter and Paul above them. Several rows of cells and churches make up two quadrangles.

Small is located inside the large, and it houses the main cathedral - Spaso-Preobrazhensky. Its five domes are a symbol of Jesus Christ and the four apostles. On the ground floor is the church of Sergius and German. Their relics are buried deep below. The bell tower consists of three tiers. It has 13 bells. The largest of them - "Apostle Andrew" - weighs fourteen tons.

During the night services, you can hear the ancient chants of the choir, which are not performed anywhere else.

Karelia, Valaam island: how to get

You can fly to Valaam, which is all surrounded by water, but the waterway is most often used. Meteor, a hydrofoil speedboat, will deliver a tourist or a pilgrim from St. Petersburg in four hours.

A boat trip is much more expensive and longer. Most people prefer to choose the road from Sortavala. The ship takes two and a half hours, and the Meteor takes fifty minutes. But they do not have a clear schedule. It is most convenient to board a speedboat with a guided tour. Only the remaining free places suit loners. And if there are not enough places, then you have to spend time on the pier. The pilgrimage service of the monastery will also provide a similar service.

Lake Ladoga can be stormy and stormy. Then all flights are canceled. In this case, the tourist will either be refunded or offered to wait 2-3 days. This must be remembered and have in stock a few days.

Wooden architecture on Lake Onega

Such beauty as the Church of the Transfiguration of the Lord or the Intercession of the Holy Virgin, next to which there is a restored tented bell tower, can not be found anywhere else. The Church of the Transfiguration of the Lord is a true wonder.

Another unusual place in Russia

At the beginning of the 15th century, two hermits arrived at the Solovetsky Islands, which are located far, far in the north, in the White Sea: German and Savvaty. The place was swampy, with many lakes, and covered with birch forest. They apparently had to live hard. Trusting only in the mercy and help of the Lord, they put up a cross and a cell. After the death of Savvaty, monks began to sail here. We know the date of foundation of the monastery - 1436. He was rich and built of huge stones and bricks.

So far, no one has even figured out who was the builder of the monastery, since some stones of the Kremlin and the walls of the fence weigh about eight tons. The lichen that covers them is about two to three thousand years old.

One hundred years after the foundation of the monastery, the monks connected all the lakes with canals. Traveling on them will take at least ten hours.

There is also a ground track in it, the length of which is 1.5 km.

At the end of the 19th century, a botanical garden was created on the grounds of the monastery, in the greenhouses of which watermelons, melons, and peaches were grown.
