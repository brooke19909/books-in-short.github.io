Cartagena (Colombia): ancient history, attractions, recreation

The port city, located in northern Colombia and called a real treasury of world culture, is extremely attractive for curious tourists. Ancient forts and structures erected during the colonial era, which Cartagena is famous for, reverently preserve the ancient history, and numerous local monuments are protected by UNESCO.

The history of the ancient city

After excavations carried out by archaeological expeditions, it was found that the first people in the Colombian city appeared before our era. The ancient Indian tribes tried for a long time to restrain the colonization of land by Europeans, until they retreated in front of the Spanish detachment that landed in 1533 in search of treasures.





It is believed that it was then that Cartagena was founded. Colombia is proud to have preserved its national and cultural dignity with a unique atmosphere. And he got his name from the Spanish settlement, from where the conquerors arrived.

Place of export of treasures to Europe

Through the city, which became the main port in the Caribbean Sea, looted innumerable treasures were exported to Europe, and Britain really wanted to take such a tidbit.

It is not surprising that all the famous pirates were engaged in robbery in this place. Researchers studying the ancient history of the city know that the proud Cartagena was subjected to at least 5 times brutal attacks. Colombia shuddered when, in the 16th century, the English pirate Francis Drake, who did not know compassion, burned the main cathedral of the city and held the local population hostage for 3 months, later receiving an ransom that was unbelievable at the time.

The defense, independence and decline of Cartagena

Concerned with this situation, the Spaniards think about the defense of the city, and soon a huge and powerful fortification system is being built around Cartagena, the forts of which are still the main local attraction.





In the XVIII century, a new British attack on the port took place, and only thanks to the competently erected 11-kilometer fences survived the heavy and long siege of Cartagena. Colombia in the 19th century fought a lot and spent time negotiating, and the declaration of independence by the city caused a new round of pressure on the courageous port, only from the Spanish side.

The year 1821 marks the beginning of the decline of Cartagena: hostilities, sieges, famines and epidemics lead to the loss of trade ties and the complete destruction of the heroic city.

The long-awaited revival

At the beginning of the 20th century, the long-awaited revival takes place: black gold deposits are found in the country, and the construction of an oil refinery in Cartagena begins, after which the pearl of the Caribbean Sea is actively developing. Now the city, actively engaged in trade and industry, is becoming a tourist mecca for many travelers.



Cartagena Attractions

Cartagena with amazingly picturesque structures of limestone shells in a pleasant lemon color is secretly named after the Latin American Venice. Its historical center is the Old Town, surrounded by a fortified wall, which was built for almost 200 years.

During the long sieges, it was destroyed more than once and rebuilt again, and now the structure surprises with the power of all tourists. Admirable and a large fortress of impressive size, erected to withstand the British.

Museum of torture

In the most Spanish city in the world, as locals call it, you can not pass by the unique Palace-Museum of the Inquisition. This vivid historical evidence stores genuine instruments of terrible torture for witches and at the same time is a monument to all those who suffered from them. In the museum, a mysterious and gloomy exposition opens in the eyes of visitors in the walls that used to be cameras, from which no one came out alive.

Luxury vacation

The city of Cartagena attracts not only those who are interested in history, it is a great place for all lovers of spearfishing and surfing. And everyone who prefers a carefree vacation under the bright sun will be surprised by truly luxurious beaches, the largest and most equipped of which is Bocagrande. Hosting not only tourists, but also local residents, this picturesque place is always crowded, and you need to be prepared for this.





Not far from the city is the beautiful archipelago of Rosario - the only Colombian park located under water and consisting of 38 islands that store pristine beauty. The beautiful Cartagena, reviews about the rest of which is always full of emotions, provides an opportunity to spend rich and fun not only day but also night. Probably nowhere is there such a vibrant club life as here.

Popular tourist route

Cartagena (Colombia) is considered a very calm city in terms of security, and the locals are friendly to all travelers. A wonderful combination of the tropical sun, caressing the sea, colonial buildings that store the courageous and heroic history of an independent city, attracts new tourist flows here every year.
