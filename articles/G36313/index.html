How to find out if there is a loan debt: methods, rules and recommendations of specialists

Today we will try to study with you all the options that will help you understand how to find out if there is a loan debt. This question interests many citizens. Especially those who receive constant notifications that he needs to pay a loan. With all this, he did not receive any loans. How to be in this situation? What to do? Payments

Frankly, there are not many options for the development of events. But few people even guess about them. Can I find out the loan debt? Yes. And the first, most obvious scenario is the receipt of a notice of payment thereof directly to the post office.

That is, it is enough to wait until you receive the appropriate receipt at the postal address. Usually she comes once a month, at the very end. In it, you can easily see the amount of payment and your debt, if any. Among other things, it is worth paying attention to the fact that the size of the penalty payment is also indicated here. Nothing difficult.

Only this is not the most reliable option. Why? Because often payment documents are faked. And citizens become victims of scammers. How can I find out if there really is a loan debt? This is not so difficult to do!

Credit history

For example, you have every right to check your credit history. It is important that the data is yours. In extreme cases - a close relative. Third parties without special authority cannot be verified by this method.

What is required of you? The first thing to find out in which BIC is your data. This is done using a certain service located on the official website of the Bank of Russia. There you enter your data and look for the appropriate office.

Once the case is completed, contact the organization in person. Carry a passport with you. Maybe in electronic format you will also be processed a request for checking debts, but usually this method fails.

Visitors

How to find out if there is a loan debt? Understanding this is not so difficult if you know where to start. The thing is that when a citizen has a desire and time, he has every right to apply to the bank, where he took a loan, and then get all the information he is interested in.



This option is good when you have either a loan agreement or a receipt for payment (even a fake). You apply to your bank with a passport, it gives you information about the existing debt in return. Or about the absence thereof. Nothing difficult. Perhaps this option is in greatest demand. You cannot be deceived in principle. You can also request an account statement. If it is positive, there are no debts.

Court

How do you know if you have a loan debt? Another rather interesting option is to appeal to the judiciary. More precisely, in the FSSP. Both personally and on the Internet. You can check whether you are being dealt with in connection with your existing debt. It is enough to either enter your personal data on the corresponding page on the network and look at the result, or personally contact the organization in your area.

True, this alignment is not always effective. It is relevant in cases where there is a likelihood of a large debt arrears. So do not be surprised if there is no information about you in the FSSP, and payment receipts do not come. It only means that court proceedings have not yet been brought against you . So, you can easily figure out how to find out if there is a loan debt, and then pay it off.

How to pay

Suppose we find out that there is a loan debt. What to do now? It all depends on the situation - if it comes to court, you have to come to the appropriate meeting. So far, is it not so serious? Scammers

Often, after citizens verify in one way or another their debts on loans and credits, it turns out that they become victims of fraudsters. What to do in this case? Especially if threat collectors are already calling you to get money out of you.

There are not so many options for solving the problem. Firstly, you can contact the anti-collection organizations. They will help for a moderate fee to resolve any conflict with the collectors, even if you have debts.

Secondly, just ignore all calls, payments, threats. If you really do not have debt, then ignoring it will not lead to anything dangerous. Most likely, scammers will simply try to intimidate you for extorting money.

Thirdly, if a specific bank is indicated in the receipt, contact there to resolve the issue. As an option - appeal to the court or the police. In any case, if you check your credit history, and you do not have any debts, the option of dealing with fraudsters and injustice is only for you to choose.
