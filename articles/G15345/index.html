"Valley of the Ten Peaks." Moraine - Lake in Canada

Moraine is a lake in Canada, in Banff National Park in Alberta, which is one of the cleanest and most beautiful reservoirs in the world. Hidden in the rocky mountains, it is characterized by clear turquoise water and the incredible beauty of the picturesque natural landscapes surrounding it.

As a rule, it takes about an hour of such a trip to experience everything that the lake has to offer. Although this time is sometimes not enough.

Lots of hiking trails

The local places are a real tourist attraction not only because of the beauty of the mountain landscapes, there are various hiking routes surrounding Moraine, a fairy tale lake. The nearby trail is called Rockpile, its length is approximately 300 meters. Walking along it, you can climb to a height of 24 meters and admire the local beauties from a height. This beautiful landscape was even captured on Canadian 20-dollar bills issued between 1969 and 1979.





A little higher is another trail, 3 km long, walking along which you can see a huge and very picturesque natural stone, acting as a natural dam on Lake Moraine. This area is usually occupied by artists who try to capture the beauty of nature and simply have a fantastic view of the lake on their canvases. Due to bearish activity, some trails have seasonal restrictions in order to maximize safety. Tourists must travel exclusively in groups of four or more.





Moraine Lake: photos

Thanks to its attractive dark blue water surface against the backdrop of towering snow-capped mountains, this incredibly beautiful pond is one of the most popular photographed places in Canada. Those who want to stay longer in this beautiful place can stay in a small hotel house by the lake. It is open from June to early October. There is also a gift shop and restaurant nearby. Canoe rental is possible from 10 am to 6 pm, it costs 50 dollars per hour. You can also rent kayaks.





How to get to Lake Moraine? Tips for those who don't like crowds

In recent years, due to the increase in the flow of tourists in the summer, temporary road restrictions on access to the lake are in effect. As a rule, the winding and narrow road at the entrance to the Moraine Pond (lake in Canada) is filled with tourist buses and vans, so there are very few parking spaces here. To avoid crowding, it is recommended to arrive as early as possible. By 10 a.m. the parking lot is almost full, and places are usually vacated only in the evening, after 5 p.m. An ideal option would be a trip on a weekday.





Especially crowded is Moraine, the lake and its environs, in the second half of September. To get the most out of your trip, you need to plan your trip to the valley in advance. In addition to a personal car, you can use shuttle buses that run every two hours. The first flight leaves at 8:30 in the morning, the last - in the evening, at 18:30. Only cash is accepted. The fare is $ 20 for adults (round trip), for children under 12 years old - $ 10.





There are also other transport options, you can hire a taxi, find a ride or rent a bike, but keep in mind that the distance from Lake Louise to the lake is 14 km, and the first 3 km pass along a steep slope. Due to heavy snowfalls and the possibility of avalanches, the road to Lake Moraine is closed for vehicles in winter.
