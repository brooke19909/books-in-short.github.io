If we talk about a complete reset of all the settings of the device to the factory settings that were pre-installed before being released for sale, this process is commonly called Hard Reset. Software method

So, if we are talking about how to reset the settings on Android, then the first one we can consider is the software method, which consists in using the standard function available in the settings of any Android system. To do this, you will need to enter the settings of your device, select the "Restore and reset" item there, and then click on "Reset Settings". Next, you will see a warning that all available data will be deleted, and a button will appear at the bottom asking for confirmation of your decision. Pressing the "Erase All" button will confirm the selected action. In systems of earlier versions, that is, prior to 2.1, the reset is located at a different address: in the "Privacy" section, where there is a "Reset Data" item.

Difficult case

Often the question arises of how to reset the settings on the "Android" if the smartphone has ceased to respond to any commands from the user. This method will help. When turning on the device, you should hold down three buttons at the same time: "Power", "Home" and "Volume". This combination should be held until the "Recovery" mode is turned on. In it you will need to find and select the section called "Wipe", and then press the "Home" button to confirm the selection.

What other problems are there?
