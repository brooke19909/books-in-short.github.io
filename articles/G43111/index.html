Why go to Sharjah?





Sharjah is an emirate located in the east of the United Arab Emirates. This is an ancient settlement founded more than five thousand years ago.

At the beginning of the 19th century, it was in Sharjah that the historic signing of the maritime treaty between Sheikh Sultan I and the British Empire took place. After that, these territories became part of the English protectorate called Treaty of Oman.

Sharjah became a part of the United Arab Emirates only in 1971, when Sheikh Khalid III signed the relevant agreement on the joining of the emirate to the UAE. This region is of particular interest to lovers of philately. The fact is that Sharjah is widely known in the world of postage stamps. The local postal ministry issued a huge number of stamps shortly before the founding of the UAE. However, most of them have never been to the countries whose names they bear. Therefore, they are not included in international catalogs, and stamps themselves are considered a rarity among philatelists.

Christians also want to go to Sharjah. Indeed, most recently, in 2011, the first Orthodox church in the United Arab Emirates was built here. Today it is a special attraction in these places.

What to see in Sharjah?





Sharjah itself is located on the coast of the Persian (or Arab) Gulf. Here is a warm sea, on the banks of which it is pleasant to relax almost all year round.

The real gem of this part of the country is the place Jebel Faya, which is about an hour drive from the Hotel Citymax Sharjah 3 * in Sharjah . Photos of finds that archaeologists have discovered here, instantly flew around the planet.

Scientists at the University of Tubingen found unique stone primitive tools. They were used by ancient people 100-125 thousand years ago. Their age was established using a special study. There, archaeologists discovered artifacts that date back to the Neolithic, Bronze and even Iron Ages. At the same time, it was not possible to find the bones of people. Why? This is one of the mysteries of Jebel Faya.
