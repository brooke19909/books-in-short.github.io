Sights of Kostroma: photos with names and descriptions, what to see, interesting facts and reviews of tourists

Kostroma is an old Russian city. Its name is associated with the house of the imperial Romanov family. It is the official residence of the Snow Maiden, the main assistant to the winter wizard. It is a quiet and provincial town. His founding father is Yuri Dolgoruky.

Almost all the sights of Kostroma are concentrated on the banks of the Volga. The town is replete with ancient churches, monuments of wooden architecture, museums and picturesque corners of the northern nature of Russia.

How to get there

Regular buses run from Moscow and St. Petersburg to Kostroma. The northern capital is connected with the city by air. The local railway station receives trains coming from the central regions of the country. The roads leading to the village are wide and even. They run through the most beautiful places of the northern possessions of Russia.

Arbor Ostrovsky

This place is considered to be a symbol of the city. The arbor rises on the picturesque bank of the Volga. Her skeleton rests on a sand embankment, which is part of an ancient settlement. It is believed that this is the best observation deck of Kostroma. Her visit is highly recommended for those who decide to explore the most important sights of Kostroma in one day.

The gazebo was built after the death of Alexander Nikolaevich Ostrovsky. The official opening date of the monument is 1956. Its architectural ensemble is represented by a colonnade, which supports the convex dome of the roof. Similar designs adorned the gardens of Russian estates. If you believe the description of the sights of Kostroma, then Alexander Nikolaevich really often visited these places.

On the way to the city, he asked to stop the crew. I went out and enjoyed the unusually beautiful panorama of the river bed. Here he drew his inspiration. Arbor Ostrovsky is located on the 1st of May.

Epiphany Monastery

At the moment, the temple is closed to public. Only the chapel is accessible to tourists. It is considered the oldest architectural element of the complex. A stone chapel was erected. Date of construction - XVI century. The Epiphany monastery was regularly plundered. Polovtsians visited him.

To protect against barbarians, high and powerful walls were installed around the temple. It was possible to save only the main premises of the Epiphany Cathedral and a separate hall for meals. Queues are sometimes formed near the shrines of the temple. The entrance to the monastery is from Simanovsky Street. Walking distance to public transport. Fire Tower

The construction is located on Simanovsky Street. In its main room is the Fire Museum. Today, Kalancha is under the protection of UNESCO. Date of construction - 1825. Kostroma Governor Karl Ivanovich Baumgarten supervised the construction. Kalancha rises near the central square of the city.

This building is designed in antique style and decorated with six porticoes. The infrastructure of the tower consisted of firefighters' relaxation rooms, living rooms, storage facilities for barrels, parking lots for cars, an observation deck. When a fire or smoke was detected, the attendant rang the bell. The construction of the tower was preceded by a series of fires.
