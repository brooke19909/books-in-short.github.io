Master class: how to make an origami owl

Origami craft, the owl, can be done in two ways. The first is simple, using one piece of paper. The second is more complex. As a result, you get an owl from the modules. We suggest you familiarize yourself with these two methods.

  2. Flatten the paper.

We make modules for owl 3D

To make a voluminous owl in the origami technique, you need to prepare blanks called modules.





How to assemble a module (master class):

  1. Take a small piece of paper.
  2. Fold the sheet in half in length (Figure 1).
  3. Fold the rectangle in half again (Figure 2).
  4. Spread the figure.
  5. Wrap the right and left parts of the rectangle to the formed fold line (Figure 3).
  6. Turn over the part (Figure 4).
  7. Cut off the bottom edges.
  8. Fold the edges as shown in Figure 5.
  9. Fold the figure in half (Figure 6).

To create an owl using the modular origami technique, you need a lot of such blanks. You also need to consider what color your craft will be. Maybe you want to make it not monophonic and highlight the muzzle and tummy.

Owl: modular origami

Any such craft begins with the fact that the first row is going to:

Example

Where your imagination and possibilities lead, this will turn out to be an owl. Modular origami, the assembly scheme of which can be very simple or vice versa - very complex, gives unlimited opportunities for creativity.





For example, you can make such a craft - an owl with ears, as in the picture above. To do this, take only two colors to create modules. When creating the first two rows, already consider that your tummy will have a highlight. Therefore, first assemble several modules of color A, and then add color B. So, completely connect the figure resembling an egg. On the sides at the top at the end, attach a pair of models that will represent the ears. And cut out the eyes from the paper and glue it on the finished craft.

Take a look at the picture below.





As you can see, you can make a more complex owl, which will have a separate body and head. First you need to make modules of different colors. For example, black, white, brown, orange and red. Then collect the lower part of the owl - the body. Alternate modules by color. White is the tummy, brown is the plumage, and orange is the edge of the wings.

When the body is ready, you need to collect your head. Here you need to highlight the eyes with white and black modules, and make a beak out of three red ones. Then just put your head on the body. All! The modular craft is ready!

You can also make a 3D owl with spread wings. But this is more difficult work, since during assembly of the main part you need to make protrusions on the sides. They are then assembled from additional modules wings.
