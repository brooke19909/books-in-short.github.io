Itkul - lake in Khakassia

From the very beginning it should be noted that the name Itkul has lakes in the Chelyabinsk, Novosibirsk regions and in Khakassia. Apart from the name, common to them is freshwater. Itkul is a toponym, that is, the name of a geographical object. In Russia there are 10 of them - villages, villages, towns, railway stations. There are four lakes, one of which, Bolshoi Itkul, is located in the Chelyabinsk region. There are similar names that differ by just one letter, and also toponyms - in the Siberian Federal District there is a natural reservoir Utkul, and not Lake Itkul. Altai Krai has two more villages, a river and a recreation center called Utkul, and is completely devoid of any geographical feature known as Itkul.

Geographic data

The deepest part of the reservoir reaches almost 17 m with an average depth of about 7. The total length of the coastline is 15 km. The height above sea level on which the lake is located is 273 meters. Itkul is a lake with a rocky and steep eastern shore and more or less gentle southwest. The opposite southeast has a section of rocks, very interesting in the composition of minerals - there are large crystals (up to 10 cm) of black hornblende.

Garnets and chrome spinels often come across. Particularly noteworthy are the beaches of the southeastern coast with white sand, in which sometimes quite large garnet crystals gleam, which is why they are called "pomegranate beaches". The lake is surrounded by mountains (the highest of them Kapabayka reaches 544 m above sea level) and beautiful dense forests. At the foot of the mountain runs a small river of the same name. Archaeological treasury

They also call it the "Second Turgoyak with the shores of Arkaim." Turgoyak is also a large freshwater lake located in the same area, near the city of Miass, and also a natural monument. Arkaim is a large archaeological center.

With a fortified settlement of the Middle Bronze Age, all located in the same Chelyabinsk region, Itkul is related by archaeological finds discovered in many places on the coastline. From them it can be judged that in the III millennium BC, blacksmithing, pottery and other types of crafts flourished here. And man lived on these shores in the 7th millennium BC. Not a single archaeological expedition returned empty-handed, the most valuable finds were always brought, and on the shore numerous traces of ancient settlements remain discovered.

Tourist paradise

Itkul is a lake that attracts thousands of tourists. Forests and hills, beaches and clear water, an abundance of fish and wonderful air - such beauty could not go unnoticed, and people came here. At first, the inhabitants of the city of Upper Ufaley, located 20 km away, came to rest, now the reservoir has turned into a tourist attraction.
