Alexandria - the capital of Egypt

Due to its territorial location, Egypt very attracted conquerors, because of which changes took place in the country and new capitals appeared, disappearing with time. After the Assyrians, the country was conquered by the Greeks and Thracians, led by Alexander the Great.

The rule of Egypt was entrusted by Macedonian to his friend Ptolemy. In gratitude, Ptolemy decided to perpetuate the name of a friend and founded a new city. Alexandria - the capital of Egypt - became the center of world culture of that time. Ptolemy's successors succeeded each other. Here the great Cleopatra ruled, enchanting Caesar with her beauty and mind. After the conquest of the Greeks, Egypt became a province of Rome, and then for a long time was part of Byzantium.

The rise of cairo

Egypt was not an Arab country. A new round in the history of Egypt began with the advent of Islam. The Arabs, having conquered Egyptian lands from Byzantium, introduced the Arabic language and Islam. The ancient capital of Egypt at that time was in the city of Al Fustat. But he could not maintain this status for a long time. After the collapse of the Arab Caliphate , the new Fatimid state was founded, which was located in the west of North Africa. The Fatimids conquered Egypt and founded the city of Al-Qahira, which became the capital of the country. The name of the city in translation means "city of victory." So in 969, Cairo was founded - the capital of Egypt at the present time.



Cairo is not just a big city, but a huge and densely populated metropolis. More than 14 million people live in this city. In the architecture of Cairo, there is a mixture of various styles, from ancient to the most modern.
