Meteorite composition

From numerous studies, scientists managed to get an idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe composition of the "alien". It is officially announced that the Goba meteorite (Namibia, 1920) consists of 84% iron, 15% nickel with impurities of cobalt. About 1% is due to impurities of other elements. The top layer consists of iron hydroxide. The crystalline structure is defined as nickel-containing ataxite.

Thus, the Goba meteorite is attributed to iron. For reference, we add that according to the classification, meteorites are divided into 3 types, based on their composition:

  1. Meteorites from mineral materials are called stone.
  2. Meteorites from metals are called siderite or iron.
  3. "Aliens" of mixed materials are called iron-stone.

Classification helps to group samples by common origin. A meteorite substance can be part of a planet, an asteroid or satellite, any object of the solar system that exists at the moment or existed in the past. But this classification is not yet final, it can and will expand.





Mysteries of the Goba: where is the crater?

A huge meteorite threw scientists a number of puzzles. One of them is the absence of a crater. For some reason, the space guest landed so softly that he was able to keep in shape and not scatter into a pile of fragments. There was no disaster during the fall, and there was no crater left. Although a small crater could appear and then collapse over time. It is possible that the fall occurred at a very small angle.

Another mystery is the unique form.

The Goba meteorite has a very unusual shape. A huge block looks like an almost regular box. Fragments of objects of the solar system of this form fell to Earth extremely rarely, and they were much smaller than the giant Goba.

Scientists are surprised not only by the shape, but also by the external surface texture of the meteorite. The alien is smooth, and its surface is almost flat. Initially, the color of the cosmic body was blue-black, but the Earth‚Äôs atmosphere contains carbon dioxide, and the native iron that the meteorite consists of has become red.
