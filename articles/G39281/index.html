What are the main forms of origami called?

Origami art is finding new fans. Many people around the world develop this art and develop new schemes, expand opportunities. And there are more schemes for paper crafts every year. At the very beginning of the development of this art form, the basic forms of origami were called as follows:

  • bird;
  • frog;
  • fish;
  • kite.

Now there are a lot of blanks, including those that were already invented in the West, and not in the homeland of origami in Japan. It is very important to learn the simplest shapes: triangle, square, pancake, double square, fish, frog. If you go further, you will need to learn the workpiece "catamaran" and "bird", from which most kusuda (balls) are made.

How to make a triangle shape?

To create any blank you need only colored paper of sufficient density, a ruler and scissors. And besides, a little patience is needed.

From the basic forms, all world-famous origami crafts are built. They will also come in handy when you perform modular (modular) origami. The basic shape of the "triangle" is the easiest and most common.





To make it, you just need to cut an even square out of a sheet of paper and bend it diagonally. A finger is well held over each fold so that the paper does not protrude.

Some flowers, simple animals from paper, which are sure to please children, are made from the ‚Äútriangle‚Äù blank.

Square Shape (Double Square)

As you can conclude, even the schoolchild will master the simplest, basic forms of origami. Any interested person will be able to understand how this or that object is built. We recommend that you purchase a book and view in color schemes all the basic forms performed in the origami technique. "Square" is one of these forms. And just like the "triangle", it is created as a part for all kinds of figures more complicated.

"Double square" is used to create boxes and various colors. To make a square, follow this instruction:

  • cut an even square;
  • bend diagonally 2 times in both directions;
  • a vertex is formed at the intersection of the diagonal lines, you need to fold the paper so that this vertex is the common angle of two square planes.




If you bend up the free corners of the squares, you get another double triangle.

The faster a person learns to add basic forms of origami, the more interesting he is to develop further in this direction.

  • Having made a deflection, remove these corners in your pockets so that they are not visible from above.
