Forte dei Marmi, Italy: reviews and itinerary

Tuscany, the birthplace of the great Leonardo da Vinci, Dante, Michelangelo, is famous all over the world for its historical and architectural ensembles, wonderful wines, the best olive oil and amazing resorts. One of the best is Forte dei Marmi. Italy has long been chosen by the Russian elite. But it is in this resort that she owns more than 30% of mansions and villas. This resort is in every way for those who like to relax royally. Let's take a short walk along its streets, the coast, take a look at some hotels and shops.



Description of the city

Forte dei Marmi is located on the Tyrrhenian Sea in the Versilia region of Lucca. It is very beautiful, drowning in evergreen vegetation, fragrant with flowers and the sea, ringing with wonderful fountains. But the town is so small that many call it a village. In the center is a pretty square, from which, like the rays of the sun in a children's drawing, clean and in every way pleasant streets diverge. One of them is called Roma Imperiale. It is here that you can see the mansions of many Russian nouveau riche. On the perimeter of the square are concentrated prestigious expensive boutiques and restaurants, and the further from it, the cheaper and simpler. On the outskirts of the city there is a good and quite affordable supermarket. There is also a train station in Forte dei Marmi, which is only 3.5 km from the center. You can get there on foot (about 40 minutes), by taxi and by bus (every hour). From the station, trains leave for many Italian cities - Milan, Pisa, Genoa and others. But most vacationers ride cars and bicycles. They are offered for rent in offices located directly on the streets.



Location

An important question for those who have chosen Forte dei Marmi as their vacation spot: "How to get there?" There are several options - by plane, by train, by road and by boat. The nearest major international airport, which bears the name of Galileo Galilei, is located in Pisa. From it to the resort 20 km (counting from the city borders). At 5 km there is another small airport with grassy runways. The planes there are only sightseeing. By car, Forte dei Marmi can be reached from anywhere in Europe. In this way, many from Russia come. Drive along the A12 towards Versilia signpost, then turn right. Having followed the third traffic light, turn again, but to the left, and move already to the city itself. Indicative figures: from Lucca to the resort 30 km, from Milan 250, from Florence 80, from Rome 300. There is a separate route from each city. From Milan, this is A1, which is followed to Fidenza, then go to A15 to La Spezia and then to A12. From Florence, take the A11 to Lucca, then turn onto Viareggio. From Rome follow the A1 to the city of Florence, and then follow the above pattern. The port is located in Viareggio, in the same place as the railway station. Restaurants

Forte dei Marmi is a very expensive resort. Accordingly, restaurants are not cheap here. The menu is different in all, but the general background of the dishes is pasta, pasta (the same pasta, only small ones), fish, seafood, vegetables and pizza. Excellent wines are offered everywhere, the cost is very different, from inexpensive on average 30-50 euros per bottle to elite, for which they ask from 500 euros. The restaurant Gilda is considered the most prestigious and most iconic, where the choice of dishes is somewhat limited, but the taste is excellent. The second most popular restaurant is Bistrot.

With kind words, note the small family restaurant Enoteca Brilliant, which is located on the outskirts of the resort. The prices there are lower, but the service and food are second to none. All food service points in Forte dei Marmi are open in the morning from 12-30 to 14-30, and in the evening - from 19-00 until the last customer, although there are also those that close at 23-00. In some restaurants you have to pay up to 6 euros just for sitting at a table.

Hotels Forte dei Marmi

Here, most hotels with 5 and 4 stars. Three stars are very few. When choosing, you must be interested in beach conditions, since you have to pay for them. Pools are a good alternative, but not every hotel has them. Room prices are very different, from 250 to 2000 euros for 7 nights in a standard room for two people without children. Among the three-star hotels, Hotel Franceschi, Hotel Bijou, La Pace enjoy a good reputation. Among four-star hotels, Villa Roma Imperiale, St Mauritius Hotel, Hotel Goya and many others receive warm reviews. Five-star resort hotels offer exceptional service, chic rooms, excellent service. Very popular Principe Forte dei Marmi, located on the very shore of the blue sea, Grand Hotel Imperiale, opened in 2007, Augustus Hotel & Resort is one of the oldest and most respectable. In addition to the hotel, the resort can rent a villa for a period of 1 month.



Historical reference

Many parts of Italy boast interesting historical values. Tourists are always attracted by Rome, Milan, Pisa, Florence. Forte dei Marmi also has something to show. It dates back to 1516, when a pier was built to send the marble mined in the area. This material was extremely in demand, so the matter developed, and a town gradually grew around the fort. These places, impressive in their extraordinary beauty, have been looked after by the Italian nobility. Here they began to build chic villas for a rural holiday. This continued until the Soviet perestroika, after which there were very wealthy Russians who began to develop this resort and actively buy real estate here. Gradually, this place became elite.



sights

The patron saint of Forte dei Marmi is Saint Hermes (Sant'Ermete), whose church is located on one of the main streets. In honor of this great martyr, a big celebration takes place every August 28th. It is worth taking a look at the pedestrian bridge, formerly a pier. Of interest are the remains of a defensive fortress, a museum of humor and satire, ancient marble warehouses, and Puccini's villa. In the vicinity of the resort there are also interesting architectural structures. But the most popular are hiking in the Apuan Alps, which offer fantastic views of the coast and the surroundings of Forte dei Marmi. Entertainment

For those who do not like outdoor activities, Forte dei Marmi offers shopping trips and restaurants. For everyone else, there are plenty of opportunities to have a great time. The resort has the opportunity to play golf, tennis, windsurf, diving, sailing, mountain climbing, horseback riding, and attend a hippodrome. The city‚Äôs nightlife is also vibrant and diverse. There are lots of nightclubs offering the most diverse program. Among cultural events, St. Hermes Day, the Puccini Festival, the Film Festival are especially popular, and in the summer famous music groups often perform.

There are statements about insufficiently clean water on the beaches, as well as Negro traders who interfere with rest. In the restaurants they cook very tasty, in hotels they try their best to please, on the streets there is beauty and perfect cleanliness, and there are such breathtaking landscapes around.
