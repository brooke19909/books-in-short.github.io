Which air conditioner is better to choose for an apartment? Reviews and Tips

Split systems

Such air conditioners have gained the greatest popularity. The standard design includes two units: internal (evaporative) and external (compressor and condenser). The undoubted advantages of split systems are the following:

  • low noise level during the work;
  • the ability to choose a model with a suitable type of indoor unit and the most comfortable location;
  • efficiency.

Which air conditioner is better to choose for an apartment? User reviews describing various models of split systems note the pros and cons of wall, floor, ceiling, cassette, channel and column models. However, universal options are gaining popularity - that is, air conditioners that make it possible to install the indoor unit in the right position.

Multisplit systems

Such air conditioners can be considered an improved version of the previous type. Their distinguishing feature is the ability to use not one indoor unit, but several (from 2 or more). Moreover, you can combine blocks of different types (ceiling, floor, wall, etc.). The number of external units does not increase.

Mobile air conditioners

Another type of device that can not be ignored, talking about which air conditioner is better to choose for an apartment. Customer reviews of these models are mostly positive. But it is important to consider that mobile air conditioners are suitable only for small rooms - their power is not high enough to cool large areas and, as a rule, does not exceed 4 kW. But such additional devices do not require any additional installation.





Window air conditioners

If saving money is a priority for you, then such a choice will, without a doubt, be optimal. Such monoblock models are simply mounted in window openings or in thin walls. Low cost and ease of installation are the undoubted advantages that window air conditioners have. However, they also have their drawbacks: so, you will not have much freedom in terms of choosing the location of the device. In addition, most window air conditioners work quite noisily, and are more suitable for offices and public buildings, rather than apartments.

Choosing a model with the right features

Speaking about which air conditioner is better for an apartment, it is necessary to pay attention not only to the type of system itself, but also to its technical characteristics:

  • cooling power (for every 10 m 2 of the room, on average, 1 kW of power should fall);
  • the presence of additional modes and functions (choice of air flow direction, additional cleaning and drying of air, the presence of automatic and night modes, access to a controlled timer);
  • presence of a remote control for easier control of the system.

Popular manufacturers

Which air conditioner is better? In fact, many manufacturers produce cooling systems. They are conditionally divided into three main classes:

  • elite air conditioners (Daikin, Hitachi, General, Panasonic, Mitsubishi, Toshiba);
  • middle class air conditioners (include cheaper models of the above manufacturers, as well as products of Fuji, Sanyo, Fujitsu);
  • economy class equipment (Airwell, Samsung, LG, Tadiran, Gree, Veko).

In no case do we urge to buy exclusively expensive elite equipment. On the contrary, practice shows that economy-class air conditioners very efficiently cope with their tasks and can serve their owners for more than one year.
