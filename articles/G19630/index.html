South African national currency - rand

In the state of South Africa, the status of the national currency is South African rand. In addition, these banknotes are used in other countries that are part of the single currency zone with this republic. It is about Lesotho and Swaziland. The Namibian dollar is still attached to the South African rand. The fact is that until 1993, Namibia was also a member of the monetary union.

Currency Unit Overview

In the international banking system, the currency of South Africa is labeled ZAR. The currency includes one hundred cents. Today, banknotes in denominations of ten, twenty, fifty, one hundred and two hundred rand are used in operations. Along with this, there are one, two, five, ten, twenty, fifty cents and one, two and 5 rand coins in circulation. It will be useful to say that coins of one and two cents have not been made since 2002, but they still participate in operations legally.

Currency design





Today, the currency of South Africa is framed using images of the so-called Big Five animals of Africa. So, on the obverse of a ten-rand bill there is the head of a white rhinoceros, on twenty rand there is an African elephant, on fifty rand there is a lion's head, one hundred rand on the obverse contain a buffalo, and two hundred rand contains a leopard. The back of the banknotes is framed using images of a variety of natural landscapes or industrial facilities. In February 2012, the South African leadership, represented by President Jacob Zuma, announced the launch of a new type of banknote. On them is a portrait of the first black ruler of the Republic, Nelson Mandela.





On the obverse of the coins of South Africa are the state emblem, year of manufacture, as well as the name of the state, inscribed in one of the local dialects. These include Ndebele, Tsonga, Swazi, Soto and Scythe. In addition, inscriptions in Afrikaans and English are used. On the opposite side, various representatives of the local fauna and flora are minted. For example, at one cent, the South African currency contains two sparrows sitting on a branch of a mimosa, at two cents you can see an image of an African screaming eagle, and at five cents there is a crane of paradise. In addition, a calla flower is displayed at ten cents, a twenty-cent coin contains the image of a royal protea flower, which is considered one of the main national emblems of South Africa. Also worth noting is a fifty-cent coin, one, two and five metal rands. They respectively depict royal strelitzia, springbok antelope, kudu antelope and wildebeest.
