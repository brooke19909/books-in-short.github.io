What architectures exist?

Rostelecom FTTx technology can work in one of several types of architectures:

- FTTN (Fiber to the Node) - the optical fiber is laid to the network node;

- FTTC (Fiber to the Curb) - the optical fiber is pulled to a neighborhood, block or several houses;

- FTTB (Fiber to the Building) - the optical fiber enters the building;

- FTTH (Fiber to the Home) - optical fiber is laid to the home.

Their difference consists only in the proximity of the optical cable to the user terminal. The first two solutions came first. The first of them is used now, but only as a budget and quickly implemented where there is already a copper distribution infrastructure, and laying an optical cable is simply unprofitable. Everyone knows the difficulties that accompany such a solution: poor quality of services associated with the special properties of copper cables, limitation on the number of connections and speed in one cable. The second type is an improved and modified version of the first, which does not have these drawbacks. The laying of copper cables in this case is carried out exclusively inside the buildings, so they are not subject to all sorts of destructive factors, while the length of the lines is not so great. All these factors make it possible to achieve an improvement in speed in a section that does not use fiber. A similar offer applies if FTTx xPON is used. This is aimed at ADSL operators, as well as cable television operators. The implementation of such an architecture gives them the opportunity for significant savings while increasing the number of connected users. Each of them will be allocated a large bandwidth. Most often in Russia, this type is used by small network operators.

To do this, you need to place an FTTx router on each device.

The network must be planned so that the endpoint is as close to the subscriber as possible. The construction of new areas is most correctly accompanied by bringing it directly to the building, which will be convenient both at the beginning and in the future.
