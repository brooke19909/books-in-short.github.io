Cultural and entertainment complex "National Village" in Orenburg

Orenburg is a unique city located at the junction of Asia and Europe. Since time immemorial, representatives of various nationalities and nationalities have lived here side by side. And thanks to this fact, the history and culture of the region boasts brightness and versatility. To learn more about the traditions of the most numerous ethnic groups living in these places, a new cultural and entertainment complex - the "National Village" in Orenburg will help.





Symbol of friendship of peoples and peaceful neighborhood

Orenburg is located near the state border. That is why in the region, representatives of different nationalities and religious denominations coexist safely. The Orenburg region has common borders with the republics of Kazakhstan and Bashkortostan, as well as the Samara and Chelyabinsk regions. Representatives of 119 nationalities live in the region, and the doors of a huge number of religious centers belonging to 20 faiths are always open for parishioners. The idea to tell more about the culture and traditions of various peoples arose in 2004. The National Village complex in Orenburg opened in 2008 and immediately became incredibly popular with tourists and residents.





Description of the cultural and entertainment center

For the convenience of visitors, the park is divided into ten zones, here they are called courtyards. Each of them is dedicated to a separate state and its people. The center of the National Village is the Friendship fountain, symbolizing the unity and brotherhood of all the peoples of the earth. This object is effectively highlighted in the dark, due to which the complex looks even more elegant and beautiful. Near the fountain there is an information stand with a detailed layout of the courtyards and general information about the cultural center. Many museums in Orenburg tell about the history of the region and the rich national composition of the population. But no other exposition can boast of such visibility and immersion in the material being studied, such as "National Village".





Each hut has its own rattles

There are ten courtyards in the open-air museum. And each of them is a complex of various buildings with the appropriate design of the adjacent territory. Russian, Tatar, Ukrainian, Bashkir, Kazakh, Chuvash, Mordovian, Armenian, Belarusian and German - all these are the courtyards in the "National Village". Orenburg actually boasts a more magnificent national composition, but only the most numerous nations are represented in the complex. Each compound has its own museum, an example of a traditional residential building, a cafe or restaurant, a souvenir shop, as well as a library and a publishing house that produces a newspaper in the "local" language. In fact, the complex is Orenburg in miniature. Sights and interesting objects

The creators of the cultural and entertainment complex sought to make not only a beautiful copy of national courtyards, but also quite reliable. The buildings are made in traditional techniques. For example, in a Russian village you can see a wooden temple, during the construction of which not a single nail was used. The "National Village" in Orenburg visually acquaints guests with the architectural styles of different peoples and traditional handicrafts. It is simply impossible to visit the entire open-air museum in one day and participate in a tasting of all cuisines. Tourists from other regions can stay right in the "National Village". The doors of the Ukrainian hotel "Nadezhda" are always open for guests; there are rooms in other national farmsteads.





Excursion program

In the "National Village" is never boring. Various festivals and concerts are regularly held here. Even on ordinary weekdays, there are enough representatives of various nationalities in traditional costumes on the territory of the complex, thanks to which the courtyards look truly residential and cozy. Local restaurants and cafes offer romantic evenings, corporate parties and personal celebrations. Be sure to have a snack in one of the national institutions. According to many residents of Orenburg, local restaurants are some of the best in the city. Do not forget to visit the local mini-museums. Exposure, of course, is not too large, but incredibly interesting. You can see national musical instruments, art objects, dishes and household items in them. Some collections may be envied by the state museums of Orenburg.





"National Village" in Orenburg: address and current information for visitors

An open-air museum is open daily. You can visit it without an appointment. The exact address of the attraction: Orenburg, st. Altai, 3. To organize a banquet or to book a room in a hotel, contact the administration of the courtyard of interest. The "National Village" in Orenburg will delight guests with reasonable prices. Keep in mind that there is a symbolic fee for entry to some museums (up to 100 rubles per person), the order in the restaurant is paid according to the menu, you can buy souvenirs in specialized shops. Photo and video shooting is free in the whole complex (if tourists have the equipment).
