Secret cities of Russia

In our country there are 23 closed cities. 10 of them belong to the "atomic" (Rosatom), 13 - to the Ministry of Defense, in whose jurisdiction 32 ZATOs with settlements. Closed formations of an administrative type are under a special protection regime. The activity of industrial enterprises and military facilities in an isolated territory is a state secret.

Cities of the closed type (ZG) in the USSR were classified and were not indicated on any map. The population was attributed to the nearest regional centers. The numbering of bus routes, houses and institutions was not carried out from the beginning, but continued to be introduced in the regional cities, which included ZATO. For example, school number 64 in Sverdlovsk-45 (now Lesnoy).

Arrivals were screened at the checkpoint. A single pass, a travel order gave the right to enter. Persons registered in a closed city or town had permanent passes. It was mandatory to give a non-disclosure subscription, its violation could even lead to criminal liability.

Privileges of ZG residents

The state compensated for the difficulties of living in an isolated facility with benefits and privileges. High-level supply made it possible to purchase goods in stores that were scarce for other citizens of the country. Everyone, regardless of their field of activity, was charged a 20% salary bonus. The social sphere, medicine, and education were well developed.

Many secret cities of Russia today are surrounded by rows of walls with barbed wire. The right to enter can be obtained if a local resident applies for a permit to a relative, but kinship must be proved. You can get to sporting events in some ZATOs according to your passport.

Now not in all closed cities there are fences and checkpoints, in some they are not guarded. It depends on the privacy mode. Sarov, the former Arzamas-16, is under serious protection: rows of barbed wire, control strip, modern tracking equipment, and vehicle screening.

The total population of ZATO is more than a million people. Almost every 100th citizen of the Russian Federation lives in a closed city or village.

Invisible Cities Today

The collapse of the USSR put the closed cities of Russia not just in difficult conditions, but on the brink of extinction. R&D funding was discontinued with a drop in demand, and there were no more privileges granted to secret facilities. The decline in production due to the narrow production profile was inevitable. People with high qualifications began to receive "pennies" at best, at worst - they were left without work.

The market dictated its conditions. The presence of orders for mass production did not help create jobs, but led to unemployment. It was an order of magnitude higher in closed cities than in Russia. By the end of 1995, 20% of the population were ‚Äúwithout work‚Äù in ZATO. The unique potential of the intellectual elite, scientists, designers turned out to be unclaimed.

The problem of ‚Äúbrain drain‚Äù was acute, which did not pass without a trace. There is US intelligence data about former experts of closed cities developing nuclear weapons for Brazil, Libya, Iran.

A more significant problem was the "retention" of personnel to prevent possible disasters and preserve technology. In 1998, tax benefits were introduced for businesses in ZATO. New firms created jobs. Since 2000, the benefits have been partially canceled, and in 2004 they stopped completely.

The secret cities of Russia today still stand out from the ordinary. The sphere of culture, medicine, and education are developed. Clean streets drowning in green spaces and flower beds, architectural ensembles. High-class specialists still work here: nuclear scientists, engineers, and designers. They know how to work with super-modern technologies, but, unfortunately, most of them are not engaged in scientific work. So, without the support of the state and big business, the unique potential of closed cities is draining away drop by drop.
