Ecology and tourism

Clean air, beautiful landscapes, vineyards and orchards - all this is provincial France. Annecy is the most environmentally friendly corner of the country. Tales of an unusual turquoise lake is not an advertising ploy. The water is really amazingly blue due to the special composition of phytoplankton.

A strict ban on the discharge of industrial and municipal wastewater into the reservoir was introduced in 1960. Since then, the popularity of the lake as a beach holiday destination has significantly increased. They come to Annecy all year round, but most tourists prefer the summer period. In the quiet provincial part of eastern France, the spirit of medieval romance still hovers. Annecy is a network of narrow cobbled streets, canals and bridges. Three rivers flow through the old part, some houses stand directly on the water, which served as a reason for comparing the city with Venice. Tourists enjoy walking along the lake and around the city.
