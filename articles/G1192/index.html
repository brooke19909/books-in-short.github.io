Acoustics floor JBL: reviews

A wide audience of music lovers, JBL audio maker is well known for portable speakers. Mobile players seamlessly combine ergonomics, functionality and reliability. But at the same time they do not allow revealing the full spectrum of the frequency series with full depth and detail. The traditional JBL floor acoustics format is less popular, because in this niche, American developers have to compete with brands like Yamaha, DALI and Heco. Nevertheless, a number of proprietary developments, coupled with the concept of balanced sound, make this model line attractive. However, floor acoustics JBL is also valued for other qualities, and a wide selection of products allows you to take into account the smallest details and characteristics of systems.





Features of floor systems JBL

If in the development of portable and shelf systems, audio engineers have to connect optimization schemes to organize compact speaker placement, then floor models are designed without restrictions. Most representatives of this line have a massive structure with high-quality insulation and modern finishes. In particular, JBL floor acoustics received a metal edging and speakers with a soft texture. In the Studio 270 modification, a group of front speakers up to 165 mm in size are combined with diffusers made of PolyPlas material.

As for sound, the company seeks to achieve the level of studio recording, and in some cases cinema systems. This task is achieved through tweeters with minimal distortion and a horn HDI system. The floor acoustics JBL is distinguished by its wide communication capabilities. Interfaces are represented by gold-plated connectors in a standard format, and in some versions there is also a screw port for interfacing with a powerful amplifier.

E80 Model Reviews





Users of this system note the evenness of the tonal balance of the sound with little emphasis on the lower range. The bass speaker produces dense and deep bass, which is typical for almost all representatives of the JBL floor range. Therefore, the kit is often used to work with rock music and electronic compositions. Moreover, the ability to control the bass volume is not limited by failures. Across the entire spectrum of adjustment, owners note a stable bass exposure. There are also few complaints regarding vocals - the JBL E80 floor acoustics optimally reveals the potential of solo performers, regardless of their movement around the stage. Unless at increased volume there are comments on dry playback. In general, the owners of the system recommend it specifically for modern dynamic music, which needs a pronounced emphasis on bass.

Studio 280 model reviews





This is a powerful phase inverter system that allows you to confidently process the spectrum of 40-22 000 Hz when connected to an amplifier with a power of about 200 watts. The advantages of the speakers include acceptable sound and implementation of the finish. And if the sound against the background of other representatives of the family is at a strong average level, then the classic design of the design is admired by many. The JBL Arena 180 floor acoustics makes a similar impression, but it has a more modern look. At home, the system creates the feeling of being at a concert. This effect is noted by users who associate speakers with a powerful amplifier like the Pioneer A-10-K. The output is a sensitive signal processing with bright high and medium frequencies. The lower spectrum, however, is characterized by blur.

Arena 180 model reviews





The model is a continuation of the successful Arena 170 complex, but with obvious advantages. According to users, this equipment outperforms the previous generation due to its high sensitivity, power and detail. But here also weaknesses appear that the JBL Arena 180 floor acoustics sin. Reviews indicate large speaker sizes, so this option is hardly suitable for a modest room. But you can be sure of the reliability of the design. The manufacturer used high-quality MDF panels, which also contribute their stylistic connotation to the design.

How much is floor acoustics JBL?

The range of floor JBL speakers offers models costing an average of 10 to 30 thousand rubles. In this case, two price bars can be distinguished. The first are Arena 180 and E80 systems, costing about 13-15 thousand, and the second is the Studio 280 model, available for 30-33 thousand. But there are more substantial offers in the company's assortment. So, the professional complex in modification 4429 is estimated at 200 thousand. For this money, the user receives titanium horns, a 800 Hz crossover and the ability to reproduce frequencies in the corridor from 40 to 45 000 Hz. Budget JBL floor acoustics, of course, cannot boast of such advantages, but developers are responsible for this category as well. The same home system E80 is provided with high-quality components and no less worthy acoustic "stuffing".





Conclusion

The implementation of JBL phase-inverted floor speakers is based on the principles of versatility, reliability and good sound. Deep construction of the music scene with a detailed study is the corporate identity of the company. In different models, this concept manifests itself in its own way, but in most cases, users note naturalness and purity of sound at the output. In the general segment, floor acoustics JBL also looks presentable. If you break away from the sound characteristics, then American speakers can confidently compete with the best examples of Japanese and German equipment. As for the obvious shortcomings, they are expressed in high cost and individual errors in reproducing different frequency ranges. However, most of the interference in playback is due to the selection of inappropriate accessories and accessories. Therefore, both the manufacturer and experienced audiophiles are advised to carefully approach the choice of component base when organizing a music center.
