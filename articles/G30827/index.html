Royal Botanic Gardens (Sydney) - a peaceful natural oasis

Every city has wonderful corners where you want to return again and again. In Australian Sydney, such a place is the main attraction - a fragrant natural corner, which is certainly worth a visit.

History of creation

In the XVIII century, this place was the country's first agricultural farm. Unfortunately, due to the badlands, it did not bring income, despite the constant cultivation. In 1816, not far from the main business district of the city, the Royal Botanical Garden was founded on the ennobled territory by the governor of the island of Macquarie . Sydney is rightfully proud of and takes care of the oldest scientific institution with a long history of collecting plants.



The name of the natural corner unequivocally speaks of the relationship between the royal house of Great Britain and Australia and emphasizes the special scope of the cultural object.

Flora Natural Oasis

The Royal Botanic Gardens (Sydney) from the first day of its foundation plays a huge role in the acclimatization of plants imported from different countries.



For example, in a well-guarded zone, ancient Wolem pines grow, discovered in a mountain gorge 20 years ago and considered the oldest representatives of the flora.

Here you can see the largest flower on the planet brought from Indonesia - titanium aronnik. The three-meter plant became famous for its specific aroma, which can not be called pleasant, and its flowering occurs every 20 years. Despite the terrifying smell, those wishing to admire the yellow pestle resembling a banana are not reduced.

Garden fauna

In addition to unique collections of plants and trees, the Royal Botanic Gardens (Sydney) is known as a habitat for the wildlife of the tropics. From early morning until late evening, the green oasis is filled with the screams of exotic birds, in the dark, cute opossums, flying foxes and mice, which cannot be touched, lead an active lifestyle, which is carefully warned by the appropriate signs.

A strict ban is related to the fact that animals carry viruses that are dangerous to humans.

Children and adults with great pleasure watch funny koalas resembling little cubs, and sociable cockatoos flying from branch to branch are waiting for almond nuts. Parrots that aren‚Äôt afraid of people will sit on the shoulders of especially liked visitors as a sign of appreciation.

Fascinating walk

Located on the shores of Farm Cove Bay, the Royal Botanic Gardens in Sydney cannot be bypassed entirely in a few hours. Independently walking tourists are advised to allocate a whole day to visit a unique natural corner. Tired guests can relax in paid trams running around the perimeter of the garden.

For a few dollars, you can hire a guide who will conduct a fascinating tour and tell you about all the plants no less interesting.

Proximity to nature

Couples in love retire on decorative bridges overhanging neat ponds; kids play cheerfully in small pools while their parents examine greenhouses and greenhouses where flowers are sensitive to temperature extremes.



The garden is replete with original sculptures hiding in the bushes and ancient architectural monuments, against which tourists like to be photographed.

Favorite vacation spot

Fatigued guests of Sydney, walking along specially laid kilometer routes, stop in a cozy cafe in the garden. And those who took care of food in advance are located right on the lawns, having picnics, because the administration of the institution does not prohibit this. On the sign above the entrance there is an inscription urging you to walk on the grass, communicate with flowers and birds, hug trees to feel a complete fusion with nature.



Immersed in peace, the Royal Botanic Garden of Sydney (Australia) is filled with visitors who want to relax from the bustle in the fresh air. A natural oasis is the most favorite vacation spot for tourists and citizens.

Interesting Facts

  • Entrance to the seven-day Royal Botanic Gardens (Sydney) is free.
  • The collection of plants totals approximately nine thousand copies, trees - five thousand. Some representatives of the flora are on the verge of extinction.
  • Colonies of flying foxes, according to the administration, are responsible for the death of rare specimens. To clear the territory of animals, the sounds of metal objects hitting each other are transmitted through the loudspeakers.
  • Popular architectural monuments inside the institution are a magnificent governor's fountain, a statue of a hunter with a dog, and the Glass House pyramid greenhouse.
