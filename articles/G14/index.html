Capital of iraq

Once again, hearing the name of Saddam Hussein, the words "political instability", "American troops" and others, only one country immediately comes to mind - Iraq. And it is very sad that associations with this country are far from being connected with its customs, traditions or culture. Let's imagine that for the first time we hear about the existence of this country, and study it a little.

The capital of Iraq is the wonderful city of Baghdad. Since all Muslims are believers, it is not for nothing that they gave such a name to the city, because in translation it means "given by God." This wonderful city has an excellent location, which is famous for fertile soil and, importantly, includes many trade routes.

The capital of Iraq is a very ancient city, it was repeatedly subjected to various attacks. Basically, all the attractions located in the state of Iraq, Baghdad stores in its territories. The country is famous for its rich historical world, ancient culture and numerous architectural works, one of which is the famous Golden Mosque. Many tourists also highlight the beautiful buildings of educational institutions, erected in the 12th century.



As for the culture of this country, it is significantly different from the usual European. Therefore, before the capital of Iraq greets you, it is necessary to familiarize yourself with its customs and traditions.

First of all, this is expressed in the relationship between the opposite sexes, women should pay special attention to their wardrobe. The body should be as closed as possible, and the head should be covered with a scarf, which could cover the face. In turn, men cannot wear trousers that would fit their legs; clothes should also cover as much as possible. The stronger sex cannot do without a veil covering the hands and ankles. It is worth noting that in relation to other Muslim countries, women are given more privileges. An interesting tradition of the locals is eating: when it gets dark. However, do not be so afraid, this applies only to the time of Ramadan.



Iraq is the capital of meat cooking, true gourmets can always be sure of this. Lamb and beef are the main dishes. Iranians are a very hospitable people, as evidenced by the presence of various sweets in the house. Each meal is accompanied by drinks, especially tea and coffee. A common alcoholic drink is anise vodka.
