Sin City Las Vegas

The state of Nevada is, first of all, the "city of sins" alluring with night illumination. Those who come to try their luck immediately go to the unofficial entertainment capital, which became famous after 1931, when gambling in America was legalized.

Today, Las Vegas is a real desert oasis, which is visited by about 40 million people a year. The fact that of the 15 largest hotels in the country in Las Vegas is 14, says a lot, and the total number of rented rooms has long exceeded 130 thousand.

Game Industry Center

A huge glass pyramid of Cheops rises above the main street of Las Vegas, and in front of the entrance to the casino, amazed by the scope of the city's guests, a statue of the Egyptian Sphinx, exceeding the size of the present, is met. More than 300 entertainment centers, decorated in various subjects, are trying to surprise with external surroundings and attract tourists.



In the amazing dream city, there will be a mini copy of New York with skyscrapers and a statue of liberty, the Eiffel Tower and the erupting volcano, the imperial palace and Venice with gondolas.

Wedding capital

Las Vegas is famous not only for hotels, clubs and casinos, it is here that lovers from all over the globe come to sign in local chapels and arrange magnificent weddings. In a city that never sleeps, the procedure for marriage is maximally simplified, and only a passport is required to obtain a stamp. At the request of lovers, an unusual wedding will be held by a double of Elvis Presley or Marilyn Monroe, everything will depend on the imagination and thickness of the wallet.

Bellagio Fountains

One cannot but mention the unique dancing fountains of the Bellagio, located in front of the hotel of the same name in Las Vegas (Nevada). The sights, striking with intertwining and curiously curving jets of water to musical rhythms, are a unique extravaganza. At night, a light laser show begins to work, effectively highlighting giant fountains.



Other major cities in Nevada

Valley of Fire

In the north-east of the world gambling center is an interesting park in America, which Nevada has declared a national monument. Open in 1935, the Valley of Fire got its name because of the red-brown color of the huge rocks. On a bright sunny day, visitors to the natural monument think that it is truly engulfed in a blazing flame.



But the ancient valley is also unique in that during the day the rocks change their colors. The frozen monumental formations of the most intricate forms awaken the imagination of those who came to admire the stone landscapes.

Zone 51, Nevada (USA)

All ufologists of the world consider the military base in the Mojave desert a cult. Zone 51 is the place where, according to famous science fiction writers, alien ships crash . The CIA ten years ago officially recognized the existence of the base and revealed the secrets of the test site associated with its development, and not aliens.

Over the territory mentioned in the series "The X-Files", the film "Independence Day" and other films, flights were banned, which triggered rumors that the Zone would conduct 51 experiments on the bodies of aliens captured by the US military. Many scientists believe that much remains hidden from the public eye.

Black desert

About 7 thousand years ago, the huge Lake Laontan disappeared, its dried bottom is now called Black Rock. The dark space of the desert is dotted with geysers, which are famous throughout the world for Nevada. The photo of the black plateau captivates with unearthly landscapes, it seems that this is a scene from a science fiction film. Various festivals are held on this territory with colorful installations that fit into the strange desert landscape.
