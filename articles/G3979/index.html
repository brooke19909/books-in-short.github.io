Crochet collar: pattern. Crochet openwork collars: description

Crochet openwork collars are always in fashion. We offer to master their crochet. Collars, schemes and descriptions of which offer many options for performance, give the image sophistication and femininity. Moreover, it will take very little time to make a cute and beautiful little thing.

Such an accessory is not only worn with dresses or blouses, it can be transformed with a sweater collar and even a coat, and a school crochet collar can be a wonderful decoration if the requirements for appearance are strict.

What is needed

To get started, you need to prepare a centimeter tape, hook and yarn. Preference should be given to natural, for example, cotton threads. They are easily erased and starch. Use thin threads to make a light crochet lace collar. The scheme, if present, and the description will be useful, they should be carefully studied.

The optimal size of the hook should be selected depending on the thickness of the thread. A centimeter tape will be needed to measure the neck of the clothes with which the collar is supposed to be worn.

Combined openwork collars with crochet look interesting and unusual, for this you will need yarn of several colors. This lesson requires patience - needlewomen who painstakingly master crochet, the collars of even the most intricate designs will be able to.

How not to make a mistake with the length

Natural fiber shrinks after steaming. Therefore, at first it is recommended to perform the sample with any simple viscous, for example, several rows of single crochet columns. And only after processing it with steam, it is necessary to measure the length and calculate how many loops are in one centimeter. Accordingly, the total number of air loops should be calculated to start crocheting. Collars in this case will be the desired length.

Classic white-collar workers can give a touch of antiquity, as if they were taken from an old chest inherited. Moreover, vintage style is very fashionable now. For this, the finished work can be painted, and we will use natural dyes, which are absolutely definitely in every house: tea or natural coffee.

A hue with an orange tint will turn out if you use ordinary black tea, peach color will give green tea. A cream shade or ivory is characteristic of coffee. There is no exact recipe on how to achieve this or that color, so you have to experiment on a thread cut from a coil of 15 centimeters long.

Take 2 tablespoons of tea or coffee, add one and a half liters of water and a tablespoon of salt. Tea should be heated to boiling point, and brew coffee. We immerse the lace in a hot solution (about 70 degrees) and hold there for 10-15 minutes. For a darker color you can boil.

From time to time, take out the lace and check the color intensity, but note that after rinsing it will become lighter. If overexposed, wash the lace quickly until it is dry. To fix the color, the collar can be rinsed in water acidified with vinegar.
