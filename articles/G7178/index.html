Botanical Garden, Moscow: how to get there. Address and Feedback

If you cover the sights of the capital, which everyone is obliged to visit, then the list of the most important of them will certainly include the Main Botanical Garden, named after its first director Nikolai Vasilyevich Tsitsin. Located in the eastern part of Moscow, near VDNH, the Botanical Garden welcomes its guests from late April to mid October. Before the opening of each season, as well as after its completion, regular planting of cultivated plants is carried out in the garden.

Botanical Garden (Moscow), address, opening hours

The closest metro station is Vladykino, and a route 76 bus runs from it to the place, where people who want to visit the country's largest botanical garden will travel only 4 stops to the Ostankino Hotel. From April 29, GBS is open daily from 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. The season ends according to tradition on October 19. Guests planning to visit the exhibition should carefully read the work schedule. Some expositions are closed 2 days a week for maintenance work. Such an exhibition as the Japanese Garden, from Tuesday to Friday has a shortened mode of operation.



A wide range of expositions and greenhouses

The Botanical Garden (VDNH, Moscow) includes a diverse collection of plants brought from around the world. The beginning of the country's richest botanical collection was laid back in the spring of 1945. Since then, outstanding botanists and breeders have worked to replenish the expositions. Garden guests can visit the following exhibits:

  • The famous "Japanese Garden".
  • The best collection of tropical aquatic plants in Europe.
  • "Arboretum".
  • The Rosary.
  • "A continuously flowering garden."
  • "Exhibition of cultivated plants."
  • The Shadow Garden.
  • Numerous greenhouses.
  • Exposition of natural flora.
  • A collection of flowering ornamental plants.

GBS botanists and breeders do not want to rest on the laurels of past achievements and plan to expand existing collections, as well as build new exhibits.



Botanical Garden (Moscow), how to get a visitor

If guests of the capital are poorly oriented on the ground, especially if they decide to visit the GBS for the first time, then it will be useful for them to find out the following: the main thing is to find the route to the Vladykino metro station on subway maps. You will have to walk about 10 minutes from the station to the main entrance to the expositions. The main gate is located on the side of the Botanical Street. In addition to the main entrance, there are several gates around the perimeter of the garden. On the way from the exit from the metro you will see a small gate. There are also inputs from the border with VDNH.

Traveling by car

Many nature lovers travel by personal transport, so the question arises when they want to visit the Botanical Garden (Moscow): "How to get to the place from Dmitrovsky or Altufevsky Highways, and which route is better to choose?" Altufevskoe highway passes through the Otradnoye area up to the GBS territory. If you go along Dmitrovskoe highway, you will need to get to the intersection with Bolshaya Akademicheskaya Street.





Routes of public transport from the metro VDNH

Of course, the Vladykino metro station is not the only one from where you can catch a bus and go to the Botanical Garden (Moscow). How to get to the place, leaving at the metro station "VDNH"? Bus routes 24, 85 and 803 run to the Main Botanical Gardens of the country, as well as trolleybuses 9, 36 and 73.

How much do entrance tickets cost?

Children under 7 years old accompanied by adults, as well as senior citizens, can use the free entrance to the territory. For all other categories of the population, the cost of entry is:

  • For adults - 50 rubles
  • For students and schoolchildren - 30 rubles.

As you can see, the amount for admission is purely symbolic. Next we follow the most popular exhibits. Entrance to the rose garden and the exposition of decorative flowers costs 100 rubles per adult. Children and senior citizens receive discounts. Adult tickets for visiting the unique exhibition "Japanese Garden" on weekdays cost 150 rubles (due to a shorter working hours), on weekends and holidays - 200 rubles. Now we learned, deciding to inspect the Botanical Garden (Moscow), how to get to it, and how much the entrance tickets cost. It remains to determine with what exposure to start the inspection.



Anniversary of the Botanical Garden

In 2015, GBS celebrates its 70th anniversary. The opening of the huge glass building of the New Greenhouse is planned for this significant event. The entire surrounding area is cleaned and ennobled daily. And now we can safely say that the celebrations will be held in conditions of perfect order and beauty. Since the opening took place at the end of World War II, much attention is paid to exhibits imported from Germany, which can be viewed in the Stock Conservatory.



Top exposure

Rose garden

If we talk about the rose garden, then it‚Äôs worth starting with the history of the exposure. Back in the middle of the last century, the scientist breeder Ivan Shtanko brought out amazing varieties of roses that instantly became popular even abroad. To this day, the varieties Aurora, Yasnaya Polyana and Morning of Moscow are very popular outside of Russia. The total area occupied by the rose garden totals 2.5 hectares. In total, more than 270 different varieties of prickly beauty grow on the territory of GBS. If you measure the number in the bushes, then the figure will be about 6,000 units. Over the long history of the exposition, the best varieties of roses from around the world are collected here. Many foreign rosological firms consider it an honor to cooperate with such a well-known organization as the Botanical Garden (Moscow). The GBS address in all the years of its existence has repeatedly become the destination for considerable gratuitous gifts from partners.



For completeness, numerous ponds and ponds framed by centuries-old oaks are located on the territory. There is an exposition called "Natural Flora", which includes trees and shrubs of different regions of the country. These include:

  • Plantations of the European part of Russia.
  • Types of forests in Siberia.
  • Representatives of the cultures of the Far Eastern Forest.
  • Seedlings imported from Central Asia.
  • Caucasian plantings.

Visitors, walking around the territory, can familiarize themselves with the standard of landscape design of the 50s of the last century, which is presented in the exposition "Garden of Continuous Flowering".
