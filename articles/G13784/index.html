RS-485 (converter interface): wiring diagram and description

RS-485 is a type of converter that is used in industrial networks. It is also used in differential lines. Most often there are modifications for USB-connectors. The principle of operation of the element is based on the transmission of data by sending low-frequency pulses.

Converters differ among themselves primarily in the output resistance parameter. It should also be borne in mind that models are produced with different data rates. In order to understand them in more detail, it is necessary to consider the connection scheme of the modification.





Standard wiring diagram for the converter

The usual converter connection scheme includes self-regulating resistors, as well as an expander. A thyristor is most often used as a switched type. In order to avoid network congestion, linings are applied. In some cases, electromagnetic type kenotrons are installed to transmit the signal. In total, the circuit can have up to five capacitors. On average, the output impedance does not exceed 5.5 ohms. If we consider the industrial RS-485 interface with galvanic isolation, then a pulse expander is used there.





100 bps model

This type of RS-232/485 interface converter is connected exclusively through a transceiver. In this case, the maximum length of one segment does not exceed 1200 nm. On average, the current consumption parameter is 130 mA. If we consider the converter of Ethernet interfaces RS-485 without expanders, then it uses a buffer amplifier.

Designed to connect a maximum of five transceivers. In this case, the signal stability depends on the current throughput. The RS-485 interface cable provides a logic zero level of 200 mV. The sensitivity of the cascade depends solely on the type of transceiver.

Devices 300 bits per second

Modifications of this type are designed, as a rule, to support no more than five transceivers. Devices are suitable for half-duplex communication.
