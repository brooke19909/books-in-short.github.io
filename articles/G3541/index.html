Processors for smartphones: rating, description, specifications and reviews

When you choose a smartphone or tablet, you are sure to study its hardware specifications. Reading the descriptions of the processor, it is not immediately possible to understand what this or that designation implies. However, there is a certain rating of the performance of mobile smartphone processors, on the basis of which you can purchase a fast device. What do these terms mean and what do you need to pay attention to?





What is a processor?

The processor acts as a transmitter. That is, a request is sent to him that you want your smartphone to comply. Early mobile phones were mostly similar to traditional landlines. Smartphones, however, are laptop computers that have telephone facilities built into the system. Under this amazing touch screen is a full-fledged computer, responsible for the operation of your applications, giving you instructions (how to act in certain situations), GPS coordinates, as well as communication with others. The processor is the brain of any operation.

What is a core?

This is an element located in the main processor that reads and executes instructions. The release of such devices began with single-core processors, but modern developers have created more powerful devices by including more cores in one device. This has led to the advent of dual-core gadgets. Soon, quad-core processors (based on four cores) appeared, and today you can find hexa- (six) and octa- (eight) nuclear smartphones and tablets on sale.





What are the benefits of multiple cores?

The more cores, the faster they can process the commands that you give your phone. This means that several of these components will share the load among themselves and will perform any work very quickly. You can upload high-quality photos or high-definition videos, and then browse your collection without pause. Animations and videos play smoothly, without stuttering. Games do not freeze. Of course, there are exceptions, but, as a rule, the more cores, the better the performance.

Will an octa-core smartphone be twice as fast as a quad-core smartphone?

It's impossible. An octa-core processor is faster than a quad-core processor only when it processes an application that uses its capabilities, or in multitasking. For example, one core can handle requests in a web browser, while the other can be in standby mode. The challenge comes, and it gets to work. As a result, your work in a web browser and your phone call will go without a hitch, but not necessarily twice as fast.





What other factors may be involved?

Several cores - this is only one of the components of a smart device (you can imagine them as a certain part of the car engine). In addition, the graphics processor, random access memory for short-term storage, antennas for Wi-Fi and GPS, and much more are responsible for the operation of this component. They all work together as a whole, and all should be considered when you buy a smartphone or tablet.

Choosing a Mobile Phone

Recently, many smartphone manufacturers have released their new products, which undoubtedly make the user feel a certain perplexity when choosing. In connection with the release of new devices, processors for smartphones are changing, the rating of which is quite difficult to update. For example, last year, the Apple A9 took first place in all comparative lists, and then Hisilicon Kirin 950 replaced it. Today, Qualcomm, Apple and Samsung are fighting for primacy with varying success.

Performance Evaluation

As the chip performance rating has already shown, the Qualcomm Snapdragon 820 processor took first place and showed much greater power than the Apple A9. In addition, the Samsung Exynos 8890 also has good results, and its performance is already close to the A9.





The recently launched Qualcomm Snapdragon 652 and 650 also feature high functionality and power. Qualcomm 652 is already close in its parameters to the 810 in performance. Qualcomm Snapdragon 650 has already surpassed the 808, which leads the market for mid-range smartphones. Since MediaTek MT 6797 phones are not currently available, and only models released in previous years can be found on the market, the rating of MTK processors for smartphones is no longer relevant.

GPU Performance Ranking

In recent years, smartphones have been increasingly focusing on GPU performance, as it is closely related to playing games and working with images on a mobile phone, and this has a direct impact on the actual user experience. Currently, Qualcomm Snapdragon 820 (Adreno530) is distinguished by high performance in this regard. The Samsung Exynos 8890 (Mali-T880 MP12) is almost equal to the Apple A9 in power, as already noted. As for Hisilicon Kirin 950 (Mali-T880 MP4), which is well known to everyone, its GPU performance is not good enough, and the test results are similar to Qualcomm Snapdragon 808 (Adreno418) and Qualcomm Snapdragon 652 (Adreno510).

In fact, the performance of the graphics chip is also directly related to the screen resolution of your smartphone. Currently, the main resolution is 1080P, and 2K has become a trend in the development of high-end smartphones. However, without the support of a powerful GPU, even such a high resolution will continue to lead to degradation of the device.





Of course, the factors that determine the performance of a mobile phone are much larger than the CPU and GPU values. However, it is necessary to fully realize that nothing but the powerful performance of the chips is the basis of an uninterruptedly working device. Without support for high performance, any user experience will be "empty." This can be described by an old proverb: "You cannot make something out of nothing."

The above information concerning processors for smartphones, the rating of which is presented above, cannot be considered as a permanent rule. Today, new devices are regularly released that have increasingly powerful specifications.
