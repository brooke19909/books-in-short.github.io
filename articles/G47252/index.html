Glass mosaic in the home interior

Mosaic - one of the most ancient finishing materials. This word from the Latin language translates as "a work dedicated to the muse." It was made mainly of colored stones, pieces of ceramics or smalt. Glass mosaic was a very expensive material. To decorate their homes, only the richest people in society could buy it. Temples, palaces, fountains were decorated with original panels from small multi-colored pieces of glass.



And at present, a mosaic can serve as an interesting and stylish decor for a home interior. The glass from which it is made has now become cheaper. Therefore, glass mosaic is now available to almost everyone. Glass has many advantages over other materials. It is very durable, resistant to chemical influences, does not pass and does not absorb water, is resistant to large temperature extremes and ultraviolet radiation, is characterized by heat resistance and frost resistance. Glass is able to withstand up to 100 cycles of transition from plus to minus temperatures (up to -30 degrees), without losing its quality.



Glass mosaic is much stronger than glass itself, although they are the same in chemical composition. It depends on two reasons. Firstly, after pouring the glass mass into molds, it is fired at high temperature - up to 800 degrees Celsius. Secondly, due to the small size of the mosaic module, it is very difficult to break it.

Glass mosaics are very resistant to microorganisms and synthetic detergents. Thanks to the continuous structure, it is remarkably cleaned of all pollutants.

Glass mosaic is great for wall cladding in bathrooms and kitchens, in pools and saunas, that is, where there is frequent contact with water. It is also used to decorate stoves and fireplaces, furniture and building facades.

Excess mortar and bumps are removed with the same spatula. After complete drying with a sponge, erase the remains of the grout and wash the mosaic. Now she will get a finished look.
