Location

It is the largest island in Italy. Even in its location, Sicily is unique. The map shows that it is located between Africa and Europe, washed by the Ionian, Tyrrhenian and Mediterranean Seas.

From the history of the island

The extremely favorable position on the Mediterranean routes has led to numerous conquests of the island over many years.

This is a very ancient region, and in its history left a trace of the culture of many peoples. At first it was conquered by the Greeks, later by the Vandals, Normans, Arabs, Byzantines. The conquerors changed, but a particle of their spirit remained on the island. This is evidenced by various monuments of architecture and art.

This city can not leave anyone indifferent.

Sicily: Attractions Map

We want to warn you right away that in one trip (even quite a long one) to this fabulous island you will not be able to see all the monuments and interesting places. As you already know, the largest island in Italy is Sicily. Attractions are in each of its provinces, in any locality.

Royal Norman Palace

In Sicilian Palermo has its own main attraction - the residence of the kings. If you look at it from the west side, you will see a real fortress, and if from the south - a typical building made in the Renaissance style. At one time, the Norman Palace housed the royal residence. In addition, in Palermo you can see the Cathedral of the Assumption of Our Lady. By decree of King William the Second, it was built in the twelfth century. Today in his exposition are sarcophagi of emperors and kings of the Norman dynasty. In Palermo you can see the famous exhibition of mummies - the Catacombs of Capuchins.

Messina - Attractions

In this city is the oldest cathedral of which Sicily is proud. Sights of the XII century are represented by the Duma Cathedral, designed in the Norman style. On the bell tower you can see the largest astronomical clock of the eighteenth century.



Province of Agrigento

Here you can see a unique park - the Valley of the Temples. On its territory are the tomb of Theron, Temple of Hercules, Temple of Concord, Temple of Hera. Most of these structures date back to the fifth century BC. e.

Striking in its size Temple of Zeus. Its height is more than thirty meters, its width is more than fifty, its length is one hundred and twelve meters.

Mysterious Syracuse

This city captivates all who come here not only with its unique attractions, but also with a reverent attitude of local residents to their history.

In the thirteenth century, under Frederick the Second, Maniach Castle was built in this city. In those days, he played an important political role in the life of a provincial city. At different times, families of kings stayed here and lived. In the fourteenth century, the Parliament of Sicily sat in this building.

The small town of Catania is known all over the world thanks to the largest volcano in Europe - Etna. Accompanied by an experienced guide, you can climb to its top.



Volcano in sicily

A certain part of tourists coming to this famous Italian island arrive with their own eyes to see the legendary volcano Etna.

The ancient Greeks were firmly convinced that the forge of God Hephaestus was located in the vent of the volcano. That is why he was called Aitna, which translates as "fiery mountain."

Its height is three thousand five hundred and fifty meters, its area is one thousand two hundred and fifty square meters. The eruptions formed more than four hundred craters. Once every three months, an eruption of lava occurs.

By car, you can climb to a height of one thousand nine hundred meters and see the extinct Sylvester crater.



Those who will not have enough impressions can continue the tour. Accompanied by a professional guide on the cable car, those who wish will be raised to a height of two thousand five hundred and fifty meters, and an incredibly beautiful panorama of the existing craters will open in front of them.

If you decide on such a climb, take warm clothes with you - on the top of Etna it is windy and cool, and the temperature is not higher than fifteen degrees.

A visit to Mount Etna can be combined with a walk through the city of Taormina. This is perhaps one of the most popular resorts of Sicily, which is located on the slopes of Mount Tauro.

You can see the villa Del Casale. Its appearance dates back to the fourth century BC. and today is protected by UNESCO.

Of course, many tourists will be interested in shopping at the Sicilian Village outlet. Here you can buy clothes of the most famous Italian brands.
