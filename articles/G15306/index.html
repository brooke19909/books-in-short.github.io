What to see in Moscow in 3 days. Moscow sights

This is your first visit to Moscow and, taking advantage of the availability of several free days, would you like to get to know the capital? In a review article, we will tell you what to see in Moscow in 3 days.

The best time of the year to explore Moscow

You can get acquainted with the capital of Russia at any time of the year. But Moscow best meets guests in the summer. The historic city center, although a little, but receives a little coolness from the river. Closer to dawn, watering machines leave for the streets and wash off the dust accumulated during the day from the roads, filling the air with freshness and coolness.

The length of the day also contributes to longer walks. After all, Moscow is not only museums and palaces, many architectural forms of various directions and eras, but also its inhabitants. It is walks through the streets of Moscow that will give you the opportunity to see, understand and love this ancient city, in which there is something to see.

In Moscow, in 3 days you will not have time to get tired of the city heat, because you will be fully occupied with the vision of the beauties of the capital.

the Red Square

You will definitely come here. This is the main square of the country, and being in Moscow (if only not passing through), one should not visit here in any case. In addition, it is surrounded on all sides by numerous attractions.





Contrary to speculation, the name of the square appeared due to its beauty and enormous size. She always played a major role in the life of Moscow.

Standing on ancient stones, you will feel the Russian spirit - its breadth and scope. The majestic buildings framing Red Square leave no one indifferent.





Having checked your watch with the main chimes of the country located on the Spasskaya Tower of the Kremlin, you can visit the Mausoleum - a symbol of a bygone, turning point in the history of our state.

Kremlin

Thinking what to see in Moscow in three days, be sure to visit the Kremlin. The creation of Yuri Dolgoruky is striking in its grandeur. Once in the Kremlin and taking a ticket for a free tour, you can spend an exciting day in this historic place. Here you can visit:

  • The Armory

  • Patriarchal Palace;
  • Diamond Fund of Russia.

GUM

Right on Red Square is the main department store of the country. Today it is not only a lot of luxury stores. This is a place to relax, and a huge cultural site.





It often hosts exhibitions of contemporary art, public mass events, all kinds of presentations. The internal architecture of GUM makes it a palace of commerce, where it is impossible to resist shopping.

Zoo

In Moscow, more than 150 years ago, near the Presnensky reservoirs where fish were bred, the first menagerie was arranged in the capital. In those days, he was not much like today's splendor, but he successfully pleased the residents and visitors of the city.

The animal habitat in the metropolitan zoo is as comfortable as possible for pets. This is confirmed by the recent birth of the baby elephant and the replenishment in the gorilla family. In total, the zoo in Moscow contains more than 6 thousand inhabitants, requiring care, love and affection from the staff. 174 species of mammals are waiting for visitors in the enclosures. In addition, here you can see:

  • 180 species of reptiles;
  • 57 species of amphibians;
  • 286 species of birds;
  • 240 species of fish and 186 species of invertebrates.

All this diversity covers 21 hectares of land in almost the center of Moscow. In addition, the 193 hectare zoo provides a natural habitat for many animal species. If you use the metro to travel to the Moscow Zoo, you need to get to the Krasnopresnenskaya or Barrikadnaya stations.

Moscow Metro

Metro stations are considered one of the most beautiful in the world. These architectural monuments of the Soviet era continue to serve man today. When traveling around the capital and choosing what to see in Moscow in 3 days, pay attention to the metro station. Muscovites have long been accustomed to the magnificent decoration of the stations and treat them quite mundane.





Guests of the capital, having first descended into the subway, will discover the splendor of architectural forms of various styles. The rich and unique finish is striking - it was all created for ordinary citizens of the Soviet Union. Getting to the Komsomolskaya station, you will find yourself in a real underground palace.

Mayakovskaya Station will take you to the futuristic, fast-moving world of the future. Using the services of the subway, we recommend that you visit the following stations for familiarization:

  • "Novoslobodskaya";
  • "Revolution square";
  • Novokuznetskaya;
  • Kropotkinskaya;
  • "Kiev".

Traveling through unique architectural stations underground stations, you can easily spend all day exploring this metropolitan landmark. The cost of such a trip will amount to one trip on the subway.

Museums of the city

Being in the capital, be sure to visit one or several museums - they have something to see. In Moscow, in 3 days you, of course, will not have time to visit all the sights physically, but there are museums where the city‚Äôs guest must go.
