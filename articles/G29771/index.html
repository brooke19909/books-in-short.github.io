Karelia, river rafting. Features

To participate in most water trekking routes , you can be a normal city dweller in every way. No special preparation needed. But, of course, some safety rules should be followed. In addition, you need to be prepared to endure certain difficulties and discomfort. Overnight during such trips, usually in the field, in tents and sleeping bags, cooked around the campfire. Some routes are quite feasible for children, but there are those where they take only extremals and experienced rafters. Before each trip, instructors conduct special short courses on the management of rafts, kayaks or other means.



Routes

River rafting (Karelia) can be of varying lengths and complexity. For example, rafting day trips are popular. They last two to three hours. Often they are practiced on the Shuya River. The most popular routes are three days. Beginners usually prefer hiking in Shuya, since this river is the most full-flowing and easiest to overcome the rapids. Nevertheless, visiting it, you will see all the classic beauty of Karelia. And the longest routes are designed for 11-12 days. Those who go there are not sorry for the time and effort to see the most beautiful lakes, overcome turbulent rivers (for example, ‚Äúride‚Äù 60 kilometers along Vonge or 110 along Pista) and then swim along the White Sea. On the way, as a rule, there are many thresholds. They are complex, but quite safe. Thresholds are combined, extended, cascading, and they must be treated carefully. In addition to short and long trips, there are many other routes. The most interesting of them we will describe below. Usually they are planned so that tourists have the opportunity not only to "swallow" adrenaline, but also relax on the beautiful sandy beaches, almost untouched by man, go fishing among the reeds, admire the lakes and other beautiful landscapes, and even pick mushrooms and berries in a pine forest.



What time is better to organize rafting on the rivers of Karelia

These places are most suitable for water trips in late spring. When snow melts at the end of April, the small Karelian rivers suddenly turn into huge stormy streams. Traveling along them is not only pleasant, but also safe. Therefore, the best month to organize rafting on the river in Karelia is May. And really, than gathering for a barbecue picnic with boredom, isn‚Äôt it better to ride on vices and kayaks on vices and rapids, especially since high water will give you the opportunity to do this without unnecessary effort? Both experienced tourists and beginners try not to miss such a chance. No wonder the May holidays are a time when lovers of rafting on the rivers of Karelia vying with each other flock to calm Shuya, treacherous Uksu or stormy Tokhma. You can limit this excursion to two or three days, or you can stretch it for the entire first half of May. It depends on what means and time you have. However, the May rafting requires attention and caution. Rough waters can overturn a kayak or raft, and the temperature at this time is low. Therefore, it is better to have a wetsuit, a splash-proof jacket and pants, as well as a warm sleeping bag.



Uksa

This trip is often an extreme tour. Therefore, only adults, at least tourists older than 16 years, are often taken there. This is a river where you get an excess of adrenaline. Rafting on the Uksa River (Karelia) is available only in late spring and early summer. The fact is that with the onset of heat, the water level drops sharply and travel becomes impossible. The length of the route is about forty kilometers. Most often, groups leave from the bridge across the river. Tourists have many steep rapids on their way - Mostovaya, Staircase, Kolokoski, Saucer, Salmon, Pink Elephant, Mill and others. It takes two or three days to get through all these breathtaking water cliffs. The campaign ends at the camp "Three Elements".

This river does not like beginners. She prefers experienced tourists. Rafting along the Krasnenkaya River (Karelia) is often combined with traveling along other water streams - mainly Kutsayoki and Tumche. Such trips are most often made in the summer. They conquer these rivers on catamarans, less often on kayaks. In this campaign, severe northern beauty opens. You will see powerful waterfalls and impregnable cliffs, and a trip through the vices of Krasnenkaya will make you feel like real heroes, the winners of the seething elements. Here there are sections of the 5th category of complexity. Of particular interest are the rapids Kamenisty, Ostrovnoy, Leap, Caucasian. Whitefish

This river is rather shallow. Its length is about 55 kilometers. Sports rafting on the Sig river (Karelia), as a rule, is not practiced. But here is a good fishing. The places along which this river flows are sparsely populated. Only recently has it begun to attract the attention of tourists and lovers of rafting. The rapids are quite complex, but the lakes and shores are extremely picturesque. Tourists recommend kayaking on this river in May-June, because it is then that floods occur. Whitefish flows into the White Sea. But you can go out on a kayak only in the presence of high water. The fact is that it flows into the sea in steps. Therefore, it is necessary to overcome the thresholds to the very end. And the last rifts will lead you actually to the sea.

Keret

Many water trips are famous for Karelia. The Keret River, the rafting of which is considered the most popular among tourists, is one of the most interesting. This route most often runs in the summer and autumn. The name of the river comes from the Sami word, which in translation means "mountain peak". The length of the route is 120 kilometers. It starts from Lake Varlamovo. They overcome it on catamarans and rafts with a motor, as well as kayaks. During the trip, there are many rapids from 400 meters to 3 km. But since there is a lot of calm water between them, all these sections pass under the motor. This is Lake Bezymyannoye, Crooked, Varatsky. It is on this journey that tourists can not only prove themselves, but also admire the fabulous nature of forest Karelia. And the campaign ends, as a rule, with access to the White Sea and a visit to its islands. Good fishing is often organized there. Rest on the White Sea on the islands of Keret and Oleniy is especially good. This rafting lasts from 3 to 7-8 days, depending on how much time the tourists have.



Pisto

One of the most interesting routes where all the charms of Karelia (rafting) are combined is rafting along the border zone. There flows the Pisto River, which originates in Finland. Here, too, there are practically no cities or villages, and the area is ecologically clean. The route along this river has a length of 85 kilometers, and along the way there are 25-30 rapids. Although this direction is more complicated than other similar trips in Carrelia, both extreme people and beginners make a trip along this water stream. The latter use rafts with a motor. Since the rafting takes place in remote and inaccessible places, instructors have satellite communications, the necessary facilities for medical care, and even a portable bath. A photo session in the most beautiful places is provided. Such a trip takes 9-10 days.
