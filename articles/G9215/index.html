There is, for example, in the Odessa region the wonderful village of Borshchi. Where did this name come from, and no need to explain. The famous Ukrainian dish, which gave the settlement its name, was masterly able to cook in a local inn in the 18th century. Travelers who looked into the inn for lunch and rest, ate delicious borsch and praised. And then not only they themselves tried again to come back here, but they also advised others that they should stay in this village, because there is no such delicious dish in the vicinity. It is not surprising that the traditions of cooking borsch in the village with that name have been preserved to this day - every self-respecting housewife here cooks this tasty dish according to her own special recipe.

Tails? Twist? No, do not bake!

Again Ukraine - we find cool names of settlements in the Lviv region. Oddly enough, in the village of Pikhikhvostyv there are no recipes for unusual dishes with the participation of tails, and its inhabitants prefer baked potato pancakes. However, the legend of how such a name appeared has survived to this day. They say that during the attack of the Tatars on these lands all the surrounding people found refuge in a large cave. But when fleeing, they were in a hurry to escape, so much so that they took very little food with them, but somehow it was necessary to survive.

So the villagers had to eat animals taken with them, including baking the tails of bulls and horses. And then came the name of the Oven Tails. As you can see, the ridiculous names of settlements often come from serious history. Residents are used to the name of the village. There are a lot of creative people here, cheerful and talented.

Of course, funny names for settlements in Russia are also available. A very large percentage of Russians now live in villages and villages with funny names. And everyone has a special story. Often the village received a ridiculous name nicknamed the vibrant person who lived in it, and sometimes the name of the village was given to offended residents of neighboring villages, thus seeking revenge.



Or there is a village in the Moscow region Durykino, Solnechnogorsk district. According to legend, Peter I himself had a hand in the name of this settlement. Once, the great Russian sovereign ordered raw chicken eggs from villagers, which at that time were used to strengthen clay and lime mortars during the construction of walls. And the villagers, having decided that it was not worth the king to send the raw eggs, took them and cooked them. As the sovereign estimated the magnitude of the intelligence of the inhabitants, one can guess by the name of the village. Alas, now Durykino is almost empty, but the old-timers do not forget the legend.

Happiness with raisins

Funny names have not only small settlements. Unusual city names are also found. Here, for example, the city of Happiness in the Luhansk region of Ukraine is unique in its name. According to legend, the founders of the city in the distant 18th century were serfs who escaped from the landlords. Once, Catherine II passed through these places, who was glad for the free people living here, and said that they must have happiness here. And so it was.



But the real highlight is not far from Kharkov - the city of Raisins. And even though the emblem of the city depicts grapes, there is still no version confirming the origin of the name of the city from raisins. Historians believe that the city was named after the Izyumets river and the Izyumsky ford, known from antiquity, on which the settlement was founded.
