Islands of Portugal: description, weather conditions, attractions

Portugal occupies the western part of the Iberian Peninsula. A European country has long been compared with an open museum: in all its parts you can see ancient architectural buildings that have been erected for many centuries. They are of particular value because they have been preserved almost in their original form. Portugal is also the Azores, surrounded by greenery. They are distinguished by amazing beauty. Every year, thousands of tourists come to the islands of Portugal to enjoy the picturesque scenery. It has a wonderful warm climate, which is very good for health.

Azores

They are an amazingly beautiful archipelago that occupies the northern part of the Atlantic Ocean. The archipelago arose under the influence of volcanic processes. Currently, there are 1,766 volcanoes on it, of which 9 are active. Azores consists of 9 islands, the largest of which is San Miguel. The highest point of the archipelago is the volcano of the island of Pico, the highest point of which is located at an altitude of 2351 meters above sea level.

Getting to the islands of Portugal is not easy, but worth it. Tourists leave here with unforgettable impressions and emotions. The islands got their name from the Italian azzurre and Spanish azul, meaning "blue" and "blue", respectively. This is the most popular version of the origin of their unusual name.

Many travelers come to the Azores. This place is interesting for divers, lovers of fishing and sailing. Tourism infrastructure is under development. Visitors are offered a high level of service.

The archipelago is very quiet. If desired, travelers can go down to the crater of an inactive volcano to see caves, an underground lake and thermal springs.

The most important local entertainment, enthralling all visiting guests, is the observation in the immediate vicinity of whales, sperm whales, dolphins.

Madeira

This island is called "paradise on earth." It is a rocky oasis of volcanic type, buried in flowers. Madeira Island is considered the largest in the eponymous archipelago. It is located 980 km from Lisbon. And about half this distance begins the African coast. They come to Madeira Island (Portugal) to enjoy the unique beauty of its landscape, a variety of parks, natural areas. Tourists admire the magnificent mountain peaks shrouded in clouds; visit volcanic caves, lava baths with healing waters, fishing villages and botanical gardens. Locals periodically organize festivals and dance carnivals on the island.

Sunny weather reigns here from May to September. This is the peak of the tourist season. Tourists come to the islands who want to swim or dive in the clear sea waters.

This is the largest island of the archipelago located in the eastern part of Azores. It extends 60 kilometers in length and 14 in width. Locals call it the "green island" for its magnificent nature and rich vegetation.

This is the southernmost part of the archipelago. Scientists believe that it was discovered the first of all the islands. It is the only village of Vila do Porto.
