Cyprus, Paphos: hotels, beaches, holiday reviews and photos

Paphos is a city located on the shores of the Mediterranean Sea. It occupies the southern tip of the territory of Cyprus and rests at the foot of the Troodos mountain range. First impression

Holidays in Paphos in Cyprus are not recommended for families with children. Local hotels are not focused on organizing leisure activities for kids and their parents. There are practically no playgrounds in the city, but the village is replete with respectable restaurants, gourmet cafes and secluded sandy bays.

Excursion into the past

Paphos's business card in Cyprus is a cultural heritage whose history spans several millennia. Agapenor, a military leader, a direct participant in the battle of Troy, is considered the founding father of the city. Active development of the settlement began after the construction of the temple in honor of the goddess of love. A huge number of people flocked to these lands. Many remained, contributing to the strengthening of the ancient polis.

After changing the city plan, Paphos in Cyprus was conditionally divided into two large areas. The modern border between these quarters runs along the bustling St. Paul Avenue. The enrichment of the settlement was also facilitated by the visiting Romans. They provided him with the capital status, which the city lost during the period of the Byzantine Empire.

Transport accessibility

Paphos in Cyprus serves the local airport. Many Russian travelers arrive at its passenger terminals. Some tourists choose the Larnaca air gate. The flight takes about four hours. Municipal transport runs to the city center from the airport.

Our compatriots who paid for tours to Paphos in Cyprus prefer a taxi or group transfer. The cost of a bus ticket is 120 rubles. For a trip by car will have to pay 2000.

Administrative division

Shopping centers, museums, art galleries and office buildings are located on a hill in the old part of the city. Administrative buildings, a farmer's market were located right there. The beaches of Paphos (Cyprus) stretch along the relatively new areas of the resort. They focus on hotels and bars, cafes, snack bars, discos, taverns.

A few years ago, the city acquired a new embankment, which today is the epicenter of the entertainment life of the municipality. The promenade takes its beginning in the center, passes by the beaches of Paphos (Cyprus) and ends on the pier. Dozens of snow-white yachts and boats moored along the pier. They can be rented.

Coast

Picturesque beaches attract tens of thousands of tourists to the city. Even in March in Paphos, Cyprus is crowded. Tourists impressively stroll along the sea. In June, the coast becomes noisy. Arriving travelers from the UK, Germany, France and Russia. The best place for swimming is Central Beach. All other recreational areas have a lot of shortcomings in the form of large boulders or a narrow beach strip. The thing is that Europeans prefer to swim in the pools, and not in the Mediterranean.

The Russians choose a vacation in Coral Bay. Privacy lovers set off on the coast of Lara Bay. There is a long beach, in which wild green turtles live. The sea in Paphos, Cyprus from the winds is closed by dilapidated and partially crumbling rocks. They hang over the coast, casting long shadows in the afternoon.
