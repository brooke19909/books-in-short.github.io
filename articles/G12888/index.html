Many argue that walking along the Sverdlovsk embankment, you can make some startling discoveries. Is it really? Should tourists who come to St. Petersburg for a few days go to this place, or is it better to spend time somewhere in the center, visiting the sights known around the world?

This article will help the reader to draw up his route correctly, taking into account almost every trifle, so that a short trip along the Sverdlovsk embankment turns out to be not only interesting and eventful, but also tireless.

General description of the main artery of the city



This 3100-meter coastal street is located in St. Petersburg. It originates from Arsenalnaya and runs along Komarovsky bridge, capturing at the same time some part of Okhta.

At present, one of the main roads on the right bank of the Neva, a six-lane transport highway, passes on the two-story embankment.

The history of the Sverdlovsk embankment

Since the beginning of the XVIII century. on the right bank of the Neva River began to develop land. Here wealthy citizens built cottages and country houses. At that time, the territory of the embankment was called Polyustrovskaya.





In 1773-1777 on this territory a two-tier front terrace-pier was erected. Its mainsail and side staircases had granite lining. The majestic terrace was decorated with vases and decorated with four sculptures of sphinxes. On both sides of the pier, guns for salutes and signals were installed.

The pier of the current Sverdlovsk embankment was destroyed during the Great Patriotic War. In the years 1959-1960. it was restored. It got its name familiar to our ear in 1938 - in honor of party worker Y. M. Sverdlov.

In 1967, a large-scale reconstruction began. It lasted more than twenty years. During this time, a high granite wall was erected, on the site from Piskarevsky Avenue to Okhta, opposite the Smolny Cathedral, a wide descent into the water was built from granite.

In 2007, the boundaries of the embankment were finally determined: its small part of the territory from Krasnogvardeiskaya Square to Okhta was transferred to the Maloohtinskaya Embankment.

Famous buildings

Almost every old house on Sverdlovskaya Embankment in St. Petersburg is unique.

For example, here is a monument of classicism and architecture - the cottage Durnovo. This country villa was built in 1780. Its owner was P.P. Bakunin. After a series of sales in 1813, its owner was a prominent official D.N. Durnovo. He did a complete reconstruction of the site, as a result of which a park with nightingales was added to the mansion.

We can say with confidence that Sverdlovskaya embankment (St. Petersburg) played an important role in the life of the Northern capital. Before the October Revolution, the headquarters of the anarchists was located in the country, and later the Bolsheviks kept their weapons here.

In Soviet times, the Durnovo cottage housed the museum and club of the Leningrad Metal Plant.





After the collapse of the USSR, this architectural monument almost died, as it fell into complete decline. And only in 1996, a fence was dismantled at the dacha, but here, fate decreed otherwise. During a fire in 1998, not only completely burned out the second floor of the building, but the famous pediment was destroyed.

Until recently, the estate was completely devastated, but restoration work began in March 2014: an architectural monument on Sverdlovskaya Embankment began, as they say, to transform before our eyes.

On the embankment, in addition to Durnovo cottage, there are several more well-known structures not only in the country, but also beyond its borders. For example, here are located the production warehouses of the New Bavaria company, Kusheleva Dacha (a manor with the famous "lion" fence), a barracks and a building of a spinning mill.
