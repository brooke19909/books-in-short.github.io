Izhevsk: attractions, photos, reviews

Izhevsk ... The sights of this city cannot but attract increased attention. Every year, both locals and numerous tourists go to the zoo, enjoy visiting museums and cathedrals, and, of course, they are surprised at unusual and quite original monuments.

We can say with confidence that Izhevsk, whose photo always becomes a worthy decoration of the family photo archive of even experienced travelers, is usually remembered for a long time.

This article will introduce readers to the chosen direction in more detail. Everyone will receive accurate and reliable information about the amazing city called Izhevsk, whose attractions will be listed with the provision of the most important and valuable facts.

general description



As you know, Izhevsk is located on the Izh River, and today it is the capital of the Udmurt Republic. This is the country's economic, transport and trade center, one of the twenty largest Russian cities.

Izhevsk, whose sights are found in almost every guide to Russia, is also known worldwide for its defense, metallurgy and engineering industries.

In addition, the city has a developed transport system and is considered a major transport hub in Udmurtia. Despite its remoteness from Moscow (over 1,100 km), you can get here without any problems: both land and air routes operate. In the Udmurt capital there are a lot of places of interest to tourists.

Izhevsk is a modern city where representatives of many nationalities live: Udmurts, Chuvashs, Tatars and Russians, Belarusians and Ukrainians, etc. The population in 2011 was about 628 thousand people.

This is a developed industrial, educational and cultural center of Udmurtia. The famous Izhevsk arms factory produces various types of weapons, and the Izhevsk Electromechanical produces air defense systems. Other enterprises of the defense industry work in Izhevsk.

Great importance is given to the education system. There are many kindergartens and schools, higher educational institutions. The city has open theaters, museums, galleries and parks.

The historical part of Izhevsk is concentrated in the region of the royal treasury factories and offices. The city is divided by the river Izh into two parts, interconnected by a dam and bridges.

You can get to the center by tram. In good weather, it is recommended to visit city parks or go for a walk along the promenade. It should be noted that a great place to relax is also the Izhevsk Zoo.

In the suburbs is a unique architectural and ethnographic museum "Ludorvay". The territory of the reserve is about 40 hectares. Here you can get acquainted with the life of the Udmurt people, with its traditions and culture, crafts and architecture. Museum visitors enjoy tasting national dishes, soaring in the bathhouse or riding a horse.

St. Michael's Cathedral



Tourists who are primarily interested in religious shrines will also like a city like Izhevsk. The sights of this settlement include many temples.

So, the Cathedral of St. Michael's Cathedral was built in Izhevsk, which is one of the most beautiful and large architectural monuments of the Russian Orthodox Church. For residents of the city - this is a symbol of the revival of Udmurtia.

These lands were consecrated in 1765 during the construction of the Trinity Chapel. Later, in 1784, the chapel was converted into a church, which was destroyed by fire in 1810. In the middle of the XIX century. a stone chapel appeared on the site of the temple. The construction was built in honor of the patron saint of gunsmiths Archangel Michael.

In 1876, donations began to be collected for the construction of the new cathedral, in which the workers of the arms factory took part. With the outbreak of war and revolutionary unrest, the construction of the temple was suspended. But in November 1915, the throne was illuminated in the name of the Holy Archangel Michael. In 1920, the church was closed. In the years 1932-1937. there was a museum of local lore, destroyed in 1937.

The decision to recreate the cathedral was made in 2000. The solemn laying of the temple took place in 2004. In 2007, Patriarch Alexy II, in the presence of the head of Udmurtia, consecrated the main throne of the cathedral. The Cathedral, 67 meters high, is located on the highest point of the city.

At the foot of the temple stands a seven-meter cross, the surrounding area is decorated with flower beds, there are benches and lanterns. The cathedral is especially magnificent at night in the light of illuminating lights. Its dome is visible from anywhere in the city.
