Geography and climate

A small town is located in the European part of the Russian Federation, washed by the waters of the Baltic Sea - Primorsky Bay and Kaliningrad Bay. This is the westernmost territory of the country, having borders with the Polish Republic and Lithuania. The hydrological objects of the area where the city of Primorsk is located, have a huge impact on the formation of weather conditions in the village.





The sea climate is temperate with mild and short winters, cool summers, sufficient rainfall (about 800 mm) and prevailing winds all year round. The warmest month is July and the coldest is February. West winds from the Atlantic, Icelandic minimum, Azores anticyclone significantly affect the climate of the area. Hot weather is infrequent, it should be expected during the period of receipt of the Mediterranean air masses. Rains are a frequent occurrence for the region where the city of Primorsk is located. In Russia, the territory of the Kaliningrad region is an exclave of the country, and Primorsk is a municipality of the Baltic administrative region.

Historical reference

The fortification was expanded, strengthened and became interesting for the religious supreme organ of the Catholic faith of the Dome chapel to move into it. The castle has long served as the residence of the bishops of Koenigsberg.

After the defeat of the order in the war with Poland, the Grand Master Albrecht of Brandenburg swore allegiance to the Polish king Sigismund. He transferred monasteries, castles and a residence in favor of the state. So the territory where the city of Primorsk is located became the lands of Prussia.





The settlement, which is more than 700 years old, not only survived the period of the Thirteen Years War of the Order with Poland, but also suffered the ruin of the Swedish army for six years, was besieged by the British and was the site of the French during the Seven Years War in Europe. The former granary of the German Empire during World War II in 1945 was liberated from German invaders by Russian troops. And in 1947, Fischhausen became Primorsky.
