Sights of Kyshtym: what to see for a tourist?

What might be interesting for tourists Kyshtym? There are not many attractions . But if you search well, you can find several interesting objects here. This small industrial town has ancient buildings, and in the immediate vicinity there are unique natural monuments.

In this article you will find photos with descriptions of the sights of Kyshtym - architectural, natural and historical-cultural.

general information

Kyshtym is a small town in the South Urals with a population of 37 thousand inhabitants. Located 90 kilometers from Chelyabinsk. Administratively, it is part of the Chelyabinsk region. The city is located on the Kyshtym river of the same name. From almost all sides it is surrounded by forests, lakes and low mountains.





Kyshtym dates back to 1757, when the famous Russian industrialist Nikita Demidov founded two ironworks here. The name of the city comes from two Bashkir words: ‚Äúkysh‚Äù (winter) and ‚Äútymyk‚Äù (quiet). Most often, this toponym is interpreted as a "quiet winter hut."

Modern Kyshtym is an important industrial center of the Chelyabinsk region. The mining, metallurgical and engineering industries are well developed here. The city has about 50 industrial enterprises. Today, Kyshtym produces high-quality copper, crystalline graphite, refractory clay, quartz concentrate, wood, various equipment for mines and much more.

Kaolin quarry

Perhaps the most popular attraction of Kyshtym is an abandoned kaolin quarry. It is located about three kilometers from the suburban village of Kaolinovy. Locals nicknamed this place ‚ÄúBali‚Äù for the amazing combination of the azure water of the flooded quarry with its dazzling white shores. Swimming in this man-made lake is not recommended. Therefore, tourists simply walk along its shores, often arranging professional photo shoots.

Christ Church

One of the main architectural sights of Kyshtym is located in the city center, on Lenin street, 22. This is the Cathedral of the Nativity of Christ, built in 1857 in the Russian style. The massive structure is decorated with five onion heads. Nearby stands a multi-tiered bell tower. In the 1930s, the temple was closed, but already in the early 1940s, it resumed its activities. Inside the cathedral, valuable artistic murals dating from the beginning of the twentieth century have been preserved.
