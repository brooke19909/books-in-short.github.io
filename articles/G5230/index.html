Whip up snowflake

If you don‚Äôt have time to create home decorations, but want to add atmosphere, we suggest you weave the simplest snowflakes and hang them a few at home, on windows and a Christmas tree. What is needed to weave them:

  • beads;
  • beads;
  • wire;
  • glue or thread.

First of all, cut a few pieces of wire, at least there should be three. You can vary the number and size of segments depending on the desired result. The larger the snowflake you need, the more stretches you will need.

If you want to hang your snowflakes, make sure that one of the segments is a little longer, this will be needed to make a loop and stretch the ribbon.

Use glue or light thread to fasten the beams exactly in the middle. The segment should protrude a little longer so that the excess can be bent, make sure that there are equal gaps between the rays. When the glue dries, for each beam, collect beads and beads, alternate, vary colors, creating bright snowflakes.

Fasten the rows of beams, use a pair of pliers to form a loop, stretch the ribbon - and voila, your easiest snowflake is ready!

Snowflake made of pins

Do you know how to make a snowflake from beads and pins? It's simple, to create such a New Year‚Äôs crafts you will need:

  • pins
  • white beads;
  • red beads;
  • silver beads;
  • red beads (larger than silver);
  • green beads (slightly larger than red ones);
  • fishing line.

This very simple weaving will be to your taste, now we will tell you how to gradually make a snowflake from beads.

Take a pin, open it, and on the needle, type beads and beads in this order:

  • 2 pcs. white beads;
  • 1 silver bead;
  • 1 red bead;
  • 1 silver bead;
  • 1 green bead.

Fasten the pin and set aside, 8 pins of these need to be made.

Measure out a small piece of fishing line and pull through all the small ears of the pins, connect and tie. Pins fan out, observing the same distance between them.

Measure out another piece of fishing line with a length spanning two and a half circles from the pins. Fix the fishing line on one of the heads of the pin, type in it in ascending order 3 white beads of different sizes, 1 small red beads and 3 more beads in descending order. Pull the line through the next eye and repeat the action. Do this until the circle ends.

Do not cut the line, continue to weave it. On the second circle, passing the fishing line through the last eye, collect 6 white beads, 1 red bead and 6 more white ones and again pass through the eye. Repeat the action until the circle ends.

At the end, fasten the fishing line, creating a loop for the ribbon if necessary.

This is a bright, cheerful snowflake - a great decor for a window or a Christmas tree.

Snowflake with crystals

What a charming snowflake with crystals, it seems to shine thanks to the golden bugle, as if reflects the bright sun on a frosty morning. To weave this snowflake you will need:

  • gold beads;
  • golden bugles;
  • blue crystals;
  • some blue beads;
  • fishing line.

We also attach a step-by-step diagram of how to make a snowflake from beads, glass beads and crystals.

See how simple these bright, sparkling snowflakes are woven. First, draw a circle of 8 crystals of blue color, then, following the diagram, withdraw the fishing line and pass the bugle, forming from the beads loops on which the next row will be held.

Tighten the fishing line tightly so that the snowflake holds its shape and can be hung on a window, Christmas tree or chandelier.
