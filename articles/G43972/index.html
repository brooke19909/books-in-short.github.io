Low-frequency oscillations: technological aspects of application

Among the whole variety of oscillatory movements, the closest person is in contact with low-frequency oscillations. In general, the classification of low-frequency oscillatory processes accepted in physics, and that one, is based on a person's ability to sense these vibrations in the form of sound. According to this classification, sound, also other vibrations, are divided into:

- infra-frequency (infrasound) range, where there are oscillations with frequencies up to 10 Hz;

- sound range, with fluctuations from 15 to 100 Hz;

- ultrasonic range, with oscillations above 1 kHz.

The most widespread fluctuations in the sound range, and not only because we can perceive them more often than others with the help of the senses, especially hearing. In addition, it should be taken into account that the peculiarity of human perception of vibrational phenomena in general is that our body perceives them selectively, precisely depending on the frequency. That is, if some frequencies we can perceive with the help of hearing, then other frequencies already affect vision, the vestibular apparatus, the circulatory system, etc.

By virtue of this circumstance, low-frequency oscillations become the subject of scientific interest of representatives of various sciences: radiophysics, astrophysics, wave research, military affairs, and even theories of historical pulsations.

Representatives of certain areas of science, technocrats, argue that low-frequency oscillations, their application, takes place only in the technogenic field created by man, technologies. Others, believe that the Universe itself, including social life, also represents low-frequency oscillations, the sources of their origin are seen in the existence of certain general laws of the development of being (dialectics).

To give a complete list of all these views is simply not necessary, but we will try to show the most unexpected aspects of life, where low-frequency oscillations manifest themselves.
