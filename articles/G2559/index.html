Do you know how to weave baubles from a mouline thread with patterns?

One of the most popular jewelry among teenagers has long been baubles. And this is understandable, because they look original and, in addition, the color of each thread woven into this bracelet carries a special meaning. So, for example, a bauble can become a symbol of strong friendship or a declaration of love. For a long time, Indians knew how to weave baubles from a mouline thread with patterns, and a little later, adherents of the hippie movement adopted this art. Today, these jewelry are an integral part of the stylish image of the younger generation. The process of creating these cute trinkets is very exciting and does not require any special skills, so anyone can cope with weaving a stylish bauble.



Materials and color matching

First of all, before you start work, you need to prepare materials for baubles made of mouline thread; patterns whose patterns also need to be selected in advance. They can be invented by yourself or use a sample. So, what you need to create a bracelet:

  • mouline thread of different colors;
  • scissors;
  • tailor pins.

If before, special attention was paid to the choice of thread color, now combinations for bracelets are selected, rather, simply from aesthetic preferences. However, each color has its own meaning. For example, a white thread means freedom and purity, yellow - beauty and faith, red - passionate love, black carries a hint of loneliness, green carries harmony, and purple symbolizes dreams.



Masters distinguish two types of weaving knots for a bracelet - simple and double. Patterns for baubles made of floss are also divided into straight and oblique.

Direct weaving

How to weave baubles from a mouline thread with stripe patterns using direct weaving? To begin work, a pair of threads is cut five times longer than the finished product, each color has two segments, laid out in the desired sequence, tied and fixed with tailor pins on a small pad.

Then they start weaving from left to right, tying all the segments alternately with an extreme thread, directing the knots to the left. In the same way, each time starting work from the left edge, they knit a bracelet of the desired length. It is very important to carefully knit knots of the same density, so that as a result get an even and smooth braid. Having understood the principle of how to weave baubles from a mouline thread with patterns of direct weaving, you can easily knit any inscription or pattern on such a braid.

After the desired length is reached, strong knots are tied on both sides and fasteners fastened. If there are none, then you can just tightly tie a knot and braid the braid from the free ends of the threads.



Oblique weaving

How to weave baubles made of floss with patterns "corners" or oblique weaving? For this decoration, paired threads are also needed, which are located in a mirror image. For example, two black, two blue, two yellow and two white - the first part, the second from the middle will go the opposite, starting with white. They begin work by linking the two central threads, after knitting knots, alternately departing in each row along the thread from the center. Going to the edge of the baubles, weaving is done first from the left edge to the middle, then from the right. To fix a row, two central threads are connected among themselves. For the tie, just as in the first description of a simple bauble, the free ends are woven into a tight braid.
