How to check debt before traveling abroad and not stay at home

A huge number of Russians travel abroad every day. Everyone has different goals: tourist trips, business trips, visits to relatives. Against the background of recently tightened debt collection measures by bailiffs of many of

travelers are concerned about the question of how to check the debt before traveling abroad.

Reasons for travel restrictions

There are quite a few reasons why traveling abroad may be prohibited. At the same time, verification is carried out directly at customs using an internal database.

The following categories of citizens are prohibited from traveling abroad:

  • Persons who, on duty, have access to state secrets. It is important to remember that the ban on leaving remains for several years after dismissal. All conditions are specified in the employment contract.
  • Persons in military or alternative service.
  • Criminal suspects and defendants prior to sentencing a court.
  • Convicted persons before the expiration of the sentence or until release.


There are few such people; they usually know that they are forbidden to travel abroad. Many more are those whose movements are limited due to their unpaid financial obligations. It is important for them to know how to check debts before going abroad.

Check out mechanism

Theoretically, each debtor, without any checks, knows about the ban on traveling abroad. The restriction mechanism is discussed below.

First, a lawsuit for collecting debt is filed with the court. After its satisfaction, a writ of execution is drawn up , on the basis of which the bailiffs submit to the debtor a demand for payment of the debt. However, it does not always reach the addressee, because the debtor can change his place of residence.

It is important to remember that traveling abroad may be limited if you do not pay the following types of payments:

  • taxes
  • fines, including traffic police;
  • alimony;
  • credit payments.

When planning trips, you can not worry only to those who are completely free from such obligations. The rest may simply not be aware of the presence of debt.

However, in any case, if money is not received in payment of the debt, the bailiffs check through the migration service information about the availability of a foreign passport from the debtor. After that, information on non-payment of debt is transmitted to the Border Control Office. The debtor is informed that his departure abroad is limited. Even if such information was not received at the citizen's address in order not to get into an unpleasant situation, it is important to check the availability of your name in the list of debtors.



Actions to verify

Those who are considering how to check their debts before traveling abroad can be advised to do this online on the official website of the Bailiff Service in the section on information on debt availability. However, it is important not to forget that the information on the site may be updated late. More accurate information can be obtained by contacting the bailiff's office at the place of registration personally.

If there is a debt for those traveling, they should be paid as soon as possible. At the border, the receipt will not help - you will need to wait for the exclusion from the list of debtors. This usually takes about three weeks. Therefore, when planning to travel abroad, the debt should be paid in advance.

Many, looking for information on how to check debts before traveling abroad, find out that restrictions can be circumvented. However, it is better to pay all payments on time.
