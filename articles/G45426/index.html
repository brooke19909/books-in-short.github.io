Marina Melia is a famous psychologist.

The famous psychologist Marina Melia from childhood knew what her calling was. She knew that she would help people find themselves. Therefore, she chose a profession related to psychology.

A brief biography of the psychologist



Marina Melia was born in Moscow in January 1948. Her mother was an oncologist, and her father was a military doctor. From the age of 15, she began working as a nurse in a hospital in the department of seriously ill people in order to gain experience for admission to a medical institute. From childhood she was fond of sports, she even received the title of USSR master of sports in skiing and cycling. Due to her great love for sports, she continued her studies at the Institute of Physical Culture with a degree in Sports Psychology. She also taught the psychology of sports and was the dean of the faculty. She also worked as a psychologist in various national teams of the Soviet Union. She was the head of the psychological service of sports of the USSR.

At the beginning of perestroika, Marina Melia, with her partner Vladimir Stolin, as well as a group of other psychologists from Moscow State University, created the first psychological cooperative Interact. She took the post of director there from 1987 to 1989. The first time they started offering services for various speeches, trainings, negotiations. A few years later, Marina Melia became the director of a Soviet-American company, where for the first time she used the assessment method to evaluate managers, conducting various seminars on the mission with the development of programs for each company.



Then she worked as a director of a consulting company, and since 1999, Marina - general director of the consulting company MM-Class. In this company, the main direction is various coaching techniques. In 2004, she was appointed a member of the board of directors of the famous Avangard Bank.
