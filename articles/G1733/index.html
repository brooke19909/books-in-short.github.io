Should I go to China in January - features, weather, interesting facts and reviews

January is the time of the New Year holidays, when you can relax from work and recharge with positive emotions. Someone chooses to meet with friends and lazy rest in front of the TV. Someone buys tickets and sets off on a long journey for new experiences. Recently, exotic lovers are increasingly traveling to China. In January, you can find a lot of entertainment for every taste.





Weather

January in China is a dry and rather cold month. The country is located in three climatic zones, so the weather in its different parts is significantly different. Northern provinces are known for frosty winters. People who want to go skiing come here. The temperature in China in January is on average -13 ...- 15 ¬∞ C. At night, drops to -25 degrees are possible. However, truly severe frosts are noted - up to the level of -30 ¬∞ C and below.

The central part is more comfortable. The average temperature is 0 degrees. Possible reductions to -5 o C. This does not deter lovers of excursions. The main thing is to stock up on warm clothes.

The southern areas are warm despite strong winds. On the mainland, the temperature rises to +19 during the day, slightly drops at night, but rarely drops below + 10 . The most southern point is Hainan in China. Weather in January on this subtropical island allows you to get a uniform tan. The average air temperature during the day is 24 degrees heat, water - 25 degrees. However, cold winds blow, and in the evening it becomes pretty cool - up to +13 .

Cost

Many companies offer to buy their tours to China in January. Prices traditionally rise in the first half of the month. This is due to the New Year holidays. But after the 15th, travelers can significantly save. The trip will be cheaper by 2-3 times. Please note that flights will cost more on weekdays. A package tour from operators will also save you from unnecessary expenses.



Tourists note that China is a good choice for spontaneous trips. Visa processing will take about 5 days. You can do it faster for an additional fee. Some cities, such as Hong Kong, Russians can visit without a visa if they are not going to stay there for more than two weeks.

Ski resorts

Fans of snow-capped peaks and downhill skiing are unlikely to be afraid of winter frosts. True, reservations should be made in advance at the best resorts. An excellent choice would be:

  • Yabuli. This resort is a national park. The best skiers of the country train here. It offers tourists more than 15 high-quality tracks of different levels of difficulty. Most are for downhill skiing, but cross-country skiers will not be disappointed either. Jumps are made for jumping. In addition to skiing and snowboarding, tourists will be offered many other winter activities.
  • Wanlun. This is a young resort, but quite popular. It is suitable for fans of freestyle and snowboarding, because there is a special zone for moguls. This is the name for skiing down the hilly slopes.
  • Nanshan. It is only 80 km from Beijing and is ideal for beginners. In your free time, you can try the sled track, play soccer in the snow or ride a snowmobile.

Exploring Attractions

However, you can see a lot of interesting things in other parts of the country. Holidays in China in January are great for visiting local attractions. Entrance tickets are cheaper, fewer tourists. True, national parks can open later than in the main season, and buses do not go so often, but you can adapt to this.





It is better to choose big cities and those parts of the country where the climate is quite mild. Tibet, for example, is not suitable for a winter pilgrimage due to low temperatures and unheated hotels. It will be much more comfortable to travel to:

  • Beijing, where you can admire the snow-covered Chinese wall, enter the Forbidden City, see many parks, temples, museums.
  • Hong Kong, where in January you can shop with big discounts, as well as enjoy the views of the modern metropolis, bow to the Big Buddha and personally count all 10,000 Buddhas in the monastery of the same name.
  • Guangzhou, where you can see 123 historical sights, as well as feel the special Chinese flavor of an industrial city.

Festivals and Holidays

If you travel to China in January, then keep in mind: January 1 is not customary to celebrate here on a grand scale, as in European countries. Of course, you can enjoy an amazingly beautiful salute against the background of the Lost City in Beijing at exactly 24:00, but it is much more interesting to be in the country for the Chinese New Year. It is celebrated on the first day of the new moon. It can occur in late January or early February. This is a noisy holiday when thousands of firecrackers and fireworks soar into the sky, and traditional red lanterns are lit everywhere. On the squares you can see theatrical performances, concerts. Fun lasts for 15 days.





Those who are not afraid of the cold should head to Harbin. In January, many tourists flock to the festival of ice sculptures. In the park you can stroll among the copies of world famous palaces, temples, mythical heroes and creatures. Illuminated from the inside, they amaze with their beauty and leave a lasting impression.





Wellness Tours

Winter is a time when you need to pay special attention to your health. Chinese medicine differs from European medicine in that it treats not individual diseases, but the whole organism. Many go to the Celestial Empire in the hope of restoring health, strengthening weakened immunity.

The best areas for the treatment of various diseases are:

  • Dalian, where many modern medical centers are located.
  • Removal with unique cold springs that help with heart problems, cure skin diseases, are useful for blood vessels, joints.
  • Anshan, where is the center "Tangan". Here they will help you recover after a stroke, successfully treat patients with paralysis, cerebral palsy.
  • Weihai, where you can get a general diagnosis of the body, as well as get help with gynecological diseases, problems with joints, digestion.
  • Hainan, where you can not only improve your health, but also rejuvenate.




Tourists reviews

What do travelers who have already visited China in January say? First of all, you should not rely on good weather and beach holidays. Those who prefer to splash around in the sea and bask in the hot sun should choose a different region. Even in Hainan, a windbreaker will be required in the evening, and in the central and northern provinces, a waterproof jacket, hat and scarf are indispensable.

But the cost of rest is pleasantly pleasing. At this time, you can see numerous attractions at very affordable prices. Experienced travelers are advised to visit the southern provinces, where winters are quite mild, as well as large cities, where you can always warm up in a cafe or shopping center. A lot of excitement is caused by the Chinese baths, visiting thermal springs. And of course, ski enthusiasts praise steep slopes and mountain peaks.

If you are looking for vivid emotions, colorful landscapes, exotic traditions - go to China. In January, it receives the most seasoned and hardy tourists. Those who are not afraid of dampness and cold winds. Those who are always open to the new and ready to go on a journey towards the unknown.
