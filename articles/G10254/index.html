International code: where does it come from?

Experienced travelers know that each international airport has its own designation with three letters of the Latin alphabet. These letters can be found on airline tickets, itinerary receipts, or even road signs. Where do they come from?

The fact is that before opening every international airport must pass certification. As a result of this audit, he receives admission to service of foreign companies and an individual code. In the case of Thessaloniki, the airport code is SKG.

An individual three-letter code is assigned by the International Air Transport Association, which certifies all airports. Also, this organization is a checking and controlling body that monitors the quality of services provided at each airport in the world.

International Airport: A Brief History

SKG Airport dates back to the thirties of the last century, then it was called Thessaloniki Mikra Airport and was located in the suburbs of Thessaloniki. An airfield was built for military purposes and only in the middle of the last century began to serve civilian aircraft. Every year, its passenger flow increased, and the Greek authorities were forced several times to rebuild and modernize such an important air hub.

In the sixties of the twentieth century, the airport was moved to a new place and completely rebuilt. At the same time, it received a modern name and its international code, which is still in use today.
