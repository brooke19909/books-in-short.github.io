History and Modernity

More than half a century ago, in the sixties of the last century, near the village of Shukolovo in the suburbs, a training base for the children and youth school of the Olympic reserve was founded. It was led by Leonid Tyagachev. This school has nurtured more than one generation of Olympians. On the slopes in Shukolovo mastered the skill of many became famous Russian skiers. After the collapse of the Soviet Union, the base did not remain forgotten or abandoned. On the contrary, it turned into a ski resort named after Leonid Tyagachev. The ban on letting outsiders here (not students of the Sports School) was lifted, and now everyone can try their hand at skiing. Although the members of the Russian national team in snowboarding, slalom and other winter sports continue to train here. Where is the ski resort

We already mentioned that the training base is located near the village of Shukolovo. This is Dmitrovsky district, in the northern part of the Moscow region. Getting to the ski resort is pretty easy. If you decide to go on vacation in your car, you need to turn off the Moscow Ring Road onto Dmitrovskoye Shosse. After forty kilometers there will be a pointer to Shukolovo and the Tyagachev Active Leisure Club. The ski resort is also available to those tourists who travel by public transport. Nearby is a train station where trains stop from Moscow. The recreation center named after Tyagachev is known to all local residents. After all, it has been working since 2001, and functions year-round. For many Muscovites, it has become a favorite destination for winter and summer holidays.



Resort Description
