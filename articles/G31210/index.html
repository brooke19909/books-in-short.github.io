Oled Technology

First, a few words about the technology itself. Oled (from English organic light-emitting diode) is a special technology based on a semiconductor device made of special organic compounds. These compounds can actively emit light if an electric current is passed through them. The fact that organic materials are capable of electroluminescence (non-thermal luminescence, which is excited by electricity) was learned back in the 1950s. The discovery was made by the French scientist Andre Bernanoz.

But how is this technology used in televisions? What are the highlights of Oled TVs? What it is? You can read about this and much more in this article.

Oled TVs: what is it?





Old technologies can be found in the modern world. A great example of this is Oled TVs. What is it and what does it eat with? You will find answers to these questions here.

A characteristic feature of Oled-TVs is the matrix, in which organic LEDs are the main functional elements. That is, in these televisions, organic elements are combined with electronics. By passing an electric discharge through natural compounds, they get a bright glow. And using phosphors, you can get high-quality glow of any color.

In theory, this sounds very good, but how is everything in practice? In fact, TVs with an Oled-screen have a lot of advantages, which we will talk about below.

Positive features





Perhaps the first and most important advantage is the incredibly high brightness and contrast settings. Organic LEDs provide a wide range of illumination. As for contrast, on the Oled-TV you can get a phenomenal level of 10,000,000 to 1. Of course, such indicators are unattainable for other TVs.

Another advantage is compactness. Oled-TVs have very small dimensions and weight. Again, all thanks to the very same organic LEDs that have amazing electroluminescence. Because of this, the need for backlighting is completely eliminated. Accordingly, without backlight, a lot of space is saved in the case.

In addition, unusual LEDs allow manufacturers to produce new matrices that have a very large viewing angle. This means that the Oled-TV can be viewed from any angle and the picture quality will remain at the same level without any distortion. This feature will probably come in handy for those who like to watch movies in the family circle or in a large group of friends.

It is also worth noting that the matrices for Oled-TVs have an amazing response time. Even in the most dynamic scenes or when the landscape changes abruptly, you will not notice an inertial footprint that is directly related to the great response.

disadvantages





In addition to the advantages of Oled TVs, there are also disadvantages. Perhaps the main drawback of such a device is its fragility. Organic LEDs cannot boast a long lifespan. Red and green LEDs can work for tens of thousands of hours (a pretty decent indicator). The main problem is that the blue LED works for about 18 thousand hours (something about two years). At the moment, active development of durable LEDs is underway. In the meantime, buyers are forced to enjoy the amazing image for only a couple of years, and then watch the distorted image.

Another drawback that does not allow many to purchase an OLED TV is the price. Compared with Led TVs, Oled models are sky-high. After all, the most interesting thing is that everything should be the other way around. In theory, the production of Oled displays is much less expensive. And this means that TVs with organic LEDs should be cheaper than liquid crystal ones. But why is everything completely different in practice? The thing is that the production technology of Oled displays has not yet been fully mastered. This, in turn, leads to a significant increase in price.
