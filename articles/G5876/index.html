M. "Alekseevskaya". Peace Avenue

M. "Alekseevskaya" is located on the Kaluga-Riga line. The station has only one exit, and it leads to Mira Avenue and Novoalekseevskaya Street. The station has been renamed several times. Why did she change the name? What was previously where the metro station " Alekseevskaya " is located today?





Village Alekseevskoe

Like many other Moscow stations, metro station " Alekseevskaya " inherited its name from the village. According to historical data, in the XV century there was a small village, which included no more than twenty yards. True, this settlement was called a little differently - " Olekseevskoe " . He is mentioned for the first time in the spiritual letter of Basil I.

In the documents of the 17th century, the spelling of the name of the village changed, but still did not coincide with the modern one. Where today is located Art. m. " Alekseevskaya " , once there was the estate of Zakhary Kopytov , hence the name " Kopytovo Alekseyevsky identity " At the beginning of the 17th century the village passed to Dmitry Trubetskoy , who built a stone church on its territory. Later it was renamed Alekseevskoe.

Station "Scherbakovskaya"

The construction of metro station " Alekseevskaya " began in the early fifties. The opening took place in 1954. But almost immediately, the station was renamed. She was named in honor of the then politician Shcherbakov. And four years later it was renamed again. Station " Mir " was present at the Moscow metro very briefly. M. "Prospect Mira", metro station "Alekseevskaya" are located on the same branch. Both the first and the second belong to the Kaluga-Kaluga line. From Prospekt Mira , a transition to the ring branch is carried out.

In order to avoid confusion, and also for a number of reasons, the station, which is discussed in today's article, was renamed several times in the 50-60s. But the modern name of metro station " Alekseevskaya " received only in the nineties. For more than thirty years it was called " Shcherbakovskaya " .





Architectural features

This station, as already mentioned, has only one way out. The inclined path for escalators was built from the bottom up - a unique case in the history of the construction of the Moscow metro.

The depth of the station is 51 meters. It was built according to the design of Yu. Kolesnikova and S. Kravets . The appearance of the lobby of the metro station " Alekseevskaya " has much in common with such stations as " Frunzenskaya " , " VDNH " , " Riga " , " Prospect Mira " , " University " . The interior of the hall is decorated in a rather restrained style, milky-colored tiles were used in the decoration. The floor is laid out in red and gray granite. The station is lit by chandeliers suspended from the ceiling. It is worth telling about one of the streets onto which the exit from metro station " Alekseevskaya " is carried out.





Way to Yaroslavl

The avenue is located between the Garden Ring and Sukharevskaya Square, is a continuation of the Yenisei Street and Sretenka. In the 17th century , the road to Yaroslavl began from here. In Moscow, this path passed through Rostokino, Alekseevskoye and other villages. Around this time, the Meshchanskaya Sloboda arose here, after which several Moscow streets were later named.

In the second half of the XIX century, the territory of the modern Avenue of the World was actively built up with mansions and apartment buildings. At the end of the next century, a number of buildings appeared here, most of which, of course, have not survived to this day. At the beginning of the thirties of the last century, Mira Avenue, like many other Moscow streets, was paved, but not very successfully. Specialists from an American company who apparently did not know anything about severe Russian frost took part in the construction work.

Construction Avenue

The coating crumbled after two years, unable to withstand the winter cold. In 1934, the street was expanded, eliminating tram tracks. For the same purpose, the front gardens located next to residential buildings were eliminated. In 1936, a large-scale reconstruction of the avenue began. Design and construction was carried out in a short time. As a result of the hasty reconstruction, the architectural appearance of the street was significantly damaged. Specialists, creating projects of new houses, did not take into account the style of buildings that have existed for several centuries. And soon mass housing construction unfolded here. After the war, in 1949, several hundred poplars were planted along the avenue. Eight years later, the Second Rostokinsky Bridge was built.
