Denon DA-10

Hello from Japan. Denon DA-10 is a typical representative of an amplifier with a USB-DAC from the Land of the Rising Sun. As is the case with other models of the company, the TI PCM1795 chip became the heart of the amplifier. The chip is capable of converting analog sound at a frequency of 192 kilohertz by the method of pulse code modulation. The case of the device is made of plastic with decorative aluminum inserts. Nothing interesting, it looks too simple and even a little tasteless. And it also affects the damage resistance.





As for the sound, everything is much better here. The ASIO driver is configured perfectly.
