About JBL

This company was founded in 1946 by Mr. Lansing. He was an outstanding acoustic engineer. The very first speaker system released under the JBL brand has become very popular. Since then, companies have been successful. And all would be fine, but Mr. Lansing was a bad businessman. He drove a prosperous company into debt and then hanged himself. The case was inherited by the vice president. And already with him, the company turned to full. She released several speakers that have become truly popular. Then the company was sold to the famous concern "Harman". But this did not affect the quality of the products. The JBL brand still exists and pleases users with high-quality speaker systems. Headphones deserve special mention. The manufacturer's assets include both professional overhead models and in-ear headphones for everyday use. A striking representative of the last class is the JBL T100 headphones. We will review reviews about them later. In the meantime, let's talk about the scope of delivery.

Key Specifications

These in -ear headphones are equipped with a dynamic type emitter with a diameter of 8 millimeters. Expect something more from the product for such ridiculous money is not worth it. However, this emitter provides a fairly high sound quality. The lower bar of the frequency range starts at 20 hertz. The maximum sampling rate is 22,000 hertz. This is a great indicator for very budget headphones. The sensitivity of these in-ear headphones is at a level of 100 dB. This is a great result. Their resistance is such that almost any smartphone can swing them, not to mention specialized players. The length of the wire is 1.2 meters. The type of plug is 3.5 millimeters. The connector itself is gold-plated and straight-shaped, which makes its use with a smartphone even more convenient. Now let's talk about the sound quality in the JBL T100. Sound quality

So, what can these in-ear headphones offer the average user? The sound they have is quite peculiar. The company's engineers clearly focused on low and high frequencies. As a result, the middle turned out to be a little greased. In this case, the equalizer will help out well. Just unscrew the middle a little and remove the tall ones. And the bass can not be touched at all. Then the headphones will play. However, due to the characteristics of the sound, they play reliably only in certain genres. Electronic music, pop and other simple styles work well. Fans of instrumental rock, metal, punk, doom and other things will have to fiddle with the equalizer for a long time. But it's worth it. If you find the optimal settings, then these headphones will sound no worse than the Sennheiser, which are quite expensive. Headphones JBL T100 (black and white - no other options are provided) play much better than expected "plugs" of this class. Now let's move on to user reviews.

Negative headphone reviews

There were those who criticized the JBL T100. Reviews of a negative nature are surprisingly constructive. The headset has problems. And users mark it. For example, true connoisseurs of sound say that the headphones sound fundamentally wrong: the middle is failed, low and high are twisted to the maximum. And indeed it is. Only a quality equalizer can save the situation. Also, the owners note that although the wire is of high quality, but at the slightest touch it makes noise in the headphones themselves. And this is not good. Another complaint is the direct 3.5 mm connector. Most users would be happy if it were in the shape of the letter "G". It is more comfortable. Another complaint is the lack of a special clip for the wire. It seems to be a trifle, but more pleasant when the wires do not hang out anywhere. Negative comments were also awarded to the headphones themselves (more precisely, their design). The location of the membranes is not round, but oval.
