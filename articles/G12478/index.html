Moscow, River Station (metro station)

The metro is a reason for pride among residents of a city like Moscow. River Station is one of its terminal stations, which is located in the northern part of the capital. It got its name thanks to the Northern River Station, which is a few meters away and is now one of the busiest metro stops in the capital.

The station at the station was repeatedly planned: first in 1938, then in 1947 and 1957. Construction began only in the early 60s and was completed by the end of 1964. Until 1975, the station was the northernmost of all in the Moscow metro.

The station was repeatedly offered to rename, but each time the initiative did not find support from the population. Since the 60s, the northern part of the green line (where the Rechnoy Vokzal station is located) has been one of the busiest, which is why it often organizes express trains from the terminal station to Voikovskaya.

The appearance of the station





Rechnoy Vokzal - a metro station (Moscow), made according to a standard project, it was built at a depth of 6 meters. It was built during the time of N. S. Khrushchev, who demanded that his subordinates eliminate all the excesses that were present in construction and design at that time. Thanks to this, the station has a very modest appearance. All walls are decorated with white and light green ceramic tiles. When creating the station, red marble with white spots and gray granite were also used.

Paleontologists call the station unique: it is here that you can see a small number of fossils in the form of various shells. In the middle of the hall there is a pair of benches, their number is small, since the station is final and passengers usually do not linger on it. The vestibules were made using standard techniques: glass and reinforced concrete structures - the main materials.

How does the station work?





All metro stations in the capital begin to work at different times, which is what distinguishes Moscow from other cities. M. "River Station" opens for passengers at 5:35 in the morning on odd numbers and 10 minutes later - on even numbers . Last passengers must leave the subway and this station before one in the morning. As of 2016, the station serves up to 300 thousand passengers daily, which is very important for it.

The city with the largest number of metro trains in the world so far is Moscow. River Station serves approximately 8% of their total. The first train leaves the station at 5:40 a.m. on odd numbers and at 5:54 a.m. on even numbers . At the weekend, the departure schedule remains practically unchanged, with the exception of even numbers, then the first train leaves for the center at 5:55. When traveling, be sure to consider that on weekends the interval between trains is longer than on weekdays.
