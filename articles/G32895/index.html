How to close loops when knitting

The knitting process is very exciting and takes long hours of woman's free time. Three basic principles that make the knitting process possible are patience for the knitter, the necessary yarn and tools for making the product.



No matter how strange it may seem, in ancient times only men were engaged in this type of needlework, and all the knitting methods known to us were invented by them. The first ancient knitting needles were made of ordinary twigs. Later, women joined the process and left the privilege to engage in this skill for themselves.

The range of knitting accessories is now so large that you can choose for yourself knitting needles or a hook made of materials such as metal, plastic, bone, wood. It is enough to make a choice and you can begin to engage in an exciting hobby. Before starting, they usually choose the necessary model of the product. As a rule, knitting methods are so diverse that you can create the same vest with completely different patterns.

The process ends by closing the row. At the very end, the needlewoman needs to know how to close the loops on the details of the product. This can be done with knitting needles or with a knitting hook.

Close the loops with knitting needles

Now we will learn how to close the loops with knitting needles in two ways.



The first way. The hinges that have already been closed look like a pigtail, even and neat in appearance. You can close them tightly or weakly - it depends on the quality and volume of the threads. Closing the hinges in this way, we can be sure that the edge of the product will never stretch. You can apply this method on any product or pattern. It looks like stretching loops, one through the other.

We take the edge loop, we knit together with the first loop with the front viscous, clinging to the back walls. As a result, you get one loop. Now we carry it from the right knitting needle in the opposite direction - to the left knitting needle. We knit it together with the next loop with the front viscous, always clinging to the back walls. We do such manipulations to the very end of the row. At the very end, we break the thread, leaving a tail 4-5 centimeters long. Now you know how to close the hinges, but make sure that the canvas is not tightly tightened. With this method, you can use a short crochet hook. It allows you to not tighten the loop strongly, and the process itself will go much faster.





The second way. The loops thus closed represent an elastic pigtail that is able to stretch well.

It can be used for any patterns, even for the most prominent.

Any details of your product can also be made in this way.

Remove the edge loop on the right knitting needle, and knit the second one strictly according to the picture: it can be a front or wrong knitting.

You should get two loops on the right needle. Insert the left knitting needle into the removed loop, pull lightly and pull the second through it.

Then knit the third loop, stretching through it the second on the right knitting needle. Do all the manipulations to the end of the row. Now you are familiar with how to close the loops in a second, no less easy and interesting way.
