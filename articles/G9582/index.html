Geneva City, Switzerland - Attractions, Features and Weather

The capital of Switzerland, Geneva, is the country's most popular cultural center. The city, surrounded by picturesque mountains, has its own symbol, which is an image of a fountain, personifying the desire upward, towards the sky, to the kingdom of spirits.

The capital of Switzerland is also the administrative center of the state federal unit of the same name. It is located on the western shore of Lake Geneva, the largest in the country.



The capital of Switzerland is a real crossroads of human life and thought. This is a place that many great people have visited. It is worth saying that on the territory of the modern city in 58 BC By the great Julius Caesar, a border post was established to protect the Empire.

History

Geneva (Switzerland) until 534 was part of the Kingdom of Burgundy. After it, the French conquered. By the end of the ninth century, Geneva had become the capital of the newly created Kingdom of Burgundy.

The heyday of the present capital of Switzerland fell on the period from 1290 to 1536. Then the rulers of the city were the Dukes of Savoy. In these historical times Protestantism found a hot response in the hearts of Geneva.

In the seventeenth century, the city received great development in the economic sphere. During this period, he became the world capital of watchmakers and jewelers. In the eighteenth century, Voltaire and Rousseau worked in Geneva.

At the turn of the nineteenth and twentieth centuries, Geneva (Switzerland) became the seat of the League of Nations and the international organization Red Cross. After the end of World War II, the UN headquarters was located here.

Geneva (Switzerland) has a reputation for being a center for various conferences and international congresses. It operates more than one hundred and twenty banks. That is why Geneva is also called a major center of finance and business.

Nature

Geneva (Switzerland) is a city whose landscapes are simply breathtaking. Parks and bays, alleys and rivers - the surrounding nature is striking in its virgin beauty. To the north of the city is the famous winemaking area. In this territory, landscapes delight the eye with centuries-old handsome oaks and peaceful small villages.



There are magnificent parks along Lake Geneva. Many on its shores and small towns. The magnificent Alps decorate the city landscape. It seems that they can be reached by hand.

Quite interesting is the very location of the city of Geneva (Switzerland). The intersection of two rivers, whose waters are found in the capital, is an amazing sight. At this point, a bright contrast of colors is visually observed. Why is Ron and Arva so different? Geneva (Switzerland) is a meeting place for rivers that differ in the number and type of suspended solids.

The source of the Rhone is Lake Leman. Approaching Geneva, the river enriches its waters with a high level of silt masses. Arva nutrition, on the contrary, comes from glaciers located in the Chamonix Valley. Meeting together, the light and dark waters of the two rivers flow for some time in parallel. This is where an amazing contrast of two colors is observed.

Residents of the capital

The city of Geneva (Switzerland) until 1870 was considered the most densely populated in the country. Currently, Zurich is in first place for this indicator. Somewhat ahead of Geneva and Basel. The population of this city is several thousand more than the capital. Today, one hundred eighty thousand inhabitants live in Geneva.





This is the population of the city. However, the entire Geneva territory includes seven more small settlements. Their list includes the following:

- Vernier, whose population totals thirty thousand people;

The capital of Switzerland ranks first in the number of foreigners living in the city. Forty percent of the population is represented by more than one hundred and eighty nationalities.

Climate

The weather conditions in Geneva are characteristic of the Central European territory. In spring, the average air temperature ranges from three to thirteen degrees. Summer is cool. In the warmest period, the air warms up to sixteen to eighteen degrees. In winter, a slight frost can be observed. The average air temperature in this period is in the range from minus four to plus four degrees. Geneva autumn is also cool. It is characterized by an average temperature of six to twelve degrees of heat.

Old Town Tours

Sights of Geneva (Switzerland) will not leave indifferent any tourist. An unforgettable impression will certainly leave the fountain of Railway O. It is the main attraction of the city. Within a second, the fountain ejects five hundred liters of water. In this case, the jets reach a height of 147 m. You can always determine the direction of the wind from the water loop of the fountain .



A unique and curious attraction of the city is the floral clock. Located on the Promenade du Lac, they have the world's largest second hand. Its length is two and a half meters. The total diameter of the watch is 5 m.
The Rhone River divides the capital of Switzerland into two parts. On the left bank is the old center of Geneva with a town hall, a cathedral and narrow streets. In this part of the city there are many cafeterias, shops, as well as interesting museums. The right bank is occupied by international Geneva. In this part of the city is the UN Palace, as well as the buildings of world-famous organizations.



The main attraction of the left-bank of Geneva is St. Peter's Basilica. Not far from this Protestant temple is the Tavel House. By visiting it, you can find out the detailed history of the city. A little further away is the square with the Town Hall. In one of the halls of this historic building in 1864 the signing of the Geneva Convention of the Red Cross took place.

A tour of the old part of the city will certainly lead any tourist to the bastion park. This is a beautiful place located on the remains of former city fortifications. On the one hand, the park borders on the university, and on the other, on the so-called Wall of Reformers.

The monument erected by General Dufour can be seen in the very center of New Square. This commander at one time united Switzerland. The Rat Museum, which is the site of the largest art exhibitions, is also located on the same square. Nearby is the old Plenaple Cemetery. On its territory is the tomb of Jorge Luis Borges. On the island, located in the middle of the Rhone River, is the Center for the Arts, open in the premises where slaughterhouses were located in the past. There you can admire the ancient tower, which was once built by bishops.

Transport

The most practical and cheapest way to get around the capital of Switzerland is by walking. Only they will allow tourists to feel the measured rhythm of the city and enjoy its views. For those who want to save time, there is public transport. Rides on it are quite affordable. A large number of bus and tram lines in Geneva start in front of the main train station, on Cornavin Square. Using bus route number 8, you can easily get directly to the Palace of Nations.

Public transport runs from five in the morning until midnight. Tickets can be purchased either at vending machines installed at stops, or at the central station in the offices of transport companies, where you will be offered free maps of local routes.



There are also trolleybuses and commuter trains in Geneva. In order to use their services, you will need to purchase tickets directly at the stops.

Geneva can rent a bike. It is best to ride in urban areas. Cycling through the streets of the capital is difficult due to the large traffic flow and cobblestone bridges.

How to get there

The city of Geneva (Switzerland) has a privileged geographical position. From Paris or Milan you can reach it in just an hour, and from Rome, London or Madrid - in two. From Moscow, you can get there in three and a half hours.

So, your destination is Switzerland (Geneva). The airport of this city is located on the territory of two states simultaneously. In addition to Switzerland, it is also France.

Today, Geneva International Airport is able to accept all existing types of aircraft and serve 12 million passengers a year. In order to get to the capital of Switzerland upon arrival, you need to get a free train ticket in a special machine that runs to the city. The trip from the airport to Geneva takes only six minutes.
