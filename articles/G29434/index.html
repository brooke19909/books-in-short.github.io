Samples of balance sheets. Sample balance sheet filling

Many Russian companies are required to provide the Federal Tax Service balance sheet. This document includes figures reflecting business turnovers, expenses, and liabilities. Therefore, the balance sheet is the most important source of information indicating the level of enterprise performance. How should this document be compiled? What can look like samples of balance sheets?

What is a balance sheet?

To start, let us dwell on some theoretical questions. So, before you examine the samples of the balance sheets, it is useful to consider the essence of this phenomenon. The term under consideration most often refers to the methodology for grouping and classifying information that reflects the organization‚Äôs property assets in financial terms.





The balance sheet, as a rule, has 2 sections - asset and liability. The first reflects property distributed by structure and location, the second - is classified based on its sources. The balance sheet is provided at the end of the year.

Is a balance sheet required?

In the general case, it is mandatory. Drawing up a balance sheet for Russian enterprises is a procedure enshrined in law. An exception is established for individual entrepreneurs - provided that entrepreneurs keep a book of accounting for income and expenses. Enterprises registered as LLC must provide balance, even if they operate using the simplified tax system.

Balance Forms

The key document is legally defined by which the company must report in the aspect of accounting indicators to the state - form 0710001, corresponding to OKUD. Or, as it is often informally called an accountant, form 1. The balance sheet, a sample of which we will consider in the article, can also be complete or simplified. Based on what criteria can an enterprise choose the first or second reporting option?

Which form to choose: full or simplified?

Strictly speaking, the choice does not depend on the preferences of the organization‚Äôs leadership, but on the provisions of the law. Therefore, the main thing is to interpret them correctly in order to then fill out the correct form of the balance sheet. Of course, the company in any case can choose the full scheme of compiling the appropriate type of reporting. But working with form 1 in this version is characterized by an incomparably greater complexity than when choosing a simplified document. Firms, therefore, whenever possible strive to work according to a second, simpler scheme. But for this they must meet a number of criteria:

  • the share of the authorized capital of the company owned by legal entities should not exceed 25%;
  • the average number of employees per year should not exceed 100 people;
  • the company's revenue should not be more than 400 million rubles. in a year.

Only in accordance with the above points can a company send a simplified balance sheet to the Federal Tax Service. We will consider a sample of reporting in this format a bit later. Now we will study the specifics of compiling Form 1 in its full version. We agree that we have to draw up a balance sheet for the year. A sample of filling out the relevant document for other reporting periods (quarter, half year and 9 months) will generally be characterized by a similar structure.

Balance sheet requirements

Before studying the actual algorithm for compiling the required reports at the Federal Tax Service, we will consider some legislative nuances regarding the requirements for documents of this type.





So, many lawyers recommend paying attention to the following requirement: financial indicators reflecting the activities of all structures of a legal entity, including branches and representative offices in any settlements, should be included in the balance sheet. Another important requirement is that if the balance is passed by the LLC, then the figures reflected in the document must be approved at the general meeting of the company owners. Form 1 should be supplemented by audit reports, if provided by joint-stock companies, banks or insurance organizations.

Filling the balance sheet: what to look for

So, we examined the samples of the balance sheets - complete and simplified. It‚Äôs easy to fill out the relevant documents if you have access to the necessary accounts for which the balance is recognized. However, no matter how logical the sample of the enterprise balance sheet is, it is useful to take into account a number of the most important nuances of filling it out. Consider them.





Form 1 of the balance sheet must be signed by two officials - the director and chief accountant of the company. This ensures that the information provided by the company to the Federal Tax Service is correct.

Before filling out the balance, you need to make sure that work is in progress with Form 1, approved by order of the Ministry of Finance No. 66n of July 2, 2010, and not with any other document.

When compiling the appropriate type of reporting, it is recommended to be guided by the criteria prescribed in PBU 4/99, namely in section 4 of this source.

If the company has been operating for more than a year, then indicators for previous periods should be indicated in the current report. In accordance with paragraph 10 of PBU 4/99, the corresponding figures can be adjusted (if there are necessary explanations for the balance sheet).

If any indicators in Form 1 are negative, then they must be entered using parentheses. Any correct sample balance sheets must meet this criterion.

When to provide balance in the Federal Tax Service?

It is necessary to submit Form 1 to the Federal Tax Service before April 1 of the year following the reporting year. The date of fulfillment of this obligation corresponds to the time of sending the specified document by mail or its actual transfer to the Federal Tax Service. However, if it is a day off, then the date on which Form 1 is provided is the next business day. Therefore, firms should be careful when reporting close to deadlines.

Balance sheet value

The provision of the appropriate type of reporting, as we noted above, is the obligation of Russian enterprises established by law. However, not only the Federal Tax Service may be interested in obtaining the necessary figures from the company. The fact is that the indicators recorded in Form 1 are able to give, on the whole, a quite adequate idea of ‚Äã‚Äãhow things are going on at the enterprise, and how effective its business model is.





This information may be useful, for example, for investors, lenders, potential partners of the company. In this regard, the interest in the timely preparation of the balance sheet should be shown primarily by the management of the company.
