Aquafiltration process

Almost all vacuum cleaners have a single operating principle, which is the accumulation of dust in special bags. As a rule, this affects the intensity of absorption and the quality of cleaning. The water filter operates on a different principle. Water is considered a natural filter that can trap absorbed chemical compounds. When the vacuum cleaner absorbs dust and debris, they fall into the water together with air, where they are deposited. Purified air passing through a thin filtration system exits. At the same time, the filters are not clogged, and the process taking place in the aquafiltration mode does not affect the dust suction power.





Using HEPA filters, a multilayer cleaning system is created, which is almost one hundred percent. Additional porous filters absorb even spores of fungi.

After the next cleaning, wash the tank and dry it well to avoid unpleasant odors. This is especially important after cleaning with an aquafilter. When wet cleaning use special cleaning solutions.
