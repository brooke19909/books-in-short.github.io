Origami tulip: scheme for children, a master class

This type of creativity, such as origami, is in great demand among people of different ages, because from an ordinary sheet of paper in just a few minutes you can create an original product, whether it be a flower, a toy or an animal figurine. It is very easy to make an origami-tulip according to the scheme for children, but before you start creating masterpieces from plain paper, you need to delve deeper into history and learn more about this type of decorative art.

What is origami?

Origami is a type of creativity in which paper is the only material. However, before starting to create creations, you need to familiarize yourself with the basic rules for creating such products.





The art of translating into reality the incredible beauty of paper crafts was born in 610 in Japan. In the East, the square was treated with special respect. In Buddhism, he was considered a reflection of the universe. The Japanese themselves considered paper to be something light, short-lived and fragile. In things from it, they caught the breath of eternity, which you need to be able to feel. Thus, origami art was born. The miracle of technology is that glue, scissors and other improvised tools are not needed to create paper crafts. It is enough just to have a great desire to build a beautiful craft - an origami tulip from paper with your own hands.

Today, everyone is keen on this art - from small to large. Paper figures are created by children, housewives, teenagers, artists. This technique is taught at school, in creative circles, books and magazines are published on origami.

If we talk about children, the great interest among young creators is the opportunity to create a new toy. But an origami tulip, the scheme for which is quite simple for children, is also easy.

Types of Origami

Origami has many performance techniques. At the very beginning of the revival of this art, all the figures were composed exclusively of a square sheet of paper. Over time, sheets of various shapes began to be used to make paper figures. So there was a non-classical origami, which is called modular. Its principle is that the craft is assembled from small parts-modules that are inserted into each other. According to the same scheme, an origami tulip of modules is formed.





In Japan, the modular figure of a tyrannosaurus is in great demand, in Canada they even released a book that details the process of assembling this animal from paper.

Paper tulip

The origami paper tulip pattern for children is so simple that even beginners can handle it. Putting an origami flower is not at all difficult. The main thing is to clearly follow the pattern. Crafts of such a plan can be added to children of preschool and school age. If you collect the whole class, you can create a huge paper bouquet of colorful tulips.

Origami Tulip Pattern

Before folding an origami tulip with your own hands, you need to prepare the necessary materials for this. For crafts, you will need:

  • colored paper;
  • scissors;
  • ruler.

Tulips are flowers that can be presented in different shades. It would be better if you use special origami paper for crafts, but if you don’t have one at hand, ordinary A4 paper is suitable, from which you need to cut a square. If several children participate in the process, it is necessary to prepare a certain number of paper sheets.

So, how is an origami tulip made for children? First you need to add up the basic origami form - the “water bomb”. The square must be folded in half, the field of which to do it again. Next, the tulip blank must be turned over and folded again. When folding a tulip, you need to follow the pattern and do it strictly along the intended lines.

As a result, a large triangle should form a sheet of square shape. The corners of the figure must be bent up, turn the workpiece over and bend them again. Corners should be bent to the points indicated in the figures. Then one corner needs to be tucked inside another.





Next, the workpiece is turned over and the same actions are carried out with it. For the tulip to blossom, you need to blow into its base, the field of which slightly bend the petals, as shown in the diagram.

Origami-tulip, made according to the scheme for children, is ready, now you need to make a stem for it. To do this, take green paper and collect according to the scheme. The square must be folded diagonally and deployed. The sides should be bent to the central fold. The lower corners should also be bent to this place.





The bottom corner should be folded up. Fold the blank in half by the dotted line, the field of which it should be bent from the stem. The last stage is the assembly of two parts of the flower. Creating an origami-tulip according to the scheme for children, you need to fold two blanks - a bud and a stalk.
