Boston (Massachusetts) - The Cradle of the American Revolution

It was from this oldest city, founded in 1630, that the war for US independence from Britain began. The economically developed Boston (Massachusetts) became the birthplace of the American Revolution. It was here that the first clashes took place. Now it is a thriving city with a developed economy and well-established trade relations, and in the field of higher education, it has been and remains a pioneer, because the universities of the state capital are world-famous. And this place is famous for the fact that it was here that the social network Facebook appeared.

English corner

Boston (Massachusetts), ranked among the most beautiful cities in the United States, is considered the English corner of America. Urban architectural structures are markedly different from other structures in the country. Tourists who come to see local sights always note that the appearance of Boston is similar to the European one.





Freedom Trail

All the cultural and historical monuments of the city are close to each other, and an exciting journey begins with the Freedom Trail - a tourist route highlighted in bright color on the cobblestones. 16 amazing sights dating back to the period of struggle for independence, pass through the whole of Boston. Photos in some of them (for example, in the house of the national hero Paul Revere) are prohibited, they immediately warn about this at the entrance.

Boston University

One of the world's most famous educational institutions is included in the list of major universities of the country. This is a cultural monument, founded in the middle of the XIX century, and there are taught bachelors and masters in 250 programs, 2 educational buildings are located in the center of the capital. A well-developed campus is the pride of the university.

City garden

Boston (Massachusetts) is famous for its picturesque city garden - a favorite corner of all citizens and tourists. Located in the very center of the capital, it is a real oasis of lush greenery and centuries-old trees framing the fountains of fantastic beauty.





The park is easy to walk around along trails ornately located around a small pond. Unhurried walks necessarily end with the becoming traditional among the locals boating in the shape of swans.

Witch Museum of Salem

We can not talk about an unusual museum located in a suburb of Boston. Famous for his many horror films, Salem several centuries ago staged a show trial for girls who were recognized as possessed. After that, the city became involved in a real witch hunt, which became an integral part of that time.





Visitors shudder at the sight of terrible and sophisticated torture devices, and the half-darkness, the corresponding scenery and figures in hoodies seem to plunge into the nightmarish history of a small city. Upon leaving the gloomy museum with an oppressive atmosphere, tourists are waiting for souvenir shops with the corresponding witch's attributes.

Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum

The city of Boston offers all genuine art lovers a visit to another museum that will leave only the most positive impressions. Numerous exhibits have been collected for a long time by an American who adores art and bequeathed a rich collection to the city after her death.

Here are stored unique works of Ancient Rome, the Middle Ages, ancient manuscripts that have no price. And the private museum became known to the whole world after a daring robbery in 1990, when the kidnappers stole paintings of Rembrandt, Manet and other painters in excess of $ 500 million. Until today, stolen brilliant works have not been returned to Boston (Massachusetts).

And he will tell many things himself: visitors listen to a recorded video in which the politician recalls cases from childhood and school years, and also shares with all the moments of the formation of his career.
