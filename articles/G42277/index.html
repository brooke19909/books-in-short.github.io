Tanais - ancient city

This is the northernmost point of ancient civilization. It is located on the Dead Donets River. Historians and geographers are still arguing about which river in those days was called Tanais - Seversky Donets, Don or Dead Donets.



Tanais was founded in the 3rd century BC by Greek merchants. They came from the Bosporus kingdom, located in eastern Crimea. He became the northernmost Greek city. An important strategic role was assigned to him - control of the entire northern Black Sea coast.

Tanais was surrounded on all sides by a variety of tribes - from the Scythians to the Sarmatians. This left an imprint on his culture and lifestyle of citizens. Today we can say with confidence that our own, special culture has formed here.

Through Tanais, trade was conducted between Europe and Asia. It was the border of the ancient world, the main point of exchange of goods between the barbarian tribes and the Greeks.

First excavation

The ancient city of Tanais was first discovered in 1823 by Colonel I. A. Stempkovsky - correspondent of the Paris Academy. The very first excavations were led by P.M. Leontyev, a professor at Moscow University, and later this work was headed by Professor V. G. Tizengauzen. Since 1870 (almost 50 years), the monument has not been protected.



In 1955, the Academy of Sciences of the Soviet Union created the Lower Don Archeological Expedition, which, in collaboration with the RSU and the Museum of Local Lore, began excavating the site. Four years later, this territory was declared protected. Soon Tanais became known to the general public. The Museum-Reserve was opened in 1961. Since then, everyone can see the ancient city with their own eyes, observe the excavations, get acquainted with the discovered artifacts.

Necropolis

A necropolis is located on the western, eastern, and northern sides in the settlement, in which archaeologists of the Lower Don expedition discovered more than a thousand soil burials and barrows. Over a long history, the city was inhabited by a rather motley, from an ethnic point of view, population. This is confirmed by a variety of funeral rites.
