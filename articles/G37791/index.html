But perhaps the main attraction is the Cathedral in Kaliningrad, a city that until July 4, 1946 was called Koenigsberg. The versatility of the main temple

In addition, the cathedral is the tomb of significant historical figures, such as the first Prussian ruler and the last master of the legendary Teutonic Order - Duke Albrecht. A fragment of the tombstone of his grave has survived to the present day.

Born again

Needless to say that, being in the very center of Europe, the Cathedral in Kaliningrad was repeatedly captured by the opposing forces, destroyed, burned and rebuilt. After the Allied bombing (British aviation) in 1944, which destroyed all the supporting structures and ceilings, the temple, like the whole Kneipkoff, was destroyed. The few walls of the cathedral, the tomb of Kant, some underground rooms have been preserved. The old organ, restored in 1905, was also destroyed. The temple itself was fully restored only in 1992. A new organ appeared in him. Today, the musical complex of the cathedral, consisting of two organs (large and small), has no analogues. It is the largest in Europe. The dignity and uniqueness of the complex is the ability to play two instruments at the same time. The large organ is the largest in Russia, it has 90 registers. He is crowned by the Phoenix - a bird, which is a symbol of rebirth, in this case, both the organ and the cathedral.

Rich history
