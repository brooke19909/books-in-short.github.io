History of the Curonian Spit

This landscape object was created by nature about five thousand years ago thanks to the sand driven by the waves of the Baltic Sea and the wind. And about three hundred years ago, the Curonian Spit was transformed by the hands of man, moreover, specifically. It is for this reason that the peninsula (as a common Lithuanian-Russian international site) was included as a cultural landscape, and not a natural site, in the UNESCO World Heritage List.



Where did this name come from? Before the colonization of Prussia by the Germans, there lived the Curonian tribe - ancient tribes, that‚Äôs the name they came from. In the tenth century, the Vikings lived in these places, which was confirmed by excavations in 2008, although the first traces were discovered back in 1893.

What is this object now

What is the Curonian Spit today? Photos of local beauties and landscapes will help us to give an answer to this question. Only here in one day you can see completely different, sometimes completely opposite landscapes: sandy deserts and meadows covered with lichen and moss, dry pine forests and damp alders, stunted mountain pine and tall pine trees, southern taiga and broad-leaved forest, flat fields and sandy mountains , lowland and upland swamps.



Sandy beaches in these places stretch for tens of kilometers like lake and flood shores, several scientific stations and fishing villages. On the spit, human activity and natural, natural processes are very closely intertwined, which have significantly transformed local reliefs over the past thousand years. The same continues today, due to which the natural system of the braid is very vulnerable and fragile.

What is the Curonian Spit geographically? The map clarifies this quite intelligibly. The sandy peninsula has a total length of 98 km, including the Russian part - 48 km. Climate and local infrastructure

The weather here changes very often, and the seasons have the following characteristics: winter is mild, summer is moderately warm, autumn is warm, spring is cool. What else can the Curonian Spit boast of? A photo of the area invites us to look at the magnificent beach by the bay, near the village of Morskoye. Due to the shallow depth of the bay, up to a maximum of four meters, the water temperature in summer reaches 26 degrees.

A bird migration path runs along the spit, connecting Karelia, Finland and the Baltic states with Africa and southern Europe. In autumn and spring there is a very high density of migration flow. All this creates very favorable conditions for their ringing. Therefore, back in 1901, Professor Tinemann organized one of the first ornithological stations in the world in these places. Not so long ago, since 1957, it is a biostation of the Institute of Zoology of the Academy of Sciences of the Russian Federation.

Dangers awaiting tourists on the Curonian Spit

The entire Curonian Spit was examined, tours to famous places were conducted. What to do next? You can swim and relax on the sea beaches located at the villages of Morskoye, Rybachy, Lesnoye, at the Museum of the National Park, at the tourist base "Dunes", not far from the car parks of the routes Vysota Efa and Korolevskiy Bor. But, traveling through the forest, passing along the routes, you must always remember the dangers that await at almost every step. In these places, ixodid ticks are common, which are carriers of Lyme disease and tick-borne encephalitis.



Before entering the forest, dress your clothes, do everything possible to maximize the probability of getting a tick on your body. Upon returning home, carefully inspect the body and clothing. In July-August, marching silkworm, a caterpillar, causes a rather strong allergic reaction with its hairs in some forest places.
