Visual thinking - what is this?

Visual-figurative thinking is an extraordinary ability of a creative person to see non-standard in everyday things. Thanks to the ability to think creatively, new projects are born in the world, discoveries are made and life does not become stereotyped, but unique for everyone who knows how to see in it not an ordinary cycle of events, actions and objects.





Why is the development of thinking outside the box important?

Each of us is born a unique person. At one time in the last century, society and the framework of personal growth made people a united and united team, the working and middle classes lived under the regime and the minimum required manifestations of their needs, but creative people always stood out from the crowd - in the 19th century this type of people was a part of high society, then the creative fraternity (beau monde) ceased to be an aristocratic cell due to the fact that every vermin was torn there. But today, the visual-figurative type of thinking is a special state of mind inherent in the people who need the world. At present, the ability to see the world in one's own projections is not limited to painting, translations, and writing talent, although these qualities are very much appreciated to a proper degree. Computer technology and the development of diverse fields of activity, where freedom of style and expression are welcome, entailed the development of the qualities of innovative thinking. Children are geniuses of non-standard solutions

The creative abilities of the child do not need to be developed. What is important is not to ruin them. Neither analytics nor logic are present in children's actions and decisions; their answers to tricky questions are most often sincere and direct, but each of them is truth and creativity of purest water.

What is the mechanism of children's perception? Coming into the world, a little man endlessly and constantly learns all the space around him. A huge amount of information for the newborn and the pace of brain development over several years simultaneously cover all areas of life. Developing visual-figurative and visual-effective thinking are inevitable processes of self-awareness and cognition of the surrounding world.
