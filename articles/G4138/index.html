Where did the Kotelnicheskaya embankment begin? Is it possible to get housing here today?

Some places in the Russian capital are known far beyond the borders of the country. Their names enchant both native Muscovites and foreigners. The Kotelnicheskaya Embankment, known for its Stalin-era residential skyscraper at number 1/15, located along this street is no exception. What is the history and modernity of this place, is it possible to live in a legendary building?

Historical facts



Kotelnicheskaya Embankment stretches along the left bank of the Moscow River. It got its name thanks to the Kotelnicheskaya Sloboda, which was here earlier, in which craftsmen who made dishes and decorative objects from metal lived. The place was not chosen by chance, because it was here that the warehouse was located, in which the metal delivered along the river from the Urals was stored.

In the eighteenth century, artisans were supplanted by merchants and representatives of the nobility. And already in the 1870-1880s, a stone embankment was built. For all the time of its existence, it survived only one large-scale reconstruction - in the 30s of the twentieth century. The construction of the famous skyscraper was begun in 1938. The fully finished building was commissioned in 1952.

Secrets of the building



As soon as the house on Kotelnicheskaya Naberezhnaya was completed, the apartments began to be distributed among the new owners. Ordinary mortals could only dream of getting inside, because there lived military men, politicians and artists. It was considered special chic to have an apartment in the central section with a front entrance. For many years, the building was the highest residential in all of Moscow. It is mentioned in many literary works. The legendary house managed to appear in many films, both Soviet and modern. Despite the emergence of newer and more luxurious apartments, Kotelnicheskaya Embankment does not lose its popularity. The house 1/15 have their own apartments, many celebrities.

Can I live next to the elite?



In the Stalinist skyscraper, even today, apartments are sold and rented. Kotelnicheskaya embankment, 1/15 - an elite residential complex. Prices are appropriate - an apartment with two rooms will cost its new owner at least 25 million rubles. In this case, we consider its location in the lateral section and the average state. In the apartments of the central section, similar in size (with a tower), you can settle for 40-50 million rubles. If you want to live on one of the upper floors and move into an apartment renovated, you will have to pay a lot more.

For those who cannot afford to buy a home, there is a rental option. The approximate monthly cost is 70-150 thousand rubles, it all depends on the repair, furniture of the apartment and its location. Be sure to inspect your favorite accommodation in person; only apartments with windows facing the Kremlin are valuable in this skyscraper. The yard is small and too narrow, in addition, it is always dark there. Regarding infrastructure, Kotelnicheskaya Embankment has only one problem - the lack of parking spaces. There are enough entertainment facilities and shops near the Stalinist skyscraper.
