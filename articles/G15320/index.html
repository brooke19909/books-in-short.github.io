City of Treviso. Italy and its features

A small paradise that has grown literally from the waters of the Gulf of Venice is the town of Treviso. Italy is a colorful, vibrant country , here even the smallest province can be full of attractions and architectural monuments. Nothing like this description? Naturally, this town is a prototype, one might say, a miniature of Venice, which, incidentally, is located in the vicinity of Treviso. Italy in all its glory, its features and history are captured here, because we are starting a virtual tour of this area.

How to get to paradise on the water

Naturally, tourists from our country fly to Italy by air. These are international public flights that land in major cities in European countries. There are also charter flights, which just very often deliver passengers to Treviso (Italy). The airport, which is three kilometers from this province, and also twenty kilometers from Venice, is named after the great sculptor Antonio Canova. Here mainly private liners, following the routes from our country, land here. If you fly from other European cities (London, Berlin, Paris), you can find a ticket from one of the low cost airlines that send planes to the airport of Treviso.



Italy - a country of unique Mediterranean cuisine

Just note that the restaurants and cafes of Treviso are very cozy and secluded establishments. There is no fuss, a large flow of tourists, noise. Many of them are hidden, and to locate them, you should talk with local residents. So, mainly on the tables of Treviso is always wine. Among them are such famous varieties as Creata de la Conca d'Oro, Amarone della Valpolicella, Soave Classico and many others. They are often tasted, but any guest can order such a masterpiece for dinner. Mediterranean cuisine, which is in use here, is presented in the form of fish dishes. The variety of marine life, which is held in high esteem here, is very large, but the river fish is considered the trump card of the city. In local waters catch eels, trout, as well as crayfish and shrimp.



The architectural beauty of the city

Well, we figured out the kitchen, now consider the sights of Treviso. Italy is a country that was built up mainly in the Middle Ages. Therefore, in the central square of Del Signori we meet chic palaces in the Gothic style. The brightest of them is the Plazzo Dei Trecento, which was built in the 13th century. Nearby is the town hall, designed in the same architectural style, and next to it is the Cathedral of San Nicolo. The most picturesque corner of the city is considered to be Vicolo Molinetto. An old mill has been built here, and the hill offers excellent views of the nearby islet of Peskeria. Also on this square, a fish market often opens , where you can buy products of this kind at low prices to cook them at home. The old part of the city is still guarded by defensive walls with watchtowers, which have been preserved in excellent condition. Also in the old quarters is the Cathedral of San Francesco, where the daughter of Petrarch and the son of Dante are buried.



Events and Festivals

One of the most striking and colorful, despite its size, cities in the country is Treviso. Italy is famous for its carnivals and various cultural events, and here they are held at the same high level as in any other major city. The main festival is the effervescent Prosecco. The bottom line is that all tourists and locals are given cards with bars on them. In them, the participant must drink a glass of wine. It is noteworthy that only the most persistent come to the finish line. The festivals of guitar music are also famous in the city. All musicians, even street ones, can take part in them. Entrance to such an event is free, because you can enjoy the live sound of the guitar and listen to the most beautiful voices of our planet.
