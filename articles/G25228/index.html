Sodom and Gomorrah: the meaning of phraseology, history and biblical legend

We often come across the expression "Sodom and Gomorrah", but few people know about its meaning and origin. In fact, these are two cities about which the biblical legend tells. According to the story, they were burnt due to the sins of the people who lived there. What sins are we talking about? Did these cities really exist? We will try to give an answer to these and many other questions in this article. So, Sodom and Gomorrah: the meaning of phraseology, biblical legend and history ..

Bible legend

For the first time, Sodom and Gomorrah is mentioned as the southeastern tip of Canaan, located east of Gaza, while the land here is called the eastern bank of the Jordan River. Lot, the nephew of Abraham, came here. The Bible even says that Jerusalem borders Sodom on the south and south-east. The inhabitants of Sodom were called the Philistines or Hanakas in the Jewish manner, and the king of the city was a monarch named Ber.





According to the Bible, the time of the life of Abraham dates back to the war that took place between the army of Kedorlaomer and the army of Sodom, which was subsequently defeated, and Abraham's nephew Lot was captured by enemies. The biblical tales say that Sodom was a rich and developed city, but the Lord God decided to punish the inhabitants for being extremely sinful and evil, had many vices that righteous people would not accept. Tradition tells us that God shed sulfur and fire on these cities in order to destroy both the lands themselves and their inhabitants for their misconduct. In addition, according to the Bible, Adma and Seboim were destroyed, although to date there is no evidence that they actually existed. After the fire, the land of Sodom was inhabited by the descendants of Lot, the only ones who managed to avoid the fire, and it became known as Moab.

Attempts to find cities

Since Sodom and Gomorrah are widely known even to non-religious people, many times attempts have been made to find out more about their location and finally find evidence that they existed. So, not far from the Dead Sea, on its southwestern coast, there are mountains that consist mainly of rock salt and are called the Sodomites. It would seem that this should be somehow connected with the biblical city, but in reality there is no reliable data on why this name was chosen.

The interest in the biblical legend is so wide that in the period from 1965 to 1979, five attempts were made to find a city that died due to the sins of its inhabitants, but they were inconclusive. The history of Sodom and Gomorrah did not leave indifferent the Russian scientists who, together with the Jordanian, tried to discover what was left of the ancient city.

Michael Sanders Expedition

In 2000, the British scientist Michael Sanders became the leader of an archaeological expedition aimed at finding destroyed cities. Their work was based on images that were obtained from the American space shuttle. According to these images, the city could be located northeast of the Dead Sea, contrary to all the data from the Bible. Scientists believed that they were able to find the most accurate location of Sodom, the ruins of which, in their opinion, are at the bottom of the Dead Sea.

Jordan valley

Some scholars also believe that the ancient ruins located in Tell el Hammam in Jordan can be the biblical city of sinners. Therefore, it was decided to undertake research in this area in order to confirm or refute the hypothesis. Excavations led by Stephen Collins, an American scientist who relied on these books of Genesis, reinforce the assumption that Sodom was in the southern region of the Jordan Valley, which is surrounded on all sides by depressions.

The Lord, according to the legend, having destroyed the cities, punished the sinners in order to show the whole world that he expects those who resort to unconventional sexual practices and disobey him.

True or fiction?

The biblical story of the cities of sinners is known throughout the world. Vices such as sodomy, idleness, pride, selfishness caused the deaths of the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah. The legend tells of the people of the Philistines who were so mired in sin that they became unworthy to walk on the earth of the Lord God.



Now, so many centuries after the events described, it is impossible to say whether these cities existed in reality, and whether they were burned with "rain of sulfur and fire" for the misconduct of their inhabitants. A huge number of attempts were made to find the remains of these settlements, but in reality not one of them was successful.

Conclusion

According to legend, when two angels came to the city to find at least ten righteous people, they saw only vice and debauchery there. And then the Lord, angry, decided to burn the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah. That this happened in this way is written in the book of Genesis, but the legend remains a legend, and archaeological evidence that could prove this has not been found. However, whether this actually happened or this, like many other ancient legends, is an absolute fiction, it is not so important. The most important thing here is to be able to draw a lesson from this story so that modern people are not mired in the same vice and debauchery and are not punished in the same way as the ancient Philistines, which caused the burning of Sodom and Gomorrah, two cities overflowing with sinners.
