Leonov's exit into outer space. When Leonov went into outer space

The release date is known to every student: on March 18, 1965, the valiant pilot-hero of the Soviet Union Alexei Leonov became the first person to find himself in an open airless space. Aleksey Leonov‚Äôs entry into space was the number one event in the Russian and foreign press, a huge leap forward of all mankind in the development of space.

Leonov went into outer space from the board of the Voskhod-2 experimental ship. It was significantly different from the Gagarin's Voskhod-1: it already housed a couple of places for pilots, in addition, it was equipped with a Volga camera, which was inflated during a space flight.

The crew of the ship consisted of only two people. The commander of the apparatus, too, often flashed in the press at that time. It was Pavel Belyaev, and the pilot was appointed Alexei Leonov. Especially for this mission, the Golden Eagle spacesuit was made. It is he who will fail the astronaut at the most inopportune moment.

Leonov's release into outer space is a very well-known date: the start was made from Baikonur at ten in the morning Moscow time. Difficulty flying

It was very tightly connected with the aircraft Leonov, everything was calculated to the smallest detail, the cable length was five meters. Five times the astronaut approached and moved away from the ship during his stay in vacuum. The danger began to be felt almost from the first minute: the pressure suit inflated the pressure suit. When it came time to return, Leonov had to break two points of strict instructions from Earth. Due to the dimensions, he reduced the pressure inside the suit and penetrated the ship forward with his head, and not his feet.

But the terrible misfortunes, unfortunately, did not end there. Due to the temperature difference, a rather large cleft formed directly in the hatch, which could lead to depressurization of the ship and the death of the astronauts. Automatic systems "Voskhod-2" at the same time worked to increase the supply of oxygen mass, so everything could end in an explosion. It was possible to overcome the problems only after seven hours, only then the pilots could feel safe.

Landing

Voskhod-2 made as many as nineteen orbits around the Earth before descending to the solid surface of the planet. Just imagine: the calculations did not correlate with reality, so the landing did not happen at all where it was originally planned. Two hundred kilometers from the city of Perm, in a cold and inhospitable deserted taiga, far from civilization and pilots sat down. For two days the astronauts were waiting for rescue, later they were sent to Perm, and from there by plane again to Baikonur.

Error handling

Leonov very often still recalls his flight: in many interviews he said that there were a lot of mistakes, that they could be avoided and that in many ways only a happy combination of circumstances helped him and his comrade to survive.





Just imagine: the spacesuit was practically not tested, since it was almost impossible to create proper test conditions on Earth, it was based on calculations only on the basis of its developments. But this was not enough, the spacesuit was the first to fail.

The flight altitude was significantly greater than originally planned. A few tens of meters higher - and the astronauts would have received strong radiation exposure. The reason for this fact was not identified even after the flight.
