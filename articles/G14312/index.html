Losinoostrovskaya Station: summary

Losinoostrovskaya station in Moscow (Yaroslavl direction) was opened in 1902. It includes one main and two intermediate platforms. One of them is designed for high-speed trains going from the capital to Mytishchi, Bolshevo and Pushkino. This station owes its name to the Elk Island national park. The exchange of wagons with the District Station has been the main focus of its work for over 100 years.

History of development

The Losinoostrovskaya station at the end of the 19th century was a small suburban platform. It operated a wooden station, similar to a country house. He had fanciful awnings around the edges. This station building was used until 1970, and some old buildings are located on the station to date.

With the beginning of the Losinoostrovskaya station, a working village began to grow in this territory. In 1925, it became a city, since 1939 it began to be called Grandma. The city received its name in honor of the illustrious polar pilot who was born in these places. In 1960, Babushkin became one of the territories of Moscow.

When the capital‚Äôs district railway was built, Losinoostrovskaya became the main link connecting Okruzhnaya and Severnaya stations along certain connecting lines. The volume of her work has increased significantly. Currently, the Losinoostrovskaya station passes about 13,000 passengers daily.

Technical specifications

The platforms of this railway station are equipped with awnings. They are interconnected by pedestrian bridges. Visitors can get to the platforms only bypassing the turnstiles, however, in the south of the station there is easy access.

Losinoostrovskaya is a sorting station. It has eight fleets of departure, sorting and reception of railway transport. A fire train is deployed at the station to extinguish possible fires, and there is a recovery crew.

In the old days, this station served as the final destination for certain electric trains. Until 1987, there was a branch connecting this freight station with Beskudnikovo.

Station Losinoostrovskaya: how to get

You can get to this station by public land transport and metro. Buses with links to Losinoostrovskaya Street depart according to a schedule. The nearest metro station "Babushkinskaya" is located from this railway station at a distance of 1400 meters. From the Shchelkovskaya metro station, Losinoostrovskaya station (many people are interested in getting to it) is located in sixteen bus stops. Bus number 601 runs between this railway station and Abramtsevskaya street, bus number 601 from Losinoostrovskaya goes to the VDNH hotel , etc.

Travel time by metro from the Baumanskaya station will take about 55 minutes, from the Kazan railway station - 52 minutes, from the Maryina Roshcha metro station - 60 minutes.

Closest to Losinoostrovskaya street is the Belokamennaya railway station and the Yauza platform.

During the Great Patriotic War, the Losinoostrovskaya sorting station played an important role in the transportation of military goods and trains. She was under the scrutiny of the Nazis, was subjected to constant bombing. In the winter of 1941, a terrible tragedy occurred at the station, killing 396 people. Early in the morning there was a stunning explosion. An echelon exploded in which soldiers were sent to the front. To date, a memorial plaque located in the lobby of the station recalls this terrifying event.

Losinoostrovskaya is one of the largest sorting stations in the capital. Over the years, it has become a modern railway station.
