From the whole group of simplest seams: ‚Äúforward the needle‚Äù, vestibule, buttonhole, stalk - the ‚Äúgoat‚Äù seam is the most popular and multifunctional. Despite primitiveness, the goat seam can be subjected to a large number of metamorphoses. You can optionally vary the length of the stitch and its endings, each time a new pattern will be obtained. Embroidery technique with this seam





In any kind of needlework, certain skills are required. So, the ‚Äúgoat‚Äù seam - how to sew in this way? When hemming, the tucked-up part of the bottom of the skirt is attached to the fabric as fashionably more often, performing the function of overcasting, but there should be no trace of thread on the front of the product. That is, one must be able to capture not only the thread, but even a part of it, especially if the fabric is thin. The embroidery or hemming process itself proceeds as follows: the needle "looks" from right to left, but advances the other way around. All the upper stitches, as well as the lower ones, are ideally made at the level of one weft thread (at the top - your own, at the bottom - your own), in order to ensure standardization and clarity of the pattern. The number of picked up longitudinal threads should also be exactly the same, both below and above.
