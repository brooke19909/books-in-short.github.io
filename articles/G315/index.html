Koktebel

A full rest in the Crimea in Koktebel is guaranteed to those who will not spend all their time on the beach. This place is falling in love gradually. The valley, surrounded by low hills, forms a microclimate in which sea, mountain and steppe air is mixed. It is transparent and drunk with the bitter aroma of wormwood. Light sea breezes refresh the air in the heat. At the foot of the Kara-Dag mountain in Koktebel, there is a village on the bay. He is very appreciated by creative people and students. Koktebel is always associated with the name of M.A. Voloshin.

House museum





A philosopher and artist, translator and poet settled at the foot of Kara-Dag in Koktebel after the revolution, in the house that his mother built. Now it is a memorial museum. Almost a hundred years have passed, and interest in M. Voloshin does not fade away. As during the Silver Age, when famous cultural figures gathered here, now fans of poetry and history are arriving in Koktebel. They can see not only photographs, personal letters and documents, but also a workshop composed of genuine furnishings that reflect the delicate taste of the owner. It has become a tradition in August to honor the memory of this outstanding person. From the tower you can see Mount Kuchuk-Yanyshar, where M. Voloshin is buried. Mount Kara-Dag, which is located on the other side, resembles its profile.

Vegetable world

Flora of the conservation zone is divided into three parts.

  • The first belt, at an altitude of up to 250 m , is the steppe. Separate trees grow in it, but mostly it is covered with shrubs.
  • In the second zone, which is located at an altitude of 450 meters, forests of hornbeam and rocky oak grow.
  • The third strip is located three hundred meters from the top, it is occupied by fluffy and rocky oak and hornbeam forests.

Microlandscapes are also distinguished:

  • Holy Mountain and the Beregovoy Range (volcanic complex).
  • The totality of sedimentary rocks is the rest of the reserve.

Of great scientific interest are those plants that besides Kara-Dag do not grow anywhere else. These are perennial herbs, and Poyarkova hawthorn, which has large edible fruits.

Fauna

The animal world is much poorer than the vegetable one, but this makes it no less interesting. Of particular note are badgers, foxes and field mice. Birds that nest in the reserve, more than two hundred species. Here you can see the peregrine falcon, which has survived only in Kara-Dag and has now successfully begun to breed. It is impossible not to mention that half of the species of fish living in the Black Sea are found in the bays of Kara-Dag.

Excursions

Passage to the reserve is possible only as part of an excursion group, which includes ten to twenty people. The passage along the ecological trail takes three to four hours. The route is seven kilometers long. Be sure to have comfortable shoes, clothing that protects from the sun, a hat and water.

Boat trips allow you to see bays that are inaccessible from the coast. They last no more than an hour and are canceled if the sea is stormy.

Kara-Dag is one of the treasures of the Crimea, it is unique, has great cultural and natural significance.
