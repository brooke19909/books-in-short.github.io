Europe bus tour: tourist tip from Russia





Even with the advent of the low-cost market in the Russian market, bus tours still remain the cheapest way to see Europe. And even the fact that the consular departments of the Schengen countries do not favor such excursions, delaying the examination of applications, increasing the package of necessary documents, etc., these trips remain the most popular. What did the Russian tourist bus in Europe like so much ? Feedback from an average user will help to identify all the pros and cons of knowing this part of the world on wheels.

An independent tourist has to take care of everything himself: buy tickets, book hotels, figure out where to go and how to get there. Plus, it is desirable to know at least superficially the language of a country in order to explain reasonably well. All these troubles are deprived of a vacationer traveling on a bus tour in Europe. Recalling enthusiastic tourists is a frequent occurrence for this type of vacation. Among the listed advantages, the tour contains a thought-out program of excursions, as well as a guaranteed table and shelter in places of stops. If an independent traveler needs to study Wikipedia in order to learn about the sights of the city in advance, an experienced Russian-speaking guide will literally "by hand" take you to places of interest, tell and show.



Of all the possible trips abroad, the bus tour in Europe is considered the most budgetary. Feedback, by the way, such a tour often gets positive. In one fell swoop you look at several countries at once - Poland, Hungary, the Czech Republic, the Balkans, Italy or France. To travel all of them, an independent traveler would have to burn a lot of expensive gasoline in Europe or lay out a lot of money on public transport. If you approach the organization of your own vacation with a calculator in hand, then no doubt, the choice will fall on a trip with a group on the bus.

Now about the excursion program. It is extremely saturated, and every day brings a lot of information. Here you should think: can you withstand this frantic rhythm? After all, you have to get up early, ride all day, or even walk. Everything is not as straightforward as it might seem at first glance. For people of different generations and different impressions leaves a bus tour of Europe. Review, for example, of a young tourist under 35 years old: "This is a developing rest," and the elderly person is "exhausting." One thing is clear: if you cherish pleasant impressions more than a calm sleep, you will be satisfied with such a trip. If you prefer measured and leisurely, then you can also choose a suitable tour in Europe. Reviews advise such tourists to book trips that include a 5, 7-day or even longer stay somewhere on the Adriatic coast. Thus, a rich excursion program will be replaced by a long-awaited relaxation, impressions will be digested, and the brain will again be ready to absorb new information.





The bus package includes compulsory and so-called optional excursions. The cost of the first is already included in the price of the tour. But for the second you have to pay extra or go to them yourself. Sightseeing tours in Europe collect reviews for the most part positive. They are called very useful, but basic. Often they include an overview of the city with a visit to one or two of the most famous attractions. If you have never been abroad before, you can take a closer look and think about what you would like to see in Europe next time. And be sure to return here again, with a bus tour or on your own - it's up to you.
