Golitsyno, Sochi - Mecca for believing parishioners and modern tourists

Far from the bustle of the city along the road from the Adler district to Krasnaya Polyana there is the Mzymta River. On its banks there is a small settlement - the village of Golitsyno (Sochi). The settlement belongs to the Moldovan rural district. The settlement is located 35 kilometers from the Black Sea and 42 km from the central part of Sochi. As of 2010, about 1000 people lived in the village. There are two places of interest in the area.

Counties

On the territory of the monastery in Golitsyno (Sochi) there is the Vladimir Church of the Icon of the Mother of God. There is a font in it, where a baptismal ceremony is held. This font is located on the banks of the Psaho River.

On the second floor of the church there is a shrine of the monastery - part of the relics of St. Nicholas, as well as the Belts of the Virgin and the fathers of the Kiev Pechersk.

On the territory of the monastery there is a bell tower and a chapel "Satisfy my sorrows." There is an iconostasis and stained glass windows, a wedding church. Pilgrims and travelers come here to get rid of bad thoughts. On the lands of the monastery there is a holy spring where hydrogen sulfide water flows. Not far from the source is a beautiful arbor.

The nuns of the temple in Golitsyno (Sochi) run their own farm, there are greenhouses with vegetables, their own bakery and livestock. Constant construction is underway on the territory - the complex is constantly developing.





Canyon

The village of Golitsyno (Sochi) is famous not only for the temple complex, but also for the canyon of the Psakho River. This place is the most interesting recreational place of the resort. The district has unique groves and caves of the karst period. It is believed that in this place were parking primitive people.

On the way, the first stop for all tourists is an observation deck overlooking the Mzymty valley and the Akhshtyrskaya gorge, in which there are many slopes covered with forests and boxwood. It was in it that the primitive parking of people was found. Tourists will be surprised by the untouched nature and frequently encountered limestone cliffs with relic plants. And these are boxwoods, yews and mozhaniki.

The gorge is conditionally divided into Wet and Dry canyons. In the Wet Canyon there is a very narrow mouth of the river - from 0.5 to 10 meters.

How to get there

The end of the tourist route, behind the sign "End of the recreational facility", begins the Dry Canyon, formed as a result of an earthquake. The high walls of the limestone formations are revealed here, and the bottom of the canyon itself was once covered by an ancient ocean called Tethys.

There are only two ways to get here:

  • on foot;
  • on an SUV.

In an ordinary passenger car, it is not recommended to go here. To the entrance of the canyon - yes, but not to the canyon itself. A dirt road leads there, mostly washed after heavy rains and with fords. If you get to the Kamenka-2 stop by car or bus, it is better to walk about 40 minutes, enjoying the surrounding beauties, and get into the canyon.
