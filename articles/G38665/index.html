The history of the origin of money

The history of the origin of money originates in the most ancient times. Until a certain period, trade was carried out through the exchange of goods. But once a single equivalent was introduced, which was supposed to be present on commodity markets, measuring cost.

The function of this equivalent was first performed by various products. So, for example, in ancient Iceland - the country of fishermen and sailors - fish was considered "money" (everything was bought and sold for cod). In Homer's poems, the number of bulls indicated the value of one or another product.
