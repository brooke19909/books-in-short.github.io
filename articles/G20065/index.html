Stephen Hawking: life and work

The name of Stephen Hawking is known today to almost everyone, regardless of proximity to classes in mathematics or physics. Indeed, in addition to a significant contribution to the development of modern physics and cosmology, the scientist is famous for popularizing science, explaining in his books the complex concepts of the structure of the Universe in simple language.

Stephen Hawking. Biography

The future astrophysicist was born in January 1942. Hawking Stephen William became the first child in the family of the researcher of the medical center Frank Hawking and his wife -

Isabelle Hawking. Later, two more girls appeared in the family. Upon graduation, the eldest son enters Oxford University in 1959. After graduating in 1962, Stephen Hawking continues his own education and scientific activities in the field of cosmology already in Cambridge. Today, everyone knows the image of an almost completely paralyzed scientist, confined to a wheelchair and able to communicate exclusively using a device that synthesizes voice. This problem manifested itself at a young age. In his third year at the university, when he was 21 years old, the young man noticed that he had problems with spatial coordination. A survey of doctors gave disappointing results: amyotrophic lateral sclerosis and only another two years of life. However, the young man not only survived, but also retained an interest in the world around him, which gave him strength to engage in scientific activities.





Unfortunately, the disease could not be completely overcome, and by the age of thirty, Stephen Hawking was confined to a wheelchair. Moreover, the disease is inexorably progressing to this day, nerve after nerve, muscle after muscle immobilizing the scientist. However, this did not prevent him from leading an active social and personal life. In 1965, he marries his university friend Jane Wilde, who later gave him three children. In 1979, Stephen Hawking became the head of the Department of Theoretical Physics and Applied Mathematics at Cambridge University. This same department was once headed by Issac Newton. In 1991, the scientist is undergoing a divorce from Jane. And four years later, he marries Alvin Mason, with whom he lived until 2006.
