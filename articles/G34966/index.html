CDP service - what is it in Beeline, how to disable it?

Mobile operators along with third-party content providers offer their users a number of information subscriptions and newsletters. Most of these services are paid: payments can be debited daily or one-time, at the time of the request. But a good half of those users of mobile gadgets who have information subscriptions on their numbers do not even know about their existence. At the same time, it is impossible not to notice that some amounts of money periodically disappear from the account - this is what becomes an occasion for customers to clarify what is nevertheless connected to their SIM card.

In the details received at the office or by e-mail (after pre-ordering in your personal account), you can see a little informative decryption - cdp. What is it in Beeline: an additional service from a content provider, connected by negligence or an imposed option from the operator? In this article we will talk about how to avoid connecting unnecessary services and debiting funds from the balance sheet for receiving unnecessary information.





Cdp - what is it in Beeline?

Clients of the black-and-yellow operator are well acquainted with such an intrusive service offered by Beeline as Chameleon. This program has long been installed on the SIM cards of all clients. Thus, by purchasing a SIM card in the cabin, you can be sure that the "Chameleon" is already activated on it. What is hiding under this name?

"Chameleon" is an information and entertainment resource for users of black and yellow SIM cards. Its effect is manifested in the fact that every day from eight in the morning until ten in the evening the subscriber displays informational headlines of various topics - news, jokes, categories +18, etc. - this is the cdp content in Beeline. Some of these offers are free, while others, on the contrary, imply some payment (by the way, the cost of the request is displayed on the screen along with the header). As soon as the client touches the pop-up message, a content request or subscription to the channel is carried out.





How to disable cdp content in Beeline

The desire to turn off messages whose appearance is triggered by the Chameleon service is associated not only with the fact that accidental contact can lead to a one-time debiting of funds from the account or even subscription to some unnecessary information channel. Many customers of the mobile operator are somewhat annoyed by the regular appearance of messages on the screen of the gadget, which not only interferes with the normal operation of the device, but also leads to a decrease in battery power.

How to get rid of a boring imposed service providing cdp subs content, how to disable it? Beeline offers two options for its deactivation.





Disable through the USSD service

Among the useful USSD requests that should be filled in to subscribers of the Beeline mobile operator is a command to disable Chameleon. Remembering it is quite simple: * 110 * 20 # . In such a simple way, you can refuse to receive unnecessary news and other offers of various topics. The client will be notified of the result of the command through a text message.

By the way, if you want to connect this service back, then instead of 20 in the aforementioned command, enter the numbers 21.

Disable pop-up offers through BiInfo

An application is installed on the SIM card of the Beeline operator, which is also displayed in the interface, or rather, in the menu of a smartphone or tablet. Most often, users hide unnecessary programs using regular means of the gadget's operating system. So, in order to disable informational notifications, you should go to the BiInfo application. In the list of sections you need to select the "Chameleon" menu and find the item "turn off the newsletter." After completing these steps, you can forget about cdp (which is what we told Beeline earlier). You can activate the newsletter in the same way by selecting the "Enable newsletter" command.

If there is no BiInfo menu in a smartphone, what should I do?

In case it is not possible to refuse to receive informational messages or the BiInfo menu is not on the SIM card, it is recommended to visit the communication salon. Most likely, in this case, the subscriber will be offered to replace the SIM card with a working one, naturally while preserving the number. Having received it in your hands, you can deactivate an unnecessary option through the BiInfo menu. Having got rid of the "Chameleon", the client of the black-and-yellow operator will not receive offers to order the content of cdp subs "Beeline".





Checking other options and subscriptions activated on the number

In addition to the newsletters present on the SIM card, the reason for debiting funds may be additional options and services of third-party content providers that the subscriber connected on purpose or by accident. You can check their availability through your personal account. Here you can find a list of activated services.

Recommendations for excluding unnecessary services

In order not to wonder about Cdp - what is it in Beeline - turn off Chameleon, unless of course you are amused by the notifications you receive, and you are sure that you will not be able to subscribe to any news channel by chance. Also, follow these tips:

  • Do not type short commands consisting of numbers, asterisks, and trellises that you received in an advertising message from the operator. Only if you are sure that you need it and the cost of the request is known.
  • Before leaving your number on any news, entertainment portal, read the terms and conditions. Most likely, information on the cost of the resulting content will be given in small print.
  • Refuse to send requests to short numbers, even if you are assured that they are free. If you still need to do this, then check what price you have to pay for it. To this end, send a message with a question mark to that short number. Such a request is not charged - a notification about the cost of sending messages will be received in response.
  • If fraudsters are found among organizations providing content, notify the operator of this by contacting the Beeline contact center.




Conclusion

In this article, we examined how to get rid of the information subscriptions that are offered by the Beeline telecom operator through the Chameleon program. However, in addition to standard content, there are additional subscriptions from third-party content providers. To disable newsletters of this kind from a short number, it is enough to send a message with the text: "stop" in response.
