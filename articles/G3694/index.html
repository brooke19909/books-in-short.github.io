A bit of history

Emissions from a solid-state diode were first noticed in 1907 by Henry Round. He found that electroluminescence was observed when current passed through a metal and silicon carbide. And then similar phenomena were noticed by the Russian scientist Losev. Who even published his discovery, but due to the fact that the science of that time did not allow a full explanation of the phenomenon, it remained almost unnoticed. Losev was able to evaluate the practical significance of his discovery, which made it possible to create small-sized solid-state (vacuum-free) light sources with a very low supply voltage and unlimited potential for speed. LED operation principle

The operation of any LED is based on electron-hole conductivity. In practice, this means that the element has two different semiconductors with different degrees of conductivity. One of them has a positive type, the second negative. That is, hole and electronic, respectively. At the moment when an electric current is passed through the LED, the transition of electrons to free holes occurs at the place of contact of the two semiconductors. At the same moment, energy is released, which is expressed by glow.

SMD 5630 LED

The design of the element is a panel with dimensions of 5 mm by 3 mm by 0.8 mm. The case is made of heat-resistant plastic, inside of which a powerful crystal is placed. SMD 5630 has 4 pins. However, only 2 are involved. In all specifications, it is customary to designate the anode with the number 4, and the cathode with the number 2. On the back side there is a special heat-conducting substrate.

Parameters

Specifications for the SMD 5630 are often manufacturer-specific. But almost all of them are subject to common standards and manufacture elements within acceptable standards. This means that power can be between 0.5 and 1.1 watts. The voltage range varies from 3 to 3.8 volts. Operating and pulse currents are respectively 150 and 400 milliamps. The declared service life is usually 30,000 hours.

Application area

Since the SMD 5630 LED has exceptional characteristics, its range of application is quite wide. It is used in tapes to provide illumination of various devices and devices. Cars, shop windows, as well as the interior of living quarters. In some cases, good intensity allows you to use the technical characteristics of SMD 5630 LEDs in street lighting.

The structure and design of the elements allow it to be mounted on a printed circuit board. With independent soldering, it is recommended not to exceed the temperature of the soldering iron more than 200 degrees. You should also refrain from prolonged touching the element.

Characteristics of SMD 5630 LEDs allow its use almost everywhere. To illuminate large surfaces, a set of several elements or the so-called rulers are used. As an example, the SMD 5630 72 LED line. Standard bulbs for lighting rooms are gradually starting to leave the market, in their place come LEDs that are more profitable in technical characteristics. A 100-watt light bulb can easily be replaced by an LED analogue with a power of only 11. And this is a significant energy savings.

The LED lamp for home lighting usually includes several LEDs. Together, they allow you to create much greater brightness than a conventional incandescent lamp.

LED strips based on SMD 5630 are also actively used as decorative elements in interior lighting. If the room is equipped with decorative niches, various elements that stand out, then accenting them using LED lighting will give the room elegance and a special style. LED strips contain several LEDs that are placed with a certain step. This allows you to diffuse light, which gives the room the effect of full illumination. Such tapes are mounted, as a rule, through a special adapter, which can supply the entire set of LEDs with the necessary power and stable voltage.

Conclusion

LEDs have had a great impact on almost all types of industry. It has become possible to create cost-effective lighting fixtures. And this in turn led to a decrease in the amount of pollution and emissions into the environment. The compactness and convenience of working with LEDs has attracted a large number of specialists in this area. Almost everyone can deal with the connection of ready-made LED strips to the adapter, and then to the mains.

The use of LED backlighting in high-tech products has also not gone unnoticed. Modern televisions began to give a more saturated bright picture. Smartphones have acquired an energy-efficient backlight system for their screens. Various means of indicating devices using LEDs have become much smaller and more economical.
