History of Starocherkassk

The first mention of the Cossack town located on the right bank of the Don River is found in a document from 1593. However, the famous Russian historian Vasily Tatishchev in his main work reports that the ‚Äúcity of Cherkassky‚Äù was founded under Tsar Ivan the Terrible. Be that as it may, throughout the 17th century this locality played an important role in the defense of the southern borders of Russia mainly from the Turks, and in 1644 the main Don army was transferred there. Having become the capital of the Cossacks, Cherkassk began to develop rapidly, and its Maidan, which was located next to the wooden Voskresensky Cathedral (not preserved), often became the place where the most important issues determining the fate of the Don lands were decided on the Army circles.





In the second half of the 17th century, all of Russia learned about Cherkassk, since it became the place where the famous uprising of Stepan Razin began, and a century later a temple was built there, consecrated in the name of the holy apostles Peter and Paul. Starocherkassk, the sights of which are recognized as the property of mankind, remained the capital of the Don until the fire of 1744, after which it was never able to recover to its previous level. As a result, it was decided to establish a new administrative center for the Don Army, which Novocherkassk became in the early 19th century, and Cherkassk got its modern name. Thus, the city lost its significance and by 1900 became a village.

Temples of Starocherkassk

The Cossack capital of the Don is also known for its temples. In particular, in this city is the first in the Rostov region stone Resurrection Wax Cathedral. It was built in the Ukrainian Baroque style and until 1805 was the main place where the Don Army was blessed. Despite its modest appearance, the Resurrection Cathedral impresses with its magnificent huge iconostasis, consisting of 149 icons of ancient letters. In addition, it is notable for a tented bell tower about 46 m high, which is architecturally unique in the south of Russia. Another attraction of the Resurrection Cathedral - shackles, which, according to legend, ataman Stepan Razin was chained before execution.





Starocherkassk, a map of which will help tourists to navigate when choosing an excursion route, is also known for another church - the Church of the Don Icon of the Mother of God. It is located on the Ephraim family compound and was consecrated in 1761. In general, this manor and the palace located on its territory deserve special mention. In particular, the former residence of the chieftain Danila Efremov was built in the St. Petersburg style, and today it houses a museum, which contains many interesting exhibits.

Another church of Starocherkassk - the church of Saints Peter and Paul mentioned above - is notable for the fact that in infancy one of the future heroes of the Patriotic War of 1812 - Ataman Matvey Platov was baptized.

Festival "No more free Don Quiet"

Every year in the summer months, many amateur and professional groups from the Krasnodar and Stavropol Territories, Rostov and Volgograd Regions come to Starocherkassk. The festival "No more free than Don Tikhi" is held on the last Sunday of June, July and August and gathers a lot of spectators who want to get acquainted with Cossack folklore and become a participant in workshops on the production of traditional dishes and various crafts from clay, wood and straw.
