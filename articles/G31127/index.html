Every corner of Russia is a unique natural monument. The Pechora River extends over the territory of the Komi Republic (northeast of the European part of Russia). This article will briefly describe some of the nature features of these protected sites of Pechora: sights, landscapes, etc.



A peculiar relief of this area was created due to the last glaciation of the Ural Mountains. Most of the ridges, river valleys, slopes, and small hills formed under the influence of glacial channels. In these places, the bulk of the vast lowland zones of Pripechorye are covered with ice sheets.

Pechory (Pskov region): relief, description

The territory of the nature reserve of the Pskov region according to the relief features and its geological structure is divided into 3 largest regions.



1. Pripechorsky lowland - a zone within which the Yakshinsky site is located. This is a large flat terrain, the foundation of which is laid out by Permian sediments, on top of the overlapped ice layers. The heights (marked absolute) here do not exceed 175 meters. The central sections of this region are swampy.

2. The Predgorny district (along the other hilly) is located on the western territory of the Ural section and extends to the very base of the Main Ural Range. Tributaries of Pechora

Almost all of its largest right tributaries flow from the Urals (from the peaks of Ilych, Sugor, Podcherie and Usa). On the hills of the Bolshezemelsky ridge have the origins of Lai, Shapkin and Kolva. Northern Mylva, Tansy, Izhma, Sula and Tsilma (the left tributaries of Pechora) originate on the Timan Ridge, and Unya - in the mountains of the Ural Range Silver Belt. The rivers Kozhva, Ski, Lemu and Velha flow from the Lemyu Upland.
