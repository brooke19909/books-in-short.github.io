Seville: Attractions That Enchant

One of the best cities in sunny Spain is Seville. This southern city is the capital of Andalusia - a commercial and industrial region with a rich history. According to legend, it was founded by a mythical Greek hero - Hercules, son of Zeus. Today, there are about seven hundred thousand inhabitants on the two banks of the Guadalakvir River. The city is divided by a water artery into two parts - Triana and Seville. The historical part is located on the left bank, here you can see the main attractions of Seville.



The amazing city of Seville! The sights of it conquer everyone who has decided to look at them with at least one eye. Amazing temples, magnificent palaces, magnificent mansions - all this can be examined for hours. Each map of Seville with attractions will offer you to visit the Cathedral, the construction of which lasted a century. From 1401 to 1519, the country's best masters erected a building 116 meters high and 76 meters long. Majestic columns, a high dome, a fantastically rich interior and exterior retain a prayer atmosphere. Here you can touch the stone coffin of Christopher Columbus, see the paintings of Goy, Velazquez and many other famous Spanish painters.



Sunny Seville, whose attractions attract travelers from all over the world, knows how to surprise guests. You can not miss the Giralda Tower, which is a symbol of the city. Not far from these ancient monuments are located the square of the Holy Patroness of Kings and the famous Alcazar Palace. Once in this fantastic palace-fortress, built in the Arabic style, the Moorish rulers lived, and after their expulsion - Christian kings.

This region tells about the life that Spain used to live. Seville, the sights of which are so diverse, preserved the Golden Tower of the thirteenth century almost intact - part of the city fortifications, at different times it was a lighthouse, a chapel, a prison, and a treasury. Today it was turned into a museum of marine history.



It is worth looking at the Lonha Exchange, dating to the late sixteenth century. Trade deals were concluded here until the mid-seventeenth century. Today, the General Archives of the New World is huddled there, which has unique documents, maps and letters from the period of the discovery of America.

Awesome Seville! Its sights are not only ancient buildings that can tell a lot about the life of the population of this fertile region. The guests of the city will definitely be shown the Plaza de Toro Maestranza - one of the largest and most significant arenas in the country, where bull fights are regularly held. It is also emerald parks and gazebos, amazing cuisine and fun dancing. These are enchanting landscapes, the beach season almost all year round and hospitable people who always smile at tourists.

Spanish Seville, whose sights will plunge you into the magical atmosphere of the synthesis of the Arab world and medieval romance, is waiting for you!
