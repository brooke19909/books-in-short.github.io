Participation in the first tournaments

Since Steve Davis began to go to the club with his father, they stopped seeing empty space in it. Now many members of the club treated the guy in a friendly manner, occasionally giving advice or correcting his mistakes. In 1976, Steve decided on a very serious step - a statement about himself. He applied for participation in the National Championship among players under the age of 19 years. The young snooker player prepared carefully for this event: he practiced blows, tested himself for his reaction and visual acuity, practiced a lot both at home and in the club.

And again, training and patience gave good results: Davis became the winner of the tournament. After that, he began to notice the largest people in the world of sports. One famous businessman named Barry Hearn watched the young winner for a long time after the championship, and shortly thereafter, he offered Steve cooperation.

Snooker Rewards

Having received so many titles for spectacular and professionally performed parts, Davis simply could not stay away from awarding outstanding people. Player Qualities

Few knew that the short boy, who was even afraid to shake hands with a stranger, was now a famous snooker player named Steve Davis. Interesting facts: in childhood and throughout the teenage period, Davis was an impossibly shy child. He did not feel very comfortable in large companies and constantly blushed at any word spoken by someone else or even by himself. If the guy did not get into snooker, he most likely would not have gained his current confidence.

Both spectators and former rivals of the athlete are unanimous that Steve Davis was always very calm and even phlegmatic. He rarely succeeded in getting himself a bad game, but a good one - it aroused excitement in the player. Everyone knows that Steve Davis has been a successful snooker player for 40 years, but no one can even suspect the qualities of this person outside the pool table.

In fact, a man has a wonderful sense of humor - he knows how to joke and, when appropriate, can laugh at himself. For his easy character and quick grip on the whole new Steve, they gave the nickname Self-taught. And it should be noted that this name really fully embodies the attitude of Davis to snooker. He loves to learn something new from a wide variety of players - both those who are much older and the younger generations.
