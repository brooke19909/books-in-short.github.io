Types of accounting. Types of accounting accounts. Types of accounting systems

Accounting is one of the key mechanisms for ensuring the functioning of the enterprise. The management of the company, building a financial development strategy, the language of communication with investors is largely determined by how successfully the specialists of the company will conduct the relevant activities in accounting.

What is accounting

Accounting, according to a common definition, is a system within the framework of which the collection, fixing and generalization of information about the property, contracts and monetary assets of the organization, as well as their movement within the company in the aspect of inter-corporate interaction, is carried out.

The following types of accounting are distinguished.

1. Management

This type of accounting is a methodology within which work is carried out with accounting information in order to optimize management policies at the enterprise. In some cases, the goal of implementing the corresponding mechanism is the formation of an internal corporate information system. Basically, in management accounting , cost analysis is carried out, the cost of production is calculated.





The information obtained in the course of the relevant analytical procedures is used by the management of the company in order to optimize technological processes, improve work with personnel, and reduce costs.

2. Financial

It is a process of collecting accounting information related to the costs and revenues of a company, debts, the availability of certain funds, etc.

3. Tax

Some experts also include him in the types of accounting, although he is mainly associated with interaction with the external structure - the Federal Tax Service. It is a collection of information that is subsequently used to calculate the tax base. The purpose of implementing this type of accounting is to ensure the correctness of the interaction between the enterprise and the main fiscal-controlling body - the Federal Tax Service, as well as other departments.





Each of the marked categories may reflect the corresponding types of accounting systems. They can work within the framework of dissimilar methodologies, but at the same time they can be applied subject to common goals. In addition, in the framework of management, financial and tax areas, as a rule, specific types of activities are determined in accounting. Work in the relevant fields is usually performed by highly qualified professionals.

Accounting meters

Having considered what are the main types of accounting, we can study such an aspect as the meters used by accountants in the process of their activities. Among these may be criteria related to the type of natural. They use the following meters:

- mass units (in tons, kilograms, grams, etc.);

- quantity (pieces, sets, etc.).

Another type of criteria is labor. They are used if it is necessary to calculate the amount of time spent by company employees on manufacturing products. The key meters here are days, hours, sometimes minutes. The practical significance of labor criteria is the ability to calculate labor productivity. And, as a result, optimize the corresponding item of expenses.





Probably the most important criterion reflecting the measuring aspect in accounting is financial. It is used when the company reflects business processes and their analytical generalization in monetary units. A financial criterion is a tool that allows company management to calculate the total value of assets. The main measure here is the currency of the country, that is, in Russia it is rubles and kopecks.

Accounting functions

Having studied the main types of accounting, as well as key meters, we can consider what functions are characterized by the phenomenon studied by us. Experts distinguish their following list.

Firstly, it is a function called supervisory. It is a tool for monitoring the availability and movement of various kinds of funds, objects of labor, financial resources, the correctness and relevance of the enterprise‚Äôs interaction with government departments. The main types of control that are carried out within the framework of this function are preliminary, current (current), as well as subsequent.

Secondly, it is an information function. Its use involves the timely dissemination of relevant information reflecting the work of the enterprise among its management and employees (as well as for subjects of intercorporate interaction). The main requirements for information collected through accounting are reliability, verifiability, objectivity, relevance.

Third, experts highlight the protective function. Its essence proceeds from the task associated with the need to ensure the safety of the company's balance sheet property assets. The main criterion for the quality of the implementation of this function is the presence of a methodology in the enterprise that allows for detailed inventory control.

Fourthly, accounting has the function of organizing feedback between the enterprise and various entities related to its activities - investors, buyers, and in some cases also with regulatory authorities.

Fifthly, accounting is also characterized by an analytical function. Its main purpose is to identify flaws, shortcomings, gaps in the management of the company and financial policy and the subsequent development of appropriate optimization mechanisms.

Accounts

What are the criteria for their classification? What are the types of accounting accounts? Let's start with the classification criteria. Their experts highlight a few.

1. Economic content

The account belonging to one or another type is determined based on the specifics of what is taken into account on it.

2. Structure

Based on this criterion, types of accounting accounts are divided into:

- inventory;

- stock;

- accounting and settlement;

- passive;

- active.

3. The degree of detail

It is considered one of the basic classification criteria. Based on its essence, accounts are divided into three types - synthetic, analytical, as well as the so-called sub-accounts. Consider the features of each.

Synthetic accounts include sufficiently generalized information about the assets and liabilities of the company, which are expressed in financial meters. Examples of these are 50 ("Cashier"), 01 ("Fixed assets") or, for example, 80 ("Authorized capital").





Analytical accounts are intended to present information from synthetic in more detail. They no longer reflect generalized, but rather detailed information on specific types of assets and liabilities. Moreover, they can be measured not only in financial indicators, but also, for example, in labor.

In turn, sub-accounts are a kind of "hybrid" of the first two types. Their practical application is advisable in cases where it is necessary, for example, to group analytical accounts within a specific synthetic. Accounting in subaccounts is usually carried out in financial meters, sometimes in kind, in labor almost never.

Also, experts identify other types of accounts based on other criteria. For example, such as operating, costing, regulatory, budget allocation.

Having studied the types of accounting, its functions, we now consider such an aspect as accounts. The practical use of accounts in many cases is carried out through a method called double entry. It reflects the principle in which business transactions are recorded simultaneously twice - in the debit of one account and in the credit of another. Closely interact with each other accounts and various types of accounting registers (about this aspect - a little more).

Double Entry Facts

Let's study the method of "double recording" a little more. Why is it, in fact, needed in accounting? According to the methodology common in the professional accounting environment, most business transactions are characterized by duality, as well as by such an aspect as reciprocity. That is, if funds are debited from one account, then, probably, they are simultaneously credited to another. Thus, the money transaction is always under control.

The mechanism of "double entry" is implemented through two main tools - correspondence and posting. How does this happen?

Correspondence is a channel of interaction between two accounts on which an accounting transaction is reflected. In turn, the posting is, in fact, the practical use of this channel, the correct execution of operations, recording information about the debit and credit. It can be of two types - simple and complex.





The way a double-entry is presented depends on what types of accounting forms are used. There are several of them. There is a memorial or fragmented form - within the framework of it, operations are recorded twice in separate registers. There is an order, or combined form. In it, the registers are used so that the operation is recorded simultaneously for the debit and credit of the account.

Registers

We said above that accounts are closely related to accounting registers. What are the latter? What are the types of accounting registers? Consider this aspect in more detail.

What are accounting registers ? According to a common definition, they are tools used to systematize and collect information that is contained in primary documents for the purpose of reflection on accounts and reporting. May constitute a trade secret.

Experts name the following classification criteria for accounting registers.

1. Based on the design

These can be books, cards, tables, registrars.

2. Based on the destination

In this regard, registers can be chronological or systematic. Combined options are also possible.

3. Based on the content

Like the corresponding type of accounts, registers, based on this criterion, are divided into synthetic and analytical.

4. Based on the form

Experts distinguish four main ones - unilateral, bilateral, made in the form of a table, as well as presented in a chess format.

Costs

Having studied the types of accounting, its functions, accounts, registers, we can determine the means by which the costs are fixed. What are the types of expenses in accounting? What are the criteria for their classification?





Actually, the organization‚Äôs expenses, based on a common definition, are understood as a decrease in financial and economic benefits due to a decrease in capital on the balance sheet of the company and other forms of cash withdrawal. Experts distinguish the following main types of expenses in accounting:

- associated with profit;

- Received outside the income channels;

- forced.

Regarding the first: this mainly includes costs associated with production and sales, investments. The second include the costs associated with the payment of bonuses, charitable activities. Compulsory expenses include taxes, payments to the PFR, FSS, MHIF, and the conclusion of insurance contracts. Some experts attribute the costs of the third type to those associated with the consequences of economic sanctions.

Income

Speaking of expenses, we cannot ignore the aspect of income. What are the main criteria for their classification?

First, what is income. In Russian law, they are considered to be resources that determine the increase in the financial and economic benefits of the enterprise and the growth of business capitalization. The main types of income in accounting are as follows:

- from ordinary activities;

- related to others.

In the Russian legislation there are criteria by which certain money receipts on the balance sheet of the organization are not included in the income. Among those:

- deposits of owners (shareholders);

- the amount of taxes and fees;

- commissions in favor of the partner;

- receipts as part of prepayment, advance payment, deposit;

- receipt of payment for the issued loan.

Income and expenses of the company in the appropriate manner are recorded on the accounts of the organization.

Documents

Having said a lot about what types of bookkeeping are based on the types of accounts, on the classification of income and expenses, we will simultaneously consider such an aspect as workflow. What is its structure? What are the types of documents in accounting? In Russian practice, the following criteria are adopted.

1. Composition of documents

According to this criterion, there is a separation of sources into incoming, outgoing, as well as internal corporate ones. The first are documents that are sent to the company by other organizations. The second are documents, which, in turn, are characterized by a reverse orientation. Internal type sources are not subject to withdrawal from the company.

2. Purpose

Based on this criterion, the documents can be administrative, executive, as well as represent accounting sources.





The first are sources in which orders, various kinds of instructions and orders of management related to the implementation of necessary business operations are reflected. Executive documents are intended to record the facts of the relevant operations. Examples of these are acts of acceptance, various kinds of warrants. There is also a combined type of paper. They may possess features of both administrative and executive documents. Depending on the nature of the assignment of certain types of sources, they may have features that allow them to be classified as accounting.

3. Frequency of compilation

According to this criterion, documents are one-time as well as cumulative. The former, as a rule, draw up a specific business transaction and are subsequently not supplemented. The latter are intended to sequentially accumulate information in relation to a particular period. They can summarize indicators reflecting the progress of homogeneous or identical business operations.

4. Compilation time

This criterion classifies documents into primary type and summary. The former record business operations at a particular point in time. Consolidated documents are designed to combine indicators, grouping them on the basis of facts contained in primary sources.

Most of the documents that are present in the accounting turnover can be simultaneously classified according to each of the indicated criteria.
