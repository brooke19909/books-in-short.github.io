Features of taking measurements

So, taking measurements is very simple. You just need to prepare a centimeter tape, a piece of paper and a pen. Then make measurements:

  • girth of the chest or hips (the highest value is taken);
  • product length - from the bottom edge to the shoulder;
  • neck girth - at the base;
  • the height of the armpits - from the lower edge to the armpits;
  • sleeve length - from the tip of the shoulder to the estimated level of the cuff;
  • girth of the arm at the location of the cuff.

How to determine the desired units

When knitting any product, the needlewoman is guided by the number of loops and rows. It is these parameters that determine the size of the idea. And in order not to check each step with a centimeter and not get a big or, conversely, a small thing, it is more reasonable to make calculations in advance. To do this, knit a square 10x10 cm in size. It is important to work with prepared yarn and tools, as well as knit with a pattern that is selected for the Bat pullover. On the knitting needles we loop, guided by rapport. Therefore, sometimes a square can get a larger size. In any case, further actions will be as follows:

  1. We carefully consider how many loops and rows fit in the sample.
  2. Divide the loops by the width of the sample, and the rows by the length.
  3. Thus, we manage to find out the number of loops (P) and rows (P) in one centimeter.
  4. Now it will not be difficult for us to calculate the parameters of our ideas. To do this, multiply P by all horizontal rows, and P by all vertical ones.
  5. For convenience, it is better to draw the desired product and indicate on it the necessary units of measurement obtained by simple calculations.

Knit pullover from bottom to top

Experienced needlewomen say that those who have been able to carry out competent training will easily knit the Bat pullover for a woman, girl or girl. But it is also important to follow the instructions provided:

  1. We collect the number of loops equal to the circumference of the hips or chest.
  2. We knit an arbitrary number of rows without increases and decreases.
  3. After we start adding loops strictly along the line of the side seams. To simplify the work, you can calculate how many additives are in each row. To do this, divide the length of the sleeve by the height of the armpits.
  4. We knit, relying on our calculations and forming a t-shaped canvas.
  5. Next, we knit the front and back separately. We move back and forth without making increases or decreases. Each part is raised half the width of the cuff.
  6. Close the loops and sew the fabric with a sewing needle and thread. But do not forget in the middle of each part to select the hinges for the gate - 1/2 neck circumference.
  7. In conclusion, we take a hook, we collect loops, we transfer to ring spokes of the same size or smaller and we knit small cuffs.
