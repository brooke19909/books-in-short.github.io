Zenit-TTL: description, specifications

The Zenit-TTL camera is intended for amateur shooting on a color and black-and-white film. This device has an exponential semi-automatic device with a function for measuring light that passes through the lens (this is the TTL system), a self-timer, and also a synchronization mechanism designed to operate with a flash lamp. Zenit-TTL allows the use of interchangeable lenses that have an M24x1 connecting thread and a working distance of 45.5 mm.





The camera is used for the following types of special shooting:

- reproduction works in which extension rings are used;

- shooting small products in close-up, from very small distances (this type is called "macro photography");

- using a microscope (this type is called "micrography"), etc.

Zenit-TTL has several advantages:

1. An exponential semi-automatic device of the TTL system provides the installation of the necessary exposure when shooting with standard and interchangeable lenses, light filters, nozzle lenses, extension rings.
