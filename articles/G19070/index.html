The village of Princely Mountains of the Tver region: photo, description

The history of this village is very long and interesting. In the surrounding lands, people have settled since ancient times. This territory in the Middle Ages was part of the Kholm principality, which was an integral part of the principality of Tver, the path to which was blocked by the Nikolsky monastery, located in the village of Nikolo-Pustyn. This settlement in the XIII-XIV centuries was more significant than now.

The article provides information about the village of Princely Mountains, located in the Tver region.

Location

The village is located in the Zubtsovsky district. Here passes the Moscow-Rzhev-Riga railway line, which is the historical Moscow-Vindava railway. The distance from this settlement to the Moscow-Riga station is about 173 kilometers.

The village of Knyazhye Gory is the administrative center of the Knyazhyegorsky settlement.

In 1918-1921, the village was the center of the Krasnokholmsky volost of the Zubtsovsky district, and then from 1922 to 1925 it began to belong to the Rzhevsky district.

In the village of Knyazhye Gory, there is now an administration of the rural district, a kindergarten, a school, a library, a culture center, an outpatient clinic, a hospital and 6 shops. There is also a fraternal cemetery of soldiers who died in the Second World War.

The main enterprise in the village is the Knyazhegorsky timber industry enterprise, which manages the railway. By 2009, narrow-gauge railway traffic was discontinued. Previously, the UZhD exported the extracted forests and production waste. Today, almost the entire highway has been dismantled, and as of 2013, the narrow gauge railway remained only on the territory of the enterprise for domestic transportation.

In 1997, the population of the village was 1,250; by 1999, it was 1,500. In 2008, the population decreased to 1,089 people, and according to the 2010 census, it amounted to 989 people.

How it all started

More than a century ago, there was neither a station nor a village on geographical maps. At that time, only the village known as the Princely Mountains was known, which was the estate of the Shakhovsky princes. By the name of this small settlement, the Moscow-Vindavskaya railway station was named. village, built 12 kilometers from the settlement.

During the period of construction work, the well-known Ulyanovsk merchants began to move to these places with their farm: Ryabov, Krylov and Zhelezkin. In September 1901, the movement of trains was opened. At that time, the station had a fairly large cargo turnover.

The history of the development of the village

The pristine settlement of Knyazhy Gory began to develop rapidly. Residential buildings and trading shops were built. In a two-story brick building there is a tavern with rooms for rest of passing passengers. Inns appeared for the poorer. The main street was well equipped.

Unfortunately, all this was doomed to destruction. The fact is that the station was built on the watershed of two rivers - Shosha and Derzh, and 2 streams flowed through the village: the small river Chubeyevka flowed into Shosha, and Kishkinka - into Derzh. At the last, a dam was erected, which contributed to a rise in the water level by 3 meters. And the reservoir was intended for refueling passing locomotives. All this led to catastrophic consequences: the groundwater level rose so much that the soils everywhere began to become waterlogged, and the terrain became very swampy. Around the once prosperous village of Princely Mountains, the number of swamps increased.

And the Second World War left a tangible mark in this village. The population remained under occupation for 99 days. In January 1941, the village was liberated, and its restoration began. The very current Knyazhegorsky timber industry enterprise was created in 1954.
