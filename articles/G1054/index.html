Stone arches of Legzira

The local landscape on the beach of Legzira is truly mesmerizing. Arches made of stone, which rise above the sand, appeared due to the thousand-year work of the sea current. The red rocky cliff at sunset is shrouded in the last rays of the sun, and it takes on a colorful terracotta or orange color. The blue water in the ocean is reflected on the stone, and it casts a radiance of a shade of metal.





Is Legzira Beach (Morocco) destroyed?

Legzira, which attracts both vacationers and locals, is located near a village called Sidi Ifni. This is the southwestern part of the country. According to many foreign print media, Legzira can be considered one of the most picturesque beaches on the planet.

The reason why he was so fond of photographers, artists and just people who prefer to enjoy natural views is the presence of two stone arches. They protrude from the rocks, were formed in a huge amount of time, because the mountains are washed away by the tides of the sea.

Recently, the press reported that the beach was destroyed. However, this is not entirely true. In fact, on the beach (Morocco, Legzira), the collapse affected only one stone arch, which became almost a local attraction. The event took place in September 2016. Today, unfortunately, only pieces of ruined rock remain from one of the natural structures. Specialists can not yet say why this happened. It is believed that the stones collapsed due to the influence of ocean waves, eroding the base of the arch. Already in the spring, a large area fell off the cliff, and in the south of the formation an impressive crack was visible.





Should I go to the beach of Legzira (Morocco) after the collapse?

If you are not afraid of the collapse, you still want to see the remains of the famous natural miracle with your own eyes, then you must visit the beach. You can not worry about where you will spend the night, many put up tents right on the territory of Legzira, but if you like comfort, small hotels are nearby. There are also small restaurants where you can eat. It is best to come to the beach at sunset when the rocks are painted in bright orange by the setting sun.





How to find a beach?

Legzira Beach (Morocco) is one hundred and sixty kilometers south of the city of Agadir and ten kilometers north of the city of Sidi Ifni. At the entrance to this picturesque place, you probably cannot find signs, but you can turn to the beach by a dirt road. A guide for tourists can be the presence of a large stone, on which a mountain was painted with paint. You can leave your car in the parking lot, which is located near the shore.





You can also get to the beach by bus. Public transport travels from the city of Agadir to Sidi Ifni and Tiznit in the mornings, in the afternoon, and in the evenings. Travel to the last costs forty Moroccan dirhams (four dollars) one way, and to the first - a little more. From Tiznit and Sidi Ifni, they reach Legzira by taxi. The price is around one hundred and fifty dirhams one way (about fifteen dollars). Taxis pass infrequently by Legzira, so (especially if you are alone) it is best to ask the driver to wait so that after inspecting the beach you can drive to the city in the same car. Excursion trips are also made in this direction. You can look at Legzira with a guide, having left Agadir, for two hundred and fifty dirhams (twenty-five dollars) for one. The price includes meals and a tour of Tiznit, as well as exploring other local attractions.
