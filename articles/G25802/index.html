Caserta city, Italy: description, history and interesting facts

The city of Caserta (Italy) is located in the southwest of the Iberian Peninsula. It is part of the administrative region of Campania with the main city of Naples and is the center of the province of the same name. The settlement is divided into 5 districts: old Caserta - the historical center, Piedimonte di Casolla with a Benedictine monastery (style - Arabic-Romanesque), Vacceria - the stables of the monks, San Leucche - weaving mill, Falciano.



Caserta in the time of the Lombards (end of the 1st century A.D.)

In ancient times, there was a small, nameless settlement for us. Feudal wars swept the land. Count of Capua Pando the Greedy set off to conquer the lands of his nephew Landenulf. He defeated his small detachment and settlement. At this point, the count built a watchtower, which exists to this day. Residents went to the mountains 10 km from the houses. So Casertavecchia ("old Caserta") was formed.

On Mount Tifatini

This place was chosen by people in ancient times. There stood the town of Kazam Irtam. Immigrants strengthened it by the 9th century, as they suffered from an attack by the Saracens. The center of the bishopric moved to Casertavecchia. And two centuries later, a cathedral was built, which was dedicated to Michael the Archangel. At the same time, Senor Ricardo di Lauro received this town as a gift from the emperor of the Holy Roman Empire. With him, the town is experiencing its heyday. His further life was connected with the advent of the Aragonese dynasty in the 15th century. A century later, residents move from the mountain to the valley and begin to settle down around the tower, which was built by Pando Greedy. The city with the name Caserta (Italy) will appear later.

Owners of these lands

In the sixteenth century, the territories in question are owned by the Spanish aristocrats of Aquaviva, and then by Caetani. In the 18th century, they sold their possessions to the Neapolitan King Charles VII of Bourbon. He was looking for a place to build a magnificent summer residence. So in 1752 the city of Caserta appeared. Italy considers it a young city.

Bourbon Palace

The place was supposed to be safe from attacks from the sea, so they choose the town of Caserta. The settlement is moved 10 km to the side, and the construction of the grandest royal palace of Reggia Caserta begins. He must by his brilliance and grandeur overshadow Versailles. Royal park





The regular part extends three kilometers in length with two parallel alleys along which the most beautiful fountains are located: Margarita, the Eola fountain, Ceres, waterfalls, fountains of Venus and Adonis, Diana and Acteon (the most famous is located at the foot of a large cascade). Then he goes into a landscape park in a free English style. Its western part is called the Old Forest. It existed long before construction. It has paths that lead to the Little Castle - a small two-story octagonal pavilion. It has the shape of a tower and is surrounded by a moat. To the north of the Old Forest there is a huge lake. It was supplied with water from the aqueduct system developed by Luigi Vantivelli. In the center is an artificial island on which Queen Maria Carolina loved to retire.

San leuccio

Silk-spinning manufactory created in the 18th century and the settlement of weavers around it in San Leuccio appeared on the territory of the park. Today, this factory has become one of the attractions of Caserta and turned into a Museum of silk. There you can look at old looms and follow all the operations for the production of expensive fabrics. Today, the silks of Caserta (Italy) are famous far beyond its borders. They adorn the interiors of the royal palaces of Europe and our Kremlin.
