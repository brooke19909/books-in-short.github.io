Where is Angkor Wat pictured and what does it look like?

Angkor Wat is a gigantic complex of Hindu temples located in the ancient capital of the Khmer state. Once its area occupied about three thousand square kilometers. And in the capital lived more than half a million inhabitants - an unheard of number for the Middle Ages. Now this complex of buildings is included in the UNESCO list as a monument to the world heritage of mankind and is the pride of Cambodia in whose territory it is located. In this article we will tell you where Angkor Wat is depicted and how it looks. You will also learn how deeply the image of this temple complex penetrated the mass consciousness and how it is used in culture.



What is Angkor Wat?

This temple complex is the largest of all religious buildings ever erected. But that is not all. Angkor is the capital of the ancient Khmers. The city is spread over a vast space, and only a part of it was occupied by the sacred buildings combined into a complex. We know that Angkor Wat is dedicated to the god Vishnu, but we do not know the real name of the city. The first temples were built in the VI century, but the giant complex itself, which we partially see today, arose under the ruler of the ancient Khmers Suryavarman II (1113-1150). The capsule hidden during the laying of the first brick was not found, as well as any entries in the documents of that era. Therefore, we can only assume that the temple complex was called Vara Vishnu-lok (Location of St. Vishnu). And since people lived here from time immemorial, in the local language it began to be called Angkor Wat. Where is this complex, every resident of Cambodia knows, because it is a national treasure of the country. It is located six kilometers north of the city of Siem Reap, which is the capital of the province of the same name.



Angkor Wat and its history

The ruler of the ancient Khmer Suryavarman II ordered the construction of the capital of his kingdom here. But he died when the main complex dedicated to Vishnu was not yet completed. The following rulers moved their throne to other cities. Therefore, some bas-reliefs remained unfinished. The city gradually lost its significance and the capital's gloss, "shrunk" in size. The population has also declined. And although this place was never abandoned by people, the temple has remained abandoned since the XV century. However, the huge defensive moat surrounding the ancient Angkor Wat complex served as an effective defense against the attack of the jungle. Most of the temples survived. Therefore, in 1992, the complex of the ancient capital was taken by UNESCO under its patronage. Now it is the main tourist attraction of Cambodia.

What does Angkor Wat look like?

The entire temple complex is united by the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe mythical Mount Meru. According to the beliefs of Hinduism, the gods live there. The main sacred building is built in a unique style, testifying to the peak of maturity of Khmer architecture. It consists of three rectangular buildings of different heights, converging in the center. There are five towers in the shape of a blossoming lotus. The central one (with a total height of 65 meters) rises above the rest of the buildings by 42 meters. The entire complex is surrounded by a wall and a moat 190 meters wide. It surrounds not only the platform where the historical temple of Angkor Wat is located, but also the outer courtyard. A road leads to the main building, along the sides of which are sculptural images of seven-headed snakes. Three buildings are connected by roundabout galleries.

If we consider the image of Angkor Wat in accordance with the cardinal points, then it can be seen that each of them has its own gate tower - the group. The largest of them is the western one.
