Needlework for home and family. Crocheted blanket

Knitting is a fascinating activity. The old-fashioned hobby is quite popular all over the world. All kinds of clothes and other necessary things with great zeal and love knit craftswomen. Such products never go out of fashion. To create comfort in the house, it is enough to have a hook or knitting needles and yarn, as well as a great desire and imagination. Many useful things for home and family can be hand-made.



Crocheted rugs and bedspreads are practical and always necessary things. Choose the color scheme of the future thing in such a way that it is a wonderful addition to the existing environment.

Crocheted blanket is a symbol of warmth and coziness. This thing can be done from separate fragments. The old method of "grandmother's squares" is always relevant. Crocheted blanket is made up of many separate squares connected to the canvas. Using one universal scheme, you can connect excellent original things that will be indispensable for the whole family.

For this needlework you will need a hook and yarn. Crocheted blanket according to the proposed pattern can be one-color or multi-colored. The craftswoman chooses the colors herself.



Squares are knitted in one pattern. The first row begins with a set of chains of four air loops. They are connected in a ringlet. Next, a chain of three air loops is knitted for lifting. Now fill the middle of the ring. To do this, we perform two double crochets, one air loop, three double crochets, one air loop, again three double crochets, one air loop and again three double crochets and one air loop. We end the row with two half-columns.

Go to the next row. It fits in the same order as the previous row. We start with a chain of 3 air loops. We knit in a circle. We knit three double crochet into the hole between the columns of the first row, make an air loop. The next step in the work will be knitting three columns with a crochet into the hole between the columns of the first row, one air loop and then similarly knit 2 more times. The second row ends with two half columns. The resulting fragment takes the form of a square.



We knit the third row, like the previous one. First, a chain of three loops, two double crochet posts. In the holes that formed between the double crochets of the second row, we knit three double crochets. We sew six double crochet stitches into the corner gaps.

The following rows are knitted in the same sequence. When the "grandmother's square" reaches the required size, we proceed to the next fragment. The remaining squares are knitted according to the same pattern. We connect the finished knitted fragments with crochets or sewn together.

We crochet the plaid around the entire perimeter with half-columns or single crochet columns or use any pattern for tying the edge of the product. The connection of related fragments is easiest to do according to a previously invented and drawn square assembly diagram. Also, the success of needlework will depend on the right choice of colors, which should be carefully selected. They can be contrasting, but complement each other. In this case, the finished crocheted blanket will look great. The remains of all kinds of yarn will be suitable for work. It can be woolen or half-woolen, as well as synthetic.



A great gift would be a crocheted knitted blanket. The knitting pattern of "Grandma's squares" is universal. She is pretty simple. Having mastered it, you can knit wraps for chairs and chairs, pillowcases for pillows and other things.
