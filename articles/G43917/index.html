Anna Tikhonova. Seventeen Moments of Glory

They were seen everywhere together: father and daughter. Anna Tikhonova thought that everyone loved and respected her father, because he was the best, truthful and purest, but then, having matured and got to know the acting environment more closely, she realized that not everyone thinks the same way she does.



Having played Prince Bolkonsky, the scout Isaev and other goodies, Vyacheslav Tikhonov was very cautious about the remaining roles. He compared all subsequent characters with the prince, Stirlitz and the teacher from "We'll Live Until Monday", so he could not afford to play negative characters. This expressed his holistic nature, first of all, he thought about the viewer and could not let him down.

Parents

Parents met at Mosfilm: Tamara by this time graduated from the philological department of Moscow State University, worked as a teacher of French at a school on Arbat and still worked as a translator. In "Mosfilm" she was invited to help translate some scenes from the movie "Man and Woman." The male role was voiced by Tikhonov. By this time he was already forty years old, he had an unsuccessful marriage with Nonna Mordyukova, and Tamara was only 24 years old, but she managed to divorce her first husband and was about to marry the Frenchman again.

When Vyacheslav found out that she had a groom, he was upset, but did not give up. After some time, they were together, got married, and Tikhonova Anna Vyacheslavovna was soon born. Their marriage was happy, Tikhonov did not like noisy companies, parties, Tamara did not like them either. They understood each other at first sight. In the summer, they always went to the country, in general, in recent years they have lived in the country on Nikolina Gora.



Vyacheslav Vasilievich did not like the city with its bustle, and having built a summer house with his own hands, he was very proud of it. Unfortunately, a lot of unpleasant rumors around his family appeared after the departure of the great artist. Whatever vices were attributed to his wife Tamara for not appearing in public during the funeral service. But she had a broken hip joint, which was difficult to treat, and it was difficult for her to leave the house.

Anna Tikhonova: personal life

At fifteen, Anna fell in love with the star of the Italian pop, composer and singer Toto Cutugno. Dad was ready for her to get the moon from the sky, and so he found tickets for the Cutugno concert, bought flowers and in the break held his daughter backstage to the singer. From happiness and horror, Anna could not utter a word, but the translator came running and introduced the famous singer to an equally famous artist.



Anna Tikhonova early on felt like an artist: she spent all her childhood dancing, playing school, but her father did not want her to become an actress, he considered this profession too difficult and dependent. But she nevertheless tried to enter both the Shchukin School and the Moscow Art Theater School, but eventually entered the VGIK, and there she found her love.

Nikolai Vorontsov studied in a parallel flow, and Anya drew attention to him. He was handsome, tall, slender with long hair. They fell in love with each other, and when Anna fell ill, he came to her house to visit, and then his parents first saw him. Anna recalled that Nikolai had taken off his shoes in the hallway, and when Vyacheslav Vasilyevich saw his shoes of enormous size, she began to call the future son-in-law a "bigfoot."

Then Anna realized that she had loved Nikolai all her life. In February 2002, they got married, and already in 2005 they had twins: Slava and George. They named the children in honor of their fathers.
