Digma e-book: reviews, model overview, description and instructions

The Digma brand is owned by the Chinese company Nippon Klick Co. The latter is engaged in the production and sale of mobile gadgets in Eastern Europe. The company's products are of quite acceptable quality at its more than affordable cost.

Many probably know Digma from tablets and e-books. A good half of the models are in the budget segment and pleases consumers with attractive price tags. And if things are not so clear with tablets, then the reviews about Digma e-books are mostly positive.

Gadgets of such a plan from Digma have shown themselves well, they are quite worthy of coping with the tasks. The brand offers a wide range of models for reading, and getting lost in all this diversity is quite easy. Digma e500 / e501

These are models with a five-inch screen, and they differ from each other only in the type of matrix. The e-book Digma e500 is responsible for the graphic component E-Ink Vizplex, and e501 - Pearl. In the first case, we have colder tones, and in the second - warm. This is how to compare a fluorescent lamp with a conventional bulb. Both matrices are almost equally popular, so here everything depends on personal preferences.

Managing the device is very simple, you can even do without reading the long instructions for the Digma e-book. The keys located under the screen on the sides are responsible for turning pages. There, in the center, there is a joystick designed to navigate menu branches. The interface of the device is intuitive, so there should not be any problems with its development. In addition, the platform received competent Russian-language localization.

Model Features

E-books Digma e500 and e501 series have a resolution quite comfortable for their diagonal - 600 by 800 pixels, with a pixel density of 200 ppi. So, as such, pixelation is not here, and individual points are noticeable only with careful consideration.

Users mostly leave positive reviews on the Digma e-books of these series. The interface of the models does not slow down, the local codec supports a solid list of text formats (including graphic), and the visualization is quite tolerable, as well as the battery life (up to 1600 pages to view).

The only drawbacks that consumers sometimes complain are the lack of backlight and a glossy screen. The latter does not allow normal reading on a bright sunny day, acting as a mirror. So here we have home gadgets, and for travel they are clearly not suitable.
