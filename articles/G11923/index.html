Botanical Garden of Rostov-on-Don: photos and reviews

Often, expecting nonresident guests, we ask ourselves: what places should they show? After all, everyone wants visitors to have the best impressions of his hometown. One of the best places for walking with guests is the Botanical Garden of Rostov-on-Don. The photos in the article represent the beauty of this unique natural site.





Location

The Botanical Garden of Rostov-on-Don is located in the Western microdistrict, northwest of the city railway station and the park named after Gorky (address: Lesoparkovaya St., 30a). According to numerous reviews, the Botanical Garden of SFU (Rostov-on-Don) is one of the most picturesque and interesting sights of the city. The abbreviation means the name of the Southern Federal University of Rostov-on-Don, which has an impressive history.





History

The garden was founded in 1927. The initiators were two scientists of the university - V.N. Vershkovsky and V.F. Khmelevsky, but the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãits creation was born much earlier, back in 1915. The organization of its implementation became possible only after the establishment of Soviet power. In 1927, a site near the Temernik River was allocated for the breakdown of the garden. In 1933, its area was increased from 74 to 259 hectares. Since 1928, the garden has been a kind of educational and research laboratory at SFU (formerly North Caucasian State University).

During its existence, the garden has established itself as a major educational, cultural and information center. Every year the Botanical Garden of Rostov-on-Don is developing and growing. Today, there are about 6,500 species of shrubs, trees, and grass plants. It is known that garden staff do not plan to stop there.

Scientific activity

The mission of the Botanical Garden of SFU is to study, preserve, mobilize and rationally use the diversity of plants representing the steppe zone. In addition, the garden preserves the world flora in expositions and collections, enriches the flora cultivated in the region. The garden staff developed the scientific principles and foundations for the formation of an assortment of wood varieties used for landscaping the southern settlements of the country. The taxonomic composition of the new assortment is several times higher than the previous one. In addition, his life forms and ecotypes are more diverse.

Achievements

In 2017, the Botanical Garden in Rostov-on-Don will celebrate 90 years since its foundation. In the scientific world, it is recognized as the largest educational, resource, information and cultural object. The value of his contribution to the development of the educational and cultural environment is invaluable for the benefit of present and future generations. The Botanical Garden in Rostov-on-Don is a unique "museum" of nature, located within the southern capital of Russia. It has an interesting collection of growing trees (there are more than 5000 species), shrubs and herbaceous plants. A collection of representatives of the tropical and subtropical flora of Africa, Australia, South and North America, Southeast Asia, collected in the greenhouse, reaches 1600 forms and species.
