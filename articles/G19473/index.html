Natalia Sedova - the wife of a famous revolutionary

Trotsky Lev Davidovich - one of the most famous revolutionary leaders of the early XX century. His name is familiar to everyone who is fond of the history of his native country. However, only a few know that his most faithful companion was his second wife - Natalia Sedova. It was this woman who was with him at the time of his triumph, and on the day when the glory of the revolutionary faded forever.





early years

Natalya Ivanovna Sedova was born on April 5, 1882 in the small town of Romny, in Ukraine. Her parents were wealthy merchants. Thanks to this, the girl grew in abundance and was able to get a good education. She even entered the Kharkov Institute of Noble Maidens.

However, Natalya Sedova did not study there for long. The strong-willed temper of the girl often led to the fact that she began to argue with teachers. She was later convicted of revolutionary beliefs. Note that the charge was fair. Natalya Ivanovna repeatedly gave provocative speeches to students. Moreover, she organized an underground circle, where together with like-minded people she read forbidden literature.

Such self-will could not go unpunished. Soon, all revolutionaries were expelled from the institute - the Russian Empire did not need self-will. Fortunately, the parents were able to hush up the incident, Natalia was not brought to justice. However, just in case, the girl was sent to study in France.





Fateful meeting

In Paris, Natalia Sedova studied art history. She had a craving for beauty since childhood. Therefore, her parents were not surprised by this choice of her daughter. Moreover, they were more concerned about her safety than the direction of education.

True, Natalya Ivanovna herself did not give up her revolutionary convictions. Acquainted with the Russian diaspora in France, she meets with Vladimir Ilyich Lenin himself. Together with him, the girl begins to work on the Iskra newspaper, which later became the real voice of communism.

At one of the meetings, she hears fiery speech from the lips of a young man who fascinates everyone around. It was Trotsky Lev Davidovich. Then she did not yet know that a series of dizzying changes had come to her life.

The fall of the Trotsky family

After the victory of Stalin, Trotsky fell out of favor. All that was left for him was to leave the country. However, the new leader of the Soviet Union did not allow him to leave so easily. He understood that Lev Danilovich was a serious threat to his rule.

In 1937, Stalin shoots the youngest son of Natalia Sergey. The reason for this mundane is treason to the motherland. In 1938, his older brother Leo perishes. According to official figures, the reason for this was an unsuccessful operation to remove appendicitis. But Trotsky himself understood that this was most likely a contract murder.

In 1940, the NKVD removed Lev Danilovich himself. That day, Natalia Sedova was saved by a miracle. However, in fear, she had to flee to Mexico. Here she continued the underground struggle against the power of Stalin, but, as you know, this did not lead to anything.
