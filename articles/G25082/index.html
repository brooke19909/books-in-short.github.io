Alberta Canada Welcomes Immigrants

In the west of Canada is one of the most prosperous provinces, named after the wife of the Governor-General of the country and the daughter of Queen Victoria - Louise Carolina Alberta. Therefore, this fourth largest region is often called the province of the princess.

The economic growth

Immigrants are warmly welcomed here, because thanks to their work, the Alberta province was built, in which more than 3.7 million people live. They have unlimited business opportunities. Now the region is developing rapidly economically, and the unemployment rate is only four percent.





Alberta (Canada) is an attractive place to do business. Various companies open their representative offices here, which are attracted by skilled labor, low taxes and production costs, and the regional authorities maintain free competition and create favorable conditions for new manufacturers.

Oil Leader

Alberta is Canada’s real energy storehouse, as it is the center of the country's gas and oil business. It occupies part of the plateau of the same name, rich in mineral deposits.

In the 30s of the last century, oil fields were discovered on the territory, and in 1947 - the Ledyuk field. In the present Klondike, a workforce was drawn, and its influx allowed the region to make a sharp economic leap. The province produces gas, rock salt, sulfur, iron ore, and brown coal.

Plateau Settlement

Once, about 10 thousand years ago, the Albert Plateau was a huge glacier. After warming in the south, it turned into a desert, and the primitive people who settled in the area experienced enormous difficulties for many centuries: it was unbearably hot in summer and incredibly cold in winter. It is believed that the first residents who appeared where Alberta is now located moved from the territory of modern Siberia, first to Alaska, and then arrived in North America.





This is one of the most attractive places for living, however, in an economically developed region there are serious environmental problems. For example, the waters of Lake Athabasca are poisoned by mercury, and various harmful substances are found in the meat of wild animals.

Four natural areas

For the traveler, the province of Alberta is a universal terrain that lies in four natural areas. Many are attracted by the diverse landscape, and if you go around the whole territory, you can see the prairies, rocks, and dense forests, and glaciers.

In the vast territory of the region, cold prevails, but in the south the frosts are not so severe. Winters are long and summers are short. Locals joke about the often changing weather: "If you don’t like something, then wait a few minutes."

Province nature

The picturesque lake Abraham (Abraham) formed during the construction of the dam attracts the attention of all connoisseurs of natural beauty. In winter, its surface is dotted with openwork patterns that consist of frozen air bubbles.





Wood Buffalo National Park is the largest in all of North America. It was created to preserve a herd of bison.

Alberta (Canada) is famous for its international water park, Waterton-Glacier, which differs from all the others in its abrupt transitions from prairies to mountains.

The Jasper Nature Reserve, protected by UNESCO, is one of the country's most visited sites. Here you can admire the transparent lakes, huge glaciers, magnificent waterfalls, picturesque canyons.

Cities of Alberta (Canada)

The provincial capital is Edmonton - a city extremely rich in attractions. Here are the Royal Museum, a huge zoo, an art gallery and a giant shopping center, recognized as the largest in the world. The oil capital also boasts a beautiful planetarium. It hosts a theater festival, winter festivals of light and ice sculptures.

The largest city is Calgary, named after the bay in Scotland. The village, in which the spirit of its first inhabitants was preserved, is recognized as the business center of the country. Tourists rush to the annual cowboy festival here to take part in a real rodeo, visit the amazing Calgary Zoo`s Prehistoric Park, where you can admire the remains of dinosaurs and get acquainted with the variety of prehistoric plants.





The beauty of the local nature and numerous attractions attract tourists from all over the world. The Canadian province of Alberta gives an unforgettable experience to all guests who leave here a piece of their soul.
