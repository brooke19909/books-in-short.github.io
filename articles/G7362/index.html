Carpathians Rest similar to a fairy tale

The Carpathians are a mountain range stretching for 1,500 kilometers, covering the Central Asian lowland in an arc. Carpathian mountains are divided into South, West and East. The Southern Carpathians are located on the territory of Romania, the Eastern - on the territory of Ukraine, the Western - on the territory of the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland and Hungary. It is here, in the Western Carpathians, is the highest point of the Carpathians - Mount Goverla, located at an altitude of 2061 meters above sea level.

The Carpathian mountains are not characterized by rocky ledges. On the contrary, these are flat mountains with numerous and picturesque karst caves. On the tops of the mountains are plateaus with magnificent alpine meadows covered with many plant species. Blueberries and lingonberries grow in large numbers here, and on the slopes of the mountains you can find thickets of blackberries. Carpathians! Rest in the fall here can be perfectly combined with the collection of berries, mushrooms and medicinal herbs.

In the Carpathians, unique relict forests have been preserved. Beech and coniferous forests, through which the human foot has not yet set foot in some places, cover the bulk of the mountains. Many rivers of Western Ukraine originate in the mountains, among which the purest river of Europe - Limnitsa. On the territory of the Carpathian mountains there are many famous and unknown mineral springs, whose healing power is entertaining legends among the people and tourists.

The main business sector in the Carpathians is tourism. It has a well-developed tourism infrastructure - numerous hotels and resorts, recreation centers and cozy private boarding houses await guests, providing them with every conceivable convenience of living, all year round. Many of them specialized in winter recreation, which is very popular in the Carpathians. Every year tourists come here to not only relax, but also to heal. Numerous resorts of the Carpathians are located here. They are famous for the clean mountain air, freshness of which gives a high content of ozone and aroma of pine needles. That is why many sanatoriums located in the Carpathians treat respiratory diseases.

Five months a year, the mountain peaks are covered with snow. Therefore, thousands of tourists go skiing in the Carpathians. Rest in winter here is especially attractive. Ski areas, motels and ski resorts are located everywhere suitable for skiing. The best resorts of Volovets-Podobovets, Dragobrat, Bukovel and Slavske invite you to rest in the Carpathians. Rest in the winter spent in the mountains for a long time leaves unforgettable impressions for the tourists who visited here. Very convenient lifts, located even in the most remote places, quickly deliver tourists who, during the ascent, can enjoy the picturesque surroundings, to the place. Vacationers are offered excellent, almost at the modern European level, living conditions. Ski equipment can be rented. The hosts can offer jeep transfers and a hot bath. In the summer, there is also something to do here - this is quad biking, horseback riding, and walks in the woods. You can just sit, breathe in the healing mountain air, sipping tea infused with herbs.

In the Carpathians, there are not only excellent ski resorts and pistes. The excellent climatic and balneological resorts of the Carpathians are located here. They are known for such balneological resorts as Zhdenievo, Shayany and others. It is not crowded and you can, enjoying the silence, take a break from the bustle of the city.

Carpathian holidays - these two concepts are inseparable from each other!
