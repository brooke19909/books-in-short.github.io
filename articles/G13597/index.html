Underwriting in insurance is risk management for a profitable insurance portfolio. Essential terms of the insurance contract

Underwriting in insurance is, first of all, services that are provided by financial institutions such as banks and insurance companies. They guarantee receipt of payments in the event of any financial losses. Depending on the industry, several types of underwriting vary. Companies that perform these functions are called underwriters.

Basic concepts

We can say that this term is directly the very essence of the area under consideration. In this regard, the development of an adequate criterion for assessing the effectiveness of underwriting in insurance is the first step towards increasing profits and the market value of the organization. To date, there has been a significant increase in interest in the industry in general and its theoretical foundations in particular.

One of the issues of interest is underwriting, which is the key business process of any insurance organization. After all, the main function of any relevant institution is the effective management of customer risks.

In simple terms, this is underwriting. According to the definition of the term, which is given in the dictionary, this concept refers to the process by which the company determines whether it is worth accepting the policyholder‚Äôs proposal to draw up the contract, and if so, on what conditions. True, such a definition is considered too general and does not say anything about what stages the underwriting itself consists of.

It is worth using the following definition of this term: activities aimed at assessing risks, determining adequate tariffs and conditions along with the formation of an insurance profitable portfolio.

Functions

Underwriting in insurance is a service whose main purpose is:

  • Risk assessment. These are characterized by certain parameters: a quantitative criterion (the probability of damage, its mathematical expectation, and so on) and qualitative (analysis of the fire safety system along with the presence of an alarm, level of training and pilot experience).
  • Determination of normal insurance rates. They are calculated in an actuarial way, and the underwriter‚Äôs task is to select adequate reducing and increasing coefficients to the base value, depending on the specifics of a particular agreement. What else does the insurance risk assessment system imply?
  • Definition of conditions. One of the tools in the framework of managing the organization‚Äôs profitability and tariffs is such criteria as the breadth of coverage, exclusion from insurance events, and so on.
  • Formation of a profitable portfolio. By such is meant the sum of all gross premiums that were received during the underwriting year for a certain type of insurance or for all its types minus the costs of attracting clients (agent and brokerage fees) and ignorance.

What are the basic principles of underwriting in insurance?

Underwriting organization

Its process is the most specific in the company's activities. Effective organization of underwriting requires, as a rule, specialized knowledge, and in addition, a lot of experience. The entire insurance procedure can be presented consisting of individual components: marketing, development of insurance services, sales, support of contracts, settlement of losses. In practice, these processes are repeated and mixed. In a broad sense, the following operations are included in underwriting:

  • Analysis, including, in turn, the study and collection of information about insurance objects and their inherent risks.
  • Classification and selection, as well as hazard assessment.
  • Making decisions on insurance of selected risks or a complete denial of service.
  • Determination of an adequate tariff for insurance facilities.
  • Coordination of the amount and calculation of the premium.
  • Determination of risk coverage conditions.
  • Concluding an insurance agreement.
  • Carrying out the development of measures to reduce risks.
  • Control over the state of the insured objects and factors that contribute to or prevent the occurrence of danger.
  • Risk mitigation measures.

All these operations are closely interconnected and interdependent.

Property insurance

According to article No. 929 of the Civil Code, such an agreement provides for the obligations of specialized organizations to policyholders or other beneficiaries for the stipulated premium, in the event of an appropriate case, to pay losses that are associated with the loss or damage of the insured property, in the amount determined by the document. Property insurance rules must be strictly followed.

Under a property insurance agreement, companies have the right to protect the following interests:

  • Risks of death, loss, damage or shortage of specific property.
  • Danger of loss from entrepreneurial activity, which arises as a result of violation by the counterparties of the entrepreneur of their obligations or due to a change in the conditions of activity due to circumstances beyond his control. This risk category also includes non-receipt of expected income.
  • Risks of liability for obligations arising from damage to life or property.

Profitable insurance portfolio

Such a portfolio is the sum of all contracts concluded between the company and its customers. Its effectiveness is evaluated by profitability parameters, by the level and type of risks, and at the same time by profitability. The insurance portfolio does not at all act as a separate document or a list of such. In fact, this is the basis of all the insured persons of a particular organization who have purchased policies and therefore have the right to demand compensation from it upon occurrence of relevant cases. A personal account is opened for each client, on which all the information collected before and after the conclusion of the contract is recorded. Based on such information, the risk level of the agreement and the amount of mandatory payments are calculated.

What does the formation of a profitable insurance portfolio imply? The more contracts on the volume of cash contributions the insurer was able to conclude, the greater the stability of the portfolio. This rule works for all companies, regardless of the specifics of the insured objects. True, the more agreements are included in the portfolio, the more difficult it is to assess the level of risks and profitability. For some companies, the volume of signed documents can reach several hundred thousand, and sometimes millions. In order to accelerate the process of evaluating profitability, special methods and techniques for assessing risks and profitability are used:

  • Homogeneous objects are considered as one. For example, when two or more citizens insure their homes, which are located on the same street, in case of fire or flood. Such objects should be considered as one, since in the event of a natural disaster, it is very likely that they will all suffer.
  • The cost of the policy, however, as well as the amount of payments, must be calculated on the basis of the physical condition of the objects of insurance.
  • Take into account statistics for a specific region, country and field of activity of the insured object.

All policies are classified according to the degree of probability of occurrence of risks and the size of payments. It is customary to divide the danger into specific, high, moderate and low. Accordingly, after the first assessment of the insurance object and the preparation of the contract, it is assigned to a certain risk category, and only then the entire portfolio is evaluated. Next, we discuss its types.

Types of Insurance Portfolios

All of them are classified by area of ‚Äã‚Äãactivity, for example, home insurance or car insurance, as well as by degree of risk. The following types are distinguished:

  • The classic portfolio includes those types of objects that have been used for a long time. This includes voluntary and compulsory property insurance contracts. In this case, the risk is moderate, and the profitability is small, however, stable. To use this type of service, a company must have a significant margin of financial strength.
  • Specialized portfolio. This is such a kind when the sphere of activity with objects, the use of which differs in highly specialized specialized purposes, plays the role of an object. For example, space insurance, protection of currency and exchange risk. Risks for such facilities are specific, and returns are high. Legal entities that have the necessary authorized capital, who have a state license to conduct this type of activity are entitled to engage in the varieties under consideration.
  • The combined portfolio combines the elements of a specialized and classic type of insurance. Such an approach makes it possible to lower the risk, but profitability may also decrease.

In addition to the options listed above, there is another special type, namely a balanced portfolio. Its feature is that all objects are homogeneous in terms of profitability, and even the price of each policy included in the portfolio is the same in terms of risk.
