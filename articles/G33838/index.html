Why do you need homemade copy aircraft in the age of prefabricated models

The manufacture of prefabricated models is engaged in an entire industry. Enterprises around the world manufacture high and low pressure polyester gluing kits. Every year, Japanese and German, Chinese and Russian, Italian and Ukrainian firms roll out large-scale new copies of tanks, automobiles and aircraft to the market. It is unclear to uninitiated people why homemade airplanes are needed, when there are prefabricated models and even ready-made models of very good quality. But still there are cases when a person who is keen on modeling, most often, a collector wants to get a copy that no manufacturer does. Usually this desire arises among experienced modelers who possess such skills that make it possible to "pile" a very rare thing.





Needless to say, the realization of such a dream requires a wide variety of technical and artistic skills. You need to be able to read blueprints, own locksmith and turning tools, and sometimes machine tools, be fluent with airbrushes and brushes. But even this is not the main thing, all skills are acquired, but the love of technology, for example, aviation, is the main incentive for their development.

Homemade model airplanes - this is the aerobatics of mock art.





If an amateur sets himself the task of constructing a copy of an old biplane or triplane, then the first thing he takes on is the construction of his supporting frame. In compliance with all similarity criteria , frames, spars and ailerons are recruited, and a wide variety of materials can be used - from metal wire to wood, plywood and veneer.

Homemade aircraft, like real ones, require patience and delicacy in all operations. Particular care is required when performing the dashboard, pilot's seat and controls. Materials should completely imitate the surface of the skin and matter. For example, toilet paper is used to create a fabric texture. It lays well on the desired areas and is easily painted, while maintaining a naturalistic roughness, and if necessary, then folds can be formed on it.





The modeler takes the first time for a makeshift plane, as a rule, not from a good life, but simply because of a lack of initial set, but then, getting a taste, he gets various useful equipment, right down to a small lathe - and then he can no longer be stopped. After all, it’s practically impossible to find such a prefabricated model that has wings folded, like a deck attack aircraft of the Second World War, but I want to do just that!

Homemade copy aircraft of the time of the First World War may look very interesting. Their design is saturated with braces, and steering rods were in sight. A special achievement will be the following of some historical prototype, for example, the “Newpore-4” pilot Nesterov or the “Fokker” “Red Baron”. It should also take into account such engineering features as open cylinders and other parts of the power plant, which simply must look “lively”.

Holes, peeling paint, and other signs of participation in air battles give high realism.

And, of course, home-made aircraft will be a wonderful decoration for the interior and the children's room, and personal account.
