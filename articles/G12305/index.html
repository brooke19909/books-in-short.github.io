History of the creation of the oprichnina

Tired of the love of power of the boyars and the arbitrariness of the princely aristocracy, which began to imagine itself co-ruler of the sovereign, on February 3, 1565, Ivan the Terrible introduced reform by his decree. It was called the oprichnina. Its purpose was to take away from the king‚Äôs opponents all meaning and power. From now on, the whole country was divided into two parts: the oprichnina and the zemstvos (territories not included in the oprichnina). The first included the northeastern lands, where a small number of estates were concentrated. The oprichnina existed for seven years, but her memory is still fresh.

The essence of the activities of the guardsmen

From the lands united in the tsarist estates all the descendants of the specific princes were deduced, who seemed to Ivan the Terrible suspicious. All of them were subject to relocation to new lands and the very outskirts of the state. According to the king, there traitors could not pose any danger to the throne. Small landowners and nobles settled on the former land estates of the resettled people.



The day of the guardsman consisted of the ruin and expulsion of the old nobility. Ivan the Terrible called it "sorting out people." Obligations of the guardsmen

The leak of important state information posed an immediate threat to the ruler. Therefore, the day of the oprichnik included the protection of Ivan IV.
