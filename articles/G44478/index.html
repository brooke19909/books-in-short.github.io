Big Ben

It is definitely worth starting to get acquainted with the sights of England from the capital of this wonderful state. It is impossible to meet a person who would not want to see London with his own eyes. So, the Big Ben clock tower is a visiting card of both the capital itself and the whole country. This symbol can be found on souvenirs, postcards. In addition, Big Ben is on the list of the largest watches in the world.

Unfortunately, ordinary tourists cannot get inside the tower, these are the safety rules, but this fact does not prevent you from taking a wonderful photo on its background.

Tower of London

This building was erected in the second half of the XI century and still attracts tourists with its unsurpassed view. If we talk about the main attractions of England, the Tower of London tops this list. As soon as the story inside these walls did not change. The tower acted as a prison, a mint, and even a zoo. But it is better if you yourself see everything with your own eyes.

Address: London EC3N 4AB.

Tower Bridge

The symbol of London and one of the most beautiful bridges in Europe. This structure at the same time refers to the hanging and adjustable structures. Tourists have chosen the main attraction of England and regularly use it as panoramic sites of the city.

Tower Bridge is located in the heart of the city above the River Thames. Its name comes from the Tower. In the dark, the bridge is even more transformed, because evening lights are lit, and the Gothic towers give the bridge a certain charm.

Address: Tower Bridge Rd, London SE1 2UP.

Westminster Abbey is an outstanding landmark of England. Description

The main shrine of England and the most famous temple of the country is proudly called Westminster Abbey. This place is imbued with a majestic spirit and a Gothic atmosphere. The abbey building is one of the most beautiful man-made buildings that have perfectly preserved their history to this day. It acts as a traditional place of coronation and burial of the monarchs of Great Britain. Not far from the abbey building is the Church of St. Margarita and the Palace of Westminster.

Trafalgar Square

One of the most significant places in the UK. The importance of this place for the whole British people is explained by the historic victory of the British at Trafalgar, from which the name of the square follows. It was also here that Churchill announced the end of World War II.

In modern London, no holiday of national importance can do without Trafalgar Square. It regularly hosts concerts, rallies and festivities.

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace currently acts as the London residence of Queen Elizabeth II and regularly gathers around itself crowds of tourists who have arrived from all over the world. You can get to its territory exclusively from August to September, when the Queen of England leaves her apartment. Also, visitors are given a unique opportunity to purchase one of the copies of interior items in a local gift shop.

Madame Tussauds museum

The largest wax museum, where each of the exhibits is able to move and respond to visitors. This trophy is also located in London, so if you intend to visit the capital of England, you should definitely take the time and visit this place. A distinctive feature of Madame Tussauds is the ability to freely touch the figures. Here you will find an exact copy of your idol from the field of cinema, music, show business or politics.

Address: Marylebone Rd, London NW1 5LR.

The Beatles Museum

The Beatles are widely known throughout the world for their melodic compositions, dance songs and legendary ballads. At one time, these guys made a musical breakthrough. Starting with the imitation of the classics of American rock and roll of the 1950s, The Beatles came to their own style and sound that will forever remain in the hearts of millions of listeners.

In Liverpool, the birthplace of the famous musical group, the museum of the same name is open, a ticket to the territory of which is valid for two days. This is an excellent opportunity for a thorough study of the museum in several stages. The staff is properly trained to serve even visitors with disabilities.

Address: Britannia Vaults, Albert Dock, Liverpool L3 4AD.

Sherwood Forest

So we gradually got to the natural heritage of England. The famous reserve is very popular not only among tourists, but also among local residents. The park zone of Sherwood is divided into several compartments, but the main asset is the centuries-old oak, considered a real miracle. A variety of seedlings of trees grown in the national reserve are also offered to visitors.

Stonehenge

Have you ever been able to see dolmens? On the territory of our country, such structures can be found in the Caucasus, but the English Stonehenge is something else and unique.

Stonehenge stones are included in the UNESCO World Heritage List and are a real treasure of antiquity. However, many tourists and scientists still do not understand what is the meaning of this building. And some are completely disappointed with what they saw. The best way to check is to see for yourself. An interesting fact is that walking between stones is prohibited, perhaps this has its own magic.

The easiest way to get to Stonehenge is to rent a car or taxi. Since there is no direct flight from London, you cannot get on the bus. Tourists will have to change trains in Salisbury.

Oxford

The history of this place dates back to the time of the Saxons, the first mention of which was recorded in the IX century. Previously, a monastery was located on this site, and today one of the oldest and most magnificent universities in the world flaunts here. This southeastern city of England also boasts the remains of a Norman castle and the Church of Christ.

Conclusion

England is an incredible country that can surprise with its personality and unsurpassed style. The variety of cultural traditions, historical monuments and grandiose structures can drive any tourist crazy. All the names of the sights of England will not fit on one paper sheet. This country needs to be studied and learned for years. Believe me, having come here once, you will want to come back again and again.
