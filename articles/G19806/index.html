Sibenik, Croatia: history, attractions, natural wealth

One of the first cities to appear in Croatia was ≈†ibenik. It is located on the shores of the Adriatic Sea, near the confluence of the Krka River and the international expressway. Originating on the shores of the sea bay, it began to grow, like an amphitheater, over the centuries. The population of the city with a thousand-year history is currently less than 40 thousand people.

Tourists from all over the world come to rest in Croatia. Sibenik is no exception. The city attracts travelers with its unique geographical location. And no wonder! After all, it is simultaneously located on three bodies of water: sea, river and lake. Thanks to the unique combination of water elements, the city became famous for its wellness, relaxing and relaxation procedures.



History

The first mention of Sibenik dates back to 1066. Historical information about its development is full of drama. According to available data, the status of the city of Sibenik acquired in 1298, but after 114 years it came under the rule of Venice. In the 15th and 16th centuries, the Turks attacked the city in order to capture, however, all their actions did not give any results.

In 1797, ≈†ibenik moved to Austria, the reason for this was the fall of the Venetian republic. For a short time the city was ruled by France, but in 1813 it again became Austrian.

During the First World War, Italy captured the city, but after the end of the armed struggle, it became part of the Kingdom of Yugoslavia. In 1991, after the independence of Croatia was proclaimed, ≈†ibenik became a part of it.

Sightseeing in Sibenik (Croatia)

The central symbol of the city is the Cathedral of St. James. For its construction, solid stone blocks were used. Not far from the Cathedral of St. James, the main attraction of Sibenik, is the city Lodge (1542). The church of St. Barbara, also nearby, presents a huge collection of medieval art.



Tourists who have arrived for the first time in Sibenik should definitely visit the Princely Palace, which is currently the city museum. The number of exhibits presented in it slightly does not reach 200 thousand. Fans of old frescoes will appreciate the New Church, built in the 16th century.

According to professional architects, the construction of local attractions was carried out under the influence of the Venetian fashion of past times. The bright shades of stones used in the quarries of the island of Brac used in the construction of buildings make the city incredibly beautiful and bright.

The natural wealth of Sibenik

When walking around the outskirts of the city, tourists are delighted with the amazingly colorful scenery of nature untouched by human hands, which include the Krka National Park and the Kornati National Park.



The first is named after a river flowing in the area. Since 1985, Krka Park has become considered a national park. From Sibenik it can be reached in half an hour (by transport). Krka National Park is a series of amazing lakes and waterfalls that form over millennia. Most of the visitors seek to visit 7 waterfall cascades in height from 8 to 46 meters. Swimming in the lake is not prohibited in these places.

Kornati National Park is located 15 km from Sibenik. It consists of 140 small islands. Access to the archipelago is possible only by water. Since there is no ferry service, the only option for a tourist to get to Kornati Park is to rent a boat or buy an excursion ticket.

Legend has it that the archipelago was formed from a handful of stones that remained with God after he created the world. The rich marine flora and fauna, as well as the unique geomorphology, were the reason for the unification of some of the islands into a national park.



Holidays in Sibenik (Croatia)

Wide pebble beaches, lush vegetation and a mild climate - all this attracts tourists from different parts of the globe. On average, about 300 sunny days a year in Sibenik. It is best to come here to relax in August. At this time, the water temperature in the sea is 27 degrees.

Resting in Sibenik, tourists can not only soak up the sun, but also strengthen their body. The best sandy beaches are located at a distance of 6 kilometers from the city center. In Sibenik for visitors there will always be classes for everyone, from scuba diving with special equipment (diving) to sports rafting on mountain rivers in rafts (inflatable boats designed for 2, 4 or 6 people). In addition, here you can hunt small game, go sailing on a yacht or enjoy the stunning views of the city's surroundings, opening from the windows of a helicopter. The presence of numerous water slides will not leave indifferent not only children, but also adults.



Tourists about ≈†ibenik (Croatia) leave positive reviews, noting the extraordinary beauty of nature and fascinating clear water. The only drawback of the resort, according to visitors, is that it is already cool to relax in September, and in summer not everyone has the opportunity to take vacations. For travel lovers and those wishing to visit the Balkan countries, it is best to start with Sibenik.

Where to stay in Sibenik?

The city has a fairly large selection of hotels, but not everyone has enough space, so it is advisable to book rooms in advance. In addition, in this case, accommodation costs a little cheaper.

Tourists can stay in one of the three hotels leading in sales by travel agencies. This is the choice of vacationers. True, they are located 6 kilometers from Sibenik (Croatia):

  • Solaris Beach Hotel Niko. Surrounded by a park, the resort offers comfortable rooms with a balcony. It is located in close proximity to the beach. An outdoor pool is on site.
  • Located in the Solaris Beach Resort complex, Solaris Beach Hotel Jakov offers cozy rooms with air conditioning and a refrigerator. The main advantage of the hotel is its direct proximity to the water park and the beach.
  • Solaris Beach Hotel Andrija. The hotel, also located in the Solaris Beach Resort complex, is chosen by families with children, because it is decorated in a Disney style. For younger visitors, there are many entertainment programs, a special menu and water slides in the water park.

How to get to Sibenik?

The city is connected with the main centers of Croatia and Europe by bus, and with the main coastal Italian cities - by ferry. The distance from the Adriatic city of Trogir is 58 kilometers, from Zadar - 64 km.
