St. Petersburg areas: full list

St. Petersburg is one of the largest cities in Russia, it is located in the northwestern part of the country near the border with the Baltic countries - Estonia and Finland.

Previously, it bore a different name: first Petrograd, and then Leningrad. The people call this city differently: the city on the Neva, the cultural capital, Venice of the North and the Northern Capital.

It is one of the largest megacities in Europe, its great cultural heritage is truly the property of Russia.

Let us consider in more detail the districts of St. Petersburg, how the administrative division is represented.





Vasileostrovsky

It is an island unconnected by land borders with the rest of the city. To be more precise, it includes three islands: Vasilievsky, Golodai and Serny. The Vasileostrovsky district, along with the Admiralteysky, Tsentralny and Petrogradsky, is located in the historical center of the city - all four of these administrative-territorial units represent the central regions of St. Petersburg.

Vasileostrovsky district is close to the main business centers and famous sights of the city, but at the same time due to its location it has weak transport links with the mainland. Residents of St. Petersburg call this area simply Vaska.

Moscow

It is not for nothing that this name was given to this territorial unit of St. Petersburg. Indeed, from this area from Moskovsky Prospekt runs a highway that goes to the capital of the Russian Federation. The avenue is the oldest street of the district, existing since the founding of the city. The Moscow region is the southernmost district of St. Petersburg and one of the most prestigious and beautiful.

Admiralteysky

It houses a famous landmark, in whose honor it is named - this is the Admiralty, and now the Main Headquarters of the Russian Navy. The whole history of this region is directly connected with the fleet. It is located in the central part of the city on the left bank of the Neva.

Frunze

A heavily populated area in the south of the city. It is named after the famous military leader Mikhail Vasilyevich Frunze. This is an industrial zone: about 25% of the total area is occupied by industrial enterprises. In addition, there are massive residential buildings, representing sleeping areas. A considerable problem still lies in its poor transport accessibility.

Krasnoselsky

It occupies a large area and is located in the south-west of St. Petersburg. It includes the city of Krasnoye Selo and a number of villages. It is notable for its environmental friendliness, since it has a small number of industrial enterprises. There are large parks, many lakes and rivers. Its peculiarity is that there is not a single metro station in the area.

Nevsky

The Nevsky District of St. Petersburg is one of the largest regions of St. Petersburg and is the only one located on two banks of the Neva. At the same time, the left-bank and right-bank zones are radically different from each other. The Nevsky district of St. Petersburg on the left bank has more old houses and more industrial enterprises in the housing stock than on the right, which has more comfortable living conditions.

Kronstadt

It has an island location. It is the city of Kronstadt, which is mostly located on the island of Kotlin. Until recently, it was considered a closed zone, where it was impossible to freely get to everyone.

Vyborg

Vyborgsky district of St. Petersburg is one of the oldest districts of the city. It has a large length from the north from the center of St. Petersburg to its outskirts in the south. This is a densely populated area, the population of which is about 450 thousand. Despite the development of industry, this territory is one of the most landscaped in the city.

Seaside

It is considered the leader in terms of population among all areas of St. Petersburg: the number of residents has exceeded the mark of half a million. Located in the northwest of the city. Industry is quite developed here. The territory is a kind of dividing zone between the resort and urbanized parts of the city. At present, it is the most dynamically developing region in St. Petersburg.

Resort

It is distinguished by its favorable location and impressive size. However, the population, compared with its area, is small and amounts to approximately 70 thousand people. This is a picturesque corner of nature with a climate close to marine. There are many famous resorts and resorts.

Kolpinsky

It includes the city of Kolpino, as well as a number of villages. It is located on the banks of the Izhora River in the southeast of the city. It is characterized by a large concentration of large industrial enterprises, which are mainly located outside the residential area. Through Kolpino passes the border dividing the territory into the city and the Leningrad region.

Central

This is the historical center of St. Petersburg, it is located on the left bank of the Neva. Its name itself speaks for itself - here is the center of architectural monuments and the most important state institutions, including the city administration. The construction of new industrial enterprises is prohibited on the territory within the district.

Pushkinsky

It includes two cities - Pushkin and Pavlovsk, as well as three villages. Most of the population lives in the city of Pushkin, which previously had a well-known name - Tsarskoye Selo. This southern region is recognized as one of the most environmentally friendly in the city. This is a major scientific and tourist center.

Petrodvorets

Located in the west of St. Petersburg. It includes: Peterhof, Lomonosov and Strelna. In the north, the area borders the Gulf of Finland. This area is considered prestigious. There are many attractions and green spaces.

Petrogradsky

This is a district with a huge historical past and its oldest territory. It was once the center of St. Petersburg. He took his name from one of the seven islands that make up the composition. The region is notable for the fact that there is the Peter and Paul Fortress, which became the foundation of the Northern capital.

Kalininsky

Kalininsky district of St. Petersburg lies in the north of St. Petersburg. First of all, this territory is a gigantic industrial center, in addition, it can be called a scientific and educational center. Kalininsky and Primorsky - the largest areas of St. Petersburg.

Kirovsky

Kirovsky district of St. Petersburg ranks second in the number of industrial enterprises, in this regard, the environmental situation is quite tense. It has developed transport and social infrastructure. Kirovsky district of St. Petersburg is located in the southwestern part.

Krasnogvardeisky

Located on the right bank of the Neva. It is an ancient district of the city. Earlier it was annexed to the Kalinin district, later regained independence as an administrative-territorial unit.

Despite its large territory and population, St. Petersburg has a fairly simple administrative division. The districts of St. Petersburg are different from each other, and each of them has its own unique features.
