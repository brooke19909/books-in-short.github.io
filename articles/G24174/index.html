The capital of Ghana. Population, economy, sights

Accra is the capital of Ghana, a country in West Africa. It stretched along the coast of the Gulf of Guinea along a hilly plain. It is better to get acquainted with the city by taking a walk along its central streets. In the center of the capital is the Makola market, where tourists can visit the batik and bugle shops, in the west is the Kanesha market. It sells a wide variety of spices and products. It is also worth visiting James Town, which is located on the peninsula, in a southwest direction from the center.

The capital of Ghana is a major transport center. There is an airport and a railway. Passengers are transported by private companies, buses and taxis.




Population

Almost four million people live in Accra. Every year, the population of the city increases by about three percent. Almost all residents are black. There is also a community of descendants of people who immigrated from Syria and Lebanon. In recent years, the size of the Chinese community has been growing. The capital of Ghana is considered a young city, as more than half of the population is younger than 24 years old.

What sectors of the economy could Ghana develop?

Accra is a city with developed textile, metalworking, woodworking, food, oil refining and pharmaceutical industries. Recently, the real estate market is developing rapidly. There is a stock exchange. Almost all of Accra's large firms are controlled by Chinese, British, or Lebanese capital.

Many residents of the capital are engaged in jewelry production, as well as the sale of fruits and vegetables. Accra has a large market for cocoa beans and diamonds.

The most important sights of Accra




Independence Square is the venue for the parades. It can accommodate up to 30,000 people. Located east of the center of Accra. Walking along Independence Square, the first thing you should pay attention to Ozu Castle.

This is the most famous building. But at present, the castle cannot be visited, as it is used by the government.

The building of the Center for the Arts, which is located between Independence Square and the center of the capital, deserves attention. It is used mainly by artisans for the sale of handmade products. It is here that you can see folk dances and various theatrical performances.

The capital of Ghana is famous for its nightlife. Particularly noteworthy are nightclubs, which are almost all located near Nkrumah Square. The interior of some clubs is supplemented by carved wooden figures of idols with frightening masks.




The capital of Ghana is also famous for its unusual beaches. The best beaches are a few kilometers from Accra.

Unfortunately, the most popular and beautiful of them can only be reached by private transport. The exception is Coco Beach. There are also places for surfing.

Tourists can also visit the numerous fishing villages and coastal forts located west of the city of Accra. Ghana is an amazing country! There are fifteen castles and forts, in some of them you can spend the night for only two dollars.
