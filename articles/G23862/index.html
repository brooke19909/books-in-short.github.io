How to make a bubo on a hat: description, photo, master class

Fluffy pompom is a traditional decoration for many models of knitted hats. Therefore, when the knitter starts to manufacture such a product, she should ask in advance how to make a bubo for a hat.

Fortunately, this is a snap. If the necessary materials are available, the craftswoman can master the work with any, even insignificant, experience in knitting.

What is required

This article will describe the most common and simple ways of how to make a bubo of thread on a hat. The principle of pompon formation is the same for all methods, only the way of work and the materials used differ.

You can take absolutely any yarn to create a fluffy decoration. Of course, if you want the color and texture of the material to match the one that was used to knit the hat, then this thread should be taken. However, in order to save money or to give a decorative effect, you can make a pompom from a completely different material.

Before you make a bubo on a hat, you need to consider its color and size. Headgear with one large bubo on the crown looks extremely attractive, but many products (especially for children) provide for the placement of several small elements.

We offer you a master class "How to make a bubo." This will require:

  • yarn;
  • thick cardboard;
  • scissors;
  • a knitting needle (or just with a wide eye).

Sequencing



The most common way to create pompons is to wind yarn on cardboard circles. It‚Äôs not difficult to do this.

  1. Before making a bubo on a hat, draw a circle on cardboard, the size of which should be 25-30% larger than the planned diameter of the pompom. To get the correct circle, use a compass or circle an object of suitable shape.
  2. Inside the big circle, draw a smaller one. You should follow the comparison of the centers of two circles, otherwise the product will be asymmetric. Remember, the wider the inner circle, the more magnificent the pompom will come out. However, if the hole is too wide, the shape of the product will become oval.
  3. Cut the circle in duplicate.
  4. Having compared two identical cardboard parts, we begin to wind yarn on them. For convenience, you can make an incision passing to the inner circle. The thread should be wound evenly, avoiding the formation of areas with excessive compaction or bald spots.
  5. Continue these steps until the inner circle is completely filled with yarn. When it becomes difficult to wind, you can use the needle to continue filling.
  6. Carefully cut the wound threads. To do this, place the point of the scissors under all layers of yarn between two cardboard parts and cut around the circumference.
  7. Now the pompom looks like a large number of short pieces of thread with cardboard parts in the center. Drag the circles a little in different directions and tightly dress the segments with one strong thread.

That's the whole algorithm that describes how to make a bubo of thread per cap. Cut off the finished jewelry from all sides, giving the desired shape, and fix it on the knitted product.

Process features

When working with several colors of yarn, it should be borne in mind that the last wound layers form the center of the pompom. Therefore, a description of how to make a bubo on a hat with an even distribution of several colors will consist of alternating layers.

Alternatively, you can apply the same method by which the pompom was made in the photo above: connecting the threads of two colors when winding on cardboard and achieving the effect of melange.

Using thick yarn will allow you to make the decoration faster, and it will look more fluffy.

Smaller pompons

As mentioned above, small and medium buboes perform almost the same way as large ones. In contrast to the method of winding yarn onto cardboard parts, it is possible to use coils formed with fingers.



As can be seen in the photo, the thread is wound on an even number of fingers (two or four). Then they intercept in the middle with a strong tie and remove the resulting loops.

At the next stage, they need to be cut and cut from all sides. Before you make a pompom (bubo) on the cap in this way, you should consider that it is relatively expensive. The resulting lengths of threads come out very different in length, and they have to be significantly shortened. However, the undoubted plus can be called the volume of the finished pompom.

Alternative ways to create buboes

Using a fork for winding yarn will help to make pompons of a very small size. This method is quick and easy, since the resulting turns will be almost the same size.

To produce a large number of identical elements, you can use the method that perfectly illustrates the following image.





The yarn selected for pompons is wound on the legs of an inverted chair, bandaged in several places and subsequently cut. The volume of finished jewelry depends on the number of layers wound.





The use of pompons is not limited to sewing on caps. They are widely used for decorating mittens, scarves, sweaters, making soft toys or home decoration. Mats made of buboes look very original.
