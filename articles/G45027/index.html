Aivazovsky Park (Partenit) in Crimea

Partenit is one of the small resort villages of Crimea. This is a quiet and comfortable place where tourists choosing a beach and nature vacation come. There are many resorts and family holiday homes. But there are not so many sights and entertainment facilities. History of creation



In Partenit and the surrounding area there is a huge number of sanatoriums and dispensaries. Each medical institution has its own landscaped area for leisure travelers. In the 60s of the last century, Aivazovsky Park was laid around one of the local health resorts. Partenit remained a quiet place for relaxation and treatment. Tourists came here for rest and treatment, including the sanatorium around which the park was laid out. For a long time, the green zone did not receive proper care and gradually became more and more like a wild forest. This is a common story for southern parks. Everything changed in 2003 - then the famous landscape architect Anatoly Annenkov took up the green zone, and in record time he managed to turn the abandoned garden into a real attraction. That is how Aivazovsky Park gained its modern appearance, Partenit has become a popular place for relaxation and day trips from other cities and towns of Crimea.

Original concept



The recreation area is not just landscaped and decorated with new sculptures and plants. The territory of the park tells guests the full and true history of Crimea. Mythical plots and ancient legends here are closely intertwined with references to visits of real historical characters. In 1998, the park was recognized as a monument of landscape gardening art. Today it is a full-fledged attraction. For the sake of her, many tourists come to the Crimea (Partenit). Aivazovsky Park is very popular among the rich and famous. Weddings, anniversaries and other events are held here. Once, the world famous singer Jennifer Lopez even performed in this park.

Vegetable world



Today, about 300 different plant species grow in the park, a significant part of which was brought from all over the world. One of the most interesting objects is an old olive grove. It is believed that these trees were planted more than 200 years ago. Previously, the grove belonged to the garden of the Raevsky estate. Trees still bear fruit today, during the tour you can buy a jar of olives prepared according to the original recipe. During your vacation, be sure to take the time to visit the village of Partenit, a park. Crimea after this story will certainly remain in your memory as a real wonderland. In the green recreation area, themed corners are highlighted: Japanese, Mexican, traditional landscape garden, Spring Garden. The most interesting sculptures

The park pleases visitors with an abundance of decorative statues, arbors, a riot of greenery and flowers, as well as lakes and streams. There are statues of Poseidon, Flora, Eurynome and other ancient Greek gods and goddesses. Many buildings are also made in the antique style - these are majestic colonnades and romantic gazebos. There is also a monument to A.S. Pushkin in the park and realistic sculptures of animals: golden horses, deer. Before going to the park, do not forget to charge the camera - this place is literally created for photo shoots.

Aivazovsky Park (Partenit): how to get there and do I need to sign up for an excursion?



The magic relaxation area today belongs to the health-improving complex. But there is good news: anyone can get here. Attention: entrance is paid. But, believe me, what they see justifies it exceeds the price of the ticket. You can explore the territory on your own or as part of a tour group with a guided tour. In the second case, you can not only get aesthetic pleasure, but also learn a lot about exotic plants and listen to the history of each statue. The exact address where the picturesque sanatorium park is located: Crimea, Partenit, st. Vasilchenko, 1A. During the tourist season, the relaxation area is open daily during daylight hours. For independent inspection of the park, an appointment is not required.

Travel Tips



The most spectacular park looks from spring to autumn. In winter, heat-loving plants are covered with greenhouses; improvement works are being carried out. The park occupies a fairly large territory. If you want to fully enjoy the tour, set aside at least 2 hours for sightseeing. On the territory of the green zone there is a shop where you can buy souvenirs for memory, as well as natural products of local production: jam, jam, herbal gatherings and teas.

Of the many resort towns and towns of Crimea, excursions to Aivazovsky Park are offered. Partenit is the village where this attraction is located; remember this name if you plan to travel by yourself. In fact, organizing an excursion on your own is quite easy. In Partenit buses and minibuses from other settlements go, and if you stayed somewhere nearby, it will be inexpensive to get by taxi.
