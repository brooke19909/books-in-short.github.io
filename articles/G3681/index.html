What are ringtones and how to create them?

What are ringtones? Ringtone refers to a melody, song or other audio file that is played by a cell phone during an incoming call. It can also be played with incoming SMS and MMS messages.

Depending on how technically advanced the cell phone is, it can play individual sounds, and maybe melodies and songs. History of the creation of ringtones





The origin of the ringtone is taken from an American company engaged in the field of telephony. It was called AT&T. By right, it is considered to be precisely its founder of ringtones. The thing is that the company produced such products as landlines. They, in turn, had seven combinations at once in the device, which made it possible to set an audio alert in case of an incoming phone call.

Combinations of sounds made it possible to distinguish between the devices from which the sound emanated. Otherwise, if each phone had only one ringtone, it would be quite problematic to determine the clearly needed device in a couple of seconds.

In addition, some signals were developed specifically for those people who had hearing problems. They were louder, which allowed users not to leave the call unattended.

What are the ringtones?

They are divided into 3 main groups:

1) Realtones. Their role is played by ordinary songs and music tracks recorded in digital formats. Users who ask the question so often what ringtones are, do not even suspect that they encounter them when they just listen to music. Typically, representatives of this category have the MP3 format as the most common and readable.

2) Monaural. At one time in such ringtones there is no more than one note.

3) Polyphonic. More than one note can be played back one measure, several at once.
