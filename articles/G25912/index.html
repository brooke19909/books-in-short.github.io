How to make cloves from napkins

Implementation technologies are slightly different: different improvised tools, frame elements and objects that facilitate the work process are used. The principle for all methods is common: cut the blanks, join and fasten the paper circles, paint and set on the stalk.

A step-by-step technique on how to make carnations from napkins with your own hands (master class) is described below. Differences in manufacturing principles are as follows:

  • As a stem, use a wire or a cocktail tube. In the first case, you will need to make two holes in the stack of blanks to thread and secure the frame made in the form of a loop. The plastic tube is fixed with plasticine, the ball of which is inserted inside the bud.
  • To cut circles for workpieces evenly, you can fasten the layers with two staples, or you can connect in the middle with a stapler.
  • The edges of the layers are left even or cut with scissors in the form of a fringe.
  • Sepals are cut out from a separate sheet of colored paper or made from green napkins. In a simple version, they can be omitted altogether.
  • The stalk is wrapped with adhesive tape or colored paper using glue.
  • To make blanks from napkins, compasses are used, you can cover the cans as a stencil.

As you can see, the options are not too different. The choice depends on what you have at hand, or how easier it is to make this or that part.





What you need to prepare

Making cloves from a napkin will not require expensive materials. So you need the following:

  • ordinary paper napkins;
  • floristic or any other wire, as well as a suitable tube or stick;
  • green tape or strip of paper and glue;
  • a compass or a round pattern (a cover from a can);
  • pencil;
  • scissors;
  • felt-tip pens or markers;
  • plasticine, stapler (depending on the manufacturing method that you choose).

As you can see, everything is cheap and affordable. Most likely, the necessary materials and tools are already available at home.

Do-it-yourself carnations from napkins: master class

When everything is ready for you, proceed to the elements of your floral composition.



The sequence of work will be as follows:

  1. Take a few napkins and stack them on top of one another. The more they will be, the more magnificent the flower will turn out.
  2. Fix them together with paper clips or connect in the middle with a stapler.
  3. Draw a circle with a compass or circle the pattern.
  4. Cut the workpiece with scissors.
  5. Stitch the edges with the desired color. If desired, you can cut the edges with a fringe.
  6. Make a hole in the center of the circle (one for the duct, two for the wire frame).
  7. Secure the stem with plasticine (thread the wire in the form of a loop).
  8. Fold the top circle to the center.
  9. Do the same with everyone else. Do not try to make the creases exactly the same.
  10. When the flower is formed, fluff it.
  11. Wrap the tube with a green ribbon or strip of paper, gluing it every few centimeters.
  12. Make sepals from a separate piece of paper, fix them on a stalk near a flower.




In fact, to make such a beauty is not difficult. Even a child can handle the craft. For kids, paper blanks can be cut out by adults, the same goes for the hole for the stick. Entrust everything else to young self-made.

Bouquet

So, a flower from napkins (cloves) is ready, but one it will not look as good as several. You can make the buds the same or different.

If you use several templates, you will get blanks for a sprig of cloves. In this case, it is good to make buds and half-opened flowers. To do this, it is necessary to cut the sepals detail and attach them to the main part. Make a twig and place flowers on it. Put your carnations in a vase or fasten them together with a ribbon and a bow.



Topiary

The resulting "roses" from napkins can be used to create a decorative tree in the form of a ball. You will need the following:

  • a base, for example, an unnecessary ball or foam blank;
  • kernel;
  • a pot or vase;
  • glue;
  • pins
  • gypsum or alabaster;
  • additional decor.

The sequence of work will be as follows:

  1. Blanks made of napkins can be tightly attached to one another with a pin. It is also good to use glue.
  2. Attach the resulting ball for the topiary to the rod. If it‚Äôs more convenient for you, first assemble the entire frame (ball and stem), and then decorate it.
  3. The whole structure is installed in a pot / vase, after which the container is filled with gypsum (alabaster), diluted to the consistency of sour cream.
  4. After hardening, the surface is decorated with beads, beads, coffee beans.

You can wrap the wand with a satin ribbon or a strip of paper of a suitable shade. Decorate the topiary with additional details (beads, ribbons, bows, coins). Such a souvenir will be a wonderful gift for any holiday.
