Mark Thatcher: biography and photos

Mark Thatcher from birth was only the son of an "iron lady." During his life, he tried himself in jewelry, entrepreneurship, lobbying, car racing and the coup in Equatorial Guinea. How successful were his designs, what is the role of Margaret Thatcher in them , can be found in the article.

short biography

Mark Thatcher was born with his twin sister Carroll on 08/15/1953. Due to Margaret Thatcher's complications, the doctors made a cesarean section. The family's father, Dennis Thatcher, found out what happened the next day.

Childhood and youth





By the age of nine, young Mark Thatcher was assigned to a prestigious private school - Harrow. Over the years of training, he was remembered for being one of the stupidest students. Classmates made fun of him in every possible way, inventing various offensive nicknames for him.

In 1971, at the age of eighteen, the young man graduated from school. He could hardly get a certificate of education. His knowledge was not enough to go to university. This fact was extremely unpleasant for his mother, who already held the post of minister in the field of education and science.

He tried various possibilities in order to determine his life:

  • three times entered accounting courses (unsuccessfully);
  • opened jewelry business (the market was in recession);
  • production of carts for supermarkets (did not arouse interest among entrepreneurs);
  • opening an international consulting company.

Racing career





The young man tried his hand not only in business. Once he came up with the idea to participate in races, namely in the Paris-Dakar rally. The idea ended rather deplorably.

Mark Thatcher (race car driver) could not finish the race, lost in the Sahara. Mother and father organized a large-scale campaign to find a son. Due to the incident, Margaret Thatcher was only once seen crying.

The press closely followed the search and was extremely disappointed with Mark's behavior. The fact is that at the time of his salvation, he did not shake hands with his own father and did not thank the rescue team from Algeria. The saved one only muttered to everyone that he was glad to see everyone in health.

Family status

Conspirator

In South Africa, Thatcher Mark was originally a pretty famous person. Due to scandals in lobbying, he and his family moved to Africa before the parliamentary elections. The issue of the premier venue in which Margaret Thatcher took part was being decided. Despite the fact that the scandalous son was not in the UK, his statements were constantly published in the British media. This was not in favor of the mother, who lost the election.





During his first time in a new country, Mark was quiet. He did not deal with major transactions, spending more time at social events. The last time he was in England was at his father's funeral in 2003.

Living in the prestigious part of Cape Town, the Thatcher family talked with fairly eminent neighbors, among whom were:

  • Nelson Mandella - a fighter with apartheid;

  • Mark Rich is a scandalous billionaire;

This did not save his person from scandals. The newly-minted sir was detained by South African special forces, accusing him of conspiracy to overthrow the government of Equatorial Guinea, a friendly state for South Africa. It all started with the detention in 2004 at a Zimbabwean airport of a plane carrying weapons and mercenaries led by British military Simon Mann. The investigation saw in the attempted coup d'etat the possibility of Communion Thatcher.

He was facing fifteen years in prison. Mark's mother could not stand aside and, returning from a trip to the USA to London, began to save her son. She managed to secure his release from custody by making a bail.

The accused did not admit his guilt, but in 2005 a South African court sentenced him to probation and a fine.

Barony

Mark Thatcher was not born into a simple family. It's not just about the mother, who was the seventy-first prime minister of Great Britain. Mark's father was a fairly successful businessman. In addition, in 1990 he was granted the title of chivalry of the baronet, which was inherited.

In 2003, Sir Denis Thatcher died and his title passed to his only son Mark. Thus, he became the second baronet.
