Interesting Facts About Books

Papyrus Prissa is the oldest book, miraculously preserved to this day. The author created his work back in 3350 BC. The history of the book indicates that it was discovered in Thebes, where it was located in one of the pyramids.





Surprisingly, the topic that the author touches on is of interest to the inhabitants of the modern world. Priss in his work talks about the conflict of generations. The creator of the oldest book complains about the vices of the youth who lived at that time. He reproaches boys and girls for laziness and lack of education, ascribes various vices to them. Today, the papyrus is owned by the National Library, located in Paris.

Diamond Sutra

Interesting facts about the first printed book are also noteworthy. The Diamond Sutra, an Indian work translated into Chinese, is considered such. Chinese Wang Chi printed the essay on May 11, 868.





Amazing book is a scroll consisting of six sheets of text. There is also an engraving sheet depicting the Buddha. It is known that the Chinese craftsman worked for a long time on the creation of this work. The hieroglyphic stamps were made by the master from clay, then he burned them using a special stove. Several times it was necessary to re-start the creation of stamps, but Wang Chi was a stubborn person. The paper on which the Chinese stopped was strong and thick, as he strove for the long-term preservation of his book.

The invention of printing

It is known that the Johannes Gutenberg, a German , was the inventor of typography ; this happened around 1445. Gutenberg created not only a printing press, but also an alloy designed to create printed letters, a word-casting apparatus, a collapsible font. Also on his account is the development of a special composition of printing ink.





Gutenberg's Bible is the first printed book created by a brilliant German. This happened around the 50s of the fifteenth century. These were times when the church had great power. Not surprisingly, about a third of all first-printed books were devoted to religion. The first publishers did not seek a large number of copies - on average there were up to three hundred. In those years, books still lacked a title page. Information about the author, title of the work, year of publication and so on was offered at the end. Only by the end of the 15th century, this information began to be placed on the first page.

The Apostle is the first printed book published in Russia, information about which has survived to this day. This honor belongs to the printer Ivan Fedorov, who lived in Moscow. It is known that work on this work was completed in 1564. Fifty copies that have survived to this day are considered rare. The existence of books released before this event is not excluded.

The most distant book

The Bible is perhaps the most long-suffering work in the world that has survived to this day. About Libraries

Of course, there are other amazing facts about books that may interest readers. For example, in the old days books were exhibited on the shelves with roots inward. This was necessary due to the fact that the roots of the works did not differ in strength. If they were treated carelessly, they instantly came off, as a result of which the books scattered.





Centuries ago, reading was a costly hobby, and it was accessible mainly to wealthy people. Books that were in public libraries were impossible to steal. To protect the valuable volumes, long chains were used to secure them to the shelves.

The biggest

Interesting facts about children's books cannot be ignored. It is to the youngest readers that the work "The World's Largest Book for Babies" is oriented. The brainchild of the publishing house "In" was born in 2004. The length of the miracle volume is six meters, the width is three meters. For comparison, the average room in a small apartment has the same dimensions. The impression is made by the weight of the book, which has 492 kg. It contains poems by famous Russian authors, and there are colorful illustrations.





Earlier, the status of the largest book in the world was possessed by Superbook, published in Denver back in 1976. The weight of the volume is 252 kg, it is inferior to the "World's Largest Book for Babies" and in terms of dimensions.

The smallest

The smallest book in the world is also considered a work intended for kids. This is a fairy tale "Old King Cole", published in 1985. The first thing that impresses is the size of the volume, which does not go beyond the millimeter squared. Obviously, reading a fairy tale without special equipment is not possible. In total, there are 85 copies of the "Old King Cole."

Other books included in the list of the smallest volumes in the world have similar sizes. For example, the French Constitution, the publication of the Qur'an, an English dictionary containing about 12 thousand words.

Amazing collections

Of course, unusual facts about books do not end there. It's no secret that they can not only be read, but also collected. An amazing collection compiled by the Italian Rio Coselli. This person dreams of acquiring all the most boring works in the world and has already achieved obvious successes in this field. His home collection contains more than ten thousand copies. Legend has it that a loser poet tried to get his hands on himself when he was informed that all of his creations are in the Coselli collection.





Book collections are collected in different ways; owners buy them, inherit them or as a gift. The most original way was chosen by Stephen Bloomberg, who simply stole rare works.
