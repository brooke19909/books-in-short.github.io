Openwork crochet: patterns

Beginning needlewomen are faced with the question of why a crochet is necessary (the diagrams are presented below) of the edge of the finished product. Which pattern is best for this? Yes, so that he gives the blouse a finished look or makes a simple sirloin elegant.





What is it for?

There are several reasons why you might need a crochet (patterns are required). Firstly, it gives a finished look to the product. So the neck or cuffs, the sides of the jackets or the bottom of the product look more beautiful and neater.

Secondly, the fabric knitted or crocheted is twisted from the bottom. This spoils the look of the product. The tight binding slightly pulls it down, which prevents it from twisting.

Thirdly, it can play an auxiliary role in connecting parts of the product. For example, skirts and ruffles or blouses with a yoke.

Fourth, it simply performs a decorative function. This is true for napkins and curtains.





It is used not only for knitwear, but also for those that are sewn from fabric.

Wrapping step

It is used when a very thin and neat outline is needed. Before you start crocheting the hemming scheme in this way, you need to evaluate its appearance. If it is uneven, then the first thing is a row of crochets.

"Rachich step" is also a single crochet, only it runs in the opposite direction. Usually the direction of knitting goes from right to left. In the "step", the movement is from left to right.





Long fringed shawl harness

Of course, it can be done by simply cutting the threads into pieces and securing along the edge. But such a fringe will not look neat, it will become confused. Better if it's still a quality crochet shawl. Her schemes are so simple that even a beginner needlewoman will cope with them.

It is recommended to start processing the edge with a number of double crochet posts. They will be the basis for a long fringe. The second row is the fringe itself. Depending on the thickness of the selected thread and the desired length of the fringe, you need to dial a chain of air loops. It is clear that they should not be less than 25. Otherwise, the fringe of the harness will be very short.

Then close a small ring on the edge of the fringe, making a single crochet in the fifth loop from the hook. Then tie another chain of such a number of loops so that it is 5 shorter than the previous one. Attach the chain to the canvas, skipping one column of the previous row. One turn of the fringe is ready. Continue knitting them around the edge of the shawl.

This fringe looks interesting if made from chains of different lengths. You can even tie it in waves. First, gradually increasing the length of the fringe, and then also decreasing. It will be beautiful.
