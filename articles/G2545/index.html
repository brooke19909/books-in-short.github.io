Radischevsky Museum (Saratov): exhibitions, paintings and the official website

The Radishchev Museum in Saratov, among other things, is notable for the fact that it is one of the oldest provincial museums in Russia. It exists since 1885 and is widely known in art circles. Often people from afar come to the city mainly to visit the Radishchev Museum. Saratov, among other things, is also known in the world for this attraction.

How it all began

The actual history of the museum began eight years earlier than the date of its official opening. The basis of his collection was a private collection of the Russian artist A.P. Bogolyubov, who lived in Paris, who was the grandson of A.N. Radishchev. The collection was a collection of works of art and a library of rare books and manuscript documents. All this was donated to the city of Saratov, subject to the provision of decent premises for a permanent museum exhibition. The name of the future museum should be immortalized the name of an outstanding Russian thinker, writer Radishchev. A prerequisite was also to ensure the accessibility of the collection for anyone who wishes to get acquainted with it. This circumstance turned out to be one of the most serious problems that the future Radishchev Museum had faced even before its opening. Saratov simply did not have a building that would fully comply with the stated requirements.



Museum building

The city authorities of Saratov were forced to build a new, worthy building in order to be able to accept as a gift a collection of artworks from the collection of professor of painting A.P. Bogolyubov. This architectural work could not be ordinary in its status as a museum of art and historical values. The project was entrusted to the famous St. Petersburg architect I.V. Shtrom. And the place chosen was quite appropriate - in the historical center of the city. The construction was supervised by the local architect A. M. Salko. The two-story stone building, made in the style of Russian classicism, was completed two years later. A suitable room appeared on Theater Square where the Radishchevsky Museum could be housed. Saratov was looking forward to this event. At the opening of the museum, in addition to many officials, the famous founder of the Tretyakov Gallery was also present.



Collection replenishment

The project was administratively supported by historical figures such as Konstantin Pobedonostsev and the new Emperor of Russia Alexander the Third, who takes over the board . The monarch even presented several paintings from his collection to the new museum. So began the Radishchev Museum. Saratov was the first Russian provincial city to open a collection of art of such a level to the general public. This event did not go unnoticed in Russia. In the first year of operation alone, the museum was visited by over sixty thousand people. The basic part of the collection was soon supplemented with new arrivals from the Hermitage and the St. Petersburg Academy of Arts.



After the revolution

During the Soviet period of history, the museum‚Äôs collection was replenished repeatedly. But the largest influx of new works of art occurred shortly after the end of the Civil War, when exhibits from the State Museum Fund were transferred to the funds of the Saratov Museum, along with other provincial cultural centers. Today, the paintings of the Radishchev Museum cover the entire period of the formation and development of Russian painting - from the beginning of the eighteenth century to the present day. A collection of European art is also very representative. And of course, in the halls and funds of the Saratov Museum, the cultural heritage of the Soviet era is widely represented - works that correspond to the traditions of socialist realism. Formed by the beginning of the 21st century, the collection allows us to classify the Radishchevsky Museum among the cultural centers of world significance.
