Kusadasi (Turkey) - a popular resort on the shores of the Aegean

The name "Kusadasi" is translated from Turkish as "bird island". This is one of the most famous resorts in the world. Kusadasi resort (Turkey) is located on the shores of the Aegean Sea. The symbol of this place is an ancient fortress on the Pigeon Island, which was built by the famous pirate Barbarossa in the 16th century. This fortress served as his residence.





A cozy resort in recent years has become quite popular among tourists, and for good reason. So, the richest excursion program is offered in Kusadasi. Turkey is famous for ancient monuments, which are located in the immediate vicinity of this resort.

Interesting places

The city is surrounded on almost all sides by antiquities. This is Ephesus with a perfectly preserved facade of the ancient library of Celsus, an antique amphitheater for 24 thousand spectators, a fountain of the emperor Hadrian, Roman baths - this and much more can be seen in Kusadasi (Turkey). The map is filled with famous places: the outstanding city of Troy, described in the Iliad by Homer, the largest ancient Pergamum, the city of Didima, in which the temple of Apollo is located.

Guvergin Island or Pigeon Island

The symbol of Kusadasi, the island of Guvergin, is connected to the coast by a long dam. The island throughout the history of the city played a significant strategic role in the life of Kusadasi. His main task was to protect the coast from the sea.





An interesting attraction of the island is the fortress, which for a long time served to protect against attacks by enemy troops. The impressive towers with loopholes indicate this. Today, a museum is located in the central part of the fortress.

Calais District

Without a visit to the Kale region, which is the most attractive part of the resort, it is impossible to relax in Kusadasi (Turkey). Narrow streets, old houses in a traditional style, bazaars and a mosque help to imagine how many centuries ago this city looked.

The Kaleici Mosque was erected in 1618 by the Great Vizier Ekus Mehmed Pasha, and it is considered the most impressive mosque in Kusadasi. The mosque occupies a huge area - 1800 square meters. m., it can simultaneously accommodate 550 people. In the central part of the mosque is a huge lead dome, which is supported by 12 arches.

Surroundings of the resort of Kusadasi, Turkey

A little south of the resort, in the vicinity of Kusadasi, is the city of Davutlar, famous for its Kursundu monastery. It is believed that earlier this monastery was an Orthodox church built in the II century. On the territory of the monastery there is a cemetery and a small chapel. It is built at an altitude of 600 meters above sea level. Therefore, when visiting this wonderful place you can enjoy a picturesque view of Kusadasi, the island of Samos and Guzelchamly.





The famous sources of Balchov, which are famous for their thermal properties, are also located in the vicinity of Kusadasi (Turkey). Balchova is the largest of them. According to ancient legends, King Agamemnon, after a battle with the Trojans, led his wounded army to these hot springs. In a few days, the warriors completely healed their wounds and regained strength, and the sources have since been called the Baths of Agamemnon. Today, there is a modern thermal complex, where you can get healthy with mud and mineral baths.

Another famous resort destination is the Zeus Cave, located near the entrance to the Dilek Peninsula National Park. The cave is surrounded by olive trees and bright flowers, and inside it is a pool with clear water, in which, according to legend, Zeus swam in the company of the most beautiful girls in the neighboring village.
