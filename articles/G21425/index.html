What is famous for ancient Ulm (Germany)?

This German city, the unique atmosphere of which is celebrated by all tourists, harmoniously combines the past and the present. Located between Stuttgart and Munich, it is an important economic center of the country. On the left bank of the Danube is the glorious Ulm, which will be discussed in the article, and on the right - its twin city, modern New Ulm.

A bit of history

It is known that the first mention of the village is dated 854. The center of the Duchy of Swabia was repeatedly attacked by an enemy who wanted to conquer a proud city. After military clashes, Ulm (Germany) was turned into real ruins, and the only surviving building was a small church, on the site of which later a magnificent cathedral appeared, which became the hallmark of the city.





But perhaps the most difficult test was the Second World War. It was then that the cultural capital of the country suffered so much that it was necessary to rebuild it. Local residents made an important decision, which they never regretted, to restore the destroyed historical buildings, and now in the city the restored buildings are peacefully adjacent to the modern ones that appeared recently.

The main cathedral is the tallest in the world

Tourists coming to Ulm (Germany) think that they were transported into past eras as if by a time machine, and the architectural monuments of the Middle Ages become a vivid reminder of the historical past. Chief among them is the cathedral dominating the surrounding area, recognized as a symbol of the city.





The building, defining the appearance of the German tourist center, is famous for its huge spire (161 meters), piercing the sky through. Luxurious architectural masterpiece

Residents are proud of the temple, founded in the XIV century, and its highest spire in the world. For almost five hundred years, the construction of the cathedral, preserved from destruction by the Most High itself, lasted. In a serious bombardment of 1944, he survived, which was an incredible miracle, and today it captures the imagination of everyone who came to enjoy the amazing landscapes in the city of Ulm (Germany).





I must say that not only the architectural solution of the Gothic masterpiece surprises tourists, but also the decoration of the most beautiful temple, which stores many works of art. Colorful stained-glass windows, carved benches and wonderful old frescoes turn the cathedral into a real museum, telling about the strength of the human spirit.

Tourists will not fail to tell that it was here that the great Mozart played the organ.

Fishing quarter

Next to the cathedral is the Fisherman's Quarter, which consists of narrow streets, which are crowded with houses built in the traditional German style - half-timbered. Lightweight frame structures stand in the water, and tourists have the feeling that they are not in respectable Germany, but in fairy-tale Venice.





The quarter connected by old bridges made Ulm (Germany) famous throughout the world. Photos of the restored buildings make tourists irresistible desire to visit the German pearl.

By the way, here is the famous crooked house, listed in the Guinness Book of Records. The building, which is leaning towards the water, has been converted into a luxury hotel, and for 130 euros anyone can spend the night at local attractions.





City Hall

Old Ulm is a city in Germany, famous not only for its elegantly executed cathedral, but also for its town hall with bright frescoes on the facade, the images of which tell of virtues and vices. Once it was an ordinary trading shop, and the underground floor served as a prison for criminals. The well-preserved southern outbuilding with a stepped pediment, huge astronomical and sundials are the main features of the structure.

Sberkassa spent on 20 million euros

But we must admit that not only historical monuments glorious beautiful Ulm (Germany). In 2006, interest in the city was caused by the appearance of a new savings bank building, which appeared as part of a major construction project. A record amount of 20 million euros was spent on the construction of a four-story glass structure, and after the opening of the Neue Mitte became a symbol of the modern city. The light-filled construction successfully met the expectations of the public, who did not want to see the usual concrete building on this site.





For this project, the architect received an award in the field of construction.

Pyramid library

Unforgettable Ulm (Germany), whose attractions are diverse, delights lovers of unusual architecture. Another building that turned the consciousness of the townsfolk appeared in 2004. The glass pyramid houses the city library, which celebrated its 500th anniversary. The modern building fits in perfectly with the look of the historic center.

Almost four thousand square meters of the cultural institution has a library, a music department and modern reading rooms. The transparent building is equipped with the latest technology, and interactive facades and climate control make it attractive to visitors.





Tourists love Ulm (Germany), in which historical roots and modern life are closely intertwined. He beckons guests who have already heard about the main attractions that have become his calling cards. The hospitable and vibrant city, saturated with the spirit of the times, attracts at first sight, and for this travelers from all over the world adore it.
