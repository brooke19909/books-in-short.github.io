Germany, Mannheim - history, attractions, personalities

When traveling in Germany, in no case do you need to bypass Mannheim. The city attracts with beautiful parks, legendary palaces and a rich infrastructure. The local nature fascinates with the beauties of Neckar and the Rhine. In Germany, Mannheim is located next to Ludwigshafen and Heidelberg. Twin cities are also Rida, Toulon and others.

Historical reference

Mannheim appeared in the status of a village earlier than 766 in Germany. Mannheim received city status after almost a hundred years, in 1607. During this period, the central part of the city began to form in the shape of a horseshoe. In the center of the square is a palace with straight streets extending from it, in the style typical of Germany. Mannheim in 1720 took over the residence of the Electors. Then the city underwent the grandiose construction of the Mannheim Palace. The widest street of the city is called Kurpfaiz, it is important. Along it is a huge number of shops and office buildings.

Mannheim, Germany - Attractions

Once in Mannheim, the first thing you should visit the Mannheim Palace. The building is located in the coastal part of the Rhine and is made in the Baroque style. Its construction took the Germans more than forty years. A distinctive feature of the palace is a facade with a length of 450 meters. On the territory of the building 500 rooms of different directions. Each of them is distinguished by its exquisite decoration, magnificent paintings, and exclusive sculptures. The prototype of this building is the world-famous Versailles Palace. The main difference of the Mannheim Palace is the presence of a larger number of windows. There are more than one of them. This masterpiece of architecture was seriously affected by the bombing of the Second World War. His current condition is the result of a large-scale restoration. Currently, this building is the abode of science. Here is the prestigious university of Germany.

Mannheim also boasts the largest water tower. It is made in the best traditions of modernity. Sculptures of centaurs, mermaids, newts are used as its decoration. But the main pride is still the statue of the wife of Poseidon. The height of this structure is amazing - 60 meters. Around the tower is a beautiful park area.

A frequent place for visiting city guests is the temple of St. Sebastian. The tower of the church rushes into the sky to a height of fifty-seven meters. Three times a day a peaceful bell ringing is heard from it. Inside the church is a beautiful altar and a divine statue of the Virgin.

Tourists who want to enjoy the natural beauties of Mannheim just need to stroll around Luisenpark. This place is one of the most beautiful in the whole of Germany. Famous personalities

Germany, Mannheim is just a storehouse of talented people. Here, at one time, the German psychologist Wilhelm Wundt was born and raised . It was he who became the creator of the world's first laboratory working in the format of experimental psychology. The talented artist Walter Stahlwitz and the famous composer Giseler Klebe hail from here. In Mannheim, the notorious Albert Speer was born . During the existence of Nazi Germany, he was engaged in important public affairs, and was also Hitler's personal architect. Often on this German soil, future footballers were born.
