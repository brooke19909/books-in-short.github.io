Domodedovo urban district - history and modernity

In 2005, the Domodedovo district of the Moscow region was abolished. Following this, the Domodedovo urban district was formed, which included all the settlements of the district (including the airport), which is 150 settlements. The total population was 167,907 people, they live on 82 hectares. The administrative center of the district is the city of Domodedovo.

History of the city

In 1900, a railway station was opened (on the Moscow-Pavelets direction) called Domodedovo. In those days, there was a village under the same name. Mention of the village is still in the spiritual letter of the XV century. It is known that then these were the possessions of the Romanov family.

In 1898, the construction of factories and plants began in the village; alabaster-lime, brick and cement plants worked here. During the period of revolution and civil war, enterprises did not work, but in the 20s of the last century, the restoration of industry began. At that time, a little more than 100 people lived in Domodedovo, but there was already an elementary school here. In the summer season, the situation changed dramatically, as Muscovites gathered in the village for recreation. So, already by 1924 a suburban summer cottage was formed, the number of permanent residents increased. Since the 30s, new factories and cooperatives began to appear, and a high school was rebuilt.

After the war, the settlement turned into a city of regional subordination. In 1970, a cinema, libraries, clubs, the House of Pioneers, and other infrastructure appeared in the city.





Economics and industry

The urban district of Domodedovo shows a fairly stable growth in the economy. As of the first quarter of this year, economic turnover grew by 25.7%. The growth rate of the volume of shipped goods also shows an increase (by 95.6% compared to the same period last year). The district has a consistently high salary, the average is over 40 thousand rubles. The unemployment rate in the region is low, as of April 1, 2017, it amounted to only 0.47%, with the country's total - 5.8%.

There is an increase in revenues from taxes and other fees by approximately 129.2%. 35% of the budget of the urban district of Domodedovo is formed by air transportation. For the first quarter of this year, passenger traffic grew by 4.4%.





Housing and communal services and infrastructure

Out of dilapidated housing, 28 families were settled in the first quarter alone. 479 pits on public roads were eliminated in the okrug, construction of the Kashirskoye Shosse - Kiselikha Highway is ongoing. The construction of kindergartens in the district of South and North continues. To eliminate the second shift in the existing schools, construction of two more is ongoing. The construction of the Lira Children's Home in the Western district has been completed.





Ecology

In the urban district of Domodedovo, the environmental situation is favorable. A significant place in the economy of the region is occupied by enterprises engaged in the extraction of sand, clay and limestone. Because of this, the natural landscape is practically not preserved.

There are 173 enterprises located on the territory of the okrug, which are sources of negative impact on the environment. The most dangerous is Domodedovagrostroy CJSC. The total stationary emission of harmful substances per year is 5,413.595 tons.

The main pollutants in the county are carbon monoxide and nitrogen dioxide. Their share in the overall statistics reaches 60%. Strongly pollute the air and cars. The main problem of the region is the lack of dust removal and gas purification plants in the territories of industrial enterprises and near highways.

A huge problem is with unorganized discharges, which form about 150 local enterprises. There are about 3 111.772 tons of waste, most of which goes into water bodies. Most industrial enterprises do not have storm sewers and do not carry out wastewater treatment.

In the urban district of Domodedovo, Moscow Region, many unauthorized landfills. On the right bank of the Pakhra River (near the town of Podolsk), the closed TBPO landfill carries a huge danger, the radiation background is increased here, because the radioactive waste of the chemical plant is stored nearby.
