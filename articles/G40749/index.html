Quilling technique

Quilling is a type of needlework. The source material is long thin strips of paper of different colors. They are twisted into spirals from which voluminous compositions are created. Elements are glued together, as a result, very interesting models are obtained. This technique can be used to decorate a room, create jewelry, decorate gifts.

For this needlework there is no need for large expenses, no special tools are required. Any work can be done with handy colored double-sided paper, glue, scissors, tweezers, pair of compasses, a ruler, a pencil, a stencil and a rod.

In addition, this type of needlework requires accuracy and perseverance. Before you begin to perform work in this technique, you must first master the basic types of twisting. There are about thirty spiral twisting elements. All of them involve three steps: roll up, glue, pinch. Finished spirals are pinched into different shapes, from which the final work is then created.

Easter symbol

The egg is a symbol of the main religious holiday of Orthodoxy. It symbolizes life, since it is itself the source of a new life. The red color in which the Easter eggs are painted symbolizes the blood of the crucified Christ. Thus, Orthodox Christians celebrate the resurrection of Jesus. One of the most beautiful legends is associated with the tradition of dyeing eggs for Easter . The Roman emperor Tiberius received an egg from Mary Magdalene with the words "Christ has risen." He was surprised and said that no one could be resurrected. And then the egg began to blush.

Smart easter egg

The Easter egg made using the quilling technique is very elegant. The master class offers a step-by-step instruction on how to complete this craft.

First, make a blank. To do this, take a raw egg, apply a puncture from two ends with a thin needle and blow out the contents. Leave for a day or two to dry.

At the next stage, we proceed to the manufacture of curls. They will need so many to complete any drawing. We will make a chicken on a background of blue sky and grass, so we will prepare the following amount: blue and green pieces of 10-15 each, depending on the size of the original workpiece; 2-3 white for clouds; two yellow and two red for the chicken; 6 pieces of red and yellow for stand.





Now you can decorate our egg. First, make a stand. We will give the curls a look of a drop and glue them together, for which we use PVA glue.

Next, at one end of the egg we lay out two rows of grass and put the egg on a stand. It should be placed on a small sheet of paper and now you can easily rotate it to stick the rest of the picture. We put a chicken on the grass, then there is a sky with clouds. We give our craft to dry well and the quilling Easter egg is ready. Different ideas

We make the handle out of cardboard and also decorate it. We glue the bottom of the box with velvet paper and cover it with weed cut from green paper. Now we are doing the quilling Easter egg. How to make it, consider in more detail. From colored paper we wind 16-20 pieces of washers. They must be tight. Then carefully squeeze out half of the egg from each washer and glue 2 pieces together. It turns out a bright Easter egg using the quilling technique. The master class demonstrates how it is carried out. We fill the basket with these elegant eggs and use it as a decoration.
