"Balashikha-park" - a complex for a comfortable life in the Moscow region

East of the capital of Russia is the largest city in the region - Balashikha. You can get into it along several highways: Shchelkovsky, Bypass, Enthusiasts. Only 6 kilometers separate Balashikha from the Moscow Ring Road, which makes the city very attractive for developers and immigrants from other cities.

A bit about the city

The largest city in the Moscow region, whose population is approaching half a million inhabitants and is growing steadily. For the period 2015-2016 it has almost doubled. This is facilitated by the status of the city as the most economically developed. Where there are jobs, residents will only stay. Developers consider the development of Balashikha promising. And one of the most striking examples is Balashikha Park.

Description of the residential complex

RC "Balashikha-Park" is a whole microdistrict consisting of panel houses of different floors. Several houses were built on an individual project. Administrative buildings complement the landscape. A central boiler house operates in the microdistrict, providing all residential buildings with water and heating. The developer used both proven and the latest innovative technologies in construction.

Apartments in the complex: cost

Apartments in Balashikha Park are presented for every taste and budget. From modest one-room apartments to spacious four-room ones. The smallest in area is 36 square meters. The largest area reaches 141 m 2 . All apartments are equipped with large balconies or balconies. The windows are double glazed with wooden frames.

The cost of one square meter of housing in the complex "Balashikha-park" starts from 60 thousand rubles. It is not difficult to calculate that a two-room apartment of 60 square meters can be purchased for about 3,600,000 rubles.

Some disadvantages of the microdistrict

Unfortunately, it cannot be said that the Balashikha-Park residential complex and life in it have the same advantages. Perfectly chosen place for building, convenient transport accessibility and developed infrastructure - all these are undoubted advantages. But there are also downsides.

The construction of the microdistrict was begun in the 2000s. At that time, the land plot was an open space that crossed the current high-voltage power line. Residential buildings, infrastructure facilities were built in the immediate vicinity of it, thereby violating the norms of the current legislation. Currently, the line is a serious danger, exerting a strong electromagnetic effect and threatening the life and health of people in the event of wire breakage or their fall.
