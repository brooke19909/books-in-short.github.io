For strength, they were covered with lime mixed with brick dust. It is for this reason that all the people who saw the fortress from afar thought it was bright white. But unfortunately, over the years, the whiteness has gone, and the walls have become darker. Only the sonorous name remains.





Medzhibozh Castle: history, location

The first records of this castle appeared at the beginning of the formation of Kievan Rus. Due to the fact that the city of Medzhibozh was geographically located very convenient, local merchants could conduct active trade with Poland, Kiev and Lviv. In this castle, very often brave conquerors rested. After long hikes, they gained strength here, rested and replenished supplies of food and water.

To protect the region from the regular invasions of the Turks and the constant attacks of the Tatars, it was decided to build an outpost of wood. At the beginning of 1255, the Medzhibozh Castle, Medzhibozh-grad and other surrounding villages completely destroy the Tatars. Subsequently, for a hundred years they dominated these lands. Only with the onset of 1362 did the Great Olger manage to return the lands and destroy the conquerors. He returned the legal lands to both the Lutsk and Lithuanian principalities.

New fortress

In mid-1507, it was decided to re-establish the defenses. But this time around the perimeter erected high and massive walls of stone. They acted as fencing and protection. Also, the walls were excellent positions for archers who could see from above all approaching enemies. This allowed this fortress to become the most massive. It was almost impossible to attack and capture.





Voivode Sinyavsky

In the middle of 1540, the rights to own the castle passed to the tycoons, influential at that time, and the noble governors Sinyavsky. This was made possible thanks to their many services to the Polish Empire. It is Sinyavsky who decide to build additional towers in all corners of the defensive wall. This is done in order to achieve maximum security and power in front of the advancing enemy.

So, the idea came to build a large and beautiful Medzhibozh castle in the very center of the defensive shelter. The description of those times says that the governors do not forget about religious principles. Not far from the castle, builders erect a chapel of impressive size. Everyone could pray for their sins before the Lord and ask for his blessings before a responsible battle.

After two centuries, the city receives the Magdeburg Law, which allows you to fully conduct and develop trade. But in the middle of 1640, this castle with a defensive fortress became the immediate shelter of Bogdan Khmelnitsky himself and his large detachment of brave Cossacks. Indeed, it was in those years that they took an unbearable part in the Polish-Ukrainian war.

The decline of the fortress

In 1730, the last rightful owner of the castle, who was from the Sinyavsky clan, dies. Thus, the Czartoryski became full owners. But, unfortunately, the new owners do not care about security at all. Therefore, the former defensive shelter becomes a full-fledged castle, which is decorated with numerous decorations.
