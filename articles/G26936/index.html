ASUS Fonepad 8 tablet: review, description, specifications and reviews

Asus is known for its high-quality yet inexpensive tablet computers. They combine functionality, efficiency, affordable price and other positive qualities, due to which the equipment from this manufacturer has gained such wide popularity.

The object of today's review will be one of the company's products - the Asus Fonepad 8 gadget. This is a fairly sold device, presented in two versions. Each of them is distinguished by its short index, consisting of numbers and letters, however, in terms of technical parameters and the appearance of the device is identical. In the review, we will simultaneously describe both the Asus Fonepad 8 FE380CXG and the FE380CG.

Gadget Positioning and Cost

The tablet was released in 2014, and since then, of course, it can already be considered obsolete. At least, in the line of devices from Asus it is not represented in all stores - ZenPad models replaced it. And where sales still continue, the device costs about 15-16 thousand rubles (about $ 250). Thus, we are talking about a budget tablet from a well-known manufacturer, equipped at a fairly strong level.





Read more about the configuration and characteristics that the Asus Fonepad 8 has, read on. But, of course, one should take into account what niche (in the price segment) in the market was given to manufacturers for this device.

So, with packaging from Asus, everything is very simple. The device is located on top, and on the lower row, directly below it, there is a section with a charging adapter, consisting of a cord and an adapter for an outlet. Appearance

The second indicator is the design itself. If you believe the technical description, the model can be found in white, black, blue, red and golden colors. Obviously, such a set makes it possible to choose a device to your liking.

Attractive in the front of the tablet are the slim bezels around the display. By their area they occupy only about 17% of the entire device. It is also worth noting the small thickness of the entire tablet - about 8.9 millimeters.





The material from which the Asus Fonepad 8 tablet is made is no different - it's plastic that has a fine texture. Thanks to her, the device lies comfortably in the hand, which is a pleasure. Navigation keys are traditionally located on the right side surface, while on the left there is a plug under which the SIM cards are hidden (there are two of them in Fonepad 8) and a slot for installing a memory card.

A charging port is installed at the top of the gadget - next to it is a connector for 3.5 mm headphones. This arrangement of all systems allows you to handle the device in a way convenient for the owner - to hold it in horizontal or vertical orientation, with one or two hands. Also, while we tested our Asus Fonepad 8 FE380CG, a reduced weight of the device was noticed (328 grams are mentioned in the specifications).

Display

The device has an 8-inch tablet with a resolution of 800 by 1280 pixels. It is based, of course, on the IPS technology common among mobile devices, which makes the image on it bright and clear enough to work in any conditions. The picture quality is average - the tablet has a density of 189 pixels per inch, which makes the graininess noticeable when watching HD video. In sunlight, you will have to set the maximum brightness for comfortable operation.

Also, as described in the specifications of Asus Fonepad 8, the tablet screen has a special oleophobic coating, which significantly reduces the number of fingerprints during use. This is convenient because it‚Äôs really hard to work on gadgets without one.

Battery

An important indicator in the operation of any mobile device is the battery. It determines how long the gadget can function without additional charge.





Asus Fonepad 8 (3G) is quite autonomous. In technical parameters, a battery with a capacity of 3950 mAh is declared, which is an average for an 8-inch tablet. If you use the device at least 1-2 hours a day (in active mode), it will last for 3 days. Reviews indicate that in standby mode the device is able to use up to 10% charge per night. In general, there are no complaints about autonomy, since the compact dimensions of the tablet and the optimized economical charge consumption make it possible to speak of such a low energy consumption.

The tablet provides for the use of 2 SIM cards - we already reported about this above. In addition to GSM, the Asus Fonepad 8 FE380CG (16GB) supports Bluetooth (for transferring files to other devices and receiving them), GPS (navigation using a satellite signal). Multimedia





Video and audio can be played using a standard player from Asus, which has enough settings for better display.
