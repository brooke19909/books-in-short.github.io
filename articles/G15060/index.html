What is a poker set?

Let's get it right. A set in poker or thrips are alternative names for such a combination as a triple. That is, if a player has collected 3 cards of the same value, he already agrees to usually participate in the drawing of the bank.

The remaining cards are not significant at the same time. But sometimes these "extra" single cards decide the fate of the game. They can play the role of kickers.

The role of kickers

Cards that are not capable of creating any combination, but deciding the course of the game in controversial situations, are called kickers. If you decide to play with a bad three, it is better to have good kickers in stock - a king or an ace.

When 2 players collect the same set in poker, the highest card of the two then determines the winner. If a situation arises when the same set is set in poker by two players, and the kickers are also equal, then the bank is divided into two participants. But this almost never happens in practice.

If you have thrips on hand and you open on the flop, then the probability of winning by kicker is 30 to 70. Each player must take these probabilities into account, and the decision must be made quickly.
