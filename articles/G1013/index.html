Depth of the Mariana Trench



For a person, everything unexplored has always been of great interest. And the depths of the sea keep so many secrets that more than one generation of scientists will be provided with work.

But there are points on the map that are not just covered with a veil of secrecy, but are the main theme of mystical stories.

One of these places - the Mariana Trench or the gutter - is a typical relief element of the continental-ocean transitional zones. In such places, there is a lowering of the ocean floor, which in shape is a narrow long hollow. The deepest gutters are the Pacific.

The Mariana Islands gave the name to one of the deep-sea ocean trenches with a length of two and a half thousand kilometers. It differs by a flat bottom, the width of which is 1-5 kilometers, with steep slopes of a V-shaped. The maximum depth of the Mariana Trench is approximately 11 kilometers. This is the deepest point of the entire oceans. It is more likely an abyss or an abyss than a hollow.

What else is known to man about this mysterious place? The exploration of the Mariana Trench began in the 19th century, when the Challenger ship with the members of the English expedition on board set off to measure the Pacific depths.
