Prague in November: reviews of tourists, tours, weather

The capital of the Czech Republic, the ancient city of Prague, is located in the geographical center of Europe. Due to its unique position at the intersection of cultural and trade routes, over its centuries-old history, it has become a true treasury of European civilization. Modern Prague - one of the centers of world tourism - is of undying interest.





Dress for the weather

The Czech Republic lies in the zone of temperate continental climate of Central Europe. The high tourist season lasts from December to October and weakens by November. The weather in the Czech Republic in November is unstable. However, many are not stopped by the windy and rainy weather in Prague in November: traveler reviews indicate the benefits of visiting the city during the off-season. At this time, crowds are thinning, prices in hotels, restaurants and shops are falling, sometimes by 50%. Experienced tourists advise in late autumn to dress in layers, stock up on waterproof clothing, shoes and not to forget about the umbrella.

Reviews of tourists who know a lot about time travel recommend starting their acquaintance with the city in the Hradcany district, the center of which is Prague Castle.





Residence of kings and presidents

This fortress was founded in the 9th century on the left high bank of the Vltava River and for centuries has been the residence of the rulers of the country - from kings to presidents. The first courtyard of the City is occupied by Theresian Palace - an example of Viennese classics of the 18th century. The second compound is decorated with a fountain. Here are the Chapel of the Holy Cross, which holds the royal regalia, and the art gallery, which exhibited paintings by great masters. In the third courtyard of the City stands one of the most famous buildings in Europe - the Gothic St. Vitus Cathedral. The beginning of its construction dates back to the XIV century, then it was completed for centuries, reorganized and was completed only in 1929.

The charm of the Middle Ages

Crossing the other side of the Vltava along the famous Charles Bridge, built in the middle of the XIV by order of Emperor Charles IV, travelers find themselves in Stare Mesto - the Old Town, where medieval Prague appears before their eyes.





Reviews of tourists who visited here and managed to immerse themselves in the charm of antiquity warn that in the maze of these winding narrow streets it is easy to get lost. Gothic buildings here are adjacent to the mansions of the Renaissance and Baroque, the lower floors are occupied by art galleries, souvenir shops, small restaurants and cafes. Center Stare Mesto - Old Town Square. It is surrounded by medieval buildings - residential buildings, the Cathedral of the Virgin in front of Tyn and the town hall building, decorated with a magnificent example of engineering art of the Middle Ages - chimes of Eagle.

Jewish quarter

In the north of Stare Mesta is the Josefov district - the Jewish quarter, a medieval ghetto where Jews were ordered to settle. This is a real open-air museum, there are 5 medieval synagogues and an old Jewish cemetery with 12 thousand tombstones. Burials on the graveyard form 12 layers.





Continuing our acquaintance with the city

From the Old Town, bypassing the Old Tower Square, you can go to Wenceslas Square - a vibrant tourist and commercial center.





On the right bank of the river there is another historical center - the ancient Vysehrad fortress, built in the X century, and in the middle of the XIV - rebuilt by Charles IV. In this place is the National Cemetery-Memorial of prominent figures of the country's national culture.

City of museums and music

In the Czech capital, in any weather, it is never boring: Prague offers its guests the widest opportunities to join a rich cultural life. In November - the reviews of guests and residents of Prague coincide in this - the best time comes to visit numerous Prague museums, art galleries, and concerts. In addition to the famous museums and galleries of Prague Castle, the National Gallery, the National Museum, the city has many original exhibits - the Museum of Torture, the Franz Kafka Museum, the Museum of Sex Machines, the Museum of Communism. Young guests of Prague will enjoy visiting the Planetarium, the Toy Museum, the Museum of Legends and Ghosts.





Organ music lovers can go to any church during the service and listen to the works of great composers. Classical music in the Czech Republic is very popular, and in November, when the tourist flow drops, the guest has a chance to get into prestigious concert halls - the Public House, Rudolfinum, the Mirror Chapel in Clementinum and others.
