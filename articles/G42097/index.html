Madeira Islands, Portugal: description, tips for tourists before visiting, photos

Madeira is an archipelago located in the north of the Atlantic Ocean. It consists of two inhabited, several uninhabited islands and rocks. It is an autonomous region of Portugal with the capital in the city of Funchal. The main island of the archipelago is Madeira. This place is considered one of the best for a relaxing beach holiday.

Where is?

The Madeira Islands are located 500 kilometers west of the African continent and 1000 kilometers southwest of Portugal.

Description

Madeira is an archipelago of volcanic origin. The relief of the islands is mostly mountainous. They, in fact, are the tops of underwater rocks. The most flat is Porto Santo. It is the second largest island of the archipelago. On its coast stretch of sandy beaches with a length of nine kilometers.

Climate

The archipelago is located in the subtropic zone and has a rather mild climate. In summer, daytime temperatures reach 21-23 degrees above zero, and in winter - 15-17 degrees heat. Water does not cool below 17 degrees, so Madeira is a year-round resort. Most precipitation occurs in autumn and winter.
