History of the city





The activities of A.V. Suvorov in the Kuban are inextricably linked with the history of the foundation of a settlement called the city of Kropotkin (Krasnodar Territory). It was on his instructions that here, on the banks of the river, the first Cossack guard post was established, initially fortified by a small redoubt.

The settlement of the adjacent territory has been active since 1778, when the Romanovsky post was established.

On June 18, 1874, the first train was sent from a well-equipped railway station.

And finally, in 1894, the first census was conducted in the Romanovsky farm, according to which, there were already 959 yards and 8147 inhabitants in this territory.

However, the mass resettlement here, of course, did not end there, and in 1905 there were already 1,619 yards and about 20 thousand people.

On February 4, 1921, the Romanovsky farm was renamed the city of Kropotkin. The Krasnodar Territory was replenished with another territorial unit, which received its name in honor of the then famous scientist, geographer and geologist, anarchist theorist P. A. Kropotkin.

City symbols





Not so long ago, on June 30, 2005, the local emblem was approved. It is based on the glorious history of the origin of the village.

The purple emblem depicts Cossack golden tiled spears with tassels and a sentinel tower. Color symbolizes fame, dignity and nobility. It was also once used in the form of Don Cossacks, who were the first inhabitants and defenders of these places.

Between the gold spears, two green stripes are distinguished, also crossed among themselves. They symbolize the intersection of roads, because the city of Kropotkin (Krasnodar Territory), as mentioned above, is a large transport hub where many railway lines and motorways intersect.

A flag was developed on the basis of the coat of arms, which was approved on November 24, 2005. The banner is purple with a slanting green cross below, crowned with four golden spears with tassels, and a sentinel tower of the same color at the top.

Local Attractions





Kropotkin (Krasnodar Territory), a photo of which can be found in guidebooks in Russia, is one of the most picturesque, comfortable and well-known cities in the Kuban.

It is rich in its distinctive history and has many different archaeological sites. Both on the territory of the city itself and beyond, 16 mounds of the Bronze Age and the Middle Ages, as well as ancient Meot fortifications, have survived to this day.

In addition, it should be noted that one of the main architectural attractions of this settlement is the railway station, which was built back in 1903.

In particular, the city of Kropotkin (the Krasnodar Territory as a whole) is famous for its Holy Intercession Cathedral, also known as the Church of the Intercession of the Virgin. It was built in 1913.

Transport system





It should be noted that today the city of Kropotkin has an economically advantageous geopolitical location. It is the administrative center of several settlements and nearby territories, as well as a major transport hub where roads and rail lines converge.

There are four fairly popular railway lines. At the station, all passing trains stopping and changing locomotives, traveling further distances.

In addition, there are routes of regional and federal significance (M29, P217, P251)

On the M29 "Caucasus" highway, the bus station ("Kazan Mountain") serves intercity destinations. Suburban is mainly served by the bus station ("Central Market").

Buses, shuttle transport and taxis run around the city.
