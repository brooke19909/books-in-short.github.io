Panorama is one of the main attractions of the city.

What you need to see in Sevastopol is the main attraction that describes the military affiliation of the city. The canvas shows heroism during the war, it is dedicated to the period of defense of the city. And Sevastopol has such beauty. Panorama is a famous monument, to which the main alley of the Historical Boulevard leads. The author of the monument is Franz Alekseevich Roubaud (the opening took place on 05/14/1905). This is a building of unique architecture, which has no analogues in the world, decorated with lawns and a beautiful multi-level fountain; inside it is a canvas with the image of the Malakhov Kurgan.

During the Second World War, the canvas and the building were partially destroyed, but skillfully restored by Soviet artists and builders, they did not lose their value and beauty. On the building itself, you can see the portraits of the great admirals, the defenders of the city. All the faces and limits of admiration at the sight of this are simply erased, you can stand silently for hours and hours and be touched by beauty.





Monument to the Scuttled Ships

There is something else that every tourist needs to see in Sevastopol. This is the magnificence and grandeur of the monument to the sunken ships. The entire pavement leading to this monument is lined with stone, and the approach to it is washed by raging waves. It is located 20 meters from the coast, recreated as an artificial rock in the form of a pedestal with a column for a bronze eagle towering on it. On the contrary, on the wall, or rather in the wall, there are two black admiral anchors in chains, dedicated to the armed uprising of the Black Sea Fleet in 1905, led by Lieutenant Schmidt.
