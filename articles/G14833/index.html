Florida, Orlando: city attractions, photos, tips for tourists

The city of Orlando (Florida) is located between the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico and is the warmest area in the country. This is one of many reasons why it is considered a fairly popular resort in the United States. Its entire coastal strip is occupied by beaches, hotels, parks and recreation areas. There is also a well-developed tourist infrastructure, and there are many attractions that make this city very attractive for travelers around the world.

Description

The sunniest state of America is Florida. Orlando is located in its center with a population of 2,976,000 people, making it one of the largest megacities in the southern United States. It was named after the heroic soldier Orlando Reeves, who died during the War of Independence.





This resort got its worldwide fame thanks to the largest thematic attractions located nearby and various amusement parks: Disney World, Universal Orlando Resort and many others. In addition, in this metropolis there is almost always a bright sun shining, as well as a huge number of restaurants, museums, supermarkets, chic hotels and many interesting places with beautiful nature.

Therefore, by far the most popular tourist destination in America is the state of Florida. Orlando is visited annually by more than 52 million vacationers. Such an influx of visitors allows the region to actively develop.

Climatic conditions

This state may have different weather conditions. Florida (Orlando) is influenced by the subtropics, so the year here is divided into two seasons. In early June, a hot and rainy period begins, which lasts until about October. Heavy rainfall in Orlando at this time of the year, and air temperature rarely even drops below +22 degrees Celsius even in the dark . In the daytime, the thermometer can go up to +38 , and the increased humidity in the street creates a feeling of even greater heat.

The second season in Florida begins in late September and lasts until May. During this period, mild, pleasant and sunny weather is observed in Orlando. Humidity is low, and the average temperature during the day is 22 degrees Celsius. At night in winter, the thermometer can drop to +10 degrees.

Famous expositions of the metropolis

In addition to all kinds of entertainment and parks, there is also something to see in Orlando (Florida). The attractions of this city, attracting millions of tourists, are museums. One of them is the famous Ripley's Believe It or Not, which is able to give its visitors the opportunity to see unimaginable spectacles with their own eyes. In its exhibition halls are collected all kinds of interesting exhibits from all over the world. Here, to my great surprise, you can find the skeleton of a mermaid, the skulls of cannibals, the sculpture of Marilyn Monroe, made of banknotes, and much more.

One of the most famous science museums in the world is Orlando Science Center, located in the city of Orlando (Florida). Photos taken in his galleries show that here you can see a set of exciting interactive projects, as well as learn everything about the world and replenish your astronomical knowledge.

The metropolis also houses a museum dedicated to American art and features a large number of traveling exhibitions.





Restaurants and hotel complexes

This resort has beautiful and comfortable hotels. Orlando (Florida) can offer apartments with various amenities for every taste. For example, a room in a two-star hotel starts at $ 60 per night, and in a five-star hotel from 300.

You can eat in this metropolis, as in many other states of the USA, in places where fast food is prepared. At first, it might seem that there is nothing else besides the McDonald's. But this is only at first glance, since Orlando has a large selection of restaurants with European cuisine, offering all kinds of dishes.

Fans of meat dishes are advised to head to Charley's Steak House. Those who prefer quiet family dinners will surely enjoy the Citrus Restaurant, inspiring with its cozy atmosphere and excellent service. Also, a large number of institutions of this type are located in the central part of the city and on the shores of Lake Eola.





Reviews and travel tips

Travelers who visited this American resort speak of it as the world capital of children's entertainment. From the first minutes of your stay at the airport of this city, you might think that you find yourself in a certain fairy tale.

Life in Orlando (Florida) is much cheaper than, for example, in Chicago or New York. But many experienced tourists advise those who are going on a trip not to forget that in this state a tax of 11% is included for all services and products. Since it is not registered in any price in this city, you should add it to the value of a particular thing in advance in your mind.

In general, all tourists who have been to Orlando take home a lot of positive emotions and indescribable impressions.
