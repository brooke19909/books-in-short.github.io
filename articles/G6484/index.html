Brooklyn Botanic Garden - Concrete Jungle Paradise Corner

In order to get acquainted with the culture of different parts of the world, residents and guests of New York do not need to travel. The Brooklyn Botanical Garden has incorporated the best features of many art movements. The photo, address and features of this park can be found in the material.

General information

The history of the square began long before its opening. The territory under green spaces was allocated in 1897. Then it was a swampy, impassable land. Fenced off 39 acres.





They opened an amazing park in 1910. He got his name from the name of the Brooklyn area in which it is located. Since 1912, Harold Kaparni worked on the garden. For 30 years, he added various sections to the park one after another.

Has its own system of discounts on entry for students and seniors Brooklyn Botanical Garden. Complex Address: Washington Avenue 900, New York. The day off is Monday.

Of course, the square could not have grown so well, if not for the help of specialists. Nearby are laboratories, administrative and scientific centers in which they study plants and improve their living conditions. All this colossal work is performed by not so many people. In total, 165 people work here on an ongoing basis and another 90 seasonally. However, a significant contribution is made by five hundred volunteers.

Tourism without borders

It is beautiful and cozy, regardless of the time of year. In March, white and yellow daffodils bloom. They are replaced by unique sakura. Further you can admire azaleas, tulips and lilacs. Summer is the time for rhododendrons and roses. In the autumn, breathtaking from the gold of bushes and trees. And in winter, guests enjoy a tropical paradise in covered pavilions.

You can get to the complex by taxi. But nearby there is a public transport stop. Borders on the territory of Prospect Park Brooklyn Botanical Garden. New York has a rather confusing subway. But you can get to the place by the underground road. Nearby are the Prospect Park metro stations (lines B, Q) and Eastern Parkway (lines 2 and 3).





This green island can give a lot of positive and good mood. However, those who love picnics in nature will be disappointed. It is strictly forbidden to bring your own food to the park; you also cannot sit on the lawns. But under blooming cherries, they are allowed to lie on the ground. Tourists and guests of the garden eat in the cafe, which are located on the territory. Here are souvenir shops and shops with goods for those who wish to engage in gardening.

Particle of Peerless Asia

Brooklyn Botanical Garden is especially beautiful in spring. Thousands of tourists come here for amazing entertainment, the original name of which is the khans. The art of this business is admiring flowers. Sakura blooms here in spring. About two hundred trees were planted. The collection includes more than 40 types of pink cherry. The flowering period is in April and lasts until mid-May.

Trees appeared here after the First World War. It was a gift to New York from the Japanese authorities. Experts say that cherry is more beautiful only in Asia. The landscape of this part is rich in hills, waterfalls and islets, wooden bridges and stone lanterns.

It should be noted that the site, which was opened by the Brooklyn Botanical Garden, annually broadcasts the process of sakura blossom. Therefore, someone who is unable to come to the United States can enjoy this unique phenomenon through a monitor.





Park with accents

Supporters of literature will also find entertainment to their liking. Another highlight of the garden is Shakespeare's corner. On a separate section of the park, 80 plants were planted, which the attentive reader met in the works of an English writer. There are signs next to flowers and trees. Excerpts from the text mentioning this instance can be read on them.

The Brooklyn Botanic Garden takes care of people with disabilities. There is an aromatic corner. Most plants in this zone are very fragrant. Leaves and flowers can not only be sniffed, but also rubbed in the hands. This is especially important for blind people who are unable to appreciate the various colors of nature. There are braille signs here . Walkways and walkways are wheelchair accessible. There are ramps on the stairs. You can wash your hands in the fountains without getting up from your chair.





For every taste

Another area the complex is proud of is the Family Plant Collection. Here, flowers, bushes and trees are arranged in order of evolution. Therefore, each guest can independently track the development and change of certain species.

Those who visit the Brooklyn Botanical Garden should definitely go to the rose garden. The collection of beautiful flowers consists of thousands of copies. The peak of flowering occurs in the month of June. Roses here abound in flowerbeds, cascade down stairs and braid into gratings.

The next visiting card is the Bonsai Museum. More than 350 dwarf trees grow here. But, in addition to Japanese culture, the complex introduces the Italian and English direction of garden architecture.

At the entrance to the institution, visitors are offered a free card. Those who are for the first time in the park should definitely use it, because without it you can get lost.

It occupies one of the first places in the list of attractions of the Brooklyn Botanical Garden. New York is proud of this square.

Place for thought

The total number of plants that have been planted in this garden is over 12,000. All this adorns 20 different cultural sectors. Every year, an unusual park is visited by more than 700,000 people from different parts of the world. Interestingly, most of the funds are donations. Philanthropists not only give money for the arrangement of the park, but also offer themes.





Not only entertaining, but also cognitive, is the Brooklyn Botanical Garden. It is intended to interest a person in more detail with one or another cultural direction. In addition to demonstrating various exotic plants, the administration holds scientific conferences, research experiments, and social programs. However, garden guests come here for fresh air filled with the aroma of flowers, peace and beauty.
