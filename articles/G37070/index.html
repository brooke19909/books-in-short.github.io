Sights of Crimea. Aivazovsky park: photos and reviews of tourists

A small resort village of the urban type Partenit is located a bit east of the slope of the legendary Ayu-Dag mountain. Sometimes it is called Bear Mountain. The unusual name of the village comes from the Greek word "parthenos", which translates as "virgin". In these places is a magnificent modern park Aivazovsky. It is located on the territory of the Aivazovskoye sanatorium complex, on the slopes of the Kuchuk-Lambada Bay amphitheater. The park is constantly evolving, combining a variety of styles and trends of the world landscape art.

Partenit Village

This village occupies a small territory near Ayu-Dag Mountain. It is part of the city district of Alushta. Located at a distance of 59 km from Simferopol. The population is about 9 thousand people. On this land there is a mountain that is surrounded by numerous legends and a magnificent park. There were A. Griboedov, A. Pushkin, I. Aivazovsky.



Despite the long history, the resort is quite a young village - it is no more than 30 years old.

Park history

Aivazovsky Park (Crimea) has its history since the mid 60-ies of the last century. Previously, vineyards were broken down in sunlit territory. Even at that time the place was called paradise. But after the transformation of the park, which took place in 2003, its popularity has increased significantly. And today this amazing corner of the Crimea continues to develop.



Description of the park

Today, many tourists visit the village of Partenit. Aivazovsky Park must be included in their excursion program. This magnificent complex does not have a single compositional space; it is divided into several separate zones.

On the slopes of the mountains are large areas planted with plants, a decorative garden with a mountain terrace (rockery). You can go down the slopes to the coast along the paths - special walking paths. On the Black Sea is a boat station, promenade and coastal recreation area.

The natural flow of water was skillfully turned into a cascading mountain stream. Artificial thresholds are created here. Places that are prone to landslides are seeded with rocks of special plants that block this process.

Among tropical and subtropical plants (more than 150 species) you can see Atlas cedars, very rare for the Crimea, lanceolate cunningamia, large-flowered magnolia, cork oaks. There are a lot of palm trees in the park; you can visit cactus greenhouses.



Terraced Gardens

Experts say that the highlight of the park is the combination of different landscape design directions in one space.

The English garden is an uneven terrain, winding paths, forest and field perennials.

Landscape is characterized by natural nature, large lawns. Here is the arbor and sculpture of the goddess Flora.



The Japanese garden represents eastern philosophy: stone slides, ponds with bridges, small dwarf trees.

The Italian garden is a lot of stones, artificially created reservoirs decorated with fountains, evergreens.

Mexican is distinguished by bright colors, fruit trees, cacti, prickly pears, agaves with large and fleshy leaves. This plot is located in the driest and sunniest place.

The antique garden is decorated with sculptures of the corresponding era, amphora. Here everyone who came to Partenit (Aivazovsky Park) will certainly be interested in a white rotunda brought from Italy. This is a round arbor with a domed roof and columns. It is famous for its acoustics, which amplifies the sound many times.



Terraced gardens bloom from early spring to late autumn. In early July and August, hydrangea bushes, lavender, sage, marjoram, juniper, fotinia and other landscape gardening plants fragrant in the park.

Olive grove

Aivazovsky Park is famous for its main grove - olive. Her age is more than two hundred years. In the distant XIX century, it belonged to M.N. Raevsky, who owned Partenit.

It is the basis of the exposition, which is associated with the colonization of Crimea by the Greeks, because it is no secret that many associate olive with the Mediterranean. There is a version that in ancient times there was a manor of the ancient Greeks at this place. The compositional axis of this corner of the park was a stream crossing a grove.



Neat glades of lime in tones of sun will open to your eyes. This background is diluted with fiery red roses. In the center are olive trees surrounded by pines and cypresses.

Central staircase

Many tourists today are attracted to Crimea. Fans of combining a beach holiday with a guided tour of Alushta. Aivazovsky Park allows you to have a great time after a stay on the beach.

Passing through the main entrance, visitors to the park see the long staircase of Raevsky. At different levels there are large and small observation platforms with benches for relaxation. Do not rush to go down: appreciate the landscapes that open to the eye, and the bronze sculptures of the Greek gods. Going down, look to the left. Here is a sculpture of Count Raevsky.



Recently, the stairs were reconstructed, and now work is underway on the observation deck. While the fountain installed here still does not work, there are no fences, but all these shortcomings compensate for the amazing views of the Crimea.

Small architectural forms

Aivazovsky Park has a special layout. Here, special attention is given to landscape gardening sculpture. The nature of the peninsula is complemented by "interspersed" sculptural ensembles, statues. There are also functional sculptures - flower vases. Original benches emphasize the unusual design.

By the originality and beauty of numerous arbors and statues, man-made lakes and streams, Aivazovsky Park is not inferior to its eminent fellow - the famous Nikitsky Botanical Garden. Sculptures attract the eye with flowing and saturated colors.

Here are the golden horses of the ex-president of Ukraine, Kuchma, whom he presented to the park.





And over there imposingly located in the shadow of the park trees Nikita Khrushchev. He peacefully enjoys the healing Crimean air. I wonder if he thought what his royal gift would turn out for many.



Then, in an open area, you will see a statue of the Greek sea lord. Poseidon with a rather formidable look inspects his possessions. The composition is adorned with its presence by the beautiful goddess of the seas Amphitrite. We can say that the heroes of Greek myths are very comfortable in the park.

Wellness complex

For many years, the Aivazovskoye recreation complex has been operating in the Partenit village, of which the Aivazovsky park is a part. It consists of three buildings. Today, two are working, one building is closed for reconstruction.

The favorable microclimate of the park helps to cope with many diseases, including respiratory and cardiovascular systems.



Cost of visiting the park

If you decide to visit Aivazovsky Park, prices will not be a serious obstacle for you. A ticket for an adult visitor costs 200 rubles, for a child - 100 rubles. Entrance fee includes access to the park and beach area. Three hours later, free excursions take place in the park. You can visit Aivazovsky Park daily. Opening hours - from 9.00 to 18.00 hours. Aivazovsky park: how to get there?

Those traveling by public transport must come to the capital of Crimea. From Simferopol to Partenit you will be delivered by trolleybus number 52. You can also take a minibus number 122.

From Yalta, you can get to the village from the city bus station by bus number 110.

Motorists can get this way: from the Alushta-Yalta highway, turn along the Partenit sign. You will find yourself at the crossroads of three roads, two of them turn right and one turn left. You don‚Äôt have to turn anywhere. It is necessary to enter the gates of the Aivazovskoye complex. The park is located on its territory.
