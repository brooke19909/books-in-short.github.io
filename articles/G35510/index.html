Rayon and natural. Their differences

In ancient times, silk was considered inaccessible and expensive material. Such fabrics could afford osb of high origin. Silk was worth its weight in gold, they could be paid like money. In those days, the production of fabric was not available to every state. His secrets were kept more than an eye - and that is why he was so valuable. Over time, humanity has learned to produce artificial silk.



Rayon (viscose) is a mixture of fibers that is obtained artificially. For the production of such silk, natural polymers (cellulose) are used. This fabric is the first industrially manufactured chemical fiber. The main type of artificial thread is acetate.

Industrial methods for producing silk:

It is advisable to subject artificial silk not to mechanical washing, but to wash it by hand. You need to dry it by unfolding it or hanging it on the dryer, it is advisable not to hang it on the battery. Artificial silk is easily smoothed and does not require an iron.

Natural silk is a noble material. This fabric is a product of excretion.

silkworm secret mulberry tracks. Caterpillars, eating mulberry leaves, secrete a liquid that turns into a strong thread. An insect weaves its cocoon from this thread. When they are collected, they are placed in a special solution for soaking. At the last stage, the soaked cocoons are unwound and only after that a natural thread can be used to produce silk.

Silk fabric is soft and durable. It does not cause allergies. Artificial silk can be distinguished from natural silk by tactile sensations. Natural silk is very delicate and pleasant to the touch. If you set fire to several threads from the product, then by the smell you can immediately understand what silk is in front of us. The fact is that artificial silk burns and smells of wool, while natural silk melts and smells of burnt paper.
