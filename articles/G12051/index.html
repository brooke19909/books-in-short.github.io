A complex that Moscow can be proud of: Olympiysky Sports Complex. Description and location of the stadium

Many of its magnificent buildings and cultural institutions are famous for the capital of Russia - Moscow. IC "Olympic" can rightfully be considered one of the main attractions of this metropolis. Over the entire history of its existence, various world stars have been sold out in its famous walls. And in 2009, the complex became famous all over the world, becoming the venue for a popular music contest called Eurovision. In one year, more than three million viewers can visit it. Over a similar time period, about two hundred spectacular events take place here.

History

In 1980, Moscow hosted the Olympic Games . Olimpiysky Sports Complex was built on this occasion and opened on July 19 of the same year. To this day, it is considered one of the largest structures in Europe. The engineers who created the wonderful project of the complex were awarded the Lenin Prize.



In the eighties of the last century, football matches of the USSR were held here until other stadiums were built, which were specially intended for this game. A little later, the building was also decided to be used as a music venue. A huge number of foreign and domestic stars managed to take Moscow. SC "Olympic" is exactly the place where their performances took place.

The capacity of Olimpiysky Sports Complex (Moscow) is thirty-five thousand spectators. The central arena of the structure is as large as a standard football field. Unique is that it can be divided into two autonomous parts. Thus, it is possible to conduct various events at the same time.

A lot of interesting competitions and concerts are held at the Olimpiysky Sports Complex (Moscow). The layout of the hall in the building, as in other modern complexes, can vary depending on the type of event. For example, at the championships in gymnastics or other sports, a dance ground is not needed, but during concerts it is set. When buying a ticket, you just need to consider not only proximity to the scene, but also the position of the place in space.



In addition, more than twenty national teams are training here, as well as a book fair. Every winter, the complex has a free ice rink. Everyone knows that many participants in various conferences are hosted by Moscow. Olimpiysky Sports Complex has two press centers and a specially equipped hall for holding such events on its territory. In this building there are also various cafes, restaurants, shops and beauty salons.

Stadium

This part of the structure includes sports halls, a central arena, an ice rink and a training center. The roof in this wing of the building is made of a membrane whose thickness is six millimeters. At the site of the stadium, you can lay a variety of coverings and even turn it into a field of artificial ice. A unique feature of the place is a high partition that completely does not allow sound and divides the stadium arena into two parts. This is what allows you to conduct several events of any type and complexity here at once. For example, exhibitions, festivals, corporate parties, competitions and unique concert programs.





Pool

This part of the structure consists of three bowls. The first of them is a demo. It has a length of fifty meters and has ten tracks. Here, mainly those who have a subscription swim. There are also classes in water aerobics and water polo. The training pool is the same size as the previous one. Here holders of a single or reduced subscription can swim. And the third bowl is a hopping bath having a depth of six meters. Any of the pools can be rented. In addition, there are saunas and massage rooms.





Contact details and how to get?

Not far from the historical center is located SC "Olympic" (Moscow). It is easy to find his address in any directory: Olimpiysky Avenue, building 16. All the necessary information can be obtained by calling this number: +7 (495) 786-33-33.

It can be reached by public transport to the Olympiysky Sports Complex (Moscow). The metro, which is located in close proximity to it, is called "Prospect Mira". Having reached this metro station, you will need to go out and turn right. Then you need to keep your way along the pedestrian lane to the intersection with Shchepkina Street. Next, the structure itself will appear.

Interesting Facts

It turns out that earlier on the territory of the complex there was a training base, and then the song theater of the famous and popular Russian singer Alla Pugacheva. The stadium of this grandiose building is still considered the largest in Europe. In the "Olympic" there are various sports sections and circles, which are visited daily by more than six thousand people. In addition, approximately 50,000 residents of the capital train and engage in recreational activities here. This building also houses one of the largest book markets in the whole country.



The sports complex undoubtedly impresses with its huge scale, beauty and unsurpassed design. It is a favorite venue for various events. The building has all the necessary amenities for both spectators and participants in competitions, concerts, and for the press covering these events.
