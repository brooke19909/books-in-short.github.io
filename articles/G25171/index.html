Hotel "Dinler" (Alanya, Turkey): photos and reviews of tourists

Since Turkey still leads in the list of popularity among tourists, in our article we want to talk about one of its hotels. The Dinler complex (Turkey) was built in 1997. Its total area is 8050 square meters. In 2012, the hotel was completely renovated.

A bit about the hotel ...

Hotel Dinler (Alanya) is one hundred and forty kilometers from Antalya Airport and fifteen kilometers from the center of Alanya, which has long been known as a resort.

Alanya is a small beautiful town located on a rocky peninsula, at the very foot of the Toros Mountains, in the midst of lemon and orange gardens. The resort is known for its very special climatic conditions. Alanya is also famous for its beautiful beaches and majestic mountain fortifications of the Seljuk era (18th century). The old town has characteristic narrow streets and colorful shops, it has countless fish restaurants. And along the coast lined a lot of courtyards for tea.



Hotel Dinler (Alanya) is a six-story building located at: Kayseri cad. PR 51, 50400 Urgup / Nevsehir.

Number of rooms

Hotel "Dinler" (Turkey) offers its guests 215 comfortable rooms. All are equipped with air conditioning, bathrooms, hairdryers, televisions with satellite channels, telephones and minibars (for a fee). Apartments are cleaned daily.

All rooms are divided into: standards, family two-room rooms, two-room suits.

Dining at the hotel

Hotel Dinler (Alanya) operates on an all-inclusive basis. There are two restaurants and five bars on site. I would like to note that the food is organized at a good level. Vacationers are served many olives, herbs, berries and fruits, and once a week you can taste delicious baklava. A local chef cooks turkey and chicken perfectly. Sometimes served beef, fish. As a side dish, guests are offered Turkish manti, mashed potatoes, spaghetti, rice.

The hotel menu has a wide variety of desserts. But national Turkish dishes are served a little, more emphasis is placed on European, Italian cuisine. This, in general, is understandable, since not everyone can taste oriental food. Near the complex there is a supermarket where you can buy some of the products, although this is completely pointless, since it is simply impossible to be hungry at the hotel. Even if you skipped breakfast, dinner or lunch, you can have a bite to eat in one of the bars.



Breakfast includes delicious croissants. On the beach from 4 p.m. to 5 p.m., all vacationers are treated to watermelons and hamburgers. And at 17 o'clock near the pool, pasta is fried in oil with a wide variety of sauces. At the same time, they are very interestingly turned over in a pan with a fire of oil. It all looks quite impressive, so there is a desire to certainly try the dish, even if you are not at all hungry.

Hotel "Dinler" (Alanya) has swimming pools on its territory. One of them is open, and the other is indoor. There is also a children's area with special slides.

The complex has a fairly developed infrastructure, here you can find everything you need for a good rest. The hotel has free parking for cars, in addition, there is a car rental service for guests. Tourists can use the laundry and dry cleaning, as well as visit the beauty salon, hairdresser.

Excursions

Let's talk about what the Hotel Dinler (Turkey) has to offer leisure travelers. Tours prepared by local guides are very interesting and informative. Of course, most tourists come here just for the sake of the sea. But local attractions are worthy of attention. Moreover, this will help to know a little country, its people and traditions. After all, the bright lights of hotels on the coast - this is far from the whole of Turkey. According to experienced tourists, you definitely need to have a hundred dollars per person on excursions. With such an amount, you can see and learn a lot of new and interesting things.

Hotel Dinler (Turkey) is located at some distance from Alanya, so the city can be reached by bus.

The central attraction of the town is the fortress. It is perfectly preserved and to this day appears before the guests of the country in all its glory. The fortress of Alanya differs from other similar structures of the country in its impressive size. At one time, on its territory was a mint, a monastery, a mosque, the winter palace of the Sultan, a bathhouse and much more. The fortress was built in the thirteenth century. Not far from it there is another historical monument - a lighthouse erected in 1880.

In the city you can visit the archaeological museum located near the central park. The exposition of the museum is very large. It contains historical monuments of Byzantium, Ancient Rome, Ancient Greece.



In the port of Alanya is the famous tower of Kyzyl-Kul - one of the main historical places of the city. A large number of tourists visit it annually. For many years, the tower protected the inhabitants of the city from enemies. Gradually, she became a symbol of Alanya, she is even depicted on the flag of the city.

Dim tea is a sight that not all experienced tourists know about. This is the valley of a mountain river, which locals love to visit. On its banks are many cafes, restaurants and leisure complexes. During the season, entertainment runs until night. Free buses run from the center of Alanya.

Another attraction is the Suleymaniye Mosque, located near the Byzantine fortress. Its feature is excellent acoustics. The builders achieved this effect thanks to a little trick, hanging fifteen balls under the dome.

Entertainment

In Alanya there is entertainment for every taste that will be interesting not only for adults but also for children. For families, the Sealanya Park is perfect. It is located near the city. You can walk in the park all day to see everything, it is worth taking advantage of the Slow River attraction. In addition, there is a dolphinarium, performances which take place several times a day. And in the very center of the park there is a large lake. Tourists are invited to wear costumes, sink to its very bottom and see the underwater world.

No less interesting are the caves of Alanya. There are many grottoes and caves on the Mediterranean coast. The most interesting for tourists are Dalmatash Magarasy, Karain Magarasy and Dim Magarasy. In them you can see stalactites and stalagmites, whose age exceeds fifteen thousand years.



Tourists love visiting the Tersane shipyard. In the evening, it is very beautifully illuminated by lights. The shipyard functioned until 1361. And now it is used as a pier and for walks.

Shopping

Alanya is famous for silks. The special local fabric of the Turks is called "buryumdzhuk". She is very pleasant to wear during the summer heat. At the bazaars, it is sold quite cheaply, in addition, there is a significant selection of scarves.
