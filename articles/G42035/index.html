We power up the amplifier

Newcomers who first encounter car sound are amazed to the core by the thickness of wires, which is rarely seen in everyday life (well, unless you are used to using welding machines or something like that in this case, then this parameter will not surprise you).

Contrary to common misconceptions, amplifiers consume decent currents from the on-board network. So, a four-channel device with 50 watts per channel will consume almost 17 A. Not bad, is it? Of course, that for the power supply will have to use wires with a really large cross-section. So before you connect the amplifier in the car, visit specialized stores.

Classic version

Let's analyze the classic connection diagram. In this case, the wiring directly clings to the car battery. It is no secret that in most cases the battery is located under the hood, while the amplifier is located inside the car. Accordingly, the positive cable goes through almost the entire interior of the car. And therefore it is extremely important to protect the wiring with a high-quality fuse, and it should be located no further than 30 cm from the battery.

So before you connect the amplifier in the car, visit the electronics store and buy a normal fuse.

A bit about isolation

In addition, do not forget to additionally insulate the cable, since from 17 A jokes are bad. Particularly carefully, protect the space between the engine compartment and the passenger compartment, as the cable is often frayed there. As a rule, there is a rubber gasket, but there are exceptions. If so, it does not hurt to use plastic sleeves made of durable and fairly thick plastic. Responsibly approach this issue, since it is clearly not worth it to allow exposed frayed cable to hit the body metal!



The wire through the cabin also needs to be pulled, preventing it from falling under its moving parts. There were times when people threw a cable under the seat rails. What consequences this can lead to, you can come up with yourself. So how to connect the amplifier in the car?

Choose a cable

Do not forget about the golden rule: the less connections, the higher the security! The abundance of wires with different cross sections is fraught with high energy losses, and from the point of view of fire safety this does not look the best way.

In addition, the cross section of the negative wire should be equal to that of the positive cable. The current in the network is one, its value is constant. If the wires have a different cross-section, they will overheat, which again is fraught with a fire in the car.

Once again, thin cords are high resistance. In no case do not save on cables that go from the battery to the amplifiers, as we have already talked about the current strength that passes through them.

A bit of physics and mathematics

First, let's check what is the maximum current consumption of the amplifier you use. To do this, look at the fuse (or fuses). Suppose your amplifier can theoretically withstand 100 A. immediately. It is better to place it in the trunk. For a typical car, it is preferable to take at least five meters.

I = 100 A; L = 5 m.

Even under the condition of maximum current use, the voltage drop at the positive end of the wire should not exceed 0.5 V. We recall the simplest school formula for calculating resistance, after which we carry out a simple calculation:

R total = U / I = 0.5 V / 100 A = 0.005 Ohm.

Thus, the cable resistance should not exceed 0, 005 Ohms. Since we plan to use five meters at once, it is necessary to calculate the resistance on a segment in meter:

R meter = R Total / L = 0, 005/5 = 0, 001 Ohms.

As you can see, we need a wire with running resistance not higher than 0, 001 Ohm / m. And now you can take a catalog of any manufacturer, and then choose something suitable.

Cable connection

After you lay the wire in the cabin, you can connect it to the positive terminal of the amplifier. The negative cable is connected directly to the body, and for this you need to use an instance with the same cross section as the plus. In no case do not resort to the help of self-tapping screws! You can only fasten with bolts, and the connection point must be cleaned as high as possible from traces of paint and other contaminants.

The positive battery wire should be connected to the + 12V terminal, and the negative wire to GND (ground - ground, ground). Before connecting the amplifier to the radio, find the remote contact, because it is designed to hook the wire that just comes from it. It is in it that the very 12Vs appear, and therefore the radio tape recorder can control the functions of the amplifier. Most often, this cable is blue (or blue with a white stripe). Connect the cable to control the amplifier

We already said that it is necessary to lay wires to control the amplifier itself. By the way, it is advisable to do this by laying interconnect wire. At one end, it is attached to the amplifier (once again we remind that it is painted in blue or blue-white), and the other to the radio. Very important notes

Differences in connecting different types of amplifiers

If you put a two-channel amplifier, then you need a similar interconnect cable. If four-channel - a pair of double wires, or a special four-channel.



And how to connect the amplifier to the radio, if it has only a couple of linear outputs, and the amplifier itself is four-channel? In this case, special Y-adapters (splitters) can help you. Cunning Chinese have created a device that provides a pair of outputs for each input. Thus, from one stereo pair we get two at once. Conveniently, you will not say anything. Of course, doing this with acoustics of the highest price category is not worth it, since the sound quality is noticeably reduced.

There is another way. All normal models of modern amplifiers (Audison, for example) are equipped with the function of internal parallel distribution of inputs. In a word, in this case you will not need any splitters.

If your radio does not have linear outputs at all, then it can be connected directly to the speakers (high-level signal). Almost all normal devices currently have corresponding terminals (Hi level input). For connection, the most ordinary acoustic wires are used, and it would be better not to mix up the poles.

We connect a single - channel amplifier (it is a monoblock)

Single-channel amplifiers have only one function - connecting to a subwoofer. They have a very decent power, and also have the ability to work under low resistance load. What is it, by the way? In ordinary car speakers, the impedance is only 4 ohms, and all other equipment has much lower characteristics (3 ohms, 2 ohms, 1 ohms). This is called a low-impedance load. All these amplifiers belong to the D-class.

Today they all have a built-in LPF filter that cuts all frequencies above those that you set as maximum for the subwoofer. The scheme for connecting it to disgrace is simple. Subwoofers are connected in parallel or in series, or only one speaker is mounted. In parallel, you can connect even more than two low-frequency speakers at once, but it is important to consider that not all subwoofer models can cope with significantly increased resistance.

Here is the simplest and most common installation diagram. The linear signal from the radio goes to the amplifier, is summed and amplified. The wiring diagram for the control and transmission wires should be found in the instructions from your manufacturer.

Four channels!

Other tips

If you carefully read the article, then you must have repeatedly seen references to fuses. Which models are best used to protect the system?

The head unit can be protected from combustion by a conventional flag fuse. Since it is located directly under the hood, you need to buy a holder in the most moisture-resistant design. But powerful speakers are much more appropriate to protect with ANL elements (they are flat, in the form of tablets). As an alternative to such devices, you can take circuit breakers, which immediately turn off the system in the event of a short circuit or a sharp increase in voltage.
