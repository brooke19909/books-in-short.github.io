The structure of water and its properties

Water is a very complex and poorly studied system. The structure of the water is dynamic. Weak hydrogen bonds combine in chains. In water, associates of molecules easily form, disintegrate, and pass into each other. At the same time, they are exposed to many factors that were previously not taken into account or studied by traditional science at all.

Memory of water

Science has proved that the physical properties of water and the numerous short-term hydrogen bonds of neighboring oxygen and hydrogen atoms in a water molecule form special associate structures (clusters). They are able to perceive, store and transmit all kinds of information.

A cluster is a structural unit of water. It consists of clathrates, the nature of which is determined by the distant Coulomb forces. The water structure stores encoded information about the interactions that took place between water molecules. Thus, water, the structure of which includes many heterogeneous clusters, forms a spatial hierarchical liquid crystal structure that can perceive and store significant amounts of information.
