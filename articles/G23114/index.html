Desna (river) - the largest tributary of the Dnieper

The longest tributary of the Dnieper River, Ukraine‚Äôs largest waterway, is Desna. The river originates in Russia, in the Smolensk region, and flows into the Dnieper just above Kiev. The total length of the Desna is 1130 km.

Location

The Desna River, a map of which will be given below, flows in only four areas. Two of them are Russian: these are the Bryansk and Smolensk regions. In Ukraine, Desna flows in the Kiev region and, to the greatest extent, in Chernihiv.





General characteristics

The depth of the bottom is on average from 2 to 4 meters, but in some places this value can increase to 17. At the mouth of the river, the width of the water reaches 450 meters. The difference in width often occurs due to the fact that the water artery is very ornate, flows through different types of terrain and has a huge number of turns, so sometimes it is not clear whether the big river is in front of us or small.



A related factor is that the flow velocity is different. People who love relaxation on the Desna River choose places where it is more or less calm so that they can swim and relax without incident, which, incidentally, is quite common. The speed of the current is such that the inept swimmer can easily carry to the depths or sweep the wave. The bottom of the Desna is uneven: either you stand waist-deep in the middle of the river, or suddenly under the very shore you cannot reach the bottom.

The statistics of tragic cases, unfortunately, is not so small already, but this, in principle, is no longer associated with the course of the river, but with the fact that people are careless and do not know the basic rules of behavior on the water. From shipping to cleaning issues

It was also noted that regular navigation from Novgorod-Seversky to the estuary and cruising of a bulk carrier upstream are taking place on the river. But the situation is such that every year the Desna (river) becomes smaller. Increasingly, in the vast expanses of water you can only see motor boats or tourist boats. The problem is that, unfortunately, no one is engaged in river cleaning. All sand, silt, garbage, waste is generously dumped both by ordinary citizens, and by the whole enterprises. For the river to be full, clean, full, you need to clean it, but you need to spend money on it. And the state, as usual, does not have budget funds for these expenses.



Where does the water in the Desna come from?

The water of the river is fed to the greatest extent by melting snow. In the spring, usually the Desna is very spilled, and this has been observed for quite a long time. Its waters are flooded with meadows, which make up the lion's share of the territories in its valley. The more snow falls in the winter, the cooler the spring, the more vast and longer it fills these same Desna meadows (river). The photo of the flood at the monastery in Novgorod-Seversky describes in the best possible way what kind of landscape can be expected in the spring.

In addition, it flows through forest strips, and also in its valley there are many bays and lakes. To call the Desna monotonous and uninteresting will not turn anyone's tongue.

What is remarkable can be seen along the river?

There are many ancient cities of Ukraine on the Desna River. For example, Novgorod-Seversky, already mentioned above, is the city of Drevlyans, founded in 989. It is located in the Chernihiv region.

The city is of great historical value: many events related to Kievan Rus took place in it, and even in earlier times. There are also many interesting cultural and historical monuments. But, unfortunately, over the years, Novgorod-Seversky is becoming less and less in terms of population. The economy and industry in the city are not developing, even a powerful tourist base is simply idle unnecessarily, and young people prefer to go to larger cities, where there are at least some prospects.
