Bariloche Argentina: location, attractions, interesting places, photos

Argentina is not only one of the largest, but also the most economically developed countries in Latin America. Her nature is diverse. Many of Argentina's sights are not in cities, but scattered throughout the country. Tourists are attracted by many things, for example, the famous Iguazu waterfalls, which since 2011 have been called one of the natural wonders of the world, delicious vineyards that can be seen in the Mendoza wine region, and, of course, the popular ski resort of San Carlos de Bariloche. Argentina annually hosts more than one hundred thousand fans of this extreme sport. You can ride along the picturesque islands of the Andes from June to October.

The city of San Carlos de Bariloche was founded in the late nineteenth century by immigrants from Germany. It was originally conceived as an Argentine outpost on the land of hostile Indians. Bariloche was built up as a typical alpine settlement, so many of the buildings here are built of stone in combination with a tree.

Location

The resort of San Carlos de Bariloche is located in the central part of the Patagonian Andes, in the province of Rio Negro. The city is 1,600 kilometers away from Buenos Aires, the capital of Argentina. Bariloche - so shortly called by the locals this resort. About 115 thousand people live in it, but this figure is constantly increasing. The fact is that many Argentines move here from large cities, including the capital.

The snow-covered mountain slopes of Bariloche (Argentina) take tens and even hundreds of thousands of ski lovers all year round. Tourists have a unique opportunity in the summer to move in the real winter in just a few hours from the heat and heat

How to get there

Given the fact that Bariloche in Argentina is considered one of the most popular resort areas, there is a well-developed transport connection. It is most convenient to get to it by plane or by bus from Buenos Aires.

Where to stay

The issue of accommodation here can be solved without difficulty. In Bariloche (Argentina) - the largest tourist center of the country - many magnificent hotels have been built. Here you can book both expensive luxury apartments and rooms in three-star hotels with a more affordable price. Students and young people traveling on a budget prefer to stay in cheap hostels.

Especially popular among tourists are such hotels as LaCascada, Nevada, Acongagua, Edelweiss, etc.

Ski resort

First of all, Bariloche (Argentina) is known for its magnificent ski resort. By the beauty of landscapes and the level of technical equipment, it competes with the best European tracks.

In total, forty-seven excellent tracks with a total length of about seventy kilometers are equipped here. They are located on the slopes of the largest mountains: Otto and Catedral. The difference in elevation here ranges from 1,500 to 2,500 m. Bariloche (Argentina) is a ski resort where the best six-seat lift in all of South America is located. There are all conditions for a good outdoor activity: several ski and snowboard rental points are available, there are training centers for beginners, as well as a snow park with a freestyle area.

Most of the tracks of the Bariloche resort in Argentina have an average level of difficulty, and therefore are excellent for those who ride for a long time, but at the same time unprofessionally. There are also special descents for beginners, people who take their first steps on skis or snowboards. If you wish, you can contact professional instructors.

In Bariloche there are also several "black" tracks, designed for professionals. Here, everyone will find a descent to their taste, depending on the capabilities and level of training.

What to see in Bariloche (Argentina)

The main cultural objects are concentrated in the city, in San Carlos de Bariloche. In this small village, which has a very modest area, there are many places that you must visit. Here you will find the old chapel of San Eduardo, the main cathedral, city hall, etc. Sweet lovers will love the famous Trieberpolma chocolate factory located a few kilometers from the city. During the tour you can not only look at the process of making sweets, but also taste local sweets. Most tourists take away chocolate as a souvenir from here.

Wonderful nature

In Bariloche (Argentina), sights are not only historical buildings, but also miraculous monuments. Here, the local elite rested for many years.

And today musicians, artists, businessmen and writers often come here. Bariloche is one of the most prestigious resorts on the continent, attracting travelers, including its picturesque nature.

Here is a real expanse for lovers of fishing and hunting. The outskirts of the city are a real land of lakes. Thanks to the convenient landscape, the region has created several magnificent golf courses.

Drive to Nahuel Huapi

Resting here, you should definitely find time to go to this famous park. Nahuel Huapi is located about three hundred kilometers from the city of Bariloche. It should be borne in mind that in some places the road passes along dangerous mountain routes, so the travel time can be about eight to ten hours. But such a trip will fully pay off with the beauty that will open to the eyes of tourists. Beautiful views, judging by the reviews, are breathtaking, amazing.

At least three days should be allocated for the inspection of the national park, of which two will take the road. Tour groups usually arrive at their destination in the evening. However, the inspection of Nahuel Huapi begins only in the morning. First of all, travelers climb the funicular to the highest peak of the mountain. The tour also includes a catamaran ride on a clean mountain lake.

Useful tips for tourists

Those who are going to Bariloche (Argentina) to ride on the snowy slopes, you can not take skis and snowboards with you. Right on site, all the necessary equipment, with the highest quality, can be rented. Points for renting skis, etc., work at almost every step.

For those who do not know what to buy as a souvenir to relatives and friends, experienced travelers are advised to bring the famous chocolate. And yet, the region is famous for its wonderful products that are made from vicuna wool - a local animal that resembles something of a llama. In Bariloche, you can also buy traditional Argentinean souvenirs. Judging by the reviews, our compatriots brought knives and silver jewelry, a poncho, mate vessels, a poncho, small wicker baskets and leather goods from there.

And most importantly: you should definitely take a camera with you on a trip. Surely every tourist will be amazed and inspired by the beauty of nature Bariloche (Argentina). Photos taken as a keepsake will remind you of a wonderful trip to this amazing corner on the planet.
