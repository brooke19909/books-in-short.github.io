Sardinia: tourist reviews, beaches, hotels, attractions

The island of Sardinia is considered one of the most striking and unusual places in Italy. It has its own history and even language, and many exotic countries can envy the many kilometers of beaches of this luxurious resort . On the one hand, the island attracts with its typically Mediterranean beauty, and on the other, it is so distinctive that it cannot but arouse the interest of travelers. Unfortunately, this tourist destination has not yet become popular among Russians. Tour operators attribute this to the high cost of such a vacation. However, those who were lucky enough to visit the coast of Sardinia will never be able to forget these paradise places, and will always strive here again. Today our article will reveal for you some of the secrets of this resort, and perhaps your next vacation you will spend on its amazing beaches.





A few historical facts about Sardinia

Of course, from the reviews of tourists about Sardinia, you can find out a lot about the island. However, historical facts often elude the eyes of travelers, although they are very interesting, to say the least, extraordinary.

Few people know that the main language of local residents is not Italian at all, but sardis. This dialect is native to the indigenous population of the island, which for many centuries has been influenced by different peoples, dynasties and empires.

Interestingly, the island became part of Italy only in the second half of the nineteenth century. However, he was always in some confrontation with the mainland, so in the mid-twentieth century, Sardinia turned into autonomy.

Until the sixties of the last century, tourism on the island was not developed at all. However, in just a few years, Sardinia has become a Mecca for tourists from all over the world. Today, this resort is visited annually by several million travelers.

The benefits of a holiday in Sardinia

If you are in doubt whether Sardinia is suitable for you, reviews from tourists will help to identify the benefits of a holiday in this beautiful place on the Mediterranean Sea.

Most tourists come to the island because of its stunning coast. The beaches of Sardinia stretch for almost two thousand kilometers, while the water off the coast has an incredibly beautiful turquoise hue. It is interesting that in different parts of the island the sea has a different color, but it always strikes the imagination of vacationers with deep shades.

Many people believe that the most beautiful sea can be seen in the Caribbean. But those who happened to compare one and the other resort, confidently make a choice in favor of the beaches of Sardinia.

Despite the fact that the Mediterranean Sea cannot boast of tropical corals and an abundance of underwater inhabitants, there are resorts specializing in diving on the island. Moreover, customers are always satisfied with their immersion and enthusiastically recommend a similar pastime to friends.

Another plus on the Mediterranean island is the weather. It is conducive to tourism almost all year round. For most of the year, the gentle sun shines in Sardinia, and a comfortable temperature allows you to spend whole days in the fresh air. High season starts in May and lasts through October. Sardinia in September, for example, is surprisingly hospitable. In the afternoon, the air warms up to twenty-five to thirty degrees, and evening walks are refreshing. You can swim on the island until the end of October, but there are tourists who come to the island with great pleasure in the cold season.

In the reviews of tourists, Sardinia seems to be a place where rest can be extremely diverse. It is very easy to find an interesting lesson for yourself, and if you wish, you can create a new and rich cultural program for yourself every day. Travelers say that Sardinia is rich in sights, national parks, cultural centers and places where delicious national dishes are prepared. All this makes the rest truly unforgettable.

Also, the advantages of vacationing in Sardinia include the low cost of renting villas. All of them are located in luxury resorts, are fully equipped and cost much less than any hotel on the island.





How to get to the resort?

When planning a trip, many tourists are confused by the fact that they do not know what is the best way to get to the Mediterranean island. This is actually pretty simple. Tour operators will offer you two options:

  • air;
  • water.

Air traffic with Sardinia is quite well established. Direct flights fly here from Moscow, so travelers will not have any special problems on the road. There are three international airports on the island: Cagliari, Alghero, Olbia. In each of them you can buy a ticket to Russia.

How much to fly to Sardinia from Moscow? This question is asked by many tourists who are afraid of long flights. In fact, the flight will take about three hours, during which time travelers will overcome almost three thousand kilometers.

After arrival at any airport, you can rent a transport to get to the desired resort. Also between the resorts runs public transport and taxis.

Ferries to Sardinia run regularly; this is provided by four transport companies. You can get to the island from different cities of Italy. Most often, tourists travel to Sardinia from Palermo, Livorno and Naples. Water travel to the island from Sicily is also quite common.





Island beaches

If you want to choose the best beach of the island, then reviews of tourists about Sardinia will not help you in this difficult task. After all, the island officially has more than three hundred and sixty beaches. Moreover, they all have their own personality and character. Some are formed by limestone cliffs, creating secluded coves. Others are distinguished by dunes of the most delicate pink color, and still others are buried in greenery and stand out by gentle descents into the water. It is impossible to list all the beach areas of Sardinia within the framework of one article, but we have identified five places that you must see with your own eyes, once on the island:

  • Poetto Beach
  • Le Bombardt;
  • Pisinas dunes;
  • Spiaggia Dell Principe Bay;
  • Cala Luna beach.

We will now talk about each of these amazing places.

The most popular beach in Cagliari

This beach, located in one of the most popular resorts in Sardinia, is ideal for those who like to spend time actively at sea. Poetto beach is characterized by white sand and cleanliness. The last factor applies to all coastal areas of the island. The municipal, wild and private beaches of Sardinia are always surprisingly clean, which the locals are very proud of.

Poetto is easily accessible from the city center, as public transport runs here. And it is in this place on the weekend that the townspeople are in a hurry, which emphasizes the special status of the beach area.

Beach in Alghero

Not all tourists know about this place. Usually, while relaxing in Alghero, they try to get to Port Side. It is always quite noisy and there are many tourists, so those who prefer a quiet and secluded vacation away from the crowd should look for Le Bombardt beach.

It is famous for its golden sand and the scent of coniferous forests that surround this beautiful bay. It rarely storms, the sea has a very unusual shade, and beckons to itself. There are never many vacationers on the beach, but there are many cafes and small restaurants on it.

Dunes Pisinas

This place is called by many "the soul of Sardinia." In September, it is quite deserted, so this month is ideal for exploring the dunes. The beach is located near Arbus, with your own car you can drive almost to the coast by dirt road. And then a fantastic sight will appear before your eyes - for many kilometers around you will find a real kingdom of sand, sparkling with different shades. Some dunes reach a height of up to fifty meters, several times a day they change their shape and size due to the wind blowing.

It is noteworthy that this beach is considered wild and one of the cleanest on the island. In its vicinity you can even see sea turtles and other animals.





Spiaggia Dell Principe Bay

This beach is famous for its perfectly clear water. The bay itself has the shape of a crescent and is distinguished by the smallest, slightly creaking sand. Once this amazing place was discovered by one of the Arab princes, and since then it has been incredibly popular with locals and tourists.

Since the water in the bay of Spiaggia Dell Principe is very clear, this place is ideal for snorkeling, diving and fishing. It is here that the most luxurious resorts of Sardinia are located and villas of the richest people of the world are built.

Resort Cala Gonone

Cala Luna Beach is located on the east coast of the island at some distance from the resort itself. It got its name due to the unusual snow-white sand and shining cliffs that stand out against the background of turquoise sea waves.

The beach is not so easy to find, on all sides it is surrounded by cliffs and green thickets. Many tourists rent a boat to get to Cala Luna by water. You can also walk to the resort from it, however, in this case, be prepared to cover a distance of four kilometers.

What to see in Sardinia?

Tourists often write that not to see all the sights of Sardinia is a criminal omission. Therefore, once on this wonderful island, try to use your time to the maximum extent possible. And we, in turn, will advise you that it is mandatory to look at Sardinia:

  • Nuragi. On the island, archaeologists have found more than eight thousand stone buildings, which are tentatively attributed to the Bronze Age. The exact purpose of the nuraga is unknown, but scientists believe that they could be used as temples or fortifications. Nothing like these buildings in the world anymore.
  • La Maddalena National Park. On a small island of the same name, located near Sardinia, tourists will find a corner of unspoiled nature. Here you instantly forget about all the troubles and worries, falling under the unusual charm of these places.
  • Bastion of San Remy. Crowds of tourists who are dreaming from above to see the capital of the island, Cagliari, are trying here. It is noteworthy that it was erected on seven hills, and the bastion is the highest point of the city. It offers stunning views of the bay, city streets and the old cathedral, built in the Romanesque style.
  • Sardinian mines. Even before our era, the mining industry was very important for the inhabitants of the island. It is dotted with mines, tunnels and narrow passages. Today, many mines are repaired and are an unusual attraction for tourists. It is interesting that the locals restored the old mining enterprises, turning them into fashionable hotels. There is no end to lodging in such institutions, and those who simply want to get to know this page of the Sardinian history will be happy to take a fascinating tour.

Many tourists write that they enjoyed the contemplation of yachts in Porto Chevo and learned a lot during their excursion to the ruins of the Roman Empire in Nora. Our compatriots who have visited the island are sure that you will remember your vacation in Sardinia for a long time. Where it is better to relax, you may think about it after reading our article. Unfortunately, this question is almost impossible to answer. After all, every corner of the island is good in its own way, and the locals are very sincere and welcoming people who, with great care and attention, treat all tourists without exception. Therefore, go to Sardinia yourself, and let a part of this island forever remain in your soul.
