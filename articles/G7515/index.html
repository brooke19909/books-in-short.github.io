Who is Naramsin?





In Akkad, the king was ruled, whose name was Naram-Suen, which in translation from Akkadian means "favorite of Sina." In local mythology, Sin was the god of the moon. Ruled Naramsin around 2237-2200 BC.

  • Didanum tribe - the king defeated the Western Semitic cattle-breeding tribe;
  • the kingdom of Ebla - was known as a commercial city-state, which was located on the lands of ancient Syria, was completely destroyed by the Akkadian ruler;

  • Namar is a Hurrian state in whose territory the Akkadian king built a palace of impressive size;
  • Simurrum - the country was located on the Lower Zab River, was captured along with local rulers;
  • Elam - an ancient state that was located on the territory of modern Iran, under the agreement was to coordinate its actions with the Akkadian king.

Stele Information





It is not known who was the author of the stele of Naramsin. It was created from sandstone. Its height is approximately two meters. The stela is still considered a masterpiece of Mesopotamian art.

Reason for creating

The stele of Naramsin was made in the years 2300-2100 BC. It is dedicated to the campaign of the Akkadian king against the powerful Lullubeys. That was the name of the ancient people who lived in the upper reaches of the Diyala River. The name of the tribe translates as ‚Äústrangers,‚Äù although this information is not accurate.

The people were distinguished by their militancy, but Naramsin managed to subjugate the Lullubeys. It was this event that was immortalized on the stele. However, Akkad could not long keep the warlike tribe in subjection. Soon they again became independent and were able to continue attacks on the territory of the southern Mesopotamia.

Artifact moving

Originally, the Stela of King Naramsin was established in Sippar. Translated from the Sumerian language, this name means "city of birds." The state existed on the territory of modern Iraq, where the Tigris and Euphrates rivers are closest to each other. The city was engaged in trade and did not seek to strengthen its political power. There, the stele stood for a thousand years.

After she was moved to Susa. This was done after a successful campaign on the Babylonia of the Elamite king Jutruk-Nahhunte I. Susa were located on the territory of modern Iran (Khuzestan province). Description of the historical monument

The victorious stele of Tsar Naramsin has a spiral-like construction of figures on a plane. In this, it differs significantly from the Sumerian composition creation technique. For the plot, military actions in the mountains were chosen. The stele itself resembles the top of a mountain, tapering to the top.





Creation consists of three compositional lines. They go almost parallel from the bottom up. This made it possible to show the path of Naramsin‚Äôs troops, his ascent to the top of the mountain. Warriors of the king ascend the parallel slopes. This practically does not happen in life, usually people climb the winding spiral roads.

Also, the place of transition from the slope to the slope is not indicated. Spectators can only guess how the king was on top. There are also violations in proportions in the image. For example, the warriors who climb the hillside look smaller than the figure of Naramsin, who stands on top.

Description of the figure of Naramsin





The central figure of the stele of Naramsin, the description of which is given in this article, is the Akkadian ruler. It is distinguished by size, as well as compositional construction.

A man stands on top as a hero who defeated the Lullubeys. The remaining characters of the stele are shown not so significant, so their bodies are smaller. Mountain terraces serve as a pedestal, on which the victory of the king.





There are no images of gods in the composition. Only the signs of the divine bodies are shown, which are located above the figure of the victor of Naramsin.

The whole composition was created to show the triumph of the human God, by whom Naramsin positioned himself. The Akkadian relief was able to convey the dynamic and targeted movement of the warriors, while time stops around the king. The creators of creation have achieved this main thing - they recorded an ever-lasting triumph.
