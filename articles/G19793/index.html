The meaning of the term

This term refers to objects that are part of a network of research institutes or agricultural experimental stations. In field experiments, the effectiveness of various technologies of cultivating crops, mineral and organic fertilizers, methods of combating soil erosion, plant protection products, etc. is studied. In addition, the Experimental Field is a geographical name.

The experimental field in Krasnoyarsk: how did it all begin?

Historically, in the Yenisei province, berries and fruits were quite expensive. Apples were brought here from other provinces. It is known that in 1909 a large number of fruits were imported from Samara and Syzran. As a result, wholesale purchase prices decreased, but booths still sold for 10-12 kopecks. kilo. Those who wanted to save money bought vitamins at the market, where apples were much cheaper on carts.





In the winter of 2010, the city council laid the foundation for the development of an experimental field in Krasnoyarsk. In the future, an experimental station was supposed to grow on its basis, in which the work of other experimental institutions would be combined. In sec. Kazachinsky, near the city of Minusinsk, in the Minusinsk district, near the beet-sugar factory, Tagarsky island, experimental fields were already functioning at that time. The Duma returned to this issue in 1912. A decision was made on the provision of leased urban land for the construction of an experimental field in Krasnoyarsk.

What was the site?

The allocated site, in fact, was an inter-facet located between pasture peasant and Cossack lands in the village of Solontsovsky, in Zaledeevsky volost, and an adjacent narrow strip to the river. The station staff was to consist of 2 agronomists, several workers and employees. In 1912, D. D. Nashchokin was appointed the head of the experimental field.

History

It is known that in the beginning, housing was built for staff, an office, a chemical laboratory, a stable, a weather station, a vegetation house, storage rooms and a library. In addition, a fence was built around the site with a length of 2 thousand fathoms. The head of the field was allocated an apartment of 6, the assistant - of 4, the observer of the weather station - of 2 rooms and a kitchen. The premises were furnished at the expense of the city budget.

On the experimental field, tests were conducted of potatoes, various varieties of cereals: oats, wheat, oats, spring rye, herbs. An exchange of experience was established with US farmers.





In 1914, D. D. Nashchokin collected a collection of samples of bread seeds from different counties of the Yenisei province. The scientist's goal was to study the quality of grain of bread, the selection of the most profitable varieties for growing in the region. Scientific work was interrupted by the outbreak of World War I. D. D. Nashchokin worked on the experimental field for eight years. In April 1921, he was replaced by VV Sabashnikov, who was tragically killed in the dungeons of the OGPU.

Current days

The Soviet government reorganized the experimental field into a fruit and berry station. Great professionals worked here. In the late 30s, S. M. Shcherbachenko was appointed director of the station. In 1941, all the men, employees of the station, went to the front. After returning, S. M. Shcherbachenko served as the director of a nursery in Achinsk. Until 1968, at the Krasnoyarsk station, N. S. Simonov worked as director.

Today, after more than 100 years, the Krasnoyarsk Territory takes one of the leading positions in Siberia in the development of gardening. Thanks to the activities of the employees of the experimental station, Krasnoyarsk residents have a huge variety of varieties of fruit trees: plums, apple trees, currants, etc.

Ulyanovsk

The history of the Experimental Field in Ulyanovsk dates back to the distant 1859, when, through the efforts of Count V.P. Orlov-Davydov, an agricultural society was created to assist landowners.

In February 1971, the village was included in the city of Ulyanovsk. In addition to the regional office of beekeeping and the wax processing plant, which have been operating for several decades, they have opened a procurement office, a motor transport enterprise, a plant protection station, a fire department, as well as a number of construction organizations.





In recent years, a microdistrict has grown near the old grove, consisting of five-story houses equipped with all modern conveniences, and several two- and three-story cottages have appeared.
