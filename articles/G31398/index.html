Republic of Ireland: attractions, history, photos

The Republic of Ireland (the capital is Dublin) is a state located on the island of Ireland. The country is a popular tourist destination. Here, travelers will find amazingly beautiful landscapes, unique natural sites, deep lakes and bays, interesting castles and cathedrals. From this publication you will learn all about the Republic of Ireland (sights, history and culture are discussed below).

From the history of the country

The first settlers arrived on the island more than 6 thousand years ago. In the 4th century BC e. this territory was inhabited by Celtic tribes, and in the VIII century. - by the Vikings. In the XII century. the English appeared on the island. In the XVII century. English and Scottish Protestants were settled in the northern regions of the island. Over time, the majority of the population of Ireland began to be Protestants. This was made possible due to the displacement of the indigenous population of the northern regions of the island by Scottish immigrants. In 1921, the southern provinces of Ireland, whose majority were Catholics, separated from the Protestant north (Easter Revolution). An independent state of Ireland was created on this territory.

Considering the historical past of the island, the concepts of "Northern Ireland" and "Republic of Ireland" should not be confused. The Republic of Ireland is an independent state. North Ulster, including 6 northern counties, is under the protectorate of Great Britain.





sights

Ireland is an amazingly beautiful country. Mountain landscapes, natural landscapes, clear lakes, ancient architecture and cultural monuments - all this, of course, attracts travelers.

There are several popular tourist routes in the country.

  • Dublin is the capital of the state, its cultural and economic center. In the city and its environs, most popular tourist places are located. Among them are Dublin Castle, Ashtown Castle , Presidential Residence, St. Patrick's Cathedral, Cathedral Mosque.
  • Cork is a large city located in the southwestern part of the island. Cork has long been famous for its historical sites. In its vicinity are the famous castles of Desmond and Blackrock, and in the city itself there are a large number of interesting architectural monuments.
  • County Kerry is an ideal place for families. Thousands of tourists come here every year to see the beautiful lakes of Killarney, the amazing islands of Blasket and the famous Stone Ring Urag.




Kailmore Abbey

Kailmore Abbey, located in the western part of the state, has rightfully been awarded the title of the most romantic place in the country. It is located on the shores of a beautiful lake at the foot of the Druchruach mountain. Mountain cliffs, dense forest, clear lake water - all this makes this place fabulous. Kylemore Castle, where Mitchell and Margaret Henry lived, was built in Victorian style.

In addition, the abbey houses a beautiful neo-Gothic church and a unique garden surrounded by Victorian walls.





"Ring of Kerry"

"Ring of Kerry" - a tourist route that runs through the main attractions of the county of the same name. In recent decades, this destination has been particularly popular among travelers from all over the world. A tour of the Kerry ring is a unique chance to get acquainted with the most amazing sights of Ireland.

  • Killarney Park is a great place for families.
  • A trip to the villages of Kersivin, Waterville, Killorglin is a unique opportunity to get acquainted with the life of local residents and, of course, drink a glass of delicious Irish beer.
  • Torc Waterfall.
  • View of Killarney Lake.
  • Macross House Manor is a manor museum located in the vicinity of Killarney. In 1861, Queen Victoria herself visited this place!
  • Ross Castle is a symbol of Irish resistance to British rule.




Malahide Castle

The Republic of Ireland has long been famous for its beautiful castles, and one of them is Malahide. The estate, built back in the XII century., Located not far from the capital of the state. Today, a museum is opened in the castle, where everyone has the opportunity to see not only old furniture and famous paintings, but also the family jewels of the Tabolt and Corbet families who once lived here. In addition, the castle is also known for its mystical traditions, namely, the legend of the five ghosts that still appear here.





Dublin Castle

Dublin Castle is a real gem of the capital of Ireland. It was built in the XIII century. (during the stay of the Vikings). For more than 700 years, the castle has been the seat of the English government. Today, this place is included in the list of the most famous sights of Ireland. Going on an excursion to Dublin Castle, every traveler will also be able to see the ancient Holy Trinity Church and the Norman Tower Record (XIII century).
