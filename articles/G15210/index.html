Italy, Livorno: interesting facts and attractions

The large port of Tuscany and the favorite stopping place of all cruise liners is a true paradise for vacationers. Located in the bay of the Ligurian Sea, Livorno is called the ideal city of the Renaissance. The center of the province of the same name with centuries of history is famous throughout the world for its magnificent nature and cultural and historical monuments, which all of Italy is proud of.

Livorno, which arose on the site of a fishing village a thousand years ago, is not in vain called the pearl of Italy. The busy city with small streets is saturated with the special atmosphere of an eternal holiday. He will captivate each guest with a huge amount of entertainment and make him lost among the old buildings and picturesque canals.

Port history

The origins of the modern resort originate from the XV century, since the reign of the Medici family. The Grand Duke of Tuscany ordered the chief architect of the courtyard to turn a small settlement into the most beautiful port, and very soon the whole of Italy started talking about the city, eclipsed by its splendor Naples and Rome. Livorno is recognized as the main commercial center of the Mediterranean, and after the expansion of borders, foreign ships enter the state‚Äôs sea gate.



Cosmopolitan city

At the end of the 16th century, a tolerance law was passed, and the port became a haven for all dissenters. For Catholic Italy, this was an exceptional case, and residents of other cities of Tuscany reacted to this manifestation of tolerance very ambiguously. In a cosmopolitan city, uniting people of all nationalities and different faiths, they are sure to find something akin to natives of all countries of the world. No wonder they say that only in Livorno you can find the most incredible cultural and ethnic combinations.

Livorno (Italy): beaches

Now the largest business center of Tuscany enjoys stunning success among foreign tourists. In the city itself, the beaches are not very adapted for a comfortable stay, and the proximity of the port affects the purity of the water. However, in the northern and southern parts of the center of the province are wonderful places for swimming, where tourists rush.

Querchanella

The village on the edge of Livorno is known far beyond Italy, and it is not for nothing that Quercianella is called the Tuscan pearl. The village washed by the azure sea is far from the noisy port, and here you can completely relax, succumbing to the charm of warm water and the friendly sun. The rocky landscape is called the main feature of a well-equipped beach for relaxation.



Fresh air, the friendly attitude of local residents, delicious Italian cuisine will leave an indelible impression on all travelers who arrived in a picturesque place.

Tyrrhenia

Another beautiful beach located between Livorno (Italy) and the mouth of the Arno River attracts thousands of tourists annually. Tirrenia, hidden in a pine grove, is not the best place for those who seek solitude. The territory of the sandy beach stretching for ten kilometers is loved not only by foreign guests, but also by Italians, so it is extremely lively here on weekends. In Terrenia you can go spearfishing and diving, and play tennis or football at the equipped sports fields.

Palazzo Hugens

In the center of Venezia Nuova is the residence of local aristocrats Palazzo Hugens, in which the Grand Duke of Medici once stayed. The strict four-story building, often changing owners, was restored in the 70s of the last century. The entrance to the majestic Palazzo Huigens Palace is decorated with a balcony, and covered galleries with original moldings open onto a quiet courtyard.



Promenade
