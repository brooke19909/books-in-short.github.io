Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 * (Egypt / Sharm El Sheikh)

In the place where the Sinai Desert meets the Red Sea, Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 opens its doors to tourists. The Sinai granite mountains, whose silhouettes are visible to hotel guests, are a reliable barrier to desert air masses. Thanks to them, the Red Sea coast is the resort part of the Sinai Peninsula, which has a classic Mediterranean climate. These places are called the Egyptian Riviera.

Here, along the coast of Naama Bay, the resort town of Sharm el-Sheikh was built. It is fundamentally different from any typical Egyptian city. After all, its buildings and infrastructure are modeled on the Mediterranean European resorts. It is in Sharm el-Sheikh that the Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 is a second-line hotel. It is only 300 meters from the sea from it.





Hotel architecture

The hotel complex is memorable, it was created in a colorful Andalusian style (whose homeland is the southern coast of Spain). This characteristic design of Arab-Spanish origin is characterized by bright warm colors with a predominance of white and terracotta colors. It is characterized by elegant semicircular "Arabian" arches, balconies with curly balusters. Design Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4, with just such generic features, characterized by a desire for elegance. The snow-white exterior of its four two-story buildings borders the inner Andalusian courtyard. True, in this case it is quite spacious. That, in fact, is the territory of the hotel complex. There is a large outdoor pool with a relaxation area, small neat English lawns.

Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 is on the outside a city building of closed architecture, and on the courtyard side it is the embodiment of open architecture.

Hotel rooms



The hotel has 140 apartments. Of these, 71 are standard rooms (22 m 2 ), 65 superior (26 m 2 ), 2 small suites (with a living room), 3 non-smoking rooms (economy class). Inspire immediately upon arrival. Cleaning in the rooms of visitors - daily. Bed and towel change - twice a week. The rooms are decorated in an Andalusian style. The furniture is stylish, made of dark natural wood, high, with rectangular shapes and inlaid with mother of pearl. On the walls - stucco, in Andalusian painted in bright warm colors. The organizers did not save on the floors either. It is lined with artificial marble. There are heavy curtains on the windows. Bedspreads and pillows with a characteristic complex ornament. The sconces harmoniously combined with the design of the rooms illuminate the rooms of Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4. Reviews indicate a certain lack of natural light in the standard apartments due to the small window space and the absence of a balcony in them. Therefore, according to guests, going to a boarding house, you should order the rooms "superior". However, some lack of lighting is a hallmark of the Andalusian style.

The rooms have air conditioning, TV, telephone, hairdryer.

There is no refrigerator in the rooms, there is a minibar, however its cooling function is rather weak.

Beach

To come to the hotel beach, you should cross the road. The coveted sand strip itself - the goal of our walk - is directly adjacent to the first line Dais Inn Gafe hotel. Perhaps Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 (Egypt) rents its beach from the aforementioned hotel. Sun loungers and sun umbrellas are free here. Moreover, there are plenty of them on the beach. At any time of the day, a vacationer who comes here will find a free deck chair waiting for him. But a beach towel will only be issued to a spa traveler after he presents a receipt at the reception. Entry into the water - bulk, sandy, peeled of corals, but not worth the risk, after all - the Red Sea. Enter the water should be in special shoes. On the beach of Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 (Sharm el Sheikh, Naama Bay) there are pontoons that allow more experienced swimmers to enjoy the deep underwater world.

If you swim a little to the right, you can watch the charming underwater world of a small reef. You will, of course, be instructed before that - you should not feed the fish. In fact, the fish of the Red Sea are not our crucians. If you offer them bread, then it will remain uneaten, but only clog the water area.

But if you show a little imagination and offer the local fish a little tuna fish, you will be rewarded with a real show. Hundreds of colorful underwater inhabitants will sail to you and will unceremoniously literally wrest their prey from your hands. However, do not get involved. Take only a small piece, because moray eels and stingrays can sail to the feast. The bite of a fish surgeon and scorpenops can be fatal.

Diving

How to dive? How to breathe there? How to behave there in various typical situations? What can not be done under water? An interested person will find the answer to all these and other questions by attending diving classes.

It is no accident that it was on the Red Sea that diving turned into one of the applications of the tourism business. The reason for this is a universally recognized fact: the local underwater world is represented by the richest fauna and flora in the world. The sea water area near Oriental Rivoli Hotel 4 has good opportunities for exploring it. Almost all tourists take home the photos of the underwater world from here.

Indeed, there are more than 150 species of corals in the Red Sea alone! From ancient times, the wise people of Egypt kept this national treasure. There has never been fishing here since the ancient world. Residents considered the consumption of agricultural and livestock products to be worthy of nutrition. Eating fish was considered a sin. In our time, the Red Sea is under state protection. Nowhere in the world are there penalties of this size for attempting commercial fishing. At the same time, fishing is practiced as an exotic paid vacation for tourists (but we will talk about this later).

Giza Plateau



Near the suburbs of Cairo, on the Giza plateau, there is an ancient architectural complex of world significance, which is called the Pyramids of Giza. This is the only of the eight ancient wonders of the world that has survived to our time. And the pyramids were built around the III millennium BC. The complex consists of the pyramids of Cheops, Hafra and Menkaur. These structures are a tribute to the Egyptian people to the pharaohs. Near them are smaller pyramids. They, in turn, are dedicated to the queens. To the east of the pyramids is the legendary Sphinx. According to archaeologists, his face is that of Pharaoh Hafra.

Remember Boris Grebenshchikov‚Äôs song about the city of gold? She is about Jerusalem. Indeed, the buildings here are constructed of marbled gold-colored limestone. Nowhere else in the world is there such a stone. This ancient city is a unique monument of three religions. Each of them has its own shrines here. For Christians, this is the Church of the Holy Sepulcher. For Muslims - a complex of mosques al-Haram al- Sharif. For Jews, the Temple Mount and the Wailing Wall.

Luxor



This city grew up on the site of the ancient Thebes. Tourists seek to visit it to see the City of the Living on the right bank of the Nile and the City of the Dead on the left. The city of the Living is famous for the Luxor Temple and the Avenue of the Sphinxes, the Temple of Amun Ra. The city of the Dead is famous for its necropolises of the Valley of the Kings and the Valley of the Queens, the funerary temples of Queen Hatshepsut, Medinet Abu.
