Temple of the Golden Buddha - one of the main attractions of Bangkok

Thailand has many interesting places to visit. But their greatest concentration, of course, is in the capital of the country.

The landmark of Bangkok is located on the Tri Mit Street, near the train station. It is in great demand among tourists. The architecture and structure of the temple's decorations are unique, but the statue of a seated Buddha is of the greatest value and interest. It is made of gold, the pupils are made of sapphires, and the squirrels are made of pearls. The weight of the statue is 5.5 tons, height 3 meters. The estimated cost is estimated at 250 million dollars.

It was estimated that the Golden Buddha was built during the kingdom of Sukhothai (1238-1438). But there are no written records of its origin. In the eighteenth century, the Buddha was completely covered with gypsum, so that the enemy army, which invaded during the struggle against Burma, considered this an ordinary statue. The trick worked too well. The secret has been forgotten for centuries. No one knows the true reason for hiding. It was assumed that the statue was closed immediately before the Burmese invasion in the middle of the 18th century in order to hide its true value from the enemy.

In 1955, while the ‚Äúcement‚Äù statue was lifted into her new home, the ropes could not stand it and the sculpture fell. The next day, the surprised monk saw gold, where a piece of gypsum was chipped. Then the remaining cover was removed and a golden statue was found.

Patpong Night Market is one of Bangkok's attractions that is worth a visit. After dusk, from 18:00 to 1:00, the night bazaar begins its work. A couple of hours before the opening, the locals mount the counters, spread the goods, work on the lighting.

On the market you can find everything that you want: a large number of souvenirs, jewelry, watches, clothes, exotic goods that you will not find in Europe, for example, stingray leather bags, crocodile leather products, python. Temple of the Emerald Buddha

In Bangkok, the capital of Thailand, a huge number of attractions, one of which is the temple of the "Emerald Buddha." It is believed that this building is one of the most sacred places in the whole Kingdom. It is visited not only by tourists, but also by a huge number of local residents. It is located in the heart of Bangkok, on the territory of the Grand Royal Palace, and is considered to be one of the most popular and visited places.

Going deep into the history of the construction of this temple, it turns out that the construction began in 1784. However, the Buddha statue, which is now in it, was born earlier. According to one legend, this statue was discovered in the 15th century, in a stupa destroyed by a thunderstorm. From above it was covered with gypsum or clay, but one monk saw that a green stone was visible from under the upper layer. From here the name ‚ÄúEmerald Buddha‚Äù came, while the statue itself was erected from jadeite.

The statue, for the entire time of its existence, visited various cities, but in 1778 returned to its homeland. By the decision of the king, it was decided to erect Wat Phra Kaew - the temple of the "Emerald Buddha", as the locals call it, for this statue, which will also serve as the personal temple of the ruler.

There are some interesting facts about the Temple of the Emerald Buddha:

  • the entrance to the temple is guarded by twelve bronze statues of lions, as well as other mythical characters who are designed to scare away evil spirits from it;
  • only the king or his heir is allowed to touch the statue of Buddha himself;
  • three times a year, the king himself changes clothes on the statue to attract good luck in the new season: summer, winter or rainy season.

Wat Yan Nava

Wat Yan Nava is a famous Buddhist temple complex located in Bangkok. It consists of several separate buildings erected at different times. The temple complex is now under the protection of the royal family. You can find it 30 km from Pattaya.

The main temple was erected in the 19th century, during the reign of King Rama III. This building looks like a ship. On the territory of the temple is one of the main Buddhist schools of Wat Yan Nava. The territory of the complex occupies 145 hectares.

Here you can see:

  • dozens of buildings, pavilions;
  • mythical sculptures of various creatures;
  • images of the king.

The architecture of the building impresses with its diversity, made in the Indian, Thai, Chinese style. There are many murals, sculptures: holy faces, figures of gods and monks, large lions. You can see a large number of cult objects.

The complex includes temples where local monks and female novices live. You can visit the meditation center, which hosts trainings and seminars.

The schedule of events can be better checked with the administration of the complex. On the territory of Wat Yan Nava there are two lakes covered with lotus, and arbors are installed nearby. Entrance to the complex is free, there are only restrictions on clothing for women.
