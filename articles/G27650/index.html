Veronika Polonskaya is a Soviet film and theater actress. Her fate is closely connected with Mayakovsky. Polonskaya was the last love of the great poet. And she is the last who saw Mayakovsky alive. Veronica witnessed his suicide.

Biography of Veronica Polonskaya

Veronica Polonskaya was born on June 6, 1908 in the family of Russian actors of the Maly Theater. Her father, Vitold Polonsky, was known and popular in Russian pre-revolutionary cinema. Mother, Olga Gladkova, also acted in films. Veronica Polonskaya relatives and friends affectionately called Nora.





Where did Polonskaya study

When Veronika Polonskaya was 16 years old in 1924, she entered the Moscow Art Theater to study. After graduation, Nora remained to work in it. But the study did not end there. She took acting lessons from Konstantin Stanislavsky and Nikolai Batalov.

Career Polonskaya

Veronika Polonskaya is an actress of Soviet cinema. Her career began in childhood. The first time she starred in a movie as a child, along with her father, in the film "When the Lilac Blossoms", which was released in 1917. In the picture, Veronica played the role of Alla. The film was made based on the novel "Pan" by Laurich Bruun. After the first filming, Veronica was fascinated by the magic of cameras and decided to devote her life to cinema.

In 1918, Polonskaya's father signed a very lucrative contract with Hollywood. And Veronica had to leave with her parents in the United States. But on January 5, 1919, Vitold Polonsky suddenly died. As a result, Nora and her mother remained at home.





Many remembered the performance "Our Youth", in which Veronica played. Polonskaya's debut in Soviet cinema took place in the film Glass Eye. This was the first picture in which the matured Veronica played. From 1927 to 1935, Polonskaya constantly played in performances. The last place of work of the actress is the theater. Ermolaeva. In 1973, Polonskaya retired.

The personal life of the actress

In 1925, Veronika Vitoldovna Polonskaya married actor Mikhail Mikhailovich Yanshin. Despite the love affair with Mayakovsky, she could not admit her husband to treason. And for many years he was in the dark. The whole truth about the love affair of Polonskaya and Mayakovsky surfaced after the poet's suicide, thanks to his farewell note in which he recognized Veronica as his heiress along with his relatives. Polonskaya was dishonored throughout the country. When the betrayal opened, a divorce followed.

Polonskaya married Valery Alexandrovich Azersky for the second time. From him in 1936 gave birth to a son. It was named after Mayakovsky - Vladimir.
