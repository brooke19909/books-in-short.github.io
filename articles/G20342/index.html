Money transfer "Leader": tariffs and addresses of points of reception in Moscow

Money transfer "Leader" is a great opportunity to transfer money to the CIS countries, as well as near and far abroad at relatively low tariffs.

History and development of the system

The wide development of money transfer systems in the countries of the former Soviet Union began in the late 20th - early 21st centuries. Until that time, people used mostly mail services to transfer funds. The money went long enough, but there were no other options.

The Leader money transfer system was founded by a Russian non-banking organization back in 2003. The service is constantly evolving, gaining an increasing number of regular users. Currently, over 130,000 points work with these money transfers. They can be sent to 132 countries.

Money transfer "Leader": shipping rates

For the transfer of money from the sender to the recipient, a certain payment is necessarily taken. Money for the service is paid by the sender. Receiving funds at the point of issue is always free. The recipient must present a passport.





So, we will consider the tariffs for sending a transfer to different countries. If a person wants to send a transfer through the Leader system within Russia, then with a transfer amount of up to 5000 rubles, the commission will be 50 rubles. If the transfer amount ranges from 5,000 to 100,000 rubles, then the money transfer fee will be 1% of it. In rubles, you can also send money to Abkhazia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Georgia, Ukraine, Moldova, Tajikistan and Uzbekistan. The maximum transfer amount to these countries is 590,000 rubles. Fee for sending money ranges from 50 to 2200 rubles. If you calculate the commission in relation to the amount of the transfer, then on average it will be from 1 to 1.5% of it.

The Leader money transfer system also works in euros. Transfers can be sent to the CIS countries, European and Asian countries. When sending a transfer to the post-Soviet states, the client will have to pay from 2 euros (amount from 0 to 2000 euros) to 90 euros (from 9 to 20 thousand euros). If the transfer amount falls between the indicated values, the Leader money transfer system will take a commission in the amount of 1% of the transfer amount. Now let's talk about the European direction. So, in Germany you can send up to 500 euros for 2.5% of the amount. Transferring money to Italy will cost 3 euros (for amounts up to 100 euros) and 3% if the transfer size is from 100 to 500 euros. Clients will be able to receive money through the Leader system also in Egypt, Mexico, Poland and some European countries.





The system set approximately the same tariffs for sending foreign currency transfers in dollars. The only difference is a wider list of countries for sending money.
