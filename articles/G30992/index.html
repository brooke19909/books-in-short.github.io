Phones with 4 SIM cards: an overview of representatives of a special category

Progress faithfully and confidently strides through the world of high technologies, gradually transforming them, bringing to new levels. Each step is an attempt to make life easier, to change the way a person communicates with information technology, but this does not apply to mobile operators. Domestic so-called OPPSs offer a fairly wide range of services and opportunities for their clients, but each, because of his thirst for profit and nothing to let the client out of his clutches, often creates unprofitable conditions. People are literally torn between mobile operators. Someone offers fast and reliable Internet, someone has inexpensive roaming, while someone has an SMS package cheaper than others. Therefore, a modern person is a person with SIM cards of several operators. And if two SIM cards can be installed in almost any modern smartphone, for those who do not like to limit themselves to even this, you need something more serious, namely phones with 4 SIM cards.

Philips Xenium

Initially, it is worth understanding that it is not easy to find such an exclusive, because this is a rather rare beast - phones with 4 SIM cards. Philips, for example, like many other manufacturers, offers alternative options, phones with only 3 SIM cards. They do not quite fit into the described category, nevertheless, a successful phone like Philips Xenium cannot be ignored.

Texet TM-420

For those who are not ready to compromise and are looking for the ultimate solution among communication devices, the domestic manufacturer Texet has prepared a great gift, phones with 4 SIM cards and a powerful battery.

One of these pearls was the phone, codenamed TM-420. In fact, we are faced with a plain and far from the most advanced gadget. A fairly compact phone, equipped with only the most necessary, including a lousy 2 megapixel camera and an FM receiver. The main pride of the phone was the battery, thanks to which it is able to work on a single charge of more than 9 hours (talk time).
