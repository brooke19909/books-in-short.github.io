For a small office, a wall or floor-ceiling type is more suitable, the main thing is to correctly determine the necessary power of the equipment. When choosing such a technique, it is better to turn to the help of an organization professionally engaged in its sale and installation. Many people ask, what is the difference between air conditioning and a split system? So: split-system - this is the air conditioner, consisting of indoor and outdoor units, interconnected by copper tubes and electric wires.

Floor-to-ceiling air conditioners differ from wall-mounted ones in the way of installation. They are mounted under the ceiling, or, like batteries, at the bottom of the wall.





For some reason, the room may not be able to mount the equipment on the wall - the space is occupied, or the construction material (glass) is not intended for such manipulations, or the type of indoor unit does not fit into the interior of the room at all. In these cases, ceiling air conditioners come to the rescue.



They are ideal for rooms with a large area - up to 150 sq.m. Properly selected and installed equipment can cool an enclosed space with high ceilings and any configuration. Shops, restaurants and cafes are places where you can often see ceiling air conditioners, especially in cases where there are no false ceilings in the rooms. The advantage of this equipment is also in the unique air distribution system. Due to the special design of the indoor unit, a powerful stream of chilled air is directed along the ceiling - if the equipment is mounted under it, or walls - in cases where the installation was carried out in its lower part. This ensures a comfortable and even distribution of "cold" in the room, and the people in it are practically spared the opportunity to get under a stream of icy air.

In addition to the main cooling function, there are a number of useful features that air conditioners have. Ceiling, depending on the model, can, if necessary, perform heating, which is especially important during the so-called off-season, when the heating does not work.





Air ventilation is another necessary option. Working in this mode, the equipment purifies the air, and also eliminates it from excessive humidity. Almost all ceiling air conditioners are equipped with a special filtration system consisting of several steps.
