Thor well

This natural attraction on the Pacific coast is known not only to the entire state of Oregon. Few people in the world have heard of Cape Perpetua, where ancient volcanic activity created such a heap of granite blocks that a well - this huge natural funnel, a hollow among sharp rocks - is completely invisible. For local Indians, this place was called, which in meaning corresponds to our expression "the gates of the underworld."

Only at low tide you can get close to the well, but there is not enough time to consider it well, because the tide begins, and hell itself opens at this place. After a few minutes, everything around is transformed. Ocean waves roll with a monstrous roar on the rocks surrounding the well, and form an enchanting waterfall. Water covers the well in huge thickness. But the well never overflows, it is bottomless! Or connects to the ocean underground. But the waves beat everything, measured and tireless. And finally, the well answers. A powerful water column erupts from its depths, scattering by a kilometer, and everyone who saw it takes away with them a feeling of primitive horror.

Maar

Maar is a crater, and Hole-in-Ground is one of the most notable Maars on our planet. It is like a hole in the surface of the earth remaining after the destruction of the aerial part of the ridge, which happens after a strong earthquake or as a result of volcanic activity. Hole-in-Ground is simply amazing in size and with spectacular views. Oregon is generally seismically riotous, no wonder that nature has left such amazing traces here. The shape of the crater is round, very wide - more than one and a half kilometers, and a depth of one hundred and fifty meters, and with its appearance, the inner platform resembles alien scorched terrain.

Scientists say that a crater formed about eighteen thousand years ago. Since then there have been traces of a large lake stretching around this volcano, which disappeared during the Hole-in-Ground eruption. Magma swam so close to the surface that from its heat the lake began to boil and went into the atmosphere in the form of steam. And the crater itself filled the soil from the rising earth's crust, as if plugging it with a giant cork. Magma accumulated, but there was not a single gap for its exit. And then the volcano exploded. Huge fragments of the mountain and now are scattered throughout the district.
