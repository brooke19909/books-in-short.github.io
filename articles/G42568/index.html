Kallithea (Halkidiki): sights and beaches of the Greek resort

Kallithea (Halkidiki) is one of those famous resorts that are abundantly offered to holidaymakers in mainland Greece. With the beginning of the swimming season, young people flock here in large numbers, attracted by a wide range of entertainment. Well-known tourists love Kallithea for its natural beauties and comfortable beaches. So, what is known about this fabulous place that you do not want to leave?

Kallithea (Halkidiki): location

Known for its three-tooth form, the Halkidiki peninsula is located in the northern zone of the Aegean Sea. In fact, it consists of three elongated peninsulas resembling fingers. One of them is Kassandra, which lures tourists with magnificent beaches and ancient sights that cannot be seen even in a month. The most popular place in Kassandra, attracting thousands of vacationers every year, is Kallithea. Halkidiki is a peninsula on the territory of which there are many beautiful cities and towns, however it is worth to get to know Kallithea in the first place.





The name of the settlement from the Greek language can be translated as "beautiful view." To make sure that Kallithea lives up to its name, the guests of the village have the opportunity to climb to the observation deck located in the center. The famous resort is located on the coast of the Gulf of Toroneo. Travelers staying on the territory of Kallithea can also admire the summit of Mount Athos, weather permitting, to enjoy the view of neighboring Sithonia, which opens from the beaches.

Kallithea is a small village located at a distance of 80 km from the airport of Macedonia, located in Thessaloniki. You can get to this village by regular bus, rented car or taxi. In terms of price, the first option is most accessible.

A bit of history

Kallithea (Halkidiki) is an amazing place whose history goes back centuries. Researchers are still trying to restore it by studying the traces of the presence of ancient civilizations, which are constantly being discovered during archaeological excavations. It is known that for centuries these lands were owned by the monks of the monastery of St. Panteleimon, the exact date of whose foundation remains a mystery. By the way, the temple of the same name annually attracts crowds of people who want to see the ancient shrine. Of great interest to travelers is the church of St. Nicholas.





The construction of the village of Kallithea (Halkidiki) was begun only in 1925. The merit of its foundation belongs to the Greek refugees who arrived here from Asia Minor. Gradually, this place turned into a famous resort, which is famous all over the world for its crystal clear sea and white beaches. Of course, on the territory of Kallithea, a huge number of hotels were erected, ready to accept tourists with various financial capabilities.

Weather

Of course, tourists who dream of a comfortable beach vacation are interested in the weather in Kallithea. Halkidiki is the peninsula that is best visited from May to September. In the holiday season, which opens at the end of April, the average air temperature is +27 degrees. The sea warms up to about +26 degrees. Of course, these indicators can rise even higher, depending on the month.





Kallithea is a settlement whose inhabitants know little about storms, since the hills of Sithonia and Kassandra reliably protect this place from gusts of wind. February, March and November are considered the rainiest months, so you should not plan a vacation for this time. It is known that Kallithea comes to life around mid-spring and plunges into hibernation towards the end of autumn.

Which beaches to choose

Greece, Halkidiki, Kallithea - words familiar to all party people who can not imagine relaxation without an active nightlife. However, the main purpose of visiting the village is still a beach holiday. Tourists who want to combine tanning with admiring natural beauties will love a place called Kriopigi. This beach is located approximately two kilometers from the center of the village. He greets guests not only with emerald-green water, but also with coniferous forest, approaching the sea.





Kriopigi beach is well-equipped, vacationers can rent sunbeds and umbrellas at an affordable price. On its territory is a shaded terrace, from where you can admire amazing views. Cold keys spurting from the seabed will help to freshen up in hot weather, contrast pleasantly with warm sea water.

Where else can tourists who find themselves in such a magical place as Kallithea (Halkidiki) have a rest? The beach of Polychrono is comfortably stretched along the promenade, its length is about two kilometers. There are coastal bars and cafes offering refreshing drinks and masterpieces of local cuisine. It is also possible to rent umbrellas and sunbeds. Of course, water activities are also offered, designed for representatives of various age groups.

Mount Athos

Not all interesting places that tourists can see who have been selected as the resting place of Kallithea (Halkidiki) are listed above. The attractions described above do not compete with the famous Mount Athos, which is located on the Aion Oros peninsula. Here is an international monastic republic, including 20 monasteries. Among the inhabitants of the monasteries you can meet representatives of all regions of the planet.





For the first time Mount Athos is mentioned in the writings of the famous ancient Greek poet Hesiod. The creator pays tribute to her in his work Theogony, describing the battle of the titan Athos with the ruler of the seas Poseidon. Legend has it that Mount Athos became the place where the powerful brother of the thunderer Zeus found his last refuge.

Unfortunately, the fair sex will be able to enjoy the beauties of the holy Mount Athos exclusively from afar, since for over a millennium a law has been in force prohibiting women from visiting this place. Male representatives have the opportunity to step on the holy land. The approximate cost of a sea cruise is 50 euros.

The ancient city of Afitos

The town of Afitos is another mysterious place, thanks to which the village of Kallithea (Halkidiki) attracts tourists , the sights of which are difficult to see in one visit. Travelers can join the excursion group or visit this town on their own, using a taxi or regular bus. Afitos is considered the oldest settlement that can be found on the Halkidiki Peninsula. The approximate date of its foundation is the seventh century before the birth of Christ.





During the reign of the famous Alexander of Macedon, the city reached its highest peak, its inhabitants even minted their own coin. The settlement did not turn out to be abandoned in the era of Roman rule. Unfortunately, the invasion of Turkish soldiers in 1821 led to the partial destruction of the city. However, already in 1830 Afitos was restored.

The ruins of ancient temples

The village of Kallithea (Halkidiki) attracts travelers with other interesting sights. For example, in 1969, an ancient temple dedicated to the thunderer Zeus himself was accidentally found on its territory. One of the main attractions of the village is located directly on the sea coast.

Continuing the excavations, scientists discovered another ancient sanctuary. It was a temple, whose servants worshiped the god Dionysus. The temple is located in a picturesque cave, the approximate date of its founding is the second century BC. The construction of the temple by historians is attributed to the ancient Ebeyans. It is known that it was this civilization that exalted the god Dionysus, as well as some sea deities.
