Fairytale Finland. Lahti - the sports and cultural center of Scandinavia

Finland attracts tourists with interesting sights, developed infrastructure, unusual entertainment programs and picturesque landscapes. Lahti is one of the major cities, which is also the cultural and sports center of the country. It is located near the lakes P√§yanne. Helsinki to Lahti is just an hour away by car. There are three ski jumps in the city that have become its symbol; numerous international competitions are held here.



Archaeological excavations have shown that the city of Lahti has existed for 9300 years. Finland in those days was a inhabited area. The first mention of Lahti dates from 1445, but the status of the city was received only in 1905. Various international festivals are constantly held here, local residents are proud of their orchestra, which has gained world fame. Seminars of poets and writers take place in Lahti.

There are many museums in the city, it is definitely worth a visit dedicated to history, military medicine, radio and television. Informative excursions will allow you to learn more about the culture, traditions and history of such a wonderful country like Finland. Lahti is proud that in 1924 the world's first radio masts were built here. In memory of that great day when the townspeople heard the sound of a radio, a museum was opened.



You can find out about the history of the development of military medicine from the times of World War II to modern times in the thematic museum. The building was opened in 1983, a large number of documents, exhibits and photographs have been collected here. Finland is also interesting for its centuries-old history. Lahti managed to collect many exhibits for the historical museum designed by Hjalmar Aberg in 1895. Initially, it was the estate of August Fellman.

On the shore of the lake is a huge wooden congress hall - Sibelius House. The building hosts not only congress meetings, but also concerts by the symphony orchestra and other important events. One of the most beautiful architectural structures of the city is the Ristinkirkko church, built in 1978 in the Art Nouveau style. For fans of theatrical performances, Finland also provided entertainment programs. Lahti has one of the largest theaters in the country, it seats about 120 thousand spectators. Musicals, dramatic performances, and performances for children are held here daily.



Many will be interested to see the sculptures of Lana in the Cariniemi Park. In this place you can not only enjoy amazing landscapes and get acquainted with rare representatives of the flora, but also look at the sculptures of Professor Olavi Lana - human silhouettes decorated with stones and tree branches. Spend free time near the musical fountain is loved not only by tourists, but also by residents of Lahti (Finland). Every day, splashes of water make a dance to the rhythms of famous melodies from movies and musicals. Many will be interested in visiting the port, because this is one of the most romantic places in the city. It offers a wonderful view of the lake, old iron ships, historical buildings. A trip to Lahti will be remembered for a long time. Tourists who have been here at least once will want to come again and again.
