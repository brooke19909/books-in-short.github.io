To help young craftsmen: how to make a tetrahedron from paper



If you were suddenly given a task in a geometry lesson, or if you yourself had a desire to refresh the properties of volumetric figures, you should definitely read how to make a tetrahedron out of paper, and be sure to put our advice into practice. Indeed, this figure, due to its simplicity, is one of the basic models of three-dimensional modeling.

The word "tetrahedron" itself can be translated from Greek as "tetrahedron". Indeed, the object in question has four triangular faces, as well as four vertices and six edges. There are several subspecies of this figure, but we will not discuss all possible options. In this article, you can learn about how to make paper out of paper.

So how to make a tetrahedron from paper, having all the necessary materials? There are several ways. The first and most common option: cut four separate triangles with the so-called "petals", which are then glued together. You can draw an equilateral triangle yourself or use a ready-made template. The advantages of this method include

the ability to make the figure multi-colored by taking paper in different colors and shades. This will give the product brightness and a more interesting look.

The second method consists in gluing not individual triangles, but already interconnected (the so-called scan of the tetrahedron). In this case, there may also be several options, namely: the petals for gluing can be on all open faces or only on some, those that represent a minimum for reliable fixation.
