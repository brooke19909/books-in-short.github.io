What is interesting Sukko Valley (Anapa)

Sometimes a small resort is called unique, special, because the valley stretches for 7 km from east to west and opens at the seashore with a wide estuary framed by hills. Slopes covered with forests serve as a natural protection from the winds for the village and beaches on the coast. Hundreds of years ago, the climate in the foothills of the Caucasus was more humid, slopes covered with dense forests rich in game. Then the name of the river might appear, meaning "a watering place for wild boars."

There is another version of Sukko's translation - "valley of water." In time immemorial, a powerful mountain stream caused a giant landslide. Natural disaster led to the appearance of Cape Abrau. Much of its origin is not yet clear, the more interesting it is to investigate this ledge, overgrown with relict trees. It is believed that many plant species have survived from preglacial times.





Natural conditions of the valley

The resort village of Sukko is comfortably located on the Abrau Peninsula, which smoothly passes into the mountains of the North Caucasus. Peaks reaching 400 m surrounded the village of Sukko and the valley on three sides. The southern and southwestern slopes are steeper, behind them is a wooded highland. It is here that the river Sukko originates, carrying its waters into the sea along the bottom of the valley. The width of the watercourse is not more than 4 m, and the average depth is about 30 cm. Heavy rains lead to spills, then the water leaves the current channel. There is little rainfall during the beach season; few guests manage to admire the riot of water.

The microclimate of the village of Sukko

In summer, the river valley is not so hot and dry as on the streets of Anapa. Precipitation falls a little more, and the steppe northeast wind blows a little weaker. The airy ocean is saturated with sea salts, steppe and forest smells, warmed by the southern sun. Thickets of juniper and other local plants release medicinal volatile production. The microclimate of the Sukko Valley is characterized by warm and long autumn, short mild winters, favorable summer temperatures without sweltering heat. The water is a little cooler than in Anapa, but its temperature in the summer is also comfortable for swimming, +22 ¬∞ C.

What to see in the vicinity of Sukko

At the beginning of the last century, the peninsula on which the villages of Sukko and Utrish are located was described by the authors of the handbooks as "poetically beautiful, high, wooded shore." Here was the estate of the descendants of an outstanding Russian military leader, Count Loris-Melikov. Centuries passed, and the guests of the Black Sea coast of the Caucasus with invariable pleasure visit the beautiful places that the surroundings of Sukko village are famous for (the valley, the city of Anapa).
