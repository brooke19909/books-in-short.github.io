Greece: Corfu island and its historical heritage

Resorts, incredible history and indescribable beauty - all this is fraught with the country of Greece. The island of Corfu is part of a vast archipelago belonging to this power, and at the same time it is considered a place where the traditions of the leading countries of Europe are mixed. The reason was both history and geopolitics, and even the locals themselves, who never considered themselves purebred Greeks. About why you should definitely visit this mysterious place, what wonders await any tourist there - read in this article.



The famous island of Corfu is located "at the entrance" to the Adriatic Sea, which is why since ancient times it has been the cause of endless disputes between countries that have access to these waters. The original owners of these lands were Greeks, but after they were pressed by the Romans, they were followed by other Italian ethnic groups. In the Middle Ages, both the French and the British dominated here. During the Second World War, the Germans took power, and only in peacetime Greece began to own this region. The island of Korfa, therefore, was able to reunite the traditions and beliefs of all those peoples that once reigned here, and especially imbued with Italian culture. Getting here, every tourist almost immediately becomes convinced that this is far from Greece.



The island of Korfa is a few small towns whose streets are so narrow that, perhaps, only two people will stretch out on them. This region is built up with typically Italian houses and temples.There are chic palaces with massive columns, similar to those that were mainly built in Italy.
