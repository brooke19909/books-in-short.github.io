Origami for beginners: schemes, detailed instructions

It has long been no secret that origami as a certain type of creativity is incredibly popular. With the help of "paper" art, kids begin to develop hand motility, and adults create real masterpieces, a mention of which can even be found in the Guinness Book of Records.

Paper Craftsman

Origami for beginners, the scheme of which we will give later, contains all the basic elements necessary for creativity. If you study them at the very beginning, you can use this technique to create more complex jobs.

The main advantage of this type of creativity is that you only need paper - one of the most familiar materials for us, which in case of an error is not a pity to throw away. And thanks to its plasticity, origami schemes for beginners will help turn white sheets into figures of animals, plants, birds, people.

Origami: color schemes for beginners

Making a flower out of paper is hard. But if you consider that this lesson is designed only for a basic level, then the tulip, which can be used at various holidays, will be an ideal option. Let's get down to this kind of art.



  1. Take a sheet of red paper and fold it diagonally, from one edge to the other. Now put this triangle base down, bend the right side and fold it to the left.
  2. We do the opposite with the left corner. We bend the lower corner of the resulting figure, put it aside to make a stalk.
  3. Again we take a square of paper, but it is already green and fold diagonally.

The scheme of the finished origami is understandable even to beginners, so everyone can repeat it.

Origami Dragon Pattern for Beginners

Now you can try to create a dragon - an origami figure is more complicated. This does not mean that it will take you a lot of time, but more patience and perseverance will be needed for sure. In addition, it is based on all the same techniques that were used in the manufacture of the flower.



  1. Take a square of paper of any color and fold it so that all the corners are bent toward the center. Turn the workpiece over.
  2. Now we determine 1/5 of the half of the square obtained by eye and bend this corner, as shown in the diagram.
  3. Fold the blank in half, and then again, but in a different direction. It turns out a small "pocket", which we need to fold, separating from it two parts: the head and tail. We pull them to the sides, away from the body of the dragon.
  4. We lift up the parts under which the wings are supposed to be, bend their lower corners so that they can easily fold and unfold. You can also optionally pre-fold the wings with an accordion, so that they acquire a "leather-webbed" structure. Origami schemes for beginners significantly simplify the process of perceiving information, which means we get the result faster.
