Denia (Spain). sights

The town itself also boasts its own interesting places. This, of course, the Toy Museum is the attraction that is closest to the train station. It is worth visiting the city churches - especially the Church of the Assumption. They conquer not so much with their antiquity as with the typical Spanish Baroque with his love of suffering and sophistication. The city hall is very beautiful, which even boasts inscriptions from ancient Roman times. But the most important thing is Castello, the former castle (now the Museum of Archeology). Like almost all of Spain, Denia was an ancient Roman territory in the era of antiquity. However, the castle was built by the Arabs, and still in its architecture you can see the remains of the eastern bliss. Notes of luxury and wealth added the Renaissance. Especially characteristic in this regard is the Red Tower.
