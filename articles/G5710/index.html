Cliffs of Moher, Ireland: description, photos and reviews of tourists

Cliffs of Moher (Ireland) are sheer cliffs above the water, on the ocean. They are so picturesque that in order to admire the scenery that opens from them, almost a million tourists come to the country. In 2006, they were recognized as one of Ireland's most popular attractions. This is the most famous natural landscape of the Green Island. We can say that along with Guinness beer these cliffs are one of the recognizable brands with which Ireland is associated. Let's take a virtual trip with you and get to know this natural wonder.



Location

Cliffs of Moher (Ireland) are located in County Clare. This is the west coast of the country. Traditionally, this area is called Burren, and administratively - Liscannor. It is known for the fact that here archaeologists have found many ancient burials. Near the cliffs there are many medieval castles or their ruins. There are also interesting caves for cavers. In the immediate vicinity of the cliffs, in 2007 an original and environmentally friendly tourist center was built. Outwardly, it looks like a round hill covered with green grass. Cliffs of Moher (Ireland): how to get there

To get here, you need to go from Dublin to the north. Will have to cross almost the whole country. And it should be said that this is a considerable segment of the path. For example, if you are in your own or rented car, and we will be counting from the Dublin airport, then the road will take you five hours in both directions. Therefore, you need to move along the highways - M6 and M4. There are paid sections on these roads, so get ready, in addition to gas costs, to spend an extra 15 euros. If you are without your own transport, then there are two options for you. The first is to go express to Galway and catch a tourist bus there, which will take you directly to the cliffs of Moher (Ireland). A photo and an interesting story will be provided to you. The second is to take the tourist bus in Dublin and do the same. In both cases, the price will be approximately the same - about 45 euros.



Geological structure and history

Cliffs of Moher (Ireland) are a cliff. This is a cliff that formed under the action of the surf. Rocks consist mainly of black shales and sandstones. Here you can see sections of different breeds. They were formed three hundred million years ago. Geologists call this time the Upper Carboniferous period. By the way, the shale slabs from these cliffs were used by locals to build hedges around the fields. On them you can still see the prints of fossil creatures. And the current form of the cliff took about six thousand years BC and, as they say, they still remember the first immigrants from the continent. Once there was an Irish fort called "Moher." From him got a name and rocks. But during the wars with Napoleon, the British destroyed this fortress and built a signal tower instead. And another legend connects the cliffs with the suicide of the Druids, who allegedly, not wanting to recognize the victories of Christianity in Ireland, rushed down from the highest cliff.



Description

Cliffs of Moher (Ireland) are an eight-kilometer ridge of rectangular cliffs stretched along the coast. The spread of heights on the cliffs ranges from 120 m on the stone island of Heads Head (Head of the Witch) to 214 m (a point eight kilometers from the O'Brien Tower). The latter is an observation deck, built in the 19th century. Even then, tourists began to visit such an attraction as the cliffs of Moher (Ireland). A description of such travels can be found in memoirs of the eighteenth century, for example, in notes by John Loyd. Sir Cornelius O'Brien (a local nobleman and, incidentally, a descendant of one of the Irish kings) erected this tower in 1835 in order to better contemplate the landscape. They also say that he wanted to impress female visitors. In fine weather, you can see the hills and valleys of Connemara, the Aran Islands and Galway Bay from here. In order to get here, you need to pay a few euros.



Who lives here

Cliffs of Moher (Ireland) are mainly a home for birds. Twenty species live here. A total of ornithologists calculated that about thirty thousand individuals live on the rocks. On the cliffs lives one of the largest populations of rare birds such as the Atlantic puffins. But petrels, loons, gulls, cormorants also feel great here. In 1989, the rocks were declared a conservation area. If you want to watch the birds, come here from April to July. This is just the nesting period. On the paths along the cliffs there are special telescopes that allow you to consider everything in detail. Of plants, those that can cling to cracks in the rocks are common - wild herbs and flowers, mosses, liverworts.
