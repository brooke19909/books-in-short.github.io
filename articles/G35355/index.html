The income of officials. Declared income

The income of officials has always been of interest to ordinary people who are curious about where a deputy has a car worth more than 10 million rubles, given that he receives only 50 thousand a month.

And everything, as usual: the rich get richer, the poor - even poorer. Every time we see officials, we perceive them as corrupt officials. They are not to blame, the system itself, which allows you to take bribes, have "left" money, is to blame.

Moreover, the income statement of officials is not a weapon in the fight against corruption. That's because our smart politicians have long learned to stay clean thanks to their families.

What makes officials fill out an income statement?

For a long time, everyone learned how to design their business for relatives and close friends, this has become the norm for any wealthy person. Why do deputies hide their income?

The main problem of the modern government of the Russian Federation is that almost all the deputies are businessmen or represent the interests of not specific people, but of some specific groups. All this leads to the fact that the real income of officials is hidden from the general public. They do not want the public to know all the information about party financing and business projects.

What are the incomes abroad?

In different countries of the world, the income of officials is different. In some it is at such a level that they are the richest not only within their own country, but also in the whole world. In other states, these are ordinary people with an average level of income. But in any case, the income of Russian officials is at a very high level, especially when compared with the average earnings of ordinary citizens.

And this is not surprising. Indeed, in developed countries, to be an official is simply to work for your state and people. In countries that are developing, being a deputy means having unlimited access to the state feeder.

Poroshenko overtook all political leaders in terms of income

The Ukrainian president Petro Poroshenko earned the most. In 2014, he managed to earn $ 6.49 million in revenue. He earned the lion's share thanks to his business, especially at the expense of the Roshen company, which he promised to sell immediately after the election, if he wins.





Does the presence of business have a bad effect on political activity? It has long been proven that this state of affairs stimulates corruption and the adoption of laws that are necessary to promote their business projects, and not to improve the lives of ordinary citizens.

All other presidents, compared with Petro Poroshenko, earned at least 15 times less.

For comparison, it can be noted that the average Ukrainian salary in 2014 amounted to 3500 hryvnia per month. At today's foreign exchange rate, this amounts to 166.67 US dollars. In one year, the average Ukrainian earned $ 2,000.

What to do?

The income declaration, which officials must fill out, does not justify itself to the extent necessary. It is enough to rewrite your business to a friend - and that's it, it will be very difficult to track business flows.





The declared incomes of officials are not the amount by which you can navigate, trying to make out in this problem. In the fight against corruption, a number of reforms are needed. For example, most civil servants complain about the lack of high salaries, which does not encourage the game to be fair. Because of this, the need arises and the search for ways for additional income. But even the official income of officials over the past five years has grown 4.5 times, judging by their tax returns.

But, it would seem, what does it mean for a person who earns more than 1 million rubles in his business, an addition to his salary? In the total amount of income, the earnings that they are paid from the state budget make up a very small percentage, which is not able to radically change the attitude of an official to his work.

First of all, it is necessary to clearly distinguish between the business and political activities of each deputy. Their work only prevents them from working.

Secondly, it is necessary to toughen the punishment for various manifestations of corruption. Penalties should frighten those who contemplate whether or not to take a bribe. Moreover, there should be not only fines, but also imprisonment.

Thirdly, it is necessary to strengthen public control over the activities of civil servants, to hold open events where people will exchange experiences, share comments.

And most importantly, the average salary of an official should not increase faster than the income level of ordinary citizens of the Russian Federation.
