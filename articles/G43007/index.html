What is the Achilles shield?

Since ancient times, adventurers have been attracted by historical artifacts. Such things possessed beauty and personality, carried the magical properties that endowed their owner. Risking their lives, people went in search of treasures to the farthest corners of the world for the chance to become the owner of the desired instance. One of these artifacts is the Achilles shield, which will be discussed in this article.

What it is?

The description of the shield of Achilles occupies a special place in Homer's work "Iliad", which tells about the last tragic months of the Trojan civilization. One of the most significant figures in the work is Achilles. In the text, a large section is reserved for his shield, which was unique in significance, beauty and mysticism. It was he who was supposed to bring victory to Achilles and his people in a ten-year war with the Trojans. And brought it.





Hero of the Trojan War Achilles (Achilles). Mythology

Achilles was born as a result of the marriage of the king of the Myrmidon Peleus and the sea goddess Thetis.

The goddess knew that fate had prepared her son the opportunity to play an important role in the history of the Greeks in the capture of the city of Troy. That people will remember him for a long time as a brave, courageous, glorious young man, equal in strength to whom he will not be in the world for a long time. But she also knew about the sad end of his life under the walls of Troy.

By all means, the mother tried to protect the child from the inevitable ending. The fetis was rubbed with ambrosia, kept on fire, bathed in the waters of the underground river Styx at night. All these actions promised him immortality and protection, and only the heel of the child, for which the mother held the child above the water, remained defenseless. Subsequently, this will play a fatal role in the life of Achilles.

Achilles was handed over by his father to the centaur Chiron, who fed him with the brains and internal organs of bears and lions.

While still very young, the future hero was killing ferocious animals, in running he was superior to the fastest-legged animals, and he masterfully possessed weapons and healing. His power and strength were unsurpassed. The mother watched the life of her son, rejoiced at his successes and was always there to help Achilles in difficult situations and protect him from any danger. Her help and participation was needed when the young hero was left without his armor during the siege of Troy. The father handed the armor, the spear from Chiron and the immortal horses from Poseidon to Achilles, when he himself could no longer participate in the long-term Trojan war.

Achilles, having cordially provided these things to his friend Patroclus at the battle of Troy, was left without uniforms. Thetis for new armor for her son went up the mountain to the god Hephaestus. He could not refuse the beautiful goddess a request and in one night forged a shield for the hero.



Hephaestus was a blacksmith or artist?

Victory and death

With this shield, the young warrior Achilles brought victory to Troy to his people, as the astrologer prophesied even before the birth of the hero. But here, under the walls of the defeated city, Achilles said goodbye to life in a battle with his enemy Paris. He hit him with a quick arrow in the heel, the same one that was never dipped into the waters of the Styx River. Immobilized Achilles could not dodge the second arrow of the enemy, and she pierced his chest mortally. As the soothsayer promised, Troy was defeated, the hero's age was short-lived, but bright. Legends and songs were composed by people in his honor. Temples and statues were created for the offerings of the laity.

He carried the attraction of glory, power and all the riches of life. For its owner, money and gold flowed like water.

The disappearance of the shield and the discovery of Troy

After the death of Achilles, the shield was taken away by the gods to Olympus, since they believed that people were not worthy of its mystical qualities. Over time, the gods could not share the artifact of such power among themselves, and since then the shield has disappeared forever.

For a long time, historians and archaeologists searched for confirmation of the existence of a Trojan civilization in the past, its hero Achilles and the magic shield, which was second to none. The German archaeologist Heinrich Schliemann managed to find and unearth the ruined city of Troy in 1873. Subsequently, Henry tried to find the grave of Achilles himself, in order to find the famous shield, which gives power, but the search was unsuccessful. The shield was not found. Hitler also made attempts to find this artifact, but the whereabouts of the shield is unknown to this day.
