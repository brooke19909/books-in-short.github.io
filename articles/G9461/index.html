Game History

There is no consensus among poker theorists regarding the origin of this card game. There is no reliable information about which game was the predecessor of poker. Researchers agree that it is a symbiosis of several card games at once.

Some people believe that poker, like most ancient gambling, originated in China in the 10th century AD. Instead of cards, the Chinese used dominoes with a special pattern. Also, the predecessor of poker can be the German game "Pochspiel" or the Indian 96-card game "Ganjifa". The course of the game and the rules of poker have some similarities with these ancient variations.

Most likely, the ancestor of poker in a modern form was the French game of the XV century called "Pogue". In the gameplay, 52 cards were used (as in the modern one) and four jokers. The game included bets and the possibility of bluffing. It was in this form that it was brought by French travelers to Canada, and then to the United States.





She was also fond of the military during the Civil War. However, the progenitor of the modern version of poker, which gave the game its name, is Jonathan Green, who learned in prison how to play poker. He studied the rules thoroughly, as he was a true fan of this gambling pastime.

There are several modern varieties of the game:

Possible player actions

Texas Hold'em poker rules provide for the following actions of players during the bidding process:

1. Bet (bet) - the attacking bet of the player in the absence of bets in front of her.

2. Call (call) - equalization of rates of previous players.

3. Raise, re-raise (raise, re-raise) - raising the rate of previous players.

4. Fold (pass) - an action that indicates discarding cards and refusing to continue the game until the next distribution.

5. Check (check) - a zero bet that transfers the right to move to the next player. A check from all players allows you to see the next card for free.

6. All-in (all-in) - a bet equal to all the chips of the player. It is placed as confirmation of the strength of one's own hand, or if the number of player chips is less than the opponent‚Äôs previous bet.

Why put blinds

The defining feature of poker is the need to make mandatory bets in each hand. This increases the excitement and dynamics of the game as a whole. The following is crucial information for those who want to learn how to play poker. The rules of this game provides for the position of the dealer, which is symbolically indicated by a large chip (button) and moves throughout the game in a clockwise direction. The position of the dealer is the most promising and profitable, since it leaves the player with the right to the last move.





Two players who are after the long loaf have the worst position, since they must make mandatory bets, regardless of the strength of their hands. From their contributions, called blinds, the initial bank is formed.

So, the first player making a mandatory bet is located to the left of the dealer and is called small blind (small blind). This is followed by big blind (big blind) - a player whose mandatory bet is two times higher than that of the small blind. After placing mandatory bets, these two players get complete freedom of action.

Preflop

The first round of bidding, on which bets are placed depending on the strength of the hands handed out and position on the loaf. If the cards are too weak, or the position is disadvantageous, it is advisable to fold the cards. In other cases, you can call, raise or bet all your chips. After this round of bidding, a primary bank is formed.

Flop

On the flop (flop), three community cards are dealt and laid out on the table, which are primary when forming combinations and prospects (draws) for the combination. Seeing this, players make decisions about the strength of their cards (available and possible) and conduct a second round of bidding.

Turn

The next stage, which not all players reach, is the turn. The next community card (already fourth) is put on the table. All players who have not discarded cards in a pass make bets in the same order using the options described previously.

River

At the final stage of bidding, the fifth card, called the river , is laid out on the table . Now all players have fully formed combinations. The last round of bidding is held, which brings clarity to the winner of the distribution.

Showdown and determination of the winner





After the end of the last round of bidding, the winner is determined, who will take the bank for himself. The winner is the player who has collected the best combination of five cards. Players can also divide banks with equal combinations between both two and several players (split-bank).
