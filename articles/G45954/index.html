How much alcohol can be exported from the Czech Republic - features, requirements and recommendations

So, the visit to the Czech Republic came to an end. Suitcases are collected, tickets are bought, it remains for small - to buy something in memory of the Czech Republic. A tourist from Russia (however, like from any other country) will certainly want to bring home 1-2 bottles of real Czech beer or any other alcoholic drink. This raises the logical question of how much alcohol can be exported from the Czech Republic. It's time to get acquainted with some customs rules.

Czech customs

The structure works according to the general rules of the European Union with some amendments to local features. A tourist arriving in the Czech Republic by plane may bring:

  • 200 cigarettes, 250 grams of tobacco or 50 cigars;
  • 0.5 kg of coffee or 100 grams of tea;
  • medicines necessary for personal use;
  • no more than 50 ml of perfume (toilet water no more than 250 ml);
  • foreign and Czech currency (not more than 200 000 CZK).

In the Czech Republic, as in any country of the European Union, it is forbidden to import drugs, ammunition and firearms, wild animals and birds, meat and dairy products (except for baby and diet food), as well as poisonous and explosives.

What can not be exported from the Czech Republic:

  • any items of artistic value;
  • goods of historical or artistic value, as well as antiques;
  • more than 1 liter of spirits or more than 2 liters of wine or beer.

There is a TaxFree system in the Czech Republic. Thanks to it, you can return up to 20% of the cost of purchases at the border, provided that the goods are purchased in stores that support this system. The minimum purchase amount is 100 euros.

How much beer can I take out of the Czech Republic?

This question is asked by every tourist who has visited one of the most beautiful European countries. The taste of real Czech beer is incomparable with any other drink. Therefore, citizens of Russia are doing their best to bring several bottles of alcoholic beverages across the border.

An interesting point: alcohol from the Czech Republic can be exported in unlimited quantities, but alcohol cannot be imported into Russia in barrels. This means that one adult can bring with him a maximum of 5 liters of alcoholic beverages from the Czech Republic.

Russian law does not see the difference between the strength of drinks. This means that you can take out both 3 liters of beer and 3000 ml of Czech becherovka.

Useful Tips

Knowing how much alcohol can be exported from the Czech Republic, it becomes interesting what other restrictions apply at customs. Tourists should be warned about what moments may arise on the Czech border:

  • Bohemian glass and other fragile souvenirs from the Czech Republic should be wrapped with bubble wrap.
  • Before the trip, you should charge all gadgets, as customs officers will ask you to turn on your mobile devices to check if they are a container for transporting prohibited goods.
  • Security officers must check those passengers who are insecure when boarding an airplane. To save yourself from unnecessary verification and not lose precious time, you should behave confidently, do not look around, keep your head straight and not worry.
