Free fall: characteristic of this physical indicator

Free fall is the movement of bodies under the action of gravity. If an individual object falls in the air, then the medium‚Äôs resistance also begins to act on it, so this movement cannot be considered a free fall, which is possible only in a vacuum.



The value that shows the speed of this indicator is called the acceleration of gravity. It is directed vertically downward and is the same for all bodies (regardless of their mass, but in the absence of resistance). This pattern is reflected in the law that was established by Galileo Galilei: all bodies approach the earth with the same acceleration, reaching its surface at the same time if they are not affected by extraneous factors.

To make sure that free fall is characterized by just such regularities is quite easy with the help of a Newton tube (the so-called stroboscopic method). This is a glass tube, the length of which reaches 1 meter. One of its ends is sealed, the crane is placed on the other. If you place a pellet, a cork and a feather in it, and then quickly turn this tube over, you can see a certain feature - all bodies reach the bottom at different times. The pellet will fall first, after it the cork, the feather will be the last. It is worth noting that in this way bodies fall only when there is air in the tube. If it is pumped out with a special sediment, and then the Newton‚Äôs tube is turned over again, then you can make sure that all three objects will fall simultaneously. This is free fall.
