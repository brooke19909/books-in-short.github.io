Beautiful dress for the beach. Preparatory work

A white beach dress is a must for a summer vacation. And if it is also connected from natural material ...

Beautiful dress for the beach. We knit the back and before

So, we knit a white beach dress. To begin work on the back, you need to make a chain of 129 (145) 161 air loops + 3 air lifting loops and knit a pattern consisting of rhombs.

To knit a side bevel, the product must be skipped in turn from two sides in each 6th + 8th row from the initial row 7 x 1 cm.





When 63 (61) 59 cm have been passed from the beginning of the work, you should skip two centimeters for the armhole on both sides and for rounding in each next row 1 x 2 cm and 3 (5) 7 x 1 cm.

When 74 cm have been passed from the beginning of work, it is necessary to skip the middle 6 (9) 12 cm for the neck on both sides and tie both sides separately.

To round off the inner edge, you should skip three centimeters three times in each of the following rows. On those loops that remained (these loops are part of the neck), 78.5 cm from the start of work, you can complete it.

Beautiful dress for the beach. Putting the pieces together

Now a long beach dress, crocheted, is almost ready , because such a product can be knitted both knee-length and ankle-length. First you need to make side seams.

To make the neckline, you will need to knit a chain of air loops: depending on the size - 232 (250) 265 pieces plus one more air lifting loop and knit with pattern No. 2. After the five rows from the beginning of work are connected, knitting can be completed.





Now the seam of the bar is performed and its middle is marked. With the help of the initial row, the placket is sewn in the neck of the front and back of the dress so that the seam of the plank is in the middle of the back and the middle of the placket in the middle of the front. Attention should be paid to the fact that the armholes are the same size.

At the very end of the work, one should tie around the armholes in one circular row of connecting posts, capturing the lower edge of the strap.
