Marneuli (Georgia): is it worth it to go

Marneuli (Georgia) - the district center of Marneuli region. The town is located at a distance of about 30 kilometers south of Tbilisi. From the center of Tbilisi can be reached by minibus. The distance from Marneuli to Bolnisi is 22 kilometers, and from the Sadakhlo border crossing - 33. This is the region of Kvemo Kartli, Georgia. Regular buses from Tbilisi to the Samgori metro station go there from Tbilisi. Ticket price for 2018 is 2 GEL.

Economy

The economy of the town of Marneuli (Georgia) is represented by factories and a haberdashery factory. It produces its own canned food, cheese, butter, reinforced concrete products. You can get to the city by the Tbilisi -Vanadzor railway. There are shops, markets, cinemas, shopping centers.

Tourism

Marneuli in Georgia is not considered a tourist town, as its location does not attract travelers to natural attractions, which can not be said about its surrounding villages. Historically, this city had a different name - Sarvani. It was renamed in 1952. The Algeti River flows near the town. Nearby is the Khrami River. It was the point of the Georgian offensive in the 1918 Armenian war. In 2008, the airfield, which survived the bombing, became a modern historical monument.

Another attraction of the city was located a little north. This is the Marabdin field and a memorial to those who died in the battle of Marabdin in 1925, which took place between Georgia and Persia. This place is famous in history as the place of death of Teymuraz Mukhranbatoni, the brothers Khekheulidze and 9 standard bearers. Architecture

Architectural monuments in Marneuli are few in number. Sights as if hidden in the area. Here, near the village of Akhkerpi, an ancient Armenian temple of the 12th century is located. There is also a monastery of the same time built. And in the village of Tsopi is a castle. A special celebrity is the village of Sadakhlo. What else to see?

Tourists will be interested in excursions to places of the Georgian-Armenian war of 1918. Those who like to plunge into the depths of centuries will be curious to visit the village of Shulaveri, (now Shaumyani). It remained in history as a place of fighting on December 25-28, 1918. And in 1921 there were fights of the Soviet 11th army. Near the city of Marneuli is a small ridge of Yalguja. They were going to carry out the bombing of Tbilisi from him during the distant battle for the city.

The era of the Middle Ages did not leave its mark in this city. However, in the X-XI centuries, this land historically belonged to the Armenian kingdom of Tashir-Dzoraget. Since then, the fortress of Gaga has been preserved.

In the town of Marneuli (Georgia) you can get on two routes. They are both strategically important. One to Armenia from the Sadakhlo checkpoint, the other to Azerbaijan from the Red Bridge checkpoint.
