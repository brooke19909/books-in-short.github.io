Cheboksary, the capital of the Chuvash Republic, is a very beautiful city. He attracts tourists not only with interesting places and sights for history, but also with the natural beauty of the city. Not every modern urbanized capital in our country can afford this wealth (and be proud of it).

Parks and squares in Cheboksary

On 250 square kilometers of Cheboksary there are several dozen large green parks, small but beautiful squares, beautiful alleys, gardens and even several natural groves. All this pleases the gaze of local residents and surprises guests of the capital. Parks and squares are special places for recreation, walks and pastime in the fresh air. They remind of proximity to nature and allow you to feel this natural connection. Today we will talk about one of the parks of the capital, which was created in honor of it.



Park named after the 500th anniversary of Cheboksary

In 1969, when the capital celebrated its 500th anniversary of the appearance on the pages of history, on the Volga coast, next to the Chernyshevsky ravine, the laying of a new park began. However, its discovery dragged on for almost a decade.

Park named after the 500th anniversary of Cheboksary as a branch of the Lakreevsky forest

In 1957, the Lakreevsky Forest park was created on the basis of a real natural oak grove, but it was called differently - the City Park named after the Great October Fortieth. However, very soon it began to be called as it was before, simply - Lakreevsky forest. The fact is that earlier (in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries), on the site of the modern forest, a huge oak forest stretched far. All these possessions belonged to one landowner - Fedor Andreevich Lakreev-Panov. That's where the name came from. In the future, from this rich oak forest there was only a small massif, on the basis of which the eponymous park appeared.

The Rose of the World (2008)

An unusual architectural composition with meaning was established in the park in 2008. This is the "Rose of the world." It is dedicated to the unity of peoples and cultures. This is a huge monument, at the bottom of which, at the bottom, is an octahedron with carved sayings about peace, love, kindness and friendship. Future plans



In the coming years they want to modify this place: to expand and complement. It is assumed that on the basis of this green zone there will be an ethnocomplex called "Amazonia". According to one of the projects, this complex includes a water park, an aquarium, the Smesharik Land entertainment center, a sports complex and a yacht club. Plans for the future are quite grandiose, as many are difficult to achieve for a number of reasons. It is worth noting once again that the plan project is still under consideration and under development. Whatever the fate of the park, I would like it to perform not only (or even not so much) entertaining functions, but also not lose its natural connection with nature.
