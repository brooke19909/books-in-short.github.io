Pattern of a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics: step-by-step instructions, diagram and features

Artistic gymnasts, for whom sport is a matter of life, devote the lion's share of time to training and participating in competitions. During this period, they manage to demolish more than a dozen gymnastic swimsuits. Therefore, the mothers of young athletes are constantly faced with the problem of acquiring “work clothes”. And if the training form is more or less clear (you can buy it at a sports store), then the outfits for competitions are a “star” task. And you can solve it by sewing the right outfit with your own hands.





Where to begin?

This will help the pattern of a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics. But before you do it, you should buy the right material and thread (nylon will do). Usually for sewing such outfits choose a supplex or mesh, but polyamide can be used in combination with lycra and cotton. Let us consider in more detail how to build a leotard pattern for rhythmic gymnastics.

First, we decide on the sketch of the suit (this is an important point, since there are rules approved by the technical committee for their design and cut).

Features

So, the pattern of a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics must comply with the following nuances:

  1. Correct design, transparency of the swimsuit material from neck to chest is not allowed.
  2. Gymnastic swimsuits with thin straps are prohibited.
  3. On the chest, the maximum depth of the cut should not be lower than the middle of the chest, on the back - the end of the shoulder blades.
  4. The lower part of the swimsuit cannot be cut out, above the inguinal folds.
  5. The leotard for rhythmic gymnastics should be tailored along the contours of the body, not a fluffy skirt is allowed.
  6. Costumes should be the same length on both sides covering the legs, but different patterns or patterns can be used.

Of course, you can simplify your task, because on the Internet for a small amount there is always a basic ready-made pattern for a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics. But, if you still decide to go all the way yourself, detailed instructions on cutting and sewing will help. The advice of specialists will not be superfluous.





Advice

In order for the swimsuit to sit properly after the cut, you should very carefully take measurements and build a pattern that exactly matches the size.

Moreover:

  • The sports leotard is sewn without tucks and only with the main seams, since they (albeit slightly) hamper the movement, which is not very convenient for gymnasts.
  • It is better if the base is solid, then it’s convenient to sew a skirt on top of it and adjust it higher or lower for a more favorable fit.
  • The cutout for the sleeve should fit tightly under the armpit.
  • The skirt is best done asymmetric (longer in the back than in the front).
  • A thoughtful stylish design in itself looks expensive and profitable, and then you need to decorate the product at a minimum, which will save on consumables.




Pattern of a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics: step by step instructions

Any pattern begins with measurements. So, measure and record: chest, waist, sleeve length.

  1. The next step is to build a preliminary pattern of the pattern on paper. To do this, you need to take the most comfortable panties of your young athlete and circle them on a contour on paper from two sides, slightly increasing the cutout on the hips. This is done so that during the performances nothing would hamper the movements of the gymnast.
  2. The daughter’s old t-shirt will help in creating the upper part of the swimsuit. It must be placed next to the panty pattern on paper and likewise outlined (taking into account the proportionality of the two parts of the swimsuit). T-shirt sleeves are extended to the desired size along the side seam.

The resulting pattern is cut out and applied to the fabric. Adding 2 cm to the allowances, we cut from it separate halves of a swimsuit. In order not to spoil the knitwear, it is better to circle the pattern with chalk.





Final stage

We try on the “draft” of the bathing suit on our daughter (having previously swept it off) and mark the conceived depth of the neck with chalk, measure the length of her circumference and cut out the appropriate strips for cutting (width - about 5 cm).

Further, the pattern of a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics involves the processing of the side, lower and shoulder seams. The next step is to grind the neck. It is carried out by stitching to the cutout of a previously cut strip of knitted material (both parts of the fabric should face each other). The rolled-up strip should be swept to the product, then stitching (front side).

Then follows the decoration of the bottom of the panties and the sleeve of the gymnastic leotard. At this stage, it is important not to forget to insert a cotton gusset. The final stage is the design of the waist line with an elastic band so that it moves freely and does not hinder movements.

Leotard pattern for rhythmic gymnastics, step by step the creation instruction of which is given above can be done in other ways.





Consider another simple option.

Sequence of steps

  1. Creating a sketch of a swimsuit with marking details, the arrangement of sequins and jewelry.
  2. The construction of the pattern of all fragments of the product from the grid (back, front and two lower parts). It is very important to remember the seam allowances when cutting parts.
  3. Cut the same details, but directly from the fabric, which should be stretching and elastic (stretch, lycra, supplex).
  4. Marking fragments of fabric and mesh, their stitching on a sewing machine.

Decoration and decoration

Building a leotard pattern for rhythmic gymnastics is the basis for creating any costume for competitions. Ornaments make it original and corresponding to the theme of the performance. There are several ways to make a gymnastics leotard unique:

  1. Sequins and rhinestones - pebbles with special glue are applied to a previously thought out drawing on a suit.
  2. Painting on the fabric of the gymnastic leotard is performed before stitching fragments of the leotard.
  3. Acrylic paint is also able to make the outfit for performances unique. Pre-diluted to a liquid state, the paint is applied with a brush to wet material (it is better to first practice on an unnecessary piece of fabric). To avoid the spread of paint, it must immediately be dried with a hairdryer. The advantage of this method is that if the drawing is unsuccessful, the swimsuit can be washed and re-drawn.

To make the acrylic firmly fixed, they pass through a cloth with a hot iron through a cloth (but only after the paint has dried).





Summing up, it can be noted that the pattern of a leotard for rhythmic gymnastics, created by oneself, is a task for the loving mother. And skillful hands, imagination and a natural taste will help with sewing and finishing the outfit so that your daughter will make an indelible impression on the audience and judges at the competitions.
