Victory Park on Poklonnaya Hill. Moscow Victory Park. Poklonnaya Gora: how to get there?

Today, Victory Park on Poklonnaya Hill has become a magnificent architectural monument with an emphasized solemn atmosphere that embodies the spirit of heroic patriotism of the participants of the last world war.



Amazing in its beauty and scale, the park has become a favorite vacation spot, captivating and winning the hearts of its visitors.

"Cases of bygone days ..."

For several centuries, the gentle slope on which this park is located has been called Poklonnaya Gora. From these heights, travelers still looked at Moscow and its environs in the sixteenth century, bowing to her and her inhabitants (hence the name of this historical place). Visitors were fascinated by the city panorama, local beauties and, bowing, left gifts as a sign of respect. However, according to another version, a bow (even in feudal times) was a tax that is mandatory for all travelers entering the city.

The Last Frontier

And several centuries later, historical documents testify to how Napoleon stood there, looking forward to the keys to the gates of the capital and the triumphal celebration of victory, but ... his hopes were vain.



It was Poklonnaya Gora that became the last frontier to play a crucial role in those turbulent times.

Memory of contemporaries

During the Great Patriotic War, bypassing Poklonnaya Gora, the defenders of the Motherland went to the front. Already in the second year of the war, it was at this place that it was proposed to implement a draft memorial dedicated to the future victory over the invaders. But the implementation of such a project at that time was completely impossible. A return to the previously unfulfilled plan occurred in 1958, when the Moscow Victory Park was laid, planting trees on its territory and installing a commemorative granite sign.



On the 9th anniversary of the victory over the Nazi invaders, on May 9, 1995, the grand opening of the stunning memorial complex, including a park and museum, monuments and religious churches, the magnificent square and the Eternal Flame, as well as other interesting monumental and historical objects, was timed.

Poklonnaya Gora: how to get there?





Ceremoniously meeting visitors, invariably pleases with a variety of concerts and performances, open-air exhibitions and attractions, the hospitable Poklonnaya Gora.

How to get to this place by metro? The closest thing is to go from the Kutuzovskaya metro station; A little more time (within 10 minutes) will take the road from Victory Park.

From the Bagrationovskaya station from the city center, several public transport stops should be taken along Kutuzovsky Prospekt, and from Kievskaya in the direction of the park you can get by bus or taxi.

Moreover, the exposition of this open-air museum is constantly being updated, introducing enthusiastic euphoria, especially in children's souls.





Museum relics

The Central Museum of the Great Patriotic War deserves special attention, in the special display cases of which the Memory Book, consisting of 385 volumes, is stored. On its pages the names of people who died during the Nazi invasion are captured. One of the most significant relics of those years is the victorious banner, hoisted over the defeated Berlin Reichstag on April 30, 1945. Museum funds carefully store a huge number of handwritten and photographic documents, samples of fine art, household items and other certificates that reliably tell about the most difficult years for our country.

Active extreme vacation





Poklonnaya Gora Park is best suited for skating, rollerblading, skateboards and bicycles thanks to breathtaking slopes, ascents and numerous steps. Their extreme skill attracts a myriad of onlookers, enthusiastically watching the incomprehensible tricks comparable to circus art.

Some of the outside observers subsequently themselves become involved in such an active and impressive form of recreation, which is favored by rental points for sports equipment, a sufficient amount of which is available in the park. Children's playgrounds and swings, an amusement park and a virtual cinema attracts young tomboys Poklonnaya Gora. Moscow cordially invites people interested in history on an exciting tour by road train. A study tour, accompanied by an electronic commentator, summarizing a lot of interesting facts, leaves a pretty good impression.

Variety of activities





It is quite understandable why the annual events on Poklonnaya Hill dedicated to Victory Day make such an indelible impression on the young generation and, to some extent, raise the morale of veterans of the past war. In a festive atmosphere, an equestrian parade takes place, and at the end of it - a concert of symphonic music and contemporary domestic artists. After completion of the planned events, a traditional salute blooms.

In addition to this grand celebration, various informational, cultural and charity events take place in the park area.



Of particular interest are sporting events, many demonstration tournaments on specially constructed sports grounds. Everyone can participate in exciting master classes conducted by eminent Russian athletes, as well as just check their own physical fitness and its compliance with the standards of the TRP.

Student celebrations

In early September, Victory Park on Poklonnaya Hill meets freshmen and conducts their initiation into students. As part of this action, the Oath is given and the anthem of Moscow students is performed, and then a grand procession along the streets of the capital and incendiary music events are held.

Annually, over 40 thousand Moscow freshmen take part in the solemn parade dedication. In addition, Victory Park regularly hosts weddings, and you can organize private and business events.





The well-groomed flower beds and the elegance of lawns in the best way affect the popularity of this park, attracting aesthetes. In general, it should be borne in mind that it is impossible to take a week to see all the park sights and take part in various solemnly entertaining and sporting events.
