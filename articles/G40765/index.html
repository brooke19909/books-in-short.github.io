General information about the capital of Catalonia

Barcelona is not only the largest metropolis of the country, but also a large Mediterranean port. Catalonia itself is part of the Spanish kingdom. However, this does not prevent local residents from advocating their own independent state. Today, Barcelona attracts thousands of tourists due to its huge historical heritage. The rich history of the region, excellent cuisine, unique sights and special atmosphere of the city make the trip to Barcelona a very bright and unforgettable event. From an administrative point of view, the capital of Catalonia is divided into ten large areas. The most popular among tourists are the Old Town, Eixample, Gracia and Montjuic.

Barcelona - port city

The capital of Catalonia - Barcelona also has the status of the sea capital of all of Spain. The city's port has been over two thousand years old. It consists of several parts, and so the cruise part takes the fourth place in the world and is inferior in size to the largest ports of the Caribbean.

Climate

The climate of the capital of Catalonia can be described as Mediterranean. In general, it is quite mild in the winter and warm in the summer. The best time for a vacation in Barcelona and excursions is summer. At this time, there is no suffocating heat and no rain. But in the fall it becomes quite wet.

Beaches

The capital of Catalonia is known not only for its attractions, but also for beaches that stretch over 4 kilometers. The beach area consists of four parts. The most popular is Barceloneta, which is located in the very center of Barcelona. It is not only tourists who like to spend time here, but also the townspeople. A little north, a ten-minute walk along the promenade are beach clubs that offer additional services.



Barcelona also has the beaches of Marbella and Ikaria. The most distant from Sitges, the road to it from the station takes half an hour.

Attractions of the capital of Catalonia

There are a lot of attractions in Barcelona. It is rightly called the cultural capital of the country. Here once lived and created such celebrities as Salvador Dali, Gaudi, Picasso. All sorts of eras and styles mixed in the city. And there are so many attractions here that it is simply impossible to see them all in one vacation.

Gothic Quarter

One of the main items on the list of interesting places in Barcelona is the Rambla. Its length from the square of Catalonia to the coast of the sea is 1.2 kilometers. The most famous pedestrian street of the city is at the same time one of the most beautiful places in the city. There are always crowds of holidaymakers here, and along the shops and cafes are street vendors and living statues. On the Ramble there is an old market called Boqueria, which translates only as an abyss. Passing by this picturesque place is simply impossible, because the bright counters simply lure tourists to themselves. Many beautiful legends and signs will be associated with the Rambla Boulevard. One of them says that everyone who gets drunk from the fountain, which is located near Plaza Catalunya, will certainly return to Barcelona again.



Near the Rambla Boulevard is the center of the Old Town, which is known as the Gothic Quarter. It represents the intricacies of medieval streets and alleys. It was from here that the history of Barcelona once began. In the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Old Town, a record number of ancient buildings and churches are concentrated. This is the church of Santa Maria del Mar, the Cathedral and much more.

Many buildings of the quarter are dated XIV-XV centuries. And at the same time, on its streets there are a large number of cozy modern cafes, restaurants and clubs. The entire Gothic quarter is a monument of the Roman era.

Its main building can rightfully be called the Cathedral of the Holy Cross and Holy Eulalia. In the people it is better known as the Cathedral, which is a very high structure. Its longest spire rises 93 meters. The building is both elegant and massive, it is made in the style of Catalan Gothic architecture with multi-level openwork arches and ornaments.

No less interesting for tourists is the central square of the quarter, named after St. James. It is located on the site of the Roman Forum. Today it houses the neoclassical city hall and the Palace of the Government of Catalonia. On Sundays, residents come to San Jaume to dance, and all kinds of events are held here during the holidays.



The hallmark of the historic center is the famous Bridge of Sighs, which is known far beyond the capital of Catalonia (photos are given in the article). It was built in the early twentieth century by Jean Rubio. The purpose of its construction was to unite the Palace of Government and the residence of the President of Catalonia. The bridge is next to the Cathedral.

Museum of History

Among all regions of Spain, the capital of Catalonia is the most interesting and popular among tourists. It is Barcelona that is visited annually by the largest number of travelers; no other city in the country enjoys such popularity among vacationers. This is largely due to cultural and historical monuments. To learn more about this beautiful city, you should visit the Museum of the History of Barcelona. The exposition of the institution opens up an opportunity for visitors to see the historical panorama, covering the period from the time of the Roman Empire to the present. The main building of the institution is located in the palace of the fifteenth century. The glass windows of the museum contain unique artifacts that have survived to this day or were found during excavations.

It is also worth visiting the Museum of Frederic Mares, where you can see a large collection of sculptures from the ancient Roman period to the twentieth century.



The cultural heritage of the city consists not only of ancient attractions. In the art gallery you can see classic works familiar to our eyes. To this day, followers of Pablo Picasso and Salvador Dali live and create in Barcelona. In the halls of the gallery are collected original works of contemporaries, and in the courtyard of the building unusual sculptures are presented.

Jewish quarter

In the thirteenth century, many Jews lived in Barcelona who successfully conduct business and flourish. The Jewish community was the richest in the whole city. But the situation has changed a lot after some two hundred years. All Jews were expelled from the city in general hysteria associated with the terrible disease that at that time consumed all of Europe. Currently, in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Gothic Quarter, you can see the small district of El Col, which once lived the Jewish community. The local streets still have a medieval spirit. In the museum you can learn a lot about the creative path of the founder of cubism, as well as see his work created in different creative periods. Tibidabo Hill

In Barcelona you can find many picturesque places, however, if you want to admire the panorama of the city from a decent height, then you simply can not find a better place than Tibidabo hill. Here you can have a great time walking along the slopes covered with forests, as well as stop by the Museum of Natural Science or try to find a way out of the landscape maze. The main pearl of the hill, located at the very top is the Temple of the Sacred Heart.

Spanish village

All tourists will certainly go to the hill of Montjuic. Translated, its name sounds like a Jewish mountain. The hill is a favorite place for walking for all residents and tourists. In this area there are many interesting sights, the inspection of which will take at least one day. For many centuries, the hill was considered a strategic object, which is confirmed by an ancient fortress built on the very top. In anticipation of the 1992 Olympics, sports facilities were also built on the hill. Here you can see the Spanish village. Skillful architects in a small area managed to build copies of buildings located in various parts of the country. Here you can admire the castle gates from Avila, buildings from Aragon and Castile, as well as Andalusian, Valencia, Basque and other thematic neighborhoods.
