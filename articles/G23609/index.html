Severny village in Moscow: history, description, photo

Little is known about this unique place in the capital. Meanwhile, in the village of Severny in Moscow, located twenty-two kilometers from the Kremlin, you can not hear the rumble of cars. The air here is cleaner than in other metropolitan areas, there are a lot of flowers and greenery, and squirrels live in the park, which the inhabitants feed with pleasure. People here have known each other since childhood.





Severny Village (Moscow): Acquaintance

A cozy urban settlement is located on the MKAD line. The direction of the village of Severny is along Dmitrovskoye Shosse. The area has a highly developed infrastructure (the presence of cozy playgrounds, large shops and healthcare facilities creates additional comfort for residents). The construction of the North began in the 30s of the last century. Today, the village is a modern place of residence, comfortable for people of all ages, which can be safely classified as a satellite city. A map of the village of Severny is presented later in the article.





History

His heart is the Northern Waterworks (today it is called a water treatment plant). The facility is located in the northern part of the district. For the station‚Äôs employees, from 1948 to 1956 a working village was built, equipped with full infrastructure, which ensured its autonomous existence. The decision to start the construction of a water treatment station was made in 1939, but it did not begin before the Great Patriotic War. The station started operating in 1952.

Birth

The date of birth of the village is considered June 12, 1952. On this day, by a government decree, the village was given its current name. Residents of the village of Severny (Moscow) have always used Moscow registration. They proudly called their place of residence "a piece of Moscow in the Moscow region.", Due to the fact that on all sides Severny is surrounded by the lands of the capital and its environs.

The village became part of Moscow in 1985, at which time its territorial unification with Moscow took place: the Moscow Region lands located between Severny and MKAD were connected with it. The village became the main modern district of the same name in the capital.

In 1991, as the interim municipal administrative district of Severny, it became part of the newly created Northeastern AO, in 1995 it received the status of one of the metropolitan areas. Until the middle of 2000, Severny continued to be considered a separate urban settlement.

Territory, infrastructure

The territory of the village of Severny (Moscow) can not be called too large. It can be walked around the perimeter, enjoying nature, the scent of flowers, birdsong, heady fresh air, in just an hour and a half.

During the walk, attention is drawn to architectural structures of amazing beauty, which can not be found in other metropolitan areas, built up with "typical" buildings similar to each other.

In the construction, the architectural style of the so-called Stalinist neoclassicism. Dwelling houses, as a rule, on two floors, are popularly called "cottages." Their construction began in the 50s and was carried out using German projects. The center of cultural and creative life of the village was the House of Culture, erected in 1953, its doors are still hospitably open for northerners.

The infrastructure of the village consists of shopping centers, clinics, kindergartens, schools, hotels, an experimental complex of higher sports.

Cottage Village North: architecture

The village is built up with 2-storey residential buildings, each of which has four apartments.





Basically, the architecture of the cottage village of Severny attracts two types of buildings: some of them provide for the presence of a pediment and rounded windows on the ground floor, others are made in a more simple style, with fewer decorative elements. Both types of structures alternate, which eliminates the uniformity of the appearance of the settlement.





Streets

In the village there are nine street-lines. Each of them is called: 1st, 2nd, ... - 9th Northern line. Until 1985, the streets were called: 1st, 2nd, ... - 9th line of the village. Northern. Until 1994 - 1st, 2nd, ... - 9th line of the North. water station. The central axis of the village is formed by the 3rd Northern line, stretching from the through water supply. station to the house of culture.

Designers

Severny settlement (Moscow) was designed by architects Z. Gosudareva, K. Kislova, N. Selivanova. Most of the administrative and public buildings, as well as residential buildings in the village, were designed by N. N. Selivanov, who is the author of projects for managing the SHS, the canteen, and the fire station.

Settlement House of Culture, located on the 3rd North. lines in d. No. 17, designed by Z.O. Ford. The former school, now a kindergarten (d. No. 15 along the 3rd Northern line), was built according to the standard project of A.P. Velikanova and N. M. Vavirovsky.

Building

Actively built up the village began in the 2000s. As of December 2011, multi-storey residential districts were erected: the 1st, 4th and 9th, as well as the 2nd microdistrict, consisting of cottage villages Arkhangelskoye-Tyurikovo, Sev. settlement and Sev. settlement-2.

In addition, a bypass road was opened, the task of which was to divert traffic from the intersection of the Moscow Ring Road and Dmitrovskoye Shosse. In oct. In 2016, the road was named Acad. Flerova.

Conclusion

The population of the village consists of 16 thousand people. Residents are proud that everyone in the North can feel "among their own."



Near the northern border of the village, it is planned to build highway No. 4966. Not so long ago, entry to the North was opened on Dmitrovskoye Shosse. The village with its convenient access roads, well-developed infrastructure, communications is the real pride of the Moscow region.
