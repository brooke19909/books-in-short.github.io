Visa regime

Vietnam is quite loyal to tourists from Russia, however, to stay in this country for more than fifteen days, our fellow citizens will have to issue an invitation to enter. Upon arrival, to pass customs control, you must submit a passport with two photos. At the same time, cash must be on hand to pay the fee. If tourists need a single-entry visa for a period of thirty days, then they will have to pay twenty-five to the treasury, and those who plan to visit the country again will have to pay thirty dollars. But not only upon arrival, you can get permission to enter. A visa to Vietnam can be issued in Russia, in the embassy of this country. But then the cost of a visa increases slightly: it is fifty-five dollars with a deadline of up to seven business days.

Hotels

Nha Trang resort is primarily chosen by locals. Apparently, this explains the fact that along the entire coast of the city there are numerous mini-hotels, most of which are quite simple and work mainly only in summer. They provide a fairly decent service for a fairly low price.

In addition, the resort can be very inexpensive to live in motels and boarding houses, the cost per night in which sometimes is even less than twenty dollars. But at the same time they all have a private bathroom in the rooms, air conditioning, TV and even a refrigerator. Most even have Wi-Fi: if not in rooms, then in public areas.

For those who prefer comfort on vacation, local luxury hotels, for example, Ana Mandara, etc. are quite suitable. This is the most famous fashionable hotel chain in Nha Trang. In general, along the coast of the resort there are many luxurious apartments with SPA centers and chic restaurants.

Budget hotels are mainly located in a relatively quiet side street on the second line from the beach, not far from the tourist center. They are quite popular among Russians. Olympic Hotel (Nha Trang) 3 * is no exception.

Excursions

To come to Nha Trang and not see the local attractions will be simply unforgivable. Many of them adorn the covers of glossy calendars and magazines. The sights of this Vietnamese resort are visited every year by an army of tourists coming from all over the world. Some of them are objects of cultural heritage, and therefore are mandatory points in the programs of any tour in Vietnam.

In general, excursions in Nha Trang are in high demand among tourists, therefore this industry in the former fishing village, and now the recreation center, is very well developed.

sights

One of the most famous is a magnificent monument of Buddhist philosophy - Longshon Pagoda, founded in the nineteenth century. Behind her is a huge snow-white statue of Buddha sitting on a lotus flower. This massive sculpture is visible from almost anywhere in Nha Trang. Near the fourteen-meter-high Buddha figure, there is a viewing platform with magnificent views of the resort itself and its surroundings: the whole city is seen by tourists at a glance.

Another attraction of Nha Trang are the Tyam towers, or Po Nagar. This is one of the oldest urban buildings in the region, attracting thousands of tourists every year.

Not so long ago a mud bath was opened in this Vietnamese tourist center, in the territory of which the hot springs famous in the country of Thakpa are located. Specialists of this medical complex offer such procedures as special coating, bathing directly in hot springs or in special tubs, as well as washing and rubbing with mud.

Here are two lakes - for adults and for children. The complex itself is located on the picturesque bank of the medium-sized Kai River. The therapeutic baths are based on the effect obtained from a mud mass of inorganic origin. It is obtained directly from the source and is very effective for the treatment of diseases of the endocrine and nervous system and musculoskeletal system.
