Characteristic (briefly)

The length of the strait is about 75 km, the width varies from the south side about 20 km, but from the north about 43 km. In this water area, great depths are observed. The following capes are located on the shores of the bay: Ivanovsky, Alekhine, Znamenka. Many islands flow from the islands into the strait. It is difficult to call the Kunashir Strait habitable. Functioning settlements are currently present mainly on the Japanese side. These are the villages of Sibetsu and Raus. There are also small settlements on the island of Hokkaido. On the Russian coast of the strait, on the island of Kunashir, there is only a small village Golovino. The population in it is very small, about 100 people.



Strait Features

The Kunashir Strait, like most waters of the Kuril ridge, is a flooded saddle between volcanic cones (islands). It is located in close proximity to the active Golovin volcano, which is located in the very south of Kunashir Island. In the local water area, strong tidal currents are often observed. Their average value varies within 1 m.

Climate

One of the warm currents of the Sea of Japan , Soya, passes through the strait; therefore, winter is somewhat warmer here than directly on the Pacific coast. Although in winter, due to the cold East Sakhalin current, the Kunashir Strait is filled with ice.

Animal world

The Kunashir Strait and adjacent territories are the habitat of some species of seals (Steller Sea Lions). Sea beavers, dolphins, minke whales and killer whales live here. In this area you can find Pacific cod, herring, capelin, and pollock.
