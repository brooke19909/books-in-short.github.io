One of the oldest Victorian buildings

One of the oldest Victorian buildings in Manchester is the Royal Exchange Theater. The Theater itself was opened only in 1976, but the building in which it is located is ancient and incredibly beautiful. Manchester, the sights of which we are considering, at one time was one of the centers of the cotton trade. In 1792, the first exchange was opened in the city. It has been functioning for eight years. In 1809, the building of the new exchange was built. In 1849, thanks to the reconstruction, it expanded. Then Manchester was nicknamed "Cottonpolis," since the prosperity of the cotton trade fell at that time.

From 1867 to 1874 the construction of the third exchange was carried out. It was one of the largest trading floors in the UK. The bombing that took place during World War II substantially destroyed the Royal Exchange Theater. But gradually the building was rebuilt, however, the large trading hall is now not at all big.
