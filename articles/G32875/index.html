Loud start

The first book that immediately brought the scientist fame among experts and the general public was the Selfish Gene published in 1976. Richard Dawkins proclaimed in this work the vitality of Darwinian ideas of evolution. It complements and develops them with achievements from the most progressive areas of biology - genetics and ethology. With a name that is slightly provocative, the author also emphasized the role of the gene as the main object of evolution, and not of the individual or the entire population. The radicality of the new vision was that organisms seemed only a vehicle for genes seeking to preserve themselves in new generations.



He takes evolutionary theory further beyond genetics. It introduces the concept of the behavioral equivalent of a gene called a meme. The example given in the book concerned song motifs passed down from generation to generation by some species of songbirds.
