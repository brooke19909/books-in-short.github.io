The concept of temperament

The problem that will be discussed, humanity has been interested in more than 25 centuries. In the V century. BC e. in Greece, the doctrine of the types of temperament. It argued that health is determined by a combination of various fluids that make up the human body.

Each liquid has certain properties and purpose. With their disproportionate mixing, the development of various diseases is possible. This teaching has dominated science for more than two millennia. It was used to identify individual characteristics of both healthy and sick people.

Based on such a theory, it is possible to identify a typology of temperaments. Claudius Galen (II century BC) successfully coped with a similar task. He suggested that there is a connection between the amount of human fluids with its certain psychological properties and the specific behavior.

Galen identified four basic types of temperament, psychologists use them today.

Scientific point of view on temperament

Gradually, this theory ceases to be relevant, there are versions of alchemists about the connection of temperament with mercury, sulfur, salts. There were also such theories, supporters of which determined the differences between people by mixing in the body of ether and air. Russian teacher and biologist P.F. Lesgaft suggested that human temperament is directly related to the structure of blood vessels, their diameter, thickness and elasticity of the walls.

A common drawback of all theories was that they were one-sided, their creators took into account only one direction of the body's work and did not prove their studies by experiment.
