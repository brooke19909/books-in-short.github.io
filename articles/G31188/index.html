Where to go in Samara? Tips & Tricks

Where to go in Samara? Probably, every inhabitant of this amazing city will be perplexed upon hearing such a very unusual question. What is so strange about him? The fact is that if you only find yourself in Samara, you will immediately understand that you can endlessly walk, wander and admire this settlement. This is a wonderful modern city with a developed tourist infrastructure. Rest, entertainment and educational excursions - each tourist has the opportunity to get the most vivid and unforgettable experience.

Section 1. Where to go in Samara? General information about the city



The official year of foundation of Samara is considered to be 1586. Why exactly this period of time? Yes, because the construction of a fortress by order of Tsar Fedor Ioannovich belongs to this period. The construction of this structure was necessary to protect the country in the south. As you know, Samara is a fairly large settlement of the Russian Federation, located in the Middle Volga. Thanks to the vast territory, it can be easily viewed not only on the map of our country, but also on a medium-sized globe. By the way, today more than 1 million people live here permanently.

In addition, it should be noted that the Samara buildings, built in different eras, now form a unique architectural appearance of the entire settlement.

Section 2. Where to go in Samara? Local Attractions

A city that captivates from the first minutes of acquaintance is, of course, Samara. Where to go on the weekend or what to see first, if fate has thrown you here for only a few days? Let's try to advise.

Invariably attracts the attention of tourists Orthodox convent Iversky. Its foundation as a community of sisters of mercy is 1850. Before the revolutionary events, the monastery developed, and therefore by 1917 there were already 4 churches on its territory. In Soviet times, the Assumption Cathedral on the territory of the Iversky Monastery was blown up, and the bell tower, whose height was 65 m, was destroyed. And only in the 1990s the Orthodox monastery was revived.



In your free time you can watch something related to the culture of the region. For example, in the Samara Regional Museum of History and Local Lore - one of the oldest in the Volga region - there is a collection that has more than 200 thousand exhibits. Both adults and young travelers will love it.

The Art Museum in Samara is also one of the largest in the Volga region. By the way, it is worth paying attention to the fact that today it houses one of the richest collections of Russian art of the 17-19th centuries and a collection of graphics and paintings of the 19-20th centuries.

The museum-estate of A. N. Tolstoy recreated the atmosphere of the provincial city of the Russian Empire at the turn of the 19th and 20th centuries. The archives of the writer and his mother are kept here.

Section 3. Where to go in Samara? You need to know about it



In order to feel comfortable in any corner of the globe, experienced tourists recommend preparing in advance for a trip or a walk. For example, did you know that it was in Samara that Maxim Gorky worked at the beginning of his career. In addition, at different times in this city there were famous artists: Ilya Repin, Vasily Surikov, Ivan Aivazovsky.

Also in this city are many temples. Very beautiful Spaso-Vozdvizhensky and Pokrovsky Orthodox cathedrals, the Catholic Cathedral on the street. Frunze, Samara Choral Synagogue, built in accordance with the features of the Moorish style.

An ancient and very beautiful city is this Samara. Where to go with the baby? After all, we often enough try to travel with children. Local citizens recommend choosing one of the many parks, as well as walking, for example, in the park near Voronezh Lake, which has been maintaining a very romantic atmosphere for many years.

The oldest park in Samara is the Strukovsky Garden, overlooking the Volga. This is the place where city festivals are traditionally held. For tourists there are souvenir shops, cafes, and restaurants, so you can have a great time here!

The Volga Embankment is generally a wonderful place for outdoor activities. In summer, lovers spend time here cycling, rollerblading and skateboarding.

History connoisseurs will surely like the labyrinths near the Volga, secret bunkers and caves. All this annually attracts adventurers from different parts of the globe to Samara.
