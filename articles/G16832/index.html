An ordinary person does not always have the time and desire to delve into the intricacies of geography. Meanwhile, tourists await India. Goa Islands are located in the ocean, not far from its coast.

It so happened that the smallest of all the states of India over the past few decades has become one of the most popular tourist destinations not only in this country, but throughout the world. On the shores of the Indian Ocean

The coast of the Indian state of Goa became widely known in the sixties of the last century. It is this section of the globe that numerous hippies from America and Europe have chosen as a place for pilgrimage. And they can be understood - it is not so easy to find in the world another, equally paradise, corner for escape from civilization. In this country of eternal summer and the sun, one could exist as long as you like without thinking about tomorrow and without straining. But since then many years have passed. And over the past half century, thanks to its many kilometers of ocean beaches, Goa has received the status of an internationally recognized resort. Considerable investments were made in the business, due to which a tourist infrastructure of a modern level was built on the state coast. The Goa Islands are less affected by civilization.





Far from shore and in depth

There are eight islands off the coast of the state. But when the islands of Goa are mentioned, most often they mean only three of them - St. George, Netrani and Grand. They are the most popular among tourists and sports divers. The rest are less known. Holidays on the islands of Goa are usually associated with an introduction to the underwater world. These sections of land in the Indian Ocean are especially attractive for lovers to dive to the depth, which is explained by a number of circumstances. From a comfortable subequatorial climate for a person to a large number of merchant sailing ships that had the misfortune to sink in these waters in previous centuries. And of course, the enchanting beauty of the underwater flora and fauna, which exceeds the wildest expectations, also matters.





What does a man seek in depth?

It is interesting to note the fact that, once on the islands of Goa, diving is even engaged in those who had not previously thought of it. The situation here is favorable. In the Indian Ocean, even regular office workers often become athlete divers. So there were circumstances. The islands of Goa on the map are barely distinguishable, but in the entire set of climatic conditions they can be considered unique. Only the transparency of the waters off the coast exceeds 25 meters. And in the Indian Ocean, an extremely stable temperature is always within 25 degrees Celsius. And one who plunges into this water can enjoy communication with the inhabitants of the depths, of which there are countless numbers, without any problems. But most vividly against the backdrop of multi-colored corals , angelfish, hedgehogs, tuna and moray eels look. A lot of turtles, dolphins, crabs, cuttlefish and shellfish. There are even sharks.





St george island

But with all the fascination of communicating with the underwater world, one should approach its development with caution. The ocean does not tolerate self-neglect. Diving to depth requires a certain level of sports training. On the island of St. George there are several bases for diving enthusiasts. Here, experienced instructors in a short time will help to master the basic skills of staying in the underwater environment, even those who do not have them (skills) at all. For an amount equivalent to forty dollars, you can take the primary diving course. It takes one day and boils down to the sequential complication and development of deeper dives. Of course, the development of the subsequent stages of this skill takes a little longer and requires additional costs. And also, heading to St. George and other islands of Goa, you can count on good fishing. It goes well with scuba diving.

What places to visit

What else is important not to miss, being on the coast of Goa? Excursions to the islands are a must for the stay program, but there are many attractions in the mainland of the Indian state. Goa's cultural heritage was shaped by two multidirectional trends. Here, the spiritual heritage of Hinduism and powerful European, primarily Portuguese, influence are closely intertwined. The period of Portuguese colonization is considered by many to be the cultural heyday of this territory. Traces of the characteristic colonial architecture can still be found today along the coast of the Indian state of Goa. As a rule, these are Catholic cathedrals erected by Portuguese missionaries. They quite peacefully coexist with Hindu temples. First of all, you should pay attention to such objects as Shantha Durga in Cavelle, Mahals in Mardol and Mangesh in Mangeshi. It is interesting that the local population celebrates both Catholic and traditional Hindu holidays with great joy.

Goa natural parks deserve special attention - there are six of them here. Mostly they are located in the interior of the state, in the foothills of the Sahyadri. A special delight for those who still managed to get to it is the Dudhsogar Falls. Its height exceeds six hundred meters. The sight is bewitching! Powerful streams of water, sparkling with all colors of the rainbow, water dust make an indelible impression!
