Walkie-talkie Motorola: description, instructions and reviews

Motorola walkie-talkies are presented on the market in a wide range, thanks to which the equipment of this brand is used in various fields of activity - from security companies to recreation, hunting and fishing. Compact PMR radio stations are the most convenient for radio exchange, since they do not require permission from Rossvyaznadzor. For their correct choice, it is worth relying on the results of the conducted testing, for which Motorola walkie-talkies were chosen.

Why choose a Motorola walkie-talkie?

For nearly a century, Motorola has maintained an excellent reputation among consumers. Product quality is constantly improving by engineers working to reduce the size of Motorola walkie-talkies while maintaining signal power and quality.

Motorola radio stations attract consumers with an excellent price - quality ratio and wide functionality.

Today, the company focuses on the development and release of new models of walkie-talkies.





Motorola T80

Motorola T3-T8 series radios are considered the simplest and most massive. They belong to the economy class: E4 models can be purchased for 1300-1800 rubles, a set of T6 - for 1500 rubles. The T3 model, however, will have to look for a used one, since it was discontinued several years ago.

Walkie Talkie Motorola T80

High quality, reliability and weather protection - this is what distinguishes the Motorola walkie-talkie of this model. A waterproof case, a wide range of functions and a communication radius of up to 10 kilometers allow you to use the radio in extreme conditions and stay in touch anywhere in the world.

Using the radio Motorola TLRK TR 80 Extreme does not require a license. The radio station is equipped with a liquid crystal display, can support the operation of 8 communication channels and transmits a signal at a distance of up to 10 kilometers. The built-in LED flashlight and the case protected from moisture influence allow to use the Motorola T80 handheld transceiver in any situation.





Features walkie-talkies

Motorola walkie-talkie models of the T3-T8 line have the same electronic base, since they are equipped with the same microprocessor, which explains some similarities in characteristics and disadvantages. Only T6 is different, it is more convenient in operation and attractive in appearance.

When compared with analogues of other brands, Jet mini, Vortex and Midland devices are comparable to Motorola radios, and some of them are inferior to Motorola in technical specifications and outperform in price.

To exchange messages on the air, it is enough to tune all the walkie-talkies used to the same frequency and make sure that all the devices are programmed for the same subcodes.

Charger and Walkie Talkie Power

The IXTH4016A charger, equipped with a special glass for two walkie-talkies and a power adapter with a maximum current of 300 mA / h and a voltage of 9 V, is supplied with the T6 models.

The full energy capacity of a discharged battery connected to this charger is restored in 2-3 hours, which is a very good indicator for walkie-talkies of a similar class. The instructions for the Motorola walkie-talkie indicate the time required to fully charge the battery - 16 hours. This discrepancy between theory and practice is explained by the fact that the LED red indicator in the glass is lit as long as the walkie-talkie is in the charger, while the degree of battery charge does not affect its performance.

On the side panel of each walkie-talkie included in the kit, there is a power connector designed for an external source with a voltage of 9 V. DC is used.

In terms of catering, Motorola T6 compares favorably with its predecessors - the T3 and T4 models. Despite this, she also has its drawbacks.





Walkie Talkie Range

In the passport data of the walkie-talkie, the manufacturer indicates the radius of action. With a power of 0.5 W, the maximum communication range between two stations of the same model is from 5 to 10 km, depending on the particular walkie-talkie.

The communication range varies on rough terrain and in the city, as well as in buildings between floors, and reaches a maximum of 500 meters. Similar inconsistencies in the data specified in the passport are identified when testing on the terrain of any other Motorola radio stations.

Various data are displayed on the wide-function display of Motorola walkie-talkies, including the battery charge level, channel number, subcode (if it is turned on) and other modes activated and used by the user.





Features of working with subcodes of walkie-talkie

In portable walkie-talkies operating in the PMR range, one of the useful and separate functions is the subcode, designed for traveling outdoors, tourism, short-distance radio communications within the city limits - for example, between neighboring houses or apartments.

Programming the subcode makes it possible to eliminate interference and clear the radio from listening to other walkie-talkies operating at the same frequency. In Motorola T3-T8 series models, you can specify 121 combinations of different subcodes on eight available channels - 83 DCS codes and 38 codes in CTCSS format. In general, a value of 968 different combinations is available.

Each of the mentioned models is equipped with a scanning mode of all available channels - 8 for each of the walkie-talkies. The device automatically stops scanning on the channel on which any radio is being exchanged. Pausing the general scan is signaled by a sound signal, after which the reception is turned on, that is, listening to the broadcast frequency.





Common flaws for all models

In data transfer mode, all Motorola walkie-talkies include overload protection. In practice, this is done as follows: after continuous operation of the walkie-talkie in transmission mode for 60 seconds, it automatically turns off. Before stopping the operation, the flashing ‚Äútransfer‚Äù mode icon appears on the display and a loud beep sounds. The icon on the display lights up without blinking if the device is functioning normally, without temporary overloads. The safety mode used in Motorola walkie-talkie protects the output stage of the transmitter from malfunctions and failure in case of overload and overheating. This element on high-frequency high-power transistors is the most expensive and complex in design.

One of the drawbacks of the Motorola walkie-talkies of the T3, T4, T6 and T8 models is the limitation of signal transmission time to one minute. Repeated broadcasting is possible only with a break for a few seconds. Such a device does not allow the transfer of large amounts of information to another correspondent, however, Motorola radios are used extremely rarely for such purposes.





In the passport data and on the device box, the communication range is indicated under ideal conditions: in clear sunny weather with perfect visibility of subscribers at a temperature of 20 ¬∞ C without precipitation and in the absence of interference like trees, buildings, power lines. This parameter is theoretical for the indicated wavelength - 70 centimeters. With a signal power of 0.5 W, the real communication range in the forest is 2-3 km, in the field 3-4 km, at sea 5-6 km, in the urban area 500-600 meters, which is standard for any PMR radio stations brands and models.

Motorola walkie-talkies compare favorably with their counterparts of other brands with better technical characteristics and more affordable cost. The communication range of the devices is standard and sufficient for data transmission and reception in extreme conditions.
