How to come here

Numerous travel agencies offer tours to Strasbourg, but it is much better to go to this mystical city on your own. Slowly walking along the narrow streets, stopping on the bridges, you are more imbued with the atmosphere of this place than running around it with a noisy crowd of unfamiliar people. How to get to Strasbourg? Unfortunately, there is no direct flight from Russia, but there are convenient connecting routes via Paris, Prague or Amsterdam. The airport is located a few kilometers from the city, and you can get there by navette train, which runs every quarter of an hour. Based on the proximity to the German border, you can consider this option: by plane to Frankfurt or Karlsruhe, and from there by train or bus to Strasbourg. On the TGV high-speed train, the journey from Paris to the capital of Alsace will take only two and a half hours.

Where to settle

All sights of Strasbourg, with few exceptions, are concentrated in the center. The historical part of the city is declared by UNESCO a world heritage of mankind. Therefore, it is natural that hotels located in ancient half-timbered houses are not cheap. You will find more democratic housing in new areas, fortunately, since the XIV century the city has grown. With public transport in Strasbourg, everything is in order. On a tram that looks like a spaceship, it's worth a ride out of purely tourist interest. And in order to make their stay in Strasbourg completely budgetary, many experienced travelers prefer to live ... in neighboring Germany. Having crossed the bridge over the Rhine by bus, you find yourself in the German town of Kehl, where prices - and not just housing - will pleasantly surprise you. Covered Bridges

Embankment Saint-Jean once represented the outskirts of the city. To get outside the walls, it was necessary to cross the bridge, which was guarded. It was covered, made of wood. Over the centuries, this material has deteriorated, and the roof of most bridges has disappeared. Now the river Ile is ringed by transitions from stone. Having examined the church of St. John, we enter the city and get to the embankment of Paris. Further it is possible to advance as you like, without moving away, however, from the river. Each bridge has its own history and is beautiful in its own way. Along the way, you can visit the church of St. Peter-Youth, the palace of the archbishop and, finally, approach the main attraction of Strasbourg - the cathedral. Do not rush to go inside. Go around the majestic building from the outside, admire the statues on the portal, known from history books as classic art of the Middle Ages. Now leave the cathedral square, go around the Rogan Palace and leave on the pier of tourist water trams. An exciting walk through the canals and locks of Strasbourg awaits you.

These nice boats in the cold weather glaze with panoramic windows, so that on board you can take great pictures. After all, Strasbourg is not only the capital of the department of the Lower Rhine and the province of Alsace. It houses the Parliament and the Council of Europe. But in order to look at these modern and interesting buildings from the point of view of architectural thought, one should leave the Big Island.
