Brave Vikings: Ship and Lifestyle

History has been going on for a long time by different people and nations. A huge contribution to the development of human civilization was made by scientists and dreamers. But no less merit is the warriors: during the campaigns they studied the face of the planet, made a map, got acquainted with the variety of flora and fauna. Powerful conquerors, terrifying all coastal lands, and brave travelers, conquerors of water and skilled traders. I wonder who we are talking about? Of course, these are the Vikings. Their ship was both a home, and a means of transportation, and the last refuge in which they went to another world. The significance of this building in the life of the Scandinavians is difficult to overestimate.



The scarcity of nature, difficult living conditions determined not only the occupation and nature of the Vikings. It was reflected in the development of the culture of their neighbors, both closest and more distant. The Scandinavians lived in the clans ruled by the Konungs. Often these sea kings did not have their own land holdings, but controlled a certain water area. All members of their clan entered their squad and went camping. The Vikings, whose ship was considered the main wealth and value of the tribe, were required to carefully take care of the ship. He was sheltered from the weather, stored in a special barn when not in use.



The Viking ship, the photo of which is given in this article, was built by the whole population, in a fold. After all, this building was not only expensive, but also time-consuming and complicated. Equipment and provisions were also stocked up by joint forces - all further life of a community depended on a successful campaign.

In that distant era, ships of various types were built by Vikings. A ship of one kind was intended for sailing at the mouths of rivers and along the coast cut by fjords. Others were more stable and maneuverable, so they boldly went to the waters of the Atlantic. With the development of seafaring, ships became larger with a higher carrying capacity. Already at the end of the tenth - at the beginning of the eleventh century, the Norwegian kings could launch a ship fifty meters long, for which thirty or more pairs of rowers were needed. Also brave Normans made extensive use of sail.

There is no doubt that some astronomical knowledge was not alien to the brave sailors.
