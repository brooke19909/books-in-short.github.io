The most useful crochet openwork patterns: schemes, photos

The main advantage of the crochet technique is that with its application you can get a variety of openwork fabrics. They are suitable for making clothes or home decoration. For example, summer tops, shirts, dresses, hats, as well as pillows, curtains and bedspreads.

General principles of knitting openwork

Crochet openwork patterns (diagrams are presented below) can be knitted in different ways. Techniques differ significantly in many ways.



But there are several recommendations that will be useful when performing any kind of lace:

  • The air loops (VP) that make up the chain of patterns should be tightly bound. Weak knitting will lead to the appearance of unplanned holes, the ornament will look blurry.
  • If according to the scheme it is necessary to knit single crochet (RLS) or double crochet (CCH) columns over an arch of several VPs, then do not try to match each column with a loop. You can thread the hook directly under the arch.

However, there are exceptions, you need to look at the situation.

  • Columns with several crochets should be knitted carefully and tightly. During the set of yarn over the hook, you can hold the threads with your finger so that they do not unwind. Crochet openwork patterns, the schemes of which include these elements, are distinguished by a large number of holes and a rather complex pattern. Therefore, it is so important to preserve its geometry.

Types of openwork patterns

Depending on the method that the craftswoman uses to create an openwork canvas, it can be:

  • Equal.
  • Circular.
  • Typing.

Typesetting canvases, as the name implies, consist of separately connected and connected by a grid of elements (flowers, geometric shapes, abstraction, cords and much more).

Knitting in a circle

Crochet circular lace patterns gained wide popularity. Schemes of such patterns start from the center. Their knitting requires strict adherence to the pattern, since the formation of the fabric occurs with a sequential increase in the number of elements.





This shawl perfectly demonstrates how the expansion of the canvas was carried out with the introduction of new pattern repeatings while maintaining their initial size. Quite often they use the opposite method: increasing the sizes of rapports.

As a rule, each row of circular canvases should expand by six elements (RLS, SSN, VP). However, this order may vary due to the shape of the canvas, its size and the pattern used.

Canvases with corners (squares, rectangles) are also referred to as circular, since the principle of knitting is preserved: from the center in a spiral to the outer edge.
