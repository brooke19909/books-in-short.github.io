So, the iPad is a line of tablet computers that are developed and sold by Apple and run on the iOS platform. The first iPad was released on April 3, 2010, and the most recent gadget models - the fourth generation and iPadMini - appeared in November 2012. The user interface is based on a touch screen, including a virtual keyboard. The iPad has built-in Wi-Fi, and some models have cellular connectivity.

The iPad can take videos, take photos, listen to music, and perform functions related to the Internet, such as visiting email. Other functions - games, links, GPS navigation, social networks, etc. - can be enabled by downloading and installing applications. To do this, you need to connect your iPad to the Internet. As of June 2013, the AppStore had over 900,000 programs and games from Apple and other developers.

In total, five versions of the iPad went on sale. The first generation had some design features (screen size and button layout) that are stored on all models. The iPad-2 received a dual-core Apple A5 processor and also 2 cameras - front VGA and rear 720p resolution, designed for FaceTime video calls. The third generation was supplemented by a Retina Display and a quad-core graphics processor, as well as a 5-megapixel camera, the ability to record HD video 1080 p 4G (LTE). The fourth generation received an Apple A6X processor and a new digital connector. The iPad Mini has a reduced screen size of 7.9 inches, unlike the standard size of 9.7, and has specifications similar to the iPad-2.
