Carpenters are a profession!

It can be assumed that from the moment our ancestors came to the need to use wood as a building material, the profession of a carpenter appeared.





Carpenter's Tasks

This skill remains in demand in construction and in solving various everyday problems. In a nutshell, the carpenter‚Äôs job is that by machining he turns an uncouth piece of wood into a useful part or even a whole structure. Carpenters are people without whom it would be impossible to decorate our housing with a tree.

What does this master do?

More specifically, the carpenter has to:

  • build or repair various wooden structures, including residential buildings, baths, saunas, beam bridges;
  • install wooden fences, scaffolding;
  • to make beams and arches, wooden supports for organizing communication lines, flooring and much more;
  • to cut, saw, drill, connect wooden elements with each other, as well as process timber with various chemical compounds in order to protect them from premature destruction.

It will not be difficult for an experienced craftsman to make a log house, perform flooring on a wooden floor, install doors and window frames. Carpenter is a profession that is in demand in modern society.





Qualities a carpenter should have

A person who has connected his entire conscious life with the craft of a carpenter should:

  1. To be hardy, to be in good physical shape, to have developed coordination of movements.
  2. To have not only a strong trunk and muscles of the limbs, but also moving hands.
  3. To be able to concentrate, be accurate, attentive, have a good memory. Carpenters are people who must be attentive to their work.
  4. Have an accurate eye and spatial imagination.
  5. Suppress the fear of heights, as representatives of this profession often have to work, being at a distance of several meters above the surface of the earth.

Carpenters are not an easy profession. These people help to build houses and decorate rooms with unusual interior items.





Qualification requirements

Most carpenters are limited to secondary education. When a representative of this profession writes an application for a job, the personnel department does not always focus on the candidate‚Äôs experience. If a person has a great desire to work in his specialty, this quality will come with time.

For a carpenter, it is important:

  • distinguish between species of wood, know what properties of each of them are inherent, how best to process this material and where to apply;
  • navigate in the finished drawings at the appropriate level, have basic concepts;
  • well versed in the ways of marking and creating all kinds of wood structures;
  • own the fundamentals of the exact sciences (physics, mathematics) to a sufficient extent, at least within the school curriculum, in order to apply this knowledge in their activities;
  • know when and where to use special compounds to protect wood products;
  • perfectly master the carpentry tool, carry out any operations with it.

A carpenter carpenter is a person who knows exactly how to turn an ordinary piece of wood into furniture or a decorative element.

Carpenter and joiner. What is the difference?

Many uninitiated people have no idea about the existing differences between a carpenter and a joiner, confuse one concept with another. After all, both are dealing with wood. Carpenters are wood craftsmen, joiners also work with wood.

One of the differences is that the carpenter who processes the wood in any mechanical way does not bother with those types of operations that relate to the artistic component. In simple terms, a carpenter, using a limited set of simple tools - an ax, a hacksaw and a hammer - performs only that part of the work that can be called rough.





Another thing is a carpenter. Hand tools in this case are represented by a wider range. In addition, the joiner has a woodworking machine, which makes it possible to perform not only the delicate, but also the exact work that is required for decorative processing, stylish interior decoration, and even the creation of aircraft models. In a word, the craft of a carpenter is much closer in its content to professions of a creative nature - an artist-designer or designer.

Secondly, the profession of a joiner is diverse, versatile. If the need arises, he will be able to cut threads on a metal part or assemble a steel structure. Despite the fact that such operations are not part of the carpenter‚Äôs tasks, his golden hands are no substitute for the construction of a wooden house.

The carpenter-concrete worker is also appreciated. This is a master who works flawlessly not only with wood but also with concrete.
