Easy kusudama can be made even by a child

The origin of kusudama is rooted in the deep past. The technique is very similar to another ancient art - origami. Initially, this is a piece of paper in the form of a square, from which the figure was created. The square for the inhabitants of the East is a symbol of the earth, which has firmness, indestructibility and constancy. Kusudama differs from origami in cutting and gluing paper. To make a kusudama, you will need a little: a scheme, glue and scissors. Some also use fabric and everything imagination can allow: sheets of glossy magazines, wallpapers, and even plastic.
