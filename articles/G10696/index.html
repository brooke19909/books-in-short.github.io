What is a sari?

This is a long seamless piece of fabric from 5 to 12 meters. Initially, there were two pieces, one wrapped around the hips, the second on the chest, like a topic. Over time, the sari was a one-piece fabric that wound around the skirt and moved upstairs, covering the head and shoulders. At the same time, part of the fabric hanging from the shoulders is most richly painted and decorated in order to show all the richness and beauty of the clothing.

Under the influence of European colonists, girls without a topic and skirt did not wear a single Indian sari. A photo of traditional Indian clothing shows how modern the sari is rich in colors, stylish and elegant. There are costumes consisting of pants with a shirt or a tank top and harem, covered with sarees. Moreover, it can be long, short, transparent. A classic sari has one or two borders on the side (this is the edge with a pattern).

The early sari was distributed by color value. For example, brides wore only a red sari with a gold pattern, a woman after childbirth wore a yellow robe, a widow wore white, lower classes in blue. But now color symbolism has lost its meaning.
