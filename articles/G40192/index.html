Modular Origami: Floor Vase Patterns for Beginners

These masterpieces are obtained from paper by those who have mastered the technique of modular origami. But if a person is just learning, he better start with a vase pattern.

Modular origami is a technique of folding interesting shapes from many details. Various animals, flowers, cartoon characters from small modules turn out to be colorful and almost real. Since they fully retain their bulk shape. So, let‚Äôs see how modular origami is formed in stages - vase schemes, in our case.

How are the circuits assembled?

Parts (modules) assembled according to a certain scheme are not difficult in themselves. But the figures of them require patience. For large-scale large-scale crafts, sometimes a huge amount of these details is required.

As for the question of how simple modular origami is made, patterns of a vase, for example, or a swan, these crafts serve as the "basis" of training.

The rules for building any object in a modular origami are simple. The first row of modules combines and holds the second row, which is superimposed on top of the bottom row. And the third row holds the second, and serves as the basis for 4 - and so on.

It is only necessary in the correct sequence to add colored elements. If you disrupt the order of colored elements, the pattern embedded in the scheme will be lost. Simple drawings are visible visually, and if desired, you can not go astray. But more complex drawings require special care.

How to make corner modules?

Acquaintance with modular origami begins with the construction of elementary particles of this world - with modules. Before you sort out the little things how a vase is made up of modules, you still need to learn how to make modules, and know how they are interconnected.

Divide the A4 sheet into equal rectangles, cut them out. Each rectangle bends in half twice. We bend corners along the second deflection, we raise the remaining squares below. bend the resulting triangle in half. This item will be used for crafts.

Simple vase with diagonal stripes of modules

We assemble a simple vase, stacking the necessary modules one by one in a checkerboard pattern according to a simple pattern. Two parts of the first row and one detail of the second; two second rows - one third. So we go through 3 rows .





But first, we explain one subtle rule. The bottom of the vase is thinner, then the craft expands in volume. And to expand this craft, you need to increase the number of modules in the circle. To do this, on each tooth of the previous row, we apply 1 module of the next row, only the next 3 modules should be set according to the basic rule. After all, the figure should hold tight. And so we alternate the modules starting with 4 rows.

Already from 3 rows, you need to make 2 details of a different color to create a diagonal pattern. After each row, it is necessary to bend the folded elements in the right direction. Thus, the diameter of the vase first expands, then, in the same sequence and with the same pattern, gradually decreases .





And when the top of the vase becomes as thin as the bottom, you can make beautiful triangular racks to raise the vase a little longer. In total, it is required to prepare 392 modules of one color and 296 of another .





The result is such a vase with diagonal stripes along. This is a very interesting scheme for beginners.
