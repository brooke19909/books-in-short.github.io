Only in Venezuela there is a huge amount of tepui - these are mountains with truncated peaks, and therefore their appearance is rather unusual and strange. People call them table mountains. Their peaks are flat, they have sheer vertical walls. Therefore, even after light rainfall, water accumulates there, and then flows down into numerous waterfalls. A bit about the surrounding area

Tourists, probably, would like to learn more about where Angel Falls is located. And it is located, as already mentioned above, in Venezuela, in the highlands, which is called Guyana. It is on the Carrao River, which is one of the tributaries of Orinoco. From the same Spanish language, Angel is translated as "angel".

Since ancient times, the name Churun-Meru has been assigned to the attraction. So he was called the Indian tribes living in this area. The plateau from which the waterfall falls is called Auyan-Tepui, which means "Devil‚Äôs Mountain." This name was given because the plateau is constantly shrouded in dense fog. Where Angel Falls is located, tropical forests grow everywhere, so there are no special roads to it. Thus, you can get here either by air or by water.





National park and journey to it

Angel is part of the Venezuelan Canaima National Park. To this miracle of nature, hundreds of excursions are organized annually. But since the place is quite inaccessible, people prefer air walks. If you decide to get to Angel by river, you can do this as follows: first you have to swim by motor canoe to the village of Canaima, and then walk about two to three kilometers. A dirt road leads to Canaima , and local authorities have arranged a small airport in the neighborhood, which provides services to guests and tourists. Travelers can use the shops, bars and comfortable hotels. The waterfall, together with the territories adjacent to it, managed to preserve the charm of the wild. If you are lucky, then during the walk you can see the local animals that went to the watering hole. Jaguars, three-fingered sloths, anteaters, giant otters and other interesting representatives of the fauna live in the park jungle.

The height of Angel Falls provides an opportunity to admire the observation deck and the nearby picturesque lagoon, which has the same name as the park. Powerful streams of water are thrown here from around the ledges.





Who and why renamed the waterfall

In 2009, the then president of Venezuela, Hugo Chavez, said that Angel Falls should be renamed Kerepakupai-Meru. True, the option of Churun-Meru was first considered. But one of the daughters of the ruler remembered that a waterfall with the same name already exists in this area. In addition, it is quite small. Then Hugo Chavez decided to opt for Kerepakupai-Meru. The leader motivated his action with the fact that the attraction should not be named after an American, since long before the opening the waterfall was in Venezuela and belonged only to it.

Interesting about the waterfall

Where Angel Falls is located, many different plants grow. The third part of them is not found anywhere else on the planet. The waterfall itself is fed by the waters of the rains, so when the dry season begins, it turns into a thin stream. When precipitation falls again, the attraction takes the form of a powerful, immense and so beautiful giant.



A few things that few people know about.

It is believed that the first person from Europe to see Angel was Ernest Sanchez La Cruz. The attraction was opened to his gaze in 1910. There is also evidence that even earlier this giant was noticed by the Spanish conquistadors and Catholic missionary monks. Nevertheless, James Angel brought fame to the miracle of nature. Later, the coordinates of Angel (waterfall) were established and its height was determined.

The broken plane of James Angel lay on the top of the mountain for 33 years. In 1970, the remains of the car were removed from tepui. It was restored, and today the plane stands at the entrance to the city airport of Ciudad Bolivar. After the death of James (1960), his ashes were scattered over the highest waterfall on Earth, which was named after the pilot. That was Angel‚Äôs last wish.
