Yaremcha - summer holidays and the main attractions of the resort

Yaremcha is a Carpathian resort that has been known since the end of the 19th century. Here you can not only improve your health, but also find something to see. That is why the resort of Yaremcha is developing every year, the rest on which leaves a lot of positive impressions. Today, more and more people choose it as an alternative to a traditional beach holiday.

Yaremcha - rest in the summer and in the winter!

Yaremcha is a place where you can come both in summer and in winter. Here you can relax and get healthy at any time of the year. Mountain hikes, horseback riding, waterfalls, mineral springs and wooden churches - all this is Yaremcha! Rest in this beautiful corner of the Carpathians cannot but like.





This small town perfectly combines the charming nature and real Hutsul flavor. And in terms of housing, the resort of Yaremcha can offer a lot. Rest here is available to everyone, regardless of the thickness of the wallet. In Yaremche, you can quite inexpensively rent a house or a room or settle in expensive luxury apartments with a pool in the courtyard.

Absolutely for all ages, Yaremcha is suitable - a vacation here leaves an unforgettable experience! Students will definitely be interested in active types of recreation: rafting, sledding and biking, long hikes on the mountain slopes. Elderly tourists can recover in one of the local sanatoriums, drinking some water from the springs and contemplating the beautiful panoramas of the local nature.

Yaremcha (Carpathians): how to get there?

The resort is located in the intermountain depression, in the valley of the Prut River. The nearest mountain range is Gorgan. Administratively, the city is located within the Ivano-Frankivsk region of Ukraine, in its southwestern part. On all sides, the city of Yaremcha is surrounded by mountain ranges, the absolute height of which does not exceed 1,500 meters.

Resort History

The city was founded in 1787. The development of Yaremchi as a resort begins at the end of the 19th century. In the 1890s, several dozen expensive villas were built here, the owners of which were representatives of the Austrian elite. At the same time, the first tourist club "Yaremchansky" appeared in the city, which was engaged in marking mountain trails, landscaping and popularizing the sights of Yaremche.

At the beginning of the 20th century, about 1,500 people vacationed in this area annually. In 1907, the first guide to Yaremche was published. To attract tourists, concerts, festivals, lotteries and humorous evenings were regularly organized in the city. In the interwar period, the first sanatoriums arose in Yaremche.

Since the beginning of the 1950s, the city has been actively developing as a Soviet resort. Thousands of tourists from different parts of the USSR annually came to Yaremcha to improve their health, get acquainted with the Hutsul culture and see with their own eyes the Carpathian Mountains, which are "self-sufficient and perfect" (as defined by the Ukrainian writer Taras Prokhasko).

Souvenir market in Yaremche

The largest souvenir market in the whole Carpathian Ukraine is located in Yaremche. This is not just a bazaar, but also one of the main enterprises of the city‚Äôs economy.

The market is located in the center of the resort. Several hundred sellers offer tourists to buy souvenirs, products, as well as works of art made by local craftsmen. Here you can buy beautiful wood products, pottery, landscape paintings, warm sheepskin blankets and much more. The souvenir market in Yaremche is also a museum with thousands of different exhibits, where you can walk for hours.

Breakdown waterfall

Perhaps the city‚Äôs most popular attraction is the Breakdown waterfall. This is a cascade type waterfall located on the Prut River, in the center of Yaremche. Water falls from a 7-meter height with a huge noise.





The waterfall is very picturesque; gray Yamnensky sandstones come to the surface along the banks. You can admire this natural monument from the 20-meter bridge, which was built directly above the cascades.

Masterpieces of wooden architecture

At least ten monuments of wooden (Hutsul) architecture have been preserved in Yaremche. The most famous among them is the Hutsulshchyna Restaurant. A chic wooden building was built in 1952 without a single nail.





In addition, six wooden churches dating from the 18th-19th centuries have been preserved in the city. Among them is the oldest church of John the Merciful 1663. It was built in Yamna (the historical district of Yaremche) in the best traditions of Hutsul architecture. In the interiors of the old church there are many icons and embroideries. Near the temple, it is also interesting to see the old cemetery with ancient tombstones.

Thus, Yaremcha is not only an excellent mountain resort, but also a city with a rich history and numerous monuments of culture and architecture.
