How to get from Tyumen to Moscow?

Tyumen and Moscow are two prosperous industrial cities of Russia. Why do people travel from one point to another? First of all, for work purposes. They also do this for the sake of traveling and visiting guests. In total, there are several options for vehicles that allow you to get from one city to another.





By car

The distance from Tyumen to Moscow in km is approximately 2110. Of course, the path ahead is not the closest, but many drivers confidently overcome it in a personal vehicle. The total travel time will be 1 day and 3 hours. But if you take into account traffic jams, stops and overnight, this figure can significantly increase. Your route should be laid as follows:





  • The first thing to do is to leave the city of Tyumen.
  • Next, go to the R-351 highway. There are several other options for overcoming this distance, but this is the most optimal. The main advantages of the route are smooth road surface, bright markings, the presence of gas stations, cafes and car workshops. It is better to refuel the car in advance, as closer to the capital the price for it will begin to increase.
  • This route leads to the city of Yekaterinburg. You should drive on the right edge of it. Then you need to move towards the R-242 highway.
  • By evening, the driver will be in Perm. It is in this place that it is recommended to stop for the night, and then continue on your way along the same highway.
  • The next day, you will drive through two more major cities - Kirov and Yaroslavl.
  • It only remains to go to the international highway M8 and, following the signs, safely get to Moscow.

A similar movement can be made in the opposite direction.

Features of this vehicle

If you look at the map of Russia, you can see that the path between Tyumen and Moscow represents one straight line. There are no forks, obstacles or sharp turns. This greatly facilitates the task of the driver. However, you will have to sit behind the wheel for more than a day, which is quite difficult. It is recommended for this to combine two traffic participants and drive one vehicle in turn.





The route needs to be thought out in advance, choosing places for stops and overnight. You should not rely on the navigator and road signs. It is recommended to buy or pre-print a card that will allow you to stay on track.

The road ahead is not long, so it is recommended to take care of the means of entertainment in advance. For example, tune the radio and download music.

By train

The most comfortable option, allowing you to get from Tyumen to Moscow, is a train. Daily from the Tyumen international station, the train number 109M is sent. At 22:56 he departs, and a day later at 10:30 the passenger ends up in the Russian capital at the Yaroslavl station. The total travel time will be only 1 day and 11 hours.

In addition, a few more passing trains from Nizhnevartovsk and Novy Urengoy follow every other day.





In summer, it is recommended to book tickets in advance, as there is a lot of tourists rush. This can be done on the official website of Russian Railways or at the box office of absolutely any railway station. The approximate cost per person will be 3,500 rubles. For children, pensioners and preferential categories of citizens there are additional discounts.

By plane





You can quickly get by plane from Tyumen to Moscow. The flight takes only 2 hours, 15 minutes. Transport is provided by Pobeda Airlines, it has proven itself among passengers. Departure takes place at 6:20 at Roshchino airport. Already at 8:35 a landing in Vnukovo is carried out. There are also several passing flights to Domodedovo, but few are happy with this option because of the high cost. If you take only the first option into account, then a ticket will cost from 4000 to 5500 rubles, depending on the class level.

By bus

Due to the large distance from Tyumen to Moscow, a direct bus flight has not yet been created that could transport passengers from one city to another. However, there is an opportunity to overcome this distance with transfers in large cities. The total travel time with all transfers and stops will be more than three days. If traffic jams are present on the roads, this figure may still increase significantly. Of course, few people may like such a tiring method of moving, so they rarely use this vehicle. The total cost of the road will be more than 5000 rubles.

Many people annually make trips from Tyumen to Moscow. The reasons that motivate them to overcome such a distance can be different: travel, work, visit to relatives and friends, other personal goals. Fortunately, there are several optimal options suitable for all travel lovers to overcome this long road.
