Polyacrylate. What kind of fabric? Description, composition, characteristics, reviews.

Synthetic fibers for sewing or knitting clothes have been used for a long time. Currently, polyacryl is often used in the manufacture of clothing. What kind of fabric is this? Why did she become so in demand? Let's look at the features, characteristics, useful qualities of the material.



History

In the forties of the last century, a new material hitherto unknown to clothing manufacturers appeared - polyacryl. What kind of fabric? The United States was the first to answer this question, because it was the American company DuPont that produced the first synthetic fibers, which were called polyacrylonitrile.

The first meter of material was obtained in 1948. He made a decent competition to the then popular nylon. At first, the material was very difficult to stain. But after several years of refinement and research, manufacturers were able to get the perfect synthetic fabric.

Composition

Polyacrylate. What kind of fabric? There is no definite answer to this question. In today's world, most materials contain it. Acrylic fibers are added to wool, mohair, cotton, angora, coat , etc. The percentage can fluctuate and can be completely different. Some fabrics will be 95 percent acrylic, others only five.

No matter how the clothing manufacturers insist on one hundred percent wool or cotton, in most cases their products contain polyacryl. What fabric can have a long service life, and besides, for many years, socks to maintain their original appearance? There is simply no such thing. All modern cotton, mohair or woolen things have such qualities thanks to the addition of polyacryl.

disadvantages

Quite often, people who wear synthetics use special anti-static aerosols. Polyacrylum accumulates static electricity - this is another drawback.
