Protect your smartphone from moisture

This waterproof smartphone with two SIM cards (Cubot X10) has a declared degree of protection IP65. What does this marking mean? The first digit tells the buyer the degree of protection that the shell creates:

  1. Protection of people from contact with hazardous parts: penetration into the shell of a product that a person holds is prevented or limited.
  2. Protection of equipment located inside the shell from contact with external solid objects.

For this model, the second item is the most relevant. The number 6 indicates that the device is dustproof. In fact, there is complete protection against contact with dust.

Indeed, this smartphone is non-separable, has no joints, crevices, its connectors are tightly covered with plugs, so that dust cannot penetrate the device.

The number 5 symbolizes protection against water flow. You can check this item yourself: pour the smartphone with tap water. You will see that the device will remain operational after the test. But if you decide to take a dip with the phone in the pool, watch out! Through the earpiece, water will get inside, and you will have to dry the device for a long time with a hairdryer.

Of course, the manufacturer does not claim that the product will be completely protected from water. And how are technically waterproof smartphones equipped with 2 SIM cards of this model equipped? Yes, just great! Each device has an eight-core processor, thanks to which you can run any modern game.
