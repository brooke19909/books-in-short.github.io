What is he like?





The Philips Xenium X623 has a 5-megapixel camera, equipped with fully automatic focusing, a fairly high-quality screen matrix, an additional microphone that provides effective noise suppression during a conversation, as well as a specialized light sensor that automatically adjusts the screen backlight to the surroundings.

The positioning of this model is quite simple. Design

The design of the device is strict and classic. The Philips Xenium X623 is rectangular and has only slightly smoothed edges. The upper half is slightly flattened, while the lower half is slightly sharpened. The back cover, the rim of the camera and a certain part of the front panel are made of specialized metal, which is painted black. The lower edge and the edging are also black, but in this case, the material is already semi-gloss plastic. A bronze insert located on the upper end of the mobile phone is also made from it.

How reliable is the case?

During the long-term operation of this device, no creaking, backlash or crunching appears even if the phone is squeezed hard enough. Of course, against the background of standard mobile devices that most modern users are used to, such a device will be somewhat heavy, but at the same time it is quite comfortable in the hand thanks to its pleasant streamlined shape, small width and extremely balanced weight.

Fingerprints and other traces can remain exclusively on the Philips Xenium X623 display, and at the same time erasing them is not so easy as it might seem at first glance, so many users of this device recommend using all kinds of protective films.

At the top of the front panel there is a specialized light sensor. This device is intended to automatically change the power of the backlight depending on how lit is the whole room. In the predominant majority of cases, it is customary to install such sensors on modern touchscreen smartphones, because there is a fairly strong energy consumption in screens with a large diagonal.

Speaker

Special attention should be paid to the fact that the Philips Xenium X623 cell phone has a fairly quiet speaker, as a result of which it often becomes difficult to talk with the other person in a noisy place, but the developers compensated for the speaker volume not too high with an extremely high degree of intelligibility and clarity of sound. The device has a special system of subtractive dynamic noise reduction, that is, any sounds are initially passed through a large series of filters and only then are sent cleaned to the subscriber.

Special attention should be paid to the fact that this phone provides a hot-swap function.

On the right side there is a mechanical button that activates the camera, as well as two separate keys designed to control the volume. Display





The display of this device is quite standard, and its diagonal is 2.4 inches. The resolution of this screen is 240x320 pixels, while its density is 166 pixels per inch. Battery

The battery for Philips Xenium X623 is quite voluminous, and the charge indicator is located in the upper right part of the screen and provides for two divisions.

The mobile phone itself includes a lithium-ion battery with a capacity of 2000 mAh. The manufacturer says that in standby mode the device can work for more than 50 days, while while maintaining a constant conversation will work for 23 hours. Thus, on average, the Philips Xenium X623 battery allows you to maintain the phone in working condition for about a week.

During testing, the information received by the manufacturer was only partially confirmed, because during the conversation this phone lasted only 17 hours, while in standby mode it remained in working condition for about a month. If you listen to music on the phone, the battery lasts about 30 hours, regardless of which headset is used for the phone.

Charging

Charging in the kit is delivered rather weak, since with its help to fully recharge the battery you need to spend more than three hours. However, if you use a standard USB cable, the total time of this process will increase even more.

Performance and menus

The device operates on the so-called proprietary operating system, but during this time almost none of the users said that it slows down or freezes during its operation.
