Pillar of Katskhi in Georgia. Church on the pillar of Katskhi in Georgia

Georgia is a very beautiful state located in the western part of Transcaucasia. There are many natural and architectural attractions that deserve a visit. And how unique is the Georgian flavor! One of the important places in the republic is the pillar of Katshi. In Georgia, this place for tourists is considered relatively new, but has already managed to earn popularity. They say that soon it will become a tourist center of Imereti.

Where is the attraction located?

The pillar is located in Imereti - a region and a historical region in the western part of the state, near the city of Chiatura. The village in which the attraction is located is called Katshi. Actually, from it comes the name of the pillar. The rock rises above the surrounding area, so it can be seen from afar. But the impression that the pillar is close, deceptive: after the attraction falls into view, you need to go to it along the mountain road for about an hour.

What is the pillar of Katshi in Georgia

This hill is a 40-meter stone rock, consisting of a calcareous monolith, common in Imereti. At its top is a temple, which is also clearly visible from afar. The rock is narrow, with a metal staircase leading down from its top. The Katskhi Pillar is a unique natural attraction. Locals believe that the hill was created by God, and symbolizes the unity of heaven and earth, namely, the Lord and the people. When a person climbs up the stairs, he draws near to God.

History of the Imereti Pillar

The first mention of the pillar dates back to the 18th century. Then the Georgian geographer and historian Vahushti Bagrationi wrote about him. But a relatively new attraction appeared much earlier. Historians suggest that the church located above was built by pagans in the 6th-7th centuries as a thanks to the fertility god for his generous gifts.

When Christianity came to the republic, the people began to perceive the pillar of Katskhi in Georgia as a religious building. This place seems to have been specially created to lead an ascetic lifestyle. Locals believed that when a person is above, he becomes as close as possible to God. But during the reign of Ottoman Turkey, the temple lost its meaning. First, they stopped holding religious ceremonies, and then completely abandoned it.

The temple returned its fame only in the year 44 of the last century, when the famous climber Alexander Dzhaparidze and Levan Gotua decided to climb the summit. They determined that the temple was previously located on a rock. After the establishment of this fact, local residents began to make pilgrimages to the pillar.

Church on the pillar of Katskhi in Georgia (Imereti) today

The temple, built on a rock between the VI-XVII centuries, is poorly preserved. When A. Dzhaparidze and L. Gotua managed to climb the summit, they saw that there were only ruins, several elements of the fresco and a crypt with the burial of the last monk-pillar. A little later, local residents initiated the collection of finances for the restoration of the monastery. Fixed assets from the state budget were allocated in 1999. The construction of the new temple was completed 10 years later.

Even before the restoration of the temple, in 1993, Maxim the Confessor moved to the village. For two years he lived in a grotto under a stone column, hiding almost under a tarp alone. Actually, this prompted the people to raise funds to restore the pillar of Katskhi in Georgia.

First, material assistance came from a friend of Maximus the Confessor. Then he turned to the local diocese with a request to allow him to rebuild the temple. After receiving approval, they opened a fund and began to raise funds. The restoration was carried out on their own - this was done by local residents and monks. A device for lifting building materials upward was also constructed independently. The new temple was named after the hermit Maxim. A little later, one of the locals paved the access road, then installed a staircase and led lighting.

What else is on top of the Katskhi pillar

Over the past few years, the territory of the temple has become larger. In addition to the church and the hermit's cloister, two more monks, one novice and deacon, live on the territory. But they are located downstairs in recently built houses. A small museum is also organized here, which stores archaeological artifacts found during excavations of the ruins of the old temple, and photographs of the church.

Possibility of visiting the Katskhi pillar

For some time now, climbing uphill has been forbidden not only to women, but also to men. If you want to go to the temple, you will have to ask for approval from the Georgian patriarch in Tbilisi, who gave instructions to ban absolutely everyone from going upstairs.

How to get to the pillar of Katskhi in Georgia? The village where the religious attraction is located is located 28 km from the town of Zestafoni, which you need to go along the S22 highway towards Chiatura. If you plan a route in Georgia, it will be convenient to include this point between visiting Tbilisi and Kutaisi. Also, the village can be reached from Tbilisi by public transport, and then ask the locals for directions. In order to understand the location of the attraction, it is recommended to see the map.
