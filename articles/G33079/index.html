Holidays in Portugal in November

Portugal is a small country on the very edge of Europe, full of ancient traditions, rich culture, national color, delicious cuisine and hospitable locals. There are ancient cities, breathtaking landscapes, as well as an alluring ocean. Holidays in this country are good at any time of the year. Portugal in November is also interesting for a trip, as in the warmer months. After all, visiting sights, on the contrary, is better in a cooler time, besides when there is no influx of tourists.



Portugal: November temperature

The weather in Portugal by the end of autumn is generally quite comfortable. Daytime temperatures range between 17-19 degrees. At night, as a rule, the thermometer does not fall below 11. At the end of autumn, the country has the fewest sunny days. The weather in Portugal in November is generally comfortable for long walks, long excursions, trips to small charming towns, which are numerous in the country. It often rains and cloudy weather lasts for almost a month.



Water temperature does not differ much from air temperature and fluctuates on average in the country from 16 to 17 degrees.

Where better to relax in late autumn in Portugal

In Lisbon and Porto at the end of autumn it is not as comfortable to relax as in summer, and the reason for this is the rains.



The coldest in Evora. There, the average daytime temperatures are about 15-16 degrees, and at night the thermometer drops to 8 degrees. In Porto, there is also a cooling down to 8 degrees, but in the afternoon there are 17-18 degrees.

The temperature in the Algarve, in the south of the state, practically does not change. There is water and air in the daytime - about 19-20 degrees, which is warmer than in the capital and elsewhere in the country (on its mainland). It is quite comfortable temperature for relaxation.

In addition to the mainland, there are still islands such as Madeira and Azores. In Madeira, temperatures fall slightly in late autumn and the water remains around 20 degrees. It is perhaps best to rest there. Thanks to the warm Gulf Stream, the water remains suitable for swimming, and the autumn rains are so warm that they will not spoil the impression of relaxation.

In the Azores, it is not particularly hot in the summer, and in November the temperature does not drop much, only a few degrees. So, November is the time to visit the emerald islands in the heart of the Atlantic.

Festivals and holidays in late autumn

Portugal is a country with an interesting culture and people with a wide soul, so the holidays are held on a grand scale, and tourists from all over the world come to local festivals.



11th in the whole country celebrate St. Martin, which marks the end of agricultural work. The streets are solemn processions, and children make lanterns of pumpkin and other vegetables. A special treat at the festival are roasted chestnuts.

With truly Portuguese proportions, the Day of All Saints is celebrated here on the 1st day of the month.

Portugal also hosts a horse fair in November. At the beginning of the month, hundreds of horse lovers, in particular Lusitano breeds, gather in the very heart of the country in the small town of Golega. Great grooms, gorgeous costumes and more are sold here.

Last weekend of the month, a fun festival of sweet potatoes is held in Aljezur. It offers fun contests, various tastings, as well as a lot of sweet potato!

Leisure activities in Portugal in late autumn

Portugal in November is perfect for surfing enthusiasts. The water temperature is comfortable, the waves are excellent, while there is no such influx of athletes, as in October, when the country hosts the World Surfer Festival.

In late autumn, it's time to learn to stay on the waves. It offers vacationers - numerous surf schools in Baleal, Peniche, Ericeira. The coastline has a huge number of beaches, there are about 300. There are many schools in the "capital of the waves" - Peniche. Here, anyone, both a beginner and a professional, will find his spot.

Excursion vacations in November in the native country of Columbus

Tours in Portugal in November are ideal for those who want to get acquainted with the culture of the ancient country without annoying crowds of other tourists. Lisbon is one of the three most visited capitals in Europe, but in November there are not many people here. You can take a leisurely walk around the Jeronimos Monastery, St. George's Castle and other attractions of the city, ride the famous yellow trams and try the freshest ocean fish.

Not far from the capital there is a small town of Cascais, where the famous cliff of the Devil's Mouth is located. It is also worth a trip to Sintra, where the colorful Palace of Pena is located, as well as the Battle with its unique abbey with carved fairy-tale elements in architecture.

Portugal is full of small charming towns. Wander through the narrow streets of Obidos, maneuvering between snow-white houses with a tiled roof, and tired, treat yourself with cherry ginja liquor.

Beach holidays in the fall in the homeland of Columbus

Portugal in early November in its southern regions is still suitable for a beach holiday. On the sandy beaches of Albufeira, Algarve, you can fully enjoy the last sunny days of the year, without the annoying heat.





The weather in Portugal in November is comfortable for sunbathing. You can fully enjoy Portuguese cuisine on Nazare beach in the town of Leiria, learn to ride the waves in Ribeira d'Illas in Ericeira, admire the luxurious ethereal sunsets on the white sand of Adraga beach, wander between the golden cliffs on the coast of Don Ana.

Portugal with children in late autumn

The determining reason for choosing Portugal for holidays with kids in November is the weather. Reviews about holidays with children are mostly positive. Although there are almost no large chain hotels with animation and entertainment from morning to evening, here there are comfortable mild temperatures.

The main region for families with kids is the Algarve. Here, golden sand, mild climate, beautiful landscapes, and in the parks and entertainment centers in the area you can ride on water slides, various attractions. There are also zoos, aquariums, and the beautiful Ria Formos Park, where pink flamingos live.

Hydrangea and hibiscus are blooming here, and happy cows graze on green meadows. You can go on a tour of the island, ride the world famous "cheesecakes" - tobagans, see the life of real volcanoes, and all this without the sweltering heat and heat.
