What is the effect of an old photo

In the early 60's, people had the opportunity to take only black and white photos. Such a service was usually carried out in photo shops, since in our country at that time there were not enough cameras on sale. After a decade and a half, color photos appeared, which were also taken only by professionals. True, at this time it was already possible to buy your own camera without any problems. The pictures that were obtained using this technique pleased all shades of black and gray on a white background. Then came the improved apparatuses, which the people nicknamed "soap dishes." With their help, color photos were obtained.

Over time, color photography began to gain new momentum. Cameras capable of making high-quality and resolution cards began to appear. Now they have been replaced by professional SLR cameras, capable of accurately transmitting the picture that the human eye sees.

However, fashion dictates its own rules. People gradually began to ‚Äúmove away‚Äù from professional shots, preferring retro-style photos. Since it is now almost impossible to purchase a camera from the 60s, the effect of the old photograph in the pictures is achieved by other methods.

How to make a retro photo on Photoshop

To give photos a vintage look using Adobe Photoshop, you need to use high-quality textures with scratches and damage. As the main subject in the picture, it is best to choose some old object to make the photo look even more realistic. For example, it can be a road bridge built in the 60s. In this case, it will be necessary to "crop" all modern objects from the photo - cars, signs, people in clothing of the 21st century.

After that, you need to select the desired texture and paste it into the photo. To do this, use the Multiply mode. Using the Free Transform tool, you need to reduce the size of the texture and place it above the photo, and then save the changes.

In the same way, you can apply another layer of texture to the photo, if necessary, reduce its "opacity". If the original photo looks too sharp, you need to blur all the images a little.

Create vintage photos with Lightroom

Lightroom is another popular photo-processing application among photographers, which allows you to achieve the effect of a film on a photo. You can turn any photo into a frame from a movie in three simple steps.

First you need to make a dark gray background from black, which is characteristic of all the pictures of that time. To do this, you need to work with the Curves tool. Having chosen the point display of the curves, you need to drag the black point (located at the very bottom) a little higher. After that, all the black colors in the photo instantly acquire a dark gray hue.

Then the same manipulation must be repeated with the point responsible for the white color (located at the very top). It needs to be dragged a little lower. So all the white objects in the photo will become light gray.

After that, you need to work with color in the Hue, Saturation and Luminance section. It is recommended to lower the value of blue, preferring green. Also, a vintage effect is achieved by using yellow and purple colors. The graininess in the photo is another important element that gives the old look. The grain size can be changed in the Develop module by selecting the Effects panel section and the Adding grain subsection.

Tips from experienced photographers

Professionals know how to make the effect of an old photograph so that the pictures look natural and unobtrusive. Beginners often overdo it with filters, glare and grain in the photo, thereby moving it away from the desired "ideal."

Experienced photographers advise you not to be afraid to follow your own intuition when processing images. Indeed, almost every instruction on the network, as a rule, is attached to a specific photo, so substituting it with absolutely any picture will not work.

It is important to learn how to ‚Äúfeel‚Äù the frame, how to work with light, shades, and how to properly apply filters. Then all the photos will look really high-quality and natural. To achieve this result, it will take a lot of practice, experimentation, the study of similar works and attempts to reproduce them.

You can start learning from ordinary photos stored on your phone, try to process them using different programs, apply and adjust filters, change additional settings. When it starts to work out, it's worth starting a more professional photo processing, for example, using Photoshop and Lightroom.
