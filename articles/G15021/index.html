Location and history of the city

First of all, you need to find out where the city of Nancy (France) is located.

Nancy is a town and commune in France. The village is located on the Moselle River, at the place where it is crossed by the Marne Rhine Canal. France is a transcontinental state, the main territory of which is located in Western Europe. The country's motto is ‚ÄúFreedom, Equality and Brotherhood‚Äù. France is a nuclear power; it is on the list of five permanent members of the UN Security Council. It is worth noting that this is a highly developed industrial-agricultural country. The economy is based on the mining of iron and uranium ores, bauxites. Of great importance is agriculture and winemaking. In addition, tourism is superbly developed.

The city of Nancy (France) began to be mentioned only in the 7th century. In the 12th century the settlement became the capital of the Duchy of Lorraine. In 1766, Nancy became part of France. During the Franco-Prussian war of the late 19th century, it was occupied by German troops, but after the conclusion of a peace treaty remained in the French Republic. During World War II, fierce battles were fought near the city, after which there was a hotbed of the Resistance movement. Only in the autumn of 1944 was the city liberated.

The climate here is temperate continental. In summer, the average temperature does not rise above +26 ... + 28 degrees. In winter, the thermometer bars do not fall below 0 degrees.
