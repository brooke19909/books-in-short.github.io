Caucasian beauty

Not only in Russia people are interested in what nature reserves are in our country. The name of the Caucasian Nature Reserve, the largest in Europe, is known abroad. Its total area is two hundred eighty hectares.

Unique mountain and foothill ecosystems have been preserved here. Most of the reserve is covered by untouched forest. Alpine meadows, as well as glacial landscapes , lie on the rest of the territory. The extraordinary diversity of flora and fauna, including rare species and populations, attracts the interest of tourists and scientists. The main pride of the reserve is fir forests. The Caucasus Conservation Area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Siberian pride

Kuznetsk Alatau, located in the south of Central Siberia, is a vivid example of what nature reserves are in our country. This place is amazing and unique due to its ecosystem. Altai nature

The list of world cultural and natural heritage adorns the Altai Reserve. The area surrounded by high mountains is occupied by a wild forest, which is only occasionally traced by the modest paths of foresters. There is not a single highway in the reserve. This unique place is rich in lakes, the number of which is 1190. The huge Teletskoye Lake, adjacent to the protected area, closes the reservoirs with cold clear water. Landscapes of extraordinary beauty open from Belukha Mountain, the highest Altai mountain. An extremely rare and noble beast lurking here is listed in the Red Book - the snow leopard.

Seven Wonders of Russia

What nature reserves are there in our country on the Kamchatka Peninsula? There is one of the oldest protected reserves - Kronotsky. It adjoins the Pacific Ocean, but it is famous for not its geographical location, but the active volcano called Kronotskaya Sopka, a large number of waterfalls, thermal lakes and the stunning Geyser Valley, included in the list of Seven Wonders of Russia.





"Ice" reserve

The Big Arctic Reserve is the largest reserve in all of Eurasia. This area is covered with ice and glaciers, there are arctic deserts and tundra. The coastline is washed by the Arctic Ocean. It is here that the natural abode of polar bears is located.

Unique swamps

If tourists come to the Arctic cold, then they rarely dare to enter the Vasyugan marshes of Western Siberia. These huge swamps flooded with water have become home to rare animals and birds, but people here are infrequent guests.





Nature of the Long East

The Far Eastern Marine Reserve was created with the aim of preserving the rarest species of marine and coastal representatives of the plant and animal world. The sight of the tourist will please the clear sea, narrow grassy beaches, deciduous forests. Unfortunately, some species of the reserve are endangered.
