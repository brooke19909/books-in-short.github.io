Off-balance accounts

The principle of the balance sheet is the double registration of events that are reflected in the duplication of loans and debit accounts. If necessary, off-balance accounts are also used that do not fall into the balance, since they are not displayed as a double entry. For various reasons, many objects are recorded through a single entry, in which case the account becomes credit or debit.

Off-balance accounts are used for registration and reflection of objects that are not owned by the company by the right of ownership, but are in temporary use. This group includes the main leased assets, goods and material assets that are taken for safekeeping, accepted for commission, as well as equipment accepted for installation, and the main assets leased out.

The indicated objects are taken into account according to a simple scheme on single accounts. Be sure to keep off-balance sheet accounts and analytical accounting.

Off-balance accounts are used to summarize data on the movement and the presence of temporarily held assets of the organization, contingent liabilities and rights. In addition, they are used to control certain business transactions.

The system of accounts of accounting includes the following accounts (off-balance).

Fixed assets leased are displayed on account 001, which is intended to reflect data on the movement and availability of fixed assets that are leased by the enterprise. Analytical accounting is made for each object (according to inventory numbers), for lessors. Fixed leased assets that are located outside the country are accounted for separately.
