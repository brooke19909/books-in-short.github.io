Snow-covered hut of Santa Claus



Decor from natural material looks very impressive. Try to make a snow-covered hut. Collect in advance a sufficient number of medium-thick twigs, rinse and dry them well. Prepare the base - glue the walls of the house from cardboard or use a box of a suitable size. Windows and doors can be cut through or done using the applique technique. Glue the prepared frame with twigs, vertically or horizontally, cutting a suitable length. Make a roof separately from two identical rectangles or one bent in the middle. Go to the decor: the winter house should be all snowy or at least covered with hoarfrost. Use white paint, styrofoam, cotton wool or sparkles for this. New Year's house from waste material



You may be surprised, but in fact, you can make interesting crafts from the most banal materials that can be found today in any home. Empty plastic bottles and food trays, cardboard boxes from products - all this we throw away every day. The beautiful hand- made article "Winter House of Santa Claus" with your own hands can be made precisely from such waste material. As a basis, take a cardboard box of milk or kefir, the bottom part of a plastic bottle or a small square container is also suitable. An interesting idea is to use toilet paper bushes or wallpaper tubes for the manufacture of house walls. If necessary, cut the selected workpiece, then glue it with white paper or paint it. When the facade of the future "building" dries, you can make a roof, windows and door. Your winter house will turn out to be more interesting if you combine several design techniques at once and use various materials for decoration.

How to make snowdrifts?

Souvenirs that are ‚Äúnoticed‚Äù or covered with snow on the sides look interesting. How to make such snow caps at home? This is easier than it might seem at first glance: pour PVA glue into some container and moisten a thin layer of cotton wool in it. Squeeze out the excess and spread a piece of mass on the base, gently pressing. Thus, you can decorate the entire roof, the stand on which the house stands, or form real snowdrifts near the walls and porch. In a similar technique, you can try to create artificial snow from thick white paper napkins. Craft "Winter House" can be snowed in another way. Coat the base with clear glue and sprinkle liberally with sugar, salt or semolina. After creating the snow cover, leave the craft to dry for at least 4 hours, and even better - for the whole night.
