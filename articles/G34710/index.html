Attractions Port El Kantaoui

Resting in El Mouradi Port El Kantaoui 4 * (Tunisia), you should not be limited only to the beach and the sea, you should definitely look at local attractions and visit the city of Sousse.

In Port El Kantaoui itself, there are no architectural or historical sites. The resort area was built in the nineties. To see interesting places, you need to go to Sousse, where you can see the colorful medina, the Khalifa tower, a large mosque, the house museum of Dar Essid.

Sights of the city of Sousse

Speaking about the resort, it is necessary to say a few words about the famous city of Sousse, to which the Port El Kantaoui area belongs. Sousse is the country's most popular youth resort. There is a huge number of hotels: from the simplest to five-star luxury. The city has a large amount of entertainment for tourists. Sousse is conditionally divided into three parts. For vacationers, the old part of the city with historical monuments and the northern one, in which all the hotels are concentrated, are most interesting. The central area is filled with ordinary residential buildings.

All the attractions of Sousse are concentrated in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Medina. You can see them without using transport. One day will be enough. Tourists love to just walk in the quiet streets of the Medina. You can see the city on your own, without the help of guides. But for trips to distant places it is better to use sightseeing tours.



On the winding streets of the old town there are houses in blue and white colors, creating a unique oriental atmosphere. Medina of Sousse is a stunningly beautiful place that you definitely need to see. It was built in the ninth century and is under the protection of UNESCO. The medina is surrounded by powerful stone walls that protect against attacks. On its territory are towers, ancient mosques, catacombs with burials of the era of early Christianity, a market.

In the medieval fortress of Ribat, you should see the tower of Khalifa. The fortress was built during the reign of the Aglabites, it has a square shape and has been well preserved to this day. Its main purpose was to repel attacks from the sea. In peacetime, the soldiers who served here studied Islam and prayed. Behind the ancient walls of Ribbat is the courtyard, where you can see the cells-cells. Tourists are very fond of visiting the second floor, where there is a footprint between the columns. There is a legend saying that, having stepped on the trail, one should make a wish, it will certainly come true. From the high walls there is a wonderful view of the surroundings and Medina.

In Sousse you can see the Sofra reservoir. It is a large tank about thirty meters long, going deep underground. The dough itself is surrounded by a wall. It is still not known exactly who built this reservoir: either the Arabs, or the Romans. But at one time the cistern was a source of water for the townspeople. Liquid was delivered to it through a pipe. There is no artificial lighting inside the reservoir; light penetrates into it only through small openings in the roof.

Near the Ribat fortress is a large mosque with two minaret towers. Over its long history, it has been rebuilt several times, although it looks very simple in appearance, without pomp and luxury. Perhaps she served as an additional protective structure along with the fortress. Inside it there is a courtyard and prayer rooms. Tourists can examine it, but it is strictly forbidden for non-Muslims to go inside the rooms where they pray.

There is a city museum in Sousse located in an old castle. In it you can see statues, mosaics, collections of masks. No less interesting are the Oasis Perk El Kantaoui Botanical Gardens and the Museum of Archeology.
