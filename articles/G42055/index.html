"Megaphone - All Inclusive" (150 rubles): tariffs and connection

It is no secret that when working with mobile telecom operators, a more profitable approach is to order an integrated tariff plan, and not each service separately. For example, you need to pay for a tariff that provides 100 SMS, minutes for calls and megabytes of the Internet than if you purchased the same amount of services separately.

Today we will talk about tariff plans from the Russian operator Megafon. They are designed just for the purchase of "all at once" at a lower price. The package is called, which offers "Megaphone", "All inclusive". 150 rubles a month - such a starting price it was at the start of the service.

After some time, the cost increased to 199 rubles - it is for this price that subscribers are now ordering these services. Nevertheless, their essence has not changed. Read about what these tariffs are and how beneficial they are for ordinary subscribers.

For whom?

To begin with, the rates given in this article are suitable for a wide range of users, starting with those who use the phone only for calls and ending with subscribers who regularly watch movies on their tablet. This is the advantage of the Megafon All Inclusive tariff plan: 150 rubles (or rather 199) is a single fee that is required to receive a range of services, both minutes for calls and traffic for using Internet access. Actually, due to this, the tariff plan also attracts people of various age groups and dissimilar preferences. This is a truly universal solution for your smartphone.





For which devices?

By the way, regarding the gadget with which this tariff will ideally manifest itself, it is definitely quite difficult to say anything. What does Megafon declare on its official website? "All inclusive" (150 rubles, of course, it will not cost anymore) is ideal for working with a smartphone. True, the tariff is divided into 5 more plans - XS, S, M, L and VIP. The logic of the developers is quite simple: each subsequent plan is more expensive than the previous one, but also provides more services. Thus, if we take, for example, the penultimate tariff in the line ‚ÄúMegaphone - All Inclusive L‚Äù, within the framework of which 8 GB of traffic in 3G or 4G format is provided (at the choice of the subscriber), we can confidently say that this is enough for online games, watching TV shows and even several movies online. So can we say with confidence that these tariffs are exclusively for smartphones? Unlikely. It all depends primarily on the intensity of Internet use by the subscriber.

Tariff Features





An important nuance in working with this set of plans is debiting and charging data. In the event that at the time of the transition to the service or its activation by the end of the month exactly half of it remains, the user is charged a monthly fee divided in half. The same rule is observed if the proportion changes: how many days remain until the end of the service period ‚Äî the subscriber will pay the same part of the monthly fee for the tariff. Funds are withdrawn in full in one payment.

Another important point concerns the Internet. Formally, within each tariff, the user is provided with a certain amount of traffic, for example 4 GB. However, after it is consumed, the service does not turn off - just the data transfer rate drops to 64 kbps. This, of course, causes inconvenience to the subscriber, but allows you to download the page of the site or update the application of the social network.

International challenges

Billing international calls for subscribers served on plans "All inclusive" is the same. The main criterion for how much a minute of call will cost is the user's territorial location. In particular, calls to neighboring countries - the CIS, Abkhazia, Georgia and Ukraine, are charged at 20 rubles per minute. Calling to European countries, as well as to such popular destinations as the USA and Canada, is possible at a price of 30 rubles per minute. A minute of calling to Australia costs 40 rubles, and to Asia - 50. Other countries are charged roaming at 60 rubles per minute. The most expensive for calls can be called territories where only satellite communications are active. A minute of a call from there will cost a subscriber of Megaphone 313 rubles. Recall that these figures are equivalent for each of the five tariff plans in question today.
