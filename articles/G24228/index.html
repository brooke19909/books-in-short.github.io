Rosemary Kennedy: biography, the consequences of lobotomy

Rosemary Kennedy is the daughter of a famous family whose life, until recently, was shrouded in mystery. The name of this woman became widely known after the publication of the book "Vanished Kennedy", authored by E. Kohler-Pentacroff. What was the reason that the beloved child was hidden from the whole world and put into oblivion? Look for the answer to this article.

While nothing portended trouble ...

The Kennedy family could not even think that such a joyful event - the birth of the first daughter, who was already the third child, would eventually be overshadowed by tragic events. In the meantime, the bright and joyful day of September 13, 1918 was beautiful and happy. In such a respectable family, a very beautiful and healthy girl appeared very similar to her mother.

The child was given the name Rose Mary Kennedy. Parents who were at the time of birth: mother - 28 years old, father - 30 - called her loving Rosie or Rosemary.

First signals

At first, the baby was no different from other newborn children, but over time, parents noticed that the child still lags behind their peers in development. Rosemary was not the first child in this family, so there was nobody to compare with. It was noticeable that she began to crawl, speak and walk much later than the due date.

The surviving parents soon calmed down, because the luminaries of medicine who examined the girl promised that everything would work out soon - the child would catch up with his peers, and there was nothing wrong with this lag. However, this time the doctors issued an erroneous verdict. It soon became clear that Rosemary Kennedy was really behind development, she was mentally retarded.

Family decision

The first reaction to such a terrible diagnosis was dictated by a huge tenderness for an innocent and deeply beloved child. The Kennedy family decided to work together to make the little girl live a happy life and be surrounded by love and attention.



The father of the family insisted that the daughter would live with them. All offers to send the girl to specialized institutions were rejected. Rosemary's parents and siblings hoped they could heal her with their love.

Witnesses say that over the course of several years this attitude towards the girl really yielded results.

Lobotomy: a brief medical report

Lobotomy of the brain is an operation associated with the destruction of the cortex of the frontal lobes of the brain. Previously, this surgery was carried out in order to facilitate the course of diseases such as depression, schizophrenia, psychosis, criminal behavior, anxious fears and conditions. Nowadays, such an operation is practically not done.





Lobotomy often causes a complete change in personality, leading to mental inferiority.

Medical statistics say that from 1936 to 1951 in the United States more than 18 thousand patients underwent this operation. In the USSR since 1940, lobotomy is prohibited.
