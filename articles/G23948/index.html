Borovets Church

The city of Naberezhnye Chelny is famous for its cultural architectural monuments. Among them, there are those that relate to memorials of republican significance. They are protected by the state. These include the Holy Ascension Church. These include the Borovets Church.

Initially, her walls were wooden, later they became stone. The Three Altar Church of Borovets was erected (for the most part) at the expense of the famous writer and Elabuga merchant Dmitry Ivanovich Stacheev.

Before the revolution, the parish of the temple totaled 2 thousand people. After the revolution, the building left almost ruins. And only the efforts of the parishioners, workers and builders in 1989 helped to rebuild the temple (by the way, the highest in the area). Thanks to their efforts, a new high bell tower appeared at the church; a beautiful openwork fence around the territory of the parish. And the area around the temple is more like a park area.

Nur Ihlas Mosque

About 20 years ago, the largest Muslim religious building appeared on the territory of the city. "Nur-Ihlas" (translated as "Fidelity") is a building built according to all the canons of modernity, but the style of architecture is more reminiscent of post-modern.





According to various sources, the construction of the mosque began in 1993 and continued until 2005. V.A. became the project architect. Manukyan. The building can accommodate 500 people. Remarkably, different entrances are intended for men and women. High portals at the doors, a hemispherical dome, mosque volumes of different heights, flat coverings, a high steeple-shaped minaret - it would seem that all this does not combine. But how beautifully and restrained it reflects at the same time the culture of stone architecture of the Volga Bulgaria and the modern Tatar style.

How wonderful it will be if you manage to visit Victory Park. Naberezhnye Chelny is beckoning to see cultural and architectural monuments. Nur-Ihlas is the only mosque of its kind in size and beauty. The picturesque volumetric composition attracts tourists due to the unique combination of East Muslim, traditional Tatar and Bulgarian cultures in the external and internal decoration of the religious building.

The mosque was erected in 1989-1992. on the occasion of a significant date: 1100th anniversary of the adoption of Islam by the Bulgarian state. According to the ideas of the authors of the project, the construction was erected in the Art Nouveau style, while the tent covering of the hall and minaret remained traditional.
