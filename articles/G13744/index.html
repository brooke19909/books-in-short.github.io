Changes in exchange rates in 2015

The whole of 2015 was very difficult for the Russians due to the strong depreciation of the ruble. But by the end of the year, experts are reporting positive changes that, under the same conditions, will lead to a course appreciation and stabilization. Today, political events in the world and a sharp drop in oil prices have affected the relationship between the ruble and the dollar. The news of the return of Iranian oil to the market, which was previously under US restrictive sanctions, has long been circulated. This greatly increases the demand for American currency and reduces the level of investment in the Russian market.





The current exchange rate situation

The expert assessment is very mixed and the opinions of many financial analysts regarding the forecasts for the Russian ruble do not coincide. Today, the price of oil does not fluctuate as much as at the beginning of the year, but many are inclined to believe that the capabilities of the Russian national currency are slightly exaggerated. Representatives of OPEC countries completely dictate the existing ruble exchange rate by setting the price of oil. By the end of 2015, another decrease in its value is expected. This refers to one of the seasonal fluctuations, but they do not affect the welfare of the country's inhabitants in periods of stability.

Now the fall and fluctuation of the exchange rate may become noticeable, but many analysts believe that after this the rise will begin quickly, and the marks will exceed the current rate. The beginning of 2016 promises to bring a strengthening currency. Stabilization

Despite the fact that the crisis was caused by foreign policy factors, state authorities can influence the situation from the inside:

  • introduction of the course on import substitution - production of our own goods instead of those that were previously imported (this allows us not only to fill the market with domestic products, but also to create new enterprises, renew and modernize suspended companies);
  • restricting the purchase of foreign currency, increasing the use of the national currency and the demand for it among the population;
  • introduction of own progressive payment system MIR.
