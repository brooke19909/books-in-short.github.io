Sky Sea Hotel 3 * (Turkey / Bodrum) - photos, prices and reviews of tourists

Bodrum is one of the most popular holiday centers in Turkey. The city, surrounded by numerous bays of the Aegean Sea and hotel complexes, attracts many tourists.

When choosing a hotel, it is useful to read detailed information and get acquainted with the reviews of other travelers. This article provides information about Sky Sea Hotel 3.

Bodrum

This is a small tourist town 300 km from Antalya, which is hardly typed by 35 thousand local residents, but tens of thousands of vacationers from around the world come in season.





Almost three thousand years ago, at this place stood the rich capital of ancient Greek Caria - the city of Halicarnassus, which was also called Zephyria because of the constant sea breeze (Zephyr is the god of wind in Greek mythology).

In the Middle Ages, the Knights of Rhodes built a castle here, which later became the fortress of the Ottoman Empire. Then the city acquired its current name - Bodrum, which in Turkish means "basement" (that is, the underground fortifications of the castle).

Now the city has gained fame as the noisiest and busiest resort in Turkey. Russian travelers, for example, call it ‚Äúpeppy Bodrum‚Äù, and English-speaking visitors call this international recreation center ‚Äúthe bed of Europe‚Äù (since the pronunciation of the word ‚ÄúBodrum‚Äù is similar to the English word ‚Äúbedroom‚Äù).

Here at every step there are bars, night clubs, restaurants, taverns and discos where tourists flock from hotel complexes built in fourteen bays of the ancient peninsula.

Vacationers living in the Sky Sea Hotel 3 (Bodrum) can visit any city in Turkey - buses go from Bodrum to anywhere. For example, to Gyukova Bay (with a transfer to Mugla), Pamukalle (10 minutes, via Denizli for 3 euros), Istanbul (13 hours), Izmir (4 hours) or Ankara (10 hours).

You can travel by car - renting a small car in Bodrum costs from 30 euros per day.

Climate and beaches

Bodrum has a temperate Mediterranean climate - it is not as stuffy here as in other resorts, thanks to the refreshing breeze from the Aegean Sea (which is cooler compared to the Mediterranean).

In summer, the temperature is quite comfortable, there is no such heat as in Antalya in Bodrum. On average, in July - 28 ¬∞ C, and in October - 21 ¬∞ C.

In ancient Zephyria, a gentle gentle wind blows from the sea, so fans of windsurfing, kitesurfing and other water sports gather on the beaches.

Sky Sea Hotel 3 (Bodrum, Bodrum) has its own beach, but according to tourists' reviews, it is almost not cleaned and is a rather dirty and rocky piece of wild coast.

Nearby there are many beaches of other hotels - also small pebble areas or concrete platforms with imported sand. There is also a public beach (250 m) - without sunbeds, but with beautiful sand, where there are no big waves (behind La Blanche, opposite the playground).

Many go swimming in the beach of the five-star Agean Dream Hotel, 100 meters from the Sky Sea Hotel 3. There is good access to the sea, excellent sand and sunbeds (everyone can come).

Hotel

Sky Sea Hotel 3 (Turkey) is an economy class hotel built in 1991 on the seafront near the famous tourist villages of Turgutreis (1 km) and Bodrum (18 km), which is 55 km from Kos Ippokratis Airport and 65 km from the airport of Dalaman

The hotel consists of one three-story building, including 40 rooms (35 standard, 3 Extra Large Rooms and 2 Suites). The cost of a standard room is from 1775 rubles per night.

The staff does not speak Russian. Below is an image of Sky Sea Hotel 3. The photo shows the general view of the building (many tourists believe that the advertising photographs of this hotel are retouched, but in reality it is quite ‚Äúragged‚Äù).





The hotel has an outdoor pool and a children's pool, 3 bars, a restaurant, a first-aid post, a laundry, a bathhouse (sauna and Turkish hammam - for a group of at least 15 people only), as well as currency exchange and mobile phone rental.

A safe is provided ($ 3 per day), beach towels are not issued, no animation and no kids club.

From the airport you can get to Bodrum for 8 euros by bus or 50 euros by taxi. The road takes 45 minutes.

Rooms

Standard rooms at Sky Sea Hotel 3 (Bodrum) include the following services:

  • Bathroom with shower and hairdryer (many tourists note a strong smell of sewage and rusty plumbing).
  • Paid air conditioning ($ 5 per day).
  • TV (no Russian channels).
  • Paid minibar.
  • Telephone.
  • Balcony with sea or pool view.




Guestrooms provide complimentary Internet access.





The floor is tiled. Change of linen and towels - on request.

The reviews noted such inconveniences as traces of water leaking on the ceiling, crumbling plaster over the beds, lack of a door in the shower, rusty plumbing and cockroaches in the room.

Hot water is heated by solar panels and served on schedule, so you can only take a shower during lunch.

Nutrition

Hotel Sky Sea Hotel 3 provides half board (HB), that is, breakfast and dinner are included, and you can have lunch at an additional cost in the hotel restaurant or in the surrounding taverns.

The menu is standard. Despite the declared "buffet", almost all dishes are served only portionwise (as reported in the reviews), the additive is not allowed (including in relation to water, tea and coffee, as well as wine and beer).

Breakfast: fried potatoes, boiled eggs, cheese, sausage (soy), vegetables, jam, watermelon.

Dinner: salads (tomato cucumbers), olives, grilled vegetables, rice-beans-potatoes (alternating), pasta (every day), chicken (once every 3 days), meat with vegetables (once every 10 days), fish ( every 14 days), a dessert of balls of dough in honey (rarely).

Pastries (buns with cheese) are served in the afternoon snack at 16.00 only in a limited quantity - one dish for the entire dining room, therefore a crush.

For breakfast and afternoon tea and coffee - for free (traditionally of poor quality). At dinner, you need to pay 2 lira for one cup of tea or coffee.

Regarding the food in Sky Sea Hotel 3, the reviews are mostly negative. It is reported that the portions are very small, and queues and scandals for a piece of bread are humiliating. The restaurant is constantly dirty tablecloths, there are no dishes for children. The highchair is only one and never washed.

Drinking water until 22.00, in the evening the cooler is removed. Pouring water into bottles and taking it with you is not allowed.

It is forbidden to bring your own food and drinks to the hotel restaurant (the staff simply takes them away).

It is also forbidden to shift tables (for a large friendly company) - this leads to noisy quarrels with staff.

Tourists report in reviews that you can eat deliciously in the nearby fish restaurants for 14 lire - cold appetizer, vegetable salad, grilled dorado, melons and watermelons. There is also a good inexpensive cafe for La Blanche.

Travel Tips

Experienced travelers give the following tips for travelers who are planning a vacation at Sky Sea Hotel 3:

  • Take food and drinks from Russia on the first day (night). If you are late for breakfast or dinner, you can stay hungry. In addition, arriving at Turkish airports, Russians immediately feel the heat and stuffiness, and a can of soda there costs 5 euros.
  • You need to pay for the remote control from the air conditioner at the reception, but our tourists turn on the air conditioning perfectly and without the remote control (you just need to learn before the trip).
  • Take a kettle and tea and coffee bags with you.
  • You can go to children's discos in the neighboring Agean Dream Hotel (at 9 p.m. except Saturday).
  • It is better to buy products at the Migros supermarket (go half an hour). At the first visit you need to buy a ‚Äúmoney card‚Äù - there will be good discounts
  • In the same Migros, you can successfully exchange currency - pay $ 100 for a penny and get change in lira at a very favorable rate
  • To buy all excursions in the city (from a hotel guide is twice as expensive). There is a good boat trip on the ship for only $ 20 (8 hours at sea with lunch and afternoon tea, and children are free).
  • The water park costs $ 22 (at a travel agency), children under 6 years old are free, pensioners from 60 years old are also free (no certificate is required).
