Where is it located and how did it originate?

If you look at Temryuk on a map, you can see that it is located in the center of the Temryuk district (in the Krasnodar Territory). The city is located on the right side of the Kuban River, in the place where it flows into the Temryuk Bay of the Sea of ‚Äã‚ÄãAzov.

Taman lands have always been attractive to people: mild climate, fertile soil, magnificent nature. Here, from time to time, small and rather large settlements appeared: they flourished, experienced times of death, some were reborn again, others were forever erased from the face of the earth. In the twelfth century, at this place was the Genoese colony of Cope. But it ceased to exist a century later as a result of the invasion of the Mongol-Tatars.

In 1556, Tumnev was destroyed by the Kabardian prince Temryuk. The Russian troops helped him in this. And at this place the commander erected a fortress, which he called his name. But the prince did not own it for long, only fifteen years. After this time, she became the property of the Crimean Khan, who called her Adis.

In 1778, Temryuk (Krasnodar Territory) became part of Russia. The Starotemryuk fortification was erected here. In the thirties of the nineteenth century, a hen belonging to the Black Sea Cossacks formed near it. In 1843 he was transformed into a village. And in 1860, the village became a full-fledged city with a certain status.



Temryuk in the twentieth century

At the beginning of the last century, a seaport began to operate in the city. As a result of the civil war, Temryuk was severely destroyed. He changed hands twice. No less damage was inflicted to the village during the Great Patriotic War.

At the end of hostilities, the importance of the Temryuk port fell significantly. The port began to revive after many decades, when the Soviet Union collapsed. Only at the end of the twentieth century Temryuk (Krasnodar Territory) was honored to receive the status of a port of international importance. Modern Temryuk is a tourist center and a famous port-type city. And at the beginning of this century, the entire Taman Peninsula was declared a federal resort.

Symbolism of Temryuk

Temryuk (its attractions and resorts) every year causes more and more interest among lovers of seaside recreation. For a more complete acquaintance with Temryuk, let's talk about his coat of arms and flag. The local coat of arms, as it is known today, was approved on December 29, 2005. The heraldic shield depicts the sea, a golden anchor, a bunch of grapes and two fish. An anchor symbolizes calm, reliability and firmness. A pair of fish is a symbol of the current city as a center of fisheries and fishing. A bunch of grapes has become a reflection of the main agricultural sector of the village. The flag is a simplified imitation of the city coat of arms. A lot of fun in Temryuk on the birthday of the city, which is celebrated annually on the last September weekend.
