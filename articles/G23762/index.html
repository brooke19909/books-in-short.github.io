The simplest of the crafts - in the style of origami

It is not necessary to be an experienced craftsman to get an unusual postcard-shirt made of paper. If you are just starting to master the ancient art of origami, this master class will help you learn how to perform the simplest techniques. Usually even elementary school students are happy to craft such gizmos, complementing them with their own fresh ideas. Perhaps in the future, becoming interested in miraculous transformations, they will express a desire to create something else from this series or move on to more complex models. Paper shirt - souvenir or postcard? Other ideas

For what purposes can I craft this craft? First of all, it can be just an unusual and pleasant surprise. Such a shirt, made of paper with one's own hands, will surprise many - after all, glue is not used to obtain it. The main skill is sleight of hand and an accurate eye. Being a folding subject, it can act in two roles - a greeting card with text written on its inner surface, and a mini-envelope for storing a message or money when giving at any holiday. If the purpose of your work is to create a special "little thing" as a keepsake, take care of additional decoration. To do this, you can combine colors, apply different symbols, complement the image according to the theme, provide a combination with other gifts, etc. There are no restrictions for imagination, so the result will depend directly on creative capabilities, spatial thinking and the availability of a variety of material for work.





Material needed

To get a shirt made of paper, you need to use moderately dense material. In addition to colored sheets with double-sided coloring, you can take medium-thickness cardboard. Holiday models will look great if you use a dense beautiful fabric, which tends to hold folds well when bending and twisting. Often use material like foil, but you need to work with it very carefully to avoid breakouts. In order not to make a mistake in the necessary proportions and not to bend the material several times in different places, it is advisable to do the training work first. To do this, take, for example, a newspaper and cut the template to the desired size. After going through all the stages from beginning to end, you can start working with good paper, without fear of making a mistake.

Make a tie

To make the craft look completed to the end, you need to complement it with a stylish accessory. For these purposes, you can fold out of paper, for example, a tie or bow-tie. We offer instructions for folding the first of these items:

  • To make a tie, take a square of paper and fold it diagonally. Then flatten and unfold in the form of a rhombus so that the fold is vertical.
  • Place two side corners up so that the sides are closed on the midline.
  • Wrap the workpiece around its axis.
  • Divide visually the product in height into three parts. Turn the top (sharp corner) forward, and then half from this superimposed part - back, but slightly not reaching the vertical top line.
  • Fold up the resulting part so that at the junction there is a crease separating the main tie web and the ‚Äúknot‚Äù.
  • Re-rotate the craft around its axis so that it is the front side on the table.
  • Fold the two sides to the middle, making the tie slightly narrower.
  • Fasten the top with the upper folds.
  • Unfold the tie facing you.
  • To fix it on a card, you can use a little glue.
