Townhouses - an old tradition or a modern invention?



Russians first heard about such a housing option as townhouses in the mid-90s of the last century. Over the past 10 years, real estate experts have noted a steady increase in the popularity of this category among buyers. Buying a Townhouse: Legal Details of the Deal



Not only the choice of a townhouse, but also its purchase has its own characteristics and subtleties. Typically, such houses are sold in shared ownership. From a legal point of view, the entire townhouse is a single property that can be sold to several buyers (each of which becomes the owner of one section). Before making a transaction, it is important to study the documents on the land on which the house stands. It is important to understand that the site adjacent to the purchased section does not become the property of the buyer.

There is no such thing as a town house in Russian law. Typically, houses in this category are decorated in much the same way as ordinary typical high-rise buildings. And this means that the owner of the land on which the building stands is HOA. Accordingly, the owner of the section in the townhouse can use and improve "his" land at his own request, but does not have the right to erect capital constructions on it.

Moreover, some residential areas have their own internal rules. They must also be examined before purchase. Often, owners are not allowed to significantly change the architectural appearance of the building and the adjacent territory. This means that it is possible to put garden furniture and break a flower garden, but you cannot build a high fence. Sale of a section in a townhouse is also carried out according to special rules. First you need to inform about the desire to sell your share of all other owners of the building. They have priority purchase rights. If none of the owners expressed a desire to buy a section for sale, you can make a deal with a third-party buyer.
