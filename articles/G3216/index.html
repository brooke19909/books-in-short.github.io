Knitting for beginners: socks (beginning) with knitting needles. Manufacturing tips and tricks

For beginner knitters, making socks seems like a lot of work. In fact, this is not so. Having understood all the "subtleties" of manufacturing this product, each needlewoman will be able to create her own warm and comfortable garment without problems. From our article you will learn how to properly knit socks with knitting needles. For beginners, there are many useful tips for making this wardrobe item. We study them and gain experience.



Choose yarn and knitting needles

To make knitting socks for beginners become initially a pleasant and easy task, you need to take care of the correct selection of threads and tools for the product. Much depends on it. The thickness of the thread and knitting needles should clearly correspond to each other. Otherwise, the knitting process may cause you difficulty. If the knitting needles are thicker than the thread, the fabric will be "holey" and stretched. If, on the contrary, the yarn is thick and the needles are thin, then the product will come out very dense. It will be difficult to knit it, since the loops will be very tight. An ideal option for making socks is considered to be yarn wool or half-wool 300 m in 100 g and knitting needles number 2.5.

How to calculate the number of loops?

We master knitting for beginners (socks). The beginning of this product consists in taking measurements and calculating the required number of loops. How to do it? We measure the volume of the legs around the ankle. Next, we knit a sample of the pattern with the selected yarn and knitting needles. We consider loops of 1 cm. For example, the leg volume is 23 cm. According to the sample of 1 cm of knitted fabric - 3 loops. So, you need 23 x 3 = 69. You need to dial 69 loops. But it will be more convenient to knit socks if the number of stitches is divisible by 4. So dial them 72.



Knitting for beginners: socks (beginning) with knitting needles. Choose a pattern

The upper part of the product, cuffs, is made with an elastic band 1 x 1 or 2 x 2. The canvas connected with these patterns is dense and elastic. In fact, there may be many gum options: 1 x 2, 3 x 3, English, French, wicker. The main thing here is for the gum to fulfill its function - it holds the product well on the leg, preventing it from sliding.

Similar patterns can be used to complete the entire cuff. But most often they make out 6-7 centimeters with an elastic band, and then they switch to the "front surface" pattern and perform it right up to the start of the heel. Why exactly this picture? The canvas connected by it has a smooth structure, quite dense and warm, and this is exactly what you need for winter socks. If you want to decorate the product with different colors of yarn, then it is in this part of the work that you can enter other strings.
