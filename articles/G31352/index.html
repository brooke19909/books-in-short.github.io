Crown of the British Empire: the story of creation

The history of the aforementioned crown has an interesting origin. It underwent many transformations, it was destroyed and restored again, collected in parts, made exact copies of previous samples. This is not just a monarch jewelry. The imperial crown of the British Empire is the property of the united kingdom, a symbol of power and position in society.

In the 17th century, England abandoned the monarchy. The British decided to completely get rid of all the attributes of the kingdom. They committed an act of vandalism, breaking the royal crown. Gemstones and pearls were seized and sold, gold was smelted. But this is far from all the trials that the crown of British power survived.

The history of the crown of St. Edward is generally shrouded in mystery. Quite controversial are all the legends regarding the relic. One of the stories says that the crown drowned with all the gold in 1216. But if you study the materials in detail, it becomes clear that she simply disappeared. There are no exact facts indicating that the crown has drowned. It may well be that it was hidden by John Landless. The next few centuries, the crown was remade by everyone who could. Gems changed over and over again. Her weight was constantly changing. She was constantly weighted and immediately facilitated. What remained unchanged in the crown of St. Edward was the design. It was a crown decorated with four crosses, alternating with lilies, over which towered a half-arc converging into a ball with a cross. The size of the product has also been repeatedly changed. In the mid-1600s, the crown will undergo tremendous changes again. Oliver Cromwell will give the name to the crown: ‚Äúa symbol of disgusting royal self-government‚Äù and order to get rid of it. In 1660, Charles II completely restored the symbol of the greatness of his dynasty. But the transfiguration of the royal crown will not stop there.

Wilhelms and Georges are thoroughly refined over the head symbol of kings and queens. The crowns of power during their reign will take on rather bizarre forms. And only in the early 1800s, Queen Victoria will stop this mess. She will establish a unified state relic. But fate decides otherwise - in 1845, during the parliamentary session, the court duke Argyll will drop the crown. And again the crown of St. Edward will become a symbol of the monarchy.

Changes awaited the crown of power back in 1937 and 1953, but they were completely insignificant. Until our time, the crown of the British Empire has remained unchanged.

Nowadays, the Queen of Great Britain does not wear the crown daily. It is worn only in two cases:

  1. Exclusively before the monarch goes to the coronation. It is strange that the symbol of the kingdom does not participate in the coronation.
  2. The monarch puts on a sign of power before the opening of the session of Parliament.
