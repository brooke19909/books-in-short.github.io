Supermhl

A key element in creating a user-friendly user interface has been the integration of SuperMHL technology. It provides a single connection of an 8K signal source (for example, set-top boxes, disk players, media streamers) with a TV. Previous prototypes required as many as four HDMI connections to provide sufficient bandwidth to carry video and audio.

In addition, SuperMHL provides ultra-high-definition video speeds of 120 fps (HDMI 2.0 allows only 24 fps) at 48-bit color depth with wide coverage according to the BT.2020 specification, power up to 40 watts.

Speaking of audio, the standard promoted by NHK supports 22.2 audio channels. This is more than enough to support all existing surround formats, as well as those that will appear in the future. Nevertheless, it remains to be seen whether this potential will be realized at the consumer level.

Quantum dots

A quantum dot is an artificial nanocrystal with semiconductor properties that are used to improve the brightness and color reproduction of static images and video on LCD screens. When photons from an external light source (blue LED) get into it, light is emitted whose wavelength and, accordingly, color, depend on the size of the crystal. Larger nanocrystals emit red, while smaller ones emit green light. In combination with the main blue, a full-color image is formed, the characteristics of which exceed the capabilities of modern displays.

Although 8K resolution still has a long way to go to mainstream, its use in film restoration and master-copying in movie studios specializing in preserving classic 8K digital films for recording on Blu-ray discs is already growing. , and other super high definition copies.

In addition, this means that there is no need to constantly improve the clarity of the master copy every time a new image format appears.
