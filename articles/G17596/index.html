Ontogenesis - what is psychology in psychology?

The process of ontogenesis is determined by successive changes in the body from lower to higher levels of life. There is a structural and functional improvement of the individual.



Ontogenesis studies are carried out in several scientific disciplines. So, for example, morphophysiological ontogenesis (formation of an organism) is an object of study of biological science. In turn, mental and social ontogenesis is studied in various fields of psychology (psychogenetics, age and child psychology, social and pedagogical psychology).

The concepts of phylogeny and ontogenesis

The term "phylogenesis" (Greek "phyle" - "species, genus, tribe", and "genos" - "origin") is used to denote the process of occurrence and historical development of a species. In psychological science, this is the development of the psyche of animals in the process of evolution, as well as the evolution of forms of human consciousness.

The concept of ontogenesis has a more particular meaning. This (in psychology) is the process of development of the psyche of an individual. In this case, we are talking about the permanent nature of development - from the birth of a person to the moment of his death. The concept of phylogeny and ontogenesis, psychological science borrows from biology, their author is the German biologist E. Haeckel.

The problem of the driving forces of mental development

A separate fundamental psychological problem is the question of what factors are leading in the development of the psyche, which determine its ontogenesis. This in psychology is determined by the concept of the driving forces of mental development. There are two main approaches to solving this problem - biogenetic (natural) and sociogenetic (social).

Proponents of the first direction emphasized the genetic factor (heredity), considering it the leading factor in the process of individual development of the psyche. Accordingly, the role of the social factor was minimized. Among the most famous representatives of the biogenetic approach are R. Descartes, FJ. Russo, G. Spencer, S. Hall, D. Baldwin.

The opposite, sociogenetic approach as the driving forces of mental development highlighted the social factor - the role of the public environment. Man, therefore, acts as a product of external (indirect) influence. The importance of the individual‚Äôs heredity was ignored by the proponents of this approach. Representatives - J. Locke, E. Durkheim, P. Janet.

The two-factor theory of the ontogenesis of the psyche

Attempts were also made to combine both factors - hereditary and social - to explain the psychic specificity of the concept of ontogenesis. This in psychology resulted in a third direction - the theory of two factors. The first researcher was V. Stern, who formulated the principle of convergence of two factors. According to this principle, the hereditary line in the development of personality intersects with the line due to its social environment (convergence occurs).

Accordingly, the ontogenesis of human psychology is carried out in the process of merging the internal and external conditions of the functioning of the psyche. For example, the innate instinct of the game will determine how and when the child begins to play. In turn, the material and process conditions will be determined by the current external environment.



Special methods were needed to identify the specifics of the ratio of external and internal factors that determine ontogenesis. In developmental psychology, this is a twin method.

Important Details

The twin method was based on a comparative analysis of the mental development of mono- and dizygotic twins. It was understood that if dizygotic twins (DZ - different heredity) in equal social conditions develop differently, therefore, the genetic factor is crucial. If development is at about the same qualitative level, the main factor is the social factor. With monozygotic twins (MoH - the same heredity), the situation is similar. Subsequently, the coefficients of differences between DZ and MZ twins living in different / identical conditions are compared. The twin method is actively used in psychogenetics.



Thus, the psychology of personality development in ontogenesis, according to the theory of convergence, is due to two axes:

  • X-elements of heredity.
  • Y-elements of the environment.

For example, the famous British psychologist G. Eisenck considered intelligence as a derivative of the external environment by 80%, and internal (hereditary) - only 20%.

The disadvantage of the two-factor theory of personality development is considered to be its limitedness resulting from the mechanical addition of hereditary and social indicators. In turn, ontogenesis is (in psychology) a more complex process, not reducible only to mathematical calculations. It is important to consider not only their quantitative ratio, but also the qualitative specificity. In addition, there is always room for individual differences in such patterns.

Psychoanalytic approach to the concept of "ontogenesis" in psychology

What is ontogenesis from the point of view of psychoanalysis? If in the previous theory we observed the convergence (convergence) of the axes of hereditary and social elements, then in the theory of Z. Freud the reverse process occurs. Ontogenesis and activity

The introduction of the category of activity, according to the domestic psychologist DB Elkonin, allows to a certain extent to solve the problem of highlighting the dominant factors in the ontogenesis of the psyche. The development process is, first of all, the activity of the subject itself, due to its objective activity.

As for hereditary and social factors, they act as conditions for development, but not in the role of its dominant. They do not determine the process of development of the psyche itself, but only its variations within the normal range.
