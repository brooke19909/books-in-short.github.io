How to sew a skirt in a pleat by yourself?

A pleated skirt has not lost its popularity for many years. She is a mandatory attribute in the wardrobe of women of any age and profession, from young

schoolgirls to wise life-giving ladies in years. The presence of a pleated skirt in your wardrobe allows you to come up with new looks, combining it with a suitable top and a variety of accessories.

It is appropriate to wear such a skirt in any situation, starting from the office, ending with a romantic walk in the park. This type of clothing is suitable for all women, regardless of body shape and height. By modeling the length of the product and the width of the folds, you can create styles of skirts for full women, which will hide all the flaws of the figure.

The figure of each woman is individual, so it is not easy to find the right model in stores that will appeal to you. Unsuccessfully bypassing all the shops of the city, each of us in the end will ask ourselves the question of how to sew a skirt into a crease itself? Is it possible to create such a model at home? Of course, yes. Sewing such a product at home is not only possible, but extremely simple. Having spent

minimum amount of time, you will receive an exclusive skirt tailored specifically for your figure according to measurements taken from you.

Before embarking on the sewing process itself, it is necessary to build a pattern for the future product. It is built the same way for any figure. Patterns of skirts for obese women do not require any additional measurements. We need to know the circumference of the waist and hips, and then calculate the depth of the folds. To do this, we need to subtract the value of the hip circumference from the number of centimeters of the width of our entire fabric, and then divide the resulting number by the desired number of folds - this way we get one depth. However, it should be remembered that the total length of the fabric should be at least three times greater than the circumference of the hips, otherwise the appearance of the product will be unsightly.

There is nothing complicated in how to sew a skirt into a crease. As a material for a future product, it is better to choose woolen or silk

the cloth. The fact is that cotton fabrics will not hold on well.

To make it easier and more understandable, we will provide step-by-step instructions on how to sew a skirt into a crease.

First of all, take measurements, which include the length of the skirt, waist and hips.
