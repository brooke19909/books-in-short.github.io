Rules of Macrame and Shambhala

Pick up a small chopping board. It will be a weaving machine. Drive a pair of carnations into it, the distance between them is 45 cm. Place the machine upright and work. Tie the end of the fishing line from the reel to one nail, cut about 50 cm, string the beads (10 pieces) and tie it to the second nail. Now we still need 2 m of fishing line; the segment should be brought under the string with beads, tied with a knot, only so that both ends of the free ones are of equal length. After this, the base knots should be knitted according to the macrame system - the Shambhala weaving technique is based on them . When it comes to the beads, they are braided, and knitting continues. The final number of knots after the pebbles should be 20. Then the fastener is made.
