LiPo batteries: description, specifications, reviews

The autonomy of equipment and digital technology in recent years has become one of the decisive factors in choosing in favor of a particular product model. Batteries in the form of battery packs play a critical role on this site. Their small size, coupled with high working potential, significantly expand the capabilities of the target device. It remains only to choose the appropriate type of battery. And if recently LiOn elements were popular, today developers are increasingly looking at LiPo blocks. Batteries of this type have a wide range of advantages, although they are not free from the disadvantages.





Item Overview

LiPo devices belong to the group of modern lithium batteries, which led to their some similarity with ionic counterparts. The fundamental difference lies in the type of electrolyte used. If a gel electrochemical composition is used in LiIon cells, in a LiPo is a polymer with a lithium-containing mixture. The concept of a polymer power source is based on the property of this electrolyte to provide a semiconductor effect in conjunction with lithium. As a result, electrochemical qualities significantly increase the operational efficiency of a LiPo battery. A description of the general principle of operation of polymer cells will be incomplete without classification by the types of electrolytic solutions that are used in such batteries. In addition to gel-polymer devices, dry electrolytes and non-aqueous saline solutions are also isolated. In the first case, the basis is polyethylene oxide with lithium salts, and in the second, non-aqueous salt mixtures sorbed in a matrix of polymers with small pores.

Charge features





You can charge the cells from direct current sources until the battery goes into stabilized voltage mode. In other words, when the replenishment reaches 80%, you can stop the process, but not earlier. A full charge gains its potential in 2 hours. During this time, in particular, standard 12V LiPo batteries with a capacity of 1500 mAh are charged. A set of these indicators allows complete control over the charging process.

Positive reviews

Polymer electrolytes do not offer many advantages over ion batteries, but they are obvious when compared with previous generations of batteries. In practice, owners of devices with such batteries notice stable voltage maintenance, modest dimensions and large capacity. Suffice it to say that compact single-cell batteries are able to provide mobile devices for several days with intensive use. In addition, LiPo batteries are characterized by a reduced memory effect. This means that the owner will incur less operating costs during the execution of charging cycles. An important positive feature for ordinary consumers is environmental safety. So far, manufacturers are not able to make batteries that are completely devoid of toxic substances, but polymer models are the least dangerous in this regard.





Negative reviews

The complete crowding out of lithium-ion batteries is hindered by the disadvantages of the polymer electrolyte, which, among other things, are reflected in the usual practice of use. First of all, this is the problem of aging. Such batteries do not "live" for a long time, which is especially noticeable on the example of the same mobile devices. After 3-4 years, owners note a decrease in capacity. That is, the element remains operational, but the duration of work on a single charge is gradually reduced. Sensitive to many users and the price factor. The fact is that LiPo-batteries, due to the need to integrate additional protective circuits, significantly add to the cost. Even compared to lithium-ion models, they cost 10-15% more.

Conclusion





The outlook for LiPo batteries is mixed. The advantages that polymer electrolyte cells can boast today are quite enough for the modern user of mobile devices. However, their potential is significantly limited and it is possible that hybrid batteries will replace ionic competitors . Nevertheless, 6S LiPo batteries with high discharge current are capable of delivering unprecedented high semiconductivity. Of course, this applies to the segment of compact devices designed for stand-alone portable equipment. Also, hopes with the future development of this direction are reinforced by the resistance of the element to external influences - including temperature. In terms of mechanical strength, they can compete with a magnesium battery, but if we take into account the preservation of working electrochemical properties, the LiPo separation will be much higher.
