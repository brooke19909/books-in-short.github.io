Scientists got a lot of new facts about the history of Samarkand after they carefully studied the local settlement Afrosiab. Mausoleum of Chashma-Ayub (Bukhara)

This mausoleum is located in the center of Bukhara and dates from the 14th century. It was erected by masters from Khorezm under Tamerlan.





The mausoleum consists of four adjacent rooms, each of which is crowned by a dome. The building also houses the holy spring, on the basis of which the water museum today operates.

Tourists are often told one legend associated with this building. The preacher Ayub, according to legend, once passed through Bukhara. The locals, dying of thirst, asked him for help. And then Ayub raised his staff and hit him hard on the ground. In that place, a spring with cool and clear water immediately appeared, which exists in the mausoleum to this day.

Khoja Nasreddin - the national symbol of Uzbekistan

Khoja Nasreddin is the legendary character of oriental folklore, which in an amazing way combines naivety and profound wisdom. The hero of numerous tales, jokes and legends. The question of the reality of this person remains controversial, although some historians claim that such a person really existed and lived in Turkey.

Today, the wandering philosopher Nasreddin is immortalized in five states - Russia, Ukraine, Kazakhstan, Turkey and, of course, in Uzbekistan.





A bronze monument to Khoja Nasreddin in Bukhara was erected in 1979. The sage is depicted sitting on a donkey. At the same time, he holds a coin with the fingers of his right hand, and greets all passers-by with his left.
