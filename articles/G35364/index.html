Hotel Louis Leger 4 * (Czech Republic, Prague): photos and reviews of tourists

Our article will be interesting to those who are going to visit Prague in the near future, because we want to talk about one of its hotels. Louis Leger is a wonderful four-star hotel located in the very center of the city.

A bit about the hotel

Hotel Louis Leger 4 * (Prague) has a fairly convenient location. Five minutes from it is the IP Pavlova metro station, and fifteen - Wenceslas Square and the National Museum. And Charles University and the Congress Center are located a kilometer from the hotel.



Louis Leger 4 * is located in the building of a beautiful palace, built at the end of the nineteenth century. The hotel has been operating for quite some time, and in 2003 it was completely reconstructed. What distinguishes this complex from many others in Prague is its exquisite architecture and stylish interior, which are harmoniously complemented by quality furniture from eminent Italian designers and interesting decorative details.

How to get to the hotel?

From the main train station and airport, getting to Louis Leger 4 * is not at all difficult. There is a bus stop on the territory of the airport itself, where you should catch the AE bus, the final destination of which is the railway station. Then you need to take the Museum metro and go just one stop to the Pavlova station. As we have already mentioned, this particular station is located near Louis Leger 4 *. If you come to Prague by train and do not have heavy luggage, then you can walk to the hotel, it will take about 20-25 minutes. Or you can use the subway. Getting a ticket for transport is very simple. This can be done at the airport, and at the station, and at bus stops, newsstands, shopping centers, in automatic ticket offices. The ticket is valid in any transport, and its cost depends on the validity period (starting from half an hour, and ending with three days).

What is good about Louis Leger 4 * (Prague) is that from it you can easily get to anywhere in the city. In twenty minutes you can walk to the Old Town Square, from where trams run to all areas. The exact address of the hotel: Czech Republic, Legerova 9, Prague 2, Louis Leger 4 * (Czech Republic, Prague).

Number of rooms

And now let's dwell on the numbers. The most popular option for travelers purchasing a ticket to Louis Leger 4 * is Standard. Prague is a special city with a truly fabulous atmosphere. Many people come here to feel the spirit of old Europe. Not everyone can afford to stay in luxury apartments, many prefer to save a little on housing so that there are more funds left for excursions, souvenirs and entertainment.

Louis Leger 4 * (Prague) offers its guests 54 luxurious rooms. All of them are very beautiful and comfortably decorated in the Italian style:

  • Double standard. The apartment is equipped with a double bed. Includes free Wi-Fi, a fridge and minibar, desk, safe and ceiling fan. The bathroom has a shower, hairdryer and standard toiletries.
  • This single room has a single bed. There is Wi-Fi, minibar, refrigerator, safe, desk, ceiling fan.
  • The quadruple room has two double beds. The rest of the equipment is no different from the rest.
  • The family room accommodates six people.
  • Family apartment for five guests.
  • Double deluxe.




Absolutely all rooms are cleaned daily. They have all the necessary toiletries and detergents, replenished regularly. Baby cots are available upon request. The hotel has 24-hour room service.

Hotel infrastructure

Hotel Louis Leger 4 * provides ticketing, dry cleaning and laundry services. Car rental available. If necessary, you can order babysitting services for the child. The hotel also has a local guide who can advise guests on all kinds of excursions. For business people, the Louis Leger 4 * hotel (Prague) offers three conference rooms decorated in a modern style with all the necessary equipment.

Nutrition

Guests can eat in the restaurant Louis Leger 4 * (Praha). A daily breakfast awaits tourists with a wide selection of drinks, salads, fresh pastries and desserts. In addition, the hotel has a very cozy lobby bar, which is nice to sit in the evening. The hotel administration offers its guests a shuttle service, but for a fee.

Symbols of Prague

One of the most interesting and beautiful cities of old Europe is Prague. Louis Leger 4 * is located not so far from the center, and therefore hotel guests will conveniently get to all attractions. It is simply impossible to come to this delightful old city and not to walk its wonderful streets. You fall in love with Prague from the very first glance. Ancient monuments of architecture and completely new modern buildings are incredibly combined here. The city forever leaves warm memories in the hearts of its guests, memorized by orange tiled roofs, the charm of towers and an old pavement of large cobblestones.

Perhaps one of the symbols of the city is Charles Bridge, which is known even to those people who have never been to Prague. This is an amazing piece of medieval architecture. The first stone was laid in the foundation of the bridge by Charles the Fourth in 1357. But the construction got its present name only in 1870. This is not the first place through the stormy Vltava, the previous ones were demolished by floods during the flood. Currently, Charles Bridge is the most favorite place for guests of Prague. Every tourist wants to walk along it and admire the medieval sculptures and turrets. There is even a belief that says that one should make a cherished desire and touch the statue of St. John of Nepomuk, and it will certainly come true. True or not, it can only be verified by personal experience.





Another attraction of the city and at the same time a symbol of the country is Prague Castle. It is the largest castle in the country. And he was founded the first representative of the Przemyslovich family, the prince of all Czechs, the famous Borzhivoy in the ninth century. The castle is located on a hilltop on the left coast of the Vltava. Several temples and palaces of different eras have survived on its territory. Here, for example, is the well-known St. Vitus Cathedral. Now Prague Castle is the residence of the President of the Czechoslovak Republic. At the front gate is always a guard of honor of the serious Guardians of the City. It is near them that city guests like to be photographed.

St. Vitus Cathedral is the pearl of the city. For many Czechs this is a holy place. The majestic spiers of the cathedral go up, the Gothic facades are decorated with lace stone carvings. Since 1836, all Czech kings have been crowned here. At the same place in the ninth century stood the rotunda of St. Vitus, later the Romanesque basilica, and currently there is a cathedral. The chapel houses the remains of the protector and ruler of the Czech Republic, and jewels for coronation are stored at the top.

Nearby Attractions

Hotel Louis Leger 4 *, as we have already mentioned, is located near Old Town Square, which is considered to be the heart of the Old Town. It is here that a monument to the hero of the Czech Republic, Jan Hus, is erected. The square is surrounded by historic buildings. The most famous of them are: Tyn Church, St. Nicholas Cathedral, Kinsky Palace.





Among tourists, the Town Hall with an astronomical clock is especially popular. Every hour, crowds of vacationers gather under it with movie cameras and cameras. With the battle of the old chimes, the performance begins, during which the twelve apostles pass in front of the audience, Voluptuousness, Greed, Panache and Death alternately peek out from the opening windows. There are always a lot of people in the Old Town Square. Musicians play here, carriages are waiting for gentlemen, artists are performing, and tourists at this time are chilling in numerous cafes. Festivities, fairs and holidays are also taking place on the square.

Wenceslas Square, which is more like a wide boulevard, is no less interesting for guests of the city. This is the heart of Prague. Once there was a horse market. And now it is a very beautiful place. Restaurants and shops work in the square, people rush about their business, and life does not stop raging here even at night. There is also a monument to St. Wenceslas, near which people from Prague make appointments and just meetings, and tourists gather for excursions.

Modern attractions

It is very interesting to see the Dancing House. This is one of the new attractions (1996), which is unique in its architecture and design. The house consists of two cylindrical towers: curved and straight. They symbolize the famous Broadway duo of Fred Astaire and Ginger Roberts. At one time, the construction of this facility caused numerous disputes between citizens, critics and architects. Nevertheless, now it is one of the most famous places.



A unique view opens from the observation deck of the Petrin Tower. It is very similar to the Eiffel, but significantly higher than it, because it is located on a hill.

The pearl and pride of Prague is the National Theater. This stunningly beautiful building with a blue dome, crowned with a golden crown, is located on the Vltava embankment. In general, theater is something special for Czechs. He is a symbol of rebirth. It was built on donations from the people. Initially, it was planned to stage only Czech works in it, but now world performances also appear in the repertoire.

Louis Leger 4 * (Prague): reviews

The hotel, according to tourists, is pleasant in every way. It is located in an old building, however this creates a special romantic atmosphere. The design of the hotel rooms is designed in the same style. The main advantage of the hotel is its location. It is convenient for guests of Prague because of the fairly good transport interchange, allowing you to get in the right direction. This is a very important point, since almost all guests seek to see as much as possible in this beautiful city.





All apartments of the hotel are spacious and made in the Italian style. Tourists note that the furniture and furnishings are not new. This is pretty noticeable. For example, there are scuffs on the furniture. However, in general, this does not affect the rest. The rooms have central heating and the hotel is open all year. Especially a lot of people come here during the Catholic Christmas period, and therefore the issue of heating is very relevant. Guests note that the apartment is warm and comfortable. But there are no air conditioners in them. Each room has a ceiling fan. The rooms have a safe (it is quite expensive), a minibar (paid), TV.

The maids clean the rooms daily, clean the towels according to the internal schedule (it can be found in the bathroom, it is glued to the wall), but sometimes there are problems with this.

Service Reviews

The hotel staff deserves special attention. Absolutely all vacationers praise its employees. Employees are always friendly and try to help in solving problems. Girls at the reception settle very quickly, no matter what time of day you arrive. Almost no one knows the Russian language at the hotel, but you can somehow explain, even if you do not know English. It is very pleasant that you can always change the number without problems and surcharges if it does not suit you for some objective reasons.

Food: reviews

Tourists can eat in the hotel restaurant. A local chef prepares delicious and varied dishes. The buffet in the morning is filled with several types of salads, yoghurts, boiled eggs and omelettes, cereals, numerous sausages, cheeses, meat, pastries and desserts. According to tourists, the European breakfasts in Louis Leger 4 * are very hearty and nutritious. No less tasty food can be ordered for dinner and lunch. But, as a rule, active guests simply do not have time for them if they walk around local attractions.



And is it worth it to rush if there are so many historical beautiful places around. In Prague there are a lot of various cafes and bars on calm and crowded streets. You can always have a tasty and relatively inexpensive meal in them. The local cuisine is simply amazing, it is impossible to break away from such wonderful dishes. Near the hotel there are also budgetary establishments, always open for tourists.

Instead of an afterword

Summing up the conversation, I would like to note that the Louis Leger 4 * hotel is a good place for those who decide to stay in Prague. All the advantages of this ancient place more than cover the minor flaws that are here. In general, the hotel and its staff make a very good impression, which causes a desire to return here again.
