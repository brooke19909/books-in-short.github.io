State of New York: Buffalo - City of Light

Buffalo is a town in the northeastern part of the US state of New York, bordering Canada: Ontario and the city of Fort Erie. This is the second city after New York State. The village is located at the source of the Niagara River, on the east coast of Lake Erie, not far from the world-famous Niagara Falls.

The city of Buffalo (New York) is really small, with only 258 thousand citizens living here, with more than 1.2 million people in the county.

Economic situation

Since 2009, Buffalo, New York has experienced a difficult economic environment. The unemployment rate does not fall below 10%. More than 25% of the total population of the city is below the poverty line.

The average per capita income is 20 thousand US dollars per month.

National and age composition

Mostly white people live in the city, approximately 82%. African Americans represent 13% of the population, with Hispanics (3.3%), Asians (about 1.32%), and Indians (0.6%).

On average, the population of the agglomeration and the city itself is represented by citizens aged about 38 years. This is not to say that all residents of the state and city graduated from school, of which there are only 83%. There are even fewer people who have received a bachelor's degree: about 23%.

Buffalo (New York) is conditionally divided into several areas. Mostly African Americans live in the eastern part, Spaniards and Puerto Ricans in the west, although Italians used to live here. Now the Italians have moved to the north of the city. Jews live mainly in the western part. These are mainly migrants from Germany, Poland and Slovakia. The Jewish community in the city is very small, with no more than 26 thousand people.
