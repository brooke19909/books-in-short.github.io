Crocheted openwork stoles. Schemes and Description

A woman’s wardrobe is unique in that there is always a little thing in it with which a everyday look can become stylish and fashionable. Today we’ll talk about wraps that are popular and adored by fashionable women, such as crocheted crocheted fishnet stoles. Thanks to the many patterns, these scarves are made in various interesting variations, which makes them interesting and unique.





What are stoles?

The name was given to the scarves by fashionistas from France, throwing a large rectangular piece of fabric over their heads and shoulders. To date, crocheted openwork stoles have gained special popularity. With their help, any outfit of a woman is transformed and becomes more romantic and unique. The scarves from the large-knitted openwork pattern look original. They can be made of thin yarn. Knitted stoles are combined with any form of clothing, which makes them a universal outfit. The variety of species allows you to wrap them both on the dress in summer and in autumn over a coat or jacket.

Stylists of our time recommend using knitwear made of natural wool. For example, crocheted stoles made from merino yarn have a fine texture and amazing softness. Despite their lightness, they retain heat in cool times and pass air well. Cashmere yarn deserves similar attention. It is very pleasant to the touch, holds its shape well, and it is easy to knit interesting patterns and prints from it.

You can wear stoles in various ways: as a hood on your head, in the form of a scarf on your shoulders, wrap your neck, putting on a shawl like a collar and in other versions.





Crochet Fishnet Stoles

Summer versions of wraps are made of cotton or viscose mixed with viscose. Demi-season shawls can be knitted from threads with a small addition of wool. To create warm stoles, mohair, cashmere, natural wool or yarn with acrylic content is used.

Having chosen a pattern for knitting, you can try to change or supplement it. It is allowed to increase or decrease the number of loops, columns, rapports, various elements. Based on the scheme of openwork stoles, crochet can be supplemented with the original strapping of the sides, to create brushes or fringe.

Wash wool products carefully, without changing the temperature of the water. This will help to avoid compaction of the yarn structure, tangles and loss of pattern. Knitted stoles are dried by placing the product on a towel or a dense piece of cotton material. Otherwise, knitting wool will damage the pattern and, accordingly, the product itself.





Principles of Knitting Wraps

Often crochet patterns and descriptions of openwork palatines contain chains of several air loops, single crochet or crocheted columns of a certain amount, as well as elements containing several columns.

Basically, stoles have a width that, when wrapped, covers the back to the waist. Also, when throwing a scarf over his head, neck and shoulders should be covered. These characteristics distinguish stoles from scarves. These wraps can be knitted as a direct fabric, or collected from motifs of the same size and shape.





Rectangular Patterns

The rapport of the proposed openwork pattern consists of 13 loops. Initially, you need to dial a chain of the number of loops, a multiple of 13, and additionally add 1 loop for knitting the next row. Then, in the next loop after lifting, tie a regular column, dial a chain of 5 loops and, having missed 3 loops of the previous set row, repeat the column again. Thus, continue to crochet the arches to the end of the first row.

Then climb 5 loops and connect the chain to the middle of the first arch with a simple column.

In the next arch, knit 8 columns with one crochet. Make a connection and repeat the action from a chain of 5 loops. At the end of the row in the middle of the last chain we knit the connection, then two loops and form a double crochet.

We climb one loop, unfold the fabric, knit a simple column and knit a similar amount of columns into the next fan, creating one air loop between them.

We connect the last column having a yarn with an arch of 5 loops of the last row with a regular column. Next, we continue to knit fans, alternately connecting them with arches. The next row will contain chains of 5 loops that need to be evenly distributed, following the pattern.

Do not forget to knit a single crochet between the fans. Repeat knitting from the second row.

Schemes of individual motives

Stoles can be knitted from separate elements interconnected by chains of loops. Common parts are in the form of a square, circle, or triangle. There are also floral motifs of different sizes for a symmetrical and neat connection. Knitting an element begins with creating a circle from a ring having 8 loops into which 12 simple columns are knitted. Further, according to the scheme, a square of 5 rows is knitted. To connect the elements, you can knit another row of arches with air loops.





To knit a stole, you can use patterns of napkins or tablecloths. The length of the cape is often three times longer than its width. For example, 3 elements are sewn along the short side, and 8 or 9 corresponding parts along the long side.

In the article, we looked at crocheting palatines with openwork patterns. By learning how to create different patterns, you can knit original wraps and update your wardrobe with warm and irreplaceable stoles.
