DIY flowers from chiffon (photo)

Decorating women's outfits with flowers has always been popular. To keep the beauty of the decor longer, make the flowers yourself from chiffon. Thin graceful petals are so similar to natural that you might think you bought a bouquet in a flower shop. The manufacturing technology of such souvenirs is simple, and materials are available.

Practical and enjoyable activity

Many make flowers with their own hands from chiffon, felt, paper, cotton pads, plastic masses - in a word, from what is at hand. This type of hobby has become quite popular lately. If you are engaged in sewing, there are always small patches of fabric that can just be used. In addition, you can decorate home-made flowers with interior details, for example, curtains, clothes, make hair clips and hair bands.



Tools and materials

To make flowers of chiffon with your own hands, you need the following:

  • the fabric itself;
  • template paper;
  • scissors;
  • a candle or lighter for edging;
  • thread with a needle;
  • stem frame (floristic or plain wire, toothpick);
  • glue or thermal gun.

Everything, except the thermogun and fabric, is definitely at your place. Material can be bought. There are shops that sell it by weight. Usually they offer remnants of different shades that are just right for needlework.

Thermogun - a heating device in which, under the influence of temperature, special glue sticks melt. This tool is very convenient when you need to quickly connect parts or surfaces, for example, in hard to reach places. If you are going to engage in the manufacture of crafts or decorations for the interior, this device is definitely useful.

Types of material

Before you start making flowers from chiffon with your own hands, it is worth getting acquainted with the properties of existing options for the fabric with which you will work. The colors and possibilities, from the point of view of design and decorative use, are huge for chiffon.

The following types of this fabric are on sale:

  • crepe chiffon;
  • jacquard;
  • chiffon satin;
  • chameleon;
  • with spraying;
  • with lurex;
  • pearl chiffon;
  • stretch chiffon;
  • with color transition;
  • printed.

The first is a natural silk plain weave fabric . Jacquard chiffon is dense and opaque. Maybe with a picture. In this form, a special method of weaving threads is used. The third type is a wonderful combination of chiffon and satin properties. Option "chameleon" is very thin, shimmering in different shades. The fabric with a dusting is beautiful as it has the applied silver or golden layer. View No. 6 has in its structure a lurex shiny thread. Pearl chiffon has a pleasant pearl overflow. The stretch chiffon is dense and stretching. In a fabric with a color transition in one section, a smooth shade changes from light pink, for example, to dark. Printed fabric is a chiffon with a pattern.

As you can see, there are many opportunities to make beautiful flowers with your own hands from chiffon. Even if you choose the simplest technology and use one template, due to the variety of shades and quality of the fabric, you can get great floral compositions.

DIY Chiffon Flowers

Whatever option you choose, the sequence of work will be as follows:

  1. Cut petal patterns out of paper. You can draw them yourself, focusing on the image of a natural object, or take blanks on the Internet.
  2. Attach the template to the fabric, circle it, cut out the petal.
  3. So that the edges do not crumble, you need to process them over the flame of a candle or lighter.
    In addition, this operation will help to get a more natural, slightly curved shape. Hold the workpiece with tweezers. Instead, you can sew the petals from two identical parts, followed by turning the parts so that the seam remains inside.
  4. When all the petals are ready, proceed with the assembly using a thread with a needle. Some parts will have to be glued.
  5. Make sepals from green chiffon. Usually they are made from a square.
  6. Fasten the assembled object to the stalk.

DIY flower made of chiffon. So make roses, daisies, gerberas, lilies, daffodils. You can use other methods of forming the product.

Chiffon Flower: Workshop

Usually, the petals are made from individual parts cut out according to the template, but there is also a faster way. However, this method will only be able to accomplish what has a terry structure: roses, carnations, peonies.



So, we make a simple flower from chiffon. The pattern will be a long rectangle, so no paper patterns are needed. There can be several such tapes connected together. Manufacturing technology is as follows:

  1. Cut a strip of fabric. Its dimensions are determined by the dimensions of the flower.
  2. Fold it in half in length and stitch it manually or on a typewriter on all sides except the bend. The stitches and steps between them should be large.
  3. Assemble the fabric on the threads.
  4. From the resulting workpiece, you begin to form a flower by twisting the strip in a spiral and connecting the layers with a needle and thread.
  5. Sew the reverse side, if you are going to fix the flower on a flat surface, or make a sepal with a stalk for volumetric composition.

Flower made of round patterns.

The manufacturing technology described at the beginning of the article talked about cutting out patterns for individual petals and their subsequent processing. You can simplify the work and make a flower of chiffon (with your own hands) from circles with a curly edge.

It is so easy to get, for example, poppy or any stylized pattern. The work will be as follows:

  1. Make a paper template in the form of a circle with notched or pronounced pointed, rounded or other petals.
  2. On the workpiece, perform several parts of chiffon.
  3. Work the edges.
  4. Lay them one on top of the other, sew with a needle. In the middle you can sew a bead or make, for example, a box of poppy in the form of a "bag" of chiffon filled with scraps of fabric.
  5. Make sepals and stalk if necessary.

Where can I apply finished products

So, there are several ways to make such jewelry. There are many options for using the resulting decorative elements. They are used for decorating clothes, curtains, hairpins and headbands for hair, bags, and interior.



Volumetric bouquets of such flowers are also very beautiful and look like real ones. An excellent souvenir will be a topiary made of chiffon flowers. This popular decor imitates a ball trimmed decorative tree in a pot. To make it, you will need flower blanks made by any of the above methods, a spherical blank, a stem for a stem, a pot or a vase. It is better to glue the flowers on the surface of the base with a thermal gun.
