Pune city (India, Maharashtra): description, attractions

Most travelers set off on beaten paths, buying holiday packages from companies specializing in this. In recent years, people have appeared who build routes themselves in search of interesting places, unprecedented miracles, natural pearls, new experiences, with the aim of self-awareness.

India - beach vacation or self-knowledge

India is a country where an infinite number of historical monuments, centuries-old traditions and customs, wonderful sandy beaches and a warm sea. Each traveler will be able to find what is necessary, and maybe even more - he knows himself.



Yes, the country is not rich. But her poverty is measured only by a material measure. The richest history, the most ancient civilization, unique energy - this is what thousands of people come here every year for.

Maharashtra State: Key Features

Very close to the favorite tourists of Goa is the state, which is the third largest and second largest population in India. It occupies 307.7 thousand square meters. km and is the most developed in India economically. On its territory, many crops are grown both for the domestic market and for export. Metallurgical, machine-building, chemical, textile enterprises of this state bring to the state piggy bank over 40% of the total budget.

Maharashtra is an unforgettable interweaving of thousands of years of history and modernity. The area where Buddhist temples and the Bollywood film studio, Ellora's caves and the production of modern gadgets adjoin.

Mumbai, formerly called Bombay, is the capital. It is in this city that up to 70% of the total population of the state lives. And the second largest in the state is the city of Pune - the center of the eponymous district. And if Mumbai is the economic capital of the state, then Pune is cultural.

Pune city

Many of the objects found during excavations indicate that already in the 9th century this city existed in the form of a fairly developed settlement with a large temple complex. The current city of Pune is inhabited by almost 6 million people.



The city is very actively being built and developed. On its territory there are production of large automobile concerns and companies involved in information technology.

Pune University is a leading university in India with over 400 courses in various disciplines. It consists of about 6 interdisciplinary centers, more than 450 colleges and about 200 institutes.

How to get to the city of Pune on your own?

Pune (India, Maharashtra) has its own international airport, which is 10 km from the city center. There are no direct flights from Russia. You can get to the state with transfers, most often the route looks like this: the flight Moscow - Abu Dhabi - Mumbai, then by taxi or minibus. This is the most economical of the options. You can also use the plane or train.



If the traveler arrives in Delhi or Goa, then it is possible to get there by train. One has only to think about what he is ready for the sights. You can go by expensive express train, or you can taste the local flavor sleeper (second) class. Such a trip takes more than 30 hours. With air travel, the final point of travel will be reached much faster, but this pleasure will cost more.

Landmarks within the city limits

The most ancient monuments of the city of Pune (India) are in its old part, called Peshwa. Shanivarvada was built in the early 18th century as a palace for the rulers of the principality. Currently, it is partially damaged and destroyed. There was practically nothing left of its former splendor. Previously, the floors of all its halls were made of marble in the form of mosaic panels, and the teak walls were decorated with carvings. Now only a few ancient frescoes with elements of scenes from the Ramayana are available for inspection.



The Aga Khan Palace - the construction of the end of the 19th century. This is a rather interesting architectural structure, made in the style of the Venetian palazzo and having a rather extensive shady garden. On its territory is the Gandhi Museum.

The famous collection of the Indian poet Dinkar Kelkar, collected during his travels around the country, can be seen in the museum, located in one of the mansions of the city. The exhibition presents many household items of art residents.

Going to the western part of the city, you can visit the Panchaleshvara cave, which has been a sanctuary for about a thousand years.

In the vicinity of the city there are a number of ancient fortresses and temples. When viewing all the sights, time in India will fly by. Travelers who have come to know themselves, to receive information from the upper layers of life and to recharge with energy do not even notice it.

Meditation Resort

The Osho Ashram in Pune is not quite what it seems to be about spiritual organizations. This place combines the ability to relax in an expensive resort and at the same time meditate. The center offers a variety of programs that can interest people studying modern areas of isoteric techniques and teachings.



The hotel complex at the ashram has a capacity of only 60 rooms. This is not enough to accommodate everyone who wants to join the study during the day. Therefore, most visitors rent a living space in Pune itself.

To visit the center, you must go through an interview with the security service and take a blood test for HIV. A ticket that allows you to go to the territory must be purchased daily. In addition to the ticket, special cards are distributed that pay for the necessary services. After passing a certain course, he is struck off the list in the map.



At the ashram there are two dining rooms that provide guests with food, but the prices are quite high. Therefore, most travelers eat outside the center.

On the territory there are a number of rules that are strictly followed:

  • the allowed color of clothes is exclusively burgundy;
  • all visitors greet each other with a hug;
  • It is strictly forbidden to take pictures;
  • It is not recommended to bring children with you.

Many travelers, having visited the center once, now come to the country exclusively for the ashram.

Entertainment and shopping

The city of Pune (India) offers many recreational activities. Discos, night clubs and bars, restaurants, many shops with traditional goods. There is a pretty colorful market.



None of the travelers will be left without souvenirs. It can be elements of clothing, jewelry, figurines, masks, tea and spices. There is nothing that could not be acquired upon arrival in the city of Pune. India is rich in traditions regardless of the remoteness of the central tourist destinations.
