Mallorca, island (Spain), or a ticket to a fairy tale

The largest among the Balearic Islands is Mallorca. The island (Spain) is located near Menorca. It is dominated by hilly and mountainous terrain. The northwestern part of the island is covered by the mountains of Serra de Tramuntana. As for its eastern part, here are the lower mountains of Serres de Lievant. The central part is occupied by the plain of Lanura del Centro. In its northeastern part there are two beautiful bays - Badia d'Alcudia and Badia de Pollenza. A map of this amazing corner will present in more detail the island of Mallorca (Spain) . It is also necessary to know that this island is protected by the state, as more than 40 protected areas are located here.



This area is very green and environmentally friendly. It has gorgeous sandy beaches that are perfect for both youth recreation and for families with children. The southern part of the island is more lively. Here is the capital of Mallorca - Palma de Mallorca, and there are also beautiful bays with cliff beaches. The northern part is calm and has warmer waters than the southern. It is here that the best beaches are located. Summarizing all the above, we can highlight a number of advantages that Mallorca has: the island (Spain) is famous for its rocky cliffs, completely covered with pine protected forests, majestic mountain ranges, a mild Mediterranean climate and magnificent beaches. All this attracts many tourists here.



Mallorca is an island (Spain), on which prestigious resorts are concentrated, having a well-developed infrastructure, magnificent nature, many kilometers of beaches. These are Valldemossa, Illetas, Playa de Palma, Palma Nova, Cala Blava and others. In addition, it is recommended to visit the capital of the island, which has a number of interesting sights. A remarkable example of medieval Gothic is the Cathedral, the construction of which took place on the site of an Arab mosque, starting from the 13th century, and lasted almost 3 centuries.

Arriving on the island of Mallorca (Spain), it is recommended to make a trip to the mountain village of Valldemossa. The majestic architectural structure of this village is a medieval monastery, which now houses a historical museum. In addition, there are several museums, including the museum of the traveler and writer of El Salvador.



There are 200 caves on the island, but only 5 tourists will be able to explore. In the eastern part are Drak Caves, which stretch for 1700 meters. Here is located the largest underground lake not only of the whole island, but of the whole of Europe. For guests here, in a huge hall, amazing concerts are held. Musicians play while on a boat, which slowly floats on a lake illuminated by colorful lanterns.

Mallorca is an island (Spain), on which there are a number of other beautiful places. Including the medieval town of Alcudia with powerful fortified walls. Nearby you can see the ruins of the ancient Roman city of Pollentia.
