The term culture and its meaning



The very concept of culture cannot be described as a separate object. This is a completely abstract meaning, which is a designation of different aspects of social relations, the nature of a person and his lifestyle. The term "culture" has several meanings. Most often it began to be used from the beginning of the twentieth century. For this, many different prerequisites have arisen.

First of all, this was necessary in order to systematize many factors that characterized cultural values and norms, certain aspects of social life. In the modern world, the concept of culture has different meanings. It has found wide application in designating the level of human behavior. Here, the term "culture" carries an estimated meaning. Such qualities as reliability, consciousness, accuracy, etc. are associated with it.

Certain lifestyles are associated with this term. For example, urban life, level of education, upbringing. It also includes the ability to understand art, literature, the presence of philosophical knowledge and other skills.

But culture cannot be described as an object completely subordinate to human activity and reason. It is a consequence of the existence of people and includes some objects of his labor. This process does not always have reasonable and meaningful motives.

The term "culture" is understood as follows:

1. This is a manifestation of the spiritual and universal values inherent in man.

3. The whole creative potential of mankind.

Initially (in antiquity), it denoted methods of cultivating the land and agriculture as a whole. With the improvement of skills, improvement of living standards and the development of social relations, there was an increase in cultural values. This concept is associated with human activity aimed at creation. This is the material side.

Then this value began to be used to indicate the level of education, enlightenment or education. In this case, culture acquires a spiritual side. All its directions are interconnected.

We can distinguish the main properties of culture:

Firstly, it is necessarily associated with human activities and is subject to social laws. For her are inherent and their own laws.

Secondly, the object and subject of culture is man.

Thirdly, it unites all aspects of people's life and it cannot be attributed to any particular area.

And last, it characterizes the qualitative side of society.

The main functions of culture are to transform society, increase its level of education. Culture also has aesthetic value. Its components instill in people the concept of fine and high art.

Culture can be called the keeper of centuries-old knowledge, experience, tools and other consequences of the activities of people that accumulate from generation to generation. This is not only the activity itself, but also the relationships that arise as a result of its implementation. This includes economic, political, moral and other factors.
