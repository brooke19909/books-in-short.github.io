Smart watch Samsung Gear S2. Overview and positioning

The Samsung company, like any other brand, has its own fans, and there are more and more such fans every year. They are quite loyal to all products of the manufacturer, look at new items with a positive attitude and are always ready to try out some chips and innovations.





Judging by the Samsung Gear S2 model, a review of the first generation of S1 watches revealed their extensive functionality and was enjoyed by many admirers of the product. The next generation S2 is a completely different watch, where the company was guided by a different ideology and experience. The difference is not even that the S1 series is an ergonomic rectangle on the arm, and the S2 has a familiar dial, the differences are hidden much deeper.

The hero of today's article - Samsung Gear S2: an overview of the main characteristics, design, ergonomics and performance of the model, read on.

Design

Samsung has not had a round watch for a very long time, and although other manufacturers sell it in tons, the company for some reason excluded this type from its turnover.





For the Samsung Gear S2 Classic lineup, the review should not begin with the patterns and standards of modern electronics (the company is of little interest to this), but with the watch manufacturers, on which the engineers and designers of the brand rely.

The case material is stainless steel type 316L, and the entire filling of the watch is conveniently placed inside. The user does not have access to the inside of the model, and therefore the company first applied eSIM (Nano) technology - this is a variation of one of the electronic SIM card formats.

Adaptation

In this case, the Samsung Gear S2 smartwatch may experience some problems in the territory of the Russian Federation, as the main mobile operators can not always provide their customers with special eSIM cards. But in the near future, Samsung promised to provide its models with pre-installed SIM cards.





As an alternative, the brand began producing variant models of the Samsung Gear S2. The review in the first case showed that S2 is more suitable as a companion for your phone or tablet, in the second case it is a full-fledged watch with an eSIM card. The difference between them is especially noticeable in terms of operating time, and if the "companion" can work up to three days without recharging, then the option with a SIM card will not last longer than a day and a half, and this despite the solid battery of 300 mAh.

Ergonomic Samsung Gear S2

A review of the ergonomics of watches can be reduced to two types: classic and regular S2. In the first case, the watch has a leather belt with a body that is easily recognizable and habitually sitting on the hand. A key element of the usual option was the bezel around the screen, with which you can manage menu items and lists.

Display

The latest Samsung Gear S2 models (release date - October 2015) have an exclusively round screen, without any rectangular inserts, as in previous MOTO 360 models. The company's engineers preferred to equip the new watch with a proven type of screen and a matrix in the face of SuperAMOLED with a diagonal of 1.2 inches at a resolution of 360 by 360 pixels.

The display has a clear picture, excellent brightness and visibility. The display goes into standby mode after a few seconds of inactivity, so in order to see the time, you need to tap the dial or raise your hand in a certain gesture.





The clock settings allow you to set the desired brightness factor within 10 gradations and set the screen off modes. At 30%, the display saturation is enough for an average day with a little, but everything is purely individual, it depends on the number of notifications and how you use the gadget.
