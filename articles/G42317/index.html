Architecture

The second most important attraction of the city is the New Town Hall. It was built in the period from 1580 to 1582. And in 1650 it was completely rebuilt. Since then, the appearance of the building has not changed.

There, on New Square, there is a monument to Maria Theresa. This is a unique bronze statue. Its peculiarity is that it is the only monument in the world on which Maria Theresa stands. The Holy Trinity Column is a monument erected in 1860, which has its own history and religious value. Initially, it was made of wood and installed on the Square of the Holy Spirit (between the hospital and the old cemetery) in gratitude to the Almighty for getting rid of the plague. Replacement with a stone structure took place in 1863, after the liberation of Vienna from the Turks. The column acquired a triumphant image. This attraction can be found today in the Old Square. The column was moved here in 1965, when the construction of the railway station began on the Holy Spirit Square.

To the north of the Old Square, guests of the city will see the St. Aegis, built in the XIV century. This is a church with memorial plaques, on the bell tower of which an observation deck is equipped. It is also recommended to visit Domkirche, which is located three blocks south of New Square.
