The most beautiful cities in Bavaria: names, historical facts and attractions

Bavaria is a region that is part of Germany. It is the largest in area in the country, and its most beautiful alpine meadows and mountains, lakes and rivers will not let indifferent even the most experienced travelers. The fabulous cities and castles of Bavaria attract tourists. In every corner of this region you can find inherent in it an amazing local flavor.

Bavaria is beautiful at any time of the year, and each city in this region has its own unique zest. What route should be made for a tourist traveling alone? To do this, it is recommended to study the rating of the most beautiful cities in Bavaria.

Munich

We begin our acquaintance with the most beautiful cities in Bavaria, with a description and photos of these amazing places, from the capital of the region. This is the ancient city of Munich, which was founded in 1158. It is no coincidence that it is included in the list of cities in Bavaria that are worth a look for a traveler who has visited Germany. On its territory there are a large number of buildings erected during the Middle Ages, magnificent gardens, as well as masterpieces of modern architecture.

What to visit in Munich?

The city, which is considered the tourist center of Bavaria, is worth starting to explore from the Marienplatz square, located in the center. This is the busiest place where annual festivals and holidays are held.

Being on this square, travelers can admire the pearl of Munich - the New Town Hall. This building is considered the most beautiful in the city. It was built relatively recently, in 1905. Nevertheless, the town hall literally amazes with its magnificence. The architectural style of the building is neo-Gothic. Its main decoration are numerous columns and upward peaks. All these elements make the building quite strict and very elegant. On the facade of this three-story building there are decorations in the form of sculptures of dukes, kings and various mythical characters.

On the other side of the central square stands the Old Town Hall. It was built in the 14th century. This building is a masterpiece of Gothic architecture, inside of which today a toy museum is open.

Representatives of five generations of the Bavarian Wittelsbach dynasty were engaged in its construction, expansion and reconstruction.

Regensburg

In the list of cities in Bavaria that are worth a look, there is also this one, which has an interesting history and architecture. Regensburg was founded in 1245. It is also one of those places that tourists prefer to visit.

The city is located where Regen flows into the Danube. These rivers divide it into two parts. In Regensburg, tourists can admire the large number of old buildings that preserve the spirit of the Bavarian region.

For a long period, this city was the richest in the region, while being the capital of the dukes. The most ancient evidence of its ancient history is Porta Praetorium. This building is nothing but the northern gate that previously led to the ancient camp of legionnaires. They were built in 179.

Nuremberg

Which cities to visit in Bavaria? In the north of the region is its largest economic and cultural center, which still retains the atmosphere of the Middle Ages. For a long period, Nuremberg was the residence of the kings of Germany. Today, tourists tend to get into this one of the most beautiful cities in Bavaria to enjoy its sights.

Rothenburg ob der Tauber

Among the most beautiful cities in Bavaria is this small town located in the Middle Franconia district. There are few locals here - only 11 thousand people. But at the same time for tourists, he is on the list of the best cities in Bavaria to visit. The fact is that Rothenburg ob der Tauber has a special charm inherent only to him. In the medieval city center, it seems that the gingerbread houses located here have descended from the pages of fairy tales.

Tourists are given the opportunity to inspect the town hall and visit the market square, which is the place for theatrical performances and musicians.

Among the interesting historical monuments of the city can be noted the House of Crafts, the Church of St. Jacob, as well as several museums with interesting exhibits.

Bamberg

This is one of the most beautiful cities in Bavaria located on seven hills. Its territory is divided by the river Regnitz. This city is very distinctive and incredibly cute. Tourists love it not only for interesting historical buildings, but also for landscapes that amaze with its picturesqueness and beauty.

Those areas of Bamberg, which are located near the river, are not accidentally called German Venice. They can be admired endlessly. In some places, river waters are adjacent to sidewalks and houses, while creating amazing romantic landscapes.

An interesting architectural monument is the Bamberg Town Hall. This building is 14th century. stands in the middle of a river on an artificial island. Work on its construction continued in different eras. That is why in the facade of the building there are features of various architectural styles - Baroque, Rococo and Gothic.

On one of the hills of the city, which is called Domberg, is the Rose Garden. It transforms the landscape so much that it seems like a true paradise.

Bamberg Cathedral is also popular with tourists. It is a religious landmark built in the 11th century.

There is a cathedral in the city. Peter and George. The first king of Germany, Henry II, was buried on its territory. There is also the tomb of Clement II - Pope. Those who want to get acquainted with the history of the city can visit the historical museum.

However, there are few architectural monuments in the city, since in 1945 this settlement was destroyed by British aircraft.

Lindau

This city is located by Lake Constance. In this part of Bavaria, it is the largest. The old part of Lindau is located on the island, and modern areas are on the banks of a picturesque harbor among orchards growing at the foot of the mountains.

From the embankment of the city offers a beautiful view of Lake Constance, which is also called Constance. The entrance to the harbor is bordered on one side by a lighthouse, whose height reaches 33 m, and on the other - a huge marble sculpture of a Bavarian lion mounted on a 6-meter pedestal.
