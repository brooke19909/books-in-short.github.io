What is a scaffold and what was it used for

In most countries, the death penalty is a thing of the past. But stories are still alive about those who, by their own example, learned what a scaffold is.

Scaffold purpose

For many centuries, the execution of punishment was not only a way of implementing a court decision, but also a public spectacle. Many people gathered for execution, among whom were children. And in order to make it easier for all who came to see how justice is done, a wooden platform was erected. It was he who began to be called the scaffold.





As a rule, such a structure was erected immediately before the execution. Therefore, the prisoner could hear how they saw and hammer boards for the platform on which his life would end. Well, what is a scaffold, probably already understood. However, it must be taken into account that the defendants were not always deprived of life on him. On the scaffold, for example, they could flutter or chop off one of the limbs, say, a hand (a punishment very often used in the olden days for theft).

Anna Boleyn - Queen who died from the sword

Over the years, the death penalty has deprived many famous and even noble people. Among them were representatives of power, for example, monarchs.

Perhaps the most famous queen who knew what a scaffold was, was the English ruler Anna Boleyn, the mother of Queen Elizabeth I and the second wife of Henry VIII Tudor. Accused of treason and witchcraft, Anna was sentenced to death. Accompanied by a servant, she climbed the scaffold. The execution was quick.
