The beginning of the mail of St. Petersburg

The word "mail" was first mentioned in 1667 during the armistice between Russia and the Commonwealth as a result of the thirteen-year war.

Already during the reign of Peter I there was a special postal service - emergency. In 1714, the first St. Petersburg postmaster Heinrich Gottlieb Krauss began the device of the St. Petersburg-Riga post. Initially, only two people worked there - directly Mr. Krauss and his assistant Osipov Gavrila, who served as scribe and translator. But there was a lot of work, and the post office building was first enlarged, then new spacious outbuildings and individual buildings began to be erected.

At first, the shopkeepers accepted letters. What was notified by signboards: "Receive letters to the city post number ...". The price of the service is from 10 kopecks - the value and purpose of the message mattered.

In those unhurried times, mail was collected three times (!) A day by a letter carrier from shopkeepers, taken to the department, and then, sorted, delivered to the indicated addresses. That's why in classical literature we read about morning, afternoon and evening mail.

January 29, 1833 is a significant date for the post office. The first city post office in Russia began to work - "for sending letters living in the capital without investing money and things."

A year later, mail is already delivered along with passenger traffic. This made it possible to quickly open post offices throughout almost the entire territory of the Russian Empire.

In 1848, the first mailbox was installed in St. Petersburg. And in its modern form - with a hinged lid, the mailbox was designed by designer P. N. Shabarov in 1910.

Years of devastation and change

During the revolution, the Civil War, postal service in the country was practically minimized. There was very little chance of receiving a sent letter . There were times when messages arrived later than their sender at the specified address. This introduced a certain confusion in people's lives and curiosity.

By 1950, the work of postal services throughout the country was fully restored. And letters, parcels, parcels, telegrams flew from one end of our vast country to the other. And the time for delivery of correspondence began to rapidly decrease.

And even in the most remote villages post offices were organized and earned joy for all residents, which also performed an additional function - a meeting place for discussing the latest relevant "news" and gossip.

Steps to the future with human hands.

Despite a sharp technological breakthrough - the Internet and e-mail appeared in our lives, the work of Russian postmen of all ranks is not diminishing. It has become profitable and convenient to make purchases in online stores, and delivery is most affordable only at the Russian Post.

The abstract of a candidate or doctoral scientific work should be sent only through the post office. Thus, no matter how technical progress develops, no matter how equipment is changed or modernized, people will receive and issue correspondence and other shipments. And this means that such a concept as the human factor will not go anywhere from the scope of delivery of mail.
