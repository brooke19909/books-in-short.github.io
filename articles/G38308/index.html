Description

Lucca (Italy), the sights of which we will consider in the article, is located near the River Serkio.

Its history is rich in various events. Founded even before our era, the city of Lucca in the period of early feudalism occupied a very favorable position. It was at the intersection of roads that led to Rome, Parma, Florence, Pisa and Looney. In addition, the city was on the way of pilgrims who went from northern Europe to Rome. From century to century, the rulers of the city have changed. By the end of the eighth century, the city was the residence of the Dukes of Lombard, then passed under the auspices of the Frankish Count. It was the capital of the Margraves of Tuscany.

In the Middle Ages it was a rich city. Mainly due to the development of banking and the sale of silk. The city achieved particular power in the fourteenth century, under the rule of Castruccio Castrani. Lucca Fortress

What is Italy, Lucca proud of? The attractions of this place are really interesting. The city is famous for its fortress. Especially beautifully preserved are two of the four gates of the once powerful fortress. The Borga Gate, built at the end of the twelfth and beginning of the thirteenth century, as well as the gates of San Gervasio erected in the first quarter of the twelfth century, have great architectural and historical value. Gates made in the Romanesque style, framed on the sides by two beautiful and powerful towers, skillfully decorated with limestone. In the inner part of the hemisphere, rare murals have been preserved. With the image of the Virgin and several saints. Tourists can admire the San Donato Gate, built in 1590. Their exterior facade is decorated with sculptures of two lions made of marble.

The city walls are the oldest in Europe. Their length is more than four kilometers. True, in very small numbers, even the walls built during the Roman Empire have been preserved here. This wall served to protect the city until the beginning of the eleventh century, until it was destroyed by the Marquis Bonifacodi Tuscany for the rebellion of the townspeople. A fragment of the ancient Roman wall is part of the wall of the Church of Our Lady of the Rose. Particularly seriously, the city's inhabitants engaged in the reconstruction of their defensive walls in the sixteenth century, rebuilding them using the best trends in the construction of defensive structures of the time. At the beginning of the nineteenth century, by order of Maria Louise Bourbon, part of the wall was landscaped and turned into a magnificent park. Where in modern times there are not only great places for walking, but also cozy cafes.

St. Martin's Cathedral

It is often said that a city like Lucca is a city of hundreds of temples. The Cathedral of St. Martin was built in the eleventh century, and then was several times rebuilt and reconstructed.





This magnificent cathedral with a marble facade, columns and portals, decorated with sculptures, has priceless relics inside the building. For example, the work of Tintoretto "The Last Supper", "Madonna and Saints" Ghirlandaio.





And sacred and the altar of the temple have the status of a museum. In the cathedral is the most famous relic of the city - the Holy Face.

Church of the Archangel Michael

What else is interesting Lucca (Italy)? Reviews of tourists about this city are usually positive. People write that they liked the local shrines. For example, in the historical part of the city, on the square of San Michele In Foro, is the church of the Holy Archangel Michael. Four tiers of arcades rise above the main facade. At the very top is a statue of the Archangel Michael, slaying a dragon. And on the sides are sculptures of angels. According to legend, an emerald is hidden in the statue of St. Michael. At certain times of the day and with certain lighting, you can see its green glow. And in the left corner of the facade is a statue of the Virgin. According to legend, it was installed by residents in 1480 in gratitude for the miraculous deliverance of the city from the plague epidemic.
