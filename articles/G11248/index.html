How to crochet a cardigan: schemes and description

Crocheted coats and cardigans are a great way to show your good taste and creativity. They are perfect for changeable weather in the off-season, when it is completely unclear whether there will be rain, sun, wind or all at once throughout the day.



Types of Cardigans

Almost every knitter, regardless of experience and existing skills, will be able to crochet a cardigan. Schemes and descriptions of many models abound in the pages of traditional and online publications.

There are several main types of cardigans:

  • Classical.
  • Based on a circular web.
  • From a rectangle sewn in a special way.

All these models can be made from almost any yarn and using a variety of patterns. Of course, denser fabrics should be used to make warm products, and openwork patterns will come in handy to crochet a lightweight cardigan. Schemes and descriptions of those and other patterns will follow below.

Thick Thread Cardigan

The use of this combination of materials and technology allows you to very quickly and effectively crochet a cardigan. Schemes and description for such a model may be required only for beginners, because the cut is extremely simple. Its peculiarity is that the cardigan is not knitted from the bottom up, but with a whole fabric, across: from one sleeve to another.

Work starts from the bottom edge of one of the sleeves. The cuff is made of a very popular pattern with embossed columns. They knit in the same way as ordinary double crochet, but when forming a new hook, it should be wound not under the loops of the previous row, but under the column itself. The gum effect was achieved by alternating the relief columns connected before work (facial) and those that are called purl (at work).

After knitting, the cuffs proceed to the manufacture of the sleeve. This is an elementary rectangle. By the way, all the details of such a cardigan are rectangular.

When the length of the sleeve is sufficient, it is time to proceed to the formation of the parts of the front and back. They are knitted at the same time. To do this, on both sides of the upper part of the sleeve, additional air loops are gathered and the front and back webs are knitted.

To form two split shelves, you must perform the following steps:

  • When the height of the large fabric becomes equal to the width of one shelf, knitting should be continued only on the part that will be the back.
  • Partial build-up of the canvas should be performed until this fragment is equal to 15-17 cm. This is the standard neck width.
  • Then you should dial as many air loops that are necessary for knitting the second shelf.
  • Perform further work by checking the first half of the cardigan.
