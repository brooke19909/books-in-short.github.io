What attracts tourists to the colorful Macedonia? Ohrid, who will not let anyone get bored

Many travelers who meet the Balkan country, declare their love to her. This fairy tale gives all vacationers not subject to the influence of time Macedonia. Ohrid, which is considered its main pearl, is a tourist Mecca, protected by UNESCO. Travelers who have been here once will certainly return.

A bit of history

The first mention of a settlement located on the shores of Lake Ohrid occurs in the 5th century, but scientists believe that people lived here much earlier. The modern city was built on the site of the ancient Greek colony Lichnidos, captured by the Romans. After a strong earthquake, it was completely rebuilt by the conquerors.

At a time when Macedonia was part of the Byzantine Empire, Ohrid, which received this name in 879, was the main shopping center located on the Egnatius road leading to Constantinople. In the IX century, the city, inhabited by Slavs, is part of the Bulgarian kingdom, and a century later it is recognized as its capital.



At the end of the 14th century, Ohrid was ruled by the Ottoman Empire, gradually losing its greatness. In 1913, after the end of the First Balkan War, the settlement, where more than 15 thousand people live, leaves Serbia, and since 1991 it has been part of Macedonia.

When to hit the road?

Tourists who have visited a fabulously beautiful city admit that even a week is not enough to get acquainted with all its sights. It is best to come here in May - June, but in July and August in Ohrid, located in the subtropical zone, it is too hot and the temperature exceeds 30 degrees.

Located in the south-west of the country, its spiritual center lives off tourists, and at the height of the high season, prices bite here. Those who want to save money, can be advised to go on an exciting journey in September - early October. But even in winter, when the temperature fluctuates between 3-4 degrees, vacationers from different countries of the world rush to Ohrid.

The main spiritual center of the country

In a small and comfortable city, famous for its huge number of churches, there are important religious sights, which everyone who comes to the Republic of Macedonia must visit. Ohrid, called "Slavic Jerusalem", has preserved many monuments of ancient Byzantine-style architecture.



The current church of St. John the Evangelist, built in the 15th century, delights with its unique beauty. The cross-shaped structure was rebuilt several times. Decorative facades, Byzantine frescoes, a new iconostasis, portraits of Jesus Christ and John the Evangelist evoke a feeling of awe.

The Church of St. Sophia is one of the most significant religious attractions of Macedonia. The exact date of construction was not preserved in the manuscripts, it is only known that in the XI century an important monument was restored. Over the centuries, the appearance of the church has changed. After the Turkish capture of the city, it turned into a mosque, Orthodox frescoes were painted over, and a minaret appeared above the main dome, which distorted the harmonious architectural appearance of the attraction.

And only in the 50s of the last century a large-scale reconstruction of the church was carried out, and it was given its original appearance. Purified frescoes with portraits of the leaders of the patriarchate appeared in the central part of the religious building.

Ancient fortress

It is impossible not to mention the oldest historical attraction, which has become a popular tourist attraction. The fortress of King Samuel is a massive building with 18 towers that rise one hundred meters above the city and the lake.

The powerful citadel was repeatedly destroyed, but was immediately restored again. Ancient Macedonia is incredibly proud of its calling card .

Ohrid: rest

I must say that the main natural attraction is Lake Ohrid, which received the name from the city. The miraculous object is protected by UNESCO and is characterized by clear water, in which you can see the inhabitants of the bottom, listed in the Red Book. The deepest lake in the Balkans, Ohrid (Macedonia) has retained its unique ecosystem. Its beauty was admired by the ancient Greeks, Romans and Slavs.

The border between Albania and Macedonia runs along the lake, and both states are sincerely proud of the landmark that appeared more than five million years ago as a result of geotectonic deflection. In the south and north of the reservoir there are various centers, restaurants and other objects for tourists in a hurry to enjoy the soothing beauty of a natural pearl.



In addition to the opportunity to soak up the water, guests of the country can visit the Galichitsa National Park, famous for its unique flora, three karst caves and 18 villages, whose inhabitants will acquaint themselves with the local culture.

Guest reviews

All tourists who visited quiet Ohrid (Macedonia) have extremely positive reviews. Some admire the panoramic views from the cliffs and admire the unique nature of the city with a special atmosphere, others visit religious attractions and admit that they relax here with their souls. Many people think about the meaning of life and their place in this world.

A city that will not let anyone get bored can not be seen in a few days, and travelers who arrived from different countries say that it was the most memorable adventure. Architectural attractions cause a feeling of admiration, and the developed infrastructure makes the life of guests comfortable.





An unforgettable vacation and a hospitable welcome will give everyone amazing Macedonia. Ohrid, which has retained its original appearance, attracts the attention of foreign tourists who claim that this place was created for the enjoyment of the eyes.
