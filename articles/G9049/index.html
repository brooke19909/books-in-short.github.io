Pavlovsk: attractions, history, photos

Pavlovsk is a city in the Voronezh region, the administrative center. It is located on the left bank of the Don and attracts travelers from all over Russia with its beautiful landscapes and legendary history. In this article you will find the most interesting information about Pavlovsk (attractions, photos, facts and much more).

From the history of the city

The city of Pavlovsk was founded in the early 18th century. by the great emperor Peter I. By his order merchants from Azov and Taganrog were resettled here, thanks to which this place became an important shopping center. Initially, Pavlovsk played the role of a strategically important military shipyard and fortress. It is important to note that in the first period of the existence of the settlement, prisoners of war captured by the Petrine army in the Battle of Poltava were engaged in its arrangement . In 1711, Pavlovsk received city status. This place became the birthplace of the great Russian fleet. It was here, at the Pavlovsk shipyard, that ships were built that became the backbone of the imperial army. In addition, foundries and cannons were founded in the city. Guns, cores and bells were cast in their walls. That is why in the 18th century. the city was called "small St. Petersburg." Over time, a sewing factory also appeared here.





Pavlovsk today

Today Pavlovsk, the sights of which we will consider below, attracts tourists with its ancient architecture and beautiful landscapes. This city is the pearl of the Voronezh region.

A temperate climate, rich flora and fauna, clean ponds, beautiful landscapes create excellent conditions for families. In addition, the city has more than 40 historical and architectural monuments, acquaintance with which is an obligatory part of the excursion program. Today this place claims to be the main tourist center of the Voronezh region (its map is shown in the photo below). Pavlovsk and its inhabitants are always happy to welcome travelers and provide everyone with comfortable conditions for relaxation.





Shipova Dubrava

Pavlovsk (Voronezh region) has long been famous for its incredibly beautiful nature. The visiting card of this region is Shipova Dubrava. Locals call this ship forest the pearl of the Don region. And this is really a unique place! Among the steppes lies a whole green island with amazingly rich flora. Here you can see the mighty oaks, which are more than 150 years old! In addition, in Shipova Dubrava there are maples, linden, hazel. The fauna of this forest will also surprise travelers with its diversity. Martens, deer, foxes, wild boars, and roe deer live in the oak forest.

Odintsov Mansion

Pavlovsk, whose sights preserve the secrets of history, should be visited by people who are interested in the past of their homeland. This city is rich in unique examples of architecture, and its monuments will impress everyone with its beauty and grandeur.

The real visiting card of Pavlovsk was the house of the merchant Odintsov. This is the most beautiful old building in the city. The Historical Museum is located on the ground floor, the workers of which will tell each visitor the most interesting information about the past of this region.





The building itself, once owned by the merchant Odintsov, fits perfectly into the architectural ensemble of the city. In addition, a unique front porch made of cast iron has been preserved here.

Theological county school

The spiritual district school in the city was founded in the first half of the 19th century. The children of local priests studied here. The school building was erected by the famous architect A.F. Shchedrin. It is an excellent example of the era of classicism.

Today, the complex of the Theological School includes 4 buildings and the Church of Three Saints.

Church of the Kazan Icon of the Mother of God

This temple is located near the city Historical Museum. He is an excellent example of architecture of the late 18th century. The church was erected at the expense of Kazan merchants, transferred to the city on the orders of Peter I after the Battle of Poltava.

Today, in its building, beautiful 18th century icons are kept: Barsanuphius, Germanus and Guria.





Transfiguration Cathedral

In 1712, on the site of the modern Transfiguration Cathedral, a church was erected, named after Peter and Paul. She got her current name after 12 years. In 1773, the temple burned to the ground as a result of a fire. A few years later, a new church was erected in its place, which still impresses tourists with its grandeur and beauty.
