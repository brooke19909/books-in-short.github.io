Life in the USSR: education, culture, life, holidays

The further the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics goes into the past , the more there are those who want to return to it. Life in the USSR was not ideal, but people are bored, remembered and compared. Today, this era still excites and excites compatriots. Sometimes serious debates unfold in society, figuring out how happy the Soviet people were and how they lived in the USSR.





Differently

According to the recollections of most compatriots, it was a simple and happy life for millions of people who were proud of their great power and aspired to a brighter future. Stability was the hallmark of the time: no one was afraid of tomorrow, nor of price increases, nor dismissal. People had a strong base under them, because, as they say, they could sleep peacefully.

On the streets, passers-by willingly helped to find the way lost, bring heavy bags or cross the roadway to the old man. Participation and attention were shown to everything. It is no wonder that visiting foreigners fell in love with this country, shocked by the warmth they met here.

Today, isolation, seclusion and alienation are more and more characteristic - a person may not know who lives next to him on the site. Study, study and study

Indeed, Soviet citizens had the right to free education - this was another pride of the Land of Soviets. Moreover, secondary education was universal and compulsory. And anyone could enter a university after successfully passing the entrance exams.

The attitude to school in the USSR, and to education in general, is very different from modern. A pupil or student could not even have thought of skipping classes. The main source of knowledge was his synopsis, his performance depended on how he would listen and record the teacher.

A separate point should be emphasized with respect to teachers. There was always silence in the classrooms, no unnecessary conversations and noise, there was an absolute focus on the lesson. And God forbid someone is late for classes - you won‚Äôt be ashamed.

All treatments were available. And going to the clinic, there was no need to be puzzled how much money might be needed and whether it would be enough. Housing problem

Life in the USSR was not sweet in many respects, however, every Soviet citizen from the age of 18 had the right to housing. Of course, this is not about palaces, but no one was left on the street. The apartments received were not private property, as they belonged to the state, but they were assigned to people for life.

It should be noted that the housing problem was one of the sore points of the Soviet Union. Only a tiny percentage of the families registered have received new housing. Housing lines stretched for many, many years, despite the fact that annually housing construction reported the delivery of new housing estates.





Other values

Money was never an end in itself for a Soviet person. People worked, and worked hard, but it was an idea, a dream. And any interest or desire for material wealth was not considered worthy. Neighbors and colleagues easily lent each other a three-ruble paycheck and did not count the days of her return. Money didn‚Äôt solve anything, they decided relations, everything was built on them.

Salaries in the USSR were decent, such that half of the country could afford to fly airplanes without compromising the family budget. It was available to the masses. What are student scholarships worth? 35-40 rubles, with excellent students - all 50. It was quite possible to do without the help of mom and dad.

The labor of the craftsmen was especially appreciated. A qualified specialist at the plant could receive more than its director. And that was normal. There were no shameful professions, a janitor and a technician respected no less than an accountant. Between the "tops" and "lower classes" there was not that insurmountable abyss that can be observed now.

As for the value of the ruble itself in the USSR, this is one of the most popular cash of the time. Its owner could afford to buy a choice of the following: two large packs of ravioli, 10 pies with meat, 3 liters of kefir, 10 kg of potatoes, 20 trips by metro, 10 liters of gasoline. This cannot but impress.





Deserved rest

By law, the state guaranteed Soviet citizens material security in old age. Pension in the USSR allowed older people to live in relative abundance. There was no need to go for extra work. Old grandchildren nursed, worked in dachas, went to rest in a sanatorium. Nowhere was such a picture observed that a pensioner counted pennies for medicine or milk, and even worse, he stood with his hand outstretched.

The average pension in the USSR ranged from 70 to 120 rubles. Military or personal pensions were certainly higher. At the same time, only 5 rubles were spent on housing and communal services. Pensioners did not survive then, but lived, and also helped their grandchildren.

But in fairness, it should be noted that not everything was so rosy with the collective farmers. For them, only in 1964 the law on pensions and benefits was adopted. And these were mere pennies.
