How to make Minecraft from paper without a printer: a master class

The appearance of the game

A distinctive feature of Minecraft, for which everyone loves it, is the gameplay. It is a heap of blocks that can be rearranged, interchanged, creating very interesting and functional designs. Knowing how to make Minecraft out of paper without a printer can make the game world a reality.

We are preparing a drawing

Introducing Minecraft and figures from it, mentally we see cubes and squares in front of us, which consist of a certain number of pixels. They can be taken as a basis.





  • Take a regular copybook sheet and imagine that each pixel is one cell.
  • We will not focus on ordinary blocks, but make a real Steve. Let's start with his head. Its size will be 6 by 8 pixels. We draw this number of cells. For convenience, you can use the ruler.
  • How to make Minecraft out of paper without a printer will tell you the finished sample with the dimensions indicated on it. Just draw it in a notebook, carefully counting the number of cells.
  • Thus, we draw all the details we need: arms, legs, torso and head of Steve.
  • Now we proceed to the most interesting part of the drawing - fill our Minecraft from paper with color. Color schemes can be used ready-made, or you can show your rich imagination and make the figure completely original. Do not forget to draw the character eyes, nose, mouth and hairstyle.

General assembly

Our stencil is completely ready, and you can proceed to gluing it. There are several important points that must be taken into account:

  • the notebook sheet is very thin, therefore, so that the finished Steve turns out to be stable, it is better to stick the prepared schemes onto cardboard;
  • then there is a general assembly, in which we first glue the head, legs, arms and body transversely in the form of a ring, and then bend and connect the upper and lower parts of the parts to each other;




Having figured out how to make Minecraft out of paper without a printer, you can tackle other game characters, such as Zombies, Creeper, cute pigs and sheep. All of their schemes are very simple, and self-drawing and preparation of blanks can be not only a fascinating process, but also necessarily cause admiration from friends.
