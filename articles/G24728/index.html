Singapore Botanic Garden: history, photos, how to get there?

Singapore surprises its city and country with its exoticism, an unusual combination of modern architecture and corners with preserved pristine nature. The stunning flora and fauna of the region is represented in zoological and botanical gardens. Huge manicured parks in the very center of shopping areas impress travelers from all over the world. And the Singapore Botanic Gardens are one of the best on the UNESCO list. This is one of the main attractions of the country.





History

The garden was established in the 20s of the XIX century. The main purpose of its creation was the cultivation of cocoa beans, spices and some other crops. Then, for several decades, the need for plant breeding disappeared, and the territory was not used. Serious work on the creation of the park began in 1859. Gradually, the plant world was enriched, seedlings from other regions began to be brought.

In the XX century, many new species of tropical plants appeared in the Singapore Botanical Garden. Soil studies were conducted here, new varieties were bred. During this period, the first hybrids of orchids began to take root. Subsequently, the garden survived the period of occupation and was not plundered. The history of the Singapore Botanical Garden does not end there: now, it is trained biologists, they continue to ennoble the territory and develop new varieties. This park is visited annually by a huge number of people: not only tourists, but also local residents who come here to relax, relax or exercise in the fresh air.

sights

Walking through the Singapore Botanic Gardens in Singapore, you can see beautiful alleys, ponds and fountains. The names of the plants you like can be read from the special plates installed nearby. Several separately located Orchid Garden. Entrance there is paid, but it is worth a visit: picturesque places can be found here. Tourists will be impressed by the variety of blooming orchids.



There are waterfalls, small sculptural compositions that fit perfectly into the surroundings. The moss-covered sculptures give visitors the impression that they are in a fairyland. In some pavilions, the air temperature is specially lowered to make plants comfortable.

In 2007, the first in Asia was opened a garden for children, under the name of D. Ballas. You can get there from a separate entrance, but this is also the territory of the Singapore Botanical Garden. Young visitors can not only look at the plants, but also learn more about their breeding and human benefits. There is also a games area and a separate cafe.

How to get to the Singapore Botanic Garden?

Getting to the park while on the island is not difficult. The easiest way to do this is by metro. Exit should be at the station Botanic Gardens. From there, signs and the entrance to the garden will be immediately visible.

Another way to get to the sights is by taxi. But for this it is advisable to familiarize yourself with the approximate prices in advance (Singapore dollars are used here) and think over the return route. Weather permitting, you can walk on foot, especially if the hotel or guest house is nearby. There are hotels located very close to the park.

Note to visitors wishing to travel by public transport: several entrances lead to the garden. There are several buses along the route.
