Hotel Chaika 3 * (Bulgaria, Saints Constantine and Helena): overview, description of rooms, reviews

Saints Konstantin and Elena are the oldest seaside resort in Bulgaria. It is located next to a huge national park, so the air here is much cleaner than in other parts of the country. It is suitable for a quiet and leisurely pastime, so families with young children and elderly tourists often come here. Most of the hotels here offer budget holidays at low prices. One of them is the Chaika 3 * complex ("The Seagull" 3 *), the description of which you can read in our article.

Where is the hotel located?

This small three-star hotel is located on the first beach line of the resort of Saints Constantine and Elena. Distance to the sea is only 40 meters. The hotel does not have its own stretch of coastline, so tourists usually visit a public sandy beach. The only downside is the lack of free sun loungers and sun umbrellas. They must be paid for before each visit to the beach. Along the coast there is a pier, a small night club, a balneological wellness center and several public pools filled with fresh or mineral water. Both adults and children can ride on a water slide. A grocery store is open next to the Chaika hotel ("Chaika" 3 *, St. Konstantin and Elena, Bulgaria).





Also nearby is the souvenir market, where tourists can buy not only products with national Bulgarian symbols, but also famous cosmetics with the addition of rose oil. In the evening, there is an amusement park and a carousel for children. You can move around the resort on a special tourist steam train. You can get to the hotel from Varna International Airport, which is approximately 25 km away. The journey by bus to the resort takes half an hour on average, and by taxi only 10-15 minutes.

Important information

Hotel Chaika 3 * (St. Konstantin and Elena, Bulgaria) was opened back in 1956. This small hotel complex accepts tourists only in the summer season from early May to the end of September. Its three-story building is located in the shadow of a huge park. On the green lawn there is an outdoor pool with an area where guests can enjoy a lot of sunbathing. Full update of all rooms of the complex took place in 2011. In total, the hotel has 98 apartments that are designed for small families. Children are provided with a small cot for free. The restaurant also provides them with high chairs, as well as a special children's menu.





As in other hotels in Bulgaria, check-in at the Chayka 3 * complex (St. Konstantin and Elena, Bulgaria) starts at 14:00. Tourists who arrived earlier than this time can leave their belongings in a storage room and go to the pool or the beach. The hotel staff speaks a little Russian, but understands English or Bulgarian best. Pets are not allowed here. Guests must leave their apartments before 12:00. If the hotel has free rooms, you can always extend your stay for several days or hours.

Housing stock

As mentioned above, the complex has 98 rooms. They are fully consistent with the declared class of the hotel, so it is unlikely to surprise you with new furniture or luxurious furnishings. All apartments at Chaika Hotel St. Konstantin (Bulgaria, Saints Constantine and Helena) has its own open balcony, which has 2 chairs. It offers a good view of the pool and the coastal park area. Each room has a private bathroom. Cleaners daily carry out easy tidying in living rooms, take out garbage and make beds. Linen and towels are changed regularly 2 times a week.





Coming here, tourists will be accommodated in one of the following types of apartments:

  • Double Room. Three adults or a family of two parents and a child are allowed. An extra bed is a folding chair. Room size is 16 square meters. m
  • Family apartments. Spacious room suitable for a company of three adults. A family with two children can also live here.

Room amenities

The equipment of the apartments of the Chaika 3 * hotel (Bulgaria, Saints Konstantin and Elena) fully corresponds to the declared category. The living room has an individual split system that maintains a cool temperature in the room even in the hottest weather. There is also a TV. It is quite old, with a large kinescope, but it broadcasts several satellite channels ("First", "Russia"). You can use the telephone to contact the hotel staff. It can also be used to call home, but such calls will have to be paid separately. For storing food, fruits, vegetables and drinks in the room is a small refrigerator.





A hairdryer, iron and ironing board are available at the reception. However, after use, they must be returned back. In the bathroom for guests, several towels are prepared for the body and face, toilet paper, but there is no set of cosmetics. Internet in the room is connected only at an additional cost.

Dining at the hotel

For guests here are several food options. Guests who prefer to spend time outside the complex can pay for breakfast or half board, which also includes dinner. Also, the Chaika hotel ("Seagull" 3 *, St. Konstantin and Elena) offers a lightweight all-inclusive concept. It includes all the main meals, as well as a light bite of cookies with hot drinks between lunch and dinner. The price also includes alcoholic drinks produced in Bulgaria. Tourists can try draft beer, homemade wines, aniseed vodka. Mineral and sparkling water, concentrated juices, tea, coffee and cream are also offered.





All drinks are served in the bar between the pools until 22:00. You can buy them later than this time only for an additional fee. Meals are served at the Seagull Restaurant. All meals are served at the buffet. The menu consists of local and international dishes. Once a week there is also a theme evening dedicated to cooking fish and seafood dishes. The bar also offers paid imported alcohol and freshly squeezed juices.

Infrastructure Details

This hotel offers a fairly budget accommodation option, therefore, the list of services and entertainment provided here is not very diverse. However, there is everything that a lowly tourist may need. Consider the main infrastructure facilities located on the territory of the Chaika 3 * complex (Bulgaria, Saints Constantine and Elena):

  • Paid safe in which guests can leave their documents, money and valuables before going to the beach. It is located at the reception and is paid by the day.
  • Currency exchange point. There is also a 24-hour ATM near the hotel.
  • Shared living room located on the ground floor. It is equipped with sofas, armchairs, coffee tables, a TV and air conditioning.
  • Car parking. Tourists who come to the hotel by their own car can leave it here for free at any time.
  • 2 pools: adult and children's. There is also a sun terrace with sun loungers and parasols.
  • Massage room. It offers its services for a fee. Playground for children.
  • A hall for playing billiards, as well as equipment for dominoes and chess.




Positive opinions

Resort hotel Chaika 3 * (Bulgaria, Saints Constantine and Elena) often get good reviews. The guests consider that the service here is quite acceptable for an inexpensive price. They describe the following benefits of relaxation here:

  • Good location. Within walking distance there are shops, restaurants and cafes. A bus stop is 200 meters away. The sea is also close, just a few minutes walk.
  • The rooms are not luxurious, but all rooms are clean, always tidy. All equipment in the apartment works well.
  • Good housekeeping. Towels and linen are changed every 3 days, but you can ask to update them earlier if they get dirty.
  • The hotel is always very quiet, so you can sleep with an open balcony.
  • The water in the pools is constantly updated, so it does not smell like bleach.
  • The hotel area is very clean. Hotel workers are constantly cleaning garbage, mowing the lawn.




Negative opinions

Like other budget-class complexes, Chaika 3 * (Bulgaria, Saints Constantine and Elena) is often criticized. Dissatisfied guests in their reviews talk about such service deficiencies:

  • The plumbing in the room is quite old, so it would be nice to replace it. The water in the shower leaves poorly, and the water is served warm rather than hot.
  • Breakfast is not varied. The same set of dishes is served daily. From fruits, there are only apples, oranges, bananas and plums.
  • Free internet almost never works. It’s impossible to even open the page on social networks.
  • Very cramped rooms with a small corridor and bath.
  • The hotel staff ignores requests and comments from tourists. Some employees may be rude to guests.

Should I come here on vacation?

After reading the description and reviews of this hotel, we can say that the hotel "Chaika" 2 * (Bulgaria, St. Konstantin and Elena) is well suited for a budget holiday. Cons here are not too significant, however, tourists still recommend taking vouchers without meals. In their opinion, there are many restaurants nearby where you can eat cheaper and much tastier. The hotel will be a great option for families with children, the elderly and tourists who love a relaxing holiday.
