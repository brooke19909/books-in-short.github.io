History of Lake Seliger

For the first time, the lake is mentioned in chronicles of the 12th century, of which it is known that it was used by Novgorod merchants to access the Volga, which originates on the Valdai Upland. In those days, he was called Sereger, which in translation from ancient Finnish meant "rugged" or "clean" lake.

Long from the time when people settled in these places, the lake was already over 20,000 years old. Scientists call the cause of its occurrence a glacier. After the end of the ice age, when the snow melted, the Valdai Upland was formed. Meltwater filled the indentations between the hills, forming Seliger.

The calm waters of the lake, full of fish, and the forests surrounding it, which were full of game, attracted people who began to build settlements in these parts.

Cultural program

On one of the Seliger islands is the famous Nilova Deserts monastery, for visiting which it is advisable to set aside time among the attractions. It was founded by Neil Stolobensky, who for a long time lived as a hermit in a cell near the Seremkhi River. The fame of his holiness began to lead people to him for a blessing, which greatly burdened him, as he sought solitude with God.





In 1528, he settled on Stolbny Island, on Seliger. Before his death (1555), he bequeathed to build a monastery on this site, which was done in 1594.

Today it is a shrine of the Russian Orthodox Church of extraordinary beauty. His visit will be a rewarding spiritual experience.
