How to make a dove out of paper - a symbol of peace

The manufacture of paper crafts has been occupying and captivating many people in different countries for centuries. The ancient Japanese art of folding and folding paper allows you to create a wide variety of geometric shapes, silhouettes of birds, animals, plants, landscapes, buildings, and any other shapes imagination can tell you.

Origami not only gives you the opportunity to have a good and good rest, focus or be distracted, but also develops spatial thinking in children and adults , as well as rallies them, unites them and makes them closer to each other. That is why it is so useful to put the figures together with the child, fantasizing and coming up with new schemes and games.





A lot of interesting, and even unique, offers an amazing and unique world of origami. Schemes for each figure are always made up very detailed and phased, so it is quite possible to master them yourself, taking the time and focusing.

One of the simplest and most accessible figures in origami is a dove. This symbol of universal peace and love has been known to people for more than five thousand years. Of course, he deserves to appear in creativity from time to time. The resulting cute bird can please not only a child, but also an adult, becoming a kind of decoration at home.





How to make a pigeon out of paper? Everything is quite simple. First of all, you need to take a suitable sheet of paper, square in shape. Now it is folded in such a way that the result is a large triangle (that is, just diagonally). Now, the obtuse corner of the resulting triangle needs to be bent up so that the long side of the figure and the fold line are parallel. Then the bent obtuse angle should be bent again (take the corner and fold it down, about half), so that in the end you get an accordion.

The next stage is that one of the existing small triangles needs to be bent up, and then the resulting figure is folded exactly in the middle. If everything is done exactly as it should be, then the wings will turn out to be the same in size. But in principle, they can vary slightly. The most important thing is that the main, central fold line looks neat, even and beautiful. Now you need to carefully bend the wings up, and the bending will not be done parallel to the back of the bird, but slightly obliquely. How to make a dove out of paper? Very, very simple. When the wings are ready, a beak is made: a small angle is bent diagonally from the body, and in any direction, and then folds inward.





That's all, the bird of the world is ready. Those who understand how to make a dove out of paper can decorate it with a symbolic olive branch, draw their eyes, and even paint their wings or the whole bird. After all, the main thing is to show imagination and enjoy the process. This is the main purpose and meaning of origami classes. Now, having learned how to make a dove out of paper, you can switch to more complex shapes.
