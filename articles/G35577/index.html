In social science, a person is a person who has certain social properties. Based on the definition, we can understand that this concept is inextricably linked with society. Let's analyze it in more detail.

How social studies connect personality and society

In all the various interpretations of the concept of "personality" is common. It turns out that the main thing with which it is inextricably linked is the collective. That is, in social science, a personality is a human individual endowed with a system of socially significant traits. But is it possible to combine these concepts?





Difference from the individual

The individual is a separate member of society. Like an ant in an anthill. It is enough to get into a crowded transport, as we will see dozens of individuals. In other words, this is a concept that is used to designate an individual in society, about which we do not know what features, talents, qualities, etc.

In social science, a person is something more, since this term also implies special properties of a person.



Difference from personality

Now we are closer to the described concept. But confuse it with the concept of individuality is not worth it. The last term focuses on the characteristics of a person, his unique qualities, which are in no way connected with society.

To make it clearer, we give an example. A person very well draws volumetric pictures with a pencil. His work is unique. No one can repeat them. The master himself does not understand why this is happening. He has a sense of style, beauty, which he cannot explain. Talented creative people usually associate this with a divine, supernatural gift. Of course, we will not delve into theology and explain it. It is in this case that we are dealing with individuality - an excellent master.

And in social science, personality is a social, social concept. This is an individual who is endowed with certain qualities, but they appear in the collective. For example, a person always asserts his rights. He is not used to losing and goes all the way. Everyone around him says that this is hopeless. None of this will work out. But man is sure. This is precisely what is a vivid manifestation of the term "personality." That is, these are the features that appear in the team.

Here we come to another concept that social science emphasizes - a "strong personality."

Strong personalities most often go into politics, sports, and business. They, as a rule, find themselves in leading social roles. And since there is always competition, envy, malice in the world, such people are not afraid, challenging others.





Distinctive features of a strong personality

A strong personality is distinguished by:

  • Willingness to fight, to defend their rights and interests.
  • Purposefulness, the desire to develop and go only forward. And when one goal is achieved, always set another - higher.
  • Lack of fear of difficulties, as well as immunity to failure.

All this has a social personality. Social studies connect this concept with the collective.

Summary
