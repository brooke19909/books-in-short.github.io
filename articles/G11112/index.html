Kos Island: Hippocrates Airport Awaits Guests

Kos Island is part of the Dodecanese archipelago, which is better known as the Southern Sporades. It is located in the Aegean Sea in Greece, but only 4 kilometers away are the resorts of Bodrum, Turkey. Every year, thousands of tourists flock to this amazing island - Kos. Hippocrates Airport is the only airborne harbor of this paradise.



Description

The air gate is located 26 kilometers from the administrative center of the island, which is also called Kos. The airport is relatively large, consists of two terminals. Every day, these "heavenly gates" take planes from the mainland. Charter flights, in turn, have a wide range of destinations. The airport operates in heavy duty during the hot summer months. In the hot tourist season, it becomes the sixth largest passenger turnover throughout Greece.

History

Hippocrates Airport was built in 1964. At this time, the total length of the runways did not exceed 1,200 meters. It was then that this small airport gave an impetus to the development of the tourism industry in this territory. Since Kos began to enjoy wildly popular, ten years later, the total length of the stripes was doubled. By the end of the 1980s, the island's air harbor was already overloaded. This gave impetus to the transformation and expansion of the complex. The new terminal, built in the 90s of the last century, now only accepts tourists. Whereas the old one sends them home from the island of Kos. The airport has begun a new life!

Services

The services provided in the Hippocratic building may seem meager compared to other similar European airports. You will not find business or conference rooms here. But there is a police station with friendly staff, as well as a medical center. In stores that offer duty-free products, you can buy alcohol, cigarettes and perfumes. Here you can find many restaurants, cafes and bars that serve this airport. Kos Island is not a place where you can change money. This procedure is best done in Athens.



How to get to Kos

The airport mainly accepts only domestic and charter flights. Residents of Russia mainly get to the island through the capital of Greece, Athens. Flight with a transfer usually takes about 12 hours. The cost of one-way tickets varies, starting at 7,000 rubles. The main airlines serving this route are Ageen and Air Berlin. You may have to choose a combination to get to this airport.

Greece, Kos - what to see?

Kos island is not only amazing beaches, gentle sun and white sand. First of all, this place keeps in memory the history and culture of Ancient Greece. Among the popular attractions of the island are:

  • Ancient Asklepion, where Hippocrates himself created the foundations of modern Western medicine.
  • Archaeological Museum, which was opened in the 1930s. It stores the unique relics found on the island.
  • The largest Roman villa is located here. It has 37 rooms that have preserved the rich atmosphere of houses of the III century AD.

Thus, if you are determined to visit the amazing island of Kos, the Hippocrates airport is always waiting for its guests!
