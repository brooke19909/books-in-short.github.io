Historical information

As early as 1827, in Crimea, an eccentric who liked to explore all kinds of ancient buildings met a barge littered with stones, which the inhabitants later used to build their houses. His attention was attracted by two plates of unknown origin with relief traces on their surface. On one was an image of an ancient horseman, on the other - an inscription in the ancient Greek manner. When excavations were carried out, a lot of historical values ‚Äã‚Äãwere discovered at the local quarry: a piece of marble stone with a rock profile of two men (old and young), coins of Greece and Rome, an inscription about Scythian kings who lived many years ago.

The name of one of them was known from the descriptions of the historian of the city of Olbia. After comparing the drawings found on marble and coins, it was found that this is an image of the ruler of Skilur. Thanks to these comparisons, an ancient state was discovered - Naples Scythian. Simferopol - its location. The location for the city was very well chosen. One side looks to the north, to the east there is a steep cliff of the Peter Rocks, and in the west you can see a defensive wall and an impassable beam. The Scythians for a long time waged war with the main competitor - Chersonesos. Later the city was attacked by the Goths and completely defeated Naples Scythian - Simferopol still remembers those events.

origin of name

Naples Scythian (Simferopol) is considered one of the royal fortresses of the Crimean peninsula. Perhaps in this city was the residence of Scythian overlords. Translated from Greek, the word "Naples" means "the newest city." Of course, the Greeks were just uncomfortable pronouncing such a long name. Therefore, without thinking twice, they renamed it to a more convenient and understandable name - Naples.

This is not the only case in the history of the Crimean peninsula when other peoples revise and rename the original name. After the war, many other cities suffered the same fate. Even small rivers and Tatar villages were renamed . According to the ancient Greek geographer, the fortress was built in order to defend itself in a war against the Greek people. But this is not yet proven.
